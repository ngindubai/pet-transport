1. Business Overview

The Opportunity

People relocating internationally need to bring their pets. This is a route-based service: "Ship my dog from UK to Australia" or "Pet transport from Dubai to Germany". The route-based URL structure creates thousands of combinatorial pages, and no competitor owns the route-level SEO.

Scored 8.5/10 in the cross-border opportunity report. Ranked #3 overall. Massive page potential through route combinations (150+ countries = 22,500 route pairs). PetRelocation.com exists but only has country-level pages, not route-level.

Market Size

IPATA estimates 500,000+ international pet relocations annually. Growing fast with remote work and expat mobility. UK alone: ~50,000 pets imported/exported per year.

Average Deal Value

$2,000-$8,000 per booking. Higher for long-haul routes (US→Australia: $5K-$8K). Dogs cost more than cats. Exotic pets cost most. Margin: 30-50% after airline cargo, vet certificates and customs clearance.

Competition

PetRelocation.com is the biggest. 150+ countries, 40K pets shipped, 20+ years. They have country pages but no route-level pages. That's the gap. Nobody ranks for "ship dog from Thailand to UK" with a dedicated page.

2.2 URL Architecture: Route-Based Combinatorial Pages

The Power of Route Combinations

Pet transport is inherently route-based. "Ship dog from Thailand to UK" has different requirements, costs, airlines and timelines than "Ship dog from Thailand to Australia" (which has Australia's strict 10-day quarantine). Each route combination is genuinely unique content because the import rules of the DESTINATION country combined with the export rules of the ORIGIN country create a unique regulatory picture.

5. Google Compliance & Quality Standards

Critical Challenge: Scaled Content at 5,000+ Pages

With 5,000+ route pages, Google's scaled content abuse policy is the biggest risk. Every route page must pass the "would this page exist if search engines didn't?" test. The answer is YES: because the regulatory requirements differ per route, each page serves a genuine informational need.

The gates:

  1. Google Quality Gate: Unique H1, unique meta, 800+ words of body content, 40%+ unique content per page, 3+ FAQs with route-specific answers, HowTo + FAQPage schema.
  2. Regulatory Accuracy Gate: Import requirements match the official government portal for that country. Export requirements verified. Airline policies current. Quarantine info correct. Breed bans accurate.
  3. Anti-Template Gate: No two route pages should feel like a find-and-replace job. The Chameleon must vary structure, sentence patterns, and anecdotes across pages.

17. SEO & LLM Optimization Plan (Parallel Workstream)

Why This Runs in Parallel

The original build plan treats SEO as a task within each phase (task 1.11, 5.5). That is not enough. SEO and LLM optimization must run continuously from the moment Phase 1 route pages go live. While Phase 2 routes are being built, Phase 1 route pages should already be indexed, ranking, and being refined. Every week a live route page sits without monitoring is a week of lost ranking signal.

LLM ranking (Generative Engine Optimization / GEO) is entirely absent from the original plan. As of 2026, a growing share of pet transport queries are answered by ChatGPT, Perplexity, Google AI Overviews, and Gemini. Expat families researching relocation increasingly ask AI assistants "how do I ship my dog from Dubai to the UK?" or "what are Australia's pet quarantine rules?". If route pages are not structured for LLM citation, we lose this entire channel.

Workers responsible: The Optimiser (on-page SEO + LLM structure), The Scout (monitoring + intelligence), The Connector (authority signals + backlinks).

17.1 LLM Ranking Strategy (Generative Engine Optimization)

What LLMs Need to Cite Your Content

LLMs (ChatGPT, Perplexity, Gemini, Claude) cite sources when they find content that is: (1) authoritative and well-sourced, (2) structured clearly enough to extract clean answers, (3) unique data not available elsewhere, and (4) consistently entity-rich. Pet transport is ideal for LLM citation because users ask specific route questions ("Can I fly my French Bulldog from Singapore to the UK?") and expect factual, sourced answers about quarantine, vaccinations, and airline policies.

LLM Optimization TechniqueImplementationWorkerApply From
Definitive Answer Format Every FAQ uses "Question > Direct 1-sentence answer > Supporting detail with named source" pattern. LLMs extract the first sentence as the citation.
