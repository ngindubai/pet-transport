Checklist

What your pet needs

Every item below must be completed and verified before your pet can travel. Expand each category for the detail.

Microchip
15-digit ISO 11784, or Annex A of ISO 11785, compliant microchip required before entry. Source: PetTravel.com, 'Zambia Pet Import Requirements,' citing Zambian government import guidance, 2026.
Rabies vaccination
Dogs and cats over 3 months old must be vaccinated for rabies between 30 days and 12 months before entering Zambia. Source: PetTravel.com, 2026, citing Zambian import guidance.
Rabies titre test
Not mandated by Zambia's own import rules as a condition of entry. It is still sensible to keep proof of a valid titre result on file, since transiting countries or airlines further along a connecting route may ask for one.
Quarantine
Waived for pets that meet the documentation and vaccination requirements. Non-compliant animals face quarantine, return to origin, or destruction, all at the importer's cost. Source: PetTravel.com, 2026.
Import permit
Veterinary Permit required in advance from the Department of Research & Specialist Services (the former Department of Veterinary & Tsetse Control Services). No published online application; apply directly and allow processing time before travel. Source: Embassy of the Republic of Zambia, Washington D.C., 'Procedures for Importation of Livestock and Pets into Zambia,' zambiaembassy.org, accessed 2026.
Health certificate
Health certificate from the exporting country's official vet, prepared using the Zambian import permit as the template, must accompany the pet on arrival. The importer must notify the nearest Zambian Veterinary Officer on arrival for inspection. Source: Embassy of the Republic of Zambia, 2026.
Export permit
Not a separate permit; covered by the export health certificate below.
Veterinary inspection
Official (APHA-registered) vet inspection required before the certificate is signed, checking microchip, vaccination record and general fitness to travel.
Government endorsement
Applied for online through the government's export health certificate service; the vet must also be registered and checked by APHA before they can sign.
Health certificate
Export Health Certificate 3928, 'Export cats and dogs to Zambia,' currently version 3, guidance notes updated January 2024. Applied for at exporter.apply-export-health-certificate.service.gov.uk (England, Scotland, Wales) or via DAERA's DECS system in Northern Ireland. Requires a Government Gateway account and a Defra account. Source: GOV.UK, 'Export cats and dogs to Zambia: certificate 3928,' 2026.

Airlines

Carriers on this route

Not all airlines accept live animals on this route. Confirm the operating carrier's current policy for every flight segment.

Ethiopian Airlines
Flies Heathrow to Addis Ababa (ADD), then onward to Lusaka (LUN), with 1-2 flights daily on the London-Addis leg. Small pets up to 8kg with an approved container can travel in the cabin depending on aircraft type; larger animals go as cargo. Snub-nosed breeds are refused outright.
Cabin and Cargo
Kenya Airways
Nonstop from Heathrow to Nairobi (KQ100, up to twice daily), then a connecting cargo flight on to Lusaka. Live animals travel as cargo only on Kenya Airways, never in the cabin or as checked baggage, aside from certified assistance dogs.
Cargo Only
South African Airways
No direct UK service, but has historically flown Johannesburg (JNB) to Lusaka, subject to current schedule. A connecting carrier such as British Airways or Virgin Atlantic covers the Heathrow-Johannesburg leg.
Cargo Only
British Airways
No direct Lusaka route. IAG Cargo can accept a live animal booking out of Heathrow, but it needs to be arranged through an IPATA- or ATA-registered pet relocation agent for the onward interline transfer into Zambia.
Cargo Only
Costs

What this route typically costs

UK vet visits and any required booster vaccination: GBP 60-150
APHA Export Health Certificate 3928: official vet inspection and paperwork, typically GBP 150-300; this is a vet-set fee, not a fixed government tariff
Zambia import permit from the Department of Veterinary Services: fee not published; apply directly and budget for correspondence time
IATA-compliant travel crate: GBP 70-250
Air cargo London to Lusaka via Addis Ababa, Nairobi or Johannesburg: GBP 1,200-2,800
Total typical range: GBP 1,800-3,800

Critical points

EHC 3928 needs both a Government Gateway/Defra account and an APHA-checked official vet in place before the certificate can be applied for; leaving vet registration to the last minute is a common delay.

Zambia's import permit has no published online application. It comes from direct correspondence with the Department of Veterinary Services in Lusaka, so build in real lead time rather than assuming a same-week turnaround.

The rabies vaccination has to fall inside Zambia's 30-day-to-12-month window before entry. A vaccination given too early or too close to travel can fall outside that window.

No airline flies London to Lusaka nonstop. Every routing adds a connection, most reliably via Addis Ababa or Nairobi, so book the export certificate appointment and the flight together rather than separately.

The rules for bringing a pet into Zambia

Zambia keeps its requirements reasonable, though a few steps have fixed timing. Map those out first and the rest falls into place.

How to keep this move on track

Use an official, government-authorised vet for the health certificate, not just your usual clinic. Many destinations only accept certificates signed by an approved vet.

If this is your first international move, consider an IPATA-registered pet transport agent. They handle the documents, airline booking and crate sizing, and can troubleshoot problems.

Get your pet used to the travel crate weeks in advance. A calm, crate-familiar animal travels far better than one meeting the crate for the first time at the airport.

Confirm live animal acceptance on every flight segment before booking. Not all aircraft or connections take pets, and cargo terms can differ leg to leg.
