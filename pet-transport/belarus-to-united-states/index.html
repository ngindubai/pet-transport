Checklist

What your pet needs

Every item below must be completed and verified before your pet can travel. Expand each category for the detail.

Microchip
Universal scanner-detectable microchip required for dogs, implanted before the rabies vaccination used for the titre test. A vaccine given first is considered invalid. No federal microchip rule applies to cats. Source: CDC, 'Bringing a Dog into the US,' updated 15 April 2026.
Rabies vaccination
Belarus sits on the CDC's high-risk list for dog rabies, so a foreign-issued vaccination record and a government-endorsed certificate govern entry rather than a simple vaccination check. First vaccination at 12 weeks or older, and at least 28 days before entry. No federal rabies rule applies to cats at all. Source: CDC, 'Entry Requirements for Foreign-Vaccinated Dogs from High-Risk Countries,' 2026.
Rabies titre test
Required for a dog whose CDC Dog Import Form travel history includes Belarus within the last 6 months, unless the owner accepts a 28-day quarantine instead. Blood must be drawn at least 30 days after the first rabies vaccination and at least 28 days before US entry, tested only at a CDC-approved laboratory. Cats face no titre requirement of any kind.
Quarantine
No routine quarantine for a dog presenting a passing CDC-approved titre result on arrival. A 28-day quarantine at a CDC-registered animal care facility applies only where no valid titre is presented, entirely at the importer's cost. Cats face no quarantine requirement.
Import permit
No formal import permit. Every dog needs a free CDC Dog Import Form, completed online and valid for a single entry on the stated arrival date. Cats need no import form or permit at federal level.
Health certificate
Certification of Foreign Rabies Vaccination and Microchip form, completed by the Belarusian vet and endorsed by the territorial veterinary office, valid 30 days from signature. This functions as the health declaration for a dog; cats need nothing equivalent. Source: CDC, 2026.
Export permit
No standalone export permit beyond the veterinary certificate below for a single owned pet. Moving 5 or more animals, or any pet changing ownership, follows a different commercial process; confirm with the State Veterinary and Food Supervision Department before booking a multi-pet or rescue move.
Veterinary inspection
Pre-export examination and certification by a Belarusian state veterinarian, checking the microchip, vaccination record and general health.
Government endorsement
International veterinary certificate endorsed by the local territorial veterinary office. The same office also acts as the government-endorsing authority for the CDC's own Certification of Foreign Rabies Vaccination and Microchip form, a separate US-specific document covered below.
Health certificate
International veterinary health certificate confirming microchip, rabies vaccination and general fitness, alongside the CDC's Certification of Foreign Rabies Vaccination and Microchip form, completed no more than 30 days before travel and valid for 30 days from signature. Source: State Veterinary and Food Supervision Department, Ministry of Agriculture and Food of the Republic of Belarus; CDC, 'Entry Requirements for Foreign-Vaccinated Dogs from High-Risk Countries,' 2026.

LOT Polish Airlines
Flies Warsaw direct to Chicago O'Hare, JFK, Newark and Miami. No flight touches Belarus itself, so this is the transatlantic leg once a pet has cleared the overland transfer and the EU border crossing. Bookings run through LOT Cargo, not the passenger channel.
Cargo Only
Turkish Airlines
A useful alternative via Istanbul when one relocation agent needs to manage both the Belarus exit and the US arrival. Critical points

No airline has flown between Belarus and any EU, UK or US airport since June 2021, and the ban was widened again in December 2025. Every pet leaving Belarus starts on the road, into Poland, before any flight is possible.

The CDC requires the dog to land at the specific airport hosting its reserved CDC-registered animal care facility. That has to be reconciled with the fact that no flight starts in Belarus. Book the overland transfer and the transatlantic leg together, not separately, or the two constraints can point at different airports.

A dog without a passing titre result faces a 28-day quarantine at a CDC-registered facility, entirely at the owner's cost. Missing the 30-day-after-vaccination minimum for the blood draw, or the 28-day-before-entry deadline, forces that choice.

None of the CDC's high-risk paperwork, titre test or facility reservation applies to cats. A cat leaving Belarus for the US needs the same Belarusian export certificate as a dog and nothing further at the US border.

United States entry requirements at a glance

Getting a pet into United States means meeting a demanding set of rules with no shortcuts. Start from the earliest deadline and work forward from there.

Getting the details right

Work backwards from your travel date and add a buffer. Fixed waiting periods mean a single slipped appointment can push the whole move back by weeks.

Check the airline’s crate size and labelling rules early. An undersized or wrongly labelled crate is a frequent reason pets are refused at check-in.

Ask about temperature embargoes for your travel window. Summer and winter cargo restrictions can rule out certain routes and dates entirely.

Book the titre test laboratory well ahead. Approved labs have limited slots and the sample takes time to process, so this is not something to leave to the final weeks.
