Requirements

Singapore entry requirements

Every item below must be in place before your pet can enter. We verify and track each one.

Microchip
ISO 11784/11785 chip required, fitted before the rabies vaccination.
Rabies vaccination
Required before export. Because Laos is Schedule III, the pet is also revaccinated against rabies on arrival regardless of the DLF vaccination record.
Rabies titre test
WOAH-prescribed rabies neutralising antibody test, minimum 0.5 IU/ml, blood drawn at least 28 days after vaccination, at a WOAH reference laboratory or one in a Schedule I or II country. Few laboratories inside Laos handle this test to WOAH standard, so the sample typically ships to Thailand or further afield for processing, adding real time on top of standard turnaround. The result is valid for export between 90 days and 12 months after the draw.
Quarantine
Minimum 30 days at the Animal Quarantine Centre (AQC). Current rates (1 December 2025 to 30 November 2026): SGD 26 a day fan-cooled, SGD 35 a day air-conditioned, plus a SGD 75 transfer fee.
Import permit
AVS import licence via GoBusiness Licensing Portal. Fee SGD 50 standard, SGD 100 express.
Health certificate
DLF-endorsed certificate in AVS format. Mandatory AVS-recognised pet agent for CAPQ clearance from 1 April 2026.
Leaving Laos

Export requirements

Export permit
Export licence required from the Department of Livestock and Fisheries (DLF). Application must reach DLF at least 15 days before the export date. DLF inspects the animal and issues the Animal Health Certificate and Export Licence together.
Health certificate
Official health certificate from a DLF-registered veterinarian, reformatted to AVS Singapore's certificate model and issued within 7 days of departure.

Critical points

Scoot operates a nonstop Singapore-Vientiane flight up to seven times a week, but Scoot does not accept any live animal as checked baggage or cargo. Do not assume this convenient direct route can carry a pet; it cannot, and the pet must route via Bangkok on a different carrier entirely.

Laos defaults to AVS Schedule III since it is named on neither Schedule I nor Schedule II, confirmed against avs.nparks.gov.sg, fetched 21 July 2026.

DLF's 15-day export notice is short compared with the 90-day AVS titre wait, so it rarely drives the timeline, but missing it still delays the export licence.

From 1 April 2026, CAPQ clearance for Schedule III arrivals must go through an AVS-recognised pet agent.

How Singapore handles incoming pets

Singapore has a detailed set of import rules, and each one needs to be satisfied before travel. None of it is unusual, but the order and timing are what catch people out.

What experienced owners do

Book the titre test laboratory well ahead. Approved labs have limited slots and the sample takes time to process, so this is not something to leave to the final weeks.

Microchip before you vaccinate. The microchip must be in place before the rabies shot for the vaccination to count, and getting this order wrong is the most common and costly mistake.

Use an official, government-authorised vet for the health certificate, not just your usual clinic. Many destinations only accept certificates signed by an approved vet.

If this is your first international move, consider an IPATA-registered pet transport agent. They handle the documents, airline booking and crate sizing, and can troubleshoot problems.
