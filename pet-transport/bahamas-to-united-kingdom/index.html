Step by step

The Bahamas to United Kingdom import process

01
Before the rabies vaccination used for the titre test.
Microchip implanted (ISO 11784/11785), if not already done.

Responsible: Bahamian vet

02
As early as possible; the two waiting periods below both run from later dates.
Rabies vaccination given, or a current booster confirmed, after the microchip is in place.

Responsible: Bahamian vet

03
No sooner than 30 full days after vaccination.
Blood drawn for the rabies antibody titre test and sent to an approved laboratory.

Responsible: Bahamian vet / approved laboratory

04
Results typically take 2 to 3 weeks; the 3-month clock is already running.
Titre result confirmed at 0.5 IU/ml or above. The 3-calendar-month wait is counted from the blood draw date, not the result date.

Responsible: Owner tracks the dates

05
Close to departure, once the flight is booked.
BAHFSA export health certificate obtained.

Responsible: Bahamian vet / BAHFSA

06
As soon as the end date of the 3-month wait is known.
Flight booked, direct via British Airways or via Miami on American Airlines, checking IAG Cargo's temperature embargo dates.

Responsible: Owner / relocation agent

07
Within 10 days of arrival in Great Britain.
GB Pet Health Certificate issued once the 3-month wait has passed.

Responsible: Accredited vet in the Bahamas

08
24 to 120 hours before landing in Great Britain.
Dog given praziquantel tapeworm treatment (not required for cats).

Responsible: Vet at the point of treatment

Requirements

United Kingdom entry requirements

Every item below must be in place before your pet can enter. We verify and track each one.

Microchip
ISO 11784/11785 microchip required, implanted before or on the same date as the rabies vaccination used for the titre test. Source: GOV.UK, 2026.
Rabies vaccination
The Bahamas does not appear on either of GB's Part 1 or Part 2 listed-country tables under the retained Regulation (EU) No 577/2013, so it's treated as unlisted, the same category as most of the Caribbean and Central America. Source: legislation.gov.uk, Annex II; GOV.UK, 'Bringing your pet dog, cat or ferret to Great Britain,' 2026.
Rabies titre test
Rabies antibody titre test required, blood drawn no sooner than 30 full days after vaccination, with a result of at least 0.5 IU/ml.
Quarantine
None for a pet that meets the unlisted-country pet travel rules in full.
Import permit
None needed for a compliant pet under the standard unlisted-country route. An import licence only applies to pets going into approved quarantine because they haven't met the rules.
Health certificate
Great Britain Pet Health Certificate, valid 10 days from issue to arrival.
Leaving Bahamas

Export requirements

Export permit
BAHFSA's Trade Facilitation Unit, which runs the ePermit system for animals entering the Bahamas, also handles export certification for pets leaving Nassau. There's no published export fee schedule online in the same detail as the import side, so confirm the current form and any charge directly with BAHFSA before travel.
Veterinary inspection
Pre-export examination by a Bahamian-registered vet, confirming general health and a current rabies vaccination.
Government endorsement
Government endorsement of the vet's certificate by BAHFSA, where required for the destination country. Confirm with BAHFSA whether GB entry needs this extra step, since the authority's published guidance focuses mainly on animals arriving into the Bahamas.
Health certificate
Veterinary health certificate confirming rabies immunity and fitness to travel, typically dated close to departure by a Bahamian-registered vet.
Costs

What this route typically costs

BAHFSA export health certificate and any endorsement fee: BSD 50-150 (confirm current fee directly with BAHFSA)
Bahamian vet visits, vaccination and blood draw: USD 150-300
Rabies titre test / laboratory fee: USD 150-250
GB Pet Health Certificate: GBP 150-300
IATA-compliant travel crate: USD 100-300
Air cargo Nassau to Heathrow via British Airways / IAG Cargo, including handling agent: GBP 900-2,200

Critical points

The Bahamas is unlisted for GB pet travel despite being a short flight from the US mainland. The titre test and the 3-month wait from the blood draw date apply in full, the same as for far less accessible origins.

IAG Cargo's temperature embargo, above 29C/85F or below 7C/45F at any hub, regularly affects Nassau bookings given the Bahamas' climate. Late spring through early autumn departures often need an early-morning slot to stay inside the window.

Confirm BAHFSA's current export certificate fee and turnaround directly. The published fee schedule online covers the import side, the USD 11.20 ePermit, in detail, but not export.

Brachycephalic breeds face a 10% larger minimum crate size and extra restrictions above 75F/24C on British Airways / IAG Cargo.

Hurricane season runs June to November in the Bahamas, and can disrupt flight schedules and cargo handling at Nassau with little notice; building a buffer into the travel date is worth it.

What United Kingdom requires

The requirements for United Kingdom are thorough rather than difficult. Work through them methodically and confirm anything you are unsure about with the official source.

Avoiding the common mistakes

Confirm live animal acceptance on every flight segment before booking. Not all aircraft or connections take pets, and cargo terms can differ leg to leg.

Keep every original document together and take copies. Border officials want originals, and a missing signature or date can hold up clearance.

Work backwards from your travel date and add a buffer. Fixed waiting periods mean a single slipped appointment can push the whole move back by weeks.

Check the airline’s crate size and labelling rules early. An undersized or wrongly labelled crate is a frequent reason pets are refused at check-in.
