Requirements

Armenia entry requirements

Every item below must be in place before your pet can enter. We verify and track each one.

Microchip
Required. ISO 11784/11785 (15-digit). Must be readable on arrival. Armenia aligns with international standards on microchipping.
Rabies vaccination
Required and current at time of arrival. A valid primary vaccination (not expired) is sufficient from Belgium, an EU country with a controlled rabies programme.
Rabies titre test
Not required for pets arriving from Belgium. Belgium is an EU member state and is not on Armenia's titre test requirement list for personal companion pet imports.
Quarantine
No routine quarantine for compliant pets arriving with a valid FASFC-endorsed health certificate and current vaccinations.
Import permit
No advance import permit required for personal companion pets. Customs declaration and vet inspection on arrival at Zvartnots (EVN) cargo terminal.
Health certificate
EU-format official health certificate endorsed by FASFC. Must cover microchip, rabies vaccination status, and general health status. Presented to Armenian customs and SSFS inspectors on arrival.
Leaving Belgium

Export requirements

Export permit
No formal export permit required for personal companion pets leaving Belgium.
Veterinary inspection
Pre-travel clinical inspection by an FASFC-authorised vet. Pet must be fit to travel and free of visible signs of disease.
Health certificate
EU-format official veterinary health certificate prepared by a FASFC-authorised vet. Endorsed by FASFC (Federal Agency for the Safety of the Food Chain) before departure. Armenia accepts EU-endorsed certificates for import. Valid 10 days from examination.

Critical points

Armenia does not require a titre test from EU-origin pets. However, if you are planning onward travel from Armenia to any third country, confirm that country's requirements. Some destinations require the titre test regardless of where the journey began.

Brussels has two airports: Brussels Airport (BRU, Zaventem) and Brussels South Charleroi (CRL). Cargo handling for pet transport is primarily from BRU. Confirm your carrier's departure point before booking.

Temperature embargoes during summer months on Gulf hub carriers (Emirates, Qatar, Flydubai) can restrict pet cargo acceptance. Plan travel for spring or autumn where possible.

Brachycephalic breeds (Bulldogs, French Bulldogs, Pugs, Persian cats) face restrictions on some carriers. Verify before booking if your pet is flat-faced.

Meeting Armenia's entry requirements

Getting a pet into Armenia is a middle of the road process. The rules are well defined, so most of the work is simply staying on schedule.

Advice worth following

Confirm live animal acceptance on every flight segment before booking. Not all aircraft or connections take pets, and cargo terms can differ leg to leg.

Keep every original document together and take copies. Border officials want originals, and a missing signature or date can hold up clearance.

Work backwards from your travel date and add a buffer. Fixed waiting periods mean a single slipped appointment can push the whole move back by weeks.

Check the airline’s crate size and labelling rules early. An undersized or wrongly labelled crate is a frequent reason pets are refused at check-in.
