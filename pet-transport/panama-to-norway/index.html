Requirements

What your pet needs to enter Norway

Every item below must be in place before your pet can travel. We manage and verify each one.

Microchip

Rabies vaccination
Required. At least 21 days post-primary before travel.

Critical points to read before you book

Panama to Norway has TWO distinct mandatory requirements that both must be met: (1) the FAVN titre test and 90-day wait (unlisted country requirement), and (2) the Echinococcus tapeworm treatment for dogs 24-120 hours before arrival (Norway-specific requirement). Both must be completed. Cats are exempt from the Echinococcus treatment.

The Echinococcus treatment must be administered and documented separately from the titre test process. A vet at the layover airport (London, Amsterdam, or Frankfurt) must administer praziquantel or epsiprantel and sign the health certificate addendum. Allow at least four to six hours at the layover for this.

Norway is not an EU member. While it follows EU-equivalent rules, confirm requirements with Mattilsynet at mattilsynet.no or with PetTransportGlobal before finalising travel.

Norway entry requirements at a glance

Bringing a pet into Norway involves a fair amount of documentation. Keep everything organised and check each item against the current requirements before you book.

Getting the details right

Ask about temperature embargoes for your travel window. Summer and winter cargo restrictions can rule out certain routes and dates entirely.

Book the titre test laboratory well ahead. Approved labs have limited slots and the sample takes time to process, so this is not something to leave to the final weeks.

Microchip before you vaccinate. The microchip must be in place before the rabies shot for the vaccination to count, and getting this order wrong is the most common and costly mistake.

Use an official, government-authorised vet for the health certificate, not just your usual clinic. Many destinations only accept certificates signed by an approved vet.
