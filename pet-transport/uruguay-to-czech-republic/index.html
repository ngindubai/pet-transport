Critical points to read before you book

Uruguay is an unlisted third country. The 90-day waiting period after the blood draw is mandatory and cannot be shortened. Total preparation is 20-26 weeks from microchip to travel.

The multi-leg routing from Uruguay adds handling risk at each cargo transfer. Confirm live animal cargo acceptance on each individual sector before finalising the booking. Not all flights on each airline accept live animals on every route.

Prague Václav Havel Airport cargo terminal requires pre-notification for live animal shipments. SVS inspectors are thorough in checking titre test documentation. The blood draw date plus 90 days must clearly predate the arrival at PRG.

Meeting Czech Republic's entry requirements

The requirements for Czech Republic are thorough rather than difficult. Work through them methodically and confirm anything you are unsure about with the official source.

Advice worth following

Ask about temperature embargoes for your travel window. Summer and winter cargo restrictions can rule out certain routes and dates entirely.

Book the titre test laboratory well ahead. Approved labs have limited slots and the sample takes time to process, so this is not something to leave to the final weeks.

Microchip before you vaccinate. The microchip must be in place before the rabies shot for the vaccination to count, and getting this order wrong is the most common and costly mistake.

Use an official, government-authorised vet for the health certificate, not just your usual clinic. Many destinations only accept certificates signed by an approved vet.
