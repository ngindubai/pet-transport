Checklist

What your pet needs

Every item below must be completed and verified before your pet can travel. Expand each category for the detail.

Microchip
Required. ISO 11784/11785-compliant, implanted before the rabies vaccination series so the number appears correctly on every subsequent document.
Rabies vaccination
Given no less than 60 days and no more than 1 year before departure, per CFIA's Brunei-specific export guidance. Puppies and kittens under 3 months are exempt from the vaccination requirement but not from the rest of the process.
Rabies titre test
A rabies neutralising antibody titre test (FAVN or equivalent) is mandatory, drawn at least 30 days after vaccination at an approved laboratory. A result of 0.5 IU/mL or higher passes, and it stays valid for 1 year from the sample date.
Quarantine
Canada is not on Brunei's short list of rabies-free 'scheduled' countries (Australia, Ireland, New Zealand, Sabah, Sarawak, Singapore and the UK), so quarantine applies regardless of paperwork. With a passing titre test and valid rabies certificate, the minimum is 14 days in a government facility, balance served at home. Without a titre test, government sources on this point cite 30 days minimum. Confirm the current figure directly with Brunei's Department of Agriculture and Agrifood before booking flights, as quarantine terms are set at the department's discretion.
Import permit
Required before the animal leaves Canada. Apply to the Biosecurity Division of Brunei's Department of Agriculture and Agrifood (DAA), under the Ministry of Primary Resources and Tourism (MPRT). Dogs must arrive via Brunei International Airport (BWN) as manifested cargo, with the Biosecurity Officer notified at least 7 days ahead of arrival.
Health certificate
Endorsed by a government veterinarian close to travel; sources vary on the exact window, some citing 10 days, others 14. Treat 10 days as the safer planning figure and reconfirm with DAA at the permit stage.
Export permit
No general export permit exists for a personal pet leaving Canada. What's required instead is an export health certificate, completed by a licensed vet and then endorsed by an official CFIA veterinarian.
Veterinary inspection
A licensed Canadian vet examines the animal, confirms the ISO microchip, and completes the export certificate ahead of the CFIA appointment.
Government endorsement
An official CFIA veterinarian must endorse the certificate in person, at a pre-booked appointment, while the pet is still physically in Canada. CFIA has no process to endorse a certificate after the animal has already flown, so leaving this appointment too late is the single most common way owners get stuck.
Health certificate
Timed to sit inside Brunei's own validity window on arrival, typically issued close to departure and carried alongside the CFIA-endorsed export certificate.

Airlines

Carriers on this route

Not all airlines accept live animals on this route. Confirm the operating carrier's current policy for every flight segment.

Cathay Pacific Cargo
Toronto or Vancouver to Hong Kong under the Cathay Live Animal service, IATA CEIV Live Animals accredited. From September 2025, Cathay only accepts bookings from IPATA, ATA or IATA LAR-trained agents, so this leg has to run through a registered pet shipper, not a direct owner booking.
Cargo Only
Malaysia Airlines Cargo (MASkargo)
Connects onward from Kuala Lumpur to Bandar Seri Begawan (BWN). MASkargo explicitly accepts pets to Brunei as manifested cargo, but not as checked baggage on that specific route, and drop-off is at the MASkargo Animal Hotel at least 8 hours before departure.
Cargo Only
Royal Brunei Airlines Cargo
Flies BWN to Hong Kong and BWN to Kuala Lumpur, making it the practical final-leg carrier from either hub. Its own cargo division states IATA Live Animal Regulations compliance for household pets, but excludes a long list of dog breeds outright, including all Terriers, Boxers, Pugs, Bulldogs, Spaniels, Mastiffs, Chow Chows, Pit Bulls, Shih Tzus and Chihuahuas.

Costs

What this route typically costs

CFIA export certificate endorsement fee: approximately CAD 20-40 per certificate.
Rabies titre test (FAVN or equivalent) at an approved Canadian lab: roughly CAD 150-250.
IATA-compliant crate sized for the pet, plus registered agent handling fee: CAD 400-900.
Air freight, Toronto or Vancouver to Hong Kong or Kuala Lumpur, then on to BWN: CAD 2,500-4,500, depending on weight and routing.
Brunei DAA import permit and quarantine facility charges, billed in Brunei dollars (BND, pegged 1:1 to SGD): confirm the current fee schedule with DAA directly, as this varies by facility and length of stay.
Total typical range excluding quarantine fees: CAD 3,000-5,700 over 10-14 weeks.

Critical points

Quarantine is not avoidable on this route. Even a dog or cat with a clean titre test and every document in order still serves a minimum 14 days in a Brunei government facility, because Canada sits outside the handful of countries Brunei treats as rabies-free.

There is no direct Canada-Brunei flight, and Royal Brunei Airlines does not serve North America at all. Every shipment connects through a third country, most practically Hong Kong or Kuala Lumpur.

Singapore Airlines Cargo excludes Brunei from its live animal routes entirely, despite Singapore being a common connection point for the wider region. Don't assume a Singapore routing works for the final leg without checking the carrier first.

A long list of dog breeds is banned outright from import, including Akita, Pit Bull types, Japanese Tosa and several mastiff-type breeds. Cats carry no equivalent breed restriction. Confirm your specific breed against DAA's current list before committing to any date.

Cathay Pacific Cargo now only accepts pet bookings from registered IPATA, ATA or IATA LAR-trained agents, a change that took effect in September 2025. A direct owner booking on that leg will be turned away.

The rules for bringing a pet into Brunei

Brunei takes biosecurity seriously, and the requirements reflect that. The paperwork is exacting, so accuracy matters as much as timing.

How to keep this move on track

Keep every original document together and take copies. Border officials want originals, and a missing signature or date can hold up clearance.

Work backwards from your travel date and add a buffer. Fixed waiting periods mean a single slipped appointment can push the whole move back by weeks.

Check the airline’s crate size and labelling rules early. An undersized or wrongly labelled crate is a frequent reason pets are refused at check-in.

Ask about temperature embargoes for your travel window. Summer and winter cargo restrictions can rule out certain routes and dates entirely.
