In short: Preparing to move a pet from Uganda to Portugal typically takes around 20-28 weeks. There is no routine quarantine on arrival for compliant pets. A rabies titre test is required, which adds a fixed waiting period, so start early.

Requirements

Portugal entry requirements

Every item below must be in place before your pet can enter. We verify and track each one.

Microchip
Required. ISO 11784/11785, before the rabies vaccination.
Rabies vaccination
Required. Administered after microchipping, at least 21 days before the titre test blood draw.
Rabies titre test
Required. Uganda is not on the EU's approved list under Implementing Regulation (EU) 2026/636. Titre test at an approved laboratory, minimum 0.5 IU/ml, blood drawn at least 30 days after vaccination, with a 90-day gap before the certificate is issued. Source: Commission Delegated Regulation (EU) 2026/131.
Quarantine
No quarantine for compliant arrivals. DGAV inspection at the Lisbon Airport cargo terminal.
Import permit
Not required for personal companion pets.
Health certificate
EU-format health certificate, MAAIF-endorsed, valid 10 days from examination date. Inspected by DGAV officials at LIS.
Leaving Uganda

Export requirements

Export permit
No formal export permit required for personal companion pets leaving Uganda.
Health certificate
Official export health certificate from a MAAIF-authorised vet, endorsed by MAAIF, reformatted to EU standard. Issued within 10 days of travel.
Costs

What this route typically costs

Titre test and international sample shipping: EUR 100-220
MAAIF certificate and endorsement: UGX 100,000-250,000 (approx EUR 24-60)
Cargo Entebbe to Lisbon via chosen hub: EUR 900-2,000 depending on crate size
IATA-compliant travel crate: EUR 80-300
Pet transport agent coordination: EUR 400-900
Total typical range: EUR 1,500-3,500 over a 5-7 month window

Critical points

DGAV inspects every incoming pet at Lisbon's cargo terminal. Incomplete paperwork on arrival can mean delay or refusal, so match every document to the health certificate exactly.

The 90-day wait runs from the blood draw date, not the date you receive the titre result.

Microchip before vaccination, without exception. Reverse the order and the sequence restarts.

No approved laboratory operates within Uganda, so international shipping of the blood sample adds lead time that needs confirming with your vet.

What Portugal requires

Bringing a pet into Portugal involves a fair amount of documentation. Keep everything organised and check each item against the current requirements before you book.

Avoiding the common mistakes

Ask about temperature embargoes for your travel window. Summer and winter cargo restrictions can rule out certain routes and dates entirely.

Book the titre test laboratory well ahead. Approved labs have limited slots and the sample takes time to process, so this is not something to leave to the final weeks.

Microchip before you vaccinate. The microchip must be in place before the rabies shot for the vaccination to count, and getting this order wrong is the most common and costly mistake.

Use an official, government-authorised vet for the health certificate, not just your usual clinic. Many destinations only accept certificates signed by an approved vet.
