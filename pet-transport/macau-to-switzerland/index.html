In short: Preparing to move a pet from Macau to Switzerland typically takes around 18-24 weeks. There is no routine quarantine on arrival for compliant pets. A rabies titre test is required, which adds a fixed waiting period, so start early.

Step by step

The Macau to Switzerland import process

01
Early, ideally before any vaccination booster is due.
Confirm the ISO microchip is implanted and scannable.

Responsible: Vet in Macau.

02
At least 21 days before travel, and at least 30 days before the titre blood draw.
Confirm the rabies vaccination is current, and confirm the pet is at least 7 months old.

Responsible: IAM-recognised veterinarian.

03
At least 30 days post-vaccination.
Draw blood for the rabies antibody titre test.

Responsible: Vet and an approved laboratory.

04
Runs in parallel with transfer and cargo booking.
Wait out the 3-month period from the blood draw date.

Responsible: Owner.

05
Once the titre result is confirmed, ahead of the flight booking.
Apply for the FSVO import licence for direct air entry.

Responsible: Owner or agent, via the FSVO.

06
2-4 weeks before travel.
Arrange the Macau-to-Hong Kong transfer and book SWISS WorldCargo or Cathay Pacific to Zurich.

Responsible: Owner or agent.

07
Certificate close to departure; check happens on landing.
Obtain the final health certificate and clear the veterinary check at Zurich Airport on arrival.

Responsible: Vet in Macau; Swiss border veterinary officer.

Requirements

Switzerland entry requirements

Every item below must be in place before your pet can enter. We verify and track each one.

Microchip
ISO 11784/11785 compliant microchip required, implanted before or on the same day as the first rabies vaccination.
Rabies vaccination
Required, minimum age 12 weeks. Entry from a higher-risk third country is not permitted for a dog, cat or ferret under 7 months old, regardless of documentation, since the full vaccination and titre sequence cannot be completed by that age.
Rabies titre test
Required. Macau isn't confirmed on the list of countries Switzerland treats as low-risk or listed, a status this site has already documented as absent for Macau under the closely aligned EU framework. Blood drawn at least 30 days after vaccination, minimum result greater than 0.5 IU/mL, then a 3-month wait from the blood draw date before entry. Source: Federal Food Safety and Veterinary Office (FSVO/BLV), blv.admin.ch, 'Imports from third countries', 2026.
Quarantine
No routine quarantine for a pet arriving with complete, compliant documentation and clearing the on-arrival veterinary check.
Import permit
An import licence from the FSVO is required for a pet entering Switzerland directly by air from a higher-risk third country such as Macau, applied for before travel.
Health certificate
Official veterinary health certificate covering microchip, vaccination and titre test results, issued shortly before travel by the exporting country's official or recognised veterinary authority.
Leaving Macau

Export requirements

Export permit
No separate export permit for a personal pet dog or cat leaving Macau, beyond the standard health certificate process handled by IAM.
Health certificate
Official veterinary Health Certificate of Animal, issued by an IAM-recognised veterinarian shortly before travel, recording microchip number, rabies vaccination date and titre test result, reformatted to meet Switzerland's entry certificate for a higher-risk third country. Source: Instituto para os Assuntos Municipais (IAM), iam.gov.mo and gov.mo.
Costs

What this route typically costs

Health certificate and IAM paperwork in Macau: MOP 400-900.
Rabies titre test and laboratory fee: MOP 1,200-2,000.
FSVO import licence application: CHF 30-90.
Cross-border transfer, Macau to Hong Kong (bridge coach or ferry, including crate handling): CHF 90-270.
Cargo fee, Hong Kong to Zurich: CHF 1,500-2,700 depending on crate size and season.
IATA-compliant travel crate: CHF 130-370.
Total typical range: CHF 2,150-4,000 over 4-6 months.

Critical points

Switzerland refuses entry from a higher-risk third country to any dog, cat or ferret under 7 months old. Check this before starting the process if your pet is young.

Macau isn't confirmed on Switzerland's list of low-risk or listed countries, mirroring the same gap this site has documented under the closely aligned EU framework. The full titre test, 3-month wait and FSVO import licence apply.

There is no direct flight from Macau to Switzerland. Every pet makes a Hong Kong transfer, which needs its own booking and crate handling on top of the FSVO paperwork.

What Switzerland requires

The requirements for Switzerland are thorough rather than difficult. Work through them methodically and confirm anything you are unsure about with the official source.

Avoiding the common mistakes

Work backwards from your travel date and add a buffer. Fixed waiting periods mean a single slipped appointment can push the whole move back by weeks.

Check the airline’s crate size and labelling rules early. An undersized or wrongly labelled crate is a frequent reason pets are refused at check-in.

Ask about temperature embargoes for your travel window. Summer and winter cargo restrictions can rule out certain routes and dates entirely.

Book the titre test laboratory well ahead. Approved labs have limited slots and the sample takes time to process, so this is not something to leave to the final weeks.
