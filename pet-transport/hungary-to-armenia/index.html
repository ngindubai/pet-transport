Requirements

Armenia entry requirements

Every item below must be in place before your pet can enter. We verify and track each one.

Microchip
ISO 11784/11785 15-digit microchip strongly recommended. Not strictly required under Armenian regulations but standard practice and expected by SSFS inspectors at Zvartnots. All EU pets arrive chipped and inspectors rely on the chip to verify identity against the certificate.
Rabies vaccination
Required. Vaccine must be valid: administered at least 21 days before entry and no more than 11 months before entry (or within the manufacturer's stated validity period). EU-issued rabies vaccinations are accepted. Source: SSFS Armenia, ssfs.am.
Rabies titre test
Not required from EU member states. Hungary is an EU country and Armenian authorities accept the EU health certificate without a titre test.
Quarantine
None for compliant arrivals. Pets meeting all certificate and vaccination requirements are released after inspection at Zvartnots International Airport (EVN).
Import permit
Not required for 1 to 2 personal companion pets. Larger numbers or commercial import would require prior authorisation from SSFS.
Health certificate
Nébih-endorsed EU-format AHC issued within 10 days of travel. Presented to SSFS inspectors on arrival at EVN.
Leaving Hungary

Export requirements

Export permit
No export permit required for personal companion cats and dogs leaving Hungary.
Veterinary inspection
Clinical examination by an official vet confirming the pet is fit to travel, vaccinations are current, and the microchip matches the documentation. Carried out at the time of health certificate preparation.
Health certificate
EU-format official animal health certificate issued by a Nébih-authorised official veterinarian and endorsed by Nébih (Nemzeti Élelmiszerlánc-biztonsági Hivatal, nebih.gov.hu). Valid from the date of examination for 10 days. Allow 1-2 working days for Nébih endorsement after the vet appointment.
Costs

What this route typically costs

Nébih-endorsed EU health certificate: approximately EUR 60-150 including official vet fee and Nébih endorsement
Cargo BUD to EVN via IST: EUR 500-1,300 depending on crate size, pet weight, and season
IATA-approved travel crate (if needed): EUR 80-350
Veterinary preparation in Hungary: EUR 30-100 for check-up and documentation
IPATA-registered agent (recommended): EUR 400-900
Total typical range: EUR 1,100-2,800

Critical points

The Nébih health certificate must be issued within 10 days of the pet's arrival in Armenia, not 10 days from the BUD departure date. If your routing involves an overnight layover, factor that into the 10-day window.

Armenia is not in the EU. Once your pet leaves Hungary, EU free-movement rules no longer apply. SSFS inspectors at EVN are the entry control point and their decision is final on the day.

Wizz Air is based at Budapest but does not accept live animal cargo in the hold in 2026. Do not attempt to book a Wizz Air service for cargo.

Pet age restriction: pets must be over 4 months old to enter Armenia. SSFS inspectors will check.

The rules for bringing a pet into Armenia

Armenia keeps its requirements reasonable, though a few steps have fixed timing. Map those out first and the rest falls into place.

How to keep this move on track

Keep every original document together and take copies. Border officials want originals, and a missing signature or date can hold up clearance.

Work backwards from your travel date and add a buffer. Fixed waiting periods mean a single slipped appointment can push the whole move back by weeks.

Check the airline’s crate size and labelling rules early. An undersized or wrongly labelled crate is a frequent reason pets are refused at check-in.

Ask about temperature embargoes for your travel window. Summer and winter cargo restrictions can rule out certain routes and dates entirely.
