Step by step

The United States to Iceland import process

01
Start of planning, at least 5 months before travel.
Implant the ISO microchip, then begin the rabies vaccination course.

Responsible: USDA-accredited vet

02
Runs alongside the rabies vaccination schedule, finishing close to travel for the canine influenza dose.
Complete leptospirosis, distemper, hepatitis, parvovirus, parainfluenza and, for dogs, canine influenza vaccination. Canine influenza must be given no sooner than 14 days before import.

Responsible: USDA-accredited vet

Around 4-5 months before travel, allowing for lab turnaround.
Draw blood for the titre test at least 30 days after vaccination, send to an approved laboratory. The 90-day Category 2 wait starts from this date.

Responsible: USDA-accredited vet and the laboratory

04
At least 4-6 weeks before travel.
Apply to MAST for the import permit.

Responsible: You or your agent

05
As early as possible.

Responsible: You or your agent, direct with the station

06
Final month before travel.
First parasite treatment 21-28 days before import, second 5-10 days before import.

Responsible: USDA-accredited vet

07
Within the certificate's validity window, close to departure. Allow 3-5 business days for endorsement.
Complete the USDA APHIS health certificate and get State Veterinarian endorsement.

Minimum 14 days in isolation before release.

Responsible: Quarantine station staff

Requirements

Iceland entry requirements

Every item below must be in place before your pet can enter. We verify and track each one.

Microchip
ISO 11784/11785 microchip required, implanted before the rabies vaccination course begins. Source: MAST, mast.is, 2026.
Rabies vaccination
Primary rabies vaccination after the microchip. MAST also requires leptospirosis cover, plus current distemper, hepatitis, parvovirus and parainfluenza vaccination for dogs. Because the US sits on MAST's Category 2 list, dogs need an additional canine influenza vaccination, given no sooner than 14 days before import, a condition that does not apply to pets arriving from most Category 1 countries. Source: MAST guidance notes, mast.is, 2026.
Rabies titre test
Required. Blood drawn at least 30 days after the rabies vaccination, minimum result 0.5 IU/ml. Because the United States is Category 2 rather than Category 1, a further 90 days must pass from the date of the blood draw before the pet can enter Iceland, on top of the 30-day post-vaccination wait. Source: MAST, mast.is, 2026.
Quarantine
Mandatory minimum of 14 days at one of two approved isolation stations, Einangrunarstoedin i Reykjanesbae or Einangrunarstoedin Mosel, for every dog and cat entering Iceland regardless of origin. Arrivals are only accepted on predetermined days, generally between 06:00 and 17:00. Source: MAST, mast.is, 2026.
Import permit
Required from MAST before travel, submitted with the application form, a photo of the pet, the health certificate and laboratory results. Base fee is around ISK 40,000 (roughly USD 290). Quarantine station space is booked separately and on a limited schedule. Source: MAST, mast.is, 2026.
Health certificate
MAST's Category 2 import certificate, completed by the USDA-accredited vet and carrying the USDA APHIS endorsement.
Leaving United States

Export requirements

Export permit
No export permit required from the US side for companion animals.
Health certificate
USDA APHIS Veterinary Health Certificate (VS Form 7001 or equivalent international health certificate) completed by a USDA-accredited veterinarian and endorsed by the USDA APHIS State Veterinarian. Allow 3-5 business days for endorsement.
Costs

What this route typically costs

USDA APHIS endorsement fee: USD 38 per certificate, plus courier costs if posting documents
Titre test at an approved laboratory: USD 170-350
Leptospirosis, core and canine influenza vaccination course: USD 90-220
MAST import permit base fee: around ISK 40,000 (roughly USD 290)
Quarantine, 14 days: around ISK 220,000 for a dog (roughly USD 1,600), ISK 100,000-110,000 for a cat (roughly USD 720-800)
IATA-compliant travel crate: USD 80-300
Cargo fee, US gateway to Keflavik: USD 900-2,200 depending on crate size and season
Agent and handling fees: USD 350-700
Total typical range: USD 2,700-4,900 for a dog, lower for a cat

Critical points

The US is a Category 2 country on MAST's list, alongside Canada and Turkey, not Category 1 like most EU states. That adds a fixed 90-day wait from the titre blood draw, on top of the standard 30-day post-vaccination timing, so budget five months minimum before this route even reaches the flight stage.

Dogs from the US, Canada or Singapore specifically need a canine influenza vaccination no sooner than 14 days before import, a requirement that does not apply on most other Iceland routes on this site. Confirm your vet has this on the schedule, since it is easy for a US clinic unfamiliar with Iceland's rules to miss it.

The 14-day quarantine applies to every pet entering Iceland, US-origin or otherwise. It is not a penalty for incomplete paperwork, it is the baseline rule, and isolation station space is booked on a limited schedule that fills up, particularly in summer.

Icelandair stopped taking pets as checked baggage on 1 November 2024. Confirm current Icelandair Cargo capacity from your chosen US gateway, or price the Lufthansa-via-Frankfurt alternative, before committing to a travel date.

MAST does not accept a standard USDA health certificate without its own Category 2 form layered on top. Brief your USDA-accredited vet early and confirm the exact document MAST expects before the appointment, not after.

Iceland entry requirements at a glance

The requirements for Iceland are thorough rather than difficult. Work through them methodically and confirm anything you are unsure about with the official source.

Getting the details right

Microchip before you vaccinate. The microchip must be in place before the rabies shot for the vaccination to count, and getting this order wrong is the most common and costly mistake.

Use an official, government-authorised vet for the health certificate, not just your usual clinic. Many destinations only accept certificates signed by an approved vet.

If this is your first international move, consider an IPATA-registered pet transport agent. They handle the documents, airline booking and crate sizing, and can troubleshoot problems.

Get your pet used to the travel crate weeks in advance. A calm, crate-familiar animal travels far better than one meeting the crate for the first time at the airport.
