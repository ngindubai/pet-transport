Step by step

The Honduras to United Kingdom pet import process

Each step must be completed in a precise sequence. Start early. We manage every stage and deadline.

1
Microchip implanted (ISO 11784/11785), if not already done.
Responsible: Honduras-based vet
Before the rabies vaccination used for the titre test.
2
Rabies vaccination given, or a current booster confirmed, after the microchip is in place.
Responsible: Honduras-based vet
As early as possible; both waiting periods below run from later dates, so vaccinating early shortens the whole process.
3
Blood drawn for the rabies antibody titre test and sent to an approved laboratory.
Responsible: Honduras-based vet / approved laboratory
No sooner than 30 full days after vaccination.
4
Titre result confirmed at 0.5 IU/ml or above. The 3-calendar-month wait is counted from the date the blood was drawn, not the date the result arrives.
Responsible: Owner tracks the dates
Results typically take 2-4 weeks; the 3-month clock is already running.
5
SENASA Certificado Zoosanitario de Exportación obtained via the SECEH online platform.
Responsible: SENASA-registered Honduran vet / exporter
Close to departure, once the flight is booked.
6
Cargo routing and IPATA agent booked for the Honduras-to-UK move, typically via Miami or Houston.
Responsible: You / IPATA pet relocation agent
As soon as the end date of the 3-month wait is known.
7
GB Pet Health Certificate issued once the 3-month wait has passed.
Responsible: Accredited vet in Honduras or at the transit point
Within 10 days of arrival in Great Britain.
8
Dog given praziquantel tapeworm treatment (not required for cats).
Responsible: Vet at the point of treatment
24 to 120 hours before landing in Great Britain.
Requirements

What your pet needs to enter United Kingdom

Every item below must be in place before your pet can travel. We manage and verify each one.

Microchip
ISO 11784/11785 microchip required, implanted before or on the same date as the rabies vaccination used for the titre test. A vaccination given before the microchip is not valid for this route. Source: GOV.UK, 'Bringing your pet dog, cat or ferret to Great Britain,' 2026.
Rabies vaccination
Current rabies vaccination required. Honduras does not appear on either of GB's listed-country tables (Part 1 or Part 2), so the vaccination alone is not enough to travel; it starts the clock for the titre test below. Source: GOV.UK, 'Bringing your pet dog, cat or ferret to Great Britain: Where you're travelling from,' 2026.
Rabies titre test
Rabies antibody blood test (titre test) required, taken no sooner than 30 full days after the rabies vaccination, with a result of at least 0.5 IU/ml. Source: GOV.UK, 2026.
Quarantine
No routine quarantine for a pet that meets the unlisted-country pet travel rules in full. An APHA import licence, and the quarantine that goes with it, only applies to animals that do not meet these rules. Source: GOV.UK, 2026.
Import permit
No APHA import licence needed for a compliant pet travelling under the standard pet travel rules from an unlisted country. An import licence is only required if the animal is going into APHA-approved quarantine because it hasn't met the titre test and waiting period rules. Source: GOV.UK, 2026.
Health certificate
Great Britain Pet Health Certificate, issued by an official or accredited vet and endorsed as required, valid for 10 days from the date of issue to arrival in Great Britain. Source: GOV.UK, 2026.
Export permit (Honduras)
No general export permit beyond the SENASA certificate below. A separate Permiso Zoosanitario de Importación only comes into play on the Honduran import side if bringing 3 or more pets into Honduras, not on export.
Costs

What this route typically costs

SENASA export certificate: Lps 450 fixed fee, plus 2 vet college stamps at Lps 10 each (roughly USD 18-20 total)
Honduran vet visits, vaccination and blood draw: USD 80-200
Rabies titre test / laboratory fee: USD 150-250
GB Pet Health Certificate: USD 150-300
Travel crate, IATA-compliant: USD 80-300
IPATA pet relocation agent handling the Miami or Houston interline transfer: USD 800-2,000
Air cargo Honduras to Heathrow via a US hub: USD 1,200-3,500 depending on crate size and season
Total typical range: USD 2,500-6,500

Critical points to read before you book

Honduras is not on either of GB's listed-country tables, so the rabies titre test and the 3-month wait from the blood draw date, not the result date, are mandatory. Vaccinating and testing out of order resets the whole timeline.

No airline flies Honduras to London directly, and Avianca specifically excludes UK flights from its pet transport service. Routing goes via Miami or Houston with a change of carrier, which normally needs an IPATA-registered agent to manage the interline transfer.

The SENASA export certificate itself is quick and cheap, issued electronically for Lps 450. It has no bearing on the GB side of the process. The date that actually controls travel is the end of the UK's 3-month post-titre wait.

United Kingdom import rules, explained

United Kingdom asks for several pieces of paperwork, and they all need to line up. A checklist and a clear timeline are your best tools here.

Practical tips for this move

Get your pet used to the travel crate weeks in advance. A calm, crate-familiar animal travels far better than one meeting the crate for the first time at the airport.

Confirm live animal acceptance on every flight segment before booking. Not all aircraft or connections take pets, and cargo terms can differ leg to leg.

Keep every original document together and take copies. Border officials want originals, and a missing signature or date can hold up clearance.

Work backwards from your travel date and add a buffer. Fixed waiting periods mean a single slipped appointment can push the whole move back by weeks.
