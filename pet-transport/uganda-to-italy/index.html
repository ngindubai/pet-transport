Requirements

Italy entry requirements

Every item below must be in place before your pet can enter. We verify and track each one.

Microchip
Required. ISO 11784/11785, 15-digit. Implanted before the rabies vaccination, not after.
Rabies vaccination
Required. Minimum age 12 weeks. Blood for the titre test cannot be drawn until 30 days after this vaccination.
Rabies titre test
Required. Uganda does not appear on the EU's list of approved countries under Implementing Regulation (EU) 2026/636, so a rabies antibody titration test is mandatory. Result must reach at least 0.5 IU/ml, drawn at least 30 days after vaccination, and at least 90 days must pass between the blood draw and the date the health certificate is issued. Source: Commission Delegated Regulation (EU) 2026/131 and Implementing Regulation (EU) 2026/705.
Quarantine
No routine quarantine for compliant arrivals at Rome Fiumicino or Milan Malpensa.
Import permit
Not required for non-commercial pet movements.
Health certificate
EU-format Animal Health Certificate, issued by a MAAIF-authorised vet and endorsed by MAAIF, valid 10 days from issue to entry.
Leaving Uganda

Export requirements

Export permit
No formal export permit required for personal companion pets leaving Uganda.
Health certificate
Official export health certificate issued by a MAAIF-authorised veterinarian, endorsed by the Ministry of Agriculture, Animal Industry and Fisheries (MAAIF). Reformatted to EU standard for entry into Italy. Issued within 10 days of travel.
Costs

What this route typically costs

Titre test at an EU-approved laboratory plus international sample shipping: EUR 100-220
MAAIF health certificate and endorsement: UGX 100,000-250,000 (approx EUR 24-60)
Cargo Entebbe to Rome or Milan via Addis Ababa, Istanbul or Doha: EUR 900-2,100 depending on crate size and carrier
IATA-compliant travel crate: EUR 80-300
Pet transport agent coordination: EUR 400-900
Total typical range: EUR 1,500-3,600 over a 5-7 month preparation window

Critical points

Chip before vaccine, always. Reverse the sequence and Italian border vets will not count the vaccination, forcing a restart of the entire 120-day biological clock.

The 90-day wait is counted from the blood draw date. Owners who plan around the day the lab reports the result routinely find themselves several weeks short of the real entry date.

No EU-approved laboratory operates inside Uganda. Confirm your vet's shipping arrangement for the blood sample well ahead, since courier delays can eat into your planning buffer.

Italy has no national breed restriction, but individual airlines still apply their own brachycephalic policies for cargo. Check that separately from the country's entry rules.

Italy import rules, explained

Italy asks for several pieces of paperwork, and they all need to line up. A checklist and a clear timeline are your best tools here.

Practical tips for this move

Keep every original document together and take copies. Border officials want originals, and a missing signature or date can hold up clearance.

Work backwards from your travel date and add a buffer. Fixed waiting periods mean a single slipped appointment can push the whole move back by weeks.

Check the airline’s crate size and labelling rules early. An undersized or wrongly labelled crate is a frequent reason pets are refused at check-in.

Ask about temperature embargoes for your travel window. Summer and winter cargo restrictions can rule out certain routes and dates entirely.
