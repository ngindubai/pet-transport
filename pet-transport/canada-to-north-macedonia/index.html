Checklist

What your pet needs

Every item below must be completed and verified before your pet can travel. Expand each category for the detail.

Microchip
ISO 11784/11785 microchip required, implanted before the rabies vaccination. A vaccination given first does not count and has to be repeated.
Rabies vaccination
Current rabies vaccination required, given at least 21 days before entry, matching the standard EU-aligned wait rather than an unlisted-country timeline.
Rabies titre test
Not required. North Macedonia's Food and Veterinary Agency (FVA) exempts arrivals from any country the EU classifies as rabies-controlled under Annex II to Implementing Regulation (EU) 2026/636, and Canada carries its own listing on that Annex, independent of the UK's. That's a genuinely different position to Montenegro, also covered on this site, whose own guidance does not describe the same exemption for Canadian arrivals.
Quarantine
No routine quarantine for pets arriving with correct, complete documentation.
Import permit
Not required for an owner travelling with their own pet dog or cat.
Health certificate
The CFIA-endorsed export certificate, examined by FVA veterinary officials on arrival, functions as the import document. North Macedonia does not require a separate Macedonian-issued certificate for an accompanied pet arriving under this exemption.
Export permit
No standalone export permit for a personal pet. North Macedonia's own guidance expects a CFIA-endorsed certificate rather than a vet's signature alone.
Veterinary inspection
A clinical examination by a CFIA-accredited veterinarian, confirming the pet is fit to travel and meets North Macedonia's stated health conditions.
Health certificate
A CFIA-accredited vet completes the certificate, then an official CFIA veterinarian endorses it at a booked regional office appointment. CFIA cannot endorse a certificate once the pet has left Canada, so this has to happen before departure. Processing typically runs 2 to 5 business days. Source: CFIA, Pets: Export certificates, inspection.canada.ca, 2026.

Critical points

The exemption from the titre test rests on Canada's continued place on the EU's Annex II list, a classification set at EU level rather than fixed permanently by North Macedonia. Confirm current status with FVA close to travel.

CFIA cannot endorse the export certificate once the pet has left Canada. Book the regional office appointment well ahead of departure.

The certificate has a short validity window before arrival. Book the vet appointment against the flight date, not weeks ahead of it.

North Macedonia import rules, explained

North Macedonia has a clear set of import requirements that are manageable with some planning. Follow them in order and keep an eye on the dates that cannot move.

Practical tips for this move

Get your pet used to the travel crate weeks in advance. A calm, crate-familiar animal travels far better than one meeting the crate for the first time at the airport.

Confirm live animal acceptance on every flight segment before booking. Not all aircraft or connections take pets, and cargo terms can differ leg to leg.

Keep every original document together and take copies. Border officials want originals, and a missing signature or date can hold up clearance.

Work backwards from your travel date and add a buffer. Fixed waiting periods mean a single slipped appointment can push the whole move back by weeks.
