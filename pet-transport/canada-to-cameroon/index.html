Checklist

What your pet needs

Every item below must be completed and verified before your pet can travel. Expand each category for the detail.

Microchip
Not formally compulsory, but standard practice regardless, and the reference point every other document on this route ends up citing. A 15-digit ISO 11784/11785 chip is the accepted format.
Rabies vaccination
Required for all cats and dogs over 3 months old, given at least 30 days before entering Cameroon. A multi-year vaccine only counts if it was given within the 12 months before travel.
Rabies titre test
Not required. A pet arriving from Canada does not need a rabies antibody titre test to enter Cameroon, regardless of Cameroon's own high-risk classification for pets travelling the other way.
Quarantine
No systematic quarantine for a dog or cat that meets the health requirements and shows no sign of contagious disease on arrival.
Import permit
No formal import permit published for an owner travelling with a personal dog or cat.
Health certificate
The CFIA-endorsed export health certificate, confirming current rabies vaccination and general fitness to travel, functions as the entry document alongside the vaccination record.
Export permit
No CFIA export permit required for a personal pet leaving Canada. Some destinations want CFIA endorsement of the health certificate; confirm with MINEPIA whether Cameroon expects this before travel.
Health certificate
Health certificate issued by a CFIA District Veterinarian or an accredited Canadian vet, endorsed by CFIA if the certificate needs it.

Critical points

Wolf hybrid dogs, and Savannah or Bengal cats, need five generations of documented separation from that wild ancestry before MINEPIA will admit them. This is a pedigree paperwork check, not something a standard health certificate resolves, so confirm it well before booking a flight.

There's no formal country-specific import protocol published for Cameroon beyond the vaccination and certificate rules described here. A relocation agent with recent Douala experience, or direct contact with MINEPIA, is worth the fee if this is a first move to Central Africa.

No direct flight runs from Canada to Cameroon. Royal Air Maroc's Casablanca connection and Ethiopian's Addis Ababa connection both keep the pet on a single carrier's network; a cheaper multi-carrier itinerary usually means a harder transfer.

What Cameroon requires

Getting a pet into Cameroon is a middle of the road process. The rules are well defined, so most of the work is simply staying on schedule.

Avoiding the common mistakes

If this is your first international move, consider an IPATA-registered pet transport agent. They handle the documents, airline booking and crate sizing, and can troubleshoot problems.

Get your pet used to the travel crate weeks in advance. A calm, crate-familiar animal travels far better than one meeting the crate for the first time at the airport.

Confirm live animal acceptance on every flight segment before booking. Not all aircraft or connections take pets, and cargo terms can differ leg to leg.

Keep every original document together and take copies. Border officials want originals, and a missing signature or date can hold up clearance.
