Checklist

What your pet needs

Every item below must be completed and verified before your pet can travel. Expand each category for the detail.

Microchip
Microchip identification required before the rabies vaccination used for entry.
Rabies vaccination
Primary rabies vaccination given between 30 days and 10 months before entry; boosters must be given before the previous dose expires. Confirm the current window directly with BAHFSA before booking, since published figures vary slightly across sources.
Rabies titre test
Not required. The Bahamas doesn't ask any country of origin, Canada included, for a rabies antibody titre test.
Quarantine
None published for a pet arriving with a valid ePermit, current rabies vaccination and vet health certificate.
Import permit
A BAHFSA ePermit, applied for through the government's Click2Clear online system, is required before travel. Register first by emailing click2clear@bahamas.gov.bs for a username and password, then apply for the pet permit. Since BAHFSA moved its import permitting from the older Mygateway system to Click2Clear on 1 September 2024, the fee is USD 50 and the permit runs for 6 months from the intended travel date rather than covering a single fixed trip. Confirm the current fee directly with BAHFSA before applying. Source: bahfsabahamas.com, ePermitting for Imports; BAHFSA Pet Import Permits Step-by-Step User Guide, 2025.
Health certificate
Veterinary health certificate dated within 48 hours of embarkation, confirming health and active rabies immunity. That window is tight for a Canadian departure, so book the vet appointment against the confirmed flight time, not days ahead for convenience.
Export permit
No general export permit for a personal pet leaving Canada. Most destinations, the Bahamas included, still need the health certificate endorsed by an official CFIA veterinarian rather than a private vet's signature alone.
Health certificate
A licensed Canadian vet completes the certificate to BAHFSA's format, and CFIA's general pet export policy expects it endorsed by an official CFIA veterinarian at a booked appointment before the pet leaves the country. That appointment system runs 2 to 5 business days for processing, depending on the district office. Whether BAHFSA specifically waives this endorsement step for Canadian-origin pets, the way US APHIS has confirmed it does for its own exporters, isn't published on either CFIA's or BAHFSA's public pages, so it's worth a direct call to a CFIA district office before assuming either way.

Airlines

Carriers on this route

Not all airlines accept live animals on this route. Confirm the operating carrier's current policy for every flight segment.

Air Canada
Flies Toronto Pearson direct to Nassau year-round, the busiest of the three carriers on this route. Small pets travel in the cabin under Air Canada's standard limits, with Air Canada Cargo handling a larger dog.
Cabin and Cargo
WestJet
Runs four nonstop Toronto-Nassau flights a week as of May 2026, with cabin space for a small pet and a cargo option for a bigger one on the same direct routing.
Cabin and Cargo
Porter Airlines
A more recent addition to the Pearson-Nassau route. Cabin only across Porter's network, so it suits a small pet rather than a dog needing a cargo booking.
Cabin Only

Critical points

CFIA's own general export policy expects an official CFIA veterinarian to endorse the certificate before the pet leaves Canada, booked by appointment rather than a walk-in service. Whether BAHFSA treats Bahamas-bound Canadian pets the same lighter way the US arrangement does, skipping that endorsement, isn't published anywhere for Canadian owners to check. Budget for the appointment and confirm directly, rather than assuming the US precedent carries over.

The 48-hour certificate validity window is genuinely tight. A vet visit three days before the flight produces a certificate that's already expired by boarding, so time it against the confirmed departure, not a convenient date in the diary.

Only Air Canada and WestJet offer a cargo option for a dog too large for the cabin. Porter Airlines carries pets in the cabin only across its whole network.

Hurricane season, June to November, can disrupt Nassau arrivals with little notice. A rebooked flight can mean a lapsed 48-hour health certificate that has to be redone from scratch.

How Bahamas handles incoming pets

Bahamas keeps its requirements reasonable, though a few steps have fixed timing. Map those out first and the rest falls into place.

What experienced owners do

Confirm live animal acceptance on every flight segment before booking. Not all aircraft or connections take pets, and cargo terms can differ leg to leg.

Keep every original document together and take copies. Border officials want originals, and a missing signature or date can hold up clearance.

Work backwards from your travel date and add a buffer. Fixed waiting periods mean a single slipped appointment can push the whole move back by weeks.

Check the airline’s crate size and labelling rules early. An undersized or wrongly labelled crate is a frequent reason pets are refused at check-in.
