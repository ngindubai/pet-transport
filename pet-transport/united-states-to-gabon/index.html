Checklist

What your pet needs

Every item below must be completed and verified before your pet can travel. Expand each category for the detail.

Microchip
Described in compiled guidance as recommended rather than an absolute condition, regardless of country of origin. Confirm current status directly with the Direction Generale de l'Elevage before travel.
Rabies vaccination
Required, administered at least 30 days before travel and within a one-year validity window. Nothing in the published guidance suggests a different rule for a US-origin pet compared with a pet from anywhere else.
Rabies titre test
Not confirmed as a requirement in the sources available for this route either. Gabon's own published guidance focuses on vaccination and the import permit rather than a blood test, but this is worth confirming directly rather than assuming it applies universally or not at all.
Quarantine
None described for a pet meeting the published import conditions, US-origin or otherwise.
Import permit
Import permit required from the Direction Generale de l'Elevage before travel. Gabon's own processing guidance recommends starting 4 to 6 months ahead of the intended date, a considerably longer runway than most destinations on this site, whatever country the application originates from.
Health certificate
Export health certificate issued by a licensed vet in the country of departure, submitted in French alongside the import permit application.
Export permit
No export permit is required from the US side for a companion animal. Gabon's own import permit, applied for through the Direction Generale de l'Elevage, is the document that governs entry.
Veterinary inspection
A USDA-accredited veterinarian examines the animal, confirms the microchip and vaccination status, and completes the international health certificate close to the travel date.
Government endorsement
Endorsed by USDA APHIS, in most cases electronically through the Veterinary Export Health Certification System (VEHCS), live since 29 April 2020. Allow 3-5 business days for the endorsement itself.
Health certificate
USDA APHIS international health certificate, translated into French to match Gabon's own documentation language requirement. Source: aphis.usda.gov/live-animal-export/export-live-animals-gabon, cross-referenced with compiled Gabon import guidance from catabroad.com and petsnomad.com, checked July 2026.

Airlines

Carriers on this route

Not all airlines accept live animals on this route. Confirm the operating carrier's current policy for every flight segment.

Ethiopian Airlines
Runs a nonstop Washington Dulles to Addis Ababa service, ET501 in the reverse direction, an Airbus A350 route, connecting onward to Libreville. This is the most direct single-carrier option for a US departure, using an already-established Africa-facing hub.
Cabin and Cargo
Royal Air Maroc
Flies New York JFK to Casablanca nonstop, roughly 13 times a week as of July 2026, then connects onward with its own nonstop Casablanca-Libreville service. Checked pets up to 70kg combined travel through the standard baggage process; heavier or unaccompanied animals go through the cargo desk, booked at least 48 hours ahead.
Cabin and Cargo
Air France
A US pet can route through several Air France US gateways into Paris, then join the carrier's nonstop CDG-Libreville service, AF926, on a Boeing 787. Pets under 8kg including carrier can use the cabin on some sectors; brachycephalic breeds are barred from the hold entirely.

Critical points

Gabon's own guidance recommends starting the import permit process 4 to 6 months before travel, whoever the applicant is. Leaving this until a few weeks out risks missing the move entirely, regardless of how straightforward the US export side is.

All Gabon-bound paperwork needs to be in French. Build translation time into the schedule if the USDA certificate and supporting records start out in English, which they will for a US departure.

Wolf hybrids, and Savannah or Bengal cats fewer than five generations removed from a wild ancestor, cannot be imported into Gabon.

No airline flies directly from the US to Libreville. Every workable option adds a long-haul connection, through Addis Ababa, Casablanca or Paris, on top of the permit's own multi-month lead time.

The rules for bringing a pet into Gabon

Gabon has a detailed set of import rules, and each one needs to be satisfied before travel. None of it is unusual, but the order and timing are what catch people out.

How to keep this move on track

Get your pet used to the travel crate weeks in advance. A calm, crate-familiar animal travels far better than one meeting the crate for the first time at the airport.

Confirm live animal acceptance on every flight segment before booking. Not all aircraft or connections take pets, and cargo terms can differ leg to leg.

Keep every original document together and take copies. Border officials want originals, and a missing signature or date can hold up clearance.

Work backwards from your travel date and add a buffer. Fixed waiting periods mean a single slipped appointment can push the whole move back by weeks.
