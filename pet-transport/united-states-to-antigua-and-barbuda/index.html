In short: Preparing to move a pet from United States to Antigua and Barbuda typically takes around 12-16 weeks. There is no routine quarantine on arrival for compliant pets. A rabies titre test is required, which adds a fixed waiting period, so start early.

Checklist

What your pet needs

Every item below must be completed and verified before your pet can travel. Expand each category for the detail.

Microchip
ISO 11784/11785-compliant 15-digit microchip, implanted before the rabies vaccination given for this trip. Getting this order wrong invalidates the vaccination as far as the paperwork is concerned.
Rabies vaccination
Required, given between 30 days and 12 months before arrival. The Veterinary and Livestock Division's own published guidance is specific here: only an annual rabies vaccine satisfies the requirement on an ongoing basis; a three-year vaccine alone is not accepted. Source: Government of Antigua and Barbuda, Veterinary and Livestock Division published pet import requirements, checked July 2026.
Rabies titre test
Required, because the United States is explicitly named in Antigua and Barbuda's own guidance as a rabies-endemic country of origin, alongside Canada, Grenada and Trinidad and Tobago. A Rabies FAVN (fluorescent antibody virus neutralisation) test at a WOAH-approved laboratory has to show a result within accepted levels. There is one meaningful exception: if the pet's annual rabies vaccine has been kept continuously current, a second FAVN test isn't required, a genuinely useful detail for anyone already vaccinating on a yearly rather than three-year schedule.
Quarantine
No quarantine is imposed if the requirements are met. Published guidance doesn't specify a fallback quarantine period for a failed FAVN result; failure to meet the requirements can instead mean the pet is refused entry or, in the guidance's own wording, confiscated. That's a harder outcome than a quarantine stay, so timing the FAVN test correctly matters more here than on a route where quarantine is simply the fallback.
Import permit
Veterinary Import License required from the Veterinary and Livestock Division, Ministry of Agriculture, applied for at least 2 weeks before travel.
Health certificate
Official Government-issued Health Certificate confirming good health and the required vaccinations, issued within 7 days of entry, submitted alongside the FAVN result and microchip certification.
Export permit
No export permit required from the US side for a companion animal. The document that matters is Antigua and Barbuda's own Veterinary Import License, applied for from the Antigua side.
Veterinary inspection
A USDA-accredited veterinarian examines the animal, confirms the microchip reads before checking vaccination history, and arranges the FAVN blood draw where it applies.
Government endorsement
Endorsed by USDA APHIS, in most cases electronically through the Veterinary Export Health Certification System (VEHCS), live since 29 April 2020. Allow 3-5 business days for the endorsement once the certificate is submitted.
Health certificate
USDA APHIS international health certificate, issued within 7 days of entry per Antigua and Barbuda's own requirement, completed by an accredited vet and endorsed by APHIS.

Airlines

Carriers on this route

Not all airlines accept live animals on this route. Confirm the operating carrier's current policy for every flight segment.

American Airlines
AA2579/AA2580 flies Miami to Antigua nonstop, roughly 3 hours 23 minutes. A small pet under 9kg including carrier can travel in the cabin for USD 150 each way, since this route doesn't touch the UK or Ireland. Larger dogs move through American Airlines Cargo's PetEmbark service, up to 45kg combined, for USD 150-200 each way.
Cabin and Cargo
Caribbean Airlines
Flies New York JFK to Antigua nonstop. Pet cargo bookings go through the airline's Antigua cargo desk, (268) 480-5728, and this service has resumed after a 2025 ground-handling change briefly suspended it.
Cargo Only
Costs

What this route typically costs

USDA-accredited vet visits, microchip and rabies vaccination: USD 100-220
FAVN titre test at a WOAH-approved laboratory: USD 150-300, unless the vaccination history exemption applies
USDA APHIS endorsement fee: USD 38 per certificate
Antigua and Barbuda Veterinary Import License application fee: not published centrally; contact the Veterinary and Livestock Division directly
IATA-compliant travel crate: USD 60-400 depending on whether the pet travels in-cabin or as cargo
American Airlines cabin pet fee, under 9kg including carrier: USD 150 each way; PetEmbark cargo for larger dogs: USD 150-200 each way plus the flight segment
Total typical range: USD 700-1,600 for a small pet travelling in-cabin, or USD 1,800-3,600 for a larger dog needing the FAVN test and cargo transport

Critical points

Antigua and Barbuda's own guidance names the United States directly as a rabies-endemic country of origin, in the same bracket as Canada, Grenada and Trinidad and Tobago. Don't assume this route is as light as a rabies-free-country move, since it genuinely isn't.

Keeping the rabies vaccination on an annual schedule, rather than a three-year one, is what avoids a second FAVN test. If the pet is currently on a three-year vaccine protocol, budget both the time and the lab fee for the titre test.

Dogs of the pitbull breed, or any mix that could be mistaken for one, cannot enter Antigua and Barbuda. A dog of ambiguous type needs kennel club certification proving otherwise before booking.

Published guidance doesn't describe a quarantine fallback for a failed FAVN result; it describes refusal of entry or confiscation instead. That makes getting the test timing right a harder requirement than on a route where quarantine is simply the safety net.

How Antigua and Barbuda handles incoming pets

Antigua and Barbuda asks for several pieces of paperwork, and they all need to line up. A checklist and a clear timeline are your best tools here.

What experienced owners do

Check the airline’s crate size and labelling rules early. An undersized or wrongly labelled crate is a frequent reason pets are refused at check-in.

Ask about temperature embargoes for your travel window. Summer and winter cargo restrictions can rule out certain routes and dates entirely.

Book the titre test laboratory well ahead. Approved labs have limited slots and the sample takes time to process, so this is not something to leave to the final weeks.

Microchip before you vaccinate. The microchip must be in place before the rabies shot for the vaccination to count, and getting this order wrong is the most common and costly mistake.
