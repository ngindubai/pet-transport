Checklist

What your pet needs

Every item below must be completed and verified before your pet can travel. Expand each category for the detail.

Microchip
ISO 11784/11785 microchip required. Must be implanted before or on the same day as the rabies vaccination. Source: GOV.UK, bring-pet-to-great-britain.
Rabies vaccination
Current rabies vaccination required. At least 21 days must have elapsed after the first vaccination (or the last in the primary course) before travel. Boosters with no break in cover require no additional wait. Source: GOV.UK.
Rabies titre test
Not required. Chile is a listed (Part 2) country under UK pet travel rules, so the rabies antibody titre test is waived. This significantly shortens preparation time compared to unlisted countries. Source: GOV.UK listed countries, 2026.
Quarantine
No routine quarantine for dogs and cats arriving with correct documentation. Penalty quarantine of up to 4 months applies if papers are incomplete or the pet arrives via an unapproved route. Source: GOV.UK.
Health certificate
Animal Health Certificate (AHC) issued by a SAG-authorised Chilean official vet, valid for 10 days from examination. Must cover microchip number, rabies vaccination, and tapeworm treatment for dogs.
Export permit
No formal export permit required from Chile for companion animals.
Health certificate
Official health certificate issued by a SAG-authorised official veterinarian in Chile, endorsed by SAG (Servicio Agricola y Ganadero). This serves as the Animal Health Certificate for UK entry. Must be issued within 10 days of travel.

Airlines

Carriers on this route

Not all airlines accept live animals on this route. Confirm the operating carrier's current policy for every flight segment.

LATAM Airlines
LATAM operates from Santiago (SCL) to Madrid, New York, and other hubs with onward connections to London. LATAM Cargo handles pets as manifested cargo. Connecting cargo can be arranged via Madrid (MAD) to London on partner carriers. Confirm routing and availability before booking.
Cargo Only
British Airways
British Airways does not operate a direct SCL-LHR route but offers the return UK-to-Chile leg. Inbound cargo from Santiago typically routes via Madrid (Iberia, a BA partner) or via US connections. BA World Cargo accepts pets at Heathrow. Confirm with a cargo agent.
Cargo Only
Iberia
Iberia operates SCL-MAD direct, and Iberia Cargo connects to London Heathrow via MAD transit. A well-established routing for South America to UK pet cargo. Madrid transit times are generally manageable for animal welfare.
Cargo Only
Air France-KLM Cargo
Via Paris (CDG) or Amsterdam (AMS) with connections from Santiago. Air France-KLM Cargo accepts pets on the Europe-South America corridor. Confirm availability and transit arrangements for the UK final leg.
Cargo Only
Costs

What this route typically costs

SAG-authorised official vet health certificate: CLP 100,000 to CLP 250,000 (approximately GBP 90-220)
Tapeworm treatment (dogs): CLP 20,000 to CLP 50,000
IATA-compliant travel crate: GBP 60-250 if needed
Pet cargo from Santiago to London via Madrid: GBP 700-1,800 depending on crate size and weight
UK port inspection fee: approximately GBP 30-60
Pet transport agent fees (optional but recommended): GBP 200-400
Total typical range: GBP 1,100-2,800

Critical points

Chile's listed-country status is the critical advantage on this route. It removes the 3-month titre test wait that applies to pets from unlisted countries. Verify Chile's current listed status on GOV.UK before final planning, as country classifications can change.

The tapeworm treatment timing window is strict. Treatment administered outside the 24-120 hour window before your scheduled UK arrival time is non-compliant and can result in the pet being refused entry or held for penalty quarantine.

The AHC must be issued within 10 days of travel. This means your vet appointment must happen close to departure. Plan the cargo booking and vet appointment in the same week.

Cargo routing from Santiago to London almost always involves a connection, usually Madrid. Check that the connecting carrier accepts live animals and that transit times are appropriate for your pet.

Meeting United Kingdom's entry requirements

The requirements for United Kingdom are light, but not zero. A little planning goes a long way on a route like this.

Advice worth following

Use an official, government-authorised vet for the health certificate, not just your usual clinic. Many destinations only accept certificates signed by an approved vet.

If this is your first international move, consider an IPATA-registered pet transport agent. They handle the documents, airline booking and crate sizing, and can troubleshoot problems.

Get your pet used to the travel crate weeks in advance. A calm, crate-familiar animal travels far better than one meeting the crate for the first time at the airport.

Confirm live animal acceptance on every flight segment before booking. Not all aircraft or connections take pets, and cargo terms can differ leg to leg.
