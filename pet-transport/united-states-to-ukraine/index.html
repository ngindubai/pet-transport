Overview

The journey from United States to Ukraine for a pet is straightforward to plan once the key dates are mapped out. Plan for approximately 20-28 weeks. This is a rigorous route with little room to compress the schedule. Compliant pets are not held in quarantine on arrival.

Work through the steps below in order and you will arrive in good shape.

Checklist

Ukraine: entry requirements

RequirementDetail
MicrochipISO-compliant microchip required, implanted before or at the same time as the rabies vaccination.
Rabies vaccinationCurrent rabies vaccination required, using a licensed vaccine. If this is a primary course, at least 21 days must pass before the titre test blood draw.
Rabies titre testPublished guidance on entering Ukraine, as summarised by USDA APHIS, does not document any exemption based on origin country. The United States is not classified as dog-rabies-free, so a titre test applies without question here: blood drawn at least 30 days after vaccination, result at least 0.5 IU/ml, then a 3-month wait from the draw date. Confirm current practice directly with Derzhprodspozhyvsluzhba before travel.
QuarantineNo routine quarantine documented for personal pets that meet the certificate and titre requirements. Confirm current practice at your intended border crossing, since procedures have varied by post since 2022.
Import permitNo formal import permit. An international veterinary certificate, exchanged from your USDA-endorsed certificate at the border, takes its place.
Health certificateInternational veterinary certificate, completed in Ukrainian and English, obtained by presenting your USDA export certificate and titre test results at the Ukrainian border checkpoint.

Leaving United States of America

Export requirements

RequirementDetail
Export permitNo general US export permit for pets. As with most destinations, Ukraine's entry rules are satisfied through a USDA-endorsed export health certificate rather than a separate US permit.
Health certificateInternational health certificate issued by a USDA-accredited veterinarian, endorsed through the USDA APHIS eFile system. The endorsement fee is roughly USD 38 per certificate as published on the APHIS pet travel pages.

Critical points

Read before you book

⚠ No commercial airline flies into Ukraine. Ukrainian civilian airspace has been closed since 24 February 2022 and remains closed as this guide is written in mid-2026, with EASA's restriction currently extended to at least 31 July 2026. Your pet's journey ends with an overland border crossing from Poland or Romania, not a flight.
⚠ Available guidance on Ukraine's entry requirements does not document any relaxation based on origin country, and the United States is not classed as dog-rabies-free in the first place. Plan for the full titre test and 3-month wait, and confirm the current position with Derzhprodspozhyvsluzhba before you commit to a travel date.
⚠ Border crossing points and conditions can change with the security situation. Build flexibility into your travel plan and check current advice close to your departure date.
Carriers

Airlines on this route

LOT Polish Airlines
No airline flies into Ukraine, so LOT covers the first leg only: a daily nonstop from New York JFK to Warsaw on Boeing 787 equipment, with small pets accepted in the cabin (under 8kg with carrier) or larger dogs via cargo. Your pet then continues into Ukraine overland.
Cabin and Cargo
United Airlines
Flies several US gateways to Warsaw and Frankfurt, giving an alternative first leg for owners not departing from New York. Confirm current pet cargo acceptance and any seasonal heat embargoes before booking.
Cargo Only
Lufthansa
Connects US gateways via Frankfurt or Munich onward to Warsaw or Bucharest, a useful option for owners routing toward a Romanian border crossing rather than Poland.
Cargo Only

Ukraine import rules, explained

Ukraine takes biosecurity seriously, and the requirements reflect that. The paperwork is exacting, so accuracy matters as much as timing.

Practical tips for this move

Use an official, government-authorised vet for the health certificate, not just your usual clinic. Many destinations only accept certificates signed by an approved vet.

If this is your first international move, consider an IPATA-registered pet transport agent. They handle the documents, airline booking and crate sizing, and can troubleshoot problems.

Get your pet used to the travel crate weeks in advance. A calm, crate-familiar animal travels far better than one meeting the crate for the first time at the airport.

Confirm live animal acceptance on every flight segment before booking. Not all aircraft or connections take pets, and cargo terms can differ leg to leg.
