Pet Transport from Australia to Ivory Coast

Taking your dog or cat from Australia to Ivory Coast comes down to good timing and the right paperwork. Around 10-14 weeks is sensible here, with a few steps that must happen in a strict order. No routine quarantine applies as long as every requirement is met. …

In short: Preparing to move a pet from Australia to Ivory Coast typically takes around 10-14 weeks. There is no routine quarantine on arrival for compliant pets. A rabies titre test is not required.

Responsible: Ivorian veterinary officials

Critical points

The import permit runs through two DSV email addresses, not a portal or an online form. Send the application and rabies certificate copy well ahead of travel and follow up directly if there's no reply within a couple of weeks.

No titre test is required to enter Cote d'Ivoire from Australia, matching the UK and US routes, but note that Cote d'Ivoire is itself classed high-risk for dog rabies by Australia's own biosecurity rules on the reverse direction. That classification has no bearing on this outbound route; it only matters if the same dog were ever coming back.

The health certificate has to be in French or carry a certified French translation. An English-only certificate, however well prepared by an Australian vet, risks being rejected on arrival.

There is no direct flight from Australia to Abidjan. Every routing involves at least one change of carrier at a Gulf or Addis Ababa hub, and every airline on this route restricts or excludes snub-nosed breeds from the hold.

Cabin and Cargo

Meeting Ivory Coast's entry requirements

Bringing a pet into Ivory Coast involves a fair amount of documentation. Keep everything organised and check each item against the current requirements before you book.

Advice worth following

Confirm live animal acceptance on every flight segment before booking. Not all aircraft or connections take pets, and cargo terms can differ leg to leg.

Keep every original document together and take copies. Border officials want originals, and a missing signature or date can hold up clearance.

Work backwards from your travel date and add a buffer. Fixed waiting periods mean a single slipped appointment can push the whole move back by weeks.

Check the airline’s crate size and labelling rules early. An undersized or wrongly labelled crate is a frequent reason pets are refused at check-in.
