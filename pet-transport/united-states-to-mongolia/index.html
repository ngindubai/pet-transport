Requirements

Mongolia entry requirements

Every item below must be in place before your pet can enter. We verify and track each one.

Microchip
ISO 11784/11785 microchip required in practice, since the chip number has to appear on the export certificate presented at the border, implanted before the rabies vaccination.
Rabies vaccination
Required, given between 30 days and 12 months before arrival.
Rabies titre test
Not required for a pet entering Mongolia from any origin, the US included. There's no CDC-style high-risk paperwork on this leg at all, a genuine contrast with the return journey, where Mongolia's own CDC high-risk classification for dog rabies brings in a titre test or 28-day quarantine.
Quarantine
No quarantine for pets arriving with a complete, compliant certificate. Facilities for holding non-compliant animals are limited, so incomplete paperwork risks the pet being refused entry rather than simply delayed.
Import permit
Not required for a personal pet travelling with its owner. Only commercial imports of animals need a separate import permit.
Health certificate
The USDA-endorsed health certificate, checked on arrival by veterinary officials at Chinggis Khaan International Airport, serves as the import document.
Leaving United States

Export requirements

Export permit
No export permit required from the US side for companion animals.
Health certificate
USDA APHIS Veterinary Health Certificate (VS Form 7001 or equivalent international health certificate) completed by a USDA-accredited veterinarian and endorsed by the USDA APHIS State Veterinarian. Allow 3-5 business days for endorsement.
Costs

What this route typically costs

USDA APHIS endorsement fee: USD 38 per certificate, plus courier costs if posting documents

IATA-compliant travel crate: USD 100-350 depending on size
Cargo or cabin fee, US to Ulaanbaatar via Seoul or Istanbul: USD 900-2,400 depending on routing and crate size
Pet transport agent fee, recommended given the limited routing options: USD 400-900
Total typical range: USD 1,500-3,900

Critical points

There is no CDC high-risk paperwork on this leg. That apparatus, the titre test or 28-day quarantine, only applies to dogs entering the United States, not leaving it, so a dog heading to Mongolia skips it entirely on the way out.

Air China's dog suspension is scoped to flights into the United States. It doesn't, by its own wording, block a dog flying from the US to Beijing and on to Ulaanbaatar, but this is a narrow reading of a policy that could be applied more broadly in practice. Confirm directly with Air China rather than assuming the routing works.

There are no direct flights from the US to Ulaanbaatar. Seoul, served by 8 weekly Korean Air flights and 25 weekly MIAT flights from Incheon, is the dominant connection point. Istanbul via Turkish Airlines or MIAT is the main alternative.

Verify current requirements directly with GAVS or a receiving vet before booking. Mongolia's own published guidance for this route is thinner than for most destinations on this site.

Mongolia entry requirements at a glance

The path into Mongolia is straightforward to follow once you know the steps. Give the time-sensitive items priority and check the details below.

Getting the details right

Microchip before you vaccinate. The microchip must be in place before the rabies shot for the vaccination to count, and getting this order wrong is the most common and costly mistake.

Use an official, government-authorised vet for the health certificate, not just your usual clinic. Many destinations only accept certificates signed by an approved vet.

If this is your first international move, consider an IPATA-registered pet transport agent. They handle the documents, airline booking and crate sizing, and can troubleshoot problems.

Get your pet used to the travel crate weeks in advance. A calm, crate-familiar animal travels far better than one meeting the crate for the first time at the airport.
