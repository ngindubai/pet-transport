In short: Preparing to move a pet from Ukraine to United Kingdom typically takes around 20-28 weeks. There is no routine quarantine on arrival for compliant pets. A rabies titre test is required, which adds a fixed waiting period, so start early.

The Ukraine to United Kingdom pet import process

Each step must be completed in a precise sequence. Start early. We manage every stage and deadline.

Fly from Warsaw, Krakow or Rzeszow to an approved GB port with a pet-carrying airline.
Responsible: Owner plus airline cargo/check-in desk
Within the 10-day certificate window.
Requirements

What your pet needs to enter United Kingdom

Every item below must be in place before your pet can travel. We manage and verify each one.

Microchip
ISO 11784/11785 microchip required, implanted before the rabies vaccination. A vaccination given before the chip does not count and the process must restart.
Rabies vaccination
Required. Primary course must be completed at least 21 days before the titre test blood draw. WHO-approved inactivated or recombinant vaccine only.
Rabies titre test
Required. Ukraine is treated as an unlisted country for GB entry, so the full protocol applies: blood drawn at least 30 days after vaccination, minimum result 0.5 IU/ml at an APHA-approved laboratory, then a fixed 3-calendar-month wait from the draw date, not the result date.
Quarantine
No routine quarantine for pets that meet the titre test and documentation requirements. Penalty quarantine of up to 4 months applies if paperwork is wrong or incomplete on arrival.
Import permit
No formal import permit. Pets without a passing titre test and correct documentation must instead apply for a GB import licence, which allows up to 5 pets per applicant and can include home isolation instead of quarantine kennels for those entering under Ukraine's UK visa schemes.
Health certificate
Great Britain Pet Health Certificate, issued by an official vet, valid 10 days from issue for entry.
Export permit (Ukraine)
No standalone export permit for personal pets. Owners obtain Veterinary Certificate Form No. 1 from a state veterinary clinic, which is then exchanged for an international veterinary certificate at the border checkpoint, completed in Ukrainian and English.

Critical points to read before you book

Ukrainian civilian airspace has been closed to all commercial flights since 24 February 2022 and remains closed as this guide is written in mid-2026 (EASA's conflict zone information bulletin covers Ukraine at least until 31 July 2026). There is no direct flight from any Ukrainian airport. Every pet move currently requires an overland leg to a working airport in a neighbouring country, most commonly Poland via Lviv, or Romania.

Ukraine is treated as an unlisted country for GB entry, so the titre test and 3-month wait are mandatory. The GB guidance written for Ukrainians fleeing the war does not lower this bar; it sets out the same testing requirement, with a separate licence route (and possible home isolation instead of kennel quarantine) for pets that cannot meet it in time.

Budget carriers used heavily on Poland and Romania to UK routes, including Wizz Air, do not carry pets in the cabin or as cargo under any circumstances. Confirm your chosen airline accepts live animals before you book the onward flight.

Cabin and Cargo

United Kingdom entry requirements at a glance

Entry to United Kingdom is tightly controlled, and officials verify each requirement in turn. Treat it as a sequence where every step gates the next.

Getting the details right

Microchip before you vaccinate. The microchip must be in place before the rabies shot for the vaccination to count, and getting this order wrong is the most common and costly mistake.

Use an official, government-authorised vet for the health certificate, not just your usual clinic. Many destinations only accept certificates signed by an approved vet.

If this is your first international move, consider an IPATA-registered pet transport agent. They handle the documents, airline booking and crate sizing, and can troubleshoot problems.

Get your pet used to the travel crate weeks in advance. A calm, crate-familiar animal travels far better than one meeting the crate for the first time at the airport.
