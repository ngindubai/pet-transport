In short: Preparing to move a pet from Switzerland to Iceland typically takes around 10-14 weeks. Expect 14 days of quarantine on arrival. A rabies titre test is required, which adds a fixed waiting period, so start early.

Checklist

What your pet needs

Every item below must be completed and verified before your pet can travel. Expand each category for the detail.

Microchip
ISO 11784/11785 microchip required, implanted before the rabies vaccination. Source: MAST, mast.is, checked July 2026.
Rabies vaccination
Primary rabies vaccination required after the microchip. Also required: two leptospirosis doses 3 to 4 weeks apart, plus current distemper, infectious canine hepatitis, parvovirus and parainfluenza cover for dogs. Source: MAST guidance notes, checked July 2026.
Rabies titre test
Required regardless of origin. Blood drawn at least 30 days after vaccination, tested at an approved laboratory, minimum 0.5 IU/ml. Switzerland sits on MAST's Category 1 list, so once the result clears there is no further fixed wait before travel, unlike Category 2 countries, which face a 90-day wait from the blood draw. Source: MAST, mast.is, checked July 2026.
Quarantine
Mandatory. Every dog and cat entering Iceland spends a minimum of 14 days at one of two approved isolation stations, Einangrunarstodin i Reykjanesbae or Einangrunarstodin Mosel, regardless of origin. There is no exemption for Category 1 pets. Source: MAST, mast.is, checked July 2026.
Import permit
Required from MAST before travel, submitted at least 30 days ahead with the application form, a photo of the pet, the health certificate and laboratory results. Base fee around ISK 40,000 (roughly CHF 260). Quarantine station space is booked separately, with intake on set days a few weeks apart. Source: MAST, mast.is, checked July 2026.
Health certificate
MAST's Category 1 certificate (Form D1 for dogs, Form C1 for cats), completed by the Swiss vet and endorsed by the cantonal veterinary office.
Export permit
No formal export permit for a private pet move. The health certificate is the export document.
Health certificate
MAST does not accept a standard EU Animal Health Certificate or Swiss pet passport for this route. The Swiss vet must complete MAST's own Category 1 certificate form for dogs (Form D1) or cats (Form C1), then have it endorsed by the cantonal veterinary office. Source: mast.is, checked July 2026.

Airlines

Carriers on this route

Not all airlines accept live animals on this route. Confirm the operating carrier's current policy for every flight segment.

Icelandair
Operates Zurich (ZRH) to Keflavik (KEF) direct, a genuine nonstop sector. Since 1 November 2024, Icelandair no longer accepts pets as checked or excess baggage in the passenger hold. A pet now moves as manifested air cargo through Icelandair Cargo, booked through a cargo agent rather than at check-in.
Cargo Only
Lufthansa
Flies Zurich to Frankfurt with an onward connection to Keflavik, a workable alternative if Icelandair Cargo capacity is tight on your dates, using Lufthansa's own Frankfurt Animal Lounge for the connecting leg.

What this route typically costs

Leptospirosis and core vaccination course: CHF 90-200

IATA-compliant travel crate: CHF 60-250
Cargo fee, Zurich to Keflavik: CHF 400-850 depending on crate size and season
Agent and handling fees: CHF 250-550
Total typical range: CHF 2,000-3,500 for a dog, lower for a cat

Critical points

The 14-day quarantine is not a penalty for messy paperwork. It applies to every pet entering Iceland, including a fully documented, Category 1 Swiss dog. Budget the isolation station fee and the two weeks into your plan from the outset.

Icelandair stopped taking pets as checked baggage in the passenger hold on 1 November 2024. Confirm current Icelandair Cargo capacity on the direct Zurich sector before you commit to a travel date, or price the Lufthansa-via-Frankfurt alternative.

Keflavik is the only approved entry point for pets in Iceland, regardless of the direct flight into Zurich you left from.

MAST does not accept the standard Swiss export health certificate for this route. The Swiss vet needs the specific Category 1 form (D1 or C1), a different document from what most Swiss pet exports use.

How Iceland handles incoming pets

Iceland asks for several pieces of paperwork, and they all need to line up. A checklist and a clear timeline are your best tools here.

What experienced owners do

Use an official, government-authorised vet for the health certificate, not just your usual clinic. Many destinations only accept certificates signed by an approved vet.

If this is your first international move, consider an IPATA-registered pet transport agent. They handle the documents, airline booking and crate sizing, and can troubleshoot problems.

Get your pet used to the travel crate weeks in advance. A calm, crate-familiar animal travels far better than one meeting the crate for the first time at the airport.

Confirm live animal acceptance on every flight segment before booking. Not all aircraft or connections take pets, and cargo terms can differ leg to leg.
