Requirements

What your pet needs to enter India

Every item below must be in place before your pet can travel. We manage and verify each one.

Microchip
ISO 11784/11785 microchip or AVID microchip required.
Rabies vaccination
Required. Administered more than one month but within 12 months before arrival in India.
Rabies titre test
Not required. A rabies titre test is not mandatory for entry into India from any country, including Japan.
Quarantine
15-day quarantine at an AQCS quarantine facility on arrival. Standard for all pet imports. Can be extended to 30 days if there are any health concerns.
Health certificate
Official health certificate from country of origin confirming the pet is free from infectious and contagious diseases including rabies, distemper, parvo and leptospirosis.
Export permit (Japan)
No export permit required for pet dogs and cats leaving Japan. AQS (Animal Quarantine Service) export inspection applies.

Critical points to read before you book

The AQCS NOC must be obtained before the pet departs Japan. Arriving in India without an NOC results in the pet being held at the airport while the paperwork is resolved. Apply at least two weeks before travel.

Quarantine in India is 15 days for standard cases. Dogs arriving from Japan (a rabies-free country) are unlikely to need the 30-day extension, but this is at the AQCS officer's discretion.

Japan's AQS conducts an export inspection at the departure airport. Allow extra time at the airport for this check. The MAFF health certificate must be presented at AQS.

India only accepts pets arriving at airports with AQCS quarantine stations: Delhi, Mumbai, Chennai, Kolkata and Hyderabad. Do not route the pet through an airport not on this list.

Cats from Japan to India face the same requirements as dogs. The rabies vaccination and 15-day quarantine apply to both species.

India import rules, explained

India has a clear set of import requirements that are manageable with some planning. Follow them in order and keep an eye on the dates that cannot move.

Practical tips for this move

Microchip before you vaccinate. The microchip must be in place before the rabies shot for the vaccination to count, and getting this order wrong is the most common and costly mistake.

Use an official, government-authorised vet for the health certificate, not just your usual clinic. Many destinations only accept certificates signed by an approved vet.

If this is your first international move, consider an IPATA-registered pet transport agent. They handle the documents, airline booking and crate sizing, and can troubleshoot problems.

Get your pet used to the travel crate weeks in advance. A calm, crate-familiar animal travels far better than one meeting the crate for the first time at the airport.
