Requirements

What your pet needs to enter India

Every item below must be in place before your pet can travel. We manage and verify each one.

Microchip
ISO 11784/11785 or AVID microchip required.
Rabies vaccination
Required. Administered more than 1 month before arrival. Within 12 months of the travel date. Thailand is rabies-endemic, so India's AQCS will check vaccination dates carefully.
Rabies titre test
Not required. India does not mandate a FAVN titre test from Thailand or any other country.
Quarantine
15 days at an AQCS quarantine facility on arrival. Can extend to 30 days if health concerns arise during the observation period.
Health certificate
DLD export health certificate confirming freedom from infectious disease including rabies, distemper and leptospirosis. Must be current and clearly dated.
Export permit (Thailand)
No formal export permit required for pet dogs and cats leaving Thailand.

Critical points to read before you book

The AQCS NOC must be in hand before the pet's flight departs Bangkok. India does not issue the NOC on arrival. A pet arriving without one is detained at the airport.

Thailand is rabies-endemic. India's AQCS will pay close attention to the rabies vaccination dates on the DLD health certificate. Ensure the certificate is complete and clearly dated.

Only land at AQCS-equipped Indian airports: Delhi, Mumbai, Chennai, Kolkata or Hyderabad. Other Indian airports cannot process live animal arrivals.

The 15-day quarantine is mandatory and begins on the day of arrival. It cannot be shortened by documentation, route choice or breed. Build this into your timeline and budget.

Cats face the same requirements as dogs on this route: microchip, rabies vaccination, AQCS NOC and 15-day quarantine.

India entry requirements at a glance

Getting a pet into India is a middle of the road process. The rules are well defined, so most of the work is simply staying on schedule.

Getting the details right

Work backwards from your travel date and add a buffer. Fixed waiting periods mean a single slipped appointment can push the whole move back by weeks.

Check the airline’s crate size and labelling rules early. An undersized or wrongly labelled crate is a frequent reason pets are refused at check-in.

Ask about temperature embargoes for your travel window. Summer and winter cargo restrictions can rule out certain routes and dates entirely.

Microchip before you vaccinate. The microchip must be in place before the rabies shot for the vaccination to count, and getting this order wrong is the most common and costly mistake.
