Checklist

What your pet needs

Every item below must be completed and verified before your pet can travel. Expand each category for the detail.

Microchip
Recommended rather than strictly mandatory for entry, though every downstream document ends up referencing the microchip number, so fitting a 15-digit chip meeting ISO Standard 11784/11785 is worth doing regardless.
Rabies vaccination
Required, dated between 30 days and 12 months before entry. Where a primary dose or booster falls outside the previous vaccination's validity period, an additional 21-day wait after the new dose applies.
Rabies titre test
Not required. Cote d'Ivoire doesn't ask for a rabies antibody titre test from any country of origin, Canada included. This is the direct opposite of the CDC's own rules for a Cote d'Ivoire-origin dog entering the US, where the country's place on the CDC's high-risk list makes a titre test, or a 28-day quarantine instead, mandatory. Canada's own CFIA classifies Cote d'Ivoire as high-risk too, but that classification runs the other direction, governing entry into Canada, not entry into Cote d'Ivoire.
Quarantine
No quarantine for a pet arriving with compliant documentation. Veterinary services at the airport can place an animal under observation if paperwork is incomplete or disease is suspected, but that's a holding check rather than a fixed quarantine period.
Import permit
Required, issued by DSV (Direction des Services Veterinaires) under MIRAH, by email rather than through an online system. The completed application form and a copy of the rabies vaccination certificate go to carv.dsvci@gmail.com and dsv.sdsa2017@gmail.com. No turnaround time is published.
Health certificate
Health certificate completed close to travel, 5 to 10 days before arrival depending on the airline, required to be in French or accompanied by a certified French translation before officials will accept it on arrival at Port Bouet International Airport, Abidjan.
Export permit
No CFIA export permit required for a personal pet leaving Canada.
Health certificate
Health certificate issued by a CFIA District Veterinarian or an accredited Canadian vet, endorsed by CFIA if the destination requires it.

Carriers on this route

Connects Toronto to Addis Ababa nonstop, roughly seven times a week, then on to Abidjan on Ethiopian's own daily service. A single-carrier network end to end once past the first sector.

Critical points

This route runs opposite to Canada's own rules for the reverse direction. Cote d'Ivoire doesn't require a titre test coming in, but the same pet flying back into Canada would run into CFIA's high-risk classification for Cote d'Ivoire, a separate system with its own considerations.

The import permit runs through two DSV email addresses, not a portal. Send the application and rabies certificate copy at least 8-10 weeks ahead and follow up directly if there's no response within a couple of weeks.

The health certificate needs to be in French or carry a certified translation. A CFIA-endorsed, English-only certificate risks rejection on arrival without it.

Snub-nosed breeds face restrictions or exclusion from the hold on every carrier covering this route. Confirm your pet's breed against each airline's policy before booking.

What Ivory Coast requires

The requirements for Ivory Coast are thorough rather than difficult. Work through them methodically and confirm anything you are unsure about with the official source.

Avoiding the common mistakes

Ask about temperature embargoes for your travel window. Summer and winter cargo restrictions can rule out certain routes and dates entirely.

Microchip before you vaccinate. The microchip must be in place before the rabies shot for the vaccination to count, and getting this order wrong is the most common and costly mistake.

Use an official, government-authorised vet for the health certificate, not just your usual clinic. Many destinations only accept certificates signed by an approved vet.

If this is your first international move, consider an IPATA-registered pet transport agent. They handle the documents, airline booking and crate sizing, and can troubleshoot problems.
