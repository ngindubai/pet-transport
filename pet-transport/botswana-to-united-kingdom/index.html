Step by step

The Botswana to United Kingdom pet import process

Each step must be completed in a precise sequence. Start early. We manage every stage and deadline.

Responsible: Your vet in Botswana
Start of planning, 5-6 months before travel.

Responsible: Your vet and the approved laboratory

Responsible: No action needed, just planning

Responsible: You or your agent

Requirements

What your pet needs to enter United Kingdom

Every item below must be in place before your pet can travel. We manage and verify each one.

Microchip
ISO 11784/11785 microchip required, implanted before the rabies vaccination used for the titre test. A vaccine given first, chip second, does not count. Source: GOV.UK, 2026.
Rabies vaccination
Current rabies vaccination required. Botswana does not appear on either of GB's listed-country tables, so the vaccination itself doesn't clear a pet for travel; it starts the clock for the titre test below. Source: GOV.UK, 'Where you're travelling from,' 2026.
Rabies titre test
Required. Blood drawn no sooner than 30 days after vaccination, tested at an APHA-approved laboratory, with a result of at least 0.5 IU/ml. A further fixed 3 calendar months must then pass from the date blood was drawn, not the date the lab confirms a pass, before the pet can travel. Source: GOV.UK, 2026.
Quarantine
No routine quarantine for a pet that meets the unlisted-country rules in full. Non-compliant paperwork can trigger penalty quarantine of up to 4 months at the owner's cost. Source: GOV.UK, 2026.
Health certificate
Great Britain Pet Health Certificate, completed by the DVS-endorsed vet in Botswana and valid for 10 days from signature. It must still be valid on the date the pet lands in Great Britain.
Export permit (Botswana)
A standalone export permit from the Department of Veterinary Services (DVS), issued separately from the health certificate below. Typical processing time is 3 to 5 working days once the application and supporting paperwork are complete. Source: gov.bw and Botswana pet export guidance, 2026.

Critical points to read before you book

Botswana is unlisted for UK pet travel purposes. The titre test and the 3-month wait from the blood draw date are fixed, with no waiver and no way to shorten the clock once it starts.

There is no direct flight between Botswana and the UK. Every pet on this route transfers through Johannesburg, first on Airlink or Air Botswana, then on British Airways into Heathrow. Confirm both legs accept live animals in the hold before committing to a travel date.

Botswana's Department of Veterinary Services requires a standalone export permit, issued separately from the health certificate. Namibia's equivalent office doesn't ask for this extra document, so don't assume the two countries' paperwork is interchangeable if you're comparing routes.

Carriers approved for this route

Not all airlines accept live animals on this route. We know which carriers to use and how to book.

Airlink
Regional carrier connecting Sir Seretse Khama International (GBE) to O.R. Tambo, Johannesburg (JNB). Combined with Air Botswana, this sector runs around 60 scheduled departures a week, one of the busiest international routes out of Botswana and a genuinely reliable first leg for a live animal booking.
Cargo Only
AIR BOTSWANA
Air Botswana
The national carrier also flies GBE to JNB, alongside domestic routes to Maun, Kasane and Francistown. Confirm live animal acceptance and current cargo capacity directly, since capacity on regional turboprop and narrowbody equipment is limited.
Cargo Only

British Airways
Operates the Johannesburg to London Heathrow sector daily, with pet cargo handled through IAG Cargo. A Botswana-origin booking normally needs an IPATA-registered agent to manage the Johannesburg handover between the regional and long-haul legs.
Cargo Only

What United Kingdom requires

The requirements for United Kingdom are thorough rather than difficult. Work through them methodically and confirm anything you are unsure about with the official source.

Avoiding the common mistakes

Ask about temperature embargoes for your travel window. Summer and winter cargo restrictions can rule out certain routes and dates entirely.

Book the titre test laboratory well ahead. Approved labs have limited slots and the sample takes time to process, so this is not something to leave to the final weeks.

Microchip before you vaccinate. The microchip must be in place before the rabies shot for the vaccination to count, and getting this order wrong is the most common and costly mistake.

Use an official, government-authorised vet for the health certificate, not just your usual clinic. Many destinations only accept certificates signed by an approved vet.
