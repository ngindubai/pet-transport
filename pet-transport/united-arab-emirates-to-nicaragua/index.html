In short: Preparing to move a pet from United Arab Emirates to Nicaragua typically takes around 6-9 weeks. There is no routine quarantine on arrival for compliant pets. A rabies titre test is not required.

Checklist

What your pet needs

Every item below must be completed and verified before your pet can travel. Expand each category for the detail.

Microchip
Not mandated by Nicaragua's published general requirements, but worth fitting anyway since it's the fastest way to match an animal to its paperwork if a question comes up at the border. Source: IPSA general requirements, ipsa.gob.ni.
Rabies vaccination
Current rabies vaccination required, given no more than 365 days before the shipment date. Source: IPSA general animal quarantine requirements, ipsa.gob.ni.
Rabies titre test
Not required. IPSA's general rules don't ask a rabies titre test of any country of origin, the UAE included. This is the mirror image of the outbound route: a Nicaragua-origin pet needs a UAE titre test, but a UAE-origin pet does not need one to enter Nicaragua.
Quarantine
No period of physical confinement for a pet arriving with correct paperwork. Nicaragua's so-called quarantine permit is an administrative document issued at the port of entry, not a stay in kennels. Source: IPSA general requirements, ipsa.gob.ni.
Import permit
Not required for a personal pet dog or cat, only the quarantine permit collected on arrival. A separate import permit applies only to commercial shipments, such as multiple animals for sale or a change of ownership. Source: IPSA general requirements.
Health certificate
MOCCAE health certificate presented on arrival, along with the rabies vaccination record. IPSA doesn't publish a UAE-specific documentation pathway the way it does for the US, so budget time to confirm whether the certificate needs Spanish translation or consular legalisation before you fly. Source: IPSA general requirements, ipsa.gob.ni.
Export permit
No separate export permit for a personal pet. A MOCCAE veterinary health certificate, issued after a physical exam and valid 30 days from issue, is the core document. MOCCAE's own service explicitly exempts companion animals from needing a certificate of origin or a copy of the destination country's import approval.
Veterinary inspection
Physical exam by a UAE-licensed vet, confirming the pet is fit to fly and that the microchip, vaccination and any other records are consistent.
Government endorsement
No further government endorsement is required beyond the MOCCAE-issued certificate itself for a personal pet leaving the UAE.
Health certificate
MOCCAE veterinary health certificate, reformatted or supplemented to meet whatever documentation IPSA's Ventanilla Unica asks for on the Nicaraguan side, since Nicaragua has no published UAE-specific import checklist.

Airlines

Carriers on this route

Not all airlines accept live animals on this route. Confirm the operating carrier's current policy for every flight segment.

Emirates SkyCargo
Emirates' new non-stop Dubai to Miami service, flight EK213/EK214, runs four times a week on a Boeing 777-300ER, backed by existing SkyCargo freighter capacity. This is currently the fastest single leg out of the UAE toward Central America.
Cargo Only
American Airlines
Onward from Miami, American operates a daily non-stop to Managua (MGA). PetEmbark, American Airlines Cargo's pet programme, is open to the general public according to aacargo.com, not restricted to military shipments as some third-party guides claim.
Cargo Only
Turkish Airlines Cargo
Alternative first leg from Dubai to Istanbul, then Turkish Airlines' three-times-weekly Istanbul to Panama City service (flight TK903), with an onward Copa Airlines connection into Managua. Useful when Miami capacity is tight, though it adds a third handover.
Cargo Only
Copa Airlines
Handles the Panama City to Managua leg on the Turkish Airlines alternative routing. Copa Pets accepts cargo bookings Monday to Thursday only, and excludes several breeds including Pit Bull Terrier and English Bulldog.
Cargo Only
Costs

What this route typically costs

MOCCAE health certificate: AED 100 discounted personal-consignment fee, or AED 400 standard fee (roughly USD 27-109)
IATA-compliant travel crate: AED 350-1,100 (roughly USD 95-300)
Cargo fare, Dubai to Managua via Miami with Emirates SkyCargo and American Airlines: USD 2,000-3,800
Possible translation or consular legalisation of the export certificate for Nicaragua: USD 50-150 if IPSA requires it
Quarantine permit fee collected at Managua on arrival: modest, paid in Nicaraguan Cordoba (NIO)
Agent fees: USD 300-650
Total typical range: USD 2,600-4,900

Critical points

There is no non-stop flight from the UAE to Nicaragua. Even with Emirates' new Miami non-stop, the routing still needs a second confirmed cargo booking with a different carrier for the final leg into Managua.

Nicaragua has no published UAE-specific import checklist, unlike its documented pathway for US-origin pets. Confirm directly with IPSA whether the MOCCAE certificate needs Spanish translation or legalisation, rather than assuming it will be accepted as issued.

Copa Airlines only accepts pets as cargo Monday to Thursday. If the Turkish Airlines-Panama City alternative is your routing, plan the final leg around that restriction.

Nicaragua entry requirements at a glance

The path into Nicaragua is straightforward to follow once you know the steps. Give the time-sensitive items priority and check the details below.

Getting the details right

Work backwards from your travel date and add a buffer. Fixed waiting periods mean a single slipped appointment can push the whole move back by weeks.

Check the airline’s crate size and labelling rules early. An undersized or wrongly labelled crate is a frequent reason pets are refused at check-in.

Ask about temperature embargoes for your travel window. Summer and winter cargo restrictions can rule out certain routes and dates entirely.

Microchip before you vaccinate. The microchip must be in place before the rabies shot for the vaccination to count, and getting this order wrong is the most common and costly mistake.
