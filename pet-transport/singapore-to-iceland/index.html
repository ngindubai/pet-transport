Overview

A move from Singapore to Iceland with a dog or cat is mostly about preparing the documents in the right order. Expect around 12-16 weeks of lead time to line up the documents and the fixed waiting periods. Factor in 14 days of quarantine at the destination.

You will need a rabies titre test, which adds a mandatory wait, so begin it as soon as possible. Read on for the requirements, timing and carrier options for this route.

Checklist

Iceland: entry requirements

RequirementDetail
MicrochipISO 11784/11785 microchip required, implanted before the rabies vaccination. Source: MAST, mast.is.
Rabies vaccinationPrimary rabies vaccination after the microchip. MAST also requires two leptospirosis doses 3 to 4 weeks apart, plus current distemper, hepatitis, parvovirus and parainfluenza cover for dogs. Source: MAST guidance notes, 2026.
Rabies titre testRequired. Blood drawn at least 30 days after vaccination, minimum result 0.5 IU/ml. Singapore is named on MAST's own Category 1 list, alongside the EU member states, so once the result clears there is no further fixed wait before travel, the same treatment MAST gives Germany or France. This is a notably easier pre-export timeline than the reverse direction, since Singapore's own AVS does not extend Iceland the same courtesy. Source: MAST, mast.is, 2026.
QuarantineMandatory 14 days minimum at one of two approved isolation stations, Einangrunarstoedin i Reykjanesbae or Einangrunarstoedin Mosel, for every dog and cat entering Iceland, Category 1 origin or not. Source: MAST, mast.is, 2026.
Import permitRequired from MAST before travel, with the application form, a photo, the health certificate and lab results. Base fee is roughly ISK 40,000 (about EUR 270). Quarantine station space is booked separately and has limited intake dates. Source: MAST, mast.is, 2026.
Health certificateMAST's Category 1 certificate (Form D1 or C1), completed by the AVS-accredited vet in Singapore and endorsed by AVS.

Critical points

Read before you book

⚠ The 14-day quarantine applies regardless of Singapore's Category 1 status with MAST. That status shortens the pre-export titre test wait, not the isolation period on arrival.
⚠ No airline flies Singapore to Keflavik directly. Every move on this route transits at least one European hub, and the connecting Iceland-bound leg has to be booked as cargo since Icelandair no longer carries pets as checked baggage.
⚠ Keflavik is the only approved pet entry point in Iceland. Whatever hub you route through, that is where the final leg has to land.
⚠ MAST's Category 1 certificate must be completed by an AVS-accredited vet within 7 days of departure, a tighter window than the 10-day certificate validity used for most other EU-adjacent routes. Time this appointment carefully against your booked flight.
Carriers

Airlines on this route

Singapore Airlines Cargo
Handles the long-haul leg out of Changi to a European hub such as Frankfurt or Amsterdam. No single carrier flies Singapore to Keflavik, so this is booked as the first of two or more cargo legs.
Cargo Only
Lufthansa Cargo
Covers the final Frankfurt to Keflavik sector, roughly 8 flights a week, receiving pets into Lufthansa's own Frankfurt Animal Lounge for the transfer between the long-haul and short-haul legs. Icelandair also flies Frankfurt to Keflavik but, since 1 November 2024, only accepts pets as manifested cargo rather than checked baggage, worth confirming against Lufthansa's own terms before choosing between them.
Cargo Only

What Iceland requires

Bringing a pet into Iceland involves a fair amount of documentation. Keep everything organised and check each item against the current requirements before you book.

Avoiding the common mistakes

Ask about temperature embargoes for your travel window. Summer and winter cargo restrictions can rule out certain routes and dates entirely.

Book the titre test laboratory well ahead. Approved labs have limited slots and the sample takes time to process, so this is not something to leave to the final weeks.

Microchip before you vaccinate. The microchip must be in place before the rabies shot for the vaccination to count, and getting this order wrong is the most common and costly mistake.

Use an official, government-authorised vet for the health certificate, not just your usual clinic. Many destinations only accept certificates signed by an approved vet.
