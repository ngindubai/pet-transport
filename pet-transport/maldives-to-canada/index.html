Checklist

What your pet needs

Every item below must be completed and verified before your pet can travel. Expand each category for the detail.

Microchip
Not mandatory under CFIA's general personal pet policy, though most airlines and agents require one for practical handling and identification.
Rabies vaccination
Required for any cat aged 3 months or older. A rabies vaccination certificate in English or French, from a licensed vet, naming the animal, breed, colour, weight and vaccine details, dated within the last 3 years. Kittens under 3 months are exempt.
Rabies titre test
Not required. Canada does not ask a personal pet cat for a rabies antibody titre test, from any country, including the Maldives. Source: Canadian Food Inspection Agency (CFIA), 'Bringing animals to Canada: Importing and travelling with pets', inspection.canada.ca, 2026.
Quarantine
None required for a compliant cat. Canada quarantines only animals that arrive without valid paperwork or show signs of illness on inspection, and typically clears a compliant cat within 15 to 30 minutes at the port of entry.
Import permit
Not required for a personal pet cat.
Health certificate
The rabies vaccination certificate is the core document. Canada does not require a separate general veterinary health certificate for a personal pet cat.
Export permit
No dedicated export permit or licensing office exists for pets leaving the Maldives. There's no form to file.
Health certificate
Export health certificate from a private vet in Male confirming the cat's vaccination status and fitness to fly. No government endorsement stamp is standard practice, since no single agency issues one.

Critical points

Canada's process for a personal pet cat is markedly lighter than either the UK or EU pathway from the Maldives covered elsewhere on this site. There is no titre test and no 90-day wait; the rabies vaccination certificate alone, in English or French, generally carries the file.

The Maldives is regarded as low risk for rabies by other governments, the UAE classifies it as low risk and South Korea lists it as rabies-free, though Canada's requirement for a cat doesn't hinge on that classification the way some destinations' rules do.

This is a cat route in practice. Dogs face no legal bar to leaving the Maldives, but the country's own ban on dog imports means almost none exist there to make this trip.

There is no direct flight from Male to Canada. Every pet on this route makes at least one Gulf hub connection, and that transfer, not the Canadian entry, is where delay is most likely.

How Canada handles incoming pets

Canada keeps its requirements reasonable, though a few steps have fixed timing. Map those out first and the rest falls into place.

What experienced owners do

Use an official, government-authorised vet for the health certificate, not just your usual clinic. Many destinations only accept certificates signed by an approved vet.

If this is your first international move, consider an IPATA-registered pet transport agent. They handle the documents, airline booking and crate sizing, and can troubleshoot problems.

Get your pet used to the travel crate weeks in advance. A calm, crate-familiar animal travels far better than one meeting the crate for the first time at the airport.

Confirm live animal acceptance on every flight segment before booking. Not all aircraft or connections take pets, and cargo terms can differ leg to leg.
