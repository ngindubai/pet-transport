Overview

Getting a pet from United Kingdom to New Zealand is a step by step process, and each stage has its own timing. Plan for approximately 20-32 weeks. This is a rigorous route with little room to compress the schedule. Plan for 10 days of quarantine on arrival.

This route requires a rabies titre test with a set waiting period, so do not leave it late. Start with the step by step process below and build your plan from there.

Checklist

New Zealand: entry requirements

MicrochipRequired. ISO standard microchip, implanted before the rabies vaccination.
Rabies vaccinationRequired from most countries. The UK is a rabies-free approved country; MPI specifies the exact vaccination and testing conditions in the permit.
Rabies titre testRequired where specified by MPI. Rabies antibody titre and other disease tests (such as leptospirosis and brucellosis screening for dogs) per the permit.
QuarantineMinimum 10 days at an MPI-approved quarantine facility on arrival. Must be pre-booked before departure.
Import permitImport permit required from MPI before travel. Apply well in advance; the testing regime must be planned around it.

Critical points

Read before you book

⚠ New Zealand has a strict testing regime beyond rabies, including disease screening for dogs (such as leptospirosis and brucellosis) with specific timing windows. Plan around the MPI permit conditions exactly.
⚠ A minimum 10-day quarantine at an MPI-approved facility is mandatory and must be pre-booked before departure. Spaces are limited.
⚠ The import permit must be in place before travel and the health certificate must match it exactly.
⚠ Microchip must pre-date the rabies vaccination.
⚠ New Zealand prohibits certain breeds including Pit Bull Terrier types, Dogo Argentino, Brazilian Fila and Japanese Tosa.

New Zealand import rules, explained

New Zealand takes biosecurity seriously, and the requirements reflect that. The paperwork is exacting, so accuracy matters as much as timing.

Practical tips for this move

Check the airline’s crate size and labelling rules early. An undersized or wrongly labelled crate is a frequent reason pets are refused at check-in.

Ask about temperature embargoes for your travel window. Summer and winter cargo restrictions can rule out certain routes and dates entirely.

Book the titre test laboratory well ahead. Approved labs have limited slots and the sample takes time to process, so this is not something to leave to the final weeks.

Microchip before you vaccinate. The microchip must be in place before the rabies shot for the vaccination to count, and getting this order wrong is the most common and costly mistake.
