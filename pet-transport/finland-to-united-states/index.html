Critical points

Finland is rabies-free, which is the best possible starting point for any international pet move. This makes the US entry especially smooth.

Finnish winters are severe. If you depart Helsinki between November and March, check whether your airline applies cold weather cargo embargoes. Very cold temperatures in the hold can be as much a risk as heat for live animals.

Finnair's direct HEL to JFK service is a good option because it avoids a connecting leg, but cargo pet space is limited. Book as early as possible.

There is no tapeworm treatment required for dogs moving from Finland to the US. The tapeworm rule applies to dogs entering the UK, not the US.

Meeting United States's entry requirements

Bringing a pet into United States is one of the simpler processes. Cover the core requirements and you should have few surprises.

Advice worth following

Confirm live animal acceptance on every flight segment before booking. Not all aircraft or connections take pets, and cargo terms can differ leg to leg.

Keep every original document together and take copies. Border officials want originals, and a missing signature or date can hold up clearance.

Work backwards from your travel date and add a buffer. Fixed waiting periods mean a single slipped appointment can push the whole move back by weeks.

Check the airline’s crate size and labelling rules early. An undersized or wrongly labelled crate is a frequent reason pets are refused at check-in.

Common questions

Finnair does not allow pets in the cabin on transatlantic routes. Pets travel as cargo in the pressurised hold. On shorter European flights, small pets may travel in-cabin, but not on the Helsinki to US direct service.
No. Tapeworm treatment (specifically for Echinococcus multilocularis) is required for dogs entering the UK, Ireland, and some other countries from outside, but not for dogs entering the United States. You do not need this treatment on the Finland-to-US route.
The CDC does not require rabies vaccination for dogs from CDC low-risk countries like Finland. However, virtually all airlines that carry pets as cargo will require a current rabies vaccination. In practice, an unvaccinated dog will be refused by the airline before it ever gets to US customs. Get the vaccination at least 21 days before travel to allow the EU-standard post-vaccination wait period.
