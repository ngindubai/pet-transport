Checklist

What your pet needs

Every item below must be completed and verified before your pet can travel. Expand each category for the detail.

Microchip
ISO 11784/11785 (15-digit) microchip required, recorded in the EU pet passport.
Rabies vaccination
Current rabies vaccination required. First vaccination: minimum 21 days before entry. Booster within valid period: no wait.
Rabies titre test
Not required. Italy and Slovenia are both EU member states. No rabies antibody titre test under the EU Pet Travel Scheme.
Quarantine
Not required for compliant arrivals from other EU member states.
Import permit
Not required for non-commercial companion animal movements within the EU.
Health certificate
EU pet passport. Valid throughout the EU including Slovenia.
Export permit
No export permit required for companion animals moving between EU member states.
Health certificate
EU pet passport issued by a veterinario ufficiale in Italy. Records microchip number, rabies vaccination history, and owner details. No additional export health certificate needed for intra-EU movements.

Carriers on this route

Not all airlines accept live animals on this route. Confirm the operating carrier's current policy for every flight segment.

Overland from Trieste or Venice to Ljubljana
The drive from Trieste (Italy) to Ljubljana (Slovenia) is approximately 100km and takes under 1.5 hours via the A1 motorway. From Venice, the drive to Ljubljana is approximately 2 hours. Both Italy and Slovenia are EU Schengen members with open borders. No pet border check, no customs stop. A completely practical option for all pet sizes.
Cabin Only
Lufthansa via Frankfurt and then Austrian to Ljubljana
Lufthansa connects Rome FCO and Milan MXP to Frankfurt (FRA). Austrian Airlines connects VIE to LJU. This double-connection routing works for cargo pets if driving is not an option. Frankfurt Animal Lounge handles live animal cargo at FRA.
Cargo and Cabin
Austrian Airlines via Vienna
Austrian Airlines operates from FCO to Vienna (VIE) with onward VIE to Ljubljana (LJU) services. A cleaner two-leg option than the Frankfurt route. Austrian accepts cabin pets and cargo animals on both legs.
Cargo and Cabin
Ryanair (cabin only, seasonal)
Ryanair operates seasonal Italy-Ljubljana services from Rome Ciampino (CIA). Cabin pets only. No cargo service. Only for cats and very small dogs.

Critical points

For owners in northeastern Italy, driving to Ljubljana is genuinely the easiest option. The Trieste-Ljubljana highway is well-maintained and fast. No pet document checks at the Italian-Slovenian border.

Slovenia classifies some breeds as potentially dangerous (Rottweilers, Pit Bull-type dogs) requiring muzzling in public. Check UVHVR guidance at uvhvr.gov.si before moving with these breeds.

Ljubljana Airport (LJU) is small. Live animal cargo capacity is limited. For large dogs by air, Austrian Airlines via Vienna is preferred over trying to book direct cargo into LJU.

EU pet passport must be signed by a veterinario ufficiale. Not all private Italian vets have official authorisation.

Meeting Slovenia's entry requirements

Bringing a pet into Slovenia is one of the simpler processes. Cover the core requirements and you should have few surprises.

Advice worth following

Confirm live animal acceptance on every flight segment before booking. Not all aircraft or connections take pets, and cargo terms can differ leg to leg.

Keep every original document together and take copies. Border officials want originals, and a missing signature or date can hold up clearance.

Work backwards from your travel date and add a buffer. Fixed waiting periods mean a single slipped appointment can push the whole move back by weeks.

Check the airline’s crate size and labelling rules early. An undersized or wrongly labelled crate is a frequent reason pets are refused at check-in.
