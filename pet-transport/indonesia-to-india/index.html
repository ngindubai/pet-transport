In short: Preparing to move a pet from Indonesia to India typically takes around 6-10 weeks. Expect 15 days of quarantine on arrival. A rabies titre test is not required.

Step by step

The Indonesia to India import process

Responsible: You or Indonesian export agent

AQCS NOC applied for via aqcsindia.gov.in, at least 15 days before India arrival.

Requirements

India entry requirements

Every item below must be in place before your pet can enter. We verify and track each one.

Microchip
ISO 11784/11785 microchip required. Source: AQCS India (aqcsindia.gov.in), 2026.
Rabies vaccination
Rabies vaccination required. More than 1 month before travel, within 12 months. Core vaccines required. Source: AQCS India, 2026.
Rabies titre test
Not required for pets importing to India. Source: AQCS India, 2026.
Quarantine
15-day quarantine at an AQCS facility at the port of entry. Owner pays all costs. Source: AQCS India, 2026.
Import permit
AQCS NOC (No Objection Certificate) required. Must be processed at least 15 days before arrival. Applied for via the AQCS portal to the station at the entry airport. Source: AQCS India (aqcsindia.gov.in), 2026.
Leaving Indonesia

Export requirements

Export permit
BARANTAN export recommendation required before travel. Source: BARANTAN Indonesia, 2026.
Health certificate
Official health certificate from a BARANTAN-authorised veterinarian, meeting India AQCS requirements. Source: BARANTAN Indonesia, 2026.
Costs

What this route typically costs

BARANTAN health certificate and export permit: IDR 500,000-1,500,000 (approx. USD 31-92)
AQCS NOC fee: INR 500-2,000 (approx. USD 6-24)
AQCS quarantine (15 days): INR 1,500-5,000 (approx. USD 18-60)
Cargo CGK to BOM/DEL via hub: USD 1,500-4,500
IATA crate: USD 80-300
Agent fees: USD 200-500
Total typical range: USD 1,835-5,476

Critical points

The AQCS NOC must be lodged at least 15 days before your pet arrives in India. Apply to the AQCS station at your arrival airport. Missing this deadline means the pet cannot enter.

AQCS quarantine stations are at Delhi, Mumbai, Chennai, Kolkata, and Hyderabad. Direct the NOC application to the station serving your specific entry airport.

How India handles incoming pets

India keeps its requirements reasonable, though a few steps have fixed timing. Map those out first and the rest falls into place.

What experienced owners do

Use an official, government-authorised vet for the health certificate, not just your usual clinic. Many destinations only accept certificates signed by an approved vet.

If this is your first international move, consider an IPATA-registered pet transport agent. They handle the documents, airline booking and crate sizing, and can troubleshoot problems.

Get your pet used to the travel crate weeks in advance. A calm, crate-familiar animal travels far better than one meeting the crate for the first time at the airport.

Confirm live animal acceptance on every flight segment before booking. Not all aircraft or connections take pets, and cargo terms can differ leg to leg.
