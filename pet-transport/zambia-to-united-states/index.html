Overview

Shipping a dog or cat from Zambia to United States rewards owners who start planning early. Expect a long lead time of roughly 12-16 weeks; the waiting periods here cannot be rushed. There is no routine quarantine, provided the paperwork is in order.

Depending on origin, a rabies titre test may be part of the process here. The sections below cover each requirement in detail.

Step by step

The Zambia to United States process

1
Start of planning, at least 12-14 weeks before travel.
Microchip the dog, then administer the rabies vaccination in that order. Cats need no CDC vaccination but should still be current on Zambia's own paperwork.

Responsible: Zambia-based vet

2
Roughly 8-10 weeks before travel.
Draw blood at least 30 days after vaccination for the rabies titre test, sent to a CDC-approved laboratory since none is confirmed operating within Zambia.

Responsible: Vet and the CDC-approved laboratory

3
As soon as the titre result is confirmed, always before the flight.
Once a passing result is confirmed, complete the CDC Dog Import Form and book a reservation at a CDC-registered animal care facility at the intended US arrival airport.

Responsible: Owner

4
4-6 weeks before travel.
Obtain the Certification of Foreign Rabies Vaccination and Microchip form, endorsed by a Zambian government vet.

Responsible: Zambia-based official vet

5
Once the titre result and facility reservation are both confirmed.
Book the routing: Kenya Airways via Nairobi into JFK, Ethiopian via Addis Ababa into Dulles, or Emirates via Dubai into any of the four CDC-registered gateways it serves.

Responsible: Owner or agent, with the airline's cargo desk

6
Within the certificate's stated validity.
Collect the final Zambian health documentation, valid only for a short window before travel.

Responsible: Zambia-based vet

7
Day of arrival.
Arrival at the CDC-registered facility airport. If the titre result was not passing, the 28-day quarantine begins here instead of a border check.

Responsible: CDC-registered facility staff and CBP

Checklist

United States: entry requirements

RequirementDetail
MicrochipA universal-scanner-readable microchip is required before the rabies vaccination is given. Because Zambia is on the CDC's high-risk list for dog rabies, a vaccine given before the chip does not count towards US entry at all; the sequence has to be right the first time. Source: CDC, cdc.gov/importation, updated 15 April 2026.
Rabies vaccinationRequired for dogs, first dose no earlier than 12 weeks old. Cats face no federal CDC rabies vaccination requirement whatsoever, only a general health check on arrival.
Rabies titre testRequired for a dog travelling on a Zambia-issued vaccination certificate. Blood must be drawn at least 30 days after vaccination and at least 28 days before the flight lands in the US, tested at a CDC-approved laboratory. Zambia has no confirmed government-approved rabies serology laboratory of its own on record, so the sample is normally couriered overseas, which adds transit time on top of the standard lab turnaround. A dog without a passing result still enters the country, but only after a 28-day quarantine at a CDC-registered facility. Source: CDC, 'Entry Requirements for Foreign-Vaccinated Dogs from High-Risk Countries,' cdc.gov, 2026.
QuarantineNone for a dog arriving with a valid passing titre and a completed CDC Dog Import Form. Without the titre, a mandatory 28-day quarantine at a CDC-registered animal care facility applies, at the owner's cost. Cats face no CDC quarantine requirement.
Import permitCDC Dog Import Form, a free form completed online by the owner before travel, one per dog, with a current photograph. A reservation at a CDC-registered animal care facility must also be booked ahead of departure, since Zambia is high-risk. No equivalent form exists for cats.
Health certificateCDC does not itself demand a Zambia-endorsed export certificate for entry, but airlines generally want a veterinary health certificate issued within 10 days of travel, and a Customs and Border Protection officer can refuse entry to any animal that looks unwell regardless of paperwork.

Leaving Zambia

Export requirements

RequirementDetail
Export permitNo published online export portal for pets. Exporters deal directly with the Department of Veterinary Services (Ministry of Fisheries and Livestock) for the documentation the animal needs to leave the country.
Veterinary inspectionA government or government-recognised veterinarian must examine the animal, confirm the microchip reads correctly and confirm vaccination status before any export paperwork is issued.
Government endorsementThe Certification of Foreign Rabies Vaccination and Microchip form, the document CDC actually asks for, has to be completed by the treating vet and endorsed by a government veterinarian in Zambia. A general health certificate on its own does not satisfy this requirement.
Health certificateCertification of Foreign Rabies Vaccination and Microchip form, vet-completed and government-endorsed, plus a standard veterinary health certificate most airlines will also want. Source: CDC, 'Bringing a Dog into the United States,' cdc.gov/importation, updated 15 April 2026.
Costs

What this route typically costs

1Zambian vet visits, microchip and vaccination: USD 60-150. No single published government fee schedule; confirm locally.
2Rabies titre test plus courier to an approved overseas laboratory: USD 150-300
3Zambian export documentation from the Department of Veterinary Services: fee not published; contact Mulungushi House, Lusaka directly
4CDC Dog Import Form: free
5IATA-compliant travel crate: USD 150-400
6Cargo fare from Lusaka to a CDC-registered US airport, via Nairobi, Addis Ababa or Dubai: USD 2,200-4,000 depending on crate size and season
7CDC-registered facility fee if the 28-day quarantine option applies: set individually by each facility, not published centrally, get a written quote before booking
8Total typical range for a compliant dog with a passing titre: USD 2,600-4,800. Cats cost considerably less, since none of the CDC dog-specific fees apply.
Critical points

Read before you book

⚠ Zambia's high-risk status on the CDC's dog rabies list, last updated 15 April 2026, means the microchip-before-vaccine sequence, the titre test from a CDC-approved lab, and a booked reservation at a CDC-registered facility airport are not optional extras. Miss any one and the fallback is a 28-day quarantine, or the airline refusing to board the dog at all.
⚠ No airline flies Lusaka to the US nonstop. Every workable routing adds at least one stop, through Nairobi, Addis Ababa or Dubai.
⚠ Zambia has no confirmed government-approved rabies serology laboratory, so the titre blood sample usually has to travel before the result comes back. Build that courier time into the schedule.
⚠ Cats get a dramatically lighter federal rule than dogs on this exact route. No CDC rabies vaccination, no titre test, no facility reservation. Zambia's own export paperwork still applies to cats in full, but the US side is close to a formality.
Carriers

Airlines on this route

Kenya Airways
Currently the only airline flying Nairobi to New York nonstop, as flight KQ2, roughly 15 hours on a Boeing 787. A Lusaka-Nairobi connection feeds into it, and JFK happens to be one of the six CDC-registered facility airports, so a pet on this routing lands directly at a compliant airport without needing a second domestic leg. Live animals travel as cargo only on Kenya Airways, never in the cabin.
Cargo Only
Ethiopian Airlines
Connects Lusaka to Washington Dulles via its Addis Ababa hub, roughly daily on the Lusaka-Addis sector, with the Addis-Dulles leg flown as ET501 on an Airbus A350. Dulles is also a CDC-registered facility airport. Small pets in an approved container can travel in the cabin depending on aircraft type; larger dogs go as cargo. Snub-nosed breeds are not accepted at all.
Cabin and Cargo
Emirates SkyCargo
Emirates already flies Lusaka to Dubai nonstop as EK713/EK714, and separately runs nonstop Dubai services into JFK, LAX, Miami and Washington Dulles, all four CDC-registered airports. Every leg on this option stays with the same carrier, which some owners prefer for a live animal move over an interline handover.
Cargo Only

United States entry requirements at a glance

Entry to United States is tightly controlled, and officials verify each requirement in turn. Treat it as a sequence where every step gates the next.

Getting the details right

Work backwards from your travel date and add a buffer. Fixed waiting periods mean a single slipped appointment can push the whole move back by weeks.

Check the airline’s crate size and labelling rules early. An undersized or wrongly labelled crate is a frequent reason pets are refused at check-in.

Ask about temperature embargoes for your travel window. Summer and winter cargo restrictions can rule out certain routes and dates entirely.

Book the titre test laboratory well ahead. Approved labs have limited slots and the sample takes time to process, so this is not something to leave to the final weeks.
