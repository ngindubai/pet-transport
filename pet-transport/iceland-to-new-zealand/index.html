Checklist

What your pet needs

Every item below must be completed and verified before your pet can travel. Expand each category for the detail.

Microchip
ISO 11784/11785 microchip required, implanted before any vaccination and confirmed by the certifying vet. Source: MPI, mpi.govt.nz, checked July 2026.
Rabies vaccination
A current rabies vaccination is generally expected on the health certificate, even though the titre test itself does not apply. Many Icelandic pets have never been vaccinated against rabies at all, since MAST's own border quarantine substitutes for a domestic vaccination programme, so this is worth raising with an Icelandic vet early rather than assuming the pet is already covered.
Rabies titre test
Not required. MPI places Iceland on its recognised list of rabies-free countries, the same group covering Japan, Singapore and Hawaii, which skips the FAVN titre test and the 180-day wait that applies to most European countries, Germany and the UK included. Source: MPI, mpi.govt.nz, cross-checked against independent pet transport industry sources, July 2026.
Quarantine
Mandatory minimum 10 days at an MPI-approved quarantine facility. Pets must arrive at Auckland International Airport (AKL) or Christchurch Airport (CHC). Source: MPI, mpi.govt.nz, checked July 2026.
Import permit
MPI import permit required before travel, applied for online. Allow at least 6 weeks for processing. Source: MPI, mpi.govt.nz, checked July 2026.
Health certificate
MPI's model certificate for rabies-free-recognised countries, completed by a MAST-accredited vet and endorsed by MAST. Issued close to the travel date.
Export permit
No separate export permit. MAST's endorsement of the health certificate is the export step, the same as any Icelandic departure.
Health certificate
Not the standard EU-form certificate used on Iceland's European routes. MPI publishes its own model certificate for cats and dogs from countries it recognises as rabies-free, and this is the form a MAST-accredited vet must complete, then have endorsed by MAST. Source: MAST, mast.is; MPI model certificate, mpi.govt.nz, checked July 2026.

Critical points

MPI's rabies-free recognition removes the FAVN titre test and the 180-day wait, not the quarantine or the import permit. Owners moving from a Category 1-style origin sometimes assume the whole process is light. It is not, though it is considerably shorter than the 36 to 44 weeks quoted for a Germany-to-New Zealand move on this site.

Many Icelandic pets have no rabies vaccination history at all, since domestic vaccination is rarely needed inside Iceland's own border controls. Confirm with the vet at the very first appointment whether one is genuinely required for MPI's certificate, and do not assume it is already in place.

No airline flies Keflavik direct to Auckland or Christchurch. Every routing runs through at least one hub, and since 1 November 2024 Icelandair carries pets only as manifested cargo on every sector it operates.

Pets must land at Auckland or Christchurch specifically. No other New Zealand airport is approved for an international pet arrival, whatever your final destination in the country.

What New Zealand requires

Bringing a pet into New Zealand involves a fair amount of documentation. Keep everything organised and check each item against the current requirements before you book.

Avoiding the common mistakes

Ask about temperature embargoes for your travel window. Summer and winter cargo restrictions can rule out certain routes and dates entirely.

Microchip before you vaccinate. The microchip must be in place before the rabies shot for the vaccination to count, and getting this order wrong is the most common and costly mistake.

Use an official, government-authorised vet for the health certificate, not just your usual clinic. Many destinations only accept certificates signed by an approved vet.

If this is your first international move, consider an IPATA-registered pet transport agent. They handle the documents, airline booking and crate sizing, and can troubleshoot problems.
