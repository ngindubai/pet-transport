Requirements

Algeria entry requirements

Every item below must be in place before your pet can enter. We verify and track each one.

Microchip
ISO 11784/11785 microchip required, implanted before the rabies vaccination. A vaccination given first is not recognised.
Rabies vaccination
Current rabies vaccination required, given 21 to 30 days before entry (30 days is the safer margin to avoid disputes at the border), valid for up to 12 months.
Rabies titre test
A rabies antibody titre test is not routinely required for a pet arriving from the UK, since the UK is treated as a controlled-rabies-risk country of origin. Requirements for individual cases can vary, so confirm current status with DSV, the Algerian consulate, or the receiving vet before travel.
Quarantine
No mandatory quarantine for a pet arriving with complete, compliant documentation. Non-compliant animals may face quarantine where facilities allow, forced re-export, or refusal of entry.
Import permit
Sources differ on whether a standalone import authorisation from DSV is required for an owner travelling with their own pet; some describe it as unnecessary for accompanied pets, others as advisable to arrange in advance through the Algerian consulate or DSV directly. Confirm directly with DSV or the consulate before travel rather than assuming either position.
Health certificate
The UK-issued Export Health Certificate, in French or Arabic or with certified translation, examined on arrival. Should be dated within 10 days of the health exam.
Leaving United Kingdom

Export requirements

Export permit
An export application (EXA) is submitted to APHA with the certificate request; there is no separate standalone export permit for a companion pet.
Veterinary inspection
Clinical examination by an APHA-authorised Official Veterinarian (OV), confirming the pet meets Algeria's stated import conditions.
Government endorsement
Only an APHA-authorised OV can issue and sign the Export Health Certificate. Applications submitted more than 7 working days ahead are processed 7 working days before the certification date. Source: GOV.UK, 'Taking your pet dog, cat or ferret abroad: Getting an animal health certificate,' 2026.
Health certificate
Export Health Certificate covering microchip, rabies vaccination and general health, valid a limited number of days, commonly 10, before travel. Algerian authorities expect this in French or Arabic, or with a certified translation attached.

Critical points

Algeria treats certain dog breeds, including Pit Bull-types and Staffordshire Bull Terriers, as a border-control issue in their own right. A restricted breed can be refused entry even with every other document perfectly in order, so confirm the position with DSV or the Algerian consulate before booking any travel.

Guidance on whether a standalone DSV import authorisation is required for an accompanied pet is genuinely mixed across sources. Don't assume either way; get a direct answer from DSV or the Algerian consulate well before travel so there's time to apply if one turns out to be needed.

Air Algérie will not carry pets in the hold on flights to or from the UK, despite operating direct routes into Heathrow, Stansted and Manchester. Bookings have to go through Air Algérie Cargo as freight or a different carrier's cargo service entirely.

Export certificates issued only in English can cause delays at the Algerian end. Arrange a certified French or Arabic translation alongside the APHA certificate rather than after it.

What Algeria requires

Getting a pet into Algeria is a middle of the road process. The rules are well defined, so most of the work is simply staying on schedule.

Avoiding the common mistakes

If this is your first international move, consider an IPATA-registered pet transport agent. They handle the documents, airline booking and crate sizing, and can troubleshoot problems.

Get your pet used to the travel crate weeks in advance. A calm, crate-familiar animal travels far better than one meeting the crate for the first time at the airport.

Confirm live animal acceptance on every flight segment before booking. Not all aircraft or connections take pets, and cargo terms can differ leg to leg.

Keep every original document together and take copies. Border officials want originals, and a missing signature or date can hold up clearance.
