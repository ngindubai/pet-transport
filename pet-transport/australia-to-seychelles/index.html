Requirements

Seychelles entry requirements

Every item below must be in place before your pet can enter. We verify and track each one.

Microchip
Microchip number must be declared on the National Biosecurity Agency (NBA) application. Industry practice points to ISO 11784/11785 compliance, though the current NBA document doesn't restate the standard explicitly.
Rabies vaccination
Minimum age 3 months for an inactivated rabies vaccine. Booster given 30 days to 12 months before export. All vaccinations, not just rabies, must be given at least 2 weeks before departure. Minimum age to travel is 16 weeks.
Rabies titre test
The NBA's current 2024 import document describes a single 'rabies-affected countries' pathway requiring a titre test and a fixed 3-month wait from the blood draw, and no longer spells out a distinct lighter route for rabies-free-country pets, even though the companion application form still references a shorter 4-week timeline for that category. This is a genuine, unresolved inconsistency between two live official Seychelles documents. Given Australia's internationally recognised rabies-free status, it's worth asking the NBA directly whether the lighter pathway the application form still names actually applies before assuming the full titre-test route. Source: NBA, 'Importation of Cats and Dogs,' 2024, and companion application form.
Quarantine
Mandatory by law, no exceptions. The 2024 document states 4 weeks (28 days) minimum for a compliant pet from a rabies-affected origin; the general baseline in the same document is 14 days. Which applies to a genuinely rabies-free Australian pet is exactly the ambiguity flagged above, and worth confirming with the NBA directly.
Import permit
Required from the NBA, issued within 7 working days of a complete application and valid for 1 month from the intended arrival date. The NBA's application form suggests 4 weeks' notice for rabies-free-country pets versus 4 months for rabies-affected-country pets, another reason to clarify Australia's actual treatment before finalising the timeline.
Health certificate
Health certificate confirming microchip, vaccination and, if required, titre result. Dogs also need clearance for Brucella canis, Trypanosoma evansi, Babesia gibsoni, Leishmania and Erlichia canis unless the exporting country's vet certifies the country free of these diseases.
Leaving Australia

Export requirements

Export permit
Required under Australia's Export Control Act 1982. Any state-registered veterinarian can prepare a companion animal for export. The owner submits a Notice of Intention (NOI) to Export Live Animals to DAFF's regional office, at least 10 working days before departure.
Health certificate
DAFF issues the export permit and health certificate once satisfied the animal meets Seychelles' published import requirements and Australian export law. The final veterinary check must happen within 72 hours of departure, and the export permit itself is valid for only 72 hours after issue.
Costs

What this route typically costs

DAFF export inspection and certificate: time-based fee, confirm current rate with the regional DAFF office.
Titre test and laboratory fee, if required: AUD 220-400.
NBA import permit and Ministry of Finance customs permit: fees set at application.
Quarantine facility, if the 4-week pathway applies: SR 1,000 for the first 2 weeks, plus SR 500 per week thereafter.
IATA-compliant travel crate: AUD 130-450.
Cargo, Australia to Mahe via Dubai or Abu Dhabi: AUD 2,200-4,600.
Total typical range: AUD 3,500-11,000, with the timeline and much of the cost hinging on which NBA pathway applies to Australia's rabies-free status.

Critical points

Seychelles' own 2024 procedural document and its companion application form disagree on whether a rabies-free-origin pet, which would include an Australian pet, gets a lighter pathway. Confirm directly with the NBA before assuming either the full titre-test route or the shorter one.

If the full pathway applies, the 3-month wait after the titre blood draw is fixed and cannot be shortened, and quarantine at Seychelles' single Mahe facility runs around 4 weeks even for a compliant pet.

Seychelles' health screen for dogs goes beyond rabies, covering Brucella canis, Trypanosoma evansi, Babesia gibsoni, Leishmania and Erlichia canis. Confirm this panel with the treating vet regardless of which pathway applies.

How Seychelles handles incoming pets

Seychelles asks for several pieces of paperwork, and they all need to line up. A checklist and a clear timeline are your best tools here.

What experienced owners do

Get your pet used to the travel crate weeks in advance. A calm, crate-familiar animal travels far better than one meeting the crate for the first time at the airport.

Confirm live animal acceptance on every flight segment before booking. Not all aircraft or connections take pets, and cargo terms can differ leg to leg.

Keep every original document together and take copies. Border officials want originals, and a missing signature or date can hold up clearance.

Work backwards from your travel date and add a buffer. Fixed waiting periods mean a single slipped appointment can push the whole move back by weeks.
