Requirements

What your pet needs to enter New Zealand

Every item below must be completed and verified. We manage and cross-check each one.

Entry into New Zealand
Microchip
Required (ISO 11784/11785). Must be implanted before or on the same day as the first rabies vaccination.
Required
Rabies vaccination
Two vaccinations required. Both administered after microchipping. At least 28 days apart. Must be inactivated virus type.
Required
Rabies titre test
Mandatory for all countries. FAVN test at an MPI-approved laboratory. Minimum result 0.5 IU/ml. Blood drawn at least 30 days after the second vaccination. 180-day wait from blood draw date before entry. No exemptions.
Critical
Quarantine
Mandatory. Minimum 10 days at an MPI-approved quarantine facility at owner's expense. This is in addition to the 180-day wait which happens before travel.
Critical
Import permit
Required. NZSTAP (New Zealand Standard Treatment and Approval Process) approval needed before travel. Apply via Biosecurity New Zealand.
Required
Health certificate
APQA-issued health certificate in MPI format, within 10 days of travel.
Required
Leaving South Korea
Export permit
No formal export permit for personal pets. APQA issues the export health certificate.
Required
Health certificate
APQA-issued health certificate in NZ MPI format, within 10 days of travel. Must confirm microchip, two rabies vaccinations, titre test result, and health status.
Required

Critical points

New Zealand requires a titre test and 180-day wait for ALL countries without exception. There is no fast-track entry.

10-day quarantine is mandatory on arrival, even after the 180-day pre-travel wait.

Apply for NZSTAP approval early, processing takes time and your pet can't travel without it.

MPI requirements are strict and enforced. An IPATA-registered agent with NZ experience is strongly recommended.

Meeting New Zealand's entry requirements

Getting a pet into New Zealand means meeting a demanding set of rules with no shortcuts. Start from the earliest deadline and work forward from there.

Advice worth following

Confirm live animal acceptance on every flight segment before booking. Not all aircraft or connections take pets, and cargo terms can differ leg to leg.

Keep every original document together and take copies. Border officials want originals, and a missing signature or date can hold up clearance.

Work backwards from your travel date and add a buffer. Fixed waiting periods mean a single slipped appointment can push the whole move back by weeks.

Check the airline’s crate size and labelling rules early. An undersized or wrongly labelled crate is a frequent reason pets are refused at check-in.
