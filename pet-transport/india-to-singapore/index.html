Requirements

Singapore entry requirements

Every item below must be in place before your pet can enter. We verify and track each one.

Microchip
Required. ISO 11784/11785 microchip. Must be implanted before vaccination for the vaccination to count.
Rabies vaccination
Required. Blood draw for titre test must be at least 28 days after rabies vaccination.
Rabies titre test
Required. India is a Schedule III country for Singapore AVS. Rabies antibody titre test required: minimum 0.5 IU/ml. Blood draw must be at least 28 days after rabies vaccination. Test result valid for 90 days to 12 months before Singapore entry. Source: AVS NParks, avs.nparks.gov.sg.
Quarantine
Mandatory. 30 days at the Singapore Animal Quarantine Centre (AQC). All Schedule III arrivals undergo 30-day quarantine. Quarantine fees are owner-paid.
Import permit
Required. Apply to AVS NParks at least 30 days before arrival. Permit specifies all conditions for import.
Health certificate
Required. Issued by a government-authorised vet in India in AVS-approved format, issued within 10 days of travel.
Leaving India

Export requirements

Export permit
No Objection Certificate (NOC) from the Animal Quarantine and Certification Service (AQCS), India. Required for export of pets. Apply through the relevant AQCS office in your state.
Health certificate
Health certificate issued by a government-authorised veterinarian in India. Endorsed by the Department of Animal Husbandry and Dairying (DAHD) or state animal husbandry authority. Must comply with AVS NParks Singapore import format requirements.

Airline cargo fees from India to Singapore: typically INR 20,000-60,000 depending on crate size, weight, and city pair

AQCS NOC processing fee: varies by state; typically INR 500-2,000
Pet transport agent fee (strongly recommended): INR 15,000-50,000 for India-Singapore coordination

Critical points

India is Schedule III for Singapore. 30-day quarantine is mandatory and cannot be reduced or waived, regardless of health status or documentation.

The titre test blood draw must be at least 28 days after the rabies vaccination. Test results are valid for 90 days to 12 months before Singapore entry. Time these carefully or the test will expire.

All pets arriving from India are vaccinated against rabies again on arrival at AQC Singapore, as required for Schedule III countries.

The AQCS NOC is required in addition to the Indian health certificate. Both must be in hand before travel.

Singapore has a strict breed ban. Bull Terriers, Pit Bulls, Neapolitan Mastiffs, and other specified breeds cannot be imported. Check the full list on avs.nparks.gov.sg before beginning any process.

Singapore import rules, explained

Singapore runs a strict import regime, so every document is checked closely on arrival. Build your plan around the fixed waiting periods, because they set the pace for everything else.

Practical tips for this move

Keep every original document together and take copies. Border officials want originals, and a missing signature or date can hold up clearance.

Work backwards from your travel date and add a buffer. Fixed waiting periods mean a single slipped appointment can push the whole move back by weeks.

Check the airline’s crate size and labelling rules early. An undersized or wrongly labelled crate is a frequent reason pets are refused at check-in.

Ask about temperature embargoes for your travel window. Summer and winter cargo restrictions can rule out certain routes and dates entirely.

FAQ

Common questions about this route

India is classified as a Schedule III country by Singapore AVS NParks. All cats and dogs from Schedule III countries must complete 30 days at the Singapore Animal Quarantine Centre (AQC) on arrival. This is set by Singapore’s biosecurity regulations and cannot be reduced. Source: AVS NParks, avs.nparks.gov.sg.
A rabies antibody titre test showing a minimum result of 0.5 IU/ml is required. The blood draw must be taken at least 28 days after the rabies vaccination. The test result is valid for between 90 days and 12 months before Singapore entry. If the test fails the minimum threshold, your pet must be revaccinated and retested.
Allow 20 to 28 weeks from starting preparations to your travel date. The titre test waiting period, AVS licence processing, and AQCS NOC are the main time drivers. Starting the microchip and vaccination process as early as possible is the only way to keep to your schedule.
Singapore Airlines is the most established option for this route, offering cargo and potentially cabin services from multiple Indian cities to Singapore Changi. Air India and Vistara also offer cargo options. Confirm current pet acceptance with any carrier before booking, as policies change.
Singapore is sometimes used as an intermediate stop for pets moving from India to Australia, but factor in the 30-day quarantine in Singapore when planning. Australia has its own separate import requirements. The Singapore quarantine adds significantly to the total timeline, so assess whether a direct India-Australia routing may be faster depending on your circumstances.
