Each step must be completed in a precise sequence. Start early. We manage every stage and deadline.

Confirm an ISO microchip is fitted before any rabies vaccination is given (or repeat the vaccination if the order was wrong).
Responsible: Your Guatemalan vet
4-5 months before travel.

Requirements

What your pet needs to enter United Kingdom

Every item below must be in place before your pet can travel. We manage and verify each one.

Microchip
ISO 11784/11785 microchip required, and it must be implanted before the rabies vaccination is given, or the vaccination has to be repeated. Source: GOV.UK, Bring your pet dog, cat or ferret to Great Britain, checked 13 July 2026.
Rabies vaccination
Current rabies vaccination required, given after the microchip. Source: GOV.UK, checked 13 July 2026.
Rabies titre test
Required. Guatemala does not appear on Great Britain's list of Part 1 (listed, low-rabies-risk) countries, so it falls under the unlisted pathway. A rabies antibody blood sample must be drawn at least 30 days after the primary vaccination and sent to an APHA-approved laboratory, with a result of at least 0.5 IU/ml. Travel cannot happen until a full 3 calendar months have passed from the date of that successful blood sample. Source: GOV.UK, Which pet travel document you need, and GOV.UK, Where you're travelling from, checked 13 July 2026.
Quarantine
No routine quarantine for a pet that completes the process correctly. Non-compliant animals (missing titre test, incomplete paperwork, or an unapproved route) can be held for up to 4 months at the owner's expense. Source: GOV.UK, checked 13 July 2026.
Import permit
No APHA import licence is needed for a normal non-commercial move of up to 5 pets travelling with, or within 5 days of, their owner. A pet flown as unaccompanied manifest cargo must clear customs at Heathrow or Gatwick and the consignment needs pre-notifying on IPAFFS (Import of Products, Animals, Food and Feed System) at least one working day ahead of arrival. Source: GOV.UK, Import of products, animals, food and feed system (IPAFFS), checked 13 July 2026.
Health certificate
Great Britain Pet Health Certificate, issued by an official veterinarian in Guatemala no more than 10 days before travel. It records the microchip, vaccination, titre test result and, for dogs, the tapeworm treatment. Source: GOV.UK, checked 13 July 2026.
Export permit (Guatemala)
No separate export permit for a single pet. The International Zoosanitary Export Certificate (Certificado Zoosanitario Internacional de Exportacion) is the operative document for travel outside Central America, obtained through MAGA's export window (VUPE, Viceministerio de Sanidad Agropecuaria y Regulaciones). Fee: USD 6.25. Source: MAGA/VISAR, VUPE export services, and La Hora, 17 October 2024.

Critical points to read before you book

Guatemala is not on Great Britain's Part 1 listed country list, so the titre test and 3-month wait are mandatory, not optional. Skipping this step is the single most common reason pets get held at the border. Source: GOV.UK, checked 13 July 2026.

There is no direct passenger or cargo route between Guatemala City and London. Avianca's own policy rules out carrying pets into the UK at all outside declared cargo, so do not assume a standard PetSafe-style booking will work; you need Avianca Cargo, or a routing through Miami or Houston on American or United with a UK interline handover.

Ground-temperature embargoes (commonly 85F/29C on US carriers) apply at every airport on the route, not just the origin. A Miami or Houston transit in summer can bump a booking even if Guatemala City itself is well within range.

Airlines

Carriers approved for this route

Not all airlines accept live animals on this route. We know which carriers to use and how to book.

AVIANCA
Avianca
Avianca's own pet travel policy states that, because of Defra and UK Animal Reception Centre restrictions, it does not carry pets as accompanied or PetSafe-style baggage into the UK at all, on any route. A pet can still move as declared cargo through Avianca Cargo, booked and handled separately from any passenger ticket. Confirm current acceptance and routing directly with Avianca Cargo before relying on this option. Source: avianca.com, pet transport policy, checked 13 July 2026.
Cargo Only
AMERICAN
American Airlines
American Airlines Cargo (PetEmbark) serves Guatemala City (GUA) with a connection through Miami (MIA), where onward cargo can be arranged to a UK gateway. American applies a ground-temperature embargo of 85F (29C) at any airport on the routing, which is a real constraint on the Miami leg in warmer months even when Guatemala City, at around 1,500m elevation, stays within range. Source: aacargo.com, checked 13 July 2026.
Cargo Only
UNITED
United Airlines
United connects Guatemala City to Houston (IAH), with onward options to the UK via partner or interline cargo. Houston in summer carries the same kind of heat-embargo risk as Miami. Confirm live animal acceptance and the UK-bound leg with United Cargo or an agent before booking.
Cargo Only
BRITISH
British Airways
British Airways does not fly Guatemala City directly and has no single-carrier routing from GUA. BA World Cargo can handle the final leg into Heathrow once the animal reaches a US or European gateway on another carrier. A specialist agent is the practical way to coordinate the handover.

United Kingdom entry requirements at a glance

The requirements for United Kingdom are thorough rather than difficult. Work through them methodically and confirm anything you are unsure about with the official source.

Getting the details right

Ask about temperature embargoes for your travel window. Summer and winter cargo restrictions can rule out certain routes and dates entirely.

Book the titre test laboratory well ahead. Approved labs have limited slots and the sample takes time to process, so this is not something to leave to the final weeks.

Microchip before you vaccinate. The microchip must be in place before the rabies shot for the vaccination to count, and getting this order wrong is the most common and costly mistake.

Use an official, government-authorised vet for the health certificate, not just your usual clinic. Many destinations only accept certificates signed by an approved vet.
