Requirements

United Arab Emirates entry requirements

Every item below must be in place before your pet can enter. We verify and track each one.

Microchip
ISO 11784/11785 microchip required, implanted before the rabies vaccination.
Rabies vaccination
Current rabies vaccination required. If this is a first vaccination, or there is a gap in your pet's vaccination history, 21 days must pass before the titre blood draw. A valid booster does not need this wait. Source: MOCCAE guidance compiled 2026.
Rabies titre test
Unclear for Iceland specifically, and this matters. MOCCAE exempts roughly 50 named low-risk countries from the titre test. Some pet transport operator sites place Iceland on that list; others do not mention it at all, and MOCCAE's own portal blocks automated verification, so neither claim is fully checkable. Plan on the cautious path: FAVN or RNATT titre test at an accredited laboratory, 0.5 IU/ml minimum, blood drawn at least 30 days after vaccination, then a 90-day wait counted from the draw date, not the result date. This is the standard MOCCAE route for any country not confirmed on the low-risk list. Call MOCCAE or an IPATA-registered agent to check Iceland's current status before you plan around a shorter timeline.
Quarantine
No routine quarantine for a compliant pet. Penalty quarantine, at owner expense, applies only where paperwork is wrong or incomplete.
Import permit
Required from the municipality of the destination emirate, Dubai Municipality or Abu Dhabi's ADAFSA. AED 200-300, valid 30 days from issue. Processing typically 3-7 business days, but allow two weeks. Source: PetTransportGlobal UAE country guide, compiled from MOCCAE and municipality guidance, 2026.
Health certificate
Government-endorsed veterinary health certificate issued within 10 days of travel, confirming microchip number, rabies vaccination status and freedom from infectious disease.
Leaving Iceland

Export requirements

Export permit
No separate export permit. MAST's endorsement of the health certificate is the export step, the same as any Icelandic departure.
Health certificate
Not the EU-standard AHC used for Iceland-to-EU routes. MOCCAE publishes its own model veterinary health certificate for exporting cats and dogs to the UAE, and this is the form your MAST-accredited vet must complete, then have endorsed by MAST. Valid 10 days from endorsement to UAE entry. Source: MAST, mast.is; MOCCAE model certificate hosted at dkc.ae, 2026.
Costs

What this route typically costs

Rabies vaccination and vet check: ISK 15,000-30,000 (roughly EUR 100-200)
MAST certificate endorsement fee: around ISK 6,600 (roughly EUR 44)
Titre test at an accredited laboratory, if required: EUR 150-300
IATA-compliant travel crate: EUR 60-250
Cargo fees, Keflavik to Dubai or Abu Dhabi via a European hub, two separate cargo legs: EUR 900-1,800
UAE import permit: AED 200-300 (roughly EUR 50-75)
Agent fees, given the two-carrier routing: EUR 300-600
Total typical range: EUR 1,700-3,300, before any titre test costs if the 90-day path applies

Critical points

Whether Iceland sits on MOCCAE's low-risk list is genuinely unclear from public sources, and MOCCAE's own portal cannot be checked automatically. Confirm directly with MOCCAE or an IPATA agent rather than assuming either answer.

There is no direct flight from Keflavik to Dubai or Abu Dhabi. Every routing involves a carrier switch between Icelandair Cargo and a Gulf carrier at a European hub, which needs its own coordination and separate booking, not a single through-ticket.

Icelandair stopped accepting pets as checked baggage on 1 November 2024. It now moves through Icelandair Cargo as manifested freight.

Summer temperature embargoes at Dubai and Abu Dhabi airports, roughly June to September, can suspend cargo acceptance entirely. Avoid booking within that window if you can.

The UAE import permit is valid for only 30 days from issue. Apply too early and it expires before you travel.

How United Arab Emirates handles incoming pets

United Arab Emirates has a detailed set of import rules, and each one needs to be satisfied before travel. None of it is unusual, but the order and timing are what catch people out.

What experienced owners do

Book the titre test laboratory well ahead. Approved labs have limited slots and the sample takes time to process, so this is not something to leave to the final weeks.

Microchip before you vaccinate. The microchip must be in place before the rabies shot for the vaccination to count, and getting this order wrong is the most common and costly mistake.

Use an official, government-authorised vet for the health certificate, not just your usual clinic. Many destinations only accept certificates signed by an approved vet.

If this is your first international move, consider an IPATA-registered pet transport agent. They handle the documents, airline booking and crate sizing, and can troubleshoot problems.
