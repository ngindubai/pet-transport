Step by step

The Tunisia to United Kingdom pet import process

Each step must be completed in a precise sequence. Start early. We manage every stage and deadline.

1
Implant ISO 11784/11785 microchip
Responsible: Vet in Tunisia
First. Must happen before any rabies vaccination counts toward the sequence.
2
Rabies vaccination
Responsible: Vet in Tunisia
After the microchip is scanned and confirmed. Starts the clock on the 30-day wait to the titre test blood draw.
3
Blood drawn for FAVN or ELISA titre test, sent to an APHA-approved laboratory
Responsible: Vet and approved laboratory
At least 30 days after vaccination. Results typically take 2-4 weeks once the lab receives the sample.
4
3-calendar-month wait from the blood draw date
Responsible: Owner
Fixed. Counted from the draw, not from the result letter.
5
DGSV-endorsed Tunisian export health certificate
Responsible: Official vet + DGSV
Close to travel, once the wait has passed. Allow 2-3 working days for DGSV endorsement.
6
Great Britain pet health certificate completed by an official vet
Responsible: Official veterinarian in Tunisia
Within 10 days of arrival in Great Britain.
7
Tapeworm treatment for dogs (praziquantel)
Responsible: Vet in Tunisia
24 to 120 hours before scheduled GB arrival.
8
Travel day, TUN to LHR (direct on Tunisair, or via CDG on Air France)
Responsible: Owner + airline cargo or check-in desk
After every wait above has been satisfied and the health certificate is still within its 10-day window.
Requirements

What your pet needs to enter United Kingdom

Every item below must be in place before your pet can travel. We manage and verify each one.

Microchip
Required. ISO 11784/11785 standard, implanted before the rabies vaccination. A non-compliant chip read on arrival invalidates the whole sequence that follows it.
Rabies vaccination
Required. Tunisia does not appear on Great Britain's Part 1 or Part 2 listed-country tables, so it is treated as unlisted. The full unlisted-country sequence applies, not the 21-day listed-country wait.
Rabies titre test
Required. FAVN or ELISA rabies antibody test at an APHA-approved laboratory. Blood drawn at least 30 days after vaccination, minimum result 0.5 IU/ml, then a fixed 3-calendar-month wait from the blood draw date before entry.
Quarantine
No routine quarantine for a pet arriving with complete, correct documentation. Incomplete paperwork can mean quarantine at the owner's cost or the animal being refused entry.
Import permit
Not required.
Health certificate
Great Britain pet health certificate, completed by an official veterinarian in Tunisia. Valid for 10 days from the date of issue to the date of entry.
Export permit (Tunisia)
No general export permit for an accompanied personal pet. An export health certificate is the working document.
Costs

What this route typically costs

FAVN or ELISA titre test at an APHA-approved laboratory: GBP 100-250, plus international courier costs for the blood sample
DGSV export health certificate and endorsement: TND 100-250 (approximately GBP 25-65)
GB pet health certificate and veterinary examination: TND 250-500 (approximately GBP 65-130)
Tunisair or Air France cargo/checked baggage fee, TUN-LHR: GBP 400-1,200 depending on crate size and routing
IATA-compliant travel crate: GBP 80-350
Pet transport agent fee (recommended for the titre test logistics): GBP 500-1,000
Total typical range: GBP 1,200-3,500, over a minimum four-month preparation window

Critical points to read before you book

Tunisia is unlisted for Great Britain pet travel. The 3-calendar-month wait from the blood draw is a hard floor. There is no fast-track version of this route, whatever an airline or agent promises.

A failed titre test result (below 0.5 IU/ml) means revaccination and a fresh 30-day wait before the blood can be redrawn. Build in slack for a possible retest, especially on a first attempt.

Tunisair does not accept Pit Bull, Rottweiler or Tosa Inu type dogs in any cabin, hold or cargo class. Confirm your dog's type against the airline's list before booking, not after.

Airlines

Carriers approved for this route

Not all airlines accept live animals on this route. We know which carriers to use and how to book.

TUNISAIR
Tunisair
Operates the only nonstop service on this route, Tunis-Carthage (TUN) to London Heathrow (LHR), around three flights a week and roughly 3 hours 5 minutes flying time. Pets under 8kg including carrier can travel in the cabin; heavier dogs travel as checked baggage up to 32kg combined weight, or as manifested cargo above that. Pit Bull, Rottweiler and Tosa Inu type dogs are not accepted in any class. Reservations must be made in advance through the Tunisair special services desk, not the general booking system.
Cabin and Cargo
AIR FRANCE
Air France
A one-stop alternative via Paris CDG when a Tunisair booking will not line up with the compliance dates. Pets up to 8kg travel in the cabin; up to 75kg combined weight go in the hold; anything heavier moves through Air France KLM Martinair Cargo. Brachycephalic breeds such as pugs, bulldogs and Persian cats are not accepted in the hold on medical grounds.
Cabin and Cargo

United Kingdom entry requirements at a glance

The requirements for United Kingdom are thorough rather than difficult. Work through them methodically and confirm anything you are unsure about with the official source.

Getting the details right

Microchip before you vaccinate. The microchip must be in place before the rabies shot for the vaccination to count, and getting this order wrong is the most common and costly mistake.

Use an official, government-authorised vet for the health certificate, not just your usual clinic. Many destinations only accept certificates signed by an approved vet.

If this is your first international move, consider an IPATA-registered pet transport agent. They handle the documents, airline booking and crate sizing, and can troubleshoot problems.

Get your pet used to the travel crate weeks in advance. A calm, crate-familiar animal travels far better than one meeting the crate for the first time at the airport.
