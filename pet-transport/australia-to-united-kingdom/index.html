Pet Transport Australia to UK: Your 2026 Guide

Relocating a pet from Australia to United Kingdom follows a clear path once you understand what United Kingdom asks for. Set aside roughly 8-12 weeks for this route and keep a close eye on the sequencing. Compliant pets are not held in quarantine on arrival. …

In short: Preparing to move a pet from Australia to United Kingdom typically takes around 8-12 weeks. There is no routine quarantine on arrival for compliant pets. A rabies titre test is not required.

Critical points

Tapeworm treatment for dogs has a strict 24 to 120 hour window before arrival in Great Britain. Too early or too late means non-compliant documentation and potential penalty quarantine.

Finding an Official Veterinarian (OV) in Australia who is familiar with the DEFRA AHC format for Great Britain takes time. Ordinary vets cannot issue this certificate. Search APHA's contacts for Australia-based OVs or use a specialist agent.

The AHC must be issued within 10 days of travel. An examination on day 11 or earlier is non-compliant.

Microchip must pre-date the rabies vaccination. Australian vets generally sequence this correctly but always verify before starting the AHC process.

Great Britain bans Pit Bull Terrier types, Japanese Tosa, Dogo Argentino and Fila Brasileiro. These breeds cannot be imported.

United Kingdom import rules, explained

United Kingdom asks for several pieces of paperwork, and they all need to line up. A checklist and a clear timeline are your best tools here.

Practical tips for this move

Get your pet used to the travel crate weeks in advance. A calm, crate-familiar animal travels far better than one meeting the crate for the first time at the airport.

Confirm live animal acceptance on every flight segment before booking. Not all aircraft or connections take pets, and cargo terms can differ leg to leg.

Keep every original document together and take copies. Border officials want originals, and a missing signature or date can hold up clearance.

Work backwards from your travel date and add a buffer. Fixed waiting periods mean a single slipped appointment can push the whole move back by weeks.

Rabies vaccination given in Australia by a licensed vet is accepted for UK entry, provided the microchip was implanted before the vaccination and at least 21 days have passed since the first vaccination in the primary course. Australia is a listed country for UK purposes, so there is no titre test requirement. The Official Veterinarian issuing your AHC will review and include the vaccination record.
Australia does not issue a standard EU-format pet passport. What you will have is the vaccination and microchip records in whatever document your Australian vet uses, plus the AHC issued for the journey. The AHC is the primary entry document for Great Britain. There is no format called a pet passport in the Australian system; the AHC replaces that concept for this route.
The examination must fall within 10 days of your scheduled arrival time in Great Britain. Most people schedule the OV appointment 5-8 days before departure, which keeps the certificate well within the 10-day window while allowing time for any administrative steps. Book the OV appointment 2-3 weeks in advance; they can be difficult to schedule at short notice.
Yes. Great Britain has approved entry routes that include ferry ports as well as airports. Heathrow, Gatwick, Manchester and Edinburgh are approved airports. Several ferry ports and the Eurotunnel terminal are also approved. However, arriving from Australia by sea is not realistic for the main journey. You would fly to a European hub and then consider crossing by sea, but that adds EU documentation complexity. The direct flight to Heathrow is the standard route.
Agents for Australia to UK pet moves typically charge AUD 800 to AUD 2,500 for the Australian side of the process. The main value they provide is OV coordination, AHC preparation, airline cargo booking, and ensuring tapeworm timing is correct. The route is manageable independently if you are prepared to research OV contacts and work precisely on timing. The cost saving from going alone is real but so is the risk of a timing error on the tapeworm treatment, which is the most common compliance issue on this route.
