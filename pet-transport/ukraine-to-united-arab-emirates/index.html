Checklist

What your pet needs

Every item below must be completed and verified before your pet can travel. Expand each category for the detail.

Microchip
ISO 11784/11785 microchip, implanted before the rabies vaccination. Source: MOCCAE, moccae.gov.ae/en/services/import-permit-pets, 2026.
Rabies vaccination
Minimum age at first vaccination is 12 weeks generally, but because Ukraine sits outside MOCCAE's approved low-risk list, the minimum age for a pet entering from Ukraine rises to 15 weeks. A first vaccination, or any break in the vaccination record, means a mandatory 21-day wait before the titre blood draw can be taken.
Rabies titre test
A rabies antibody titration (FAVN) test is mandatory for pets travelling from Ukraine, since it falls outside MOCCAE's roughly 50-country low-risk list. The result must read at least 0.5 IU/ml, from a laboratory recognised by the exporting country's competent authority. The certificate stays valid 365 days provided the vaccination remains current and no booster is given in the meantime.
Quarantine
No routine quarantine for a pet that arrives with a compliant import permit, health certificate and titre result. A pet without full paperwork is held at an approved facility at the owner's cost until it resolves.
Import permit
Applied for online through the MOCCAE e-service before travel, valid 90 days from issue, costing AED 200. Limited to two companion animals per person, per year. A release fee of AED 500 per dog or AED 250 per cat is charged on arrival.
Health certificate
MOCCAE's Model Veterinary Health Certificate (see origin side), issued in MOCCAE's required format and matched precisely to the pet's microchip number.
Export permit
No standalone export permit for a personal pet. The owner obtains Veterinary Certificate Form No. 1 (VET-1) from a state veterinary clinic in Ukraine, then exchanges it for an international veterinary certificate at the border crossing into Poland or Romania, since no functioning border-crossing vet post currently operates at any Ukrainian airport.
Veterinary inspection
Pre-export clinical exam by a licensed Ukrainian vet, plus internal and external parasite treatment recorded on the certificate within 14 days of the flight, since that is MOCCAE's requirement rather than a Ukrainian one.
Government endorsement
The international certificate is countersigned by an official state veterinarian once the pet has crossed the Polish or Romanian border.
Health certificate
MOCCAE's own Model Veterinary Health Certificate for exporting cats and dogs to the UAE must be completed, in the exact format MOCCAE specifies, by an accredited vet. Because Ukraine has no operating export vet post at an airport, most owners get this finished once across the border, in Poland or Romania.

Airlines

Carriers on this route

Not all airlines accept live animals on this route. Confirm the operating carrier's current policy for every flight segment.

LOT Polish Airlines
No airline flies out of Ukraine, so the pet travels overland into Poland first. LOT itself has no nonstop Warsaw-Dubai service, but it connects passengers and cargo through its European network to a partner carrier for the Gulf leg, most commonly Emirates at Warsaw Chopin.
Cargo Only
Emirates SkyCargo
Emirates runs EK179, a daily Boeing 777-300ER service from Dubai to Warsaw Chopin, roughly six hours in the air. Run that routing in reverse for a Ukraine departure: overland from Ukraine to Warsaw, then EK179 back to Dubai as manifested cargo.
Cargo Only
Turkish Airlines Cargo
A Ukraine-to-Romania overland crossing connects into Turkish Airlines' Istanbul hub, which runs several daily widebody rotations to Dubai. Worth considering for owners in southern or western Ukraine closer to the Romanian border than to Poland.
Cargo Only
Costs

What this route typically costs

Ukrainian vet fees for microchip, vaccination and the FAVN titre draw: roughly 3,000-6,000 UAH (about USD 70-140)
Overland transport from Ukraine to Warsaw or Bucharest: USD 300-900
MOCCAE Model Veterinary Health Certificate, completed at a border-area clinic: USD 80-150
MOCCAE import permit: AED 200 (about USD 55)
IATA-compliant travel crate: USD 100-350
Warsaw-Dubai or Istanbul-Dubai cargo fare: USD 800-2,000 depending on crate size
MOCCAE release fee on arrival: AED 500 for a dog, AED 250 for a cat

Critical points

Ukrainian civilian airspace has been closed since 24 February 2022 and remains closed in mid-2026, despite a Cabinet-approved Civil Aviation Development Strategy and a new coordination centre announced in July 2026. No airline has a confirmed restart date, so every pet on this route needs the overland leg into Poland or Romania before any flight is possible.

Ukraine sits outside MOCCAE's low-risk country list, which pushes the minimum age to 15 weeks and makes the FAVN titre test mandatory, not optional as it would be for a pet from a listed country.

The titre sample must go to a laboratory the exporting country's authority recognises. Ukrainian domestic labs are not always accepted for this purpose, which is one reason many owners submit the sample once they have crossed into Poland or Romania rather than before leaving Ukraine.

The MOCCAE import permit is valid for 90 days only. Given how unpredictable the overland leg can be, book the permit application to leave a comfortable margin rather than cutting it close to the flight date.

What United Arab Emirates requires

Getting a pet into United Arab Emirates means meeting a demanding set of rules with no shortcuts. Start from the earliest deadline and work forward from there.

Avoiding the common mistakes

Microchip before you vaccinate. The microchip must be in place before the rabies shot for the vaccination to count, and getting this order wrong is the most common and costly mistake.

Use an official, government-authorised vet for the health certificate, not just your usual clinic. Many destinations only accept certificates signed by an approved vet.

If this is your first international move, consider an IPATA-registered pet transport agent. They handle the documents, airline booking and crate sizing, and can troubleshoot problems.

Get your pet used to the travel crate weeks in advance. A calm, crate-familiar animal travels far better than one meeting the crate for the first time at the airport.
