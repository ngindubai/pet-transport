Requirements

What your pet needs to enter Finland

Every item below must be in place before your pet can travel. We manage and verify each one.

Microchip
Required. ISO 11784/11785 standard. Must precede the first rabies vaccination.
Rabies vaccination
Required. Current vaccination. Primary vaccination: minimum 21 days before entry.
Rabies titre test
Required. Finland is EU. Azerbaijan is EU unlisted. Under EU Regulation 2020/692, FAVN rabies antibody titre test at an EU-approved lab required. Blood drawn at least 30 days after primary vaccination. Minimum 0.5 IU/ml. 90-day wait from blood draw date. Source: Ruokavirasto (Finnish Food Authority), ruokavirasto.fi/en.
Quarantine
No routine quarantine for compliant arrivals. Conditional quarantine possible for borderline cases.
Import permit
Not required for companion pets at approved entry points.
Health certificate
EU-format non-commercial health certificate for unlisted third countries. SAQV-endorsed. Valid for 10 days. Entry only via Helsinki Vantaa Airport (HEL) or Vaalimaa land border.
Export permit (Azerbaijan)
No formal export permit required for companion pets leaving Azerbaijan.

Critical points to read before you book

All pet cargo from non-EU origins entering Finland must clear at Helsinki Vantaa Airport (HEL). No other Finnish airport handles this clearance. Confirm in writing with your carrier that HEL is the final destination and that the border inspection post there will process your pet's entry.

Azerbaijan is EU unlisted. The SAQV's veterinary system is respected internationally but does not grant any exemption from EU unlisted country import requirements. The FAVN titre test and 90-day wait apply in full.

Ruokavirasto's country-specific online tool at ruokavirasto.fi/en allows you to look up current requirements for Azerbaijan before starting your preparation. Always verify against official sources rather than third-party summaries.

The 90-day wait is from the blood draw date, not the results date. Processing time at the lab (2-4 weeks) is part of the wait period, not added to it.

How Finland handles incoming pets

Finland asks for several pieces of paperwork, and they all need to line up. A checklist and a clear timeline are your best tools here.

What experienced owners do

Book the titre test laboratory well ahead. Approved labs have limited slots and the sample takes time to process, so this is not something to leave to the final weeks.

Microchip before you vaccinate. The microchip must be in place before the rabies shot for the vaccination to count, and getting this order wrong is the most common and costly mistake.

Use an official, government-authorised vet for the health certificate, not just your usual clinic. Many destinations only accept certificates signed by an approved vet.

If this is your first international move, consider an IPATA-registered pet transport agent. They handle the documents, airline booking and crate sizing, and can troubleshoot problems.
