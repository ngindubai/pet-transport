Pet Transport Dominican Republic to Hungary: 2026 Guide

If you are relocating a pet from Dominican Republic to Hungary, the process is manageable once you know the sequence. Allow about 20-24 weeks; the requirements are detailed but predictable once mapped out. Compliant pets are not held in quarantine on arrival. …

In short: Preparing to move a pet from Dominican Republic to Hungary typically takes around 20-24 weeks. There is no routine quarantine on arrival for compliant pets. A rabies titre test is required, which adds a fixed waiting period, so start early.

Critical points

The Dominican Republic is an EU unlisted country. A titre test and 90-day wait are legal requirements. There is no shortcut.

The 90-day wait starts from the DATE THE LABORATORY CONFIRMS the passing result, not the blood draw date.

Hungarian import oversight is handled by Nebih (nebih.gov.hu). Border veterinary inspectors at Budapest Ferenc Liszt International Airport check documentation in detail. Ensure the original laboratory report, vaccination record, and DIGEGA-endorsed health certificate all travel with your pet.

How Hungary handles incoming pets

Hungary asks for several pieces of paperwork, and they all need to line up. A checklist and a clear timeline are your best tools here.

What experienced owners do

Get your pet used to the travel crate weeks in advance. A calm, crate-familiar animal travels far better than one meeting the crate for the first time at the airport.

Confirm live animal acceptance on every flight segment before booking. Not all aircraft or connections take pets, and cargo terms can differ leg to leg.

Keep every original document together and take copies. Border officials want originals, and a missing signature or date can hold up clearance.

Work backwards from your travel date and add a buffer. Fixed waiting periods mean a single slipped appointment can push the whole move back by weeks.

FAQ

Common questions

Hungary applies EU Regulation 2020/692, which requires a rabies antibody titre test for pets arriving from unlisted third countries. The Dominican Republic is classified as an unlisted country, with no veterinary equivalence agreement with the EU. The FAVN or ELISA titre test at an EU-approved laboratory confirms a minimum antibody level of 0.5 IU/ml. The 90-day wait from the passing result date is a fixed legal requirement before Hungarian entry.
A minimum of 20 to 24 weeks. The sequence is: microchip, then vaccination, then wait at least 30 days for the blood draw, then wait for the lab result (two to four weeks), then a mandatory 90-day wait from the successful result date. Only after those 90 days can your pet legally enter Hungary. Six months is the sensible planning horizon.
Yes. The titre test requirement under EU Regulation 2020/692 applies equally to cats and dogs arriving from unlisted countries, including the Dominican Republic. The process is the same for both species: microchip before vaccination, blood draw at least 30 days after vaccination at an EU-approved laboratory, a minimum result of 0.5 IU/ml, and a 90-day wait from the passing result date before Hungary entry.
Budapest Ferenc Liszt International Airport (BUD) is the main international entry point for pet cargo arriving in Hungary. It is served by cargo connections from Madrid, Paris CDG, and Amsterdam, all of which are used for Dominican Republic to Hungary shipments. Nebih border veterinary inspectors at BUD will check all documentation on arrival, so ensure the original laboratory report and DIGEGA-endorsed health certificate are included with your pet.
