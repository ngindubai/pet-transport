In short: Preparing to move a pet from Nicaragua to Canada typically takes around 3-6 weeks. There is no routine quarantine on arrival for compliant pets. A rabies titre test is not required.

Step by step

The Nicaragua to Canada import process

01
4-6 weeks before travel
Confirm the rabies vaccination is current and no more than 365 days old on the planned shipment date.

Responsible: Your vet in Nicaragua

02
Any time before the export certificate is completed
Fit an ISO microchip. Not legally required for a Nicaragua-origin pet, but standard practice for matching the animal to its documents.

Responsible: Your vet in Nicaragua

03
Within 2 weeks of travel
Vet exam within 2 weeks of departure, plus internal and external parasite treatment, meeting IPSA's general requirements.

Responsible: Your vet in Nicaragua

04
1-2 weeks before travel
Confirm the export certificate process with IPSA's Ventanilla Unica and obtain the endorsed health certificate, formatted to meet CFIA's identification requirements.

Responsible: You or your agent

05
3-4 weeks before travel
Book cargo space via Miami (American), Houston (United) or Panama City (Copa), noting Copa's Monday-to-Thursday cargo limit.

Responsible: You or a pet relocation agent

06
Day of arrival
Travel. Present the IPSA export certificate and rabies record to CBSA and CFIA officials on arrival at a designated Canadian airport.

Responsible: Owner, airline ground handler, CBSA/CFIA

Requirements

Canada entry requirements

Every item below must be in place before your pet can enter. We verify and track each one.

Microchip
Not legally required for a personal pet dog or cat from a country CFIA doesn't classify as high-risk for dog rabies, which covers Nicaragua. Fitting one anyway makes the identification on the health certificate airtight and is expected practice among agents working this route.
Rabies vaccination
Required for dogs and cats aged 3 months or older: a valid certificate in English or French, signed by a licensed veterinarian, identifying the animal by breed, colour, weight and any microchip number, and stating the vaccine's duration of immunity.
Rabies titre test
Not required. Nicaragua does not appear on CFIA's list of countries classed as high-risk for dog rabies, last reviewed and dated 13 March 2025. That single classification removes the titre test, the 7-month minimum age rule, and the mandatory ISO microchip mandate CFIA applies to dogs from countries that are on the list. Source: CFIA, Countries at high-risk for dog rabies, inspection.canada.ca.
Quarantine
No routine quarantine for a pet dog or cat arriving with correct documentation. CFIA's commercial dog rabies ban, in force since 28 September 2022, applies only to commercial dogs (for sale, breeding, rescue or exhibition) from high-risk countries, and Nicaragua is not on that list regardless.
Import permit
Not required for a personal, non-commercial import of 1 or 2 animals. A CFIA import permit only applies to commercial imports, which is not the pathway a relocating pet owner uses.
Health certificate
International health certificate in English or French, issued by a licensed vet, covering species, identification, and vaccination details. No CFIA pre-approval or import permit accompanies it for a standard personal pet import.
Leaving Nicaragua

Export requirements

Export permit
No dedicated pet export permit is published by IPSA, but the export health certificate must come from a veterinarian IPSA recognises for this purpose. Confirm the current process directly with IPSA's Ventanilla Unica before booking, since the pet-specific export pathway is not fully documented online. Source: IPSA (Instituto de Proteccion y Sanidad Agropecuaria), ipsa.gob.ni.
Veterinary inspection
Health exam within 2 weeks of departure, confirming the animal is clinically healthy and free of parasitic or infectious disease signs. Source: IPSA general animal quarantine requirements, ipsa.gob.ni.
Government endorsement
IPSA does not run a separate endorsement step beyond the vet's signature on the export certificate.
Health certificate
Veterinary health certificate recording the rabies vaccination date, no more than 365 days old at shipment, and confirmation of internal and external parasite treatment with an approved product. Written in a form that meets CFIA's identification requirements: species, breed, colour or markings, and microchip number if fitted. Source: IPSA general requirements, ipsa.gob.ni.

Critical points

Nicaragua's absence from CFIA's high-risk list is confirmed as of the list's 13 March 2025 review date, but CFIA reviews and updates it regularly. Check the current list again close to your travel date rather than relying on this page alone.

There is no non-stop flight from Managua to Toronto or Montreal. Every routing adds at least one connection, most often Miami, Houston or Panama City, each needing its own confirmed cargo booking.

Copa Airlines only accepts pets as cargo Monday to Thursday. If Panama City is your planned routing, a weekend arrival won't work for the animal even if your own ticket is flexible.

The rules for bringing a pet into Canada

Canada has a modest set of rules compared with stricter destinations. Get the essentials in place and confirm the finer points below.

How to keep this move on track

If this is your first international move, consider an IPATA-registered pet transport agent. They handle the documents, airline booking and crate sizing, and can troubleshoot problems.

Get your pet used to the travel crate weeks in advance. A calm, crate-familiar animal travels far better than one meeting the crate for the first time at the airport.

Confirm live animal acceptance on every flight segment before booking. Not all aircraft or connections take pets, and cargo terms can differ leg to leg.

Keep every original document together and take copies. Border officials want originals, and a missing signature or date can hold up clearance.
