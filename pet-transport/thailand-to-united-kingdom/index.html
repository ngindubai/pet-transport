Requirements

United Kingdom entry requirements

Every item below must be in place before your pet can enter. We verify and track each one.

Microchip
ISO 11784/11785 microchip required. Must be implanted before the rabies vaccination.
Rabies vaccination
Current valid rabies vaccination with at least 21 days elapsed since first vaccination.
Rabies titre test
Thailand is not on the GB listed country schedule. Titre test mandatory. Blood drawn at least 30 days after vaccination. Result at least 0.5 IU/ml. Three calendar months must pass after the blood draw date before GB entry.
Quarantine
No routine quarantine with correct documentation. Penalty quarantine up to 4 months if documentation is wrong.
Import permit
APHA Import Licence required. Free. Allow 2-4 weeks processing time.
Health certificate
GB Pet Health Certificate from a DLD-authorised OV-equivalent vet, endorsed by the DLD. Valid 10 days from examination to GB arrival.
Leaving Thailand

Export requirements

Export permit
No formal export permit required, but the DLD (Department of Livestock Development) health certificate is mandatory for all pet exports from Thailand.
Health certificate
Official health certificate from a DLD-authorised vet, endorsed by the DLD as Thailand's competent authority. Must comply with GB format. Issued within 10 days of GB arrival.

Critical points

The 90-day wait from the titre test blood draw is non-negotiable. Start planning 6 months before your target UK entry date.

Thailand is not GB-listed. No workaround or waiver exists.

The DLD health certificate has a 10-day validity window (examination to GB arrival). Time the exam carefully around your flight booking.

What United Kingdom requires

Entry to United Kingdom is tightly controlled, and officials verify each requirement in turn. Treat it as a sequence where every step gates the next.

Avoiding the common mistakes

Work backwards from your travel date and add a buffer. Fixed waiting periods mean a single slipped appointment can push the whole move back by weeks.

Check the airline’s crate size and labelling rules early. An undersized or wrongly labelled crate is a frequent reason pets are refused at check-in.

Ask about temperature embargoes for your travel window. Summer and winter cargo restrictions can rule out certain routes and dates entirely.

Book the titre test laboratory well ahead. Approved labs have limited slots and the sample takes time to process, so this is not something to leave to the final weeks.

No routine quarantine, provided all documentation is correct and complete. If documentation is wrong or the entry does not follow APHA requirements, penalty quarantine of up to 4 months applies at the owner’s expense.
The DLD (Department of Livestock Development) health certificate is the official Thai export document for pets. It is issued by a DLD-authorised vet and endorsed by the DLD office. It must be in GB-compatible format, cover the full vaccination and titre test history, and be issued within 10 days of GB arrival.
The UK’s Dangerous Dogs Act restricts Pit Bull Terriers, Japanese Tosas, Dogo Argentinos, and Fila Brasileiros. These breeds cannot be imported. For all other breeds, the standard documentation process applies. Thailand also has its own list of prohibited dog breeds for entry into Thailand, but this is relevant only if you are moving in the opposite direction.
