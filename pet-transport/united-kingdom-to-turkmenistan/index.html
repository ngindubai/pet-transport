Requirements

Turkmenistan entry requirements

Every item below must be in place before your pet can enter. We verify and track each one.

Microchip
ISO 11784/11785 microchip required, implanted before the rabies vaccination is given.
Rabies vaccination
Required, given between 30 days and 12 months before arrival. Multi-year vaccines are not accepted for entry purposes: even a pet on a valid UK 3-year rabies vaccine schedule needs a booster within 12 months of travel, a stricter rule than most destinations on this site apply to a UK-origin pet.
Rabies titre test
Not clearly mandated in any published Turkmenistan source for pets arriving from a low rabies-risk country such as the UK. Turkmenistan publishes very little English-language guidance on this point, so confirm directly with the receiving vet or a relocation agent rather than assuming the position hasn't changed.
Quarantine
No quarantine imposed if the microchip, vaccination and certificate requirements are met. Non-compliant pets face quarantine where facilities exist, or return to the country of origin, entirely at the owner's expense.
Import permit
Required only when three or more animals travel together. An owner bringing one or two pets does not need a separate import permit.
Health certificate
The UK Export Health Certificate, examined by a government veterinarian on arrival at Ashgabat International Airport, functions as the import document. It should be issued within 10 days of travel.
Leaving United Kingdom

Export requirements

Export permit
An export application (EXA) is submitted to APHA alongside the certificate request; there is no separate standalone export permit for a companion pet.
Veterinary inspection
Clinical examination by an APHA-authorised Official Veterinarian (OV), confirming the pet is fit to travel and meets Turkmenistan's stated health conditions.
Government endorsement
Only an APHA-authorised OV can issue and sign the Export Health Certificate (EHC). Applications submitted more than 7 working days ahead are processed 7 working days before the certification date. Source: GOV.UK, 'Taking your pet dog, cat or ferret abroad: Getting an animal health certificate,' 2026.
Health certificate
Bilingual Export Health Certificate confirming microchip, rabies vaccination and general health, valid for a limited window around the travel date, typically 10 days.
Costs

What this route typically costs

APHA Official Veterinarian consultation and Export Health Certificate: GBP 200-400
Microchip and rabies vaccination, including a fresh booster if the last dose is close to 12 months old: GBP 40-100
IATA-compliant travel crate: GBP 70-250
PetAir UK or agent handling for the export booking: GBP 700-1,500, toward the higher end given how little cargo capacity Ashgabat has
Air cargo Heathrow to Ashgabat via Istanbul or Dubai: GBP 900-2,300 depending on crate size and season
Total typical range: GBP 1,900-4,500, excluding the owner's own visa costs and travel

Critical points

Turkmenistan's own visa system is the real bottleneck on this route, not the pet paperwork. Tourist entry still requires a Letter of Invitation issued by a licensed Turkmenistan travel agency, and the e-visa system legislated in April 2025 had no confirmed launch date as of February 2026. In practice, this route is used almost entirely by people relocating for work, oil and gas contracts, or diplomatic postings, not casual pet owners planning a move on their own timeline.

Turkmenistan does not accept a multi-year rabies vaccine as current. Even a pet with a valid UK 3-year vaccination needs a booster inside the 12 months before travel, an easy step to miss if you're working from the UK certificate's own expiry date instead of Turkmenistan's rule.

Ashgabat's scheduled international cargo capacity is thin. Book 6-8 weeks ahead and keep both an Istanbul and a Dubai routing in mind in case one falls through.

Confirm the rabies titre test position directly with the receiving vet or a relocation agent before travel. Published guidance for this route is limited, and it isn't safe to assume last year's position still holds.

What Turkmenistan requires

Entry to Turkmenistan is tightly controlled, and officials verify each requirement in turn. Treat it as a sequence where every step gates the next.

Avoiding the common mistakes

Ask about temperature embargoes for your travel window. Summer and winter cargo restrictions can rule out certain routes and dates entirely.

Book the titre test laboratory well ahead. Approved labs have limited slots and the sample takes time to process, so this is not something to leave to the final weeks.

Microchip before you vaccinate. The microchip must be in place before the rabies shot for the vaccination to count, and getting this order wrong is the most common and costly mistake.

Use an official, government-authorised vet for the health certificate, not just your usual clinic. Many destinations only accept certificates signed by an approved vet.
