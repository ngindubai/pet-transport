Requirements

Kyrgyzstan entry requirements

Every item below must be in place before your pet can enter. We verify and track each one.

Microchip
Not required by Kyrgyzstan, though strongly recommended: a non-encrypted, ISO 11784/11785-compliant 15-digit microchip, with the number recorded on the health certificate if fitted.
Rabies vaccination
Required. Current rabies vaccination given no sooner than 21 days and no more than 11 months before entry. A multi-year vaccine needs the vet's confirmation of ongoing validity noted on the certificate.
Rabies titre test
Not required. Kyrgyzstan does not ask for a rabies antibody titre test on a pet arriving from any country. This sits in sharp contrast to the CDC's own rules for the reverse direction: Kyrgyzstan is on the CDC's high-risk list for dog rabies, so a Kyrgyzstan-origin dog entering the US needs a titre test or a 28-day quarantine. None of that machinery applies going the other way.
Quarantine
No quarantine for pets arriving with complete, compliant documentation.
Import permit
Not required for an owner travelling with up to five dogs and/or cats, per APHIS export guidance for this specific corridor. Commercial or unaccompanied shipments, or larger numbers of animals, need additional permits.
Health certificate
Government-endorsed veterinary health certificate issued within 5 days of travel. US owners use a shared regional certificate form (the same one used for Armenia and Kazakhstan), submitted and endorsed through APHIS's Veterinary Export Health Certification System (VEHCS). Cats also get a clinical check for dermatophytosis (ringworm); a cat with active lesions cannot be imported.
Leaving United States

Export requirements

Export permit
No export permit required from the US side for companion animals.
Health certificate
USDA APHIS Veterinary Health Certificate (VS Form 7001 or equivalent international health certificate) completed by a USDA-accredited veterinarian and endorsed by the USDA APHIS State Veterinarian. Allow 3-5 business days for endorsement.

Critical points

Kyrgyzstan's 5-day health certificate validity window is tighter than USDA's own typical rhythm for many destinations. Confirm the flight date first, then time the vet appointment and APHIS endorsement to land inside that window.

Up to five pets per traveller can enter Kyrgyzstan without a separate import permit, per APHIS guidance for this route, more generous than several neighbouring Central Asian destinations. Above that, or for unaccompanied shipments, additional permits apply.

There is no direct US-Bishkek flight. Turkish Airlines via Istanbul is the only realistic single-carrier long-haul option, since Air Astana has no scheduled US service.

Cats need a clinical dermatophytosis check before travel. A cat with active ringworm lesions cannot be imported into Kyrgyzstan under any circumstances.

Kyrgyzstan entry requirements at a glance

Getting a pet into Kyrgyzstan is a middle of the road process. The rules are well defined, so most of the work is simply staying on schedule.

Getting the details right

Get your pet used to the travel crate weeks in advance. A calm, crate-familiar animal travels far better than one meeting the crate for the first time at the airport.

Confirm live animal acceptance on every flight segment before booking. Not all aircraft or connections take pets, and cargo terms can differ leg to leg.

Keep every original document together and take copies. Border officials want originals, and a missing signature or date can hold up clearance.

Work backwards from your travel date and add a buffer. Fixed waiting periods mean a single slipped appointment can push the whole move back by weeks.
