Requirements

Armenia entry requirements

Every item below must be in place before your pet can enter. We verify and track each one.

Microchip
ISO 11784/11785 required.
Rabies vaccination
Required. Current vaccination within manufacturer validity.
Rabies titre test
Not required. Armenia's State Service for Food Safety (SSFS) does not run an origin-risk classification system for pet imports, so no titre test applies regardless of whether the pet is coming from Singapore, the UK or elsewhere. This is a genuinely lighter requirement than Singapore's own AVS applies in the reverse direction.
Quarantine
No routine quarantine. SSFS veterinary inspection on arrival at Zvartnots International Airport (EVN).
Import permit
Import documentation from Armenia's SSFS. Confirm current requirements before travel, as Armenia has simplified its import paperwork in recent years.
Health certificate
AVS-endorsed Singapore health certificate, accepted by SSFS in English.
Leaving Singapore

Export requirements

Export permit
AVS Export Licence required, applied for through the CITES e-Licence system before departure.
Health certificate
Official health certificate completed by an AVS-accredited vet in Singapore, endorsed by AVS as the competent authority of origin, and reformatted to whatever documentation SSFS requests. Issued within 7 days of departure.

Critical points

Armenia's SSFS does not apply an origin-risk category to imports, so Singapore-origin pets face the same light process as UK-origin pets. Confirm current documentation requirements before travel since Armenia has updated its import rules in recent years.

No airline flies Singapore to Yerevan directly. Turkish Airlines via Istanbul and Emirates via Dubai are the two workable single-connection routes.

Bring original documents to Zvartnots airport. SSFS inspectors do not accept copies.

Armenia import rules, explained

Armenia keeps its requirements reasonable, though a few steps have fixed timing. Map those out first and the rest falls into place.

Practical tips for this move

Microchip before you vaccinate. The microchip must be in place before the rabies shot for the vaccination to count, and getting this order wrong is the most common and costly mistake.

Use an official, government-authorised vet for the health certificate, not just your usual clinic. Many destinations only accept certificates signed by an approved vet.

If this is your first international move, consider an IPATA-registered pet transport agent. They handle the documents, airline booking and crate sizing, and can troubleshoot problems.

Get your pet used to the travel crate weeks in advance. A calm, crate-familiar animal travels far better than one meeting the crate for the first time at the airport.
