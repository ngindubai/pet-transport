Requirements

What your pet needs to enter Spain

Every item below must be in place before your pet can travel. We manage and verify each one.

Microchip
Required. ISO 11784/11785 standard (15-digit). Must precede the first rabies vaccination.
Rabies vaccination
Required. Current vaccination. Primary vaccination: 21-day minimum before entry. Boosters within prior vaccine validity are immediately effective.
Rabies titre test
Not required for intra-EU movement. Both Latvia and Spain are EU member states.
Quarantine
Not required for compliant intra-EU pet movements.
Import permit
Not required for companion pets between EU member states.
Health certificate
EU pet passport (EU-resident owners) or AHC. April 2026: passports restricted to EU-resident owners.
Export permit (Latvia)
No export permit required for companion pets moving from Latvia to Spain.

Critical points to read before you book

Ryanair and Wizz Air dominate cheap Riga-to-Spain routes but neither accepts pets. Book with airBaltic, Lufthansa, LOT, or Iberia.

Spain requires microchipping of all dogs and registration in the Anagrafe Canina if residing in Spain. This is a post-arrival domestic obligation, separate from EU travel requirements.

April 2026: EU pet passports valid only for EU-resident owners. Latvian nationals and other EU residents use the passport. Non-EU nationals on Latvian permits: use the PVD AHC.

Summer travel: some cargo carriers apply temperature restrictions on live animal cargo during extreme heat. If flying to Spain in July or August, confirm with the cargo carrier that temperature-controlled live animal acceptance applies on your specific route.

Meeting Spain's entry requirements

The requirements for Spain are light, but not zero. A little planning goes a long way on a route like this.

Advice worth following

Use an official, government-authorised vet for the health certificate, not just your usual clinic. Many destinations only accept certificates signed by an approved vet.

If this is your first international move, consider an IPATA-registered pet transport agent. They handle the documents, airline booking and crate sizing, and can troubleshoot problems.

Get your pet used to the travel crate weeks in advance. A calm, crate-familiar animal travels far better than one meeting the crate for the first time at the airport.

Confirm live animal acceptance on every flight segment before booking. Not all aircraft or connections take pets, and cargo terms can differ leg to leg.
