Checklist

What your pet needs

Every item below must be completed and verified before your pet can travel. Expand each category for the detail.

Microchip
ISO 11784/11785 standard required. Must be implanted before the first rabies vaccination.
Rabies vaccination
Required. At least two vaccinations, with the first at least 30 days before the FAVN titre test blood draw.
Rabies titre test
FAVN (Fluorescent Antibody Virus Neutralization) test required. Minimum 0.5 IU/ml. 180-day wait period runs from the blood draw date before entry.
Quarantine
10 days at an APQA-approved facility on arrival. Owner pays all costs.
Import permit
Formal import permit required from APQA (Animal and Plant Quarantine Agency). Apply at least 30 days before travel.
Health certificate
Issued by an APHA-authorised Official Veterinarian within 14 days of travel. Must reference microchip, vaccinations, and titre test result.
Export permit
No UK export permit required for dogs and cats.
Health certificate
Health certificate issued by an APHA-authorised Official Veterinarian in the UK, meeting South Korea's APQA requirements. Must include the microchip number, full vaccination history, and FAVN titre test result.

Critical points

The 180-day wait runs from the blood draw date for the FAVN titre test. Start the titre test process at least 7-8 months before your planned move. A missed deadline means restarting the wait period.

Korean Air allows small dogs and cats in the cabin on direct London to Seoul flights. The pet plus cage must not exceed 7 kg. Book the cabin space when you book your own seat.

British Airways does not allow pets in the cabin on any international route. Pets travel via IAG Cargo.

The 10-day quarantine on arrival is mandatory and applies regardless of documentation quality. Budget for quarantine accommodation costs in South Korea.

If the titre test result is below 0.5 IU/ml, the process must restart with a new vaccination and blood draw. A failed titre test adds months to the timeline.

The rules for bringing a pet into South Korea

South Korea asks for several pieces of paperwork, and they all need to line up. A checklist and a clear timeline are your best tools here.

How to keep this move on track

Keep every original document together and take copies. Border officials want originals, and a missing signature or date can hold up clearance.

Work backwards from your travel date and add a buffer. Fixed waiting periods mean a single slipped appointment can push the whole move back by weeks.

Check the airline’s crate size and labelling rules early. An undersized or wrongly labelled crate is a frequent reason pets are refused at check-in.

Ask about temperature embargoes for your travel window. Summer and winter cargo restrictions can rule out certain routes and dates entirely.

Common questions

Before the blood draw, your pet needs at least two rabies vaccinations with the second at least 30 days before the blood draw. Add the APQA permit application time (30 days before travel) and the health certificate window (14 days before travel), and the full process typically takes 8 months from a standing start.
Yes, on Korean Air’s direct London Heathrow to Seoul Incheon flight. Small dogs and cats are accepted in the cabin provided the pet plus cage weighs no more than 7 kg and the carrier fits under the seat. Asiana Airlines also offers cabin pet travel on qualifying routes. British Airways does not allow cabin pets on any international flight.
APQA is South Korea’s Animal and Plant Quarantine Agency, the body responsible for regulating pet imports. APQA requires: an ISO microchip, a rabies vaccination course, a FAVN titre test (with 180-day wait from blood draw), an import permit (applied for at least 30 days before travel), and a health certificate from an official veterinarian. On arrival, all pets serve 10 days in an APQA quarantine facility at the owner’s expense.
APQA quarantine costs are set by the facility and typically run KRW 200,000 to KRW 600,000 (approximately GBP 120 to GBP 360) for 10 days, depending on the facility and pet size. These costs are paid by the owner directly to the quarantine facility. Budget for this in addition to cargo fees and health certificate costs.
You can complete the process independently, but a professional pet relocation agent is worth considering for this route given the complexity and the interdependence of the steps. A missed deadline in the titre test sequence adds months to your timeline. Agents typically charge GBP 300 to GBP 800 for coordination services and are familiar with APQA permit applications.
