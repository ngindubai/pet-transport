In short: Preparing to move a pet from Iceland to United Kingdom typically takes around 4-7 weeks. There is no routine quarantine on arrival for compliant pets. A rabies titre test is not required.

Requirements

United Kingdom entry requirements

Every item below must be in place before your pet can enter. We verify and track each one.

Microchip
ISO 11784/11785 microchip required, implanted before the rabies vaccination. Source: GOV.UK, 2026.
Rabies vaccination
Current rabies vaccination required, given at least 21 days before entry to Great Britain.
Rabies titre test
Not required. Iceland is a Part 1 listed country under the UK's pet travel rules, the same tier as EU member states, Norway and Switzerland. No blood test applies to entry.
Quarantine
None for a pet arriving with complete, compliant documentation. Iceland's own strict 14-day import quarantine has no equivalent on the UK side; it applies only to pets entering Iceland, not leaving it.
Import permit
No standalone import permit for an owner travelling with their own dog or cat.
Health certificate
Because Iceland is Part 1 listed, GOV.UK names three documents that will clear a pet through UK border control: a pet passport, an Animal Health Certificate, or the MAST-issued GB pet health certificate described above. Most owners use the MAST-issued certificate, since it is also the export document. Source: GOV.UK, 'Which pet travel document you need,' 2026.
Leaving Iceland

Export requirements

Export permit
No separate export permit for a companion pet. MAST's certificate endorsement is the export step, but the certificate itself is not the same form used for a pet heading into the EU.
Government endorsement
MAST endorses the certificate once a MAST-accredited vet has completed it. Booking runs through form 2.40 on MAST's online portal (umsokn.mast.is) or by emailing petimport@mast.is with the pet's microchip number and vaccination date, choosing an appointment at one of MAST's offices in Reykjavik, Selfoss, Akureyri, Saudarkrokur or Egilsstadir. Source: MAST, mast.is, 2026.
Health certificate
Iceland uses a document called the pet health certificate for the non-commercial movement to Great Britain, not the EU-standard Animal Health Certificate form used for travel into an EU member state. Valid 10 days from MAST's endorsement to the documentary check at the UK point of entry. Source: MAST, mast.is, 2026.
Costs

What this route typically costs

MAST vet consultation and GB pet health certificate: ISK 15,000-30,000 (roughly GBP 85-170)
Tapeworm treatment, dogs only: ISK 3,000-6,000 (roughly GBP 17-35)
IATA-compliant travel crate: GBP 60-250
Cargo fee, Keflavik to Heathrow: GBP 400-950 depending on crate size and season
Agent or handling fee: GBP 150-400
Total typical range: GBP 700-1,900

Critical points

Iceland's own 14-day import quarantine belongs to the opposite direction. It applies to pets entering Iceland, not leaving it. A compliant pet flying out to the UK faces neither a titre test nor a quarantine hold.

MAST does not issue the same certificate for a UK-bound pet as it does for one heading into the EU. Ask your vet specifically for the GB pet health certificate, since the standard EU-format form is the wrong document for this route.

Icelandair stopped taking pets as checked baggage on 1 November 2024. Unusually for a route out of Iceland, British Airways also flies Keflavik to Heathrow nonstop, so there are two direct cargo options rather than the single carrier most Iceland routes rely on.

The tapeworm treatment window is measured against your dog's actual Heathrow landing time, not the Keflavik departure. A delayed flight can push the treatment outside the 120-hour limit if it was timed too close to the edge.

How United Kingdom handles incoming pets

United Kingdom has a modest set of rules compared with stricter destinations. Get the essentials in place and confirm the finer points below.

What experienced owners do

Use an official, government-authorised vet for the health certificate, not just your usual clinic. Many destinations only accept certificates signed by an approved vet.

If this is your first international move, consider an IPATA-registered pet transport agent. They handle the documents, airline booking and crate sizing, and can troubleshoot problems.

Get your pet used to the travel crate weeks in advance. A calm, crate-familiar animal travels far better than one meeting the crate for the first time at the airport.

Confirm live animal acceptance on every flight segment before booking. Not all aircraft or connections take pets, and cargo terms can differ leg to leg.
