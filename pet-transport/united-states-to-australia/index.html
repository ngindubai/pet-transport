Pet Transport USA to Australia: The Complete 2026 Guide

Bringing your dog or cat from United States to Australia takes some coordination, but the requirements are well defined. Expect a long lead time of roughly 26-40 weeks; the waiting periods here cannot be rushed. Your pet will spend 10 days in quarantine after …

Critical points

The 180-day wait after the titre test is mandatory with no exceptions. Begin this process at minimum 7 months before your intended travel date.

USA is classified as Group 4 by DAFF, which is a higher-risk classification than the UK (Group 3). The titre test and quarantine requirements are the same, but the permit process may require additional documentation.

United Airlines has suspended its PetSafe cargo program for the general public. Do not book United for this route.

Quarantine at Mickleham must be pre-booked before departure. Arriving without a confirmed booking will cause delays and additional costs.

The DAFF import permit must be in place before the pet departs the US. Permits are not issued retrospectively.

Australia entry requirements at a glance

Entry to Australia is tightly controlled, and officials verify each requirement in turn. Treat it as a sequence where every step gates the next.

Getting the details right

Microchip before you vaccinate. The microchip must be in place before the rabies shot for the vaccination to count, and getting this order wrong is the most common and costly mistake.

Use an official, government-authorised vet for the health certificate, not just your usual clinic. Many destinations only accept certificates signed by an approved vet.

If this is your first international move, consider an IPATA-registered pet transport agent. They handle the documents, airline booking and crate sizing, and can troubleshoot problems.

Get your pet used to the travel crate weeks in advance. A calm, crate-familiar animal travels far better than one meeting the crate for the first time at the airport.

Your pet will fly to Melbourne (MEL or Tullamarine), complete the 10-day quarantine, and then you can arrange domestic transport to your final destination. If you are moving to Perth or Brisbane, factor in the additional domestic leg from Melbourne.

The DAFF-approved laboratory will evaluate the titre test result regardless of where the vaccination was given. What matters is the titre result (0.5 IU/ml or above) and the timing documentation.
There are no extensions to the 180-day waiting period. If your travel date moves and you are within the 180-day window from your titre test, you must wait until the 180 days have passed. If your titre test result expires (titre tests are valid indefinitely if vaccination cover is maintained, but if vaccination lapses, a new test may be required), the timeline can restart. The combination of USDA-accredited vet, USDA APHIS endorsement, DAFF-format certificate, BICON permit application, airline cargo booking, and quarantine facility reservation is technically manageable alone but operationally complex. A single document error at any point can delay the move significantly. US-based agents who specialise in the Australian route typically charge $500 to $2,000 and coordinate all steps on your behalf.
Australia bans the import of Pit Bull Terriers, Japanese Tosa, Dogo Argentino, Fila Brasileiro, and perros de presa Canario. These are absolute bans with no exemptions. A dog that substantially conforms to the type characteristics of a banned breed may also be refused, even without a formal breed certificate. Additional state-level breed restrictions may apply once the dog is in Australia.
Agent fees for the US to Australia route typically run $500 to $2,000 on the US side, plus AUD 2,000 to AUD 4,000 for the Australian quarantine handling agent (usually required). The saving from not using an agent is rarely worth the risk on this particular route. Documentation errors that require the animal to be returned or held cost significantly more than the agent fee. Most experienced relocators on this corridor use an agent for both the US side and the Australian arrival process.
