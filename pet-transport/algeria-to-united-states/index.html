In short: Preparing to move a pet from Algeria to United States typically takes around 10-14 weeks. There is no routine quarantine on arrival for compliant pets. A rabies titre test may be required depending on your pet's origin, so check the requirements below.

Checklist

What your pet needs

Every item below must be completed and verified before your pet can travel. Expand each category for the detail.

Microchip
Universal scanner-detectable microchip required for dogs, implanted before the rabies vaccination used for the titre test. No federal microchip rule applies to cats. Source: CDC, 'Bringing a Dog into the US,' updated 15 April 2026.
Rabies vaccination
Algeria is one of the CDC's listed high-risk countries for dog rabies, so a foreign-issued vaccination record and a government-endorsed certificate govern entry. First vaccination at 12 weeks or older, and at least 28 days before entry. No federal rabies rule applies to cats. Source: CDC, 'Entry Requirements for Foreign-Vaccinated Dogs from High-Risk Countries,' 2026.
Rabies titre test
Required for a dog whose CDC Dog Import Form travel history includes Algeria within the last 6 months, unless the owner accepts a 28-day quarantine instead. Blood must be drawn at least 30 days after the first rabies vaccination and at least 28 days before US entry, tested only at a CDC-approved laboratory. Cats face no titre requirement of any kind.
Quarantine
No routine quarantine for a dog presenting a passing CDC-approved titre result on arrival. A 28-day quarantine at a CDC-registered animal care facility applies only where no valid titre is presented, entirely at the importer's cost. Cats face no quarantine requirement.
Import permit
No formal import permit. Every dog needs a free CDC Dog Import Form, completed online and valid for a single entry on the stated arrival date. Cats need no import form or permit at federal level.
Health certificate
Certification of Foreign Rabies Vaccination and Microchip form, completed by the Algerian vet and endorsed by DSV, valid 30 days from signature. DSV normally issues Algeria's own paperwork in French or Arabic; since the CDC form itself is in English, having the completed form checked against a certified translation before submission is a sensible precaution. Source: CDC, 2026.
Export permit
No separate export permit is published specifically for a pet leaving Algeria; the endorsed health certificate below is the controlling document.
Veterinary inspection
Examination by a licensed Algerian vet confirming the animal is fit to travel and free from disease, close to the departure date.
Government endorsement
The vet's certificate is endorsed by the Direction des Services Vétérinaires (DSV), within Algeria's Ministry of Agriculture and Rural Development. DSV also acts as the government-endorsing authority for the CDC's own Certification of Foreign Rabies Vaccination and Microchip form, a separate US-specific document.
Health certificate
Official veterinary health certificate in French or Arabic, covering identification, vaccination record and fitness to travel, alongside the CDC's Certification of Foreign Rabies Vaccination and Microchip form.

What this route typically costs

Algerian vet visits, vaccination, DSV-endorsed certificate and translation combined: EUR 300-650
Rabies titre test and courier to a CDC-approved laboratory: EUR 150-300
CDC-registered animal care facility examination and revaccination fee, set independently by each facility: typically USD 300-600, confirmed at the time of booking
IATA-compliant travel crate: EUR 80-300
Agent handling the Turkish Airlines or Air France freight booking: EUR 700-1,600
Air freight Algiers to a CDC-facility US airport, via Istanbul or Paris: EUR 1,400-3,200 depending on crate size and season
Total typical range: EUR 2,900-6,400

Critical points

No airline flies Algiers to the US directly. Every routing goes through a hub; Istanbul and Paris are the two with genuine onward cargo capacity to CDC-facility airports.

A dog without a passing titre result faces a 28-day quarantine at a CDC-registered facility, entirely at the owner's cost. Missing either the 30-day-after-vaccination minimum or the 28-day-before-entry deadline for the blood draw forces that choice.

DSV issues Algeria's own export paperwork in French or Arabic, while the CDC's Certification of Foreign Rabies Vaccination and Microchip form is in English. Confirm directly with DSV how it wants to handle the endorsement rather than assuming the standard export certificate process covers it automatically.

None of the CDC's high-risk paperwork, titre test or facility reservation applies to cats. A cat leaving Algeria for the US needs the standard DSV-endorsed export certificate and nothing further at the American border.

How United States handles incoming pets

United States asks for several pieces of paperwork, and they all need to line up. A checklist and a clear timeline are your best tools here.

What experienced owners do

Book the titre test laboratory well ahead. Approved labs have limited slots and the sample takes time to process, so this is not something to leave to the final weeks.

Microchip before you vaccinate. The microchip must be in place before the rabies shot for the vaccination to count, and getting this order wrong is the most common and costly mistake.

Use an official, government-authorised vet for the health certificate, not just your usual clinic. Many destinations only accept certificates signed by an approved vet.

If this is your first international move, consider an IPATA-registered pet transport agent. They handle the documents, airline booking and crate sizing, and can troubleshoot problems.
