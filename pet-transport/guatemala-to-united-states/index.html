Overview

Planning a pet relocation from Guatemala to United States is easier when you work backwards from your travel date. This route takes some organising, and roughly 8-11 weeks of preparation is about right. Pets with correct documentation skip quarantine entirely.

The guide below walks through each stage so nothing gets missed.

Step by step

The Guatemala to United States process

1
8-10 weeks before travel.
Fit the ISO microchip, then give the rabies vaccination. Confirm the dog is at least 12 weeks old.

Responsible: Your Guatemalan vet

2
Around 6-7 weeks before travel.
Draw blood for the rabies titre test at least 30 days after vaccination, send to a CDC-approved laboratory, and get a result of 0.5 IU/ml or above.

Responsible: Your vet and the CDC-approved lab

3
As early as possible, ideally alongside step 1.
Reserve a slot at a CDC-registered animal care facility airport (Atlanta, Los Angeles, Miami, JFK, Philadelphia or Washington Dulles) and book your routing to land there.

Responsible: You or your agent

4
Shortly before travel, matched to the confirmed arrival date.
Complete the CDC Dog Import Form online for each dog and keep the emailed receipt.

Responsible: You

5
1-5 days before departure.
Arrange the screwworm inspection certificate with a full-time government veterinarian through MAGA/VISAR, timed to fall within 5 days of shipment.

Responsible: MAGA/VISAR official veterinarian

6
2-4 weeks before travel.
Apply for the International Zoosanitary Export Certificate through MAGA's VUPE window and pay the USD 6.25 fee.

Responsible: You, via MAGA/VUPE

7
Day of arrival.
Fly, and present the CDC Dog Import Form receipt, titre result and screwworm certificate on arrival at the CDC-registered facility airport.

Responsible: Airline ground handler and CDC officer

Checklist

United States of America: entry requirements

RequirementDetail
MicrochipISO-compatible microchip readable with a universal scanner, implanted before the rabies vaccine is given. Source: CDC, Bringing a Dog into the United States, reviewed 22 July 2024.
Rabies vaccinationCurrent rabies vaccination required. The dog must be at least 12 weeks old at the time of vaccination, and the first dose must be given at least 28 days before arrival in the US. Source: CDC, reviewed 22 July 2024.
Rabies titre testGuatemala is classified by the CDC as high-risk for dog rabies (confirmed on the CDC's high-risk countries list, last reviewed 15 April 2026, alongside neighbours Belize, Honduras and El Salvador). A blood sample must be drawn at least 30 days after vaccination and at least 28 days before the dog enters the US, tested at a CDC-approved laboratory. A result of 0.5 IU/ml or above, the OIE cut-off for adequate rabies immunity, avoids the automatic quarantine below. This is markedly quicker than the UK's unlisted-country rule, which forces a fixed 3-month wait after a passing blood draw. The US only asks that the blood draw sit 28 days ahead of arrival, not a fixed period after a passing result.
QuarantineNone for a dog with a valid CDC Dog Import Form and a passing titre result, arriving through a CDC-registered animal care facility airport. Without a valid titre, the CDC requires a mandatory 28-day quarantine at a CDC-registered facility, at the owner's expense. Source: CDC, reviewed 22 July 2024.
Import permitNo general import permit for personal dogs or cats. High-risk-country dogs must instead hold a confirmed reservation at a CDC-registered animal care facility airport before travel: currently Atlanta (ATL), Los Angeles (LAX), Miami (MIA), New York JFK, Philadelphia (PHL) or Washington Dulles (IAD). Source: CDC, CDC-registered Animal Care Facilities, last updated 8 May 2024.
Health certificateThe CDC does not mandate a general veterinary health certificate for dog entry itself; the Dog Import Form and, where relevant, the screwworm certificate carry the regulatory weight. Airlines will still want a standard vet health certificate for boarding, so get one issued in Guatemala regardless.

Leaving Guatemala

Export requirements

RequirementDetail
Export permitNo separate export permit for a single pet. The International Zoosanitary Export Certificate (Certificado Zoosanitario Internacional de Exportacion) is the operative document, obtained through MAGA's export window (VUPE, Viceministerio de Sanidad Agropecuaria y Regulaciones). Fee: USD 6.25. Source: MAGA/VISAR, VUPE export services, and La Hora, 17 October 2024.
Health certificateVeterinary health certificate signed and stamped by a Guatemalan vet, recording the animal's description, vaccination history and the rabies vaccination date. Source: MAGA/VISAR and La Hora, 17 October 2024.

Critical points

Read before you book

⚠ Guatemala is both a CDC high-risk rabies country and a New World Screwworm affected country at the same time. Missing either the titre test or the screwworm certificate is enough to get a dog held or quarantined on arrival, so build both into the same timeline rather than treating them as separate errands.
⚠ Dogs must land at a CDC-registered animal care facility airport: Atlanta, Los Angeles, Miami, New York JFK, Philadelphia or Washington Dulles. A routing that skips these gateways is not usable for a high-risk-country dog, whatever the airline quotes you.
⚠ United's cargo pet service is currently suspended for regular customers. If you own a large dog, book Avianca or American from the outset rather than assuming United's Houston connection can carry it.
Carriers

Airlines on this route

Avianca
Flies Guatemala City (GUA) direct to Miami, New York JFK, Los Angeles and Washington Dulles, all four of which happen to be CDC-registered animal care facility airports. Small pets travel in the cabin under 10kg combined weight; larger dogs and cats go as cargo in an IATA-compliant crate. Confirm current pet acceptance and brachycephalic breed restrictions before booking.
Cabin and Cargo
American Airlines
Runs GUA to Miami (MIA), one of American's own hubs and a CDC-registered facility airport, with cargo handled through American Airlines Cargo (PetEmbark). An 85F (29C) ground-temperature embargo applies at Miami, worth checking against your travel dates.
Cabin and Cargo
United Airlines
Serves GUA to Houston (IAH). United's own published policy currently limits it to in-cabin pets only; it does not run a general cargo pet service, so a larger dog will need Avianca or American instead.
Cabin Only
Delta Air Lines
Connects GUA to Atlanta (ATL), a CDC-registered facility airport. Delta's cargo service is currently restricted to active US military and State Department personnel with PCS orders, so this is a cabin-pet option only for most owners.
Cabin Only

The rules for bringing a pet into United States

United States has a detailed set of import rules, and each one needs to be satisfied before travel. None of it is unusual, but the order and timing are what catch people out.

How to keep this move on track

Book the titre test laboratory well ahead. Approved labs have limited slots and the sample takes time to process, so this is not something to leave to the final weeks.

Microchip before you vaccinate. The microchip must be in place before the rabies shot for the vaccination to count, and getting this order wrong is the most common and costly mistake.

Use an official, government-authorised vet for the health certificate, not just your usual clinic. Many destinations only accept certificates signed by an approved vet.

If this is your first international move, consider an IPATA-registered pet transport agent. They handle the documents, airline booking and crate sizing, and can troubleshoot problems.
