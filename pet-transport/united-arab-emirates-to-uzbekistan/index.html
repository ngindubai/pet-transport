Requirements

Uzbekistan entry requirements

Every item below must be in place before your pet can enter. We verify and track each one.

Microchip
Required. ISO 11784/11785 compliant, 15-digit chip, implanted before or at the time of rabies vaccination and matched on every document.
Rabies vaccination
Required. Minimum age 12 weeks for the first dose, given between 30 days and 12 months before arrival.
Rabies titre test
Not required. Uzbekistan does not ask for a FAVN or ELISA rabies antibody titre test provided vaccination timing is met, a lighter pathway than the titre test the UAE itself applies to Uzbekistan-origin pets travelling the other way.
Quarantine
Waived for a fully compliant pet. Non-compliant arrivals can face quarantine at the owner's cost, return to origin, or in the worst case destruction, at the discretion of the Tashkent airport veterinary post.
Import permit
Sources genuinely disagree. Some describe entry for an accompanied pet without a separate import permit; a 2026 process review states the Committee of Veterinary and Livestock Development requires a permit application through its vetgov.uz portal before travel. Budget for the permit route and confirm directly with the Committee before booking flights, given this is a thinly documented route.
Health certificate
MOCCAE-issued health certificate, with a clinical exam close to the flight date, typically within 5 days, also expected by Uzbek entry officials.
Leaving United Arab Emirates

Export requirements

Export permit
No separate export permit for a personal pet. A MOCCAE veterinary health certificate, issued after a physical exam and valid 30 days from issue, is the core document. Companion animals are explicitly exempt from needing a certificate of origin.
Health certificate
MOCCAE veterinary health certificate, reformatted to meet Uzbekistan's entry documentation.
Costs

What this route typically costs

MOCCAE health certificate: AED 100 discounted personal-consignment fee, or AED 400 standard fee.
Possible vetgov.uz import permit fee: not published; confirm directly given the ambiguity noted above.
IATA-compliant travel crate: AED 350-1,100.
Direct cargo, Dubai or Sharjah to Tashkent: AED 3,200-6,800.
Total typical range: AED 3,800-8,300 over 6-10 weeks.

Critical points

Whether Uzbekistan requires a separate vetgov.uz import permit for a UAE-origin pet is genuinely unresolved in public documentation. Confirm directly with the Committee of Veterinary and Livestock Development before booking.

No titre test is required, but the microchip must still be implanted before or at the same time as the rabies vaccination. Reverse that order and Uzbek entry officials can treat the vaccination as invalid.

This is one of the few routes in this series with a genuine direct flight in both directions, which meaningfully shortens the practical timeline compared with connecting-flight origins.

Uzbekistan import rules, explained

Uzbekistan keeps its requirements reasonable, though a few steps have fixed timing. Map those out first and the rest falls into place.

Practical tips for this move

Microchip before you vaccinate. The microchip must be in place before the rabies shot for the vaccination to count, and getting this order wrong is the most common and costly mistake.

Use an official, government-authorised vet for the health certificate, not just your usual clinic. Many destinations only accept certificates signed by an approved vet.

If this is your first international move, consider an IPATA-registered pet transport agent. They handle the documents, airline booking and crate sizing, and can troubleshoot problems.

Get your pet used to the travel crate weeks in advance. A calm, crate-familiar animal travels far better than one meeting the crate for the first time at the airport.
