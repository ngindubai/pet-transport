Step by step

The Estonia to Belgium pet import process

Each step must be completed in a precise sequence. Start early. We manage every stage and deadline.

1
Confirm microchip is in place (ISO 11784/11785)
Responsible: Estonian vet
If not yet chipped, do this first. Everything else is tied to the chip number.
2
Ensure rabies vaccination is current
Responsible: Estonian vet
If primary vaccination, allow 21 days before travel to Belgium. If a current booster within the valid period, it is immediately effective.
3
Obtain or update EU pet passport or arrange AHC
Responsible: Authorised Estonian vet (+ VTA endorsement for AHC)
Any VTA-authorised Estonian vet can issue or update the EU pet passport. AHC must be issued within 10 days of travel if used instead of the passport.
4
Book flight from Tallinn to Brussels
Responsible: Owner
3-4 weeks before travel. Confirm airline pet policy (cabin size limits, cargo booking procedure).
5
Travel to Brussels
Responsible: Owner + airline
Travel day. Tallinn to Brussels via connection is typically 3-5 hours.
Requirements

What your pet needs to enter Belgium

Every item below must be in place before your pet can travel. We manage and verify each one.

Microchip
Required. ISO 11784/11785 standard. Must be implanted before or at the same time as the first rabies vaccination.
Rabies vaccination
Required. Current vaccination. Primary vaccination: at least 21 days before entry into Belgium. Boosters within the prior vaccine's valid period are considered immediately effective.
Rabies titre test
Not required. Both Estonia and Belgium are EU member states. The EU pet travel framework for intra-EU movement does not require a rabies antibody titre test.
Quarantine
Not required for EU residents with compliant documentation. No routine quarantine applies on intra-EU pet movements.
Import permit
Not required for companion pets moving between EU member states.
Health certificate
EU pet passport (valid for the life of the pet with current vaccination entries) or a single-use EU Animal Health Certificate (AHC) endorsed by the VTA. April 2026 change: EU pet passports are now restricted to pets whose owners are primarily resident in the EU. Most Estonian residents are EU residents and continue to use the passport. Non-EU nationals residing in Estonia should use the AHC.
Export permit (Estonia)
No export permit required for companion pets moving from Estonia to Belgium.
Costs

What this route typically costs

EU pet passport issuance or update: EUR 30-80 at a VTA-authorised vet
AHC (if used instead of passport): EUR 50-120 including VTA endorsement
Rabies vaccination booster (if due): EUR 20-60
Airline cabin pet fee: EUR 30-100 per leg depending on carrier
Airline cargo fee (larger pets): EUR 150-500 depending on route and weight
IATA travel crate (if needed): EUR 50-200
Total typical range: EUR 150-800 depending on pet size and airline choice

Critical points to read before you book

April 2026 EU rule change: from 22 April 2026, EU pet passports are only valid for pets whose owners primarily reside in the EU. Estonian residents are EU residents and can use EU pet passports as normal. Non-EU nationals resident in Estonia (on long-term visas or permits) should use an AHC issued by a VTA-authorised vet for their trip.

Budget carriers including Ryanair operate from Tallinn Airport to Belgian destinations (Brussels Charleroi, not Brussels Airport). Ryanair does not accept pets in cabin or as cargo. If booking a budget fare, confirm the airline's specific pet policy.

The EU pet passport records the microchip number, vaccination dates, and issuing vet details. Any mismatch between the chip number in the passport and the chip in your pet will cause problems. Ask your vet to scan the chip and confirm the number before travel.

Belgium's FASFC does not typically impose additional border checks for EU-to-EU pet movements with valid documentation. Present the passport or AHC at airline check-in and carry it during travel.

Airlines

Carriers approved for this route

Not all airlines accept live animals on this route. We know which carriers to use and how to book.

AIRBALTIC
airBaltic
Tallinn (TLL) is served by airBaltic connecting to its Riga (RIX) hub, from which Brussels (BRU) connections are available. airBaltic allows small pets (up to 8 kg combined weight with carrier) in the cabin on its flights. Larger pets travel as cargo. A practical option for small pets on this corridor.
Cabin and Cargo
BRUSSELS
Brussels Airlines
Belgium's national carrier operates connections into Brussels Airport (BRU). Does not fly from Tallinn directly but may operate the hub-to-Brussels leg. Small pets allowed in cabin on some Brussels Airlines flights.
Cabin and Cargo
LUFTHANSA
Lufthansa
Via Frankfurt (FRA), then FRA to Brussels. Small pets in cabin on Lufthansa flights where the carrier fits under the seat. Larger pets as cargo. FRA provides excellent cargo handling.
Cabin and Cargo
LOT POLISH
LOT Polish Airlines
Via Warsaw Chopin (WAW), then WAW to BRU. LOT allows small pets in cabin on most European routes. A budget-friendly option for Tallinn-to-Brussels.
Cabin and Cargo

Belgium entry requirements at a glance

Bringing a pet into Belgium is one of the simpler processes. Cover the core requirements and you should have few surprises.

Getting the details right

Work backwards from your travel date and add a buffer. Fixed waiting periods mean a single slipped appointment can push the whole move back by weeks.

Check the airline’s crate size and labelling rules early. An undersized or wrongly labelled crate is a frequent reason pets are refused at check-in.

Ask about temperature embargoes for your travel window. Summer and winter cargo restrictions can rule out certain routes and dates entirely.

Microchip before you vaccinate. The microchip must be in place before the rabies shot for the vaccination to count, and getting this order wrong is the most common and costly mistake.

FAQ

Common questions about Estonia to Belgium pet transport

No additional documents are needed for movement within Belgium once your pet has arrived with a valid EU pet passport or AHC and current vaccination. Belgium does not require domestic registration of dogs or cats at the federal level for visitors, though long-term residents may have local registration obligations. Check with your local Belgian municipality if you are relocating permanently.
airBaltic allows cats and small dogs in the cabin on its EU routes, provided the combined weight of the pet and carrier is within the airline’s limit (typically 8 kg total). The pet fee applies per leg. For the TLL-RIX-BRU routing, you would pay the cabin pet fee on both legs and must ensure the carrier fits under the seat in front of you. Book the cabin pet space at the time of flight booking, not separately.
EU pet passports do not expire. The passport itself is valid for the pet’s life. What must be current is the rabies vaccination recorded inside it. Check that the most recent rabies vaccination entry is within its valid period on the date of travel. If the vaccination has lapsed, your Estonian vet can administer a booster, add the entry to the existing passport, and you can travel once 21 days have passed from a primary vaccination (or immediately if it is a booster within the prior vaccine’s valid period).
