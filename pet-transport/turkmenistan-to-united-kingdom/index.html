In short: Preparing to move a pet from Turkmenistan to United Kingdom typically takes around 20-26 weeks. There is no routine quarantine on arrival for compliant pets. A rabies titre test is required, which adds a fixed waiting period, so start early.

Checklist

What your pet needs

Every item below must be completed and verified before your pet can travel. Expand each category for the detail.

Microchip
Required (ISO 11784/11785 standard). Must go in before the rabies vaccination, or the vaccine doesn't count.
Rabies vaccination
Required. Minimum age 12 weeks. 21-day wait after the first vaccination, or the last dose of an initial course, before travel.
Rabies titre test
Required. Turkmenistan is not on the UK's Part 1 or Part 2 rabies-listed country list (GOV.UK, checked July 2026), so a blood test applies regardless of vaccination history. Blood drawn at least 30 days after vaccination, at an APHA-approved FAVN or ELISA laboratory, minimum result 0.5 IU/ml, then a 90-day wait from the test date before entry.
Quarantine
No routine quarantine for pets arriving with fully compliant paperwork. Penalty quarantine of up to 120 days applies at the owner's expense if documentation, vaccination timing or the travel route is wrong.
Import permit
Not required. The Great Britain pet health certificate serves as the travel document for this unlisted-country route.
Health certificate
Required. Great Britain pet health certificate issued by an official vet in Turkmenistan, valid 10 days from issue to entry. Dogs also need tapeworm treatment recorded on it, given 24 to 120 hours before scheduled UK arrival.
Export permit
No standard, publicly documented export permit process. The State Veterinary Service, under Turkmenistan's Ministry of Agriculture and Environmental Protection, issues export documentation on request. Turkmenistan publishes very little English-language guidance on pet export, so confirm the process directly with the ministry or an experienced relocation agent before booking anything.
Veterinary inspection
Required. Pre-export clinical inspection by a State Veterinary Service veterinarian.
Government endorsement
The export health certificate must be endorsed by the State Veterinary Service. Given how tightly administrative processes are controlled in Turkmenistan, allow more lead time than you would for almost any other origin on this site.
Health certificate
Required. Issued by a State Veterinary Service veterinarian in Turkmenistan, confirming rabies vaccination, microchip number and fitness to travel.

Carriers on this route

Not all airlines accept live animals on this route. Confirm the operating carrier's current policy for every flight segment.

Turkmenistan Airlines
The national carrier accepts small pets in the cabin, up to 8kg including carrier, tucked under the seat for the whole flight. A published cargo policy for larger pets isn't clearly available; confirm current capability directly with the airline before assuming it exists.
Cabin Only
Turkish Airlines
Operates nonstop Ashgabat-Istanbul, the same route Turkmenistan Airlines also flies, connecting onward to Heathrow, Gatwick and other UK airports via Turkish Cargo for larger pets or the cabin for small ones.
Cabin and Cargo
flydubai
Serves Ashgabat-Dubai, one of the few scheduled links out of Turkmenistan with pet-carriage capability, giving access to Dubai's onward cargo network for pets travelling as manifested freight.
Cargo Only
Emirates
Picks up the Dubai-London leg via Emirates SkyCargo after a flydubai Ashgabat-Dubai connection. Summer heat embargoes in the Gulf can affect the transfer, so avoid booking through Dubai in peak summer months if possible.
Cargo Only
British Airways
No service into Ashgabat, so BA only features as the UK-side leg via an approved cargo agent at Heathrow, handling pets in British Airways World Cargo's temperature-controlled hold.

Costs

What this route typically costs

Microchip, vaccinations and pre-export vet checks in Turkmenistan: no fixed published fee; budget roughly US$50-150
Rabies titre test plus international courier of the blood sample: US$150-300
IATA-compliant travel crate: US$100-350 depending on size
State Veterinary Service export health certificate and endorsement: fee not publicly published, plus meaningful agent time given the country's closed administrative style
Airline cargo or cabin fee, Ashgabat-Istanbul-London or Ashgabat-Dubai-London: roughly US$1,000-3,000, and often at the higher end given how limited scheduled cargo capacity out of Ashgabat is
Pet relocation agent fee: close to essential on this route; US$700-1,800
Total estimate: roughly US$2,000-5,600, excluding UK-side vet costs after arrival

Critical points

Turkmenistan is one of the most tightly state-controlled countries for international travel and export logistics. Expect slower administrative timelines than almost anywhere else on this site, and build in extra buffer rather than working to a fixed date.

The Alabai, or Central Asian Shepherd Dog, cannot be exported from Turkmenistan under any circumstances. It's protected as a national symbol, and no export health certificate will be issued for the breed.

Turkmenistan is unlisted on the UK's Part 1/Part 2 system, so the rabies titre test is mandatory regardless of vaccination history.

Turkmenistan Airlines' cargo pet policy isn't clearly published anywhere. Confirm current capability directly with the airline or a relocation agent before assuming cargo space exists on any given route.

Meeting United Kingdom's entry requirements

Entry to United Kingdom is tightly controlled, and officials verify each requirement in turn. Treat it as a sequence where every step gates the next.

Advice worth following

Confirm live animal acceptance on every flight segment before booking. Not all aircraft or connections take pets, and cargo terms can differ leg to leg.

Keep every original document together and take copies. Border officials want originals, and a missing signature or date can hold up clearance.

Work backwards from your travel date and add a buffer. Fixed waiting periods mean a single slipped appointment can push the whole move back by weeks.

Check the airline’s crate size and labelling rules early. An undersized or wrongly labelled crate is a frequent reason pets are refused at check-in.
