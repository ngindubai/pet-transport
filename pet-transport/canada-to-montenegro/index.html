In short: Preparing to move a pet from Canada to Montenegro typically takes around 19-25 weeks. There is no routine quarantine on arrival for compliant pets. A rabies titre test is required, which adds a fixed waiting period, so start early.

Checklist

What your pet needs

Every item below must be completed and verified before your pet can travel. Expand each category for the detail.

Microchip
ISO 11784/11785 microchip required, implanted before the rabies vaccination is given. A vaccination given first is not recognised.
Rabies vaccination
Current rabies vaccination required. Published Montenegro-specific guidance places the titre blood draw at least 30 days after this vaccination, so the two dates need planning together.
Rabies titre test
Required, and no exemption for any particular country of origin is described anywhere in the published guidance, whether EU member states, the UK, the US or Canada, all of which sit on rabies-controlled or approved-country lists elsewhere. A FAVN or RNATT titre test is drawn no sooner than 30 days after the primary rabies vaccination, with a further wait of more than 3 calendar months from that test before entry. This puts Montenegro in a genuinely different position to North Macedonia, also covered on this site, which extends the EU's approved-country exemption to Canadian arrivals. Given how thin the published detail is on Montenegro's own rule, confirm the current requirement directly with AFSVPA before booking travel.
Quarantine
No routine quarantine for pets arriving with compliant paperwork. Guidance warns that a pet without valid documentation risks quarantine, refusal of entry, or return to the country of departure at the owner's expense.
Import permit
No standalone import permit is described for an owner travelling with their own pet dog or cat.
Health certificate
International veterinary health certificate, issued within 10 days of travel, presented on arrival alongside the vaccination record and titre test result.
Export permit
No standalone export permit for a personal pet. Montenegro's own guidance expects a CFIA-endorsed certificate rather than a vet's signature alone.
Veterinary inspection
A clinical examination by a CFIA-accredited veterinarian, confirming the pet is fit to travel and meets Montenegro's stated health conditions.
Health certificate
A CFIA-accredited vet completes the certificate, then an official CFIA veterinarian endorses it at a booked regional office appointment. CFIA cannot endorse a certificate once the pet has left Canada, so the appointment has to happen before departure. Processing typically runs 2 to 5 business days. Source: CFIA, Pets: Export certificates, inspection.canada.ca, 2026.

Airlines

Carriers on this route

Not all airlines accept live animals on this route. Confirm the operating carrier's current policy for every flight segment.

Lufthansa
Direct Toronto to Frankfurt or Munich, connecting onward to Podgorica or Tivat, using the Frankfurt Animal Lounge for hold transfers. Keeping the pet on one airline group for the whole journey avoids a handover at a second hub.
Cabin and Cargo
Austrian Airlines
Direct Toronto-Vienna service around six times weekly, connecting on to Podgorica. Cabin pets accepted to 8kg; snub-nosed breeds excluded from hold transport entirely.
Cabin and Cargo
Turkish Airlines
Flies Toronto to Istanbul nonstop, with a separate daily Istanbul-Podgorica sector. Live animal cargo acceptance on this specific two-leg combination isn't clearly documented in published guidance, so confirm current policy directly with the airline's cargo desk before booking; Lufthansa and Austrian Airlines are the better-established options for this route.

Critical points

Montenegro's titre requirement does not appear to bend for any country's own approved status elsewhere. Do not assume Canada's EU Annex II listing, which works in Canada's favour on the Moldova and North Macedonia routes, carries across to Montenegro.

Published detail on Montenegro's exact import rule is thin and inconsistent between sources. Confirm directly with AFSVPA before committing to a travel date, rather than relying on any single secondary guide, including this one.

CFIA cannot endorse the export certificate once the pet has left Canada. Time the appointment against the end of the 3-month titre wait, not the other way round.

What Montenegro requires

The requirements for Montenegro are thorough rather than difficult. Work through them methodically and confirm anything you are unsure about with the official source.

Avoiding the common mistakes

Work backwards from your travel date and add a buffer. Fixed waiting periods mean a single slipped appointment can push the whole move back by weeks.

Check the airline’s crate size and labelling rules early. An undersized or wrongly labelled crate is a frequent reason pets are refused at check-in.

Ask about temperature embargoes for your travel window. Summer and winter cargo restrictions can rule out certain routes and dates entirely.

Book the titre test laboratory well ahead. Approved labs have limited slots and the sample takes time to process, so this is not something to leave to the final weeks.
