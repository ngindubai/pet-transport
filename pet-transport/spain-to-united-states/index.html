Requirements

United States entry requirements

Every item below must be in place before your pet can enter. We verify and track each one.

Microchip
Required for all dogs. Must be implanted before the rabies vaccination, or the vaccine is considered invalid by CDC rules.
Rabies vaccination
Strongly recommended for dogs but not required by CDC for pets from low-risk countries. Cats: no federal vaccination requirement.
Rabies titre test
Not required. Spain is classified as a rabies-free or low-risk country for CDC purposes. Dogs from low-risk countries need only the CDC Dog Import Form receipt.
Import permit
Not required.
Health certificate
No specific CDC-mandated health certificate for dogs from low-risk countries. A standard vet-issued health certificate or EU pet passport is typically required by the airline and is good practice.
Leaving Spain

Export requirements

Export permit
No formal export permit required from Spain.
Health certificate
Spanish official veterinarian issues a health certificate or EU pet passport. USDA APHIS does not require a specific Spanish government endorsement for export from the EU, but a vet-issued health certificate is standard for airline cargo requirements.

Critical points

Dogs must be at least 6 months old to enter the USA. This applies to all dogs regardless of origin country.

The CDC Dog Import Form is free and can be completed online. You need the receipt for the CDC/CBP inspection. Keep it accessible on arrival.

Cats from Spain face no federal US import requirements. There is no CDC form, no vaccination requirement, and no health certificate required at the federal level, though individual states may have their own rules and airlines require vet documentation.

Some US states have breed-specific legislation (BSL) affecting Pit Bulls, Rottweilers, and other breeds. Check the rules for your destination state or city before travel.

CDC Dog Import Form: what it is and how it works

The CDC Dog Import Form is an online registration form completed by the dog owner before travel. It is free, takes around 15 minutes to complete, and is valid for 6 months and multiple entries if the dog has remained in low-risk countries. You receive a receipt by email; have it available on arrival.

For dogs from Spain (a low-risk country), the CDC Dog Import Form receipt is the primary document at the US port of entry. A CBP officer scans the microchip and checks that it matches the form. For compliant pets, the inspection is brief and the dog is released to you on arrival.

Dogs must be at least 6 months old, appear healthy on arrival, and have a readable microchip that matches the form. No additional vaccination records are required by CDC for low-risk countries, though rabies vaccination is strongly recommended and required by most airlines.

FAQ

Common questions about this route

Spain is classified as a low-risk (non-high-risk) country by the CDC. Dogs from Spain do not need a titre test, quarantine, or certification from a USDA-accredited vet. The only CDC requirement is the free CDC Dog Import Form completed online before travel. The dog must be at least 6 months old, microchipped, and appear healthy on arrival.
The CDC Dog Import Form receipt (free, online) is the primary document. Airlines also require a vet health certificate or EU pet passport and a valid rabies vaccination record. The dog must be microchipped. Keep all documents accessible during the flight and at US customs. Dogs from Spain can arrive at any US airport or border crossing.
At the federal level, there are no US requirements for importing pet cats from Spain. USDA APHIS has no health restrictions on cat imports. In practice, airlines require a vet-issued health certificate, and some US states have their own rules. Check your destination state’s requirements and your airline’s policy before travel.
Iberia has the most direct options from Madrid (MAD) to US gateways including JFK, MIA, LAX, and ORD, and has established live animal cargo handling on this corridor. American Airlines Cargo also serves this route. British Airways is an option with a connection through London Heathrow. Delta allows small pets in cabin on some routes but its cargo service is limited to US military. Confirm current pet acceptance with each airline at the time of booking.
There are no federal breed restrictions for dogs entering the USA. However, some US states, counties, and cities have breed-specific legislation (BSL) affecting breeds such as Pit Bull Terriers, Rottweilers, and American Staffordshire Terriers. Check the rules for your destination city or county before travel. Florida, for example, has banned BSL at state level, while some cities in other states retain it.
