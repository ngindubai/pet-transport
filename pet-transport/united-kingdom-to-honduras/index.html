Requirements

What your pet needs to enter Honduras

Every item below must be in place before your pet can travel. We manage and verify each one.

Microchip
Not explicitly required by SENASA's own import instructions, which ask only for a current vaccination card and the origin country's veterinary export certificate. Fit one anyway: it's already on the UK export paperwork and is useful if the pet is ever lost. Source: SENASA, 'Instructivo para Trámites de Importación de Mascotas a Honduras,' 2023.
Rabies vaccination
A current, in-date rabies vaccination recorded on the vaccination card (cartilla de vacunación) is required. SENASA's instructivo doesn't set a fixed day-count window; it simply requires the vaccination to be valid ('vigente') on arrival. Source: SENASA import instructivo, 2023.
Rabies titre test
Not required. Honduras does not ask for a rabies antibody titre test for pets arriving with a valid vaccination record and export certificate. Source: SENASA import instructivo, 2023.
Quarantine
No fixed kennel quarantine for the ordinary case of 1 or 2 pets arriving with correct paperwork; a Cuarentena official inspects the animal at the port of entry and charges a Lps 525 (roughly USD 20) fee. Bringing 3 or more pets at once switches to a different process that includes vet-supervised home quarantine (Cuarentena Fiduciaria). Source: SENASA import instructivo, 2023.
Import permit
Not required for 1 or 2 pets; documents are presented directly to Cuarentena at the port of entry. For 3 or more pets, a Permiso Zoosanitario de Importación must be applied for in advance through SENASA's online Plataforma de Importaciones. Source: SENASA import instructivo, 2023.
Health certificate
Certificado Veterinario de Exportación from the country of origin (the UK's APHA-endorsed export health certificate), presented with the current vaccination card. Source: SENASA import instructivo, 2023.
Export permit (United Kingdom)
No separate UK export permit is required for a personal pet move. The APHA-endorsed health certificate is the operative document on the UK side.

Critical points to read before you book

There is no direct flight from the UK to Honduras. Every routing changes carrier in the US (Miami, Dallas-Fort Worth or Houston) or Central America, and Avianca does not carry pets on its UK flights, so it can't form part of the transatlantic leg.

Honduras's own import rules are genuinely light for the usual 1 or 2 pet move: no titre test, no import permit, no fixed kennel quarantine. Moving 3 or more pets at once switches to a slower process requiring a Permiso Zoosanitario de Importación and vet-supervised home quarantine.

SENASA's import instructivo relies on the pet's current vaccination card, not a separate Honduran-format certificate, to confirm vaccination status at the port of entry. Travel without it and the Cuarentena inspection has nothing to check against.

How Honduras handles incoming pets

Honduras keeps its requirements reasonable, though a few steps have fixed timing. Map those out first and the rest falls into place.

What experienced owners do

Confirm live animal acceptance on every flight segment before booking. Not all aircraft or connections take pets, and cargo terms can differ leg to leg.

Keep every original document together and take copies. Border officials want originals, and a missing signature or date can hold up clearance.

Work backwards from your travel date and add a buffer. Fixed waiting periods mean a single slipped appointment can push the whole move back by weeks.

Check the airline’s crate size and labelling rules early. An undersized or wrongly labelled crate is a frequent reason pets are refused at check-in.
