Critical points to read before you book

Uruguay is an unlisted country. The complete titre test pathway and 90-day wait are mandatory. Start 20-26 weeks before your target arrival in Italy.

Italy has restricted breeds under Ministry of Health Ordinance of 3 March 2009 (as updated). Check the current restricted breed list for dogs at salute.gov.it before travel. Some breeds face in-country ownership restrictions.

Milan Malpensa (MXP) and Rome Fiumicino (FCO) both handle live animal cargo but have different cargo terminal procedures. Confirm arrival and inspection procedures with the freight forwarder before departure from Uruguay.

Italy import rules, explained

Italy has a detailed set of import rules, and each one needs to be satisfied before travel. None of it is unusual, but the order and timing are what catch people out.

Practical tips for this move

Book the titre test laboratory well ahead. Approved labs have limited slots and the sample takes time to process, so this is not something to leave to the final weeks.

Microchip before you vaccinate. The microchip must be in place before the rabies shot for the vaccination to count, and getting this order wrong is the most common and costly mistake.

Use an official, government-authorised vet for the health certificate, not just your usual clinic. Many destinations only accept certificates signed by an approved vet.

If this is your first international move, consider an IPATA-registered pet transport agent. They handle the documents, airline booking and crate sizing, and can troubleshoot problems.

A passing FAVN result (0.5 IU/ml or above) remains valid for EU entry for as long as the rabies vaccination remains current. There is no expiry date on the titre test result itself. If your dog’s rabies vaccination has been kept up to date with boosters given within the valid period of the previous dose, the original titre test result should still be valid. If the vaccination has ever lapsed and been restarted, a new titre test and 90-day wait would be required. Confirm the vaccination history with your MGAP-registered vet and bring all documentation.
