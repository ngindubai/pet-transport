Checklist

What your pet needs

Every item below must be completed and verified before your pet can travel. Expand each category for the detail.

Microchip
Expected alongside the rabies vaccination as the standard identification pairing Sudan checks on arrival, fitted before the vaccine in line with the pattern nearly every other destination on this site follows.
Rabies vaccination
A current rabies vaccination is required, recorded on the health certificate together with the microchip number. Source: compiled Sudan import guidance from pettravel.com, cross-referenced with Sudan's Ministry of Animal Resources and Fisheries structure, checked 21 July 2026.
Rabies titre test
Not required, regardless of country of departure. That's genuinely unusual set against how many of this site's other African and Middle Eastern destinations demand a titre test specifically from a US-origin pet. Sudan simply doesn't ask for one from anyone.
Quarantine
Not imposed on a pet arriving with complete, correct paperwork. An animal that doesn't meet the published conditions can be quarantined, returned to the US, or, in the most severe published outcomes, euthanised, all at the owner's cost. That range of consequences argues for care with the certificate rather than complacency about the lighter titre rule.
Import permit
No standalone import permit process is confirmed as currently, consistently operating in a published form. Sudan's administrative capacity has been disrupted since the 2023 conflict began, so get direct, current confirmation of what the Ministry of Animal Resources and Fisheries actually expects rather than relying on older guidance.
Health certificate
International health certificate from a licensed vet in the departure country, which for this route means the USDA-endorsed US certificate. Sudan's guidance recommends a direct flight or transit only through a rabies-controlled country, so check the connecting airport as carefully as the departure one.
Export permit
No export permit is required from the US side for a companion animal. Sudan's own conditions, confirmed with the Ministry of Animal Resources and Fisheries, are what actually govern entry.
Veterinary inspection
A USDA-accredited veterinarian examines the animal, confirms the microchip reads correctly and the rabies vaccination is current, then completes the international health certificate.
Government endorsement
Endorsed by USDA APHIS, in most cases electronically through the Veterinary Export Health Certification System (VEHCS), live since 29 April 2020. Budget 3-5 business days for the endorsement once the vet submits the paperwork.
Health certificate
USDA APHIS international health certificate, completed by an accredited vet and endorsed by APHIS. Given Sudan's thin published receiving requirements, confirm directly with the Ministry of Animal Resources and Fisheries what the certificate needs to show, rather than assuming a generic template covers it.

Airlines

Carriers on this route

Not all airlines accept live animals on this route. Confirm the operating carrier's current policy for every flight segment.

Qatar Airways
Started three-weekly Doha to Port Sudan International Airport flights around 2 July 2026, Tuesdays, Thursdays and Saturdays, part of a broader African network expansion from 16 June 2026. Qatar Airways connects from several US gateways to Doha, so this is a genuinely new single-stop option, provided Port Sudan itself, not Khartoum, is the actual destination.
Cargo Only
EgyptAir
Connects several US cities to Cairo, then onward to Khartoum following Sudan Airways' resumed Cairo-Khartoum service from 16 May 2026. Live pet cargo acceptance specifically on the Cairo-Khartoum leg has not been confirmed as currently, reliably operating; get written confirmation before booking.
Cargo Only
Turkish Airlines
Connects numerous US gateways to Istanbul, where a newly launched Badr Airlines service to Turkey, begun 22 May 2026, could offer an onward link toward Khartoum. This connection is genuinely unproven for pet cargo and worth investigating directly rather than assuming it works like an established interline routing.
Cargo Only

Critical points

Sudan doesn't ask for a rabies titre test from a US-origin pet, unlike most of the CDC high-risk-adjacent destinations on this site. That's a genuine point in this route's favour, but it doesn't remove the operational uncertainty around actually getting a pet there.

Qatar Airways' Doha to Port Sudan service is new, confirmed, and running three times weekly since around 2 July 2026, but Port Sudan is roughly 800km from Khartoum. Confirm which city the pet is actually headed to before booking around this flight.

No airline's live pet cargo acceptance out of the US toward Sudan, whichever hub is used, could be confirmed as currently, reliably operating as of this page's research on 21 July 2026. Passenger service resuming and a cargo desk actually confirming a live animal booking are two different things.

Sudan's guidance favours a direct flight or transit only through a rabies-controlled country. A routing through an unlisted hub is worth checking against that guidance before assuming it's automatically fine.

Sudan import rules, explained

Sudan runs a strict import regime, so every document is checked closely on arrival. Build your plan around the fixed waiting periods, because they set the pace for everything else.

Practical tips for this move

Get your pet used to the travel crate weeks in advance. A calm, crate-familiar animal travels far better than one meeting the crate for the first time at the airport.

Confirm live animal acceptance on every flight segment before booking. Not all aircraft or connections take pets, and cargo terms can differ leg to leg.

Keep every original document together and take copies. Border officials want originals, and a missing signature or date can hold up clearance.

Work backwards from your travel date and add a buffer. Fixed waiting periods mean a single slipped appointment can push the whole move back by weeks.
