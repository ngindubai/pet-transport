Requirements

What your pet needs to enter Australia

Every item below must be in place before your pet can travel. We manage and verify each one.

Microchip
ISO 11784/11785 microchip, implanted before any rabies vaccination used for the RNATT titre test.
Rabies vaccination
Current rabies vaccination administered or reconfirmed during the French residency period.
Rabies titre test
RNATT required, sample arriving at an approved laboratory between 180 days and 12 months before the Australia-bound flight. A first-time-vaccinated dog needs 3 to 4 weeks after vaccination before the blood draw counts. Source: DAFF.
Quarantine
Mandatory minimum 10-day quarantine at Mickleham Post Entry Quarantine Facility, Melbourne, weekday arrivals only. The 10-day minimum requires microchip verification before the RNATT draw and at least 180 days before export; otherwise the stay extends to 30 days. Source: DAFF.
Import permit
DAFF import permit via BICON, applied for during the French residency once the titre result is confirmed. Fee: AUD 420.
Health certificate
DAFF-format health certificate from a French vet, valid 5 days from issue, dated at the end of the residency period.
Export permit (Ivory Coast)
No standalone export permit separate from the health certificate. The Direction des Services Veterinaires (DSV), under the Ministry of Animal and Fisheries Resources (MIRAH), endorses the certificate that clears the animal to leave Cote d'Ivoire for the initial move.
Government endorsement
DSV endorsement is required before any airline accepts the paperwork. DSV publishes no fixed turnaround time online, so confirm directly with a registered vet in Abidjan and build in at least two weeks of buffer.
Costs

What this route typically costs

DSV endorsement and vet documentation in Cote d'Ivoire: FCFA 60,000-150,000 (roughly USD 100-250).
French RNATT titre test at an approved laboratory: EUR 150-300.
Cost of living in France during the 180-day residency: highly variable, not a fixed transport cost.
DAFF import permit via BICON: AUD 420.
Mickleham 10-day quarantine (if secured): AUD 1,800-3,500.
Cargo fares, Abidjan to Paris then Paris to Melbourne via Singapore: USD 3,000-5,600 combined.
Total direct transport and processing costs, excluding French living costs: USD 5,300-9,300.

Critical points to read before you book

Cote d'Ivoire's non-approved DAFF status makes the 180-day French residency unavoidable. There is no shortcut and no exception.

DSV does not publish a fixed processing time or fee schedule online. Confirm both directly with a registered vet in Abidjan and add at least two weeks of buffer before booking any flight date.

The microchip verification must happen before the RNATT blood draw and at least 180 days before export, or the Mickleham stay triples from 10 to 30 days.

This same pet, moved to the UAE instead, needs only a titre test and an import permit, with no residency requirement anywhere. DAFF and MOCCAE assess Cote d'Ivoire's rabies risk very differently.

Cabin and Cargo

Meeting Australia's entry requirements

Getting a pet into Australia means meeting a demanding set of rules with no shortcuts. Start from the earliest deadline and work forward from there.

Advice worth following

Work backwards from your travel date and add a buffer. Fixed waiting periods mean a single slipped appointment can push the whole move back by weeks.

Check the airline’s crate size and labelling rules early. An undersized or wrongly labelled crate is a frequent reason pets are refused at check-in.

Ask about temperature embargoes for your travel window. Summer and winter cargo restrictions can rule out certain routes and dates entirely.

Book the titre test laboratory well ahead. Approved labs have limited slots and the sample takes time to process, so this is not something to leave to the final weeks.
