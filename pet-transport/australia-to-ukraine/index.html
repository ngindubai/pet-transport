Pet Transport from Australia to Ukraine

Planning a pet relocation from Australia to Ukraine is easier when you work backwards from your travel date. Set aside roughly 10-16 weeks for this route and keep a close eye on the sequencing. Pets with correct documentation skip quarantine entirely. The …

In short: Preparing to move a pet from Australia to Ukraine typically takes around 10-16 weeks. There is no routine quarantine on arrival for compliant pets.

Critical points

The Australian government's Smartraveller service maintains a standing 'Do Not Travel' advisory for Ukraine, reissued in May 2026, and specifically flags Lviv, the usual overland crossing point for these routes, as no longer the relatively safer city it once was given intensified drone and missile strikes. This is not a decision this guide can make for an owner; check the current advisory yourself before committing to travel.

No flight lands in Ukraine, full stop. The pet's last leg on this route is always by road, so treat the Warsaw landing as roughly the halfway point, not the finish line.

The titre test position for a rabies-free-origin pet is not clearly documented anywhere checked for this page. Confirm directly with a vet near the intended crossing point rather than assuming the vaccination exemption also waives the titre test.

Australia's export certificate and permit are valid only 72 hours from issue. A delay at the Ukrainian border, which is common given the current security situation, can run that window out before the overland leg is finished. Build a genuine margin into the schedule.

Ukraine import rules, explained

Ukraine has a detailed set of import rules, and each one needs to be satisfied before travel. None of it is unusual, but the order and timing are what catch people out.

Practical tips for this move

Book the titre test laboratory well ahead. Approved labs have limited slots and the sample takes time to process, so this is not something to leave to the final weeks.

Microchip before you vaccinate. The microchip must be in place before the rabies shot for the vaccination to count, and getting this order wrong is the most common and costly mistake.

Use an official, government-authorised vet for the health certificate, not just your usual clinic. Many destinations only accept certificates signed by an approved vet.

If this is your first international move, consider an IPATA-registered pet transport agent. They handle the documents, airline booking and crate sizing, and can troubleshoot problems.
