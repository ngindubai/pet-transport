Requirements

What your pet needs to enter Bahamas

Every item below must be in place before your pet can travel. We manage and verify each one.

Microchip
Microchip identification required before the rabies vaccination used for entry.
Rabies vaccination
The UAE is not recognised by WOAH as rabies-free, so the residency-based exemption some countries qualify for doesn't apply here. Instead, BAHFSA's standard vaccine-duration rule governs: a one-year vaccine given no more than 10 months and no less than 1 month before entry, or a three-year vaccine given no more than 34 months and no less than 1 month before entry. Source: BAHFSA Trade Facilitation Unit, bahfsabahamas.com, 2026.
Rabies titre test
Not required. The Bahamas doesn't ask for a rabies antibody titre test from pets arriving from any country, the UAE included.
Quarantine
None published for a pet arriving with a valid ePermit, current rabies vaccination and vet health certificate.
Import permit
ePermit required from BAHFSA before travel, applied for through the government's Click2Clear online system. Register first by emailing click2clear@bahamas.gov.bs for a username and password, then apply for the pet permit. Processing fee USD 10 plus 12% VAT, USD 11.20 total. Source: bahamas.gov.bs, 'Application for Permit to Import Domestic Animals,' 2026.
Health certificate
International veterinary health certificate dated within 48 hours of embarkation, confirming rabies immunity, freedom from internal parasites, and active immunisation against Distemper, Hepatitis, Leptospirosis, Parvovirus and Adenovirus.
Export permit (United Arab Emirates)
No separate export permit exists. MOCCAE's veterinary health certificate for export or re-export of live animals is itself the export clearance document, applied for through the MOCCAE portal, which requires a UAE Pass login.
Government endorsement
The MOCCAE-approved airport clinic vet endorses the certificate directly. No further government office visit is required.
Costs

What this route typically costs

BAHFSA ePermit processing fee: USD 11.20.

Total typical range: USD 2,000-4,300 over 8-12 weeks.

Critical points to read before you book

There is no nonstop flight from the UAE to Nassau on any carrier. Every routing adds a connection through London or Miami, so build the transfer time into the certificate timing, not just the departure date.

The Click2Clear registration step, emailing BAHFSA for a username and password, can take a few working days by itself. Starting it only once flights are booked is a common cause of last-minute delay.

The BAHFSA-format health certificate has a tight 48-hour validity window before embarkation, separate from and shorter than the MOCCAE certificate's own 30-day window. Time the final vet visit around the actual flight date, not the MOCCAE appointment.

Hurricane season, June to November, can disrupt Nassau arrivals with little warning. A rebooked connecting flight can mean the 48-hour BAHFSA certificate has lapsed by the time a new departure slot comes up.

Bahamas entry requirements at a glance

Getting a pet into Bahamas is a middle of the road process. The rules are well defined, so most of the work is simply staying on schedule.

Getting the details right

Microchip before you vaccinate. The microchip must be in place before the rabies shot for the vaccination to count, and getting this order wrong is the most common and costly mistake.

Use an official, government-authorised vet for the health certificate, not just your usual clinic. Many destinations only accept certificates signed by an approved vet.

If this is your first international move, consider an IPATA-registered pet transport agent. They handle the documents, airline booking and crate sizing, and can troubleshoot problems.

Get your pet used to the travel crate weeks in advance. A calm, crate-familiar animal travels far better than one meeting the crate for the first time at the airport.
