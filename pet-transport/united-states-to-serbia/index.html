Overview

A move from United States to Serbia with a dog or cat is mostly about preparing the documents in the right order. Expect about 5-9 weeks. Nothing here is unusually complex, but the deadlines matter. There is no routine quarantine, provided the paperwork is in order.

Read on for the requirements, timing and carrier options for this route.

Checklist

Serbia: entry requirements

RequirementDetail
MicrochipISO 11784/11785 compliant microchip required, implanted before or at the same time as the rabies vaccination recorded on the export certificate.
Rabies vaccinationPrimary or booster rabies vaccination required. Serbia's Veterinary Directorate runs a two-tier system: pets from EU member states and countries on Serbia's own favourable-risk annexes need only a 21-day wait after a first vaccination, while pets from non-listed third countries need a rabies titre test and a 3-month wait. Source: vet.minpolj.gov.rs, movement-of-pets guidance.
Rabies titre testUnclear for US-origin pets from the English-language sources checked. Serbia's own published guidance describes the two tracks above but does not name the United States on either side of the split in the material we could access. The UK is confirmed as sitting on the lower-risk, no-titre-test side under this same Serbian system, and the US typically sits alongside the UK on the equivalent EU-model list used elsewhere in Europe, but treat that as a reasonable expectation rather than a confirmed fact. Confirm directly with the Veterinary Directorate or a USDA-accredited vet before assuming the shorter 21-day pathway applies, particularly if your travel date is close.
QuarantineNo quarantine for a pet that arrives with a valid certificate and current vaccination record matching whichever pathway applies.
Import permitNo import permit needed for a non-commercial move of a pet travelling with its owner (under five animals).
Health certificateNon-commercial health certificate in Serbia's own import format, generally valid for around four months of onward travel within Serbia provided the rabies vaccination it records does not expire in that window.

Leaving United States of America

Export requirements

RequirementDetail
Export permitNo general US export permit for pets. Serbia, like most destinations, works from a USDA-endorsed export health certificate rather than a separate export licence.
Health certificateInternational health certificate issued by a USDA-accredited veterinarian, endorsed through the USDA APHIS eFile system. The endorsement fee is roughly USD 38 per certificate, as published on the APHIS pet travel pages. Build the certificate to Serbia's specific format rather than a generic template, since the Veterinary Directorate checks it against the animal on arrival.

Critical points

Read before you book

⚠ Whether Serbia treats a US-origin pet as needing the titre test and 3-month wait, or only the 21-day post-vaccination wait, is not clearly stated in the public guidance we could check. Confirm this directly with the Veterinary Directorate or a USDA-accredited vet before you set a travel date, since guessing wrong here costs three months, not three days.
⚠ Air Serbia has no cargo hold pet option on any route. A dog above the 8kg combined cabin weight cannot use the direct JFK-Belgrade flight regardless of how the paperwork works out, and needs a Frankfurt or Istanbul cargo routing instead.
⚠ Build the USDA export certificate to Serbia's own import format from the start. A generic US export certificate not matched to Serbia's requirements can be rejected on arrival, so tell your USDA-accredited vet the destination before the exam.
Carriers

Airlines on this route

Air Serbia
Flies New York JFK to Belgrade (BEG) direct, on Airbus A330 equipment, one of the few genuine non-stops between the US and the Balkans. Cabin only: dogs and cats up to 8kg combined weight with the carrier, carrier capped at 40x30x24cm, fee around EUR 130. No cargo hold pet service exists on Air Serbia at all, so this option is closed to any dog above the cabin limit.
Cabin and Hold
Lufthansa Cargo
Routes a larger pet from a US gateway to Frankfurt (FRA), then a short onward cargo leg into Belgrade. Booked through a registered freight forwarder or pet transport agent, travelling in a pressurised, temperature-controlled hold.
Cargo Only
Turkish Airlines Cargo
Routes via Istanbul (IST) from a US gateway, then a short hop to Belgrade. Maximum crate size 125x75x70cm under current Turkish Airlines cargo limits, and its US network reaches more American cities directly than most European alternatives.
Cargo Only

What Serbia requires

The path into Serbia is straightforward to follow once you know the steps. Give the time-sensitive items priority and check the details below.

Avoiding the common mistakes

Ask about temperature embargoes for your travel window. Summer and winter cargo restrictions can rule out certain routes and dates entirely.

Book the titre test laboratory well ahead. Approved labs have limited slots and the sample takes time to process, so this is not something to leave to the final weeks.

Microchip before you vaccinate. The microchip must be in place before the rabies shot for the vaccination to count, and getting this order wrong is the most common and costly mistake.

Use an official, government-authorised vet for the health certificate, not just your usual clinic. Many destinations only accept certificates signed by an approved vet.
