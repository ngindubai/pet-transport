Checklist

What your pet needs

Every item below must be completed and verified before your pet can travel. Expand each category for the detail.

Microchip
Required. ISO 11784/11785 (15-digit). Before rabies vaccination.
Rabies vaccination
Required. Administered after microchipping. At least 21 days before titre test blood draw.
Rabies titre test
Required. Rwanda is EU-unlisted. FAVN titre test at an EU-approved laboratory. Minimum 0.5 IU/ml. Blood drawn at least 30 days after vaccination. 90-day wait from blood draw before entering Portugal. Source: EU Regulation 576/2013.
Quarantine
No quarantine for compliant arrivals. DGAV inspection at Lisbon Airport cargo terminal.
Import permit
Not required for personal companion pets.
Health certificate
EU-format health certificate endorsed by origin authority. Valid 10 days from examination date. Inspected by DGAV officials at LIS.
Export permit
No formal export permit required for personal companion pets.
Health certificate
EU-format health certificate from an RAB-authorised veterinarian, endorsed by Rwanda Agriculture Board (RAB) before departure.

Critical points

The 90-day wait runs from the blood draw date, not from when the lab issues the titre result. This is the single most common planning error on this route.

The microchip must be implanted before the rabies vaccination. Reverse the order and the vaccination doesn't count for the titre test sequence.

A titre result under 0.5 IU/ml means re-vaccination and a fresh blood draw, resetting the 90-day wait from the new draw date.

DGAV checks documentation at Lisbon's cargo terminal, not at passenger arrivals. The health certificate must be in EU format and valid within its 10-day window on arrival.

The rules for bringing a pet into Portugal

Portugal has a detailed set of import rules, and each one needs to be satisfied before travel. None of it is unusual, but the order and timing are what catch people out.

How to keep this move on track

Book the titre test laboratory well ahead. Approved labs have limited slots and the sample takes time to process, so this is not something to leave to the final weeks.

Microchip before you vaccinate. The microchip must be in place before the rabies shot for the vaccination to count, and getting this order wrong is the most common and costly mistake.

Use an official, government-authorised vet for the health certificate, not just your usual clinic. Many destinations only accept certificates signed by an approved vet.

If this is your first international move, consider an IPATA-registered pet transport agent. They handle the documents, airline booking and crate sizing, and can troubleshoot problems.
