Requirements

What your pet needs to enter Seychelles

Every item below must be in place before your pet can travel. We manage and verify each one.

Microchip
Microchip number must be declared on the National Biosecurity Agency (NBA) application. Industry practice points to ISO 11784/11785 compliance, though the current NBA document doesn't restate the standard explicitly.
Rabies vaccination
Minimum age 3 months for an inactivated rabies vaccine. Booster given 30 days to 12 months before export. All vaccinations, not just rabies, must be given at least 2 weeks before departure. Minimum age to travel is 16 weeks.
Rabies titre test
Required. Canada's internationally recognised status is 'rabies-controlled', not rabies-free, so it doesn't sit anywhere near the short list of countries Seychelles treats as low-risk, and there's none of the documentation ambiguity that complicates a genuinely rabies-free origin such as Australia. Blood drawn at least 30 days after vaccination, tested within 6 months of departure, result above 0.5 IU. Entry is only permitted 3 months after the blood draw date, a fixed wait. Source: NBA, 'Importation of Cats and Dogs,' 2024.
Quarantine
Mandatory by law, no exceptions. Compliant pets from a rabies-affected origin, which includes Canada, face a minimum of 4 weeks (28 days) at Seychelles' single quarantine facility on Mahe. Non-compliant pets face a minimum of 6 months quarantine or return to origin at the owner's expense.
Import permit
Required from the NBA, issued within 7 working days of a complete application and valid for 1 month from the intended arrival date. Apply at least 4 months ahead given the rabies-affected-origin pathway. A second Ministry of Finance import permit is needed to clear customs on arrival.
Health certificate
Health certificate in English or French, confirming microchip, vaccination and titre result. Dogs also need clearance for Brucella canis, Trypanosoma evansi, Babesia gibsoni, Leishmania and Erlichia canis unless the exporting country's vet certifies the country free of these diseases.
Export permit (Canada)
No CFIA export permit for a personal pet. CFIA does not regulate a private pet leaving the country the way it regulates imports, so the paperwork centres on the health certificate rather than a permit application. Source: CFIA, 'Exporting animals from Canada', inspection.canada.ca, accessed July 2026.
Costs

What this route typically costs

CFIA-accredited vet exam and health certificate, with CFIA endorsement: CAD 150-300.
Titre test and laboratory fee: CAD 200-380.
NBA import permit and Ministry of Finance customs permit: fees set at application.
Quarantine facility, approximately 4 weeks: SR 1,000 for the first 2 weeks, plus SR 500 per week thereafter.
IATA-compliant travel crate: CAD 150-450.
Cargo, Toronto or Vancouver to Mahe via Dubai or Abu Dhabi: CAD 3,200-6,200.
Total typical range: CAD 4,000-8,800 over 5-7 months, including the mandatory wait and quarantine.

Critical points to read before you book

Canada's rabies status is 'rabies-controlled', not rabies-free, so there's no equivalent to the documentation ambiguity that complicates this site's Australia-to-Seychelles route. The full titre-test pathway is simply the one that applies here, without a lighter alternative worth chasing.

The 3-month wait after the titre blood draw is fixed, and the NBA's own guidance recommends applying for the import permit at least 4 months ahead. Budget the better part of six months from a standing start.

Quarantine is mandatory by law for every pet arriving in Seychelles, with no exception for a compliant Canadian-origin animal: roughly 4 weeks at the single Mahe facility, and non-compliant pets face up to 6 months.

Cabin and Cargo

Seychelles import rules, explained

Seychelles has a detailed set of import rules, and each one needs to be satisfied before travel. None of it is unusual, but the order and timing are what catch people out.

Practical tips for this move

Keep every original document together and take copies. Border officials want originals, and a missing signature or date can hold up clearance.

Work backwards from your travel date and add a buffer. Fixed waiting periods mean a single slipped appointment can push the whole move back by weeks.

Check the airline’s crate size and labelling rules early. An undersized or wrongly labelled crate is a frequent reason pets are refused at check-in.

Ask about temperature embargoes for your travel window. Summer and winter cargo restrictions can rule out certain routes and dates entirely.
