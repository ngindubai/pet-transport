Step by step

The import process, in full

1
Before any other preparation.
Check Malaysia's current DAFF group status via BICON at agriculture.gov.au. This confirms whether a direct move or Singapore staging is required.

Responsible: Owner or agent

2
3-4 months before planned travel.
Microchip, DVS export permit, and rabies vaccination. If staging via Singapore: start Singapore preparation in parallel (titre test, AVS licence, appoint AVS-recognised pet agent).

Responsible: Owner and DVS-registered vet

3
On arrival in Singapore, or on Australia-bound booking date.
If staging via Singapore: pet arrives SIN, completes 30-day AQC quarantine. If direct (DAFF-approved): apply for DAFF permit and proceed directly.

Responsible: Pet agent or owner

4
During Singapore stay.
If via Singapore: complete 180-day continuous residency. Apply for DAFF import permit. Pre-book Mickleham.

Responsible: Owner

5
Within 5 days of travel.
DAFF-format health certificate issued within 5 days of Australia departure.

Responsible: Government vet (Singapore or Malaysia depending on pathway)

6
After confirmed preparation pathway completed.
Travel to Australia. 10-day Mickleham quarantine.

Responsible: Airline and DAFF

Checklist

What your pet needs

Every item below must be completed and verified before your pet can travel. Expand each category for the detail.

Microchip
ISO 11784/11785 microchip required. Must predate all vaccinations.
Quarantine
Mandatory 10-day quarantine at Mickleham Post Entry Quarantine Facility, Melbourne. All imported cats and dogs quarantine at Mickleham. Pre-book in advance.
Import permit
DAFF import permit via BICON (agriculture.gov.au). Approximately AUD 420. Allow 20-30 days processing.
Health certificate
DAFF-format health certificate issued within 5 days of departure for Australia.
Export permit
DVS export permit required for pets leaving Malaysia. Apply through your regional DVS office. Allow 2-4 weeks.
Health certificate
Health certificate issued by a DVS (Department of Veterinary Services, Jabatan Perkhidmatan Veterinar)-registered veterinarian and endorsed by DVS. Required for export and for transit country documentation.

Airlines

Carriers on this route

Not all airlines accept live animals on this route. Confirm the operating carrier's current policy for every flight segment.

Malaysia Airlines
Malaysia Airlines operates Kuala Lumpur (KUL) to Melbourne (MEL) and Sydney (SYD). Malaysia Airlines Cargo handles pets. If Malaysia is confirmed as a DAFF approved country, this direct routing is relevant. Confirm Malaysia's DAFF group status via BICON before booking.
Cargo Only
Malaysia Airlines (KUL to Singapore)
Malaysia Airlines also operates KUL to SIN for the transit staging route. Short flight, frequent departures.
Cargo Only
Singapore Airlines (Singapore to Australia)
Singapore Airlines operates SIN to SYD and MEL daily. Singapore Airlines Cargo handles live animals at Changi with temperature-controlled hold. Primary cargo option for the Singapore to Australia leg.
Cargo Only
Qantas (Singapore or direct KUL to Australia)
Qantas Freight handles pet cargo on Australia-bound services. If routing via Singapore, Qantas operates SIN to SYD and MEL. Confirm current routing options and live animal acceptance before booking.
Cargo Only
Costs

What this route typically costs

DVS Malaysia export permit and health certificate: MYR 200-600
If staging via Singapore: Singapore AQC quarantine: SGD 1,500-3,000 (approximately AUD 1,700-3,400)
DAFF Australia import permit: AUD 420
Air cargo to Australia (direct KUL or via SIN): approximately AUD 1,200-3,000
Mickleham 10-day quarantine: AUD 1,800-3,500
Total direct animal costs: approximately AUD 5,000-9,500 (varies by pathway)

Critical points

Always verify Malaysia's current DAFF classification via BICON before starting any preparation. Country classifications can change. Do not assume approved or non-approved status without checking.

If Malaysia is non-approved: the 180-day Singapore residency is mandatory and cannot be shortened.

Mickleham does not operate on weekends or Australian public holidays. Time your travel so your pet's arrival falls on a weekday.

If travelling via Singapore from April 2026: an AVS-recognised pet agent is required for CAPQ clearance. Appoint one before the Malaysia-to-Singapore leg.

The rules for bringing a pet into Australia

Australia asks for several pieces of paperwork, and they all need to line up. A checklist and a clear timeline are your best tools here.

How to keep this move on track

Keep every original document together and take copies. Border officials want originals, and a missing signature or date can hold up clearance.

Work backwards from your travel date and add a buffer. Fixed waiting periods mean a single slipped appointment can push the whole move back by weeks.

Check the airline’s crate size and labelling rules early. An undersized or wrongly labelled crate is a frequent reason pets are refused at check-in.

Ask about temperature embargoes for your travel window. Summer and winter cargo restrictions can rule out certain routes and dates entirely.

FAQ

Common questions

Use the BICON (Biosecurity Import Conditions) database at agriculture.gov.au. Search for Malaysia under cats and dogs import conditions. BICON shows the current approved group status and the specific requirements that apply. Always check BICON directly rather than relying on secondary sources, as classifications can change.
Malaysia Airlines operates KUL to MEL and SYD with cargo services. If Malaysia is a DAFF approved country, this direct route is a practical option. If Malaysia is non-approved, the direct route cannot be used, and staging via Singapore is required. Check BICON before making any airline bookings.
Mickleham Post Entry Quarantine Facility in Melbourne is the only DAFF-approved centre for imported cats and dogs. All arriving pets spend at least 10 days there regardless of origin or transit country. The facility is staffed by vets throughout. Owners can visit. Costs are owner-paid: approximately AUD 1,800-3,500 for 10 days depending on animal size.
If Malaysia is approved and a direct move is possible: approximately 4-6 months (DVS export prep, DAFF permit, Mickleham booking, health certificate). If the Singapore staged route is needed: approximately 9-13 months (Singapore prep, 30-day AQC quarantine, 150 more Singapore days, Australia travel, Mickleham).
