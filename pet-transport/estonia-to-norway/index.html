Checklist

What your pet needs

Every item below must be completed and verified before your pet can travel. Expand each category for the detail.

Microchip
Required. ISO 11784/11785 (15-digit). Must be implanted before or on the same day as first rabies vaccination.
Rabies vaccination
Required. Current and at least 21 days old after a primary vaccination. Boosters within the valid period need no additional wait. Norway follows the same rules as the EU. Source: Mattilsynet, mattilsynet.no.
Rabies titre test
Not required. Estonia is an EU member state and is a listed country for Norway. No titre test needed.
Quarantine
No routine quarantine for compliant arrivals with correct documentation.
Import permit
Not required for compliant pets from listed countries.
Health certificate
EU pet passport accepted by Norway as the entry document. Valid for 10 days from issue.
Export permit
No export permit required for companion animals travelling from Estonia.
Health certificate
EU pet passport issued by an official veterinarian in Estonia, authorised by the Pohllumajandus- ja Toiduamet (Agricultural Board of Estonia). Sweden, Norway, Denmark and Finland all accept the EU pet passport. The passport records your pet's microchip, rabies vaccination history, and issuing vet details.

Airlines

Carriers on this route

Not all airlines accept live animals on this route. Confirm the operating carrier's current policy for every flight segment.

SAS
SAS connects Tallinn (TLL) to Oslo Gardermoen (OSL) via Stockholm (ARN) or Copenhagen (CPH). SAS Cargo is the practical option for larger dogs travelling in the hold. For cabin pets, SAS allows small animals in approved carriers under the seat on European routes.
Cargo and Cabin
Norwegian Air Shuttle
Norwegian operates direct TLL-OSL services on some schedules. Cabin only for small pets. No cargo hold option for animals. Suitable for cats and small dogs meeting the carrier size and weight limits. Book the pet space at the same time as your ticket.
Cabin Only
airBaltic
airBaltic connects Tallinn (TLL) via Riga (RIX) to Oslo with live animal cargo available on longer legs. A practical option if SAS direct cargo is unavailable or fully booked. Check connections carefully to minimise layover time for animals in hold.
Cargo and Cabin

Critical points

All dogs entering Norway from any country outside Norway must receive a tapeworm treatment using Praziquantel. This must happen between 1 and 5 days before arrival and must be recorded in the EU pet passport by a vet. Cats are exempt from this requirement. Source: Mattilsynet, mattilsynet.no.

Microchip must be implanted before or on the same day as the first rabies vaccination. If the vaccination predates the microchip, Norway may not accept the vaccination as valid.

Norway is not an EU member state but follows EU pet travel rules as part of the EEA and Schengen area. The EU pet passport is accepted. The rules are aligned with those in force within the EU.

For cargo dogs, SAS via a Scandinavian hub is the most practical option. airBaltic via Riga also carries live animals in hold on connections to Oslo.

How Norway handles incoming pets

Norway has a modest set of rules compared with stricter destinations. Get the essentials in place and confirm the finer points below.

What experienced owners do

Confirm live animal acceptance on every flight segment before booking. Not all aircraft or connections take pets, and cargo terms can differ leg to leg.

Keep every original document together and take copies. Border officials want originals, and a missing signature or date can hold up clearance.

Work backwards from your travel date and add a buffer. Fixed waiting periods mean a single slipped appointment can push the whole move back by weeks.

Check the airline’s crate size and labelling rules early. An undersized or wrongly labelled crate is a frequent reason pets are refused at check-in.
