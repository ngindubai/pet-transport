Requirements

What your pet needs to enter Australia

Every item below must be in place before your pet can travel. We manage and verify each one.

Microchip
ISO 11784/11785 microchip, implanted before the rabies vaccination used for the RNATT titre test.
Rabies vaccination
Current rabies vaccination administered or reconfirmed during the South African residency.
Rabies titre test
RNATT required, sample sent to an accredited laboratory, in practice usually the Onderstepoort Veterinary Institute in Pretoria, arriving between 180 days and 12 months before the Australia-bound flight. Source: DAFF.
Quarantine
Mandatory minimum 10-day quarantine at Mickleham Post Entry Quarantine Facility, Melbourne, weekday arrivals only. The 10-day minimum requires the microchip to be officially verified before the RNATT draw and at least 180 days before export; otherwise the stay extends to 30 days. Source: DAFF.
Import permit
DAFF import permit via BICON, applied for during the South African residency once the titre result is confirmed. Fee: AUD 420.
Health certificate
DAFF-format health certificate from a South African vet, valid 5 days from issue, dated at the end of the residency period.
Export permit (Eswatini)
Export permit from the Department of Veterinary and Livestock Services, Ministry of Agriculture, required for the initial move out of Eswatini. Apply at least 6 weeks ahead.
Government endorsement
Health certificate endorsed by the Department of Veterinary and Livestock Services before any airline accepts the booking.
Costs

What this route typically costs

Department of Veterinary and Livestock Services permit and Eswatini vet fees: SZL 800-2,000 (roughly USD 45-115).
RNATT titre test in South Africa, typically via Onderstepoort: USD 90-250.
Cost of living in South Africa during the 180-day residency: highly variable, not a fixed transport cost.
DAFF import permit via BICON: AUD 420.
Mickleham 10-day quarantine (if secured): AUD 1,800-3,500.
Cargo fares, Manzini to Johannesburg then Johannesburg to Perth: USD 2,200-4,200 combined.
Total direct transport and processing costs, excluding South African living costs: USD 4,600-8,100.

Critical points to read before you book

There is no international long-haul carrier based in Eswatini. Every pet, regardless of destination, transits Johannesburg first. This route is no exception, and it turns out to be the reason South Africa doubles as both the transit hub and the required staging country.

Eswatini's non-approved DAFF status means the 180-day residency is not optional. No agent shortcuts it.

Get the microchip verification done before the RNATT blood draw and at least 180 days before export, or Mickleham's quarantine triples from 10 to 30 days.

The same pet moving to the UAE instead needs only a titre test and an import permit, no residency requirement at all. DAFF and MOCCAE read Eswatini's rabies risk very differently.

Australia entry requirements at a glance

Entry to Australia is tightly controlled, and officials verify each requirement in turn. Treat it as a sequence where every step gates the next.

Getting the details right

Confirm live animal acceptance on every flight segment before booking. Not all aircraft or connections take pets, and cargo terms can differ leg to leg.

Keep every original document together and take copies. Border officials want originals, and a missing signature or date can hold up clearance.

Work backwards from your travel date and add a buffer. Fixed waiting periods mean a single slipped appointment can push the whole move back by weeks.

Check the airline’s crate size and labelling rules early. An undersized or wrongly labelled crate is a frequent reason pets are refused at check-in.
