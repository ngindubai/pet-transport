Requirements

Singapore entry requirements

Every item below must be in place before your pet can enter. We verify and track each one.

Microchip
ISO 11784/11785 or ISO 11785 Annex A compliant microchip, fitted before the rabies vaccination that starts the serology-test clock.
Rabies vaccination
Required before export. Uzbekistan does not appear on AVS Schedule I or Schedule II, so it falls under Schedule III, and pets are also vaccinated against rabies again on arrival in Singapore regardless of the Uzbek record. Source: AVS, avs.nparks.gov.sg, fetched July 2026.
Rabies titre test
A rabies serology test, not a lower-bar titre check, is required: blood sampling for rabies serology at least 90 days and within 12 months before export, from a WOAH reference laboratory or an approved laboratory in a Schedule I or II country. No APHA- or FAVN-approved laboratories operate inside Uzbekistan, so the sample has to travel abroad, adding real time on top of standard lab turnaround.
Quarantine
Minimum 30 days at the Animal Quarantine Centre (AQC). Current fees, in force to 30 November 2026: SGD 26 a day fan-cooled, SGD 35 a day air-conditioned, plus a SGD 75 airport transfer fee.
Import permit
AVS import licence via the GoBusiness Licensing Portal, applied for within 90 days of arrival. SGD 50 standard, SGD 100 express.
Health certificate
Territorial veterinary service certificate, completed between 2 and 7 days of the export date. Book the CAPQ inspection at least 5 days ahead of arrival; a walk-in inspection costs SGD 133 an hour.
Leaving Uzbekistan

Export requirements

Export permit
No separate export permit for a single companion pet. The owner applies to the territorial veterinary service in the region of departure, which issues the export veterinary certificate directly, exempt from the extra Chief State Veterinary Inspector sign-off larger commercial shipments need.
Health certificate
Export veterinary certificate from the territorial veterinary service, processed through the Committee's electronic system and finalised at the border veterinary checkpoint, reformatted to meet AVS Singapore's certificate requirements.

Critical points

No APHA-, FAVN- or WOAH-approved laboratory operates inside Uzbekistan for this test, so the blood sample has to be shipped abroad, adding transit time beyond the standard laboratory turnaround.

Uzbekistan is not on AVS Schedule I or Schedule II, so it defaults to Schedule III: the serology test, the 90-day-to-12-month result window, and 30 days at the AQC, plus revaccination against rabies on arrival.

No published fee schedule exists for Uzbekistan's territorial veterinary service export certificate. Confirm current cost and timing directly rather than assuming a figure.

Turkish Airlines is currently the only single-carrier routing between Tashkent and Singapore. Confirm live animal cargo space on both the TAS-IST and IST-SIN legs separately.

Singapore entry requirements at a glance

The requirements for Singapore are thorough rather than difficult. Work through them methodically and confirm anything you are unsure about with the official source.

Getting the details right

Microchip before you vaccinate. The microchip must be in place before the rabies shot for the vaccination to count, and getting this order wrong is the most common and costly mistake.

Use an official, government-authorised vet for the health certificate, not just your usual clinic. Many destinations only accept certificates signed by an approved vet.

If this is your first international move, consider an IPATA-registered pet transport agent. They handle the documents, airline booking and crate sizing, and can troubleshoot problems.

Get your pet used to the travel crate weeks in advance. A calm, crate-familiar animal travels far better than one meeting the crate for the first time at the airport.
