Requirements

Algeria entry requirements

Every item below must be in place before your pet can enter. We verify and track each one.

Microchip
15-digit ISO 11784/11785 microchip required, implanted before the rabies vaccination and matching every document presented on arrival.
Rabies vaccination
Current rabies vaccination required, given between 30 days and 12 months before entry. Puppies and kittens under three months old cannot be vaccinated and are not permitted entry. Source: APHIS and pettravel.com, Algeria pet import requirements, cross-checked 17 July 2026.
Rabies titre test
Not required to enter Algeria. A titre test only becomes relevant later, if the pet is ever brought back into the US, since Algeria sits on the CDC's high-risk list for dog rabies for that return journey, an entirely separate American classification that has no bearing on entry into Algeria itself.
Quarantine
No mandatory quarantine for a pet arriving with complete, compliant documentation. Non-compliant pets can face quarantine where facilities exist, forced return, or in the worst case euthanasia, all at the importer's expense.
Import permit
No standalone import permit is required for a pet dog or cat travelling with its owner, per both APHIS and independently published Algerian import guidance.
Health certificate
The APHIS Form 7001, in French or Arabic or with a certified translation attached, examined against the microchip and vaccination record on arrival. Must be issued and endorsed within 10 days of departure, and Algeria separately expects the underlying document to be no older than 14 days from a licensed vet's examination.
Leaving United States

Export requirements

Export permit
No export permit required from the US side for companion animals.
Health certificate
USDA APHIS Veterinary Health Certificate (VS Form 7001) completed by a USDA-accredited veterinarian and endorsed by the USDA APHIS State Veterinarian, issued and endorsed within 10 days of departure. Allow 3-5 business days for endorsement. Source: APHIS, 'Pet Travel From the United States to Algeria,' aphis.usda.gov, checked 17 July 2026.

What this route typically costs

Certified French or Arabic translation of the certificate: USD 60-150
Cargo US to Algiers via Paris or Istanbul: USD 1,500-3,400 depending on pet weight and crate size
IATA-approved travel crate: USD 80-300 depending on size

Total typical range: USD 2,400-5,700 over 6-10 weeks preparation

Critical points

APHIS's own guidance for this destination states plainly that pets must be dewormed before export. It's an easy step to miss since so few other destinations on this site require it, and a certificate missing the record can hold up clearance at the Algerian end.

Algeria's breed position is inconsistent across sources: some describe Pit Bull-types and Staffordshire Bull Terriers as liable to be refused entry outright, others don't mention a restriction at all. Confirm the current position with DSV or the Algerian consulate directly if this could affect your dog, well before booking a flight.

The 30-day-to-12-month rabies vaccination window is wider than several comparable destinations but still has a floor. A vaccination given less than 30 days before arrival, or one that's aged past 12 months, will not clear the border.

Requirements can change. Verify current import conditions with DSV directly before finalising travel plans.

How Algeria handles incoming pets

Algeria has a clear set of import requirements that are manageable with some planning. Follow them in order and keep an eye on the dates that cannot move.

What experienced owners do

Check the airline’s crate size and labelling rules early. An undersized or wrongly labelled crate is a frequent reason pets are refused at check-in.

Ask about temperature embargoes for your travel window. Summer and winter cargo restrictions can rule out certain routes and dates entirely.

Microchip before you vaccinate. The microchip must be in place before the rabies shot for the vaccination to count, and getting this order wrong is the most common and costly mistake.

Use an official, government-authorised vet for the health certificate, not just your usual clinic. Many destinations only accept certificates signed by an approved vet.
