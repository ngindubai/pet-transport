Requirements

Georgia entry requirements

Every item below must be in place before your pet can enter. We verify and track each one.

Microchip
Required. ISO 11784/11785 (15-digit). Readable on arrival at Tbilisi cargo terminal.
Rabies vaccination
Required. Current and valid rabies vaccination at time of arrival. EU-vaccinated pets are accepted without additional vaccination requirements.
Rabies titre test
Not required for pets arriving from Belgium. Belgium as an EU country is not subject to Georgia's titre test requirement for personal companion pet imports.
Quarantine
No quarantine for compliant pet arrivals with valid FASFC-endorsed health certificate and current vaccination records.
Import permit
No advance import permit required for personal companion pets. Customs and veterinary inspection at Tbilisi International Airport (TBS) on arrival.
Health certificate
FASFC-endorsed EU-format health certificate covering microchip, vaccination, and health status. Presented to Georgian National Food Agency (NFSA) inspectors on arrival.
Leaving Belgium

Export requirements

Export permit
No formal export permit required for personal companion pets departing Belgium.
Veterinary inspection
Clinical examination by an FASFC-authorised vet confirming the pet is fit to travel and free of infectious disease signs.
Health certificate
EU-format official health certificate prepared by a FASFC-authorised vet, endorsed by FASFC before departure. Georgia accepts EU-endorsed certificates for pet import. Valid 10 days from examination.

Critical points

Georgia does not require a titre test from EU-origin pets. However, confirm requirements have not changed since this guide was published. The NFSA (nfa.gov.ge) publishes current import conditions.

The FASFC health certificate is valid 10 days from examination. Do not complete the examination too early, or you risk the certificate expiring before travel.

There is a direct Wizz Air service from Brussels to Tbilisi that accepts small cabin pets. This is the simplest option for qualifying small animals and keeps the pet with you.

Confirm whether your chosen hub carrier applies breed restrictions before booking. Brachycephalic breeds face restrictions on Emirates and some Turkish Airlines routes.

Georgia import rules, explained

Georgia has a clear set of import requirements that are manageable with some planning. Follow them in order and keep an eye on the dates that cannot move.

Practical tips for this move

Get your pet used to the travel crate weeks in advance. A calm, crate-familiar animal travels far better than one meeting the crate for the first time at the airport.

Confirm live animal acceptance on every flight segment before booking. Not all aircraft or connections take pets, and cargo terms can differ leg to leg.

Keep every original document together and take copies. Border officials want originals, and a missing signature or date can hold up clearance.

Work backwards from your travel date and add a buffer. Fixed waiting periods mean a single slipped appointment can push the whole move back by weeks.

FAQ

Common questions about this route

No titre test is required for pets from Belgium entering Georgia. A valid FASFC-endorsed EU health certificate with current microchip and rabies vaccination is the main requirement.
Wizz Air operates a direct Brussels to Tbilisi route and accepts small dogs in the cabin if they meet weight limits (typically under 8kg including carrier). Larger dogs must travel as cargo via a hub such as Istanbul or Frankfurt.
The National Food Agency (NFSA) of Georgia (nfa.gov.ge) conducts veterinary inspections at Tbilisi International Airport for arriving pets. They check the EU health certificate and the pet’s physical condition.
The EU-format health certificate endorsed by FASFC is valid for 10 days from the date of the veterinary examination. Travel must take place within that window.
Istanbul (IST) via Turkish Airlines and Frankfurt (FRA) via Lufthansa Cargo are the most practical hub options. Both have established pet cargo handling and onward connections to Tbilisi.
