Requirements

Dominican Republic entry requirements

Every item below must be in place before your pet can enter. We verify and track each one.

Microchip
Required (ISO 11784/11785)
Rabies vaccination
Required. Must be current at time of travel.
Quarantine
No routine quarantine if documentation is complete. Inspection on arrival at Las Americas International Airport (SDQ) or Puerto Plata (POP).
Import permit
Required. Issued by the Dominican Republic Ministry of Agriculture (MIDA - Ministerio de Agricultura). Apply at least 4-6 weeks before travel.
Health certificate
Required. Issued by APHA-authorised Official Vet in the UK and endorsed by the Animal and Plant Health Agency. DR authorities require an official government-endorsed certificate.
Leaving United Kingdom

Export requirements

Export permit
No general export permit required. An Animal Health Certificate (AHC) issued by a UK APHA-authorised Official Vet is required for export.
Health certificate
Animal Health Certificate (AHC) issued by an APHA-authorised Official Vet. Valid for 10 days from the date of the veterinary examination. Must cover microchip, current rabies vaccination, and health status.

Critical points

The Brucellosis test for dogs must return a negative result within 30 days of travel. Timing matters.

The DR import permit from MIDA takes 4-6 weeks. Do not leave this until late in your preparation.

If routing via the USA, your pet will need to meet US APHIS import requirements as a transit animal. This adds complexity. Consider the Spain routing via Iberia to avoid this.

Always confirm current DR MIDA requirements with the authority or an experienced pet transport agent before finalising paperwork.

What Dominican Republic requires

The path into Dominican Republic is straightforward to follow once you know the steps. Give the time-sensitive items priority and check the details below.

Avoiding the common mistakes

Keep every original document together and take copies. Border officials want originals, and a missing signature or date can hold up clearance.

Work backwards from your travel date and add a buffer. Fixed waiting periods mean a single slipped appointment can push the whole move back by weeks.

Check the airline’s crate size and labelling rules early. An undersized or wrongly labelled crate is a frequent reason pets are refused at check-in.

Ask about temperature embargoes for your travel window. Summer and winter cargo restrictions can rule out certain routes and dates entirely.

Common questions about this route

You need current rabies vaccination, microchip, Brucellosis test (for dogs), import permit from MIDA, and an official UK Animal Health Certificate. Always confirm with MIDA before travel as requirements can change.
You apply to the Dominican Republic Ministry of Agriculture (MIDA, agricultura.gob.do). The permit specifies the conditions your pet must meet and is required before travel. Allow 4-6 weeks for processing. Working with a pet transport agent who regularly handles DR imports can speed this process up.
There is no direct UK-to-Dominican Republic commercial flight that carries pet cargo. The most commonly used routing is via Madrid on Iberia or Air Europa. American Airlines connections via Miami are possible but introduce US APHIS transit requirements, adding complexity.
Brucella canis is a bacterial infection. The Dominican Republic requires dogs to have a negative Brucellosis blood test within 30 days of travel. Cats are not required to have this test. Ask your UK vet to arrange the test and ensure the result is included in your documentation.
APHA-authorised Official Vets are listed through the APHA official vet finder and the RCVS register. Not every vet practice has an OV. Book early - some OVs have limited appointment slots and you need the certificate within 10 days of your travel date.
