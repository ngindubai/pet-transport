Critical points

No airline flies Dubai or Abu Dhabi to Nadi direct. Every pet on this route needs at least one live-animal transfer, most realistically via Brisbane on Emirates or Fiji Airways, or via Sydney on Qantas, arranged as manifest cargo and confirmed on both legs before booking dates.

The 30-day Koronivia quarantine applies to the UAE under BAF's Group B framework, whatever the pet's own health record shows. It is not a penalty for weak paperwork.

BAF import permits run for 6 months from issue. Apply once the titre result is confirmed, not before, or the permit risks lapsing before the flight is booked.

Fiji import rules, explained

Fiji runs a strict import regime, so every document is checked closely on arrival. Build your plan around the fixed waiting periods, because they set the pace for everything else.

Practical tips for this move

Book the titre test laboratory well ahead. Approved labs have limited slots and the sample takes time to process, so this is not something to leave to the final weeks.

Microchip before you vaccinate. The microchip must be in place before the rabies shot for the vaccination to count, and getting this order wrong is the most common and costly mistake.

Use an official, government-authorised vet for the health certificate, not just your usual clinic. Many destinations only accept certificates signed by an approved vet.

If this is your first international move, consider an IPATA-registered pet transport agent. They handle the documents, airline booking and crate sizing, and can troubleshoot problems.
