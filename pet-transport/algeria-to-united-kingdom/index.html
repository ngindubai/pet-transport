Requirements

United Kingdom entry requirements

Every item below must be in place before your pet can enter. We verify and track each one.

Microchip
ISO 11784/11785 microchip implanted before the rabies vaccination used for the titre test; vaccinating first invalidates the sequence. Source: GOV.UK, 'Bringing your pet dog, cat or ferret to Great Britain,' 2026.
Rabies vaccination
Current rabies vaccination required. Algeria does not appear on either of GB's listed-country tables, so vaccination alone doesn't clear the pet; it starts the clock for the titre test. Source: GOV.UK, 'Bringing your pet dog, cat or ferret to Great Britain: Which pet travel document you need,' 2026.
Rabies titre test
Rabies antibody blood test taken no sooner than 30 full days after vaccination, result at least 0.5 IU/ml, run by an approved laboratory. Source: GOV.UK, 'Bringing your pet dog, cat or ferret to Great Britain: Blood tests for rabies,' 2026.
Quarantine
No routine quarantine for a pet that meets the unlisted-country rules in full. Quarantine applies only alongside an APHA import licence for a non-compliant animal. Source: GOV.UK, 2026.
Import permit
No APHA import licence needed for a compliant pet under the standard unlisted-country process. Source: GOV.UK, 2026.
Health certificate
Great Britain Pet Health Certificate, issued by an official or accredited vet, valid 10 days from issue to arrival. Source: GOV.UK, 2026.
Leaving Algeria

Export requirements

Export permit
No separate export permit is published specifically for a pet leaving Algeria; the endorsed health certificate below is the controlling document.
Veterinary inspection
Examination by a licensed Algerian vet confirming the animal is fit to travel and free from disease, close to the departure date.
Government endorsement
The vet's certificate is endorsed by the Direction des Services Vétérinaires (DSV), within Algeria's Ministry of Agriculture and Rural Development, before export.
Health certificate
Official veterinary health certificate in French or Arabic (or accompanied by a certified translation), covering identification, vaccination record and fitness to travel.
Costs

What this route typically costs

Algerian vet visits, vaccination, blood draw, DSV-endorsed certificate and translation combined: roughly EUR 280-600, no fixed government tariff published
Rabies titre test and laboratory fee: EUR 150-280
GB Pet Health Certificate: GBP 150-300
IATA-compliant travel crate: GBP 80-300
IPATA agent handling the Air Algérie Cargo, BA/IAG Cargo, or Istanbul freight booking: GBP 800-2,000
Air freight Algiers to Heathrow, direct or via Istanbul: GBP 1,000-2,800 depending on crate size and season

Critical points

Air Algérie explicitly bans pets from travelling in the hold on any flight to or from the UK. Owners assuming they can check a pet in as they would checked baggage on this carrier will be turned away; the booking has to go through Air Algérie Cargo as freight, or with a different carrier entirely.

Algeria is not on either of GB's listed-country tables, so the titre test and 3-calendar-month wait from the blood draw date are mandatory, not optional. Vaccinating and testing out of sequence resets the timeline.

Export paperwork must be in French or Arabic, or carry a certified translation. A certificate produced only in English by an unfamiliar vet can cause delays at the Algerian end that have nothing to do with the animal's health.

The DSV certificate moves independently of the GB titre-test clock. Finishing the Algerian paperwork early creates no shortcut on the British side; the 3-month wait still has to run its course.

What United Kingdom requires

Bringing a pet into United Kingdom involves a fair amount of documentation. Keep everything organised and check each item against the current requirements before you book.

Avoiding the common mistakes

Work backwards from your travel date and add a buffer. Fixed waiting periods mean a single slipped appointment can push the whole move back by weeks.

Check the airline’s crate size and labelling rules early. An undersized or wrongly labelled crate is a frequent reason pets are refused at check-in.

Ask about temperature embargoes for your travel window. Summer and winter cargo restrictions can rule out certain routes and dates entirely.

Book the titre test laboratory well ahead. Approved labs have limited slots and the sample takes time to process, so this is not something to leave to the final weeks.
