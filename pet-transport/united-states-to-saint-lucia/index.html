Checklist

What your pet needs

Every item below must be completed and verified before your pet can travel. Expand each category for the detail.

Microchip
ISO 11784/11785 standard, implanted before the rabies vaccination.
Rabies vaccination
Required, administered at least 30 days before travel.
Rabies titre test
Not required in the guidance available for a US-origin pet with a compliant vaccination record.
Quarantine
No routine quarantine for a pet arriving with a valid import permit and complete paperwork. Entry is restricted to Hewanorra International Airport.
Import permit
Required from the Veterinary and Livestock Services Division. The health certificate itself has to be part of the import permit application, a specific sequencing point: the certificate isn't just presented on arrival, it's needed to get the permit approved in the first place. Applications are typically processed within about 2 business days once all documents are submitted.
Health certificate
USDA-endorsed international health certificate, issued within 14 days of travel, submitted with the import permit application and presented again on arrival.
Export permit
No export permit is required from the US side for a companion animal. Saint Lucia's own import permit, issued by its Veterinary and Livestock Services Division, governs entry instead.
Veterinary inspection
A USDA-accredited veterinarian examines the animal, confirms the microchip and rabies vaccination, and completes the health certificate that Saint Lucia requires as part of the import permit application itself.
Government endorsement
Endorsed by USDA APHIS, in most cases electronically through the Veterinary Export Health Certification System (VEHCS), live since 29 April 2020. Allow 3-5 business days for the endorsement.
Health certificate
USDA APHIS international health certificate, issued within 14 days of travel. Source: aphis.usda.gov/print/pdf/node/3689, checked July 2026.

Airlines

Carriers on this route

Not all airlines accept live animals on this route. Confirm the operating carrier's current policy for every flight segment.

American Airlines
Flies Miami to Hewanorra close to daily, roughly 15 flights a week as of June 2026, the most frequent scheduled service on this route by some distance. A weekly Saturday JFK to Hewanorra flight is also due to return from 20 December 2026. Small pets travel in the cabin under 8kg including carrier; larger dogs go as cargo.
Cabin and Cargo
JetBlue
Runs JFK to Hewanorra year-round nonstop, with a second weekly Saturday flight added from late November through January for the winter peak. Cabin-only, up to roughly 9kg combined pet and carrier weight, so this suits a small pet rather than a larger dog.
Cabin Only
Caribbean Airlines
Connects Saint Lucia onward to Miami, New York and Toronto through its regional network, a useful backup if the direct American or JetBlue schedule doesn't fit. In-cabin acceptance is approved route by route and needs confirming directly.

Costs

What this route typically costs

USDA-accredited vet visits, microchip and rabies vaccination: USD 100-220
USDA APHIS endorsement fee: USD 38 per certificate
Saint Lucia import permit fee: roughly EC $45 (around USD 17), confirm the current rate directly with the Division
IATA-compliant travel crate: USD 100-380
Airfare or cargo fare from a US gateway to Saint Lucia: USD 400-1,800 in-cabin, or USD 1,200-2,800 as cargo, depending on airline and season
Pet transport agent fee, optional on this simpler route: USD 300-900
Total typical range: USD 800-4,600, spanning cabin-only small pet trips through to a full cargo booking for a large dog

Critical points

Saint Lucia bans several bull-type breeds outright, including Pit Bull Terrier, Bull Terrier, American Staffordshire Terrier and related types, and separately restricts Rottweilers. A restriction is a different, and sometimes navigable, condition from an outright ban, but check the current rule for a Rottweiler specifically before assuming standard paperwork covers it.

The health certificate has to be submitted as part of the import permit application, not just shown on arrival. Getting the sequencing wrong, applying for the permit before the certificate exists, delays the whole process.

Entry is restricted to Hewanorra International Airport. George F. L. Charles Airport near Castries is not a compliant entry point for an internationally travelling pet.

JetBlue is cabin-only. A dog too large for an in-cabin carrier needs American Airlines instead, not JetBlue.

Saint Lucia entry requirements at a glance

The requirements for Saint Lucia are light, but not zero. A little planning goes a long way on a route like this.

Getting the details right

Microchip before you vaccinate. The microchip must be in place before the rabies shot for the vaccination to count, and getting this order wrong is the most common and costly mistake.

Use an official, government-authorised vet for the health certificate, not just your usual clinic. Many destinations only accept certificates signed by an approved vet.

If this is your first international move, consider an IPATA-registered pet transport agent. They handle the documents, airline booking and crate sizing, and can troubleshoot problems.

Get your pet used to the travel crate weeks in advance. A calm, crate-familiar animal travels far better than one meeting the crate for the first time at the airport.
