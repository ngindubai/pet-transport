Requirements

What your pet needs to enter Singapore

Every item below must be in place before your pet can travel. We manage and verify each one.

Microchip
Required (ISO 11784/11785). Before vaccination.
Rabies vaccination
Required. At least 30 days before titre test. Within 12 months of export.
Rabies titre test
Required (Category C). FAVN/ELISA at AVS-approved laboratory. Minimum 0.5 IU/ml. Blood drawn 30+ days post-vaccination. 6-month wait from blood draw.
Quarantine
Mandatory. 30 days at AVS-approved Singapore Quarantine Station (SQS). Pre-booking required. Owner pays.
Import permit
Required. AVS import permit via e-service portal. Apply 30+ days before arrival.
Health certificate
DDPP-endorsed health certificate in AVS format. Within 7 days of departure.
Export permit (France)
No formal export permit from France. Official health certificate endorsed by DDPP (Direction Départementale de la Protection des Populations).

Critical points to read before you book

France is Category C for AVS. Titre test + 6-month wait + 30-day quarantine all apply. Total preparation: 36-44 weeks.

SQS quarantine spaces are limited. Book as soon as your travel window is known.

Health certificate window: within 7 days of France departure AND 10 days of Singapore arrival. Both apply.

AVS category for France may be updated. Always verify at nparks.gov.sg/avs before starting the process.

The rules for bringing a pet into Singapore

Singapore takes biosecurity seriously, and the requirements reflect that. The paperwork is exacting, so accuracy matters as much as timing.

How to keep this move on track

Check the airline’s crate size and labelling rules early. An undersized or wrongly labelled crate is a frequent reason pets are refused at check-in.

Ask about temperature embargoes for your travel window. Summer and winter cargo restrictions can rule out certain routes and dates entirely.

Book the titre test laboratory well ahead. Approved labs have limited slots and the sample takes time to process, so this is not something to leave to the final weeks.

Microchip before you vaccinate. The microchip must be in place before the rabies shot for the vaccination to count, and getting this order wrong is the most common and costly mistake.

FAQ

Common questions about France to Singapore pet transport

Singapore’s AVS classifies France as a Category C country. All pets from Category C countries must complete 30 days of quarantine at an AVS-approved Singapore Quarantine Station on arrival, regardless of vaccination or titre test history. This applies even after completing the 6-month pre-travel waiting period.
Allow 36-44 weeks. The FAVN titre test requires blood drawn 30 days post-vaccination, followed by a 6-month wait. Then add the AVS import permit processing (30+ days), cargo booking, and the DDPP health certificate. The 30-day quarantine on arrival is on top of all this.
The AVS import permit is a mandatory Singapore government document required before your pet travels. Apply through the AVS e-service portal at nparks.gov.sg/avs at least 30 days before planned arrival. A licensed agent can manage this process on your behalf.
Air France Cargo and Singapore Airlines Cargo both operate direct CDG-SIN services with live animal capability. Air France has strong cargo infrastructure at Charles de Gaulle; Singapore Airlines has deep familiarity with AVS import procedures at Changi. Either is a sound choice.
