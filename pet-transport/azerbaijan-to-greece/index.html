What your pet needs

Every item below must be completed and verified before your pet can travel. Expand each category for the detail.

Microchip
Required. ISO 11784/11785, before the first rabies vaccination.
Rabies vaccination
Required. Minimum age 12 weeks. 21-day wait before the titre test blood draw.
Rabies titre test
Required. Azerbaijan is not on the EU's approved list under Implementing Regulation (EU) 2026/636. FAVN or ELISA titre test at an approved laboratory, minimum 0.5 IU/ml, drawn at least 30 days after vaccination, then a fixed 90-day wait. Source: Commission Delegated Regulation (EU) 2026/131.
Quarantine
No routine quarantine for compliant arrivals.
Import permit
Not required for non-commercial companion pet movements.
Health certificate
EU-format Animal Health Certificate, endorsed by the State Veterinary Service, issued within 10 days of entry into Greece.
Export permit
No formal export permit required for companion cats and dogs leaving Azerbaijan.
Health certificate
Official export Animal Health Certificate from a State Veterinary Service-authorised vet, endorsed by the State Veterinary Service. Reformatted to EU standard for Greece. Issued within 10 days of travel.

Critical points

The 90-day wait counts from the blood draw date, not the day the result arrives.

Microchip has to precede the rabies vaccination. A reversed order restarts the entire sequence.

The EU-format certificate is valid only 10 days. Time the endorsement to land close to the actual Athens arrival, allowing for the Istanbul connection.

What Greece requires

Getting a pet into Greece is a middle of the road process. The rules are well defined, so most of the work is simply staying on schedule.

Avoiding the common mistakes

Ask about temperature embargoes for your travel window. Summer and winter cargo restrictions can rule out certain routes and dates entirely.

Book the titre test laboratory well ahead. Approved labs have limited slots and the sample takes time to process, so this is not something to leave to the final weeks.

Microchip before you vaccinate. The microchip must be in place before the rabies shot for the vaccination to count, and getting this order wrong is the most common and costly mistake.

Use an official, government-authorised vet for the health certificate, not just your usual clinic. Many destinations only accept certificates signed by an approved vet.
