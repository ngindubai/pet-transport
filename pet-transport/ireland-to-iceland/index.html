Requirements

Iceland entry requirements

Every item below must be in place before your pet can enter. We verify and track each one.

Microchip
ISO 11784/11785 microchip required, implanted before the rabies vaccination. Source: MAST, Guidance Notes D1, updated 1 July 2025.
Rabies vaccination
Primary rabies vaccination required at least 45 to 60 days before the import date. Source: MAST, Guidance Notes D1, updated 1 July 2025.
Rabies titre test
Required regardless of Ireland's rabies-free status. Blood drawn no earlier than 30 days after vaccination, tested at an EU-approved laboratory, result at least 0.5 IU/ml. Ireland sits on MAST's Category 1 list, the same tier as the UK and most of the EU, so once the result clears there is no further fixed wait, unlike Category 2 countries, which face an additional 90-day wait from the blood draw date. Source: MAST, mast.is, 2026.
Quarantine
Mandatory minimum 14 days at one of two approved isolation stations, Einangrunarstoedin i Reykjanesbae or Einangrunarstoedin Mosel, for every dog and cat entering Iceland, Ireland origin included. Category 1 status shortens the pre-export paperwork; it does nothing to the quarantine. Stations admit pets only within a fixed window roughly every three weeks, and Keflavik accepts arriving pets only between 06:00 and 17:00 on those dates. Source: MAST, Guidance Notes D1, updated 1 July 2025.
Import permit
Required from MAST before travel, applied for at least 30 days ahead via the online portal (application 2.15) or, for applicants without an Icelandic ID number, by email to petimport@mast.is. Base fee around ISK 39,600 (roughly EUR 270), covering administration, the arrival inspection and the quarantine discharge check. Valid one year from issue. Source: MAST, Guidance Notes D1, updated 1 July 2025.
Health certificate
MAST's own Certificate of Health and Origin, Form D1 for dogs or C1 for cats, completed by the Irish vet and sent to petimport@mast.is no later than 5 days before the import date.
Leaving Ireland

Export requirements

Export permit
No standalone export permit for a companion pet. The document MAST actually checks on arrival is its own Form D1 (dogs) or C1 (cats), not a general Irish export certificate.
Veterinary inspection
Clinical examination and certification by an Ireland-based veterinarian, confirming the pet meets MAST's stated conditions for a Category 1 country of origin.
Health certificate
MAST will not accept Ireland's standard Animal Health Certificate for this route. The vet must instead complete MAST's own Certificate of Health and Origin, Form D1 for dogs or C1 for cats, and send it to petimport@mast.is no later than 5 days before the import date. Source: MAST, Guidance Notes D1, updated 1 July 2025.

Critical points

Ireland's rabies-free status only removes the extra 90-day wait that Category 2 countries face after a titre test. Every dog and cat entering Iceland, Irish origin included, still spends a minimum of 14 days at Reykjanesbae or Mosel.

MAST will not accept an ordinary Irish Animal Health Certificate. The vet needs to complete MAST's own Form D1 (dogs) or C1 (cats), including negative Brucella canis and, for intact dogs, Leishmania results, and get it to petimport@mast.is no later than 5 days before the import date.

Icelandair is the only nonstop carrier on this route and stopped taking pets as checked baggage on 1 November 2024. Every booking runs through Icelandair Cargo, with its own space allocation and lead times.

Quarantine station admission runs on a fixed calendar, roughly a three-day window every three weeks, and Keflavik accepts arriving pets only between 06:00 and 17:00 on those dates. Book the quarantine slot before the flight, not after.

Iceland import rules, explained

Iceland has a detailed set of import rules, and each one needs to be satisfied before travel. None of it is unusual, but the order and timing are what catch people out.

Practical tips for this move

Keep every original document together and take copies. Border officials want originals, and a missing signature or date can hold up clearance.

Work backwards from your travel date and add a buffer. Fixed waiting periods mean a single slipped appointment can push the whole move back by weeks.

Check the airline’s crate size and labelling rules early. An undersized or wrongly labelled crate is a frequent reason pets are refused at check-in.

Ask about temperature embargoes for your travel window. Summer and winter cargo restrictions can rule out certain routes and dates entirely.
