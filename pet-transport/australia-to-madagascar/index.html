Pet Transport from Australia to Madagascar

Bringing your dog or cat from Australia to Madagascar takes some coordination, but the requirements are well defined. Around 12-16 weeks is typical, with a straightforward sequence to work through. There is no routine quarantine, provided the paperwork is in …

In short: Preparing to move a pet from Australia to Madagascar typically takes around 12-16 weeks. There is no routine quarantine on arrival for compliant pets. A rabies titre test is not required.

Critical points

Emirates' Dubai-Antananarivo service was suspended for a period and resumed on 23 October 2025. Confirm the current schedule directly with Emirates SkyCargo before committing to travel dates, since a route with a recent disruption history is worth double-checking closer to booking.

Madagascar's import permit is a letter addressed to the Direction des Services Veterinaires, not an online application form. Apply at least 12-14 weeks out given the lack of a published turnaround.

No titre test applies from Australia, the same position that holds for the UK and US routes to Madagascar on this site, but that's Madagascar's current policy, not a permanent guarantee. Confirm with DSV close to travel.

A small arrival fee (30,000 Ariary per pet, paid at Ivato's Livestock Fund counter) is separate from anything paid in Australia before departure. Confirm accepted payment methods with your agent in advance.

Meeting Madagascar's entry requirements

The path into Madagascar is straightforward to follow once you know the steps. Give the time-sensitive items priority and check the details below.

Advice worth following

Check the airline’s crate size and labelling rules early. An undersized or wrongly labelled crate is a frequent reason pets are refused at check-in.

Ask about temperature embargoes for your travel window. Summer and winter cargo restrictions can rule out certain routes and dates entirely.

Microchip before you vaccinate. The microchip must be in place before the rabies shot for the vaccination to count, and getting this order wrong is the most common and costly mistake.

Use an official, government-authorised vet for the health certificate, not just your usual clinic. Many destinations only accept certificates signed by an approved vet.
