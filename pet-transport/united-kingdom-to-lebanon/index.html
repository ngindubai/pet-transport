Requirements

Lebanon entry requirements

Every item below must be in place before your pet can enter. We verify and track each one.

Microchip
Required (ISO 11784/11785)
Rabies vaccination
Required. Must be current at time of travel.
Quarantine
Inspection on arrival at Beirut Rafic Hariri International Airport. Short veterinary inspection required. Quarantine is possible if documentation is incomplete.
Import permit
Required. Issued by the Lebanon Ministry of Agriculture. Apply at least 4-6 weeks before travel.
Health certificate
Required. Official health certificate from an APHA-authorised UK Official Vet, endorsed by APHA and apostilled if required.
Leaving United Kingdom

Export requirements

Export permit
No general export permit required from the UK. An Animal Health Certificate (AHC) issued by a UK APHA-authorised Official Vet is required.
Health certificate
Animal Health Certificate (AHC) from an APHA-authorised Official Vet. Valid 10 days from the vet examination date. Must cover microchip, vaccination status, and health declaration.

Critical points

Lebanon's administrative environment can cause delays in import permit processing. Apply as early as possible and maintain follow-up contact with the Ministry.

Verify MEA Cargo is currently accepting pet shipments on the BEY route before booking. Operating conditions in Lebanon can affect cargo services.

All documentation must be original paper copies with wet signatures. Do not rely on digital copies.

Confirm current Lebanon Ministry of Agriculture requirements before finalising documentation, as requirements may be updated.

Lebanon entry requirements at a glance

The path into Lebanon is straightforward to follow once you know the steps. Give the time-sensitive items priority and check the details below.

Getting the details right

Get your pet used to the travel crate weeks in advance. A calm, crate-familiar animal travels far better than one meeting the crate for the first time at the airport.

Confirm live animal acceptance on every flight segment before booking. Not all aircraft or connections take pets, and cargo terms can differ leg to leg.

Keep every original document together and take copies. Border officials want originals, and a missing signature or date can hold up clearance.

Work backwards from your travel date and add a buffer. Fixed waiting periods mean a single slipped appointment can push the whole move back by weeks.

FAQ

Common questions about this route

Lebanon does not currently require a titre test for pets from the United Kingdom. You need current rabies vaccination, microchip, import permit from the Lebanon Ministry of Agriculture, and an official UK Animal Health Certificate. Confirm current requirements with the Ministry before starting preparations, as rules can change.
Apply to the Lebanon Ministry of Agriculture directly. Allow 4-6 weeks for processing. Given Lebanon’s current administrative environment, apply as early as possible after confirming your travel date. An IPATA-registered agent with recent Lebanon experience can submit the application on your behalf and follow up with the Ministry.
Yes. Middle East Airlines (MEA) and British Airways operate direct London Heathrow to Beirut services and carry pets as cargo. Emirates via Dubai and Qatar Airways via Doha offer indirect alternatives with strong cargo operations and high departure frequencies.
On direct flights to Beirut with MEA or BA, cabin pet options are not typically available on transatlantic or long-haul routes. On Lufthansa routing via Frankfurt, small pets may travel in cabin on the London-Frankfurt leg. Check cabin pet policies with your chosen carrier before booking.
Bring originals of: the Lebanon import permit, the UK Animal Health Certificate (endorsed by APHA), microchip documentation, rabies vaccination certificate, and any other test or treatment records required by the Ministry of Agriculture. Do not rely on photocopies or digital documents at the inspection.
