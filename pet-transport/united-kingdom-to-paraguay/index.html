Requirements

What your pet needs to enter Paraguay

Every item below must be in place before your pet can travel. We manage and verify each one.

Microchip
ISO 11784/11785 microchip required, matching the number recorded on the CVI presented at the port of entry.
Rabies vaccination
Current rabies vaccination required for dogs and cats. First-time vaccination needs a 21-day wait before the animal can travel. Dogs also need up-to-date distemper, parvovirus, infectious hepatitis and leptospirosis cover; cats need panleukopenia, rhinotracheitis and calicivirus cover. Source: SENACSA, viewed July 2026.
Rabies titre test
No titre test is required by SENACSA for companion animals entering Paraguay under the standard non-commercial route. Confirm this directly with SENACSA before travel, since requirements are not always published in English and can be updated without much notice.
Quarantine
No standard quarantine period is published for pets arriving with a complete, correctly endorsed CVI. The animal is inspected documentarily and clinically by a SENACSA vet at the port of entry rather than held for a fixed period.
Import permit
No separate import permit for non-commercial pet movement. SENACSA allows up to 5 companion dogs and cats per owner under the non-commercial category; a sixth animal or more is treated as a commercial import with its own process.
Health certificate
Certificado Veterinario Internacional (CVI) issued by the exporting country's competent authority, in this case APHA on behalf of a UK Official Veterinarian, presented to the SENACSA inspector on arrival along with vaccination records.

Critical points to read before you book

Paraguay bans the import of several breeds outright, including Pit Bull-type dogs, American Staffordshire Terrier, American Bully, Cane Corso, Boerboel and multiple mastiff varieties. Check the current SENACSA banned breed list against your dog's breed and any mix before booking anything.

SENACSA treats a sixth pet in one shipment as a commercial import, with a different, more involved process. If you are moving a multi-pet household, count carefully before you assume the standard non-commercial route applies.

SENACSA's own published English-language guidance is thin. Where this page states a requirement is not needed, such as a titre test, verify that directly with SENACSA or a Paraguay-based agent close to your travel date rather than relying solely on this page.

Airlines

Carriers approved for this route

Not all airlines accept live animals on this route. We know which carriers to use and how to book.

AIR EUROPA
Air Europa
Air Europa flies Madrid to Asuncion nonstop, the only nonstop link between Europe and Paraguay, several times a week on a Boeing 787. The practical routing from the UK is London to Madrid on British Airways, Iberia or Air Europa, then the Air Europa nonstop leg into Asuncion.
Cargo Only
BRITISH
British Airways
British Airways flies Heathrow to Madrid multiple times daily. IAG Cargo can hand the crate to Air Europa in Madrid for the onward Asuncion leg, avoiding a longer routing through Sao Paulo or Buenos Aires.
Cargo Only
IBERIA
Iberia
Iberia, part of the same IAG group as British Airways, also operates London-Madrid and works with the same cargo handling for the Madrid transfer. A useful alternative if British Airways cargo space is not available on the date needed.
Cargo Only

The rules for bringing a pet into Paraguay

Paraguay asks for several pieces of paperwork, and they all need to line up. A checklist and a clear timeline are your best tools here.

How to keep this move on track

Check the airline’s crate size and labelling rules early. An undersized or wrongly labelled crate is a frequent reason pets are refused at check-in.

Ask about temperature embargoes for your travel window. Summer and winter cargo restrictions can rule out certain routes and dates entirely.

Book the titre test laboratory well ahead. Approved labs have limited slots and the sample takes time to process, so this is not something to leave to the final weeks.

Microchip before you vaccinate. The microchip must be in place before the rabies shot for the vaccination to count, and getting this order wrong is the most common and costly mistake.
