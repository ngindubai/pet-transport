What your pet needs

Every item below must be completed and verified before your pet can travel. Expand each category for the detail.

Microchip
ISO 11784/11785 standard required. Must be implanted before the rabies vaccination.
Rabies vaccination
Required. At least 21 days must have passed since the primary vaccination course before travel to Great Britain.
Rabies titre test
South Korea is on the UK's approved countries list, so a rabies titre test is not required for pets travelling directly from South Korea with a valid vaccination record.
Quarantine
No routine quarantine for pets arriving with correct documentation on an approved route. Penalty quarantine of up to 4 months applies if documentation is incomplete.
Import permit
No formal import permit required.
Health certificate
Animal Health Certificate issued by an authorised vet in South Korea, valid for 10 days from issue for GB entry.
Export permit
No South Korean export permit required for companion dogs and cats.
Health certificate
Animal Health Certificate (AHC) or equivalent official certificate issued by an authorised South Korean veterinarian. Must include microchip number, vaccination history, titre test result if required, and general health status. UK import rules specify required format.

Critical points

Tapeworm treatment for dogs has a strict 24 to 120 hour window before scheduled arrival in Great Britain. Treatment outside this window means the pet is non-compliant. Cats and ferrets are exempt from the tapeworm requirement.

South Korea is on the UK's approved countries list, meaning a rabies titre test is not required. However, your pet must have a current, valid rabies vaccination and the 21-day post-vaccination wait must be complete.

Travel must use an approved route into Great Britain. Heathrow is the most practical entry point from Seoul. Check GOV.UK for the current list of approved routes.

Penalty quarantine of up to 4 months at the owner's expense applies if documentation is incorrect or the pet arrives on an unapproved route. This is expensive and stressful. Get the paperwork right.

GB-issued pet passports are not relevant here since the pet is departing from South Korea. The AHC is the required document for entry into Great Britain.

United Kingdom import rules, explained

United Kingdom keeps its requirements reasonable, though a few steps have fixed timing. Map those out first and the rest falls into place.

Practical tips for this move

Keep every original document together and take copies. Border officials want originals, and a missing signature or date can hold up clearance.

Work backwards from your travel date and add a buffer. Fixed waiting periods mean a single slipped appointment can push the whole move back by weeks.

Check the airline’s crate size and labelling rules early. An undersized or wrongly labelled crate is a frequent reason pets are refused at check-in.

Ask about temperature embargoes for your travel window. Summer and winter cargo restrictions can rule out certain routes and dates entirely.
