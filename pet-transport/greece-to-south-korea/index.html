Requirements

South Korea entry requirements

Every item below must be in place before your pet can enter. We verify and track each one.

Microchip
Required. ISO 11784/11785, implanted before the rabies vaccination is recorded.
Rabies vaccination
Required. Current and valid for the travel period.
Rabies titre test
Not required. Greece's EU membership gives it QIAS equivalency status, so the FAVN titre test applied to non-EU origins does not apply.
Quarantine
QIAS inspection on arrival at Incheon, typically 1-3 days for EU-origin pets, shorter than the 5-10 day hold for rabies-endemic non-EU countries but not waived entirely.
Import permit
Advance QIAS notification required before your pet's arrival at Incheon.
Health certificate
Korean QIAS-format certificate signed by a Greek official vet and endorsed by the Ministry. The EU pet passport is not accepted on its own.
Leaving Greece

Export requirements

Export permit
No standalone export permit. The Ministry endorsement on the health certificate is the export authorisation for a non-EU destination such as South Korea.
Health certificate
Korean QIAS-format health certificate, not the EU pet passport. Issued by a Greek official veterinarian and endorsed by the Ministry of Rural Development and Food before departure. Valid 10 days from issue.

Critical points

The EU pet passport is not accepted at Incheon. The Korean QIAS-format certificate, endorsed by the Ministry, is the document that matters.

Turkish Cargo's live-animal booking availability varies by date and aircraft type; confirm space on the IST-ICN sector before confirming the Athens departure.

The 1-3 day QIAS inspection is not guaranteed. Incomplete documentation or a health concern on arrival can extend it.

If you live on an island rather than the mainland, factor in the extra travel and time needed to reach an official vet in Athens or Thessaloniki for the Ministry-endorsed certificate.

The rules for bringing a pet into South Korea

South Korea keeps its requirements reasonable, though a few steps have fixed timing. Map those out first and the rest falls into place.

How to keep this move on track

Ask about temperature embargoes for your travel window. Summer and winter cargo restrictions can rule out certain routes and dates entirely.

Microchip before you vaccinate. The microchip must be in place before the rabies shot for the vaccination to count, and getting this order wrong is the most common and costly mistake.

Use an official, government-authorised vet for the health certificate, not just your usual clinic. Many destinations only accept certificates signed by an approved vet.

If this is your first international move, consider an IPATA-registered pet transport agent. They handle the documents, airline booking and crate sizing, and can troubleshoot problems.
