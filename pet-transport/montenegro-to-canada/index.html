Requirements

Canada entry requirements

Every item below must be in place before your pet can enter. We verify and track each one.

Microchip
Not legally mandated for a personal pet arriving from a country CFIA doesn't class as high-risk for dog rabies, Montenegro included, though a scannable ISO 11784/11785 chip is recommended and expected in practice by most airlines and border staff.
Rabies vaccination
Required for dogs, current and documented on a certificate in English or French. Cats face no federal rabies rule. Source: CFIA, inspection.canada.ca, 2026.
Rabies titre test
Not required. Montenegro does not appear on CFIA's list of countries at high-risk for dog rabies, last updated 13 March 2025 and confirmed directly against that published list on 22 July 2026. That's a genuinely different position to the outbound leg, where Montenegro's own import rules apply a titre test to a Canadian pet with no exemption for any country of origin.
Quarantine
No routine quarantine for a pet arriving with correct, current documentation as a personal, owner-accompanied animal.
Import permit
Not required for personal pets, defined by CFIA as one or two animals travelling with an owner who isn't transferring them to someone else on arrival. Montenegro's absence from the high-risk list makes this an even lighter question than it is for a listed origin.
Health certificate
Veterinary health certificate confirming vaccination status and general health, issued close to travel and presented in English or French at the port of entry.
Leaving Montenegro

Export requirements

Export permit
No formal export permit for personal pets. Clearance from AFSVPA (Administration for Food Safety, Veterinary and Phytosanitary Affairs) precedes departure.
Veterinary inspection
Pre-export examination by an authorised Montenegrin vet, checking microchip, vaccination and general health.
Health certificate
Veterinary health certificate issued by an authorised Montenegrin vet, built to CFIA's English or French format rather than any EU-style layout, since Canada shares no negotiated certificate template with Montenegro.

Critical points

Montenegro sitting outside CFIA's high-risk list removes the titre test question entirely for this route. That's the opposite of the outbound leg, where Montenegro's own rules apply a titre test to a Canadian pet with no exemption described for any origin country.

No airline flies Podgorica or Tivat to Canada directly. Every booking connects through Belgrade, Frankfurt, Munich or Vienna.

Air Serbia's cabin option only fits a pet and carrier combined under 8kg. Larger dogs need Lufthansa or Austrian Airlines instead, both of which involve hold transport and exclude brachycephalic breeds outright.

Meeting Canada's entry requirements

The requirements for Canada are light, but not zero. A little planning goes a long way on a route like this.

Advice worth following

Check the airline’s crate size and labelling rules early. An undersized or wrongly labelled crate is a frequent reason pets are refused at check-in.

Ask about temperature embargoes for your travel window. Summer and winter cargo restrictions can rule out certain routes and dates entirely.

Microchip before you vaccinate. The microchip must be in place before the rabies shot for the vaccination to count, and getting this order wrong is the most common and costly mistake.

Use an official, government-authorised vet for the health certificate, not just your usual clinic. Many destinations only accept certificates signed by an approved vet.
