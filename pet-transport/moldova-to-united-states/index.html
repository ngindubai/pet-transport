In short: Preparing to move a pet from Moldova to United States typically takes around 12-18 weeks. There is no routine quarantine on arrival for compliant pets. A rabies titre test may be required depending on your pet's origin, so check the requirements below.

Checklist

What your pet needs

Every item below must be completed and verified before your pet can travel. Expand each category for the detail.

Microchip
Required for dogs, must be implanted before the rabies vaccination or the vaccination does not count. Not a federal requirement for cats, but airlines require one regardless of species.
Rabies vaccination
Dogs only. Moldova is a CDC high-risk country for dog rabies, so dogs need either a US-recognised foreign rabies vaccination plus a passing titre test, or 28-day quarantine at a CDC-registered facility on arrival. Cats have no federal rabies vaccination requirement. Source: CDC, cdc.gov/importation.
Rabies titre test
Required for dogs unless the owner accepts 28-day quarantine instead. Blood drawn at least 30 days after the first vaccination and at least 28 days before US entry, tested at a CDC-approved laboratory. Not required for cats.
Quarantine
No quarantine for dogs with a valid passing titre test and complete documentation, and no quarantine at all for cats. Dogs without a valid titre face mandatory 28-day quarantine at a CDC-registered animal care facility, entirely at the owner's expense.
Import permit
No formal import permit. The CDC Dog Import Form, completed online before travel, is the required registration for every dog. Not required for cats.
Health certificate
No standalone US health certificate requirement for personal pets. The Certification of Foreign Rabies Vaccination and Microchip form, endorsed by ANSA in Moldova, serves this function for dogs. Cats need no equivalent document at federal level.
Export permit
No standalone export permit for personal pets. ANSA (National Food Safety Agency, ansa.gov.md) oversees the veterinary export process.
Veterinary inspection
Clinical examination by a licensed Moldovan vet close to travel, confirming fitness to fly and recording microchip and vaccination details.
Government endorsement
The Certification of Foreign Rabies Vaccination and Microchip form must be completed by a Moldovan vet and endorsed by a government veterinary official at ANSA before travel.
Health certificate
Health certificate issued by an ANSA-endorsed vet, dated close to departure. No standalone USDA-style endorsement process exists on the Moldova side; ANSA performs the government endorsement role.

Airlines

Carriers on this route

Not all airlines accept live animals on this route. Confirm the operating carrier's current policy for every flight segment.

Turkish Airlines
Chisinau to Istanbul, then a single-carrier connection to US gateways including New York JFK and Chicago O'Hare. Cabin pets under 8kg accepted (brachycephalic breeds excluded from cabin and hold since October 2021). Cargo hold via Turkish Cargo, flat USD 150 fee regardless of crate weight tier up to 50kg. Ground temperature embargo applies between roughly May and September when any airport on the route exceeds 29C.
Cabin and Cargo
Austrian Airlines
Chisinau to Vienna on Austrian's own network, with Austrian's own long-haul services from Vienna to several US cities including Newark and Chicago, keeping the pet on a single carrier's handling chain rather than an interline transfer. Cabin pets under 8kg; hold pets up to 14kg per animal, two animals maximum per container. Brachycephalic breeds excluded from the hold.
Cabin and Cargo
Lufthansa
Chisinau to Frankfurt or Munich, then Lufthansa's extensive US network. Cabin pets up to 8kg. All brachycephalic dog and cat breeds have been banned from the cargo hold since 1 January 2020, they may only fly in the cabin under the weight limit. Seasonal heat embargoes apply on some southern routes.
Cabin and Cargo
LOT Polish Airlines
Chisinau to Warsaw, then LOT's direct routes to New York JFK, Chicago and Newark. Cabin pets up to 8kg on B787/B737 aircraft. Live animal cargo must be booked through LOT Cargo and an approved shipping agent rather than the standard ticket booking system.

Critical points

Moldova is on the CDC's high-risk list for dog rabies. This applies to dogs only; cats face no federal rabies requirement entering the US at all, a real practical difference if you're moving a mixed-species household.

There are no direct flights from Chisinau to the United States. Every route needs at least one connection, via Istanbul, Vienna, Frankfurt or Warsaw. If your dog does not have a valid titre test, it must arrive at the specific US airport with a CDC-registered facility, with no domestic connections permitted, which narrows your usable routing further.

The Certification of Foreign Rabies Vaccination and Microchip form is single-use and valid only 30 days from the vet's signature. Time the ANSA endorsement to sit inside your actual travel window, not before it.

Confirm CDC-registered facility availability and airport before booking flights if you are not doing the titre test. Facilities have limited capacity and reservations should be made well ahead of the 28-day quarantine start date.

What United States requires

Getting a pet into United States means meeting a demanding set of rules with no shortcuts. Start from the earliest deadline and work forward from there.

Avoiding the common mistakes

If this is your first international move, consider an IPATA-registered pet transport agent. They handle the documents, airline booking and crate sizing, and can troubleshoot problems.

Get your pet used to the travel crate weeks in advance. A calm, crate-familiar animal travels far better than one meeting the crate for the first time at the airport.

Confirm live animal acceptance on every flight segment before booking. Not all aircraft or connections take pets, and cargo terms can differ leg to leg.

Keep every original document together and take copies. Border officials want originals, and a missing signature or date can hold up clearance.
