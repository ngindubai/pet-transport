Overview

Relocating a pet from Tunisia to United States follows a clear path once you understand what United States asks for. Set aside roughly 13-17 weeks for this route and keep a close eye on the sequencing. Pets with correct documentation skip quarantine entirely.

A rabies titre test may apply depending on where your pet is coming from, so check the requirements below. Follow the sequence below and keep a buffer for anything that slips.

Step by step

The Tunisia to United States process

1
Start of planning, 3-4 months before travel for dogs.
Confirm the dog is at least 12 weeks old, fit an ISO microchip, then administer or verify the primary rabies vaccination. Not required for cats.

Responsible: Vet in Tunisia

2
Around 2-3 months before travel.
Draw blood for the rabies titer at least 30 days after vaccination, send to a CDC-approved laboratory, and confirm the result reaches 0.5 IU/ml. Skip for cats.

Responsible: Vet and the CDC-approved laboratory

3
6-8 weeks before travel, earlier if possible.
Book a reservation at a CDC-registered animal care facility (Miami, JFK, Atlanta, Los Angeles, Philadelphia or Washington Dulles) and confirm the final arrival airport matches it.

Responsible: Owner or agent

4
Once the flight is booked, at least a few weeks before travel.
Complete the CDC Dog Import Form online and print the receipt. Valid for one entry only when arriving from a high-risk country.

Responsible: Owner, shipper, or agent

5
2-3 working days before travel, close to the flight date.
Apply for DGSV endorsement of the Tunisian export health certificate.

Responsible: Owner and DGSV-registered vet

6
6-8 weeks before travel.
Book the Tunis to European hub leg (Paris, Frankfurt or Rome) and confirm the connecting US carrier lands at the CDC-registered facility airport.

Responsible: Owner or a pet transport agent

7
Day of departure.
Carry the titer report, CDC Dog Import Form receipt and DGSV-endorsed health certificate together on the day of travel.

Responsible: Owner and airline cargo desk

Checklist

United States: entry requirements

RequirementDetail
MicrochipFor dogs: an ISO-compatible microchip, implanted before the rabies vaccination, or the vaccination does not count for CDC purposes. Not federally required for cats. Source: CDC, cdc.gov/importation/dogs, updated 15 April 2026.
Rabies vaccinationDogs only: first rabies vaccination at a minimum age of 12 weeks, given at least 28 days before the dog enters the US. Cats face no federal rabies vaccination requirement at all. Source: CDC.
Rabies titre testRequired for dogs. Tunisia sits on the CDC's high-risk countries for dog rabies list, last updated 15 April 2026. Blood must be drawn at least 30 days after the rabies vaccination and at least 28 days before the dog enters the US, tested at a CDC-approved laboratory, with a result of 0.5 IU/ml or above. Not required for cats. Source: CDC, cdc.gov/importation/dogs/high-risk-countries.html.
QuarantineNone for a dog arriving with a valid titer and complete paperwork. A dog without a valid titer result faces a mandatory 28-day quarantine at the CDC-registered animal care facility on arrival, at the owner's cost. Cats are not subject to this rule. Source: CDC.
Import permitCDC Dog Import Form completed online before travel, producing a receipt valid for a single entry when the dog is coming from a high-risk country. A reservation at a CDC-registered animal care facility is also mandatory, and the dog must land at the specific airport where that facility is based. Source: CDC, dog-import-form-instructions.html.
Health certificateHealth certificate from the DGSV-endorsed vet in Tunisia, plus the completed CDC Dog Import Form receipt and the titer lab report, all presented together on arrival. Dogs must also be at least 6 months old at entry.

Critical points

Read before you book

⚠ Tunisia is on the CDC's high-risk list for dog rabies, last confirmed 15 April 2026. Skip the titer test or get the 30-day and 28-day windows wrong and the dog faces a mandatory 28-day quarantine on arrival, easily adding USD 2,500 or more. This applies to dogs only, cats are unaffected.
⚠ There is no nonstop flight between Tunisia and the United States. Every booking runs through a European hub, most realistically Paris, Frankfurt or Rome on Tunisair's own network, then a second carrier across the Atlantic. Two carriers means two separate pet policies to confirm, and the connection itself needs enough time for a live animal transfer.
⚠ A dog can only clear US entry from a high-risk country at one of six airports with a CDC-registered animal care facility: Miami, JFK, Atlanta, Los Angeles, Philadelphia or Washington Dulles. Book a connection that lands anywhere else and the dog cannot legally clear entry there, whatever the paperwork says.
Carriers

Airlines on this route

Air France
Tunisair's Tunis to Paris CDG sector connects onto Air France's own transatlantic network or an Air France KLM Martinair Cargo booking into a US gateway. A two-carrier itinerary with a Paris transfer, so confirm pet acceptance on both legs and allow enough connection time for a live animal transfer.
Cargo Only
Lufthansa
Tunisair flies Tunis to Frankfurt (FRA), Lufthansa's main hub, which then connects to several US gateways including its Miami and JFK services. Frankfurt is a well-used transfer point for live animal cargo, though heat embargoes on the US leg can apply in summer.
Cargo Only
Tunisair
Operates the first leg out of Tunis-Carthage (TUN) to Paris, Frankfurt, Rome or Madrid, several times weekly. There is no Tunisair service to the US itself, so this carrier only ever covers the European leg of the journey. Pit Bull, Rottweiler and Tosa Inu type dogs are not accepted in any class.
Cabin and Cargo
Delta Air Lines
Operates transatlantic service from several European hubs, including Paris and Rome, into US gateways with a CDC-registered animal care facility. Useful as the second leg of a two-carrier booking when Air France or Alitalia-successor connections from Tunis don't line up.
Cargo Only

United States import rules, explained

United States has a detailed set of import rules, and each one needs to be satisfied before travel. None of it is unusual, but the order and timing are what catch people out.

Practical tips for this move

Keep every original document together and take copies. Border officials want originals, and a missing signature or date can hold up clearance.

Work backwards from your travel date and add a buffer. Fixed waiting periods mean a single slipped appointment can push the whole move back by weeks.

Check the airline’s crate size and labelling rules early. An undersized or wrongly labelled crate is a frequent reason pets are refused at check-in.

Ask about temperature embargoes for your travel window. Summer and winter cargo restrictions can rule out certain routes and dates entirely.
