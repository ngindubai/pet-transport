Checklist

What your pet needs

Every item below must be completed and verified before your pet can travel. Expand each category for the detail.

Microchip
Required. ISO 11784/11785 standard, implanted before the rabies vaccination or the vaccination does not count.
Rabies vaccination
Required from 12 weeks old. At least 21 days must pass after the first vaccination before travel.
Rabies titre test
Required. Angola does not appear on the UK's Part 1 or Part 2 list of rabies-listed countries (source: GOV.UK, Bringing your pet dog, cat or ferret to Great Britain, checked 17 July 2026), so it is treated as unlisted. Blood must be drawn at least 30 days after vaccination, tested at an APHA-approved FAVN or ELISA lab to a minimum 0.5 IU/ml result, then a 90-day wait from the blood draw before entry.
Quarantine
No routine quarantine for pets that travel with full, compliant paperwork. Penalty quarantine of up to 120 days applies if a pet arrives with the wrong documents, wrong route, or a lapsed vaccination, and the owner pays every cost of it.
Import permit
No formal import permit. The Animal Health Certificate (AHC) does that job instead.
Health certificate
AHC from an official Angolan vet, valid for 10 days from issue to entry. For dogs, tapeworm treatment must be recorded between 24 and 120 hours before the scheduled UK arrival time. Cats and ferrets are exempt from the tapeworm step.
Export permit
No standalone export permit for accompanying pets has been published. The government-endorsed health certificate from the Instituto dos Serviços de Veterinária (ISV) is what stands in for one. Pet exports from Angola are uncommon, so confirm the current process directly with ISV or a IPATA-registered agent well before booking.
Veterinary inspection
Pre-export clinical examination by a licensed Angolan veterinarian, arranged as close to travel as the certificate rules allow.
Government endorsement
Government endorsement by the Instituto dos Serviços de Veterinária (ISV), Angola's national veterinary authority under the Ministry of Agriculture and Forests (MINAGRIF, Luanda).
Health certificate
ISV-endorsed veterinary health certificate. Because so few pets are exported from Angola each year, build in extra time to get the certificate format agreed with APHA before the appointment, rather than assuming a standard template exists.

Airlines

Carriers on this route

Not all airlines accept live animals on this route. Confirm the operating carrier's current policy for every flight segment.

TAP Air Portugal
Flies Luanda to Lisbon most days, with onward connections to Heathrow and Gatwick. The most heavily used connection for this corridor, so cargo pet space through Lisbon is the practical default.
Cargo Only
TAAG Angola Airlines
Angola's flag carrier flies Luanda to Lisbon. Cats and small dogs up to 8kg including carrier can travel in the economy cabin; larger pets go as manifested cargo, quoted separately by TAAG Cargo. No TAAG service reaches London directly, so this only covers the first leg.
Cabin and Cargo
Lufthansa
Routes via Frankfurt, which has a dedicated Frankfurt Animal Lounge for cargo pets in transit. Munich cannot be used for a cargo pet connection, only Frankfurt, so check the routing before booking. Brachycephalic breeds are barred from the hold.
Cabin and Cargo
Ethiopian Airlines
An alternative routing via Addis Ababa. Cabin pets up to 8kg in a box no larger than 55x40x20cm; cargo pets cost a flat $200 booking fee, reserved at least 3 days ahead. Snub-nosed breeds are not accepted.

Critical points

Angola is unlisted, not Part 2 listed, so the titre test and 90-day wait are non-negotiable. There is no faster listed-country pathway for this route.

There is no direct Luanda to London flight. Every routing goes via Lisbon, Frankfurt, or a Gulf hub, adding transit time and a second set of transit rules to check.

Cargo pet capacity through Lisbon and Frankfurt on this corridor is thin. Confirmed pet space, not just a flight booking, should come before you fix a travel date.

Get the blood draw date right. A titre test drawn even a few days too early after vaccination will be rejected, and starting over adds a further 30-day wait before the 90-day clock can begin.

The rules for bringing a pet into United Kingdom

United Kingdom has a detailed set of import rules, and each one needs to be satisfied before travel. None of it is unusual, but the order and timing are what catch people out.

How to keep this move on track

Get your pet used to the travel crate weeks in advance. A calm, crate-familiar animal travels far better than one meeting the crate for the first time at the airport.

Confirm live animal acceptance on every flight segment before booking. Not all aircraft or connections take pets, and cargo terms can differ leg to leg.

Keep every original document together and take copies. Border officials want originals, and a missing signature or date can hold up clearance.

Work backwards from your travel date and add a buffer. Fixed waiting periods mean a single slipped appointment can push the whole move back by weeks.
