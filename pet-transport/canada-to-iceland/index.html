In short: Preparing to move a pet from Canada to Iceland typically takes around 16-20 weeks. Expect 14 days of quarantine on arrival. A rabies titre test is required, which adds a fixed waiting period, so start early.

Requirements

Iceland entry requirements

Every item below must be in place before your pet can enter. We verify and track each one.

Microchip
ISO 11784/11785 microchip required, implanted before the rabies vaccination. Source: MAST, mast.is.
Rabies vaccination
Primary rabies vaccination after the microchip. MAST also requires two leptospirosis doses 3 to 4 weeks apart, plus current distemper, hepatitis, parvovirus and parainfluenza cover for dogs, on top of the rabies course. Source: MAST guidance notes, 2026.
Rabies titre test
Required. Blood drawn at least 30 days after vaccination, minimum result 0.5 IU/ml. Canada is classified on MAST's Category 2 list, since rabies is present in Canadian wildlife (notably raccoons, skunks, foxes and bats) even though it is well controlled. A 90-day waiting period applies from the date of the blood draw, not the date the lab result comes back, before travel is permitted. Source: MAST, mast.is, 2026.
Quarantine
Mandatory 14 days minimum at one of two approved isolation stations, Einangrunarstoedin i Reykjanesbae or Einangrunarstoedin Mosel, for every dog and cat entering Iceland. Source: MAST, mast.is, 2026.
Import permit
Required from MAST before travel, with the application form, a photo, the health certificate and lab results. Base fee is roughly ISK 40,000 (about EUR 270). Quarantine station space is booked separately and has limited intake dates. Source: MAST, mast.is, 2026.
Health certificate
MAST's Category 2 certificate (Form D2 or C2), completed by a CFIA-accredited vet in Canada.
Leaving Canada

Export requirements

Export permit
No formal export permit for a private pet move out of Canada.
Health certificate
MAST's Category 2 certificate (referred to in MAST's own documentation as Form D2 for dogs, Form C2 for cats), completed by a CFIA-accredited veterinarian. Source: MAST, mast.is, 2026.
Costs

What this route typically costs

Rabies titre test at an approved laboratory: CAD 130-260
Leptospirosis and core vaccination course: CAD 100-220
MAST import permit base fee: around ISK 40,000 (roughly EUR 270)
Quarantine, 14 days: around ISK 220,000 for a dog (roughly EUR 1,450), ISK 100,000-110,000 for a cat (roughly EUR 670-730)
IATA-compliant travel crate: CAD 100-350
Cargo fee, Toronto to Keflavik direct: EUR 600-1,400 depending on crate size and season
Agent and handling fees: EUR 300-600
Total typical range: EUR 2,400-3,900 for a dog, lower for a cat

Critical points

The 90-day wait runs from the blood draw date, not the date the lab result arrives and not the date of vaccination. Owners who count from the wrong date routinely miss their planned travel window by weeks.

The mandatory 14-day quarantine applies regardless of the direct flight and regardless of how straightforward the Canadian export paperwork is. Budget it in from the start, not as an afterthought once the flight is booked.

Despite the nonstop Toronto to Keflavik flight, the pet still travels as manifested cargo, not checked baggage, since Icelandair stopped accepting pets in the passenger hold on 1 November 2024.

MAST's Category 2 certificate (Form D2 or C2) is a different document from Canada's standard export paperwork used for most other destinations. Confirm your vet is CFIA-accredited and familiar with MAST's specific format before the appointment.

MAST's banned breed list applies on arrival regardless of Canadian export rules. A Pit Bull Terrier type or American Bulldog cannot enter Iceland even with complete Canadian paperwork.

Iceland entry requirements at a glance

The requirements for Iceland are thorough rather than difficult. Work through them methodically and confirm anything you are unsure about with the official source.

Getting the details right

If this is your first international move, consider an IPATA-registered pet transport agent. They handle the documents, airline booking and crate sizing, and can troubleshoot problems.

Get your pet used to the travel crate weeks in advance. A calm, crate-familiar animal travels far better than one meeting the crate for the first time at the airport.

Confirm live animal acceptance on every flight segment before booking. Not all aircraft or connections take pets, and cargo terms can differ leg to leg.

Keep every original document together and take copies. Border officials want originals, and a missing signature or date can hold up clearance.
