Pet Transport from Rwanda to Spain: 2026 Guide

A move from Rwanda to Spain with a dog or cat is mostly about preparing the documents in the right order. Around 22-28 weeks is sensible here, with a few steps that must happen in a strict order. There is no routine quarantine, provided the paperwork is in …

In short: Preparing to move a pet from Rwanda to Spain typically takes around 22-28 weeks. There is no routine quarantine on arrival for compliant pets. A rabies titre test is required, which adds a fixed waiting period, so start early.

Critical points

There are no direct flights from Kigali to Spain. All routings require at least one connection through a European or Middle Eastern hub. Each leg must be confirmed separately for live animal cargo acceptance.

EU-approved laboratories for the titre test may not be available in Rwanda. Budget for sample shipping time and costs when calculating your timeline.

The 3-month wait runs from the blood draw date, not from the result date. Calculate the earliest Spain entry date before booking any flights.

Meeting Spain's entry requirements

The requirements for Spain are thorough rather than difficult. Work through them methodically and confirm anything you are unsure about with the official source.

Advice worth following

Ask about temperature embargoes for your travel window. Summer and winter cargo restrictions can rule out certain routes and dates entirely.

Book the titre test laboratory well ahead. Approved labs have limited slots and the sample takes time to process, so this is not something to leave to the final weeks.

Microchip before you vaccinate. The microchip must be in place before the rabies shot for the vaccination to count, and getting this order wrong is the most common and costly mistake.

Use an official, government-authorised vet for the health certificate, not just your usual clinic. Many destinations only accept certificates signed by an approved vet.

FAQ

Common questions

No direct flights operate between Kigali (KGL) and Spain. All routings require at least one connection. Brussels Airlines via Brussels (BRU), Turkish Airlines via Istanbul (IST), and Ethiopian Airlines via Addis Ababa (ADD) are the main options. Each leg must be confirmed separately for live animal cargo acceptance.
Allow 22 to 28 weeks from the day you start. The mandatory EU steps (vaccination, 30-day wait, titre test, 3-month wait from blood draw) account for most of that time. If the blood sample also needs to be shipped to a laboratory outside Rwanda, factor in an additional one to two weeks for shipping and results. None of the mandatory waiting periods can be shortened.
RAB (Rwanda Agriculture and Animal Resources Development Board) is Rwanda’s official authority for animal health exports. RAB’s endorsement on the official veterinary health certificate is what makes the document legally valid for EU border inspection purposes. A certificate signed only by a private vet and not endorsed by RAB will not be accepted at Madrid Barajas. Allow several working days for the endorsement.
The EU publishes a list of approved laboratories for the FAVN titre test. As of 2026, no Rwandan laboratory features on this list, which means blood samples typically need to be shipped to an EU-approved lab in Europe or South Africa. Your official vet in Rwanda can advise on the logistics. The EU-approved lab list is available at food.ec.europa.eu. Always check the current list before drawing blood, as approvals can change.
Brussels Airlines via Brussels is often the most practical choice, as the BRU cargo hub has established procedures for European live animal arrivals and direct connections to Madrid are available. Turkish Airlines via Istanbul is a close second, particularly if the KGL-IST-MAD schedule suits your timeline better. Compare cargo fees and confirmed live animal acceptance on all sectors before committing.
