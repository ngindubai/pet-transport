Belgium Pet Export Guide 2026: AHC, Crate, Brussels Cargo Routes

By Pet Transport Global Editorial Team  ·  13 June 2026  ·  9 min read

Need help moving your pet internationally? Get a free quote

Moving a pet out of Belgium in 2026 involves different paperwork depending on where you are going. For travel within the EU, the process is simple: an EU pet passport covers everything. For non-EU destinations, including the UK, USA, UAE, and Singapore, you need an EU Animal Health Certificate (AHC) endorsed by Belgium’s FAVV/AFSCA authority. This guide covers the export process from Belgium, what Brussels Airport’s cargo service looks like in practice, and what the destination requirements mean for the four most common long-haul routes.

Who issues a Belgian pet export health certificate?

In Belgium, all export health documentation for pets goes through the FAVV (Federaal Agentschap voor de Veiligheid van de Voedselketen) in Dutch or AFSCA (Agence Federale pour la Securite de la Chaine Alimentaire) in French. This is Belgium’s Federal Agency for the Safety of the Food Chain, which oversees food and animal health matters.

For EU and EFTA destinations, your official vet issues an EU pet passport. This document records the microchip number (ISO 11784/11785, 15-digit) and rabies vaccination history and is valid throughout the European Union. No FAVV endorsement is required for intra-EU travel.

For non-EU destinations, including the UK, USA, UAE, and Singapore, the process is more involved. Your official vet completes the EU Animal Health Certificate (AHC), and then the FAVV/AFSCA must endorse it before travel. The endorsement confirms that the issuing vet is authorised and that the document is authentic. Without FAVV endorsement, most non-EU countries will not accept the AHC at their border.

The AHC for non-commercial pet movements is governed by EU Regulation 576/2013 and implementing legislation. Updated AHC formats came into force from April 2026 (EU Regulation 2026/131 for non-commercial movements; the old formats remain valid for endorsements issued before 30 September 2026). Ask your vet to confirm they are using the current format for your travel date.

Allow time for the endorsement step. The FAVV/AFSCA endorsement process takes 1-3 business days depending on the local office and submission method. If you are working with a pet transport agent, they typically handle this. If you are self-managing, contact your regional FAVV/AFSCA office to understand their current process.

Brussels Airport live animal cargo: how does it work?

Brussels Airport (BRU) handles live animal cargo through its dnata-operated Animal Care and Inspection Centre (ACIC). The facility opened in 2020 and received IATA CEIV (Centre of Excellence for Independent Validators) Live Animals certification in 2022. Since opening, the ACIC has handled over 10,000 mammals, including family pets, police dogs, race horses, and zoo animals.

For a dog or cat being exported from Belgium, the typical cargo handling process at BRU works like this. You or your transport agent delivers the pet to the ACIC on the day of travel, within the airline’s specified check-in window. The ACIC team verifies the animal’s documents, checks the crate dimensions against IATA live animal regulations, and confirms the pet’s condition for travel. The animal is then kept in temperature-controlled holding, with access to water, until the flight.

The main cargo airlines operating live animal services from BRU include Lufthansa Cargo (via Frankfurt FRA), Qatar Airways Cargo (via Doha DOH), Emirates SkyCargo (via Dubai DXB), Brussels Airlines cargo services, and KLM Cargo connections via Amsterdam (AMS). The best routing depends on the destination.

Key practical point: the ACIC at BRU is open 24 hours but you must book in advance. Contact the airline or your transport agent well before the travel date to confirm the check-in window and any specific ACIC booking requirements.

Belgium to UK, USA, UAE, Singapore: what’s different per destination?

Belgium to UK

The UK left the EU in January 2020, which changed pet travel from the UK and into the UK significantly. Belgian pets (or any EU-origin pets) no longer use the EU pet passport for entry into Great Britain.

For a dog or cat from Belgium entering Great Britain in 2026, you need an EU AHC endorsed by the FAVV/AFSCA. Belgium is an EU-listed country under UK APHA guidance, which means no rabies titre test is required for dogs or cats from Belgium entering the UK. This is an important saving compared to pets from unlisted countries.

Dogs also need a tapeworm treatment administered by a vet between 24 and 120 hours (1-5 days) before arrival in Great Britain. The timing window is strict. If the treatment is given outside that window, the dog will be refused entry. The tapeworm treatment must be recorded in the AHC.

The AHC is valid for 10 days from the date of issue by the Belgian vet. Travel must happen within that window. Direct routes from Brussels (BRU) to London Heathrow (LHR), London City (LCY), or other UK airports are straightforward. Eurostar and Eurotunnel also allow EU pet travel with the correct AHC.

Cost for Belgium to UK (medium dog): approximately EUR 150-300 for the AHC process, EUR 200-600 for cargo or cabin fees depending on size, plus the tapeworm vet visit.

Belgium to USA

The USA’s CDC dog import rules changed in 2023 and the framework from 2023 applies in 2026. Belgium is classified as a low-risk country for rabies under the CDC framework (the dog was born or resided in Belgium), which means no serological titre test is required to bring a dog into the USA from Belgium.

The required documentation for a dog from Belgium entering the USA includes a US-acceptable health certificate completed by a Belgian official vet, records of microchip and rabies vaccination, and confirmation of low-risk country residency. For cats from Belgium entering the USA, there are no specific federal health requirements, though airlines impose their own conditions.

For cargo shipments from Brussels to the USA, common routings from BRU include direct or single-connection services via London Heathrow (LHR), Frankfurt (FRA), or Amsterdam (AMS), then transatlantic to JFK, ORD, IAD, or other US gateways. Travel time from Belgium to the US East Coast is typically 8-12 hours cargo-to-cargo.

Cost for Belgium to USA (medium dog): approximately EUR 1,200-3,000 depending on size, airline, and whether you use a transport agent.

Belgium to UAE

The UAE requires an import permit for all live animals entering the country. The permit is issued by the UAE Ministry of Climate Change and Environment (MOCCAE). For dogs and cats, the owner or agent must apply for the permit before shipping the pet.

Beyond the import permit, the UAE requires a health certificate endorsed by the FAVV/AFSCA, an ISO microchip, current rabies vaccination, and in some cases additional vaccination records (distemper, parvo, hepatitis for dogs; leukemia and other vaccines for cats, depending on the emirate).

Some dog breeds are banned in the UAE, including bull-type dogs in most emirates. Confirm whether your breed is restricted before booking.

Routing from Brussels to Dubai (DXB) or Abu Dhabi (AUH) is straightforward: Emirates SkyCargo and Etihad operate live animal services from BRU or via European hub connections. Flight time to Dubai is approximately 7 hours.

Cost for Belgium to UAE (medium dog): approximately EUR 1,500-3,500 depending on size and whether the import permit requires any third-party agent handling in Dubai.

Belgium to Singapore

Singapore’s Animal and Veterinary Service (AVS) operates a tiered import system based on the rabies risk of the origin country. Belgium falls into the medium-risk tier (Category B as of 2026 AVS guidance).

This means a dog or cat exported from Belgium to Singapore requires an AVS import licence (applied for in Singapore by the owner or receiving agent), a FAVN rabies titre test (blood drawn after a valid rabies vaccination, minimum result 0.5 IU/ml, waiting period of 30 days after a positive result before travel), and a mandatory quarantine at the Singapore animal holding facility (SHF) on arrival for up to 10-30 days depending on the titre test date.

The FAVN titre test alone takes 4-6 weeks to process and must be done at an EU-approved laboratory. Combined with the 30-day wait post-positive result, the total preparation for Belgium-to-Singapore is a minimum of 12-16 weeks. This is not a last-minute move.

The health certificate from Belgium must be endorsed by the FAVV/AFSCA and contain the titre test results. Routing from Brussels to Singapore Changi (SIN) typically goes via Frankfurt (FRA) or Dubai (DXB) as connecting hubs.

Cost for Belgium to Singapore (medium dog, including titre test and quarantine): approximately EUR 2,500-5,000 or more depending on size.

Five things to do before exporting your pet from Belgium

  1. Confirm your vet is authorised by the FAVV/AFSCA to issue AHCs. Not every private vet in Belgium holds official authorisation. Ask directly before booking your appointment.

  2. Check the microchip is ISO 15-digit compliant. Belgium has used EU-standard chips for many years, but if your pet was chipped before 2011 or by a vet trained outside the EU, verify the chip reads correctly on a European scanner.

  3. Start any destination-specific processes early. FAVN titre tests (Singapore, Japan, Australia) add 3-6 months to the timeline. UAE import permits need to be in place before shipping. Do not book flights before these are confirmed.

  4. Choose the right crate size. The IATA Live Animals Regulations specify the minimum crate dimensions based on the animal’s measurements. A crate that is too small will be refused at BRU. Your vet or the dnata ACIC team can advise on sizing.

  5. Brief yourself on Brussels Airlines brachycephalic restrictions. Brussels Airlines bans Bulldogs, French Bulldogs, Pugs, Persian cats, and similar brachycephalic breeds from hold travel entirely. These animals can only fly in cabin if they meet the weight limit. If you have a brachycephalic breed, confirm the airline policy before booking and consider alternative carriers via FRA, AMS, or other hubs where the policy may differ.
