content:
  h1: "Importing Your Pet to Portugal"
  overview: |
    Portugal is an EU member state and uses the EU Pet Travel Scheme. For pets coming from another EU country or a country listed under EU regulations, the process is relatively smooth. For pets from outside the EU, the process is more involved.
    
    Portugal does not require mandatory quarantine for pets arriving from EU-listed countries. Pets from non-listed countries may face more detailed checks including potential quarantine. The microchip, vaccination, and health certificate requirements follow the EU standard.
    
    For most English-speaking countries (UK, USA, Canada, Australia), the process involves a valid EU-format health certificate, rabies vaccination proof, and in some cases a titre test. UK pets specifically need an AHC (Animal Health Certificate) following Brexit.
  sections:
    - heading: "EU rules vs. non-EU origins"
      body: |
        Portugal follows EU pet import rules administered by DGAV (Direção-Geral de Alimentação e Veterinária). For pets from EU/EFTA countries with an EU pet passport, entry is straightforward. For pets from listed countries (UK, USA, Canada, etc.), an AHC or equivalent is required, endorsed by the origin country's official authority.
        
        For pets from non-listed countries, a rabies titre test result and a 3-month waiting period after the successful test date are required before the pet can enter Portugal. Contact DGAV directly to confirm the current listing status of your origin country.
    - heading: "Travelling through Portugal to other EU countries"
      body: |
        If your destination is another EU country but you're routing through Lisbon or Porto, the same rules apply as for Portugal itself. Your pet must meet EU import requirements at the first EU port of entry. Portugal uses TRACES (Trade Control and Expert System) for documentary checks.
faqs:
  - question: "Is quarantine required when bringing my pet to Portugal?"
    answer: |
      No routine quarantine is required in Portugal for pets arriving with correct documentation. Penalty quarantine may apply if any documentation is missing or incorrect. Get everything right before you travel.
  - question: "Is a rabies titre test required to bring my pet to Portugal?"
    answer: |
      No, Portugal does not generally require a rabies titre test. Your pet still needs a valid rabies vaccination and microchip. Verify current requirements with DGAV before travel.
  - question: "Can I bring a cat to Portugal?"
    answer: |
      Yes, cats can be imported into Portugal. The same microchip, vaccination, and health certificate requirements apply as for dogs. Some rules (such as tapeworm treatment) apply to dogs only. Verify current requirements with DGAV.
  - question: "What health certificate does my pet need to enter Portugal?"
    answer: |
      A government-issued veterinary health certificate is required, endorsed by the official veterinary authority in your origin country. The certificate must be issued close to the travel date (typically within 7-10 days). Contact DGAV or a pet transport agent for the required certificate format.
