# Importing Pets to Mauritius: Complete Guide

Mauritius has some of the world's strictest pet import controls. The island is rabies-free and intends to stay that way. The Veterinary Services Division administers a system comparable to Australia's in its strictness: mandatory quarantine, approved country groups, 180-day wait. Import permits are issued in very limited numbers.

## Key requirements for pet import to Mauritius

| Requirement | Detail |
|-------------|--------|
| Microchip | ISO 11784/11785 required |
| Rabies vaccination | Required |
| Titre test | Required for: All pets from most countries. Minimum: 0.5 IU/ml. 180-day wait after successful result. |
| Quarantine | Mandatory quarantine at Government quarantine station, Mauritius (30 days minimum) |
| Import permit | Required -- apply in advance |
| Banned breeds | American Pit Bull Terrier, Dogo Argentino, Japanese Tosa, Fila Brasileiro |
| Authority | Veterinary Services Division, Ministry of Agro-Industry and Food Security (VSD-MU) |

## Why Mauritius is so strict

Mauritius is a small island nation that has never had a recorded case of rabies. The government takes extraordinary measures to keep it that way. Every cat and dog entering Mauritius, regardless of origin, must complete 30 days quarantine at the government facility.

A 180-day residency requirement applies: your pet must have lived in an approved, rabies-free country for 6 months before the travel date (after a successful titre test). This is in addition to the 180-day wait after the titre test.

## Getting a Mauritius import permit

The Veterinary Services Division issues a limited number of import permits. Apply at least 3-4 months before your intended travel date. Permits are not guaranteed.

The permit specifies exactly which country your pet must be in during the 6-month pre-travel period, which approved quarantine station will be used, and which flight routing is accepted. Do not book travel until the permit is confirmed.

## Sources and regulatory authority

Requirements are sourced from Veterinary Services Division, Ministry of Agro-Industry and Food Security (VSD-MU) official guidance. Regulations can change. Always verify current requirements directly with VSD-MU or your pet transport agent before finalising travel arrangements.
