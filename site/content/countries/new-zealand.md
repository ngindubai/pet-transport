overview: |
  Importing a pet to New Zealand is strict. The country is rabies-free and protects that status aggressively. Expect a lengthy preparation timeline and mandatory quarantine on arrival.
sections:
  - heading: "Import requirements for New Zealand"
    body: |
      **Microchip:** Required (ISO 11784/11785). Must be implanted before rabies vaccination
      
      **Rabies Vaccination:** Required. Two vaccinations needed, given not less than 1 month and not more than 12 months apart
      
      **Rabies Titre Test:** Required. Blood drawn at least 180 days before arrival. Minimum titre: 0.5 IU/ml from an approved laboratory
      
      **Quarantine:** Mandatory. Minimum 10 days at MPI-approved quarantine facility in Auckland
      
      **Import Permit:** Required. Must be obtained from MPI (Ministry for Primary Industries) before travel
      
      **Health Certificate:** Required. Issued by government veterinarian within 10 days of export
  - heading: "Breed restrictions in New Zealand"
    body: |
      New Zealand bans the import of the following breeds under the Dog Control Act 1996: American Pit Bull Terrier, Brazilian Fila, Dogo Argentino, Japanese Tosa, and Perro de Presa Canario. Cross-breeds of these dogs are also banned.
  - heading: "Quarantine in New Zealand"
    body: |
      All imported cats and dogs must complete a minimum 10-day quarantine at the MPI-approved facility at Auckland Airport. Quarantine fees are payable by the owner. Booking quarantine space well in advance is strongly recommended as capacity is limited.
faqs:
  - question: "What vaccinations does my pet need to enter New Zealand?"
    answer: "Two rabies vaccinations are required, given at least 1 month apart but no more than 12 months apart. Your pet must also be microchipped before the first vaccination."
  - question: "Is there quarantine in New Zealand?"
    answer: "Yes. All imported cats and dogs must complete a minimum 10-day quarantine at the MPI facility at Auckland Airport. There are no exemptions."
  - question: "How long does it take to prepare a pet for import to New Zealand?"
    answer: "Allow a minimum of 8 to 10 months. The 180-day wait after the titre test is the longest single requirement. Factor in time for two rabies vaccinations, blood tests, and permit processing."
  - question: "What breeds are banned or restricted in New Zealand?"
    answer: "New Zealand bans American Pit Bull Terriers, Dogo Argentinos, Brazilian Fila dogs, Japanese Tosa Inus, and Perro de Presa Canarios. If you own one of these breeds, importation is not permitted under any circumstances. Check the New Zealand Dog Control Act 1996 for the current list before making any plans."
