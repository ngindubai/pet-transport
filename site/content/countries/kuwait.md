content:
  h1: "Importing Your Pet to Kuwait"
  overview: |
    Kuwait requires an import permit from PAM (Public Authority for Agricultural Affairs and Fish Resources) before any pet can enter the country. The permit process typically takes 2-4 weeks. Dogs require a health certificate listing all vaccinations, microchip number, and a statement of good health from an official vet.
    
    Kuwait has breed restrictions, particularly for large and working breeds. A number of breeds are prohibited outright. Check the current list with PAM before making any travel arrangements if your pet might be in a restricted category.
    
    Kuwait International Airport has a dedicated veterinary inspection point for arriving animals. Have all original documents available for inspection. Copies are not accepted.
  sections:
    - heading: "Import permit process for Kuwait"
      body: |
        Submit the import permit application to PAM well in advance. Required documents: microchip certificate, vaccination records (rabies and others), health certificate endorsed by the official vet authority in your origin country.
        
        Allow at least 3-4 weeks for the permit to be processed and issued. The permit has a limited validity period. Your pet must arrive within the permit window. If your travel dates change, you may need to request an amended permit.
    - heading: "Breed restrictions in Kuwait"
      body: |
        Kuwait restricts or prohibits a number of dog breeds. Bull-type breeds, large guard dogs, and breeds perceived as aggressive are commonly on the restricted list. The exact list can change, so always verify with PAM or a specialist agent before travel.
        
        Cats are generally unrestricted by breed in Kuwait. Dogs that are not on any restricted list follow the standard permit process. If in doubt about your breed, get written confirmation from PAM before booking flights.
faqs:
  - question: "Is quarantine required when bringing my pet to Kuwait?"
    answer: |
      No routine quarantine is required in Kuwait for pets arriving with correct documentation. Penalty quarantine may apply if any documentation is missing or incorrect.
  - question: "Is a rabies titre test required to bring my pet to Kuwait?"
    answer: |
      Yes, a rabies titre test is required for pets entering Kuwait from most origins. Blood must be drawn at least 30 days after vaccination, and there is a 90-day waiting period after a successful result before entry.
  - question: "Do I need an import permit to bring my pet to Kuwait?"
    answer: |
      Yes, an import permit is required. Apply through Public Authority for Agricultural Affairs and Fish Resources (PAM) before booking your flight. The permit must be obtained before travel.
  - question: "Can I bring a cat to Kuwait?"
    answer: |
      Yes, cats can be imported into Kuwait. The same microchip, vaccination, and health certificate requirements apply as for dogs. Some rules (such as tapeworm treatment) may apply to dogs only. Verify current requirements with PAM.
  - question: "What health certificate does my pet need to enter Kuwait?"
    answer: |
      A government-issued veterinary health certificate is required, endorsed by the official vet authority in your origin country. The certificate must be issued close to the travel date (typically within 7-10 days). Contact PAM or a pet transport agent for the required format.
