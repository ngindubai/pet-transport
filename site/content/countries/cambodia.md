# Importing Pets to Cambodia: Complete Guide

Cambodia is regulated by the General Directorate of Animal Health and Production (GDAHP). Pets from Cambodia are treated as coming from a rabies-endemic country. An export permit from GDAHP is required. The expat community in Phnom Penh and Siem Reap is active.

## Key requirements for pet import to Cambodia

| Requirement | Detail |
|-------------|--------|
| Microchip | ISO 11784/11785 required |
| Rabies vaccination | Required |
| Titre test | Required for: Check with GDAHP; requirements change. Minimum: 0.5 IU/ml. |
| Quarantine | No routine quarantine for compliant pets. |
| Import permit | Required -- apply in advance |
| Banned breeds | None confirmed |
| Authority | General Directorate of Animal Health and Production, MAFF (GDAHP) |

## Export documentation from Cambodia

GDAHP export permit and endorsed health certificate are both required. Most people apply through a registered Cambodian pet transport agent, as the process requires in-person steps at GDAHP offices.

For the UK, Australia, and New Zealand: titre test + waiting period applies. For Singapore and other Southeast Asian destinations: requirements are simpler but still require a health certificate and often a destination country import permit.

## Airlines from Phnom Penh and Siem Reap

Direct routes from Cambodia are limited. Most pets travel via Bangkok (Suvarnabhumi), Singapore, Kuala Lumpur, or Hong Kong. Check that your transit airline and airport will handle pets in transit. Singapore Changi handles pet transits well. Bangkok can be more complex.

## Sources and regulatory authority

Requirements are sourced from General Directorate of Animal Health and Production, MAFF (GDAHP) official guidance. Regulations can change. Always verify current requirements directly with GDAHP or your pet transport agent before finalising travel arrangements.
