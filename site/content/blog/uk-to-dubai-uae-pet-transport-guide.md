Moving a pet from the UK to the UAE is one of the more manageable international relocations when it is planned properly. There is no quarantine requirement for pets arriving with complete documentation, no titre test for UK-originating animals, and a permit process that, outside of summer, runs to a predictable schedule. The problems that catch people out are almost always the same: booking flights before securing the permit, misjudging the summer heat embargo window, or discovering a breed restriction too late to change plans.

This guide covers what the move actually costs, how the permit system works at emirate level, which airlines operate the route, and what the heat embargo period means for your planning. The figures and regulatory details here are current for 2026.

## How much does pet transport from the UK to the UAE cost in 2026?

For a medium-sized dog travelling unaccompanied cargo from the UK to Dubai or Abu Dhabi, the total all-in cost sits between £2,200 and £3,800. Cats and small dogs, because of their smaller crate dimensions, typically come in between £1,600 and £2,800. These figures cover the full chain: cargo fee, crate, vet certificate, government endorsement, UAE permit, and agent coordination.

Here is where the money goes:

**Airline cargo fee:** Airlines charge by the greater of actual weight or volumetric weight. A 25kg dog in a 90cm x 60cm x 67cm crate will be priced on volumetric weight (roughly 30-35kg equivalent). Budget £700 to £1,400 depending on the airline, route, and season.

**IATA-compliant travel crate:** £80 to £300 new, depending on size. The crate must meet IATA standards: solid base, ventilation on at least three sides, secure door fittings, and absorbent material lining the floor. Airlines reject crates that do not comply, and there is no opportunity to resolve this at the airport.

**Official health certificate (vet fee):** £150 to £350. The certificate must be issued by an APHA-authorised official veterinarian, which is not every vet in the UK. APHA-authorised vets with experience of UAE-specific certificates charge accordingly. The certificate is valid for 10 days from issue, so timing the appointment correctly is essential.

**APHA endorsement fee:** £43 to £120 depending on the certificate type. APHA endorsement adds 1 to 2 working days to the process.

**UAE import permit:** AED 200 to 400 from the destination emirate's authority (approximately £43 to £86 at current rates).

**IPATA agent coordination fee (if used):** £500 to £1,200. A professional agent handles airline cargo booking, the APHA-authorised vet appointment, government endorsement, and destination customs clearance. For a straightforward UK-to-UAE move with a non-restricted breed, some owners manage without an agent. For first-time international moves or for tight timing windows, the coordination fee is money well spent.

For a broader look at international pet transport cost structures, the [full pricing breakdown](/blog/cost-to-transport-a-pet-2026/) covers how these figures compare across other routes.

Always ask for an itemised quote. A single round number without a line-by-line breakdown is a red flag. The items to check are: whether crate purchase is included or extra, who covers destination customs clearance, and whether vet fees are fixed or estimated.

## What is the MOCCAE import permit and how long does it take?

The term "MOCCAE import permit" is widely used for UAE pet imports, but the practical permit for dogs and cats is issued at emirate level, not directly by MOCCAE (the federal Ministry of Climate Change and Environment). MOCCAE oversees wildlife and livestock imports nationally, but the day-to-day pet import permits are the responsibility of each emirate's municipal authority.

**For Dubai:** The Dubai Municipality issues import permits via its e-services portal or the DubaiNow app. Processing typically takes 3 to 5 business days. The fee is approximately AED 200 to 300. The permit is valid for 30 days from the date of issue.

**For Abu Dhabi:** ADAFSA (Abu Dhabi Agriculture and Food Safety Authority) issues permits for Abu Dhabi emirate. Processing typically takes 3 to 7 business days. The fee is approximately AED 200 to 400.

**For other emirates:** Contact the relevant municipality directly. Sharjah, Ras Al Khaimah, and other emirates have their own permit processes that broadly mirror the Dubai and Abu Dhabi systems.

**What you need to apply:**
- Pet species, breed, sex, date of birth
- ISO microchip number
- Rabies vaccination certificate, current and from a veterinarian
- Passport copy of the owner
- Proof of residence in the destination emirate in some cases (tenancy agreement or landlord NOC)

Apply at least 3 weeks before your planned travel date. The permit's 30-day validity window must overlap with your flight date and the 10-day validity window of the health certificate. These timing constraints mean the sequence of steps matters as much as completing them.

The permit number must appear on the official health certificate. Do not have the health certificate issued before the permit is confirmed, because the certificate references the permit number and cannot be amended once endorsed.

The import permit must be in your possession before the pet leaves the UK. Airlines will check for it at check-in. If it is missing or expired, the pet will not be loaded.

## Why do summer heat embargoes matter on this route?

Between June and September, moving a pet from the UK to the UAE becomes significantly harder, and during peak summer it can be temporarily impossible on standard cargo routes.

Ground temperatures at Dubai International Airport (DXB) and Abu Dhabi International Airport (AUH) regularly exceed 40 degrees Celsius during the summer months and can reach 45 to 50 degrees on the hottest days. Aircraft hold temperatures on the ground, during loading and ground handling, can follow ambient conditions before the hold is pressurised and conditioned. For live animals, this period on the ground is the highest-risk phase of the journey.

Airlines have responded with embargo policies that restrict or suspend live animal cargo bookings during the hottest months. The embargo dates vary by airline and by year:

**Emirates SkyCargo:** Typically restricts live animal cargo to Dubai between approximately mid-May and mid-September. The exact dates are published each year. Temperature-controlled hold space on select aircraft types may still be bookable during embargo season, but availability is limited, lead times are longer, and costs are higher.

**Etihad Cargo:** Applies comparable restrictions to Abu Dhabi routes. Embargo windows are published on the Etihad Cargo website and updated annually.

**British Airways / IAG Cargo:** BA applies heat restrictions on Gulf routes during summer. Current embargo dates must be confirmed directly with the cargo desk, as they differ from the Emirates and Etihad schedules.

**Qatar Airways:** Routes via Doha involve a transit environment that must also be assessed for heat. Qatar applies its own embargo policies, and the Doha transit adds a factor for summer moves.

What this means in practice: if your move is flexible on dates, plan arrival in the UAE between October and April. These months give you access to the full range of cargo options, lower prices (because summer is peak demand in reverse, with more owners trying to fit into a narrower window), and fewer breed-specific restrictions layered on top of heat rules.

If your move cannot shift, talk to an IPATA agent early. Some agents have access to temperature-controlled cargo allocations not available through public booking channels. Private air charter is a further option, though at substantially higher cost.

**Brachycephalic breeds face year-round restrictions, not only in summer.** French Bulldogs, Pugs, English Bulldogs, Persian cats, and similar short-nosed breeds are refused or heavily restricted by most cargo carriers on this route regardless of season. This is because the stress of cargo travel increases the risk of respiratory distress in these breeds. If your pet is brachycephalic, you need specialist advice before booking anything else. The options exist, but they are narrower and need to be identified before the rest of the planning begins.

## Which airlines actually fly pet cargo from London to Dubai or Abu Dhabi?

Pet cargo on the UK-UAE route travels as unaccompanied cargo in a pressurised, temperature-controlled hold. Your pet is not checked baggage. Here is what operates the route:

**Emirates SkyCargo (Heathrow to Dubai, DXB):** The most direct option for Dubai arrivals. Emirates operates multiple daily services from Heathrow to Dubai and accepts dogs and cats on applicable flights outside embargo periods. For booking procedures, current breed restrictions, and crate specifications, see the [Emirates pet cargo policy](/pet-transport/airlines/emirates/).

**Etihad Cargo (Heathrow to Abu Dhabi, AUH):** The primary option for Abu Dhabi arrivals, and used as a transit hub for pets continuing to other Gulf destinations or to the Indian subcontinent. Etihad Cargo accepts live animals subject to embargo schedules and breed checks. Confirm current dates and availability with Etihad's cargo desk directly.

**British Airways / IAG Cargo (Heathrow):** BA operates cargo between Heathrow and Dubai on many of its long-haul services. IAG Cargo accepts dogs and cats subject to current breed approval lists and IATA crate specifications. Useful as an alternative when Emirates space is booked out, or when your preferred dates fall within the Emirates embargo window but not BA's.

**Qatar Airways (via Doha, DOH):** The Qatar route involves a transit at Hamad International Airport in Doha before continuing to Dubai or Abu Dhabi. The transit adds an extra leg and an additional set of heat considerations in summer, but Qatar Airways may have availability when direct routes are embargoed. For details on this routing, see [Qatar Airways via Doha](/pet-transport/airlines/qatar-airways/).

**flydubai and Air Arabia:** Budget carriers serving UAE destinations. Pet cargo policies on budget airlines change more frequently than on main carriers. Always confirm directly with the airline at the time of booking.

For any airline, you need three things confirmed before anything else: cargo space available on your date, the airline will accept your pet's breed and crate size, and your documentation window can be made to fit the flight date. These three must align before the health certificate appointment is booked.

## What paperwork does an Animal Health Certificate need for the UAE?

The UK uses an official health certificate (OHC or export health certificate) for pets travelling to third countries outside the EU. For the UAE, this certificate is issued by an APHA-authorised official veterinarian in the UK and endorsed by APHA before the pet departs.

**What the certificate must contain:**

- Pet identification: species, breed, sex, date of birth, colour and distinguishing markings
- Microchip number and date of implantation
- Rabies vaccination record: date administered, product name, batch number, expiry or validity date
- Statement that the pet is clinically fit to travel and free of signs of infectious disease at the time of examination
- Reference to the UAE import permit number

**The documentation sequence:**

1. Microchip implanted (this must happen before the rabies vaccination)
2. Rabies vaccination given after microchip implantation and confirmed as current
3. UAE emirate-level import permit applied for and confirmed
4. Airline cargo space confirmed with the permit details available
5. APHA-authorised vet appointment booked within 10 days before the flight
6. Health certificate issued at the appointment
7. Certificate sent to APHA for endorsement (1 to 2 working days)
8. Endorsed certificate returned, ready to present at cargo check-in

**No titre test required.** UK pets are not subject to the UAE's titre test requirement. The UK is classified as an approved country, so a current rabies vaccination satisfies the vaccination component without an additional blood test.

**Rabies vaccination validity:** The vaccination must be within the validity period stated by the manufacturer, either 1 year or 3 years depending on the vaccine used. A vaccination that lapsed before travel is not valid. Check this before booking anything else. If the vaccination has expired, the pet needs a new vaccination and a minimum 21-day wait before the health certificate can be issued.

For the complete regulatory picture on UAE pet imports, including the breed restriction list and both Dubai and Abu Dhabi permit processes in detail, see [UAE import rules in detail](/pet-transport/countries/united-arab-emirates/).

## Planning the timeline: four to six weeks is realistic

Working back from your intended travel date:

- **At least 4 weeks before:** Apply for the UAE import permit from the destination emirate authority. Confirm cargo space with the airline. Verify your pet's vaccination and microchip records are in order.
- **3 weeks before:** Permit should be confirmed. If there are delays, you still have time to resolve them without pressure.
- **Within 10 days before flight:** APHA-authorised vet appointment for the official health certificate. Certificate must reference the permit number.
- **Allow 1 to 2 working days:** For APHA endorsement of the certificate.
- **Day of travel:** Present endorsed certificate, permit, and all supporting documents at cargo check-in.

The bottlenecks are the permit processing window, the APHA-authorised vet availability, and APHA endorsement. Book the vet appointment early. APHA-authorised vets with UAE experience are not in every town, and their diaries fill up.

If you are working with an IPATA agent, they will map this timeline for you and hold the critical dates. If you are managing the process yourself, write the sequence on paper before touching a booking portal.

The summer embargo period compresses this timeline further. During June to September, cargo space is limited, and the few available slots on temperature-controlled flights fill quickly. If you are moving to the UAE in summer and have a large or brachycephalic dog, speak to an agent before setting a travel date with your employer or landlord.
