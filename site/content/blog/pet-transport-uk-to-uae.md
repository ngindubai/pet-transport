Moving a pet from the UK to the UAE is one of the more demanding international pet transport tasks. The distance is manageable, the airlines are good, and the UAE is genuinely pet-friendly as a destination. What makes it complicated is the combination of a required import permit, a rabies titre test with a three-month waiting period, paperwork that has to be endorsed at a government level before departure, and a summer heat embargo that closes the cargo window for up to four months of the year.

Get those four things right in the right order, and the move itself goes smoothly. Miss any of them and the costs, delays, or welfare risks go up sharply.

## The summer heat embargo: the first question to answer

Before any paperwork, the most important question for UK to UAE pet owners is when they want to travel. Dubai ground temperatures in summer exceed 40°C regularly, and Abu Dhabi is no different. Airline cargo conditions for live animals require ambient temperatures to stay within safe ranges at every stage of the journey, including apron time, loading, and transit. When temperatures exceed carrier-specific thresholds, live animal cargo acceptance is suspended.

Emirates typically suspends live animal cargo acceptance for the warmest months of the year. The specific dates vary by season, but the embargo period broadly covers June through mid-September. Etihad operates a similar policy. During the embargo, there is no practical cargo route for pets from London to Dubai or Abu Dhabi on the major carriers.

If your relocation window falls between June and September, the options are:

- Travel before the embargo, with the animal moving to the UAE before you or on the same flight if scheduling allows.
- Travel after the embargo lifts, typically from mid-September onwards.
- Send the pet via a temperature-controlled cargo routing through a hub that does not share the extreme summer temperatures, though this adds significant cost and complexity.

Planning the move for October through May avoids the embargo entirely and is the most straightforward path.

## What UAE requires: the MOCCAE import permit

The UAE's Ministry of Climate Change and Environment (MOCCAE) is the competent authority for all live animal imports. Every pet entering the UAE needs a MOCCAE import permit, applied for before departure from the UK.

The permit application requires:

- Details of the owner and the animal (species, breed, age, microchip number)
- Proof of microchip implantation (ISO 11784/11785, 15-digit)
- Vaccination records including rabies
- Titre test results for dogs (see below)
- The endorsed health certificate from the UK

MOCCAE processes applications through its online portal. The permit is granted before travel and must accompany the animal. Without the permit, the animal will not be cleared for import at Dubai or Abu Dhabi.

The UAE also imposes breed restrictions for dogs. Certain breeds are prohibited from import, including fighting breeds such as Pitbull Terriers, American Staffordshire Terriers, and Rottweilers. The full current list should be checked with MOCCAE before submitting any permit application, as the list can be updated. If your dog is a prohibited breed, contact MOCCAE directly to understand what options, if any, exist.

## The titre test: the reason the timeline is so long

For dogs moving from the UK to the UAE, a rabies antibody titre test is required. The process has a fixed minimum timeline:

1. The dog must be microchipped (must be done before the rabies vaccination if not already completed).
2. A rabies vaccination must be given or confirmed as current.
3. A blood sample for the titre test is taken at least 30 days after the rabies vaccination.
4. The sample is sent to a UAE-approved laboratory (approved labs include EU-certified labs accepted by MOCCAE).
5. The result must show a minimum rabies antibody level of 0.5 IU/ml (the RNATT standard).
6. If the result passes, a three-month waiting period begins from the date of the blood draw.
7. The dog can travel after the three-month wait has passed and all other documentation is in order.

The minimum possible timeline from blood draw to travel is three months. From a standing start with a puppy or an unvaccinated dog, you need to add the time to vaccinate, wait 30 days, draw blood, and receive results. In practice, planning 5 to 6 months ahead is sensible for dogs.

Cats are not subject to the titre test requirement under current MOCCAE rules. Cat owners should still confirm the current entry requirements with MOCCAE, as the regulations can be updated.

## The paperwork chain: UK vet, APHA, and the health certificate

Post-Brexit, UK pets do not use EU pet passports. For a move from the UK to the UAE, the travel document is an Animal Health Certificate (AHC). The AHC must be issued by an Official Veterinarian (OV) in the UK, who is authorised by the Animal and Plant Health Agency (APHA). Not every vet in the UK is an OV. You need to specifically find and book an APHA-authorised Official Veterinarian.

The AHC for UAE entry covers the standard information: microchip number, rabies vaccination records, titre test results (for dogs), and the issuing vet's details. Once the OV has issued the certificate, it typically needs to be endorsed by APHA before departure, to confirm the OV's authority and the document's validity. The endorsement process adds time and a fee, so factor both in when scheduling the vet appointment.

The AHC has a limited validity period from the date of issue. For UAE moves, the window is tight enough that timing the vet appointment close to the departure date is important. Do not arrange the certificate months in advance if the departure date is not confirmed.

## Airlines: cargo-only from London to Dubai and Abu Dhabi

No major UK to UAE carrier permits pets in the cabin on international long-haul flights. Emirates, Etihad, and British Airways all carry pets in the cargo hold only (with the exception of recognised assistance dogs on some carriers, subject to strict conditions and advance approval).

**Emirates SkyCargo**: Emirates operates frequent LHR to DXB services. Emirates SkyCargo is one of the largest cargo operations in the world, and their live animal handling at Dubai is well-developed. During the non-embargo months, Emirates SkyCargo is the primary carrier option for UK to UAE pet moves. Bookings go through Emirates SkyCargo, not the passenger booking system.

**Etihad Cargo**: Etihad operates LHR to AUH. Etihad Cargo handles live animals and can be an alternative for pets destined for Abu Dhabi or pets whose owners prefer to arrive into Abu Dhabi and drive to Dubai (approximately 90 minutes). Etihad Cargo also applies its own live animal temperature thresholds that overlap with the general summer embargo.

**British Airways World Cargo**: BA operates LHR to DXB and can handle pets in cargo. Confirm current live animal policy with BA World Cargo directly, as BA's cargo animal policies have varied in recent years.

For all carriers, bookings must be made through the cargo division, not the passenger team. The cargo booking requires the IATA crate dimensions, animal weight, microchip number, and the MOCCAE permit number. Book at least 6 to 8 weeks before departure, and confirm closer to travel.

## Costs breakdown

UK to UAE pet transport costs more than most European moves because of the titre test requirements, the APHA endorsement step, and the long-haul cargo fees.

Indicative cost breakdown for 2026:

**Documentation and compliance:**
- MOCCAE import permit fee: approximately £50-£100 depending on current MOCCAE rate
- Rabies titre test (blood draw plus lab fee): £150-£250
- APHA-endorsed Animal Health Certificate: £150-£300 (OV consultation plus APHA endorsement)
- Rabies vaccination update if needed: £50-£80

**Travel costs:**
- Cargo fee LHR to DXB, small cat (under 5kg with crate): approximately £600-£900
- Cargo fee LHR to DXB, medium dog (10-20kg with crate): approximately £1,000-£1,800
- Cargo fee LHR to DXB, large dog (30kg+ with crate): approximately £1,500-£3,000
- IATA-approved travel crate (if needed): £60-£300

**Total approximate costs:**
- Small cat or small dog: £2,200-£2,800
- Medium dog: £2,500-£3,300
- Large dog: £3,000-£3,800+

Using a specialist pet transport agent adds a coordination fee of £300-£800 depending on the level of service, but for a move this complex, the agent manages the permit application, the APHA endorsement, the cargo booking, and the timing across all steps. On a move with a MOCCAE permit and a titre test waiting period, the cost of a coordination error can far exceed the agent fee.

## Planning the timeline: when to start

For dogs travelling to the UAE:

- **6 months before travel**: Start vaccination check and titre test process if not already done. Identify an APHA-authorised Official Veterinarian.
- **4-5 months before travel**: Submit blood sample for titre test after 30-day post-vaccination wait. Apply for MOCCAE import permit (some steps can run in parallel with titre test wait).
- **3 months before travel**: Titre test results received and passing. Three-month waiting period begins.
- **6-8 weeks before travel**: Book cargo with Emirates SkyCargo or Etihad Cargo. Arrange IATA crate if needed.
- **10-14 days before travel**: Arrange OV appointment and AHC issue. APHA endorsement may take several days after OV signs the certificate.
- **Day of travel**: Animal delivered to cargo terminal at Heathrow with AHC, MOCCAE permit, and booking confirmation.

For cats, the titre test waiting period does not apply, so the timeline can compress to 6 to 8 weeks if the rabies vaccination is already current and the microchip is in place.

## A note on the destination: Dubai or Abu Dhabi

Dubai International (DXB) and Abu Dhabi International (AUH) are both practical arrival points. For pets destined for Dubai, Emirates into DXB is the natural choice. For Abu Dhabi or for owners flexible on the arrival point, Etihad into AUH is worth comparing on schedule and cargo capacity.

At both airports, a MOCCAE-authorised inspection takes place on arrival. Carry all documentation, including the MOCCAE permit and the AHC, and be ready for the inspector. Clearance for a compliant animal is typically completed within the same day.

The UAE is a well-organised destination for pets once the paperwork is correctly in place. The country has a large expatriate population with pets, and the infrastructure for veterinary care, pet boarding, and pet-friendly accommodation in Dubai and Abu Dhabi is developed. The move is demanding on the preparation side. On the destination side, it is manageable.
