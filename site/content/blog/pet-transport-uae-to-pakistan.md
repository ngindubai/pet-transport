Pakistan requires advance paperwork that catches a lot of UAE-based pet owners off guard. The country does accept pet imports, and the UAE-Pakistan air corridor is well-served with direct flights, but you need a No Objection Certificate (NOC) from Pakistan's Animal Quarantine Department before your pet can legally enter. Miss that step and the pet may be refused at the border. This guide covers everything you need to know for a UAE-to-Pakistan pet move in 2026.

## Why does Pakistan still require an import permit (NOC)?

Pakistan operates under animal import controls governed by the Ministry of National Food Security and Research. The Animal Quarantine Department (AQD) under that ministry issues NOCs for pets arriving from abroad. The NOC requirement exists to manage disease risks at the border: Pakistan has recorded rabies and other zoonotic diseases, and the import control system is how the country regulates what enters.

The NOC is not a formality. It is a formal authorisation that the airline cargo department and the customs authority at the Pakistani airport will ask to see. Without it, your pet cannot clear the border, and airlines may refuse to accept the cargo booking in the first place.

The requirement applies to both dogs and cats. It applies whether you are relocating permanently or returning after a period abroad.

## How do you apply for the NOC from the Animal Quarantine Department?

The application process is handled by email with Pakistan's Animal Quarantine Department. The contact used by most agents in 2026 is director@aqd.gov.pk. There is no formal online portal for NOC applications at the time of writing: the process is email-based.

**What to include in your NOC application:**

- Pet details: species, breed, age, sex, colour, microchip number (ISO 15-digit)
- Owner details: name, UAE address, Pakistani destination address
- Copies of: valid rabies vaccination certificate (vaccine given between 30 days and 12 months before travel), veterinary health certificate
- Copy of your passport (and visa if relevant)
- Intended date of travel and entry airport (Karachi, Lahore, or Islamabad)

The AQD will respond with either the NOC or a request for additional information. Processing times vary: allow a minimum of 2 to 3 weeks, and longer during peak periods. Apply as early as possible.

**Vaccination timing:** The rabies vaccination must have been administered no less than 30 days before travel and no more than 12 months before the date of travel. If your pet was vaccinated within the last 30 days or the vaccination expired, it will not be accepted.

**Microchip:** ISO 11784/11785 standard chips are accepted. Ensure the microchip number on the health certificate matches the chip exactly.

## Which UAE-Pakistan cargo routes work in 2026?

All UAE-to-Pakistan pet transport is done as cargo, not in-cabin. The UAE-Pakistan corridor is one of the busier air routes in the region, and several carriers operate it.

**Emirates (cargo):** Emirates operates from Dubai (DXB) direct to Karachi Jinnah International (KHI), Lahore Allama Iqbal International (LHE), and Islamabad New Islamabad International (ISB). Emirates SkyCargo handles live animals and is one of the more experienced carriers on this corridor. Emirates is generally the preferred option for larger dogs and crates that may be close to dimension or weight limits.

**Flydubai (cargo):** Flydubai operates from DXB to all three Pakistani gateways. Flydubai Cargo accepts live animals on suitable aircraft. The service is direct on most routes, making it a practical choice for medium-sized crates.

**Air Arabia (cargo from Sharjah):** Air Arabia operates from Sharjah (SHJ) to Pakistani gateways including KHI and LHE. Air Arabia Cargo capacity for live animals should be confirmed on the specific route before booking.

**PIA (Pakistan International Airlines):** PIA operates from DXB and Abu Dhabi to Pakistani gateways. PIA Cargo handles live animals. As the national carrier, PIA can be a useful option, particularly for Islamabad-bound shipments. Confirm live animal acceptance and crate dimension limits in advance.

The routing decision often comes down to which carrier will accept your specific crate dimensions and animal weight on the date you need. Emirates SkyCargo has the most consistent live animal acceptance policy for this route.

## What does UAE to Pakistan pet transport cost?

Costs on this corridor are lower than on intercontinental routes, given the direct 3 to 4 hour flights. Typical figures for 2026:

These are estimates. Emirates SkyCargo charges by weight and crate dimensions. A small dog in a 50cm crate will be at the lower end. A large breed in a 100cm crate will be at the higher end or potentially above it. Request a written quote from the cargo carrier using the actual crate dimensions and weight before committing.

Agent fees vary widely. Some UAE-based pet transport agents charge a flat fee; others charge a percentage. If you handle the NOC application and cargo booking yourself, you can reduce the total cost, but the NOC application process requires careful attention to documentation. An error in the NOC application often means starting again.

## Customs at Karachi, Lahore and Islamabad: what to expect

On arrival in Pakistan, the process typically runs as follows:

**At the airport:** The cargo agent or airline notifies you (or your designated representative) when the shipment has landed. You or your representative must collect the pet from the cargo terminal.

**Documentation check:** The AQD officer or customs official at the arrival airport checks: the NOC, the MOCCAE health certificate, the rabies vaccination certificate, and the microchip documentation. The microchip is scanned to confirm it matches the documentation.

**Customs clearance:** Pakistan does not impose a routine quarantine on pet dogs and cats from the UAE with correct documentation. The pet clears customs once the documentation is verified. If documentation is incomplete, the pet may be held at the airport facility at the owner's expense while the issue is resolved.

**Collecting the pet:** Once cleared, the pet is released to you or your representative. Arrange collection as quickly as possible after the flight arrives: airport animal holding facilities vary in quality.

Karachi (KHI) is the largest and most established entry point for live animal imports. Lahore (LHE) and Islamabad (ISB) are viable but have less frequent live animal cargo arrivals. If you have flexibility on destination city, Karachi tends to offer the most straightforward clearance process.

## FAQs: UAE to Pakistan pet relocation

**Do I need the NOC before booking the flight?**
Yes. Get the NOC first, then book cargo space. Most cargo agents will want to see the NOC reference or copy before confirming the booking. Do not reverse this order.

**Can my pet travel in-cabin from UAE to Pakistan?**
No. Pakistani import regulations require pets to travel as cargo. In-cabin pet travel is not an option on this route.

**Does my dog need any treatment before leaving the UAE?**
Yes. MOCCAE (the UAE Ministry of Climate Change and Environment) requires an anti-parasitic treatment within 14 days before travel as part of the health certificate process. Your UAE vet handles this at the same time as issuing the MOCCAE health certificate. The certificate is valid for 30 days from the date of issue.

**What happens if my flight is delayed beyond the 30-day health certificate validity?**
The MOCCAE certificate is valid for 30 days. If your travel is delayed past that, you will need a new certificate from a UAE vet. Plan travel close to the certificate issue date to avoid this.

**Is there a Pakistani government fee for the NOC?**
The NOC application to AQD is currently handled by email and does not carry a published government fee. If using an agent, their service fee covers the application management. Confirm with AQD directly if the process has changed by your travel date.

**Can I bring more than one pet?**
Yes, but each pet needs its own NOC, health certificate, and documentation set. Multiple pets can travel on the same flight as separate cargo consignments.

**What breeds are restricted in Pakistan?**
Pakistan does not publish a formal breed restriction list for imports at the national level, but certain municipalities have local restrictions on dog breeds. Confirm with the destination city authority if you are importing a breed typically considered a restricted type.
