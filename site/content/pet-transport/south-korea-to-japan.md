## South Korea to Japan: Despite Geographic Proximity, Maximum Complexity

Seoul to Tokyo is a 2-hour flight. The pet relocation process for that journey takes **40–52 weeks**. This surprises many owners, South Korea's proximity to Japan and its well-developed veterinary infrastructure seem like they should make it easy. Japan's classification system does not work that way.

South Korea is a non-designated country under AQS rules. That means the full 180-day protocol, no exceptions.

---

## The 180-Day Protocol from Seoul

**Step 1: Microchip (ISO 11784/11785)**
Implant before any vaccination. Verify the chip reads on a universal ISO scanner. Korean vets routinely use ISO-compliant chips.

**Step 2: Rabies Vaccination #1**
Administer after chip confirmation. Inactivated vaccine, pet must be at least 91 days old.

**Step 3: Rabies Vaccination #2**
Minimum 30 days after vaccination #1. Record vaccine brand, batch number and expiry date precisely.

**Step 4: FAVN Titre Test**
Blood sample drawn no earlier than 30 days after vaccination #2. Sent to VSL (UK), ANSES (France) or Sciensano (Belgium) via biological sample courier. Result must show ≥0.5 IU/ml. **The 180-day wait starts from the blood draw date.**

**Step 5: 180-Day Residency**
The animal stays in South Korea for 180 days from the titre test blood draw. Korean winters and summers present no specific issues for this, South Korea's climate is manageable for most breeds.

**Step 6: QIAS Export Health Certificate**
Arranged through QIAS 10–14 days before departure. Your Korean vet prepares the health certificate; QIAS inspects and endorses. The certificate is valid for 10 days.

**Step 7: AQS 40-Day Advance Notice**
File via MAFF's AQS online portal at least 40 days before arrival. Upload all documents. AQS confirms the inspection appointment at NRT, HND, KIX or NGO.

**Step 8: Arrival Quarantine**
Your pet enters the AQS quarantine facility at the arrival airport. Duration 2–7 days. Owners cannot visit during quarantine. Release with an AQS clearance certificate.

## Getting the Titre Test Done from Korea

This is the logistical bottleneck for Seoul-based owners. Steps:

1. Find a Korean vet experienced in international relocations (several clinics in Seoul specialise in this)
2. After vaccination #2, wait 30 days exactly
3. Have the vet draw blood into the EDTA tubes specified by your chosen lab
4. Package according to IATA P650 dangerous goods rules for biological substances
5. Book a courier (FedEx International Priority or DHL Medical/Biological) directly to the overseas lab
6. Email the lab a scanned copy of the submission form
7. Results returned in 5–10 business days; the lab emails a PDF certificate

Keep both the lab result PDF and the original paper certificate, AQS will want both.

## Airlines: ICN to NRT/HND

**Korean Air (KE)** is the most frequently used carrier on this route for pet cargo. KE operates multiple daily ICN–NRT and ICN–HND services. Pet cargo must be booked through Korean Air Cargo at least 72 hours in advance.

**Asiana Airlines** also offers ICN–NRT/HND pet cargo with similar requirements.

**ANA** and **JAL** accept cargo on their ICN services. Japan-side AQS coordination is smooth with these carriers given their domestic experience with AQS procedures.

None of the low-cost carriers on this route accept international pet cargo, stick with full-service carriers.
