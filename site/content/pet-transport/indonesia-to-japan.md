## Indonesia to Japan: Plan a Full Year Ahead

This is one of the most demanding pet relocation routes in Asia. Japan's AQS protocol for non-designated countries — which includes Indonesia — requires a minimum of **40 weeks** of preparation, and 52 weeks is a more realistic planning horizon once you account for lab turnaround times, vet scheduling and AQS advance notice filing.

If you are relocating from Jakarta, Bali, Surabaya or elsewhere in Indonesia to Japan, begin the protocol the day you know you are moving.

---

## The AQS 180-Day Protocol: Every Step

**Microchip (ISO 11784/11785) — first, before anything else**
AQS requires the microchip to be implanted *before* any vaccination is given. A vaccination administered before the chip is in place cannot be counted. Confirm the chip reads on a universal ISO scanner at the vet clinic and get the chip number in writing.

**Rabies vaccination #1**
Given after microchip confirmation. Must be an inactivated vaccine. The pet must be at least 91 days old.

**Rabies vaccination #2**
Minimum 30 days after vaccination #1. Your vet records the precise date. Both vaccination records must state the vaccine brand, batch number and expiry.

**FAVN Titre Test**
Blood sample drawn no earlier than 30 days after vaccination #2. Sent via approved biological courier to an AQS-approved laboratory (VSL/UK, ANSES/France, Sciensano/Belgium). Result must show ≥0.5 IU/ml by the FAVN method. **The 180-day wait starts from the date of blood collection, not the test result.**

**180-Day Residency Period**
The animal must remain in Indonesia (or another AQS-acceptable country) for the full 180 days from the titre test blood draw. Do not transit through unacceptable countries during this period.

**BARANTAN Export Health Certificate**
Obtained from BARANTAN 7–14 days before departure. Must include all vaccination records, titre test details and confirmation of residency. BARANTAN inspectors may physically inspect the animal before endorsement.

**AQS 40-Day Advance Notice**
File online via MAFF's AQS advance notification portal at least 40 days before arrival. Upload all documents. AQS assigns an inspection slot at the destination airport quarantine facility.

**Arrival and Quarantine**
Your pet enters the AQS quarantine facility at NRT, HND, KIX or NGO on arrival. Duration is 2–7 days. If all documentation is correct and the animal is healthy, 2–3 days is common. Owners cannot visit during quarantine.

## BARANTAN Offices and Endorsement

BARANTAN offices relevant to live animal exports are located in Jakarta (Soekarno-Hatta area), Denpasar (Bali), Surabaya and other major cities. Call or visit your nearest BARANTAN office early in the process to confirm their current requirements and the health certificate format they will accept for Japan.

Some Indonesian vets experienced in international relocations can assist with BARANTAN documentation — using a vet with prior Japan export experience significantly reduces the risk of documentation errors.

---

## Airlines from Indonesia to Japan

**Garuda Indonesia (GA)** is the most direct option with CGK–NRT and CGK–HND services. Garuda accepts pet cargo with advance booking through Garuda Cargo. Confirm current embargo periods for brachycephalic breeds.

**Singapore Airlines via SIN** and **Cathay Pacific via HKG** are reliable alternatives with strong pet cargo track records. Both have cargo ground teams experienced with AQS documentation at Narita and Haneda.

**ANA and JAL** do not codeshare from Jakarta but accept manifested cargo arriving via partner flights. Confirm handling arrangements when booking.

## Cost Estimate

This is an expensive move. Approximate costs:

- BARANTAN health certificate endorsement: IDR 500,000–1,000,000
- FAVN titre test (lab + courier): approximately USD 150–250
- IATA cargo crate: USD 80–400 depending on size
- Airline cargo Jakarta–Tokyo: USD 400–1,200 depending on weight
- AQS quarantine facility fees (Japan): approximately JPY 15,000–40,000
- Professional relocation agent: USD 800–2,000

Total: approximately USD 1,500–4,000 depending on size of animal and whether an agent is used. Given the complexity, most owners on this route use a specialist agent.
