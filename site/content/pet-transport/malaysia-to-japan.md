## Malaysia to Japan: The Full AQS Protocol

Relocating a pet from Malaysia to Japan is one of the most documentation-intensive moves in Asia. Japan's Animal Quarantine Service (AQS) classifies Malaysia as a **non-designated country**, which triggers the full 180-day pre-entry protocol. There are no shortcuts and no waivers, the process takes what it takes.

Budget **40 to 52 weeks** from the day you start. Most owners who try to compress this end up repeating steps or facing quarantine extension. Begin on your first vet visit.

---

## Step-by-Step: The AQS 180-Day Protocol

**Step 1: Microchip first (ISO 11784/11785)**
The microchip must be implanted *before* any other step. If a rabies vaccination was given before the chip, AQS will not count it and the clock restarts. Confirm the chip is ISO-compliant and reads on a universal scanner.

**Step 2: Rabies vaccination #1**
Administered after microchip confirmation. The vaccine must be an inactivated (killed) product and the pet must be at least 91 days old at the time of vaccination.

**Step 3: Rabies vaccination #2**
Given no sooner than 30 days after the first dose. Your vet records both dates precisely, AQS will scrutinise these.

**Step 4: FAVN titre test**
Blood sample collected no earlier than 30 days after vaccination #2. Sent to an **AQS-approved overseas laboratory**, VSL in the UK, ANSES in France, or Sciensano in Belgium are the most commonly used. Labs within Malaysia are not approved. Confirm the result is ≥0.5 IU/ml. **The 180-day countdown starts from the date of blood collection, not the result date.**

**Step 5: 180-day wait**
Your pet must remain in a country acceptable to AQS (Malaysia qualifies) for the full 180 days from titre test blood draw. No travel to a third country that would reset the protocol.

**Step 6: DVS Malaysia export health certificate**
Obtained from the Department of Veterinary Services 10 days before departure. The certificate must be issued by a government-authorised vet and include all vaccination dates, microchip number, titre test result and laboratory reference.

**Step 7: AQS 40-day advance notice**
File online via the MAFF AQS advance notification system at least 40 days before arrival. Upload all documents. AQS assigns an inspection appointment at the arrival airport.

**Step 8: Arrival and quarantine**
Upon landing at NRT, HND, KIX or NGO, your pet goes directly to the AQS quarantine facility. Quarantine lasts 2–7 days. If documentation is complete and correct, 2–3 days is typical. Owners receive a release certificate once the animal clears.

## Airlines from Kuala Lumpur to Japan

**Malaysia Airlines (MH)** operates direct KUL–NRT and KUL–HND services and accepts pets as excess baggage (small dogs and cats in cabin on some domestic sectors, cargo only for international). Confirm pet cargo booking 72 hours in advance with MH's cargo desk.

**ANA and JAL** accept pet cargo on their KUL routes. Both have experience with AQS documentation and their cargo teams are familiar with the 40-day notice requirement.

**Cathay Pacific via HKG** and **Singapore Airlines via SIN** are viable connecting options with established pet cargo programmes. Connections add transit time: ensure the total journey does not exceed safe cargo hold limits for your pet's breed.

Avoid budget carriers for this route. The AQS arrival procedure requires same-day clearance and dedicated handling, only carriers with proper cargo infrastructure should be used.

## DVS Malaysia Export Requirements

The Department of Veterinary Services issues health certificates for live animal exports. For Japan specifically, the DVS vet will complete an AQS-format health certificate confirming:

- Microchip number and implant date
- Rabies vaccination dates, vaccine brand and batch number
- Titre test date, laboratory name, reference number and result
- That the animal has resided in Malaysia throughout the 180-day period
- That the animal is free from signs of infectious disease

Allow 5–10 working days for DVS to prepare the certificate. Take the original plus two certified copies on travel day.

---

## Breed Restrictions

Japan has no national breed ban list for importation, but airlines have their own restrictions. Brachycephalic breeds (Pugs, French Bulldogs, Bulldogs, Persian cats) face cargo embargoes on many carriers. Confirm with your chosen airline well in advance. AQS itself does not restrict breeds.

## Cost Estimate

The Malaysia to Japan pet move is expensive relative to regional routes:

- DVS export health certificate: ~MYR 200–400
- FAVN titre test (including overseas lab courier): MYR 800–1,400
- IATA-compliant cargo crate: MYR 400–1,200 depending on size
- Airline cargo fees KUL–NRT/HND: MYR 1,500–4,000 depending on weight
- AQS quarantine facility fees: approximately JPY 15,000–40,000 (charged per day in Japan)
- Professional relocation agent (optional but recommended): MYR 2,500–5,000

Total end-to-end cost typically runs MYR 6,000–12,000 (approximately USD 1,300–2,600) excluding the agent fee.

## Common Mistakes That Extend Quarantine

1. **Microchip implanted after the first vaccination**: the entire vaccination sequence is void for AQS purposes
2. **Titre test blood drawn before day 30 post-vaccination #2**: the test result will not be accepted
3. **Using a non-approved laboratory**: any lab not on the AQS approved list produces an invalid result
4. **Forgetting the 40-day advance notice**: AQS will not accept animals without prior registration; the flight must be changed
5. **Documents not in the correct AQS format**: get the AQS health certificate template from the Japan MAFF website and have your DVS vet complete that specific form

Given the complexity, most owners on this route work with a professional pet relocation agent who manages the protocol calendar, overseas lab logistics and AQS advance notice filing.
