## Indonesia to South Korea: A Manageable Move

Moving a pet from Indonesia to South Korea is one of the more approachable routes in the region. No titre test, no quarantine, clear government processes on both sides. Budget **4–8 weeks** to do it properly.

---

## Requirements Summary

- **Microchip**: ISO 11784/11785, implanted before vaccination
- **Rabies vaccination**: Current, administered at least 30 days before travel
- **BARANTAN health certificate**: Government-endorsed, within 10 days of departure
- **QIAS pre-registration**: Online, at least 7 days before arrival
- **Entry airport**: Incheon International (ICN) only for live animal imports

---

## BARANTAN Health Certificate Process

BARANTAN offices are located across Indonesia in all major cities. The process:

1. Have your vet prepare a health certificate including microchip number, vaccination dates, breed description and a declaration of clinical health
2. Book a BARANTAN appointment and bring the animal for physical inspection
3. BARANTAN inspectors verify documents and examine the animal
4. Certificate is stamped and endorsed, valid for 10 days from endorsement

For Bali-based owners, the BARANTAN office is near Ngurah Rai Airport. For Jakarta owners, the relevant BARANTAN office is at Soekarno-Hatta. Allow extra time for peak travel periods and public holidays.

---

## QIAS Pre-Registration

Create an account on the QIAS online portal and submit your registration at least 7 days before arrival. Upload a scanned copy of the BARANTAN-endorsed health certificate and vaccination records. QIAS will confirm your registration and assign an inspection slot at Incheon.

On arrival at ICN, go to the quarantine inspection counter before luggage collection. The inspector will check documents, scan the microchip and visually assess the animal. Once cleared, you receive a release document and can proceed.

---

## Airlines

**Garuda Indonesia (GA)** flies CGK–ICN and accepts pets as cargo. Advance booking with Garuda Cargo is required.

**Korean Air (KE)** operates CGK–ICN with pet cargo acceptance. Korean Air's ground handling at Jakarta has experience with BARANTAN documents.

**Asiana Airlines** serves CGK–ICN with pet cargo options.

**AirAsia X** connects via Kuala Lumpur: adding a transit may be an option if direct fares are not available, but ensure transit does not affect BARANTAN certificate validity (10-day window).
