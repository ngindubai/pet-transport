  overview: |
    Kosovo doesn't appear by name on the CDC's high-risk countries list for dog rabies, last updated 15 April 2026, which puts it in the same simplified bracket as the UK, Canada and Australia for US entry purposes. No titre test, no CDC-registered facility booking, just the free CDC Dog Import Form. Given the region's rabies history, that surprises most owners who've already researched the UK side of this route, where Kosovo sits firmly in the stricter unlisted category.

    The practical part of the journey needs more thought than the paperwork does. There's no direct Pristina to US flight, and Wizz Air, the airport's biggest carrier, won't take a pet under any circumstances. Turkish Airlines via Istanbul and Austrian Airlines via Vienna both offer genuine single-carrier routings into US gateway cities instead.

    Budget two to three months. Most of that is booking logistics and the FVA-endorsed certificate, not a fixed regulatory wait.
  sections:
    - heading: "Why the CDC's low-risk pathway applies here"
      body: |
        The CDC's rules for dogs split the world into two camps: high-risk and everything else. High-risk countries trigger a titre test, a CDC-registered facility booking, and tight restrictions on where the flight can land. Everything else, the low-risk category, just needs the free CDC Dog Import Form and a healthy-looking dog on arrival.

        Kosovo sits in the second group. It isn't named on the high-risk list, so none of the extra machinery applies. Rabies vaccination is recommended rather than mandated, and there's no minimum waiting period built into the federal side of this route at all.

        This is worth treating as a snapshot rather than a permanent guarantee. The CDC updates its high-risk list periodically, and a regional change in reported rabies activity could shift Kosovo onto it. Check the current list before you commit to a travel date.
    - heading: "Getting out of Pristina: Wizz Air isn't an option"
      body: |
        Pristina's cheapest, most frequent flights run on Wizz Air, and none of that capacity is available to a pet owner. Wizz Air excludes all dogs and cats from cabin and hold alike, with the sole exception of a certified assistance dog arranged well in advance.

        Turkish Airlines and Austrian Airlines fill the gap. Turkish routes through Istanbul to more than a dozen US cities on its own aircraft the whole way; Austrian routes through Vienna to a nonstop Vienna-JFK service six days a week. Both keep the pet under one airline's care rather than switching carriers mid-journey.
    - heading: "What could still change"
      body: |
        Kosovo's low-risk treatment under CDC rules doesn't reflect a formal bilateral agreement the way some countries' listings do; it simply reflects the country's absence from a periodically updated named list. That distinction matters because it means the classification can move without much advance warning.

        None of this affects the paperwork side today. Kosovo's own FVA endorsement process runs the same way regardless of the US classification, and the UK and US both recognising Kosovo's independence since 18 February 2008 means the certificate itself is accepted without complication either way.
