  sections:
    - heading: "Sweden's Jordbruksverket: What to Expect on Arrival"
      body: |
        Jordbruksverket (Swedish Board of Agriculture, jordbruksverket.se) is the competent authority for pet imports into Sweden. As an EU member state, Sweden applies Commission Delegated Regulation (EU) 2026/131 and the listed/unlisted country framework under Implementing Regulation (EU) 2024/1130.

        For a pet from Panama, Jordbruksverket requires at Stockholm Arlanda Airport (ARN):

        Microchip: ISO 11784/11785, implanted before the rabies vaccination. Scanned on arrival.

        Rabies vaccination: Current, post-microchip, with 21 days elapsed since the primary vaccination.

        FAVN titre test: Result of 0.5 IU/ml or above from an EU-approved laboratory. Blood drawn at least 30 days after vaccination. 90-day wait from blood draw date must have elapsed before arrival.

        Health certificate: EU Annex IV non-commercial format, DINASA-endorsed, issued within 10 days of travel. Titre test details included.

        No Echinococcus treatment required (unlike Norway and Finland). No quarantine. No import permit.

    - heading: "Panama vs Listed Countries: The Regulatory Gap"
      body: |
        One question that comes up frequently from families moving from Panama to Sweden is why the process is so much more involved than for a neighbour like Jamaica.

        The answer lies in the listed/unlisted classification under EU pet import rules. Jamaica is listed under Commission Implementing Regulation (EU) 2024/1130, which means the EU has assessed Jamaica's rabies control programme and determined that a titre test is unnecessary. Panama has not received this designation. The EU classifies Panama as an unlisted third country, which triggers the full titre test and 90-day wait pathway.

        There is no shortcut. The only way to reduce the preparation timeline is to begin as early as possible. Contact PetTransportGlobal as soon as your relocation date is set.

    - heading: "Flight Routing from Panama to Stockholm"
      body: |
        Stockholm Arlanda Airport (ARN) is the primary cargo entry point for Sweden. All live animal shipments from Panama route via a European hub, most commonly Amsterdam (AMS), Madrid (MAD), or Frankfurt (FRA).

        Copa Airlines is Panama's main carrier to European hubs. PTY to AMS pairs well with a KLM or SAS connection to ARN. SAS Scandinavian Airlines operates AMS to ARN and handles live animal cargo.

        Copa to MAD via Iberia Cargo is another option. From MAD, SAS or a partner carrier connects to ARN. Madrid is Iberia's home hub and the Central America to Europe cargo route is well established.

        Lufthansa via Frankfurt gives a third routing choice. FRA to ARN is served by SAS and Lufthansa. For large dogs, Frankfurt's animal centre is a good transit point.

        Gothenburg (GOT) and Malmö (MMX) airports handle limited cargo. ARN is strongly recommended as the arrival point for international pet shipments.

    - heading: "Sweden vs Norway and Finland: A Key Difference"
      body: |
        Families comparing Nordic destinations should note a meaningful practical difference between Sweden and its neighbours.

        Norway and Finland both require dogs from non-exempt countries (including Panama) to receive a mandatory Echinococcus (fox tapeworm) treatment administered by a vet before arrival. Norway's window is 24-120 hours before arrival; Finland's is 1-5 days. This adds a logistical step: a vet visit must be arranged at the layover airport during transit.

        Sweden has no such requirement. Echinococcus multilocularis is already present in Sweden's wildlife, so the pre-border treatment rule does not apply. For families moving from Panama, choosing Sweden over Norway or Finland removes one logistical complication from an already complex process.

        Regular deworming is still good veterinary practice once in Sweden. Your Swedish vet will advise on a schedule.

    - heading: "Documentation Checklist"
      body: |
        Before departure from Panama, confirm you have the following:

        Original DINASA-endorsed EU health certificate, issued within 10 days of travel. Check the issue date carefully.

        Original FAVN laboratory report showing the blood draw date, result in IU/ml, and the laboratory's name and approval number.

        Vaccination record with microchip number, vaccine product name, batch number, date administered, and expiry.

        Your passport and the airline airway bill for the live animal shipment.

        Copies of all documents held separately from the originals.

        At ARN, Jordbruksverket will inspect the originals. Do not send originals ahead with a courier; carry them or confirm they are with the cargo documentation.

faqs:
  - question: "If our pet has already completed the titre test pathway, how long is the result valid for Swedish entry?"
    answer: "A passing FAVN result (0.5 IU/ml or above) remains valid for EU entry for the lifetime of the current rabies vaccination, as long as the vaccination does not expire. There is no expiry on the titre test result itself. If the rabies vaccination expires and a booster is given within the validity period of the previous dose, no new titre test is needed. If the vaccination lapses entirely and a new primary course is required, a new titre test and 90-day wait would be needed before EU re-entry."
  - question: "Does Sweden require any additional registration or documentation for imported pets after arrival?"
    answer: "Sweden does not require a separate post-arrival registration with a national pet database in the same way that some other EU countries do, but registration with a local vet and an annual rabies booster are good practice. Some municipalities require dogs to be registered for local dog tax purposes. Check with your local commune (kommun) after arrival."
  - question: "Can we bring more than one pet from Panama to Sweden on the same journey?"
    answer: "Yes, up to five pets per owner can travel on a single EU health certificate for non-commercial movement. Each pet must be individually identified by microchip on the certificate, and each must have its own titre test result and vaccination records. If you have more than five pets, a second certificate is required. For large numbers of animals, additional cargo bookings will be needed as cargo space per animal requires its own slot."
