  overview: "Taking a pet from Estonia to Norway is straightforward by EU standards, with one important addition for dogs: Norway requires a tapeworm treatment between 1 and 5 days before arrival, recorded in the EU pet passport. Outside of that, the route follows standard EU pet travel rules. Estonia is a listed country for Norway, so no titre test or quarantine applies.   sections:
    - heading: "Tallinn to Oslo: flight options and what to book"
      body: |
        SAS is the main option for dogs travelling in cargo, connecting Tallinn (TLL) to Oslo Gardermoen (OSL) via Stockholm (ARN) or Copenhagen (CPH). SAS Cargo accepts live animals in the hold on these routes, but book early: cargo spaces for pets are limited and fill quickly. Norwegian Air Shuttle operates some direct TLL-OSL services and is a good cabin-only option for cats and small dogs. airBaltic connects via Riga (RIX) with live animal cargo on longer legs, which can work well if SAS is booked up. When connecting dogs through a Scandinavian hub, keep layovers as short as possible and confirm the connecting flight also accepts live animal cargo before booking.

    - heading: "Tapeworm treatment: the one rule Norway adds for dogs"
      body: |
        Norway requires tapeworm treatment for every dog arriving from outside Norway, regardless of origin country. This applies even when travelling from fellow EU member states such as Estonia. The treatment must use Praziquantel and must be administered by a vet between 1 and 5 days before the dog's arrival in Norway. It cannot be done too early: if your dog is treated 6 or more days before arrival, you will need a second treatment closer to travel. The vet records the treatment date, drug name, and dose in the EU pet passport. Border control in Norway checks for this entry. Cats travelling on the same journey are not subject to this requirement.

faqs:
  - question: "Does my cat need tapeworm treatment before entering Norway from Estonia?"
    answer: "No. The tapeworm treatment requirement in Norway applies only to dogs. Cats travelling from Estonia to Norway on a valid EU pet passport with a current rabies vaccination are not required to have tapeworm treatment."
  - question: "How long before my dog's arrival must the tapeworm treatment be given?"
    answer: "Between 1 and 5 days before arrival in Norway. No earlier and no later. The vet records it in the EU pet passport with the date, drug name and dose. If travel is delayed after treatment is given, you may need to repeat it to stay within the window."
  - question: "Do I need a titre test to bring my dog from Estonia to Norway?"
    answer: "No. Estonia is an EU member state and is a listed country for Norway. Titre tests are only required for dogs travelling from unlisted countries. No titre test, no quarantine."
  - question: "Can I travel with a large dog in cargo from Tallinn to Oslo?"
    answer: "Yes, via SAS Cargo through Stockholm or Copenhagen, or via airBaltic cargo through Riga. You need an IATA-approved travel crate sized correctly for your dog, and you must book the cargo space directly with the airline. Confirm breed restrictions at the time of booking, particularly for brachycephalic breeds."
