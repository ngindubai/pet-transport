content:
  h1: "Pet Transport Canada to Dominican Republic"
  overview: |
    Air Canada and WestJet both treat this as a mainstream route, not a niche one, thanks to the sheer volume of Canadians heading to Punta Cana every winter. That translates into genuinely useful direct flight options from Toronto, Montreal and Calgary, a real advantage for anyone moving a pet rather than just booking a holiday.

    The Dominican Republic's own requirements stay light for Canadian pets: no titre test, no import permit for up to five animals, just a microchip, current vaccination, and a CFIA-endorsed health certificate issued within 15 days of travel.

  sections:
    - heading: "Why this corridor asks for so little"
      body: |
        For up to five personal pets, the Dominican Republic doesn't require an import permit at all. A microchip, rabies vaccination given between 30 days and 12 months before arrival, and a CFIA-endorsed health certificate issued within 15 days of travel cover the full requirement. No titre test applies, since the Dominican Republic only imposes that on countries it classifies as high-rabies-risk, and Canada isn't one of them. DGAG officials check the microchip against the paperwork on arrival at either SDQ or PUJ, and compliant pets clear without quarantine.

    - heading: "CFIA's export process"
      body: |
        A CFIA-accredited veterinarian issues the health certificate in the format the Dominican Republic requires. There's no separate export permit for a personal pet leaving Canada, which keeps this side of the process comparatively quick, provided the certificate is timed to fall within the 15-day window Santo Domingo and Punta Cana both enforce.

    - heading: "Winter-sun capacity works in your favour"
      body: |
        Air Canada's Toronto-based network into both SDQ and PUJ runs heavy seasonal schedules given how popular the Dominican Republic is with Canadian winter travellers. WestJet adds Calgary departures into the mix. That volume generally means more available cargo space than routes with thinner passenger demand, though booking early during peak winter months is still worth doing.
