  sections:
    - heading: "FAVN titre test and Panama's 72-hour certificate window"
      body: |
        Panama is classified as an unlisted third country under EU Implementing Regulation 2026/636, making the FAVN titre test mandatory for Denmark. Blood must be drawn at least 30 days after the most recent rabies vaccination and must show at least 0.5 IU/ml at an EU-approved laboratory. The 90-day waiting period runs from the blood draw date.

        Once the 90 days have elapsed, the export health certificate can be issued. In Panama, this certificate is produced through the SITERPA online portal and countersigned by DECA within MIDA. It is valid for only 72 hours from the DECA countersignature. The EU-format health certificate for Denmark is then valid for 10 days from the MIDA endorsement. Plan the SITERPA appointment to fall within 72 hours of your actual departure time from Tocumen International Airport (PTY).

    - heading: "Danish breed restrictions and Border Control Posts"
      body: |
        Before any other preparation, confirm your dog is not one of the 13 breeds prohibited under the Danish Dog Act. The restricted list includes pit bull terrier, Tosa Inu, American Staffordshire Terrier, Fila Brasileiro, Dogo Argentino, American Bulldog, Boerboel, Kangal, Central Asian Ovtcharka, Caucasian Ovtcharka, South Russian Ovtcharka, Tornjak, and Sarplaninac. Crossbreeds are also prohibited. No documentation or vaccination history can override this ban.

        Denmark designates three airports as Border Control Posts for non-EU arrivals: CPH (Kastrup), BLL (Billund), and AAL (Aalborg). Copenhagen is the largest and most consistently staffed. Routing from Panama is most practical via Iberia's direct PTY-MAD service, then onward to CPH.
