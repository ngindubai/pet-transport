  h1: "Pet Transport from Ireland to Armenia"
  overview: |
    Armenia does not require a titre test for EU-origin pets, and there is no quarantine for compliant arrivals at Zvartnots International Airport in Yerevan. Ireland's EU membership means the export process is the standard EU official health certificate route through DAFM, which is well-established and broadly understood by Irish vets.

    The practical challenge is the cargo routing. There are no direct cargo flights from Dublin to Yerevan. Turkish Airlines via Istanbul is the primary option, with Qatar Airways via Doha as a useful alternative during summer when Turkish embargoes may apply.

    Ireland also has a valuable characteristic that affects the preparation timeline: Irish pets have no rabies titre test requirement for Armenia, so preparation can typically be completed in three to five weeks assuming vaccinations are current.

  sections:
    - heading: "What Armenia requires at the border"
      body: |
        Armenia's import rules are administered by the State Service for Food Safety (SSFS), ssfs.am. Officers conduct veterinary inspection at Zvartnots International Airport (EVN) in Yerevan before releasing your pet.

        The core requirements are a valid rabies vaccination (at least 21 days before entry, within 11 months), an official health certificate issued within 10 days of travel, and an ISO-compliant microchip. Armenia does not require a titre test for EU-member-state pets, which removes one of the longer-lead requirements common on non-EU routes.

        For 1-2 personal companion animals, no import permit is required. If travelling with more animals, check the commercial threshold with SSFS before booking. Armenia does not have a published breed ban for common companion animals, but verify with SSFS directly if your pet is a breed that faces restrictions elsewhere.

    - heading: "DAFM health certificate process from Ireland"
      body: |
        Ireland's export health certificate for non-EU destinations is the EU-format official veterinary certificate. It must be signed by a DAFM-registered official vet and then endorsed by the Department of Agriculture, Food and the Marine (DAFM). Both steps must complete within 10 days of departure.

        Schedule the vet examination within 10 days of your travel date. After signing, submit the certificate to DAFM for endorsement and allow 1-2 working days. Your regular vet can handle vaccinations and microchipping, but the official health certificate must come from a DAFM-registered official vet. Check gov.ie/agriculture for the list of authorised official vets.

        Note that DAFM is the same body that handles TRACES notifications for EU pet movements, so the endorsement process is well-established in Irish veterinary practice. Book the official vet appointment well in advance, as slots at authorised vets can be limited.

    - heading: "Cargo routing from Dublin to Yerevan"
      body: |
        There is no direct cargo service from Dublin (DUB) to Yerevan (EVN). Turkish Airlines Cargo via Istanbul (IST) is the primary option. Turkish Airlines operates DUB-IST regularly, and IST to EVN is an established route.

        Book through an approved Turkish Airlines cargo agent. Confirm pet acceptance on both the DUB-IST and IST-EVN legs. If travelling June to September, check specifically for temperature embargoes on the IST connection. Brachycephalic breed restrictions apply year-round.

        Qatar Airways Cargo via Doha (DOH) is a practical alternative, particularly during summer. Qatar Airways accepts live animals in cargo and serves EVN from DOH. Book through a Qatar Cargo agent and confirm acceptance on both legs. Doha airport has good live animal facilities.
