content:
  h1: "Pet Transport from Switzerland to Panama"
  overview: |
    Panama is a popular destination for pets relocating with families moving to Central America, and the import rules are straightforward provided you account for one extra step that surprises many people: the health certificate must be authenticated by the Embassy of Panama in Bern before your pet travels. MIDA (Ministerio de Desarrollo Agropecuario) governs pet imports and will not accept a certificate that has only Swiss federal endorsement without Panamanian consular authentication.

    Switzerland's BLV-authorised official vet issues the health certificate within 10 days of travel. FSVO/BLV endorses it in one working day. The certificate then goes to the Embassy of Panama in Bern, which typically takes 2-3 working days. That is potentially 4 working days from vet appointment to authenticated document. If you leave the vet appointment until the last few days of the 10-day window, you risk the Embassy processing running past your travel date.

    Flights from Zurich (ZRH) or Geneva (GVA) to Tocumen International Airport (PTY) in Panama City route through at least one hub. Copa Airlines, Iberia via Madrid, and KLM via Amsterdam are the main cargo options. Panama has no quarantine. MIDA inspects on arrival at PTY and collects a small registration fee, typically around USD 20. With the right documents, the inspection is quick.

  sections:
    - heading: "Embassy of Panama in Bern: The Authentication Step"
      body: |
        After your FSVO/BLV-endorsed certificate is ready, you need to submit it to the Embassy of Panama in Bern for consular authentication. This is a formal legalisation step confirming to Panama's MIDA authority that the Swiss federal endorsement is genuine. The Embassy is located in Bern; if you are based in Zurich, Geneva, or elsewhere in Switzerland, factor in courier time for getting the document to Bern and back.

        Authentication fees are approximately CHF 40-80. Processing takes 2-3 working days, though this can vary. Contact the Embassy in advance to confirm current processing times and whether appointments are required for document submission. Some pet transport agents handle this step as a matter of routine and have existing relationships with the Embassy, which can speed up the process slightly. The authenticated original must travel with your pet's cargo booking as the primary import document.

    - heading: "MIDA Inspection at Tocumen Airport: What Happens on Arrival"
      body: |
        MIDA veterinary officers are based in the cargo arrivals area at Tocumen International Airport (PTY) in Panama City. They will scan your pet's microchip, check it against the health certificate, and verify that all vaccination dates are within the accepted windows. The certificate must bear both the FSVO/BLV endorsement and the Panamanian Embassy authentication stamp from Bern. Without both stamps, MIDA will not clear your pet for entry.

        Once the inspection is complete and the MIDA registration fee is paid (approximately USD 20), your pet is released directly to you. There is no quarantine period. If you are using a local pet transport agent in Panama, they can meet the cargo at PTY and handle the MIDA paperwork on your behalf, which is worth considering if your personal arrival flight is on a different schedule to the cargo flight.
