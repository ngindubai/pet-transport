  overview: "Nepal is classified as a high-risk country for dog rabies by the US CDC. Dogs from Kathmandu require either the titre test pathway (blood drawn 30+ days after vaccination, result 28+ days old at US entry) or 28-day quarantine at a CDC-registered facility.   sections:
    - heading: "DLS and the Nepal export certificate for US travel"
      body: |
        Nepal's Department of Livestock Services (DLS), part of the Ministry of Agriculture and Livestock Development, is the authority for animal health documentation. The health certificate for US import must be issued by a DLS-authorised official vet and endorsed by DLS.

        The certificate must include your pet's microchip number, vaccination history with titre test result (USDA-recognised laboratory, blood draw date, result in IU/ml), owner details, and fitness to travel. Contact DLS (dlsnep.gov.np) for the current list of authorised vets and certificate format.

        For FAVN titre testing in Nepal, blood samples are typically sent to laboratories in India, the UK, or other USDA/APHA-approved countries. A specialist pet transport agent familiar with this corridor can advise on blood sample shipping logistics from Kathmandu.

    - heading: "Kathmandu altitude and routing to the USA"
      body: |
        Tribhuvan International Airport (KTM) in Kathmandu sits at approximately 1,400 metres above sea level, which can reduce maximum cargo payload on some aircraft. For most dogs and cats in standard crates, this is generally manageable, but very large dogs in heavy crates may encounter weight restrictions on certain aircraft.

        No airlines fly direct from Kathmandu to the USA. The two main cargo options are Qatar Airways via Doha (DOH) and Emirates via Dubai (DXB). Both Gulf carriers have good Kathmandu frequency and connect to multiple US gateway airports with CDC-registered animal care facilities.

        Confirm the specific US destination airport has a CDC-registered facility for your routing. JFK, LAX, ORD, MIA, and IAD all qualify.
