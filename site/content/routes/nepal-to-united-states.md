  overview: "Nepal is a CDC high-risk country. Dogs from Kathmandu moving to the USA need the DLS-endorsed Certification of Foreign Rabies Vaccination and Microchip form, a CDC Dog Import Form, and either a valid serology titre (blood drawn at least 30 days post-vaccination and 28 days before US entry at a CDC-approved laboratory) or a 28-day stay at a CDC-registered facility on arrival. All cargo routes from Kathmandu involve multiple transits, typically via Doha (Qatar Airways), Istanbul (Turkish Airlines), or Dubai (Emirates). Cats have no federal US barriers. The combination of CDC requirements, DLS documentation, and KTM cargo logistics makes this a route that benefits from specialist handling."

  sections:
    - heading: "Why Kathmandu cargo adds a layer of planning"
      body: |
        Most international pet cargo routes run through large, well-resourced hub airports. Kathmandu Tribhuvan International (KTM) is not one of those. It sits at 1,338 metres altitude, which affects cargo handling temperatures and procedures, and its international cargo capacity is more limited than Delhi or Mumbai. Schedules are fewer, carriers less numerous, and live animal policies on specific departure dates can differ from what the airline states generally.

        The practical effect is this: before you set a travel date, confirm with the carrier that live animals are accepted on your specific routing and dates. Qatar Airways from KTM to Doha and on to JFK or LAX is the most used routing for Nepal to USA pet cargo. Turkish Airlines via Istanbul is a solid alternative with good US connectivity. Emirates via Dubai is an option for October through May travel, avoided in summer due to heat embargoes at DXB.

        For the US CDC system, your dog must arrive at an airport with a CDC-registered animal care facility. JFK and LAX are the most practical end points for Gulf and Turkish hub routings. Both have CDC-registered facilities in or near the airport.

    - heading: "DLS, the CDC pathway, and running both in parallel"
      body: |
        Two separate bureaucratic processes need to complete before your dog can travel: the DLS export documentation from Nepal, and the CDC vaccination and titre verification from the US side.

        Nepal's Department of Livestock Services (DLS) issues the export permit and endorses the Certification of Foreign Rabies Vaccination and Microchip form, which is the document the CDC requires from high-risk countries. In some cases, DLS requires the animal to be presented at a DLS office for inspection before issuing the permit. Allow six weeks minimum for this process, and longer if you are not based in Kathmandu.

        The CDC serology titre requires blood drawn at least 30 days after the primary rabies vaccination and at least 28 days before your dog arrives in the USA. Running the titre test process and the DLS permit application at the same time, rather than one after the other, is the difference between a 14-week preparation and a 22-week one. Start both together.

faqs:
  - question: "Is Nepal CDC high-risk for dog imports to the USA?"
    answer: "Yes. Nepal is on the CDC's list of high-risk countries for dog rabies. All dogs from Nepal entering the USA require the full CDC high-risk pathway: DLS-endorsed vaccination certification, CDC Dog Import Form, and either a valid serology titre (blood drawn 30+ days post-vaccination, 28+ days before US entry, at a CDC-approved lab) or 28-day quarantine at a CDC-registered facility. Source: CDC cdc.gov/importation/dogs, 2026."
  - question: "Which US airports can a dog from Nepal arrive at?"
    answer: "Dogs on the CDC high-risk pathway must arrive at airports with CDC-registered animal care facilities. New York JFK and Los Angeles LAX are the most practical for Qatar Airways and Turkish Airlines routings from Kathmandu. Houston IAH and Atlanta ATL are also options. Check the current list at cdc.gov before booking."
  - question: "Are there any direct flights from Kathmandu to the USA for pet cargo?"
    answer: "No. All Nepal to USA cargo routes involve at least one transit, typically two segments: Kathmandu to a Gulf or Istanbul hub, then onward to a US city. The total journey time is typically 20 to 28 hours or more depending on transit times. Qatar Airways via Doha to JFK or LAX is the most used routing."
  - question: "Do cats from Nepal need anything to enter the USA?"
    answer: "No federal CDC or USDA requirements apply to cats from Nepal. Cats can enter any US airport without titre test, CDC processing, or quarantine. Individual US states may have their own requirements, but at the federal level, cats face no barriers. Source: USDA APHIS."
  - question: "Can the 28-day quarantine replace the titre test for dogs from Nepal?"
    answer: "Yes. Dogs from CDC high-risk countries (including Nepal) that do not have a valid serology titre can arrive in the USA and complete a 28-day quarantine at a CDC-registered facility. The quarantine must be at an approved facility; you cannot quarantine a pet at home. For most owners the titre test pathway is preferable given the cost and disruption of quarantine, but it requires advance planning."
