faqs:
  - question: "Is quarantine mandatory for pets from Greece entering Malaysia?"
    answer: "Yes. Malaysia requires a quarantine period for pets from non-scheduled countries, including EU member states like Greece. As of the December 2024 DVS update, the quarantine is 14 days at a DVS-designated facility at the owner's expense. This cannot be waived. Additionally, a 3-month waiting period from the FAVN titre test result applies before travel."
  - question: "What is a non-scheduled country in Malaysia's system?"
    answer: "DVS classifies countries into scheduled (low-risk, with simplified import requirements) and non-scheduled (requiring FAVN titre test, waiting period, and quarantine). Greece is a non-scheduled country under the DVS system. Most EU countries are non-scheduled. Scheduled countries typically include Australia, New Zealand, Singapore, Japan, and some other low-rabies-risk nations."
  - question: "Can the 14-day quarantine be done at a private veterinary clinic?"
    answer: "No. Quarantine must be carried out at a DVS-designated quarantine facility, not a private clinic. DVS specifies the approved facilities, and they are located at or near the major international airports. Confirm the current approved facilities with DVS or your agent before booking."
  - question: "Do cats and dogs have the same requirements for Malaysia from Greece?"
    answer: "Yes. Both dogs and cats are subject to the FAVN titre test, 3-month wait, DVS import permit, and 14-day quarantine. The minimum age for vaccination applies to both (16 weeks). Check with DVS for any species-specific variations in the current permit requirements."
