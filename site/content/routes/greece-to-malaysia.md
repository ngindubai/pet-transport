  overview: |
    Moving a pet from Greece to Malaysia requires a DVS (Department of Veterinary Services) Malaysia import permit, a Hellenic Ministry-issued official health certificate, and arrival at Kuala Lumpur International Airport. There is no routine quarantine for compliant pets entering Peninsular Malaysia, which makes this a more accessible Southeast Asian destination than Indonesia or India. Emirates via Dubai and Qatar Airways via Doha are the main cargo routing options from Athens. Note that Sabah and Sarawak in East Malaysia have separate, potentially stricter requirements.

    - heading: "DVS Malaysia import permit and arrival process"
      body: |
        The Department of Veterinary Services (DVS) Malaysia, under the Ministry of Agriculture and Food Industries, regulates pet imports. An import permit must be obtained from DVS Malaysia before the pet departs Greece. The permit specifies entry conditions and the required documentation format.

        On arrival at Kuala Lumpur International Airport (KUL), DVS Malaysia inspects the pet and documentation. Peninsular Malaysia has been declared rabies-free since 1999, which has streamlined some import procedures. If the pet is destined for Sabah or Sarawak in East Malaysia, separate and potentially stricter requirements apply due to recent rabies outbreaks in those regions.

        Contact DVS Malaysia (dvs.gov.my) for the current permit application process and the health certificate format required from Greece.
