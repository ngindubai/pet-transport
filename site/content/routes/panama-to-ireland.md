  sections:
    - heading: "FAVN titre test and the 90-day wait"
      body: |
        Ireland applies EU Implementing Regulation 2026/636 in full. Panama is classified as an unlisted third country, making the FAVN titre test mandatory. Blood must be drawn at least 30 days after the most recent rabies vaccination. The result must show at least 0.5 IU/ml at an EU-approved laboratory. The 90-day waiting period runs from the blood draw date. DAFM grants no exemptions.

        Panama's export health certificate is processed via SITERPA and countersigned by DECA within MIDA. The certificate is valid for 72 hours from the DECA countersignature. Schedule the appointment within 72 hours of departure from Tocumen International Airport (PTY). The EU-format health certificate for Ireland is then valid for 10 days from the MIDA endorsement.

    - heading: "Tapeworm treatment, DAFM notification, and Dublin entry"
      body: |
        Ireland requires all dogs to receive praziquantel tapeworm treatment between 24 hours and 5 days before arrival. The vet records the product name, dose, date, and time in the health certificate. The window is measured against actual arrival time at the Irish BCP.

        DAFM also requires 24-hour advance notification via the online system at agriculture.gov.ie. Submit this notification and confirm receipt before travel. Dublin Airport (DUB) is the primary BCP; Shannon (SNN) and Cork (ORK) are alternatives with more restricted hours. Iberia's direct PTY-MAD service is the most practical European hub routing from Panama, with onward connections to Dublin from Madrid. Cats are not subject to the tapeworm requirement.
