  h1: "Pet Transport from Panama to Ireland: 2026 Guide"
  overview: |
    Two rules stack on this route in a way they do not on most others in this build: the full EU unlisted-country titre test, since Panama carries no rabies-control equivalence, and Ireland's tapeworm treatment, which applies to dogs from nearly every origin rather than a handful of high-risk countries.

    Neither rule is unusual on its own. Together, they mean a Panama City pet needs its FAVN titre test, its 90-day wait, and a precisely timed praziquantel dose before DAFM will clear a Dublin arrival. Budget five to seven months from the first vet visit.

  sections:
    - heading: "Why Panama triggers the full unlisted-country process"
      body: |
        Panama has not been assessed as having rabies control equivalent to the EU's own standards, so Ireland applies the same sequence it would apply to any other unassessed country: FAVN or ELISA titre test at an EU-approved laboratory, a result of at least 0.5 IU/ml, blood drawn 30 or more days after vaccination, then a 90-day wait from that draw date.

        The clock is fixed to the blood draw, not the result date, so a fast laboratory turnaround buys no time back.
    - heading: "Ireland's tapeworm rule and the DAFM Advance Notice Portal"
      body: |
        Dogs entering Ireland need a praziquantel treatment against Echinococcus multilocularis unless they are arriving from Finland, Malta, Norway or Northern Ireland. Panama is not on that list, so the treatment applies in full, timed to a 24-to-120-hour window before landing at Dublin Airport.

        Separately, and easy to overlook amid the titre test planning, DAFM requires an Advance Notice Portal filing at least 24 hours before arrival. Set this up once your flight is confirmed rather than leaving it for the final days.
