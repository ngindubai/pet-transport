  sections:
    - heading: "Azerbaijan's entry requirements for pets from Estonia"
      body: |
        Pet import into Azerbaijan is overseen by the State Veterinary Service under the Ministry of Agriculture. For personal companion animals from Estonia, the requirements are focused on two things: a current rabies vaccination and a health certificate from the Estonian competent authority. There is no titre test requirement and no routine quarantine for compliant arrivals at Heydar Aliyev International Airport (GYD) in Baku.

        On arrival, an Azerbaijani veterinary officer inspects the documentation. If the health certificate is in order and the vaccination is current, entry is straightforward. Azerbaijan's approach to pet imports from European countries is generally cooperative, though getting the certificate format right matters.

        Baku is a busy international hub and Heydar Aliyev Airport handles live cargo regularly. You can expect a proper inspection process, so documentation needs to be complete and consistent throughout.

    - heading: "The Estonian Agricultural Board certificate process"
      body: |
        When your pet travels within the EU, the EU pet passport covers everything. For Azerbaijan, that document is not recognised. You need an official export health certificate from an authorised official veterinarian in Estonia, endorsed by the Pohllumajandus- ja Toiduamet (Agricultural Board) at agri.ee.

        Start by identifying an authorised official vet in your area. Not all vets in Estonia hold official status for export certificates, so check with the Agricultural Board first. The certificate must be issued within 10 days of travel, which means the vet appointment should be timed carefully relative to your departure.

        Ask the Agricultural Board whether Azerbaijan requires any specific format or additional fields in the certificate. Getting this right upfront avoids complications at Baku Airport.

    - heading: "Getting from Tallinn to Baku"
      body: |
        The most practical routing from Tallinn Airport (TLL) to Baku (GYD) goes via Istanbul Ataturk (IST). Turkish Airlines Cargo has established live animal handling at IST, and the IST to GYD leg is a regular Caucasus connection of around two hours.

        airBaltic connects Tallinn to Riga (RIX) and to Frankfurt or Amsterdam, from where Lufthansa Cargo or Turkish cargo connections reach Baku. AZAL, Azerbaijan's national carrier, may offer cargo options from certain European hubs but confirm live animal acceptance before making any commitments.

        Book live cargo well in advance and note summer heat restrictions. Between June and September, temperatures at IST can trigger carrier embargoes on brachycephalic breeds or small animals. Confirm the exact seasonal policy with your carrier when booking.
