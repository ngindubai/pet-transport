  sections:
    - heading: "airBaltic from Riga to Brussels: the main option"
      body: |
        Riga International Airport (RIX) is airBaltic's home base, and Brussels Airport (BRU) is one of the destinations on its network. This gives the Latvia-to-Belgium route a relatively direct option compared to other Baltic states, which often require two carrier changes.

        airBaltic allows small pets in the cabin (combined weight of pet and carrier up to 8 kg), paying the per-leg cabin pet fee at booking. For larger dogs that must travel as cargo, airBaltic's cargo arm handles live animals. Book cargo space well in advance, as live animal slots on specific routes can fill up quickly.

        Ryanair also operates RIX to Brussels South Charleroi (CRL, about 60km from central Brussels). Ryanair does not accept pets, and CRL is further from central Brussels than the main airport (BRU). Stick with airBaltic for this corridor when travelling with a pet.

    - heading: "Belgium on arrival: practical matters"
      body: |
        Belgium does not have federal-level mandatory dog registration, unlike some other EU countries. However, individual Belgian communes (municipalities) may have local registration requirements for dogs. If you are moving to Belgium permanently, check with your local commune.

        Belgium's FASFC does not conduct systematic border checks for intra-EU pet arrivals. Your EU passport or AHC should be presented at airline check-in and carried with you during travel. If you are passing through a Belgian border or airport security for any reason, carrying the documentation avoids any complications.

        If you are moving to Belgium for work, note that any non-EU national coming to Belgium may need a separate Belgian visa, but this is independent of the pet transport process.
