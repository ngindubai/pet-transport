  h1: "Pet Transport from Armenia to the United Kingdom"
  overview: |
    Moving a pet from Armenia to the UK involves the full APHA non-listed country protocol: microchip, rabies vaccination, titre test, 3-month wait, tapeworm treatment for dogs, and an SSFS-endorsed health certificate. The preparation minimum is 5-6 months.

    Routing from Yerevan is more constrained than from other origins. Emirates via Dubai and Qatar Airways via Doha are the primary reliable cargo options. Russian carrier services are not recommended given current operational disruptions and sanctions complications. Budget airlines like Wizz Air are an option only for very small pets travelling in cabin.

    One practical complication: there may not be an EU/UK approved titre test laboratory in Armenia. Blood samples may need to be shipped to a laboratory in Georgia, Germany, or Turkey. Your vet will need to confirm laboratory arrangements before drawing the sample.
  sections:
    - heading: "UK APHA requirements for pets arriving from Armenia"
      body: |
        Armenia is not on the UK's approved country list. The full APHA import protocol applies: ISO microchip implanted before rabies vaccination, current rabies vaccination, titre test result of at least 0.5 IU/ml at an EU/UK approved laboratory (blood drawn at least 30 days after vaccination), 3-month wait from blood draw date, tapeworm treatment for dogs 24-120 hours before UK arrival, and Armenia SSFS-endorsed official health certificate valid for 10 days.

        The 3-month wait is fixed from the blood sampling date. This date is recorded on the laboratory results certificate. Do not calculate from any other date.

        Source: UK APHA, gov.uk/bring-pet-to-great-britain. Confirm current requirements before booking, as rules are subject to change.
    - heading: "Cargo routing from Yerevan: Emirates and Qatar are the main options"
      body: |
        Emirates SkyCargo via Dubai is the most frequently used cargo route for Caucasus-to-UK pet moves. Yerevan (EVN) to Dubai (DXB) is a direct Emirates route, and Dubai to London Heathrow or Gatwick runs multiple times daily. Emirates SkyCargo is experienced with this routing, and the end-to-end booking is straightforward.

        Qatar Airways Cargo via Doha is the other main reliable option. Yerevan to Doha then Doha to London. Qatar Cargo has a strong reputation on Caucasus routes and the Doha connection point is well-equipped for pet transits.

        Both Emirates and Qatar apply temperature embargoes during peak Gulf summer months (typically June through mid-September). If you are planning summer travel, confirm embargo dates with the carrier before finalising your dates.

        Brachycephalic breed restrictions apply on Emirates segments. If you have a Bulldog, Pug, French Bulldog, Persian cat, or similar breed, verify this against Emirates' breed list before booking.

        Russian carriers are not recommended given current sanctions, airspace restrictions, and operational disruptions. Even if a service appears bookable, the risk of disruption to your pet mid-journey is too high to consider.
    - heading: "Budget airline options for very small pets: Wizz Air from Yerevan"
      body: |
        Wizz Air operates from Yerevan (EVN) to London Luton and accepts small pets in the cabin. This is only viable for very small cats or toy-breed dogs, typically under 8kg including the carrier. The pet travels under the seat in front of you.

        This is not a pet cargo option. Large dogs, any breed over the weight limit, and crate-size animals cannot use this route. If your pet qualifies by size, it removes the hub connection entirely and the pet stays with you throughout the journey.

        Always confirm Wizz Air's current cabin pet policy, weight limits, and breed restrictions before booking. Budget airline policies change and cabin pet availability is limited.
    - heading: "Titre test logistics: finding an approved laboratory for Armenia"
      body: |
        The titre test must be carried out at a laboratory approved by the EU or UK. There may not be an approved laboratory in Armenia. This means your vet in Yerevan will draw the blood sample and it will need to be shipped to an approved lab - potentially in Germany, Georgia, or Turkey.

        Shipping a blood sample internationally requires careful cold-chain packaging and a laboratory that accepts international samples. The sample must arrive at the lab in acceptable condition. Your vet or an IPATA agent with Caucasus experience will have current guidance on which approved labs accept samples from Armenia.

        Factor 2-3 weeks into your timeline for sample shipping and laboratory result return. The 3-month wait starts from the blood draw date, not when results arrive, so get the sample shipped quickly after the blood draw.
