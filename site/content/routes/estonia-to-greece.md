content:
  h1: "Pet Transport from Estonia to Greece: 2026 Guide"
  overview: |
    Two member states, one shared rulebook, and a document most Estonian pet owners already carry. Moving a dog or cat from Tallinn to Athens is intra-EU travel from start to finish, so there is no titre test, no quarantine, and no import permit to arrange. The only real decision is which connection, Warsaw or Frankfurt, fits your travel dates better, since there is no direct flight between the two capitals.

  sections:
    - heading: "Choosing your connection: Warsaw or Frankfurt"
      body: |
        LOT Polish Airlines flies Tallinn to Warsaw non-stop, with onward service into Athens, keeping the journey within one airline's network and cabin pet booking system. Lufthansa via Frankfurt is the alternative, drawing on Lufthansa's dense European schedule. Aegean Airlines also serves the Athens end well from either hub.

        Neither routing has a clear edge for a healthy small pet. Pick on flight timing and price, but reserve the cabin pet space on both legs at the time of booking regardless of which carrier you choose, since availability is never guaranteed at check-in.

    - heading: "The paperwork: passport, AHC, and the April 2026 change"
      body: |
        The EU pet passport, issued by a VTA-authorised vet in Estonia, is valid for the pet's life provided the rabies vaccination inside it stays current. Since 22 April 2026, that passport is restricted to owners primarily resident in the EU, a rule that leaves the overwhelming majority of Estonian pet owners unaffected.

        Non-EU nationals resident in Estonia need a single-use AHC instead, issued within 10 days of travel and endorsed by the VTA. Whichever document applies, Greece's Directorate General of Veterinary Services checks it against the microchip on arrival, so confirm the chip number matches exactly before you fly.
