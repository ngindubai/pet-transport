content:
  h1: "Pet Transport from Canada to Moldova"
  overview: |
    Canada's own place on the EU's approved rabies list, not any borrowed status from Britain or Brussels, is what keeps this route free of a titre test. Moldova, tracking the EU's framework as an accession candidate, exempts arrivals from any country on Annex II, Part 2 of the bloc's own rabies regulation, and Canada carries an independent listing there.

    What's left is familiar ground for anyone who has read a CFIA export guide before: a 30-day wait after rabies vaccination, no repeat jab needed if the standard 3-year Canadian vaccine is still current, and a certificate that has to clear a CFIA regional office appointment before the pet ever boards a flight.

  sections:
    - heading: "Why the exemption isn't borrowed from anyone"
      body: |
        It would be easy to assume Moldova's light treatment of Canadian pets is some kind of knock-on effect from the UK's own EU-adjacent status, since the two countries get the same practical outcome on this route. That's not how the listing works. Canada appears on Annex II, Part 2 of Implementing Regulation (EU) No 577/2013 in its own right, a classification Brussels reviews on Canada's own record, separate from anything the UK does.

        Moldova's animal health authority extends its EU-aligned exemption to any country on that list, Canada included. Reconfirm the current position with ANSA close to your travel date regardless, since the underlying EU list changes from time to time and this page can't guarantee it hasn't shifted since publication.

    - heading: "The CFIA appointment: the one date that can't move"
      body: |
        A Canadian vet can complete the export certificate at short notice. Getting an official CFIA veterinarian to endorse it is the part that actually needs a diary entry, since it runs through a regional office appointment system, not a walk-in service, and CFIA is explicit that it cannot endorse a certificate for a pet that has already left the country.

        Processing generally takes 2 to 5 business days once the appointment happens, longer during busier periods. Book this the moment your flight is confirmed, not the week before.

    - heading: "Three direct routes out of Toronto"
      body: |
        Chisinau has no direct link to Canada, but Toronto does fly nonstop to all three of the European hubs that connect onward. Turkish Airlines via Istanbul, Austrian Airlines via Vienna and LOT Polish Airlines via Warsaw each run a single-ticket connection through to Chisinau, so the choice mostly comes down to crate size and whichever schedule lines up with the CFIA appointment.

        Austrian's hold limit of 14kg per animal suits a mid-sized dog well. A bigger dog fits more comfortably through Turkish Cargo's dedicated live animal handling in Istanbul.
