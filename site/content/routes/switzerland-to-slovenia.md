      body: |
        The most practical route from Switzerland to Slovenia runs eastward through Austria. From Zurich, the drive to Ljubljana via Innsbruck and the Karawanken Tunnel takes roughly four to five hours. All three countries (Switzerland, Austria, Slovenia) are Schengen members, so there are no routine border checks between them.

        In practice, border spot checks do occur occasionally at Schengen internal borders, particularly at the Austria-Slovenia crossing. Carry the Swiss pet passport at all points during the journey. Switzerland is outside the EU, so technically your pet is transitioning from a non-EU country through Austria (EU) into Slovenia (EU). The Swiss pet passport covers this movement due to the bilateral agreements.

        If you prefer to fly, Ljubljana Joze Pucnik Airport (LJU) receives connections from Zurich via Austrian Airlines (through Vienna) and some budget carriers. The flight time is around one hour direct or two hours with a connection.
