  sections:
    - heading: "Madrid to Kigali: the cargo routing options"
      body: |
        Madrid Barajas (MAD) does not have a direct service to Kigali International Airport (KGL), so Spain-to-Rwanda pet moves involve at least one hub. Two main options present themselves.

        Ethiopian Airlines via Addis Ababa (ADD) is typically the most direct path. Ethiopian Cargo handles a large volume of live animal freight across East Africa, and the ADD-KGL leg is a short final connection. Ethiopian operates from Madrid to ADD, making this a two-leg routing.

        Brussels Airlines via Brussels (BRU) is the other established option. Iberia or another carrier covers MAD-BRU, then Brussels Airlines Cargo operates BRU-KGL. Brussels Airlines has built a consistent record on its Central and East Africa routes, and Iberia Cargo handles live animals from Barajas.

        RwandAir, Rwanda's national carrier, has expanded its European network in recent years. Check whether they offer services accessible from Madrid or a nearby hub, and whether they accept live animal cargo.

    - heading: "RAB import permit from rab.gov.rw"
      body: |
        Rwanda's Agriculture and Animal Resources Development Board (RAB) at rab.gov.rw manages the import of companion animals. Apply for the permit at least 8-10 weeks before travel with your pet's ISO microchip number, vaccination history, breed, and travel date.

        Compared to some African destinations, Rwanda operates a more organised administrative system. The RAB permit process is generally predictable, but early application and follow-up remain essential. Do not finalise cargo bookings until the permit is confirmed.

        At Kigali International Airport, the RAB inspection team checks the permit alongside the MAPA health certificate on arrival. Compliant arrivals proceed without quarantine.

    - heading: "MAPA export health certificate from Spain"
      body: |
        For non-EU destinations, Spain's Ministry of Agriculture (MAPA) governs the export health certificate process through the Dirección General de Sanidad de la Producción Agraria (DGSVA). An official veterinarian (Servicio Oficial de Veterinaria) issues the health certificate. The DGSVA endorses it.

        Contact a Servicio Oficial de Veterinaria in Spain to book the pre-export examination. The vet checks the ISO microchip, vaccination records, and general health, then issues the certificate. The DGSVA endorsement follows. The endorsed certificate must be issued within 10 days of travel.

        The Spanish EU pet passport is for intra-EU travel only and is not valid for Rwanda. The MAPA-endorsed health certificate is the correct document. Madrid Barajas is the natural cargo departure point from Spain, with live animal handling facilities at the airport.
