  sections:
    - heading: "Navigating the Vilnius carrier landscape"
      body: |
        Vilnius Airport (VNO) has grown significantly in recent years, with Ryanair operating a substantial number of routes to Western Europe including Belgium. For pet owners, this creates an awkward situation: the cheapest and most frequent Vilnius-to-Brussels route is operated by a carrier that does not take animals.

        The practical alternatives are LOT Polish Airlines, which operates from Vilnius to Warsaw with strong frequency and then connects to Brussels; airBaltic, which connects Vilnius to Riga and from Riga to Belgium; and Lufthansa, which offers connections via Frankfurt to Brussels with good cargo handling for larger dogs.

        For a small cat or dog in-cabin, LOT via Warsaw is often the simplest and most affordable full-service option on this corridor. For a large dog as cargo, Lufthansa via Frankfurt is the most reliable choice.

    - heading: "The VMVT-endorsed AHC and when you need it"
      body: |
        Most Lithuanian pet owners will have or can easily obtain an EU pet passport from their vet. The passport is issued once and updated with each vaccination, valid for the pet's lifetime.

        From 22 April 2026, the EU restricted passport use to pets whose owners primarily reside in the EU. Lithuanian nationals and other EU citizens residing in Lithuania are EU residents and continue to use the passport normally. If you are a non-EU national living in Lithuania (for example, on a work permit or long-term visa), you should use an AHC instead.

        The AHC is issued by a VMVT-authorised Lithuanian vet and endorsed by VMVT, which typically takes 1-3 business days. The AHC is valid for one trip and must be issued within 10 days of entry into Belgium. If you travel regularly between Lithuania and Belgium with your pet, getting an EU passport is simpler than obtaining a new AHC each time.

faqs:
  - question: "Does Belgium have any additional requirements for pets from Lithuania?"
    answer: "No. Belgium applies the standard EU intra-EU movement rules for pets arriving from other EU member states. A valid EU pet passport or AHC, current microchip, and current vaccination are all that is needed. The Belgian FASFC does not conduct routine border inspections for intra-EU pet arrivals at Brussels Airport."
  - question: "Can I take my two Lithuanian cats to Belgium on the same flight?"
    answer: "EU rules allow up to five dogs, cats, or ferrets per person in a non-commercial move. Each cat needs its own EU pet passport or AHC. Confirm with your airline how many pets per passenger are allowed in the cabin; most airlines limit cabin pets to one or two per passenger. If you have two cats, one may need to travel as cargo while the other is in-cabin, depending on the airline's policy."
  - question: "My dog has a non-ISO chip (a 10-digit chip rather than 15-digit). Can I still travel to Belgium?"
    answer: "Non-ISO chips are not automatically compliant with EU travel requirements. If the chip predates EU ISO standards and the vaccination was administered when the chip was already in place, some carriers may still accept it. Belgium's FASFC requires ISO 11784/11785 compliance. If there is doubt, consult your vet: implanting a second ISO-compliant chip alongside the existing chip is the standard solution, with both chip numbers recorded in the passport."
