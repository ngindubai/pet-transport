content:
  h1: "Pet Transport from South Korea to Iceland"
  overview: |
    South Korea recognises Iceland as rabies-free and waives the titre test for a pet travelling the other way. Iceland doesn't return the courtesy. MAST's Category 1 and Category 2 lists between them cover roughly 40 countries, and South Korea is on neither, which means this direction runs on a narrow exception rather than routine paperwork.

    The way in is the same exception that applies to any non-listed origin: the pet must have lived with its owner in South Korea for at least 6 months, and the owner must be genuinely relocating to Iceland. Clear that gate and the process runs to Category 2 rules, complete with a 90-day post-titre wait and Iceland's mandatory 14-day quarantine.

  sections:
    - heading: "An asymmetry worth knowing before you commit"
      body: |
        Look at the reverse route on this site, Iceland to South Korea, and it's one of the simplest journeys covered here: no titre test, same-day release possible at Incheon, because APQA already lists Iceland as rabies-free. Coming the other way, MAST offers no equivalent recognition of South Korea.

        That's not a contradiction. It reflects two different regulators making two different, independent judgements about the same country pair. South Korea trusts Iceland's rabies-free record. MAST simply hasn't classified South Korea one way or the other.
    - heading: "The exception that decides whether this move happens"
      body: |
        Without a country classification, MAST's standard door is closed. The workaround exists for exactly this situation: an owner who has genuinely lived with their pet in South Korea for at least 6 months and is relocating permanently to Iceland. It is not a route for a pet bought and shipped without that residency history.

        Clear that gate and the health requirements mirror the US-to-Iceland process already published on this site: vaccination, a titre test 30 days later, then a further 90-day wait from the blood draw, on top of the fixed 14-day quarantine at Reykjanesbae or Mosel.
    - heading: "Seoul to Keflavik in two legs"
      body: |
        Korean Air and Asiana both fly Seoul Incheon to major European hubs with dependable cargo capacity, and Icelandair covers the final connection into Keflavik. Two separate bookings rather than one through fare, so build in a realistic gap between the legs.
