content:
  h1: "Pet Transport USA to Nigeria: Getting to Lagos with Your Dog or Cat"
  overview: "Nigeria's NAQS (Nigeria Agricultural Quarantine Service) manages live animal imports, and the process for a dog or cat from the USA requires planning ahead. An import permit from NAQS is mandatory and must be obtained before travel. Dogs need a long list of vaccinations: not just rabies, but distemper, hepatitis, adenovirus, leptospirosis, and parvovirus. Cats have their own vaccination requirements too. With the NAQS permit, a USDA-endorsed health certificate, and current vaccinations, pets from the USA enter Nigeria without quarantine. Allow six to eight weeks from start to travel day."

  sections:
    - heading: "Getting the NAQS permit and preparing documents"
      body: |
        The NAQS import permit is the first thing to sort. Apply through the NAQS portal at naqs.gov.ng or through a Nigerian embassy. Processing times vary, and delays are possible, so starting this four to six weeks before your planned travel date is sensible.

        Once you have the permit, your USDA-accredited vet can complete the health certificate covering all required vaccinations and health status. The certificate is endorsed by USDA APHIS and must confirm freedom from contagious disease and fitness to travel. All vaccinations must be current on the day of examination: check expiry dates for each one, not just rabies.

        A pet transport agent who knows the NAQS process is worth considering for this route. Nigeria's import requirements are detailed, and the consequences of a missing vaccination record or incorrectly formatted document can mean the animal is held at Lagos airport pending resolution.

    - heading: "Flying to Lagos and the NAQS inspection"
      body: |
        Lagos Murtala Muhammed International Airport (LOS) is Nigeria's main international hub. British Airways via London Heathrow, Virgin Atlantic via Heathrow, and KLM via Amsterdam are the most commonly used routings for the USA-Nigeria pet cargo route. Ethiopian Airlines via Addis Ababa is a solid option for a wider range of US departure cities.

        On arrival at LOS, NAQS inspectors review the import permit, health certificate, microchip, and vaccination records. A visual health check is conducted. Pets arriving with correct documentation are not placed in quarantine. If any vaccination is missing from the record or the import permit is not in order, the animal may be held at the NAQS facility until the issue is resolved.

        Lagos is a hot city. Average temperatures are 25 to 32 degrees Celsius year-round. Airline heat embargoes for brachycephalic breeds apply on the final Lagos leg during the hottest months. Confirm eligibility with your carrier before booking.

faqs:
  - question: "Do I need a NAQS import permit to bring my pet to Nigeria from the USA?"
    answer: "Yes. An import permit from Nigeria Agricultural Quarantine Service (NAQS) is required before your pet can enter Nigeria. Apply through the NAQS portal at naqs.gov.ng. Allow several weeks for processing. Travelling without a NAQS permit will likely result in your pet being detained at the airport."
  - question: "What vaccinations does my dog need to enter Nigeria?"
    answer: "Dogs entering Nigeria from the USA need: rabies (at least 30 days before travel, no more than 12 months old), distemper, hepatitis, adenovirus, leptospirosis, and parvovirus. All must be current at the time of travel and recorded on the USDA-endorsed health certificate. Source: NAQS, naqs.gov.ng."
  - question: "Does my cat need a titre test to enter Nigeria from the USA?"
    answer: "No titre test is routinely required for cats from the USA entering Nigeria. Cats need rabies vaccination (current), feline viral rhinotracheitis, panleukopenia, and feline leukaemia vaccinations, plus a USDA-endorsed health certificate and NAQS import permit."
  - question: "Is there quarantine for pets arriving from the USA in Nigeria?"
    answer: "No routine quarantine applies to dogs and cats from the USA with correct documentation. NAQS conducts a document and health inspection on arrival at Lagos airport. Pets that appear healthy and have complete, correct paperwork are released. Pets with missing documentation or signs of illness may be held at the NAQS facility."
  - question: "Which airlines fly pet cargo from the USA to Lagos?"
    answer: "British Airways (via London Heathrow), Virgin Atlantic (via Heathrow), KLM (via Amsterdam), and Ethiopian Airlines (via Addis Ababa) are the main carriers. Delta also has cargo connections via West African hubs. All operate pets as manifested cargo only. Confirm pet cargo acceptance on both legs of the routing before booking."
  - question: "How much does it cost to ship a dog from the USA to Nigeria?"
    answer: "Expect to pay USD 250 to USD 500 for vet fees and vaccinations, USD 38 for USDA APHIS endorsement, and USD 900 to USD 2,500 for cargo depending on dog size and routing. Add a crate if needed. Total for a medium dog is typically USD 1,200 to USD 3,100."
