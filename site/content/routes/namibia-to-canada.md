content:
  h1: "Pet Transport from Namibia to Canada"
  overview: |
    Namibia is one of more than a hundred countries CFIA classes as high-risk for dog rabies, on the list last updated 13 March 2025. That sounds like it should mean more paperwork. For a personal pet travelling with its owner, it doesn't. CFIA's high-risk designation is really a commercial-import rule: dogs shipped for resale, breeding or fostering from a listed country have been barred outright from Canada since September 2022, but a single family dog or cat isn't caught by that ban.

    What's left is the standard CFIA checklist, current rabies vaccination, a health certificate in English or French, no titre test, no quarantine, on top of a routing that always transfers through Frankfurt or Johannesburg since nothing flies Windhoek to Canada directly.

  sections:
    - heading: "What the high-risk listing actually changes"
      body: |
        CFIA's high-risk list groups Namibia with most of Sub-Saharan Africa, alongside dozens of countries across the Americas and Asia. It's easy to assume that listing triggers the same kind of titre test and multi-month wait that GOV.UK or the CDC apply to unlisted or high-risk origins. Canada's rule works differently.

        The listing's practical effect falls on commercial dog imports, which CFIA stopped accepting from high-risk countries entirely in September 2022. A personal pet, defined as one or two animals travelling with the owner who isn't handing them to someone else on arrival, sits outside that ban and follows the same light process CFIA applies to most origins: vaccination, certificate, no test, no quarantine.

    - heading: "Building the certificate for CFIA, not GOV.UK"
      body: |
        A Namibian vet who's already issued export certificates for the UK route knows that format well. Canada's isn't the same document. CFIA doesn't require the titre test or the 3-month wait GB's unlisted-country rule demands, so a certificate built around that GB template carries information Canada doesn't ask for and can miss detail CFIA does want, like the English or French language requirement.

        Flag the destination clearly when booking the vet appointment, and confirm the certificate is built to CFIA's own expectations rather than reused from a different route.

    - heading: "Routing through Frankfurt or Johannesburg"
      body: |
        Windhoek's Hosea Kutako International has no direct service to Canada. Discover Airlines' year-round Frankfurt-Windhoek route, paired with Air Canada's own Toronto-Frankfurt service, is the cleanest single-change routing. Airlink's Johannesburg-Windhoek leg is the alternative if a Gulf or UK first leg into Toronto suits the schedule better.
