content:
  h1: "Pet Transport from Serbia to the United Kingdom"
  overview: |
    Air Serbia flies Belgrade to Heathrow directly, close to daily, and it feels like the obvious way to bring a dog or cat home. It is not an option. Air Serbia does not carry pets on any UK route, in the cabin or the hold, a restriction it applies only to the UK, the UAE and Qatar. Anyone planning this move around that flight needs to replan around a connection instead.

    The bigger factor is regulatory, not logistical. Serbia does not appear on either part of the UK's listed-country annex, which puts it in the unlisted category alongside countries like India and Brazil. That means a rabies titre test, a laboratory result of at least 0.5 IU/ml, and a fixed three-month wait from the day blood is drawn, not the day the lab responds. There is no shortcut. Realistic total preparation time from microchipping to touchdown in Great Britain runs from four and a half to six months.

  sections:
    - heading: "Why the direct Belgrade-London flight is not an option"
      body: |
        This trips up more owners on this route than any single visa or paperwork issue. Air Serbia operates roughly ten flights a week between Belgrade Nikola Tesla (BEG) and London Heathrow, a two-hour-forty-five-minute hop that looks like the natural choice for a stressed pet. The airline's own pet policy rules it out for any journey touching the UK, along with the UAE and Qatar, regardless of crate size or cabin versus hold.

        The practical fix is a connection. Austrian Airlines runs Belgrade to Vienna, a short regional sector that suits a smaller pet travelling as accompanied checked baggage with an adult passenger, with true unaccompanied cargo shipments handled by Lufthansa Cargo for the leg onward. Lufthansa's own Frankfurt or Munich connection is the alternative, with the pet moving through Lufthansa Cargo's pressurised, temperature-controlled hold. Either route adds a transfer and a day or so of planning that a direct flight would have avoided, but it is the only way to get a pet from Belgrade to London by air.

    - heading: "Serbia's export process and the UK's unlisted-country pathway"
      body: |
        On the Serbian side, the Ministry of Agriculture, Forestry and Water Management's Veterinary Directorate oversees pet exports. A Serbian vet examines the animal and prepares the health certificate, and a government veterinary inspector endorses it, usually within two to three working days. There is no separate export permit to apply for beyond this certificate for a non-commercial move of your own animal.

        The UK side is the harder part. Serbia is an EU candidate country, but candidacy does not put it on the UK's list of low-risk rabies countries. Checking the retained EU Regulation 577/2013 annex that the UK still applies confirms Serbia sits outside both Part 1 (Andorra, Switzerland, Iceland and similar) and Part 2 (a longer list running from Australia to the United States). That makes it unlisted, which triggers the full pathway: microchip before vaccination, a titre test blood draw at least 30 days after that vaccination, a passing result of 0.5 IU/ml or higher from an EU or UK-approved lab, and then a three-month wait counted from the blood draw itself.

        Dogs also need a tapeworm treatment for Echinococcus multilocularis, given by a vet between 24 and 120 hours before arrival and logged in the GB Pet Health Certificate. Cats are exempt from this step. Get any part of the sequence out of order and APHA can detain the animal on arrival at the owner's expense, so build the timeline backwards from your travel date rather than forwards from vaccination day.
