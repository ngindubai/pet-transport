  h1: "Pet Transport from Austria to the Dominican Republic"
  overview: |
    Moving a dog or cat from Vienna to Santo Domingo is a manageable process when the paperwork is prepared in the right order. The Dominican Republic's veterinary authority, the DGAG (Direccion General de Ganaderia), requires a microchip, a current rabies vaccination, and an official health certificate issued within 15 days of travel. There is no quarantine, but inspection at Las Americas International Airport (SDQ) is standard and your documents need to be correct on arrival.

    Austria's export process runs through AGES (Agentur fur Gesundheit und Ernahrungssicherheit). Your health certificate must be signed by an AGES-registered official vet, known as an Amtstierarzt, and then endorsed by AGES itself. This is not the same as a certificate from your regular private vet. The certificate is valid for 10 days from issue, so timing your vet appointment carefully relative to your travel date is important.

    There is no direct passenger flight from Vienna to Santo Domingo, so all pets travel as manifest cargo on connecting routes, most commonly via Madrid or Paris. Routing and crate requirements vary by airline, so booking cargo space early and confirming the connection timing with your chosen carrier is worth doing several weeks before you travel.

  sections:
    - heading: "DGAG Import Requirements: What the Dominican Republic Expects"
      body: |
        The DGAG sets out clear rules for personal pet imports. Dogs must have a current DHPP vaccination covering distemper, adenovirus, parainfluenza, and parvovirus. Cats must be vaccinated against panleukopenia, rhinotracheitis, and calicivirus. Both species require a rabies vaccination given no earlier than 30 days and no later than 12 months before entry. There is no titre test requirement for pets arriving from Austria.

        Up to five personal pets can enter without an import permit. The health certificate must be issued within 15 days of travel, and the DGAG inspection at SDQ is conducted by government veterinarians on arrival. Keep original vaccination records and the endorsed health certificate accessible throughout the journey, not packed inside checked luggage. Inspectors at SDQ will ask to see them immediately.

    - heading: "Getting the Official Health Certificate from Austria"
      body: |
        Austria's official export health certificate cannot be issued by a standard private vet. You need an Amtstierarzt, an official government-registered veterinarian, to carry out the clinical examination and sign the certificate. AGES then endorses it. The examination must happen within 15 days of your departure date, and the certificate expires 10 days from issue, so coordinate the appointment carefully.

        Contact your local Bezirksverwaltungsbehoerde (district administrative authority) to find the assigned Amtstierarzt for your area. In Vienna, this is managed through the MA 60 veterinary office. Allow time for the AGES endorsement step, which typically takes one working day. Travel with the original endorsed certificate; a copy will not be accepted at SDQ.

    answer: "No. Personal pet imports of up to five animals do not require an import permit from the DGAG. The required documents are a microchip, current rabies and core vaccinations, and an official health certificate issued within 15 days of travel."
  - question: "Is there quarantine for pets arriving in the Dominican Republic?"
    answer: "No. Pets are inspected by DGAG veterinarians at Las Americas International Airport (SDQ) on arrival, but there is no quarantine period provided the documents are in order."
  - question: "Can my pet fly in the cabin from Vienna to Santo Domingo?"
    answer: "No. There are no direct flights, and on the long-haul connecting legs to the Dominican Republic, pets travel as manifest cargo rather than in-cabin. Airlines such as Iberia and Air France Cargo handle this."
  - question: "What happens if my rabies vaccination is more than 12 months old when I arrive?"
    answer: "The DGAG will not accept a rabies vaccination that is more than 12 months old at the time of entry. Your pet would need a new vaccination, and then you must wait at least 30 days before travelling. Check the expiry date before booking any flights."
