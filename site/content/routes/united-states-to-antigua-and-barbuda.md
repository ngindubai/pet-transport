  h1: "Pet Transport from the United States to Antigua and Barbuda"
  overview: |
    Antigua and Barbuda's own guidance draws a specific line, and the United States sits on the harder side of it. Four countries get named explicitly as rabies-endemic origins: the US, Canada, Grenada and Trinidad and Tobago. That single classification pulls in a full FAVN titre test at a WOAH-approved laboratory, a requirement the UK route this site also covers skips entirely, since the UK counts as rabies-free.

    There's a genuinely useful escape hatch, though. Keep the pet's rabies vaccine on an annual schedule rather than the common three-year protocol, and a second FAVN test isn't required. That's a specific, verifiable fact worth checking against a pet's actual vaccination history before assuming the blood test is unavoidable.

    Two nonstop options exist from the US mainland, American Airlines from Miami and Caribbean Airlines from New York JFK, and either can have a compliant pet moving within roughly 12 to 16 weeks, most of that taken up by the FAVN test timing rather than the flight itself.

  sections:
    - heading: "Why the US route is genuinely harder than the UK one"
      body: |
        This site's UK-to-Antigua page describes a comparatively simple process: no titre test, because the UK is rabies-free. A US-origin move doesn't get that treatment. Antigua and Barbuda's Veterinary and Livestock Division names the US directly as a rabies-endemic country, which triggers the full package: vaccination certificate, microchip certification, and a FAVN test result within accepted levels from a WOAH-approved lab.

        That's a materially different planning problem. Where the UK route budgets a couple of weeks for the export certificate, this one has to budget for a blood draw, transit to an approved laboratory, and the result landing before the health certificate can be finalised.

    - heading: "The annual-vaccine exemption, and why it's worth checking"
      body: |
        Buried in the detail is a genuinely useful shortcut: if the rabies vaccine has been kept current annually, rather than on the three-year protocol many US vets default to, a second FAVN test isn't required. For an owner who already vaccinates yearly, that removes a real cost and a real delay from this route.

        It's worth confirming this directly with the vet handling the certificate, since the exemption depends on the vaccination history actually matching the annual pattern, not just on asking for it after the fact.

    - heading: "Getting there once the paperwork clears"
      body: |
        American Airlines' nonstop Miami-Antigua service, AA2579/AA2580, works for a small pet in the cabin under 9kg including carrier, since this route doesn't touch the UK or Ireland restrictions American applies elsewhere. A larger dog goes through PetEmbark cargo instead, still on the same flight number.

        Caribbean Airlines' nonstop New York JFK service is the other option, useful for owners closer to the north-east, and its Antigua cargo desk has resumed bookings after a brief 2025 suspension during a ground-handling change.

    answer: "Generally yes. Antigua and Barbuda's own guidance names the United States as a rabies-endemic country of origin, which requires a Rabies FAVN test at a WOAH-approved laboratory. The one exception is a pet whose rabies vaccine has been kept continuously current on an annual, rather than three-year, schedule, in which case a second FAVN test isn't required."
  - question: "What's the difference between an annual and a three-year rabies vaccine for this route?"
    answer: "Antigua and Barbuda's guidance accepts only an annual vaccination as satisfying its ongoing requirement on its own; a three-year vaccine doesn't clear the FAVN requirement by itself. Confirm which protocol the pet is currently on with the vet handling the export certificate."
  - question: "What happens if my pet's FAVN test doesn't pass?"
    answer: "Published guidance describes refusal of entry or confiscation as the consequence of unmet requirements, rather than a quarantine period as a fallback.
