content:
  h1: "Pet Transport Canada to Jamaica"
  overview: |
    Air Canada's Toronto-Kingston route runs direct, which makes the actual travel day one of the easier legs of this journey. Getting to that day is the harder part. Jamaica's Veterinary Services Division works sequentially: a Preliminary Application Form has to clear before an owner can start the rabies vaccination and FAVN titre test steps that count towards entry.

    None of that is specific to Canadian pets; it's how VSD processes every arrival. The advantage for owners flying from Toronto is a genuinely direct flight once the paperwork side finally clears.

  sections:
    - heading: "Why the Preliminary Application comes before anything else"
      body: |
        VSD requires this form approved before an owner begins the vaccination and titre test sequence that counts towards Jamaican entry. Getting ahead of that approval, whether by vaccinating early or booking the titre test too soon, risks the whole file being rejected. Realistically, build in 4-5 months from the very first step.

    - heading: "The titre test and what happens at Plumb Point"
      body: |
        A FAVN titre test, drawn 30 days after the qualifying rabies vaccination at a VSD-approved laboratory, needs to clear 0.5 IU/ml. A first-time rabies vaccination in a pet over 12 months old doesn't qualify for entry until three months after that dose, layered on top of the titre wait.

        Quarantine at the National Quarantine Facility, Plumb Point, runs up to 14 days, with VSD inspectors holding discretion to release a healthy, well-documented pet sooner. Plan for the full period regardless.

    - heading: "A direct route once the paperwork clears"
      body: |
        Air Canada flies Toronto to Kingston non-stop, and WestJet adds a seasonal direct service during the busiest winter travel months. Once the CFIA-endorsed certificate, VSD import permit, and titre test are all in order, the journey itself is one of the more straightforward legs in this entire process.
