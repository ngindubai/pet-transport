content:
  h1: "Pet Transport from Bolivia to the United Kingdom"
  overview: |
    There is no direct flight between Bolivia and the UK. Every pet making this move flies Santa Cruz to Madrid first, on the joint BoA and Air Europa service, then connects onward into London. That single fact shapes most of the planning below.

    Bolivia also sits in the unlisted category under the UK's pet travel rules, the same group as nearly every other South American country. That means a rabies titre test at an approved laboratory, then a fixed three-month wait counted from the blood draw date, not the result date. Add the SENASAG export paperwork on the Bolivian side and a health certificate valid for only 10 days, and the whole process runs to around four to five months from a standing start.

  sections:
    - heading: "The three-month wait, and why the date on the lab report matters"
      body: |
        The 90-day clock starts on the day your vet draws blood for the titre test, not the day the laboratory emails back a pass. Get that date wrong in your planning and you will miss your intended travel window by weeks.

        Work backwards. If you want to travel in December, blood needs drawing by early September at the latest. The rabies vaccination needs to sit at least 30 days before that blood draw, so early August. Four to five months of lead time is not a padding figure, it is close to the practical minimum.

        One relief: if your pet already holds a passing titre result from a previous international move, and the rabies vaccination behind it has not lapsed, that result can still count. Ask your vet to check the dates before assuming you have to start again.

    - heading: "Getting through Madrid with a live animal on board"
      body: |
        Santa Cruz Viru Viru International Airport (VVI) is Bolivia's main long-haul gateway, and BoA's nonstop service to Madrid is currently the only way out of the country toward Europe without a stop somewhere else in South America first. The flight runs around 11 hours, operated jointly with Air Europa on Boeing 787 aircraft.

        From Madrid, Iberia and British Airways both fly the short hop into London several times a day, which gives some scheduling flexibility for the connection. What it does not give you is control over how a Madrid transfer with a live animal is handled airside. Confirm with the carrier, or with a pet relocation agent who has actually moved an animal on this exact routing, that both legs will accept your pet and that the transfer time between them is workable for the crate and the season.

        SENASAG's own export process adds a step most owners do not expect: you have to write to SENASAG first, asking for the UK's specific requirements, before the certificate can be drawn up. Build that correspondence into your timeline rather than leaving it for the final month.
