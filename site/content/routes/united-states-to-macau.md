content:
  h1: "Pet Transport from the United States to Macau"
  overview: |
    Macau's own import authority, IAM, eased its rules for compliant pets around June 2026, dropping the mandatory 30-day quarantine that used to apply. That change doesn't touch the titre test, though. The United States isn't on IAM's short list of rabies-free exempt origins, so the standard blood test and three-month wait still apply before an American pet can land in Macau.

  sections:
    - heading: "Why the US doesn't skip the titre test"
      body: |
        IAM only waives the titre requirement for a small number of rabies-free origins, and the US is not among them. The sequence runs the same as it would from most other countries: blood drawn at least 30 days after vaccination, a minimum result of 0.5 IU/mL, then roughly three months from the draw date itself before entry is permitted.

        Compliance is judged on four things together: microchip, current vaccination, a passing titre result, and the official health certificate. An IAM vet can still hold a pet at arrival if any one of them is missing, even though the general quarantine requirement no longer applies to a fully compliant animal.

    - heading: "No direct flight: the Hong Kong transfer"
      body: |
        Macau International Airport carries no transpacific cargo, so the practical routing is a US gateway city, commonly LAX or SFO, to Hong Kong on Cathay Pacific Cargo or United Airlines Cargo, followed by a road transfer across the Hong Kong-Zhuhai-Macau Bridge, or a ferry, about an hour once the crate clears Hong Kong customs.

        The PS-1092 import licence is usually issued within three working days but expires after 30, so time the application against a confirmed flight rather than a placeholder date.
