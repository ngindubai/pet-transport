  overview: |
    Switzerland eliminated fox-mediated rabies through an oral vaccination programme, declared terrestrial-rabies-free since 1999. That's the kind of record IAM's own general rule rewards: no titre test for a pet from a country with no rabies incidents in the past two years. Switzerland clears that bar by decades, not months.

    What's missing is a name on a published list. Nothing found for this guide confirms Switzerland by name on any IAM exemption roster, so treat the favourable reading as a strong likelihood and check with IAM directly before finalising a titre-free schedule.

  sections:
    - heading: "Twenty-seven years free, and still worth confirming"
      body: |
        A 1999 declaration is not a recent or fragile claim. Switzerland's rabies-free status has held for over a quarter of a century, verified by the same international body, the World Organisation for Animal Health, that underpins Hong Kong's own exemption elsewhere on this site.

        IAM's rule is written in general terms rather than as a country-by-country list made public in full. Where the general rule and a specific named confirmation diverge, the honest planning position is to confirm rather than assume, however strong the underlying case looks.

    - heading: "SWISS and Cathay Pacific, near-daily, through Hong Kong"
      body: |
        Macau's own airport carries no long-haul cargo, so the transfer to Hong Kong is unavoidable regardless of how clean the rabies paperwork ends up. SWISS runs Zurich-Hong Kong roughly 14 times weekly with cargo through SWISS WorldCargo, and Cathay Pacific covers the same route close to daily on Airbus A350s. Either gives reasonable schedule flexibility once the Macau-side import licence and inspection slot are lined up.
