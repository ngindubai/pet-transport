  sections:
    - heading: "CDC high-risk: the key question for your dog"
      body: |
        The US Centers for Disease Control classifies Indonesia as a high-risk country for dog rabies. What that means in practice depends on one question: where was your dog vaccinated?

        If your dog was vaccinated in the US before moving to Indonesia, and you have documentation of that US-issued vaccination, the return process is more straightforward. You need a current CDC Dog Import Form and proof of the US vaccination.

        If your dog was vaccinated in Indonesia (the normal situation for expats living there), you must follow the foreign-vaccination pathway. This requires arriving at a CDC-approved airport with a prior reservation at a CDC-registered animal care facility already in place. The facility handles entry processing. Without that reservation, the dog may be turned back or placed in a 28-day quarantine. The CDC website has the current list of approved airports and registered facilities. Check the live list before booking, as it is updated periodically.

    - heading: "Cats from Indonesia: no CDC concerns"
      body: |
        If you are moving a cat from Indonesia to the US, the CDC high-risk classification does not apply. Cats from all countries are treated the same under current US import rules: standard customs inspection, no quarantine, no facility reservation required.

        Some airlines require a basic health certificate for cats travelling as cargo on international routes, but this is an airline requirement, not a US entry requirement. With a clean certificate and healthy cat, entry is straightforward.

    - heading: "Routing via Singapore or Dubai"
      body: |
        There are no direct cargo services from Indonesia to the United States. The standard routing is via Singapore Changi (SIN) on Singapore Airlines, or via Dubai on Emirates SkyCargo. Both are experienced hubs for live animal cargo, with climate-controlled facilities and established handling procedures.

        The Singapore routing is generally shorter overall journey time. The Dubai routing gives access to Emirates' broad US network. Either way, confirm that live animal cargo is accepted on your specific connecting flights, as not every scheduled service carries live animals. An experienced pet transport agent can identify the best routing for your travel dates.

faqs:
  - question: "Can my dog enter the US from Indonesia without quarantine?"
    answer: "Yes, if you follow the CDC high-risk pathway correctly. Dogs vaccinated in Indonesia must arrive at a CDC-approved airport with an advance reservation at a CDC-registered animal care facility. The facility handles entry processing and the dog is not subject to a quarantine stay if all documentation is complete. The key is making the facility reservation before you travel, not on arrival. Start the CDC registration process well ahead of your departure date."
  - question: "Which US airports accept dogs from Indonesia?"
    answer: "The CDC maintains a list of airports approved for dogs from high-risk countries. Major airports typically on the list include JFK, LAX, Chicago O'Hare, Miami International, and Atlanta Hartsfield. Your routing via Singapore or Dubai should connect to one of these. Always check the current CDC approved airport list before booking, as the list changes."
  - question: "Does my cat need any special documentation to enter the US from Indonesia?"
    answer: "No. Cats from Indonesia enter the US through standard customs health inspection. There is no CDC requirement, no titre test, no facility reservation. Your airline may require a basic health certificate for the cargo booking, but this is an airline policy, not a US federal entry requirement."
  - question: "How do I find a CDC-registered animal care facility in the US?"
    answer: "The CDC website lists currently registered facilities. These are specialist animal facilities that handle the entry processing for dogs from high-risk countries. Spaces at popular facilities book out weeks in advance. A US-based or Indonesia-based specialist pet transport agent will have established relationships with these facilities and can manage the reservation process for you."
  - question: "Does the Bali rabies history affect my dog's US entry?"
    answer: "The CDC high-risk classification applies to Indonesia as a whole, not just Bali. If your dog was vaccinated in Indonesia, the foreign-vaccination pathway applies regardless of which island you live on. Good documentation of your dog's vaccination history is important: the vaccination date, product name, and administering vet should all be clearly recorded. Keep the original vaccination certificates."
