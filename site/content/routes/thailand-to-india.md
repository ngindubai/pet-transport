  h1: "Pet Transport from Thailand to India"
  overview: |
    Moving a pet from Thailand to India comes with a significant commitment: India requires a mandatory 30-day quarantine for all incoming pets, with no exemptions. Your dog or cat spends that month in an AQCS-approved facility at your expense. Factor this into your relocation timeline before booking anything.

    Beyond the quarantine, this route also requires an India import permit (applied for before departure), an export health certificate from Thailand's Department of Livestock Development, and a rabies vaccination given at least 30 days before entry. Total preparation time runs to 12-16 weeks including the quarantine stay. Thailand to India is not a last-minute move.
  sections:
    - heading: "India's mandatory pet quarantine explained"
      body: |
        India treats all incoming pets as a biosecurity matter and requires a 30-day quarantine at an AQCS-approved quarantine facility on arrival, regardless of the pet's vaccination history or country of origin. The quarantine is supervised by veterinary staff, and the owner pays the facility fees for the entire period.

        The quarantine cannot be conducted at home or reduced by presenting test results. It is a fixed government requirement under India's Ministry of Fisheries, Animal Husbandry and Dairying.

        To avoid delays on arrival, pre-confirm your quarantine reservation with the AQCS before you travel. AQCS operates facilities at major international airports including Delhi, Mumbai, Chennai, and Kolkata. Contact the AQCS directly to confirm availability for your arrival date.
    - heading: "India import permit and how to apply"
      body: |
        A formal import permit is required before your pet can enter India. Permits are issued by AQCS (Animal Quarantine and Certification Services) or the State Animal Husbandry Department, depending on your port of entry.

        The application process typically takes 3-6 weeks. Start it at least 6-8 weeks before your travel date. Do not book flights until you have permit approval in hand -- arriving without a permit means your pet will either be held or returned.

        Your pet transport agent can handle the AQCS permit application if you are unfamiliar with the process. For a route this complex, using an agent is worth considering.
    - heading: "Thailand export process: DLD health certificate"
      body: |
        Thailand's Department of Livestock Development (DLD) issues export health certificates for pets leaving the country. The certificate is issued by a DLD-accredited veterinarian and must be valid within 14 days of your departure.

        Your pet must have a current rabies vaccination at least 30 days old before travel and an ISO microchip. The DLD certificate confirms all of these. Apply through the DLD in advance; do not wait until the week before travel.

        As a practical point: Bangkok Suvarnabhumi (BVK) and Chiang Mai (CNX) are the main departure points. Both Thai Airways and Air India have cargo capability from Bangkok to major Indian cities.

    answer: "Yes. India mandates a 30-day quarantine at an AQCS-approved facility for all incoming pets, without exception. There are no exemptions for vaccinated animals, rabies-free countries, or prior pet history. Pre-book the quarantine reservation with AQCS before you travel."
  - question: "How do I apply for the India pet import permit from Thailand?"
    answer: "Apply through AQCS (Animal Quarantine and Certification Services) or the State Animal Husbandry Department at your Indian destination port. Allow 3-6 weeks for processing. Submit vaccination and microchip records with your application. A pet transport agent can manage this on your behalf."
  - question: "How long does the rabies vaccination wait last for India entry?"
    answer: "The rabies vaccination must have been given at least 30 days before your pet enters India. This is slightly longer than the EU's 21-day rule. Plan your vaccination timing accordingly and do not schedule travel fewer than 30 days after the jab."
  - question: "What does the Thailand DLD export health certificate cover?"
    answer: "The DLD export health certificate confirms your pet is healthy, microchipped, vaccinated against rabies, and cleared for export from Thailand. It is issued by a DLD-accredited vet within 14 days of travel. Present it alongside the India import health certificate at check-in."
