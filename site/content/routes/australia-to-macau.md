content:
  h1: "Pet Transport from Australia to Macau"
  overview: |
    Being rabies-free doesn't buy an Australian pet any shortcut here. Macau's Instituto para os Assuntos Municipais only exempts a short list of origins from its titre test, and Australia isn't on it, so the same blood test and roughly three-month wait apply as they would from most other countries.

    The upside: IAM dropped its mandatory 30-day quarantine for compliant pets around June 2026, so a fully documented arrival clears without the holding period that used to apply.

  sections:
    - heading: "Why Australia's rabies-free status doesn't shorten this route"
      body: |
        It's a common assumption that a rabies-free country gets an easier ride on the way out, and it's wrong here. IAM's exemption list is short and specific, and Australia isn't currently on it. The sequence runs the same as it would from a higher-risk country: blood drawn at least 30 days after vaccination, a minimum result of 0.5 IU/mL, then roughly three months from the draw date before entry into Macau.

        Compliance is judged as a package: microchip, current vaccination, a passing titre result, and the official health certificate together. Miss one, and an IAM vet can still hold the pet at arrival despite the general quarantine exemption.

    - heading: "No direct flight: routing through Hong Kong"
      body: |
        Macau International Airport has no long-haul cargo service from Australia, so the practical path is Sydney or Melbourne to Hong Kong with Cathay Pacific Cargo or Qantas Freight, followed by a road transfer across the Hong Kong-Zhuhai-Macau Bridge, or a ferry, about an hour once the crate clears Hong Kong customs.

        Time the PS-1092 licence application against a confirmed flight date. IAM usually issues it within three working days, but it expires after 30, so applying too early risks it lapsing before travel.
