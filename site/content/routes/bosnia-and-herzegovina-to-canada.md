content:
  h1: "Pet Transport from Bosnia and Herzegovina to Canada"
  overview: |
    Sarajevo has no shortage of European connections, but not one of them reaches Canada without a change of aircraft somewhere along the way. That's the entire practical challenge on this route. The regulatory side barely registers by comparison: Bosnia and Herzegovina does not appear anywhere on CFIA's high-risk list for dog rabies, so a dog needs a standard vaccination certificate and nothing close to the age rule, import permit or designated-airport restriction that apply to a listed country.

    A cat needs even less. CFIA's federal rules are built around dog rabies specifically, so a cat clears the Canadian border on a basic health certificate alone.

  sections:
    - heading: "What CFIA's standard-risk pathway actually asks for"
      body: |
        CFIA's high-risk list, last reviewed 13 March 2025, runs across a long stretch of Africa, Asia, the Middle East, Eastern Europe and parts of the Americas and Caribbean. Bosnia and Herzegovina sits outside all of it. That removes the 7-month minimum age rule, the mandatory CFIA import permit, and the restriction to a handful of designated airports, all of which apply only to a dog from a listed country.

        What's left for a Bosnian dog is close to the bare minimum: a rabies vaccination certificate current within 3 years, from a licensed vet, checked visually by CBSA on arrival alongside BiH's own export paperwork.

    - heading: "The flight is the actual bottleneck"
      body: |
        No airline flies Sarajevo to a Canadian city directly. Turkish Airlines via Istanbul is a strong option, with Turkish's own Istanbul-Toronto service running 9 times a week and its dedicated Pet Lounge easing the connection. Lufthansa via Frankfurt is a solid alternative, with Frankfurt, not Munich, being the group hub that actually handles a connecting cargo pet, and a genuinely long-established Frankfurt-Toronto route on the far side.

        Austrian Airlines via Vienna needs a caveat. Its own long-haul network to Canada has shrunk over recent years, so the transatlantic leg of a Vienna-routed booking often ends up flown by a Star Alliance partner rather than Austrian itself. Confirm the actual operating carrier before committing to that routing, since pet policy can differ between the airline booked and the one that flies the long leg.

    - heading: "The paperwork gap CFIA hasn't filled"
      body: |
        Search CFIA's own export-side documentation for a Bosnia-specific certificate template and there isn't one, a genuine parallel to the gap USDA APHIS has confirmed on its own equivalent US pages. That's a description of a documentation gap, not a missing requirement: BiH's own Veterinary Office publishes its rules directly, and a general international health certificate, built to match what BiH expects, does the job in practice.

faqs:
  - question: "Is Bosnia and Herzegovina on Canada's high-risk list for dog rabies?"
    answer: "No. CFIA's high-risk-for-dog-rabies list, last reviewed 13 March 2025, does not include Bosnia and Herzegovina. That means no CFIA import permit, no 7-month minimum age rule, and no restriction to a designated Canadian airport, all of which only apply to a dog from a listed high-risk country."
  - question: "Does my dog need a rabies titre test to enter Canada from Bosnia and Herzegovina?"
    answer: "No. A titre test only applies to a dog from a CFIA-designated high-risk country, and Bosnia and Herzegovina isn't one of them. A standard rabies vaccination certificate, current within 3 years, is what CBSA checks on arrival."
  - question: "Is there a direct flight from Sarajevo to Canada?"
    answer: "No. You'll connect through a European hub, most practically Istanbul with Turkish Airlines or Frankfurt with Lufthansa, both of which run their own established onward services to Toronto. Vienna with Austrian Airlines works too, though the transatlantic leg on that routing is often operated by a Star Alliance partner rather than Austrian itself."
  - question: "What does my cat need to enter Canada from Bosnia and Herzegovina?"
    answer: "Very little. CFIA has no federal rabies vaccination or microchip requirement for cats. A basic BiH health certificate for the airline's sake is sensible, but nothing specific to cats is checked at the Canadian border."
  - question: "How long does the export process take on the Bosnian side?"
    answer: "The State Veterinary Office endorsement typically takes 2 to 3 working days once the health certificate is prepared by a BiH vet. Combined with booking a connecting flight, 6 to 10 weeks is a realistic total timeline for most pets."
