  h1: "Pet Transport from Ivory Coast to the United States"
  overview: |
    The CDC's list of high-risk countries for dog rabies runs to more than a hundred entries, and Cote d'Ivoire, confirmed on the list updated 15 April 2026, is one of them. That single line of classification triggers the full foreign-vaccinated-dog pathway for any dog on this route: a titre test with its own fixed clock, a reservation at one of only six CDC-registered facility airports, and no domestic connection allowed after landing.

    Two airlines happen to solve the facility-airport problem cleanly. Royal Air Maroc's Casablanca to New York JFK service and Ethiopian Airlines' Addis Ababa to Washington Dulles service are both genuine single-carrier nonstops that land exactly where the facility reservation needs to be.

    Cats clear the federal side of this route far more lightly: no rabies vaccination, titre test or facility booking required. Realistically, budget four to five months for a dog, driven almost entirely by the titre test's 30-plus-28-day minimum.
  sections:
    - heading: "The titre test and the 28-day alternative"
      body: |
        A dog travelling on a Cote d'Ivoire-issued vaccination has two paths into the US. The first: a passing rabies titre result, blood drawn at least 30 days after vaccination and at least 28 days before the flight, tested at a CDC-approved laboratory. The second, for a dog without a valid titre: a mandatory 28-day quarantine at a CDC-registered facility, paid for by the owner.

        Most owners choose the titre route, since it avoids both the quarantine cost and the month-long separation. The maths is unforgiving, though: miss the 30-day-post-vaccine window for the blood draw, or the 28-day pre-flight window for the result, and the flight either slips or the quarantine option becomes the only one left.

        Nothing about DSV's own paperwork in Cote d'Ivoire shortens this. The two processes, US titre requirements and Ivorian export documentation, run in parallel rather than one substituting for the other.
    - heading: "Why JFK and Dulles are the routings to aim for"
      body: |
        Only six US airports have a CDC-registered animal care facility, and a high-risk-country dog has to land at one of them directly, with no domestic connection afterward. That rule catches out owners who book the cheapest available itinerary without checking where it actually lands.

        Royal Air Maroc's Casablanca to JFK nonstop and Ethiopian Airlines' Addis Ababa to Dulles nonstop both avoid the problem outright. Each keeps the dog on a single carrier's network from Abidjan to a facility airport, with one connection in between rather than a chain of separate bookings.
    - heading: "Cats: a much shorter list of federal requirements"
      body: |
        A cat making this same journey faces almost none of the machinery built around high-risk-country dogs. No CDC rabies vaccination requirement, no titre test, no facility reservation, no quarantine risk.

        What doesn't change is the Ivorian side. DSV's export health certificate and endorsement still apply to cats exactly as they do to dogs, and most airlines will want their own current health certificate regardless of species before accepting the booking.
