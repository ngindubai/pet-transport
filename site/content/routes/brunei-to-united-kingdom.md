content:
  h1: "Pet Transport from Brunei to the UK: 2026 Guide"
  overview: |
    There is no direct pet cargo flight out of Bandar Seri Begawan. Royal Brunei Airlines, the only carrier at Brunei International Airport, does not run a standard live animal cargo service for privately owned dogs and cats, so the first move for almost every owner is a road or regional transfer to Singapore Changi before the long-haul leg even begins.

    Add the UK's non-listed country process on top of that, and this becomes one of the longer routes on this site: a mandatory titre test, a fixed three-month wait from the blood draw date, and an APHA Import Licence. Expect close to six months from first vet visit to touchdown at Heathrow.

  sections:
    - heading: "Why this route starts in Singapore, not Brunei"
      body: |
        Brunei International Airport (BWN) has limited cargo infrastructure and Royal Brunei Airlines has never published a standard commercial pet cargo product for privately owned animals. In practice, owners move their pet by road to Singapore, or occasionally via a regional flight, and complete the international leg from Changi.

        This adds a step most owners on other Southeast Asian routes do not have to plan for. It also adds cost. Factor a domestic transfer fee, plus the logistics of coordinating the DAA export paperwork with whichever carrier picks the pet up in Singapore.

        Kuala Lumpur is the other common transfer point, using Malaysia Airlines Cargo for the first leg. Either way, a specialist agent who already has a working relationship with a Brunei-Singapore or Brunei-KL handler is worth the fee on this corridor.
    - heading: "The titre test and the 90-day clock"
      body: |
        Brunei sits outside the UK's listed country schedule, so the standard non-listed process applies in full. Blood is drawn at least 30 days after the rabies vaccination and must show a result of at least 0.5 IU/ml. From the date of that blood draw, three calendar months must pass before the pet can legally enter Great Britain.

        This is the step that decides your whole schedule. Book vaccination and testing appointments early, because the 90-day wait is not negotiable and does not shorten for any reason, including a prior passing titre result from years earlier.

        Once past the 90-day mark, the rest of the process (APHA Import Licence, GB Pet Health Certificate, tapeworm treatment for dogs) runs on a shorter, more familiar timeline of weeks rather than months.
    - heading: "Cost and airline reality on the BWN to LHR corridor"
      body: |
        Expect GBP 3,000 to GBP 7,000 for a medium-sized dog once the transfer leg, crate, titre test, health documentation, and cargo fees are all in. Cat moves generally sit toward the lower end, since cats are exempt from the tapeworm treatment step and often travel in smaller crates.

        British Airways World Cargo, working with PetAir UK on the ground, is the most commonly used onward carrier from Singapore or Kuala Lumpur to Heathrow. Qatar Airways via Doha is a solid alternative if BA capacity is tight on your preferred dates.
