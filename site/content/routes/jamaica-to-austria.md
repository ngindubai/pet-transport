  sections:
    - heading: "Jamaica's listed status: no titre test for Austria"
      body: |
        EU Implementing Regulation 2026/636 classifies Jamaica as a listed third country, meaning pets from Jamaica are exempt from the FAVN titre test and the 90-day waiting period when entering EU member states including Austria. BAVG (the Austrian Federal Office of Consumer Health Protection) still requires the standard EU-format non-commercial health certificate.

        The VSD in Jamaica issues the export health certificate through a VSD-registered official veterinarian, along with a VSD export permit (approximately J$2,000). DHLPP vaccination is required by VSD in addition to rabies. The practical preparation involves confirming microchip and vaccinations, obtaining the VSD permit (4-6 weeks lead time), booking cargo, submitting 1 working day advance notification to BAVG, and completing the health certificate appointment within 10 days of departure.

    - heading: "Routing to Vienna and the BAVG notification requirement"
      body: |
        Vienna International Airport (VIE) is Austria's primary Border Control Post for non-EU pet arrivals. Linz Airport (LNZ) is also designated but has more restricted coverage. No direct service exists from Jamaica to Vienna; routing via London (British Airways via LHR), Frankfurt (Lufthansa Cargo), or Amsterdam (KLM) are the practical options.

        Austrian Airlines is based at VIE and connects from major European hubs. BAVG requires at least 1 working day advance notification before non-EU pets arrive. Use the BAVG portal at bavg.gv.at or contact the Border Control Post at VIE directly. Provide the pet's details, certificate reference, and flight details.
