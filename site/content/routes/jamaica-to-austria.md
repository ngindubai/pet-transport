content:
  h1: "Pet Transport Jamaica to Austria: 2026 Guide"
  overview: "Moving a pet from Jamaica to Austria is one of the more straightforward Caribbean-to-Europe routes, largely because Jamaica holds EU Group 2 listed status under Implementing Regulation (EU) 2024/1130. That status removes the need for a rabies titre test and the lengthy 90-day waiting period that applies to unlisted countries. With correct microchipping, a valid rabies vaccination, and a properly endorsed health certificate, most dogs and cats can complete preparation in four to eight weeks."

  sections:
    - heading: "Why Jamaica's EU Listed Status Changes Everything"
      body: |
        Most countries in the Caribbean and Latin America are classified as unlisted third countries for EU pet import purposes. That classification triggers a mandatory FAVN rabies titre test, a minimum 30-day wait after the blood draw, and then a further 90-day wait before the pet can enter the EU. The total delay can run to six months.

        Jamaica is different. Under Commission Implementing Regulation (EU) 2024/1130 (which updated Annex II of Regulation (EU) No 576/2013), Jamaica sits in Group 2 of the listed third countries and territories. This means the titre test pathway is bypassed entirely. Your pet needs a microchip, a valid rabies vaccination (with 21 days elapsed since the primary dose), and an official health certificate. That is all.

        This distinction matters if you are comparing notes with other pet owners relocating from, say, Panama or Colombia, where the titre test and 90-day wait are both mandatory. Jamaica's listed status is a genuine advantage, and it cuts preparation time significantly.

    - heading: "Austrian Import Requirements in Full"
      body: |
        Austria follows EU non-commercial pet movement rules. The governing regulation from April 2026 is Commission Delegated Regulation (EU) 2026/131, which consolidates the framework previously set out under Regulation (EU) No 576/2013.

        The requirements are:

        Microchip: ISO 11784/11785 compliant transponder, implanted before the rabies vaccination. If your pet was chipped after the vaccination date recorded in the documents, the vaccination date is treated as the chip date, and the 21-day waiting period restarts from that point.

        Rabies vaccination: Must be current and administered after the microchip was implanted. A minimum of 21 days must have elapsed since the primary vaccination before travel. Booster vaccinations given within the valid period of the previous dose are accepted as immediately effective.

        Health certificate: EU Annex IV non-commercial health certificate format, signed by a VSD-authorised veterinarian in Jamaica and endorsed by the Veterinary Services Division. The certificate is valid for 10 days from the date of signing, so timing matters.

        The oversight body in Austria is the Federal Agency for Food Safety (AGES, ages.at). Veterinary checks on entry to Austria typically occur at Vienna International Airport (VIE) for air arrivals.

    - heading: "Routing Options: KIN and MBJ to Vienna"
      body: |
        There is no direct air cargo service from Jamaica to Vienna. You will need a two-leg journey, almost always through London Heathrow (LHR) or Amsterdam (AMS).

        The most commonly used path is Jamaica to LHR on British Airways World Cargo or Virgin Atlantic Cargo, followed by an onward cargo transfer to Vienna on British Airways or Austrian Airlines. BA World Cargo handles live animals and has established procedures for the KIN route. Austrian Airlines cargo operates the LHR-VIE sector and accepts live animals on approved bookings.

        KLM Royal Dutch Airlines offers an alternative via Amsterdam. KLM Cargo operates transatlantic routes from KIN to AMS, and the AMS-VIE leg is short and well served. This is worth considering if space on the LHR routes is unavailable or if your travel dates align better with KLM's schedules.

        Cargo space for live animals is limited and often fills weeks in advance, especially in summer and around the Christmas period. Book as early as you can, well before the health certificate is even issued.

    - heading: "IATA Crate Standards and Breed Considerations"
      body: |
        All live animals travelling as cargo must be in IATA-compliant crates. The crate must be large enough for your pet to stand, turn around, and lie down naturally. Airlines will refuse animals in crates that do not meet these dimensions, so measure your pet carefully before purchasing.

        Brachycephalic (flat-faced) breeds such as French Bulldogs, Pugs, English Bulldogs, and Persian cats face additional restrictions. Some carriers refuse these breeds entirely as cargo, and Austria's veterinary regulations do not override airline policies on this point. Confirm with the airline before booking. If your pet is a brachycephalic breed, contact us early so we can identify which carriers will accept them.

    - heading: "Documents to Carry on the Day"
      body: |
        When you arrive at the cargo terminal at KIN or MBJ, and when the pet is collected at VIE, you will need the following:

        - The VSD-endorsed EU-format health certificate (original, not a copy)
        - Vaccination records showing the microchip number, vaccine batch details, and dates
        - Your own passport or travel ID
        - The airline airway bill for the live animal shipment

        Austrian border veterinary officials are thorough. Documents that are incomplete, expired, or that show inconsistencies between the microchip number and the vaccination record will result in the animal being held. Carry everything in a single waterproof folder and keep copies separately.

faqs:
  - question: "Does my pet need a rabies titre test to enter Austria from Jamaica?"
    answer: "No. Jamaica is listed as an EU Group 2 country under Implementing Regulation (EU) 2024/1130. This means the FAVN titre test and the associated 90-day waiting period are not required. Your pet needs a microchip, a current rabies vaccination (21 days elapsed since primary dose), and a VSD-endorsed health certificate."
  - question: "How long before departure does the health certificate need to be issued?"
    answer: "The health certificate must be issued within 10 days of travel and endorsed by the VSD in Jamaica. Do not have it signed earlier than 10 days before departure, as it will expire before your pet arrives in Austria."

Check with your chosen airline for the specific rules on the second leg."
