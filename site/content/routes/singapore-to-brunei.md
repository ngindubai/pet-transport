  h1: "Pet Transport from Singapore to Brunei"
  overview: |
    Only five countries make Brunei's scheduled, rabies-controlled list, and Singapore is one of them, sitting alongside Australia, Ireland, New Zealand and the UK. A pet that has lived in Singapore continuously for six months or more can, in principle, skip the titre test and avoid facility quarantine entirely on arrival.

    Better still, this is a genuinely direct route. Royal Brunei Airlines flies Singapore Changi to Brunei International non-stop and accepts pets as manifested cargo through its own Cargo Department. No hub, no second carrier, no transfer leg. Ten to sixteen weeks covers the whole move for a qualifying pet.

  sections:
    - heading: "What Singapore's scheduled status actually buys you"
      body: |
        Brunei's Department of Agriculture and Agrifood treats a short list of origin countries differently from the rest of the world, and Singapore has been on that list for years. A pet with continuous Singapore residency of six months or more can typically avoid both the titre test and the facility quarantine that non-scheduled origins face.

        This isn't automatic. DAA reviews eligibility when the import permit application goes in, checking residency evidence alongside the standard microchip and vaccination records. A pet that has moved between countries recently, without six months of continuous Singapore residency, should plan for the full non-scheduled process instead, including the FAVN titre test.

    - heading: "A direct flight, start to finish"
      body: |
        Royal Brunei Airlines operates the Singapore-Brunei sector as one of its core regional routes, and its cargo policy confirms live animals travel as manifested cargo through the airline's own Cargo Department. Since the departure city is also where the AVS export paperwork gets completed, there's no coordination needed across a second international leg.

        This puts Singapore-to-Brunei in a different category from most of this site's Southeast Asia routes, where a hub transfer through Bangkok, Hong Kong or Dubai is standard. Confirm cargo space with Royal Brunei's team directly, since booking runs through their cargo desk rather than a passenger-style online system.

    - heading: "Breed rules that apply regardless of scheduled status"
      body: |
        Brunei bans a handful of breeds outright: Pit Bull Terriers, Dogo Argentino and Japanese Tosa cannot be imported under any circumstances, scheduled-country status or not. A second tier, including Bull Mastiff, Doberman and German Shepherd, is permitted with conditions: a minimum age at import, mandatory neutering, and a public muzzling requirement.

        Check your dog's breed against DAA's current list early. Scheduled status shortens the paperwork and skips quarantine for a qualifying pet; it does nothing for a banned breed.
