  h1: "Pet Transport from Saint Lucia to the United Kingdom"
  overview: |
    Saint Lucia sits on the UK's Part 2 list of rabies-classified non-EU countries and territories, which strips out the one step that makes so many other Caribbean and Pacific routes slow: the rabies titre test. No blood test, no 90-day wait. Just the standard vaccination, a 21-day clock, and an Animal Health Certificate.

    Getting there is the part that has changed recently. Virgin Atlantic flew London to Saint Lucia for years; that service ended on 14 March 2026. British Airways is now the only nonstop option, running a lighter schedule outside the winter season before becoming a daily service on 25 October 2026.

    Plan for 10 to 14 weeks. Most of that is the vaccination wait plus getting cargo space confirmed on a route with fewer weekly seats than it had a year ago.

  sections:
    - heading: "What Part 2 listing removes"
      body: |
        The UK sorts non-EU countries into three groups: Part 1, Part 2, and unlisted. Unlisted countries get the full titre-test treatment. Saint Lucia is Part 2, alongside places like the USA, Canada and Australia, which means the paperwork trail is simpler: microchip, vaccination, 21-day wait, then an AHC. Dogs also need the standard tapeworm treatment in the 24 to 120 hours before UK arrival; cats skip that step entirely.

        This is a genuinely easier route on paper than an unlisted-country move. The complication sits with getting off the island, not with what the UK asks for once the pet lands.

    - heading: "One carrier, fewer seats than there used to be"
      body: |
        Virgin Atlantic's withdrawal from the London-Saint Lucia route in March 2026 left British Airways as the sole nonstop operator, flying Gatwick to Hewanorra. British Airways has confirmed this becomes a daily standalone service from 25 October 2026, adding roughly 51,000 seats for that winter season. Outside that window, the schedule is currently lighter, tagged onto other Caribbean routings rather than running as a dedicated daily flight.

        The practical effect: book earlier than you might expect for a Part 2 listed route, and check the current frequency for your travel month rather than assuming a daily flight exists year-round.

    - heading: "The connecting alternative"
      body: |
        If British Airways' schedule doesn't line up, a connection through Miami on American Airlines, close to daily at around 15 flights a week, or through JFK on JetBlue or American, gets a pet off the island and onto a separate transatlantic leg. JetBlue only carries pets in the cabin, so it suits small dogs and cats rather than larger animals, which need American's cargo service or the direct British Airways option instead.
