  h1: "Pet Transport from Tajikistan to Canada"
  overview: |
    Compare this route with its own US equivalent and the gap is stark. A dog leaving Tajikistan for the United States needs a passing rabies titre test or a 28-day stay at a CDC-registered facility, a process that runs 22 to 26 weeks, driven mostly by the fact that Tajikistan has no such facility of its own to book locally. Heading to Canada instead, none of that applies. CFIA's own high-risk list, current as of 13 March 2025, does include Tajikistan, but the classification's practical force lands on commercial dog imports, not a family pet travelling with its owner.

    What's left is a routine process: current vaccination, a certificate built to CFIA's format, and a connection through Istanbul, since no airline flies Dushanbe to Canada directly.

  sections:
    - heading: "The same country, two very different rulebooks"
      body: |
        Tajikistan sits on both the CDC's and CFIA's high-risk lists for dog rabies, which might suggest the two destinations treat an arriving dog the same way. They don't. The CDC's rule forces a choice between a serology titre test, timed against a 30-day-after-vaccination and 28-day-before-entry window, or 28 days of quarantine at a CDC-registered facility on arrival, of which Tajikistan has none locally to book in advance. CFIA has no equivalent machinery for personal pets at all. Its high-risk classification bars commercial dog shipments outright, a rule in force since 28 September 2022, but an owner-accompanied dog or cat needs nothing more than the ordinary vaccination and certificate.

        That difference alone can save months of planning for a family choosing between the two destinations, or moving on to Canada after already researching the US route.

    - heading: "The one detail worth checking regardless"
      body: |
        CFIA presumes a dog under 8 months old, not travelling with its named owner, is a commercial import. From a high-risk country like Tajikistan, that presumption has genuine consequences, since commercial dogs from listed countries are barred outright. A dog old enough, and clearly accompanied by the owner named on its papers, sits outside that presumption. Confirm directly with CFIA if either detail could apply.

    - heading: "Getting to Toronto via Istanbul"
      body: |
        Turkish Airlines runs a genuine single-carrier option here: Dushanbe to Istanbul roughly seven times a week, connecting to Turkish's own nonstop Istanbul-Toronto service. Air Astana and Emirates both work but need at least one further connection beyond their own regional hubs before reaching Canada, a longer and less predictable chain than Turkish's single change of aircraft.
