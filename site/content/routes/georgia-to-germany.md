  sections:
    - heading: "Georgia's EU candidate status and what it does not change"
      body: |
        Georgia was granted EU candidate status in December 2023, and the EU-Georgia Association Agreement has been in force since 2016. These agreements are meaningful for trade and political alignment, but they do not change EU veterinary import classifications for pets.

        The EU listed country register for pet travel is maintained separately from trade agreements. Georgia is not on this register, and until a formal equivalence assessment is completed and Georgia is added, the full unlisted process applies. In practical terms: FAVN titre test at an EU-approved lab, blood drawn at least 30 days after vaccination, then a mandatory 90-day wait from the successful blood draw date before entry to Germany or any other EU member state. Source: EU Regulation 2020/692.

        This catches people out when they assume that Georgia's EU relationship translates into simpler pet travel rules. It does not, at the time of writing.

    - heading: "Getting the FAVN test done from Tbilisi"
      body: |
        The FAVN titre test must be processed at an EU-approved laboratory. Most approved labs are in Europe, the UK, or North America. From Georgia, the process involves your vet drawing the blood sample, storing it in an appropriate preservative medium, and shipping it internationally via courier to the approved lab.

        Turnaround time from draw to result is typically 2-4 weeks, including shipping. This is within the 90-day wait, so the wait itself does not extend because of laboratory processing time. The 90-day clock runs from the blood draw date.

        Ask your Tbilisi vet whether they have done this before. International titre test sample shipping is not the same as a domestic blood test, and an error in storage or documentation wastes a month and forces a repeat test. The NFA or a Georgian pet relocation agent can direct you to vets with this experience.
