  h1: "Pet Transport from Turkmenistan to the United Arab Emirates"
  overview: |
    flydubai flies Ashgabat to Dubai directly. That single fact makes the flight side of this route far simpler than most other corridors out of Turkmenistan covered on this site, which typically need a connection through Istanbul or a second onward leg entirely.

    The medical side doesn't get the same shortcut. MOCCAE's exemption list for rabies-controlled countries runs to around 50 names, the UK, Australia, Japan and Hong Kong among them, and Turkmenistan isn't one of them. Expect the full titre test: blood drawn at least 30 days after vaccination, a passing result, then a 90-day wait before travel.

    Add Turkmenistan's own closed administrative process on the export side, and this route still needs 20 to 26 weeks even with the flight problem largely solved.

  sections:
    - heading: "The one part of this route that isn't a bottleneck"
      body: |
        Every other Turkmenistan route on this site opens with a flight problem: Ashgabat barely connects to the rest of the world, and cargo capacity is scarce. Dubai breaks that pattern. flydubai's own Ashgabat-Dubai service is scheduled, direct, and has documented pet-carriage capability, making it the most dependable single leg covered anywhere in Turkmenistan's route set on this site.

        That doesn't mean booking last minute. Confirm current cargo capacity with flydubai or an agent 4-6 weeks out, since schedules on this corridor can still shift with little warning.

    - heading: "Why the titre test still applies in full"
      body: |
        MOCCAE's exemption list spares roughly 50 countries with recognised rabies control programmes from the blood test altogether. Turkmenistan's own dog rabies burden is high and largely uncontrolled by comparison, which is presumably why it doesn't appear on any version of that list checked for this guide.

        Budget for the full sequence: vaccination, a 30-day wait, the blood draw at an approved laboratory abroad, since none exists inside Turkmenistan, and then 90 days from a successful result before the pet can travel. This is the part of the route that actually sets the calendar, not the flight.

    - heading: "The Alabai, and Turkmenistan's own pace"
      body: |
        A 2022 law requires Alabai puppies to be registered in a government pedigree book and carry a passport, with special government permission needed before the breed can leave the country. Reporting at the time varied between calling this an outright ban and a restriction; in practice, no full pedigree Alabai has been documented leaving under this process, so treat it as a de facto block rather than a formality to work around.

        More broadly, Turkmenistan's administrative pace runs slower than almost any other origin on this site. English-language guidance from the State Veterinary Service is thin, and a relocation agent with direct Turkmenistan experience is close to essential rather than optional.
