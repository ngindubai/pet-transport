  sections:
    - heading: "Flight options and journey times"
      body: |
        Copenhagen Kastrup (CPH) to Vilnius (VNO) by air is about 1.5 to 2 hours direct. SAS operates this route on certain schedules and is the most direct option. airBaltic connects via Riga, where the transfer adds an hour or so to the total journey time but provides access to airBaltic's cargo facilities at RIX.

        For pets small enough to travel in the cabin, the direct SAS flight is convenient. For larger dogs, the cargo booking process is the priority. SAS Cargo and airBaltic Cargo both cover this market, but booking ahead matters, especially in summer when flights fill quickly.

        Ryanair serves CPH-VNO on some schedules but is cabin-only. Worth considering for a small dog or cat if the price advantage is meaningful.

    - heading: "Lithuania's VMVT: what to carry, what to expect"
      body: |
        VMVT (Valstybine maisto ir veterinarijos tarnyba) is Lithuania's State Food and Veterinary Service, the authority responsible for companion animal imports. For an intra-EU arrival from Denmark, VMVT requires the EU pet passport. That's the entirety of the documentation requirement.

        The passport must record: an ISO 15-digit microchip implanted before the rabies vaccination, a current rabies vaccination, and the issuing vet's details. VMVT may conduct a spot check at VNO, particularly if the arrival is flagged as a cargo pet. These are routine and straightforward for a compliant arrival.

        Lithuania has PPP (potentially dangerous or prohibited) dog regulations. The vast majority of pets travelling this route won't encounter this issue, but if you're moving a large guard breed or a dog that might be classified under Lithuanian law, check VMVT's current guidance before travel.
