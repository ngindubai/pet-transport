  sections:
    - heading: "Madrid vs Miami: why the transit choice matters"
      body: |
        No airline flies directly from the UK to San Jose, Costa Rica. All routes involve at least one transit hub. The choice of hub affects the complexity of the move significantly.

        Routing via Madrid on Iberia keeps the journey within the European and Latin American cargo network and avoids USDA entry requirements entirely. Iberia has a strong connection to Costa Rica from Madrid and the cargo handling is established. KLM via Amsterdam offers a similar advantage.

        Routing via Miami on British Airways or American introduces a US transit point. USDA veterinary inspection requirements can apply to animals transiting through US airports, even when the final destination is elsewhere. The Miami transit is workable but adds a step. For straightforward moves, the European hub routing is preferable.

    - heading: "SENASA's online declaration and why it must be done first"
      body: |
        Costa Rica's SENASA (Servicio Nacional de Salud Animal) has moved its import clearance process online. The health declaration for your dog or cat must be submitted via the SENASA portal before travel. Once submitted and approved, you receive the import clearance documentation to present with your APHA health certificate on arrival.

        This is similar in function to the AGROCALIDAD permit in Ecuador or the SENASICA permit in Mexico: it is the destination country's pre-approval. Complete it before your health certificate appointment, as having both documents ready for travel is essential.
