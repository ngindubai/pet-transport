  sections:
    - heading: "The SENASA inspection at San Jose airport"
      body: |
        All international pets entering Costa Rica go through SENASA (Servicio Nacional de Sanidad Animal) inspection at Juan Santamaria International Airport (SJO) in San Jose. SENASA inspectors review the health certificate, check vaccination records, and verify parasite treatment documentation.

        The inspection is straightforward for well-prepared owners. SENASA will check: the ISO microchip number against the certificate, that the rabies vaccination was given at least 30 days before and no more than 12 months before entry, that core vaccines are current, and that parasite treatment was given within 15 days of travel and is documented with product name and date.

        If any item is missing or out of the required timeframe, the pet may be held in quarantine at the owner's expense while SENASA determines the correct course of action. Getting the documentation right before travel is far less stressful than dealing with this at the airport.

    - heading: "Core vaccines and the parasite treatment requirement"
      body: |
        Beyond rabies, SENASA requires current core vaccinations. For dogs, this means distemper, hepatitis, parvovirus, and leptospirosis. For cats: feline rhinotracheitis, calicivirus, and panleukopenia. All must be within 12 months of travel.

        The parasite treatment requirement is specific: the vet must treat your pet for internal parasites (deworming) and external parasites (fleas, ticks) within 15 days of travel. The treatment product name, dose, and date of administration must be listed on the health certificate. This is not a general recommendation but a documented, vet-administered treatment.

        Book the vet appointment for all of these steps as a single visit 10 to 15 days before travel: health examination, parasite treatment, and health certificate issue. This keeps the certificate timing within the 15-day window and ensures all documentation is completed together.

faqs:
  - question: "Do I need a titre test for Costa Rica from the UK?"
    answer: "No. Costa Rica does not require a rabies antibody titre test for pets from the UK. Rabies vaccination (given at least 30 days before travel and valid for up to 12 months) is sufficient, along with the APHA-endorsed health certificate and current core vaccines."
  - question: "Is there quarantine for pets arriving in Costa Rica from the UK?"
    answer: "No routine quarantine for compliant pets. SENASA carries out an inspection at Juan Santamaria Airport. If documentation is complete and timing requirements are met, the pet is cleared. If documentation is incomplete, quarantine at the owner's expense is possible."
  - question: "What import permit do I need for Costa Rica?"
    answer: "A SENASA import permit is required for pets travelling as cargo. This is arranged in advance, either directly with SENASA (senasa.go.cr) or through a customs agent in Costa Rica. Pets travelling as accompanied baggage or in-cabin with their owner go through a simplified arrival process, though the health certificate requirements still apply."
  - question: "Which airlines carry pets from the UK to Costa Rica?"
    answer: "Most routes connect through a European hub (Madrid, Amsterdam) or through Miami. Iberia/British Airways via Madrid, KLM via Amsterdam, and American Airlines via Miami all handle pet cargo to San Jose. Direct UK-to-Costa Rica flights are not available, so all routes involve at least one connection."
  - question: "How long does preparation take for UK to Costa Rica?"
    answer: "Allow 6 to 10 weeks. The rabies vaccination must be given at least 30 days before travel. The health certificate and parasite treatment must be within 15 days. If you need to apply for a SENASA import permit, allow 4 to 6 weeks for processing."
