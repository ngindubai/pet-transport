content:
  h1: "Pet Transport from the Netherlands to Thailand"
  overview: |
    The moment you decide to bring your dog or cat with you to Thailand, two things become clear very quickly. First, this is absolutely doable. Second, the paperwork has to happen in the right order, and one step in particular needs to happen before you even think about booking your flights.

    That step is the Thai Embassy endorsement. Your official health certificate, once signed by a Dutch official veterinarian, has to be taken to the Royal Thai Embassy in The Hague and stamped before you fly. You cannot get this done on arrival in Bangkok. Get the appointment booked first.

    Start planning eight to twelve weeks before your travel date. The DLD import permit alone needs 15 days' lead time, and the embassy visit needs its own window around that.
  sections:
    - heading: "Thailand's entry requirements: what the DLD is looking for"
      body: |
        Thailand's Department of Livestock Development is the authority that controls all pet imports. They want to see three things when your pet arrives at Suvarnabhumi: a valid ISO microchip, a current rabies vaccination with proper records, and an endorsed import permit in hand.

        The permit application goes through dld.go.th. If you are using a pet transport agent, they can handle this on your behalf. The permit will specify your arrival airport. Do not change airports after the permit is issued without contacting DLD first.

    - heading: "The embassy endorsement: plan around this"
      body: |
        After your NVWA-authorised official vet in the Netherlands signs the health certificate, it needs to go to the Royal Thai Embassy in The Hague. They will review and stamp it.

        Call ahead for an appointment. Walk-ins are not reliable. Allow a full week between getting the certificate signed and needing it endorsed. This is the step that creates the tightest timing window in the whole process.

    - heading: "Flying Amsterdam to Bangkok with a pet"
      body: |
        KLM is the natural first choice. It flies direct from Schiphol to Bangkok Suvarnabhumi and has a well-established cargo process for pets. Book cargo space through the KLM Cargo desk, not the passenger booking system.

        Thai Airways also flies this route and allows small cabin pets on certain departures. Emirates and Qatar both offer connections via their Gulf hubs if scheduling suits you better.

        For larger dogs, cargo is the only option. The KLM and Emirates cargo holds are temperature-controlled, which matters on long-haul flights.

    - heading: "What happens when you land in Bangkok"
      body: |
        Arrive prepared. At Suvarnabhumi, the Animal Quarantine checkpoint is a separate process from passenger arrivals. Present the DLD permit and the endorsed health certificate. With everything in order, clearance typically takes a few hours. No mandatory quarantine applies for pets from the Netherlands with complete documentation.
