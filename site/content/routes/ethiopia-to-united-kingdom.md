  sections:
    - heading: "The unlisted country requirements in plain English"
      body: |
        For pets from unlisted countries like Ethiopia, the UK requires proof that the rabies vaccination has taken effect, not just that it was administered. That proof comes from the FAVN titre test: a blood test that measures antibody levels.

        The sequence is: microchip (before vaccination), rabies vaccination, wait 30 days, blood draw for FAVN test, wait for laboratory result (2-4 weeks), then if the result passes, wait 90 days before travelling to Great Britain.

        If the result fails (below 0.5 IU/ml), you revaccinate and restart the 30-day wait before testing again. A passing result that took three test attempts adds months to the timeline.

        The AHC is issued within 10 days of travel. The tapeworm treatment (for dogs) is administered in the 24 to 120 hour window before UK arrival. These final steps are straightforward once the titre test wait is complete.

    - heading: "Ethiopian Airlines as a route option"
      body: |
        Ethiopian Airlines is the flag carrier of Ethiopia and one of Africa's largest airlines. It operates a direct service from Addis Ababa Bole International Airport (ADD) to London Heathrow, which is a significant advantage for this route. A direct flight avoids the hub layover and the additional handling that comes with it.

        Ethiopian Airlines has a cargo programme that accepts live animals on most routes. Confirm with their cargo division that the specific flight you want to book accepts pets, and whether your crate size and breed are permitted. Not all flights accept cargo pets even on routes where the airline generally accepts them.

        Addis Ababa is at high altitude (2,300 metres), which affects some aircraft's pressurisation systems on the ground. This is rarely an issue for cargo pet travel, but it is worth mentioning if your vet has concerns about your pet's health at altitude.

    - heading: "Shipping blood samples for the titre test from Ethiopia"
      body: |
        The FAVN titre test blood sample needs to reach an APHA-approved laboratory. Most approved labs are in Europe, North America, or Australia. Your vet in Addis Ababa draws the blood and ships it cold via a specialist veterinary courier.

        Not all vets in Addis Ababa have experience with international FAVN blood shipping. Ask your vet directly whether they have done this before. If not, a pet transport agent with Ethiopian experience can connect you with a vet who has. The laboratory result certificate is what starts your 90-day clock, so accuracy at this stage matters.
