  h1: "Pet Transport from Czech Republic to Maldives"
  overview: |
    Prague has, in recent seasons, had something few European capitals get: a direct charter service to Male, flown by the Italian leisure carrier Neos as part of its European package-holiday network. It's not guaranteed to run every year, so confirm current dates before assuming it covers your travel window.

    The flight option doesn't touch the harder question. Dogs cannot legally enter the Maldives, a Maldives Customs Service rule with no standard exception, whichever airline carries them. Cats have a workable pathway: microchip, rabies vaccination 30 days to 12 months before arrival, the core feline vaccines, and a certificate signed within 10 days of travel.

      body: |
        The Maldives is one of Europe's biggest package-holiday destinations, and Neos operates seasonal leisure routes built around exactly that market. Prague has featured on its schedule in past seasons. Charter carriers built for tourist traffic don't always handle live animal cargo the same way scheduled airlines do, with different booking lead times and sometimes different crate requirements, so confirm directly with Neos or an agent rather than assuming standard cargo rules apply.
    - heading: "The dog answer, regardless of flight"
      body: |
        No confirmed government pathway exists for a dog entering the Maldives, service animals included. Some forums describe informal exceptions; we have not found a primary Maldivian government document backing that up as a repeatable process, so we're not presenting it as one. If a service dog is genuinely involved, get written confirmation from Maldivian authorities directly.
    - heading: "What the cat file needs"
      body: |
        Microchip, then rabies vaccination in the window Maldivian guidance specifies, plus the core feline vaccines. A certificate signed within 10 days of entry closes it out. Whether a general import permit or titre test applies to a cat travelling with its owner is genuinely unsettled between sources, so treat both as open questions, not assumptions.
    - heading: "If the charter season doesn't line up"

        Turkish Airlines via Istanbul is the most consistent scheduled alternative, with frequent onward connections. Qatar Airways via Doha is the other established option, though summer heat embargoes on live cargo can affect scheduling between roughly May and September.
