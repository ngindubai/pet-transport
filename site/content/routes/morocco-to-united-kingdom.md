  h1: "Pet Transport from Morocco to the United Kingdom: 2026 Guide"
  overview: "Morocco is classified as an unlisted country for UK pet travel. That means the process is significantly longer than for pets coming from EU or listed countries. The 90-day wait after a successful rabies titre test is the defining constraint: combined with the vaccination and titre test timelines, you need to start preparations at least five to six months before your planned travel date. Get this wrong and you cannot shorten the wait. Get it right and your pet arrives in the UK without quarantine."

  sections:
    - heading: "Why Morocco is unlisted and what that means for your pet"
      body: |
        The UK's pet travel scheme classifies countries as Part 1 listed (straightforward process), Part 2 listed (more requirements), or unlisted. Morocco falls into the unlisted category, which triggers the rabies titre test requirement and the mandatory 90-day waiting period that follows.

        The logic is straightforward: unlisted countries have not met the criteria the UK uses to determine that dog rabies is controlled to an acceptable level. Morocco does have rabies in its wildlife and dog population, which is why it sits in this category.

        For your pet, this means a longer preparation timeline but not an impossible one. Thousands of pets travel from unlisted countries to the UK every year. You simply need to start earlier and plan more carefully.

    - heading: "The sequence that cannot be rushed"
      body: |
        Step one is always the microchip. It must go in before the vaccination. If a vet vaccinates your dog and then chips it afterwards, the vaccination does not count under UK rules. This is one of those requirements where the order genuinely matters.

        Once the chip is in and the vaccination is administered, you must wait at least 30 days before the titre test blood is drawn. The blood goes to an APHA-approved laboratory (your vet in Morocco should know which labs are accepted, or you can check the APHA website). The laboratory processes the sample and issues a result. If the result is 0.5 IU/ml or above, the clock starts: 90 days from that date before your pet can enter Great Britain.

        Nothing speeds up the 90-day window. You cannot pay for a faster approval or use a different lab to bypass it. The process is what it is. Start it early.

    - heading: "Airlines and approved routes from Morocco to the UK"
      body: |
        Royal Air Maroc and British Airways both operate direct services from Casablanca (CMN) to London Heathrow (LHR). Both have cargo programmes that carry pets, though availability varies by season and breed.

        One critical point: not every route is an approved route for pet entry into Great Britain. The UK specifies which departure airports, carriers, and arrival ports are approved. Heathrow is the main approved UK airport for pet arrivals from most countries. Check GOV.UK approved routes before booking.

        Summer heat in Morocco (June to September) can affect cargo pet acceptance on morning departures from Casablanca. This is worth monitoring, but Casablanca's Atlantic coast climate is typically more moderate than Marrakech or inland cities.

faqs:
  - question: "How long does it take to move a pet from Morocco to the UK?"
    answer: "Allow a minimum of 5 to 6 months from starting the microchip and vaccination process to travel day. The mandatory steps are: microchip, vaccination (21-day wait in EU terms), 30-day wait before titre test blood draw, titre test result, then 90 days. That takes you past the 5-month mark under the best conditions. Add time for paperwork and booking."
  - question: "What is the FAVN titre test and where do I get it done in Morocco?"
    answer: "The FAVN (Fluorescent Antibody Virus Neutralisation) test is a blood test that measures your pet's level of rabies antibodies. It confirms the vaccination has been effective. In Morocco, your vet draws the blood and sends it to an APHA-approved laboratory, which may be in France or another European country with an approved facility. Confirm which laboratory your vet uses and verify it is on the APHA approved list before proceeding."
  - question: "Can my pet face quarantine when it arrives in the UK from Morocco?"
    answer: "There is no routine quarantine for pets arriving with correct documentation from unlisted countries. Quarantine is a penalty for arriving without proper paperwork, or with documentation that does not comply. If your microchip, vaccination, titre test, AHC, and tapeworm treatment are all correct and in sequence, your pet should clear without quarantine."
  - question: "Does my cat need a titre test too?"
    answer: "Yes. The titre test requirement for unlisted countries applies to cats as well as dogs. The sequence (microchip before vaccination, 30-day wait before blood draw, 90-day wait after successful result) is the same for cats. The tapeworm treatment, however, is only required for dogs."
  - question: "What is an AHC and who issues it in Morocco?"
    answer: "An Animal Health Certificate (AHC) is a standardised government-issued document confirming your pet is healthy and meets the destination country's requirements. In Morocco, it is issued by an ONSSA-authorised official veterinarian and must match the UK APHA format. It is valid for 10 days from the date of issue, so you get it shortly before you fly."
