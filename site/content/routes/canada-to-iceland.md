content:
  h1: "Pet Transport from Canada to Iceland"
  overview: |
    Canada has rabies in its wildlife. Raccoons, skunks, foxes and bats carry it in pockets across the country, and that single fact is why MAST places Canada in Category 2 rather than the more favourable Category 1 tier reserved for places like Australia or most of the EU. The practical result is a 90-day wait, counted from the date of the titre test blood draw, before a pet can travel.

    The one genuine convenience on this route is the flight. Icelandair operates Toronto Pearson to Keflavik nonstop, a rarity among Iceland's international pairings on this site. It does not shorten the 90-day wait or the 14-day quarantine that follows landing, but it does mean the journey itself is a single sector rather than a chain of connections.

  sections:
    - heading: "Category 2 and the 90-day clock"
      body: |
        MAST sorts import origins by rabies risk, and Canada sits in Category 2 alongside the USA, Turkey, Poland and several other countries where rabies persists in wildlife populations even under good veterinary control. For a Category 2 origin, the titre test blood draw happens at least 30 days after vaccination as usual, but the result clearing at 0.5 IU/ml or above does not end the process. A further 90 days must pass from the blood draw date itself before the animal is eligible to travel.

        This catches out owners more often than any other step on the route. The 90 days counts from when the blood was drawn, not from when the lab confirms the result, and not from the vaccination date. Get this date wrong and the whole schedule slides by weeks, sometimes past a booked flight.

    - heading: "A direct flight, which does not mean a simple one"
      body: |
        Most of Iceland's long-haul pairings on this site, to Singapore, to countries further afield, involve at least one hub connection. Canada is an exception: Icelandair flies Toronto Pearson to Keflavik nonstop, close to daily depending on season, a flight of roughly 5 hours 45 minutes. Air Canada operates the same sector on some schedules as a joint service.

        That single-leg convenience doesn't extend to how the pet actually travels. Icelandair stopped accepting pets as checked or excess baggage across its network on 1 November 2024, so even this direct flight moves the animal as manifested cargo through Icelandair Cargo, with its own booking process and lead times separate from a standard passenger fare.

    - heading: "Quarantine, whatever the flight looks like"
      body: |
        Every animal entering Iceland, Category 2 origin or not, spends a minimum of 14 days at one of two approved isolation stations, Einangrunarstoedin i Reykjanesbae or Einangrunarstoedin Mosel. Neither the direct flight nor Canada's own generally straightforward export process changes this. Book a station slot as soon as a travel window is realistic, since both facilities take pets on a limited intake schedule rather than on demand.
