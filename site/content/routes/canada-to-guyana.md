content:
  h1: "Pet Transport from Canada to Guyana"
  overview: |
    Look up which countries GLDA treats as rabies-free for its own import purposes and you'll find Barbados, Jamaica, the UK, Ireland, New Zealand and Australia among others. Canada isn't there. That's a genuine, checkable gap, and it means a Canadian-origin pet can be reviewed the same way GLDA reviews an unlisted-country pet, titre test and quarantine assessment included, rather than getting an automatic pass.

    None of that shows up as a fixed rule published in advance. GLDA decides at the point of import permit application, weighing the exporting country's own classification, which is exactly why this route carries a wider planning window than the flight itself would suggest. The flight, at least, is now simple: Caribbean Airlines flies Toronto to Georgetown nonstop every day.

  sections:
    - heading: "The list Canada isn't on"
      body: |
        GLDA publishes a specific set of countries it recognises as rabies-free for its own import decisions: Antigua and Barbuda, Australia, Barbados, Dominica, the Republic of Ireland, Jamaica, Montserrat, New Zealand, St Kitts and Nevis, St Lucia, St Vincent and the Grenadines, and the United Kingdom. Canada doesn't appear.

        That's not a reflection of Canada's actual rabies status. Domestic dog rabies has been eliminated in Canada for decades, and CFIA doesn't treat Canada as any kind of risk on its own outbound side. It's simply that GLDA's list is GLDA's own tool, built for GLDA's own import decisions, and Canada isn't currently on it. Whether that means an automatic titre test, a discretionary one, or nothing at all in practice isn't published clearly enough to state as settled fact here.

    - heading: "Why the permit application is the moment that matters"
      body: |
        GLDA's own guidance describes the classification decision, and any resulting titre test or quarantine period of up to 90 days, as something settled when the import permit application is reviewed, not fixed in advance. That puts real weight on getting GLDA's answer early, ideally before committing to a flight date, rather than assuming the lightest possible outcome and adjusting later.

        Ask directly, in writing, whether a Canadian-origin pet will need a titre test on this specific application. Keep the answer on file. It's the single most useful document in this entire process.

    - heading: "A simple flight, an uncertain runway"
      body: |
        Caribbean Airlines' new daily Toronto to Georgetown nonstop, running since 1 July 2026 and operated jointly with Air Transat, removes the flight itself as a source of complexity. What's left is entirely regulatory, and entirely dependent on GLDA's own classification call. Budget the widest end of the timeline range until that call comes back, and treat anything tighter as optimistic rather than planned.
