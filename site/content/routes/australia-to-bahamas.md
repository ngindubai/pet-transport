content:
  h1: "Pet Transport from Australia to Bahamas"
  overview: |
    No rabies vaccine. That's the headline for a pet leaving Australia for the Bahamas, and it isn't a loophole: BAHFSA's own published conditions treat Australia as a WOAH-recognised rabies-free country, which removes the rabies vaccination requirement entirely, replacing it with proof the pet has lived in Australia continuously for the six months before travel.

    Compare that with the UK or the US, neither of which WOAH classes as rabies-free, and both of which need a rabies vaccine given within a 10-month or 34-month window depending on the product. Australia's pets skip that step. What still applies is an ePermit through BAHFSA's Click2Clear system, a set of core vaccinations, and a negative internal parasite test.

    Getting there needs a change of carrier somewhere along the way. No single airline covers Sydney or Perth to Nassau, so plan the cargo transfer as one movement, not two separate bookings.

  sections:
    - heading: "Why the rabies vaccine disappears from this route"
      body: |
        BAHFSA splits its import rules by the exporting country's own WOAH rabies classification, not by a blanket policy. Countries WOAH lists as rabies-free get a residency-based certificate instead of a vaccine timing rule. Australia, having never recorded a case of terrestrial rabies, sits in that group.

        The trade-off is documentation, not medicine. The international veterinary certificate has to attest the pet was continuously present in Australia for six months before the flight, or since birth if younger. A pet that recently arrived in Australia from elsewhere doesn't automatically qualify, and that residency history is worth confirming with BAHFSA well before assuming the lighter pathway applies.

    - heading: "What still has to happen"
      body: |
        Core vaccinations don't disappear along with the rabies shot. Dogs still need current cover for distemper, hepatitis, leptospirosis, parvovirus and adenovirus; cats need feline leukaemia, rhinotracheitis, calicivirus, panleukopenia and pneumonitis. A negative internal parasite result, using the modified McMaster flotation technique specifically, also has to sit on the certificate.

        The ePermit process runs through Click2Clear, the same online system every BAHFSA import uses regardless of origin. Register early; the account setup step alone can take a few working days before the actual permit application even starts.

    - heading: "No direct route, so plan the handover"
      body: |
        Qantas's Sydney to Los Angeles service connects onward to American Airlines' Miami hub, where American PetEmbark handles the final Nassau leg. Owners closer to Perth have a genuine alternative: Qantas's codeshare with British Airways over London, landing on BA's nonstop Heathrow to Nassau flight.

        Either way, a pet changes carriers at least once. Book it as a single interline cargo movement with an agent who can manage the handover, rather than two flights bought separately and hoping the connection holds.
