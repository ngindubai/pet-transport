  overview: "Helsinki-Vantaa is one of a limited number of Finnish entry points approved for animals arriving from outside the EU, and that detail matters more than most owners expect when routing a pet from Beirut. Beyond the entry point restriction, Finland applies the same rules as any EU member state to an unlisted country like Lebanon: microchip, rabies vaccination, a titre test, and a 90-day wait from the blood draw.   sections:
    - heading: "Why the entry point matters"
      body: |
        Finland does not accept pets arriving from outside the EU at just any airport. The Finnish Food Authority, Ruokavirasto, restricts non-EU arrivals to approved border inspection posts, and Helsinki-Vantaa is one of them. This is worth confirming explicitly when booking, particularly if a routing involves a Finnish domestic connection after the international leg, since a pet cleared through customs at HEL should not need a second inspection stop elsewhere in the country.

        It's a detail most owners moving from EU-listed countries never encounter, because listed-country pets face lighter checks generally. Coming from Lebanon, an unlisted country, the full inspection applies, and Ruokavirasto officials will check the endorsed EU-format certificate, the titre test result and the vaccination record against the microchip on arrival at HEL.

    - heading: "The titre test and 90-day sequence"
      body: |
        The order is fixed: microchip, then rabies vaccination, then a 30-day gap before blood is drawn for the titre test at an EU-approved laboratory. A result of 0.5 IU/ml or higher starts a 90-day countdown that runs from the draw date, never the day the result is confirmed.

        Only once those 90 days pass can the pet legally enter Finland. The EU-format health certificate that finalises the paperwork is valid for just 10 days, so it needs issuing close to the actual travel date rather than early in the process.

    - heading: "Routing from Beirut and the Lebanese export side"
      body: |
        Lufthansa Cargo via Frankfurt is the steadiest routing into Helsinki given Lufthansa's Northern European network and Frankfurt's Animal Lounge for the layover. Turkish Airlines Cargo via Istanbul is a close alternative worth quoting in parallel. Qatar Airways via Doha covers busier periods but adds transit hours and occasional summer heat restrictions on live animal cargo.

        Before any of that, Lebanon's Ministry of Agriculture has to issue and endorse the export permit and health certificate, reformatted to EU standard. Budget 2 to 4 weeks for that endorsement, timed to land near the end of the 90-day wait so the 10-day certificate validity lines up with the actual flight.
