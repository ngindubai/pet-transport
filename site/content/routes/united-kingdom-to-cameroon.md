  h1: "Pet Transport from United Kingdom to Cameroon"
  overview: |
    Savannah and Bengal cats need five generations of separation from their wild ancestry before Cameroon's authorities will let them in. It's one of the more unusual checks on this route, and it sits well ahead of anything to do with rabies paperwork in terms of how easily it catches an owner out.

    The rest of the process is more familiar. No titre test applies to a pet leaving the UK, since Cameroon doesn't ask for one from British-vaccinated animals. What it does ask for is a rabies vaccination given within 30 days of arrival, and, if you're relying on a multi-year vaccine, one given within the last 12 months regardless of how long the product is licensed to protect for.

    Getting there means a transit. Douala has no direct connection from London, so Istanbul, Addis Ababa, Brussels or Paris all serve as the practical route in.

  sections:
    - heading: "The vaccine timing rule that trips up multi-year boosters"
      body: |
        Most UK vets now offer 3-year rabies vaccines as standard, and most owners assume that certificate stays valid for however long the product label says. Cameroon doesn't work that way for import purposes. The vaccination has to fall within 30 days of entry and, where a multi-year product was used, within the 12 months before travel.

        In practice, that means checking the date on your pet's last rabies jab against your travel plans early, not assuming last year's paperwork will do. If it won't, a booster is a quick fix, but it still needs the full 30-day wait to clear before the flight.

    - heading: "Breed and hybrid restrictions worth checking before you book"
      body: |
        Wolf hybrid dogs, and Savannah or Bengal cats, are excluded from import unless the pedigree shows five clear generations removed from that ancestry. This isn't something your vet's health certificate can confirm; it needs breed registry paperwork, and it's worth resolving before you spend money on flights or a crate.

        Most household cats and dogs won't run into this at all. It matters specifically for owners of these particular breeds, and it's worth a five-minute check with the breeder or registry rather than an assumption either way.

    - heading: "Four hubs, no direct flight"
      body: |
        Douala International Airport takes the bulk of Cameroon's cargo traffic and is the more practical arrival point for a pet shipment over Yaounde. Getting there from Heathrow means choosing a connection: Turkish Airlines via Istanbul, Ethiopian Airlines via Addis Ababa, Brussels Airlines via Brussels, or Air France via Paris.

        Turkish Cargo's Pet Lounge in Istanbul is a genuine advantage if your connection runs long, giving animals a proper rest point mid-journey rather than sitting crated on the tarmac. Whichever carrier you pick, confirm live animal acceptance and the connection time before booking, since a short layover that looks efficient on paper can mean a rushed transfer for a crated animal.

faqs:
  - question: "Does a pet need a rabies titre test to travel from the UK to Cameroon?"
    answer: "No. Cameroon does not require a rabies antibody titre test from a pet arriving with a UK-issued Export Health Certificate and a current rabies vaccination. The vaccination itself has to be given at least 30 days before entry."
  - question: "Can I take a Bengal or Savannah cat to Cameroon?"
    answer: "Only if the cat's pedigree shows five generations removed from its wild ancestry. Cameroon restricts import of these breeds, along with wolf hybrid dogs, without that documented generation gap. Confirm with the breeder or registry before booking any travel."
  - question: "My pet has a 3-year rabies vaccine. Does that satisfy Cameroon's rules?"
    answer: "Only if it was administered within the last 12 months. Cameroon does not recognise a multi-year vaccine's full licensed duration for import purposes; it has to have been given within the 12 months before travel, in addition to the standard 30-day pre-entry wait."
  - question: "Is there a direct flight from the UK to Cameroon for pet cargo?"
    answer: "No. There is no direct passenger or cargo routing between London and Douala or Yaounde. Turkish Airlines (via Istanbul), Ethiopian Airlines (via Addis Ababa), Brussels Airlines (via Brussels) and Air France (via Paris) all offer connecting options that accept pets as cargo."
  - question: "Will my pet be quarantined on arrival in Cameroon?"
    answer: "No systematic quarantine applies to a dog or cat that meets the health requirements and shows no sign of contagious disease on arrival. Officials still check the certificate, vaccination record and microchip against the animal at the airport."
