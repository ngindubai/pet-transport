content:
  h1: "Pet Transport from Seychelles to Singapore: 2026 Guide"
  overview: |
    Mahe has no scheduled service to Singapore, direct or otherwise, so every pet on this route starts its journey with a connection through the Gulf. That single geographic fact does as much to shape this move as any regulation.

    On the regulatory side, Seychelles' genuine rabies-free record buys nothing with Singapore's own import authority. AVS names only four countries in its top tier and around 31 in the tier below that, and Seychelles sits in neither, so it defaults to Schedule III: a titre test, a 90-day wait, and 30 days at the AQC on arrival. Realistically, plan for four and a half to five months.

  sections:
    - heading: "Rabies-free at home, Schedule III on arrival"
      body: |
        Seychelles has never recorded rabies and runs its own strict controls to keep it that way. None of that changes how Singapore's AVS classifies an incoming pet. AVS's Schedule I covers only Australia, New Zealand, Ireland and the UK; Schedule II covers a longer but still specific list, including most of the EU, the USA and Canada. Seychelles appears on neither, so it defaults to Schedule III, the tier AVS applies to any country not named on the shorter two lists.

        That default means a WOAH-prescribed titre test, blood drawn at least 28 days after vaccination, at a laboratory the WOAH or a Schedule I or II country recognises, since Seychelles keeps none of its own on that particular list. A 90-day wait runs from the blood draw date to the earliest possible travel date, and rechecking this against the lab result date rather than the sampling date is the single most common planning mistake on routes like this one.

    - heading: "No non-stop option: routing through Abu Dhabi or Dubai"
      body: |
        Air Seychelles' own network carries small pets as accompanied checked baggage, useful only if the owner is flying on the same aircraft. For an unaccompanied relocation shipment, Etihad Cargo through Abu Dhabi and Emirates SkyCargo through Dubai are the two workable options, both running dedicated live-animal facilities at their respective hubs before the onward leg to Singapore Changi.

        Confirm cargo space on both legs of whichever routing you choose. A confirmed Mahe-to-Gulf booking does not guarantee the connecting Gulf-to-Singapore leg has space on the same dates, and the two are usually booked separately.

    - heading: "Timing the paperwork against Singapore's tight certificate window"
      body: |
        AVS expects the health certificate completed between 2 and 7 days of the export date, a narrower window than many owners planning their first international pet move expect. Book the vet appointment against a confirmed flight date, not the other way round, since a certificate signed too early falls outside that window and has to be redone closer to travel.
