  h1: "Pet Transport from Poland to Armenia"
  overview: |
    Moving a pet from Poland to Armenia is a straightforward process by Caucasus standards. Armenia does not require a titre test from EU-origin animals, and there is no quarantine period on arrival. The route runs via Istanbul on Turkish Airlines, making Warsaw Chopin Airport (WAW) your natural starting point regardless of which Polish city you are departing from.

    Poland's EU membership works in your favour here. The GIW (Glowny Inspektorat Weterynarii) health certificate system, operating through district PIW offices across the country, produces the official documentation that Armenian SSFS officers expect to see at Zvartnots Airport. The format is recognised, and the process is well-worn for EU pet owners relocating to Armenia.

    The main discipline on this route is timing. Your rabies vaccine must be both recent enough (no more than 11 months old) and established enough (at least 21 days before entry). Book your district vet appointment at the PIW office carefully, as the health certificate is only valid for 10 days from the examination date.

  sections:
    - heading: "Getting Your Polish Export Paperwork Right"
      body: |
        Poland processes pet exports through a two-tier system. Your private vet handles vaccinations and maintains the EU pet passport. But the official health certificate, the document that clears your pet to cross the border, comes from the district official vet: the Powiatowy Lekarz Weterynarii at your local PIW (Powiatowy Inspektorat Weterynarii) office.

        No export permit is required for personal pets leaving Poland. What you do need is an official health certificate issued and endorsed at district level. The Powiatowy Lekarz Weterynarii will examine your pet, read the microchip, and verify vaccination records against the EU pet passport before signing off. GIW (the national Chief Veterinary Inspectorate at wetgiw.gov.pl) oversees the process, but the actual certificate is issued at PIW level.

        The certificate is valid for 10 days from the examination date. Build your travel booking around this window, not the other way around. If your departure date slips, you may need a fresh examination and a new certificate.

    - heading: "What Armenia Requires on Arrival"
      body: |
        Armenian import rules for pets from EU countries are published by the SSFS (State Service for Food Safety) at ssfs.am. The requirements are manageable: ISO 11784/11785 microchip, valid rabies vaccination (21 days to 11 months before arrival), and an official health certificate issued within 10 days. No titre test is required from Poland. No import permit is needed for 1-2 personal pets.

        Inspection takes place at Zvartnots International Airport (EVN) in Yerevan. SSFS officers check the health certificate, verify the microchip against the documentation, and confirm vaccination dates. If everything aligns, your pet clears without delay.

        Your pet must be at least 4 months old to enter Armenia. This is worth noting if you are moving with a young puppy or kitten, as the rule applies regardless of vaccination status.

    - heading: "Flying Poland to Armenia: The Istanbul Connection"
      body: |
        There is no direct commercial flight for live animal cargo between Poland and Armenia. The standard routing is Warsaw Chopin (WAW) to Istanbul (IST), then IST to Zvartnots (EVN). Turkish Airlines is the primary carrier for this combination, with established live animal cargo handling at both Istanbul and Yerevan airports.

        LOT Polish Airlines, Warsaw's national carrier, also operates cargo services from WAW and can work in combination with Turkish Airlines on this routing. Confirm live animal acceptance on the specific sectors you are booking, as interline cargo arrangements vary.

        All pets travel as cargo on international routes, in IATA-compliant crates. The crate must allow the animal to stand, turn, and lie down naturally. Turkish Airlines publishes crate dimension limits in its live animals section; contact the cargo department directly for current availability and seasonal restrictions before committing to a travel date.
