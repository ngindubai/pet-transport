  h1: "Pet Transport from Lesotho to Canada"
  overview: |
    One flight decides the shape of every pet move out of Lesotho, and it isn't the long-haul leg. Moshoeshoe I International Airport in Maseru has exactly one scheduled international connection, to Johannesburg, flown solely by Airlink. Everything else, whichever airline eventually carries the pet to Canada, builds on top of that fixed first hop.

    Lesotho also sits on CFIA's high-risk-for-dog-rabies list, updated 13 March 2025. That sounds more alarming than it turns out to be for an ordinary family pet: the listing's real force falls on commercial dog imports, banned outright since 2022, not on an owner travelling with their own dog or cat.

    From Johannesburg, Ethiopian Airlines' nonstop service into Toronto, the only direct link between continental Africa and Canada, picks up where Airlink leaves off.

  sections:
    - heading: "Johannesburg first, always"
      body: |
        There's no negotiating this part. Airlink is the sole scheduled carrier flying out of Maseru, roughly 21 times a week on the 65-minute hop to O.R. Tambo. Every long-haul option, Ethiopian to Toronto or British Airways via Heathrow, starts from that same regional airport, on a separate air waybill from whatever comes next.

        Book the Airlink sector at least 48 hours ahead. It's a smaller regional operation with its own booking rhythm, entirely separate from the long-haul arrival it feeds into.

    - heading: "What the high-risk listing actually changes"
      body: |
        CFIA's high-risk classification, current as of 13 March 2025, groups Lesotho with several dozen other countries. It's tempting to read that as a strict entry regime, and for commercial dog imports it is: those have been barred from high-risk countries since 28 September 2022. A personal pet, one or two animals travelling with the named owner, follows the lighter standard process instead. Vaccination, certificate, no titre test, no import permit.

        The one detail worth checking is CFIA's 8-month age presumption. A younger dog not accompanied by its named owner risks being treated as commercial, a serious outcome from a listed country. Confirm directly with CFIA if that description is anywhere close to your situation.

    - heading: "Ethiopian's Toronto link makes the second half easy"
      body: |
        Ethiopian Airlines runs up to three flights a day between Johannesburg and Addis Ababa, feeding its own nonstop Addis Ababa-Toronto service, genuinely the only direct passenger route between continental Africa and Canada. That keeps the pet under one carrier's handling for the two longest legs of the journey, with only the initial Airlink hop and the O.R. Tambo transfer sitting outside that chain.

    answer: "Yes. Lesotho appears on CFIA's list of countries at high-risk for dog rabies, last updated 13 March 2025. For a personal pet this doesn't trigger a titre test; the listing's practical effect is a ban on commercial dog imports, in force since September 2022."

  - question: "Does my dog need a rabies titre test to enter Canada from Lesotho?"
    answer: "No. CFIA doesn't require a rabies antibody titre test for a personal pet dog, even one arriving from a high-risk country. A current vaccination, recorded in English or French, is what's checked at the border."
  - question: "Why does Lesotho's high-risk status matter if my pet doesn't need a titre test?"
    answer: "It matters most for CFIA's commercial-versus-personal distinction. A dog under 8 months old travelling without its named owner is presumed commercial, and commercial dogs from high-risk countries are barred outright. Confirm your pet's status with CFIA directly if age or accompaniment could put this in question."
