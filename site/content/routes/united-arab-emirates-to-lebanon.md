  h1: "Pet Transport United Arab Emirates to Lebanon"
  overview: |
    A pet leaving Dubai for Beirut has the easier end of this corridor. Every animal exiting the UAE needs a MOCCAE export certificate, full stop, regardless of destination, so that part of the process is identical whichever way this route runs. What changes is the Lebanese side: a straightforward import permit from the Ministry of Agriculture and a health certificate check on arrival at Beirut-Rafic Hariri International, with no titre test and no quarantine for a pet travelling with the right paperwork.

    The flight itself is the standout feature. Emirates SkyCargo and FlyDubai both operate non-stop Dubai-Beirut cargo services, and Middle East Airlines flies the same route into its home base. Three direct options on one corridor is rare in this build; most routes involve at least one connection.

  sections:
    - heading: "What MOCCAE requires before your pet can leave"
      body: |
        Every pet leaving the UAE, no matter where it's going, needs a MOCCAE export health certificate. The process starts online through the MOCCAE portal, which requires a UAE Pass login, followed by an in-person appointment at an approved clinic in Dubai or Abu Dhabi. The vet there checks vaccination records and the pet's general health before endorsing the certificate. A second, final inspection happens at the airport itself, right before the flight.

        That certificate is only valid for 30 days once issued. Book your cargo space first, then time the MOCCAE application against that date. Doing it the other way round risks a certificate that expires before the flight actually happens.

    - heading: "Lebanon's import side: permit first, arrival second"
      body: |
        Lebanon asks for an import permit from the Directorate General of Agriculture before your pet ever boards a plane. There's no titre test requirement and no quarantine for pets arriving with a valid MOCCAE certificate and current rabies vaccination, which keeps this route considerably simpler than the reverse direction into some EU destinations.

        On arrival at Beirut-Rafic Hariri International, present the MOCCAE-endorsed certificate along with the import permit. Beirut's import process is less bureaucratic than several other capitals in this build, and most pets clear within a few hours of landing.

    - heading: "Three direct flights, one corridor"
      body: |
        Emirates SkyCargo and FlyDubai both fly Dubai to Beirut non-stop, and Middle East Airlines, Lebanon's flag carrier, covers the same route into its own hub. That gives owners a genuine choice of direct cargo options rather than the single-connection routings typical elsewhere in this build.

        The one seasonal factor worth planning around: Gulf summer heat embargoes on live animal cargo, which can affect scheduling out of both DXB and AUH between roughly June and September. Outside that window, booking tends to be more flexible.
