  sections:
    - heading: "Portugal's entry requirements for EU pets"
      body: |
        Portugal's DGAV (General Directorate of Food and Veterinary) administers companion animal imports. For personal pets from EU member states like Latvia, the requirements are exactly as the EU framework specifies: EU pet passport, valid microchip (implanted before vaccination), and a current rabies vaccination given at least 21 days before arrival.

        No additional documentation is needed. Portugal does not require tapeworm treatment, a titre test, or any country-specific health certificate for EU pets. A DGAV veterinary check at the arrival airport is brief and routine for pets with complete documentation.

    - heading: "Riga to Portugal: the routing picture"
      body: |
        Riga International Airport (RIX) is a busy regional hub in the Baltic states. Air Baltic, the Latvian carrier, connects Riga to multiple European hubs including Amsterdam, Frankfurt, Helsinki, and others, from which TAP Air Portugal and other carriers offer onward service to Lisbon and Porto.

        For small pets in the cabin, Ryanair operates seasonal routes from Riga to Portuguese airports (Lisbon, Porto, Faro). For larger pets requiring cargo, Air Baltic connections via Amsterdam or Frankfurt, then TAP cargo to Lisbon, are the most practical arrangements.

  - question: "What documentation does Portuguese customs need when I arrive?"
    answer: "For a personal companion animal from an EU member state, Portuguese customs and the DGAV require the EU pet passport. The officer verifies the microchip number (read by scanner), checks the rabies vaccination is current and at least 21 days old, and confirms the passport is valid. Bring the original passport and keep it accessible during the arrival process."
  - question: "Does my Latvian dog need a health certificate as well as the EU passport for Portugal?"
    answer: "No. For intra-EU movement, the EU pet passport replaces the health certificate. No separate IATA health certificate, export endorsement, or veterinary statement is required for a Latvian pet entering Portugal. The EU passport is the complete travel document."
