  h1: "Pet Transport from the United Kingdom to Serbia"
  overview: |
    This is one of the more forgiving non-EU routes out of the UK on paper. Serbia treats the UK as a lower-risk source country, so there is no titre test to arrange and no three-month wait to plan around, just a 21-day gap after a first rabies vaccination and a correctly completed export certificate. Most owners can be ready inside two months.

    The catch sits with the flight, not the paperwork. Air Serbia runs the only direct London-Belgrade service, out of Heathrow, close to daily. It also refuses to carry pets on any UK route, cabin or hold, without exception. Anyone assuming the direct flight will work needs to replan around Vienna with Austrian Airlines or Frankfurt or Munich with Lufthansa Cargo instead, both of which add a transfer and a bit more lead time to the booking.

  sections:
    - heading: "Certificate 3567 and the UK export process"
      body: |
        GOV.UK publishes a dedicated form for this route: Export Health Certificate 3567, currently on version 4, for cats and dogs travelling from Great Britain to Serbia. You apply through the online export health certificate service, which needs a Government Gateway and Defra account for owners in England, Scotland or Wales; Northern Ireland applies separately through DAERA's upload portal. Your Official Veterinarian has to be registered with the service and verified by APHA before they can sign the form, so check this early rather than the week before travel.

        The certificate needs a current microchip, implanted before or alongside the rabies vaccination, and proof that at least 21 days have passed since a first vaccination (a maintained booster does not need to restart this wait). The OV completes the clinical exam and signs the certificate close to your travel date, since the details on it need to still be accurate on arrival day. There is no general export licence required for a non-commercial move of one or two pets, only the certificate itself.

    - heading: "Serbia's import rules and why Air Serbia is not an option"
      body: |
        Serbia's Veterinary Directorate, part of the Ministry of Agriculture, Forestry and Water Management, treats pets arriving from the UK under its lower-risk import pathway. No titre test applies. A microchip, a valid rabies vaccination at least 21 days old, and the endorsed health certificate are the whole of it, with the certificate remaining valid for four months of onward travel within Serbia as long as the vaccination it records stays current. There is no quarantine for a pet that arrives correctly documented.

        Getting there is the harder half. Air Serbia flies Heathrow to Belgrade directly, roughly ten times a week, and would be the obvious booking if the airline carried pets on it. It does not, on any UK route, in any class of travel. The workable alternatives both run through a European hub: Austrian Airlines via Vienna, a short onward hop to Belgrade well suited to a smaller pet travelling with its owner, or Lufthansa Cargo via Frankfurt or Munich for a fully unaccompanied cargo shipment. Confirm cargo space directly with whichever carrier's cargo division you use, since it is booked separately from a passenger seat and fills up faster in summer.
