  h1: "Pet Transport from United Kingdom to Mozambique"
  overview: |
    Five months of titre testing and a fixed 90-day wait apply to a pet going the other way on this corridor. None of that applies here. Mozambique doesn't ask for a rabies antibody test from a UK-origin pet at all, a detail easy to miss if you've read the reverse route first.

    What Mozambique does ask for is a DINAV import permit, its own document with no published processing time, plus proof the rabies vaccination is at least 30 days old and isn't a multi-year product. Neither of those steps exists on the export side heading toward Britain.

    The flights are the same three carriers either way. Ethiopian Airlines, TAP Air Portugal and Qatar Airways each connect Heathrow to Maputo through Addis Ababa, Lisbon or Doha, all with workable hold or cargo pet handling.

  sections:
    - heading: "Why this direction skips the titre test"
      body: |
        The UK's own rules for pets arriving from Mozambique are demanding: unlisted country status, a FAVN or ELISA blood test, and a 90-day wait after a passing result. None of that machinery exists on the Mozambican side for a pet arriving from the UK.

        Mozambique's published requirements ask for a microchip, a rabies vaccination at least 30 days old, and the standard set of core vaccines. A titre test is recommended, not required, mainly to smooth over a customs query rather than to satisfy a legal minimum. Multi-year rabies vaccine products are the one vaccination detail that trips owners up: Mozambique's paperwork expects an annual-labelled dose, so a pet vaccinated on a 3-year product may need a fresh jab before travel.

        That leaves the DINAV import permit as the genuine long-lead item, not a blood test.

    - heading: "DINAV's import permit: the step the reverse route doesn't have"
      body: |
        DINAV, the Direcção Nacional de Veterinária under Mozambique's Ministry of Agriculture, issues export clearance for pets leaving Mozambique and also controls the import permit for pets entering it. That permit is compulsory for a UK-origin dog or cat, and Mozambique doesn't publish a fixed processing time for it.

        This is a real asymmetry worth flagging: a Mozambique-origin pet needs no formal import permit to enter the UK, since the endorsed export certificate does that job. A UK-origin pet needs the DINAV permit specifically, applied for well before the flight, usually through a Mozambique-based agent or the Mozambican High Commission in London.

        Get the permit application moving as early as the vaccination schedule allows. Waiting until the flight is booked is the most common way this step runs late.

    - heading: "Flying into Maputo: the same three carriers, opposite direction"
      body: |
        Ethiopian Airlines' Heathrow to Addis Ababa daily service connects to a daily Addis to Maputo sector, keeping the pet within one airline's network for most of the journey. TAP Air Portugal flies London to Lisbon, then a daily nonstop of roughly 11 hours down to Maputo. Qatar Airways is the only Gulf option with a direct Doha to Maputo connection, and its Live Animals Centre gives a longer transit animal a proper walk.

        All three carry pets in the cabin under 8kg and in the hold or as cargo above that, with weight limits varying by carrier and route. Book the flight only once the DINAV permit application is confirmed in progress, since the certificate and permit both need to line up with the travel date.
