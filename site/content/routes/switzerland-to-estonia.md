content:
  h1: "Pet Transport Switzerland to Estonia: 2026 Guide"
  overview: "Switzerland has bilateral agreements with the EU that make its pet passport equivalent to the EU pet passport. This means travelling from Switzerland to Estonia is almost as simple as EU-to-EU movement. Your pet needs an ISO microchip implanted before the rabies vaccination, a current rabies vaccination (at least 21 days old), and a valid Swiss pet passport from your Swiss vet. No titre test, no quarantine, and no separate AHC is needed for Switzerland-to-EU travel. The main challenge is the routing, as there is no direct Zurich-Tallinn cargo pet service."

  sections:
    - heading: "The Swiss pet passport and EU equivalence"
      body: |
        Switzerland is not an EU member, but the bilateral agreements between Switzerland and the EU include recognition of the Swiss pet passport as equivalent to the EU pet passport. In practical terms, this means a Swiss pet passport holder can travel from Switzerland to Estonia (and other EU countries) without needing to obtain a separate Animal Health Certificate (AHC) for each trip.

        Your Swiss vet issues the Swiss pet passport. It records the microchip number, rabies vaccination history, and vet details, just as an EU pet passport does. The key rule applies equally to both: the microchip must be implanted before the rabies vaccination for the vaccination to count.

        This equivalence was confirmed by the BLV (Federal Food Safety and Veterinary Office) as current in 2026 following updates to EU pet travel legislation in April 2026.

    - heading: "Getting from Zurich to Tallinn with your pet"
      body: |
        Zurich (ZRH) does not have a direct cargo pet service to Tallinn (TLL). The most practical routing involves connecting through Riga on Air Baltic, or through Helsinki on Finnair. Both carriers accept live animal cargo on their connecting services.

        For small pets travelling in the cabin, connecting flights via Riga or Frankfurt are available from Zurich. Swiss International Air Lines, as part of the Lufthansa Group, can also coordinate cargo connections.

faqs:
  - question: "Does Switzerland have an EU pet passport?"
    answer: "Switzerland does not issue EU pet passports, because it is not an EU member. Instead, Swiss vets issue a Swiss pet passport that is equivalent to the EU pet passport under bilateral agreements between Switzerland and the EU. For travel to Estonia and other EU countries, the Swiss pet passport works in the same way as an EU passport and does not require a separate health certificate."
  - question: "Is a titre test needed for a Swiss pet entering Estonia?"
    answer: "No. Estonia does not require a titre test for pets from Switzerland. Switzerland is recognised as equivalent to EU-listed status for pet travel purposes. A current rabies vaccination, microchip, and Swiss pet passport are all that is needed."
  - question: "Does my dog need tapeworm treatment to enter Estonia?"
    answer: "No. The tapeworm treatment requirement (praziquantel administered by a vet 24-120 hours before arrival) applies to pets entering Ireland, Finland, Malta, and Norway. It does not apply to Estonia."
