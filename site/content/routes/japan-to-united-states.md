  sections:
    - heading: "The CDC Dog Import Form"
      body: |
        The CDC Dog Import Form is required for all dogs entering the United States, regardless of where they come from. It is completed online by the owner or their agent and is linked to the dog by microchip number. The form confirms the dog's vaccination status and travel history.

        Japan is not classified as a high-risk country for dog-related rabies, so dogs from Japan do not face the additional requirements (such as USDA-recognised facility certification) that apply to dogs from higher-risk origins. You will still need a USDA-endorsed health certificate from a Japanese official veterinarian, and a current US-licensed or equivalent rabies vaccination.

        Present the completed CDC form and the health certificate at the CDC inspection desk on arrival. Inspections are typically brief for well-documented pets from Japan.

    - heading: "Hawaii: a separate set of rules"
      body: |
        If your final destination is Hawaii, the process is significantly more complex. Hawaii maintains its own quarantine system separate from the federal CDC requirements, and all dogs and cats arriving in Hawaii must comply with the Hawaii Department of Agriculture (HDOA) rules, regardless of where they flew from.

        The HDOA 5-Day Or Less programme requires advance paperwork, titre tests, and specific timing. Pets that arrive without qualifying for the 5-Day programme may face up to 120 days of quarantine at the owner's expense. If Hawaii is your destination, research the HDOA requirements independently well in advance of your move.

faqs:
  - question: "Does my dog need to quarantine when entering the USA from Japan?"
    answer: "No, there is no routine quarantine for dogs entering the USA from Japan with correct documentation. You need a completed CDC Dog Import Form, a USDA-endorsed health certificate from a Japanese official vet, and a current rabies vaccination. CDC inspection at the arrival airport is a formality for well-documented pets from Japan. Hawaii is the exception: it has its own quarantine programme that applies to all incoming pets."
  - question: "Do cats need any paperwork to enter the USA from Japan?"
    answer: "There are no federal documentation requirements for cats entering the USA from Japan. Individual US states may impose their own rules, so check your destination state. Airlines have their own health certificate requirements, so confirm these with your carrier. Hawaii is again the exception and applies strict quarantine rules to cats as well as dogs."
  - question: "Can my pet fly in the cabin from Japan to the USA?"
    answer: "Japan Airlines and ANA do not allow pets in the passenger cabin on international flights. All pets travel as checked baggage in the hold. American Airlines and Delta accept small pets in the cabin on some international routes, but policies vary by route. Confirm directly with the airline at the time of booking for your specific Japan to USA flight."
  - question: "What is the CDC Dog Import Form?"
    answer: "The CDC Dog Import Form is an online registration that all dog owners must complete before bringing a dog into the United States from any country. It links the dog's microchip number to the travel record and confirms vaccination status. There is no fee. Complete it before travel; do not leave it until the day of travel. The form applies to dogs of all breeds and ages, including puppies."
