    - heading: "What Canada requires: the CFIA process"
      body: |
        Canada's federal pet import process is run by CFIA. For personal pets (1-2 animals, not for sale or breeding), the requirements are manageable.

        Dogs aged 3 months and older must be vaccinated against rabies. The health certificate from an AVS-authorised vet must be endorsed by AVS and include the pet's microchip number, vaccination records, and a declaration of good health.

        At the Canadian port of entry (Vancouver or Toronto for most SIN routes), CBSA inspects documentation. For compliant pets, the inspection is brief and your pet is released to you the same day.
faqs:
  - question: "Does my pet need a titre test to go from Singapore to Canada?"
    answer: "No. A titre test is not required for personal pet imports from Singapore to Canada. The main requirements are a current rabies vaccination for dogs aged 3 months and over, and a health certificate issued by an AVS-authorised vet and endorsed by AVS."
  - question: "Is there quarantine for pets entering Canada from Singapore?"
    answer: "No routine quarantine is required for compliant pets from Singapore arriving in Canada. CBSA inspects documents at the port of entry and releases your pet to you the same day if paperwork is correct."
  - question: "Which airline is best for Singapore to Canada pet transport?"
    answer: "Singapore Airlines is the strongest choice, with a direct SIN-Vancouver (YVR) service and a dedicated live animal facility at Changi. Air Canada and Cathay Pacific are alternatives, typically with one connection."
  - question: "What does AVS export endorsement mean?"
    answer: "All pets leaving Singapore require clearance from AVS (Animal and Veterinary Service). An AVS-authorised vet examines your pet and issues the health certificate. AVS endorses it with an official stamp. This is the Singapore equivalent of the UK's APHA endorsement or New Zealand's MPI endorsement. Without it, your pet cannot board a flight from Singapore."
