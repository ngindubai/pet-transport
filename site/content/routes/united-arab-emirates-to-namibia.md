  h1: "Pet Transport from the United Arab Emirates to Namibia"
  overview: |
    A MOCCAE export health certificate for a single pet leaving the UAE for personal reasons costs AED 100, one of the cheaper line items on this route. The bigger planning task is timing: Namibia wants a rabies vaccination that's neither older than 12 months nor younger than 30 days on the day of arrival, a narrower window than most destinations set.

    No titre test applies on either end of this route, which keeps it faster than the outbound Namibia-to-UAE trip. Realistically 6 to 10 weeks, mostly spent on permit processing and the Emirates or Ethiopian Airlines booking through Johannesburg or Addis Ababa.

  sections:
    - heading: "Namibia's vaccination window, and why it cuts both ways"
      body: |
        Namibia doesn't require a rabies titre test from the UAE, or from anywhere else. What its Directorate of Veterinary Services does insist on is a vaccination dated between 30 days and 12 months before the pet lands in Windhoek.

        That's an unusual pair of limits. Most destinations only worry about a vaccine going stale; Namibia also rejects one given too recently. A booster administered the week before departure can fail the same check as one given fourteen months earlier, so the timing needs planning as much as the paperwork does.

    - heading: "The UAE's export side is the quick part"
      body: |
        Every pet leaving the UAE, regardless of destination, needs a MOCCAE veterinary health certificate. It's inexpensive for a personal pet consignment of up to two animals: AED 100, against AED 400 for larger or commercial shipments.

        The certificate itself is valid 30 days from issue, but a government vet has to inspect the animal in person, usually at the airport, within 7 days of the travel date. Book that appointment around the actual flight, not around how long the certificate technically stays valid.

    - heading: "Getting from the Gulf to Hosea Kutako"
      body: |
        There's no nonstop flight from Dubai or Abu Dhabi to Windhoek. Emirates SkyCargo's Dubai-Johannesburg route, expanded to four daily flights on 1 July 2026, connects with Airlink's regional service into Hosea Kutako International for the standard two-leg routing.

        Ethiopian Airlines offers a single-carrier alternative through its Addis Ababa hub, which it serves from Dubai several times a day, with an onward connection to Windhoek. Either way, budget for a transfer and a second document check, not a single through-flight.
