  sections:
    - heading: "Poland's Import Rules Under the GIW"
      body: |
        Poland's animal health border authority is the Main Veterinary Inspectorate, known by its Polish initials as GIW (Główny Inspektorat Weterynarii, wetgiw.gov.pl). GIW applies EU non-commercial pet movement rules at Warsaw Chopin Airport (WAW) and other entry points.

        For a pet arriving from Jamaica, GIW requires:

        Microchip: ISO 11784/11785 standard, implanted before the rabies vaccination date on record. GIW inspectors routinely scan microchips and verify them against the health certificate. Any mismatch will result in the animal being detained.

        Rabies vaccination: Current and post-microchip. The primary vaccination must be at least 21 days old at the time of travel. A booster given within the validity period of the previous vaccination is considered continuous and does not restart the 21-day count.

        Health certificate: EU Annex IV non-commercial format, signed by a VSD-authorised vet in Jamaica and endorsed by the Veterinary Services Division. Must be issued within 10 days of travel.

        No quarantine and no import permit are required for compliant arrivals.

    - heading: "Airline Routing from Jamaica to Warsaw"
      body: |
        Warsaw Chopin Airport (WAW) is the main international cargo hub for Poland. Most live animal shipments from Jamaica route through London Heathrow (LHR), Amsterdam (AMS), or Frankfurt (FRA) before the final sector to Warsaw.

        British Airways World Cargo operates live animal cargo from both KIN and MBJ to LHR. From LHR, LOT Polish Airlines is the natural onward carrier to WAW and handles live animal cargo. British Airways also serves LHR-WAW on some days.

        KLM Cargo offers an alternative transatlantic path via Amsterdam. KLM has strong live animal procedures and AMS to WAW is a short, frequently served connection. This routing is worth considering if LHR space is tight.

        Lufthansa via Frankfurt gives a third option. Lufthansa's animal lounge at FRA has temperature-controlled facilities and specialised staff. FRA to WAW is well served by Lufthansa and LOT Polish Airlines.

        Book cargo space early, especially for travel during summer (June to August) when demand peaks across all European routes.

    - heading: "Preparing Your Pet for a Long Cargo Journey"
      body: |
        The total journey from Jamaica to Warsaw, including layover time at the hub airport, typically runs 18-30 hours. This is a significant period for any animal, and good preparation makes a difference.

        Crate training is the most important thing you can do before travel. Begin using the IATA-approved crate as a regular resting space several weeks before departure. Feed meals inside the crate, leave it open in your home, and let your pet choose to enter it. An animal that is comfortable in its crate will cope significantly better with the noise, motion, and unfamiliar environment of cargo travel.

        Feed a small meal 4-6 hours before the check-in time and give water up to the point of crating. Do not fast your pet for more than a few hours, but equally avoid a large meal immediately before travel, as motion sickness is possible.

        Sedation is not recommended. Most veterinary bodies, including the British Veterinary Association (BVA), advise against sedating pets for air cargo because sedation can interfere with normal respiratory and cardiovascular function at altitude.

    - heading: "Documents and What to Carry"
      body: |
        Travel with the following documents in hard copy, in a waterproof folder:

        - VSD-endorsed EU health certificate (original)
        - Vaccination record with microchip number, vaccine name, batch number, and dates
        - Your passport
        - The airline airway bill for the live animal shipment
        - A copy of the cargo booking confirmation

        Polish GIW officers inspect the original health certificate. Copies are useful as backups but the original must be presented. If the originals are with the cargo, make sure you have your contact details and the cargo tracking number so you can locate the shipment quickly on arrival.

    - heading: "Costs: What to Expect"

faqs:
  - question: "Is there a direct flight for pet cargo from Jamaica to Poland?"
    answer: "No direct cargo service currently operates this route. All shipments require a connection at LHR, AMS, or FRA. The two-leg journey is standard for Jamaica-to-Poland pet transport and is well established by the major cargo carriers."
  - question: "Can I use the pet passport instead of the EU health certificate for my Jamaica-originating pet?"
    answer: "No. Pet passports are issued by EU or listed country authorities and are used for movement within the EU or from listed countries as the accompanying document. For a pet travelling from Jamaica, the correct document is the EU Annex IV non-commercial health certificate endorsed by the VSD. The GIW will not accept a Jamaican pet passport as a substitute."
  - question: "My pet's rabies vaccination expires three weeks after travel. Is it still valid for entry to Poland?"
    answer: "Yes, as long as the vaccination is still within its validity period on the date of travel. GIW checks the expiry date on the vaccination record. A vaccination that expires a few weeks after travel is fine, provided it is current on the day of entry. You should revaccinate once you are settled in Poland before the vaccination expires."
