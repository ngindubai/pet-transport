  sections:
    - heading: "Jamaica's listed status: no titre test for Poland"
      body: |
        EU Implementing Regulation 2026/636 classifies Jamaica as a listed third country, meaning pets from Jamaica are exempt from the FAVN titre test and the 90-day waiting period when entering EU member states including Poland. GIW (Glowny Inspektorat Weterynarii, the Chief Veterinary Inspectorate) still requires the standard EU-format non-commercial health certificate issued by a VSD-registered official veterinarian and endorsed by VSD.

        The VSD issues the export health certificate through a VSD-registered official veterinarian, along with a VSD export permit (approximately J$2,000). DHLPP vaccination is required by VSD in addition to rabies. The practical preparation involves confirming microchip and vaccinations, obtaining the VSD permit (4-6 weeks lead time), booking cargo to WAW via a connecting hub, and completing the health certificate appointment within 10 days of departure.

    - heading: "Routing to Warsaw and the GIW entry process"
      body: |
        Warsaw Chopin Airport (WAW) is the only Polish airport designated as a Border Inspection Post for non-EU pet arrivals by air. No other airport in Poland can receive direct non-EU animal shipments. All cargo from Jamaica must route to WAW.

        No direct service operates from Jamaica to Warsaw. The most practical routings involve London Heathrow (British Airways to LHR, then LOT Polish Airlines or other carrier to WAW), Frankfurt (Lufthansa Cargo with connections to WAW), or New York or Newark to Warsaw via LOT Polish Airlines. LOT Polish Airlines is the main hub carrier at WAW and offers cargo connections from several North American gateways. GIW inspectors at WAW conduct documentary and identity checks. Ask your Jamaican vet to scan and confirm the chip number before completing the certificate to avoid any mismatch at the BIP.
