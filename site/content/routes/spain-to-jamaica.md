  sections:
    - heading: "The import permit: apply before anything else"
      body: |
        Before you book a flight, order a crate, or schedule a vet appointment, apply for the VSD import permit. Email vsdpermits@moa.gov.jm with your pet's species, breed, age, sex, microchip number, and country of origin (Spain). The VSD may request additional documentation. Allow 8-12 weeks from application to receiving the permit. The fee is J$5,000 (approximately USD 32-35).

        Jamaica maintains a restricted breeds list. Dogo Argentino, Fila Brasileiro, Japanese Tosa, and Pit Bull Terrier face import restrictions. If your dog belongs to one of these breeds, or could be interpreted as a cross, contact the VSD directly before applying. Entry will not be granted at the airport if your breed is restricted.

        Once the permit is confirmed in writing, finalise your travel date and book cargo from Madrid Barajas (MAD) or Barcelona (BCN) to Norman Manley International Airport (KIN) in Kingston.

    - heading: "Parasite treatments: timing drives your preparation calendar"
      body: |
        The VSD's parasite treatment protocol requires two rounds each of internal and external treatments, 14 days apart. The second internal treatment must be within 7 days of transport. The second external treatment must be within 48 hours of departure.

        Work backward from your cargo departure date. If your flight leaves on a Thursday, the second external treatment happens on Tuesday or Wednesday. The first external treatment is 14 days earlier. Schedule both vet appointments before confirming your cargo booking. If a flight date changes, the parasite cycle may need to restart.

        Your vet must document both rounds with the product used, dose, and date of application. These records travel with your pet and are inspected at KIN and at Plumb Point.
