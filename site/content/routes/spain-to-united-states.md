  h1: "Pet Transport from Spain to the United States"
  overview: |
    Spain is classified as a low-risk country for canine rabies under the CDC's dog import framework. This means dogs from Spain have a straightforward path into the USA: complete the free CDC Dog Import Form online before travel, ensure the dog is microchipped and at least 6 months old, and the pet can arrive at any US airport or border crossing. There is no titre test, no quarantine, and no import permit.

    Cats from Spain face no federal US import requirements at all. USDA APHIS has no health requirements for importing pet cats from foreign countries. Individual airlines still require a vet health certificate, and some US states have their own rules, but at the federal level cats from Spain move freely into the USA.

    The main logistics challenge is the transatlantic cargo booking. Iberia operates the most direct service from Spanish airports (Madrid, Barcelona) to major US gateways. American Airlines and British Airways (via London) are alternatives.

    - heading: "CDC Dog Import Form: what it is and how it works"
      body: |
        The CDC Dog Import Form is an online registration form completed by the dog owner before travel. It is free, takes around 15 minutes to complete, and is valid for 6 months and multiple entries if the dog has remained in low-risk countries. You receive a receipt by email; have it available on arrival.

        For dogs from Spain (a low-risk country), the CDC Dog Import Form receipt is the primary document at the US port of entry. A CBP officer scans the microchip and checks that it matches the form. For compliant pets, the inspection is brief and the dog is released to you on arrival.

        Dogs must be at least 6 months old, appear healthy on arrival, and have a readable microchip that matches the form. No additional vaccination records are required by CDC for low-risk countries, though rabies vaccination is strongly recommended and required by most airlines.

    answer: "Spain is classified as a low-risk (non-high-risk) country by the CDC. Dogs from Spain do not need a titre test, quarantine, or certification from a USDA-accredited vet. The only CDC requirement is the free CDC Dog Import Form completed online before travel. The dog must be at least 6 months old, microchipped, and appear healthy on arrival."

    answer: "The CDC Dog Import Form receipt (free, online) is the primary document. Airlines also require a vet health certificate or EU pet passport and a valid rabies vaccination record.   - question: "Do cats from Spain need any documents to enter the USA?"
    answer: "At the federal level, there are no US requirements for importing pet cats from Spain. USDA APHIS has no health restrictions on cat imports. In practice, airlines require a vet-issued health certificate, and some US states have their own rules. Check your destination state's requirements and your airline's policy before travel."
