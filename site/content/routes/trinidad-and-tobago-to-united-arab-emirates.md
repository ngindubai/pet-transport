  overview: |
    Piarco International has exactly one nonstop long-haul option, British Airways to London, and that shapes the entire routing for a pet heading onward to Dubai or Abu Dhabi. From Heathrow, Emirates, Etihad or British Airways itself picks up the Gulf leg.

    Trinidad and Tobago isn't on MOCCAE's low-risk list, so a rabies titre test applies before the UAE import permit can be requested. With no fixed wait after the blood draw, the realistic timeline runs 10 to 14 weeks, most of it spent on the titre test and its laboratory turnaround.

  sections:
    - heading: "Why the London connection matters here"
      body: |
        British Airways' Piarco-Gatwick service is the backbone of almost every serious pet move out of Trinidad and Tobago toward Europe, the Middle East or Asia, and this route is no exception. IAG Cargo handles the booking on the first leg, and a connecting carrier, most often Emirates or Etihad, covers the onward run into Dubai or Abu Dhabi. A US gateway routing via Miami or New York onto Qatar Airways is a genuine second option when London scheduling doesn't work.

    - heading: "The titre test, without the EU's fixed wait"
      body: |
        MOCCAE's rule set treats Trinidad and Tobago as an unlisted-risk origin, so the standard titre test applies: blood drawn after vaccination, a result of at least 0.5 IU/ml, tested at an accredited laboratory. What the UAE doesn't add is a fixed waiting period once that result comes back. The pet can travel as soon as the test passes and the MOCCAE import permit is confirmed, which is the main reason this route runs faster than an equivalent EU-bound move.
