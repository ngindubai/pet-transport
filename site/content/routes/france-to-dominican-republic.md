  sections:
    - heading: "What the Dominican Republic requires from your pet"
      body: |
        Pet imports to the Dominican Republic are regulated by the DGAG (Direccion General de Ganaderia) under the Ministerio de Agricultura, agricultura.gob.do. Veterinary inspection happens on arrival at Las Americas International Airport (SDQ) in Santo Domingo.

        The requirements are straightforward: a microchip conforming to ISO 11784/11785, a valid rabies vaccination given between 30 days and 12 months before entry, and a health certificate issued within 15 days of travel. Dogs need current DHPP vaccination (distemper, adenovirus, parainfluenza, parvovirus). Cats need rhinotracheitis, calicivirus, and panleukopenia vaccines on record. All pets need an internal and external antiparasitic treatment within 10 days of departure, documented by a vet.

        No import permit is needed for personal companion animals (up to five travelling with their owner), and there is no quarantine for arrivals with compliant paperwork. The DGAG inspection at SDQ checks microchip, vaccination records, health certificate, and antiparasitic documentation.

    - heading: "French export documentation: the DGAL-endorsed AHC"
      body: |
        France is an EU member state and follows harmonised EU veterinary export procedures. For travel to non-EU destinations including the Dominican Republic, you need an AHC from a veterinaire officiel, endorsed by the DGAL or its local representative.

        The local endorsing authority is typically the DDSPP (Direction Departementale de la Protection des Populations) or its regional equivalent. Find your nearest official vet through the DGAL website or your local departement's veterinary services office. Book early: official vet appointments often have limited availability, especially in the weeks before French school holidays.

        The certificate must include your full name and address, plus the animal's name, breed, sex, colour, age, and microchip number. The Dominican Republic accepts certificates up to 15 days old, but this is not an invitation to delay. Issue the AHC as close to your departure date as you can, particularly if other time-sensitive steps (the antiparasitic treatment window) are running close.
