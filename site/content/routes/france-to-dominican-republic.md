  overview: "France to the Dominican Republic is one of the more straightforward outbound pet transport routes from Europe. No import permit is needed for personal companion animals (up to 5 pets), and there is no quarantine for compliant arrivals.   sections:
    - heading: "Dominican Republic requirements for pets from France"
      body: |
        DGAG (Direccion General de Ganaderia), within the Ministerio de Agricultura (agricultura.gob.do), governs pet imports. For pets arriving from France, the requirements are: ISO 11784/11785 microchip; rabies vaccination administered between 30 days and 12 months before arrival; current core vaccinations (distemper, parvovirus, hepatitis, leptospirosis); and an official health certificate endorsed by the DDPP, issued within 15 days of travel.

        No import permit is required for up to 5 personal companion animals. No quarantine for compliant arrivals. DGAG inspects pets on arrival at Las Americas International Airport (SDQ) in Santo Domingo.

    - heading: "Core vaccination requirements"
      body: |
        French pets travelling to the Dominican Republic must have their core vaccinations current: distemper, parvovirus, infectious hepatitis, and leptospirosis, in addition to rabies. All must be recorded in the health certificate with dates and batch numbers.

        French pets with EU pet passports will typically have core vaccinations documented, but the official export health certificate must reference all of them explicitly in the DGAG-required format. Discuss the certificate content with your official veterinarian before the appointment to ensure nothing is omitted.

    - heading: "Routing from Paris to Santo Domingo"
      body: |
        Paris Charles de Gaulle (CDG) has direct cargo connections to Santo Domingo (SDQ) via Air France's Caribbean routes. Air France handles cargo including live animals on approved routes, and CDG-SDQ is one of the more direct European connections to the Dominican Republic.

        If direct capacity is unavailable, a routing via Madrid (MAD) with Iberia is an alternative: Iberia also serves MAD-SDQ and handles live animal cargo on Caribbean routes. Book cargo space early and confirm live animal acceptance on the specific flight.

    - heading: "DDPP export endorsement from France"
      body: |
        France's DDPP (Direction Departementale de la Protection des Populations) endorses official export health certificates at the department level. An official veterinarian prepares the certificate in English or Spanish, referencing the microchip, all vaccinations, and their dates.

        Allow three to five business days for the DDPP endorsement. The health certificate must be issued within 15 days of travel (the Dominican Republic allows 15 days rather than the standard EU 10-day period). Keep the certificate as recent as practical to avoid any questions on arrival.

faqs:
  - question: "Is there a limit on how many pets I can bring to the Dominican Republic from France?"
    answer: "Up to 5 personal companion animals may enter without an import permit. If you are travelling with more than 5, or for any commercial movement of animals, contact DGAG (agricultura.gob.do) before travel to determine additional permit requirements."
  - question: "Does the Dominican Republic require tapeworm treatment for dogs from France?"
    answer: "DGAG does not mandate a specific tapeworm or Echinococcus treatment for dogs arriving from France. The core vaccination and health certificate requirements are the primary focus. Confirm with DGAG at the time of travel as requirements can be updated."
  - question: "Can I use my pet's French EU pet passport as the official health certificate?"
    answer: "No. The EU pet passport is a vaccination record for intra-EU travel, not an official export health certificate for non-EU destinations. For the Dominican Republic, you need an official health certificate prepared by an official veterinarian and endorsed by the DDPP."
  - question: "What does the DGAG inspection at SDQ involve?"
    answer: "DGAG inspectors at Las Americas Airport check the endorsed health certificate, scan the microchip, and review the vaccination records. For compliant pets with all required vaccinations documented, the inspection is routine and typically takes 30-60 minutes after the cargo is unloaded."
