  overview: |
    Bringing a pet from Uganda to the UK follows the same APHA protocol as other non-listed countries: microchip, rabies vaccination, titre test, 3-month wait, tapeworm treatment for dogs, and an official health certificate. The minimum preparation timeline is 5-6 months.

    Uganda has a practical advantage on this route: British Airways operates direct service between Entebbe International Airport and London Heathrow. A direct flight means no connecting hub, no transit documentation complications, and the shortest possible journey time for your pet.

    Kampala and Entebbe have veterinary services experienced with international pet moves. NGO and development sector relocations are relatively common from Uganda, which means there is experience in the system - but the documentation sequence is still exacting and must be followed correctly.
  sections:
    - heading: "UK entry requirements: what Uganda-origin pets must complete"
      body: |
        Uganda is not on the UK's approved country list. The full APHA protocol applies: microchip (ISO 11784/11785) implanted before rabies vaccination, current rabies vaccination, titre test with result of at least 0.5 IU/ml from an EU/UK approved laboratory, 3-month wait from blood draw date, tapeworm treatment for dogs (24-120 hours before UK arrival), and an official Uganda health certificate valid for 10 days.

        The titre test is the step that most often trips people up. The blood must be drawn at least 30 days after the rabies vaccination, and the 3-month wait starts on the day of blood sampling - not when results arrived. Many owners get this right in principle but miscalculate by a week or two. Even a day's error means the pet cannot legally enter the UK. Calculate the date precisely.

        The health certificate from Uganda must be endorsed by the Ministry of Agriculture, Animal Industry and Fisheries (MAAIF). Allow time for this endorsement process - it does not happen instantly.
    - heading: "Comparing flight options from Entebbe to London"
      body: |
        British Airways direct from Entebbe (EBB) to London Heathrow is the most straightforward option. BA World Cargo has handled Uganda-UK pet moves regularly. Your pet travels in a single pressurised hold without intermediate handling or connection risk. Book cargo space 4-6 weeks before travel.

        Qatar Airways via Doha and Emirates via Dubai are both excellent alternatives. Both carriers have well-established pet cargo operations on Africa-UK routes. The trade-off is a 2-5 hour connection in the Gulf. Neither carrier allows brachycephalic breeds on some segments without additional paperwork - if your dog is a Bulldog, Pug, or similar flat-faced breed, check this before booking.

        Ethiopian Airlines via Addis Ababa has strong schedule frequency from Entebbe and a solid cargo operation. This is worth considering if BA cargo space is full at your preferred travel time.

        Kenya Airways via Nairobi is another option, but be aware that transiting through Nairobi may technically require Kenyan veterinary documentation. This is worth confirming with Kenya Airways Cargo or your agent before booking.
    - heading: "Working with an IPATA agent versus arranging independently"
      body: |
        This route is manageable for organised owners who follow the steps carefully. However, there are several points where experience helps: identifying an EU/UK approved laboratory in East Africa, navigating the MAAIF endorsement process, securing cargo space on BA during busy periods, and ensuring the tapeworm treatment timing is precisely documented.

        IPATA-registered agents with Uganda experience handle the MAAIF paperwork, know which laboratories hold the required approvals, and maintain relationships with cargo desks at Entebbe airport. Their fee is typically USD 300-800 on top of airline cargo costs, and for many owners the peace of mind is worth it.

        If arranging independently, use the IPATA member directory (ipata.com) to find an agent to at least answer specific questions, even if you are not paying for full case management.
