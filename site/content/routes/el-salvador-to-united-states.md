  h1: "Pet Transport from El Salvador to the United States"
  overview: |
    A dog leaving El Salvador for the US now runs into a problem that has nothing to do with El Salvador's own paperwork: several of the biggest US carriers have pulled back checked-pet cargo altogether, and the ones still flying have tightened up further because El Salvador sits on the CDC's high-risk list for dog rabies. Cats sit outside almost all of this. No federal rabies rule applies to them at the US border, so a cat's journey from El Salvador is, in practice, a fairly ordinary one.

    For dogs, the practical route runs through Avianca or Copa rather than American. Avianca flies widest, direct to several US cities from San Salvador. Copa connects through Panama City, itself not a high-risk country, so nothing extra gets added at that leg. Either way, the dog still needs the full CDC high-risk package: microchip first, then vaccination, then a titre test at a CDC-approved lab, then a reservation at one of only six US airports equipped to process it.

    El Salvador's own side of the paperwork is comparatively quick. MAG's Santa Tecla office turns around the export certificate in a matter of days, not weeks, provided the vaccination record and a recent health certificate are ready.

  sections:
    - heading: "Why El Salvador's dog-rabies status changes the airline choice, not just the paperwork"
      body: |
        Most owners assume the CDC rules are the only complication. They are not the only one on this route. American Airlines has suspended general-public checked-pet cargo across its network, restricting the PetEmbark service to active military and State Department staff on official orders. Delta and United have gone further and closed their public cargo programmes entirely, with no announced return date. None of that is specific to El Salvador, but it removes three of the biggest US names from the list of workable carriers here.

        That leaves Avianca and Copa carrying the load for dogs needing the cargo hold. Both still accept dog and cat bookings and both still expect the CDC paperwork completed correctly for a high-risk-country dog: the microchip before vaccination, the titre drawn at least 30 days after that vaccination, and a reservation confirmed at one of the six CDC-registered facility airports before the flight is booked, not after.

    - heading: "El Salvador's own export process is the easy part"
      body: |
        MAG's zoosanitary export certificate for pets is one of the more efficient processes in the region: apply 3 to 5 business days before travel, at the Santa Tecla office or by email, with a vaccination record showing the complete prophylactic plan for the animal's age and a health certificate no older than 15 days. There is no lengthy pre-export quarantine and no separate consulate step to chase.

        The tighter deadline cuts both ways, though. Because MAG only wants the certificate a few days out, it is tempting to leave it until late in the process. Don't. The US-side titre test and facility reservation both need months of lead time for a dog, so build the MAG certificate into that same timeline rather than treating it as a last-minute errand.
