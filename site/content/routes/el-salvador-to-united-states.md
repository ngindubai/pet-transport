  h1: "Pet Transport from El Salvador to the United States"
  overview: |
    El Salvador is on the CDC's list of countries classified high risk for dog rabies, verified directly against cdc.gov, whose high-risk countries page was last reviewed on 15 April 2026. That single classification is the fact that shapes this entire route for a dog: a titre test, a CDC Dog Import Form, entry restricted to a handful of airports with a CDC-registered animal care facility, and, less obviously, an outright booking problem. American Airlines Cargo currently embargoes new cargo bookings for dogs originating in any CDC high-risk country, El Salvador included, despite running convenient nonstop flights from San Salvador to Miami and Dallas-Fort Worth.

    None of this touches cats. The high-risk classification addresses the canine rabies variant specifically, so a cat moves on a much shorter, ordinary process: microchip, a standard health certificate, no CDC titre requirement, no ACF airport restriction.

    On the El Salvador side, the export process barely changes based on where the pet is headed. MAG's zoosanitary export certificate costs USD 16.95 and takes three to five working days, same as any other destination.

  sections:
    - heading: "The CDC pathway for a dog, in order"
      body: |
        Microchip first, always before vaccination. Then the rabies vaccine, at least 28 days ahead of the US arrival date, and only once the dog has reached 12 weeks old. Thirty days after that vaccination, blood gets drawn for the titre test and sent to a CDC-approved laboratory. A result of 0.5 IU/mL or above is a pass.

        With that result in hand, the next move is booking a reservation at a CDC-registered animal care facility, one of a short list of US airports equipped to receive dogs from high-risk countries. Only then can the CDC Dog Import Form be completed online, generating a single-entry receipt tied to the confirmed arrival date. Skip the titre, and the dog still gets in, but only after a mandatory 28-day quarantine at the facility, paid for by the owner.

        A separate document, the Certification of Foreign Rabies Vaccination and Microchip form, gets completed by the vet in El Salvador and endorsed by an official MAG veterinarian. It is only valid for 30 days, so time it close to departure rather than early in the process.

    - heading: "The airline problem, and the workaround"
      body: |
        American Airlines flies San Salvador to Miami and to Dallas-Fort Worth every day of the year, which looks like the obvious booking. It is not, currently, an option for a dog: American Airlines Cargo embargoes new bookings for dogs coming from any CDC high-risk country, and that list includes El Salvador.

        Avianca is the better fit here, and not by accident. San Salvador was TACA's home base before the merger, and Avianca still runs year-round nonstop service into Miami, New York JFK and Los Angeles, all three of which double as CDC-registered animal care facility airports. Delta's nonstop into Atlanta covers the same ground, since Atlanta hosts the Dandie Scottie Kennel facility. Either carrier avoids the American embargo issue and lands somewhere the CDC will actually accept the dog.

    - heading: "Why cats have a much shorter list"
      body: |
        A cat does not need the titre test, does not need a CDC Dog Import Form, and is not restricted to a CDC-registered animal care facility airport. A microchip and a standard international health certificate cover most of what US entry requires for a cat, and any of the nonstop carriers out of San Salvador, American included, can be booked without the complications a dog faces. If your household is moving a dog and a cat together, budget for two very different processes running on the same calendar.

    - heading: "El Salvador's export side stays simple"
      body: |
        MAG's Quarantine Division in Santa Tecla issues the Certificado Zoosanitario de Exportacion de Mascotas for USD 16.95 per animal, using a private vet certificate no older than 15 days, a full vaccination record, and the MAG application form. Three to five working days is the usual turnaround, with a final clearance stamp applied by quarantine inspectors at the airport itself. Time this to land in the same final week as the CDC-side paperwork, since both sets of documents need to be current on the day of travel.
