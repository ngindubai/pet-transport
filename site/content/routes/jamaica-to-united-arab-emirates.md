content:
  h1: "Pet Transport from Jamaica to the UAE"
  overview: |
    Two rulebooks, two different answers, same pet. Jamaica carries an EU Group 2 listing under Implementing Regulation (EU) 2024/1130, which waives the rabies titre test for pets entering the EU. None of that reaches the UAE. MOCCAE keeps its own short low-risk list, Jamaica isn't on it, and the titre test is still required for a dog or cat flying from Kingston or Montego Bay to Dubai.

    The good news sits in how MOCCAE handles the test once it's done. There's no fixed 90-day wait counted from the blood draw, the way the EU requires. MOCCAE simply checks the result, 0.5 IU/ml minimum, valid for 365 days provided vaccination stays current. Jamaica's Veterinary Services Division endorses the export certificate close to the travel date, and the route itself runs through London Heathrow, first on British Airways, then Emirates SkyCargo for the final leg into Dubai.

  sections:
    - heading: "Why the EU exemption doesn't help here"
      body: |
        It's an easy mistake to make. Jamaica's EU Group 2 status under Implementing Regulation (EU) 2024/1130 removes the titre test requirement for pets travelling into the EU, and owners sometimes assume that exemption travels with the pet wherever it goes next. It doesn't. MOCCAE runs its own separate low-risk list for UAE entry, built on its own criteria, and Jamaica sits outside it.

        That means the standard unlisted-country package applies: microchip before vaccination, rabies vaccination at least 21 days before travel for a first dose, then a titre test at an accredited laboratory. Minimum age for entry is 15 weeks. The one relief MOCCAE does offer is timing: no fixed wait after the blood draw itself, just the test result and its year-long validity.

    - heading: "Routing through London to Dubai"
      body: |
        British Airways covers the Kingston or Montego Bay to London Heathrow leg, a well-worn path for Caribbean cargo generally. Emirates SkyCargo picks up the connection from Heathrow into Dubai, drawing on established handling procedures for live animal cargo at both ends.

        Two airlines means two separate documentation checks, so confirm the crate meets both carriers' specifications ahead of departure. Build the London transit time into your planning; it's not a quick changeover, and pets need appropriate rest and water arrangements across the connection.
