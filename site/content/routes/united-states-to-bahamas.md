  h1: "Pet Transport from the United States to the Bahamas"
  overview: |
    BAHFSA changed how it charges for pet import permits back in September 2024, moving the whole process onto its Click2Clear portal. The fee that shows up on a lot of older guidance, a little over USD 11, isn't what applicants pay now: it's USD 50, and the permit itself now covers a 6-month window rather than a single fixed trip.

    Everything else about this route stays close to what US owners might expect from a short Caribbean hop. No titre test, frequent direct flights from Florida, Atlanta and New York, and a health certificate that a regular US vet can sign without an extra USDA endorsement step. The one genuine trap is timing that certificate: it's only valid for 48 hours before the flight.

  sections:
    - heading: "The fee changed, and a lot of guidance hasn't caught up"
      body: |
        BAHFSA's move from the older Mygateway system to Click2Clear on 1 September 2024 came with a fee change alongside the new online process. Plenty of pet travel guidance, including material still circulating from before that switch, quotes the old figure of USD 10 plus 12% VAT, USD 11.20 total. The current fee is USD 50.

        The permit itself also works differently now. Rather than being tied to one specific trip, it's valid for 6 months from the intended travel date. That's genuinely useful if a flight date shifts, since a rebooked departure doesn't automatically mean a fresh application, provided it still falls inside that 6-month window. Confirm the live fee directly with BAHFSA before applying, since a government portal fee schedule can move again without much notice.

    - heading: "Why the US side needs no APHIS endorsement here"
      body: |
        Most of the destinations on this site route the export health certificate through a two-step process: a USDA-accredited vet signs it, then a USDA APHIS State Veterinarian endorses it, adding a few business days and a USD 38 fee. The Bahamas skips the second step entirely. A licensed US vet's signature is enough on its own.

        That simplifies the paperwork side of this move considerably, but it doesn't loosen the timing. BAHFSA wants that certificate dated within 48 hours of the flight, a narrower window than the certificate's own simplicity might suggest. Book the vet appointment against the confirmed departure time, not a few days ahead for convenience.

    - heading: "Getting on a plane from three different US cities"
      body: |
        American Airlines runs the most frequent US-Nassau schedule, mostly out of Miami, with cabin space for a small pet and American PetEmbark handling anything too large for the cabin. Delta connects Atlanta and New York, and JetBlue flies from New York and Fort Lauderdale, both cabin-only with no fallback cargo option for a bigger dog.

        For a larger animal, American Airlines is currently the only one of the three offering a cargo path on this specific corridor. That's worth knowing before booking a Delta or JetBlue flight on price alone, only to discover the dog doesn't fit under the seat.
