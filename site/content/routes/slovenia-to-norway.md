    - heading: "Norway Entry Requirements: What Your Pet Needs"
      body: |
        Norway follows EU Regulation 576/2013 for non-commercial pet movements, even though it is not an EU member state. Pets arriving from Slovenia need an ISO 11784/11785 microchip, a current rabies vaccination (at least 21 days old after a primary dose), and a valid EU pet passport. No titre test is required; Slovenia is a listed country. For dogs specifically, there is an additional requirement: praziquantel tapeworm treatment, given by a vet and recorded in the pet passport, between 1 and 5 days before the animal arrives in Norway. This applies to every dog, every time, from every country outside Norway. Mattilsynet (mattilsynet.no) oversees all animal imports.

SAS offers connections via Scandinavian hubs for pets already routed through Stockholm or Copenhagen. faqs:
  - question: "Why does my dog need tapeworm treatment when moving from Slovenia (an EU country) to Norway?"
    answer: "Norway's tapeworm rule applies to all dogs arriving from outside Norway, without exception. It is a biosecurity measure specific to Norway and is not waived for EU member states. The treatment must be praziquantel, given by a vet 1 to 5 days before arrival, and recorded in the EU pet passport. Cats do not need this treatment. The requirement is set by Mattilsynet: mattilsynet.no."
  - question: "Is my EU pet passport valid for entry into Norway?"
    answer: "Yes. Norway accepts the EU pet passport even though it is not an EU member state. The passport must show a valid ISO 11784/11785 microchip, a current rabies vaccination at least 21 days old (after a primary dose), and, for dogs, the praziquantel tapeworm treatment recorded within 1 to 5 days of arrival."
  - question: "Does my cat need tapeworm treatment to enter Norway?"
    answer: "No. The tapeworm treatment requirement applies to dogs only. Cats travelling from Slovenia to Norway need a valid EU pet passport with microchip and current rabies vaccination. No tapeworm treatment is required for cats."
  - question: "How do I arrange cargo from Ljubljana when there are no direct flights to Oslo?"
    answer: "The most established option is Lufthansa cargo via Frankfurt (FRA). You book the full LJU-FRA-OSL journey as a single cargo booking through Lufthansa or a licensed pet transport agent. The agent handles the live animal documentation at FRA and confirms handover to the Oslo-bound leg. Booking 8 to 12 weeks in advance is recommended, as live animal cargo space at LJU is limited."
