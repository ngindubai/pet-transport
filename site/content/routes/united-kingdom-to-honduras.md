content:
  h1: "Pet Transport from the United Kingdom to Honduras"
  overview: |
    Honduras is one of the lighter-touch destinations on the import side. There's no rabies titre test, no import permit for the usual 1 or 2 pet move, and no fixed kennel quarantine, just a current vaccination card and the UK's own APHA-endorsed export certificate presented to Cuarentena on arrival.

    The catch is getting there. No airline flies the UK to Honduras nonstop, and Avianca, a major regional carrier, will not carry pets on UK flights at all. Every booking runs through a US gateway or a second Central American hub, which is where the real planning effort in this route sits.

  sections:
    - heading: "Honduras asks less of you than most Latin American destinations"
      body: |
        SENASA's own import instructivo is short, and it shows. For 1 or 2 pets, there's no advance permit application at all: you turn up at the port of entry with the UK's export health certificate and a vaccination card showing current vaccines, a Cuarentena officer inspects the animal, and you pay a flat Lps 525 fee.

        That changes once a third pet joins the shipment. From 3 pets upward, SENASA requires a Permiso Zoosanitario de Importación applied for in advance, plus a vet-supervised home quarantine arrangement called Cuarentena Fiduciaria. Anyone moving a multi-pet household, not just a single dog or cat, needs to budget extra time and a different set of paperwork.

    - heading: "The routing is the hard part, not the regulations"
      body: |
        Palmerola International (XPL) near Tegucigalpa and Ramón Villeda Morales in San Pedro Sula (SAP) are the two airports that matter. American Airlines connects both from Miami and Dallas-Fort Worth; United connects XPL from Houston. Getting a pet onto one of these US-hub flights is usually the easy half of the journey.

        The transatlantic half needs more coordination. British Airways' cargo network reaches Miami but not Honduras directly, so the onward leg has to be booked separately, typically through an IPATA-registered pet relocation agent who can manage the handover between carriers. Build that agent fee into your budget from the start rather than discovering it partway through booking.
