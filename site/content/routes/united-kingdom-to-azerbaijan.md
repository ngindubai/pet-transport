faqs:
  - question: "Does my pet need a titre test to travel from the UK to Azerbaijan?"
    answer: "No. Azerbaijan does not require a titre test for pets from the United Kingdom. You need a current rabies vaccination, ISO microchip, and a UK Animal Health Certificate from an APHA-authorised Official Vet. Confirm current State Veterinary Service requirements before travel."
  - question: "Are there direct flights from London to Baku for pet cargo?"
    answer: "Yes. Both AZAL (Azerbaijan Airlines) and British Airways operate direct London Heathrow to Baku (GYD) service. AZAL Cargo and BA World Cargo both handle pets on this route. Direct routing is recommended to minimise handling and journey time."
  - question: "Can I take my small pet in the cabin on AZAL to Azerbaijan?"
    answer: "AZAL may allow small pets in the cabin on the LHR-GYD route. Cabin pet policies vary and change. Confirm AZAL's current cabin pet weight limits and accepted carrier dimensions before booking."
  - question: "What documents do I need to bring my pet from the UK to Azerbaijan?"
    answer: "You need: ISO-standard microchip, current rabies vaccination record, UK Animal Health Certificate from an APHA-authorised Official Vet (valid 10 days), and any import documentation required by Azerbaijan's State Veterinary Service. Bring original documents - copies are not sufficient at Baku airport."
  - question: "What happens when my pet arrives at Heydar Aliyev airport in Baku?"
    answer: "A State Veterinary Service inspector checks your pet on arrival at Heydar Aliyev International Airport (GYD). They verify the AHC, microchip, vaccination records, and import documentation. For compliant pets, there is no extended quarantine. Allow extra time for the veterinary inspection process."
