content:
  h1: "Pet Transport from France to Brunei"
  overview: |
    Two airlines fly nonstop from Paris to Singapore. Air France runs its own aircraft on flights AF182 and AF256, and Singapore Airlines covers the same city pair, which gives this route more direct capacity out of a single European airport than most Brunei-bound corridors get. Neither carrier goes any further than Changi, though.

    France sits outside the small group of countries Brunei treats as low risk for rabies, so the destination side of this move doesn't get any easier because the flight does. A titre test and a minimum 14 days of quarantine both apply, the same as they would from Germany or the United States. Budget three to four months, and treat DAA's own confirmation of current terms as the genuine starting point.

  sections:
    - heading: "Two direct flights to Singapore, neither one finishing the job"
      body: |
        Air France's Paris CDG to Singapore Changi service, flights AF182 and AF256, is Air France's own metal rather than a codeshare arrangement, and it runs at around 13 hours. Singapore Airlines flies the identical city pair nonstop as well, so a Brunei-bound pet leaving France has a genuine choice between two direct carriers rather than the single option some origins get.

        What neither one does is fly onward to Bandar Seri Begawan. Royal Brunei Airlines doesn't serve France at all, and where it does operate elsewhere, it only carries pets as manifested cargo booked through a registered freight forwarder. The practical result: book the Paris-Singapore leg with confidence, then arrange a second carrier, typically via a regional service or a coordinated road transfer through Kuala Lumpur, for the final hop.

    - heading: "Why two nonstop options don't shorten Brunei's own requirements"
      body: |
        Brunei splits origin countries into two tiers. A short scheduled list, Australia, Ireland, New Zealand, Sabah, Sarawak, Singapore and the UK, can see quarantine reduced for a pet with sufficient residency history. France isn't on it. The full pathway applies: a FAVN or ELISA titre test with blood drawn at least 30 days after vaccination, and a minimum 14-day quarantine stay on arrival.

        Get the titre result early. Once it clears, the import permit application, the flight booking and the AHC endorsement can all move in parallel, and that's really the only place where having two direct flight options actually helps: more schedule flexibility once the medical clock has already been set running.

    - heading: "The breed check, before anything else gets booked"
      body: |
        Three breeds cannot enter Brunei under any circumstances: Pit Bull Terrier, Dogo Argentino and Japanese Tosa. A second group, including Bull Mastiff, Doberman and German Shepherd, can travel, but only with a minimum age, mandatory neutering, and a requirement to muzzle the dog in public once it lands. Confirm your dog's breed against DAA's current list before spending anything on flights or lab work.
