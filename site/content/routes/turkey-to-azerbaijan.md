content:
  h1: "Pet Transport from Turkey to Azerbaijan"
  overview: |
    Istanbul to Baku reflects how closely connected the two countries are: two national carriers, both flying the route direct, several times a day between them. That density carries over to the pet transport side, where cargo space is rarely the constraint.

    Azerbaijan doesn't ask for a titre test from Turkey, so once vaccination and the health certificate are in order, this is one of the more straightforward routes in this build.

  sections:
    - heading: "A corridor with two national carriers"
      body: |
        Turkish Airlines and AZAL both operate direct Istanbul-Baku flights daily, a reflection of the close political and cultural ties between the two countries. For pet cargo, that means if one airline's hold is full on your preferred date, the other is a realistic same-day alternative. Turkey's own export side is a single health certificate from a TRGM-authorised vet, with no export permit required.

    - heading: "Azerbaijan's straightforward entry"
      body: |
        Azerbaijan doesn't require a rabies titre test from any origin, Turkey included, and there's no import permit for a personal pet entering with its owner. The microchip and current rabies vaccination, given at least 21 days before entry for a primary dose, are the core requirements, alongside the health certificate itself.
