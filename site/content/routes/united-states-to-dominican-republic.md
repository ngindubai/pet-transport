content:
  h1: "Pet Transport United States to Dominican Republic"
  overview: |
    A three-hour flight and no import permit for up to five pets. Compared with almost every other corridor in this build, that's about as light as international pet transport gets. American Airlines, JetBlue and Delta all fly this route non-stop out of Miami, New York or Atlanta, and the Dominican Republic's own paperwork is limited to a microchip, a valid rabies vaccination, and a health certificate.

    The one detail that catches owners out is the 15-day validity window on that health certificate. Get it signed too early relative to your actual travel date, and it expires before your pet ever boards the plane.

  sections:
    - heading: "Why the Dominican Republic asks for so little"
      body: |
        For up to five personal pets, no import permit is required at all. A microchip, a rabies vaccination given between 30 days and 12 months before arrival, and a health certificate endorsed by USDA APHIS within 15 days of travel cover the whole requirement. No titre test applies, since the Dominican Republic reserves that step for countries it classifies as high-rabies-risk, and the US doesn't fall into that category. On arrival at Las Americas International (SDQ) or Punta Cana International (PUJ), a DGAG official checks the microchip against the paperwork, and compliant pets clear without quarantine.

    - heading: "The USDA APHIS process, start to finish"
      body: |
        A USDA-accredited veterinarian examines the pet and completes the health certificate, which then needs APHIS endorsement, typically through the VEHCS electronic system. Processing usually takes 2 to 10 business days, longer around US federal holidays, so build that into the 15-day window rather than assuming same-day turnaround.

    - heading: "Direct flights from three US hubs"
      body: |
        American Airlines Cargo flies Miami direct to both Santo Domingo and Punta Cana. JetBlue covers the same routes from Miami and adds a JFK-SDQ option for owners on the East Coast. Delta rounds out the choices with Atlanta and New York departures. Whichever hub you're travelling from, there's a realistic non-stop option, which puts this route well ahead of the connection-heavy corridors elsewhere in this build.
