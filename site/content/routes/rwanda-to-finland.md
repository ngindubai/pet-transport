  sections:
    - heading: "Finland's approved entry point rule"
      body: |
        This one catches people out because it sits alongside the EU sequence rather than replacing it. Ruokavirasto, the Finnish Food Authority, restricts non-EU pet arrivals to designated border inspection posts. Helsinki-Vantaa (HEL) is one of the approved points, which makes it the natural choice for cargo booked from Kigali. It's worth confirming this directly with your airline or agent before booking, since a routing that technically works for cargo logistics could still fall outside the approved list for live animal customs clearance. This is separate from, and in addition to, the microchip, vaccination and titre test sequence below.

    - heading: "The unlisted-country sequence from Rwanda"
      body: |
        Finland is an EU member state, but Rwanda's status as an unlisted third country means pets still need the full sequence under EU rules: microchip before vaccination, a 30-day wait before blood is drawn for the titre test, a result of at least 0.5 IU/ml from an EU-approved lab, and then 90 days from the draw date before the pet may enter Finland. Get the chip-then-vaccine order wrong and the clock resets from scratch. Ruokavirasto checks documentation at the point of entry; conditional quarantine is possible if anything is missing, though there's no routine quarantine for pets that arrive fully compliant.

    - heading: "Routing from Kigali and the Rwanda export step"
      body: |
        Ethiopian Airlines Cargo via Addis Ababa is a solid starting option given the flight distance involved, and both Brussels Airlines and RwandAir connect Kigali to Brussels with an onward leg toward Helsinki. KLM Cargo via Nairobi and Amsterdam is a further alternate, drawing on Amsterdam's dense onward network. On the Rwanda side, no formal export permit is needed, only the EU-format health certificate from an RAB-authorised vet, endorsed by the Rwanda Agriculture and Animal Resources Development Board within 10 days of travel. Time that endorsement against your actual departure date, factoring in the transit stop.

faqs:
  - question: "Can a pet from Rwanda land at any Finnish airport?"
    answer: "No. Finland restricts entry from non-EU countries to approved border inspection posts. Helsinki-Vantaa is one of the approved points, so cargo routing should be built around it rather than a general connecting airport."
  - question: "Does the approved entry point rule replace the titre test requirement?"
    answer: "No, it applies on top of it. The approved entry point rule governs where the pet can physically clear customs. The titre test, 30-day wait and 90-day wait are separate requirements tied to Rwanda's status as an EU-unlisted country, and both sets of rules apply together."
  - question: "How long does the 90-day wait actually take from a practical standpoint?"
    answer: "It runs from the day blood is drawn for the titre test, not from the day the result arrives. Combined with the vaccination and 30-day pre-test wait, most owners are looking at 20 to 26 weeks total before the pet can legally enter Finland."
  - question: "What happens if my pet's documents are incomplete when it lands in Helsinki?"
    answer: "Conditional quarantine can apply. Finland does not routinely quarantine pets that arrive with the correct paperwork, but incomplete documentation removes that exemption, so confirm every certificate and test result before travel."
