  overview: |
    Compare this route to Madagascar's UK and US pages on this site and one thing jumps out: neither of those has a single-carrier option. Both need a second airline picked up at a connecting hub in Paris, Nairobi or Addis Ababa. This route doesn't. Emirates flies Dubai to Antananarivo via Mahe six days a week, on its own aircraft, with its own cargo handling from end to end.

    The regulatory side still asks something of Madagascar that the UAE doesn't ask back. Madagascar sits outside MOCCAE's roughly 50-country low-risk list, so a titre test applies, though there's no fixed wait once it passes. Realistically, budget 10 to 14 weeks, most of it spent waiting on a titre result that has to travel to an accredited lab outside the country.

        Every other Madagascar destination covered here needs a hub carrier: Air France through Paris, Kenya Airways through Nairobi, Ethiopian through Addis Ababa. This route skips that entirely.     - heading: "The titre test MOCCAE still asks for"
      body: |
        MOCCAE sorts every origin country into two groups: on its low-risk list, or not. Madagascar isn't on it, so a titre test at an accredited laboratory applies, with a result of at least 0.5 IU/ml. Unlike Great Britain's 3-month rule, MOCCAE attaches no mandatory wait once that result comes back. The pet can travel as soon as the test passes and the import permit is confirmed.

        The friction is Madagascar's own lab access, not MOCCAE's rules. No domestic laboratory is confirmed as internationally accredited for this test, so blood typically travels to South Africa or Mauritius, adding real courier time to a process that otherwise moves quickly.
