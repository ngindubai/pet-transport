content:
  h1: "Pet Transport from Maldives to Portugal: 2026 Guide"
  overview: |
    Live animal freight into Lisbon doesn't come through the main passenger building. It clears Terminal 6, the airport's dedicated cargo facility, handled on the ground by CEAL Cargo, the operator formerly known as Cargo Express Logistics. It's a detail most Maldives route guides skip, and one worth knowing before you plan a collection.

    None of that changes the paperwork out of Male. Portugal treats the Maldives as an EU-unlisted country, so microchip, rabies vaccination, an FAVN titre test at an EU-approved lab and a 90-day wait from the blood draw date all still apply before a cat can legally board.

  sections:
    - heading: "A cat route, not a dog ban"
      body: |
        A resident dog faces no legal restriction on leaving the Maldives. What limits this route to cats in practice is the Maldives Customs Service's own ban on dog imports, leaving almost no resident dog population to begin with. If you're the rare exception, this isn't a standard export job.
    - heading: "Exporting from Male without a ministry to sign off"
      body: |
        There's no export permit and no government counter-signature built into a routine departure. A private Male vet issues the certificate, and agents typically accept it as issued. Finding cargo capacity out of Velana International, which handles far more resort freight than live pets, is usually the harder part of the job.
    - heading: "The FAVN titre test and Portugal's 90-day wait"
      body: |
        Because the Maldives sits outside Implementing Regulation (EU) 2026/636's approved list, Commission Delegated Regulation (EU) 2026/131 requires blood drawn at least 30 days after vaccination to clear 0.5 IU/ml at an EU-approved lab, with the 90-day wait counted from the draw date, not the result. Get that wrong and a flight gets booked before the cat is legally cleared to enter Portugal.
    - heading: "What happens at Lisbon's Terminal 6"
      body: |
        DGAV, Portugal's food and veterinary authority, inspects every arriving pet against its paperwork at Terminal 6, the dedicated cargo building rather than the passenger arrivals hall. CEAL Cargo, the ground handler formerly known as Cargo Express Logistics, manages the physical freight side, so confirm collection procedures with them or your agent well before the flight lands.
