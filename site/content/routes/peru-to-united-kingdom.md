  sections:
    - heading: "SENASA's role in the Peru export process"
      body: |
        SENASA (Servicio Nacional de Sanidad Agraria) is Peru's agricultural health authority, responsible for issuing and endorsing the health certificates required for pet exports. The health certificate for UK import must be issued by a SENASA-authorised official vet (not just any vet) and endorsed by SENASA before travel.

        The certificate must include microchip number, vaccination history, the titre test result with laboratory details and blood draw date, the calculated end of the 90-day waiting period, and for dogs the tapeworm treatment details. SENASA offices are in Lima and other major cities. Contact SENASA (senasa.gob.pe) for the list of authorised vets and the current certificate format.

        Allow time for SENASA endorsement after the vet completes the certificate. The complete endorsed certificate must be in your hands within 10 days of travel.

    - heading: "Lima altitude and cargo routing from Peru"
      body: |
        Lima's Jorge Chavez International Airport (LIM) sits close to sea level, which means no altitude-related cargo restrictions of the kind that apply to Quito or Bogota. This is an advantage for pet cargo from Peru.

        Most cargo routes from Lima to the UK connect through Madrid (via LATAM or Iberia), Amsterdam (via KLM), or Paris (via Air France). Transit at these European hubs is generally well-handled. Confirm with your airline that live animal cargo is accepted on each leg, as aircraft type and cargo configuration can change.
