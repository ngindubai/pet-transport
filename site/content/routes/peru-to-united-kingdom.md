content:
  h1: "Pet Transport Peru to UK: What the Titre Test Requirement Means and How to Plan It"
  overview: "Peru sits in the unlisted category under UK pet travel rules, which means a FAVN rabies titre test is part of the process. The blood draw must happen at least 30 days after the primary vaccination, and once the test passes, a 90-day waiting period runs before the pet can travel. That adds up to around four to five months of preparation. After the wait: SENASA export authorisation from Lima, the official health certificate from a SENASA vet, and cargo departure from Jorge Chavez International Airport via Madrid or another European hub. Dogs also need a tapeworm treatment in the five-day window before UK arrival."

  sections:
    - heading: "Planning around the 90-day wait"
      body: |
        The 90-day wait after a successful titre test is the thing most owners do not factor in early enough. It starts from the date of the blood draw that produces the passing result. If your vet draws blood, sends it to the lab, and the result comes back at 0.5 IU/ml or above, the 90-day clock starts from the blood draw date, not the result date.

        This means that if you intend to travel in, say, October, the blood draw needs to happen by mid-July at the latest. The vaccination needs to have happened by mid-June at the latest (30 days before the blood draw). Work backwards from your intended travel date and you will quickly see why four to five months is the minimum.

        Once the titre test has been successfully passed and your pet stays current on rabies boosters, the titre result stays valid. If your pet has already passed a UK-standard titre test for a previous move and the vaccination has not lapsed, you may be able to travel within weeks rather than months.

    - heading: "Lima cargo options and SENASA documentation"
      body: |
        Lima's Jorge Chavez International Airport handles substantial cargo volume to Europe. Iberia via Madrid is the most direct routing for Lima to London pet cargo. LATAM Cargo from Lima is also a strong option with connections to European hubs. The transit through Madrid is generally manageable for animal welfare given the relatively short duration.

        SENASA (Servicio Nacional de Sanidad Agraria) handles both the export authorisation and the endorsement of the health certificate. Apply for the export authorisation online at least four weeks before travel. Your SENASA-authorised official vet issues the health certificate within 10 days of departure, covering all required information for UK border inspection.

faqs:
  - question: "Is there any way to skip the titre test when moving a pet from Peru to the UK?"
    answer: "No. Peru is an unlisted country under UK pet travel rules, and the FAVN titre test is mandatory for all dogs and cats. There is no waiver available. If your pet has already passed an equivalent titre test for a previous international move and the rabies vaccination has not lapsed, that valid result may still apply. Check with an APHA-registered vet or pet transport specialist."
  - question: "Which airport handles pet cargo from Lima to London?"
    answer: "Lima Jorge Chavez International (LIM) is the primary departure airport. Most cargo to the UK connects via Madrid (Iberia Cargo) or via other European hubs. Arrival in the UK is at Heathrow (LHR) or Gatwick (LGW), both of which are approved UK entry ports for pets. Confirm the full routing with your cargo agent before booking."
  - question: "How much does pet transport from Lima to London cost?"
    answer: "Budget GBP 1,600 to GBP 3,700 all-in. This covers the FAVN titre test (around USD 150-350), SENASA authorisation and health certificate, tapeworm treatment for dogs, the cargo flight from Lima via Madrid or similar, and the UK port inspection fee. Agent fees add GBP 200-500 at the top end. Total cost depends significantly on crate size."
  - question: "Are there breed restrictions for dogs entering the UK from Peru?"
    answer: "Yes. The UK Dangerous Dogs Act 1991 bans importation of Pit Bull Terrier type dogs (assessed by type, not breed name), Japanese Tosa, Dogo Argentino, and Fila Brasileiro. These restrictions apply regardless of origin. Peru's own PPP regulations cover some of the same breeds within Lima. Verify your dog's type before starting the process."
  - question: "Does the tapeworm treatment timing matter?"
    answer: "Yes, very much. The treatment must be administered between 24 and 120 hours before your dog's scheduled UK arrival time. Five days is the window, and it ends 24 hours before arrival. A treatment given six days before arrival is non-compliant. Your Peruvian vet administers it and records it in the health certificate. Time it carefully against your cargo flight's UK arrival time."
