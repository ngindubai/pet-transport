  h1: "Pet Transport from Italy to Maldives: 2026 Guide"
  overview: |
    Italy takes a relaxed line on dog breeds, having scrapped its national breed-specific legislation back in 2009. None of that helps here. The restriction on this route sits entirely on the Maldivian side: import of dogs is prohibited outright under Maldives Customs Service rules, with no standard exception route for any breed, however calm or well-documented.

    Cats have a genuinely workable path: microchip, rabies vaccination given 30 days to 12 months before arrival, the core feline vaccines, and a health certificate signed within 10 days of travel. Permits and titre tests are where published guidance thins out, so treat this route as one worth a direct check before booking.

  sections:
    - heading: "Italy's no breed ban meets the Maldives' no dog policy"
      body: |
        It's a small irony worth stating plainly. Italy would let almost any dog breed leave the country without a second look. The Maldives won't let any dog in, breed notwithstanding, because dog import sits on the Maldives Customs Service's prohibited list alongside items like alcohol and pork products. There's no standard assistance-animal exception either.
    - heading: "What a cat from Italy actually needs"
      body: |
        Microchip, then rabies vaccination given 30 days to 12 months before arrival, plus viral rhinotracheitis, calicivirus, panleukopenia and feline leukaemia cover. A health certificate signed within 10 days of departure closes the file. None of it demands the multi-month wait a titre test route would.
    - heading: "The permit question published sources don't agree on"
      body: |
        Some describe no import permit requirement for a travelling pet cat at all; others suggest a general permit covers all pet imports. No single current government page resolves this cleanly, so treat it as an open item for your vet or agent to confirm directly, not a settled fact.
