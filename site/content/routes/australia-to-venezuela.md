content:
  h1: "Pet Transport from Australia to Venezuela"
  overview: |
    Qantas has never flown a codeshare pet booking anywhere near Caracas, and there has never been a one-connection route between Australia and Venezuela in the first place. That's the starting reality here, before the June 2026 earthquake even enters the picture: Sydney or Melbourne to Santiago, Santiago to Bogota, Bogota to Valencia, has long been the only realistic pattern.

    On paper, the Venezuelan side is one of the lighter imports covered on this site. INSAI doesn't ask for a titre test from Australian-origin pets, only a current rabies certificate and the standard vaccination paperwork. The genuine unknown right now is the flight itself, and where the arrival permit actually gets processed while Maiquetia stays shut.

  sections:
    - heading: "Why this has always been a three-leg route"
      body: |
        Even before the earthquake, no airline connected Australia to Venezuela in fewer than two stops. Qantas Freight's Sydney-Santiago service links into LATAM Cargo's South American network, which reaches Bogota, and from Bogota a Venezuela-bound carrier picks up the final sector. It's a long way round, and it always has been.

        What's changed since 24 June 2026 is that final sector. It used to end at Maiquetia. It currently ends at Valencia instead, on Avianca's contingency schedule, which adds a fourth leg's worth of uncertainty to a routing that was already multi-stop.

    - heading: "A light import, undercut by an airport that isn't fully working"
      body: |
        INSAI's own checklist for a pet arriving from Australia reads much like it does for the UK or the US: a current rabies certificate, an international health certificate, the combined vaccination card, and a deworming certificate. No titre test. That's genuinely one of the easier import profiles this site tracks.

        The catch is the sanitary mobilisation permit, normally paid and stamped at the INSAI desk inside Maiquetia on arrival. With Maiquetia not handling scheduled commercial traffic, it isn't clear from anything published so far whether that desk has an equivalent at Valencia. Confirm this with INSAI or a Venezuela-based agent before you commit to a travel date, not after.

    - heading: "What DAFF asks of you before departure"
      body: |
        The Australian side is comparatively procedural. Lodge the Notice of Intent with your state or territory's DAFF regional office at least 10 working days out, get the final health and welfare exam done inside the 72 hours before departure, and make sure the export health certificate DAFF issues is the document that travels with the crate, not a private vet's own letter. INSAI works from government-issued paperwork, so a certificate that skips the DAFF process gets rejected on the other end.
