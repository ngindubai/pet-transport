  h1: "Pet Transport from the United Arab Emirates to Algeria"
  overview: |
    Book Emirates today and the flight works. Six times a week, Dubai to Algiers, direct, on a 777-300. Look past today, though, and the picture gets uncertain: Algeria has begun ending its own air services agreement with the UAE, and this exact route is reportedly at risk of suspension by early 2027.

    The regulatory side is settled by comparison. No titre test, no quarantine, and DSV's current position is that an accompanied pet doesn't need a standalone import permit either. What actually decides whether a pet gets refused at the border is its breed, not its paperwork.

  sections:
    - heading: "Why no titre test applies here, unlike the Albania page in this batch"
      body: |
        Algeria's DSV doesn't run a rabies-risk country list the way the EU or Albania's own EU-aligned rules do. A rabies vaccination given between 30 days and 12 months before entry is enough, whatever the pet's country of origin. That's a genuinely simpler position than the one built for Albania elsewhere in this batch, where the UAE's absence from an approved-country list triggers a mandatory blood test and a three-month wait.

        Don't read too much into that simplicity, though. It doesn't extend to breed.

    - heading: "The one thing that stops a pet at the border regardless of paperwork"
      body: |
        Pit Bull-type dogs and Staffordshire Bull Terriers can be turned away at Houari Boumediene Airport on breed grounds alone, no matter how current the vaccination or how properly endorsed the MOCCAE certificate is. Wolf hybrids need documented proof of five generations of pure domestic ancestry. Savannah and Bengal cats face the same rule.

        Check this before booking a flight, not after. A confirmed appointment and a paid cargo fare don't count for anything if the breed itself is the problem.

    - heading: "One certificate, possibly no translation needed"
      body: |
        Algeria expects paperwork in French or Arabic. A UK-origin certificate, English by default, always needs a certified translation added. MOCCAE's own digital platform runs bilingually in Arabic and English, which is a genuine point in this route's favour, provided the printed certificate itself carries the Arabic text DSV is looking for. Confirm that detail with the MOCCAE clinic at the point of endorsement rather than assuming it.

    - heading: "The flight, and its expiry date"
      body: |
        Emirates' direct Dubai-Algiers service runs up to six times weekly on a 777-300, the obvious choice while it lasts. Algeria's move to end its air services agreement with the UAE puts that service at genuine risk of suspension by early February 2027, the same warning attached to the reverse Algeria-to-UAE route built alongside this one.

        Turkish Airlines via Istanbul, with its own Pet Lounge for longer layovers, is worth having priced and ready as a fallback, particularly for anyone planning a move that sits close to that date.
