      body: |
        South Africa's Department of Agriculture, Land Reform and Rural Development (DALRRD) oversees pet exports. The process begins with a registered South African veterinarian, who examines your pet and prepares the health certificate. This certificate must then be endorsed by a state veterinarian.

        The state vet endorsement is the step that takes time and coordination. Registered vets familiar with UK exports will know the process, but it is worth starting 3-5 weeks before travel to allow for the endorsement and any administrative steps. A specialist pet transport agent in South Africa can manage the DALRRD process, which many owners find simpler than coordinating it independently.

  - question: "Does my pet need to quarantine on arrival in the UK from South Africa?"
    answer: "No. South Africa is on the UK's approved list, so pets do not require quarantine on arrival with correct documentation: microchip, current rabies vaccination (21-day wait completed), DALRRD-endorsed health certificate, and tapeworm treatment for dogs. Your pet is released at the Heathrow Animal Reception Centre after the APHA inspection, which is usually quick when paperwork is in order."
  - question: "How long does the DALRRD export process take?"
    answer: "Allow 3-5 weeks for the DALRRD documentation, including the state veterinarian endorsement. The process starts with a registered South African vet who prepares the health certificate, which is then endorsed by the state vet. The endorsement step is the main time factor. Starting early avoids last-minute pressure, especially since the health certificate examination must fall within 10 days of travel."
  - question: "Which airline is best for South Africa to UK pet transport?"
    answer: "British Airways operates a direct Johannesburg to Heathrow cargo pet service via IAG Cargo, which is the most common and convenient option.   - question: "Will moving via South Africa affect a later move to the USA?"
    answer: "It can. The US CDC classifies South Africa as a high-risk country for dog rabies. A dog that has been in South Africa within the 6 months before US entry may be subject to the high-risk process (titre test or CDC facility quarantine). If your pet moves South Africa to UK and then settles in the UK, the 6-month travel history clock works in your favour over time. If a US move is planned soon after, factor this in."
