  sections:
    - heading: "UK entry requirements for pets arriving from Kazakhstan"
      body: |
        Kazakhstan is not on the UK's approved country list. The full APHA protocol applies: ISO microchip implanted before vaccination, current rabies vaccination, titre test (blood drawn at least 30 days post-vaccination, result at least 0.5 IU/ml at an EU/UK approved laboratory), 3-month wait from blood sampling date, tapeworm treatment for dogs 24-120 hours before arrival, and a Kazakhstan veterinary authority-endorsed health certificate valid 10 days.

        The blood draw date is the critical date. Everything else in the timeline works backward from there. Get this date recorded clearly on the laboratory results certificate.

        Source: UK APHA, gov.uk/bring-pet-to-great-britain.
    - heading: "Finding an approved laboratory for the titre test in Central Asia"
      body: |
        The EU and UK maintain lists of laboratories approved for rabies titre testing. Kazakhstan does not currently have an approved laboratory, which means blood samples drawn in Almaty or Astana need to be shipped to an approved lab elsewhere.

        Turkey and Germany both have approved labs that accept international samples. Your vet or a pet transport agent should confirm the current approved lab list and provide cold-chain sample shipping guidance. Allow 2-3 weeks for the round-trip sample transit and results return.

        The 3-month wait starts from the day blood was drawn, not from when results came back. This means even a 2-week lab turnaround does not extend the waiting period - it just leaves you waiting slightly longer after results arrive.
