  h1: "Pet Transport from United Arab Emirates to Mongolia"
  overview: |
    Emirates and Air China together fly Dubai to Beijing nonstop 88 times a week. None of those flights go on to Ulaanbaatar, so every pet moving from the UAE to Mongolia needs at least one connection, whichever carrier gets the booking.

    Air China is the more direct option once in Beijing, running its own Beijing-Ulaanbaatar service. Its dog transport suspension, in force since 1 February 2025, made headlines, but it's scoped specifically to flights landing in the US and shouldn't touch this itinerary, worth confirming directly rather than assuming. Turkish Airlines via Istanbul, connecting to its own or MIAT's onward service, is the other realistic route. Mongolia's own import rule is close to a formality: no titre test, no permit, no quarantine for a pet with the right paperwork. Budget 6 to 9 weeks either way.

  sections:
    - heading: "Two routings, two different risk profiles"
      body: |
        Beijing via Air China cuts out a European detour and uses a carrier that already flies the Dubai leg 88 times a week combined with Emirates. The catch is confirming, in writing, that Air China's well-known dog restriction genuinely doesn't extend to this specific city pair, since the airline's own February 2025 policy change was widely reported and easy to misremember as a blanket ban.

        Istanbul via Turkish Airlines or MIAT avoids that question entirely, at the cost of a longer routing and MIAT's unusually wide brachycephalic exclusion list, 18 breeds rather than the more typical handful. A Cane Corso or Dogue de Bordeaux owner needs to check that list closely before booking either MIAT leg.

    - heading: "Mongolia's own rule barely slows things down"
      body: |
        GAVS asks for a microchip, a current rabies vaccination and a MOCCAE-endorsed export certificate. That's it. No titre test regardless of origin, no import permit for an owner's own pet, no quarantine for a compliant arrival.

        The genuinely limiting factor on this route is capacity, not paperwork. Ulaanbaatar's international schedule stays thin whichever hub feeds it, so a 6 to 8 week booking window is the sensible minimum rather than the 4 weeks that might suffice on a busier corridor.
