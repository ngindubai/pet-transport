  h1: "Pet Transport from Lesotho to Australia"
  overview: |
    South African Airways flies nonstop from Johannesburg to Perth eight times a week, on a four-engined Airbus A340-300 built for exactly this kind of long oceanic crossing. For most origin countries on this site, that's simply a useful fact about routing. For Lesotho, it happens to solve two separate problems at once.

    Lesotho doesn't appear on any of DAFF's approved country groups, so 180 consecutive days of residency somewhere approved come first, no matter the destination. Every pet leaving Lesotho already starts that journey the same way, on Airlink's short Maseru-to-Johannesburg hop, since Moshoeshoe I International Airport has no other scheduled destination.

    South Africa itself is DAFF Group 3 approved. So the residency clock, the vaccination, the titre test, and eventually the flight to Australia can all run through a single neighbouring country, rather than adding a long-haul detour to Europe or Asia purely to satisfy DAFF's rule.
  sections:
    - heading: "Why South Africa is also DAFF Group 3 approved, not just convenient"
      body: |
        It would be easy to assume South Africa works here purely because it's next door. That's true, but it isn't the whole reason. South Africa sits on DAFF's Group 3 list in its own right, alongside the UK, the US and most EU states, meaning a pet resident there for the qualifying period follows a well-established pathway rather than an improvised one.

        That combination, geographic necessity and genuine DAFF approval landing on the same country, doesn't happen for every non-approved origin. Compare a Balkan or Central Asian origin, where the natural first flight and the DAFF-approved staging country are usually two different places entirely.
    - heading: "The titre test still applies, wherever the residency happens"
      body: |
        South Africa's Group 3 status skips nothing on the regulatory side. A rabies vaccination, a 30-day wait, and an RNATT blood draw at a DAFF-approved South African laboratory are all required during the residency, exactly as they would be if the same 180 days were spent in the UK instead.

        What South Africa does remove is the extra flight. There's no need to book a long-haul sector purely to reach an approved country, since the Airlink hop to Johannesburg that every Lesotho pet takes anyway already puts the animal inside one.
    - heading: "Closing the loop with a flight that doesn't exist for most origins"
      body: |
        Once the 180 days and the titre test clear, South African Airways' Johannesburg-Perth nonstop, roughly 11 hours on an Airbus A340-300, is the practical way to finish the journey. Eight flights a week is a solid frequency for a route this long, though it means checking the schedule against Mickleham's weekday-only arrival rule well before booking.

        Most non-approved origins on this site end up routing their final Australia-bound leg through the UK or a European hub. Lesotho's routing through South Africa gets there just as legally, on a shorter overall itinerary.
