    Vietnam stands out among Southeast Asian destinations for UK pet owners: no quarantine on arrival, no titre test required, and no import permit needed for up to two pets. The comparison with Thailand (30-day quarantine, titre test) or Malaysia (30-day quarantine, titre test) is striking.     - heading: "What the DAGAH health certificate needs to cover"
      body: |
        The DAGAH (Department of Animal Health) is the Vietnamese authority that inspects imported pets on arrival. DAGAH inspectors at Noi Bai (Hanoi) and Tan Son Nhat (Ho Chi Minh City) check the AHC against the animal in front of them.

        The AHC must record: the ISO microchip number and confirmation that the chip was implanted before the rabies vaccination; current rabies vaccination details (vaccine name, batch, date, expiry); core vaccine history; and a statement that the pet was clinically examined and found healthy within 10 days of the planned Vietnam arrival date.

        DAGAH may ask for the original document. Carry the original AHC with APHA endorsement stamp as a physical document in your carry-on or with the cargo paperwork, not stored electronically. If any detail on the certificate is incorrect or missing, the DAGAH inspector can refuse entry.

        For moves with more than 2 pets, contact the DAGAH or MARD in advance. The permit-free limit of 2 pets is the standard rule, but the process for additional animals can be arranged with advance notice and preparation.
