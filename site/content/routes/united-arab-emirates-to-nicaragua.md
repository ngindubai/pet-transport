content:
  h1: "Pet Transport from United Arab Emirates to Nicaragua"
  overview: |
    IPSA doesn't ask a rabies titre test of a pet entering Nicaragua, whatever country it left. That's the reverse of the pathway the same animal would face travelling the other way, since the UAE requires a titre result from any Nicaragua-origin pet under MOCCAE's rules. A UAE-origin dog or cat needs a current vaccination, a MOCCAE health certificate and a flight, nothing more exotic than that.

    What Nicaragua's process lacks in blood tests, it makes up for in thin documentation. There's no published UAE-specific checklist the way there is for pets arriving from the United States, so confirming translation and legalisation requirements directly with IPSA is worth doing early.

  sections:
    - heading: "What the UAE asks before departure"
      body: |
        MOCCAE keeps the export side simple. There's no separate export permit for a personal pet, and companion animals are explicitly carved out of the requirement for a certificate of origin or proof of the destination country's own import approval. What matters is the health certificate itself: a physical exam by a UAE-licensed vet, issued within 30 days of travel, confirming the microchip and vaccination records line up.

        That certificate then has to work for Nicaragua's side of the process, and this is where the route gets less straightforward than the paperwork alone suggests.

    - heading: "The asymmetry: no titre test needed to land in Nicaragua"
      body: |
        It's worth stating plainly, because it surprises people moving in this direction: Nicaragua does not require a rabies titre test from any country of origin. A pet leaving the UAE skips the FAVN blood test, the 21-day post-vaccination wait, and the lab turnaround that would apply travelling the other way. IPSA's general rules ask for a current rabies vaccination, no more than 365 days old at shipment, and that's essentially it on the medical side.

        This isn't a UAE-specific concession. Nicaragua applies the same light-touch entry standard to pets from the United States, the UK, and everywhere else, based on IPSA's own published general requirements.

    - heading: "Routing: Dubai to Managua via Miami"
      body: |
        Emirates' new non-stop Dubai to Miami service, four times weekly on a Boeing 777-300ER, is the strongest first leg currently available. From Miami, American Airlines' daily non-stop to Managua completes the journey with just one handover.

        An alternative exists for owners who can't fit the Miami schedule: Dubai to Istanbul, then Turkish Airlines' Istanbul-Panama City service three times a week, followed by a Copa Airlines connection into Managua. It's a longer routing with an extra handover, and Copa's cargo desk only runs Monday to Thursday, so plan the timing carefully if this is your path.

    - heading: "The one open question: legalising the certificate"
      body: |
        Nicaragua's published general import requirements don't name a UAE-specific documentation pathway, unlike the well-worn US-Nicaragua route, where USDA's ink-signed certificate process is clearly set out. That gap doesn't mean anything is wrong, it just means the answer isn't published anywhere obvious.

        The safe approach is to contact IPSA's Ventanilla Unica directly before booking flights, and ask specifically whether a MOCCAE-issued certificate needs a Spanish translation, a consular stamp, or neither. Budget a little slack in the timeline for that answer to come back.
