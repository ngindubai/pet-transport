  overview: |
    Portugal has emerged as one of the top destinations for Americans seeking a European base, driven by the D7 passive income visa, the Non-Habitual Resident tax regime, and a lower cost of living compared to much of Western Europe. Portugal follows EU pet import rules, making the entry process consistent: no quarantine, no titre test, no import permit. The requirements are an ISO microchip, current rabies vaccination, and a USDA-endorsed health certificate issued within 10 days of arrival.

    TAP Air Portugal is the natural carrier choice because it flies direct from multiple US East Coast cities (JFK, Newark, Boston, Miami) to Lisbon and offers cargo services for larger pets. Small pets may travel in cabin on some TAP transatlantic routes; check availability at the time of booking. British Airways offers an indirect option via London Heathrow.

    DGAV (Direcao-Geral de Alimentacao e Veterinaria) handles border checks at Lisbon (LIS) and Porto (OPO). The inspection for compliant pets is brief and the pet is released on arrival day.

faqs:
  - question: "Is there quarantine for pets entering Portugal from the USA?"
    answer: "No. Portugal does not impose routine quarantine on pets from the USA. The DGAV border vet at Lisbon or Porto airport checks the USDA-endorsed health certificate, scans the microchip, and verifies the vaccination record. Compliant pets are cleared and released on arrival day."
  - question: "Does my dog need a titre test to enter Portugal from the USA?"
    answer: "No. The USA is listed as an approved third country under EU Regulation 2026/131. No titre test is required. The requirements are: ISO microchip (implanted before vaccination), current rabies vaccination (21-day wait after first dose), and a USDA-endorsed health certificate issued within 10 days of arrival in Portugal."
  - question: "Which airline should I use for my pet from the USA to Portugal?"
    answer: "TAP Air Portugal is the best starting point, with direct cargo services from JFK, Newark, Boston, and Miami to Lisbon. If you are based on the US West Coast, you will need to connect via an East Coast city or use British Airways via London. Confirm current live animal acceptance before booking, as cargo pet policies can change."
  - question: "Can my cat travel in cabin from New York to Lisbon?"
    answer: "TAP Air Portugal may allow small cats (under the cabin weight limit including carrier) on some transatlantic routes. Check TAP's current cabin pet policy for transatlantic services at the time of booking. If the cat is too large or the airline does not accept cabin pets on the transatlantic leg, cargo is the alternative. Portugal imposes no quarantine on cats from the USA."
  - question: "What documents do I need for my dog from the USA to Portugal?"
    answer: "You need: a USDA-endorsed official health certificate (from a USDA-accredited vet, issued within 10 days of Portuguese arrival); a readable ISO microchip (implanted before rabies vaccination); a current rabies vaccination with 21-day wait after first dose; and the USDA endorsement (USD 38 fee, 2-10 business days processing). No titre test, no import permit, no quarantine."
