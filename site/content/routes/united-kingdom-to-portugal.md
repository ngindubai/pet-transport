    Portugal has become one of Europe's most popular destinations for British expats, driven by the D7 passive income visa, the NHR tax regime, and a well-established English-speaking expat community in the Algarve, Lisbon, and Porto. The post-Brexit paperwork change is the AHC, which must be issued fresh for every trip.

    DGAV (Direcao-Geral de Alimentacao e Veterinaria) is Portugal's competent authority. Border vets at Lisbon (LIS) and Porto (OPO) carry out the document checks on arrival.

        Many of the UK nationals moving to Portugal under the D7 visa or via the NHR tax scheme are bringing pets for what they expect to be a permanent or long-term move. A few practical points for these moves:

faqs:
  - question: "Can I take my small dog or cat in the cabin from the UK to Portugal?"
    answer: "Yes. TAP Air Portugal accepts small pets (under 8 kg including the carrier) in the cabin on its UK-Portugal routes, including Heathrow to Lisbon and Heathrow to Porto. You book the cabin space directly with TAP at the time of your flight booking. This avoids the cargo system entirely for qualifying pets. Check TAP's current weight limit and availability when booking."
  - question: "Do I need a titre test to take my pet to Portugal from the UK?"
    answer: "No. A titre test is not required for pets travelling from the UK to Portugal. The UK is on the EU list of approved third countries under EU Regulation 2026/131. The requirements are: ISO microchip, current rabies vaccination (21-day wait after first dose), and a fresh AHC issued within 10 days of arrival in Portugal."
  - question: "Is there quarantine for pets entering Portugal from the UK?"
    answer: "No. Portugal imposes no routine quarantine for compliant pets arriving from the UK. The DGAV border vet at Lisbon or Porto airport checks the AHC, scans the microchip, and verifies the vaccination record. Compliant pets are cleared on arrival day."
  - question: "Can I use Ryanair or easyJet to transport my pet to Portugal?"
    answer: "No. Ryanair and easyJet do not accept pets in cabin or cargo on any route. If you are flying with one of these carriers, your pet will need a separate cargo booking through British Airways, TAP, or another full-service carrier on the same route."
  - question: "Once I am resident in Portugal, do I still need an AHC for future EU travel?"
    answer: "Once you are resident in Portugal and your pet is registered there, your Portuguese vet can issue an EU pet passport. An EU pet passport allows travel within EU member states without the need for a new AHC on every trip, as long as the pet's vaccinations remain current. For travel back to Great Britain, however, you will still need to follow UK Pet Travel Scheme rules, including the tapeworm treatment for dogs."
