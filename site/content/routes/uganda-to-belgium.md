      body: |
        Most East Africa to Europe pet corridors involve at least one connection. This one does not. Brussels Airlines operates Entebbe to Brussels non-stop, and the airline has a long-standing presence in East African cargo, which means live animal handling on this specific route is well established rather than improvised.

        That said, a direct flight only solves the transit part of the journey. It does nothing to shorten the 90-day wait or the MAAIF endorsement process, both of which run on their own clocks regardless of how the pet eventually travels.

    - heading: "FASFC and what happens at Brussels Airport"
      body: |
        The Federal Agency for the Safety of the Food Chain inspects every arriving pet and its paperwork at the BRU cargo terminal. A MAAIF-endorsed, EU-format health certificate has to be presented, current within its 10-day validity window, alongside proof of the titre test result and the full vaccination record.

        FASFC does not distinguish between a pet that arrived via a direct flight and one that connected through three airports. The documentation standard is identical either way, so treat the direct routing as a convenience for your pet's welfare in transit, not a reason to shortcut the paperwork.
