content:
  h1: "Pet Transport from Andorra to the United Kingdom"
  overview: |
    Two hundred kilometres separate Andorra from the nearest departure lounge. There's no commercial airport within the principality's borders, so every pet heading to Britain starts this move on a road, not a runway, usually to Barcelona-El Prat or Toulouse-Blagnac. The upside: Andorra sits in Part 1 of the UK's own listed-country table, the same group as Switzerland and Norway, which strips out the titre test and the 3-month wait that trip up owners moving from most of the world.

  sections:
    - heading: "Why there's no flight from Andorra itself"
      body: |
        Andorra-La Seu Airport (LEU) exists, technically, in the border town of La Seu d'Urgell, but its commercial schedule is thin and seasonal. In practice, owners use Barcelona-El Prat, about 200km and 2.5 to 3 hours by road, or Toulouse-Blagnac, a similar distance to the north. Barcelona has the deeper flight network into Heathrow and Gatwick; Toulouse suits owners in northern Andorra parishes closer to the French border, and connects onward through Paris.

        Whichever gateway you pick, the road transfer isn't an afterthought. It's the first leg of the journey, and it needs booking with the same care as the flight itself.

    - heading: "Part 1 status: what it removes, and what it doesn't"
      body: |
        Being listed in Part 1 of GB's retained Annex II country table matters more than it sounds. It means a current rabies vaccination, given after the microchip, is enough on its own. No blood draw, no 3-calendar-month wait. That single fact can save four to five months compared with an unlisted origin.

        It doesn't remove everything, though. Dogs still need the praziquantel tapeworm treatment in the 24 to 120 hours before landing, and the GB Pet Health Certificate still has to be issued within 10 days of arrival. Skip either and the pet gets turned back at the UK border, Part 1 status or not.

    - heading: "Getting the Andorran paperwork right"
      body: |
        Andorra's Ministeri de Medi Ambient, Agricultura i Sostenibilitat runs the country's veterinary service, and a registered Andorran vet issues the fitness-to-travel certificate and confirms the vaccination record. Because Andorra isn't in the EU, it doesn't issue EU pet passports through the same bureaucracy as, say, France, though it recognises the EU format under its Regulation 577/2013 Part 1 listing.

        The practical effect is that owners often find less written guidance online than they'd get from a French or Spanish vet. A short call to the ministry, or to the vet handling the paperwork, before booking flights is worth the ten minutes it takes.

    - heading: "Barcelona or Toulouse: which gateway to pick"
      body: |
        Barcelona wins on frequency. British Airways, Vueling and easyJet between them run dozens of weekly flights to London, and IAG Cargo's live-animal handling at BCN is well established. Toulouse is quieter, but Air France's connection through Paris-Charles de Gaulle works well for owners already routing that way, and the drive from northern Andorra parishes like Canillo can be shorter than the run to Barcelona.

        Neither airport takes bookings for a live animal casually. Space is limited on most short-haul European routes, and a booking made without confirming a cargo slot first is a booking that can fall through.
