  h1: "Pet Transport from Germany to Macau"
  overview: |
    Lufthansa Cargo's freighters leave Frankfurt for Hong Kong nearly every day of the week, an MD-11F six times over and a Boeing 777F added on Saturdays through its partner AeroLogic. Getting a crate on one of those flights is rarely the bottleneck on this route. The three-month wait for a titre test result is.

    Germany's status as a rabies-free EU member counts for a great deal inside the bloc. It counts for nothing on Macau's short exemption list, the same position this site has already documented for France. A healthy dog with a spotless German vaccination record still sits through the full unlisted-country pathway, and IAM's own quarantine rule change from June 2026 only takes effect once that pathway is complete.

  sections:
    - heading: "Frequency doesn't shortcut the paperwork"
      body: |
        Lufthansa Cargo's Frankfurt-Hong Kong sector runs close to daily, split between an MD-11F six times a week and a Saturday Boeing 777F rotation flown by AeroLogic. That's more schedule flexibility than a lot of European routes on this site get, and it means booking cargo space is rarely what holds up a Germany-to-Macau move.

        What does hold it up is the titre test. Germany isn't on Macau's short list of rabies-free exempt origins, so blood has to be drawn at least 30 days after the rabies vaccination, at an EU-approved laboratory, with a result of 0.5 IU/mL or above. From that draw date, not the vaccination date, roughly three months pass before IAM will clear the pet for entry.

        A microchip has to go in before the vaccination, not after. German vets handle this sequence routinely, but it's worth confirming your Amtstierarzt is briefed on the EU non-commercial movement format IAM expects, not just a general export certificate.

    - heading: "German export bureaucracy has its own names"
      body: |
        There's no formal export permit as such. What matters is the pre-export clinical check by an authorised veterinarian, followed by endorsement from the Landesamt or Kreisveterinäramt, Germany's state or district veterinary office, operating under the federal food and agriculture ministry, BMEL. That's a different chain of offices than France's DDPP system, worth knowing if you've read this site's France-to-Macau page and assumed the same names apply here.

        The resulting certificate gets reformatted to match IAM's own health certificate model, referenced as PS-1086, before it travels with the pet.

    - heading: "What changed in Macau on 12 June 2026, and what didn't"
      body: |
        IAM removed the standing 30-day quarantine for compliant dogs and cats from 12 June 2026 onward. A pet that clears four conditions, microchip, current rabies vaccination, a passing titre result and an official health certificate, walks through an on-arrival inspection instead of a holding pen.

        Walks through, not skips. An IAM veterinary surgeon still checks the paperwork against the animal at the point of entry, and the Import Licence for Live Animals, PS-1092, still has to be applied for through the Macao SAR Government Portal and used within 30 days of issue. Time that application against a confirmed flight date, not the other way round.
