    - heading: "Prague to Tallinn: the hub connection"
      body: |
        Prague Václav Havel (PRG) has no scheduled direct cargo flights to Tallinn (TLL). The two most practical connecting options are:

        Via Warsaw (WAW) on LOT Polish Airlines: LOT connects Prague to Warsaw, from which LOT operates service to Tallinn. Warsaw Chopin is a major hub with established live animal cargo handling. LOT Cargo manages the booking across both legs. For owners moving a large dog, this is often the cleaner option as LOT handles the full journey.

        Via Riga (RIX) on airBaltic: airBaltic connects Prague to Tallinn via Riga, which is the airline's home hub. airBaltic Cargo at Riga is well-equipped and the RIX to TLL sector is a short hop. For cabin pets, either routing works within standard carrier policies.

        If your dog is small enough for the cabin, Ryanair may offer a direct or near-direct Prague to Tallinn service on some schedules. For anything in the cargo hold, LOT or airBaltic is the route to take.

    - heading: "EU pet passport: the one document you need"
      body: |
        For a Czech pet moving to Estonia, the EU pet passport is the only travel document required. It must be issued or updated by an authorised vet in the Czech Republic. The SVA (Státní veterinární správa) governs the authorisation of vets to issue EU pet passports.

        The passport must show: the ISO 15-digit microchip number recorded before the rabies vaccination entry, and a current rabies vaccination with valid dates. That sequence, microchip first then vaccination, is the most important compliance point. Passports where the vaccination appears before the microchip are technically non-compliant and can cause problems at border checks.

        Estonia's VTA (Veterinaar- ja Toiduamet) accepts EU pet passports from Czech Republic in the same way as any other EU member state. The entry process is routine for a compliant arrival: microchip scan matched to passport, vaccination dates confirmed. No advance notification is required.

    answer: "Via Warsaw on LOT is generally the cleaner option for cargo. LOT handles the live animal booking across both legs and Warsaw Chopin has a solid cargo setup.   - question: "Can I use a Czech EU pet passport issued several years ago, or does it need to be renewed?"
    answer: "The EU pet passport is a permanent document and does not expire. What matters is that the rabies vaccination recorded in it is current. If the vaccination has lapsed, have your vet update the passport with a new vaccination. The microchip number and other fixed details do not need to be re-entered."
  - question: "Does VTA at Tallinn Airport check every pet on arrival, or is it occasional?"
    answer: "VTA spot checks are occasional for intra-EU arrivals from low-risk countries. For a Czech pet arriving in Estonia with a valid EU pet passport and current rabies vaccination, the process is straightforward if a check does occur. Carry the passport and be prepared to show it."
