  sections:
    - heading: "Entry Requirements for Denmark"
      body: |
        Denmark follows EU Regulation 576/2013 for non-commercial pet movements. A pet arriving from Slovenia needs a valid EU pet passport showing an ISO 11784/11785 microchip implanted before or on the same day as the first rabies vaccination, and a current rabies vaccination at least 21 days old after the primary dose. No titre test is needed because Slovenia is an EU member state. No import permit is required. The Fodevarestyrelsen (Danish Veterinary and Food Administration) governs animal imports; their guidance is at fvst.dk. Denmark also has breed-specific legislation, so if you own a restricted breed it is worth checking the Danish list before booking.

    - heading: "Routing and Airlines from Ljubljana to Copenhagen"
      body: |
        Ljubljana (LJU) to Copenhagen (CPH) requires a connecting flight via a Central European hub. The two most established cargo routes are Lufthansa via Frankfurt (FRA) and KLM via Amsterdam (AMS). Both Frankfurt and Amsterdam have dedicated live animal cargo facilities with experienced handlers. For small pets travelling in-cabin, some carriers offer cabin acceptance on the first leg out of LJU, but you will need to confirm whether the onward leg from the hub to CPH also allows in-cabin travel. SAS, as Denmark's national carrier, is also worth exploring for connections from hub airports. Book cargo at least 8 to 12 weeks in advance due to the limited live animal capacity at Ljubljana Airport.

faqs:
  - question: "Do I need a titre test to take my pet from Slovenia to Denmark?"
    answer: "No. A rabies antibody titre test is only needed when entering Denmark from a country not on the EU listed countries register. Slovenia is an EU member state, so the standard EU pet passport with a current rabies vaccination is sufficient. Fodevarestyrelsen confirms this at fvst.dk."
  - question: "Are there any breed restrictions for dogs entering Denmark?"
    answer: "Yes. Denmark has national breed-specific legislation that prohibits or restricts certain dog breeds. The restrictions are applied at the border regardless of where the dog has come from. Check the Fodevarestyrelsen website (fvst.dk) before travel if your dog is a breed that may be affected."
  - question: "How do I ship a large dog from Ljubljana to Copenhagen when there are no direct flights?"
    answer: "The most practical cargo route is Lufthansa via Frankfurt (FRA) to Copenhagen (CPH). You book the full journey as a single live animal cargo shipment through Lufthansa or a licensed pet transport agent. The agent coordinates the documentation handover at FRA and ensures the animal is correctly transferred to the CPH-bound leg. Alternatively, KLM via Amsterdam (AMS) is a well-used cargo route with similar facilities."
  - question: "What is the health certificate window for travel from Slovenia?"
    answer: "The health check carried out by your official vet in Slovenia should be completed no more than 10 days before departure, in line with EU pet travel rules. The EU pet passport itself remains valid as long as vaccinations are current. Ensure your vet signs and dates all entries correctly before you leave."
