  sections:
    - heading: "Flying non-stop: KLM's Amsterdam to Mexico City service"
      body: |
        A direct flight changes the maths on a route like this. No connection means no second airline's pet policy to check, no risk of a missed hub transfer with a live animal aboard, and one set of documents to present rather than two.

        Small pets under 8kg including the carrier can travel in KLM's cabin. Everything larger goes through KLM Cargo, in the same pressurised and temperature-controlled hold as the aircraft's checked baggage. Ten to fourteen weekly departures gives more scheduling flexibility than most European capitals get on this corridor. Aeromexico also sells seats on this route as a codeshare partner, so double-check which airline's ground crew actually handles your pet at Schiphol.

        Book the pet space as early as you book the flight itself. Cargo capacity for live animals is limited per departure and airlines will not guarantee a slot at short notice.
    - heading: "NVWA paperwork and what SENASICA wants on landing"
      body: |
        There's no export permit to apply for. What you need is a health certificate issued by a Dutch vet who is authorised under the NVWA framework, recording the microchip number, rabies vaccination date and a statement that the animal is fit to travel.

        On the Mexico side, SENASICA (Mexico's national agri-food health service) inspects the pet at the airport rather than processing paperwork in advance. Present the health certificate, the vaccination record and the completed FF-SENASICA-003 form together. If the documents are complete and the animal looks healthy, clearance is usually a matter of thirty to sixty minutes. No import permit application is needed beforehand, which shortens the admin compared with some Latin American destinations.
faqs:
  - question: "Is there a direct flight for pets from the Netherlands to Mexico?"
    answer: "Yes. KLM operates a non-stop Amsterdam Schiphol to Mexico City service, roughly ten to fourteen times a week, using the Boeing 787. Small pets can travel in the cabin under weight and carrier limits; larger pets travel with KLM Cargo on the same route."
  - question: "Do I need an import permit before flying my pet into Mexico?"
    answer: "No. Mexico's SENASICA does not require an advance import permit for a personal companion pet. Clearance happens at the airport on arrival, based on the health certificate, rabies vaccination proof and the completed FF-SENASICA-003 form."
  - question: "Does my pet need a rabies titre test to enter Mexico from the Netherlands?"
    answer: "No. Mexico does not require a FAVN or any other rabies antibody titre test for pets arriving from the Netherlands. A current rabies vaccination, given at least 30 days before entry, is what's required."
  - question: "How long is the health certificate valid for Mexico entry?"
    answer: "Where the certificate doesn't state its own expiry, Mexico treats it as valid for 15 days from issue to arrival. Time the vet appointment against your confirmed flight date, allowing a short buffer in case of delay."
  - question: "Will my pet need to quarantine on arrival in Mexico?"
    answer: "No, not if the documentation is complete. SENASICA carries out a physical inspection at the port of entry rather than a quarantine period. Pets without correct paperwork can be held, so get the health certificate and import form right before you fly."
  - question: "What happens if KLM's cabin pet slots are full?"
    answer: "Book the cabin allocation as early as possible, since only a small number of pets are permitted per flight. If it's sold out, KLM Cargo or Air France's cargo service via Paris are the practical alternatives, both of which carry pets on this corridor."
