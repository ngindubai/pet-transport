  sections:
    - heading: "Who moves from the UK to Belgium?"
      body: |
        Brussels is home to the EU institutions, NATO headquarters, and a large international community. Families relocating for work at the European Commission, NATO, or in the corporate sector frequently need to bring pets.

        This is not a tourist-style pet move. Many owners relocating to Belgium are making a multi-year commitment, and the pet paperwork needs to be done properly rather than casually. The AHC process is manageable: one vet appointment in the final 10 days before travel, using an APHA-authorised official vet. The certificate covers your pet for that entry and for up to 4 months of onward EU travel.

    - heading: "Belgium's regional breed rules"
      body: |
        Belgium has no single national law on dog breed restrictions. The three regions (Flemish, Walloon, and Brussels-Capital) each have their own legislation. Rules in one region may not apply in another.

        Broadly, the legislation in all three regions targets Pit Bull-type dogs and certain similar breeds. Before importing a dog of a restricted type, confirm with FASFC or the relevant regional authority whether the breed is permitted in the region where you will be living and working.

        Note that Belgium also hosts a number of international schools and office parks where specific landlord or housing association rules may apply to pet ownership, regardless of what national or regional law says.

faqs:
  - question: "Does my pet need a rabies titre test to enter Belgium from the UK?"
    answer: "No. Belgium applies EU rules and the UK is listed as an approved third country. No titre test is required for UK pets entering Belgium. You need the AHC, a current ISO microchip (implanted before the rabies vaccination), and a valid rabies vaccination (at least 21 days from the primary course)."
  - question: "How many times can I use the same AHC for travel between the UK and Belgium?"
    answer: "An AHC covers one entry from the UK into the EU. After your pet enters Belgium, the AHC is valid for up to 4 months of travel within EU member states. It cannot be reused for a subsequent trip from the UK. Each UK-to-EU journey needs a new AHC."
  - question: "Are there breed restrictions in Belgium?"
    answer: "Yes, but they are set at regional level rather than nationally. The Flemish, Walloon, and Brussels-Capital regions each have separate legislation, generally targeting Pit Bull-type dogs. Research the rules for your specific destination region. If you have a breed that could be classified as a Pit Bull type, seek confirmation from the relevant regional authority before making the move."
  - question: "Can I bring my pet by train through the Channel Tunnel?"
    answer: "Eurostar does not accept pets in passenger cars. The Eurotunnel Le Shuttle vehicle service is a separate product that allows cars (and passengers with pets in their car) to use the tunnel. However, this is a road journey rather than a Belgian air cargo service, and the distance from Calais to Brussels still needs covering by road. For most people flying from London to Brussels, air cargo is the practical solution."
