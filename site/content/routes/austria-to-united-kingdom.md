    - heading: "Austria's Bundesland breed rules"
      body: |
        Austria is a federal state and each of the nine Bundeslaender sets its own breed restrictions. Vienna, Lower Austria, Styria, and others differ on which breeds need special permits, muzzle requirements, or cannot be kept at all. This patchwork means the rules for your specific Austrian state need to be checked, not just a national rule.

        For export to Great Britain, Austria's state-level rules affect what documentation you need to travel out of Austria. When you arrive in Great Britain, UK law (the Dangerous Dogs Act 1991) takes over. Great Britain prohibits four types: Pit Bull Terrier, Japanese Tosa, Dogo Argentino, and Fila Brasileiro. If you own one of these types, specific legal advice is needed before attempting UK entry.

faqs:
  - question: "Does my pet need a titre test to travel from Austria to the UK?"
    answer: "No. Austria is a Part 1 listed country under APHA rules. No rabies titre test is required for pets from Austria entering Great Britain. You need the AHC, a valid ISO microchip (implanted before the rabies vaccination), and a valid rabies vaccination (at least 21 days from the primary course). Dogs also need the tapeworm treatment recorded in the AHC."
  - question: "What are the breed restrictions in Austria?"
    answer: "Austria sets breed restrictions at the state (Bundesland) level, so the rules depend on which Austrian state your dog is registered in. Common restrictions across several states target Pit Bull-type dogs, American Staffordshire Terriers, and Rottweilers, with requirements varying from special permits to muzzle requirements to outright bans. Check with your specific state's authority (Amt der Landesregierung) for the current rules."
  - question: "Which airline should I use for a large dog from Vienna to London?"
    answer: "Austrian Airlines and British Airways both offer cargo pet services on the Vienna to London Heathrow route. Austrian Airlines is part of the Lufthansa Group and uses a consolidated cargo service. For a large dog requiring an IATA-compliant crate, a cargo booking through one of these carriers is the right approach. Contact the airlines directly or use a pet transport agent to coordinate the booking and paperwork."
  - question: "How close to my travel date does the tapeworm treatment need to be?"
    answer: "For dogs entering Great Britain, the treatment must be given between 24 and 120 hours before arrival in Great Britain (not before you leave Vienna). On a direct Vienna to London flight of about 2.5 hours, a treatment given the day before or the morning of travel will typically fall within the window. The treatment and timing must be recorded in the AHC."
