  overview: |
    Twenty-eight days: that's how far back from the US arrival date the CDC counts its serology testing window, and it's the number that shapes everything else on this route. Uzbekistan sits on the agency's high-risk list for dog rabies, current as of its 15 April 2026 update, so a titre test, the CDC Dog Import Form, and a landing at a CDC-registered animal care facility all apply in full.

    None of that is optional or negotiable at the border. Plan for 16 to 24 weeks from a standing start, most of it spent on the vaccination-to-blood-draw sequence and the CDC facility reservation rather than on Uzbekistan's own, comparatively lighter export process.

  sections:
    - heading: "Why Uzbekistan sits on the CDC's high-risk list"
      body: |
        The CDC updates its high-risk countries for dog rabies list periodically, and Uzbekistan has remained on it through the current 15 April 2026 revision. That single classification drives the entire US-side requirement set: a serology test at a CDC-approved laboratory showing at least 0.5 IU/ml, blood drawn no sooner than 30 days after vaccination and no later than 28 days before the dog actually lands in the US.

        Cats face none of this. The CDC's rabies import rules apply to dogs only, so a cat travelling from Uzbekistan needs current vaccination and a standard airline health certificate, nothing more on the federal side.

    - heading: "Booking cargo around the CDC facility reservation"
      body: |
        A dog can only enter the US at an airport with a CDC-registered animal care facility, and that reservation needs to be locked in before the flight is booked, not after. Turkish Airlines' Istanbul connection offers the widest choice of eligible US gateways given the daily frequency out of Tashkent; Uzbekistan Airways' direct New York service is convenient when the schedule lines up, but its limited weekly frequency makes it a poor fit if the CDC's 28-day window is tight.
