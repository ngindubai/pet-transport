  sections:
    - heading: "UK entry requirements for pets from Georgia: the complete picture"
      body: |
        Georgia is not on the UK's approved country list. The full protocol applies: ISO microchip implanted before vaccination, current rabies vaccination, titre test at an EU/UK approved laboratory (blood drawn 30+ days post-vaccination, result at least 0.5 IU/ml), 3-month wait from blood draw date, tapeworm treatment for dogs 24-120 hours before UK arrival, and Georgia National Food Agency-endorsed health certificate valid 10 days.

        The 3-month wait is calculated from the date blood was drawn, recorded on the laboratory certificate. This date is fixed and cannot be shortened by any means.

        Source: UK APHA, gov.uk/bring-pet-to-great-britain.
    - heading: "Getting the titre test done in or near Georgia"
      body: |
        The rabies titre test must be performed at an EU/UK approved laboratory. Georgia itself may not have an approved laboratory, which means blood samples often need to be shipped to a lab in Turkey, Germany, or another approved country.

        Your vet draws the blood in Tbilisi and ships it in appropriate cold-chain packaging to the approved lab. Allow 2-3 weeks for sample transit and results return. The 3-month wait starts from the date blood was drawn, not the date results arrived, so dispatch the sample quickly.

        Some IPATA agents in the Caucasus region maintain relationships with specific approved labs and can advise on the fastest option. This is one area where an agent saves meaningful time.
