  overview: |
    A dog cannot legally enter the Maldives, full stop. A cat, on the other hand, can be on a nonstop Singapore Airlines flight to Male in under five hours, flights SQ432 and SQ438, roughly 14 times a week. Of the four countries covered in this batch of new route pages, this is the only one with a genuine direct, single-carrier connection to Singapore.

    That doesn't make the paperwork simple, just short. A cat needs a microchip, rabies vaccination, core feline vaccines, and a health certificate signed within 10 days of travel, on top of Singapore's own AVS export process. What's thinner is official guidance on Maldivian permits and titre testing, which this site flags honestly rather than guessing at.

  sections:
    - heading: "The dog question, settled first"
      body: |
        There's no age limit, no extra document and no assistance-animal carve-out that gets a dog into the Maldives. It sits on the Maldives Customs Service's prohibited imports list, alongside items like alcohol and pork products, with no standard pet-owner exception. If a service dog is genuinely involved, get written confirmation directly from the Maldivian government rather than assuming a general pet transport process applies to something the rule doesn't allow.

    - heading: "What a cat needs, and where the guidance runs thin"
      body: |
        Microchip first, rabies vaccination second, given at least 30 days before arrival, plus viral rhinotracheitis, calicivirus, panleukopenia and feline leukaemia cover. Close the file with a health certificate signed within 10 days of departure. None of it demands the multi-month wait a titre test route would add.

        Some sources describe no import permit requirement at all for a travelling pet cat; others suggest a general permit applies to all pet imports. There's no single current government page that settles this cleanly, so treat it as an open item for your vet or agent to confirm directly with Maldivian authorities, not a fact to build a flight date around.
