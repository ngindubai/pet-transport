content:
  h1: "Pet Transport from Trinidad and Tobago to the USA: 2026 Guide"
  overview: |
    Since 1 August 2024, a dog leaving Piarco for Miami needs exactly one US government document: the CDC Dog Import Form. Trinidad and Tobago has never appeared on the CDC's high-risk list for dog rabies, so none of the titre tests, government-endorsed certificates or CDC facility reservations that complicate routes from high-risk Caribbean neighbours apply here.

    Cats have it easier still. There is no CDC paperwork for cats at all. The real planning work on this route sits with the airline, not the government, because Caribbean Airlines and American Airlines handle pets differently, and one of those two options has quietly closed to most civilian passengers.

  sections:
    - heading: "Why Trinidad and Tobago skips the high-risk pathway"
      body: |
        The CDC only publishes a list of high-risk countries; everywhere else, including Trinidad and Tobago, is treated as dog-rabies-free or low-risk by default. That single classification decision removes the serology titer, the government-endorsed certification form and the CDC-registered facility reservation that dogs from the Dominican Republic or several other Caribbean origins still need.

        In practice this means a healthy dog at least 6 months old, carrying an ISO microchip and a completed CDC Dog Import Form, can travel to any US airport, seaport or land border crossing without further CDC involvement. The form itself is free and its confirmation receipt stays valid for six months, provided the dog has not visited a high-risk country in the meantime.

    - heading: "Choosing between Caribbean Airlines and American Airlines"
      body: |
        Caribbean Airlines still accepts dogs and cats as ordinary checked baggage in the hold on its nonstop Piarco to Miami and Piarco to JFK services, up to 70 lb combined pet and crate weight, with 72 hours' notice requested. For most pet owners this remains the simpler booking.

        American Airlines has moved differently. Checked baggage for live animals on American is now limited to active-duty US military and State Department Foreign Service personnel travelling on official orders, so a civilian owner flying American from Piarco to Miami needs to book through American Airlines Cargo's PetEmbark service instead, a separate cargo booking rather than an add-on to a passenger ticket.
