  h1: "Pet Transport from Azerbaijan to Sweden"
  overview: |
    Azerbaijan to Sweden is a five to six month process. Azerbaijan is EU-unlisted, so the full EU protocol applies: microchip first, vaccination, FAVN titre test, 90-day wait, and Ministry of Agriculture-endorsed EU health certificate.

  sections:
    - heading: "Sweden's requirements for Azerbaijani pets"
      body: |
        Sweden applies EU Regulation 576/2013 for pets from unlisted third countries. Azerbaijan is unlisted. The steps: ISO microchip before vaccination, rabies vaccination, FAVN titre test at EU-approved laboratory (minimum 0.5 IU/ml, blood drawn 30+ days after vaccination), 90-day wait from blood draw date, and Azerbaijani Ministry of Agriculture-endorsed EU health certificate valid 10 days.

        Jordbruksverket (jordbruksverket.se) inspects incoming pets at Stockholm Arlanda (ARN) cargo terminal. Compliant arrivals are released without quarantine.

    - heading: "AZAL and Turkish Airlines: two viable paths"
      body: |
        AZAL (Azerbaijan Airlines) operates from Heydar Aliyev International Airport (GYD, Baku) to Frankfurt (FRA) and potentially other European hubs. If AZAL Cargo accepts live animals on the GYD-FRA segment, this route connects to Stockholm via a short FRA-to-ARN leg on Lufthansa or SAS. The advantage is keeping the main long-haul leg with the Azerbaijani national carrier.

        Turkish Airlines via Istanbul (IST) is the simpler single-carrier option. GYD to IST to ARN on Turkish Airlines Cargo is a well-established routing. Temperature embargoes during Turkish summer months apply.

        Finnish and Polish hub options (Finnair via Helsinki, LOT via Warsaw) are alternatives that work particularly well for Sweden-bound cargo given the geographical proximity of HEL and WAW to ARN.

    - heading: "Titre test logistics from Baku"
      body: |
        FAVN titre testing must be done at an EU-approved laboratory. Check with your vet in Baku whether approved labs are accessible locally or in a nearby country such as Georgia. If a local or regional option exists, it saves international cold-chain shipping and speeds up result turnaround.

        If blood must be shipped internationally, factor two to four weeks for shipping and laboratory processing. The 90-day wait starts from the blood draw date in Baku, so these weeks are absorbed into the waiting period rather than extending the overall timeline.
