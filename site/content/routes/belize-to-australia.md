content:
  h1: "Pet Transport from Belize to Australia"
  overview: |
    Australia will not accept a pet import filed directly from Belize. DAFF classifies Belize as Non-Approved, the same category as the rest of Central America, which rules out any version of this move that skips a staging country first.

    What makes the Belize case a little heavier than some of its regional neighbours is what happens before the staging clock even starts. Belize is currently classed high-risk for dog rabies by the CDC and sits on the current New World Screwworm affected-country list, so a US-staged move means clearing that entire process, titre test and screwworm certificate included, before the 180-day Australian residency period can begin. Realistically, that puts the whole route at 40 to 53 weeks from a standing start.

  sections:
    - heading: "Two clocks, not one, if you're staging via the US"
      body: |
        Most owners researching this route assume the 180-day residency requirement is the only real delay. For a Belize-origin pet staging through the United States, it isn't. Belize's own CDC high-risk classification and its screwworm-affected status mean the pet needs to clear the full Belize-to-US process first, the titre test, the CDC Dog Import Form, the screwworm inspection certificate, before it's even legally resident in a country DAFF will recognise.

        That first stage alone runs 9 to 12 weeks. Only once it's complete does the 180-day Australian residency clock start counting, and DAFF requires the vaccination and RNATT titre sequence to run inside that residency period too, not before it.

    - heading: "Why Los Angeles or Miami still makes sense as the starting point"
      body: |
        Despite the extra layer, staging via the US remains the practical choice for most Belize owners, mainly because American Airlines flies direct into both Los Angeles and Miami from Belize City. Neither city is DAFF-approved on its own, obviously, residency has to happen somewhere in the country, but the flight connection itself is more direct than routing a Belize-origin pet through, say, Singapore.

        Singapore is a DAFF Group 2 country and would skip the RNATT titre test that a US or UK staging period still requires for the Australia leg. It comes at the cost of a far less convenient flight out of Belize City in the first place, so weigh the saved months against the harder journey to actually get there.

    - heading: "Mickleham, the fixed final step"
      body: |
        Wherever the staging period happens, the route ends the same way for every pet entering Australia: a mandatory minimum 10-day stay at Mickleham Post Entry Quarantine Facility near Melbourne, the country's only approved facility for imported cats and dogs. Book the slot as soon as the staging country's own export certificate date is confirmed, and plan the final flight to land on a weekday, since Mickleham doesn't take weekend arrivals.
