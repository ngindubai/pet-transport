content:
  h1: "Pet Transport from Switzerland to the Dominican Republic"
  overview: |
    Relocating a dog or cat from Switzerland to the Dominican Republic involves a manageable set of requirements from both the Swiss and Dominican sides. Switzerland's export process goes through FSVO/BLV (Bundesamt fur Lebensmittelsicherheit und Veterinarwesen). Your health certificate must be issued by a BLV-authorised official vet and then endorsed by BLV. This is distinct from a standard private vet examination, so finding and booking an appointment with the right official vet is an early step.

    The Dominican Republic's DGAG (Direccion General de Ganaderia) requires a microchip, current core vaccinations, and a rabies vaccination given between 30 days and 12 months before entry. Up to five personal pets can arrive without an import permit. There is no quarantine; DGAG veterinarians inspect pets on arrival at Las Americas International Airport (SDQ) in Santo Domingo. With correct documents, the inspection is quick.

    Switzerland does not have a direct long-haul cargo route to Santo Domingo, so pets travel via Madrid, Paris, or Amsterdam. All three options are well served from both Zurich (ZRH) and Geneva (GVA). Booking cargo space early is advisable, particularly in summer when demand is high.

  sections:
    - heading: "Switzerland's BLV Export Certificate Process"
      body: |
        Switzerland is not an EU member state, which means its official export health certificate is issued under Swiss federal veterinary authority rather than EU formats. The FSVO/BLV administers this. You need a BLV-authorised official vet, referred to in Switzerland as an amtlicher Tierarzt, to carry out the clinical examination and issue the certificate. Your regular private vet cannot sign this document. Contact your cantonal veterinary office to find the correct official vet for your area.

        After the examination, FSVO/BLV must endorse the certificate, which typically takes one working day. The certificate is valid for 10 days from issue for most destinations, but the Dominican Republic accepts one issued within 15 days of travel. This gives a slightly more comfortable window. All the same, book the appointment early enough that the BLV endorsement step does not push you up against the travel date.

    - heading: "What to Expect at Las Americas Airport in Santo Domingo"
      body: |
        DGAG veterinarians at SDQ carry out the import inspection in the cargo arrivals area. They will scan the microchip, check it against the health certificate, and verify vaccination dates. Dogs travelling to the Dominican Republic must have a current DHPP vaccination; cats must be vaccinated against panleukopenia, rhinotracheitis, and calicivirus. The BLV-endorsed health certificate must be the original document, not a copy.

        Inspection at SDQ is generally efficient when documents are complete. The DGAG fee is nominal and collected on the day. If you are using a connecting flight, confirm with your airline that the live animal cargo will be available in the international arrivals cargo hall rather than in transit. Some airlines require advance notification to ensure cargo is not inadvertently forwarded.
