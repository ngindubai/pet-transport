  overview: |
    Most codeshare arrangements between a long-haul carrier and a regional African airline get described in general terms, a partnership covering "southern and eastern Africa" or similar. Emirates' agreement with Airlink is more specific than that: it names Lilongwe directly as a destination reachable through the partnership, an unusually explicit detail for a secondary city this far from Dubai.

    Malawi's own entry rules don't reward that convenience with anything harder to satisfy. No titre test, from any country of origin, just a vaccination record, a microchip, and an import permit from the Director of Animal Health at DAHLD. The UAE gets no exemption from that permit requirement, since Malawi reserves the waiver for its SADC neighbours.

    Ethiopian Airlines via Addis Ababa runs alongside the Emirates-Airlink option as a genuine second choice. Budget ten to fourteen weeks, with the DAHLD permit application driving most of that timeline rather than anything on the UAE export side.

  sections:
    - heading: "A codeshare that actually names the city"
      body: |
        Emirates flies Dubai to Johannesburg four times daily, and its codeshare partnership with Airlink specifically lists Lilongwe among the destinations that arrangement covers. That's worth noting because it's more concrete than the general regional coverage language codeshare agreements often use, and it gives an owner or agent something firmer to book against than a vague partnership description.

        Ethiopian Airlines offers a real alternative rather than a fallback: its own Addis Ababa-Lilongwe service, fed by up to four daily Dubai-Addis Ababa flights, is an established route in its own right. Comparing the two on schedule and current pricing is worth the time before committing to either.

    - heading: "Why the UAE still needs the full DAHLD permit"
      body: |
        Malawi waives its import permit requirement for pets arriving from fellow SADC states, Botswana, Lesotho, Namibia, South Africa, Eswatini and Zimbabwe. The UAE doesn't qualify, so the standard application applies regardless of how straightforward everything else about the trip looks. Once issued, the permit runs for six months, which gives some scheduling room either side of the actual travel date.

        No processing timeline is published for that application, so the sensible approach is contacting DAHLD's Director of Animal Health ten to twelve weeks out, not the six to eight weeks that might feel adequate for a lighter administrative step. A pet transport agent with recent Malawi experience earns their fee mainly through knowing who to follow up with when an application goes quiet.
