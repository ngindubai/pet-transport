content:
  h1: "Pet Transport from Canada to Laos"
  overview: |
    Canada asks a departing pet owner for almost nothing. There's no CFIA export permit for a personal pet, and a health certificate only enters the picture because Laos's side of the process calls for one. That single fact covers most of the Canadian half of this move.

    Laos is the side that does the asking: a Department of Livestock and Fisheries import permit, a microchip fitted before vaccination, and a rabies jab that has to land between 30 days and 12 months before travel. No titre test applies, since DLF reserves that step for pets arriving from countries it rates as high-rabies-risk, and Canada isn't on that list. Budget 8 to 14 weeks, most of it spent on permit processing and booking a workable connecting flight, since nothing flies nonstop from Canada into Vientiane.

  sections:
    - heading: "Air Canada's nonstop Bangkok link, and why it matters here"
      body: |
        Vancouver to Bangkok is Air Canada's only nonstop route into mainland Southeast Asia, flown three times a week at roughly 13.5 hours. For a Laos-bound pet, that single long-haul leg beats any alternative built from two or three shorter connections, and Lao Airlines or Bangkok Airways covers the remaining Bangkok to Vientiane hop within a few hours.

        The trade-off is frequency. Three flights a week means your travel date has to work around Air Canada's schedule rather than the other way round. Korean Air's routing through Seoul Incheon runs daily on the Lao Airlines feeder leg, which buys flexibility if the Bangkok dates don't line up, in exchange for a second transfer instead of one.

        Whichever carrier you choose, confirm current pet acceptance and any active temperature embargo before booking. Air Canada Cargo pulls back from carrying dogs and cats on affected sectors when ground temperatures climb, and Bangkok's climate makes that a real consideration, not a theoretical one.

    - heading: "The vaccination window and DLF's import permit"
      body: |
        Laos doesn't ask for a titre test from a Canadian-origin pet, which makes it tempting to treat this route as simple. The real catch sits in the vaccination timing rule: the rabies jab has to fall no earlier than 30 days before travel and no later than 12 months before it. A pet vaccinated eighteen months ago at its annual check-up doesn't qualify. Neither does one vaccinated two weeks before a booked flight.

        Set the vaccination date around your intended travel window from the outset, rather than working backward from whatever's already on file. It saves a repeat dose and the delay that follows one.

        The DLF import permit references that vaccination date directly, so apply for it only once the timing is locked in. Processing takes several weeks, and a permit built around the wrong date can send you back to the vet before it's approved.
