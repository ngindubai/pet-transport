The Dominican Republic is not on the EU listed country register, so the FAVN titre test (blood drawn at least 30 days after vaccination, result 0.5 IU/ml or above at an EU-approved laboratory) and a mandatory 90-day wait from the blood draw date are both compulsory before entry.   sections:
    - heading: "Denmark's breed ban: check this first"
      body: |
        Denmark's Dog Act bans 13 breeds from the country. The breeds and their crosses are prohibited by law, not just by policy, and no permit or documentation changes this. The list includes: Pitbull Terrier, American Staffordshire Terrier, Tosa inu, Dogo Argentino, Bordeaux Dog (Dogue de Bordeaux), Fila Brasileiro, Kangal, Central Asian Shepherd, Caucasian Shepherd, South Russian Ovcharka, Tornjak, Sarplaninac, and Boerboel.

        If your dog is one of these breeds, or could be interpreted as such at the border, contact Foedevarestyrelsen (foedevarestyrelsen.dk) before starting any paperwork. Dogs refused entry at Copenhagen Airport are a serious and expensive problem. Checking breed status is a five-minute step that can prevent significant complications.

    - heading: "The EU unlisted country process for Dominican Republic pets"
      body: |
        Denmark follows EU pet movement rules administered by the Danish Veterinary, Food, Agriculture and Fisheries Agency (Foedevarestyrelsen). For pets from unlisted third countries such as the Dominican Republic, Commission Delegated Regulation (EU) 2026/131 applies.

        The sequence: ISO 11784/11785 microchip implanted before any vaccination; rabies vaccination administered after the chip, with 21 days elapsed before travel; FAVN titre test drawn at least 30 days after the vaccination, at an EU-approved laboratory, with a result of 0.5 IU/ml or above; 90-day wait from the blood draw date; then travel to Denmark with an EU Annex IV health certificate endorsed by the Dominican Republic's DIGEGA authority.

        There is no shortcut and no appeal against the 90-day wait. Minimum total preparation time from microchip to departure is approximately 20 weeks, assuming the titre test passes on the first attempt.

    - heading: "Routing from Santo Domingo to Copenhagen"
      body: |
        Las Americas International Airport (SDQ) in Santo Domingo has cargo connections to European hubs via Iberia and Air France. The typical routing is SDQ to Madrid (MAD) via Iberia, then MAD to Copenhagen Kastrup (CPH) via SAS Scandinavian Airlines cargo. SAS has its main hub at CPH and accepts live animal cargo from MAD.

        From Paris CDG, Air France or SAS connect to Copenhagen. Each cargo transfer requires documentation and pre-notification of live animals. Using a specialist freight forwarder with Caribbean experience reduces the risk of booking errors on multi-leg journeys.

    - heading: "The DIGEGA export process from the Dominican Republic"
      body: |
        The Direccion General de Ganaderia (DIGEGA), within the Ministerio de Agricultura (agricultura.gob.do), endorses export health certificates for companion animals. A licensed veterinarian prepares the EU Annex IV format document, which must include the microchip number, vaccination details, and FAVN titre test result (with blood draw date, laboratory name, and result in IU/ml).

        DIGEGA endorses the certificate after review. Allow three to five business days for this step. The endorsed certificate is valid for 10 days from issue and must be presented at Copenhagen Airport. Do not have it issued more than 10 days before departure.

faqs:
  - question: "Is Denmark's breed ban an airline policy or a legal prohibition?"
    answer: "It is Danish law. The 13 breeds are banned by the Danish Dog Act, not just airline policy. No permit, documentation, or airline agreement can authorise entry for these breeds. If your dog is a banned breed, Denmark is not a viable destination."
  - question: "Does Denmark require tapeworm treatment for dogs from the Dominican Republic?"
    answer: "No. Denmark does not have an Echinococcus treatment requirement for dogs entering from the Caribbean. This is different from Norway and Finland, which both require tapeworm treatment. For a direct Dominican Republic-to-Denmark route, no tapeworm treatment is needed."
  - question: "Can I use a titre test from a laboratory in the Dominican Republic for EU entry into Denmark?"
    answer: "Only if the laboratory is on the EU-approved list under Commission Decision 2000/258/EC. Most Dominican laboratories are not on this list. Your veterinarian will need to ship the blood sample to an EU-approved laboratory abroad, such as ANSES in France or Kansas State University Rabies Laboratory in the US. Confirm with the laboratory that they accept samples from the Dominican Republic before arranging the shipment."
  - question: "What documents does Foedevarestyrelsen check at Copenhagen Airport?"
    answer: "Inspectors check the EU Annex IV health certificate (DIGEGA-endorsed, issued within 10 days of travel); vaccination records showing the microchip number, vaccine batch, and dates; the FAVN titre test result with the blood draw date and laboratory details; and the microchip scan. All documents must be internally consistent."
