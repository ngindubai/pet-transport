  overview: "Moving a dog or cat from the Dominican Republic to Denmark requires the FAVN titre test and its mandatory 90-day waiting period. The Dominican Republic is classified as an unlisted third country under EU Implementing Regulation 2026/636, so the titre test cannot be skipped.   sections:
    - heading: "FAVN titre test: the 90-day constraint"
      body: |
        The FAVN titre test is the factor that defines the preparation timeline for this route. Blood must be drawn at least 30 days after the most recent rabies vaccination and must show a neutralising antibody level of at least 0.5 IU/ml at an EU-approved laboratory. The 90-day waiting period runs from the blood draw date, not the date the laboratory report arrives.

        Fødevarestyrelsen (the Danish Veterinary and Food Administration) applies EU rules without exemption. Once the 90 days have elapsed, the DIGEGA health certificate can be issued. That certificate is valid for 10 days from the DIGEGA endorsement date to the documentary checks at the Danish Border Control Post. Keep the original laboratory report: it must accompany the health certificate throughout the journey.

        Work with a vet in the Dominican Republic who has experience shipping blood samples to EU-approved laboratories. International shipping adds several days; plan accordingly and allow time for the endorsement step at DIGEGA after the vet appointment.

    - heading: "Entering Denmark: three Border Control Posts"
      body: |
        Denmark designates three airports as Border Control Posts for non-EU pet arrivals: Copenhagen Kastrup (CPH), Billund (BLL), and Aalborg (AAL). CPH handles the largest volume and is the most consistently staffed. Billund and Aalborg cover the Jutland region but have more restricted inspection hours; confirm availability with Fødevarestyrelsen before routing through either.

        The most practical routing from Santo Domingo is via a major European hub. Iberia and Air Europa fly direct from SDQ to Madrid (MAD), from where connections reach CPH. KLM connections via Amsterdam (AMS) also serve CPH. If your routing passes through another EU country first, the border veterinary checks happen at that first EU entry point, not Denmark. Discuss the routing carefully with your cargo agent.

        Denmark's breed ban is a separate requirement with no documentation override. If your dog is one of the 13 prohibited breeds or a cross thereof, entry is not possible. Confirm the breed status before making any other preparation.
