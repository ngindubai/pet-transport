  overview: |
    flydubai flies direct from Dubai to Bishkek eleven times a week. None of those seats, or cargo holds, will take a dog or a cat. flydubai's pet policy excludes all live animal transport on every route it operates, and Bishkek is no exception.

    That single fact reroutes the whole journey. Turkish Airlines' Bishkek-Istanbul-Dubai connection, with roughly 14 weekly Istanbul-Dubai flights, becomes the realistic option instead of the direct flight that looks obvious on a flight search.

    The regulatory side is more forgiving than the flight search. Kyrgyzstan isn't on MOCCAE's low-risk list, so a titre test is required, but the UAE attaches no fixed wait once the result clears, a genuinely lighter rule than the 90-day wait already governing Kyrgyzstan's UK route on this site.
  sections:
    - heading: "The direct flight that can't carry your pet"
      body: |
        A quick flight search turns up flydubai's Dubai-Bishkek route immediately: roughly 11 flights a week, around 4 hours 45 minutes, a schedule most airlines would envy. It's also entirely off-limits for a pet. flydubai's policy, consistent across every route it flies, accepts no dogs or cats as cabin passengers or as cargo, and Bishkek doesn't get a special exception.

        Turkish Airlines via Istanbul is the practical substitute, connecting Bishkek to Dubai on a well-established two-leg routing that this site's Kyrgyzstan-to-UK page already relies on for a different destination.
    - heading: "The titre test, and why the wait here is shorter than the UK's"
      body: |
        Kyrgyzstan sits outside MOCCAE's roughly 50-country low-risk list, so a rabies antibody titre test at an accredited laboratory is required, blood drawn at least 21 days after vaccination, result at least 0.5 IU/ml. No UK-approved or MOCCAE-recognised lab operates domestically, so samples usually travel by courier, adding real weeks beyond the 21-day floor.

        Once the result clears, though, MOCCAE doesn't apply a further waiting period the way the UK's 90-day post-blood-draw rule does. The pet can travel as soon as the import permit and routing are confirmed, making this route noticeably quicker end to end than the UK equivalent, despite needing the same underlying blood test.
