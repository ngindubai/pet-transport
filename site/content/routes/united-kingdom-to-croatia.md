  overview: "Croatia joined the EU in 2013 and has been fully integrated into the Schengen zone and Eurozone since January 2023. UK pets travelling to Croatia follow the standard EU listed third country route: Animal Health Certificate from an APHA-authorised official vet, current microchip, and a valid rabies vaccination with the primary course completed at least 21 days before travel. No titre test. No quarantine for compliant pets. The main airports are Zagreb (ZAG) for central Croatia, Split (SPU) for Dalmatia, and Dubrovnik (DBV) for the south. Summer heat is a factor at coastal airports, particularly Dubrovnik, from June through August."

  sections:
    - heading: "Coastal Croatia and the summer heat consideration"
      body: |
        Many people relocating to Croatia are heading to the Dalmatian coast or the islands rather than Zagreb. This creates a logistics issue worth planning around.

        All international pet cargo enters Croatia through the main international airports (Zagreb, Split, or Dubrovnik). There is no direct international service to Croatia's islands. If your destination is Hvar, Brac, Korcula, or one of the other Dalmatian islands, your pet arrives at Split airport (or Dubrovnik for the southern islands) and you need to arrange ferry transport from there. Jadrolinija and other ferry operators connect the coast to the islands, but their policies on transporting pets in travel crates vary by route and season.

        The Dalmatian coast also gets very hot in summer. Dubrovnik averages around 28 degrees Celsius in July, with peaks above 35 degrees. Airlines apply temperature thresholds for pet cargo, and bookings can be refused or cancelled if ground temperatures breach these limits on the day. For summer moves to Dalmatia, build in a contingency plan and confirm the airline's specific heat embargo policy.

    - heading: "The AHC and what to bring on travel day"
      body: |
        The AHC is the single most important document for this journey. It must be issued within 10 days of travel by an APHA-authorised official vet (not just any vet). APHA keeps a register of authorised vets, and your regular vet may or may not be on it. Book early to avoid a last-minute scramble.

        The AHC confirms your pet's microchip number, vaccination history, and health status at the time of issue. On arrival in Croatia, a vet from the Croatian Veterinary Directorate checks the microchip number against the certificate, verifies vaccination dates, and inspects the pet.

        Keep the original AHC with you. Do not put it in checked luggage. If there is any discrepancy between the microchip number and the certificate, or if vaccination timing does not meet the 21-day rule, entry can be refused or delayed.

faqs:
  - question: "Do I need a titre test to take my pet from the UK to Croatia?"
    answer: "No. The UK is listed as an approved third country for EU pet travel. No titre test is required for UK pets entering Croatia. You need a current ISO microchip, a valid rabies vaccination with the primary course completed at least 21 days before travel, and an AHC from an APHA-authorised official vet issued within 10 days of travel."
  - question: "My pet is already in Croatia for the summer. Can I travel back to the UK and return without a new AHC?"
    answer: "Each time you re-enter an EU country from the UK you need a new AHC, issued within 10 days of that specific journey. The AHC issued for your initial entry is valid for up to 4 months for onward travel within EU member states, but it does not cover re-entry from the UK after a return visit. Plan vet appointments accordingly."
  - question: "Which airport should I route my pet through for the Croatian islands?"
    answer: "For the central and northern Dalmatian islands (Hvar, Brac, Vis), Split Airport (SPU) is the best entry point. For the southern islands and Dubrovnik area, use Dubrovnik Airport (DBV). All island connections require ferry transport from the mainland after clearing customs."
  - question: "Can I take my pet to Croatia by ferry from the UK?"
    answer: "There are no direct UK-to-Croatia ferry services for pet owners. Most owners travel by air cargo (separate from passenger) or on a combination of ferry to France or Spain and road through Europe. If driving, your pet needs the AHC for EU entry at the first crossing point into an EU country, and that certificate covers 4 months of EU travel."
  - question: "How long does the process take for a UK to Croatia pet move?"
    answer: "With current microchip and rabies vaccination, the process takes 2 to 4 weeks. The main step is booking the AHC appointment within 10 days of travel. If your pet's vaccinations need updating, allow at least 3 weeks for the primary course and 21-day waiting period."
