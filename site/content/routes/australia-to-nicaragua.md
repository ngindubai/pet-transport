  h1: "Pet Transport from Australia to Nicaragua"
  overview: |
    Zero. That's how many rabies titre tests Nicaragua asks of a dog or cat arriving from Australia, because IPSA has no country-risk list to place Australia on in the first place. A pet leaving one of the most tightly regulated import systems on earth lands in one of the lightest.

    What actually shapes this route is the flight, not the paperwork. Nothing connects Sydney or Melbourne to Managua without at least two stops, so the practical work is booking Emirates through Dubai and Miami, then American Airlines for the final leg into Nicaragua, and confirming with IPSA ahead of time whether the Australian certificate needs any translation.

  sections:
    - heading: "What DAFF asks before a pet leaves Australia"
      body: |
        Leaving Australia is short work compared with arriving there. No general export permit applies to a personal pet dog or cat, and standard biosecurity checks at the departure airport are the only government step beyond the health certificate itself. An accredited vet completes that certificate, and it then has to be reformatted for whatever Nicaragua's side expects, since IPSA hasn't published an Australia-specific checklist the way it has for US-origin pets.

        That gap isn't a red flag. It just means a direct call to IPSA's Ventanilla Unica desk, asking plainly whether the Australian paperwork needs a Spanish translation or a consular stamp, is worth making before flights are booked.

    - heading: "One country's strict entry rules say nothing about another's"
      body: |
        It's easy to assume a country with famously tough import rules for incoming pets must also make life hard for pets leaving it, or that a country asking little of arrivals must be equally relaxed everywhere. Neither assumption holds here. Australia's own entry system, titre tests, 180-day waits, mandatory Mickleham quarantine, applies to pets coming in, not going out. Nicaragua's light entry standard applies the same way to a pet from Australia, the United States, or anywhere else, because IPSA simply doesn't run a country-by-country risk model.

        A current rabies vaccination and a properly issued health certificate cover the medical side completely. There's no blood test, no waiting period, and no kennel stay tied to it.

    - heading: "Routing: Sydney or Melbourne to Managua via Dubai and Miami"
      body: |
        Emirates SkyCargo's long-standing service from Sydney or Melbourne to Dubai is the strongest first leg, and its newer four-times-weekly non-stop from Dubai to Miami, flight EK213/EK214 on a Boeing 777-300ER, keeps the routing to two carriers rather than three. American Airlines then completes the final sector with its daily non-stop Miami to Managua, and its PetEmbark cargo programme is bookable by the general public rather than restricted to military shipments, based on the current policy published on aacargo.com.

        Qantas Freight via Los Angeles is a workable alternative if Dubai capacity is tight, though it adds a separate American Airlines connection through Miami rather than folding into one continuous partnership routing.
