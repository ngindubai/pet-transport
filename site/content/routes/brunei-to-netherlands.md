  h1: "Pet Transport from Brunei to the Netherlands"
  overview: |
    A dedicated Animal Hotel at Schiphol is the kind of detail that makes a long, multi-leg move feel less daunting. Once a pet clears the Brunei-to-Singapore transfer and the transatlantic sector to Amsterdam, that jointly run Air France-KLM-Martinair Cargo facility handles the NVWA inspection on site rather than through a general freight shed.

    Getting there still means clearing the EU's full unlisted-country process first. Brunei isn't on the bloc's low-risk list, so a titre test and a fixed 90-day wait apply before any of that matters.

  sections:
    - heading: "From Bandar Seri Begawan to Schiphol: two legs, not one"
      body: |
        Royal Brunei Airlines has no live animal cargo product for a privately owned pet, so the practical starting point is a road transfer into Singapore. From Changi, KLM Cargo runs a direct service into Amsterdam, landing the pet at one of the better-equipped live animal facilities in Europe.

        The Schiphol Animal Hotel, run jointly by Air France, KLM and Martinair Cargo, is where NVWA officers carry out the border inspection. Booking that slot ahead of time through your cargo agent avoids the pet sitting in a general holding area while paperwork is checked.

    - heading: "The titre test clock that decides your schedule"
      body: |
        Brunei sits outside the EU's list of low-risk third countries, so the Netherlands applies the same sequence as every other EU member state: microchip before vaccination, a 30-day wait before the titre blood draw, a passing result of at least 0.5 IU/ml, then 90 further days from the draw date itself.

        None of the Singapore transfer logistics or DAA's own export sign-off need to wait for that clock to finish; they can run in parallel. What can't be shortened is the 90 days itself, so start the vaccination and testing steps as early in the process as you can.
