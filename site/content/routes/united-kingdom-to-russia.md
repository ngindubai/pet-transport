  h1: "Pet Transport from the United Kingdom to Russia"
  overview: |
    No titre test, no 3-month wait, no quarantine for a compliant animal: Russia sits on Great Britain's low-risk list, so the paperwork side of this route is genuinely light. The catch is the flight. There has been no direct London-Russia service since February 2022, and both things are true at once. Planning for only one of them is where most delays happen.

    Every route currently runs through a third country. Istanbul is the default, since Turkey has stayed outside both the Russian airspace closure and the UK and EU carrier bans. Dubai works as a fallback. Either way, your pet needs two separate cargo bookings and a supervised transfer, arranged around Russia's fairly light but time-sensitive paperwork.
  sections:
    - heading: "Why there is no direct flight, and what that means for booking"
      body: |
        Russia closed its airspace to UK, EU and allied aircraft in late February 2022. The UK and EU answered with bans of their own on Russian-registered carriers, including Aeroflot, entering their airspace. Both restrictions are still in force in mid-2026, with the EU's transport sanctions package renewed rather than lapsed.

        Turkey sits outside both bans. Turkish Airlines and other Western carriers still fly London to Istanbul; Russian and Turkish carriers still fly Istanbul to Moscow. Your pet needs a separate booking, cleared by a separate cargo team, on each leg, because no single through-fare currently spans the gap. Dubai offers the same two-leg pattern as a second option, generally used when Istanbul dates or capacity do not work.

        Source: theflyingengineer.com and OPSGROUP's Ukraine/Russia airspace tracker, cross-checked against the European Commission's sanctions-on-transport page, current mid-2026.
    - heading: "What Russia actually requires from a UK-origin pet"
      body: |
        Russia does not require a rabies titre test from any country of origin, the UK included, which puts it ahead of most non-EU destinations for paperwork simplicity. What it does require is precise: rabies vaccination at least 20 days old and never more than 12 months old on arrival, whatever the vaccine's stated duration, plus a full vaccine record for dogs (distemper, hepatitis, parvovirus, adenovirus) and cats (panleukopenia) alongside rabies.

        Up to 2 pets travel under the standard personal-use rules, with no import permit and no quarantine for a compliant animal. Bring a third pet, or a species other than a dog or cat, and a Rosselkhoznadzor permit becomes necessary. Confirm your pet count against this threshold before booking, since it changes the paperwork track entirely.
    - heading: "Matching the UK certificate to Russia's entry window"
      body: |
        Russia's import certificate for a UK-origin pet needs to be endorsed close to your travel date, not weeks in advance. Comparable routes, confirmed directly with USDA APHIS for pets travelling from the United States, use a 5-day endorsement window before departure. UK owners should expect something similar, but the exact current template and window are best confirmed with Rosselkhoznadzor or an IPATA agent before the vet appointment is booked, since these bilateral certificate formats do get updated.

        On arrival, a Rosselkhoznadzor veterinary officer checks the paperwork at the airport, whether Sheremetyevo, Domodedovo or Vnukovo in Moscow, or Pulkovo in St Petersburg, and issues the internal Russian document that covers the pet's onward movement in the country. Getting this step right on the day matters more here than in most destinations, since there is no titre test or quarantine buffer to absorb a paperwork mismatch.
