content:
  h1: "Pet Transport Canada to Uruguay"
  overview: |
    Uruguay is one of the few Latin American destinations in this build that pairs a no-titre-test policy with a mandatory import permit, an odd combination until you understand the logic: MGAP wants to control who's importing pets, without necessarily testing every animal's rabies antibody levels first.

    Dogs face one requirement unique to this corridor: a praziquantel-based echinococcus treatment, timed to a tight 72-hour-to-30-day window before arrival. Cats skip this step entirely. The journey itself runs through a South American hub, since no airline flies direct from Canada into Montevideo.

  sections:
    - heading: "The echinococcus treatment, dogs only"
      body: |
        Dogs need documented treatment against Echinococcus (Taenia), using a praziquantel-based product, given no less than 72 hours and no more than 30 days before arrival in Uruguay. This sits entirely separate from the rabies vaccination schedule, so track it as its own date on the calendar rather than assuming it happens automatically alongside other vet visits.

    - heading: "MGAP's import permit: the step Panama skips"
      body: |
        Uruguay requires an import permit from MGAP-DGSG before a pet can enter, a genuine difference from Panama's approach on the same no-titre-test framework. Apply for this permit well in advance; it's not something to leave until the final weeks before travel.

    - heading: "CFIA's process and the route through South America"
      body: |
        A CFIA-accredited vet examination and endorsement complete the Canadian side, issued within 10 days of travel. From there, Air Canada and LATAM Cargo both route through Sao Paulo or Santiago for the final leg into Montevideo's Carrasco International Airport, since no direct Canada-Uruguay cargo service exists.
