  sections:
    - heading: "DIGEGA and the EU health certificate from the Dominican Republic"
      body: |
        The Dirección General de Ganadería (DIGEGA), operating under the Ministry of Agriculture of the Dominican Republic, is the official authority for animal health certification. For EU export, a DIGEGA-authorised veterinarian issues the health certificate in EU-compatible format, and DIGEGA endorses it before departure.

        Not all veterinary clinics in the Dominican Republic are set up for EU export health certificates. The DIGEGA or a local pet transport specialist can identify authorised practitioners. The certificate itself must be issued within 10 days of arrival in Germany.

        DIGEGA's endorsement process typically takes 2-5 working days. Build this into your timing: the vet appointment should be early enough in the 10-day window to allow for endorsement and still have days remaining for travel.

    - heading: "The Madrid hub: how most DR pets reach Germany"
      body: |
        Santo Domingo Las Américas International (SDQ) is the main international airport in the Dominican Republic, with regular transatlantic services to Madrid via Iberia and Air Europa. Madrid Barajas (MAD) is the primary European hub for Caribbean-to-Europe cargo connections.

        From Madrid, connections to Frankfurt, Munich, or Berlin are available via Iberia partner carriers or Lufthansa Group. The full journey from SDQ to FRA via MAD is typically 11-13 hours including the connection.

        For those based near Punta Cana (PUJ), Condor operates seasonal direct services from PUJ to Frankfurt. This route is mainly leisure-oriented, so confirm live animal cargo acceptance on your specific Condor flight before booking. When it is available, it removes the Madrid connection and reduces transit time by 2-3 hours.

    answer: "DIGEGA stands for Dirección General de Ganadería, the General Directorate of Livestock under the Dominican Republic's Ministry of Agriculture. It is the competent authority for animal health and export certification. The EU requires that health certificates for pets entering from non-EU countries be issued and endorsed by the official government authority. DIGEGA plays this role for pets leaving the Dominican Republic."
