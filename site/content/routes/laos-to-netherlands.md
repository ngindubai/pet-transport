    Amsterdam Schiphol's Animal Hotel is one of the better-equipped arrival points on this site's Southeast Asia network, a joint Air France-KLM-Martinair Cargo facility where the NVWA carries out its border check on site. Getting there from Vientiane still means transferring through Bangkok first, since Laos has no long-haul cargo option of its own.

    Laos sits outside the EU's list of low-risk third countries, so the Netherlands applies the same titre test and 90-day wait sequence used across the bloc.

  sections:
    - heading: "Bangkok first, then Amsterdam"
      body: |
        Lao Airlines' twice-daily Vientiane-Bangkok service feeds into KLM Cargo's onward routing to Amsterdam. This is the default path most agents use for Laos-origin pet moves into the Netherlands, given Bangkok's established cargo infrastructure for the region.

        On arrival, the Schiphol Animal Hotel handles the NVWA inspection directly rather than routing the pet through general freight. Confirm the booking with your cargo agent in advance, since slots are managed separately from ordinary cargo.

    - heading: "The EU titre test sequence and DLF's 15-day rule"
      body: |
        The Netherlands applies the standard EU protocol for pets from an unlisted third country: microchip before vaccination, a 30-day gap before the titre blood draw, a result of at least 0.5 IU/ml, then a fixed 90 days from the draw date, under Commission Delegated Regulation (EU) 2026/131.

        Laos has few, if any, EU-approved laboratories of its own, so plan for the blood sample to be couriered abroad. DLF's own 15-day notice period for the export licence sits comfortably inside that longer wait and generally isn't the step that decides your final schedule.
