content:
  h1: "Pet Transport from Turkmenistan to Canada"
  overview: |
    Ask about the Alabai law on the outbound Canada-to-Turkmenistan route and the honest answer is that it doesn't apply. Ask about it here, and the answer flips entirely. Turkmenistan's national dog breed, protected since a 2022 law as a heritage symbol, cannot leave the country without special government clearance, whatever the destination. That's the single fact most worth knowing before anything else on this specific leg.

    Beyond that, the shape of the route is familiar to anyone who has read CFIA's rules before. Turkmenistan sits on CFIA's high-risk list, current as of 13 March 2025, but that classification bars commercial dog imports rather than adding a titre test for a family pet. What eats the calendar is Ashgabat's own thin scheduled flight capacity, not the paperwork.

  sections:
    - heading: "The Alabai law, the other way round"
      body: |
        Turkmenistan's 2022 law protecting the Alabai, or Central Asian Shepherd Dog, restricts the breed from being exported out of the country, full stop, regardless of where it's headed. On the Canada-to-Turkmenistan route this site also covers, that law is irrelevant, since it governs animals leaving Turkmenistan, not entering it. Here, on this exact leg, it's the opposite: an Alabai attempting to leave Turkmenistan for Canada runs straight into the restriction, and no amount of paperwork or fee resolves it without the government clearance the law requires.

        If your dog has significant Alabai ancestry, confirm eligibility with the State Veterinary Service before committing to anything else on this route.

    - heading: "What the high-risk listing actually restricts"
      body: |
        CFIA's list, updated 13 March 2025, groups Turkmenistan with dozens of other countries. Its practical force falls on commercial dog imports, stopped entirely since 28 September 2022, not on an owner-accompanied pet. That's a genuinely lighter position than the CDC's rule for the identical dog entering the United States, which requires a passing titre test or 28 days of quarantine at a CDC-registered facility. CFIA has no such machinery for personal pets. Current vaccination, a correctly built certificate, and confirmation the pet isn't caught by CFIA's 8-month commercial-import age presumption cover the requirement.

    - heading: "Thin capacity out of Ashgabat, whichever direction"
      body: |
        Turkish Airlines' nonstop Ashgabat-Istanbul service, connecting to its own nonstop Istanbul-Toronto sector, remains the most dependable single-carrier routing. flydubai's Ashgabat-Dubai connection, feeding into Emirates' own Dubai-Toronto service, is the workable alternative. Neither runs a dense schedule, so book 6-8 weeks ahead rather than assuming a last-minute slot will be available.
