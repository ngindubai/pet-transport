    A charter airline most Maldives guides never mention has, at times, flown Prague to Male directly.   sections:
    - heading: "The charter option nobody else writes about"
      body: |
        Scheduled carriers dominate almost every Maldives route we cover, which makes Neos's seasonal Prague service worth a specific mention. Leisure charter airlines built around the European package-holiday trade sometimes carry live animal cargo differently to scheduled carriers, with different booking windows and different crate rules, so confirm directly with Neos or a booking agent rather than assuming it works the same way as Turkish Airlines or Qatar Airways cargo.
    - heading: "Getting blood off the island before anything else can happen"
      body: |
        Male has no laboratory capable of running the FAVN or ELISA titre test. Every sample leaves the country first, adding a courier delay before the 30-day post-vaccination window has even closed, on top of whatever time the lab needs once the sample arrives.
    - heading: "SVA's sequence and the 90-day rule"
      body: |
        The State Veterinary Administration enforces the same order every unlisted-country pet faces: microchip before vaccination, a 30-day gap, a titre result of 0.5 IU/ml or higher, then 90 days counted from the draw date. Reverse the microchip and vaccination order and the SVA won't accept the sequence as valid, forcing a restart.
    - heading: "If the charter isn't running this season"
      body: |
        Turkish Airlines via Istanbul is the steadiest scheduled fallback, with frequent onward connections into Prague. Qatar Airways via Doha covers the same ground with a dedicated animal handling facility at the Doha end, though summer heat embargoes can affect scheduling between roughly May and September.
