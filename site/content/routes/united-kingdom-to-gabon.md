  h1: "Pet Transport from the United Kingdom to Gabon"
  overview: |
    APHA doesn't have a ready-made certificate for Gabon. That's unusual on a site where dozens of destinations, Antigua and Barbuda among them, get their own dedicated export certificate number. Owners moving a pet to Gabon have to work from a general APHA template instead, translated into French, since that's the language Gabon's own paperwork runs in.

    Gabon's guidance on its own import permit is equally distinct: start the application 4 to 6 months before travel, a runway most owners don't expect to need. Rabies vaccination is required, timed at least 30 days before travel and within a one-year window, but the sources available don't confirm a titre test requirement one way or the other, which is worth checking directly rather than assuming either way.

    No airline flies London to Libreville nonstop. Every route adds a connection, through Paris on Air France or Casablanca on Royal Air Maroc, and with the import permit's own lead time, 18 to 24 weeks is the realistic planning window.

  sections:
    - heading: "Why there's no off-the-shelf certificate for this route"
      body: |
        Compare this to the UK's route into Antigua and Barbuda, where APHA publishes certificate 6592, a form built specifically for that destination. No equivalent exists for Gabon in APHA's current export health certificate finder, checked in July 2026. That doesn't make the move impossible, but it does mean the vet completes a general export certificate and adapts it, with the paperwork translated into French to match what Gabon's own authorities expect.

        This is worth raising with APHA directly before committing to a travel date, since certificate coverage does get added over time and a dedicated Gabon certificate may exist by the time you book.

    - heading: "The 4 to 6 month runway Gabon itself recommends"
      body: |
        Most destinations on this site work on a run-up measured in weeks. Gabon's own published guidance talks in months: start the import permit application with the Direction Generale de l'Elevage 4 to 6 months before the intended travel date. Rabies vaccination has its own window too, at least 30 days before travel and within a year of it, which needs to sit comfortably inside that longer permit timeline rather than being treated as a separate deadline.

        A titre test is not confirmed as required in the guidance reviewed for this page, which focuses on the vaccination and permit route rather than a blood test pathway. Given how much else about Gabon's rules sits outside the templates this site sees elsewhere, confirm that point directly with the Direction Generale de l'Elevage before ruling it out.

    - heading: "Two ways through Europe, since nothing flies UK-Libreville direct"
      body: |
        Air France offers the shorter overall routing on paper: London to Paris, then Air France's nonstop AF926 into Libreville on a Boeing 787. A small pet under 8kg including carrier can use the cabin on the London-Paris leg; anything up to 75kg combined can go in the hold, except brachycephalic breeds, which Air France won't carry in the hold under any circumstances.

        Royal Air Maroc runs the alternative, via Casablanca, with its own nonstop Casablanca-Libreville service. Checked pets up to 70kg combined travel through the standard baggage process; heavier or unaccompanied animals go through the airline's cargo desk, which needs at least 48 hours' notice.
