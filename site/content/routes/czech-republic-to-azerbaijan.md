  overview: |
    Azerbaijan sits at the crossroads of Eastern Europe and Central Asia, and Baku has become an increasingly common destination for international relocations. For pet owners moving from Czech Republic, the process is manageable: no quarantine, no titre test, and a clear two-step export process from Prague.

    The key points to check early are the rabies vaccination dates, the Echinococcus treatment requirement for dogs, and the fact that all pets flying into Azerbaijan must arrive via Heydar Aliyev International Airport (GYD) in Baku.   sections:
    - heading: "Czech export paperwork: SVS and the two-step process"
      body: |
        Exporting a pet from the Czech Republic follows EU Regulation 576/2013 format. The health certificate is first issued by an SVS-authorised official vet (distinct from a standard practice vet unless they hold specific SVS authorisation) and must then be endorsed by SVS, the Státní veterinární správa (State Veterinary Administration), before the document is accepted by foreign authorities.

        This two-step process is straightforward but needs lead time. Book your official vet appointment at least five days before departure: SVS regional office endorsement typically takes one to two working days. The certificate itself is valid for 10 days from the date of examination, so timing the appointment correctly means it covers your travel date without expiring.

        For dogs travelling to Azerbaijan, the health certificate must also document Echinococcus treatment. Your SVS-authorised vet will include this on the certificate. Confirm the correct treatment product and timing before the appointment, as Azerbaijan's Ministry of Agriculture checks this field specifically.

    - heading: "Flying from Prague to Baku: the Istanbul routing"
      body: |
        There are no direct flights from Prague Václav Havel Airport (PRG) to Heydar Aliyev International Airport (GYD) in Baku. The most reliable routing for cargo pets from Central Europe is Turkish Airlines via Istanbul Atatürk (IST), operating the PRG-IST and IST-GYD legs under Turkish Airlines Cargo.

        Turkish Airlines has established live animal procedures at both PRG and IST. When booking, confirm the specific connection time at Istanbul and ask whether your pet transits airside between flights. IATA live animal regulations set minimum connection times, and Turkish Airlines Cargo staff will manage the transit process.

        Lufthansa via Frankfurt is an alternative worth considering if cargo space on the direct Turkish Airlines routing is unavailable. Frankfurt Airport has the Frankfurt Animal Lounge, which provides professional temporary care for transit pets. The routing is longer via FRA, but it is a well-established option with good standards.

    - heading: "Arrival at Baku: GYD is the only entry point"
      body: |
        Azerbaijan requires all pets entering by air to arrive at Heydar Aliyev International Airport (GYD) in Baku. No other airports in Azerbaijan currently accept imported pets, so your cargo routing must terminate at GYD regardless of which airline you use.

        Ministry of Agriculture veterinary officers inspect pets on arrival. They will check the SVS-endorsed health certificate (valid within 10 days), the microchip number against the certificate, the rabies vaccination date and validity, and for dogs, the Echinococcus treatment record. Everything must be consistent across documents.

        No quarantine is imposed for compliant pets. Once documentation passes inspection, your pet is released. The inspection is typically completed within an hour for well-prepared arrivals, but allow additional time on the day in case of queries.
