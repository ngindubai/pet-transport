content:
  h1: "Pet Transport from Laos to Singapore"
  overview: |
    Scoot flies Singapore to Vientiane and back seven days a week, non-stop, about three hours in the air. It looks like the obvious way to move a pet on this corridor. It is not an option at all: Scoot's own policy excludes every live animal, in the cabin, as checked baggage, or as cargo, with no exception beyond service dogs.

    So the pet goes the long way round, via Lao Airlines to Bangkok and then Thai Airways or Singapore Airlines Cargo onward. Layer Singapore's AVS Schedule III process on top, since Laos sits on neither of AVS's shorter schedules, and this becomes an 18 to 24 week undertaking despite the tantalisingly short direct flight sitting right there on the schedule board.

  sections:
    - heading: "The flight that exists but can't be used"
      body: |
        A nonstop Singapore-Vientiane service is a rare thing in this part of Southeast Asia, and Scoot runs one daily on most weeks. For a passenger, it's the fastest way between the two cities by a wide margin. For a pet, it's irrelevant.

        Scoot does not offer cargo or checked-baggage transport for any live animal, a stricter policy than most regional low-cost carriers. The workaround is Lao Airlines' twice-daily Vientiane-Bangkok hop, feeding into Thai Airways Cargo or Singapore Airlines Cargo for the Bangkok-Singapore leg. Two connections instead of none, purely because of one airline's blanket exclusion.

    - heading: "Schedule III, and a sample that has to travel"
      body: |
        Laos isn't named on AVS Schedule I or Schedule II, so the full Schedule III sequence applies: a WOAH titre test at least 28 days after vaccination, a fixed 90-day wait from the blood draw, and 30 days at the Animal Quarantine Centre on arrival.

        Few laboratories inside Laos process this test to the standard AVS requires, so the sample usually ships to Thailand or beyond. Time this alongside the DLF export application, which only needs 15 days' notice and rarely becomes the binding constraint once the 90-day AVS clock is running.

    answer: "No. That nonstop service is operated by Scoot, and Scoot does not accept any live animal as cargo or checked baggage, with no exception beyond service dogs. Pets must route via Bangkok instead, using Lao Airlines for the Vientiane-Bangkok leg and Thai Airways or Singapore Airlines Cargo for the onward leg to Singapore."
  - question: "Why is Laos classed as AVS Schedule III?"
    answer: "AVS names only four Schedule I countries and 31 Schedule II countries. Laos appears on neither, so it defaults to Schedule III, carrying a WOAH titre test, a 90-day wait from the blood draw, and 30 days of quarantine at Singapore's Animal Quarantine Centre. Confirmed against avs.nparks.gov.sg, fetched 21 July 2026."
  - question: "How much notice does Laos require before a pet can be exported?"
    answer: "The Department of Livestock and Fisheries needs at least 15 days' notice before the export date. This is far shorter than the 90-day AVS titre wait, so it usually runs in parallel rather than adding to the timeline."
  - question: "Do I need a pet agent to bring a dog into Singapore from Laos?"
    answer: "Yes. From 1 April 2026, AVS requires all Schedule III arrivals, including pets from Laos, to be cleared through CAPQ by an AVS-recognised pet agent."
