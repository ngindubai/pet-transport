content:
  h1: "Pet Transport Uruguay to Italy: 2026 Guide"
  overview: "Uruguay to Italy is a route that brings two distinct challenges together: the long titre test preparation required for all unlisted countries, and a multi-leg cargo journey from South America to the Mediterranean. Uruguay is classified as an unlisted third country under Commission Delegated Regulation (EU) 2026/131, so the FAVN rabies titre test, a 30-day post-vaccination wait, a result of 0.5 IU/ml or above, and a 90-day post-blood-draw wait are all mandatory. Italy's Ministry of Health carries out the border inspection on arrival at Milan or Rome. Total preparation is 20-26 weeks."

  sections:
    - heading: "Italy's Import Rules for Uruguay-Origin Pets"
      body: |
        The Italian Ministry of Health (Ministero della Salute, salute.gov.it) applies EU non-commercial pet movement regulations at Milan Malpensa (MXP) and Rome Fiumicino (FCO) airports. For a pet from Uruguay, the inspection covers:

        Microchip: ISO 11784/11785, implanted before vaccination. Customs and veterinary officials scan the chip on arrival.

        Rabies vaccination: Current, post-microchip, 21 days elapsed since the primary dose.

        FAVN titre test: 0.5 IU/ml or above at an EU-approved laboratory, blood drawn at least 30 days after vaccination. The 90-day wait from the blood draw date must have elapsed before entry into Italy.

        Health certificate: EU Annex IV non-commercial format, DGSG-endorsed, issued within 10 days of travel. Full titre test details must appear on the certificate.

        No quarantine. No import permit. The inspection is typically completed within two hours of the aircraft's arrival at the cargo terminal.

    - heading: "Iberia's South Atlantic Route: The Backbone of This Journey"
      body: |
        Iberia Cargo operates some of the most direct Uruguay-to-Europe connections available. From Buenos Aires Ezeiza (EZE), which is easily reached from Montevideo (MVD) via a short regional hop, Iberia flies to Madrid (MAD). From MAD, Iberia or ITA Airways (formerly Alitalia) connect to both MXP and FCO.

        This routing covers the transatlantic leg in a single flight from EZE to MAD, reducing the number of cargo transfers and the overall time the animal spends in transit. Iberia Cargo has experience with live animal shipments from South America and Madrid Barajas has dedicated live animal handling facilities.

        LATAM Airlines via São Paulo (GRU) is an alternative, particularly if MVD to GRU direct is more convenient. GRU connects to European hubs via LATAM's partner network or via Air France (GRU-CDG) or Lufthansa (GRU-FRA). From CDG or FRA, Italian connections to MXP and FCO are frequent.

        Air France via Paris (CDG) is worth considering for relocations to Rome. Air France operates GRU-CDG and CDG-FCO. For Rome-based families, CDG is a practical connection point with direct flights to Fiumicino.

    - heading: "Milan or Rome: Choosing the Arrival Airport"
      body: |
        For Uruguay-to-Italy pet cargo, the choice of arrival airport depends primarily on your final destination in Italy.

        Milan Malpensa (MXP) is the larger cargo hub and serves northern and central Italy. It has more cargo connections from European hubs including FRA, AMS, CDG, and MAD. If you are relocating to Milan, Turin, Genoa, Florence, or anywhere in northern Italy, MXP is the practical choice.

        Rome Fiumicino (FCO) is the main cargo entry for central and southern Italy. Air France (CDG-FCO), Iberia (MAD-FCO), and other carriers serve the route. The Italian Ministry of Health inspection facilities at FCO are well established for live animal cargo.

        For either airport, pre-notify the cargo terminal of the live animal shipment and confirm the inspection appointment time with the freight forwarder.

    - heading: "The Health Certificate: Common Issues on South American Routes"
      body: |
        Health certificates from South American countries are sometimes rejected at EU borders due to preventable errors. The most common issues are:

        Date format inconsistency: EU health certificates use DD/MM/YYYY. If the MGAP-registered vet uses MM/DD/YYYY (US format), the blood draw date may appear to be in a different month, which confuses the 90-day calculation.

        Microchip number transcription error: A single digit difference between the certificate and the scanned chip means the animal cannot be verified. Double-check this field.

        Missing laboratory approval number: The EU-approved laboratory's approval number (assigned under Commission Decision 2000/258/EC) must appear on the titre test result and should be referenced in the health certificate.

        DGSG endorsement timing: The DGSG stamp must appear on the certificate, and the certificate must be issued within 10 days of travel. If the DGSG endorsement date is more than 10 days before arrival at MXP or FCO, the certificate is expired.

        Review the endorsed health certificate against this checklist before the pet departs Montevideo.

    - heading: "Uruguay and Italy: Some Context"
      body: |
        Uruguay and Italy have a historical connection. Uruguay's Waldensian communities and significant Italian-origin population make this a route with real personal resonance for many families relocating between the two countries.

        Italian customs and veterinary officials are generally well disposed toward pets arriving from Uruguay and familiar with the transatlantic South American route. The documentation requirements are standard EU rules, applied consistently.

        Once in Italy, register with a local veterinarian (veterinario) within the first week. Italy has a national pet registry (Anagrafe Nazionale degli Animali d'Affezione) and your pet should be registered using the chip number. Annual rabies vaccination is legally required in most Italian regions.

  - question: "My dog has an existing FAVN titre test result from 18 months ago. Is it still valid for Italian entry?"
    answer: "A passing FAVN result (0.5 IU/ml or above) remains valid for EU entry for as long as the rabies vaccination remains current. There is no expiry date on the titre test result itself. If your dog's rabies vaccination has been kept up to date with boosters given within the valid period of the previous dose, the original titre test result should still be valid. If the vaccination has ever lapsed and been restarted, a new titre test and 90-day wait would be required. Confirm the vaccination history with your MGAP-registered vet and bring all documentation."
  - question: "Can we import two dogs and a cat from Uruguay to Italy on the same health certificate?"
    answer: "Yes, up to five pets per owner can be listed on a single EU Annex IV non-commercial health certificate. Each animal must be individually identified by microchip number, and each must have its own vaccination record and titre test result included. If you have more than five animals, a second certificate is required. All three animals (two dogs and a cat) must each have completed the FAVN titre test pathway independently."
