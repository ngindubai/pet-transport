  overview: |
    No titre test and no quarantine make this one of the more approachable Latin America routes for an Irish pet owner, but one step needs planning ahead: the DAFM-endorsed health certificate has to be legalised by Panama's diplomatic representation before your pet can travel, and Panama does not maintain a resident embassy in Dublin.

  sections:
    - heading: "What MIDA checks on arrival at Tocumen"
      body: |
        Panama's Ministry of Agricultural Development (MIDA) inspects your pet's microchip, vaccination records, health certificate and antiparasitic treatment documentation at Tocumen International Airport. The registration fee is approximately USD 20 per pet, paid in cash at the MIDA airport office.

        Your pet must be over 4 months old, with a rabies vaccination at least 30 days old and current, plus core vaccines for dogs (distemper, hepatitis, leptospirosis, parvovirus) or cats (rhinotracheitis, panleukopenia, feline leukaemia). No titre test and no import permit apply for personal companion animals.
    - heading: "The legalisation step: the one Ireland-specific wrinkle"
      body: |
        Where an EU-based owner can visit a Panamanian embassy in their own capital, Ireland-based owners face an extra layer: Panama's diplomatic presence for Ireland is often handled through a nearby EU capital rather than a resident mission in Dublin. Confirm the current arrangement early, since this step can add days that a Brussels or Madrid-based owner would not face.

        Given the AHC's 10-day validity window, book the DAFM vet appointment with the legalisation timeline already mapped out, not as an afterthought once the certificate is in hand.
