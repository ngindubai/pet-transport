  sections:
    - heading: "Uruguay's import requirements for EU pets"
      body: |
        Uruguay's animal health authority, MGAP (Ministerio de Ganaderia, Agricultura y Pesca), handles pet imports through its DIGEGRA division. MGAP veterinary officers inspect arriving pets at Carrasco International Airport (MVD) in Montevideo, the only air entry point for companion animals.

        The core requirements are: a rabies vaccination given at least 30 days before entry and not more than 12 months before arrival, a MAPA-endorsed health certificate from Spain, and an import permit from MGAP obtained before travel. No titre test is required from Spain.

        Dogs have one additional requirement: praziquantel treatment against Taenia echinococcus (hydatic cyst). This treatment must be given between 72 hours and 30 days before admission to Uruguay, documented with the product used, the dose, and the date.

        Uruguay also has breed restrictions. Pit Bull Terrier, American Staffordshire Terrier, Bull Terrier, and Dogo Argentino face import restrictions. Confirm your breed's status with MGAP before applying for the permit.

    - heading: "Getting the MGAP import permit from Spain"
      body: |
        The MGAP import permit is a prerequisite for travel, not something you arrange on arrival. Apply to MGAP (mgap.gub.uy) directly, or through the Uruguayan consulate in Spain. Allow at least six to eight weeks for the process.

        The permit specifies conditions that must be met: the vaccinations required, the health certificate format, and the antiparasitic requirements. Read it carefully when it arrives.

        Once the permit is confirmed, finalise your travel date and book cargo from Madrid (MAD) or Barcelona (BCN) to Carrasco Airport (MVD) in Montevideo. Iberia operates cargo from Madrid to MVD and is the most direct option from Spain. Air Europa and LATAM are alternatives. Confirm live animal cargo acceptance on all legs before committing to a date.
