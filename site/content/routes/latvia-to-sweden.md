  sections:
    - heading: "Documents and the tapeworm check for dogs"
      body: |
        The EU pet passport issued by a PVD-authorised Latvian vet is the simplest document for this journey. It covers all EU member state entries as long as vaccinations remain current. If you do not have an EU passport, a single-use AHC endorsed by an official Latvian vet within 10 days of travel is the alternative.

        Dogs travelling from Latvia to Sweden need one additional check: Sweden requires Echinococcus tapeworm treatment for dogs from countries where the parasite is present. The Jordbruksverket (Swedish Board of Agriculture) maintains the list, which is updated periodically. If Latvia is on the treatment list, your Latvian vet gives a praziquantel dose and records it in the EU passport or on a separate certificate. This must happen between 1 and 5 days before the dog enters Sweden. Cats are exempt from this requirement.

        There is no quarantine, no titre test, and no import permit for this route.

    - heading: "Flying from Riga to Sweden"
      body: |
        Riga is airBaltic's hub, and the carrier operates direct flights to Stockholm Arlanda (ARN) and Gothenburg (GOT). This makes Latvia-to-Sweden one of the easier Baltic routes for pet travel. Small pets (up to 8 kg including carrier) can travel in the cabin on airBaltic services; book the cabin pet space at the time of the flight purchase.

        SAS Scandinavian Airlines also serves Stockholm from Riga on some schedules and is an established carrier for pet transport. For larger dogs moving as cargo, Stockholm Arlanda's cargo terminal handles live animals. Book directly with the airline's cargo department and provide IATA crate dimensions and documentation.
