    - heading: "Getting the EU pet passport right in Latvia"
      body: |
        An official veterinarian in Latvia, authorised by the Partikas un veterinarais dienests (PVD, pvd.gov.lv), issues the EU pet passport. Sweden accepts this document as the sole entry requirement for compliant pets. There are two things to confirm at the vet appointment. First, that the microchip was implanted on or before the date of the first rabies vaccination: Sweden follows EU Regulation 576/2013 on this and will not accept a vaccination given before chipping. Second, that the current rabies vaccination is within its valid period. A booster given within the valid window of the previous dose does not require a new 21-day wait. If the vaccination has lapsed at all, even by a short period, the 21-day wait applies from the new dose. For questions specific to your pet's situation, the Jordbruksverket (jordbruksverket.se) is the Swedish authority.
