  sections:
    - heading: "Georgia's broader vaccination requirement for dogs"
      body: |
        Georgia requires dogs to be vaccinated not only against rabies but also against distemper, hepatitis, leptospirosis, parvovirus, and parainfluenza. In practice, most Austrian dogs vaccinated to EU standards will already have these as part of a standard combination vaccine (commonly known as the DHPP or L4 series). Check your dog's existing vaccination booklet against each of these diseases.

        The rabies vaccination must have been given between 21 days and 11 months before entry. If your dog's combination vaccine is up to date, the main task is confirming the dates are within Georgia's required window.

        Cats from Austria have a simpler profile: current rabies vaccination and a health certificate are the main requirements.

    - heading: "Parasite treatment: what Georgia expects"
      body: |
        Before a dog leaves Austria for Georgia, it must be treated for internal parasites (using anthelmintics) and for external parasites (fleas and ticks). The treatment date, product name, and dosage must be recorded in the health certificate.

        Your BMSGPK-authorised Austrian vet administers or records this treatment when issuing the export health certificate. It is not a complex step, but it is easy to overlook if you are focused on the vaccination requirements. Make sure this is noted explicitly in the documentation before you travel.

    - heading: "Vienna to Tbilisi: cargo and airline options"
      body: |
        There is no direct cargo pet service from Vienna to Tbilisi. The most practical routing is via Istanbul, where Turkish Airlines Cargo accepts live animals and has an established connection between Istanbul Ataturk and Tbilisi. Austrian Airlines, as part of the Lufthansa Group, can book cargo from Vienna to connections onward.

        Allow time for connecting cargo transit and confirm that both legs of the journey accept live animals before booking. Tbilisi International Airport (TBS) handles international live animal arrivals and there are no specific breed bans related to airport entry.
