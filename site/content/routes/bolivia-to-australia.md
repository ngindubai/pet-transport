content:
  h1: "Pet Transport from Bolivia to Australia: 2026 Guide"
  overview: |
    Boliviana de Aviación's nonstop flight from Santa Cruz Viru Viru to Miami is the only direct link Bolivia has toward any country DAFF actually approves for pet imports. That single fact points most Bolivia-based owners toward the United States as the practical staging base, rather than a further connection to Singapore or Europe.

    Because Bolivia doesn't appear on DAFF's Group 1, 2 or 3 lists, Australia won't process an import application until the pet has genuinely lived in an approved country for 180 consecutive days. Only after that does the familiar Group 3 sequence begin: RNATT titre test, BICON import permit, Mickleham quarantine. Altogether this runs 40 to 52 weeks, among the longest routes this build covers.

  sections:
    - heading: "Why Miami, and not somewhere else"
      body: |
        Every non-approved origin needs a staging country. What's specific to Bolivia is that its one nonstop international air link happens to land exactly where the staging requirement can be served. The US sits in DAFF's Group 3, so a Bolivia-based owner who flies the direct BoA service into Miami has already reached an approved country in a single leg, no onward connection needed just to start the 180-day clock.

        Copa Airlines through Panama City works too, and opens up other approved destinations if Miami doesn't fit the owner's plans, but it adds a second leg and a second carrier's live animal policy to confirm.

    - heading: "What 180 days actually means"
      body: |
        DAFF is specific about this, and it trips owners up regularly. Genuine, continuous residency, documented, is what counts. A holiday stopover doesn't. Neither does bouncing between countries during the period. The pet, and realistically the owner or a trusted carer, needs to actually be based in the approved country for the full 180 days before export to Australia can even be applied for.

    - heading: "The Group 3 process once staging is done"
      body: |
        From there it's the standard track used across this build for non-approved origins: microchip identity verification before the RNATT blood draw, the titre test itself, a BICON import permit that can take DAFF up to 123 business days to process, and Mickleham quarantine on arrival, 10 days with correctly timed identity verification, 30 days otherwise.
