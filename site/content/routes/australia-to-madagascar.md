  h1: "Pet Transport from Australia to Madagascar"
  overview: |
    Emirates flies Dubai to Antananarivo four times a week, on Tuesdays, Thursdays, Saturdays and Sundays. That service was suspended for a period and came back on 23 October 2025, and it's the single fact that makes this route noticeably simpler than it looks on a map: a Melbourne, Sydney, Perth or Brisbane pet reaches Madagascar's capital in two legs, both largely on one airline group's own network, rather than the three-carrier journeys that UK and US owners typically have to piece together via Paris, Nairobi or Addis Ababa.

    The regulatory side doesn't change with the shorter routing. No rabies titre test applies from Australia, the same rule that holds for every origin on this site's Madagascar pages; the requirement is a vaccination record, not a blood result. What does take time is the import permit, a letter addressed to Madagascar's Direction des Services Veterinaires (DSV) rather than an online form.

    Allow twelve to sixteen weeks. The DSV letter, not the flight or the vet work, is what sets that timeline.

  sections:
    - heading: "A shorter route than the UK or US get"
      body: |
        Owners moving a pet from London or New York to Antananarivo are stuck stitching together a journey through Paris, Nairobi or Addis Ababa, each involving a change of carrier partway through. From Australia, the picture is genuinely simpler: Emirates' own Dubai-Antananarivo service means a pet can travel Melbourne (or Sydney, Perth, Brisbane) to Dubai to Antananarivo on largely one airline's cargo network, no third carrier required.

        That service has had a recent disruption, a suspension followed by a resumption on 23 October 2025, so it's worth a direct schedule check with Emirates SkyCargo closer to any booked travel date rather than assuming permanent, unbroken frequency.

    - heading: "No titre test, despite Madagascar's biosecurity reputation"
      body: |
        Madagascar's isolation and unique wildlife might suggest an entry regime built around extra blood tests. For a companion dog or cat, that's not what happens, from Australia or anywhere else. The requirement is a rabies vaccination given between 30 days and 12 months before arrival, full stop, with no titre test asked for regardless of origin country.

        This was checked specifically for Australia's own rabies-free status rather than assumed to follow automatically from the UK and US pages already published on this site: Madagascar's rule turns out to be genuinely origin-blind.

    - heading: "The DSV letter: apply well before booking a flight"
      body: |
        Madagascar's process runs through a letter of request addressed to the Directeur des Services Veterinaires, listing the owner's name and address, the animal's name, breed, age and sex, a photocopy of the vaccination record, the country of origin, the arrival date, the transport mode and the airport of disembarkation.

        Processing can take several weeks, and no fixed turnaround is published. Send it at least twelve to fourteen weeks before the intended travel date, and confirm the correct current submission channel with DSV directly.
