content:
  h1: "Pet Transport from Australia to Serbia"
  overview: |
    DAFF gives a pet exactly 72 hours to leave Australia once the government health certificate is signed. That single rule shapes the whole plan: book the flight first, then arrange the final vet check and certificate, not the other way round. Get that sequence backwards and the certificate expires before the pet ever reaches the airport.

    Once in the air, this is one of the more forgiving routes into Serbia. Australia sits on the Veterinary Directorate's Annex 2 list, so only a 21-day wait after rabies vaccination applies, no titre test and no three-month hold.

  sections:
    - heading: "The export side: an NOI, a certificate, and a 72-hour clock"
      body: |
        DAFF does not require a general export licence for a companion animal. What it does require is a Notice of Intention to Export Live Animals, lodged at least 10 working days ahead, followed by a final pre-export vet check close to the travel date and a government-issued health certificate. That certificate is only valid for 72 hours before the pet must leave the country, which means the flight needs booking before the certificate is signed, not scheduled around it afterwards.

    - heading: "Serbia's side: no titre test needed for an Australian pet"
      body: |
        Serbia's Veterinary Directorate places Australia on Annex 2, its list of non-European countries with a low incidence of rabies. That means a current rabies vaccination and a 21-day wait cover the requirement in full. No titre test, no three-month wait, and no import permit for a personal move of a single pet or two.

        Getting there still means a Doha or Dubai connection, since no airline flies Australia to Belgrade non-stop. Qatar Airways Cargo and Emirates SkyCargo both handle the sector, and either works well provided the booking is locked in before the DAFF certificate starts its 72-hour clock.
