content:
  h1: "Pet Transport from Senegal to the United States"
  overview: |
    Delta flies direct from Dakar to New York JFK. That's the first thing most owners find, and it's true. What's less obvious is that Delta's cargo pet service on that route is currently limited to active US military and State Department personnel moving under official orders. For everyone else, the direct flight exists for people, not for pets.

    The bigger driver of cost and timeline is Senegal's status on the CDC's high-risk list for dog rabies, in place since the August 2024 rule change. Every dog needs either a serology titre test from a CDC-approved lab or a 28-day stay at a CDC-registered facility on arrival. There's no third option.

    Cats have a much lighter path. The CDC's high-risk framework is written for dogs; a cat crosses with comparatively little federal paperwork.

  sections:
    - heading: "High-risk classification: the titre-or-quarantine choice"
      body: |
        The CDC sorts every country into two groups for dog rabies risk. Senegal sits in the high-risk group, alongside more than a hundred other countries. That status means a foreign-vaccinated dog needs one of two things before US entry: a serology titre report from a CDC-approved laboratory, or 28 days in a CDC-registered animal care facility on arrival.

        The titre route needs a blood draw at least 30 days after the rabies vaccination and at least 28 days before travel, plus lab turnaround on top. Realistically, that's a process measured in months, not weeks, once you factor in getting the sample to an approved lab and back.

        Skip the titre test and you're committing to the 28-day quarantine instead, at your own cost, arriving at a facility that has confirmed space for your dog. Neither path is quick. Choose early and plan around it.

    - heading: "Delta flies direct. Its cargo hold, for most owners, does not"
      body: |
        DL216 and DL217 connect Dakar and New York JFK several times a week on a Boeing 767, a genuinely useful direct option if you're travelling yourself. The pet cargo side of that same flight is a different matter: Delta currently limits it to active US military and State Department personnel under PCS orders.

        For anyone outside that category, Air France via Paris is the more workable option, with roughly 14 weekly Dakar-Paris flights and an established cargo pet programme open to the public. Turkish Airlines via Istanbul and Brussels Airlines via Brussels (capped at 4 pets per flight) round out the realistic choices.

        Whichever carrier you use, arrival airport matters more here than on most routes. If your dog is entering without a valid titre, it must land at a US airport with a CDC-registered animal care facility, with no domestic leg beforehand. Confirm that airport before you book anything else.

    answer: "The CDC classifies countries into high-risk and low-risk groups for dog rabies, based on a rule change effective 1 August 2024. Senegal is on the high-risk list. That triggers a mandatory serology titre test or a 28-day facility quarantine, neither of which applies to dogs coming from low-risk countries."
  - question: "What happens if my dog doesn't have a valid rabies titre result in time?"

  - question: "Does my cat face the same high-risk rules as a dog?"
    answer: "No. The CDC's high-risk country framework applies specifically to dogs. Cats travelling from Senegal face far fewer federal requirements at the US border, though you should still check the specific rules of your arrival state, since these aren't covered by CDC policy."
