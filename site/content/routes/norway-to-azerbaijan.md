  overview: |
    Azerbaijan accepts pets from Norway without a titre test requirement, and there is no quarantine for compliant arrivals at Baku's Heydar Aliyev International Airport. The paperwork is manageable for an EEA-origin country, but the cargo routing requires a hub connection and careful advance planning.

    There are no direct cargo flights from Oslo (OSL) to Baku (GYD). The established options are Turkish Airlines via Istanbul or Lufthansa via Frankfurt. Either route works, but summer months bring temperature embargoes that can restrict certain breeds on hub connections. Confirm your specific route well before your travel date.

    Norway is EEA rather than EU, but veterinary standards are equivalent. The health certificate must be issued by a Mattilsynet-authorised official vet and endorsed by the Mattilsynet regional office, which adds a 1-2 day buffer to the preparation timeline. Build this into your schedule.

  sections:
    - heading: "What Azerbaijan requires at the border"
      body: |
        Azerbaijan's veterinary border controls are administered by the Food Safety Agency (FSA). Officers inspect documentation at Heydar Aliyev International Airport (GYD) in Baku before releasing your pet.

        The core requirements are a valid rabies vaccination administered at least 21 days before entry, an official health certificate issued within 10 days of travel, and an ISO-compliant microchip. Azerbaijan does not require a rabies titre test for pets arriving from EEA countries, which removes one of the longer-lead requirements that apply on some non-EU routes.

        For personal companion animals of 1-2 pets, no import permit is needed. If you are travelling with more animals, check the commercial threshold with FSA Azerbaijan before booking. Verify the current position on any breed-specific restrictions directly with FSA if your pet is a breed that faces restrictions in other countries.

    - heading: "The Norwegian health certificate process"
      body: |
        Norway's official health certificate for non-EU destinations follows a two-step process. The certificate must be signed by a Mattilsynet-authorised official vet, then submitted to the Mattilsynet regional office for endorsement before it is valid for travel. Both steps must complete within the 10-day window before departure.

        Book the official vet appointment so the examination falls within 10 days of your travel date. After signing, allow 1-2 working days for the regional office endorsement. If your travel date is a Monday, book the vet appointment no later than the preceding Wednesday to have the endorsed document in hand before you travel.

        Your regular vet can handle vaccinations and microchipping, but the international health certificate must come from an official vet registered with Mattilsynet. Check mattilsynet.no for authorised official vets in your region.

    - heading: "Cargo routing from Oslo to Baku"
      body: |
        There is no direct cargo service between Oslo (OSL) and Baku (GYD). The two main options are Turkish Airlines Cargo via Istanbul (IST) and Lufthansa Cargo via Frankfurt (FRA).

        Turkish Airlines operates regular cargo services from Oslo to Istanbul, with onward connections to GYD. Book through an approved Turkish Airlines cargo agent. Confirm pet acceptance on both the OSL-IST and IST-GYD legs, and ask specifically about temperature embargo dates if you are travelling between June and September.

        Lufthansa Cargo connects Oslo to Frankfurt, and Frankfurt's Animal Lounge is a well-equipped live animal transit facility. From FRA, Lufthansa or partner carriers connect to Baku. This adds some route complexity but is a reliable alternative if Turkish Airlines restrictions apply to your specific pet.
