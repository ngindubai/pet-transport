  overview: |
    No airline flies directly out of Eswatini to anywhere beyond the region. That's the starting point for this route. Every pet leaving King Mswati III International Airport goes to Johannesburg first, on Airlink or Eswatini Air, before picking up an international connection to London.

    Add to that the titre test. Eswatini isn't on the UK's Part 1 or Part 2 list, so the standard unlisted-country rules apply: blood test, 90-day wait, no shortcuts. Between the regulatory timeline and the two-leg flight reality, this route rewards patience more than most.

    The Department of Veterinary and Livestock Services, under Eswatini's Ministry of Agriculture, handles the export paperwork. Get the permit application in early; it runs on its own clock separate from the titre wait.
  sections:
    - heading: "Why every pet transits Johannesburg"
      body: |
        King Mswati III International Airport, near Manzini, serves Eswatini's regional connections well but has no long-haul international carriers. Airlink and Eswatini Air both fly the short SHO-JNB hop into O.R. Tambo, Johannesburg, which is where the actual intercontinental journey begins on British Airways or South African Airways to Heathrow.

        This isn't a minor detail. A pet changing planes at Johannesburg needs a proper interline cargo handover, not a passenger-style transfer. Confirm with your booking agent, or the airlines directly, that the crate will move airside between the regional and international carrier without needing to clear South African customs as though it were entering the country. Get this wrong and you risk delays, extra fees, or the pet missing its connection.

    - heading: "The titre test timeline, and what to do while you wait"
      body: |
        Eswatini sits outside the UK's Part 1 and Part 2 lists, so the FAVN or ELISA titre test applies. Blood drawn at least 30 days after vaccination, sent to an APHA-approved lab, and a 90-day wait from a passing result before your pet can travel. That's roughly four months minimum before the wait even finishes.

        Use that time well. The Department of Veterinary and Livestock Services permit application, the flight bookings, and the crate purchase can all happen during the 90-day wait rather than after it. Only the final Animal Health Certificate needs to wait until close to your actual travel date, since it's only valid for 10 days.
