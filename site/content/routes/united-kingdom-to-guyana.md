content:
  h1: "Pet Transport from the United Kingdom to Guyana"
  overview: |
    The United Kingdom is one of just eleven origin countries the Guyana Livestock Development Authority currently treats as rabies-free, sitting alongside Ireland, Australia, New Zealand, Jamaica and a handful of small Caribbean states. That classification strips out the rabies titre test and the quarantine decision that pets from almost every other origin face on arrival in Georgetown.

    What's left is still real work. An import permit has to be issued by GLDA, a receiving vet has to be notified before the flight lands, and the only through-service, British Airways' twice-weekly Gatwick-St Lucia-Georgetown route, doesn't fly every day. Most of the planning on this route is about timing those three things together.

  sections:
    - heading: "What Guyana's rabies-free classification actually removes"
      body: |
        GLDA's own procedure is blunt about it: at the point the import permit application is reviewed, the officer checks whether the country of origin is high-rabies-risk, and if it is, up to 90 days of quarantine can follow. The UK isn't on that list. For a UK-origin pet, that decision resolves in the owner's favour before the application is even submitted, provided the vaccination and certificate are in order.

        It's worth treating that list as a live thing rather than a fixed fact, though. GLDA reviews it, and country classifications elsewhere in the world (the UK's own listed-country system for GB entry works the same way) do shift. Confirming the UK's current status with GLDA before booking the flight costs nothing and avoids a nasty surprise on arrival.

    - heading: "The single flight this route runs on"
      body: |
        British Airways' BA2159 is the only through-service between the UK and Guyana: London Gatwick to Georgetown, same aircraft and flight number, with a technical stop in St Lucia, operating on a Boeing 777 twice a week. There's no change of carrier and no separate cargo handover, which is a genuine advantage over most of the other unlisted-origin routes this site covers.

        A Miami connection via American Airlines exists as a fallback if BA2159's schedule doesn't fit, transferring at MIA onto American's nonstop Georgetown sector. It works. It's also an extra handover most owners don't need if the twice-weekly BA schedule can be planned around.

    - heading: "GLDA's import permit and the arrival vet"
      body: |
        The import permit itself, G$20,000, roughly USD 95, is normally issued within 3 working days once the application, the UK vet certificate and the vaccination records are complete. GLDA also assigns a vet to be present for the arrival, and asks to be notified at least 24 hours ahead of landing so that inspection can be arranged.

        None of that is unusual by international standards. It's just easy to forget when the headline news is "no titre test, no quarantine," since it can read as though nothing else is required.
