content:
  h1: "Pet Transport from Estonia to Turkey"
  overview: |
    An EU pet passport takes a chipped, vaccinated pet anywhere in the EU with no titre test, no quarantine, almost no friction. It's easy to assume that same document works for Turkey too. It doesn't. Turkey sits outside the EU's own low-risk country list, and the passport that's carried your pet freely between Tallinn and every other EU capital in this build stops being enough the moment the destination is Istanbul.

    What replaces it is the standard unlisted-origin protocol: a separate export certificate, a FAVN titre test, and a 90-day wait. It's a genuinely different process from the rest of Estonia's routes, and worth planning for as such.

  sections:
    - heading: "Why the EU passport doesn't carry over"
      body: |
        Every other Estonian route built in this project, into Poland, the Netherlands, Spain and beyond, relies on the EU pet passport doing all the work: no titre test, no separate certificate, no quarantine. Turkey breaks that pattern entirely. As a non-EU country classified outside the bloc's low-risk list, Turkey requires the same FAVN titre test protocol applied to unlisted origins across this build, plus a standalone export health certificate that follows Turkey's own model rather than the EU passport format.

        The Estonian Veterinary and Food Board (VTA) issues that separate certificate; it doesn't require a distinct export permit on top of it, so the extra step is the certificate itself, not additional bureaucracy.

    - heading: "The titre test and the flight"
      body: |
        Blood for the FAVN test must be drawn at least 30 days after the rabies vaccination, needs to clear a result of 0.5 IU/ml, and then a 90-day wait counted from that draw date has to pass before travel. A booster given after the test triggers its own separate 21-day wait, so time any revaccination carefully if it falls close to your travel date.

        Turkish Airlines flies Tallinn to Istanbul direct, a well-used route given the volume of travel between the two cities, so once the paperwork clears, the flight itself is the simplest part of the process.
