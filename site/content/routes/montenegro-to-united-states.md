  h1: "Pet Transport from Montenegro to United States"
  overview: |
    Here is the fact that surprises most owners planning this move: a dog leaving Montenegro for the United States needs no titre test at all. Montenegro simply does not appear on the CDC's high-risk countries for dog rabies list, last updated 15 April 2026, which means dogs travel under the low-risk pathway introduced on 1 August 2024. Rabies vaccination is recommended, not mandatory. There is no 90-day wait, no serology lab, no CDC-registered facility reservation.

    That is a sharp contrast with the same pet's route to the UK, where Montenegro's unlisted status triggers a full titre test and a fixed 90-day hold. The difference comes down to how each country classifies risk, not to anything about the pet itself.

    The paperwork that does remain is a CDC Dog Import Form for dogs (free, online, mandatory since August 2024) and a vet health certificate for the airline. Air Serbia's direct Belgrade-JFK service is the fastest route out, though its 8kg cabin-only limit rules out most dogs over small-breed size.

  sections:
    - heading: "Why the CDC treats Montenegro differently to the UK"
      body: |
        The CDC's binary system, introduced in August 2024, splits the world into high-risk and low-risk countries for dog rabies. Anywhere not named on the CDC's published high-risk list counts as low-risk by default. Montenegro is not on that list. The UK, by contrast, uses its own separate listed and unlisted framework, and Montenegro falls on the wrong side of that one.

        For a low-risk-origin dog, the requirements are administrative rather than veterinary. Complete the CDC Dog Import Form online before travel, and arrive at any US port of entry, air, sea or land. Rabies vaccination is strongly recommended by the CDC but is not a federal entry requirement for a low-risk country. Cats face no CDC requirements from any origin, Montenegro included.

        Owners who have already researched the UK route sometimes assume the same 90-day wait and titre test carry over. They do not. Checking the current CDC high-risk list before assuming otherwise avoids months of unnecessary delay.

    - heading: "The fastest way out: Air Serbia's direct New York service"
      body: |
        Montenegro's own airports, Podgorica and Tivat, have no direct US service. The shortest path runs through Belgrade, where Air Serbia operates a nonstop flight to New York JFK roughly five times a week outbound, taking around nine hours. Air Serbia's pet policy for this sector is cabin only, with a EUR 100 fee and an 8kg combined weight limit for pet and carrier, so this only works for small dogs and cats.

        For a larger dog, Turkish Airlines via Istanbul or Lufthansa via Frankfurt or Munich both carry hold pets to major US airports. Turkish Airlines' well-known refusal to carry pets into the UK does not extend to US-bound flights, so it is a genuine option here even though it was ruled out on the UK route page for the same city pair. Austrian Airlines via Vienna is a fourth option, though it adds a EUR 150 routing surcharge.

        Whichever carrier is chosen, book at least four to six weeks ahead. None of these routes are daily nonstops from Montenegro itself, so connection timing and crate handover between flights need checking directly with the airline.
