  h1: "Pet Transport from India to the United States"
  overview: |
    India is classified as a high-risk country for dog imports by the CDC, based on the presence of dog rabies. This means moving a dog from India to the USA is more complex than a standard low-risk country move. There are two pathways: the titre test pathway (provide a valid FAVN titre test result showing 0.5 IU/ml or above, drawn at least 30 days after vaccination and at least 28 days before arrival) or the quarantine pathway (agree to a 28-day quarantine at a CDC-registered animal care facility, at the owner's expense).

    For the quarantine pathway, dogs must arrive directly at a US airport where a CDC-registered facility is located. There can be no domestic connection first: the dog goes straight from the plane to the CDC facility. For the titre test pathway, dogs can arrive at any US airport.

    Cats from India face no US federal restrictions. USDA APHIS has no health requirements for pet cat imports.

    - heading: "The CDC titre test timing for India to USA"
      body: |
        The FAVN titre test must be conducted at a CDC-approved laboratory. Blood must be drawn at least 30 days after the most recent valid rabies vaccination. The titre test result must be from a sample drawn at least 28 days before US entry. Practically, this means starting the process at least 60 days before your planned travel date.

        The titre test result must show a result of 0.5 IU/ml or above. If the result is below 0.5 IU/ml, the dog cannot use the titre test pathway and must either re-vaccinate and re-test, or use the quarantine pathway on arrival.

        Keep the original titre test certificate. It must be presented at the US port of entry as part of the documentation set.
faqs:
  - question: "Why is India classified as a high-risk country for CDC dog imports?"
    answer: "The CDC classifies countries as high-risk based on the presence of dog-mediated rabies. India has a significant dog rabies challenge and is on the CDC's high-risk list. This means dogs from India cannot use the simple low-risk pathway (CDC Dog Import Form only) and must instead complete the high-risk documentation process, which includes either a valid FAVN titre test or a 28-day quarantine at a CDC-registered facility."
  - question: "What is the cheapest way to avoid the 28-day quarantine for my dog from India?"
    answer: "The titre test pathway avoids quarantine entirely. Have a valid FAVN titre test result (0.5 IU/ml or above) from a CDC-approved laboratory, drawn at least 30 days after the most recent valid rabies vaccination and at least 28 days before US entry. If the titre test result is satisfactory and all other documentation is in order, your dog arrives at any US airport and is released after a CDC inspection, without quarantine."
  - question: "Which US airports have CDC-registered animal care facilities?"
    answer: "Contact the CDC directly for the current list of registered facilities, as this list can change. As of recent records, facilities have operated at airports including JFK, LAX, and Miami. If you are using the quarantine pathway, confirm the facility is active at your intended entry airport before booking. Dogs must arrive directly at that airport with no domestic US connection."
  - question: "Do cats from India need special documents to enter the USA?"
    answer: "No. Cats face no US federal import requirements from India or any other country. USDA APHIS has no health restrictions on cat imports. Airlines require vet documentation for cargo shipments, and some US states may have their own rules, but federally cats from India move without restriction."
  - question: "Can my dog from India arrive at any US airport?"
    answer: "It depends on the pathway. If you have a valid titre test, yes, the dog can arrive at any US airport, seaport, or land crossing. If you are using the quarantine pathway (no titre test), the dog must arrive at an airport with an active CDC-registered animal care facility. No domestic connections are allowed before the facility inspection is complete."
