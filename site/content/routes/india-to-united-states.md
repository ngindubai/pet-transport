content:
  h1: "Pet Transport from India to the United States"
  overview: |
    Relocating a dog from India to the United States involves a specific CDC pathway that many owners do not know about until it is almost too late. India is classified by the CDC as a high-risk country for dog rabies, which means the rules for bringing a dog into the USA from India are more involved than for pets coming from low-risk countries. The key variable is how and where your dog was vaccinated against rabies. That single factor determines whether your dog arrives freely or faces a mandatory 28-day quarantine. Cats have no federal entry requirements, though airlines have their own documentation rules.

    On the Indian export side, the process mirrors other India-origin routes: an AQCS No-Objection Certificate is required before travel, plus an official health certificate in USDA APHIS format, endorsed by AQCS. This typically takes 4-6 weeks to arrange, so factor that into your timeline alongside the vaccination pathway decisions.

    This corridor is heavily used by H-1B tech workers, students on F-1 visas, and families relocating for work. The process is entirely manageable, but the CDC vaccination rules need to be understood before you vaccinate your dog, not after. Source: CDC cdc.gov/importation; USDA APHIS aphis.usda.gov.

  sections:
    - heading: "CDC dog import rules: what India's high-risk classification means for you"
      body: |
        The CDC divides countries into standard-risk and high-risk for dog rabies. India falls into the high-risk group. For dogs coming from high-risk countries, the CDC requires proof that the rabies vaccination was administered by a USDA-accredited veterinarian or at a CDC-recognised foreign laboratory. If that condition is met, the dog can enter the USA without quarantine. If it is not met, the dog must undergo a 28-day quarantine at a CDC-registered facility in the USA, at the owner's cost. Source: CDC, cdc.gov/importation.

        The CDC maintains a list of approved foreign rabies vaccination laboratories. As of 2024, the list includes specific laboratories in certain countries. Check the current list at cdc.gov/importation before vaccinating, as it is updated periodically. Many owners in India vaccinate their dogs at a standard Indian vet without checking this list first, which creates a problem at the US border.

        All dogs entering the USA must have a completed CDC Dog Import Form filed electronically before travel, regardless of origin country. This is a separate requirement from the airline's own documentation. The form is completed at cdc.gov/importation and is linked to the dog's vaccination record and entry details.

    - heading: "The AQCS export process and health documentation from India"
      body: |
        The Indian export side of this move requires an AQCS (Animal Quarantine and Certification Services) No-Objection Certificate, applied for through the regional AQCS office nearest your departure city. Offices are in Delhi, Mumbai, Chennai, Kolkata, Bangalore, and Hyderabad. Allow 4-6 weeks for the NOC process. The health certificate for US entry must be in USDA APHIS format, prepared by an Indian official vet and endorsed by AQCS. Two clinical examinations are required: the first approximately 7 days before departure, the second within 2 days of travel. The certificate is valid 14 days from issue.

        For cats, there are no federal US import requirements beyond what your airline specifies for cargo. Airlines typically require a health certificate for cats travelling as cargo. Check with your specific carrier well in advance.

        An IPATA-registered pet transport agent based in India is the most practical resource for managing the AQCS process, the USDA health certificate format, and the CDC form coordination simultaneously. Errors in any one of these three elements can cause problems at the US border.

    - heading: "Airlines for India to USA pet cargo transport"
      body: |
        All pet transport on India-USA international routes is cargo only. There is no cabin pet option on any long-haul India-US sector. Your pet will travel in a pressurised, temperature-controlled hold in an IATA-compliant crate. Non-stop options include Air India on Delhi-New York and Mumbai-New York routes. American Airlines, United Airlines, British Airways via London, and Emirates via Dubai provide connecting options to a wider range of US cities.

        Book cargo space well in advance. Live animal cargo capacity is limited and managed separately from passenger bookings. Some carriers require the CDC Dog Import Form reference to be provided at cargo check-in for dogs from high-risk countries. Your agent can confirm the exact requirements of your chosen carrier.

        Temperature restrictions apply seasonally. Both India and the USA have periods of extreme heat that can trigger embargoes on live animal cargo. Check restrictions for your travel date, particularly for summer months.

  - question: "What happens if my dog was vaccinated at a standard vet in India, not a CDC-approved one?"
    answer: "Your dog will face mandatory 28-day quarantine at a CDC-registered facility in the USA on arrival. The cost is paid by the owner and typically runs USD 1,500-3,000 or more. If you have not yet vaccinated your dog, check the CDC list of approved foreign laboratories first at cdc.gov/importation. If the vaccination has already been given at a non-approved vet, your options are to plan for quarantine or to re-vaccinate at an approved facility and wait for the new vaccination to take effect."
  - question: "Do cats need any vaccinations or permits to enter the USA from India?"
    answer: "No. The CDC has no federal rabies vaccination or health certificate requirements for cats entering the USA from any country, including India. However, airlines transporting cats as cargo typically require their own health documentation. Check with your specific carrier before travel."
  - question: "What is the CDC Dog Import Form and how do I complete it?"
    answer: "The CDC Dog Import Form is an electronic form required for all dogs entering the USA, regardless of origin. It must be completed before departure at cdc.gov/importation. It captures the dog's vaccination details, microchip number, origin country, and US destination. Dogs without a completed form will not be admitted to the USA. Keep a printout to present at cargo check-in."
  - question: "What is the AQCS NOC and why does my pet need it?"
    answer: "AQCS (Animal Quarantine and Certification Services) issues the No-Objection Certificate required for all pet exports from India. Without it, the health certificate cannot be correctly endorsed and airlines will not accept your pet on an international flight. Apply to the AQCS regional office in your city at least 4-6 weeks before travel. Source: aqcsindia.gov.in"
  - question: "How long does it take to prepare a pet for transport from India to the USA?"
    answer: "For cats and dogs with CDC-compliant vaccinations: 8-12 weeks from starting preparations, mostly taken up by the AQCS process and health certificate timing. For dogs whose vaccination does not meet CDC standards: factor in either re-vaccination time or the 28-day US quarantine. Start the process as early as possible and confirm your vaccination pathway first."
