  h1: "Pet Transport from India to the USA"
  overview: |
    India is on the CDC's list of high-risk countries for dog rabies. That means the standard CDC Dog Import Form process is not enough: your dog also needs a serological test from a CDC-approved laboratory, and must enter the USA at an airport with a CDC-registered animal care facility. Plan for a four to six month preparation window.

    The AQCS export process on the India side runs in parallel: a No Objection Certificate from the AQCS in your city, plus a health certificate from an AQCS-authorised vet. United and Delta both have services from Delhi and Mumbai, and Lufthansa via Frankfurt is a reliable alternative for the transatlantic leg. An agent with experience managing both the AQCS export and the CDC high-risk protocol is the most effective way to handle this route.

    - heading: "The CDC serology test for high-risk countries: key details"
      body: |
        For dogs from high-risk countries like India, the CDC serology test works like this: a vet draws blood at least 30 days after the rabies vaccination. The sample goes to a CDC-approved laboratory. The result must show at least 0.5 IU/ml. Results typically take 10-21 days.

        Once you have a passing result, it is valid for the life of the dog provided the vaccination coverage does not lapse and the laboratory stays CDC-approved. This means if your dog already has a passing result on file from a previous test done at a CDC-approved lab, with continuous vaccination coverage, you may not need a new test. Verify this with your vet and the CDC guidance before assuming.

        The blood draw date must be at least 28 days before your US entry date. That is 28 days from the blood draw, not 28 days from when results arrive. Plan the blood draw date first, then build your travel date around it.

faqs:
  - question: "Why is India classified as high-risk by the CDC?"
    answer: "The CDC classifies countries based on the presence of dog-mediated rabies. India has endemic dog-related rabies, which places it in the high-risk category. This triggers additional requirements: a serological test at a CDC-approved lab and entry only at airports with CDC-registered animal care facilities. The designation reflects disease risk, not the quality of Indian veterinary care."
  - question: "How long does the India to USA pet move take?"
    answer: "Allow 14-22 weeks. The minimum gap is around 12 weeks from vaccination to US entry (30 days post-vaccination before blood draw, 28 days from blood draw to US entry), but getting results, completing the CDC form, booking cargo, and obtaining the AQCS NOC adds several more weeks. A 16-20 week plan is realistic for most owners."
  - question: "Do I need the AQCS NOC if my pet was born in India?"
    answer: "Yes. The AQCS NOC is required for any dog or cat being exported from India, regardless of where the pet was born or how long it has lived there. Apply at the AQCS office in your city with your pet's microchip confirmation, vaccination records, and the titre test result."
  - question: "Do cats need anything to enter the USA from India?"
    answer: "No federal documentation is required for cats entering the USA from India. Your airline will likely require a health certificate, typically issued within 10 days of travel. If your destination is Hawaii, the state's HDOA quarantine programme applies to cats and must be handled separately. For the continental US, cats face no federal entry restrictions."
  - question: "Which US airport is best for arriving from India?"
    answer: "You need an airport with a CDC-registered animal care facility. New York (JFK/EWR), Chicago (ORD), Houston (IAH), Los Angeles (LAX), and San Francisco (SFO) are among the airports with registered facilities. Check the CDC's current approved list before booking, as the list can change."
