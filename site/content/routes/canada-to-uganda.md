  sections:
    - heading: "MAAIF: Uganda's import authority"
      body: |
        Uganda's Ministry of Agriculture, Animal Industry and Fisheries (MAAIF) at maaif.go.ug processes companion animal imports. The MAAIF import permit is the document that authorises your pet's entry. Without it, the inspection team at Entebbe cannot clear your animal.

        Apply with your pet's details: microchip number, species, breed, vaccination history, and intended travel date. Eight to ten weeks of lead time is recommended. MAAIF is a functioning authority that processes permit applications from a range of countries, though turnaround times can vary. Follow up after submitting to confirm your application is in progress.

        On arrival at Entebbe International Airport (EBB), MAAIF's team inspects the permit alongside the CFIA health certificate. For pets from Canada with complete documentation, clearance proceeds without quarantine.

    - heading: "Canada to Entebbe: the two main routing options"
      body: |
        Brussels Airlines via Brussels (BRU) and Ethiopian Airlines via Addis Ababa (ADD) are the two most commonly used options for Canada-to-Uganda pet cargo.

        Brussels Airlines operates BRU-EBB directly as part of its established East Africa network. Getting from Toronto or Montreal to Brussels means a transatlantic hop on Air Canada or another carrier, followed by the Brussels Airlines East Africa service. This routing works well and Brussels Airlines is experienced on the BRU-EBB corridor.

        Ethiopian Airlines via Addis Ababa (ADD) gives access to Ethiopian Cargo's extensive African network. ADD-EBB is a regular short hop within Ethiopian's African operation. From Canada, reaching Addis involves connections through European or Gulf hubs. The overall routing is longer but Ethiopian's live animal procedures on African routes are well established.

        Kenya Airways via Nairobi (NBO) is a third option. NBO-EBB is a short East African regional service, and Kenya Airways connects from Gulf and European hubs. Total transit from Canada to Entebbe runs 18-28 hours depending on routing.

    - heading: "CFIA endorsement for Uganda"
      body: |
        The Canadian Food Inspection Agency (CFIA) endorses health certificates for international pet exports. Your accredited vet prepares the health certificate; CFIA's regional office then endorses it.

        For Uganda, confirm that the certificate format meets Uganda's specific import requirements, which differ from EU-format certificates. Your vet or a pet transport agent experienced in African exports will know the correct format. CFIA endorsement takes 1-5 working days through the regional office network.

        Time the vet appointment and CFIA endorsement carefully within the 10-day validity window. A vet appointment 7-8 days before travel with 2-3 days buffer for endorsement is a practical approach.
