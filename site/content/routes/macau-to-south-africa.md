content:
  h1: "Pet Transport from Macau to South Africa"
  overview: |
    DALRRD's easy path, no titre test, applies mainly to a short list of countries with a bilateral veterinary agreement: the UK, the USA, the EU and Australia among them. Macau isn't on that list, so the full rabies antibody titre test applies here, blood drawn at least 30 days after vaccination, then a 90-day wait before the pet can travel.

    The consolation is what happens after. South Africa doesn't run a routine quarantine for pets that turn up with everything in order, a genuinely lighter landing than several other destinations on this site once the pre-travel medical work is done.

  sections:
    - heading: "Why the bilateral-agreement gap matters here"
      body: |
        DALRRD's titre test exemption isn't about a country's rabies status in the abstract; it's about whether South Africa has a specific bilateral veterinary arrangement with that country. The UK, the USA, the EU member states and Australia sit inside that small group. Macau doesn't, regardless of how well-controlled rabies is within its own borders, so the standard titre pathway applies in full.

        Budget the sequence accordingly: vaccination, a 30-day wait, the blood draw, then a further 90 days before the pet is eligible to fly. There's no way to compress this once the blood has been drawn.

    - heading: "No routine quarantine, if the paperwork is right"
      body: |
        Once the titre wait clears and the DALRRD import permit is issued, South Africa doesn't add a mandatory holding period at the airport the way Australia or Hong Kong do. A state veterinarian inspects the pet against its documentation on arrival at Johannesburg or Cape Town, and a compliant animal is released without a facility stay.

        That inspection is only a formality if the documentation actually matches. Incomplete or incorrect paperwork can still trigger a penalty quarantine, so keep the original certificates, not copies, ready for the inspecting vet.

    - heading: "Routing through Hong Kong"
      body: |
        Macau International Airport has no long-haul cargo capacity of its own, so every pet on this route makes the same overland or ferry transfer into Hong Kong that this site documents for Macau's other international moves. From there, Cathay Pacific Cargo's direct Hong Kong-Johannesburg sector is the most established option, with Emirates SkyCargo via Dubai and Qatar Airways Cargo via Doha as alternatives worth comparing on price and transit time.
