  h1: "Pet Transport from the United Kingdom to Grenada"
  overview: |
    A completed Pet Permit Application Form, stating the animal's age, sex, colour and breed, has to sit in the hands of Grenada's Veterinary and Livestock Division at least two weeks before the pet's actual arrival date. Miss that window and the flight books itself around a permit that isn't ready yet, which is the single most common way this route goes wrong.

    Set that against a relatively light set of health requirements, ISO microchip before vaccine, a rabies shot given 30 days to 12 months ahead, and Grenada's published guidance doesn't describe a titre test for pets coming from a rabies-controlled country such as the UK. No blood test, no 90-day wait, just the permit timing and the standard vaccination sequence.

    Getting there means British Airways, and only British Airways; no other carrier connects Gatwick with Maurice Bishop International. Even that route isn't a single nonstop hop, recent schedules run through a technical stop in the Eastern Caribbean before landing in Grenada.

  sections:
    - heading: "The two-week permit window, and why it decides the schedule"
      body: |
        Grenada's Veterinary and Livestock Division wants the Pet Permit Application Form, complete with the animal's age, sex, colour and breed, alongside the vet-issued health certificate and rabies certificate, at least two weeks ahead of arrival. That's not a target to aim for loosely; it's the point the whole booking timeline should be built backward from.

        Compared with routes that require a 90-day titre wait, two weeks sounds forgiving. It is, provided the paperwork actually lands with the Division on time, in the right combination, rather than trickling in piece by piece.

    - heading: "A lighter health requirement than it might look"
      body: |
        Grenada asks for the standard sequence: microchip before vaccine, rabies vaccination given no earlier than 12 months and no later than 30 days before travel. What Grenada's published guidance doesn't describe is a titre test for pets arriving from a country like the UK with a controlled rabies risk.

        That's worth double-checking directly with the Veterinary and Livestock Division rather than assuming, since published guidance and current practice don't always match perfectly on smaller destinations. But on the evidence available, this route skips the blood test and the long wait that comes with it.

    - heading: "British Airways, via a stop in the Eastern Caribbean"
      body: |
        Flight BA2159 runs Gatwick to Grenada on a Boeing 777, roughly ten to eleven hours including a technical stop, commonly St Lucia or Tobago on recent schedules. Pets travel through IAG Cargo rather than as hold baggage, since British Airways doesn't carry live animals as checked baggage on any route.

        There's no alternative direct carrier to compare it against. Anything else means routing through a European or North American hub first, which usually adds cost and transit time without necessarily improving on British Airways' own schedule.
