  overview: |
    A road-shortened version of this journey doesn't exist. Vientiane has no long-haul cargo option of its own, so every pet transfers through Bangkok, Hanoi or Seoul first, and the EU side of the process runs on its own separate clock once that transfer is arranged.

    Laos sits outside the EU's list of low-risk third countries, so France applies the standard titre test and 90-day wait sequence in full, the same rule that applies across the bloc regardless of destination city.

  sections:
    - heading: "Bangkok as the default transfer point"
      body: |
        Lao Airlines flies Vientiane to Bangkok twice daily, feeding into onward services toward Paris. This is the routing most agents on this corridor default to, since Bangkok's cargo infrastructure is well established for connecting Southeast Asian departures to Europe.

        Once in Paris, the Roissy Animal Hotel, run jointly by Air France, KLM and Martinair Cargo, handles the arrival inspection on site. Booking that slot ahead of the flight avoids delays at the general cargo terminal.

    - heading: "The titre test sequence that sets the calendar"
      body: |
        France applies the same EU protocol as every member state: microchip before vaccination, a 30-day gap before the titre blood draw, a passing result of at least 0.5 IU/ml, then a fixed 90-day wait from the draw date, under Commission Delegated Regulation (EU) 2026/131 and Implementing Regulation (EU) 2026/636.

        Laos has few, if any, EU-approved laboratories of its own, so plan for the blood sample to be couriered abroad for testing, adding time beyond the usual lab turnaround. DLF's 15-day export notice period runs well within that wait and rarely changes the overall schedule.
