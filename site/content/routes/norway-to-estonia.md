  sections:
    - heading: "Why Norway's document is different from an EU pet passport"
      body: |
        EU member states issue EU pet passports to their resident pets. Norway, as an EEA member but not an EU member, sits in a different category. Under EU Regulation 576/2013, Norway is classified as a listed third country, meaning its pets can enter EU member states without a titre test or quarantine, but the document they carry is an official health certificate in the EU-approved format for listed countries, not an EU pet passport.

        In practice, this is a minor administrative difference. The certificate records the same information: microchip number, rabies vaccination history, and the issuing vet's details. It is issued by a Mattilsynet-authorised official vet in Norway. The key practical point is to get this certificate from a vet who is specifically authorised as an official vet under Mattilsynet, not just any vet in Norway.

        Estonia's VTA (Veterinaar- ja Toiduamet) accepts this certificate for Norwegian pets in the same way it accepts EU pet passports. The requirements are otherwise identical: ISO 15-digit microchip implanted before the rabies vaccination, and a current rabies vaccination.

    - heading: "Oslo to Tallinn: route and carrier options"
      body: |
        Oslo Gardermoen (OSL) to Tallinn (TLL) is served by SAS on direct or connecting services. SAS Cargo handles the live animal booking for larger dogs. For cabin pets, SAS's standard policies apply on this sector.

        airBaltic connects OSL to Tallinn via Riga, which takes the total journey time to around 3 to 4 hours with the connection. airBaltic Cargo at Riga is a well-run operation given RIX is the airline's home hub.

        Norwegian Air Shuttle operates Oslo to Tallinn on some schedules but takes only cabin pets. For anything larger than a cabin-sized dog or cat, Norwegian is not the right carrier on this route.

  - question: "What is the difference between the Norwegian health certificate and an EU pet passport?"
    answer: "An EU pet passport is issued to pets resident in EU member states and serves as both an identity document and a travel document for intra-EU movements. Norway's official health certificate for listed countries serves the same function for travel to EU member states, but it is an export document issued specifically for travel, not a permanent passport. It has a validity window, so timing the appointment close to travel matters."

    answer: "Yes. Norway is a listed country under EU Regulation 576/2013, meaning its pets are treated equivalently to EU pets for rabies risk classification. No titre test is required for Norwegian pets entering Estonia."
