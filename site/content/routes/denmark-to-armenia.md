  overview: |
    Armenia is an increasingly accessible destination for pet travel from Denmark. The route does not require a titre test for EU-origin pets, and there is no quarantine for arrivals with the right paperwork. That makes it a more straightforward move than many non-EU destinations, though the logistics still need careful planning.

    The main practical challenge is the cargo routing. There are no direct cargo flights from Copenhagen (CPH) to Yerevan (EVN). All routes connect through a hub, most commonly Istanbul (IST) on Turkish Airlines. You will need to book through a cargo agent, confirm pet acceptance on both legs, and allow for any seasonal restrictions the carrier applies during summer months.

    Preparation time of three to five weeks is realistic for most pets, assuming vaccinations are already current. The Foedevarestyrelsen endorsement of your health certificate adds a working-day buffer you should factor in when timing the vet appointment.

  sections:
    - heading: "What Armenia requires at the border"
      body: |
        Armenia's import rules for pets are administered by the State Service for Food Safety (SSFS), ssfs.am. Veterinary inspection takes place at Zvartnots International Airport (EVN) in Yerevan. Officers check documentation before releasing your pet, so having originals to hand matters.

        The core requirements are a valid rabies vaccination (at least 21 days before entry, within 11 months), an official health certificate issued within 10 days of travel, and an ISO-compliant microchip. Armenia does not require a rabies titre test for pets arriving from EU member states, which removes one of the longer waiting periods that other non-EU routes impose.

        For personal companion animals of 1-2 pets, no import permit is needed. If you are travelling with more animals, confirm the commercial threshold with SSFS before your trip. Armenia does not have a published breed ban list for common companion animals, but verify this with SSFS directly if you own a breed that draws restrictions in other countries.

    - heading: "The health certificate process in Denmark"
      body: |
        Denmark's export health certificate for non-EU destinations is an EU-format official certificate. It must be signed by a Foedevarestyrelsen-authorised official vet, then endorsed by the Foedevarestyrelsen regional office before it is valid for travel. This two-step process adds time that catches people out if they leave it too late.

        Book your official vet appointment so the examination falls within 10 days of your travel date. After signing, the certificate goes to the Foedevarestyrelsen regional office for endorsement. Allow 1-2 working days for that step. If your travel date is a Monday, this means booking the vet appointment no later than the Wednesday of the previous week to have the endorsed document in hand.

        Your regular vet can handle vaccinations and microchipping, but the health certificate must be from an authorised official vet. These are typically found at larger veterinary practices or specialist practices registered with Foedevarestyrelsen. Check fvst.dk for the list of authorised vets in your region.

    - heading: "Cargo routing from Copenhagen to Yerevan"
      body: |
        There is no direct cargo service between Copenhagen (CPH) and Yerevan (EVN). The most established route is Turkish Airlines cargo via Istanbul (IST). Turkish Airlines operates a significant cargo network from Copenhagen and serves EVN from IST, making it the default choice for this route.

        Book cargo space through an approved Turkish Airlines cargo agent rather than directly through the airline's passenger booking system. Pets as cargo require a separate booking, crate measurements, and confirmation of pet acceptance on both the CPH-IST and IST-EVN legs. Summer months (June to September) see temperature embargoes on some routes. If you are travelling in this period, confirm acceptance early and check whether your pet's breed falls under brachycephalic restrictions.

        An alternative is to use SAS Cargo from Copenhagen to a major hub (Frankfurt or Amsterdam), then connect to another carrier serving EVN. This adds complexity but provides options if Turkish Airlines summer restrictions are a problem for your specific pet. Whichever routing you choose, confirm the full chain with a cargo agent before committing to a travel date.
