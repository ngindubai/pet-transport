  h1: "Pet Transport from United Arab Emirates to Cameroon"
  overview: |
    Addis Ababa to Douala belongs to exactly one airline. Ethiopian Airlines flies it nonstop roughly nine times a week, and no rival carrier competes on that specific city pair at all. Combine that with Ethiopian's own Dubai-Addis Ababa service, up to four times daily, and a pet leaving the UAE for Cameroon can stay on one airline's network from Dubai all the way to Douala, never changing hands between carriers.

    The regulatory side is comparatively light. No titre test applies from the UAE, matching Cameroon's position toward every other origin country on this site. What does apply, and catches owners out more than the paperwork does, is a breed rule: Savannah cats, Bengal cats and wolf hybrid dogs are barred unless the pedigree shows five generations clear of that wild ancestry.

    MOCCAE handles the UAE export side through a single online process. Budget six to ten weeks overall, with the 30-day rabies vaccination window setting the earliest realistic travel date.

  sections:
    - heading: "Why Ethiopian owns this route"
      body: |
        Ethiopian Airlines is currently the only airline flying nonstop between Addis Ababa and Douala, a route it covers roughly nine times a week. Combined with its own frequent Dubai-Addis Ababa service, up to four flights a day on a Boeing 777, this means a pet can travel the entire Dubai-to-Douala journey on Ethiopian's aircraft and under Ethiopian's own live animal handling, with just one stop rather than a full carrier change.

        Turkish Airlines via Istanbul remains a genuine alternative, and Turkish Cargo's Pet Lounge is a real advantage if the connection runs long. But it does mean a different airline and a different cargo terminal partway through the trip, something the Ethiopian routing avoids by design rather than luck.

    - heading: "The breed rule that has nothing to do with vaccines"
      body: |
        Cameroon's toughest requirement on this route isn't medical at all. Wolf hybrid dogs, and Savannah or Bengal cats, need a pedigree showing five clear generations removed from their wild ancestry before MINEPIA will let them in. No health certificate, however thorough, resolves this; it's a breed registry document, and it needs checking before a flight or a crate gets booked.

        Most household pets never touch this rule. It matters specifically for these named breeds and crosses, and the fix, where a fix exists, is producing the paperwork the breeder or registry already holds, not anything a vet visit can generate on the spot.
