  sections:
    - heading: "Getting the SENASA certificate right for UK entry"
      body: |
        The health certificate for UK entry must be issued by a vet authorised by SENASA (Argentina's national food safety authority). The certificate must meet APHA's specific requirements for the certificate format used for Argentine-origin pets.

        Not every Argentine vet is familiar with the UK-specific requirements. Buenos Aires has several veterinary practices that work regularly with international pet moves, including to the UK. Using one of these experienced vets is worth the effort, as a certificate in the wrong format is rejected at the UK border inspection post regardless of whether the animal is healthy and vaccinated.

        The certificate must be issued within 10 days of travel. This 10-day window is tight for a long-haul booking. Confirm your travel date well in advance and book the vet appointment for the final week before departure.

    - heading: "Microchip standards in Argentina"
      body: |
        Argentina uses microchips, but the ISO 11784/11785 standard is not universally followed. Some Argentine-registered chips use the 125 kHz FDX-A format rather than the ISO 15-digit standard, and UK border scanners may not read them correctly.

        Before starting any other preparation, ask your Argentine vet for the chip's frequency and standard in writing. If it is not ISO 11784/11785, get a new ISO-standard chip implanted. The new chip must go in before any vaccination that counts toward UK entry requirements. If the timeline is tight, this needs to be sorted immediately.

faqs:
  - question: "Does my pet need to quarantine when entering the UK from Argentina?"
    answer: "No. Argentina is a listed country under APHA rules. No quarantine is required for compliant arrivals. Your pet needs a current health certificate in the APHA-specified format for Argentine-origin pets, a valid ISO microchip implanted before the rabies vaccination, and a valid rabies vaccination (at least 21 days since primary). Dogs need tapeworm treatment 24-120 hours before UK arrival."
  - question: "Can I connect through the USA when flying from Argentina to the UK?"
    answer: "You can, but it adds complexity for dogs. If your journey stops at a US airport (even in transit), US import requirements for dogs apply, including microchip and documentation checks. Argentina is not a CDC high-risk country, so the process is less fraught than for some other South American countries, but it is still an additional layer. A direct or European-connecting (via Madrid or Paris) route is simpler."
  - question: "How long does the overall process take?"
    answer: "Allow 6-10 weeks from starting preparation to travel. The main timeline driver is whether vaccinations and microchip are already current. If everything is in order, the process is 2-3 weeks (for certificate appointment and booking). If the microchip needs replacing or vaccinations need updating, the 21-day wait pushes the minimum to about 6-8 weeks."
  - question: "Which airline is best for pet cargo from Buenos Aires to London?"
    answer: "British Airways operates a direct Heathrow to Buenos Aires Ezeiza service and is the most straightforward option. IAG Cargo manages the pet transport. Iberia via Madrid and Air France via Paris are alternatives that involve one connection. LATAM also has cargo services on this route. For large breeds on a very long flight, discuss heat protocols and crate specifications with the carrier."
