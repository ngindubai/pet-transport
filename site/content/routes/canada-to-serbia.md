content:
  h1: "Pet Transport from Canada to Serbia"
  overview: |
    An appointment book, not a vet's diary, sets the pace on the Canadian side of this move. A licensed vet can complete the export certificate any time, but it means nothing to Serbian border control until an official CFIA veterinarian endorses it, in person, at a booked appointment, before the pet leaves the country. Leave that until the week of travel and there may not be a slot.

    Serbia's own entry rules are the easier half. Canada sits on the Veterinary Directorate's Annex 2 list, so a 21-day wait after rabies vaccination is all that's required, no titre test.

  sections:
    - heading: "The CFIA appointment is the part that needs booking first"
      body: |
        A vet completing a health certificate is routine. Getting an official CFIA veterinarian to endorse it is the step that actually takes planning, since it runs through a district office appointment system rather than a walk-in service, and processing runs 2 to 5 business days depending on location and season, longer in summer. CFIA is explicit that it cannot endorse a certificate for a pet that has already left Canada, so this has to happen before departure, not as a formality tidied up afterwards.

    - heading: "A new Toronto option, and the two fallbacks either side of it"
      body: |
        Air Serbia's twice-weekly Toronto-Belgrade service, launched in May 2026 after 34 years away from the route, is a genuine convenience for a small pet, at the same roughly EUR 130 fee it charges on its established New York and Chicago services. It only runs through the northern summer, though, and only for pets under 8kg, since Air Serbia offers no cargo hold pet option on any route.

        For a bigger dog, or a move that falls outside that seasonal window, Lufthansa Cargo through Frankfurt and Turkish Airlines Cargo through Istanbul both run year-round into Belgrade.

    - heading: "Serbia's Annex 2 entry: the easier half of this move"
      body: |
        Once the pet lands, Serbia's own Annex 2 treatment of Canada keeps the rest of the process simple. A current vaccination and a 21-day wait cover the whole requirement, no titre test, no import permit for a personal move of one or two animals. It's worth confirming that classification hasn't shifted before you rely on it, since Serbia updates its own country lists on its own schedule, separate from anything CFIA does on the Canadian side.

    - heading: "What actually costs the most time"
      body: |
        Owners moving from Canada tend to assume the flight is the bottleneck. It usually isn't. The CFIA appointment and the microchip-before-vaccination sequence matter more, because both are fixed points that can't be compressed no matter how much is spent on cargo space. Build the schedule backwards from the CFIA appointment date, not forwards from a preferred travel date.
