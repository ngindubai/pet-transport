content:
  h1: "Pet Transport from Laos to United Arab Emirates: 2026 Guide"
  overview: |
    No direct flight links Vientiane to the Gulf, so every pet on this route makes at least one stop, most often in Bangkok, where Emirates, Etihad and Qatar Airways all run established cargo services into Dubai, Abu Dhabi and Doha.

    Laos isn't on MOCCAE's named low-risk list, so a rabies titre test applies before the UAE import permit can be requested. The UAE doesn't add the EU's fixed 90-day wait after the blood draw, which keeps the overall timeline to roughly 10 to 14 weeks, most of it spent on the titre test and its international lab turnaround.

  sections:
    - heading: "Why the titre test applies, and what speeds it up"
      body: |
        MOCCAE only exempts origin countries it names directly on its low-risk list, and Laos isn't one of them. The standard sequence follows: microchip before vaccination, then a titre test at an accredited laboratory showing at least 0.5 IU/ml. What the UAE doesn't add, unlike the EU's 90-day rule, is a fixed wait once that result is confirmed. The moment the test passes and the MOCCAE import permit is issued, the pet can travel.

        The practical bottleneck is the test itself. Laos has few, if any, laboratories accredited to the standard MOCCAE requires, so blood typically needs to be shipped abroad, adding real time on top of ordinary lab processing.

    - heading: "Routing through Bangkok or Hanoi"
      body: |
        Lao Airlines' Vientiane-Bangkok service, running twice daily, is the workhorse connection for this route, feeding into any of the three major Gulf carriers' well-established cargo operations from Bangkok. Vietnam Airlines' Vientiane-Hanoi service is the practical alternative when Bangkok scheduling doesn't line up, though it typically means a longer overall transit.
