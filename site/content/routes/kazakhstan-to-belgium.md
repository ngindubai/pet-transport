content:
  h1: "Pet Transport from Kazakhstan to Belgium"
  overview: |
    Kazakhstan to Belgium is a five to six month process. Kazakhstan is EU-unlisted, so the full EU import protocol applies: microchip before vaccination, FAVN titre test, 90-day wait, and Kazakh Ministry of Agriculture-endorsed EU health certificate.

    Air Astana from Almaty or Astana to Amsterdam (with a short AMS-to-BRU connection) is the most natural routing. Turkish Airlines via Istanbul is the alternative. One important note: Kazakhstan primarily uses EAEU-format certificates; you will need your vet to prepare an EU-format certificate for Belgian entry.

  sections:
    - heading: "Belgium's EU requirements for Kazakh pets"
      body: |
        Belgium applies EU Regulation 576/2013 to all pets from unlisted third countries. Kazakhstan is unlisted. The steps: ISO microchip implanted before vaccination, rabies vaccination, FAVN titre test at an EU-approved laboratory (minimum 0.5 IU/ml, blood drawn 30+ days after vaccination), 90-day wait from blood draw date, and EU-format health certificate endorsed by the Kazakh Veterinary Committee, valid 10 days.

        FASFC inspects incoming pets at Brussels Airport (BRU). Compliant pets are released without quarantine.

    - heading: "EU-format health certificate from Kazakhstan: an important distinction"
      body: |
        Kazakhstan is a member of the Eurasian Economic Union (EAEU). For movements between EAEU countries, Kazakhstan uses the EAEU-format veterinary certificate. This format is not the same as the EU-format required for pet entry into Belgium.

        For your pet to enter Belgium, your Kazakh vet must prepare and have endorsed an EU-format health certificate complying with EU Regulation 2020/692 for non-commercial movement from unlisted countries. Discuss this specifically with your vet in Kazakhstan, or with an IPATA-registered agent, before starting the documentation process. Not all vets in Kazakhstan routinely prepare EU-format export certificates, so finding one with the right experience is important.

    - heading: "Routing from Kazakhstan to Brussels"
      body: |
        Air Astana operates from Almaty (ALA) and Astana (NQZ) to Amsterdam Schiphol (AMS). This is the most natural long-haul option: Air Astana Cargo handles live animals on these routes, and Amsterdam is close to Brussels. From AMS, a feeder flight (Brussels Airlines, Ryanair, or others) or surface transport completes the journey to Belgium.

        Turkish Airlines via Istanbul is the simpler single-carrier option if you prefer to avoid the AMS connection. ALA or NQZ to IST then IST to BRU on Turkish Airlines Cargo.

        Flydubai via Dubai and Lufthansa Cargo via Frankfurt are alternatives if neither Air Astana nor Turkish Airlines works on your preferred dates.
