content:
  h1: "Pet Transport India to Greece: The FAVN Titre Test Route"
  overview: "Greece is an EU member state, and that means India is treated as an unlisted third country for pet travel. Your dog or cat needs a microchip, a rabies vaccination, a FAVN titre test with a result of at least 0.5 IU/ml, and then a 90-day wait before travel is allowed. Get the sequence right and your pet arrives in Greece without quarantine. Allow at least five to six months from starting the process to travel day."

  sections:
    - heading: "Why the 90-day wait exists"
      body: |
        Under EU Regulation 2016/429, third countries are either listed (considered low-risk, no titre test needed) or unlisted. India is unlisted, which means the EU requires proof that your pet's rabies vaccination has produced adequate immunity, confirmed by a blood titre test at an EU-approved laboratory.

        The 90-day wait is not arbitrary. It reflects the incubation period for rabies in unvaccinated animals and the time needed to confirm sustained immunity. The clock starts on the date the laboratory confirms a result of 0.5 IU/ml or above, not the date of the vaccination or the date you receive the result. Keep the laboratory report as it records the blood draw date.
    - heading: "Departure from India: the AQCS step"
      body: |
        India does not issue a formal export permit for pets, but the Animal Quarantine and Certification Services (AQCS) plays an important role on departure day. When you check your pet in as cargo at DEL, BOM or MAA, an AQCS officer inspects the animal and the documentation, and issues an NOC (No Objection Certificate) on the spot.

        Have all documents in order before you arrive: the health certificate, the titre test result, the vaccination record, and the AHC issued within the previous 10 days. The AQCS inspection is quick when paperwork is complete; delays happen when documents are missing or out of date.

faqs:
  - question: "Does Greece require quarantine for pets from India?"
    answer: "No. Greece does not impose quarantine on pets from India, provided the full EU import protocol is complete before arrival. That means an ISO microchip, a valid rabies vaccination, a FAVN titre test result of 0.5 IU/ml or above, a 90-day wait from the blood draw date, and an EU Animal Health Certificate issued within 10 days before entry. If documentation is incomplete on arrival, the pet can be held at the owner's expense."
  - question: "How long does the FAVN titre test take in India?"
    answer: "The test itself is processed in a few days at an EU-approved laboratory. However, the blood must be drawn at least 21 days after the rabies vaccination, and the 90-day wait starts only once a valid result is confirmed. Turnaround from an approved Indian laboratory is typically 5 to 14 days. Factor in the full sequence when planning: vaccination, 21 days, blood draw, 5 to 14 days result, 90 days wait, then travel."
  - question: "Which airports in India have AQCS facilities for pet export?"
    answer: "AQCS offices operate at Indira Gandhi International (DEL), Chhatrapati Shivaji Maharaj International (BOM) and Chennai International (MAA). If you are in another city, your pet will need to transit through one of these three airports on departure. Check with your cargo agent that the animal transfer between airports is handled correctly."
  - question: "Can I fly my pet as cabin baggage from India to Greece?"
    answer: "No. Greece is not reachable by cabin baggage with a pet from India. The journey requires a connecting flight, and most airlines on this route only accept pets as manifest cargo. Your dog or cat travels in the aircraft hold in an IATA-compliant crate, either on the same flight as you or on a separate cargo booking."
