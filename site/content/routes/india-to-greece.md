    - heading: "The FAVN titre test and 90-day wait: India to EU"
      body: |
        Because India is not on the EU's list of approved third countries, all pets travelling from India to any EU member state (including Greece) must satisfy the FAVN titre test requirement. The blood must be drawn at least 30 days after the primary rabies vaccination, and the test must be performed at an EU-approved laboratory. The result must be at least 0.5 IU/ml.

        Once the blood draw date is established and the result is satisfactory, the pet must wait 90 days from the blood draw date before entering Greece or any other EU state. This is a fixed regulatory requirement, not subject to waiver. If the titre falls below 0.5 IU/ml, the process must restart.

        The AQCS-endorsed health certificate for Greece must include: the microchip number and implant date, rabies vaccination details, FAVN test result, blood draw date, and the EU-approved laboratory name.
