  sections:
    - heading: "Finnish Requirements for Pets Arriving from Lithuania"
      body: |
        Finland accepts pets from EU member states under the standard EU pet travel rules (EU Regulation 576/2013). Your pet must have an ISO 11784/11785 microchip (15-digit) implanted before the first rabies vaccination, a current rabies vaccination recorded in an EU pet passport, and a 21-day wait after any primary vaccination.

        No titre test is required for pets arriving from Lithuania. Finland does not impose quarantine on compliant arrivals. There is no tapeworm treatment requirement for dogs coming from EU countries, unlike the Norway route. The Ruokavirasto (Finnish Food Authority) oversees these rules and publishes current requirements at ruokavirasto.fi. Check there for the latest guidance before travel.

    - heading: "Vilnius to Helsinki: Airlines and Routing"
      body: |
        Finnair is the primary carrier connecting Helsinki Vantaa (HEL) and Vilnius (VNO), with services running direct on some schedules and via Riga on others. Finnair operates a live animal cargo programme and accepts cabin pets on eligible flights. The Vilnius to Helsinki journey is around 90 minutes by air, which is comfortable for most cats and dogs.

        airBaltic can route Vilnius pets toward Helsinki via Riga, covering both segments with cabin and cargo options. Ryanair offers budget fares on some VNO to HEL routes but accepts cabin pets only, with no cargo service. When booking, register the live animal at the same time as the ticket and confirm that the animal booking covers every flight segment if your route involves a connection. Contact the airline's live animal desk at least 4 to 6 weeks before travel.

    answer: "No. Finland does not require tapeworm treatment for dogs arriving from EU member states. Tapeworm treatment on entry is a Norwegian requirement. For Finland, your dog needs a microchip, current rabies vaccination, and EU pet passport."
  - question: "Does my pet need a titre test to move from Lithuania to Finland?"
    answer: "No. Lithuania is an EU member state and Finland does not require a titre test for pets arriving from other EU countries. A current rabies vaccination (at least 21 days old) and an EU pet passport are sufficient."
  - question: "Is there a direct flight from Vilnius to Helsinki?"
    answer: "Finnair operates some direct connections between Vilnius (VNO) and Helsinki (HEL), though some schedules route via Riga. Check current timetables when booking. Confirm that your live animal booking covers all segments of your itinerary, including any connection."
  - question: "Who issues the EU pet passport in Lithuania?"
    answer: "Only official veterinarians authorised by the Valstybine maisto ir veterinarijos tarnyba (VMVT, State Food and Veterinary Service) can issue EU pet passports in Lithuania. Not all private vets hold this status. Check vmvt.lt or ask your vet to confirm their authorisation before booking your appointment."
