  sections:
    - heading: "DVS export process from Malaysia"
      body: |
        Malaysia's Department of Veterinary Services manages pet exports through district offices and the Animal Passport portal. Your vet, registered with DVS, prepares the health certificate documenting the microchip number, rabies vaccination, and current health status.

        DVS carries out a physical inspection of the animal before issuing the export health certificate. Allow at least a week for the process, and apply early in the 90-day waiting period so the certificate is ready when you need it. The DVS certificate and the EU AHC are separate documents; both are required for entry into Greece.

The choice of hub affects total transit time, which matters for pet welfare in the crate.

faqs:
  - question: "Is Greece quarantine-free for pets from Malaysia?"
    answer: "Yes, provided the full EU unlisted country protocol is followed. That means ISO microchip, valid rabies vaccination, FAVN titre test result of 0.5 IU/ml or above, completed 90-day wait from the blood draw date, and EU AHC issued within 10 days before arrival. If all that is in order, Greece applies no quarantine on arrival."
  - question: "Can I use the FAVN titre test done for Malaysia's own import process to satisfy the EU requirement?"
    answer: "It depends on timing. If you previously did a FAVN titre test for another purpose and have a valid result of 0.5 IU/ml or above, the 90-day EU waiting period would start from that blood draw date, provided the result is still current and the vaccination has not lapsed. Talk to your vet about whether an existing titre result can be used. A fresh titre test is usually cleaner and avoids disputes at the EU border."
  - question: "Which laboratory in Malaysia is EU-approved for the FAVN titre test?"
    answer: "The EU Commission publishes a list of designated laboratories for rabies antibody titration. Check the current list on the European Commission Food Safety website before submitting a sample, as the approved laboratory list is periodically updated. Your pet transport agent or vet can confirm which laboratories are currently approved for samples from Malaysia."
  - question: "How are pets transported from KUL to ATH?"
    answer: "As manifest cargo in an IATA-compliant crate. Pets do not travel in the passenger cabin on this route. Your pet will be in the temperature-controlled hold. You can travel on the same aircraft or ship separately as unaccompanied cargo. Either way, the crate must meet IATA Container Requirement 82 or equivalent for live animals."
