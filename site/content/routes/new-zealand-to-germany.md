  sections:
    - heading: "EU-listed status: what it means for New Zealand pets entering Germany"
      body: |
        New Zealand appears on the EU's list of third countries from which pet movements are permitted under the same rules as EU member states (EU Regulation 576/2013, Annex II). The practical effect is significant: the FAVN titre test and 3-month wait from blood draw are not required. Source: food.ec.europa.eu, 2026.

        What is required is a valid ISO microchip, a current rabies vaccination, and an MPI-endorsed EU-format health certificate issued within 10 days of travel. If the vaccination is up to date, 4-6 weeks is sufficient preparation time. If a primary vaccination is needed, allow 10-12 weeks for the 21-day post-vaccination wait.

    - heading: "Auckland to Frankfurt: routing options via hub"
      body: |
        There is no direct cargo service from Auckland (AKL) to Frankfurt (FRA). All routings connect via a hub. The two principal options are Singapore Airlines via Changi (SIN) and Emirates via Dubai (DXB). Both have temperature-controlled live animal cargo handling at their transit hubs.

        Singapore Airlines via SIN is the most commonly used routing for New Zealand to Europe moves. The SIN-FRA sector is a major long-haul route with daily operations. Emirates via Dubai is a solid alternative. Confirm live animal acceptance on your specific flight when booking, 4-6 weeks ahead.

    - heading: "Germany Bundesland breed rules for New Zealand pet owners"
      body: |
        Germany has a federal breed ban covering Pit Bull Terrier and Tosa Inu. Beyond this, each Bundesland sets its own rules. Bavaria is the most restrictive, adding Rottweiler, Dogo Argentino, Fila Brasileiro, American Bulldog, and Cane Corso to its state list.

        For most breeds commonly kept in New Zealand, including Labrador Retrievers, Golden Retrievers, Border Collies, and German Shepherds, no restriction applies. If you have a breed that may fall under Bundesland rules, check your destination state specifically before starting the process.
