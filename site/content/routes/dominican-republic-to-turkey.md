content:
  h1: "Pet Transport from the Dominican Republic to Turkey"
  overview: |
    The Dominican Republic has one of the more forgiving entry processes into the EU in this build: no titre test, no import permit for up to five personal pets. None of that leniency travels with the pet once the destination is Turkey instead. Istanbul applies its own stricter standard, and Santo Domingo doesn't get an exception.

    That means the FAVN titre test, the 90-day wait, and a connecting flight through Madrid, since Las Americas International has no direct Istanbul service. It's a longer process than the DR's usual European routes, and worth planning for from the outset rather than discovering partway through.

  sections:
    - heading: "Why this route is harder than the DR's EU routes"
      body: |
        Pets moving from the Dominican Republic into the EU skip the titre test entirely, since the DR isn't treated as a high-risk origin under EU rules. Turkey has no equivalent exemption for the DR. A FAVN or RNATT test is required, blood drawn at least 30 days after vaccination, a result of at least 0.5 IU/ml, and then a 90-day wait counted from the draw date itself, not from when the lab confirms the result.

        Miss the test, and Turkish authorities apply 21 days of quarantine at the owner's expense rather than refusing entry outright, but that's a costly and stressful fallback compared with simply completing the process before departure.

    - heading: "Getting from Santo Domingo to Istanbul"
      body: |
        Las Americas International Airport (SDQ) has no direct cargo route to Istanbul. Air Europa's Santo Domingo-Madrid service is the standard first leg for pet cargo out of the DR, and Turkish Airlines runs its own Madrid-Istanbul connection onward, which keeps the paperwork and handling consistent for the second half of the trip even though two separate carriers are involved.

        DIGEGA, the Dominican agriculture directorate, doesn't require a separate export permit for a personal pet. Its role is endorsing the health certificate, reformatted to Turkey's own model rather than the EU-style document used on the DR's other routes.
