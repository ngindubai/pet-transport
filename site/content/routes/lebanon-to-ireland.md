  h1: "Pet Transport from Lebanon to Ireland"
  overview: "Ireland asks for one thing most of its EU neighbours don't: a TRACES pre-notification before your pet even boards the flight. Add that to the standard unlisted-country sequence Lebanon faces (microchip, rabies vaccination, FAVN titre test, 90-day wait from blood draw) and you're looking at 20 to 26 weeks of planning. Ireland is also stricter than most about non-compliance, with no informal waivers for incomplete documentation. Cats face the identical process to dogs."

  sections:
    - heading: "TRACES: the extra step Ireland asks for"
      body: |
        Most EU states accept a valid EU-format health certificate and leave it there. Ireland adds a pre-arrival notification through TRACES, the EU's Trade Control and Expert System, before the pet lands. This isn't a replacement for the health certificate. It runs alongside it, and it needs to be submitted ahead of travel, typically arranged by your vet or pet transport agent on your behalf.

        Skipping this step, or getting the timing wrong, can mean delays at Dublin Airport or a refused entry, which is a far more expensive outcome than the notification itself. If you're used to routes into France, Italy or Poland, don't assume Ireland works the same way. It doesn't.

        The Department of Agriculture, Food and the Marine (DAFM) is also notably firm about non-compliant documentation generally. Where some states might allow a short conditional quarantine to sort out a paperwork gap, Ireland's default is closer to penalty quarantine or return of the animal, so there's little margin for error here.

    - heading: "The titre test and 90-day wait"
      body: |
        Lebanon sits outside the EU's approved country list, so the same unlisted-country sequence applies as everywhere else in this guide: microchip first, rabies vaccination second, then a minimum 30-day wait before blood is drawn for an FAVN titre test at an EU-approved lab. A result of at least 0.5 IU/ml starts a 90-day countdown, measured from the draw date. Only once that period ends can the pet legally enter Ireland.

    - heading: "Flight options from Beirut to Dublin"
      body: |
        Emirates SkyCargo via Dubai has a long track record on this connection, though it's the longest of the four routings by transit time. Turkish Airlines Cargo via Istanbul is often quicker and just as reliable. Lufthansa Cargo via Frankfurt gives access to the Frankfurt Animal Lounge, useful for pets that need a calmer stopover, and Qatar Airways Cargo via Doha rounds out the options, subject to seasonal heat restrictions on live cargo out of the Gulf.

  - question: "Do cats need TRACES pre-notification too?"
