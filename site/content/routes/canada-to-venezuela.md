content:
  h1: "Pet Transport from Canada to Venezuela"
  overview: |
    Air Canada already runs a nonstop service to Bogota, one of 25 weekly nonstop Air Canada flights linking Canada and South America. That existing connection matters more than usual right now, because Simon Bolivar International Airport at Caracas has been shut since the earthquakes of 24 June 2026, and Bogota is where a Canadian-origin pet currently picks up its onward leg into Venezuela.

    The paperwork is the easier half. INSAI does not ask a Canadian-vaccinated pet for a rabies titre test, just a current vaccination certificate, a health certificate and the standard vaccination and deworming records, the same light checklist applied to arrivals from the UK, the US and most other countries. Budget six to nine weeks, most of it spent confirming flight capacity rather than waiting out fixed regulatory periods.

  sections:
    - heading: "A lighter import process than Canada's own rules would suggest"
      body: |
        INSAI's checklist reads no differently for a Canadian pet than for one arriving from almost anywhere else: a rabies certificate no more than a year old, an international health certificate valid one month, the combined vaccination card, and a deworming certificate valid three months. No titre test. No fixed waiting period.

        A sanitary mobilisation permit, priced at 5 Unidades Tributarias, is normally settled at the INSAI office inside Maiquetia airport on arrival. That part is genuinely unresolved while the airport stays shut. INSAI's published guidance has not, as far as current sources show, been updated to name where this happens now that flights land at Valencia instead.

    - heading: "Why Bogota is good news for this particular route"
      body: |
        Every Venezuela-bound route on this site currently runs through a connecting hub, since Maiquetia is closed. Canada has a genuine advantage here: Air Canada already flies nonstop to Bogota, as part of a wider South American network that also reaches Santiago, Rio de Janeiro and Cartagena. The first leg of this journey is not a workaround bolted on for the emergency; it is a flight that already existed.

        From Bogota, Avianca's contingency service to Valencia's Arturo Michelena airport picks up the connection. Copa Airlines offers a second option via Panama City if Avianca's capacity is tight on your dates. Neither Valencia service is a settled, permanent fixture yet, so reconfirm cargo space close to the actual travel date rather than booking it and forgetting about it.

    - heading: "What CFIA still expects on the way out"
      body: |
        No CFIA export permit applies to a personal pet leaving Canada, for this route or any other. The main task is a health certificate from a CFIA-accredited vet, formatted against INSAI's checklist. Whether INSAI currently expects a further government endorsement on top of that vet's signature is not spelled out clearly in current published guidance, so raise it directly with INSAI or a Venezuela-based receiving agent before the vet appointment, rather than assuming either way and finding out at the cargo desk.
