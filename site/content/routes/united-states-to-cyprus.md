    - heading: "London, Doha or Istanbul: three transit options compared"
      body: |
        Moving a pet from the USA to Cyprus requires choosing a transatlantic transit hub. Each of the three main options has different characteristics.

        London Heathrow on British Airways is a familiar route for US travellers. BA accepts pets, and Cyprus connections from London are reliable. The downside is that London adds a European transit step before reaching Cyprus, and the full journey can be 20-24 hours.

        Doha on Qatar Airways is an increasingly popular choice. Qatar Airways Cargo has excellent live animal facilities at Doha and serves Larnaca directly. The journey from a US East Coast city to Doha and on to Larnaca is competitive in time.

        Istanbul on Turkish Airlines is the third option, with broad US city coverage and good Larnaca connections. Turkish Cargo handles large live animal volumes at Istanbul.

    - heading: "Cyprus's treatment requirements and the USDA certificate"
      body: |
        The EU-format health certificate used for Cyprus must record both the tapeworm treatment and the tick treatment for dogs. These treatments need to be added to the certificate by a vet close to travel, within the treatment windows (tapeworm 24-120h, tick 24-48h before Cyprus arrival).

        The USDA-endorsed certificate from the endorsement process covers the microchip and vaccination. The treatment records are typically added in a final vet visit close to departure, once the treatment windows are calculable from the confirmed travel date.
