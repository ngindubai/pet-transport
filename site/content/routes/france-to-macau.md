content:
  h1: "Pet Transport from France to Macau"
  overview: |
    IAM, Macau's Municipal Affairs Bureau, stopped requiring mandatory 30-day quarantine for compliant dogs and cats on 12 June 2026. That's the headline change. It doesn't touch the paperwork chain a French pet still has to clear, and it doesn't invent a direct flight where none exists.

    France's mainland rabies-free status, confirmed by the World Organisation for Animal Health since 2001, counts for a great deal inside the EU. It counts for nothing on Macau's short exemption list. A titre test is still required, and every pet crosses through Hong Kong before it ever reaches Macanese soil.

  sections:
    - heading: "What the 12 June 2026 rule change actually removed"
      body: |
        Before June, a dog or cat landing in Macau sat in quarantine for 30 days regardless of how well documented it arrived. IAM's policy update removed that default. Now a pet that clears four conditions, microchip, current rabies vaccination, a passing titre result and an official health certificate, can walk (or be carried) straight through an on-arrival inspection instead.

        Walk through, not skip. An IAM veterinary surgeon still checks the paperwork against the animal at the airport, and a pet missing any one of those four items can still be held. Treat the quarantine exemption as conditional on getting the rest right, not as a general relaxation of the rules.

    - heading: "Why France's disease status doesn't shorten the timeline"
      body: |
        It's a fair question: if France has had no confirmed indigenous rabies case in nearly a quarter of a century, why does a French pet need the same titre test as one from a country with active wildlife rabies? Because Macau's exemption list is short and specific, built around a handful of named territories, and France simply isn't on it, the same position Canada and the UK are in on this site's other Macau routes.

        Practically, that means blood drawn at least 30 days after the rabies vaccination, a result of at least 0.5 IU/mL, then roughly three months counted from the draw date, not the jab date, before IAM will let the pet through without quarantine.

    - heading: "The routing reality: Hong Kong first, always"
      body: |
        Macau International Airport doesn't handle long-haul cargo from Europe. Air France Cargo flies CDG to Hong Kong direct, which is the strongest first leg for most French shippers, with Cathay Pacific Cargo running the same city pair as a solid alternative.

        From Hong Kong, the crate crosses by road over the Hong Kong-Zhuhai-Macau Bridge, or by ferry, in around an hour, before IAM's inspection. Build that transfer into your plan as its own leg. It needs its own booking, its own paperwork check, and ideally a handler on the Hong Kong side who already knows the crossing.
