content:
  h1: "Pet Transport from Azerbaijan to Singapore"
  overview: |
    Azerbaijan Airlines, the national carrier, flies to Moscow, London, Frankfurt and Istanbul from Baku. Singapore is not on that list, and it never has been. Every pet leaving Heydar Aliyev International for Changi does so on a different airline entirely, usually Turkish Airlines through Istanbul or Emirates through Dubai.

    On the compliance side, Azerbaijan isn't named on either of AVS's shorter schedules, so it defaults to Schedule III: a WOAH titre test, a 90-day wait from the blood draw, and 30 days at Singapore's Animal Quarantine Centre. Confirmed against avs.nparks.gov.sg, fetched 21 July 2026. Realistically, budget four to five months.

  sections:
    - heading: "Why AZAL isn't part of this route"
      body: |
        Azerbaijan Airlines runs a compact international network out of Baku, focused on Europe, Russia and the Middle East plus a handful of Asian cities. Singapore has never featured on that map. For a Baku-to-Singapore pet move, that rules out the direct-carrier convenience some Caucasus routes on this site enjoy.

        Turkish Airlines via Istanbul is the more practical choice, feeding into a nonstop Istanbul-Singapore sector that Turkish Airlines and Singapore Airlines both fly, roughly 17 times a week combined as of mid-2026. Emirates via Dubai is the other reliable option, particularly outside the Gulf's peak summer heat.

    - heading: "Schedule III, and the lab that has to be somewhere else"
      body: |
        Blood for the titre test can't be drawn until 28 days after vaccination, and the result only counts for export between 90 days and 12 months after that draw. Azerbaijan has no WOAH-recognised laboratory of its own, so the sample needs to travel internationally to a WOAH reference lab or a facility in a named Schedule II country.

        Run the AVS import licence application and the AQC quarantine booking in parallel with the 90-day wait rather than afterward. Both steps proceed independently of the titre clock, and doing them concurrently keeps the whole move closer to four months than five.
