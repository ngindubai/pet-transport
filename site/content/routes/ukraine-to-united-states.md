content:
  h1: "Pet Transport from Ukraine to the United States"
  overview: |
    The CDC classifies Ukraine as high-risk for dog rabies, and that single classification shapes almost everything about this move. A dog without a completed titre test faces 28 days at a CDC-registered facility on arrival. A dog with one can land at any US port of entry. There is no middle option.

    Then there's the flight itself, or rather the lack of one. No airport in Ukraine has handled a scheduled commercial flight since February 2022, so the journey to the States starts on the road, not in the air. Most owners cross into Poland near Lviv and pick up a flight from Warsaw; some cross south into Romania instead.

    Cats have it far simpler. The US federal system asks almost nothing of them, no titre, no CDC form, no facility stay. This guide focuses mainly on dogs, where the real complexity sits.

  sections:
    - heading: "The titre test versus the CDC facility, and why the decision matters early"
      body: |
        Dogs from a high-risk country like Ukraine have two paths into the United States. The first: a rabies titre test, blood drawn at a CDC-approved laboratory at least 30 days after vaccination and at least 28 days before arrival, with a result confirming adequate antibodies. Get that, and your dog clears the border like any other.

        The second path is the facility stay. No titre means 28 days at a CDC-registered animal care facility, paid for entirely by the owner, with no exceptions for a long journey already behind the dog. Costs run into the thousands of dollars, and the facility must be booked before departure, not arranged on landing.

        Given the choice, almost every owner picks the titre route. It takes longer to prepare but avoids weeks of confinement for an animal that has likely already had an unsettling few years.

    - heading: "Getting to a working airport in the first place"
      body: |
        Ukraine's airspace closed on 24 February 2022 and has not reopened. A working group formed in March 2026 is preparing Boryspil, Lviv and Zhuliany for an eventual phased return to service, and AirBaltic has publicly said it would resume flights once the security and insurance picture clears. None of that has translated into an actual flight yet.

        So the plan for now is overland first. Owners in western Ukraine typically cross near Lviv, at Shehyni-Medyka or Krakovets-Korczowa, then continue to Rzeszow, Krakow or Warsaw for the international leg. Southern and central Ukraine routes tend to run through Romania instead. Either way, build the crossing time into your schedule. Six to eight hours is typical, though queues lengthen at busy periods, and it needs to happen before the Foreign Rabies Vaccination certificate's 30-day clock runs out.

    - heading: "No US humanitarian shortcut for pets, unlike the UK"
      body: |
        It's worth saying plainly: the United States has nothing like the UK's Ukraine-specific import licence, which allows up to five pets per applicant and sometimes swaps kennel quarantine for home isolation. Humanitarian parole programmes covering people, including Uniting for Ukraine, sit entirely apart from the CDC's animal import rules.

        That means a family relocating under any US immigration route still faces the same titre test or facility stay as any other dog owner from a high-risk country. Plan the pet side of the move as its own project, separate from visa paperwork, because one does not speed up the other.

    answer: "You need either a titre test or a 28-day stay at a CDC-registered facility.   - question: "Do cats face the same rules as dogs on this route?"
    answer: "No. Cats entering the United States face essentially no federal requirements, regardless of where they travelled from. The CDC's high-risk-country rules, including the titre test and the facility stay, apply to dogs only."
  - question: "Is there a special US pathway for pets belonging to Ukrainians arriving under humanitarian parole?"
    answer: "No. Programmes such as Uniting for Ukraine govern a person's immigration status and have no effect on the CDC's animal import requirements. A dog travelling under any of these routes still needs the standard titre test or facility stay that applies to any dog from a high-risk country."
