  h1: "Pet Transport from Ukraine to the United States"
  overview: |
    JFK, Los Angeles, Miami, Atlanta, Washington Dulles or San Francisco. Those six airports are the only places in the US where a dog from Ukraine is allowed to land, because they are the only ones with a CDC-registered animal care facility on site. Book a flight into anywhere else and the airline, or CBP on arrival, will not accept the dog.

    That restriction exists because Ukraine sits on the CDC's high-risk countries list for dog rabies, last updated 15 April 2026. Add the fact that no flight leaves any Ukrainian airport at all, since the country's civilian airspace has been closed since 24 February 2022, and this becomes one of the more involved routes on the whole site. Every pet first travels overland into Poland or Romania, then flies from there into a CDC-eligible US gateway. Cats have it far easier: USDA APHIS sets no federal rabies import requirement for cats, so a cat on this same route skips almost all of what follows.

  sections:
    - heading: "Why the closed airspace comes before the CDC paperwork"
      body: |
        Nothing about the CDC process can start with a flight booking out of Ukraine, because there isn't one. Civilian airspace closed the day the invasion began and has not reopened. A working group inside the Ukrainian government is planning a phased return to service, but no airline currently has a confirmed restart date.

        Owners cross overland instead, most commonly through Shehyni-Medyka or Krakovets-Korczowa near Lviv into Poland, or via Romania's border crossings for those further south. Rzeszow, Krakow and Warsaw all serve as staging points on the Polish side once across. It's the same overland pattern already established on the Ukraine-to-UK route, just with a different flight destination once your pet reaches a working airport.

    - heading: "The CDC's foreign-vaccinated, high-risk-country pathway"
      body: |
        Ukraine's place on the CDC's high-risk list means a dog vaccinated there cannot simply travel on a standard export certificate the way a US-origin dog can. The microchip has to go in before the rabies vaccine, full stop; get the order wrong and the vaccination doesn't count. The vaccine itself must be given at least 28 days before US entry.

        A titre test isn't legally compulsory, but skipping it means an automatic 28 days of quarantine at the arrival airport's CDC-registered facility, at the owner's full cost. Drawing blood at least 30 days after vaccination and at least 28 days before travel, then sending it to a CDC-approved laboratory, is worth the extra step for almost anyone planning ahead. A passing result lasts the dog's whole life, as long as vaccination stays current.

        Two forms carry the weight here. The CDC Dog Import Form is free, completed online, and tied to one dog and one arrival date. The Certification of Foreign Rabies Vaccination and Microchip form is separate, has to be endorsed by an official government vet, and is only valid for 30 days from signature, which makes timing it against the flight date a genuine planning constraint rather than a formality.

    - heading: "Six airports, and why the rest are off limits"
      body: |
        The CDC restricts foreign-vaccinated dogs from high-risk countries to airports with a CDC-registered animal care facility on site: Atlanta, Washington Dulles, New York JFK, Los Angeles, Miami and San Francisco. LOT Polish Airlines' daily nonstop from Warsaw to JFK lines up neatly with that list, which is a large part of why it's the most workable single connection for this route. A Warsaw-to-Chicago or Warsaw-to-Newark booking, by contrast, simply will not work for a dog carrying Ukrainian vaccination records, whatever the airline's own pet policy says.

        This distinction doesn't apply to cats. USDA APHIS has no federal animal-health import requirement for pet cats at all, so a cat owner on this same route can, in practice, fly into any US airport that accepts pets in the cabin or hold.

    - heading: "No pet provision inside the US humanitarian route"
      body: |
        The UK built a specific licence pathway, including home isolation instead of kennel quarantine, into its Homes for Ukraine scheme for pets that couldn't complete the standard rabies protocol before their owners had to leave. The US has nothing equivalent. Uniting for Ukraine, the American humanitarian parole route, covers people and makes no separate accommodation for animals. A Ukrainian family arriving in the US under that programme still has to clear the same CDC high-risk-country process described above, on the same timeline, with the same six-airport restriction, as any other Ukraine-origin dog owner.
