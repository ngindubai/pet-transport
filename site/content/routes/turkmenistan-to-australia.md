  h1: "Pet Transport from Turkmenistan to Australia"
  overview: |
    Skip the idea of a direct move here. Turkmenistan doesn't sit on any of DAFF's approved country lists, so a pet cannot travel straight from Ashgabat into Australia's standard import pathway at all.

    What DAFF requires instead is a stopover with teeth: the pet has to live in an approved country, Singapore and Hong Kong are the two most commonly used, for a full 180 consecutive days, completing its vaccination and rabies titre test there rather than in Turkmenistan. Only once that residency period closes does the familiar Australian process, import permit, then Mickleham quarantine in Melbourne, begin.

    Layer onto that Ashgabat's thin flight schedule and Turkmenistan's own tightly controlled export administration, and this sits among the more demanding routes covered on this site. Not because any single step is unusual, but because three separate systems, Turkmenistan's, the staging country's and Australia's, all have to line up.

  sections:
    - heading: "Why Turkmenistan can't go straight to Mickleham"
      body: |
        DAFF sorts origin countries into risk-based groups, and Turkmenistan appears in none of them. It isn't in Group 1's short, low-risk list, nor Group 2's mid-tier list of island territories and a handful of others. Group 3 is described as countries where rabies is present but well controlled, a description that doesn't sit comfortably with Turkmenistan's largely uncontrolled dog rabies burden.

        The practical result: Turkmenistan is treated as a non-approved country. DAFF's own published guidance for this situation is specific. A pet must be continuously resident in an approved Group 1, 2 or 3 country for at least 180 days before export, undergo its vaccination and titre test in that country, and complete the post-titre wait there too. The residency requirement and the medical wait aren't two separate 180-day blocks stacked on top of each other; they run over the same period, provided the pet stays put in the approved country throughout.

    - heading: "Choosing the staging country"
      body: |
        Singapore and Hong Kong both work as DAFF-approved staging points, and both have established pet import processes of their own that a Turkmenistan-origin pet would need to clear first. This is not a small administrative step. It means genuinely relocating, or having someone relocate the pet, to a different country for six months before the Australian process can even start.

        Given Turkmenistan Airlines and flydubai's limited networks, routing to either staging country realistically goes via Dubai or Istanbul, adding a connection and its own paperwork before the pet has even left the region.

    - heading: "The Alabai restriction, still very much in force"
      body: |
        A 2022 Turkmenistan law requires Alabai, or Central Asian Shepherd Dog, puppies to be registered in a government pedigree book and carry a passport before leaving the country, with special government permission required for export. Some reporting at the time called it an outright ban; the underlying law is closer to a registration and permission requirement, but in practice permission for a full pedigree Alabai to leave has not been documented as granted. If your dog has significant Alabai ancestry, raise this with the State Veterinary Service before committing to any other part of this plan.

    - heading: "Mickleham, once the pet finally reaches Australia"
      body: |
        The end of this route looks like any other DAFF Group 2 or 3 arrival: an import permit through BICON, then a minimum 10-day stay at the Mickleham Post Entry Quarantine Facility in Melbourne, the only facility DAFF uses. All pets land in Melbourne regardless of their final Australian destination, and quarantine slots need booking well before the flight itself is confirmed.
