content:
  h1: "Pet Transport from Turkey to Latvia"
  overview: |
    Riga sees plenty of EU pet passport traffic in this build, pets moving freely between member states with no titre test at all. A pet leaving Turkey doesn't get that treatment. The EU's own classification puts Turkey outside its low-risk group, so the full FAVN protocol applies on this route, the same as it would from any unlisted country.

    That's the single biggest thing to plan around here: the flight is a couple of hours, the paperwork timeline is measured in months.

  sections:
    - heading: "Why this route doesn't behave like an EU move"
      body: |
        Most of Latvia's routes elsewhere in this build run on the EU pet passport, a document that eliminates the titre test entirely for pets already inside the bloc. Turkey isn't in the bloc, and the EU treats it as an unlisted, higher-risk rabies country regardless of how short the flight to Riga actually is. The result: blood drawn at least 30 days after vaccination, a result of at least 0.5 IU/ml, and a 90-day wait counted from that draw date before the pet can board.

        TRGM, Turkey's agriculture ministry, doesn't require a separate export permit, but the certificate has to follow the EU's specific format for unlisted third countries rather than Turkey's own domestic template.

    - heading: "Once the wait clears, the flight is simple"
      body: |
        Turkish Airlines flies Istanbul to Riga direct, and airBaltic's own network from its Riga base offers a workable connecting alternative if cargo space on the direct flight is tight. There's no import permit requirement on Latvia's side, and no quarantine for pets arriving with a compliant, correctly timed titre test result.
