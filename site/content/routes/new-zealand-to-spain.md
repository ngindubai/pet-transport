  sections:
    - heading: "EU-listed status: no titre test for New Zealand pets entering Spain"
      body: |
        New Zealand is on the EU's listed third countries schedule (EU Regulation 576/2013, Annex II). Pets from listed countries enter Spain on the same documentation basis as EU member state pets. The FAVN titre test and 3-month wait from blood draw do not apply. Source: food.ec.europa.eu, 2026.

        A valid ISO microchip, current rabies vaccination, and MPI-endorsed EU-format health certificate issued within 10 days of travel are the requirements. With a vaccination already in place, 4-6 weeks is sufficient preparation time.

    - heading: "Auckland to Madrid: routing options"
      body: |
        There is no direct cargo service from Auckland to Madrid Barajas (MAD). All routings involve at least one transfer. Emirates via Dubai (DXB) connects to MAD with a single transfer and live animal cargo handling throughout. Singapore Airlines via Changi often requires a second connection to reach Madrid.

        For Spain specifically, the Dubai routing via Emirates tends to give a more direct connection to MAD. A pet transport agent can confirm current routing options and book cargo space 4-6 weeks ahead.

    - heading: "Spain PPP: ongoing obligations for certain breeds"
      body: |
        Spain's PPP (Perro Potencialmente Peligroso) law applies to Pit Bull Terrier, American Staffordshire Terrier, Rottweiler, Dogo Argentino, Fila Brasileiro, Tosa Inu, Akita Inu, and similar breeds. The import itself is not restricted; PPP creates ongoing obligations once the dog is in Spain: muzzle in public, maximum 2m leash, civil liability insurance, and a PPP licence from the local municipality.

        For most breeds common in New Zealand, including Labrador Retrievers, Golden Retrievers, and Border Collies, no PPP obligation applies.
