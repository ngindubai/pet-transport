    - heading: "The tapeworm rule catches more owners than the titre test does"
      body: |
        Mattilsynet, the Norwegian Food Safety Authority, requires every dog entering the country to have received Praziquantel treatment between 1 and 5 days before arrival, administered by a vet and recorded directly in the health certificate. Too early and it does not count for entry; too late and there is no time to arrange it before the flight.

        This rule is genuinely separate from the microchip, vaccination and titre test sequence that Uganda's unlisted status already requires, which is exactly why owners planning around the 90-day wait sometimes forget it entirely until the final week. Cats are exempt from this specific requirement, though everything else in the standard sequence still applies to them.

    - heading: "The standard unlisted-country sequence, plus Norway's own layer"
      body: |
        Norway is not an EU member state, but it applies equivalent rules through the EEA Agreement, aligned with the EU's current 2026 pet travel framework. For Uganda, that means microchip first, rabies vaccination second, a 30-day gap, then blood drawn for a titre test at an approved laboratory. A result of at least 0.5 IU/ml starts a 90-day countdown from the draw date, and only once that finishes can the pet legally enter Norway, tapeworm treatment included.
