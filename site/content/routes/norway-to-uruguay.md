  overview: |
    Relocating a pet from Norway to Uruguay involves more steps than a typical European pet move but remains manageable with the right timeline. Uruguay requires an import permit from its agricultural authority MGAP, and dogs must receive a Praziquantel treatment against Taenia Echinococcus within a defined window before arrival. There is no quarantine for compliant arrivals and no titre test is required for Norway-origin pets.

    Allow 6 to 8 weeks from starting preparations to travel day. The cargo routing is long, going via Madrid with Iberia or via South American hubs with LATAM. This is a route where using a specialist pet transport agent to coordinate the MGAP permit and cargo booking is worth considering.

  sections:
    - heading: "MGAP permit and import requirements"
      body: |
        Uruguay's animal import rules are administered by MGAP (Ministerio de Ganaderia, Agricultura y Pesca). Before your pet can travel, you need an import permit from MGAP. Apply early: processing takes time and you cannot confirm a departure date without the permit reference.

        The requirements for personal companion pets from Norway are: an ISO-compliant microchip, a rabies vaccination administered between 30 days and 12 months before entry, current vaccination records, and an official health certificate. Dogs require Praziquantel treatment against Taenia Echinococcus within 72 hours to 30 days before arrival. This treatment prevents the tapeworm from entering Uruguay's ecosystem and is strictly enforced.

        No quarantine applies for compliant arrivals. MGAP veterinary inspectors at Carrasco International Airport (MVD) in Montevideo check documentation and release your pet once all papers are in order.

    - heading: "The Praziquantel requirement for dogs"
      body: |
        Uruguay requires that all incoming dogs be treated against Taenia Echinococcus (hydatid tapeworm) with a Praziquantel-containing antiparasitic. The treatment must be administered between 72 hours and 30 days before the pet's arrival in Uruguay. Work backwards from your arrival date to schedule this treatment with a Norwegian veterinarian.

        The dose is calculated per kilogram of body weight. The treating vet records the product name, dose, date, and microchip number on the health certificate. MGAP veterinarians will check this record at Carrasco Airport. If the treatment was administered outside the specified window, the documentation will not be accepted.

        This requirement applies to dogs. Cats are not subject to the Praziquantel requirement.

    - heading: "Cargo routing from Oslo to Montevideo"
      body: |
        Norway to Uruguay is a long-haul cargo route with no direct connections. The two main European routing options are via Madrid (MAD) or via Lima (LIM).

        Via Madrid, connect from Oslo Gardermoen (OSL) to MAD on SAS or Norwegian, then continue with Iberia cargo to Carrasco International Airport (MVD) in Montevideo. Iberia operates services to Uruguay and has established cargo procedures for live animals.

        Via Lima, LATAM Airlines connects European origins to Montevideo through Peru. The routing from Oslo requires a European hub connection before the transatlantic leg. LATAM Cargo handles live animal bookings and the Lima-to-MVD leg is a regular service.

        Journey times via South America are long. Plan for 16-24 hours including connection time. Ensure your crate meets IATA standards for the expected journey duration.
