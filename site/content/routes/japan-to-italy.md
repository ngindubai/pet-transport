  h1: "Pet Transport from Japan to Italy"
  overview: |
    For pets moving from Japan to Italy, the EU's approved third countries list works in your favour. Japan holds listed-country status, which means no titre test, no 3-month waiting period, and no quarantine -- just a microchip, a current rabies vaccination, and the correct EU health certificate.

    The health certificate is the detail that catches people out. Pets from Japan entering the EU need the EU Annex IV listed-country model certificate -- a specific template completed by a MAFF-accredited vet in Japan and endorsed by the MAFF regional office. It's not a standard Japanese health certificate, and it must be issued within 10 days of your pet entering Italy.

    No airline flies direct from Japan to Italy for pets. Lufthansa via Frankfurt (FRA) or Munich (MUC) is the most reliable routing for cargo, with good connections to Rome Fiumicino (FCO) or Milan Malpensa (MXP). ANA Cargo also serves MXP. Enter at FCO if possible -- it has the strongest BIP veterinary inspection capacity in Italy. Allow 4-8 weeks from starting preparation.
