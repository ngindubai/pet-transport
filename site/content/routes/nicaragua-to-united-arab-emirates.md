    Emirates now flies non-stop between Miami and Dubai four times a week on a Boeing 777-300ER, the fastest link currently running between the Americas and the Gulf. For a dog or cat leaving Managua, that still means two cargo handovers rather than one: American Airlines to Miami first, then the new Emirates sector on to Dubai.

The good news is that once a valid titre result is in hand, the UAE does not add the kind of extended post-test waiting period some other destinations apply.

  sections:
    - heading: "Why the titre test applies to a Nicaragua-origin pet"
      body: |
        MOCCAE splits the world into two groups. Roughly 50 named low-risk countries, the UK, Germany, Australia, Japan, Singapore, New Zealand and Sweden among the examples given in MOCCAE's own guidance, skip the blood test entirely. Everywhere else, Nicaragua included, needs the FAVN rabies antibody titration test: a result of at least 0.5 IU/ml from an accredited laboratory, with the sample drawn no more than 12 months before travel.

        There's a genuine upside here worth knowing about. Unlike the UK's rules, which force a 3-month wait counted from the blood draw date regardless of the result, MOCCAE doesn't impose an extra waiting period once the titre comes back passing. That single difference can shave two months off a route that otherwise looks similar on paper.

        Get the sequencing right, though. If the vaccination is a first dose or a lapsed booster, 21 days have to pass before the sample is drawn. Skip that gap and the lab result won't count.

    - heading: "Getting a dog or cat from Managua to Dubai"
      body: |
        American Airlines' daily non-stop from Managua to Miami remains the most reliable first leg, and its PetEmbark cargo programme is open to any member of the public, not restricted to military bookings as some older guides claim. From Miami, Emirates' new non-stop to Dubai, flight EK213/EK214 on a Boeing 777-300ER, four times weekly, is the standout option: it's currently the only non-stop passenger service linking the two cities, backed by Emirates SkyCargo's existing freighter capacity out of Miami.

        Copa Airlines via Panama City is a workable alternative first leg, though its cargo programme only runs Monday to Thursday and excludes several breeds outright. A third path exists too: Panama City to Istanbul on Turkish Airlines, three times a week, then on to Dubai. Istanbul's live-animal facility is sizeable, built to hold up to 78 animals at once with round-the-clock veterinary cover, which makes it a genuine backup when Miami cargo space runs tight.

    - heading: "What MOCCAE checks before your pet clears customs"
      body: |
        Two rules catch owners out more than the paperwork itself. First, MOCCAE bans a specific list of breeds and their crosses, from American Pit Bull Terrier and American Bully through to Cane Corso, Dogue de Bordeaux and Japanese Tosa, and enforcement leans on appearance as much as documents. A dog that looks like one of the banned types can be turned away regardless of what the certificate says.

        Second, the import permit itself is short-lived: AED 200, applied for online, valid for only 30 days. Apply once your flights are confirmed, not before. MOCCAE also caps personal imports at two pets per person per year, worth checking early if you're moving more than one animal.
