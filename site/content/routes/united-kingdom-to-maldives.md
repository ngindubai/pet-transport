  h1: "Pet Transport from United Kingdom to Maldives: 2026 Guide"
  overview: |
    Ask this question with a dog in mind and the honest answer is: you cannot do it. Import of dogs into the Maldives is prohibited outright, a Maldives Customs Service rule that sits alongside bans on alcohol and pork products, and there is no standard visa-style exception a pet owner can apply for.

    Cats are a different story, and a fairly manageable one. A rabies vaccination given 30 days to 12 months before arrival, the core feline vaccines, a microchip and a health certificate signed within 10 days of travel cover most of what's published. Where the guidance gets thinner is permits and titre tests, so this is one route worth a direct check with Maldivian authorities before you commit to flight dates.

  sections:
    - heading: "The dog answer, stated plainly"
      body: |
        Dogs cannot enter the Maldives. Not with extra paperwork, not with a longer wait, not for an assistance animal in the way that would work in most other countries. Some blogs mention a rare government-approval pathway for service dogs, but we could not find a primary Maldivian government document confirming that as a working, repeatable process, so we are not going to present it as one. If a service dog is genuinely involved, get written confirmation directly from the Maldives government or your nearest Maldives High Commission before assuming any exception applies.
    - heading: "What a cat actually needs"
      body: |
        Microchip first, then rabies vaccination, with the Maldives asking for it to have been given between 30 days and 12 months before arrival. Add the standard feline vaccines: viral rhinotracheitis, calicivirus, panleukopenia and feline leukaemia. A health certificate signed within 10 days of entry closes out the paperwork. None of this requires the multi-month wait that a titre test route would demand.
    - heading: "Two things published guidance does not fully settle"
      body: |
        Some sources say a pet cat travelling with its owner needs no import permit at all. Others describe a general import permit requirement for pets entering the Maldives. We have not found a single, current, government-issued page that resolves this cleanly, so treat it as an open question to confirm with your vet or agent, not a settled fact.
    - heading: "A breed detail most guides miss"
      body: |
        Savannah and Bengal cats are not treated the same as an ordinary domestic shorthair. Under guidance summarised by PetTravel.com, they are only accepted once the animal is five generations removed from its wild ancestor, which most Savannahs and Bengals bred for the pet market will meet, but it is worth a pedigree check before booking if you own one of these breeds.
    - heading: "Getting to Male"
      body: |
        British Airways' seasonal Gatwick-Male service is the only direct option, and only in the winter window. Outside that, Emirates via Dubai and Qatar Airways via Doha are the established cargo routings, both needing an IATA-spec crate and a licensed agent.
