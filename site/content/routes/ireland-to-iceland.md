content:
  h1: "Pet Transport from Ireland to Iceland"
  overview: |
    MAST will not take an Irish vet's standard Animal Health Certificate for this route. Its own Form D1, not the document that clears an Irish pet into France or Germany, is what actually gets checked at Keflavik. Ireland's rabies-free Category 1 status trims the pre-export timeline, no 90-day wait once a titre test clears, but does nothing to the mandatory 14-day quarantine on arrival, which applies to every incoming pet regardless of origin.

    A dog needs more than rabies cover here. MAST also wants negative Brucella canis and, for an intact dog, Leishmania results, plus protection against Angiostrongylus vasorum. None of it is optional, and a missing result can hold up the whole booking.

  sections:
    - heading: "What Category 1 status changes, and what it leaves untouched"
      body: |
        Ireland's place on MAST's Category 1 list affects one thing: the pre-export timeline. Once the rabies titre test clears at 0.5 IU/ml or above, drawn at least 30 days after vaccination, there is no further fixed wait before travel. A Category 2 country's pet faces an additional 90-day wait counted from the blood draw itself.

        The quarantine is untouched by any of this. Every dog and cat lands at Keflavik to a minimum 14 days at Reykjanesbae or Mosel, Irish passport or not. It is a genuine biosecurity measure for an island with no endemic rabies, distemper or parvovirus, and it surprises owners who assume EU membership buys a shortcut on this particular border.

    - heading: "Form D1: not the certificate most Irish vets keep on file"
      body: |
        The standard AHC that satisfies most of this site's other Ireland-outbound routes is the wrong form here. MAST needs its own Certificate of Health and Origin, Form D1 for dogs or C1 for cats, built around its own conditions rather than the EU template.

        That means negative Brucella canis and, for an intact dog, Leishmania spp. results, a test or preventive treatment for Angiostrongylus vasorum, and two rounds of parasite treatment timed 21-28 days and then 5-10 days before import. The completed form, with lab reports attached, needs to reach MAST at petimport@mast.is no later than 5 days before the pet is due to land, or MAST can reject the import outright.

    - heading: "One direct flight, a fixed quarantine calendar"
      body: |
        Icelandair flies Dublin to Keflavik nonstop about seven times a week, roughly 2 hours 35 minutes, and it is the only carrier on the route. It stopped carrying pets as checked baggage on 1 November 2024, so booking runs through Icelandair Cargo, separate from a normal passenger ticket.

        The harder constraint is on the ground. Reykjanesbae and Mosel each admit new arrivals only within a fixed window roughly every three weeks, and Keflavik will not accept an arriving pet outside 06:00 to 17:00 on those dates. Reserve the quarantine slot before locking in the flight.
