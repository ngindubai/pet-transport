content:
  h1: "Pet Transport from Lebanon to Sweden"
  overview: "Jordbruksverket, Sweden's board of agriculture, checks the microchip number on your pet against the certificate the moment cargo lands at Stockholm Arlanda. Any mismatch holds the shipment. That's the sharp end of a process that otherwise mirrors the standard EU unlisted-country sequence used across the bloc. Lebanon does not sit on the EU's approved list, so Swedish authorities require the same microchip, rabies vaccination, FAVN titre test and 90-day wait as they do for every other EU-unlisted country. Cats go through an identical process. Expect the whole journey, door to door, to run 20 to 26 weeks."

  sections:
    - heading: "The unlisted-country sequence Sweden applies to Lebanon"
      body: |
        Microchip, then rabies vaccination, never the reverse. Once vaccinated, wait a minimum of 30 days before blood is drawn for the FAVN titre test at an EU-approved laboratory. A result under 0.5 IU/ml fails outright and forces a restart from a fresh vaccination.

        Pass that threshold, and the clock on the real wait begins: 90 days from the date the blood was actually drawn. Not from the date the lab confirms the result, which can itself take two to four weeks to arrive. Sweden, like every EU member state, applies this under EU Regulation 576/2013 and Commission Delegated Regulation 2020/692, with no exemption for Lebanon.

        Run the numbers and the floor is around 120 days from vaccination to eligibility, before any Lebanese admin or flight booking is added on top.

    - heading: "Getting the Lebanese paperwork endorsed"
      body: |
        The health certificate for this route has to be completed in EU format by an official vet, then endorsed by the Lebanese Ministry of Agriculture before your pet can travel. This step alone is worth building a generous buffer around: Lebanese government processing is not always fast, and 2 to 4 weeks for the endorsement is a realistic planning figure, not a worst case.

        Time the certificate's issue date carefully. It is valid for only 10 days, so it needs to be completed close to the actual flight, after your 90-day wait has finished and cargo space is confirmed, not before.

    - heading: "Routing from Beirut to Stockholm Arlanda"
      body: |
        There's no direct flight for pet cargo between Beirut and Stockholm, so every option here involves a connection. Lufthansa Cargo via Frankfurt is the most practical for a Northern European destination like Sweden, using the purpose-built Frankfurt Animal Lounge as a stopover. Turkish Airlines Cargo via Istanbul offers the widest network and strong availability if Frankfurt is full. Emirates via Dubai and Qatar Airways via Doha both work, though the Gulf routings add hours in transit and can be affected by summer heat restrictions on live animal cargo. Book the flight only once your titre test result and Ministry endorsement are both in hand.

faqs:
  - question: "Why does the 90-day wait matter so much for this route?"
    answer: "Because it starts from the blood draw date, not the date you receive the titre test result. Owners who count from the result date routinely find they are weeks short of the real entry date, which can mean re-booking flights and cargo space."
  - question: "Does Sweden accept a standard vet certificate instead of the EU-format one?"
    answer: "No. Jordbruksverket requires the EU-format non-commercial health certificate, endorsed by the Lebanese Ministry of Agriculture. A standard Lebanese vet certificate without that endorsement will not be accepted at Stockholm Arlanda."
  - question: "Is there a direct flight from Beirut to Stockholm for pet cargo?"
    answer: "No. All four main carrier options, Lufthansa via Frankfurt, Turkish Airlines via Istanbul, Emirates via Dubai and Qatar Airways via Doha, involve at least one connection before reaching Stockholm Arlanda."
  - question: "What happens if my pet's microchip cannot be read on arrival in Sweden?"
    answer: "Jordbruksverket relies on the microchip to match your pet to its certificate. If the chip cannot be scanned or the number does not match the paperwork, the shipment can be held at Arlanda until the discrepancy is resolved, so confirm the chip reads correctly before you travel."
