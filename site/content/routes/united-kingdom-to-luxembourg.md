  sections:
    - heading: "Relocating post-Brexit: why the AHC matters for this corridor"
      body: |
        A significant number of UK financial and legal professionals have relocated to Luxembourg since Brexit, and many have brought their pets. This has made the UK-Luxembourg pet transport corridor one of the better-understood routes among APHA-authorised vets and pet transport agents in the UK.

        Before Brexit, a UK pet passport covered travel to and from EU countries with no additional paperwork. Since January 2021, every journey from the UK to an EU country requires a new AHC issued within 10 days of travel. There is no residual validity from a UK pet passport.

        If you are relocating permanently, the AHC requirement applies to your first journey. Once registered with a Luxembourg vet, your pet will be issued with an EU pet passport, which covers future EU travel without the need for repeated AHCs. This is one advantage of permanent relocation over repeated short visits.

    - heading: "Living in Luxembourg with a pet: what to know after arrival"
      body: |
        Luxembourg requires all dogs and cats to be registered in the national animal registration database. Contact the Administration des Services Veterinaires (ASV) on arrival to register your pet and obtain a Luxembourg EU pet passport from a recognised vet.

        The EU pet passport simplifies all future travel within the Schengen area, covering the neighbouring countries of France, Belgium, Germany, and the Netherlands. Luxembourg City is also within easy driving distance of all four, making cross-border vet care straightforward.

        Luxembourg has no breed bans on dogs, though individual communes may have additional regulations. Check with the ASV on arrival for any local requirements that apply to your dog's breed.
