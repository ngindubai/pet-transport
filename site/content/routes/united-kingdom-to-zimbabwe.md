  overview: "Moving a pet from the UK to Zimbabwe requires an advance import permit from the Director of Veterinary Services, an APHA Export Health Certificate using the Zimbabwe-specific format (certificate 3929), and a current rabies vaccination given between 30 days and 12 months before entry. There is no routine quarantine for compliant pets, but all arriving pets face inspection at Harare Airport.   sections:
    - heading: "The Zimbabwe-specific EHC format and why it matters"
      body: |
        The UK's APHA maintains a list of destination-specific Export Health Certificate formats for different countries. Zimbabwe uses certificate 3929, which is distinct from the generic EHC or the EU AHC format. When booking your APHA-authorised vet appointment, specifically request the Zimbabwe format (3929) to ensure the correct certificate is completed.

        The certificate lists microchip number, species, breed, rabies vaccination history (product, batch number, date, expiry, and administering vet), and health examination findings. APHA endorsement follows the vet's completion, typically within 1-5 working days.

        GOV.UK maintains an up-to-date list of all EHC formats and their destinations at the export health certificates section. Check this before booking to confirm the current Zimbabwe format is 3929 and that your APHA-authorised vet is familiar with it.

    - heading: "Routing via Addis Ababa, Nairobi, Dubai or Johannesburg"
      body: |
        There are no direct flights from the UK to Harare. All routes involve one or more connections. The main hub options are:

        Ethiopian Airlines via Addis Ababa (ADD): Addis is the largest cargo hub for Africa and has strong UK connections. Ethiopian Airlines Cargo has experience with live animal shipments across Africa.

        Kenya Airways via Nairobi (NBO): Nairobi is well-connected to southern Africa. Kenya Airways Cargo accepts live animals on suitable routes.

        Emirates SkyCargo via Dubai (DXB): Emirates has temperature-controlled live animal facilities and a strong global network. The DXB-HRE route is available.

        For each connection, check the transit-country requirements for your pet. Most hub countries (Ethiopia, Kenya, UAE) do not require pets in transit to clear customs as long as they stay airside, but confirm with your airline and the transit country's veterinary authority.
