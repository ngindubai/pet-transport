  h1: "Pet Transport from Georgia to Belgium"
  overview: |
    Georgia to Belgium is a route that requires careful planning. Georgia is an EU-unlisted third country, which means the full EU import protocol applies: microchip first, rabies vaccination, FAVN titre test, 90-day wait, and an NFSA-endorsed EU health certificate. Allow five to six months from the first vet appointment.

    A practical advantage of Georgia compared with Armenia or Azerbaijan is that there may be EU-approved titre test laboratories in Georgia or in nearby countries, making the blood testing logistics easier to manage.

  sections:
    - heading: "EU requirements for pets from Georgia"
      body: |
        Belgium follows EU Regulation 576/2013 for all incoming pets from unlisted third countries. Georgia is unlisted. The steps are: ISO microchip implanted before the rabies vaccination, rabies vaccination, FAVN titre test at least 30 days after vaccination with a result of 0.5 IU/ml or above, 90-day wait from blood draw date, and an NFSA-endorsed EU health certificate issued within 10 days of travel.

        FASFC (Federal Agency for the Safety of the Food Chain) inspects incoming pets at Brussels Airport (BRU) cargo terminal. Compliant pets are released without quarantine.

    - heading: "FAVN titre test logistics from Tbilisi"
      body: |
        Georgia is better positioned for titre test logistics than some other Caucasus countries. There may be EU-approved laboratories in Georgia itself or in nearby countries that accept samples from Tbilisi. Check this with your vet in Tbilisi before drawing blood.

        If no approved lab is accessible locally, blood can be shipped to approved labs in Germany or Turkey. The cold-chain shipping requirements and sample handling are the same as for any international sample shipment.

        The 90-day wait starts from the blood draw date. Laboratory processing time and the 90-day wait run concurrently, so waiting for results does not extend the overall timeline.

    - heading: "Cargo options from Tbilisi to Brussels"
      body: |
        Turkish Airlines via Istanbul (IST) is the most practical cargo option. TBS to IST is a regular Turkish Airlines route, with connecting cargo services from IST to Brussels Airport (BRU).

        Wizz Air operates from Tbilisi to several European cities and accepts cabin pets on qualifying routes. If a Wizz Air route connects TBS to Brussels South Charleroi (CRL) or another Belgian airport directly, and your pet qualifies by size (typically under 8kg including carrier), cabin travel is significantly simpler than cargo.

        Lufthansa Cargo via Frankfurt and Georgian Airways connecting to European hubs are additional options for cargo-size pets.
