  sections:
    - heading: "What Lebanon requires at the border"
      body: |
        Lebanon's veterinary import requirements are administered by the Ministry of Agriculture. Officers inspect documentation at Beirut Rafic Hariri International Airport (BEY) on arrival.

        The requirements are a valid rabies vaccination administered at least 30 days before entry (note: 30 days, not 21), an official health certificate issued within 10 days of travel, and an ISO-compliant microchip. Lebanon does not require a titre test for EU-origin pets. No quarantine applies for compliant arrivals.

        Check current Slovenian and Lebanese government travel advisories before booking. The operational situation at Beirut airport can change and should be confirmed before committing to a travel date.

    - heading: "UVHVVR health certificate process from Slovenia"
      body: |
        Slovenia's export health certificate for non-EU destinations is the EU-format official veterinary certificate signed by a UVHVVR-authorised official vet and endorsed by UVHVVR. Both steps must complete within 10 days of departure.

        Book the vet examination within 10 days of your travel date. Allow 1-2 working days for UVHVVR endorsement. Check uvhvvr.gov.si for authorised official vets in your area.

    - heading: "Cargo routing from Slovenia to Beirut"
      body: |
        There is no direct cargo service from Ljubljana (LJU) to Beirut (BEY). The standard routing is Austrian Airlines LJU to Vienna (VIE), then Turkish Airlines VIE to Istanbul (IST), then IST to BEY.

        Originating at Vienna Airport simplifies the booking to two legs. Turkish Airlines cargo from VIE to IST to BEY is well-established. If travelling June to September, check for temperature embargoes on the IST-BEY connection. Brachycephalic breed restrictions apply year-round on Turkish Airlines cargo.
