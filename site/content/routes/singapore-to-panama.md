  h1: "Pet Transport from Singapore to Panama: 2026 Guide"
  overview: |
    Panama keeps an actual embassy in Singapore, at 16 Raffles Quay, and that single fact changes this route more than any regulation does. Most owners moving a pet from Southeast Asia to Latin America assume a consulate step means shipping documents abroad and waiting weeks. Here, it means a short trip across town.

    Beyond that, this is one of the lighter destinations in this build. No titre test, no import permit, no quarantine for a compliant pet. The genuine catch is a step most people don't expect: after AVS endorses the Singapore health certificate, it still needs a stamp from that Panamanian embassy before MIDA will accept it at Tocumen.

  sections:
    - heading: "Why Panama skips the titre test, and what it asks for instead"
      body: |
        Panama does not apply a rabies antibody titre test to pet imports from any country. What MIDA does require: an ISO microchip, rabies vaccination at least 30 days before entry, documented parasite treatment within 14 days of travel, and a health certificate. That's a notably shorter checklist than the Schedule III process Singapore itself applies to most Asian and Latin American arrivals.

    - heading: "The consulate step, and why Singapore is easier than it looks"
      body: |
        After a Singapore vet completes the health certificate and AVS endorses or QR-verifies it, the document still needs a stamp and signature from a Panamanian consulate or embassy before the pet travels. Skip this and MIDA's inspection at Tocumen won't accept the certificate, regardless of how correct everything else is.

        The genuinely useful fact here: Panama's embassy in Singapore sits at 16 Raffles Quay, in the Hong Leong Building. Owners moving a pet from many other Asian countries have to route documents to a regional mission, often in another country entirely. A local embassy visit is a real advantage this particular route has over several of its neighbours in this build.

    - heading: "Routing through Istanbul on a single carrier"
      body: |
        Turkish Airlines operates Singapore Changi to Istanbul nonstop, around 11 hours on a 777-300ER, and Istanbul to Panama City Tocumen nonstop, roughly 12 hours 51 minutes on an A350, close to 21 times a week. Both legs run on the same airline's own cargo operation, which avoids the handover risk that comes with changing carriers mid-journey.

        Singapore Airlines reaches Frankfurt, Amsterdam or several US cities with equally strong live-animal handling, connecting onward via Iberia, KLM, Copa Airlines or American Airlines. That routing works, but it means at least one extra carrier change compared with the Turkish Airlines option.
