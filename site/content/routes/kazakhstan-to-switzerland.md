  sections:
    - heading: "Sequencing the FSVO permission and vet preparation correctly"
      body: |
        The FSVO permission and the veterinary preparation can run in parallel once the FSVO application is submitted, but the permission must be in hand before the travel date. A practical approach:

        Submit the FSVO application at blv.admin.ch as soon as you decide to relocate your pet to Switzerland. The application takes at least 3 weeks to process, and processing times can extend during busy periods. While the application is being processed, start the microchip and vaccination sequence.

        By the time you have a passing titre test result and the 3-month wait is approaching its end, you should have the FSVO permission confirmed. Use this period to book the cargo and arrange the Committee for Veterinary Control-endorsed health certificate.

        Do not set a cargo date before the FSVO permission is confirmed. If the permission is delayed, your cargo date may be before the permission arrives, which means the pet cannot enter Switzerland on that date.

    - heading: "Air Astana from Almaty: what cargo owners need to know"
      body: |
        Air Astana's scheduled services to Frankfurt (FRA) and Amsterdam (AMS) are a genuine advantage for Kazakhstani owners compared to other Caucasus countries that depend entirely on Turkish Airlines or Flydubai.

        For Zurich-bound moves, the ALA-FRA-ZRH routing via Air Astana and SWISS or Lufthansa is practical. Frankfurt Airport's Animal Lounge handles the transit between flights well. Total elapsed time from Almaty to Zurich via this routing is approximately 10-12 hours.

        Before booking this routing, confirm live animal cargo acceptance with Air Astana Cargo specifically for the ALA-FRA segment. Air Astana Cargo handles IATA-compliant shipments but live animal policies have route and season-specific restrictions. Summer months at Almaty can see temperatures that trigger embargo conditions.

faqs:
  - question: "What makes the Switzerland process different from entering an EU country like Germany?"
    answer: "Two differences: First, FSVO advance permission is required for pets from Kazakhstan, whereas EU countries do not require advance permission under EU Regulation 2020/692. Second, the health certificate must follow the FSVO format rather than the standard EU AHC template. The core requirements (microchip before vaccination, titre test, 3-month wait) are equivalent to EU unlisted country rules."
  - question: "Can I avoid the Swiss FSVO permission if my pet already lives in a EU country?"
    answer: "If your pet has been resident in an EU country and is fully documented under EU pet travel rules (microchip, vaccination, EU passport), the entry process from the EU country is significantly simpler. The high-risk country rules from Kazakhstan apply to the movement from Kazakhstan, not from an EU member state. If you are moving via a stop in an EU country first, the Switzerland entry process is from the EU country's rules."
  - question: "Which Zurich Airport terminal handles live animal cargo from Kazakhstan?"
    answer: "Live animal cargo at Zurich Airport (ZRH) is handled by SWISS Cargo and other carriers at the cargo terminal, separate from passenger terminals. SWISS Cargo at ZRH has dedicated live animal facilities and handles FSVO entry documentation. Your cargo agent coordinates the arrival and inspection process with the Swiss border vet."
