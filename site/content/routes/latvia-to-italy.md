  h1: "Pet Transport Latvia to Italy: 2026 Guide"
  overview: "Latvia to Italy is a standard intra-EU pet move. Both countries operate under the EU pet travel framework: your pet needs a microchip, current rabies vaccination, and an EU pet passport or AHC. The challenge, as with most Baltic-to-Southern-Europe routes, is finding a carrier that accepts pets, since the most common budget routes from Riga to Italy do not take animals. airBaltic, Lufthansa via Frankfurt, or LOT via Warsaw are the right carriers for this corridor.   sections:
    - heading: "Why budget carriers are not an option from Riga to Italy"
      body: |
        Riga Airport (RIX) has direct or near-direct connections to Italian cities on Ryanair and Wizz Air. These are popular and affordable for passengers, but they do not transport pets in any form, either in the cabin or as cargo. For a pet owner, this means planning around the full-service carrier options.

        airBaltic is Riga's home carrier and offers the best connectivity for EU destinations including Italy. Its routes to Italian cities typically connect through other airports, but the pet can travel in-cabin if small enough (combined weight with carrier up to 8 kg) or as cargo for larger animals. Lufthansa via Frankfurt gives the best large-dog cargo option for this corridor, with the Frankfurt Animal Lounge providing intermediate care.

        For cats and small breeds, the airBaltic or LOT routing is often simpler and cheaper. For a large dog, plan on the Lufthansa FRA routing or work with a specialist cargo agent.

    - heading: "What Italy requires after arrival"
      body: |
        EU pet travel documentation covers entry into Italy but is separate from Italy's domestic dog registration rules. Italy requires all dogs to be registered in the Anagrafe Canina (national dog register) through the local Azienda Sanitaria Locale (ASL), which is the public health authority. If you are moving to Italy permanently, this registration should be done within 30 days of establishing residence and gives your dog an Italian ID code.

        This registration is a domestic Italian legal requirement, not an import requirement. Your EU pet passport covers entry. The Anagrafe registration records the dog's microchip number and your Italian address. A vet usually assists with this process at your first Italian veterinary appointment.

    answer: "Yes. A German Shepherd can travel as cargo from Riga to Rome. The most practical routing is via Frankfurt (Lufthansa Cargo, with the Frankfurt Animal Lounge) or via Warsaw (LOT Cargo). airBaltic handles cargo from Riga and may have connections. Contact the cargo department of your chosen airline directly, provide the dog's weight and exact crate dimensions, and confirm live animal acceptance on each segment. Total journey time for cargo including the intermediate stop is typically 5-8 hours."
  - question: "Does my pet need to be seen by an Italian vet on arrival?"
    answer: "No mandatory vet check is required on arrival for intra-EU pet movements with a valid EU passport or AHC and current vaccination. Italy's Servizi Veterinari does not typically conduct spot checks at airports for EU arrivals. However, keeping a local Italian vet appointment within the first few weeks of arrival is sensible to establish a patient record and to begin the Anagrafe registration process if you are staying permanently."
  - question: "Can I bring prescription medication for my dog when flying Latvia to Italy?"
    answer: "Yes. Prescription medications for your own pet can be carried when travelling within the EU. Carry documentation from your Latvian vet confirming the medication is prescribed for your specific animal (with microchip number). Quantities should be appropriate for personal use. Some medications that are prescription-only in Latvia may have different classifications in Italy, but for personal companion animal medication this rarely causes issues if properly documented."
