  sections:
    - heading: "The CDC Dog Import Form and why it matters"
      body: |
        The CDC Dog Import Form is required for every dog entering the United States, regardless of which country it is coming from. It is completed online before travel and links the dog's microchip number to its travel record and vaccination status. There is no fee.

        The Netherlands is not classified as a high-risk country for dog-related rabies, so dogs from the Netherlands do not face the additional USDA facility certification requirements that apply to dogs from higher-risk origins. Your Dutch official vet issues the health certificate, which the NVWA (Netherlands Food and Consumer Product Safety Authority) endorses. Present the CDC form and the endorsed health certificate at the CDC inspection desk on arrival in the USA.

        Complete the CDC form before travel and carry a printed or digital copy with your travel documents.

    - heading: "KLM cabin travel across the Atlantic"
      body: |
        KLM Royal Dutch Airlines is based at Amsterdam Schiphol and operates direct flights to multiple US cities: New York JFK, Los Angeles, Atlanta, Houston, Boston, Chicago, Miami, San Francisco, Washington DC, and Minneapolis among others. KLM allows small dogs and cats in the passenger cabin, provided the pet plus bag weighs no more than 8 kg and the carrier fits under the seat.

        On a transatlantic journey of 8-9 hours, cabin travel is significantly more comfortable for a small pet than the cargo hold. Book the cabin pet space at the same time as your own seat; cabin pet places are limited per flight. The KLM cabin pet fee is typically EUR 75 to EUR 125 each way.

        For larger pets that exceed the 8 kg cabin limit, KLM Cargo handles pets in the pressurised hold from Amsterdam Schiphol. The crate must be IATA-compliant and sized appropriately for your pet.

faqs:
  - question: "Does my dog need to quarantine when entering the USA from the Netherlands?"
    answer: "No. There is no routine quarantine for correctly documented dogs from the Netherlands entering the USA. You need a completed CDC Dog Import Form, a current rabies vaccination, and a health certificate from a Dutch official vet (NVWA-endorsed). CDC inspection at the arrival airport is brief for well-documented pets from the Netherlands. Hawaii applies its own quarantine programme separately, regardless of where the pet flew from."
  - question: "Can my small dog or cat fly in the cabin from Amsterdam to the USA?"
    answer: "Yes, on KLM. KLM allows small dogs and cats in the passenger cabin on direct Amsterdam to USA routes, provided the pet plus bag weighs no more than 8 kg and the carrier fits under the seat. Book the cabin space when you book your own ticket. Delta, as KLM's SkyTeam partner, may also offer cabin pet options on some transatlantic routes. Check at the time of booking."
  - question: "What documentation does my dog need to enter the USA from the Netherlands?"
    answer: "Dogs entering the USA from the Netherlands need: (1) a completed CDC Dog Import Form (completed before travel, no fee); (2) a current rabies vaccination administered by a licensed vet; (3) a health certificate issued by a Dutch NVWA-authorised official vet and endorsed by the NVWA. An ISO microchip is required. Present the CDC form and health certificate at the CDC inspection desk on arrival."
  - question: "Do cats need paperwork to enter the USA from the Netherlands?"
    answer: "Cats have no federal documentation requirements when entering the USA from the Netherlands. Your airline will likely require a health certificate, so confirm with your carrier. Hawaii applies strict quarantine rules to cats, separately from the federal requirements."
