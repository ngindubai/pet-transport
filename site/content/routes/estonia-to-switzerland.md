  h1: "Pet Transport Estonia to Switzerland: Your 2026 Checklist"
  overview: "Switzerland sits outside the EU, but do not let that worry you. Under its bilateral veterinary agreement with the EU, Switzerland accepts EU pet passports and follows rules that closely mirror EU standards.   sections:
    - heading: "Why Switzerland is simpler than you might expect"
      body: |
        The bilateral veterinary agreement between Switzerland and the EU was established to align animal health rules across the two jurisdictions. In practice, this means that a dog or cat with a valid EU pet passport and a current rabies vaccination can enter Switzerland without a titre test, without quarantine, and without an import permit.

        The Swiss Federal Food Safety and Veterinary Office (FSVO, also known as BLV in German) governs pet imports and publishes its requirements at blv.admin.ch. Border officers at Zurich, Geneva, and Basel airports, and at land crossings, may conduct spot checks. Having your documentation ready and accessible makes this quick.

        One thing to confirm before you travel: your pet's rabies vaccination must be current. Switzerland checks that the vaccine is within its valid period. If your pet is due a booster, get it done before departure.

    - heading: "Getting from Tallinn to Switzerland"
      body: |
        There are no direct flights from Tallinn (TLL) to Swiss airports. The most common connections are via Riga (airBaltic) or via Frankfurt (Lufthansa) to Zurich (ZRH), Geneva (GVA), or Basel (BSL). SWISS, part of the Lufthansa Group, is an excellent carrier for this route once you reach a connecting hub.

        Small pets travel in the cabin on airBaltic and Lufthansa services. SWISS also allows small pets in the cabin on most routes. Larger dogs travel as checked baggage or in the cargo hold. The hold is temperature-controlled and pressurised, though brachycephalic breeds face restrictions with most airlines.

        When booking, confirm with the airline that your pet reservation covers the entire itinerary. A booking made on the first leg does not automatically transfer to the second.

    - heading: "Documents to prepare"
      body: |
        Your Estonian vet can issue an EU pet passport once your pet is microchipped and vaccinated. This document stays with your pet for life and is the most convenient option for any pet that will travel more than once.

        If this is a one-way move, an Animal Health Certificate (AHC) is an alternative. It is a single-use document that must be endorsed by an official Estonian vet within 10 days of your arrival in Switzerland. Time the appointment carefully to stay within the window.

        Switzerland may require the AHC to follow the EU format. Check with the issuing vet that the certificate meets Swiss requirements as well as EU standards. In most cases this is automatic, but it is worth confirming.

Speak to your airline before booking."
