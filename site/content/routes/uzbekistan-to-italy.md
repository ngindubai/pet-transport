    Two airlines fly Tashkent to Milan Malpensa nonstop, year-round: Uzbekistan Airways and Qanot Sharq. That's an unusual amount of direct capacity for a corridor this size, and it puts Uzbekistan-to-Italy among the more straightforwardly routed moves in this build's Central Asia cluster.

        Uzbekistan Airways and Qanot Sharq both operate the Tashkent-Milan Malpensa route nonstop, year-round, which gives this corridor genuine flexibility compared with routes that rely on a single weekly direct flight or a mandatory transfer. Turkish Airlines via Istanbul remains a solid backup with more frequent overall departures if live animal cargo space is tight on either direct carrier.

    - heading: "The EU sequence, and no breed ban to worry about"
      body: |
        Italy applies the standard unlisted-country protocol: microchip before vaccination, a 30-day gap before the titre blood draw, a result of at least 0.5 IU/ml, then a fixed 90 days from the draw date, under Commission Delegated Regulation (EU) 2026/131. Italy has carried no national breed-specific legislation since 2009, though airline-level brachycephalic restrictions are separate and still apply.

        On the Uzbekistan side, the territorial veterinary service, under the Committee of Veterinary and Livestock Development that replaced the old State Committee in 2023, issues the export certificate at the TAS border checkpoint close to departure.
