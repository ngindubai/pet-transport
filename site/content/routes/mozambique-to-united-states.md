content:
  h1: "Pet Transport from Mozambique to United States"
  overview: |
    A dog leaving Mozambique for the United States faces the CDC's full high-risk pathway, the same set of rules introduced on 1 August 2024 for any country on the CDC's high-risk dog rabies list. Mozambique is on that list. There is no low-risk shortcut here, unlike some other African and Balkan origins this site now covers.

    The process centres on one specific document: the Certification of Foreign Rabies Vaccination and Microchip form, completed by a Mozambican vet and endorsed by a DINAV government vet. From there, owners choose between a serology titre test, which avoids quarantine, or accepting a 28-day stay at a CDC-registered Animal Care Facility on arrival. Either way, the CDC Dog Import Form is mandatory, and the flight has to land at the specific US airport holding the facility reservation.

    Cats, as with every US route, are exempt from almost all of this. The CDC's dog-focused rules simply do not apply to them.

  sections:
    - heading: "The certification form that actually matters"
      body: |
        A general DINAV export health certificate, the kind used for most other destinations, is not what the CDC checks for a high-risk dog. The document that matters is the Certification of Foreign Rabies Vaccination and Microchip form, which a Mozambican vet completes and a DINAV government vet then endorses. It records the microchip number, the vaccination date, and the vet's attestation, and it expires 30 days after signature, for single use only.

        From there, two paths diverge. Path one: draw blood at least 30 days after vaccination, send it to a CDC-approved laboratory (Maputo has none locally, so expect courier time), and if the titre passes, the dog avoids quarantine entirely, with the result valid for life provided vaccination cover never lapses. Path two: skip the titre and accept 28 days of quarantine at a CDC-registered Animal Care Facility on arrival, entirely at the owner's cost. Most owners choose the titre route because it is cheaper overall, but a tight travel deadline sometimes makes the quarantine path the more realistic option.

        Whichever path is chosen, the CDC Dog Import Form still has to be completed online before travel. It is free, but it is not optional, and a dog arriving without it risks being turned away regardless of how well the rest of the paperwork was handled.

    - heading: "Landing at the right airport: why the routing is not flexible"
      body: |
        High-risk dogs cannot simply fly to whichever US city is most convenient. The flight must arrive at the specific airport where the owner has reserved space at a CDC-registered Animal Care Facility, and the dog cannot make a domestic connection to a different city first. Facilities are limited: Pender Pet Retreat at Washington Dulles (IAD) is one, alongside facilities at JFK, ATL, LAX, MIA and PHL.

        Ethiopian Airlines' daily Maputo-Addis Ababa connection reaches Washington Dulles on its US network, matching up neatly with the Pender Pet Retreat reservation. Qatar Airways connects Maputo through Doha to New York JFK, another facility-equipped port, with the added benefit of Doha's dedicated Live Animals Centre for longer transits. TAP Air Portugal's daily nonstop from Lisbon also reaches JFK, though its hold weight limit drops to 32kg specifically on US-touching sectors, tighter than the 45kg allowed on its other routes.

        LAM Mozambique Airlines, worth ruling out early, has no transatlantic service at all. It can carry the pet as far as Maputo from a domestic or regional point, but the US leg has to be one of the three carriers above.
