  sections:
    - heading: "What happens when your pet lands at Lisbon"
      body: |
        DGAV, Portugal's food and veterinary authority, checks every arriving pet against its paperwork at the LIS cargo terminal. The health certificate has to be in EU format, MAAIF-endorsed, and no older than 10 days from the vet's examination date to the day of travel.

        There is no routine quarantine for a pet that arrives with everything in order. That is the reward for correct paperwork. But an incomplete file, a chip number transcription error, or a certificate that has expired by even a day can mean the pet sits at the cargo terminal while the paperwork gets sorted, at the owner's expense. Build a buffer of a few days either side of the 10-day certificate window so a delayed flight does not invalidate the whole document.

    - heading: "The titre test sequence and flying from Entebbe"
      body: |
        Uganda sits outside the EU's approved country list, so Portugal applies the full sequence: microchip first, rabies vaccination second, a 30-day gap, then blood drawn for a titre test at an approved laboratory clearing 0.5 IU/ml. A 90-day countdown starts from the draw date, and only once it finishes can the pet legally enter Portugal.

        Ethiopian Airlines via Addis Ababa is generally the strongest option given Ethiopian's African cargo depth. Turkish Airlines' non-stop Entebbe-Istanbul leg shortens the first stage of the journey, and Qatar Airways via Doha is a further alternative.
