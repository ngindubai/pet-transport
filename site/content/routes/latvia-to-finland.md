  sections:
    - heading: "Finnish Import Requirements for Pets from Latvia"
      body: |
        Finland's pet import rules for animals arriving from EU member states are clear and consistent with the wider EU framework. Your pet must have an ISO-compliant microchip (15-digit, implanted before the rabies vaccination), a current rabies vaccination recorded in the EU pet passport, and that vaccination must have been given at least 21 days before you travel.

        There is no titre test requirement for pets arriving from Latvia. There is no quarantine for compliant arrivals. Finland does not require tapeworm treatment for dogs coming from EU member states (this is a requirement for Norway, not Finland). The EU pet passport issued by your Latvian vet is sufficient for entry. Source: Ruokavirasto (ruokavirasto.fi).

    - heading: "Riga to Helsinki: Flight Options and Pet Booking"
      body: |
        The Riga (RIX) to Helsinki Vantaa (HEL) route is one of the shorter flights in the Baltic-to-Nordic corridor, typically around one hour. airBaltic operates this route with regular departures and accepts both cabin pets and cargo animals. Small cats and dogs that meet the airline's size and weight limits can travel in the cabin in an approved carrier. Larger dogs must travel as cargo.

        Finnair also flies this route and operates a live animal cargo programme for pets that cannot travel in cabin. Ryanair offers budget fares but takes cabin pets only, no cargo. When booking any airline, register the live animal at the same time as your ticket purchase. Space for pets, especially cargo, is limited and airlines apply per-flight caps. Contact the airline's live animal desk 4 to 6 weeks before travel to confirm current policies and rates.
