  sections:
    - heading: "MAAIF import permit: start here"
      body: |
        Uganda's Ministry of Agriculture, Animal Industry and Fisheries (MAAIF) at maaif.go.ug is the competent authority for companion animal imports. The import permit from MAAIF is not optional. Without it, the inspection team at Entebbe International Airport cannot process your pet's arrival.

        Apply with your pet's microchip number, vaccination history, breed and species, travel date, and your contact details. Eight to ten weeks of lead time is recommended. MAAIF processes applications from a range of countries, and turnaround times are generally reasonable, but early application gives you room to handle any queries or requests for additional documentation.

        Once the permit arrives, keep it with your travel documents. Present it at Entebbe alongside the DAFF-endorsed health certificate. MAAIF's inspection team at EBB is the final clearance gate.

    - heading: "Routing: Ethiopian Airlines via Addis Ababa is the most direct option"
      body: |
        From Sydney or Melbourne, the most commonly used routing for Entebbe connects through Addis Ababa (ADD) on Ethiopian Airlines. Ethiopian operates from Australia via Singapore or Bangkok to Addis, and Ethiopian Cargo connects ADD to EBB as part of its wide African network. The ADD-EBB leg is short, around 2 hours.

        Kenya Airways via Nairobi (NBO) is an alternative for owners who prefer the NBO connection point. NBO-EBB is another short East African hop. Getting from Australia to Nairobi usually requires a Gulf or European hub transfer first.

        Brussels Airlines via Brussels (BRU) is useful if your cargo naturally connects through Europe. Brussels Airlines operates BRU-EBB directly as part of its East Africa network. This routing works well for owners who want a European hub for other logistical reasons.

        Total transit times run approximately 24-32 hours depending on the routing and connection times. Book 6-8 weeks ahead and confirm live animal cargo acceptance on every individual leg.

    - heading: "DAFF: endorsing the health certificate for Uganda"
      body: |
        Uganda is a non-EU destination, so the Australian export health certificate is endorsed by DAFF in a format meeting Uganda's requirements, not the EU pet travel format.

        Your accredited vet examines your pet and issues the health certificate. DAFF endorses it. Allow 1-3 working days for DAFF processing. The endorsed certificate must be issued within 10 days of travel, so time the vet appointment carefully within that window.

        DAFF endorsement is the standard Australian process for international pet exports and is well understood by vets experienced in international pet travel. If your vet has not endorsed certificates for non-EU African destinations before, consider using a pet transport agent who can coordinate the documentation.
