  sections:
    - heading: "The bilateral agreement: what it means in practice"
      body: |
        Switzerland and the EU have maintained a bilateral veterinary agreement since 2002 that aligns Swiss animal import standards with EU rules. For a dog or cat travelling from Lithuania, the practical result is that your EU pet passport is accepted directly at the Swiss border, no titre test is needed, and there is no quarantine for compliant animals.

        The Swiss Federal Food Safety and Veterinary Office (FSVO, or BLV in German) oversees pet imports. Border officers at Zurich (ZRH), Geneva (GVA), and Basel (BSL) airports, and at land crossings, may check documents. The inspection is quick when paperwork is in order.

        The one thing to verify before travel: your pet's rabies vaccination must be current. Switzerland enforces the expiry of the recorded vaccination, not just the fact that one exists. If a booster is due, arrange it before departure.

    - heading: "Routing from Vilnius to Swiss airports"
      body: |
        No direct flights connect Vilnius to Switzerland. The most common routes are via Warsaw with LOT Polish Airlines, or via Frankfurt with Lufthansa. Both options can reach Zurich (ZRH), Geneva (GVA), or Basel (BSL) with a single connection.

        Small pets travel in the cabin on LOT and Lufthansa services, subject to the standard 8kg cabin limit (including carrier). SWISS, part of the Lufthansa Group, handles the Zurich end of these connections and also allows small pets in the cabin.

        For larger dogs travelling in the hold, Lufthansa Cargo is well established on this corridor. Confirm the cargo booking on all legs, not just the departure from Vilnius.

    - heading: "Lithuanian vet requirements before departure"
      body: |
        Lithuania's State Food and Veterinary Service (VMVT) authorises vets to issue EU pet passports. The passport is valid for the lifetime of your pet once issued; you only need to keep the vaccination records current. It is recognised by Switzerland under the bilateral agreement and across all EU member states.

        For a one-way relocation, an AHC is a practical alternative. The endorsement must be within 10 days of arriving in Switzerland. Co-ordinate the vet appointment to fall within that window relative to your flight date.

        Ryanair and Wizz Air both operate flights from Lithuania but neither accepts pets. Book with LOT, Lufthansa, or airBaltic.
