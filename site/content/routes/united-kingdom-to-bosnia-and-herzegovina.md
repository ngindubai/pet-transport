  h1: "Pet Transport from the United Kingdom to Bosnia and Herzegovina"
  overview: |
    Bosnia and Herzegovina keeps its own list of rabies-risk countries, set out in Annex IV of a 2012 Official Gazette regulation, and the United Kingdom sits in Part A: the same low-risk group as EU and EFTA states. That single classification means no titre test and no waiting period beyond the standard 21 days after vaccination.

    The paperwork description hasn't quite caught up with Brexit. Annex IV Part A still calls for an "EU passport" for UK pets, a document APHA stopped issuing to Great Britain owners years ago. An Export Health Certificate does the job in practice, but it's worth confirming that at the border rather than assuming.

    Getting a flight that actually carries the pet is the bigger practical hurdle. Sarajevo's two direct London routes, on Wizz Air and Ryanair, carry no animals at all.
  sections:
    - heading: "Why the UK avoids the titre test here"
      body: |
        Bosnia and Herzegovina's Veterinary Office publishes its own two-part country list rather than simply mirroring the EU's current rules. Part A covers EU and EFTA members plus a handful of microstates; Part B covers a longer list of recognised low-risk countries including the United States, Canada, Japan and Australia. Both groups skip the titre test entirely. Only a pet arriving from a country absent from Annex IV altogether needs the FAVN blood test and its three-month wait.

        The United Kingdom appears by name in Part A (Official Gazette of Bosnia and Herzegovina, No. 34/12 and 68/12). That puts UK-origin pets on the fastest available pathway into the country: microchip, 21-day wait after vaccination, and the accompanying certificate.

    - heading: "A document gap worth flagging"
      body: |
        Part A's wording specifies an "EU passport completed and issued by an authorised veterinarian" as the accompanying document. That phrase made sense when the UK was still issuing those blue booklets to its own residents. It hasn't, for outbound pets, since the Brexit transition ended.

        In practice, the Export Health Certificate that APHA now issues for all non-EU destinations serves the same function, and Bosnian entry points generally accept it without complaint. But since the underlying Bosnian regulation was never formally updated to reflect the change, a quick confirmation with the Veterinary Office of Bosnia and Herzegovina before travel closes off an unlikely but real gap.

    - heading: "The flight booking trap, again"
      body: |
        London to Sarajevo has two direct options on paper: Wizz Air to Luton and Ryanair to Stansted, both running seasonally from April to October. Neither will take a pet, cabin or hold, beyond a certified assistance dog.

        Turkish Airlines via Istanbul, Austrian Airlines via Vienna, and Lufthansa via Frankfurt all carry pets on a connecting itinerary instead. Istanbul's dedicated Pet Lounge for animals with a layover over 75 minutes makes that routing a genuinely comfortable option rather than a fallback.
