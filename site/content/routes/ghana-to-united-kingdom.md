    - heading: "The VSD and the Ghana export certificate"
      body: |
        Ghana's Veterinary Services Directorate (VSD), operating under the Ministry of Food and Agriculture, is the authority responsible for animal health documentation for exports. The health certificate for UK import must be issued by a VSD-authorised official vet and endorsed by VSD with an official stamp.

        The certificate format must satisfy UK APHA requirements and include: microchip number, vaccination history with titre test result (laboratory, blood draw date, result in IU/ml, and the date 90 days from the draw), and for dogs the tapeworm treatment details.

        Contact VSD (vetagri.gov.gh) for authorised vets and the current certificate format for UK export. Accra has VSD offices, and the endorsement process typically takes 1 to 3 working days after the vet completes the certificate.

    - heading: "The direct Accra to Heathrow advantage"
      body: |
        British Airways and Virgin Atlantic both operate direct flights from Kotoka International Airport (ACC), Accra to London Heathrow (LHR). This direct routing is a genuine advantage for pet cargo from Ghana compared to countries where all routes require transit connections.

        A direct flight from Accra to Heathrow takes approximately 6 hours. There are no transit country veterinary complications, and the total journey time for your pet is shorter. Both BA and Virgin Cargo accept live animals on suitable aircraft; confirm availability, crate dimensions, and seasonal restrictions directly with the cargo desk.
