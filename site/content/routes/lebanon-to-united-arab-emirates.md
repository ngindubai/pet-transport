  h1: "Pet Transport from Lebanon to the UAE"
  overview: |
    No 90-day wait. That's the detail that separates this route from most of the other unlisted-country corridors in this build. The UAE's Ministry of Climate Change and Environment, MOCCAE, asks for a rabies antibody titre test from Lebanon, same as the EU does, but it does not hold pets to a fixed waiting period afterwards. Once the test clears 0.5 IU/ml, that result is valid for a year, and the pet can travel as soon as the import permit and health certificate are ready.

    That single difference can cut months off the preparation timeline compared with routes into EU countries, where the same test triggers a mandatory 90-day countdown from the blood draw. Lebanon's own side of the process runs through the Ministry of Agriculture, which issues the export permit and endorses the health certificate close to the travel date.

    Beirut also has something few of the corridors in this build can offer: a direct flight. Emirates SkyCargo and FlyDubai both operate non-stop Beirut-Dubai cargo services, so there's no long connection to plan around.

  sections:
    - heading: "MOCCAE's import permit and titre test, explained"
      body: |
        MOCCAE splits exporting countries into two risk categories, and Lebanon sits outside its short low-risk list. That means a rabies antibody titre test at an accredited laboratory, with a minimum result of 0.5 IU/ml, alongside the import permit MOCCAE issues online before departure. The permit itself runs for 90 days from the date it's granted, so timing the application against your actual travel plans matters.

        Where this route differs sharply from EU entry: there's no fixed wait after the blood draw. The EU treats the 90 days from the draw date as non-negotiable. MOCCAE instead treats the test result as valid for 365 days, provided the pet's rabies vaccination stays current throughout. Minimum age for a pet arriving from a country in Lebanon's risk category is 15 weeks.

    - heading: "Getting the export paperwork from Beirut"
      body: |
        Lebanon requires an export permit from the Ministry of Agriculture before any pet leaves the country, on top of the official health certificate. The certificate itself follows MOCCAE's own model format rather than the EU-style document used on routes into Europe, and it's endorsed close to the actual travel date rather than weeks in advance.

        Beirut-Rafic Hariri International (BEY) handles the departure. Emirates SkyCargo and FlyDubai both fly direct to Dubai, which keeps the journey to a single leg. Qatar Airways Cargo via Doha is a solid alternative if either direct option is fully booked, though Gulf summer heat embargoes can occasionally affect live animal cargo scheduling on that routing.
