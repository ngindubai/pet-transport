    - heading: "Routing your pet to Budapest"
      body: |
        Budapest Liszt Ferenc Airport (BUD) is not served by direct transatlantic routes from the USA. Getting your pet there means a connecting itinerary, typically via Frankfurt (FRA) with Lufthansa, Vienna (VIE) with Austrian Airlines, or Warsaw (WAW) with LOT Polish Airlines. Each of these hubs has strong US connectivity and established pet cargo infrastructure.

        The critical point is confirming that both legs of the journey accept pet cargo. A transatlantic booking with Lufthansa Cargo from New York to Frankfurt, for example, does not automatically include the onward Budapest connection under the same contract. You'll need to confirm cargo space for each segment separately, or work with a pet relocation agent who handles the complete booking.

        LOT Polish Airlines from Chicago or New York to Warsaw with a connection to Budapest is worth considering for those in the midwest or northeast. LOT has experience with pet cargo and the Warsaw-Budapest route is short.

    - heading: "Arrival in Hungary and what to expect"
      body: |
        Budapest's Veterinary Border Inspection Post is located at the cargo facility at BUD. Dogs and cats from the USA with correct documentation typically clear quickly. The border vet checks the USDA-endorsed health certificate, microchip, and vaccination records. Keep all originals in your hand luggage for easy access on arrival.

        Hungary is a member of the Schengen Area, so once your pet clears the EU border post at BUD, movement within the Schengen zone is straightforward. Hungarian regulations on dogs in public include leash and muzzle requirements for certain breeds in public places. Check whether your breed has any specific requirements under Hungarian law before travelling.

faqs:
  - question: "Does a dog or cat need a titre test to enter Hungary from the USA?"
    answer: "No. Hungary is an EU member state, and the USA is on the EU's list of approved third countries. Pets from the USA do not need a rabies antibody titre test to enter Hungary. Required documents are: ISO-compliant microchip, valid rabies vaccination, and a USDA-APHIS endorsed health certificate in EU Annex IV format."
  - question: "Can I use Wizz Air to fly my pet to Hungary?"
    answer: "No. Wizz Air does not carry pet cargo on international routes to or from Budapest. For a USA-to-Hungary pet move, use Lufthansa Cargo (via Frankfurt), Austrian Airlines (via Vienna), LOT Polish Airlines (via Warsaw), or another carrier with pet cargo services on both legs. Confirm cargo availability for the complete routing before booking."
  - question: "Is there a direct flight from the USA to Budapest for pet cargo?"
    answer: "No. There are no direct transatlantic pet cargo routes to Budapest Liszt Ferenc Airport (BUD) from the USA at present. All pet shipments to Hungary require a European hub connection, typically Frankfurt, Vienna, or Warsaw. Confirm pet cargo acceptance on both legs of the journey."
  - question: "Does Hungary have breed restrictions for dogs?"
    answer: "Yes. Hungary has breed-specific regulations for dogs considered dangerous (veszélyes ebek). Certain types require muzzle and lead conditions in public. Some breeds may face stricter requirements. Check the current list under Hungarian legislation before travelling with a restricted breed. The National Food Chain Safety Office (NÉBIH) can provide guidance."
  - question: "How long does the USDA health certificate process take for Hungary?"
    answer: "The vet examination and certificate completion can be done in a single appointment. USDA APHIS then endorses the certificate within two to five business days by mail, or faster by electronic VEHCS if your vet uses it. The entire health certificate is valid for 10 days from examination to arrival in Hungary."
  - question: "What happens if my pet's connecting flight to Budapest is cancelled?"
    answer: "If a connecting flight is disrupted, contact your airline's cargo team immediately. Your USDA health certificate has a 10-day validity from examination to arrival in Hungary. If a significant delay pushes the arrival beyond that window, you will need a new health certificate. Keep the vet's number accessible for any emergency rebooking situation."
