  sections:
    - heading: "The Ministero della Salute certificate and who can sign it"
      body: |
        Only a veterinario ufficiale, a vet formally authorised under the Ministero della Salute, can issue the export health certificate Malaysia will accept. Your family vet in Milan or Rome may need to refer you to the local ASL veterinary office if they do not hold this authorisation themselves.

        The certificate has to match the format specified in the DVS import permit, which is a Malaysian document, not an Italian one. Timing the certificate close to travel and confirming the exact format with your Malaysian-side agent avoids a rejected certificate at check-in.

    - heading: "The FAVN pathway and the 14-day quarantine at KLIA"
      body: |
        Blood for the FAVN titre test can only be drawn 30 days or more after the rabies vaccination. A passing result of 0.5 IU/ml or above starts a 90-day wait, tightened under the December 2024 DVS update alongside the current 14-day quarantine period at a DVS-designated facility.

        None of this is specific to Italy. It is the standard non-scheduled country pathway, and EU membership does not create a shortcut. The permit sequence, DVS pre-approval plus a separate MAQIS import permit, has to be complete before your pet is shipped, or it will be held at KLIA on arrival.

    - heading: "Getting a pet out of Rome or Milan"
      body: |
        ITA Airways handles pets in its cargo hold from Fiumicino and asks for 48 hours' notice, useful when you are also juggling permit deadlines. Emirates SkyCargo via Dubai and Singapore Airlines via Singapore are the two strongest long-haul alternatives, both with established temperature-controlled cargo programmes.

        Watch for Gulf summer heat embargoes on the Emirates routing and book your crate size against current IATA specifications well before the flight, since Kuala Lumpur's own climate rules out any pet flying without proper ventilation.
