  sections:
    - heading: "LBVD and the Myanmar export certificate for US travel"
      body: |
        The Livestock Breeding and Veterinary Department (LBVD), under Myanmar's Ministry of Agriculture, Livestock and Irrigation, is responsible for animal health documentation for exports. The health certificate for US import must be endorsed by LBVD.

        The certificate must include your pet's microchip number, vaccination history with titre test result (USDA-recognised laboratory, blood draw date, result in IU/ml), owner details, and fitness to travel. Given the current situation in Myanmar, accessing official government services may be more complex than in neighbouring countries. Use a specialist agent familiar with current LBVD procedures.

        For the titre test, blood samples typically need to be sent to USDA-recognised laboratories outside Myanmar, usually via Bangkok or Singapore. Factor blood sample shipping time into the planning timeline.

    - heading: "Asian hub routing from Myanmar and logistical considerations"
      body: |
        Yangon International Airport (RGN) has no direct flights to the USA. Bangkok Suvarnabhumi (BKK) is the most practical connecting hub and is served frequently from Yangon by Myanmar National Airlines, Thai Airways, and Bangkok Airways. Singapore Changi (SIN) is another option. Chinese carriers via Guangzhou or Shanghai serve Yangon and can connect to some US destinations.

        For CDC compliance, confirm that the specific US destination airport has a CDC-registered animal care facility before booking. LAX, JFK, ORD, MIA, and IAD all qualify. Bangkok Airways and Thai Cargo serve the Yangon-Bangkok leg; confirm live animal acceptance on the Bangkok-to-US leg with the onward carrier.

        The political situation in Myanmar following the 2021 military coup has created uncertainty around official processes. Working with an experienced pet transport specialist who has current on-the-ground knowledge is advisable for this corridor.
