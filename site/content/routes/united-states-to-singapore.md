  sections:
    - heading: "What Category B means in practice"
      body: |
        Singapore's Animal and Veterinary Service classifies origin countries by rabies risk. The USA is Category B, one tier above the lowest-risk Category A countries such as the UK.

        Category B requires two things that Category A does not: a rabies antibody titre test before travel (with a result of at least 0.5 IU/ml from an approved laboratory), and a 30-day home quarantine after arrival. The home quarantine is significant but more comfortable than a facility stay: your pet stays at your registered Singapore address, and AVS may carry out inspections during the period.

        Plan the titre test early in your timeline. The test has specific timing requirements relative to vaccination and travel, and a result that does not meet the threshold will require re-testing and delay.

    - heading: "The 30-day home quarantine explained"
      body: |
        Unlike Australia's facility quarantine, Singapore's Category B quarantine for US pets is served at home. After your pet clears AVS inspection at Changi Airport, it travels to your registered Singapore address and remains there for 30 days.

        During this period, the pet must stay at the address and may not be taken out. AVS reserves the right to inspect to confirm compliance. The requirement exists as a precaution; provided your pet is healthy and documented, the 30 days pass without issue and the pet is then free to move normally.

        Confirm before travel that your Singapore housing is suitable: it must be a permitted address for the pet, and you must be able to keep the animal confined for the full 30 days.

  - question: "When does the rabies titre test need to be done?"
    answer: "The titre test must be done with the correct timing relative to the rabies vaccination and travel date. The blood draw is taken after vaccination, and the result must meet the 0.5 IU/ml minimum from an AVS-approved laboratory. Because the timing requirements are specific and a re-test causes delay, plan the titre test early, ideally as one of the first steps once you confirm the move. Your vet or agent can advise on the exact timing for your situation."
