  h1: "Pet Transport from United Kingdom to Seychelles: 2026 Guide"
  overview: |
    Every pet entering Seychelles goes into quarantine. That's not a consequence of missing paperwork, it's the law, and it applies to a UK dog with a clean rabies-free history just as much as it applies to an arrival from anywhere else. The only variable is length: 14 days at the sole facility on Mahe for pets from the UK, against up to 180 days for pets arriving from high-rabies countries.

    Get the paperwork right and this is a manageable, well-documented process. Get the quarantine booking wrong, and there's nowhere else on the islands to take a pet instead.

  sections:
    - heading: "Why quarantine applies even from a rabies-free country"
      body: |
        Seychelles protects its own rabies-free status by controlling what comes in, not by trusting what a certificate says about where an animal has been. The Border Control and Support Division, which now runs the veterinary import functions previously handled by the Seychelles Agricultural Agency, applies a blanket quarantine law to every arriving cat and dog. A UK pet gets the shorter 14-day version because the UK counts as rabies-free or rabies-controlled, but it still goes through the door of the Mahe facility like everything else.

    - heading: "One facility, one booking window"
      body: |
        Seychelles has a single quarantine facility, on Mahe, for the entire country. That single point of pressure means the booking has to go in at least 2 weeks before arrival and be reconfirmed 24 hours out. Miss that window and there's no second facility to fall back on, only a delayed departure while a new slot is found.

    - heading: "The titre test and the import permit, in the right order"
      body: |
        The Chief Veterinary Officer's import permit needs to be in hand before the export side of the paperwork gets too far along, since Seychelles won't accept an animal without one regardless of how clean the UK certificate is. The rabies titre test follows the same rule as everywhere else this site covers it: draw blood too soon after vaccination, less than 30 days, and the result isn't valid; leave it too long, past 6 months, and it has expired by the time the flight lands.
