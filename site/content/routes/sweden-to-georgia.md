    Sweden to Georgia is a well-defined route. Georgia accepts EU health certificates from Sweden without requiring a titre test, which keeps preparation under five weeks. The cargo routing via Istanbul is the most practical path from Stockholm Arlanda.

  sections:
    - heading: "What Georgia requires from Swedish pets"
      body: |
        Georgia accepts personal companion pets from EU countries including Sweden with a valid EU health certificate endorsed by Jordbruksverket, current microchip, and up-to-date rabies vaccination. No titre test is required.

        The National Food Agency (NFSA, nfa.gov.ge) inspects incoming pets at Tbilisi International Airport (TBS). Compliant pets are released without quarantine.

    - heading: "Cargo from Stockholm to Tbilisi"
      body: |
        Turkish Airlines is the most practical cargo option from Stockholm Arlanda (ARN) to Tbilisi (TBS). Daily flights connect ARN to Istanbul (IST), with Turkish Airlines Cargo onward to TBS.

        Finnair via Helsinki (HEL) is an alternative for some pets, with connections to Eastern European hubs that can then reach Tbilisi. LOT Polish Airlines via Warsaw (WAW) also provides a viable routing.

        For small pets, Wizz Air operates cabin pet acceptance on some Scandinavian routes. Direct or near-direct routes to Tbilisi on Wizz Air from Stockholm area airports may be available. Check weight limits and book the cabin pet space in advance.

    - heading: "Sweden's official vet process"
      body: |
        Sweden's export health certificate must be issued by a Jordbruksverket-authorised Officiell veterinär (OV). The OV conducts a clinical examination, prepares the EU-format certificate, and submits it for Jordbruksverket endorsement.

        Endorsement takes one to three working days. Plan the timing so the certificate (valid 10 days from examination) covers your full travel date including any transit time at the hub.
