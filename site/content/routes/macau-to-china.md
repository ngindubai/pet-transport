content:
  h1: "Macau to China: A Direct Flight, But Not a Simple Border"
  overview: |
    Forget the Hong Kong transfer every other long-haul Macau route on this site needs. Air Macau flies straight into Beijing, Shanghai, Guangzhou and a dozen other mainland cities, no bridge crossing, no ferry, no second airline booking. For once, the flight itself is the easy part.

    The border, despite "one country, two systems," is not a formality. Macau and mainland China run separate customs and animal-health systems, and GACC treats this as a real international pet movement: a health certificate in the correct format, likely no titre test given Macau's apparent designated status, but a mandatory quarantine of 30 days for a dog or 7 for a cat regardless, at the owner's cost, once the flight lands.

  sections:
    - heading: "Same country, different animal-health border"
      body: |
        It's tempting to assume that a Special Administrative Region moving a pet into the mainland is barely different from a domestic transfer. It isn't. Macau's Instituto para os Assuntos Municipais and the mainland's General Administration of Customs are separate authorities running separate rulebooks, and GACC's pet import requirements apply to a Macau-origin animal much as they would to one arriving from any other place outside mainland China's own customs territory.

        The one genuine advantage Macau has over most other origins on this site is the flight itself. Air Macau, China Southern and China Eastern all serve GACC's principal approved ports, Beijing, Shanghai and Guangzhou, directly from Macau International Airport.

    - heading: "A designated list found in secondary sources, not confirmed directly"
      body: |
        Several sources describe Macau as sitting on GACC's list of designated, lower-risk countries and territories, the same category that includes Hong Kong, Japan and Singapore, which would mean skipping the FAVN titre test most other origins need. This site's research could not load GACC's own primary listing directly during this pass, so treat the exemption as likely rather than settled, and confirm with GACC or a licensed agent before building a schedule around it.

        What doesn't change either way is the quarantine. Designated status affects the titre test, not the mandatory hold: 30 days for a dog, 7 for a cat, at a GACC-approved facility, paid entirely by the owner.

    - heading: "Beijing's and Shanghai's own breed rules"
      body: |
        GACC's national rules are only half the picture. Beijing bans many dog breeds over 35cm at the shoulder within its urban districts, and Shanghai runs its own separate restricted list. A dog cleared for national import can still be turned away, or simply become unregisterable, at the city level. Check the destination city's own municipal rules before assuming national compliance is enough.

    - heading: "A land crossing exists, but not for everyone yet"
      body: |
        There is, as of this research, a working land border crossing for pets between Macau and mainland China: Hengqin Port, which links Macau directly to the Guangdong-Macao In-Depth Cooperation Zone in Zhuhai. IAM's own president has confirmed that residents of the Macau New Neighbourhood development there can bring one dog or one cat across, microchip scanned at the border, with a quarantine certificate and rabies vaccination proof for dogs.

        That's a narrow, resident-specific allowance, not a general public route yet. IAM has said it is in talks with mainland authorities about wider pet border-crossing rules, but no date or scope for a broader rollout had been confirmed at the time of writing. For now, the air route through an approved GACC port remains the practical default for most owners.
