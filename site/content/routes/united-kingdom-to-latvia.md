  - question: "Does my pet need a titre test to travel from the UK to Latvia?"
    answer: "No. The United Kingdom is a listed third country under EU Regulation 576/2013. No titre test is required. You need a current rabies vaccination, ISO microchip, and a UK Animal Health Certificate in the correct EU format."
  - question: "Can I take my pet in the cabin on airBaltic from the UK to Riga?"
    answer: "Yes. airBaltic allows small cabin pets on UK-Riga routes. Weight limits apply (pet plus carrier typically up to 8kg). Confirm current airBaltic cabin pet policy and fees before booking."
  - question: "What AHC format does my UK vet need to issue for Latvia?"
    answer: "Your APHA-authorised Official Vet must issue the AHC in the EU format for listed third countries under EU Regulation 576/2013. This is specifically for pets moving from a listed non-EU country (UK) into the EU. Tell your OV the destination is Latvia so they issue the correct certificate type."
  - question: "My pet already has an EU pet passport. Can I use it for UK-to-Latvia travel?"
    answer: "An EU pet passport issued before 1 January 2021 relates to EU travel. Since your pet is now moving from the UK (a third country), a UK AHC is required. Speak to your APHA-authorised OV about what documentation is needed for UK-to-EU travel."
