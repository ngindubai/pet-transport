  sections:
    - heading: "Air Astana as a cargo option for the Almaty to Europe leg"
      body: |
        Air Astana, Kazakhstan's national carrier, operates scheduled services from Almaty (ALA) to Frankfurt (FRA) and Amsterdam (AMS). This makes it one of the few Caucasus-region airlines with direct European connections, and Air Astana Cargo handles live animals on some routes.

        For the Almaty-to-Vienna routing via Frankfurt, Air Astana covers the Almaty-FRA leg and Austrian Airlines or Lufthansa connects FRA-VIE. Frankfurt Airport's Animal Lounge provides temperature-controlled live animal holding during any transit at FRA.

        Before confirming this routing, contact Air Astana Cargo to verify live animal acceptance on the specific ALA-FRA flight you intend to book. Cargo policies can vary by flight and season. Summer heat at Almaty (July and August, when temperatures can exceed 30 degrees Celsius) may also trigger temperature embargo restrictions.

    - heading: "The Kazakhstan Committee for Veterinary Control AHC process"
      body: |
        The Animal Health Certificate for travel from Kazakhstan to Austria must be endorsed by the Committee for Veterinary Control and Supervision under Kazakhstan's Ministry of Agriculture. This is the competent authority for pet export documentation in Kazakhstan.

        Your vet in Almaty prepares the certificate in the EU format required by Regulation 2020/692 for unlisted third countries. The committee endorses the certificate, confirming that the vet is authorised and the certificate meets the required standard.

        The endorsement process in Almaty takes several working days. Book the vet appointment at least 2 weeks before your cargo date to ensure the endorsed certificate is ready before your pet is checked in. The AHC is valid for 10 days from issue.
