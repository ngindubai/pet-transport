content:
  h1: "Pet Transport from El Salvador to Australia"
  overview: |
    There is no direct route for a pet moving from El Salvador to Australia, because El Salvador does not sit in any of DAFF's approved country groups. That single classification changes everything: rather than following El Salvador's own export process straight into an Australian import, the pet has to relocate first, live for 180 consecutive days in an approved Group 2 or Group 3 country such as the UK, Canada, the USA or Singapore, and only then begin the standard Australian import sequence from that new country.

    Once that residency period is under way, the rest of the process is the familiar DAFF pathway: two rabies vaccinations, a titre test at a DAFF-approved laboratory, a BICON import permit, and a minimum 10-day stay at the Mickleham Post Entry Quarantine facility outside Melbourne on arrival. None of it is fast, and a realistic floor for the whole move, El Salvador to residency country to Australia, sits at around 40 to 52 weeks.

  sections:
    - heading: "Why El Salvador can't export directly to Australia"
      body: |
        DAFF places every country into one of four groups: Group 1 (New Zealand and a couple of nearby territories, no titre or quarantine), Group 2 (rabies-free countries, no titre but a 10-day quarantine), Group 3 (rabies-controlled countries such as the UK, USA, Canada and most of the EU, requiring the titre test and 180-day wait), and everyone else, classified non-approved. El Salvador falls into that last group.

        A pet from a non-approved country cannot be imported into Australia under any pathway that starts in that country. The only route in is via an approved country, and that means genuinely relocating, not just transiting.

    - heading: "The 180-day residency requirement, in practice"
      body: |
        Once the pet is living in a chosen approved country, whichever country that is sets its own rules for the vaccination and titre test timeline, on top of Australia's requirements. If the approved country is itself Group 3, the titre test and its own waiting period can often be timed to overlap with the 180-day residency clock, since both run from broadly similar milestones. Getting the sequencing wrong, say, drawing blood before the pet has properly settled, or before local vaccination requirements are met, risks the whole clock resetting.

        This is not a step to plan casually. An agent experienced with DAFF's non-approved country pathway is worth the fee here, mostly to avoid a restart on a 180-day wait that has already cost months.

    - heading: "Mickleham: the only door into the country"
      body: |
        Every pet entering Australia, regardless of the country it actually departs from, arrives in Melbourne and goes to the Mickleham Post Entry Quarantine facility. Minimum stay is 10 days, cost is the owner's, and space has to be booked in advance. Moving to Perth, Brisbane or Sydney afterwards doesn't change this first stop.

    - heading: "What this actually costs, once the residency leg is counted"
      body: |
        The Australia-side costs alone, titre test, BICON permit, quarantine and the final cargo booking, run into several thousand Australian dollars before living costs during the 180-day residency period are even added. Because that residency happens in a separate country with its own cost of living, there is no single meaningful total figure for this route the way there is for most others. Budget for two international pet cargo bookings and months of living arrangements in between, not one flight and one crate.
