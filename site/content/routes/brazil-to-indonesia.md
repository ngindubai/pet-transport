    - heading: "Comparing Brazil's quarantine requirements: Indonesia vs India vs Thailand"
      body: |
        For owners relocating from Brazil to Southeast Asia, the quarantine differences across destinations are significant.

        Indonesia requires the FAVN titre test, a BARANTAN permit, and 30-day facility quarantine. Total minimum preparation: 10-16 weeks.

        India requires an AQCS NOC and 15-day facility quarantine, but no titre test. Total minimum preparation: 6-10 weeks.

        Thailand requires a DLD import permit but has no routine quarantine. Total minimum preparation: 4-8 weeks.

        These differences matter when choosing where to base in Southeast Asia. Indonesia has the most complex process; Thailand has the least demanding.

    - heading: "FAVN test from Brazil: where to get it done"
      body: |
        The FAVN (Fluorescent Antibody Virus Neutralisation) test must be done at a BARANTAN-approved laboratory. Brazil has approved laboratories in major cities. Your MAPA-authorised vet can advise on which labs are on the current BARANTAN approved list.

        Blood is drawn at your vet in Brazil, then sent to the laboratory. Results typically take 10-14 days. The blood draw must happen at least 30 days after the rabies vaccination. Once you have the result (minimum 0.5 IU/ml), count at least 30 days before your Indonesia arrival date.
