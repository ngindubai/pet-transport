  overview: |
    Moving a pet from Brazil to Indonesia requires prior import permit from BARANTAN (the Indonesian Agriculture Quarantine Agency), a MAPA-endorsed health certificate, and a minimum 14-day quarantine on arrival. Bali has additional restrictions as a rabies-free island. Emirates via Dubai and Singapore Airlines via Singapore are the main cargo routing options for this long-haul corridor. Use a specialist agent familiar with both Brazilian and Indonesian regulations.

        Emirates via Dubai is often the most practical option for Brazil-to-Indonesia pet cargo given the frequency of both legs. Singapore Airlines via Singapore provides excellent Jakarta and Bali connections but requires an additional intermediate connection from Brazil to Singapore.

    - heading: "BARANTAN import permit and Indonesian quarantine"
      body: |
        BARANTAN (Badan Karantina Pertanian, the Agriculture Quarantine Agency) is Indonesia's authority for animal imports. An import permit must be obtained from BARANTAN before the pet arrives in Indonesia. The permit specifies the entry airport and any conditions.

        All pets arriving in Indonesia must undergo a minimum quarantine period (14 days as of 2026) at a BARANTAN-approved quarantine facility. Costs vary. Pets entering Bali via Ngurah Rai Airport (DPS) may face additional requirements given Bali's declared rabies-free status.

        Contact BARANTAN (pertanian.go.id) for the current import permit application procedure and the health certificate format required from Brazil.
