  h1: "Pet Transport from Guyana to Canada"
  overview: |
    Caribbean Airlines has flown Georgetown to Toronto nonstop every single day since 1 July 2026, a genuine step change from the limited schedule that came before it. Barely two and a half weeks old at the time of writing, it's now the obvious, and arguably the only sensible, way to fly a dog on this route.

    Guyana's own GLDA export process is fast: a G$5,000 certificate, four working days, done. CFIA's side is the one with more moving parts, because Guyana sits on its high-risk-for-dog-rabies list. That means a 7-month minimum age for the dog and entry restricted to five specific Canadian airports, Toronto among them, which happens to be exactly where the new daily flight lands.

  sections:
    - heading: "A brand new daily flight, and why it matters for dogs specifically"
      body: |
        BW616 and BW617 replaced Caribbean Airlines' old, sparser Georgetown-Toronto timetable on 1 July 2026, operated jointly with Air Transat. That's good news generally, and genuinely important for anyone moving a dog: American Airlines' Miami and JFK options are effectively closed to dogs from Guyana, since American Airlines Cargo won't accept a new dog booking from a country on the CDC's high-risk rabies list, and Guyana is on it.

        Cats face no such restriction and can still route through a US gateway if that ever suits better. For dogs, Caribbean Airlines' new nonstop isn't just convenient, it's close to the only workable option.

    - heading: "Why the CFIA side, not the GLDA side, sets the pace"
      body: |
        GLDA's own export certificate is about as quick as this site sees anywhere: a vet inspection, a G$5,000 fee, four working days. The GLDA process doesn't hold this route up.

        CFIA does, a little. Guyana's place on the high-risk list means the dog has to be at least 7 months old on arrival, the microchip has to go in before the vaccine, an import permit needs applying for in advance, and the pet can only clear at five specific Canadian airports. None of that is a titre test, notably, unlike the equivalent US process for the same list of countries; it's procedural weight rather than a blood draw.

    - heading: "Keeping the two clocks straight"
      body: |
        The GLDA certificate and the CFIA import permit run on entirely separate timelines, and it's worth tracking them separately rather than assuming one process covers both. Get the CFIA permit moving as soon as the vaccination and age are settled, and leave the GLDA certificate until closer to the confirmed flight date, since it's designed to be quick.
