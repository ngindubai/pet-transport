content:
  h1: "Pet Transport from France to Iceland"
  overview: |
    Matvaelastofnun, Iceland's food and veterinary authority, does not care that your cat already carries a French-issued EU pet passport. France sits on MAST's Category 1 list, which shortens the titre test wait considerably against a non-EU country, but the mandatory 14-day quarantine on arrival applies to every pet regardless of where it started.

    Two clocks run on this route: the paperwork clock before you fly, and the fixed two weeks your pet spends at an approved isolation station once it lands. Both matter for planning, and the isolation station needs booking well before you finalise a flight date.

  sections:
    - heading: "What Category 1 status actually buys you"
      body: |
        France's place on MAST's Category 1 list affects the pre-export timeline only. The titre test blood draw must happen at least 30 days after vaccination, and once the lab confirms 0.5 IU/ml or above, there's no additional fixed wait before travel. A Category 2 country faces a further 90-day wait counted from the blood draw date instead.

        What Category 1 status does not do is shorten or waive the quarantine. Every dog and cat entering Iceland spends a minimum of 14 days at Einangrunarstoedin i Reykjanesbae or Einangrunarstoedin Mosel, French or otherwise. This is a genuine biosecurity measure protecting Iceland's disease-free island status, not an administrative penalty, and it catches out owners who assume EU freedom of movement rules apply the way they would moving between, say, France and Belgium.

    - heading: "France has no direct flight to Iceland on Air France"
      body: |
        Icelandair is currently the only airline flying Paris CDG to Keflavik nonstop, roughly 8 times a week. Air France's own services to Iceland route through a stopover rather than flying direct, a genuine difference from most of France's other EU-adjacent routes on this site.

        Since 1 November 2024, Icelandair no longer accepts pets as checked or excess baggage on any route, including this one. A pet now travels as manifested cargo, booked through Icelandair Cargo rather than at the ordinary passenger check-in desk. If that timeline does not work, routing via Frankfurt on Lufthansa, which flies FRA to KEF direct and operates its own Frankfurt Animal Lounge for live animal handling, is a workable alternative, though it adds a connection.

    - heading: "The isolation stations, and why booking early matters"
      body: |
        Iceland runs exactly two approved facilities for incoming pets: Einangrunarstoedin i Reykjanesbae and Einangrunarstoedin Mosel. Both take in pets on a limited schedule, not on demand, so a booking made after your flight and permit are locked in can leave you with nowhere for your pet to go on arrival.

        Fees run to roughly ISK 220,000 for a dog's full 14-day stay, around EUR 1,450, and somewhat less for a cat. That covers housing, feeding, general care and the veterinary checks the station carries out during the stay. Confirm space at your preferred station before you commit to a flight date, not after.
