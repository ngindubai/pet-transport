        KLM Cargo, routing through Nairobi and Amsterdam, is a strong first option, drawing on the established KLM/Kenya Airways cargo link out of East Africa with a short final leg into Copenhagen.     - heading: "The unlisted-country sequence, in the order it has to happen"
      body: |
        Denmark is an EU member state, but that does not exempt pets arriving from Rwanda, an EU-unlisted third country, from the full sequence set out in EU Regulation 2020/692. Chip first, then vaccination, then a wait before blood can legally be drawn for the titre test. The result must clear 0.5 IU/ml at an EU-approved laboratory. Only once that result is confirmed does the real wait begin: 90 days from the date blood was drawn. Fodevarestyrelsen inspects documentation on compliant arrivals; anything short of the full paper trail risks detention. There's no quarantine track for animals that arrive with the paperwork in order, but that is exactly why the paperwork has to be right the first time.

    - heading: "Rwanda's side of the process"
      body: |
        No export permit is needed for a personal pet leaving Kigali. What matters is the EU-format health certificate, issued by an RAB-authorised vet and endorsed by the Rwanda Agriculture and Animal Resources Development Board shortly before departure. Keep the 10-day validity window in mind against your actual flight date, including any layover in Brussels, Amsterdam or Addis Ababa, so the certificate is still current when Danish officials check it on arrival.

faqs:
  - question: "Can I skip the titre test because Denmark is inside the EU?"
    answer: "No. The titre test requirement is triggered by Rwanda's status as an EU-unlisted third country, not by which EU state you're travelling to. Denmark applies the same EU Regulation 2020/692 sequence as every other member state."
  - question: "How long is the wait after the titre test blood draw?"
    answer: "90 days, counted from the day blood was drawn, not from the day the laboratory sends back the result. Plan around the draw date, and treat the result date as a separate, earlier milestone."
  - question: "What if my dog's vaccination happened before the microchip was implanted?"
    answer: "It won't count. Denmark, like the rest of the EU, requires the chip to be in place before the rabies vaccination used for the titre sequence. A vaccination given first means a new, post-chip vaccination is needed, and the 30-day and 90-day waits both start again."
  - question: "What happens if my pet arrives in Copenhagen without full documentation?"
    answer: "Non-compliant arrivals can be detained or returned to the country of origin at the owner's expense. Denmark does not offer an informal route around missing or incomplete paperwork, so confirm every document before booking the flight."
