content:
  h1: "Hong Kong to Macau: The Pet Move That Doesn't Need an Airline"
  overview: |
    Every other Macau route on this site starts with the same problem: no long-haul cargo capacity at Macau International Airport, so a Cathay Pacific or Qantas Freight sector into Hong Kong, followed by a road or ferry transfer. Hong Kong-to-Macau skips that whole structure. The pet is already in Hong Kong. The trip is the ferry itself, or the Hong Kong-Zhuhai-Macau Bridge, not a flight.

    There's a second, quieter advantage here. IAM's rabies titre test defaults to mandatory unless a pet comes from a place with no rabies incidents in the past two years. Hong Kong has had no animal rabies case since 1987 and carries World Organisation for Animal Health rabies-free recognition, a stronger position than Australia, the UK or the UAE hold on their own Macau route pages on this site, none of which get the exemption.

  sections:
    - heading: "A ferry ticket, not a cargo booking"
      body: |
        Cotai Water Jet and TurboJet both run the Hong Kong-Macau crossing with small pets permitted in an approved carrier, up to roughly 20kg. Owners who would rather not sit with their pet in the shared cabin can book a private room on the Cotai service for around HKD 2,200. The Hong Kong-Zhuhai-Macau Bridge offers a coach alternative, roughly an hour door to door, though pet policies here vary more by operator than the ferry routes do.

        Whichever way you cross, the Macau import approval still has to be sorted before you travel; a ferry ticket doesn't replace the IAM licence.

    - heading: "Why Hong Kong likely skips the titre test"
      body: |
        IAM's baseline rule treats the rabies antibody titre test as the default requirement, waived only for pets arriving from a country or territory with no rabies incidents in the past two years. Hong Kong's last animal rabies case was recorded in 1987, and the territory holds World Organisation for Animal Health recognition as rabies-free. That's about as clean a rabies record as any origin on this site.

        Treat this as a strong likelihood rather than a guaranteed exemption confirmed name-by-name in a published IAM list. Check current status with IAM directly before relying on it, particularly if your pet's paperwork will be prepared close to the travel date.

    - heading: "The breeds Macau won't let in"
      body: |
        IAM's import rules bar five breeds and their crosses outright: Tibetan Mastiff, Fila Braziliero, Dogo Argentino, Pit Bull Terrier and Japanese Tosa. This is separate from anything to do with vaccination or titre status; it applies regardless of how clean the rest of the paperwork is. Check this before applying for the PS-1092 licence, not after.
