  h1: "Pet Transport from El Salvador to the United Kingdom"
  overview: |
    San Salvador has no direct passenger or cargo service to London, and the CDC's high-risk rabies classification for El Salvador (in force since July 2021) has pushed at least one major US carrier to stop accepting dog cargo bookings from this origin. That single fact reshapes the whole route: dogs and cats end up on different practical paths, and routing decisions have to be checked airline by airline rather than assumed.

    The good news is that El Salvador's own export process is quick and cheap. MAG (the Ministerio de Agricultura y Ganaderia) issues its zoosanitary export certificate for USD 16.95 in three to five working days. The slow part is entirely on the UK side: the non-listed country pathway, with its titre test and fixed three-month wait, sets the real timeline.
  sections:
    - heading: "Why the UK side of this route takes months, not weeks"
      body: |
        El Salvador does not appear on Great Britain's list of low-rabies-risk countries, so the full non-listed pathway applies. Microchip first, then rabies vaccination, then a blood sample for the titre test at least 30 days after vaccination. The result has to reach at least 0.5 IU/ml at an EU or GB approved laboratory. Only then does the clock start on a fixed three calendar month wait, counted from the date the blood was drawn, not from when the lab result comes back.

        Dogs need a praziquantel tapeworm treatment 24 to 120 hours before check-in; cats are exempt. Because a relocated pet almost always travels as manifest cargo rather than tucked under an owner's seat, the move counts as commercial and needs an APHA import licence on top of the standard paperwork. Source: UK APHA, gov.uk/bring-pet-to-great-britain, checked 13 July 2026.
    - heading: "The routing problem: no direct flight, and a rabies-origin complication for dogs"
      body: |
        San Salvador (SAL) connects to London only via a hub. Historically that has meant Miami on American Airlines, or Houston, with a same-carrier or interline leg on to Heathrow. Avianca, whose predecessor TACA was headquartered at SAL, launched a seasonal San Salvador-Madrid passenger service on 15 June 2026 using a wet-leased Wamos Air Airbus A330, four times a week. It is a genuinely new option, but nothing confirms yet that it carries manifest cargo pets, and it only runs through the summer season.

        The complication is specific to dogs. Because the CDC classifies El Salvador as high-risk for canine rabies, American Airlines Cargo currently embargoes new cargo bookings for dogs originating there, and any itinerary that transits the US brings CDC rules into play regardless of the final destination. Cats are not affected by this classification, since it addresses the canine rabies variant specifically. An IPATA-registered agent with Central America experience is worth the fee here, simply to confirm which carrier will actually take the booking on the date you need.
    - heading: "El Salvador's export process: straightforward if you get the timing right"
      body: |
        MAG's Quarantine Division, based in Santa Tecla, handles the Certificado Zoosanitario de Exportacion de Mascotas. You need a private vet health certificate no older than 15 days, a complete vaccination record, and the MAG application form. The fee is USD 16.95 per animal. MAG recommends completing this three to five working days before travel, and a final clearance stamp is applied by quarantine inspectors at the point of departure, not at the MAG office itself.

        This part of the process is genuinely simple compared with the UK side. Where it can go wrong is sequencing: because the MAG certificate has to be current, it should be timed to land within days of departure, well after the UK's titre test and three-month wait have already been satisfied.
