content:
  h1: "Pet Transport from Iceland to New Zealand"
  overview: |
    Germany's route to New Zealand, already covered on this site, runs 36 to 44 weeks because MPI treats most of Europe as a country needing the full FAVN titre test and a 180-day wait after the blood draw. Iceland does not face that. MPI keeps a separate list of countries it already recognises as rabies-free, the same group as Japan, Singapore and Hawaii, and Iceland sits on it.

    That single classification shaves months off the timeline. What it does not remove is the import permit, the parasite treatments, or the minimum 10 days a pet spends at an MPI-approved facility once it lands at Auckland or Christchurch. Nor does it solve the flight problem: nothing leaves Keflavik for New Zealand without at least one stop.

  sections:
    - heading: "Why Iceland skips the test most of Europe cannot avoid"
      body: |
        MPI's biosecurity system separates countries by genuine rabies risk rather than simple continental grouping. Iceland's own import rules, the mandatory 14-day quarantine on every arriving pet regardless of origin, have kept the country rabies-free for decades, and MPI's own list reflects that: Iceland sits alongside Japan, Singapore and Hawaii as a recognised rabies-free origin, not with the bulk of the EU.

        The practical effect is that the FAVN blood test, and the 180-day wait tied to it, simply does not apply here. A pet can be flight-ready in a fraction of the time a German or British pet needs for the same New Zealand move.

    - heading: "The vaccination question nobody expects"
      body: |
        Here is the twist. Because Iceland relies on border quarantine rather than domestic vaccination to stay rabies-free, plenty of Icelandic dogs and cats have simply never had a rabies shot. MPI's certificate for recognised rabies-free countries generally still expects proof of current vaccination, even without the titre test behind it. An owner assuming their pet is "already covered" because Iceland has no rabies at all can be caught out here. Raise this directly with the vet at the first appointment, since starting a vaccination course from scratch adds real weeks to the schedule.

    - heading: "Quarantine and the permit still apply in full"
      body: |
        Skipping the FAVN test does not mean skipping New Zealand's border controls. Every arriving pet, recognised-country or not, needs an MPI import permit, applied for online with roughly six weeks of processing time, plus two topical tick and flea treatments before departure. On arrival, a minimum 10-day stay at an MPI-approved facility near Auckland or Christchurch is fixed, whatever the paperwork says.

        Book the quarantine slot early. Space is limited, and it should not be treated as an afterthought once the rest of the process clears.
