content:
  h1: "Pet Transport from Botswana to Canada"
  overview: |
    There's no single flight that gets a Botswana pet to Canada. Every booking runs through Ethiopian Airlines' Addis Ababa hub, roughly seven connections a week from Gaborone, a genuinely different routing from the Johannesburg-to-Heathrow path Botswana's UK-bound pets typically take.

    The paperwork side runs on two parallel tracks rather than one. Botswana's DVS issues its own standalone export permit, while CFIA, because Botswana sits on its high-risk-for-dog-rabies list, requires a 7-month minimum age, a mandatory microchip-before-vaccination rule and its own import permit. Neither authority's process substitutes for the other.

  sections:
    - heading: "Why the routing looks nothing like the UK path"
      body: |
        Owners who've researched Botswana's route to the UK often expect a Johannesburg connection and stop there. Canada doesn't work that way. Ethiopian Airlines' Addis Ababa hub is the only realistic long-haul link from Gaborone to Toronto, roughly 7 times weekly, since neither Air Canada nor any Star Alliance partner flies a Johannesburg-to-Toronto sector.

        That's a longer routing in practice, and a different agent conversation. Confirm cargo acceptance on both the Gaborone-to-Addis Ababa leg and the Addis Ababa-to-Toronto leg separately; a single interline booking doesn't automatically guarantee both carriers handle a live animal the same way.

    - heading: "Two permits, not one"
      body: |
        CFIA's high-risk pathway doesn't ask for a titre test the way some owners expect, having researched the CDC's mirror process for the US. What it does ask for is stricter than the default: a 7-month minimum age, a chip implanted before the vaccine, its own import permit, and entry limited to five Canadian airports.

        None of that replaces Botswana's own side. DVS still issues a standalone export permit, separate from the health certificate, typically inside 3 to 5 working days once the paperwork's complete. Run both applications at the same time rather than waiting for one to finish before starting the other.

    - heading: "Budgeting for a longer route"
      body: |
        With no direct flight and two separate permits to manage, this sits among the more involved Southern Africa routes this site covers. An IPATA-registered agent experienced in the Addis Ababa handover is worth the fee here, since coordinating a live animal transfer between two long-haul legs isn't something to leave to a standard booking.
