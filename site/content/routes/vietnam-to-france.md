  h1: "Pet Transport from Vietnam to France: EU Titre Test and MARD Certificate"
  overview: |
    France applies EU-wide pet travel rules for third-country imports. Vietnam is not on the EU Annex II approved third-country list, so pets from Vietnam require an FAVN or ELISA titre test at an EU-approved laboratory and a mandatory 90-day wait from the blood draw date before EU entry. The Vietnam export process is straightforward: no separate export permit, just a health certificate in EU AHC format from a MARD-authorised vet, endorsed by the Department of Animal Health (DAH). Total preparation: 20-28 weeks. Source: EU pet travel regulations, ec.europa.eu.

    One routing advantage for France over other EU destinations: Air France operates direct cargo services from both Ho Chi Minh City (SGN) and Hanoi (HAN) to Paris Charles de Gaulle (CDG). This avoids the hub connection complexity required for Spain (which has no direct cargo from Vietnam) or Italy (typically needs a CDG or FRA hub). Vietnam Airlines also operates to CDG.

    For owners moving from Vietnam to other EU destinations (Germany, Netherlands, Italy, Spain), the process is identical in terms of requirements. The difference is routing and the first EU entry airport.

  sections:
    - heading: "EU import requirements for pets from Vietnam: what France applies"
      body: |
        The EU framework applies uniformly across all member states for pets from unlisted third countries like Vietnam. The process: ISO microchip (implanted before or same day as first vaccination); rabies vaccination primary course (21-day wait from initial vaccination); FAVN or ELISA titre test at an EU-approved laboratory (result 0.5 IU/ml or above); blood draw at least 30 days after final vaccination; mandatory 90-day wait from blood draw date; EU-format AHC from MARD-authorised official vet in Vietnam, endorsed by DAH, issued within 10 days of EU entry. No quarantine on arrival for compliant pets.

        France does not add requirements beyond the EU standard for pets from Vietnam. There are no French federal breed bans comparable to Germany's four-breed restriction. However, French municipalities and departments may have local breed classification rules for dogs classified as "chiens dangereux" (dangerous dogs). Check locally for your specific destination in France if your dog is a breed that might fall under these classifications.

    - heading: "Air France direct cargo: Vietnam's route advantage to France"
      body: |
        The most direct routing for Vietnam-to-France live animal cargo is Air France from Ho Chi Minh City (SGN) or Hanoi (HAN) directly to Paris Charles de Gaulle (CDG). This avoids the transit documentation requirements of hub airports such as Dubai (Emirates), Frankfurt (Lufthansa), or Hong Kong (Cathay Pacific). Vietnam Airlines also operates CDG services. CDG is the principal French entry airport and the most commonly used for live animal imports from Vietnam.

        For owners departing from cities other than Ho Chi Minh City or Hanoi, connections via SGN or HAN are usually necessary before taking the direct European service. If using a hub routing (via Dubai or Frankfurt), note that the EU entry point shifts to the hub country, which affects which AHC documentation is prepared.

    - heading: "France versus other EU destinations from Vietnam"
      body: |
        All five major EU destination routes from Vietnam (Germany, France, Netherlands, Spain, Italy) have identical requirements: EU-unlisted country titre test process, MARD health certificate, 90-day wait. Differences are in routing. Germany (Frankfurt) has Lufthansa connections from Vietnam. France (Paris CDG) benefits from Air France direct services. The Netherlands (Amsterdam AMS) has KLM direct connections from SGN. Italy and Spain have no direct cargo services from Vietnam and require hub connections.

        For owners choosing between France and Germany, the practical documentation difference is the EU entry airport (CDG versus FRA), which determines where the AHC is presented on arrival. Both are well-served routes from Vietnam. France has a routing advantage over Spain, which has no direct Vietnam-to-Spain cargo and requires a hub at CDG, AMS, or FRA.
