    - heading: "Routing options for UK-to-Romania pet cargo"
      body: |
        Bucharest Henri Coanda International Airport (OTP) is Romania's main international hub. The UK-to-Romania route does not have the same frequency of direct pet cargo services as busier corridors like London-Frankfurt or London-Paris. TAROM, Romania's national airline, is the most straightforward option for the LHR-OTP routing, and TAROM Cargo accepts pets on this service.

        If TAROM cargo space is not available on your preferred dates, hub alternatives are practical. Lufthansa via Frankfurt (FRA) or Munich (MUC) connects to Bucharest on a regular schedule, and the FRA-OTP or MUC-OTP legs are well served. Austrian Airlines via Vienna (VIE) is another option within the Lufthansa Group network.

        For any multi-leg routing, confirm that pet cargo acceptance applies to both the Heathrow departure leg and the onward connection. Some airlines handle cargo differently on regional European legs.

    - heading: "ANSVSA and what happens at Bucharest Airport"
      body: |
        Romania's National Sanitary Veterinary and Food Safety Authority (ANSVSA) manages veterinary border controls at OTP. On arrival, ANSVSA inspectors check the AHC, microchip, and vaccination records. For pets arriving from the UK with complete documentation, the process is a document review and a brief physical check.

        No quarantine applies for UK pets with a valid AHC and current vaccination record. Pets are released after the ANSVSA check.

        Romania has a large expatriate community, and Bucharest has established veterinary facilities for ongoing care after your pet arrives. ANSVSA can advise on local registration requirements for imported pets.

  - question: "Can I use my UK pet passport to take my dog or cat to Romania?"
    answer: "No. UK pet passports issued in Great Britain (England, Scotland, Wales) are not valid for EU entry since January 2021. You need an Animal Health Certificate (AHC) issued by an APHA-authorised official vet within 10 days of travel. Source: UK government, gov.uk/taking-pet-abroad."
  - question: "Does my pet need a titre test to enter Romania from the UK?"
    answer: "No. Romania applies EU regulations, and the UK is listed as an approved third country. No titre test is required for personal pets from the UK. You need the AHC, a current ISO microchip, and a valid rabies vaccination with the primary course completed at least 21 days before travel."
  - question: "Which airlines fly pets from the UK to Bucharest?"
    answer: "TAROM (Romania's national carrier) operates from London Heathrow to Bucharest (OTP) and accepts pets as manifested cargo. Lufthansa Cargo via Frankfurt or Munich is an alternative. Austrian Airlines via Vienna also connects to Bucharest. Air France-KLM via Paris or Amsterdam is another option. Confirm cargo pet acceptance on the specific routing before booking."
  - question: "Are there breed restrictions in Romania?"
    answer: "Yes. Romania has breed-specific legislation administered by ANSVSA covering breeds classified as dangerous, including Pit Bull-type dogs. If your dog's breed might be on the restricted list, confirm with ANSVSA (ansvsa.ro) before starting the move."
  - question: "Is there quarantine for pets arriving from the UK in Romania?"
    answer: "No routine quarantine for dogs and cats from the UK with correct documentation. Romania follows EU pet travel rules. ANSVSA conducts a document and health check on arrival. Pets with complete, correct paperwork are released after inspection."
  - question: "How long does the UK-to-Romania pet move process take?"
    answer: "Two to four weeks is typically enough. The key steps are checking vaccination and microchip records, booking cargo space to Bucharest (noting limited direct options), and getting the AHC issued within 10 days of travel. Starting a few weeks early gives time to arrange a vet booster if needed and to confirm cargo availability on your preferred routing."
