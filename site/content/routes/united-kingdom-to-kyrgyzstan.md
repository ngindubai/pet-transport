  overview: |
    No rabies titre test. That is the single fact that separates this route from most of the unlisted-country moves on this site, and from the reverse direction of this exact corridor: a Kyrgyzstan-origin pet entering the UK needs the full blood test and a 3-month wait, but a UK pet entering Kyrgyzstan needs neither.

    What's left is more straightforward: a rabies vaccination timed to a specific window, an APHA-issued Export Health Certificate, and a flight that does not exist as a direct service. Turkish Airlines via Istanbul and Air Astana via Almaty are the two realistic options, both running limited weekly capacity into Bishkek.

    Budget six to nine weeks from the first vet visit to travel day. The tightest single constraint is Kyrgyzstan's own health certificate window, which expires faster than most owners expect.
  sections:
    - heading: "Why there is no titre test on this leg"
      body: |
        Kyrgyzstan's import rules do not ask for a rabies antibody titre test from a pet arriving from any country, the UK included. This puts the outbound direction of this corridor in a smaller and less demanding category than the return trip: a Kyrgyzstan-origin pet flying into Great Britain has to clear the UK's full unlisted-country protocol, complete with a blood draw, an approved laboratory and a fixed three-month wait.

        None of that applies here. A current rabies vaccination, given between 21 days and 11 months before entry, plus a government-endorsed health certificate, covers the medical side. Microchipping is not mandated by Kyrgyzstan either, though fitting one before any vaccination is still sound practice and keeps documentation consistent if the pet ever needs to travel again.

        Source: State Inspectorate for Veterinary and Phytosanitary Safety of the Kyrgyz Republic, Bishkek, and USDA APHIS country guidance for the same import framework.
    - heading: "The certificate window is shorter than you'd expect"
      body: |
        APHA's standard Export Health Certificate is usually built around a 10-day validity window between the vet exam and arrival. Kyrgyzstan asks for something tighter: the certificate has to be issued within 5 days of travel.

        That means the OV appointment cannot be booked to APHA's usual rhythm. It needs to sit inside the narrower Kyrgyz window, which in practice means confirming the flight first and working backwards from it, rather than getting the paperwork done early and hoping the travel date lines up.
    - heading: "Getting from Heathrow to Bishkek"
      body: |
        Search flights to Bishkek and the routing options thin out quickly. Turkish Airlines' Heathrow-Istanbul-Manas connection is the default choice most agents use, backed by an established AVIH cargo programme. Air Astana offers a genuine alternative: a nonstop Heathrow-Almaty service on its long-range A321LR, followed by a short regional hop on to Bishkek.

        British Airways does not fly this route at all, though its cargo partner PetAir UK can handle the UK-side export paperwork for owners who want to arrange departure through IAG Cargo before connecting onward separately. Aeroflot, which historically served Bishkek, is off the table: sanctions on Russian carriers since Russia's invasion of Ukraine remain in force across EU and UK airspace.
