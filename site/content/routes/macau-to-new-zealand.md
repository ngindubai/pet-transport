content:
  h1: "Pet Transport from Macau to New Zealand"
  overview: |
    New Zealand sorts approved countries into three risk categories, and Macau sits in Category 3, rabies absent or well controlled, alongside the Netherlands and Switzerland. That's the middle tier, not the easiest one. Only Australia, MPI's single Category 1 country, skips New Zealand's mandatory quarantine altogether.

    For everyone else, including Macau, the path runs through a titre test with a genuine 3-month minimum wait, an MPI import permit, and 10 days at an approved quarantine facility once the pet actually lands. Add the mandatory Hong Kong transfer that every long-haul Macau route needs, and this becomes one of the longer corridors this site covers.

  sections:
    - heading: "Category 3, not Category 1: what that actually costs you"
      body: |
        MPI's three-tier system rewards Australia specifically, not the broader idea of a low-rabies-risk country. Category 3 covers a long list of otherwise very safe origins, this site's own Netherlands and Switzerland Macau routes sit in the same tier, and every one of them still needs the FAVN or RFFIT titre test plus 10 days of quarantine on arrival.

        The test itself has an unusual shape compared with the EU-style routes elsewhere on this site: the blood sample has to be collected not less than 3 months, and not more than 24 months, before the shipment date, giving a wider window than the fixed 90-day EU wait, but still a real floor that can't be rushed.

    - heading: "Auckland only, and a booking that starts with the quarantine slot"
      body: |
        Every pet lands at Auckland Airport, New Zealand's dedicated port for animal quarantine intake, whatever part of the country you're actually moving to. Book the 10-day facility slot early. MPI capacity is finite, and turning up with a confirmed flight but no quarantine booking is a real way to lose weeks at the worst possible point in the process.

        Macau's own airport still has no long-haul cargo capacity, so the road or ferry transfer to Hong Kong International Airport comes first, before Cathay Pacific's direct 11-hour sector to Auckland, or a Singapore Airlines routing via Changi if that capacity is tighter.
