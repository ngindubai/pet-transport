  sections:
    - heading: "Starting both processes at the same time"
      body: |
        The mistake to avoid on this route is treating the Australian and Azerbaijani processes sequentially. They should run in parallel. Apply for Azerbaijan's veterinary import permit from the State Veterinary Service at the same time as you start your DAFF export permit application in Australia.

        DAFF processes take eight to ten weeks. The Azerbaijan import permit can also take several weeks. If you wait for one before starting the other, you extend the preparation timeline unnecessarily.

        A pet transport agent experienced on Australia-to-Caucasus routes can manage both applications simultaneously, monitoring progress on each and ensuring the documentation from each side aligns. For most owners, the investment in an agent on this particular route is worthwhile.

    - heading: "DAFF export from Australia: what it involves"
      body: |
        Australia's Department of Agriculture, Fisheries and Forestry (DAFF) controls the export of companion animals as part of its biosecurity framework. You need an export permit specific to Azerbaijan, which you apply for through agriculture.gov.au. DAFF will tell you exactly what health conditions and documentation your pet must meet for Azerbaijan's entry.

        A DAFF-accredited vet then conducts the pre-export health examination and issues the health certificate. The certificate must accurately reflect the documentation Azerbaijan requires. If there is any mismatch, DAFF may require amendments.

        Your pet must appear visibly healthy at the time of the pre-export inspection. This is not a routine check that can be passed easily with a sick animal. Start the process early to give yourself time to deal with any queries DAFF raises.

    - heading: "Dubai to Baku: a reliable final leg"
      body: |
        Emirates operates from Sydney and Melbourne to Dubai (DXB), and from Dubai there is a connection to Baku's Heydar Aliyev International Airport (GYD). Emirates SkyCargo has established live animal cargo procedures on the long-haul Australian legs, and the DXB-GYD segment is a manageable final stretch.

        Turkish Airlines via Istanbul is an alternative, with the Australian leg typically routed via Singapore or Dubai to IST, then IST to GYD. Turkish Airlines Cargo covers the IST-GYD corridor regularly.

        Qatar Airways Cargo via Doha (DOH) can also route to GYD. Confirm the DOH-GYD connection availability at the time of booking, as this depends on current schedule and cargo space.

        The total journey from Australia to Baku is typically 20 to 26 hours with connections. Present all documents at GYD on arrival: import permit, DAFF health certificate, microchip documentation, and vaccination record. The State Veterinary Service inspection team will clear compliant arrivals without quarantine.
