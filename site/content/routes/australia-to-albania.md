  h1: "Pet Transport from Australia to Albania"
  overview: |
    Turkish Airlines added something to this route in 2026 that didn't exist before: a single-carrier path from Istanbul to both Melbourne and Sydney, which means a pet can now travel Tirana to an Australian city changing planes just once, on one airline's cargo network, using the same Pet Lounge Turkish already runs for connecting animals at Istanbul.

    The regulatory side hasn't changed at all, and that's good news. Australia sits on the EU's approved list of low-rabies-risk countries, the same list that spares UK pets a blood test, so Albania waives the titre test here too. What's left is the familiar shape: microchip, a 21-day wait after vaccination, and a DAFF certificate rather than a passport.

  sections:
    - heading: "Does an Australian pet get the same break as a UK pet?"
      body: |
        Essentially, yes. Albania's import rules, closely aligned with the EU model even though Albania isn't a member state, waive the rabies titre test for any pet whose country of origin sits on the EU's own approved third-country table. Australia is on that table, in the same section as the UK, so the practical requirements land in the same place: no blood test, just a current vaccination at least 21 days old and a properly endorsed certificate.

        It's not a guarantee stamped into Albanian law forever. The EU reviews that list from time to time, so a quick check with AKU before booking anything costs nothing and rules out an unlikely surprise.

    - heading: "What changed with Turkish Airlines' new Australian routes?"
      body: |
        Before 2026, an Australia-Albania pet move meant transiting through a European hub on one airline, then handing off to a different carrier for the Tirana leg. Turkish Airlines' expansion changes that. Istanbul to Melbourne runs six times a week via a Singapore stop, and Istanbul to Sydney runs four times a week via Kuala Lumpur, with a nonstop Sydney service planned once the airline's first Airbus A350-1000 arrives later in 2026.

        Neither route is nonstop yet, so build the technical stop into your planning rather than expecting a single unbroken flight. But it does mean one airline can now carry the animal the entire way from an Australian departure city through to Tirana, which simplifies the handover risk that used to sit at the Istanbul changeover.

    - heading: "What does the paperwork actually involve?"
      body: |
        Start with the DAFF Notice of Intention to Export Live Animals, lodged at least 10 working days before departure, which books the pre-export vet appointment. The accredited vet's certificate has to be written to Albania's specific conditions, microchip, vaccination record, general health, rather than a one-size-fits-all export form.

        From there it's a matter of timing the vaccination so it clears 21 days before the Tirana landing, and getting the certificate issued within the window Albanian port officials expect on arrival, typically 10 days from the health exam.
