content:
  h1: "Pet Transport from Hungary to Jamaica"
  overview: |
    The Hungary to Jamaica pet transport route is demanding, with a mandatory import permit, 14-day quarantine at Plumb Point, two rounds of parasite treatment, and a strict microchip-before-vaccinations rule. Start the process no later than 10 weeks before your intended departure date; 12 weeks provides more room to deal with any delays in the VSD permit process.

    Jamaica's Veterinary Services Division (VSD) administers all pet imports. The import permit is the first step, and nothing else should begin until it is in hand. Contact the VSD directly at the outset: vsdpermits@moa.gov.jm, +1 876-977-2489. Requirements are subject to change, and the VSD is the only authoritative source.

    Hungary's export process is managed through Nebih (Nemzeti Elelmiszerlanc-biztonsagi Hivatal, nebih.gov.hu). The health certificate must be issued by a Nebih-authorised official vet and endorsed by Nebih. Budapest Ferenc Liszt International Airport (BUD) connects to Kingston (KIN) via London Heathrow on British Airways, via Amsterdam on KLM, and via Paris CDG on Air France. Wizz Air operates from Budapest to London Gatwick and Heathrow, which supports the British Airways cargo routing.

  sections:
    - heading: "Obtaining the VSD Import Permit"
      body: |
        Apply to the VSD 8-12 weeks before your planned travel date. The application requires details of your pet (species, breed, age, microchip number if already implanted) and your intended travel dates. VSD issues the permit once the application is assessed. Do not book flights, arrange vaccinations, or contact vets until the permit arrives. Processing times vary and cannot be reliably predicted in advance.

        Once you have the permit, the preparation sequence begins with microchipping. The microchip must be implanted before any vaccination is given. Jamaica's rules are explicit: if vaccinations were administered before the microchip was implanted, they are considered invalid and the full course must be repeated post-microchipping. Confirm the microchip number on the VSD permit matches the chip in the animal before any vet work proceeds.

    - heading: "Parasite Treatment Schedule"
      body: |
        Jamaica requires two rounds of internal and external parasite treatment, each pair spaced 14 days apart. The second internal treatment must be given within 7 days of transport; the second external treatment must be given within 48 hours of transport. Both dates and products must appear on the official health certificate.

        Plan these backwards from your confirmed departure date. The 48-hour external parasite window means treatment on the day before or the morning of travel. The 7-day internal parasite window is in the week before departure. The first treatments of each type are 14 days before those second doses. Your private vet handles the treatments; the Nebih-authorised official vet records them on the certificate.

    - heading: "Hungarian Export Certification"
      body: |
        Nebih-authorised official vets conduct pre-export examinations and issue the EU-format health certificate. Nebih then endorses it. Not all private vets in Hungary hold Nebih authorisation; contact Nebih (nebih.gov.hu) or ask your regular vet to refer you to an officially authorised colleague if they do not hold this status.

        The certificate is valid for 10 days from signing. Nebih endorsement takes one to three working days. Schedule the official vet appointment so that all parasite treatments are complete and documented, and so that the endorsement and travel both fall within the 10-day validity window. Budapest Ferenc Liszt International Airport (BUD) handles all international cargo departures.

    - heading: "14-Day Quarantine at Plumb Point"
      body: |
        Every dog and cat arriving in Jamaica is taken to Plumb Point National Quarantine Facility in Kingston for up to 14 days. The VSD manages the facility and charges daily fees; request current rates from them before travel. Owners cannot visit during the quarantine period, but the VSD can provide updates.

        VSD inspectors at Norman Manley International Airport (KIN) check all documents before releasing the animal to the quarantine facility. A missing or incorrect document at this stage can result in delays, additional costs, or refusal of entry. Thorough preparation in Budapest before departure is the only safeguard.
