  sections:
    - heading: "Finnish official vets and the health certificate for US travel"
      body: |
        The health certificate for US import from Finland must be issued by a Finnish official vet authorised by the Finnish Food Authority (Ruokavirasto). This is a different qualification from a standard private practice vet, though in Finland many practices have official vet authorisation.

        The certificate must include your pet's microchip number, species and breed, vaccination history (including a current rabies vaccination), owner details, and a declaration of fitness to travel. USDA APHIS may require authentication of the certificate: confirm the current process at usda.gov/aphis/pet-travel for Finland, as requirements can change.

        The fee for a Finnish official vet health certificate is typically EUR 80 to EUR 200 depending on the practice and the time involved. Most Finnish veterinary practices are familiar with the health certificate format for international travel.

    - heading: "Finnair direct from Helsinki to the USA"
      body: |
        Finnair operates direct flights from Helsinki-Vantaa Airport (HEL) to New York JFK, Chicago O'Hare (ORD), Los Angeles, and Miami. This direct transatlantic connectivity from Helsinki is a practical advantage: it avoids European hub connections and minimises total journey time.

        Small pets (cats and small dogs meeting Finnair's weight and carrier size limits) may be eligible to travel in the cabin on Finnair flights. Larger pets travel as cargo or checked baggage. Finnair's pet policies should be confirmed directly with the airline before booking, as weight limits and seasonal restrictions can change.

        American Airlines codeshares with Finnair and operates some transatlantic routes from Helsinki. If your destination is a city not served directly by Finnair, connections via JFK, ORD, or another Finnair-served gateway are straightforward.
