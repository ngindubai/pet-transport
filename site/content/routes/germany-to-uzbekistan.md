    - heading: "No titre test, but a permit question that needs a direct answer"
      body: |
        Uzbekistan doesn't classify Germany as a high-rabies-risk origin, so the FAVN or ELISA titre test that shapes most long-haul EU routes doesn't apply here. What still matters is sequencing: the microchip has to go in before or at the same appointment as the rabies vaccination, and the vaccination itself needs to sit inside a 30-day-to-12-month window before arrival.

        The import permit is the genuine grey area. Some accounts describe a pet arriving on the strength of the health certificate alone; a 2026 process review states the Committee of Veterinary and Livestock Development expects a vet.uz application filed ahead of travel. Both can't be fully right for every case. Apply for the permit and confirm directly with the Committee rather than assuming the lighter reading holds.
