  h1: "Pet Transport from the United Kingdom to Tunisia"
  overview: |
    A pet leaving the UK for Tunisia has an easier run than one making the reverse trip. No titre test, no lengthy waiting period, no import permit for an owner travelling with their own animal. The paperwork centres on one document: Export Health Certificate 3028, arranged through an APHA-registered Official Vet.

    The catch sits in the breed rules rather than the timeline. Tunisia refuses entry to Pit Bull, Rottweiler, Tosa and Mastiff-type dogs and their crosses, and Tunisair applies its own near-identical carriage ban on top. Get the breed question settled before booking a flight or an OV appointment. For anything else, four to six weeks of preparation is realistic.

  sections:
    - heading: "Why this direction is simpler than Tunisia to UK"
      body: |
        Tunisia's import rules class the UK among rabies-controlled countries, so the titre test that a Tunisia-origin pet must sit through does not apply here. A current rabies vaccination, given between 30 days and 12 months before arrival, is enough. First-time vaccinations still carry a fixed 30-day wait; a pet with an existing valid booster usually clears this requirement without any extra delay.

        The one document that matters is Export Health Certificate 3028. It has to be applied for online through APHA (or DAERA in Northern Ireland), examined and signed by an Official Veterinarian, and it is only valid for a defined window close to travel, so book the OV appointment once a flight date is fixed, not before.

    - heading: "The breed restriction that catches people out"
      body: |
        Tunisia's veterinary authority bans Pit Bull, Rottweiler, Tosa and Mastiff-type breeds and their crosses from import, full stop, regardless of documentation. Tunisair separately refuses Pit Bull, Rottweiler and Tosa Inu type dogs in cabin, hold and cargo. The two lists overlap almost entirely but aren't identical, so check both before assuming a mixed-breed dog is clear. A dog that a UK vet would describe as a "Staffie cross" or similar bull-breed mix is worth a direct query to DGSV or a transport agent before any booking goes ahead.

        There is no appeal process built into the border check. A dog turned away at Tunis-Carthage faces return to origin at the owner's cost, which makes this the single highest-stakes check on the whole route.

    - heading: "Flying from London to Tunis"
      body: |
        Tunisair runs the only nonstop service, Heathrow to Tunis-Carthage, roughly three times a week and just over three hours' flying time. Cabin travel suits pets under 8kg including the carrier; larger dogs go as checked baggage or, past 32kg combined weight, as cargo. With only a handful of weekly departures, book the pet space as soon as the travel date is set.

        Air France via Paris CDG is the fallback when the Tunisair schedule doesn't match the certificate's validity window, with a larger hold allowance and a dedicated cargo service for bigger dogs.
