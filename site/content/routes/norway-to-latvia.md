  sections:
    - heading: "The Norwegian health certificate and what Latvia needs"
      body: |
        Norway is recognised as a listed third country under EU pet travel rules. This means Norwegian pets can enter Latvia without a quarantine or titre test, but they need an official health certificate rather than an EU pet passport.

        The certificate is issued by a Mattilsynet-authorised official vet in Norway. It must be on the approved EU format for listed countries and cover: ISO 15-digit microchip, current rabies vaccination, and veterinary identification details. It has a validity period, so the appointment should be scheduled close to the travel date.

        Latvia's PVD (Pārtikas un veterinārais dienests) accepts this certificate for Norwegian pets arriving at Riga. The verification process is the same as for an EU pet passport check: microchip scan matched to the certificate, vaccination dates reviewed, issuing vet confirmed.

    - heading: "Oslo to Riga: why airBaltic is the natural choice"
      body: |
        Riga Airport (RIX) is airBaltic's home hub, and the airline operates direct Oslo Gardermoen to Riga services. For Norwegian owners moving a pet to Latvia, this direct route is the most practical option.

        Cabin pets travel under airBaltic's standard policies. For large dogs in cargo, airBaltic Cargo handles bookings for the OSL-RIX sector. The RIX cargo infrastructure is well-developed given the volume of Baltic Sea trade through Riga.

        SAS is an alternative, serving Oslo to Riga on some schedules with SAS Cargo available for hold animals. If airBaltic capacity doesn't suit your dates, SAS is worth checking.
