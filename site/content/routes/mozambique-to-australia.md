  h1: "Pet Transport from Mozambique to Australia"
  overview: |
    Ruling out Singapore Airlines is the first useful thing to know about this route. Its Johannesburg-Singapore-Melbourne network looks like an obvious way to cover both the staging leg and the Australia leg, but Singapore Airlines carries no pets into or out of either Australia or South Africa, on any sector. That single fact reshapes the whole booking before anything else gets planned.

    The rest of the route follows the pattern this site has already set out for other DAFF non-approved African countries. Mozambique isn't on any of DAFF's Group 1, 2 or 3 lists, so 180 days of residency in an approved country come first, and South Africa is the practical choice given how many Mozambican pet moves already transit Johannesburg. What genuinely differs here is the final destination: the flight has to end in Melbourne, the only Australian port with a quarantine facility, not Perth, however tempting a newer direct Johannesburg-Perth service might look.

  sections:
    - heading: "Why does a non-approved country need 180 days somewhere else first?"
      body: |
        DAFF classifies every source country as Group 1, Group 2, Group 3, or non-approved. Mozambique sits in the last category, alongside most of the rest of Africa and large parts of Asia and South America. There is no direct booking available at any price, from any agent. The pet has to live continuously in an approved country for 180 days before DAFF starts processing the import at all.

        It's a fixed biosecurity requirement, not a documentation gap that a faster courier or a better-connected agent can close. The 180 days apply regardless of the animal's own health record.

    - heading: "Why South Africa, and why Singapore Airlines is off the table"
      body: |
        Johannesburg is already where Mozambican pet moves regularly transit, thanks to South African Airways' regular Maputo-Johannesburg service. South Africa is DAFF Group 3 approved, so starting the 180-day clock there needs no separate long-haul detour to the UK, USA or Singapore.

        Singapore Airlines is worth ruling out specifically, since its own network might otherwise look like a tidy way to link Johannesburg and Melbourne on one airline. It doesn't carry pets to or from Australia or South Africa at all. Emirates via Dubai or Qatar Airways via Doha are the workable alternatives for the eventual Australia-bound leg.

    - heading: "The titre test, and the quarantine detail worth planning around early"
      body: |
        Once in South Africa, the standard RNATT titre test applies: blood drawn at least 30 days after vaccination, tested at a DAFF-approved lab (Onderstepoort Veterinary Institute near Pretoria handles most of these), with a 180-day wait from the date the lab receives the sample. That wait can run alongside the residency period rather than stacking on top of it, provided the test happens early.

        One detail changes the cost of this route meaningfully. If the microchip identity is verified by the competent authority before the RNATT blood draw, and at least 180 days before the flight, Mickleham quarantine falls to a 10-day minimum instead of the default 30 days. Source: DAFF, agriculture.gov.au. Building this sequencing in from day one of the South Africa residency is worth more than most of the other planning decisions on this route combined.

    - heading: "Melbourne only, not Perth, whatever the map suggests"
      body: |
        Qantas and South African Airways both now fly a genuine direct Johannesburg-Perth service, and it's tempting to assume that solves the Australia leg neatly. It doesn't, for this purpose. DAFF only processes imported cats and dogs through Melbourne, and Qatar Airways states this outright in its own carriage terms for Australia-bound animals: Melbourne, regardless of the owner's actual destination city.

        Emirates via Dubai, or Qatar Airways via Doha, both reach Melbourne on a workable cargo routing. Whichever is chosen, plan the Johannesburg-to-Melbourne leg specifically, rather than assuming any Australia-bound flight from Johannesburg will do.
