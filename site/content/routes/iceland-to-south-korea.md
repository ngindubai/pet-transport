content:
  h1: "Pet Transport from Iceland to South Korea"
  overview: |
    South Korea's APQA keeps its own list of rabies-free countries, and Iceland is on it, the same list that covers Australia, New Zealand, Japan and the UK. That status removes the titre test most other origins on this site face for South Korea entirely. What it doesn't remove is the rabies vaccination itself, a distinction worth knowing before assuming rabies-free means no jab at all.

    Get the paperwork right and a compliant pet clears Incheon the same day it lands, after a document check and a clinical exam. There's no direct flight from Keflavik, so the route runs through a European hub with Korean Air or Asiana covering the onward leg.

  sections:
    - heading: "Rabies-free removes the titre test, not the vaccine"
      body: |
        APQA's rabies-free list exists specifically to waive the titre test, the blood draw and result most non-listed origins have to produce. Iceland qualifies. That's the genuine time saving on this route: no 30-day post-vaccination wait for a lab result, no waiting period after that.

        What still stands is the current rabies vaccination itself, recorded on the government health certificate MAST issues. Some owners assume "rabies-free country" means the vaccine drops out of the process entirely. It doesn't, and APQA's own guidance is specific on this point.
    - heading: "Same-day release, if the paperwork matches"
      body: |
        A pet with a complete, correct health certificate and a passing clinical exam clears APQA's desk at Incheon the same day. There's no routine quarantine hold built into the process for a compliant arrival.

        The most common way to lose that same-day release is a document mismatch, a microchip number that doesn't match between the certificate and the chip reader, or a date that doesn't line up. Check every figure on the certificate against the actual chip and vaccination record before travel.
    - heading: "Keflavik to Seoul: one connection, not one flight"
      body: |
        Icelandair's network runs Keflavik to a European hub, and Korean Air or Asiana then handle the long-haul leg into Seoul Incheon. Two cargo bookings rather than one, so leave a workable gap between the connecting flights.
