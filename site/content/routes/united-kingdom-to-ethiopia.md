  sections:
    - heading: "The 8-week permit window and titre test"
      body: |
        Ethiopia's Animal and Plant Health Regulatory Directorate (APHRD) requires the import permit application to be submitted at least 8 weeks before travel, which is notably longer than many countries. The application requires microchip details, full vaccination history, titre test documentation, and planned entry date. Do not begin booking transport until the permit is confirmed in writing.

        The titre test adds further lead time. Blood must be drawn at least 30 days after your pet's most recent rabies vaccination, and laboratory processing takes 2-3 weeks. Combined with the permit window, working backwards from your travel date, you need to have started the vaccination and titre test process at least 12 weeks out.

    - heading: "Getting to Addis Ababa and the quarantine period"
      body: |
        Ethiopian Airlines operates direct flights from Heathrow to Bole International Airport in Addis Ababa and handles live animal cargo. This makes it the most practical option for this route. Book cargo space well in advance, as capacity for live animals is limited, particularly in peak travel periods.

        On arrival in Addis Ababa, your pet goes into a 10-day quarantine at a designated facility. This is mandatory and cannot be shortened. The owner is responsible for all quarantine costs. Confirm current facility rates and visiting arrangements with APHRD at the time you apply for the permit, so there are no surprises when your pet arrives.

  - question: "Is there quarantine when my pet arrives in Ethiopia from the UK?"
    answer: "Yes. All imported pets spend 10 days in mandatory quarantine at a designated facility in Addis Ababa. There is no exemption for any country of origin. The owner bears all quarantine costs for the full period. Factor this into your relocation schedule; you will not be able to take your pet home on the day they arrive."

  - question: "Which airlines fly pets from the UK to Ethiopia?"
    answer: "Ethiopian Airlines is the primary carrier for this route, operating direct flights from Heathrow to Bole International Airport in Addis Ababa.   - question: "Does my pet need a titre test to enter Ethiopia?"
    answer: "A titre test is required for pets from most countries travelling to Ethiopia. For UK pets, the titre test requires a blood draw at least 30 days after the most recent rabies vaccination, followed by 2-3 weeks of laboratory processing. The result must show a minimum of 0.5 IU/ml. Verify the current requirement with APHRD when applying for the permit, as classification lists can be updated."
