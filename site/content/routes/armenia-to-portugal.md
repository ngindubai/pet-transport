  sections:
    - heading: "Portugal's EU entry requirements for Armenian pets"
      body: |
        Portugal is an EU member state. Armenia is not on the EU's approved country list under Regulation 576/2013. Every pet from Armenia going to Portugal must follow the full unlisted country procedure.

        The sequence is fixed: microchip implanted before vaccination, rabies vaccination, FAVN titre test blood draw at least 30 days after vaccination, result of at least 0.5 IU/ml at an EU-approved laboratory, then a 90-day wait from the blood draw date before Portugal entry.

        DGAV is Portugal's competent authority and conducts inspections at Lisbon Airport (LIS) cargo terminal. The EU health certificate endorsed by SSFS must accompany the pet and be valid on arrival.

    - heading: "Finding an EU-approved laboratory from Armenia"
      body: |
        There is likely no EU-approved FAVN titre test laboratory in Armenia. Your vet in Yerevan must draw the blood correctly (using EDTA tubes, stored cold) and ship it to an approved laboratory outside Armenia. Common destination labs include facilities in Germany, Georgia, and Turkey.

        The sample must arrive at the lab in acceptable condition. Your vet needs experience with international blood sample shipping. The laboratory receiving the sample must confirm it accepts international samples before you proceed.

        Factor two to four weeks for sample transit and result return on top of the 90-day wait. The 90-day clock runs from the draw date regardless of when results arrive.

    - heading: "Cargo from Yerevan to Lisbon"
      body: |
        Turkish Airlines via Istanbul (IST) is the main cargo option for Yerevan (EVN) to Lisbon (LIS). Daily flights on both segments make this a reliable routing. Turkish Airlines Cargo has established live animal handling at EVN and at IST.

        Emirates via Dubai and Qatar Airways via Doha are alternatives if Turkish Airlines dates do not work. Both extend the journey time significantly but have strong cargo infrastructure.

        Lisbon Airport (LIS) is Portugal's main international hub. DGAV veterinary inspection takes place at the cargo terminal after the pet's arrival. Ensure your IPATA agent or handling agent at LIS is briefed on the documentation requirements in advance.

faqs:
  - question: "What makes Armenia an EU-unlisted country?"
    answer: "The EU maintains a list of approved third countries for pet travel based on disease risk assessments. Armenia is not on this list, which means pets from Armenia must follow the stricter unlisted country process including the FAVN titre test and 90-day wait. This applies regardless of the individual pet's health status."
  - question: "When exactly does the 90-day wait start?"
    answer: "The 90-day wait starts from the date blood was drawn for the FAVN titre test. It does not start when results arrive. Record the blood draw date accurately; this is the date all waiting period calculations are based on."
  - question: "Can I start the titre test process in Armenia?"
    answer: "Yes. Your vet in Yerevan can draw the blood after the microchip and vaccination. The blood then needs shipping to an EU-approved laboratory outside Armenia. The 90-day wait starts from the Armenian blood draw date, so starting in Armenia is the correct approach."
  - question: "Who inspects my pet on arrival at Lisbon Airport?"
    answer: "DGAV (Direcção-Geral de Alimentação e Veterinária) conducts the veterinary inspection at Lisbon Airport cargo terminal. They check the SSFS-endorsed EU health certificate, verify the microchip, and confirm documentation is in order."
  - question: "Is there any quarantine for Armenian pets arriving in Portugal?"
    answer: "No quarantine for compliant arrivals. DGAV inspection is documentary and physical on arrival day. Healthy pets with complete SSFS-endorsed EU health certificates are released without detention. Non-compliant pets may be refused entry."
