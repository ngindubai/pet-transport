  sections:
    - heading: "GNFA: Georgia's national food agency and import process"
      body: |
        The Georgian National Food Agency (GNFA) at gnfa.gov.ge manages companion animal imports into Georgia. Apply for the import permit 8-10 weeks before travel with your pet's microchip number, vaccination history, breed, and travel date.

        Georgia does not apply EU-style FAVN titre test requirements for pets from EU member states like the Netherlands. The GNFA's requirements for Dutch-origin pets focus on current rabies vaccination, ISO microchip, and the NVWA export health certificate. The GNFA inspection team at Tbilisi International Airport (TBS) checks documents on arrival. Compliant arrivals from the Netherlands proceed without quarantine.

    - heading: "Amsterdam to Tbilisi: Turkish Airlines via Istanbul"
      body: |
        Turkish Airlines connects Amsterdam Schiphol (AMS) to Istanbul (IST) with regular daily services. From IST, Turkish Airlines operates multiple daily flights to Tbilisi International Airport (TBS). Turkish Airlines Cargo handles live animal freight and IST-TBS is a well-used short-haul Caucasus connection.

        The AMS-IST-TBS routing takes approximately 6-9 hours depending on the connection time at Istanbul. Schiphol is one of Europe's largest cargo hubs and departures for Caucasus-bound animal cargo are managed professionally.

        Georgian Airways operates from various European cities to Tbilisi and may have routes from Amsterdam or connections accessible from AMS. If Georgian Airways cargo service is available and suits your dates, it is worth comparing with the Turkish Airlines routing.

    - heading: "NVWA export health certificate"
      body: |
        For Georgia, a non-EU country, the Dutch EU pet passport is not valid. The correct document is an official health certificate from an NVWA-authorised veterinarian (Officieel Dierenarts).

        Book a pre-export examination with an NVWA-authorised vet. They issue the health certificate covering microchip, vaccination status, and health attestation. NVWA endorses the certificate. The endorsed document must be issued within 10 days of travel.

        NVWA-authorised veterinary practices are available across the Netherlands. Your regular vet may have this authorisation, or can refer you to a nearby NVWA-authorised practice. The process is well established for European pet owners moving to non-EU destinations.
