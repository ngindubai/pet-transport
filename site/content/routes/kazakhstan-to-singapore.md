  h1: "Pet Transport from Kazakhstan to Singapore"
  overview: |
    Air Astana flies Almaty to Bangkok every day of the week, and that single fact does more to make this route workable than anything on the compliance side. Kazakhstan isn't named on either of AVS's shorter schedules, Schedule I or Schedule II, so it defaults to Schedule III: the tier that carries a WOAH-prescribed titre test, a fixed 90-day wait from the blood draw, and 30 days at Singapore's Animal Quarantine Centre on arrival. Confirmed directly against avs.nparks.gov.sg, fetched 21 July 2026.

    Where Kazakhstan differs from other Schedule III origins on this site is the flight. Rather than the three-hub itineraries some Central Asia-to-Southeast-Asia pairs need, a daily Air Astana service into Bangkok connects straight into one of the busiest air corridors in the region, Singapore Airlines' frequent BKK-SIN flights. Budget four to five months, almost entirely the titre test clock rather than the travel logistics.

  sections:
    - heading: "Air Astana to Bangkok: the practical routing"
      body: |
        Most Central Asia-to-Southeast Asia pet moves need to change carriers two or three times before reaching a final destination. This one doesn't, quite. Air Astana operates a daily direct Almaty (ALA) to Bangkok (BKK) service, an established part of the airline's Southeast Asian network as of the airline's own late-2025 route expansion. From Bangkok, Singapore Airlines and several other carriers run multiple daily flights into Changi, one of the region's busiest passenger and cargo links.

        That gives a genuine two-carrier, single-connection itinerary: Air Astana ALA-BKK, then Singapore Airlines BKK-SIN. Confirm live animal cargo acceptance on both legs in writing before committing, since a passenger booking on either sector doesn't automatically guarantee cargo space for a live animal.

    - heading: "Schedule III: the same 90-day clock as everywhere else on this tier"
      body: |
        Kazakhstan sits outside AVS's Schedule I (four countries) and Schedule II (31 named countries), so the full Schedule III process applies. Blood for the WOAH titre test can't be drawn until at least 28 days after vaccination, and the result only counts for export between 90 and 365 days after that draw. Kazakhstan has no WOAH-recognised laboratory of its own, so the sample travels internationally, adding real time on top of the standard turnaround.

        Run the AVS import licence application and the AQC quarantine booking during the 90-day wait, not after it. Both processes can proceed in parallel with the titre clock, and doing so keeps this closer to a four-month move than a five-month one.
