content:
  h1: "Pet Transport from Belize to Canada"
  overview: |
    Seven months. That's the minimum age CFIA sets for a dog entering Canada from Belize, a full four months later than the 3-month threshold non-listed Caribbean origins face. The reason is a single classification: Belize appears on CFIA's high-risk-for-dog-rabies list, last updated 13 March 2025, alongside most of Central America.

    None of that means a titre test, though. CFIA's own published high-risk pathway leans on age, a mandatory microchip timing rule, an import permit and five restricted airports rather than a blood test, a genuinely different shape from the CDC's mirror process for the same list of countries entering the United States. Add BAHA's own thin published guidance on exporting a pet, and this route rewards starting early.

  sections:
    - heading: "What Belize's high-risk listing actually changes"
      body: |
        CFIA doesn't ask a high-risk-origin dog for a blood test the way some destinations do. Instead it tightens four things: the dog must be at least 7 months old on arrival, the microchip must go in before or on the same day as the vaccination, a CFIA Import Permit has to be issued in advance, and the dog can only land at one of five designated airports with proper inspection facilities.

        Owners who've researched the CDC's own high-risk process for entering the United States sometimes assume Canada mirrors it exactly, titre test included. It doesn't, at least not as CFIA currently publishes it. Confirm that hasn't changed through CFIA's own interactive tool before relying on it.

    - heading: "BAHA's paperwork gap"
      body: |
        Search BAHA's site for bringing a pet into Belize, and the detail is thorough: fees, vaccination lists, notice periods. Search for taking one out, and there's markedly less to go on. That's not evidence nothing's required. It's a reason to call the Animal Health Department at Central Farm directly and get the outbound process confirmed in writing.

        Do this early. A CFIA Import Permit application needs complete veterinary records attached, and chasing BAHA for missing documentation close to a flight date is a real risk on this route.

    - heading: "A seasonal flight, and the Miami fallback"
      body: |
        WestJet and Air Canada both fly Toronto to Belize City nonstop, but only from late October through early May, stepping up to as many as 4 weekly WestJet departures at the December-to-April peak. Outside that window, the nonstop simply doesn't exist, and American Airlines' year-round Belize City to Miami service becomes the practical first leg toward a Canadian gateway instead.

        Whichever route you take, land at Toronto, Montreal, Vancouver, Calgary or Halifax. No other Canadian airport is set up to clear a high-risk-origin pet.
