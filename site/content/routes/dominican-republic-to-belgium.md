content:
  h1: "Pet Transport Dominican Republic to Belgium"
  overview: |
    Moving a pet from the Dominican Republic to Belgium requires the full EU unlisted country process. The Dominican Republic is not on the EU listed country register, which means a FAVN titre test, a mandatory 90-day wait from the blood draw date, and an EU-format health certificate endorsed by DIGEGA. The total preparation time is typically 20 to 24 weeks.

    The cargo route runs primarily via Amsterdam with KLM or via Madrid with Iberia, connecting to Brussels Airport (BRU). FASFC veterinary officers inspect arriving pets at the BRU border post. Start preparations well in advance: the 90-day wait is the controlling factor and cannot be shortened.

  sections:
    - heading: "The FAVN titre test and the 90-day wait"
      body: |
        Belgium follows EU Regulation 2020/692 for non-commercial pet movement from unlisted countries. The Dominican Republic is classified as unlisted, which means every dog or cat entering Belgium from the Dominican Republic must have a current rabies vaccination and a successful FAVN (or ELISA) titre test result of at least 0.5 IU/ml from an EU-approved laboratory.

        The sequence is fixed: microchip first, then rabies vaccination, then blood draw at least 30 days after vaccination. The 90-day wait runs from the date of the blood draw, regardless of when the laboratory result arrives. Shipping a blood sample from the Dominican Republic to a European lab and receiving the result typically takes 2-4 weeks on top of the 90-day wait. The total preparation window from vaccination to EU entry is a minimum of 20 weeks and usually longer in practice.

        International sample shipping from the Dominican Republic requires sodium azide preservative medium and a courier experienced with biological samples. Confirm your vet or a local pet transport specialist can coordinate this before booking any travel.

    - heading: "DIGEGA health certificate and Belgium arrival"
      body: |
        The export health certificate must be in EU format, issued by a DIGEGA-authorised vet in the Dominican Republic and endorsed by DIGEGA (Direccion General de Ganaderia) under the Ministry of Agriculture. This certificate must be issued within 10 days of travel to Belgium and must include the microchip number, rabies vaccination record, titre test result, and laboratory report reference.

        FASFC (Federal Agency for the Safety of the Food Chain) veterinary officers inspect arriving pets at the border inspection post at Brussels Airport (BRU). Compliant arrivals are released without quarantine. FASFC's regulations align with EU Regulation 2020/692. Check favv-afsca.be for the current approved non-commercial movement requirements for unlisted countries.

    - heading: "Cargo from Santo Domingo to Brussels"
      body: |
        Santo Domingo Las Americas (SDQ) is the main international cargo departure point in the Dominican Republic. No direct cargo service connects SDQ to Brussels Airport (BRU). The two main routings are via Amsterdam Schiphol (AMS) with KLM, and via Madrid Barajas (MAD) with Iberia. Both hubs have established live animal cargo programmes and connections to Belgium.

        Brussels Airlines also operates from Caribbean-connected hubs and may offer a more direct routing depending on the specific schedule at the time of booking. Use the 90-day wait period to confirm cargo availability and book in advance. Contact a cargo agent to compare routings and confirm live animal acceptance on all segments.

faqs:
  - question: "Why does my pet need a titre test to enter Belgium from the Dominican Republic?"
    answer: "Belgium follows EU rules for unlisted countries. The Dominican Republic is not on the EU list of countries with equivalent rabies controls, so a FAVN titre test is mandatory regardless of vaccination history. The test confirms that the rabies vaccination has produced an adequate immune response (at least 0.5 IU/ml). A 90-day wait then applies from the blood draw date before EU entry is permitted."
  - question: "How long does the whole process take from start to travel day?"
    answer: "Typically 20 to 24 weeks from the date of the blood draw. Allow 4 weeks from vaccination to blood draw, then 90 days wait (13 weeks), then 4-6 weeks for booking, health certificate, and cargo logistics. If the rabies vaccination needs to be given first, add that to the start of the sequence."
  - question: "Is there quarantine in Belgium for pets from the Dominican Republic?"
    answer: "No quarantine for compliant arrivals. FASFC veterinary officers at Brussels Airport inspect documentation and release pets whose paperwork is in order. Non-compliant arrivals may be held at the border post's facilities at the owner's cost."
  - question: "What is DIGEGA and why is its endorsement required?"
    answer: "DIGEGA (Direccion General de Ganaderia) is the Directorate General of Livestock under the Dominican Republic's Ministry of Agriculture. It is the official competent authority for animal health certification. EU regulations require that health certificates for pets entering from non-EU countries be issued and endorsed by the official government authority. DIGEGA fills this role for pets leaving the Dominican Republic."
