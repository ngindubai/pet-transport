content:
  h1: "Pet Transport Jamaica to Belgium"
  overview: |
    Moving a pet from Jamaica to Belgium requires the full EU unlisted country process. Jamaica is not on the EU listed country register, so a FAVN titre test, a mandatory 90-day wait from the blood draw date, and a VSD-endorsed EU-format health certificate are all required. The total preparation time is 20 to 24 weeks.

    British Airways via London Heathrow or KLM via Amsterdam are the main cargo options from Kingston. FASFC veterinary officers inspect arriving pets at Brussels Airport. Start the process early: the 90-day wait is the controlling factor and there is no way to compress it.

  sections:
    - heading: "FAVN titre test: the key requirement"
      body: |
        Belgium applies EU Regulation 2020/692 for unlisted countries. Jamaica is classified as unlisted, which means every dog or cat entering Belgium from Jamaica must have a successful FAVN (or ELISA) titre test result of at least 0.5 IU/ml from an EU-approved laboratory.

        The required sequence is: microchip implantation before the rabies vaccination, then vaccination, then blood draw at least 30 days after vaccination. The 90-day wait runs from the blood draw date. Shipping a Jamaican blood sample to a European lab and getting results back typically adds 2-4 weeks to the timeline. The total minimum from vaccination to Belgium entry is around 20 weeks. International sample shipping from Jamaica requires correct temperature management and an experienced international courier for biological samples.

    - heading: "Cargo from Kingston to Brussels"
      body: |
        Norman Manley International Airport (KIN) in Kingston is the main cargo departure point from Jamaica. British Airways World Cargo operates from KIN to London Heathrow (LHR), from where connections to Brussels Airport (BRU) are available. KLM also operates from KIN to Amsterdam Schiphol (AMS), with Brussels accessible from AMS via a short onward connection.

        Sangster International Airport (MBJ) in Montego Bay is an alternative departure point with some North American connections and seasonal European services. Book cargo well in advance during the 90-day wait period and confirm live animal acceptance on all segments before finalising the travel date.

    - heading: "VSD export certificate and FASFC arrival inspection"
      body: |
        The export health certificate must be in EU format, issued by a VSD-authorised vet in Jamaica and endorsed by the Veterinary Services Division (VSD) under the Ministry of Agriculture, Fisheries and Mining. This certificate must be issued within 10 days of travel and include the microchip number, vaccination history, titre test result, and laboratory report reference. Confirm with VSD whether an export permit is also required: vsdpermits@moa.gov.jm.

        FASFC (Federal Agency for the Safety of the Food Chain) veterinary officers inspect arriving pets at the border inspection post at Brussels Airport. Compliant arrivals are released without quarantine.
