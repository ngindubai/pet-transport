content:
  h1: "Pet Transport from Lithuania to the UAE"
  overview: |
    Twelve weeks old is old enough. That's the minimum age MOCCAE applies to pets travelling from Lithuania, three weeks younger than the threshold used for countries outside its low-risk list. Being an EU member state buys Lithuanian pet owners that lower age bar and, more importantly, no rabies titre test at all.

    What still has to happen is real: a current vaccination, a MOCCAE import permit filed online and valid for 90 days, and a health certificate on the MOCCAE model endorsed by Lithuania's own veterinary service. Vilnius has no direct Dubai flight, so the route runs through either Istanbul or Riga depending on pet size and airline preference.

  sections:
    - heading: "Istanbul or Riga: choosing the connection"
      body: |
        Turkish Airlines Cargo via Istanbul suits larger dogs needing a dedicated cargo hold rather than a cabin seat. Its live animal handling at IST is well established, and capacity on the Vilnius-Istanbul leg is generally easier to secure than on smaller regional routes.

        airBaltic via Riga works well for small cats and dogs light enough to travel in the cabin on the first leg. It keeps the pet closer to the owner for the Baltic portion of the journey, with the onward Dubai leg handled separately.

    - heading: "MOCCAE's low-risk classification for Lithuania"
      body: |
        MOCCAE treats every EU member state as low-risk for rabies, Lithuania included. That removes the titre test entirely from this route: no accredited-lab blood draw, no result to wait on. A current rabies vaccination, given at least 21 days before travel if it's a first dose, is what MOCCAE actually checks.

        The import permit remains a separate step regardless of risk category. It's applied for online, valid for 90 days once granted, and needs to line up with a confirmed travel date rather than a rough one.
