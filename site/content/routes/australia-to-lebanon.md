  sections:
    - heading: "The Lebanese import permit: first thing to do"
      body: |
        Lebanon's Directorate General of Agriculture (DGA), part of the Ministry of Agriculture, controls companion animal imports. Before booking your pet's cargo space or finalising any vet appointments, apply for the import permit at agriculture.gov.lb.

        The permit application typically asks for your pet's microchip number, vaccination history, origin country, breed, and expected travel date. Processing takes a variable amount of time. Given Lebanon's current administrative pressures, allow 8-10 weeks and follow up to confirm receipt of your application.

        Without the permit, your pet cannot legally enter Lebanon. The DGA inspection team at BEY checks the permit on arrival alongside the health certificate. Both must be present and correctly formatted.

    - heading: "Routing from Australia to Beirut"
      body: |
        No direct cargo service connects Australia to Beirut. All routes involve at least one hub connection. The Gulf hubs at Dubai (DXB) and Doha (DOH) are the most efficient for Australia-to-Lebanon cargo, with Emirates and Qatar Airways offering live animal freight on the Australia legs and short-haul connections to BEY.

        Emirates SkyCargo via DXB is generally the most straightforward routing. Emirates operates SYD and MEL to Dubai with regular frequency, and the DXB-BEY segment is short, reducing overall transit time. Confirm that your pet's crate dimensions and breed are accepted before booking.

        Turkish Airlines via Istanbul (IST) is an alternative routing. Australia-to-IST typically connects via Dubai or Singapore, and Turkish Airlines operates BEY regularly from IST. Turkish Airlines Cargo handles live animals on many routes and IST is well set up for Middle East-bound live animal freight.

        Total transit time from Sydney or Melbourne to Beirut runs approximately 22-28 hours depending on hub and connection times. For healthy adult dogs and cats in compliant crates, this is within the accepted range for long-haul cargo moves.

    - heading: "DAFF endorsement for a non-EU destination"
      body: |
        Most of Australia's outbound pet travel documentation uses the DAFF (Department of Agriculture, Fisheries and Forestry) endorsement process. For EU destinations, this produces an EU-format health certificate. For Lebanon, a non-EU, non-Commonwealth destination, the certificate must meet Lebanon's specific requirements rather than EU format.

        Your accredited Australian vet issues the health certificate after a pre-travel clinical examination. DAFF then endorses it. The endorsed certificate is the document your Lebanese permit issuer and the DGA inspection team at BEY want to see.

        The certificate must be issued within 10 days of travel. Time the vet appointment and DAFF endorsement carefully around your flight booking. DAFF endorsement typically takes 1-3 working days, so a vet appointment at the start of the 10-day window with a day or two for endorsement is sensible.
