  h1: "Pet Transport from Maldives to Austria"
  overview: |
    Austrian Airlines flies Male to Vienna non-stop. Nowhere else in this batch of five European destinations gets that. Every other route needs a Dubai or Doha changeover; this one doesn't, and for a cat that's already spent a full day at a vet clinic and an airport, cutting out a connection is worth more than it sounds.

    The paperwork itself doesn't get any shorter for it. Austria treats the Maldives as an EU-unlisted country, so the standard sequence applies in full: microchip, rabies vaccination, an FAVN or ELISA titre test, and 90 days from the draw date before travel is legal. Realistic planning window: 18 to 24 weeks.

  sections:
    - heading: "A non-stop flight most Maldives routes don't have"
      body: |
        Austrian Airlines' Vienna-Male sector runs at roughly 8 hours 45 minutes, direct. For a route where nearly every other European destination forces a change of aircraft in Dubai or Doha, that's a genuine difference, not a marketing line. Fewer connections means fewer chances for a missed hot-weather embargo, a rebooked crate, or a cat sitting on a tarmac between flights longer than planned.
    - heading: "Why the lab result takes longer here than from an EU country"
      body: |
        Male has no laboratory that runs the FAVN or ELISA titre test. Every sample leaves the island first, usually the same direction the cat will eventually fly, and that courier step adds time on top of whatever the lab itself needs. Confirm with your vet exactly where the sample goes and how it travels before setting a vaccination date around a fixed moving deadline.
    - heading: "AGES, BMSGPK, and the 90-day rule"
      body: |
        Austria splits pet import oversight between two bodies: AGES handles the technical and laboratory side, BMSGPK sits above it at ministry level. Neither shortens the underlying rule. Microchip before vaccination, a 30-day gap, a titre result of 0.5 IU/ml or higher, then 90 days measured from the draw date, not the result date. Get the order wrong at the start and the whole sequence restarts.
