  overview: |
    New Zealand applies its biosecurity rules equally to all countries: every dog and cat, from every origin, must complete a titre test, observe a 180-day waiting period from the blood draw date, and then spend 10 days in quarantine at the MPI facility on arrival. There are no approved-country exemptions. Moving a pet from the USA to New Zealand takes at least 36-44 weeks from the start of planning.

    MPI (Ministry for Primary Industries) manages New Zealand's strict import biosecurity programme. Pre-clearance under MPI's Import Health Standard is required before travel. Air New Zealand operates direct cargo from Los Angeles to Auckland, which is the most straightforward routing for the USA to New Zealand pet move.

    - heading: "MPI Import Health Standard: the pre-clearance process"
      body: |
        New Zealand's Import Health Standard (IHS) for dogs and cats is the regulatory framework that governs what is required before and after entry. MPI must grant pre-clearance before your pet can travel. This involves submitting your pet's health records, titre test result, vaccination history, and travel plan to MPI for approval.

        The IHS specifies the exact treatments your pet must have received before travel, the tests required, the residency period in the country of origin, and the quarantine conditions on arrival. For pets from the USA, the standard IHS requirements include: ISO microchip, rabies vaccination (at least 30 days before titre blood draw), FAVN titre test with a result of at least 0.5 IU/ml, 180-day wait from blood draw, and 10-day quarantine at MAFBNZ (the MAF Biosecurity New Zealand facility at Auckland).

        Apply for MPI pre-clearance as soon as you have your titre test result. Do not book cargo without MPI approval confirmed in writing. The MPI pre-clearance is tied to a specific travel window: if you change your travel date, notify MPI and reconfirm clearance.

        The quarantine facility at Auckland International Airport handles all arriving pets. Owners pay the quarantine costs directly. Contact MPI or a New Zealand pet relocation agent for current quarantine fee schedules, as these are updated periodically.

faqs:
  - question: "Does my US-origin dog really need a titre test to enter New Zealand?"
    answer: "Yes. New Zealand requires a titre test from all countries without exception. There is no approved-country list that exempts pets from the titre test requirement. Your dog needs: a current vaccination, a titre test with a result of at least 0.5 IU/ml from blood drawn at least 30 days after vaccination, a 180-day wait from the blood draw date, and 10-day quarantine on arrival."
  - question: "How long does the USA to New Zealand pet move take?"
    answer: "Allow 36-44 weeks minimum. The 180-day wait from the titre test blood draw is the dominant constraint. Add vaccination timing (at least 30 days before the blood draw), MPI pre-clearance processing (4-8 weeks), cargo booking, and the 10-day quarantine on arrival, and a 10-month total plan is realistic."
  - question: "Can the 10-day quarantine in New Zealand be shortened or waived?"
    answer: "No. The 10-day quarantine at MAFBNZ is mandatory for all dogs and cats arriving in New Zealand from all countries. There is no waiver and the period cannot be shortened, regardless of documentation quality or country of origin."
  - question: "Is Qantas via Sydney a good option for USA to New Zealand pet transport?"
    answer: "Qantas via Sydney adds an Australian transit, which brings Australian biosecurity rules into the picture at the transit point. This adds complexity compared to Air New Zealand or United direct transpacific services to Auckland. For most USA-NZ pet moves, a direct transpacific route is strongly preferred to avoid the additional biosecurity checks at the Sydney transit."
  - question: "What is MPI pre-clearance and how do I apply?"
    answer: "MPI pre-clearance is New Zealand's import approval process for pets. You submit your pet's vaccination records, titre test result, and travel plan to MPI, and they confirm clearance under the Import Health Standard. Apply as soon as your titre test result is confirmed. MPI clearance is tied to a specific travel window. Applications go through MPI (mpi.govt.nz). Using a NZ-experienced pet relocation agent simplifies the process."
