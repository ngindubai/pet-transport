content:
  h1: "Pet Transport from Germany to Seychelles"
  overview: |
    Germany's EU rabies-free classification does one useful thing on this route: it keeps quarantine at fourteen days rather than the twenty-eight, plus a fixed three-month wait, that a rabies-affected origin like the US faces under the National Biosecurity Agency's 2024 rules. What it changes nothing about is whether quarantine applies at all. Seychelles quarantines every arriving pet by law, no exceptions by origin.

    Between the titre test, the import permit, and a health screen that runs well beyond rabies for dogs, this sits among the more demanding routes on this site even with the lighter German tier. Twelve to sixteen weeks is a realistic floor, most of it NBA permit processing and quarantine booking rather than the flight itself.

  sections:
    - heading: "Why rabies-free doesn't mean quarantine-free"
      body: |
        Seychelles' National Biosecurity Agency makes quarantine mandatory by law for every arriving cat and dog, whatever its country of origin. The only variable is length. Germany's EU rabies-free status places it in the same lighter tier as the UK, which typically means around 14 days at the single Mahe facility rather than the longer combination of a fixed post-draw wait plus an extended facility stay that a rabies-affected origin such as the US gets under the same 2024 rules.

        The titre test itself still needs a result above 0.5 IU/mL, drawn at least 30 days after vaccination and tested within 6 months of travel. Germany's rabies-free classification changes the length of quarantine on the other end. It doesn't remove the test.

    - heading: "A health screen wider than most destinations ask for"
      body: |
        Beyond rabies, Seychelles wants dogs cleared for Brucella canis, Trypanosoma evansi, Babesia gibsoni, Leishmania and Erlichia canis, unless the exporting vet can certify Germany free of a given disease. It's a broader panel than most destinations in this build require, and worth raising with the German vet early rather than close to the flight.

        A separate list of breeds, including Rottweiler, German Shepherd, Doberman and several others, needs mandatory surgical sterilisation before entry, with a vet-endorsed certificate to prove it. This isn't a soft recommendation; it's a condition of the import permit itself.
