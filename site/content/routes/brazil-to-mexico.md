  h1: "Pet Transport Brazil to Mexico: MAPA Certificate, SENASICA Import and the South American Route"
  overview: |
    Mexico is a relatively straightforward pet import destination from Brazil compared to many other international moves. A MAPA-endorsed health certificate valid within 10 days of entry, a current rabies vaccination, and microchip are the core requirements. SENASICA (Mexico's agricultural safety authority) oversees pet imports, and there is no routine quarantine for compliant pets. Copa Airlines via Panama and LATAM via Lima or Bogota are practical routing options from São Paulo.

    - heading: "SENASICA health certificate requirements"
      body: |
        SENASICA (Servicio Nacional de Sanidad, Inocuidad y Calidad Agroalimentaria) is Mexico's food and agriculture safety authority. The health certificate for Mexico import must be issued by a MAPA-authorised vet in Brazil and meet SENASICA's format requirements. The certificate must be issued within 10 days of the pet's entry into Mexico.

        The certificate must include your pet's vaccination history (current rabies vaccination within 12 months), microchip number, species and breed, owner details, and a declaration of fitness to travel and absence of disease. SENASICA inspects pets at the airport of entry.

        Contact SENASICA (gob.mx/senasica) for the current health certificate format required for imports from Brazil.
