  sections:
    - heading: "Brazil to Mexico vs Brazil to Indonesia: comparing complexity"
      body: |
        For owners choosing between Mexican and Southeast Asian destinations, the documentation gap is significant.

        Mexico requires no titre test, no advance permit, and no quarantine. The MAPA health certificate covers the Brazil-side obligation and Mexico's SENASICA inspects on arrival. Preparation time is 4-6 weeks from the point your pet's rabies vaccination is current.

        Indonesia requires the FAVN titre test (30-day wait after vaccination, then 30 days from blood draw before entry), a BARANTAN import recommendation, and 30-day facility quarantine on arrival. Preparation time is 10-16 weeks and the cost is substantially higher.

        If flexibility over destination exists, Mexico's process is considerably less demanding on time and paperwork.

    - heading: "Which routing works for cargo pets from Brazil to Mexico?"
      body: |
        From São Paulo Guarulhos (GRU), the four main routing options to Mexico City (MEX) each involve one connection.

        LATAM via Lima or Bogota is the most established South America-to-Mexico corridor for cargo. Lima (LIM) and Bogota (BOG) are both competent cargo hubs with good South American connections.

        Copa Airlines via Panama City (PTY) is a single-stop option with a relatively short layover. PTY handles live animal cargo well and Copa has strong connections across Latin America.

        Aeromexico connects GRU to MEX with or without a connection, depending on the schedule. Mexican carriers tend to have clear live animal handling procedures on this corridor.

        Miami (MIA) is a longer routing but gives access to American Airlines Cargo with well-established live animal handling at one of the world's busiest cargo airports.
