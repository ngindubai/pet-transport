  h1: "Pet Transport from Malawi to the United States"
  overview: |
    Qatar Airways started codeshare service to Lilongwe in October 2025, flying alongside Kenya Airways. That's a genuinely new option for this route, and it matters because it opens a Doha to Washington Dulles connection, landing at one of the six US airports with a CDC-registered animal care facility.

    That facility restriction exists because Malawi is CDC high-risk for dog rabies. A dog from Malawi needs a government-endorsed vaccination and microchip certificate, a facility reservation, and either a passing titre result or 28 days of quarantine on arrival. A cat needs a microchip and to look healthy at the border. The federal gap between the two species on this route is enormous.

    Budget three to four months from a standing start if the titre pathway is used, longer if quarantine is the fallback plan.

  sections:
    - heading: "The new Qatar Airways option, and why the arrival airport matters"
      body: |
        Kenya Airways and Ethiopian Airlines have long served Lilongwe through their East African hubs, Nairobi and Addis Ababa respectively. Qatar Airways' codeshare, added in October 2025, adds a third route: Lilongwe to Doha, then Doha to Washington Dulles.

        All three options matter for a specific reason. A dog travelling without a valid rabies titre result can only land at one of six US airports with a CDC-registered animal care facility: JFK, Washington Dulles, Atlanta, Miami, Los Angeles or San Francisco. Kenya Airways lands at JFK. Ethiopian Airlines and Qatar Airways both reach Washington Dulles. Pick the routing based on the facility, not just the cheapest fare.
    - heading: "Dogs face the full CDC protocol; cats face almost none of it"
      body: |
        Malawi's place on the CDC's high-risk list means a dog needs the complete foreign-vaccinated pathway: vaccination and microchip certification endorsed by a Malawian vet, a completed CDC Dog Import Form, and a reservation at a CDC-registered facility booked before the flight.

        A cat crossing the same border needs a microchip and a clean bill of health on arrival. There's no federal health certificate requirement, no titre test, no facility booking. If a household is moving both a dog and a cat from Malawi, expect the two to need almost entirely separate preparation timelines.
