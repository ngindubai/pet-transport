  sections:
    - heading: "Dominican Republic import requirements: the checklist"
      body: |
        The DGAG (Direccion General de Ganaderia) under the Ministerio de Agricultura regulates all pet imports into the Dominican Republic. DGAG veterinary officers inspect arriving pets at Las Americas International Airport (SDQ) in Santo Domingo on arrival.

        The requirements are: a microchip conforming to ISO 11784/11785, a valid rabies vaccination given between 30 days and 12 months before entry, a health certificate issued within 15 days of travel, and current DHPP vaccination for dogs (distemper, adenovirus, parainfluenza, parvovirus). Cats need rhinotracheitis, calicivirus, and panleukopenia vaccinations on record. All pets need internal and external antiparasitic treatment within 10 days of departure, documented by a vet.

        No import permit is required for personal companion animals (up to five animals with their owner). There is no quarantine for compliant arrivals.

    - heading: "Portugal's export documentation: the DGAV process"
      body: |
        Portugal's export documentation for non-EU destinations uses the AHC issued by a medico veterinario oficial and endorsed by the DGAV (Direccao-Geral de Alimentacao e Veterinaria). Contact your local DGAV regional directorate (Direccao Regional de Agricultura e Pescas) to find the nearest official vet and confirm the endorsement procedure for the Dominican Republic.

        TAP Air Portugal's Lisbon to Santo Domingo service makes Portugal one of the better-positioned EU countries for this route. That said, always confirm live animal cargo acceptance on your specific booking date, as cargo capacity is separate from passenger operations.

        The Dominican Republic accepts AHC certificates up to 15 days from issue. Issue the certificate as close to departure as your cargo booking allows, and ensure the antiparasitic treatment window (within 10 days of departure) aligns with your travel date.
