  overview: |
    The CDC's high-risk list forces a full titre test and a facility quarantine option on a Zambia-origin dog heading to the United States. Send that same dog to Canada instead, and CFIA's own high-risk classification of Zambia, confirmed against its list dated 13 March 2025, changes almost nothing for a personal pet: no antibody test, no import permit, just the vaccination and certificate checklist CFIA applies broadly.

    Ethiopian Airlines keeps the flight side just as clean, running its own nonstop Toronto-Addis Ababa service onward to Lusaka, a single-carrier option most Zambia routes on this site can't offer.

  sections:
    - heading: "Two high-risk lists, two very different outcomes"
      body: |
        Zambia appears on both the CDC's high-risk-for-dog-rabies list and CFIA's equivalent, last confirmed 13 March 2025. It's tempting to assume the two agencies treat that classification the same way. They don't.

        The CDC's version triggers a full foreign-vaccinated dog pathway for entry to the US: a titre test or a 28-day CDC-registered facility stay, arrival restricted to designated ports. CFIA's high-risk list mainly targets commercial dog imports, banned outright since September 2022. A personal pet dog or cat travelling with its owner isn't caught by that ban and moves on Canada's standard, lighter process.

    - heading: "One certificate for the UK, a different one for Canada"
      body: |
        Zambia's Department of Veterinary Services has a bespoke certificate for the UK route, EHC 3928, negotiated specifically for cats and dogs bound for Great Britain. No equivalent Canada-specific certificate number exists.

        That doesn't mean Canada gets less scrutiny, just a different document. The department issues its general health certificate for this route instead, built to CFIA's own format rather than the EHC 3928 template. Flag the destination clearly with the vet handling the paperwork so the right version gets prepared.

    - heading: "Ethiopian Airlines and the single-carrier option"
      body: |
        Most routes into Lusaka on this site need at least one change of airline. Ethiopian Airlines is the exception here too: its Toronto-Addis Ababa nonstop, paired with its own onward Addis Ababa-Lusaka service, keeps the whole booking on one carrier's cargo system.

        South African Airways' historical Johannesburg-Lusaka link remains a schedule-dependent alternative, useful if the Ethiopian routing doesn't line up with travel dates, though it needs a separate transatlantic carrier for the Toronto or Montreal leg.
