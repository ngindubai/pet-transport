  sections:
    - heading: "Helsinki to Ljubljana: planning the hub connection"
      body: |
        Helsinki-Vantaa (HEL) has no direct cargo flights to Ljubljana (LJU) on scheduled services. The standard approach is a two-leg journey, with the most common routing being:

        Helsinki to Vienna (HEL-VIE) on Finnair, then Vienna to Ljubljana (VIE-LJU) on Austrian Airlines. Vienna is the natural gateway for Slovenia given its proximity and Austrian Airlines' established connections to Ljubljana.

        Helsinki to Frankfurt (HEL-FRA) on Finnair or Lufthansa, then Frankfurt to Ljubljana (FRA-LJU) on Lufthansa. Frankfurt's Animal Lounge provides an excellent transit facility for pets waiting at the hub.

        For cabin pets, both routings work with the standard carrier policies. For large dogs in cargo, the Vienna routing is often preferred as it produces a shorter total journey and a smaller final-leg distance.

    - heading: "Ruokavirasto, UVHVVR, and what each country requires"
      body: |
        From Finland's side, the EU pet passport is issued by an official vet authorised under Ruokavirasto (the Finnish Food Authority). No export endorsement or certificate is needed from Ruokavirasto for a companion animal leaving Finland for another EU member state.

        From Slovenia's side, UVHVVR (Slovenia's food safety and veterinary authority) requires the EU pet passport with a valid ISO microchip and current rabies vaccination for an intra-EU arrival. No import permit, no titre test, no quarantine.

        The point to watch is Ljubljana's cargo capacity. LJU is a small airport and cargo space is limited. Book through the carrier's cargo office well in advance and confirm closer to travel. An unconfirmed cargo booking sometimes means the animal doesn't get loaded, which is avoidable with a direct call to the cargo team.

faqs:
  - question: "Does my dog need tapeworm treatment when leaving Finland for Slovenia?"
    answer: "No. Finland's Echinococcus (tapeworm) treatment requirement is for dogs entering Finland, not leaving. Slovenia has no tapeworm treatment requirement for dogs arriving from EU member states."
  - question: "Why is Ljubljana such a problem for cargo pets from Finland?"
    answer: "Ljubljana Airport (LJU) is a regional airport handling a relatively small volume of traffic. It doesn't have direct scheduled cargo connections from Helsinki. Pets must connect through a major hub such as Vienna or Frankfurt. This adds a transit step and requires coordination between two cargo booking systems."
  - question: "Can I collect my pet in Vienna and drive to Slovenia instead of putting them on a second plane?"
    answer: "Yes. Many owners arriving with a pet from Finland at Vienna choose to drive the final leg to Ljubljana or another Slovenian destination. The drive from Vienna to Ljubljana is approximately 2 to 3.5 hours depending on traffic and border wait times. Your pet arrives by air at VIE and travels the last section by road with you."
  - question: "Does UVHVVR need to be notified before a pet arrives from Finland?"
    answer: "No pre-notification is required for non-commercial companion animal movements from EU member states. Carry the EU pet passport and be ready for a spot check if requested by UVHVVR at Ljubljana Airport."
