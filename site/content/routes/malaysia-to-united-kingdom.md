  h1: "Pet Transport from Malaysia to the United Kingdom"
  overview: |
    Malaysia is not on the UK's Pet Travel Scheme approved list. The country has active rabies, which means all dogs and cats from Malaysia must complete a full FAVN titre test process before entering Great Britain. The process requires a FAVN titre test (blood drawn at least 30 days after a valid rabies vaccination), a passing result of 0.5 IU/ml or above, and then a 90-day wait from the blood draw date. The minimum total timeline is 5-6 months.

    The point that trips people up most often is where the 90-day count starts. It starts from the blood draw date, not the day the results arrive. If the lab takes two or three weeks to return results, those weeks count against your 90-day wait. Your travel date must be at least day 91 from the blood draw, not day 91 from receiving the results.

    Malaysia Airlines and British Airways handle cargo on the Kuala Lumpur to Heathrow corridor. Cathay Pacific via Hong Kong is an established alternative. Emirates via Dubai works outside the June to September heat embargo period. A specialist pet transport agent familiar with APHA requirements and the DVS export process is strongly recommended for this route.

    - heading: "Dogs entering the UK from Malaysia: tapeworm treatment"
      body: |
        Dogs only (not cats) must be treated for tapeworm before entering Great Britain from Malaysia. The treatment must be with praziquantel, administered by a veterinarian, and must take place no less than 24 hours and no more than 120 hours before the dog's scheduled arrival time in GB.

        The 24-120 hour window is strict and timed to your UK arrival time, not departure. Work backwards from your expected landing time at Heathrow or Gatwick to identify the correct treatment window in Malaysia. The treatment date, time, and product must be recorded in the health certificate or travel document.

        If the tapeworm treatment falls outside the window (too early or too late), the dog may be refused entry or held in quarantine. Time it carefully.
faqs:
  - question: "Why does Malaysia require a titre test for UK entry?"
    answer: "Malaysia is not on the UK's Pet Travel Scheme approved list because rabies is present in the country. The UK only allows pets from non-approved countries to enter without quarantine if they have completed the FAVN titre test process: a valid rabies vaccination, blood drawn 30+ days after that vaccination showing a titre of 0.5 IU/ml or above, and then a 90-day wait from the blood draw date. This applies to all pets from Malaysia regardless of their personal vaccination history."
  - question: "How long does the titre test process take from Malaysia?"
    answer: "The minimum is approximately 5-6 months: confirm microchip and rabies vaccination are in place (or administer), wait 30 days, draw blood for the FAVN titre test, receive results (typically 2-4 weeks), then wait 90 days from the blood draw date. If the titre test fails (below 0.5 IU/ml), re-vaccinate and restart the 30-day and 90-day sequence. Start as early as possible."
  - question: "Does my cat from Malaysia also need a titre test for UK entry?"
    answer: "Yes. The titre test and 90-day wait apply to both dogs and cats from Malaysia. The UK non-listed country rules apply to all species. Cats need the same process as dogs: microchip, rabies vaccination, titre test (blood drawn 30+ days after vaccination), 90-day wait, then travel with an endorsed health certificate. Cats do not need the tapeworm treatment that is required for dogs."
  - question: "Which airlines carry pets in cargo from Kuala Lumpur to London?"
    answer: "Malaysia Airlines offers direct cargo from KLIA (KUL) to Heathrow (LHR) and is familiar with DVS-to-APHA documentation. British Airways World Cargo is an alternative on the same corridor. Cathay Pacific via Hong Kong (HKG) is a reliable one-stop option. Emirates via Dubai (DXB) works for travel outside June to September, when heat embargoes restrict live animal shipments at DXB. All pets travel as manifested cargo.     answer: "If a pet arrives in Great Britain from Malaysia without a valid titre test result or without the completed 90-day wait, APHA may impose penalty quarantine of up to 4 months (120 days) at an APHA-authorised facility. The owner pays all quarantine costs. In some cases the pet may be returned to Malaysia. Do not travel until the full titre test process is complete and the 90-day period has elapsed from the blood draw date."
