  h1: "New Zealand to Macau: The Cleanest Rabies Record on This Site"
  overview: |
    No country on this site's Macau routes has a cleaner rabies history than New Zealand. It has never recorded a single case, in a person or an animal, ever. IAM's own stated policy waives the titre test for exactly this kind of record: no rabies incidents in the past two years. New Zealand clears that with room to spare.

    What the country doesn't have is a name on a public IAM list confirming it. Treat the favourable reading as likely, not certain, and build the outbound side of the process, MPI's own export paperwork plus the Hong Kong transfer, around a confirmed travel date regardless of how the titre question resolves.

  sections:
    - heading: "Never had rabies, still worth a phone call"
      body: |
        New Zealand's biosecurity system exists largely because the country has kept rabies, and much else, out entirely. That's a genuinely rare position; even Hong Kong's own exemption, documented elsewhere on this site, rests on a last case recorded back in 1987, not a record of never at all.

        IAM's stated rule doesn't require a name on a list, strictly speaking, just no rabies incidents in the past two years. But a stated rule and a confirmed classification are different things on paper, and this guide treats the gap honestly rather than assuming the shorter path. Confirm New Zealand's status with IAM directly.

    - heading: "MPI's own paperwork comes first"
      body: |
        Before the Macau side even starts, New Zealand's own export process needs sorting: an Animal Welfare Export Certificate through an MPI-listed veterinary practice, and, where Macau has an Overseas Market Access Requirement on file, an official assurance certificate confirming the pet meets it. This runs in parallel with, not after, the IAM import licence application, and shouldn't be treated as an afterthought once the flight is booked.

        From there, the route is the same one every long-haul Macau corridor on this site needs: Cathay Pacific's direct 11-hour Auckland-Hong Kong sector, then a road or ferry transfer across the Hong Kong-Zhuhai-Macau Bridge to clear IAM's arrival inspection.
