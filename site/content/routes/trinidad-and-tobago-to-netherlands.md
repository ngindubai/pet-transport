content:
  h1: "Pet Transport from Trinidad and Tobago to the Netherlands"
  overview: |
    Amsterdam Schiphol's dedicated Animal Hotel is a genuine advantage on the arrival end of this route, but getting there from Piarco still takes two flights, not one. British Airways is the only carrier flying nonstop out of Trinidad, and its destination is London, not continental Europe.

  sections:
    - heading: "The mandatory London transfer, then Schiphol"
      body: |
        With Caribbean Airlines having ended its only European service back in January 2016, British Airways through IAG Cargo is the sole nonstop option out of Piarco. From Gatwick, KLM Cargo picks up the connecting leg into Amsterdam, where the Air France-KLM-Martinair Cargo Animal Hotel handles the NVWA inspection on site rather than through general freight.

    - heading: "The EU titre test sequence"
      body: |
        Trinidad and Tobago sits outside the EU's list of low-risk third countries, so the Netherlands applies the standard sequence: microchip before vaccination, a 30-day gap before the titre blood draw, a result of at least 0.5 IU/ml, then a fixed 90 days from the draw date, under Commission Delegated Regulation (EU) 2026/131.

        The London connection can be arranged in parallel with that wait. It's the 90-day clock, not the transfer flight, that ultimately decides when the pet can travel.
