    Nicaragua does not ask for a titre test, so this route is lighter on paperwork than most of the reverse journeys on this site.   sections:
    - heading: "What Nicaragua actually asks for"
      body: |
        IPSA's general requirements for dogs and cats entering Nicaragua are shorter than the UK's own rules for the reverse direction. A rabies vaccination within the last 365 days, a recent health exam, parasite treatment, and a UK-issued export health certificate presented in original and copy form on arrival.

        A microchip is not mandatory by Nicaragua's own rules, though fitting one before travel is sensible, since it is the fastest way to confirm the animal matches its documents if any question comes up at the airport.

        The "quarantine permit" name causes more worry than it should. It is paperwork, not confinement. You, or whoever is meeting the pet at Augusto C. Sandino International Airport, obtains it at the port of entry once the health certificate has been checked. There is no kennel stay attached to it for a pet that arrives compliant.

    - heading: "Getting from the UK to Managua"
      body: |
        British Airways does not fly directly to Managua, and neither does any other UK carrier. The workable options both involve a Central American or US hub. American Airlines runs a daily Miami to Managua non-stop, which pairs naturally with a transatlantic leg into Miami. Copa's Panama City hub offers a second option that keeps the routing within Latin America for the final leg.

        Either way, book the cargo space on both legs before committing to a travel date. A crate cleared for the transatlantic flight still needs separate acceptance confirmation for the onward leg to Managua, and airlines do not automatically hold a connecting booking for live animal cargo the way they might for checked baggage.
