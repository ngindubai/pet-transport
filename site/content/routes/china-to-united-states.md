  h1: "Pet Transport from China to the USA"
  overview: |
    China is on the CDC's list of high-risk countries for dog rabies. That designation means the standard CDC Dog Import Form process is not enough: you also need a serological (antibody) test at a CDC-approved laboratory, and your dog must enter the USA at an airport with a CDC-registered animal care facility. Start planning at least three to four months before your target travel date.

    The good news for cat owners: US federal rules have no documentation requirements for cats arriving from China (though your airline will still need a health certificate, and Hawaii's state quarantine rules apply separately). For dogs, the GACC export process and the CDC high-risk protocol run in parallel. An agent who manages both sides is a significant advantage on this route.

    - heading: "The CDC serology test: what makes it different from standard CDC process"
      body: |
        For dogs from low-risk countries, the CDC process is relatively simple: a CDC Dog Import Form, a microchip, and appearing healthy on arrival. For dogs from China (a high-risk country), two additional requirements apply.

        First, a serological test: blood drawn by a vet at least 30 days after the rabies vaccination, tested at a CDC-approved laboratory, showing at least 0.5 IU/ml. This result is valid for the life of the dog provided vaccination coverage does not lapse and the laboratory remains CDC-approved. If your dog already has a passing result on file, you may not need a new test.

        Second, a reservation at a CDC-registered animal care facility at your US entry airport. The CDC publishes an approved list of airports. You must book your US arrival city based on this list, not based on which route is most convenient for your final US destination. The facility staff coordinate with CDC inspectors on arrival.

        Both requirements are firm. There is no appeal process or waiver for dogs from high-risk countries.

faqs:
  - question: "What makes China a high-risk country for CDC dog imports?"
    answer: "The CDC classifies countries as high-risk based on the presence of dog-mediated rabies. China has endemic dog-related rabies, which triggers the additional requirements: a serological test at a CDC-approved lab, and entry only at airports with CDC-registered animal care facilities. This applies regardless of where the dog was born or where it usually lives."
  - question: "Does my dog need to quarantine in the USA after arriving from China?"
    answer: "Not in the traditional sense. Dogs with valid serology results and correct documentation are cleared by CDC inspectors at the entry facility and released to the owner, typically within hours. If documentation is incomplete or the serology result is invalid, the dog may be held at the owner's expense while the issue is resolved. There is no fixed quarantine period for properly documented dogs from China."
  - question: "Do cats need any paperwork to enter the USA from China?"
    answer: "No federal documentation is required for cats entering the USA from China. However, your airline will require a health certificate, typically issued within 10 days of travel. If your US destination is Hawaii, the state's HDOA quarantine rules apply to cats and must be handled separately. For the continental US, cats from China have no federal entry requirements."
  - question: "Which US airports have CDC-registered animal care facilities?"
    answer: "The CDC publishes a current list on their website. Major transpacific entry points including Los Angeles (LAX), San Francisco (SFO), Seattle (SEA), New York JFK, and Chicago (ORD) typically have registered facilities. Always verify the current list before booking, as approvals can change. Your US entry airport must have a registered facility for dogs from high-risk countries."
  - question: "How much does the China to USA pet move cost?"
    answer: "For a medium-sized dog, expect total costs in the range of USD 3,500 to USD 8,000 depending on the dog's size, routing, and whether you use an agent. The serology test, GACC health certificate, IATA crate, transpacific cargo fees, and CDC facility reservation fee are the main components. Agent fees typically add USD 800 to USD 2,000 on top of direct costs."
