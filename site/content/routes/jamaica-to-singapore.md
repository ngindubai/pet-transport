content:
  h1: "Pet Transport from Jamaica to Singapore"
  overview: |
    Two separate governments have to sign off before a Jamaican pet lands in Singapore, and neither process waits for the other. Jamaica's Veterinary Services Division issues its own export permit, a step that has nothing to do with AVS and has to be started 8-12 weeks out regardless of anything else on this list. Singapore, for its part, classes Jamaica under Schedule III, the AVS tier for countries not named on its shorter Schedule I or Schedule II lists, confirmed directly against avs.nparks.gov.sg on 21 July 2026.

    Schedule III means a WOAH-prescribed titre test with a fixed 90-day wait from the blood draw, then 30 days at Singapore's Animal Quarantine Centre on arrival. Add a routing that has to change hands at London Heathrow, since nothing flies Kingston to Singapore directly, and this sits among the more demanding Caribbean-to-Southeast-Asia moves this site covers. Expect four and a half to five months from a standing start.

  sections:
    - heading: "The VSD permit: a Jamaica-specific step that has to come first"
      body: |
        Before booking a flight, ordering a crate or scheduling anything with a vet, apply for the VSD export permit. It's issued by the Veterinary Services Division under Jamaica's Ministry of Industry, Commerce, Agriculture and Fisheries, and the fee sits around JMD 2,000-5,000. This is separate from anything Singapore requires and runs on its own 8-12 week timeline.

        Not every Jamaican vet clinic is authorised by the VSD to issue export certificates. Confirm your chosen vet is on that list before the appointment, since a certificate from an unauthorised clinic won't clear either Jamaican customs or AVS's acceptance criteria on the Singapore end.

    - heading: "Schedule III and the 90-day titre clock"
      body: |
        Jamaica isn't named on AVS's Schedule I list of four countries or its 31-country Schedule II list, so it defaults to Schedule III. That triggers the WOAH rabies neutralising antibody test, blood drawn at least 28 days after vaccination, run at a laboratory the WOAH or a Schedule I/II country recognises, since Jamaica has none of its own on that list.

        The result is only valid for export between 90 days and 12 months after the blood draw. Owners who count from the day the lab emails a result, rather than the day blood was actually drawn, consistently underestimate their earliest travel date by several weeks. Run the AVS import licence application and the 30-day AQC quarantine booking during this wait, not after it.
