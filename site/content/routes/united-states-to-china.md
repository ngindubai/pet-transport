  overview: |
    The USA to China route involves the USDA-APHIS export process on the US side and GACC import requirements on the China side, including a titre test, proof of two lifetime rabies vaccinations, and the possibility of a 30-day quarantine on arrival. Budget four to six months for preparation from start to travel day.

    The quarantine risk is real but manageable. With a complete documentation set and a China-side agent who can pre-brief the GACC office at your arrival airport, many owners successfully avoid the 30-day hold. But this needs to be set up in advance, not sorted out on the day of arrival. The China-side logistics are where most problems occur on this route, so choosing the right agent is as important as getting the paperwork right.

    - heading: "GACC documentation: what must be in order before you travel"
      body: |
        GACC requires a specific documentation chain. If any element is missing or dated incorrectly, the outcome at arrival is unpredictable. Here is what must be in order:

        Microchip: 15-digit ISO, pre-dating all vaccinations. This is non-negotiable and easy to verify; do it first.

        Vaccinations: Two lifetime rabies vaccinations on record. Most recent within 12 months of arrival and at least 30 days before the titre test blood draw. If your dog has only had one lifetime vaccination, the second one resets the clock.

        Titre test: GACC-approved lab, FAVN method, 0.5 IU/ml result or above. Blood drawn at least 30 days after the most recent vaccination.

        Health certificate: GACC-accepted format, APHIS-endorsed, valid 14 days from examination to arrival. Your USDA-accredited vet prepares this; APHIS endorses it.

        GACC pre-clearance: Arranged through your China-side agent with the GACC office at your destination airport. Without this, even perfect documents do not guarantee a smooth arrival.

faqs:
  - question: "Will my dog definitely have to quarantine for 30 days when arriving in China from the USA?"
    answer: "Not necessarily. With a complete documentation set (two lifetime rabies vaccinations, valid titre test from a GACC-approved lab, ISO microchip, GACC-format APHIS-endorsed health certificate) and advance communication between your China-side agent and the GACC office at your destination airport, quarantine can often be avoided. It is not guaranteed, but owners who have prepared correctly and used an experienced agent frequently avoid the 30-day hold."
  - question: "What laboratories are GACC-approved for the titre test?"
    answer: "GACC publishes an approved laboratory list. Many of the labs accepted by CDC, APHIS, and major international veterinary authorities are also GACC-approved, but the lists do not overlap entirely. Confirm with your agent or the GACC website that your chosen lab is currently on the approved list before the blood draw, not after."
  - question: "How long does the USA to China pet move take from start to travel day?"
    answer: "Allow 16-24 weeks as a minimum. This includes the vaccination history check, titre test blood draw, titre test result waiting time (2-4 weeks), GACC pre-clearance arrangement, and cargo booking. If your vaccinations are not already current and in order, add another 4-6 weeks."
  - question: "Can I bring my cat to China from the USA without quarantine?"
    answer: "Cats are subject to the same GACC import requirements as dogs, including the microchip, vaccination, and titre test requirements. The 30-day quarantine risk applies to cats as well. With correct documentation, a waiver is possible. Cats do not face the city-level ownership limits that apply to dogs in many Chinese cities."
  - question: "Are any dog breeds banned from entering China?"
    answer: "China does not have a national breed ban list equivalent to some other countries, but individual cities have enacted their own restrictions. Beijing, Shanghai, and other major cities have local rules on breed size, weight, and in some cases specific breeds. Check the regulations for your specific destination city before assuming your breed is permitted."
