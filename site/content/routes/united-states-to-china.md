  h1: "Pet Transport from the United States to China"
  overview: |
    China is one of the most complex pet import destinations in the world for US owners. The GACC (General Administration of Customs) sets strict requirements: a GACC import permit, multiple lifetime rabies vaccinations, a FAVN titre test at a GACC-approved laboratory, and a mandatory 6-month wait from the blood draw date before the pet can enter China. After all of that, a 30-day quarantine at a GACC-designated facility applies on arrival for dogs.

    The US has GACC-approved laboratories for the titre test, which means the blood does not need to be shipped internationally for testing. USDA-endorsed health certificates are the standard export documentation from the US side. The GACC import permit process requires working with a licensed pet relocation specialist who knows the Chinese customs channels.

    A digital Customs Declaration via the GACC WeChat mini-programme or official GACC app must be submitted within 24 hours before landing in China. All pets must arrive at a GACC-designated entry airport.

    - heading: "Can dogs from the USA avoid quarantine in China?"
      body: |
        Some sources indicate that dogs from the USA with a valid titre test may avoid quarantine and enter China through any port. However, the situation under GACC regulations can change and the standard rule is a 30-day quarantine for dogs. The safest approach is to plan for the full quarantine period and confirm the current GACC position with your specialist at the time of travel.

        Cats from approved countries may qualify for a reduced 7-day quarantine. The list of approved countries is maintained by GACC and should be verified before travel.

        Quarantine facilities are operated by GACC at the designated entry airports. The owner pays all costs. Contact the facility in advance to understand current rates and conditions.
faqs:
  - question: "What is the minimum time to prepare my pet for transport from the USA to China?"
    answer: "The minimum is approximately 7-8 months. The process requires: confirming or establishing a vaccination history (at least two lifetime rabies vaccinations), drawing blood for the FAVN titre test (at least 30 days after the last vaccination), then waiting 6 months from the blood draw date. During that wait, the GACC import permit process is initiated. The health certificate is arranged within 10 days of travel. In practice, 8-9 months is a safer estimate to allow for paperwork delays."
  - question: "Which laboratories in the USA are approved by GACC for the titre test?"
    answer: "GACC maintains a list of approved laboratories. In the USA, Kansas State University Rabies Laboratory is one of the well-known GACC-approved labs. Confirm the current approved list with GACC or your China pet relocation specialist, as approved lab lists can change."
  - question: "Do cats from the USA also need a titre test for China?"
    answer: "Yes. The GACC titre test requirement applies to both dogs and cats. The 6-month wait after the titre test blood draw also applies to cats. Cats from certain approved countries may qualify for a reduced 7-day quarantine rather than the standard 30 days, but the titre test and wait period still apply."
  - question: "What does the GACC import permit involve?"
    answer: "The GACC import permit is applied for through official GACC channels or via a licensed pet relocation company with GACC authorisation. The permit confirms that your specific pet is approved for import into China. The process requires the pet's identification and vaccination details. A licensed specialist handles the application on your behalf and coordinates with GACC. Allow significant lead time for the permit alongside the 6-month titre test wait."
  - question: "What if my dog comes from a US state with breed restrictions that also apply in China?"
    answer: "China does not have a federal breed ban list comparable to some other countries. However, individual cities may have local restrictions. Check the regulations for your specific destination city before travel. The GACC import process itself does not distinguish between breeds for most dogs, but city-level rules can affect ownership after arrival."
