content:
  h1: "Pet Transport from Suriname to Canada"
  overview: |
    Suriname sits on CFIA's high-risk-for-dog-rabies list, last confirmed against the agency's own published table dated 13 March 2025. On paper that reads like a red flag. In practice, for a family dog or cat travelling with its owner, it changes almost nothing: no titre test, no import permit, just the standard microchip, vaccination and certificate checklist CFIA applies to most personal pets.

    The catch that does matter is the flight. Surinam Airways runs the only nonstop service out of Paramaribo, into Amsterdam, and every Canada-bound booking rides that single sector before a KLM or Air Canada connection finishes the job.

  sections:
    - heading: "What the high-risk listing does and doesn't do"
      body: |
        CFIA's high-risk list groups Suriname with most of South America. The instinct is to assume that means extra tests or a longer wait, the way it would with GOV.UK's unlisted-country rules or the CDC's high-risk pathway into the US.

        Canada draws the line differently. The high-risk designation is really about commercial dog imports, blocked outright from listed countries since September 2022. A personal pet, one or two animals travelling with their owner, sits outside that ban entirely and moves on CFIA's standard process.

    - heading: "One flight out of Suriname, then a choice of two into Canada"
      body: |
        Johan Adolf Pengel International, just outside Paramaribo, has exactly one nonstop link to anywhere outside South America: Surinam Airways' service into Amsterdam, flown on a leased Boeing 777. Everything from Suriname connects through that single sector first.

        From Amsterdam, both KLM and Air Canada run their own services into Toronto and Montreal, giving a genuine choice for the second leg rather than forcing a single carrier's schedule. That flexibility on the Canadian side is worth using, since there's none at all on the Suriname side.

    - heading: "Building the certificate for the right destination"
      body: |
        A Suriname vet who already issues Canada or UK certificates on other routes knows the general shape of the process. What changes is the format: CFIA's expectations aren't the same as GOV.UK's, and a certificate built for one destination doesn't automatically satisfy the other.

        Flag Canada clearly when booking the appointment, and confirm with the Directoraat Veterinaire Diensten what specifically needs to appear on a CFIA-facing document, since Suriname's own published English-language guidance on this point is thin.
