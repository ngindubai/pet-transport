  sections:
    - heading: "Pacific routing vs Gulf routing from Mexico City"
      body: |
        Mexico to Bangkok is a long-haul cargo move with no direct service. The two practical routing options are via a Pacific hub or via the Gulf.

        Pacific routing typically goes MEX to a US gateway (LAX, IAH, or JFK), then to Tokyo (NRT), Seoul (ICN), or Hong Kong (HKG), with a final connection to Bangkok Suvarnabhumi. Thai Airways, Cathay Pacific, and Korean Air Cargo all handle this routing. Tokyo and Hong Kong both have well-established live animal transit facilities.

        Gulf routing goes via Mexico to a US or European gateway, then to Doha (DOH) or Dubai (DXB), with a connection to Bangkok. Qatar Airways Cargo and Emirates Cargo both handle this well. Gulf hub transit for live animals is generally smooth at both DOH and DXB.

        The route you choose depends mainly on available cargo space and agent connections. Journey time from Mexico to Bangkok is typically 24-36 hours door-to-door across either routing.

    - heading: "Getting the DLD import permit from Mexico"
      body: |
        The Thailand DLD import permit is issued by the Animal Quarantine Station at the port of entry. For Bangkok, that is the Suvarnabhumi AQS. For Chiang Mai arrivals, it is the Chiang Mai AQS.

        Apply by email to the relevant AQS with your pet's microchip number, vaccination records, proposed travel date, and a completed application form (available from the DLD website or your destination agent). Processing takes 5-7 Thai business days once all documents are received. The permit is valid for 60 days from issue, so you have flexibility on the exact travel date once it is in hand.

        A Thailand-based pet transport agent can handle this application on your behalf and ensure the permit lists the correct entry airport.

    answer: "No. Thailand's current import rules do not require a titre test for dogs or cats.     answer: "Not routinely. If your import permit is valid and all documentation is in order, Thailand does not impose standard quarantine. However, the quarantine officer has discretion to detain any animal for up to 30 days if they consider it necessary. Complete and accurate documentation is the best protection. Source: Thailand DLD, 2026."
