content:
  h1: "Pet Transport from the United States to Tunisia"
  overview: |
    Rottweiler, Pit Bull, Tosa and Mastiff-type dogs cannot enter Tunisia at all, whatever paperwork a US owner brings. That single fact settles the question for some owners before timelines or costs matter. For every other pet, the move is comparatively light: a USDA-endorsed export certificate, a current rabies vaccination, and no titer test, since Tunisia treats the US as rabies-controlled.

    The flight is the part with no shortcut. Nothing connects Tunis-Carthage to a US airport directly, so every booking runs through Paris, Frankfurt or Rome first. Budget five to seven weeks from a standing start, most of that scheduling the two-carrier flight rather than clearing paperwork.

  sections:
    - heading: "Why the breed rule matters more than the timeline"
      body: |
        Tunisia's veterinary authority refuses entry to Pit Bull, Rottweiler, Tosa and Mastiff-type dogs and their crosses. It's a blanket prohibition, not a case-by-case review, and it applies whether the dog is coming from the US, the UK or anywhere else. Tunisair layers its own near-identical carriage ban on top, which matters here because Tunisair covers the final leg into Tunis on almost every realistic routing.

        A dog a US vet would describe loosely as a "pit mix" or "American Bully" needs a direct check against both lists before a flight or an APHIS appointment gets booked. There's no appeal process at the border. A dog turned away at Tunis-Carthage goes back to its point of origin at the owner's expense, which makes this the highest-stakes check on the whole route, well above anything vaccination-related.

    - heading: "The USDA side is genuinely simple"
      body: |
        A USDA-accredited vet issues the export certificate, endorsed electronically through APHIS eFile for around USD 38. No titer test, no waiting period beyond the standard 30-day window for a first rabies vaccination. Owners moving from a country with a longer, titer-driven process onto this route often find the US paperwork the easiest part of the whole move by some distance.

    - heading: "Routing through Europe, not around it"
      body: |
        Tunis-Carthage has no US service. Every pet on this route flies a US gateway into a European hub first: Paris on Air France, Frankfurt on Lufthansa, or occasionally Rome on Delta, then a Tunisair or partner-carrier connection into Tunis. That's two separate airline pet policies to confirm, not one, and a transfer window that needs to work for a live animal, not just a human connection.

        Build in enough time between legs. A tight connection that works fine for a passenger can fail for a crated pet if the ground handler needs longer to move it airside, particularly at a hub as busy as Paris CDG or Frankfurt.
