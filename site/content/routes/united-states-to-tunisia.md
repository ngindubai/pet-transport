content:
  h1: "Pet Transport from the United States to Tunisia"
  overview: |
    Rottweilers, Pit Bulls, Tosas and Mastiff-type dogs don't get past Tunis-Carthage. Tunisia's import ban on those breeds and their crosses is checked against current Tunisian customs guidance, and it applies before anything else on this route matters: paperwork, flights, crate size, none of it helps if the dog's type is on the list.

    For every other dog, and for cats, the process is comparatively short. A microchip, a current rabies vaccination, a USDA-endorsed international health certificate and a connecting flight through Paris, Frankfurt or Istanbul cover most of it. Six to nine weeks is realistic for a pet that's already vaccinated and microchipped, most of that spent on the certificate and endorsement timing rather than any waiting period.

  sections:
    - heading: "The breed ban, and why it matters before anything else"
      body: |
        Tunisia's prohibition on Rottweiler, Pit Bull, Tosa and Mastiff-type dogs is a national entry rule, not an airline policy, and it applies to crosses as well as purebreds. There's no waiver process at the airport. A dog turned away on landing becomes the owner's problem to solve on the spot, in a country where the pet has no legal right to stay.

        Get an honest read on a dog's breed and type before booking a vet appointment, a crate or a flight. If there's genuine doubt about a mixed-breed dog's classification, that's a conversation to have with a Tunisian-based agent or the DGSV directly, not a guess to make once the flight is booked.

    - heading: "USDA endorsement and the 10-day certificate window"
      body: |
        A USDA-accredited vet completes the international health certificate, which then needs APHIS endorsement through VEHCS before it's valid for travel. APHIS ink-signs and embosses the certificate even when the vet's part was done electronically, and the endorsement fee for a pet needing no laboratory tests currently sits at USD 101.

        The certificate is only good for 10 days between issue and entry into Tunisia. That's tight enough that the vet appointment, the endorsement, and the flight need to be booked as one connected sequence rather than three separate errands spread over a month.

    - heading: "No direct flights: routing through Europe or Istanbul"
      body: |
        There's no nonstop service between the US and Tunisia. Lufthansa, Air France and Turkish Airlines each fly directly into Tunis-Carthage from Frankfurt, Paris and Istanbul respectively, and each of those hubs connects to a wide range of US departure cities on the same carrier's long-haul network.

        Booking both legs with the same airline, rather than mixing carriers, generally makes it easier to keep live animal space confirmed on both the transatlantic leg and the short hop into Tunis. Whichever routing is chosen, arrange for the airport's veterinary post to expect the pet: Tunis-Carthage handles pet arrivals, but the DGSV vet on duty needs advance notice to inspect the paperwork on landing.
