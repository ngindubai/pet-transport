content:
  h1: "Pet Transport from India to the United Kingdom"
  overview: |
    India to the UK is one of the most document-intensive pet corridors in the world. India is not on the UK's Pet Travel Scheme approved list, which means pets from India must complete a full rabies antibody (titre) test process before they can enter Great Britain. This includes a FAVN titre test (blood drawn at least 30 days after the most recent valid rabies vaccination), a result of 0.5 IU/ml or above, and then a 90-day wait before travel. The total minimum process from start to finish is typically 5-6 months.

    The 90-day wait begins from the date the blood was drawn, not from the date the results were received. This distinction matters: if blood is drawn on day 1 and results take 3 weeks to come back, the 90-day clock has already been running. The pet can travel on day 91 from the blood draw date, assuming the titre result was satisfactory.

    British Airways, Air India, Emirates (via Dubai), and Qatar Airways all operate cargo on this corridor. All large pets travel as manifested cargo. An experienced pet transport specialist on this route is strongly recommended given the complexity and the consequences of a documentation error.

    - heading: "Which laboratory should I use for the FAVN titre test from India?"
      body: |
        The FAVN titre test for UK entry must be carried out at an APHA-approved laboratory. The current list of approved laboratories is maintained by APHA (Animal and Plant Health Agency) and published on GOV.UK. Laboratories may be based in Europe, the USA, or occasionally in the country of origin, but they must be on the APHA-approved list.

        Blood is drawn in India by your AQCS-authorised vet and sent to the approved laboratory. Results typically take 2-3 weeks. Keep a copy of the original result certificate: it must be presented to the APHA border vet at the UK entry point.

        If the first titre test result is below 0.5 IU/ml, the process must restart: re-vaccinate, wait 30 days, re-test, then wait another 90 days from the new blood draw date.
faqs:
  - question: "Why does India require a titre test for UK pet travel when other countries do not?"
    answer: "India has active rabies in its dog and wildlife population. The UK's Pet Travel Scheme lists countries where pets can enter without a titre test, based on the rabies risk that country presents. India is not on the UK's approved list, so all dogs and cats from India must complete the FAVN titre test process, including a 90-day wait after a passing result. This is a UK rule applied to all non-listed countries, not specific to India."
  - question: "What is the minimum time to prepare a pet for travel from India to the UK?"
    answer: "The minimum is approximately 5-6 months from the start of the process. The sequence is: microchip (if not already done), rabies vaccination (if not current), wait 30 days, draw blood for FAVN titre test, wait for results (2-3 weeks), then wait 90 days from the blood draw date. After the 90-day wait has passed, you can book travel and arrange the AHC within 10 days of travel."
  - question: "Which airlines carry pets in cargo from India to the UK?"
    answer: "British Airways (direct from Delhi, Mumbai, Bangalore, Chennai to Heathrow), Air India (cargo to LHR), Emirates via Dubai (cargo), and Qatar Airways via Doha (cargo) are the main options. All require booking through a cargo agent. Note that Emirates has summer heat embargoes at Dubai (June-September) which can restrict this routing option during those months."
  - question: "Does my cat from India also need a titre test to enter the UK?"
    answer: "Yes. The titre test and 90-day wait apply to both dogs and cats from India. The UK's non-listed country rules apply to all pet species, not just dogs. The process for cats is the same: microchip, rabies vaccination, titre test (blood draw 30+ days after vaccination), 90-day wait, then travel with AHC."
  - question: "What happens at Heathrow if my pet's documentation is not in order?"
    answer: "If a pet from India arrives without a satisfactory titre test result or without the completed 90-day wait, the pet may be placed in penalty quarantine (up to 120 days) at an APHA-authorised facility in the UK. The owner bears all quarantine costs. In some cases, the pet may be returned to India. Do not travel until the full process is complete."
