  h1: "Pet Transport from India to the UK"
  overview: |
    India to the UK requires working through two regulatory systems: the AQCS (Animal Quarantine and Certification Service) on the India export side, and APHA on the UK import side. Because India is not on the GB listed country schedule, a rabies titre test and a three-month waiting period from the blood draw date are mandatory. From start to travel day, allow at least five to seven months.

    British Airways and Virgin Atlantic both operate direct cargo services from Delhi and Mumbai to Heathrow, which means the routing itself is relatively straightforward. The complexity is in the documentation chain. An AQCS-authorised vet, the AQCS NOC, the titre test at an accepted laboratory, and the APHA Import Licence all need to come together in the right order. An agent who knows both the India and UK sides of this process is worth the fee.

    - heading: "The AQCS process: what it involves and why it matters"
      body: |
        The Animal Quarantine and Certification Service (AQCS) is the Indian authority that certifies pet exports. Before your pet can leave India, you need two things from AQCS: a No Objection Certificate (NOC) confirming the pet is cleared for export, and an AQCS-endorsed health certificate in the format required by the UK.

        The NOC is applied for at the AQCS office in your city. India has designated AQCS offices in major cities including Delhi, Mumbai, Chennai, Kolkata, Hyderabad, and Bengaluru. You need to present your pet's complete vaccination and microchip documentation when applying.

        The health certificate must be issued by a vet authorised by AQCS and then endorsed at the AQCS office. It is valid for 10 days from the examination date to the GB arrival date. Because of this short window, the health certificate is the last major document to be prepared, after everything else is in order.

        Allow at least 4-6 weeks to sort out the AQCS side of the process, especially if it is your first time dealing with AQCS. Different offices can have slightly different processing times.

    answer: "Yes, it is mandatory. The No Objection Certificate from the Animal Quarantine and Certification Service (AQCS) is the official Indian clearance for pet export. Without it, your pet cannot legally leave India as cargo. Apply at the AQCS office in your city with your pet's microchip confirmation and vaccination records."
  - question: "Does my pet need a titre test to travel from India to the UK?"
    answer: "Yes. India is not on the GB listed country schedule. A rabies antibody titre test is mandatory. Blood must be drawn at least 30 days after a valid rabies vaccination, the result must be at least 0.5 IU/ml, and a minimum of three calendar months must pass from the blood draw date before your pet can enter Great Britain."
  - question: "Can my pet fly in the cabin from India to the UK?"
    answer: "No. UK law requires all dogs, cats, and ferrets to enter Great Britain as manifested cargo. In-cabin and checked baggage pet travel are not permitted on any international route into the UK. Your pet will travel in the cargo hold with British Airways, Virgin Atlantic, or another approved carrier."
  - question: "Are there breed restrictions for pets entering the UK from India?"
    answer: "The UK's Dangerous Dogs Act restricts certain breeds including the Pit Bull Terrier, Japanese Tosa, Dogo Argentino, and Fila Brasileiro. These breeds cannot be imported into the UK regardless of origin. For other breeds, the standard documentation process applies."
