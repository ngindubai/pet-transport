content:
  h1: "Pet Transport from the United States to Ukraine"
  overview: |
    A rabies titre test drawn too close to the departure date is the most avoidable mistake on this route. Ukraine's requirement, per USDA guidance, is a blood draw at least three months before travel. Miss that window and the whole trip slips, because the wait cannot be compressed.

    The other fixed reality is geography. No flight lands in Ukraine. Airports there have taken no scheduled traffic since February 2022, so every move ends with a flight into Poland, Romania or Moldova, then a road crossing into Ukraine itself. It is not a difficult crossing if the paperwork is right, but it is an extra stage most routes don't require.

    Get the microchip, vaccination and titre test sequenced correctly from the US side, and the Ukrainian half of the process, no import permit, no routine quarantine, is genuinely straightforward by comparison.

  sections:
    - heading: "The three-month titre window that decides your timeline"
      body: |
        USDA APHIS guidance for this route sets the titre test at least three months before travel, drawn at an approved laboratory after the rabies vaccination has taken effect. Some independent pet travel advisories add an upper limit of roughly 12 months on how long that result stays valid, though this figure is not confirmed with equal consistency across sources, so treat it as a planning guide rather than a fixed rule until the State Service confirms it for your dates.

        Practically, this means working backwards from your target travel date. If your pet's rabies vaccination and microchip are already current, the three-month titre wait is usually the longest single stretch of preparation. Build in extra time rather than cutting it close, since a border crossing is a poor place to discover a certificate has expired.

    - heading: "Why the flight is only half the journey"
      body: |
        Ukrainian airspace closed to commercial traffic on 24 February 2022 and, despite a March 2026 working group preparing Boryspil, Lviv and Zhuliany for an eventual reopening, no scheduled service has resumed. AirBaltic has said it is ready to fly again once conditions allow, but that is a statement of intent, not a timetable.

        So the practical route is a flight to Warsaw, Bucharest, or another airport near Ukraine's western border, followed by a road crossing. Crossings such as Shehyni-Medyka and Krakovets-Korczowa handle regular pet traffic, and the US health certificate is exchanged for a Ukrainian international veterinary certificate at the checkpoint itself. Build 6 to 8 hours into your plan for the crossing, more at busy periods, and remember this stage happens after the certificate's validity clock has already started ticking.

    - heading: "What Ukraine does not ask for"
      body: |
        Compared with entering the United States from a high-risk country, Ukraine's own requirements for a US pet are relatively light. There's no import permit for a personal pet and no routine quarantine if the paperwork is correct. The State Service of Ukraine on Food Safety and Consumer Protection is the named authority, and its role at the border is largely a documentation check rather than a lengthy process.

        The genuine risk sits with the border crossing itself. These posts are not set up to fix a paperwork problem on the spot, so a missing endorsement or an early titre draw becomes a real obstacle a long way from any US consulate or accredited vet. Get every document verified before departure, not after.
