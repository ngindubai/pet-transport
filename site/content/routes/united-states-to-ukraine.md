content:
  h1: "Pet Transport from the United States to Ukraine"
  overview: |
    A flight will get your pet as far as Warsaw, Krakow, Rzeszow or a Romanian city. Getting into Ukraine itself takes a car, because civilian airspace there has been closed since Russia's invasion in February 2022 and no airline has a confirmed date to resume service. That single fact shapes almost everything else about this route.

    On the US side, the paperwork is the same well-worn USDA process used on most outbound routes: an accredited vet, an APHIS eFile endorsement costing roughly USD 38, done within days of travel. Ukraine's side is less forgiving. The country is not documented as offering any titre test exemption for pets arriving from rabies-free or low-risk nations, and the United States is not one of those nations in any case, so the full protocol applies without a shortcut.

  sections:
    - heading: "Why the flight only covers half the journey"
      body: |
        Ukraine's airspace closed to civilian traffic on 24 February 2022 and has stayed that way. EASA's Conflict Zone Information Bulletin covering Ukraine remains active, extended to at least 31 July 2026 as this is written, and while a Ukrainian government working group is planning a phased reopening starting with airports like Lviv, no carrier has a confirmed restart date.

        LOT Polish Airlines flies a daily nonstop from New York JFK into Warsaw, with pets accepted in the cabin under 8kg or via cargo above that. United and Lufthansa offer alternative first legs from other US gateways through Frankfurt or Munich. Whichever carrier gets your pet to Europe, the final stretch, commonly through Krakovets-Korczowa or Shehyni-Medyka near Lviv, has to be driven. Budget European carriers used on onward legs within Poland, Wizz Air among them, do not carry pets under any circumstances, so keep that off the itinerary entirely.

    - heading: "Ukraine's titre test: no shortcut for a US-origin pet"
      body: |
        Some countries drop the titre test for pets arriving from a rabies-free or officially low-risk nation. Ukraine's published guidance, as summarised by USDA APHIS, does not describe an equivalent exemption, and the point is moot here anyway: the United States isn't classified as dog-rabies-free, unlike the UK, so there was never a shortcut available on this particular route.

        That means the standard requirement stands in full. Blood drawn at least 30 days after the rabies vaccination, a result of at least 0.5 IU/ml, then a fixed 3-month wait counted from the draw date, not the day the lab confirms the result. Given how border administration has shifted in Ukraine since 2022, it's worth confirming the current position directly with Derzhprodspozhyvsluzhba, or with the specific border post you plan to cross at, before locking in travel dates.

    - heading: "The border exchange itself"
      body: |
        At the checkpoint, your USDA export certificate and titre results get exchanged for a Ukrainian international veterinary certificate, completed in both Ukrainian and English. This happens at the crossing, not beforehand, so build slack into the schedule: officials at busy posts can take six to eight hours to process a routine crossing, longer if the security situation adds delays that day.
