  h1: "Pet Transport from the USA to Ireland"
  overview: |
    Ireland is an EU member state, which means EU pet import rules apply to pets arriving from the USA. The USA is a third country for EU purposes. As of April 22, 2026, the EU introduced a new Animal Health Certificate format for pets arriving from non-EU countries. Any move from the USA to Ireland requires the USDA-APHIS health certificate to follow this new EU AHC template.

    The DAFM (Department of Agriculture, Food and the Marine) is Ireland's competent authority and manages EU Border Inspection Post checks at Dublin Airport. Ireland has no routine quarantine for correctly documented pets. Aer Lingus Cargo offers direct transatlantic services from several US cities to Dublin, making this one of the more accessible routes between the USA and the EU.

    - heading: "The new EU AHC format from April 2026: what changed"
      body: |
        From April 22, 2026, the EU introduced a new standardised Animal Health Certificate template for pets arriving from third countries (non-EU). This replaced the previous travel document format that had been in use since 2013.

        The new EU AHC format must be used for all pets entering EU member states from third countries, including the USA. If your USDA-accredited vet is using an older template, the certificate will not be accepted by DAFM staff at the Dublin Border Inspection Post.

        The USDA-APHIS endorsement process remains the same: your USDA-accredited vet issues the certificate, and APHIS provides the official stamp and endorsement. The key change is that the certificate template itself must match the new EU format. Confirm with your vet before the examination that they have the correct current template for Ireland/EU entry.

        The health certificate must be the original physical document. DAFM inspectors at the Dublin BIP require the original USDA-stamped AHC to accompany the animal. Electronic copies and photocopies are not accepted.
