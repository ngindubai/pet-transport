content:
  h1: "Pet Transport from Maldives to United Kingdom: 2026 Guide"
  overview: |
    There is no dog side to this guide. Dogs cannot be legally imported into the Maldives under Maldives Customs Service rules on prohibited items, so almost nobody living there owns one to bring home. Every genuine enquiry we get on this route is about a cat, usually belonging to a resort employee, a diplomat, or an expat finishing a posting in Male.

    The paperwork itself is not exotic. It is Great Britain's standard unlisted-country pathway: rabies vaccination, a blood titre test at least 30 days later, then a three-month wait before the cat can fly. What makes the Maldives version slower than, say, a European posting is logistics: the blood sample has to leave the island before anyone can even start the three-month clock.

  sections:
    - heading: "Why this route is a cat route"
      body: |
        The Maldives has banned dog imports for years. It is listed among prohibited items by the Maldives Customs Service, alongside pork products and alcohol, and the restriction is applied without exception for pet dogs. A handful of police sniffer dogs exist under government control, but they are not private pets and nobody is shipping them to London. If you are researching this route because you already have a dog on the island, that is a different and far more complicated conversation with the Maldivian authorities directly, not a standard export job.
    - heading: "Getting a cat off Velana International"
      body: |
        There is no formal pet export permit to apply for. A private vet in Male issues a health certificate confirming the cat is fit to fly and current on vaccines, and most airlines are satisfied with that plus the microchip record. The harder part is finding an agent who regularly books manifest cargo out of Male, since Velana International handles far more resort supply cargo than live pet shipments.
    - heading: "The GB blood test and the three-month wait"
      body: |
        Because the Maldives sits outside GB's list of low-rabies-risk countries, APHA requires a blood sample taken at least 30 days after the rabies vaccination, tested at a GB-approved laboratory, showing an antibody level of 0.5 IU/ml or above. Travel then cannot happen until three calendar months have passed from the date that sample was drawn, not the date the result comes back. Miss that detail and you will book flights before the cat is legally allowed to travel.
    - heading: "Why the lab result takes longer here than in Europe"
      body: |
        A cat vaccinated in Dublin or Berlin can have its blood tested at a lab a short courier run away. From Male, the sample generally has to be flown out, which adds a week or two most owners do not budget for. Confirm with your vet exactly which lab they use and how the sample will travel before you fix a rabies vaccination date around a house move deadline.
    - heading: "Booking the flight"
      body: |
        British Airways runs its Gatwick-Male route seasonally, and it is worth checking whether the dates line up before assuming a connecting Gulf routing is your only option. Outside that season, Qatar Airways via Doha and Emirates via Dubai are the two most established cargo paths, both requiring an IATA-spec crate and a licensed pet shipping agent rather than a same-day cargo drop-off.
