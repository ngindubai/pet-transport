content:
  h1: "Pet Transport from Hong Kong to Iceland"
  overview: |
    Hong Kong does not appear anywhere on MAST's published list of approved exporting countries, and that gap, not a missing vaccination, is what decides whether this move happens at all. MAST splits the world into Category 1 (rabies-free) and Category 2 (rabies-controlled) origins. Hong Kong sits in neither. The only way through is a narrow exception: the pet must have lived with its owner in Hong Kong for at least 6 months, and the owner must be genuinely relocating to Iceland.

    Clear that gate and the rest of the process runs to Category 2 rules, the same tier as the United States and Canada: titre test, a 90-day wait from the blood draw, and Iceland's mandatory 14-day quarantine on arrival. There is no direct flight either. Every routing goes through London.

  sections:
    - heading: "Why Hong Kong needs a different first question"
      body: |
        Ask most owners moving a pet internationally what comes first, and they say the vaccination schedule. For Hong Kong to Iceland, it's a different question: does this pet qualify to leave at all? MAST's approved list runs to roughly 40 countries across two categories, and Hong Kong isn't one of them.

        The workaround exists precisely for people in this situation, a resident of the origin country relocating permanently and bringing their own pet with them. It is not designed for a breeder, a rehoming case, or a pet bought and shipped without that residency history. Confirm the paperwork MAST wants to prove residency and ownership before spending a cent on vaccinations.
    - heading: "What Category 2 treatment actually involves"
      body: |
        Once MAST accepts the import, the health requirements mirror the US-to-Iceland route already covered on this site: microchip, rabies and leptospirosis vaccination, a titre test at least 30 days post-vaccination, and then a further 90-day wait from that blood draw before travel. None of it is shortened by the eligibility exception. It's the standard Category 2 schedule, just reached by a different door.

        The 14-day isolation stay at Reykjanesbae or Mosel sits on top of all of this and applies to every incoming pet without exception. Book the slot as soon as the projected travel window firms up, not once every other document clears.
    - heading: "Hong Kong to Keflavik in two legs"
      body: |
        No airline flies Hong Kong direct to Keflavik. Cathay Pacific runs Hong Kong to London Heathrow as its own long-haul cargo route, and from there Icelandair or British Airways covers the short hop to Keflavik. Two separate cargo bookings, two handling fees, and two sets of paperwork to keep in sync.

        Build in a buffer between the two legs. A delayed long-haul sector into Heathrow can miss a tightly booked connecting cargo slot to Iceland, and Icelandair Cargo capacity on that short route isn't unlimited.

    answer: "Yes, a mandatory minimum 14 days at Einangrunarstoedin i Reykjanesbae or Einangrunarstoedin Mosel. This applies to every pet entering Iceland regardless of origin or how the eligibility question was resolved."

    answer: "No. The practical routing is Cathay Pacific from Hong Kong to London Heathrow, then Icelandair or British Airways from Heathrow to Keflavik, both cargo only. Book each leg separately and allow a buffer between them."
