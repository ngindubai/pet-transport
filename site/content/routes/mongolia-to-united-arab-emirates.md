  h1: "Pet Transport from Mongolia to the United Arab Emirates"
  overview: |
    No airline flies Ulaanbaatar to Dubai direct. That's the first fact worth knowing. The second is that a workable route exists anyway: MIAT or Turkish Airlines to Istanbul, then Turkish Airlines' own daily Istanbul-Dubai service, a 4.5-hour sector run 14 times a week. It isn't a single nonstop flight, but it is a genuinely simple two-leg journey rather than a scattered multi-hub itinerary.

    The regulatory side asks the standard MOCCAE questions of a high-risk origin. Mongolia sits outside the roughly 50-country low-risk list, so a titre test applies, though without the fixed multi-month wait the UK or Australia would impose on the same pet. Breed matters more here than the paperwork does: MIAT's 18-breed brachycephalic exclusion list and Turkish's blanket flat-faced ban between them rule out cabin-or-hold hold transport for a lot of dogs many owners don't think of as flat-faced.

  sections:
    - heading: "Why Istanbul, not Beijing or Seoul?"
      body: |
        Ulaanbaatar's direct international network points mainly at Seoul, Frankfurt and Istanbul. Of those, Istanbul is the one that actually connects onward to Dubai on a daily basis, courtesy of Turkish Airlines' well-established 4.5-hour Istanbul-Dubai sector, flown 14 times a week under flight numbers like TK760 and TK762.

        Beijing looks geographically tempting, and Seoul offers more weekly Mongolia departures overall, but neither city has the same daily onward Dubai service that Istanbul does. For a UAE-bound pet, Istanbul is simply the more direct chain of connections, even without a single nonstop flight covering the whole journey.

    - heading: "Does Mongolia get the MOCCAE titre test, and does it come with a wait?"
      body: |
        Yes to the test, no to a fixed wait. Mongolia isn't on MOCCAE's roughly 50-country low-risk list, the one that includes places like Montenegro, so a rabies antibody titre test applies: blood drawn at least 30 days after vaccination, result of 0.5 IU/ml or above, valid for a year provided vaccination cover doesn't lapse.

        What MOCCAE doesn't add is the EU or UK-style multi-month waiting period after that result clears. Once the titre passes and the import permit is confirmed, the pet can travel. Lab turnaround, not a government-imposed clock, is what actually sets the pace on this leg.

    - heading: "Breed restrictions could rule out both realistic carriers"
      body: |
        This is the part of the route that deserves more attention than the paperwork. MIAT excludes 18 breeds from its cargo hold, covering the obvious ones plus some owners don't expect, such as Cane Corso and Mastiff. Turkish Airlines goes further for its own network, refusing brachycephalic breeds in both cabin and hold since October 2021.

        For a flat-faced dog under 5kg, MIAT's cabin option might still work. For anything larger, there may be no realistic carrier on this specific corridor. Check current breed policy with both airlines directly before committing to travel dates, since this is a bigger constraint on this route than the titre test or the import permit ever will be.
