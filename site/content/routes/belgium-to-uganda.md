    - heading: "Brussels Airlines BRU to Entebbe"
      body: |
        Brussels Airlines operates East Africa services from Brussels Zaventem (BRU), with Entebbe International Airport (EBB) among its established African destinations. For Belgian pet owners, the BRU-EBB routing reduces the total journey compared with reaching Entebbe via a more distant European hub.

        Contact Brussels Airlines Cargo at BRU to confirm live animal acceptance on the BRU-EBB service, applicable crate sizes, and any route-specific conditions. If live animal cargo is not available on your preferred dates, Ethiopian Airlines via Addis Ababa (ADD) is the reliable fallback: Ethiopian Cargo handles extensive African live animal freight and ADD-EBB is a short East African connection.

        Kenya Airways via Nairobi (NBO) is a third option. NBO-EBB is a short regional hop within East Africa. Getting from Brussels to Nairobi involves a Gulf or European connection, but Kenya Airways is a well-established carrier on the NBO-EBB segment.

    - heading: "MAAIF: Uganda's import permit process"
      body: |
        Uganda's Ministry of Agriculture, Animal Industry and Fisheries (MAAIF) at maaif.go.ug manages companion animal imports. The MAAIF import permit authorises your pet's entry and must be obtained before travel. Apply with your pet's microchip number, species, breed, vaccination history, and intended travel date.

        Allow 8-10 weeks for the permit process. Follow up after submitting to confirm your application is in progress. MAAIF officers at Entebbe International Airport inspect the permit alongside the FAVV/AFSCA health certificate. Without the permit, Uganda's entry cannot be cleared.

        Do not finalise cargo bookings until the MAAIF permit is confirmed. Permit processing times can vary, and the permit governs the travel date.

    - heading: "FAVV/AFSCA export health certificate"
      body: |
        Belgium's EU pet passport is valid for intra-EU travel only and will not be accepted at Entebbe. The correct document for Uganda is an official export health certificate issued by a FAVV/AFSCA-authorised official veterinarian and endorsed by FAVV.

        Book a pre-export examination with a FAVV-authorised official vet. They check the ISO microchip, vaccination records, and health condition, then issue the certificate. FAVV endorses it, typically within 2-3 working days. The endorsed certificate must be issued within 10 days of travel, so plan the vet appointment and FAVV endorsement to fall within that 10-day window.

        Brussels Zaventem is one of Europe's major cargo hubs, and FAVV processes export health certificates routinely for African destinations.
