  sections:
    - heading: "Georgia's GNFA and the import permit"
      body: |
        The Georgian National Food Agency (GNFA) at gnfa.gov.ge manages companion animal imports. The import permit from GNFA is the entry requirement for your pet. Apply in advance with your pet's details: microchip number, vaccination history, breed, species, and travel date.

        Georgia has a straightforward relationship with EU-origin pets. The country does not require the FAVN titre test for pets arriving from EU member states. Current rabies vaccination, ISO microchip, and the Italian health certificate are the core documentation alongside the GNFA permit. The GNFA inspection team at Tbilisi International Airport (TBS) checks documents on arrival.

        Confirm with GNFA at the time of application what format the Italian health certificate should follow. Some countries have specific requirements for the certification format; GNFA can confirm what they expect for Italy-origin movements.

    - heading: "Istanbul as the transit hub"
      body: |
        Turkish Airlines connects both Rome Fiumicino (FCO) and Milan Malpensa (MXP) to Istanbul Ataturk (IST) with regular frequency. From IST, Turkish Airlines operates multiple daily services to Tbilisi (TBS). Turkish Airlines Cargo handles live animal freight on this corridor and IST is well set up as a transit point for Caucasus-bound cargo.

        The FCO-IST-TBS routing takes approximately 6-9 hours total, depending on connection time at Istanbul. This is a comfortable transit for healthy adult dogs and cats in compliant crates.

        Georgian Airways, Georgia's national carrier, operates services from various European cities and may have options from Rome or Milan. Contact Georgian Airways Cargo to confirm whether their Italy routes include live animal cargo acceptance.

    - heading: "The Italian ASL process explained"
      body: |
        Italy's export health certificate for non-EU destinations is issued by a veterinario ufficiale at your local ASL (Azienda Sanitaria Locale), the regional health authority. The EU pet passport is not valid for Georgia and should not be presented as the export document.

        To get the certificate, book an appointment at your local ASL veterinary department. The official vet examines your pet, checks the microchip and vaccination record, and issues the certificato sanitario per l'esportazione. This document is then endorsed by the relevant authority.

        The certificate must be issued within 10 days of travel. Contact your ASL 3-4 weeks before travel to check appointment availability and the endorsement turnaround time. ASL offices vary in how quickly they can process endorsements.
