content:
  h1: "Pet Transport Dominican Republic to Sweden: 2026 Guide"
  overview: |
    Sweden skips one requirement that trips up owners moving pets into its Nordic neighbours. Norway and Finland both mandate a praziquantel tapeworm treatment before entry; Sweden does not, because Echinococcus multilocularis is already present in the country's own fox population, so the pre-border treatment other Nordic states apply serves no purpose there. What Sweden does still require, in common with every EU member state, is the full unlisted-country sequence for a Dominican Republic-origin pet: a titre test and a 90-day wait.

  sections:
    - heading: "The tapeworm treatment Sweden doesn't ask for"
      body: |
        This detail catches out plenty of owners planning a Scandinavian move, since Norway and Finland both require dogs to receive a praziquantel treatment in a specific pre-arrival window, and it's easy to assume the rule applies across the whole region. It doesn't. Sweden's own fox population already carries Echinococcus multilocularis, so the pre-entry treatment that keeps it out of Norway and Finland has no equivalent purpose in Sweden. Regular deworming remains good practice once the pet is settled, but it is not a border requirement.

    - heading: "The FAVN titre test still applies in full"
      body: |
        Sweden, like every EU member state, splits non-EU countries into those with assessed rabies-control equivalence and those without. The Dominican Republic sits in the unassessed group, so the standard sequence applies: microchip, vaccination, a blood draw at least 30 days later, a result of at least 0.5 IU/ml, then a 90-day wait from that draw date. None of it moves faster for an urgent travel date, and the wait counts from the draw, not the result.

    - heading: "Getting from Santo Domingo to Stockholm"
      body: |
        No direct service runs this route, so pets travel via Madrid with Iberia, which offers a cabin option for smaller pets on the transatlantic leg, or via Amsterdam with KLM. SAS handles the final connection into Stockholm Arlanda on either routing, and Arlanda is Sweden's main international cargo airport.
