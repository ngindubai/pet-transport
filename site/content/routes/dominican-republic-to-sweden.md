content:
  h1: "Pet Transport Dominican Republic to Sweden: 2026 Guide"
  overview: "Dominican Republic to Sweden requires the full EU unlisted country process. The Dominican Republic is not on the EU listed country register, so the FAVN titre test (blood drawn at least 30 days after vaccination, result 0.5 IU/ml or above at an EU-approved laboratory) and a mandatory 90-day wait from the blood draw date are both compulsory before entry. Total preparation is 20-26 weeks. Sweden does not require a tapeworm treatment for dogs, which keeps the arrival process straightforward once the titre test requirements are met. The DIGEGA authority in Santo Domingo endorses the export health certificate, and Jordbruksverket oversees import at Stockholm Arlanda."

  sections:
    - heading: "Sweden's import requirements for pets from the Dominican Republic"
      body: |
        Sweden follows EU pet movement rules. For pets arriving from unlisted third countries such as the Dominican Republic, Jordbruksverket applies the requirements of Commission Delegated Regulation (EU) 2026/131.

        The requirements are: a microchip implanted before the rabies vaccination; a current rabies vaccination with 21 days elapsed since the primary dose; a FAVN titre test at an EU-approved laboratory with a result of 0.5 IU/ml or above, blood drawn at least 30 days after vaccination; a 90-day wait from the blood draw date; and an EU Annex IV non-commercial health certificate issued within 10 days of travel.

        Sweden does not impose a tapeworm treatment requirement on dogs arriving from the Caribbean. For direct Dominican Republic-to-Sweden movements, this step does not apply. Border inspection at Stockholm Arlanda Airport (ARN) is standard. The Jordbruksverket inspector checks the health certificate, microchip, vaccination record, and titre test documentation. Compliant paperwork makes this a routine check.

    - heading: "The FAVN titre test from the Dominican Republic"
      body: |
        FAVN testing requires an EU-approved laboratory, and most approved facilities are in Europe, North America, or Canada. From the Dominican Republic, your veterinarian draws the blood, stores it in the correct preservative medium, and ships it by international biomedical courier.

        ANSES in France (lda.anses.fr) and Kansas State University Rabies Laboratory in the US are commonly used for Caribbean samples. Ask the laboratory explicitly whether they accept samples from the Dominican Republic and what their shipping requirements are. Allow two to four weeks from the blood draw to receive the result.

        The 90-day mandatory wait starts from the blood draw date, not from when the result arrives. If the titre test passes on day 14, the wait still runs to day 90 from the blood draw. Starting the microchip and vaccination process early is the only way to compress the total preparation timeline.

    - heading: "Routing from Santo Domingo to Stockholm"
      body: |
        Las Americas International Airport (SDQ) in Santo Domingo has cargo connections to European hubs via Iberia and Air France. The most practical routing is SDQ to Madrid (MAD) via Iberia, then MAD to Stockholm Arlanda (ARN) via SAS or Iberia cargo. SAS Scandinavian Airlines has its main hub at Copenhagen but operates cargo services from Madrid to ARN.

        From Paris CDG, Air France or SAS connect to Stockholm. From Frankfurt, Lufthansa or SAS have ARN connections. SDQ is a moderately well-connected Caribbean airport for cargo, with Iberia as the primary European carrier. Book cargo space early for the ARN leg, where live animal capacity can be limited outside of peak periods.

    - heading: "DIGEGA export documentation from the Dominican Republic"
      body: |
        The Direccion General de Ganaderia (DIGEGA), within the Ministerio de Agricultura (agricultura.gob.do), endorses companion animal export health certificates from the Dominican Republic. A licensed veterinarian prepares the EU Annex IV format certificate, which must include the titre test result (blood draw date, laboratory name, result in IU/ml), the vaccination record, and the microchip number.

        DIGEGA reviews and endorses the document. Allow three to five business days for this step. The health certificate must be issued within 10 days of travel. Do not have it prepared earlier than 10 days before departure.

faqs:
  - question: "Does my dog need tapeworm treatment to enter Sweden from the Dominican Republic?"
    answer: "No. Sweden does not require Echinococcus treatment for dogs arriving from the Caribbean. If your journey involves a stop in Norway or Finland, those countries require it, but for a direct Dominican Republic-to-Sweden move, no tapeworm treatment is needed."
  - question: "Can I enter Sweden through Gothenburg instead of Stockholm?"
    answer: "Yes. Gothenburg Landvetter Airport (GOT) has a Jordbruksverket border inspection post and accepts international pet arrivals. If your cargo routing makes GOT more convenient, confirm with Jordbruksverket that the specific arrival will be inspected."
  - question: "Which EU-approved laboratory should I use for the FAVN test from the Dominican Republic?"
    answer: "The most commonly used EU-approved laboratories for Caribbean samples are ANSES in France (lda.anses.fr), the Animal and Plant Health Agency (APHA) in the UK, and Kansas State University Rabies Laboratory in the US. Contact the laboratory before drawing blood to confirm they accept samples from the Dominican Republic and to get their specific shipping instructions."
  - question: "What if the titre test result comes back below 0.5 IU/ml?"
    answer: "A result below 0.5 IU/ml means the vaccination has not produced sufficient antibody response for EU entry. Your vet must revaccinate the animal, a new 30-day wait must elapse, a new blood draw arranged, and the 90-day mandatory wait restarts from the new draw date. This adds several months to the timeline, which is why starting as early as possible matters."
