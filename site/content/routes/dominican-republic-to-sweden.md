  overview: "Moving a dog or cat from the Dominican Republic to Sweden involves two distinct hurdles: the FAVN titre test with its mandatory 90-day waiting period, and the requirement to arrive at a designated Swedish Border Control Post. The Dominican Republic is classified as an unlisted third country under EU Implementing Regulation 2026/636, so the titre test cannot be bypassed.   sections:
    - heading: "FAVN titre test and the 90-day wait"
      body: |
        The FAVN titre test is the defining constraint on this route. Swedish rules, set by Jordbruksverket (Swedish Board of Agriculture), require that pets from unlisted countries including the Dominican Republic carry a passing FAVN result before the health certificate can be issued. The blood sample must be drawn at least 30 days after the most recent rabies vaccination, and the test must show a neutralising antibody level of at least 0.5 IU/ml.

        The 90-day waiting period then begins from the blood draw date. This is not the date the result arrives; it is the date the blood was collected. Jordbruksverket states clearly that this wait cannot be shortened by any exemption. Your pet's health certificate cannot be issued until after those 90 days have elapsed. The health certificate is then valid for 10 days from the DIGEGA endorsement date to the documentary checks at Arlanda or Landvetter.

        Book the FAVN blood draw at a vet in the Dominican Republic who works with an EU-approved laboratory. Shipping the blood sample to an approved lab in Europe adds a few days; plan accordingly. Once you have a passing result, keep the original laboratory report as it must accompany the health certificate throughout the journey.

    - heading: "Routing from Santo Domingo to Stockholm or Gothenburg"
      body: |
        There is no direct flight from Santo Domingo (SDQ) to Sweden. The most practical route for pet cargo is via Madrid (MAD): Iberia and Air Europa operate direct SDQ-MAD services, and from Madrid both Iberia and SAS connect to Stockholm Arlanda (ARN) and Gothenburg Landvetter (GOT).

        If travelling via Amsterdam (AMS), KLM's well-established live animal cargo operations at Schiphol can handle connections to Swedish airports. Routing via North American hubs (Miami, New York) with onward connections to Stockholm is also an option but adds handling steps. Fewer transfers reduce the risk of documentation or routing errors.

        Sweden's designated entry points for non-EU pets are Arlanda and Landvetter only. If your routing passes through another EU country first (for example, a connection at Frankfurt or Amsterdam), the border veterinary checks happen at that first EU entry point, not in Sweden. Confirm with your cargo agent which airport handles the entry checks for your specific itinerary.

faqs:
  - question: "Why does the Dominican Republic require a titre test for Sweden when some other countries do not?"
    answer: "Sweden applies EU pet travel rules, which classify third countries as either listed or unlisted based on their rabies control status. Listed countries (such as the USA and Jamaica) are exempt from the FAVN titre test. The Dominican Republic is classified as unlisted under EU Implementing Regulation 2026/636, so the titre test and 90-day wait are mandatory for all pets entering Sweden from there."
  - question: "Can my pet enter Sweden through Copenhagen airport in Denmark?"
    answer: "If your routing involves a connection at Copenhagen (CPH), the entry checks would happen in Denmark, not Sweden. Each EU country has its own designated Border Control Posts. If you want your pet to be cleared at a Swedish entry point, book a routing that arrives directly at Arlanda (ARN) or Landvetter (GOT) from outside the EU."

    answer: "Allow at least 20-26 weeks from the initial microchip and vaccination to travel day. The timeline is: chip and vaccinate, wait 30 days minimum, draw blood for FAVN, wait 90 days from that blood draw date, then issue the certificate and travel within 10 days. Any delays in getting a passing FAVN result or in booking cargo push the timeline further out."
  - question: "What does the DIGEGA export health certificate cover?"
    answer: "The DIGEGA health certificate is issued by a DIGEGA-authorised official veterinarian in the Dominican Republic and endorsed by DIGEGA (Dirección General de Ganadería). It records the pet's microchip number, vaccination history, and FAVN titre result. It must be issued within 10 days of the travel date and is the document Jordbruksverket inspectors check at the Swedish Border Control Post."
