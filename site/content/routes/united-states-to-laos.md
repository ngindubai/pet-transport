content:
  h1: "Pet Transport from United States to Laos: 2026 Guide"
  overview: |
    A dog or cat leaving the US for Laos skips the titre test entirely. Laos's Department of Livestock and Fisheries reserves that requirement for pets coming from countries it rates as high-rabies-risk, and the US isn't one of them. What replaces it is a narrower but still exacting rule: the rabies vaccination has to sit inside a 30-day-to-12-month window before the travel date.

    Add a DLF import permit and a USDA-endorsed health certificate, and a US-to-Laos move runs on paperwork timing and connecting-flight logistics rather than medical waiting periods. Six to twelve weeks is realistic for most owners.

  sections:
    - heading: "The vaccination window that catches owners out"
      body: |
        Because Laos doesn't require a titre test from a low-risk country like the US, it's tempting to treat this route as simple. The catch is the vaccination timing rule: rabies vaccination given no earlier than 30 days before travel and no later than 12 months before it. A pet vaccinated at its annual check-up eighteen months ago doesn't qualify, and neither does one vaccinated two weeks before a booked flight.

        Plan the vaccination date around the intended travel window from the start, rather than working backward from whatever vaccination record already exists. It avoids a repeat dose and the delay that comes with it.

    - heading: "Seoul, Ho Chi Minh City or Bangkok: the three realistic connections"
      body: |
        No airline flies non-stop from the US to Vientiane, so a connection is unavoidable. Korean Air's Seoul Incheon hub reaches a wide spread of US gateways and connects into Lao Airlines' daily Seoul-Vientiane service, making it the most flexible option for most departure cities.

        Vietnam Airlines' San Francisco to Ho Chi Minh City service is the only nonstop flight between the US mainland and Vietnam, which makes it worth considering for West Coast departures, with Lao Airlines covering the short final leg into Vientiane. Thai Airways via Bangkok remains a solid third option with the widest US gateway coverage of the three.
