content:
  h1: "Pet Transport from Russia to Australia"
  overview: |
    Fourteen months is a realistic floor for this move, and that's before anything goes wrong. Russia sits outside Australia's list of DAFF-approved countries, in the same bracket as Kazakhstan, Armenia and Azerbaijan. A pet cannot go straight from Moscow to Melbourne under any circumstances. It has to live in an approved country first, most commonly the UK, Germany or France, and complete the entire Australian preparation process there.

    What makes the Russia version of this route distinct is a second layer most transit-country guides don't have to deal with. Getting a pet from Russia to that transit country at all now requires the same Istanbul or Dubai stopover that every other Russia-to-West route in this build needs, because of the airspace closures both sides imposed after February 2022. Two long, independent problems, stacked rather than shared.

  sections:
    - heading: "Why Russia can't send a pet straight to Australia"
      body: |
        DAFF assesses each country of origin against its own disease control record before granting approved status, and Russia has not received it. That puts it alongside Kazakhstan, Armenia and Azerbaijan, all of which need the same workaround: relocate the pet to an approved country, complete DAFF's full preparation there, then make the final Australia leg from that country's veterinary authority, not Russia's.

        Confirm this directly at agriculture.gov.au before spending money on vaccinations or flights. Country classifications are reviewed periodically, and this page cannot replace checking the live BICON listing for Russia.

    - heading: "The first bottleneck: getting out of Russia at all"
      body: |
        Long before DAFF's requirements come into play, there's the same problem every Russia-to-West route in this build shares. Russian airspace has been closed to UK, EU and allied aircraft since February 2022, and UK, EU and allied sanctions keep Russian carriers out of their airspace in return. Turkey sits outside both bans, which makes Istanbul the default connection for reaching London, Frankfurt or Paris. Dubai works too, and has become more viable since Aeroflot resumed its Moscow-Dubai service on 1 June 2026, moving to twice daily a month later.

        Either way, this is a two-leg booking with its own risk of a missed connection, well before the Australian side of the process even starts.

    - heading: "Choosing the transit country, and what each path costs in time"
      body: |
        The UK (Group 2) skips the 180-day wait after the titre test blood draw, which is the single biggest time saver available. Via the UK, expect roughly 15 to 18 months from the first vaccination in the transit country to Australian arrival. Via Germany or France (Group 3), the 180-day wait adds around six months on top, closer to 21 to 24 months in total.

        If you are relocating to one of these countries for other reasons anyway, the extra months are absorbed into ordinary life there rather than felt as dead time. If the choice is open, the UK route is faster on paper, though it depends on your own visa and residency situation, not just the pet's paperwork.

    - heading: "The DAFF permit, Mickleham, and what to lock down before booking a flight"
      body: |
        The BICON import permit application, AUD 420 and non-refundable, is issued against the transit country, not Russia, and takes 20 to 30 working days. Apply during the waiting period rather than at the last minute. Mickleham quarantine space in Melbourne must be pre-booked before you buy the final flight; the 10-day stay is fixed and paid for by the owner, currently around AUD 1,877 to AUD 2,400.

        Given how many moving parts this route has, both the Russian exit and the Australian import ends benefit from a single agent managing both legs rather than two separate arrangements that only meet at the transit country's border.

    answer: "No, for two separate reasons. Russia is not on Australia's DAFF-approved country list, so a direct import isn't accepted regardless of paperwork. Separately, there's no direct flight between Russia and Australia at all, so every booking runs through a third country such as Turkey or the UAE."
  - question: "Which transit country is fastest for a Russia-to-Australia move?"
    answer: "The UK, because Group 2 countries skip the 180-day wait that applies after a Group 3 country's titre test. Via the UK, allow roughly 15 to 18 months in total. Via Germany or France, the 180-day wait adds around six months, closer to 21 to 24 months."
  - question: "Does my pet quarantine on arrival in Australia?"
    answer: "Yes. Every pet entering Australia completes a mandatory 10-day stay at the Mickleham Post Entry Quarantine Facility in Melbourne, currently costing roughly AUD 1,877 to AUD 2,400, paid by the owner. All pets arrive via Melbourne regardless of their final Australian destination."
  - question: "Why does this route take so much longer than a typical Russia-to-West move?"
    answer: "Two problems stack rather than overlap. Once there, the pet still has to complete Australia's full non-approved-country process from scratch, which is where most of the 65 to 100 week timeline comes from."
