    Of the three UAE routes built in this batch, this is the only one with a genuinely uncomplicated flight.   sections:
    - heading: "Why is the flight itself the easy part of this route?"
      body: |
        Emirates operates a direct Luanda-Dubai service three times a week. That's a single leg, one carrier, and none of the geopolitical uncertainty that shadows the Algeria-Dubai route built alongside this one, where Algeria's move to end its air services agreement with the UAE puts the whole corridor at risk within a couple of years.

        Ethiopian Airlines, via Addis Ababa, is a solid second option if Emirates' three-weekly schedule doesn't line up with a preferred travel date, though it adds a connection the direct Emirates flight doesn't need.

    - heading: "Does the titre test still apply, given Angola isn't a MOCCAE low-risk country?"
      body: |
        Yes. Angola sits outside MOCCAE's roughly 50-country low-risk list, the same list Albania is on, so a rabies antibody titre test at an accredited laboratory is required, blood drawn at least 30 days after vaccination with a result of at least 0.5 IU/ml. As with the other MOCCAE routes on this site, no further fixed wait applies once that result passes, a genuine contrast with the EU's or UK's three-month rule.

        Lab turnaround, not a government-imposed clock, is what actually sets the pace here, particularly since the sample is likely to travel to an accredited facility outside Angola.

    - heading: "So what actually makes this route take 12 to 16 weeks?"
      body: |
        Mostly Angola's own side of the process. Pet exports out of Luanda are uncommon enough that a standard ISV certificate format isn't well established the way it is for busier corridors, so budget extra time to confirm exactly what the vet appointment needs to produce, rather than assuming a routine visit will cover it.

        Once that certificate and the MOCCAE permit are both in hand, the flight itself is the most straightforward part of the whole journey, a real change of pace from the multi-hub routings this site's other Angola pages describe.
