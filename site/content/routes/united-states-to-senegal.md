content:
  h1: "Pet Transport from United States to Senegal"
  overview: |
    Senegal's own paperwork rule does something most destinations don't: it skips the standard USDA federal endorsement wait. If your vet is USDA-accredited and notes their national accreditation number on APHIS Form 7001, that certificate doesn't need to go to a federal office for a separate endorsement stamp, the 3 to 5 business day step that slows down many other routes.

    That saved time doesn't mean the process is simple, just lighter on one particular bottleneck. Senegal still requires its own Import Authorisation, applied for directly by email to the Directorate of Veterinary Services rather than through any online system, and it's only valid for 3 months once granted.

    Delta flies direct from New York to Dakar, which is the first thing most owners find when they search this route. For nearly everyone outside military or State Department orders, that flight's cargo hold isn't actually available, in either direction.

  sections:
    - heading: "No APHIS endorsement wait, if your vet ticks one box"
      body: |
        Most international pet moves from the US involve a mandatory step: a USDA-accredited vet completes the health certificate, then it goes to a USDA APHIS federal veterinarian for endorsement, typically adding 3 to 5 business days. Senegal is an exception. If the vet who signs APHIS Form 7001 is USDA-accredited and their national accreditation number appears on the form, no separate federal endorsement is required at all.

        It's a real time saving, but it hinges on one administrative detail. Confirm your vet knows to include their accreditation number on the form itself; skip that and you're back to the standard endorsement process, with its added wait, by default.

    - heading: "Applying for Senegal's Import Authorisation by email"
      body: |
        Rather than a fee-paying government portal, Senegal's entry permit for pets is requested by emailing the Directorate of Veterinary Services directly, providing details of the pet, the owner, and the planned entry. It's a genuinely informal-looking process for what is, in practice, a mandatory government authorisation.

        The permit runs for 3 months from the date it's issued. Apply once your flight is realistically fixed. A permit granted too early against a date that later slips is a permit you may have to request again.

    - heading: "Delta flies direct. Most owners still can't book a pet on it"
      body: |
        DL216 and DL217 connect New York JFK and Dakar several times a week on a Boeing 767, a genuinely useful option if you're travelling yourself. The pet cargo side of that same service is currently limited to active US military and State Department personnel under PCS orders, in both directions.

        For everyone else, Air France via Paris is the more workable choice, with an established cargo pet programme open to the public. Turkish Airlines via Istanbul and Brussels Airlines via Brussels, capped at 4 pets per Brussels-Dakar flight, are the realistic alternatives.
