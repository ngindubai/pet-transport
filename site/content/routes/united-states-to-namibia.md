content:
  h1: "Pet Transport from the United States to Namibia"
  overview: |
    USDA APHIS will not put its endorsement on a US pet export certificate for Namibia until the owner already holds a Namibian import permit number. That single sequencing rule catches out more US-to-Namibia moves than any vaccination deadline, because it reverses the order most owners expect: the destination country's paperwork has to exist before the US side will sign off, not after.

    Once that's understood, the rest of the route is comparatively light. Namibia asks for no rabies titre test from any country, the US included, just a vaccination dated between 30 days and 12 months before arrival. Budget 10 to 14 weeks, most of it spent getting the Namibian permit sorted early enough.

  sections:
    - heading: "The permit-before-certificate sequence, and why it trips people up"
      body: |
        USDA's own guidance for this destination states plainly that a Namibian import permit number is required before APHIS will endorse the health certificate. Most export routes run the other way: get the US paperwork done, then sort the destination country's import permit. Namibia flips that order.

        Practically, this means contacting the Directorate of Veterinary Services and getting the permit application moving before booking the USDA-accredited vet appointment, not after. There's no published processing time on the Namibian side, so treat "early" as a real instruction, not a suggestion.

    - heading: "A published certificate for dogs, silence for cats"
      body: |
        USDA APHIS maintains a Namibia-specific International Health Certificate for dogs, a downloadable PDF form built around Namibia's stated requirements. Cats get no equivalent. APHIS's own guidance says it has not been officially informed by Namibia of separate cat requirements, which means a cat travels on the general international certificate rather than a form built specifically for this route.

        That gap is worth raising directly with the Directorate before the pre-export vet visit. It's not that cats face no rules, it's that the standard US paperwork process has no Namibia-specific template to fall back on for them.

    - heading: "No titre test, but a vaccination window that runs both ways"
      body: |
        Namibia doesn't put a rabies antibody test on its checklist for any origin country. What it does insist on is a vaccination dated no earlier than 12 months and no later than 30 days before the pet lands in Windhoek. Time the booster wrong in either direction and the paperwork won't clear, even though the overall process moves faster than most of Namibia's other long-haul routes.
