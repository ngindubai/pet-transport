content:
  h1: "Pet Transport from the United States to Honduras"
  overview: |
    A titre test is not part of this journey. Honduras asks for a current rabies vaccination card and a USDA-endorsed export certificate, nothing more, for the ordinary move of one or two pets. SENASA's own import instructions set no minimum wait after vaccination and no advance permit for a small household.

    Flying is the more involved half. Nothing connects the US mainland to Tegucigalpa or San Pedro Sula except the direct services from Miami, Atlanta and Houston, so the choice of US departure city matters more here than the paperwork does.

  sections:
    - heading: "Why this route is lighter than most of Latin America"
      body: |
        SENASA's import instructivo for Honduras is short by regional standards. There is no rabies antibody titre test requirement at all for a pet arriving with a current vaccination record, which puts Honduras well ahead of most South American destinations on paperwork burden. A Cuarentena official checks the vaccination card and the USDA-endorsed export certificate at the port of entry, charges a flat Lps 525 fee, and that is the end of it for one or two pets.

        Three pets or more changes the picture. SENASA then requires a Permiso Zoosanitario de Importación applied for in advance through its online platform, plus a vet-supervised home quarantine arrangement called Cuarentena Fiduciaria. Anyone moving a multi-pet household needs to start that application well before travel, not assume it can be sorted at the airport.

    - heading: "The USDA side is routine"
      body: |
        A USDA-accredited vet issues the export certificate, endorsed electronically through the APHIS eFile system for roughly USD 38. This is the same process used across most of the site's US-origin routes, and it is well-worn. The vaccination itself just needs to be current on the travel date; there is no 30-day or 3-month wait imposed by Honduras on this side.

    - heading: "Three gateways, three straightforward routings"
      body: |
        American Airlines flies Miami direct to both San Pedro Sula and Palmerola, plus Dallas-Fort Worth to Palmerola. Delta connects Atlanta to San Pedro Sula. United connects Houston to both Honduran airports. None of these carriers requires the CDC facility-airport restriction that applies in the opposite direction, since that CDC rule only governs animals entering the United States, not leaving it, so Houston is a perfectly workable gateway on this route even though it would not be for a dog travelling the other way from a high-risk country.

        Palmerola International (XPL), which replaced the old Toncontín airport in 2021, serves Tegucigalpa; Ramón Villeda Morales International (SAP) serves San Pedro Sula. Pick whichever US departure city sits closest to home, since the paperwork does not change between them.
