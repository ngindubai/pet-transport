  h1: "Pet Transport from United States to Honduras"
  overview: |
    A Lps 525 fee, about USD 20, paid to a quarantine officer at the airport is often the single biggest formality standing between a pet and its owner on this route. Honduras does not ask for a titre test, and it does not run a fixed quarantine stay for one or two pets. A current vaccination card and a US health certificate cover most of what SENASA wants to see.

    That is a lighter list than the CDC's own rules for a pet travelling the opposite direction, since Honduras is one of the CDC's high-risk countries for dog rabies. Owners moving in this direction do not inherit that complexity; it only bites if the pet is later vaccinated in Honduras and needs to re-enter the US.

    Getting there by air narrows to fewer carriers than the route map suggests. United still flies Houston to Palmerola as a passenger service, but won't carry a pet as general cargo. American Airlines, serving Miami and Dallas-Fort Worth to both San Pedro Sula and Palmerola, ends up doing most of the work.

  sections:
    - heading: "Why this direction is lighter than it looks"
      body: |
        SENASA's own instructions, for anyone bringing in 1 or 2 pets, ask for exactly three things at the port of entry: the export health certificate from the country of departure, a vaccination card with current shots, and a report to the quarantine authority for inspection. There is no titre test requirement, no minimum waiting period after vaccination, and no facility stay if those three things are in order.

        That inspection carries a flat fee of Lps 525, and the officer is required to hand over a receipt. Keep it. It is the only proof the fee was paid and the pet was cleared, and Honduran customs at land, sea, and air entry points can all ask for it.

    - heading: "Where the rules tighten: 3 pets or more"
      body: |
        The simple process above only applies to 1 or 2 pets travelling together. From 3 pets upward, SENASA requires a formal Permiso Zoosanitario de Importación, which means a private, licensed vet has to sign off on a fiduciary quarantine (supervised at the owner's home rather than a government facility), a TGR1 fee of Lps 750 has to be paid online, and 2 stamps of Lps 10 each have to be bought from the veterinary college. All of it is submitted through SENASA's online import platform before the pets travel, not arranged on arrival.

        Multi-pet households moving from the US should budget extra time for this. The 1-2 pet process can be handled close to the flight date; the 3-plus pet process needs the vet sign-off and platform approval sorted well in advance.

    - heading: "American over United: choosing the flight"
      body: |
        American Airlines connects Miami to San Pedro Sula several times a week and also links Miami and Dallas-Fort Worth to Palmerola near Tegucigalpa, with its PetEmbark and ExpediteFS cargo channels open to anyone booking through aacargo.com. That combination of route coverage and a genuinely bookable pet service makes it the default choice for most owners on this route.

        United's Houston-to-Palmerola passenger route exists, but its cargo hold is not open to a typical pet move; only active-duty military and State Department staff under official orders can book a live animal with United right now. Avianca is a workable alternative for an owner travelling with the pet as accompanied checked baggage into San Pedro Sula, within its 10 kg cabin and 70 kg hold weight limits, though it excludes brachycephalic breeds outright.
