    - heading: "Titre test and ONSSA permit in sequence"
      body: |
        ONSSA uses a country classification approach to determine whether a titre test is required. Since the USA is not classified as a rabies-free country, a titre test for US pets is the most likely requirement. Confirm this directly with ONSSA or through a pet transport agent before starting preparations. If required, blood must be drawn at least 30 days after the most recent rabies vaccination, adding 5-7 weeks to the timeline.

        Once the titre test result is back, apply for the ONSSA import permit. The application requires microchip details, vaccination records, the titre test result, and the planned entry point. Royal Air Maroc operates direct flights from New York JFK and Washington Dulles to Casablanca Mohammed V Airport, making this a relatively accessible route from the US East Coast.

    - heading: "Breed restrictions and arrival in Casablanca"
      body: |
        Morocco bans the import of Pit Bull Terrier types, Rottweilers, and Dogo Argentinos. These are firm restrictions enforced on arrival. If your dog is one of these breeds or a cross-breed with strong physical similarities, contact ONSSA before making any preparations. For other breeds, the process is straightforward.

        On arrival at Casablanca or another Moroccan entry airport, ONSSA veterinary officers inspect incoming pets. For pets with a valid permit, endorsed USDA health certificate, and correct vaccination and titre test documentation, no extended hold follows. The health certificate has a 14-day validity window for Morocco, which is more flexible than the 10-day window required by some other destinations.
