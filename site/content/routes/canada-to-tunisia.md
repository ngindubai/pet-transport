content:
  h1: "Pet Transport from Canada to Tunisia"
  overview: |
    CFIA doesn't issue a permit for a pet leaving Canada, only a certificate, signed by a licensed vet and endorsed in person by an official CFIA veterinarian before departure. That endorsement has to happen on Canadian soil, which is the one step owners most often book too late.

    Once that's done, Tunisia asks for surprisingly little: no titre test, no import permit, just a current rabies vaccination and a certificate that matches DGSV's format. The one rule with no workaround is Tunisia's outright breed ban. Pit Bull, Rottweiler, Tosa and Mastiff-type dogs cannot enter, whatever a Canadian vet's paperwork says.

    The flight has no shortcut either. Nothing connects Tunis-Carthage to a Canadian airport directly, so every booking runs through Paris or Frankfurt first.

  sections:
    - heading: "The CFIA side: a certificate, not a permit"
      body: |
        A licensed Canadian vet completes the export health certificate first. An official CFIA veterinarian then has to endorse it, in person, at a booked appointment, while the pet is still in the country. CFIA has no mechanism to endorse a certificate retroactively once the animal has already flown, which trips up more owners than any single Tunisia-side requirement.

        The fee sits around CAD 20 to 40 per certificate. Rabies vaccination timing sets the rest of the calendar if the pet isn't already current, at least 30 days before travel for a first dose.

    - heading: "Tunisia's breed ban applies to a Canadian dog too"
      body: |
        DGSV refuses entry to Pit Bull, Rottweiler, Tosa and Mastiff-type dogs and their crosses, a blanket rule that has nothing to do with where the dog is coming from. A rescue with mastiff features and an immaculate Canadian medical history still gets turned away at Tunis-Carthage.

        Check this before any CFIA appointment gets booked, not after. A dog refused entry goes back to Canada at the owner's expense, by some distance the highest-stakes outcome on this route.

    - heading: "No titre test, unlike the reverse direction"
      body: |
        Tunisia sits on Canada's own list of countries considered high-risk for dog rabies, the same classification Algeria, Libya, Egypt and Morocco carry. That matters going the other way, but it has no bearing here. Tunisia's DGSV doesn't ask a Canada-origin pet for a titre test at all, just a rabies vaccination given between 30 days and 12 months before arrival.

    - heading: "Routing through Europe"
      body: |
        Tunis-Carthage has no Canadian service. Every pet on this route flies a Canadian gateway, most practically Toronto Pearson or Montreal-Trudeau, into a European hub first: Paris on Air France, which explicitly lists Tunisia among the destinations its pet service covers, or Frankfurt on Lufthansa. A Tunisair or partner-carrier connection completes the journey into Tunis.

        That's two separate airline pet policies to confirm, and a transfer window that has to work for a live animal, not just a human connection. Build in slack, particularly through a hub as busy as Paris CDG.
