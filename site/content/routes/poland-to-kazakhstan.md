  h1: "Pet Transport from Poland to Kazakhstan"
  overview: |
    Kazakhstan sits in a different regulatory category from the other Caucasus and Central Asian destinations on this guide, because it is an EAEU (Eurasian Economic Union) member state. That one fact adds an extra step that many pet owners miss: before you have your Polish health certificate issued, you should confirm with the Kazakhstani Embassy in Warsaw whether they accept the standard EU-format certificate or whether they prefer an EAEU-format equivalent. Getting this wrong means arriving with a document that does not match what the inspectors expect.

    Beyond the EAEU format question, the route is workable. No titre test is required, there is no quarantine on arrival, and import permits are not needed for 1-2 personal pets. The rabies vaccination waiting period is 30 days (longer than Georgia or Armenia's 21-day requirement), so the preparation timeline is a minimum of 5-7 weeks.

    Two routing options are available from Poland. Turkish Airlines via Istanbul is the more direct option from Warsaw. Air Astana, Kazakhstan's own carrier, operates from Amsterdam (not Warsaw), so that route involves getting to Amsterdam first. Both are legitimate options and the choice often comes down to crate size, scheduling, and current live animal availability.

  sections:
    - heading: "The EAEU Certificate Question: Why You Must Ask Before Booking"
      body: |
        Poland's GIW system produces EU-format health certificates, issued and endorsed by the Powiatowy Lekarz Weterynarii at district PIW offices. This format is accepted across most of the countries in this guide. Kazakhstan is different.

        As an EAEU (Eurasian Economic Union) member, Kazakhstan may prefer or require a certificate format aligned with EAEU veterinary standards rather than the EU model. This is not always enforced consistently, and in practice many owners arrive with an EU-format certificate without issue. But the risk of a mismatch is real enough that it is worth one phone call or email to the Kazakhstani Embassy in Warsaw (Ambasada Republiki Kazachstanu w Warszawie) before you book your PIW appointment.

        If the Embassy confirms EU format is acceptable, proceed with the standard PIW process. If they indicate an EAEU format is preferred, ask your PIW district vet whether they can issue the document in that format. GIW (Glowny Inspektorat Weterynarii at wetgiw.gov.pl) can provide guidance on format compatibility if the PIW office is uncertain.

    - heading: "Poland's PIW Health Certificate Process"
      body: |
        No export permit is required for personal pets leaving Poland. The official health certificate comes from your district PIW (Powiatowy Inspektorat Weterynarii) office, issued and endorsed by the Powiatowy Lekarz Weterynarii after a physical examination of your animal.

        Your private vet handles the EU pet passport and vaccinations. The PIW appointment is for the official examination and certification only. Bring the EU pet passport, microchip card, and any additional vaccination records. The Powiatowy Lekarz Weterynarii will scan the microchip, verify all dates, and complete the certificate. The appointment generally takes under an hour if your documentation is in order.

        The certificate is valid for 10 days from the examination date. Book the PIW appointment so it falls within 10 days of your departure. For Kazakhstan, where the rabies waiting period is 30 days (not 21), plan the vaccination first, wait the full 30 days, then schedule the PIW appointment in the 10-day window before travel.

    - heading: "Routing Options: Istanbul or Amsterdam?"
      body: |
        There is no direct live animal cargo service between Poland and Kazakhstan. Two routing options are commonly used.

        Turkish Airlines via Istanbul is the more straightforward option from Warsaw Chopin Airport (WAW). The routing is WAW to IST then IST to Almaty (ALA) or Astana (NQZ, now also referred to as Astana after the city's renaming). Turkish Airlines accepts live animal cargo on both sectors, subject to crate dimensions and seasonal availability. Contact Turkish Airlines Cargo directly to confirm current policies and available dates for your specific routing.

        Air Astana, Kazakhstan's national carrier, is an alternative but does not depart from Warsaw. Their European hub is Amsterdam (AMS). If you choose this option, travel from Warsaw to Amsterdam first (LOT flies WAW-AMS), then take Air Astana from AMS to ALA or NQZ. Confirm live animal cargo availability with Air Astana Cargo directly before planning this routing.

        LOT Polish Airlines, Warsaw's home carrier, can serve as the first leg for either option: to IST for the Turkish Airlines connection, or to AMS for the Air Astana option. Both are valid and the choice often comes down to scheduling, price, and crate size compatibility.
