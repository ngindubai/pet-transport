    South African Airways covers almost this entire journey on its own network: a Perth to Johannesburg nonstop, then an established Johannesburg-Lilongwe regional service, run by the same airline group start to finish.   sections:
    - heading: "One carrier, almost the whole way"
      body: |
        Most of the African routes on this site involve stitching together two or three different airlines to reach a landlocked or less-connected destination. Malawi from Australia is an exception. South African Airways flies Perth to Johannesburg nonstop eight times a week, and the same airline group already runs an established Johannesburg-Lilongwe regional service, the one used on the UK and US routes to Malawi as well.

        The practical upshot: a single booking, largely one carrier's cargo handling from Perth through to Lilongwe, rather than a handover between two unrelated airlines partway through.

    - heading: "Why no titre test is needed, and what to check instead"
      body: |
        Malawi doesn't operate a listed-country system. It asks for a current rabies vaccination, given between 30 days and 12 months before arrival, and doesn't ask for antibody proof from any origin country. That's true for Australia specifically, not simply assumed because the UK and US pages for this destination report the same thing; Malawi's rule is genuinely about the vaccination record, not the origin country's own rabies classification.

    - heading: "The DAHLD import permit: start this before anything else"
      body: |
        Malawi requires an import permit from the Director of Animal Health for any pet entering the country, unless the point of departure is a fellow SADC member state: Botswana, Lesotho, Namibia, South Africa, Eswatini or Zimbabwe. Australia doesn't qualify, so the full application applies regardless of how straightforward the rest of the paperwork looks.

        Once issued, the permit is valid for six months. The catch is that no processing timeline is published in English, so DAHLD contact should happen ten to twelve weeks out, not six to eight. A pet transport agent with recent Malawi experience is worth the fee here, mainly for knowing who to call when an application goes quiet.
