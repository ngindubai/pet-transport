content:
  h1: "Pet Transport from India to Japan"
  overview: |
    India to Japan is one of the most time-consuming pet transport routes in the world. Japan's Animal Quarantine Service (AQS) requires a minimum 180-day waiting period after a successful rabies titre test - a rule that cannot be shortened, expedited, or waived.

    Starting from scratch, the minimum timeline is 36-40 weeks. The sequence is fixed: microchip first, first rabies vaccination, wait 30 days, second vaccination, wait 30 days, titre test at an AQS-approved lab, wait 180 days, then submit advance notification to AQS at least 40 days before travel.

    India is classified as a non-designated region for Japan AQS purposes, which means the full extended protocol applies. Using an IPATA-registered agent with Japan experience is strongly recommended.

      body: |
        | Requirement | Specification | Consequence of non-compliance |
        |---|---|---|
        | Microchip | ISO 11784/11785, before vaccination | Vaccine does not count. Entire sequence restarts. |
        | First rabies vaccination | After microchip. Any approved vaccine. | Must precede titre test by at least 60 days. |
        | Second rabies vaccination | At least 30 days after first. | Both vaccinations required. |
        | Titre test | FAVN or RFFIT at AQS-approved lab. 0.5 IU/ml minimum. | Test at non-approved lab = does not count. |
        | 180-day wait | From date of blood draw. Cannot be shortened. | Quarantine at AQS facility (owner's expense). |
        | Advance notification | At least 40 days before arrival. | May be refused entry. |
        | Health certificate | Within 10 days of travel. Official vet endorsement. | Entry refused or quarantine. |
        | Entry point | AQS-designated airport (Narita NRT recommended). | Must enter via AQS-approved port. |

        Source: Japan Animal Quarantine Service (AQS), aqs.maff.go.jp
    - heading: "AQS-approved laboratories: where to get the titre test done from India"
      body: |
        Japan AQS publishes a list of approved laboratories for the rabies titre test. The blood sample must be tested at an AQS-approved facility. The approved lab list for India includes a small number of options - some owners ship samples to approved labs outside India.

        | Region | Approved lab options |
        |---|---|
        | India | Limited. Confirm current approved labs with Japan AQS before booking. |
        | South Korea | KSVS (Korea Veterinary Science Institute) - frequently used for Asian routes |
        | Japan | Nihon University, Tottori University - accept imported samples |
        | Europe | Various EU-approved labs that are also AQS-listed |

        Always confirm the current approved lab list directly with Japan AQS (aqs.maff.go.jp) before drawing blood. Using a non-approved lab means the test does not count.
faqs:
  - question: "How long does it take to transport a pet from India to Japan?"
    answer: "Allow at least 36-40 weeks from start to travel day. Japan's AQS requires a mandatory 180-day wait from the date of the rabies titre test blood draw. This cannot be shortened. Add time for two vaccinations (60+ days), the titre test itself, and advance AQS notification (40 days before travel)."
  - question: "My pet is already vaccinated in India. Does the vaccination count for Japan AQS?"
    answer: "Only if the microchip was implanted before the vaccination. Japan AQS requires the ISO microchip to be in place before any vaccination for that vaccination to count. If your pet was vaccinated before being chipped, the sequence must restart with a new vaccination after chipping."
  - question: "What happens if my pet arrives in Japan before the 180-day wait is complete?"
    answer: "Your pet will be quarantined at an AQS facility in Japan at your expense for the remaining days of the 180-day period. AQS facility quarantine costs are significant. The 180-day rule has no exceptions or waivers."
  - question: "Which laboratory can I use for the titre test in India?"
    answer: "The titre test must be done at a Japan AQS-approved laboratory. Japan AQS publishes the approved lab list at aqs.maff.go.jp. The approved options available in or accessible from India are limited - confirm the current list with Japan AQS before booking your blood draw. Some owners send samples to South Korea (KSVS) or to Japanese universities that accept imported samples."
