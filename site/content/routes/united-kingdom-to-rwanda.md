    UK to Rwanda is a moderately complex route. You need an Animal Health Certificate from a UK Official Vet and a Rwanda import permit from the Rwanda Agriculture and Animal Resources Development Board (RAB). There is no titre test required by Rwanda for UK-origin pets, which keeps the timeline to around 6-10 weeks.

  sections:
    - heading: "What Rwanda requires from UK pets: import permit and inspection"
      body: |
        Rwanda's import requirements for pets from the UK include a current ISO microchip, valid rabies vaccination, an official UK Animal Health Certificate, and an import permit from the Rwanda Agriculture and Animal Resources Development Board (RAB).

        The RAB permit authorises the pet to enter Rwanda and specifies the conditions under which it must travel. It must be applied for before booking travel and carried in original form. Allow 3-4 weeks for RAB processing.

        On arrival at Kigali International Airport, a Ministry of Agriculture vet will inspect your pet and verify documentation. If everything is in order, there is no extended quarantine. If documentation is missing or incorrect, your pet may be held.
    - heading: "RwandAir direct versus hub connections: which is better for your pet?"

        RwandAir's direct London Heathrow to Kigali service removes the hub connection entirely. For pet cargo, direct means less handling, shorter total journey time, and no risk of a connection disruption leaving your pet overnight in a foreign hub. If cargo space is available, RwandAir direct is the recommended first choice.

        Qatar Airways via Doha is the next best option. Qatar Cargo has a strong operational record for Africa-UK routes and the Doha connection adds a manageable 5-6 hours to the journey.

        Ethiopian Airlines via Addis Ababa adds slightly more time but has good frequency from London and strong connections to Kigali via the Addis hub.

  - question: "Does my pet need a titre test to travel from the UK to Rwanda?"
    answer: "No. Rwanda does not currently require a titre test for pets from the United Kingdom. You need a current rabies vaccination, ISO microchip, UK Animal Health Certificate, and a Rwanda RAB import permit. Confirm current requirements with RAB before finalising documentation."
  - question: "How do I get the Rwanda import permit for my pet?"
    answer: "Apply to the Rwanda Agriculture and Animal Resources Development Board (RAB) before travel. Allow 3-4 weeks for processing. An IPATA-registered agent with Africa experience can submit the application on your behalf and manage follow-up. The permit must be in hand before your pet travels."
  - question: "Are there direct flights from the UK to Rwanda for pet cargo?"
    answer: "Yes. RwandAir operates direct London Heathrow to Kigali (KGL) service and RwandAir Cargo handles pets. Confirm current cargo acceptance with RwandAir before booking. Qatar Airways via Doha and Ethiopian Airlines via Addis Ababa are reliable alternatives."
  - question: "What is the UK Animal Health Certificate and how do I get one?"
    answer: "The AHC is the official document required for pets leaving the UK. It is issued by an APHA-authorised Official Vet in the UK, valid for 10 days from the examination date. Book your OV appointment 3-4 weeks before travel to secure a slot, even though you will collect the certificate within 10 days of departure."
  - question: "What happens at Kigali airport when my pet arrives?"
    answer: "A Rwanda Agriculture and Animal Resources Development Board (RAB) vet will inspect your pet on arrival at Kigali International Airport. They will verify the import permit, Animal Health Certificate, microchip, vaccination record, and health status. With all documents correct, there is no extended quarantine. Bring originals of everything."
