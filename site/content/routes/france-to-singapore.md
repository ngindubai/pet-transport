faqs:
  - question: "Why does my French pet need 30 days quarantine in Singapore?"
    answer: "Singapore's AVS classifies France as a Category C country. All pets from Category C countries must complete 30 days of quarantine at an AVS-approved Singapore Quarantine Station on arrival, regardless of vaccination or titre test history. This applies even after completing the 6-month pre-travel waiting period."
  - question: "How long does the entire France to Singapore pet transport process take?"
    answer: "Allow 36-44 weeks. The FAVN titre test requires blood drawn 30 days post-vaccination, followed by a 6-month wait. Then add the AVS import permit processing (30+ days), cargo booking, and the DDPP health certificate. The 30-day quarantine on arrival is on top of all this."
  - question: "What is the AVS import permit and how do I get one?"
    answer: "The AVS import permit is a mandatory Singapore government document required before your pet travels. Apply through the AVS e-service portal at nparks.gov.sg/avs at least 30 days before planned arrival. A licensed agent can manage this process on your behalf."
  - question: "Which airline should I use from Paris to Singapore?"
    answer: "Air France Cargo and Singapore Airlines Cargo both operate direct CDG-SIN services with live animal capability. Air France has strong cargo infrastructure at Charles de Gaulle; Singapore Airlines has deep familiarity with AVS import procedures at Changi. Either is a sound choice."
  - question: "Can the quarantine period be reduced?"
    answer: "No. 30-day quarantine is mandatory for pets from Category C countries. AVS does not issue exemptions or reductions. Pre-booking a quarantine space is required before your pet travels."
