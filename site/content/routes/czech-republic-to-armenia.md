  overview: |
    The Czech Republic to Armenia route is a practical move for anyone relocating to Yerevan or the wider South Caucasus. Armenia does not require quarantine for compliant pets arriving from the EU, and the documentation process from Prague is well-defined once you understand the two-step Czech export procedure.

    Start preparations three to five weeks before travel. The main constraint is the rabies vaccination window: your pet's vaccine must have been given at least 21 days before entry into Armenia, and must still be within its 11-month validity at the point of arrival. Everything else builds around that date.

    This guide covers the SVS export certificate process, the Turkish Airlines routing from Prague via Istanbul, and what SSFS inspectors check at Zvartnots Airport in Yerevan.

  sections:
    - heading: "The SVS export process from Prague"
      body: |
        The Czech Republic is an EU member state, so pet exports follow EU Regulation 576/2013 format. The export health certificate is issued by an SVS-authorised official vet (not your regular practice vet unless they hold SVS authorisation) and then endorsed by SVS, the Státní veterinární správa (State Veterinary Administration, svscr.cz).

        The certificate must be issued within 10 days of travel. Book the official vet appointment with enough lead time to get the SVS endorsement completed before your departure date: endorsement typically takes one to two working days at regional SVS offices. If you are in Prague, the Central Bohemian regional SVS office is the most commonly used.

        The health certificate will record your pet's microchip number, rabies vaccination date and batch, the issuing vet's details, and the SVS endorsement stamp. Armenian SSFS inspectors at EVN will check all of these fields. Missing or illegible entries are a common reason for delays at arrival, so confirm the certificate is complete before leaving the vet clinic.

    - heading: "Flying from Prague to Yerevan: the Istanbul connection"
      body: |
        There is no direct flight from Prague Václav Havel Airport (PRG) to Zvartnots International Airport (EVN) in Yerevan. The standard routing is via Turkish Airlines through Istanbul Atatürk (IST), which is by far the most reliable connection from Central Europe to the South Caucasus for cargo live animals.

        Turkish Airlines Cargo handles live animals on both the PRG-IST and IST-EVN legs. The connection time at Istanbul matters: IATA regulations require a minimum connection time for live animals in cargo, and Turkish Airlines Cargo staff will manage the transit. Confirm the connection time at booking and ask specifically whether your pet will be transiting airside or will need re-booking at IST.

        Lufthansa via Frankfurt is an alternative, particularly if you prefer the Frankfurt Animal Lounge for longer layovers. LOT Polish Airlines via Warsaw is a third option but connectivity from Warsaw to Yerevan is less consistent. For most moves from Prague to Armenia, Turkish Airlines via IST is the first choice.

    - heading: "What happens at Zvartnots Airport on arrival"
      body: |
        SSFS (State Service for Food Safety) veterinary inspectors operate at Zvartnots International Airport (EVN) and check all arriving pets. The inspection is documentation-based: inspectors will scan the microchip, check the health certificate is within its 10-day validity, confirm the rabies vaccination dates, and review the SVS endorsement.

        Armenia does not require a titre test for pets arriving from EU member states such as the Czech Republic. There is no routine quarantine for compliant pets. If the documentation is in order, your pet is released to you after inspection.

        Pets must be over four months of age to enter Armenia. If you are travelling with a young dog or cat, confirm the age requirement is met before booking. For one or two personal pets, no advance import permit is needed. For three or more animals, contact SSFS at ssfs.am before travel to confirm the correct procedure.
