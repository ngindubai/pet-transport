content:
  h1: "Pet Transport from Montenegro to United Kingdom"
  overview: |
    Montenegro has no route pages on this site until now, and no pet has an easy path from Podgorica or Tivat to the UK either. The reason is administrative, not medical: Montenegro sits outside the UK's rabies-listed country system, so APHA treats it the same as any unlisted country. That triggers a blood test and a fixed 90-day wait, regardless of how healthy or well-travelled the pet is.

    The paperwork on the Montenegrin side runs through AFSVPA, the Administration for Food Safety, Veterinary and Phytosanitary Affairs, whose director also serves as the country's Chief Veterinary Officer. It is a functioning, EU-candidate veterinary system, so the certification itself is not the hard part. The hard part is the calendar: microchip, vaccination, a 30-day wait, the titre test, a further 2-3 weeks for the lab result, and then 90 more days before travel is legal.

    Flights are the other complication. Podgorica and Tivat both connect easily to Belgrade and Istanbul, but neither Air Serbia nor Turkish Airlines will carry a pet into the UK. Lufthansa and Austrian Airlines, via Frankfurt, Munich or Vienna, are the two carriers that actually complete this journey.

  sections:
    - heading: "Why Montenegro is unlisted, and what the 90-day wait actually means"
      body: |
        The UK divides the world into listed and unlisted countries for pet travel. Listed countries, mostly the EU plus a short list of others, only need a current rabies vaccination. Everywhere else, including Montenegro, is unlisted, which means a serological test is required to prove the vaccine actually worked.

        That test is FAVN or ELISA, run at an APHA-approved laboratory, with a pass mark of 0.5 IU/ml. Blood cannot be drawn until at least 30 days after vaccination. Once the lab confirms a pass, the clock starts: 90 days, calculated from the date on the certificate, not from the sampling date. Nothing shortens this. A pet that has lived in Montenegro for years and been vaccinated annually still needs the same 90-day gap as a pet vaccinated for the first time last month.

        In practice this means starting the process four to five months before a planned move. Owners who leave it to eight weeks are the ones who end up rebooking flights, or leaving a pet behind with a sitter while the wait finishes.

    - heading: "Getting out of Montenegro: the airlines that actually work"
      body: |
        Podgorica has the busier schedule of the two airports, with roughly 14 weekly flights to Vienna on Austrian Airlines and a daily Istanbul service on Turkish Airlines. Tivat is smaller and more seasonal. Neither of those obvious connections helps a pet reach the UK. Turkish Airlines has a blanket rule against carrying pets on any UK-bound flight, connections included, and Air Serbia excludes the UK entirely from its pet network, alongside the UAE and Qatar.

        That leaves Lufthansa and Austrian Airlines as the realistic options, routing through Frankfurt or Munich, or through Vienna. Lufthansa keeps the pet on its own metal the whole way, which avoids a handover between carriers at a European hub. Frankfurt's dedicated Animal Lounge handles the transfer for larger dogs and cats travelling in the hold. Austrian's Vienna routing works too, though it adds a EUR 150 surcharge and, unlike Lufthansa, will not carry snub-nosed breeds in the hold at all.

        Either way, book the flight during the 90-day wait rather than after it ends. Cargo and hold space on these routes is limited, and leaving the booking until the wait is nearly over risks missing the next available slot by a week or more.
