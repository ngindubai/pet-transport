  sections:
    - heading: "AQCS NOC and titre test: what Indian owners need to start"
      body: |
        India requires an AQCS (Animal Quarantine and Certification Service) NOC for all pet exports. Apply at least 30 days before travel. India is also not EU-listed, so the FAVN titre test is mandatory with a 3-month wait from the blood draw date.

        The DAHD endorses the EU-format certificate, which must be dated within 10 days of travel. Start the process five to six months before your travel date. Source: food.ec.europa.eu, 2026.

    - heading: "Cargo options from India to Madrid"
      body: |
        No direct cargo flight connects Indian airports to Madrid. The main routing options are Emirates via Dubai to MAD (two legs, well-established), or via European hubs Frankfurt (Lufthansa) or Paris CDG (Air India/Air France) with onward connections to Barajas.

        Emirates via Dubai is often the most practical option due to Emirates' strong cargo network and live animal handling. The Dubai transit adds time; ensure the transit is not excessively long when booking.

    - heading: "Spain PPP: planning for certain breeds from India"
      body: |
        Spain's PPP law covers Pit Bull Terrier, American Staffordshire Terrier, Rottweiler, and similar breeds. Import is permitted; PPP creates ongoing obligations once in Spain: muzzle, maximum 2m leash, civil liability insurance, and a PPP licence from the local municipality.

        For Indian families moving to Spain with breeds commonly kept in India, including German Shepherds, Labradors, and similar, no PPP obligation applies.
