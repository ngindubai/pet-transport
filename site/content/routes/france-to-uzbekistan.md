    Twice a week, an Uzbekistan Airways Airbus leaves Paris Charles de Gaulle and lands in Tashkent under seven hours later, no stops. Flight HY252 is easy to overlook, since Uzbekistan Airways' better-known European nonstop routes run into Frankfurt and Milan. No titre test applies either way, which keeps the medical side of this route lighter than most long-haul corridors on this site. Ten to fourteen weeks is realistic from a standing start.

        France's EU rabies-free status keeps this route off the FAVN or ELISA titre test track. What still matters is sequencing: the microchip goes in before or at the same appointment as the rabies vaccination, and the vaccination itself has to sit inside a 30-day-to-12-month window before arrival.

Some accounts describe a pet arriving on the strength of the health certificate alone; a 2026 process review states the Committee of Veterinary and Livestock Development expects a vet.uz application filed ahead of travel. Both can't be fully right for every case. File the permit application and confirm directly with the Committee rather than assuming the lighter reading holds.
