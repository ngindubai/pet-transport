  h1: "Pet Transport from the United Kingdom to Moldova"
  overview: |
    A UK-vaccinated pet gets a genuine advantage travelling to Moldova, one that isn't obvious from the country's small size or non-EU status. The UK sits on the EU's own approved list of low rabies-risk countries, and Moldova, as an EU accession candidate aligning its animal health rules with the EU model, extends that same exemption to UK arrivals. No titre test, no 3-month wait.

    What's left is familiar: microchip, a 30-day wait after rabies vaccination (the standard UK 3-year vaccine is accepted, no need to repeat it early), and an APHA Export Health Certificate rather than an EU pet passport, since Moldova sits outside the passport scheme.

    The one trap on this route has nothing to do with rabies rules. Wizz Air dominates the London-Chisinau market by flight frequency and carries no pets whatsoever.

  sections:
    - heading: "Why the UK skips Moldova's titre test"
      body: |
        Moldova's animal health rules for arriving pets closely track the EU's own framework, a natural consequence of its EU accession candidacy since 2022. Under Implementing Regulation (EU) No 577/2013, the UK appears in Annex II, Part 2, the list of third countries treated as equivalent to EU member states for rabies purposes. Moldova extends that same treatment to UK-origin pets, meaning the titre test and its associated wait, mandatory for pets from unlisted countries, simply doesn't apply here.

        This is worth reconfirming close to your travel date rather than assuming permanently, since the underlying EU list is reviewed periodically and a change there would carry through to Moldova's own rules.

    - heading: "Booking the flight without the Wizz Air trap"
      body: |
        Search London to Chisinau and Wizz Air's frequent low-cost service appears first almost every time. It's also the one airline on this corridor that categorically will not carry a pet, in the cabin or the hold, with the sole exception of a certified assistance dog.

        Austrian Airlines via Vienna, Turkish Airlines via Istanbul, and LOT Polish Airlines via Warsaw all carry pets on this route and connect through to Chisinau on a single ticket. Turkish Airlines' flat USD 150 cargo rate, regardless of crate weight tier, is worth knowing if you're moving a larger dog, where per-kilo pricing on other carriers gets expensive.

    - heading: "Moving more than one pet"
      body: |
        Moldova caps non-commercial pet movement at five animals per owner, a specific number worth knowing if this move involves several cats, dogs, or a mixed household. Bringing more than five requires switching to the commercial import process, which carries different documentation and inspection requirements entirely, so check this early if it applies to you.
