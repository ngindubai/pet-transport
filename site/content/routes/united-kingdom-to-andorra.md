content:
  h1: "Pet Transport from the United Kingdom to Andorra"
  overview: |
    Since 22 April 2026, an EU-issued or GB-issued pet passport no longer gets a dog or cat out of Great Britain and into the EU. A UK vet has ten days, not the old passport's open-ended validity, to issue the Animal Health Certificate the move now runs on. Andorra adds its own twist: no scheduled flight goes there directly, so the AHC has to carry a pet through France or Spain first, before a final road leg across an open, largely unchecked border.

  sections:
    - heading: "The AHC replaced the pet passport. Here's what changed."
      body: |
        Before April 2026, a GB-issued EU pet passport was reusable for years, provided vaccinations stayed current. That's gone for anyone travelling from Great Britain. The Animal Health Certificate now has to be issued within a 10-day window before each trip, by an Official Veterinarian, and it covers one outbound journey rather than a standing document.

        Booking the OV appointment is now as time-critical as booking the flight. Too early, and the certificate expires before travel. Too late, and there's no time left to fix a problem the vet finds at the appointment.

    - heading: "No flight, no border post: getting into Andorra"
      body: |
        Barcelona-El Prat and Toulouse-Blagnac are the two practical gateways, each roughly 200km from Andorra. British Airways and Vueling dominate the Barcelona corridor; Air France serves Toulouse via Paris. Whichever route is chosen, the flight only gets the pet as far as France or Spain.

        The last leg is a drive across a border that, for most travellers, has no formal checkpoint at all. That's convenient in one sense. It also means the AHC, and the vaccination record behind it, is effectively the only document standing between an owner and a problem if Andorran or transit-country officials do ask questions.

    - heading: "Why Andorra doesn't need its own separate paperwork"
      body: |
        Andorra isn't in the EU, but it sits inside the bloc's animal health logic in practice, recognising the same documentation Spain and France require of arriving pets. There's no separate Andorran import permit published for a routine, owner-accompanied move.

        That said, published English-language detail from Andorran authorities is thin. Where GOV.UK gives a precise, dated answer for EU countries, Andorra's own position rests more on inference from its Part 1 status and its open borders. A quick check with the Ministeri de Medi Ambient, Agricultura i Sostenibilitat before travel is sensible, not excessive caution.

faqs:
  - question: "Do I need a new Animal Health Certificate every time I take my pet to Andorra?"
    answer: "Yes. Since 22 April 2026, each trip from Great Britain needs its own AHC, issued by an Official Veterinarian within 10 days of departure. It isn't a reusable document like the old pet passport. Source: GOV.UK, 'Pet travel to Europe and Northern Ireland,' 2026."
  - question: "Can I fly directly from the UK to Andorra with a pet?"
    answer: "No. Andorra has no scheduled commercial airport. Pets fly from Heathrow or Gatwick to Barcelona-El Prat or Toulouse-Blagnac, then complete the journey by road, roughly 2.5 to 3 hours from either airport."
  - question: "Does a pet need a titre test to travel from the UK to Andorra?"
    answer: "No. A current rabies vaccination given after the microchip, plus a valid AHC, is sufficient. Andorra doesn't require a rabies antibody titre test for pets arriving via France or Spain."
  - question: "Is there a formal border check for pets entering Andorra?"
    answer: "Not in the way GB or EU border posts operate. Andorra's land borders with France and Spain are largely open, so the AHC and vaccination record function as the practical proof of compliance, even without a dedicated checkpoint stamping documents on arrival."
