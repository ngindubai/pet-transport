  h1: "Pet Transport from Poland to Azerbaijan"
  overview: |
    Azerbaijan has one firm rule that shapes every pet move from Poland: all pets entering the country by air must arrive at Heydar Aliyev International Airport (GYD) in Baku. There is no flexibility on this. When booking cargo, confirm the routing ends at GYD, not at a domestic transfer point.

    Beyond the mandatory entry airport, the requirements are manageable for a Polish owner. No titre test is needed from EU member states, and there is no quarantine period if your paperwork is in order. The main additions compared to simpler EU routes are the echinococcus treatment requirement for dogs and the need to work through Poland's GIW district vet system for the official health certificate.

    The route runs from Warsaw Chopin Airport (WAW) via Istanbul to Baku, with Turkish Airlines as the primary option for live animal cargo. Allow 4-6 weeks of preparation time to accommodate the rabies vaccination waiting period and the echinococcus treatment timing.

  sections:
    - heading: "Poland's GIW Export Process: District Vets and PIW Offices"
      body: |
        Poland handles pet exports through a structured two-tier system. Your private vet manages day-to-day care and maintains the EU pet passport, including vaccinations. The official health certificate, however, must come from the district official vet: the Powiatowy Lekarz Weterynarii, based at the local PIW (Powiatowy Inspektorat Weterynarii) office.

        No export permit is required for personal pets. The PIW examination involves the Powiatowy Lekarz Weterynarii physically examining your animal, scanning the microchip, and checking all documentation against the EU pet passport before endorsing the certificate. The process is supervised by GIW (Glowny Inspektorat Weterynarii) nationally, but the certificate is issued at district level.

        The certificate has a 10-day validity from the examination date. Because Azerbaijan's import rules require this document, and it cannot be issued more than 10 days before travel, you need to book your PIW appointment to fall within that window. Most PIW offices can accommodate this with a few days' notice if you call ahead.

    - heading: "Azerbaijan's Import Rules: What to Prepare"
      body: |
        Azerbaijan's Ministry of Agriculture (agro.gov.az) sets the import requirements. Microchip (ISO 11784/11785) and rabies vaccination (21 days to 12 months before entry) are mandatory. No titre test is required from Poland. No import permit is needed for personal pets.

        The requirement that catches people out is echinococcus (tapeworm) treatment for dogs. This must be administered by a vet, recorded in the EU pet passport with the product name, dosage, and date, and the treatment typically needs to have occurred within 1-5 days before entry. Confirm the exact timing window with your vet, as it is specific to the product used.

        All of this is checked at GYD on arrival by Azerbaijani veterinary inspection authorities. The process is generally straightforward if documents are complete. Missing or inconsistent documentation is the main cause of problems on this route.

    - heading: "Flying Warsaw to Baku: The Istanbul Routing"
      body: |
        There is no direct flight for live animal cargo between Warsaw and Baku. The standard route is WAW to Istanbul (IST) then IST to GYD, with Turkish Airlines handling live animal cargo on both sectors. Turkish Airlines has established cargo procedures at Baku Heydar Aliyev Airport and is familiar with the Azerbaijani import documentation requirements.

        LOT Polish Airlines, Warsaw's national carrier, can operate as the first leg to Istanbul, connecting to Turkish Airlines or other carriers for the Baku sector. Confirm live animal handling arrangements on any interline combination before booking.

        When booking cargo, specify GYD (Baku) as the final destination from the outset. Do not assume a booking to Azerbaijan automatically routes to GYD. Azerbaijan's mandatory entry airport rule means your pet's itinerary must show GYD as the arrival airport, and cargo staff need to confirm this before the animal is accepted.
