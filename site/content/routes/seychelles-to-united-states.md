    Seychelles doesn't appear on the CDC's list of high-risk countries for dog rabies. That one omission strips out the blood titre test that dominates so many other long-haul routes on this site, leaving the CDC Dog Import Form as the entire federal requirement on the US side.

  sections:
    - heading: "Why the US leg is unusually light"
      body: |
        The CDC's rabies rules for dogs turn on a single question: has the dog been in a high-risk country within the last 6 months? Seychelles isn't one, based on the CDC's published list, checked July 2026, so a dog travelling straight from Mahe skips the titre test, the wait times and the CDC vaccination certification that apply to arrivals from countries on that list. Since 1 August 2024, the CDC Dog Import Form has been the sole federal document for a dog in this position, submitted online by the owner rather than a vet.

    - heading: "Seychelles' own export side still applies"
      body: |
        None of the above removes the need for Seychelles' export paperwork. The islands still require a vet-issued export health certificate confirming vaccination and general health before any animal leaves Mahe, endorsed by the government veterinary authority. It's a lighter process than the destination-side requirements many countries impose, but it isn't nothing.

    - heading: "Abu Dhabi and Dubai: the only two realistic routings"
      body: |
        With no direct Seychelles-US service, every shipment connects. Etihad Cargo's Pet Oasis in Abu Dhabi and Emirates SkyCargo's Dubai facility both handle live-animal transfers as a core part of their cargo operation, which is the reassurance worth paying for on a route with a long total transit time.
