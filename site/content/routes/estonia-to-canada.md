    - heading: "What Canada actually requires from Estonian pets"
      body: |
        The Canadian Food Inspection Agency (CFIA) manages pet import. For dogs from Estonia, the requirements are: a current rabies vaccination, a health certificate issued by an accredited Estonian vet, and CFIA endorsement. For cats, rabies vaccination is not federally required but most airlines and some provinces do require it.

        No titre test is needed. No quarantine applies unless the documentation is incomplete on arrival. Estonia's EU membership means its official veterinary system is trusted, and the VTA-endorsed certificate is accepted by CFIA.

        What catches people out on this route is the 10-day certificate window. The health certificate must be issued within 10 days of arrival in Canada, and the VTA endorsement adds time. Book your vet appointment with the endorsement turnaround in mind.

    - heading: "Cargo routing from Tallinn to Canadian cities"

        Tallinn Airport (TLL) connects to major European hubs via KLM (to Amsterdam), Lufthansa partners (to Frankfurt or Munich), and various carriers to Helsinki, Stockholm, and London. All cargo to Canada transits a major European hub before the transatlantic leg.

        Toronto (YYZ), Montreal (YUL), and Vancouver (YVR) are the main Canadian entry points for pet cargo. Air Canada Cargo operates from Amsterdam, Frankfurt, and London. KLM has established transatlantic cargo routes from Schiphol.

        Journey time from Tallinn to Toronto is typically 12-16 hours including the European hub connection. Book cargo pet space at least 6 weeks ahead and get written confirmation of live animal acceptance on every leg.
