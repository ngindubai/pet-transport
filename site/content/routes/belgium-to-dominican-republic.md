  sections:
    - heading: "Dominican Republic import requirements: the checklist"
      body: |
        The DGAG (Direccion General de Ganaderia) under the Ministerio de Agricultura regulates all pet imports into the Dominican Republic. DGAG veterinary officers inspect arriving pets at Las Americas International Airport (SDQ) in Santo Domingo on arrival.

        The requirements are: a microchip conforming to ISO 11784/11785, a valid rabies vaccination given between 30 days and 12 months before entry, a health certificate issued within 15 days of travel, and current DHPP vaccination for dogs (distemper, adenovirus, parainfluenza, parvovirus). Cats need rhinotracheitis, calicivirus, and panleukopenia vaccinations on record. All pets need internal and external antiparasitic treatment within 10 days of departure, documented by a vet.

        No import permit is required for personal companion animals (up to five animals with their owner). There is no quarantine for compliant arrivals.

    - heading: "Belgium's export documentation: the FASFC process"
      body: |
        Belgium's export documentation for non-EU destinations uses the AHC issued by a FASFC-authorised official veterinarian and endorsed by the FASFC (Federaal Agentschap voor de Veiligheid van de Voedselketen). The FASFC has provincial offices across Belgium. Contact the FASFC website (fasfc.be) or your local provincial centre to find the nearest official vet and book the appointment.

        The Dominican Republic accepts AHC certificates up to 15 days from issue, giving slightly more scheduling room than most EU destinations. Book the appointment as close to your cargo departure as possible, allowing enough time for the endorsement process.

        Belgium's proximity to Amsterdam Schiphol means KLM is a practical cargo option in addition to Brussels Airlines and connections via Madrid on Iberia.
