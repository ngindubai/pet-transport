content:
  h1: "Pet Transport from United Kingdom to Eswatini"
  overview: |
    Send a pet the other way, Eswatini to Britain, and the paperwork includes a blood test and a 90-day wait. Coming from the UK, neither applies. Eswatini's own import rules ask for a vaccination record, a microchip, and a permit from the Director of Animal Health, nothing more exotic than that.

    What doesn't get any easier is the flight. King Mswati III International Airport has no long-haul service of its own. A UK-origin pet flies Heathrow to Johannesburg on British Airways, then picks up a short regional connection into Eswatini on Airlink or Eswatini Air. The paperwork is the light part of this route; the routing is the part that needs an agent's attention.

  sections:
    - heading: "Why the import side is lighter than you'd expect"
      body: |
        Eswatini's Director of Animal Health, within the Ministry of Agriculture, does not require a rabies titre test for pets entering from any country, the UK included. That's worth pausing on, because the reverse direction of this exact route does require one: a pet leaving Eswatini for Britain needs a FAVN or ELISA blood test and a 90-day wait, purely because the UK's own rules classify Eswatini as unlisted. Eswatini's rules don't return the favour.

        What Eswatini does ask for is a Veterinary Import Permit, needed because the UK sits outside the Southern African Development Community bloc that gets a waiver on this step. The permit is valid for 6 months once issued, so it's worth applying for early and getting it out of the way well before the flight date is locked in.

    - heading: "Booking the Johannesburg connection properly"
      body: |
        British Airways flies Heathrow to O.R. Tambo nonstop in around 11 hours, through British Airways World Cargo. From there, Airlink and Eswatini Air both run the short JNB-SHO hop into King Mswati III International Airport several times a day.

        Treat this as one booking, not two. A pet changing planes at Johannesburg needs a genuine interline cargo transfer, moved airside between the long-haul and regional carriers, rather than being cleared through South African customs as a fresh import. A cargo agent with Johannesburg experience is the difference between a smooth handover and a missed connection.
