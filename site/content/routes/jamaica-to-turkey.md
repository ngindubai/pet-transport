content:
  h1: "Pet Transport from Jamaica to Turkey"
  overview: |
    Jamaica gets an easier ride into the EU than most Caribbean nations, thanks to its Group 2 listing under EU rabies rules. Turkey isn't the EU, and it doesn't recognise that listing. A pet leaving Kingston for Istanbul needs the full FAVN titre test and a 90-day wait, the same protocol Turkey applies to countries with no special EU status at all.

    That's the detail worth knowing before anything else on this route. Everything downstream, the timeline, the connecting flight through Miami, the paperwork from VSD, follows from treating Turkey as its own separate rulebook rather than assuming Jamaica's EU exemption travels with the pet.

  sections:
    - heading: "Jamaica's EU exemption doesn't carry over to Turkey"
      body: |
        Jamaica sits on the EU's Group 2 country list under Implementing Regulation (EU) 2024/1130, which is why Jamaica-to-EU routes in this build skip the titre test entirely. Turkey runs its own classification system, entirely independent of the EU's, and Jamaica isn't on Turkey's short low-risk list. The result: a full FAVN titre test, blood drawn at least 30 days after vaccination, a 90-day wait from that draw date, and a minimum result of 0.5 IU/ml.

        Arrive without it, and Turkish authorities hold the pet in 21-day quarantine at the owner's expense rather than turning it away outright, which is at least some reassurance, though not one worth planning around.

    - heading: "Routing through Miami"
      body: |
        Neither Norman Manley International in Kingston nor Sangster International in Montego Bay has a direct cargo service to Istanbul. The practical routing goes through Miami, where Turkish Airlines runs its own transatlantic cargo service onward to Istanbul, keeping the pet on one carrier for the longer leg of the journey rather than switching handlers mid-route.

        The VSD, Jamaica's Veterinary Services Division, doesn't require a separate export permit for a personal pet. Its role is endorsing the health certificate itself, reformatted for Turkey's model rather than the EU-style document used on Jamaica's European routes.
