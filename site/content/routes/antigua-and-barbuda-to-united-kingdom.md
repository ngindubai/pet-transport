  h1: "Pet Transport from Antigua and Barbuda to the United Kingdom"
  overview: |
    Antigua and Barbuda sits in Part 2 of Annex II to Commission Implementing Regulation (EU) No 577/2013, the list the UK still uses as the backbone of its post-Brexit Part 2 country list. That one classification means a dog or cat leaving V.C. Bird International skips the rabies titre test and 90-day wait that trip up owners flying in from unlisted countries.

    What doesn't disappear is the flight itself. Only one airline flies this route nonstop, and it doesn't carry pets in the cabin under any circumstances. Everything moves as cargo, booked through a specialist agent rather than the normal passenger system.

    Budget 6 to 8 weeks. Most of that is the 21-day wait after the rabies vaccination plus the paperwork windows either side of it, not the flight itself.

  sections:
    - heading: "Why the Part 2 listing matters more than the flight"
      body: |
        A titre test adds weeks and a courier delay to almost every route on this site where it applies. Antigua and Barbuda's Part 2 status removes that step entirely. The UK still wants the standard 21-day wait after the first rabies vaccination, an Animal Health Certificate issued within 10 days of travel, and tapeworm treatment for dogs in the 24 to 120 hour window before arrival, but none of that compares with a titre test and a three-month hold.

        The local side is lighter too. Antigua and Barbuda's own export paperwork is a single Official Government Health Certificate, valid 7 days, confirming the animal is healthy and has had a recent parasite treatment. There's no separate export permit to chase.

    - heading: "Getting a pet onto BA2157, and what happens if there's no space"
      body: |
        British Airways flies Gatwick to Antigua nonstop as BA2157, on a Boeing 777, and it's the only nonstop option either direction. It doesn't take pets in the cabin at all; every animal travels as manifested cargo, booked through PetAir UK rather than a normal ticket.

        Cargo holds on a route like this fill fast, so a booking made weeks in advance still isn't guaranteed. If BA's hold is full, American Airlines Cargo is a genuine fallback: fly Antigua to Miami on the nonstop AA2579/AA2580 service, then use American Airlines Cargo's transatlantic pet service into Heathrow or Manchester, a service the airline runs even though it won't check a pet as ordinary baggage on a UK-bound passenger flight.
