    - heading: "Category 2, and the no-quarantine policy"
      body: |
        Trinidad and Tobago's country list, revised January 2023, sorts every origin into four categories. The US sits in Category 2, requiring a FAVN or RFFIT titre test: blood drawn at least a month after vaccination, a result above 0.5 IU/mL, sent directly to the Chief Veterinary Officer by the testing laboratory. USDA's own official certificate template for this exact route confirms the same threshold.

        What doesn't apply is quarantine. Trinidad and Tobago has run a no-quarantine policy for compliant pets since April 2013, a genuine outlier among Caribbean and island-nation destinations, where a passing titre test usually still means a holding period on arrival.
