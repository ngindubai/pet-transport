  h1: "Pet Transport from Eswatini to United Arab Emirates"
  overview: |
    Book Etihad for this route and the booking will fail. Etihad Airways currently carries no pets on any flight touching Johannesburg, and Johannesburg is where every Eswatini pet has to go first, since King Mswati III International Airport has no long-haul service of its own. Emirates, which runs a nonstop overnight JNB-DXB service, is the airline that actually gets the job done.

    The regulatory side is lighter than the flight logistics. MOCCAE treats Eswatini as high-risk, so a titre test applies, but there is no residency requirement and no fixed wait once the result passes. Twelve to sixteen weeks covers this route comfortably, driven mostly by the export permit and titre test turnaround rather than any mandatory holding period.

    The Department of Veterinary and Livestock Services in Eswatini handles the export side: permit, inspection and certificate. Start that alongside the titre test, not after it.

  sections:
    - heading: "The Johannesburg bottleneck, and why Etihad is not an option"
      body: |
        Every long-haul pet move out of Eswatini starts the same way, with a short regional hop from King Mswati III International Airport to O.R. Tambo, Johannesburg, on Airlink or Eswatini Air. From there, the international leg has to actually accept live animal cargo out of Johannesburg specifically, which is where Etihad falls away: the airline currently lists no pet travel on any route touching that airport.

        Emirates fills the gap with a nonstop overnight service between Johannesburg and Dubai, booked through Emirates SkyCargo as manifested cargo. For this particular route, Emirates is not a preference among several options, it is the workable one.

    - heading: "What MOCCAE requires, and what it does not"
      body: |
        Eswatini sits outside MOCCAE's roughly 50-country low-risk list, so the FAVN or RSNT titre test is mandatory, blood drawn at least 21 days after vaccination, result of at least 0.5 IU/ml. What does not apply is a fixed forward wait after that result clears. The test simply has to be dated within a year, and at least three months, of the travel date, whichever end of that window suits the owner's schedule.

        That is a real saving against the EU or UK equivalent routes on this site, both of which impose a 90-day wait after a passing titre. Owners who assume every destination works that way will overbudget this leg by months.

    - heading: "A sharply different picture for Australia"
      body: |
        It is worth flagging how differently DAFF treats the same passport. Eswatini's non-approved status under Australia's rules means 180 consecutive days of residency in an approved third country before any import file even opens, a process that runs to 32 weeks or more elsewhere on this site. MOCCAE asks for a fraction of that. Same country of origin, two governments, two very different rabies risk assessments.
