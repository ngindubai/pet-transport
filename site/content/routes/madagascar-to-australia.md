content:
  h1: "Pet Transport from Madagascar to Australia"
  overview: |
    Six days a week, an Emirates 777 leaves Dubai, touches down in Mahe, then continues to Antananarivo, carrying roughly 22 tonnes of belly-hold cargo on the Madagascar sector alone. That flight already exists. For an Australia-bound pet, it also happens to solve half the hardest problem on this route.

    Madagascar sits on DAFF's non-approved list, so a direct booking to Melbourne or Perth simply isn't offered. The pet needs 180 consecutive days living in a DAFF-approved country first. Seychelles is Group 3 approved, and it's the same stop Emirates' existing schedule already passes through. That's a genuinely rare overlap: the staging country and the transit hub are one and the same, unlike most non-approved-country routes on this site, which need a flight to an unrelated third continent just to start the clock.

    None of that removes Australia's own requirements once the residency period is under way: a rabies titre test, and Mickleham's 10-day quarantine at the very end.

  sections:
    - heading: "Why Seychelles, and not South Africa or the UK"
      body: |
        Owners researching this route often assume South Africa, the usual Group 3 staging country for Southern African moves, is the obvious choice. For Madagascar specifically, Seychelles has the edge: it's Group 3 approved in its own right, and it's already on the Antananarivo flight most owners are looking at anyway, rather than adding a separate booking to Johannesburg or further afield.

        That doesn't make Seychelles free of paperwork. A pet still needs its own entry requirements met on arrival there, on top of everything Australia later asks for. Treat the Seychelles leg as a real move with its own compliance, not just a layover with a long residency attached.

    - heading: "Timing the titre test so two waits become one"
      body: |
        DAFF's RNATT guidance counts its 180-day wait from the date the approved laboratory receives the blood sample, not the vaccination date or the result date. Draw that blood in the first weeks of the Seychelles residency, once the standard post-vaccination gap has passed, and the titre wait runs alongside the 180-day residency requirement rather than adding months on top of it.

        Leave the blood draw until late in the residency and the two clocks stop overlapping. That single scheduling decision is the difference between a nine-month process and something closer to fourteen.

    - heading: "Mickleham, and the flight that closes it out"
      body: |
        Every pet entering Australia, whatever its route, finishes with a minimum 10-day stay at Mickleham Post Entry Quarantine Facility in Melbourne. Book the slot as soon as the Seychelles export paperwork has a firm date, and route the final leg through Dubai on Emirates, since the same carrier that started the journey in Antananarivo also runs the Mahe-Dubai-Australia connection. Mickleham takes weekday arrivals only, so build the flight around that, not the other way round.
