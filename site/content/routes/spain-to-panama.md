  sections:
    - heading: "MIDA requirements: what Panama checks on arrival"
      body: |
        Panama's Ministry of Agricultural Development (MIDA) handles pet import controls at Tocumen International Airport (PTY) in Panama City. MIDA veterinarians check your pet's microchip, vaccination records, health certificate, and antiparasitic treatment documentation on arrival. The registration fee is approximately USD 20 per pet, paid in cash at the MIDA airport office.

        Your pet must be over 4 months old to enter Panama. Pets younger than this are not admitted. The rabies vaccination must have been given at least 30 days before entry and must be current. Dogs need current distemper, hepatitis, leptospirosis, and parvovirus vaccinations. Cats need rhinotracheitis, panleukopenia, and feline leukaemia on record.

        No quarantine is required for compliant arrivals. No import permit is needed for personal companion animals. Antiparasitic treatment must be carried out within 30 days of departure, documented with the product name, dose, and application date.

    - heading: "The embassy authentication step: Madrid makes it manageable"
      body: |
        Panama requires the health certificate to be authenticated by its diplomatic mission in the origin country. From Spain, this means the Embassy of Panama in Madrid.

        The sequence is: veterinario oficial examination and AHC issue, MAPA endorsement (via the autonomous community's official veterinary service), then submission to the Embassy of Panama in Madrid for authentication. The embassy typically takes 2-3 working days and charges a consulate fee (confirm the current amount when you book your appointment).

        Given that the AHC is valid for 10 days from issue, the timing is tight but manageable from Madrid where Copa also offers direct cargo to PTY. If your cargo departs on a Friday, the vet appointment should be no later than Monday. Book the embassy appointment before fixing your cargo date.
