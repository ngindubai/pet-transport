content:
  h1: "Pet Transport from Iceland to Australia"
  overview: |
    Group 2 is the card Iceland holds in Australia's biosecurity system, and it's a good one. No rabies titre test, no 90 or 180-day wait tied to a blood draw, because DAFF already treats Iceland as rabies-free. That single fact cuts months off a process that grinds on for most other origin countries.

    What Group 2 does not remove is the 10-day stay at Mickleham, DAFF's only quarantine facility, or the import permit, or the need for a government vet to check the microchip in person before departure. Nor does it change the flight problem: nothing flies Keflavik to Melbourne without at least one stop, and since Icelandair pulled pets from its passenger cabin holds in November 2024, every leg of that journey now moves as cargo.

  sections:
    - heading: "What Group 2 actually buys you, and what it does not"
      body: |
        DAFF sorts countries into six groups by rabies risk. Iceland sits in Group 2, the rabies-free tier that also covers Japan and Singapore. For a Group 2 origin, the rabies titre test simply does not apply, and neither does the waiting period that comes with it for Groups 3 and above.

        That is the whole benefit. Everything else on the Australian side stays in place: the microchip, the DAFF import permit through BICON, the pre-export identity check by a government-approved vet, and the mandatory 10 days at Mickleham. Owners moving from a country with obvious rabies exposure sometimes assume Iceland's clean bill of health means no quarantine at all. It doesn't work that way. Mickleham is the price of entry for every group, Group 2 included.

    - heading: "Melbourne or nowhere: the direct-flight rule"
      body: |
        Australia will only accept an arriving pet on a direct international flight into Melbourne, the sole city with a quarantine-approved facility. Sydney, Brisbane and Perth are not options for the final sector, whatever your actual destination in Australia turns out to be. New Zealand and Norfolk Island departures get an exemption from this rule. Iceland does not.

        In practice this means booking a routing where the last leg is a genuine nonstop into Melbourne, not a connection through another Australian city. Emirates' Dubai-Melbourne sector, run in cooperation with Qantas's shared Heathrow livestock facility, is one workable way to satisfy that condition after the initial Keflavik-London hop.

    - heading: "No direct flight, and cargo-only since November 2024"
      body: |
        Icelandair's KEF-LHR service is the natural first leg out of Iceland, running close to daily. From London, the onward long-haul sector into Melbourne needs a carrier with both a live-animal facility and, ideally, a direct final approach into Melbourne rather than a same-country connection.

        Since 1 November 2024, Icelandair does not carry pets as checked or excess baggage on any of its routes, this one included. The animal travels as manifested cargo through Icelandair Cargo from the first leg, which changes the booking process and the lead times compared with a standard passenger ticket.

faqs:
  - question: "Does a pet from Iceland need a rabies titre test to enter Australia?"
    answer: "No. Iceland is classified in DAFF's Group 2, the rabies-free tier, which removes the titre test and its associated waiting period. The pet still needs a microchip, a DAFF import permit, an identity check by a government-approved vet, and 10 days of quarantine at Mickleham on arrival."
  - question: "Is quarantine required for Iceland-origin pets entering Australia?"
    answer: "Yes. Every dog and cat arriving in Australia, Group 2 origins included, completes a minimum of 10 days at the Mickleham Post Entry Quarantine Facility in Melbourne. Group 2 status shortens the pre-export process, not the quarantine itself."
  - question: "Can I fly my pet directly from Iceland to Australia?"
    answer: "No direct flight exists between Keflavik and any Australian city. Routing runs through at least one hub, typically London Heathrow, with the final international sector required to be a direct flight into Melbourne, the only DAFF quarantine-approved port. Since 1 November 2024, Icelandair moves pets as cargo rather than checked baggage on every route it flies."
  - question: "Are any dog breeds banned from entering Australia from Iceland?"
    answer: "Yes, and this has nothing to do with Iceland's own rules. The American Pit Bull Terrier, Japanese Tosa, Dogo Argentino, Fila Brasileiro and Perro de Presa Canario are banned outright from import under Australian federal law, along with wolf-dog hybrids. There is no permit or exemption for these types, regardless of origin country."
  - question: "How long does the whole Iceland to Australia process take?"
    answer: "Allow 10 to 14 weeks. The DAFF import permit takes 20-30 days to process, and you should run the Mickleham quarantine booking and the government vet's identity check alongside that. Because Iceland's Group 2 status removes the titre test and its waiting period, this route is considerably shorter than the 22 to 40 weeks typical of Group 3 and Group 4 origins such as the UK, the USA or most of the EU."
