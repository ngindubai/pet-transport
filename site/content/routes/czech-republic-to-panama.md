content:
  h1: "Pet Transport from Czech Republic to Panama"
  overview: |
    Moving a pet from the Czech Republic to Panama is a manageable process once you understand the authentication requirement. Panama's MIDA (Ministerio de Desarrollo Agropecuario) requires the health certificate to be authenticated by the Embassy of Panama in Prague before departure. That step comes after the SVS endorsement and must fit within the 10-day validity window of the certificate. Coordinating those two steps in the right order is the main challenge on this route.

    There is no import permit, no quarantine, and no titre test. Your pet needs an ISO microchip, a rabies vaccination given at least 30 days before entry, and an official health certificate issued within 10 days of travel. A MIDA inspection takes place on arrival at Tocumen International Airport (PTY) in Panama City, and a small registration fee of approximately USD 20 applies.

    On the Czech side, the SVS (Statni veterinarni sprava, svscr.cz) manages the endorsement of the EU-format health certificate. Only an SVS-authorised official vet can issue it. Prague (PRG) connects to Panama City (PTY) via Copa Airlines, Iberia via Madrid, or Air France via Paris CDG.

  sections:
    - heading: "The Embassy Authentication Step"
      body: |
        Panama requires the origin-country health certificate to be authenticated by a Panamanian Embassy or Consulate before the animal travels. For Czech Republic residents, this is the Embassy of Panama in Prague. Contact the Embassy in advance to find out current processing times, required documents, and fees. Processing typically takes two to five working days, but this can vary.

        The sequence is: SVS-authorised vet signs the certificate, SVS endorses it, then the Embassy of Panama in Prague authenticates it. All of this must happen within the 10-day validity window of the certificate. If the Embassy requires several working days, you may need to schedule the vet appointment earlier in the window and arrange swift delivery of the document to and from the Embassy. A pet transport agent can handle this for you.

    - heading: "Czech Export Requirements"
      body: |
        The Czech export process follows standard EU procedures. No export permit is needed. An SVS-authorised official vet conducts the pre-travel examination and issues the EU-format health certificate. The SVS then endorses it. Finding an SVS-authorised vet early in the process is sensible; not all private clinics hold this status, and appointment availability varies.

        The certificate is valid for 10 days from the date of the vet's signature. Given that you also need Embassy authentication within that same window, the appointment should be scheduled as close to departure as the Embassy's processing time allows, working backwards from your flight date. Vaclav Havel Airport Prague (PRG) is the departure hub for all cargo on this route.

    - heading: "Panama Import Rules"
      body: |
        MIDA inspectors at Tocumen International Airport (PTY) check the microchip, rabies vaccination record, and the authenticated health certificate. The rabies jab must have been given at least 30 days before arrival, and the pet must be older than four months. No titre test is required, and there is no quarantine period.

        The MIDA registration on arrival costs approximately USD 20. This is paid at the airport. Have the original health certificate, vaccination records, and microchip documentation ready for the MIDA inspector. Documents in Spanish are preferred; English documents are generally accepted but a Spanish translation can smooth the process.

    - heading: "Airline Options from Prague"
      body: |
        Copa Airlines is the most direct option for Panama routes, operating via its Panama City hub at PTY. Iberia cargo via Madrid (MAD) and Air France via Paris CDG both offer connecting services. All three accept pets as manifest cargo; IATA-compliant crates are required. Cargo bookings should be made four to six weeks before travel. Confirm acceptance of your specific animal and crate dimensions with the airline's live animal desk before finalising the booking.

        Costs for cargo from Prague to PTY typically run EUR 800-2,000 for the air freight alone, depending on pet size and crate. Add SVS and official vet fees (EUR 100-250), the IATA crate if needed (EUR 60-220), Embassy authentication (variable), and agent fees if used (EUR 250-600). Most single-pet moves from Prague to Panama come out between EUR 1,300 and EUR 3,000 all-in.
