content:
  h1: "Pet Transport from Australia to Paraguay"
  overview: |
    Australia has a reputation, deserved, as the hardest country in the world to bring a pet into. None of that applies on the way out. DAFF asks for no formal export permit and no BICON approval for a personal pet leaving the country, just a vet's health certificate close to departure.

    Paraguay's side is lighter still than most owners expect. SENACSA doesn't publish a titre test requirement for companion animals arriving under its standard non-commercial route, and Australia's rabies-free status removes the usual reason to ask for one. What doesn't relax is the breed check: several breeds are refused outright, with zero exceptions, and that needs settling before anything else is booked.

  sections:
    - heading: "Why leaving Australia is the easy part"
      body: |
        There's no BICON permit, no group classification, and no waiting period on the Australian export side. A DAFF-authorised vet examines the pet, issues a health certificate, and that document becomes the foundation for whatever SENACSA needs on arrival. It's a five-minute contrast with the country's own import rules, and worth pointing out to anyone who assumes "strict country" means strict in both directions.

        Practically, that means the timeline for this route is set almost entirely by Paraguay's requirements and by getting a booking on the Santiago or Sao Paulo corridor, not by anything DAFF demands of you.

    - heading: "What SENACSA actually asks for"
      body: |
        A dog or cat entering Paraguay needs an ISO microchip, current core vaccinations, and a CVI built from the Australian health certificate. SENACSA caps non-commercial imports at five pets per owner, and bans a specific list of breeds, including Pit Bull-type dogs, American Staffordshire Terrier, American Bully, Cane Corso, Boerboel and several named mastiff breeds, regardless of documentation or temperament.

        None of that is published with total clarity in English. If your pet is anywhere near the banned list by type or mix, confirm directly with SENACSA before you spend money on anything else.

    - heading: "Santiago or Sao Paulo: picking the routing"
      body: |
        LATAM's nonstop Melbourne-Santiago service, six times a week, is the strongest single-carrier option, with Qantas codesharing three of those departures. From Santiago, LATAM's own network reaches Asuncion directly. Qatar Airways offers a genuine alternative through Doha and Sao Paulo, useful if your schedule or fare options favour the Gulf routing instead, though it adds a transfer.
