  overview: |
    Dogs leaving Singapore for Montevideo need a treatment most owners moving a pet elsewhere in this build have never heard of: a praziquantel-based echinococcus dose, timed to a narrow window of 72 hours to 30 days before arrival. Cats skip it entirely.

    Beyond that specific step, this route follows a pattern common to several of Uruguay's lighter corridors: no titre test, but an MGAP import permit that some neighbouring Latin American countries don't ask for at all. The flight itself needs at least two connections, since nothing links Singapore to Montevideo directly.

  sections:
    - heading: "The echinococcus treatment: narrow, dog-specific, easy to miss"
      body: |
        Dogs entering Uruguay need documented treatment against Echinococcus (Taenia), using a praziquantel-based product, given no less than 72 hours and no more than 30 days before arrival. It's entirely separate from the rabies vaccination timeline, so track it as its own date on the calendar rather than assuming it falls into place automatically alongside everything else.

        Cats face no such requirement. If you're moving a mixed household, keep the two species' checklists separate rather than working from a single combined timeline.

    - heading: "An import permit that catches out no-titre-test assumptions"
      body: |
        Uruguay requires an import permit from MGAP-DGSG before a pet can enter the country, on top of skipping the titre test most owners expect on a long-haul move like this one. Panama, which shares the same no-titre-test policy, doesn't ask for an import permit at all. Apply for Uruguay's permit well ahead of the travel date rather than assuming a lighter overall process just because the titre test is off the list.

    - heading: "Getting from Changi to Carrasco"
      body: |
        No airline flies Singapore to Montevideo directly. Qatar Airways' expanded 2026 South American network, running through Doha and connecting via Sao Paulo, offers a plausible route on a single carrier group, though each sector still needs separate cargo confirmation. LATAM's regional dominance covers the final Sao Paulo or Santiago to Montevideo leg for owners connecting through a different long-haul carrier first.
