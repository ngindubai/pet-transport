  h1: "Pet Transport South Africa to Canada: 2026 Guide"
  overview: "South Africa to Canada is a straightforward pet move compared to South Africa to Australia. Canada does not require a titre test, quarantine, or Ehrlichia canis testing for personal pets.   sections:
    - heading: "Canada: a more accessible destination than Australia"
      body: |
        Canada's pet import requirements are relatively light compared to Australia. No titre test, no quarantine, no group classification system. For personal pets from South Africa, the requirements are: current rabies vaccination, health certificate in CFIA format from a DALRRD-authorised vet, and microchip.

        The Canadian Food Inspection Agency (CFIA) manages animal imports. Their requirements are practical and do not create the multi-month preparation timelines that Australia's regime demands. A South African owner who is moving to Canada can realistically prepare their pet for the journey in 6-8 weeks, assuming vaccinations are already in order.

        There is one caveat: commercial dog imports to Canada are subject to additional restrictions, and CFIA periodically updates its rules on dogs imported in numbers. For a single family dog or cat, this is not relevant. For anyone importing more than 2 dogs, confirm the current commercial import rules with CFIA before making arrangements.

    - heading: "The journey: long-haul with a connection"
      body: |
        No airline operates a direct South Africa to Canada service. The typical routing is either via Dubai with Emirates (then to Toronto, Vancouver, or Calgary) or via London with British Airways (then connect to any Canadian city on Air Canada or other carriers).

        The total journey time, including connections, is typically 18-24 hours. For pets travelling as cargo, this means carefully chosen carriers with good animal welfare standards during the transit. Emirates SkyCargo and IAG Cargo (BA) both have established pet transport protocols.

        Long-haul journeys with connections are harder on pets than short direct flights. Brachycephalic breeds (Bulldogs, Pugs, French Bulldogs) should be assessed carefully before committing to this journey, particularly in summer months when heat embargoes are in place.

faqs:
  - question: "Does my pet need a rabies titre test to enter Canada from South Africa?"
    answer: "No. Canada does not require a rabies titre test for personal pets from South Africa. The requirements are a current rabies vaccination, an ISO microchip, and a CFIA-format health certificate from a DALRRD-authorised South African vet. No quarantine is required for compliant arrivals."
  - question: "Which airline is best for a large dog from South Africa to Canada?"
    answer: "Emirates via Dubai to Toronto or Vancouver is the most commonly used route for this journey. Emirates SkyCargo has experience with South Africa to North America pet moves. British Airways via London and then Air Canada to the Canadian city is another option. Both involve a connection; discuss the transit conditions with the carrier when booking."
  - question: "Does my dog need tick treatment before going to Canada from South Africa?"
    answer: "Canada does not formally require tick treatment as an entry condition. However, South Africa has significant tick-borne disease risk, and a dog that leaves South Africa with a tick infestation or active tick-borne infection may become seriously ill after arrival. Tick prevention and, ideally, tick-borne disease testing before departure is strongly recommended by pet transport agents who work on this route."
  - question: "How long does the DALRRD export process take?"
    answer: "Allow 3-4 weeks for the export permit from DALRRD. The health certificate itself is issued within 10 days of travel by the DALRRD-authorised vet. In total, start the DALRRD process 4-6 weeks before your travel date."
