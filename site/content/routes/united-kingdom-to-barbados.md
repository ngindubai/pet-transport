content:
  h1: "Pet Transport from the United Kingdom to Barbados"
  overview: |
    Barbados will only let a dog or cat off a plane if that plane flew non-stop from London and lands on a weekday. It's an unusual, specific rule, written into the Barbados import conditions that the UK's own export certificate for this route, 3890EHC, spells out in full. Get the flight wrong and no amount of correct paperwork fixes it.

    Everything else about this move is more familiar. A UK-resident pet skips the rabies blood test entirely, needs an import permit that costs BDS $25.00, and faces no quarantine on arrival if the documents are right.

  sections:
    - heading: "One flight, one airline, no exceptions"
      body: |
        This is the detail that catches people out. Barbados's import conditions specify that a pet arriving by air must come in on a non-stop flight from London; connecting through any other city, even briefly, doesn't meet the rule. In practice that means British Airways, the only carrier flying Heathrow or Gatwick to Bridgetown without a stop.

        The day of the week matters too. Arrivals have to land Monday to Friday, and ideally within the 8.15am to 4.30pm working hours of Barbados's Veterinary Services, or an overtime inspection fee applies. A weekend or bank holiday arrival isn't blocked outright, but it costs more and needs advance planning with the vet on duty.

    - heading: "Why a UK-resident pet skips the blood test"
      body: |
        The UK sits in Barbados's Category 1 group, its lower-risk tier for rabies, alongside around 70 other countries and territories. A pet that's lived in the UK for at least 3 months, or since birth, gets that written onto certificate 3890EHC as a residency declaration, and the FAVN titre test that Category 2 pets need simply doesn't apply.

        That's a meaningful saving in both time and money. Miss the residency declaration, or move a pet that's only recently arrived in the UK from somewhere else, and the process reverts to a blood sample, a laboratory wait, and a 90-day hold before Barbados will let the animal in.

    - heading: "The permit, the notice period, and the paperwork chain"
      body: |
        Barbados's Import Permit has to be applied for ahead of the flight, through Veterinary Services, for a flat BDS $25.00. It's valid for 6 months once granted, which gives some flexibility if a flight date shifts.

        Less well known: the exporter or their agent has to formally notify Barbados's Veterinary Services at least 3 working days before the pet lands, giving the owner's Barbados address, the customs broker's contact details, and the specific flight number. It's a small step, easy to overlook next to the certificate and the permit, but it's the one that actually gets the animal cleared at the airport.
