content:
  h1: "Pet Transport from the United States to Belize"
  overview: |
    Leaving the US side of this move is close to routine. It's landing in Belize where the detail matters: a BAHA import permit that costs just BZ$50 and covers up to 10 pets, set against a health certificate that expires if it's more than 14 days old on arrival.

    Four US carriers now fly direct into Belize City, more choice than this route has ever had. Only one of them, American Airlines, has a working cargo pet option for anything larger than a small in-cabin dog or cat, which narrows the practical booking decision considerably once you know it.

  sections:
    - heading: "The permit is cheap. The timing is the real cost"
      body: |
        BZ$50 for an import permit that covers up to 10 animals and stays valid for 90 days is about as light as pet import paperwork gets. Don't let that lull you into leaving the certificate to the last minute, though, because Belize only accepts one issued within 14 days of arrival.

        Sequence matters here. Vaccination first, timed so it sits at least 30 days before your actual Belize landing date, not your US departure date. Then the permit application, once flights are booked. Then the certificate last, inside that 14-day window. Doing it in a different order is the most reliable way to end up with a document that's technically expired by the time your pet touches down.

    - heading: "Why American is the practical choice for anything but a small pet"
      body: |
        Delta flies Atlanta to Belize City. United flies Houston to Belize City. Both look like solid options on a flight search, and for a small dog or cat travelling in the cabin, either works fine. Neither, though, currently offers a general cargo pet service: Delta's is restricted to military and State Department relocations, and United's has been suspended for regular customers for years.

        That leaves American Airlines, flying direct from Miami, Dallas/Fort Worth, Charlotte and Los Angeles, as the only carrier of the four actually set up to move a larger dog into Belize City's cargo hold. Confirm current acceptance before booking regardless, since American's own cargo pet availability has had gaps in the past.
