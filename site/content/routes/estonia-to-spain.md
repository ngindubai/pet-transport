content:
  h1: "Pet Transport Estonia to Spain: 2026 Guide"
  overview: "Estonia and Spain are both EU member states, so this move is covered by the EU pet travel framework. Your pet needs a microchip, a current rabies vaccination, and an EU pet passport or AHC. No quarantine, no titre test, no import permit. The practical challenge is finding a carrier that accepts pets: many of the most affordable Tallinn-to-Spain routes are with budget airlines that do not take animals. Full-service carriers connecting via Riga, Warsaw, Frankfurt, or Amsterdam are the better options. The journey takes 5-9 hours depending on the connection."

  sections:
    - heading: "Carrier options from Tallinn to Spain"
      body: |
        Tallinn Airport (TLL) has decent connectivity to Spain, but most direct-ish routes run on budget carriers. Ryanair offers services to Madrid Barajas (MAD), Barcelona El Prat (BCN), Alicante (ALC), Malaga (AGP), and others. Wizz Air has some Spain routes too. Neither accepts pets.

        For a pet owner, the options are: airBaltic via Riga, LOT via Warsaw, or Lufthansa via Frankfurt. airBaltic's TLL-RIX connection feeds into its European network and reaches Spanish airports with cabin pet acceptance on the connecting flights. LOT's Warsaw hub offers connections to Madrid and Barcelona with a smaller pet in cabin.

        For a large dog travelling as cargo, Frankfurt (Lufthansa) or Amsterdam (KLM) are the most reliable intermediate hubs with live animal cargo handling. The FRA-MAD or FRA-BCN Lufthansa cargo segments are well-served.

    - heading: "Spain's domestic requirements after arrival"
      body: |
        Once in Spain, dogs have domestic registration and licensing obligations that are separate from the EU travel documentation. Spain requires dogs to be registered with the local municipality. The specifics vary by region (each Spanish autonomous community sets some of its own rules), but in general you need to register with the Registro de Animales de Compania at your local Ayuntamiento (town hall) and obtain a licence.

        Cats do not have a mandatory national registration scheme in Spain, though some municipalities have local cat registration systems. Check the rules for your specific Spanish city or town.

        These are post-arrival domestic obligations. Your EU pet passport covers the travel itself. Having the passport available when registering in Spain can be useful as it provides a clear record of vaccinations and microchip information.

faqs:
  - question: "Can I take two dogs from Estonia to Spain in a single trip?"
    answer: "Yes, for non-commercial movement. EU rules allow up to five dogs, cats, or ferrets to travel with an owner in a non-commercial move, as long as each has a valid EU pet passport or AHC and meets all vaccination and microchip requirements. Each animal needs its own documentation. Some airlines limit the total number of pets per passenger, so check with your chosen carrier before booking."
  - question: "My rabies vaccination expires during my journey. Is this a problem?"
    answer: "You should ensure the vaccination is current on the date of entry into Spain, not just on the date of departure from Estonia. If the vaccination expires mid-journey or very shortly after arrival, get a booster before travel. A lapsed vaccination at the Spanish border, even if technically in a transit situation, puts your pet's entry at risk."
  - question: "Does Spain's MAPA conduct checks at airports for intra-EU pet arrivals?"
    answer: "Spain does not routinely check EU pet passports or AHCs at airports for intra-EU arrivals. Checks happen primarily at airline check-in and, if applicable, on entry from non-EU countries. However, you must carry the documents. Spanish authorities can request them at any time, and in the event of any animal health concern, documentation will be checked."
