content:
  h1: "Pet Transport Uruguay to Czech Republic: 2026 Guide"
  overview: "Uruguay to the Czech Republic is a high-complexity international pet transport route. Uruguay is an unlisted third country under Commission Delegated Regulation (EU) 2026/131, requiring the FAVN titre test pathway before EU entry. The journey itself involves multiple cargo legs from Carrasco International Airport (MVD) in Montevideo through South American and European hubs to Prague. Preparation runs 20-26 weeks. With careful planning, the process is entirely manageable, and the Czech Republic's State Veterinary Administration (SVS) has no quarantine requirement for compliant arrivals."

  sections:
    - heading: "SVS Requirements: What the Czech Republic Needs"
      body: |
        The State Veterinary Administration (SVS, svscr.cz) handles veterinary border checks at Prague Václav Havel Airport (PRG). For a pet from Uruguay, SVS requires:

        Microchip: ISO 11784/11785 standard, implanted before the rabies vaccination. SVS scans the chip on arrival and matches it against the health certificate.

        Rabies vaccination: Current, post-microchip, with 21 days elapsed since the primary vaccination.

        FAVN titre test: Performed at an EU-approved laboratory. Blood drawn at least 30 days after vaccination. Result of 0.5 IU/ml or above. A minimum of 90 days must have elapsed from the blood draw date to the date of arrival at PRG.

        Health certificate: EU Annex IV non-commercial format, signed by a MGAP-registered vet and endorsed by DGSG, issued within 10 days of travel. The titre test details must be included in full.

        No quarantine. No import permit. But documentation must be precise.

    - heading: "Uruguay's Export Authority: MGAP-DGSG"
      body: |
        The Dirección General de Servicios Ganaderos (DGSG) of Uruguay's Ministerio de Ganadería, Agricultura y Pesca (MGAP, mgap.gub.uy) is the authority responsible for endorsing health certificates for companion animal exports from Uruguay.

        The certificate is prepared by a MGAP-registered vet and must follow the EU Annex IV non-commercial health certificate format. It must include:

        - The microchip number (ISO 11784/11785)
        - Rabies vaccination details: product name, batch number, date administered, and expiry
        - FAVN titre test details: laboratory name, blood draw date, result in IU/ml, confirmation that 90 days have elapsed from the draw date

        DGSG endorses the document. Allow three to five business days for the endorsement process. The certificate is only valid for 10 days from the date of issue, so time it carefully relative to your cargo departure from MVD.

    - heading: "Multi-Leg Routing from Montevideo to Prague"
      body: |
        MVD does not have direct long-haul cargo services to Central Europe. The journey to Prague typically involves two or three legs.

        The most common routing uses LATAM Airlines from MVD to São Paulo (GRU) or Santiago (SCL), then a transatlantic carrier (LATAM, Air France, KLM, or Lufthansa) from GRU or SCL to a European hub (FRA, AMS, CDG, or MAD), and finally a shorter second leg to Prague (PRG).

        Iberia Cargo is a strong alternative, routing via Buenos Aires Ezeiza (EZE) or direct MVD-MAD. From MAD, Iberia or a partner carrier connects to PRG.

        KLM via Amsterdam is practical from São Paulo. GRU to AMS via KLM Cargo, then AMS to PRG via KLM or partner. The AMS to PRG sector is short and well served.

        Lufthansa via Frankfurt offers access to the best animal handling infrastructure in Europe. GRU or EZE to FRA, then FRA to PRG. Frankfurt to Prague is one of Europe's shortest cargo hops.

        Book each sector of the journey and confirm live animal acceptance on every leg. Not all flights on every carrier accept live animals, even if the carrier routinely handles pet cargo on other routes.

    - heading: "Managing a Three-Leg Cargo Journey"
      body: |
        A three-leg cargo journey from MVD to PRG via a South American hub and a European hub requires careful logistics. Here is what happens at each transfer:

        At the South American hub (GRU or EZE): the animal stays in the crate in the live animal facility while the cargo is transferred to the transatlantic carrier. Handlers check the crate, provide water if needed, and prepare the animal for the next leg. Your freight forwarder coordinates this transfer.

        At the European hub (FRA, AMS, or MAD): the animal transfers to the final carrier bound for PRG. Again, handlers attend to the animal at the live animal facility.

        At PRG: SVS inspection takes place before the animal is released to you.

        Total travel time from MVD to PRG is typically 36-48 hours. This is long, but well within what healthy adult dogs and cats can manage.

    - heading: "Document Verification at SVS Inspection"
      body: |
        When SVS inspectors at PRG review your documentation, they are looking for complete and consistent information across all documents:

        The chip number on the health certificate must match the chip scanned from the animal.

        The vaccination date on the health certificate must be after the chip implantation date.

        The blood draw date for the FAVN test must be at least 30 days after the vaccination date.

        The arrival date in the Czech Republic must be at least 90 days after the blood draw date.

        All four of these checks involve arithmetic. Practise these calculations yourself before the certificate is endorsed, and check them again when you receive the DGSG-endorsed document.

faqs:
  - question: "Can the FAVN blood draw happen in Argentina rather than Uruguay if we have connections there?"
    answer: "Yes. The location of the blood draw does not have to be Uruguay, as long as the sample is sent to an EU-approved laboratory and the timing conditions are met (at least 30 days after vaccination). If your MGAP-registered vet in Uruguay draws the blood or if you use a vet in Argentina, what matters is the EU-approved laboratory that analyses the sample. The result must be on the official laboratory report and the blood draw date must be clearly recorded."
  - question: "How long does the FAVN test result take to come back from the laboratory?"
    answer: "It depends on the laboratory and the shipping time for the sample. International samples shipped from Uruguay to labs in France, the UK, or the US typically take 10-21 days from the date the blood is drawn to the date the result is received. Factor this into your timeline planning. The 90-day wait begins from the blood draw date regardless of when the result arrives, so the result does not affect the wait period start date."
  - question: "We need to move in 12 weeks. Is there any way to get our cat from Uruguay to the Czech Republic in that time?"
    answer: "No. The mandatory titre test pathway for Uruguay-to-EU pet transport requires a minimum of 20 weeks from the start of preparations. The 30-day pre-draw wait plus the 90-day post-draw wait account for 120 days (17 weeks) on their own, before travel can happen. There is no legal shortcut. If your timeline is 12 weeks, options include leaving the cat with a trusted carer in Uruguay until the titre test process is complete, or beginning the process now and adjusting your travel plans. Contact PetTransportGlobal to discuss the options."
