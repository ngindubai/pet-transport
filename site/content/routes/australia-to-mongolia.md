  h1: "Pet Transport from Australia to Mongolia"
  overview: |
    Korean Air flies Sydney to Seoul every day of the week. It also, by its own published policy, does not carry pets to or from Australia at all. That single fact rules out the gateway this site's UK and US Mongolia pages depend on, and it's the first thing worth knowing before booking anything.

    What replaces it is two real options: Air China's nonstop Sydney-Beijing service, connecting to MIAT's short hop on to Ulaanbaatar, or Turkish Airlines' Sydney-Istanbul route, which only started running daily in January 2026. Neither existed as a clean single-carrier path from Australia much before this year.

    The regulatory side barely deserves the word demanding. No titre test, no import permit for a personal pet, no quarantine on a compliant arrival. Mongolia's rules don't reference country of origin at all, so Australia gets exactly what the UK and the US get.

  sections:
    - heading: "Why Korean Air's Seoul gateway is a dead end from here"
      body: |
        Search this route and Korean Air's daily Sydney-Seoul flight, plus its onward Incheon-Ulaanbaatar service, looks like the obvious answer. It's the same airline this site's UK and US Mongolia pages rely on, after all.

        Korean Air's own published list of destinations it will carry a pet to or from does not include Australia. That's not a Mongolia-specific restriction, it's a blanket one, and it means the frequent, comfortable Sydney-Seoul flight simply isn't available for this leg of the journey, no matter how far ahead you book it.

    - heading: "Beijing and Istanbul: the two doors that do work"
      body: |
        Air China runs the only nonstop service between Australia and China, six times a week between Sydney and Beijing on a Boeing 787, connecting to MIAT Mongolian Airlines' short hop on to Ulaanbaatar. Air China suspended dog transport specifically on flights into the United States from 1 February 2025. Read literally, that shouldn't touch a Sydney-Beijing-Ulaanbaatar routing at all, since neither leg goes anywhere near America. Confirm it directly with Air China Cargo before relying on the distinction.

        Turkish Airlines is the newer option. Its daily Sydney-Istanbul service only started in January 2026, currently flown via Kuala Lumpur, and connects to the same single-ticket Istanbul-Ulaanbaatar routing this site's UK page already documents. A newly launched route can mean tighter live animal capacity than an established one, so book with some slack either way.

    - heading: "Getting the paperwork right, since there isn't much of it"
      body: |
        A microchip, a rabies vaccination given within the last year, and a health certificate issued close to travel cover the entire medical side. No titre test. No import permit for an owner travelling with their own dog or cat. Mongolia's rules simply don't split arrivals by country of origin, so there's nothing extra here for an Australian pet compared with any other.

        The trade-off, as with the UK and US versions of this route, is thin published guidance. Confirm the current position directly with GAVS or a vet close to Ulaanbaatar before finalising a travel date.
