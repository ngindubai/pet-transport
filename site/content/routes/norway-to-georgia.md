  h1: "Pet Transport from Norway to Georgia"
  overview: |
    Georgia is a straightforward destination for EEA-origin pets: no titre test, no quarantine, and veterinary inspection at Tbilisi International Airport on arrival. The documentation is manageable, and preparations can be completed in three to five weeks assuming vaccinations are current.

    The main logistical consideration is the cargo routing. There are no direct cargo flights from Oslo to Tbilisi. Turkish Airlines via Istanbul is the primary option, with Lufthansa via Frankfurt as an alternative for summer travel when temperature embargoes may affect Istanbul connections.

    Norway's Mattilsynet endorsement process adds a 1-2 working day buffer to the health certificate timeline. This is easily planned around, but it means you cannot book the official vet examination for the day before departure.

  sections:
    - heading: "What Georgia requires at the border"
      body: |
        Georgia's veterinary import requirements are administered by the National Food Agency (NFA), nea.gov.ge. Officers check documentation at Tbilisi International Airport (TBS) on arrival before releasing your pet.

        The core requirements are a valid rabies vaccination administered at least 21 days before entry, an official health certificate issued within 10 days of travel, and an ISO-compliant microchip. Georgia does not require a titre test for pets arriving from EEA countries, which means preparation is significantly shorter than on some non-EU routes.

        For 1-2 personal companion animals, no import permit is required. If you are travelling with more animals, confirm the commercial threshold with NFA Georgia before travel. Georgia does not operate a breed ban list for common companion animals, but verify with NFA directly if your pet is a breed that faces restrictions elsewhere.

    - heading: "The Norwegian health certificate process"
      body: |
        Norway's official health certificate for destinations outside the EU requires a two-step process. The certificate is signed by a Mattilsynet-authorised official vet, then submitted to the Mattilsynet regional office for endorsement. The endorsed certificate is what the Georgian border authority will inspect.

        Time this so the vet examination falls within 10 days of your departure date. After the vet signs the certificate, submit it to the regional Mattilsynet office immediately. Allow 1-2 working days for the endorsement. If travelling on a Monday, the vet appointment should be no later than the preceding Wednesday.

        Your regular vet can administer vaccinations and implant microchips, but the official health certificate must be issued by a Mattilsynet-authorised official vet. Check mattilsynet.no for the list of authorised vets in your area.

    - heading: "Cargo routing from Oslo to Tbilisi"
      body: |
        There is no direct cargo service from Oslo (OSL) to Tbilisi (TBS). Turkish Airlines Cargo via Istanbul (IST) is the standard option. Turkish Airlines operates from OSL to IST, with connections to TBS from Istanbul's main hub. Book through an approved Turkish Airlines cargo agent and confirm pet acceptance on both legs.

        During June to September, Turkish Airlines applies temperature embargoes on certain route segments, and brachycephalic breed restrictions also apply year-round. If your travel date falls in summer or your pet is a flat-faced breed, confirm acceptance in detail before finalising the booking.

        Lufthansa Cargo from Oslo to Frankfurt (FRA) with onward connection to Tbilisi provides an alternative. Frankfurt's Animal Lounge handles regulated live animal transfers, and the routing avoids the Istanbul summer embargoes. Coordinate all legs through your cargo agent before committing to a travel date.
