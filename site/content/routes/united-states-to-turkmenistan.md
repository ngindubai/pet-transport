content:
  h1: "Pet Transport from United States to Turkmenistan"
  overview: |
    An Alabai can move to Turkmenistan. It just can never move away again. That's the genuine asymmetry worth knowing before anything else on this route: the breed's 2022 export ban, already documented on this site's Turkmenistan-to-US page, has no mirror-image rule on the import side. A US owner relocating with a Central Asian Shepherd Dog faces no special breed barrier getting in.

    The real gate on this route isn't the pet at all. It's the owner's own Letter of Invitation, certified by Turkmenistan's State Migration Service and required before entry for anyone without an existing visa category, tourist or otherwise. That single requirement is why this corridor realistically serves people with a confirmed reason to be in Ashgabat already, a job, a posting, a sponsored move, rather than a speculative relocation.

  sections:
    - heading: "The visa comes before the vet appointment matters"
      body: |
        Most routes on this site treat the pet's paperwork as the long pole in the timeline. Here, the owner's own entry document usually is. A Letter of Invitation has to be obtained through a licensed Turkmenistan travel agency or a sponsoring employer, certified by the State Migration Service, and it's only valid for 3 months from that certification. Processing commonly takes 15-20 working days on top of whatever lead time the agency itself needs.

        A presidential decree signed in April 2025 points toward e-visas and dropping the LOI requirement for some visitor categories in the future. Whether that's landed by the time you're planning this move is worth confirming directly rather than assuming; the traditional LOI process remains the one to plan around until it's clearly gone.

    - heading: "What actually changes for the Alabai, and what doesn't"
      body: |
        Turkmenistan's 2022 law, requiring every Alabai puppy to be entered in a government pedigree book with its own passport, is an export control. It stops the breed leaving the country as a form of cultural protection. Nothing in that law, or in Turkmenistan's general pet import guidance, restricts an Alabai coming in.

        That means a US owner bringing a Central Asian Shepherd Dog, or a dog with meaningful Alabai ancestry, to Turkmenistan follows the same personal-pet rules as any other breed: microchip, vaccination, antiparasitic treatment, health certificate. The catch arrives only if that same dog, or its Turkmenistan-born puppies, ever needs to leave the country again. At that point, the export ban applies in full, with no workaround.

    - heading: "No titre test in, a full one on the way back"
      body: |
        Turkmenistan's import rules don't ask for a rabies antibody titre test from any country of origin, the US included. A vaccination dated between 30 days and 12 months before arrival, on a microchipped animal, satisfies the requirement, with no blood test and no waiting period beyond that window.

        Flip the direction, though, and the picture changes completely. CDC treats Turkmenistan as high-risk for dog rabies, which means a Turkmenistan-origin dog returning to the US faces the full foreign-vaccinated pathway: titre test or a 28-day CDC-registered facility stay, arrival restricted to designated ports. Anyone planning a return move, not just an outbound one, should budget for that gap now rather than discovering it later.

    - heading: "Getting to Ashgabat"
      body: |
        Turkish Airlines is the standout option here, flying direct from several major US gateways into Istanbul, then operating its own nonstop Istanbul-Ashgabat sector, the same routing already used on this site's Turkmenistan-to-US and Turkmenistan-to-UK pages in reverse. That single-carrier consistency is genuinely useful given how thin Ashgabat's overall scheduled capacity is.

        Emirates and flydubai offer a Dubai-routed alternative, worth comparing on schedule fit, though Gulf summer heat embargoes can disrupt that transfer during the hottest months. Either way, book earlier than usual: 6 to 8 weeks ahead is a sensible floor given how little slack exists in Ashgabat's flight schedule.
