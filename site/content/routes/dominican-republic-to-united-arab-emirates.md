content:
  h1: "Pet Transport from the Dominican Republic to the UAE"
  overview: |
    Blood test, yes. Ninety-day wait, no. That's the distinction that matters most for a pet leaving Santo Domingo for Dubai. MOCCAE puts the Dominican Republic outside its low-risk list, so a rabies antibody titre test is required, but the UAE checks the result itself rather than counting days from the draw the way the EU does. Once the test clears 0.5 IU/ml, it's valid for a year.

    DIGEGA, the Dominican Republic's Direccion General de Ganaderia, handles the export side: no formal export permit, but a health certificate on the MOCCAE model format, endorsed close to the actual travel date. There's no direct flight to the Gulf from Santo Domingo, so the route runs through Madrid, first with Iberia, then Emirates SkyCargo for the final leg into Dubai.

  sections:
    - heading: "The titre test, and why the EU's 90-day rule doesn't apply here"
      body: |
        A rabies antibody titre test is required for entry from the Dominican Republic, drawn at an accredited laboratory, with a minimum result of 0.5 IU/ml. Where this differs from routes into EU countries: MOCCAE does not impose a fixed waiting period counted from the day of the blood draw. The EU does, at 90 days. MOCCAE instead treats the test result as valid for 365 days, provided the rabies vaccination underneath it stays current.

        That difference can shorten the practical preparation time by months compared with an EU-bound route using the same test. Minimum age for entry from the Dominican Republic is 15 weeks, and the MOCCAE import permit, filed online before departure, runs for 90 days once granted.

    - heading: "Getting from Santo Domingo to Dubai via Madrid"
      body: |
        Iberia flies SDQ to Madrid, a route with steady demand and generally reliable cargo capacity for pets. From Madrid, Emirates SkyCargo picks up the onward leg into Dubai, drawing on its established live animal process at the Dubai end.

        Two carriers means two separate booking conversations and two sets of documentation checks, once in Madrid and again on arrival in the UAE. Confirm the crate meets both airlines' specifications before departure; a crate accepted by Iberia isn't automatically cleared for the Emirates SkyCargo leg without checking first.
