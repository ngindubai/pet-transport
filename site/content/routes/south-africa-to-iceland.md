content:
  h1: "Pet Transport from South Africa to Iceland"
  overview: |
    South Africa doesn't appear on either of MAST's published lists, not the rabies-free Category 1 tier, and not the rabies-controlled Category 2 tier either. That's a more serious gap than it might first look. Unlike Hong Kong, which is likely just missing from a list that hasn't caught up, South Africa carries a genuine, documented wildlife rabies reservoir, the same risk basis behind the US CDC's own high-risk classification for dogs resident there.

    The way through is the same narrow exception either way: the pet must have lived with its owner in South Africa for at least 6 months, and the owner must be genuinely relocating to Iceland. Clear that and the process runs to Category 2 rules, complete with a 90-day post-titre wait and Iceland's mandatory 14-day quarantine.

  sections:
    - heading: "Why South Africa isn't on either MAST list"
      body: |
        MAST's Category 1 and Category 2 lists between them cover roughly 40 countries. South Africa is missing from both, and unlike some smaller, lower-traffic origins that simply haven't been assessed yet, South Africa's absence lines up with a real epidemiological picture: a documented rabies reservoir in wildlife, the reason the US CDC itself flags dogs resident there as high-risk.

        None of that changes the practical question for an owner: without the 6-month residency and relocation exception, MAST has no standard door open for this move. Confirm eligibility before anything else gets booked.
    - heading: "Category 2 timeline if the exception applies"
      body: |
        Once accepted, the schedule mirrors the US-to-Iceland route already published on this site: microchip, rabies and leptospirosis vaccination, a titre test at least 30 days later, and then a further 90-day wait from that blood draw. None of it is shortened by having cleared the eligibility gate first.

        The 14-day isolation stay at Reykjanesbae or Mosel sits on top of all of this regardless. Book the slot as soon as the travel window is realistic, since both stations run on limited schedules.
    - heading: "Johannesburg to Keflavik in two legs"
      body: |
        South African Airways and British Airways both fly Johannesburg to European hubs with dependable cargo capacity, and Icelandair covers the final leg on to Keflavik. Two separate bookings, so allow a genuine buffer between the connecting flights rather than a tight same-day transfer.
