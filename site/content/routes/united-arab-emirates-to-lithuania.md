  h1: "Pet Transport United Arab Emirates to Lithuania"
  overview: |
    Vilnius sits at the end of two workable connections out of the Gulf: through Istanbul on Turkish Airlines Cargo, or through Riga on airBaltic. Neither is a single leg, so building the connection time into the schedule matters as much as the regulatory side of this route.

    That regulatory side is the real driver of the timeline. Lithuania treats the UAE as an Annex II country, the EU's higher-risk classification, which means a rabies titre test and a fixed 90-day wait from the blood draw both apply in full.

  sections:
    - heading: "Lithuania's titre test requirement, explained"
      body: |
        The State Food and Veterinary Service (VMVT) requires a microchip before vaccination, a current rabies vaccination, and a FAVN or equivalent titre test drawn at least 30 days after that vaccination at an EU-approved laboratory. The result needs to clear 0.5 IU/ml, and then 90 days must pass from the blood draw date itself before the pet can enter Lithuania. This applies to UAE-origin pets because the UAE sits in Annex II, not the low-risk category that exempts EU and UK pets from testing altogether.

    - heading: "The UAE export side runs the same as always"
      body: |
        A MOCCAE export certificate is required for every pet leaving the UAE, whatever the destination, via the standard online application and in-person clinic exam. For Lithuania specifically, an EU-format health certificate is also needed, valid for just 10 days, so get it issued close to your confirmed travel date.

    - heading: "Two connections, one destination"
      body: |
        Turkish Airlines Cargo via Istanbul is the more consistently available option for larger dogs, with established live animal handling at IST. airBaltic's route through Riga is the shorter regional hop into Vilnius if your Gulf departure aligns with that network. Either way, book the connecting leg early.
