  h1: "Pet Transport from Australia to Seychelles: 2026 Guide"
  overview: |
    Two Seychelles government documents, both current, don't agree with each other on this exact question: does Australia's genuine rabies-free status earn a lighter import pathway? The 2024 procedural rules describe only the full titre-test route. The companion application form still lists a shorter 4-week timeline for rabies-free-country pets. Nobody outside the National Biosecurity Agency can resolve that from the published text alone.

    That honest uncertainty is worth building into the plan from day one: confirm directly with the NBA before booking anything, since the answer changes this route from an 8-week move to a 7-month one.

  sections:
    - heading: "An open question worth asking before booking anything"
      body: |
        Most of the routes in this series have a settled answer for what a given origin country's status means. This one genuinely doesn't, at least not from the public documentation. Seychelles' current import rules, updated in 2024, only spell out the pathway for what they call rabies-affected origins: a titre test, a fixed three-month wait from the blood draw, and roughly four weeks in quarantine. The rabies-free pathway that used to exist in older Seychelles paperwork isn't described in the current procedural document, even though the application form itself still references a lighter four-week timeline for that category.

        Given Australia's rabies-free status is about as well-documented internationally as any country's, this is exactly the kind of gap worth resolving with a direct question to the NBA rather than assuming either the favourable or the demanding answer.

    - heading: "Australia's own export process either way"
      body: |
        Whatever the NBA confirms, the export side stays the same: an ordinary state-registered vet prepares the animal, DAFF reviews the paperwork against Seychelles' requirements, and issues an export permit valid for only 72 hours after issue, with a final vet check required within 72 hours of departure. Build that tight window into the plan regardless of which import pathway ends up applying on the Seychelles side.
