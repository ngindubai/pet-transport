content:
  h1: "Pet Transport from the USA to Brunei: 2026 Guide"
  overview: |
    The USA does not appear on Brunei's short list of scheduled, rabies-controlled countries. That single classification decides most of what follows: a mandatory titre test, a government-approved quarantine facility on arrival, and no shortcut around either one.

    Add the fact that Royal Brunei Airlines does not carry pet cargo for private owners, and this route means a transfer through Singapore or Kuala Lumpur before the pet ever reaches Bandar Seri Begawan. Between the quarantine and the transfer, this is one of the more demanding Southeast Asian corridors on this site.

  sections:
    - heading: "Why the USA gets the full non-scheduled process"
      body: |
        Brunei's Department of Agriculture and Agrifood divides origin countries into scheduled and non-scheduled categories. Scheduled countries, a short list including the UK, Australia, Ireland, New Zealand and Singapore, can see the titre test and quarantine waived for pets with sufficient residency history. The USA is not on that list.

        That means every US-origin pet needs the FAVN titre test, drawn at least 30 days after vaccination at an approved lab, with a result of 0.5 IU/ml or higher. It also means facility quarantine on arrival is close to unavoidable, even with a spotless paper trail.

        A passing titre result stays valid for a year, so owners who move a pet to Brunei more than once within that window may not need to repeat the blood draw. Confirm with DAA before assuming this applies to your situation.
    - heading: "What quarantine on arrival actually looks like"
      body: |
        Pets arriving from a non-scheduled country with both a rabies certificate and a passing titre test typically spend around 14 days at a government-approved facility before the remainder of the observation period continues at home under DAA supervision. Skip the titre test and the facility stay runs longer.

        Get any part of the documentation wrong, and DAA has the discretion to extend the quarantine well beyond the standard period, or in the most serious cases order the pet returned to its country of origin or euthanised. This is not a routine administrative risk elsewhere on this site's Southeast Asian routes, and it is worth building in extra review time with your vet and agent before submitting anything to DAA.

        Facility fees are owner-paid and should be confirmed directly with DAA or your agent before travel, since they vary with the pet's size and the length of stay.
    - heading: "Getting off the ground: the Singapore or Kuala Lumpur transfer"
      body: |
        There is no long-haul pet cargo product out of Bandar Seri Begawan for private owners. Cathay Pacific via Hong Kong and Qatar Airways via Doha are the two most reliable options for the US-to-Southeast-Asia leg, both landing in Singapore or Kuala Lumpur rather than Brunei itself.

        From there, Singapore Airlines Cargo or a coordinated road transfer completes the journey into Brunei. Build this handoff into your schedule early. It is an extra coordination point that most US-to-Southeast-Asia routes on this site do not require, and it is where delays most often creep in.
