content:
  h1: "Pet Transport from Latvia to Greece: 2026 Guide"
  overview: |
    A direct flight between Riga and Athens exists for part of the year, and that single fact changes the shape of this route. When airBaltic's seasonal service is running, a pet can travel from Latvia to Greece in one hop. Outside that window, the trip needs a connection, but the paperwork stays exactly the same either way: this is intra-EU travel, so there is no titre test, no quarantine, and no import permit to arrange.

    The only document that matters is the EU pet passport, or a single-use AHC for owners who do not qualify for one. Both are issued through Latvia's Food and Veterinary Service, the PVD, and both are checked against the pet's microchip on arrival in Greece.

  sections:
    - heading: "The direct route: airBaltic's seasonal Riga-Athens service"
      body: |
        airBaltic operates its Riga-Athens link seasonally, typically through the warmer months when demand justifies the schedule. When it is running, this is the simplest way to move a pet on this route: one airline, one booking, cabin space reserved for pets under its weight limit and cargo handling for larger animals.

        Outside the season, Aegean Airlines via a Western European hub, commonly Frankfurt or Vienna, is the practical alternative. Neither the direct nor the connecting option changes any of the entry requirements below. Book cabin or cargo space as early as you can once your travel dates are fixed, particularly if you are flying in peak summer.

    - heading: "Passport, AHC, and the PVD's role"
      body: |
        The EU pet passport issued by a PVD-authorised vet in Latvia lasts for the pet's life, provided the rabies vaccination recorded inside it stays current. Since 22 April 2026, the passport has been restricted to owners primarily resident in the EU, a change that leaves the great majority of Latvian pet owners untouched.

        Non-EU nationals resident in Latvia need a single-use AHC instead, issued within 10 days of travel and endorsed by the PVD. Whichever document you carry, Greek border vets check it against the microchip on arrival, so confirm the chip number is recorded correctly before you fly.
