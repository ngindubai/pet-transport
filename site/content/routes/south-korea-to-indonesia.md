  overview: |
    Incheon to Jakarta is a well-served direct route, up to three flights a day between Korean Air, Garuda Indonesia and other carriers, roughly seven hours in the air. Indonesia's own entry standard doesn't shorten for it: the FAVN titre test, the two-stage rekomendasi permit, and mandatory quarantine on arrival all apply exactly as they do from any other origin.

    Frequent, direct service is a genuine convenience here, but it shouldn't be mistaken for a fast overall process.

        Korean Air and Garuda Indonesia both fly ICN-Jakarta non-stop, giving real flexibility on departure timing once the pet's paperwork is ready to travel. That's a genuine advantage over routes elsewhere on this site that need a connection.

    - heading: "Barantin's process, unaffected by flight frequency"
      body: |
        The rekomendasi permit's two-stage approval needs to be secured before vaccination or titre testing starts. The FAVN titre test requires a 30-day wait after vaccination, and quarantine on arrival typically runs 7 to 14 days. Budget 16-20 weeks overall, regardless of how easy the flight itself is to book.
