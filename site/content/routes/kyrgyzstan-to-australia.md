  h1: "Pet Transport from Kyrgyzstan to Australia"
  overview: |
    Singapore, not the UK, is the quicker fix for a Kyrgyzstan-origin pet's Australia problem. Kyrgyzstan sits outside DAFF's approved country groups entirely, the same non-approved status this site's Kyrgyzstan-to-UK route already flags for a different destination, so 180 days of residency somewhere approved is compulsory regardless of the destination.

    Where the pet spends those 180 days matters more than owners often assume. Stage in a Group 3 country, the UK included, and a rabies vaccination plus an RNATT titre test are both required before DAFF will move forward. Stage in Singapore, a Group 2, rabies-free-approved territory, and neither applies at all.

    Turkish Airlines' new Istanbul-Singapore-Melbourne service, launched January 2026, means the entire journey, Bishkek to the staging country to Australia, can run through airlines already covering this corridor rather than a route built from scratch.
  sections:
    - heading: "Why Singapore beats the UK for this specific origin"
      body: |
        DAFF sorts approved countries into three groups. Group 1 and Group 2 are rabies-free; Group 3 has rabies present but well controlled, which is where the UK sits. A Kyrgyzstan-origin pet staged in the UK, the route already documented on this site's Kyrgyzstan-to-UK page, would need a rabies vaccination and an RNATT titre test during that residency, on top of the 180 days itself.

        Stage the same 180 days in Singapore instead, and neither the vaccination nor the titre test applies. Group 2 status treats the resident pet as coming from rabies-free territory, full stop. The residency requirement doesn't go away, but two of the more demanding regulatory steps do.
    - heading: "What residency in a Group 2 country actually skips"
      body: |
        It's worth being precise about what changes and what doesn't. The 180 consecutive days still have to happen, continuously, in an approved country. The microchip still has to be fitted and readable. The DAFF import permit through BICON still has to be applied for and processed, typically 20 to 30 days.

        What disappears is the rabies vaccination requirement and the RNATT blood test, both mandatory under the Group 3 pathway. For an owner who would otherwise be managing a vet appointment, a 30-day wait, and a laboratory result on top of the residency clock, that's a genuine reduction in moving parts, not just a different flight.
    - heading: "Closing the loop: Mickleham and the final flight"
      body: |
        Once the 180 days clear, Singapore Airlines flies Changi to Melbourne and Sydney several times daily, a straightforward mainline booking rather than routing back through Istanbul. Mickleham Post Entry Quarantine Facility in Melbourne still takes every arriving pet for a minimum 10 days, weekday arrivals only, regardless of which approved country the pet is coming from.

        Time the final Singapore-Australia flight around that weekday rule from the start. A Friday arrival means a weekend stuck at the airport rather than moving straight into quarantine.

    answer: "Singapore is DAFF Group 2, an approved rabies-free territory, so a resident pet needs neither a rabies vaccination nor a titre test to satisfy DAFF. The UK is Group 3, which requires both. The 180-day residency itself applies either way."
  - question: "Does the pet still need a microchip if no titre test is required?"
    answer: "Yes. The ISO 11784/11785 microchip requirement applies regardless of which approved group the staging country falls into, and it's checked again on arrival in Australia."
