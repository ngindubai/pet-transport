content:
  h1: "Pet Transport from Guatemala to the United Kingdom"
  overview: |
    A rabies titre test and a fixed three-month wait apply to every dog or cat leaving Guatemala for the UK, because Guatemala sits outside Great Britain's list of low-rabies-risk countries. The blood sample only has to be taken 30 days after the rabies vaccination, but the clock on the three-month wait does not start until a passing result, 0.5 IU/ml or higher, comes back from an APHA-approved lab.

    Guatemala's own side of the paperwork is comparatively light. MAGA's export certificate for international travel costs USD 6.25 and is processed through the VUPE window in Guatemala City. The harder part is the flight: there is no direct service from La Aurora International Airport (GUA) to London, and Avianca, the main carrier out of Guatemala City, has stated it will not carry pets into the UK outside declared cargo.

  sections:
    - heading: "Why the flight is the hard part, not the paperwork"
      body: |
        Most owners assume the regulatory side, titre test, vaccination timing, certificates, is the bottleneck on an unlisted-country route. For Guatemala to the UK it usually isn't. MAGA's process is quick and cheap once the underlying UK requirements are met.

        The routing is a different matter. Avianca, which operates the bulk of scheduled traffic out of Guatemala City, publishes a policy stating it does not accept pets as accompanied or PetSafe-equivalent baggage on UK-bound itineraries, citing Defra and Animal Reception Centre restrictions. That rules out the simplest booking path many owners expect. The realistic options are Avianca Cargo as declared freight, or a connection through a US gateway (Miami on American, Houston on United) with an interline handover to a carrier that will actually fly the animal into Heathrow or Gatwick. Either way, this is not a route to book without a specialist agent confirming acceptance at every leg first.

    - heading: "The three-month wait: plan around the blood draw date, not the flight date"
      body: |
        Owners sometimes book flights first and then discover the titre test timeline does not fit. It works the other way round on this route.

        Once the rabies vaccination is at least 30 days old, the blood sample can be drawn. Results typically take one to three weeks to come back from an APHA-approved lab. The three-calendar-month wait only starts from the date of a successful draw, not the date the vaccination was given and not the date the result letter arrives. Add the certificate window (issued within 10 days of travel) and the tapeworm treatment window (24 to 120 hours before arrival, dogs only) on top, and a realistic minimum from a standing start is four and a half to five and a half months.
