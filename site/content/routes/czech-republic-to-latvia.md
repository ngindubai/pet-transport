    - heading: "EU pet passport for a Czech pet entering Latvia"
      body: |
        Latvia's PVD (Pārtikas un veterinārais dienests) accepts the EU pet passport for intra-EU arrivals. For a Czech pet, the passport must be issued by an official vet authorised under the SVA (Státní veterinární správa). The required entries are: ISO 15-digit microchip recorded before the rabies vaccination, and a current rabies vaccination within its valid period.

        No titre test is required, no quarantine applies, and no import permit is needed. The PVD spot check process at Riga Airport is straightforward for a compliant arrival: microchip scan matched to passport, vaccination dates reviewed. Spot checks are occasional rather than routine for EU arrivals.

        The one point to verify before travel is the microchip-vaccination sequence in the passport. If the rabies vaccination entry appears before the microchip number, the passport is technically non-compliant and may not be accepted. Check with your vet before flying.
