  sections:
    - heading: "Be honest about the routing before you book anything"
      body: |
        India to Brazil is a genuine long-haul, multi-leg journey for a pet in cargo. There is no single carrier flying nonstop between the two countries, so the choice is really about which connecting hub gives your pet the shortest ground time between flights.

        Dubai and Doha have the widest Indian city coverage through Emirates and Qatar Airways, but summer heat embargoes at both hubs restrict brachycephalic breeds and can delay bookings in June to September. Frankfurt via Lufthansa runs cooler year-round and suits larger dogs, at the cost of a longer first leg from India. Istanbul via Turkish Airlines is a middle option worth comparing on price and transit time for your specific city pair.

        Whichever routing you choose, confirm every single leg, not just the first one, accepts live animals as cargo. A booking that is fine from Mumbai to Dubai can still fail if the Dubai-to-Sao Paulo leg is full or embargoed on your date.
    - heading: "AQCS: India's export process, and why the 7-day window matters"
      body: |
        The Animal Quarantine and Certification Service, working under the Department of Animal Husbandry and Dairying, is the authority that clears every pet leaving India. You submit self-attested copies of the vaccination record and health certificate, along with the original and the pet itself, at the AQCS quarantine office at least 7 days before your planned embarkation, by prior appointment.

        AQCS examination and NOC issuance typically takes around 5 working days from that submission. The resulting export certificate and NOC are valid for 10 days from the date of examination, which then has to overlap with Brazil's own 10-day CVI validity window counted from the same document's endorsement. On a route with a connecting flight and possible layover, get these dates worked out on paper before you book anything, ideally with an agent who has handled the AQCS process before.

        A separate application form is needed for each individual pet, so a two-dog or two-cat household should expect the AQCS paperwork to roughly double, not simply repeat.

faqs:
  - question: "Is there a direct flight from India to Brazil for pets?"
    answer: "No. There is no direct passenger or cargo service between any Indian city and Brazil. Every pet travels via at least one connecting hub, most commonly Dubai, Doha, Istanbul or Frankfurt, which adds 24 hours or more to the total journey including the connection."
  - question: "What is the AQCS NOC and how long does it take?"
    answer: "The No Objection Certificate is issued by India's Animal Quarantine and Certification Service after examining the pet and its documents in person, at least 7 days before embarkation. Processing typically takes around 5 working days. Both the NOC and the export health certificate are valid for 10 days from the examination date."
  - question: "Does Brazil require a titre test for pets from India?"
    answer: "No. Brazil does not ask for a FAVN titre test from India-origin pets. The requirement is a current rabies vaccination, ISO microchip, antiparasitic treatment for dogs, and the AQCS-endorsed CVI, all within the standard 10-day validity window on arrival."
  - question: "Is quarantine required for pets entering Brazil from India?"
    answer: "No mandatory quarantine applies, provided the documentation is complete and correct. MAPA carries out a documentary and health check at the arrival airport, most commonly Sao Paulo Guarulhos, rather than a quarantine hold."
  - question: "Which hub is best for a brachycephalic breed travelling from India to Brazil?"
    answer: "Frankfurt via Lufthansa tends to be the safer choice for flat-faced breeds, since it avoids the Gulf heat embargoes that Dubai and Doha impose on brachycephalic cargo during the warmer months. Confirm current embargo dates directly with the airline before booking, since these change seasonally."
  - question: "How long should I budget for the whole process, from first vet visit to travel day?"
    answer: "Around 8 to 12 weeks for a straightforward case. This covers the microchip and vaccination sequence, the 7-day AQCS submission window plus roughly 5 working days of processing, and time to book cargo space across a multi-leg route. Add extra time if your pet needs its first rabies vaccination, which triggers a 30-day wait on the Brazil side."
