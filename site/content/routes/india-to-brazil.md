content:
  h1: "Pet Transport India to Brazil: AQCS Certificate and the VIGIAGRO Arrival Process"
  overview: |
    Moving a pet from India to Brazil requires an AQCS/DAHD-endorsed health certificate and export clearance from India, followed by a VIGIAGRO inspection on arrival in Brazil. There is no routine quarantine in Brazil for compliant pets. The main challenge on this corridor is sourcing reliable cargo capacity: few airlines accept live animals on the India-to-South-America routing. Emirates via Dubai is the most commonly used option. Allow extra lead time for the AQCS endorsement process in India.

      body: |
        | Requirement | India to Brazil | India to Denmark | India to Indonesia |
        |---|---|---|---|
        | Import permit | No (cert + inspection) | No (cert-based EU entry) | Yes (BARANTAN) |
        | Quarantine on arrival | No routine | No routine (EU) | 14 days minimum |

        Brazil is a comparatively accessible destination from India, with no import permit, no quarantine, and no titre test requirement. Denmark requires a FAVN titre test plus 90-day wait because India is not on the EU's approved third-country list. Indonesia adds a mandatory quarantine.

    - heading: "Airline comparison: India to Brazil"
      body: |
        | Airline | Hub | Route | Notes |
        |---|---|---|---|
        | Emirates | DXB | DEL/BOM-DXB-GRU | SkyCargo. Most reliable India-South America live animal routing |
        | Lufthansa | FRA | DEL/BOM-FRA-GRU | Lufthansa Cargo accepts live animals |
        | Air France | CDG | DEL/BOM-CDG-GRU | AFKLMP Cargo handles live animals |

        Airlines willing to carry live animals from India on cargo routes are fewer than on other corridors. Emirates via Dubai is generally the most reliable option for India-to-Brazil pet cargo. Book cargo space early and confirm live animal acceptance explicitly.

    - heading: "AQCS export clearance and VIGIAGRO arrival process"
      body: |
        In India, AQCS (Animal Quarantine and Certification Service) under DAHD is responsible for both import and export health certification. Before a pet can be exported, an official vet issues a health certificate that is then endorsed by AQCS. On the day of departure, AQCS inspectors at the departure airport carry out a final check and provide export clearance.

        On arrival in Brazil, VIGIAGRO (Brazil's agricultural border inspection service under MAPA) inspects the documentation, microchip, and health certificate. For compliant pets with complete documentation, this is a documentary check rather than a quarantine hold.

        Contact MAPA/VIGIAGRO (gov.br/mapa) for the current health certificate format required for India-origin pets entering Brazil.
