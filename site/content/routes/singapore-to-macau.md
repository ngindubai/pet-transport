content:
  h1: "Pet Transport from Singapore to Macau"
  overview: |
    It's tempting to assume Singapore's rabies-free status, one of the reasons the country sits on the easy end of most other destinations' entry rules, would carry over here too. It doesn't. Macau's IAM works from its own short list of exempt origins, and nothing found during research for this guide confirms Singapore is on it, any more than Australia is on the equivalent list for its own Macau route documented elsewhere on this site.

    So the standard pathway applies: a titre test, a wait of roughly three months from the blood draw, and a mandatory transfer through Hong Kong, since Macau International Airport carries no long-haul cargo of its own.

  sections:
    - heading: "Rabies-free doesn't mean exempt"
      body: |
        Being internationally recognised as rabies-free is not, on its own, the same thing as appearing on Macau's specific exemption list. Australia is a useful comparison: unambiguously rabies-free, and still not exempted from IAM's titre requirement on the route this site has already documented in the other direction. Nothing turned up in research for this page naming Singapore on whatever short list IAM actually uses, so the safer planning assumption is that the same logic applies.

        That means the familiar sequence: microchip before vaccination, blood drawn at least 30 days after that vaccination, a result of at least 0.5 IU/mL, then roughly three months counted from the draw date, not the result date, before the pet can enter Macau.

    - heading: "A nonstop passenger route that skips pets entirely"
      body: |
        Scoot, the Singapore Airlines Group's budget carrier, flies Singapore to Macau nonstop. For a passenger, that's the fastest way between the two cities. For a pet, it's irrelevant: Scoot doesn't carry live animals in the cabin or as cargo on any route it operates.

        The workable path runs through Hong Kong instead. Singapore Airlines Cargo or Cathay Pacific handle the SIN-HKG sector, both well established on this corridor, then the pet crosses into Macau by road over the Hong Kong-Zhuhai-Macau Bridge, or by ferry, about an hour once customs clearance in Hong Kong is done.

    - heading: "What changed for Macau's quarantine rule in June 2026"
      body: |
        A pet that clears every pre-entry requirement no longer sits in mandatory quarantine on arrival in Macau. IAM dropped that requirement from 12 June 2026 for compliant dogs and cats, a genuine easing that applies regardless of where the pet is travelling from.

        It's conditional, not automatic. Microchip, current vaccination, a passing titre result and the correctly formatted health certificate all have to be in order and confirmed at the IAM inspection, or a vet can still hold the pet at arrival, quarantine exemption or not.
