  sections:
    - heading: "Flying: LOT direct in under 1 hour"
      body: |
        LOT Polish Airlines operates direct flights between Vilnius Airport (VNO) and Warsaw Chopin Airport (WAW). The flight takes under an hour, making it one of the shortest international pet journeys possible. Small pets (up to 8kg including the carrier) travel in the cabin beside you. Larger dogs go in the pressurised hold as checked baggage.

        Book your pet's cabin space when you purchase your own ticket. LOT limits the number of animals per flight. For hold travel, contact LOT directly to confirm the checked-baggage pet booking process for your specific flight.

        Ryanair and Wizz Air also operate from Lithuanian airports but do not accept pets under any circumstances. Stick to LOT, airBaltic, or Lufthansa.

    - heading: "Driving: Vilnius to Warsaw in around 5 hours"
      body: |
        The road distance between Vilnius and Warsaw is approximately 470km. The drive takes around 5 hours on the Via Baltica (E67) through Kaunas and Suwalki. Both Lithuania and Poland are Schengen Area members, so there are no passport controls at the border, though pet document checks may still occur.

        For a dog that is anxious about flying, or for any pet where the cabin weight limit is a barrier, driving is a genuinely good option on this route. Your pet needs the same documentation as for a flight: microchip, valid rabies vaccination, and EU pet passport or AHC. The AHC must be endorsed within 10 days of crossing the Polish border.

        Make sure your car is properly equipped: a secure crate or harness, water, and planned rest stops for longer drives.

    - heading: "Polish entry requirements"
      body: |
        Poland's Chief Veterinary Inspectorate (GIW) follows EU rules for incoming pets. Microchip (ISO 11784/11785), valid rabies vaccination, and EU pet passport or AHC are required. The 21-day post-vaccination wait applies to first-time vaccinations only.

        There is no national breed ban in Poland, though certain breeds must be muzzled and kept on leads in public under Polish law. This applies after arrival, not at the border.

        For this particular route, the short distance and excellent transport links mean even last-minute moves are manageable, as long as the 21-day vaccination wait has been observed.
