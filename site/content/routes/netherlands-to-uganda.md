  sections:
    - heading: "Three routes from Amsterdam to Entebbe"
      body: |
        Amsterdam Schiphol (AMS) to Entebbe International Airport (EBB) can be done via three established cargo paths, and each has different strengths.

        KLM via Nairobi (NBO) is the most natural option for Netherlands-based pet owners: KLM Cargo from Schiphol to Kenya, then NBO-EBB as a short East African hop. Nairobi is East Africa's dominant cargo hub, and the NBO-EBB connection is short and well-serviced.

        Brussels Airlines via Brussels (BRU) is the dedicated Belgium-to-East Africa route. From Amsterdam, you reach Brussels easily by road, rail, or KLM feeder flight, and Brussels Airlines Cargo has a consistent record on the BRU-EBB corridor. If you are in western Netherlands, Brussels is not far.

        Ethiopian Airlines via Addis Ababa (ADD) brings you into ADD, East Africa's largest aviation hub, then onto EBB. Ethiopian Cargo handles a high volume of live animal freight across East Africa.

        Compare live animal acceptance, crate size limits, and total transit times across all three before booking. A pet transport agent familiar with East Africa routes can help identify which routing is most reliably available for your dates and pet size.

    - heading: "MAAIF import permit: applying from the Netherlands"
      body: |
        Uganda's Ministry of Agriculture, Animal Industry and Fisheries (MAAIF) at maaif.go.ug is the competent authority for companion animal imports. Apply for the import permit at least 8-10 weeks before travel, providing your pet's ISO microchip number, vaccination records, breed, and travel date.

        Uganda's administrative processes can be slower than some destinations. Apply as early as possible and follow up after submitting to confirm your application has been received and is being processed. Do not book cargo until the permit is confirmed.

        At Entebbe International Airport, the MAAIF inspection team checks the permit alongside the NVWA health certificate. A complete, consistent document set proceeds without quarantine.

    - heading: "NVWA export health certificate from the Netherlands"
      body: |
        For Uganda, a non-EU destination, the Dutch EU pet passport does not apply. The required document is an official health certificate from an NVWA-authorised official veterinarian (Officieel Dierenarts), endorsed by the NVWA.

        Book a pre-export examination with an NVWA-authorised vet in the Netherlands. They check the ISO microchip, vaccination status, and general health, then issue the export certificate. NVWA endorsement follows. The endorsed certificate must be issued within 10 days of travel.

        NVWA-authorised veterinary practices are available across the Netherlands. Your regular vet may hold this authorisation or can refer you to a nearby practice that does. Amsterdam Schiphol is the natural departure point, with live animal cargo handling facilities available for East Africa-bound shipments.
