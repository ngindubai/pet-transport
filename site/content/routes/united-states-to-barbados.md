content:
  h1: "Pet Transport from the United States to Barbados"
  overview: |
    The US sits on Barbados's Category 1 list, the lower-risk tier that covers roughly 70 countries and skips the FAVN titre test most owners dread. A 35-day wait after vaccination, an import permit, and a USDA-endorsed health certificate carry most of the weight here.

    One detail catches people out. If the pet spent any time in a Category 2 country in the three months before leaving the US, the titre test comes back into play, along with its own 30-day-post-vaccination testing window. Recent movers to the US, particularly with a pet that has lived in more than one country recently, should check this before assuming the lighter pathway applies.

  sections:
    - heading: "The 35-day wait, and the Category 2 exception worth checking"
      body: |
        Barbados's Category 1 exemption from the FAVN titre test is generous by Caribbean standards: no blood sample, no laboratory wait, just 35 full days between a rabies vaccination and travel. That is shorter than the UK route's own comparable wait and far shorter than the 90-day hold a Category 2 pet faces.

        The exception is narrow but real. A pet that lived in a Category 2 country within the three months before departure from the US, or one arriving in Barbados by sea, still needs the FAVN test, drawn no sooner than 30 days after vaccination. Get the pet's recent travel history confirmed early, since discovering this requirement late in the process can add a month or more to the timeline.

    - heading: "USDA's endorsement process, and the paperwork that follows it"
      body: |
        A USDA-accredited vet, not just any licensed vet, has to complete the Official Export Health Certificate before USDA APHIS can endorse it. That endorsement costs USD 38 and takes two to ten working days depending on how it is submitted, so build that processing window into the plan rather than treating it as a same-day step.

        Dogs carry two extra requirements the US export side adds on top of Barbados's own rules: a negative heartworm test inside 30 days of departure, and tick and tapeworm treatment inside 7 days of departure. Neither applies to cats. Miss either and the certificate is incomplete regardless of how current the rabies vaccination is.

    answer: "The USDA endorsement fee is USD 38 per certificate, on top of the accredited vet's own exam fee. The certificate then has to be used within 10 days of the pet's arrival in Barbados."
  - question: "Do dogs need a heartworm test to leave the US for Barbados?"
