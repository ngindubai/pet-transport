content:
  h1: "Pet Transport Dominican Republic to Norway: 2026 Guide"
  overview: "Dominican Republic to Norway involves two preparation tracks. The first is the EU/EEA unlisted country process: FAVN titre test, 30-day post-vaccination wait before the blood draw, result of 0.5 IU/ml or above at an EU-approved laboratory, and a mandatory 90-day wait from the blood draw date. That alone takes 20-26 weeks. The second applies to dogs only: Echinococcus treatment with praziquantel or epsiprantel must be given between 24 and 120 hours before arriving in Norway. Missing that window causes problems at Oslo Gardermoen. Norway is EEA but not EU and applies equivalent pet travel rules."

  sections:
    - heading: "Norway's requirements for pets from the Dominican Republic"
      body: |
        Norway's Norwegian Food Safety Authority (Mattilsynet) applies the same unlisted-country rules as EU member states. For a pet from the Dominican Republic, the standard process applies: microchip before vaccination; rabies vaccination with 21 days elapsed since the primary dose; FAVN titre test at an EU-approved laboratory at least 30 days after vaccination; result of 0.5 IU/ml or above; 90-day wait from the blood draw date.

        At Oslo Gardermoen (OSL), Mattilsynet inspectors check the health certificate, the titre test result (with blood draw date clearly stated), the vaccination record, and the microchip. The health certificate must be the EU Annex IV non-commercial format, endorsed by the Dominican Republic's DIGEGA authority within 10 days of travel.

        For cats, that is the complete process. For dogs, there is a further requirement.

    - heading: "The tapeworm treatment requirement for dogs entering Norway"
      body: |
        All dogs entering Norway must be treated for Echinococcus (fox dwarf tapeworm) before arrival. The treatment must contain praziquantel or epsiprantel and must be administered between 24 and 120 hours before the dog arrives in Norway. Mattilsynet is precise about this window: treatment given 121 hours before arrival is non-compliant.

        A veterinarian must document the treatment, recording the product name, the exact date and time of administration, and the dosage. This record must accompany the health certificate. Mattilsynet inspectors check it at OSL.

        The timing calculation is based on your arrival time in Norway. Given the transit time from SDQ to OSL of approximately 12-20 hours via a European hub, the treatment can typically be administered in Santo Domingo before departure or at a European hub layover, provided the total time from treatment to OSL arrival is within the 24-120 hour window. Calculate the exact hours based on your specific itinerary and discuss with your freight forwarder.

    - heading: "Routing from Santo Domingo to Oslo"
      body: |
        Las Americas International Airport (SDQ) in Santo Domingo has cargo connections to European hubs via Iberia and Air France. The most common routing is SDQ to Madrid (MAD) via Iberia, then MAD to Oslo Gardermoen (OSL) via SAS Scandinavian Airlines cargo. Iberia has strong Caribbean coverage and handles live animal cargo on the transatlantic leg.

        From Paris CDG, Air France or Norwegian Air Shuttle connect to Oslo. Given that the SDQ-to-OSL transit time is relatively short by Caribbean standards, the Echinococcus treatment window can usually be managed with careful calculation. Book cargo space early for the OSL connection, where live animal capacity can be limited.

    - heading: "DIGEGA export documentation from the Dominican Republic"
      body: |
        The Direccion General de Ganaderia (DIGEGA), within the Ministerio de Agricultura (agricultura.gob.do), endorses companion animal export health certificates. A licensed veterinarian prepares the EU Annex IV format certificate, referencing the titre test result (blood draw date, laboratory name, result in IU/ml) and the vaccination record.

        DIGEGA endorses the document after review. Allow three to five business days for this step. The health certificate must be issued within 10 days of travel. The Echinococcus treatment record for dogs is a separate document and must travel with the health certificate.

faqs:
  - question: "Do cats need tapeworm treatment to enter Norway from the Dominican Republic?"
    answer: "No. The Echinococcus praziquantel treatment requirement in Norway applies to dogs only. Cats from the Dominican Republic need the standard EU/EEA unlisted country process: microchip, rabies vaccination, FAVN titre test with 90-day wait, and a DIGEGA-endorsed health certificate."
  - question: "Can I administer the tapeworm treatment at a European hub rather than in Santo Domingo?"
    answer: "Yes, provided the timing works out. If your dog arrives at OSL at 10:00 on a Friday, the treatment window opens at 10:00 on Wednesday (24 hours before) and closes at 10:00 on Monday the previous week (120 hours before). If your SDQ-to-OSL transit involves a hub layover of several hours, you can arrange treatment there with a local vet or at an airport veterinary facility. Confirm the timing calculation with your freight forwarder before booking."
  - question: "Norway is not in the EU. Does the Dominican Republic's unlisted country status still apply?"
    answer: "Yes. Norway is EEA and follows EU pet travel rules under the EEA Agreement. The FAVN titre test, 90-day wait, and health certificate requirements for pets from unlisted third countries such as the Dominican Republic are the same as for EU member states. Mattilsynet is the competent authority."
  - question: "What if the titre test result is below 0.5 IU/ml from a Dominican Republic laboratory?"
    answer: "A result below 0.5 IU/ml means the test has not passed. Revaccinate the animal, allow 30 days, arrange a new blood draw at an EU-approved laboratory, and the 90-day wait restarts from the new draw date. This can add several months to the timeline. Use a high-quality, EU-approved rabies vaccine and give the vaccine sufficient time to produce a response before drawing blood."
