  sections:
    - heading: "FAVN titre test and the 90-day wait"
      body: |
        Norway applies the same titre test requirements as EU member states through the EEA Agreement. The Dominican Republic is classified as an unlisted third country, so the FAVN titre test is mandatory. Blood must be drawn at least 30 days after the most recent rabies vaccination. The sample must show a neutralising antibody level of at least 0.5 IU/ml at an EU-approved laboratory.

        The 90-day waiting period runs from the blood draw date. Mattilsynet (the Norwegian Food Safety Authority) states clearly that no exemption to this wait period is granted. Once the 90 days have elapsed, the DIGEGA health certificate can be issued and is valid for 10 days from the DIGEGA endorsement date to the documentary checks at Oslo Gardermoen.

        Work with a vet in the Dominican Republic who has experience shipping blood samples internationally. Factor in shipping time to the laboratory and allow time for the DIGEGA endorsement step after the vet appointment.

    - heading: "Tapeworm treatment for dogs and advance notification"
      body: |
        All dogs entering Norway must receive treatment against Echinococcus multilocularis (the fox tapeworm) before arrival. The treatment must use praziquantel at a minimum dose of 2.5 mg/kg body weight and must be administered no less than 24 hours and no more than 120 hours before arrival at Oslo Gardermoen.

        The treating vet must record the product name, dose, exact date, and exact time of treatment in the health certificate. This is not a flexible requirement: if the treatment window falls outside the 24-120 hour range on arrival, Mattilsynet can refuse entry. Plan the treatment timing against your flight schedule carefully, and coordinate with the vet who will complete the health certificate.

        In addition to the tapeworm treatment, owners must notify Mattilsynet at least 48 hours before the pet's arrival at OSL. Use the Mattilsynet online notification portal or contact the Border Control Post at Oslo Gardermoen directly. Late notification can delay clearance on arrival.
