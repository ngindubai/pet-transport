  overview: |
    Norway sits outside the EU but mirrors its pet travel rules closely, so a Dominican Republic-origin pet faces the same unlisted-country sequence it would face entering Denmark or Germany: microchip, vaccination, FAVN titre test, then a 90-day wait from the blood draw date. On top of that, Norway adds a rule that applies to every dog regardless of origin: a praziquantel tapeworm treatment 24 to 120 hours before landing at Oslo Gardermoen.

    That last rule carries no exemptions based on origin country, unlike Ireland's or Finland's versions. Every dog gets treated, every time, close to the travel date.

That means Mattilsynet requires the same FAVN or ELISA titre test, 0.5 IU/ml minimum result, and 90-day post-draw wait that Denmark, Sweden or Germany would require.

        Where Ireland exempts a handful of countries and Finland applies the rule only to non-EU arrivals, Norway requires the praziquantel treatment for every dog entering the country, including dogs arriving from other EU or EEA states.

        Since the treatment window closes just 24 hours before arrival and opens only 120 hours out, schedule the final vet visit, covering both the export certificate and the tapeworm treatment, for the days immediately before your confirmed flight.
