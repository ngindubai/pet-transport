content:
  h1: "Pet Transport from the US to Saudi Arabia: 2026 Process"
  overview: "US-based expats relocating to Saudi Arabia face a two-part documentation challenge: the USDA APHIS endorsement process on the US side, followed by Saudi embassy attestation of that endorsed certificate.   sections:
    - heading: "The dog restriction is the first thing to resolve"
      body: |
        Saudi Arabia's MEWA only permits the import of dogs that fall within specific working classifications: guard dogs, guide dogs, and hunting dogs. Companion dogs, regardless of breed or temperament, are not generally permitted under standard import channels.

        Many US expats heading to Saudi Arabia have dogs that do not fit any of these categories. If that is your situation, you need specialist advice on whether an employer-sponsored arrangement or other special permission exists before you invest time and money in the MEWA permit process. Some employers with significant operations in Saudi Arabia have established relationships with MEWA that may help. But this is not guaranteed.

        Cats have no equivalent classification restriction and can be imported using the standard permit and health certificate process.

    - heading: "USDA endorsement and Saudi embassy attestation"
      body: |
        Getting a health certificate from Saudi Arabia requires two layers of official verification that most other international pet moves do not. First, the health certificate from your USDA-accredited vet must be endorsed by USDA APHIS. This confirms the vet's credentials to Saudi authorities. The USDA APHIS endorsement takes three to ten business days depending on your regional office and whether you use express processing.

        Once the USDA endorsement is in place, the endorsed certificate must be presented to the Saudi Arabian embassy in Washington DC (or a consulate) for attestation. This attestation step adds another one to two weeks and a separate fee. Only after both steps are complete is the health certificate accepted by MEWA for entry.

        Plan for a total of two to four weeks for the full documentation chain after your vet issues the base certificate.

    - heading: "Airlines from the US to Saudi Arabia"
      body: |
        Saudia operates the most direct routes from the US to Saudi Arabia, including services from New York JFK and Washington Dulles to Riyadh. These are cargo-only routes for pets, but Saudia's direct service reduces the number of transit handling points, which is better for the animal.

        United Airlines connects via European hubs. Emirates SkyCargo via Dubai offers flexibility for owners who need to route through a Middle East hub. Whichever airline you choose, confirm the route accepts live animal cargo for the specific date you need, as cargo capacity for live animals is not guaranteed on every flight.

faqs:
  - question: "Does my dog from the US need a titre test to enter Saudi Arabia?"
    answer: "Saudi Arabia does not universally require a titre test for dogs from the United States in the way that UK or Australian entry does. The MEWA requirements focus on current rabies vaccination with minimum 6 months remaining validity, along with the import permit and attested health certificate. That said, some MEWA inspectors have requested titre test results as additional evidence. Having a recent FAVN test result available, even if not formally required, is useful insurance for this route."
  - question: "How does the USDA endorsement process work?"
    answer: "Your USDA-accredited veterinarian completes the health certificate on the APHIS-standard form. You then submit this to your regional USDA APHIS Animal Care office for official endorsement, which adds a federal stamp confirming the vet's accreditation. Standard processing takes 3 to 10 business days. USDA APHIS offers an expedited service for an additional fee, which takes 1 to 3 business days. The endorsement step is mandatory: Saudi Arabia will not accept a US health certificate without it."
  - question: "Where do I go in the US for Saudi embassy attestation?"
    answer: "The Saudi Arabian embassy is located in Washington DC, with consulates in other major cities including Houston, Los Angeles, and New York. You can submit the endorsed health certificate in person or via courier. The embassy generally processes attestations within 5 to 10 business days. Contact the embassy or consulate for current fees and processing times before submitting, as these can change."
  - question: "What breed restrictions does Saudi Arabia have?"
    answer: "Saudi Arabia's MEWA regulations list Pit Bull Terriers as banned. Rottweilers face additional restrictions. The working-dog classification requirement effectively excludes many breeds from standard companion pet importation channels regardless of these specific bans. If your dog is a restricted or banned breed, the import is not possible. If your dog is a non-restricted companion breed, the classification issue remains the primary hurdle to address."
  - question: "Is there quarantine for US pets arriving in Saudi Arabia?"
    answer: "No routine quarantine applies for compliant pets arriving in Saudi Arabia. Your dog or cat will go through a veterinary inspection at the port of entry. If all documentation is in order, including the MEWA import permit and the attested health certificate, your pet should clear inspection and be released the same day. Incomplete or incorrect documentation can result in the animal being held pending resolution."
