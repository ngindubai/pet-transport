  sections:
    - heading: "Georgia's pet import rules in practice"
      body: |
        Georgia's National Food Agency (NFA) at nfa.gov.ge manages companion pet imports. For a pet from Lithuania, the NFA requires a current rabies vaccination and an official health certificate from the Lithuanian VMVT. No titre test. No quarantine. Border veterinary inspection at Tbilisi International Airport (TBS) checks the certificate and vaccination record on arrival.

        The NFA is the kind of authority that expects documentation to be correct. If the certificate has any inconsistency with the pet's vaccination record or identification, the inspection will take longer. Getting the certificate cross-checked before travel by your transport agent or the VMVT is a sound precaution.

        Georgia does restrict certain dog breeds. If your dog is a breed commonly classified as dangerous or fighting-type, check with the NFA before booking transport.

    - heading: "VMVT certificates for non-EU destinations"
      body: |
        Within the EU, the EU pet passport is your standard document. For Georgia, it does not apply. An official export health certificate from a VMVT-authorised official veterinarian, endorsed by the Valstybine maisto ir veterinarijos tarnyba (VMVT) at vmvt.lt, is the required document.

        Contact the VMVT to find an authorised official vet. Not all vets in Lithuania hold this status, so confirm before making an appointment. The certificate must be issued within 10 days of travel. Confirm with the VMVT or your transport agent whether the Georgian NFA requires any specific format for the certificate.

    - heading: "Routing from Vilnius Airport to Tbilisi"
      body: |
        Vilnius Airport (VNO) is a regional airport with fewer direct cargo connections than Warsaw or Riga. For pet cargo to Tbilisi, the most practical route uses LOT Polish Airlines from VNO to Warsaw (WAW), then a connecting cargo flight to Istanbul Ataturk (IST) via Turkish Airlines, and then IST to Tbilisi (TBS). Turkish Airlines Cargo handles live animals at IST regularly.

        Alternatively, connecting through Riga (RIX) via airBaltic gives access to Frankfurt or Amsterdam for onward cargo. Whatever routing you choose, confirm live animal cargo acceptance on every leg in writing before booking.
