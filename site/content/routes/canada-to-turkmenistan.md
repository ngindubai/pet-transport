  h1: "Pet Transport from Canada to Turkmenistan"
  overview: |
    Ask a Turkmenistan-bound pet owner what's holding up their move, and the answer is rarely the paperwork for the animal. It's the human visa. Turkmenistan still requires a Letter of Invitation from a licensed local travel agency for most tourist entries. Almost everyone who actually completes this route is relocating for an oil and gas contract, a diplomatic posting, or a similar employer-sponsored move, not planning a personal trip around a pet's schedule.

    One question worth answering directly, since it comes up often: Turkmenistan's well-publicised law protecting the Alabai, its national dog breed, bars that breed from being exported out of the country. It has no bearing on a Canadian dog, of any breed, entering Turkmenistan. That's an export control running the opposite direction to this route.

    Against that visa backdrop, the pet's own requirements are comparatively simple: a microchip, a rabies vaccination inside the last 12 months (multi-year vaccines aren't accepted here), and a CFIA-endorsed certificate. What eats the calendar, beyond the visa, is Ashgabat's own limited scheduled flight capacity.

  sections:
    - heading: "The real constraint is the visa, not the vet"
      body: |
        Most route pages on this site treat the pet's medical timeline as the longest lead item. Not here. Turkmenistan's visa regime, one of the most restrictive anywhere, narrows the practical audience for this route considerably.

        If you're moving for work, an employer or the relevant embassy typically handles the visa side, and the pet paperwork runs alongside it. If you're planning a personal move without that backing, budget serious extra time for the Letter of Invitation process, and don't assume the pet can travel before your own entry is confirmed.

    - heading: "The Alabai law runs the other way"
      body: |
        Turkmenistan passed a law in 2022 requiring every Alabai puppy to be registered in a government pedigree book, with special government permission needed to export the breed. It's framed around national heritage protection, and Turkmenistan marks an annual Alabai Day in its honour.

        None of that touches a Canadian pet coming into the country. The restriction is an export control on animals leaving Turkmenistan, not an import bar of any kind. A Canadian dog, Alabai or otherwise, enters under the standard microchip, vaccination and certificate requirements above, with no reference to this law at all.

    - heading: "Annual vaccination, not Canada's usual 3-year cycle"
      body: |
        A pet vaccinated on Canada's standard 3-year rabies schedule can still be years away from its next domestically required booster and yet be out of date for Turkmenistan. The rule here is a flat 12-month window: vaccination given between 30 days and 12 months before arrival, with no allowance for a longer-duration vaccine product.

        If your pet's last rabies shot falls outside that 12-month window, get a fresh booster before the export certificate is issued.

    - heading: "Getting a cargo slot into Ashgabat"
      body: |
        There's no direct Canada-Turkmenistan service, for pets or people. Turkish Airlines' direct Toronto-Istanbul flight connects to the same nonstop Ashgabat leg that Turkmenistan Airlines also flies. Emirates covers the Toronto-Dubai leg direct, feeding into flydubai's Dubai-Ashgabat service.

        Scheduled capacity into Ashgabat is genuinely limited on both routings. Book 6-8 weeks ahead rather than the 3-4 weeks that might suffice elsewhere, and keep a second routing option in mind in case the first doesn't have space on your dates.
