content:
  h1: "Pet Transport Panama to Hungary: 2026 Guide"
  overview: "Panama to Hungary is a high-complexity route that demands careful planning from the outset. As an unlisted third country under Commission Delegated Regulation (EU) 2026/131, Panama triggers the full titre test pathway for EU pet imports: microchip, rabies vaccination, a minimum 30-day wait, a FAVN blood draw with a result of 0.5 IU/ml or above, and a 90-day wait from the blood draw date before entering Hungary. Total preparation runs 20-26 weeks. Hungary's Nébih authority applies these rules strictly at Budapest Ferenc Liszt International Airport."

  sections:
    - heading: "Nébih's Requirements for Panama-Origin Pets"
      body: |
        Hungary's National Food Chain Safety Office (Nébih, nebih.gov.hu) applies EU non-commercial pet movement rules. For pets from unlisted countries like Panama, the full titre test sequence applies.

        On arrival at Budapest Ferenc Liszt International Airport (BUD), Nébih checks:

        Microchip: ISO 11784/11785 standard, implanted before the rabies vaccination. Nébih scans the chip and verifies it against the health certificate number.

        Rabies vaccination: Current, post-microchip. At least 21 days must have elapsed since the primary vaccination.

        FAVN titre test: Result of 0.5 IU/ml or above from an EU-approved laboratory. The blood must have been drawn at least 30 days after the vaccination. The 90-day wait from the blood draw date must have elapsed before arrival in Hungary.

        Health certificate: EU Annex IV non-commercial format, DINASA-endorsed, issued within 10 days of travel. All titre test details must be included.

        No quarantine. No import permit. But all documents must be complete and consistent.

    - heading: "Why the 90-Day Wait Cannot Be Skipped"
      body: |
        The 90-day waiting period after the blood draw is the element that most surprises families relocating from Panama. It is a regulatory requirement set out in Commission Delegated Regulation (EU) 2026/131, and it exists because the titre test alone does not guarantee that the animal is free from rabies exposure at the time of vaccination. The 90-day period acts as an incubation window.

        This period begins on the date of the blood draw, not the date the result is received, and not the date of the vaccination. It is a fixed 90 days, calculated precisely.

        If your pet arrives in Hungary before the 90 days have elapsed, even by one day, Nébih is required to refuse entry or place the animal in quarantine at your expense. There are no exceptions.

        Begin the process as early as possible. If you know you are relocating to Hungary in 12 months' time, starting the titre test process now means you will have the wait period well behind you before your departure date.

    - heading: "Flight Options from PTY to Budapest"
      body: |
        Budapest Ferenc Liszt International Airport (BUD) does not have direct long-haul cargo services from Panama. The standard routing is a two-leg journey via Amsterdam, Madrid, Frankfurt, or Paris.

        Copa Airlines operates from Tocumen (PTY) to Amsterdam (AMS). From AMS, KLM or partner carriers connect to BUD. Amsterdam is a practical hub for central European connections.

        Iberia Cargo via Madrid (MAD) is the other major option. Iberia's Central America to Europe route via PTY to MAD is well established. From MAD, onward cargo to BUD can be arranged via Vueling, Iberia, or LOT Polish Airlines connections.

        Lufthansa via Frankfurt (FRA) is particularly useful for large animals. FRA to BUD is a short sector served frequently by Lufthansa and local carriers. The Lufthansa live animal centre at FRA handles the longer Panama-to-Europe leg comfortably.

        Air France via CDG is a fourth option if other hubs are full on your preferred dates. CDG to BUD is served by Air France and partner carriers.

    - heading: "Preparing the DINASA Health Certificate"
      body: |
        The health certificate for Hungary must be issued by a MIDA-registered veterinarian and endorsed by DINASA. It must:

        - Follow the EU Annex IV non-commercial health certificate template
        - Include the ISO microchip number
        - Reference the rabies vaccination: drug name, batch number, date administered, valid until date
        - Include the FAVN titre test details: date of blood draw, laboratory name and approval number, result in IU/ml
        - State that the 90-day waiting period has been satisfied
        - Be issued within 10 days of travel

        Allow adequate time for the DINASA endorsement, typically two to four business days. Do not have the certificate signed more than 10 days before departure.

    - heading: "Life in Hungary for Your Pet After Arrival"
      body: |
        Once your pet clears Nébih inspection at BUD, it is free to travel and live in Hungary without further veterinary border requirements. Registration with a local vet is recommended within the first week.

        Hungary requires dogs to be registered with the local municipality in some jurisdictions. Ask your local authority (önkormányzat) whether registration is required in your specific district. A tag or licence may be needed, and annual rabies vaccination is typically required by law.

        For certain breeds under Hungarian breed-specific legislation, additional requirements apply: muzzling in public, specific lead and collar requirements, and third-party liability insurance. Check the current Nébih guidance and local ordinances for your breed before arrival.
