  h1: "Pet Transport from the United States to Fiji"
  overview: |
    Getting a pet from the US to Nadi is, for once, the easy part of this route. Fiji Airways flies its A350 nonstop from Los Angeles and San Francisco, no connections, no transfer to a second carrier partway across the Pacific. Landing is where the real process starts.

    A dog or cat arriving from the United States goes straight into a minimum 30-day government quarantine at Koronivia, near Nausori. That's not a penalty for missing paperwork. Fiji's own biosecurity framework sorts arriving pets by origin country, and the US sits in the same tier as continental Europe, not the shorter 7-day tier reserved for Australia and New Zealand.

    Everything else on this route runs on its own clock alongside that quarantine: a rabies titre test, a BAF import permit valid for 6 months from issue, and a USDA-endorsed health certificate that has to be timed to the flight, not the paperwork.

  sections:
    - heading: "Why a direct flight doesn't mean a simple import"
      body: |
        It's worth being clear about what's genuinely straightforward here. Fiji Airways' nonstop A350 service from Los Angeles or San Francisco means no live-animal transfer mid-journey, no second carrier to coordinate, no risk of a missed connection stranding the animal on a tarmac somewhere in the Pacific. That puts this route ahead of several others on the flight logistics alone.

        None of that touches Fiji's import side. BAF classifies incoming pets by a two-tier risk framework: Group A countries, mainly Australia and New Zealand, face a 7-day stay at Koronivia; everyone else, the US included under the tier third-party relocation sources describe, faces a minimum 30 days. The flight is one leg of the trip. The quarantine is the part that actually shapes the timeline.

    - heading: "The titre test and the permit clock"
      body: |
        A rabies neutralising antibody titre test sits at the centre of the paperwork. BAF does not publish a single, definitive timing window on its own website, and different sources describe slightly different figures for exactly when the blood draw needs to fall relative to export. That's a genuine gap in the public information, not something worth guessing around: confirm the current window with BAF directly when applying for the import permit, rather than relying on any one third-party source, including this page.

        Two other clocks run in parallel. The import permit itself is valid for 6 months from issue, so applying too early risks it lapsing before travel and applying too late risks missing your flight window. The Koronivia quarantine slot also needs booking well ahead, since space is limited and a missed booking can delay the whole move by weeks.

    - heading: "What actually gets checked at the US end"
      body: |
        The US export side is comparatively routine. A USDA-accredited veterinarian completes the health certificate within 10 days of travel, then submits it through the Veterinary Export Health Certification System for APHIS endorsement, a process that usually takes 2 to 10 business days and costs USD 38 per certificate. There is no separate US export permit for a single non-commercial pet.

        The one wrinkle worth planning around: if you're not starting from Los Angeles or San Francisco, the domestic leg to reach a Fiji Airways gateway is a separate booking, not an automatic connection. Line that up with whoever is handling the international cargo shipment, ideally before you commit to a travel date.
