content:
  h1: "Pet Transport from the United States to Fiji"
  overview: |
    Thirty days. That is the minimum a dog or cat leaving the US will spend in a government quarantine facility after landing in Fiji, whatever its paperwork looks like. Fiji's biosecurity authority, BAF, does not judge US-origin pets by the CDC's low-risk rating for Fiji; it runs its own tiered system, and it places the US in the same Group B bracket as continental Europe.

    The rest of the process runs alongside that fact rather than around it: a rabies titre test timed to a window BAF confirms case by case, an import permit that takes about a week to process but needs applying for well ahead of travel, and one genuine piece of good news, a nonstop Fiji Airways flight from Los Angeles straight into Nadi.

  sections:
    - heading: "Why the quarantine happens regardless of your paperwork"
      body: |
        Fiji sorts incoming pets into risk tiers of its own design. Group A, largely Australia and New Zealand, gets a 7-day stay at Koronivia. Everyone else, the US and continental Europe included under the framework BAF uses, falls into Group B and faces a minimum 30-day stay, with monitoring continuing for a year after release.

        This has nothing to do with whether your dog's titre result comes back clean. It's a fixed feature of how a nation that has kept rabies out entirely manages risk from any country it doesn't treat as equivalent to its own rabies-free status. Treat the 30 days as the floor, not a worst case you might avoid with better documentation.

    - heading: "The titre window is not pinned down to one figure"
      body: |
        Different sources describing BAF's framework don't fully agree on the exact blood-draw timing. Some put the acceptable window at roughly 60 days to 12 months before export. A more detailed third-party account instead ties the quarantine length itself to how close the blood draw falls to a 180-day mark: draw it 180 days or more ahead of arrival, and the 30-day minimum applies; draw it closer to travel, and the stay can stretch to 45, 60, 90 or even 115 days.

        BAF doesn't publish one definitive public table pulling this together. The only safe approach is to confirm the current window directly with BAF at the point you apply for the import permit, and to build a margin into your planning rather than working to the shortest figure you've read somewhere.

    - heading: "One genuine piece of good news: a direct flight"
      body: |
        Fiji Airways flies Los Angeles to Nadi nonstop, daily, the same aircraft pair that runs the return leg. That's a real advantage over routes like UK to Fiji, where nothing flies direct and every pet needs a live-animal transfer between two separate carriers.

        It still isn't a simple booking. The flight only carries pets as manifest cargo, reserved through the Fiji Freight Department or an IATA cargo agent rather than a normal ticket, and the airline holds a firm line on size and age: nothing under 6 months old, nothing under 6 lbs (3 kg). If you're departing from somewhere other than Los Angeles, budget for a domestic cargo connection into LAX first, and confirm both legs before booking a date.
