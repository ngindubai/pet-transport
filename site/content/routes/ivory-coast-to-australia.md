  h1: "Pet Transport from Ivory Coast to Australia"
  overview: |
    Two flights a day. Air France's Abidjan-Paris schedule, already the busiest option covering this pair for the UK and US routes on this site, does double duty here as the leg that opens Australia's only real path in. Cote d'Ivoire holds no DAFF approval of any kind, so a direct import from Abidjan simply is not on offer. What is on offer is 180 days living in an approved country first, and France, reachable up to twice daily, is the practical pick.

    Thirty to thirty-six weeks is the honest estimate once the residency, the RNATT titre test and Mickleham's quarantine are all stacked up. None of those steps compress, whatever an agent promises.

    Set this against the same pet's UAE journey, described elsewhere on this site: a titre test and an import permit, nothing more. No residency, no staging country, no 180-day wait. The gap between how DAFF and MOCCAE treat the same Ivorian passport is not subtle.

  sections:
    - heading: "Why France works twice over"
      body: |
        DSV, Cote d'Ivoire's veterinary authority, already handles the export paperwork for Ivorian pets heading to France on Air France's frequent Abidjan-Paris service. That same flight now serves double duty: it is also the flight that starts the 180-day clock DAFF requires before any Australian application can begin, since France holds Group 3 approval.

        Owners moving to Australia from a non-approved country often have to add a detour just to reach an approved one. This route does not need that, since the existing, frequent air link into France covers both jobs at once.

    - heading: "What happens during the 180 days"
      body: |
        The rabies vaccination and RNATT titre test both happen inside the French residency window, with the blood sample needing to reach an approved laboratory somewhere between 180 days and a year before the Australia-bound flight. Get the microchip identity verification done before that blood draw, and make sure it lands at least 180 days before the planned export date: doing so is what shrinks Mickleham's quarantine from 30 days to 10.

        Miss that sequencing and the extra 20 days of quarantine land squarely on the owner's bill, since Mickleham charges by the day.

    - heading: "Paris to Melbourne, and why Singapore is in the mix"
      body: |
        Qantas does not currently fly Paris directly, so the Australia-bound leg typically routes through Singapore instead, using the SATS Coolport live animal facility at Changi. It is a small irony that the transit point, Singapore, is itself a DAFF-approved Group 2 country, even though the actual residency was served in France.

        However the pet arrives, it lands in Melbourne and heads to Mickleham. There is no alternative port of entry for quarantine purposes, whatever city in Australia is the final destination.
