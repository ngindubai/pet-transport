content:
  h1: "Pet Transport from United States to Nicaragua"
  overview: |
    Nicaragua's own paperwork for a pet arriving from the US amounts to two documents and a permit picked up on landing. The slower part, oddly, sits on the American side: the export certificate has to be posted to USDA for an ink signature and an embossed stamp, not filed electronically the way it can be for many other destinations.

    No titre test, no waiting period tied to a blood draw, and no kennel stay on arrival despite the alarming name attached to Nicaragua's entry permit. A current rabies vaccination, a vet exam within 2 weeks of departure, and a flight through Miami, Fort Lauderdale or Panama City cover the whole route.

  sections:
    - heading: "The USDA certificate: why it needs the postal service"
      body: |
        Most APHIS country-specific export pathways now run through the eFile system, an electronic endorsement that typically costs around USD 38 and clears in a day or two. Nicaragua is not one of them. The certificate is completed by a USDA-accredited vet through VEHCS, then has to be physically ink-signed and embossed by USDA and mailed to accompany the animal.

        That mail step is easy to underestimate. Add postal transit in both directions to the standard processing time and this single requirement can take longer than every other step in the process combined. Book the vet exam within the 2-week validity window, but do not treat "2 weeks before travel" as when the paperwork process starts.

    - heading: "What actually happens when your pet lands in Managua"
      body: |
        Nicaragua calls its entry document a quarantine permit, which understandably worries owners expecting a kennel stay. It is paperwork, not confinement. IPSA and USDA APHIS both describe the same process: present the original export certificate and rabies record at the port of entry, and the permit is issued there.

        This applies the same way whether the pet is arriving from the US, the UK, or anywhere else. There is no separate, harsher rule reserved for one country of origin over another.

    - heading: "Three ways into Managua"
      body: |
        American Airlines' daily non-stop from Miami is the most established routing, paired with a PetEmbark cargo booking that aacargo.com confirms is open to the general public. Avianca's newer Fort Lauderdale to Managua non-stop is worth comparing, though as a recently added route it is sensible to double-check current frequency before locking in dates.

        Copa Airlines, via Panama City, is a genuine third option, but its cargo programme only runs Monday to Thursday. Plan around that if a weekend arrival is otherwise convenient, since the airline will not shift on it.
