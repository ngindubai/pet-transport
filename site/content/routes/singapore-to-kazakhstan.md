  sections:
    - heading: "The KVCS permit: the step that sets your earliest travel date"
      body: |
        Unlike Kazakhstan's import rules generally, which don't currently ask for a titre test, the KVCS import permit is unavoidable and slow. Apply with your pet's species, breed, age, microchip number, vaccination records and intended entry point, then expect 10-12 weeks before written confirmation. The KVCS's process is less predictable than some other national authorities, so build in contingency time rather than assuming the fastest published estimate.

        Kazakhstan sits within the Eurasian Economic Union, but that affects trade flows between EEU member states, not a pet import from Singapore. The standard KVCS process applies in full regardless.

    - heading: "Bangkok, not a European hub, is the practical way in"
      body: |
        Most Central Asia-bound pet moves from Southeast Asia end up routing through Europe or the Middle East, adding a long connection. This one doesn't have to. Air Astana operates daily between Bangkok and Almaty, a route the airline expanded as part of its broader Southeast Asian network push in late 2025, and Singapore to Bangkok runs multiple times a day on several carriers.

        Confirm Air Astana's live animal cargo acceptance on the specific dates you need, since a passenger seat booking doesn't guarantee cargo hold space. If Bangkok doesn't work for your timing, Turkish Airlines' Istanbul hub, already verified on this site's Germany-Kazakhstan route, is the established fallback, though it adds its own connection from Singapore.
