  sections:
    - heading: "The three fixed waiting periods that determine your timeline"
      body: |
        On the Armenia-to-Poland route, three waiting periods control when travel is possible. None of them can be shortened.

        The first: after the rabies vaccination, you must wait at least 30 days before drawing blood for the FAVN titre test. This is the minimum immunological response window. Drawing blood earlier gives an inaccurate result and the test fails.

        The second: after the blood draw, the sample goes to the EU-approved laboratory. Lab processing takes 2-4 weeks from receipt of sample. This is not a regulatory wait but a practical one. Factor it into your plans.

        The third: once you have a passing titre test result (0.5 IU/ml or above), a mandatory 90-day wait starts from the blood draw date before your pet can enter Poland. Not from the results date. From the draw date. If results arrive on day 20 after the draw, you have 70 more days to wait.

        Add the microchipping appointment (before vaccination) and the SSFS health certificate window (within 10 days of travel), and 22-28 weeks is a realistic total.

    - heading: "Getting the health certificate right for Poland"
      body: |
        The SSFS health certificate is the document that covers your pet for the journey from Yerevan to Warsaw. It is issued by an SSFS-authorised vet in Armenia, endorsed by the SSFS, and is valid as an entry document for 10 days from the date of issue.

        The certificate must be in the EU format for non-commercial pet movements from unlisted third countries. The format is specified in EU Regulation 2020/692. Your Armenian vet must use the current template, not an older format.

        Book the vet appointment at least two weeks before your planned travel date. The SSFS endorsement step is not instant: the vet issues the certificate, submits it to the SSFS, and the SSFS endorses it. In Yerevan this process takes several working days. If you leave it until 3-4 days before travel, you risk the endorsement arriving after your scheduled departure.

faqs:
  - question: "Does Poland have any pet import requirements beyond the EU baseline for unlisted countries?"
    answer: "No. Poland applies standard EU rules under Regulation 2020/692 for pets from unlisted third countries: microchip, rabies vaccination, FAVN titre test at 0.5 IU/ml or above, 90-day wait from blood draw, and an SSFS-endorsed AHC issued within 10 days. The GIW (Chief Veterinary Inspectorate) checks documentation on arrival at Warsaw Chopin. No Polish-specific additions."
  - question: "Can I travel to Warsaw on the same cargo flight as my pet?"
    answer: "Your pet travels as cargo, and the cargo terminal at WAW is separate from the passenger terminal. If you fly on the same day, your pet's cargo booking and your passenger ticket are handled separately, but both arrive at Warsaw Chopin Airport (WAW). Your IPATA agent coordinates the cargo paperwork and drop-off/collection schedule."
  - question: "What happens if my pet's FAVN titre test fails?"
    answer: "If the result is below 0.5 IU/ml, the test is classed as a failure. You must wait at least 30 days from the first blood draw before re-vaccinating or waiting for a booster, then draw blood again. The 90-day wait starts fresh from the date of the new successful blood draw. A failed test adds a minimum of 3-4 months to your timeline."
