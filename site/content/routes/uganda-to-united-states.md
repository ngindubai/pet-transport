  h1: "Pet Transport from Uganda to the United States"
  overview: |
    Uganda to USA is one of the more demanding international pet moves, and the complexity centres on a single document chain. Because Uganda is classified as a CDC high-risk country for dog rabies, every dog imported to the US from Uganda must go through a triple endorsement chain: a US-licensed vet issues the CDC Dog Import Form (Form CDC 6-527), a USDA APHIS-accredited vet then endorses it, and MAAIF in Uganda endorses the full package. Getting each endorsement in the correct order, without errors, within the valid window takes careful coordination and several weeks.

    Alongside the endorsement chain, your dog needs a rabies vaccination, a titre test at a CDC-recognised laboratory with blood drawn at least 30 days after vaccination, and a confirmed reservation at a CDC-registered facility in the US before the flight departs Entebbe.

    Cats have no federal CDC requirements but must appear healthy on arrival.

    Allow 16-24 weeks of preparation time. An IPATA-registered agent with CDC high-risk country experience is strongly advisable for this route.
  sections:
    - heading: "The triple endorsement chain: what it is and why it matters"
      body: |
        The CDC's high-risk country classification for Uganda triggers a documentation requirement that goes beyond a standard vet-issued health certificate. The CDC Dog Import Form (Form CDC 6-527) must be endorsed by three separate parties, in a fixed order, before your dog can enter the US.

        Step one: a US-licensed veterinarian prepares and issues the CDC form. Step two: a USDA APHIS-accredited veterinarian endorses the form. Step three: MAAIF (Uganda's Ministry of Agriculture, Animal Industry and Fisheries) endorses the complete package. Reversing any step or skipping one invalidates the form and will prevent entry.

        Co-ordinating a US-licensed vet, USDA APHIS, and MAAIF from Uganda takes time. Allow 4-6 weeks for the endorsement chain alone, separate from the titre test timeline. Your IPATA agent can manage this process for you, but the instructions and timing need to be right from the start.

        Source: CDC, cdc.gov/importation/dogs.html. Verify current requirements before booking, as CDC rules are updated periodically.

    - heading: "Cargo routing from Entebbe: airlines and connections"
      body: |
        All pet cargo from Uganda departs from Entebbe International Airport (EBB). There are no direct Uganda-to-USA cargo routes. All options involve at least one hub connection.

        Ethiopian Airlines Cargo via Addis Ababa (ADD) is the most established option for East Africa to USA. The Entebbe-Addis Ababa leg connects to Ethiopian's long-haul service into Washington Dulles (IAD) or New York JFK. Ethiopian Cargo handles East African pet exports regularly and the routing is well-tested for live animals.

        Emirates SkyCargo via Dubai (DXB) and Qatar Airways Cargo via Doha (DOH) both offer broad US coverage as alternatives. Gulf carriers apply temperature embargoes during summer months, which can restrict pet cargo acceptance between roughly June and September. Plan around these windows if your travel date falls in that period.

        Turkish Airlines Cargo via Istanbul (IST) is a practical fourth option, particularly if space on the other three is unavailable for your travel dates.

        Book cargo space 4-8 weeks ahead. Pet cargo space is limited and popular departure dates fill quickly.

    - heading: "MAAIF export requirements: what Uganda needs before your pet leaves"
      body: |
        Uganda's Ministry of Agriculture, Animal Industry and Fisheries (MAAIF) at maaif.go.ug is the competent authority for animal exports. Two documents are required from MAAIF before your pet can board at Entebbe.

        The export health certificate is prepared by a MAAIF-authorised veterinarian. It records the microchip number, vaccination history, titre test results, and the animal's health status on examination. MAAIF then endorses the certificate. This document is also the foundation for the US-side endorsement chain, so any errors on it flow through to the CDC form.

        The MAAIF export permit is a separate authorisation required for the export of live animals from Uganda. Allow time for both documents, as MAAIF processing times vary. Your IPATA agent or a local clearing agent in Kampala will have current guidance on turnaround times.

        Keep originals and copies of all documents: microchip record, vaccination certificate, titre test result, CDC form with all endorsements, facility reservation, export permit, and the endorsed health certificate.
