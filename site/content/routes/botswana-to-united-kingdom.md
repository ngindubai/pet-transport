  h1: "Pet Transport from Botswana to the United Kingdom"
  overview: |
    Gaborone's Sir Seretse Khama International Airport runs around 60 scheduled departures a week to Johannesburg alone, split between Airlink and Air Botswana. That's a genuinely busy regional sector, and it matters, because it's also the only realistic way out of Botswana for a pet heading to the UK. No airline flies Gaborone to London directly.

    Botswana also sits outside both of Great Britain's listed-country tables, the same unlisted category that covers most of Africa. A rabies titre test at an approved laboratory is required, followed by a fixed three-month wait counted from the day blood is drawn, not the day the result lands. Add Botswana's own export paperwork, which runs a separate permit alongside the health certificate, and this route typically takes four and a half to five and a half months from a standing start.

  sections:
    - heading: "Why the blood draw date, not the result date, controls your calendar"
      body: |
        Great Britain doesn't count the three-month wait from when your vet emails you a passing titre result. It counts from the day the needle went in. Miss that distinction in your planning and you'll misjudge your travel window by however long the lab took to respond, which can easily be several weeks.

        Work backwards from your intended travel date. Blood needs to be drawn at least three months and thirty days before you fly, since the thirty-day gap after vaccination has to happen first. For a dog or cat that's already current on its rabies jab, the process moves faster: the vaccination step is already done, so you're only counting the thirty-day gap (if it hasn't already passed) plus the three-month wait.

        One thing worth checking early: if your pet already holds a passing titre result from an earlier international move, and the underlying vaccination hasn't lapsed, that result can sometimes still count. Ask your vet to check the dates rather than assuming you have to start the whole process again.

    - heading: "Botswana's export permit, and why it's not the same as Namibia's process"
      body: |
        Botswana's Department of Veterinary Services asks for something a little more involved than some of its neighbours. A standalone export permit, separate from the health certificate itself, has to be issued before the pet can leave. Namibia's equivalent office, by contrast, controls exports through the health certificate alone, with no separate permit step. If you've researched a Namibia route and assumed Botswana works the same way, budget the extra three to five working days DVS typically needs to process the permit.

        Once that's sorted, the physical journey runs through Johannesburg. Airlink and Air Botswana both fly the Gaborone to O.R. Tambo sector multiple times daily, which gives real flexibility on timing. From Johannesburg, British Airways' daily service into Heathrow, handled by IAG Cargo, covers the long-haul leg. An IPATA-registered agent who has actually coordinated a Johannesburg handover is worth involving, since the transfer between a regional carrier and a long-haul one with a live animal on board isn't something to leave to a standard airline booking.
