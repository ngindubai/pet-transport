  h1: "Pet Transport from Kazakhstan to Sweden"
  overview: |
    Kazakhstan to Sweden takes five to six months from first vet appointment to arrival. Kazakhstan is EU-unlisted, so the full EU protocol applies. The standout routing advantage is Air Astana's direct service from Almaty or Astana to Amsterdam Schiphol, followed by a short hop to Stockholm Arlanda. This keeps the long-haul leg on Kazakhstan's national carrier with excellent cargo infrastructure.

    The EU-format health certificate from Kazakhstan requires specific attention: standard Kazakh export documentation uses the EAEU format, which is not valid for Sweden.

  sections:
    - heading: "Sweden's EU import requirements for Kazakh pets"
      body: |
        Sweden applies EU Regulation 576/2013 to all pets from unlisted third countries. Kazakhstan is unlisted. The required steps: ISO microchip implanted before vaccination, rabies vaccination, FAVN titre test at an EU-approved laboratory (minimum 0.5 IU/ml, blood drawn 30+ days after vaccination), 90-day wait from blood draw date, and Kazakh Veterinary Committee-endorsed EU-format health certificate valid 10 days.

        Jordbruksverket (jordbruksverket.se) inspects incoming pets at Stockholm Arlanda Airport (ARN). Compliant arrivals are released without quarantine.

    - heading: "Air Astana: from Almaty to Amsterdam to Stockholm"
      body: |
        Air Astana's direct service from Almaty (ALA) or Astana (NQZ) to Amsterdam Schiphol (AMS) is the strongest routing option for Kazakhstan to Sweden. Air Astana Cargo accepts live animals on these routes.

        From Amsterdam, Stockholm Arlanda (ARN) is just under two hours by air. SAS, Norwegian, and KLM all offer regular AMS-to-ARN services. Your pet arrives at AMS from Kazakhstan, is transferred to the feeder carrier (or you coordinate cargo through to ARN), and arrives at Arlanda in good time.

        The alternative is Turkish Airlines via Istanbul, which operates as a single-carrier routing from ALA or NQZ through IST to ARN. This is simpler in terms of cargo booking but involves a Middle Eastern hub rather than the Amsterdam-to-Stockholm Scandinavian routing.

    - heading: "EU-format certificate: the documentation challenge"
      body: |
        Kazakhstan routinely issues EAEU-format veterinary certificates for exports to Russia, Belarus, Armenia, and other EAEU members. Sweden requires an EU-format health certificate under EU Regulation 2020/692 for non-commercial pet movement from unlisted third countries.

        These are different documents with different structure and content. Not all vets in Kazakhstan will be familiar with or able to prepare the EU-format version. Work with an IPATA-registered agent experienced on Kazakhstan-to-EU routes, or identify a Kazakh vet with EU export documentation experience, before beginning the process.
