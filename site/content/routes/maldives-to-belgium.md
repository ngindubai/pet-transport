content:
  h1: "Pet Transport from Maldives to Belgium"
  overview: |
    No airline flies Male to Brussels non-stop. That single gap in the schedule shapes almost every planning decision on this route, because it means every cat travels through a third country before it ever sees Belgian soil. Add the fact that the Maldives has no laboratory capable of running a rabies antibody titre test, and the blood sample itself becomes a small international shipment before the 90-day clock can even start ticking.

    Dogs are not banned from leaving the Maldives. What's banned is bringing one in, which is why almost nobody there owns one and why every genuine enquiry on this route concerns a cat, usually belonging to a resort worker or a diplomat finishing a Male posting.

  sections:
    - heading: "Getting blood off the island"
      body: |
        A titre test needs an EU-approved laboratory, and none exists on the Maldives. The vet in Male draws the sample, packs it correctly, and ships it abroad, most commonly toward Europe on the same Dubai or Doha routing the cat will eventually fly. That courier leg typically adds one to two weeks before the 30-day post-vaccination window has even closed, on top of whatever time the lab itself needs to issue a result.

    - heading: "FASFC's sequence, and where the 90 days start"
      body: |
        Belgium treats the Maldives exactly as it treats any country outside the EU's approved list: microchip, rabies vaccination, a titre test at 0.5 IU/ml or above, and 90 days measured from the draw date. Not the result date. FASFC checks all of this at the Brussels Airport cargo terminal itself when the cat lands, so the endorsed certificate needs to be in hand, not packed away in the crate's paperwork pocket.

    - heading: "Why there's no straight route to Brussels"
      body: |
        Emirates and Qatar Airways both run dozens of weekly connecting services between Male and Brussels via their home hubs, and either is a workable choice on paper. Brussels Airlines itself doesn't touch the Male leg; its own booking system routes through one of those two carriers as a codeshare, which means the airline whose name is on your ticket may not be the one whose cargo hold your cat actually travels in. Confirm the operating carrier before locking in dates.
