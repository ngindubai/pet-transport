  h1: "Pet Transport from Moldova to Canada"
  overview: |
    This route runs the opposite way to the story most owners already know about Moldova. A Canadian pet heading into Moldova gets an easy ride: Canada's own place on the EU's Annex II approved list means no titre test at that end. Coming back, Moldova itself sits on CFIA's high-risk-for-dog-rabies register, updated 13 March 2025. Read only that fact and the two directions look mismatched.

    They aren't, not for an ordinary family pet. CFIA's high-risk listing mostly targets commercial dog shipments, banned outright since 2022. A dog or cat travelling with its owner still needs nothing more than a current vaccination and a correctly built certificate.

  sections:
    - heading: "Two lists, two countries, one light outcome"
      body: |
        It's worth being precise about which classification governs which direction. Moldova's own animal health rules exempt Canadian pets from a titre test because Canada sits independently on the EU's Annex II list, the same fact that makes the Canada-to-Moldova route genuinely simple. CFIA's high-risk list, which does include Moldova, governs the reverse direction instead, and it works on a different logic entirely: it bars commercial dog imports rather than adding a blood test for personal pets.

        Put together, a family moving a dog or cat between Canada and Moldova, in either direction, ends up facing a lighter process than the country names alone might suggest. The genuine friction sits in flight routing and paperwork format, not in serology.

    - heading: "The age line that decides which rule bites"
      body: |
        CFIA presumes a dog under 8 months old, not travelling with its named owner, is a commercial import. From a high-risk country like Moldova, that presumption has real consequences, since commercial dogs from listed countries are barred outright. A dog old enough, and genuinely accompanied by the owner named on its papers, sits outside that presumption. Confirm directly with CFIA if either detail could be in question for your move.

    - heading: "Three connecting hubs, no nonstop option"
      body: |
        Chisinau has no direct link to Canada, so the choice comes down to which European hub suits the schedule. Turkish Airlines via Istanbul offers a nonstop Istanbul-Toronto onward sector and a dedicated live animal facility for the layover. Austrian Airlines via Vienna and LOT Polish Airlines via Warsaw both connect onward to Canadian gateways through their own networks or partner carriers, useful alternatives if Turkish's schedule or crate limits don't fit.
