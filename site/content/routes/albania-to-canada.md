  h1: "Pet Transport from Albania to Canada"
  overview: |
    Turkish Airlines is the one airline that can carry a pet from Tirana all the way to Toronto or Montreal without a change of carrier. That single fact does more to simplify this route than any regulatory quirk does, because the regulatory side was already light before the flight came into it.

    Albania doesn't sit on CFIA's list of countries at high risk for dog rabies, a list last updated 13 March 2025. A personal pet needs a current rabies vaccination and a correctly formatted certificate. No blood test, no import permit, no waiting period beyond getting the paperwork right.

  sections:
    - heading: "Why the titre test never comes up here"
      body: |
        CFIA splits the world into high-risk and everything else for dog rabies purposes, and Albania falls into the second group. That's a different classification system entirely from the EU's Annex II list that governs Albania's UK route, or the CDC's separate high-risk table that shapes its US route. Three countries, three lists, and Albania happens to land on the lighter side of all three for different reasons.

        The practical result for Canada is straightforward. A dog needs a current vaccination recorded in English or French. A cat needs even less. Neither needs blood drawn, sent to a lab, or waited on.

    - heading: "One carrier, one connection, no changeover"
      body: |
        Turkish Airlines already flies Tirana to Istanbul, a leg well established on this site's other Albania pages. What makes the Canada route different is that Turkish also operates Istanbul to Toronto and Istanbul to Montreal nonstop, so a pet can travel on a single airline's paperwork and handling from departure to arrival, with one layover rather than a change of carrier partway through.

        That's genuinely uncommon on this site's Albania-origin routes, most of which need a European carrier for one leg and a different one for the next. Anyone whose schedule doesn't fit Turkish's slots still has a fallback: Air Canada to Frankfurt, then Lufthansa on to Tirana, working the same journey in reverse.

    - heading: "Building the certificate for the right destination"
      body: |
        An AKU-registered vet who has already issued export certificates for Albania's UK or US pages knows those formats well. Canada's isn't the same document. CFIA doesn't ask for the titre test or the 3-month wait that GB's unlisted-country rule demands, and it wants the certificate in English or French rather than the bilingual Albanian/English layout used elsewhere.

        Flag Canada clearly at the vet appointment. A certificate built for a different destination can be technically accurate and still miss the specific detail a CFIA officer checks on arrival.
