  sections:
    - heading: "Why flying is the only sensible plan on this route"
      body: |
        Slovenia's shorter intra-EU routes into Hungary or the Czech Republic can be driven in half a day. Portugal is a different scale of distance entirely: over 2,000 kilometres and roughly 20 hours behind the wheel one way. Almost nobody does that with a dog or cat in the back seat, and for good reason.

        A connecting flight through Munich, Frankfurt or another European hub is the practical choice for every pet on this route, from a small cat to a large dog. Book cabin space early if your pet qualifies; cargo capacity is more flexible but needs longer lead time for larger crates.

    - heading: "The paperwork: passport, AHC and the April 2026 change"
      body: |
        The EU pet passport, issued by a UVHVVR-authorised vet in Slovenia, is valid for the pet's life provided the rabies vaccination inside it stays current. Since 22 April 2026, that passport is restricted to owners primarily resident in the EU, a rule that leaves the overwhelming majority of Slovenian pet owners unaffected.

        Non-EU nationals resident in Slovenia need a single-use AHC instead, issued within 10 days of travel and endorsed by UVHVVR. DGAV, Portugal's veterinary authority, checks the microchip against the passport or AHC on arrival, so confirm the numbers match before departure.

    - heading: "Flight options via Munich, Frankfurt or a direct Lisbon connection"
      body: |
        Lufthansa's Munich and Frankfurt hubs both connect onward to Lisbon, with cabin space for small pets and a cargo service for larger ones. TAP Air Portugal, the national carrier, is often the strongest choice for the final leg into Lisbon itself, since it's built around that airport as its home base. Compare total transit time across both options rather than booking the first connection you find; a shorter layover matters more to a travelling pet than a marginally cheaper fare.
