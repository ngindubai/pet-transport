  h1: "Pet Transport from Tunisia to the United Arab Emirates"
  overview: |
    Tunisair and Emirates both fly Tunis-Carthage to Dubai nonstop, roughly 13 times a week between them, in about six hours. That's a genuinely direct route, rare on this site. It doesn't shorten the paperwork.

    MOCCAE keeps Tunisia off its short list of roughly 50 low-risk countries, so a FAVN titre test is mandatory, not the vaccination-only route a UK or EU-origin pet gets. Every animal also travels as manifest cargo into the UAE, never in the cabin or as checked baggage, whatever the airline's normal pet policy allows on other routes.

    Budget eight to ten weeks. Most of that is the titre test and lab turnaround, not the flight itself.

  sections:
    - heading: "A direct flight, an indirect paperwork trail"
      body: |
        There's no European hub to route through here, which sets this route apart from Tunisia's transatlantic and UK pages. Tunisair's own network reaches Dubai nonstop, and Emirates covers the same city pair with its own metal. Both carry pets, but only as manifest cargo, a separate booking with its own air waybill, not a checked-bag add-on to a human passenger ticket.

        That distinction catches owners out. A Tunisair flight that would happily carry an 8kg cat in the cabin between Tunis and London won't do the same into Dubai. MOCCAE's cargo mandate applies to the destination, not the carrier, so it overrides whatever the airline would otherwise allow.

    - heading: "Why the titre test still applies on a route this short"
      body: |
        MOCCAE splits the world into a short low-risk list, roughly 50 countries including the UK, most of the EU, Japan and Australia, and everyone else. Tunisia sits outside that list, so the FAVN titre test applies in full: blood drawn at least 21 days after a first vaccination, a laboratory result of at least 0.5 IU/ml, and a sample no more than 12 months old on arrival.

        There's no extra waiting period after a passing result, unlike the three-month wait Great Britain and the EU both impose. Once the number comes back at or above 0.5 IU/ml, the pet can travel as soon as the rest of the paperwork and cargo booking line up.

    - heading: "Manifest cargo only, and what that means in practice"
      body: |
        No pet can enter the UAE as cabin luggage or checked baggage, full stop. Every animal, regardless of size, moves as manifest cargo with its own air waybill, booked through Tunisair Cargo or Emirates SkyCargo rather than the passenger reservation system. That adds a cargo agent into the process for most owners, since manifest cargo bookings aren't typically self-service the way a checked pet fare is.

        Build this into the 3-4 week booking window. Cargo capacity on a specific date isn't guaranteed just because passenger seats are available on the same flight.

    - heading: "The breed check that overrides the paperwork"
      body: |
        MOCCAE bans Pit Bull Terriers, Japanese Tosa and several mastiff-type breeds from entering the UAE, and it applies phenotypic identification: what the dog looks like at the cargo desk, not what the certificate says. A rescue mix with mastiff features can be refused on sight, whatever the microchip record claims about lineage. Check this before any vet appointment gets booked, since it's the one part of this process with no appeal.
