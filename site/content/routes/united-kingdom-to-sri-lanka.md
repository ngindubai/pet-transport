  h1: "Pet Transport UK to Sri Lanka: The 90-Day Wait and Everything That Follows"
  overview: "Sri Lanka has one of the most involved pet import processes in South Asia. A titre test is required for all dogs and cats, followed by a mandatory 90-day waiting period after the test. On top of that, the DAPH import permit requires a 3-month application lead time, and the health certificate must be attested by the Sri Lanka High Commission in London before travel. After all this, a 30-day home quarantine applies on arrival. Allow 6-7 months to prepare. Thousands of pets have made this journey successfully with careful planning."

  sections:
    - heading: "The titre test and the 90-day wait"
      body: |
        This is the step that defines the timeline for UK to Sri Lanka pet transport. All dogs and cats must have a rabies antibody titre test at a DAPH-approved laboratory. The blood draw must be at least 30 days after the most recent rabies vaccination, and once the result comes back (it must be 0.5 IU/ml or above), you must wait 90 days before your pet can travel. That 90-day wait is non-negotiable.

        Working backwards, if you plan to fly to Sri Lanka in late November, your titre test blood draw must happen before late August, which means your rabies vaccination needs to be done no later than late July. And the DAPH import permit application, which takes 3 months, must go in by late August at the latest. These timelines overlap, so plan everything simultaneously rather than sequentially.

    - heading: "Health certificate attestation and home quarantine"
      body: |
        After the DAPH import permit is issued, the final documentation step is the APHA Export Health Certificate, which must be APHA-endorsed and then separately attested by the Sri Lanka High Commission in London. The attestation confirms the authenticity of the APHA endorsement for Sri Lankan authorities. Without it, the certificate will not be accepted. The certificate itself must be issued within 14 days of travel, so the attestation needs to happen in the final days before departure. Build in time for the High Commission's processing schedule.

        After arriving at Bandaranaike International Airport in Colombo, you will sign a Quarantine Surveillance Agreement with the DAPH Quarantine Officer present at the airport. From that point, your pet stays at your Sri Lankan home address under DAPH surveillance for 30 days. DAPH officers may conduct spot checks. Your pet should not leave the premises during this period. After 30 days, DAPH clears the quarantine and your pet is free to move normally.

faqs:
  - question: "Why is there a 90-day wait after the titre test for Sri Lanka?"
    answer: "Sri Lanka's DAPH requires 90 days after the titre test as a post-vaccination observation period, consistent with the approach used by several rabies-free island nations. The titre test demonstrates that your pet has produced adequate antibodies against rabies, and the 90-day wait provides additional confidence that the protection has been established. This cannot be shortened by any means, including re-vaccination or additional testing."

  - question: "What is the Sri Lanka home quarantine and how does it work?"
    answer: "30-day home quarantine means your pet stays at your home address in Sri Lanka under surveillance by DAPH officers, rather than at a government facility. On arrival at the airport, you sign a Quarantine Surveillance Agreement with the DAPH Quarantine Officer. For the following 30 days, your pet must remain on the premises. DAPH may conduct unannounced checks. At the end of 30 days, DAPH releases the quarantine and your pet is free to move normally."

  - question: "What is the Sri Lanka High Commission attestation and where do I get it?"
    answer: "The health certificate attestation confirms to Sri Lankan authorities that the APHA endorsement on your health certificate is genuine. You take the APHA-endorsed health certificate to the Sri Lanka High Commission in London, where they add their official stamp and signature. This must happen after the health certificate is issued (within 14 days of travel) and before you fly. Check the Sri Lanka High Commission website for current processing times and fees, as these can vary."

  - question: "Are any dog breeds banned from entering Sri Lanka?"
    answer: "American Pit Bull Terriers are banned from import into Sri Lanka under DAPH regulations. Other breeds are not subject to a specific national ban. If your dog has Pit Bull characteristics or resembles the banned type, contact DAPH before applying for the import permit. Do not attempt to import a banned breed, as this results in the animal being refused entry."
