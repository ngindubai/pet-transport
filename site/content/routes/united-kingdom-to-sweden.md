  sections:
    - heading: "What changed after Brexit for UK pets going to the EU"
      body: |
        Before January 2021, UK-issued EU pet passports were valid across the EU, including Sweden. That is no longer the case. UK pets now need an Animal Health Certificate (AHC) for each trip to the EU.

        The AHC is issued by an official vet who has been authorised by the UK's Animal and Plant Health Agency (APHA). Your regular vet may be authorised; if not, they can refer you to one who is. The certificate confirms your pet's microchip number, vaccination history, and health status.

        The 10-day window is fixed. Even if your vet has the appointment scheduled earlier, the certificate cannot be dated more than 10 days before your travel date. Book the vet appointment accordingly.

    - heading: "Sweden is one of the easier EU destinations for UK pets"
      body: |
        Sweden's import rules for pets from listed countries like the UK are uncomplicated. Microchip, current rabies vaccination with the correct timing, and an AHC covers you. No titre test, no import permit, no quarantine.

        Sweden also has no national breed-specific legislation, which means all breeds of dog are permitted. A few other EU countries have breed bans that UK owners moving with certain breeds need to plan around. Sweden is not one of them.

        The main practical consideration is air cargo for larger dogs. SAS and British Airways both serve London-Stockholm, and both have cargo pet services. For smaller dogs, some airlines allow cabin travel in an approved carrier.

faqs:
  - question: "Do I need a new Animal Health Certificate every time I travel from the UK to Sweden?"
    answer: "Yes. Each trip from the UK to Sweden (or any EU country) requires a new AHC issued within 10 days of that journey. The AHC is valid for one entry into the EU and then for up to 4 months of travel within the EU before it expires. It is not reusable for a subsequent trip."
  - question: "Does my pet need a rabies titre test to enter Sweden from the UK?"
    answer: "No. Sweden follows EU rules and the UK is listed as an approved third country. No titre test is required for UK pets entering Sweden. You need the AHC, a valid microchip (implanted before the rabies vaccination), and a current rabies vaccination that has been valid for at least 21 days."
  - question: "Are any dog breeds banned from entering Sweden?"
    answer: "No. Sweden has no breed-specific legislation at the national level. All breeds of dog can be imported. This contrasts with some other European countries that restrict Pit Bull types and related breeds. Always check local Swedish regulations where you are settling, as some municipalities may have separate bylaws."
  - question: "How long does the process take to prepare my pet for the move?"
    answer: "If your pet's microchip and rabies vaccination are already in order, the main task is booking the AHC appointment within 10 days of travel and arranging the cargo or cabin space on your flight. The paperwork side takes 2-4 weeks. If vaccinations need updating, allow at least 4-6 weeks so the 21-day wait is satisfied before you travel."
