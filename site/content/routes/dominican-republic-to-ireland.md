content:
  h1: "Pet Transport from Dominican Republic to Ireland"
  overview: "Two separate compliance steps stand between a pet in Santo Domingo and a home in Dublin. One is the FAVN titre test that Ireland demands from every EU-unlisted country, the Dominican Republic included. The other is the TRACES pre-arrival notification that Ireland demands from everyone, titre test or not. Together they push the realistic timeline to 20 to 26 weeks. Cats face the same sequence as dogs, no shortcuts either way."

  sections:
    - heading: "The FAVN titre test: why the Dominican Republic needs it"
      body: |
        The Dominican Republic sits outside the EU's approved country list, which means the unlisted-country sequence applies in full. Microchip first, rabies vaccination second, then a minimum 30-day wait before blood goes to an EU-approved lab for the FAVN titre test. A result of at least 0.5 IU/ml starts a 90-day countdown from the draw date. Only once that period ends can the pet legally enter Ireland.

        There's no way to compress this. Owners sometimes ask about running the vaccination and blood draw earlier to save time; the vaccination has to come after a confirmed microchip, and the blood draw has to wait 30 days after that. Plan from day one, not from the flight date.

    - heading: "TRACES: the step that applies whatever else is going on"
      body: |
        Most EU states stop at a valid health certificate. Ireland adds a pre-arrival notification through TRACES, the EU's Trade Control and Expert System, submitted ahead of travel and usually arranged by the vet or transport agent. It runs alongside the titre test result and the health certificate, not instead of them.

        This is worth flagging because it isn't tied to whether a titre test was needed at all. Jamaica-origin pets, for instance, skip the titre test under an EU listing exemption but still need TRACES for Ireland, exactly like Dominican Republic pets do. The Department of Agriculture, Food and the Marine (DAFM) treats missing or late TRACES notifications the same way it treats a missing health certificate: as a compliance failure, not a formality.

    - heading: "Getting from Santo Domingo to Dublin"
      body: |
        Iberia's Santo Domingo to Madrid service is the most direct route out, with a connection onward to Dublin. Small pets can fly in the cabin on the transatlantic leg; larger dogs travel as cargo. If the Madrid connection doesn't fit the travel dates, Aer Lingus covers the Dublin end from a different European hub, though it's cargo only with no cabin option.
