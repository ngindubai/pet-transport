  sections:
    - heading: "FAVN titre test and the 90-day wait"
      body: |
        Ireland applies EU pet travel rules in full. The Dominican Republic is classified as an unlisted third country under EU Implementing Regulation 2026/636, making the FAVN titre test mandatory. Blood must be drawn at least 30 days after the most recent rabies vaccination. The result must show a neutralising antibody level of at least 0.5 IU/ml at an EU-approved laboratory.

        The 90-day waiting period runs from the blood draw date, not the result date. DAFM states clearly that no exemption to this wait is granted. Once the 90 days have elapsed, the DIGEGA health certificate can be issued and is valid for 10 days from the endorsement date to the DAFM checks on arrival. Keep the original laboratory report as a supporting document throughout the journey.

    - heading: "Tapeworm treatment, advance notification, and entry airports"
      body: |
        All dogs entering Ireland must receive treatment against Echinococcus multilocularis using praziquantel. The treatment must be administered not less than 24 hours and not more than 5 days before arrival. The treating vet must record the product name, dose, date, and time of treatment in the health certificate. Cats are not subject to this requirement.

        In addition to the tapeworm treatment, DAFM requires 24-hour advance notification before non-EU pets arrive at an Irish Border Control Post. Use the DAFM online notification system or contact the BCP at Dublin Airport directly. Late or missing notification can delay clearance or result in your pet being held.

        Dublin Airport (DUB) is Ireland's primary BCP for non-EU pet arrivals by air. Shannon (SNN) and Cork (ORK) are designated but have more restricted coverage hours. Confirm inspection availability at whichever airport you plan to use. No direct flight operates from Santo Domingo to Dublin; routing via Madrid (Iberia), a US gateway (Aer Lingus), or London (British Airways) are the practical options.
