  sections:
    - heading: "What EU unlisted country status means for your Armenian pet"
      body: |
        The EU classifies countries for non-commercial pet movements into listed and unlisted. Listed countries (USA, UK, Canada, Japan, Singapore, and several others) share equivalent rabies control standards with the EU and benefit from simplified entry: no titre test, just a valid health certificate and current vaccination.

        Armenia is not on the EU listed country register. Neither is Georgia, Azerbaijan, or Kazakhstan. This classification applies regardless of the individual pet's health status or vaccination record. It is about the country's national disease controls, not your pet specifically.

        For Austria, this means: microchip first (before any vaccination), then rabies vaccination, wait 30 days, FAVN titre test at an EU-approved laboratory, then a mandatory 90-day wait from the blood draw date. Only after those 90 days have elapsed can your pet travel to Vienna. The regulation is EU Regulation (EU) 2020/692, and it applies uniformly across all 27 EU member states.

    - heading: "Getting the FAVN titre test done from Yerevan"
      body: |
        The FAVN test is the step that most people underestimate. EU-approved laboratories for the FAVN test are mostly in Western Europe or North America. From Armenia, this means your vet draws the blood in Yerevan, packs it correctly (typically with sodium azide preservative), and ships it via international courier to the approved lab. Results take 2-4 weeks from sample arrival.

        Ask your vet directly whether they have sent samples to EU-approved FAVN laboratories before. If the blood is packed or shipped incorrectly, the sample degrades and you lose 4-6 weeks while waiting for a retest. The AGES (Austria's food and safety agency) maintains the EU-approved laboratory list, and a current version is on the European Commission's food safety portal.

        If your vet is not familiar with international titre test shipping, contact an IPATA-registered agent in Armenia or the nearest country. They coordinate this regularly and can prevent the most common errors.
