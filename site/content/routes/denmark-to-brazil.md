  h1: "Pet Transport Denmark to Brazil: DVFA Certificate and the VIGIAGRO Arrival Process"
  overview: |
    Moving a pet from Denmark to Brazil is relatively straightforward compared to many other international routes. A DVFA-issued official health certificate, a current rabies vaccination, and a microchip are the core requirements. VIGIAGRO (Brazil's agricultural inspection authority under MAPA) inspects pets on arrival at São Paulo or Rio de Janeiro, but there is no routine quarantine for compliant pets. KLM via Amsterdam and Air France via Paris are the most practical cargo routing options from Copenhagen.

    - heading: "VIGIAGRO arrival process in Brazil"
      body: |
        VIGIAGRO is the international agricultural inspection service of Brazil's Ministry of Agriculture, Livestock and Food Supply (MAPA). Pets arriving from Denmark are inspected at the border control point at São Paulo Guarulhos (GRU) or Rio de Janeiro Galeão (GIG).

        The inspection verifies the health certificate, microchip, and vaccination record. For compliant pets from EU member states, the process is typically a documentary check rather than a quarantine hold. Documentation must be complete and the health certificate must be issued by a DVFA-authorised official vet in Denmark.

        Contact MAPA/VIGIAGRO (gov.br/mapa) for the current health certificate format and entry requirements for EU-origin pets.
