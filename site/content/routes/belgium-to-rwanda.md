  sections:
    - heading: "Brussels Airlines BRU to KGL: Belgium's routing advantage"
      body: |
        Brussels Airlines operates direct services from Brussels Zaventem (BRU) to Kigali International Airport (KGL) as part of its established East Africa network. For Belgian pet owners, this is the standout routing option: a single-carrier, direct flight of approximately 8-9 hours, avoiding any hub transfer or re-handling at a connecting airport.

        Contact Brussels Airlines Cargo at BRU to confirm live animal acceptance on the BRU-KGL service, crate size limits, and any seasonal conditions. Brussels Zaventem is a major cargo hub and Brussels Airlines has long-standing experience on East African routes.

        If the Brussels Airlines service is not available for your travel dates or pet size, Ethiopian Airlines via Addis Ababa (ADD) is the established alternative. Ethiopian Cargo's ADD-KGL connection is a short East African hop, and Ethiopian has the widest live animal freight programme in Africa.

    - heading: "RAB import permit: applying from Belgium"
      body: |
        Rwanda's Agriculture and Animal Resources Development Board (RAB) at rab.gov.rw manages companion animal imports. Apply at least 8-10 weeks before travel, providing your pet's ISO microchip number, species, breed, vaccination history, and intended travel date.

        The RAB permit process is generally straightforward, but apply early and follow up after submission to confirm your application is progressing. You present the RAB permit alongside the FAVV/AFSCA health certificate at the RAB inspection desk at Kigali International Airport on arrival.

        RwandAir has been expanding its European network and may offer cargo connections from Brussels or nearby hubs. If RwandAir Cargo accepts live animals on accessible routes, compare routing and costs with Brussels Airlines and Ethiopian.

    - heading: "FAVV/AFSCA health certificate for non-EU destinations"
      body: |
        The Belgian EU pet passport applies to intra-EU travel only. For Rwanda, the correct document is an official health certificate from a FAVV/AFSCA-authorised official veterinarian, endorsed by FAVV (the Federaal Agentschap voor de Veiligheid van de Voedselketen / Agence fédérale pour la sécurité de la chaîne alimentaire).

        Book a pre-export veterinary examination with a FAVV-authorised official vet. They verify the ISO microchip, vaccination records, and health status, then issue the certificate. FAVV endorses it. The certificate must be issued within 10 days of travel, so time the appointment and endorsement carefully within that window.

        Allow 2-3 working days for FAVV endorsement after the vet appointment. BRU has well-established cargo operations and veterinary export processes are handled routinely at Brussels.
