  sections:
    - heading: "Portugal's EU import rules for Georgian pets"
      body: |
        Portugal applies EU Regulation 576/2013 for pets from unlisted third countries. Georgia is unlisted. The mandatory steps are: ISO microchip before vaccination, rabies vaccination, FAVN titre test at an EU-approved laboratory (minimum 0.5 IU/ml, blood drawn 30+ days after vaccination), 90-day wait from blood draw date, and NFSA-endorsed EU health certificate valid 10 days.

        DGAV (dgav.pt) inspects incoming pets at Lisbon Airport (LIS) cargo terminal. Compliant pets are released without quarantine.

    - heading: "Titre test from Georgia: a practical advantage"
      body: |
        Compared with some Caucasus origins, Georgia may have EU-approved FAVN titre test laboratories accessible locally or in a nearby country. If your vet in Tbilisi can process the blood sample at an EU-approved lab without international shipping, this simplifies both logistics and cost.

        Confirm this with your vet before drawing blood. If an approved lab is accessible in Georgia or in the region, the titre test result is often returned more quickly, and the 90-day wait clock starts earlier.

    - heading: "Cargo routing from Tbilisi to Lisbon"
      body: |
        Turkish Airlines via Istanbul is the most direct cargo option from Tbilisi (TBS) to Lisbon (LIS). Daily flights connect TBS to Istanbul, with Turkish Airlines Cargo onwards to LIS. The journey is typically 7 to 10 hours total.

        Emirates via Dubai is an alternative, though it extends the journey considerably. Both carriers apply temperature embargoes during summer months.

        DGAV inspection at Lisbon Airport cargo terminal is straightforward. Ensure your NFSA-endorsed health certificate is complete and the microchip, vaccination, and titre test records are all in order before the pet leaves Tbilisi.
