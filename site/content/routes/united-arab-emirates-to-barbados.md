  h1: "Pet Transport from the United Arab Emirates to Barbados"
  overview: |
    The UAE sits inside Barbados's Category 1 group, its low-rabies-risk tier that also covers the UK, the US and most of the EU. That single classification means a UAE-based pet skips the FAVN blood test altogether and only needs the standard 35-day wait after vaccination, the same rule that applies to a pet moving from London.

    Getting there is the harder part. No airline connects Dubai or Abu Dhabi to Barbados directly, and Barbados's own rules add a twist: the final leg into Bridgetown has to be the specific non-stop London flight, whichever Gulf carrier handled the first stage of the journey.

  sections:
    - heading: "Why the UAE's Category 1 status simplifies the medical side"
      body: |
        Barbados splits the world into two tiers for pet import purposes, and the UAE, alongside roughly 70 other countries and territories, sits in the lower-risk Category 1 group. That status removes the rabies titre test from the process entirely; a UAE vet vaccinates the pet, and Barbados asks for a 35-day wait from that vaccination date, not a blood sample and a 90-day hold.

        A booster given on schedule, without a lapse, doesn't reset that 35-day clock at all. It only applies fresh to a first vaccination or to a booster given late after an expired one, which is worth checking against the pet's own vaccination history before assuming the wait applies in full.

    - heading: "MOCCAE's export process and the order it has to happen in"
      body: |
        Leaving the UAE means an online application through MOCCAE's e-services portal, using a UAE Pass login, followed by a physical inspection at an approved clinic at Dubai or Abu Dhabi airport. The fee is modest, AED 100 for up to 2 pets, and the certificate is valid for 30 days once issued.

        There's a sequencing catch. MOCCAE's application asks for a copy of the destination country's import permit to be attached, so Barbados's own Import Permit needs to already exist before the UAE certificate can be finalised. Apply for the two in the wrong order and the MOCCAE paperwork has to be redone.

    - heading: "The routing rule that decides which flights are usable"
      body: |
        No direct service links the UAE to Barbados, so a connection is unavoidable. Etihad Cargo out of Abu Dhabi, Emirates SkyCargo out of Dubai, or Qatar Airways Cargo via Doha can all carry the first leg to London.

        What can't change is the last leg. Barbados's own import condition requires an air-arriving pet to land on a non-stop flight from London, on a weekday, which in practice means the specific British Airways service into Grantley Adams. Whatever carrier handles the Gulf-to-London stage, the final sector has to be that one flight, arranged and timed to fit its schedule rather than the other way round.
