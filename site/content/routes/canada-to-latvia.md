        KLM is the most convenient carrier for the Canada-to-Latvia corridor. From Toronto Pearson (YYZ), KLM operates a direct flight to Amsterdam Schiphol (AMS). From AMS, KLM's direct service to Riga International Airport (RIX) completes the journey. With both legs on KLM, the cargo booking is through one carrier, the live animal handling protocols are shared, and there is no interline cargo transfer.

      body: |
        The sequence for the Canada-to-Latvia move: your licensed Canadian vet completes the EU AHC form (using the current EU template available via the CFIA instructions page). You submit the signed AHC to your nearest CFIA office for endorsement. The CFIA reviews and stamps the document, typically within 1-4 business days depending on location and season. You then have the endorsed AHC and must use it to enter Latvia within 10 days of the signing date.

        The CFIA's instructions for the EU non-commercial AHC are detailed and include country-specific guidance. Read them at inspection.canada.ca/en/animal-health/terrestrial-animals/exports/pets/eu-non-commercial before your vet appointment. Your vet needs to use the current EU template, not an older version.

faqs:
  - question: "Does Canada being a listed third country mean my pet does not need a titre test for Latvia?"
    answer: "Correct. Canada is on the EU's list of third countries for which the rabies titre test is waived for entry into EU member states. Latvia applies the same EU rules as all other member states. A current vaccination (at least 21 days old) and the CFIA-endorsed EU AHC are the requirements."
  - question: "Who endorses the EU AHC in Canada?"
    answer: "The CFIA (Canadian Food Inspection Agency) endorses the EU AHC. After a licensed Canadian vet completes and signs the form, you submit it to a CFIA office. The CFIA endorsement confirms the vet's credentials and the document's authenticity. Contact your nearest CFIA office or submit via the CFIA online system. Instructions are at inspection.canada.ca."
  - question: "What crate sizes fit on the Amsterdam-to-Riga leg?"
    answer: "The AMS-to-RIX sector is operated on medium or regional aircraft depending on the schedule. IATA crates up to size 500 (approximately 61 x 46 x 43 cm) typically fit. Sizes 700 and above may not. Contact KLM Cargo before booking to confirm the maximum crate dimensions for your specific flight."
  - question: "Is there a direct flight from Canada to Riga?"
    answer: "No direct Canada-to-Riga (RIX) cargo service exists. A connection via Amsterdam (AMS) is the most common routing, operated by KLM. Alternative connections are via Frankfurt (FRA) or London (LHR). The total journey from Toronto to Riga via Amsterdam is approximately 12-15 hours."
