  h1: "Pet Transport from Tajikistan to United States"
  overview: |
    A cat and a dog leaving Dushanbe for the same US city face almost entirely different paperwork. That's the fact worth knowing before anything else on this route.

    Tajikistan is on the CDC's high-risk list for dog rabies, last updated 15 April 2026, so dogs need either a valid rabies serology titre or a 28-day quarantine booking at a CDC-registered facility on arrival. Cats need neither. APHIS has no federal health requirement for pet cats at all, regardless of where they're flying from.

    There's no direct flight either way. Routing runs through Istanbul on Turkish Airlines or through Dubai via Somon Air and Emirates. Expect 22 to 26 weeks from a standing start if you're moving a dog, driven mostly by the titre test timeline rather than the flight itself.
  sections:
    - heading: "The dog process, step by step"
      body: |
        Microchip first, then vaccinate, in that order. Get that sequence wrong and the vaccination doesn't count for CDC purposes.

        The titre blood draw has to happen at least 30 days after vaccination and at least 28 days before your dog enters the US. Miss that second window and you're looking at the 28-day quarantine option instead, at a CDC-registered facility, entirely at your expense.

        The Certification of Foreign Rabies Vaccination and Microchip form needs a government vet's endorsement in Tajikistan, which in practice means the State Veterinary Service. It's single-use and expires 30 days after signature, so don't get it done too early.
    - heading: "Why cats have it easier"
      body: |
        There's no equivalent process for cats. No CDC form, no titre test, no facility booking, no federal quarantine. State-level rules occasionally add something, so check the destination state's agriculture department, but at the federal level a cat from Tajikistan enters the US with none of the machinery that applies to dogs.

        This asymmetry catches people off guard when they're moving a dog and a cat together and expect the same checklist for both.
