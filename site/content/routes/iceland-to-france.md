content:
  h1: "Pet Transport from Iceland to France"
  overview: |
    An Icelandic-registered cat with a valid EU pet passport can, in principle, land at Paris CDG and walk through ordinary customs. No titre test. No quarantine. Nothing that a Belgian or Portuguese cat would not also face. Iceland's EEA status means outbound moves into any EU member state, France included, run on standard intra-EEA terms.

    The one thing that does not simplify is France's own dog category system, which applies regardless of where the dog started its journey. If you own a Category 2 type such as a registered American Staffordshire Terrier or Rottweiler, that paperwork sits alongside the Iceland-side steps, not instead of them.

  sections:
    - heading: "The easy half of this route: no titre test"
      body: |
        Because Iceland participates in the EU pet travel framework as an EEA state, a pet leaving Iceland for France needs a microchip, a current rabies vaccination with the standard 21-day wait, and a certificate on the EU form, endorsed by MAST. That's it on the regulatory side. No FAVN or ELISA titre test, no fixed waiting period beyond the 21 days, and no quarantine once the pet lands in France.

        This is the mirror image of the France-to-Iceland direction on this site, where the same pet moving the other way faces a mandatory 14-day isolation period. The asymmetry comes down to biosecurity: Iceland guards its own borders far more tightly than the EU guards its.

    - heading: "France's category system does not care where the dog came from"
      body: |
        France classifies certain dog types under Category 1 (banned from import outright) and Category 2 (permitted but restricted). An unregistered Pit Bull type or Boerboel cannot enter France under any circumstances, Icelandic origin or otherwise. A registered American Staffordshire Terrier or Rottweiler can be imported as Category 2, but the owner needs a permis de detention, civil liability insurance, and must keep the dog muzzled and on a lead in public.

        This has nothing to do with Iceland's own import rules and everything to do with French domestic legislation. If your dog might fall under either category, start the French paperwork well before booking a flight, since the permis de detention process takes its own time separate from anything MAST requires on the Iceland side.

    - heading: "Flying out of a country with no direct Icelandair hold service"
      body: |
        Icelandair flies Keflavik to Paris CDG nonstop, roughly 8 times a week. Since 1 November 2024, that flight no longer carries pets as checked or excess baggage, meaning the pet moves as manifested air cargo through Icelandair Cargo rather than through the ordinary ticketing desk.

        An alternative worth pricing is routing through Frankfurt: Lufthansa flies Keflavik to Frankfurt direct and operates its own Frankfurt Animal Lounge, a purpose-built live animal facility, before a connecting leg into France. It adds a transfer, but it may suit your dates better than waiting on Icelandair Cargo space.

faqs:
  - question: "Does my Icelandic pet need a titre test to enter France?"
    answer: "No. Iceland is treated as an EEA territory under the EU pet travel framework, so a pet moving from Iceland to France needs only a microchip, a current rabies vaccination with a 21-day wait, and a MAST-endorsed certificate on the EU form. No titre test applies."
  - question: "Can I bring a Category 2 dog from Iceland into France?"
    answer: "Yes, but with conditions. Registered American Staffordshire Terriers, Rottweilers and registered Tosa types are Category 2 under French law and can be imported, provided the owner holds a permis de detention and civil liability insurance and keeps the dog muzzled and leashed in public. Unregistered Pit Bull, Boerboel and Tosa types fall under the banned Category 1 and cannot be imported regardless of origin."
  - question: "Can I still fly my pet with Icelandair from Iceland to France?"
    answer: "Not as checked baggage. Icelandair stopped accepting pets in the passenger hold from 1 November 2024. The pet now travels as manifested cargo through Icelandair Cargo, booked separately from a normal ticket. Routing via Frankfurt on Lufthansa, which operates its own live animal facility, is a practical alternative if Icelandair Cargo does not fit your dates."
  - question: "Is there a quarantine for pets entering France from Iceland?"
    answer: "No. There is no quarantine for a compliant pet entering France from Iceland. France only applies penalty quarantine when documentation is missing or incorrect, and this is unrelated to the strict quarantine Iceland itself imposes on incoming pets."
