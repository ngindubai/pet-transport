  h1: "Pet Transport from United Arab Emirates to Venezuela"
  overview: |
    Whether a MOCCAE stamp or an ADAFSA one clears your pet's exit from the UAE depends on which emirate it's registered in, not which airport the flight leaves from. That split alone catches out owners who assume the two systems are interchangeable.

    Once past that step, the Venezuelan side is one of the lighter imports this site covers: no titre test, just a current rabies certificate and standard paperwork. The complication is entirely on the flight, since Venezuela's main airport has been shut since the June 2026 earthquake and every route in currently goes via Madrid, Bogota and Valencia rather than a direct Gulf to Caracas service that never existed anyway.

  sections:
    - heading: "MOCCAE or ADAFSA: it comes down to registration, not geography"
      body: |
        Dubai and Abu Dhabi run genuinely separate systems. A pet registered with Dubai Municipality needs its export permit through MOCCAE, the federal ministry. A pet registered in Abu Dhabi goes through ADAFSA's own online portal instead, typically clearing in 2 to 5 working days. Owners who've moved between emirates within the UAE sometimes assume the airport of departure decides which applies. It doesn't. The registration record does.

        Get this wrong and the wrong authority's paperwork simply won't be accepted at check-in, since airlines and INSAI both expect government-issued documents matched to where the pet is actually registered.

    - heading: "A light Venezuelan import, held up by an airport still recovering"
      body: |
        INSAI asks very little of a pet coming from the UAE: a current rabies certificate, an international health certificate, and the standard vaccination and deworming paperwork, no titre test. On paper, this is a fast process.

        What isn't fast, right now, is the flight itself. There has never been a direct or single-connection service between the Gulf and Venezuela, so the route has always run through Madrid and Bogota. What's changed since 24 June 2026 is the final leg, which now lands at Valencia rather than Maiquetia, on Avianca's contingency schedule. Confirm that schedule close to your travel date rather than booking it months out and assuming it holds.
