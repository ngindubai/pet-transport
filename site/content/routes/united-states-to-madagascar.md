content:
  h1: "Pet Transport from the United States to Madagascar"
  overview: |
    Madagascar sits on the CDC's own high-risk list for dog rabies. That fact drives a long, facility-restricted process for any Madagascar-origin dog trying to enter the US. It changes nothing for a US pet travelling the other way, because that direction runs entirely through Madagascar's own rules, administered by the Direction des Services Veterinaires (DSV), and DSV doesn't ask for a titre test from any country.

    What DSV does ask for is a letter, not a form: full owner and animal details, a copy of the vaccination record, and the flight and arrival information, sent well before travel. A small fee, 30,000 Ariary per pet, gets paid on landing at Ivato International Airport's Livestock Fund counter.

    Allow twelve to sixteen weeks. The letter-based permit process, which has no published turnaround, is what stretches the timeline, not the USDA paperwork.

  sections:
    - heading: "One high-risk list, two very different directions"
      body: |
        It's a natural assumption that a country's CDC high-risk rabies classification would apply symmetrically. It doesn't. The CDC's list, and everything that flows from it, a titre test or 28-day quarantine, a facility reservation, an arrival restricted to one of six US airports, governs entry into the United States. A US pet entering Madagascar is subject to Malagasy rules instead, through DSV, and those rules don't include a titre test for any origin country, US included.

        That's a genuinely useful distinction to hold onto, because it means the US-to-Madagascar direction is, in practical terms, considerably less document-heavy than the return trip for a dog.

    - heading: "DSV's letter-based permit process"
      body: |
        Rather than an online application, Madagascar's import permit runs through a letter of request addressed to the Directeur des Services Veterinaires, covering the owner's details, the animal's name, breed, age and sex, a photocopy of the vaccination record, country of origin, arrival date, transport mode and disembarkation airport.

        There's no published processing time, and guidance consistently describes it as taking several weeks. Send the letter twelve to fourteen weeks before the planned travel date, and confirm the current DSV contact point directly rather than relying on an address found in an older source.

    - heading: "Reaching Antananarivo from a US airport"
      body: |
        Air France offers the most comfortable option for a small pet: it connects from major US gateways through Paris Charles de Gaulle, and pets under 8kg including their carrier can fly in the cabin on the Paris-Antananarivo sector specifically. Kenya Airways, through Nairobi, and Ethiopian Airlines, through Addis Ababa, are the two established cargo-only alternatives for larger pets or when Air France's schedule doesn't fit.

        None of these is a true single ticket door to door, so build in time at the connection and confirm through-checking of the live animal booking with whichever carrier is chosen.
