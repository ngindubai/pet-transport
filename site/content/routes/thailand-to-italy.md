  sections:
    - heading: "The DLD email application: how to approach it"
      body: |
        Thailand's Department of Livestock Development does not have a centralised online portal for export permits. You email the local DLD district office with the pet's health certificate, microchip record, and vaccination history. The DLD processes the application and confirms the permit, which is presented to the DLD officer on departure day at Suvarnabhumi.

        Allow at least 2 weeks for a response. The DLD inspection at BKK involves a physical check of the animal and a review of all documents. Arrive early at the cargo terminal on the day of departure.
    - heading: "Italy entry at FCO or MXP"
      body: |
        International pet cargo to Italy enters at approved border inspection posts, typically Rome Fiumicino (FCO) or Milan Malpensa (MXP). Your cargo agent pre-notifies the arrival through EU TRACES, and an Italian Ministry of Health veterinary inspector checks the AHC and microchip on arrival.

        For compliant pets from Thailand, no quarantine applies. The EU entry inspection confirms the documentation is in order, and the pet is released to the owner or cargo agent. Italy has no additional requirements beyond the standard EU AHC protocol for unlisted country arrivals.

faqs:
  - question: "Does Italy require any additional documentation beyond the EU AHC for pets from Thailand?"
    answer: "No additional documentation is required beyond the standard EU AHC (Animal Health Certificate) issued within 10 days before arrival. Italy follows EU Regulation 2016/429 without additional national requirements for pets from unlisted third countries like Thailand. The EU AHC, combined with the DLD export health certificate, constitutes the full document set."
  - question: "How long is the cargo flight from Bangkok to Rome?"
    answer: "With one stop (Doha, Dubai, or Frankfurt), the total journey time is typically 12 to 16 hours. Qatar Airways via Doha and Emirates via Dubai are common options with transit times of around 2 to 4 hours. For pets in the hold, the temperature and ventilation of the crate matter more than the total time, but a shorter total duration is always preferable."
  - question: "Can the FAVN test blood draw be done in Thailand?"
    answer: "Yes. Your vet in Thailand draws the blood sample, which is sent to an EU-approved laboratory for analysis. Check the European Commission's current designated laboratory list to identify which laboratories accept samples from Thailand. Commonly, samples go to laboratories in the USA or Europe. Your Thai vet or pet transport agent will know the current approved options."
  - question: "Is an IATA crate required for this route?"
    answer: "Yes. All airlines on the Bangkok to Italy cargo corridor require pets to be transported in IATA-compliant crates (IATA Container Requirement 82 for live animals). The crate must have correct ventilation, a solid floor, secure door fittings, and meet size requirements for the aircraft. Contact your chosen airline for their specific crate dimension and weight limits before purchasing the crate."
