  sections:
    - heading: "Two authorities, one certificate"
      body: |
        AGES, the Austrian Agency for Health and Food Safety, is the body that actually checks the titre test result, the microchip number and the vaccination record against your MAAIF-endorsed certificate. The BMSGPK sits at a policy level above AGES and sets the framework AGES operates under.

        In practice this rarely causes a problem for a well-prepared file. It matters mainly because some owners, expecting a single point of contact the way MAAIF functions in Uganda, get confused about who to call with a query. For a standard compliant arrival, AGES is the body actually doing the checking at the Vienna end.

    - heading: "Routing options out of Entebbe"
      body: |
        Turkish Airlines' non-stop Entebbe-Istanbul service is usually the quickest way to start this journey, with Istanbul's dense network reaching Vienna reliably. Ethiopian Airlines via Addis Ababa is the deeper-network alternative, drawing on Ethiopian's long history moving live animal cargo across the continent. Qatar Airways via Doha rounds out the options, though Gulf summer heat can restrict live animal bookings between roughly May and September, so check current embargo dates if travelling in that window.
