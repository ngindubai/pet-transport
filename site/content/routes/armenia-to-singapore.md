content:
  h1: "Pet Transport from Armenia to Singapore"
  overview: |
    A titre test blood sample from Yerevan cannot simply travel to the nearest approved lab. For the UK route, Georgia or Turkey will do. For Singapore's AVS, neither counts, because neither sits on Schedule I or II. That single difference adds real days to an already long clock.

    Armenia is not named on either of AVS's shorter schedules, so it defaults to Schedule III: a WOAH-prescribed titre test, a fixed 90-day wait from the blood draw, and 30 days at Singapore's Animal Quarantine Centre on arrival. Confirmed directly against avs.nparks.gov.sg, fetched 21 July 2026. Budget close to five months.

  sections:
    - heading: "Why the nearby labs that work for the UK route don't work here"
      body: |
        Owners moving a pet from Armenia to the UK often ship the titre test blood sample to a laboratory in Georgia or Turkey, since both are geographically close and accept international submissions. Neither option transfers to the Singapore route.

        AVS only accepts a WOAH reference laboratory or one located in a country named on its own Schedule I or Schedule II list. Georgia and Turkey are on neither. In practice this means the sample from Yerevan needs to go further afield, commonly to Germany or another EU country carrying Schedule II status, adding shipping time on top of the standard laboratory turnaround.

    - heading: "Schedule III: the same 90-day clock, a longer sample journey"
      body: |
        Blood for the WOAH titre test cannot be drawn until at least 28 days after the rabies vaccination, and the result only counts for export between 90 and 365 days after that draw. That part of the process is identical across every Schedule III origin on this site.

        What differs for Armenia is getting the sample to a lab that actually counts. Run the AVS import licence application, the AVS-recognised agent appointment, and the AQC quarantine booking during the 90-day wait rather than after it, since all three can proceed in parallel with the titre clock.

    - heading: "Routing from Yerevan: two workable single-connection options"
      body: |
        Turkish Airlines and Singapore Airlines both fly Istanbul to Singapore nonstop, roughly 17 times a week combined as of mid-2026, a genuine ten-hour-plus sector rather than a patchwork of short legs. Paired with Turkish Airlines' own Yerevan-Istanbul service, that gives a clean two-leg routing.

        Emirates via Dubai is the alternative, useful if Istanbul dates don't line up. Dubai-Yerevan is a well-served sector in its own right, with roughly 56 flights a week across all carriers combined.
