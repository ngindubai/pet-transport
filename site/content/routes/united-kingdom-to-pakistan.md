  overview: "Shipping a pet from the UK to Pakistan requires more lead time than many routes.   sections:
    - heading: "Getting the import permit and understanding quarantine"
      body: |
        Pakistan's Animal Quarantine Department, sitting within the Ministry of National Food Security and Research, issues the import permit your pet needs before travel. Applications typically take 4-6 weeks to process. The Department will want to see your pet's microchip details, vaccination history, and health certificate particulars. Do not purchase a flight ticket until the permit is confirmed in writing.

        On arrival in Pakistan, all imported pets go through a quarantine period of 7-14 days at a designated facility. The exact duration depends on how the Animal Quarantine Department classifies the UK at the time of your pet's arrival, and whether all documentation is in order. The owner is responsible for quarantine costs throughout this period, so factor this into your relocation budget.

    - heading: "Documentation and the titre test question"
      body: |
        The UK holds good rabies-free status with many countries, but Pakistan's requirements are stricter and the regulations do evolve. Before committing to a travel date, contact the Animal Quarantine Department directly to check whether pets arriving from the UK are currently required to have a rabies antibody titre test. The data recommends carrying titre test documentation as a precaution regardless of the current ruling, as requirements can change between when you start planning and when you travel.

        Your APHA Export Health Certificate must be issued within 10 days of travel, so time the vet appointment carefully. APHA endorsement typically takes 1-5 working days depending on your region. Book the APHA-authorised vet appointment as soon as your travel date is confirmed.
