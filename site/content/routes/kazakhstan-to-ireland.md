content:
  h1: "Pet Transport Kazakhstan to Ireland: 2026 Guide"
  overview: "Kazakhstan to Ireland is one of the longer international pet relocation routes, both in geographic distance and preparation time. Kazakhstan is EU unlisted, so the FAVN titre test and 90-day wait apply. On top of the standard EU unlisted requirements, Ireland requires TRACES pre-notification through DAFM before the pet arrives at Dublin Airport. The Ministry of Agriculture in Kazakhstan issues the health certificate. Plan for 5-6 months from start to arrival. Cargo from Almaty or Astana routes via Istanbul, Frankfurt, or other European hubs to Dublin, with total journey times of 12-18 hours."

  sections:
    - heading: "Why Kazakhstan-to-Ireland takes more planning than shorter routes"
      body: |
        The preparation timeline for this route is the same as for any EU destination from Kazakhstan: roughly 5-6 months. But the cargo journey itself is longer and more complex than a Caucasus-to-nearby-EU move. Almaty to Dublin involves multiple carrier segments across a significant distance, and each segment needs confirmation of live animal acceptance.

        The most established routing is via Istanbul with Turkish Airlines Cargo. Almaty International Airport (ALA) has Turkish Airlines services to IST, and from IST there are regular services to Dublin. The IST connection point is important: Turkish Airlines Cargo at Istanbul has live animal handling infrastructure and experience with Central Asian pet moves.

        For summer travel, summer heat restrictions are a real consideration. Both Central Asian and Middle Eastern hub airports can impose cargo embargoes during extreme heat. If your timeline falls in July or August, work with your agent to either route via a cooler hub (Frankfurt or Amsterdam) or plan travel in the early hours of the day when temperatures are lower.

    - heading: "Coordinating the TRACES notification from Kazakhstan"
      body: |
        TRACES is the EU's pre-arrival notification system for animal imports. For Ireland, this notification must be in the system before the pet travels. From Kazakhstan, this typically means either your transport agent in Kazakhstan has EU TRACES access (some do), or your agent in Ireland handles the notification on your behalf.

        The TRACES notification must include your pet's chip number, the health certificate details, the titre test result, and the expected arrival time and flight at Dublin Airport. Submit it with enough lead time to allow DAFM to process it before the flight. 48 hours before departure is a comfortable minimum; 24 hours is the stated minimum.

        If you do not have an Irish agent, setting up TRACES access as a private individual is possible but has a setup time. Do this during the 90-day wait, not in the final days before travel.

faqs:
  - question: "How does the cargo from Kazakhstan clear customs in Ireland given the long journey?"
    answer: "Live animal cargo arriving at Dublin Airport from Kazakhstan (via connecting carriers) is cleared at the Dublin Airport border inspection post by DAFM border vets. The cargo manifest will list the animal as a live cargo shipment. The connecting carriers manage the animal transfer between segments. Your TRACES pre-notification and physical documents (health certificate, titre test, vaccination record) are checked by the DAFM vet on arrival in Dublin. If all documents are in order and the TRACES record matches, clearance is granted and you can collect your pet."
  - question: "Can I use a Russian carrier to transit through Moscow on this route?"
    answer: "Technically possible in terms of transit, but routing via Russia adds complexity including Russian transit documentation requirements and potential geopolitical complications affecting cargo flights. Routing via Istanbul, Frankfurt, or Amsterdam is more reliable and avoids these complications. Most pet transport agents handling Kazakhstan-to-Ireland moves avoid Russian routing."
  - question: "What is the maximum time a titre test result remains valid after the 90-day wait?"
    answer: "Under EU Regulation 2020/692, a titre test result from an EU-approved laboratory at 0.5 IU/ml or above remains valid as long as the rabies vaccination that preceded it remains in its valid period. Most rabies vaccines are valid for 1-3 years depending on the product. As long as you keep the vaccination current with boosters, the same titre test result continues to apply for subsequent trips. For a first move from Kazakhstan to Ireland, the titre test and 90-day wait happen once, and subsequent entries within the vaccination validity window do not require a new titre test."
