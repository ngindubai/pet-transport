content:
  h1: "Pet Transport from Australia to Namibia"
  overview: |
    Qantas flies the only direct long-haul sector this route depends on, Johannesburg to Perth, and it runs three times a week rather than daily. That single scheduling fact shapes the booking timeline more than any regulation on either end.

    The regulatory side is comparatively forgiving. Namibia doesn't require a rabies titre test from any country of origin, Australia included, just a vaccination dated between 30 days and 12 months before arrival. DAFF's own export process on the Australian side is the more bureaucratic half of this route. Realistically, budget 8 to 12 weeks.

  sections:
    - heading: "A three-times-weekly flight, not a daily one"
      body: |
        Most Australia-outbound routes on this site treat the long-haul leg as a scheduling afterthought, something to slot in once the paperwork clears. Johannesburg-Perth doesn't work that way. Qantas runs the sector three times a week, and it's the only direct link between the two countries relevant to this journey. Miss the window on your intended travel date, and the next available slot could be several days out, not the next morning.

        Owners flying from Sydney or Melbourne need a domestic connection to Perth first, adding a step most routes through Johannesburg via other Australian gateways don't require. Book the whole itinerary as one sequence rather than the cargo leg alone.

    - heading: "DAFF's export process: the slower half of this route"
      body: |
        Australia treats a pet leaving the country as a biosecurity matter, not a formality. A DAFF-accredited vet has to examine the animal and confirm it's fit to travel before the export permit is issued, and the permit itself has to be built around Namibia's specific import conditions.

        Start this process eight to ten weeks out. Pets that don't pass the fitness check at the pre-export examination have the whole export delayed, so timing the vet visit with some slack built in is worth more than trying to compress the schedule.

    - heading: "What Namibia doesn't ask for"
      body: |
        Namibia skips the rabies titre test entirely, for pets arriving from Australia or anywhere else. That's a genuine time saver compared with routes into countries that demand a titre result and a multi-month wait. What Namibia does insist on is precision: the vaccination has to sit inside a 30-day-to-12-month window on arrival, not before it and not after.

        The Namibian import permit itself, N$150 and applied for through the Directorate of Veterinary Services, has no published processing time. Given the Qantas schedule constraint above, starting this application early is less about bureaucratic caution and more about making sure the permit is ready before a viable flight even comes up.
