content:
  h1: "Pet Transport from Serbia to Canada"
  overview: |
    Two North American regulators look at the same Serbian rabies vaccination and reach different conclusions. The US CDC doesn't treat Serbia as a high-risk country for dog rabies. The Canadian Food Inspection Agency does. That single classification difference is why a dog moving from Belgrade to Toronto faces a CFIA import permit and designated-airport entry that a Belgrade-to-New-York move simply doesn't.

    The flight side changed only recently. Air Serbia brought back a direct Belgrade-Toronto service in May 2026, its first in 34 years, running twice a week through the end of September. It carries small pets in the cabin under the same EUR 130 fee it applies to its US route, but it has no cargo hold option anywhere in its network, so a larger dog, or a move outside that seasonal window, needs a different plan.

  sections:
    - heading: "Why CFIA treats Serbia differently to the US CDC"
      body: |
        Compare this route to Serbia-to-US, covered elsewhere on this site: the CDC's high-risk list does not include Serbia, so American-bound dogs use a simpler pathway. The CFIA's own high-risk list does include Serbia, which brings in a CFIA import permit, a minimum age of the dog to be confirmed with a Serbian vet before travel, and entry restricted to designated airports with animal inspection capacity: Toronto Pearson, Montreal Trudeau, Vancouver, Calgary or Halifax.

        The good news for personal pet owners is that CFIA's current policy does not require a titre test even for a high-risk-origin dog, just the vaccination certificate, the microchip sequence in the right order, and the import permit itself.

    - heading: "A new seasonal direct flight, with real limits"
      body: |
        Air Serbia's Toronto route is genuinely new, launched in May 2026 after a 34-year gap, and it's a welcome option for a small pet. It runs only twice weekly, Wednesdays and Saturdays, and only from late May through late September. Outside that window, or for any dog above the 8kg combined cabin weight, it simply isn't available, since Air Serbia runs no cargo hold pet service on any route it flies.

        Lufthansa Cargo via Frankfurt and Turkish Airlines Cargo via Istanbul both cover the gap, routing a larger dog, or a move planned for the off-season, into whichever CFIA-designated Canadian airport suits the traveller.

    - heading: "No titre test, but the age and airport rules still bite"
      body: |
        CFIA's current personal-pet policy doesn't ask for a blood titre even from a high-risk country, which surprises owners who've read about the process for other destinations. What it does ask for is precise: the microchip has to go in before or on the vaccination day, not after, and entry only clears through Toronto Pearson, Montreal Trudeau, Vancouver, Calgary or Halifax. A correctly timed vaccination arriving at the wrong airport is still a rejected booking.
