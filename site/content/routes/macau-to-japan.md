content:
  h1: "Pet Transport from Macau to Japan"
  overview: |
    Six places get the shortcut into Japan: Iceland, Australia, New Zealand, Fiji, Hawaii and Guam. Macau isn't one of them, so this route runs the full sequence MAFF applies to every non-designated origin. Two rabies vaccinations, 30 days apart. A titre test 30 days after the second. Then a fixed 180-day wait from the blood draw before the pet can land.

    Here's the part worth knowing before assuming the worst: none of that 180 days is spent inside a Japanese quarantine facility. Get every step right, submit the advance notification on time, and the physical hold on arrival can be a matter of hours. Get any part of the sequence wrong, and that changes fast.

  sections:
    - heading: "Why Macau doesn't get MAFF's shortcut"
      body: |
        MAFF's designated regions list is short by design: Iceland, Australia, New Zealand, Fiji, Hawaii and Guam, territories MAFF treats as carrying negligible rabies risk with a simplified entry pathway. Macau doesn't appear on it, in any source checked for this guide, so the standard non-designated-region process applies without exception.

        That means two rabies vaccinations spaced at least 30 days apart, rather than a single dose, followed by a titre blood draw at a MAFF-approved laboratory at least 30 days after the second shot. Only once that result clears 0.5 IU/mL does the 180-day clock start.

    - heading: "The 180 days is a wait, not a stay"
      body: |
        It's a genuinely common mix-up, treating Japan's rabies programme as a 180-day quarantine facility stay. It isn't, for a pet whose paperwork is in order. The 180 days runs from the blood draw date, and the pet can spend that time at home in Macau. Submit the advance notification to Japan's Animal Quarantine Service at least 40 days before your planned arrival, confirm the intended arrival port has quarantine facilities available, and a fully compliant pet can clear inspection at the airport in as little as half a day.

        The 180-day figure that does apply on arrival is reserved for pets that turn up without the correct sequence completed, a genuinely different and far more disruptive outcome. Getting the paperwork right the first time is what keeps this route to the estimate above rather than considerably longer.

    - heading: "Hong Kong, not a direct Osaka flight"
      body: |
        Air Macau's own NX856 service reaches Osaka Kansai directly, but it carries no animals at all. The workable routing for a pet is the same one this site documents for Macau's other overseas moves: a road or ferry transfer to Hong Kong International Airport, then a Cathay Pacific Cargo or JAL Cargo sector on to Japan.
