content:
  h1: "Pet Transport Uruguay to Denmark: 2026 Guide"
  overview: "Uruguay to Denmark requires the full EU unlisted country process. Uruguay is not on the EU listed country register, so the FAVN titre test (blood drawn at least 30 days after vaccination, result 0.5 IU/ml or above at an EU-approved laboratory) and a mandatory 90-day wait from the blood draw date are both compulsory before entry. Total preparation is 20-26 weeks. Denmark adds one important national rule on top of standard EU requirements: a statutory ban on 13 dog breeds, which cannot enter Denmark regardless of any paperwork. Check this before starting the process."

  sections:
    - heading: "Denmark's breed ban: check this first"
      body: |
        Denmark's Dog Act bans 13 breeds from the country. The breeds and their crosses are prohibited by law, not just policy, and no amount of documentation or prior permits changes this. The list includes: Pitbull Terrier, American Staffordshire Terrier, Tosa inu, Dogo Argentino, Bordeaux Dog (Dogue de Bordeaux), Fila Brasileiro, Kangal, Central Asian Shepherd, Caucasian Shepherd, South Russian Ovcharka, Tornjak, Sarplaninac, and Boerboel.

        If your dog is one of these breeds, or could be interpreted as such at the border, contact Foedevarestyrelsen (foedevarestyrelsen.dk) before starting any paperwork. Dogs that are refused entry at Copenhagen Airport are a serious problem to resolve. Checking breed status is a five-minute step that can prevent a very expensive outcome.

    - heading: "The EU unlisted country process for Uruguay"
      body: |
        Denmark follows EU pet movement rules administered by the Danish Veterinary, Food, Agriculture and Fisheries Agency (Foedevarestyrelsen). For pets from unlisted third countries such as Uruguay, Commission Delegated Regulation (EU) 2026/131 applies.

        The sequence: ISO 11784/11785 microchip implanted before any vaccination; rabies vaccination administered after the chip, with 21 days elapsed before travel; FAVN titre test drawn at least 30 days after the vaccination, at an EU-approved laboratory, with a result of 0.5 IU/ml or above; 90-day wait from the blood draw date; then travel to Denmark with an EU Annex IV health certificate endorsed by Uruguay's DGSG authority.

        There is no shortcut and no appeal against the 90-day wait. The minimum total preparation time from microchip to departure is approximately 20 weeks, assuming the titre test passes on the first attempt.

    - heading: "Routing from Montevideo to Copenhagen"
      body: |
        Carrasco International Airport (MVD) in Montevideo does not have direct flights to Copenhagen. The typical cargo routing is: MVD to a South American hub (GRU in Sao Paulo or EZE in Buenos Aires), then transatlantic to a European hub (FRA, AMS, CDG, or MAD), then a final leg to Copenhagen Kastrup (CPH).

        SAS Scandinavian Airlines has its main hub at CPH and accepts live animal cargo on approved routes from European connections. LATAM Airlines covers the MVD-GRU-European hub segment well. Iberia via Madrid is a practical routing for the transatlantic leg, with SAS or Iberia arranging the MAD-CPH connection. From CDG, Air France or SAS handle the CPH leg.

        Each cargo transfer requires documentation and pre-notification of live animals. Using a specialist freight forwarder with South American experience reduces the risk of booking errors on multi-leg journeys.

    - heading: "The DGSG export process from Uruguay"
      body: |
        Uruguay's MGAP-DGSG authority (Ministerio de Ganaderia, Agricultura y Pesca, mgap.gub.uy) endorses export health certificates for companion animals. A MGAP-registered veterinarian prepares the EU Annex IV format document, which must include the microchip number, vaccination details, FAVN titre test result (with blood draw date, laboratory name, and result in IU/ml), and the certifying vet's details.

        The DGSG endorses the certificate after review. Allow three to five business days for this step. The endorsed certificate is valid for 10 days from issue and must be presented at Copenhagen Airport. Do not have it issued more than 10 days before departure.

faqs:
  - question: "Is the breed ban in Denmark an airline policy or a legal prohibition?"
    answer: "It is Danish law. The 13 breeds are banned by the Danish Dog Act, not just airline policy. This means no airline, permit, or documentation can authorise entry for these breeds. If your dog is a banned breed, Denmark is not a viable destination."
  - question: "Does Denmark require tapeworm treatment for dogs from Uruguay?"
    answer: "No. Denmark does not have an Echinococcus treatment requirement for dogs entering from South America. This is different from Norway and Finland, which both require tapeworm treatment. For a direct Uruguay-to-Denmark route, no tapeworm treatment is needed."
  - question: "Can I use a titre test result from a laboratory in Uruguay for EU entry into Denmark?"
    answer: "Only if the laboratory is on the EU-approved list under Commission Decision 2000/258/EC. Most Uruguayan laboratories are not on this list. Your MGAP-registered vet will need to ship the blood sample to an EU-approved laboratory abroad (ANSES in France, AHVLA in the UK, or Kansas State University in the US are commonly used). Confirm with the laboratory that they accept samples from Uruguay before arranging the shipment."
  - question: "What documents does Foedevarestyrelsen check at Copenhagen Airport?"
    answer: "Inspectors check: the EU Annex IV health certificate (DGSG-endorsed, issued within 10 days of travel); vaccination records showing the microchip number, vaccine batch, and dates; the FAVN titre test result with the blood draw date and laboratory details; and the microchip scan. All documents must be consistent with each other."
