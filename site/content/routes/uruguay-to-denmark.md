content:
  h1: "Pet Transport from Uruguay to Denmark: 2026 Guide"
  overview: |
    Five to seven months. That is the realistic window for moving a pet from Montevideo to Copenhagen once the FAVN titre test and its 90-day wait are built into the schedule. Uruguay has no EU-equivalence listing for rabies control, so Denmark applies the same unlisted third country process it applies to any other unlisted origin: microchip, vaccination, a laboratory titre test, and a fixed wait that starts on the day blood is drawn, not the day results arrive.

    None of the individual steps are unusual by regional standards. What catches owners out is the sequencing: the vaccination has to precede the blood draw by 30 days, the blood draw has to precede entry by 90 days, and the export certificate has to be dated within 10 days of the flight. Get any one of those dates wrong and the whole plan slips by weeks.

  sections:
    - heading: "Why Uruguay triggers the full titre-test process into Denmark"
      body: |
        The EU splits non-member countries into those it has formally assessed as having equivalent rabies control and those it has not. Uruguay sits in the unassessed group, alongside most of South America, so Denmark treats a Uruguayan pet exactly as it would treat one from any other unlisted country.

        That means a FAVN or ELISA test at an EU-approved laboratory, a result of at least 0.5 IU/ml, and blood drawn no sooner than 30 days after the rabies vaccination. From the date of that draw, a 90-day clock starts. Nothing shortens it, not a fast lab turnaround, not an earlier certificate, not a direct flight.
    - heading: "Getting the sample to an EU-approved lab from Montevideo"
      body: |
        Uruguay has no EU-approved FAVN laboratory of its own. Your vet draws the blood in Montevideo, and it then has to travel, refrigerated, to an approved lab, most of which sit in Europe or North America. Ask directly whether your vet has coordinated this kind of international shipment before; a mishandled sample means restarting the 30-day wait from scratch.

        Once the export certificate is in hand and endorsed by MGAP-DGSG, cargo from Carrasco International (MVD) to Copenhagen runs through a European hub. Madrid and Amsterdam are the two established connections, and either adds a day or so to total transit time compared with a theoretical direct service that does not exist on this corridor.
