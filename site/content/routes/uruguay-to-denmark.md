  sections:
    - heading: "FAVN titre test and the 90-day wait"
      body: |
        Uruguay is classified as an unlisted third country under EU Implementing Regulation 2026/636. Fødevarestyrelsen (the Danish Veterinary and Food Administration) requires a passing FAVN titre test result before the health certificate can be issued. Blood must be drawn at least 30 days after the most recent rabies vaccination. The result must show at least 0.5 IU/ml at an EU-approved laboratory.

        The 90-day waiting period runs from the blood draw date. Once the 90 days have elapsed, the MGAP-accredited vet issues the CVI and MGAP-DGSG endorses it. The CVI is valid for 10 days from the MGAP endorsement date to the checks at the Danish Border Control Post. Allow 1-2 working days for the endorsement step after the vet appointment.

    - heading: "Danish breed restrictions and entry points"
      body: |
        Before any other preparation, confirm your dog is not one of the 13 breeds prohibited under the Danish Dog Act: pit bull terrier, Tosa Inu, American Staffordshire Terrier, Fila Brasileiro, Dogo Argentino, American Bulldog, Boerboel, Kangal, Central Asian Ovtcharka, Caucasian Ovtcharka, South Russian Ovtcharka, Tornjak, and Sarplaninac. Crossbreeds are also prohibited. This ban applies regardless of vaccination or documentation.

        Denmark designates three Border Control Posts for non-EU pet arrivals: CPH (Kastrup), BLL (Billund), and AAL (Aalborg). Copenhagen is the most consistently staffed. Iberia and LATAM offer connections from Montevideo (MVD) via Buenos Aires or direct to Madrid (MAD), from where connections reach CPH.
