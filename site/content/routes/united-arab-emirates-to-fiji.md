  overview: |
    A dog or cat leaving the UAE for Fiji still spends a minimum of 30 days in a government quarantine facility, no matter how clean the paperwork is. Fiji's own biosecurity authority places UAE-origin animals in the same risk tier it uses for continental Europe and the USA, not the shorter track reserved for Australia and New Zealand.

    Layer on top of that a titre test with a fixed window, and a flight that has to be pieced together, since nothing connects Dubai or Abu Dhabi to Nadi without at least one change of aircraft, most often through Brisbane.

  sections:
    - heading: "The quarantine tier, and why the UAE doesn't get the shorter one"
      body: |
        BAF's shortest quarantine track is reserved for a small handful of countries it treats as genuinely rabies-free. The UAE isn't among them. That places it in the same broader tier as most of Europe and North America, carrying the 30-day minimum at Koronivia rather than the 7-day stay Australian-origin pets get on the reverse route.

        Because BAF doesn't publish one definitive list mapping every country to a tier, confirm the UAE's current classification directly with BAF before locking in vaccination dates. Getting this wrong after months of prep is the costliest mistake on this route.

    - heading: "No direct flight, and a titre clock running in parallel"
      body: |
        Emirates and Fiji Airways both serve the Dubai-Nadi corridor, but only with a change of aircraft, most commonly through Brisbane. Qantas via Sydney is a workable second option. Either way, book the connection as manifest cargo through a specialist agent, not a standard interline ticket, and confirm acceptance on both legs before committing to dates.

        While that's being arranged, the titre test clock runs on its own schedule: blood drawn between 60 days and 12 months before the export date. Line the BAF import permit application up once that result is back, since the permit itself only stays valid for 6 months from issue.

    - heading: "What this route tends to cost"
      body: |
        Two figures drive most of the budget here: the connecting cargo fare through Brisbane or Sydney, and the 30-day Koronivia stay. Neither is cheap, and neither is optional on this corridor, so build both into your planning from the start rather than treating the quarantine fee as an afterthought once the flight is booked.

        A specialist agent fee sounds like an extra cost, but on a route with a connection, a titre window and a quarantine booking to coordinate, it usually pays for itself in avoided delays.

    - heading: "How this compares with Australia's shorter track"
      body: |
        It's worth knowing that not every origin gets the same 30 days. Australia and New Zealand sit in BAF's Group A tier and face just 7 days at Koronivia, because Fiji treats them as genuinely rabies-free. The UAE doesn't get that treatment, and there's no shortcut around it: the tier is set by BAF's own risk assessment, not by anything an owner can do differently with the paperwork.
