  sections:
    - heading: "New Zealand EU-listed and Netherlands no breed ban: a practical combination"
      body: |
        New Zealand appears on the EU's listed third countries schedule (EU Regulation 576/2013, Annex II). Pets from listed countries do not need the FAVN titre test or the 3-month waiting period. Source: food.ec.europa.eu, 2026.

        The Netherlands removed breed-specific legislation in 2008. There is no national breed ban. The NVWA carries out a documentary inspection at Schiphol on arrival, but no breed classification is applied. For owners of breeds restricted in Germany (Rottweiler, American Bulldog) or France (Pit Bull type), the Netherlands is the more permissive EU entry point.

    - heading: "Auckland to Amsterdam Schiphol: hub routing"
      body: |
        There is no direct cargo service from Auckland to Amsterdam. Singapore Airlines via Singapore (SIN) is the primary option, with a direct SIN-AMS sector giving a single transfer. Emirates via Dubai (DXB) provides an alternative. KLM Cargo connects to Schiphol via hub partners on both routings.

        Book 4-6 weeks ahead and confirm live animal acceptance on your specific flights. Auckland to Amsterdam is one of the longer long-haul cargo routings; a single-transfer path keeps transit time as short as possible.

    - heading: "MPI certificate: the export step from New Zealand"
      body: |
        MPI (Ministry for Primary Industries) authorises vets in New Zealand to sign EU-format export health certificates. The certificate is endorsed by MPI before departure and must be issued within 10 days of the date of arrival in the Netherlands.

        MPI endorsement takes a few working days. Book the vet appointment with enough lead time for both the examination and MPI processing to fall within the 10-day window. Confirm MPI authorisation with your vet before booking.
