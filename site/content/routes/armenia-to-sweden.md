  overview: |
    Armenia to Sweden is a five to six month process. Sweden is an EU member state and Armenia is EU-unlisted, so the full EU unlisted country protocol applies: microchip before vaccination, FAVN titre test, a mandatory 90-day wait, and an SSFS-endorsed EU health certificate. Plan around the waiting period.

  sections:
    - heading: "Sweden's import requirements for Armenian pets"
      body: |
        Sweden applies EU-wide rules for pets from unlisted third countries. Armenia is unlisted, so the requirements are: ISO microchip implanted before any vaccination, rabies vaccination, FAVN titre test at an EU-approved laboratory with a result of at least 0.5 IU/ml, a 90-day wait from the blood draw date, and an SSFS-endorsed EU health certificate valid 10 days before travel.

        Jordbruksverket (Swedish Board of Agriculture) is the competent authority for incoming pets. Jordbruksverket officials inspect at Stockholm Arlanda Airport (ARN) cargo terminal.

        Confirm current requirements at jordbruksverket.se before travel.

    - heading: "Titre test logistics from Yerevan"
      body: |
        There is likely no EU-approved FAVN titre test laboratory in Armenia. Your vet in Yerevan draws the blood after the vaccination and at least 30 days later. The blood sample then needs shipping to an approved laboratory, typically in Georgia, Germany, or Turkey.

        Using cold-chain shipping (temperature-controlled packaging, overnight or express courier) is standard practice. Your vet needs experience with this. Once the sample is sent, allow two to four weeks for laboratory processing and result return.

        The 90-day wait starts from the blood draw date regardless of when results arrive. The wait and the laboratory processing time run concurrently, so there is no additional delay caused by the lab processing itself.

    - heading: "Cargo from Yerevan to Stockholm Arlanda"
      body: |
        Turkish Airlines offers the most direct cargo routing: Yerevan (EVN) to Istanbul (IST) then IST to Stockholm Arlanda (ARN). Both legs are Turkish Airlines Cargo services, making it a single-carrier booking.

        Finnair via Helsinki (HEL) is a useful alternative for this Scandinavia-bound route. Helsinki is a well-positioned hub for onward cargo to Stockholm. LOT via Warsaw is another option.

        For very small pets, check whether Wizz Air operates a cabin-pet-eligible service from Yerevan that reaches Swedish airports. Wizz Air serves some Scandinavian destinations, and cabin travel would simplify the process considerably for qualifying small animals.
