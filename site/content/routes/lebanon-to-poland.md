content:
  h1: "Pet Transport from Lebanon to Poland"
  overview: "GIW, the Chief Veterinary Inspectorate of Poland, applies the same unlisted-country rules to Lebanon as every other EU state in this guide. No shortcuts, no exceptions for cats versus dogs. Microchip, rabies vaccination, an FAVN or ELISA titre test, and a 90-day wait from the blood draw all have to happen before a pet from Beirut can legally land in Warsaw. Realistic planning window: 20 to 26 weeks, once Lebanese export processing is factored in."

  sections:
    - heading: "Start with the Lebanese paperwork, not the flight"
      body: |
        It's tempting to book flights first and sort documents later. Don't. The Lebanese Ministry of Agriculture has to issue an export permit and endorse the health certificate before departure, and this step is not fast. Two to four weeks is a realistic estimate for the endorsement alone, and that assumes the application is complete and correct the first time.

        Submit the export permit application while the 90-day titre test wait is still running, so the two processes overlap instead of stacking on top of each other. Owners who leave Lebanese admin until the EU clock has already finished routinely add weeks to their timeline unnecessarily.

    - heading: "The unlisted-country sequence and the 90-day wait"
      body: |
        Poland treats Lebanon as an EU unlisted third country under Regulation 2020/692. The microchip goes in first, always before the rabies vaccination. After vaccination, wait at least 30 days before blood is drawn for a titre test at an EU-approved lab. A passing result, at least 0.5 IU/ml, starts a 90-day countdown from the draw date, not the result date. Only once those 90 days have run can the pet enter Poland. GIW inspects all paperwork on arrival, and a health certificate issued outside its 10-day validity window before entry will be rejected.

    - heading: "Airline options from Beirut to Warsaw"
      body: |
        Lufthansa Cargo via Frankfurt is a dependable choice for Central European destinations like Poland, with the Frankfurt Animal Lounge available for the connecting leg. Turkish Airlines Cargo via Istanbul often gets there faster given the frequency of connections into Warsaw. Qatar Airways Cargo via Doha and Emirates SkyCargo via Dubai are both viable too, though each adds transit distance, and Doha routing can be affected by seasonal heat restrictions on live animal cargo.
