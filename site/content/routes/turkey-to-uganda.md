  h1: "Pet Transport from Turkey to Uganda"
  overview: |
    Until July 2025, a pet flying Turkish Airlines from Istanbul to Entebbe had to transit through Kigali, Rwanda. That changed with the launch of TK591, a genuine non-stop service, though it runs only twice a week. The regulatory side is straightforward by comparison: a MAAIF import permit, a current rabies vaccination, and TRGM's health certificate from the Turkish side.

    The main planning constraint on this route now is flight frequency rather than paperwork.

  sections:
    - heading: "A new non-stop flight, not a settled one"
      body: |
        Turkish Airlines launched direct Istanbul-Entebbe service in July 2025 on flight TK591, using a Boeing 737, twice weekly. Before that, the airline's Entebbe route included a stop in Kigali. The non-stop option is a real improvement, but with only two flights a week, cargo space needs booking well ahead rather than assumed available on short notice.

    - heading: "MAAIF's import permit, applied for before the flight"
      body: |
        Uganda's Ministry of Agriculture, Animal Industry and Fisheries issues the import permit that governs entry, applied for online at maaif.go.ug with the pet's details and the intended travel date. Since the permit references a specific travel date, apply for it before locking in a flight booking, not after. There's no routine quarantine for a compliant arrival at Entebbe, and no rabies titre test requirement on this corridor.
