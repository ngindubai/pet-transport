  h1: "Pet Transport from Kazakhstan to Canada"
  overview: |
    Canada's rules for personal companion pets are among the simpler you will encounter on a long-haul international move. Dogs over 3 months old need a current rabies vaccination certificate in English or French. There is no titre test, no quarantine, and no complex government endorsement chain on the Canadian side.

    The CFIA officer at the port of entry checks the certificate, and if everything is in order, your dog is through. Compare that with the US route from Kazakhstan, which requires a three-authority endorsement chain, a CDC titre test, a pre-booked CDC facility reservation, and a landing at a specified airport list, and you can see why Canada is a considerably more approachable destination.

    The preparation effort on this route sits mainly in the logistics: all services from Kazakhstan to Canada involve at least one connection through a European hub, and the KVCS export process in Kazakhstan still requires careful timing.
  sections:
    - heading: "What Canada requires at the border"
      body: |
        The Canadian Food Inspection Agency (CFIA) sets the rules for dogs entering Canada. Source: CFIA, inspection.canada.ca.

        **Requirements at a glance**

        | Requirement | Dogs over 3 months | Cats |
        |---|---|---|
        | Rabies vaccination certificate | Required (current, in English or French) | Not required on Canada side |
        | Titre test | Not required | Not required |
        | Quarantine | None | None |
        | Import permit | Not required for personal pets (1-2 animals) | Not required |
        | Health certificate format | Standard vet certificate accepted | KVCS export certificate required on KZ side |

        Dogs under 3 months of age have separate rules. Contact CFIA at inspection.canada.ca if your dog is not yet of vaccination age.

        Cats have no federal import requirements on the Canadian side. The only documentation needed for a cat is the KVCS export health certificate from Kazakhstan.

        Commercial imports (pets being brought in for sale, breeding, adoption, or in quantities greater than 2 dogs) require a CFIA import permit and follow a different process. The above covers personal companion animals only.
    - heading: "Kazakhstan export requirements"
      body: |
        The KVCS (Committee for Veterinary Control and Supervision, Ministry of Agriculture of Kazakhstan) governs all animal exports from Kazakhstan. Your veterinarian must be authorised by KVCS to issue the export health certificate. Once issued, KVCS applies their own regional endorsement before the certificate is valid for travel.

        **What you need before departure from Kazakhstan**

        - ISO 11784/11785 microchip, implanted before or at the same time as the rabies vaccination
        - Current rabies vaccination on record against the microchip number
        - Official export health certificate from a KVCS-authorised veterinarian
        - KVCS regional office endorsement on the certificate
        - Export permit if required for the specific animal and destination

        Kazakhstan is a member of the Eurasian Economic Union. Canada's import rules operate independently of EAEU agreements; the KVCS export health certificate is the document that matters for the Canadian side.

        The certificate's validity window is typically around 10 days before the departure date. Allow time for the KVCS endorsement appointment, but do not obtain the certificate so far in advance that it expires before you travel.

        **Departure airports:** Almaty International Airport (ALA) is the primary cargo hub. Nursultan Nazarbayev International Airport (NQZ) in Astana also handles international cargo; confirm pet acceptance with your airline before booking from NQZ.
    - heading: "Airlines and routing"
      body: |
        No direct services connect Kazakhstan to Canada. All routes involve a hub connection, most commonly through Istanbul (IST), Frankfurt (FRA), or Amsterdam (AMS).

        **Routing options at a glance**

        | Carrier | Routing | Notes |
        |---|---|---|
        | Turkish Airlines Cargo | ALA-IST then IST-YYZ | Most direct routing to Toronto; confirm cargo pet policies and heat embargoes |
        | Air Astana | ALA-FRA or ALA-AMS, then to Canadian gateway | Confirm partner cargo on the transatlantic leg |
        | Lufthansa Cargo | ALA-FRA then FRA-YYZ | Reliable on documentation; brachycephalic breeds restricted |
        | KLM Cargo | ALA via AMS to YYZ or YVR | Temperature-controlled hold; seasonal embargoes apply |

        Toronto Pearson (YYZ) is the most common Canadian arrival point given the routing options. Vancouver (YVR) and Montreal (YUL) are also viable depending on your final destination and the available cargo connections at time of booking.

        Summer heat embargoes can affect cargo hold acceptance for large dogs during warmer months. Your pet transport agent will check current embargo dates before confirming the booking.

Cargo booking should be confirmed as early as possible, especially for larger dogs where space is more limited."
