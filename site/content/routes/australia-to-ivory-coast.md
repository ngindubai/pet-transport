content:
  h1: "Pet Transport from Australia to Ivory Coast"
  overview: |
    Two Gmail addresses, not a government portal, decide how quickly a dog or cat clears Cote d'Ivoire's import permit, whether that pet started its journey in Sydney, London or New York. DSV, the Direction des Services Veterinaires, processes applications by email and publishes no fee or turnaround time for the service.

    That manual step, not a blood test, is the main source of delay on this route. Cote d'Ivoire doesn't require a rabies titre from an Australian pet any more than it does from a British or American one, and nothing in its published rules suggests Australia's own rabies-free status changes that either way.

    Getting to Abidjan means at least one change of carrier. Emirates and Ethiopian Airlines, via Dubai and Addis Ababa, form the most workable routing from Australia; Air France via Paris is a genuine alternative for owners who prefer a European gateway.

  sections:
    - heading: "DSV's permit: two email addresses, no portal"
      body: |
        The Direction des Services Veterinaires, operating under the Ministry of Animal and Fisheries Resources (MIRAH), issues the import permit for a pet entering Cote d'Ivoire. USDA APHIS's own guidance confirms the mechanism: send the completed application form and a copy of the rabies vaccination certificate to carv.dsvci@gmail.com and dsv.sdsa2017@gmail.com.

        There's no published processing guarantee attached to those addresses, and nothing about an Australian application changes that. Send it early, keep a dated record of when it went out, and follow up directly if a couple of weeks pass without a reply.

    - heading: "No titre test, whichever direction the rabies-free logic points"
      body: |
        Cote d'Ivoire doesn't require a rabies antibody titre test from any country of origin, as far as its published rules show. That's true for the UK, the US, and Australia alike. Australia's WOAH rabies-free status doesn't buy anything extra here, unlike the lighter pathway it opens up on some other routes on this site.

        Worth flagging the mirror image, too: Cote d'Ivoire itself sits on Australia's own list of higher-risk rabies countries for the reverse direction, meaning a pet travelling from Abidjan back to Australia faces a far more demanding process. None of that touches this outbound route; it's a completely separate rulebook governing entry to Australia, not entry to Cote d'Ivoire.

    - heading: "Getting there: Dubai, Addis Ababa, or Paris"
      body: |
        Emirates covers the long first leg from Australia to Dubai, connecting onward to Ethiopian Airlines' Addis Ababa hub and its established live animal handling, before the final short hop to Abidjan. Air France offers a genuine alternative via Paris for owners whose travel dates suit that gateway better.

        Every one of these carriers restricts or excludes snub-nosed breeds from the hold, so confirm your pet's breed against each airline's policy before booking, on top of the standard live animal acceptance check on every leg.
