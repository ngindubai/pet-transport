  h1: "Pet Transport Uruguay to Sweden: 2026 Guide"
  overview: "Uruguay to Sweden requires the full EU unlisted country process: a FAVN titre test, a 30-day wait after vaccination before the blood draw, a result of 0.5 IU/ml or above at an EU-approved laboratory, and then a mandatory 90-day wait from the blood draw date before entering Sweden. Total preparation is 20-26 weeks. The export process runs through Uruguay's MGAP-DGSG authority in Montevideo, and the Jordbruksverket (Swedish Board of Agriculture) oversees import at Stockholm Arlanda. Sweden does not require a tapeworm treatment for dogs, which keeps the arrival process straightforward once the titre test wait has been completed."

  sections:
    - heading: "Sweden's import requirements for pets from unlisted countries"
      body: |
        Sweden follows EU pet movement rules. For pets arriving from unlisted third countries such as Uruguay, Jordbruksverket applies the requirements of Commission Delegated Regulation (EU) 2026/131, which consolidated and updated the previous Regulation (EU) No 576/2013 from April 2026.

        The requirements are: a microchip implanted before the rabies vaccination; a current rabies vaccination (21 days elapsed since primary dose); a FAVN titre test at an EU-approved laboratory with a result of 0.5 IU/ml or above; a 90-day wait from the successful blood draw date; and an EU Annex IV non-commercial health certificate issued within 10 days of travel.

        Sweden does not impose a tapeworm treatment requirement on dogs arriving from South America, which is a distinction worth noting if you are also considering a stop in Norway or Finland, both of which require Echinococcus treatment for dogs. For direct Uruguay-to-Sweden movements, this step does not apply.

        Border inspection at Stockholm Arlanda Airport (ARN) is standard. The Jordbruksverket inspector checks the health certificate, microchip, vaccination record, and titre test documentation. Compliant paperwork makes this a routine check.

    - heading: "The FAVN titre test from Uruguay: what to arrange"
      body: |
        Uruguay's MGAP-registered vets can draw blood and prepare the sample, but the laboratory analysis must be done at an EU-approved facility. Most approved laboratories are in Europe, the US, or Canada. Common options include ANSES (France, lda.anses.fr), the Animal Health Trust (UK), and Kansas State University Rabies Laboratory (US). Ask the laboratory explicitly whether they accept samples from Uruguay and what preservative medium and shipping instructions they require.

        Your vet draws the blood, stores it correctly, and ships via an international biomedical courier. Allow two to four weeks from the blood draw date to receive the result. The 90-day mandatory wait starts from the blood draw date, not from when the result arrives.

        If the test result is below 0.5 IU/ml, the pet needs to be revaccinated, a new 30-day wait must elapse, a new blood draw arranged, and the 90-day clock restarts from the new draw date. Getting the timing and vaccine quality right the first time matters significantly.

    - heading: "Routing from Montevideo to Stockholm Arlanda"
      body: |
        Carrasco International Airport (MVD) in Montevideo does not have direct long-haul cargo routes to Scandinavia. The practical routing involves two or three legs: MVD to a South American hub (GRU in Sao Paulo or EZE in Buenos Aires), then transatlantic to a European hub (Frankfurt FRA, Amsterdam AMS, Madrid MAD, or Paris CDG), then a final leg to Stockholm Arlanda (ARN).

        LATAM Airlines operates from MVD to GRU and SCL (Santiago), connecting to European hubs via LATAM's international partners. Iberia flies from Buenos Aires (EZE) or via Madrid, which has good onward connections to ARN via Iberia or SAS. Air France connects via Paris CDG, from which SAS or Air France can arrange the final leg to Stockholm.

        SAS Cargo operates to Stockholm Arlanda from Frankfurt, Amsterdam, and Paris. Confirm that SAS accepts live animals on the specific connecting flight before booking. Each cargo transfer requires pre-notification and full live animal documentation. Using a freight forwarder experienced in South American routes simplifies this considerably.

    - heading: "The DGSG export certificate: allow enough time"
      body: |
        Uruguay's official export authority for companion animal health certificates is the Direccion General de Servicios Ganaderos (DGSG), part of the Ministerio de Ganaderia, Agricultura y Pesca (MGAP). A MGAP-registered veterinarian prepares the EU Annex IV health certificate template, including the titre test result, blood draw date, and all vaccination details. The DGSG reviews and endorses the document.

        Allow three to five business days for the DGSG endorsement. The health certificate must be issued within 10 days of travel and presented at ARN on arrival. Do not have it prepared earlier than 10 days before departure, as it expires quickly. Coordinate the MGAP vet appointment and the DGSG endorsement step within that 10-day window, leaving at least one to two days for any delays.

    answer: "No. The 90-day wait from the blood draw date is mandated by Commission Delegated Regulation (EU) 2026/131. It cannot be shortened or paid for. The clock starts on the blood draw date. The only way to reduce total preparation time is to start the microchip, vaccination, and titre test process as early as possible."
  - question: "Which EU-approved laboratory should I use for the FAVN test from Uruguay?"
    answer: "The EU-approved laboratory list is maintained under Commission Decision 2000/258/EC. Approved labs in France (ANSES), the UK, and the US (Kansas State University) are commonly used for samples from South America. Ask your MGAP-registered vet which laboratories they have worked with before, and confirm with the laboratory that they accept samples from Uruguay."
