  sections:
    - heading: "FAVN titre test and the 90-day wait for Uruguay"
      body: |
        Uruguay is classified as an unlisted third country under EU Implementing Regulation 2026/636. This means Jordbruksverket (the Swedish Board of Agriculture) requires a passing FAVN titre test result before your pet's health certificate can be issued. Blood must be drawn at least 30 days after the most recent rabies vaccination. The result must show a neutralising antibody level of at least 0.5 IU/ml at an EU-approved laboratory.

        The 90-day waiting period runs from the blood draw date, not when the result arrives. Jordbruksverket states clearly that no exemption to this wait is granted. After the 90 days, the MGAP-accredited vet issues the CVI and MGAP-DGSG endorses it. The CVI is valid for 10 days from the MGAP endorsement date to the documentary checks at Arlanda or Landvetter. Allow 1-2 working days for the MGAP-DGSG endorsement step after the vet appointment.

    - heading: "MGAP-DGSG export process and routing via Madrid"
      body: |
        The CVI for companion animal exports from Uruguay is issued by an MGAP-accredited veterinarian and endorsed by MGAP-DGSG (Dirección General de Servicios Ganaderos within the Ministerio de Ganadería, Agricultura y Pesca). The endorsement fee is approximately UYU 666. The FAVN titre result must be included in the CVI for EU destinations.

        Montevideo Carrasco International Airport (MVD) is the departure point for international pet cargo from Uruguay. Iberia and LATAM offer the most practical connections to Madrid (MAD), from where Iberia and SAS serve Stockholm Arlanda and Gothenburg Landvetter. Air France connections via Paris and KLM via Amsterdam are alternatives. Fewer transfers reduce the risk of documentation or handling errors on a long-haul journey from the Southern Hemisphere.

  - question: "What is MGAP-DGSG and what does it do for pet exports from Uruguay?"
    answer: "MGAP-DGSG is the Dirección General de Servicios Ganaderos within Uruguay's Ministerio de Ganadería, Agricultura y Pesca. It endorses the CVI (Certificado Veterinario Internacional) issued by an MGAP-accredited vet for international pet movements. The MGAP-DGSG endorsement (visado) is what makes the certificate officially recognised for EU entry. The endorsement fee is approximately UYU 666."
