  overview: |
    Greece to Mexico is a manageable long-haul route for pet transport. A Hellenic Ministry-issued official health certificate (valid within 10 days of Mexico entry), a current rabies vaccination, and a microchip are the core requirements. SENASICA inspects pets on arrival at Mexican airports but does not impose routine quarantine on compliant pets. Iberia via Madrid and Air France via Paris are practical routing options from Athens, given both carriers' direct services to Mexico City.

    - heading: "SENASICA requirements: the 10-day certificate rule"
      body: |
        SENASICA (Servicio Nacional de Sanidad, Inocuidad y Calidad Agroalimentaria) is Mexico's food and agriculture safety authority. The health certificate for Mexico import must be issued by a Hellenic Ministry-authorised official vet and must meet SENASICA's format requirements.

        The critical timing constraint is that the health certificate must be issued within 10 days of the pet's entry into Mexico. If the flight is delayed or rescheduled beyond the 10-day window, a new certificate will be required. Schedule the vet appointment as close to the travel date as possible.

        Contact SENASICA (gob.mx/senasica) for the current health certificate format required for EU-origin pets entering Mexico.
