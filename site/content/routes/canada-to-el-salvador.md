  overview: |
    Avianca's nonstop Toronto-San Salvador service runs about five hours and up to twelve times a week, a genuinely direct connection compared with most Central American routes from Canada, which usually mean a US layover. Montreal gets a nonstop option too, a newer addition to Avianca's network that spares Quebec-based owners the usual Toronto or US detour.

    On the paperwork side, Canada has no dedicated CFIA export permit for a personal pet, so most owners travel on the Canadian International Health Certificate instead, endorsed by a CFIA vet before departure. El Salvador's import side barely reacts to the country of origin: no titre test, an import authorisation costing USD 9.04, and no routine quarantine for a compliant arrival.

  sections:
    - heading: "Why the flight is easier than most Central American routes from Canada"
      body: |
        Most Canada-to-Central-America pet moves involve at least one US connection. This route doesn't have to. Avianca flies Toronto Pearson to San Salvador nonstop in around five hours, with up to twelve weekly departures, and has more recently added a Montreal-Trudeau service too. Either option avoids the extra paperwork and transfer time a US layover can add, particularly for a dog, where US-transit rules sometimes complicate things depending on the origin country involved elsewhere in the itinerary.

    - heading: "The paperwork: lighter than the flight suggests"
      body: |
        Canada doesn't run a blanket CFIA export permit scheme for personal pets. Instead, the Canadian International Health Certificate covers most destinations that, like El Salvador, don't have their own specific certificate negotiated with CFIA. A Canadian vet completes it, and a CFIA veterinarian has to endorse it before the pet actually leaves the country. Leave that endorsement until after departure and it simply can't happen.

        On arrival, MAG doesn't ask for a titre test or impose routine quarantine, just the health certificate and an import authorisation that costs USD 9.04 and is valid for 30 days, single entry only. It can even be finished at the airport on the day if there wasn't time to apply in advance.
