  overview: "Sri Lanka is a CDC high-risk country. Dogs from Colombo moving to the USA need the DAPH-endorsed Certification of Foreign Rabies Vaccination and Microchip form, CDC Dog Import Form, and either a valid serology titre (blood drawn 30+ days post-vaccination and 28+ days before US entry at a CDC-approved lab) or a 28-day quarantine at a CDC-registered facility. The journey from Colombo to US airports routes via Gulf hubs (Dubai or Doha) or European connections. Cats have no federal US barriers. This route benefits from specialist coordination given the DAPH permit complexity and the long cargo journey."

  sections:
    - heading: "The CDC documentation from Sri Lanka and how DAPH fits in"
      body: |
        For the CDC high-risk pathway, the DAPH (Department of Animal Production and Health) needs to endorse the Certification of Foreign Rabies Vaccination and Microchip form. Your Sri Lankan official vet completes the form; DAPH endorses it. This is separate from the DAPH export permit, which is also required.

        Because DAPH processes are known to take time, starting both the DAPH permit application and the serology titre test preparation at the same time is strongly advisable. If you run them sequentially, the total preparation time easily stretches to six months or more.

        A local agent in Sri Lanka who is familiar with DAPH paperwork requirements can significantly reduce the number of rounds of revision and the time spent waiting for queries from the department.

    - heading: "Colombo to US: routing through the Gulf or Europe"
      body: |
        Colombo Bandaranaike International (CMB) does not have direct cargo flights to US cities. All routings involve at least one transit. The Gulf hub options (Emirates via Dubai, Qatar Airways via Doha) are the most efficient for journey time: Colombo to Dubai is around four hours, then Dubai to JFK or LAX is around 14-15 hours.

        The Gulf routing introduces summer temperature embargo risk. Emirates SkyCargo and Qatar Airways Cargo both apply live animal embargoes during extreme heat months at Gulf airports (typically June through September). If your travel falls in this window, the routing via Europe (SriLankan via London, then onward to the US) may be the only option, though it adds transit time.

faqs:
  - question: "Is Sri Lanka CDC high-risk for dog imports?"
    answer: "Yes. Sri Lanka is on the CDC's list of high-risk countries for dog rabies. All dogs from Sri Lanka require the full CDC high-risk pathway: DAPH-endorsed vaccination certification, CDC Dog Import Form, and either a valid serology titre or 28-day quarantine at a CDC-registered facility after US arrival. Source: CDC cdc.gov/importation/dogs, 2026."
  - question: "How long does DAPH take to process an export permit in Sri Lanka?"
    answer: "DAPH processing can take 6-12 weeks and sometimes longer if additional documentation is requested or a physical inspection is required. Allow at least three months. Start the DAPH application at the same time as the titre test process, not after it."
  - question: "Which US airports are accessible from Colombo for CDC-required facility routing?"
    answer: "New York JFK and Los Angeles LAX are the most practical for Gulf hub routings (Emirates, Qatar). Both have CDC-registered facilities nearby. Atlanta ATL is another option. Check cdc.gov for the current list of airports with CDC-registered animal care facilities."
  - question: "Does the summer Gulf heat embargo affect my dog's travel from Sri Lanka?"
    answer: "Yes, potentially. Emirates SkyCargo and Qatar Airways Cargo both apply live animal embargoes during extreme heat periods at Dubai and Doha airports, typically June through September. If your travel falls in this window, confirm current embargo rules with the carrier before booking. European hub routing via SriLankan Airlines avoids this issue."
  - question: "Are there any requirements for cats from Sri Lanka entering the USA?"
    answer: "No federal CDC or USDA requirements apply to cats from Sri Lanka. Cats can enter any US airport without titre test, CDC processing, or quarantine. Individual US states may have their own requirements, but federally, cats face no barriers. Source: USDA APHIS."
