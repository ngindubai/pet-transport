  sections:
    - heading: "Dominican Republic import requirements: the checklist"
      body: |
        The DGAG (Direccion General de Ganaderia) under the Ministerio de Agricultura regulates all pet imports into the Dominican Republic. DGAG veterinary officers inspect arriving pets at Las Americas International Airport (SDQ) in Santo Domingo.

        The requirements are: a microchip conforming to ISO 11784/11785, a valid rabies vaccination given between 30 days and 12 months before entry, a health certificate issued within 15 days of travel, and current DHPP vaccination for dogs (distemper, adenovirus, parainfluenza, parvovirus). Cats need rhinotracheitis, calicivirus, and panleukopenia vaccinations on record. All pets need internal and external antiparasitic treatment within 10 days of departure, documented by a vet.

        No import permit is required for personal companion animals (up to five animals with their owner). There is no quarantine for compliant arrivals. The inspection at SDQ checks microchip, vaccination records, health certificate, and antiparasitic documentation.

    - heading: "Spain's export documentation: the veterinario oficial and MAPA endorsement"
      body: |
        Spain's export documentation for non-EU destinations uses the AHC issued by a veterinario oficial and endorsed by the competent authority (the autonomous community's official veterinary service, acting under MAPA). Contact the official veterinary service in your autonomous community to book the appointment and confirm the endorsement procedure, as processing times can vary between regions.

        From Madrid, Iberia operates established cargo services to Santo Domingo, which makes this route one of the more convenient EU-to-Caribbean moves. The DGAG accepts certificates up to 15 days from issue, giving slightly more scheduling room than most EU destinations.

        The certificate must include your full name and address, plus the animal's name, breed, sex, colour, age, and microchip number.
