  overview: |
    Thailand is on the CDC's high-risk list for dog rabies. Moving a dog to the USA from Thailand means the CDC Dog Import Form alone is not sufficient: you also need a serological test at a CDC-approved laboratory and must enter at a US airport with a CDC-registered animal care facility.

    There is no direct Bangkok to US service that carries live animals, so your pet will transit via a hub, typically Hong Kong (Cathay Pacific) or a connection through a US gateway. The DLD health certificate on the Thailand side needs to be in original form: no photocopies or electronic versions accepted by the DLD or US authorities.

    - heading: "The difference between high-risk and low-risk CDC entry for Thailand"
      body: |
        The practical difference between high-risk and low-risk CDC entry is significant. For a dog from Singapore (low-risk), the CDC process is: fill in the CDC Dog Import Form, confirm microchip, appear healthy. Done.

        For a dog from Thailand (high-risk), add: serology test at a CDC-approved lab (blood drawn at least 30 days after rabies vaccination, result 0.5 IU/ml, at least 28 days before US entry); plus a pre-booked reservation at a CDC-registered animal care facility at your US entry airport.

        The CDC-registered facility receives your dog on arrival, coordinates with CDC inspectors for the health check, and releases the dog to you once cleared. This is typically same-day, but you need to be at the airport to collect. The facility charges a fee, which varies by airport and facility.

        If your dog already has a passing serology result on file from a previous move (and vaccination coverage has not lapsed), you may not need to repeat the test. Verify this with your vet before assuming.

faqs:
  - question: "Does my dog need a serology test to enter the USA from Thailand?"
    answer: "Yes. Thailand is classified as high-risk for dog rabies by the CDC. A serology test at a CDC-approved laboratory is required. The result must be at least 0.5 IU/ml. Blood must be drawn at least 30 days after the rabies vaccination and at least 28 days before US entry."
  - question: "Can I fly directly from Bangkok to the USA with my pet?"
    answer: "There are no direct Bangkok-US cargo services that carry live animals with the required handling. Your pet will transit via a hub, most commonly Hong Kong (Cathay Pacific) or through another Asian or Pacific gateway. This adds transit handling time, but the major hubs used on this route all have capable live animal facilities."
  - question: "My cat is travelling from Thailand to the USA. What does it need?"
    answer: "No federal documentation is required for cats entering the continental USA from Thailand. Your airline will require a health certificate from a DLD-authorised vet, typically issued within 10 days of travel. If your destination is Hawaii, the state HDOA quarantine rules apply separately to cats."
  - question: "Which US airports can I fly into from Thailand?"
    answer: "You must arrive at a US airport with a CDC-registered animal care facility. Los Angeles (LAX), San Francisco (SFO), Seattle (SEA), Chicago (ORD), New York (JFK/EWR), and Houston (IAH) are among the airports with registered facilities. Verify the current list at cdc.gov before booking."
  - question: "How long does the Thailand to USA move take?"
    answer: "Allow 12-20 weeks from vaccination to US arrival. The minimum is around 12 weeks (30 days post-vaccination before blood draw, plus 28 days pre-entry). DLD health certificate preparation, cargo booking, and CDC form completion add practical time beyond the minimum."
