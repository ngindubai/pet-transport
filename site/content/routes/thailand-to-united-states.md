faqs:
  - question: "Does my dog from Thailand need a titre test to enter the USA?"
    answer: "There are two pathways. The titre test pathway: provide a valid FAVN titre report (from a CDC-approved lab, blood drawn 30+ days after vaccination and 28+ days before US entry). With a passing titre test, the dog can arrive at any US airport and is released after CDC inspection. The quarantine pathway: if no valid titre test, the dog undergoes 28 days of quarantine at a CDC-registered animal care facility in the USA. The quarantine pathway also restricts arrival to airports with registered facilities only."
  - question: "Do cats from Thailand need any documents to enter the USA?"
    answer: "No. Cats face no US federal import requirements from Thailand or any other country. USDA APHIS has no health restrictions on cat imports. Airlines require vet documentation for cargo shipments, and some US states may have their own rules, but federally cats from Thailand are unrestricted."
  - question: "What is the Certification of Foreign Rabies Vaccination and Microchip form?"
    answer: "This is the CDC-required document for dogs from high-risk countries. It is completed by the DLD-registered vet in Thailand and then endorsed by the Thai DLD official veterinary authority. It is single-use and valid for 30 days after the vet's signature. It certifies the dog's microchip number, rabies vaccination history, and titre test result (if applicable). It forms part of the documentation set at the CDC inspection on arrival in the USA."
  - question: "Which US airports can my dog from Thailand arrive at?"
    answer: "If using the titre test pathway: the dog can arrive at any US airport, seaport, or land crossing. If using the quarantine pathway (no titre test): the dog must arrive directly at an airport with an active CDC-registered animal care facility. Contact CDC for the current list. No domestic US connection is permitted before the CDC facility inspection."
  - question: "Which airlines carry pets in cargo from Bangkok to the USA?"
    answer: "Most options involve a connection through an Asian hub. Korean Air via Seoul Incheon (ICN) to US cities is a well-established routing. Cathay Pacific via Hong Kong (HKG) and Japan Airlines via Tokyo (NRT/HND) are alternatives. Thai Airways also has cargo services to the US via connections. Confirm current live animal acceptance with the airline or a cargo agent before booking."
