  sections:
    - heading: "The 180-day wait explained"
      body: |
        South Korea's titre test requirement is a sequential process that cannot be rushed. The FAVN test measures your pet's rabies antibody level. The blood must be drawn at least 30 days after the second rabies vaccination, and the 180-day wait period begins from the blood draw date, not from when the result is confirmed.

        This means the total minimum preparation time from a standing start is roughly:
        - First rabies vaccination
        - Wait at least 30 days
        - Second rabies vaccination (if required)
        - Wait at least 30 days
        - Blood draw for FAVN test (180-day wait starts)
        - Plus approximately 4-8 weeks for final paperwork and cargo booking

        In practice, start 8 months before your planned move to allow for any delays or failed titre tests.

    - heading: "Korean Air cabin travel from London"
      body: |
        Korean Air operates a direct London Heathrow to Seoul Incheon route and allows small dogs and cats in the passenger cabin. The pet plus cage must weigh no more than 7 kg, and the carrier must fit under the seat in front of you. This is one of the most convenient options for small pet owners on this route, as cabin travel keeps your pet with you throughout the flight.

        Book the cabin pet space when you reserve your own ticket. Spaces are limited per flight. Korean Air's cabin requirement applies to both cats and small dogs, so this option suits owners of smaller breeds regardless of species.

        For larger pets that exceed the cabin weight limit, Korean Air Cargo and Asiana Cargo are the main direct options, with British Airways via IAG Cargo as an alternative.

  - question: "What is the APQA and what does it require?"
    answer: "APQA is South Korea's Animal and Plant Quarantine Agency, the body responsible for regulating pet imports. APQA requires: an ISO microchip, a rabies vaccination course, a FAVN titre test (with 180-day wait from blood draw), an import permit (applied for at least 30 days before travel), and a health certificate from an official veterinarian. On arrival, all pets serve 10 days in an APQA quarantine facility at the owner's expense."
  - question: "How much does the 10-day quarantine cost in South Korea?"
    answer: "APQA quarantine costs are set by the facility and typically run KRW 200,000 to KRW 600,000 (approximately GBP 120 to GBP 360) for 10 days, depending on the facility and pet size. These costs are paid by the owner directly to the quarantine facility. Budget for this in addition to cargo fees and health certificate costs."
