  h1: "Pet Transport from Mozambique to United Kingdom"
  overview: |
    Mozambique has had no route pages here until now. The reason owners rarely find guidance for it is straightforward: this corridor combines the UK's slowest legal pathway with some of the longest lab logistics on the continent. Mozambique sits on APHA's High risk rabies classification and is not on the UK's Part 1 or Part 2 listed country list, which means the titre test and 90-day wait apply in full.

    On the ground, DINAV (Direcção Nacional de Veterinária) under the Ministry of Agriculture and Rural Development handles export clearance, including a parasite treatment step that most owners moving from Europe never encounter. The bigger time cost, though, is the blood sample: Maputo has no local FAVN-approved laboratory, so the sample travels internationally before results come back, often adding a week or two beyond the standard turnaround.

    Getting the pet out of Maputo is the more solvable part. Ethiopian Airlines, TAP Air Portugal and Qatar Airways all fly daily or near-daily connections that reach London, each with a workable hold or cargo pet service.

  sections:
    - heading: "DINAV, the titre test, and why the courier step matters"
      body: |
        DINAV, Mozambique's National Directorate of Veterinary Services, requires a pre-export examination that includes parasite treatment with locally approved products, on top of the standard microchip and vaccination checks. A licensed Mozambican vet carries out the exam and prepares the health certificate, which DINAV then endorses.

        The part that catches owners off guard is the titre test logistics. Blood must be drawn at least 30 days after vaccination and tested at an APHA-approved laboratory, and there is no such lab in Mozambique. The sample is couriered out, usually to a lab in Europe or South Africa, which stretches the normal 2-3 week turnaround to closer to 4 weeks in practice. Only once a passing result (0.5 IU/ml or higher) comes back does the 90-day countdown start, and that period cannot be shortened regardless of how the pet has been vaccinated before.

        Given the combined 30-day wait, courier and lab time, and the fixed 90 days, five to seven months from first vet visit to UK arrival is a realistic planning window, not a worst case.

    - heading: "Flying out of Maputo: three carriers that reach London"
      body: |
        Maputo International Airport connects to seven airlines, but only a handful carry pets on routes that actually reach the UK. Ethiopian Airlines flies daily to Addis Ababa, with an established live animal handling process and onward connections to Heathrow. TAP Air Portugal flies daily nonstop to Lisbon, roughly 11 hours, with its own hold pet service through to London. Qatar Airways is the only Middle East carrier serving Maputo directly, connecting through Doha, where its Live Animals Centre gives longer-transit pets a walk and a bigger kennel.

        LAM Mozambique Airlines, the national carrier, is worth ruling out early: it serves domestic routes and regional African points such as Johannesburg, but nothing in Europe, so it cannot carry a pet on the UK-bound leg at all.

        South African Airways connects Johannesburg to Maputo regionally, but SAA's international long-haul sectors now require live animals to travel as manifested cargo rather than checked baggage, following infrastructure changes at OR Tambo. That makes a Johannesburg routing on SAA more complex to arrange than booking directly through Addis Ababa, Lisbon or Doha.
