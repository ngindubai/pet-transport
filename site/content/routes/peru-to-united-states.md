  sections:
    - heading: "SENASA and the Peru export certificate for US travel"
      body: |
        SENASA (Servicio Nacional de Sanidad Agraria) is Peru's agricultural health authority and the authority responsible for endorsing health certificates for pet exports. The health certificate for US import must be issued by a SENASA-authorised official vet and endorsed by SENASA before travel.

        The certificate must include your pet's microchip number, vaccination history with titre test result (laboratory name, blood draw date, result in IU/ml), and fitness to travel. SENASA has offices in Lima and other major Peruvian cities. Contact SENASA (senasa.gob.pe) for the list of authorised vets and the current certificate format.

        Allow time for SENASA endorsement after the vet completes the certificate. The endorsed certificate must satisfy both Peruvian export requirements and CDC/USDA documentation standards.

    - heading: "Lima altitude, LATAM cargo, and CDC airport choices"
      body: |
        Lima's Jorge Chavez International Airport (LIM) sits close to sea level, unlike some other South American cities such as Bogota or Quito. This means there are no altitude-related cargo payload restrictions affecting the transport of pets from Peru.

        LATAM Airlines operates cargo from Lima to Miami (MIA) and New York JFK, both of which have CDC-registered animal care facilities. American Airlines via Miami is an alternative. Both Miami and JFK are well-suited US entry points for dogs from Peru under CDC high-risk rules.

        Confirm live animal acceptance and crate dimension requirements directly with LATAM Cargo (latamcargo.com) before booking. Seasonal temperature restrictions may apply on some routes.
