content:
  h1: "Pet Transport from the United Kingdom to Macau"
  overview: |
    Macau runs its own import system, separate from mainland China and Hong Kong, through the Instituto para os Assuntos Municipais, IAM. Since a policy change that took effect around June 2026, most compliant pets no longer face mandatory quarantine there.

    That doesn't remove the paperwork. The UK isn't on Macau's short list of rabies-free exempt origins, so a titre test still applies, and there's no direct flight: every pet on this route makes a stop in Hong Kong before crossing into Macau by road or ferry.

  sections:
    - heading: "Why the UK still needs the titre test"
      body: |
        IAM exempts only a short list of rabies-free origins from the titre requirement, and the UK is not currently on it. That means the same sequence applies as it would from most other countries: blood drawn at least 30 days after vaccination, a minimum result of 0.5 IU/mL, then roughly a three-month wait from the draw date itself before the pet can enter Macau.

        Compliant means four things done in order and confirmed at arrival: microchip, current rabies vaccination, a passing titre result, and the official health certificate. Miss one, and an IAM vet can still hold the pet at the airport despite the general quarantine exemption.

    - heading: "The routing problem: no direct flight, and a licence that expires"
      body: |
        Macau International Airport doesn't carry long-haul cargo from Europe, so the practical path is London Heathrow to Hong Kong with Cathay Pacific Cargo or British Airways World Cargo, followed by a road transfer across the Hong Kong-Zhuhai-Macau Bridge, or a ferry, about an hour once the crate clears Hong Kong customs.

        IAM's Import Licence for Live Animals, PS-1092, is usually issued within three working days but is only valid for 30 days. Apply against a confirmed flight date, not a provisional one, to avoid either an expired licence or a missed booking window.
