content:
  h1: "Pet Transport from Canada to Algeria"
  overview: |
    Algeria's own Australia page on this site opens with 180 consecutive days staged in France before DAFF will even look at the paperwork. Read that page next to this one and the contrast is the whole story: CFIA has no equivalent residency rule, no country-approval groups, nothing that stages a Canadian pet anywhere before it can travel.

    What Algeria does ask for, from Canada as from most origins, is a rabies vaccination inside a 30-day-to-12-month window and a pre-export deworming treatment most destinations on this site skip entirely. No titre test, no import permit, just a certificate built correctly and a flight that has to connect through Europe or Turkey either way.

  sections:
    - heading: "No residency requirement, and that's worth spelling out"
      body: |
        Australia's DAFF sorts every country into approved groups or non-approved, and Algeria falls into the last category there, meaning 180 days somewhere else come first regardless of the pet's health. Canada runs on nothing like that framework. CFIA doesn't grade countries by approval status for outbound pets, and Algeria's own import rules don't ask a Canadian pet to spend time anywhere else first.

        It's a genuinely different shape of route from Algeria's Australia page, not just a lighter version of the same rules. There's no staging country to choose, no 180-day clock to start, no second country's residency requirements layered on top.

    - heading: "The deworming step almost nobody expects"
      body: |
        Most destinations on this site ask for a microchip, a vaccination record and a certificate, full stop. Algeria's own guidance for pets entering the country adds a line most Canadian owners don't see coming: the animal has to be dewormed before export, with the treatment recorded on the certificate.

        Build this into the vet visit that also produces the certificate signature, rather than treating it as a separate errand. A certificate missing the deworming record is the kind of small gap that causes friction at the Algerian end that has nothing to do with the pet's actual health.

    - heading: "Two hubs, and a breed question worth settling early"
      body: |
        No airline flies Algiers to Canada directly. Turkish Airlines via Istanbul, already established on this site's other Algeria routes, is one workable option. Air France's busy Algiers-Paris connection, paired with its own Montreal-Paris service or an Air Canada joint-venture booking on the same route, is the other.

        Separately, and worth checking before either flight is booked: multiple sources describe Algeria as liable to refuse entry to dogs it considers dangerous, Pit Bull-types and Staffordshire Bull Terriers among them, though no single official banned list is published in the way some countries maintain. A direct written check with DSV or the Algerian consulate costs little and avoids a pet held at the airport with an otherwise perfect set of documents.
