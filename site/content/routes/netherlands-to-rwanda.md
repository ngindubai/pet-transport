  sections:
    - heading: "KLM's direct Amsterdam to Kigali service"
      body: |
        KLM, the Dutch national carrier, connects Amsterdam Schiphol (AMS) directly to Kigali International Airport (KGL). For Netherlands-to-Rwanda pet moves, this is the most significant routing advantage available: a direct flight of approximately 9-10 hours, with no hub transfer and no re-handling at a connecting airport.

        Contact KLM Cargo to confirm live animal acceptance on the AMS-KGL service, crate size restrictions, and any seasonal or route-specific conditions. The Schiphol cargo team is experienced with live animal freight, and KLM Cargo handles animals from Amsterdam on several African routes.

        If KLM cargo is not available for your travel dates or pet size, Ethiopian Airlines via Addis Ababa (ADD) is the reliable alternative for East Africa-bound cargo. Ethiopian Cargo is the dominant carrier on Africa-bound animal freight from Europe, and ADD-KGL is a short East African connection.

    - heading: "RAB import permit: applying from the Netherlands"
      body: |
        Rwanda's Agriculture and Animal Resources Development Board (RAB) at rab.gov.rw manages companion animal imports. Apply for the import permit at least 8-10 weeks before travel, providing your pet's ISO microchip number, full vaccination history, breed, and intended travel date.

        Rwanda operates a cleaner administrative environment than many African destinations, and the RAB permit process is generally more predictable. That said, apply early and follow up after submission to confirm your application is being processed. You will present the RAB permit to the inspection team at Kigali International Airport (KGL) alongside the NVWA health certificate.

        RwandAir, Rwanda's national carrier, has expanded its European network in recent years and may offer connections from Amsterdam or nearby hubs. If RwandAir Cargo accepts live animals on routes accessible from AMS, it is worth comparing against KLM and Ethiopian.

    - heading: "NVWA export health certificate"
      body: |
        For non-EU destinations, the Dutch EU pet passport does not apply. The correct document for Rwanda is an official health certificate from an NVWA-authorised veterinarian (Officieel Dierenarts), endorsed by the NVWA (Nederlandse Voedsel- en Warenautoriteit).

        Book a pre-export examination with an NVWA-authorised vet in the Netherlands. They verify the ISO microchip, vaccination records, and general health, then issue the export certificate. NVWA endorses it. The endorsed certificate must be issued within 10 days of travel, so time the vet appointment and NVWA endorsement to fall within that window.

        Amsterdam Schiphol is one of Europe's largest cargo hubs and departures for East Africa-bound animal cargo are managed routinely from AMS.
