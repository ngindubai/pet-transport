  h1: "Pet Transport from United Arab Emirates to Mozambique"
  overview: |
    Ethiopian Airlines flies Dubai to Addis Ababa roughly 29 times a week, close to twice daily, then connects onward to Maputo once every day. No airline flies Dubai to Maputo direct, which makes Ethiopian's dense Addis Ababa frequency the most reliable way to keep a total journey time manageable on this corridor.

    Mozambique's own rule is unusually clear for the region it sits in: no titre test from any origin, but a compulsory DINAV import permit with no published processing time, so it needs to start early rather than being left as a last-minute item. Expect 7 to 10 weeks overall, most of it waiting on DINAV rather than anything MOCCAE demands.

  sections:
    - heading: "Why Addis Ababa beats the alternatives"
      body: |
        Qatar Airways can get a pet to Maputo too, but only after first connecting Dubai to Doha, since Qatar flies from its own hub rather than Dubai directly. That's an extra leg most owners don't need when Ethiopian already runs close to twice-daily Dubai-Addis Ababa service feeding a daily Addis-Maputo sector on the same airline.

        LAM Mozambique Airlines, connecting through Johannesburg on an Emirates first leg, is the fallback when Ethiopian's schedule doesn't fit a particular travel window, though it hands the pet between two separate carriers mid-journey rather than keeping it with one.

    - heading: "DINAV, not the flight, is what to start first"
      body: |
        Mozambique's Directorate of Veterinary Services issues the mandatory import permit, and nothing in its published guidance states how long that takes. Apply through an agent or Mozambique's diplomatic mission as soon as a move is confirmed, well before booking the actual flight.

        Everything else on the Mozambique side is close to standard: a 15-digit ISO microchip, a rabies vaccine at least 30 days old with an annual label rather than a multi-year one, and the core canine or feline vaccinations. No titre test applies from any country, the UAE included, a genuine contrast to how strict DINAV is about the import permit itself.
