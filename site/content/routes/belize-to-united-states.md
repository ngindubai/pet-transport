  h1: "Pet Transport from Belize to the United States"
  overview: |
    Belize had been free of New World Screwworm since 1995. That changed in December 2024, when cases turned up again in the country's livestock, and by August 2025 Belize had confirmed its first human case too. For anyone moving a dog to the US now, that reappearance means a screwworm inspection certificate sits alongside the standard CDC paperwork, a step that simply didn't exist for this route a couple of years ago.

    The CDC side follows the same shape as any high-risk-country move: a titre test drawn 30 days after vaccination and 28 days before arrival, a Dog Import Form, and a mandatory reservation at one of six CDC-registered facility airports. What's easy to miss is the newer 6-month minimum age rule, which applies on top of the usual 12-week vaccination age and has nothing to do with how well-prepared the paperwork is.

  sections:
    - heading: "The screwworm certificate is new, and it isn't optional"
      body: |
        For most of the last three decades, screwworm wasn't something a Belize pet owner needed to think about at all. That ended quietly in December 2024. By early 2026, PAHO was reporting active surveillance and clinical response work across Belize alongside its Central American neighbours, all of which had seen the same resurgence.

        The practical result for dog owners is a certificate from a full-time salaried government vet, arranged through BAHA, confirming the dog was inspected within 5 days of shipment and found clear. It has to come from a government vet, not your usual clinic, so build the BAHA appointment into your schedule rather than assuming your regular vet visit covers it.

    - heading: "Six months old, not twelve weeks, and why the gap catches people out"
      body: |
        Most CDC guidance owners read talks about the 12-week minimum age for the first rabies vaccination. That's real, but it isn't the whole story for a high-risk country. Since the CDC's rule change took effect, a dog also has to be at least 6 months old on the actual date it enters the US, a separate and later threshold.

        A puppy vaccinated correctly at 12 or 14 weeks, with a passing titre test soon after, can still be refused entry if it's travelling before its 6-month birthday. If you're moving a young dog, work backwards from that date first, then fit the vaccination and titre timeline around it, not the other way round.

    - heading: "Miami and LA do double duty as gateway and facility"
      body: |
        American Airlines flies Belize City direct to both Miami and Los Angeles, and both happen to be CDC-registered animal care facility airports. That's useful, since CDC rules require a high-risk-country dog to land at the airport where its facility reservation actually sits, no domestic connections allowed.

        Delta's Atlanta route looks equally convenient on paper, Atlanta being a facility airport too, but Delta's cargo service is currently limited to military and State Department moves. United's Houston route and Southwest's new Houston and Baltimore services don't work for a dog needing the cargo hold at all: Houston isn't a facility airport, United's cargo programme is suspended, and Southwest doesn't carry pets internationally in any form.
