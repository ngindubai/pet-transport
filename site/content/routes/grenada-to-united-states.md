  h1: "Pet Transport from Grenada to the United States"
  overview: |
    A dog leaving Zambia or dozens of other CDC-listed countries has to clear a titre test, land at one of six designated US airports, and book a CDC-registered facility months in advance. A dog leaving Grenada does none of that. Grenada simply isn't on the CDC's high-risk countries list for dog rabies, last updated 15 April 2026, so it sits in the same low-risk bracket as the UK, Canada and Australia.

    That single fact does most of the work of shaping this route. Microchip before vaccine still matters. A free CDC Dog Import Form still has to be filed. But there's no blood test, no facility booking, and no restriction on which US airport the flight lands at. Cats have an even lighter path, with no CDC rabies requirement at all.

    Getting there is straightforward too. American Airlines flies Miami to Grenada nonstop most days of the week, and Delta and JetBlue add year-round nonstop options from Atlanta and New York respectively, so most US owners can avoid a connecting flight altogether.

  sections:
    - heading: "What being off the high-risk list actually saves"
      body: |
        Skip the titre test and the whole shape of the route changes. No blood draw 30 days after vaccination, no 28-day wait for results, no six-airport restriction that forces a specific arrival city. A Grenada dog can land at Miami, Atlanta or JFK, whichever flight is cheapest or most convenient, and clear inspection the same day.

        The microchip-before-vaccine rule still applies, and it's still worth getting right first time; CDC won't recognise a vaccination given before the chip regardless of a country's risk category. Everything else on this route moves faster because the rest of the high-risk pathway simply doesn't apply.

    - heading: "Three nonstop routes, and why cats have it even easier"
      body: |
        American Airlines operates the most frequent option, seven nonstop flights a week between Miami and Grenada as of July 2026. Delta's Atlanta service and JetBlue's JFK route both run year-round, giving owners on the East Coast or in the South-East a nonstop option without connecting through Miami first. None of the CDC-registered-facility restrictions that shape a high-risk-country route apply here, so the choice between the three really comes down to which US city the owner is heading to, plus whichever carrier has cargo space free on the intended date.

        Federal US rules for cats stay lighter still, whatever the country of origin: no CDC rabies vaccination requirement, no titre, no import form equivalent to the dog version. Grenada's own export paperwork applies to cats exactly as it does to dogs, microchip, vet exam, health certificate from the Veterinary and Livestock Division, but the lighter side of this move is entirely on the US end.
