  overview: "Moving a pet from the UK to Ecuador requires an AGROCALIDAD import permit (applied for online, approximately USD 30, processed in 1-2 weeks), an APHA-endorsed health certificate issued within 10 days of travel, current rabies vaccination (given at least 21 days before travel), and documented parasite treatment.   sections:
    - heading: "Quito altitude and the Guayaquil alternative"
      body: |
        Quito's international airport (Mariscal Sucre, UIO) sits at 2,850 metres above sea level. This makes it one of the highest airports in the world for commercial operations. At this altitude, the air is thin, engines produce less thrust, and maximum payload is reduced. Some airlines have weight restrictions on cargo bookings to Quito that are more restrictive than their standard policies.

        For most dogs and cats in appropriate crates, this is not a barrier. But for very large dogs in heavy crates, altitude-related weight restrictions can be a problem. Contact your airline's cargo desk specifically about Quito (UIO) and confirm the weight limit per consignment.

        Guayaquil's Jose Joaquin de Olmedo Airport (GYE) is Ecuador's other major international airport and sits at sea level. It has connections via the same carriers (KLM, Iberia, Avianca, LATAM) and does not have the altitude weight restriction issue. If your final destination is in the coastal or Galapagos region, Guayaquil is the logical entry point anyway. If you are heading to Quito or the highlands, consider whether Guayaquil plus road (a 4-hour drive or short domestic flight) is more practical for your pet.

    - heading: "The AGROCALIDAD permit and what it covers"
      body: |
        Ecuador's agricultural health authority, AGROCALIDAD, requires an import permit for dogs and cats entering from abroad. The permit is obtained online at agrocalidad.gob.ec. The cost is approximately USD 30, and the processing time is typically 1 to 2 weeks. The permit is valid for 60 days from the date of issue, so apply at least 3 weeks before travel to allow for processing time and a buffer.

        AGROCALIDAD inspectors at the port of entry check the import permit alongside the health certificate. The permit is the authorisation to import; the health certificate is the record of your pet's health status. Both are required.

        The online application asks for your pet's details (species, breed, age, microchip number), your personal information, and the intended port of entry. Make sure the port of entry on the permit matches your actual routing.
