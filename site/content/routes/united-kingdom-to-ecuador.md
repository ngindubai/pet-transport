  sections:
    - heading: "AGROCALIDAD permit first, certificate second"
      body: |
        Ecuador's agricultural health authority, AGROCALIDAD (Agencia de Regulación y Control Fito y Zoosanitario), requires a zoosanitary import permit before any dog or cat can enter the country. The permit application asks for the animal's details, vaccination history, and origin. Processing takes four to six weeks.

        The health certificate from your UK vet cannot be obtained until close to travel (within 10 days), so the permit and the certificate run as two separate steps. Apply for the permit first, confirm it is granted, then schedule the health certificate appointment.

    - heading: "Routing via Europe vs via the USA"
      body: |
        Flights from the UK to Ecuador do not run direct. All routings involve at least one transit. The choice of transit point matters because US transit brings in USDA live animal entry rules, even for animals passing through to a final destination.

        KLM via Amsterdam to Quito and Iberia via Madrid to Quito are the cleanest routings, avoiding US transit entirely. British Airways routes to Ecuador typically transit Miami, which introduces a USDA veterinary inspection. For most pets this is not insurmountable, but it adds a step and a potential delay. The European hub routings are simpler.

    answer: "No. Ecuador does not require a rabies titre test for dogs or cats arriving from the UK. The requirements are a current rabies vaccination, a valid APHA-endorsed health certificate, and an AGROCALIDAD import permit. Source: AGROCALIDAD, 2026."
  - question: "What is AGROCALIDAD and what permit do I need?"
    answer: "AGROCALIDAD is Ecuador's Agency of Phyto and Zoosanitary Regulation and Control. The zoosanitary import permit is issued by AGROCALIDAD before travel and allows your dog or cat to enter Ecuador. Apply online via the AGROCALIDAD portal at least 4-6 weeks before travel."
  - question: "Does transiting through the USA affect my UK to Ecuador pet move?"
    answer: "Yes. If your routing stops at a US airport (such as Miami), USDA live animal entry rules apply even in transit. Routing via Amsterdam (KLM) or Madrid (Iberia) avoids this. Confirm with your carrier whether any US connection requires clearing US customs."
