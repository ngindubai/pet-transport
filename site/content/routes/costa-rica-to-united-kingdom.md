  sections:
    - heading: "SENASA Costa Rica: what it covers and what you apply for"
      body: |
        SENASA (Servicio Nacional de Salud Animal) is Costa Rica's animal health authority. It handles the export permit for live animals leaving Costa Rica and endorses the official health certificate issued by your vet. Both are required for UK entry.

        The permit application goes through the SENASA online portal and takes at least two weeks to process. Apply before you finalize the cargo booking, as having the permit in hand early allows you to coordinate the certificate timing with the cargo departure. The health certificate itself is issued within 10 days of travel by your SENASA-registered official vet.

        Because SENASA operates online, the permit process is accessible and not particularly complex. The titre test requirement from the UK end is the more demanding constraint, and the SENASA process is organised around it once the 90-day wait has cleared.

    - heading: "Routing from San Jose to London"
      body: |
        Juan Santamaria International Airport (SJO) is well connected to both European and US hubs. Iberia via Madrid is the most common routing for Central America to UK pet cargo, with a single European transit and a direct onward connection to Heathrow. American Airlines and United offer San Jose to US East Coast routings that can connect onward to London via partner carriers.

        The cargo transit through a US airport introduces additional considerations: US customs clearance for transit, carrier-to-carrier handover, and live animal welfare during connection. Madrid is generally considered the cleaner transit option for pets because it avoids US customs procedures and keeps the connection within one carrier network.

faqs:
  - question: "Is there a direct flight for pet cargo from Costa Rica to the UK?"
    answer: "No direct cargo routes operate between San Jose (SJO) and London. The two main options are via Madrid on Iberia, or via a US gateway (Miami, New York, or Houston) with an onward connection to London. The Madrid routing involves a single transit within the Iberia network and is generally the simpler option for live animal cargo."
  - question: "Does Costa Rica require a titre test for UK pet travel?"
    answer: "The UK requires the titre test because Costa Rica is unlisted under UK pet travel rules, not because of Costa Rican requirements. The FAVN titre test (blood drawn at an APHA-approved lab, minimum result 0.5 IU/ml) and a 90-day waiting period are the UK's conditions for accepting pets from unlisted countries. Source: GOV.UK, 2026."
  - question: "How long is the preparation process for a dog from Costa Rica to the UK?"
    answer: "16 to 20 weeks minimum. Microchip first, then vaccination, then 30-day wait before blood draw, then titre test (lab result takes 1-3 weeks), then 90-day wait. Add 4-8 weeks for SENASA permit, cargo booking, and the final health certificate. Starting 5 months before your intended travel date is advisable."
  - question: "Which vet issues the health certificate in Costa Rica?"
    answer: "A SENASA-registered official veterinarian in Costa Rica issues and signs the health certificate. It must then be endorsed by SENASA (Servicio Nacional de Salud Animal) to be valid for UK border inspection. The certificate is issued within 10 days of travel and covers all required elements: microchip, vaccination, titre test, and tapeworm treatment for dogs."
  - question: "Is there quarantine for dogs arriving from Costa Rica in the UK?"
    answer: "No routine quarantine for dogs and cats from Costa Rica arriving with correct documentation. Penalty quarantine of up to 4 months is imposed if documentation is incomplete, the titre test is invalid, or an unapproved route is used. Source: GOV.UK."
