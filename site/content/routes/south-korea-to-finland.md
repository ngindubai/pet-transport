  h1: "Pet Transport from South Korea to Finland"
  overview: |
    South Korea to Finland requires careful planning on two fronts. South Korea is not on the EU approved third-country list under EU Implementing Regulation 2026/636, so a FAVN titre test and a 90-day waiting period from the blood draw date are compulsory. Finland also requires Echinococcus tapeworm treatment for all dogs, given by a vet within 24 to 120 hours before arrival. The standout advantage on this route is Finnair's direct service from Incheon (ICN) to Helsinki Vantaa (HEL): a non-stop flight that removes the need for a hub connection. APQA issues the export health certificate at Incheon Airport. Allow five to six months from start to travel day.

  sections:
    - heading: "What does a pet from South Korea need to enter Finland?"
      body: |
        Finland follows EU Implementing Regulation 2026/636 as an EU member. South Korea is not on the EU approved list, so the full unlisted-country process applies.

        The sequence is: ISO 11784/11785 microchip first (before any rabies vaccination), then rabies vaccination (minimum 21 days before blood draw), then FAVN titre test at an EU-approved laboratory (blood drawn at least 21 days after vaccination, minimum result 0.5 IU/ml), then the 90-day waiting period counted from the blood draw date. After the wait, APQA endorses an EU Animal Health Certificate (AHC, Annex IV for unlisted countries) at Incheon Airport within 10 days of travel.

        Finland requires Echinococcus multilocularis tapeworm treatment for all dogs. A vet must administer a praziquantel-based product between 24 and 120 hours before the dog arrives in Finland. The treatment time, product name, and dose must appear on the APQA-endorsed health certificate. Cats do not require tapeworm treatment. There is no national breed ban in Finland.

    - heading: "Which airlines carry pets from South Korea to Finland?"
      body: |
        Finnair's direct service from Incheon (ICN) to Helsinki Vantaa (HEL) is the standout option for this route. Finnair Cargo accepts live animals on its own directly operated flights, and a non-stop ICN-HEL service avoids the hub connection that all other South Korea to Finland routings require. Confirm live animal cargo availability with Finnair Cargo at the time of booking, as policies can vary by aircraft configuration.

        If Finnair cargo space is unavailable, Korean Air Cargo flies ICN to Frankfurt (FRA) and Amsterdam (AMS), with onward Finnair or Lufthansa connections to Helsinki. Lufthansa Cargo and KLM Cargo are alternatives for the long-haul leg, connecting at FRA or AMS respectively.

    - heading: "How long does the South Korea to Finland process take, and what does it cost?"
      body: |
        Allow five to six months from start to travel day. The 90-day wait from the titre test blood draw is the fixed constraint; add the 21-day gap between vaccination and blood draw. Do not book flights until you have a confirmed blood draw date and can calculate the exact earliest permitted entry date into Finland.

        Costs typically run from EUR 1,400 to EUR 3,600. The FAVN titre test costs EUR 200 to EUR 400. APQA inspection and AHC endorsement at ICN adds KRW 50,000 to KRW 150,000 (roughly EUR 35 to EUR 110). Cargo from ICN to HEL costs EUR 900 to EUR 2,500, with the Finnair direct option potentially reducing transit costs and complexity. Finnish border inspection runs EUR 100 to EUR 250. An IPATA agent adds EUR 350 to EUR 750.
