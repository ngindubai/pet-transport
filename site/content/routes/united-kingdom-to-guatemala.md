content:
  h1: "Pet Transport from the United Kingdom to Guatemala"
  overview: |
    No titre test and, on MAGA's published general guidance, no fixed quarantine apply to a dog or cat travelling from the UK to Guatemala. What Guatemala does ask for is straightforward: a current rabies vaccination given at least a month before travel, a vet-signed health certificate, and a small inspection fee, around USD 14, paid to OIRSA at the port of entry, based on the animal's weight.

    The flight is the part that needs planning. No airline flies direct from the UK to Guatemala City, and Avianca, one of the main carriers serving Guatemala, will not carry pets into or out of Britain except as declared cargo. A US-hub connection through Miami or Houston is the usual alternative, which brings its own temperature and transit rules into the booking.

  sections:
    - heading: "OIRSA's USD 14 inspection fee: what it actually covers"
      body: |
        OIRSA, the regional agricultural health body that Guatemala shares with its Central American neighbours, inspects pets at the point of entry, whether that's air, sea or land. The fee is scaled to the animal's weight and sits around USD 14 for a typical dog or cat. You submit copies of the UK health certificate and vaccination record at this point, so bring physical copies rather than relying on a phone screen.

        This inspection is separate from, and in addition to, the UK-side APHA certificate process. Neither authority substitutes for the other.

    - heading: "Why UK-specific guidance is thinner than the US-Guatemala protocol"
      body: |
        MAGA's published material goes into detail for pets arriving from the United States, down to naming the core vaccines (distemper, hepatitis, leptospirosis and parvovirus for dogs; panleukopenia, rhinotracheitis, calicivirus and leukaemia for cats) and a parasite treatment, under a specific bilateral arrangement. Nothing equivalent, specific to UK-origin pets, turned up in this research.

        That doesn't mean UK owners face those extra conditions. It means the general MAGA requirements, health certificate, vaccination card, OIRSA inspection, are the documented baseline, and a phone call to MAGA or a Guatemalan import agent before booking flights is the sensible way to confirm nothing extra applies to a British pet specifically.
