  h1: "Pet Transport Armenia to Lithuania: 2026 Guide"
  overview: "Moving a pet from Armenia to Lithuania requires careful planning over a minimum of five to six months. Lithuania is an EU member state and Armenia is an EU unlisted country, which means the full EU unlisted pet travel process applies: microchip, rabies vaccination, FAVN titre test with a 90-day wait, and an SSFS-endorsed health certificate. There is no shortcut to the 90-day wait. The routing from Yerevan to Vilnius is more complex than some Baltic routes because Vilnius Airport has limited direct cargo connections, requiring a multi-leg journey via Istanbul or a Western European hub."

  sections:
    - heading: "Why the 90-day wait applies and what it means for planning"
      body: |
        Lithuania, as an EU member state, applies the EU pet travel framework for animals arriving from unlisted third countries. Armenia is classified as an EU unlisted country, which means your pet must complete the FAVN titre test process before entering Lithuania.

        The sequence is: chip implanted first, then primary rabies vaccination, then at least 30 days later a blood draw for the FAVN titre test at an EU-approved laboratory. If the result is 0.5 IU/ml or above, the 90-day wait begins from the blood draw date. Travel to Lithuania cannot happen until at least day 91.

        The 90-day wait is calculated from when the blood was drawn, not when the results came back. If you draw blood and the results take two weeks, those two weeks count as part of your 90-day wait. Starting the process as early as possible after you know you are moving is the right approach.

    - heading: "What happens at Vilnius Airport"
      body: |
        Vilnius Airport (VNO) has border veterinary inspection for live animal cargo arriving from non-EU origins. The Valstybine maisto ir veterinarijos tarnyba (VMVT) oversees these controls. Border vets at VNO will check: the SSFS-endorsed health certificate (issued within 10 days of travel), the FAVN titre test result, the vaccination record, and the microchip.

        The chip must be scannable and the number must match every document precisely. If the titre test shows a successful result but the 90-day wait is incomplete, entry is refused. Lithuanian border vets apply EU rules strictly. There is no flexibility for incomplete documentation.

    - heading: "Routing from Yerevan to Vilnius"
      body: |
        There is no direct cargo service from Yerevan Zvartnots (EVN) to Vilnius (VNO). Vilnius Airport is a smaller regional airport with limited direct international cargo connections. The routing almost always requires two or more legs.

        The most practical option uses Turkish Airlines Cargo from EVN to Istanbul Ataturk (IST), then connects through a European hub. From Warsaw (WAW), LOT Polish Airlines serves Vilnius directly. From Frankfurt or Amsterdam, connecting carriers reach VNO. The total journey from Yerevan to Vilnius via IST and a European hub is typically 9-15 hours depending on transit times.

        Book during the 90-day wait and confirm live animal cargo acceptance on every segment in writing. A pet transport agent with experience in Caucasus-to-Baltic routing is strongly recommended for this move.

    answer: "A minimum of 20-26 weeks from start to finish. This accounts for: microchipping, primary vaccination, 30-day minimum before blood draw, the titre test process, the 90-day wait from blood draw, and certificate preparation. Starting as soon as you know you are moving is essential."
  - question: "What is the FAVN titre test and where can I get it done in Armenia?"
    answer: "The FAVN (fluorescent antibody virus neutralisation) test measures your pet's rabies antibody levels. Blood must be drawn by a licensed vet in Armenia and sent to an EU-approved laboratory (laboratories in France, Germany, and the UK are commonly used from Armenia). The blood draw must happen at least 30 days after the primary rabies vaccination. If the result is 0.5 IU/ml or above, the 90-day wait begins from the blood draw date."
  - question: "Can my pet enter Lithuania via a land border from another EU country?"
    answer: "EU-internal land borders between member states are open for pet movements using an EU pet passport or AHC. However, your Armenian pet needs to have completed the full unlisted country process before entering any EU member state for the first time. Simply crossing from Poland into Lithuania after flying into Warsaw does not bypass the requirements."
  - question: "What EU-approved laboratories can handle Armenian pet blood samples?"
    answer: "Several EU-approved laboratories accept samples from non-EU countries. In practice, blood drawn in Armenia is often shipped to laboratories in France (such as Laboratoire National de Reference de la Rage), the Netherlands, or Germany. Your Armenian vet and transport agent can recommend which laboratory is most practical and reliable for Yerevan-origin samples."
