content:
  h1: "Pet Transport from Switzerland to Uruguay"
  overview: |
    Moving a dog or cat from Switzerland to Uruguay requires preparation well in advance of your travel date. MGAP (Ministerio de Ganaderia, Agricultura y Pesca) administers pet imports in Uruguay and requires an import permit before travel. This is the starting point; nothing else can be finalised until the permit is in hand. Apply via gub.uy/ministerio-ganaderia-agricultura-pesca with your pet's details as soon as you have a confirmed move date.

    Uruguay also requires dogs to receive a Praziquantel treatment against Taenia Echinococcus, a tapeworm species that Uruguay works actively to keep out of the country. The timing of this treatment is calculated against your arrival date at Carrasco International Airport (MVD) in Montevideo, and the window is fixed at 72 hours to 30 days before arrival. Cats are exempt from this requirement. Get the timing right from the start and it is a simple step; get it wrong and you will need to repeat the dose.

    On the Swiss export side, FSVO/BLV (the Federal Food Safety and Veterinary Office) oversees health certificate issuance. Your certificate must come from a BLV-authorised official vet and be endorsed by FSVO/BLV within 10 days of travel. Cargo routing from Zurich (ZRH) or Geneva (GVA) to Montevideo (MVD) goes via Madrid or South American hubs such as Lima or Sao Paulo. Uruguay has no quarantine; MGAP inspects on arrival at MVD.

  sections:
    - heading: "MGAP Permit and Praziquantel: The Two Steps That Define This Route"
      body: |
        Most South American destinations require advance permits, and Uruguay is no exception. MGAP reviews the permit application and confirms Switzerland's status as an acceptable origin before issuing authorisation. Submit the application with your pet's species, breed, sex, age, and microchip number. Once the permit arrives, you can finalise cargo bookings and schedule the Swiss official vet appointment.

        For dogs, the Praziquantel requirement is specific to Uruguay and reflects the country's biosecurity policy around hydatid disease. The product must be a licensed Praziquantel formulation at the recommended dose for your dog's weight. Your vet issues a dated certificate naming the drug and dose. MGAP inspectors at MVD will check this certificate, along with all other documents. If the treatment date falls outside the 72-hour to 30-day window, MGAP will not clear your dog. Build the timing calculation into your planning before you book the outbound flight.

    - heading: "BLV Health Certificate: Switzerland's Non-EU Export Process"
      body: |
        Because Switzerland is not an EU member, Swiss pets use federal health certificates rather than EU Animal Health Certificates. FSVO/BLV (Bundesamt fur Lebensmittelsicherheit und Veterinarwesen) is the issuing authority. You need a BLV-authorised official vet, an amtlicher Tierarzt, to carry out the examination. Contact your cantonal veterinary office to identify the correct official vet for your region; in larger cities such as Zurich, Geneva, and Bern, appointments are usually available within a few days.

        After the examination, FSVO/BLV endorsement takes approximately one working day. The certificate is valid for 10 days from issue. Uruguay accepts this document without requiring consular authentication, which is one less step compared to the Panama route. The endorsed original must travel with the cargo booking. MGAP at MVD requires the original, not a scan. Keep it accessible throughout the journey and do not put it into checked luggage.
