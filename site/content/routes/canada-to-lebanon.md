  sections:
    - heading: "Lebanese import permit: apply first, book flights second"
      body: |
        The Directorate General of Agriculture (DGA), part of Lebanon's Ministry of Agriculture, controls companion animal imports. The import permit from the DGA is the prerequisite for entry. Without it, your pet cannot be cleared at Beirut Rafic Hariri International Airport (BEY).

        Apply at agriculture.gov.lb with your pet's microchip number, breed, vaccination history, travel date, and your contact details. The DGA processes applications but its turnaround time is not published as a fixed figure. Given Lebanon's current economic and administrative pressures, apply as early as possible. Eight to ten weeks is a sensible minimum.

        Once the permit arrives, store it with your health certificate. Both documents are checked by the DGA inspection team at BEY on arrival.

    - heading: "Toronto or Montreal to Beirut: routing options"
      body: |
        From Toronto Pearson (YYZ) and Montreal (YUL), the most direct routing to Beirut connects via Istanbul on Turkish Airlines. Turkish Airlines operates regular transatlantic services from both YYZ and YUL, and Turkish Cargo runs live animal freight on many international routes. The IST-BEY segment is a short regional hop.

        Air Canada via London Heathrow (LHR) or Frankfurt (FRA) gives you a European hub option. From LHR or FRA, Middle East Airlines (MEA) or other carriers connect to BEY. This routing involves more connections but uses carriers your pet transport agent may have established relationships with.

        Emirates via Dubai is a longer routing from Canada but Emirates SkyCargo is well set up for live animal freight and DXB-BEY is short. Total transit from Canada to Beirut runs 14-20 hours depending on the routing and connection times.

    - heading: "CFIA endorsement: the Canadian side of the paperwork"
      body: |
        The Canadian Food Inspection Agency (CFIA) endorses health certificates for companion animal exports from Canada. Your accredited vet issues the certificate after examining your pet. CFIA then endorses it through its regional office network.

        For Lebanon, a non-EU destination, the certificate format must meet Lebanon's requirements. CFIA offices are experienced with international export documentation, but confirm with your vet or local CFIA office that the certificate covers Lebanon's specific requirements. The endorsed certificate must be issued within 10 days of travel.

        CFIA endorsement typically takes 1-5 working days. Plan your vet appointment to give enough buffer for the endorsement to complete and for the certificate to reach you in time for travel.
