    answer: "Yes, on Air France or United. Air France allows small pets in the cabin on many routes, with larger pets travelling in cargo via Air France or Delta.   - question: "Does my pet need to quarantine in France?"
    answer: "No. France does not quarantine pets arriving with correct documentation. With a valid USDA-endorsed EU health certificate, an ISO microchip implanted before the rabies vaccination, and a current rabies vaccination (21-day wait complete), your pet enters France without quarantine and without an import permit."
  - question: "How long does the USDA endorsement take?"
    answer: "Electronic submission via VEHCS typically processes in 2-5 business days; mail takes 5-10. Since the EU health certificate examination must be within 10 days of travel, time the endorsement carefully. Most owners schedule the vet examination 7-8 days before travel and submit immediately via VEHCS so the endorsed certificate is ready before departure."
  - question: "Are there breed restrictions in France?"
    answer: "Yes. France classifies dangerous dogs into two categories. Category 1 (Pit Bull Terrier types and certain others) are banned. Category 2 (American Staffordshire Terrier, Rottweiler, Tosa) are permitted subject to a muzzle and lead in public, registration, and ownership conditions. Check whether your dog falls into either category before moving, as these rules affect keeping the dog in France."
