  sections:
    - heading: "The Lebanese import permit: start here"
      body: |
        Lebanon's Directorate General of Agriculture (DGA) under the Ministry of Agriculture issues the import permit required for companion animals. Apply at agriculture.gov.lb with your pet's details: microchip number, vaccination history, breed, and travel date.

        Processing times can vary, particularly given Lebanon's current administrative environment. Apply 8-10 weeks before travel and follow up by email or phone after submitting. The permit is the first step; do not book cargo until it is confirmed.

        At Beirut Rafic Hariri International Airport (BEY), the DGA inspection team checks both the import permit and the Italian health certificate. Compliant arrivals from Italy do not face quarantine.

    - heading: "MEA's Rome to Beirut direct service"
      body: |
        Middle East Airlines (MEA) is Lebanon's national carrier and operates services between Rome Fiumicino (FCO) and Beirut (BEY). If MEA Cargo accepts live animal freight on this route, the direct FCO-BEY service removes the need for an Istanbul or other hub transfer, cutting transit time to approximately 3-4 hours.

        Contact MEA Cargo directly to confirm current live animal acceptance, crate size limits, and any seasonal restrictions. MEA's cargo capacity on the FCO-BEY route may be limited; confirm early if you are planning around this option.

        If MEA cargo is not available or does not suit your dates, Turkish Airlines via Istanbul (IST-BEY) is the reliable alternative. Turkish operates from both FCO and Milan Malpensa (MXP) to IST, with regular IST-BEY connections. Turkish Airlines Cargo handles live animals and this is a well-used routing for Italy-to-Lebanon pet transport.

    - heading: "Italy's ASL export certificate for Lebanon"
      body: |
        Lebanon is outside the EU, so the Italian EU pet passport is not the correct document for this journey. You need the specific export health certificate from a veterinario ufficiale at your local ASL (Azienda Sanitaria Locale).

        Book an appointment at your local ASL veterinary department. The official vet examines your pet, checks the microchip and vaccination history, and issues the certificato sanitario per l'esportazione in the format required for Lebanon. The document is then endorsed by the relevant authority.

        Allow 2-3 working days for the endorsement step. The certificate must be issued within 10 days of travel. Time your vet appointment accordingly, allowing enough days for endorsement plus travel.
