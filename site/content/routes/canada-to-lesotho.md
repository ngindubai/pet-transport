  h1: "Pet Transport from Canada to Lesotho"
  overview: |
    Ethiopian Airlines flies the only nonstop passenger route between Canada and continental Africa, Toronto to Addis Ababa, seven times a week. For a pet headed to Lesotho, that single fact carries most of the journey: from Addis Ababa, Ethiopian runs up to three flights a day on to Johannesburg, keeping the animal under one airline's care for two of the three legs.

    The last hop doesn't change, whatever the route in from Canada looks like. Moshoeshoe I International Airport in Maseru has exactly one scheduled international connection, to Johannesburg, flown only by Airlink. Every plan ends with that same short regional sector.

    The paperwork side is lighter than the routing. Lesotho asks for a vaccination record, a microchip, and an import permit, no blood test involved, though Canada does need the permit since it isn't a SADC member state.

  sections:
    - heading: "Why Ethiopian's Toronto route changes the calculation"
      body: |
        Most Canada-to-Africa pet moves this site covers involve a European stopover first, London, Frankfurt or Amsterdam, before an African leg even starts. Ethiopian's nonstop Toronto-Addis Ababa service skips that step entirely, and its own dense Addis Ababa-Johannesburg schedule, up to three flights daily, means the connecting leg isn't a scarce once-a-week booking either.

        That leaves a genuinely two-carrier journey rather than a three- or four-carrier one: Ethiopian for the long haul and the African connection, Airlink for the final short hop into Maseru.

    - heading: "What Lesotho's import rules actually require"
      body: |
        A Canadian pet arriving in Lesotho needs a rabies vaccination given between 30 days and 12 months before arrival, current core vaccinations, a microchip on the paperwork, and an import permit from the Director of Animal Health, part of Lesotho's Ministry of Agriculture, Food Security and Nutrition. The permit is needed because Canada isn't one of the SADC member states that gets a waiver on this step.

        No titre test features anywhere in Lesotho's own rules, for pets from any country. That's a genuinely different position from several destinations elsewhere on this site, where a titre test and a fixed waiting period are the main driver of the whole timeline.

    - heading: "Johannesburg, twice: once for the flight, once for the paperwork"
      body: |
        The two legs into Maseru sit on separate air waybills, often with different airlines and terminals. A ground agent at O.R. Tambo, used to managing this specific handoff, makes the difference between a same-day connection and a delay. Book the Airlink sector at least 48 hours ahead; it's a smaller regional operation with its own booking rhythm, separate from the long-haul arrival.
