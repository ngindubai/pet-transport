  h1: "Pet Transport UK to Mexico: Health Certificate, SENASICA Permit and 2026 Requirements"
  overview: "Moving a dog or cat from the UK to Mexico does not require a titre test or quarantine period. The two central requirements are the SENASICA zoosanitary import permit (obtained before travel from Mexico's agricultural authority) and the official UK veterinary health certificate issued by an APHA-authorised vet within 10 days of departure. British Airways and Aeromexico both operate direct services from Heathrow to Mexico City, and both accept pets.   sections:
    - heading: "The SENASICA permit and why it needs to come first"
      body: |
        SENASICA (Servicio Nacional de Sanidad, Inocuidad y Calidad Agroalimentaria) is Mexico's agricultural health authority. For dogs and cats arriving in Mexico, it requires a zoosanitary import permit to be obtained before the animal travels. This permit is separate from the health certificate and must arrive with the animal at SENASICA inspection on entry.

        The permit application process takes four to six weeks. Starting it early, before you finalise your travel date, gives you flexibility. The health certificate from your UK vet is issued within 10 days of travel, so that part is done close to departure. Get the permit first, then finalise the travel date and certificate appointment once it is confirmed.

        Working with an agent who is familiar with SENASICA requirements can reduce the chance of a rejected application. The permit application asks for information about the animal's species, breed, age, vaccination history, and origin country, all of which should be straightforward to provide.

    - heading: "Flying directly from the UK to Mexico City"
      body: |
        A direct flight from London Heathrow to Mexico City Benito Juarez International (MEX) is the simplest routing for UK to Mexico pet transport. It avoids US transit, which would bring in USDA import rules even for animals in transit.

        British Airways operates this route and accepts pets via BA World Cargo. Aeromexico operates from Heathrow to Mexico City and accepts small pets in the cabin on this route, with larger pets in the hold as checked baggage. Virgin Atlantic also operates the Heathrow-Mexico City route. Check current pet acceptance policies with the airline at the time of booking.

        KLM via Amsterdam and Air France via Paris are alternatives if Heathrow connections are not convenient. Both carriers accept pets and have established cargo networks to Mexico City.

faqs:
  - question: "Is a titre test required to take a dog from the UK to Mexico?"
    answer: "No. Mexico does not require a rabies titre test for dogs or cats arriving from the UK. You need a current rabies vaccination, a health certificate from an APHA-authorised UK vet, and a SENASICA import permit. No titre test, no quarantine. Source: SENASICA, 2026."
  - question: "What is the SENASICA import permit and where do I get it?"
    answer: "The SENASICA zoosanitary import permit is issued by Mexico's agricultural health authority before travel. You apply online via the SENASICA portal, or through an agent in Mexico who handles animal import paperwork. Allow 4-6 weeks for processing. The permit must accompany your pet and health certificate on arrival."
  - question: "Can I take my cat from the UK to Mexico without quarantine?"
    answer: "Yes. Cats from the UK can enter Mexico without quarantine, provided they have a current rabies vaccination and the correct documentation (SENASICA permit and APHA-endorsed health certificate)."
  - question: "Does my pet go through US customs if my flight to Mexico transits via the USA?"
    answer: "If your flight transits through a US airport with a layover requiring you to pass through US border control, then yes, US import rules apply including USDA animal entry requirements. Booking a direct flight to Mexico City or routing via Amsterdam (KLM) or Paris (Air France) avoids this."
  - question: "How long does the health certificate remain valid for Mexico?"
    answer: "The health certificate must be issued within 10 days of travel. Once used for entry into Mexico, it is not reusable for a return journey. A new certificate from a Mexican vet is required if the animal is to travel back to the UK."
