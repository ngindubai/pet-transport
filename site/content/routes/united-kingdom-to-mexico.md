  sections:
    - heading: "SENASICA rules for UK pets: what is different from US entry"
      body: |
        Mexico's SENASICA applies different requirements depending on where a pet originates. Pets from the United States and Canada go through a simplified inspection process, often with minimal documentation checks at the border. Pets from the UK face the standard international health certificate requirements, which are more detailed.

        For UK pets, the health certificate must be an official APHA-endorsed document, listing the microchip number, rabies vaccination (given at least 30 days before travel), core vaccinations, and documentary evidence of parasite treatment. The certificate must be issued within 15 days of travel.

        If your pet is travelling as cargo (rather than as accompanied checked baggage or in-cabin), a zoosanitary import permit from SENASICA may also be required. The process for obtaining this permit is done online or through a customs agent. Contact SENASICA at senasica.gob.mx to confirm the current permit requirement for your specific routing before making travel plans.

    - heading: "Mexico City altitude and airline cargo considerations"
      body: |
        Mexico City's international airport (Benito Juarez, MEX) sits at 2,240 metres above sea level. At this altitude, aircraft performance is reduced: engines produce less thrust, the air is thinner, and maximum cargo loads are lower than at sea-level airports. Some airlines have restrictions on live animal cargo specifically at MEX because of these performance factors.

        Before booking, confirm with your chosen airline that they will accept live animal cargo on the specific aircraft operating the LHR-MEX route. Aeromexico and British Airways both serve this route, but aircraft types change and cargo policies vary by aircraft.

        If your final destination in Mexico is a coastal city (Cancun, Puerto Vallarta, Los Cabos), you may find fewer altitude-related restrictions. These airports are at lower elevations. Consider whether routing to a coastal airport and then travelling by road to your final destination is practical.
