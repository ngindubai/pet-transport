content:
  h1: "Pet Transport from the United Kingdom to Botswana"
  overview: |
    No rabies titre test, no fixed multi-month wait: Botswana's import side is one of the lighter destinations a UK-origin pet can travel to. What it does want is a document most owners don't expect, a signed statement that no animal disease movement restrictions currently apply in the part of the UK the pet is leaving from.

    The flight itself runs through Johannesburg. British Airways covers the Heathrow to O.R. Tambo leg daily, and Airlink or Air Botswana finishes the journey into Gaborone. Between the Export Health Certificate window, the import permit application and the connecting flights, six to ten weeks is a realistic planning figure.

  sections:
    - heading: "The attestation UK owners tend to overlook"
      body: |
        Most import checklists ask for a health certificate and a permit. Botswana's Department of Veterinary Services adds a third thing: written confirmation that no movement restrictions, the kind imposed during a local disease outbreak, currently apply in the pet's home area. It's a standard biosecurity safeguard from DVS's side, but it isn't something a UK vet issues automatically as part of a routine Export Health Certificate, so raise it directly when booking the appointment.

        Get this wrong, or simply forget to ask for it, and the import permit application stalls at DVS while someone chases the missing document. Better to flag it in the first phone call than the week before travel.

    - heading: "No titre test, but the EHC still has to be right"
      body: |
        Botswana doesn't require a rabies antibody blood test from any UK pet, which removes the single biggest timeline driver on routes heading the other way. The trade-off is that the rabies vaccination on record has to already be at least 30 days old by the time the pet lands in Gaborone, so a last-minute booster won't clear customs even though no formal titre check applies.

        Only an APHA-authorised Official Veterinarian can sign the Export Health Certificate itself, and not every general practice has one on staff. Confirm this early, since discovering the gap a few weeks before departure is the kind of delay that's entirely avoidable.
