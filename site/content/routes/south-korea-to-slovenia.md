    Korean Air Cargo staff at Incheon deal with APQA-endorsed export paperwork often enough that the departure end of this route tends to run smoothly.   sections:
    - heading: "APQA and the Incheon export process"
      body: |
        The Animal and Plant Quarantine Agency inspects and endorses the EU health certificate at Incheon International Airport, and this has to happen within 10 days of the flight. An accredited vet completes the certificate first; APQA's endorsement is the step that makes it valid for EU entry.

        Korean Air Cargo's staff at ICN are used to seeing this specific paperwork, which reduces the chance of a document being rejected at the cargo desk. That said, the certificate's 10-day window is fixed regardless of which airline you fly, so timing the APQA appointment against your confirmed flight date matters more than which carrier you choose.

    - heading: "The titre test arithmetic that decides your travel date"
      body: |
        Microchip first, then rabies vaccination, then a FAVN or RNATT blood draw at least 21 days later at an EU-approved laboratory. A result of 0.5 IU/ml or above starts a 90-day countdown from the draw date, not from the vaccination date and not from when the paperwork clears.

        Work this out before you book anything else. If the blood draw happens on 1 September, the earliest entry date into Slovenia is 30 November, full stop. Airlines, agents and even Slovenian border officials cannot move that date for you.

    - heading: "Incheon to Ljubljana: Frankfurt, Amsterdam or Istanbul"
      body: |
        Korean Air Cargo runs to both Frankfurt and Amsterdam, with onward connections toward Ljubljana from either hub. Turkish Airlines Cargo's direct Incheon-Istanbul service is often the quickest single-connection option if space is available on your dates.

        As with most routes into Slovenia, confirm whether your specific booking lands directly at Ljubljana Jože Pučnik Airport or whether it finishes with a road transfer from a bigger hub. Both patterns are common, and it changes how you plan to collect your pet.

    answer: "APQA, South Korea's Animal and Plant Quarantine Agency, inspects the pet and endorses the EU health certificate at Incheon International Airport, within 10 days of the flight. Without this endorsement the certificate is not valid for entry into Slovenia."
  - question: "How is the 90-day wait counted?"
    answer: "From the date blood is drawn for the titre test, not from the vaccination date and not from when the laboratory issues the result. Confirm the exact draw date with your vet and count 90 calendar days forward before booking a flight."
  - question: "Will my pet quarantine on arrival in Slovenia?"
    answer: "No, provided the microchip, vaccination, titre test and 90-day wait are all in order and the EU health certificate is presented within its 10-day validity. Slovenia's UVHVVR does not quarantine compliant pets."
