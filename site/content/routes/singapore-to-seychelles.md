content:
  h1: "Pet Transport from Singapore to Seychelles: 2026 Guide"
  overview: |
    A genuinely clean rabies record is rare on this site's Asia-to-Seychelles routes, and Singapore has one. That single fact likely earns Singapore-origin pets the shorter end of Seychelles' quarantine scale, 14 days rather than the month-plus stay the National Biosecurity Agency reserves for countries it treats as rabies-affected.

    Nothing about that shortens the titre test itself, though, which applies to every pet regardless of where it started. Budget 12 to 16 weeks, most of it the vaccination-to-titre sequence and the NBA's own permit lead time, plus the quarantine stay once the pet actually lands on Mahe.

  sections:
    - heading: "Why Singapore likely gets Seychelles' lighter quarantine bracket"
      body: |
        Seychelles' NBA runs a blanket quarantine law: every pet entering the country goes into isolation, no exceptions by origin. What does vary by origin is the length, 14 days for a country the NBA treats as rabies-free or rabies-controlled, up to a month or more for one it treats as rabies-affected.

        Singapore has never recorded a rabies case and sits on both Australia's and New Zealand's own recognised rabies-free lists, the same company the UK keeps in most international schemes. That combination makes the 14-day bracket the reasonable planning assumption for this route. It isn't a confirmed NBA classification, though, since the agency's own published country list wasn't available in a format this research could verify directly, so treat it as the working assumption and confirm it with the NBA before locking in a quarantine slot.

    - heading: "The titre test doesn't care where you're from"
      body: |
        Unlike the quarantine length, the rabies titre test itself applies uniformly. Blood has to be drawn at least 30 days after vaccination and tested within 6 months of the planned departure, with a result above 0.5 IU/ml. Miss either end of that window and the whole test needs repeating, regardless of how clean Singapore's own rabies record is.

    - heading: "A disease panel Singapore's own vets are well placed to clear"
      body: |
        Beyond rabies, Seychelles asks dogs to clear Brucella canis, Trypanosoma evansi, Babesia gibsoni, Leishmania and Erlichia canis, unless the exporting country's vet certifies the country free of a given disease. Singapore's own tightly regulated veterinary environment and disease surveillance make that certification a realistic option worth raising with the AVS-endorsed vet early, rather than working through the full test panel by default.

    - heading: "Getting to Mahe from Changi"
      body: |
        No airline flies Singapore to Seychelles directly. Singapore Airlines covers the long-haul leg to Dubai or Abu Dhabi, where Emirates SkyCargo or Etihad Cargo take over for the final leg into Mahe, both hubs running dedicated live-animal facilities for the connection.
