content:
  h1: "Pet Transport from Tunisia to the United States"
  overview: |
    Tunisia sits on the CDC's high-risk list for dog rabies, checked directly on the agency's high-risk countries page, updated 15 April 2026. That single fact drives most of what follows: a dog cannot travel on a standard health certificate alone. A rabies serology titre from a CDC-approved laboratory and a government-endorsed Certification form are both required, on top of the microchip and vaccination steps every route needs.

    None of that applies to cats. A cat leaving Tunis needs a basic health certificate and can travel once it's fit to fly. For a dog, though, budget three to four months from a standing start, most of it spent waiting on the 30-day post-vaccination window and the laboratory turnaround, not on paperwork itself.

  sections:
    - heading: "What Tunisia's CDC high-risk status actually requires"
      body: |
        Being on the high-risk list is not a judgement about a specific dog's health. It is a country-level classification the CDC applies based on documented dog rabies transmission, and it changes the evidence a dog must carry to enter the US. Beyond the microchip and vaccination, a dog from Tunisia needs a passing rabies titre (0.5 IU/mL or higher), drawn at least 30 days after vaccination and at least 28 days before arrival, at a laboratory on the CDC's approved list rather than any accredited Tunisian lab.

        Alongside the titre, the Certification of Foreign Rabies Vaccination and Microchip form has to be signed by the treating vet and endorsed by a DGSV government vet. It is single-use and expires 30 days after the vet's signature, so the endorsement has to be timed against the travel date, not done far in advance out of convenience.

    - heading: "Landing at a CDC-registered facility"
      body: |
        A dog from a high-risk country can only enter the US at an airport where a CDC-registered animal care facility operates: JFK, ATL, ORD, MIA, EWR, LAX, SFO, IAH and BOS are among the current list. The facility examines the dog and checks the paperwork on arrival. With a passing titre already on file, the dog is released after that check. Without one, a 28-day quarantine follows at the owner's cost.

        Book the facility reservation before booking a flight, not after. Reservations at busier facilities fill up, and a confirmed flight with no facility slot at the other end leaves nowhere for the dog to land.

    - heading: "Getting from Tunis to a CDC airport"
      body: |
        There's no nonstop cargo or passenger service between Tunisia and the US, so every routing runs through a European or Turkish hub. Lufthansa flies TUN-FRA directly with onward connections to ORD, JFK and IAH; Air France flies TUN-CDG with transatlantic connections from Paris; Turkish Airlines runs TUN-IST with a wide network of US connections from Istanbul.

        Confirm live animal space on both legs of whichever routing is chosen before locking in a date. A transatlantic segment with no live animal capacity on the day will force a rebooking, and that can push the whole file outside the Certification form's 30-day window.
