  h1: "Pet Transport from United Arab Emirates to Tajikistan"
  overview: |
    Somon Air flies Dubai to Dushanbe nonstop, roughly three hours in the air. It's also the airline that will not carry a pet on that exact route. The carrier states plainly, in its own published guidance, that it does not accept animals for transport on the Dushanbe-Dubai sector, citing the UAE's own animal import restrictions as the reason.

    That single fact rules out the obvious booking and pushes every pet move onto a connecting itinerary instead, Turkish Airlines via Istanbul being the dependable option. Tajikistan's own entry rule is otherwise straightforward: no titre test from any origin, no import permit for a personal pet, just a current rabies vaccination and a UAE-endorsed certificate. Budget 6 to 9 weeks, most of it flight-booking and paperwork timing rather than anything unusually strict.

  sections:
    - heading: "The direct flight that isn't an option"
      body: |
        A quick flight search puts Somon Air's Dubai-Dushanbe service front and centre: nonstop, roughly three hours, Tajikistan's own national carrier. None of that matters for a pet. Somon Air's published animal transport guidance rules out carrying pets specifically on this sector, tied to the UAE's own restrictions on animal imports rather than anything about Tajikistan's side of the trip.

        Turkish Airlines steps in as the workable alternative, connecting Dubai to Dushanbe via Istanbul on roughly seven weekly onward flights. It's a longer journey than Somon Air's direct service would have been, but it's the one that actually takes the animal.

    - heading: "A lighter entry rule than the flight search suggests"
      body: |
        Once the airline question is settled, Tajikistan's own paperwork is genuinely manageable. No titre test applies from any country of origin, a real contrast with a Tajikistan-origin dog entering the US, which faces the CDC's full high-risk pathway in the opposite direction. No import permit is needed for an owner's own pet either.

        What does apply, more strictly than in several neighbouring Central Asian states, is the consequence of getting the paperwork wrong: quarantine, return to origin, or in the worst cases euthanasia, all at the owner's cost. The rules are light. The tolerance for missing them is not.
