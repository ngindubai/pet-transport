content:
  h1: "Pet Transport from Japan to Indonesia: 2026 Guide"
  overview: "Japan's rabies-free status is both an advantage and a constraint on the Japan-to-Indonesia route. Indonesia classifies Japan as a rabies-free country, which means the FAVN titre test requirements that apply to pets from rabies-positive countries do not apply. But it also means pets from Japan can only enter Indonesia through Jakarta (not Bali), and a minimum 14-day BARANTAN quarantine applies on arrival. Factor the quarantine into your planning timeline and destination."

  sections:
    - heading: "Why entry is restricted to Jakarta and what that means for your move"
      body: |
        Indonesia separates its territory into rabies-free and rabies-affected zones. Most of the Indonesian archipelago has rabies in its wildlife and dog populations. Bali has had significant rabies control issues in recent years. Because pets from rabies-free countries like Japan might become vectors for rabies if the protocol is not carefully controlled, Indonesia only allows them to enter through Jakarta, where proper quarantine facilities exist.

        If your destination is Bali, you need to arrive in Jakarta, complete the 14-day quarantine, and then arrange onward domestic travel. If your destination is elsewhere in Indonesia, check whether you can receive a pet from Jakarta via domestic air travel, and confirm that your destination province is within the BARANTAN-approved handling zone.

    - heading: "The BARANTAN process on arrival"
      body: |
        BARANTAN (Badan Karantina Pertanian, the Agricultural Quarantine Agency) manages the Jakarta quarantine facility at Soekarno-Hatta Airport. On arrival, your pet is taken to the facility while you complete documentation.

        For Japan-origin pets (rabies-free category), the quarantine is a minimum of 14 days. The facility inspects the pet daily. If the pet remains healthy throughout, it is released at the end of the quarantine period with a BARANTAN clearance certificate.

        Visit the quarantine facility before or after the quarantine period is possible in some cases; confirm with BARANTAN in advance whether you can visit during the quarantine. The facility is accredited and the standard of care is generally adequate, but it varies. Bringing familiar bedding and toys within BARANTAN's rules can help your pet settle.

    - heading: "The Japan-side process: AQS export inspection"
      body: |
        MAFF's Animal Quarantine Service is straightforward. Apply via the NACCS system online at least 10 days before your preferred inspection date. Bring your pet to the airport cargo facility on inspection day. The AQS officer checks the microchip, reviews the vaccination and health records, confirms the pet appears healthy and that no rabies has been reported in the area in the past 4 months, and issues the export certificate.

        The certificate specifically needs to state the area of origin is rabies-free and has had no rabies in the past 4 months. This is Japan's standard AQS certificate, which includes these statements by default.

  - question: "Does my pet have to quarantine in Jakarta even if I am moving to Bali?"
    answer: "Yes. All pets from rabies-free countries entering Indonesia must enter via Jakarta and complete the BARANTAN quarantine there. After the 14-day quarantine and clearance, you can arrange onward domestic travel to Bali. This adds time and cost to the move. There is no exemption for specific Indonesian islands or provinces."
  - question: "How long does the full process from Japan to Indonesia take?"
    answer: "Allow 8 to 14 weeks total. The BARANTAN import permit can take several weeks. The AQS inspection must be booked at least 10 days in advance. The quarantine itself is a minimum of 14 days on arrival. Start the permit application as early as possible."
  - question: "What is the BARANTAN import permit and how do I apply?"
    answer: "The BARANTAN import permit is a document from Indonesia's Agricultural Quarantine Agency authorising the import of your specific pet. Apply through BARANTAN's official portal or via a licensed Indonesian import agent. You need this permit before travel. Without it, BARANTAN cannot process your pet's arrival at Jakarta airport."
  - question: "Can I visit my pet during the 14-day quarantine in Jakarta?"
    answer: "Contact BARANTAN directly about their current visitor policy. Some facilities allow limited visits from owners during quarantine periods; others do not during the initial health observation phase. Confirm before arrival."
