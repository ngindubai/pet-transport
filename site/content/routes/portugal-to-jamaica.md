content:
  h1: "Pet Transport from Portugal to Jamaica: 2026 Field Guide"
  overview: "Jamaica requires more preparation than most Caribbean destinations. The VSD mandates an import permit applied for 8-12 weeks before travel, a mandatory 14-day quarantine at Plumb Point in Kingston, and two rounds each of internal and external parasite treatment with precise timing. The second external treatment must be given within 48 hours of departure, which makes scheduling everything around a single cargo booking date essential. Start the process at least three months before you plan to fly."

  sections:
    - heading: "The import permit: apply before anything else"
      body: |
        Before you book a flight, order a crate, or schedule a vet appointment, apply for the VSD import permit. Email vsdpermits@moa.gov.jm with your pet's species, breed, age, sex, microchip number, and country of origin (Portugal). The VSD may request additional documentation. Allow 8-12 weeks from application to receiving the permit. The fee is J$5,000 (approximately USD 32-35).

        Jamaica maintains a restricted breeds list. Dogo Argentino, Fila Brasileiro, Japanese Tosa, and Pit Bull Terrier face import restrictions. If your dog belongs to one of these breeds, or could be interpreted as a cross, contact the VSD directly before applying. Entry will not be granted at the airport if your breed is restricted.

        Once the permit is confirmed in writing, finalise your travel date and book cargo from Lisbon (LIS) or Porto (OPO) to Norman Manley International Airport (KIN) in Kingston. The most practical route is via London Heathrow with British Airways World Cargo, or via Madrid on Iberia.

    - heading: "Parasite treatments: timing drives your preparation calendar"
      body: |
        The VSD's parasite treatment protocol requires two rounds each of internal and external treatments, 14 days apart. The second internal treatment must be within 7 days of transport. The second external treatment must be within 48 hours of departure.

        Work backward from your cargo departure date. If your flight leaves on a Wednesday, the second external treatment happens on Monday or Tuesday. The first external treatment is 14 days earlier. Schedule both vet appointments before confirming your cargo booking. If a flight date changes, the parasite cycle may need to restart.

        Your vet must document both rounds with the product used, dose, and date of application. These records travel with your pet and are inspected at KIN and at Plumb Point.
