content:
  h1: "Pet Transport from Ireland to Lebanon"
  overview: |
    Lebanon does not require a titre test for EU-origin pets, and there is no quarantine for compliant arrivals at Beirut Rafic Hariri International Airport. The export process from Ireland is the standard DAFM EU health certificate route.

    The one requirement that commonly causes delays is Lebanon's 30-day pre-entry rabies vaccination rule. It is 30 days, not the 21-day minimum used in many other countries. Check your pet's vaccination date before setting a travel date.

    Also review current Irish government travel advice for Lebanon before booking. The situation at Beirut airport can affect cargo operations, and it is sensible to confirm that services are running normally before committing to dates.

  sections:
    - heading: "What Lebanon requires at the border"
      body: |
        Lebanon's veterinary import requirements are administered by the Ministry of Agriculture. Officers inspect documentation at Beirut Rafic Hariri International Airport (BEY) on arrival.

        The core requirements are a valid rabies vaccination administered at least 30 days before entry, an official health certificate issued within 10 days of travel, and an ISO-compliant microchip. Lebanon does not require a titre test for EU-origin pets. No quarantine applies for compliant arrivals. For 1-2 personal companion animals, no import permit is required.

    - heading: "DAFM health certificate process from Ireland"
      body: |
        Ireland's export health certificate for non-EU destinations is the EU-format official veterinary certificate signed by a DAFM-registered official vet and endorsed by DAFM (Department of Agriculture, Food and the Marine). Both steps must complete within 10 days of departure.

        Book the vet examination within 10 days of your travel date. After signing, allow 1-2 working days for DAFM endorsement. Your regular vet can handle vaccinations and microchipping, but the official health certificate must come from a DAFM-registered official vet. Check gov.ie/agriculture for the list of authorised official vets.

    - heading: "Cargo routing from Dublin to Beirut"
      body: |
        There is no direct cargo service from Dublin (DUB) to Beirut (BEY). Turkish Airlines Cargo via Istanbul (IST) is the primary option. Turkish Airlines operates DUB-IST regularly, and IST to BEY is a well-served route.

        Book through an approved cargo agent. Confirm pet acceptance on both the DUB-IST and IST-BEY legs. If travelling June to September, check for temperature embargoes. An alternative for the Lebanon leg is Middle East Airlines (MEA) routing via London Heathrow: DUB to LHR, then LHR to BEY on MEA. Confirm live animal cargo acceptance on all legs with your cargo agent before committing.
