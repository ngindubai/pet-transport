  sections:
    - heading: "Routing from Tallinn to Entebbe"
      body: |
        Tallinn Airport (TLL) is a regional Baltic airport with limited direct connections to major cargo hubs. Pet cargo to Uganda from Estonia requires at least two legs from Tallinn.

        AirBaltic connects TLL to Riga (RIX) and from there to Amsterdam (AMS) or Frankfurt (FRA). From AMS, a connection to Brussels Zaventem (BRU) gives access to Brussels Airlines' established BRU-EBB East Africa service. From FRA, Ethiopian Cargo or Lufthansa Cargo connects to Addis Ababa (ADD), then Ethiopian Cargo on ADD-EBB.

        A pet transport agent experienced in Baltic exports is essential for this route. They will coordinate live animal cargo acceptance on each leg, manage documentation across multiple carriers, and ensure correct handling through European cargo hubs. Do not attempt to arrange this routing independently without specialist knowledge.

    - heading: "Agricultural Board export health certificate for Uganda"
      body: |
        The Estonian EU pet passport applies to intra-EU travel only. For Uganda, the required document is an official health certificate issued by an authorised official veterinarian and endorsed by the Põllumajandusamet (Agricultural Board of Estonia) at agri.ee.

        Contact the Agricultural Board to identify an authorised official vet and understand the endorsement process. The certificate must be issued within 10 days of travel. Given the longer total journey from Estonia, time the vet appointment and endorsement carefully.

        Confirm the certificate format meets Uganda's specific requirements. Your pet transport agent can help verify this for the MAAIF inspection at Entebbe.

    - heading: "MAAIF: Uganda's import permit requirement"
      body: |
        Uganda's Ministry of Agriculture, Animal Industry and Fisheries (MAAIF) at maaif.go.ug manages companion animal imports. The MAAIF permit is the prerequisite document for Uganda entry. Apply at least 8-10 weeks before travel with your pet's microchip number, species, breed, vaccination history, and travel date.

        Follow up after submitting to confirm progress. On arrival at Entebbe International Airport, MAAIF inspects the permit alongside the Agricultural Board health certificate. No quarantine applies for compliant arrivals.
