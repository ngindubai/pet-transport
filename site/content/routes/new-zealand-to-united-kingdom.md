  h1: "Pet Transport from New Zealand to the UK"
  overview: |
    New Zealand to the UK is a long journey, both physically and administratively. The flight alone takes 22-27 hours including connections. But the compliance requirements are simpler than the distance might suggest: no titre test, no quarantine, and New Zealand's strong biosecurity reputation means the UK's APHA processes compliant NZ pets quickly.

    The main coordination points are the AHC (Animal Health Certificate, issued by an MPI-authorised vet), the tapeworm treatment timing for dogs, and choosing a routing that minimises transit handling for a very long journey.
  sections:
    - heading: "New Zealand as a GB-listed country: what it means"
      body: |
        Great Britain maintains a list of countries from which pets can enter without a titre test or quarantine, provided they have a current microchip and rabies vaccination. New Zealand is on this list.

        That means no 3-month titre test waiting period, no quarantine on arrival, and no special holding facilities. A compliant New Zealand pet arrives at Heathrow, goes through APHA inspection, and goes home with you.

        The paperwork is more involved than pre-Brexit EU travel, but far simpler than a non-listed country move.
    - heading: "The tapeworm timing challenge on a 27-hour journey"
      body: |
        Dogs travelling to Great Britain must receive a tapeworm treatment between 24 and 120 hours before arrival. For a route as long as Auckland-London, this timing requires careful calculation.

        If your pet arrives at Heathrow at 06:00, count back 24-120 hours from that moment. That puts the treatment window between 3 days and 5 days before the Heathrow landing. If you're leaving Auckland on Tuesday morning, the treatment likely happens on the Saturday or Sunday before departure.

        Your vet in Auckland records the treatment date and time in the AHC. The APHA inspector at Heathrow checks the arithmetic. Get this right before you fly.
    - heading: "Choosing your Auckland-to-London routing"
      body: |
        There is no direct Auckland-London flight. Every NZ-UK pet move involves at least one connection. The main options:

        Via Singapore (SIN): Singapore Airlines cargo from AKL or CHC to SIN, connecting to LHR. Singapore's Changi has good live animal handling, and SIN-LHR is a major SQ route. One transfer.

        Via Hong Kong (HKG): Cathay Pacific from AKL to HKG, connecting to LHR. One transfer. Similar total journey time to the Singapore routing.

        Via Dubai (DXB): Emirates from AKL to DXB to LHR. Popular with expat families. Good temperature control throughout.

        Via Los Angeles (LAX): Air New Zealand AKL-LAX, then a US carrier to LHR. More complex as it involves clearing US customs before connecting. Generally not recommended for pets.

        For pets, fewer transfers are better. Singapore and Hong Kong routings both give a single transfer with experienced live animal handling at the transit airport.
