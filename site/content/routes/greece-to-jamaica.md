content:
  h1: "Pet Transport Greece to Jamaica"
  overview: |
    The regulatory sequence, not the flight, sets the timeline on this route. Jamaica's Veterinary Services Division works in a fixed order: a Preliminary Application Form has to clear before an owner can even start the rabies vaccination and FAVN titre test steps that count towards entry. Get that timing right and the actual journey, via London or Amsterdam, adds only a day or two to the total.

  sections:
    - heading: "Why the Preliminary Application comes before anything else"
      body: |
        VSD requires this form approved before an owner begins the vaccination and titre test sequence that counts towards Jamaican entry. Vaccinating or booking the titre test too soon risks the whole file being rejected outright. Realistically, build in 4-5 months from the very first step, regardless of how straightforward the flight itself will be.

    - heading: "The titre test and what happens at Plumb Point"
      body: |
        A FAVN titre test, drawn 30 days after the qualifying rabies vaccination at a VSD-approved laboratory, needs to clear 0.5 IU/ml. A first-time rabies vaccination in a pet over 12 months old doesn't qualify for entry until three months after that dose, layered on top of the titre wait. Quarantine at Plumb Point runs up to 14 days, with VSD inspectors holding discretion to release a healthy, well-documented pet sooner, though owners should plan for the full period.

    - heading: "Getting from Athens to Kingston"
      body: |
        No direct service exists, so British Airways via London Heathrow is the most established Caribbean cargo routing available to a Greek departure, with KLM via Amsterdam as a solid second option. Once the Preliminary Application, titre test and import permit are all in order, the journey itself is one of the more straightforward legs in the whole process.
