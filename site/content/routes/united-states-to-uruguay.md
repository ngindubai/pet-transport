content:
  h1: "Pet Transport United States to Uruguay"
  overview: |
    Dogs need a specific parasite treatment here that Panama and the Dominican Republic don't ask for: a praziquantel-based echinococcus treatment, timed to within 72 hours to 30 days of arrival. It's a narrow window, and it's the detail most likely to catch out an owner who's already moved a pet to a similar Latin American destination and assumes the rules are the same.

    Beyond that, Uruguay follows a familiar pattern for this region: no titre test, but an import permit from MGAP is required, which is a step Panama skips entirely. The actual flight runs through Miami or a South American hub, since there's no direct US-Montevideo cargo service.

  sections:
    - heading: "The echinococcus treatment: specific to dogs, specific to Uruguay"
      body: |
        Dogs entering Uruguay need documented treatment against Echinococcus (Taenia), using a praziquantel-based product, administered no less than 72 hours and no more than 30 days before arrival. Cats don't need this step. It's separate from the rabies vaccination timeline entirely, so track it as its own date rather than assuming it lines up automatically with the rest of the schedule.

    - heading: "MGAP's import permit, and why it's not optional here"
      body: |
        Uruguay requires an import permit from MGAP-DGSG before a pet can enter the country, a step that some neighbouring Latin American destinations, Panama among them, don't require at all. Apply for this well ahead of your travel date rather than assuming Uruguay's no-titre-test policy means a lighter overall process.

    - heading: "USDA's side, and getting to Montevideo"
      body: |
        A USDA-accredited vet examination and APHIS endorsement complete the US side of the paperwork, issued within 10 days of travel to match Uruguay's own certificate validity window. There's no direct cargo flight from the US into Montevideo, so American Airlines Cargo via Miami or LATAM Cargo via Sao Paulo or Santiago are the realistic options, both drawing on established South American networks.
