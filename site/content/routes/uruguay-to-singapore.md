  overview: |
    Montevideo and Singapore sit on close to opposite sides of the planet, and the flight itself reflects that: at least two connections, most workably via Sao Paulo and a Gulf hub, before the pet even reaches Changi. Few routes on this site cover this much ground in one journey.

    The regulatory side follows the pattern most of Uruguay's non-EU, non-North American routes share here. AVS's Schedule III applies by default, since Uruguay sits on neither of the shorter Schedule I or Schedule II lists, bringing a titre test, a 90-day wait, and 30 days at Singapore's Animal Quarantine Centre on arrival.

  sections:
    - heading: "A genuinely long-haul route, even before the paperwork starts"
      body: |
        Uruguay's own long-haul network runs through Sao Paulo or Buenos Aires for most international connections, and reaching Singapore adds a further leg through a Gulf or another Asian hub on top of that. Qatar Airways' expanded 2026 South American network, including its Sao Paulo to Montevideo sector, offers one plausible single-carrier chain: Montevideo to Sao Paulo, Sao Paulo to Doha, Doha to Singapore. LATAM's own dominant regional network is the practical alternative, connecting to Emirates, Qatar Airways or Singapore Airlines at whichever hub has capacity.

        Whichever combination is chosen, confirm live animal cargo acceptance on every individual sector rather than assuming a through-booking covers it. A multi-leg journey across two oceans has more points where a schedule mismatch or a capacity limit can hold up the pet than a simpler two-leg route does.

    - heading: "Schedule III and the 90-day clock"
      body: |
        AVS names only four Schedule I countries and around 31 Schedule II countries. Uruguay appears on neither, so it defaults to Schedule III, the tier that covers most of Latin America. That means a WOAH-prescribed titre test, blood drawn at least 28 days after vaccination, with a result of at least 0.5 IU/ml, valid for export between 90 days and 12 months after the draw date.

        Run the AVS import licence application and the AQC quarantine booking alongside this wait rather than after it, since both steps have their own processing time on top.

    - heading: "MGAP's certificate, timed against a genuinely long journey"
      body: |
        MGAP, Uruguay's Ministry of Livestock, Agriculture and Fisheries, endorses the export health certificate through its Dirección General de Servicios Ganaderos (DGSG). AVS expects that certificate completed between 2 and 7 days of the export date. Given how many connections this route involves, book the vet appointment against a firm, fully-confirmed itinerary, since a certificate signed before every leg is locked in risks falling outside AVS's accepted window if the flight schedule shifts.
