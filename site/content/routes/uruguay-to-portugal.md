  overview: |
    Lisbon is one of the more reachable European capitals for a Uruguayan pet, thanks to TAP Air Portugal's São Paulo connection, but reachable is not the same as fast. Uruguay carries no EU rabies-control equivalence, so Portugal applies the standard unlisted third country process: FAVN titre test, a fixed 90-day wait from the blood draw, and an EU-format certificate from MGAP-DGSG.

    Five to seven months is the realistic window from a standing start. The bottleneck is never the flight, it is the sequencing of vaccination, blood draw and wait period, each of which has to land in the right order before DGAV will clear a Lisbon arrival.

  sections:
    - heading: "Why Portugal treats Uruguay the same as any other unlisted country"
      body: |
        Portugal follows the same EU framework as every other member state: countries the EU has formally assessed as having equivalent rabies control skip the titre test, and countries it has not assessed do not. Uruguay sits in the unassessed group, so the full sequence applies regardless of the individual pet's health record.

        That means microchip before vaccination, a 30-day wait before the blood draw, a FAVN or ELISA result of at least 0.5 IU/ml, and then 90 days from the draw date before DGAV will admit the pet at Lisbon Airport.
    - heading: "The São Paulo connection and why it matters for this route"
      body: |
        TAP Air Portugal's direct São Paulo-to-Lisbon service gives Uruguay-origin pets one of the shorter total transit options in this build, once cargo reaches São Paulo from Montevideo via Air Europa or LATAM. That said, capacity on the South America-to-Portugal corridor is not deep, so book both legs together rather than assuming a same-week connection will be available.

        Madrid and Paris remain workable alternatives if the São Paulo routing does not align with your travel window, at the cost of an extra connection.
