  sections:
    - heading: "Portugal's EU unlisted country requirements"
      body: |
        Portugal's DGAV (Direccao-Geral de Alimentacao e Veterinaria, dgav.pt) administers pet imports under Commission Delegated Regulation (EU) 2026/131. For pets from unlisted third countries such as Uruguay, the requirements are: ISO 11784/11785 microchip implanted before the rabies vaccination; rabies vaccination with 21 days elapsed since the primary dose; FAVN titre test at an EU-approved laboratory with a result of 0.5 IU/ml or above, blood drawn at least 30 days after vaccination; 90-day wait from the blood draw date; and an EU Annex IV non-commercial health certificate issued within 10 days of travel, endorsed by Uruguay's DGSG authority.

        No quarantine. No import permit. Border inspection at Lisbon Humberto Delgado (LIS) or Porto Francisco Sa Carneiro (OPO) is standard for compliant arrivals. Inspectors check the health certificate, microchip, titre test result (with blood draw date visible), and vaccination record.

    - heading: "FAVN titre test coordination from Uruguay"
      body: |
        The FAVN test requires an EU-approved laboratory. Most of these laboratories are in Europe or North America. From Uruguay, your MGAP-registered vet draws the blood, stores it in the correct preservative medium (typically sodium azide), and ships it to the laboratory by international biomedical courier.

        ANSES in France (lda.anses.fr) is one of the most commonly used EU-approved laboratories for South American samples. TAP Air Portugal's hub at Lisbon makes France a particularly practical option: cargo from Lisbon connects directly back to Paris CDG, and samples shipped from Uruguay to Paris can go through GRU or MVD. Ask the laboratory about their sample acceptance requirements and typical processing time. Allow two to four weeks from the blood draw for shipping and processing.

        The 90-day mandatory wait starts from the blood draw date. If the result comes back positive (0.5 IU/ml or above) on day 14, the wait still runs to day 90 from the blood draw. This wait cannot be shortened.

    - heading: "Routing from Montevideo to Lisbon"
      body: |
        The MVD-to-Lisbon routing is relatively straightforward by South American standards. The most direct option runs: MVD or Buenos Aires (EZE) to Madrid (MAD) via Iberia, then MAD to LIS, which is a one-hour cargo hop. Iberia and TAP both serve MAD-LIS with live animal cargo capacity.

        LATAM Airlines connects MVD to GRU or SCL, from where transatlantic services to Madrid, Paris, or Frankfurt connect to Lisbon. TAP Air Portugal operates direct GRU-LIS flights, which is worth considering if you are routing through Sao Paulo, as it reduces the number of cargo transfers.

        From Paris CDG, TAP or Air France connect to LIS. From Frankfurt, TAP or Lufthansa connect to LIS. Lisbon is a well-connected European hub. Book cargo space early, confirm live animal acceptance on each segment, and use a freight forwarder familiar with South American routes.

    - heading: "MGAP-DGSG export documentation"
      body: |
        The Direccion General de Servicios Ganaderos (DGSG), part of Uruguay's Ministerio de Ganaderia, Agricultura y Pesca (MGAP, mgap.gub.uy), endorses companion animal export health certificates. A MGAP-registered vet prepares the EU Annex IV format certificate, referencing the titre test result (with laboratory name, blood draw date, and result in IU/ml), the vaccination record, and the microchip number.

        The DGSG endorses the document after review. Allow three to five business days for this step. The certificate must be issued within 10 days of travel and expires quickly. Do not have it prepared too early. Carry the original (not a copy) with your pet's travel documents.

faqs:
  - question: "Can my pet enter Portugal through Porto as well as Lisbon?"
    answer: "Yes. Porto Francisco Sa Carneiro (OPO) has a DGAV border inspection post and accepts pet arrivals from non-EU countries. If your cargo routing connects through Spain (via Iberia or TAP), OPO may be a viable alternative to LIS. Confirm with DGAV that the specific flight's arrival at OPO will be inspected."
  - question: "Does Portugal require tapeworm treatment for dogs from Uruguay?"
    answer: "No. Portugal does not have an Echinococcus treatment requirement for dogs arriving from South America. This is different from Norway and Finland, which both require it. For Uruguay-to-Portugal, no tapeworm treatment is needed."
  - question: "Is TAP Air Portugal a good option for shipping a pet from Uruguay?"
    answer: "TAP operates from Lisbon to South American destinations including GRU (Sao Paulo) and potentially connections from other cities. If the routing works, a GRU-LIS TAP service reduces the cargo transfers to one. TAP Cargo accepts live animals on approved routes. Confirm live animal availability on the specific flight before committing."
  - question: "What does the DGAV inspection involve at Lisbon?"
    answer: "DGAV inspectors at LIS check the EU Annex IV health certificate (DGSG-endorsed), the vaccination record, the FAVN titre test result and blood draw date, and scan the microchip. For compliant pets, the inspection is routine and typically takes 30-60 minutes after the cargo arrives and is unloaded. The cargo agent will advise on the collection process after clearance."
