  sections:
    - heading: "Understanding the 30-day rule for rabies vaccination"
      body: |
        Cambodia requires that the rabies vaccination be given at least 30 days before entry. This is not simply about the vaccination being current: the 30-day lead time is a separate requirement. If your pet's most recent rabies booster was given 25 days before your travel date, it does not meet the Cambodian requirement, even if the vaccination is otherwise valid.

        There is also a note on multi-year vaccinations. Some countries accept 3-year rabies vaccines, and these are commonly used in the UK. Cambodia accepts multi-year vaccines, but the vaccine must have been administered within the previous 12 months. If your dog received a 3-year rabies vaccination 14 months ago, it may not be accepted, regardless of what the certificate says about the expiry date. Check this specific point with your vet well before travel.

        The safest approach is to ensure the most recent rabies vaccination was given within the previous 12 months and at least 30 days before your travel date.

    - heading: "Getting to Phnom Penh from the UK"
      body: |
        There are no direct UK-to-Cambodia flights. All routes involve connections, and the main hubs for Southeast Asia cargo are Bangkok (Suvarnabhumi, BKK), Singapore (Changi, SIN), and Hong Kong (HKG). Some routes go through Chinese hubs (Guangzhou or Shanghai).

        Bangkok is the most common connection point for Cambodia. Thai Airways, Bangkok Airways, and some cargo operators serve the BKK-PNH sector with reasonable frequency. Singapore Airlines connects via SIN. Routing through Bangkok adds a transit step but is generally well-handled for live animals.

        Confirm the transit arrangements carefully. Cambodia requires that live animals entering as cargo meet all health requirements. The transit-country rules (Thailand, Singapore) for animals in transit airside generally do not require clearance, but confirm with your airline.
