  sections:
    - heading: "Bangkok vs Singapore vs Doha: comparing the transit hubs"
      body: |
        No direct flights run from the UK to Cambodia. The main transit options each have different characteristics worth comparing.

        Bangkok Suvarnabhumi (BKK) on Thai Airways is the most direct Southeast Asian connection to Phnom Penh and Siem Reap, and the transit is relatively short. Bangkok has well-established cargo animal handling. Singapore Changi (SIN) on Singapore Airlines offers arguably the highest standard of animal cargo handling in the region; Singapore Airlines is consistently rated among the best for live animal transport.

        Doha on Qatar Airways is a Gulf hub alternative that avoids Southeast Asian transit hub complexity. Kuala Lumpur on Malaysia Airlines is a strong option for Phnom Penh connections and gives reasonable frequencies from the UK.

        For journey time and animal welfare, Singapore Airlines via Changi is the premium choice. For simplicity and cost, Thai Airways via Bangkok is often the most practical.

    - heading: "Cambodia's MAFF permit and local veterinary planning"
      body: |
        Cambodia's Ministry of Agriculture, Forestry and Fisheries handles the import permit process. Apply four to six weeks before travel, providing the animal's vaccination history, microchip number, and origin documentation. Processing times are reasonably consistent but can extend if additional documentation is requested.

        Once in Cambodia, keep your expectations for local veterinary care calibrated to what is available. Phnom Penh has several international-standard vet practices. Siem Reap is more limited. If you are moving to a rural area, identify a veterinarian before you arrive.
