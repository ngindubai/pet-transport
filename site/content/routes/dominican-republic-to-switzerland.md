  h1: "Pet Transport Dominican Republic to Switzerland: 2026 Guide"
  overview: |
    Moving a pet from the Dominican Republic to Switzerland requires planning well in advance, and the process is longer than most owners anticipate. Switzerland is not a member of the European Union, but it aligns its animal import rules closely with EU standards. For pets arriving from unlisted third countries such as the Dominican Republic, this means the same FAVN titre test and 90-day waiting period that applies across the EU. The full process from microchip to travel day takes a minimum of five to six months.

    The Swiss authority responsible for animal import is the FSVO, known in German as BLV (Bundesamt fur Lebensmittelsicherheit und Veterinarwesen, blv.admin.ch). The health certificate required for entry is in Swiss format, which is a specific distinction from the standard EU Animal Health Certificate used for EU member states. Your DIGEGA-authorised vet in the Dominican Republic will need to issue the correct form, and DIGEGA must endorse it before departure. The certificate is valid for 10 days.

    The cargo route from Santo Domingo (SDQ) to Zurich Airport (ZRH) operates via a European hub. The main options are Madrid (MAD) on Iberia, Paris CDG on Air France, and Amsterdam (AMS) on KLM. Zurich is well served from all three hubs, and ZRH has a dedicated animal reception facility. Engaging a professional pet transport agent is strongly recommended for this route, particularly to manage the titre test coordination and ensure the correct Swiss health certificate format is used.

  sections:
    - heading: "Why the Dominican Republic needs a titre test for Switzerland entry"
      body: |
        Switzerland independently classifies third countries for pet import purposes, and its approach mirrors the EU framework closely. Countries with which Switzerland has a recognised veterinary arrangement benefit from a simplified process. The Dominican Republic is not in that group, and pets from the DR are therefore subject to the full unlisted country requirements: a FAVN or ELISA titre test at an EU-approved laboratory, a minimum result of 0.5 IU/ml, and a mandatory 90-day wait from the date the laboratory confirms the passing result.

        The titre test process begins with a blood draw in the Dominican Republic, at least 30 days after the rabies vaccination. The sample must be shipped to an EU-approved laboratory for processing, which typically takes two to four weeks including transit. Once the laboratory confirms the result, the 90-day clock starts. This period cannot be reduced by any means, including additional vaccinations or booster doses.

        One important practical point: Switzerland's entry requirement is a Swiss-format health certificate, not the EU Animal Health Certificate (AHC) used across EU member states. If your DIGEGA-authorised vet issues the standard EU AHC by mistake, Swiss border authorities may not accept it. Confirm the required format with both your vet and the FSVO/BLV before the certificate is prepared.

    - heading: "Routing and documentation from Santo Domingo"
      body: |
        Las Americas International Airport (SDQ) in Santo Domingo is the primary departure point for international cargo from the Dominican Republic. Punta Cana (PUJ) is an alternative if you are based in the east of the island, though onward connections from PUJ to Central Europe are more limited. For most routes to Switzerland, SDQ is the practical choice.

        The three main hub routings to Zurich are: SDQ to Madrid (MAD) on Iberia, with a connection to ZRH; SDQ to Paris CDG on Air France, with a connection to ZRH; and SDQ to Amsterdam (AMS) on KLM, with a connection to ZRH. All three hubs have strong onward services into Zurich. Confirm live animal cargo acceptance with the airline on each segment before booking, as policies on live animals can vary between the long-haul and European legs of the journey.

        The DIGEGA export process requires a DIGEGA-authorised vet to examine your pet and issue the Swiss-format certificate, followed by DIGEGA endorsement. Endorsement typically takes one to two working days. Since the certificate is valid for only 10 days from issue, the timing of the vet appointment and DIGEGA visit must be planned carefully around your departure date.

    - heading: "How long does it take to move a pet from the Dominican Republic to Switzerland?"
      body: |
        From start to finish, the minimum realistic timeline is 20 to 24 weeks. The sequence runs as follows: microchip implantation, rabies vaccination, a minimum 30-day wait before the blood draw, two to four weeks for laboratory processing, and then a full 90 days from the date of the passing titre test result. That sequence alone requires roughly 18 to 20 weeks under ideal conditions.

        On top of this, factor in the time to identify an EU-approved laboratory, arrange international sample shipping from the Dominican Republic, book cargo space on the airline, and complete the DIGEGA endorsement process within the 10-day certificate window. Beginning preparations six months before your intended travel date is the sensible approach.
