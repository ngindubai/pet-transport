Both countries operate under the same EU pet travel rules, so the documentation is straightforward: microchip, current rabies vaccination, and an EU pet passport or AHC.   sections:
    - heading: "Carrier options from Riga to Spain"
      body: |
        Riga Airport (RIX) has regular connections to Spanish destinations including Madrid, Barcelona, Alicante, and others. Ryanair operates many of these routes at low cost, but they do not accept pets. Wizz Air similarly serves Riga-Spain routes without pet acceptance.

        For a pet owner, the viable options are: airBaltic connecting from RIX via its network to Spain, Lufthansa routing via Frankfurt (FRA) to Madrid or Barcelona, or LOT Polish Airlines routing via Warsaw (WAW). airBaltic is the most convenient starting point since RIX is its hub and connections to Spain are part of its scheduled network.

        For a large dog that cannot travel in-cabin, the Lufthansa via Frankfurt routing provides the best cargo option on this corridor. Frankfurt's Animal Lounge handles the intermediate stop professionally.

    - heading: "Spain's domestic dog requirements after arrival"
      body: |
        Relocating to Spain with a dog involves a domestic registration step that is separate from the EU pet passport. Spain operates a national dog register (Registro de Animales de Compania / Anagrafe Canina) and dogs must be registered locally with the Ayuntamiento (town hall) of your municipality, or through the local Colegio Oficial de Veterinarios (veterinary college).

        The microchip number from your Latvian EU passport is used for Spanish registration. In some regions of Spain, the local process may involve a vet visit to verify the chip before issuing the Spanish registration certificate.

        Cats are not subject to mandatory national registration in Spain, though some Spanish regions have introduced voluntary or mandatory cat registration schemes. Check the rules for your specific destination.

faqs:
  - question: "I am moving from Riga to Barcelona permanently. Do I need to re-register my dog in Spain?"
    answer: "Yes. Dogs residing in Spain must be registered with the local Spanish authorities. The registration uses your dog's existing microchip number and is linked to your Spanish address and contact details. In Catalonia (where Barcelona is), the Colegio de Veterinarios de Barcelona manages the AIAC registry. Your Spanish vet can help you complete this at your first local appointment."
  - question: "Does my dog need its Latvian vaccinations translated for Spanish vets?"
    answer: "EU pet passports are issued in a multilingual format. Your Latvian vet enters vaccination information using standardised EU format fields. Spanish vets and the Spanish Anagrafe Canina accept the EU pet passport directly without translation. If your vaccination history is recorded only in Latvian clinical records (not in the passport), your vet can summarise the relevant information in the passport to make it clear."
  - question: "Can I bring my pets on the ferry from Lithuania to Sweden and then drive through Europe to Spain?"
    answer: "Yes, overland and sea travel within the EU with an EU pet passport is permitted. You can use a ferry (for example from Riga or Tallinn to Stockholm), drive through Sweden, Denmark, Germany, France, and into Spain. Your EU pet passport covers the entire journey within EU member states. Individual ferry operators have their own pet policies for cabin access, so confirm with the ferry company whether your pet can stay in a cabin or must remain in the vehicle deck."
