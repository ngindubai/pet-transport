content:
  h1: "Pet Transport from Bolivia to Canada"
  overview: |
    One word does a lot of work in CFIA's rules for this route: commercial. Bolivia sits on the agency's list of countries considered high-risk for dog rabies, and since September 2022 CFIA has banned entry from those countries for commercial dogs, animals meant for resale, adoption, fostering, breeding, show or research. A personal pet, travelling with its owner, isn't one of those.

    Strip that restriction out and what's left is genuinely one of the lighter destinations a dog leaving Bolivia can travel to. No titre test. No fixed waiting period tied to a blood draw. Just a 7-month minimum age, a correctly timed microchip and vaccination, and SENASAG's usual export paperwork.

  sections:
    - heading: "Why the high-risk list matters less than it sounds"
      body: |
        A country appearing on a rabies high-risk list tends to signal a demanding process, titre tests, long waits, sometimes a facility booking, as it does for dogs entering the US or the UAE from Bolivia. Canada's version of that same list works differently. The restriction it triggers is narrow and explicit: commercial dogs only. CFIA's own September 2022 notice spells this out directly.

        For a family moving a personal dog or cat, that means the high-risk classification changes almost nothing about the actual process. It doesn't add a titre test. It doesn't add a waiting period. What it does add is a minimum age, 7 months, and a microchip-before-vaccination sequencing rule that dogs from high-risk countries specifically have to follow.

    - heading: "Cats have an even lighter path"
      body: |
        None of the age minimum, the microchip sequencing rule or the high-risk-country framework applies to cats at all. A cat leaving Bolivia for Canada needs a current health certificate and the standard SENASAG export paperwork, nothing tied to Bolivia's rabies classification specifically. That's a meaningfully lighter position than cats get on some other routes this build has covered.

    - heading: "The flight, in two legs"
      body: |
        Copa Airlines' Panama City hub is the standard connection between Santa Cruz Viru Viru and Canadian gateways like Toronto. Boliviana de Aviación's Miami service offers a workable alternative for owners whose onward Canadian connection runs more easily through the US. Either way, expect two legs rather than one, since nothing flies nonstop between the two countries.
