      body: |
        Most European pet transport routes to Azerbaijan go via Istanbul using Turkish Airlines. Germany has an additional option: AZAL (Azerbaijan Airlines) operates a direct Frankfurt (FRA) to Baku (GYD) service, which cuts out the Istanbul connection. This can simplify the cargo logistics and reduce transit time for your pet.

        However, AZAL's live animal cargo acceptance policy needs to be confirmed directly with the airline before you build your plans around this option. AZAL does accept live animal cargo on some routes, but the conditions, temperature restrictions, and booking procedures are not as publicly documented as Turkish Airlines Cargo. Contact AZAL's cargo division and get written confirmation of acceptance before booking.

        If AZAL is not accepting live animals on your travel dates or for your pet's size, the Turkish Airlines route via Istanbul (FRA-IST-GYD) is a reliable alternative. Turkish Airlines Cargo has well-established procedures for live animals on this corridor.

    - heading: "What to expect on arrival at Baku's Heydar Aliyev Airport"
      body: |
        Heydar Aliyev International Airport (GYD) is Azerbaijan's main international gateway and a growing regional hub. Live animal cargo arrives through the cargo terminal, where the State Veterinary Service carries out documentation and health checks.

        On arrival, the veterinary inspector checks your import permit, the EU export health certificate, the rabies vaccination certificate, and the microchip. Provided your documents are complete and your pet is in good health, clearance is straightforward. There is no routine quarantine for compliant arrivals from Germany.

        Baku has grown significantly as an aviation hub in recent years, which means cargo handling facilities have improved. That said, it is worth briefing your cargo agent in advance about the specific requirements for live animal shipments into Azerbaijan, since the process is less routine than some Western European destinations.
