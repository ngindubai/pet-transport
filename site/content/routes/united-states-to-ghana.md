  sections:
    - heading: "Titre test timeline and the VSD permit"
      body: |
        Ghana's Veterinary Services Directorate requires a titre test from most countries. Since the USA is not a rabies-free country, US pets are expected to need one. The blood draw must be at least 30 days after the most recent rabies vaccination, and results typically take 2-3 weeks. Include this in your timeline from day one of planning.

        The VSD import permit application should go in alongside the titre test process. Apply at least 6 weeks before your intended travel date. The application requires microchip details, vaccination records, the titre test result, and the entry point (Kotoka International Airport in Accra). Confirm current rates and quarantine arrangements with VSD when applying.

    - heading: "Delta direct and the quarantine period"
      body: |
        Delta's nonstop service from Atlanta to Accra is the most direct option for US pet owners. Cargo bookings with Delta go through Delta Cargo, and advance booking is recommended as live animal space is limited. From other US cities, connections via Atlanta or via European hubs are the alternative.

        A 7-day quarantine at a government facility begins immediately on your pet's arrival in Accra. This is mandatory for all imported pets and cannot be shortened. The owner bears quarantine costs for the full 7 days. Confirm current facility rates and visiting policies with VSD when applying for the permit.

  - question: "How long does the titre test process take?"
    answer: "The titre test blood draw must be at least 30 days after your pet's most recent rabies vaccination. Laboratory results take 2-3 weeks. Combined with the vaccination timing, you should allow at least 6-8 weeks just for the vaccination and titre test steps before you have the documentation needed for the VSD permit application."

  - question: "How long is quarantine when a pet arrives in Ghana from the USA?"
    answer: "7 days at a government quarantine facility managed by Ghana's Veterinary Services Directorate, starting on arrival at Kotoka International Airport. The quarantine cannot be shortened or waived for any country of origin. The owner pays all facility fees for the full period. Build this into your relocation schedule; you cannot collect your pet on the day they arrive."

  - question: "What is the import permit process for Ghana?"
    answer: "Apply to Ghana's Veterinary Services Directorate with your pet's microchip details, vaccination history, titre test result, and planned travel date and entry airport. Apply at least 6 weeks before travel. Confirm current permit fees and processing times with VSD directly. The permit must be confirmed before booking cargo. Travelling without a permit risks your pet being refused entry or held indefinitely."
