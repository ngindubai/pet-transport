content:
  h1: "Pet Transport from the United States to Botswana"
  overview: |
    Travelling the other direction, from Botswana into the US, means the CDC's full high-risk-country machinery: a microchip-before-vaccine sequence, a titre test at an approved lab, arrival restricted to one of six facility airports. Coming from the US into Botswana, almost none of that applies. No titre test, no facility reservation, no restricted arrival airport.

    What Botswana does want is a signed attestation most American owners haven't run into before, confirming that no animal disease movement restrictions apply in the pet's home area. Add a DVS import permit and the USDA's own health certificate process, and six to ten weeks is a realistic window from a standing start.

  sections:
    - heading: "A lighter import side, with one unusual document"
      body: |
        Botswana's Department of Veterinary Services doesn't ask for a rabies titre test from any country of origin, the US included, which keeps this route well short of the CDC's own requirements for the reverse journey. The catch is a document that doesn't show up on most other import checklists: written confirmation that no movement restriction, the kind imposed during a regional disease outbreak, currently applies where the pet is leaving from.

        Raise this with your USDA-accredited vet at the first appointment rather than discovering the gap once the DVS import permit application is already in progress. It's a straightforward attestation to obtain, provided someone knows to ask for it.

    - heading: "Delta's Atlanta nonstop, and the leg after it"
      body: |
        Delta operates the only nonstop flight between the US and South Africa, daily between Atlanta and Johannesburg. That single service covers the long-haul portion of almost every Botswana-bound pet move from the US, regardless of which American city the journey starts in.

        From Johannesburg, Airlink or Air Botswana finishes the trip into Sir Seretse Khama International Airport. Confirm cargo space on both legs before locking in a travel date, since a pet transferring between a long-haul carrier and a regional one needs both bookings to align, not just the first.
