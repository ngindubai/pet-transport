content:
  h1: "Pet Transport from Cameroon to United States"
  overview: |
    Six airports. That's the number that matters most for a dog moving from Cameroon to the US. Atlanta, Los Angeles, Miami, JFK, Philadelphia, Washington Dulles: those are the only US entry points with a CDC-registered animal care facility, and every dog from a CDC high-risk country, Cameroon included, must land at one of them. A valid titre test doesn't change that. It changes whether the dog is released quickly or held for 28 days, not where the plane can land.

    Cats have it easier. No federal requirement touches a cat moving from Cameroon to the US. None. If your household is moving a dog and a cat together, budget very different amounts of paperwork for each.

    MINEPIA, Cameroon's animal industries ministry, handles the export side: the vaccination certification, the endorsement, the permit. Get that moving early, because the CDC side has its own fixed waiting periods that run in parallel.
  sections:
    - heading: "The CDC high-risk pathway, and why the airport choice isn't flexible"
      body: |
        The CDC's 2024 rule change split countries into high-risk and low-risk for dog rabies. Cameroon sits firmly in the high-risk group. That triggers a specific document: the Certification of Foreign Rabies Vaccination and Microchip form, completed by your vet and endorsed by MINEPIA, plus a serology titre test or, failing that, a 28-day quarantine.

        Here's the part people miss. Even with a passing titre result, a dog from a high-risk country can only land at one of the six US airports with a CDC-registered facility. Newark, Boston, Chicago O'Hare, none of these currently have one, whatever else they offer. Pick your international routing around this fact first, then worry about price and schedule. Ethiopian Airlines' nonstop from Addis Ababa to Washington Dulles is a strong option precisely because it removes the risk of landing somewhere that doesn't qualify.

    - heading: "Cats: a genuinely different process"
      body: |
        US federal rules draw almost no line around cats. USDA APHIS has no animal health import requirements for pet cats from any country, Cameroon included. No CDC form, no titre test, no facility reservation, no quarantine.

        That doesn't mean skip the basics. A current health record and a comfortable, well-ventilated crate matter for welfare and for the airline's own acceptance rules, even where the federal government asks for nothing. Individual airlines may still ask for a general veterinary certificate before they'll carry the animal, so check with whichever carrier you book.
