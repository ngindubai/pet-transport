content:
  h1: "Pet Transport from United Arab Emirates to Ukraine"
  overview: |
    AED 100 buys the MOCCAE export health certificate that clears a pet to leave Dubai or Abu Dhabi. Getting that same pet all the way into Ukraine afterwards costs a good deal more, in time if not always in money, because the flight only ever reaches Warsaw. The rest of the journey happens by road.

    Two separate rulebooks apply here: MOCCAE's export requirements on the way out, and Ukraine's own entry requirements, which include tests the UAE side never asks for. Neither system talks to the other, so the owner ends up carrying both.

  sections:
    - heading: "Getting the MOCCAE export certificate right"
      body: |
        This part is comparatively quick. Apply online with a UAE Pass login, get the parasite treatment done within 14 days of travel, then attend a MOCCAE-approved clinic at DXB or AUH for the in-person inspection and endorsement. AED 100 covers up to two animals for a personal, non-commercial move.

        The certificate's 30-day validity is the thing to watch. It has to cover the Dubai-Warsaw flight and the overland transfer into Ukraine, and border delays are not rare given the security situation on the Ukrainian side.

    - heading: "Why the flight only gets the pet as far as Warsaw"
      body: |
        Emirates flies EK179 nonstop from Dubai to Warsaw Chopin daily, a straightforward six-hour widebody service. It's the easy part of this route.

        From Warsaw, there is no onward flight into Ukraine, because Ukrainian civilian airspace has been closed since 24 February 2022 and remains closed in mid-2026. The pet clears Polish customs, then travels overland, typically toward Shehyni-Medyka or Krakovets-Korczowa near Lviv, or a Romanian crossing for owners headed further south.

    - heading: "What Ukraine actually asks for on entry"
      body: |
        Ukraine's import rules apply to a pet arriving from any country, the UAE included: microchip before vaccination, a rabies vaccination given between 21 days and 12 months before entry, and a titre test drawn no sooner than 14 days post-vaccination, valid between 3 and 24 months old at the point of entry.

        Two tests have no equivalent on the UAE side. Dogs need a negative Toxoplasmosis result. Cats need a Dermatophytosis, or ringworm, test. Both go on the same international certificate the border vet checks, so skipping either one stalls the crossing.

    - heading: "Costs, and the tests unique to this route"
      body: |
        All told, this route runs somewhere between USD 1,300 and USD 3,200, once the MOCCAE certificate, any outstanding UAE vet work, the two Ukraine-specific tests, a crate, the Dubai-Warsaw cargo fare and the overland transfer are added together. The overland leg alone can swing widely, from a few hundred dollars for a straightforward crossing to closer to USD 900 if a dedicated transport agent manages the whole thing.

        There's no routine quarantine at the end of it, provided the paperwork on both sides is complete. Missing the Toxoplasmosis or Dermatophytosis test is the most common way this route trips up owners who otherwise did everything right.
