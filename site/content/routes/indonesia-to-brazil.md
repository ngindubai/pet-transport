  sections:
    - heading: "Brazil vs Indonesia quarantine: the paperwork goes both ways"
      body: |
        Both Brazil and Indonesia have facility quarantine requirements for incoming pets, and the documentation demands are substantial in each direction.

        Pets entering Brazil from Indonesia face a MAPA import permit requirement and a 30-day quarantine at GRU. There is no titre test. The Brazil process is demanding in terms of quarantine length but not in terms of pre-travel testing.

        Pets entering Indonesia from Brazil face the FAVN titre test (30 days after vaccination, then at least 30 days from blood draw before entry), a BARANTAN import recommendation, and a 30-day facility quarantine on arrival at CGK or SUB. The Indonesia process adds the titre test wait to an already long timeline.

        Owners returning from Indonesia to Brazil, or those considering a round trip, should plan for both sets of requirements independently. Neither country simplifies the process for the other.

    - heading: "Routing options for cargo pets from Jakarta to São Paulo"
      body: |
        Jakarta (CGK) to São Paulo Guarulhos (GRU) requires multiple connections. Two main routing options exist.

        Gulf hub routing via Dubai (DXB) or Doha (DOH) is a well-established option. Emirates SkyCargo and Qatar Airways Cargo both operate the CGK to Gulf hub leg and have connections to GRU. Both carriers handle live animals and the Gulf hubs have temperature-controlled live animal facilities.

        European hub routing via Amsterdam (AMS) is an alternative. KLM Cargo operates CGK-AMS and AMS-GRU. Amsterdam Schiphol has good live animal handling and a number of Brazil connections. Total transit time is longer than the Gulf routing but AMS is a reliable hub for live animal cargo.

        On a route this long, choose an airline that operates both segments with confirmed live animal acceptance on every leg. A single-carrier solution reduces the risk of a transfer breakdown at the connecting hub.
