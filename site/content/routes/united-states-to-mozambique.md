  h1: "Pet Transport from United States to Mozambique"
  overview: |
    DINAV runs both ends of Mozambique's pet paperwork: export clearance for animals leaving the country, and the import permit for animals arriving in it. A US-origin dog or cat needs that second document, applied for through DINAV under Mozambique's Ministry of Agriculture, with no fee schedule or processing time published anywhere online.

    What this route doesn't need is more notable than what it does. No titre test. No 28-day quarantine option. No CDC-style high-risk pathway, because none of that machinery belongs to Mozambique's own import rules, only to the CDC's rules for a dog travelling the opposite direction.

    Three carriers connect US gateways to Maputo through established African or Gulf hubs: Ethiopian Airlines via Addis Ababa, Qatar Airways via Doha, and TAP Air Portugal via Lisbon.

  sections:
    - heading: "DINAV: one authority, two very different jobs"
      body: |
        DINAV, the Direcção Nacional de Veterinária under Mozambique's Ministry of Agriculture, issues the export health certificate for a pet leaving Mozambique and separately controls the import permit for a pet entering it. Those are two different processes, and a US owner only deals with the second one.

        The import permit is required for every pet dog and cat, and Mozambique publishes no standard fee or turnaround time for it. That absence of a published timeline is the single biggest planning risk on this route, bigger than the vaccination schedule or the flight booking. Start the DINAV application as soon as the decision to relocate is made, through a Mozambique-based agent or Mozambique's diplomatic representation in the US.

    - heading: "No CDC-style pathway on the way in"
      body: |
        A dog leaving Mozambique for the US runs into the CDC's full high-risk pathway: certification form, titre test or 28-day quarantine, arrival restricted to one of six facility airports. None of that applies going the other way.

        Mozambique's own import rules ask for a microchip, a rabies vaccination at least 30 days old on an annual-labelled product, and the standard core vaccines. A titre test is recommended, not compulsory, mainly to smooth a customs check rather than satisfy a legal floor. This asymmetry, tight rules coming into the US and comparatively light rules going the other way, is worth understanding before assuming both legs of a return trip cost the same in time or money.

    - heading: "Three routes into Maputo from the US"
      body: |
        Ethiopian Airlines connects Washington Dulles to Addis Ababa on its own aircraft, then continues daily into Maputo, keeping the pet within one carrier's network for most of the trip. Qatar Airways flies from several US gateways, JFK included, to Doha, with a direct Doha to Maputo sector and a dedicated Live Animals Centre for longer transits.

        TAP Air Portugal routes JFK to Lisbon, then a daily nonstop of roughly 11 hours down to Maputo, though its hold weight limit drops to 32kg specifically on US-touching sectors rather than the 45kg allowed elsewhere on its network. Confirm the crate weight against that lower figure before booking.

    answer: "Yes. DINAV, Mozambique's Direcção Nacional de Veterinária, requires an import permit for every pet dog and cat entering the country, separate from the export process it also runs. No fee schedule or processing time is published, so start this application as early as possible."
  - question: "Is a rabies titre test required for a US dog entering Mozambique?"
    answer: "No. Mozambique doesn't require a titre test from any country of origin, including the US. This is the reverse of the CDC's own rules for a Mozambique-origin dog entering the US, which require a titre test or a 28-day quarantine at a CDC-registered facility."
  - question: "How soon before travel does the rabies vaccination need to be given?"
    answer: "At least 30 days before entry into Mozambique, and it has to be recorded on an annual-labelled vaccine product. Multi-year rabies vaccines aren't accepted for import purposes, so check the label on your pet's most recent vaccination before booking a flight."
