    - heading: "SWISS direct: Ljubljana to Zurich"
      body: |
        SWISS, part of the Lufthansa Group, operates direct flights between Ljubljana Airport (LJU) and Zurich Airport (ZRH). The flight time is around 1 hour 15 minutes. Small pets (up to 8kg including carrier) travel in the cabin. Larger dogs travel as checked baggage in the pressurised hold, or via SWISS Cargo for particularly large animals.

        SWISS has a clear pet policy that is well suited to this route. Book your pet's cabin place when purchasing your ticket; spaces are limited and popular routes fill up. For cargo bookings, contact SWISS Cargo directly.

        If SWISS schedules do not work for your dates, Lufthansa via Frankfurt to Zurich or Geneva is an alternative. Austrian Airlines via Vienna can also connect to Zurich with onward SWISS or Austrian services.

    - heading: "Switzerland's bilateral agreement with the EU"
      body: |
        Switzerland's bilateral veterinary agreement with the EU, in place since 2002, aligns Swiss animal import rules with EU standards for pets arriving from EU member states. In practice, this means:
        - EU pet passports are accepted directly.
        - No titre test is required from EU countries.
        - No quarantine for compliant animals.
        - No import permit.

        The Swiss Federal Food Safety and Veterinary Office (FSVO, or BLV in German) governs entry and may conduct spot checks at Zurich, Geneva, or Basel airports, and at land crossings. Documentation should be accessible throughout the journey.

        Your pet's rabies vaccination must be within its valid period. If a booster is due, schedule it before travel rather than after arrival.

    - heading: "Preparing documents in Slovenia"
      body: |
        Slovenia's UVHVVR-authorised vets can issue EU pet passports once your pet is microchipped and vaccinated. The passport is valid for the animal's lifetime and covers travel across the EU and to bilateral-agreement countries including Switzerland.

        An AHC is the alternative for a one-way relocation. It must be endorsed by an official Slovenian vet within 10 days of arriving in Switzerland. If you are flying into Zurich, the endorsement date should be within 10 days of your flight.

        Switzerland requires the AHC to conform to EU format. This is standard practice for vets in EU member states, but worth confirming with the issuing vet.
