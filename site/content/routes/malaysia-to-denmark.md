faqs:
  - question: "Does Denmark require quarantine for pets from Malaysia?"
    answer: "No. Denmark does not quarantine pets from Malaysia provided the full EU requirements are met: ISO microchip, valid rabies vaccination, FAVN titre test result of 0.5 IU/ml or above, completed 90-day wait from the blood draw date, and EU AHC issued within 10 days before arrival. The border inspection at Copenhagen is documentary, not a quarantine hold."
  - question: "Is a FAVN titre test required for dogs and cats equally?"
    answer: "Yes. The EU unlisted country rule applies to both dogs and cats. Both must have an ISO microchip, a valid rabies vaccination, a FAVN titre test (0.5 IU/ml or above), a completed 90-day wait, and an EU AHC to enter Denmark from Malaysia."
  - question: "What happens if the FAVN test result is below 0.5 IU/ml?"
    answer: "If the result is below the threshold, the 90-day clock does not start. The vet reviews the vaccination programme, typically booster vaccination followed by a repeat blood draw and retest. The 90-day wait then starts from the date of the first blood draw that produces a result of 0.5 IU/ml or above. This can add several months to the preparation timeline."
  - question: "How long does DVS take to issue the export health certificate?"
    answer: "Typically 3 to 7 working days, depending on the district office workload. Apply during the 90-day wait period, not at the last minute. The DVS certificate has its own validity window, so time the application so the certificate is current on the travel date."
