  overview: |
    DAFF places Trinidad and Tobago in Group 3, its tier for countries where rabies is present but managed, the same classification as most of Europe, the UK, the US and Canada. An RNATT titre test, a 180-day wait counted from when the laboratory receives the sample, and a stay at the Mickleham quarantine facility all apply here as they do on those routes.

    Add a long-haul routing through London, since nothing connects Trinidad and Tobago to Australia in fewer than two legs, and this route realistically runs 30 to 42 weeks.

  sections:
    - heading: "The 180-day wait and where it actually starts"
      body: |
        The clock on this route starts when a DAFF-approved laboratory receives the blood sample, not the day it was drawn on Trinidad. With limited local access to DAFF-approved testing, the sample typically travels internationally for analysis, and that transit time adds real weeks on top of the mandatory 180-day count itself. Building in that buffer from the outset avoids a nasty surprise close to the planned departure date.

    - heading: "The 10-day quarantine shortcut, and the London routing"
      body: |
        The default 30-day Mickleham stay can shorten to 10 days through a formal identity verification, completed by a competent authority in Trinidad and Tobago before the RNATT blood sample is drawn, at a separate appointment. Getting this sequence wrong risks having to repeat the RNATT and restart the 180-day wait entirely, so confirm the exact local process with DAFF or an experienced agent before the blood draw.

        On the flight side, British Airways' nonstop Piarco-Gatwick service is the natural first leg for almost any long-haul move out of Trinidad and Tobago, and this route is no exception, with a connecting carrier picking up the run into Melbourne from London.
