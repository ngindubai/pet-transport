  sections:
    - heading: "Why Jamaica needs a titre test for Switzerland but not for EU countries"
      body: |
        Jamaica is classified as a listed third country under EU Implementing Regulation 2026/636, which exempts pets from Jamaica from the FAVN titre test when entering EU member states such as Sweden, Denmark, Austria, and others. However, Switzerland is not an EU member state and applies its own classification system maintained by FSVO (the Federal Food Safety and Veterinary Office / BLV). Under Swiss BLV classification, Jamaica is classified as an unlisted third country.

        This means that despite being listed for EU purposes, pets from Jamaica moving to Switzerland must complete the FAVN titre test and the 90-day waiting period. Blood must be drawn at least 30 days after the most recent rabies vaccination. The result must show at least 0.5 IU/ml at an EU-approved laboratory. The 90-day wait runs from the blood draw date.

    - heading: "FSVO advance import permit and entry airports"
      body: |
        In addition to the FAVN titre test, FSVO requires an advance import permit before non-listed country pets arrive in Switzerland. Apply at blv.admin.ch at least 3 weeks before travel with the pet's microchip number, vaccination records, FAVN result, and intended travel date. The permit specifies the required certificate format and the designated Border Control Post for entry.

        Switzerland designates three airports as Border Control Posts for non-EU pet arrivals: Zurich (ZRH), Geneva (GVA), and Basel-Mulhouse-Freiburg (BSL/EAP). Swiss International Air Lines, based at ZRH, offers the most practical hub routing from Europe. No direct service from Jamaica to Switzerland exists; routing via London (British Airways) or Frankfurt (Lufthansa) are the standard options.
