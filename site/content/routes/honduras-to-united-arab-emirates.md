content:
  h1: "Pet Transport from Honduras to the United Arab Emirates"
  overview: |
    Ninety days. That's the minimum wait the UAE's Ministry of Climate Change and Environment enforces between a rabies titre blood draw and entry, for any pet arriving from a country outside its short list of recognised low-risk states, and Honduras isn't on that list.

    Add the 21-day gap that has to sit between vaccination and the blood draw, and a dog or cat leaving Honduras for Dubai or Abu Dhabi is looking at a minimum of around 16 weeks from a standing start, not counting flight booking or paperwork delays. There's no direct flight either, so every shipment routes through a third country, most workably Miami.

  sections:
    - heading: "The titre clock is the whole schedule"
      body: |
        Everything else on this route fits around one number: 90 days from the blood sample, not the result. Labs typically turn results around in two to four weeks, which means the pass or fail news often lands with two months of waiting still ahead before the pet can legally enter the UAE.

        Missing this by even a few days means starting the 90-day count over. Book the blood draw the moment the 21-day post-vaccination gap has passed, and don't let flight planning happen before the sample is already in the lab.

    - heading: "Two paperwork trails that don't talk to each other"
      body: |
        SENASA's export certificate confirms Honduras is happy for the animal to leave. It says nothing about whether MOCCAE's entry conditions are met. These are separate government systems on separate continents, and nothing forces them to reconcile automatically.

        Build the SENASA certificate into the final two weeks before travel, since it has a short shelf life, while the MOCCAE side (permit, titre result, vaccination record) gets assembled on its own, much longer, 90-day track.

    - heading: "Routing through a country neither pet nor owner is actually visiting"
      body: |
        American Airlines gets a pet from San Pedro Sula or Palmerola to Miami without much trouble; it's a year-round cargo route. The complication starts on the Miami side. Emirates flies Miami to Dubai nonstop, but on that specific pairing it requires every pet to travel as manifest cargo, meaning the animal has to be handed off as a cargo shipment in Miami rather than carried through like checked luggage on a connecting ticket.

        Qatar Airways' daily Miami-Doha A350 service is worth pricing against Emirates, particularly if the Emirates cargo slot doesn't match the date the 90-day titre wait clears. Iberia's Madrid routing exists too, but at roughly 15 hours total it's the slower and pricier fallback, not the first option.

    - heading: "What Honduras itself doesn't ask for"
      body: |
        SENASA's requirements are the light half of this move. No titre test, no lengthy waiting period, just a vet inspection and an online-processed certificate for around USD 20. The complexity here comes entirely from the destination, not the origin, which is the opposite of how the Honduras-to-US and Honduras-to-UK routes on this site tend to work.
