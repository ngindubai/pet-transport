    - heading: "AFCD quarantine: what to expect on arrival in Hong Kong"
      body: |
        Every dog and cat arriving in Hong Kong from Singapore must complete mandatory quarantine at an AFCD-approved facility. The duration for dogs is typically 7-14 days depending on documentation completeness; cats face 7 days.

        You cannot be present with your pet during quarantine. AFCD-approved facility staff provide care. Owner-paid costs vary by facility, duration, and pet size. Budget for this before committing to the move, and factor it into your relocation timeline.

        After quarantine, AFCD inspects your pet and releases it to you with a clearance certificate. Your pet is then free to live and move around Hong Kong normally.
faqs:
  - question: "Is there mandatory quarantine when bringing a pet from Singapore to Hong Kong?"
    answer: "Yes. AFCD requires mandatory quarantine for all pets arriving from Singapore. Dogs typically face 7-14 days depending on documentation; cats face 7 days. This is owner-paid and cannot be waived."
  - question: "Does my pet need a titre test to enter Hong Kong from Singapore?"
    answer: "No. A titre test is not required for pets from Singapore under current AFCD rules. The main requirements are current microchip, current rabies vaccination, an AFCD import permit, and the AVS-endorsed health certificate."
  - question: "Which airline should I use for Singapore to Hong Kong pet cargo?"
    answer: "Cathay Pacific is the standard choice. They operate multiple direct SIN-HKG daily services and have a dedicated live animal facility at Hong Kong airport. Singapore Airlines on the same route is a reliable alternative."
  - question: "How far in advance should I apply for the AFCD import permit?"
    answer: "At least 4-6 weeks before travel. The AFCD import permit must be obtained before your pet departs Singapore. Your pet transport agent can handle the application."
