  sections:
    - heading: "Getting from Ljubljana to Prague"
      body: |
        There is no direct Ljubljana to Prague service. The most natural connection is via Vienna: Austrian Airlines flies Ljubljana (LJU) to Vienna (VIE) in under 1 hour, and the Vienna to Prague segment is well served. Small pets travel in the cabin throughout, subject to the 8kg cabin limit.

        Lufthansa via Frankfurt (FRA) is an alternative that gives more departure time flexibility and access to a larger cargo operation for bigger dogs. The Frankfurt to Prague sector is short (around 1.5 hours), making total journey times comparable.

        KLM via Amsterdam is a third option, useful if your travel dates align better with its schedules or if you are connecting from elsewhere in Europe.

    - heading: "Cargo from Ljubljana: a practical note"
      body: |
        Ljubljana Airport is a smaller facility than Vienna or Frankfurt. Its cargo capacity for live animals is limited. For small pets in the cabin, this is not an issue; the cabin process is the same as at any other EU airport.

        For larger dogs that need to travel by cargo, however, using Vienna as the departure hub is often more practical. This may mean driving your dog to Vienna (around 3.5 hours from Ljubljana, as covered in the Slovenia-to-Austria guide) and departing from there, or connecting through Vienna on Austrian Airlines and transferring the cargo booking.

        Speak to a specialist pet transport agent if your dog is large. They can advise on which routing makes the most sense for cargo to Prague.

    - heading: "Czech SVA requirements"
      body: |
        The Czech State Veterinary Administration (SVA) follows EU standards. Your dog, cat, or ferret needs a microchip (ISO 11784/11785), a valid rabies vaccination with the 21-day first-vaccination wait observed, and an EU pet passport or AHC.

        The AHC, if used, must be endorsed within 10 days of arrival in Prague. For a flight that connects via Vienna, the endorsement date is measured from the moment you cross into the Czech Republic, not from Ljubljana.

        The Czech Republic has no national breed ban. Some municipalities have rules about certain breeds in public spaces, but there are no entry-level breed restrictions.
