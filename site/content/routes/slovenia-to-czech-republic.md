  sections:
    - heading: "Driving versus flying: the six to seven hour question"
      body: |
        A direct drive from Ljubljana to Prague takes six to seven hours depending on traffic and stops, noticeably longer than the four and a half hours into Budapest. It's still a manageable single day for most healthy pets, and it skips the airport process entirely. Fuel and tolls run EUR 70-110 depending on the vehicle, generally cheaper than flying once connection fees are added.

        Flying suits multi-pet households, tighter schedules, or an owner who's already booked on a flight and wants the pet travelling on a similar timeline. Austrian Airlines via Vienna and Lufthansa via Munich are the two workable connections, since there's no non-stop service between Ljubljana and Prague.

    - heading: "Paperwork: EU pet passport, AHC and the April 2026 rule"
      body: |
        The EU pet passport, issued by a UVHVVR-authorised vet in Slovenia, stays valid for the pet's life as long as the rabies vaccination inside it is current. Since 22 April 2026, the passport is restricted to owners primarily resident in the EU, which leaves the large majority of Slovenian owners unaffected.

        Non-EU nationals resident in Slovenia use a single-use AHC instead, issued within 10 days of travel and endorsed by UVHVVR. The State Veterinary Administration (SVS ČR) checks the microchip against the passport or AHC on arrival in Prague, whether the pet came by road or by air, so confirm the numbers match before setting off.

    - heading: "Flight options via Vienna or Munich"
      body: |
        Austrian Airlines' Vienna connection is the more direct of the two options, with cabin space for small pets on both legs where weight allows. Lufthansa via Munich is a solid alternative if the Vienna dates don't work, though it carries breed restrictions for cargo-hold travel that are worth checking before booking a brachycephalic dog.
