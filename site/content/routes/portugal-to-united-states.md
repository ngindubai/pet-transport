    answer: "No. Portugal is not on the CDC high-risk country list. Dogs from Portugal are treated as coming from a non-high-risk country. The process is: complete the free CDC Dog Import Form online, ensure the dog is microchipped, at least 6 months old, and appears healthy.   - question: "Which airline is best for pets from Lisbon to the USA?"
    answer: "TAP Air Portugal is the most direct option, with cargo services from Lisbon (LIS) and Porto (OPO) to JFK, Newark, Boston, and Miami. British Airways provides an alternative via London Heathrow. Confirm current live animal acceptance with your chosen airline before booking."
  - question: "Do cats from Portugal need any documents to enter the USA?"
    answer: "At the US federal level, there are no requirements for importing pet cats from Portugal. USDA APHIS has no health restrictions. In practice, airlines require a vet-issued health certificate for cargo shipments, and some US states have their own rules. Check your destination state and your airline's specific policy."
  - question: "Can my dog from Portugal arrive at any US airport?"
    answer: "Yes. Dogs from non-high-risk countries including Portugal can arrive at any US airport, seaport, or land border crossing. There is no requirement to arrive at an airport with a CDC-registered facility. The CDC/CBP inspection is brief for compliant pets and the dog is released on arrival."
  - question: "How long is the CDC Dog Import Form valid?"
    answer: "The CDC Dog Import Form receipt is valid for 6 months and can be used for multiple entries if the dog has remained in non-high-risk countries throughout that period. Complete the form online at cdc.gov before travel. It is free and takes about 15 minutes."
