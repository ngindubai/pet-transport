  sections:
    - heading: "Entry at Tbilisi: what the NFA checks"
      body: |
        Georgia's National Food Agency (NFA) at nfa.gov.ge administers companion pet imports. At Tbilisi International Airport (TBS), a border veterinary officer from the NFA inspects documentation on arrival. For a pet from Latvia, what they are looking for is a current rabies vaccination and a health certificate from the Latvian PVD. Neither a titre test nor quarantine is required for EU-origin pets.

        The NFA's border check is professional and documented. The certificate must accurately reflect the vaccination details and match the pet's identifying information (chip number if chipped). Any inconsistency between the certificate and the animal causes delays. Having your transport agent verify the certificate format before travel is a sensible precaution.

        Georgia does not impose quarantine on compliant pets. Assuming documents are in order, you will collect your pet from the cargo facility at TBS and clear veterinary inspection in a single step.

    - heading: "The PVD export certificate for Latvia"
      body: |
        Latvia's Food and Veterinary Service (PVD) at pvd.gov.lv is the competent authority for export health certificates. For Georgia, your vet cannot use the EU pet passport. Instead, an authorised official veterinarian issues an export health certificate, which the PVD then endorses.

        Contact the PVD to identify a vet with official authority for export certificates. The endorsement process and the certificate validity period (issued within 10 days of travel) mean you need to sequence the vet appointment carefully relative to your travel date. Confirm with the PVD whether Georgia requires any specific certificate format before the appointment.

    - heading: "Cargo routing from Riga to Tbilisi"
      body: |
        Riga International Airport (RIX) has good European connectivity through airBaltic. For pet cargo to Tbilisi, the most practical routing uses airBaltic from RIX to Frankfurt (FRA) or Amsterdam (AMS), then Turkish Airlines Cargo or Lufthansa Cargo onwards to Tbilisi (TBS) via Istanbul (IST) or directly from FRA.

        Turkish Airlines handles live cargo at IST regularly for South Caucasus routes. The IST to TBS sector is a short hop of around 90 minutes. Alternatively, connecting through Frankfurt gives access to Lufthansa Cargo, which also handles the Caucasus corridor.

        Whatever routing you choose, book cargo early and obtain written confirmation of live animal acceptance on every segment. Tbilisi cargo facilities handle live animals, but capacity on specific flights varies.
