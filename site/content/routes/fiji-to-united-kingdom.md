content:
  h1: "Pet Transport from Fiji to the United Kingdom"
  overview: |
    Fiji is on Great Britain's short list of low-rabies-risk countries, the same category as Australia and New Zealand. That single fact strips out the part of this move most owners dread: no rabies titre test, no three-month wait for a blood result. A dog or cat can travel 21 days after its rabies vaccination, provided the microchip went in first.

    What makes this route awkward is not paperwork, it's geography. Nadi International Airport has no scheduled service to London. Fiji Airways flies nonstop to Los Angeles, Sydney and Auckland, and the trip to the UK has to be built from there, on a second carrier, as a proper cargo booking rather than a single ticket.

  sections:
    - heading: "Why Fiji's listed status makes the UK paperwork the easy part"
      body: |
        Great Britain sorts every non-EU country into two groups. Unlisted countries need a rabies antibody titre test, drawn at least 30 days after vaccination, followed by a fixed three-calendar-month wait before the pet can fly. Fiji avoids all of that. It sits on the Part 1 listed group, alongside a small set of rabies-free or tightly controlled territories, so the requirement drops to a straightforward 21-day wait after vaccination.

        That doesn't mean skipping steps. The microchip still has to go in before the vaccine, a Great Britain Pet Health Certificate still has to be issued within 10 days of travel by a vet in Fiji, and dogs still need a tapeworm treatment in the 24 to 120 hours before landing. Cats are exempt from that last one. Get the sequence right and the regulatory side of this move is genuinely one of the simpler ones on this site.

    - heading: "The real bottleneck: there is no direct flight"
      body: |
        Fiji Airways' route map from Nadi runs to North America, Australia, New Zealand and a scatter of Pacific islands. London is not on it, not even as a codeshare with pet cargo included. That means every pet leaving Fiji for the UK has to change carriers mid-journey, most realistically via Sydney onto a UK-bound cargo service, or via Los Angeles onto a US or UK carrier's freight operation.

        Each option has a catch. A Sydney connection keeps the animal within Fiji Airways' own network for the first leg, then hands off to whichever airline is actually accepting live cargo into Heathrow or Gatwick that week. A Los Angeles connection is often the faster clock time, but it means the animal transits US territory as live freight, with its own handling and clearance requirements layered on top of the UK entry paperwork. Neither route is something to book without an agent confirming acceptance on both legs first.

    - heading: "What Fiji itself asks for before your pet can leave"
      body: |
        The Biosecurity Authority of Fiji, not an airline, controls the exit side. A vet in Fiji completes the export paperwork, then BAF's own veterinary officer has to inspect the animal and sign off at a booked appointment. Everything, vaccination records, microchip number, the vet's certificate, needs to reach BAF at least 7 days before that inspection date.

        Processing after the inspection typically takes 3 to 5 working days. Build that into your planning alongside the flight booking; leaving it until the week before travel is the most common way this route goes wrong.
