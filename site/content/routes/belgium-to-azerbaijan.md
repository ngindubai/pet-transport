  overview: |
    Belgium to Azerbaijan is a straightforward route for EU-origin pets. Azerbaijan accepts EU health certificates for incoming companion pets without requiring a titre test, which removes the main complexity of many international moves.   sections:
    - heading: "Azerbaijan's entry requirements for EU pets"
      body: |
        Azerbaijan accepts companion pets from EU member states including Belgium without a titre test requirement. The main documents are a valid ISO microchip, current rabies vaccination, and an FASFC-endorsed EU-format health certificate.

        On arrival at Heydar Aliyev International Airport (GYD), Azerbaijani Ministry of Agriculture vets conduct a documentary inspection and brief physical check. The pet is released without quarantine if documentation is in order.

        Regulations are administered by the Ministry of Agriculture (agro.gov.az). Confirm current requirements before travel, as policies can change.

    - heading: "The AZAL direct service: Brussels to Baku"
      body: |
        Azerbaijan Airlines (AZAL) operates flights between Brussels Airport (BRU) and Heydar Aliyev International Airport (GYD, Baku). If AZAL accepts live animal cargo on this route, it is the simplest option: there is no hub transit, the cargo is handled by a single carrier, and the journey time is significantly shorter than hub routings via Istanbul, Frankfurt, or Dubai.

        Check directly with AZAL Cargo about live animal acceptance on the BRU-GYD route before making it your primary plan. Cargo policies can differ from passenger schedules, and weight limits and seasonal embargoes apply.

        If AZAL direct is not available on your preferred dates, Turkish Airlines via Istanbul is the next most practical option, followed by Lufthansa Cargo via Frankfurt or Emirates/Flydubai via Dubai.

    - heading: "FASFC documentation: Belgium's export process"
      body: |
        Belgium's export health certificate for companion pets requires an examination by an FASFC-authorised vet followed by endorsement from the relevant FASFC provincial office. This is a standard EU export process, and most vets who handle international pet moves are familiar with it.

        The certificate is valid for 10 days from the examination date. Schedule the appointment carefully to ensure the certificate is still valid on arrival in Azerbaijan. FASFC endorsement typically takes one to two working days after the examination, so build that into your timeline.

        FASFC's website (fasfc.be) lists authorised vets and provincial office contacts. Some practices can submit for FASFC endorsement directly online, which speeds up the process.
