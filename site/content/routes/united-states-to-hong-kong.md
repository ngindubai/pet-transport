  sections:
    - heading: "Group II status and the AFCD permit"
      body: |
        Hong Kong's AFCD classifies origin places into groups by rabies risk. The USA is Group II, which still allows entry without quarantine but requires the Special Permit and a health certificate.

        The permit is applied for online before travel and typically takes 1-2 weeks. The permit conditions for Group II include a microchip, a current rabies vaccination, and the USDA-endorsed health certificate. There is no quarantine and no titre test for Group II pets entering Hong Kong.
    - heading: "Summer heat embargoes"
      body: |
        Hong Kong is hot and humid from May through September. Airlines restrict live animal cargo during these months to protect welfare, and brachycephalic breeds face the strictest conditions. If your move is in summer, discuss timing and routing with your airline early.

faqs:
  - question: "Does my pet need to quarantine on arrival in Hong Kong from the USA?"
    answer: "No, provided the documentation is correct. The USA is a Group II territory for AFCD purposes, which allows entry without quarantine. You need the AFCD Special Permit, a microchip, a current rabies vaccination, and the USDA-endorsed health certificate. Your pet is released after the arrival inspection."
  - question: "How long does the USDA APHIS endorsement take?"
    answer: "Electronic submission through VEHCS typically processes in 2-5 business days; mail submission takes 5-10. Since the health certificate examination must be within 10 days of travel, time the endorsement carefully. Most owners schedule the vet examination 7-8 days before travel and submit immediately via VEHCS."
  - question: "Which airline should I use for USA to Hong Kong pet transport?"
    answer: "Cathay Pacific is the primary carrier with direct cargo pet services from US hubs to Hong Kong. United offers cabin-only carriage for small pets but no cargo, so it is unsuitable for most dogs. Emirates provides a connecting option via Dubai. For most owners a direct Cathay flight is best."
  - question: "Are there breed restrictions for dogs entering Hong Kong?"
    answer: "Yes. Hong Kong regulates breeds including Pit Bull Terriers, Argentine Dogo, Brazilian Fila and Japanese Tosa under its Dangerous Dogs Regulation. Check the AFCD rules for your breed before beginning the process."
