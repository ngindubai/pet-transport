  sections:
    - heading: "What Sweden Requires for Pets Arriving from Lithuania"
      body: |
        Sweden follows the standard EU pet travel rules under EU Regulation 576/2013. For a pet arriving from Lithuania, the requirements are: an ISO 11784/11785 microchip (15-digit, implanted before or on the same day as the first rabies vaccination), a current rabies vaccination recorded in the EU pet passport, and a 21-day wait after any primary vaccination course.

        No titre test is required for pets coming from EU member states. Sweden does not impose quarantine on compliant arrivals. There is no tapeworm treatment requirement for dogs coming from EU countries (this applies specifically to Norway). The Jordbruksverket (Swedish Board of Agriculture) is the competent authority and publishes current rules at jordbruksverket.se.

    - heading: "Routing from Vilnius to Stockholm: Via Riga or Warsaw"
      body: |
        No airline currently operates a direct Vilnius (VNO) to Stockholm Arlanda (ARN) route, so your pet will travel via a hub. The most common option is airBaltic via Riga (RIX), which connects Vilnius to Stockholm with a layover in Riga. airBaltic accepts cabin pets and cargo on this routing. The Vilnius to Riga leg is short (around 40 minutes), making it a manageable connection for most animals.

        LOT Polish Airlines offers the VNO to ARN route via Warsaw (WAW) and handles cargo on the Warsaw to Stockholm leg. SAS connects through Scandinavian hubs. Whichever route you choose, confirm that the live animal booking covers all segments, not just the main leg. Cargo arrangements in particular need to be confirmed with the airline's live animal desk for each segment of a connecting flight.
