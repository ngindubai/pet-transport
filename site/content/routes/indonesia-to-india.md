  overview: |
    Moving a pet from Indonesia to India requires BARANTAN export clearance in Indonesia and prior AQCS import authorisation from India's DAHD. Both processes need to run in parallel before travel. Singapore Airlines via Singapore and Garuda Indonesia's direct India services are the most practical routing options. A quarantine period on arrival in India is possible depending on AQCS's classification of Indonesia's origin country status. Use a specialist agent for this corridor.

    - heading: "BARANTAN export and AQCS import processes"
      body: |
        At the Indonesian end, BARANTAN issues the export permit and health certificate and provides clearance at Jakarta Soekarno-Hatta (CGK) on the day of travel. At the Indian end, AQCS under DAHD issues the import authorisation before departure and carries out the inspection at the designated entry airport.

        Entry in India must be at one of the six AQCS-designated airports: Delhi (DEL), Mumbai (BOM), Chennai (MAA), Kolkata (CCU), Bengaluru (BLR), or Hyderabad (HYD). The routing must be planned so the pet arrives at a designated airport.

        Contact BARANTAN (pertanian.go.id) for the current export permit process and DAHD (dahd.nic.in) for the current AQCS import authorisation procedure.
