content:
  h1: "Pet Transport from Antigua and Barbuda to the United Arab Emirates"
  overview: |
    MOCCAE publishes a list of roughly 50 countries it treats as low-risk for rabies, and Antigua and Barbuda's name does not appear on the versions of that list checked for this guide, from MOCCAE's own portal or from UAE-based relocation agencies. That is a genuine gap, not a settled fact either way, and it changes how this route should be planned.

    Rather than assume the lighter pathway and risk a rejected booking, the safer plan treats this as a titre-test route: a Rabies Serum Neutralisation Test at 0.5 IU/mL or above, drawn three to twelve months before travel, on top of the standard microchip, vaccination and MOCCAE import permit. Confirming the actual classification directly with MOCCAE before locking in a vaccination schedule is worth the phone call.

  sections:
    - heading: "An unpublished classification, and why it matters here"
      body: |
        Most of MOCCAE's low-risk list is easy to find: the UK, most of the EU, Australia, Japan, Singapore and similar countries with well-documented rabies control appear consistently across sources. Antigua and Barbuda doesn't show up on any version checked, which likely means it falls into the larger high-risk bracket by default, but that's an inference rather than a confirmed classification.

        Getting this wrong in the optimistic direction, assuming no titre test is needed and then discovering one is, costs a cancelled flight and months of delay. Getting it wrong in the cautious direction costs a blood test that turns out to have been unnecessary. This guide plans for the cautious version and recommends confirming with MOCCAE directly before finalising a schedule.

    - heading: "What the high-risk pathway actually involves"
      body: |
        Assuming the RSNT pathway applies, a dog or cat needs the microchip fitted first, then a rabies vaccination at least 12 weeks old, then a blood draw for the RSNT test, MOCCAE's version of the FAVN, at a recognised laboratory. The blood draw has to fall between three and twelve months before the travel date, and the result needs to clear 0.5 IU/ml. Dogs also need core vaccinations against distemper, parvovirus, infectious hepatitis and leptospirosis.

        The MOCCAE import permit itself is only valid for 30 days, so it's the last document to apply for, once a firm travel date and a confirmed cargo booking are both in place.

    - heading: "Getting a pet from a Caribbean island to the Gulf"
      body: |
        There's no nonstop flight for this pairing. The shortest routing by leg count uses American Airlines' nonstop Antigua-Miami service, then Emirates' EK213/EK214, which flies Miami to Dubai nonstop four times a week on a Boeing 777-300ER, around 14 to 15 hours in the air. The alternative runs via British Airways' nonstop BA2157 to Gatwick, then a Gulf carrier onward from Heathrow.

        Confirm live animal cargo acceptance on the Gulf leg specifically; long-haul cargo holds carrying live animals over 14 hours have their own space and temperature considerations separate from the shorter Caribbean-to-US leg.
