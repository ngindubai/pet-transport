  sections:
    - heading: "How Swiss FSVO permission works in practice"
      body: |
        Switzerland's FSVO (Federal Food Safety and Veterinary Office) classifies countries by rabies risk. Georgia is classified as a country with present rabies risk. Pets from such countries require advance import authorisation from the FSVO before they can legally enter Switzerland. This is not equivalent to the entry document: the permission is a separate step, applied for in advance, and must accompany the pet at the Swiss border alongside the health certificate.

        Apply through the FSVO's official portal at blv.admin.ch. The application requires the pet's microchip number, the intended travel date, and the intended point of entry into Switzerland. Allow at least 3 weeks for processing. Your IPATA-registered agent can prepare the application on your behalf, which reduces the risk of errors that delay approval.

        Do not buy plane tickets or book cargo before the FSVO permission is confirmed. If permission is delayed or a correction is needed, you will not be able to travel on the planned date.

    - heading: "Titre test from Tbilisi: the international lab process"
      body: |
        No EU or Swiss-approved FAVN laboratory is based in Georgia. Your vet in Tbilisi draws the blood, stores it in the correct preservative medium, and ships it cold via international courier to an approved lab in Europe or North America. The process takes a total of 2-4 weeks from blood draw to results.

        The blood draw must happen at least 30 days after the rabies vaccination. Once results come back, if the result is 0.5 IU/ml or above, the 3-month wait starts from the blood draw date, not the results date.

        Ask your Georgian vet explicitly whether they have sent samples internationally before and confirm which laboratory they use. Using the wrong lab means the results are not accepted by the FSVO. Your agent can recommend approved labs with reliable sample receipt from Tbilisi.
