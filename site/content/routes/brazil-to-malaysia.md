  overview: |
    Moving a pet from Brazil to Malaysia requires a DVS (Department of Veterinary Services) Malaysia import permit, a MAPA-endorsed health certificate, and arrival at Kuala Lumpur International Airport. There is no routine quarantine for compliant pets entering Peninsular Malaysia, which makes this more straightforward than some other Southeast Asian destinations. Emirates via Dubai and Qatar Airways via Doha are the main cargo routing options. Note that Sabah (East Malaysia) has different requirements due to recent rabies outbreaks.

        Emirates via Dubai is a practical option given Dubai's frequency to Kuala Lumpur. Singapore Airlines offers a good option via Singapore's world-class cargo facilities, but requires an additional Brazil-to-Singapore connection.

    - heading: "DVS Malaysia import permit and arrival process"
      body: |
        The Department of Veterinary Services (DVS) Malaysia, under the Ministry of Agriculture and Food Industries, is responsible for regulating pet imports. An import permit must be obtained from DVS Malaysia before the pet departs Brazil. The permit specifies entry conditions and the required documentation.

        On arrival at Kuala Lumpur International Airport (KUL), the pet is inspected by DVS Malaysia. If documentation is in order, the process is typically straightforward. Peninsular Malaysia has been declared rabies-free since 1999, which has streamlined some import procedures compared to other regional destinations.

        Contact DVS Malaysia (dvs.gov.my) for the current import permit application, the health certificate format required from Brazil, and any country-specific requirements that may apply to Brazilian-origin pets.
