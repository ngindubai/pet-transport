Canada is one of the more welcoming destinations for dogs travelling from Rwanda. Where the US route demands a titre test, a CDC-registered facility booking, and a CDC import form, Canada asks for something far simpler: a current rabies vaccination certificate in English or French, and a clean health certificate from your vet. No titre test, no quarantine, and no federal pre-registration step.

That difference cuts the preparation window from four to six months down to four to eight weeks for most dogs.

## What Canada Requires: A Straightforward Set of Rules

The Canadian Food Inspection Agency (CFIA) governs the import of pet dogs and cats. For dogs over three months of age arriving from Rwanda, the requirements are:

- Current rabies vaccination certificate in English or French
- Export health certificate from an RAB-authorised veterinarian
- No titre test required for personal companion dogs
- No quarantine on arrival

The CFIA inspects dogs on arrival at the port of entry. The inspection is brief if your documents are in order, but missing or incorrectly formatted papers will cause delays. The CFIA's requirements are published at inspection.canada.ca.

Cats travelling from Rwanda to Canada face similarly light requirements. A health certificate for export from RAB is the main document, alongside standard airline-specific requirements for crate size and health.

**Rwanda to Canada: requirement comparison**

| Requirement | Rwanda to Canada | Rwanda to US |
|---|---|---|
| Rabies vaccination | Yes | Yes |
| Titre test | Not required | Required (0.5 IU/ml) |
| Pre-registration | Not required | CDC facility booking required |
| Quarantine | None | None (CDC facility stay) |
| Timeline | 4-8 weeks | 16-24 weeks |

## The Rwanda Export Process

Before a dog or cat can leave Kigali, an RAB-authorised veterinarian must issue an export health certificate endorsed by the Rwanda Agriculture and Animal Resources Development Board. The RAB's authority covers all companion animal exports, and the endorsement is not optional.

No formal export permit is required for personal companion pets. The documents you need are the health certificate, the rabies vaccination record, and microchip documentation. The certificate must accurately reflect the animal's chip number, vaccination dates, and identifying details. If your vet usually issues certificates in Kinyarwanda, arrange for an English version from the outset, since Canada requires the vaccination certificate to be in English or French.

Allow several days for the RAB endorsement before travel. Your vet may be able to advise on current processing times at the RAB office.

## Routing from Kigali to Canada

There is no direct service from Kigali to Canada. The two main cargo paths are via Addis Ababa (Ethiopian Airlines, connecting to Toronto Pearson via Ethiopian Cargo) and via Brussels (RwandAir or Brussels Airlines, connecting to transatlantic services to Toronto or Montreal). Kenya Airways via Nairobi also provides connections to carriers serving Canada.

For most routes, Toronto (YYZ) is the most frequently served Canadian cargo gateway. Montreal (YUL) is an alternative if you need a francophone port of entry or if routing via Brussels makes it more practical.

Cargo booking lead times from Kigali are typically four to six weeks during busy periods. Confirm with the airline that live animals are accepted on your specific routing and that the crate meets IATA Live Animals Regulations. A pre-travel vet check is required within the airline's specified window before departure, usually seven to ten days.
