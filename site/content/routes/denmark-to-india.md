  h1: "Pet Transport from Denmark to India"
  overview: |
    India does not require a rabies titre test or advance import permit for personal pet dogs and cats arriving with correct documentation. No mandatory quarantine either, for compliant pets. By the standards of many international pet moves, Denmark to India is relatively straightforward.

    That said, there are hard rules on where you can land. Only five Indian airports have AQCS (Animal Quarantine and Certification Service) offices: Delhi, Mumbai, Chennai, Kolkata, and Bangalore. Your pet must arrive at one of these. No exceptions.

    Allow 6-10 weeks to prepare, mostly to account for the DVFA health certificate endorsement process and cargo booking lead times.
  sections:
    - heading: "India's AQCS entry process"
      body: |
        The AQCS is the authority responsible for all animal imports into India. For personal pets, there is no advance permit application. Your pet's entry is processed on arrival by the AQCS veterinarian at the airport.

        The AQCS inspector will check your pet's microchip (must be ISO 11784/11785 and readable), rabies vaccination records (current and within validity), and the DVFA-endorsed health certificate. If all documents are in order, your pet is cleared and released. The process typically takes 1-4 hours.

        Pets that arrive without proper documentation or with expired vaccinations may be held for observation, returned to origin, or in some cases euthanised under Indian biosecurity law. This is the regulatory backstop -- it underscores why getting the documents right matters.
    - heading: "Practical notes for Denmark to India"
      body: |
        The DVFA (Danish Veterinary and Food Administration) must endorse your health certificate. The certificate is only valid for 10 days from the issue date. Book both the vet appointment and the DVFA endorsement slot for the week before travel. DVFA endorsement is not instant -- allow 2-3 working days.

        For routing, Lufthansa via Frankfurt to Delhi is the most commonly used cargo option from Denmark. It is reliable and the FRA pet facilities are well-equipped. Emirates via Dubai and KLM via Amsterdam to Delhi are solid alternatives.

        If you are flying into Mumbai or Bangalore, check direct routing options from your Copenhagen hub. Not all carriers serve every Indian gateway city from all European hubs.

        Delhi (DEL) and Mumbai (BOM) are the busiest AQCS ports with the most experienced inspection teams. If you have a choice of arrival city, these two are the most reliable in terms of process efficiency.
