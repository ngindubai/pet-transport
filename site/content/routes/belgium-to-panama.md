  sections:
    - heading: "Panama's requirements for pets from Belgium"
      body: |
        Panama's MIDA (Ministerio de Desarrollo Agropecuario, mida.gob.pa) governs pet imports. For pets arriving from Belgium, the requirements are: ISO 11784/11785 microchip; rabies vaccination administered between 30 days and 12 months before arrival; pet must be over 4 months old; official health certificate in English or Spanish, endorsed by FASFC/AFSCA and authenticated by the Panamanian Embassy in Belgium.

        No import permit is needed. No quarantine for compliant arrivals. A MIDA inspector at Tocumen International Airport (PTY) checks the documents on arrival and collects a USD 20 inspection fee.

    - heading: "The Panamanian Embassy authentication step"
      body: |
        Panama requires the health certificate to be authenticated (legalised) by the Panamanian Embassy in the country of origin before travel. For Belgium, this is the Panamanian Embassy in Brussels. Authentication confirms that the FASFC/AFSCA endorsement is genuine.

        Your official veterinarian prepares the health certificate in English or Spanish. FASFC/AFSCA endorses it. You then present the endorsed certificate to the Panamanian Embassy in Brussels for authentication. Contact the Embassy in advance to confirm their requirements, fees, and turnaround time, as these can vary. Plan this step carefully within the 10-day validity window of the health certificate.

    - heading: "Routing from Brussels to Panama City"
      body: |
        Brussels Airport (BRU) is well positioned for connections through Frankfurt (FRA) and Paris CDG. The most practical routing for live animal cargo is BRU to FRA via Brussels Airlines (a Lufthansa Group carrier) or Lufthansa, then FRA to Miami (MIA) via Lufthansa Cargo, then MIA to Tocumen Airport (PTY) via Copa Airlines. Copa has its hub at PTY and operates frequent services from MIA.

        An alternative via CDG with Air France is available for some schedules. Confirm live animal cargo acceptance on all segments before booking. The BRU-FRA connection is short and well established.

    - heading: "FASFC/AFSCA export endorsement from Belgium"
      body: |
        Belgium's Federal Agency for the Safety of the Food Chain (FASFC/AFSCA, favv-afsca.be) endorses official export health certificates for pets leaving Belgium for non-EU destinations. An official veterinarian prepares the certificate in English or Spanish, referencing the microchip, vaccination details, and the animal's age. FASFC/AFSCA reviews and endorses it.

        Allow three to five business days for the FASFC/AFSCA endorsement. After endorsement, the certificate must be authenticated by the Panamanian Embassy in Brussels before departure.
