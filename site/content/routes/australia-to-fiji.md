  h1: "Pet Transport from Australia to Fiji"
  overview: |
    Seven days. That is the entire quarantine stay a dog or cat faces on arrival in Fiji from Australia, a fraction of the 30-day minimum that pets from the UK, the USA or Europe are held for at the same Koronivia facility. The gap comes down to one classification: Australia sits in BAF's Group A tier, the small rabies-free bracket that also covers New Zealand, French Polynesia and Vanuatu.

    That head start doesn't remove the paperwork. A microchip, a full vaccination course, and a BAF import permit still have to line up before travel, and Fiji Airways runs the only direct passenger network into Nadi from three Australian cities, with cargo booked through its own freight department rather than a standard ticket.

  sections:
    - heading: "Why Group A pets get a week, not a month"
      body: |
        BAF sorts every arriving pet into one of two tiers. Group A covers the handful of countries it treats as genuinely rabies-free, Australia among them, and those animals face a 7-day stay at Koronivia. Group B, everyone else, faces a 30-day minimum, often with a titre test and its own long wait beforehand.

        Australia's inclusion isn't a formality. It reflects the country's own tightly controlled rabies-free status, and it means an owner moving a pet from Sydney or Melbourne to Nadi is dealing with a genuinely different, shorter process than most of the routes on this site.

    - heading: "What still has to happen before the flight"
      body: |
        Shorter quarantine doesn't mean no preparation. The rabies vaccination and core vaccination course both need completing at least 30 days before export, and the BAF import permit, while it only takes around 5 working days to process once submitted, still has to be applied for with the vaccination paperwork attached.

        Fiji Airways connects Nadi to Sydney, Melbourne and Brisbane directly, and picked up a new Gold Coast route in mid-2026. That gives real flexibility on where the journey starts, more than most Pacific routes on this site, where a transfer through a third country is the norm.
