  sections:
    - heading: "FAVN titre test and the 90-day wait"
      body: |
        Norway applies EU Implementing Regulation 2026/636 through the EEA Agreement. Panama is classified as an unlisted third country, making the FAVN titre test mandatory. Blood must be drawn at least 30 days after the most recent rabies vaccination. The result must show at least 0.5 IU/ml at an EU-approved laboratory.

        The 90-day waiting period runs from the blood draw date. Mattilsynet grants no exemptions. After the 90 days, the MIDA/DECA export health certificate can be issued via SITERPA. The certificate is valid for 72 hours from the DECA countersignature; the EU-format entry document is then valid for 10 days from the MIDA endorsement. Plan the SITERPA appointment within 72 hours of actual departure from PTY.

    - heading: "Tapeworm treatment and advance notification"
      body: |
        All dogs entering Norway must receive praziquantel treatment (minimum 2.5 mg/kg) between 24 and 120 hours before arrival at Oslo Gardermoen. The vet records the product name, dose, exact date, and exact time in the health certificate. Cats are not subject to this requirement.

        Mattilsynet also requires 48-hour advance notification via the online portal at mattilsynet.no before non-EEA pets arrive. Provide pet details, certificate information, and flight details. Iberia's direct PTY-MAD service followed by a SAS or Norwegian connection to OSL is the most practical routing from Panama.
