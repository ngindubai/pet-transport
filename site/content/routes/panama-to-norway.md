content:
  h1: "Pet Transport Panama to Norway: 2026 Guide"
  overview: "Panama to Norway is the most complex route in this guide for dogs, combining two distinct mandatory requirements. First, Panama's unlisted status under EU and Norwegian rules triggers the full titre test pathway: FAVN blood draw at least 30 days after vaccination, result of 0.5 IU/ml or above, and a 90-day wait from the blood draw date. Second, Norway requires a mandatory Echinococcus tapeworm treatment for all dogs from non-exempt countries, administered by a vet 24-120 hours before arrival. For cats, only the titre test pathway applies. Total preparation time is 20-26 weeks."

  sections:
    - heading: "Two Mandatory Requirements: Titre Test and Echinococcus"
      body: |
        Understanding the difference between these two requirements is essential for planning the Panama-to-Norway route.

        Requirement 1: FAVN Titre Test (applies because Panama is unlisted)

        Panama is an unlisted third country under the EU and Norwegian pet import rules. Norway, as an EEA member, applies rules broadly equivalent to Commission Delegated Regulation (EU) 2026/131. For pets from unlisted countries, a FAVN rabies antibody test is mandatory. The blood must be drawn at least 30 days after the rabies vaccination. The result must be 0.5 IU/ml or above from an EU-approved laboratory. A 90-day waiting period then runs from the date of the blood draw. This cannot be shortened.

        Requirement 2: Echinococcus Treatment for Dogs (applies because Norway is Echinococcus-free)

        Norway is one of a small number of countries that is free of Echinococcus multilocularis (fox tapeworm). To maintain this status, Norway requires all dogs from non-exempt countries to receive a treatment with praziquantel or epsiprantel. The treatment must be administered by a licensed vet 24-120 hours before the dog arrives in Norway. The date, time, drug, dose, and administering vet's details must be documented on the health certificate or an accompanying declaration. Source: Mattilsynet (mattilsynet.no).

        Cats and ferrets are exempt from the Echinococcus requirement.

    - heading: "The Echinococcus Treatment at the Hub Airport"
      body: |
        The 24-120 hour window for the Echinococcus treatment means it is rarely possible to administer it in Panama itself. The journey from PTY to OSL typically takes 18-28 hours depending on connections. If you depart Panama with 120 hours (5 days) to spare, you would need to leave at least five days before your Oslo arrival date, which conflicts with the cargo booking and does not solve the problem for a same-day departure.

        In practice, the treatment is administered at the hub airport during the layover. If routing via London Heathrow (LHR), Amsterdam (AMS), or Frankfurt (FRA), a vet near the airport is arranged for the layover period. The layover must be at least four to six hours to allow for the vet visit and documentation.

        PetTransportGlobal arranges these layover vet visits as part of the full service for Norway routes. The vet administers praziquantel at the correct dose, signs the health certificate addendum, and the animal continues to Oslo within the window.

    - heading: "Mattilsynet Requirements in Full"
      body: |
        Mattilsynet (Norwegian Food Safety Authority, mattilsynet.no) carries out veterinary border inspections at Oslo Gardermoen Airport (OSL). For a dog arriving from Panama, Mattilsynet checks:

        Microchip: ISO 11784/11785, implanted before vaccination. Scanned on arrival.

        Rabies vaccination: Current and post-microchip.

        FAVN titre test: 0.5 IU/ml or above, blood drawn 30+ days post-vaccination, 90-day wait elapsed from blood draw date.

        Echinococcus treatment: Praziquantel or epsiprantel, administered 24-120 hours before arrival, documented with the vet's details and the date/time.

        Health certificate: EU-equivalent format, DINASA-endorsed, issued within 10 days of travel. Both the titre test and Echinococcus treatment details must appear in the certificate.

        For cats: same as above except no Echinococcus treatment is needed.

    - heading: "Flight Options from PTY to Oslo"
      body: |
        Oslo Gardermoen Airport (OSL) is the main international cargo hub for Norway. Bergen (BGO) and Stavanger (SVG) have very limited international cargo. Route to OSL.

        Copa Airlines from PTY connects to AMS and MAD. Copa to AMS pairs with SAS or KLM cargo to OSL. Copa to MAD pairs with SAS or Norwegian Air Shuttle cargo to OSL.

        Iberia Cargo flies PTY to MAD, from which SAS, Norwegian, or Iberia-associated cargo continues to OSL.

        KLM via AMS is a strong option. AMS to OSL is a short sector with multiple daily services, and KLM's live animal handling at Schiphol is reliable.

        Lufthansa via Frankfurt is useful for large dogs. FRA to OSL is served by SAS and Lufthansa. The Frankfurt animal centre handles the transatlantic leg well.

        Build in enough layover time at the hub for the Echinococcus vet visit, particularly if routing via LHR or AMS. Four to six hours minimum is recommended.

    - heading: "Completing the DINASA Health Certificate for Norway"
      body: |
        The health certificate for Norway is the EU-equivalent format. Before travel, the document signed by the MIDA-registered vet and endorsed by DINASA must include:

        - The ISO microchip number
        - Rabies vaccination details (product, batch, date, expiry)
        - FAVN titre test details (lab name, approval number, blood draw date, result in IU/ml)
        - Confirmation that 90 days have elapsed from the blood draw date

        After the Echinococcus treatment at the hub airport, the administering vet adds a supplementary declaration or an amendment to the certificate showing:

        - Drug name (praziquantel or epsiprantel)
        - Dose administered
        - Date and time of treatment
        - Vet's full name, address, and licence number

        Mattilsynet checks both sections. Carry original documents; copies are not sufficient.

faqs:
  - question: "My dog is travelling from Panama to Norway. Which is arranged first, the titre test or the Echinococcus treatment?"
    answer: "They are sequential requirements, not concurrent. The titre test process takes place in Panama over 20-26 weeks and must be complete before travel. The Echinococcus treatment takes place at the hub airport during the layover, 24-120 hours before arrival in Oslo. You do not need to worry about the Echinococcus treatment until your cargo is booked and your travel dates are set. Focus first on completing the titre test and 90-day wait."
  - question: "Does the Echinococcus treatment need to be documented before the health certificate is endorsed by DINASA in Panama?"
    answer: "No. The DINASA-endorsed health certificate is prepared and endorsed in Panama covering the titre test and rabies vaccination details. The Echinococcus treatment documentation is added as a supplementary declaration or amendment at the layover vet visit, after DINASA has endorsed the main certificate. Mattilsynet accepts this combination of the main DINASA certificate plus the layover vet's supplementary declaration."
  - question: "Can I fly directly from Panama to Oslo without a stopover, to avoid the hub layover?"
    answer: "There is no direct flight between PTY and OSL. A stopover is unavoidable, and it is actually the practical solution for the Echinococcus treatment. Even if a direct route existed, the 24-120 hour window for the treatment would require the treatment to be given in Panama 1-5 days before departure, which is logistically complex. The layover approach at LHR, AMS, or FRA is the established and recommended method for this route."
