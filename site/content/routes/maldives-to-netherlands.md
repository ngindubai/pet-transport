content:
  h1: "Pet Transport from Maldives to Netherlands: 2026 Guide"
  overview: |
    Schiphol has something most European gateways don't: a purpose-built animal reception facility, the Air France-KLM-Martinair Cargo Animal Hotel, staffed around the clock with a vet on call, where the Dutch food authority actually performs its border check on arriving pets. It's a genuinely different arrival experience from a standard cargo terminal, provided the routing runs through KLM or its Air France-KLM Cargo partners.

    None of that changes the paperwork leaving Male. The Netherlands treats the Maldives as an EU-unlisted country, so microchip, rabies vaccination, an FAVN titre test at an EU-approved lab and a 90-day wait from the blood draw date all still apply before the cat can board.

  sections:
    - heading: "Why this route runs on cats, not a dog ban"
      body: |
        A Maldives-resident dog faces no legal barrier to leaving the country. What limits this route to cats in practice is the Maldives Customs Service's own ban on dog imports, which leaves almost no resident dog population to begin with. If you're the rare exception, this isn't a standard export job, it needs direct engagement with Maldivian authorities first.
    - heading: "Exporting a cat from Male with no ministry to sign off"
      body: |
        There's no export permit and no government counter-signature built into a routine departure. A private vet in Male writes the certificate, and agents typically accept it as issued. Finding cargo capacity out of Velana International, which handles far more resort freight than live pets, is usually the harder part of the job.
    - heading: "The FAVN titre test and the 90-day wait"
      body: |
        Because the Maldives sits outside Implementing Regulation (EU) 2026/636's approved list, Commission Delegated Regulation (EU) 2026/131 requires blood drawn at least 30 days after vaccination to clear 0.5 IU/ml at an EU-approved lab. The 90-day wait then runs from the draw date, not the result date, a detail that catches out more owners on unlisted-country routes than any other.
    - heading: "The Schiphol Animal Hotel, and why the routing matters"
      body: |
        Book with KLM or an Air France-KLM Cargo connection and your cat clears its NVWA check inside a facility designed for the job: climate-controlled transfer from the aircraft, trained attendants, an on-call vet. Route through Doha with Qatar Airways or through Dubai with Emirates instead, and the arrival experience at Schiphol is standard cargo processing rather than this specific facility, still compliant, just less specialised.

  - question: "Can my dog leave the Maldives for the Netherlands?"
    answer: "Legally, yes, there's no restriction on a resident dog leaving. In practice almost nobody there owns one, because the Maldives bans dog imports. This guide is written around a cat because that's what almost every real enquiry involves."
  - question: "Why does the 90-day wait start from the blood draw and not the result?"
    answer: "Because that's how Commission Delegated Regulation (EU) 2026/131 defines the clock. Waiting for the lab result before mentally starting the countdown costs you weeks you didn't need to lose."

    answer: "No, for a cat that completes every step correctly with a valid EU-format certificate. Incomplete paperwork can mean the animal is held, quarantined at the owner's cost, or refused entry."
