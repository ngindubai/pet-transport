  sections:
    - heading: "Rwanda's RAB: straightforward when you start early"
      body: |
        The Rwanda Agriculture and Animal Resources Development Board (RAB) at rab.gov.rw manages companion animal imports. The RAB is one of the more organised import authorities in sub-Saharan Africa. Apply with your pet's microchip number, vaccination records, breed, and intended travel date at least eight weeks before travel.

        Once the permit is issued, it forms the core import document alongside the Italian health certificate. At Kigali International Airport (KGL), the RAB inspection team checks the permit and health certificate on arrival. Compliant arrivals from Italy do not face quarantine.

    - heading: "Ethiopian Airlines from Rome to Kigali via Addis"
      body: |
        Ethiopian Airlines operates from Rome Fiumicino (FCO) to Addis Ababa (ADD) as part of its European network. Ethiopian Cargo handles live animal freight on this corridor and from ADD, Ethiopian connects to Kigali (KGL) as part of its extensive African route network.

        The FCO-ADD-KGL routing typically takes 10-14 hours including the connection at Addis Ababa. Ethiopian's live animal procedures at ADD are well established; the airline carries significant volumes of livestock and companion animals across Africa. Confirm live animal cargo acceptance and crate specifications with Ethiopian Cargo before booking.

        Brussels Airlines via Brussels (BRU-KGL) is the alternative if the Addis routing does not suit your dates. Getting from Italy to Brussels involves a connecting service from FCO or MXP, then Brussels Airlines' established BRU-KGL direct service. Total transit via Brussels is typically 12-16 hours.

    - heading: "Italy's export certificate: what the ASL process involves"
      body: |
        For a non-EU destination like Rwanda, you cannot use the Italian EU pet passport as the export document. The correct document is the certificato sanitario per l'esportazione, issued by a veterinario ufficiale at your local ASL (Azienda Sanitaria Locale).

        Call your local ASL veterinary department to book an appointment. The official vet examines your pet and issues the certificate covering microchip, vaccination, health status, and description. The document is then endorsed by the competent authority.

        The certificate must be issued within 10 days of travel. Contact your ASL 3-4 weeks before travel to check appointment availability, particularly in busy periods such as July and August when Italian public services can be slower.
