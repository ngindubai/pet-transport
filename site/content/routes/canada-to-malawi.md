  h1: "Pet Transport from Canada to Malawi"
  overview: |
    Two flight numbers cover almost the entire distance on this route. ET553 flies Toronto to Addis Ababa nonstop, the only such service between Canada and continental Africa. ET877 picks up from there, Addis Ababa to Lilongwe, also nonstop, also several times a week. Both are Ethiopian Airlines, both fly Boeing 787s, and a pet booked this way never changes carrier.

    Malawi's own entry conditions don't ask for a rabies titre test from any country, Canada included, a genuinely lighter position than several destinations covered elsewhere on this site. What Malawi does insist on is a permit from its Department of Animal Health and Livestock Development, since Canada doesn't qualify for the regional exemption open to Malawi's SADC neighbours.

    Ten to fourteen weeks is a realistic budget, with the DAHLD permit rather than the flight or the vet work setting that timeline.

  sections:
    - heading: "A rare single-carrier route into Southern Africa"
      body: |
        Most Canada-to-Southern-Africa pet moves this site documents need a European stopover, then an African connection, often with the pet handed between two or three different airlines' cargo systems along the way. Ethiopian Airlines collapses that into two flights under one company: ET553 nonstop from Toronto to Addis Ababa, then ET877 nonstop from Addis Ababa to Lilongwe, both flown on the same aircraft type.

        British Airways via Heathrow and Johannesburg remains a workable fallback if Ethiopian's schedule doesn't fit a specific travel date, but it brings the pet through two carrier handovers rather than none, first onto South African Airways at Johannesburg and then into Lilongwe.

    - heading: "Why no titre test, and what DAHLD checks instead"
      body: |
        Malawi doesn't operate the kind of listed-country system that drives a titre test requirement elsewhere. It asks for a current rabies vaccination, given between 30 days and 12 months before arrival, and doesn't ask for antibody proof from any origin country. A Canadian-vaccinated pet meets that condition without a laboratory involved.

        The DAHLD import permit is the part worth taking seriously instead. Once issued it's valid for six months, but no processing timeline is published, so contact should start ten to twelve weeks out rather than six to eight. Canada doesn't get the exemption available to Malawi's fellow SADC states, so the full application applies regardless of how light the rest of the paperwork looks.

    - heading: "What to confirm before booking"
      body: |
        Present the Canadian International Health Certificate to DAHLD officials within seven days of landing in Lilongwe; it stands in for a Malawi-specific certificate since none has been negotiated between the two countries. Confirm this detail with a current agent or DAHLD directly, since a summarised guidance page can lag an actual rule change.
