  h1: "Pet Transport from Hungary to Azerbaijan"
  overview: |
    The Budapest to Baku pet move is manageable in 3-5 weeks preparation time for most pet owners, provided one detail is not overlooked: dogs must receive an Echinococcus treatment 24-120 hours before arrival and that treatment must be recorded on the Nébih health certificate. Azerbaijan's Ministry of Agriculture enforces this requirement at Heydar Aliyev International Airport (GYD), and it applies to all dogs regardless of breed or size.

    There is no quarantine period for compliant arrivals and no titre test is required from EU countries. Hungary's Nébih (National Food Chain Safety Office) issues the health certificate after an authorised official vet examination, and Turkish Airlines Cargo provides the practical routing via Istanbul from Budapest Ferenc Liszt (BUD) to GYD.

    One further point that catches people out: Azerbaijan only accepts pet arrivals through Heydar Aliyev Airport in Baku. Any routing that arrives at a different airport will result in the pet being refused entry.

  sections:
    - heading: "The Echinococcus requirement for dogs"
      body: |
        Azerbaijan requires all dogs entering the country to be treated against Echinococcus (tapeworm) within a specific window: no fewer than 24 hours and no more than 120 hours before arrival at GYD. The treatment must use a praziquantel-based product and the administering vet must record it on the official health certificate.

        In practice, this means the Echinococcus treatment happens at the same appointment as the Nébih health certificate, which is already scheduled within 10 days of travel. The timing works naturally: book the vet appointment 3-4 days before the flight to ensure the 24-hour minimum has passed by the time the pet lands in Baku. The vet records the treatment date and product on the certificate, and Nébih endorses it along with the rest of the documentation.

        This requirement applies to dogs only. Cats travelling to Azerbaijan do not require the Echinococcus treatment.

    - heading: "Nébih certification and what the process involves"
      body: |
        Exporting a pet from Hungary to any third country, including Azerbaijan, requires an official animal health certificate issued under EU rules and endorsed by Nébih. The process runs as follows: an official vet (Nébih-authorised) examines the pet, prepares the certificate, and then submits it to Nébih for endorsement. Allow 1-2 working days for Nébih to process the endorsement after the vet appointment.

        The certificate is valid for 10 days from the examination date. Count 10 days forward to the arrival date in Baku, not to the departure from Budapest. If the routing includes an overnight layover in Istanbul, factor that into the calculation.

        No export permit is required for personal companion pets leaving Hungary. The health certificate, the EU pet passport or vaccination record, and the microchip are the three things Azerbaijan inspectors will verify on arrival.

    - heading: "Flying from Budapest to Baku"
      body: |
        There is no direct cargo flight between BUD and GYD for live animals. Turkish Airlines Cargo, operating via Istanbul (IST), is the standard choice from Budapest. The airline runs multiple daily BUD-IST flights, and IST has good connections to Baku. Total journey time BUD to GYD is typically 6-8 hours including the Istanbul connection.

        Book through a Turkish Airlines Cargo agent or an IPATA-registered pet relocation specialist. Confirm live animal acceptance on the specific flight and dates, and provide crate dimensions when booking, as capacity for live animals is limited per flight. Temperature restrictions can apply during summer months if ambient temperatures at BUD, IST, or GYD exceed 29-30 degrees Celsius.

        Wizz Air operates heavily out of Budapest but does not accept live animal cargo on any route in 2026. LOT Polish Airlines via Warsaw is an alternative if Turkish Airlines dates are not workable.
