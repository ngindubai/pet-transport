  overview: |
    Moving a pet from Denmark to Uruguay involves an MGAP import permit and, for dogs, a Praziquantel antiparasitic treatment within a specific window before arrival.   sections:
    - heading: "MGAP permit and core requirements"
      body: |
        Uruguay's MGAP (Ministerio de Ganaderia, Agricultura y Pesca) handles all animal import permits and inspects pets at Carrasco International Airport (MVD) in Montevideo. Apply for the import permit early: contact MGAP through gub.uy/ministerio-ganaderia-agricultura-pesca. Processing takes several weeks.

        Requirements for personal companion pets from Denmark: ISO microchip, rabies vaccination (30 days to 12 months old), full vaccination records, and a Foedevarestyrelsen-endorsed official health certificate. For dogs, Praziquantel treatment is also required. No titre test required. No quarantine for compliant arrivals.

    - heading: "Praziquantel treatment for dogs"
      body: |
        Uruguay requires all incoming dogs to be treated against Taenia Echinococcus (hydatid tapeworm) using a Praziquantel-containing antiparasitic. The treatment must be administered between 72 hours and 30 days before arrival at Carrasco Airport. The date, product, dose, and the dog's microchip number are recorded on the health certificate and checked by MGAP on arrival.

        Calculate the treatment window from your scheduled arrival date. If you arrive on a Monday, the 30-day window opens 30 days before and the 72-hour cutoff is the Friday before departure. The Foedevarestyrelsen health certificate, which records the treatment, must be issued within 10 days of travel and endorsed within the same window.

        This requirement does not apply to cats.

    - heading: "Cargo from Copenhagen to Montevideo"
      body: |
        Copenhagen Kastrup (CPH) has no direct cargo connections to Carrasco International Airport (MVD). The main routing is via Madrid Barajas (MAD) with Iberia. SAS connects Copenhagen to Madrid, and Iberia cargo serves Montevideo from Madrid.

        An alternative is via Lima (LIM) with LATAM. Connect from Copenhagen to a European hub via SAS, then LATAM cargo to Montevideo through Peru. Journey times via South America run 18-24 hours including connections. Ensure the crate is IATA-compliant and suitable for the expected transit duration. Book through a cargo agent and confirm live animal acceptance on all segments.
