content:
  h1: "Pet Transport from Czech Republic to Uruguay"
  overview: |
    Pet moves from the Czech Republic to Uruguay involve two key administrative steps beyond the standard vaccination and health certificate process: an MGAP import permit that must be obtained before travel, and a Praziquantel treatment for dogs given within a specific window before arrival. Neither is difficult, but both require planning ahead. Allow at least six weeks from start to finish; eight to ten is better if the MGAP permit needs to be obtained from scratch.

    Uruguay's MGAP (Ministerio de Ganaderia, Agricultura y Pesca) manages all pet imports at Carrasco International Airport (MVD) in Montevideo. There is no quarantine. Inspection at MVD is documentary and physical; if the paperwork is correct and the Praziquantel timing is recorded, most arrivals are straightforward.

    Prague (PRG) connects to Montevideo (MVD) via LATAM Airlines through Lima or Sao Paulo, or via European hubs with Air France or Iberia. This is a long routing, and the cargo transit times should be factored in when thinking about your pet's welfare and the timing of the health certificate.

  sections:
    - heading: "MGAP Import Permit"
      body: |
        Uruguay requires an import permit from MGAP before the animal travels. Start this application early, ideally six to ten weeks before your intended departure. The permit covers the specific animal being imported and should match the details on the health certificate: species, breed, microchip number, and owner information. MGAP can be reached via gub.uy/ministerio-ganaderia-agricultura-pesca; a pet transport agent with South American experience can often facilitate the permit application more quickly.

        Without the permit, the animal cannot enter. MGAP inspectors at Carrasco Airport (MVD) will check it against the health certificate and vaccination records on arrival. Keep the original permit with the travel documents; do not rely on a scan or copy at the airport.

    - heading: "Praziquantel Treatment for Dogs"
      body: |
        Uruguay requires all dogs to receive a Praziquantel treatment before entry. The treatment must be given between 72 hours and 30 days before arrival. That window gives some flexibility, but the exact date and dose must be recorded on the health certificate or a supporting veterinary declaration that the official vet includes with the certificate.

        The Praziquantel requirement is specifically for tapeworm (Echinococcus) control. Make sure the private vet uses a product containing Praziquantel as the active ingredient. General-purpose worming tablets may not satisfy the requirement. Confirm with the SVS-authorised official vet that the treatment record meets MGAP standards before the health certificate is finalised.

    - heading: "Czech Export Certification"
      body: |
        The export process in the Czech Republic follows standard EU pet travel rules. No export permit is required. An SVS-authorised official vet examines the animal, confirms all vaccinations and treatments, and issues the EU-format health certificate. The SVS (Statni veterinarni sprava, svscr.cz) then endorses it. The certificate is valid for 10 days from signing, so the appointment should be in the final week before departure.

        Given the long flight routing to Uruguay, consider the total transit time when scheduling the vet appointment. If your pet is in transit for 36-48 hours, the 10-day window needs to comfortably cover the full journey. Prague's Vaclav Havel Airport (PRG) is the cargo departure point.

    - heading: "Airline Options and Journey Time"
      body: |
        LATAM Airlines is the primary carrier for this route, routing cargo through Lima (LIM) or Sao Paulo (GRU) before the final leg to Montevideo (MVD). Iberia via Madrid (MAD) and Air France via Paris CDG also offer onward connections to MVD. Given the multiple connections involved, total journey time for the animal can be 24-48 hours depending on the routing and layover durations.

        Cargo from Prague to Montevideo is at the upper end of the cost range for Caribbean and Latin American routes from Central Europe: EUR 1,000-2,800 depending on animal size and crate dimensions. This is a longer, more logistically complex journey than moves to the Caribbean, and the cost reflects that. Professional coordination by a transport agent (EUR 250-600) is worth considering for a route this complex.
