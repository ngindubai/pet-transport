content:
  h1: "Pet Transport from the United States to Russia"
  overview: |
    No rabies titre test, no import permit for up to two pets, no quarantine for a compliant animal: on paper, Russia is one of the more relaxed destinations covered on this site. What makes the route genuinely hard has nothing to do with Russian import rules and everything to do with getting there at all.

    There has been no direct flight between any US gateway and Moscow since March 2022. Every move currently runs through Istanbul, Dubai or Doha, on two separate live-animal bookings rather than one through-ticket. Add Russia's unusually tight 5-day window for endorsing the US export certificate, and the real planning challenge on this route is timing and routing, not paperwork volume.

  sections:
    - heading: "Why there is no direct flight, and what that means for booking"
      body: |
        The FAA and the US Department of Transportation blocked Russian-registered aircraft from all US airspace with an order that took full effect on 2 March 2022, joining a wider international response to the invasion of Ukraine. Russia closed its own airspace to US-linked carriers in turn. Both restrictions were still in force in mid-2026. Russian officials asked Washington to lift the sanctions on Aeroflot specifically, as reported by The Moscow Times in April 2025, and a further round of Istanbul-hosted talks in 2026 has not produced a restored route.

        Turkey sits outside both bans, which is why Istanbul has become the default connection point, alongside Dubai and Doha as fallback hubs. Your pet needs a separate booking, cleared by a separate cargo team, on each leg, because no airline currently sells a single through-fare across the gap.

    - heading: "What Russia actually asks for from a US-origin pet"
      body: |
        Russia does not require a rabies titre test from any country of origin, the United States included, which is genuinely unusual among the destinations covered on this site. What it does require is precise on timing: rabies vaccination at least 20 days old and never more than 12 months old on arrival, plus the full vaccine record for dogs or cats alongside rabies.

        Up to 2 pets travel under the standard personal-use rules, with no import permit and no quarantine for a compliant animal. A third pet, or a species other than a dog or cat, needs a Rosselkhoznadzor permit arranged in advance.

    - heading: "The 5-day endorsement window, and why it drives the whole schedule"
      body: |
        Most destinations give a US owner a reasonably wide window to get the export certificate endorsed before departure. Russia does not. APHIS confirms that while the clinical exam itself can happen up to 14 days before travel, the certificate has to be issued by the USDA-accredited vet and endorsed by APHIS within 5 days of the pet's actual departure.

        On a route with a confirmed two-leg flight already booked weeks in advance, that means the vet appointment cannot be scheduled loosely. It has to sit inside a 5-day window that ends on a specific departure date, which in turn depends on both cargo legs having confirmed space. Book the flight, then work backwards to the vet appointment, not the other way round.
