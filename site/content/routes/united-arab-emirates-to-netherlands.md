  sections:
    - heading: "Why UAE to Netherlands is among the cleanest Middle East to EU moves"
      body: |
        Two factors combine to make this route less complicated than most Middle East to EU corridors. First, the UAE is listed under EU pet travel rules: no FAVN titre test, no blood sampling, no 3-month wait. The preparation window is four to six weeks rather than five months. Second, the Netherlands lifted its national breed ban in 2008. There are no breed-specific import restrictions at the Dutch border, and the NVWA does not apply breed-based criteria during the arrival inspection.

        For a Dubai family with a Rottweiler, a Husky, or any breed that might face restrictions in Germany or France, the Netherlands removes that concern entirely. The import documentation process is the same for any breed: MOCCAE export permit, MOCCAE-endorsed EU certificate, current vaccination, and ISO microchip predating the vaccination.

        Emirates and KLM both operate direct from DXB to Schiphol. The flight time is approximately seven hours, which is manageable for most pets in an IATA-compliant crate with water.

    - heading: "MOCCAE export documentation: the two steps every UAE owner needs"
      body: |
        Exporting a pet from the UAE requires two separate MOCCAE actions. The first is the export permit, which authorises the animal to leave the country. This is obtained through the MOCCAE online portal or a registered pet relocation agent. The permit is needed before departure and should be obtained several weeks in advance.

        The second is the MOCCAE endorsement of the EU-format health certificate. Your licensed vet in the UAE issues the certificate after examining your pet and reviewing the microchip and vaccination records. MOCCAE then endorses it to give it official standing for EU customs. This endorsed certificate must be dated within 10 days of your travel date.

        The two-step process is straightforward with guidance. Most UAE relocation agents handle both the permit application and the certificate endorsement as part of their service, which avoids the scheduling complications that come with managing the MOCCAE portal independently.

    - heading: "NVWA at Schiphol: what the Dutch inspection covers"
      body: |
        NVWA (Nederlandse Voedsel- en Warenautoriteit) is the Dutch authority responsible for live animal import inspections at Amsterdam Schiphol. When your pet arrives from the UAE, NVWA inspectors check the microchip number against the certificate, the EU-format health certificate with MOCCAE endorsement, the vaccination record, and the certificate issuance date within the 10-day window.

        Because the UAE is EU-listed, there is no titre test document to verify. The inspection for a UAE pet is more straightforward than for pets from non-listed countries. Inspection time for compliant pets from listed countries at Schiphol is typically one to two hours from cargo arrival to release.

        Your cargo agent tracks the inspection status and notifies you when your pet is cleared for collection from the Schiphol cargo terminal. If you are using a door-to-door relocation service, the agent arranges onward transport from the terminal.
