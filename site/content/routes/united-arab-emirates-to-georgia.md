  overview: |
    Georgia doesn't run a formal import permit system for pets, which surprises some owners moving from countries where a stamped document is the whole point. Instead, the National Food Agency asks for advance notification before your pet's arrival. Skip that step because there's no fee attached and you'll still run into problems at Tbilisi International.

    The UAE side of this route is identical to every other MOCCAE-governed departure: an export certificate, applied for online and confirmed at an in-person clinic exam. FlyDubai's direct Dubai-Tbilisi service means the journey itself is a single leg.

  sections:
    - heading: "Notification, not permit: how Georgia's system actually works"
      body: |
        Rather than issuing a formal import permit, Georgia's National Food Agency asks that pet owners notify them in advance of an arrival. This is a real regulatory step, not a formality, and needs to happen before the pet lands at Tbilisi International Airport (TBS). Current rabies vaccination and a MOCCAE-endorsed health certificate complete the requirements. There's no titre test and no routine quarantine for pets arriving with everything in order.

    - heading: "The UAE's fixed export process"
      body: |
        Every pet leaving the UAE needs a MOCCAE export health certificate, regardless of destination. The process starts online through the MOCCAE portal (a UAE Pass login is required), continues with an in-person exam at an approved clinic in Dubai or Abu Dhabi, and finishes with a final check at the airport before departure. The certificate lasts 30 days once issued, so line up your travel date first.

    - heading: "FlyDubai's direct Tbilisi service"
      body: |
        FlyDubai flies non-stop from Dubai to Tbilisi as part of its wider Caucasus network, keeping this route to a single leg rather than a connection through a third hub. Qatar Airways Cargo via Doha is a reasonable backup if FlyDubai's live animal cargo space is unavailable on your preferred date.
