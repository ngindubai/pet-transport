  h1: "Pet Transport from Ireland to Azerbaijan"
  overview: |
    Azerbaijan does not require a titre test for EU-origin pets, and there is no quarantine for compliant arrivals at Heydar Aliyev International Airport in Baku. Ireland's EU membership means the export process is the standard DAFM health certificate route, and preparation can typically be completed in three to five weeks assuming vaccinations are current.

    There are no direct cargo flights from Dublin to Baku. Turkish Airlines via Istanbul is the primary option, with Qatar Airways via Doha as a good alternative during summer. Book cargo space well in advance, as capacity on Caucasus routes can be limited.

  sections:
    - heading: "What Azerbaijan requires at the border"
      body: |
        Azerbaijan's veterinary border controls are administered by the Food Safety Agency (FSA), fsa.gov.az. Officers inspect documentation at Heydar Aliyev International Airport (GYD) in Baku before releasing your pet.

        The core requirements are a valid rabies vaccination administered at least 21 days before entry, an official health certificate issued within 10 days of travel, and an ISO-compliant microchip. Azerbaijan does not require a titre test for EU-member-state pets.

        For 1-2 personal companion animals, no import permit is required. Verify current requirements with FSA Azerbaijan if travelling with more animals or if your pet is a breed that faces restrictions in other countries.

    - heading: "DAFM health certificate process from Ireland"
      body: |
        Ireland's export health certificate for non-EU destinations is the EU-format official veterinary certificate signed by a DAFM-registered official vet and then endorsed by DAFM (Department of Agriculture, Food and the Marine). Both steps must complete within 10 days of departure.

        Schedule the vet examination within 10 days of your travel date. After signing, allow 1-2 working days for DAFM endorsement. Your regular vet can handle vaccinations and microchipping, but the official health certificate must come from a DAFM-registered official vet. Check gov.ie/agriculture for the list of authorised vets.

    - heading: "Cargo routing from Dublin to Baku"
      body: |
        There is no direct cargo service from Dublin (DUB) to Baku (GYD). Turkish Airlines Cargo via Istanbul (IST) is the primary option. Book through an approved cargo agent and confirm pet acceptance on both the DUB-IST and IST-GYD legs.

        During June to September, Turkish Airlines applies temperature embargoes on certain connections. Qatar Airways Cargo via Doha (DOH) is a practical alternative in summer: Qatar Airways accepts live animals in cargo and has good facilities at Doha's Hamad International Airport. Book through a Qatar Cargo agent and confirm acceptance on both legs.
