  overview: |
    Vienna or Istanbul, that is the real decision for a Slovenian owner flying a pet to Greece, since Ljubljana has no direct service to Athens. Whichever connection you pick, the entry requirements do not change: this is intra-EU travel, so there is no titre test, no quarantine, and no import permit.

    Austrian Airlines via Vienna is the shorter hop for most departures out of Ljubljana. Turkish Airlines via Istanbul covers the same ground on a different network, useful when Vienna cargo space or timing does not suit. Either way, the paperwork runs through Slovenia's UVHVVR and lands in front of Greek border vets who check it against the microchip.

  sections:
    - heading: "Vienna or Istanbul: picking your connection"
      body: |
        Austrian Airlines connects Ljubljana to Vienna directly, then on to Athens, and the short first leg makes this the more time-efficient option for most travel dates. Turkish Airlines via Istanbul is a solid second choice, drawing on a wider long-haul network that sometimes offers better availability during peak summer travel.

        Book cabin or cargo pet space on both legs as soon as your dates are set. Airlines cap the number of animals per flight, and that limit fills quickly on popular summer departures to Athens.

    - heading: "UVHVVR, the passport, and the April 2026 change"
      body: |
        Slovenia's veterinary authority, UVHVVR, authorises the vets who issue and update the EU pet passport. The document is valid for the pet's life provided the rabies vaccination recorded inside stays current, and since 22 April 2026 it has been restricted to owners primarily resident in the EU.

        Non-EU nationals resident in Slovenia use a single-use AHC instead, endorsed by UVHVVR and issued within 10 days of travel. Whichever document you carry, Greece's Directorate General of Veterinary Services checks it against the microchip number on arrival, so verify the chip is recorded correctly before departure.
