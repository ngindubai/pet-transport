content:
  h1: "Pet Transport from the United Arab Emirates to Tunisia"
  overview: |
    A Rottweiler with a spotless MOCCAE file still can't land in Tunis. Tunisia bans Pit Bull, Rottweiler, Tosa and Mastiff-type dogs and their crosses outright, whatever paperwork accompanies them from the UAE. That's the one fact worth confirming before anything else on this route.

    For every other pet, the move is comparatively light. No titre test applies coming from the UAE, unlike the reverse direction into Dubai. A MOCCAE health certificate, a current rabies vaccination and a DGSV-compliant reformat cover the paperwork, and Emirates and Tunisair both fly Dubai to Tunis nonstop. Five to six weeks is realistic from a standing start.

  sections:
    - heading: "The breed check comes before everything else"
      body: |
        Tunisia's DGSV refuses entry to Pit Bull, Rottweiler, Tosa and Mastiff-type dogs and their crosses, a blanket rule applied at the border regardless of where the dog is travelling from or how complete its paperwork is. A dog a UAE vet would describe as a mastiff mix needs checking against this list before a MOCCAE appointment gets booked, not after.

        There's no appeal process at Tunis-Carthage. A dog refused entry returns to its point of origin at the owner's expense, which makes this the single highest-stakes check on the whole route.

    - heading: "Exporting from the UAE is the easy half"
      body: |
        MOCCAE's own service explicitly exempts companion animals from needing a certificate of origin or a copy of Tunisia's import approval before issuing the export certificate. A physical exam, a health certificate valid 30 days from issue, and a fee of AED 100 to AED 400 depending on the service tier, that's the whole UAE-side process.

        No titre test applies on this direction. Tunisia's DGSV doesn't ask for one from pets arriving from the UAE, a lighter requirement than the FAVN test MOCCAE demands of a Tunisia-origin pet heading the other way.

    - heading: "Dubai or Abu Dhabi to Tunis"
      body: |
        Emirates and Tunisair both fly the Dubai-Tunis city pair nonstop, roughly 13 flights a week combined at about six hours' flying time. Book manifest cargo through either carrier's cargo arm; Qatar Airways via Doha is a workable fallback if neither direct option has space on the required date. Tunis-Carthage is the only Tunisian airport cleared for pet imports, so the booking has to terminate there specifically.
