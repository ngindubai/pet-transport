  h1: "Pet Transport from France to Maldives: 2026 Guide"
  overview: |
    French Bee's new Orly-Male service, starting 19 December 2026 as part of a triangle route on to Colombo, is the first scheduled French passenger link to the Maldives in years. It changes nothing about who can travel on it. Import of dogs into the Maldives is prohibited outright under Maldives Customs Service rules, and no airline route changes that.

    A cat's list is much shorter: microchip, rabies vaccination given 30 days to 12 months before arrival, the core feline vaccines, and a health certificate signed within 10 days of travel. Permits and titre tests are the areas where published guidance is thinnest, so treat this as one route worth a direct check before booking.

  sections:
    - heading: "Answering the dog question first"
      body: |
        There's no extended paperwork route, no age exception and no assistance-animal carve-out that gets a dog into the Maldives. It sits on the customs prohibited list alongside items like alcohol and pork products, applied without a standard pet-owner exception. If a service dog is genuinely involved, get written confirmation from the Maldives government directly.
    - heading: "What a cat from France actually needs"
      body: |
        Microchip, then rabies vaccination given 30 days to 12 months before arrival, plus viral rhinotracheitis, calicivirus, panleukopenia and feline leukaemia cover. Close the file with a health certificate signed within 10 days of departure. None of it demands the multi-month wait a titre test route would.
    - heading: "The permit question nobody has fully answered"
      body: |
        Some sources describe no import permit requirement for a travelling pet cat at all; others suggest a general permit covers all pet imports. No single current government page resolves this cleanly, so confirm with your vet or agent rather than assume either version is correct.
    - heading: "French Bee's new route, and the fallback that still works"
      body: |
        Once French Bee confirms live animal cargo terms on its Orly-Male-Colombo triangle, it could become a genuinely useful option for this corridor. Until then, Qatar Airways via Doha and Emirates via Dubai remain the established paths, both needing an IATA-spec crate and a licensed agent.
