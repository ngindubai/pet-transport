content:
  h1: "Pet Transport from Switzerland to Iceland"
  overview: |
    Category 1 status and a direct flight make this one of the more straightforward high-complexity routes on this site, if that sounds like a contradiction. Switzerland's place on MAST's Category 1 list spares a pet the extra 90-day wait a Category 2 country such as the United States faces. It changes nothing about Iceland's own mandatory quarantine.

    Budget for two distinct phases: the pre-departure process, which runs faster here than for most non-EU origins, and the fixed 14 days your pet spends at an approved isolation station after landing, before you can bring it home.

  sections:
    - heading: "What Category 1 status changes, and what it doesn't"
      body: |
        Switzerland's Category 1 classification works entirely on the pre-export side. The titre test blood draw needs to happen at least 30 days after vaccination, and once the result clears at 0.5 IU/ml or above, there is no further fixed wait tacked on, unlike a Category 2 country, which faces a further 90 days from the same blood draw date.

        That is where the benefit stops. Every dog and cat entering Iceland, Category 1 or not, spends a minimum of 14 days at Einangrunarstodin i Reykjanesbae or Einangrunarstodin Mosel. Compare that with the reverse route on this site, Iceland to Switzerland, which needs no quarantine at all for a compliant pet. Iceland's own biosecurity rule runs one way, not both.

    - heading: "A genuine direct flight, still cargo-only"
      body: |
        Icelandair's Zurich-Keflavik service is a real nonstop, a small practical advantage over the connecting routes several other Iceland pages on this site describe. Since 1 November 2024, though, the pet still moves as manifested cargo through Icelandair Cargo rather than as checked baggage, so book through that channel directly rather than assuming a normal passenger ticket covers it.

        Lufthansa's Zurich-Frankfurt-Keflavik connection, using its own Frankfurt Animal Lounge, is a workable fallback if Icelandair Cargo space doesn't line up with your date, though it adds a second leg to what is otherwise a single-flight journey.

    - heading: "The Swiss certificate MAST actually wants"
      body: |
        A Swiss vet unfamiliar with Iceland-bound moves may reach for the standard Swiss export certificate or an EU-style pet passport. Neither is what MAST accepts. The specific Category 1 form, D1 for a dog or C1 for a cat, endorsed by the cantonal veterinary office rather than a general practice signature, is the document Iceland's border checks against. Raise this at the first vet appointment rather than discovering the gap close to the flight date.

Switzerland's Category 1 status shortens the pre-export titre test timeline, not the quarantine itself."
