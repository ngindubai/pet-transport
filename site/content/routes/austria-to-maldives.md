  h1: "Pet Transport from Austria to Maldives"
  overview: |
    Vienna is one of the very few European cities with a non-stop service to Male, courtesy of Austrian Airlines' roughly 8-hour-45-minute direct sector. It's a genuine advantage for the flight itself. It changes nothing about the dog question.

    Import of dogs into the Maldives is prohibited outright under Maldives Customs Service rules, and a shorter flight doesn't create an exception that doesn't exist on paper. A cat, by contrast, has a workable if occasionally under-documented pathway: microchip, rabies vaccination 30 days to 12 months before arrival, the core feline vaccines, and a certificate signed within 10 days of travel.

        Microchip first, then rabies vaccination in the window Maldivian guidance specifies, plus the standard feline vaccines. A health certificate signed within 10 days of entry finishes the file. What's genuinely unclear is whether a general import permit or a titre test applies to a pet cat travelling with its owner. Different sources answer that differently. Treat it as an open question for your vet or agent to settle, not something to assume either way.

        Savannah and Bengal cats are treated as restricted breeds unless they're at least five generations removed from a wild ancestor, according to guidance summarised by PetTravel.com. Most cats bred for the pet market clear this comfortably, but check the pedigree paperwork before booking if it applies to yours.
