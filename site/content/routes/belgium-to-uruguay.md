content:
  h1: "Pet Transport from Belgium to Uruguay: 2026 Guide"
  overview: |
    An MGAP import permit and a dog-only tapeworm treatment are the two things that actually shape this route. Uruguay skips the rabies titre test for EU-origin pets entirely, so a Belgian dog or cat only needs a current vaccination, a FASFC-endorsed health certificate, and, for dogs, a praziquantel treatment against Echinococcus timed to a 72-hour-to-30-day window.

    Six to nine weeks covers most of these moves, and the biggest single risk to that timeline is applying for the import permit too late.

  sections:
    - heading: "The import permit sets the pace"
      body: |
        Uruguay's Ministry of Livestock, Agriculture and Fisheries (MGAP) requires an import permit for every incoming pet, arranged before travel and specifying the conditions your paperwork must satisfy. Owners who leave this until the health certificate stage sometimes find their documentation does not quite match what the permit calls for. Apply as soon as the move is confirmed.

        Once the permit terms are known, the rest follows a familiar EU export pattern: FASFC-authorised vet exam, ISO microchip (recommended), and a certificate valid for 10 days from issue.
    - heading: "Echinococcus treatment: for dogs, not cats"
      body: |
        Uruguay's parasite-control focus on arrival is hydatid tapeworm, not rabies. Dogs need a praziquantel-based treatment given between 72 hours and 30 days before landing at Carrasco International, documented by the FASFC vet on the health certificate. Cats travel without this step.

        Given the narrow closing end of that window, most owners schedule the final FASFC appointment for a few days before departure, combining the certificate exam and the treatment record in one visit.
