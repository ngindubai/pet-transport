content:
  h1: "Pet Transport from the United Kingdom to Bolivia"
  overview: |
    No titre test, no three-month wait. Bolivia is one of the more straightforward destinations for a pet leaving the UK, at least on paper, because it does not ask for a rabies antibody blood test from any country of origin. What it does ask for is a SENASAG import permit secured before your pet ever boards a flight, and a health certificate that has been legalised at the Bolivian Consulate in London, not just endorsed by APHA.

    The flight side is the same constraint whichever direction you're moving in: no nonstop service links the UK and Bolivia, so every pet transfers through Madrid, flying Heathrow or Gatwick to Madrid first, then the long Air Europa or BoA sector into Santa Cruz Viru Viru.

  sections:
    - heading: "What Bolivia actually asks for, and where the guidance gets thin"
      body: |
        SENASAG's published requirements cover the essentials clearly enough: a current rabies vaccination, a health certificate from the country of origin, and an import permit granted in advance. Where the guidance is thinner is on exact timing. Some sources cite a minimum wait after vaccination measured in days, others describe it differently, and SENASAG's own published material doesn't spell it out with the same precision as the UK's gov.uk pages do for pets travelling the other way.

        Practically, that means you should treat any specific day count as a starting point, not a guarantee, and confirm the current figure with SENASAG or the Bolivian Consulate in London before your vet administers anything. A quick written confirmation now saves a rebooking later.

    - heading: "The legalisation step people miss"
      body: |
        An APHA-endorsed Export Health Certificate is not, on its own, enough for Bolivia. It needs a further stamp from the Bolivian Consulate in London, a step that does not apply to most UK pet exports and one that owners moving to EU countries or the US never encounter. Add a certified Spanish translation of the whole document set, since Bolivian officials at the port of entry work from Spanish paperwork.

        Book the consulate appointment as soon as the health certificate is signed. APHA-issued certificates typically only stay valid for a matter of days, so there is little room to let the legalisation step drift.
