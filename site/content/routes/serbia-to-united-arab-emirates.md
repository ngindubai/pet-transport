    Belgrade to Dubai is one of the shortest direct flights on this site's Serbia routes: five hours and forty minutes, operated daily by Air Serbia. None of that helps.   sections:
    - heading: "A non-stop flight that will not take your pet"
      body: |
        This trips people up precisely because the direct option looks so good on paper. Air Serbia's Belgrade-Dubai route is short, frequent, and would be the obvious booking for a nervous traveller. The airline's own policy rules it out entirely for pets, the same blanket exclusion it applies to the UK and Qatar. Crate size, cabin versus hold, none of it matters; the exclusion is total.

        Turkish Airlines Cargo via Istanbul is the natural workaround, a short regional hop before Turkish's own extensive Gulf network takes over. Lufthansa Cargo via Frankfurt is the alternative for a pet transport agent already set up with that carrier.

    - heading: "MOCCAE's titre test, and the 90-day permit that has to wrap around it"
      body: |
        Serbia isn't on MOCCAE's published low-risk list, so a Rabies Serum Neutralisation Test applies: blood drawn at least 21 days after vaccination, a result of 0.5 IU/ml or above, and the sample dated somewhere between three and twelve months before travel. There's no separate fixed wait bolted on afterwards the way the UK requires one.

        The import permit is the piece that catches people out. It's only valid for 90 days from issue, so apply for it once the titre result is confirmed and book travel to land inside that window. Minimum entry age is also higher than MOCCAE's own baseline: 15 weeks for a Serbia-origin pet against the 12-week minimum used for low-risk countries.
