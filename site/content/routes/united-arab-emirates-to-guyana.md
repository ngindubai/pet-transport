  h1: "Pet Transport from the United Arab Emirates to Guyana"
  overview: |
    Land a pet in Georgetown from Dubai or Abu Dhabi and the Guyana Livestock Development Authority does not count the UAE among its rabies-free origins, the short list that currently runs to the UK, Ireland, Australia, New Zealand and a handful of Caribbean neighbours. That single fact adds a titre test to the front end of the process and a quarantine decision, up to 90 days, to the back end, made case by case once the paperwork is reviewed.

    It's the most involved of the four Guyana routes this site covers. Two viable flight paths exist, one through Miami and one through London, and which one suits a given move depends on whether the pet is a dog or a cat, and on how the quarantine question resolves.

  sections:
    - heading: "Why this route carries a real quarantine risk, not a routine one"
      body: |
        GLDA's import procedure splits the world into rabies-free origins and everywhere else. The UAE sits in the second group. That doesn't guarantee 90 days of quarantine, GLDA reviews the actual documentation, the titre result and the animal's history at the point the import permit application lands on an officer's desk, but it does mean the outcome isn't guaranteed to be quarantine-free either, the way it would be from a UK or Irish departure point.

        A clean titre test, correct vaccination timing and a complete MOCCAE export certificate are the things within an owner's control. Beyond that, the decision sits with GLDA, and neither this site nor any pet relocation agent can promise a particular outcome before the application is actually reviewed.

    - heading: "Two flight paths, and why the choice depends on the pet"
      body: |
        Emirates flies Dubai to Miami nonstop, one of its longer-established routes, and American Airlines picks up the nonstop Miami-Georgetown sector from there. That's a clean two-carrier routing through a single US hub.

        It's worth spelling out why this works here when the equivalent Miami routing doesn't work on the Guyana-to-UAE leg covered elsewhere on this site: American Airlines Cargo's rabies-risk restriction looks at the animal's country of origin, not its destination. A UAE-origin dog isn't affected by the rule that blocks a Guyana-origin dog from booking through Miami. So for this direction, Miami is genuinely open to dogs and cats alike.

        The alternative runs through London: Etihad from Abu Dhabi, or a connecting flight from Dubai, into Heathrow, then a transfer across London to Gatwick for British Airways' twice-weekly service into Georgetown. It works, and some owners prefer keeping the routing on carriers they already know from the outbound leg. It also adds a cross-city transfer that the Miami option doesn't need.

    - heading: "Timing the MOCCAE certificate against a decision you don't control"
      body: |
        MOCCAE's export certificate is only valid for 30 days from issue. GLDA's quarantine determination, on the other hand, doesn't run to a published fixed timeline; it happens as part of the import permit review, which itself is normally a 3-working-day process but can extend if quarantine is being assessed.

        That mismatch is the real planning challenge on this route. Apply for the MOCCAE certificate too early, relative to when GLDA's decision comes back, and it can expire before the flight is booked. Leave it too late, and the branch visit and fee become a last-minute scramble. Most agents handling this route submit the GLDA application first and time the MOCCAE certificate to land in the middle of that 30-day window, once a realistic travel date is in view.
