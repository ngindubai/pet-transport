  h1: "Pet Transport from Singapore to Laos"
  overview: |
    There is a nonstop flight from Changi to Wattay International, up to seven times a week, and it will not take your pet under any circumstances. Scoot's policy rules out live animals entirely, in the cabin, as baggage, and as cargo. So the shortest route on paper becomes, in practice, a two-leg journey through Bangkok.

    On the compliance side, this direction is considerably lighter than the reverse. Laos does not treat Singapore as high-rabies-risk, so the titre test that dominates a Laos-to-Singapore move generally does not apply here. Six to ten weeks, mostly spent on DLF paperwork and routing around Scoot's dead end.

  sections:
    - heading: "Why the direct flight is a non-starter"
      body: |
        Scoot operates one of the few genuine nonstop links in this part of Southeast Asia, connecting Singapore Changi to Vientiane in about three hours. For passengers, it's the obvious choice. For a pet owner, it changes nothing, because Scoot excludes every live animal from its cargo and baggage services without exception beyond service dogs.

        The workaround runs through Bangkok: Singapore Airlines or Thai Airways for the Singapore-Bangkok leg, then Lao Airlines' well-established twice-daily hop into Vientiane. Two connections replace the zero connections the direct flight would have offered, purely because of one carrier's blanket policy.

    - heading: "Where Laos is genuinely lighter than the return trip"
      body: |
        Laos's Department of Livestock and Fisheries reserves its titre test requirement for pets it considers high-rabies-risk. Singapore's internationally recognised rabies-free status puts it well outside that category, in principle, though DLF doesn't publish the kind of fixed schedule AVS does, so confirming current treatment with DLF or an agent before travel is worth the extra step.

        What DLF does insist on is the import permit, submitted at least 15 days before export, and a rabies vaccination timed precisely: no earlier than 30 days before travel, no later than 12 months. Run this alongside the AVS Export Licence application on the Singapore side rather than waiting for one to finish before starting the other.

  - question: "Can my pet fly on the direct Singapore to Vientiane flight?"
    answer: "No. That nonstop service is operated by Scoot, which does not accept any live animal as cargo or checked baggage. The pet has to route via Bangkok instead, using Singapore Airlines or Thai Airways for the first leg and Lao Airlines for the Bangkok-Vientiane hop."
  - question: "Does my pet need a titre test to enter Laos from Singapore?"
    answer: "Generally not. Laos's DLF only recommends a titre test for pets from countries it treats as high-rabies-risk, and Singapore's officially rabies-free status means it typically falls outside that group. DLF does not publish a fixed list the way Singapore's own AVS does, so confirm current treatment with DLF or an agent before assuming this applies to your booking."
  - question: "What is the vaccination timing rule for entering Laos?"
    answer: "The rabies vaccination must be given no earlier than 30 days before travel and no later than 12 months before it. A vaccination outside that window needs repeating before DLF will accept the pet."
  - question: "How long does the Singapore to Laos pet move take?"
    answer: "Allow 6-10 weeks. There's no titre test wait in most cases, so the schedule is driven by DLF's 15-day permit notice, the AVS Export Licence, and coordinating the Bangkok routing since no carrier that accepts pets flies Singapore to Vientiane directly."
