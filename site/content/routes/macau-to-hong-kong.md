content:
  h1: "Macau to Hong Kong: Group IIIA's 30-Day Quarantine"
  overview: |
    Effective 1 December 2024, AFCD split its old Group III into two: Group IIIA and Group IIIB. Macau was the first place placed in the new Group IIIA, and it came with a real benefit. Quarantine on arrival in Hong Kong dropped from 120 days to 30.

    Thirty days is still thirty days, though, and it's mandatory regardless of how clean the paperwork is. Pair that with a rabies antibody titre test now required under Group IIIA's own timing rules, and this route asks more of a pet than the short ferry crossing itself might suggest.

  sections:
    - heading: "What Group IIIA actually changed"
      body: |
        Before December 2024, Macau-origin pets faced the same 120-day quarantine as most of the rest of Group III. AFCD's press release of 26 November 2024 announced the split: Group III became Group IIIB, largely unchanged, while a new Group IIIA was created specifically to give Macau, its first and so far only member, a shorter path.

        The requirements underneath are still substantial: a compliant microchip, current rabies vaccination, a satisfactory titre test result from an AFCD-recognised laboratory, and a government-endorsed health certificate. Meet all of them and the quarantine is 30 days rather than 120. Miss one and the shorter category doesn't apply.

    - heading: "Thirty days at an Animal Management Centre"
      body: |
        AFCD's Kai Tak Animal Management Centre, or another approved facility, holds the pet for a minimum of 30 days once it clears arrival inspection. Book this slot well ahead of your travel date rather than assuming space will be available; AFCD facilities have finite capacity and don't simply expand to meet demand.

        The cost sits with the owner throughout the stay. Budget HKD 3,000-6,000 for the full 30 days, more if additional veterinary care is needed during the hold.

    - heading: "The crossing itself is the easy part"
      body: |
        Whatever happens on the regulatory side, physically getting from Macau to Hong Kong takes about an hour by ferry or bridge coach. Cotai Water Jet and TurboJet both carry small pets up to roughly 20kg in an approved carrier, with a private cabin option on the Cotai service for owners who prefer it. This is genuinely the simplest leg of the whole process; the paperwork and the quarantine booking are where the real planning time goes.
