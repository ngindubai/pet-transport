  h1: "Pet Transport from Brunei to Singapore"
  overview: |
    Brunei's own Department of Agriculture and Agrifood treats Singapore as one of just five scheduled, rabies-controlled countries in the world, alongside Australia, Ireland, New Zealand and the UK. Singapore's AVS does not return the favour. Brunei sits outside AVS's own Schedule I and Schedule II, so it defaults to the full Schedule III process on arrival, titre test, 90-day wait and all.

    There is a genuine silver lining. Royal Brunei Airlines flies Brunei International to Changi direct and accepts pets as manifested cargo through its own Cargo Department, which means this is the one Brunei route on this site where the destination and the usual transfer hub are the same place. No second international leg to plan or pay for.

  sections:
    - heading: "A one-way relationship: Brunei favours Singapore, Singapore doesn't return it"
      body: |
        Brunei's scheduled-country list runs to five names: Australia, Ireland, New Zealand, Singapore and the United Kingdom. A pet with six months of continuous residency in any of those five can generally skip Brunei's titre test and facility quarantine.

        AVS Singapore classifies countries on its own terms, and Brunei isn't named on either Schedule I or Schedule II. That means every pet moving from Brunei to Singapore goes through the full Schedule III sequence: WOAH titre test, a fixed 90-day wait from the blood draw, and 30 days at the Animal Quarantine Centre on arrival. Whatever goodwill exists on the Brunei side of this relationship doesn't flow back the other way.

    - heading: "The one Brunei route without a hidden transfer leg"
      body: |
        Brunei-to-UK and Brunei-to-USA pet moves both start with getting the animal off Bandar Seri Begawan at all, since Royal Brunei Airlines' live animal cargo isn't a standard published product and the onward international leg still has to be booked from wherever the pet ends up.

        Here, the destination is Singapore itself. Royal Brunei Airlines' own cargo terms confirm it accepts live animals as manifested cargo on its direct BWN-SIN service, arranged through the airline's Cargo Department rather than an online booking flow. Contact the cargo team directly to confirm space and current paperwork, but there's no second flight to coordinate once the pet reaches Changi.

    - heading: "Where the compliance work actually goes"
      body: |
        With the flight logistics settled early, the bulk of this route's five-month timeline sits in the AVS Schedule III sequence: the 28-day post-vaccination wait before blood can be drawn, the 90-day wait after that, and the international shipping needed to reach a WOAH reference lab or a named Schedule II laboratory, since Brunei has no qualifying facility domestically.

        Run the AVS import licence, the AVS-recognised agent appointment and the AQC booking during the 90-day wait rather than after it. Doing so keeps the total closer to four and a half months than the full six.
