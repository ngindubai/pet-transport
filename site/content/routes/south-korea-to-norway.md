content:
  h1: "Pet Transport from South Korea to Norway"
  overview: |
    South Korea to Norway is one of the more demanding pet transport routes in terms of preparation time and documentation. South Korea is not on the EU approved third-country list under EU Implementing Regulation 2026/636, which Norway applies as an EEA member. This means a FAVN titre test and a 90-day waiting period from the blood draw date are both compulsory. Norway also requires Echinococcus tapeworm treatment for all dogs, given by a vet within 24 to 120 hours before arrival. APQA issues the export health certificate at Incheon Airport. Allow five to six months from start to travel day.

  sections:
    - heading: "What does a pet from South Korea need to enter Norway?"
      body: |
        Norway is an EEA member and applies EU pet import rules for dogs, cats, and ferrets under EU Implementing Regulation 2026/636. South Korea is not on the EU approved list, so the full unlisted-country process applies.

        The sequence is: ISO 11784/11785 microchip first (before any rabies vaccination), then rabies vaccination (minimum 21 days before blood draw), then FAVN titre test at an EU-approved laboratory (blood drawn at least 21 days after vaccination, minimum result 0.5 IU/ml), then the 90-day waiting period counted from the blood draw date. After the wait, APQA endorses an EU Animal Health Certificate (AHC, Annex IV for unlisted countries) at Incheon Airport within 10 days of travel.

        Norway also requires Echinococcus multilocularis tapeworm treatment for all dogs. A vet must administer a praziquantel-based product between 24 and 120 hours before the dog arrives in Norway. The treatment time and product must appear on the APQA-endorsed health certificate. Because the flight from Korea to Norway involves at least one hub connection and takes 12 hours or more in total, the treatment is typically given by a Korean vet one to two days before departure, with timing calculated to land within the 24-120h window on arrival. Cats are not subject to the tapeworm requirement.

        Pit bull type dogs are banned in Norway under the Norwegian Dog Act. If your dog is or resembles a pit bull type, confirm status with Mattilsynet before making any bookings.

    - heading: "Which airlines carry pets from South Korea to Norway?"
      body: |
        No direct cargo service operates from Incheon (ICN) to Oslo Gardermoen (OSL). All routings require a European hub. Korean Air Cargo flies ICN to Frankfurt (FRA) and Amsterdam (AMS), with onward SAS or Lufthansa connections to Oslo. KLM Cargo covers ICN to AMS direct, then AMS to OSL. Lufthansa Cargo flies ICN to FRA direct, with FRA-OSL connections. Finnair Cargo offers an ICN to Helsinki (HEL) option, with a short HEL to OSL hop.

        Confirm live animal cargo acceptance on both the long-haul and short-haul legs. Policies can vary by aircraft type and season.

    - heading: "How long does the South Korea to Norway process take, and what does it cost?"
      body: |
        Allow five to six months from start to travel day. The 90-day wait from the titre test blood draw is the fixed constraint; add 21 days between vaccination and blood draw and you can see why starting immediately is the only approach that works. Do not book flights until you have a confirmed blood draw date and can calculate the exact earliest permitted entry date.

        Costs typically run from EUR 1,450 to EUR 3,600. The FAVN titre test costs EUR 200 to EUR 400. APQA inspection and AHC endorsement at ICN adds KRW 50,000 to KRW 150,000 (roughly EUR 35 to EUR 110). Cargo from ICN to OSL via a European hub costs EUR 950 to EUR 2,600. Norwegian border inspection runs EUR 100 to EUR 250. An IPATA agent adds EUR 350 to EUR 750.

faqs:
  - question: "Does a pet from South Korea need a titre test to enter Norway?"
    answer: "Yes. South Korea is not on the EU approved third-country list under EU Implementing Regulation 2026/636. Norway applies this regulation as an EEA member. A FAVN or RNATT titre test at an EU-approved laboratory is required. The minimum result is 0.5 IU/ml, and a 90-day waiting period from the blood draw date must pass before the pet can enter Norway."
  - question: "Is tapeworm treatment required for dogs from South Korea entering Norway?"
    answer: "Yes. Norway requires Echinococcus multilocularis treatment for all dogs. A vet must administer a praziquantel-based product between 24 and 120 hours before the dog arrives in Norway. The treatment time, product name, and dose must be recorded on the health certificate. Source: Mattilsynet, mattilsynet.no."
  - question: "What is the APQA and what role does it play?"
    answer: "APQA is South Korea's Animal and Plant Quarantine Agency. Pets travelling from South Korea must be inspected by APQA at Incheon International Airport (ICN), where APQA endorses the EU Animal Health Certificate. This inspection must occur within 10 days of travel and is the final step before departure."
  - question: "Are pit bull type dogs allowed into Norway?"
    answer: "No. Norway bans pit bull type dogs under the Norwegian Dog Act. If your dog is classified as a pit bull type or a mixed breed with those characteristics, it cannot enter Norway. Contact Mattilsynet (mattilsynet.no) to confirm your dog's classification before making any bookings."
  - question: "Is there quarantine for pets arriving in Norway from South Korea?"
    answer: "No quarantine for fully compliant pets. Mattilsynet (Norwegian Food Safety Authority) inspects at the point of entry. If the microchip, vaccination, titre test result, 90-day wait, tapeworm treatment, and APQA-endorsed certificate are all in order, the pet is cleared without quarantine."
