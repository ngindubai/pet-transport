    - heading: "Routing from Stockholm to Vilnius with a pet"
      body: |
        Vilnius (VNO) is well connected to Stockholm through two main routes: SAS direct on certain schedules, and airBaltic via its Riga hub. For small pets travelling in the cabin, both are workable. For larger animals in cargo, check what hold space is available on the specific flight you're booking.

        The airBaltic connection via Riga adds around an hour to the journey, but RIX is airBaltic's home hub with strong live animal cargo handling. If SAS Cargo availability is limited on a given date, the Riga connection is a reliable alternative.

        Ryanair also serves this route but takes cabin pets only. Worth considering if your dog or cat is small enough to fit under the seat, but it rules out anything larger.

    - heading: "VMVT and what Lithuania actually needs to see"
      body: |
        Lithuania's State Food and Veterinary Service (VMVT) is the competent authority for companion animal imports. Under the EU pet travel framework, all VMVT requires for an intra-EU arrival is a compliant EU pet passport.

        The passport needs to record: an ISO 15-digit microchip implanted before the rabies vaccination, a current rabies vaccination (primary given at least 21 days before entry, or a booster within the valid period), and the issuing vet's details. That's the entirety of the Lithuanian entry requirement for a pet from Sweden.

        Lithuania has PPP breed legislation covering dogs deemed dangerous under national law. This doesn't affect most dogs but is worth checking if you own a large guarding breed or a bull-type dog. The VMVT website lists the relevant breeds and requirements.
