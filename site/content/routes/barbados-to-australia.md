  h1: "Pet Transport from Barbados to Australia"
  overview: |
    DAFF sorts approved countries into groups, and Barbados lands in Group 2, its tier for places it treats as genuinely rabies-free. That single classification means no rabies vaccination and no titre test, a notably lighter path than Trinidad and Tobago, Jamaica or the Bahamas next door, all of which sit in Group 3 and face DAFF's RNATT test plus a 180-day wait.

    Mickleham's quarantine still applies, ten days minimum, because Australia quarantines every imported pet regardless of where it comes from. Add a routing that has to go through London, since nothing connects Barbados to Australia directly, and 10 to 14 weeks is a realistic planning window.

  sections:
    - heading: "Why Barbados sits apart from its Caribbean neighbours here"
      body: |
        DAFF's country groupings for pet imports are not geographic. They track each country's own rabies status, and Barbados has held rabies-free recognition for decades. That earns it Group 2, the same tier as territories like Iceland and Christmas Island, while Trinidad and Tobago, Jamaica, the Bahamas and Bermuda, all close neighbours, sit one tier higher in Group 3 because DAFF treats rabies there as present but controlled rather than absent.

        The practical gap is significant. A Group 3 pet needs an RNATT titre test at a DAFF-approved laboratory and a 180-day wait counted from the blood sample's arrival at the lab. A Group 2 pet from Barbados skips both steps entirely, moving straight from microchip and identity verification to the import permit and export certificate.

    - heading: "What DAFF still asks for, even without the titre test"
      body: |
        Skipping the titre test doesn't mean skipping paperwork altogether. The BICON import permit still has to be applied for and granted before travel, at an indicative fee around AUD 420 that DAFF periodically revises. A formal identity verification, confirming the microchip against the animal, is part of DAFF's standard Group 2 process and needs sequencing correctly with the export vet visit.

        Mickleham's minimum 10-day quarantine is fixed regardless of group status. It's Australia's one universal rule for every imported cat and dog, and it only accepts weekday arrivals, so the final Melbourne landing needs planning around that.

    - heading: "One connection, through London, every time"
      body: |
        British Airways' nonstop Bridgetown to Heathrow service is the only realistic first leg out of Barbados for this route, the same aircraft used on Barbados's UK-bound flights. From Heathrow, Qantas or Singapore Airlines carries the pet on to Melbourne, the sole Australian airport equipped to receive it because Mickleham sits nearby.

        There's no way to shorten this to one flight. Building real buffer time around the London transfer matters more here than almost anywhere else on the route, since a missed connection with a live animal is a welfare issue, not a scheduling inconvenience.
