content:
  h1: "Pet Transport from the United Arab Emirates to Belarus"
  overview: |
    Book the UK-to-Belarus route on this site and there's no flight at all. Belavia has been shut out of EU and UK airspace since 2021, so every UK pet ends up in Warsaw or Vilnius first, then travels the last stretch by road. Starting from the UAE removes that problem entirely: flydubai and Belavia both fly Dubai to Minsk direct, since neither carrier is barred from UAE-originating routes the way they are from European ones.

    What doesn't change is Belarus's own vaccine rule. A three-year rabies vaccine, the kind many UAE vets give by default, isn't valid here. Belarus only accepts single-year formulations, whatever airport the pet departs from.

  sections:
    - heading: "Why a Belarusian flight exists from Dubai but not from London"
      body: |
        Belavia's exclusion from EU and UK airspace, in place since May 2021 and tightened again in December 2025, is specific to those two jurisdictions. It says nothing about a Dubai departure. flydubai runs its own direct Minsk service roughly five times a week, and Belavia flies the same city pair too, both landing straight into Minsk National Airport with no Warsaw or Vilnius stopover and no overland leg to follow.

        That's a genuine structural advantage over the UK route built alongside this one, where the flight only ever reaches a Polish or Lithuanian airport and the pet finishes the journey by road.

    - heading: "The vaccine rule that doesn't care where the flight came from"
      body: |
        Belarus's State Veterinary and Food Supervision Department accepts only single-year rabies vaccine formulations, given at least 30 days before entry. A three-year vaccine, standard practice at plenty of UAE clinics, simply doesn't count, regardless of how current it is or how direct the flight was to get here.

        No titre test is legally required from any country of origin, though it's worth mentioning to the vet given the UAE's own documented rabies circulation among stray animals. It isn't a hard entry condition, but a cautious vet may still recommend it.

    - heading: "Which carrier, and which pets it won't take"
      body: |
        flydubai is the more mainstream booking of the two direct options, but its restricted breed list is long: a wide range of snub-nosed dogs and cats, French Bulldogs, Pugs, Persians and Himalayans among them, are refused outright. Belavia is the other direct carrier on this exact city pair. A pet excluded from both needs the Turkish Airlines routing via Istanbul instead, which restores the connection the direct flight was meant to avoid.

    - heading: "After landing: the paperwork isn't finished"
      body: |
        Clearing Belarusian customs with a translated certificate and current single-year vaccination doesn't end the process. The pet still has to be registered with local veterinary authorities within one month of arrival, a real administrative step rather than a formality. Confirm the exact process with a vet in Minsk shortly after arrival, since it sits outside anything the UAE-side export paperwork covers.
