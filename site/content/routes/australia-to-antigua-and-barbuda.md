content:
  h1: "Pet Transport from Australia to Antigua and Barbuda"
  overview: |
    No rabies vaccination. No titre test. That's the entire practical benefit of Australia's own rabies-free status once it hits Antigua and Barbuda's classification system, the same treatment the UK gets on this site's equivalent route. What's left is a Veterinary Import License, a health certificate covering the standard core vaccinations, and a flight that, unlike the paperwork, genuinely is the hard part of this move.

    Nothing flies Australia to Antigua direct. Every routing needs at least two long-haul legs, whether that's via Los Angeles and Miami on Qantas and American Airlines, or via London on Qantas or British Airways followed by BA's nonstop Gatwick-Antigua service. Budget 6 to 9 weeks, most of it booking cargo space rather than chasing paperwork.

  sections:
    - heading: "Why the Antigua side is lighter than it looks"
      body: |
        Antigua and Barbuda's Veterinary and Livestock Division draws a hard line between rabies-free and rabies-endemic countries of origin. Australia sits on the rabies-free side, alongside the UK, which means the vaccination certificate and titre test that apply to arrivals from the US or Canada simply don't apply here.

        What does apply is a Veterinary Import License, applied for at least two weeks ahead, and an Official Government Health Certificate confirming the core vaccinations, distemper, hepatitis, leptospirosis, parvovirus and parainfluenza for dogs. One breed rule cuts across every origin country: pitbulls, and dogs that could pass for one, cannot land in Antigua and Barbuda at all, so check this before booking if there's any doubt about a dog's type.

    - heading: "The flight is the real project here"
      body: |
        There's no way to shorten the distance. The most direct practical routing uses Qantas from Sydney or Melbourne to Los Angeles, an American Airlines connection through to Miami, and then American's nonstop AA2579/AA2580 service into Antigua for the final leg. An alternative runs through London, using Qantas or British Airways to Heathrow, then BA's nonstop BA2157 from Gatwick, the only nonstop service on that particular pairing.

        Each option involves at least two carriers and two live animal cargo bookings, made separately rather than as a single through-ticket. Confirm space on every leg before locking in a departure date; a confirmed Sydney-Los Angeles booking says nothing about whether the Miami-Antigua leg has room on the same week.

    - heading: "Getting the timing right without the pressure of a titre test"
      body: |
        Because there's no rabies vaccination wait or blood test clock running on this route, the main risk is timing mismatch rather than a missed biological deadline. The DAFF-accredited health certificate is valid only 7 days, so book it to land inside the actual departure window, not weeks ahead out of convenience. Line the Antigua-side Veterinary Import License and health certificate up against the same confirmed flight date, since both documents are checked together on arrival at V.C. Bird International.
