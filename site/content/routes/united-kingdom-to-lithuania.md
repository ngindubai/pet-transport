faqs:
  - question: "Does my pet need a titre test to travel from the UK to Lithuania?"
    answer: "No. The United Kingdom is a listed third country under EU Regulation 576/2013. No titre test is required. You need a current rabies vaccination, ISO microchip, and a UK Animal Health Certificate in the EU listed-country format."
  - question: "Can I take my dog on Ryanair from the UK to Lithuania?"
    answer: "Ryanair accepts very small pets in the cabin on UK-Vilnius and UK-Kaunas routes. Weight limits are strict (check Ryanair's current cabin pet policy for maximum weight including carrier). Larger dogs cannot travel on Ryanair and need a cargo option."
  - question: "What AHC format does my UK vet need to issue for Lithuania?"
    answer: "Your APHA-authorised Official Vet must issue the AHC in the EU format for listed third countries. Tell them the destination is Lithuania so they issue the correct certificate under EU Regulation 576/2013. This is different from the AHC used for pets entering the UK."
  - question: "Which Lithuanian airport should I fly into for pet cargo?"
    answer: "Vilnius International Airport (VNO) is the main international airport and the standard entry point. Kaunas Airport (KUN) is smaller but also has Ryanair connections. For larger cargo pets, Vilnius has better cargo handling facilities."
  - question: "How does the UK-to-Lithuania route compare to other UK-EU pet moves?"
    answer: "UK to Lithuania is similar in process to other UK-to-EU listed country routes (Estonia, Latvia, Slovenia). The key documents are the same: ISO microchip, current rabies vaccination, UK AHC in EU format. Ryanair's direct UK-Vilnius/Kaunas service makes it particularly accessible for small cabin pets."
