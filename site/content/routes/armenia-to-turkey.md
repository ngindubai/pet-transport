  sections:
    - heading: "A flight route with almost no track record"
      body: |
        Armenia and Turkey have no diplomatic relations, and the border between them has been closed since the aftermath of the 1993 Karabakh war. For most of the past three decades, there was simply no direct way to fly between Yerevan and Istanbul. That changed on 11 March 2026, when Turkish Airlines began daily scheduled service as part of a broader normalisation process between the two governments, growing to 14 weekly flights by 15 June to meet summer demand.

        For pet transport, that means booking a cargo slot on a route with limited operating history. Confirm space and current handling procedures directly with Turkish Airlines rather than assuming the settled patterns of a corridor that's been running for years. The upside is real: a direct flight exists where none did before, and land options remain closed regardless, so this is now the only practical way to move a pet between the two countries.

    - heading: "The titre test, unaffected by the diplomatic thaw"
      body: |
        None of the political change alters Turkey's own entry requirements. A FAVN titre test is still required from Armenia, blood drawn at least 30 days after the rabies vaccination, a result of at least 0.5 IU/ml, and a 90-day wait from the draw date before the pet can travel. Armenia's SSFS doesn't require a separate export permit, just its endorsement of the health certificate, reformatted to Turkey's own model.

        A pet arriving without a valid test faces 21 days of quarantine in Turkey at the owner's expense, the same standard applied across every unlisted origin in this build.
