content:
  h1: "Pet Transport from South Africa to Macau"
  overview: |
    Some of Macau's routes on this site sit in a genuine grey area over whether the titre test applies, cases where an origin country's own rabies-free status leaves room for doubt about IAM's exemption list. This isn't one of them. South Africa has endemic rabies in parts of its wildlife population and a steady annual case count tracked by its own National Institute for Communicable Diseases, so there's no credible argument for skipping the blood test here.

    Budget for the full sequence: vaccination, a 30-day wait, the blood draw, then roughly three months before the pet can travel. The one piece of good news is what happens after: IAM's policy change from around June 2026 means a compliant pet still avoids the mandatory quarantine hold that used to apply.

  sections:
    - heading: "Why this route has no titre test ambiguity"
      body: |
        IAM's baseline position waives the rabies titre test only for pets from a place with no rabies incidents in the past two years. On several of Macau's other route pages on this site, that question genuinely can't be answered with confidence for origins like Australia or the UK, since no published IAM list names them one way or the other.

        South Africa doesn't have that problem. Rabies is endemic in parts of the country's wildlife, human and animal cases are confirmed every year, and DALRRD's own surveillance data reflects an active, managed disease burden rather than an absent one. Given that, the titre test isn't a matter of interpretation here; it's a straightforward requirement.

    - heading: "What the compliant pathway actually buys you"
      body: |
        Once the titre result clears and the three-month wait is up, IAM's June 2026 policy change means a fully documented pet skips the mandatory quarantine hold that used to apply to every arrival. That's a genuine improvement over the process this route followed before the change, and it puts South Africa-to-Macau on the same footing as Macau's other compliant-pet arrivals rather than facing an additional facility stay on top of the pre-travel wait.

    - heading: "The Hong Kong leg"
      body: |
        Macau International Airport has no long-haul cargo service, so the pattern here matches Macau's other overseas routes: Cathay Pacific Cargo's direct Johannesburg-Hong Kong sector, or an alternative via Dubai with Emirates SkyCargo or via Doha with Qatar Airways Cargo, followed by the overland or ferry transfer into Macau to clear IAM's inspection.
