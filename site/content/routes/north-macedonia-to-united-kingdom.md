  h1: "Pet Transport from North Macedonia to the United Kingdom"
  overview: |
    North Macedonia sits on the UK's list of recognised countries for pet travel. That single fact removes the biggest hurdle other routes face: no rabies titre test, no 90-day wait. A pet with a valid microchip, rabies vaccination, and an Animal Health Certificate can travel once the 21-day post-vaccination wait has passed.

    There's a catch most owners don't expect. Wizz Air flies direct from Skopje to London Luton several times a week, cheaply and quickly. It just won't carry your pet. Assistance dogs only. Everyone else needs a connecting flight through Istanbul or Vienna on a carrier that actually handles live animal cargo.

    Plan around that from day one, and this is one of the more straightforward routes on the site.

  sections:
    - heading: "Why North Macedonia's listed status shortens the process"
      body: |
        The UK classifies countries by rabies risk. Some, mostly EU member states, sit in the simplest tier. North Macedonia falls into the next tier down: still listed, still exempt from the titre test, but requiring the fuller Animal Health Certificate rather than a pet passport.

        Practically, that means the process for your pet looks close to an EU move, not like the 20-plus week routes required from unlisted countries in Africa or Asia. Microchip, vaccinate, wait 21 days, get the AHC, fly.

        It's still worth double-checking your pet's status is current. If North Macedonia's classification changes, or your paperwork lapses, you're back into unlisted-country territory with a titre test and a 3-month wait.

    - heading: "The Wizz Air trap, and how to route around it"
      body: |
        Skopje International Airport has one direct link to the UK: Wizz Air to London Luton, roughly 12 flights a week. It's the flight most people search for first. It carries no pets beyond certified assistance dogs, full stop.

        Turkish Airlines through Istanbul is the more established option. Istanbul's live animal facility is purpose-built, separate from general cargo, with its own veterinary staff on site. Austrian Airlines via Vienna is the other realistic route, feeding into the Lufthansa Group's cargo network.

        Either way, book the connecting carrier directly or through an IPATA-registered pet transport agent rather than assuming a budget direct flight will work. It won't.

faqs:
  - question: "Does my pet need a rabies titre test to move from North Macedonia to the UK?"
    answer: "No. North Macedonia is a listed country for GB pet travel, so the titre test and its 90-day wait don't apply. You still need the microchip, rabies vaccination, 21-day wait, and an Animal Health Certificate."
  - question: "Can I fly my pet on Wizz Air from Skopje to London?"
    answer: "No. Wizz Air's Skopje-London Luton route is a genuine direct flight, but the airline does not accept pets in cabin or cargo, only certified assistance dogs. You'll need to book with Turkish Airlines, Austrian Airlines, or another carrier with a live animal cargo programme instead."
  - question: "How long does it take to move a pet from North Macedonia to the UK?"
    answer: "Allow 8 to 12 weeks. The fixed step is the 21-day wait after the rabies vaccination. The rest of the time covers booking cargo space, getting the AHC issued close to your travel date, and the tapeworm treatment window for dogs."
  - question: "Will my dog need a tapeworm treatment before entering Great Britain?"
    answer: "Yes. Dogs entering Great Britain need a tapeworm treatment (praziquantel or an equivalent product) given by a vet between 24 and 120 hours before scheduled arrival. This applies regardless of North Macedonia's listed status. Cats are exempt."
  - question: "Will my pet be quarantined on arrival in the UK?"
    answer: "Not routinely. Pets arriving with correct, complete documentation clear without quarantine. Penalty quarantine, up to 4 months at the owner's expense, only applies if the paperwork is wrong, incomplete, or the pet arrives via a non-approved route."
