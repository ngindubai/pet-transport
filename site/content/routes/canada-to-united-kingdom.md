  sections:
    - heading: "The Air Canada cabin advantage"
      body: |
        One of the best features of the Canada to UK route is Air Canada's cabin pet policy. Air Canada allows small dogs and cats to travel in the passenger cabin on many routes, provided the pet plus carrier fits under the seat and meets the size and weight limits.

        This is a meaningful advantage. US carriers operating transatlantic routes (Delta, American, United on the UK leg) generally do not permit pets in the cabin on those flights, so pets travel as cargo. Air Canada's cabin option means a small pet can stay with you throughout the flight, reducing stress for the animal and the owner.

        For larger pets that exceed the cabin limits, Air Canada and British Airways both offer cargo. Confirm cabin availability for your specific route and pet size when booking, as it can vary by aircraft and route.

    - heading: "CFIA export and the tapeworm window"
      body: |
        Canada's Food Inspection Agency (CFIA) handles the export certification. The process starts with a Canadian-licensed veterinarian who completes the health certificate, which is then endorsed by CFIA. Start this 3-4 weeks before travel to allow for the endorsement step.

        For dogs, the tapeworm treatment must be administered between 24 and 120 hours before arrival in Great Britain. On a direct flight from Toronto or Vancouver to Heathrow (around 7-9 hours), the treatment is typically given 1-3 days before departure. Calculate the window from your scheduled UK arrival time, and have the vet record the exact date and time in the certificate. Cats and ferrets are exempt from the tapeworm requirement.

Your pet is released at the Heathrow Animal Reception Centre after the APHA inspection."
  - question: "How long does the CFIA export process take?"
    answer: "Allow 3-4 weeks for the CFIA export certification, including the endorsement step.
