        Warsaw Chopin (WAW) is LOT Polish Airlines' home hub, and LOT operates regular service to Tallinn (TLL). For Polish owners moving a pet to Estonia, LOT is the natural carrier.

For large dogs in cargo, the booking goes through LOT Cargo and the animal travels in the cargo hold under the same temperature and welfare controls as other Baltic routes LOT operates.         airBaltic via Riga is a reliable alternative. Warsaw to Riga, then Riga to Tallinn. Riga is airBaltic's home hub and the RIX cargo facilities handle the volume well. The Warsaw to Riga sector on airBaltic, combined with the short RIX to TLL hop, is a manageable total journey.

    - heading: "Estonian requirements: what VTA checks at Tallinn"
      body: |
        Estonia's VTA (Veterinaar- ja Toiduamet) processes intra-EU pet arrivals at Tallinn Airport. For a Polish pet with a valid EU pet passport, the process is straightforward: microchip scan matched to the passport number, vaccination dates confirmed as current, issuing vet details checked. Spot checks are occasional rather than routine.

        The practical compliance point before travel is the microchip-vaccination sequence. The EU pet passport must show the microchip number first, then the rabies vaccination details. If the vaccination entry pre-dates the microchip record in the passport, it is non-compliant under EU rules. This is the most common documentation error for intra-EU moves.

        The GIW (Główny Inspektorat Weterynarii) governs the authorisation of Polish vets to issue EU pet passports. Any officially authorised vet in Poland can issue or update the document. If your passport is already current and the sequence is correct, no additional document is needed for the move.
