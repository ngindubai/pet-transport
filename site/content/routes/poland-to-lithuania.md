        LOT Polish Airlines operates Warsaw Chopin (WAW) to Vilnius (VNO) as part of its Baltic network. The flight takes approximately 45 minutes, which makes it one of the shorter intra-EU pet transport journeys available from Poland.

        For cabin pets, the brevity means minimal disruption. For large dogs in cargo, the short sector reduces time spent in the hold significantly compared to longer routes through connecting hubs.     - heading: "Lithuania's breed legislation: the one extra check"
      body: |
        Lithuania maintains a list of potentially dangerous dog breeds with specific entry and ownership requirements. Under Lithuanian law, certain bull-type and large guarding breeds are subject to restrictions including muzzling in public places, mandatory registration, and liability insurance.

        The breeds most commonly covered include Pitbull Terriers, American Staffordshire Terriers, Staffordshire Bull Terriers, and similar types. The list covers physical characteristics as well as registered breeds, so mixed-type dogs with these characteristics may also fall within the legislation.

        The EU pet passport satisfies the standard import requirements under EU Regulation 576/2013. The breed legislation is a separate domestic requirement in Lithuania that applies regardless of origin. If your dog is in any category that might be covered, contact VMVT at vmvt.lt before travel to confirm what steps apply.

        For owners of standard companion breeds, this section does not apply. The VMVT process at Vilnius Airport for a standard arrival with a valid EU pet passport is quick.
