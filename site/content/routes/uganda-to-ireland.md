  h1: "Pet Transport Uganda to Ireland: 2026 Guide"
  overview: "Ireland asks for one thing most of its EU neighbours skip: a TRACES pre-notification submitted before the pet even boards its flight. That runs alongside, not instead of, the standard unlisted-country sequence Uganda faces, and Ireland is notably firm about non-compliance, with no informal margin for incomplete paperwork the way some destinations allow a short conditional quarantine to sort things out."

  sections:
    - heading: "TRACES: the extra step Ireland asks for"
      body: |
        Most EU states accept a valid EU-format health certificate at the border and leave it there. Ireland adds a pre-arrival notification through TRACES, the EU's Trade Control and Expert System. This does not replace the health certificate; it runs alongside it, submitted ahead of travel, typically arranged by a vet or pet transport agent on the owner's behalf.

        Skipping this step, or getting the timing wrong, can mean delays at Dublin Airport or a refused entry, a considerably more expensive outcome than the notification itself. DAFM, the Department of Agriculture, Food and the Marine, is also notably firm about non-compliant documentation generally, closer to penalty quarantine or return of the animal than a grace period.

    - heading: "The titre test sequence and flying from Entebbe"
      body: |
        Uganda sits outside the EU's approved country list, so the same sequence applies as everywhere else in this guide: microchip first, rabies vaccination second, then a minimum 30-day wait before blood is drawn for a titre test at an approved laboratory. A result of at least 0.5 IU/ml starts a 90-day countdown, measured from the draw date.

        Turkish Airlines' non-stop Entebbe-Istanbul leg is often the quickest way to start the journey, with Ethiopian Airlines via Addis Ababa and Qatar Airways via Doha as established alternatives.
