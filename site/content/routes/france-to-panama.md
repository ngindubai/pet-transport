  sections:
    - heading: "Panama's requirements for pets from France"
      body: |
        Panama's MIDA (Ministerio de Desarrollo Agropecuario, mida.gob.pa) governs pet imports. For pets arriving from France, the requirements are: ISO 11784/11785 microchip; rabies vaccination administered between 30 days and 12 months before arrival; pet must be over 4 months old; official health certificate in English or Spanish, endorsed by the DDPP and authenticated by the Panamanian Embassy in France.

        No import permit is needed. No quarantine for compliant arrivals. A MIDA inspector at Tocumen International Airport (PTY) checks the documents on arrival and collects a USD 20 inspection fee.

    - heading: "The Embassy authentication step"
      body: |
        Panama requires that the health certificate be authenticated (legalised) by the Panamanian Embassy in the country of origin before travel. For France, this is the Panamanian Embassy in Paris. The authentication confirms that the DDPP endorsement is genuine.

        The process: your official veterinarian prepares the health certificate in English or Spanish. The DDPP endorses it. You then present the endorsed certificate to the Panamanian Embassy in Paris for authentication. Contact the Embassy in advance to confirm their current requirements, fees, and turnaround time, as these can vary. Plan this step carefully within the 10-day validity window of the health certificate.

    - heading: "Routing from Paris to Panama City"
      body: |
        Paris Charles de Gaulle (CDG) has strong cargo connectivity to Panama. Air France operates transatlantic services that connect to PTY via Miami (MIA) and Copa Airlines. Air France Cargo handles live animals on approved routes, and the CDG hub is one of Europe's most active for long-haul cargo.

        Copa Airlines, Panama's national carrier, operates from MIA to PTY and is well suited for live animal cargo on the second leg. Some routings may also go via Madrid (MAD) with Iberia and Copa codeshares. Confirm live animal acceptance on all segments before booking.

    - heading: "DDPP export endorsement from France"
      body: |
        France's official export health certificates for pets going to non-EU countries are endorsed by the Direction Departementale de la Protection des Populations (DDPP) of the department where the veterinarian practises. The official vet prepares the certificate in the format and language required by Panama (English or Spanish). The DDPP reviews and endorses it.

        Allow three to five business days for the DDPP endorsement. The health certificate must be issued within 10 days of travel. After the DDPP endorses it, take it to the Panamanian Embassy in Paris for authentication before departure.
