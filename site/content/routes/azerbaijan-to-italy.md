## Pet Transport from Azerbaijan to Italy

Budget 5 to 6 months for a pet move from Azerbaijan to Italy. That is not a warning designed to alarm you; it is the minimum realistic timeline built into EU law for dogs and cats arriving from unlisted countries, and Azerbaijan sits in that category. The waiting periods are fixed by EU Regulation 576/2013 and Commission Delegated Regulation 2020/692. They cannot be shortened by paying more, moving faster, or using a different vet.

If you are reading this with a move date already in the diary that is less than five months away, get in touch immediately. Options exist for some situations, but they need to be assessed against your specific pet's vaccination history.

If you have the time, the process is clear and the outcome predictable: a compliant dog or cat arriving in Italy faces no quarantine, no import permit requirement, and no additional delays at the border.

---

### Unlisted vs Listed Country Requirements

Italy, as an EU member state, applies EU pet movement rules. Azerbaijan is not on the EU list of approved third countries, which triggers a more demanding set of requirements than countries with approved status. Here is how the two tracks compare:

| Requirement | Unlisted country (Azerbaijan) | Listed country |
|---|---|---|
| Microchip | Required, before vaccination | Required |
| Rabies vaccination | Required, after microchip | Required |
| FAVN titre test | Required, blood draw 30+ days after vaccination | Not required |
| 90-day wait | Required, from date of successful blood draw | Not required |
| EU health certificate | Required, endorsed by State Veterinary Service | Required |
| Quarantine | None if fully compliant | None if compliant |
| Import permit | Not required | Not required |

The two steps that create the long timeline are the mandatory 30-day gap between vaccination and blood draw, and the 90-day wait that follows a successful titre result. Together they add a minimum of 120 days before travel is possible, even if the vaccination is given on day one.

If your pet has a prior vaccination and titre test history from another move, some of that history may count. This is worth discussing with your vet at the first appointment, but do not assume it will apply without checking against the specific test and lab records.

---

### AZAL Direct to Milan: A Genuine Differentiator

Most pets leaving Baku for Europe travel via Istanbul on Turkish Airlines Cargo, which requires a layover and a second cargo leg. For Italy specifically, there is an alternative worth examining.

AZAL (Azerbaijan Airlines) operates a direct service from Heydar Aliyev International Airport (GYD) to Milan Malpensa (MXP). If AZAL accepts your pet as live animal cargo on that route, you get a non-stop connection to northern Italy. That removes the Istanbul transit, cuts the number of handling events, and generally reduces stress for the animal.

The practical step is to contact AZAL cargo directly and confirm:

- Whether the specific flight and aircraft type accepts live animal cargo
- The size and weight limits they apply
- Any breed restrictions (some carriers restrict brachycephalic breeds on cargo)
- The booking lead time they require

If AZAL can take your pet, the direct GYD-MXP routing is the better option over Istanbul connections for Milan-bound moves. For Rome, the Istanbul connection via Turkish Airlines Cargo to FCO remains the more direct choice.

Lufthansa Cargo via Frankfurt (GYD-FRA-FCO or FRA-MXP) is the main alternative if neither Turkish Airlines nor AZAL fits your dates or size requirements. Frankfurt has dedicated live animal welfare facilities and Lufthansa's cargo procedures are well established.

### The State Veterinary Service Endorsement for the EU Certificate

The EU-format non-commercial movement health certificate is not simply a vet form. It is an official government document and it must carry the endorsement of the State Veterinary Service (Ministry of Agriculture of Azerbaijan) before it has legal standing at the Italian border.

The process in Azerbaijan has two stages. First, a State Veterinary Service-authorised vet in Baku carries out a health examination and completes the certificate, recording the microchip number, vaccination details, titre test result, and blood draw date. The document format must follow the EU model for non-commercial movements from third countries.

Second, the completed certificate goes to the State Veterinary Service for endorsement: an official stamp and signature that confirms the Azerbaijani government has verified the document.

One timing point that catches people out: the EU health certificate is valid for 10 days from the date of issue. Do not arrange the endorsement weeks in advance and then leave it sitting. Time the vet appointment and State Veterinary Service endorsement to fall within 10 days of your planned departure date.

Allow 3 to 5 working days for the State Veterinary Service endorsement step. Your authorised vet will advise on current processing times in Baku and whether submission is done in person or by courier.
