content:
  h1: "Pet Transport from Canada to North Macedonia"
  overview: |
    North Macedonia's Food and Veterinary Agency treats a Canadian pet the same way it treats one from any other country on the EU's own approved rabies list. No blood test, no three-month wait, just a microchip, a current rabies vaccination and a 21-day gap before travel. That puts this route in the simpler tier alongside Moldova rather than the far longer process this site documents for Montenegro.

    The reason is specific rather than general goodwill: Canada sits on the EU's own Annex II list in its own right, and FVA extends that same courtesy to Canadian arrivals that it extends to UK ones. Worth checking that classification hasn't shifted before relying on it, since it's set at EU level and reviewed periodically.

  sections:
    - heading: "Canada's own listing, not a borrowed one"
      body: |
        North Macedonia's import rule for rabies serology follows a simple binary. A pet arriving from a country the EU classifies as rabies-controlled skips the titre test. Everywhere else, the test applies. Canada sits on that approved list, Annex II to Implementing Regulation (EU) 2026/636, on its own record, and FVA's own guidance carries that classification through into its entry requirements for a Canadian pet exactly as it does for a UK one.

        None of this is fixed permanently by Macedonian statute alone. The underlying EU list is reviewed from time to time, so a quick check with FVA close to the travel date is worth the five minutes it takes.

    - heading: "Two direct routes out of Toronto"
      body: |
        Turkish Airlines' direct Toronto-Istanbul service connects on to Skopje using Istanbul's dedicated live animal lounge for a larger dog. Austrian Airlines' direct Toronto-Vienna flight, running roughly six times weekly, feeds into the Lufthansa Group's cargo network for the onward Skopje leg.

        Either works well for most pets. Choose based on crate size and whichever schedule lines up more comfortably with the CFIA regional office appointment.

    answer: "No. North Macedonia's FVA exempts arrivals from any country the EU classifies as rabies-controlled, and Canada is on that list in its own right. A microchip, current rabies vaccination and 21-day wait are all that's required, plus the CFIA-endorsed export certificate."
  - question: "Why does North Macedonia exempt Canadian pets from the titre test but Montenegro doesn't?"
    answer: "North Macedonia's FVA extends the EU's own approved-country exemption, the list Canada sits on independently, to pets arriving from Canada. Montenegro's published guidance does not describe the same carve-out and appears to apply the titre test regardless of origin. This is a real difference between the two Balkan states."
  - question: "How long does it take to move a pet from Canada to North Macedonia?"

  - question: "Will my pet be quarantined on arrival in North Macedonia?"
