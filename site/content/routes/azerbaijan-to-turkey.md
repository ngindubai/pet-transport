  overview: |
    Baku to Istanbul is one of the busiest air corridors between the Caucasus and Turkey, reflecting the close political and cultural ties between the two countries. Both Turkish Airlines and Azerbaijan's own AZAL fly it direct, several times a day between them, so finding cargo space is rarely the problem.

    The paperwork is where the real planning happens. Turkey's titre test rule applies to Azerbaijan the same way it applies to every unlisted origin in this build, and that 90-day wait is what actually sets your travel date, not the flight schedule.

  sections:
    - heading: "Two national carriers, a well-served corridor"
      body: |
        Turkish Airlines and AZAL both operate direct Baku-Istanbul flights daily, a reflection of how closely connected the two countries are. For pet cargo, that density of service is a genuine advantage: if one airline's cargo hold is full on your preferred date, the other is a realistic same-day alternative rather than a multi-day wait.

        Azerbaijan's State Veterinary Service doesn't require a separate export permit for a personal pet, only its endorsement of the health certificate, which needs reformatting to Turkey's own model rather than the EU-style document used on Azerbaijan's European routes.

    - heading: "The titre test sets the real timeline"
      body: |
        Frequent flights don't shorten the preparation window. A FAVN titre test is required, blood drawn at least 30 days after the rabies vaccination, a result of at least 0.5 IU/ml, and then a 90-day wait counted from the draw date itself. A booster given after the test carries its own separate 21-day wait, so time any revaccination against your travel plans rather than against the flight schedule.

        A pet arriving in Istanbul without a valid, correctly timed test faces 21 days of quarantine at the owner's expense, a costly outcome given how straightforward the rest of this route is.
