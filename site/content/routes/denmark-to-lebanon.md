  h1: "Pet Transport from Denmark to Lebanon"
  overview: |
    Lebanon is one of the more involved destinations for pet transport from Denmark. The route is not especially complicated in terms of entry requirements, but it has one feature that sets it apart: a mandatory advance import permit from the Lebanese Ministry of Agriculture that must be obtained before you book your flight. Lebanese administrative processing can be slow, and 10-12 weeks is the realistic lead time. Plan around this first.

    The vaccination requirements for Lebanon are more extensive than most destinations. Dogs need five vaccines current at travel time (distemper, hepatitis, leptospirosis, parvovirus, parainfluenza) plus rabies. Cats need four (viral rhinotracheitis, panleukopenia, calicivirus, leukemia) plus rabies. Parasite treatment is also required before entry.

    For all of that, there is no quarantine for compliant arrivals, no titre test required, and the route via Turkish Airlines through Istanbul to Beirut (BEY) is well established. Allow 8-12 weeks total from starting preparations to travel day.

  sections:
    - heading: "The import permit: why it comes first"
      body: |
        The Lebanese Ministry of Agriculture, agriculture.gov.lb, issues an advance import permit that is required before your pet can enter Lebanon. This is not a formality that can be sorted out at the airport. The permit must be confirmed and in your possession before you travel.

        Apply 10-12 weeks before your intended travel date. Lebanese government administration moves at its own pace, and delays beyond 8 weeks are not unusual. Chasing the permit while also managing a departure countdown adds significant stress. The safest approach is to begin the permit application as the very first step, before finalising your flight booking.

        Your pet transport agent can often assist with the permit application or advise on the process. If you are handling it yourself, contact the Lebanese Ministry of Agriculture directly and ask for their current requirements and processing times. Things do change, and the requirements confirmed here reflect 2026 practice but should be verified.

    - heading: "Vaccinations: Lebanon requires more than most"
      body: |
        Most destinations only require rabies for dogs and cats. Lebanon goes further. Dogs must arrive with current vaccinations for distemper, hepatitis, leptospirosis, parvovirus, and parainfluenza, in addition to rabies. Cats must have viral rhinotracheitis, panleukopenia, calicivirus, and feline leukemia vaccinations current, plus rabies. Source: Lebanese Ministry of Agriculture.

        If your pet is on a standard annual vaccination programme with a thorough vet, many of these may already be up to date. Check with your vet well in advance of travel and identify any gaps. Some vaccines require a primary course if your pet has never received them, which adds time.

        Parasite treatment is also required before entry and must be recorded in the health documentation with the product name and date administered. Your vet can combine this with the pre-travel examination. The official health certificate must be issued within 10 days of travel, so time the vet appointment to fall within that window.

    - heading: "Routing from Copenhagen to Beirut"
      body: |
        Turkish Airlines cargo is the most direct option for this route. Copenhagen (CPH) connects to Istanbul (IST), and Turkish Airlines operates regular cargo flights from IST to Beirut Rafic Hariri International Airport (BEY). Book through an approved Turkish Airlines cargo agent to confirm live animal acceptance on both the CPH-IST and IST-BEY legs.

        Middle East Airlines (MEA), Lebanon's national carrier, serves Beirut from several European cities. It is worth checking whether MEA operates a connection that works for cargo from Copenhagen, either directly or via a European hub. Contact MEA cargo directly to ask about live animal policy.

        Summer temperature embargoes on Turkish Airlines routes through Istanbul (June to September) can affect bookings. If you are travelling in high summer, ask your cargo agent to confirm whether restrictions apply to your specific route and dates. If they do, your agent can explore an alternative routing.

        Beirut's airport has seen periods of disruption related to Lebanon's wider situation. Before you finalise travel dates, check current FCO/Foreign Ministry travel advisories for Lebanon and confirm that Beirut Rafic Hariri Airport is operating normally.
