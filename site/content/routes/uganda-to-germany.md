  sections:
    - heading: "Ethiopian Airlines via Addis Ababa: the main cargo route"
      body: |
        Ethiopian Airlines is one of Africa's largest airlines and operates the most extensive cargo network on the continent. The Entebbe (EBB) to Addis Ababa (ADD) connection, with onward ADD-to-Frankfurt service, is the most established routing for Uganda-to-Germany pet moves.

        Ethiopian Airlines Cargo has experience handling live animal freight on the ADD-FRA corridor. Addis Ababa Bole International Airport handles significant volumes of live animal transit. Confirm live animal cargo acceptance on both the EBB-ADD and ADD-FRA legs, in writing, before booking.

        Brussels Airlines also operates a direct EBB-BRU service, which can be a useful alternative. From Brussels, the connection to Frankfurt or other German cities is straightforward. Your cargo agent can advise on which routing works better for your travel dates and crate size.

    - heading: "MAAIF and the health certificate process in Uganda"
      body: |
        The Ministry of Agriculture, Animal Industry and Fisheries (MAAIF) is the Ugandan government body responsible for veterinary oversight and animal health certification. MAAIF-authorised veterinarians issue EU-format health certificates, and MAAIF endorses them before departure.

        Not all veterinary clinics in Kampala or elsewhere in Uganda are authorised to issue EU export health certificates. The MAAIF can provide a list of authorised practitioners. MAAIF endorsement timing can vary, so ask in advance how long the process takes before scheduling your vet appointment.

        The FAVN test sample must be shipped internationally from Uganda. Samples travel to EU-approved laboratories in Europe or North America. DHL and FedEx handle biological specimen shipping from Entebbe. Your vet should use the correct preservative medium and confirm courier requirements before drawing blood.
