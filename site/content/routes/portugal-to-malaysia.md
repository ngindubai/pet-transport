  sections:
    - heading: "DGAV certificate for a non-EU destination"
      body: |
        Portugal's standard intra-EU pet passport does not cover this route. Because Malaysia sits outside the EU pet travel framework, your vet needs to issue a separate export health certificate authorised by DGAV, endorsed close to the travel date and matching the exact format Malaysia's DVS specifies in the import permit.

        Confirm the certificate format with your Malaysian-side agent before your DGAV appointment. A certificate built to the wrong template gets rejected, and DGAV endorsement appointments are not always available at short notice.

    - heading: "Why the FAVN pathway applies regardless of EU status"
      body: |
        DVS sorts entry countries into scheduled and non-scheduled groups. Scheduled countries, a short list including Australia, New Zealand, Singapore and Japan, get simplified entry. Portugal, like every other EU member state, sits in the non-scheduled group and follows the full pathway: microchip, vaccination, a FAVN titre test at a DVS-approved laboratory showing at least 0.5 IU/ml, then a 90-day wait from that result.

        The December 2024 DVS update set the current 14-day quarantine period at a DVS-designated facility at KLIA, at the owner's expense. This runs alongside, not instead of, the 90-day wait, so the two periods add up rather than overlap.

    - heading: "No direct route from Lisbon"
      body: |
        Turkish Airlines carries the heaviest connecting capacity from Lisbon to Kuala Lumpur, via Istanbul, and has a dependable live animal transfer process there. Emirates via Dubai is a solid alternative, though summer heat embargoes on the Gulf leg can affect scheduling. Etihad via Abu Dhabi is worth checking if the first two are full on your dates.

        Whichever carrier you choose, confirm your crate meets current IATA sizing rules and ask about connection time at the transfer airport, since a live animal sitting through a long layover needs a carrier used to handling that wait.
