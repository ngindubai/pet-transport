  h1: "Pet Transport from Australia to Honduras"
  overview: |
    SAG's list for a pet entering Honduras is short: a correctly timed rabies vaccination, an import permit, and a vet-issued health certificate. No titre test, no lengthy wait. Compared with most Latin American destinations covered elsewhere in this build, that's a genuinely light document set.

    The real friction on this route sits with distance, not paperwork. Nobody flies Australia to Honduras directly. Every pet transfers through a US gateway city, most practically Miami, or through Panama City, before the final leg lands at San Pedro Sula or Palmerola International.

  sections:
    - heading: "Honduras asks for less than most of its neighbours"
      body: |
        SAG, working through SENASA under Ministerial Agreement 331-2009, wants three things on arrival: a rabies vaccination given between 21 days and 12 months before entry, an import permit, and a health certificate from a licensed vet in the country of origin. No titre test applies, and microchipping, while sensible, isn't a standalone published requirement.

        Don't read "short list" as "low stakes." Honduras's own guidance is explicit that a pet arriving without matching paperwork can be quarantined where a facility exists, sent back to Australia, or in the worst case euthanised. Few requirements, real enforcement.

    - heading: "Australia's side adds almost nothing to the file"
      body: |
        There's no DAFF export permit to apply for here. Honduras doesn't ask for a government-endorsed export certificate, just a standard health certificate from a registered Australian vet, so the export side of this move is closer to a routine vet visit than a bureaucratic process.

        That simplicity shifts the real planning work onto the flight itself, and onto making sure the vaccination timing survives whatever the routing throws at it.

    - heading: "Two long-haul legs, minimum"
      body: |
        Qantas covers the run from an Australian east-coast gateway into Los Angeles or Dallas-Fort Worth. From there, American Airlines picks up the connection into Miami and onward to San Pedro Sula or Palmerola, the same last-leg service already relied on for this site's Honduras-to-UK and UAE-to-Honduras routes. Copa Airlines' Panama City hub is a genuine alternative worth pricing if American's cargo space or crate-size limits don't line up.

        Whichever routing is chosen, treat it as a two-carrier itinerary from the outset. Confirm the interline transfer arrangements before booking either leg, not after.
