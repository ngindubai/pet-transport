    - heading: "A rare direct flight, and why it doesn't shorten the paperwork"
      body: |
        Most routes in this series involve at least one connection through a hub airport. Athens to Beirut does not. Both Aegean Airlines, Greece's national carrier, and Middle East Airlines, Lebanon's national carrier, operate this route non-stop, a genuine convenience for a pet that would otherwise spend hours transiting through Istanbul, Dubai or Doha.

        That convenience applies only to the physical journey. The Lebanese Ministry of Agriculture's import permit still has to be secured before travel, and Lebanese government processing for this permit is rarely quick. Book the flight once the permit is confirmed, not before.

    - heading: "Working with Lebanese administrative timelines"
      body: |
        Lebanon's Ministry of Agriculture controls live animal imports, and getting the import permit is the aspect of this route that most rewards patience. Start the application at agriculture.gov.lb at least ten to twelve weeks ahead of your intended travel date, and follow up if you have not had a response within a week or two.

        A pet transport agent experienced with Lebanon can be genuinely useful here, since they typically have existing contacts within the Ministry and a realistic sense of current processing times. Once the permit and the endorsed EU export health certificate are both in hand, the short direct flight to Beirut is the most straightforward part of the whole process.
