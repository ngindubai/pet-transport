content:
  h1: "Pet Transport from Iceland to China"
  overview: |
    Iceland shows up on GACC's own list of designated rabies-free countries, the same group as Japan, the UK and Switzerland, which is genuinely good news: no rabies titre test on this leg. What isn't settled is whether that status also lets a pet skip China's standard 30-day quarantine, or whether the quarantine still applies because a titre-exempt pet hasn't completed the specific test route China's compliance system is built around.

    Sources disagree, and GACC's own systems aren't the kind of thing that can be checked automatically from outside China. Until an agent or GACC itself confirms the current position for your specific port of entry, plan for the quarantine as the safer assumption, and treat anything better as a genuine bonus.

  sections:
    - heading: "What being 'designated' actually gets you"
      body: |
        GACC sorts countries by rabies risk much like several other regulators covered on this site. Iceland's own decades-long rabies-free record, maintained through mandatory quarantine on every incoming pet rather than domestic vaccination, earns it a place on the designated list alongside Japan, the UK, Switzerland and New Zealand. The practical result: the rabies titre test, and the laboratory wait tied to it, doesn't apply.

        That single exemption removes a genuinely significant chunk of the process most other origin countries face on this exact route. It does not, by itself, resolve the quarantine question, and that is where owners should be careful about assuming too much.

    - heading: "The quarantine question this page can't fully settle"
      body: |
        China's standard compliance route lets a pet skip the 30-day quarantine if it completes the full sequence: microchip, two lifetime rabies vaccinations, and a passing titre test from a GACC-approved laboratory. An Icelandic pet, exempt from the titre test by virtue of its designated-country status, hasn't gone through that specific sequence, even though it arguably poses no greater risk. Some published sources describe designated-country pets entering without quarantine once every other requirement is met; others describe the 30-day quarantine as the default for any pet that hasn't completed the titre route, exemption or not.

        Given how much a wrong assumption here costs, both in money and in time apart from your pet, get this confirmed in writing from GACC or an IPATA-registered agent before you book a flight, not after.

    - heading: "The vaccination question, and the practicalities on arrival"
      body: |
        Because Iceland's rabies-free status rests on border control rather than routine vaccination, plenty of Icelandic pets have simply never had a rabies shot. China's health certificate for designated countries generally still expects proof of current vaccination on the paperwork, titre test or not, so this is worth raising with the vet at the very first appointment rather than discovering the gap close to departure.

        Once the pet is actually in China, national rules give way to local ones. Beijing limits households to one dog and restricts dogs over roughly 35cm within its central districts, and every dog needs registering with the local Public Security Bureau shortly after arrival, entirely separate from the customs process that got the pet into the country in the first place.

faqs:
  - question: "Does my pet from Iceland need a rabies titre test to enter China?"
    answer: "No. Iceland appears on GACC's list of designated rabies-free countries, the same group as Japan, the UK and Switzerland, which skips the titre test. Confirm this directly with GACC or an agent before relying on it, since the designation can be applied differently at different ports."
  - question: "Will my Icelandic pet still need to quarantine in China?"
    answer: "This is genuinely unclear from public sources. Some describe designated-country pets avoiding quarantine once every other requirement, microchip, vaccination and certificate, is met. Others describe the standard 30-day quarantine applying to any pet that hasn't completed China's full titre test route, which a titre-exempt pet has not. Confirm the current position with GACC or an IPATA agent, and budget for the 30 days as the safer assumption."
  - question: "Does my pet need a rabies vaccination even though Iceland is titre-exempt?"
    answer: "Very likely yes on the paperwork, even without the blood test behind it. Many Icelandic pets have never had one, since Iceland relies on its own border quarantine rather than domestic vaccination. Raise this with the vet early, since starting a vaccination course from nothing adds real weeks to the schedule."
  - question: "Is there a direct flight from Iceland to China?"
    answer: "No. No airline flies Keflavik direct to any Chinese city. A realistic routing connects Icelandair Cargo out of Keflavik to a European hub, then an onward carrier such as Air China or Cathay Pacific into a designated Chinese port, meaning at least two separate cargo bookings."
  - question: "Can I bring more than one pet into China at a time?"
    answer: "Not easily. China normally accepts only one dog or one cat per travelling passenger on entry. A second pet is generally treated as a commercial import with a different, stricter process, so a household with two pets should plan for either two travelling owners or a separate arrangement for the second animal."
