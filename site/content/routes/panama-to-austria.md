  h1: "Pet Transport Panama to Austria: 2026 Guide"
  overview: "Moving a pet from Panama to Austria is a long-lead process that takes 20-26 weeks of preparation. Panama is classified as an unlisted third country under Commission Delegated Regulation (EU) 2026/131, which means the mandatory FAVN rabies titre test pathway applies. The blood draw must take place at least 30 days after the rabies vaccination, the result must be 0.5 IU/ml or above at an EU-approved laboratory, and then a further 90-day wait begins from the blood draw date before the pet can enter Austria. Planning well ahead is the most important thing you can do."

  sections:
    - heading: "The Titre Test Pathway: Why Panama Requires It"
      body: |
        The EU divides third countries into listed and unlisted categories for pet import purposes. Listed countries have rabies control programmes that the EU considers sufficiently strong to waive the titre test. Unlisted countries, including Panama, have not received this designation.

        For pets from unlisted countries, Commission Delegated Regulation (EU) 2026/131 (which applies from April 2026 and updates the previous Regulation (EU) No 576/2013 framework) requires a FAVN (Fluorescent Antibody Virus Neutralisation) titre test. The test measures the pet's rabies antibody levels and confirms the vaccination has been effective.

        The sequence matters:
        1. Microchip (before vaccination)
        2. Rabies vaccination (after chip)
        3. Wait 30 days minimum from vaccination
        4. Blood draw for FAVN test
        5. Wait for result (0.5 IU/ml or above)
        6. Wait 90 days from the blood draw date (not from the result date)
        7. Travel to Austria

        The total minimum time from step 1 to step 7 is approximately 20 weeks, assuming the first titre test passes. If it fails, the clock restarts.

    - heading: "Austrian Entry Requirements in Detail"
      body: |
        The Austrian Federal Agency for Food Safety (AGES, ages.at) and the Federal Ministry for Social Affairs, Health, Care and Consumer Protection (BMSGPK, sozialministerium.at) oversee pet imports at Vienna International Airport (VIE).

        For a pet from Panama, the requirements on the day of arrival are:

        Microchip: ISO 11784/11785, implanted before vaccination. Scanned on arrival and cross-referenced with the health certificate.

        Rabies vaccination: Current, post-microchip, valid at the time of entry.

        FAVN titre test: Result of 0.5 IU/ml or above, drawn at an EU-approved laboratory, with the blood collected at least 30 days after vaccination. The 90-day post-draw wait must have elapsed.

        Health certificate: EU Annex IV non-commercial format, signed by a MIDA-registered vet and endorsed by DINASA, issued within 10 days of travel. The titre test results and the date of the blood draw must be clearly referenced.

        No quarantine. No import permit. But all the above must be in order.

    - heading: "Routing from Tocumen to Vienna"
      body: |
        Panama City's Tocumen International Airport (PTY) is Panama's main international hub and has direct flights to major European cities via Copa Airlines and partner carriers.

        Copa Airlines operates PTY to Amsterdam (AMS) and connections to Madrid (MAD). From AMS, KLM Cargo can arrange the onward shipment to Vienna (VIE). From MAD, Iberia or Austrian Airlines Cargo connect to VIE.

        Iberia Cargo flies PTY to MAD as a direct or one-stop service. Madrid to Vienna is a short second leg served by Austrian Airlines, Iberia, and Vueling, all of which handle live animal cargo.

        Lufthansa via Frankfurt (FRA) is a third option. This may involve routing Copa to a US gateway then Lufthansa to FRA, or Copa to MAD then onward to FRA. From FRA, Austrian Airlines or Lufthansa itself connects to VIE, which is one of the shortest European cargo hops.

        Choose the routing that offers the best live animal cargo space availability for your preferred travel window, and book well in advance.

    - heading: "DINASA Export Documentation"
      body: |
        Panama's export health certificate is issued by a MIDA-registered veterinarian and must be endorsed by DINASA (Dirección Nacional de Sanidad Animal) of MIDA (Ministerio de Desarrollo Agropecuario, mida.gob.pa).

        The certificate follows the EU Annex IV format and must include the titre test details: the date of the blood draw, the laboratory that performed the test, the result in IU/ml, and confirmation that the 90-day waiting period has been satisfied.

        The health certificate must be issued within 10 days of travel. It is a document that expires and cannot be prepared more than 10 days before departure. Timing the DINASA endorsement correctly within this window is important; allow two to three business days for the DINASA stamp if needed.

    - heading: "What to Do If the Titre Test Fails"
      body: |
        A titre test result below 0.5 IU/ml means the vaccination has not produced sufficient antibody response. This can happen due to timing, vaccine type, or the individual animal's immune response.

        If the result fails:
        - Revaccinate your pet
        - Wait at least 30 days after the new vaccination
        - Arrange a second blood draw
        - Wait for the new result
        - If it passes, a new 90-day wait begins from the new blood draw date

        This can add months to your timeline. This is why having the blood drawn at exactly 30 days post-vaccination (not before) and using a high-quality, approved vaccine from the start is important. Discuss which vaccines are accepted by EU-approved laboratories with your MIDA-registered vet before vaccinating.

faqs:
  - question: "Can I shorten the 90-day wait for my pet from Panama in any way?"
    answer: "No. The 90-day waiting period after the blood draw is mandated by Commission Delegated Regulation (EU) 2026/131. It cannot be waived, shortened, or paid for. It begins from the date of the blood draw, not from when the result is received or when the vaccination was given. The only way to shorten the total preparation timeline is to begin as early as possible."
  - question: "Does my pet need to stay in Panama during the 90-day wait, or can it travel elsewhere?"
    answer: "Your pet does not have to remain in Panama during the 90-day wait, but if it travels to another country, you would need to ensure the titre test record remains valid for the onward EU entry. If the pet travels to a listed EU or EEA country and then enters Austria from there, the rules change. Consult PetTransportGlobal if your situation involves an intermediate country."
  - question: "Which laboratories are EU-approved for the FAVN titre test from Panama?"
    answer: "The EU maintains a list of approved laboratories under Commission Decision 2000/258/EC. Approved labs in the US, Canada, the UK, and several other countries accept samples from Panama. Your MIDA-registered vet can ship the blood sample to an approved facility. Common options include ANSES (France), the Animal Health and Veterinary Laboratories Agency (UK), and Kansas State University Rabies Laboratory (US). Confirm with the laboratory that they accept samples from Panama and can provide results in the required format."
