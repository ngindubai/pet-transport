content:
  h1: "Pet Transport from Namibia to the United States"
  overview: |
    Six US airports currently hold a CDC-registered animal care facility, the only places a dog can legally land after arriving from a high-risk country without a passing titre. Washington Dulles is one of them, and it happens to be the endpoint of Ethiopian Airlines' nonstop service from Addis Ababa, the same hub that already handles Namibia's UAE-bound pet traffic. That single overlap makes Ethiopian the cleanest single-carrier option on this route.

    Namibia sits on the CDC's high-risk list for dog rabies, last confirmed on the CDC's page updated 15 April 2026. A dog needs the microchip-before-vaccine sequence, a titre test from a CDC-approved laboratory, and a facility reservation booked before departure. Cats clear the federal side far more lightly. Realistically, budget 12 to 16 weeks.

  sections:
    - heading: "Why Washington Dulles matters more than it looks"
      body: |
        CDC restricts arrivals from high-risk countries to a short list of airports with a registered animal care facility: Atlanta, JFK, LAX, Miami, Philadelphia and Washington Dulles. Land anywhere else with a dog that has not cleared the titre pathway, and there is no legal route through.

        Ethiopian Airlines flies Windhoek to Addis Ababa, then Addis Ababa to Dulles nonstop, roughly 13 hours in the air on an Airbus A350. One carrier, one transfer, and an arrival airport that already has the CDC infrastructure a high-risk-country dog needs. It's not the only way to route this journey, but it removes a variable most other options leave open.

    - heading: "The titre test, and the lab that already handles Namibia's exports"
      body: |
        Blood has to be drawn at least 30 days after the rabies vaccination, and at least 28 days before the flight lands in the US. Namibia has no domestic laboratory carrying FAVN accreditation, so the sample goes to ARC-Onderstepoort Veterinary Institute in Pretoria, the same facility already used for Namibia's UK and UAE-bound titre tests. OVI sits on the CDC's own approved-laboratory list, last updated 12 January 2026, so the result is valid for US entry without a second test at a different lab.

        Get the maths wrong on timing and you either delay the flight or arrive without a valid result. A dog without a passing titre is not refused entry outright, but does face a mandatory 28-day quarantine at the arrival facility, paid for by the owner.

    - heading: "What stays light for cats"
      body: |
        Federally, a cat needs almost none of this. No CDC rabies vaccination requirement, no titre test, no facility reservation. What stays in place is Namibia's own side of the paperwork: the state vet's health certificate and Directorate endorsement, plus whatever health certificate the airline itself wants. A lighter federal rule for cats is not the same as no paperwork at all.
