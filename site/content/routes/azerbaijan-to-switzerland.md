  sections:
    - heading: "Securing FSVO permission for an Azerbaijani pet"
      body: |
        The Swiss Federal Food Safety and Veterinary Office classifies Azerbaijan as a country with present rabies risk. For pets originating in such countries, the FSVO requires advance written import authorisation. This is separate from the health certificate, vaccination records, and titre test: it is a specific permission document that the Swiss border vet checks alongside the other paperwork.

        Apply through the FSVO portal at blv.admin.ch. You will need the pet's microchip number, the intended travel date, and the planned point of entry into Switzerland (typically Zurich Airport for cargo). Allow at least 3 weeks for processing. Keep the original permission document with the pet's travel pack.

        Your IPATA-registered agent can prepare and submit the FSVO application on your behalf. This is worth doing even if you are confident in your own language skills, because the FSVO checks for completeness before processing and an incomplete application restarts the clock.

    - heading: "The certificate format: where Switzerland differs from EU countries"
      body: |
        For EU member states, the standard document for pets from unlisted third countries is the EU AHC (Animal Health Certificate) in the format specified by EU Regulation 2020/692. Switzerland, as a non-EU country, has its own FSVO-specified format for health certificates.

        In practice, the FSVO format is similar to the EU AHC but has Swiss-specific fields and an FSVO-recognised endorsement stamp rather than an EU competent authority stamp. Your vet in Baku and the State Veterinary Service endorsement need to use the correct Swiss format, not the EU format.

        Before the vet appointment, confirm with your agent or the FSVO directly which current certificate template applies for pets from Azerbaijan. Using the wrong format means the Swiss border vet may refuse to accept it.
