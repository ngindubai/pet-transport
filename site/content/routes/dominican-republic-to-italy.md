# Pet Transport from the Dominican Republic to Italy

Here is what you need to do, in order, before your pet can enter Italy from the Dominican Republic.

1. **Implant the microchip** (ISO 11784/11785). This must happen before the rabies vaccination.
2. **Administer the rabies vaccination** after the microchip is confirmed in place.
3. **Wait 30 days**, then draw blood for the FAVN titre test.
4. **Send blood to an EU-approved laboratory** and await a result of at least 0.5 IU/ml.
5. **Wait 90 days from the blood draw date**. This is your entry window into the EU.
6. **Prepare the EU-format health certificate** with a DSA-authorised vet, then get it endorsed by DSA.
7. **Book your flight** and travel.

That sequence, from microchip to travel day, takes a minimum of 20 weeks. Most families need 22 to 26 weeks when you factor in laboratory turnaround, DSA processing, and airline booking lead times.

## Why This Takes Much Longer Than Canada

The Dominican Republic is an unlisted third country under EU Regulation 576/2013. Compare the two routes side by side:

| Requirement | Dominican Republic to Italy | Dominican Republic to Canada |
|---|---|---|
| Titre test required | Yes (FAVN, EU-approved lab) | No |
| Wait after titre test blood draw | 90 days | None |
| Microchip before vaccination mandatory | Yes | Recommended only |
| Quarantine on arrival | None (if compliant) | None |
| Minimum preparation time | 20-26 weeks | 4-8 weeks |
| Certificate endorsed by | DSA | DSA |

Canada does not require a titre test or any waiting period after vaccination. Italy, like all EU member states, applies the unlisted-country rules in full. The difference between these two routes is four to five months of preparation time.

## The Critical Sequencing Rule

The microchip must be in place before the rabies vaccination. This is a legal requirement under EU Regulation 576/2013, not a recommendation. If a vet vaccinates your pet and then implants the microchip, the EU will not accept the titre test result. You must start the whole process again.

Before the appointment, confirm with your vet in the Dominican Republic:

- The microchip will be implanted first and scanned to confirm it is reading correctly.
- The chip number will be recorded on the vaccination certificate before the vaccination is administered.

This single sequencing error is the most common reason EU pet import applications fail. It is entirely avoidable.

## Airlines and Routing

**Iberia** via Madrid is the natural routing for this corridor. SDQ to MAD, then MAD to Rome Fiumicino (FCO) or Milan Malpensa (MXP). Iberia handles pet cargo on the SDQ-MAD leg, and the connection through Madrid is well-established for Caribbean origins. Confirm live animal acceptance on both legs before buying tickets.

**Air France** via Paris Charles de Gaulle (CDG) connects to both Rome and Milan. A practical alternative if Iberia cargo space does not work for your schedule.

**ITA Airways**, Italy's national carrier, serves FCO and MXP via European hub connections. Confirm current live animal cargo policy directly with ITA before booking.

For all carriers: confirm your pet's crate meets IATA container requirements for the specific aircraft on each segment, and book your pet's place at least four to six weeks before travel.

## DSA Export Requirements

On the Dominican Republic side, the Departamento de Sanidad Animal (DSA) controls animal exports. You need a health certificate in EU-approved format, prepared by a DSA-authorised veterinarian and endorsed by DSA before departure. DSA endorsement takes several working days. The EU-format health certificate also has a validity window, so timing the endorsement correctly matters.

Obtain the DSA permit or prior notification before finalising your flight booking.

## On Arrival in Italy

Italian authorities inspect animals at the port of entry, either Rome Fiumicino (FCO) or Milan Malpensa (MXP) for most Caribbean arrivals. There is no quarantine for compliant arrivals. Inspectors check that the microchip matches the documentation, the titre test result meets the 0.5 IU/ml threshold, the 90-day wait has been observed, and the EU certificate is valid and DSA-endorsed.

If any element is missing or the timing has not been met, your pet may be held or refused entry. There is no fixing a paperwork problem at the airport.

Sources: EU Regulation 576/2013, Commission Delegated Regulation 2020/692, Italian Ministry of Health (salute.gov.it).
