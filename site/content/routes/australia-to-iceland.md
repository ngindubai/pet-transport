content:
  h1: "Pet Transport from Australia to Iceland"
  overview: |
    A rabies-free country moving a pet to a rabies-free island still runs into a 14-day quarantine. That is the part of this route owners find hardest to square with the facts. Australia's own disease-free status earns nothing at Iceland's border beyond a shorter titre test wait; MAST's Category 1 list treats Australia the same way it treats Germany or France, cutting the pre-export clock, not the isolation period.

    Two things shape this move more than anything else: no direct flight exists between the two countries, so every booking runs through at least two hubs, and the pet spends its first fortnight in Iceland at an approved isolation station regardless of how clean its paperwork is.

  sections:
    - heading: "Category 1 status: a shorter wait, not a shorter quarantine"
      body: |
        MAST classifies Australia as Category 1, its most favourable tier, on the strength of Australia's own rabies-free status. Practically, that means the titre test blood draw has to happen at least 30 days after vaccination, and once the lab confirms a result of 0.5 IU/ml or above, there is no further fixed wait before travel. Canada and the USA, both Category 2 under MAST, face an additional 90-day wait from the blood draw date on top of that.

        What Category 1 status does not touch is the 14 days every animal spends at Einangrunarstoedin i Reykjanesbae or Einangrunarstoedin Mosel after landing. Iceland runs this quarantine for every arriving pet, Category 1 origin included, as a genuine biosecurity measure protecting its rabies-free status, not as a penalty tied to any single country's paperwork.

    - heading: "Two hubs, not one, and cargo only on the final leg"
      body: |
        Getting from Melbourne or Sydney to Keflavik means at least two connections. Emirates covers the long-haul sector into Dubai, where Qantas and Emirates share a livestock handling facility, before a further connection reaches a European gateway such as London or Frankfurt. From there, Icelandair or Lufthansa runs the final short hop into Keflavik.

        Since 1 November 2024, Icelandair does not carry pets as checked baggage on any route it flies, this last leg included, so that sector moves as manifested cargo through Icelandair Cargo. Lufthansa's Frankfurt Animal Lounge, a purpose-built live animal facility, is a workable alternative for that final connection if Icelandair Cargo capacity does not fit your dates.

    - heading: "MAST's own form, and why a standard Australian certificate will not do"
      body: |
        MAST doesn't accept a generic Australian vet certificate for this route. It requires its own Category 1 form, D1 for dogs or C1 for cats, completed by an accredited vet and endorsed by DAFF as the competent authority of origin. Brief your vet on this early, since the standard domestic paperwork most Australian vets are used to writing doesn't cover MAST's specific fields.

        Book isolation station space well before finalising a flight. Both Icelandic stations take pets on a limited intake schedule, and a confirmed flight with no quarantine slot booked is a real risk given how far the animal has already travelled by the time it lands.
