  h1: "Pet Transport from Australia to Ukraine"
  overview: |
    Australia has no rabies, and Ukraine's own rules give a WOAH-recognised rabies-free country real credit for that. On paper, an Australian pet can skip the rabies vaccination Ukraine normally demands of every arrival. It is one of the few genuine advantages an Australian owner has on this site's Ukraine routes, and it comes with a catch: the guidance behind it doesn't say clearly whether the titre test still applies once vaccination itself is waived.

    None of that changes how the pet actually gets there. Ukraine's airspace has had no scheduled flights since February 2022, so the trip runs Melbourne to Dubai to Warsaw, then finishes by road across the Ukrainian border, into a country the Australian government currently advises its own citizens not to visit at all.

  sections:
    - heading: "The rabies-free exemption, and where it stops being simple"
      body: |
        Ukraine's import guidance exempts pets from WOAH-recognised rabies-free countries from the rabies vaccination requirement outright. Australia is on that list, which puts this route in a smaller group than most others covered here, where vaccination and a full titre sequence are simply assumed.

        What isn't settled is the titre test. Compiled guidance elsewhere ties the blood draw to the vaccination date, 14 days after, yet separately calls a titre test mandatory for entry "from any country." An owner relying on the exemption and skipping vaccination altogether is left without a clear answer on whether a titre test still applies, and to what it would even be measured against. Confirm this directly with a vet near the planned crossing point before deciding, rather than assuming either interpretation.

    - heading: "Warsaw is the flight; the border is the finish"
      body: |
        Emirates covers the practical long-haul sector here: Melbourne to Dubai, then on to Warsaw on the daily EK179 service, the same pairing already used elsewhere on this site for UAE-Ukraine moves. Qantas commonly codeshares the Melbourne-Dubai leg, so the ticket may say Qantas even when Emirates flies the aircraft.

        From Warsaw, there's no onward flight. The pet clears customs, then travels overland, typically toward a crossing near Lviv, to complete entry with a Ukrainian state veterinarian. Given the Australian government's current 'Do Not Travel' advisory for Ukraine, reissued in May 2026 with Lviv itself named as a target of intensified strikes, this overland leg deserves the same weight in planning as any of the paperwork above it.
