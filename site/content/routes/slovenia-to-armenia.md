  overview: |
    Armenia does not require a titre test for EU-origin pets, and there is no quarantine for compliant arrivals at Zvartnots International Airport in Yerevan. Slovenia's EU membership means the export process is the standard UVHVVR health certificate route.

    The main practical consideration for Slovenia-based owners is the routing. Ljubljana Airport (LJU) is a small airport with limited international cargo capacity. Most international pet cargo from Slovenia is routed via Vienna (VIE), which is the natural hub for this journey. This adds a connection but is well-established.

    Preparation time of three to five weeks is realistic, assuming vaccinations are current and the UVHVVR endorsement timeline is factored in.

  sections:
    - heading: "What Armenia requires at the border"
      body: |
        Armenia's import rules are administered by the State Service for Food Safety (SSFS), ssfs.am. Officers conduct veterinary inspection at Zvartnots International Airport (EVN) in Yerevan before releasing your pet.

        The core requirements are a valid rabies vaccination (at least 21 days before entry, within 11 months), an official health certificate issued within 10 days of travel, and an ISO-compliant microchip. Armenia does not require a titre test for EU-member-state pets. For 1-2 personal companion animals, no import permit is required.

    - heading: "UVHVVR health certificate process from Slovenia"
      body: |
        Slovenia's export health certificate for non-EU destinations is the EU-format official veterinary certificate. It must be signed by a UVHVVR-authorised official vet and then endorsed by UVHVVR (Uprava RS za varno hrano, veterinarstvo in varstvo rastlin). Both steps must complete within 10 days of departure.

        Book the vet examination within 10 days of your travel date. After signing, allow 1-2 working days for UVHVVR endorsement. Your regular vet can handle vaccinations and microchipping, but the official health certificate must come from a UVHVVR-authorised official vet. Check uvhvvr.gov.si for the list of authorised vets.

    - heading: "Cargo routing from Ljubljana to Yerevan"
      body: |
        Ljubljana Airport (LJU) does not have direct international cargo services to the Caucasus. The standard routing is Austrian Airlines from LJU to Vienna (VIE), then onward via Turkish Airlines or another carrier from VIE to Yerevan (EVN) via Istanbul (IST).

        This multi-leg routing is manageable but requires coordinating all legs through a single cargo agent who can confirm live animal acceptance on each segment. Turkish Airlines cargo from VIE is well-established. If travelling June to September, check for temperature embargoes on the Istanbul connection.

        Some owners prefer to drive to Vienna Airport (approximately 2 hours from Ljubljana) and originate the cargo journey at VIE. This can simplify the cargo coordination by starting at a larger hub.
