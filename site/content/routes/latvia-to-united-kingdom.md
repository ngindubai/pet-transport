  sections:
    - heading: "EU pet passport or Animal Health Certificate: what Latvia-based owners need"
      body: |
        Since the UK left the EU, only EU pet passports issued before 1 January 2021 are valid for UK entry. If your pet's passport was issued after this date, you need an EU Animal Health Certificate from an official Latvian vet. The AHC is valid for 10 days from the vet examination date and covers a single journey.

        The AHC needs to be issued by an officially designated vet - not all private veterinary practices in Latvia are authorised. Check with your vet whether they hold the official vet designation, or ask the Latvian Food and Veterinary Service for a list of authorised vets.

        If you travel regularly between Latvia and the UK with your pet, factor in the cost of a new AHC for each trip. This is an ongoing cost that did not exist when EU pet passports were fully recognised.
    - heading: "airBaltic: the most practical option for Latvia-UK pet travel"
      body: |
        airBaltic operates from Riga International Airport (RIX) to London Gatwick and other UK airports with good frequency. The direct routing is the key advantage: your pet stays on one aircraft, with no intermediate handling.

        Small pets travel in the cabin on airBaltic, stored in a carrier under the seat. Weight limits apply - check the current airBaltic cabin pet policy for maximum weight including carrier. Larger pets travel as cargo and airBaltic has handling facilities at Riga.

        Ryanair also flies Riga to UK airports and accepts very small cabin pets, but weight and carrier size restrictions are tight. For most dogs, airBaltic or cargo via Lufthansa is more practical.
faqs:
  - question: "Does my pet need a titre test to travel from Latvia to the UK?"
    answer: "No. Latvia is a listed Part 1 country for UK pet travel. No titre test is required and there is no mandatory waiting period. You need a current rabies vaccination, ISO microchip, and an EU Animal Health Certificate (or a valid EU pet passport issued before January 2021), plus tapeworm treatment for dogs."
  - question: "Can I use an EU pet passport from Latvia to enter the UK?"
    answer: "Only if the passport was issued before 1 January 2021. Passports issued on or after that date are not valid for UK entry. If your pet's passport falls into this category, get an EU Animal Health Certificate from an official Latvian vet before travel. The AHC is valid for 10 days."
  - question: "Can my small dog travel in the cabin from Riga to London?"
    answer: "Yes, airBaltic allows small pets in the cabin on its Riga to UK routes (subject to weight limits including carrier). Ryanair also accepts very small cabin pets on Riga-UK routes. For larger dogs, cargo is the option - airBaltic Cargo or Lufthansa Cargo via Frankfurt."
  - question: "Does my dog need a tapeworm treatment even though Latvia is in the EU?"
    answer: "Yes. The UK requires tapeworm treatment for all dogs arriving from abroad, including from Part 1 listed EU countries. A vet must administer Praziquantel (minimum 23.4mg) between 24 and 120 hours before your dog arrives in the UK. This is recorded in the AHC or pet passport."
  - question: "How quickly can I travel from Latvia to the UK with my pet?"
    answer: "Preparation is quick for listed countries. If vaccinations are current, you mainly need the AHC (issued within 10 days of travel) and the tapeworm treatment within the 24-120 hour window. Most owners can be ready within 2-4 weeks of deciding to travel."
