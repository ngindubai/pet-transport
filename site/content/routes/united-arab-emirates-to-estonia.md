content:
  h1: "Pet Transport United Arab Emirates to Estonia"
  overview: |
    MOCCAE's own export rules are relatively relaxed, but that doesn't carry over to how the EU treats a pet arriving from the UAE. The UAE sits in Annex II of the EU's third-country classification, the same category as most of the world outside the EU and a short list of low-risk countries, so a full rabies antibody titre test and a 90-day wait from the blood draw both apply here.

    That single fact means this route runs on EU time, not Gulf time. Once the wait is served and the paperwork is in order, the actual journey is straightforward: connect via Riga on airBaltic or via Istanbul on Turkish Airlines Cargo into Tallinn.

  sections:
    - heading: "Why Annex II status changes everything for this route"
      body: |
        Some UAE-outbound routes in this build, into Lebanon or Rwanda for instance, skip the titre test entirely because those destinations don't apply the EU risk-category system. Estonia does, and the UAE's Annex II classification puts it alongside most non-EU countries rather than the short low-risk list that includes the UK and other EU member states.

        That means the full sequence: microchip before vaccination, a FAVN titre test drawn at least 30 days after the primary dose, a result of at least 0.5 IU/ml, and a mandatory 90-day wait counted from the blood draw itself, not the vaccination date. Miss that distinction and the pet will be refused entry even with every other document correct.

    - heading: "What still needs to happen on the UAE side"
      body: |
        Every pet leaving the UAE needs a MOCCAE export certificate regardless of the EU's own requirements, applied for online and confirmed at an in-person clinic exam in Dubai or Abu Dhabi. For EU entry specifically, an EU-format non-commercial health certificate is also required, issued within 10 days of travel and endorsed by MOCCAE.

    - heading: "Getting from the Gulf to the Baltic"
      body: |
        There's no direct flight from Dubai to Tallinn. airBaltic's connection through Riga draws on its own dense Baltic network for the final leg, while Turkish Airlines Cargo via Istanbul is the sturdier choice for a larger dog needing dedicated cargo space. Either way, book the connecting leg early, since live animal allocations can tighten around peak travel periods.
