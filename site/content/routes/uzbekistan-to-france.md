content:
  h1: "Pet Transport from Uzbekistan to France"
  overview: |
    There's no nonstop flight from Tashkent to Paris, unlike the direct options Uzbekistan Airways runs to Frankfurt and Milan. Turkish Airlines fills the gap instead, connecting through Istanbul with multiple daily departures in both directions, which gives more scheduling flexibility than waiting on a limited weekly service elsewhere.

    France applies the same EU sequence as any member state facing an unlisted third country: a titre test and a fixed 90-day wait from the blood draw.

  sections:
    - heading: "Why Istanbul, not a direct flight"
      body: |
        Uzbekistan Airways serves Frankfurt and Milan nonstop from Tashkent, but Paris currently isn't part of its direct network. Turkish Airlines' multiple daily Tashkent-Istanbul flights, feeding into its own daily Istanbul-Paris service, make this the practical standard routing, and the frequency advantage over a once- or twice-weekly direct option is real: there's far more room to work around the titre test timeline.

        On arrival, the Roissy Animal Hotel, run jointly by Air France, KLM and Martinair Cargo, handles the inspection at Charles de Gaulle.

    - heading: "The EU titre test sequence and Uzbekistan's export process"
      body: |
        France requires the standard unlisted-country protocol: microchip before vaccination, a 30-day gap before the titre blood draw, a result of at least 0.5 IU/ml, then a fixed 90 days from the draw date, under Commission Delegated Regulation (EU) 2026/131.

        On the export side, the territorial veterinary service, under the Committee of Veterinary and Livestock Development that replaced the old State Committee in January 2023, issues the certificate at the TAS border checkpoint. Few EU-approved laboratories operate inside Uzbekistan, so build in courier time for the titre sample.
