  h1: "Pet Transport from Mongolia to the United States"
  overview: |
    Seoul, not any European hub, is the practical gateway for a pet moving from Ulaanbaatar to the United States. MIAT Mongolian Airlines runs 25 direct flights a week to Incheon, Korean Air runs 8, and both feed onward connections across Korean Air's extensive US network. That single fact shapes almost every other decision on this route.

    Mongolia is also CDC high-risk for dog rabies, one of over 100 countries in that category, which means a titre test or 28-day quarantine applies to dogs on top of the standard CDC Dog Import Form every dog needs. Cats, by contrast, face no federal rabies requirement at all, a real practical split if your household has both.

    One routing worth ruling out early: Air China suspended dog transport to the US in February 2025. A Beijing connection might look geographically sensible on a map, but it currently doesn't work for a dog, whatever your cat situation might allow.

  sections:
    - heading: "Why Seoul is the route, not Beijing or Moscow"
      body: |
        Ulaanbaatar's Chinggis Khaan International Airport sits almost equidistant from several regional hubs, but the numbers favour Seoul decisively. MIAT flies 25 times a week to Incheon, its highest-frequency international route by some distance. Korean Air adds 8 more weekly flights on the same city pair. From Incheon, Korean Air's own network reaches most major US gateways without a change of carrier, which matters for live animal handling continuity.

        Istanbul is the credible alternative, reached on MIAT's own 787 service or Turkish Airlines, both connecting onward to the US on a single Turkish Airlines ticket. It's a longer routing than Seoul in most cases, but useful if your dog's breed or size doesn't suit Korean Air's cabin and hold limits.

        Beijing looks tempting on a map given the short hop from Ulaanbaatar, but Air China suspended dog transport to the United States entirely from 1 February 2025. That rules the routing out for dogs regardless of any other factor, though it may still work for a cat, worth confirming directly with Air China given how recently the policy changed.

    - heading: "CDC high-risk rules and the arrival-airport constraint"
      body: |
        Mongolia's CDC high-risk classification means dogs need a Certification of Foreign Rabies Vaccination and Microchip form, completed by a Mongolian vet and endorsed by GAVS, plus either a passing titre test from a CDC-approved laboratory or 28-day quarantine at a CDC-registered facility. Source: CDC, cdc.gov/importation. The titre route needs blood drawn at least 30 days after vaccination, with the result landing at least 28 days before the flight, a two-month minimum window that doesn't compress no matter how urgent the move.

        The quarantine alternative carries a routing consequence specific to remote origins like Mongolia: the flight must land at the exact airport hosting the CDC-registered facility, with no domestic connection beforehand. Combined with Ulaanbaatar's limited direct connections, that can mean routing through Seoul or Istanbul specifically to reach a US city with a compatible facility, rather than whichever US gateway is cheapest or most convenient. Getting the titre test done removes this constraint and opens up any US port of entry, which is why most owners on this route prioritise it over quarantine.

        Cats sidestep all of this. No CDC form, no titre test, no quarantine risk. A standard airline-required health certificate is sensible, but it isn't a federal mandate.

    - heading: "GAVS documentation and timing it against your flight"
      body: |
        The General Authority for Veterinary Services in Ulaanbaatar processes export documentation through a digital veterinary certificate system that has been running nationwide since May 2019, a genuinely modern setup that speeds up what can otherwise be a slow paperwork chain elsewhere in the region. The Certification of Foreign Rabies Vaccination and Microchip form still needs a vet visit and GAVS endorsement close to your travel date, since the form is single-use and valid only 30 days from the vet's signature.

        Book your flight first, once your titre test result is in hand or your quarantine reservation is confirmed, then schedule the GAVS endorsement to fall inside that 30-day window rather than well ahead of it. Given the connection-dependent routing through Seoul or Istanbul, build in a buffer for the international leg's own scheduling, MIAT and Korean Air don't fly Ulaanbaatar to Incheon on every day of the week on every fare class.
