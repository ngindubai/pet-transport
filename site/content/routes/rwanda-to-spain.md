  overview: |
    The complexity on the Rwanda to Spain route does not come from the regulations, which are the same as for any African country entering the EU. It comes from the routing. Kigali International Airport (KGL) has no direct flights to Spain, which means every move involves connecting through Brussels, Istanbul, or Addis Ababa. Each connection adds a cargo handling step, and each carrier involved must independently confirm it accepts live animals on that specific sector.

    On top of the routing challenge, Rwanda is a non-listed third country under EU rules, so the FAVN titre test and 3-month waiting period are mandatory. Allow 22 to 28 weeks and start planning the moment your move to Spain is confirmed.

        There is no direct air service from Kigali to Madrid or Barcelona. Three main routing options are worth considering, each with different trade-offs.

        Brussels Airlines operates the KGL-BRU sector and has cargo facilities at Brussels Airport that are well-established for live animal handling. From Brussels, connections to Madrid Barajas or Barcelona El Prat are available. The Brussels route is often the most practical for pets, because the BRU cargo hub is experienced with European live animal arrivals and the connection to Spain is straightforward.

        Turkish Airlines routes via Istanbul (IST) to Madrid or Barcelona. The KGL-IST-MAD routing involves two airports but keeps connections to two points rather than three. Turkish Cargo accepts live animals on many routes and the Istanbul hub handles significant volumes of cargo from Africa.

        Ethiopian Airlines via Addis Ababa (ADD) is a further option. Ethiopian is one of the largest African carriers by cargo volume and connects well to Europe, including Madrid. Confirm live animal acceptance on the specific ADD-MAD or ADD-BCN sector before booking, as acceptance can vary by aircraft type.

        RwandAir connects Kigali to Brussels and is a useful local option for the first sector. Confirm live animal cargo acceptance before booking. For each routing, book all sectors before committing to any single leg, and confirm that the receiving carrier in Spain can accept the animal from the connecting flight.

    - heading: "What does Spain require and how does the RAB export process work?"
      body: |
        Spain follows EU Regulation 576/2013 for all pets from non-listed third countries. Rwanda is not on the EU approved list. The required sequence is: ISO microchip implanted before the rabies vaccination, vaccination (pet at least 12 weeks old), FAVN blood draw at least 30 days after vaccination, a result of at least 0.5 IU/ml at an EU-approved laboratory, and a 3-month wait from the blood draw date. Source: food.ec.europa.eu, 2026.

        On the Rwandan side, RAB (Rwanda Agriculture and Animal Resources Development Board, rab.gov.rw) is the authority that endorses the official health certificate for companion animal exports. Your official vet prepares the AHC and submits it to RAB for endorsement. Allow several working days for this process. The certificate must be issued within 10 days of your travel date, so the vet appointment and RAB submission fall in your final week before departure.

        One practical challenge on this route is the titre test itself. EU-approved laboratories may not be available within Rwanda, which means the blood sample may need to be shipped to a laboratory in Europe or South Africa that holds EU approval. Factor this shipping time into your overall timeline. The 3-month wait starts from the blood draw date regardless of how long the sample takes to reach the laboratory, so book the draw early.

        Spain's PPP (Perros Potencialmente Peligrosos) rules apply on arrival: American Pit Bull Terriers, Rottweilers, Staffordshire Bull Terriers, American Staffordshire Terriers, Dogo Argentino, Fila Brasileiro, Tosa Inu, Akita Inu, and crosses are not barred from entry but require EUR 120,000 civil liability insurance and a muzzle and lead in public once in Spain.

    - heading: "What does this route cost and what should you watch out for?"
      body: |
        Costs on the Rwanda to Spain route typically fall between EUR 1,300 and EUR 3,600. The cargo component is the main driver, running EUR 900-2,500 depending on the routing, carrier, crate size, and pet weight. Connected routings through Brussels or Istanbul cost more than a hypothetical direct flight, because multiple cargo legs are billed separately. The titre test adds EUR 85-170 plus any sample shipping costs. Add veterinary fees, the RAB endorsement charge, and EUR 80-250 for an IATA-compliant crate.

        The most common problem on this route is not confirming all cargo legs before booking. It is straightforward to confirm that Brussels Airlines accepts live animals from Kigali to Brussels. What catches people out is assuming that confirmation covers the connecting carrier from Brussels to Madrid. It does not. The onward leg is a separate booking with a separate carrier, and live animal acceptance must be confirmed for that sector independently.

        The second watch-out is sample shipping for the titre test. If the blood sample needs to go to a laboratory outside Rwanda, the shipping time is not part of the 30-day post-vaccination wait, but it does add real calendar time to your schedule. Allow one to two weeks for sample shipping and results turnaround on top of the mandatory waiting periods.
