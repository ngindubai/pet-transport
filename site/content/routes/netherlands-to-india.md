content:
  h1: "Pet Transport from the Netherlands to India"
  overview: |
    Moving a pet from the Netherlands to India is one of the more involved international pet relocations a European owner will face. India requires a pre-approved AQCS import permit, the official Dutch health certificate must be endorsed by the Indian Embassy in The Hague, and your pet must arrive through the specific port declared on the permit.

    Start planning at least twelve weeks out. The permit application, embassy endorsement, and cargo booking all have lead times that cannot be shortened. KLM is the most logical airline for this route given its Amsterdam Schiphol hub.

    Working with a registered pet transport agent (IPATA-member) is strongly recommended for first-timers on this route.
  sections:
    - heading: "India AQCS permit: the non-negotiable first step"
      body: |
        India's Animal Quarantine and Certification Service (AQCS) at aqcsindia.gov.in must issue an import permit before your pet travels. Apply 6-8 weeks before your travel date. The permit specifies your port of entry in India. You must arrive at that exact port. Mumbai (BOM), Delhi (DEL), Chennai (MAA), and Bangalore (BLR) all have AQCS quarantine facilities.

        If your plans change after the permit is issued, contact AQCS immediately to request an amendment or new permit.

    - heading: "Dutch health certificate and Indian Embassy endorsement"
      body: |
        Your pet's official health certificate must be issued by an NVWA-authorised official veterinarian in the Netherlands. The NVWA (Dutch Food and Consumer Product Safety Authority) maintains a register of authorised vets at nvwa.nl.

        After the official cert is signed, take it to the Indian Embassy in The Hague or the Indian Consulate in Amsterdam for endorsement. Book an appointment in advance. Allow a full working week for processing. The endorsed original must travel with your pet.

    - heading: "KLM Cargo from Schiphol to India"
      body: |
        KLM Cargo at Amsterdam Schiphol is one of the most well-equipped operations in Europe for pet cargo. Direct KLM services fly to Mumbai, Delhi, and Chennai. For Bangalore and other cities, connections via Delhi or Mumbai are common.

        Book cargo space through the KLM Cargo department (not the passenger desk). Confirm your IATA crate meets KLM's current specifications for the route. Temperature-controlled holds are standard on KLM's wide-body fleet.

    - heading: "Arrival in India: AQCS inspection"
      body: |
        On arrival, your pet and all documents go to the AQCS quarantine checkpoint. A 24-48 hour inspection is standard. If any document is missing or incorrect, the inspection period can extend to 7 days.

        Whoever receives the pet in India should be briefed on the AQCS process and ready to pay any applicable inspection fees promptly. Delays can be reduced by having a complete and correctly endorsed document set.
faqs:
  - question: "How do I apply for an AQCS import permit for India from the Netherlands?"
    answer: "Apply via aqcsindia.gov.in. Submit your application at least 6-8 weeks before travel and specify your port of entry in India. Your pet transport agent can apply on your behalf. The permit is port-specific and cannot be used for a different arrival city."
  - question: "Does the Dutch veterinary health certificate need embassy endorsement for India?"
    answer: "Yes. The official health certificate issued by an NVWA-authorised vet must be taken to the Indian Embassy in The Hague or the Indian Consulate in Amsterdam for endorsement before you fly. This step is mandatory."
  - question: "How long is the arrival inspection in India for pets from the Netherlands?"
    answer: "A standard AQCS inspection takes 24-48 hours. If documentation is complete and endorsed, this is routine. Any missing document or discrepancy can extend the period to 7 days."
  - question: "Which airline is best for transporting a dog from the Netherlands to India?"
    answer: "KLM Cargo from Schiphol is the most convenient option given the direct route to Indian metros. Air India, Emirates, Lufthansa, and British Airways are further options. All use cargo (manifested freight) for pets on long-haul routes."
  - question: "How much does it cost to ship a dog from the Netherlands to India?"
    answer: "Expect costs between EUR 1,200-3,500 depending on your dog's size, the crate dimensions, the route, and whether you use an agent. This covers cargo fees, crate, vet costs, permit fees, and embassy endorsement. Larger dogs cost more."
