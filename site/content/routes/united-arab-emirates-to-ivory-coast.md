  h1: "Pet Transport from United Arab Emirates to Ivory Coast"
  overview: |
    Search Air Arabia's route map for Abidjan and it won't show up. The UAE's own budget carrier flies to 81 destinations from Sharjah, Abu Dhabi and Ras Al Khaimah, an impressively wide network for a low-cost airline, and none of it reaches Cote d'Ivoire. That rules out the option most owners would check first.

    Ethiopian Airlines fills the gap properly instead. Its Addis Ababa to Abidjan sector is currently a monopoly route, no other airline flies it nonstop, and Ethiopian runs it roughly seven times a week, feeding from its own frequent Dubai-Addis Ababa service. A pet can stay on one airline's network from Dubai to Abidjan without a carrier change.

    The regulatory side asks for no titre test, which is generous, but trades that for two administrative steps that take real planning time: an import permit processed entirely by email through DSV, with no published turnaround, and a health certificate that has to arrive in French. Budget ten to fourteen weeks and expect the DSV step to be the one that actually decides the calendar.

  sections:
    - heading: "Why Air Arabia doesn't feature here"
      body: |
        It's a reasonable first instinct to check Air Arabia for any UAE-origin route to Africa or Asia, given how far its network reaches from three separate home bases. For this specific corridor, that instinct doesn't pay off. Air Arabia has no service to Abidjan, and nothing in its network gets close enough to assemble a realistic connection through its own hubs either.

        Ethiopian Airlines is the stronger choice by a clear margin anyway. Its Addis Ababa-Abidjan sector is currently the only nonstop service on that city pair at all, flown roughly seven times a week, and it connects directly from Ethiopian's own Dubai departures, up to four times daily. Turkish Airlines via Istanbul remains the practical second option if Ethiopian's schedule doesn't fit.

    - heading: "Two administrative steps that matter more than the vaccine"
      body: |
        Cote d'Ivoire skips the rabies titre test entirely, for a UAE-origin pet just as it does for one arriving from the UK or the US. What replaces that time cost is an import permit processed by DSV through two email addresses rather than any online system, with no fixed turnaround published anywhere. Send the application and the vaccination certificate copy at least eight to ten weeks out, and follow up directly if a couple of weeks pass in silence.

        The second step is a language requirement: the health certificate needs to be in French, or travel with a certified translation, before Ivorian officials at Port Bouet will accept it. A MOCCAE certificate, issued in English or Arabic, needs that translation step built into the schedule, alongside the standard vet and export process.
