content:
  h1: "Pet Transport from Russia to the UAE"
  overview: |
    Put two facts together and this becomes one of the fastest Russia export routes in the whole build. First, MOCCAE sets no fixed wait after the rabies titre test blood draw, unlike the EU's 90-day rule. Second, Aeroflot, FlyDubai and Emirates all fly Moscow to Dubai directly, with no connecting airport in between.

    That doesn't mean the titre test itself disappears. Russia isn't on MOCCAE's short list of low-risk countries, so the blood test and its 0.5 IU/ml threshold still apply, alongside the standard microchip and vaccination sequence. What it does mean is that once the test clears, there's no equivalent of the EU's three-month countdown standing between a passing result and a booked flight.

  sections:
    - heading: "The titre test without the EU-style wait"
      body: |
        A pet needs a microchip fitted before its rabies vaccination, a vaccination current for at least 21 days if it's the primary dose, and a rabies antibody titre test at an accredited laboratory clearing 0.5 IU/ml. Once that result comes back, MOCCAE treats it as valid for 365 days, with no further countdown attached. Compare that to the EU's fixed 90-day wait from the blood draw itself, and the time saved on this route is real, not marginal.

        The online MOCCAE import permit runs for 90 days from issue, so apply once a firm flight date exists rather than earlier. Minimum age from Russia's risk category is 15 weeks, same as most countries outside MOCCAE's short low-risk list.

    - heading: "A direct flight, unusual for a Russia route"
      body: |
        Most Russia routes in this build need a two-leg booking through Istanbul or Dubai, because the UK, EU and US all ban Russian carriers from their airspace and Russia bans theirs in return. None of that applies between Russia and the UAE. Aeroflot's Sheremetyevo-Dubai service, FlyDubai's Vnukovo-Dubai route and Emirates' Domodedovo-Dubai service are all genuine non-stops, and all three only resumed or increased frequency in June and July 2026, once the ceasefire ending the regional conflict let Gulf capacity recover.

        Book directly with whichever carrier suits your dates, and confirm live-animal acceptance before relying on the schedule, since none of the three has published pet policy terms specific to this particular route.
