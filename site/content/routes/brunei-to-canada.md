content:
  h1: "Pet Transport from Brunei to Canada"
  overview: |
    Royal Brunei Airlines will not carry a pet in its hold, for any private owner, on any route. That single fact shapes every Brunei departure on this site, and Canada is no exception: the move starts with a transfer to Singapore or Kuala Lumpur before a long-haul carrier is even in the picture.

    What comes after that transfer is the easy part. Canada is not on the Canadian Food Inspection Agency's high-risk list for dog rabies, last updated 13 March 2025, and it is not one of the handful of countries whose own pets can skip vaccination either. For a Brunei-origin pet arriving in Canada, that means a standard rabies certificate, no titre test, and no fixed waiting period once the vaccine is given, which makes the Canadian import side noticeably lighter than the UK or US routes from the same origin.

  sections:
    - heading: "Why this route still starts in Singapore, not Brunei"
      body: |
        Brunei International Airport (BWN) has no commercial live animal cargo product run by the national carrier. Owners typically arrange a road transfer to Singapore, crossing Malaysian territory, or a short regional flight to Kuala Lumpur, and the pet only enters the formal cargo system once it reaches one of those two hubs.

        Budget for this leg separately. It is a domestic cost most owners moving from, say, Singapore or Malaysia itself never have to think about, and it needs its own timing worked in around the DAA export paperwork.

        From Singapore, Singapore Airlines Cargo or Cathay Pacific via Hong Kong both reach Vancouver. Kuala Lumpur offers similar options through Malaysia Airlines Cargo. Either hub works; the choice usually comes down to whichever transfer leg is easier to arrange on your travel dates.

    - heading: "CFIA's requirements: the simple side of this route"
      body: |
        Canada recognises only nine countries as rabies-free for pet import purposes: Australia, Fiji, Finland, Iceland, Ireland, Japan, New Zealand, Sweden and the UK. Brunei is not one of them, so a standard rabies vaccination certificate is required for any dog over three months old.

        That certificate needs specific details: the vaccine's trade name, its serial number, its expiry date, plus the dog's breed, sex, colour and weight. Get your Brunei vet to include all of this the first time. A certificate missing any of these fields can cause delays at the Canadian border.

        No titre test applies here. Brunei also sits outside CFIA's high-risk list for dog rabies, last checked 13 March 2025, so none of the extra steps that apply on this site's Brunei-to-USA route (CDC serology, a CDC-registered entry facility) carry over to Canada. There is no CFIA import permit requirement either, since a personal move of one or two pets does not count as a commercial import under CFIA's definition.

    - heading: "Cost and airline choices on the BWN to YVR corridor"
      body: |
        Expect roughly CAD 3,500 to CAD 6,500 for a medium dog once the Brunei transfer leg, crate, DAA paperwork and the Cathay Pacific cargo fee via Hong Kong are all counted. Cat moves usually land toward the lower end of that range.

        Cathay Pacific's Hong Kong to Vancouver cargo service has an established live animal handling station at YVR, which is worth knowing if your first choice carrier has capacity issues on your travel dates. If your final home is somewhere other than Vancouver, Air Canada Cargo handles the domestic connection, though it excludes brachycephalic breeds from its hold year-round regardless of season.

faqs:

    answer: "No. There is no live animal cargo service on Royal Brunei Airlines for private owners, so every pet moving from Brunei, to Canada or anywhere else long-haul, is first transferred by road or regional flight to Singapore or Kuala Lumpur before the international leg begins."
  - question: "Does my pet need a rabies titre test to enter Canada from Brunei?"
    answer: "No. Brunei is not on the CFIA's high-risk country list for dog rabies, last updated 13 March 2025, so no titre test applies. A standard rabies vaccination certificate, showing the vaccine's trade name, serial number and expiry date, is sufficient for dogs over three months old."
  - question: "Is there a quarantine period for pets entering Canada from Brunei?"
    answer: "No. Canada does not require routine quarantine for pets arriving with correct documentation, whatever the country of origin. Border checks are typically a document review and a visual inspection at the port of entry."
