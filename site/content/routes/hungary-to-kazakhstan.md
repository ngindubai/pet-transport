  h1: "Pet Transport from Hungary to Kazakhstan"
  overview: |
    Moving a pet from Budapest to Almaty or Astana takes more routing consideration than other Caucasus destinations. There is no direct cargo flight from Budapest to Kazakhstan, and the choice of routing matters: Turkish Airlines via Istanbul (IST) is the most common option, but Air Astana via Amsterdam (AMS) is a genuine alternative, and Lufthansa via Frankfurt (FRA) works well for certain schedules.

    Kazakhstan is a member of the Eurasian Economic Union (EAEU) and generally accepts the EU-format health certificate issued by Nébih, which simplifies Hungarian paperwork considerably. The key vaccination rule to note: Kazakhstan requires the rabies vaccine to have been administered at least 30 days before entry, not 21 days as in Armenia and Georgia. That detail catches people out when planning tight timelines.

    Total preparation time is 4-6 weeks, with the longer end accounting for the 30-day vaccination minimum and the additional time needed to confirm routing and live animal cargo acceptance on the specific segments.

  sections:
    - heading: "Routing options from Budapest: IST, AMS, or FRA"
      body: |
        Budapest does not have a direct cargo service to Kazakhstan. Three practical routings exist, each with different trade-offs.

        Turkish Airlines via Istanbul (IST) is the most commonly used option. BUD has multiple daily Turkish Airlines departures, and IST operates regular flights to both Almaty (ALA) and Astana (NQZ). The total BUD-IST-ALA journey runs roughly 8-10 hours. Turkish Airlines Cargo accepts live animals on this routing but applies temperature restrictions during summer.

        Air Astana via Amsterdam (AMS) is the alternative. Air Astana operates the AMS-ALA and AMS-NQZ routes and handles live animal cargo. The additional leg from BUD to AMS (typically Lufthansa or a codeshare) adds to the total journey but may offer better summer scheduling when the Istanbul temperature embargo makes Turkish Airlines less reliable. Note that Air Astana has no service from Budapest: the BUD-AMS segment is always on a European carrier first.

        Lufthansa via Frankfurt (FRA) is a third option. Lufthansa Cargo handles live animals through FRA, and the Frankfurt Animal Lounge provides temperature-controlled holding for connections. This routing is well-suited to summer moves.

    - heading: "EAEU membership and what it means for Hungarian pet certificates"
      body: |
        Kazakhstan is a founding member of the Eurasian Economic Union (EAEU), which includes Russia, Belarus, Kyrgyzstan, and Armenia. Within the EAEU, a unified veterinary certificate (Form 1 or Form 5N) is the standard document for live animal movements. However, for companion pet imports from third countries including EU member states, Kazakhstan's veterinary authorities generally accept the EU-format health certificate issued by Nébih.

        This is a practical accommodation rather than a formally ratified bilateral agreement. In most cases, a Nébih-endorsed EU health certificate moves through Almaty or Astana without issue. To remove any residual uncertainty, your IPATA agent or the person receiving the pet in Kazakhstan can confirm with the local veterinary authority before the certificate is issued.

        If the receiving party prefers an EAEU-format certificate, it is possible to have an EAEU Form prepared in addition to or instead of the EU form, but this requires a vet familiar with the EAEU requirements. Your agent can advise.

    - heading: "Kazakhstan's entry requirements at a glance"
      body: |
        The Ministry of Agriculture Veterinary Committee handles live animal imports in Kazakhstan. Inspection points are Almaty International Airport (ALA) and Nursultan Nazarbayev International Airport (NQZ) in Astana. Both airports handle regular international cargo including live animals.

        Entry conditions for companion dogs and cats from Hungary: an ISO-compliant microchip, a rabies vaccination administered at least 30 days before arrival and no more than 1 year before arrival, and a valid health certificate issued within 10 days of travel. No titre test is required and no quarantine applies for compliant arrivals.

        The 30-day rabies minimum is the main difference from other Caucasus destinations on this corridor. If your pet was vaccinated 25 days ago, you cannot travel to Kazakhstan until day 30. Plan the vaccination appointment accordingly, particularly if you are also timing the Nébih health certificate window.
