  sections:
    - heading: "Schiphol's live animal handling and what to expect on arrival"
      body: |
        Schiphol is one of Europe's busiest cargo hubs for live animals. The NVWA (Netherlands Food and Consumer Product Safety Authority) posts veterinary officers at Schiphol's Animal Hotel, which is part of the broader cargo facilities complex. On arrival, your pet is brought to the Animal Hotel, where the NVWA officer checks the EU health certificate, the titre test result, the vaccination records, and microchip identity.

        For a pet that arrives with all documents complete and the 90-day wait met, the inspection is a formality. Delays happen when documents are incomplete or the titer result is missing. Some owners send documents ahead of the flight so the officer can review them in advance. Your pet transport agent can arrange this.

    - heading: "What 'EU unlisted' means for Tbilisi pet owners"
      body: |
        Georgia's EU Association Agreement covers trade, visa liberalisation, and some regulatory alignment. It does not include an equivalence agreement for pet travel. From the EU's perspective, Georgia is treated the same as any other unlisted country for the purposes of Regulation 2026/131.

        That means the full process applies: microchip first, then vaccination, then a FAVN titre test at an EU-approved laboratory (blood drawn at least 30 days after vaccination), then a 90-day wait from the blood draw date. After that, the NFA-endorsed EU health certificate issued within 10 days of travel completes the documentation. There is no simpler path from Georgia to an EU member state.

faqs:
  - question: "Can my pet enter the Netherlands without a titre test if it was vaccinated in Georgia before we moved there?"
    answer: "No. Any dog or cat entering the EU from Georgia must have a passing FAVN titre test regardless of when or where the vaccination was given, because Georgia is an EU unlisted country. There are no exceptions. Source: Commission Delegated Regulation (EU) 2026/131."
  - question: "What if my dog fails the FAVN test?"
    answer: "A failed test means the immune response to the rabies vaccine was insufficient. The dog must be revaccinated and a new 90-day wait begins from the date of the subsequent successful titre test. This adds several months to your timeline. A failed result does not disqualify the dog permanently; it is retested after revaccination."
  - question: "Is there a direct Tbilisi-to-Amsterdam flight for pet cargo?"
    answer: "There is no non-stop TBS-to-AMS cargo service that regularly accepts live animals. The standard routing is via Istanbul (Turkish Airlines Cargo) or via a Western European hub (Air France/KLM Cargo). Your pet transport agent confirms the specific routing for your travel dates."
