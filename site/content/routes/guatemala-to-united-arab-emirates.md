content:
  h1: "Pet Transport from Guatemala to the United Arab Emirates"
  overview: |
    A titre test applies on this route because Guatemala sits outside MOCCAE's list of roughly 50 low-risk countries. That much is standard for an unlisted origin. What isn't standard, at least not if you've researched a UK-bound move first, is what happens after a passing result comes back: nothing. No fixed three-month wait, no extra clock. Once the blood draw clears 21 days post-vaccination and the result hits 0.5 IU/ml or above, the pet is ready to travel as soon as the flights and permit line up.

    Getting there still takes routing through Miami, where American Airlines Cargo or Avianca hands the animal to Emirates SkyCargo's near-daily nonstop run to Dubai. MAGA's own paperwork is comparatively light, the USD 6.25 export certificate being the standout example of how little Guatemala itself asks for.

  sections:
    - heading: "Why this titre test is faster than it sounds"
      body: |
        Owners who've looked at a UK or EU move assume every unlisted-country titre test comes with months of waiting built in. It doesn't here. MOCCAE only requires the blood sample to sit at least 21 days after a first vaccination, or to be drawn the same day as a valid booster. Once a passing result of 0.5 IU/ml or higher is in hand, there's no additional mandatory wait before the pet can fly.

        That single difference means this route can realistically move in 8 to 11 weeks, where the equivalent Guatemala-to-UK trip needs four and a half months or more purely because of the fixed post-titre wait.

    - heading: "The 90-day permit and the Miami connection"
      body: |
        MOCCAE's import permit is valid for 90 days from issue, comfortably longer than the UAE's own 30-day export equivalent for the reverse direction, which gives some breathing room if flight dates shift. Apply once the titre result is confirmed rather than before, since the permit clock is the one thing on this route you don't want ticking down before the vet work is finished.

        The flight itself runs through Miami on American or Avianca, then Emirates SkyCargo nonstop to Dubai, a sector of around 14 hours 45 minutes. Book through an IATA-certified pet shipping agent for the Dubai leg specifically; every pet enters the UAE as manifest cargo, and a standard cargo booking without that certification will not clear.
