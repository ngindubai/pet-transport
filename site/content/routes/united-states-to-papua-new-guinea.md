    NAQIA's own guidance for a US-origin pet is specific in a way this site couldn't establish for the same route from the UK: a rabies titre test is confirmed as required, not just likely. Add internal and external parasite treatment before departure, a step that doesn't appear on most routes this site covers, and the health-side checklist here is genuinely longer than average.

    None of that changes the single biggest fact about entering Papua New Guinea: every dog and cat spends a minimum 30 days at NAQIA's Kilakila quarantine station in Port Moresby, whatever the paperwork says. It isn't a penalty for a mistake. It's the standard process, and it applies as much to a US-origin pet with a flawless certificate as to any other.

    Getting there means a long-haul connection either way, through Tokyo Narita on ANA and Air Niugini, or through Brisbane on Qantas and Air Niugini. Eighteen to twenty-two weeks covers the permit's own lead time, the added health steps, and a flight schedule that only runs twice weekly on the Narita option.
