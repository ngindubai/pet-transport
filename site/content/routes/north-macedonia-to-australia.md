content:
  h1: "Pet Transport from North Macedonia to Australia"
  overview: |
    Serbia and Montenegro are on DAFF's Group 3 approved list. North Macedonia isn't. That single gap means a pet cannot travel straight from Skopje to any Australian city, whatever the vaccination record shows.

    The workaround is the same one used from India, Thailand and dozens of other non-approved countries: relocate the pet to an approved country, most practically Singapore, and let it live there for 180 consecutive days before applying to bring it into Australia. Only then does the 10-day Mickleham quarantine near Melbourne apply, the same as for any other arrival.

    None of this is exotic. Pet relocation agents handle the Singapore stepping-stone route routinely. It's the timeline that catches owners out, not the difficulty of any individual step.

  sections:
    - heading: "Why North Macedonia can't fly directly"
      body: |
        DAFF sorts countries of export into approved groups, numbered 1 through 3 for the least restrictive pathways, with higher-numbered categories for countries assessed as controlling rabies to different degrees. North Macedonia doesn't appear in any of them. Its Balkan neighbours Serbia and Montenegro do, both Group 3, which makes the gap harder to explain than a simple regional pattern.

        Practically, this means BICON will not process an import permit application listing North Macedonia as the country of export. The pet has to already be living somewhere DAFF does recognise.

    - heading: "Singapore as the staging point"
      body: |
        Singapore is Group 2, geographically workable from Skopje through a single-stop Turkish Airlines connection via Istanbul, and it has a functioning pet import system of its own. That last point matters: Singapore runs its own three-tier risk classification for incoming pets, separate from DAFF's system entirely. Depending on how AVS currently treats North Macedonia, your pet might clear Singapore customs without a hold, or might face a period at Singapore's own Animal Quarantine Centre first.

        Either way, the 180-day Australian residency clock only starts once your pet is genuinely settled in Singapore, not on the day it lands.

    - heading: "The Mickleham stage, once residency is done"
      body: |
        With 180 continuous days in Singapore behind you and a DAFF import permit in hand, the final leg looks like any other Singapore-to-Australia pet move. Singapore Airlines Cargo or Qantas Freight fly the sector daily, and your pet completes 10 days at Mickleham on arrival before release. There's no additional titre test required at this stage, since Singapore's Group 2 status already covers it.

    answer: "Serbia and Montenegro both sit on DAFF's Group 3 approved list. North Macedonia doesn't appear on any DAFF group list at all, despite being in the same region. This is a DAFF biosecurity classification, not a reflection of North Macedonia's own veterinary standards."
