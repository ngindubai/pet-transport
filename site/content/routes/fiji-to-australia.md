content:
  h1: "Pet Transport from Fiji to Australia"
  overview: |
    Group 2. That's the classification doing the work on this route. DAFF sorts every origin country by rabies risk, and Fiji, alongside Japan and Singapore, sits in the approved rabies-free bracket, the same one that strips out the titre test and 180-day wait that trip up owners moving pets from higher-risk countries.

    What's left is still real work: a microchip, a rabies vaccination, a BICON import permit, and a mandatory 10-day stay at the Mickleham facility outside Melbourne. Every pet entering Australia lands there, whatever city is the final home.

  sections:
    - heading: "What Group 2 actually removes from the process"
      body: |
        Rabies-controlled countries face a rabies antibody titre test and a fixed wait, sometimes 180 days, from the blood draw date. Fiji's Group 2 status takes that step off the table entirely. The pet still needs a current rabies vaccination and a microchip fitted first, but there's no laboratory result to wait on and no multi-month clock running before the flight can even be booked.

        That single difference cuts months off the realistic timeline compared with a Group 3 or Group 4 origin, and it's worth knowing before assuming every Australia-bound move takes the better part of a year.

    - heading: "The permit, the flight, and Mickleham"
      body: |
        A BICON import permit still has to be granted before travel, generally taking 4 to 8 weeks and running for 12 months once issued. Fiji Airways' direct Nadi services to Sydney, Melbourne and Brisbane make the flight side straightforward, though the cargo booking itself needs to land specifically in Melbourne.

        That's because Mickleham, the only DAFF-approved post entry quarantine facility in the country, sits just outside Melbourne. A 10-day stay there is mandatory for every pet entering Australia, Fiji's lighter classification included, before the animal can travel on to wherever you're actually settling.

    - heading: "What the whole move tends to cost"
      body: |
        Budget for four separate line items: the BAF export inspection in Fiji, the BICON permit, the Mickleham stay itself, and the cargo fare. Mickleham alone runs to a few thousand Australian dollars for the 10-day minimum, and that's paid directly to DAFF rather than to an agent.

        Owners moving a family cat sometimes underestimate the total. It isn't the vet bill that adds up on this route, it's the quarantine facility fee and the cargo booking together, so get both quoted before you commit to a travel date.
