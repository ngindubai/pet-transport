  h1: "Pet Transport from United Kingdom to Senegal"
  overview: |
    Search Dakar's airport code and some older guides still point you to Léopold Sédar Senghor. That airport stopped handling scheduled commercial flights in December 2017. Blaise Diagne International, around 43km outside the city, is where your pet actually lands today, and it's worth knowing before you brief a cargo agent or arrange ground transport.

    The bigger procedural point is Senegal's Import Authorisation. It's a separate government permit from the UK's own Export Health Certificate, issued by Senegal's Directorate of Veterinary Services, and it only holds for 3 months from the date it's granted. Apply too early against a shifting travel date and you risk it lapsing before your pet actually flies.

    No titre test applies to a pet leaving the UK for Senegal. What matters more here is timing two separate pieces of paperwork, from two separate governments, against one flight.

  sections:
    - heading: "Blaise Diagne, not the airport most old guides still mention"
      body: |
        Léopold Sédar Senghor Airport, near Yoff, served Dakar's commercial traffic for decades. It closed to scheduled passenger and cargo flights in December 2017, replaced by Blaise Diagne International Airport (DSS), built further out of the city near Diass. The old airport still operates, mostly for cargo, charter and military use, which is part of why some pet travel guides and forum posts continue to reference it without the update.

        For a pet flying as manifested cargo on a scheduled passenger carrier, whether it's Brussels Airlines, Air France, Royal Air Maroc or Turkish Airlines, Blaise Diagne is the airport that matters. Confirm any ground handling arrangements name DSS specifically.

    - heading: "The Import Authorisation: a 3-month clock"
      body: |
        Senegal requires its own Import Authorisation from the Directorate of Veterinary Services before a pet can enter, separate from the UK Export Health Certificate your vet issues. It's valid for 3 months from the date it's granted, which sounds generous until a flight gets rebooked or delayed and the permit quietly expires in the background.

        Apply once your travel window is genuinely fixed, not the moment you start planning. A permit granted too early is a permit you may need to redo.

    - heading: "Flying via Brussels, Paris, Casablanca or Istanbul"
      body: |
        Brussels Airlines runs Heathrow through Brussels to Dakar with a real cabin and cargo pet programme: small pets under 8kg in the cabin on the Brussels-Dakar leg, larger dogs in the hold. Capacity is the catch, at only 4 pets per flight, so this isn't a route to leave until the last minute.

        Air France, codesharing with Air Senegal on around 14 weekly Paris-Dakar flights, offers more flexibility if Brussels is booked out. Royal Air Maroc via Casablanca and Turkish Airlines via Istanbul are the fallback options for a longer transit when the first two don't line up with your dates.
