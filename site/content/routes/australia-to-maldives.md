  overview: |
    Going this direction is the easy one of the Australia-Maldives pair. A cat with a current rabies vaccination, the core feline vaccines and a health certificate signed within 10 days of arrival clears entry at Velana International without a titre test or a lengthy quarantine stay. A dog cannot come at all, under a Maldives Customs Service prohibition applied without exceptions.

    The reverse trip, Maldives back to Australia, is a completely different scale of process because DAFF treats the Maldives as a non-approved country. If you are researching this route as part of a two-way posting, read that page too before assuming symmetry.

  sections:
    - heading: "The dog answer for this direction"
      body: |
        No dog can enter the Maldives, regardless of how it is documented in Australia. This is a blanket Maldives Customs Service prohibition, not a titre-test-and-wait situation, and no Australian export paperwork changes that on the Maldives side.
    - heading: "What a cat needs leaving Australia"
      body: |
        An accredited vet completes an export health certificate, certified through the relevant state process, alongside a current rabies vaccination given at least 30 days before arrival and the standard feline vaccines. None of this requires DAFF's usual heavyweight import machinery, since that framework governs animals coming into Australia, not leaving it.
    - heading: "What is genuinely unclear on the Maldives side"
      body: |
        Whether an import permit applies to an accompanied pet cat is not consistently answered across the sources we could find. Treat it as an open item to confirm with your agent or vet rather than something settled either way.
    - heading: "Why the return journey is nothing like this one"
      body: |
        Australia classifies countries into approved groups for pet imports, and the Maldives sits outside all of them. A cat travelling from Male to Australia would typically need to spend at least 180 consecutive days resident in an approved country first, per DAFF's non-approved country policy, something that does not apply at all in this direction.
    - heading: "Getting to Male"
      body: |
        Singapore Airlines via Singapore is a natural fit for an Australian departure, and Qatar Airways via Doha or Emirates via Dubai are the other established options. All three need an IATA-compliant crate booked through a recognised agent.
