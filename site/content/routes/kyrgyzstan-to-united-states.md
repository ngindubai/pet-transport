  h1: "Pet Transport from Kyrgyzstan to the United States"
  overview: |
    Two very different rulebooks apply here, and which one governs your move depends entirely on the species. Dogs from Kyrgyzstan face the CDC's full high-risk protocol. Cats face almost nothing.

    The CDC classifies Kyrgyzstan as high-risk for dog rabies, a list it last updated in April 2026. Since the rule change on 1 August 2024, the system is binary: a dog is either from a low-risk country or a high-risk one, with no middle tier. A high-risk, foreign-vaccinated dog needs a titre test or a 28-day quarantine, a government-endorsed vaccination certificate, and arrival at one of a short list of US airports that actually have a CDC-registered animal care facility.

    Cats get none of it. APHIS has no federal health requirements for pet cats entering the United States, a genuine and often overlooked contrast that route pages for other Central Asian corridors rarely spell out clearly.
  sections:
    - heading: "The CDC's high-risk protocol, and why it dictates your flight routing"
      body: |
        Because Kyrgyzstan sits on the CDC's high-risk list, a foreign-vaccinated dog needs one of two things: a valid rabies serology titre (blood drawn at least 30 days after vaccination and at least 28 days before entry, tested at a CDC-approved lab) or a 28-day quarantine at a CDC-registered facility at the owner's expense.

        Either way, the dog must land at the specific US airport where its reserved facility sits, and it cannot take a domestic connecting flight until that facility's processing is done. That single rule narrows your options to airports with a facility on site: JFK, LAX, MIA, SFO, ATL, IAD, and PHL. Since Turkish Airlines via Istanbul is the only realistic long-haul carrier out of Bishkek, your effective choice is which of Turkish's US gateways among that list fits your schedule and your pet's final destination.

        Source: CDC, cdc.gov/importation/dogs, list of high-risk countries last verified 15 April 2026, and the list of CDC-registered animal care facilities.
    - heading: "Cats: the same route, a completely different process"
      body: |
        If your household is moving only a cat, most of the above does not apply. APHIS Veterinary Services has no federal animal health requirements for importing pet cats into the United States from any country, Kyrgyzstan included. There is no microchip mandate, no rabies vaccination requirement, no titre test, and no CDC-registered facility restriction.

        That does not mean skip the vet entirely. Airlines still require proof of good health for cargo acceptance, and individual US states occasionally have their own import rules worth checking for your specific destination. But the federal barrier that makes dog transport from a high-risk country demanding simply does not exist for cats.
