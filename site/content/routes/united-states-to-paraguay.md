  h1: "Pet Transport from the United States to Paraguay"
  overview: |
    SENACSA's entry process for Paraguay is lighter on paperwork than the reverse journey, since no titre test applies to a US-origin pet. What catches US owners out instead is a straight numbers-and-breed problem: a 5-pet cap per owner under the non-commercial category, and a short list of banned breeds refused entry regardless of how complete the paperwork is.

    On the export side, the process is the familiar USDA APHIS route. A USDA-accredited vet issues the health certificate, endorsed through APHIS eFile for roughly USD 38, and that certificate stands in for the CVI that SENACSA's rules describe. The real planning variable is routing: nothing flies nonstop from the US into Asuncion, so every booking runs through a connecting hub.

  sections:
    - heading: "The 5-pet cap and the breed list that ends a booking before it starts"
      body: |
        SENACSA allows up to five companion dogs and cats per owner under its non-commercial import category. A sixth animal moved in the same shipment is reclassified as a commercial import, which follows an entirely different, more involved process. A US family relocating with a full household of pets needs to count before assuming the standard route covers everyone.

        Breed matters more than most owners expect. Pit Bull-type dogs, American Staffordshire Terrier, American Bully, Cane Corso, Boerboel and several named mastiff breeds are refused entry outright. This is not a quarantine outcome or an extra inspection step; it is a straight refusal that no amount of correct paperwork changes. Settle this question against the current SENACSA list before anything else on this page gets booked.

    - heading: "What the USDA side actually involves"
      body: |
        A USDA-accredited vet completes the export health certificate and gets it endorsed electronically through APHIS eFile, a process that costs roughly USD 38 per certificate and rarely takes long once the appointment is booked. SENACSA's own published guidance does not set out a mandatory titre test for pets entering Paraguay from the US, which is the main reason this route runs to weeks rather than the months a rabies-titre country like the UK, going the other way, would need.

        Vaccination timing still sets the calendar. A first-time rabies vaccination needs a 21-day wait before travel, and dogs also need current distemper, parvovirus, hepatitis and leptospirosis cover, with cats needing panleukopenia, rhinotracheitis and calicivirus. Build the certificate appointment around whichever of those falls last.

    - heading: "Routing: Panama beats a two-hub alternative"
      body: |
        Copa Airlines connects a US gateway, Miami, Orlando, New York, Washington, Denver, Las Vegas or Los Angeles, through its own Tocumen hub in Panama City and onward nonstop to Asuncion, all on one airline's network. That single-carrier path avoids a crate handover between two different cargo teams.

        Avianca, through Bogota, and LATAM, through Sao Paulo, are both workable but add a genuine interline step. LATAM's dog cargo restriction into the US does not apply to this outbound direction, but airline cargo policy on this route has changed before with little notice, so confirm current terms directly rather than relying on a prior booking's experience.
