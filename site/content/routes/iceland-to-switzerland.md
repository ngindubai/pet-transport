content:
  h1: "Pet Transport from Iceland to Switzerland"
  overview: |
    A direct flight and no titre test make the federal side of this move genuinely light. Switzerland treats Iceland the same way it treats an EU member state for pet travel, so a microchip, a current rabies vaccination and a MAST-endorsed certificate cover the national paperwork, and Icelandair flies Keflavik to Zurich nonstop.

    What the federal rules do not cover is the canton you're actually moving to. Switzerland doesn't run breed restrictions at national level; individual cantons do, and they don't agree with each other. A dog that arrives with clean paperwork can still run into a local permit requirement the federal side never mentioned.

  sections:
    - heading: "Why the federal paperwork is straightforward"
      body: |
        Iceland sits inside the same Annex II grouping the EU uses for Norway, Liechtenstein and the Faroe Islands, territories treated as equivalent to an EU member state for pet movement. Switzerland, though not an EU member itself, mirrors this treatment through its own bilateral veterinary agreement. The result: no rabies titre test, no quarantine, just the standard microchip and 21-day vaccination wait, followed by a MAST-endorsed certificate.

        Compare this with the reverse direction, Switzerland to Iceland, where MAST's own Category 1 rules still require a titre test and a mandatory 14-day quarantine on arrival. The asymmetry runs the same way it does on every other EU-adjacent route covered for Iceland on this site: outbound is simple, inbound is not.

    - heading: "One flight, direct, but check the cargo booking early"
      body: |
        Icelandair's Keflavik-Zurich sector is a genuine nonstop, a small advantage over routes that need a connection through a third country. Since 1 November 2024, though, every pet on it moves as manifested cargo through Icelandair Cargo rather than as checked baggage, a separate booking process from a normal passenger fare. Confirm cargo space alongside your own travel plans rather than assuming a seat booking covers the pet as well.

    - heading: "Why the canton matters more than the country"
      body: |
        This is the detail that trips owners up. Switzerland has no federal list of banned dog breeds, unlike Germany's fixed ban on several bully-type breeds. Instead, each of the 26 cantons sets its own policy. Zurich, Geneva and Ticino list breeds such as the Pit Bull Terrier, American Staffordshire Terrier and Rottweiler as restricted, requiring an owner permit, a behavioural assessment and compulsory liability insurance before the dog can legally live there. Fribourg and Valais currently apply no such restriction at all.

        Dog registration with the cantonal veterinary office is compulsory everywhere in Switzerland, restricted breed or not. And regardless of canton, dogs with cropped ears or tails are generally barred from import outright, a rule that has no parallel on the Netherlands or Germany routes covered elsewhere on this site. Contact the destination canton's veterinary office before you commit to a specific breed or a specific move date.

    answer: "There is no federal breed ban. Individual cantons decide this themselves. Check the specific canton you're moving to."
