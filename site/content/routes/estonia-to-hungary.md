        LOT Polish Airlines flies Tallinn to Warsaw non-stop, with onward service into Budapest, keeping the whole journey within one airline's network and one cabin pet booking system. Lufthansa via Frankfurt is the alternative, drawing on Lufthansa's dense European schedule and its own cabin pet policy for small dogs and cats.

    - heading: "The paperwork: passport, AHC, and the April 2026 change"
      body: |
        The EU pet passport, issued by a VTA-authorised vet in Estonia, is valid for the pet's life provided the rabies vaccination inside it stays current. Since 22 April 2026, that passport is restricted to owners primarily resident in the EU, a rule that leaves the overwhelming majority of Estonian pet owners unaffected.

        Non-EU nationals resident in Estonia, for instance on a work visa, need a single-use AHC instead, issued within 10 days of travel and endorsed by the VTA. Whichever document applies, NEBIH, Hungary's National Food Chain Safety Office, checks it against the microchip on arrival, so confirm the chip number matches exactly before you fly.
