  sections:
    - heading: "Hungary's breed ban: what you need to know"
      body: |
        Hungary operates one of the stricter national breed bans in the EU. Under Government Decree 35/1997, the American Pit Bull Terrier and American Staffordshire Terrier are prohibited from entry and residence in Hungary. These are not just restricted breeds with conditions attached; they are banned outright.

        Several other breeds face special conditions rather than an outright ban. Rottweilers, Bull Terriers, and Staffordshire Bull Terriers can be kept in Hungary but with specific requirements around muzzling, lead use, and owner registration. The relevant authority is NEBIH (Nemzeti Elelmiszerlanc-biztonsagi Hivatal). Contact them directly if you are travelling with any of these breeds.

        For the vast majority of dogs, and for all cats, the breed rules are not a concern. The standard EU requirements apply.

    - heading: "Standard requirements: microchip, vaccination, and documentation"
      body: |
        Every dog, cat, or ferret entering Hungary from another EU country must have an ISO 11784/11785-compliant microchip, a current rabies vaccination, and either an EU pet passport or an AHC issued within 10 days of entry.

        The 21-day wait after a first-time rabies vaccination is mandatory. If your pet has a valid booster within its vaccination period, this wait does not apply again. If travel is approaching and your pet is due a booster, get it done as soon as possible to start the clock.

        Incomplete documentation at the Hungarian border can result in conditional quarantine at the owner's expense. This is avoidable with proper preparation.

    - heading: "Getting from Tallinn to Budapest"
      body: |
        Budapest Airport (BUD) is served from Tallinn (TLL) via airBaltic connections through Riga, and via Lufthansa through Frankfurt. Total journey time including connections is typically 4-6 hours.

        Small pets travel in the cabin on airBaltic and Lufthansa routes. Larger dogs travel in the cargo hold or as checked baggage. Note that Wizz Air, which operates between Budapest and various European airports, does not accept pets under any circumstances.

        For cargo transport of larger dogs, Lufthansa Cargo is well set up for this route. Confirm booking at least 4-6 weeks before travel.

faqs:
  - question: "Can I bring an American Staffordshire Terrier to Hungary from Estonia?"
    answer: "No. Hungary prohibits the American Pit Bull Terrier and American Staffordshire Terrier under Government Decree 35/1997. These breeds cannot legally enter Hungary regardless of origin."
  - question: "Is there quarantine when moving a dog from Estonia to Hungary?"
    answer: "No, for compliant pets. If your documentation is in order, there is no quarantine. However, if documents are missing or non-compliant at the border, Hungarian authorities can impose conditional quarantine. Get your paperwork right before you travel."
  - question: "Can I take my pet by road from Estonia to Hungary?"
    answer: "Yes. The drive is approximately 1,700km and takes around 16-18 hours via Latvia, Lithuania, Poland, and Slovakia. Your pet needs the same documentation as for air travel. The AHC (if used instead of the EU passport) must be issued within 10 days of crossing into Hungary."
  - question: "What is NEBIH and do I need to contact them before travelling?"
    answer: "NEBIH is Hungary's National Food Chain Safety Office, which oversees pet imports. For standard breeds with correct documentation, you do not need to contact them in advance. If your breed is on the restricted list, contact NEBIH before making any travel plans."
