    - heading: "Hong Kong EU-listed: no titre test and the AFCD certificate for France"
      body: |
        EU Regulation 576/2013 lists Hong Kong as a recognised territory for pet movement. Hong Kong pets entering France need only a valid ISO microchip, a current rabies vaccination (21-day wait after a primary course), and the AFCD-endorsed EU-format health certificate. No titre test, no 3-month wait. Source: food.ec.europa.eu, 2026.

        The AFCD (Agriculture, Fisheries and Conservation Department) is Hong Kong's official authority for animal export health certification. An AFCD-registered vet issues the EU-format certificate within 10 days of travel; AFCD endorses it before departure. The four-to-six-week process contrasts sharply with the five-month preparation required from non-listed countries such as mainland China.

    - heading: "Cathay Pacific and Air France direct to CDG and France's breed check"
      body: |
        Cathay Pacific Cargo operates direct from Hong Kong (HKG) to Paris CDG and accepts live animal cargo on this route. Air France Cargo also flies HKG-CDG direct. Both airlines have live animal handling facilities at CDG, where French customs carries out the arrival inspection for pets from third countries.

        France's breed legislation divides restricted breeds into two categories. Category 1 covers Pit Bull type dogs without official LOF or FCI breed registration; these cannot be imported. Category 2 covers Rottweiler and American Staffordshire Terrier with recognised pedigree; these can enter with ongoing restrictions. Most breeds common in Hong Kong are unaffected, but confirm breed status before starting the process if there is any uncertainty.
