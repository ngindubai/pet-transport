content:
  h1: "Pet Transport Estonia to Belgium: 2026 Guide"
  overview: "Estonia and Belgium are both EU member states, which makes this one of the simpler international pet moves. The EU pet travel framework covers the whole journey: your pet needs a microchip, a current rabies vaccination, and either an EU pet passport or an Animal Health Certificate. There is no quarantine and no titre test. The April 2026 update to EU passport rules means the passport is now restricted to EU residents, which covers most Estonian pet owners but should be checked if you are a non-EU national residing in Estonia. Tallinn Airport connects to Brussels via several carriers, and small pets can travel in-cabin on most full-service airlines."

  sections:
    - heading: "EU pet passport or AHC: which one do you need?"
      body: |
        For intra-EU pet travel, the EU pet passport is the simplest document. It is issued once by a VTA-authorised vet in Estonia and is valid for the life of the pet, as long as vaccinations are kept current. Present it at airline check-in and at any spot check in Belgium.

        From 22 April 2026, the EU updated its rules so that EU pet passports are only valid for owners who primarily reside in the EU. Estonian nationals and other EU residents living in Estonia are EU residents and can use the passport as normal.

        If you are a non-EU national living in Estonia (for example, on a work permit), you should use an AHC instead. The AHC is issued by a VTA-authorised Estonian vet, endorsed by the VTA, and is valid for one trip only. It must be issued within 10 days of your entry into Belgium. If you travel to Belgium regularly with your pet, you need a new AHC each time.

    - heading: "Getting from Tallinn to Brussels: airline options"
      body: |
        Tallinn Airport (TLL) has several carriers serving routes to Belgium. airBaltic connects TLL to its Riga hub (RIX), with onward connections to Brussels Airport (BRU). This is a practical option for both cabin and cargo travel, though it involves a connection.

        Direct options to Brussels are more limited from Tallinn. LOT Polish Airlines routes via Warsaw Chopin (WAW) with connections to Brussels. Lufthansa connects via Frankfurt (FRA). For a small dog or cat travelling in-cabin, these one-stop routings are manageable.

        Note that budget carriers operate from Tallinn to Brussels South Charleroi Airport (CRL), which is about 60km from Brussels city centre. Ryanair, for example, does not accept pets. If you are on a budget carrier, verify the pet policy carefully before booking.

faqs:
  - question: "Does my pet need any documents to travel within Belgium after arriving from Estonia?"
    answer: "No additional documents are needed for movement within Belgium once your pet has arrived with a valid EU pet passport or AHC and current vaccination. Belgium does not require domestic registration of dogs or cats at the federal level for visitors, though long-term residents may have local registration obligations. Check with your local Belgian municipality if you are relocating permanently."
  - question: "Can I bring my cat in-cabin from Tallinn to Brussels on airBaltic?"
    answer: "airBaltic allows cats and small dogs in the cabin on its EU routes, provided the combined weight of the pet and carrier is within the airline's limit (typically 8 kg total). The pet fee applies per leg. For the TLL-RIX-BRU routing, you would pay the cabin pet fee on both legs and must ensure the carrier fits under the seat in front of you. Book the cabin pet space at the time of flight booking, not separately."
  - question: "My EU pet passport was issued in 2019. Is it still valid for Belgium in 2026?"
    answer: "EU pet passports do not expire. The passport itself is valid for the pet's life. What must be current is the rabies vaccination recorded inside it. Check that the most recent rabies vaccination entry is within its valid period on the date of travel. If the vaccination has lapsed, your Estonian vet can administer a booster, add the entry to the existing passport, and you can travel once 21 days have passed from a primary vaccination (or immediately if it is a booster within the prior vaccine's valid period)."
