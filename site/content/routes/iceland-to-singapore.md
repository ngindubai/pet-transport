content:
  h1: "Pet Transport from Iceland to Singapore"
  overview: |
    Singapore's Animal & Veterinary Service does not treat Iceland as a rabies-free country for import purposes, even though Iceland genuinely is one. That single classification decision, Schedule II rather than Schedule I, adds a rabies titre test and a 90-day wait to this route, plus a possible 10-day home quarantine on arrival. It is a longer process than most owners moving from an EEA country expect.

    There's also no direct flight. Every pet moving from Keflavik to Changi transits at least one European hub, and since Icelandair stopped carrying pets as checked baggage in late 2024, that transfer has to be arranged as cargo from the first leg onward.

  sections:
    - heading: "Why AVS treats Iceland differently from the UK"
      body: |
        Singapore's AVS splits import countries into two working tiers for dogs and cats. Schedule I, negligible risk, currently covers only Australia, New Zealand, the Republic of Ireland and the UK. Those countries' pets skip the rabies vaccination and titre test altogether. Schedule II, controlled risk, covers a much longer list, including Germany, France, the USA, Canada and, notably, Iceland itself.

        Being genuinely rabies-free does not move Iceland into Schedule I. The distinction AVS draws is about its formal country-risk assessment process, not raw epidemiology, so an Icelandic pet needs the same vaccination and titre test pathway as a German or American one: blood drawn at least 28 days after vaccination, then a 90-day wait from that blood draw before import, with a result of at least 0.5 IU/ml.

    - heading: "The quarantine question depends on your own move date"
      body: |
        AVS does not apply a blanket quarantine to Schedule II arrivals. If your pet has lived at your own address for more than 6 months and arrives in Singapore within 5 days of you, you can apply for a waiver and skip quarantine entirely. Miss either condition and a minimum 10-day home quarantine applies, with the pet wearing an AVS-issued smart collar tag that tracks compliance, charged at SGD 29 a day.

        Apply for the waiver, or the home quarantine slot if you do not qualify, at least 4 weeks before your pet's scheduled arrival. This has to be resolved before AVS issues the import licence itself, so it sits early in the timeline, not as a last step.

    - heading: "Routing a pet from an island with no Asia-Pacific flights"
      body: |
        Keflavik has no scheduled service to Singapore, direct or otherwise. The realistic path is Keflavik to Frankfurt on Lufthansa, using Lufthansa's own Frankfurt Animal Lounge for the transfer, then a long-haul cargo leg from Frankfurt into Singapore Changi on Lufthansa Cargo or Singapore Airlines Cargo.

        Since Icelandair no longer accepts pets as checked baggage on any route, this whole journey needs to be booked as manifested cargo from the Iceland leg onward, not as excess baggage with a cargo connection tacked on later. A pet relocation agent familiar with multi-leg European-to-Asia routing is worth using here, given the number of separate bookings and hand-offs involved.
