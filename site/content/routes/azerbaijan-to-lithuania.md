  sections:
    - heading: "Why the 90-day wait applies and what it means for planning"
      body: |
        Lithuania, as an EU member state, applies the EU pet travel framework for animals arriving from unlisted third countries. Azerbaijan is classified as an EU unlisted country, which means your pet must complete the FAVN titre test process before entering Lithuania.

        The sequence is: chip implanted first, then primary rabies vaccination, then at least 30 days later a blood draw for the FAVN titre test at an EU-approved laboratory. If the result is 0.5 IU/ml or above, the 90-day wait begins from the blood draw date. Travel to Lithuania cannot happen until at least day 91.

        The 90-day wait is calculated from when the blood was drawn, not when the results came back. Starting as early as possible after confirming you are moving is the most important step on this route.

    - heading: "What happens at Vilnius Airport"
      body: |
        Vilnius Airport (VNO) has border veterinary inspection for live animal cargo arriving from non-EU origins. The Valstybine maisto ir veterinarijos tarnyba (VMVT) oversees these controls. Border vets at VNO will check: the State Veterinary Service-endorsed health certificate (issued within 10 days of travel), the FAVN titre test result, the vaccination record, and the microchip.

        The chip must be scannable and the number must match every document precisely. If the titre test shows a successful result but the 90-day wait is incomplete, entry is refused. Lithuanian border vets apply EU rules strictly.

    - heading: "Routing from Baku to Vilnius"
      body: |
        There is no direct cargo service from Heydar Aliyev International Airport (GYD) in Baku to Vilnius (VNO). Vilnius is a smaller regional airport with limited direct international cargo connections. The routing requires two or more legs.

        The most practical option uses Turkish Airlines Cargo from GYD to Istanbul Ataturk (IST), then connects through Warsaw (WAW), from where LOT Polish Airlines serves Vilnius directly. Alternatively, connecting through Frankfurt or Amsterdam and then via a connecting carrier to VNO is viable. The total journey is typically 8-14 hours.

        Book during the 90-day wait and confirm live animal cargo acceptance on every segment in writing. A specialist transport agent experienced in Caucasus-to-Baltic routing is recommended.
