  overview: |
    A Brucella canis blood test is not something most UK owners have heard of, but any intact dog flying into Piarco needs one alongside the standard rabies titre. Trinidad and Tobago has run without routine pet quarantine since April 2013, and the Animal Production and Health Division, part of the Ministry of Agriculture, Land and Fisheries, will wave a compliant pet straight through. Get one document wrong, though, and the same pet can be held at a government facility at the owner's cost.

    The process centres on an import permit rather than a UK export permit, since Great Britain does not require one to leave. British Airways is the only nonstop carrier, and pet cargo runs through IAG Cargo rather than the ordinary passenger booking.

  sections:
    - heading: "The import permit and titre test sequence"
      body: |
        APHD asks for the import permit application at least one month ahead of travel, though 1.5 months is a more realistic buffer given how many applications the division processes. Running alongside that is the rabies titre test itself: FAVN or RFFIT, drawn at least one month after vaccination and sent to an approved laboratory. USDA APHIS documents this same laboratory and one-month timing rule for pets travelling from the US, and APHD applies an equivalent standard to UK-origin animals.

        Order matters throughout. The microchip has to go in before the vaccination, and the vaccination has to happen before the titre blood draw. Get the sequence wrong and the test result is void, however good the number comes back.

    - heading: "Brucella canis, parasite treatment and quarantine at the border"
      body: |
        Intact dogs face one requirement most cat owners and owners of neutered dogs never encounter: a negative Brucella canis test, done at the same lab visit as the rabies titre where possible. All cats and dogs, regardless of reproductive status, need treatment for internal and external parasites shortly before departure.

        Trinidad and Tobago has not run a routine quarantine system since April 2013, provided the paperwork is complete and current on arrival. Pets that turn up with a missing document, an expired health certificate, or visible signs of illness can be held at a government veterinary facility, with the holding cost charged to the owner. Notify the Veterinary Officer at Piarco or the Tobago port of entry at least two working days ahead, a step that is separate from the import permit and easy to overlook.
