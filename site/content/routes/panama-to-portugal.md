  overview: "Panama to Portugal is a long-preparation route with a well-defined process. Panama is not on the EU listed country register, so the FAVN titre test pathway applies: blood drawn at least 30 days after the rabies vaccination, a result of 0.5 IU/ml or above at an EU-approved laboratory, and a mandatory 90-day wait from the blood draw date before entering Portugal. Total preparation time is 20-26 weeks. The PTY-to-Lisbon corridor works efficiently via Madrid, which Copa Airlines connects to Iberia's or TAP Air Portugal's short MAD-LIS service.   sections:
    - heading: "Portugal's requirements for pets from Panama"
      body: |
        Portugal's DGAV (Direccao-Geral de Alimentacao e Veterinaria, dgav.pt) administers pet imports under Commission Delegated Regulation (EU) 2026/131. For pets from unlisted third countries such as Panama, the requirements are: ISO 11784/11785 microchip implanted before the rabies vaccination; rabies vaccination with 21 days elapsed since the primary dose; FAVN titre test at an EU-approved laboratory with a result of 0.5 IU/ml or above, blood drawn at least 30 days after vaccination; 90-day wait from the blood draw date; and an EU Annex IV non-commercial health certificate issued within 10 days of travel, endorsed by Panama's MIDA-DINASA authority.

        No quarantine. No import permit. Border inspection at Lisbon Humberto Delgado (LIS) or Porto Francisco Sa Carneiro (OPO) is standard for compliant arrivals. Inspectors check the health certificate, microchip, titre test result (with blood draw date visible), and vaccination record.

    - heading: "FAVN titre test coordination from Panama"
      body: |
        The FAVN test requires an EU-approved laboratory. Most of these are in Europe or North America. From Panama, your licensed veterinarian draws the blood, stores it in the correct preservative medium (typically sodium azide), and ships it to the laboratory by international biomedical courier.

        ANSES in France (lda.anses.fr) and Kansas State University Rabies Laboratory in the United States are commonly used EU-approved laboratories for Central American samples. Ask the laboratory about their sample acceptance requirements and typical processing time before arranging the blood draw. Allow two to four weeks from the blood draw for shipping and processing.

        The 90-day mandatory wait starts from the blood draw date, not from the date the result arrives. If the result comes back positive on day 14, the wait still runs to day 90 from the draw. This wait cannot be shortened under any circumstances.

    - heading: "Routing from Panama City to Lisbon"
      body: |
        Tocumen International Airport (PTY) in Panama City is Copa Airlines' main hub, which gives the route good connectivity to Europe. The most practical routing is PTY to Madrid (MAD) via Copa's transatlantic service or partner connections, then MAD to Lisbon (LIS), which is a one-hour cargo connection served by Iberia and TAP Air Portugal.

        Alternative routings go via Miami (MIA) to a European hub, then to Lisbon. From Paris CDG, TAP Air Portugal connects to LIS directly. Copa has partnership arrangements with Lufthansa Group, which opens further European hub options. Confirm live animal cargo acceptance on every segment before booking, and use a freight forwarder experienced in Central American cargo departures.

    - heading: "MIDA-DINASA export documentation from Panama"
      body: |
        Panama's official authority for animal health export certificates is MIDA-DINASA (Ministerio de Desarrollo Agropecuario, Direccion Nacional de Sanidad Animal, mida.gob.pa). A licensed veterinarian in Panama prepares the EU Annex IV format health certificate, referencing the titre test result (with laboratory name, blood draw date, and result in IU/ml), the vaccination record, and the microchip number.

        MIDA-DINASA endorses the document after review. Allow three to five business days for this step. The certificate must be issued within 10 days of travel. Carry the original with your pet's travel documents at all times.

    answer: "No. Portugal does not have an Echinococcus treatment requirement for dogs arriving from Central America. This is different from Norway and Finland, which both require tapeworm treatment. For a direct Panama-to-Portugal route, no tapeworm treatment is needed."
  - question: "Can I enter Portugal through Porto instead of Lisbon?"
    answer: "Yes. Porto Francisco Sa Carneiro (OPO) has a DGAV border inspection post and accepts pet arrivals from non-EU countries. If your cargo routing connects through Spain, OPO may be a viable alternative to LIS. Confirm with DGAV that the specific flight's arrival at OPO will be inspected."
  - question: "How long does the cargo journey from Panama City to Lisbon take?"
    answer: "Expect 18-30 hours of transit time from PTY to LIS, depending on the routing via your European hub. PTY to MAD via Copa connections is relatively efficient, and the MAD-LIS leg adds only about one hour. Confirm the full itinerary with your freight forwarder before booking."
  - question: "Is the FAVN test result valid for future EU entries after the initial move to Portugal?"
    answer: "A positive FAVN titre test result remains valid as long as the pet's rabies vaccinations are kept current without a gap. If the vaccination lapses, the pet may need to repeat the titre test process before re-entering the EU from an unlisted country. Verify with DGAV for the specific circumstances of any future re-entry."
