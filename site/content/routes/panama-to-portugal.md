      body: |
        Portugal follows the same EU rulebook as every member state: assessed-equivalence countries skip the titre test, unassessed ones do not. Panama sits in the unassessed group, so the sequence runs microchip before vaccination, a 30-day wait before the blood draw, a FAVN or ELISA result of at least 0.5 IU/ml, then 90 days from that draw date.

        None of these steps shorten for a fast result or an urgent travel date. The 90 days is fixed EU-wide.

        Capacity on the São Paulo-Lisbon corridor is not unlimited, though, so confirm cargo space on both legs together rather than assuming a same-week connection will be available closer to your travel date.
