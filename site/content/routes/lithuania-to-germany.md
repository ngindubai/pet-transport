  sections:
    - heading: "The VMVT and your Lithuanian pet health certificate"
      body: |
        The VMVT (Valstybes maisto ir veterinarijos tarnyba, State Food and Veterinary Service of Lithuania) is the government authority that oversees veterinary health certification in Lithuania. When a Lithuanian vet issues an AHC for your pet's move to Germany, the VMVT endorsement stamp on the certificate confirms the vet is officially authorised.

        For EU residents using an EU pet passport rather than an AHC, the VMVT involvement is already embedded in the passport issuance process: only officially authorised vets registered with the VMVT can issue or update EU passports in Lithuania. If your passport was issued by a Vilnius or Kaunas vet, it will already carry this status.

        The practical difference between the passport and the AHC: the passport is valid for life (with current vaccinations) and covers any number of EU border crossings. The AHC is single-use and valid for one entry only, issued within 10 days of travel.

    - heading: "Getting from Vilnius to Germany: airline options and routing"
      body: |
        Vilnius Airport (VNO) does not have the same range of direct Western European services as Riga (RIX) with airBaltic. The most practical options for Lithuania-to-Germany pet moves are LOT Polish Airlines via Warsaw (WAW), which connects to Frankfurt, Munich, Hamburg, and Berlin; Lufthansa via Frankfurt connections; or airBaltic via Riga.

        LOT is a good choice for cabin travel: small pets (carrier plus pet under 8 kg) are allowed in cabin, and Warsaw Chopin Airport (WAW) handles live animal cargo with reasonable facilities. The total journey time via Warsaw to Frankfurt is around 4-5 hours.

        For cargo, Frankfurt (FRA) is the preferred German arrival airport because of Lufthansa's dedicated Animal Lounge, which is widely regarded as one of Europe's best live animal facilities. Arrange cargo bookings at least 3-4 weeks in advance and confirm live animal acceptance on every leg.

faqs:
  - question: "What is the VMVT and why does it appear on Lithuanian pet certificates?"
    answer: "The VMVT (Valstybes maisto ir veterinarijos tarnyba) is Lithuania's State Food and Veterinary Service, the government authority responsible for official veterinary certification. Its endorsement on an AHC or pet passport confirms the issuing vet is officially authorised. German border vets look for this authority mark when checking Lithuanian pet documentation."
  - question: "Can I fly direct from Vilnius to Frankfurt with my pet?"
    answer: "There is no consistent direct Vilnius (VNO) to Frankfurt (FRA) non-stop service on airlines that accept pets. The usual route is via Warsaw (WAW) with LOT or via Riga (RIX) with airBaltic, both of which allow small pets in cabin. Confirm the pet policy and book the cabin space at time of ticket purchase."
  - question: "Does Germany impose a quarantine on pets from Lithuania?"
    answer: "No. Germany does not quarantine pets arriving from other EU member states with valid documentation. A current EU pet passport or AHC, a microchip, and a current rabies vaccination is all that is required."
  - question: "Is Ryanair an option for taking my cat from Vilnius to Germany?"
    answer: "No. Ryanair does not accept pets in cabin or as cargo on the vast majority of its routes, including those out of Vilnius. Even if a Ryanair route connects Vilnius to a German airport, you cannot bring your cat. Use LOT, Lufthansa, or airBaltic instead."
