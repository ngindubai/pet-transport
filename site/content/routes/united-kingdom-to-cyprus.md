  overview: "Cyprus is an EU member state and the UK holds listed third country status, so UK pets do not need a titre test or routine quarantine for entry with correct documentation. Two requirements set Cyprus apart from most other EU destinations: dogs must receive tick treatment within 48 hours before arrival, and this must be recorded on the AHC. The seasonal heat challenge is real: Cyprus has some of the hottest summer temperatures in the EU and airline heat embargoes limit cargo bookings from May through September.   sections:
    - heading: "The tick treatment requirement for dogs"
      body: |
        Unlike most EU countries, Cyprus requires that dogs receive treatment against ticks from a veterinarian within 48 hours before arrival. The tick treatment must be documented and recorded in the AHC by the treating vet.

        This requirement exists because Cyprus is a rabies-free island and the Department of Veterinary Services takes a cautious approach to disease vectors. The treatment itself is straightforward: a registered tick treatment product applied by a vet, with the product name, dose, and time of administration recorded on the AHC.

        Book the tick treatment appointment in the 48-hour window before your arrival time in Cyprus. If your flight lands at 14:00 on a Thursday, the treatment must be administered after 14:00 on the Tuesday at the earliest. Keep it close to the 24-hour mark to give yourself a comfortable buffer without cutting the window too fine.

    - heading: "Entry through Larnaca or Paphos"
      body: |
        Cyprus has two main international airports: Larnaca International Airport (LCA) in the south-east and Paphos International Airport (PFO) in the south-west. Both handle international pet cargo, though Larnaca is the larger facility with more frequent connections from the UK.

        On arrival, a government vet from the Department of Veterinary Services inspects the documentation. They verify the microchip number against the AHC, check vaccination dates, and confirm the tick treatment record. For dogs, the timing of the tick treatment is checked carefully, so make sure the recorded time is within the 48-hour window.

        The Department of Veterinary Services can provide guidance on current entry procedures. Contact details are at moa.gov.cy/dvs.

faqs:
  - question: "Do I need a titre test to take my pet from the UK to Cyprus?"
    answer: "No. The UK holds listed third country status for EU pet travel, which means no titre test is required. Your pet needs a current ISO microchip, a valid rabies vaccination with the primary course completed at least 21 days before travel, and an AHC from an APHA-authorised official vet issued within 10 days of travel."
  - question: "What is the tick treatment requirement for dogs going to Cyprus?"
    answer: "Dogs must receive treatment against ticks from a veterinarian within 48 hours before arriving in Cyprus. The treatment product, dose, and time of administration must be recorded in the AHC. This applies to dogs only. Cats are not subject to this specific requirement."
  - question: "Can I fly my pet directly to Paphos rather than Larnaca?"
    answer: "Yes, both Larnaca (LCA) and Paphos (PFO) receive international pet cargo. Larnaca has more frequent direct connections from the UK. Check with your airline whether they route cargo to Larnaca or Paphos, as this may affect logistics on arrival."
  - question: "My dog is a brachycephalic breed. Can I still move them to Cyprus?"
    answer: "Brachycephalic breeds (French Bulldogs, Pugs, English Bulldogs and similar) face additional restrictions from airlines on cargo routes to Cyprus, particularly in summer when temperatures are very high. Contact your chosen airline's cargo desk directly for current breed policies before booking. Moving outside peak summer (October to April) improves your options."
  - question: "How long does the process take from the UK to Cyprus?"
    answer: "If your pet's microchip and rabies vaccination are already current, the process takes 2 to 4 weeks. The AHC must be issued within 10 days of travel and the tick treatment within 48 hours of arrival.
