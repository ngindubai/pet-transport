  sections:
    - heading: "Chile's listed status: what it means in practice"
      body: |
        Chile is classified as a Part 2 listed country by the UK's APHA. This means that pets from Chile are treated similarly to pets from low-risk listed countries: no titre test, no mandatory quarantine (for compliant pets), and a streamlined documentation process compared to non-listed countries.

        The key implication is time: without the titre test and 90-day wait, a move from Chile to the UK can be completed in 4 to 8 weeks rather than 7 months. For owners who are relocating on short notice, Chile's listed status is a genuine advantage.

        The vaccination requirements remain: current rabies vaccination, microchip implanted before the first rabies vaccine, and a SAG-endorsed health certificate. These are the same quality of documentation expected from listed countries.

    - heading: "The tapeworm treatment timing challenge"
      body: |
        Dogs travelling from Chile to the UK must receive praziquantel tapeworm treatment from a vet between 24 and 120 hours before arriving in the UK. For a flight from Santiago that involves a transit, the timing calculation must account for total travel time, not just the final leg.

        For example: if your pet departs Santiago on a Tuesday evening and arrives at Heathrow on Wednesday afternoon after a connection via Madrid, the UK arrival time is Wednesday afternoon. The tapeworm treatment window is Wednesday afternoon minus 120 hours (Friday morning of the previous week, at the earliest) to Wednesday afternoon minus 24 hours (Tuesday afternoon). A Tuesday morning treatment in Santiago would fall within the window for a Wednesday Heathrow arrival, but a Monday treatment would be outside the 120-hour limit.

        Plan the tapeworm vet appointment carefully around your full itinerary, including connections. A specialist pet transport agent can help you calculate the exact window for your specific routing.
