  h1: "Pet Transport from Madagascar to the United States"
  overview: |
    A dog leaving Madagascar for the United States faces a federal process built specifically for countries the CDC rates high-risk for rabies. A cat leaving Madagascar faces almost nothing: no federal health certificate, no titre test, no facility reservation. That gap between the two is bigger on this route than most owners expect.

    Madagascar has sat on the CDC's high-risk list for years, most recently confirmed on the list updated 15 April 2026. For a dog, that triggers the foreign-vaccinated pathway: a government-endorsed vaccination and microchip certificate, a reservation at a CDC-registered facility, and either a passing titre result or 28 days of quarantine on arrival.

    There's no direct flight either. Routing through Nairobi on Kenya Airways or Addis Ababa on Ethiopian Airlines gets a pet to Washington Dulles or JFK, both CDC-registered facility airports, on a single itinerary.

  sections:
    - heading: "Why dogs and cats from Madagascar are treated so differently"
      body: |
        The CDC's dog rules and the rules for cats come from different legal authorities, and it shows. Dogs fall under a CDC importation regulation built around rabies risk by country. Cats fall under USDA APHIS, which currently has no federal animal health requirement for imported pet cats at all.

        For a dog leaving Madagascar, that means the full high-risk pathway applies: a Certification of Foreign Rabies Vaccination and Microchip form completed and endorsed in Madagascar, a completed CDC Dog Import Form, and a reservation at a CDC-registered animal care facility booked before the flight. A cat needs a microchip and to appear healthy on arrival. That's essentially it at the federal level, though individual US states can add their own rules, so check the destination state's agriculture department too.
    - heading: "The facility-airport restriction and what it means for routing"
      body: |
        A dog without a valid titre result must land at one of six US airports: JFK, Washington Dulles, Atlanta, Miami, Los Angeles or San Francisco, the only ones with a CDC-registered animal care facility. There's no domestic connection afterward until the facility has released the dog, so the arrival city is effectively fixed by this rule, not by where the owner lives.

        From Antananarivo, Kenya Airways via Nairobi lands at JFK, and Ethiopian Airlines via Addis Ababa lands at Washington Dulles. Both are workable single-ticket routings. Confirm the CDC facility reservation before finalising either booking; facilities have limited capacity and don't take walk-ins.
