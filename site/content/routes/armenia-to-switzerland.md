  sections:
    - heading: "Why FSVO permission comes first on this route"
      body: |
        Switzerland classifies countries for pet import based on their rabies risk status. Armenia is classified as a country with present rabies risk. For pets from such countries, the FSVO requires advance written authorisation before the animal enters Switzerland. This is not a formality that can be handled on the day of travel.

        Apply to the FSVO at blv.admin.ch well before your planned travel date. The application covers the specific animal (microchip number), the planned entry date, and the intended entry point into Switzerland. Allow at least 3 weeks for processing. Keep the permission letter with your travel documents: it is checked at the Swiss border alongside the health certificate, titre test records, and vaccination history.

        Your IPATA-registered agent can prepare the FSVO application on your behalf, which simplifies the process and reduces the risk of errors that delay approval.

    - heading: "The titre test from Yerevan: what to expect"
      body: |
        The titre test blood draw happens in Yerevan, but the analysis is done at a laboratory approved under Swiss or EU rules. Most approved labs are in Western Europe or North America.

        Your Armenian vet draws the blood, stores it correctly in a preservative medium, and ships it via international courier to the approved lab. Results typically arrive 2-4 weeks after the lab receives the sample. The blood draw must happen at least 30 days after the rabies vaccination.

        Once you have a passing result (0.5 IU/ml or above), the 3-month wait begins from the date of the blood draw, not the date of the results. If results arrive on day 20 after the blood draw, you still wait until day 90 from the draw date.

        Confirm with your vet or agent which approved laboratory will analyse the sample and ensure they have experience with international shipments from Armenia.
