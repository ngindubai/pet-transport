  sections:
    - heading: "Why the 90-day wait applies and what it means for planning"
      body: |
        Latvia, as an EU member state, applies the EU pet travel framework for animals arriving from unlisted third countries. Kazakhstan is classified as an EU unlisted country, which means your pet must complete the FAVN titre test process before entering Latvia.

        The sequence is: chip implanted first, then primary rabies vaccination, then at least 30 days later a blood draw for the FAVN titre test at an EU-approved laboratory. If the result is 0.5 IU/ml or above, the 90-day wait begins from the blood draw date. Travel to Latvia cannot happen until at least day 91.

        The 90-day wait is calculated from when the blood was drawn, not when the results came back. Starting as early as possible after confirming you are moving is the most important step on this route.

    - heading: "What happens at Riga International Airport"
      body: |
        Riga International Airport (RIX) has border veterinary inspection for live animal cargo arriving from non-EU origins. The Partikas un veterinarais dienests (PVD) oversees these controls. Border vets at RIX will check: the Kazakhstani health certificate (issued within 10 days of travel), the FAVN titre test result, the vaccination record, and the microchip.

        The chip must be scannable and the number must match every document precisely. If the titre test shows a successful result but the 90-day wait is incomplete, entry is refused. Latvian border vets apply EU rules strictly.

    - heading: "Routing from Kazakhstan to Riga"
      body: |
        There is no direct cargo service from Kazakhstan to Riga (RIX). The most practical routing uses Air Astana, Kazakhstan's national carrier, for the first leg from Almaty (ALA) or Astana (NQZ) to Frankfurt (FRA) or Amsterdam (AMS). From FRA or AMS, airBaltic connects directly to Riga, making this a well-coordinated two-leg journey to Latvia's hub airport.

        An alternative routing goes via Istanbul Ataturk (IST) with Turkish Airlines Cargo, then IST to RIX. Turkish Airlines operates IST to RIX directly. Book during the 90-day wait and confirm live animal acceptance on every segment in writing. A specialist transport agent experienced in Central Asia-to-Baltic routes is recommended.
