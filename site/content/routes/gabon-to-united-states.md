    A dog leaving Libreville without a passing rabies titre has exactly six US airports it's allowed to land at, and only two of them, Washington Dulles and New York JFK, sit within reach of Gabon's limited long-haul network. Gabon is on the CDC's high-risk list for dog rabies, the same designation this site has already flagged on several African routes, and it locks in the full foreign-vaccinated pathway.

    The timeline runs on the titre test more than anything else. Blood has to be drawn at least 30 days after vaccination, with a result landing at least 28 days before the flight, tested at a CDC-approved lab, since Gabon has no confirmed laboratory of its own. Add in export paperwork that has to be produced in French, and 14 to 18 weeks is a realistic planning window rather than a worst case.

  sections:
    - heading: "The high-risk pathway, exactly as it applies to Gabon"
      body: |
        Because Gabon sits on the CDC's high-risk list, a dog can't simply fly into any US city. The chip has to go in before the vaccine, or the vaccine doesn't count. The titre test, drawn at least 30 days after vaccination and resulted at least 28 days before arrival, has to come from a CDC-approved laboratory. And the dog has to land at one of six specific airports with a CDC-registered animal care facility, with the reservation booked before departure.

        Miss the titre and the dog still gets in, but only after a 28-day quarantine at that same facility, paid for by the owner. Cats sidestep almost all of this: no CDC rabies vaccination requirement, no titre, no facility booking.

That triggers the full foreign-vaccinated-dog pathway: microchip before vaccination, a titre test from a CDC-approved laboratory, and arrival at a US airport with a CDC-registered animal care facility."
  - question: "What happens if my dog doesn't have a valid rabies titre result?"
    answer: "The dog can still enter the US, but it has to complete a 28-day quarantine at a CDC-registered animal care facility on arrival, at the owner's cost.   - question: "Is there a direct flight from Gabon to the United States?"
    answer: "No. No airline flies Libreville to any US city nonstop. The two realistic routings connect via Addis Ababa on Ethiopian Airlines into Washington Dulles, or via Casablanca on Royal Air Maroc into New York JFK, both CDC-registered facility airports."
  - question: "Why does Gabon's paperwork need to be in French?"
    answer: "Gabon's government-endorsed export documentation, including the vet certification the CDC ultimately relies on, is issued in French. If the underlying veterinary records start out in English, translation has to happen before the export vet appointment, adding a step most owners moving from an English-speaking country don't plan for."
  - question: "Do cats need the same CDC paperwork as dogs from Gabon?"
    answer: "No. Cats face no CDC rabies vaccination or titre test requirement at all, whatever their country of origin. Gabon's own export paperwork still applies to cats, and airlines generally still want a standard veterinary health certificate, but the federal US side is far lighter for cats than for dogs."
