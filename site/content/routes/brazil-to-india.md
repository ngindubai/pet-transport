  h1: "Pet Transport Brazil to India: MAPA Certificate, AQCS Import and the Long-Haul Route"
  overview: |
    Moving a pet from Brazil to India requires prior import authorisation from AQCS (Animal Quarantine and Certification Service) under India's DAHD. The process involves a MAPA-endorsed health certificate from Brazil, AQCS approval from India, and arrival at an AQCS-designated Indian airport. Emirates via Dubai, Qatar Airways via Doha, and Lufthansa via Frankfurt are the main cargo routing options. This is a complex long-haul corridor: use a specialist pet transport agent familiar with both Brazilian and Indian requirements.

    - heading: "AQCS import authorisation and arrival procedure"
      body: |
        India's Animal Quarantine and Certification Service (AQCS) operates under the Department of Animal Husbandry and Dairying (DAHD). Import authorisation must be obtained from the AQCS before the pet departs Brazil. The authorisation specifies the entry point airport and conditions of entry.

        AQCS-designated entry airports are: Delhi (DEL), Mumbai (BOM), Chennai (MAA), Kolkata (CCU), Bengaluru (BLR), and Hyderabad (HYD). Pets cannot be imported through airports without AQCS stations. On arrival, the AQCS inspector verifies documentation, microchip, and health certificate. Depending on the origin country's rabies classification, a period of quarantine or observation may be required.

        Contact DAHD (dahd.nic.in) for the current AQCS application procedure and the health certificate format required from Brazil.
