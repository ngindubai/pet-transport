  h1: "Pet Transport from Panama to Singapore: 2026 Guide"
  overview: |
    One airline covers this entire route. Turkish Airlines flies Tocumen non-stop to Istanbul on an Airbus A350, then Istanbul non-stop to Changi on a 777, so a Panamanian pet never needs a second carrier's cargo desk to change hands. That single fact does more to simplify this move than anything in the paperwork.

    The paperwork itself runs on Singapore's side. Panama isn't named on AVS's Schedule I or Schedule II lists, so it defaults to Schedule III: a WOAH-prescribed titre test, a fixed 90-day wait from the blood draw, and 30 days at Singapore's Animal Quarantine Centre once the pet lands. Expect four and a half to five months, start to finish.

  sections:
    - heading: "Why Panama sits in Schedule III, and what that actually costs in time"
      body: |
        AVS lists only four Schedule I countries and around 31 Schedule II countries. Panama appears on neither, so it falls to Schedule III by default, the same tier that applies to most of Latin America, the Caribbean and much of Asia and Africa. That default triggers a rabies titre test at a WOAH reference laboratory, blood drawn at least 28 days after vaccination, with a result of at least 0.5 IU/ml.

        The result then has to be dated between 90 days and 12 months before the pet actually leaves Panama. Owners who count from the day the lab emails a result, rather than the day blood was actually drawn, routinely lose a few weeks off their planning. Run the AVS import licence application and the AQC quarantine booking during this wait, not after it finishes.

    - heading: "One carrier, two long legs: the Turkish Airlines routing"
      body: |
        Turkish Airlines operates Tocumen (PTY) to Istanbul (IST) nonstop, roughly 21 times a week on an Airbus A350, a flight of about 12 hours 51 minutes. From Istanbul, the same airline flies IST to Singapore Changi (SIN) nonstop on a Boeing 777-300ER, around 11 hours. Both legs run scheduled cargo alongside passengers.

        That single-carrier structure is unusual for a Panama-to-Asia pet move. Copa Airlines dominates the Tocumen hub but has no Southeast Asia network of its own, so a Copa-first itinerary means handing the crate to a second airline at a European or Gulf hub partway through the journey. Turkish Airlines avoids that handover entirely.

    - heading: "MIDA-DINASA's certificate, and Singapore's tight timing window"
      body: |
        Panama's export side runs through MIDA, the Ministry of Agricultural Development, and specifically its Dirección Nacional de Sanidad Animal (DINASA), the government veterinary authority that endorses the certificate a MIDA-registered vet prepares. AVS then expects that certificate completed between 2 and 7 days of the export date, a narrower window than many owners assume from researching other destinations.

        Book the pre-export vet appointment against your confirmed flight date, not the other way round. A certificate signed too early falls outside AVS's accepted window and has to be redone.
