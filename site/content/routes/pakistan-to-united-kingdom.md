  sections:
    - heading: "AQD and what it requires from Pakistan"
      body: |
        The Animal Quarantine Department (AQD) under Pakistan's Ministry of National Food Security and Research handles live animal exports. It issues the export permit and endorses the official health certificate for UK entry. In some cases, AQD requires the animal to be inspected at one of its approved quarantine facilities before issuing the permit.

        Apply for the AQD permit at least six weeks before travel, earlier if you are based outside Islamabad or Lahore where AQD offices are located. The permit has a validity period, so obtain it close to your travel date without being so early that it expires before departure. Your AQD-endorsed vet issues the health certificate within 10 days of travel.

    - heading: "Cargo from Lahore, Karachi, and Islamabad"
      body: |
        Three major airports serve Pakistan for international pet cargo. Lahore Allama Iqbal International (LHE) and Islamabad International (ISB) are served by both PIA and British Airways direct to London Heathrow. Karachi Jinnah International (KHI) has PIA and Gulf hub connections via Emirates or Qatar Airways.

        The direct PIA or British Airways routing from LHE or ISB to Heathrow is the simplest option, avoiding a transit. PIA Cargo is familiar with the Pakistan to UK corridor. British Airways World Cargo offers a well-supported direct service. If you are in Karachi, the Gulf hub routing via Emirates or Qatar may be more practical but introduces summer heat embargo considerations.

  - question: "Does Pakistan require a titre test for UK pet travel?"
    answer: "The UK requires the titre test because Pakistan is an unlisted country under UK pet travel rules. The FAVN titre test at an APHA-approved lab, blood drawn at least 30 days after primary vaccination, result 0.5 IU/ml or above, followed by a 90-day wait. Pakistan's AQD also requires its own export permit and certificate endorsement. Source: GOV.UK, 2026."
  - question: "Can I fly a dog from Lahore directly to London?"
    answer: "Yes. Pakistan International Airlines (PIA) and British Airways both operate direct services from Lahore Allama Iqbal International (LHE) to London Heathrow, and both accept pets as manifested cargo. This is the most convenient direct routing if you are based in Lahore or Punjab. Islamabad International (ISB) offers similar direct options."
  - question: "How long does the AQD process take?"
    answer: "Allow at least six weeks for the AQD export permit. In cases where AQD requires an animal inspection at an approved facility, this can add further time. Apply for the permit as soon as you have confirmed your titre test is in progress. Do not leave the AQD application until the last few weeks before travel."
  - question: "What is the tapeworm treatment timing for dogs from Pakistan?"
    answer: "Praziquantel treatment by a vet, recorded in the health certificate, between 24 and 120 hours before the dog's scheduled UK arrival time. That is a five-day window closing 24 hours before arrival. Time it from UK arrival, not from the Pakistani departure. Cats are exempt from the tapeworm requirement."
  - question: "Is there quarantine for pets from Pakistan arriving in the UK?"
    answer: "No routine quarantine for compliant pets. Penalty quarantine up to 4 months applies if the titre test is invalid, the AHC is deficient, or an unapproved route is used. Source: GOV.UK."
