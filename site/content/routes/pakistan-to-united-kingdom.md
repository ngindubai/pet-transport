  sections:
    - heading: "Breed restrictions in the UK"
      body: |
        Before beginning the process, verify that your dog's breed is not subject to UK breed-specific legislation. The Dangerous Dogs Act 1991 prohibits the import of four breeds: Pit Bull Terrier, Japanese Tosa, Dogo Argentino, and Fila Brasileiro. Dogs that are identified as being of one of these types, or substantially of one of these types, are not permitted to enter the UK regardless of their country of origin.

        This is not a paperwork issue that can be resolved: dogs of these types cannot legally be imported into the UK. If there is any question about your dog's breed type, seek a formal breed assessment before committing to the transport process.

        For all other breeds, the process is straightforward in terms of breed rules, though still time-consuming due to the titre test and waiting period requirements.

    - heading: "Health certificate from a Pakistan government vet"
      body: |
        The health certificate for UK entry must be issued by a vet who is authorised by the government of the country of origin. In Pakistan, this means a vet working under or authorised by the Animal Quarantine Department (AQD) or the Ministry of National Food Security and Research.

        The certificate must list: microchip number, species and breed, rabies vaccination history (with titre test result and date), any other relevant vaccinations, tapeworm treatment details (for dogs), and the vet's official stamp and government authorisation number.

        APHA inspectors at Heathrow check this documentation on arrival. If the vet is not government-authorised or the documentation is incomplete, the pet can be refused entry or placed in quarantine at the owner's cost.
