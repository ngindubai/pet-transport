  h1: "Pet Transport from Uzbekistan to Singapore: 2026 Guide"
  overview: |
    Blood drawn in Tashkent for this route usually has to leave the country before it can be read. No APHA-, FAVN- or WOAH-approved laboratory operates inside Uzbekistan, so the serology sample travels abroad, and that single logistical fact adds real time on top of the standard testing wait most owners budget for.

    Singapore's own classification does the rest. Uzbekistan isn't named on AVS's shorter Schedule I or Schedule II lists, so it defaults to Schedule III: the fuller serology and quarantine pathway, plus a rabies revaccination on arrival regardless of the vaccination record from home. Realistically, plan for four and a half to six months.

  sections:
    - heading: "A blood sample with nowhere local to go"
      body: |
        AVS's Schedule III process asks for blood sampled for rabies serology at least 90 days and within 12 months before export, tested at a WOAH reference laboratory or one approved by a Schedule I or II country. Uzbekistan has no such laboratory of its own, which the country's UK and US route pages on this site have already flagged as a genuine gap. That means the sample has to be shipped internationally, and the transit time for that shipment sits on top of, not instead of, the laboratory's own processing time.

        Plan the appointment with this in mind. A test booked assuming domestic turnaround alone routinely runs weeks behind an owner's original estimate.

    - heading: "Schedule III, and a rabies shot Singapore gives anyway"
      body: |
        AVS names only four Schedule I countries and around 31 Schedule II countries. Uzbekistan appears on neither, so it defaults to Schedule III, the tier applied to most of Central Asia, Africa, Latin America and much of the rest of Asia. Pets from Schedule III countries are revaccinated against rabies on arrival in Singapore, regardless of how current the Uzbek vaccination record is, on top of the 30-day AQC quarantine.

    - heading: "Turkish Airlines: the one carrier that covers both legs"
      body: |
        Turkish Airlines runs multiple daily flights from Tashkent to Istanbul, then a nonstop Istanbul to Singapore Changi service on a Boeing 777-300ER, around 11 hours. That single-carrier structure means one airline's cargo operation handles the pet from Tashkent all the way to Changi.

        Uzbekistan Airways flies its own limited weekly nonstop toward Europe and North America but has no Southeast Asia route of its own, so choosing it first still means transferring to a second carrier at whichever hub it serves. Turkish Airlines avoids that extra handover.
