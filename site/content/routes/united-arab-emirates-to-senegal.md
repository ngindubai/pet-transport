content:
  h1: "Pet Transport from United Arab Emirates to Senegal"
  overview: |
    Emirates has flown Dubai to Dakar direct for more than 15 years, currently up to 5 flights a week each way, plus a weekly dedicated freighter. Among the eight destinations in this batch, this is the only one with a long-standing, well-established direct passenger route from the UAE.

    That head start doesn't remove Senegal's own gatekeeping step. DSV's Import Authorisation carries a hard 3-month validity window, and applying either too early or too late can leave a pet without valid paperwork on travel day. Get that timing right and the rest of this route is close to routine: no titre test, no quarantine, a standard vaccination and microchip record. Budget 6 to 9 weeks.

  sections:
    - heading: "A genuinely direct route, for once"
      body: |
        Most Central Asian and Balkan destinations in this build batch need at least one connection out of Dubai. Senegal doesn't. Emirates' own Dubai-Dakar service, running since roughly 2010, gives this route a single-carrier option that keeps handling consistent from check-in to arrival, with pets travelling through Emirates SkyCargo.

        Air France via Paris and Turkish Airlines via Istanbul remain useful backups if Emirates' cargo allocation is full for a particular week, but neither offers anything Emirates' direct service doesn't already provide more simply.

    - heading: "DSV's 3-month clock is the actual risk"
      body: |
        Senegal's Directorate of Veterinary Services issues the mandatory Import Authorisation with a validity window of exactly 3 months from the date of issue. Apply for it before a flight is confirmed and it can lapse before travel. Apply after booking and there might not be time left to fix a problem if the application is rejected or delayed.

        Everything else in Senegal's own rule is close to standard for the region: an ISO microchip, a rabies vaccination at least 21 days old, the core canine or feline vaccines, and internal and external parasite treatment, including specific attention to Echinococcus multilocularis. No titre test applies to a UAE-origin pet.
