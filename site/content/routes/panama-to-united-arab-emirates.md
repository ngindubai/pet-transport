  sections:
    - heading: "MOCCAE's titre test versus the EU's version"
      body: |
        Both MOCCAE and the EU require a rabies antibody titre test from unlisted or unapproved origins, and Panama falls into that category for both systems. The difference is what happens next: the EU imposes a fixed 90-day wait from the blood draw, while MOCCAE accepts a passing result on its own terms, valid for 365 days as long as vaccination remains current.

        That distinction alone can save a Panama-origin pet several months compared with the equivalent EU-bound route, provided the titre test result itself comes back clean and the MOCCAE import permit process runs in parallel with the export paperwork.
    - heading: "Routing through Madrid or Miami, and the Gulf's summer restrictions"
      body: |
        No direct cargo service links Tocumen with Dubai or Abu Dhabi. Madrid, reached via Copa Airlines and Iberia, connects onward with Emirates SkyCargo or Etihad Cargo. Miami, drawing on American Airlines Cargo's strong Latin America presence, is the other established path.

        Factor in the roughly June-to-September summer heat embargo at Gulf airports, when extreme ground temperatures restrict live animal cargo handling. Scheduling outside that window avoids a late rebooking.
