        Finnair operates direct services from Tallinn (TLL) to Helsinki Vantaa (HEL), taking approximately 45 to 60 minutes. This is by far the most practical routing for pets. Finnair accepts live animals in the cargo hold on this route, and small pets can travel in cabin in an approved carrier. Ryanair also operates some budget TLL-HEL services and is cabin-only, suitable for cats and small dogs. airBaltic can connect via Riga (RIX) but adds unnecessary travel time compared to the Finnair direct service.     - heading: "Microchip and vaccination sequence: what Ruokavirasto requires"
      body: |
        Finland's rules on the microchip and rabies vaccination sequence are strict. The microchip must be implanted before the rabies vaccination is given, as specified under EU Regulation 576/2013 and enforced by Ruokavirasto (ruokavirasto.fi). If your pet's EU passport shows a vaccination date earlier than the microchip implantation date, Finnish border control may not accept the vaccination as valid. Make sure your Estonian vet enters the microchip number and implantation date before recording the vaccination. If you are updating an existing vaccination record, also check that the current dose was given before the previous one expired. A lapsed vaccination restarts the 21-day wait.
