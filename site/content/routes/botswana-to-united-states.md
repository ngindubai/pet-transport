content:
  h1: "Pet Transport from Botswana to the United States"
  overview: |
    Delta is the only airline flying nonstop between South Africa and the US, a daily Johannesburg to Atlanta service. For most CDC high-risk-country routes, that detail wouldn't matter much. For Botswana it does: Atlanta is one of only six US airports with a CDC-registered animal care facility, so a dog routed through it lands exactly where the mandatory facility reservation is, with no domestic connection to arrange afterward.

    That convenience doesn't shorten the paperwork. Botswana sits on the CDC's high-risk list for dog rabies, last updated 15 April 2026, which means the full foreign-vaccinated-dog pathway applies: microchip before vaccine, a titre test from a CDC-approved laboratory, and a facility reservation booked before departure. Cats clear the federal side far more lightly. Realistically, budget twelve to sixteen weeks.

  sections:
    - heading: "Why Atlanta is the routing to aim for"
      body: |
        CDC's rule for high-risk-country dogs is strict on this point: the flight has to land at the airport hosting the CDC-registered animal care facility, and the dog can't then connect onward domestically before the facility has processed it. For owners flying in from many high-risk countries, that means booking a long-haul flight into a hub airport, then a separate domestic leg to reach one of the six eligible cities, which adds cost and risk to the itinerary.

        A Botswana-origin dog skips that problem. Airlink or Air Botswana covers the short Gaborone to Johannesburg hop, and Delta's daily nonstop from Johannesburg lands directly in Atlanta, itself a CDC-registered facility city. One connection, one long-haul leg, no domestic transfer with a live animal after a transatlantic flight.

    - heading: "The titre test is still the timeline's biggest driver"
      body: |
        None of the routing convenience changes the underlying clock. Blood has to be drawn at least 30 days after the rabies vaccination, and the passing result needs to be in hand at least 28 days before the flight touches down. Get the maths wrong and the options narrow fast: delay the flight, or arrive without a valid result and accept the 28-day quarantine at the arrival facility, paid for by the owner.

        Botswana's own side of the paperwork runs in parallel. DVS issues a standalone export permit, distinct from the health certificate, usually within 3 to 5 working days once the file is complete. Neither document substitutes for the other, and both need to be current when the dog boards in Gaborone.

    - heading: "Cats: a federal formality, not a free pass"
      body: |
        A cat travelling this same route faces almost none of the CDC machinery built for dogs. No rabies vaccination requirement, no titre test, no facility reservation. What doesn't change is the Botswana side: the DVS export permit, the health certificate, and the vaccination and parasite treatment records it depends on all still apply, and most airlines want their own current health certificate regardless of species before they'll accept the booking.
