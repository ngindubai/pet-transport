  overview: "Relocating a pet from Panama to Poland requires 20-26 weeks of advance preparation, driven by the mandatory titre test pathway that applies to all pets from unlisted third countries. Panama is classified as unlisted under Commission Delegated Regulation (EU) 2026/131, meaning the FAVN rabies titre test, a 30-day pre-draw wait, and a 90-day post-draw wait are all mandatory before your pet can cross into Poland. The good news is that once these steps are complete, Poland's Main Veterinary Inspectorate (GIW) has no quarantine requirement and the journey itself is straightforward by cargo."

  sections:
    - heading: "GIW Requirements: Poland's Veterinary Border Rules"
      body: |
        Poland's Main Veterinary Inspectorate (GIW, Główny Inspektorat Weterynarii, wetgiw.gov.pl) applies EU non-commercial pet movement regulations at Warsaw Chopin Airport (WAW) and other border posts. As an EU member state, Poland follows Commission Delegated Regulation (EU) 2026/131 and the updated listed/unlisted country classification under Implementing Regulation (EU) 2024/1130.

        For a pet arriving from Panama, GIW requires the following on arrival:

        Microchip: ISO 11784/11785 compliant, implanted before the rabies vaccination. GIW scans the chip and cross-checks it with the health certificate.

        Rabies vaccination: Current and post-microchip. At least 21 days must have elapsed since the primary vaccination.

        FAVN titre test: The result must be 0.5 IU/ml or above from an EU-approved laboratory. The blood draw must have been performed at least 30 days after the vaccination, and 90 days must have elapsed from the blood draw date to the date of arrival in Poland.

        Health certificate: EU Annex IV non-commercial format, signed by a MIDA-registered vet and endorsed by DINASA. Must be issued within 10 days of travel. Titre test details must be on the certificate.

        No quarantine. No import permit.

    - heading: "The Titre Test in Practice"
      body: |
        The FAVN (Fluorescent Antibody Virus Neutralisation) test is the gold standard for measuring rabies antibody levels. Some EU-approved laboratories also offer an ELISA test, which is accepted by Poland's GIW.

        The process from Panama:

        Your MIDA-registered vet draws blood from your pet at least 30 days after the rabies vaccination. The blood is shipped in a chilled container to an EU-approved laboratory (list available under Commission Decision 2000/258/EC). Labs accepting international samples include ANSES in France, the Animal Health and Veterinary Laboratories Agency in the UK, and Kansas State University Rabies Laboratory in the US.

        The laboratory tests the serum and issues a certificate showing the result in IU/ml. A result of 0.5 IU/ml or above is required. If the result is below 0.5 IU/ml, the vaccination must be repeated and the full 30-day wait and 90-day wait restart.

        On receipt of a passing result, the 90-day wait is already running from the blood draw date (not from when you receive the result). Keep the laboratory report safe, as GIW will check it.

    - heading: "Flying from Panama City to Warsaw"
      body: |
        Warsaw Chopin Airport (WAW) receives live animal cargo via two-leg journeys from Panama. The standard hubs are Amsterdam (AMS), Madrid (MAD), and Frankfurt (FRA).

        Copa Airlines operates Panama's most frequent long-haul connections. PTY to AMS connects well with KLM or LOT Polish Airlines cargo to WAW. PTY to MAD via Iberia connects to LOT Polish Airlines from MAD to WAW.

        LOT Polish Airlines is the natural final-leg carrier for Poland. It operates from multiple European hubs to WAW and accepts live animal cargo. Booking the LOT sector well in advance is recommended, as live animal cargo space on Polish routes is limited.

        Lufthansa via Frankfurt is a strong alternative, particularly for larger dogs. Frankfurt's animal centre handles the Panama-to-Germany sector (typically via a US gateway or via MAD), and FRA to WAW is served by Lufthansa and LOT. This routing gives access to Lufthansa's superior live animal infrastructure.

    - heading: "Living with the 90-Day Wait"
      body: |
        For families relocating from Panama to Poland, the 90-day waiting period is often the most frustrating part of the process. A dog or cat that has passed its titre test is clinically ready to travel, but the regulatory wait must complete.

        There are a few practical things to do during this period:

        Source the IATA-approved crate and introduce it to your pet as a positive space. Leave it in the living area with the door open. Feed meals inside. Let your pet sleep in it. A pet that is comfortable in its crate before travel will find the cargo journey far less stressful.

        Book the cargo space. Cargo bookings to Poland can be made several months in advance, and booking early secures the best options. You can book with a tentative travel date and adjust it as the 90-day date firms up.

faqs:
  - question: "We are moving to Warsaw. Is there a faster route for our pet from Panama that avoids the 90-day wait?"
    answer: "No. The 90-day waiting period after the FAVN blood draw is mandated by Commission Delegated Regulation (EU) 2026/131 for pets from unlisted countries. There is no way to waive or shorten it, regardless of the destination within the EU. The only way to reduce the total preparation time is to start as early as possible."
  - question: "Can our pet fly with us on a commercial flight to Poland rather than going as cargo?"
    answer: "Small pets (typically under 8 kg including crate) can sometimes travel in the cabin on European routes, but the transatlantic leg from Panama will almost certainly require cargo transport regardless of pet size. Copa Airlines' policies on in-cabin pets vary by route. For the PTY to AMS or MAD leg, cargo is the standard approach for all but the smallest animals. The second leg from the hub to WAW may allow cabin transport for very small animals. Confirm with the specific airline when booking."
  - question: "Does GIW accept titre tests performed by Panamanian laboratories?"
    answer: "Only if the laboratory is on the EU-approved list under Commission Decision 2000/258/EC. Most Panamanian veterinary laboratories are not on this list. Your MIDA-registered vet will need to ship the blood sample to an approved laboratory outside Panama. Confirm before drawing blood that the laboratory you plan to use is approved and accepts international samples from Panama."
