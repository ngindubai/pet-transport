  h1: "Pet Transport from Ivory Coast to United Arab Emirates"
  overview: |
    Emirates markets its Dubai-Abidjan service as a single flight, but it is really a one-stop routing with a scheduled stop in Accra, Ghana, on the same aircraft. That is worth knowing before booking, since it changes how the crate is handled during the stop and how long the journey actually takes compared with a genuine nonstop.

    On the regulatory side, MOCCAE treats Cote d'Ivoire as high-risk, meaning a titre test is required, but there is no residency requirement and no fixed wait once the result clears. Fourteen to eighteen weeks covers this route comfortably, most of it taken up by DSV's uncertain processing time rather than any mandatory hold.

    DSV, the Ivorian veterinary authority, handles the export side. It does not publish a fixed fee or turnaround schedule online, so start early and confirm directly with a registered vet in Abidjan.

  sections:
    - heading: "The Emirates routing, and two alternatives"
      body: |
        Dubai to Abidjan via Emirates is booked as a single service but actually stops in Accra, Ghana, on the way, continuing on the same aircraft rather than requiring a change of airline. For live animal cargo, that stop still means the crate sits on the ground in Accra for a period, so confirm with the cargo agent exactly how that stop is handled before committing to this option over the alternatives.

        Ethiopian Airlines via Addis Ababa and Turkish Airlines via Istanbul both connect Abidjan to Dubai as well, and are worth comparing on schedule and price rather than assuming Emirates is automatically the best fit simply because it is the most recognised name.

    - heading: "MOCCAE's titre test, without the EU's extra wait"
      body: |
        Cote d'Ivoire sits outside MOCCAE's roughly 50-country low-risk list, so the FAVN or RSNT titre test applies: blood drawn at least 21 days after vaccination, minimum result 0.5 IU/ml. Once that result passes, there is no additional fixed waiting period before travel, only the requirement that the test be dated somewhere between 3 and 12 months before departure.

        That is a genuinely different shape of rule from the EU or UK's 90-day post-titre wait covered on this site's other Ivory Coast routes. Owners moving to the UAE save real time against those equivalent European journeys.

    - heading: "A much heavier process for the same pet heading to Australia"
      body: |
        It's a useful contrast to have in mind. This same Ivorian pet, moved to Australia instead, runs into DAFF's non-approved country rule: 180 consecutive days of residency in an approved third country, typically France, before any Australian paperwork even opens. MOCCAE asks for a fraction of that scrutiny. Same passport, two very different regulatory verdicts.
