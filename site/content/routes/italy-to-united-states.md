  overview: |
    Italy is not on the CDC's high-risk country list for dog imports, which means dogs from Italy have a straightforward path into the USA. The process involves completing the free CDC Dog Import Form online before travel, ensuring the dog is microchipped and at least 6 months old, and the pet can arrive at any US airport. No titre test, no quarantine, no import permit.

    Cats from Italy face no US federal restrictions at all. USDA APHIS has no health requirements for importing pet cats. Airlines still require vet documentation, and some US states have their own rules, but federally cats from Italy move freely into the USA.

    ITA Airways is the primary carrier with direct cargo from Rome and Milan to US gateways. American Airlines is an alternative. Delta and United accept small pets in cabin on transatlantic routes but do not offer general cargo for large dogs.

faqs:
  - question: "Does Italy count as a high-risk country for CDC dog imports?"
    answer: "No. Italy is not on the CDC high-risk country list. Dogs from Italy are treated as coming from a non-high-risk country. The requirement is the free CDC Dog Import Form (completed online before travel), a microchip, and the dog must be at least 6 months old and appear healthy. No titre test, no quarantine, no import permit."
  - question: "Which airline should I use for my dog from Rome to the USA?"
    answer: "ITA Airways operates direct cargo from Rome Fiumicino (FCO) and Milan Malpensa (MXP) to JFK and other US gateways. American Airlines Cargo also serves this corridor. British Airways offers an indirect route via London. Delta and United accept small pets in cabin on transatlantic routes but do not offer general cargo for large dogs. Confirm current availability before booking."
  - question: "Do I need a USDA health certificate for my Italian pet entering the USA?"
    answer: "For dogs from Italy (a non-high-risk country), CDC does not require a USDA-specific format health certificate. However, airlines typically require a standard vet health certificate for all cargo shipments. An EU pet passport or Italian vet-issued health certificate is usually sufficient for airline purposes. The CDC Dog Import Form receipt is the primary customs document."
  - question: "Do cats from Italy need any documents to enter the USA?"
    answer: "At the US federal level, there are no requirements for importing pet cats from Italy. USDA APHIS has no health restrictions on cat imports. In practice, airlines require a vet-issued health certificate for cargo or cabin travel, and some US states have their own rules. Check your destination state and your airline's policy."
  - question: "Can my dog arrive at any US airport from Italy?"
    answer: "Yes. Dogs from non-high-risk countries (including Italy) can arrive at any US airport, seaport, or land border crossing. There is no requirement to arrive at an airport with a CDC-registered facility, unlike dogs from high-risk countries. The CDC/CBP inspection happens at the port of entry and is brief for compliant pets."
