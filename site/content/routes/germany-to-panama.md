  sections:
    - heading: "Panama's requirements for pets from Germany"
      body: |
        Panama's MIDA (Ministerio de Desarrollo Agropecuario, mida.gob.pa) governs pet imports. For pets arriving from Germany, the requirements are: ISO 11784/11785 microchip; rabies vaccination administered between 30 days and 12 months before arrival; pet must be over 4 months old; official health certificate in English or Spanish, endorsed by the German Veterinäramt and authenticated by the Panamanian Embassy in Germany.

        No import permit is needed. No quarantine for compliant arrivals. A MIDA inspector at Tocumen International Airport (PTY) checks the documents on arrival and collects a USD 20 inspection fee. If documents are in order, the pet is released after inspection.

    - heading: "The Panamanian Embassy authentication step"
      body: |
        Panama requires that the health certificate be authenticated (legalised) by the Panamanian Embassy in the country of origin before travel. For Germany, this is the Panamanian Embassy in Berlin. The authentication confirms that the Veterinäramt endorsement is genuine.

        The process: your official veterinarian (Amtstierarzt) prepares the health certificate in English or Spanish. The Veterinäramt endorses it. You then present the endorsed certificate to the Panamanian Embassy in Berlin for authentication. The Embassy typically requires the original document and may charge a consular fee. Contact the Embassy well in advance to confirm their current authentication procedure, required documents, and turnaround time.

        This step adds a few business days to the process. Plan it carefully within the 10-day validity window of the health certificate.

    - heading: "Routing from Germany to Panama City"
      body: |
        Frankfurt (FRA) is Germany's primary international cargo hub, with Munich (MUC) as the secondary option. The most practical routing is FRA to Miami (MIA) via Lufthansa Cargo, then MIA to Tocumen Airport (PTY) via Copa Airlines. Copa Airlines is Panama's national carrier with its main hub at PTY and has strong connections from MIA.

        Lufthansa and Copa Airlines are both part of the Lufthansa Group and codeshare alliance network, which supports through-cargo arrangements. Confirm live animal acceptance on both legs before booking. An alternative transatlantic routing goes via Madrid (MAD) with Iberia, then to PTY via Copa connections through Central America.

    - heading: "German export documentation"
      body: |
        Germany's export health certificates for pets going to non-EU countries are prepared by an official veterinarian (Amtstierarzt) and endorsed by the local Veterinäramt (district veterinary office, operating at Kreis or city level). The Veterinäramt checks the vaccination records, microchip, and the vet's attestations before stamping and signing the certificate.

        Allow three to five business days for the Veterinäramt endorsement. The health certificate must be issued within 10 days of travel. After the Veterinäramt endorses it, you take it to the Panamanian Embassy in Berlin for authentication before departure.

    answer: "Authentication requirements vary and can change. Contact the Panamanian Embassy in Berlin directly to confirm whether in-person, postal, or digital submission is accepted. Allow more time if submitting by post to account for any delays."
  - question: "What does the MIDA inspection at PTY involve?"
    answer: "MIDA inspectors at Tocumen Airport check the authenticated health certificate, microchip, and vaccination record. They verify that the rabies vaccination is within the 30-day to 12-month window and that the pet is over 4 months old. For compliant pets, the inspection is routine and typically takes 30-60 minutes after the cargo is unloaded. The USD 20 fee is paid at the inspection point."
  - question: "Does Panama require a tapeworm or other parasite treatment for dogs from Germany?"
    answer: "MIDA does not mandate a specific tapeworm treatment for dogs arriving from Germany, unlike Jamaica or some other destinations. The health certificate must be complete and the rabies vaccination current. Check with MIDA directly for any updates to requirements before travel."
  - question: "Is there a limit on the number of pets I can bring to Panama?"
    answer: "For personal companion animals not intended for commercial purposes, MIDA generally allows entry of companion pets. If you are moving more than one or two animals, or if there is any doubt about the personal/commercial nature of the movement, contact MIDA at mida.gob.pa before travel to confirm what documentation may be needed."
