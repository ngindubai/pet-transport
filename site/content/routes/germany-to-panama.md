  sections:
    - heading: "MIDA requirements: what Panama checks on arrival"
      body: |
        Panama's Ministry of Agricultural Development (MIDA) handles pet import controls at Tocumen International Airport (PTY) in Panama City. MIDA veterinarians check your pet's microchip, vaccination records, health certificate, and antiparasitic treatment documentation on arrival. The registration fee is approximately USD 20 per pet, paid in cash at the MIDA airport office.

        Your pet must be over 4 months old to enter Panama. Pets younger than this are not admitted. The rabies vaccination must have been given at least 30 days before entry and must be current. Dogs need current distemper, hepatitis, leptospirosis, and parvovirus vaccinations. Cats need rhinotracheitis, panleukopenia, and feline leukaemia on record.

        Panama does not require quarantine for compliant arrivals, and no import permit is needed for personal companion animals. Antiparasitic treatment must be carried out within 30 days of departure and documented with the product name, dose, and application date.

    - heading: "The embassy authentication step: plan around it"
      body: |
        Germany-to-Panama involves one step beyond most EU-to-Latin America routes because Panama requires the health certificate to be authenticated by its diplomatic mission in the origin country.

        The sequence is: Amtstierarzt examination, state competent authority endorsement, then submission to the Embassy of Panama in Berlin for authentication. The embassy typically takes 2-3 working days to process the stamp. Confirm the current fee and appointment procedure when you call the embassy to schedule.

        Given that the AHC is valid for 10 days from issue, the scheduling is tight. If your cargo departs on a Friday, the Amtstierarzt appointment should be no later than Monday, so the state endorsement and embassy authentication can complete by Wednesday or Thursday. Book the embassy appointment before fixing your cargo date.
