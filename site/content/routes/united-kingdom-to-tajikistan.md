  h1: "Pet Transport from the United Kingdom to Tajikistan"
  overview: |
    No blood test stands between a UK pet and entry into Tajikistan. That single fact puts this route in a lighter category than most of the unlisted-country moves this site covers, and it is the exact reverse of the return trip: a Tajikistan-origin pet flying into the UK needs the full titre protocol and a 90-day wait, while a UK pet flying into Tajikistan needs neither.

    The trade-off sits elsewhere. Tajikistan takes non-compliance seriously: a pet that turns up with paperwork that does not match can face quarantine, be sent back to the UK, or in the worst cases be euthanised, all at the owner's cost. There is also no direct flight. Turkish Airlines via Istanbul and Air Astana via Almaty are the two realistic ways to get from Heathrow to Dushanbe.

    Plan for seven to ten weeks. Most of that time comes from the 30-day rabies vaccination wait plus building in enough slack to get every document precisely right before departure.
  sections:
    - heading: "Why the titre test disappears here, and why the stakes still feel high"
      body: |
        Tajikistan does not require a rabies antibody titre test on a pet entering from any country, the UK included. Instead, a current rabies vaccination given between 30 days and 12 months before entry, and a licensed vet's health certificate confirming good health and no unhealed wounds, cover the medical side. Puppies and kittens cannot be vaccinated before 12 weeks old, and then need the full 30-day wait before flying.

        The lighter medical bar does not mean a relaxed one. Tajikistan's own guidance is explicit that a pet without compliant paperwork can be quarantined, sent home, or in the most serious cases euthanised, entirely at the owner's expense. That is a harder consequence than Kyrgyzstan or several other Central Asian destinations apply for the same kind of mistake, so this is not a route to wing the documentation on.

        Source: State Veterinary Service, Ministry of Agriculture of the Republic of Tajikistan.
    - heading: "Getting from Heathrow to Dushanbe"
      body: |
        Turkish Airlines runs Heathrow to Istanbul, connecting to roughly seven weekly Istanbul-Dushanbe flights, with small pets travelling in the cabin under 8kg and larger pets through Turkish Cargo. Air Astana offers a genuine one-change alternative: its own nonstop Heathrow-Almaty service on the long-range A321LR, followed by an Air Astana connection on to Dushanbe around five times a week.

        Somon Air, Tajikistan's national carrier, does not fly to the UK. It announced plans in July 2026 to add a London route, but gave no launch date, so treat that as background information rather than something to plan a travel date around.
