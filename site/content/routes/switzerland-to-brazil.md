  sections:
    - heading: "Why the nonstop flight matters for a pet in cargo"
      body: |
        A direct flight removes the two things that make long-haul pet cargo riskier: a ground transfer between aircraft, and a wait at a connecting hub where temperature and handling standards vary. At just under 12 hours, ZRH to GRU sits close to the outer limit most vets are comfortable with for a healthy adult dog or cat, but it beats any routing that adds a Frankfurt, Madrid or Paris changeover on top.

        SWISS Cargo's live animal desk coordinates the booking directly, which matters because six flights a week is not a lot of slack if your preferred date is already full. Confirm crate dimensions and weight with SWISS Cargo as early as you can, ideally as soon as you have a travel date in mind.
    - heading: "The FSVO and cantonal endorsement: timing is everything"
      body: |
        Switzerland is not an EU member, so the standard EU pet passport is not the document Brazil wants. Your vet issues a CVI in the MAPA format, and the FSVO (or, depending on the canton, the cantonal veterinary office acting for the FSVO) endorses it.

        This endorsement has to land within 10 days of your pet actually arriving in Brazil, counting the flight time and any layover you have planned. Contact the cantonal office as soon as your flight is booked. Some cantons process this within a day or two; others need closer to a week, particularly outside the larger urban centres such as Zurich, Geneva and Basel.

faqs:
  - question: "Does Brazil quarantine pets arriving from Switzerland?"
    answer: "No. There is no mandatory quarantine for dogs and cats from Switzerland provided the microchip, rabies vaccination, antiparasitic treatment record (for dogs) and FSVO-endorsed CVI are all in order. MAPA carries out a documentary and health inspection at Guarulhos, not a quarantine hold."
  - question: "Is a rabies titre test needed for pets travelling from Switzerland to Brazil?"
    answer: "No. Brazil does not require a FAVN titre test from Swiss-origin pets. This is a genuine difference from destinations such as the UK or Japan, where a titre test and long waiting period apply. A current rabies vaccination and the standard 30-day wait for first-time vaccinations is all MAPA asks for."
  - question: "How many flights a week does SWISS run between Zurich and Sao Paulo?"
    answer: "Six a week, on a Boeing 777, at just under 12 hours flight time. It is the only nonstop service on this city pair, so book cargo space as soon as your travel date is fixed, particularly for departures in the Swiss summer holiday period."
  - question: "Does my pet need an import permit to enter Brazil from Switzerland?"
    answer: "No import permit is required for a personal pet travelling with its owner. The CVI, endorsed by the FSVO or cantonal office within 10 days of arrival, is the document MAPA checks on entry."
  - question: "What happens if the CVI endorsement date is more than 10 days before arrival?"
    answer: "MAPA can refuse entry or hold the pet at the owner's cost until a compliant certificate is produced. Work backwards from your SWISS departure date: book the cantonal endorsement appointment so it falls within the 10-day window that ends on your Brazil arrival date."
  - question: "Are cats treated differently from dogs on this route?"
    answer: "Mostly the same. Both need the ISO microchip, current rabies vaccination and endorsed CVI. The one difference is the antiparasitic treatment against ticks and fleas, which Brazil currently requires for dogs only, not cats."
