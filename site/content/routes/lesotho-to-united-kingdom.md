  h1: "Pet Transport from Lesotho to the United Kingdom"
  overview: |
    Lesotho is an enclave. The entire country sits inside South Africa's borders, and its only international airport, Moshoeshoe I in Maseru, has a single scheduled destination: Johannesburg. Every pet leaving Lesotho by air starts with that short regional hop, whatever the final destination.

    From Johannesburg O.R. Tambo, British Airways flies nonstop to London Heathrow in around 11 hours. South African Airways is worth checking as a comparison on the same sector. But the two legs run on different bookings, often different terminals, and the pet needs a ground agent to manage the transfer between them.

    Layer the UK's unlisted-country titre protocol on top of that routing reality and this becomes one of the more demanding corridors on the African continent for pet transport, not because any single rule is unusual, but because two separate systems, Lesotho's own limited connectivity and the UK's full rabies protocol, stack on each other.
  sections:
    - heading: "Why Maseru to London always means two flights, minimum"
      body: |
        Moshoeshoe I International Airport is Lesotho's largest and only real international gateway, and Airlink is the sole scheduled carrier there, flying to O.R. Tambo about 21 times a week. There is no direct or one-stop scheduled service from Lesotho to Europe. Every pet, and every owner, transits through Johannesburg.

        This is not a minor technicality. It means booking two separate cargo movements on two separate air waybills, usually with two different airlines, and arranging for a licensed ground agent in Johannesburg to receive the pet from the Airlink flight and hand it over to the long-haul carrier. Skipping the agent and trying to manage this handoff yourself is possible in principle but rarely advisable given South African customs and cargo terminal procedures.

        Source: Airlink route network data and O.R. Tambo cargo terminal operations.
    - heading: "The UK side: same unlisted-country protocol as most of Southern Africa"
      body: |
        Lesotho does not appear on the UK's Part 1 or Part 2 rabies-listed country lists, so the full unlisted-country protocol applies: microchip before vaccination, a titre test with blood drawn at least 30 days after vaccination, a result of at least 0.5 IU/ml at an APHA-approved laboratory, and a fixed 3-month wait from the blood draw date.

        No FAVN or ELISA laboratory operates inside Lesotho itself, so blood samples are usually couriered via South Africa, which has more established laboratory infrastructure. Build extra time into your schedule for this step rather than assuming it will move at the same pace as in a country with local testing capacity.

        Source: UK Animal and Plant Health Agency, gov.uk/bring-pet-to-great-britain.
