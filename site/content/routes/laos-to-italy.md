content:
  h1: "Pet Transport from Laos to Italy"
  overview: |
    Italy has carried no national breed-specific legislation since 2009, a genuine point of relief for owners of restricted breeds elsewhere in Europe. The rest of the process for a Laos-origin pet, however, runs the standard EU course: a titre test, a 90-day wait, and a transfer through Bangkok or Doha before Rome or Milan comes into view.

    DLF's own 15-day export notice sits comfortably inside that longer EU clock, so it's rarely the constraint that decides your travel date.

  sections:
    - heading: "Getting to Rome or Milan from Vientiane"
      body: |
        Lao Airlines' twice-daily Bangkok service is the standard starting point, feeding into Thai Airways Cargo or a Qatar Airways Cargo connection via Doha for the onward sector into Italy. Korean Air's Seoul Incheon routing works too, though it adds a second transfer.

        Rome Fiumicino and Milan Malpensa both handle live animal cargo with Italian Ministry of Health inspection on arrival. The choice between them usually comes down to whichever connection gives the shorter overall transit time from the chosen hub.

    - heading: "The titre test and DLF's role in the timeline"
      body: |
        Laos sits outside the EU's list of low-risk third countries, so Italy applies the full sequence: microchip before vaccination, a 30-day wait, a passing titre result of at least 0.5 IU/ml, then 90 further days from the blood draw. Few EU-approved laboratories operate inside Laos, so factor in courier time for the sample.

        DLF's own 15-day notice period for the export licence runs well within that wait, and in practice rarely changes the overall schedule. Submit the DLF application once the 90-day clock is close to finishing rather than treating it as an early priority.
