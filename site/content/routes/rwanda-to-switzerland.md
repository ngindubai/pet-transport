  sections:
    - heading: "The FSVO permit: the step Switzerland adds that most destinations don't"
      body: |
        Elsewhere in the EU, once the titre test and waiting period are done, a compliant health certificate is usually enough. Switzerland asks for one more thing. Because Rwanda is classified by the Swiss FSVO as a high rabies risk country, owners must apply directly to the FSVO for advance permission before the pet can enter, and that application needs to go in at least 3 weeks before the planned entry date. Miss this step and there is no workaround at the border; the pet cannot legally be admitted, regardless of how complete the rest of the paperwork is. Build the FSVO application into the schedule early, ideally as soon as the vaccination date is confirmed, so the 3-week window overlaps comfortably with the rest of the process rather than becoming a last-minute scramble.

    - heading: "Microchip, vaccination, titre test: the same sequence, a different name for the wait"
      body: |
        Switzerland follows the same biological sequence as the EU states in this batch: microchip before or on the same day as the rabies vaccination, then at least 30 days before blood can be drawn for a titre test at an approved lab. The result must clear 0.5 IU/ml. Where Switzerland differs is language, not substance. Instead of the EU's "90 days", the FSVO frames this as a 3-month waiting period from the blood draw date. It amounts to close to the same length of time, but don't let the different phrasing lead you to miscount the date. A titre result below the threshold means starting the vaccination and blood draw steps again, and the 3-month clock resets from the new draw date.

    - heading: "Kigali to Zurich or Geneva"
      body: |
        Brussels Airlines offers a direct KGL-BRU leg with a solid onward connection to Zurich, and it's usually the first option worth pricing given how often Brussels connects into Switzerland. Ethiopian Airlines Cargo via Addis Ababa reaches either Zurich or Geneva through its European partner network. KLM Cargo's joint venture with Kenya Airways runs a tested path via Nairobi and Amsterdam. RwandAir also flies the Brussels leg from Kigali and is worth comparing on cargo space and price. Whichever carrier you choose, time the booking against your confirmed FSVO approval date rather than booking cargo space before permission is granted.
