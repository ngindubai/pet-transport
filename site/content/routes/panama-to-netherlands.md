  h1: "Pet Transport from Panama to the Netherlands"
  overview: |
    Panama is one of the busiest aviation hubs in Latin America. Tocumen International Airport (PTY) connects Central America to North America, Europe, and beyond. Copa Airlines is based there. The irony for pet owners moving from Panama to the Netherlands is that this transit-hub status does not simplify a single step of the EU's import process.

    The Netherlands classifies Panama as an unlisted third country under EU Regulation 576/2013. That means the full unlisted-country protocol applies: ISO microchip before vaccination, rabies vaccination, FAVN titre test at an EU-approved laboratory, 90-day wait from blood draw date, and a MIDA-endorsed EU-format health certificate. No quarantine for compliant arrivals, but no shortcuts either.

    Start preparations at least 20 weeks before your intended travel date. The 90-day wait alone accounts for most of that window.
  sections:
    - heading: "EU unlisted vs listed country: what the difference means for Panama"
      body: |
        The EU divides countries of origin into two categories for pet movements: listed and unlisted. Listed countries have been assessed as having equivalent rabies control standards to the EU. Unlisted countries, including Panama, have not received that designation. The practical difference is significant.

        | Requirement | EU listed country | EU unlisted country (Panama) |
        |---|---|---|
        | Microchip | ISO 11784/11785 | ISO 11784/11785 |
        | Rabies vaccination | Required | Required |
        | FAVN titre test | Not required | Required |
        | Wait before entry | Not applicable | 90 days from blood draw |
        | Import permit | Not required | Not required |
        | Quarantine on arrival | None | None if compliant |

        The titre test adds roughly 4-6 weeks to your preparation (30-day post-vaccination wait before blood draw, plus lab processing time), and the 90-day wait from blood draw adds another 3 months on top. The total preparation window before the earliest possible travel date is around 20-22 weeks from microchip day.

        Source: EU Regulation 576/2013 and Implementing Regulation 2020/692. Confirm current NVWA requirements before booking.
    - heading: "Getting the MIDA export health certificate in Panama"
      body: |
        MIDA (Ministry of Agricultural Development) is Panama's veterinary authority and the body that endorses official export health certificates. The certificate itself is prepared by a MIDA-authorised veterinarian and then officially endorsed by MIDA.

        For Netherlands entry, the certificate must be in EU format and must reference your pet's microchip number, rabies vaccination details, and the FAVN titre test result including the laboratory name, result date, and value achieved. The certificate is valid for 10 days from the examination date, so timing matters: you need your travel date confirmed before booking the health certificate appointment.

        Allow 2-4 working days for MIDA to process the endorsement. Do not leave the health certificate to the last minute. If the endorsement is delayed and the certificate expires before travel, you will need to start the health certificate process again.
    - heading: "Cargo routing from Tocumen (PTY) to Amsterdam (AMS)"
      body: |
        No direct cargo flights operate from PTY to AMS. All routings involve at least one stop. The main options are:

        KLM Cargo via a US hub is the most direct European connection to AMS. KLM's home base is Schiphol, so the Amsterdam connection is well-established. Pets travel as manifested cargo in temperature-controlled holds throughout.

        Iberia via Madrid (MAD) is a well-used routing for Latin America to Europe. PTY to MAD on Iberia, then MAD to AMS. Two separate cargo bookings are needed, but Madrid is a large hub with reliable onward connections.

        Air France via Paris CDG then CDG to AMS adds another European connection but works well given Air France Cargo's coverage of Latin America.

        Copa Airlines from PTY can connect via US hubs to transatlantic services. As the PTY-based carrier, Copa is a practical first leg option depending on your connecting carrier arrangements.

        For all multi-segment cargo moves, confirm that each carrier accepts live animal cargo and that the connection times meet minimum transit requirements for pets. An IPATA-registered agent with Latin America experience will handle these confirmations and manage any embargo windows.

Pets from EU-listed countries are not required to do this."
