content:
  h1: "Pet Transport from Suriname to the United States"
  overview: |
    Suriname sits on the CDC's high-risk list for dog rabies, the same list that puts Venezuela and Bolivia through the full foreign-vaccinated-dog pathway: microchip before vaccine, a titre test from a CDC-approved laboratory, arrival at a CDC-registered facility airport. Where Suriname differs is the flight itself. Surinam Airways already routes its US service through Miami, one of the CDC's six registered facility airports, so the paperwork doesn't come bundled with a routing headache the way it does on some of this site's other South American routes.

    Cats clear the federal side far more lightly than dogs, with no titre test or vaccination mandate at all. For dogs, the timeline runs on the titre test: blood drawn at least 30 days after vaccination, a result confirmed at least 28 days before landing, and a CDC-registered facility reservation booked before departure. Ten to fourteen weeks is a realistic window from a standing start.

  sections:
    - heading: "Why the flight is the easy part here"
      body: |
        Surinam Airways runs a single scheduled service from Paramaribo through Georgetown to Miami, its main link to the United States since it stopped flying the separate Curacao-Miami sector in May 2026. Caribbean Airlines covers the same Georgetown-Miami leg with its own Paramaribo-Georgetown connection, giving a genuine second option if Surinam Airways' hold space doesn't work for your dates.

        What matters for a high-risk-country dog is that this normal routing already ends at Miami, one of only six US airports with a CDC-registered animal care facility. Compare that with Venezuela's routes on this site, which currently need a deliberate multi-leg detour just to reach a qualifying airport. Book the through-flight, but double-check the final leg lands specifically at Miami and not a different US gateway, since the CDC restriction applies to the airport, not just the country.

    - heading: "The titre test timeline, and what happens without it"
      body: |
        Work the dates backwards from your intended arrival. The titre test result needs to be in hand at least 28 days before the flight lands, and the blood draw itself has to sit at least 30 days after the rabies vaccination. Kansas State University's Rabies Laboratory, a commonly used CDC-approved option, runs the FAVN test for around USD 90 with a three to four week turnaround, so the lab's own processing time is often the tightest part of the schedule.

        Miss the window, or get a result that doesn't pass, and the dog isn't refused entry outright. It completes a mandatory 28-day quarantine at the CDC-registered facility instead, at the owner's cost, with the fee set individually by each facility rather than published centrally. Getting a written quote before you book the flight avoids an unpleasant surprise on arrival.
