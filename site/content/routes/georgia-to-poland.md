  sections:
    - heading: "Why the NFA endorsement step needs more time than owners expect"
      body: |
        The Animal Health Certificate for travel from Georgia to Poland must be endorsed by Georgia's National Food Agency (NFA). This is not just a vet's signature: the NFA is the official competent authority, and the endorsement confirms that the certificate meets EU Regulation 2020/692 requirements.

        In Tbilisi, the NFA endorsement process typically takes several working days after the vet prepares the certificate. Factor this into your travel planning. If you book cargo for a Wednesday and only arrange the vet appointment the Monday before, there may not be enough time for the NFA endorsement to process, the certificate to be ready, and for you to present it at the cargo check-in.

        A practical approach: book the cargo date first, then work backwards. Schedule the vet appointment at least 10 working days before the cargo date. The certificate is valid for 10 days from issue, so there is a window, but that window must accommodate the NFA endorsement process.

    - heading: "The FAVN titre test from Tbilisi in practice"
      body: |
        There is no EU-approved FAVN laboratory in Georgia. Your vet draws blood from your pet in Tbilisi and ships it cold to an approved lab in Europe or North America. The approved lab list is maintained by the European Commission at food.ec.europa.eu.

        Sample shipping from Tbilisi typically goes via DHL or FedEx international, with special handling for diagnostic samples. The courier journey takes 2-5 business days. Lab processing takes 1-2 weeks after receipt. Total from blood draw to result: 2-4 weeks.

        If the result comes back below 0.5 IU/ml, the test is a failure. You must re-vaccinate, wait 30 days, and draw blood again. The 90-day wait restarts from the new passing blood draw date. A failed test adds a minimum of 3-4 months to the total timeline.

  - question: "What happens at Warsaw Chopin Airport when my pet arrives?"
    answer: "Your pet arrives at the cargo terminal at Warsaw Chopin (WAW). The GIW (Chief Veterinary Inspectorate) border vet checks the microchip, vaccination record, FAVN titre test (checking blood draw date and result), and confirms the 90-day wait has elapsed. The NFA-endorsed AHC is also verified. If all documents are in order, the pet is released. The cargo terminal is separate from the passenger terminal."
  - question: "Do I need to register my pet with Polish authorities after arrival?"
    answer: "Poland requires dogs to have rabies vaccinations and some municipalities require local pet registration. After arrival, check with your local council (gmina) about pet registration requirements in your area. These are domestic requirements after import and separate from the border entry process."
