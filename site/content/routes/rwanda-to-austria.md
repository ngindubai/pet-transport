  sections:
    - heading: "Getting from Kigali to Vienna"
      body: |
        Ethiopian Airlines Cargo is usually the primary choice, connecting Kigali to Addis Ababa and on to Vienna on an established East Africa to Europe cargo path. KLM Cargo runs the KLM/Kenya Airways joint venture through Nairobi, then a further connection via Amsterdam into Vienna, a structured routing with good documentation practices. Brussels Airlines flies KGL to Brussels with a short onward hop to Vienna, while RwandAir covers the same Brussels routing and is worth checking if Brussels Airlines is fully booked.

        None of these options is a single flight. Every routing carries at least one transit stop, so confirm that the connecting airport accepts live animal cargo and that layover times meet the airline's minimum handling requirements before booking. Cargo space from Kigali typically needs 4 to 6 weeks of lead time, and it should only be booked once the 90-day wait's end date is known.

    - heading: "The titre test and the 90-day wait"
      body: |
        Rwanda does not appear on the EU's approved list, so Austria requires the full unlisted-country sequence under EU Regulation 2020/692. Microchip first, always before the rabies vaccination. Wait at least 30 days after vaccination before blood is drawn for the FAVN or ELISA titre test at an EU-approved laboratory, and the result has to reach at least 0.5 IU/ml.

        Once that result clears, the real wait begins: 90 days from the blood draw date, not the date the laboratory reports the result back to you. Results themselves can take two to four weeks, so treating the result date as your starting point routinely costs owners a month they thought they already had. Add the RAB endorsement time for the export paperwork and 20 to 26 weeks becomes a realistic total.

    - heading: "Two Austrian authorities, one file"
      body: |
        Most destinations on this route name a single food or veterinary authority. Austria splits the job. AGES, the Austrian Agency for Health and Food Safety, handles the technical inspection of arriving pets and their paperwork. Above it sits the Federal Ministry of Social Affairs, Health, Care and Consumer Protection, known as the BMSGPK, which oversees the wider policy area.

        In practice this rarely changes anything for a straightforward, compliant arrival. But it is worth knowing that a question raised at the border about your pet's documentation could route through either body, so keep copies of everything: the RAB-endorsed EU-format certificate, the titre test result, and proof of the blood draw date, in case a query needs resolving quickly.
