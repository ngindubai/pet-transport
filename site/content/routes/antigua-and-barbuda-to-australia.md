content:
  h1: "Pet Transport from Antigua and Barbuda to Australia"
  overview: |
    Antigua and Barbuda sits in DAFF's Group 3, the same approved tier as Jamaica, the Bahamas and Bermuda. That single classification means direct export to Australia is possible at all, without first establishing residency in some other approved territory. It does not make the process quick.

    A dog needs two rabies vaccinations, an RNATT blood titre test reading at least 0.5 IU/mL, and then a fixed 180-day wait counted from the day the blood sample lands at the laboratory, not the day it was drawn. On arrival, Mickleham quarantine runs a minimum of 10 days if a government vet verified the pet's identity before the blood draw, or 30 days if that step was missed. None of this is negotiable, and there is no direct flight to shorten the journey either.

  sections:
    - heading: "Group 3 status: real, but not a shortcut"
      body: |
        Being Group 3 approved means Antigua and Barbuda can export directly to Australia, unlike a non-approved country whose pets would first need months of residency somewhere on DAFF's approved list before they're even eligible to apply. That distinction matters, but it doesn't turn this into a light process.

        The RNATT titre test, the 180-day residency clock, and the DAFF import permit all apply in full, in the same sequence and to the same standard as they would for a much larger Group 3 country. Apply for the import permit first, at permit.daff.gov.au, before any Australia-specific vaccination work begins; starting the vet schedule before the permit conditions are confirmed risks repeating steps that don't match what DAFF actually asked for.

    - heading: "The identity verification step almost everyone should take"
      body: |
        One optional step decides whether Mickleham holds a pet for 10 days or 30: official identity verification by a government vet, completed in Antigua and Barbuda before the RNATT blood is drawn. Get this wrong, or skip it, and the minimum stay triples.

        It's a single extra appointment against three weeks of additional quarantine time, cost and stress for the animal. For a small island nation where government vet appointments may need booking further ahead than in a larger country, arrange this early rather than assuming it can be slotted in close to the blood draw date.

    - heading: "No direct flight, two workable routings"
      body: |
        There's no way to reach Australia from V.C. Bird International without at least two long-haul legs. American Airlines' nonstop Antigua-Miami service connects onward through the US network toward Los Angeles, where Qantas cargo picks up the transpacific run to Melbourne or Sydney. The alternative uses British Airways' nonstop BA2157 to Gatwick, then a Qantas or BA cargo connection from Heathrow.

        Confirm live animal cargo space on each separate leg before committing to a routing; a booking made months ahead on one carrier says nothing about space on the connecting flight.
