  h1: "Pet Transport from the United Arab Emirates to Belize"
  overview: |
    Belize's BAHA import permit is generous on paper: BZ$50, good for 90 days, covering up to ten cats and dogs on a single application. MOCCAE's own export permit, the document that actually gets a pet off UAE soil, is a different story. It expires in 30 days from issue, less than a third of BAHA's own window, and it's this shorter clock that ends up governing how the whole move gets sequenced.

    Landing in Belize itself is comparatively simple. No titre test applies from any origin, the UAE included, just a correctly timed rabies vaccination and a health certificate signed within 14 days of arrival. Getting a pet out of the UAE, through Miami, and into Belize City inside both permits' overlapping windows is the actual planning problem here, not anything Belize itself asks for.

  sections:
    - heading: "A 30-day permit meeting a 90-day one"
      body: |
        Book the flights first, then apply for both permits. That order matters more on this route than most, because the two documents run on completely different clocks. MOCCAE's export permit lapses after 30 days from issue; BAHA's import permit, by contrast, stays valid for 90 days and covers up to 10 pets on one application, plenty of runway if there's more than one animal moving.

        In practice, the MOCCAE side sets the real deadline. Apply for it once the Emirates or Etihad service to Miami and the connecting American Airlines leg into Belize City are both locked in, not before, so it doesn't expire before your pet actually boards.

    - heading: "Dubai to Miami, then a short hop to Belize City"

        Emirates SkyCargo's Dubai to Miami service runs close to daily, a flight of roughly 14 hours 45 minutes, one of the longest scheduled sectors the airline operates. From Miami, American Airlines covers Belize City in under two hours, part of American's expanded Belize network through 2025 and 2026.

Confirm American's current cargo pet acceptance on the second leg specifically before committing to dates, since that programme's availability has had gaps in the past.
