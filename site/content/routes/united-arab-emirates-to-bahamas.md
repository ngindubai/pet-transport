  h1: "Pet Transport from the United Arab Emirates to the Bahamas"
  overview: |
    Search for a Dubai to Nassau flight and nothing nonstop comes back, on Emirates or anyone else. The realistic path runs through London, pairing a Gulf carrier with British Airways' daily Heathrow-Nassau service, or through Miami on American Airlines. Either way, this is a two-leg move before it's anything else.

    The paperwork is lighter than the flight suggests. No titre test applies, and the Bahamas doesn't hold compliant pets in quarantine. What actually needs sorting early is BAHFSA's Click2Clear ePermit, a step that has to happen before the flight gets booked, not after.

  sections:
    - heading: "Why there's no direct flight, and what to book instead"
      body: |
        Nassau simply isn't a Gulf carrier destination. Emirates' own booking system will route a UAE-Nassau search through a US hub, sometimes adding well over a day of total transit. The workable pattern instead is a UAE-to-London leg on Emirates or Etihad, then British Airways' own direct Heathrow-Nassau service, seven days a week, through IAG Cargo.

        Miami is the other realistic option, with American Airlines running frequent onward connections to Nassau. It's often the shorter overall transit if a UAE-Miami connection lines up with the cargo booking window.

    - heading: "The vaccination rule that actually applies to a UAE-origin pet"
      body: |
        The UAE isn't recognised by the World Organisation for Animal Health as rabies-free, since rabies remains present in its stray animal population. That rules out the simpler six-month residency exemption some rabies-free-origin pets qualify for. Instead, BAHFSA applies its standard duration-based rule: a one-year vaccine given between 1 and 10 months before entry, or a three-year vaccine given between 1 and 34 months before entry.

        No titre test sits on top of that, regardless of origin. That's a genuinely lighter position than several other destinations in this build.

    - heading: "Two certificates, two different clocks"
      body: |
        MOCCAE's own export certificate runs for 30 days from issue. BAHFSA wants its international veterinary certificate dated within 48 hours of the actual flight, a far tighter window. Treat these as two separate appointments rather than one: get the MOCCAE paperwork sorted with margin to spare, then time the BAHFSA-format certificate to the confirmed departure date, not to when the MOCCAE process finishes.

    - heading: "What the two-leg routing costs"
      body: |
        Most of the spend on this route sits in the cargo booking itself, USD 1,600 to USD 3,200 depending on whether the pet travels via London or Miami and what size crate it needs. BAHFSA's ePermit fee is a fixed USD 11.20, a modest add-on next to the flight cost. All told, most owners land between USD 2,000 and USD 4,300 across an 8 to 12 week process, most of it spent lining up the connecting flight and the two certificates rather than waiting on any single mandatory clock.

faqs:
  - question: "Is there a direct flight from the UAE to the Bahamas?"
    answer: "No. No airline flies nonstop from Dubai or Abu Dhabi to Nassau. The realistic routing pairs a UAE-to-London leg with British Airways' direct Heathrow-Nassau service, or a UAE-to-Miami connection with American Airlines onward to Nassau."
  - question: "Does a pet need a rabies titre test to enter the Bahamas from the UAE?"
    answer: "No. The Bahamas doesn't require a titre test from pets arriving from any country of origin. A current rabies vaccination meeting BAHFSA's duration-based timing rule and a vet health certificate are what's checked instead."
  - question: "Why doesn't the UAE qualify for the Bahamas' simpler six-month rule?"
    answer: "That exemption applies only to pets from countries the World Organisation for Animal Health recognises as rabies-free. The UAE isn't on that list, since rabies is still present in its stray animal population, so the standard one-year or three-year vaccine-duration rule applies instead."
  - question: "What is the BAHFSA ePermit and when should I apply?"
    answer: "It's a mandatory import permit for every dog or cat entering the Bahamas, applied for online through the Click2Clear system. Register for an account by email well before booking flights, since account activation alone can take a few working days, on top of the USD 11.20 processing fee."
  - question: "How soon before the flight does the Bahamas-side health certificate need to be issued?"
