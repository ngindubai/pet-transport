        Thailand's combination of no titre test and no mandatory quarantine makes it the most convenient of the three for Japan-origin pets. Malaysia adds the variable of quarantine and potentially the titre test pathway depending on DVS classification. India requires 15 days quarantine in all cases.

        Japan's rabies-free status does not remove any requirements at the Thai end, but it does mean the Japanese export documentation is clean and the AQS exit inspection is typically smooth. Thai DLD officers are familiar with Japanese-origin pets and the process is well-established.

    - heading: "The DLD import permit: how to apply from Japan"
      body: |
        The DLD import permit is a straightforward email application to the Animal Quarantine Station at your Thai entry port. Most Japan-to-Thailand flights land at Bangkok Suvarnabhumi (BKK), so the application goes to Suvarnabhumi AQS. The email address and current application form are available on the Thai Ministry of Agriculture website.

        Include in your application: your pet's microchip number, vaccination records (rabies and all core vaccines), your travel date and the airline and flight number. The station reviews the documents and replies with a permit within about seven working days. The permit is valid for 60 days.

        Apply at least two to three weeks before travel to give yourself a buffer if the AQS station requests additional documentation. Once you have the permit, book your cargo space. Providing the permit reference to the cargo handler helps the check-in process at the departure airport.

    - heading: "Brachycephalic breeds: a note on summer travel"
      body: |
        JAL and ANA both apply temperature embargoes and breed restrictions to certain snub-nosed dogs. Japanese Shiba Inus are not brachycephalic, but French Bulldogs, Pugs, and Boston Terriers are, and these breeds face restrictions on all international cargo routes from Japan during summer months. Thai Airways applies similar policies.

        If you are moving a brachycephalic dog, check the airline's current breed restriction list and embargo dates before booking. The Tokyo summer (roughly June to September) sees the strictest embargoes. Book cargo earlier in the year or seek specialist routing advice if summer travel is unavoidable.

        For dogs that are not brachycephalic, the temperature controls in modern cargo holds are adequate for this route. Japan Airlines operates well-maintained live animal facilities at Narita and Haneda. The direct flight time from Tokyo to Bangkok is approximately seven hours, manageable for most healthy adult dogs.
