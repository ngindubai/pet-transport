  sections:
    - heading: "What Azerbaijan requires at GYD"
      body: |
        Baku's Heydar Aliyev International Airport (GYD) is where all international live cargo enters Azerbaijan. The State Veterinary Service under the Ministry of Agriculture inspects documents on arrival. For personal companion pets from Latvia, the requirements are a current rabies vaccination and a health certificate from the Latvian PVD.

        No titre test is required, and there is no routine quarantine for compliant pets. Azerbaijan's border inspection is documented and thorough, but it is not onerous for pets arriving with correct paperwork. Get the certificate format confirmed with the PVD before travel, particularly whether Azerbaijan requires any specific endorsement wording.

        Note that Azerbaijan restricts certain dog breeds. If your dog could be classified as a fighting breed or similar, check with the State Veterinary Service before booking.

    - heading: "Latvia's PVD certificate for non-EU travel"
      body: |
        The EU pet passport that covers intra-EU travel is not valid for Azerbaijan. For this move, a Partikas un veterinarais dienests (PVD) export health certificate from an authorised official veterinarian is the required document.

        The PVD at pvd.gov.lv maintains a list of vets authorised to issue official export health certificates. Not all vets in Latvia hold this status. Contact the PVD before your vet appointment to confirm authorisation and to check whether Azerbaijan requires any specific format for the certificate. The certificate must be issued within 10 days of travel.

    - heading: "Routing from Riga to Baku"
      body: |
        Riga (RIX) benefits from airBaltic's extensive European network. The most practical routing for pet cargo from Riga to Baku goes through Istanbul Ataturk (IST) via a European hub connection, with Turkish Airlines Cargo handling the IST to GYD sector. Turkish Airlines has established live animal handling at IST and regular cargo flights to Azerbaijan.

        An alternative routes through Frankfurt (FRA) using airBaltic to FRA and then Lufthansa Cargo to GYD, with or without a stop at IST. Both options are viable. Summer heat restrictions at IST between June and September can affect live cargo. If you are travelling in peak summer, confirm seasonal live animal cargo acceptance with your carrier before committing.
