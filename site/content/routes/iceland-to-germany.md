content:
  h1: "Pet Transport from Iceland to Germany"
  overview: |
    An Icelandic cat with a current rabies vaccination and a MAST-endorsed certificate clears German entry on the same terms as a cat from Bavaria moving to Berlin. No titre test, no quarantine. Iceland's EEA status covers this direction cleanly, which makes the regulatory side of an Iceland-to-Germany move genuinely simple.

    The friction sits with the flight, not the paperwork. Icelandair, the obvious carrier on the Keflavik to Frankfurt sector, stopped carrying pets as checked or excess baggage on 1 November 2024. Anyone assuming they can book a pet the way they used to needs to know that changed.

  sections:
    - heading: "Why this leg skips the titre test entirely"
      body: |
        Iceland participates in the EU pet travel framework as an EEA state, alongside Norway and Liechtenstein. That status carries through to outbound moves: a microchip, a current rabies vaccination with the standard 21-day wait, and a MAST-endorsed certificate on the EU form cover a pet moving from Iceland into any EU member state, Germany included.

        There is no rabies titre serology test on this direction and no quarantine at the German end. Compare that with the Germany-to-Iceland direction on this site, where the reverse move triggers a mandatory 14-day isolation period regardless of paperwork. The asymmetry is real: Iceland's own biosecurity rules are far stricter on pets coming in than the EU's rules are on Icelandic pets going out.

    - heading: "Getting a pet onto the Keflavik to Frankfurt sector"
      body: |
        Icelandair and Lufthansa both fly Keflavik to Frankfurt directly, a flight of under four hours. Until late 2024, Icelandair let owners check a pet in as excess baggage at the airport, much like a piece of oversized luggage. That process closed on 1 November 2024. A pet moving with Icelandair now has to be booked as manifested air cargo through Icelandair Cargo, a separate process with its own lead times and paperwork.

        Lufthansa remains a straightforward alternative on the same city pair, and receives pets into its purpose-built Frankfurt Animal Lounge on arrival. If your travel dates are tight, it is worth checking both carriers rather than assuming Icelandair will handle the pet the same way it handles your own luggage.
