content:
  h1: "Pet Transport from Iceland to Germany"
  overview: |
    This is the easy direction. No titre test, no quarantine, no import permit. Iceland sits on the same EU-equivalent list as the pets it exports, so a dog or cat leaving Keflavik for Frankfurt is, on paper, making an intra-Community move rather than a third-country import.

    That asymmetry surprises people who've just been through the stricter inbound process for the same route. Iceland demands far more of pets arriving from the EU than Germany demands of pets arriving from Iceland, because the EU accepts Iceland's own health documentation under Regulation (EU) No 577/2013.

    The flight is the part that needs the most attention now. Since Icelandair stopped carrying pets as checked baggage in late 2024, Lufthansa's direct Keflavik-Frankfurt service has become the more dependable passenger option for most dogs and cats.
  sections:
    - heading: "Why this leg skips the quarantine and titre test"
      body: |
        Iceland is listed in Part 1 of Annex II to Regulation (EU) No 577/2013, the same category as an EU member state, for the purposes of pets leaving Iceland. That listing means a pet travelling from Iceland to Germany with a valid Iceland-issued EU-format pet passport, or an equivalent official health certificate, is treated the same way as a pet moving between two EU countries.

        No rabies titre test, no import permit, and no quarantine on arrival in Germany. The one document to check is whether your pet already holds an Iceland-issued EU-format passport; if not, your vet can issue an official health certificate instead, which covers the same ground for a single trip.
    - heading: "Booking a flight that will carry your pet"
      body: |
        Lufthansa flies direct from Keflavik to Frankfurt and carries pets both in the cabin, for animals under 8kg including the carrier, and in the temperature-controlled hold for larger dogs, registered at least 72 hours ahead. Snub-nosed breeds cannot travel in the hold on Lufthansa.

        Icelandair also connects Keflavik with Frankfurt, Munich, Hamburg and Berlin, but its passenger aircraft no longer carry pets as checked baggage, a policy change from 1 November 2024. Icelandair Cargo can still move live animals out of Iceland, but through its freighter network via Los Angeles or Liege rather than a direct German routing, so it tends to suit larger dogs or cases where Lufthansa's hold restrictions rule a pet out.
faqs:
  - question: "Does my pet need a rabies titre test to enter Germany from Iceland?"
    answer: "No. Because Iceland is listed as equivalent to the EU under Regulation (EU) No 577/2013 for outbound pet movement, this counts as an intra-Community move. A current rabies vaccination and valid documentation are enough; no titre test is needed."
  - question: "Will my pet need to quarantine on arrival in Germany?"
    answer: "No. Germany does not quarantine pets arriving from Iceland. This is the opposite of the inbound direction, where Iceland's own rules require a mandatory 14-day quarantine regardless of origin."
  - question: "Is an EU pet passport issued in Iceland valid for entering Germany?"
    answer: "Yes. An Iceland-issued pet passport in the EU format, complying with Regulation (EU) No 577/2013, is accepted for entry into Germany and the rest of the EU. If your pet doesn't have one, an Icelandic vet can issue an official health certificate to cover a single trip instead."
  - question: "Can I fly directly from Keflavik to Frankfurt with my pet?"
    answer: "With Lufthansa, yes, in the cabin for pets under 8kg or in the hold as excess baggage for larger dogs, registered at least 72 hours ahead. Icelandair flies the same route for passengers but stopped carrying pets as checked baggage in November 2024, so it now routes live animals via its cargo freighters instead."
