content:
  h1: "Pet Transport from the Dominican Republic to Greece: 2026 Guide"
  overview: |
    Ninety days. That is the wait a Dominican Republic-origin pet faces after the blood draw before Greek border vets will clear it for entry. The Dominican Republic has not been assessed as an EU-listed country for pet travel, so the full unlisted third country process applies: a FAVN or ELISA titre test at an EU-approved laboratory, a fixed 90-day wait from the draw date, and an EU-format health certificate endorsed by DIGEGA.

    None of this is optional and none of it can be shortened. Plan for four to five months from the first vet appointment to boarding a flight, and start the rabies vaccination and blood draw as early as your travel timeline allows.

    There is no direct flight from Santo Domingo to Athens, so pets travel as cargo through Madrid or Paris. Iberia and Air France both handle the connection, and either carrier's cargo team can confirm crate requirements once your paperwork is in order.

  sections:
    - heading: "Why the Dominican Republic triggers the full titre-test process"
      body: |
        EU pet travel rules split third countries into two groups: those whose rabies control systems the EU has formally assessed as equivalent to its own, and those that have not been assessed or do not meet the bar. The Dominican Republic sits in the second group, so Greece treats it the same way it treats any other unlisted country.

        That means a FAVN or ELISA test, run at a laboratory on the EU's approved list, with blood drawn at least 30 days after the rabies vaccination and a result of at least 0.5 IU/ml. The 90-day wait then starts from the date of that blood draw, not from when the result comes back or when the certificate is issued.

    - heading: "Getting the DIGEGA export certificate and booking the flight"
      body: |
        DIGEGA, the Dominican Republic's Direccion General de Ganaderia, authorises the vets who can issue the export health certificate in EU format. That certificate must be issued within 10 days of travel and endorsed by DIGEGA before departure from Las Americas International Airport (SDQ).

        Since no non-stop service links Santo Domingo and Athens, your pet flies as manifest cargo via a European hub. Iberia's Madrid connection and Air France's Paris connection are the two established options; both carriers apply IATA Live Animals Regulations and can advise on crate sizing well before your travel date.
