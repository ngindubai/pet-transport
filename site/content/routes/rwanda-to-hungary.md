  sections:
    - heading: "The Rwanda export process"
      body: |
        No formal export permit is required for a personal pet leaving Kigali. The document that matters is the EU-format health certificate, issued by an RAB-authorised veterinarian and endorsed by the Rwanda Agriculture and Animal Resources Development Board. It has to be the EU format, not a generic Rwandan export certificate, and it's only valid for 10 days from issue. Time the appointment and endorsement carefully against your actual flight date so the certificate is still valid on arrival in Budapest, not just on departure from Kigali.

    - heading: "Flying from Kigali to Budapest"
      body: |
        RwandAir and Brussels Airlines both fly Kigali direct to Brussels, giving a single-connection path onward to Budapest, and it's worth pricing both since they cover the same first leg. KLM Cargo via Nairobi and Amsterdam is an alternate drawing on the KLM/Kenya Airways cargo relationship out of East Africa. Ethiopian Airlines Cargo via Addis Ababa rounds out the options for Central Europe. Confirm live animal handling specifically on the connecting flight into Budapest, not just the outbound leg from Kigali, since acceptance can differ by sector.

    - heading: "NEBIH's requirements: the full unlisted-country sequence"
      body: |
        Hungary is an EU member state, but that gives Rwanda's pets no shortcut. NEBIH, the National Food Chain Safety Office of Hungary, applies EU Regulation (EU) 2020/692 in full: chip first, vaccination after, a 30-day wait before blood can be drawn for the FAVN or ELISA titre test, a result of at least 0.5 IU/ml from an EU-approved lab, then a mandatory 90-day wait from the draw date. Conditional quarantine can be applied if documentation is incomplete or if the 90-day period hasn't actually finished when the pet arrives, so there's no informal way to shave time off the process.
