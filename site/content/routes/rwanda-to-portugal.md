content:
  h1: "Pet Transport from Rwanda to Portugal"
  overview: "Lisbon Airport handles arriving pets through a dedicated cargo terminal, where DGAV officials check the documentation rather than at passenger arrivals. Getting a pet from Rwanda to that point takes the full EU unlisted-country sequence: microchip, rabies vaccination, an FAVN titre test, and a 90-day wait from the blood draw. There's no separate import permit and no routine quarantine once the paperwork is correct. Expect 20 to 26 weeks in total, and route the flight through Addis Ababa, Brussels, or Nairobi."

  sections:
    - heading: "Kigali to Lisbon: airline options"
      body: |
        Ethiopian Airlines Cargo runs a well-established path via Addis Ababa, connecting onward to Lisbon through its European partner network, and it's usually the first option worth pricing on this route. Brussels Airlines flies KGL-BRU direct, with Portugal reachable through Brussels' regular onward connections. KLM Cargo's joint venture with Kenya Airways offers a structured alternative via Nairobi and Amsterdam. RwandAir also serves Brussels from Kigali, a useful second option if your first choice is fully booked on the date you need. Whichever carrier you go with, confirm the connecting leg accepts live animal cargo, and build in four to six weeks of lead time for the booking.

    - heading: "The EU-unlisted sequence and the 90-day wait"
      body: |
        Portugal is an EU member state, and Rwanda is not on the EU's approved list, so every animal has to go through the same steps: microchip before vaccination, a 30-day wait before blood can be drawn for the titre test, a minimum result of 0.5 IU/ml, and then 90 days from that draw date before entry into Portugal. It's rarely the vet steps that trip people up. Most of that can be done in six or seven weeks. The wait afterwards is what stretches the timeline to five or six months, and it has to be counted from the draw date, never the day the lab confirms the number.

    - heading: "Two authorities either end: RAB and DGAV"
      body: |
        On the Rwanda side, RAB authorises the vet who issues the export health certificate and endorses it before departure, no separate export permit needed for personal pets. On arrival, DGAV, Portugal's food and veterinary authority, checks that certificate at Lisbon's cargo terminal, not in the main passenger building. The certificate must be in EU format and still within its 10-day validity window from the date of examination when the flight lands. Missing either endorsement, or letting the certificate age past 10 days, is enough to hold the animal at the terminal until it's resolved.
