  sections:
    - heading: "The EU unlisted country process for Azerbaijan"
      body: |
        Belgium applies EU Regulation 576/2013 to all pets from unlisted third countries. Azerbaijan is unlisted. The protocol: ISO microchip implanted before any vaccination, rabies vaccination, FAVN titre test with a result of at least 0.5 IU/ml at an EU-approved laboratory, blood drawn at least 30 days after vaccination, then a 90-day wait from the blood draw date before Belgium entry.

        FASFC (Federal Agency for the Safety of the Food Chain) inspects incoming pets at Brussels Airport (BRU) cargo terminal. The EU health certificate endorsed by Azerbaijan's Ministry of Agriculture must accompany the pet and be valid on arrival.

    - heading: "AZAL direct: Baku to Brussels without a hub"
      body: |
        Azerbaijan Airlines (AZAL) operates a direct service from Heydar Aliyev International Airport (GYD, Baku) to Brussels Airport (BRU). If AZAL accepts live animal cargo on this route, it is the most efficient option: a direct flight with no hub transit and no multi-carrier coordination.

        Contact AZAL Cargo directly to confirm live animal cargo acceptance, weight limits, and any seasonal restrictions on the GYD-BRU route. Policies can vary from the passenger schedule.

        If AZAL direct is not available for pet cargo on your preferred dates, Turkish Airlines via Istanbul is the next most practical option.

    - heading: "Titre test and the Azerbaijan laboratory situation"
      body: |
        FAVN titre testing requires an EU-approved laboratory. Confirm with your vet in Baku whether any approved laboratories are accessible locally or in a nearby country (Georgia and Turkey are options). If an approved lab is accessible without international blood shipping, this simplifies logistics.

        If no local option exists, blood must be drawn at your vet and shipped cold to an approved lab abroad. The 90-day wait starts from the blood draw date in Baku regardless of where the lab is located. Plan for two to four weeks of laboratory turnaround in addition to the 90-day wait.
