content:
  h1: "Pet Transport from the United Arab Emirates to Guatemala"
  overview: |
    Landing in Guatemala from the UAE is the easy part. MAGA's general guidance for pets arriving from any origin asks for a rabies vaccination card, a signed health certificate, and a small OIRSA inspection fee, about USD 14, paid at the border.

    Leaving the UAE is where the timing has to be exact. MOCCAE's export permit is only valid for 30 days from the date it's issued, and every pet has to fly out as manifest cargo through an IATA-certified agent, arranged around a nonstop Emirates SkyCargo run from Dubai to Miami before a second carrier finishes the job into Guatemala City.

  sections:
    - heading: "A 30-day permit against a two-leg flight"
      body: |
        Book the flights first, then apply for the MOCCAE export permit. That order matters because the permit's 30-day validity window is fixed from the date of issue, not from your intended travel date. Apply too early and it can lapse before the animal ever boards; apply once the Emirates-to-Miami sector and the onward Guatemala City connection are both locked in, and the timing works itself out.

        Emirates SkyCargo's Dubai to Miami service runs close to daily, a flight of roughly 14 hours 45 minutes, which at least removes one variable from the plan. What isn't fixed is the connecting leg: American Airlines Cargo direct from Miami is the more reliable option, with Avianca as a fallback.

    - heading: "Why Guatemala barely asks any questions"
      body: |
        Compare this to a UAE-to-UK or UAE-to-US move and the difference is stark. Guatemala doesn't require a titre test from any origin country, doesn't publish an import permit process beyond the port-of-entry OIRSA inspection, and its documented bilateral detail (specific vaccines, a named parasite treatment, a pre-departure import permit) is written for US-origin pets, not UAE ones.

        That light landing-side process is genuinely good news, but it can lull owners into treating the whole move as low-effort. It isn't. The departure side, with its manifest-cargo rule and its 30-day permit clock, is where this route actually needs planning.

    - heading: "Choosing between a Dubai or Abu Dhabi departure"
      body: |
        Both emirates route through the same Emirates SkyCargo Miami service in practice, but the municipality-level registration steps differ slightly: Dubai Municipality handles registration and the local vaccination record for Dubai-based pets, ADAFSA does the same for Abu Dhabi. Whichever emirate the pet is registered in, the federal MOCCAE export permit and inspection sit on top of that local step, not instead of it.

        Get the local municipality paperwork settled first. MOCCAE's own export permit application moves faster once that groundwork is already in place.
