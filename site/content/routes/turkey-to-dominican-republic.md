    Santo Domingo doesn't sit on any of Turkish Airlines' non-stop routes from Istanbul, so this journey runs through a connection, most commonly a US gateway, adding real hours to what's otherwise a light regulatory process.         Turkish Airlines' own network to Santo Domingo runs via a US hub such as New York JFK, and other carriers including Pegasus, JetBlue and Avianca also cover the route with a single stop. JetBlue's strong Santo Domingo presence makes it a sensible choice for the onward leg if the itinerary allows a carrier switch at the connecting hub.

        The Dominican Republic only applies its rabies titre test requirement to arrivals it classifies as high-rabies-risk, and Turkey doesn't fall into that category. Up to 5 personal pets can also enter without a formal import permit, leaving a compliant vaccination record and a current TRGM-endorsed health certificate as the core requirement. Inspection happens on arrival at either Las Americas International in Santo Domingo or Punta Cana International, depending on where the connecting flight lands.
