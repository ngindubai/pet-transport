content:
  h1: "Pet Transport from Canada to Albania"
  overview: |
    Canada sits in Part 2 of Annex II to the EU's own rabies regulation, the same bracket as the United States. Albania's import framework, closely aligned with the EU model even though the country isn't a member state, extends the same light treatment to a Canadian-vaccinated pet that it gives a US one: a 21-day wait after vaccination, no blood test.

    There's a catch worth taking seriously before booking anything. That exemption depends on the connecting airport as much as the departure city, and the obvious first-choice routing through Istanbul runs through a country whose own status on that same EU list isn't the reassuring kind.

  sections:
    - heading: "Why no titre test, in principle"
      body: |
        Implementing Regulation (EU) 2026/636 sets out Annex II, the EU's list of third countries and territories treated as low-risk for rabies. Canada appears in Part 2 of that list. Albania's own import rules, built around the same framework, treat a pet vaccinated in a Part 2 country the way GB and the EU itself do: current vaccination, a short wait, nothing more.

        That's a genuinely lighter position than most of the routes into Albania built on this site, where an unlisted origin means a titre test and a multi-month wait. Canada avoids all of that on paper.

    - heading: "The routing question that actually matters here"
      body: |
        Albania's own guidance ties the exemption to where the pet travels from and transits through, not just where it started. Turkish Airlines is the obvious first booking, since it already connects Toronto and Montreal to Tirana through Istanbul with an established Pet Lounge for the layover. The complication is that Turkey itself doesn't appear to sit on the EU's Annex II list, which raises a real question about whether routing a Canadian pet through Istanbul preserves the exemption Canada's own vaccination record would otherwise earn.

        Air Canada's Frankfurt sector, connecting onward with Lufthansa to Tirana, sidesteps the question entirely by keeping the whole journey inside the EU's own rabies framework. It's a longer routing on paper and, for anyone who wants certainty over convenience, the safer one.

    - heading: "What the certificate and the paperwork actually need"
      body: |
        A Canadian accredited vet issues the health certificate, and CFIA endorsement is available where a destination requires it, though Albania doesn't publish a standalone import permit for an owner travelling with their own pet. The document should be dated within 10 days of the health exam Albania checks on arrival, so the vet appointment needs to sit close to the flight, not weeks ahead of it.

        Confirm the current Annex II position with AKU or the receiving vet before finalising the booking. It's a five-minute check that avoids discovering the titre-test question at the airport rather than months in advance.
