  overview: "Nepal is classified as an unlisted country under UK pet travel rules, which means the FAVN titre test and 90-day waiting period are part of the process. Plan for four to five months. Documentation runs through Nepal's Department of Livestock Services (DLS) for the export permit and certificate endorsement. Cargo departs from Kathmandu Tribhuvan International Airport, routing through Doha (Qatar Airways), Istanbul (Turkish Airlines), or Dubai (Emirates).   sections:
    - heading: "Kathmandu as a cargo departure point"
      body: |
        Kathmandu Tribhuvan International Airport (KTM) is Nepal's only international airport. It operates at 1,338 metres altitude, which affects cargo handling and temperature management. The airport handles international cargo, but its capacity and carrier frequency are more limited than Delhi or Mumbai.

        Qatar Airways from KTM via Doha to London is one of the most reliable routings for Nepal to UK pet cargo. The Doha transit is well-organised for live animals, and Qatar Cargo has experience with South Asian pet shipments. Turkish Airlines via Istanbul is a solid alternative. Emirates via Dubai works but avoid summer months due to heat embargoes.

        Because of KTM's smaller cargo environment, confirm live animal acceptance with your chosen carrier in advance of booking, not after. Cargo space fills on popular routes and the airline's stated policies may differ from what is available on specific dates.

    - heading: "DLS: Nepal's livestock authority and what it requires"
      body: |
        The Department of Livestock Services (DLS) under Nepal's Ministry of Agriculture and Livestock Development handles the endorsement of health certificates and issues export permits for animals leaving Nepal. Apply at least six weeks before travel. In some cases, DLS requires the animal to be presented at a DLS office or government facility for inspection before issuing the permit.

        The FAVN titre test is the UK's requirement. Your Nepalese vet takes the blood sample, which is sent to an APHA-approved laboratory (some laboratories in Kathmandu work with accredited overseas labs for this type of testing, or samples can be shipped). The DLS certificate endorsement and the titre test preparation should run in parallel to avoid extending the total timeline unnecessarily.
