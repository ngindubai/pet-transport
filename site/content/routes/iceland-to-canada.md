content:
  h1: "Pet Transport from Iceland to Canada"
  overview: |
    Nine countries make CFIA's own rabies-free list. Iceland is one of them, alongside Australia, Fiji, Finland, Ireland, Japan, New Zealand, Sweden and the UK. That single fact means a pet leaving Iceland for Canada skips the rabies vaccine, the titre test and any quarantine, provided the paperwork proves the animal actually lived in Iceland throughout the qualifying period.

    It also happens to be one of the few Iceland routes with a real nonstop flight. Icelandair flies Keflavik to Toronto Pearson direct, no hub, no connection, which is not something this site can say about Iceland's routes to Australia, Singapore or several EU destinations.

  sections:
    - heading: "Why this is one of the easiest routes on the site"
      body: |
        CFIA recognises exactly nine countries or territories as rabies-free for personal pet imports: Australia, Fiji, Finland, Iceland, Ireland, Japan, New Zealand, Sweden and the United Kingdom. A pet from any of these does not need a rabies vaccine to enter Canada. Instead, a vet-issued certificate confirms rabies has not been present in the country of origin for the past six months and that the animal lived there throughout that window, or since birth.

        No titre test follows from that either, since the whole point of a titre test is confirming vaccine response, and there is no vaccine requirement to confirm. No quarantine applies on arrival. The main practical task is timing the health certificate correctly: it has to be issued within 10 days of the animal's arrival in Canada, not its departure from Iceland.

    - heading: "A direct flight, for once"
      body: |
        Most Iceland routes covered on this site involve at least one hub, often two. Iceland to Australia needs a minimum of two connections. Iceland to Singapore is worse. Iceland to Canada is different: Icelandair operates Keflavik to Toronto Pearson nonstop, close to daily depending on the season, a flight of roughly 5 hours 45 minutes.

        That doesn't mean the pet travels in the cabin or the hold as ordinary baggage. Icelandair stopped accepting pets as checked or excess baggage across its entire network on 1 November 2024, so even this direct sector moves as manifested cargo through Icelandair Cargo. The booking desk and lead times differ from a standard passenger fare, but the routing itself is genuinely simple compared with most of Iceland's other long-haul connections.
