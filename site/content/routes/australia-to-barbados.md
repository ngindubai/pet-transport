  h1: "Pet Transport from Australia to Barbados"
  overview: |
    Australia's own rabies-free status earns it a spot on Barbados's Category 1 list, the same lower-risk tier as the UK, the US and Canada. That means a 35-day wait after vaccination stands in for the FAVN titre test, and there's no quarantine at the other end for a pet with the right paperwork.

    Getting there is the harder half. No airline connects Australia to Barbados in fewer than two legs, and every workable routing on this site goes through London, where British Airways picks up the final nonstop run into Bridgetown.

  sections:
    - heading: "Why Australia clears Barbados's Category 1 bar easily"
      body: |
        Barbados splits import countries into two tiers based on rabies risk. Category 1 covers roughly 70 countries it treats as rabies-free or low risk; Category 2 covers everywhere else, and comes with a FAVN titre test and a much longer wait. Australia sits comfortably in Category 1, a status that reflects its own strict biosecurity record rather than anything Barbados has to verify case by case.

        Practically, that means an Australian vet vaccinates the pet, the 35-day clock runs, and the process moves straight to the export certificate. No blood sample sent to a laboratory, no result to wait on. It's a shorter runway than the reverse journey, where DAFF's own rules demand far more.

    - heading: "Two legs, one transfer, and why London is the practical choice"
      body: |
        Qantas or a partner carrier gets a pet from Sydney or Melbourne to London, typically with one stop through a hub like Singapore, Dubai or Doha depending on the specific interline agreement in use that season. From Heathrow, British Airways' nonstop Bridgetown service, the same aircraft used on the UK's own Barbados route, completes the journey.

        Routing through the US instead is technically possible, since American Airlines flies Miami to BGI, but it adds a second country's transit rules to the mix on top of Barbados's own conditions. London keeps the trip to a single change of carrier rather than two separate customs environments.

    - heading: "The paperwork chain that actually gets a pet cleared"
      body: |
        DAFF's side is straightforward: a government-endorsed vet inspection within 5 days of export, plus the export permit itself. Barbados layers its own import permit on top, a flat BDS $25.00 through Veterinary Services, valid for 6 months once granted.

        One step is easy to overlook. The exporter or agent has to give Barbados's Veterinary Services at least 3 working days' notice before the pet lands, including the flight number and a Barbados address. Missing that notice can delay clearance even when the certificate and permit are both in order.
