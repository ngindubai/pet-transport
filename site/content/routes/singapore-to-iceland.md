content:
  h1: "Pet Transport from Singapore to Iceland"
  overview: |
    No airline flies a pet directly from Changi to Keflavik. Every move on this route transits at least one European hub, most workably Frankfurt, before the final short hop north. That single fact shapes the routing more than any regulation does.

    On the paperwork side, Singapore gets a better deal than most non-EU origins. MAST names Singapore explicitly on its Category 1 list, the same tier as Germany and France, which caps the pre-export titre test wait at 30 days rather than 90. What that status does not touch is the 14-day quarantine every pet faces on arrival in Iceland, Category 1 origin or not.

  sections:
    - heading: "Singapore's odd position on MAST's own list"
      body: |
        MAST classifies countries by rabies risk for import purposes, and Singapore sits in Category 1, the more favourable tier, alongside most of the EU, the UK, Switzerland, Norway, the UAE and Japan. That means the titre test blood draw needs to happen at least 30 days after vaccination, and once the result clears at 0.5 IU/ml or above, there is no additional fixed wait before travel.

        The relationship does not run the other way. Singapore's own AVS places Iceland in its Schedule II, controlled-risk tier, not the negligible-risk group reserved for Australia, New Zealand, Ireland and the UK. A pet moving from Iceland to Singapore faces a 90-day post-titre wait; a pet moving from Singapore to Iceland faces only 30 days on that specific step. The two authorities simply do not classify each other's rabies status the same way.

    - heading: "Fourteen days in isolation, whatever your Category status"
      body: |
        Being on MAST's Category 1 list has one job: it shortens the pre-export clock. It does nothing to the mandatory 14-day quarantine that every pet, from every origin, serves at Einangrunarstoedin i Reykjanesbae or Einangrunarstoedin Mosel once it lands. Budget the roughly ISK 220,000 dog quarantine fee, about EUR 1,450, into your plan from the outset rather than as a surprise.

        Book isolation station space early. Both facilities take pets on a limited intake schedule, and turning up with a flight booked but no confirmed quarantine slot is a genuine risk on this route given how far the pet has already travelled by that point.

    - heading: "Routing a long-haul move through a single short-haul bottleneck"
      body: |
        Singapore Airlines Cargo covers the long-haul leg out of Changi to major European hubs including Frankfurt and Amsterdam. From there, Lufthansa runs Frankfurt to Keflavik direct, roughly 8 times a week, and receives pets into its own Frankfurt Animal Lounge for the transfer between the long and short legs.

        Icelandair also flies Frankfurt to Keflavik, but since 1 November 2024 it only accepts pets as manifested cargo, not checked baggage, the same as on every other Icelandair route. Given the number of separate bookings involved across a Singapore to Iceland move, working with an agent who can coordinate the export licence, the cargo legs and the Iceland-side quarantine booking together is worth the fee.
