content:
  h1: "Pet Transport Australia to Jamaica"
  overview: |
    Two things stack against each other on this route: Jamaica's own methodical, roughly five-month import process, and Australia's total lack of a direct or even simple path to the Caribbean. Put them together and this becomes one of the longer-lead corridors covered anywhere in this build.

    Jamaica's Veterinary Services Division requires a Preliminary Application Form approved before an owner even begins the vaccination and FAVN titre test sequence. Australia's side adds a transpacific leg to Los Angeles, then a separate onward connection through Miami into Kingston.

  sections:
    - heading: "Jamaica's process runs on its own clock, regardless of distance"
      body: |
        VSD's Preliminary Application Form has to be approved before an owner starts the rabies vaccination and titre test steps that count towards entry. This applies identically whether the pet is coming from next door or from the other side of the world; Jamaica doesn't shorten its process for a longer journey. Build in 4-5 months minimum from the very first step.

    - heading: "The titre test, quarantine, and why neither shortens"
      body: |
        A FAVN titre test, drawn 30 days after the qualifying vaccination at a VSD-approved laboratory, needs a result of at least 0.5 IU/ml. A pet over 12 months old receiving its first rabies vaccination doesn't qualify until three months after that dose. On arrival, expect up to 14 days at the Plumb Point quarantine facility, with early release at VSD's discretion rather than a guarantee.

    - heading: "Two long-haul legs, two airlines"
      body: |
        There's no way to avoid a genuine multi-leg journey here. Qantas covers the transpacific run from Sydney or Melbourne into Los Angeles, and from there American Airlines Cargo, or a similar US carrier, handles the connection through Miami into Kingston. Confirm live animal transfer arrangements at both stops well before booking; this isn't a route to leave loosely planned.
