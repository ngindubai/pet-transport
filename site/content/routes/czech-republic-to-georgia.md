  overview: |
    Georgia has become one of the more accessible Caucasus destinations for pet owners relocating from EU countries. The National Food Agency (NFSA) does not require quarantine for pets arriving with correct documentation, and there is no titre test requirement for animals coming from the Czech Republic.

    The process from Prague centres on two main tasks: getting the SVS-endorsed health certificate right, and booking the Turkish Airlines cargo routing via Istanbul. Both are straightforward with enough lead time. Most moves start preparations three to five weeks out.

    This guide covers everything from the SVS export procedure at Prague Václav Havel Airport to the NFSA arrival inspection at Tbilisi International Airport.

  sections:
    - heading: "Georgia's import requirements: what NFSA checks at TBS"
      body: |
        Georgia's National Food Agency (NFSA, nfa.gov.ge) carries out veterinary inspections at Tbilisi International Airport (TBS). The inspection covers three core checks: the microchip scan, the health certificate validity, and the rabies vaccination dates.

        The health certificate must be within its 10-day validity on the day of arrival, and the rabies vaccination must have been given at least 21 days before entry but no more than 11 months before. Both conditions must be met simultaneously, so check the dates carefully when booking travel rather than after.

        Georgia does not require a titre test for pets arriving from EU member states, which removes one of the longest lead-time steps from the preparation process. No import permit is needed for personal pets. The NFSA inspection at TBS is a document check, not a detention period: compliant pets are released on the day.

    - heading: "Getting the SVS export certificate from Czech Republic"
      body: |
        Czech pet exports use the EU Regulation 576/2013 health certificate format, issued by an SVS-authorised official vet and endorsed by SVS (Státní veterinární správa). These are two separate steps and need to be planned in sequence.

        First, locate an SVS-authorised official vet in your area, as not all veterinary practices hold this authorisation. The SVS website (svscr.cz) lists authorised vets by region. Book the appointment within 10 days of your travel date, as the certificate's validity is tied to the examination date.

        After the vet issues the certificate, it goes to the SVS regional office for endorsement. Allow one to two working days for this. The endorsement confirms the vet's authorisation and the accuracy of the documentation. Georgian NFSA inspectors at TBS expect to see both the vet's signature and the SVS endorsement stamp.

    - heading: "Prague to Tbilisi: routing options and what to expect"
      body: |
        Prague Václav Havel Airport (PRG) has no direct flights to Tbilisi International Airport (TBS). The standard routing for cargo pets is Turkish Airlines via Istanbul Atatürk (IST), connecting the PRG-IST and IST-TBS legs under Turkish Airlines Cargo.

        Turkish Airlines Cargo has live animal procedures at all three airports on this route and is the most commonly used carrier for pets moving between Central Europe and the South Caucasus. Book cargo space four to six weeks in advance: summer and the pre-Christmas period are particularly busy. Confirm your pet's crate dimensions meet IATA requirements before the cargo booking is finalised.

        Lufthansa via Frankfurt is a practical alternative, especially for larger dogs that benefit from the Frankfurt Animal Lounge during long transits. The FRA to TBS leg would typically connect via IST regardless, so the overall journey time is longer, but the layover facilities at FRA are excellent.
