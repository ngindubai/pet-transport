  h1: "Pet Transport from Finland to Georgia"
  overview: |
    Georgia is one of the more accessible Caucasus destinations for pet owners leaving Finland. The country does not require a titre test from EU-origin pets, which keeps the preparation timeline to 3 to 5 weeks from start to travel day, assuming your pet's microchip and vaccinations are already in order.

    The NFSA (National Food Agency of Georgia) inspects arriving animals at Tbilisi International Airport (TBS). For compliant arrivals, inspection is quick and no quarantine is imposed. The paperwork requirements are clear: a valid ISO microchip, current rabies vaccination, and an official health certificate endorsed by Ruokavirasto.

    Georgia is increasingly popular for expat moves, particularly from Northern Europe, which means the route via Istanbul is well-worn for cargo animals. The logistical challenge is the transit: there are no direct flights from Helsinki to Tbilisi, so cargo coordination across two legs is required.

  sections:
    - heading: "NFSA requirements and what to expect on arrival in Tbilisi"
      body: |
        Georgia's NFSA (National Food Agency, nfa.gov.ge) handles veterinary inspections at Tbilisi International Airport. On arrival, a veterinary officer will scan your pet's microchip, check the vaccination dates against the entry date, and review the health certificate. For an EU-origin pet arriving with correct documentation, the check is typically completed within an hour.

        No quarantine is applied to compliant arrivals. The inspection is a formality, not a holding procedure. Where issues arise, they are almost always documentation-related: expired vaccinations, missing endorsements, or a microchip that was implanted after the rabies vaccination was recorded.

        Keep a copy of all documents accessible during transit, but present only originals to NFSA on arrival. Georgia accepts the EU-format health certificate issued and endorsed in Finland.

    - heading: "Why the microchip-before-vaccination sequence matters"
      body: |
        This is the most common and most avoidable mistake in international pet transport: the microchip must be implanted before the rabies vaccination is administered. When NFSA scans the microchip at TBS and checks it against the vaccination record, they are verifying that the microchip number was already present in the animal at the time the vaccine was given.

        If your pet was vaccinated before being microchipped, the vaccination record is treated as unverified. It cannot be backdated or corrected. The only solution is to revaccinate, wait the 21-day period again, and rebook travel accordingly.

        If you are travelling with a pet that has been microchipped and vaccinated previously, ask your vet to confirm the dates before starting any paperwork. A quick check now saves a costly delay later.

    - heading: "Helsinki to Tbilisi: the Istanbul routing in practice"
      body: |
        The standard routing for cargo from Helsinki-Vantaa (HEL) to Tbilisi International Airport (TBS) is via Istanbul (IST) with Turkish Airlines. Turkish Airlines Cargo operates live animal services from HEL, and Istanbul to Tbilisi is a regularly served route. Flight time HEL to IST is approximately 4 hours; IST to TBS is around 2.5 hours.

        The transit at Istanbul typically runs 3 to 6 hours depending on the connection. During this time, your pet remains in the cargo hold or in airline-supervised animal facilities at IST. Confirm exact transit handling procedures with Turkish Airlines Cargo at the time of booking.

        Finnair can connect to Istanbul from HEL if the timing suits, and Lufthansa offers a Frankfurt routing for those who prefer a German hub transit. Frankfurt's animal handling infrastructure is particularly well-established for longer connections.

faqs:
  - question: "Does my pet need a titre test before entering Georgia from Finland?"
    answer: "No. Georgia does not require a rabies antibody titre test for pets arriving from EU member states including Finland. A valid current rabies vaccination, given at least 21 days before entry and no more than 11 months before entry, is sufficient."
  - question: "Is there a direct flight from Helsinki to Tbilisi?"
    answer: "No direct passenger or cargo service operates between Helsinki-Vantaa (HEL) and Tbilisi International Airport (TBS). The main routing is via Istanbul (IST) with Turkish Airlines, or via Frankfurt (FRA) with Lufthansa. Confirm live animal cargo acceptance on both legs when booking."
  - question: "What happens if my pet's rabies vaccination has expired?"
    answer: "If the rabies vaccination has lapsed (more than 11 months before your entry date), your pet will need to be revaccinated and you must wait the 21-day validity period before travel. The 11-month limit for entry into Georgia means it is worth checking the vaccination date early, well before travel, rather than assuming the current record is still valid."
  - question: "What is the Ruokavirasto endorsement and do I need it for Georgia?"
    answer: "Yes. When a pet leaves Finland for a non-EU country, the official health certificate must be endorsed by Ruokavirasto (Finnish Food Authority) after it is signed by the official vet. This endorsement confirms the vet's authorisation. Allow 1-2 working days. Georgia is not an EU member, so the endorsement step is required."
  - question: "Can I use an EU pet passport for Georgia?"
    answer: "No. An EU pet passport is only valid for travel between EU member states. Georgia is not an EU member. An official veterinary health certificate (endorsed by Ruokavirasto) is required for export from Finland to Georgia."
