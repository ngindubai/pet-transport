  h1: "Pet Transport Indonesia to Slovenia: 2026 Guide"
  overview: |
    The hardest part of this route usually isn't the flight. It's finding a laboratory that can actually run the rabies titre test to EU standard, because none currently operate inside Indonesia with EU approval. Most owners end up shipping the blood sample to Singapore or further afield, and that step alone can add several weeks that a route from, say, Mexico or South Korea would not need.

    Indonesia also sits outside the EU's low-rabies-risk list under Commission Delegated Regulation (EU) 2026/131, so the full titre test and 90-day wait apply once the sample does come back clear.

  sections:
    - heading: "The titre test laboratory problem, specifically"
      body: |
        This is the detail that catches most first-time movers out. The EU requires the blood sample for the rabies antibody titre test to be analysed at an EU-approved laboratory, and as things stand there isn't one operating inside Indonesia.

        Vets typically draw the blood locally in Jakarta or Bali, then ship it under IATA P650 diagnostic packaging rules to a laboratory in Singapore or Europe. Add international courier time on top of the normal 3 to 4 week result turnaround, and this single step can push the whole timeline out by a month or more compared with a country where the lab work happens locally.

        Book this early, confirm the destination laboratory's current EU approval status before shipping, and do not assume your regular vet already has a courier relationship set up for this.

    - heading: "Barantin's export process"
      body: |
        Badan Karantina Indonesia (Barantin), which absorbed the earlier BARANTAN and BKIPM functions, controls every animal export from the country. The certificate has to be completed by a government vet, endorsed with the Barantin stamp, and issued no more than 10 days before travel.

        Offices at Jakarta's Soekarno-Hatta and Bali's Ngurah Rai airports both handle this. Book the inspection appointment two to three weeks ahead, and cross-check that the microchip number, vaccination dates and titre test result on the Barantin certificate match your pet's actual records exactly. A mismatch here is a common cause of last-minute delay.

    - heading: "Getting from Indonesia to Ljubljana"
      body: |
        There is no direct routing from Indonesia to Slovenia. Qatar Airways Cargo via Doha is the most established option for Indonesian pet cargo heading toward Europe, with Turkish Airlines Cargo's direct Jakarta-Istanbul service as a genuine alternative worth pricing against it.

        Ljubljana's own airport takes a smaller share of long-haul cargo than the bigger hubs, so some bookings finish with a road transfer from Vienna or Frankfurt rather than a direct flight into Slovenia. Confirm which applies before you plan your own travel to meet the pet on arrival.
