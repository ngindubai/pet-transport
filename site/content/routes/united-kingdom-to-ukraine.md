content:
  h1: "Pet Transport from the United Kingdom to Ukraine"
  overview: |
    There is no flight that lands in Ukraine. Every UK airline serving Warsaw, Krakow or a Romanian city can get your pet to the region, but the last stretch, across the Polish or Romanian border, has to be driven. Ukrainian civilian airspace has been closed since Russia's invasion in February 2022, and that remains the case as this guide is written in mid-2026.

    Most owners moving a pet to Ukraine right now are returning home, reuniting with family, or supporting work that requires them to be there. Whatever the reason, the practical shape of the journey is the same: UK export paperwork, a flight into a neighbouring country, then an overland crossing with its own documentation exchange at the Ukrainian border post.

  sections:
    - heading: "Why the flight only gets you halfway"
      body: |
        Ukraine's airspace closed to civilian traffic on 24 February 2022 and has not reopened. EASA's Conflict Zone Information Bulletin for Ukraine remains active, extended to at least 31 July 2026 at the time of writing, and while the Ukrainian government has set up a working group to plan a phased reopening starting with airports like Lviv, no airline currently has a confirmed date to resume service.

        The practical route from the UK runs through Poland or Romania. LOT Polish Airlines flies Heathrow and Gatwick to Warsaw with pets accepted in the cabin or cargo, and Krakow or Rzeszow are useful alternative arrival points depending on which Ukrainian border crossing you're heading for. From there, it's a road journey: commonly through Krakovets-Korczowa or Shehyni-Medyka near Lviv, both well-used crossings since the war began. Budget carriers on this corridor, Wizz Air in particular, do not carry pets at all, so check your airline's policy before booking rather than assuming the cheapest fare will work.

    - heading: "Ukraine's entry rules, and an honest gap in the guidance"
      body: |
        Ukraine's State Service of Ukraine on Food Safety and Consumer Protection (Derzhprodspozhyvsluzhba) sets the entry requirements: an ISO microchip fitted before vaccination, a current rabies vaccination, and a titre test with blood drawn at least 30 days after that vaccination, reaching at least 0.5 IU/ml, followed by a 3-month wait from the draw date.

        Here's the part worth being upfront about. Some countries waive the titre test for pets coming from rabies-free or low-risk nations, the UK among them, when entering elsewhere. Published guidance on entering Ukraine, including the summary maintained by USDA APHIS, does not describe an equivalent exemption. It reads as a uniform requirement regardless of origin. Given how much has shifted in Ukraine's border administration since 2022, this is exactly the kind of detail to confirm directly with Derzhprodspozhyvsluzhba, or with the border veterinary post you plan to cross at, before you commit to dates. Don't assume your pet's UK paperwork buys a shortcut it may not have.

        At the border itself, your UK Animal Health Certificate and titre results get exchanged for a Ukrainian international veterinary certificate, completed in Ukrainian and English. Build in time for this step: it happens at the checkpoint, not in advance.
