  h1: "Pet Transport from Kazakhstan to the United States"
  overview: |
    The endorsement chain is what makes this route genuinely complex. Three separate authorities must sign off on your dog's health certificate before the United States will accept it: a US-licensed veterinarian, a USDA APHIS-accredited veterinarian, and the Committee for Veterinary Control and Supervision (KVCS) in Kazakhstan. That three-step chain takes time and cannot be rushed.

    Add the CDC titre test requirement, which includes a mandatory 30-day wait after rabies vaccination before blood can be drawn, and you are looking at a preparation window of 16 to 24 weeks from first vaccination to travel day.

    Cats face a much simpler process. There are no federal CDC import requirements for cats arriving from Kazakhstan. Everything below about CDC forms and titre tests applies to dogs only.
  sections:
    - heading: "The CDC requirements for dogs from Kazakhstan"
      body: |
        The US Centers for Disease Control and Prevention classifies Kazakhstan as a high-risk country for dog rabies. This triggers a specific entry pathway for every dog arriving from Kazakhstan, regardless of the individual dog's vaccination history.

        **What dogs from Kazakhstan need to enter the US**

        | Requirement | Detail |
        |---|---|
        | Rabies vaccination | Current, administered by a licensed vet |
        | Titre test | Blood draw 30+ days after vaccination; result 0.5 IU/ml or above at a CDC-recognised lab |
        | CDC Dog Import Form (6-527) | Submitted online before travel |
        | Three-authority endorsed certificate | US-licensed vet, then USDA APHIS-accredited vet, then KVCS |
        | CDC-registered facility reservation | Must be booked in advance; some have waiting lists |
        | Arrival airport | Must be CDC-registered: JFK, LAX, ORD, IAD, ATL, DFW, BOS, SFO, SEA, HNL and others |

        Source: CDC, cdc.gov/importation/dogs.html

        None of these requirements can be waived. Dogs arriving without a passing titre result, a valid CDC form, or a facility reservation will be refused entry or held at the port of entry at the owner's expense.

        For cats, the position is the opposite: there are currently no federal CDC import requirements for cats entering the US. The main documentation obligation for cats is the KVCS export health certificate on the Kazakhstan side.
    - heading: "Kazakhstan export requirements"
      body: |
        The KVCS (Committee for Veterinary Control and Supervision, Ministry of Agriculture of Kazakhstan) governs all animal exports. Your veterinarian must be authorised by KVCS to issue the export health certificate. After issuance, KVCS applies their own official endorsement before the certificate is accepted by US authorities.

        Kazakhstan is a member of the Eurasian Economic Union (EAEU), which standardises some veterinary documentation procedures within the union. US import rules operate entirely independently of EAEU agreements, so EEU membership does not simplify the US-side requirements.

        **Departure airports**

        - Almaty International Airport (ALA): the primary international cargo hub; most used for pet shipments abroad
        - Nursultan Nazarbayev International Airport (NQZ): handles international cargo; confirm pet acceptance with the chosen airline before booking

        An export permit may be required depending on the species and destination regulations. Confirm the permit requirement with KVCS well ahead of the planned travel date.
    - heading: "Airlines and routing"
      body: |
        There is no direct passenger or cargo service between Kazakhstan and the United States. All routes involve at least one hub connection, most commonly Istanbul (IST), Frankfurt (FRA), or Amsterdam (AMS).

        **Routing options**

        | Carrier | Routing | Notes |
        |---|---|---|
        | Turkish Airlines Cargo | ALA-IST then IST-JFK or IST-ORD | Most-used path for KZ-US cargo; confirm brachycephalic rules and heat embargoes |
        | Air Astana | ALA-FRA or ALA-AMS, then to US gateway | Confirm partner cargo arrangements on the transatlantic leg |
        | Lufthansa Cargo | ALA-FRA then FRA to US gateway | Reliable on documentation; brachycephalic breeds restricted year-round |
        | KLM Cargo | ALA via AMS to US destinations | Temperature-controlled hold; seasonal embargoes apply |

        Confirm that the planned US arrival airport is on the CDC-registered list before booking. Your pet transport agent will check this as a matter of course, but if you are arranging travel independently it is a step that must not be skipped.

        Because this route involves multiple carriers, a complex document chain, and time-critical endorsements, using an IPATA-registered pet transport agent is strongly recommended.
faqs:
  - question: "How long does it take to move a dog from Kazakhstan to the United States?"
    answer: "Allow 16 to 24 weeks from the start of preparations to travel day. The titre test requires a 30-day wait after rabies vaccination before blood can be drawn, laboratory processing takes another 2-4 weeks, and the three-authority endorsement chain adds further time after that. Starting as early as possible is the single most effective thing you can do."
  - question: "Does my dog need a titre test to enter the US from Kazakhstan?"
    answer: "Yes. Kazakhstan is classified by the CDC as a high-risk rabies country. Dogs from high-risk countries must have a rabies antibody titre test at a CDC-recognised laboratory showing a result of at least 0.5 IU/ml. The blood draw must happen at least 30 days after a valid rabies vaccination. This requirement cannot be waived."
  - question: "Do cats from Kazakhstan need a titre test or CDC form to enter the US?"
    answer: "No. There are currently no federal CDC import requirements for cats arriving in the United States from Kazakhstan. Cats still need a KVCS export health certificate and current vaccination documentation from the Kazakhstan side, but they are not subject to the CDC Dog Import Form or the titre test requirement."
  - question: "What does the three-authority endorsement chain mean in practice?"
    answer: "Your dog's health certificate must carry the signature or stamp of three separate authorities. First, a US-licensed veterinarian prepares and signs the CDC government-endorsed form. Second, a USDA APHIS-accredited veterinarian endorses that form. Third, KVCS in Kazakhstan applies their official endorsement. Each step takes time and involves fees. Build at least 2-4 weeks into your schedule for this process alone."
