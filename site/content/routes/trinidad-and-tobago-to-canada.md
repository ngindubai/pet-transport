  h1: "Pet Transport from Trinidad and Tobago to Canada"
  overview: |
    A rabies vaccination certificate and a general health certificate. That is the entire federal paperwork list Canada asks of a pet arriving from Trinidad and Tobago, provided the certificate states the vaccine's trade name, serial number and expiry date correctly the first time.

    Trinidad and Tobago has never appeared on the Canadian Food Inspection Agency's high-risk list for dog rabies, last updated 13 March 2025, and it is not one of the nine countries whose own pets skip vaccination entirely. That single classification keeps this route free of the titre test, the fixed waiting period, and the import permit that complicate Trinidad and Tobago's routes to the UK, Germany and even its own Category 2 relationship with the United States and Canada in reverse.

  sections:
    - heading: "Why Canada asks for so little"
      body: |
        CFIA sorts pet-import origins into three practical groups: countries recognised as rabies-free, countries flagged high-risk for dog rabies, and everywhere else. Trinidad and Tobago sits in that third, default group. No serology test, no waiting period once the vaccine is given, no CFIA import permit for a personal move of one or two animals.

        That does not mean the certificate can be casual. CFIA wants the vaccine's trade name, its serial number, its expiry date, and the dog's breed, sex, colour and weight all on the same document. Ask the vet in Trinidad and Tobago to include every field the first time; a certificate that is missing one of them is a genuinely common cause of delay at the Canadian border, even when the dog itself is fully compliant.

    - heading: "The flight is easier than most Trinidad and Tobago long-haul routes"
      body: |
        Piarco to Toronto Pearson runs nonstop on two separate carriers, which puts this route ahead of most others on this site. Caribbean Airlines operates it most often, somewhere around 10 to 13 flights a week combined across both directions, with pets travelling as checked baggage up to 70 lb combined weight. Air Canada flies the same sector roughly four times a week on Boeing 737 aircraft.

        Neither airline carries brachycephalic breeds in the hold, whatever the season. That single restriction is worth checking before anything else if your pet is a flat-faced breed, since the light paperwork on this route counts for little if the flight itself will not take your dog.

    - heading: "Where the CAD 700-2,200 actually goes"
      body: |
        With no titre test and no import permit fee to pay, the flight itself becomes the largest single cost on this route rather than the paperwork. Budget CAD 500-1,200 for the checked-baggage or cargo fare between Piarco and Toronto, depending on crate size and whether you fly around Carnival season, usually February, when Trinidad-origin fares and cargo space both tighten.

        A general veterinary health certificate, TT$400-900, and a crate sized correctly for your dog or cat round out most budgets. Owners who skip an agent and book the airline leg directly still need the certificate to state the vaccine details CFIA expects. Get that document right at the vet's office in Trinidad and there is little else standing between booking the flight and touching down in Canada.
