  sections:
    - heading: "Selecting the right EU-approved laboratory for Kazakhstan"
      body: |
        The FAVN titre test for EU entry is not available domestically in Kazakhstan. Your vet draws the blood, packages it appropriately, and ships it to an EU-approved laboratory. Most approved labs are in the EU, UK, or North America. Common choices from Kazakhstan include labs in the Netherlands (like RHILZ, Lelystad) or France, though any EU-approved laboratory on the official list maintained by the European Commission will work.

        Shipping blood internationally from Kazakhstan requires a reliable courier with experience handling biological samples. DHL and FedEx have this capability from Almaty, but confirm with your vet that the sample will be appropriately packaged (chilled, with preservative, in a leak-proof container) and that the lab's specific shipping instructions are followed.

        Allow 3-5 weeks from blood draw to result receipt when planning from Kazakhstan. Some labs take longer when receiving international samples for the first time from a new vet.

    - heading: "Choosing between Almaty and Astana as the departure point"
      body: |
        Kazakhstan's two main international airports serve different parts of the country. Almaty International Airport (ALA) is in the south and handles more international cargo routes. Nursultan/Astana International Airport (NQZ) is in the north and is the country's official capital, with growing international connections.

        For a Denmark-bound pet cargo, Almaty generally has slightly more international carrier options. Turkish Airlines, Air Astana, and LOT all operate from ALA. If you are based in Astana or northern Kazakhstan, NQZ also connects to Istanbul and Frankfurt via similar carriers, so the routing options are comparable.

        Ask your transport agent which airport is preferable for the specific departure date you have in mind, as carrier schedules vary by season and some routes operate from one airport but not the other at different times of year.

faqs:
  - question: "Does the Ministry of Agriculture endorsement in Kazakhstan serve the same function as an EU vet endorsement?"
    answer: "Yes, for entry purposes. The Committee for Veterinary Control and Supervision under Kazakhstan's Ministry of Agriculture is the competent authority that certifies the health certificate for international pet movement. Denmark's Fødevarestyrelsen recognises this endorsement when the certificate is in the correct EU-required format for unlisted third countries. The key is that the format matches what EU Regulation 2020/692 requires, not just that the right Kazakhstani authority has signed it."
  - question: "How do I find a vet in Kazakhstan who has experience with EU-destination pet moves?"
    answer: "International pet transport agents operating in Almaty or Astana are the best starting point. They maintain relationships with vets who understand the EU certificate format, the FAVN titre test sample requirements, and the Ministry of Agriculture endorsement process. Avoid booking the vet appointment based on proximity alone; experience with international EU-destination moves matters for getting the certificate format right."
  - question: "If my dog fails the titre test, when can I try again?"
    answer: "A failed titre test (result below 0.5 IU/ml) means the vaccination must be repeated and a new blood draw performed at least 30 days after the new vaccination. The 90-day wait then starts fresh from the new blood draw date. A first failure adds roughly 4-5 months to the timeline. To reduce the chance of failure, ensure the vaccine used is an inactivated product administered at the full manufacturer dose, and allow at least 30 days (preferably 6-8 weeks) between vaccination and blood draw."
