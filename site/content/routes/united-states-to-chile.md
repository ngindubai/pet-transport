  sections:
    - heading: "Apostille: the step most owners miss"
      body: |
        Chile's SAG requires the USDA health certificate to be apostilled, not just endorsed by USDA APHIS. The apostille process authenticates the US government endorsement for use in Chile, a fellow Hague Convention signatory. Without it, SAG officers at Santiago Airport may reject the document.

        The apostille can be obtained at the state level (through the Secretary of State's office in the state where the certificate was issued) or at the federal level via the US Department of State for certain federal documents. Processing times vary by state, but plan for several business days after the USDA endorsement before the apostille is attached. Pet transport agents familiar with the Chile route manage this chain routinely.

    - heading: "Parasite treatment and SAG inspection at Santiago"
      body: |
        SAG requires documented evidence of internal and external parasite treatment completed within 30 days before your pet's arrival in Chile. The health certificate must list the active ingredient(s) used, the dosage, and the date of treatment. A general note that the pet was treated is not sufficient. Ask your USDA-accredited vet to record these details explicitly on the certificate.

        On arrival at Arturo Merino Benitez Airport in Santiago, SAG veterinary officers inspect all arriving pets before customs clearance. For pets with a correctly endorsed, apostilled health certificate and documented parasite treatment, the inspection is brief and no quarantine follows.

faqs:
  - question: "Does a dog or cat from the USA need a titre test to enter Chile?"
    answer: "Chile's SAG does not currently mandate a titre test for dogs and cats from the USA. Current requirements focus on rabies vaccination (at least 21 days before travel), core vaccinations, and parasite treatment. Check the current SAG country classification list for any changes before travel, as classification decisions can be updated."

  - question: "What is the apostille for the Chile health certificate and how do I get it?"
    answer: "An apostille authenticates the USDA APHIS endorsement on your health certificate for use in Chile. It is issued by the Secretary of State in the state where the health certificate was endorsed (for state-level apostilles) or by the US Department of State for federal documents. Bring your USDA-endorsed health certificate to the appropriate apostille authority or send it by mail. Processing fees and times vary by state. Pet transport agents can manage this step on your behalf."

  - question: "What parasite treatments are required for entering Chile?"
    answer: "SAG requires internal and external parasite treatment within 30 days before arrival in Chile. The health certificate must specify the product used (by active ingredient name, such as praziquantel for tapeworm), the dosage administered, and the date of treatment. Ask your vet to record this information in detail on the health certificate. General statements without specific details are not accepted by SAG."

  - question: "Can my pet fly in the cabin to Chile from the USA?"
    answer: "Yes, for small pets. American Airlines, LATAM, and United Airlines all permit small pets in the cabin on routes to Santiago. Confirm the current cabin pet policy, carrier dimension limits, and breed restrictions with your airline before booking, as policies vary by carrier and route."
