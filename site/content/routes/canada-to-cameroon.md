content:
  h1: "Pet Transport from Canada to Cameroon"
  overview: |
    No titre test stands between a Canadian pet and entry into Cameroon. What does stand in the way, for a specific slice of owners, is a pedigree check: wolf hybrid dogs and two named cat breeds, Savannah and Bengal, need five generations of documented distance from that ancestry before MINEPIA will let them in. For everyone else, the requirements are lighter than the flight itself.

    A current rabies vaccination, given at least 30 days before arrival, and a CFIA-endorsed health certificate cover most of what MINEPIA checks on landing at Douala. The harder part of this route is booking it: nothing flies direct, so Royal Air Maroc's Casablanca hub and Ethiopian's Addis Ababa hub do the real work of connecting Canada to Central Africa.
  sections:
    - heading: "What actually gets checked, and what mostly doesn't"
      body: |
        Cameroon's entry rules for a Canadian-origin pet are short. Rabies vaccination, dated correctly. A health certificate. A microchip that, while not strictly compulsory, every other document on this list quietly assumes exists. No titre test, no quarantine for a compliant arrival, no formal import permit application to file in advance.

        That short list has one exception with real teeth: the wolf hybrid and Savannah/Bengal restriction. It isn't a health rule; it's an ancestry rule, checked against pedigree documentation. Five generations removed is the bar. Anything short of that, and the animal doesn't clear MINEPIA's checks, whatever its vaccination record says.

    - heading: "Booking through Casablanca or Addis Ababa"
      body: |
        Royal Air Maroc runs Montreal to Casablanca close to daily on its own Boeing 787 fleet, connecting onward to Douala or Yaounde. Ethiopian Airlines offers a genuinely different path: a nonstop Toronto to Addis Ababa sector around seven times a week, then Ethiopian's own daily service into Douala. Air France, via Paris, is a third option worth checking if neither of the first two matches your dates.

        Each keeps the animal within one airline's cargo network for the international leg, which matters more on a long journey than shaving a few dollars off the fare with a mixed-carrier itinerary and a tighter connection.

faqs:
  - question: "Does a pet need a rabies titre test to enter Cameroon from Canada?"
    answer: "No. Cameroon does not require a titre test from a pet arriving from Canada, or from any other country. A current rabies vaccination, given at least 30 days before entry, and a health certificate are what MINEPIA actually checks."
  - question: "Can I bring a wolf hybrid dog or a Savannah cat into Cameroon from Canada?"
    answer: "Only with pedigree documentation showing five generations removed from that wild ancestry. Cameroon restricts import of wolf hybrids and Savannah or Bengal cats without that documented gap, regardless of the country of origin."
  - question: "Is there a direct flight from Canada to Cameroon?"
    answer: "No. Royal Air Maroc (via Casablanca), Ethiopian Airlines (via Addis Ababa) and Air France (via Paris) all offer connecting routings that accept pets as cargo into Douala."
  - question: "Do I need to endorse the health certificate through CFIA?"
    answer: "Confirm with MINEPIA or your relocation agent whether Cameroon expects CFIA endorsement on top of the accredited vet's signature. Cameroon hasn't published a joint certificate template with Canada the way it has with some other origin countries, so this is worth checking directly rather than assuming."
  - question: "How long should I allow for this move?"
    answer: "Eight to twelve weeks in most cases, driven by the 30-day rabies vaccination window, the pedigree check for restricted breeds if relevant, and booking a connecting flight through Casablanca, Addis Ababa or Paris."
