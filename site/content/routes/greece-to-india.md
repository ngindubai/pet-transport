    Moving a pet from Greece to India requires prior import authorisation from AQCS (Animal Quarantine and Certification Service) under India's DAHD, a Hellenic Ministry-issued official health certificate meeting AQCS requirements, and arrival at one of India's six AQCS-designated airports. Emirates via Dubai and Qatar Airways via Doha are the main cargo routing options from Athens. Use a specialist agent for this corridor.

    - heading: "AQCS import process and entry airports"
      body: |
        India's Animal Quarantine and Certification Service (AQCS), under the Department of Animal Husbandry and Dairying (DAHD), regulates all pet imports. Import authorisation must be obtained before the pet leaves Greece. The authorisation specifies the entry airport and conditions.

        Authorised entry airports are: Delhi (DEL), Mumbai (BOM), Chennai (MAA), Kolkata (CCU), Bengaluru (BLR), and Hyderabad (HYD). Pets arriving at any other Indian airport cannot be cleared. The AQCS inspector at the designated airport verifies the health certificate, microchip, and vaccination record. Depending on the origin country's classification, a period of quarantine or observation may be required.

        Contact DAHD (dahd.nic.in) for the current AQCS application procedure and the health certificate format required from Greece.
