content:
  h1: "Pet Transport from Germany to Brunei"
  overview: |
    Fourteen times a week, Singapore Airlines flies nonstop from Frankfurt to Singapore, more often than almost any other European gateway serves Southeast Asia. That frequency helps with scheduling. It does nothing to shorten what Brunei itself demands.

    Germany doesn't appear on the short list of countries Brunei treats as rabies-free, so the full pathway applies: a FAVN or ELISA titre test, and a minimum 14 days of quarantine on arrival, whatever the paperwork looks like. Budget three to four months, and treat the Department of Agriculture and Agrifood's own confirmation of current terms as step one, not an afterthought.

  sections:
    - heading: "Why the daily Singapore flight doesn't remove the quarantine"
      body: |
        Brunei splits origin countries into two tiers. Scheduled countries, Australia, Ireland, New Zealand, Sabah, Sarawak, Singapore and the UK, can see quarantine waived for a pet with sufficient residency. Germany sits outside that list entirely, so a titre test and a facility stay both apply, no matter how frequent the flight or how complete the file.

        The titre test itself sets the real pace here. Blood has to be drawn at least 30 days after the rabies vaccination, tested at an EU-approved laboratory, with a result of 0.5 IU/ml or higher. Get that number early and everything else, the permit, the flight, the certificate, can run in parallel around it.

    - heading: "Frankfurt's daily options, and where they actually land"
      body: |
        Singapore Airlines Cargo's Frankfurt-Singapore service runs nonstop, fourteen times a week, which makes it the easiest leg to book on this whole route. What it doesn't do is fly onward to Brunei itself; the pet needs a second carrier or a coordinated road transfer for the final hop into Bandar Seri Begawan.

        Emirates SkyCargo and Qatar Airways Cargo both offer workable alternatives via Dubai or Doha, useful if Singapore Airlines' capacity is tight around a particular travel date. Royal Brunei Airlines, the obvious name to search for, doesn't serve Germany at all, and even on routes it does fly, it only takes pets as manifested cargo through a registered freight forwarder, not as a straightforward owner booking.

    - heading: "The breed question, settled before anything else"
      body: |
        Three breeds are banned from entering Brunei outright: Pit Bull Terrier, Dogo Argentino and Japanese Tosa. No amount of paperwork changes that.

        A second group, including Bull Mastiff, Doberman and German Shepherd, can travel, but only with extra conditions attached: a minimum age, mandatory neutering, and a requirement to muzzle the dog in public once it's in Brunei. Check your dog's breed against DAA's current list before any of the rest of this process starts.
