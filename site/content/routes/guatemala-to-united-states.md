  overview: |
    Dogs leaving Guatemala for the US face a real fork in the road: the CDC lists Guatemala as high-risk for dog rabies, so every dog needs either a rabies titre test with a 28-day-old result, or a 28-day quarantine at a CDC-registered facility after landing. Cats sail through with none of that. No federal rabies rule applies to them at all.

    Guatemala's own paperwork is light by comparison. A vaccination card, a vet-signed health certificate and a USD 6.25 export certificate from MAGA's VUPE window cover the exit side. The flight itself is more straightforward than many Central American routes too: American flies Guatemala City direct to Miami and Dallas, and Avianca runs direct services to Los Angeles, New York and Washington, three of which already have the CDC-registered facilities a dog needs to arrive at.

    The catch is Delta and United. Both operate direct Guatemala City flights to US hubs, but neither currently books pet cargo for ordinary travellers, only military and Foreign Service moves. A route that looks like four airline options on a flight search engine is really two for a family shipping a dog.

  sections:
    - heading: "Titre test or 28-day quarantine: which one actually saves time"
      body: |
        Because Guatemala is CDC high-risk, a dog needs proof of rabies immunity one way or another. The titre test pathway means a blood draw at least 30 days after vaccination, a lab result showing 0.5 IU/ml or above, and then a wait until that result itself is 28 days old before landing in the US. Do the maths and a dog can realistically travel around two months after vaccination, assuming the lab turns the result around quickly.

        Skip the titre test and the alternative is a 28-day quarantine at a CDC-registered facility on arrival, at the owner's expense, no blood test required beforehand. It sounds simpler on paper. It usually isn't, once you weigh a month of kennel fees (commonly USD 1,500 to 4,000) against a lab bill that costs a fraction of that. Most owners with any lead time at all choose the titre route.

        Cats need none of this. There is no federal rabies vaccination requirement, no titre test and no quarantine for a cat entering the US from anywhere, Guatemala included. A health certificate from the exporting vet is sensible for the airline's sake, not because CBP or USDA demands one.

    - heading: "Why the flight looks better than it is"
      body: |
        On paper, four scheduled carriers connect Guatemala City to the US: American, Avianca, Delta and United. In practice, only two will book a pet.

        American Airlines Cargo runs PetEmbark direct from Guatemala City to Miami and Dallas-Fort Worth, and Miami already has a CDC-registered facility, useful if the dog needs the quarantine pathway. Avianca goes direct to Los Angeles, JFK and Dulles, all three CDC-registered gateways, though it will not carry brachycephalic breeds in the hold and applies seasonal restrictions on some US routes.

        Delta and United both fly the route non-stop too, Delta to Atlanta and United to Houston, but neither has taken general public pet cargo bookings since the pandemic-era suspension; both restrict the service to active military permanent-change-of-station moves and State Department Foreign Service transfers. An owner assuming a direct United flight from Houston means a straightforward pet booking will hit a wall. Build the plan around American or Avianca from the outset, and reconfirm policy close to your travel date regardless, since airline cargo rules for pets have shifted more than once in the past few years.
