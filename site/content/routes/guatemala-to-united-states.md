  overview: |
    Two separate CDC and USDA rules stack up on this route, not one. Guatemala is classified as high-risk for dog rabies, which brings in the titre test and the CDC Dog Import Form. It is also currently on the list of countries affected by New World Screwworm, which brings in a second certificate that most owners moving a pet internationally have never had to think about before.

    Neither rule is as slow as it sounds. The US titre test only needs to sit 28 days ahead of arrival, nothing like the UK's fixed three-month wait after a passing blood draw. MAGA's own export paperwork, the International Zoosanitary Export Certificate, costs a flat USD 6.25. What takes planning is coordination: the blood draw date, the screwworm inspection window and a confirmed arrival slot at one of six CDC-registered facility airports all have to line up.

  sections:
    - heading: "Why the screwworm certificate exists now"
      body: |
        New World Screwworm had been pushed out of Central America for decades. Recent detections in Belize, Costa Rica, El Salvador, Guatemala, Honduras, Mexico, Nicaragua and Panama changed that, and USDA APHIS responded by adding a certification requirement on top of the standard rabies rules for dogs from these countries.

        The certificate has to come from a full-time salaried government veterinarian, not any private vet, and it has to confirm the dog was inspected within 5 days of shipment and found clear. If the dog had been infested, quarantine and treatment until clear would need documenting instead. Build this into your MAGA/VISAR appointment rather than assuming your usual vet clinic can sign it off.

    - heading: "The titre test clock runs differently here than on UK-bound routes"
      body: |
        Owners who have researched a UK move first sometimes assume every unlisted or high-risk country works the same way: titre test, then a long fixed wait. It does not, on this route.

        The US only requires that the blood sample for the titre test is drawn at least 30 days after vaccination and, separately, at least 28 days before the dog's US arrival date. There is no extra three-month clock once a passing result comes back. That single difference can save an owner moving from Guatemala City several months compared with the equivalent UK-bound trip.

        Where it is unforgiving is the arrival airport. A CDC-registered animal care facility reservation is mandatory for a high-risk-country dog, and only six US airports currently have one. Book your routing around that fact first, then work backwards on dates.
