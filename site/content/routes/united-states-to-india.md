  h1: "Pet Transport from the USA to India"
  overview: |
    Pets moving from the USA to India face a mandatory 30-day quarantine at an AQCS (Animal Quarantine and Certification Service) facility on arrival. This is not negotiable and cannot be avoided with any documentation. It applies to all dogs and cats entering India from any international origin. Plan for this from the start and treat it as a fixed cost and a fixed delay.

    The US export side involves a USDA-APHIS endorsed health certificate, which is standard for most US pet exports. The more complex part is the India side: the titre test, AQCS import clearance, and the quarantine logistics. United and Delta both have solid services from the US to Delhi and Mumbai. Lufthansa via Frankfurt is a popular alternative.

    - heading: "The 30-day quarantine: what to expect"
      body: |
        The AQCS quarantine is a fixed part of the India import process. All pets, regardless of documentation quality, vaccination history, or country of origin, complete 30 days at the designated AQCS facility at their arrival airport.

        The facilities in Delhi and Mumbai are the two main reception points for international pet imports. During the 30 days, AQCS veterinary staff monitor the pets, check documentation, and verify the titre test results. At the end of the period, the AQCS issues a release certificate.

        Owners can visit their pets during quarantine at scheduled visiting times, though policies vary by facility. Your India-side agent can provide current visiting arrangements.

        The cost is owner-paid. Budgeting the equivalent of USD 800 to USD 2,500 for 30 days (depending on facility and pet size) is a reasonable estimate, though costs change. Get a current quote from your agent before travel.

        The 30-day quarantine is the same for dogs and cats. Neither species gets a shortened period or a waiver. Treat it as part of the journey rather than an obstacle.

faqs:
  - question: "Can the 30-day quarantine in India be shortened or waived?"
    answer: "No. The 30-day AQCS quarantine is mandatory for all dogs and cats entering India from any country. It cannot be shortened or waived by any documentation, vaccination history, or breed status. Plan for it as a fixed step in your pet's journey."
  - question: "What documentation do I need for the USDA export side?"
    answer: "A USDA-APHIS endorsed health certificate is the core US export document. A USDA-accredited vet in the USA examines your pet and issues the certificate, which is then endorsed by APHIS. The certificate must be in a format accepted by AQCS and issued within 10 days of India arrival. You also need your pet's microchip confirmation, vaccination records, and titre test result."
  - question: "Does my cat need to quarantine when entering India from the USA?"
    answer: "Yes. The 30-day AQCS quarantine applies to cats as well as dogs. All companion animals entering India from international origins must complete the 30-day period. Cats also need the microchip, vaccination, and titre test documentation."
  - question: "How much does the USA to India pet move cost in total?"
    answer: "Expect total costs in the range of USD 3,500 to USD 8,000. The 30-day AQCS quarantine and the transoceanic cargo fee are the two largest components. Titre test, health certificate, APHIS endorsement, crate, and agent fees make up the rest."
  - question: "Is there any difference in requirements for specific US states?"
    answer: "No. The APHIS export requirements are federal and apply uniformly across all US states. The India-side AQCS requirements do not change based on which US state your pet is coming from. The process is the same whether you are shipping from New York, Los Angeles, or Chicago."
