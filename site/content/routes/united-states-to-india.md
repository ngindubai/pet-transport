  h1: "Pet Transport from the United States to India"
  overview: |
    Moving a pet from the USA to India involves more paperwork and planning than most routes in this region. India requires all imported pets to undergo a quarantine period on arrival: 15 days at an AQCS (Animal Quarantine and Certification Services) facility at the receiving airport, provided documentation is complete and the pet is healthy. If illness is detected, the quarantine can be extended.

    Before travelling from the USA, the owner must obtain a No Objection Certificate (NOC) from AQCS. This is applied for online through the AQCS official portal and takes approximately 5 working days. The NOC must be in place before departure. The USDA-endorsed health certificate, covering rabies vaccination and freedom from specified diseases, is the other key document.

    Air India offers the most direct cargo service from US cities (JFK, SFO, ORD) to Indian airports. Emirates via Dubai and Qatar Airways via Doha are alternatives with one transfer. All large pets travel as manifested cargo.

    - heading: "Cats from the USA to India"
      body: |
        Cats travelling from the USA to India face the same AQCS process as dogs. The NOC must be obtained before departure, the health certificate must certify freedom from feline panleukopenia, calicivirus, rhinotracheitis, and rabies, and cats undergo the same 15-day quarantine on arrival. Rabies vaccination must also be given more than 1 month but within 12 months before embarkation.

        Unlike the USA, where cats face no federal import requirements, India treats cats and dogs identically for import purposes. Budget the same time and cost for a cat import as for a dog.
faqs:
  - question: "Is there a quarantine for pets entering India from the USA?"
    answer: "Yes. All pets imported into India undergo a 15-day quarantine at an AQCS facility at the receiving airport, provided documentation is complete and the pet appears healthy on arrival. The quarantine may be extended if illness is detected. The owner pays all quarantine costs. This applies to both dogs and cats from the USA."
  - question: "What is the AQCS NOC and how do I apply from the USA?"
    answer: "The NOC (No Objection Certificate) is issued by India's Animal Quarantine and Certification Services. It must be obtained before the pet leaves the USA. Apply online at aqcsindia.gov.in. Processing takes approximately 5 working days. You will need to provide details of the pet (species, breed, age, microchip number), travel dates, and the owner's information."
  - question: "Which airports in India accept international pet imports from the USA?"
    answer: "AQCS quarantine stations operate at Delhi Indira Gandhi (DEL), Mumbai (BOM), Chennai (MAA), Bangalore (BLR), Kolkata (CCU), Hyderabad (HYD), and Kochi (COK). Air India's direct routes from the US to India cover Delhi, Mumbai, and other major cities. Confirm the AQCS facility is accepting pets at your specific arrival airport when booking."
  - question: "Does my dog need a titre test to enter India from the USA?"
    answer: "The AQCS official requirements do not list a titre test as mandatory for entry into India. The core requirements are: ISO microchip, valid rabies vaccination (given more than 1 month but within 12 months before travel), and an official health certificate certifying freedom from specified diseases. Obtain the AQCS NOC before departure."
  - question: "How much does the India quarantine cost?"
    answer: "Quarantine costs at AQCS facilities are set by the Indian government and paid by the owner. Rates vary by facility, species, and duration. Contact the AQCS station at your intended arrival airport for current fees. For the standard 15-day period, costs typically cover accommodation, feeding, and daily veterinary inspection. Factor this into your overall budget before booking."
