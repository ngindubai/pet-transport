content:
  h1: "Pet Transport from the United Kingdom to the Bahamas"
  overview: |
    A dog or cat cannot land in Nassau without an ePermit issued through the Bahamian government's Click2Clear system, applied for online before the flight, not on arrival. That single administrative step, more than any veterinary requirement, is what shapes the timeline on this route. There's no titre test to worry about; the Bahamas accepts a straightforward rabies vaccination record from any country of origin.

  sections:
    - heading: "The Click2Clear account is step one, not step three"
      body: |
        Most owners plan a pet move around the vet, the flight and the paperwork, in roughly that order. On this route, the order matters differently. Registering for Click2Clear, BAHFSA's online permitting system, starts with an email requesting a username and password, and that can sit in a queue for a few working days before anyone replies.

        Leave it until the flight is booked, and the ePermit application can end up racing the departure date. Start it early, and it's a non-issue.

    - heading: "What UK vets need to certify, and when"
      body: |
        BAHFSA wants a health certificate dated within 48 hours of embarkation, confirming the animal is healthy and has active rabies immunity. That's a narrow window. Book the vet appointment three days before a flight and the certificate is already too old by the time boarding happens; book it the morning of, and there's no room for a delayed departure.

        The rabies vaccination itself has more breathing room: primary doses given between 30 days and 10 months before entry are accepted, and boosters simply need to predate the previous dose's expiry.

    - heading: "The direct BA option and the Miami alternative"
      body: |
        British Airways' nonstop Heathrow to Nassau service, seven days a week, is the obvious first choice, and it keeps the cargo handling to one airline throughout. The same IAG Cargo temperature embargo that applies to Bahamas-origin pets applies here in reverse: extreme heat at Nassau on arrival can push a booking to an earlier flight or a different day.

        American Airlines via Miami is the fallback when the direct slot doesn't work, adding a connection but also more flexibility on timing.
