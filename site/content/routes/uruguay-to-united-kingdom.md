  overview: |
    Uruguay to the UK is a high-preparation route: the full APHA non-listed country protocol applies, and there are no direct flights from Montevideo to London. The most common routing goes via Madrid with Iberia, which dominates the Uruguay-Spain market and offers the most natural gateway to London from the Plate region.

    The preparation sequence is fixed: microchip first, then rabies vaccination, then titre test blood drawn at least 30 days post-vaccination, then a 3-month wait from blood draw date. Total preparation time is around 5-6 months. Starting earlier than you think necessary gives you flexibility if there are any delays in laboratory results or MGAP processing.

    Uruguay's veterinary profession is well-developed and MGAP (the Ministry of Livestock, Agriculture and Fisheries) is an organised competent authority. Pet export procedures, while not common for UK-bound animals, are manageable with proper planning or an IPATA agent.
  sections:
    - heading: "UK APHA requirements for pets arriving from Uruguay"
      body: |
        Uruguay is not on the UK's approved country list. The full APHA protocol applies without exception: ISO microchip implanted before rabies vaccination, current rabies vaccination, titre test result of at least 0.5 IU/ml at an EU/UK approved laboratory (blood drawn at least 30 days after vaccination), 3-month wait from blood sampling date, tapeworm treatment for dogs 24-120 hours before UK arrival, and MGAP-endorsed official health certificate valid 10 days.

        The 3-month wait is calculated from the blood draw date. This date is recorded on the laboratory result certificate. Do not use the results issue date or any other date - it is the sampling date that counts.

        Source: UK APHA, gov.uk/bring-pet-to-great-britain.
    - heading: "Airline routing from Montevideo: comparing the European gateways"
      body: |
        Iberia is the dominant choice for Montevideo to London moves. The Madrid connection is the shortest transatlantic hop to Europe and Iberia Cargo has experience handling Latin American pets through the Madrid hub. The full route is MVD-MAD-LHR or MVD-MAD-LGW.

        Air France via Paris and Lufthansa via Frankfurt are the main alternatives. Both carriers have consistent pet cargo operations, and CDG and FRA are both experienced transit hubs for pets arriving from South America. Lufthansa allows small cabin pets on the Frankfurt-London leg, which is a genuine advantage for cats and small dogs.

        LATAM via Sao Paulo is worth noting for owners in eastern Uruguay near the Brazilian border, or if European carrier capacity is genuinely full at your preferred travel date. LATAM Cargo accepts pets within their South American network and can connect to European carriers at GRU.

        Avoid multi-stop itineraries with two long-haul connections wherever possible. The more connections, the greater the risk of handling delays and the longer your pet spends in the cargo environment.
    - heading: "MGAP documentation: Uruguay's export health certificate process"
      body: |
        Uruguay's Ministry of Livestock, Agriculture and Fisheries (MGAP) is the national veterinary authority for animal exports. Your Uruguayan vet prepares the health certificate and MGAP endorses it. The certificate is valid for 10 days from examination, so it must be prepared within 10 days of departure.

        MGAP's Veterinary Services Division (DGSG) handles endorsement. In Montevideo, offices are accessible and processing is generally within a few working days. Outside the capital, allow more time and consider whether your vet has experience with the MGAP export process.

        Bring all originals to the MGAP appointment: microchip documentation, vaccination certificate, titre test laboratory results, and the vet-prepared health certificate. MGAP will not endorse a certificate without sight of the supporting records.

        An IPATA-registered agent with South America experience can manage the MGAP process on your behalf, which is particularly valuable if you are relocating and have logistical constraints in the final weeks.

  - question: "What is the titre test and why is it required for UK entry from Uruguay?"
    answer: "The UK requires pets from non-listed countries like Uruguay to prove they have adequate rabies antibodies before entry. The titre test measures rabies antibody levels in a blood sample. The blood must be drawn at least 30 days after the rabies vaccination and the result must be at least 0.5 IU/ml from an EU/UK approved laboratory. You then wait 3 months from the blood draw date before your pet can legally enter the UK."
  - question: "What is MGAP and how do I get the Uruguayan export health certificate?"
    answer: "MGAP is Uruguay's Ministry of Livestock, Agriculture and Fisheries and the official authority for animal health exports. Your registered Uruguayan vet prepares the health certificate; MGAP endorses it. The certificate is valid 10 days from the veterinary examination. Visit MGAP's DGSG office in Montevideo with all original records. An agent can manage this process on your behalf."
  - question: "Are there approved laboratories for the rabies titre test in Uruguay or South America?"
    answer: "The UK recognises laboratories approved by the EU for rabies titre testing. Before drawing your pet's blood, confirm the laboratory holds this approval. Some laboratories in Argentina and Brazil hold approval - check the current EU/WOAH approved lab list or ask an IPATA agent for current lab recommendations in the region."
