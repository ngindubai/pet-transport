content:
  h1: "Pet Transport from Botswana to Australia"
  overview: |
    Every Botswana pet booking on this site already transits Johannesburg. For the Australia route, that turns out to matter more than usual. Botswana sits on DAFF's non-approved list, so a direct move is ruled out before anything else is considered: the pet has to live in a DAFF-approved country for 180 unbroken days first. South Africa itself is DAFF Group 3 approved, which means the staging country and the transit hub are the same place, a genuine shortcut most non-approved-country routes on this site don't get.

    That doesn't make the process quick. Group 3 status still means a rabies titre test applies before the Australia leg, and Mickleham's mandatory 10-day quarantine sits at the end regardless of where the pet staged. Add it up and 32 to 38 weeks is a realistic floor, not a worst case.

  sections:
    - heading: "Why South Africa beats the usual staging options"
      body: |
        Most non-approved-country routes on this site send owners looking at the USA, UK or Singapore as staging options, each one a separate long-haul flight away from the pet's actual starting point. Botswana doesn't have that problem. Johannesburg, the airport every Botswana pet booking already flies through on the way to anywhere, sits in a DAFF Group 3 approved country.

        That means the 180-day clock can start on the very first leg out of Gaborone, with no detour to a third continent first. It's not a loophole, DAFF's rules apply exactly the same way they would to any Group 3 country, but it does mean the logistics are considerably simpler than the equivalent route from, say, a non-approved South American country.

    - heading: "The RNATT test still applies, just without the extra wait"
      body: |
        Group 3 status isn't the same as Namibia's no-titre-test arrangement for its own Australia route. A rabies neutralising antibody titre test is still required before the Australia leg, blood drawn at least 30 days after vaccination and sent to an accredited laboratory, in practice usually the Onderstepoort Veterinary Institute in Pretoria, the OIE-recognised reference lab used across Southern Africa.

        What Group 3 status does avoid is the extended fixed wait that applies to lower-status country groups. Once the titre result passes, there's no additional multi-month hold beyond the standard test processing time, so this part of the 180-day residency period tends to run in parallel rather than adding weeks on top of it.

    - heading: "Mickleham, and the flight that gets you there"
      body: |
        However the 180 days are spent, the route ends the same way: a mandatory 10-day stay at Mickleham Post Entry Quarantine Facility in Melbourne, DAFF's only approved facility for imported cats and dogs. Book the slot as soon as the South African export health certificate date is confirmed, and plan the final flight, Johannesburg to Perth on Qantas or South African Airways, so it lands on a weekday, since Mickleham does not accept weekend arrivals.
