  overview: |
    Relocating a dog or cat from the Dominican Republic to Hungary is a process that takes a minimum of five to six months and requires careful coordination from the outset. Hungary is a full EU member state, and the Dominican Republic is classified as an EU unlisted third country, which means the full titre test pathway applies. Every pet making this journey needs a FAVN or ELISA titre test at an EU-approved laboratory, a passing result of at least 0.5 IU/ml, and a mandatory 90-day wait from the date that result is confirmed before entry into Hungary is permitted.

    Hungary's competent authority for pet import is Nebih, the Nemzeti Elelmiszerlanc-biztonsagi Hivatal, at nebih.gov.hu. Border veterinary inspectors at Budapest Ferenc Liszt International Airport (BUD) verify all incoming documentation: microchip records, vaccination history, the titre test laboratory report, and the DIGEGA-endorsed EU health certificate. The certificate is valid for 10 days from issue, so the timing of the final vet appointment and DIGEGA endorsement must be planned carefully around the departure date.

    Cargo from Santo Domingo (SDQ) to Budapest always involves at least one European hub connection. The main options are Madrid (MAD) on Iberia, Paris CDG on Air France, and Amsterdam (AMS) on KLM, all of which connect reliably to Budapest Ferenc Liszt International Airport. A pet transport agent experienced with the DIGEGA process and international titre test coordination is a sensible investment for owners using this route for the first time.

  sections:
    - heading: "Why the Dominican Republic needs a titre test for Hungary entry"
      body: |
        Hungary, as an EU member state, applies EU Regulation 2020/692 in full. The regulation categorises third countries into two groups based on their veterinary standards. Countries on the approved list can use a simplified import process. Countries not on that list, including the Dominican Republic, are subject to the full titre test requirement: a FAVN or ELISA titre test at an EU-approved laboratory, a minimum result of 0.5 IU/ml, and a 90-day wait from the date of the confirmed passing result before EU entry.

        The sequence starts with the microchip, which must be in place before any vaccination. At least 21 days after the primary rabies vaccination, your vet can administer the blood draw (though it must also be at least 30 days post-vaccination). The sample goes to an EU-approved laboratory, which typically takes two to four weeks to return a result including transit time. Once a passing result is recorded, the 90-day waiting period begins from that date, not from the draw date. This distinction matters, and miscounting it is a common source of problems.

        Nebih border inspectors at Budapest are thorough. They verify the laboratory name, the date of the blood draw, and the date of the result. If the 90-day period has not fully elapsed by the time the pet arrives, entry will be refused. There are no discretionary exceptions to this waiting period.

    - heading: "Routing and documentation from Santo Domingo"
      body: |
        Las Americas International Airport (SDQ) in Santo Domingo handles the majority of international cargo departures from the Dominican Republic. Punta Cana (PUJ) is an option for owners located in the east of the island, though its connections to European hubs are more limited and may require more advance booking to confirm live animal acceptance.

        For cargo to Budapest, the three main routings are: Iberia from SDQ to Madrid (MAD), then MAD to Budapest (BUD); Air France from SDQ to Paris CDG, then CDG to BUD; and KLM from SDQ to Amsterdam (AMS), then AMS to BUD. Budapest Ferenc Liszt International Airport is well served from all three hubs, and connections are generally reliable. Confirm with the airline that live animal cargo is accepted on both the transatlantic and European legs of the journey before making any booking, as this varies by flight.

        The DIGEGA export process requires a DIGEGA-authorised vet to examine your pet and issue the EU-format health certificate, followed by DIGEGA endorsement. The endorsement takes one to two working days on average. The certificate is valid for 10 days from issue, so the vet appointment and DIGEGA endorsement window must be planned to fit within the 10-day period before the actual departure.

    - heading: "How long does it take to move a pet from the Dominican Republic to Hungary?"
      body: |
        From the first veterinary appointment through to arrival in Budapest, the minimum realistic timeline is 20 to 24 weeks. The core milestones are: microchip implantation, rabies vaccination, a minimum 30-day gap before the titre test blood draw, two to four weeks for the EU-approved laboratory to issue its result, and then a full 90 days from the confirmed passing result date. This sequence on its own accounts for roughly 18 to 20 weeks in ideal conditions.

        In addition to these mandatory waiting periods, owners need time to identify an EU-approved laboratory, arrange international sample shipping from the Dominican Republic, secure cargo bookings, and manage the DIGEGA endorsement in the final 10-day window before departure. Starting the process six months before your planned travel date provides a reasonable buffer against delays at any of these stages.
