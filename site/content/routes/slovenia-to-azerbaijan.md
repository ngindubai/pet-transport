  h1: "Pet Transport from Slovenia to Azerbaijan"
  overview: |
    Azerbaijan does not require a titre test for EU-origin pets, and there is no quarantine for compliant arrivals at Heydar Aliyev International Airport in Baku. The export process from Slovenia uses the standard EU official health certificate through UVHVVR.

    The practical routing challenge from Slovenia is that Ljubljana Airport (LJU) is a small airport without direct international cargo services to the Caucasus. Most pet cargo from Slovenia routes via Vienna Airport (VIE), which is about two hours away by car and has well-established cargo connections to Istanbul and Baku.

  sections:
    - heading: "What Azerbaijan requires at the border"
      body: |
        Azerbaijan's veterinary border controls are administered by the Food Safety Agency (FSA), fsa.gov.az. Officers inspect documentation at Heydar Aliyev International Airport (GYD) in Baku.

        The core requirements are a valid rabies vaccination administered at least 21 days before entry, an official health certificate issued within 10 days of travel, and an ISO-compliant microchip. Azerbaijan does not require a titre test for EU-member-state pets. For 1-2 personal companion animals, no import permit is required.

    - heading: "UVHVVR health certificate process from Slovenia"
      body: |
        Slovenia's export health certificate for non-EU destinations is the EU-format official veterinary certificate. It must be signed by a UVHVVR-authorised official vet and endorsed by UVHVVR. Both steps must complete within 10 days of departure.

        Book the vet examination within 10 days of your travel date. After signing, allow 1-2 working days for UVHVVR endorsement. Check uvhvvr.gov.si for authorised official vets. Your regular vet can handle vaccinations and microchipping, but the health certificate must come from a UVHVVR-authorised official vet.

    - heading: "Cargo routing from Slovenia to Baku"
      body: |
        There is no direct cargo service from Ljubljana (LJU) to Baku (GYD). The standard routing is Austrian Airlines LJU to Vienna (VIE), then Turkish Airlines VIE to Istanbul (IST), then IST to GYD.

        Coordinating a three-leg cargo journey requires a cargo agent who can confirm live animal acceptance on each segment before you commit. Many Slovenia-based owners choose to drive to Vienna Airport and begin the journey at VIE, which simplifies the coordination to two legs rather than three.

        During June to September, check for temperature embargoes on the IST connection. If your pet is a brachycephalic breed, confirm year-round acceptance on Turkish Airlines cargo.
