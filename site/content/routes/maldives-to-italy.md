  h1: "Pet Transport from Maldives to Italy: 2026 Guide"
  overview: |
    Neos runs a direct Milan Malpensa to Male service twice a week each winter, a genuine seasonal link most guides to this route don't mention. It's still a passenger charter built around the ski-season-to-beach-holiday swap, and there's no confirmed cargo policy attached to it for a live cat.

    Italy's own rules haven't moved: the Maldives is an EU-unlisted country, so microchip, rabies vaccination, an FAVN titre test at an EU-approved lab and a 90-day wait from the blood draw date all apply, the same sequence that applies whichever airline eventually carries the crate.

  sections:
    - heading: "A cat route, and why that's different from a dog ban"
      body: |
        A resident dog is free to leave the Maldives under Maldivian law. Almost none exist to leave, because the Maldives Customs Service bans dog imports outright, which is the actual source of this route's cat-only pattern. If you're the rare owner of a Maldives-resident dog, this isn't a standard export job, it needs its own conversation with the relevant authorities.
    - heading: "Exporting from Male without a ministry to sign off"
      body: |
        There's no export permit and no government counter-signature built into a routine cat departure. A private Male vet issues the certificate, and most agents and airlines accept it as issued. Finding cargo space at Velana International, which handles far more resort freight than live pets, is usually the harder part of the job.
    - heading: "Italy's no breed ban, and the FAVN test that still applies"
      body: |
        Italy dropped its national breed-specific legislation back in 2009, a genuine point of difference from some other EU states, though it barely matters here since the traveller is almost always a cat. What does matter is the titre test: blood drawn at least 30 days post-vaccination has to clear 0.5 IU/ml at an EU-approved lab, and the 90-day wait starts on the draw date, not the result date.
    - heading: "Neos, and the routing that's actually confirmed"
      body: |
        If Neos confirms live animal cargo capacity on its Milan-Male service, it becomes a genuinely useful direct option for this corridor. Until then, Qatar Airways via Doha, with its dedicated Hamad International animal facility, is the established path, alongside Emirates via Dubai.
