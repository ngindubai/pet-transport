faqs:
  - question: "Does Denmark quarantine pets from Mexico?"
    answer: "No. No quarantine applies for pets from Mexico entering Denmark, provided the EU requirements are complete: ISO microchip, valid rabies vaccination, FAVN titre test result of 0.5 IU/ml or above, completed 90-day wait from the blood draw date, and EU AHC issued within 10 days before arrival."
  - question: "Is USDA endorsement required for the Mexican health certificate?"
    answer: "No. For pets leaving Mexico, SENASICA (not USDA) issues the zoosanitary certificate. The USDA is relevant for pets leaving the USA. The SENASICA-issued certificate is the appropriate document for a Mexican-originating pet move, and it does not require USDA endorsement."
  - question: "Can I do the FAVN test in Mexico?"
    answer: "The blood draw must be done in Mexico by a vet. The sample is then sent to an EU-approved laboratory for analysis. Commonly used options for Mexican samples include approved laboratories in the USA, which accept samples by courier. Check the European Commission's current approved laboratory list and confirm sample shipping requirements before the blood draw."
  - question: "What is the total preparation time from scratch for Mexico to Denmark?"
    answer: "From the day you start (microchip not yet implanted) to travel day, allow five to six months. The critical path is: microchip, wait 21 days, vaccination, wait 21 days, titre test blood draw, 90-day wait, then organise SENASICA certificate and EU AHC within the 10-day windows. Starting early removes the stress of tight timing."
