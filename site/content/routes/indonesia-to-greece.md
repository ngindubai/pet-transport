    - heading: "The FAVN titre test and 90-day wait: Indonesia to EU"
      body: |
        Because Indonesia is not on the EU's list of approved third countries, all pets travelling from Indonesia to any EU member state (including Greece) must satisfy the FAVN titre test requirement. The blood must be drawn at least 30 days after the primary rabies vaccination, and the test must be performed at an EU-approved laboratory. The result must be at least 0.5 IU/ml.

        Once the blood draw is carried out and the result is satisfactory, the pet must wait 90 days from the blood draw date before entering Greece or any other EU state. This 90-day wait is fixed by EU regulation and cannot be shortened.

        Alongside the titre test process, the BARANTAN export permit application should run in parallel so it is ready in time for travel once the 90-day wait has elapsed.
