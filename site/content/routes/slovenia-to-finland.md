content:
  h1: "Pet Transport from Slovenia to Finland"
  overview: "Relocating a pet from Slovenia to Finland is a straightforward EU-to-EU move on the paperwork side. Both countries are EU member states, so a valid EU pet passport covers the entry requirements. Finland does not require a titre test or tapeworm treatment for dogs arriving from EU member states, which keeps the preparation simpler than some other Northern European destinations. The main practical task is arranging the routing: Ljubljana has no direct service to Helsinki, so all journeys connect through Frankfurt or Vienna."

  sections:
    - heading: "What Finland Requires for Incoming Pets"
      body: |
        Finland follows EU Regulation 576/2013 for non-commercial pet movements. Pets arriving from Slovenia need a valid EU pet passport showing an ISO 11784/11785 microchip implanted before or on the same day as the first rabies vaccination, and a current rabies vaccination at least 21 days old after the primary dose. No titre test is required and no tapeworm treatment is needed for dogs coming from EU member states. No import permit is required. Ruokavirasto (the Finnish Food Authority) oversees all animal imports; their guidance is at ruokavirasto.fi. An Animal Health Certificate (AHC) can be used instead of the EU pet passport if required.

    - heading: "Airline Options from Ljubljana to Helsinki"
      body: |
        Ljubljana (LJU) does not have a direct service to Helsinki Vantaa (HEL). Finnair, as Finland's national carrier, offers connections via hub airports including Vienna and Amsterdam, and accepts pets as cargo. This makes it a practical first choice for this route. Lufthansa via Frankfurt (FRA) is the other main cargo option: FRA has dedicated live animal facilities and Lufthansa Cargo is experienced in EU-to-EU animal shipments. For small pets in-cabin, Austrian Airlines covers the LJU-Vienna leg, from which you would need to arrange onward cabin or cargo travel with a second carrier. Regardless of the airline, book live animal cargo space 8 to 12 weeks ahead, as LJU has limited capacity.

faqs:
  - question: "Does my dog need tapeworm treatment before entering Finland from Slovenia?"
    answer: "No. Finland does not require tapeworm treatment for dogs arriving from EU member states, including Slovenia. This is different from Norway, where tapeworm treatment is mandatory for all dogs from all countries. Finland's entry requirements are governed by Ruokavirasto (ruokavirasto.fi)."
  - question: "Can I use Finnair to transport my dog as cargo from Ljubljana to Helsinki?"
    answer: "Yes. Finnair accepts dogs as cargo on applicable routes and is the natural carrier for Ljubljana-to-Helsinki via a hub. You will need to route via an intermediate hub airport, most likely Vienna or Amsterdam, where the live animal will be transferred to the Helsinki-bound leg. Contact Finnair cargo directly or use a licensed pet transport agent to arrange the full booking."
  - question: "Is a titre test needed to bring my pet from Slovenia to Finland?"
    answer: "No. A rabies antibody titre test is only required when arriving in Finland from a country not on the EU listed countries register. Slovenia is an EU member state, so the standard EU pet passport with a valid rabies vaccination is sufficient. Ruokavirasto confirms this at ruokavirasto.fi."
  - question: "How long before the flight should I visit the vet in Slovenia?"
    answer: "The health check for the EU pet passport should be completed no more than 10 days before departure, in line with EU pet travel rules. If your pet's rabies vaccination needs updating, allow at least 21 days after the vaccination before travel. Plan ahead: if both a vaccination update and a pre-travel health check are needed, the vaccination should come first."
