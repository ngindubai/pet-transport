  h1: "Pet Transport Georgia to Estonia: 2026 Guide"
  overview: "Moving a pet from Georgia (the country in the Caucasus, capital Tbilisi) to Estonia requires careful planning over a minimum of five to six months. Estonia is an EU member state and Georgia is an EU unlisted country, which means the full EU unlisted pet travel process applies: microchip, rabies vaccination, FAVN titre test with a 90-day wait, and an NFA-endorsed health certificate. There is no shortcut to the 90-day wait. The routing from Tbilisi to Tallinn typically goes via Istanbul with Turkish Airlines Cargo."

  sections:
    - heading: "Why the 90-day wait applies and what it means for planning"
      body: |
        Estonia, as an EU member state, applies the EU pet travel framework for animals arriving from unlisted third countries. Georgia is classified as an EU unlisted country, which means your pet must complete the FAVN titre test process before entering Estonia.

        The sequence is: chip implanted first, then primary rabies vaccination, then at least 30 days later a blood draw for the FAVN titre test at an EU-approved laboratory. If the result is 0.5 IU/ml or above, the 90-day wait begins from the blood draw date. Travel to Estonia cannot happen until at least day 91.

        The 90-day wait is calculated from when the blood was drawn, not when the results came back. If you draw blood and the results take two weeks, those two weeks count as part of your 90-day wait. Starting the process as early as possible after you know you are moving is the right approach.

    - heading: "What happens at Tallinn Airport"
      body: |
        Tallinn Airport (TLL) has border veterinary inspection for live animal cargo arriving from non-EU origins. The Pohllumajandus- ja Toiduamet (Agricultural Board of Estonia) oversees these controls. Border vets at TLL will check: the NFA-endorsed health certificate (issued within 10 days of travel), the FAVN titre test result, the vaccination record, and the microchip.

        The chip must be scannable and the number must match every document precisely. If the titre test shows a successful result but the 90-day wait is incomplete, entry is refused and the pet will be returned or face penalty procedures. Estonian border vets apply EU rules strictly.

    - heading: "Routing from Tbilisi to Tallinn"
      body: |
        There is no direct cargo service from Tbilisi International (TBS) to Tallinn (TLL). The standard routing uses Turkish Airlines Cargo via Istanbul Ataturk (IST), where live animals are handled, then IST to TLL. The total journey from Tbilisi to Tallinn via IST is approximately 7-12 hours depending on transit times.

        Alternative routings go via Frankfurt with Lufthansa Cargo or via Amsterdam with KLM Cargo. Georgian Airways operates from Tbilisi to some European airports but its live cargo acceptance varies. Whatever routing you use, book during the 90-day wait and confirm live animal acceptance on every segment in writing.
