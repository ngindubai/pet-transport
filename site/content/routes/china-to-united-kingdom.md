  h1: "Pet Transport from China to the UK"
  overview: |
    China to the UK is one of the more demanding international pet moves from East Asia. The UK classifies China as a non-listed country, which means a mandatory rabies titre test and a three-month waiting period from the date of the blood draw before your pet can enter Great Britain. Add the GACC export process on the China side, and you're typically looking at a 5-7 month preparation window from start to travel day.

    On the airline side, Cathay Pacific (routing via Hong Kong) and Air China direct services both reach Heathrow. Most owners use a specialist pet transport agent for this route, and it's hard to argue against it: the documentation chain is long and a single missing endorsement means your pet is refused entry. Start early, use an accredited agent, and treat the titre test blood draw date as the single most important date in your timeline.

    - heading: "The titre test and 90-day wait: what you actually need to do"
      body: |
        The 90-day clock starts the moment the vet draws blood for the titre test, not when results come back. This is where most mistakes happen: people book flights based on when they expect results, not when blood was drawn.

        For China, the sequence is: (1) confirm microchip pre-dates rabies vaccination, (2) ensure two lifetime vaccinations are on record and the most recent is within 12 months and at least 30 days before blood draw, (3) have blood drawn at a GACC-approved lab (results take 2-4 weeks), (4) wait 90 days from blood draw date before UK entry.

        The GACC titre test requirement for China export and the UK titre test requirement for non-listed country entry are separate obligations but can often be satisfied by the same laboratory test result, provided the test was done at a lab approved by both GACC and accepted by APHA. Your vet and agent can confirm which labs meet both requirements.

        One thing to get right early: the APHA Import Licence. It's free, but processing takes 2-4 weeks, and you need it before travel. Apply online via the APHA portal during the titre test waiting period rather than at the last minute.

faqs:
  - question: "How long does it take to move a pet from China to the UK?"
    answer: "Allow 22-28 weeks as a minimum. The three-month wait after the titre test blood draw accounts for most of it. On top of that, you need the GACC export process (titre test + health certificate), the APHA Import Licence application, and cargo booking time. Start planning as soon as you know your move date."
  - question: "Does my pet need a titre test to move from China to the UK?"
    answer: "Yes. China is not on the GB listed country schedule, so a rabies antibody titre test is mandatory. The blood sample must be drawn at least 30 days after a valid rabies vaccination, the result must be at least 0.5 IU/ml, and a minimum of three calendar months must pass after the blood draw before your pet can enter Great Britain. There is no waiver or fast-track option."
  - question: "Can my pet fly in the cabin from China to the UK?"
    answer: "No. UK law requires all dogs, cats, and ferrets to enter Great Britain as manifested cargo. In-cabin and checked baggage pet transport are not permitted on any route into the UK. Your pet will travel in the temperature-controlled hold with Air China, Cathay Pacific, or British Airways World Cargo."
  - question: "What is the GACC health certificate and who issues it?"
    answer: "The GACC (General Administration of Customs of China) health certificate is the official export document for pets leaving China. It is issued by a vet authorised by GACC and must be endorsed by the local GACC office at the port of departure. It is valid for 14 days from the date of examination to the arrival date in the UK. The certificate must record the microchip number, vaccination history, and titre test result."
  - question: "Which airport can my pet fly into in the UK from China?"
    answer: "Your pet must arrive at an APHA-approved Border Control Post (BCP). London Heathrow (LHR) cargo terminal is the standard entry point for pets from China. Gatwick and Edinburgh are also approved. Not all UK airports have an approved BCP for live animals, so routing through non-approved airports is not possible."
