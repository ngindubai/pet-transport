  h1: "Pet Transport Panama to Finland: 2026 Guide"
  overview: "Panama to Finland combines all the preparation steps of a standard Panama-to-EU route with an additional Finnish-specific requirement for dogs. Panama's unlisted status triggers the FAVN titre test pathway: 30-day wait after vaccination, blood draw, 0.5 IU/ml result, and a 90-day wait from the blood draw date. Additionally, Finland requires a mandatory praziquantel Echinococcus treatment for dogs, administered by a vet 1-5 days before entry. Cats are exempt from the Echinococcus step. Total preparation time is 20-26 weeks, and logistics for dogs require careful coordination of the hub airport vet visit."

  sections:
    - heading: "Two Requirements for Dogs, One for Cats"
      body: |
        The Panama-to-Finland route has two distinct mandatory requirements. Understanding which animals they apply to is important.

        For all pets (dogs, cats, ferrets): the FAVN titre test pathway applies because Panama is an unlisted third country under Commission Delegated Regulation (EU) 2026/131. The process is: microchip before vaccination, rabies vaccination, 30-day wait, FAVN blood draw at an EU-approved laboratory, result of 0.5 IU/ml or above, and a 90-day wait from the blood draw date before entry to Finland.

        For dogs only: the mandatory Echinococcus (fox tapeworm) treatment. Finland maintains disease-free status for Echinococcus multilocularis. Under Finnish law (confirmed at ruokavirasto.fi), all dogs from countries outside the EU-exempt zone must receive praziquantel at the correct dose, administered by a licensed vet, 1-5 days before entering Finland. The treatment must be documented in the health certificate with the drug name, dose, date, time, and administering vet's details.

        Cats and ferrets are entirely exempt from the Echinococcus requirement.

    - heading: "Ruokavirasto Requirements at Helsinki Vantaa"
      body: |
        The Finnish Food Authority (Ruokavirasto, ruokavirasto.fi) carries out border inspections at Helsinki Vantaa Airport (HEL). For a dog arriving from Panama, Ruokavirasto inspects:

        Microchip: ISO 11784/11785, implanted before vaccination. Scanned on arrival.

        Rabies vaccination: Current, post-microchip, with 21 days elapsed since primary vaccination.

        FAVN titre test: 0.5 IU/ml or above, blood drawn 30+ days post-vaccination, 90-day wait from blood draw date elapsed.

        Echinococcus treatment: Praziquantel at correct dose, administered 1-5 days before entry to Finland. Drug name, dose, date and time, and administering vet's details documented on the health certificate or supplementary declaration.

        Health certificate: EU Annex IV format, DINASA-endorsed, issued within 10 days of travel.

        For cats: same as above except no Echinococcus treatment is needed.

    - heading: "Coordinating the Echinococcus Treatment at the Hub"
      body: |
        Finland's treatment window (1-5 days before entry) is slightly stricter than Norway's (24-120 hours). Here is what it means in practice for a Panama-to-Helsinki journey.

        If your pet departs Panama on Monday morning and arrives in Helsinki on Tuesday morning, the Echinococcus treatment window opens on Thursday the previous week (5 days before arrival on Tuesday) and closes on Monday (1 day before arrival on Tuesday). Since the journey itself takes roughly 24-30 hours, the treatment must be given at the hub layover (London, Amsterdam, or Frankfurt) on the same day as the final leg departs to Helsinki, or the day before.

        The most practical approach: build a sufficient layover at the hub (four to six hours minimum), arrange for a local vet near the airport to administer praziquantel, have the vet sign the health certificate addendum, and then proceed to HEL within the window.

        PetTransportGlobal coordinates these layover vet visits. Book the full journey with us and we handle the Echinococcus appointment as part of the service.

    - heading: "Airlines and Routing from PTY to Helsinki"
      body: |
        Helsinki Vantaa (HEL) is well connected to European hubs from Panama via Copa Airlines and Iberia cargo routes.

        Copa Airlines to Amsterdam (AMS) is the primary transatlantic path. AMS to HEL is served by Finnair and KLM, and both handle live animal cargo on this route. AMS is also a practical point for the Echinococcus layover vet visit if the connection time is sufficient.

        Copa to Madrid (MAD) via Iberia is the alternative. From MAD, Finnair serves HEL directly. Iberia Cargo handles the transatlantic leg competently.

        Lufthansa via Frankfurt (FRA) is particularly useful for large dogs. FRA to HEL is served by Finnair and Lufthansa. The Frankfurt animal centre handles longer journeys well, and FRA is a practical hub for the Echinococcus treatment if the layover time is adequate.

        Finnair is the natural final-leg carrier for Helsinki regardless of the hub used. Confirm Finnair Cargo live animal acceptance on your specific travel dates.

    - heading: "DINASA and the Finnish Health Certificate"
      body: |
        The health certificate for Finland follows the EU Annex IV non-commercial health certificate format. In Panama, a MIDA-registered vet completes the template, incorporating:

        - Microchip number
        - Rabies vaccination details (product, batch, date, expiry)
        - FAVN titre test details (laboratory, blood draw date, result in IU/ml, confirmation of 90-day wait)

        DINASA endorses the document. The certificate is issued within 10 days of travel.

        After the Echinococcus treatment at the hub layover, the administering vet adds a supplementary declaration:

        - Drug name: praziquantel (or epsiprantel)
        - Dose
        - Date and time of treatment
        - Vet's name, practice address, and licence number

        Ruokavirasto will check both the main certificate and the supplementary declaration. Carry originals. Copies are not accepted as primary documentation.

faqs:
  - question: "How is Finland's Echinococcus window different from Norway's?"
    answer: "Norway's window is 24-120 hours before arrival. Finland's window is 1-5 days before arrival. In practice, both mean the treatment must be given at the hub layover on the day of or day before the final leg to Helsinki or Oslo. The key difference is that Norway's window expressed in hours (24-120) is equivalent to 1-5 days, so operationally they are very similar. For Finland, use the 1-5 day counting method: if you arrive on a Tuesday, the treatment must be given on Thursday, Friday, Saturday, Sunday, or Monday of the same week."
  - question: "Can we treat our dog against Echinococcus in Panama a few days before the flight to cover Finland's window?"
    answer: "It depends on the exact timing. Finland's window is 1-5 days before entry to Finland. If the total journey from Panama to Helsinki takes 26-30 hours, giving the treatment in Panama 2-4 days before departure means the treatment was given 3-5 days before arrival in Finland, which could fall within the window. Calculate the treatment date carefully against your specific itinerary. If in doubt, the hub layover remains the safest option to guarantee the timing is correct."
  - question: "Does the FAVN titre test result need to be from a Finnish-approved laboratory specifically?"
    answer: "No. The laboratory must be approved under Commission Decision 2000/258/EC, which is the EU list. Finland, as an EU member state, accepts any laboratory on this EU-approved list. You do not need a separately Finland-approved lab. The same EU-approved laboratories used for EU entry (ANSES in France, AHVLA in the UK, Kansas State University in the US, and others) are accepted by Ruokavirasto."
