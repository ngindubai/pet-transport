  h1: "Pet Transport from Turkmenistan to United States"
  overview: |
    Turkmenistan Airlines flies to Frankfurt twice a week. That's about as close to the US as the national carrier gets, which tells you most of what you need to know about this route before the paperwork even comes into it.

    CDC treats Turkmenistan as high-risk for dog rabies, current as of its 15 April 2026 list update, so dogs need a valid serology titre or a 28-day quarantine booking at a CDC-registered facility. Cats need neither: APHIS applies no federal health requirement to pet cats at all.

    The realistic route runs via Istanbul on Turkish Airlines, or via Dubai using flydubai and onward Emirates connections. Either way, plan for 24 to 30 weeks given the combination of CDC's medical timeline and Ashgabat's genuinely thin flight schedule.
  sections:
    - heading: "Two bottlenecks, not one"
      body: |
        Most CDC high-risk routes on this site have one real bottleneck: the rabies serology titre test and its timing requirements. This route has two. Flight capacity out of Ashgabat is scarce enough that booking itself becomes a planning risk, separate from anything medical.

        Turkish Airlines' nonstop Ashgabat-Istanbul service, connecting to its own US network, is the most dependable single-carrier option. The Dubai routing via flydubai and Emirates works too, but adds a transfer and brings in Gulf summer heat embargoes as a seasonal complication.

        Don't assume you can book flights at the last minute once the medical side clears. Start looking at schedules the moment you confirm your travel window.
    - heading: "Dogs, cats and the CDC divide"
      body: |
        A dog from Turkmenistan needs the full foreign-vaccinated pathway: microchip before vaccination, vaccination at 12 weeks or older, a titre blood draw timed to sit between 30 days after the jab and 28 days before entry, and a government-vet-endorsed certification form good for just 30 days.

        A cat from the same household needs almost none of it. No CDC form, no titre, no facility booking. Check the destination US state's own agriculture rules, since some add requirements CDC doesn't, but at federal level the gap between the two species is the single biggest fact worth knowing before you start.
    - heading: "The Alabai and Turkmenistan's closed administration"
      body: |
        The Alabai, Turkmenistan's national dog breed, cannot leave the country under any export process. If your move involves a dog with significant Alabai ancestry, confirm eligibility with the State Veterinary Service before committing to anything else.

        More broadly, Turkmenistan runs one of the most centrally controlled administrative systems for any origin country on this site. English-language guidance is sparse, processing pace is slower than the CDC's own document validity windows sometimes allow for comfortably, and a relocation agent with direct experience of the country is close to essential rather than a nice-to-have.
