content:
  h1: "Pet Transport from Namibia to Australia"
  overview: |
    Namibia cannot export a pet to Australia directly. That rules out a straightforward booking before anything else gets planned: DAFF classifies Namibia as non-approved, so the pet has to live in an approved country, South Africa, the USA, the UK or Singapore among the realistic choices, for 180 unbroken days before Australia's import process even opens.

    South Africa deserves particular attention here, not because it's faster on paper, but because Johannesburg is already the hub almost every Namibian pet export passes through anyway. Staging there means using a connection the move needs regardless, rather than opening an entirely separate relocation somewhere the pet has never been. Add the RNATT titre test South Africa's own DAFF tier requires, and Mickleham's 10-day quarantine on arrival, and 40 to 52 weeks is a realistic floor for this route.

  sections:
    - heading: "Why there's no shortcut, and why South Africa is still worth naming"
      body: |
        DAFF sorts import countries into approved groups and everyone else. Namibia falls outside every approved group, the same position as most of Africa beyond South Africa itself. An owner cannot fly a pet from Windhoek to Sydney or Perth with the right paperwork; DAFF requires 180 consecutive days of residency in an approved country first, verified against travel and veterinary records for the whole period.

        South Africa is one of the approved countries, and it happens to be where nearly every Namibian pet export already goes for its first flight connection. That doesn't make the DAFF process any shorter, South Africa carries the same RNATT titre test and 180-day wait as the USA or UK, but it does mean the staging move itself doesn't require building a relationship with an unfamiliar country from a standing start.

    - heading: "Choosing a staging country: South Africa, Singapore, or further afield"
      body: |
        The choice shapes both the timeline and the logistics, if not always the raw number of weeks. South Africa's RNATT titre test and 180-day wait can run in parallel with the residency requirement itself, so a blood draw arranged early in the stay doesn't necessarily add time on top of the 180 days. Singapore skips the RNATT step for the Australia leg entirely, which can trim the schedule, but getting a pet into Singapore in the first place, and finding somewhere to live there for six months, has its own set of hurdles that Johannesburg doesn't for a Namibian owner.

        There's no universally right answer. Owners with family, work or existing ties to the USA or UK sometimes find it easier to stage there despite the extra RNATT step, while those without a fixed preference often find South Africa the path of least resistance simply because the first leg out of Namibia already goes there.

    - heading: "Mickleham, the final step regardless of staging country"
      body: |
        Whichever country the pet spends its 180 days in, the Australia leg ends the same way: a mandatory 10-day stay at Mickleham Post Entry Quarantine Facility in Melbourne, DAFF's only approved facility for imported cats and dogs. Book the slot as soon as the staging country's own export health certificate date is confirmed, and plan the flight to land on a weekday, since Mickleham does not accept weekend arrivals.
