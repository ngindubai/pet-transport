content:
  h1: "Pet Transport from Macau to Switzerland"
  overview: |
    Switzerland isn't in the EU, but its pet import rules track the EU system closely enough that Macau's own position looks familiar. The Federal Food Safety and Veterinary Office treats Macau as a higher-risk third country, meaning the titre test and a multi-month wait apply, the same shape as Macau's routes into Germany or the Netherlands.

    Switzerland adds two things of its own. A minimum age of 7 months for any pet entering directly by air from a higher-risk country, and its own FSVO import licence requirement, a step some EU destinations skip entirely.

  sections:
    - heading: "What FSVO adds that the EU doesn't"
      body: |
        The titre test itself runs the way it does everywhere Macau isn't listed: blood at least 30 days after vaccination, a result above 0.5 IU/mL, then a wait, 3 months here, before travel. Switzerland layers two extra conditions on top. First, a hard minimum age of 7 months for direct air entry from a higher-risk origin, no exceptions for an otherwise fully vaccinated younger animal. Second, an import licence from the FSVO itself, applied for ahead of the flight, a step the Netherlands' EU pathway doesn't require in the same form.

        None of this is punitive. It reflects Switzerland running its own parallel system rather than a shared EU one, even where the underlying logic matches closely.

    - heading: "SWISS flies the Hong Kong sector almost every day"
      body: |
        Macau International Airport has no long-haul cargo route, so the crate crosses by road over the Hong Kong-Zhuhai-Macau Bridge, or by ferry, in about an hour, before clearing Hong Kong's cargo terminal. From there, SWISS runs its Zurich-Hong Kong passenger route roughly 14 times a week, a 12-hour flight, with cargo bookable through SWISS WorldCargo. Cathay Pacific's near-daily Airbus A350 service on the same city pair is a solid alternative if SWISS capacity is booked out.

  - question: "Can a young puppy or kitten travel from Macau to Switzerland?"
    answer: "Not before 7 months old. Switzerland refuses entry from a higher-risk third country, which includes Macau, to any dog, cat or ferret under that age, since the full vaccination and titre sequence cannot be completed in time."
  - question: "Does Macau share the EU's exemption gap when entering Switzerland?"
    answer: "Yes, in effect. Macau isn't confirmed as listed or low-risk under Switzerland's own third-country system, which closely mirrors the EU's, so the full titre test and a 3-month wait apply."
  - question: "Does Switzerland require an import permit that the EU doesn't?"
    answer: "Yes. A pet entering Switzerland directly by air from a higher-risk third country needs an FSVO import licence, applied for ahead of travel, a step that sits alongside the titre test and health certificate rather than replacing them."
  - question: "Is there a direct flight from Macau to Switzerland for pets?"
    answer: "No. Pets transfer overland from Macau to Hong Kong first, roughly an hour by bridge coach or ferry, then fly cargo, most commonly with SWISS or Cathay Pacific, direct to Zurich."
