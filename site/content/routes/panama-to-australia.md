  sections:
    - heading: "Australia Group 3: why this route takes 8-10 months"
      body: |
        DAFF classifies countries into groups based on rabies risk and biosecurity profile. Group 1 countries (UK, New Zealand, Singapore, and others) have the shortest requirements. Group 3 countries have significant requirements.

        Panama is a Group 3 country. The RNATT (Rabies Neutralising Antibody Titre Test) is required, and Australia's waiting period after the test is 180 days, not 90 as the EU requires. This 180-day wait, combined with the DAFF import permit process, the vaccination and testing sequence, and the health certificate timing, adds up to a 32 to 40 week project.

        On arrival, 10 days of mandatory quarantine at the Australian Centre for Disease Preparedness (ACPD) in Mickleham, Victoria are required for all Group 3 arrivals. Quarantine is managed by DAFF and must be booked in advance. Costs are significant (AUD 2,000-3,500 depending on pet size).

    - heading: "Cargo routing: Panama to Australia is a long journey"
      body: |
        There is no direct cargo service from Panama to Australia. The two main routing options are via South America (LATAM via Sao Paulo or Santiago then to Sydney) or via Europe (Air France-KLM via CDG or AMS then to Sydney or Melbourne). Both are long journeys, typically 30 hours or more with connections.

        Copa Airlines from Tocumen (PTY) connects to US hubs, which then connect onward to Australia, but this routing adds complexity. LATAM via South America is often the more streamlined option for Panama-to-Australia cargo.

        Given the total journey length and the quarantine requirement on arrival, working with an experienced IPATA-registered pet transport agent is strongly recommended for this corridor. The logistics coordination is complex.

Both must be done at an approved laboratory, and the laboratory must be specifically recognised by the relevant authority (DAFF for Australia, EU for Europe)."
