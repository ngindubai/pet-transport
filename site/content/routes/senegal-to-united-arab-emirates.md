  overview: |
    A titre test stands between a Senegalese pet and UAE entry. MOCCAE's low-risk list doesn't include Senegal, so a FAVN or ELISA blood test at an accredited lab is mandatory, the same requirement that applies to well over a hundred other origin countries under the UAE's system.

    What the UAE doesn't add is a fixed wait afterwards. Compare that with the UK, where this same pet faces a rigid 90-day pause once the titre clears. MOCCAE simply doesn't publish an equivalent hold, which trims a genuine chunk of time off the total process.

    Emirates flies Dakar to Dubai direct several times a week, which keeps the flight side of this move refreshingly uncomplicated once the paperwork is sorted.

  sections:
    - heading: "The titre test, and the wait that isn't there"
      body: |
        Senegal sits outside MOCCAE's published low-risk rabies country list, so the standard unlisted-country pathway applies: microchip, vaccination, a 21-day wait, then a FAVN or ELISA blood draw at an accredited laboratory showing at least 0.5 IU/ml.

        Here's the practical difference from a UK move with the same paperwork. The UK imposes a fixed 90-day wait after a successful result, no exceptions. MOCCAE's published guidance carries no equivalent mandatory hold once the titre passes. That doesn't mean the process is instant. Lab turnaround and permit processing still take weeks. It does mean the single biggest fixed delay on the UK route simply isn't part of the UAE one.

    - heading: "Getting from Dakar to Dubai or Abu Dhabi"
      body: |
        Emirates operates the most direct option: Dakar to Dubai, roughly nine and a quarter hours, about five times a week, through Emirates SkyCargo's live animal service. Air France, codesharing with Air Senegal through Paris, is a solid alternative if Emirates' capacity is tight, and Turkish Airlines via Istanbul rounds out the realistic choices.

        Whichever carrier you use, apply for the emirate-level import permit as soon as your titre result is confirmed rather than waiting for a fixed clock to run out, since none applies here.
