content:
  h1: "Pet Transport from Honduras to the United States"
  overview: |
    The CDC lists Honduras as high-risk for dog rabies, and that single classification does most of the work in shaping this route. A dog needs either a rabies titre test, drawn at least 30 days after vaccination and aged at least 28 days by the time it lands, or a 28-day quarantine at a CDC-registered facility once it arrives. Cats sail through with a routine health check.

    A dog's vaccination history matters more than where it currently lives. One vaccinated in the US before the move to Honduras, with the paperwork filed before departure, can skip the titre test and the facility reservation entirely on the way back. A dog vaccinated only in Honduras gets no such shortcut.

    The flight side has narrowed. United and Delta have both stopped carrying pets as general cargo, reserving that space for military and State Department moves. American Airlines, flying Miami and Dallas-Fort Worth from San Pedro Sula and Tegucigalpa, is the carrier most owners will end up booking.

  sections:
    - heading: "Titre test or 28-day quarantine: picking the pathway"
      body: |
        Once the microchip and vaccination are in the right order, the titre test blood draw has to wait at least 30 days. The lab result then has to sit for a further 28 days before the dog can legally enter the US, counted from the blood draw date rather than the day the result arrives. A dog under 6 months old cannot use this pathway at all, since CDC's minimum entry age is 6 months regardless of which route is chosen.

        Add it up from a standing start (vaccination, 30-day wait, blood draw, lab turnaround, 28-day ageing period, paperwork) and 3 to 4 months is realistic. Owners who would rather skip the wait can choose the 28-day US quarantine pathway instead, paying for a stay at a CDC-registered facility on arrival rather than clearing the titre test beforehand. It costs more and separates the owner from the pet for nearly a month, but it removes the pre-travel testing window entirely.

    - heading: "SENASA's export side moves faster than the US import side"
      body: |
        Getting a pet out of Honduras is the quick half of this move. A licensed Honduran vet checks the animal is fit to travel and the vaccination card is current, then the whole application goes through SENASA's online SECEH platform for a flat Lps 450 fee plus two Lps 10 stamps. Approval usually lands in an inbox within minutes.

        Don't mistake that speed for the whole picture. The SENASA certificate confirms the pet is healthy and legally exportable; it has no bearing on whether the CDC titre test has cleared. Those two processes run on entirely separate clocks, and SENASA's side will always finish first, sometimes months before the US side is ready.

    - heading: "Why American Airlines is doing most of the flying now"
      body: |
        San Pedro Sula's Ramón Villeda Morales airport and Tegucigalpa's Palmerola International both connect to the US almost entirely through American Airlines these days: Miami from both, plus Dallas-Fort Worth from Palmerola. American's PetEmbark and ExpediteFS cargo channels remain open to the general public at aacargo.com.

        United still flies passengers between Palmerola and Houston, but its live-animal cargo booking is currently restricted to active-duty military and State Department staff moving under official orders, not a general relocation. Avianca will carry a pet as accompanied checked baggage on some of its Central American connections, within tight weight and age limits, but it is not a substitute for American's direct Miami service on this route. Miami's CDC-registered facility is another reason it tends to be the default port of entry for a foreign-vaccinated dog from Honduras.

  - question: "What is the difference between the titre test pathway and the quarantine pathway?"
    answer: "The titre test pathway requires a blood draw at least 30 days after vaccination, a result of 0.5 IU/mL or above from a USDA-recognised laboratory, and a result that is at least 28 days old at US entry, plus a CDC-registered facility reservation. The quarantine pathway skips the pre-travel testing and instead has the dog complete a 28-day stay at a CDC-registered facility on arrival, at the owner's expense. Source: CDC, cdc.gov/importation."
  - question: "Does my dog need a CDC-registered facility reservation to enter the US from Honduras?"
    answer: "If the dog was vaccinated in Honduras, yes, a reservation at a CDC-registered animal care facility at the port of entry is required alongside the titre test or quarantine pathway. A dog vaccinated in the US before it moved to Honduras, with a USDA APHIS-endorsed Certification of U.S.-Issued Rabies Vaccination completed before departure, can skip this and enter at any airport, seaport, or land crossing. Source: CDC, cdc.gov/importation."
