  h1: "Pet Transport from Hungary to Armenia"
  overview: |
    Moving a dog or cat from Budapest to Yerevan is one of the more straightforward routes leaving Hungary for the Caucasus. Armenia does not require a titre test from EU countries and there is no quarantine period for compliant arrivals, which means the preparation window runs to around 3-5 weeks rather than the 5-6 month process that applies on the reverse journey.

    The key document is the Nébih-endorsed EU-format health certificate, issued by an authorised official vet in Hungary and stamped by the National Food Chain Safety Office. Turkish Airlines Cargo via Istanbul is the standard routing from Budapest Ferenc Liszt (BUD) to Yerevan Zvartnots (EVN). On arrival, the State Service for Food Safety (SSFS) carries out a brief inspection before releasing the pet.

    One point to confirm early: your pet must be older than 4 months on the date of entry to Armenia. SSFS inspectors at Zvartnots check this.

  sections:
    - heading: "What Nébih requires before your pet leaves Hungary"
      body: |
        All cats and dogs leaving Hungary for a third country must travel on an official animal health certificate issued under EU rules and endorsed by Nébih (Nemzeti Élelmiszerlánc-biztonsági Hivatal). The process has two steps: first, an authorised official vet examines the pet and issues the certificate; second, Nébih endorses it. Allow 1-2 working days for endorsement after the vet appointment, and book the appointment at least a week before travel to avoid deadline pressure.

        The certificate is valid for 10 days from the date of the vet examination. That 10-day clock runs to the day the pet arrives in Armenia, not to the BUD departure date. If your routing includes a layover, count from the EVN arrival date backwards to confirm your appointment timing is correct.

        No export permit is required for personal pets leaving Hungary. The health certificate is the only official document needed on the Hungarian side. Your pet's vaccination record (EU pet passport or equivalent booklet) travels with the certificate and is presented to SSFS on arrival.

    - heading: "SSFS inspection at Yerevan Zvartnots"
      body: |
        The State Service for Food Safety (SSFS, ssfs.am) handles all live animal arrivals at Zvartnots International Airport (EVN). Inspectors check three things: the Nébih-endorsed health certificate, the rabies vaccination record, and the microchip reading against the certificate. For cats and dogs from EU countries, no titre test is required.

        The rabies vaccination must have been administered at least 21 days before the pet enters Armenia and must still be within its valid period (generally up to 11 months, or within the manufacturer's stated duration). If your pet was vaccinated within the last 11 months and the EU pet passport shows a current vaccination, no new injection is needed before the trip.

        SSFS does not require a pre-arrival import permit for 1 to 2 personal pets. If you are moving more than two animals, contact SSFS directly at ssfs.am before making travel arrangements, as the process differs.

    - heading: "Routing from Budapest to Yerevan"
      body: |
        No direct cargo flight operates between BUD and EVN for live animals. Turkish Airlines Cargo provides the most reliable routing: BUD to Istanbul (IST), then IST to EVN. Turkish Airlines operates multiple daily BUD-IST flights, giving reasonable scheduling choice, and the IST-EVN segment connects well. Total transit time BUD to EVN is typically 5-7 hours.

        Book through a Turkish Airlines Cargo agent or an IPATA-registered pet transport agent. Confirm live animal acceptance on the specific flight and crate dimensions well ahead of travel, not on the day. Temperature restrictions can affect cargo acceptance during summer: if temperatures at BUD, IST, or EVN exceed 29-30 degrees Celsius on the travel date, the airline may decline the booking. Summer moves need earlier confirmation and a backup plan.

        Wizz Air is headquartered in Budapest and operates frequently out of BUD, but does not accept live animal cargo in the hold in 2026. LOT Polish Airlines via Warsaw offers an alternative if Turkish Airlines dates do not suit your schedule.

    answer: "No. Armenia does not require a titre test for pets arriving from EU member states. Hungary is an EU country, so the standard Nébih-endorsed health certificate and a current rabies vaccination are sufficient. The titre test requirement applies to pets arriving from countries with high rabies risk, not from the EU."
  - question: "How long does it take to prepare a pet for travel from Hungary to Armenia?"
    answer: "Allow 3-5 weeks. The main preparation steps are confirming the microchip, ensuring the rabies vaccination is current (it needs to have been given at least 21 days before entry), booking cargo on Turkish Airlines, and obtaining the Nébih-endorsed health certificate within 10 days of travel. There is no quarantine or waiting period beyond the 21-day vaccination requirement."
  - question: "Which airline carries pets from Budapest to Yerevan?"
    answer: "Turkish Airlines Cargo is the primary option, routing via Istanbul. There is no direct cargo flight between BUD and EVN. Book through a cargo agent and confirm live animal acceptance on the specific dates, as temperature restrictions apply in summer months."
  - question: "Is there quarantine for pets arriving in Armenia from Hungary?"
    answer: "No. Compliant pets, those with a valid health certificate, current rabies vaccination, and the correct microchip, are released after the SSFS inspection at Zvartnots. No quarantine period applies for EU arrivals meeting the entry conditions."
  - question: "Can I take my pet as cabin baggage from Budapest to Yerevan?"
    answer: "Not via cargo carriers, which handle pets as manifest cargo only. Small pets in some cases travel in-cabin on passenger services, but the BUD-IST-EVN routing on Turkish Airlines would need to be confirmed directly with the passenger side of the airline for cabin rules. Most pet moves from Hungary to Armenia are handled as cargo in IATA-compliant crates."
