    - heading: "Getting the Czech SVA inspection right on arrival"
      body: |
        The SVA border vet at Prague Vaclav Havel cargo terminal carries out a six-point check on arrival. They scan the microchip (matching the 15-digit number against the AHC), confirm the chip was implanted before the vaccination date, check the vaccination record is current, verify the FAVN titre test result and blood draw date, confirm 90 days have elapsed from that blood draw date to the travel date on the AHC, and check the AHC was issued within the past 10 days.

        Preparing the documentation in clear order before travel helps this process go smoothly. Present: the original AHC with State Veterinary Service endorsement, the original FAVN titre test laboratory report, the vaccination record (in a format the SVA vet can read), and the microchip registration document.

  - question: "Does the Czech Republic accept the Azerbaijani State Veterinary Service health certificate?"
    answer: "Yes. The SVA accepts health certificates endorsed by the competent authority of any third country, provided the certificate follows the EU format required by Regulation 2020/692 for unlisted third countries. The Azerbaijani State Veterinary Service is the competent authority for Azerbaijan and their endorsement satisfies the Czech SVA requirement."
  - question: "What if my pet's FAVN titre test result is borderline?"
    answer: "The minimum acceptable result is 0.5 IU/ml. Any result below this is considered a failure. If the result is exactly at or above 0.5 IU/ml, it passes. There is no 'borderline' category. If the test fails, you must re-vaccinate if needed, wait 30 days, and draw blood again. The 90-day wait restarts from the new successful blood draw date."
