  h1: "Pet Transport from Dominican Republic to Singapore"
  overview: |
    Thirty days is the minimum stay at Singapore's Animal Quarantine Centre for any dog or cat arriving from the Dominican Republic, and that figure only applies once the harder part, a WOAH-prescribed rabies titre test with a fixed 90-day wait from the blood draw, has already cleared. AVS's current classification system runs on three schedules, not the four-tier naming this site's own background data still uses, and the Dominican Republic isn't named on Schedule I or Schedule II, which places it in Schedule III by default. Confirmed directly against AVS's import page, fetched 21 July 2026.

    None of that is unique to this one country. What makes the route itself distinctive is the flight. No carrier connects Santo Domingo to Singapore directly, or even close to it; the workable path goes via Doha on Qatar Airways and then Madrid on Iberia, the same Madrid leg this site has already verified for the Germany-Dominican Republic corridor. Budget four to five months, most of it the titre test clock, not the flight.

  sections:
    - heading: "Why Schedule III, not Schedule I or II"
      body: |
        AVS groups exporting countries into three tiers. Schedule I holds just four names: Australia, New Zealand, Ireland and the United Kingdom, with no quarantine at all. Schedule II lists 31 named countries, the USA, Canada, most of the EU, Japan and Hong Kong among them, with a shorter home-quarantine pathway for personal pets. The Dominican Republic appears in neither list, so it falls to Schedule III, the default tier for everywhere else.

        That single fact decides almost everything about this move. A Schedule III pet needs the WOAH titre test, not just a vaccination record, and faces a hard 30-day stay at the AQC on arrival rather than the shorter home quarantine a Schedule II pet gets. There's no route around it; it's simply where the Dominican Republic sits on AVS's current list.

    - heading: "The 90-day clock that sets your travel date"
      body: |
        Once the rabies vaccination is at least 28 days old, a blood sample goes to a WOAH reference laboratory, since the Dominican Republic has no lab of its own on the approved list. The result needs to clear 0.5 IU/ml. Then comes the part owners most often miscalculate: the certificate is only valid for export between 90 days and 12 months after that blood draw, not from the date the lab emails a result.

        Run the AVS import licence application and the AQC quarantine booking in parallel with this wait rather than after it. Both can be arranged while the 90-day clock is still running, which keeps the total timeline closer to four months than five.
