  sections:
    - heading: "CDC low-risk: what Taiwan's status means in practice"
      body: |
        Taiwan has been officially rabies-free since 2013 and the CDC classifies it as a low-risk country for dog rabies. The practical impact on this route is significant: dogs from Taiwan do not need to follow the CDC high-risk pathway, do not need to arrive at a CDC-approved airport, and do not need a facility reservation.

        The requirements for dogs from Taiwan are: a microchip, a current rabies vaccination, a CDC Dog Import Form completed before departure, and a BAPHIQ-endorsed health certificate. That is the full list for US entry. No quarantine, no titre test, no facility reservation.

        Cats from Taiwan face even fewer requirements: standard customs inspection on arrival, no US government health certificate requirement (though airlines may ask for one for the cargo booking).

    - heading: "Direct cargo from Taipei to the US"
      body: |
        EVA Air and China Airlines both operate direct cargo services from Taipei Taoyuan (TPE) to US West Coast cities including Los Angeles. This is a meaningful advantage: a direct service eliminates mid-journey hub transfers and reduces total journey time for your pet.

        EVA Air in particular has a strong reputation for live animal cargo handling and is the carrier most commonly recommended for this corridor by pet transport agents. Confirm live animal cargo availability on your specific flight when booking, as not every scheduled service accepts live animals.

        For US East Coast destinations, a connection is typically needed. Singapore Airlines or Cathay Pacific connections via their Asian hubs are used for some routings, depending on your destination city.

    - heading: "The CDC Dog Import Form"
      body: |
        The CDC requires a Dog Import Form to be completed for all dogs entering the US, regardless of origin. For dogs from low-risk countries like Taiwan, this form is a straightforward declaration: the dog's microchip number, vaccination details, and owner information. There is no advance registration or facility booking involved.

        The form is completed online on the CDC website before departure. Keep a copy of the completed form accessible for inspection when you arrive in the US. This is a simple step but one that should not be left to the last minute.

faqs:
  - question: "Does my dog from Taiwan need to go through a CDC facility when entering the US?"
    answer: "No. Taiwan is a CDC low-risk country, which means dogs from Taiwan do not need a CDC facility reservation. The high-risk pathway (facility reservation, approved airport arrival) applies to dogs from countries the CDC has classified as high-risk. Taiwan's rabies-free status since 2013 means your dog from Taiwan enters under the straightforward low-risk process: current vaccination, microchip, CDC Dog Import Form."
  - question: "Can my cat enter the US from Taiwan without a health certificate?"
    answer: "Yes, from a US government perspective. The US does not require a health certificate for cats arriving from Taiwan. Standard customs inspection applies. However, your airline will almost certainly require a health certificate for the cargo booking, so your vet in Taiwan will need to issue one for airline purposes even if it is not technically a US entry requirement."
  - question: "Which US airports receive direct cargo from Taiwan?"
    answer: "EVA Air and China Airlines both serve Los Angeles (LAX) with direct cargo services from Taipei. New York JFK and other East Coast cities typically require a connection. Confirm the current direct routes available on your travel dates when booking with your agent or directly with the airline."
  - question: "How much does it cost to ship a dog from Taiwan to the US?"
    answer: "For a medium-sized dog on direct cargo to Los Angeles, the airline cargo fee is typically USD 700 to USD 2,500 depending on crate size. Add the BAPHIQ-endorsed health certificate (around USD 50 to USD 120), an IATA-compliant crate if needed, and agent fees if you use one. Total all-in costs for this route are typically USD 1,000 to USD 4,000, which is lower than routes requiring titre tests or CDC facility stays."
  - question: "Is there a waiting period after rabies vaccination before my dog can travel from Taiwan to the US?"
    answer: "No. The US does not impose a waiting period after rabies vaccination for dogs from Taiwan (a low-risk country). The vaccine must be current and properly documented, but there is no 30-day or 90-day wait required before US entry. This is very different from the UK, which requires a titre test and three-month wait for dogs from Taiwan."
