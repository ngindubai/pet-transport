  h1: "Pet Transport from the USA to the Philippines"
  overview: |
    Moving a pet from the USA to the Philippines is one of the longest-lead-time routes in Southeast Asia. The BAI (Bureau of Animal Industry) requires a 180-day waiting period from the titre test blood draw date, a Sanitary and Phytosanitary Import Clearance (SPSIC) before travel, and a 30-day mandatory quarantine at a BAI-accredited facility on arrival. Allow at least 9-10 months from the start of planning.

    The US export process is relatively straightforward: a USDA-APHIS endorsed health certificate from a USDA-accredited vet. The complexity is on the Philippines side, where the timeline is dominated by the SPSIC application process and the 180-day wait. Philippine Airlines operates direct cargo from Los Angeles to Manila, which is the most convenient routing for most US pet owners.

    - heading: "The BAI SPSIC: applying from the USA"
      body: |
        The BAI Sanitary and Phytosanitary Import Clearance is required before your pet can board the flight. Applications go through the BAI online portal at bai.gov.ph. You will need to upload your pet's complete health records: microchip details, rabies vaccination certificate, titre test result, and the planned travel details.

        The BAI processes the application and issues a clearance document with a reference number. This number must appear on the USDA-APHIS health certificate. The SPSIC is typically valid for a specific travel window, so apply only once you have a realistic travel date in mind and the 180-day wait has been calculated.

        The USDA-APHIS health certificate is the standard US export document: issued by a USDA-accredited vet, endorsed by APHIS with the original USDA stamp, and valid for 10 days from examination to Philippines arrival. Photocopies and electronic copies are not accepted by the BAI at the quarantine facility.

        The 30-day quarantine begins on the day your pet arrives at Manila Ninoy Aquino (MNL). The BAI-accredited facility handles all health checks during the quarantine period. Plan your arrival in the Philippines to allow for the full quarantine period before you need your pet with you at your final destination.
