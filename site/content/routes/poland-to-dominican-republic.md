content:
  h1: "Pet Transport from Poland to Dominican Republic"
  overview: |
    Moving a pet from Poland to the Dominican Republic is a moderate-complexity route with no quarantine on arrival. The Dominican Republic's rules are clear: microchip, rabies vaccination given 30 days to 12 months before entry, core vaccinations current, and an official health certificate issued within 15 days of travel. Up to five personal pets may travel without an import permit.

    Poland's export side is administered by the GIW (Glowny Inspektorat Weterynarii / Chief Veterinary Inspectorate). The health certificate must be issued by a district-level official vet (Powiatowy Lekarz Weterynarii) and then endorsed by GIW. This is not the same as a standard private clinic appointment; you need to contact your district veterinary authority. Warsaw Chopin Airport (WAW) handles all international cargo departures.

    KLM from Amsterdam, Air France from Paris CDG, and Iberia via Madrid all serve Santo Domingo (SDQ) from Warsaw as cargo connections. LOT Polish Airlines connects Warsaw to several European hubs, which can simplify the cargo transfer arrangement. Allow four to six weeks from preparation to departure.

  sections:
    - heading: "Poland Export Requirements"
      body: |
        The GIW (Glowny Inspektorat Weterynarii, wetgiw.gov.pl) oversees Poland's official veterinary certification for pet exports. The health certificate must be issued by the Powiatowy Lekarz Weterynarii at district level: this is the official district vet authority, not a regular private clinic. Once signed by the official vet, the certificate goes to GIW for endorsement. Allow one to three working days for the endorsement to be completed.

        The EU-format health certificate is valid for 10 days from the date of issue. Since the Dominican Republic requires the certificate to have been issued within 15 days of travel, there is no conflict between the two conditions: a certificate issued within 10 days of travel satisfies both requirements. Confirm the exact Powiatowy Lekarz Weterynarii for your district early; some districts may have waiting lists for official certification appointments.

    - heading: "Dominican Republic Import Rules"
      body: |
        DGAG (Direccion General de Ganaderia, agricultura.gob.do) inspects all arriving pets at Las Americas International Airport (SDQ) in Santo Domingo. Inspectors check the microchip, rabies vaccination record, core vaccination status, and the health certificate. Dogs must have current DHPP coverage; cats must have panleukopenia, rhinotracheitis, and calicivirus. There is no titre test requirement.

        Arrival inspection at SDQ is generally quick when documents are complete. No advance permit is needed, and there is no quarantine. If you are travelling with more than five pets, different rules apply; contact DGAG before travelling. Documents in English are accepted, though Spanish copies alongside them can help.

    - heading: "Airline Connections from Warsaw"
      body: |
        Warsaw Chopin Airport (WAW) has good cargo connections to the Caribbean via European hubs. KLM via Amsterdam (AMS) offers reliable cargo services to SDQ. Air France via Paris CDG and Iberia via Madrid (MAD) also connect from Warsaw to Santo Domingo. LOT Polish Airlines operates a domestic and European network from WAW that can serve as the first leg for cargo transferred to a Caribbean-bound carrier at the hub.

        Cargo pricing from Warsaw to SDQ runs EUR 700-1,800 depending on animal size and crate. The IATA crate, health certificate, and any agent fees add EUR 400-1,000 on top of that. Most single-pet moves from Poland to the Dominican Republic land in the EUR 1,200-2,500 range all-in. Book cargo at least four weeks in advance, and confirm acceptance with the airline's live animal desk for your specific animal and crate size.

    - heading: "Vaccination and Documentation Timing"
      body: |
        The timing sequence for this route is relatively forgiving compared to more complex destinations. The rabies vaccination must be given at least 30 days before entry, so that is the earliest fixed point. Core vaccinations should be current at the time of travel. The official health certificate should be booked for the week before departure to keep it within both the 10-day EU validity and the 15-day Dominican Republic requirement.

        Keep all vaccination records organised and accessible on the day of travel. DGAG inspectors will want to see the original vaccination booklet or a printed vaccination certificate alongside the official health certificate. Presenting clear, complete documentation at the airline's cargo desk and on arrival at SDQ makes the process much smoother.

faqs:
  - question: "Who issues the official health certificate in Poland?"
    answer: "The certificate must be issued by a Powiatowy Lekarz Weterynarii (district official vet), authorised by GIW (Glowny Inspektorat Weterynarii). This is a district-level government veterinary authority, not a private clinic. Contact your local district veterinary office to find the right contact."
  - question: "Does my cat need any vaccinations for the Dominican Republic?"
    answer: "Yes. Cats need a valid rabies vaccination (30 days to 12 months before entry), plus panleukopenia, rhinotracheitis, and calicivirus vaccinations current at the time of travel."
  - question: "Is there quarantine in the Dominican Republic?"
    answer: "No. DGAG carries out a documentary and physical inspection at Las Americas International Airport (SDQ). If documents are in order, the pet is released on arrival."
  - question: "Can I travel with more than one pet?"
    answer: "Up to five personal pets may enter the Dominican Republic without an import permit. If you are travelling with more than five, contact DGAG at agricultura.gob.do before your trip."
  - question: "How far in advance should I book the cargo flight?"
    answer: "Book at least four to six weeks before travel. Cargo capacity for live animals is limited, and KLM, Air France, and Iberia all require confirmation of crate dimensions and animal details before acceptance."
