content:
  h1: "Pet Transport Estonia to Italy: 2026 Guide"
  overview: "Estonia to Italy is a straightforward EU-to-EU pet move. Both countries operate under the same EU pet travel rules, so the paperwork is minimal: a microchip, a current rabies vaccination, and an EU pet passport (or an AHC if you are a non-EU national residing in Estonia). There is no quarantine, no titre test, and no import permit. The main practical task is finding a carrier that accepts pets, since the most popular budget routes from Tallinn to Italy do not take animals. Full-service carriers connecting through Riga, Warsaw, or Frankfurt are the better options."

  sections:
    - heading: "Which airlines actually accept pets on Tallinn to Italy routes"
      body: |
        Tallinn Airport (TLL) has direct or connecting services to Italian cities including Rome, Milan, Venice, and Bologna. The bad news for pet owners: most of the cheapest fares on this corridor are with Ryanair or Wizz Air, neither of which accepts pets on board in any form.

        The good options are full-service carriers. airBaltic connects TLL to its Riga hub (RIX) and from RIX offers connections to Italian cities, accepting small pets in cabin. Lufthansa routes via Frankfurt (FRA) and accepts small pets in cabin on EU routes; large dogs travel as cargo. LOT Polish Airlines routes via Warsaw Chopin (WAW) with connections to Italian destinations and has a clear cabin pet policy for small animals.

        For a large dog that must travel as cargo, Lufthansa via Frankfurt is usually the most reliable option for Tallinn-to-Italy, given Frankfurt's Animal Lounge and the airline's established large-dog cargo procedures.

    - heading: "Italy's pet registration and microchipping requirements for long-term stays"
      body: |
        If you are relocating to Italy permanently or for an extended period, Italy has domestic pet registration rules beyond the EU travel requirements. Dogs must be registered in Italy's national pet register (Anagrafe Canina) within 30 days of establishing residence, and they must carry a tag with your Italian address and contact details.

        Cats are not required to be registered in Italy's national system, though some regions have local cat registration schemes. Check with your local municipality (Comune) for the requirements in your specific Italian city.

        These domestic registration obligations are separate from the travel documentation requirements. Your EU pet passport covers the travel; the Italian registration is a post-arrival administrative step.

Confirm your crate dimensions with the cargo carrier's specific aircraft for your route before purchasing or building a crate."
