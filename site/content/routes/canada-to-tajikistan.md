content:
  h1: "Pet Transport from Canada to Tajikistan"
  overview: |
    No blood test stands between a Canadian pet and entry into Tajikistan. That puts this route in a lighter category than most of the unlisted-country moves this site covers, and the ease runs further than it might first appear: Canada's own import rules for dogs don't tie a titre-test requirement to country of origin either, so there's no equivalent sting waiting on the return leg the way there is for some other nationalities.

    The trade-off sits elsewhere. Tajikistan takes non-compliance seriously. A pet that turns up with paperwork that doesn't match can face quarantine, be sent home, or in the worst cases be euthanised, all at the owner's cost. There is also no direct flight. Turkish Airlines via Istanbul, Air Astana via a European hub to Almaty, and Emirates via Dubai to Somon Air are the three realistic ways to reach Dushanbe from a Canadian gateway.

  sections:
    - heading: "Why the titre test disappears here, and why the stakes still feel high"
      body: |
        Tajikistan does not require a rabies antibody titre test on a pet entering from any country, Canada included. Instead, a current rabies vaccination given between 30 days and 12 months before entry, and a licensed vet's health certificate confirming good health and no unhealed wounds, cover the medical side. Puppies and kittens cannot be vaccinated before 12 weeks old, and then need the full 30-day wait before flying.

        The lighter medical bar doesn't mean a relaxed one. Tajikistan's own guidance is explicit that a pet without compliant paperwork can be quarantined, sent home, or in the most serious cases euthanised, entirely at the owner's expense. That's a harder consequence than several other Central Asian destinations apply for the same kind of mistake, so this isn't a route to wing the documentation on.

    - heading: "Three ways from Canada to Dushanbe"
      body: |
        Turkish Airlines runs a direct Toronto-Istanbul service, connecting to roughly seven weekly Istanbul-Dushanbe flights, with small pets travelling in the cabin under 8kg and larger pets through Turkish Cargo. Air Astana doesn't reach Canada directly, so the practical routing goes via a European hub such as Frankfurt or Amsterdam, both reachable nonstop from Toronto, then on to Almaty and a further Air Astana connection to Dushanbe around five times a week.

        Emirates covers the Toronto-Dubai leg direct, feeding into Somon Air's Dubai-Dushanbe service, though Gulf summer heat embargoes can disrupt that transfer between roughly May and September.

    - heading: "One clock, not the usual two"
      body: |
        Most of this site's Central Asian and Balkan routes involve either a titre-test wait or a foreign import permit sitting alongside the CFIA process. This one doesn't. The genuine planning constraint is timing the CFIA regional office appointment against Tajikistan's own preference for a certificate issued close to the actual travel date, rather than weeks in advance.

        Confirm the flight first, then book the CFIA appointment to land in the days before it, not the other way round.
