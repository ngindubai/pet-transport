content:
  h1: "Pet Transport from the United States to Malawi"
  overview: |
    Malawi sits on the CDC's high-risk list for dog rabies, a fact that matters enormously for a Malawi-origin dog trying to enter the US, and not at all for a US-origin pet heading the other way. That asymmetry is the single most useful thing to understand about this route before anything else.

    Once the CDC piece is set aside, what's left is a USDA-endorsed health certificate and a permit from the Director of Animal Health at Malawi's DAHLD. No titre test, no facility reservation, no 28-day quarantine fallback. Just a permit application that needs to start early, since no processing timeline is published.

    Budget ten to fourteen weeks. Most of that is DAHLD's permit turnaround, not anything happening on the US side.

  sections:
    - heading: "Why the CDC's high-risk list doesn't apply here"
      body: |
        It's easy to assume Malawi's CDC classification cuts both ways. It doesn't. That list, and the titre test, facility reservation and 28-day quarantine fallback it can trigger, governs a dog travelling into the United States. A US pet travelling into Malawi is subject to Malawian import rules, full stop, and those rules don't include a titre requirement for any country of origin.

        Worth double-checking with DAHLD directly before travel regardless, since a rule that isn't formally published in English is also a rule that's harder to verify has stayed the same.

    - heading: "The DAHLD import permit and where Qatar Airways fits in"
      body: |
        Malawi requires an import permit from the Director of Animal Health, and the US doesn't qualify for the SADC exemption that spares neighbouring African countries the application. Once issued, the permit runs for six months, which gives useful flexibility if a flight date slips.

        On the flight itself, Kenya Airways via Nairobi and Ethiopian Airlines via Addis Ababa are the established options. Qatar Airways' October 2025 codeshare on Kenya Airways' network adds a Doha-routed alternative from a US Qatar Airways gateway, useful if neither of the other two schedules matches travel plans. It's a new arrangement, so confirm live animal terms directly rather than assuming they mirror Kenya Airways' long-standing policy.
