  h1: "Pet Transport Uruguay to Finland: 2026 Guide"
  overview: "Moving a dog or cat from Uruguay to Finland requires the FAVN titre test and 90-day wait (Uruguay is classified as unlisted under EU Regulation 2026/636), tapeworm treatment for dogs 1-5 days before arrival, and entry exclusively through Helsinki-Vantaa Airport (HEL).   sections:
    - heading: "FAVN titre test and the 90-day wait"
      body: |
        Uruguay is classified as an unlisted third country under EU Implementing Regulation 2026/636. Ruokavirasto (the Finnish Food Authority) requires a passing FAVN titre test result before the health certificate can be issued. Blood must be drawn at least 30 days after the most recent rabies vaccination and must show at least 0.5 IU/ml at an EU-approved laboratory.

        The 90-day waiting period runs from the blood draw date. After the 90 days, the MGAP-accredited vet issues the CVI and MGAP-DGSG endorses it. Allow 1-2 working days for the MGAP-DGSG endorsement within the 10-day certificate window. For dogs, the tapeworm treatment must also be recorded in the CVI.

    - heading: "Tapeworm treatment and the HEL entry requirement"
      body: |
        All dogs entering Finland must receive praziquantel tapeworm treatment between 1 and 5 days before arrival at Helsinki-Vantaa. The vet records the product, dose, and date in the CVI. The window is measured against actual arrival at HEL. Cats are not subject to this requirement.

        Helsinki-Vantaa (HEL) is the only Finnish airport with a veterinary Border Control Post for non-EU arrivals. All cargo from Uruguay must arrive at HEL. Iberia and LATAM provide connections from Montevideo (MVD) via Madrid (MAD); Finnair then connects MAD to HEL. KLM via Amsterdam (AMS-HEL) is an alternative.
