content:
  h1: "Pet Transport Uruguay to Finland: 2026 Guide"
  overview: "Uruguay to Finland has two layers of preparation. The first is the standard EU unlisted country process: FAVN titre test at an EU-approved laboratory (blood drawn at least 30 days after vaccination, result 0.5 IU/ml or above), followed by a mandatory 90-day wait from the blood draw date. The second applies to dogs only: an Echinococcus treatment with praziquantel must be given between 24 and 120 hours before arriving in Finland. Missing either requirement causes problems at Helsinki-Vantaa airport. Plan for 20-26 weeks of preparation from first vet appointment to travel date."

  sections:
    - heading: "Finland's import requirements for pets from unlisted countries"
      body: |
        Finland's Finnish Food Authority (Ruokavirasto) applies EU Commission Delegated Regulation (EU) 2026/131 for pets arriving from unlisted third countries such as Uruguay. The requirements are: ISO 11784/11785 microchip implanted before the rabies vaccination; rabies vaccination with at least 21 days elapsed since the primary dose; FAVN titre test at an EU-approved laboratory, blood drawn at least 30 days after vaccination, with a result of 0.5 IU/ml or above; 90-day wait from the successful blood draw date; and a DGSG-endorsed EU Annex IV health certificate issued within 10 days of travel.

        Border inspection takes place at Helsinki-Vantaa Airport (HEL). Ruokavirasto inspectors check all documents on arrival. Pets must enter Finland at an approved border inspection post; HEL is the designated international airport entry point for most air shipments.

        For cats, the above is the complete process. Dogs have one further obligation.

    - heading: "Echinococcus treatment for dogs entering Finland"
      body: |
        All dogs entering Finland from countries outside Finland's list of exemptions (which covers Norway, Malta, and Ireland for direct arrivals) must be treated against Echinococcus before arrival. Uruguay is not on the exemption list. The treatment must be given 1-5 days (24-120 hours) before entry into Finland. The approved treatment contains praziquantel in a dose appropriate for the animal's weight.

        A veterinarian must issue a written treatment record stating the product name, the dose given, and the exact date and time of administration. This record is checked by Ruokavirasto inspectors at HEL alongside the health certificate.

        The practical question for Uruguay-to-Finland shipments is where to administer the treatment. Calculating the treatment window: if your dog arrives at HEL at 10:00 on Friday, the window opens at 10:00 on Wednesday (24 hours before) and closes at 10:00 on Monday the previous week (120 hours before). Given a transit of 30-48 hours from MVD, the treatment is typically administered just before departure from Montevideo or at a European hub layover. Discuss timing with your freight forwarder based on the specific itinerary.

    - heading: "Routing from Montevideo to Helsinki"
      body: |
        Carrasco International Airport (MVD) in Montevideo does not have direct cargo connections to Helsinki-Vantaa (HEL). The typical routing is: MVD to Sao Paulo (GRU) or Buenos Aires (EZE), then transatlantic to a European hub (Frankfurt FRA, Amsterdam AMS, Paris CDG, or Madrid MAD), then a final leg to HEL.

        Finnair is the home carrier at HEL and operates cargo services from major European hubs. SAS also connects to Helsinki from its Scandinavian network. From Madrid, Iberia and Finnair have a MAD-HEL connection. From CDG, Air France and Finnair connect to Helsinki.

        LATAM Airlines covers the MVD-GRU or MVD-SCL transatlantic segment effectively. Iberia serves the Buenos Aires and MVD-MAD routes. Book cargo space well in advance, particularly on the HEL second leg, where live animal capacity can be limited.

    - heading: "The MGAP-DGSG export process"
      body: |
        Uruguay's official export authority for companion animals is the Direccion General de Servicios Ganaderos (DGSG), under the Ministerio de Ganaderia, Agricultura y Pesca (MGAP, mgap.gub.uy). A MGAP-registered vet prepares the EU Annex IV health certificate, which must include full titre test details (blood draw date, laboratory name, result in IU/ml) and the vaccination record.

        DGSG endorses the certificate after review. Allow three to five business days for the endorsement step. The certificate is issued within 10 days of travel and expires on that basis. Coordinate the vet appointment and DGSG submission carefully within the 10-day window.

        The Echinococcus treatment record for dogs is a separate document. Both the health certificate and the treatment record must accompany the pet to Helsinki.

  - question: "Do cats need tapeworm treatment to enter Finland from Uruguay?"
    answer: "No. The Echinococcus praziquantel treatment requirement in Finland applies to dogs only. Cats from Uruguay need the standard EU unlisted country process: microchip, rabies vaccination, FAVN titre test with 90-day wait, and a DGSG-endorsed health certificate."
  - question: "Can I administer the tapeworm treatment in Finland after arriving?"
    answer: "No. The treatment must be administered 1-5 days before entry into Finland. Treatment given after arrival does not meet the requirement. If the treatment has not been given within the 1-5 day window, Ruokavirasto may refuse the dog entry until the issue is resolved."
  - question: "Is Helsinki-Vantaa the only airport where I can bring my pet into Finland?"
    answer: "HEL is the main approved border inspection post for international air cargo shipments. Some land border crossings (such as Vaalimaa) are also approved. For air cargo from Uruguay, HEL is the correct entry point. Confirm with Ruokavirasto before booking."

    answer: "Expect 36-54 hours of transit time from MVD to HEL including connections via a South American and a European hub. The exact time depends on routing. This transit duration is relevant for planning the Echinococcus treatment window."
