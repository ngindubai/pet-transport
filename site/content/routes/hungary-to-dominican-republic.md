  h1: "Pet Transport from Hungary to Dominican Republic"
  overview: |
    Relocating a pet from Hungary to the Dominican Republic is a moderate-complexity move. There is no quarantine on arrival, and no import permit is needed for up to five personal pets. The Dominican Republic's requirements focus on vaccination status, a valid ISO microchip, and an official health certificate issued within 15 days of travel. Hungary's Nebih (Nemzeti Elelmiszerlanc-biztonsagi Hivatal) provides the endorsement for the EU-format certificate used on this route.

    Dogs must have current DHPP vaccination; cats need panleukopenia, rhinotracheitis, and calicivirus. Rabies vaccination must be given between 30 days and 12 months before entry into the Dominican Republic. DGAG (Direccion General de Ganaderia, agricultura.gob.do) carries out a documentary inspection on arrival at Las Americas International Airport (SDQ) in Santo Domingo.

    Budapest Ferenc Liszt International Airport (BUD) connects to SDQ via Amsterdam on KLM, via Madrid on Iberia, and via Paris CDG on Air France. Wizz Air operates from Budapest to London Gatwick and Madrid, which can be useful for cargo connections at those hubs. Allow four to six weeks from start to departure.

  sections:
    - heading: "Hungary Export Requirements"
      body: |
        Hungary is an EU member state, so its export process follows the EU pet travel framework. No export permit is needed. The health certificate must be issued by a Nebih-authorised official vet and endorsed by Nebih (Nemzeti Elelmiszerlanc-biztonsagi Hivatal, nebih.gov.hu). Not all private vets in Hungary hold Nebih authorisation; confirm that your chosen clinic has the necessary status before booking the pre-export examination.

        The EU-format certificate is valid for 10 days from signing. Nebih endorsement after the vet's signature typically takes one to three working days. Schedule the official vet appointment to leave enough time for endorsement and travel within that 10-day window. Budapest Ferenc Liszt International Airport (BUD) is the departure point for all international cargo.

    - heading: "Dominican Republic Import Rules"
      body: |
        DGAG inspectors at Las Americas International Airport (SDQ) check the ISO microchip, rabies vaccination record, core vaccination status, and the official health certificate on arrival. There is no quarantine, and no import permit is required for personal pets (up to five animals per traveller).

        The rabies vaccination must have been given at least 30 days but no more than 12 months before entry. Core vaccinations are required: DHPP for dogs (distemper, adenovirus, parainfluenza, parvovirus) and panleukopenia, rhinotracheitis, and calicivirus for cats. Inspection at SDQ is generally efficient when the paperwork is complete and in good order.

    - heading: "Cargo Connections from Budapest"
      body: |
        Budapest Ferenc Liszt Airport (BUD) offers several routes to the Caribbean through European hubs. KLM operates cargo services through Amsterdam (AMS), which connects to Santo Domingo. Iberia routes cargo through Madrid (MAD). Air France connects through Paris CDG. Wizz Air runs passenger services from BUD to London Gatwick (LGW) and Madrid (MAD); for cargo, the main carriers remain KLM, Iberia, and Air France, though Wizz Air services can make the initial leg feasible in a mixed passenger-cargo arrangement with a transport agent.

        Cargo costs from Budapest to SDQ typically run EUR 700-1,800, depending on the animal's size and crate dimensions. Add the Nebih health certificate (EUR 100-250), crate if needed (EUR 60-220), and agent fees if you use a transport professional (EUR 250-600). Most single-pet moves from Budapest to Santo Domingo come out between EUR 1,100 and EUR 2,500 all-in.

    - heading: "Planning the Timeline"
      body: |
        Four weeks is the minimum realistic timeline for this route if your pet is already microchipped and the rabies vaccination is current. If either needs to be done, add time: the rabies jab must be at least 30 days before entry, so everything else starts from the date it is administered.

        Book the Nebih-authorised official vet appointment to fall within 10 days of your flight. Leave two to three working days between the vet appointment and departure for the Nebih endorsement to be processed. Cargo bookings with KLM, Iberia, or Air France should be confirmed four to six weeks before travel to secure capacity for your pet and crate.

    answer: "A Nebih-authorised official vet issues the certificate. Nebih (Nemzeti Elelmiszerlanc-biztonsagi Hivatal, nebih.gov.hu) then endorses it. Not all private vets hold Nebih authorisation; confirm your chosen vet's status before booking."
  - question: "How long is the health certificate valid?"
    answer: "The EU-format health certificate issued in Hungary is valid for 10 days from the date of signing. The Dominican Republic requires the certificate to have been issued within 15 days of travel, so the 10-day EU certificate satisfies both conditions."

    answer: "No. DGAG carries out a documentary and physical inspection at Las Americas International Airport (SDQ). If documents are correct, the pet is released on arrival."
