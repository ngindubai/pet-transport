  overview: |
    Ashgabat's international flight network barely touches Europe. That single constraint shapes this entire route more than any single piece of paperwork does.

    Turkmenistan Airlines runs to Frankfurt twice a week and little else nearby; the realistic path to the UK goes via Istanbul, where both Turkmenistan Airlines and Turkish Airlines fly the same nonstop route. From there, Turkish Airlines or a UK-side cargo agent handles the rest.

    Add to that: Turkmenistan isn't on the UK's Part 1 or Part 2 list, so the titre test and its 90-day wait apply in full. And if the pet in question is an Alabai, the journey doesn't happen at all. The breed is barred from export as a national symbol, full stop.
  sections:
    - heading: "The flight problem, not just the paperwork"
      body: |
        Most route pages on this site treat flight booking as a formality once the medical timeline is sorted. Turkmenistan is the exception. Scheduled international capacity out of Ashgabat is genuinely thin, and pet-carriage capability isn't confirmed on every carrier that flies there.

        Turkish Airlines and Turkmenistan Airlines both operate the Ashgabat-Istanbul route nonstop, making Istanbul the obvious connecting point for a UK-bound pet. flydubai's Ashgabat-Dubai service offers a second option, feeding into Emirates SkyCargo's onward network, though the Gulf heat embargo season narrows that window in summer.

        Book earlier than you would for almost any other route on this site. Six to eight weeks ahead is a sensible minimum given how little slack exists in the schedule.
    - heading: "The Alabai export restriction"
      body: |
        The Central Asian Shepherd Dog, known locally as the Alabai, holds national symbol status in Turkmenistan. It cannot be exported, and no State Veterinary Service health certificate will be issued for the breed regardless of how the paperwork is framed.

        If you're relocating with a mixed-breed dog that has significant Alabai ancestry, raise this with the State Veterinary Service directly before assuming the export will proceed. This is not a rule that flexes for individual circumstances.
    - heading: "What makes this an unlisted-country route for the UK"
      body: |
        Turkmenistan doesn't appear on the UK's Part 1 or Part 2 lists, the classification that would otherwise let a pet skip the rabies blood test. That means every pet, whatever its vaccination history, needs a titre test result at least 0.5 IU/ml, drawn at least 30 days post-vaccination, followed by a 90-day wait before travel.

        There's no local laboratory in Turkmenistan on the current APHA-approved list, so factor in courier time to get the blood sample tested abroad. This tends to be the single most underestimated part of the timeline for owners planning their first move from this route.
