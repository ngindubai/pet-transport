  h1: "Pet Transport from Cameroon to United Arab Emirates"
  overview: |
    No fixed 90-day wait. That's the detail that separates this route from its EU and UK equivalents on this site. MOCCAE treats Cameroon as high-risk, so a rabies titre test is required, but once the result clears 0.5 IU/ml there is nothing further to wait out beyond getting the import permit and booking the flight.

    The flight itself is the slower part. Nobody flies Douala to Dubai nonstop. Ethiopian Airlines' connection through Addis Ababa is the fastest option, at around 11 hours 20 minutes door to door, with EgyptAir's Cairo routing as a fallback.

    MINEPIA handles the Cameroon side: an export permit and a certificate covering the microchip, vaccination and general health of the animal. Get that moving alongside the titre test rather than after it, since both processes have their own separate timelines.

  sections:
    - heading: "What MOCCAE actually asks for"
      body: |
        Cameroon does not sit on MOCCAE's list of roughly 50 low-risk countries, a list weighted toward the UK, most of the EU, Japan, Singapore and a handful of others with established rabies control. That puts Cameroon in the high-risk bracket, meaning a titre test is mandatory: FAVN or RSNT, blood drawn at least 21 days after vaccination, result of at least 0.5 IU/ml.

        What MOCCAE does not do is set a fixed waiting period after that result. The test simply has to be dated somewhere between 3 and 12 months before the travel date. Compare that with the UK or EU pattern, where a 90-day wait after a passing titre is standard, and this route saves a genuine three months against the equivalent EU journey.

    - heading: "Routing out of Douala"
      body: |
        Three carriers serve this pair with a connection: Ethiopian Airlines through Addis Ababa, running one to two flights a day, EgyptAir through Cairo at two to three flights daily, and Emirates on its own connecting itinerary. None of the three flies direct.

        Ethiopian's routing tends to be the quickest, at roughly 11 hours 20 minutes total. Whichever carrier is chosen, confirm live animal cargo acceptance on the specific connecting leg, since policies can differ from the airline's headline pet transport terms.

    - heading: "The same passport, a very different Australian standard"
      body: |
        It's worth sitting with the contrast for a moment. A Cameroonian dog heading to the UAE needs a titre test and an import permit, a process that fits inside four months. The same dog heading to Australia instead runs into DAFF's non-approved country rule: 180 days of continuous residency in an approved third country before any Australian paperwork is even opened. Two governments assessing the same country's rabies risk have landed in very different places.

    - heading: "Timing around the Gulf summer"
      body: |
        One seasonal detail is easy to miss when everything else is booked around vet appointments. Between roughly June and September, ground temperatures at UAE airports regularly pass 45 degrees Celsius, and most airlines suspend or heavily restrict pet cargo during that window. A titre result and import permit sorted in April do a traveller no good if the only available flight lands in a July heat embargo. Aim for the October-to-May window on the Dubai or Abu Dhabi end, whatever else the schedule allows.
