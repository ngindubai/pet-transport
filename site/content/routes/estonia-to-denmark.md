Your pet travels on an EU pet passport with no titre test, no quarantine, and no import permit needed.   sections:
    - heading: "Tallinn to Copenhagen: airline options for pets"
      body: |
        SAS is the most direct option for pet cargo on this route, connecting Tallinn (TLL) to Copenhagen Kastrup (CPH) via Stockholm (ARN). SAS Cargo accepts live animals, and small pets can travel in cabin on European SAS routes. airBaltic operates Tallinn via Riga (RIX) to Copenhagen, with both cabin and live animal cargo on the RIX-CPH leg. This is a well-established route for airBaltic. LOT Polish Airlines adds a third option via Warsaw (WAW) for owners whose dates do not suit the other carriers. Ryanair serves TLL with budget fares and is cabin-only. All cargo bookings should be confirmed directly with the airline's cargo team, not through the standard booking engine.

    - heading: "What your Estonian vet needs to provide"
      body: |
        Your official vet in Estonia, authorised by the Pohllumajandus- ja Toiduamet (Agricultural Board, agri.ee), issues and updates the EU pet passport. Denmark accepts this document at entry with no additional health certificate required. Two things to verify at the appointment: first, that the microchip was implanted on or before the date of the first rabies vaccination (EU Regulation 576/2013 is clear on this). Second, that the current rabies vaccination is within its valid period. If you need to update the vaccination and it has lapsed even briefly, a 21-day wait applies before travel. The Fodevarestyrelsen (fvst.dk) is the Danish authority for any queries about specific circumstances.

faqs:
  - question: "Do I need a titre test to bring my dog from Estonia to Denmark?"
    answer: "No. Estonia is an EU member state, which makes it a listed country for Denmark. Titre tests are only required for dogs moving from unlisted countries. No titre test is needed on this route."
  - question: "Is there any quarantine for pets arriving in Denmark from Estonia?"
    answer: "No. Denmark does not impose quarantine on pets arriving from EU member states with a valid EU pet passport and compliant documentation."
  - question: "Does my dog need tapeworm treatment before entering Denmark?"
    answer: "No. Denmark does not require tapeworm treatment for dogs arriving from EU member states. This requirement applies in Norway and Finland for arrivals from countries outside the EU, not on EU-to-EU routes."
  - question: "Can I take my cat from Tallinn to Copenhagen in the cabin?"
    answer: "Yes, on SAS and airBaltic, cats in approved carriers can travel in cabin if the carrier fits under the seat. Check each airline's current weight limit for the carrier and cat combined before booking. Spaces are limited per flight."
