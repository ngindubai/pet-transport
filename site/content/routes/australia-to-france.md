  sections:
    - heading: "No titre test for Australian pets entering France"
      body: |
        Australia appears on the EU's listed third countries schedule (EU Regulation 576/2013, Annex II). Pets from Australia can enter France without the FAVN titre test or the 3-month wait from blood draw that applies to non-listed countries. Source: food.ec.europa.eu, 2026.

        The documentation required is: ISO microchip, valid rabies vaccination (21-day post-primary wait), and a DAFF-endorsed EU-format health certificate issued within 10 days of travel. 4-6 weeks preparation is sufficient for a pet whose vaccination is current.

    - heading: "Sydney or Melbourne to Paris CDG: hub routing"
      body: |
        There is no direct cargo service from Sydney or Melbourne to Paris CDG. All routings connect via a hub. Singapore Airlines via Changi (SIN) and Emirates via Dubai (DXB) are the two established options. Air France Cargo connects to CDG from both hub points via partner services.

        Singapore Airlines via SIN offers a well-established Australia to Europe corridor with strong live animal handling at Changi. Emirates via DXB is a good alternative. Book 4-6 weeks ahead and confirm live animal acceptance on specific flights.

    - heading: "France Category 1 breed check from Australia"
      body: |
        France's Category 1 classification covers Pit Bull type dogs without official LOF or FCI breed registration. These cannot be imported into France. Category 2 (Rottweiler, American Staffordshire with pedigree) can enter with restrictions.

        For most breeds common in Australia, including Labrador Retrievers, Golden Retrievers, Border Collies, and Australian Shepherds, no breed restriction applies. If your dog resembles a Pit Bull type without breed papers, verify classification before booking.
