  overview: |
    Search flight tools sometimes still list flydubai flying Dubai to Skopje. As of the airline's own published destination roster in May 2026, that route has been withdrawn, and booking a pet move around it is the single most avoidable mistake on this page.

    Take that option off the table and North Macedonia turns into one of the more workable Balkan destinations on this site for a UAE departure.   sections:
    - heading: "The flydubai route that isn't there any more"
      body: |
        flydubai served a run of Central and Eastern European cities from Dubai, and Skopje was on that list for several years. It no longer is, per the airline's own current destination roster checked in May 2026, but the old route still shows up in some search results and cached itinerary builders, which makes it an easy trap for anyone booking without checking directly against flydubai's own site.

        Turkish Airlines via Istanbul fills the gap. It's a longer routing than a direct flight would have been, but Turkish's daily Dubai-Istanbul capacity, combined with its own Skopje service, keeps the connection reliable, and unlike the UK-Macedonia route on this site, Turkish's UK-specific pet refusal has no bearing here.

    - heading: "What FVA actually checks on arrival"
      body: |
        A microchip fitted before vaccination, a rabies shot at least 21 days old, and the MOCCAE-endorsed export certificate cover the paperwork FVA wants to see. No import permit applies to a personally accompanied pet.

        The titre test is the one point worth double-checking rather than assuming. FVA's published exemption for low-risk origins beyond the EU-listed countries appears to cover the UAE, but the wording is less explicit than the direct exemption granted to UK and US arrivals, so a call to FVA before booking removes any ambiguity.
