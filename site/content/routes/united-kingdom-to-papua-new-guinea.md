  h1: "Pet Transport from United Kingdom to Papua New Guinea"
  overview: |
    Exporting a cat or dog from Great Britain to Papua New Guinea runs through a named APHA document, export health certificate 3945, applied for online and completed by a registered Official Veterinarian. On the receiving end, NAQIA runs an import system that treats quarantine as standard practice, not a penalty for getting something wrong.

    That's the detail that catches owners out most: every pet entering Papua New Guinea spends a minimum 30 days at NAQIA's Kilakila quarantine station in Port Moresby, whatever the paperwork says. A dog or cat with a perfect vaccination record and a flawless certificate still goes into quarantine. Budget both the time and the fee for it from the start.

    There's no shortcut around the flight either. No airline connects London to Port Moresby directly, so every itinerary runs through Singapore or Brisbane, with Air Niugini handling the final leg into Papua New Guinea.

  sections:
    - heading: "What certificate 3945 actually covers"
      body: |
        This is the specific export health certificate APHA uses for cats and dogs leaving Great Britain for Papua New Guinea, distinct from the certificates used for other destinations. It has to be applied for online, with a registered Official Veterinarian carrying out the examination and completing the form against APHA's current guidance notes and specimen certificate.

        Northern Ireland exporters use a separate system, DAERA's DECS platform, rather than APHA's own portal. Getting the wrong system at the start wastes time on a route that's already fairly long.

    - heading: "Quarantine as standard, not as a penalty"
      body: |
        Most routes on this site describe quarantine as something that happens if paperwork is wrong. Papua New Guinea is different. NAQIA requires a minimum 30-day stay at its Kilakila animal quarantine station for every entering dog and cat, full stop. That changes the shape of the whole plan: it's not a risk to avoid, it's a fixed cost and a fixed block of time to schedule around.

        The NAQIA import permit itself needs at least 6 weeks lead time, and vaccination has to land inside a 30-day-to-12-month window before travel, not before or after. Getting the vaccination timing wrong means restarting that clock.

    - heading: "Getting from London to Port Moresby"
      body: |
        Singapore Airlines' nonstop Heathrow to Singapore service is the most direct first leg, with Air Niugini picking up the Singapore to Port Moresby sector. The alternative runs via Qantas toward Brisbane, again connecting to Air Niugini for the final stretch, though Qantas requires any pet moving through Australia to be booked via a registered pet travel specialist rather than a standard cargo desk.

        Whichever route, Air Niugini's own booking window, 72 hours notice for an international pet cargo reservation, needs to be respected on top of the earlier legs' own lead times.
