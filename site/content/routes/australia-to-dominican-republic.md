content:
  h1: "Pet Transport Australia to Dominican Republic"
  overview: |
    The Dominican Republic doesn't make this hard. No titre test, since Australia's rabies-free status keeps it well outside the country's high-risk category, and no import permit for up to five pets. What makes this route genuinely demanding is geography: there's no direct or near-direct path from Australia to the Caribbean, so the pet is looking at a transpacific leg into the US followed by a separate onward flight.

    Qantas covers the first leg into Los Angeles, and from there, American Airlines Cargo or a similar US carrier picks up the Miami-to-Santo Domingo or Punta Cana connection.

  sections:
    - heading: "The Dominican Republic's side: genuinely light"
      body: |
        For up to five personal pets, no import permit applies. A microchip, rabies vaccination given between 30 days and 12 months before arrival, and a health certificate issued within 15 days of travel by an accredited Australian vet cover the requirement in full. No titre test applies, since the Dominican Republic reserves that step for high-rabies-risk countries, and Australia's rabies-free status puts it well outside that group.

    - heading: "Why the journey itself is the harder part"
      body: |
        There's no realistic single-connection route from Australia to the Caribbean. Qantas flies the transpacific leg into Los Angeles, and from there a US carrier, typically American Airlines Cargo, handles the onward journey into Santo Domingo or Punta Cana via Miami. That's two separate cargo bookings on two different airlines, which means confirming the transfer logistics at the US hub matters as much as either individual leg.

    - heading: "Making the 15-day certificate window work across a long journey"
      body: |
        The health certificate's 15-day validity has to cover the entire trip, both legs included, not just the departure from Australia. Book both flights and confirm the full itinerary before scheduling the vet appointment, so the certificate date lines up with the actual arrival date in the Dominican Republic rather than an earlier departure date from Australia.
