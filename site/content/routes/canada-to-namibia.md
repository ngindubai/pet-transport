content:
  h1: "Pet Transport from Canada to Namibia"
  overview: |
    No rabies titre test. That's true whichever direction you look at this corridor from, and it holds for Canada just as it does for the UK and US routes already built on this site: Namibia doesn't run an antibody blood test requirement against any country of origin.

    What Namibia insists on instead is timing. The rabies vaccination has to land somewhere between 30 days and 12 months before the pet arrives in Windhoek, and there's no direct flight to help make that window easier to hit. Every booking transfers through Frankfurt or Johannesburg.

  sections:
    - heading: "A window, not a wait"
      body: |
        Most long-haul African routes on this site run on a titre test and a fixed post-test wait. Namibia skips both. Instead, the Directorate of Veterinary Services wants a rabies vaccination dated inside a specific 30-day-to-12-month bracket at arrival, no earlier, no later.

        That's a genuinely different kind of planning problem. There's no lab result to wait for, so the constraint is entirely about picking a vaccination date and a flight date that sit correctly against each other, then not letting either slip.

    - heading: "Getting to Windhoek from Toronto or Montreal"
      body: |
        Air Canada's year-round Toronto-Frankfurt service, paired with Discover Airlines' own nonstop Frankfurt-Windhoek route, keeps this journey to a single connection. Airlink's Johannesburg-Windhoek leg is the fallback for owners whose first leg runs through the UK or the Gulf instead of Frankfurt.

        Either way, budget the connection time into the crate and cargo handling plan; a pet changing continents on one stopover still needs that transfer managed properly, even on an otherwise straightforward route.

    - heading: "What the import permit can still ask for"
      body: |
        The Namibian import permit itself isn't a rubber stamp. Depending on what the Directorate specifies for a given application, it can call for negative results on Brucella canis, Trypanosoma evansi, Leishmaniasis, Dirofilaria or Babesia, tests that have nothing to do with rabies at all.

        Get that list confirmed before the pre-export vet visit, not after. Namibia doesn't publish one fixed testing schedule across every application, so treating this as optional paperwork rather than a genuine gate is the mistake worth avoiding.
