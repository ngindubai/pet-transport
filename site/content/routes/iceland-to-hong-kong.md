content:
  h1: "Pet Transport from Iceland to Hong Kong"
  overview: |
    Iceland is about as rabies-free as a country gets, an island with a mandatory 14-day quarantine on every incoming pet to keep it that way. Hong Kong's AFCD doesn't reward that with its top tier. Iceland sits in Group II, the rabies-controlled bracket shared with the US mainland, Germany and France, not Group I alongside Australia, Japan, New Zealand and the UK.

    In practice that means an extra document most owners don't expect: a Residence Certificate confirming the pet's time in Iceland, alongside the standard Animal Health Certificate and vaccination record. Get the Special Permit conditions right and there's no quarantine at all. Miss one and the penalty is steep, a 4-month hold or a flight straight back.

  sections:
    - heading: "Rabies-free on the island, Group II on the form"
      body: |
        AFCD's classification runs on documented rabies-free history within its own group system, not a general reputation for being an isolated, low-risk country. Iceland qualifies for Group II, the second tier, which still avoids quarantine for a fully compliant pet but adds the Residence Certificate requirement that Group I countries don't face.

        It's worth knowing before booking anything, since some owners assume "rabies-free country" automatically means the easiest AFCD tier. It doesn't. Check the actual group listing rather than working from that assumption.
    - heading: "The Special Permit and what happens if it's incomplete"
      body: |
        The AFCD Special Permit is applied for online through forms AF240 and UN110, alongside an ID photocopy, and costs HKD 432 for a single animal. AFCD quotes at least 3 working days once the application is complete, though assembling the Residence Certificate and getting the vaccination timing right usually takes longer than that headline figure suggests.

        Get every condition met and the pet clears Hong Kong without quarantine. Fall short, whether that's a missing certificate or a vaccination given outside the 30-day to 12-month window, and the penalty is a minimum 4-month quarantine paid for by the owner, or the pet being returned to Iceland. There's no partial compliance option here.
    - heading: "Keflavik to Hong Kong: no through flight"
      body: |
        Icelandair covers Keflavik to a European hub, but Hong Kong sits well outside its network. Cathay Pacific then handles the onward cargo leg from that hub into Hong Kong, meaning this route runs as two separate bookings rather than one.

        Build a realistic buffer between the two legs. A short delay on the first sector can cost a tightly scheduled connection on the second, and rebooking cargo space at short notice is rarely straightforward.
