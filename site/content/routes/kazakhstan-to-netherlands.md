  sections:
    - heading: "The EU unlisted country process: what it adds to your timeline"
      body: |
        The Netherlands follows EU-wide pet import rules under EU Regulation 576/2013 and 2020/692. Countries on the EU's approved list can send pets with a simpler process. Kazakhstan is not on that list, which means the full titre test protocol applies.

        The sequence is fixed: microchip first, then rabies vaccination, then blood draw for the FAVN titre test at least 30 days after vaccination, then a 90-day wait from the blood draw date. You cannot travel before that 90-day window closes. If the blood draw is on day zero, your earliest legal travel date to the Netherlands is day 90. Most owners add a few weeks of buffer.

        The upside: once the titre test passes and the waiting period is complete, there is no quarantine in the Netherlands for compliant arrivals. The EU certificate from your KVCS-authorised vet (issued within 10 days of travel) is the final document.
