content:
  h1: "Pet Transport from Canada to Kyrgyzstan"
  overview: |
    A five-day window decides most of this route's timeline. Kyrgyzstan asks for a health certificate issued no more than five days before arrival, tighter than the ten-day rule CFIA typically works to elsewhere, which means the vet appointment has to be booked against a confirmed flight rather than the usual way round.

    What isn't tight is the rest of the paperwork. Kyrgyzstan doesn't require a rabies titre test from any country of origin, Canada included, a genuine break from the listed-country systems that govern several other destinations this site covers. A current vaccination and that narrow certificate window cover the medical side.

    Toronto's nonstop Turkish Airlines service into Istanbul is the practical advantage here: it connects directly to Turkish's own existing Istanbul-Bishkek route, a shorter chain than the UK or US versions of this same corridor.

  sections:
    - heading: "No titre test, a genuinely tight certificate clock"
      body: |
        Kyrgyzstan's import rules skip the rabies antibody titre test entirely, regardless of where the pet is travelling from. That's worth noting on its own, since several of this site's other African and Central Asian routes do carry a titre requirement in one direction or the other. Here, a rabies vaccination given between 21 days and 11 months before entry, plus a government-endorsed certificate, is the whole medical picture.

        The certificate itself is where the real constraint sits. Kyrgyzstan wants it issued within five days of the pet's arrival, a narrower window than CFIA's more typical ten-day certification rhythm for other destinations. Book the vet appointment once the flight is locked in, not before.

    - heading: "Why Toronto's Istanbul link matters here"
      body: |
        Turkish Airlines already flies Toronto to Istanbul nonstop six times a week, and separately runs its own Istanbul-Bishkek service into Manas International Airport. Put those two facts together and a Canada-origin pet reaches Kyrgyzstan through a single carrier's network with one change of aircraft, a cleaner chain than a UK or US departure, both of which need to reach Istanbul via a longer routing first.

        Air Astana remains a name worth knowing for this corridor, but it has no nonstop Canadian service. Reaching its Almaty hub from Toronto means a European or US connection before Air Astana's own aircraft even enters the picture, which usually makes it the slower option from Canada specifically.

    - heading: "The cat-specific check that's easy to miss"
      body: |
        Dogs and cats both need current rabies cover, but cats face one extra step: a clinical examination for dermatophytosis, more commonly known as ringworm. A cat showing active lesions cannot be imported into Kyrgyzstan at all, so raise this at the same appointment that produces the export certificate rather than assuming a routine health check already covers it.
