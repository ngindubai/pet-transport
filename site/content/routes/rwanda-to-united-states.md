Most cargo from Kigali to North America routes through European or East African hubs, and that is just the start of what makes this one of the more involved routes in international pet transport. Rwanda is classified as a CDC high-risk country for dogs, which adds two requirements you will not find on simpler routes: a rabies antibody titre test and a confirmed reservation at a CDC-registered facility before your dog boards the plane.

The process is achievable with the right planning window. Allow 16 to 24 weeks from first vaccination to travel day.

## What the CDC Requires for Dogs from Rwanda

The CDC classifies Rwanda as a high-risk country for dog rabies. Dogs arriving in the US from Rwanda must meet the following requirements, all of which need to be completed in sequence.

| Requirement | Detail |
|---|---|
| Microchip | ISO 11784/11785, implanted before or at vaccination |
| Rabies vaccination | Valid at time of travel |
| Titre test | Blood drawn 30+ days post-vaccination; result 0.5 IU/ml or above |
| CDC Dog Import Form | Form CDC 6-527, submitted online before travel |
| CDC-registered facility | Reservation confirmed before form submission |
| USDA APHIS health certificate | Issued within 10 days of travel, endorsed by RAB then USDA |

The dog must arrive at a CDC-registered US airport. Not every major airport qualifies, so confirm your intended port of entry before booking cargo space. The CDC's importation page at cdc.gov/importation/dogs.html lists currently registered facilities and airports; the list is updated periodically, so check it close to your travel date.

Cats travelling the same route face no equivalent CDC restriction. An RAB export health certificate is still required to leave Rwanda, but there is no titre test requirement and no CDC registration step.

## The Rwanda Export Side: RAB Certificates

On the Kigali side, the Rwanda Agriculture and Animal Resources Development Board (RAB) governs animal exports. Your vet must be RAB-authorised, and the export health certificate they issue must carry an RAB endorsement before it is valid for international travel. The RAB's website is rab.gov.rw.

No formal export permit is required for personal companion pets. What you need is the health certificate, vaccination records, microchip documentation, and the titre test result. Build in several days for the RAB endorsement before the travel date, particularly because the USDA APHIS health certificate also needs to be finalised within 10 days of the flight.

The USDA APHIS certificate is separate from the RAB export certificate. It is issued by a USDA-accredited veterinarian and must be USDA-endorsed within 10 days of departure. Coordinating the RAB endorsement alongside that 10-day USDA window is one of the trickier timing points on this route. Work backwards from your flight date and confirm each step with your vet.

## Routing from Kigali: Hub Options and Cargo Booking

There is no non-stop service from Kigali to the United States. The two most practical cargo paths are via Addis Ababa (Ethiopian Airlines, connecting to IAD or JFK via Ethiopian Cargo) and via Brussels (RwandAir or Brussels Airlines, connecting to transatlantic services). Qatar Airways via Doha is a third option with good frequency.

Whichever hub you use, confirm that the transit airport will accept live animals in cargo and that the connection time meets the airline's minimum transit requirements for live animals, often four hours or more. Ethiopian Cargo has established live animal handling at Addis Ababa, which is a practical advantage on long-haul routes.

Cargo booking lead times from Kigali run to four to six weeks during busy periods. Book space well ahead of your planned travel date, and check whether your airline's live animal embargo periods fall during your window. Northern hemisphere summer (June to August) is a common embargo period for brachycephalic breeds.
