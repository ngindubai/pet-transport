  h1: "Pet Transport from Ukraine to the United Arab Emirates"
  overview: |
    MOCCAE keeps a list of roughly fifty countries it treats as low-risk for rabies. Ukraine isn't on it, which is why every dog and cat leaving Kyiv, Lviv or anywhere else for Dubai or Abu Dhabi needs a FAVN antibody titre test, not just a vaccination record. Layer on the fact that no flight has left a Ukrainian airport since February 2022, and the paperwork turns out to be only half the job.

    This route runs in two distinct phases: get the pet compliant with MOCCAE's high-risk pathway while still in Ukraine or just across the border, then get it physically out of a country with no working civilian airport. Neither phase can really be rushed.

  sections:
    - heading: "Why Ukraine falls into MOCCAE's high-risk track"
      body: |
        MOCCAE's rules split the world into two groups. Pets from the roughly fifty countries on its low-risk list clear customs with a standard rabies vaccination and a 21-day wait. Everyone else, Ukraine included, needs a FAVN titre test reading at least 0.5 IU/ml, drawn from a laboratory the exporting authority recognises.

        That single distinction changes the minimum age too. A pet from a low-risk country can travel from 12 weeks old. From Ukraine, it's 15 weeks. Miss that by even a few days and the whole vaccination and titre sequence has to restart, because MOCCAE checks the vaccination date against the pet's date of birth on the certificate.

    - heading: "No flight leaves Ukraine: the overland-to-Warsaw routing"
      body: |
        There isn't a departure lounge for pets in Kyiv or Lviv right now. Civilian airspace closed the day the invasion began in February 2022 and, despite a working group formed in March 2026 and a Cabinet-approved aviation strategy running to 2030, it's still closed. AirBaltic became the first international carrier to publicly say it was ready to resume flights, back in April 2026, but that's a statement of intent, not a schedule.

        So the pet crosses by road. Shehyni-Medyka and Krakovets-Korczowa near Lviv are the usual crossings into Poland; Romanian border posts serve owners further south. Once across, Rzeszow, Krakow and Warsaw all work as staging points, and Warsaw is where the flight to Dubai actually starts.

    - heading: "The MOCCAE paperwork stack, in order"
      body: |
        MOCCAE wants its own Model Veterinary Health Certificate, not a generic vet letter. It has to record the microchip number exactly, the rabies vaccination date, the titre result and lab, and confirmation of parasite treatment within the 14 days before travel. Getting this signed in Ukraine is difficult given the state of the export vet network there, which is why most owners have it completed once they've crossed into Poland or Romania.

        The import permit is a separate step, applied for online, and it only lasts 90 days. Order matters: microchip, then vaccination, then the 21-day wait, then the titre draw, then the MOCCAE certificate, then the permit application. Doing these out of sequence invalidates one or more of them.

    - heading: "What this actually costs, and the quarantine trade-off"
      body: |
        Budget somewhere between USD 1,400 and USD 3,700 all told, once vet fees, the overland transfer, the MOCCAE certificate, the import permit, a crate and the Warsaw-Dubai or Istanbul-Dubai cargo fare are added up. The overland leg alone can run to USD 900 depending on distance and whether a transport agent handles the crossing.

        Get every document right and there's no quarantine at all on arrival, just the release fee. Get it wrong, even on a technicality like a titre lab MOCCAE doesn't recognise, and the pet sits in an approved facility at the owner's cost until it's sorted. That gap is wide enough to make careful sequencing worth the extra weeks it takes.
