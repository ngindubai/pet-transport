  overview: |
    Japan's EU-listed status keeps the paperwork manageable for pets heading to Portugal. There is no titre test, no quarantine, and no waiting period. Your pet needs a microchip, a valid rabies vaccination, and the EU-model health certificate -- the right version of which your MAFF-accredited vet in Japan must prepare.

    Routing is the main planning task here. No airlines fly nonstop from Japan to Portugal. Lufthansa via Frankfurt (FRA) and Iberia via Madrid (MAD) are the two most-used connections. TAP Air Portugal is a strong option for the final leg into Lisbon (LIS) -- they accept small pets in cabin. Confirm pet acceptance on every leg of your journey separately, not just the first one.

    The EU-model health certificate (Annex IV listed-country format) must be issued within 10 days of your pet entering Portugal, and the 10-day clock runs from issue to arrival -- so factor in the endorsement time at the MAFF regional office when you're setting your travel date. Allow 4-8 weeks from starting preparation to travel day.
