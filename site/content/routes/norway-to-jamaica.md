content:
  h1: "Pet Transport from Norway to Jamaica"
  overview: |
    Jamaica is one of the more involved Caribbean destinations for pet import. Two requirements make it more complex than many: a Veterinary Import Permit from Jamaica's Veterinary Services Division (VSD) must be secured before travel, and all arriving pets may be subject to up to 14 days of supervised quarantine at the Plumb Point National Quarantine Facility in Kingston.

    Allow 10 to 12 weeks from the start of preparation to travel day. Most of that time is taken up by permit processing and ensuring parasite treatment timing is correct. The cargo routing from Oslo goes via Amsterdam with KLM or via London with British Airways.

    This route is manageable with planning and ideally a specialist pet transport agent to coordinate the permit application, parasite treatment schedule, and cargo booking.

  sections:
    - heading: "The Veterinary Import Permit: apply first, everything else second"
      body: |
        Jamaica requires a Veterinary Import Permit (VIP) for all incoming dogs and cats. The permit is issued by the Veterinary Services Division (VSD), Ministry of Agriculture, Fisheries and Mining. Pets arriving without it will be refused entry.

        Apply directly to the VSD at vsdpermits@moa.gov.jm or by calling +1 876-977-2489. Processing can take several weeks, so start this step as soon as you have a firm travel date in mind. The permit specifies the animal, the route, and the conditions of entry.

        Once the permit is in hand, the veterinary preparation in Norway can begin. Do not book cargo or arrange the health certificate until the permit has been received and you have a confirmed travel date.

    - heading: "Health requirements and parasite treatments"
      body: |
        All dogs and cats must have an ISO-compliant microchip implanted before any rabies vaccination. Rabies vaccination must be within 12 months of entry. For dogs, distemper, parvovirus, leptospirosis, hepatitis, and parainfluenza vaccinations are also required. Cats need herpesvirus 1, calicivirus, and panleukopenia protection.

        Parasite treatment is one of the more demanding aspects of this route. For internal parasites, two treatments are required at least 14 days apart, with the second administered within 7 days of transport. For external parasites (fleas and ticks), two treatments are required 14 days apart, with the second within 48 hours of transport. Work backwards from your departure date to set the treatment schedule. These requirements come from the VSD and are enforced.

        The official health certificate must be issued by a Mattilsynet-authorised official veterinarian in Norway, within 10 days of travel. Contact Mattilsynet (mattilsynet.no) to find authorised official vets in your region.

    - heading: "Cargo routing and quarantine at Plumb Point"
      body: |
        From Oslo Gardermoen (OSL), the main cargo routing is via Amsterdam Schiphol (AMS) with KLM. SAS and Norwegian Air connect Oslo to Amsterdam regularly. KLM operates cargo services to Norman Manley International Airport (KIN) in Kingston. An alternative is via London Heathrow (LHR) with British Airways World Cargo.

        On arrival at KIN, your pet will be directed to the National Quarantine Facility at Plumb Point, where the VSD will conduct inspections and the quarantine period of up to 14 days will begin. During this period you will not have access to your pet but can contact the facility. Quarantine costs are charged by the VSD. Factor this into your budget and your accommodation plans in Jamaica.
