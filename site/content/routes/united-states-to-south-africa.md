    - heading: "The DALRRD permit and titre test"
      body: |
        South Africa's DALRRD issues the veterinary import permit that governs entry. The permit sets out the exact conditions, including vaccination timing and, for dogs, the rabies titre test (minimum 0.5 IU/ml from an approved laboratory).

        Apply for the permit 4-6 weeks before travel and plan the titre test early, since it has specific timing relative to vaccination and travel. The USDA-endorsed health certificate must match the permit conditions precisely.
    - heading: "Routing and the USDA endorsement"
      body: |
        Delta operates a direct Atlanta to Johannesburg service with cargo pet carriage. South African Airways and Emirates (via Dubai) are alternatives. On the US side, the health certificate must be completed by a USDA-accredited vet and endorsed by USDA APHIS, which takes 2-10 business days. Use VEHCS for the fastest turnaround, and coordinate the timing so the endorsed certificate is ready before departure.

faqs:
  - question: "Does my dog need a rabies titre test for South Africa?"
    answer: "Yes. South Africa requires a rabies antibody titre test for dogs, with a minimum result of 0.5 IU/ml from an approved laboratory and specific timing relative to vaccination and travel. The exact conditions are set out in your DALRRD import permit. Plan the titre test early, as a mistimed or failed test delays the move."
  - question: "Is there quarantine on arrival in South Africa from the USA?"
    answer: "There is no general quarantine for pets arriving with correct documentation. Provided your DALRRD import permit, microchip, vaccination, titre test (for dogs), and USDA-endorsed health certificate are all in order, your pet is released after the state vet inspection on arrival."
  - question: "How is this different from the reverse route, South Africa to USA?"
    answer: "Very different. USA to South Africa is a medium-complexity route: permit, titre test for dogs, endorsed certificate, no general quarantine. South Africa to USA is far more complex because the CDC classifies South Africa as a high-risk country, triggering a titre-test-or-28-day-facility-quarantine requirement and an arrival airport constraint. The same pair is straightforward one way and complex the other, due to the CDC classification."
  - question: "How long does the USDA endorsement take?"
    answer: "Electronic submission via VEHCS typically processes in 2-5 business days; mail takes 5-10. Since the health certificate examination must be within 10 days of travel, time the endorsement carefully. Most owners schedule the vet examination 7-8 days before travel and submit immediately via VEHCS so the endorsed certificate is ready before departure."
