  sections:
    - heading: "FAVN titre test and the 90-day wait"
      body: |
        Norway applies EU Implementing Regulation 2026/636 through the EEA Agreement. Uruguay is classified as an unlisted third country, making the FAVN titre test mandatory. Blood must be drawn at least 30 days after the most recent rabies vaccination. The result must show at least 0.5 IU/ml at an EU-approved laboratory.

        The 90-day waiting period runs from the blood draw date. Mattilsynet grants no exemptions. After the 90 days, the MGAP-accredited vet issues the CVI and MGAP-DGSG endorses it. Allow 1-2 working days for the MGAP-DGSG endorsement step within the 10-day certificate window. For dogs, the tapeworm treatment must also be recorded in the CVI before MGAP-DGSG endorses it.

    - heading: "Tapeworm treatment and advance notification"
      body: |
        All dogs entering Norway must receive praziquantel treatment (minimum 2.5 mg/kg) between 24 and 120 hours before arrival at Oslo Gardermoen. The vet records the product name, dose, exact date, and exact time in the CVI. Cats are not subject to this requirement.

        Mattilsynet also requires 48-hour advance notification before non-EEA pets arrive at OSL. Use the Mattilsynet online notification portal or contact the Border Control Post at Oslo Gardermoen directly. Iberia and LATAM connections from Montevideo (MVD) via Madrid (MAD) offer the most direct European hub routing, with SAS and other carriers connecting MAD to OSL.
