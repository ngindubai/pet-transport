content:
  h1: "Pet Transport Uruguay to Norway: 2026 Guide"
  overview: "Uruguay to Norway involves two distinct preparation tracks that run in sequence. First, the EU unlisted country process: FAVN titre test, a 30-day post-vaccination wait before the blood draw, and a 90-day wait from the draw date before entry. That alone takes 20-26 weeks. Second, and separately, dogs must receive an Echinococcus treatment (praziquantel or epsiprantel) between 24 and 120 hours before arriving in Norway. Missing the tapeworm treatment window by even a few hours is enough to cause problems at the border. Norway is EEA but not EU and applies equivalent pet travel rules to EU member states."

  sections:
    - heading: "Norway's requirements for pets from unlisted countries"
      body: |
        Norway's Norwegian Food Safety Authority (Mattilsynet) applies the same unlisted-country rules as EU member states. For a pet from Uruguay, the standard process applies: microchip before vaccination; rabies vaccination with 21 days elapsed since the primary dose; FAVN titre test at an EU-approved laboratory at least 30 days after vaccination; result of 0.5 IU/ml or above; 90-day wait from the blood draw date.

        At Oslo Gardermoen (OSL), Mattilsynet inspectors check the health certificate, the titre test result (with blood draw date clearly stated), the vaccination record, and the microchip. The health certificate must be the EU Annex IV non-commercial format, endorsed by Uruguay's DGSG authority within 10 days of travel.

        For cats, that is the complete process. For dogs, there is a further requirement.

    - heading: "The tapeworm treatment requirement for dogs entering Norway"
      body: |
        All dogs entering Norway must be treated for Echinococcus (fox dwarf tapeworm) before arrival. The treatment must contain praziquantel or epsiprantel and must be administered between 24 and 120 hours before the dog arrives in Norway. Mattilsynet is precise about this window: treatment given 121 hours before arrival is non-compliant; treatment given less than 24 hours before arrival may also cause issues because the parasite eggs can still be present in faeces for up to 24 hours after treatment.

        A veterinarian must document the treatment, recording the product name, the exact date and time of administration, and the dosage. This record must accompany the health certificate. Mattilsynet inspectors check it at OSL.

        The timing calculation is based on your arrival time in Norway, not departure from Uruguay. Work backwards from your scheduled landing at OSL. If you arrive at 06:00 on a Thursday, the treatment window opens at 06:00 on Tuesday and closes at 06:00 on Wednesday. Given the transit time from Montevideo, administering the treatment just before departure from a South American or European hub, where a vet is available, may be the most practical approach. Discuss the timing with your freight forwarder before booking.

    - heading: "Routing from Montevideo to Oslo Gardermoen"
      body: |
        Carrasco International Airport (MVD) in Montevideo has no direct connections to Oslo. The typical routing involves two or three legs: MVD to Sao Paulo Guarulhos (GRU) or Buenos Aires Ezeiza (EZE), then transatlantic to a European hub such as Frankfurt (FRA), Amsterdam (AMS), Madrid (MAD), or Paris (CDG), followed by a final leg to Oslo Gardermoen (OSL).

        SAS Cargo operates to Oslo from FRA, AMS, and other European hubs. Norwegian Air Shuttle has European routes with cargo capacity on some services. From MAD, Iberia or SAS arrange the Oslo connection. From CDG, Air France or Norwegian can carry onward.

        The Echinococcus treatment window can be managed at a European hub layover if the timing works out. If not, the treatment can be given by a vet at MVD before departure, provided the total transit time to OSL is within the 24-120 hour window. Calculate carefully.

    - heading: "The DGSG export certificate from Uruguay"
      body: |
        The Direccion General de Servicios Ganaderos (DGSG), within Uruguay's Ministerio de Ganaderia, Agricultura y Pesca (MGAP), issues and endorses export health certificates for companion animals. A MGAP-registered vet prepares the EU Annex IV format document, including titre test details and, for dogs, a reference to the Echinococcus treatment record.

        Allow three to five business days for the DGSG endorsement. The certificate must be issued within 10 days of travel. Since the health certificate is needed at Oslo and the Echinococcus treatment must happen within 24-120 hours of arrival, the certificate timing and the tapeworm treatment timing both need to be planned carefully relative to the travel date.

faqs:
  - question: "Do cats need the tapeworm treatment to enter Norway from Uruguay?"
    answer: "No. The Echinococcus treatment requirement applies to dogs only. Cats moving from Uruguay to Norway need the standard EU unlisted country process: microchip, rabies vaccination, FAVN titre test (0.5 IU/ml or above), 90-day wait from blood draw date, and an endorsed health certificate."
  - question: "Can the tapeworm treatment be given in Uruguay before departure?"
    answer: "Yes, provided the total transit time from the treatment to arrival in Norway falls within the 24-120 hour window. If your journey from MVD to OSL takes 30 hours, you can administer the treatment in Montevideo approximately 72 hours before departure, which would put the treatment at around 102 hours before arrival, within the window. Calculate the exact hours based on your specific itinerary."
  - question: "Norway is not in the EU. Does that change the titre test requirements?"
    answer: "Norway is EEA and follows EU pet travel rules under the EEA Agreement. The FAVN titre test, 90-day wait, and health certificate requirements for pets from unlisted third countries such as Uruguay are the same as for EU member states. Mattilsynet is the competent authority."
  - question: "What if the titre test result is below 0.5 IU/ml?"
    answer: "A result below 0.5 IU/ml means the vaccination has not produced sufficient antibody response. Your vet must revaccinate the animal, a new 30-day wait must elapse, a new blood draw must be arranged, and the 90-day mandatory wait restarts from the new draw date. This can add several months to the timeline, which is why starting early and using a high-quality, EU-approved vaccine is important."
