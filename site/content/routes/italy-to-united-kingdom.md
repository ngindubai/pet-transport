  overview: |
    Italy to the UK is a well-travelled route for expats and Italian nationals with British connections. The process is managed under the UK's Pet Travel Scheme, which covers all EU member states. No titre test is required, and there is no routine quarantine. The key practical requirement that trips up owners is the tapeworm treatment for dogs: it must be administered by a veterinarian within a strict 24-to-120-hour window before the pet arrives in Great Britain.

    Pets can travel using either an EU pet passport (issued in Italy by a licensed vet) or an Animal Health Certificate. The EU pet passport is the more convenient document for Italian pets because it accumulates records over time. Dogs still need a fresh tapeworm treatment entry in the passport for each UK trip.

    ITA Airways and British Airways both handle cargo on this corridor. Most pets from Italy to the UK travel as manifested cargo, cleared through Heathrow, Gatwick, or Manchester.

faqs:
  - question: "Does my dog need a titre test to travel from Italy to the UK?"
    answer: "No. Italy is an EU member state and is listed under the UK's Pet Travel Scheme. No titre test is required. The requirements are: ISO microchip, current rabies vaccination, tapeworm treatment for dogs (24-120 hours before UK arrival), and a valid EU pet passport or Animal Health Certificate."
  - question: "What exactly is the tapeworm treatment requirement for Italy to UK?"
    answer: "Dogs only (not cats) must be treated against tapeworm by a veterinarian between 24 and 120 hours before their scheduled arrival time in Great Britain. The drug must contain praziquantel. The treatment must be recorded in the EU pet passport or AHC with the date, time, drug name, and vet signature. If the treatment falls outside this window, the dog may be refused entry."
  - question: "Can I use an Italian EU pet passport to enter the UK?"
    answer: "Yes. An EU pet passport issued by a licensed veterinarian in Italy is accepted for entry into Great Britain. It must show the microchip number recorded before the rabies vaccination date, current rabies vaccination, and the tapeworm treatment entry for dogs for this specific journey."
  - question: "Which airlines carry pets from Italy to the UK?"
    answer: "ITA Airways and British Airways are the main options for cargo shipment from Italian airports to the UK. ITA Airways operates cargo from Rome Fiumicino and Milan Malpensa to Heathrow. British Airways serves the same route via BA World Cargo. Low-cost carriers Ryanair and easyJet do not accept pets. Book through an IATA-registered cargo agent for the best options."
  - question: "How long does the UK border inspection take?"
    answer: "For compliant pets, the APHA inspection at Heathrow, Gatwick, or Manchester is brief. The border vet scans the microchip, checks the travel document, and verifies the tapeworm treatment record. If all is in order, the pet is released on arrival day. Incomplete documentation can result in quarantine at an authorised facility at the owner's expense."
