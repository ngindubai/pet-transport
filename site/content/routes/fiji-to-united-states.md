  h1: "Pet Transport from Fiji to the United States"
  overview: |
    Fiji does not appear on the CDC's high-risk list for dog rabies. That single fact removes most of the friction from this route: no titre test, no 28-day quarantine at a CDC-registered facility, no restriction on which US airport you land at. A dog needs the free CDC Dog Import Form and little else at the federal border. A cat needs even less; the US has essentially no federal import rule for cats at all.

    The genuine work on this route sits on the Fiji side, not the US side. The Biosecurity Authority of Fiji still has to inspect and certify the animal before it leaves Nadi, and the flight itself only runs on one carrier. Fiji Airways operates the sole nonstop service to the US, landing at Los Angeles or San Francisco on an Airbus A350, the one aircraft type in its fleet cleared to carry live cargo on long-haul sectors.

    Complexity here is low by this site's standards, but not zero. Get the BAF export certificate timed correctly and book the Fiji Airways cargo slot early, and this is one of the more straightforward international moves a Fiji-based owner can make.

  sections:
    - heading: "Why Fiji's rabies-free status simplifies the US side"
      body: |
        The CDC sorts countries into two groups for dog imports: high-risk and everything else. High-risk countries trigger a titre test requirement or a 28-day stay at a CDC-registered animal care facility, and force the dog to land at one of a short list of approved airports. Fiji sits outside that high-risk group entirely, a status tied to its own rabies-free record.

        Checked directly against the CDC's published high-risk list on 13 July 2026, Fiji is absent. That means a dog that has lived in Fiji for the 6 months before travel needs only the CDC Dog Import Form, a microchip, and to be at least 6 months old and visibly healthy on arrival. No blood test, no waiting period tied to US entry, no restricted airport list. Cats face no federal requirement at all, though checking the destination state's own rules is still worth doing.

    - heading: "What BAF still asks for before departure"
      body: |
        None of the above changes what Fiji itself requires before a pet can leave. The Biosecurity Authority of Fiji controls the exit side of this move regardless of destination. A vet in Fiji completes the export paperwork, then a BAF veterinary officer has to inspect the animal at a booked final appointment, with all supporting documents reaching BAF at least 7 days ahead of that date.

        Processing after the inspection usually takes 3 to 5 working days. That's a short window on paper, but it still needs building into the plan alongside the cargo booking; leaving BAF paperwork until the final week before travel is the most common way this otherwise simple route goes wrong.

    - heading: "One airline, two gateways, and a closing third option"
      body: |
        Fiji Airways is currently the only carrier flying nonstop between Nadi and the United States, serving Los Angeles and San Francisco with up to 11 combined weekly flights as of mid-2026. Both routes use the Airbus A350, the aircraft Fiji Airways permits for live animal cargo; its smaller regional aircraft are not cleared for it.

        A third gateway, Dallas/Fort Worth, has been available since late 2024 but Fiji Airways confirmed it is ending that route from 7 September 2026, citing fuel costs and demand. Anyone planning travel after that date should not build a plan around Dallas. Fiji Airways' 2025 codeshare agreement with American Airlines, as part of joining the oneworld alliance, makes ticketing an onward US domestic leg more straightforward, though the pet itself still typically needs a separate cargo booking for that connection rather than a single through shipment.
