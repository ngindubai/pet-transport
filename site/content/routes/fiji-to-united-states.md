  h1: "Pet Transport from Fiji to the United States"
  overview: |
    Here's the asymmetry worth knowing before anything else: a pet leaving Fiji for the US faces no quarantine and no titre test, while a pet making the opposite journey is locked into a minimum 30-day stay at a Fiji government facility on arrival. Same two countries, wildly different rulebooks, because each side is applying its own risk framework rather than a shared one.

    For this direction, the CDC does not list Fiji among its high-risk countries for dog rabies, so dogs only need the free CDC Dog Import Form, current since August 2024. Cats face no federal US paperwork at all. Fiji's own exit process, run by the Biosecurity Authority of Fiji, is the part that actually takes planning: a vet-completed certificate, a booked BAF inspection, and a few working days of processing.

  sections:
    - heading: "Why the US side is almost a non-event"
      body: |
        Dogs from CDC high-risk countries face a rabies titre test, strict vaccination timing and sometimes a wait before travel. None of that touches Fiji. It sits outside the high-risk list entirely, so the only mandatory federal document is the CDC Dog Import Form, filled in online for free and valid for six months.

        Cats get an even shorter list: nothing. There is no federal US requirement specific to cats entering from any country, Fiji included, though your airline and the BAF export vet will still expect standard health and vaccination paperwork.

        That doesn't make the trip effortless, just administratively light on the American side. Most of the real planning happens before the plane leaves Nadi.

    - heading: "What BAF wants before your pet can leave Fiji"
      body: |
        The Biosecurity Authority of Fiji controls the exit, not the airline. A registered vet in Fiji completes the export certificate, then a BAF veterinary officer has to physically inspect the animal at a booked appointment and sign off. Everything supporting that inspection, vaccination history, microchip number, the vet's own paperwork, needs to land with BAF at least seven days ahead of the appointment date.

        Processing after the inspection usually takes three to five working days. Leave it to the final week before travel and you risk missing your flight, since there's no way to rush the booking once it's made.

    - heading: "The direct flight, and why it still needs an agent"
      body: |
        Fiji Airways flies Nadi to Los Angeles nonstop, daily, which puts this route in a different category from most Pacific-to-mainland journeys that force a transfer. No second airline, no second set of pet-acceptance rules to check.

        The catch is that this nonstop flight still only carries animals as cargo. There's no cabin option beyond a trained service dog, and no checked-baggage pathway at all. Booking goes through the Fiji Freight Department or an IATA cargo agent, not a standard airline reservation, and the airline enforces a hard minimum: 6 months old, at least 6 lbs (3 kg). A very young kitten or a toy-breed puppy under that threshold simply will not be accepted on this flight.
