  sections:
    - heading: "The ONSSA permit and breed restrictions"
      body: |
        ONSSA, Morocco's food and agricultural safety authority, handles pet import permits. Apply at least 4 weeks before your intended travel date. The application covers your pet's microchip details, vaccination history, and entry point. Morocco uses Casablanca's Mohammed V Airport as the primary entry point for cargo, though other airports handle arriving pets too.

        Morocco enforces breed restrictions and bans the import of Pit Bull Terrier types, Rottweilers, and Dogo Argentinos. This is a firm restriction, and attempting to import a banned breed without prior authorisation will result in the animal being refused entry. If your dog is any of these types, contact ONSSA before making any other preparations.

    - heading: "Titre test and health certificate timing"
      body: |
        ONSSA classifies countries by their rabies risk status, and whether a titre test is required depends on where Morocco places the UK in that classification. The UK's rabies-free status is well-established, but individual countries determine their own assessment. Verify the current position with ONSSA or through a pet transport agent before committing to the process, as this affects your preparation timeline by several weeks.

        The APHA health certificate is valid for 14 days from issue when entering Morocco, which gives slightly more flexibility than some destinations. That said, time your vet appointment carefully to ensure the certificate is still valid on arrival. APHA endorsement takes 1-5 working days after the vet visit. Royal Air Maroc is the most direct option from the UK for both cabin-eligible pets and cargo.

faqs:
  - question: "Does my pet need a titre test to enter Morocco from the UK?"
    answer: "Whether a titre test is required depends on how ONSSA classifies the UK in its country risk assessment for rabies. Verify this with ONSSA directly or through a pet transport agent before starting preparations. If required, the titre test blood draw must happen at least 30 days after the most recent rabies vaccination, and you should allow several weeks for the full process."

  - question: "Which breeds are banned from entering Morocco?"
    answer: "Morocco prohibits the import of Pit Bull Terrier types, Rottweilers, and Dogo Argentinos. These are firm restrictions and no exemptions are routinely granted. If your dog is one of these types or a cross-breed that resembles them closely, contact ONSSA before applying for an import permit. Do not attempt to travel with a banned breed without prior written authorisation."

  - question: "Is there quarantine when a dog or cat arrives in Morocco from the UK?"
    answer: "No routine quarantine applies for pets arriving with correct documentation, a valid ONSSA import permit, and compliant vaccinations. An ONSSA veterinary inspection takes place on arrival. For pets with all paperwork in order this is typically brief. Pets with non-compliant documentation may be quarantined or refused entry at the owner's expense."

  - question: "Can I fly my dog in the cabin on UK to Morocco flights?"
    answer: "Royal Air Maroc permits small dogs and cats in the cabin on many of its UK-Morocco routes, provided the combined weight of pet and carrier is within the airline's limit (typically around 8-10 kg). British Airways transports pets as cargo on this route. Budget carriers including easyJet and Ryanair generally do not carry pets. Always confirm the current cabin pet policy directly with the airline before booking."
