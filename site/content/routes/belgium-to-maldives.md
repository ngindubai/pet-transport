content:
  h1: "Pet Transport from Belgium to Maldives"
  overview: |
    Start with the dog question, because it has one answer and it isn't flexible. Import of dogs into the Maldives is prohibited under the Maldives Customs Service's own list of banned items, the same list that covers alcohol and pork products, and there's no standard visa-style exception route for a pet owner to apply through.

    A cat is a workable move. Microchip, rabies vaccination between 30 days and 12 months before arrival, the core feline vaccines, and a health certificate signed within 10 days of travel cover most of what's published. Where the guidance thins out is permits and titre tests, genuinely unsettled territory that's worth a direct check before you commit to a flight date.

  sections:
    - heading: "The dog answer doesn't change with better paperwork"
      body: |
        No standard pathway exists for a dog entering the Maldives, assistance animals included, as far as any primary Maldivian government document we could locate confirms. Some online forums mention informal exceptions. We haven't found a government source backing that up as a repeatable process, so we won't present it as one here. If a service dog is genuinely involved, get it in writing from the Maldives government directly before assuming anything.
    - heading: "What a cat needs, and where the gaps are"
      body: |
        The core sequence is straightforward: microchip first, then rabies vaccination in the 30-days-to-12-months window Maldivian guidance asks for, plus the standard feline vaccines. A certificate signed within 10 days of entry closes it out. Two questions don't have a clean answer though: whether a general import permit applies to a pet travelling with its owner, and whether a titre test is required at all. Different sources say different things. Treat both as open until your vet or agent confirms them directly, not as settled facts.
    - heading: "A breed detail owners of exotic-pattern cats should check"
      body: |
        Savannah and Bengal cats sit in a different category. Under guidance summarised by PetTravel.com, they need to be at least five generations removed from their wild ancestor before the Maldives treats them as an ordinary domestic cat. Most pet-market Savannahs and Bengals clear that bar comfortably, but check your cat's pedigree paperwork before booking if this applies to you.
    - heading: "Getting to Male from Brussels"
      body: |
        Neither Emirates via Dubai nor Qatar Airways via Doha is a direct flight, but both are established, frequent corridors handling live animal cargo as routine business. Brussels Airlines sells seats on this route yet doesn't fly the Male sector itself, routing the booking through one of those two partners instead. Confirm the actual operating carrier for the Male leg before assuming your ticket airline is the one handling the cat.
