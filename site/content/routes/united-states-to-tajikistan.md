  h1: "Pet Transport from the United States to Tajikistan"
  overview: |
    A dog flying from Dushanbe to New York runs into the CDC's full high-risk protocol: a titre test or a 28-day quarantine, a government-endorsed certification form, and arrival restricted to a short list of airports with a CDC-registered facility. The same dog flying the other way, from a US city to Dushanbe, runs into none of it.

    Tajikistan does not require a rabies antibody titre test from any country. What it does require is a rabies vaccination given between 30 days and 12 months before entry, a USDA-endorsed health certificate, and paperwork that matches exactly. Get something wrong and the consequences run further than most routes: quarantine, being sent home, or in the worst cases euthanasia, all paid for by the owner.

    Expect seven to ten weeks in total, driven mostly by the 30-day rabies wait and the lack of a direct flight rather than by anything medically demanding.
  sections:
    - heading: "One country, two rulebooks depending on direction"
      body: |
        Tajikistan sits on the CDC's high-risk list for dog rabies, current as of the list's most recent update. That classification governs what happens to a Tajikistan-origin dog trying to enter the United States, not what happens to a US pet trying to enter Tajikistan. The two directions are governed by entirely separate regulatory systems, and conflating them is the most common planning mistake on this corridor.

        Going outbound from the US, a current rabies vaccination and a USDA-endorsed health certificate cover the requirement. No titre test, no CDC-registered facility booking, no species split between dogs and cats on this side of the process.

        Source: State Veterinary Service, Ministry of Agriculture of the Republic of Tajikistan; CDC, cdc.gov/importation/dogs, for the separate reverse-direction rules.
    - heading: "Why the paperwork still deserves full attention"
      body: |
        The lighter medical bar does not translate into a forgiving arrival process. Tajikistan's guidance is direct about the consequence of incomplete documentation: quarantine, return to the country of origin, or in serious cases euthanasia, entirely at the owner's expense. That is a firmer line than Kyrgyzstan, its immediate neighbour on this site, takes for the same kind of error.

        Build in enough time to double-check the vaccination date window, the health certificate's closeness to travel, and the USDA endorsement, rather than treating any one of them as a formality.
    - heading: "Routing: Istanbul is the anchor, Dubai is the backup"
      body: |
        Turkish Airlines' network is the most dependable single-carrier option here: fly from a US gateway to Istanbul, then on to Dushanbe roughly seven times a week. Emirates SkyCargo covers the alternative path, connecting a US departure to Dubai, where Somon Air picks up the final Dubai-Dushanbe leg.

        Neither route is direct, and neither runs daily, so book the flight before finalising the vet appointment dates rather than the other way round.
