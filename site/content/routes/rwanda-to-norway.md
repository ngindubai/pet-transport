  sections:
    - heading: "Kigali to Oslo: routing options"
      body: |
        KLM Cargo runs the most structured path, connecting Kigali (KGL) through Nairobi (NBO) and Amsterdam (AMS) before the final leg into Oslo Gardermoen (OSL). This is the KLM/Kenya Airways joint venture, and it is the option agents on this route use most often for larger dogs. Brussels Airlines flies KGL-BRU directly, with a straightforward onward connection to Oslo given how much traffic moves between Brussels and Scandinavia. Ethiopian Airlines Cargo offers a well-established alternative via Addis Ababa (ADD). RwandAir also serves BRU from Kigali and is worth checking as a second option if your preferred carrier's cargo hold is full on the date you need. Whichever carrier you choose, confirm the connecting leg accepts live animal cargo before booking, since not every partner flight does.

    - heading: "The unlisted-country sequence, and why the 90-day clock matters most"
      body: |
        Rwanda is not on the EU's approved list for simplified pet travel, and Norway applies the equivalent rule under its EEA alignment. That means the microchip has to go in first, followed by the rabies vaccination, then a 30-day wait before blood can be drawn for an FAVN titre test at an EU-approved lab. The result needs to clear 0.5 IU/ml. From there, a 90-day wait runs from the draw date, not the day the lab emails you the number back. Most owners can get through the vet steps in six or seven weeks. It's the 90 days after that catches people planning a move around a fixed date.

    - heading: "The tapeworm rule Norway adds on top"
      body: |
        Norway asks for one thing the standard EU sequence doesn't cover: a Praziquantel tapeworm treatment for every dog, given by a vet between 1 and 5 days before arrival and written into the health certificate. It doesn't matter that Rwanda already goes through the full unlisted-country process; this is a separate, Norway-specific check that applies regardless of origin. Cats are not included. Timing it wrong (too early) means a repeat treatment closer to the travel date, so build it into the schedule after the flight is confirmed, not before.
