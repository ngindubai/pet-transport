  h1: "Pet Transport from Paraguay to Australia"
  overview: |
    Paraguay does not appear anywhere on DAFF's list of approved countries for cat and dog import. Not in the current three-group system, not under whatever name the classification carries by the time you read this. That single gap changes the shape of the whole move: there is no paperwork path that runs straight from Asuncion to Melbourne, no matter how complete the vaccination record is.

    DAFF's answer for a pet from a non-approved country is a 180-day residency requirement in a country that is approved, followed by that country's own titre test and quarantine process. For a Paraguay-origin pet, the US and Canada are the two stepping-stone countries worth considering first: both are DAFF-approved, and both are quicker to enter from Paraguay than the UK, since Paraguay sits outside the CDC's and CFIA's high-risk rabies lists. Realistically, budget close to a year.

  sections:
    - heading: "Why there's no direct route, and what DAFF asks for instead"
      body: |
        DAFF groups approved countries by rabies and disease risk. Paraguay is not on any of those lists, which means the standard titre-test-and-quarantine process, the one that applies to a pet coming from the US, Canada or the UK, simply doesn't have a version that starts in Asuncion. Instead, DAFF requires 180 consecutive days of residency in an approved country before it will even accept an import application from that pet.

        Only after that residency clock has started does the familiar process kick in: an RNATT titre test at a DAFF-approved laboratory, a result of at least 0.5 IU/ml, a further 180-day wait from the blood draw, a BICON import permit, and a minimum 10-day stay at Mickleham on arrival.

    - heading: "Choosing the stepping-stone: why the US or Canada beats the UK here"
      body: |
        Most guides to this kind of move default to the UK as the stepping-stone, since it's the most established path for expats leaving a non-approved country. For a Paraguay-origin pet specifically, that's not the fastest option. Paraguay is not on the CDC's high-risk dog rabies list (last checked 15 April 2026) or the CFIA's equivalent list (last revised 13 March 2025), which strips the titre test and fixed waiting period out of the entry process for both the US and Canada. Getting the pet legally into either country takes roughly four to seven weeks.

        Compare that with entering the UK from Paraguay, which needs its own rabies antibody titre test and a fixed three-month wait before the pet can even land at Heathrow. Choosing the US or Canada as the stepping-stone, rather than defaulting to the UK, is the single biggest time saving available on this route.

    - heading: "Running two 180-day clocks at once"
      body: |
        Here's the detail that separates a well-planned version of this move from a slow one. DAFF's residency requirement and the RNATT titre test's post-blood-draw wait are both 180 days, but they don't have to be sequential. If the vet draws blood for the titre test soon after the pet's arrival in the stepping-stone country, both clocks run largely in parallel, and the total added time is closer to six or seven months than twelve.

        This isn't spelled out in a single official worked example, so treat it as a plan to confirm with an experienced agent rather than a guaranteed shortcut. Get the sequencing wrong and the two waits stack instead of overlapping, adding months you didn't budget for.
