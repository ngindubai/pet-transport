  h1: "Pet Transport from India to Switzerland"
  overview: |
    Switzerland is not an EU member, but when it comes to pet import from non-listed countries like India, it follows EU-equivalent rules under its bilateral agreements. That means the same titre test and 3-month waiting period that apply for EU destinations also apply here.

    The sequence is fixed: microchip first, then rabies vaccination (after the chip), then a titre test blood draw at least 30 days later. The result must reach 0.5 IU/ml or higher at an approved laboratory, and then a 3-month wait begins before your pet can travel. The health certificate -- issued by an official government vet in India -- is only valid for 10 days from issue, so time that carefully around your departure.

    Good news on the flight side: SWISS and Air India both fly direct from Delhi (DEL) to Zurich (ZRH), keeping routing straightforward. Allow 20-24 weeks from the start of preparation. A pet relocation agent experienced with Swiss FSVO requirements can handle the health certificate format and government endorsement steps, which is worth considering on a route this involved.
