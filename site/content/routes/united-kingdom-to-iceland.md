  h1: "Pet Transport from the United Kingdom to Iceland"
  overview: |
    MAST will not take the same paperwork from a UK vet that gets a dog into France or Germany. Its own Form D1, not the familiar bilingual Export Health Certificate, is the document actually checked at Keflavik. The UK's rabies-free Category 1 status trims the pre-export timeline, no 90-day wait once a titre test clears, but changes nothing about the mandatory 14-day quarantine on arrival, which applies to every incoming pet regardless of where it boarded the flight.

    Budget for a battery of tests most owners do not expect. Alongside rabies, MAST wants negative Brucella canis and, for intact dogs, Leishmania results, plus cover for Angiostrongylus vasorum. None of this is optional. A missing result can mean the dog does not travel.

  sections:
    - heading: "What Category 1 status changes, and what it does not"
      body: |
        The UK's place on MAST's Category 1 list affects one thing only: the pre-export timeline. Once the rabies titre test clears at 0.5 IU/ml or above, drawn at least 30 days after vaccination, there is no additional fixed wait before travel. A Category 2 country's pet faces a further 90-day wait counted from the blood draw itself.

        What Category 1 status does not touch is the quarantine. Every dog and cat entering Iceland spends a minimum of 14 days at Einangrunarstoedin i Reykjanesbae or Einangrunarstoedin Mosel, British or otherwise. It is a genuine biosecurity measure protecting an island with no endemic rabies, distemper or parvovirus, not an administrative penalty for incomplete paperwork, and it catches out owners who assume a rabies-free home country buys their pet a shortcut.

    - heading: "Form D1: the certificate a UK vet has probably never filled in"
      body: |
        A standard APHA Export Health Certificate, the document that satisfies most of this site's other UK-outbound routes, is the wrong form for Iceland. MAST requires its own Certificate of Health and Origin, Form D1 for dogs or C1 for cats, completed by the same APHA-authorised OV but structured entirely around MAST's own requirements.

        That means negative blood tests for Brucella canis and, for an intact dog, Leishmania spp., a test or preventive treatment for Angiostrongylus vasorum, and two separate rounds of internal and external parasite treatment timed 21 to 28 days and then 5 to 10 days before import. The completed certificate, with lab reports attached, has to reach MAST at petimport@mast.is no later than 5 days before the dog or cat is due to land. Miss that deadline and MAST can reject the import outright.

    - heading: "Two direct flights, one fixed quarantine calendar"
      body: |
        Icelandair and British Airways both fly Heathrow to Keflavik nonstop, a genuine advantage over routes served by a single carrier. Neither takes pets as checked baggage, so the booking runs through Icelandair Cargo or through PetAir UK and IAG Cargo on the British Airways side.

        The harder constraint sits on the ground. Reykjanesbae and Mosel each admit new arrivals only within a fixed three-day window roughly every three weeks, and Keflavik will not accept an arriving pet outside 06:00 to 17:00 on those dates. Reserving a quarantine slot before locking in a flight, rather than the other way round, is what keeps this route on schedule.

    answer: "Yes. Category 1 status only removes the extra 90-day wait that Category 2 countries face after a titre test. The mandatory 14-day quarantine at an approved isolation station applies to every pet entering Iceland, UK-origin pets included, with no exemption for rabies-free countries or complete paperwork."

    answer: "MAST's own Certificate of Health and Origin, Form D1 for dogs or Form C1 for cats. This is not the standard bilingual Export Health Certificate used for most other destinations. It must be completed by an APHA-authorised Official Veterinarian and sent to MAST at petimport@mast.is no later than 5 days before the import date."

    answer: "Form D1 also requires a negative Brucella canis blood test, and for intact dogs a negative Leishmania spp. test, both within 30 days of import. Dogs also need either a negative Angiostrongylus vasorum test or a preventive spot-on treatment 5 to 10 days before import, plus current core vaccination against leptospirosis, distemper, hepatitis, parvovirus and parainfluenza."

    answer: "Yes. Both Icelandair and British Airways operate Heathrow to Keflavik nonstop. Neither carries pets as checked baggage: Icelandair moves them as cargo through Icelandair Cargo, and British Airways through PetAir UK and IAG Cargo."
