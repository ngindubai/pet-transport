  sections:
    - heading: "Why flying beats the nine to ten hour drive"
      body: |
        Nine to ten hours is a long day behind the wheel with a pet, longer than the drives into Hungary or the Czech Republic that Slovenian owners often manage without much fuss. Most owners heading to Warsaw fly instead, and the time saved usually outweighs the extra cost of a cargo or cabin fee.

        LOT Polish Airlines and Austrian Airlines both offer connecting routes through Vienna or Munich, with cabin space for smaller pets and a cargo service for larger dogs. Book the crate or cabin slot as soon as the travel date is fixed, since capacity on the connecting leg can be tighter than the first.

    - heading: "Paperwork: EU pet passport, AHC and the April 2026 rule"
      body: |
        The EU pet passport, issued by a UVHVVR-authorised vet in Slovenia, remains valid for the pet's life provided the rabies vaccination inside it is kept current. Since 22 April 2026, the passport has been restricted to owners primarily resident in the EU, a change that doesn't affect the majority of Slovenian pet owners.

        Non-EU nationals resident in Slovenia need a single-use AHC instead, issued within 10 days of travel and endorsed by UVHVVR. Poland's Chief Veterinary Inspectorate (GIW) checks the microchip against the passport or AHC on arrival in Warsaw, so confirm every number matches before departure.

    - heading: "Flight options via Vienna or Munich"
      body: |
        LOT Polish Airlines is the natural choice for the Warsaw end of the journey, with connections through Vienna or Munich depending on schedule. Austrian Airlines via Vienna is a solid second option if LOT's dates or crate availability don't line up. Both carriers offer cabin space for small pets and a cargo service for larger ones, so weigh crate size against the airline's specific limits before booking either leg.
