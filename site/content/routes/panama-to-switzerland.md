content:
  h1: "Pet Transport Panama to Switzerland: 2026 Guide"
  overview: "Panama to Switzerland is among the most preparation-intensive routes in Central America to Europe pet transport. Switzerland follows EU-equivalent rules under the Federal Food Safety and Veterinary Office (FSVO, blv.admin.ch), and Panama's unlisted status means the full titre test pathway applies: FAVN blood draw at least 30 days after vaccination, result of 0.5 IU/ml or above, and a 90-day wait from the blood draw date. Total preparation is 20-26 weeks. An important distinction: Switzerland is not an EU member and maintains its own classification system, so Panama's FSVO classification must be verified independently at blv.admin.ch."

  sections:
    - heading: "Switzerland's FSVO Rules: Similar to the EU but Independent"
      body: |
        This is the most important thing to understand about moving a pet from Panama to Switzerland. Many pet owners assume that because Switzerland follows EU-equivalent rules, the same regulations apply identically. In practice, the FSVO (Bundesamt für Lebensmittelsicherheit und Veterinärwesen, BLV) maintains its own classification of third countries, which broadly mirrors the EU list but is technically independent.

        For Panama, which is unlisted by the EU, the expectation is that Switzerland also classifies Panama as high-risk or unlisted, triggering the titre test requirement. This aligns with the EU's approach under Commission Delegated Regulation (EU) 2026/131. However, because the FSVO has its own list, this must be confirmed at blv.admin.ch before you begin preparations.

        The title test pathway, if applicable (as expected for Panama), requires:

        1. Microchip (ISO 11784/11785) before vaccination
        2. Rabies vaccination after chip
        3. Minimum 30-day wait from vaccination
        4. FAVN blood draw at an EU-approved laboratory
        5. Result of 0.5 IU/ml or above
        6. 90-day wait from the blood draw date
        7. Travel to Switzerland with a DINASA-endorsed Swiss-compatible health certificate

    - heading: "FSVO Inspection at Zurich Airport"
      body: |
        Zurich Airport (ZRH) is Switzerland's primary international cargo hub and the standard entry point for international pet cargo. The FSVO has a veterinary post at ZRH cargo terminal.

        On arrival from Panama, FSVO officials check:

        Microchip: Scanned and verified against the health certificate.

        Rabies vaccination: Current and post-microchip. FSVO checks validity dates carefully.

        FAVN titre test: Result of 0.5 IU/ml or above, with the blood draw date documented. The 90-day wait must have elapsed from the blood draw date to the date of arrival at ZRH.

        Health certificate: Swiss-compatible template, DINASA-endorsed, issued within 10 days of travel.

        Geneva Airport (GVA) can also be used for cargo arrivals in French-speaking Switzerland, but has more limited live animal handling facilities than ZRH. ZRH is the standard recommendation.

    - heading: "Airlines and Routing from PTY to ZRH"
      body: |
        Tocumen International Airport (PTY) has good connections to European hubs via Copa Airlines. The main routing options for live animal cargo to Zurich are:

        Copa to Amsterdam (AMS), then Swiss International Air Lines (SWISS) or KLM cargo to ZRH. AMS to ZRH is a short and well-served sector. SWISS operates its home route from AMS and is well placed to handle live animals arriving in Zurich.

        Copa to Madrid (MAD) via Iberia Cargo, then SWISS or Iberia to ZRH. The MAD to ZRH sector is short and SWISS operates it frequently.

        Lufthansa via Frankfurt (FRA) is particularly effective for larger animals. The Panama-to-FRA routing typically involves Copa to a US gateway and then Lufthansa transatlantic, or Copa to MAD then Lufthansa to FRA. FRA to ZRH is one of Europe's shortest cargo hops.

        Book live animal cargo space well in advance. SWISS cargo capacity from smaller European cities to ZRH can be limited.

    - heading: "The DINASA Health Certificate for Switzerland"
      body: |
        The health certificate for Switzerland is based on the EU Annex IV non-commercial health certificate template. However, Switzerland is not an EU member state and the FSVO may require a slightly adapted version of the template.

        Before having the document prepared, download the current Swiss health certificate template from blv.admin.ch or contact the FSVO directly to confirm which version applies to Panama-origin pets. This step is important because a health certificate on the wrong template, even if it contains all the correct information, may be rejected at the Swiss border.

        The certificate must include the titre test details (date of blood draw, laboratory, result in IU/ml) and must confirm that the 90-day waiting period has been satisfied. DINASA endorses the document after the vet signs it.

    - heading: "Cantonal Rules After Arrival"
      body: |
        Once your pet enters Switzerland and clears FSVO inspection at ZRH, it is free to live anywhere in Switzerland. However, individual cantons have their own rules on dog registration, mandatory third-party liability insurance (Haftpflichtversicherung), and breed-specific regulations.

        Most cantons require dogs to be registered with the cantonal pet database (ANIS). The chip number is used for registration. Check with your destination canton for its specific requirements. Some cantons require a liability insurance policy for dogs above a certain size or breed type.

        Cats generally face fewer cantonal requirements but may need to be included in the national chip register (Identitas) depending on the canton.

  - question: "Switzerland is not in the EU. Does Panama's unlisted EU status also mean it is unlisted for Switzerland?"
    answer: "The expectation is yes, as Switzerland follows broadly EU-equivalent rules and Panama is a high-risk unlisted country for the EU. However, the FSVO (BLV) maintains its own independent classification. You must verify Panama's current FSVO classification at blv.admin.ch before beginning preparations. Do not assume that EU rules apply to Switzerland automatically."
  - question: "My dog already has a valid FAVN titre test result from last year. Does this apply to Switzerland?"
    answer: "If the result is 0.5 IU/ml or above, the blood was drawn at least 30 days after vaccination, and 90 days have elapsed from the blood draw date before your arrival in Switzerland, the result should be valid for Swiss entry. The titre test result does not expire as long as the rabies vaccination remains current. Bring the original laboratory report with you and ensure the result date and details are included in the DINASA health certificate."
  - question: "Can my pet fly in the cabin for the Zurich leg of the journey?"
    answer: "On the PTY to AMS or MAD transatlantic sector, cargo is standard for live animals. On the second leg (AMS or MAD to ZRH), some carriers allow small pets in the cabin. SWISS International Air Lines permits small pets in the cabin on some European routes. Check with SWISS directly for the specific route and your pet's size before booking. However, even if cabin travel is possible on the second leg, cargo is required for the long-haul transatlantic portion."
