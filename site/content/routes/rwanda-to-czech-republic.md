  sections:
    - heading: "Why Rwanda to the Czech Republic takes 20 to 26 weeks"
      body: |
        Rwanda is not on the EU's list of approved countries for simplified pet travel, so the Czech Republic treats it the same way it treats every other unlisted country under EU Regulation (EU) 2020/692. The chip goes in first. The rabies vaccination follows. Then a 21-day wait, then a further wait so the blood draw for the titre test happens at least 30 days post-vaccination. The result has to clear 0.5 IU/ml at an EU-approved lab. None of that is the long part, though. The long part is the 90-day wait that starts on the day blood was drawn, not the day the lab emails the result back. Add cargo lead time on top and 20 to 26 weeks is a realistic full window, not a worst case.

    - heading: "Getting a dog or cat from Kigali to Prague"
      body: |
        Brussels Airlines and RwandAir both fly Kigali to Brussels, and Brussels handles live animal cargo as routine business, so either carrier gives a workable path with a single connection on to Prague. KLM Cargo is the alternate route via Nairobi, drawing on the established KLM/Kenya Airways link out of East Africa. Ethiopian Airlines Cargo via Addis Ababa is a further option if the Brussels-routed carriers are full. Whichever carrier you use, confirm the connecting flight also accepts live animals well before booking, since not every onward leg from a given hub does.

    - heading: "The Rwanda side: RAB and the export paperwork"
      body: |
        There is no formal export permit needed for a personal companion pet leaving Rwanda. What you do need is an EU-format health certificate, issued by an RAB-authorised vet and endorsed by the Rwanda Agriculture and Animal Resources Development Board before departure. This has to be the EU format specifically, not a generic export certificate; a standard Rwandan document will not satisfy Czech import checks. Time the endorsement and certificate issue so they sit inside the 10-day validity window right up until arrival in Prague, accounting for any transit stop.

faqs:
  - question: "Why does a pet from Rwanda need a titre test to enter the Czech Republic?"
    answer: "Rwanda is not on the EU's approved list of countries for simplified pet travel. Under EU Regulation (EU) 2020/692, pets from unlisted countries need a titre test showing at least 0.5 IU/ml at an EU-approved laboratory, plus a 90-day wait from the blood draw date, before entry to any EU state including the Czech Republic."
  - question: "Does the 90-day wait start from the blood draw or the result date?"
    answer: "From the blood draw date. This is the single detail that catches people out most on this route. Even if the lab result takes several weeks to arrive, the 90-day clock has already been running since the day the sample was taken."
  - question: "What happens if the microchip is implanted after the rabies vaccination?"
    answer: "The vaccination will not count for the titre test sequence. EU rules require the chip before or on the same day as the vaccination. If the order is reversed, the pet needs a fresh vaccination after the chip is confirmed, and the 30-day and 90-day waits both restart from that new vaccination date."
  - question: "Which airline is best for a dog travelling from Kigali to Prague?"
    answer: "Brussels Airlines or RwandAir, both via Brussels, are the most direct paths and Brussels is well set up for live animal cargo. KLM via Nairobi and Ethiopian Airlines via Addis Ababa are reasonable alternates if those flights are booked up or don't suit your schedule."
