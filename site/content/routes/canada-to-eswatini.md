content:
  h1: "Pet Transport from Canada to Eswatini"
  overview: |
    Eswatini's own import rules ask for less than the flight itself involves. No rabies titre test, no CFIA-style facility system, nothing that resembles the machinery built around a high-risk-country dog entering the United States. What Eswatini does want is a current rabies vaccination, a microchip, and a Veterinary Import Permit from the Director of Animal Health, since Canada sits outside the regional SADC bloc that gets a waiver on that step.

    The permit is the genuine lead-time item on this route, not a blood test. It's valid for six months once granted, so applying early costs nothing and removes one variable from the schedule. What doesn't shorten, whatever the paperwork looks like, is the flight. Nothing lands at King Mswati III International Airport from anywhere in Canada without at least two connections, since Johannesburg is the only realistic gateway into Eswatini's own regional network.
  sections:
    - heading: "A shorter checklist than the flight suggests"
      body: |
        A dog or cat arriving from Canada needs a current rabies vaccination, given between 30 days and 12 months before arrival, the standard core vaccinations, and that Veterinary Import Permit. Nothing here resembles a titre test or a fixed waiting period after a blood draw, the kind of requirement that adds months to routes into countries with stricter rabies-risk classifications.

        Apply for the import permit as soon as travel dates firm up. Six months of validity means there's no cost to applying early, and it's one less thing competing for attention against the flight booking and the health certificate timing closer to departure.

    - heading: "Two long-haul options, one unavoidable regional hop"
      body: |
        KLM's routing through Amsterdam and Lufthansa's through Frankfurt or Munich both cover the long-haul distance from a Canadian gateway to Johannesburg. Neither is a short journey, and which one to pick usually comes down to schedule fit rather than any real difference in cargo handling standards.

        From O.R. Tambo, Airlink runs the short regional hop into King Mswati III International Airport several times a day. Book the whole journey as one interline cargo movement rather than separate tickets, and use an agent with a Johannesburg presence to manage the handover between the long-haul carrier and the regional flight.
