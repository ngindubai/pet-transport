  sections:
    - heading: "Two African hub routes compared: Nairobi vs Addis Ababa"
      body: |
        Most cargo routes from the UK to Tanzania transit through an African hub. The two main options are Nairobi on Kenya Airways and Addis Ababa on Ethiopian Airlines. Both are well-established and experienced with live animal cargo.

        Kenya Airways via Nairobi to Dar es Salaam or Kilimanjaro is the shorter African-hub transit. Nairobi Jomo Kenyatta is a busy regional hub with good animal handling facilities. Ethiopian Airlines via Addis Ababa to Dar es Salaam is a reliable alternative: Ethiopian Cargo is one of the continent's largest and handles significant live animal traffic.

        Gulf hub routing (Qatar via Doha) is a viable third option and avoids African hub transit, instead connecting through Doha before the final leg to Tanzania. Journey times are similar across all three options.

    - heading: "Tanzania import permit: apply before you do anything else"
      body: |
        The import permit from Tanzania's Director of Veterinary Services is the first document you need to secure for this move. Without it, your pet cannot enter Tanzania regardless of how correct the UK documentation is. Apply four to six weeks before your intended travel date through the Ministry of Livestock and Fisheries.

        At the same time, contact the Ministry or a local agent to confirm the current quarantine position for UK pets. Tanzania's policies on arrival inspection versus routine quarantine can vary depending on the animal's origin country, vaccination status, and the specific entry point.
