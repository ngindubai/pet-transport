  overview: |
    There are no direct flights from Panama City to the UK, but several reliable European connections make this a well-trodden route for the expat and Canal Zone international community. The journey goes via Madrid, Amsterdam, Frankfurt, or Paris - and the full APHA non-listed country protocol applies on arrival in the UK.

    Plan for a 5-6 month preparation window: microchip, rabies vaccination, titre test (blood drawn 30 days post-vaccination), 3-month wait, tapeworm treatment for dogs, and MIDA health certificate. The step sequence matters and cannot be compressed.

    Panama has decent veterinary infrastructure in Panama City and the Canal Zone, and MIDA (Panama's agricultural ministry) handles export health certification regularly. An IPATA agent with Latin America experience can simplify the MIDA process significantly.
  sections:
    - heading: "What the UK requires from pets arriving from Panama"
      body: |
        Panama is not on the UK's approved country list. The full APHA import protocol applies: ISO microchip implanted before rabies vaccination, current rabies vaccination, titre test result of at least 0.5 IU/ml at an EU/UK approved laboratory (blood drawn 30 days post-vaccination), 3-month wait from blood draw date, tapeworm treatment for dogs 24-120 hours before UK arrival, and a MIDA-endorsed official health certificate valid for 10 days.

        The 3-month wait is calculated from the exact date blood was drawn, not from when results were issued or paid for. Many owners get this calculation wrong by a few days. Check this date precisely and confirm it with your vet and your agent before booking flights.

        Source: UK APHA at gov.uk/bring-pet-to-great-britain.
    - heading: "Routing from Panama City to London: comparing the hubs"
      body: |
        Iberia via Madrid is often the most logical choice. Iberia operates from Panama City (PTY) to Madrid (MAD), and Madrid to London Heathrow or Gatwick. MIDA documentation in Spanish is less of a communication barrier at a Spanish-operated hub. Iberia Cargo handles the pet end-to-end.

        KLM via Amsterdam is a strong alternative. KLM has a consistent track record for pet cargo on Latin America routes, and Schiphol handles pet transits reliably. A small pet may also travel in cabin on the Amsterdam-London leg, which can be a significant comfort advantage for the owner.

        Lufthansa via Frankfurt and Air France via Paris are both worth comparing on price and schedule. All three European carriers have solid pet cargo operations and their hubs handle Latin American transit pets regularly.

        Avoid routing via the United States unless you are prepared for US Customs and Border Protection procedures, which add significant complexity even for animals in transit.
    - heading: "MIDA health certificate: how the Panamanian export process works"
      body: |
        Panama's Ministry of Agricultural Development (MIDA) is the competent authority for animal health exports. Your vet in Panama prepares the health certificate and MIDA endorses it. The certificate is valid for 10 days from the examination date, so it must be obtained within 10 days of departure.

        MIDA offices are primarily in Panama City (Via Espana and La Exposicion). If you are based in the Canal Zone or interior Panama, factor travel time and appointment availability into your planning. Processing typically takes 1-3 working days once your vet has prepared the initial certificate.

        MIDA will need to see: microchip documentation, vaccination records, titre test results from the approved laboratory, and the health certificate prepared by your vet. Bring originals of everything - MIDA does not accept copies.

    answer: "Allow at least 22-26 weeks from starting the process. The sequence is: microchip, vaccination, titre test blood draw (30 days after vaccination), 3-month wait from blood draw date, then health certificate and departure. Starting earlier gives you buffer for any delays in laboratory results or MIDA processing."
  - question: "What is MIDA and how do I get the export health certificate?"
    answer: "MIDA is Panama's Ministry of Agricultural Development, the authority that endorses official veterinary health certificates. Your vet prepares the certificate; MIDA endorses it. The certificate is valid for 10 days. Visit a MIDA office in Panama City with your pet's original records. Processing takes 1-3 working days."

    answer: "Brachycephalic (flat-faced) breed restrictions apply with some carriers, particularly in hot weather months. Iberia, KLM, Lufthansa, and Air France all have breed restriction policies. Check your specific breed against each carrier's list before booking. Summer months (June-August) often trigger additional restrictions due to temperature in cargo holds."
  - question: "Can I take my small dog in the cabin on a flight from Panama to the UK?"
    answer: "On most long-haul Latin America to Europe flights, cabin pets are not accepted. KLM and Lufthansa allow small pets in cabin on the intra-Europe leg (Amsterdam-London or Frankfurt-London), but the transatlantic Panama-Europe leg is cargo only. Check with your carrier before booking."
