  sections:
    - heading: "ICA and the Colombia export health certificate for US travel"
      body: |
        ICA (Instituto Colombiano Agropecuario) is Colombia's agricultural health authority. The health certificate for US import must be issued by an ICA-authorised official vet, which is a distinct qualification from a standard private practice vet, and endorsed by ICA.

        The certificate must include: your pet's microchip number, species and breed, date of birth, vaccination history (including titre test result with laboratory name, blood draw date, result in IU/ml), and owner details. For CDC purposes, the titre test section is particularly important: USDA/CBP inspectors verify that the blood was drawn at least 30 days after the most recent vaccination, the result is 0.5 IU/ml or above, and the result is at least 28 days old at the time of US arrival.

        Contact ICA (ica.gov.co) for the list of authorised vets in Bogota and other Colombian cities.

    - heading: "Avianca direct to the USA: the Bogota advantage"

        Avianca's extensive direct network from Bogota El Dorado Airport (BOG) to the United States is a genuine advantage for this corridor. Direct flights to Miami (MIA), New York JFK, Los Angeles (LAX), and Orlando (MCO) avoid the additional hub transit that complicates many other South American-to-US pet cargo routes.

        Miami and New York JFK both have CDC-registered animal care facilities, making them suitable US entry points for dogs from Colombia under the CDC high-risk rules. Confirm live animal acceptance and booking procedures directly with Avianca Cargo (aviancacargo.com) for your specific routing and travel dates.

        American Airlines via Miami and United Airlines via Houston or Newark are alternatives. Houston IAH and Newark EWR also have CDC-registered facilities.
