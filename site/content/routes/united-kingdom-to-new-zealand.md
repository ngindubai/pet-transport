  sections:
    - heading: "The MPI permit and testing regime"
      body: |
        New Zealand's Ministry for Primary Industries (MPI) issues the import permit that governs the entire process. The permit sets out exactly which tests your pet needs and the timing windows for each.

        For dogs, this typically includes a rabies titre test plus disease screening (for conditions such as leptospirosis and brucellosis), each with its own timing requirements relative to travel. The testing regime is more extensive than most routes, which is why preparation takes several months. Work closely with a vet experienced in New Zealand exports, and follow the permit schedule precisely.
    - heading: "The 10-day quarantine"
      body: |
        Every pet entering New Zealand completes a minimum 10-day quarantine at an MPI-approved facility on arrival. The facility must be pre-booked before departure, and spaces are limited, so book as early as your timeline allows.

        Owners cannot visit during quarantine. After the minimum 10 days (longer if any issue arises), you collect your pet from the facility. All quarantine costs are paid by the owner. Budget NZD 2,000 to NZD 4,000 for the standard stay.
