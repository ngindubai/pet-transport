  h1: "Pet Transport from Barbados to the United Arab Emirates"
  overview: |
    No airline flies Barbados to Dubai or Abu Dhabi without a change of aircraft. Every workable routing goes through London, the one hub both ends of this journey already connect to reliably, with British Airways carrying the first leg and Emirates, Etihad or Qatar Airways picking up the second.

    The regulatory side is lighter than the logistics suggest. Barbados has no export permit to speak of, and the UAE's Ministry of Climate Change and Environment (MOCCAE) may well class Barbados among the roughly 50 countries exempt from the rabies titre test, on the strength of its long-standing rabies-free status. That exemption isn't guaranteed to stay fixed, so it's worth confirming before anything else is booked.

  sections:
    - heading: "Two carriers, one connection point"
      body: |
        British Airways is the only realistic first leg out of Barbados, a non-stop service to Heathrow that already carries pets for IAG Cargo. From there, Emirates SkyCargo reaches Dubai and Etihad Cargo reaches Abu Dhabi; Qatar Airways Cargo via Doha is a workable third option if neither has space.

        Routing through the US, rather than London, is technically possible but rarely worth the added complication: a pet transiting the US on a separate international leg runs into its own set of federal handling requirements, on top of everything Barbados and the UAE already ask for. London keeps the process to one transfer instead of two sets of rules.

    - heading: "What MOCCAE actually asks for"
      body: |
        The import permit itself is straightforward: AED 200, applied for online, usually approved within a working day, valid for 90 days. It sits alongside a health certificate that has to be issued within 10 days of arrival and a vaccination record covering rabies plus the standard core vaccines for the species.

        The titre test is the one open question. MOCCAE keeps its own list of roughly 50 low-risk countries exempt from the blood test, and Barbados's rabies-free status is the kind of profile that typically earns a spot on it. Several relocation guides currently list Barbados as exempt. Because that list is an administrative decision, not a law, it's worth a direct check with MOCCAE's own portal rather than betting a travel date on a secondary source.

    - heading: "Barbados's side of the paperwork"
      body: |
        Getting a pet off the island is the easy half. A Veterinary Services vet examines the animal, checks the vaccination record, and issues the export certificate for BDS $30.00, generally within a week of the flight. There's no Barbados-specific import permit to chase on the way out, unlike the reverse direction.

        One thing does connect the two ends: MOCCAE's own application process asks for a copy of the UAE import permit to be attached to the pet's paperwork. That permit needs to be in hand well before the Barbados export exam, not applied for afterwards.
