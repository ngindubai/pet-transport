  overview: "Moving a pet from Armenia to Latvia requires careful planning over a minimum of five to six months.   sections:
    - heading: "Why the 90-day wait applies and what it means for planning"
      body: |
        Latvia, as an EU member state, applies the EU pet travel framework for animals arriving from unlisted third countries. Armenia is classified as an EU unlisted country, which means your pet must complete the FAVN titre test process before entering Latvia.

        The sequence is: chip implanted first, then primary rabies vaccination, then at least 30 days later a blood draw for the FAVN titre test at an EU-approved laboratory. If the result is 0.5 IU/ml or above, the 90-day wait begins from the blood draw date. Travel to Latvia cannot happen until at least day 91.

        The 90-day wait is calculated from when the blood was drawn, not when the results came back. If you draw blood and the results take two weeks, those two weeks count as part of your 90-day wait. Starting the process as early as possible after you know you are moving is the right approach.

    - heading: "What happens at Riga International Airport"
      body: |
        Riga International Airport (RIX) has border veterinary inspection for live animal cargo arriving from non-EU origins. The Partikas un veterinarais dienests (PVD) oversees these controls. Border vets at RIX will check: the SSFS-endorsed health certificate (issued within 10 days of travel), the FAVN titre test result, the vaccination record, and the microchip.

        The chip must be scannable and the number must match every document precisely. If the titre test shows a successful result but the 90-day wait is incomplete, entry is refused. Latvian border vets apply EU rules strictly. There is no flexibility for incomplete documentation or timing.

    - heading: "Routing from Yerevan to Riga"
      body: |
        There is no direct cargo service from Yerevan Zvartnots (EVN) to Riga (RIX). The standard routing uses Turkish Airlines Cargo via Istanbul Ataturk (IST), where live animals are handled, then connects to RIX. Turkish Airlines operates directly between IST and RIX, making this a clean two-leg routing.

        Alternative routings go via Paris CDG with Air France Cargo, or via Amsterdam AMS with KLM Cargo. From CDG or AMS, airBaltic and other carriers connect to Riga. Route choice often depends on cargo capacity availability on the specific dates you need. Book during the 90-day wait and confirm live animal acceptance on every segment in writing.

  - question: "What is the FAVN titre test and where can I get it done in Armenia?"
    answer: "The FAVN (fluorescent antibody virus neutralisation) test measures your pet's rabies antibody levels. Blood must be drawn by a licensed vet in Armenia and sent to an EU-approved laboratory (laboratories in France, Germany, and the UK are commonly used from Armenia). The blood draw must happen at least 30 days after the primary rabies vaccination. If the result is 0.5 IU/ml or above, the 90-day wait begins from the blood draw date."
  - question: "Can my pet enter Latvia via a land border from another EU country?"
    answer: "EU-internal land borders between member states are open for pet movements using an EU pet passport or AHC. However, your Armenian pet needs to have completed the full unlisted country process before entering any EU member state for the first time. Simply driving from Lithuania to Latvia after flying into Vilnius does not bypass the requirements."
  - question: "What EU-approved laboratories can handle Armenian pet blood samples?"
    answer: "Several EU-approved laboratories accept samples from non-EU countries. In practice, blood drawn in Armenia is often shipped to laboratories in France (such as Laboratoire National de Reference de la Rage), the Netherlands, or Germany. Your Armenian vet and transport agent can recommend which laboratory is most practical and reliable for Yerevan-origin samples."
