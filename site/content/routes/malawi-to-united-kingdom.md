  h1: "Pet Transport from Malawi to the United Kingdom"
  overview: |
    Malawi doesn't appear on either of Great Britain's listed-country tables, which puts it in the same unlisted bracket as most of the African continent. A pet needs a rabies titre test and a fixed three-month wait from the blood draw date, full stop, however early the rest of the paperwork is sorted.

    Malawi's own side of the process is oddly asymmetric. The requirements for bringing a pet into Malawi are published in reasonable detail: an import permit from the Director of Animal Health, valid six months, with an exemption for pets arriving from SADC member states. The requirements for a pet leaving Malawi aren't published with the same clarity in English. That's a genuine gap worth knowing about before you start.

    Budget four and a half to five and a half months from a standing start, and confirm the export side directly with Malawi's Department of Animal Health and Livestock Development rather than assuming it mirrors the import process.

  sections:
    - heading: "Unlisted status: the titre test and the fixed three-month clock"
      body: |
        Great Britain's rules don't care how healthy a pet is or how experienced the vet is. Malawi's unlisted status means every pet, dog or cat, needs a blood test confirming the rabies vaccine worked, drawn at least 30 days after vaccination, followed by a flat three-month wait from the draw date before travel.

        That wait doesn't start when the lab emails a result. It starts the day the needle goes in. Plan backwards: vaccination, then a minimum 30-day gap, then the blood draw, then three full calendar months. Only after all of that can the health certificate and flight booking stage begin.
    - heading: "Why Malawi's export paperwork is harder to pin down than its import rules"
      body: |
        Malawi's Department of Animal Health and Livestock Development (DAHLD), under the Ministry of Agriculture, is the authority responsible for animal health, and its import permit process for pets entering the country is documented clearly: apply to the Director of Animal Health, get a permit valid for six months, skip the permit entirely if arriving from a SADC member state.

        None of that tells you what's required to export a pet from Malawi. In practice, most agents handling this route rely on a standard veterinary health certificate signed by a Malawi-registered vet, but there's no published fixed fee or turnaround time in English for the export side. Contact DAHLD directly and build a buffer into the timeline rather than assuming a fast turnaround.
