  overview: |
    Two airlines fly this route direct: FlyDubai and Azerbaijan Airlines both operate Dubai-Baku cargo services without a connection, which puts this corridor among the easier UAE-outbound journeys in this build for a pet owner to plan around.

    Every pet leaving the UAE goes through the same MOCCAE export process regardless of destination. Azerbaijan's side adds a permit from the State Veterinary Service and an arrival check at Heydar Aliyev International, with no titre test and no quarantine for a compliant pet.

  sections:
    - heading: "Getting your pet out of the UAE"
      body: |
        A MOCCAE export health certificate is required for every pet leaving the UAE, whatever the destination. It begins with an online application through the MOCCAE portal, requiring a UAE Pass account, followed by an in-person exam at an approved clinic in Dubai or Abu Dhabi. A vet there confirms the vaccination record and general health before endorsing the certificate. A final check at the airport happens just before the flight. The certificate is valid for 30 days once granted.

    - heading: "Azerbaijan's State Veterinary Service permit"
      body: |
        Azerbaijan requires a veterinary import permit from the State Veterinary Service, under the Ministry of Agriculture, before your pet arrives. Apply well ahead of travel; this isn't something arranged at the airport in Baku. Once the permit and MOCCAE-endorsed health certificate are both in hand, there's no titre test requirement and no quarantine for a pet arriving from the UAE.

    - heading: "Two direct carriers on one route"
      body: |
        FlyDubai and Azerbaijan Airlines (AZAL) both fly non-stop between Dubai and Baku, giving owners a genuine choice of direct cargo options rather than a single carrier to depend on. AZAL flying into its own home hub at Heydar Aliyev International tends to make the arrival end of the journey particularly smooth.
