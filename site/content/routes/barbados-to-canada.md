  h1: "Pet Transport from Barbados to Canada"
  overview: |
    Barbados never made CFIA's high-risk-for-dog-rabies list, the list last updated 13 March 2025 that puts Belize, Botswana and Guyana pets through a tougher entry process. That single absence keeps this route in Canada's default, lighter track: a standard vaccination certificate, no titre test, no import permit, no restriction on which airport the pet lands at.

    The flight side matches the paperwork for ease. Air Canada and WestJet share a nonstop Toronto to Bridgetown route, running as often as seven times a week between them, so there's no need to connect through a third country to get a pet across.

  sections:
    - heading: "Why Barbados isn't on CFIA's high-risk track"
      body: |
        CFIA sorts pet-import origins into three practical groups: the nine countries it recognises as rabies-free, the countries it flags as high-risk for dog rabies, and everywhere else. Barbados sits in that everyday third group, the same one Trinidad and Tobago and Jamaica fall into.

        That means no serology test, no fixed waiting period once the vaccine is given, and no CFIA import permit for a personal move of one or two animals. It also means Barbados pets skip the 7-month age floor and the five-airport restriction that apply once a country lands on the high-risk list, a real difference from three of its Caribbean and African neighbours covered elsewhere on this site.

    - heading: "Getting the Barbados paperwork right before you fly"
      body: |
        The export certificate itself doesn't take long. A vet at Barbados's Veterinary Services office examines the animal, checks the vaccination record, and issues the certificate for BDS $30.00 in cash. There's no separate export permit sitting alongside it.

        Timing is the one thing to watch closely. The exam has to happen within 7 days of departure, so book it against a real flight date, not a rough guess. Get that wrong and the certificate can lapse before the pet actually flies.

    - heading: "Two carriers, one nonstop route"
      body: |
        Air Canada and WestJet both fly Grantley Adams (BGI) to Toronto Pearson without a change of aircraft, a genuinely convenient setup for a live animal booking. Between them the two airlines can offer close to daily departures in peak season, though schedules and equipment shift through the year, so confirm cargo space on your specific date rather than assuming year-round frequency.
