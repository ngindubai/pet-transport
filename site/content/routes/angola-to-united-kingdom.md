  h1: "Pet Transport from Angola to United Kingdom"
  overview: |
    Angola is not on the UK's Part 1 or Part 2 list of rabies-listed countries. That single fact drives most of what makes this route harder than it looks: a mandatory rabies titre test, a 90-day wait after the blood draw, and no direct flight to shorten the journey.

    There is no listed-country shortcut here. Every pet leaving Luanda for the UK needs the full unlisted-country pathway: microchip, vaccination, a blood draw at an APHA-approved lab, then three months of waiting before travel is even possible. Add in indirect routing through Lisbon or Frankfurt and a genuinely limited local pet transport industry, and this becomes one of the longer-lead routes on the continent.

    Plan for 18 to 24 weeks. Some of that time cannot be compressed, no matter how good your agent is.
  sections:
    - heading: "Why the titre test cannot be skipped"
      body: |
        The UK sorts every non-EU country into one of three buckets: Part 1 listed, Part 2 listed, or unlisted. Angola sits in the third bucket. Part 1 and Part 2 countries get a simpler paperwork trail; unlisted countries do not, regardless of how the country's actual rabies situation looks on the ground.

        That means the blood sample, drawn at least 30 days after the rabies vaccination and tested to a minimum result of 0.5 IU/ml, is compulsory. So is the 90-day wait that follows, counted from the day the blood was drawn, not the day the lab confirms the result. Miss the drawing window and you are not just delayed, you are back to square one on that step.

    - heading: "Getting to London without a direct flight"
      body: |
        No airline flies Luanda to London nonstop. TAP Air Portugal's Lisbon service is the most frequently used connection for this corridor, largely because TAAG Angola Airlines already flies the Luanda-Lisbon leg daily and Lisbon has good onward links to Heathrow and Gatwick. Lufthansa via Frankfurt is a solid second option, and Frankfurt's dedicated Animal Lounge is genuinely useful on a long journey with a layover.

        One routing detail matters: Lufthansa can only carry a cargo pet through Frankfurt, not Munich, on a connecting itinerary. Get this wrong at booking stage and you will need to rebook. Whichever hub you choose, confirm actual pet cargo space, not just seat or crate availability, since this is not a high-volume pet route and space can be tighter than the passenger booking suggests.

Some neighbouring or comparable countries are listed and skip this step; Angola currently is not."
