  h1: "Pet Transport from the United Kingdom to Malawi"
  overview: |
    No blood test stands between a UK pet and entry to Malawi. That single fact makes this route noticeably calmer than the trip in reverse, where Malawi's unlisted status under GB rules forces a rabies titre test and a fixed three-month wait. Here, a current rabies vaccination and a permit from Malawi's animal health authority do the job.

    The permit is the part most owners underestimate. It comes from the Director of Animal Health at Malawi's Department of Animal Health and Livestock Development (DAHLD), and unlike several of Malawi's regional neighbours, the UK gets no exemption from applying for one. Start that conversation early: no fixed processing time is published, so a wide buffer matters more than a perfectly optimised vet schedule.

    Budget ten to fourteen weeks from a standing start, most of it spent waiting on the permit rather than the veterinary side of things.

  sections:
    - heading: "Why no titre test is needed, and what to check instead"
      body: |
        Malawi doesn't operate the kind of listed-country system GB uses. It simply asks for a current rabies vaccination, given between 30 days and 12 months before arrival, and doesn't ask for antibody proof from any origin country, the UK included. A UK-vaccinated pet meets that condition without a laboratory involved.

        That's worth sitting with for a moment, because the reverse trip looks completely different. A Malawi-origin pet entering GB needs a titre test and a fixed three-month wait purely because Malawi sits outside GB's approved country tables. The same animal, travelling the other way, faces no equivalent hurdle. Confirm the current position with DAHLD before booking anything, since guidance summarised by third parties can drift out of date faster than an official source would.

    - heading: "The DAHLD import permit: start this before anything else"
      body: |
        Malawi requires an import permit from the Director of Animal Health for any pet entering the country, unless the point of departure is a fellow SADC member state: Botswana, Lesotho, Namibia, South Africa, Eswatini or Zimbabwe. The UK doesn't qualify, so the full application applies regardless of how straightforward the rest of the paperwork looks.

        Once issued, the permit is valid for six months, which gives some room either side of the actual travel date. The catch is that no processing timeline is published in English, so DAHLD contact should happen ten to twelve weeks out, not six to eight. A pet transport agent with recent Malawi experience is worth the fee here, mainly for knowing who to call when an application goes quiet.

    - heading: "Getting from Heathrow to Lilongwe"
      body: |
        Nothing flies this route non-stop. Kenya Airways connects through Nairobi on a long-haul plus regional combination that's close to daily on the Nairobi-Lilongwe leg. Ethiopian Airlines runs the same shape of journey through Addis Ababa. British Airways gets a pet to Johannesburg, where South African Airways picks up the final short hop into Lilongwe.

        All three are workable. None is a single ticket with one carrier's name on it from start to finish, so confirm through-checking of a live animal booking across the connection before paying for anything.
