  overview: |
    The blanket ban on dog imports into the Maldives makes this one of the shortest possible answers on the whole site for a dog owner: it cannot be done through a standard process. The Maldives Customs Service lists dogs among prohibited import items, and there is no titre-test-and-wait workaround the way there would be almost anywhere else.

    For a cat, it is a normal, if slightly long-haul, move. A USDA-endorsed export certificate, a rabies and feline vaccine schedule started at least 30 days out, and a health certificate signed within 10 days of travel cover the documented requirements. The real cost driver is the flight itself: nothing goes direct from the US to Male, so every booking runs through a Gulf hub.

  sections:
    - heading: "Why we lead with the dog ban"
      body: |
        Enough American owners relocating for work in the Maldives assume that a rabies-free country of origin and a full set of vaccination records will get a dog through. It will not. The prohibition applies regardless of where the dog came from or how well documented its health history is. If a dog is part of your move, that needs direct confirmation with Maldivian authorities before you make any other plans, not a standard shipping quote.
    - heading: "The USDA side for a cat"
      body: |
        A USDA-accredited vet issues the export health certificate, which then gets endorsed electronically through the APHIS eFile system for a fee of roughly USD 38. This is a straightforward, well-worn process on the US side. The vaccination timing is what sets the calendar: rabies at least 30 days before arrival in the Maldives, and the standard feline vaccines alongside it.
    - heading: "What is thin on the Maldives side"
      body: |
        Import permit or no import permit, titre test or none, are questions where published sources genuinely disagree. We are not going to invent a confident answer where one does not exist. Confirm the current position with your vet, agent, or the Maldives Embassy before assuming either way.
    - heading: "Routing and cost"
      body: |
        Doha, Dubai and Abu Dhabi are the three realistic connection points from a major US gateway, and all three carriers, Qatar Airways, Emirates and Etihad, run live-animal cargo operations through their hub. Compare current heat-related restrictions as well as price, since a booking during peak summer can get bumped for reasons that have nothing to do with your paperwork.
