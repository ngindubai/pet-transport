  sections:
    - heading: "Documents and Entry Requirements for Sweden"
      body: |
        Sweden is an EU member state and follows EU Regulation 576/2013 for non-commercial pet movements. Pets arriving from Slovenia need a valid EU pet passport showing an ISO 11784/11785 microchip implanted before or on the same day as the first rabies vaccination, and a current rabies vaccination that is at least 21 days old after a primary dose. No titre test is required because Slovenia is a listed EU country. No import permit is needed. Jordbruksverket (the Swedish Board of Agriculture) oversees entry; their guidance is at jordbruksverket.se. Your Slovenian vet will complete and sign the EU pet passport before travel.

    - heading: "Flights and Routing from Ljubljana"
      body: |
        Ljubljana (LJU) does not have a direct service to Stockholm Arlanda (ARN). The two most practical routing options for cargo are Lufthansa via Frankfurt (FRA) and KLM via Amsterdam (AMS). Both carriers have established live animal cargo facilities at their respective hubs. Lufthansa Cargo at FRA is one of the busiest live animal transit points in Europe. For small pets travelling in-cabin, Wizz Air operates budget routes from Ljubljana but does not accept pets in hold. Whichever route you choose, book early: summer is busy and cargo space for live animals is allocated separately from passenger baggage.

faqs:
  - question: "Does my pet need a titre test to enter Sweden from Slovenia?"
    answer: "No. A titre test is only required when travelling from a country that is not on the EU listed countries register. Slovenia is an EU member state, so the standard EU pet passport with a current rabies vaccination is sufficient. Jordbruksverket confirms this at jordbruksverket.se."
  - question: "Can I take my dog in the cabin on a flight from Ljubljana to Stockholm?"
    answer: "Only if your dog is small enough to fit in an approved in-cabin carrier under the seat. Most carriers operating out of Ljubljana (LJU) apply a combined weight limit of around 8 kg including the bag. Larger dogs must travel as cargo or in the aircraft hold. Wizz Air offers cabin-only pet transport from Ljubljana but does not provide a hold option."
  - question: "How far in advance should I book cargo for a pet on this route?"
    answer: "For cargo via Frankfurt or Amsterdam, aim to book at least 8 to 12 weeks in advance, particularly between May and September. Ljubljana Airport has limited cargo capacity for live animals and hub slots fill quickly. A pet transport agent familiar with LJU cargo can help secure space and manage the transit documentation at the hub."
  - question: "What crate size does my dog need for the flight?"
    answer: "Your dog needs an IATA-compliant travel crate (compliant with IATA Live Animal Regulations). The dog should be able to stand, turn around, and lie down naturally. Measure your dog standing and add 10 cm to the height, and measure from nose to tail base and add 10 cm for the length. Check your specific crate dimensions against the airline's cargo acceptance table before purchase."
