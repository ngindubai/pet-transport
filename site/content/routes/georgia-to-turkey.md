content:
  h1: "Pet Transport from Georgia to Turkey"
  overview: |
    Most routes in this build only offer a flight. Georgia to Turkey offers a road as well. The two countries share a border at Sarpi, just south of Batumi, and owners already driving that direction can bring a pet the same way, no cargo desk required.

    The paperwork doesn't change based on how the pet travels. Turkey's titre test rule, and the 90-day wait that comes with it, applies identically whether the pet flies into Istanbul Airport or crosses at Sarpi in the back of a car.

  sections:
    - heading: "Flying via Tbilisi, or driving via Sarpi"
      body: |
        Turkish Airlines and Georgian Airways both fly Tbilisi to Istanbul direct, giving this route two carrier options rather than the single default most corridors in this build rely on. But the land option is worth knowing about even if you don't plan to use it: the Sarpi border crossing near Batumi connects Georgia directly to Turkey's Black Sea coast, and the drive from Tbilisi to Istanbul via that crossing runs roughly 6 to 7 hours once you're past the border itself.

        For owners relocating overland anyway, bringing the pet in the car removes the cargo booking, the crate purchase, and the airline handling fee entirely. The trade-off is a long drive with an animal that may find the car easier or harder to tolerate than a flight, depending on the individual pet.

    - heading: "The titre test applies the same way either route"
      body: |
        Whichever way the pet travels, Turkey's entry rule doesn't change. A FAVN titre test is required, blood drawn at least 30 days after vaccination, a result of at least 0.5 IU/ml, and a 90-day wait counted from that draw date. Georgia's National Food Agency doesn't require a separate export permit, only its endorsement of the health certificate itself, which needs to follow Turkey's own certificate format rather than the EU-style document used for Georgia's European routes.

        A pet arriving without a valid test faces 21 days of quarantine at the owner's expense, at the airport or at the land border alike.
