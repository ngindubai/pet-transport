  h1: "Pet Transport from Australia to Brunei: 2026 Guide"
  overview: |
    Brunei keeps a short list of five scheduled countries exempt from its titre test and the worst of its quarantine rules, and Australia is on it, alongside Ireland, New Zealand, Singapore and the UK. That's a genuine advantage over most of the routes covered elsewhere in this series, provided the pet has actually lived continuously in Australia for at least six months.

    DAA still checks eligibility case by case rather than granting it automatically, so the paperwork trail proving residency matters as much as the biology. With that confirmed, this route can realistically run 6 to 10 weeks.

  sections:
    - heading: "What scheduled-country status actually requires"
      body: |
        Being an Australian pet on paper isn't quite the same as qualifying for Brunei's lighter pathway. DAA's scheduled-country treatment depends on the animal having been born in Australia or continuously resident there for at least six months before travel, and DAA reviews the supporting evidence itself rather than accepting the claim at face value. Build that evidence, veterinary records, registration history, proof of address, into the file from the start rather than assembling it at the last minute.

        Get that right, and the titre test and facility quarantine that weigh down most of this build's other Brunei-bound routes generally don't apply.

    - heading: "Australia's own export process"
      body: |
        DAFF's role on the export side is narrower than it might first appear: an ordinary state-registered vet, not a special DAFF-accredited one, prepares the animal, while DAFF itself reviews the paperwork against Brunei's published requirements and issues the actual export permit and health certificate. That permit is tightly timed, valid for only 72 hours after issue, with the final vet check required within 72 hours of departure, so the export side of this move needs scheduling precision even when the destination-side requirements are relatively light.
