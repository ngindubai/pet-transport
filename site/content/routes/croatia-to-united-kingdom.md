  sections:
    - heading: "What the 24-120 hour tapeworm window means in practice"
      body: |
        The tapeworm treatment rule is the most time-sensitive part of any EU-to-UK pet move. For a direct Croatia Airlines flight from Zagreb to London (around 2.5 hours), the practical planning is: note your expected GB arrival time, then book a vet appointment that falls within 24-120 hours before that time.

        Say you land at Heathrow at 1pm on a Friday. The treatment window opens at 1pm on Wednesday (120 hours earlier) and closes at 1pm on Thursday (24 hours before). Your vet must administer Praziquantel and record the exact date and time in the AHC.

        Get the AHC issued on the same day as the tapeworm treatment if possible, or at least in the same visit. The AHC is valid for 10 days from issue, so there is no need to rush the certificate itself. The constraint is the tapeworm timing window, which is tighter.

    - heading: "Croatia Airlines cargo vs alternative hub routing"
      body: |
        Croatia Airlines offers the most direct routing for cargo pets from Zagreb to London. A single carrier, a direct flight, and no intermediate handling keeps the journey as simple as it can be from this origin.

        British Airways World Cargo is a second option on the same ZAG-LHR route. For cargo-only routes where the pet needs dedicated cargo handling, BA is a reliable alternative.

        Ryanair flies Zagreb to UK airports but does not accept pets in any form. If you want in-cabin travel for a small pet, the Croatia Airlines option is the one to check with the airline directly.
