  sections:
    - heading: "Pacific routing vs westbound European routing from Jakarta to Mexico City"
      body: |
        Jakarta to Mexico City (CGK to MEX) has no direct option and requires at least two connections. Two main routing approaches exist.

        Pacific routing goes CGK to Singapore (SIN), then across the Pacific to Los Angeles (LAX), then to Mexico City (MEX). Singapore Airlines Cargo handles the first leg well and LAX is a significant cargo hub with live animal facilities. Transit times on this routing depend on connection schedules and cargo booking.

        Westbound routing via Europe goes CGK to Amsterdam (AMS), Paris (CDG), or Frankfurt (FRA), then MEX. KLM, Air France, and Lufthansa Cargo all operate the Jakarta to European hub leg and have Mexico City connections. European hub airports have well-established live animal handling, which is relevant for a long transit.

        Both approaches work. The choice depends on available cargo space on your travel dates, airline live animal acceptance on the specific segments, and which transit hub offers the shortest overall connection time for your pet.

    - heading: "Indonesia to Mexico vs Indonesia to Brazil: comparing Latin American destinations"
      body: |
        Both Mexico and Brazil are long-haul destinations from Indonesia, but the arrival process differs considerably.

        Mexico has no advance permit requirement and no quarantine. SENASICA inspects on arrival and, if documentation is in order, your pet proceeds immediately. Preparation time is primarily set by the BARANTAN export process, typically 4-8 weeks.

        Brazil requires a MAPA import permit before travel and a 30-day facility quarantine at Guarulhos (GRU) on arrival. Preparation time is 10-16 weeks, and the quarantine adds both time and cost after arrival.

        For owners choosing between these two destinations, Mexico's arrival process is considerably more straightforward.
