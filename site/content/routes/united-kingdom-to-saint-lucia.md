  overview: |
    Certificate 3100 is the specific APHA document that gets a cat or dog out of Great Britain and into Saint Lucia, applied for online and completed by a registered Official Veterinarian. On the other end, the Veterinary and Livestock Services Division in Castries runs a comparatively light import system: no titre test, no quarantine for a pet that arrives with the right paperwork.

    The one hard stop on this route is breed, not process. Saint Lucia bans several bull-type breeds outright on entry, so a compliant certificate and vaccination record count for nothing if the breed itself isn't permitted. Check that first, before any of the other planning steps.

    Getting there means British Airways, currently the only nonstop option between the UK and Saint Lucia, on a schedule that's lighter outside the winter season before becoming daily from late October 2026.

  sections:
    - heading: "What certificate 3100 covers, and what it doesn't"
      body: |
        This is the export health certificate specific to cats and dogs travelling from Great Britain to Saint Lucia, distinct from the certificate used for other Caribbean destinations. An Official Veterinarian registered with APHA has to examine the animal and complete the form against the current guidance notes, checked before applying since Saint Lucia's own conditions can change.

        What the certificate doesn't do is override Saint Lucia's breed restrictions. Pit Bull Terrier, Bull Terrier, American Staffordshire Terrier, Staffordshire Bull Terrier, British Staffordshire Terrier, Japanese Tosa Inu, Dogo Argentino and Fila Brasileiro are all barred from entry regardless of paperwork. There is no certificate that gets a banned breed into the country.

    - heading: "A lighter import process than most Caribbean routes"
      body: |
        Saint Lucia's Veterinary and Livestock Services Division doesn't require a rabies titre test for a UK-vaccinated pet, and there's no routine quarantine for one arriving with a valid import permit. The permit itself needs at least 2 weeks lead time and roughly 2 business days to process once submitted, considerably shorter than the six-to-eight-week windows common on other new routes this site has covered.

        Entry has to happen through Hewanorra International Airport specifically. George F. L. Charles, Saint Lucia's smaller airport near Castries, isn't a compliant entry point for an internationally travelling pet.

    - heading: "One direct flight, and what to do if it doesn't fit"
      body: |
        British Airways flies Gatwick to Hewanorra nonstop, currently on a lighter schedule outside the winter season, before becoming a daily service from 25 October 2026. If those dates don't work, connecting through Antigua or Barbados on a regional carrier such as LIAT, both islands also served from the UK by British Airways, is the realistic fallback, though it adds a leg and a second cargo handling process.
