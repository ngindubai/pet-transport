  sections:
    - heading: "Jamaica's listed status: no titre test for the Czech Republic"
      body: |
        EU Implementing Regulation 2026/636 classifies Jamaica as a listed third country. Pets from Jamaica entering EU member states including the Czech Republic are exempt from the FAVN titre test and the 90-day waiting period. The SVA (State Veterinary Administration) still requires the standard EU-format non-commercial health certificate issued by a VSD-registered official veterinarian and endorsed by VSD.

        The practical preparation: confirm microchip, update vaccinations (including DHLPP as required by VSD), obtain the VSD export permit 4-6 weeks before travel, book cargo to PRG via a connecting hub, and complete the health certificate appointment within 10 days of departure. The SVA will check the certificate and chip number on arrival at Prague.

    - heading: "Routing to Prague and the SVA entry process"
      body: |
        No direct flight connects Jamaica to Prague. The most practical routings involve London Heathrow (British Airways to LHR, then connection to PRG), Frankfurt (connections via Lufthansa), or Amsterdam (KLM via AMS to PRG). Prague Václav Havel Airport (PRG) is the primary Border Control Post for non-EU pet arrivals in the Czech Republic.

        The SVA (Státní veterinární správa) inspectors at PRG conduct documentary and identity checks. They verify the health certificate, VSD export permit, and chip number. A mismatch between the chip number in the certificate and the chip physically in the animal is the most common document issue: ask your Jamaican vet to scan and confirm the chip number before completing the certificate.
