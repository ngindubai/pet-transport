  sections:
    - heading: "The Regulatory Picture for Jamaica to EU Routes"
      body: |
        EU Regulation (EU) No 576/2013, as updated by Commission Implementing Regulation (EU) 2024/1130, divides non-EU countries into listed and unlisted categories for pet import purposes. Listed countries are those where the rabies situation is considered sufficiently controlled that the titre test pathway can be bypassed. Jamaica is on that list, in Group 2.

        For pets travelling from unlisted countries, the process is significantly more involved: a FAVN rabies antibody test is required, the blood must be drawn at least 30 days after vaccination, and the pet must then wait a further 90 days from the date of the blood draw before entering the EU. That totals roughly six months of preparation.

        Jamaica sidesteps all of that. Your preparation checklist is microchip, rabies vaccination with 21-day wait, and health certificate. This is worth emphasising because some resources online still treat Jamaica as unlisted, particularly older guides that were not updated after Implementing Regulation (EU) 2024/1130 came into force.

    - heading: "Czech Republic Entry Requirements"
      body: |
        The Czech Republic's import rules for companion animals are administered by the State Veterinary Administration (SVS, Státní veterinární správa, svscr.cz). As an EU member state, the Czech Republic applies EU non-commercial pet movement rules directly.

        The requirements for a pet arriving from Jamaica are:

        Microchip: ISO 11784/11785 compliant transponder, implanted before the rabies vaccination date. This sequencing is checked carefully by SVS officials.

        Rabies vaccination: Valid, post-microchip, with at least 21 days elapsed since the primary vaccination. Boosters given within the validity period of the previous dose are treated as continuous.

        Health certificate: EU Annex IV format, signed by a VSD-authorised veterinarian in Jamaica and endorsed by the VSD. It must be issued within 10 days of travel. Older certificates are not accepted.

        No quarantine applies to compliant arrivals. Veterinary checks take place at Prague (PRG) on arrival.

    - heading: "Getting Your Pet from Jamaica to Prague"
      body: |
        There is no direct cargo service from Jamaica to the Czech Republic. The standard routing is a two-leg journey: Jamaica to a European hub (London Heathrow or Amsterdam most commonly), then onwards to Prague.

        British Airways World Cargo operates the KIN to LHR route and has an established live animal handling process. From LHR, a second cargo booking to Prague Václav Havel Airport (PRG) is arranged with British Airways, Lufthansa, or Czech Airlines. The layover time at LHR for the cargo transfer needs to be sufficient for handling and documentation checks, typically a minimum of four hours.

        KLM Royal Dutch Airlines offers a competitive alternative via Amsterdam (AMS). KLM Cargo carries live animals on the transatlantic sector, and AMS has an excellent live animal facility. The AMS to PRG sector is short and can be arranged with KLM or a partner carrier.

        Routing via Frankfurt is also possible, typically using Lufthansa Cargo on the European leg. Frankfurt is one of Europe's largest live animal cargo hubs and connects to PRG frequently.

    - heading: "Preparing the Health Certificate"
      body: |
        The health certificate is the document that creates the most problems when it is handled incorrectly. Two rules matter above all others.

        First, the microchip number on the certificate must match exactly the number scanned from your pet. Any discrepancy, even a single digit, results in the animal being held at the border.

        Second, the certificate must be signed by a VSD-authorised veterinarian and then endorsed by the VSD. The vet's signature alone is not sufficient. Factor in the time needed to get the VSD endorsement when planning the 10-day window.

        The Czech SVS will also check that the vaccination details on the certificate are consistent with any vaccination booklet or passport accompanying the animal. Bring all veterinary documentation you have, even if it is not strictly required by the template.

    - heading: "Costs to Budget For"
      body: |
        Pet transport from Jamaica to the Czech Republic involves several distinct cost components. The largest single item is usually the transatlantic cargo fee, which is calculated on the combined weight of the animal and the crate. A medium-sized dog in an appropriate crate might total 30-45 kg, which puts the cargo fee in the EUR 650-1,300 range for the Jamaica to LHR or AMS sector. The second leg to PRG adds EUR 150-400.

        The IATA-approved crate itself costs EUR 80-250 depending on size and manufacturer. You should buy this well in advance and allow your pet to spend time in it before travel, as familiarity with the crate reduces stress significantly.

        VSD documentation fees in Jamaica are modest (around USD 50-100). Czech import inspection fees are payable on arrival and vary by animal size.

    answer: "No, it is not correct. Jamaica is listed as an EU Group 2 country under Implementing Regulation (EU) 2024/1130, which means the FAVN titre test is not required. Older sources written before this regulation came into force may incorrectly classify Jamaica as an unlisted country. The current position is clear: no titre test needed from Jamaica to any EU member state including the Czech Republic."
  - question: "Which airport in the Czech Republic will my pet arrive at?"
    answer: "For air cargo from Jamaica, Prague Václav Havel Airport (PRG) is the standard destination. Brno Airport handles very limited cargo. Ensure the airway bill and all documentation specify PRG and that the live animal shipment is pre-notified to the cargo terminal."
  - question: "How many pets can travel on the same health certificate?"
    answer: "The EU health certificate for non-commercial movement covers up to five pets per owner. If you are moving more than five animals, a second certificate is required. Each pet must be individually identified by microchip number on the certificate."
