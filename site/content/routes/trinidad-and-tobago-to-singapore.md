  h1: "Pet Transport from Trinidad and Tobago to Singapore: 2026 Guide"
  overview: |
    Every pet leaving Piarco starts this journey the same way, on British Airways, since it's the only airline flying nonstop out of the island. That single fact routes this whole move through London, whatever the final destination happens to be.

    From London, Singapore Airlines covers the long-haul leg to Changi, where Trinidad and Tobago's absence from AVS's shorter Schedule I and Schedule II lists means the fuller Schedule III process applies: a titre test, a 90-day wait, and 30 days at the Animal Quarantine Centre once the pet lands.

  sections:
    - heading: "One island, one airline, one route through London"
      body: |
        Caribbean Airlines ended its only European service, to London Gatwick, back in January 2016 and has not resumed it. That leaves British Airways as the sole nonstop option between Piarco and anywhere outside the Americas, and every pet moving from Trinidad and Tobago to Singapore has to travel through that same London gateway.

        British Airways routes live animal cargo through IAG Cargo rather than the ordinary passenger check-in desk, with its own facility and a check-in window roughly four hours before departure. From Heathrow, Singapore Airlines takes over for the long-haul sector to Changi, a well-established leg with strong live-animal infrastructure on the Singapore end.

    - heading: "Why Trinidad and Tobago sits in Schedule III"
      body: |
        AVS names only four Schedule I countries and around 31 Schedule II countries. Trinidad and Tobago appears on neither, so it defaults to Schedule III, the tier that covers most of the Caribbean, Africa, Latin America and much of Asia. That default brings a WOAH-prescribed rabies titre test, blood drawn at least 28 days after vaccination, and a fixed 90-day wait from that blood draw before the pet can travel.

        Run the AVS import licence application and the AQC quarantine booking during this wait rather than after it finishes, since both can take weeks to confirm on their own.

    - heading: "The London connection adds a step Trinidad's other routes don't need"
      body: |
        A pet moving directly from Trinidad and Tobago to the UK only deals with British Airways and IAG Cargo. Adding Singapore as the final destination means a second cargo handover at Heathrow, from IAG Cargo to Singapore Airlines, before the long-haul sector even begins. Confirm live animal cargo space on both legs separately well ahead of the travel date, since a confirmed booking on one leg does not guarantee space on the other.
