  h1: "Pet Transport from the United Kingdom to India"
  overview: |
    India imposes a mandatory quarantine period on all imported pets, regardless of origin country. For pets arriving with complete documentation and appearing healthy on arrival, the quarantine is 15 days at an AQCS (Animal Quarantine and Certification Services) facility at the receiving airport. The owner pays the facility costs. If illness is detected during quarantine, the period can be extended.

    Before the pet can travel from the UK, an AQCS No Objection Certificate (NOC) must be obtained. This is applied for through the official AQCS portal and takes approximately 5 working days. Without the NOC in place before departure, the pet will be refused entry. The NOC, together with an official UK health certificate covering all required vaccinations, forms the core documentation set.

    British Airways and Air India both handle cargo on this corridor with direct flights from Heathrow to Delhi, Mumbai, Bangalore, and Chennai. Emirates (via Dubai) and Qatar Airways (via Doha) are alternative one-transfer options.

faqs:
  - question: "Is quarantine mandatory for pets entering India from the UK?"
    answer: "Yes. All imported pets undergo quarantine in India, regardless of origin. For pets with complete documentation and appearing healthy on arrival, the quarantine period is 15 days at the AQCS facility at the receiving airport. If the pet shows signs of illness during quarantine, the period can be extended. The owner pays all quarantine costs."
  - question: "What is the AQCS NOC and how do I get one?"
    answer: "The NOC (No Objection Certificate) is issued by India's Animal Quarantine and Certification Services (AQCS) and must be obtained before the pet departs the UK. It is applied for through the official AQCS portal at aqcsindia.gov.in. Processing takes approximately 5 working days. The NOC is a mandatory document: without it, the pet will not be admitted to India."
  - question: "What vaccinations does my dog need to enter India from the UK?"
    answer: "Dogs must be vaccinated against rabies (more than 1 month but within 12 months before travel), and the official health certificate must certify the pet is free from distemper, parvovirus, leptospirosis, and other infectious diseases. Cats must be certified free from rabies, feline panleukopenia, feline calicivirus, and feline rhinotracheitis. All vaccination details must appear in the official health certificate."
  - question: "Which airline is best for pets from the UK to India?"
    answer: "British Airways offers the most direct cargo options from Heathrow to Delhi (DEL), Mumbai (BOM), Bangalore (BLR), and Chennai (MAA), all of which are approved AQCS entry airports. Air India also operates cargo on this corridor. Emirates (via Dubai) and Qatar Airways (via Doha) are alternatives with one transfer. Avoid booking the Dubai routing during June-September (Emirates summer heat embargo)."
  - question: "How much does the quarantine cost in India?"
    answer: "AQCS quarantine costs are set by the Indian government and are paid by the owner. Costs vary by species, facility, and duration. For the standard 15-day period, budget for accommodation, feeding, and veterinary inspection fees at the facility. Contact the AQCS station at your intended entry airport for current rates before travel."
