  h1: "Pet Transport from the UK to India"
  overview: |
    Pets moving from the UK to India must go through the AQCS (Animal Quarantine and Certification Service), India's authority for pet imports. The mandatory 30-day quarantine at an AQCS facility on arrival is the most significant factor on this route. There is no way around it: all pets arriving in India from international origins complete 30 days in government quarantine. Budget for it early.

    The UK export side is relatively clean: an AHC from a UK OV, APHA-endorsed, is the main document. British Airways and Virgin Atlantic both operate direct services from Heathrow to Delhi and Mumbai, making the routing straightforward. The complexity is at the India end, where the AQCS process needs to be coordinated in advance.

    - heading: "What the 30-day AQCS quarantine actually involves"
      body: |
        All pets entering India from international origins go through a 30-day mandatory quarantine at an AQCS-designated facility. The main quarantine reception airports are Delhi (Indira Gandhi International) and Mumbai (Chhatrapati Shivaji Maharaj International). AQCS has facilities at these airports to receive and hold pets during the quarantine period.

        The quarantine is owner-paid. Costs vary by facility and by the size and number of pets, but owners should budget the equivalent of several hundred to a few thousand pounds for the 30-day period. Your India-side agent can provide current cost estimates for the specific AQCS facility at your arrival airport.

        During quarantine, the AQCS veterinary team monitors the pets and verifies documentation. At the end of the 30-day period, AQCS issues a release certificate and you can collect your pet. Your pet should be in good health after quarantine, as the facilities are medically supervised.

        The simplest way to prepare for the quarantine is to treat it as a scheduled event rather than a problem. The documentation needs to be correct, the AQCS office at the arrival airport needs to be notified in advance through your agent, and the 30-day cost needs to be budgeted. Given that, the process runs as a managed step rather than a disruption.

faqs:
  - question: "Is 30-day quarantine mandatory for pets arriving in India from the UK?"
    answer: "Yes, without exception. All dogs and cats imported into India must complete 30 days of quarantine at an AQCS-designated facility. There is no documentation route that avoids this. Plan for it from the start and budget the cost as part of your overall move expenses."
  - question: "Which airports in India accept international pet arrivals?"
    answer: "AQCS-designated quarantine facilities operate at the main international airports. Delhi (Indira Gandhi International) and Mumbai (Chhatrapati Shivaji Maharaj International) are the primary entry points. Your pet must fly into an airport with an AQCS facility. Check with your agent for the current list of approved entry airports."
  - question: "Does my pet need a titre test before entering India from the UK?"
    answer: "Yes. India requires a rabies antibody titre test (FAVN or HAI method, minimum 0.5 IU/ml) for pet imports. Blood must be drawn at least 30 days after the rabies vaccination. This is an India import requirement, separate from any UK export documentation."
  - question: "How much does it cost to move a pet from the UK to India?"
    answer: "Expect total costs of GBP 2,500 to GBP 6,000 depending on pet size, airline, and length of stay in India quarantine. The 30-day AQCS quarantine cost and the cargo fee are the two largest components. Agent fees typically add GBP 600 to GBP 1,500."
  - question: "Can I fly with my cat in the cabin from the UK to India?"
    answer: "No. UK law requires all pets to travel as manifested cargo from Great Britain. There is no cabin option from the UK side. On arrival in India, all pets regardless of species must complete the 30-day AQCS quarantine."
