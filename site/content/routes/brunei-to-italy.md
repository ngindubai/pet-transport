content:
  h1: "Pet Transport from Brunei to Italy"
  overview: |
    Italy dropped its national breed-specific legislation back in 2009, so a Brunei owner with a restricted-breed dog elsewhere in Europe won't hit that particular obstacle here. The rest of the process is standard EU territory: a titre test, a 90-day wait, and a Singapore transfer before the flight even begins.

    Royal Brunei Airlines carries no live cargo for privately owned pets, so the road crossing into Singapore is step one on every version of this route, whatever the eventual EU destination.

  sections:
    - heading: "The Singapore leg, then Rome or Milan"
      body: |
        With no cargo option out of Bandar Seri Begawan, the practical path is a road transfer into Singapore, then an onward booking to Italy. Qatar Airways Cargo via Doha and Emirates SkyCargo via Dubai both reach Rome Fiumicino and Milan Malpensa, giving a genuine choice of Gulf routing depending on which carrier has live animal capacity on your travel dates.

        Fiumicino and Malpensa both accept live animal cargo with Italian Ministry of Health inspection on arrival. Neither airport favours one over the other for pet clearance; the better choice usually comes down to which routing and airline gives the shortest overall transit time from Singapore.

    - heading: "No breed ban, but the EU clock still runs"
      body: |
        Italy has had no national breed-specific legislation since 2009, a genuine point of difference from some of its EU neighbours. That doesn't shorten the import timeline, though. Brunei sits outside the EU's list of low-risk third countries, so the titre test and 90-day wait under Commission Delegated Regulation (EU) 2026/131 apply in full, regardless of breed.

        Airline-level restrictions on brachycephalic breeds are a separate matter entirely and should be confirmed with the cargo carrier directly when booking, since they sit outside Italy's own import rules.
