content:
  h1: "Pet Transport from Belarus to Australia"
  overview: |
    No airline has flown between Minsk and any EU or UK airport since Belarusian carriers lost that airspace in June 2021, a restriction widened again in December 2025. Every pet leaving Belarus already has to travel roughly 550km overland to Warsaw before any flight is possible, a fact this site's Belarus-to-UK and Belarus-to-US pages both cover in detail.

    For Australia, that same forced detour turns out to solve a second problem. Belarus sits outside DAFF's approved country list entirely, so a 180-day residency stay in an approved country is required before Australia will process anything. Poland is DAFF Group 3 approved. The transfer nobody could avoid anyway happens to be the one that counts.

  sections:
    - heading: "One overland leg, two regulatory problems solved"
      body: |
        Belavia's exclusion from EU and UK airspace, following the forced landing of Ryanair Flight 4978, means Minsk has had no scheduled flight to a Western hub for over four years. A pet bound for Australia, the UK or the US all start the same way: by road, into Poland.

        DAFF's non-approved country rule asks for something separate, at least 180 consecutive days living in a Group 1, 2 or 3 country before an import file can open. Choosing Poland as that country means the transfer already forced on every Belarusian pet owner is the same one that satisfies DAFF, rather than a second detour layered on top.

    - heading: "Why the vaccination has to happen again"
      body: |
        Two rabies vaccinations, 30 to 365 days apart, are standard for any Group 3-linked import. What trips owners up is that DAFF only recognises vaccinations given inside an approved country. A course completed in Belarus before the Warsaw crossing does not count, however current it is on paper.

        Restart the course once the pet is settled in Poland instead. Time the RNATT blood draw early in the 180-day residency window, and the RNATT's own wait runs alongside the residency requirement rather than adding to it.

    - heading: "The final leg, once the wait is over"
      body: |
        Warsaw has no direct flight to Melbourne either. Emirates SkyCargo covers the route via Dubai instead, with a scheduled comfort stop for the animal at Dubai Cargo Village, roughly 22 hours from departure to Mickleham's doorstep.

        Mickleham accepts weekday arrivals only, and Melbourne is the sole Australian port for pet imports, whatever the final destination inside the country. Book the Emirates leg once the 180 days have genuinely cleared and the DAFF permit is in hand, not before.
