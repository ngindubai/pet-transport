  sections:
    - heading: "What Georgia requires at the border"
      body: |
        Georgia's veterinary import requirements are administered by the National Food Agency (NFA), nea.gov.ge. Officers inspect documentation at Tbilisi International Airport (TBS) on arrival before releasing your pet.

        The core requirements are a valid rabies vaccination administered at least 21 days before entry, an official health certificate issued within 10 days of travel, and an ISO-compliant microchip. Georgia does not require a titre test for EU-member-state pets. No quarantine applies for compliant arrivals. For 1-2 personal companion animals, no import permit is required.

    - heading: "DAFM health certificate process from Ireland"
      body: |
        Ireland's export health certificate for non-EU destinations is the EU-format official veterinary certificate signed by a DAFM-registered official vet and endorsed by DAFM (Department of Agriculture, Food and the Marine). Both steps must complete within 10 days of departure.

        Book the vet examination within 10 days of your travel date. After signing, submit the certificate to DAFM for endorsement and allow 1-2 working days. Your regular vet can handle vaccinations and microchipping, but the health certificate must come from a DAFM-registered official vet. Check gov.ie/agriculture for authorised vets.

    - heading: "Cargo routing from Dublin to Tbilisi"
      body: |
        There is no direct cargo service from Dublin (DUB) to Tbilisi (TBS). Turkish Airlines Cargo via Istanbul (IST) is the primary option. IST to TBS is a well-established route on Turkish Airlines.

        Book through an approved cargo agent. Confirm pet acceptance on both the DUB-IST and IST-TBS legs. If travelling June to September, check for temperature embargoes on the IST connection. Qatar Airways Cargo via Doha is a practical alternative in summer months, with good live animal facilities at Hamad International Airport.
