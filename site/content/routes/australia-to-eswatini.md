  overview: |
    One airline, two legs. Emirates flies Australia's major cities to Dubai and continues on its own network all the way to Johannesburg, which makes the long-haul portion of this route a genuine single-carrier itinerary, something UK and US owners moving a pet the same way don't get on this corridor.

    That convenience doesn't touch the regulatory side. Eswatini's own rules ask for no titre test from any country of origin, Australia's rabies-free status included, since that exemption was already open to UK- and US-origin pets. What does still apply is a Veterinary Import Permit from the Director of Animal Health, because Australia sits outside the Southern African Development Community bloc that gets a waiver.

    The last leg is where every route into Eswatini looks the same: no long-haul carrier serves King Mswati III International Airport directly, so a regional connection through Johannesburg on Airlink or Eswatini Air finishes the journey.

  sections:
    - heading: "A cleaner long-haul leg, the same paperwork"
      body: |
        Emirates' Dubai-Johannesburg service, run on the same airline that covers the Australia leg, means a pet on this route stays with one carrier for the entire long-haul portion of the trip. That's a genuine practical advantage over routings that need a change of airline partway through a 20-plus-hour journey.

        It doesn't change anything on the regulatory side. Eswatini's Director of Animal Health asks for the same rabies vaccination, the same core vaccine panel, and the same Veterinary Import Permit from an Australian pet as from a UK or US one. No titre test is required from any of them, and that's been true regardless of Australia's own rabies-free classification.

    - heading: "The regional hop nothing skips"
      body: |
        King Mswati III International Airport has no long-haul service of its own. Every pet, regardless of where the journey started, transfers at O.R. Tambo onto Airlink or Eswatini Air for the short final leg.

        Treat this as one booking, not two. A pet changing planes at Johannesburg needs a genuine interline cargo transfer, moved airside between the long-haul and regional carriers, rather than being cleared through South African customs as a fresh import. A cargo agent with Johannesburg experience is the difference between a smooth handover and a missed connection.

  - question: "Does a pet need a titre test to enter Eswatini from Australia?"
    answer: "No. Eswatini's own import rules do not require a rabies titre test from any country of origin, and that was already the case for UK- and US-origin pets before Australia's own rabies-free status is even considered. A rabies vaccination given between 30 days and 12 months before arrival, a microchip, and a Veterinary Import Permit are what's actually required."
  - question: "Is there a direct flight from Australia to Eswatini?"
    answer: "No. King Mswati III International Airport has no long-haul international service. Emirates flies Australia's major cities to Dubai and continues on its own network to Johannesburg, then Airlink or Eswatini Air covers the short regional leg into Eswatini."
  - question: "Does Australia get a waiver on the Eswatini import permit?"
    answer: "No. The waiver only applies to SADC member states: Botswana, Lesotho, Malawi, Namibia, South Africa and Zimbabwe. Australia, like the UK and the US, needs the Veterinary Import Permit from the Director of Animal Health."
  - question: "Is there a quarantine period for pets entering Eswatini from Australia?"
    answer: "No, provided the pet arrives with a valid microchip, a rabies vaccination given within the required window, the import permit, and a compliant health certificate."
