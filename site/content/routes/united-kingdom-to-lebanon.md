  overview: |
    The UK to Lebanon route involves moderate preparation: get your UK Animal Health Certificate, secure the Lebanon Ministry of Agriculture import permit, and choose your airline routing. A titre test is not required by Lebanon for UK-origin pets, which keeps the timeline significantly shorter than routes to countries with this requirement.

The current situation in Lebanon means administrative steps can be unpredictable, so working with a pet transport agent with current in-country experience is genuinely useful on this route.

  sections:
    - heading: "What Lebanon requires from UK pets"
      body: |
        Lebanon's Ministry of Agriculture oversees pet imports. Requirements include an ISO microchip, current rabies vaccination, an import permit issued before travel, and an official health certificate from an APHA-authorised UK Official Vet.

        Additional requirements such as internal and external parasite treatments can apply - confirm the current list with the Lebanese Ministry of Agriculture or a pet transport agent familiar with the route. Requirements in Lebanon have changed periodically and an up-to-date source is important.

        On arrival at Beirut Rafic Hariri International Airport, a Ministry veterinarian will inspect your pet and verify the documentation. If everything is in order, there is no extended quarantine. If documentation is incomplete, the pet may be held pending correction.
    - heading: "Airline options from London to Beirut: comparing your choices"
      body: |
        MEA (Middle East Airlines) is the most direct option: London Heathrow to Beirut with no change. MEA Cargo handles pet transport on this route, making it the cleanest choice logistically. Confirm pet cargo availability and capacity directly with MEA Cargo before booking.

        British Airways also operates to Beirut, with Heathrow as the London departure point. BA World Cargo can handle pet shipments, though capacity is more limited than MEA on this specific route.

        Emirates via Dubai and Qatar Airways via Doha are both well-established alternatives. The extra connection adds 5-8 hours to your pet's journey but both carriers have strong pet cargo operations and depart from Heathrow multiple times daily. Emirates restricts brachycephalic breeds on some segments - confirm your pet's breed status if relevant.

        Lufthansa via Frankfurt offers the added option of cabin travel for small pets on the London-Frankfurt leg, which some owners prefer for cats or small dogs. The Beirut segment is cargo-only for all animals.
    - heading: "The UK Animal Health Certificate: getting it right"
      body: |
        Every pet leaving the UK needs an Animal Health Certificate from an APHA-authorised Official Vet. The AHC is valid for 10 days from the examination date, so it must be one of the final steps before you travel.

        The OV will examine your pet, verify the microchip, confirm vaccination status, and complete the certificate. Lebanon may have specific requirements about how the certificate is formatted or endorsed. Check with your agent or the Lebanese Ministry before the OV appointment so there are no surprises.

        Book your OV appointment 3-4 weeks before your travel date even though you will not collect the certificate until within the 10-day window. OV availability can be limited, and missing the certificate window means delaying your travel.
