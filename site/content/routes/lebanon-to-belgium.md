      body: |
        Lufthansa Cargo via Frankfurt is the shortest practical route from BEY to BRU, and Frankfurt's Animal Lounge gives a pet a proper rest stop mid-journey rather than a bare tarmac transfer. Turkish Airlines Cargo via Istanbul covers the same ground and is worth quoting alongside Lufthansa, since availability on either leg can shift week to week.

        Qatar Airways via Doha is the fallback when neither European option has space, though it adds hours to total transit and, like other Gulf carriers, can suspend live animal acceptance during peak summer heat.

        On arrival, FASFC (the Federal Agency for the Safety of the Food Chain) inspects the pet and its paperwork directly at the BRU cargo terminal. Non-compliant documentation is not waved through; animals can be held at the owner's expense or, in the worst case, refused entry. Have the endorsed EU-format certificate physically accessible, not buried in luggage.

    - heading: "The unlisted-country sequence and Lebanese endorsement"
      body: |
        Lebanon is not on the EU's approved list, so Belgium applies the full sequence: microchip before vaccination, a 30-day gap, an FAVN blood draw at an EU-approved lab, and then 90 days before the pet can legally enter. Get the order wrong at the start and the whole thing restarts.

        The last piece is the EU-format health certificate from a Lebanese vet, endorsed by the Ministry of Agriculture, valid for only 10 days. Lebanese government processing for that endorsement is not instant. Two to four weeks is the fair estimate, and it should sit inside the tail end of your 90-day wait, not stacked on afterward.
