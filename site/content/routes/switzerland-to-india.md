  h1: "Pet Transport from Switzerland to India"
  overview: |
    Switzerland to India is a moderate-complexity route. India does not require a titre test or advance import permit, which simplifies the preparation compared to destinations like Japan or Australia. However, there are two important constraints: your pet must arrive at one of only five approved airports, and an AQCS (Animal Quarantine and Certification Service) inspection on arrival is mandatory.

    Switzerland's non-EU status means the Swiss pet passport alone is not the relevant export document. An FSVO-endorsed health certificate is required, issued by a Swiss vet and endorsed by the cantonal veterinary office.

    The most common routing from Zurich is via Frankfurt with Lufthansa.
  sections:
    - heading: "What does India require for a pet arriving from Switzerland?"
      body: |
        India's Animal Quarantine and Certification Service (AQCS) under the Ministry of Agriculture inspects all imported animals. Requirements are: ISO microchip; valid rabies vaccination; an official health certificate endorsed by Swiss authorities (cantonal or FSVO); and arrival at one of the five approved AQCS airports.

        The five approved entry airports are Delhi (Indira Gandhi International), Mumbai (Chhatrapati Shivaji Maharaj International), Chennai (MAA), Kolkata (CCU), and Bengaluru (Kempegowda International). Your pet cannot arrive at Hyderabad, Goa, or any other airport.

        There is no titre test requirement from Switzerland to India, and no advance import permit is needed. The AQCS inspection on arrival is the main procedural step.

        Source: India DAHD (Department of Animal Husbandry and Dairying), dahd.gov.in.
    - heading: "How does the FSVO health certificate work for India?"
      body: |
        Switzerland's Federal Food Safety and Veterinary Office (FSVO) is the competent authority for animal export documentation. Your Swiss veterinarian prepares a health certificate confirming your pet's microchip number, rabies vaccination status, and fitness to travel. The cantonal veterinary office endorses the certificate.

        The certificate must be issued within 10 days of departure and remain valid on arrival in India. Allow at least one week for the endorsement process at the cantonal office -- do not schedule this at the last minute.

        AQCS will inspect both your pet and the documentation on arrival. Keep all original documents accessible in your carry-on, not in checked luggage.
    - heading: "Which airline serves Zurich to India for pets?"
      body: |
        There are no direct Zurich to India flights. Lufthansa via Frankfurt is the most convenient routing. The Zurich-Frankfurt leg is short (under an hour), and Lufthansa Cargo's Frankfurt hub connects to all five approved Indian gateway airports. Small pets may travel in the cabin from Zurich to Frankfurt.

        Air India via London or other hubs is an option, particularly if you prefer the national carrier at the India end. Air India Cargo accepts pets and the airline has direct experience with AQCS on arrival at Indian airports.

        Emirates via Dubai provides broad India coverage with connections to all major Indian cities.
faqs:
  - question: "Does India require a titre test for a pet from Switzerland?"
    answer: "No. India does not require a rabies titre test for pets from Switzerland. A valid rabies vaccination, ISO microchip, and FSVO-endorsed health certificate are sufficient. The AQCS inspection on arrival is standard procedure regardless of titre test status."
  - question: "Which airports can my pet arrive at in India from Switzerland?"
    answer: "Only five airports have AQCS facilities authorised to inspect imported animals: Delhi (DEL), Mumbai (BOM), Chennai (MAA), Kolkata (CCU), and Bengaluru (BLR). Book your cargo routing to terminate at one of these airports. Arrival at any other Indian airport will result in your pet being refused or held."
  - question: "Is there quarantine for pets entering India from Switzerland?"
    answer: "No mandatory quarantine. AQCS will inspect your pet and documentation on arrival. If everything is in order, your pet is released to you on the same day. If there are documentation issues, your pet may be held pending resolution."
  - question: "Can my cat travel in the cabin from Zurich to Delhi?"
    answer: "In most cases, no -- the India leg requires cargo. For a small cat, you might be able to travel in cabin from Zurich to Frankfurt with Lufthansa, but the Frankfurt to Delhi sector is a long-haul cargo booking. Check Lufthansa's cabin pet policy for short European legs and their cargo division for the India sector."
