  overview: "Unlike UK pets, American dogs and cats are subject to Israel's full titre test requirement when travelling to Israel. The USA is not on Israel's approved rabies-free list, so the process mirrors what most countries face: titre test, 90-day wait, and a specific USDA Israel health certificate form.   sections:
    - heading: "Titre test, 90-day wait and what drives the timeline"
      body: |
        The titre test blood draw must happen at least 30 days after your pet's most recent rabies vaccination. Once the test result comes back from an OIE-approved laboratory (result must be 0.5 IU/ml or above), a mandatory 90-day waiting period begins. Your pet cannot travel to Israel until those 90 days have passed from the blood draw date.

        Alongside this, the Israeli import permit takes at least 60 days to process. These timelines overlap, so apply for the permit and begin the titre test process at roughly the same time. With vaccinations, the titre test, the 90-day wait, and the permit all running simultaneously, the realistic minimum preparation time for most US pets is 5-6 months.

    - heading: "The USDA health certificate and arrival inspection"
      body: |
        Israel requires a specific country-format USDA health certificate for pets from the USA, rather than a generic health certificate. This form is completed by a USDA-accredited veterinarian and then endorsed by USDA APHIS Veterinary Services. USDA endorsement typically takes 2-10 business days depending on whether you use drop-off, mail, or electronic submission through VEHCS. The endorsed certificate is valid for 10 days from issue.

        On arrival at Ben Gurion Airport, all imported pets go through the Quarantine Station for a mandatory veterinary inspection. With correct paperwork, the inspection hold is 1-3 days. Incomplete or incorrect documentation can extend this or result in the pet being returned. Budget for the inspection hold in your travel plans.

faqs:
  - question: "Why do US pets need a titre test to enter Israel when UK pets do not?"
    answer: "Israel classifies countries based on their rabies status. The UK is on Israel's approved list of rabies-free countries, so UK pets are exempt from the titre test. The USA is not on that list, so pets from the US must pass a rabies antibody titre test. The test result must be at least 0.5 IU/ml, drawn at an OIE-approved laboratory at least 30 days after the most recent rabies vaccination, followed by a 90-day waiting period before travel."

  - question: "What is the specific Israel USDA health certificate?"
    answer: "Israel requires its own country-specific health certificate format for US pet imports, titled 'Veterinary Health Certificate for Export of Pet Dogs and Cats from the United States of America to the State of Israel.' This is a different form from the generic USDA health certificate used for other destinations. Your USDA-accredited veterinarian should know it, or you can find the current form on the USDA APHIS website under US to Israel pet travel. The form must be completed by the vet and then endorsed by USDA APHIS."

  - question: "How long is the arrival inspection hold at Ben Gurion Airport?"
    answer: "For pets with correct documentation (import permit, USDA health certificate, titre test result, microchip), the inspection hold at the Ben Gurion Airport Quarantine Station is typically 1-3 days. Israeli Veterinary Services staff check all documentation and examine the animal. If any document is incorrect, missing, or the titre test result is below threshold, the pet may be held longer or refused entry. Build the inspection hold into your Israel arrival schedule."
