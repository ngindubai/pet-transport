  sections:
    - heading: "The Budapest departure challenge: Wizz Air and cargo pets"
      body: |
        A significant share of Budapest's international traffic moves on Wizz Air. The airline is Budapest's largest carrier, with a wide European network. But Wizz Air's policy is firm: no live animals in the cargo hold, on any route. If your dog is too large for the cabin, Wizz Air cannot be the carrier.

        For large dogs moving from Budapest to Vilnius, the practical options are:

        LOT Polish Airlines via Warsaw: Budapest Ferenc Liszt (BUD) to Warsaw Chopin (WAW) on LOT, then Warsaw to Vilnius (VNO) on LOT. Warsaw to Vilnius is under an hour. LOT Cargo handles the live animal booking across both legs. Keeping the animal within one carrier from Budapest to Vilnius removes any coordination gap at the Warsaw transfer.

        airBaltic via Riga: Budapest to Riga (RIX) on airBaltic, then Riga to Vilnius. The RIX to VNO hop is short. airBaltic Cargo at Riga, the airline's home hub, handles the arrival processing. This routing adds time compared to the LOT direct via Warsaw, but works well when LOT capacity is limited.

        Small cabin pets have more options. Wizz Air accepts cabin pets under their specific size and weight rules. LOT and airBaltic both take cabin pets on the connecting routes.

    - heading: "Lithuania's breed rules: the additional check for certain dog types"
      body: |
        Lithuania maintains domestic legislation on potentially dangerous dog breeds. The rules apply to dogs in Lithuania regardless of their EU country of origin. For a Hungarian owner moving a dog to Vilnius, the EU pet passport satisfies the import requirements under EU Regulation 576/2013. But if the dog is a Pitbull Terrier, American Staffordshire Terrier, Staffordshire Bull Terrier, or a breed or type classed as potentially dangerous under Lithuanian law, there are additional domestic requirements.

        These may include muzzling in public, registration with local municipal authorities, and insurance or liability requirements. The specific rules depend on the dog's classification. Contact VMVT (Valstybinė maisto ir veterinarijos tarnyba) at vmvt.lt before the move to confirm what applies to your dog.

        For owners of standard companion breeds, this section does not apply. The VMVT spot check at Vilnius Airport for a non-restricted breed with a valid EU pet passport is brief.
