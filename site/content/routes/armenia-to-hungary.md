  sections:
    - heading: "What NÉBIH checks when your pet arrives in Budapest"
      body: |
        Hungary's National Food Chain Safety Office (NÉBIH) carries out veterinary border checks on live animals arriving from third countries. For pets from EU unlisted countries like Armenia, they verify four things: the microchip is in place and matches the documentation; the rabies vaccination is current and was done after the microchip; the FAVN titre test result is 0.5 IU/ml or above; and the 90-day wait from the blood draw date has passed before the travel date shown on the AHC.

        They also check that the AHC was issued within 10 days of the pet's arrival in Hungary and that the SSFS endorsement is present and current.

        If any of these dates do not add up, the pet is refused entry or placed in conditional quarantine at the owner's expense. Getting the paperwork sequence right is not optional, it is the entry condition.

    - heading: "Why Turkish Airlines via Istanbul is the standard routing"
      body: |
        No direct cargo flight operates between Yerevan and Budapest for live animals. The most practical routing is Turkish Airlines Cargo, which connects Yerevan Zvartnots (EVN) to Istanbul (IST) and then IST to Budapest Ferenc Liszt (BUD).

        Turkish Airlines Cargo accepts live animals in IATA-compliant crates on both the EVN-IST and IST-BUD segments. Temperature restrictions apply during summer months, typically when temperatures at origin, transit, or destination are above 29-30 degrees Celsius. For summer moves, confirm cargo acceptance on specific dates before booking anything else.

        Wizz Air also operates on the Yerevan-Budapest corridor but does not accept live animal cargo. Ryanair serves Budapest but similarly does not carry pets in the hold. For this route, Turkish Airlines or Lufthansa via Frankfurt are the practical options.
