    - heading: "CFIA requirements: what Canada actually needs"
      body: |
        Canada's federal pet import process is run by the Canadian Food Inspection Agency (CFIA). For personal imports of 1-2 pets, the process is document-driven.

        Dogs aged 3 months or older must be currently vaccinated against rabies. The vaccination record must appear in the health certificate. The certificate must be signed by an MPI-authorised vet and endorsed by MPI, confirming the pet is microchipped, vaccinated, and appears healthy at time of examination.

        At the Canadian port of entry (Vancouver or Toronto for direct Air NZ flights), a CBSA officer inspects the documentation. For compliant pets from New Zealand, the inspection is brief and the pet is released to you the same day.
faqs:
  - question: "Do I need a titre test to bring my pet from New Zealand to Canada?"
    answer: "No. A titre test is not required for personal pet imports from New Zealand to Canada. The main requirements are a current rabies vaccination for dogs aged 3 months and over, and a health certificate issued within 10 days of Canadian arrival."
  - question: "Is quarantine required for pets entering Canada from New Zealand?"
    answer: "No. Compliant pets from New Zealand face no routine quarantine on arrival in Canada. CBSA may inspect documents at the port of entry, but the pet is released to you on arrival day if paperwork is correct."
  - question: "Which airline should I use for New Zealand to Canada pet transport?"
    answer: "Air New Zealand is the best starting point. They operate direct services from Auckland to Vancouver (YVR) and Toronto (YYZ) and accept live animal cargo on this corridor. Air Canada and Cathay Pacific are alternatives, typically with one connection."
  - question: "Do I need an import permit to bring my pet from NZ to Canada?"
    answer: "No import permit is required for personal imports of 1-2 pets. Commercial imports (pets for sale, breeding, fostering, or more than 2 dogs) do require a CFIA import permit. For a family pet, the health certificate is the primary document."
  - question: "What happens at Canadian customs when my pet arrives?"
    answer: "A CBSA officer checks the health certificate, vaccination records, and microchip details. For compliant pets from New Zealand with correct documentation, the inspection is quick. Your pet is released to you on arrival day. If documentation is incomplete, the pet may be held for further inspection at your cost."
