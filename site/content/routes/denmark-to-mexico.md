  h1: "Pet Transport Denmark to Mexico: DVFA Certificate and the SENASICA 10-Day Window"
  overview: |
    Denmark to Mexico is one of the more manageable long-haul pet transport routes. A DVFA-issued official health certificate (valid within 10 days of Mexico entry), a current rabies vaccination, and a microchip are the core requirements. SENASICA inspects pets on arrival but does not impose routine quarantine on compliant pets from most origins. KLM via Amsterdam and Air France via Paris are the primary cargo routing options from Copenhagen.

    - heading: "SENASICA requirements: the 10-day certificate rule"
      body: |
        SENASICA (Servicio Nacional de Sanidad, Inocuidad y Calidad Agroalimentaria) is Mexico's food and agriculture safety authority. For pets entering from Denmark, the health certificate must be issued by a DVFA-authorised official vet and must meet SENASICA's format requirements.

        The critical timing constraint is that the health certificate must be issued within 10 days of the pet's entry into Mexico. If the flight is delayed or rescheduled beyond the 10-day window from the certificate date, a new certificate will be needed. Plan the vet appointment as close to the travel date as possible while allowing time for any necessary endorsement.

        Contact SENASICA (gob.mx/senasica) for the current health certificate format required for EU-origin pets entering Mexico.
