  overview: |
    Copenhagen doesn't fly to Mexico City directly, but the connection is about as clean as they come: a short hop to Amsterdam feeds straight onto KLM's own non-stop Amsterdam-Mexico City service, seven times a week on a Boeing 787. Mexico's SENASICA doesn't add much on the regulatory side either, no titre test, no advance permit, clearance handled at the airport itself.

    This is one of the more straightforward long-haul routes covered on this site, both in the air and on paper.

  sections:
    - heading: "A short connection onto a genuine long-haul direct flight"
      body: |
        KLM's Amsterdam-Mexico City service, flight KL685 outbound and KL687 on the return, runs seven times weekly, roughly 13 hours on a Boeing 787. Since the Copenhagen-Amsterdam leg is also KLM, a Denmark-origin pet stays with a single carrier for the entire journey rather than transferring between unrelated airlines at Amsterdam.

    - heading: "SENASICA's port-of-entry process"
      body: |
        Mexico's SENASICA doesn't require an advance import permit for a personal pet, clearing documentation and running a physical inspection at the airport. No titre test applies from Denmark. The FF-SENASICA-003 form, completed by the owner ahead of travel, is the main document requirement alongside the Fodevarestyrelsen-endorsed health certificate.
