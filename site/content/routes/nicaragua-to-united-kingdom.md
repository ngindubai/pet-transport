  h1: "Pet Transport from Nicaragua to United Kingdom"
  overview: |
    A rabies titre test decides the timeline on this route, not the flight. Nicaragua sits outside both the EU's and Great Britain's listed-country groupings, confirmed against the current Delegated Regulation 2026/131 country tables, so a dog or cat leaving Managua needs a blood test showing a rabies antibody level of at least 0.5 IU/ml, drawn 30 days after vaccination, followed by a 3-month wait before travel.

    No airline flies MGA to London without a stop. Miami on American Airlines and Panama City on Copa are the two realistic options, and each adds a cargo handover that has to be arranged and confirmed in advance. Add IPSA's export process on the Nicaraguan side and, for most agent-booked shipments, the UK's Balai commercial import rules, and this route runs closer to five months door to door than the eight to twelve weeks some quicker European routes manage.
  sections:
    - heading: "Why Nicaragua triggers the titre test"
      body: |
        Great Britain and the EU both classify third countries as Part 1 (low rabies risk, no blood test needed), Part 2, or unlisted. Nicaragua does not appear on either the Part 1 or Part 2 tables in the current EU listing that GB's own rules mirror. That makes it unlisted by default, and unlisted means the full blood-test pathway applies regardless of how healthy or well-travelled the animal is.

        This is a paperwork and timing issue more than a medical one. The test itself is routine. What catches people out is the sequence: vaccinate, wait 30 days, draw blood, wait for a lab result, then wait another 3 months from the draw date. Compress any of those steps and GOV.UK's rules require you to restart.

        A GB Pet Health Certificate then has to be completed by an official vet in Nicaragua within 10 days of travel. Dogs also need a praziquantel treatment in the 24 to 120 hours before landing in Great Britain; cats do not.

    - heading: "Routing through Miami or Panama City"
      body: |
        Managua's Augusto C. Sandino International Airport has daily non-stop service to Miami on American and to Houston on United, plus a Panama City connection on Copa. None of those cities is London, so every pet shipment on this route needs a second, coordinated booking for the onward leg.

        Miami is usually the more workable choice because American Airlines Cargo's PetEmbark programme is bookable by the general public, not restricted to military shippers as some third-party guides suggest, based on the current policy published on aacargo.com. Nicaragua also does not appear on the CDC's high-risk countries list for dog rabies, last updated 15 April 2026, which matters if the routing takes the animal through a US airport, since that list drives extra US-side paperwork for dogs from countries that do appear on it.

        Panama City avoids the US transit altogether but depends on Copa's partner network for the transatlantic leg, and needs its own separate confirmation that live animals are accepted end to end.
