  sections:
    - heading: "Jamaica's listed status: no titre test for Denmark"
      body: |
        EU Implementing Regulation 2026/636 classifies third countries as listed or unlisted based on animal disease control status. Jamaica is classified as a listed third country, which means pets from Jamaica are exempt from the FAVN rabies titre test and the 90-day waiting period when entering EU member states including Denmark.

        Fødevarestyrelsen (the Danish Veterinary and Food Administration) still requires the standard EU-format non-commercial health certificate. The VSD in Jamaica is the issuing authority, and the certificate must be issued within 10 days of travel. The preparation timeline is 4-8 weeks rather than the 20-26 weeks required for unlisted countries.

    - heading: "Breed restrictions and Border Control Posts in Denmark"
      body: |
        Denmark's Dog Act prohibits 13 breeds and their crosses. The list includes pit bull terrier, Tosa Inu, American Staffordshire Terrier, Fila Brasileiro, Dogo Argentino, American Bulldog, Boerboel, Kangal, Central Asian Ovtcharka, Caucasian Ovtcharka, South Russian Ovtcharka, Tornjak, and Sarplaninac. No documentation or vaccination overrides this ban. If your dog is a restricted breed or a cross, entry is not possible regardless of paperwork.

        Denmark designates three Border Control Posts for non-EU pet arrivals: Copenhagen Kastrup (CPH), Billund (BLL), and Aalborg (AAL). CPH handles the largest volume and has the most consistent staffing. Routing from Jamaica will most likely be via London or Amsterdam, both of which have onward connections to CPH. Confirm live animal cargo acceptance on all legs before committing to a routing.
