  overview: "Denmark is one of the simpler European destinations for Jamaica-originating pets. As an EU member state, Denmark's Danish Veterinary and Food Administration (DVFA) applies standard EU non-commercial pet movement rules, and Jamaica's EU Group 2 listed status under Implementing Regulation (EU) 2024/1130 removes the titre test requirement. Unlike Norway and Finland, Denmark has no mandatory tapeworm treatment for arriving dogs.   sections:
    - heading: "DVFA Rules for Pets Arriving from Jamaica"
      body: |
        The Danish Veterinary and Food Administration (DVFA, fvst.dk) is the authority responsible for veterinary border checks in Denmark. For non-commercial pet movement, Denmark applies EU Regulation (EU) No 576/2013 as updated by Commission Implementing Regulation (EU) 2024/1130.

        The requirements for a pet arriving from Jamaica are clear:

        Microchip: ISO 11784/11785 transponder, implanted before the rabies vaccination. DVFA checks this sequencing.

        Rabies vaccination: Current, post-microchip, with at least 21 days elapsed since the primary vaccination. A valid booster within the previous vaccination's coverage period does not restart the 21-day count.

        Health certificate: EU Annex IV non-commercial format, signed by a VSD-authorised vet and endorsed by the VSD in Jamaica. Must be issued within 10 days of travel.

        No titre test. No quarantine. No import permit. No tapeworm treatment required.

    - heading: "Why Denmark is Simpler Than Its Nordic Neighbours"
      body: |
        Pet owners comparing the Denmark route with Norway or Finland will find a meaningful difference. Both Norway and Finland require mandatory Echinococcus tapeworm treatment for dogs from non-exempt countries (including Jamaica), administered by a vet within a specific window before arrival. Denmark has no such requirement.

        Denmark already has Echinococcus multilocularis (fox tapeworm) present in its wildlife, so the pre-entry treatment that the other Nordic countries use as a border control measure does not apply here. This simplifies the logistics considerably.

        There is no need to arrange a vet visit at the London or Amsterdam layover, as would be necessary for the Norway or Finland routes. The standard documentation is sufficient.

    - heading: "Copenhagen as the Entry Airport"
      body: |
        Copenhagen Kastrup Airport (CPH) is the main international cargo entry point for Denmark and serves as a major Scandinavian hub. Live animal cargo from Jamaica routes through CPH after connecting at a European hub, most commonly London Heathrow (LHR) or Amsterdam (AMS).

        British Airways World Cargo handles the Jamaica to LHR sector, and SAS Scandinavian Airlines Cargo connects LHR to CPH. SAS has an established live animal operation and Copenhagen airport has good cargo handling facilities.

        KLM via Amsterdam is the other strong option. AMS to CPH is a very short sector and KLM's live animal handling at Schiphol is reliable. This routing works well for pets of all sizes.

        SAS also operates its own transatlantic service from some Caribbean destinations, though this may not cover the Jamaica-specific route. Check availability, as direct SAS cargo on the Caribbean leg cuts down on handling and connection time.

    - heading: "Documents at the DVFA Border Post"
      body: |
        DVFA inspectors at CPH will check the following on arrival:

        The EU health certificate: original document, not a copy. The endorsement stamp from the VSD must be visible and legible.

        Vaccination record: the chip number, vaccine batch, date administered, and expiry must be consistent with the health certificate.

        Physical chip scan: the microchip is scanned and matched against the number on the certificate.

        Condition of the animal: DVFA may assess the animal's health and record its condition.

        Collection from the cargo terminal requires your ID, the airway bill number, and the freight forwarder's collection documentation. Allow two to four hours from the aircraft's arrival for the process to complete.

    - heading: "Travel in Summer vs Winter"
      body: |
        Cargo bookings from Jamaica to Copenhagen are particularly competitive in the summer months (June to August), when demand peaks across all Scandinavian routes. Early booking, six to eight weeks ahead of your travel date, is important during this period.

        In winter, temperature management in the cargo hold becomes more significant for the European sectors. Airlines have protocols for minimum and maximum temperature conditions, and some carriers impose embargoes on live animal cargo if ground temperatures at transit or destination airports fall below their accepted thresholds. This is most commonly an issue at northern European airports in January and February. Discuss your travel dates with PetTransportGlobal to identify any seasonal restrictions.

faqs:
  - question: "Denmark is in Scandinavia. Does it require the same Echinococcus treatment as Norway and Finland?"
    answer: "No. Denmark does not require a mandatory Echinococcus tapeworm treatment before entry. This requirement applies only to Norway and Finland. Denmark already has the relevant tapeworm species present in its wildlife, so the pre-entry treatment rule does not apply. Your dog from Jamaica can enter Denmark without a tapeworm treatment."
  - question: "Can I enter Denmark via the Oresund Bridge from Sweden after flying into Gothenburg or Stockholm?"
    answer: "If your pet has already entered the EU legally via a Swedish border post (with a completed DVFA-equivalent inspection), it can travel freely within the EU, including into Denmark via the Oresund Bridge, without additional border inspections. However, ensure that the Swedish entry was completed correctly with all documents, as Swedish customs may inspect the documentation at the Swedish side of the route."
  - question: "How far in advance should I book cargo space for the Jamaica to Copenhagen route?"
    answer: "Six to eight weeks before your travel date is the minimum recommended lead time. In summer, or around Christmas, book earlier if you can. Live animal cargo space is limited and fills up, especially during peak travel periods. Cargo booking is entirely separate from your own travel ticket."
