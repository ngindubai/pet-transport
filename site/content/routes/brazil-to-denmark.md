        The CVI (Certificado Veterinário Internacional) is valid for 10 days from the date of MAPA endorsement. This short validity window requires careful co-ordination with your cargo flight booking.

        Plan the MAPA endorsement date to fall within 10 days before your pet arrives in Denmark, not before you depart Brazil. On a direct GRU-LIS-CPH routing, the total journey time is typically 12 to 16 hours, so the endorsement date and the arrival date need to be close. Your cargo agent will advise on the exact timing for your specific flight.

      body: |
        Copenhagen Airport (CPH) is served by multiple European cargo carriers. Your cargo agent pre-notifies the arrival through the EU TRACES system before the flight departs.

        On arrival, the Danish Veterinary and Food Administration (DVFA) conducts the border veterinary inspection. For Brazil to Denmark, the inspector checks the AHC, the MAPA CVI, and the titre test result. With all documents complete and the 90-day wait observed, the inspection is administrative and your pet is cleared for entry.

faqs:
  - question: "Does Denmark quarantine pets from Brazil?"
    answer: "No. Denmark does not quarantine pets from Brazil provided the EU requirements are met: ISO microchip, valid rabies vaccination, FAVN titre test at 0.5 IU/ml or above, completed 90-day wait from the blood draw date, and EU AHC issued within 10 days before arrival. The MAPA CVI is also required as the Brazilian export document."
  - question: "Can I use a Brazil-issued FAVN test result for EU entry?"
    answer: "Yes, provided the laboratory is on the EU-approved list for rabies antibody titration. The blood draw takes place in Brazil at your vet's clinic, and the sample is sent to an EU-approved laboratory for analysis. Laboratories in the USA are commonly used for Brazilian samples. Check the European Commission's current designated laboratory list before submitting."
  - question: "What are the MAPA requirements for the dog's antiparasitic treatment for Brazil export?"
    answer: "For export from Brazil, MAPA requires the health certificate to document the pet's health status. The antiparasitic treatment requirement for Brazil applies to pets entering Brazil, not leaving it. When your pet is leaving Brazil for Denmark, the focus is on meeting Denmark's EU entry requirements rather than MAPA's import rules."
  - question: "How long does the Brazil to Denmark cargo flight take?"
    answer: "São Paulo Guarulhos to Copenhagen with one stop takes approximately 12 to 16 hours depending on the connecting hub and layover time. Via Lisbon (TAP) is typically 12 to 13 hours total. Via Amsterdam (KLM) or Frankfurt (Lufthansa) adds an hour or two depending on the layover. Your agent will recommend the routing with the best live animal welfare profile for your pet's size and breed."
