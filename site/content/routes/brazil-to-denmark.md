    Brazil is not on the EU approved list for non-commercial pet movement, which means the FAVN titre test and 90-day waiting period are mandatory for pets entering Denmark from Brazil. The MAPA (Ministério da Agricultura, Pecuária e Abastecimento) endorses the health certificate. LATAM, KLM, and Air France all serve the Brazil-to-Europe corridor with connections to Copenhagen. Total minimum timeline is 6 to 7 months.

        KLM via Amsterdam and Air France via Paris are the most common choices for Brazil-to-Denmark pet cargo. Both offer good frequency from São Paulo Guarulhos (GRU) and strong CPH connections from their hubs. Confirm live animal acceptance on both legs before booking.

    - heading: "MAPA export certification for EU travel"
      body: |
        MAPA (Ministério da Agricultura, Pecuária e Abastecimento) is Brazil's agricultural and livestock authority. The health certificate for EU import must be issued by a MAPA-authorised official vet and endorsed by MAPA. The certificate must follow the EU format specified by the European Commission for third-country pets entering the EU.

        The certificate must include: ISO microchip number, vaccination history with titre test result (laboratory name, blood draw date, result in IU/ml, and the date 90 days from the draw), species, breed, owner details, and fitness to travel. MAPA has offices throughout Brazil including São Paulo, Rio de Janeiro, and other major cities.

        The certificate is valid for 10 days from the examination date to the EU entry date. Plan the final vet appointment around your confirmed cargo booking. Contact MAPA (mapa.gov.br) for the current list of authorised vets and the EU health certificate format.
