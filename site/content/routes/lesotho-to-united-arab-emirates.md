  overview: |
    Etihad Airways will fly you from Johannesburg to Abu Dhabi in under eight hours. It won't fly your pet on that route at all. Etihad currently excludes pet travel from its Johannesburg service entirely, a gap that catches out owners who assume the fastest passenger option also works for cargo.

    Emirates fills the space instead, flying Johannesburg to Dubai nonstop daily through Emirates SkyCargo. Every Lesotho pet reaches Johannesburg the same way first, on Airlink's short Maseru hop, since Moshoeshoe I International Airport in Maseru has no other scheduled destination.

    The regulatory side moves faster than the routing might suggest. Lesotho isn't on MOCCAE's low-risk list, so a titre test applies, but the UAE sets no fixed wait once the result clears, unlike the UK's fixed 90-day rule that already governs this site's Lesotho-to-UK route.
  sections:
    - heading: "Etihad won't take the booking. Emirates will."
      body: |
        A Johannesburg-Abu Dhabi search turns up Etihad's nonstop service, under eight hours, a genuinely appealing schedule. The catch only appears once you try to book the pet: Etihad currently lists no pet travel on routes involving Johannesburg, a gap that applies to this specific city pair regardless of the aircraft or cabin class.

        Emirates, flying the parallel Johannesburg-Dubai nonstop daily through its SkyCargo division, is the workable substitute. Same continent, similar flight time, a live booking path for a live animal rather than a passenger-only option.
    - heading: "The titre test MOCCAE still requires"
      body: |
        Lesotho sits outside MOCCAE's roughly 50-country low-risk list, so a rabies antibody titre test at an accredited laboratory is required, blood drawn at least 21 days after vaccination, result at least 0.5 IU/ml. That part isn't negotiable regardless of which UAE-bound carrier eventually gets used.

        Once the result clears, though, MOCCAE doesn't add the further waiting period the UK enforces on the same test. The practical bottleneck on this route is less the regulation and more the two-leg routing itself.
    - heading: "Two legs, two waybills, one Johannesburg agent"
      body: |
        The Airlink flight from Maseru and the Emirates flight from Johannesburg run on separate bookings, usually different terminals at O.R. Tambo. A ground agent who can receive the pet from the regional arrival and hand it to the long-haul cargo terminal, typically the same day, isn't optional in practice, even though nothing about the paperwork itself demands one.

        This is the same handoff challenge every Lesotho route on this site describes. What's different here is the regulatory clock behind it: no fixed post-titre wait means the routing logistics, not a government-imposed pause, set the realistic floor on how quickly this move can happen.
