  h1: "Pet Transport from the United Kingdom to Turkmenistan"
  overview: |
    Ask a Turkmenistan-bound pet owner what's holding up their move and the answer is rarely the paperwork for the animal. It's the human visa. Turkmenistan still requires a Letter of Invitation from a licensed local travel agency for most tourist entries, and the e-visa system passed into law in April 2025 hadn't launched as of February 2026. Almost everyone who actually completes this route is relocating for an oil and gas contract, a diplomatic posting, or a similar employer-sponsored move, not planning a personal trip around their pet's schedule.

    Against that backdrop, the pet's own requirements are comparatively simple: a microchip, a rabies vaccination inside the last 12 months (multi-year vaccines aren't accepted here, unusually strict compared with most destinations covered on this site), and a UK Export Health Certificate. No titre test is clearly documented for pets arriving from a low-risk country like Britain.

    What eats the calendar is Ashgabat's own limited scheduled flight capacity. Book the Istanbul or Dubai connection weeks ahead of when you'd normally think necessary.

  sections:
    - heading: "The real constraint is the visa, not the vet"
      body: |
        Most route pages on this site treat the pet's medical timeline as the long pole in the tent. Not here. Turkmenistan's visa regime, one of the most restrictive anywhere, means the practical audience for this route is narrower than the search traffic bringing people to this page might suggest.

        If you're moving for work, your employer or the relevant embassy typically handles the visa side, and the pet paperwork below runs alongside it. If you're planning a personal move without that backing, budget serious extra time for the Letter of Invitation process, and don't assume the pet can travel before you've confirmed your own entry is sorted.

    - heading: "Annual vaccination, not the UK's usual 3-year cycle"
      body: |
        A pet vaccinated on the UK's standard 3-year rabies schedule can still be years away from its next legally required UK booster and yet be out of date for Turkmenistan. The rule here is a flat 12-month window: vaccination given between 30 days and 12 months before arrival, full stop, with no allowance for a longer-duration vaccine product.

        If your pet's last rabies shot falls outside that 12-month window, get a fresh booster before the export certificate is issued. This is worth checking early, since it's an easy detail to miss when your UK vaccination card says the pet isn't due again for another two years.

    - heading: "Getting a cargo slot into Ashgabat"
      body: |
        There's no direct UK-Turkmenistan service, for pets or people. British Airways gets a pet as far as Istanbul or Dubai via its cargo partner PetAir UK, then hands off to a carrier that actually flies into Ashgabat: Turkish Airlines or Turkmenistan Airlines on the Istanbul leg, or Emirates and flydubai via Dubai.

        Scheduled capacity into Ashgabat is genuinely limited on all of these routings. Book 6-8 weeks ahead rather than the 3-4 weeks that might suffice elsewhere, and keep a second routing option in mind in case the first doesn't have space on your dates.
