  sections:
    - heading: "The AFCD export process: what Hong Kong requires"
      body: |
        All live animal exports from Hong Kong are controlled by the Agriculture, Fisheries and Conservation Department (AFCD). Before your pet can leave, you need an export permit and a health certificate endorsed by AFCD.

        The export permit application goes to the AFCD directly. Processing typically takes 2-3 weeks. Start this well before your planned departure. The application requires details of your pet, the destination, and the carrier.

        The health certificate is signed by a vet the AFCD recognises as authorised for export documentation. Not every vet in Hong Kong holds this status. Your pet transport agent in Hong Kong will know which vets are approved. The certificate must be issued within 10 days of your arrival in Great Britain, which links it tightly to your travel dates.
    - heading: "The Cathay Pacific cargo process from Hong Kong"
      body: |
        Cathay Pacific is the natural first choice for Hong Kong-to-London pet transport. Their cargo operation at HKG is one of the most capable in Asia, with a dedicated live animal facility. The direct HKG-LHR service avoids transit handling, which reduces stress for the animal and eliminates the risk of a missed connection.

        Book the cargo space early. Cathay's live animal slots on the London route fill up, especially around summer when families relocating to the UK cluster their moves.

        Your pet travels in an IATA-compliant crate in the temperature-controlled cargo hold. Crate sizing matters. Cathay have specific crate dimension requirements and the crate must be the right size for the pet to stand, turn, and lie down. If you are buying a new crate, buy it well in advance and get your pet accustomed to it before the journey.
    - heading: "Tapeworm treatment: getting the timing right"
      body: |
        Dogs entering Great Britain must receive a tapeworm treatment between 24 and 120 hours before arriving. Cats do not need it.

        The timing is measured from the moment your dog lands at Heathrow, not from when it boards in Hong Kong. The direct HKG-LHR flight is approximately 13-14 hours. If your flight lands at Heathrow at 06:00 local time, count back 24 to 120 hours from that point.

        Most people administering the treatment in Hong Kong aim for 48-72 hours before the Heathrow landing time. That sits comfortably within the window without cutting it close. Your vet in Hong Kong records the treatment date and time in the AHC. APHA inspectors at Heathrow check this.
