content:
  h1: "Pet Transport from Hong Kong to the UK: Listed Country Advantage and AFCD Export"
  overview: |
    Hong Kong is on the UK's approved listed country and territory list, which means pets from Hong Kong can enter the UK without a titre test or quarantine. The requirements are: ISO microchip, current rabies vaccination, an AFCD live animal export licence, an AHC (Animal Health Certificate) in UK format endorsed by AFCD, and (for dogs only) a tapeworm treatment administered by a vet 1-5 days before UK arrival. Total preparation: 4-8 weeks. Source: UK Government, gov.uk; AFCD, afcd.gov.hk.

    Cathay Pacific Cargo operates direct services from Hong Kong (HKG) to London Heathrow (LHR), making this one of the most straightforwardly routed destinations for HK-based owners. British Airways World Cargo and Virgin Atlantic Cargo also operate this route.

    Comparing HK-to-UK with HK-to-New Zealand: New Zealand requires an FAVN titre test, a 180-day post-titre wait, and a 10-day Levin quarantine on arrival, with total preparation of 32-44 weeks. The UK listed country status removes these requirements entirely. For HK-based owners moving to an English-speaking destination, the UK's simpler process is a significant advantage.

  sections:
    - heading: "UK listed country status: what it means for Hong Kong pets"
      body: |
        The UK maintains a list of approved third countries and territories from which pets may enter without a titre test or post-vaccination waiting period. Hong Kong is on this list. This means that unlike pets from China, Vietnam, or Thailand (which all require a titre test and a 90-day EU waiting period for EU entry), pets from Hong Kong can enter the UK on the basis of a current rabies vaccination, microchip, and UK-format AHC. No titre test. No quarantine. Source: UK Government, gov.uk.

        Dogs must have a tapeworm treatment administered by a vet 1-5 days before UK arrival. The treatment (praziquantel or equivalent) must be recorded in the health certificate by the treating vet, with the exact date and time of administration. The UK Border Force inspects this at entry; the timing window is strict. Cats are not subject to the tapeworm requirement.

    - heading: "AFCD export licence and AHC: the Hong Kong process"
      body: |
        AFCD requires a live animal export licence for all pets leaving Hong Kong. Apply online via the AFCD licensing portal. Allow 2-3 weeks for processing. Once the licence is in place, the health certificate is issued by an APHA-listed official vet in Hong Kong in UK AHC format, endorsed by AFCD. The AHC must be issued within 10 days of UK arrival. The tapeworm treatment for dogs must also fall within the 1-5 day window before UK arrival, so coordinate the vet appointment carefully.

        Cathay Pacific Cargo from HKG to London Heathrow (LHR) is the most direct and commonly used carrier for this route. Cathay's live animal handling procedures for HKG-LHR are well-established. British Airways World Cargo and Virgin Atlantic Cargo also operate HKG-LHR. Entry into the UK for live animal imports must be via an approved POAO (Port of Official Authorised Border Inspection); London Heathrow (LHR) is the primary approved port.

    - heading: "HK-to-UK versus HK-to-New Zealand and HK-to-USA: a requirements comparison"
      body: |
        The listed country advantage makes the UK one of the simplest destinations for HK-based pet owners. UK: no titre test, no quarantine, total preparation 4-8 weeks. USA: CDC Dog Import Form required for dogs, current rabies vaccination, ISO microchip, no titre test, no quarantine, total preparation 6-10 weeks. New Zealand: titre test required, 180-day pre-travel wait, 10-day Levin quarantine, total preparation 32-44 weeks.

        For owners choosing between the UK and New Zealand as a destination, or whose primary concern is simplifying the move, the UK has a significant advantage. The USA is also simpler than New Zealand but requires the CDC Dog Import Form which adds a specific administrative step. Australia has varying requirements depending on origin country but generally also requires a titre test and longer preparation times.
