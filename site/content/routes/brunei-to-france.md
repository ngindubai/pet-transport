content:
  h1: "Pet Transport from Brunei to France"
  overview: |
    Two borders, one long flight, and a fixed 90-day clock: that's the shape of a Brunei-to-France pet move. Royal Brunei Airlines carries no live cargo for privately owned animals, so a road transfer to Singapore comes first, and only then does the EU's own process take over.

    France applies the same unlisted-country rules as its neighbours. Brunei doesn't sit on the bloc's list of low-risk third countries, so a titre test and a 90-day wait from the blood draw both apply before the pet can land at Charles de Gaulle.

  sections:
    - heading: "Getting out of Brunei: the Singapore transfer"
      body: |
        Brunei International Airport has no cargo product built for a privately owned pet moving long haul. In practice, owners cross by road into Singapore, a journey of a few hours, and pick up the international leg from Changi. Kuala Lumpur works too, using Malaysia Airlines Cargo for the domestic sector.

        Air France Cargo runs Singapore to Paris CDG direct, and the Roissy Animal Hotel, a joint Air France-KLM-Martinair Cargo facility, handles the live animal inspection on site rather than routing the pet through a general cargo shed.

    - heading: "France's EU unlisted-country process"
      body: |
        Brunei is outside the EU's list of low-risk third countries, so France applies the standard sequence under Commission Delegated Regulation (EU) 2026/131 and Implementing Regulation (EU) 2026/636: microchip before vaccination, a 30-day wait before the titre blood draw, a passing 0.5 IU/ml result, then a further 90 days from the draw date itself.

        DAA's own export endorsement runs alongside that wait rather than adding to it in most cases. The genuine time pressure on this route is the EU clock and the Singapore transfer logistics, not the Brunei paperwork.
