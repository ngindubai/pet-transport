  overview: |
    Tajikistan isn't on the UK's Part 1 or Part 2 list of rabies-listed countries. That single fact drives most of what follows: a mandatory rabies titre test, a 90-day wait after a successful result, and a Great Britain pet health certificate rather than a simple pet passport.

    There's also no direct flight. Pets travel via Istanbul on Turkish Airlines or via Almaty on Air Astana, both realistic options with genuine weekly capacity. Somon Air, the national carrier, doesn't fly to the UK yet, despite announcing plans to add a London route in July 2026.

    None of this makes the move impossible. It makes it a planning exercise with a hard floor of around 18 weeks, most of it driven by the titre test and its waiting periods rather than by paperwork you can rush.
  sections:
    - heading: "Why the titre test isn't optional here"
      body: |
        Some routes on this site let you skip the rabies blood test because the origin country is already on the UK's approved list. Tajikistan doesn't get that shortcut. As an unlisted country, every pet needs blood drawn at least 30 days after vaccination, tested at an APHA-approved FAVN or ELISA laboratory, with a result of at least 0.5 IU/ml.

        The 90-day wait after a successful result is fixed. It cannot be shortened by paying more or using a faster courier. Build your whole schedule around this date, then work backwards.

        Tajikistan doesn't have its own APHA-listed laboratory, based on current published lists. That usually means the blood sample gets couriered to an approved lab elsewhere, adding time most owners don't budget for on their first attempt.
    - heading: "Getting from Dushanbe to a UK airport"
      body: |
        Turkish Airlines runs roughly seven flights a week between Dushanbe and Istanbul, with onward connections to Heathrow, Gatwick and other UK airports through Turkish Cargo for larger pets or the cabin for small ones. Air Astana offers a genuine alternative: five flights a week from Dushanbe to Almaty, connecting to the airline's own direct Almaty-London Heathrow route.

        Compare both on price, transit time and how the airline actually handles pets at the connecting airport, not just on the headline fare. A pet transport agent with experience of Central Asian routing is worth the fee here, given how few options exist and how little English-language guidance Tajikistan's own authorities publish.

    answer: "Not currently. Somon Air, Tajikistan's national carrier, doesn't serve the UK. Pets travel via Istanbul on Turkish Airlines or via Almaty on Air Astana, both of which offer weekly capacity and onward UK connections."

    answer: "The blood draw happens at least 30 days after vaccination, then there's a fixed 90-day wait after a successful result before travel. Add time for courier delivery to an approved laboratory outside Tajikistan, since no local lab appears on the current APHA-approved list."
  - question: "Does my dog need tapeworm treatment for the UK?"
    answer: "Yes. Dogs need a praziquantel or equivalent treatment 24 to 120 hours before their scheduled UK arrival time, recorded on the health certificate by the treating vet. Cats and ferrets are exempt."
  - question: "What happens if my pet's documents aren't right on arrival?"
    answer: "There's no routine quarantine for pets with fully compliant paperwork. If something's wrong, whether that's timing, vaccination gaps or an incorrect travel route, penalty quarantine of up to 120 days can apply, and the owner pays for it."
