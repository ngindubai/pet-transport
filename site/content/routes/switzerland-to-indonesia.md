  sections:
    - heading: "The rekomendasi permit: why it sets the whole calendar"
      body: |
        Barantin's rekomendasi (formally the Surat Izin Pemasukan) works in two stages. First, a provincial recommendation letter from the destination province's animal husbandry office confirms your Indonesian address is ready to receive the pet. Second, the national application goes to the Directorate of Animal Health in Jakarta, which reviews the full document set including your FAVN titre result.

        The permit names the pet individually by breed, colour, sex and microchip number, and it names the entry port. If your permit says Jakarta and your flight lands in Surabaya, the paperwork does not cover that arrival. Once issued, it is valid for exactly 30 days, so travel dates need to be fixed only after the permit clears, not before.

        Allow 4 to 8 weeks for the two stages combined. A Swiss vet with international export experience can prepare documents in parallel with the application, but the sequence itself cannot be shortened: the titre result has to exist before the national stage will proceed.
    - heading: "Getting a Swiss dog or cat to Jakarta without a direct flight"
      body: |
        No airline flies Zurich to Jakarta non-stop, so every option runs through a hub. Qatar Airways Cargo via Doha and Emirates SkyCargo via Dubai are the two most consistently used for this corridor out of ZRH. Singapore Airlines Cargo via Singapore is worth comparing too, since the final SIN-CGK leg is short, which matters for keeping total transit time down for an anxious traveller.

        Swiss WorldCargo handles the departure leg from Zurich and interlines the crate to whichever carrier completes the Jakarta leg. Confirm the connecting carrier accepts live animals on both legs of your specific dates well before booking, since seasonal heat embargoes at Jakarta can affect acceptance during the hottest months.
