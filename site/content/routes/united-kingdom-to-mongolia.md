  h1: "Pet Transport from the United Kingdom to Mongolia"
  overview: |
    Mongolia asks less of an arriving pet than almost any country on this site. No titre test. No import permit for a personal animal. A microchip, a rabies jab given within the last year, and the standard UK Export Health Certificate cover it.

    The catch sits entirely on the flight side. Ulaanbaatar has no direct link to London, so the pet travels via Frankfurt or Istanbul on MIAT Mongolian Airlines' own long-haul network, or via Istanbul on Turkish Airlines. Both routings work; neither has much spare capacity, so book weeks earlier than the medical timeline alone would demand.

    Worth flagging early if a return trip is ever on the cards: the ease of this route runs one way. Mongolia is unlisted under UK rules, so a pet coming back faces the titre test and 3-month wait that this outbound leg entirely skips.

  sections:
    - heading: "Why the import side is unusually light"
      body: |
        Compare this route with almost any other unlisted-country move on this site and the difference is stark. A pet arriving in Mongolia needs a valid microchip, a rabies vaccination from the last 12 months, and the UK's own Export Health Certificate. That's it. No titre blood test, no waiting period, no separate import permit application for an owner travelling with their own animal.

        The trade-off is that Mongolia's own English-language guidance on pet import is thin, and there's no substitute for confirming the current position with GAVS or a receiving vet close to your travel date. Straightforward rules can still change without much international notice.

    - heading: "The flight is the real project"
      body: |
        MIAT Mongolian Airlines flies its own Boeing 787s between Ulaanbaatar and both Frankfurt and Istanbul, either of which connects onward from a UK departure. Turkish Airlines offers a genuine alternative through Istanbul, keeping the pet on a single carrier's handling chain from London through to Ulaanbaatar rather than transferring between MIAT and a partner mid-route.

        If your pet is a brachycephalic breed, check MIAT's cargo hold exclusion list before assuming either carrier works. It runs to 18 breeds, wider than most airlines' equivalent lists, and includes dogs like Cane Corso and Dogue de Bordeaux that owners don't always flag as flat-faced.

    - heading: "One-way ease: what changes on a return trip"
      body: |
        It's tempting to read Mongolia's light import rules and assume the whole corridor is simple. It isn't, if the pet is ever coming back. The UK classifies Mongolia as unlisted, the same category as India or China, which means a rabies titre test and a fixed 3-month wait apply on re-entry regardless of how uneventful the outward journey was.

        For anyone on a fixed-term posting who knows the pet is returning to the UK eventually, it's worth starting that titre test process in Mongolia well before the return date, rather than treating it as a problem for later.
