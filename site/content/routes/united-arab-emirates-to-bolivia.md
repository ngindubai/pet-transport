content:
  h1: "Pet Transport from United Arab Emirates to Bolivia"
  overview: |
    Search enough sources on Bolivia's diplomatic footprint in the UAE and you get two different answers. Some list an embassy in Abu Dhabi. Others say Bolivia has no mission there at all. That inconsistency matters here, because every other Bolivia-bound route in this build routes its exported health certificate through a Bolivian consulate in the country of departure.

    Everything else about this move is comparatively light. No titre test, no quarantine for a compliant arrival, just a current rabies vaccination and SENASAG's import permit. The open question is where the certificate gets its final legalisation stamp, and that is worth resolving with SENASAG directly before a travel date gets fixed.

  sections:
    - heading: "An open question worth settling early"
      body: |
        Two official-adjacent pictures of the same fact don't resolve themselves. Rather than assume either is right, write to SENASAG, or Bolivia's Ministry of Foreign Affairs, and ask plainly whether a document legalisation route currently exists inside the UAE, and if not, where the nearest one sits. Owners who have moved pets from the UK or the US into Bolivia went through a consulate appointment or a postal process respectively. The UAE route doesn't yet have a confirmed equivalent.

    - heading: "One stop, not three"
      body: |
        The flight side is more settled than the paperwork side. Emirates flies nonstop between Dubai and Sao Paulo, one of its longest routes on the network, which then connects to Santa Cruz Viru Viru on BoA or Gol. That's a genuine one-stop itinerary for a Gulf-to-Bolivia pet move, shorter than most Gulf-to-South-America routings this build has covered, which typically need Madrid or Miami as an intermediate hub before a further South American connection.

    - heading: "What Bolivia doesn't ask for"
      body: |
        No rabies titre test, regardless of country of origin. No fixed waiting period tied to a blood draw. That's a real point of contrast with the reverse direction, where a pet moving from Bolivia into the UAE faces MOCCAE's full unlisted-country protocol, titre test included. Coming into Bolivia, the vaccination record and the right paperwork do the job on their own.
