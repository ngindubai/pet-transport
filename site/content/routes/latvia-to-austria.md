    - heading: "airBaltic: the direct route from Riga to Vienna"
      body: |
        airBaltic operates direct flights from Riga (RIX) to Vienna (VIE), making this one of the more convenient Latvia routes for pet owners. Small pets (up to 8kg including the carrier) can travel in the cabin with you. Larger dogs travel in the cargo hold, which is pressurised and temperature-controlled.

        Book your pet's cabin place at the same time as your own ticket. airBaltic limits the number of pets per flight, and popular routes fill quickly. For cargo bookings, contact airBaltic Cargo directly or use a specialist pet transport agent.

        Lufthansa and Austrian Airlines are alternatives if the direct airBaltic schedule does not suit. These route via Frankfurt or other hubs but offer more departure time flexibility on some days.

    - heading: "EU pet passport from a Latvian vet"
      body: |
        Latvia's Food and Veterinary Service (PVD) authorises vets to issue EU pet passports. Any authorised Latvian vet can create the passport once your pet's microchip has been scanned and recorded, and once vaccinations are up to date. The passport is valid for the lifetime of your pet; what matters is that the rabies vaccination recorded in it stays current.

        If you are making a one-way move to Austria, an Animal Health Certificate (AHC) is a practical alternative. It is issued by an official vet and must be endorsed within 10 days of your arrival in Austria. The 10-day window starts on the endorsement date, so time the appointment to land within 10 days of your flight.

        For anyone who plans to travel between EU countries in future, the EU pet passport is the better investment.

    - heading: "Austrian state breed regulations"
      body: |
        Austria operates without a federal breed ban, which is good news for owners of more powerful breeds. However, each of the nine Bundesländer (federal states) has its own rules, and these vary considerably. Vienna requires a competency test (Sachkundenachweis) for certain breeds before a dog may be kept in the city. Other states have different lists or no restrictions at all.

        Check the specific rules for your destination state before you travel. If you are relocating to Vienna, check with the Vienna city authority. For other states, the state government websites publish their animal welfare and dog-keeping laws.

        For most dogs and for all cats, Austrian state rules are unlikely to be a barrier. The standard EU entry requirements are what matter for the journey itself.

  - question: "Do I need a titre test to bring my dog from Latvia to Austria?"
    answer: "No. Titre tests are only required for travel from outside the EU (or certain listed countries) to an EU destination. Latvia and Austria are both EU member states, so no titre test is needed."
  - question: "How does an AHC differ from an EU pet passport?"
    answer: "An EU pet passport is a permanent document issued once and valid for the animal's lifetime (as long as vaccinations are kept current). An AHC is a single-use document valid for one trip only, endorsed within 10 days of entry. The EU passport is more convenient for repeat travellers; the AHC is suitable for a one-way move."
  - question: "Are there breed restrictions I need to know about before taking my dog to Vienna?"
    answer: "Vienna requires owners of certain breeds (including Rottweilers and several bully-type breeds) to pass a Sachkundenachweis (competency assessment) and have their dog assessed. Check the Vienna city authority website before travelling with a restricted breed."
