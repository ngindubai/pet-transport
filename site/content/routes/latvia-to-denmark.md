  sections:
    - heading: "EU Pet Passport: What It Records and Who Can Issue It"
      body: |
        The EU pet passport is the single document that covers your pet for travel between Latvia and Denmark. It is issued by an official veterinarian authorised by the Partikas un veterinarais dienests (PVD), Latvia's national food and veterinary service. Not every private vet holds this authorisation, so check before booking your appointment.

        The passport records your pet's microchip number, vaccination history (including rabies), and the issuing vet's details. Danish border authorities check that the microchip number matches the passport, the rabies vaccination is current, and the 21-day post-vaccination wait has passed. Keep the passport accessible throughout your journey. Source: Fodevarestyrelsen (fvst.dk).

    - heading: "Flying from Riga to Copenhagen: Choosing the Right Airline"
      body: |
        airBaltic offers the most direct connection between Riga (RIX) and Copenhagen Kastrup (CPH), with multiple daily departures. For small cats and dogs that fit in an approved carrier under the seat, the cabin option is usually the least stressful. Cabin size and weight limits vary by airline, so confirm your pet's dimensions before booking.

        Larger dogs travel in the hold as checked cargo. airBaltic and SAS both handle this, but you must book the live animal space separately when you buy your ticket, not at the airport. Ryanair offers budget fares on this route but does not take cargo animals. LOT Polish Airlines via Warsaw is an alternative if direct options are full. Give yourself 4 to 6 weeks to arrange everything.

faqs:
  - question: "Do I need a titre test to move my pet from Latvia to Denmark?"
    answer: "No. Latvia and Denmark are both EU member states. No rabies antibody titre test is required. Your pet needs a microchip, a current rabies vaccination (at least 21 days old), and an EU pet passport."
  - question: "Does my dog need a tapeworm treatment to enter Denmark from Latvia?"
    answer: "No. Denmark does not require tapeworm treatment for pets arriving from EU member states. This is a requirement for Norway, not Denmark. Check the specific requirements for any country you are transiting through if your route includes a stopover."
  - question: "Can I take my cat in the cabin on a flight from Riga to Copenhagen?"
    answer: "Yes, on most airlines that allow cabin pets on this route, including airBaltic. Your cat must be in an airline-approved soft carrier that fits under the seat in front of you. Check the airline's current weight and size limits when booking, as these vary. Book the cabin pet space at the same time as your ticket."
  - question: "How far in advance do I need to plan a Latvia to Denmark pet move?"
    answer: "If your pet's rabies vaccination is current and the EU pet passport is already issued, you can travel with as little as a few days' notice (allowing time to book the airline pet space). If you need a new vaccination, allow at least 21 days for the wait period. Booking airline pet spaces 4 to 8 weeks ahead is advisable."
