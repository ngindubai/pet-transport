content:
  h1: "Pet Transport from Iceland to Japan"
  overview: |
    Every other route into Japan on this site runs on the same spine: two rabies vaccinations, a titre test, and a 180-day wait from the blood draw. Iceland skips all of it. As one of AQS's designated regions, a pet that has lived in Iceland continuously for at least 180 days, or since birth, needs no rabies vaccination and no titre test at all to enter Japan.

    What's left is genuinely light by this site's standards: a microchip, an advance notification to AQS at least 40 days before arrival, and a health certificate confirming the residency history. Get the timing right and the pet clears the airport inspection in around 12 hours.

  sections:
    - heading: "What designated region status actually removes"
      body: |
        Japan groups a handful of countries, Iceland, Australia, New Zealand, Fiji, Hawaii and Guam, as regions it trusts enough to waive the standard rabies protocol entirely for a genuinely resident pet. That's a different, much shorter list than the UK's approved-country system or MAST's own Category 1 tier, and it removes both the vaccination course and the titre test other Iceland-outbound routes on this site still require for their destinations.

        This isn't a loophole. It reflects Iceland's own decades of controlled, quarantine-backed borders, the same policy that makes every incoming pet spend 14 days in isolation on the way in.
    - heading: "The 180-day clock, and what breaks it"
      body: |
        The waiver hinges entirely on residency, not nationality or passport. A pet has to have lived in Iceland for 180 consecutive days, or since birth, for AQS to waive the rabies protocol. Move a pet into Iceland from elsewhere and try to fly it on to Japan within that window, and the waiver simply doesn't apply; the standard, much longer process takes over instead.

        Keep the paperwork trail clean. AQS checks dates carefully, and the residency evidence needs to line up with the microchip and MAST's certificate without gaps.
    - heading: "Getting to Tokyo without a direct flight"
      body: |
        Icelandair's network stops well short of Japan, so this route runs through a European hub, typically connecting to Japan Airlines or ANA for the long-haul leg into Tokyo. Two cargo bookings, not one, and each carrier has its own paperwork and cut-off times.

        Submit the AQS advance notification early. The 40-day window is measured to the actual Tokyo arrival date, so a late connection booking can push the notification uncomfortably close to the deadline.
