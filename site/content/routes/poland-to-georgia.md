  h1: "Pet Transport from Poland to Georgia"
  overview: |
    Georgia is one of the more accessible Caucasus destinations for Polish pet owners. No titre test is required from EU member states, there is no quarantine on arrival, and the import permit requirement does not apply to personal pets. The NFSA (National Food Agency, nfa.gov.ge) handles veterinary inspection at Tbilisi International Airport (TBS), and their process is generally efficient for well-documented arrivals.

    The route runs through Istanbul, with Turkish Airlines as the primary carrier for live animal cargo from Warsaw Chopin Airport (WAW) to Tbilisi. Polish owners have the advantage of Poland's EU membership: the GIW health certificate format is familiar to Georgian authorities, and the documentation process follows a clear, known path through the district PIW system.

    The key discipline is timing. Georgia's 21-day-to-11-months rabies window, combined with the 10-day validity of the Polish health certificate, requires careful coordination. Plan the sequence of vaccinations, PIW appointment, and travel date before doing anything else.

  sections:
    - heading: "The Polish Export Side: PIW Appointments and GIW Certificates"
      body: |
        Exporting a pet from Poland follows the GIW system. The GIW (Glowny Inspektorat Weterynarii at wetgiw.gov.pl) is the national authority, but the certificate that travels with your pet is issued by the Powiatowy Lekarz Weterynarii at your local PIW (Powiatowy Inspektorat Weterynarii) district office. Every district in Poland has one.

        No export permit is needed for personal pets. Your private vet handles the EU pet passport, vaccinations, and microchipping. Then you book an appointment at the PIW office, where the Powiatowy Lekarz Weterynarii examines your pet, scans the microchip, and cross-checks everything against the EU passport before endorsing the official health certificate. The whole appointment usually takes under an hour if the documentation is in order.

        The certificate is valid for 10 days from the date of examination. This is the window you have to travel. Book the PIW appointment so that it falls within 10 days of your actual departure. If you have a firm travel booking with a date that is confirmed, work backwards from that date to schedule the PIW appointment.

    - heading: "Georgia's NFSA: What the Inspectors Check at TBS"
      body: |
        Georgia's food safety and veterinary authority, the NFSA (National Food Agency, nfa.gov.ge), carries out import inspections at Tbilisi International Airport. Their officers check three things: the microchip scan, the vaccination record showing a valid rabies vaccine (21 days to 11 months before arrival), and the official health certificate issued within the past 10 days.

        No titre test is required from Poland. Georgia recognises EU-origin animals as lower risk for rabies, and the standard vaccination record is accepted. No import permit is needed for personal pets arriving in Georgia. Provided your documents are in order and the dates add up, the NFSA inspection at TBS is a straightforward process.

        The NFSA does update its requirements periodically. The information here is based on current published guidance from nfa.gov.ge, but confirm the current requirements directly with NFSA or via a pet relocation specialist before your travel date.

    - heading: "Getting from Warsaw to Tbilisi with Your Pet"
      body: |
        Turkish Airlines is the standard carrier for this journey. The routing is Warsaw Chopin (WAW) to Istanbul (IST), then IST to Tbilisi (TBS). Turkish Airlines accepts live animals in cargo on both sectors, subject to crate dimensions, species restrictions, and seasonal availability. Contact Turkish Airlines Cargo directly to confirm current live animal availability on your specific dates and routing.

        LOT Polish Airlines, Warsaw's home carrier, operates cargo from WAW and can serve as the first leg to Istanbul, connecting to Turkish Airlines for the TBS sector. If you are travelling from a city other than Warsaw, LOT connects domestic Polish airports to WAW, which then serves as the departure point for international cargo.

        All pets on this route travel as checked cargo, in IATA-compliant crates. The crate must be rigid, ventilated on at least three sides, and large enough for the animal to stand, turn, and lie down without restriction. Buy or source the crate early so your pet has time to become comfortable with it before the journey.

    answer: "No. Georgia does not require an import permit for personal pets. The health certificate from Poland's PIW system and a valid EU pet passport are sufficient. The NFSA inspection at TBS on arrival is the final checkpoint."

    answer: "PIW (Powiatowy Inspektorat Weterynarii) offices exist at district level across Poland. Search for your local PIW by district name or contact the GIW (Glowny Inspektorat Weterynarii) via wetgiw.gov.pl for a directory. Call the PIW office directly to book the health certificate examination appointment."
