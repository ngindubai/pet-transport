content:
  h1: "Pet Transport from Greece to Portugal"
  overview: |
    Greece and Portugal are both EU member states, which makes this one of the most manageable international pet moves you can undertake. No import permits, no quarantine, no embassy endorsement process.

    Your pet needs a valid EU Pet Passport with a current rabies vaccination and a readable ISO microchip. If everything is already in order, you could be ready to travel within a couple of weeks. The main bottleneck is booking cabin pet space on your chosen airline before it fills up.

    Portugal's DGAV handles animal import matters, but for intra-EU movement they defer entirely to EU Regulation 2019/2035.
  sections:
    - heading: "Portugal's requirements for EU companion animal arrivals"
      body: |
        Portugal follows EU Regulation 2019/2035 for companion animals arriving from other EU states. What that means in practice: valid EU Pet Passport, ISO microchip recorded within it, and a current rabies vaccination.

        Portugal's DGAV (dgav.pt) can be contacted for queries about specific breed restrictions or if your pet has any unusual vaccination history. Standard dogs and cats travelling with a clean EU Pet Passport will not encounter problems.

    - heading: "Preparing your EU Pet Passport in Greece"
      body: |
        The EU Pet Passport is standardised across all member states, but it must be issued or updated by a vet authorised by the Greek Ministry of Rural Development and Food.

        If your pet's passport is from another EU country, it remains valid. You do not need a new Greek passport unless you want to update records.

        Check the rabies vaccination date. The vaccination is valid from 21 days after administration. Annual boosters are needed to maintain continuous validity. There is no grace period after expiry.

    - heading: "Flying Greece to Portugal with a pet"
      body: |
        There are no direct Greece-Portugal flights in most schedules. The most common connections are via Lisbon on TAP, via Barcelona on Vueling, or via Frankfurt on Lufthansa.

        TAP Air Portugal is the most convenient carrier for this destination and accepts cabin pets (up to 8kg including carrier). Book the pet space at the same time as your ticket.

        For larger dogs, cargo is the only option. Lufthansa Cargo and KLM Cargo are reliable operators on European routes. Both use temperature-controlled holds.
