  sections:
    - heading: "Azerbaijan's import permit: how to apply"
      body: |
        Azerbaijan's State Veterinary Service, operating under the Ministry of Agriculture, issues the veterinary import permit required for all companion animals entering the country. There is no online portal for Canadian applicants; contact the State Veterinary Service directly to request the application process and a list of required documents.

        Typically, you will need to provide your pet's microchip number, vaccination history, your passport details, the intended entry date, and the entry airport (Heydar Aliyev International, GYD). Allow eight to ten weeks for the process and follow up if you do not receive an acknowledgement within two weeks of applying.

        The permit is presented together with the CFIA health certificate at GYD on arrival. The State Veterinary Service inspection team checks both documents alongside a microchip scan and vaccination record review.

    - heading: "CFIA process for Canada-Azerbaijan travel"
      body: |
        CFIA-accredited vets across Canada can issue health certificates for export to Azerbaijan. Find a CFIA-accredited vet through inspection.canada.ca and book an appointment within 10 days of your travel date, as the certificate validity is tied to that window.

        After the vet appointment, the certificate typically needs to be endorsed by the CFIA regional office in your province. This adds two to five business days to the process depending on your province and the time of year. If you are in Ontario, the CFIA regional office in Guelph handles international export endorsements. Quebec, British Columbia, and other provinces have their own regional offices.

        Contact your CFIA regional office before your vet appointment to confirm what they need from you and how to submit. Having everything ready in advance avoids last-minute delays.

    - heading: "Baku as a destination: what to expect at GYD"
      body: |
        Baku's Heydar Aliyev International Airport (GYD) is a modern facility and handles international cargo arrivals regularly. The State Veterinary Service maintains an inspection presence at GYD for arriving animals.

        On arrival, your pet's documents are checked, the microchip is scanned, and the inspection team clears the animal if everything is in order. For compliant arrivals, there is no quarantine. The inspection is a formal process but not an extended one for well-documented pets.

        Baku is a growing destination for expatriates, and the import of companion animals is a familiar process for the Azerbaijani authorities. Present your documents clearly organised and the process should go smoothly.
