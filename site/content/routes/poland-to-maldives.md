content:
  h1: "Pet Transport from Poland to Maldives"
  overview: |
    Warsaw is one of the handful of European cities with a scheduled, non-stop flight to Male, flown by LOT Polish Airlines rather than routed through Dubai or Doha like most of the continent. It's a genuinely useful fact for the journey itself, and one worth knowing before assuming a Gulf hub is unavoidable.

    It changes nothing for a dog. The Maldives prohibits dog imports outright under Maldives Customs Service rules, and no airline or routing creates an exception. A cat, on the other hand, has a manageable pathway: microchip, rabies vaccination 30 days to 12 months before arrival, the core feline vaccines, and a certificate signed within 10 days of travel.

  sections:
    - heading: "The direct flight, and its limits"
      body: |
        LOT's Warsaw-Male service is a scheduled flag-carrier route, not a seasonal charter, though how often it runs each week has shifted between years. It's worth checking the current timetable before assuming it covers your travel dates, since a route that flies several times a week in peak season can thin out considerably at other times of year.
    - heading: "The dog rule the flight can't change"
      body: |
        There is no confirmed government pathway for a dog to enter the Maldives, including as an assistance animal, based on any primary Maldivian source we could find. Some forum posts describe informal workarounds; without a government document backing that up as something repeatable, we're not treating it as one here.
    - heading: "What the cat process actually involves"
      body: |
        Microchip first, then rabies vaccination in the window Maldivian guidance sets out, plus the standard feline vaccines. A certificate signed within 10 days of entry finishes the file. Whether a general import permit or titre test applies to a pet cat travelling with its owner is genuinely unresolved between sources, so treat both as open questions your vet or agent needs to settle directly.
    - heading: "A pedigree detail for exotic-pattern cats"
      body: |
        Savannah and Bengal cats need to be at least five generations removed from their wild ancestor before the Maldives treats them as an ordinary domestic cat, per guidance summarised by PetTravel.com. Most pet-market animals of these breeds clear that bar, but check the paperwork before booking if it applies to yours.
