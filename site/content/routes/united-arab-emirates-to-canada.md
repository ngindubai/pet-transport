  overview: |
    The UAE to Canada route is one of the more straightforward international pet moves available from the Gulf. Canada's CFIA (Canadian Food Inspection Agency) has clear and manageable requirements for personal pet imports: a valid health certificate, a current rabies vaccination for dogs, and no titre test or quarantine. There are no fixed waiting periods after vaccination, so preparation time is relatively short.

    The export side in the UAE requires a health certificate from a government-approved vet, endorsed by the relevant authority (Dubai Municipality for Dubai-based pets, or MOCCAE equivalent for other emirates). The endorsed certificate is the key document, and the most important timing rule is that it must be issued within 10 days of your pet's arrival in Canada, not 10 days from when you depart the UAE.

    Emirates operates a direct cargo service from Dubai (DXB) to Toronto Pearson (YYZ), which is the primary route for UAE-to-Canada pet moves. From Abu Dhabi, Etihad Cargo is the main option. Allow 4-8 weeks from starting preparation to travel day if your pet is already vaccinated and microchipped.
  sections:
    - heading: "Canada's CFIA import requirements from the UAE"
      body: |
        Canada's federal authority for pet imports is the CFIA (Canadian Food Inspection Agency, inspection.gc.ca). For personal pet imports (1-2 animals brought in by their owner, not for sale or breeding), the requirements are manageable and do not include a titre test or quarantine period.

        Dogs aged 3 months or older must arrive with proof of a current rabies vaccination. The vaccination record must be included in the health certificate. Cats have no rabies vaccination requirement for import to Canada from the UAE. In both cases, the health certificate must come from a UAE government-approved vet and be endorsed by the relevant UAE authority before departure.

        No import permit is required for personal imports. At the Canadian port of entry (typically Toronto for direct Emirates flights), a CBSA officer and potentially a CFIA inspector will review the documentation. For compliant pets with all paperwork in order, the entry inspection is usually brief.
    - heading: "Exporting from the UAE: health certificate and endorsement process"
      body: |
        The UAE does not require a separate export permit for cats and dogs. The key document on the UAE side is the official health certificate, issued by a government-approved veterinarian and then endorsed by the relevant emirate authority. For Dubai-based pets, this endorsement comes from Dubai Municipality's veterinary services. For Abu Dhabi and other emirates, the process goes through MOCCAE or the relevant local authority. Source: MOCCAE, moccae.gov.ae.

        The certificate must state your pet's description, microchip number (if applicable), vaccination records and a declaration that the animal is clinically healthy. The critical timing point is that this certificate must be issued within 10 days of your pet's arrival in Canada. If you book a flight with a long layover or a multi-day connection, factor that into when the certificate is prepared.

        Book your vet appointment and endorsement appointment back-to-back to minimise the window between certificate issue and Canadian arrival. Your pet transport agent can coordinate this timing if you are using one.
    - heading: "Emirates Dubai to Toronto: the main cargo option"
      body: |
        Emirates SkyCargo operates direct cargo services between Dubai (DXB) and Toronto Pearson (YYZ). This is the principal routing for UAE-to-Canada pet moves and has an established process for live animal bookings. Pets travel as manifested cargo in a pressurised, temperature-controlled hold. Book through an approved Emirates cargo agent rather than through the passenger ticket system.

        Live animal cargo space is separate from passenger baggage and needs to be reserved and confirmed independently. The IATA-compliant crate must be sized correctly for your pet: the animal must be able to stand, turn around and lie down without touching the crate sides. Confirm the approved crate dimensions with the cargo agent before purchasing a crate.

        Temperature embargoes can apply during Gulf summer months when ground temperatures exceed safe thresholds, typically June to September. If you are travelling in this window, confirm availability early and have a backup date in mind. From Abu Dhabi, Etihad Cargo is the equivalent option. Connecting routes via European hubs (Lufthansa via Frankfurt, British Airways via London) are also available if direct booking is not possible for your dates.
