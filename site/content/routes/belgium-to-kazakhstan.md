  sections:
    - heading: "Kazakhstan's rules for EU-origin companion pets"
      body: |
        Personal companion pets from Belgium can enter Kazakhstan with a valid FASFC-endorsed EU health certificate, current microchip, and up-to-date rabies vaccination. No titre test is required under personal import rules from Belgium.

        One important point: Kazakhstan is part of the EAEU (Eurasian Economic Union) and uses the EAEU veterinary certificate format for intra-EAEU movements. For imports from EU countries like Belgium, the EU-format health certificate endorsed by FASFC is generally accepted, but some Kazakhstani port officials may also be familiar with the EAEU format. It is worth confirming the preferred document format with the Kazakhstani Embassy in Brussels or with an experienced IPATA agent before travel, to avoid any confusion on arrival.

        The Ministry of Agriculture's Veterinary Committee conducts the inspection at Almaty (ALA) or Astana (NQZ) cargo terminal. Compliant pets are released without quarantine.

    - heading: "Choosing between Almaty and Astana"
      body: |
        Kazakhstan has two major international airports with cargo facilities. Almaty International Airport (ALA) in the south is the busiest and has been the traditional entry point for pet cargo. Astana International Airport (NQZ, formerly Nur-Sultan Nazarbayev International Airport) serves the capital and has seen increased traffic since the capital was moved.

        If your final destination is Almaty city or southern Kazakhstan, ALA is the obvious choice. If you are moving to Astana or northern Kazakhstan, NQZ makes more sense.

        Air Astana serves both ALA and NQZ from Amsterdam Schiphol. Turkish Airlines has routes to both airports. Confirm cargo acceptance and seasonal availability on each before finalising your destination airport.

    - heading: "Getting from Belgium to the Air Astana flight at Amsterdam"
      body: |
        Air Astana does not fly from Brussels directly. The airline operates from Amsterdam Schiphol (AMS). From Belgium, reaching AMS is straightforward: Eurostar and Thalys trains connect Brussels Midi station to Amsterdam Centraal in under two hours, or a short domestic flight on Brussels Airlines or another carrier.

        For cargo, the BRU to AMS leg adds a small complexity but is manageable. Your IPATA agent can organise the full routing. Alternatively, Turkish Airlines or Lufthansa Cargo from BRU are simpler if you prefer a single-origin routing from Brussels.
