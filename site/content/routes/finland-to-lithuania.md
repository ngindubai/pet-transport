    - heading: "Getting your pet from Helsinki to Vilnius"
      body: |
        Helsinki-Vantaa (HEL) to Vilnius (VNO) is approximately 2 hours by air. Finnair serves this route on some direct schedules, making it the most convenient option. For cabin pets, Finnair's standard policies apply. For large dogs, Finnair Cargo at HEL handles the booking.

        The airBaltic connection via Riga is an alternative. RIX to VNO is around 50 minutes, and airBaltic's home hub in Riga provides reliable cargo handling. The total journey time via RIX adds an hour or so, but the additional connection means you have more scheduling flexibility.

        Ryanair operates HEL to VNO on some schedules but accepts only cabin pets. If your dog or cat is within the cabin size limits and you're comfortable with Ryanair's pet policy, this is a budget option. For anything larger, it's not suitable.

    - heading: "What VMVT requires and what Finland's tapeworm rules mean for this move"
      body: |
        One point that causes genuine confusion for owners moving from Finland: Finland requires tapeworm treatment for dogs entering Finland from certain countries. But this inbound requirement does not apply in reverse. Dogs leaving Finland for Lithuania do not need any Echinococcus treatment, and Lithuania does not require it on arrival.

        VMVT (Lithuania's State Food and Veterinary Service) requires the EU pet passport with a valid ISO microchip and current rabies vaccination. That's the entry requirement. No import permit, no titre test, no quarantine, no tapeworm treatment.

        Lithuania does have PPP breed regulations. If you own a large guarding or bull-type breed, a quick check of VMVT's breed list before travel is sensible. For most owners moving a standard family dog or cat, this step can be skipped.
