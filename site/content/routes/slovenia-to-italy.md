  sections:
    - heading: "Overland from Ljubljana to Italy: when it makes sense"
      body: |
        For most European pet transport routes, flying is the default because the distances are too large for a comfortable drive. Slovenia to Italy is the exception. The border crossing from Slovenia into Italy near Trieste or Gorizia is under 2 hours from Ljubljana, and the drive through the Karst plateau into northeast Italy is straightforward.

        If your Italian destination is Venice, Trieste, Padua, or any destination in northeast Italy, driving is faster and less stressful for your pet than flying. There are no pet transport restrictions on the overland route: EU pet passport checks by Italian border police are occasional spot checks rather than routine stops.

        For southern Italy destinations (Rome, Naples, Sicily), flying makes more sense. Austrian Airlines via Vienna or Lufthansa via Frankfurt are the practical options from Ljubljana Airport (LJU), with connections to Rome Fiumicino (FCO) or Milan Malpensa (MXP).

    - heading: "Flying from Ljubljana: knowing Ryanair won't work"
      body: |
        Ryanair is the dominant carrier from Ljubljana Joze Pucnik Airport (LJU), with several direct routes to Italian airports including Rome Ciampino and other regional airports. But Ryanair accepts no live animals: not in the cabin, not as checked baggage, and not as cargo. If you are flying from Ljubljana, Ryanair is off the table for your pet.

        Austrian Airlines connects LJU to Vienna (VIE), which then connects to most major Italian cities. For small pets, the cabin option on Austrian Airlines to VIE with an onward Austrian or Lufthansa connection to Italy is comfortable and manageable. For large dogs, Lufthansa via Frankfurt gives access to better cargo facilities at FRA for the connecting leg to Italy.

faqs:
  - question: "Can I drive from Slovenia to Italy with my dog without any paperwork?"
    answer: "You need an EU pet passport (or AHC) to prove your dog is microchipped and vaccinated. Italian border checks for EU pets are spot checks but if your dog is checked and you cannot produce a valid EU passport with current vaccination, you risk problems. Carry the original EU passport, not a copy."
  - question: "Does Italy require a titre test for dogs entering from Slovenia?"
    answer: "No. Both Slovenia and Italy are EU member states. EU-to-EU pet movements do not require a rabies titre test. Current rabies vaccination, ISO microchip, and EU passport or AHC are the only requirements."
  - question: "What is Italian Anagrafe Canina and do I need to register my dog?"
    answer: "Anagrafe Canina is Italy's national dog registry. All dogs resident in Italy must be registered with the local ASL (Azienda Sanitaria Locale) veterinary department. After arrival, visit the ASL vet in your municipality to register your dog. The registration is free and links to the microchip number. Failure to register can result in fines."
  - question: "Are there restrictions on my breed in Italy?"
    answer: "Italy's national breed-specific legislation has changed in recent years and some previous restrictions have been lifted. However, individual municipalities may still have local rules for certain dog types. American Pit Bull Terriers and similar types may require muzzle and lead in public in some areas. Confirm your breed's status with the Italian Ministry of Health guidance and your specific Italian municipality before travel."
