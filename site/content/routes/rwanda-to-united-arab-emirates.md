content:
  h1: "Pet Transport from Rwanda to the UAE"
  overview: |
    RwandAir flies a dog or cat straight from Kigali to Dubai without a single stop, one of only a handful of direct cargo corridors in this build. That fact alone changes the maths on this move: no connecting airport, no second set of cargo handlers, no extra chance of a missed transfer somewhere in the Gulf.

    The paperwork runs through two authorities. Rwanda's Agriculture and Animal Resources Development Board, RAB, signs off on the export health certificate before departure. On the UAE side, the Ministry of Climate Change and Environment, MOCCAE, treats Rwanda as outside its short low-risk country list, so the full sequence applies: microchip, rabies vaccination, a rabies antibody titre test, and an online import permit.

    Here's the detail that trips people up if they researched EU routes first. MOCCAE does not hold pets to a fixed wait after the titre test blood draw. The EU does: 90 days, no exceptions. The UAE checks the result itself instead, valid for 365 days as long as vaccination stays current. That difference alone can save months of planning.

  sections:
    - heading: "MOCCAE's risk category and what it means for a Rwandan pet"
      body: |
        Rwanda sits outside MOCCAE's short low-risk country list, so a pet travelling from Kigali needs the full import sequence: a microchip fitted before vaccination, a rabies vaccination current at least 21 days if it's the primary dose, and a rabies antibody titre test at an accredited laboratory returning a minimum of 0.5 IU/ml. The import permit, applied for online, runs for 90 days from issue, so timing the application against a firm travel date matters.

        The genuine point of difference against EU-bound routes is the titre test wait. There isn't one. MOCCAE treats the test result as valid for 365 days provided the vaccination stays current, and does not add a separate countdown from the blood draw. Minimum age for a pet arriving from Rwanda's risk category is 15 weeks.

    - heading: "Getting the paperwork out of Kigali"
      body: |
        No formal export permit is needed to leave Rwanda with a personal pet. What matters is the export health certificate: an RAB-authorised vet examines the animal and completes the certificate on MOCCAE's own model format, and RAB endorses it close to the travel date, usually within 5 to 10 days.

        Kigali International (KGL) handles departure. RwandAir's direct KGL-DXB cargo service is the fastest way through, and it removes an entire leg most East African corridors need. Qatar Airways Cargo via Doha is a solid fallback if RwandAir's capacity is tight around peak dates.
