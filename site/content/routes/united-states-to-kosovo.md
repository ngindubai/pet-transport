  sections:
    - heading: "Why this route carries less paperwork than most"
      body: |
        Kosovo's published entry conditions for pets are shorter than many countries on this site: current rabies vaccination, a health certificate, and Echinococcus treatment. No titre test, no import permit, and even the microchip is a recommendation rather than a strict federal-style mandate, though in practice almost no pet travels without one given airline requirements.

        This lighter load doesn't mean skipping steps. USDA APHIS itself has no confirmed page listing Kosovo's requirements, the same gap that appears for its neighbour Bosnia and Herzegovina. A direct check with Kosovo's Food and Veterinary Agency, or a transport agent who has recently handled this specific route, fills that gap better than the absence of a USDA page would suggest on its own.

    - heading: "Echinococcus treatment: don't skip it"
      body: |
        Tapeworm treatment against Echinococcus is one of the few hard conditions Kosovo publishes for entering pets, and it needs to be documented on the certificate rather than assumed as part of a general USDA health check. Time this with the same vet visit that produces the main health certificate, and make sure the treatment itself is written down, not just given.

    - heading: "Getting from a US gateway to Pristina"
      body: |
        No airline flies a US city to Pristina directly. Turkish Airlines via Istanbul offers the widest US network on one carrier, from JFK, O'Hare and Dulles among others, with Turkish Cargo's dedicated Pet Lounge easing the Istanbul connection. Austrian Airlines via Vienna is a strong East Coast option too, given Austrian's own nonstop Vienna-JFK service six days a week.

        Swiss International Air Lines via Zurich works only for an owner travelling with a pet in the cabin. Its cargo arm doesn't currently deliver unaccompanied pets to Kosovo, a genuine gap worth knowing before booking rather than discovering at the cargo desk.
