  overview: |
    Japan's status as an EU-approved third country makes this one of the simpler intercontinental pet moves. There is no titre test, no quarantine, and no 3-month waiting period. Your pet needs a microchip, a valid rabies vaccination, and the EU-model health certificate -- and KLM flies direct from Tokyo Narita (NRT) to Amsterdam Schiphol (AMS), which removes any routing complexity.

    The health certificate is where the detail matters. Pets from Japan entering the EU need the EU Annex IV listed-country format -- a specific template your MAFF-accredited vet in Japan must use, endorsed by the MAFF regional office. It is valid for 10 days from issue, so coordinate the endorsement and departure date carefully.

    KLM accepts small pets in cabin (up to 8kg including carrier) and larger pets via KLM Cargo. Book early, as pet spaces on this route fill up. The NVWA inspects pets at Schiphol on arrival -- bring originals of all documents. Allow 4-8 weeks from starting preparation to travel day.
