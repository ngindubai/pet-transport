content:
  h1: "Pet Transport from Kazakhstan to the UAE"
  overview: |
    Kazakhstan's membership of the Eurasian Economic Union shapes the paperwork before any pet from Almaty even reaches the UAE side of the process: the export certificate has to satisfy the Union's own veterinary format, not only MOCCAE's model. It's an extra layer few owners expect when they first start planning.

    The Committee for Veterinary Control and Supervision, KVCS, part of the Ministry of Agriculture, authorises the vet and endorses the certificate before departure. No separate export permit is needed for a personal companion pet.

    On arrival, MOCCAE treats Kazakhstan as outside its short low-risk list, so the standard sequence applies: microchip, rabies vaccination, a titre test, and an import permit valid for 90 days. The one genuine relief in all this is the titre test rule itself: no fixed wait after the blood draw, unlike the EU's 90-day countdown. The result stands on its own for 365 days.

  sections:
    - heading: "How the Eurasian Economic Union and MOCCAE rules interact"
      body: |
        A pet leaving Kazakhstan needs its microchip fitted before rabies vaccination, then a vaccination current for at least 21 days on a primary dose. Next comes the rabies antibody titre test at an accredited laboratory, minimum result 0.5 IU/ml.

        Because Kazakhstan sits in the Eurasian Economic Union, the KVCS completes the certificate to satisfy both the Union's own veterinary standards and MOCCAE's model format for the UAE. The titre test result itself, once cleared, is valid for 365 days with no separate wait after the blood draw, a real point of contrast against EU-bound routes with their fixed 90-day rule. Minimum age from Kazakhstan's risk category is 15 weeks.

    - heading: "Departure from Almaty and the route into Dubai"
      body: |
        No formal export permit stands in the way of a personal pet leaving Kazakhstan, but the KVCS-endorsed health certificate does, completed close to the flight date, usually within 5 to 10 days.

        Almaty International (ALA) is the departure airport. FlyDubai's direct ALA-DXB service is the most straightforward option, covering a genuinely long distance from Central Asia into the Gulf in one leg. Qatar Airways Cargo via Doha (DOH) is a reasonable fallback if FlyDubai's direct schedule doesn't suit.
