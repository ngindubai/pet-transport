  h1: "Pet Transport from Kyrgyzstan to Canada"
  overview: |
    CFIA lists Kyrgyzstan, under its formal name the Kyrgyz Republic, on the same 13 March 2025 high-risk-for-dog-rabies register that also covers most of Central Asia. Read that fact on its own and it sounds like trouble. It mostly isn't, not for a personal pet. The listing's real bite falls on commercial dog imports, banned from high-risk countries since September 2022; a dog travelling with its own owner still clears the border with a current vaccination and a correctly formatted certificate, no blood test involved.

    Turkish Airlines does the actual heavy lifting on this route. Its Bishkek-Istanbul-Toronto chain, all one carrier, is about as clean as a Central Asian departure to Canada gets.

  sections:
    - heading: "A high-risk listing that mostly misses personal pets"
      body: |
        CFIA runs a genuinely different system from the CDC's. Where the CDC's rules for the same Kyrgyzstan-origin dog entering the United States trigger a titre test or a 28-day quarantine at a registered facility, CFIA has no such machinery for personal pets at all, high-risk country or not. What the list actually does is stop commercial dog imports cold, a rule in force since 28 September 2022.

        That distinction matters enough to say plainly: an owner bringing one or two dogs into Canada, travelling with them and not handing them to anyone else on arrival, follows the ordinary process. Current vaccination, correct paperwork, no serology lab.

    - heading: "The age line worth knowing about"
      body: |
        CFIA treats a dog under 8 months old as a presumptive commercial import if it isn't accompanied by its named owner. That presumption matters more from a high-risk country, since a dog caught by it faces the commercial ban outright rather than a lighter personal-pet path. A dog old enough, and genuinely travelling with the owner named on its papers, sits outside that presumption entirely. Confirm the position with CFIA directly if either detail is in any doubt.

    - heading: "Turkish Airlines, and why nothing else quite matches it"
      body: |
        Bishkek has no nonstop link to Canada, so the question is which connection to use. Turkish Airlines answers it cleanly: Bishkek to Istanbul, then Turkish's own Istanbul-Toronto or Istanbul-Montreal service, one carrier from start to finish, using the airline's dedicated Pet Lounge for the layover.

        Air Astana is the name most owners in the region know, but it doesn't reach Canada on its own network. Routing through Almaty means picking up a further connection somewhere else first, a longer and less predictable chain than Turkish's single change of aircraft.
