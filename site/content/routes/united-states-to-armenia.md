  sections:
    - heading: "Getting the USDA APHIS endorsement right"
      body: |
        The USDA APHIS endorsement is the step that most often catches US pet owners off guard. Your regular vet can complete the health certificate if they are USDA-accredited, but the document then has to go to your USDA APHIS State Veterinarian's office for an official endorsement stamp. That endorsement process takes three to five business days at most offices, sometimes longer during busy periods.

        The certificate also has a validity window. Armenia requires the health certificate to be issued within a specific number of days before your pet arrives in Yerevan. Work backwards from your travel date: when does your pet need to arrive, when does the certificate need to be endorsed, and when does your vet appointment need to happen? Getting this sequence wrong is the most common cause of problems on this route.

        If the logistics feel complicated, a licensed pet transport specialist can manage the timing and submission for you. It costs money, but it reduces the risk of a last-minute problem significantly.

    - heading: "Armenia's import process at Zvartnots Airport"
      body: |
        Yerevan's Zvartnots International Airport (EVN) has a veterinary inspection unit that processes all live animal arrivals. On arrival, you will need to present the SSFS import permit, the USDA-endorsed health certificate, proof of current rabies vaccination, and evidence of ISO microchip compliance.

        Armenia does not impose routine quarantine on pets arriving from the United States, which is a recognised rabies-controlled country. If your documents are in order and your pet is in good health, clearance is typically straightforward. The SSFS veterinary officer may carry out a brief physical check of the animal.

        One practical note: Yerevan is a medium-sized international airport. Cargo handling facilities are adequate but not as large as Istanbul or Frankfurt. If your pet is travelling as live animal cargo, confirm the handling process with your cargo agent at EVN before travel to avoid any surprises on arrival.
