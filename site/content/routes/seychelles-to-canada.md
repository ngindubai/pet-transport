content:
  h1: "Pet Transport from Seychelles to Canada"
  overview: |
    Seychelles doesn't appear anywhere on the Canadian Food Inspection Agency's high-risk list for dog rabies, a list last revised 13 March 2025. That single omission strips out the blood titre test that dominates the Canada-bound paperwork for so many other origins on this site, leaving a standard vaccination certificate as the entire federal requirement.

    What replaces the paperwork burden is a logistics one. Mahe has no non-stop Canadian service, so every pet on this route connects through Dubai or Abu Dhabi, and getting that transfer right matters more than anything CFIA asks for on arrival.

  sections:
    - heading: "Why Canada asks for so little"
      body: |
        CFIA sorts every origin country into one of two relevant categories for dog rabies: a short list of nine countries it recognises as rabies-free outright, and a much longer high-risk list reviewed and dated, currently 13 March 2025. Seychelles falls into neither. It isn't one of the nine rabies-free-recognised countries, but it also isn't flagged as high-risk, so it lands in the ordinary middle ground where a standard vaccination certificate does the job.

        That certificate needs specific fields filled in correctly: the vaccine's trade name, its serial number, its expiry date, plus the dog's breed, sex, colour and weight. Get the Seychelles vet to include every one of these on the first pass. A missing field is a genuinely common reason for delay at the Canadian border, and it costs nothing to avoid.

    - heading: "Seychelles' own export side still has to happen"
      body: |
        None of the above removes Seychelles' own requirement for a vet-issued export certificate before departure, endorsed by the Ministry of Environment's Border Control and Support Division. It's a lighter step than the destination-side rules many countries impose, but it still needs a vet visit timed close enough to travel that the certificate stays within its 10-day validity window on arrival.

    - heading: "Dubai and Abu Dhabi: the only two realistic routings"
      body: |
        With no direct Seychelles-Canada service, every shipment connects. Emirates' Dubai-Toronto sector now runs daily, and Etihad's daily A380 service between Abu Dhabi and Toronto is a genuine second option, routed through the Pet Oasis facility for the transfer. If your final destination sits outside Toronto, an Air Canada domestic connection usually closes the gap, though brachycephalic breeds face restrictions on that leg year-round.
