content:
  h1: "Pet Transport from Austria to Uruguay"
  overview: |
    Uruguay is a well-organised destination for pet imports, but it requires an MGAP permit before travel and has a specific antiparasitic treatment requirement that many owners are not aware of until late in the process. MGAP (Ministerio de Ganaderia, Agricultura y Pesca) administers pet imports; apply for the permit at gub.uy/ministerio-ganaderia-agricultura-pesca. Do not book flights until the permit is confirmed.

    The distinctive requirement for Uruguay is Praziquantel treatment against Taenia Echinococcus. Dogs must receive this treatment between 72 hours and 30 days before arriving in Uruguay. The window is fixed. A treatment given too early or too late will need to be repeated, so you need to coordinate with your vet using the exact arrival date, not the departure date. Cats are not subject to this requirement.

    Austria's export process follows the AGES route: an AGES-registered Amtstierarzt issues the health certificate within 10 days of travel, and AGES endorses it. There is no export permit required. From Vienna, Iberia via Madrid and LATAM via Lima or Sao Paulo are the most practical cargo options for Carrasco International Airport (MVD) in Montevideo.

  sections:
    - heading: "MGAP Import Permit: Start Here Before Anything Else"
      body: |
        Uruguay requires an import permit from MGAP for all dogs and cats entering the country. The permit is granted after MGAP reviews your pet's details and confirms the origin country's health status is acceptable. Applications should be submitted 6-10 weeks before travel. The permit document must travel with your pet and be presented to MGAP inspectors at Carrasco Airport (MVD) on arrival.

        Contact MGAP through the official portal at gub.uy/ministerio-ganaderia-agricultura-pesca for current application requirements and fees. Pet transport agents with experience in South America will know the current processing times and can often expedite applications through established contacts. There is no quarantine in Uruguay provided documents are correct, and the MGAP inspection at MVD is straightforward once the permit is in hand.

    - heading: "Praziquantel Treatment: Timing Against Your Arrival Date"
      body: |
        This is the step that trips people up most often on the Austria to Uruguay route. MGAP requires dogs to receive a Praziquantel treatment targeting Taenia Echinococcus, and the timing is calculated from the arrival date in Uruguay, not the departure date from Vienna. The treatment must be given no earlier than 30 days before arrival and no later than 72 hours before arrival. Your vet must provide a dated and signed certificate specifying the product used, dosage, and date of administration.

        Work backwards from your landing date at MVD. If you fly on a Saturday and arrive Sunday morning, the 72-hour window opens Wednesday morning. If your flight or connection is delayed and the 72-hour window passes, you will need to discuss the situation with MGAP inspectors on arrival. To avoid this, aim to administer the treatment 3-7 days before departure, which comfortably within the 30-day maximum and well outside the 72-hour minimum for most routing times from Vienna.

  - question: "What is Praziquantel and why does Uruguay require it?"
    answer: "Praziquantel is an antiparasitic drug used to treat tapeworm infections, specifically Taenia Echinococcus, which is a public health concern in Uruguay. It is mandatory for dogs and must be administered between 72 hours and 30 days before arrival. Cats are not subject to this requirement."
  - question: "Is there quarantine for pets arriving in Uruguay?"
    answer: "No. MGAP conducts a veterinary inspection at Carrasco International Airport (MVD), but there is no quarantine period provided your permit, health certificate, and treatment records are all in order."
