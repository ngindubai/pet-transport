But the practical challenges are significant: Australian pets need a rabies vaccination before travel; the EU AHC must be DAFF-endorsed within a 10-day window; and Ljubljana Airport (LJU) is a small airport that limits crate sizes on its connections.   sections:
    - heading: "Ljubljana or Vienna: choosing the right entry point"
      body: |
        Ljubljana Airport (LJU) has a Border Control Post and can receive pets from non-EU countries. However, the airport is served mainly by regional and medium-range aircraft, which limits the hold dimensions. If your pet travels in an IATA crate larger than size 500 (approximately 61 x 46 x 43 cm), confirm that the aircraft serving the final LJU leg can accommodate it.

        Many owners moving large dogs from Australia to Slovenia choose to collect the pet from cargo at Vienna Airport (VIE) and drive to Slovenia (approximately 4-5 hours by motorway). Vienna handles long-haul cargo on wide-body aircraft with no size restrictions. If you take this approach, your pet enters Slovenia by road, and you should present the DAFF-endorsed EU AHC at the designated BCP at the Slovenian road border crossing. Confirm the road BCP location with the UVHVR before travel.

    - heading: "The DAFF endorsement and AHC timing for Australia-to-Slovenia"
      body: |
        DAFF endorsement is the step most owners underestimate in terms of time. In busy periods (peak relocation season, December-January and May-June), DAFF processing can take up to 4 business days. Factor this in when scheduling your DAFF-authorised vet appointment.

        The practical sequence: book your vet for 7-8 days before departure from Australia; the vet signs the EU AHC; you submit immediately to DAFF for endorsement; the endorsed AHC returns 2-4 days later; you depart Australia with the endorsed document and arrive in Slovenia within the 10-day validity window.

        A pet transport agent manages this entire sequence on your behalf and knows when DAFF processing is running slow.

faqs:
  - question: "Does my Australian pet need a titre test to enter Slovenia?"
    answer: "No. Australia is a listed third country under EU regulations. Slovenia, as an EU member state, applies the same rule: no rabies titre test for Australian pets. A valid current vaccination (at least 21 days old at entry) and the DAFF-endorsed EU AHC are the main requirements."
  - question: "Can I collect my pet from Vienna Airport and drive to Slovenia?"
    answer: "Yes. Many owners moving large dogs to Slovenia do exactly this. You collect the pet from Vienna cargo after it arrives on a wide-body aircraft, and then drive 4-5 hours to Slovenia. Your pet enters Slovenia at a road BCP. You need the DAFF-endorsed EU AHC for the road crossing. Confirm the road BCP process with the UVHVR before travel."
  - question: "What is the DAFF endorsement and is it different to a standard vet certificate?"
    answer: "The DAFF endorsement is the Australian government's official stamp on the EU AHC. After a DAFF-authorised vet completes the EU AHC form, DAFF (Department of Agriculture, Fisheries and Forestry) reviews it and adds an official endorsement confirming the vet's credentials and the document's authenticity. The EU requires this government stamp on health certificates from third countries. A vet-signed AHC without the DAFF endorsement is not accepted at EU border crossings."
  - question: "What vaccinations does my Australian pet need before moving to Slovenia?"
    answer: "The EU legally requires only the rabies vaccination for entry. Most Australian vets also recommend bringing your pet's other vaccinations up to date before a long international journey: for dogs, this typically means distemper, parvo, hepatitis, leptospirosis, and kennel cough; for cats, herpesvirus, calicivirus, and panleukopenia. These are not EU border requirements but are good practice before a long-haul move."
