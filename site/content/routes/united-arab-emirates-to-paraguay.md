  h1: "Pet Transport from UAE to Paraguay"
  overview: |
    Two things make this direction lighter than the reverse trip. SENACSA doesn't ask for a titre test on companion animals entering under its standard non-commercial route, and the paperwork on the UAE side is a single MOCCAE health certificate rather than a full export permit process. What doesn't get lighter is the breed check: Paraguay refuses several breeds outright, and there's no route around that refusal.

    Routing runs through Sao Paulo either way, since nothing flies direct from the Gulf into Asuncion, so the practical timeline sits closer to two months than two weeks once cargo booking and connection handling are factored in.

  sections:
    - heading: "What SENACSA does and doesn't ask for"
      body: |
        A dog or cat entering Paraguay needs an ISO microchip, current core vaccinations and a CVI built from the UAE-issued MOCCAE certificate. Unlike the reverse journey, where MOCCAE requires a titre test because Paraguay sits outside its low-risk list, SENACSA doesn't publish a mandatory titre requirement for pets entering from the UAE under the standard non-commercial route.

        That asymmetry is worth sitting with for a second: it reflects Paraguay's own risk assessment, not a coordinated standard between the two countries, and it's exactly the kind of detail that's easy to get backwards if you're planning both legs of a family's relocation. SENACSA also caps non-commercial imports at five pets per owner. Anyone moving a larger household should count carefully before assuming the standard route applies.

    - heading: "The breed list that ends a booking before it starts"
      body: |
        Pit Bull-type dogs, American Staffordshire Terrier, American Bully, Cane Corso, Boerboel and several named mastiff breeds are refused entry into Paraguay outright, regardless of documentation, temperament, or how long the animal has lived in the UAE. Check this against the actual dog and any known mix before booking a vet appointment, let alone a flight.

    - heading: "Sao Paulo is the only realistic connection"
      body: |
        Emirates SkyCargo flies the only nonstop route between Dubai and Sao Paulo, roughly seven times a week on an A380, and LATAM covers the onward Sao Paulo-Asuncion leg about five times a week. Etihad has applied for slots to bring back its own Abu Dhabi-Sao Paulo service in summer 2026, its first time on that route since 2017, which would give Abu Dhabi-based owners a second option if it launches on schedule.
