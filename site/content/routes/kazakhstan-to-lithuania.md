content:
  h1: "Pet Transport Kazakhstan to Lithuania: 2026 Guide"
  overview: "Moving a pet from Kazakhstan to Lithuania requires careful planning over a minimum of five to six months. Lithuania is an EU member state and Kazakhstan is an EU unlisted country, which means the full EU unlisted pet travel process applies: microchip, rabies vaccination, FAVN titre test with a 90-day wait, and an official health certificate endorsed by Kazakhstan's competent authority. Vilnius Airport has limited direct cargo connections, making this one of the more complex routings in the region. Air Astana from Almaty or Astana to Frankfurt, then onward to Vilnius, is the most practical approach."

  sections:
    - heading: "Why the 90-day wait applies and what it means for planning"
      body: |
        Lithuania, as an EU member state, applies the EU pet travel framework for animals arriving from unlisted third countries. Kazakhstan is classified as an EU unlisted country, which means your pet must complete the FAVN titre test process before entering Lithuania.

        The sequence is: chip implanted first, then primary rabies vaccination, then at least 30 days later a blood draw for the FAVN titre test at an EU-approved laboratory. If the result is 0.5 IU/ml or above, the 90-day wait begins from the blood draw date. Travel to Lithuania cannot happen until at least day 91.

        The 90-day wait is calculated from when the blood was drawn, not when the results came back. Starting as early as possible after confirming you are moving is the most important step on this route.

    - heading: "What happens at Vilnius Airport"
      body: |
        Vilnius Airport (VNO) has border veterinary inspection for live animal cargo arriving from non-EU origins. The Valstybine maisto ir veterinarijos tarnyba (VMVT) oversees these controls. Border vets at VNO will check: the Kazakhstani health certificate (issued within 10 days of travel), the FAVN titre test result, the vaccination record, and the microchip.

        The chip must be scannable and the number must match every document precisely. If the titre test shows a successful result but the 90-day wait is incomplete, entry is refused. Lithuanian border vets apply EU rules strictly.

    - heading: "Routing from Kazakhstan to Vilnius"
      body: |
        There is no direct cargo service from Kazakhstan to Vilnius (VNO). Vilnius Airport has limited direct international cargo connections. The routing requires at least two legs and often three.

        The most practical first leg uses Air Astana from Almaty (ALA) or Astana (NQZ) to Frankfurt (FRA). From Frankfurt, LOT Polish Airlines or airBaltic connections can reach Warsaw (WAW) and then Vilnius (VNO). Alternatively, Turkish Airlines Cargo from ALA or NQZ to Istanbul (IST) and then via Warsaw to Vilnius is a reliable option.

        Book all legs during the 90-day wait and confirm live animal cargo acceptance on every segment in writing. A specialist transport agent is strongly recommended for this long-haul, multi-leg corridor.
