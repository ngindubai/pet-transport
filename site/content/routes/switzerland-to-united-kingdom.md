  sections:
    - heading: "The tapeworm timing requirement"
      body: |
        Tapeworm treatment is required for dogs (not cats or ferrets) entering Great Britain from any country outside the UK. The timing rule is strict: the treatment must be administered no earlier than 120 hours (5 days) and no later than 24 hours before the dog's scheduled arrival time in Great Britain.

        For the Zurich to London Heathrow flight, which is typically 2 hours, this means the treatment window on a morning flight might require the treatment to be given the evening before, or in some cases the day before. Work out the treatment window from your scheduled arrival time in Heathrow, not your departure time from Zurich.

        The vet who administers the treatment must record it in the Animal Health Certificate: the exact date and time, the product name (must contain praziquantel or an equivalent effective against Echinococcus multilocularis), and the dose. A missing or incomplete record is treated as a compliance failure. Give your Swiss vet enough time to complete the record correctly before the flight.

    - heading: "SWISS cabin travel to London"
      body: |
        SWISS operates direct flights from Zurich to London Heathrow and London City. For small dogs and cats weighing up to 8 kg including the carrier, SWISS allows cabin travel on these routes. The carrier must fit under the seat in front. On a 2-hour flight, cabin travel is comfortable and avoids cargo handling entirely.

        Book the cabin pet space at the time you book your own seat. The SWISS cabin pet fee is typically CHF 75 to CHF 120. For larger pets that exceed the 8 kg limit, Swiss WorldCargo handles pets in the pressurised hold. British Airways (which also flies this route frequently) uses IAG Cargo and does not allow cabin pets.
