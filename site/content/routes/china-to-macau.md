content:
  h1: "China to Macau: The Border That Doesn't Disappear"
  overview: |
    Fly from Beijing, Shanghai or Guangzhou to Macau and you'll barely notice the border on the ground. IAM certainly does. Mainland China is classified as rabies-endemic, so a pet crossing this genuinely short distance still needs the full rabies antibody titre test and roughly a three-month wait that any other rabies-endemic origin would face, "one country, two systems" notwithstanding.

    The upside is the flight itself. Air Macau, China Southern and China Eastern all run direct services from major mainland cities, sparing this route the Hong Kong transfer that almost every other Macau corridor on this site needs.

  sections:
    - heading: "Why the titre test doesn't disappear at the border"
      body: |
        Macau and mainland China are separate customs territories for animal health purposes, run by separate authorities: IAM in Macau, GACC on the mainland. IAM's exemption from the titre test applies to origins with no rabies incidents in the past two years. Mainland China, rabies-endemic by classification, is nowhere close to qualifying, so the standard sequence applies in full: blood drawn at least 30 days after vaccination, a result of 0.5 IU/mL or higher, then roughly three months before entry is permitted.

        This is a genuinely different situation from Hong Kong's, which has been free of animal rabies since 1987 and clears IAM's bar comfortably. Political unity and animal-health classification are not the same question, and IAM treats them separately.

    - heading: "A short flight, a long wait either side of it"
      body: |
        Once the titre test and wait are behind you, the physical journey is the easiest part of this route. Air Macau's mainland network covers Beijing, Shanghai, Guangzhou, Chengdu and a long list of other cities with direct service into Macau, no bridge crossing or ferry transfer required. China Southern and China Eastern add further options depending on your departure city.

        A narrower alternative exists for a specific group: residents of the Macau New Neighbourhood development in Hengqin, part of the Guangdong-Macao In-Depth Cooperation Zone, can currently bring one pet across Hengqin Port by land, with a quarantine certificate and rabies proof rather than a flight booking. IAM has said broader rules are under discussion with mainland authorities, but nothing wider had been confirmed at the time of writing, so most owners should plan around the standard flight and IAM process.
