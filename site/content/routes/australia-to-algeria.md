content:
  h1: "Pet Transport from Australia to Algeria"
  overview: |
    Emirates looks like the obvious carrier here. It already flies Sydney, Melbourne, Perth, Brisbane and Adelaide direct to Dubai, and up to six times a week onward to Algiers. There's a real problem with leaning on it, though: Algeria has started ending its air services agreement with the UAE, and the Dubai-Algiers route itself is due to stop by early February 2027 unless the two governments reach a new deal.

    Qatar Airways sidesteps the whole dispute. Its Algiers-Doha service runs roughly seven times a week and connects cleanly to Qatar's own Australian network, making it the steadier bet even though Emirates still looks like the bigger name today.

  sections:
    - heading: "Why shouldn't I just book Emirates and be done with it?"
      body: |
        Because the route itself is under diplomatic pressure. Algeria has begun the process of ending its air services agreement with the United Arab Emirates, and reporting puts the Dubai-Algiers Emirates service at risk of suspension by early February 2027 if no replacement agreement is signed. Etihad already abandoned its own plan to launch a competing Abu Dhabi-Algiers route for similar regulatory reasons.

        None of that means the flight is cancelled today. It means booking a move that depends entirely on this one corridor, months or years out, carries a genuine and specific risk that most routes on this site don't have.

    - heading: "Is Qatar Airways really the safer choice?"
      body: |
        On the numbers, yes. Qatar Airways flies Algiers to Doha nonstop roughly seven times a week, a route that sits entirely outside the UAE-Algeria dispute, and Doha already connects to multiple Australian cities on Qatar's own long-haul network. That gives a single-carrier option that isn't hostage to a bilateral aviation negotiation.

        It's still worth pricing both. Emirates continues to operate today, and for owners travelling well before 2027 it remains a workable choice, just not one to build a long-lead plan around without a backup.

    - heading: "What does Algeria actually check, regardless of which flight is used?"
      body: |
        Because Australia is treated as a controlled-rabies-risk country of origin, the same bracket the UK sits in on Algeria's own import rules, a titre test isn't the default requirement. A vaccination 21 to 30 days old and a properly endorsed certificate are what matter most.

        Breed is a separate and firmer issue. Algeria restricts entry for dogs it considers dangerous, Pit Bull-types and Staffordshire Bull Terriers among them, no matter where the pet is flying from. Owners of a restricted or borderline breed should raise it with DSV or the Algerian consulate before booking anything.
