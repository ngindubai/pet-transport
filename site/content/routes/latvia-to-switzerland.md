  sections:
    - heading: "Switzerland and the EU: why it is simpler than it looks"
      body: |
        Switzerland is not an EU member, but it has maintained a bilateral veterinary agreement with the EU since 2002. This agreement aligns Swiss animal import rules with EU standards for pets arriving from EU member states, which means EU pet passports are directly accepted, no titre test is required, and there is no quarantine for compliant animals.

        The Swiss Federal Food Safety and Veterinary Office (FSVO, known as BLV in German) oversees pet imports and publishes requirements at blv.admin.ch. Officers at Zurich (ZRH), Geneva (GVA), and Basel (BSL) airports, and at land border crossings, may conduct spot checks. Having your documentation in order and readily accessible is all that is required.

        One point to watch: your pet's rabies vaccination must be current at the time of entry. Switzerland checks that the vaccine is within its valid period, not just that the animal was vaccinated at some point. If a booster is due, schedule it before travel.

    - heading: "Riga to Zurich: airBaltic and other options"
      body: |
        airBaltic's direct Riga to Zurich (ZRH) service is the most straightforward option for most Latvia-to-Switzerland moves. Small pets travel in the cabin (up to 8kg including carrier); larger dogs go in the cargo hold. Book the pet reservation at the same time as your ticket.

        SWISS (part of the Lufthansa Group) is another strong option for travel to Switzerland. It operates from Zurich and is well set up for pet transport, including cabin travel for small animals and cargo for larger dogs. SWISS can be accessed via connecting hubs.

        Lufthansa routes via Frankfurt to Zurich or Geneva. If your pet is larger and needs cargo transport, Lufthansa Cargo is a reliable choice for this corridor.

    - heading: "Documents to prepare in Latvia"
      body: |
        Latvia's Food and Veterinary Service (PVD) authorises vets to issue EU pet passports. The passport is the most convenient document for this journey and for any future travel within the EU or to bilateral-agreement countries like Switzerland.

        An AHC is a viable alternative for a one-way move. It must be endorsed by an official Latvian vet within 10 days of your arrival in Switzerland. If you are flying into Zurich, the AHC endorsement date should be within 10 days of your flight date.

        Switzerland may require the AHC to conform to EU format. In practice, the standard EU AHC is accepted, but confirm with the issuing vet that it meets Swiss requirements.
