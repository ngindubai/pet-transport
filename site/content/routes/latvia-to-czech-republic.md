  sections:
    - heading: "Direct flight from Riga to Prague"
      body: |
        airBaltic's direct service from Riga (RIX) to Prague (PRG) takes around 2 hours and is the most efficient routing for this move. Small pets (up to 8kg including carrier) travel in the cabin. Larger dogs go in the cargo hold, which is pressurised and temperature-controlled.

        Cabin pet spaces are limited, so book as early as possible, particularly for summer travel. Contact airBaltic directly when booking to add the pet reservation; this cannot always be done online.

        Lufthansa via Frankfurt is an alternative if the airBaltic schedule does not suit your travel dates. Czech Airlines operates from Prague and can be useful for the return journey.

    - heading: "What the Czech SVA requires on arrival"
      body: |
        The Czech State Veterinary Administration (SVA) follows EU rules for incoming pets from EU member states. Your dog, cat, or ferret needs an ISO 11784/11785 microchip, a current rabies vaccination (with the first-vaccination 21-day wait observed), and an EU pet passport or AHC.

        SVA officers check documentation at Prague Airport (PRG) on arrival. The process is quick for compliant pets. If anything is missing or out of date, the pet may be refused entry or held pending resolution. Getting the paperwork right before departure avoids this entirely.

        Prague Airport is the main entry point. If driving, Czech border officers at land crossings may also check pet documents.

    - heading: "Municipality rules after arrival"
      body: |
        The Czech Republic has no national breed ban, but local municipalities have some latitude to impose their own rules. Prague, Brno, and other cities have regulations about dogs in public transport, parks, and certain public spaces.

        For most breeds, this means keeping your dog on a lead and carrying a muzzle for situations where one might be required. Check the rules for your specific destination city, particularly if you have a breed that might be considered powerful or aggressive.

        Cats face no breed restrictions in the Czech Republic.

  - question: "What is an AHC and when would I use one instead of an EU pet passport?"
    answer: "An AHC (Animal Health Certificate) is a single-use travel document issued by an official vet. It is a valid alternative to an EU pet passport for a one-way trip. The AHC must be endorsed within 10 days of your arrival in the Czech Republic. If you plan to travel between EU countries in future, the EU pet passport is the more practical long-term option."
  - question: "Is there a road or rail option from Latvia to Czech Republic?"
    answer: "Yes. Driving is possible (approximately 1,300km, around 13-15 hours via Lithuania, Poland, and into the Czech Republic). Your pet needs the same documentation as for a flight, and the AHC must be within its 10-day validity window when you cross the Czech border."
