  h1: "Pet Transport from Canada to Madagascar"
  overview: |
    A letter beats a laboratory on this route. Madagascar's entry rules for pets skip the rabies titre test entirely, from any country including Canada, and ask instead for a written request to the Direction des Services Veterinaires (DSV) in Antananarivo, describing the owner, the animal and its vaccination history.

    The flight is the part that needs more thought. Emirates and Air France both reach Antananarivo through their respective hubs, but Ethiopian Airlines offers something neither can match from a Canadian departure point: a nonstop Toronto-Addis Ababa sector connecting straight into its own nonstop Addis Ababa-Antananarivo service, six times a week, no carrier change either side.

    Twelve to sixteen weeks is the realistic budget, and the DSV letter, not the vet work, is what sets that timeline.

  sections:
    - heading: "Ethiopian's Addis Ababa link, and the alternatives"
      body: |
        Emirates is the airline most owners think of first for Madagascar, given its dedicated Dubai-Antananarivo service, and the Air Canada-Emirates codeshare partnership, extended through 2032, makes booking the Toronto-Dubai leg straightforward. It's worth knowing, though, that Emirates' onward Dubai-Antananarivo flight actually routes via a linked Seychelles service rather than flying nonstop, a detail that adds transit time even though the two cities are marketed as directly connected.

        Ethiopian Airlines avoids that complication entirely. Its Toronto-Addis Ababa sector is genuinely nonstop, and so is its separate Addis Ababa-Antananarivo service, six times a week. Air France remains a third option, connecting through Paris on a route Air Canada also serves nonstop from Toronto and Montreal, and it's the only one of the three that will carry a small pet in the cabin, on the Paris-Antananarivo sector specifically.

    - heading: "No titre test, despite Madagascar's biosecurity reputation"
      body: |
        Madagascar's isolation and unique wildlife might suggest a stricter entry regime built around blood tests and lab results. For an ordinary companion dog or cat, that's not what happens. The requirement is a rabies vaccination given between 30 days and 12 months before arrival, full stop, with no titre test asked for regardless of origin country.

        Madagascar's caution shows up elsewhere instead, mainly around CITES-restricted wildlife species, a category that doesn't touch a family pet with valid paperwork.

    - heading: "The DSV letter: apply well before booking a flight"
      body: |
        Rather than a standard online import permit form, Madagascar's process runs through a letter of request addressed to the Directeur des Services Veterinaires, listing the owner's name and address, the animal's name, breed, age and sex, a photocopy of the vaccination record, the country of origin, the arrival date, the transport mode and the airport of disembarkation.

        Processing can take several weeks with no fixed turnaround published. Send it at least twelve to fourteen weeks before the intended travel date, and confirm the current submission channel with DSV directly.

    answer: "It's a letter, not a form. The letter of request goes to the Direction des Services Veterinaires (DSV) in Antananarivo and needs to include the owner's and animal's details, a copy of the vaccination record, and the arrival flight details. Processing can take several weeks."
