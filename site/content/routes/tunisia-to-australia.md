content:
  h1: "Pet Transport from Tunisia to Australia"
  overview: |
    There's no compliant way to fly a pet straight from Tunis to Melbourne, whatever a booking site's routing options suggest. Tunisia isn't on any of DAFF's approved country lists, Group 1, 2 or 3, which means the pet has to establish 180 continuous days of residency in a country that is approved, completing the full import protocol there, before Australia's own process can even start.

    Layer the current RNATT titre test rules on top, a 180-day wait from the blood draw rather than the shorter figure some older guides still reference, and the realistic floor for this route sits at 10 to 13 months. Most of that time is the residency requirement and the titre wait running in parallel, not active paperwork.

  sections:
    - heading: "Why Tunisia can't be the country of export"
      body: |
        DAFF sorts countries into three approved groups. Group 1 is essentially New Zealand and a handful of Pacific territories. Group 2 covers a short list of rabies-free-recognised countries. Group 3 covers rabies-controlled nations such as the UK, the US, Canada, France, Germany and Spain, which demand the fullest pre-export protocol but are, at least, on the list at all.

        Tunisia is on none of them. For a pet in that position, DAFF's rule is blunt: reside continuously for 180 days in an approved Group 2 or 3 country, and complete every step of that country's own import protocol while there. Tunisia's DGSV certificate gets the pet as far as the departure gate in Tunis. It carries no weight with DAFF beyond that.

    - heading: "Two 180-day clocks, mostly running together"
      body: |
        The residency requirement is 180 continuous days. The RNATT wait, once the titre test blood is drawn, is also currently 180 days, source: DAFF and secondary industry guidance checked 15 July 2026. These aren't sequential add-ons; a vet in the approved country can draw the blood a few weeks into residency, so the two clocks overlap for most of their length.

        What they don't do is cancel each other out. Vaccination has to happen first, then a 30-day gap before the blood draw is even possible, then the 180-day wait from that draw date. Add the time needed to actually settle into the approved country and book a vet appointment, and 10 to 13 months is the honest range, not the four to six months a simpler route might suggest.

    - heading: "What actually satisfies DAFF once the pet is in an approved country"
      body: |
        Once residency is established, the process looks like any other Group 3 import: microchip before vaccination, RNATT at a DAFF-recognised laboratory, a BICON-lodged import permit, and a government health certificate from that country's own vet, not from Tunisia. Get the identity verification steps done correctly before the RNATT blood draw and Mickleham quarantine on arrival runs 10 days. Miss that and it jumps to a minimum of 30, at real extra cost.

    - heading: "The flight itself is the easy part"
      body: |
        Tunisair covers the opening leg out of Tunis to Paris, Frankfurt, Rome or Madrid, all viable Group 3 starting points. Months later, once both 180-day clocks have run their course, Emirates, Singapore Airlines or Qatar Airways carry the final sector into Melbourne, the only Australian airport cleared to receive an imported pet. None of that logistics is the hard part of this route. The hard part is the 10 to 13 months before any of those flights matter.
