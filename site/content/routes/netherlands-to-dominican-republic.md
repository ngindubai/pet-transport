  sections:
    - heading: "Dominican Republic requirements for pets from the Netherlands"
      body: |
        DGAG (Direccion General de Ganaderia), within the Ministerio de Agricultura (agricultura.gob.do), governs pet imports. For pets arriving from the Netherlands, the requirements are: ISO 11784/11785 microchip; rabies vaccination administered between 30 days and 12 months before arrival; current core vaccinations (distemper, parvovirus, hepatitis, leptospirosis); and an official health certificate endorsed by the NVWA, issued within 15 days of travel.

        No import permit is required for up to 5 personal companion animals. No quarantine for compliant arrivals. DGAG inspects pets on arrival at Las Americas International Airport (SDQ).

    - heading: "Core vaccination requirements"
      body: |
        Pets from the Netherlands must have current core vaccinations alongside rabies: distemper, parvovirus, infectious hepatitis, and leptospirosis. All must be recorded in the health certificate with dates and batch numbers. Dutch EU pet passports document core vaccinations, but the official export health certificate must reference them explicitly in the format required by DGAG.

        Discuss the certificate content with your official veterinarian before the appointment to ensure all required vaccinations are listed correctly.

    - heading: "Routing from Amsterdam to Santo Domingo"
      body: |
        Amsterdam Schiphol (AMS) is KLM's main hub with Caribbean route coverage. KLM has services to Dominican Republic destinations including Santo Domingo (SDQ) and Punta Cana (PUJ). KLM Cargo handles live animals on approved Caribbean routes. If direct capacity is not available on a specific date, routing via Madrid (MAD) with Iberia or via Paris CDG with Air France provides alternatives to SDQ.

        Confirm live animal acceptance on your specific flight well in advance, as Caribbean cargo slots can be limited.

    - heading: "NVWA export endorsement from the Netherlands"
      body: |
        The NVWA (Nederlandse Voedsel- en Warenautoriteit, nvwa.nl) endorses official export health certificates for pets leaving the Netherlands for non-EU destinations. An official veterinarian prepares the certificate in English or Spanish, referencing the microchip, all vaccinations, and their dates.

        Allow three to five business days for the NVWA endorsement. The health certificate must be issued within 15 days of travel. Keep the timing tight to avoid any last-minute complications.

faqs:
  - question: "Does the Dominican Republic require tapeworm treatment for dogs from the Netherlands?"
    answer: "DGAG does not mandate a specific tapeworm or Echinococcus treatment for dogs arriving from the Netherlands. Core vaccinations and the health certificate are the primary requirements. Confirm with DGAG at agricultura.gob.do for any changes before travel."
  - question: "Can I use my pet's EU pet passport as the official health certificate for the Dominican Republic?"
    answer: "No. The EU pet passport is a vaccination record for intra-EU/EEA travel, not an export health certificate for non-EU countries. The Dominican Republic requires an official health certificate prepared by an official veterinarian and endorsed by the NVWA."
  - question: "Is Las Americas Airport (SDQ) the only entry airport for pets in the Dominican Republic?"
    answer: "SDQ (Las Americas International Airport near Santo Domingo) is the primary entry airport. If your cargo routes to Punta Cana (PUJ), confirm with DGAG that PUJ has an inspection facility for arriving pets, as inspection capabilities can vary by airport."
  - question: "What does the DGAG inspection at SDQ involve?"
    answer: "DGAG inspectors at Las Americas Airport check the endorsed health certificate, scan the microchip, and review the vaccination records. For compliant pets, the inspection is routine and typically takes 30-60 minutes after the cargo arrives."
