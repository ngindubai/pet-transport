  sections:
    - heading: "Dominican Republic import requirements: the checklist"
      body: |
        The DGAG (Direccion General de Ganaderia) under the Ministerio de Agricultura regulates all pet imports into the Dominican Republic. DGAG veterinary officers inspect arriving pets at Las Americas International Airport (SDQ) in Santo Domingo on arrival.

        The requirements are: a microchip conforming to ISO 11784/11785, a valid rabies vaccination given between 30 days and 12 months before entry, a health certificate issued within 15 days of travel, and current DHPP vaccination for dogs (distemper, adenovirus, parainfluenza, parvovirus). Cats need rhinotracheitis, calicivirus, and panleukopenia vaccinations on record. All pets need internal and external antiparasitic treatment within 10 days of departure, documented by a vet.

        No import permit is required for personal companion animals (up to five animals with their owner). There is no quarantine for compliant arrivals.

    - heading: "Netherlands export documentation: the NVWA process"
      body: |
        The Netherlands' export documentation for non-EU destinations uses the AHC issued by an officieel dierenarts and endorsed by the NVWA (Nederlandse Voedsel en Warenautoriteit). The NVWA is the Dutch national authority for food safety and animal health. Contact the NVWA or an NVWA-registered official vet to book the appointment and confirm the current endorsement process for the Dominican Republic.

        The DGAG accepts AHC certificates up to 15 days from issue, giving slightly more scheduling room than most EU destinations. Still, issue the certificate as close to departure as your cargo booking allows, and do not let the extended validity become an excuse to delay.

        KLM operates out of Amsterdam Schiphol and is well placed to handle Caribbean cargo. Book cargo space early, particularly in the summer months when leisure travel demand can reduce available live animal cargo capacity.
