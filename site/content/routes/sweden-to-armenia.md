  sections:
    - heading: "Armenia's entry requirements for Swedish pets"
      body: |
        Armenia accepts companion pets from EU countries including Sweden with a valid EU health certificate, current microchip, and up-to-date rabies vaccination. No titre test is required.

        The SSFS (State Service for Food Safety) inspects incoming pets at Zvartnots International Airport (EVN). Compliant pets with complete Jordbruksverket-endorsed certificates are released without quarantine.

        Confirm current requirements at ssfs.am or with the Armenian Embassy in Stockholm before travel.

    - heading: "Cargo routing from Stockholm Arlanda to Yerevan"
      body: |
        No direct cargo service links Stockholm Arlanda (ARN) and Yerevan (EVN). The most practical routing is Turkish Airlines via Istanbul.

        Turkish Airlines connects ARN to Istanbul (IST) with regular flights, and Turkish Airlines Cargo operates from IST to EVN. The IST hub is the most commonly used connection point for Scandinavia-to-Caucasus pet cargo movements.

        LOT Polish Airlines via Warsaw (WAW) is an alternative, with onward connections to Yerevan. If Turkish Airlines schedules do not suit, LOT provides a European hub option with a well-run cargo operation.

    - heading: "Jordbruksverket: Sweden's export certificate process"
      body: |
        Sweden's EU export health certificate must be issued by an Officiell veterinär (OV), an official vet authorised by Jordbruksverket (Swedish Board of Agriculture). Not all private practice vets hold OV status for export documentation.

        The OV examines the pet, completes the EU-format health certificate, and submits it for Jordbruksverket endorsement. Endorsement typically takes one to three working days. The 10-day validity of the certificate begins from the examination date, not the endorsement date.

        Find authorised OVs through Jordbruksverket (jordbruksverket.se). Many clinics that handle international pet moves have an OV on staff or can direct you to one.
