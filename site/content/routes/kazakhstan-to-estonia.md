  h1: "Pet Transport Kazakhstan to Estonia: 2026 Guide"
  overview: "Moving a pet from Kazakhstan to Estonia requires careful planning over a minimum of five to six months. Estonia is an EU member state and Kazakhstan is an EU unlisted country, which means the full EU unlisted pet travel process applies: microchip, rabies vaccination, FAVN titre test with a 90-day wait, and an official health certificate endorsed by Kazakhstan's competent authority. The distance from Kazakhstan to Estonia is significant, making Air Astana's direct European routes to Frankfurt or Amsterdam a practical first leg. There is no shortcut to the 90-day wait."

  sections:
    - heading: "Why the 90-day wait applies and what it means for planning"
      body: |
        Estonia, as an EU member state, applies the EU pet travel framework for animals arriving from unlisted third countries. Kazakhstan is classified as an EU unlisted country, which means your pet must complete the FAVN titre test process before entering Estonia.

        The sequence is: chip implanted first, then primary rabies vaccination, then at least 30 days later a blood draw for the FAVN titre test at an EU-approved laboratory. If the result is 0.5 IU/ml or above, the 90-day wait begins from the blood draw date. Travel to Estonia cannot happen until at least day 91.

        The 90-day wait is calculated from when the blood was drawn, not when the results came back. Starting as early as possible after confirming you are moving is the most important step on this route.

    - heading: "What happens at Tallinn Airport"
      body: |
        Tallinn Airport (TLL) has border veterinary inspection for live animal cargo arriving from non-EU origins. The Pohllumajandus- ja Toiduamet (Agricultural Board of Estonia) oversees these controls. Border vets at TLL will check: the Kazakhstani health certificate (issued within 10 days of travel), the FAVN titre test result, the vaccination record, and the microchip.

        The chip must be scannable and the number must match every document precisely. If the titre test shows a successful result but the 90-day wait is incomplete, entry is refused. Estonian border vets apply EU rules strictly.

    - heading: "Routing from Kazakhstan to Tallinn"
      body: |
        There is no direct cargo service from Kazakhstan to Tallinn (TLL). The most practical routing uses Air Astana, Kazakhstan's national carrier, for the first leg from Almaty (ALA) or Astana (NQZ) to Frankfurt (FRA) or Amsterdam (AMS). From FRA or AMS, connections to Tallinn are available via airBaltic, Lufthansa, and other carriers.

        An alternative routing goes via Istanbul Ataturk (IST) with Turkish Airlines Cargo, then IST to TLL. Turkish Airlines handles live animals at IST regularly. The choice between the two main routings often depends on Air Astana cargo capacity and availability. Book during the 90-day wait and confirm live animal acceptance on every segment in writing.

  - question: "What is the FAVN titre test and where can I get it done in Kazakhstan?"
    answer: "The FAVN (fluorescent antibody virus neutralisation) test measures your pet's rabies antibody levels. Blood must be drawn by a licensed vet in Kazakhstan and sent to an EU-approved laboratory. The blood draw must happen at least 30 days after the primary rabies vaccination. If the result is 0.5 IU/ml or above, the 90-day wait begins from the blood draw date. Your vet or a specialist transport agent can advise which EU-approved laboratories accept samples from Kazakhstan."
