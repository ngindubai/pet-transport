  h1: "Pet Transport from Hungary to Georgia"
  overview: |
    Hungary to Georgia is one of the cleaner routes from Central Europe to the Caucasus. Georgia does not require a titre test from EU countries, there is no quarantine for compliant arrivals, and the National Food Agency (NFSA) inspection at Tbilisi airport is straightforward for pets arriving with correct documentation.

    The Nébih health certificate, endorsed by Hungary's National Food Chain Safety Office, is the central document. It needs to be issued within 10 days of arrival in Tbilisi, and it must accompany the EU pet passport showing a valid rabies vaccination. Turkish Airlines Cargo provides the most practical routing: Budapest Ferenc Liszt (BUD) to Istanbul (IST), then on to Tbilisi International Airport (TBS).

    Total preparation time is 3-5 weeks for most moves from Hungary, with the timing driven mainly by ensuring the rabies vaccination is current and scheduling the cargo booking early enough to secure space.

  sections:
    - heading: "Georgia's NFSA inspection at Tbilisi airport"
      body: |
        The National Food Agency (NFSA, nfa.gov.ge) is Georgia's competent authority for live animal imports. Inspectors at Tbilisi International Airport (TBS) check all incoming pets before release. The inspection covers three things: the Nébih-endorsed health certificate, the rabies vaccination record, and a microchip scan to confirm the number matches the certificate.

        For pets from EU countries, no titre test is required. Georgia accepted the EU's biosecurity standards for companion animals from member states, which means the preparation steps are significantly simpler than for pets travelling from non-EU countries in the region.

        NFSA does not require a pre-arrival import permit for personal companion pets. If you are moving more than two animals or travelling commercially, contact nfa.gov.ge directly before making any travel arrangements.

    - heading: "Nébih certification: the step-by-step process"
      body: |
        Every cat or dog leaving Hungary for a non-EU country requires an official animal health certificate endorsed by Nébih (Nemzeti Élelmiszerlánc-biztonsági Hivatal). The certificate is produced at a single appointment with an authorised official vet, who examines the pet, checks the microchip, reviews the vaccination record, and prepares the documentation. Nébih then endorses the certificate, which usually takes 1-2 working days.

        Book the vet appointment at least a week before your planned travel date to leave room for Nébih processing and any last-minute corrections. The certificate is valid for 10 days from the date of examination to the date of arrival in Georgia, not from the date of departure from Budapest. If you have an overnight connection in Istanbul, count the 10 days to the TBS arrival day, not the BUD departure.

        No export permit is required for personal pets leaving Hungary. The endorsed certificate, the EU pet passport with the vaccination record, and a functioning microchip are the three documents Georgia requires.

    - heading: "Cargo options from Budapest to Tbilisi"
      body: |
        Turkish Airlines Cargo, via Istanbul (IST), is the most reliable and widely used routing for live animal cargo between BUD and TBS. Budapest has several Turkish Airlines passenger and cargo departures daily, and IST-TBS connections operate regularly. The total journey from BUD to TBS via IST is typically 5-7 hours.

        Book through a Turkish Airlines Cargo agent or an IPATA-registered pet relocation agency. Specify that you are moving a live animal, provide the IATA crate dimensions, and confirm acceptance on the specific flight. Live animal cargo space is limited per flight and books out, particularly during summer months. Temperature embargoes apply when ambient temperatures at any point on the routing exceed 29-30 degrees Celsius.

        LOT Polish Airlines via Warsaw provides an alternative if Turkish Airlines availability does not work. LOT Cargo handles live animals and Warsaw Chopin is a functional Central European hub for onward Caucasus connections.
