content:
  h1: "Pet Transport Ireland to Jamaica"
  overview: |
    Jamaica's own process, not the flight, is what stretches this route out. VSD requires a Preliminary Application Form approved before an owner can even start the rabies vaccination and FAVN titre test steps that count towards entry, a sequence that applies to every origin country, Ireland included. The upside for Irish owners is a genuinely well-served London connection once that paperwork clears.

  sections:
    - heading: "The Preliminary Application: the step Ireland shares with every other origin"
      body: |
        None of Jamaica's process is specific to Irish pets. VSD applies the same Preliminary Application requirement to every arrival, and vaccinating or booking the titre test before that form is approved risks the whole file being rejected. Build in 4-5 months from the very first step, regardless of how direct the flight itself turns out to be.

    - heading: "The titre test and Plumb Point quarantine"
      body: |
        A FAVN titre test, drawn 30 days after the qualifying rabies vaccination at a VSD-approved laboratory, needs to clear 0.5 IU/ml. A first-time rabies vaccination in a pet over 12 months old doesn't qualify for entry until three months after that dose. Quarantine at Plumb Point runs up to 14 days, with VSD inspectors holding discretion to release a healthy, well-documented pet sooner, though plan for the full period regardless.

    - heading: "Dublin to Kingston via London"
      body: |
        British Airways' Dublin-Heathrow-Kingston routing is the most established path, drawing on BA World Cargo's long-standing Caribbean network. Aer Lingus offers an alternative feed into a partner carrier's transatlantic service if BA's schedule doesn't fit. Once the paperwork side clears, this is one of the more straightforward legs of the entire process.
