        No airline flies direct from any UK airport to Kathmandu. The main options are Qatar Airways via Doha, Turkish Airlines via Istanbul, and Emirates via Dubai. Each involves your pet spending time in the hold for two flight legs plus a connection period. Before booking, confirm with the airline's cargo team that live animals are accepted on the specific aircraft type for both legs, as some aircraft configurations do not carry pets.

        Total crate time for a UK to Kathmandu routing is typically 12-18 hours including connections. Ensure the crate is large enough for your pet to stand, turn, and lie down, and attach a water supply.     - heading: "Nepal quarantine and what it means for your arrival"
      body: |
        Nepal imposes a 7-day quarantine on all imported pets on arrival at Tribhuvan International Airport. The quarantine is managed by the Department of Livestock Services, and the owner is responsible for fees throughout. You will not be able to collect your pet on the day they land. Confirm current quarantine rates and visiting arrangements with DLS when you apply for the import permit.

        The process leading up to travel is also more involved than many routes. The titre test requires blood drawn at least 30 days after rabies vaccination, and laboratory results take 2-3 weeks. The DLS import permit application takes at least 6 weeks. Working backwards from a departure date, you need 12-16 weeks of preparation at minimum.

faqs:
  - question: "Are there direct flights from the UK to Nepal for pet cargo?"
    answer: "No. There are no direct UK to Kathmandu flights. All routing involves at least one connection, typically via Doha (Qatar Airways), Istanbul (Turkish Airlines), or Dubai (Emirates). Before booking, confirm that the specific aircraft operating both the UK to hub and hub to Kathmandu legs accepts live animals in the cargo hold. Your pet's total crate time will be 12-18 hours including the connection."

  - question: "How long is the quarantine when my pet arrives in Nepal?"
    answer: "7 days, managed by Nepal's Department of Livestock Services at a facility in Kathmandu. The quarantine applies to all imported dogs and cats and cannot be shortened. The owner pays the quarantine costs for the full period. Build this into your relocation schedule so you are not expecting to collect your pet on the day of arrival."

  - question: "Does my pet need a titre test to enter Nepal from the UK?"
    answer: "Nepal requires a titre test for pets from most countries. The blood draw must happen at least 30 days after the most recent rabies vaccination. Verify the current requirement with the Department of Livestock Services before starting the process, as the precise requirement for UK-origin pets can be confirmed at that stage. Laboratory results take 2-3 weeks, so include this in your timeline."

  - question: "What is the import permit process for Nepal?"
    answer: "The permit is issued by Nepal's Department of Livestock Services (DLS) in Kathmandu. Apply at least 6 weeks before travel with your pet's microchip details, vaccination history, and titre test documentation. The permit confirms that Nepal's veterinary authorities have reviewed your pet's records and granted clearance for import. Do not book cargo space before receiving the permit in writing."
