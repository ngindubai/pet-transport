  sections:
    - heading: "The export permit requirement: a Jamaica-specific step"
      body: |
        Jamaica requires an export permit for dogs and cats leaving the country. This is issued by the Veterinary Services Division (VSD) of the Ministry of Industry, Commerce, Agriculture and Fisheries. The permit application should be submitted well in advance of travel, not left to the week of departure.

        The VSD also authorises the veterinarians who may issue the export health certificate for EU entry. Not all private vets in Jamaica are on this approved list. The VSD can confirm which practitioners are authorised for EU-format export health certificates.

        The export permit and the EU health certificate are separate documents. The permit governs leaving Jamaica; the health certificate governs entering Germany. Both are required.

    - heading: "Routing from Kingston to Frankfurt via London"
      body: |
        Kingston Norman Manley International (KIN) is Jamaica's main international airport. British Airways operates regular services from KIN to London Heathrow (LHR), and British Airways World Cargo accepts live animal freight on this route.

        From London Heathrow, connections to Frankfurt, Munich, Dusseldorf, and other German cities are available via Lufthansa Group and British Airways partners. The full KIN-LHR-FRA routing takes approximately 14-16 hours total.

        One point to manage carefully: you need live animal cargo acceptance confirmed on both the KIN-LHR and LHR-FRA legs separately. A booking on British Airways from KIN to LHR does not automatically guarantee onward live animal cargo acceptance at LHR. Your cargo agent should handle this as a single coordinated booking.

        Montego Bay Sangster International (MBJ) is Jamaica's second airport. Some seasonal services operate from MBJ, including Condor leisure flights that sometimes include cargo to Germany. Confirm availability for your dates before treating MBJ as a primary option.

faqs:
  - question: "Does Jamaica have any agreement with Germany or the EU that simplifies the pet import process?"
    answer: "No. Jamaica does not have a veterinary equivalence agreement with the EU. It is an EU unlisted country for pet travel purposes, and the full unlisted process applies: FAVN titre test at an EU-approved laboratory, 90-day mandatory wait from the blood draw date, and an EU health certificate endorsed by the VSD. In addition, Jamaica requires its own export permit for outgoing pets."
  - question: "What is the Veterinary Services Division and how do I contact them?"
    answer: "The Veterinary Services Division (VSD) is part of Jamaica's Ministry of Industry, Commerce, Agriculture and Fisheries (MICAF). It is responsible for animal health regulations, export permits, and authorising veterinarians for export health certificates. Contact them via the MICAF website at micaf.gov.jm or visit their Kingston office directly to initiate the export permit process."
  - question: "Can I use a laboratory in the USA for the FAVN test?"
    answer: "Yes, provided the laboratory is on the EU-approved list (published at food.ec.europa.eu). Several US laboratories are EU-approved for FAVN testing, including Kansas State University Veterinary Diagnostic Laboratory. However, the blood sample must be drawn in Jamaica and shipped correctly to the US lab, which adds complexity. European labs may offer comparable turnaround with less complex shipping logistics from the Caribbean."
