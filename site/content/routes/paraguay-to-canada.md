content:
  h1: "Pet Transport from Paraguay to Canada"
  overview: |
    The Canadian Food Inspection Agency's high-risk dog rabies list, last revised 13 March 2025, runs to dozens of countries across four regions, and Paraguay isn't one of them. Bolivia, Brazil, Colombia, Ecuador and Guatemala all are. That single classification strips out the mandatory microchip, the 7-month minimum entry age and the tighter documentation CFIA applies to dogs from listed countries, which makes this one of the more straightforward South American moves this site covers.

    A rabies vaccination certificate with the vaccine's trade name, serial number and expiry date, correctly completed in English or French, does most of the work on the Canadian side. Paraguay's own SENACSA process, a CVI issued close to departure, handles the rest.

  sections:
    - heading: "What Paraguay's off-list status actually removes"
      body: |
        Dogs from a CFIA high-risk country need a mandatory 15-digit ISO microchip, must be at least 7 months old on arrival, and face closer document scrutiny. None of that applies here. A Paraguay-origin dog just needs a correctly completed rabies certificate, current within the standard window, and CFIA does not mandate a titre test or a fixed waiting period on this route.

        Cats have even less to clear, since CFIA's rabies certificate requirement and the general absence of a titre or quarantine step apply the same way regardless of species classification.

    - heading: "The part CFIA doesn't cover: provincial law"
      body: |
        Clearing the federal border is not the end of the story if the destination is Ontario. The province's Dog Owners' Liability Act bans owning, breeding or importing Pit Bull-type dogs outright, a rule that sits entirely outside CFIA's remit. A dog that clears customs at Pearson can still run into a provincial problem the moment it's registered locally. Check the destination province specifically, not just the federal checklist, before assuming the move is done once the plane lands.

    - heading: "Routing: Panama or Sao Paulo"
      body: |
        Copa Airlines, through its Tocumen hub in Panama City, is the most direct single-carrier option from Asuncion into Toronto or Montreal. It comes with one hard rule: no live animal cargo Friday through Sunday, since the vet who clears transiting pets at Tocumen only works weekdays.

        LATAM's Asuncion-Sao Paulo service, connecting into Air Canada's nonstop daily Sao Paulo-Toronto flight, is the workable alternative when Copa's schedule doesn't fit. Both carriers exclude a handful of breeds from cargo outright, so check that list against the actual dog before booking either one.
