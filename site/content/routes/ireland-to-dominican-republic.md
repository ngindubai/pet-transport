  sections:
    - heading: "Why the Dominican Republic asks for so little from Irish pets"
      body: |
        For up to five personal pets, the Dominican Republic requires no import permit at all. What it does require is straightforward: an ISO microchip, a rabies vaccination administered between 30 days and 12 months before entry, and a health certificate issued within 10 days of travel and endorsed by DAFM. No titre test applies, since that requirement is reserved for countries DIGEGA classifies as high-rabies-risk, and Ireland is not one of them.

        On arrival at Las Américas International Airport or Punta Cana International Airport, officials check the microchip against the paperwork, and there's no quarantine for a compliant pet.
    - heading: "Routing an Irish pet to Santo Domingo"
      body: |
        No airline flies Dublin to Santo Domingo directly, so plan on a connection through Madrid with Iberia, which offers a cabin option for smaller pets on the transatlantic leg, or Amsterdam with KLM. Aer Lingus typically handles the Dublin-Madrid segment.

        Given the AHC's 10-day validity, book the DAFM vet appointment once your cargo or cabin space is confirmed, not before, so the certificate stays valid right through to your actual departure date.
