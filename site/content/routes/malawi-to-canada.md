  h1: "Pet Transport from Malawi to Canada"
  overview: |
    Two flight numbers, run in reverse, cover almost the whole trip. ET877 flies Lilongwe to Addis Ababa nonstop; ET553 continues Addis Ababa to Toronto, also nonstop. Both are Ethiopian Airlines, both fly Boeing 787s, and a pet booked this way never changes carrier, the same single-airline chain that makes the outbound Canada-to-Malawi route work.

    Malawi also sits on CFIA's high-risk-for-dog-rabies list, current as of 13 March 2025. For a family pet, that classification changes very little. Its real force is a ban on commercial dog shipments, in place since 2022, not a titre test for an owner bringing home their own dog or cat.

  sections:
    - heading: "The high-risk listing, and what it actually restricts"
      body: |
        CFIA's high-risk list groups Malawi with dozens of other countries, a classification that sounds like it should trigger blood tests and quarantine bookings the way the CDC's equivalent list does for the same dog entering the United States. Canada's system works differently. The listing's practical bite falls on commercial dog imports, stopped entirely since 28 September 2022. A personal pet, travelling with the owner named on its documents, follows the lighter standard process: vaccination, certificate, no serology test, no quarantine.

        Worth checking regardless: CFIA presumes a dog under 8 months old, not accompanied by its named owner, is a commercial import. That presumption has teeth from a high-risk country, so confirm directly with CFIA if age or travel arrangements sit anywhere near that line.

    - heading: "Why the same two flight numbers work both ways"
      body: |
        Most Southern Africa-to-Canada pet moves this site documents need a European stopover before an African connection even begins. Ethiopian Airlines collapses this route into two flights under one company, run in reverse of the outbound version: ET877 nonstop from Lilongwe to Addis Ababa, then ET553 nonstop from Addis Ababa to Toronto, both flown on the same aircraft type.

        Qatar Airways' newer codeshare into Lilongwe, added alongside Kenya Airways in October 2025, is worth knowing about as it matures, but confirm live-animal cargo terms directly before relying on it. Ethiopian's established single-carrier routing remains the more dependable choice for now.
