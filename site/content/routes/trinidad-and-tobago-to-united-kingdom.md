  overview: |
    British Airways is the only airline flying nonstop between Piarco and London Gatwick, and it will not carry a dog or cat in the cabin under any circumstances. Every pet on this route travels in the hold, booked through IAG Cargo rather than the normal passenger desk, and Trinidad and Tobago's status as an unlisted country under GB's pet travel rules means the paperwork takes months, not weeks, to complete.

    The core obstacle is a rabies antibody titre test followed by a fixed 90-day wait, which cannot be shortened regardless of how quickly the lab turns the result around. Dogs also need a timed tapeworm treatment in the days before arrival. Cats skip that step but face the same titre and waiting period as dogs.

  sections:
    - heading: "Why Trinidad and Tobago triggers the full unlisted-country process"
      body: |
        Great Britain sorts pet-import origins into listed and unlisted countries, and Trinidad and Tobago sits in the unlisted group. That means a rabies antibody blood test is compulsory, taken at least 30 days after the primary vaccination, with a result of at least 0.5 IU/ml. From the date that blood was drawn, GB imposes a flat 90-day wait before the pet is allowed to land, a rule that applies however clean the paperwork looks.

        Trinidad's own rabies history complicates the picture slightly. Dog-mediated rabies was eliminated from the island in 1914, according to the Global Alliance for Rabies Control, but Trinidad still records occasional bat-transmitted rabies cases, a risk Tobago does not share. That distinction affects local vet risk assessments more than it affects the GB entry rules, which apply the same unlisted-country process regardless of which island the pet departs from.

    - heading: "The tapeworm rule and the IAG Cargo booking process"
      body: |
        GB's tapeworm treatment requirement for dogs applies to arrivals from every country except Finland, Ireland, Northern Ireland, Malta and Norway. Trinidad and Tobago is not exempt, so any dog on this route needs a praziquantel treatment given between 24 and 120 hours before landing at Gatwick, with the exact date and time recorded by the treating vet. Cats are not subject to this rule at all.

        Booking the flight itself works differently from a normal passenger ticket. British Airways routes all live animal cargo through IAG Cargo, which checks pets in at a separate cargo facility roughly four hours before departure, not at the terminal check-in desk. Most owners submit a pets enquiry through IAG Cargo's website first and get matched with a pet travel partner who handles the crate inspection and paperwork check before the booking is confirmed.
