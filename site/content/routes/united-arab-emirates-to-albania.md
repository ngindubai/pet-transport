content:
  h1: "Pet Transport from the United Arab Emirates to Albania"
  overview: |
    Run this route in reverse, Albania to the UAE, and MOCCAE waives the rabies titre test outright, because Albania sits on its own short list of low-risk countries. Run it the way this page does, and the test comes back. The UAE has no matching entry on the EU-aligned approved list Albania itself follows, so a UAE-origin pet needs the blood test and the three-month wait that goes with it.

    Nothing else about this route is unusual. Microchip, vaccination, a bilingual health certificate, no quarantine for a compliant pet. It's the titre test and its wait that stretch this to sixteen weeks or more, not the flight and not the destination paperwork.

  sections:
    - heading: "Why the same test skips one direction and not the other"
      body: |
        Albania's import rules mirror the EU's own framework closely enough that its practical effect is the same: pets from a shortlist of roughly 40 countries, the UK, US, Canada, Australia among them, clear entry without a blood test. The UAE isn't on that list. Rabies is still present in its stray dog and cat population, which is exactly the kind of fact that keeps a country off it.

        So the direction matters more than the route itself. A pet moving from Albania to the UAE clears MOCCAE's own low-risk exemption without a titre test. The same pet moving the other way, into Albania, needs one. Confirm current status with AKU close to travel regardless, since approved-country lists get reviewed.

    - heading: "What MOCCAE asks of a departing pet"
      body: |
        Leaving the UAE follows the same process whatever the destination: an online MOCCAE application through the UAE Pass portal, parasite treatment inside the 14 days before travel, and a final in-person check at an approved clinic at DXB or AUH. AED 100 covers the certificate for up to two animals on a personal move. It's valid 30 days and can't be reissued once that runs out.

        Time this certificate against the end of the titre wait, not against when you first start the process. Getting it too early wastes the 30-day window before the pet is actually clear to fly.

    - heading: "Getting off the ground: Istanbul or Doha, never nonstop"
      body: |
        Turkish Airlines flies Dubai to Istanbul, then on to Tirana, the same pairing that moves pets the opposite way on the Albania-to-UAE route. Istanbul's dedicated Pet Lounge takes connecting animals on a layover over 75 minutes, a genuine benefit given that neither leg here is a direct flight.

        Qatar Airways via Doha is a real alternative if the Istanbul connection doesn't fit the certificate and titre dates. Check cargo space on the specific connecting sector either way; a seat being available on the passenger cabin says nothing about whether the hold has room for a crate that day.

    - heading: "What actually sets the sixteen-to-twenty-week timeline"
      body: |
        Strip out the titre test and this route would take six to eight weeks, similar to Albania's other UAE-bound pages. The test itself adds at least four months once the 30-day pre-draw wait and the 3-month post-draw wait are both counted, and neither can be compressed by paying more or moving faster.

        Costs run lighter than the timeline suggests. MOCCAE's own fee is modest, and the biggest single cost is the Dubai-Tirana cargo booking itself, typically USD 900 to USD 2,200 depending on crate size and whether the flight runs through Istanbul or Doha.
