content:
  h1: "Pet Transport from United Arab Emirates to Brunei: 2026 Guide"
  overview: |
    Brunei keeps a short, named list of scheduled countries exempt from its titre test and the worst of its quarantine rules: Australia, Ireland, New Zealand, Singapore and the UK. The UAE isn't on it. That single fact means a pet leaving Dubai or Abu Dhabi for Bandar Seri Begawan faces the full non-scheduled pathway: a FAVN titre test, and a facility quarantine stay of around two weeks even with a clean result.

    Add the mandatory Singapore transfer, since Royal Brunei Airlines carries no pet cargo for private owners, and this route runs a realistic 14 to 18 weeks door to door.

  sections:
    - heading: "Why UAE-origin pets don't get Brunei's lighter treatment"
      body: |
        Brunei's scheduled-country system exists to reward pets from places DAA considers a low enough rabies risk to skip the titre test and shorten quarantine, provided the pet has lived there continuously for at least six months. The named list is short and specific, and the UAE simply isn't part of it. That means the standard sequence applies in full: microchip before vaccination, a 30-day gap, a FAVN blood draw showing at least 0.5 IU/ml, and then a quarantine stay of roughly 14 days at a government-approved facility even when every document checks out.

        None of this is a UAE-specific penalty. It's the default treatment Brunei applies to any country that hasn't earned scheduled status, and it's worth planning for from the outset rather than assuming a lighter path.

    - heading: "The Singapore leg, and Brunei's breed rules"
      body: |
        Every pet on this route transfers through Singapore, since Royal Brunei Airlines has no live animal cargo product for private movers. Emirates' Dubai-Singapore leg is the natural first step, feeding into a regional connection onward to Bandar Seri Begawan. It's worth checking Brunei's breed restrictions before committing to this move too: Pit Bull Terriers, Dogo Argentino and Japanese Tosa are banned outright, while Bull Mastiffs, Dobermans and German Shepherds face conditions including mandatory muzzling in public.
