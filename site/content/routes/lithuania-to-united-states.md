  sections:
    - heading: "EU low-risk status: what it changes and what it doesn't"
      body: |
        When CDC introduced the mandatory Dog Import Form (DIF) in August 2024, it also classified countries into risk categories. EU countries including Lithuania are low-risk. For low-risk countries, the DIF process is administrative: no advance scheduling with CDC, no veterinary review by CDC, no additional waiting periods. You fill in the form, submit it, and receive a confirmation to present at the US border.

        What the low-risk status does not change is the mandatory nature of the DIF itself. Every dog entering the US must have one, regardless of low-risk status. It is the form that catches people, not the requirements around it.

        Cats entering from Lithuania face no DIF requirement. The VMVT-endorsed health certificate and evidence of current vaccination are what matter for cats.

    - heading: "Airports and routing from Lithuania to the US"
      body: |
        Lithuania's main airports are Vilnius International (VNO) and Kaunas (KUN). Both have connecting services to major European hubs, though Vilnius has the wider selection.

        Warsaw (WAW) via LOT Polish Airlines is a practical first connection. LOT Cargo operates to some US cities directly. KLM via Amsterdam is another well-used route. Lufthansa connections via Frankfurt give access to FRA's Animal Lounge and its strong US cargo network.

        The full journey from Vilnius to an East Coast US city typically takes 12-15 hours including the European hub connection. West Coast US cities (LAX, SEA, SFO) add 3-4 more hours. All legs of the cargo booking need written confirmation of live animal acceptance before you finalise.

faqs:
  - question: "What is the CDC low-risk classification for Lithuania and what does it mean in practice?"
    answer: "CDC classifies countries based on rabies risk and screwworm status. Lithuania, as an EU member state, is low-risk. In practice, this means the CDC Dog Import Form process is administrative only: no advance CDC approval, no veterinary confinement period, no additional waiting period. You complete the DIF online, receive a confirmation, and present it at the US border alongside the USDA health certificate."
  - question: "How does the VMVT endorsement work for the USDA health certificate?"
    answer: "Your Lithuanian vet issues the health certificate in USDA-compatible format. The VMVT (State Food and Veterinary Service of Lithuania) then endorses the certificate, confirming the vet is officially authorised and the certificate meets international standards. This is equivalent to the USDA APHIS endorsement step done in the USA when moving pets in the other direction. VMVT endorsement typically takes 1-3 working days."
  - question: "Which US states can my pet enter directly from cargo at a major airport?"
    answer: "Most major US airports accept live animal cargo imports: JFK, EWR, ORD, LAX, IAD, MIA, ATL, and others. Pets enter through USDA and CBP inspection at the airport of first US arrival. Hawaii requires its own HDOA programme and cannot be the first US entry point for most pets without prior HDOA registration. Check your specific destination airport's live animal cargo capabilities."
