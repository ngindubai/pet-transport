  overview: "Slovakia is an EU member state, and UK pets follow the standard listed third country rules: Animal Health Certificate from an APHA-authorised official vet, current microchip, and valid rabies vaccination. No titre test. No quarantine for compliant pets. The main practical consideration for this route is airport connectivity.   sections:
    - heading: "The Vienna routing for Slovakia"
      body: |
        Bratislava Airport (BTS) serves mainly Ryanair and Wizz Air low-cost routes, which are not suitable for pet cargo. For pets, the practical routing is through Vienna International Airport (VIE), which has extensive UK connections via Austrian Airlines, British Airways, and Lufthansa Cargo.

        Vienna is approximately 60km from Bratislava, around 45 minutes by road. A pet transport agent can arrange road collection from the cargo terminal at Vienna and delivery to your address in Slovakia. Alternatively, if you are driving to Slovakia, you can pick up your pet from the Vienna cargo facility yourself.

        For pets entering the EU at Vienna, the AHC is checked at the Austrian border inspection post (BIP). Once cleared, the pet can travel freely within the Schengen area, including into Slovakia, without further checks at internal borders.

    - heading: "Driving to Slovakia as an alternative"
      body: |
        Some owners relocating to Slovakia find it simpler to drive, with their pet accompanying them in the car on a ferry crossing to France or Belgium. The AHC is checked at the first EU entry point (typically Calais or a Belgian port). Once past the EU border, the pet can travel in the vehicle to Slovakia without further border documentation.

        This works well for dogs and cats that travel calmly in vehicles and for owners who have a manageable amount of driving time. The AHC is valid for 4 months for onward EU travel after first entry. Pack the original document in the car, not in luggage.
