  h1: "Pet Transport from United States to Lesotho"
  overview: |
    Delta's nonstop from Atlanta to Johannesburg runs about 17 hours, one of the longest scheduled passenger sectors anywhere. On the return journey, that flight matters because Atlanta has a CDC-registered animal care facility. Heading out from the US to Lesotho, none of that machinery is relevant at all.

    Lesotho's Director of Animal Health asks for a vaccination record, a microchip, and an import permit. No titre test, no facility reservation, no CDC airport restriction. What doesn't change in either direction is the routing: Moshoeshoe I International Airport has one scheduled international connection, and it's Johannesburg.

  sections:
    - heading: "Why the CDC doesn't feature here"
      body: |
        A dog leaving Lesotho for the US, a CDC high-risk country for dog rabies, needs a serology titre test or a 28-day quarantine at a CDC-registered facility on arrival. That's the reverse direction of this exact route. Heading the other way, from the US into Lesotho, none of it applies. The CDC governs animals entering the United States; it has no jurisdiction over what Lesotho asks of arriving pets.

        What Lesotho does ask for is a rabies vaccination given between 30 days and 12 months before arrival, current core vaccinations, a microchip on the documentation, and an import permit from the Director of Animal Health, needed because the US sits outside the SADC bloc that gets a waiver on this step.

    - heading: "The Atlanta-Johannesburg-Maseru routing"
      body: |
        Delta's Atlanta to Johannesburg nonstop is the natural fit for this corridor, long as it is. From O.R. Tambo, Airlink is the only scheduled carrier into Maseru, flying the route roughly 21 times a week on a journey of about 65 minutes.

        The two legs sit on separate air waybills. Budget for a ground agent at O.R. Tambo to manage the same-day handoff, and book the Airlink sector at least 48 hours ahead, since it runs on its own booking rhythm separate from the long-haul flight.

faqs:
  - question: "Does the CDC's high-risk country rule apply when sending a pet from the US to Lesotho?"
    answer: "No. The CDC's rules, including the titre test and the facility-airport requirement, govern pets entering the United States. They don't apply to a pet leaving the US for Lesotho. Lesotho's own Director of Animal Health sets the rules for this direction, and a titre test isn't one of them."
  - question: "Is there a direct flight from the US to Lesotho?"
    answer: "No. Moshoeshoe I International Airport in Maseru has one scheduled international connection, to Johannesburg, flown only by Airlink. Delta's Atlanta to Johannesburg nonstop is the natural long-haul leg, followed by the Airlink transfer into Maseru."
  - question: "Does my pet need a titre test to enter Lesotho?"
    answer: "No. Lesotho's own import rules do not require a rabies titre test from any country of origin. A rabies vaccination given between 30 days and 12 months before arrival, current core vaccinations, a microchip, and an import permit from the Director of Animal Health cover the requirements."
  - question: "Who issues the Lesotho import permit for a US-origin pet?"
    answer: "The Director of Animal Health, within Lesotho's Ministry of Agriculture, Food Security and Nutrition. The permit is required because the US is not a SADC member state. Apply as early as convenient, since the office doesn't publish a fixed processing time."
  - question: "How long should I budget for this route overall?"
    answer: "Eight to twelve weeks in most cases. There's no titre test or CDC step to plan around on this direction, so the main lead times are the USDA endorsement, the Lesotho import permit application, and coordinating the Delta booking with the Airlink connection into Maseru."
