content:
  h1: "Pet Transport from Germany to Iceland"
  overview: |
    Fourteen days. That's how long a fully vaccinated, EU pet passport holding German dog still spends locked in isolation the moment it lands at Keflavik, because Iceland's quarantine rule does not bend for EEA neighbours. Germany sits on MAST's Category 1 list, which shortens the titre test wait considerably compared with a non-EU country, but it does nothing to the quarantine requirement itself.

    Budget for two separate things here: the paperwork before departure, and the two weeks after arrival that your pet spends at an approved isolation station before you can take them home. Both are non-negotiable, and the isolation station space needs booking early because intake dates are limited.

  sections:
    - heading: "Why Category 1 status still ends in quarantine"
      body: |
        Germany's place on MAST's Category 1 list works entirely on the pre-export side. The rabies titre test blood draw needs to happen at least 30 days after vaccination, same as most EU routes, and once the result clears at 0.5 IU/ml or higher, there is no additional fixed wait tacked on. Compare that with a Category 2 country, where a further 90-day wait runs from the blood draw date. Germany avoids that.

        What Category 1 status does not touch is the isolation period on arrival. Every dog and cat entering Iceland, regardless of where it travelled from, spends a minimum of 14 days at Einangrunarstoedin i Reykjanesbae or Einangrunarstoedin Mosel. It is a genuine biosecurity measure protecting Iceland's rabies-free, largely disease-free island status, not a penalty for incomplete paperwork. Owners moving from elsewhere in the EU are sometimes caught out by this, since most other EU destinations on this site require no quarantine at all for a compliant pet.

    - heading: "Booking around Icelandair's cargo change"
      body: |
        Icelandair flies Frankfurt to Keflavik directly, close to 10 times a week, in under four hours. Until late 2024 that was the obvious way to move a pet on this route as excess or checked baggage. That option closed on 1 November 2024. Pets now travel as manifested cargo through Icelandair Cargo, which means booking through a cargo agent rather than adding a pet at airport check-in.

        Lufthansa also flies the Frankfurt to Keflavik sector, roughly 8 times weekly, and operates its own live animal facility at Frankfurt, the Frankfurt Animal Lounge. If Icelandair Cargo's schedule or space does not line up with your dates, Lufthansa is worth pricing as the alternative, particularly since its Frankfurt hold facility is well established for pet handling.
