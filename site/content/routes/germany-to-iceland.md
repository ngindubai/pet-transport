  h1: "Pet Transport from Germany to Iceland"
  overview: |
    Iceland has never had a case of rabies in a domestic dog or cat, and it intends to keep it that way. That single fact explains almost everything unusual about this route: the mandatory 14-day quarantine, the titre test requirement that catches EU owners off guard, and the limited quarantine capacity that decides your travel date more than any flight schedule does.

    Most owners moving within the EU assume an EU pet passport will cover them anywhere in the EEA. It won't here. MAST, Iceland's Food and Veterinary Authority, runs a national scheme that goes well beyond the EU baseline, because Iceland is not in the EU customs and veterinary union in the way most member states are. Germany's export side is simple. Iceland's import side is not.

    Plan around the quarantine station, not the airline. Stations take new arrivals roughly every three weeks and book out months in advance, so that reservation, not your flight, should be the first thing you lock in.
  sections:
    - heading: "Why an EU pet passport doesn't work here"
      body: |
        Iceland is a member of the EEA and Schengen area, and its own pets travel to the EU almost as easily as an EU member state's would, because Iceland is listed as equivalent to the EU under Regulation (EU) No 577/2013 for outbound movement. Coming the other way is different.

        MAST does not accept the standard blue EU pet passport, or the third-country Animal Health Certificate format used for non-EU arrivals into most of Europe. Instead it requires its own Certificate of Health and Origin, completed by a German vet using MAST's category-specific template (D1 for dogs, C1 for cats). Germany's classification as a Category 1, rabies-free country simplifies one part of the process: once your pet's titre test result is satisfactory, there's no further 90-day wait, unlike pets from countries where rabies is merely controlled rather than absent. The quarantine and the MAST-format certificate still apply regardless.
    - heading: "Getting a flight that will actually carry your pet"
      body: |
        This route has fewer live workable options than the flight schedules suggest. Lufthansa flies direct from Frankfurt to Keflavik and will carry pets, in the cabin under 8kg or as excess baggage in the hold for larger animals, registered at least 72 hours ahead. Snub-nosed breeds cannot travel in the hold.

        Icelandair serves four German cities into Keflavik, but its passenger aircraft stopped carrying pets as checked baggage on 1 November 2024. Live animals now move exclusively on its cargo freighters, which fly only via Los Angeles or Liege in Belgium, so an Icelandair booking from Germany means trucking your pet to Belgium first through a licensed cargo agent. For most German departure points, Lufthansa's direct routing is the more practical choice.
    - heading: "The quarantine stay itself"
      body: |
        Two MAST-approved stations currently take pets: Einangrunarstodin i Reykjanesbae, close to Keflavik airport, and Einangrunarstodin Mosel. Both charge a flat fee for the 14-day stay, currently around ISK 220,000 (roughly EUR 1,500), which covers transport from the airport, food, housing and veterinary checks, plus a separate booking deposit.

        Book this before anything else. New intake happens on set dates roughly three weeks apart, and slots fill up well ahead of the date. Owners who book flights first and quarantine second are the ones who end up rearranging everything.
faqs:
  - question: "Does my pet need to quarantine in Iceland even though Germany is in the EU?"
    answer: "Yes. Iceland's 14-day quarantine applies to every arriving pet regardless of origin, including from other EU and EEA countries. It is Iceland's own national rule, driven by its rabies-free status, not an EU requirement, so being an EU country doesn't exempt Germany from it."
  - question: "Is a rabies titre test really necessary for a pet moving from Germany, an EU country?"
    answer: "Yes. Unlike most intra-EU moves, where a pet passport and current rabies vaccination are enough, Iceland requires a rabies antibody (FAVN) titre test from every arriving pet. Because Germany is classed by MAST as a rabies-free Category 1 country, there's no additional 90-day wait after a satisfactory result, which is the one part of the process Iceland does simplify for EU arrivals."
  - question: "Can I fly my pet directly from Frankfurt to Keflavik?"
    answer: "With Lufthansa, yes, in the cabin for pets under 8kg or in the hold as excess baggage for larger dogs, registered at least 72 hours before departure. Icelandair also flies Frankfurt-Keflavik for passengers, but it stopped carrying pets as checked baggage in November 2024, so an Icelandair booking now means routing your pet via its cargo freighters through Liege or Los Angeles instead."
  - question: "How far in advance should I book the Iceland quarantine station?"
    answer: "As early as possible, ideally as soon as your travel plans firm up. The two approved stations take new arrivals only around every three weeks and their slots are commonly booked out months ahead. This is usually the step that fixes your final travel date, more than flight availability does."
