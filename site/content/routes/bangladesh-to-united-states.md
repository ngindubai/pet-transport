  sections:
    - heading: "The CDC high-risk classification and how it affects your move"
      body: |
        The US Centers for Disease Control and Prevention (CDC) classifies Bangladesh as a high-risk country for dog-mediated rabies. This classification triggers specific requirements for all dogs entering the United States from Bangladesh, regardless of the dog's vaccination history or the owner's nationality.

        The titre test pathway is the preferred option for most owners. The dog must have a valid rabies vaccination; blood is drawn at least 30 days after the most recent vaccination; the sample is tested at a USDA-recognised laboratory; and the result (0.5 IU/ml or above) must be at least 28 days old at the time of US arrival. A reservation at a CDC-registered animal care facility at the US destination airport is also required, even with a valid titre test result.

        The alternative is a 28-day quarantine at a CDC-registered facility at the US airport of entry, at the owner's expense. This pathway does not require a pre-travel titre test but is significantly more costly and involves 28 days of separation from your dog after you arrive in the USA.

        Cats moving from Bangladesh to the USA have no federal vaccination or titre test requirements. A health certificate from a Bangladeshi vet is recommended for airline travel.

    - heading: "Choosing your US airport and the role of the CDC Dog Import Form"
      body: |
        Dogs from CDC high-risk countries must arrive at a US airport that has a CDC-registered animal care facility. Not every US airport qualifies. Before booking any cargo space, confirm that your intended US destination airport is on the current approved list at cdc.gov/importation. Major airports with qualifying facilities include New York JFK, Los Angeles LAX, Chicago ORD, Miami MIA, Atlanta ATL, Washington Dulles IAD, and San Francisco SFO, among others.

        The CDC Dog Import Form is a mandatory online form that must be completed before dogs from high-risk countries arrive in the USA. It documents the dog, vaccination history, titre test result, and CDC-registered facility reservation. The form is completed at cdc.gov/importation. Arriving without a completed form can result in the dog being held or refused entry.

        Qatar Airways via Doha and Emirates via Dubai are the most practical routing options from Dhaka to major US gateway airports, with good frequency and live animal cargo acceptance on suitable aircraft.
