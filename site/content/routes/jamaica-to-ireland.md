  h1: "Pet Transport Jamaica to Ireland: 2026 Guide"
  overview: "Jamaica is classified as a listed third country under EU Implementing Regulation 2026/636, which removes the FAVN titre test and 90-day waiting period from the Jamaica-to-Ireland preparation. The Ireland-specific requirements are a tapeworm treatment for dogs (24 hours to 5 days before arrival) and 24-hour advance notice to DAFM (the Department of Agriculture, Food and the Marine).   sections:
    - heading: "Jamaica's listed status and the VSD export process"
      body: |
        EU Implementing Regulation 2026/636 classifies Jamaica as a listed third country, meaning pets from Jamaica are exempt from the FAVN titre test and the 90-day waiting period that applies to unlisted countries. Ireland, as an EU member state, follows this classification in full.

        The Veterinary Services Division (VSD) within MICAF handles companion animal exports from Jamaica. A VSD-registered official veterinarian issues the export health certificate, and a separate VSD export permit is required (approximately J$2,000). DHLPP vaccination is required by VSD in addition to rabies. Apply for the export permit 4-6 weeks before travel; the health certificate is issued within 10 days of departure.

    - heading: "Tapeworm treatment and the DAFM notification requirement"
      body: |
        Ireland requires all dogs entering from outside the EU to receive tapeworm treatment using praziquantel between 24 hours and 5 days before arrival. The treating vet must record the product name, dose, date, and exact time of treatment in the health certificate. The window is measured against the actual arrival time, not the departure date, so coordinate carefully between your vet and cargo agent when scheduling the treatment.

        DAFM requires 24-hour advance notification before non-EU pets arrive at an Irish Border Control Post. Submit via the DAFM online notification system at agriculture.gov.ie or contact the BCP at Dublin Airport directly. Provide the pet's details, health certificate reference, flight number, and expected arrival time. Late notification can delay clearance on arrival.

        Dublin Airport (DUB) is Ireland's primary BCP for non-EU pet cargo arrivals. Shannon (SNN) and Cork (ORK) are designated but have more restricted coverage. Routing from Jamaica will typically be via a US gateway (Aer Lingus flies JFK-DUB and BOS-DUB) or via London (British Airways via LHR).
