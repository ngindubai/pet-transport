  overview: "Jamaica pets get to skip the FAVN titre test on the way to Ireland. The TRACES pre-arrival notification, though, applies regardless, and skipping that one causes exactly the same problems at Dublin Airport as skipping a vaccination would.   sections:
    - heading: "Why no titre test: the EU Group 2 listing"
      body: |
        Jamaica appears on the EU's Group 2 list under Implementing Regulation (EU) 2024/1130, a classification that removes the FAVN titre test requirement for pets entering EU member states, Ireland included. That's a genuine time saving. The unlisted-country sequence used by countries like the Dominican Republic, with its 30-day wait then 90-day wait either side of the blood draw, simply doesn't apply here.

        It's tempting to read the exemption as "less paperwork all round." It isn't. It only removes the titre test.

    - heading: "TRACES: the step the exemption doesn't touch"
      body: |
        Ireland's TRACES pre-arrival notification sits outside the titre test question entirely. It's a separate EU system, the Trade Control and Expert System, used to notify Irish authorities before a pet arrives. Every pet entering Ireland needs it, whether the country of origin required a titre test or not.

        Compare this with the Dominican Republic route: that one needs both the titre test and TRACES. Jamaica needs only TRACES. The paperwork burden is genuinely lighter, but "lighter" isn't "absent," and DAFM applies the same non-compliance rules either way: no informal waivers, penalty quarantine or return of the animal for gaps in the documentation.

    - heading: "Flight options from Kingston or Montego Bay to Dublin"
      body: |
        British Airways via London Heathrow is the most established connection for pets leaving Jamaica for Ireland, cargo only with no cabin service. Aer Lingus, also cargo only, covers the Heathrow to Dublin leg or an alternative routing depending on which dates line up. Neither carrier currently offers a cabin option on this corridor, so crate booking needs to happen early.
