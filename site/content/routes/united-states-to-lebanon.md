  sections:
    - heading: "Lebanese Ministry of Agriculture permit: the first task"
      body: |
        Lebanon's Directorate General of Agriculture (DGA) controls companion animal imports. You need their permit before your pet can enter. Apply at agriculture.gov.lb with your pet's microchip number, vaccination records, breed, and planned travel date.

        The Lebanese permit process can be slower than its documentation suggests, particularly in recent years. Apply 8-10 weeks before travel and follow up after submitting. Do not book cargo until you have the permit confirmed.

        At Beirut Rafic Hariri International Airport (BEY), the DGA inspection team checks the permit alongside the USDA APHIS health certificate. A complete document set clears without quarantine.

    - heading: "USDA APHIS endorsement for Lebanon"
      body: |
        USDA APHIS is the US federal authority that endorses health certificates for international pet exports. Your USDA-accredited vet examines your pet and issues the certificate. USDA APHIS then endorses it through their regional office network or the VSPS (Veterinary Services Process Streamlining) online system.

        For Lebanon, a non-EU destination, the certificate format covers Lebanon's requirements. APHIS endorsement fees are fixed federally: approximately USD 38 per endorsement plus the vet's own examination fee. APHIS endorsement via a regional office typically takes 1-3 business days; the VSPS portal may be faster.

        The endorsed certificate must be issued within 10 days of travel to Lebanon. Time your vet appointment and APHIS endorsement accordingly.

    - heading: "New York or Chicago to Beirut: routing"
      body: |
        Turkish Airlines via Istanbul (IST) is generally the most direct routing from JFK or Chicago O'Hare (ORD). Turkish operates regularly on both transatlantic routes and IST-BEY is a short Middle Eastern hop. Turkish Airlines Cargo handles live animal freight on many routes.

        Gulf hub options via Emirates (DXB) or Qatar Airways (DOH) work well from JFK or Washington Dulles (IAD). Both DXB and DOH are close to Beirut, keeping the final leg short. Emirates SkyCargo and Qatar Airways Cargo both have established live animal procedures.

        Middle East Airlines (MEA), Lebanon's own carrier, connects to BEY from various European hubs. From the USA, routing via London or Paris then MEA to Beirut is possible if you prefer a European transit point. MEA Cargo handles live animals on select services.

        Total transit from JFK to BEY via Istanbul typically runs 14-18 hours. Gulf hub routings are similar.
