content:
  h1: "Pet Transport from the United States to Zambia"
  overview: |
    Fly a dog from the US to Zambia and Emirates can keep the whole booking on one airline: a nonstop hop to Dubai, then its own nonstop service on to Lusaka. Not every Zambia route on this site can say that. It also happens to be the easier half of this move. Zambia's own import rules ask for less than the US demands of a pet travelling the opposite direction, since Zambia isn't on any equivalent high-risk list of its own.

    That doesn't make this route paperwork-free. The Zambian import permit runs on direct correspondence with the Department of Veterinary Services in Lusaka rather than an online system, and getting that permit in hand before the USDA certificate expires is the part that actually needs planning.

  sections:
    - heading: "Why this direction is lighter than the return trip"
      body: |
        A pet flying from Zambia into the US faces the CDC's full high-risk-country pathway: microchip before vaccine, a titre test from a CDC-approved lab, arrival at one of six designated facility airports. None of that applies here. Zambia's own rules simply want a rabies vaccination given between 30 days and 12 months before entry, a microchip, and a Veterinary Permit obtained in advance.

        Worth keeping proof of a titre result on file anyway, even though Zambia doesn't require one itself. If the connecting routing passes through a country or airline with its own serology rule, having the paperwork ready avoids a scramble at a transit point.

    - heading: "The USDA side: straightforward, but not instant"
      body: |
        A USDA-accredited vet has to carry out the health examination, usually within 10 days of travel, and complete the certificate. That certificate then goes to USDA APHIS Veterinary Services for endorsement, a fixed USD 38 fee, processed in 2 to 10 business days depending on whether it's submitted by drop-off, mail or the VEHCS electronic system.

        Not every vet holds USDA accreditation. Confirm this before booking the appointment rather than discovering it the week before travel, since a certificate signed by a non-accredited vet won't clear USDA's endorsement step.

    - heading: "Getting to Lusaka, and the arrival step that's easy to forget"
      body: |
        Emirates' Dubai-Lusaka service, flown as EK713/714, already appears elsewhere on this site as an established nonstop route, and pairing it with one of Emirates' own nonstop US-to-Dubai sectors, out of JFK, LAX, Miami or Washington Dulles, keeps the whole journey on a single carrier. Ethiopian Airlines via Addis Ababa and Kenya Airways via Nairobi both work too, with Ethiopian offering a small-pet cabin option that Emirates and Kenya Airways don't.

        Whichever routing gets used, the move isn't finished at customs. The importer has to notify the nearest Zambian Veterinary Officer on arrival at Kenneth Kaunda International so the pet and its documents can be inspected. Skipping that step, even with otherwise compliant paperwork, is a documented ground for the animal to be held.
