  overview: |
    IAM waives its rabies titre test for pets from a country or territory with no rabies incidents in the past two years. The Netherlands qualifies on paper: no domestic terrestrial rabies, and World Organisation for Animal Health recognition to match. What's missing is a published IAM list that names the Netherlands specifically the way this site's Hong Kong-to-Macau guide could point to Hong Kong's own decades-long record.

    Treat this as a genuine likelihood rather than settled fact. Confirm with IAM before assuming the test is skipped, and build the rest of the journey, a Hong Kong transfer plus the 30-day IAM import licence, around a confirmed flight date either way.

  sections:
    - heading: "A clean record, an unconfirmed exemption"
      body: |
        The reasoning here isn't a stretch. IAM's own stated rule is simple: no rabies incidents in the past two years, no titre test required. The Netherlands meets that bar by a wide margin. But a rule stated in general terms and a name checked off a specific list are two different things, and only the second one removes all doubt.

        Where a fact can't be pinned down on a site that deals in pet health and safety, the honest approach is to say so rather than assume the more convenient answer. Confirm the Netherlands' status directly with IAM before finalising a titre-free schedule.

    - heading: "KLM's own aircraft rules out its own route"
      body: |
        Macau International Airport carries no long-haul cargo, so every pet transfers through Hong Kong regardless of origin. The twist on this particular corridor is that KLM, the Dutch national carrier, flies Amsterdam to Hong Kong directly on flight KL887, but does so on a Boeing 787, an aircraft type KLM's own policy bars from hold pet transport. Cathay Pacific, running both passenger and dedicated freighter capacity on the same city pair, picks up the booking instead.
