  overview: |
    There is no such thing as a nonstop Caribbean-to-continental-Europe pet cargo booking out of Trinidad. British Airways is the only airline flying nonstop from Piarco at all, and it goes to London Gatwick, so every pet destined for Frankfurt makes a second transfer once it lands in the UK.

    Germany then applies the standard EU sequence for an unlisted third country: a titre test and a fixed 90-day wait from the blood draw, the same rule that governs every EU-bound move on this site regardless of the ultimate destination city.

  sections:
    - heading: "Why every route to Germany goes via London first"
      body: |
        Caribbean Airlines ended its only European service, the Piarco-Gatwick route, back in January 2016, and no carrier has replaced it with a Trinidad-to-continental-Europe option since. British Airways, through IAG Cargo, remains the sole nonstop carrier out of Piarco, which makes London the mandatory first stop for any EU-bound pet from Trinidad and Tobago.

        From Gatwick or a connecting Heathrow sector, Lufthansa Cargo or Condor complete the leg into Frankfurt. Build this second transfer into your planning as its own step, with its own booking and its own handling fee, rather than assuming the London connection happens automatically.

    - heading: "The EU's titre test sequence"
      body: |
        Trinidad and Tobago sits outside the EU's list of low-risk third countries, so Germany requires the full sequence: microchip before vaccination, a 30-day gap before the titre blood draw, a result of at least 0.5 IU/ml, then a fixed 90 days from the draw date before entry, under Commission Delegated Regulation (EU) 2026/131.

        None of the London transfer logistics need to wait for that 90-day period to finish; book the connecting flight once the wait is nearly over, and treat the EU clock, not the UK transfer, as the step that decides your overall schedule.
