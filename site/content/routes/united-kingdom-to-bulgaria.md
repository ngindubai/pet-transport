  sections:
    - heading: "Bulgaria is not rabies-free: why the vaccination record matters"
      body: |
        Unlike some Western European neighbours, Bulgaria is not classified as rabies-free. There is an ongoing low-level risk in wildlife in some rural regions, and Bulgarian authorities take rabies vaccination compliance seriously at the border.

        For UK pets, the vaccination record needs to show a valid current rabies vaccination, with the primary course completed at least 21 days before travel. If your pet is overdue for a booster, address this before booking flights. A booster given before the previous vaccination expired (still within the valid period) takes effect immediately. A booster given after the previous vaccination has already expired is treated as a new primary vaccination, which restarts the 21-day waiting period.

        Keep vaccination documentation clear and complete. The BFSA border inspector checks not just that a vaccination was given, but that the timing meets the 21-day rule.

    - heading: "Choosing the right airline for pet cargo to Bulgaria"
      body: |
        Many UK-to-Bulgaria routes are served by low-cost carriers such as Wizz Air and Ryanair, which are popular for passenger travel but are not appropriate for most pets. These airlines only allow very small pets in the cabin (typically under 8kg including carrier) and do not offer in-hold cargo for pets.

        For medium or large dogs, and for any pet that cannot travel in-cabin, you need a full-service carrier with a cargo division. Bulgaria Air is the national carrier and the most direct option, flying from Heathrow to Sofia Airport (SOF). British Airways also serves the route via IAG Cargo. Lufthansa Cargo is an alternative via Frankfurt.

        Contact the cargo desk of your chosen airline directly to confirm pet acceptance, crate dimensions, and whether your specific route and aircraft type permit live animals. Not all aircraft types carry live animals, so confirm the specific equipment before booking.

faqs:
  - question: "Do I need a titre test to take my pet to Bulgaria from the UK?"
    answer: "No. The UK holds listed third country status for EU pet travel, so no titre test is required. You need an ISO microchip, a valid rabies vaccination with the primary course completed at least 21 days before travel, and an AHC issued within 10 days of travel by an APHA-authorised official vet."
  - question: "Can I take my pet to Bulgaria on Wizz Air or Ryanair?"
    answer: "Only if your pet is very small and can travel in the cabin in a carrier. Wizz Air and Ryanair allow small pets in-cabin (typically up to around 8kg including carrier) but do not offer cargo services for pets. Medium and large dogs, and most cats that cannot travel in-cabin, cannot use these airlines. Full-service carriers such as Bulgaria Air or British Airways are needed for pet cargo."
  - question: "Is Bulgaria safe for pets given the wildlife rabies risk?"
    answer: "Bulgaria has a low-level wildlife rabies risk in some rural areas, but this is not a barrier to importing compliant pets from the UK. Your pet needs a current rabies vaccination, and you should keep up-to-date boosters throughout your time in Bulgaria. Contact a Bulgarian vet on arrival to establish ongoing care and ensure vaccinations are maintained."

    answer: "Sofia Airport (SOF) is the main international cargo entry point. Varna Airport (VAR) serves the Black Sea coast and handles some international flights, but Sofia handles the bulk of cargo connections from the UK. Confirm with your airline which airport your cargo routes through."
