  sections:
    - heading: "Jamaica's listed status: no titre test for Hungary"
      body: |
        EU Implementing Regulation 2026/636 classifies Jamaica as a listed third country, meaning pets from Jamaica are exempt from the FAVN titre test and the 90-day waiting period when entering EU member states including Hungary. NÉBIH (Nemzeti Élelmiszerlánc-biztonsági Hivatal, the National Food Chain Safety Office) still requires the standard EU-format non-commercial health certificate issued by a VSD-registered official veterinarian and endorsed by VSD.

        The VSD issues the export health certificate through a VSD-registered official veterinarian, along with a VSD export permit (approximately J$2,000). DHLPP vaccination is required by VSD in addition to rabies. For dogs, the tapeworm treatment record must be completed in the health certificate before departure. The practical preparation involves confirming microchip and vaccinations, obtaining the VSD permit (4-6 weeks lead time), arranging tapeworm treatment within the correct window, booking cargo to BUD via a connecting hub, and completing the health certificate appointment within 10 days of departure.

    - heading: "Tapeworm treatment, airline choice, and the BUD entry process"
      body: |
        Hungary requires all dogs entering from non-EU countries to receive praziquantel tapeworm treatment between 24 and 120 hours before arrival at the Border Inspection Post at Budapest. The treating vet must record the product name, dose, date, and exact time of treatment in the health certificate. Cats are not subject to this requirement.

        One critical point on airline choice: Wizz Air is the dominant carrier at Budapest Ferenc Liszt International Airport and accounts for a large share of its traffic, but Wizz Air does not accept pets in cabin or cargo on any route. Book through Lufthansa (connections from Jamaica via North American hubs or London to FRA, then FRA-BUD), Austrian Airlines (routing via Vienna, VIE-BUD), or British Airways (LHR connections to BUD). NÉBIH inspectors at BUD conduct documentary and identity checks. Ask your Jamaican vet to confirm the chip number by scanning before completing the certificate to prevent any mismatch on arrival.
