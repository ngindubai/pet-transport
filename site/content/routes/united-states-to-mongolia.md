  h1: "Pet Transport from the United States to Mongolia"
  overview: |
    Every US pet owner researching Mongolia eventually runs into the CDC's high-risk country list and assumes the worst. Relax on that point specifically: the CDC's titre test and quarantine apparatus governs dogs entering the United States, not dogs leaving it. A pet heading from America to Ulaanbaatar skips all of that.

    What replaces it is simpler than expected. Mongolia asks for a microchip, a rabies vaccination from the past year, and a USDA-endorsed health certificate. No titre test, no import permit for a personal animal.

    The genuine planning problem is the flight. Seoul, not any US gateway city directly, is the practical connection point, reached via Korean Air or MIAT Mongolian Airlines' 25 weekly Incheon-Ulaanbaatar service. Istanbul is the credible alternative.

  sections:
    - heading: "Why the CDC high-risk rules don't apply here"
      body: |
        Mongolia sits on the CDC's high-risk list for dog rabies, a fact that matters enormously for a dog entering the United States from Mongolia and barely at all for a dog leaving the United States for Mongolia. The CDC's foreign-vaccinated dog pathway, the titre test, the 28-day quarantine option, all of it governs entry into the US. None of it touches this outbound leg.

        What Mongolia itself asks for on arrival is far lighter: a valid microchip, a current rabies vaccination, and the standard USDA-endorsed export certificate. Confirm the current position with GAVS or a receiving vet close to travel, since published guidance for this specific route is thin, but there's no equivalent CDC-style apparatus to plan around here.

    - heading: "Seoul, not Beijing, is the dependable routing"
      body: |
        Korean Air and MIAT Mongolian Airlines both fly Seoul Incheon to Ulaanbaatar, MIAT at a striking 25 times a week, Korean Air 8 times. Either connects to a US departure through Korean Air's own extensive American network, keeping handling relatively consistent for a live animal in transit.

        Istanbul, reached on Turkish Airlines' own US routes, offers a genuine second option through MIAT or Turkish Airlines' onward Ulaanbaatar service.

        Beijing looks geographically obvious on a map, and there's a real nuance worth knowing: Air China's suspension of dog transport, in effect since 1 February 2025, is written to cover flights into the United States. Read literally, that shouldn't block a dog flying the other way, from the US toward Beijing and on to Ulaanbaatar. It's not a routing to bet on without confirming directly with Air China first, since airlines sometimes apply a restriction more broadly than its stated scope, but it's a detail worth raising with the airline rather than ruling out on assumption alone.

    - heading: "Keeping the return trip in mind"
      body: |
        If this move has a return date attached, even an informal one, it's worth starting the CDC paperwork for the dog's eventual re-entry to the US well before that date arrives. Mongolia's high-risk classification means the titre test and its fixed timing windows apply in full on the way back, even though none of it applies now. Planning both legs from day one avoids a scramble later.
