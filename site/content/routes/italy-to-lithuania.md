  sections:
    - heading: "Breed handling requirements in Lithuania"
      body: |
        Lithuania does not have the same outright Pit Bull ban as Latvia. Instead, Lithuanian law requires muzzling and lead use in public for a list of potentially dangerous breeds: American Pit Bull Terrier, Rottweiler, American Staffordshire Terrier, Bull Terrier, Dogo Argentino, Fila Brasileiro, Tosa Inu, and crossbreeds.

        If you are moving to Lithuania with one of these dogs, you need to be prepared for these handling requirements from arrival. The State Food and Veterinary Service (SFVS) at vmvt.lt is the authority to consult. Some Lithuanian municipalities add restrictions beyond the national rules.

        For all other breeds, the process is standard EU: EU pet passport, ISO microchip, current rabies vaccination. Simple and relatively quick to arrange.

    - heading: "LOT via Warsaw: the most practical Italy-Vilnius connection"
      body: |
        LOT Polish Airlines is the best-developed carrier for Italian connections to Vilnius. LOT operates from Rome Fiumicino (FCO) and Milan Malpensa (MXP) to Warsaw Chopin (WAW), with regular LOT services to Vilnius (VNO). The WAW-VNO leg is about 1 hour.

        Warsaw is a well-organised hub for Baltic connections. LOT accepts cabin pets on its European routes, and cargo for larger dogs is available with advance booking.

        Ryanair also operates some Italian city-Vilnius routes seasonally, but only for cabin pets. For large dogs, LOT or Lufthansa via Frankfurt is required.
