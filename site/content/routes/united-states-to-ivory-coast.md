content:
  h1: "Pet Transport from United States to Ivory Coast"
  overview: |
    A dog making this trip in reverse, from Abidjan to the US, runs into the CDC's full high-risk rabies pathway: a titre test, a facility airport reservation, no domestic connection allowed afterward. Flying the other way, none of that applies. Cote d'Ivoire doesn't ask a US-origin pet for a rabies antibody test at all.

    What it does ask for is a health certificate in French, or with a certified translation, and an import permit processed by two DSV email addresses rather than any online system. Neither of those steps carries a published fee or turnaround time, which makes them the real planning risk on this route, not a blood test.

    Royal Air Maroc's Casablanca to JFK nonstop and Ethiopian Airlines' Addis Ababa to Dulles nonstop both run in the opposite direction too, giving a US owner the same single-carrier routing options into Abidjan.

  sections:
    - heading: "The CDC's high-risk rules don't apply this direction"
      body: |
        Cote d'Ivoire's place on the CDC's high-risk list for dog rabies governs one direction of travel only: a dog entering the United States from Cote d'Ivoire. That classification triggers a titre test, a CDC Dog Import Form, and arrival restricted to one of six facility airports.

        None of that machinery belongs to Ivorian import law. A US-origin dog or cat enters Cote d'Ivoire needing a rabies vaccination dated between 30 days and 12 months old, a microchip in practice if not strictly by law, and the DSV import permit. No titre test, no facility reservation, no CDC form. The two directions of this same corridor run under entirely separate rulebooks, each written by the destination country, not by some symmetric standard both sides share.

    - heading: "An email permit and a French certificate, not a blood test"
      body: |
        DSV, the Direction des Services Veterinaires under MIRAH, issues the import permit by email. USDA APHIS's own official guidance names the two addresses directly: carv.dsvci@gmail.com and dsv.sdsa2017@gmail.com. Send the completed application and a copy of the rabies certificate there, and expect no published turnaround guarantee in return.

        The health certificate itself carries its own requirement: French language, or a certified translation attached, before Ivorian officials will accept it at Port Bouet International Airport. A USDA-endorsed certificate that's otherwise perfect but written only in English risks being turned away on this point alone, so build translation time into the schedule alongside the standard 3-5 business days for USDA endorsement.

    - heading: "Two nonstops that solve the routing cleanly"
      body: |
        Royal Air Maroc's New York JFK to Casablanca service runs 13 times a week, continuing to Abidjan on the same airline's aircraft throughout. Ethiopian Airlines matches that with a nonstop Washington Dulles to Addis Ababa sector, then its own daily Addis to Abidjan service. Both keep the pet on a single carrier's network from a US gateway to Abidjan, with just one connection in between.

        Air France offers a third option, reaching Paris from several US cities and running up to two daily flights on to Abidjan, useful if neither JFK nor Dulles suits the travel dates. Every one of these carriers restricts snub-nosed breeds from the hold, so confirm that detail against your pet's breed before locking in a booking.
