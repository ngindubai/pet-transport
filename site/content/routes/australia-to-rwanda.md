  sections:
    - heading: "Rwanda's RAB: a professional import process"
      body: |
        The Rwanda Agriculture and Animal Resources Development Board (RAB) at rab.gov.rw handles all companion animal imports. By sub-Saharan African standards, the RAB is an organised body. Its import permit process is documented, its staff are reachable, and the requirements are clear.

        Apply at least eight weeks before travel. You will need your pet's microchip number, full vaccination history, breed, weight, crate dimensions, and intended travel date. Once the RAB issues the permit, you have the central document around which the rest of your preparation organises.

        On arrival at Kigali International Airport (KGL), the RAB inspection team checks the permit alongside the DAFF-endorsed health certificate. For pets from Australia, a low-risk rabies country with proper documentation, the process is thorough but not slow.

    - heading: "Getting from Australia to Kigali"
      body: |
        The shortest cargo routing from Sydney or Melbourne to Kigali goes via Addis Ababa on Ethiopian Airlines. Ethiopian operates from Australia via Singapore (SIN) or Bangkok (BKK) to Addis (ADD), and from ADD, Ethiopian Cargo flies KGL directly. Ethiopian has deep experience across its African network and is the most frequently used option on this corridor.

        Brussels Airlines via Brussels (BRU) is the alternative for owners who prefer a European hub routing or who have other reasons to transit through Europe. You fly Sydney or Melbourne to a major European hub (Amsterdam, Paris, Frankfurt), connect to Brussels, and board a Brussels Airlines BRU-KGL service. Brussels Airlines is a Lufthansa Group carrier with established East Africa operations.

        Total transit time matters for a healthy pet. Addis Ababa routing typically takes 24-30 hours. Brussels routing runs 28-34 hours. Both are within industry norms for southern hemisphere-to-Africa pet cargo, but the journey is substantial. Talk to your vet about fitness to travel for your specific dog or cat before booking.

    - heading: "DAFF endorsement for sub-Saharan Africa"
      body: |
        DAFF (Department of Agriculture, Fisheries and Forestry) endorses Australian export health certificates for all international destinations, not just Europe. For Rwanda, the certificate format follows Rwanda's requirements rather than EU format.

        Your accredited vet issues the certificate after a pre-travel clinical examination. They record microchip number, vaccination details, breed, description, and confirm fitness to travel. DAFF endorses the completed certificate, typically within 1-3 working days.

        The endorsed certificate must be issued within 10 days of travel. Time your vet appointment with a day or two of buffer for DAFF endorsement. The RAB wants to see this document alongside the import permit at KGL.
