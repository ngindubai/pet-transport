  h1: "Pet Transport from Montenegro to Australia"
  overview: |
    Here's the fact that separates this route from most others built for the Balkans on this site: Montenegro sits directly on DAFF's Group 3 approved list, the same category as the United Kingdom or Germany. Albania, right next door, doesn't. That means no 180-day staging country, no detour through a third continent just to qualify.

    It doesn't make this an easy route, though. A rabies titre test with its own 180-day wait after the lab receives the sample, a mandatory Mickleham quarantine stay, and a flight that has to pass through at least two hub cities before it reaches Melbourne, all still apply. Group 3 status removes one layer of complexity. Australia's baseline requirements are demanding regardless of which approved country a pet starts in.

  sections:
    - heading: "What does Group 3 status actually save?"
      body: |
        DAFF sorts every source country into Group 1, Group 2, Group 3 or non-approved. Montenegro is Group 3, the same tier as the UK, Germany, Canada and around 70 other rabies-controlled countries. That status alone means a Montenegrin pet can go straight into Australia's standard process rather than first living somewhere else for six months.

        Compare that with Albania, this site's other recent Balkan build, which is non-approved and needs 180 days staged in the UK or another Group 3 country before Australia will even begin processing the import. Two neighbouring countries, one meaningful difference in DAFF's own classification system.

    - heading: "The RNATT test and the quarantine detail most owners miss"
      body: |
        Group 3 status doesn't skip the titre test. Blood drawn at least 30 days after vaccination, sent to a DAFF-approved lab, needs a result of 0.5 IU/ml or above. From the date that lab receives the sample, a 180-day wait applies before Australian entry, with no shortcuts.

        There is one genuine lever worth pulling, though. If the microchip is identity-verified by the competent authority before that blood draw, and at least 180 days ahead of the flight, Mickleham quarantine drops to a 10-day minimum instead of the default 30 days. It's a sequencing detail, not extra paperwork, and it's worth building into the plan from day one rather than discovering it after the RNATT result is already back. Source: DAFF, agriculture.gov.au.

    - heading: "Getting from Podgorica to Melbourne without a single through-ticket"
      body: |
        Neither Podgorica nor Tivat has a flight anywhere near Australia. The practical chain runs Podgorica to Istanbul on Turkish Airlines, Istanbul to Dubai on Turkish Airlines' own daily service, then Dubai to Melbourne on Emirates SkyCargo, whose established route into Melbourne is one of its regular animal-handling corridors.

        That's two carrier handovers, at Istanbul and at Dubai, each needing its own confirmed cargo transfer arrangement. It's more coordination than a single-airline booking, and it's exactly the kind of detail a pet relocation agent earns their fee sorting out, particularly since Melbourne, not any closer Australian city, is where the flight has to end regardless of where the owner actually lives.
