  - question: "Can I use my pet's EU pet passport for the Indonesia move?"
    answer: "No. Barantin requires a health certificate in its own specified format, not the standard EU pet passport. A UVHVVR-endorsed certificate covering the same information but in the correct format is needed, alongside the FAVN titre test and the rekomendasi import permit."
  - question: "How long does the Barantin rekomendasi take to process from Slovenia?"
    answer: "Plan for 4 to 8 weeks across the two stages: the provincial recommendation letter and the national approval from the Directorate of Animal Health in Jakarta. Begin the application once your FAVN titre result of 0.5 IU/ml or above is confirmed."
  - question: "Does my dog need to be a certain age to enter Indonesia?"
    answer: "Yes. Dogs must be at least 6 months old at the point of entry into Indonesia, regardless of Slovenian export rules. Confirm this before finalising the rekomendasi application, since a younger dog cannot be imported."
  - question: "What happens if my pet's documents don't match on arrival at Jakarta?"
    answer: "Barantin quarantine officers check the rekomendasi, the health certificate, the titre result and the microchip record against each other on arrival. A mismatch, such as an entry port that doesn't match the permit or an expired certificate, extends the standard 7 to 14 day quarantine, sometimes by weeks."
  - question: "Is Bali reachable directly from Slovenia?"
    answer: "No. Pets enter through a mainland port, usually Jakarta, and complete Barantin quarantine there before an onward domestic transfer to Bali. Name Bali as your destination province in the rekomendasi application; the named entry point will still be the mainland port."
