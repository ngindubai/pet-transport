content:
  h1: "Pet Transport from Maldives to Australia: 2026 Guide"
  overview: |
    Nine months is the realistic floor here, not a worst case. DAFF classifies the Maldives as a non-approved country for cat and dog imports, and its own published policy states plainly that direct import from a non-approved country is not permitted. The workaround is a staging country: somewhere on Group 1, 2 or 3 of DAFF's list, where the cat must live for at least 180 consecutive days before an Australian import permit can even be considered.

    Cost is dominated by that residency, not by the flight. A relocation to Singapore or the UK for six months, plus the RNATT titre test, the BICON permit application and a mandatory Mickleham quarantine stay of at least 10 days, adds up to a genuinely different scale of project than any other route in this guide.

  sections:
    - heading: "Why direct import does not exist for this route"
      body: |
        DAFF sorts countries into Group 1 (New Zealand and a couple of Pacific territories), Group 2 (recognised rabies-free countries), Group 3 (rabies-controlled countries such as the UK, US and most of Europe), and everything else, which is non-approved. The Maldives sits in that last category. DAFF's published policy on non-approved countries states that import is not permitted directly and that pets must instead complete residency in an approved country first, assessed case by case.
    - heading: "The 180-day staging period"
      body: |
        The cat needs to live continuously in the chosen staging country for at least 180 days before export to Australia, with its microchip identity verified by a competent authority before the RNATT blood draw. Get the order of operations wrong (verify the chip after the blood draw instead of before) and the shorter 10-day quarantine option is off the table, replaced by a longer minimum stay.
    - heading: "The RNATT test and its 12-month clock"
      body: |
        The Rabies Neutralising Antibody Titre Test result is valid for 12 months from the sample date, and the import permit itself runs on the same expiry. Time the blood draw too early relative to your actual travel date and you risk the test lapsing before DAFF finishes processing the permit.
    - heading: "BICON and the permit wait"
      body: |
        DAFF's own figures put permit processing anywhere from 20 to 123 business days. That is not a typo. Apply as early as the staging country residency requirement allows, and do not book Mickleham quarantine until the permit number actually exists.
    - heading: "Mickleham: the mandatory quarantine everyone underestimates"
      body: |
        Unlike the UK or the EU, where a satisfactory titre test avoids quarantine altogether, Australia quarantines every imported cat and dog at Mickleham near Melbourne regardless of paperwork, for a minimum of 10 days if everything above was done correctly, longer otherwise. Budget for the per-night fee, veterinary inspection charges, and getting the cat to and from the facility once it arrives at Melbourne.
    - heading: "Choosing where to stage"
      body: |
        Singapore is a frequently used staging country from this part of the world, being itself DAFF-approved and reasonably reachable from Male via Singapore Airlines. The UK is another common choice for owners with existing ties there. Either way, plan the 180 days as a real relocation, not a formality.
