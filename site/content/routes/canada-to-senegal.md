  h1: "Pet Transport from Canada to Senegal"
  overview: |
    Montreal offers a genuinely useful shortcut on this route that isn't available from most North American or European cities: a direct Royal Air Maroc service to Casablanca, roughly eight times a week, feeding straight on to Dakar. It's worth weighing against the more familiar Paris connection on Air France, especially if Montreal is closer to home than Toronto.

    The paperwork side has two separate clocks running. Senegal's Import Authorisation, issued by the Directorate of Veterinary Services, only holds for 3 months from the date it's granted. Canada's own CFIA-endorsed certificate has to be booked through a regional office appointment that can't happen after the pet has already left the country. Neither clock waits for the other, so both need planning against the same confirmed flight date.

    No titre test applies to a pet leaving Canada for Senegal. The genuine work here is timing two government documents, from two different countries, against one departure.

  sections:
    - heading: "Blaise Diagne, not the airport some older guides still mention"
      body: |
        Léopold Sédar Senghor Airport, near Yoff, served Dakar's commercial traffic for decades. It closed to scheduled passenger and cargo flights in December 2017, replaced by Blaise Diagne International Airport (DSS), built further out of the city near Diass. The old airport still operates, mostly for cargo, charter and military use, which is part of why some pet travel guides continue to reference it without the update.

        For a pet flying as manifested cargo, whether on Royal Air Maroc, Air France or Turkish Airlines, Blaise Diagne is the airport that matters. Confirm any ground handling arrangements name DSS specifically.

    - heading: "Montreal-Casablanca: a direct option worth weighing against Paris"
      body: |
        Royal Air Maroc's Montreal-Casablanca service runs close to daily and connects on to Royal Air Maroc's own Dakar flights, a genuinely direct single-connection option that isn't replicated from most other North American cities. It's worth booking pet cargo through the airline's cargo desk specifically, since this runs separately from the standard passenger booking flow.

        Air France's Toronto or Montreal to Paris service, codesharing with Air Senegal on around 14 weekly Paris-Dakar flights, offers more day-of-week flexibility if the Casablanca schedule doesn't line up. Turkish Airlines via Istanbul is the fallback for a longer transit when neither of the first two suits your dates.

    - heading: "Two clocks, one departure date"
      body: |
        Senegal's Import Authorisation runs on a 3-month validity window from the date it's granted, not from the date you apply. Apply too early relative to a shifting flight date and it can quietly expire before travel. Canada's CFIA endorsement has the opposite problem: it has a hard deadline before the pet leaves the country and cannot be arranged retroactively.

        Fix the flight date first, then work both government processes backward from it. Treating either one as flexible around the other is the most common way this route runs late.
