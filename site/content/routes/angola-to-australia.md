  h1: "Pet Transport from Angola to Australia"
  overview: |
    TAAG Angola Airlines already flies Luanda to Lisbon every day, the same connection this site's Angola-to-UK page calls the most established route out of the country. For an Australia-bound pet, that daily flight turns into the first step of a much longer journey: Angola is a DAFF non-approved country, so 180 consecutive days in an approved country come first, and Portugal, Group 3 approved, is the obvious place to spend them.

    Nothing about that period moves quickly. A titre test still has to clear at a Portuguese lab during the stay, and Mickleham's 10-day quarantine still waits at the very end, weekday arrivals only.

  sections:
    - heading: "Why does a non-approved country need 180 days somewhere else first?"
      body: |
        DAFF sorts every country into Group 1, 2, 3, or non-approved, and Angola falls into the last category. A direct booking simply isn't offered, whatever an agent might suggest. The animal has to live continuously in an approved country for 180 days before DAFF will even begin processing the import.

        It's a fixed biosecurity period tied to the country of origin's status, not a step that speeds up with extra paperwork.

    - heading: "Why does Portugal make more sense than the UK or France here?"
      body: |
        Because TAAG Angola Airlines already runs the Luanda-Lisbon leg daily, the busiest and most reliable connection out of Angola on this site's own research. Portugal is also DAFF Group 3 approved, so the 180-day clock can start the moment the pet lands in Lisbon, with no separate long-haul flight needed just to reach an approved country.

        Compare that with routes that stage in the UK or France: those work fine for countries whose existing flight network already runs there, but Angola's own traffic runs through Lisbon first. Following the traffic, rather than fighting it, is the genuine advantage.

    - heading: "What closes out the journey once the 180 days are done?"
      body: |
        A rabies titre test, run at an accredited Portuguese laboratory at least 30 days after vaccination, has to clear before DAFF will process the import permit through BICON. Once it does, Qatar Airways flies Lisbon to Doha daily, with direct onward services to Sydney, Melbourne, Perth, Brisbane and Adelaide, a genuinely clean single-carrier finish to the trip.

        Mickleham Post Entry Quarantine Facility in Melbourne takes every arriving pet for a minimum 10 days and only accepts weekday landings. Build the final Lisbon-Australia flight around that rule, not the other way round.
