  overview: |
    Until 11 March 2026, this route didn't exist as a scheduled flight. Turkish Airlines launched direct Istanbul-Yerevan service that day, part of a broader thaw between two countries that still have no formal diplomatic relations and a land border that has stayed shut since 1993. By mid-June the airline had grown it to 14 flights a week.

  sections:
    - heading: "A new flight route on a corridor closed for decades"
      body: |
        Armenia and Turkey have no diplomatic relations, and the land border between them has been shut since the aftermath of the 1993 Karabakh war. For most of the past three decades, there was no scheduled way to fly between the two capitals at all. Turkish Airlines changed that on 11 March 2026, launching daily service as part of a wider normalisation process, and grew it to 14 weekly flights by 15 June to meet demand.

        Because the route is new, confirm current cargo capacity and handling procedures directly with the airline rather than relying on the settled patterns of an established corridor. There's no land alternative either, unlike the Sarpi crossing between Turkey and Georgia, so this flight is genuinely the only way to move a pet between the two countries.

    - heading: "Armenia's simple entry standard"
      body: |
        Once the flight itself is sorted, Armenia's own rules are light. No rabies titre test is required from any country, including Turkey, and no import permit applies to one or two personal pets. Microchipping isn't formally mandated by Armenian law, though every transporter chips anyway, since the health certificate references the chip number and it's the most reliable way to confirm identity at Zvartnots on arrival.

        The certificate itself has a tighter window than many destinations in this build: it must be completed within 5 days of travel, not the 10-day window common elsewhere, so time the vet appointment accordingly.
