  h1: "Pet Transport UK to Mauritius: The Strict Island Rules and 6-Month Process"
  overview: "Mauritius runs one of the strictest pet import regimes in the world. Every requirement is mandatory with no exceptions: FAVN titre test (blood drawn at least 31 days after rabies vaccination), 90-day wait after the blood draw, import permit, neutered or spayed pet, cargo-only transport, and 30-day quarantine on arrival. The total preparation timeline is a minimum of 6 months from starting the process. There are no shortcuts. Missing any step does not result in a warning: it results in the pet being refused entry or held in extended quarantine at the owner's cost. Start planning well in advance."

  sections:
    - heading: "Why Mauritius is so strict and what to prepare for"
      body: |
        Mauritius is a rabies-free island nation in the Indian Ocean. The government's position is that maintaining this status protects both the local animal population and the tourism industry. The import rules are designed to ensure that only animals with verified, documented immunity to rabies, from a country with its own strong disease controls, can enter.

        The requirements are not bureaucratic hurdles: each one exists for a specific biosecurity reason. The titre test confirms the pet has responded immunologically to the rabies vaccine. The 90-day wait after the blood draw gives time to verify the result and manage any animal that has not mounted an adequate immune response. The neutering requirement prevents unsupervised breeding. The 30-day quarantine on arrival provides a final safety window.

        Understanding this framing helps owners approach the process correctly. This is not a route where cutting corners is possible or where an agent can find workarounds. Start preparation at least 6 months before your planned travel date, and ideally 8 months to allow for any unforeseen delays at the laboratory or permit stage.

    - heading: "The titre test and the 90-day rule"
      body: |
        The FAVN titre test is a blood test that measures your pet's antibody response to rabies vaccination. The blood sample must be drawn at least 31 days after the most recent rabies vaccination, and the sample must be sent to an APHA-approved laboratory for testing.

        A result of 0.5 IU/ml or above is required. If the result is below this threshold, the pet cannot travel to Mauritius in the planned window. The vaccination course must be repeated and the process restarted.

        Once a successful titre test result is obtained, a minimum of 90 days must pass from the date the blood was drawn before the pet can enter Mauritius. This waiting period is mandatory and cannot be shortened, even if the titre test result was obtained quickly.

        Count the days carefully: blood draw date + 31 days minimum (for the draw to be valid after vaccination) + 90 days wait = at least 121 days from drawing the blood to the earliest possible travel date. Add the vaccination lead time before the blood draw and you are looking at 5-6 months from the first vaccination appointment.

faqs:
  - question: "Why does Mauritius require a titre test when the UK is rabies-free?"
    answer: "Mauritius applies the titre test as a condition of entry regardless of the origin country's rabies status. The test confirms your individual pet has actually responded to the vaccine and has protective antibody levels. Country-level rabies-free status is not sufficient for Mauritius; individual pet immunity confirmation is required."
  - question: "My pet is not neutered. Can I still move them to Mauritius?"
    answer: "No. Neutering is a non-negotiable requirement for entry into Mauritius. Intact dogs and cats are refused entry. Your pet must be neutered and fully recovered from the procedure before travel. Neutering should be one of your first steps in the preparation process."
  - question: "Can I fly with my pet to Mauritius in the cabin?"
    answer: "No. Mauritius strictly prohibits pets from travelling as accompanied baggage or in the cabin. Cargo transport only. Air Mauritius, British Airways, and Emirates all offer cargo services to Mauritius."

    answer: "Quarantine costs are set by the Mauritian government quarantine facility and are paid by the owner. Contact the Division of Livestock and Veterinary Services (petimport@govmu.org) for current pricing before committing to the move."
