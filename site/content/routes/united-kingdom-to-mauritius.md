  sections:
    - heading: "The quarantine requirement: what it means in practice"
      body: |
        Mauritius is an island nation that has historically maintained strict biosecurity to protect its animal population from rabies and other diseases. The result is a mandatory quarantine for all incoming dogs and cats, with no exceptions for origin country.

        For UK pets, the quarantine period at the government facility (the Animal Welfare and Conservation Division manages this) is typically around one month. During this time, your pet is held at the facility, which provides basic care. You may be able to visit on scheduled days, but your pet is not released to you until the quarantine period is complete.

        The quarantine must be reserved and paid for before your pet travels. You cannot arrive without a confirmed booking. Contact the Mauritius National Parks and Conservation Service (NPAS) well in advance, ideally three to six months before travel, to understand the current process, costs, and booking procedure.

    - heading: "Direct flights: Heathrow to Mauritius"
      body: |
        The UK to Mauritius flight situation is more straightforward than the documentation process. Air Mauritius operates direct from London Heathrow to Sir Seewoosagur Ramgoolam International (MRU) and accepts pets as cargo. British Airways operates the same route and also accepts pets via BA World Cargo. Both are direct, which is preferable for a long-haul journey (approximately 12 hours from London).

        Emirates via Dubai is an alternative if direct flights are fully booked, though the hub transit extends the journey. The direct Heathrow options are better for your pet's welfare given the quarantine that follows on arrival.
