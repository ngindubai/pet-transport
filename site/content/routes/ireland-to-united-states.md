  overview: |
    Ireland is one of the simplest origins for moving a pet to the USA. Ireland is classified as low-risk for dog rabies by the CDC, which means the extended requirements that apply to high-risk countries (serology test, CDC-registered facility entry) do not apply. The process for a dog is: current microchip and rabies vaccination, CDC Dog Import Form completed before travel, and a DAFM-endorsed health certificate from an Irish Official Veterinarian.

    Aer Lingus Cargo operates direct transatlantic services from Dublin to several US cities, making this one of the more straightforward international pet moves. The DAFM (Department of Agriculture, Food and the Marine) is Ireland's competent authority for pet export health certificates.

    - heading: "CDC low-risk: what it means for Ireland pets in practice"
      body: |
        The practical difference between a low-risk and high-risk CDC origin is significant. For a dog from Ireland (low-risk), the CDC process is: fill in the CDC Dog Import Form before travel, confirm the microchip is readable and matches, confirm the rabies vaccination is current, present the health certificate at the US port of entry. No pre-booked facility, no serology test, no extended waiting period.

        For high-risk countries like India, China, Thailand, or Malaysia, the same process adds: a serology test at a CDC-approved laboratory (at least 30 days post-vaccination, at least 28 days before US entry), a pre-booked reservation at a CDC-registered animal care facility at the US entry airport, and the associated costs and logistics.

        Ireland's rabies-free status is the reason for the low-risk classification. The disease risk to the USA from an Irish-origin pet with a current rabies vaccination is considered negligible by the CDC, so the additional checks are not required.
