  h1: "Pet Transport from Australia to Kyrgyzstan"
  overview: |
    No rabies titre test. That single fact makes this route lighter on paperwork than most of the routes into Central Asia covered on this site, and it holds for Australia exactly as it does for the UK and US: Kyrgyzstan simply doesn't ask for a blood result from any country of origin, only a current vaccination record.

    What does change, travelling from Australia rather than London, is the routing. There's no way to reach Bishkek in two hops from Australia the way the UK page can via Istanbul or Almaty direct from Heathrow. This one runs three legs: a Gulf hub first, then Almaty, then the short regional flight into Manas International Airport.

    Plan for seven to eleven weeks. The tightest single constraint is Kyrgyzstan's own 5-day certificate window, which has to be timed against the final leg of a three-flight itinerary, not the first.

  sections:
    - heading: "Why there's still no titre test, verified for Australia specifically"
      body: |
        Kyrgyzstan's import rules don't ask for a rabies antibody titre test from a pet arriving from any country. That's true whether the pet is coming from a rabies-free country like Australia or from anywhere else; the rule is about a current, correctly timed vaccination, not a laboratory result. Nothing here is being carried over unchecked from the UK and US Kyrgyzstan pages already on this site: the underlying State Inspectorate rule is genuinely origin-blind, so it applies to Australia the same way.

        A rabies vaccination given between 21 days and 11 months before entry, plus a government-endorsed health certificate, covers the medical side. Microchipping isn't mandated by Kyrgyzstan either, though fitting one before vaccination is still sound practice.

    - heading: "Three legs, because nothing flies Australia to Bishkek in two"
      body: |
        From London, a pet reaches Bishkek via a single connection, Istanbul on Turkish Airlines or Almaty on Air Astana. From Australia, that shortcut doesn't exist. Emirates or Qatar Airways covers the first leg, Melbourne, Sydney, Perth, Adelaide or Brisbane to Dubai or Doha, both running daily or near-daily services. From there, Air Astana's Dubai-Almaty route, itself flown roughly 23 times a week, picks up the second leg, before the same short Almaty-Bishkek regional hop used on the UK route finishes the journey.

        That's a longer, three-carrier itinerary rather than the two-carrier connection available from Europe. It's not a reason to avoid the route, but it is a reason to book the Air Astana legs with enough buffer that a delayed Gulf departure doesn't strand the pet mid-journey.

    - heading: "The certificate window still governs the whole schedule"
      body: |
        Kyrgyzstan only accepts a health certificate issued within 5 days of travel. With three flight legs involved, "travel" here means the date the pet actually lands in Bishkek, not the date it leaves Australia. Confirm every connection first, then book the vet appointment to land inside that window, working backwards from the Manas arrival date rather than the Melbourne or Sydney departure.
