content:
  h1: "Pet Transport from Australia to Bolivia"
  overview: |
    Bolivia has no embassy, consulate or honorary consulate anywhere in Australia. The last one, an honorary post in Sydney, has closed. That single fact is the real complication on this route, not the veterinary side, which is genuinely light: no titre test, no quarantine, just a current rabies vaccination and the right permit.

    Everything else about moving a pet from Australia to Bolivia is a fairly ordinary DAFF export process paired with SENASAG's import framework. It's the certificate legalisation step, normally handled at a local Bolivian consulate as it is for pets leaving the UK or the US, that has no obvious address here. Confirming that path with SENASAG directly is worth doing before anything else on this list.

  sections:
    - heading: "A legalisation step with no local address"
      body: |
        Every Bolivia-bound route this build has covered so far runs the exported health certificate through a Bolivian consulate in the country of origin, London by appointment, Washington DC by post. Australia has neither option. There is currently no Bolivian diplomatic mission of any kind operating on Australian soil.

        That doesn't mean the requirement disappears. It means the practical answer, whether that's mailing documents to a Bolivian mission in a third country, or a different arrangement SENASAG accepts for Australian departures, needs confirming directly rather than assumed. Get this in writing early. It is the one part of this route genuinely unresolved by published guidance.

    - heading: "What DAFF actually asks of you"
      body: |
        The Australian side follows its standard export shape. Lodge a Notice of Intention to Export with your regional DAFF office at least 10 working days out, have a state-registered vet carry out the health check, and collect the signed export certificate. That certificate and the accompanying vet check both only hold for 72 hours, so this step gets scheduled around the confirmed flight, not booked loosely in advance.

    - heading: "Two legs, through Santiago"
      body: |
        Nothing flies nonstop between Australia and Bolivia. The workable path runs Sydney or Melbourne to Santiago on Qantas or LATAM, then Santiago on to Santa Cruz Viru Viru on LATAM's own network. Booking both legs on the one carrier where possible keeps the live animal transfer in Santiago under a single airline's process rather than two separate cargo agreements.
