content:
  h1: "Pet Transport from Turkey to Lithuania"
  overview: |
    Vilnius takes plenty of pets on the strength of an EU pet passport alone elsewhere in this build. A pet arriving from Turkey doesn't get that treatment. The EU's classification of Turkey as an unlisted, higher-risk country applies to this route in full, titre test and 90-day wait included.

    It's a genuinely longer process than a two-and-a-half-hour flight suggests, and worth planning for from the start of the pet's vaccination schedule rather than close to the travel date.

  sections:
    - heading: "The EU passport pattern, and why Turkey sits outside it"
      body: |
        Lithuania's other corridors in this build rely on the EU pet passport doing all the work: no titre test, minimal paperwork, near-instant entry. Turkey isn't in the EU, and the bloc's own risk classification places it outside the low-risk group that exempts EU-resident pets from the titre test. Blood for the FAVN test needs to be drawn at least 30 days after the rabies vaccination, the result has to clear 0.5 IU/ml, and a 90-day wait from that draw date has to pass before the pet can travel.

        TRGM doesn't require a separate export permit for this move, but the health certificate has to follow the EU's specific model for unlisted third countries, not Turkey's own standard format.

    - heading: "The flight itself, once the wait clears"
      body: |
        Turkish Airlines flies Istanbul to Vilnius direct, and airBaltic's routing via Riga is a workable fallback if the direct cargo allocation is full. There's no import permit requirement on Lithuania's side beyond the titre test and certificate, and no quarantine for a pet arriving with everything correctly timed.
