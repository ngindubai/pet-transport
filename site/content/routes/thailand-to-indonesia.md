  h1: "Pet Transport from Thailand to Indonesia: 2026 Guide"
  overview: "Thailand to Indonesia is more demanding than the equivalent Japan to Indonesia route because Thailand is not classified as rabies-free. BARANTAN requires a FAVN rabies titre test before travel and imposes a 30-day quarantine on arrival in Jakarta, compared to 14 days for pets from rabies-free countries. Factor at least 4 months of preparation time and a full month of quarantine in Jakarta into your planning."

  sections:
    - heading: "Why Thailand faces a different BARANTAN process than Japan"
      body: |
        Indonesia's BARANTAN (Agricultural Quarantine Agency) divides incoming pets by the rabies status of the origin country. Japan is classified as rabies-free, which means its pets face a 14-day quarantine in Jakarta. Thailand is classified as a rabies-affected country, which changes the requirements significantly.

        For pets from Thailand, BARANTAN requires a FAVN titre test (or equivalent rabies antibody test) demonstrating that the pet's vaccination has produced a sufficient immune response. The titre test result must meet BARANTAN's minimum level. Only after this is confirmed can the pet travel.

        On arrival, the quarantine period is 30 days rather than 14 days. BARANTAN vets inspect the pet daily during quarantine. If the pet remains healthy throughout, it is released with a clearance certificate at the end of the 30-day period.

    - heading: "The FAVN titre test from Thailand: practical steps"
      body: |
        The FAVN (or equivalent) titre test blood sample is drawn in Thailand by your vet and shipped to a BARANTAN-approved laboratory. Most approved laboratories are outside Thailand, so your vet needs to arrange international cold-chain shipping.

        The sequence is: microchip first, then rabies vaccination, then wait at least 30 days before the blood draw. After the draw, allow 2 to 4 weeks for the laboratory result. If the result passes the minimum level, you can proceed with the permit and travel arrangements.

        Not all vets in Bangkok have experience shipping blood samples internationally for titre tests. Ask your vet directly whether they have done this before. A pet transport specialist can connect you with a vet who has the required experience if needed.

    - heading: "Jakarta is the only entry point"
      body: |
        All international pet arrivals in Indonesia must go through Jakarta (Soekarno-Hatta International Airport, CGK). This applies regardless of your final destination in Indonesia.

        If you are moving to Bali, you complete the 30-day quarantine in Jakarta and then arrange onward domestic travel. If your destination is elsewhere in Indonesia, confirm that domestic carriers will accept your pet from Jakarta and that your destination falls within a BARANTAN-approved domestic movement zone.

        The 30-day quarantine is at the BARANTAN facility at Soekarno-Hatta Airport. Contact BARANTAN in advance about their current policy on owner visits during the quarantine period, and whether you can bring specific items such as familiar bedding.

  - question: "How does the 30-day quarantine work in Jakarta?"
    answer: "Your pet is taken to the BARANTAN facility at Soekarno-Hatta Airport on arrival. BARANTAN vets inspect the pet daily. If the pet remains healthy throughout the 30-day period, it is released with a BARANTAN clearance certificate. The quarantine cannot be shortened. Contact BARANTAN in advance about the current daily rate and visitor policy."
  - question: "How does the FAVN titre test differ for Thailand compared to Japan?"
    answer: "Japan is rabies-free, so BARANTAN does not require a titre test for Japan-origin pets. Thailand is a rabies-affected country, so BARANTAN requires the FAVN titre test to confirm immunity. The test must be done at a BARANTAN-approved laboratory and must meet the minimum antibody level. This adds several weeks to the preparation timeline."
  - question: "What if my pet's titre test result fails?"
    answer: "If the first titre test result is below the minimum level, you need to revaccinate and restart the 30-day wait before drawing blood again. A failed first test adds 2 to 3 months to the timeline. This is why starting early is important."
