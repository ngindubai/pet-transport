content:
  h1: "Pet Transport from Canada to Barbados"
  overview: |
    Barbados already treats Canada as free of leishmaniosis and screwworm. That's not a courtesy exemption dreamed up for this route; it's CFIA's own stated position on what Barbados's Competent Authority recognises, which means a Canadian-origin pet skips two tests that some other origins still have to run.

    What's left is a fairly ordinary Caribbean import: a microchip, a vaccination, an Official Export Health Certificate, and a 6-month Import Permit from Barbados's Veterinary Services. Air Canada and WestJet fly the Toronto to Bridgetown route nonstop, sharing up to seven weekly departures between them, so the logistics side rarely forces a connection.

  sections:
    - heading: "What Canada's own recognised status actually buys you"
      body: |
        A lot of pet export guidance reads the same regardless of origin: get the certificate, book the flight, hope nothing's missing. This route is a genuine exception. CFIA's own published notes for the Barbados route single out Canada's recognised freedom from leishmaniosis and screwworm, meaning neither test needs arranging before export, and Hendra, Nipah and heartworm testing don't apply either.

        That's real, useful information for anyone comparing quotes: a vet unfamiliar with this specific route might otherwise price in tests the animal simply doesn't need.

    - heading: "The permit, the notice period, and the certificate"
      body: |
        Barbados's own side of the process runs on three things: the microchip and vaccination completed in the right order, an Import Permit from Veterinary Services valid for 6 months, and an Official Export Health Certificate carried by the pet on arrival. None of it is unusually slow, but the permit and the certificate both need timing against a real flight date.

        One detail worth building into the plan early: Veterinary Services asks for at least 3 working days' notice of the pet's expected arrival time. Miss that and the office may not have a vet available to meet the flight.

    - heading: "Flying nonstop, and what isn't published"
      body: |
        Air Canada and WestJet's shared nonstop route keeps this move simpler than most long-haul options on this site. Where Barbados's own guidance goes quiet is on quarantine: nothing published describes a holding facility for a compliant pet, but nothing states outright that quarantine never applies either. Treat that as a genuine open question to close with Veterinary Services directly, not as a settled zero-quarantine guarantee.
