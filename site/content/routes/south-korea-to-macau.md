content:
  h1: "Pet Transport from South Korea to Macau"
  overview: |
    Macau eased its own quarantine rule on 12 June 2026, dropping the mandatory hold for pets that meet the pre-entry conditions. That change doesn't erase the titre test for a South Korean pet, though, and the reason isn't guesswork.

    South Korea hasn't reported a domestic animal rabies case since February 2013, a genuinely strong record. But the disease still circulates in a wildlife reservoir, raccoon dogs near the demilitarised zone, and the government keeps active surveillance running there. That's a different picture from Hong Kong, which has gone rabies-free since 1987 and gets treated accordingly on Macau's other route pages. Here, the honest reading of IAM's own rule points the other way: plan for the titre test, plus a three-month wait, even though the quarantine itself is now avoidable.

  sections:
    - heading: "Why South Korea doesn't get Hong Kong's shortcut"
      body: |
        IAM's exemption from the titre test is narrow: no rabies incidents in the country or region for the past two years. Hong Kong clears that easily, decades clear in fact, which is why this site's Hong Kong-to-Macau route page treats the titre test as likely waived.

        South Korea sits in a different spot. Its last confirmed animal case was in 2013, well over two years ago, which on paper might look close to qualifying. But that case, and others before it, trace back to a raccoon dog population near the DMZ that the government still monitors rather than declares clear. No titre-test waiver for South Korea appears in any source checked for this guide, and given the ongoing wildlife surveillance, that's not a surprising gap. Budget for the test.

    - heading: "Quarantine is gone, but the calendar isn't"
      body: |
        Since 12 June 2026, a pet that arrives with a compliant microchip, current vaccination, valid titre result and the right health certificate skips Macau's old 30-day quarantine hold entirely, provided it clears the IAM vet's inspection on arrival. That's a real improvement over the route this site documented before that date.

        What doesn't shorten is the run-up. The titre test result has to sit for more than three months from the blood draw before the pet can enter Macau, a wait measured from a different starting point than the 30-days-post-vaccination rule used for the test itself. Miscount either date and the travel window slips by weeks, not days.

    - heading: "One nonstop flight, two carriers, and a hold that isn't available"
      body: |
        Korean Air and Jeju Air both fly Incheon to Macau nonstop, and both take a small pet in the cabin. That's more choice than most Macau routes on this site offer, where Air Macau is often the only nonstop service and it carries no animals at all.

        A larger dog still hits a wall on the direct flight, though. Korean Air's A321neo, the aircraft on this route, isn't cleared for cargo-hold pets on international sectors under the airline's own rules. The workaround is the one this site documents on nearly every other Macau corridor: fly cargo to Hong Kong, most practically with Cathay Pacific Cargo, then cross by ferry or bridge to clear IAM's inspection in Macau itself.
