  h1: "Pet Transport from United Kingdom to Ivory Coast"
  overview: |
    Two Gmail addresses, not a government portal, decide how quickly a dog or cat clears Cote d'Ivoire's import permit. DSV, the Direction des Services Veterinaires, processes applications by email, and publishes no fee or turnaround time for the service.

    That manual step is the main source of delay on this route, not a blood test. Cote d'Ivoire doesn't require a rabies titre from a UK-origin pet, which puts this leg well ahead of the reverse journey into Britain on speed, provided the vaccination is dated correctly and the health certificate carries a French translation.

    Four airlines reach Abidjan from London: Royal Air Maroc and Ethiopian Airlines as genuine single-carrier one-stops, Air France with the most frequent departures via Paris, and Brussels Airlines as a once-weekly fallback.

  sections:
    - heading: "DSV's permit: two email addresses, no portal"
      body: |
        The Direction des Services Veterinaires, operating under the Ministry of Animal and Fisheries Resources (MIRAH), issues the import permit for a pet entering Cote d'Ivoire. USDA APHIS's own guidance confirms the mechanism: send the completed application form and a copy of the rabies vaccination certificate to carv.dsvci@gmail.com and dsv.sdsa2017@gmail.com.

        There's no published processing guarantee attached to those addresses. Treat this the way you'd treat any manual, unautomated government process: send the application early, keep a dated record of when it went out, and follow up directly if a couple of weeks pass without a reply.

        This is a genuinely different problem from the export side of this corridor, where DSV's own turnaround for endorsing a Cote d'Ivoire pet's outbound certificate is at least a known, named process. The import permit for a pet arriving from the UK has less of a track record to go on.

    - heading: "No titre test, but the certificate needs French"
      body: |
        Cote d'Ivoire doesn't require a rabies antibody titre test from any country of origin. A UK pet skips the blood test, the 30-day wait for the draw, and the 90-day wait for a result that a Cote d'Ivoire-origin pet faces entering the UK.

        What replaces that time cost is a language requirement: the health certificate has to be in French, or travel with a certified French translation, before Ivorian officials will accept it on arrival. This is easy to miss for an owner used to English-only paperwork on other routes, and it adds a translation step and a few days to the certificate's preparation, even though the underlying medical requirements are lighter than many other unlisted-country moves.

    - heading: "Getting out of London: four carriers, two real one-stops"
      body: |
        Royal Air Maroc's Heathrow to Casablanca to Abidjan routing and Ethiopian Airlines' Heathrow to Addis Ababa to Abidjan routing both keep the pet on a single airline's network for the whole trip, which limits the handling risk of a mid-journey transfer between carriers.

        Air France runs up to two flights a day between Paris and Abidjan, the most frequent schedule of any option here, though it needs a separate London to Paris leg first. Brussels Airlines only flies to Abidjan once a week, on Saturdays, making it a fallback rather than a first choice unless the travel date happens to align.

        Every carrier on this route restricts or excludes snub-nosed breeds from the hold, so confirm your pet's breed against each airline's policy before booking.
