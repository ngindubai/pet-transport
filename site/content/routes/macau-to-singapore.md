content:
  h1: "Pet Transport from Macau to Singapore"
  overview: |
    Hong Kong SAR appears by name on Singapore's official list of Schedule II countries and territories. Macau does not, on either of AVS's two named lists, Schedule I or Schedule II.

    That single gap decides the whole route. Where a Hong Kong-origin pet gets the moderate Schedule II pathway, a Macau-origin pet defaults to Schedule III, the bracket AVS applies to any country or region it hasn't named elsewhere: a titre test, a 90-day pre-export wait measured from the blood draw rather than from the vaccination, and a mandatory 30 days at Singapore's Animal Quarantine Centre on arrival.

  sections:
    - heading: "Named versus unnamed: why Macau lands in the strictest bracket"
      body: |
        AVS's Schedule I list is short: Australia, New Zealand, the Republic of Ireland and the United Kingdom, no quarantine and no titre test required. Schedule II runs to 34 countries and territories, Hong Kong SAR, Japan, the USA and most of the EU among them, each needing vaccination and a titre test but a shorter home or facility quarantine.

        Macau doesn't appear on either list. Neither does mainland China, for what it's worth, so this isn't a slight aimed specifically at Macau; it's simply what happens to any country or territory AVS hasn't explicitly named. The practical effect, though, is real: a Macau-origin pet gets treated more strictly than one crossing from Hong Kong, despite the two cities sitting an hour's ferry ride apart.

    - heading: "The 90-day window most routes don't use"
      body: |
        Most of the destinations this site covers ask for a titre test drawn at least 30 days after vaccination, with the waiting period running afterward. Singapore's Schedule III rule works differently: the blood sample has to be drawn at least 90 days, and no more than 12 months, before the date the pet actually leaves Macau. Miscount which date the clock starts from and the export date slips by months, not days.

        Once in Singapore, the pet is revaccinated against rabies on arrival, on top of whatever vaccination was already current in Macau. That's a genuine extra step specific to the Schedule III pathway, not a duplication error.

    - heading: "A nonstop passenger flight that can't carry a pet"
      body: |
        Air Macau operates a nonstop Macau-Singapore passenger service roughly four times a week, the only true direct link between the two cities. It doesn't accept pets in the cabin or as cargo, so it's not an option here whatever the schedule looks like.

        The practical routing is the same one used on every other Macau corridor this site documents: a road or ferry transfer to Hong Kong International Airport across the Hong Kong-Zhuhai-Macau Bridge, roughly an hour, then a direct HKG-SIN cargo sector on Cathay Pacific or Singapore Airlines Cargo, both well established on this specific route.
