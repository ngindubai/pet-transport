  sections:
    - heading: "South Korea's export process: APQA at Incheon"
      body: |
        The Animal and Plant Quarantine Agency (APQA) is South Korea's authority for animal health at borders. For export, APQA officers at Incheon International Airport inspect the pet and issue the export health certificate. The document records the microchip number, vaccination details, and the APQA officer's inspection findings.

        Book the APQA inspection in advance. The inspection is typically on the day of or day before departure, so co-ordinate this with your cargo flight booking. The APQA certificate, combined with the EU AHC, forms the document pair for Denmark entry.
    - heading: "Copenhagen Airport: practical arrival information"
      body: |
        Copenhagen Airport (CPH) handles international cargo arrivals, including live animals. The EU TRACES system is used for pre-notification of pet cargo arrivals in EU member states. Your cargo agent will submit the TRACES notification before the flight.

        On arrival, the Danish Veterinary and Food Administration (DVFA) carries out the border veterinary inspection, checking the AHC and verifying the microchip. For compliant pets from South Korea, this is a formality rather than a welfare check. No quarantine is imposed.

faqs:
  - question: "Is Denmark quarantine-free for pets from South Korea?"
    answer: "Yes. Denmark does not quarantine pets from South Korea provided the EU requirements are met: ISO microchip, valid rabies vaccination, FAVN titre test (0.5 IU/ml), completed 90-day wait from the blood draw date, and EU AHC issued within 10 days before arrival. The key step is the 90-day wait, which cannot be shortened."
  - question: "Does Denmark require a bilingual health certificate?"
    answer: "No. Denmark does not require a bilingual health certificate for pets from third countries. The EU Animal Health Certificate in English, completed by an authorised vet in South Korea, satisfies Denmark's entry documentation requirement."
  - question: "What is the difference between the APQA certificate and the EU AHC?"
    answer: "The APQA export health certificate is issued by South Korea's Animal and Plant Quarantine Agency after inspection at Incheon. It is the Korean government's document confirming export compliance. The EU Animal Health Certificate (AHC) is a standardised EU document issued by an authorised vet in Korea, confirming compliance with EU entry requirements. Both are needed; one does not replace the other."
