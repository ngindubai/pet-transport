  h1: "Pet Transport from Maldives to Singapore"
  overview: |
    A nonstop Singapore Airlines flight already links Male to Changi most days of the week, flights SQ432 and SQ438, a genuine four-and-a-half-hour hop on an A350 or 737 MAX 8. It's the shortest, most direct pairing in this entire round of new route pages. The paperwork tells a different story.

    AVS classes the Maldives the same way it classes every country not named on its Schedule I or Schedule II lists: Schedule III. A WOAH titre test, a fixed 90-day wait from the blood draw, and 30 days at Singapore's Animal Quarantine Centre on arrival, none of which the direct flight changes. And because Maldivian law bars dogs from entering the country in the first place, this route, like this site's Germany-Maldives guide, is written for a cat.

  sections:
    - heading: "The flight is the easy part"
      body: |
        Singapore Airlines operates SQ432 and SQ438 nonstop between Male's Velana International (MLE) and Changi, roughly 14 departures a week, with journey time of about 4 hours 35 minutes. That's a real advantage over the other three corridors in this batch, none of which has a single-carrier direct service. Pets still travel in the cargo hold, since Singapore Airlines doesn't carry pets in the cabin on any route, but a single flight with a single airline removes an entire layer of connection risk.

        Qatar Airways via Doha and Emirates via Dubai remain sensible fallbacks if the direct Singapore Airlines cargo hold is unavailable on your dates, both established options this site has already documented on the Maldives-Germany route.

    - heading: "Why the direct flight doesn't shorten the compliance timeline"
      body: |
        None of that changes the Schedule III process on the Singapore end. The Maldives isn't named on AVS's four-country Schedule I list or its 31-country Schedule II list, so the full WOAH titre pathway applies: blood drawn at least 28 days after vaccination, a result valid for export only between 90 days and 12 months after the draw, and 30 days at the AQC once the cat lands.

        Male has no WOAH-recognised laboratory that this site can confirm, so the blood sample leaves the islands before testing even starts, the same bottleneck this site has already flagged on the Maldives-Germany route. That single step, not the flight, is what actually sets your earliest possible travel date.
