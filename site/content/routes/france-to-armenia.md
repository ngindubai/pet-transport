  sections:
    - heading: "French export documentation: DGAL and the DDPP"
      body: |
        Exporting a pet from France to a non-EU country like Armenia requires a specific export health certificate, not the EU pet passport you might already have. The certificate is issued by a DGAL-authorised official veterinarian and must be endorsed by the DDPP (Direction Departementale de la Protection des Populations) in your department.

        The DDPP is the local branch of DGAL responsible for animal health and food safety. Each department has its own DDPP office. The endorsement process typically takes a few working days, but can vary. Contact your local DDPP at the start of your planning to understand their timeline and submission requirements.

        The health certificate must be issued within the time window specified by Armenia, typically within ten days of travel. Book your vet appointment and DDPP submission around your travel date, not the other way around. If you are working with a pet transport agent based in France, they will manage this timing for you.

    - heading: "Routing from Paris to Yerevan"
      body: |
        Paris Charles de Gaulle Airport (CDG) is a major live animal cargo hub. Air France Cargo and multiple partner airlines handle pet shipments from CDG to destinations across the world.

        For Yerevan (EVN), the most common routing is CDG-IST-EVN via Turkish Airlines. Turkish Airlines connects CDG to Istanbul with regular flights, and from IST, Turkish Airlines operates multiple daily flights to Yerevan. The total transit time is typically 8-12 hours, which is moderate for an international cargo move.

        Air France Cargo can sometimes arrange connections to EVN via hub partners, though this routing is less frequently used than the Turkish Airlines connection. If you prefer to use Air France's own network, ask their cargo team about the current CDG-to-EVN routing options. Emirates via Dubai is a third option if your travel dates align better with DXB connections.
