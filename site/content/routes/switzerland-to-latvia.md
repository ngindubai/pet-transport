  sections:
    - heading: "Swiss pet passport: what it means for Latvia"
      body: |
        Switzerland and the EU have bilateral agreements that make the Swiss pet passport equivalent to the EU pet passport for travel between Switzerland and EU member states. Latvia, as an EU member, accepts the Swiss pet passport without requiring a separate Animal Health Certificate (AHC).

        This simplified process was confirmed as current by the BLV (blv.admin.ch) following EU pet travel legislation updates in April 2026. Your Swiss vet issues and maintains the passport in the same way an EU vet maintains an EU pet passport. The microchip must pre-date the rabies vaccination for the vaccination entry to be valid.

    - heading: "Zurich to Riga: Air Baltic and other options"
      body: |
        Air Baltic, Latvia's national airline, operates between Zurich and Riga (RIX) and accepts small pets in the cabin on most routes. For owners with small dogs or cats, this is a convenient direct option. Confirm Air Baltic's current weight and carrier size limits when booking.

        For larger dogs that need cargo transport, Swiss International Air Lines and Lufthansa Group connections can arrange cargo to Riga. Some cargo routes involve a connection at Frankfurt or another Central European hub. Book cargo spaces at least four to six weeks in advance.
