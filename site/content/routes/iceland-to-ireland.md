content:
  h1: "Pet Transport from Iceland to Ireland"
  overview: |
    Seven nonstop flights a week link Keflavik to Dublin, and Icelandair operates every single one. No competing carrier flies this city pair direct, a genuine contrast with the Iceland-to-UK route, which has two.

    The regulatory side is lighter than the flight logistics might suggest. Iceland sits in Part 1 of Annex II to Regulation (EU) No 577/2013, so a pet leaving for Ireland, an EU member state, is treated as moving between EU countries rather than arriving from a third country. No titre test applies. What does apply, and catches some owners out, is the tapeworm treatment: Ireland still requires it for dogs, and Iceland is not one of the handful of countries exempt from giving it.

  sections:
    - heading: "Easy on paper, tight on the calendar"
      body: |
        A pet leaving Iceland for Ireland needs a microchip, a rabies vaccination held for 21 days if it's the first dose, and a MAST-endorsed EU-format certificate. Dogs also need the praziquantel dose, timed to the Dublin arrival window rather than the Keflavik departure. That's the entire regulatory picture.

        This is the mirror image of the direction covered elsewhere on this site, where an Irish pet flying into Iceland faces Form D1, a rabies titre test and a mandatory 14-day quarantine regardless of its documentation. Iceland guards its own border tightly. It extends none of that caution to pets leaving.

    - heading: "The right certificate, and why it's not the GB one"
      body: |
        Iceland issues a different export document depending on destination. For the UK, MAST completes a GB-specific pet health certificate. For an EU member state like Ireland, the certificate follows the standard EU non-commercial movement format instead, endorsed by MAST and valid 10 days to the border check. Ask for the certificate by destination when booking the vet appointment; the two forms aren't interchangeable.

        Appointments run through MAST's own portal, form 2.40, or by email to petimport@mast.is for owners without an Icelandic ID number. MAST operates from five offices around the country. Booking a few weeks ahead of the flight date avoids a last-minute scramble.

    - heading: "One carrier, no cabin option"
      body: |
        Icelandair flies Keflavik to Dublin nonstop about seven times a week, roughly 2 hours 35 minutes, and no other airline serves the route direct. Since 1 November 2024, Icelandair carries no pet as checked or excess baggage on any route, this one included, so every booking runs through Icelandair Cargo rather than a passenger check-in desk.

        Having a single carrier is a real difference from some of Iceland's other European routes. If cargo space is tight on the date you want, the alternative is a connection through London Heathrow, which adds a leg, a second cargo handler, and a second round of document checks, rather than a straightforward fallback.
