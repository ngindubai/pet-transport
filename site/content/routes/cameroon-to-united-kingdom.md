  h1: "Pet Transport from Cameroon to United Kingdom"
  overview: |
    Cameroon does not appear on the UK's Part 1 or Part 2 list of rabies-listed countries. That single fact drives most of what follows: a titre test, a 90-day wait, and a longer runway than routes from EU or Part 2 countries need.

    There's no direct flight either. Douala to London always means a transit, through Istanbul, Addis Ababa, Brussels or Paris, and the connection you pick affects both cost and how long your pet spends in transit. MINEPIA, Cameroon's livestock and animal industries ministry, handles the export side: permit, inspection, and the certificate endorsement that makes your paperwork valid at the UK border.

    None of this is unusual for an unlisted country. It just needs four to five months, not four to five weeks.
  sections:
    - heading: "Why the titre test isn't optional"
      body: |
        The UK groups countries into Part 1, Part 2, and unlisted. Cameroon sits in the third group. Unlisted means your pet needs a blood test proving rabies antibody levels, not just the vaccination certificate that Part 1 and Part 2 countries can rely on.

        The test itself is simple: blood drawn at least 30 days after the rabies vaccination, sent to an APHA-approved laboratory, and a result of 0.5 IU/ml or higher counts as a pass. What catches people out is the 90-day wait afterwards. It starts from the date of the successful test, not the date you get the paperwork back, and there is no way to shorten it. Plan your travel date around this wait, not the other way round.

    - heading: "MINEPIA and the Douala departure"
      body: |
        MINEPIA, the Ministry of Livestock, Fisheries and Animal Industries, is the government body that endorses your pet's export certificate. Without that endorsement, the health certificate your vet writes doesn't carry the weight it needs to at UK border control. Apply for the export authorisation at least six weeks out, and expect the possibility of a physical check on the animal before it's granted.

        Douala International Airport handles more cargo volume than Yaounde, which usually makes it the better departure point. Turkish Airlines via Istanbul and Ethiopian Airlines via Addis Ababa are the two most established connecting options for pet cargo on this corridor. Brussels Airlines and Air France offer alternatives through Europe. Whichever you choose, confirm live animal acceptance and the connection time at the transit airport before booking. A short layover sounds convenient; for a pet in a crate, a very short one can mean a rushed transfer.

    answer: "No. There's no direct passenger or cargo route between Douala or Yaounde and London. Turkish Airlines (via Istanbul), Ethiopian Airlines (via Addis Ababa), Brussels Airlines (via Brussels) and Air France (via Paris) all offer connecting routings that accept pets as cargo."
  - question: "What does MINEPIA do in the export process?"
    answer: "MINEPIA, the Ministry of Livestock, Fisheries and Animal Industries, is Cameroon's animal health authority. It endorses the export health certificate your vet completes, and issues the export permit needed to move an animal out of the country. Apply at least six weeks before travel."
