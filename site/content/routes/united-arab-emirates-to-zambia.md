content:
  h1: "Pet Transport from the United Arab Emirates to Zambia"
  overview: |
    A MOCCAE export certificate costs somewhere between AED 200 and 500 depending on the emirate and lasts just 30 days, a tight window set against Zambia's own import permit, which runs through direct correspondence with Lusaka rather than an online system and can take weeks to arrive.

    That mismatch is the whole story of this route. The UAE side moves fast once the paperwork is in motion; the Zambian side is the pacing item, and getting the order of operations right matters more here than on most routes this site covers.

  sections:
    - heading: "Start with Zambia, not the UAE certificate"
      body: |
        It's tempting to get the MOCCAE export certificate sorted first, since it's the more familiar process for a UAE-based owner. Do it in that order and the certificate risks expiring before Zambia's import permit even arrives, because the certificate only holds for 30 days from issue.

        The better sequence is to write to Zambia's Department of Veterinary Services in Lusaka first, at Mulungushi House, and treat the MOCCAE certificate as the last document requested, once a realistic travel date is in view.

    - heading: "Dubai Municipality or ADAFSA, and Zambia's own paperwork"
      body: |
        The UAE side splits by emirate. A Dubai-based pet goes through Dubai Municipality's Veterinary Services Section; an Abu Dhabi-based pet goes through ADAFSA, which runs its own separate system. Both feed into the same underlying MOCCAE framework, but timelines and exact fees differ, so confirm which applies before booking a vet appointment.

        On the Zambian side, the health certificate the UAE vet prepares has to follow the format the Zambian import permit specifies, not a generic export template. Once the pet lands at Kenneth Kaunda International, the importer is responsible for notifying the nearest Veterinary Officer for inspection: a step that's easy to miss after a long flight but genuinely required.

    - heading: "Flying Dubai direct beats routing through Abu Dhabi"
      body: |
        Emirates flies Dubai to Lusaka nonstop, about 7 hours 10 minutes on a Boeing 777, and Emirates SkyCargo carries pets as manifested cargo throughout. Etihad has no equivalent nonstop service out of Abu Dhabi; a pet departing from there needs a connection, pushing total travel time out to around 10 hours 40 minutes.

        If the departure emirate isn't fixed yet, that flight-time gap is a reasonable factor in choosing Dubai over Abu Dhabi as the starting point, on top of whichever export authority turns out to be quicker to deal with.
