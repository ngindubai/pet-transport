content:
  h1: "Pet Transport from Laos to Canada"
  overview: |
    Laos has sat on Canada's list of high-risk countries for dog rabies since well before that list's last refresh on 13 March 2025. Read only the label and it sounds like a serious complication. In practice it bans one thing: commercial dog imports, a category that has nothing to do with a family pet flying with its owner.

    What actually shapes this route is Laos's own export process. The Department of Livestock and Fisheries needs 15 days' notice before it will issue an export licence, and there's no nonstop cargo link to Canada, so a connection through Seoul Incheon or Bangkok is unavoidable. Budget 8 to 12 weeks, most of it spent on flight logistics rather than any medical waiting period, since Canada doesn't require a titre test even from a country on its own high-risk list.

  sections:
    - heading: "What CFIA's high-risk classification actually restricts"
      body: |
        Since 28 September 2022, CFIA has refused entry to commercial dogs from any country on its high-risk list for dog rabies, Laos included. Commercial in this context means dogs imported for resale, breeding, fostering, adoption, exhibition or research, not a pet accompanying the person who owns it.

        For a personal pet dog, the practical requirement barely changes from what applies on a low-risk route: a rabies vaccination certificate showing the vaccine's trade name, serial number and expiry date, plus the dog's breed, sex, colour and weight. If that certificate isn't available for some reason, a Rabies Neutralising Antibody Titre Test result of at least 0.5 IU/ml, drawn three to four weeks after vaccination, is an accepted alternative rather than a mandatory extra step.

        One thing worth watching: CFIA's own 2022 notice states it is exploring options to strengthen requirements for personal pet dogs and assistance dogs from high-risk countries specifically. Nothing has changed at the time of writing, but a rule review that's already been flagged in an official notice is worth checking again shortly before you travel, not just once at the start of planning.

    - heading: "Seoul Incheon or Bangkok: the two realistic connections"
      body: |
        Lao Airlines flies Vientiane to Seoul Incheon daily, feeding into Korean Air Cargo's service to both Toronto and Vancouver. That daily frequency on the Laos side makes it the routing with the most scheduling give, useful if your travel date shifts at short notice.

        Air Canada's alternative is a nonstop flight from Bangkok to Vancouver, three times a week, reached from Vientiane via Lao Airlines or Bangkok Airways. It's a shorter total routing in flight-hour terms, but the reduced frequency means fixing a date around Air Canada's schedule rather than the other way round. Either way, confirm current pet acceptance and any active temperature embargo before booking, since Air Canada Cargo pulls dogs and cats from affected sectors during periods of high heat.
