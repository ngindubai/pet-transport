  h1: "Pet Transport from Finland to Armenia"
  overview: |
    Finland to Armenia is a route that catches people off-guard because of how straightforward the paperwork appears at first glance and how the timing can still catch you out.

    Armenia does not require a titre test for pets from EU countries, which keeps the timeline to 3 to 5 weeks from start to travel day. The critical elements are the rabies vaccination window (no fewer than 21 days before entry, no more than 11 months), your pet being over 4 months old, and the Ruokavirasto endorsement step that the Finnish export certificate requires before it is valid for travel.

    There is no direct flight from Helsinki-Vantaa to Yerevan's Zvartnots International Airport. All routings connect via Istanbul, Frankfurt, or another hub. Plan the air logistics early, as cargo pet bookings on connecting itineraries require coordination between carriers.

  sections:
    - heading: "The Ruokavirasto endorsement: what Finnish pet owners need to know"
      body: |
        When your pet leaves Finland for a non-EU country, the health certificate is issued by a Ruokavirasto-authorised official vet and then endorsed by Ruokavirasto's regional office before it is valid for export. This is not optional and it is not instant.

        The endorsement typically takes 1 to 2 working days. Given that the certificate must be issued within 10 days of travel, you need to factor in this window carefully. The practical approach: book your vet appointment for day 1 or 2 of the 10-day window and submit for endorsement the same day, so the endorsed document is back with you by day 3 or 4 at the latest.

        Ruokavirasto was previously called Evira (Finnish Food Safety Authority) before the two agencies merged in 2019. If you find older guidance referring to Evira, the process is unchanged; only the name has changed. Current guidance at ruokavirasto.fi.

    - heading: "Routing from Helsinki to Yerevan"
      body: |
        The most direct routing for cargo from HEL to EVN is via Istanbul (IST) with Turkish Airlines. Turkish Airlines Cargo handles live animals, and IST has well-established handling facilities for animals in transit. The flight time HEL to IST is approximately 4 hours, then IST to EVN is around 2.5 hours. Total journey including transit is typically 8 to 12 hours depending on the connection.

        An alternative is routing via Frankfurt (FRA) with Lufthansa Cargo or via a Finnair-operated leg to a European hub. Frankfurt has one of the largest dedicated animal handling facilities in Europe. For owners who want the most experienced cargo team managing transit, FRA is a strong option.

        Whichever routing you choose, the cargo booking must be made for each leg separately. Your pet transport agent, if you use one, handles this coordination. If you are organising it yourself, contact each airline's cargo division directly and confirm live animal acceptance before purchasing the passenger ticket.

    - heading: "What SSFS inspects on arrival at EVN"
      body: |
        The SSFS (State Service for Food Safety) veterinary officer at Zvartnots International Airport (EVN) will inspect your pet's documentation and conduct a basic health check on arrival. The check covers: microchip scan matched against the certificate, rabies vaccination date verified against the entry date (21-day minimum, 11-month maximum), and overall condition of the animal.

        For compliant arrivals from EU countries, this process is straightforward. No quarantine is imposed. Pets are released to their owner the same day once inspection is complete.

        Keep all original documents together: the endorsed health certificate, your pet's vaccination booklet, and microchip documentation. SSFS accepts EU-format certificates from Finland. Copies are not accepted in place of originals.

faqs:
  - question: "Does my pet need a titre test to enter Armenia from Finland?"
    answer: "No. Armenia does not require a rabies antibody titre test for pets arriving from EU member states, including Finland. The requirement is a valid rabies vaccination (no fewer than 21 days before entry, no more than 11 months before entry) and an endorsed official health certificate."
  - question: "What is Ruokavirasto and why does the certificate need their endorsement?"
    answer: "Ruokavirasto is Finland's Finnish Food Authority (formerly Evira), the national authority responsible for authorising official vets and overseeing animal exports. When a pet leaves Finland for a non-EU country, the official health certificate must be endorsed by Ruokavirasto to confirm the signing vet's authorisation. Allow 1-2 working days for this endorsement and factor it into the 10-day validity window of the certificate."
  - question: "Is there a direct flight from Helsinki to Yerevan?"
    answer: "No. There is no direct passenger or cargo service between Helsinki-Vantaa (HEL) and Zvartnots International Airport (EVN) at the time of writing. The standard routing is via Istanbul (IST) with Turkish Airlines, or via Frankfurt (FRA) with Lufthansa. Both are established routes for cargo animals."
  - question: "How old does my pet need to be to enter Armenia?"
    answer: "Pets must be over 4 months old to enter Armenia. Animals younger than 4 months will not be admitted regardless of vaccination status."
  - question: "Can I bring two pets to Armenia without an import permit?"
    answer: "Yes. Armenia does not require an import permit for 1 or 2 personal companion animals. If you are travelling with 3 or more animals, contact SSFS (ssfs.am) in advance, as different rules may apply."
