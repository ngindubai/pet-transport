  sections:
    - heading: "Routing Copenhagen to Ljubljana via Vienna or Frankfurt"
      body: |
        Ljubljana Airport (LJU) handles regional traffic and has limited long-haul cargo connections. For a pet move from Copenhagen, the two main options are:

        Via Vienna (VIE): Austrian Airlines serves CPH to VIE with onward connections to Ljubljana. The VIE to LJU leg takes around 45 minutes, and Austrian Airlines Cargo has experience managing live animals through the Vienna hub. Vienna is arguably the natural European gateway to Slovenia given the proximity.

        Via Frankfurt (FRA): Lufthansa connects CPH to FRA with onward connections to Ljubljana. Frankfurt's Animal Lounge is a high-spec cargo facility for a transfer point. The FRA to LJU leg is under an hour.

        For cabin pets, either hub works with the usual carrier policies. For large dogs in cargo, the Vienna routing is often preferred because Austrian Airlines has direct connections to LJU and is familiar with Ljubljana's cargo handling.

    - heading: "UVHVVR and Slovenia's requirements at the border"
      body: |
        UVHVVR (Uprava Republike Slovenije za varno hrano, veterinarstvo in varstvo rastlin) is Slovenia's food safety and veterinary authority. For an intra-EU companion animal arrival from Denmark, UVHVVR requires the EU pet passport with a valid ISO microchip and current rabies vaccination. No import permit, no titre test, no quarantine.

        Ljubljana Airport processes intra-EU pet arrivals through standard procedures. If your pet is travelling in cargo, the UVHVVR officer at LJU may conduct a document and chip check. This is routine and quick for a compliant arrival.

        Worth planning for: Ljubljana's cargo handling is more manual than larger airports. Confirm cargo bookings directly with the carrier's cargo office and follow up closer to travel to ensure the animal is properly manifested.
