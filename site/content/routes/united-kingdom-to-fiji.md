content:
  h1: "Pet Transport from the United Kingdom to Fiji"
  overview: |
    A UK dog or cat heading to Fiji still spends a minimum of 30 days in government quarantine on arrival, whatever its paperwork looks like. That surprises a lot of owners. Britain has had no terrestrial rabies since 1922, but Fiji's own biosecurity framework treats UK-origin pets the same way it treats pets from continental Europe, not the shorter route reserved for Australia and New Zealand.

    The rest of the process runs in parallel with that fact rather than around it: a rabies titre test timed to fall 60 days to 12 months before export, a BAF import permit that takes about a week to process but has to be applied for well ahead of travel, and a flight routing that has to be pieced together because nothing flies London to Nadi direct.

  sections:
    - heading: "Why the quarantine happens even with a clean titre test"
      body: |
        Fiji sorts incoming pets into two risk tiers. Group A, mainly Australia and New Zealand, gets a 7-day stay at Koronivia. Everyone else, including pets from the UK and the rest of Europe under the framework BAF uses, falls into Group B and faces a minimum 30-day stay, with monitoring that continues for a year after release.

        This isn't about whether your paperwork is in order. It's a fixed feature of how Fiji, a nation that has kept rabies out entirely, manages the risk of any animal arriving from outside the small handful of countries it treats as equivalent to its own rabies-free status. Budget for it from the start; treating the 30 days as an optional worst case is the most common planning mistake on this route.

    - heading: "Two certificates, and only one of them is valid"
      body: |
        Here's a detail that trips owners up. When BAF issues the import permit, it comes with a sample health certificate attached, showing the format BAF would accept in general terms. That is not the document your vet should actually complete.

        For a UK-origin pet, Defra publishes a specific export health certificate, model 2919EHC, and APHA's own guidance to vets states plainly that the Defra version must be used, with the BAF model disregarded. Handing your OV the wrong template, or letting a well-meaning vet fill in BAF's sample form instead, is an easy way to lose weeks re-doing paperwork close to your travel date.

    - heading: "Getting there: no direct flight, and a permit clock to manage"
      body: |
        There's no scheduled passenger or cargo service between the UK and Fiji. The practical routing runs through Sydney or Auckland, where the animal transfers onto Fiji Airways for the final leg to Nadi, or through another Pacific gateway depending on what a specialist agent can confirm at the time.

        Two clocks run alongside this booking. The BAF import permit is valid for 6 months from issue, so applying too early risks it lapsing before travel; applying too late risks missing your flight window. And the titre test has its own fixed 60-day minimum wait from the blood draw. Line the permit application up once the titre result is back, not before, and build the flight booking around both.
