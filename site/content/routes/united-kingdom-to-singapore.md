  sections:
    - heading: "What Category A means for UK pets"
      body: |
        Singapore's Animal and Veterinary Service classifies origin countries into categories (A through E and a Prohibition list) based on disease risk. The United Kingdom is Category A, the lowest-risk tier.

        Category A means: no quarantine on arrival, no titre test required, and the standard import requirements (microchip, rabies vaccination, import permit, health certificate) are sufficient. Compare this to Singapore's Category C countries, which require a titre test and 30 days of home quarantine after arrival.

        The no-quarantine outcome is a significant practical advantage. Your pet arrives at Changi Airport, clears AVS inspection, and goes home with you the same day, provided all documentation is correct.

    - heading: "The AVS import permit process"
      body: |
        The import permit is issued by the Singapore Food Agency's Animal and Veterinary Service and must be obtained before your pet departs the UK. Permit applications are made online through the agri-food.gov.sg portal or the OASIS licensing system.

        You will need to provide your pet's microchip number, vaccination records, breed, species and sex, your Singapore address, and confirmation that your housing permits pets. Processing typically takes 2-4 weeks. During peak periods it can be longer.

        Once the permit is issued, keep it safely. The permit number must be referenced on your health certificate, and you will present the original permit at Changi Airport on arrival.

    - heading: "Breed restrictions in Singapore"
      body: |
        Singapore has an explicit list of banned and restricted breeds. The ban applies regardless of origin country or documentation. Breeds that cannot be imported include American Pit Bull Terriers, American Staffordshire Terriers, Staffordshire Bull Terriers over 40cm at the shoulder, Bull Terriers, Miniature Bull Terriers, Neapolitan Mastiffs, and a number of others listed on the AVS website.

        Additionally, in HDB (public housing) apartments, only specific breeds from the HDB approved breed list are permitted. Private housing (condominiums and landed properties) generally has fewer breed restrictions, but individual buildings and management committees may impose their own rules.

        If you have a breed that might be borderline or on any restricted list, confirm its status with AVS directly before beginning the permit process.

faqs:
  - question: "Does my cat need an import permit to enter Singapore from the UK?"
    answer: "Yes. Both dogs and cats require an AVS import permit for Singapore entry. Cats are also subject to the Category A classification (no quarantine, no titre test) and must have a microchip, current rabies vaccination, and the endorsed health certificate. The permit application process is the same for both species."
  - question: "How many pets can I bring to Singapore?"
    answer: "Singapore limits pets per household. In HDB (public housing), the limit is 1 dog (from the approved breed list only) and 1-3 cats. In private housing (condominiums, landed property), up to 3 cats are typically allowed; dog limits depend on the estate management. Import permits are issued per individual animal, so each pet needs its own permit application. Confirm your specific housing arrangement's pet policy before importing multiple animals."
  - question: "Which airlines are approved for pet imports into Singapore?"
    answer: "Singapore AVS maintains an approved airline list. Singapore Airlines is fully approved and is the most common choice for UK to Singapore pet moves. British Airways via IAG Cargo is also an approved route. Emirates (via Dubai) and Lufthansa (via Frankfurt) are used for connecting journeys. Always confirm with AVS that your specific airline and routing is on the approved list before booking. The list is updated periodically."
  - question: "How long does the direct UK to Singapore flight take for my pet in cargo?"
    answer: "Direct Singapore Airlines or British Airways flights from Heathrow to Changi take approximately 12-13 hours. The cargo hold is climate-controlled and pressurised. For dogs sensitive to long journeys, discuss preparation with your vet. Some dogs handle long-haul cargo well; others find it stressful. Your vet can advise on appropriate crate training, exercise before travel, and whether any calming support is appropriate for your specific animal."
  - question: "What happens on arrival at Changi Airport?"
    answer: "Your pet arrives at the Changi Airport cargo terminal (separate from the passenger terminal). Singapore AVS officers inspect the import permit, health certificate, and microchip. For dogs, the microchip is scanned and checked against the documentation. For Category A animals from the UK with correct documentation, the process is typically straightforward and takes 1-3 hours. You collect your pet from the cargo facility after clearance."
