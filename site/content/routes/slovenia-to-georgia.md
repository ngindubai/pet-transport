  overview: |
    Georgia does not require a titre test for EU-origin pets, and there is no quarantine for compliant arrivals at Tbilisi International Airport. Slovenia's EU membership means the export process is the standard UVHVVR health certificate route.

  sections:
    - heading: "What Georgia requires at the border"
      body: |
        Georgia's veterinary import requirements are administered by the National Food Agency (NFA), nea.gov.ge. Officers inspect documentation at Tbilisi International Airport (TBS) on arrival.

        The core requirements are a valid rabies vaccination administered at least 21 days before entry, an official health certificate issued within 10 days of travel, and an ISO-compliant microchip. Georgia does not require a titre test for EU-member-state pets. No quarantine applies for compliant arrivals. For 1-2 personal companion animals, no import permit is required.

    - heading: "UVHVVR health certificate process from Slovenia"
      body: |
        Slovenia's export health certificate for non-EU destinations is the EU-format official veterinary certificate signed by a UVHVVR-authorised official vet and endorsed by UVHVVR. Both steps must complete within 10 days of departure.

        Book the vet examination within 10 days of your travel date. Allow 1-2 working days for UVHVVR endorsement. Check uvhvvr.gov.si for authorised official vets in your area.

    - heading: "Cargo routing from Slovenia to Tbilisi"
      body: |
        There is no direct cargo service from Ljubljana (LJU) to Tbilisi (TBS). The standard routing is Austrian Airlines LJU to Vienna (VIE), then Turkish Airlines VIE to Istanbul (IST), then IST to TBS.

        Many owners originate the journey at Vienna Airport to reduce the number of cargo legs. Vienna is well-connected to Istanbul, and Turkish Airlines cargo from VIE to TBS via IST is a reliable route. Book through a cargo agent who can manage all legs, and confirm pet acceptance on each segment before committing.
