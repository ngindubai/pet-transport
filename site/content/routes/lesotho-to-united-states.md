  h1: "Pet Transport from Lesotho to the United States"
  overview: |
    Delta's Johannesburg to Atlanta service is one of the longest scheduled nonstop passenger routes anywhere, at roughly 17 hours. It also happens to land at Atlanta, one of only a handful of US airports with a CDC-registered animal care facility on site. For a country with as few flight options as Lesotho, that alignment matters.

    Every pet leaving Lesotho starts the same way: Airlink from Moshoeshoe I in Maseru to O.R. Tambo in Johannesburg, the only scheduled international route out of the country. From there, Delta's nonstop to Atlanta avoids adding a second US domestic connection, which is a real advantage given the CDC's rule that high-risk-country dogs without a titre cannot connect onward before their facility processing is done.

    As with the UK route, species matters more than most owners expect. A dog from Lesotho faces the CDC's full high-risk protocol. A cat faces almost no federal barrier at all.
  sections:
    - heading: "Why the Delta nonstop into Atlanta is the natural fit"
      body: |
        The CDC's high-risk rule for dogs without a titre is strict: the animal must arrive at the exact US airport hosting its reserved CDC-registered animal care facility, with no domestic leg beforehand. Only seven US airports currently have such a facility: JFK, LAX, MIA, SFO, ATL, IAD, and PHL.

        Delta's Johannesburg-Atlanta route flies directly into one of them. Combined with Lesotho's Airlink-only connection to Johannesburg, this gives a two-leg routing (Maseru-Johannesburg-Atlanta) rather than the three-leg routing a less fortunate airport pairing might require. It is still not a simple journey, but it avoids compounding Lesotho's own limited connectivity with an extra domestic US sector.

        Source: CDC list of registered animal care facilities, and Delta's published Johannesburg-Atlanta route data.
    - heading: "Cats: a fraction of the paperwork"
      body: |
        None of the CDC's high-risk machinery, the titre test, the facility reservation, the government-endorsed vaccination form, applies to cats. APHIS has no federal health requirements for pet cats entering the United States from any country, Lesotho included.

        A cat from Lesotho still needs to clear the Airlink-to-Johannesburg leg and the Department of Livestock Services export paperwork on the Lesotho side, and still needs a health certificate for airline acceptance. But once the flight lands in the US, there is no CDC facility requirement, no port restriction, and no quarantine to plan around.
