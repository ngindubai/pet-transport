  overview: "Lithuania and Italy are both EU member states, so this move operates under the standard EU pet travel framework. The requirements are simple: microchip, current rabies vaccination, and an EU pet passport or AHC. No quarantine, no titre test, no import permit. The practical challenge is carrier selection. Ryanair and Wizz Air have the cheapest Vilnius-to-Italy routes but neither accepts pets. LOT Polish Airlines via Warsaw is the most convenient full-service option for this corridor.   sections:
    - heading: "Finding a carrier that takes pets from Vilnius to Italy"
      body: |
        Vilnius Airport (VNO) has grown considerably and now handles a reasonable number of routes to Italian cities. Most of them run on budget carriers. Ryanair covers destinations including Rome Fiumicino, Milan Bergamo, and others. Wizz Air also has Italy coverage. Neither airline accepts pets.

        For a pet owner, the route comes down to three realistic options: LOT Polish Airlines via Warsaw, airBaltic via Riga, or Lufthansa via Frankfurt. LOT is often the simplest starting point because the VNO to Warsaw Chopin (WAW) leg is frequent and well-served, and from Warsaw there are direct connections to Rome (FCO) and Milan (MXP). LOT accepts small pets in the cabin on EU flights.

        airBaltic from Vilnius connects to its Riga hub (RIX), from which you can pick up connections to Italian airports. This routing adds a leg but can work well, particularly for small pets in-cabin.

        For a large dog that must travel as cargo, the Lufthansa routing via Frankfurt is the strongest option. Frankfurt's Animal Lounge is well-equipped for intermediate live animal care, and the FRA to FCO or MXP segments are reliably served.

    - heading: "Italy's domestic dog registration after arrival"
      body: |
        Arriving in Italy with a valid EU passport or AHC completes the import side of the move. But if you are relocating to Italy permanently, there is a further step: registering your dog in the Anagrafe Canina, Italy's national dog register.

        The Anagrafe Canina is managed through local Aziende Sanitarie Locali (ASL), the public health authority offices. Registration uses the microchip number already recorded in your Lithuanian EU passport and links it to your Italian address. The registration should be done within 30 days of establishing Italian residency.

        Your Italian vet can walk you through the process at your first local appointment. Cats are not subject to national registration in Italy, though some regions have their own voluntary registration schemes. Dogs that are not registered can face fines under Italian regional animal welfare legislation.

faqs:
  - question: "Can I fly my cat from Vilnius to Rome with LOT Polish Airlines?"
    answer: "Yes. LOT accepts cats in the cabin on its EU routes, provided the combined weight of the cat and its carrier does not exceed 8 kg and the carrier fits under the seat in front of you. Book the cabin pet space when you buy your ticket; LOT limits the number of pets per flight, and cabin pet bookings can fill up, particularly in summer. The cabin pet fee applies per flight segment."
  - question: "My dog weighs 30 kg. Can it travel from Vilnius to Milan as cargo?"
    answer: "Yes. For a dog of that size, cargo is the standard option. The most reliable routing from Vilnius is via Frankfurt with Lufthansa Cargo, which handles the VNO-FRA and FRA-MXP segments and provides intermediate care at the Frankfurt Animal Lounge. You will need to provide exact crate dimensions, the dog's weight in crate, and confirm live animal acceptance on both legs before booking. Allow extra time for cargo documentation compared to cabin pet travel."
  - question: "Does my Lithuanian EU pet passport need to be translated for Italian authorities or vets?"
    answer: "No. The EU pet passport is issued in a standardised multilingual format. Lithuanian vets enter vaccination and microchip information using the same field structure used across all EU member states. Italian vets and ASL offices accept the passport directly without translation. If your vaccination history is only in Lithuanian clinical records and not entered into the passport, ask your VMVT-authorised vet to update the passport before you travel."
