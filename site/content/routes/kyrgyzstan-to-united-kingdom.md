  overview: |
    Kyrgyzstan does not appear on the UK's Part 1 or Part 2 rabies-listed country lists. That single fact drives everything else about this route: a full titre test, a fixed 3-month wait, and no shortcut around either.

    There is no direct flight between Bishkek and London. Two hub options exist. Turkish Airlines connects through Istanbul and is the route most agents use by default. Air Astana runs a short domestic-style hop to Almaty and then flies its own nonstop Airbus A321LR service to London Heathrow, a genuine alternative worth pricing against the Istanbul routing.

    Plan for roughly five to six months from the first vet visit to boarding. The State Inspectorate for Veterinary and Phytosanitary Safety, based in Bishkek, is the government body that endorses the export paperwork. Owners outside the capital should factor in travel time to reach an accredited vet and the Inspectorate's office.
  sections:
    - heading: "Why Kyrgyzstan triggers the UK's full unlisted-country protocol"
      body: |
        The UK's rabies rules split the world into listed and unlisted countries. Kyrgyzstan sits firmly in the unlisted, high-risk category, alongside most of Central Asia. That means the shortened pathway available to pets from listed countries (EU states, the US, Canada, Australia) does not apply here.

        Instead, after the rabies vaccination and a 30-day wait, blood is drawn and sent to an APHA-approved FAVN or ELISA laboratory. A result of at least 0.5 IU/ml starts a fixed 3-month clock. Only once that period has passed can the pet legally enter Great Britain. Miscalculate the blood draw date by even a few days and the whole schedule slips by weeks, not days.

        Source: UK Animal and Plant Health Agency, gov.uk/bring-pet-to-great-britain, and the government's published rabies risk classification by country.
    - heading: "Choosing between Istanbul and Almaty for the connecting leg"
      body: |
        Turkish Airlines via Istanbul is the workhorse option. Its AVIH cargo programme is well established for Central Asian routes, and IST has deep experience handling transit pets bound for Europe. The tradeoff is a longer overall journey with two transfers.

        Air Astana's nonstop Almaty to London Heathrow service, flown on the long-range A321LR, cuts the number of transfers to effectively one meaningful connection once the Bishkek-Almaty hop is done. It only operates 3 to 4 times a week, so the schedule needs checking against your titre-test clearance date well ahead of booking.

        Aeroflot still appears in some older guides as a Bishkek option because it historically served the route, but it cannot be used here. Sanctions imposed on Russian carriers since Russia's invasion of Ukraine block Aeroflot from EU and UK airspace and airports, and that restriction remains in force.
