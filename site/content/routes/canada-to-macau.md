content:
  h1: "Pet Transport from Canada to Macau: 2026 Guide"
  overview: |
    Macau runs its own quarantine and import system, separate from mainland China's GACC and Hong Kong's AFCD. The Instituto para os Assuntos Municipais, IAM, is the Municipal Affairs Bureau that decides whether your dog or cat needs to sit in quarantine, and since a policy change that took effect around June 2026, most compliant pets no longer do.

    That change matters, but it doesn't remove the paperwork. A rabies antibody titre test is still required because Canada carries wildlife rabies and isn't on Macau's short exemption list, and there's no direct flight, so every pet on this route makes a stop in Hong Kong or on the Chinese mainland before crossing into Macau by road or ferry.

  sections:
    - heading: "Why Macau is not the same as mainland China or Hong Kong"
      body: |
        Three separate governments, three separate rulebooks, all packed into the Pearl River Delta. Mainland China's import system runs through GACC and still requires quarantine on arrival. Hong Kong's AFCD has its own scheme. Macau's IAM sits apart from both, and it moved first: as of around June 2026, IAM stopped requiring mandatory quarantine for dogs and cats that arrive fully compliant, a change it credits to World Organisation for Animal Health guidance.

        Compliant means four things done in order and confirmed at arrival: an ISO microchip, a current rabies vaccination, a passing rabies antibody titre test, and an official health certificate. Miss one and an IAM vet can still hold your pet at the airport, so treat "no quarantine" as conditional, not automatic.
    - heading: "The routing problem: there is no direct flight"
      body: |
        Macau International Airport doesn't carry transpacific cargo. Air Macau's network runs to regional Asian destinations, not North America. So the practical path is Toronto or Vancouver to Hong Kong with a carrier like Cathay Pacific Cargo, followed by a road transfer across the Hong Kong-Zhuhai-Macau Bridge, or a ferry, that takes about an hour once the crate clears Hong Kong customs.

        Build that transfer into your planning as its own leg, with its own handling fee and its own paperwork check. Agents who work this corridor regularly will already have a relationship with a cross-border pet handler on the Hong Kong side; ask for one by name rather than assuming your cargo agent has it covered.
    - heading: "The licence that expires: PS-1092"
      body: |
        IAM issues the Import Licence for Live Animals, PS-1092, through the Macao SAR Government Portal, usually within three working days of a complete application. It's then valid for 30 days only. Apply too far ahead of your flight and you'll be reapplying at your own cost; apply too close to it and a processing delay could bump you past your booked date. Line the application up against a confirmed flight, not a provisional one.
