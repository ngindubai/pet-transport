content:
  h1: "Pet Transport from Algeria to the United Arab Emirates"
  overview: |
    Book Emirates today and the flight works fine. Dubai to Algiers runs up to six times a week on a Boeing 777-300, and it's the obvious direct choice for this route. Look further out, though, and the picture changes: Algeria has started ending its air services agreement with the UAE, and this specific corridor is reportedly at risk of suspension by early February 2027.

    The regulatory side is more settled. Algeria doesn't sit on MOCCAE's low-risk list, so a rabies antibody titre test applies, but MOCCAE attaches no fixed wait once that result clears, unlike the three-month rule the UK enforces on unlisted-country pets.

  sections:
    - heading: "Does a titre test apply here, and how long does it add?"
      body: |
        Yes. Algeria sits outside MOCCAE's roughly 50-country low-risk list, the same list Albania is on, so a rabies antibody titre test at an accredited laboratory is required, blood drawn at least 30 days after vaccination with a result of at least 0.5 IU/ml. Once that result passes, MOCCAE sets no further mandatory waiting period, so the pet can travel as soon as the import permit is confirmed.

        That's a genuinely lighter rule than the UK's or the EU's three-month post-titre wait. Most of the real delay on this route comes from lab turnaround, not a government-imposed clock.

    - heading: "What's actually wrong with just booking Emirates?"
      body: |
        Nothing, today. The problem sits in the future rather than the present booking. Algeria has begun the process of ending its air services agreement with the United Arab Emirates, and multiple reports put the Dubai-Algiers Emirates route at risk of suspension by early February 2027 if the two governments don't reach a replacement deal. Etihad already dropped its own plan for a competing Abu Dhabi-Algiers service over similar friction.

        For a move happening soon, this changes nothing. For anyone planning further ahead, or simply wanting a fallback, Turkish Airlines' Algiers-Istanbul-Dubai routing, using its twice-daily Istanbul-Dubai service, is worth booking with in mind from the outset rather than discovering the gap later.

    - heading: "What does the whole move cost?"
      body: |
        Algerian-side costs, vet visits, the titre test and the DSV certificate, typically total EUR 430 to EUR 880. The MOCCAE import permit itself is a modest fixed online fee.

        Air cargo makes up the largest share, EUR 1,000 to EUR 2,400 depending on whether the pet flies direct on Emirates or via Istanbul on Turkish Airlines. All in, most owners land between EUR 1,700 and EUR 4,000, a route whose cost is driven far more by the crate and cargo booking than by any regulatory fee.
