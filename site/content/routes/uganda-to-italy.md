  sections:
    - heading: "What changed in April 2026, and why it matters here"
      body: |
        Since 22 April 2026, entry rules for pets from third countries run through a new set of EU instruments: Commission Delegated Regulation (EU) 2026/131, Implementing Regulation (EU) 2026/705 for the certificate format, and Implementing Regulation (EU) 2026/636 for the country list. These replace the older 576/2013 and 577/2013 framework that most pet transport guidance online still cites.

        For a Ugandan pet owner, the substance has not changed. Uganda is not on the approved list, so the microchip-vaccination-titre test-90 day sequence remains exactly as it was. What has changed is the paperwork citation your vet's certificate should reference, and it is worth confirming your MAAIF-authorised vet is using the current format rather than an outdated template.

    - heading: "No breed ban, but two airports and two vets to choose between"
      body: |
        Rome Fiumicino and Milan Malpensa both accept live animal cargo with Ministero della Salute veterinary inspection on arrival. Neither city holds a monopoly on which airline serves it best from Entebbe. Ethiopian Airlines via Addis Ababa reaches Rome most directly given Ethiopian's strong FCO service; Qatar Airways via Doha connects well into Milan. Turkish Airlines, which flies Entebbe to Istanbul non-stop, can route into either Italian city depending on the week's schedule.

        The absence of a breed ban is genuinely useful information for owners of restricted breeds elsewhere in Europe, but it does not touch airline-level brachycephalic restrictions, which apply independently of where the pet is landing.
