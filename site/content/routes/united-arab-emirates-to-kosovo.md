  h1: "Pet Transport from United Arab Emirates to Kosovo"
  overview: |
    Dubai flies direct to Sarajevo and direct to Bishkek, both covered elsewhere on this site, but not to Pristina. That single gap shapes the whole route: a pet leaving the UAE for Kosovo always changes planes at least once, whichever carrier ends up booked.

    Turkish Airlines via Istanbul is the more established choice, backed by Turkish Cargo's Pet Lounge for the connection. Emirates to Vienna, linking onward to Austrian Airlines' daily Vienna-Pristina service, is the genuine second option, though it means two separate bookings on two different airlines rather than one continuous itinerary.

    Kosovo's own entry rules stay light either way. No titre test, under current guidance, and no import permit. The one hard condition worth planning around is Echinococcus treatment, a tapeworm requirement that has to be recorded on the certificate rather than assumed as part of a routine health check.

  sections:
    - heading: "Why this is the one Balkan route without a UAE nonstop"
      body: |
        flydubai's own network reaches Sarajevo and Bishkek directly, both genuine advantages for owners on those specific routes. Pristina isn't on that list. Whatever the reason, commercial demand, aircraft range, slot availability, the practical result for an owner is the same: every UAE-to-Kosovo pet movement runs through a connecting hub.

        Turkish Airlines via Istanbul carries the most established live animal infrastructure of the realistic options, including a dedicated Pet Lounge for animals with a longer layover. Emirates to Vienna, then Austrian Airlines on to Pristina, is worth comparing on schedule and price, though it's genuinely two bookings rather than one, and confirming the live animal transfer between airlines matters more here than on a single-carrier routing.

    - heading: "Echinococcus treatment: easy to miss, hard to fix late"
      body: |
        Kosovo's published entry conditions specifically list Echinococcus, tapeworm, treatment for dogs and cats, recorded on the accompanying certificate. It's a genuinely separate condition from the general fitness-to-travel check MOCCAE's own exam covers, so it's worth raising directly with the certifying vet at the same appointment rather than assuming it's bundled in automatically.

        Because Kosovo hasn't published a formal risk-tiered country list, the wider guidance that no titre test applies rests on established practice reported by pet travel sources rather than a numbered regulation. A direct check with Kosovo's Food and Veterinary Agency closes that gap before committing to non-refundable flights.
