  h1: "Pet Transport Dominican Republic to Austria: 2026 Guide"
  overview: |
    Relocating a dog or cat from the Dominican Republic to Austria requires the full EU unlisted country process, and the timeline is longer than most pet owners expect. The Dominican Republic sits on the EU list of unlisted third countries, meaning it has no special veterinary equivalence agreement with the European Union. As a result, every dog and cat making this journey needs a FAVN or ELISA titre test at an EU-approved laboratory, followed by a mandatory 90-day wait from the date the laboratory confirms a passing result. From the moment you start preparations to the day your pet boards the plane, you are looking at a minimum of five to six months.

    Austria is a full EU member state, and its import rules follow EU Regulation 2020/692 in full. The Austrian competent authority for animal health is BMSGPK (Bundesministerium fur Soziales, Gesundheit, Pflege und Konsumentenschutz), supported by AGES at ages.at. At the border, inspectors will verify the microchip number, vaccination records, titre test result and date, and the DIGEGA-endorsed EU health certificate. The health certificate is valid for 10 days from issue, so timing the vet appointment and DIGEGA endorsement correctly is important.

    The cargo route from Santo Domingo (SDQ) to Vienna International Airport (VIE) runs via a European hub, most commonly Madrid (MAD) on Iberia, Paris (CDG) on Air France, or Amsterdam (AMS) on KLM. None of these airlines offer a direct transatlantic service to Vienna, so every shipment involves at least one connection. A professional pet transport agent is worth considering for this route, particularly to coordinate the titre test sample shipment and the DIGEGA endorsement process.

  sections:
    - heading: "Why the Dominican Republic needs a titre test for Austria entry"
      body: |
        Austria, as an EU member state, applies EU Regulation 2020/692 to all incoming pets from third countries. The regulation divides third countries into two categories: listed countries (those with standards considered equivalent to the EU, and therefore eligible for a simplified process) and unlisted countries (those not on that list, where a titre test and 90-day wait are mandatory). The Dominican Republic is an unlisted country.

        The practical consequence is straightforward, if time-consuming. Your vet in the Dominican Republic draws a blood sample at least 30 days after the rabies vaccination. That sample is shipped to an EU-approved laboratory, which processes it and issues a result. If the result is 0.5 IU/ml or above, the 90-day countdown begins from that result date. Only after those 90 days have passed is your pet legally permitted to enter Austria or any other EU member state.

        The titre test result does not expire as long as your pet stays up to date with booster vaccinations. This means that if you plan a future move to another EU country after establishing residence in Austria, the same titre result can be used again, provided the boosters are current. Keep all laboratory documentation permanently.

    - heading: "Routing and documentation from Santo Domingo"
      body: |
        Las Americas International Airport (SDQ) in Santo Domingo is the main international departure point for pet cargo out of the Dominican Republic. Punta Cana International Airport (PUJ) is an alternative, though its cargo connections to Central Europe are less frequent. For most owners relocating to Austria, SDQ is the practical starting point.

        The most straightforward routing is SDQ to Madrid (MAD) on Iberia, then MAD to Vienna (VIE) via Iberia or a partner carrier. Air France offers an alternative via Paris CDG, and KLM covers the Amsterdam (AMS) hub. On all routings, confirm live animal cargo acceptance on every segment before booking. Cargo terms can differ between the transatlantic leg and the European connection, and not all carriers accept live animals on all routes.

        The DIGEGA export health certificate must be issued by a DIGEGA-authorised veterinarian in the Dominican Republic and then endorsed by DIGEGA before departure. The endorsement process typically takes one to two working days. Since the certificate is only valid for 10 days from issue, the vet appointment needs to be planned carefully: allow time for DIGEGA endorsement while still keeping sufficient days for the actual travel window.

    - heading: "How long does it take to move a pet from the Dominican Republic to Austria?"
      body: |
        The minimum realistic timeline from first vet appointment to travel day is around 20 to 24 weeks. This breaks down as follows: microchip and vaccination in week one, a wait of at least 30 days before the blood draw, two to four weeks for the laboratory to process the titre test, then a full 90 days from the date of the passing result. That sequence alone accounts for roughly five months under ideal conditions.

        Add to that the time needed to source an EU-approved laboratory, arrange international sample shipping from the Dominican Republic, book cargo space with an airline, and organise the DIGEGA endorsement within the 10-day certificate window. Starting preparations at least six months before your planned travel date gives you a reasonable buffer for any delays.
