  h1: "Pet Transport from Slovenia to the United Kingdom"
  overview: |
    Slovenia is a UK Part 1 listed country, so this route is one of the simpler international pet transport scenarios. No titre test, no waiting period, no quarantine for correctly documented pets.

    You need: ISO microchip, current rabies vaccination, EU Animal Health Certificate from an official Slovenian vet (or a valid EU pet passport issued before January 2021), and tapeworm treatment for dogs. The AHC is valid for 10 days.

    Ljubljana Airport (LJU) does not have direct connections to the UK. The most practical routing goes via Vienna with Austrian Airlines, or via Frankfurt or Munich with Lufthansa. Both allow cabin pets on the European leg to London.
  sections:
    - heading: "Routing from Ljubljana: why Vienna is usually the best option"
      body: |
        Ljubljana International Airport (LJU) is a small airport with limited international connections. There are currently no direct scheduled flights to UK airports from Ljubljana.

        Vienna (VIE) is the most practical hub, around 6 hours by road or 45 minutes by air from Ljubljana. Austrian Airlines connects Vienna to London Heathrow multiple times daily, and the connection is seamless for pet cargo. Small pets may travel in the cabin on the Vienna-London leg.

        Alternatively, Lufthansa connects through Frankfurt (FRA) or Munich (MUC), both easily reachable from Slovenia by road (Ljubljana to Munich is about 3.5 hours). The Frankfurt-London and Munich-London services have excellent frequency and Lufthansa Cargo is reliable for larger pets.

        Some owners drive to a hub airport rather than flying domestically first, which is worth considering if your pet would find a road journey more comfortable than a connecting flight.
    - heading: "EU pet passport rules after Brexit: what Slovenian owners need to know"
      body: |
        EU pet passports issued before 1 January 2021 are valid for UK entry. Those issued on or after that date are not. This is a common source of confusion for EU owners travelling to the UK.

        If your pet's passport was issued in 2021 or later, you need an EU Animal Health Certificate from an official Slovenian vet. The AHC covers a single trip and is valid for 10 days from the examination date. It needs to be issued by an officially authorised vet, so check whether your regular vet holds this designation.

        The AHC replaces the old EU pet passport for UK travel and serves the same practical purpose, just with a shorter validity window.
