  h1: "Pet Transport from Barbados to the United States"
  overview: |
    Check the CDC's high-risk countries list for dog rabies and Barbados is not on it. That single fact strips this route down to its simplest form: a CDC Dog Import Form, a microchip, and a Barbados export certificate. No titre test, no 28-day wait, no restriction to one of the handful of US airports with a CDC-registered animal care facility.

    American Airlines' seven-times-weekly service into Miami is the obvious first choice, not least because Miami is itself one of those registered-facility cities, useful if plans change but not required here. Cats clear the paperwork side even more lightly than dogs do.

  sections:
    - heading: "Why Barbados sits in the easy category"
      body: |
        The CDC only publishes a list of high-risk countries; everywhere else, Barbados included, defaults to low-risk treatment. That means a dog can arrive at any US airport, seaport or land crossing, not just Miami, JFK, LAX, Atlanta, Philadelphia or Washington Dulles, the six cities with a CDC-registered facility that high-risk-country dogs are confined to.

        Compare that with a route like Venezuela to the US, where a titre test, a facility reservation and a fixed arrival airport are all mandatory. Barbados owners skip every one of those steps. The CDC Dog Import Form is still required, free and completed online, but it is a registration step, not a barrier.

    - heading: "The one piece of Barbados paperwork that actually takes planning"
      body: |
        Barbados's own side of the process is short: a Veterinary Services vet in St Michael examines the animal, checks vaccination history, and issues the export certificate for BDS $30.00 cash. It is timed close to departure, generally within about a week, so book it against a confirmed flight date rather than an estimate.

        There is no equivalent here to the numbered certificate 3890 that governs Barbados's UK route, and no non-stop-flight-only rule tied to a specific origin city. Any airline that will accept the crate and the paperwork can fly the route.
