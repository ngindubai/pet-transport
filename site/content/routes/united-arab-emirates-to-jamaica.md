content:
  h1: "Pet Transport United Arab Emirates to Jamaica"
  overview: |
    Start with the paperwork, not the flight. Jamaica's Veterinary Services Division requires a Preliminary Application Form before a pet owner is allowed to begin the vaccination and FAVN titre test sequence specifically for this destination, and skipping ahead risks having the whole file rejected. Add the mandatory titre test, the 8-to-12-week import permit window, and up to 14 days of quarantine at Plumb Point, and this becomes one of the longer-lead corridors in this entire build.

    The UAE side is comparatively simple: the same MOCCAE export certificate required for every departure. It's Jamaica's own process, methodical rather than difficult, that sets the real timeline.

  sections:
    - heading: "Why the Preliminary Application Form comes first"
      body: |
        VSD's process is sequential by design. The Preliminary Application Form has to be submitted and approved before an owner starts the rabies vaccination and FAVN titre test steps that count towards Jamaican entry. Vaccinating early and hoping it lines up afterwards is a real risk; VSD's guidance is explicit that tests and treatments completed before approval may not be accepted. Build in 4-5 months from the very first step to a realistic travel date.

    - heading: "The titre test and quarantine, in full"
      body: |
        A FAVN titre test, carried out 30 days after the qualifying rabies vaccination at a VSD-approved laboratory, must return a result of at least 0.5 IU/ml. For a pet over 12 months old receiving its first-ever rabies vaccination, entry doesn't qualify until three months after that dose, on top of the 30-day titre wait.

        Quarantine at the National Quarantine Facility in Plumb Point, Kingston, runs up to 14 days. VSD inspectors have discretion to release a pet earlier if it shows no signs of illness and the paperwork is complete, but that's a possibility, not something to plan your travel dates around.

    - heading: "MOCCAE's export process, unchanged for this route"
      body: |
        Leaving the UAE follows the same process regardless of destination: an online MOCCAE application, an in-person clinic exam in Dubai or Abu Dhabi, and a final airport check before the flight. The certificate lasts 30 days, so time it against your confirmed cargo booking, not the other way round.

    - heading: "Routing through London or Miami"
      body: |
        There's no direct Dubai-Kingston service. British Airways World Cargo via London Heathrow and American Airlines Cargo via Miami are the two realistic paths, both well established for Caribbean-bound live animal cargo. Miami tends to offer a shorter overall connection.
