  overview: |
    Portugal to the UK is a busy corridor for both Portuguese nationals in the UK and British expats returning home after time in the Algarve, Lisbon, or Porto. Portugal is an EU member state, so the UK's Pet Travel Scheme applies: no titre test, no quarantine, and no import permit. The only requirement specific to UK entry that adds complexity is the tapeworm treatment for dogs.

    TAP Air Portugal is the most convenient carrier for small pets (under 8 kg including carrier) because it allows them in the cabin. This means the pet travels in the main cabin with you rather than in a hold, which many owners strongly prefer. Larger pets travel in the hold or as cargo with BA.

    The tapeworm treatment must be administered by a vet in Portugal between 24 and 120 hours before the dog's scheduled arrival time in Great Britain. The timing needs to be calculated from the GB arrival time, not the departure time.

faqs:
  - question: "Does my dog need a titre test to travel from Portugal to the UK?"
    answer: "No. Portugal is an EU member state listed under the UK's Pet Travel Scheme. No titre test is required. The requirements are: ISO microchip (implanted before rabies vaccination), current rabies vaccination, tapeworm treatment (dogs, 24-120 hours before GB arrival), and an EU pet passport or AHC."
  - question: "Can I bring my cat in the cabin from Lisbon to London?"
    answer: "Yes, if your cat (plus carrier) is under 8 kg. TAP Air Portugal accepts small pets in cabin on Lisbon-London and Porto-London routes. Book the cabin pet space when booking your flight. Cats over the weight limit travel in the hold. No tapeworm treatment is required for cats."
  - question: "What is the tapeworm treatment rule for dogs entering the UK?"
    answer: "Dogs travelling to Great Britain must be treated against tapeworm by a vet in Portugal between 24 and 120 hours (5 days) before their scheduled arrival time in GB. The drug must contain praziquantel. The treatment must be recorded in the travel document with the date, time, product name, and vet signature. Treatment outside this window makes the dog non-compliant."
  - question: "Which airports does APHA inspect pets at when arriving from Portugal?"
    answer: "Approved UK entry points for flights from Portugal include Heathrow, Gatwick, Manchester, and Edinburgh. For TAP flights from Lisbon and Porto, most arrive at Heathrow. The APHA border vet checks the travel document, scans the microchip, and for dogs verifies the tapeworm treatment. Compliant pets are released on arrival day."
  - question: "Do I need a new health certificate every time I travel between Portugal and the UK?"
    answer: "If your pet has an EU pet passport issued in Portugal, you can use that document for every trip. You do not need a new AHC each time. For dogs, you do need a new tapeworm treatment entry in the passport for each UK trip, administered within the correct window. The EU pet passport itself is a standing document; only the tapeworm entry changes."
