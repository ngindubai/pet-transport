  h1: "Pet Transport from Uganda to Canada"
  overview: |
    Uganda to Canada is one of the more straightforward long-haul pet moves you can make from East Africa. Canada does not require a rabies antibody titre test for personal companion dogs, which removes the main bottleneck that stretches the Uganda-to-US route to 16-24 weeks. For Canada, a current rabies vaccination certificate (in English or French) and a properly endorsed export health certificate from MAAIF in Uganda are the core requirements.

    The Canadian Food Inspection Agency (CFIA) handles pet imports. For personal companion animals, there is no titre test, no quarantine, and no import permit required for 1-2 pets. The Ugandan side requires an export health certificate endorsed by the Ministry of Agriculture, Animal Industry and Fisheries (MAAIF), so you need to factor in time for that endorsement step.

    Allow 4-8 weeks from starting preparations to travel day. There are no mandatory waiting periods equivalent to a titre test clock, so the timeline is largely driven by MAAIF processing times and airline cargo booking windows.
  sections:
    - heading: "Uganda to Canada vs Uganda to US: the key differences"
      body: |
        For pet owners with flexibility on destination, the Uganda-to-Canada route is considerably less demanding than Uganda-to-US. The comparison below shows what changes between the two.

        **Uganda to US:** Titre test required (blood drawn at least 30 days after vaccination, CDC-recognised lab, 0.5 IU/ml minimum). CDC Dog Import Form (6-527) required online. Triple endorsement chain (US vet, USDA APHIS, MAAIF). Reservation at a CDC-registered facility required. Must land at a CDC-registered airport. Total preparation time: 16-24 weeks.

        **Uganda to Canada:** No titre test. No CDC form. No triple endorsement chain. No restricted airport list. No facility reservation. Current rabies vaccination certificate in English or French. MAAIF-endorsed export health certificate. Total preparation time: 4-8 weeks.

        Canada's CFIA position is that a valid rabies vaccination certificate is sufficient evidence of protection for personal companion dogs from Uganda. There is no additional requirement based on Uganda's rabies risk classification for personal pets, which is a significant difference from the US approach.

    - heading: "Cargo routing from Entebbe to Canadian airports"
      body: |
        All pet cargo from Uganda departs from Entebbe International Airport (EBB). There are no direct flights to Canada. All routings involve at least one hub connection.

        Ethiopian Airlines Cargo via Addis Ababa (ADD) is the most established option for East Africa to Canada. The Entebbe-Addis Ababa leg connects to Ethiopian's transatlantic service into Toronto Pearson (YYZ). Ethiopian Cargo has a well-established live animal operation on this corridor and is often the most straightforward choice.

        Emirates SkyCargo via Dubai (DXB) and Turkish Airlines Cargo via Istanbul (IST) are both viable alternatives for YYZ. Brussels Airlines via Brussels (BRU) offers good connections to multiple Canadian gateways, particularly useful if direct ADD-YYZ availability is limited.

        Each routing involves at least two flights, and live animal cargo acceptance must be confirmed with each carrier on each leg independently. Book 4-6 weeks ahead; cargo space for live animals fills quickly on popular departure dates.

    - heading: "MAAIF export requirements from Uganda"
      body: |
        Uganda's Ministry of Agriculture, Animal Industry and Fisheries (MAAIF) at maaif.go.ug is the competent authority for animal exports. Two documents are required before your pet can board at Entebbe.

        The export health certificate is prepared by a MAAIF-authorised veterinarian. It records the microchip number, vaccination history, and the animal's health status on examination. MAAIF then endorses the certificate. The certificate must be completed within 10 days of departure, which is a standard requirement for international pet health certificates. Plan the MAAIF submission so the endorsed certificate is ready well before your departure window.

        The MAAIF export permit is a separate authorisation required for the export of live animals from Uganda. Allow 2-4 weeks for MAAIF processing of both documents. Your IPATA agent or a local clearing agent in Kampala can advise on current turnaround times.

        Your documentation pack for Canada should include: the MAAIF-endorsed export health certificate, the rabies vaccination certificate (in English or French), and the microchip record. Canadian Border Services Agency (CBSA) officers at the port of entry will inspect these documents on arrival.

    answer: "No routine quarantine applies if your pet arrives with the correct documentation. Pets arriving without correct paperwork can be held or returned at the owner's expense."
