content:
  h1: "Pet Transport from the UK to Brunei: 2026 Guide"
  overview: |
    Here is the fact that changes this route entirely: the UK sits on Brunei's short list of scheduled, rabies-controlled countries, alongside Australia, Ireland, New Zealand and Singapore. A dog or cat that has lived in the UK continuously for at least six months can, in principle, skip the titre test and avoid facility quarantine altogether.

    That advantage does not remove the logistics problem. Royal Brunei Airlines has no live animal cargo product for private owners, so every UK pet still transfers through Singapore or Kuala Lumpur before completing the final leg into Bandar Seri Begawan. Breed restrictions on the Brunei side also need checking early, since a handful of breeds are banned outright.

  sections:
    - heading: "What scheduled-country status actually gets you"
      body: |
        Brunei's Department of Agriculture and Agrifood treats a small number of origin countries differently from the rest of the world. The UK is one of them. A pet with continuous UK residency of six months or more can typically avoid both the titre test and the facility quarantine that non-scheduled countries face.

        This is not automatic paperwork. DAA reviews eligibility at the point of the import permit application, checking residency evidence alongside the standard vaccination and microchip records. Owners whose pet has moved between countries recently, or who cannot show six months of continuous UK residency, should plan for the full non-scheduled process instead.

        Confirm your pet's status with DAA before assuming the shortcut applies. Getting this wrong late in the process is the single most disruptive mistake on this route.
    - heading: "The Singapore or Kuala Lumpur transfer"
      body: |
        Brunei International Airport has limited long-haul cargo capacity, and Royal Brunei Airlines does not sell pet cargo space to private owners. Every practical routing runs the pet through Singapore Changi or Kuala Lumpur International first.

        British Airways World Cargo, supported by PetAir UK on the ground, is the most commonly used option from Heathrow to Singapore. Singapore Airlines Cargo then handles the final leg, sometimes combined with a short road transfer across the border through Malaysian Sarawak territory.

        Build this transfer into your planning from day one. It adds cost and a coordination step that most UK-to-Southeast-Asia routes on this site do not require.
    - heading: "Breed rules that catch UK owners out"
      body: |
        Brunei bans a handful of breeds outright, including Pit Bull Terriers, Dogo Argentino and Japanese Tosa. These cannot be imported under any circumstances, regardless of documentation.

        A second tier of breeds, including Bull Mastiff, Doberman and German Shepherd, is permitted but with added conditions: a minimum age at import, mandatory neutering, and a requirement to muzzle the dog in public spaces once in Brunei.

        Check your dog's breed against DAA's current list before doing anything else. This is a genuinely different rule set from Malaysia or Singapore, and it is not something a UK vet will flag automatically.
