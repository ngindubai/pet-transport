  sections:
    - heading: "The SENASA permit and what it covers"
      body: |
        SENASA, Peru's national agriculture and food safety service, issues the import permit for pet animals entering the country. Apply at least 4 weeks before travel. The application requires your pet's microchip details, full vaccination history, and the intended entry date and airport. Once the permit is issued, keep it with the health certificate throughout the journey.

        The titre test question is worth checking early. SENASA classifies countries by their rabies risk status, and whether UK pets are currently required to provide a titre test can depend on that classification. Contact SENASA directly or work with a pet transport agent to confirm the current position before committing to a travel date or starting the titre test process, which takes additional weeks if required.

    - heading: "Arriving in Lima with your pet"
      body: |
        On arrival at Jorge Chavez International Airport, SENASA veterinary officers inspect all arriving animals. For pets with correct documentation and a valid import permit, this is a brief inspection rather than quarantine. Your pet will not be held for extended periods if the paperwork is in order.

        Peru does have breed restrictions in some regions, particularly around Lima. Pit Bull Terrier types are subject to restrictions, and the regulations around dangerous dog breeds vary between municipalities. If your dog is a Pit Bull type or has a close physical resemblance to one, confirm with SENASA whether import is permitted before you travel.

faqs:
  - question: "Do I need an import permit to bring my pet from the UK to Peru?"
    answer: "Yes. A SENASA import permit is required for all pet dogs and cats entering Peru. Apply to SENASA Peru at least 4 weeks before your intended travel date. The permit must be obtained before you book cargo space. Applications require your pet's microchip details, vaccination records, and travel plans. Do not travel without a confirmed permit, as your pet may be refused entry."

  - question: "Is there quarantine when my pet arrives in Peru?"
    answer: "No routine quarantine applies for pets arriving with correct documentation and a valid SENASA import permit. A SENASA veterinary inspection takes place on arrival at Jorge Chavez International Airport in Lima. The inspection is typically brief for compliant pets. Extended holds or return of the pet can occur if documentation is incomplete."

  - question: "Does my pet need a titre test to enter Peru from the UK?"
    answer: "SENASA requires titre tests for pets from some countries based on their country classification. Whether this applies to UK pets should be verified directly with SENASA Peru before your preparations begin. If a titre test is required, the blood must be drawn at least 30 days after your pet's rabies vaccination. Allow 6-8 additional weeks if a titre test is part of the process."

  - question: "Are there breed restrictions for dogs entering Peru?"
    answer: "Peru has breed restrictions in some regions, particularly around Lima, which restrict Pit Bull Terrier types and similar breeds. The regulations are enforced at the municipal level rather than a single national list. If your dog is a Pit Bull type or shares physical characteristics with restricted breeds, contact SENASA and the relevant municipality before applying for an import permit."
