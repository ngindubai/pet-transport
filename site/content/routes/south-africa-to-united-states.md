content:
  h1: "Pet Transport South Africa to USA: High-Risk Country Process"
  overview: "South Africa is classified as a high-risk country by the US CDC for dog rabies, which makes this one of the more complex routes into the USA. Dogs need either a valid rabies titre test from a CDC-approved laboratory (avoiding quarantine) or a 28-day stay at a CDC-registered facility on arrival. Combined with the DALRRD export process and indirect routing, this requires careful planning. Cats, by contrast, face no US federal requirements. Allow 10-20 weeks for a dog."

  sections:
    - heading: "The two pathways for dogs"
      body: |
        Because South Africa is a CDC high-risk country, dogs have two routes to US entry:

        **Pathway 1 (titre test):** Your dog has a valid rabies serology titre from a CDC-approved laboratory, with blood drawn at least 30 days after vaccination and at least 28 days before US entry. A valid titre removes the quarantine requirement and allows arrival at any US port.

        **Pathway 2 (CDC facility):** Without a valid titre, your dog is directed to a CDC-registered animal care facility for 28 days on arrival, at your expense. The facility may administer a US rabies vaccine and titre test during the stay, with possible early release.

        Most owners choose the titre test pathway to avoid the facility cost and the welfare impact of a 28-day stay. This requires planning the titre test well in advance, since the blood draw timing is specific and a re-test causes significant delay.

    - heading: "Cats are completely different"
      body: |
        It is worth stating clearly: the CDC high-risk rules above apply to dogs only. Cats entering the USA from South Africa face essentially no US federal requirements.

        Your cat will need DALRRD export documentation and airline paperwork for the cargo booking, but at the US border, cats are not subject to CDC inspection or quarantine. A healthy cat from South Africa arrives and is released. Some US states have their own requirements, so check your destination state, but the federal process for cats is negligible.

    - heading: "Routing and the arrival airport constraint"
      body: |
        There are limited direct South Africa to USA cargo options, so most pet journeys route via Dubai (Emirates) or London (British Airways). This adds transit time and handling, which matters for animal welfare on an already long journey.

        Importantly, if your dog does not have a valid titre test, it must arrive at a US airport that has a CDC-registered facility. You cannot fly to a small regional airport. Plan the routing so the final US arrival point has a registered facility, and confirm the facility list with the CDC before booking. A specialist agent is strongly recommended on this route to coordinate the indirect routing and the CDC requirements.

faqs:
  - question: "Why is South Africa to USA so much more complex than South Africa to UK?"
    answer: "The difference is the CDC's high-risk classification. The UK treats South Africa as an approved country (standard entry, no titre test). The US CDC classifies South Africa as high-risk for dog rabies, which triggers the titre-test-or-quarantine requirement, the Foreign Vaccination certification form, and the arrival airport constraint. The same dog faces a simple process to the UK and a complex one to the USA, purely because of how each country classifies South Africa."
  - question: "How long does the titre test pathway take?"
    answer: "Allow 10-16 weeks at minimum if your dog already has a current rabies vaccination. The titre test blood draw must be at least 30 days after vaccination and at least 28 days before US entry. The result takes 1-2 weeks to come back. Combined with the DALRRD export documentation, the CDC forms, and indirect cargo routing, a 10-20 week total timeline is realistic. Start early."
  - question: "Does my cat face the same high-risk process?"
    answer: "No. The CDC high-risk rules apply to dogs only. Cats entering the USA from South Africa face no US federal import requirements: no titre test, no quarantine, no CDC form. Your cat needs DALRRD export documentation and airline paperwork for the cargo booking, but the US border process for cats is minimal. Check your destination US state for any state-level cat rules."
  - question: "What happens during the 28-day facility quarantine if I don't do the titre test?"
    answer: "If your dog arrives without a valid titre, it is held at a CDC-registered animal care facility for 28 days at your expense (typically $100 to $400 per day). During the stay, the facility may give a US-licensed rabies vaccine and perform a titre test, with possible early release if requirements are met. The facility must be pre-booked before departure, and the dog must arrive at an airport where a registered facility is located. Most owners do the titre test in advance to avoid this."
