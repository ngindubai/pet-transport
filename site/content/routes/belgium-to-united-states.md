  sections:
    - heading: "Belgian official vets, FASFC and the health certificate"
      body: |
        The health certificate for US import from Belgium must be issued by a Belgian official vet (veterinaire officiel/officieel dierenarts) and endorsed by FASFC (the Federal Agency for the Safety of the Food Chain, known in French as AFSCA). FASFC is Belgium's competent authority for animal health documentation.

        The certificate must include your pet's microchip number, vaccination history (including a current rabies vaccination), species and breed, owner details, and fitness to travel. Confirm with USDA APHIS (usda.gov/aphis/pet-travel) whether authentication of the FASFC-endorsed certificate is required for the USA, as documentation requirements can change.

    - heading: "Direct Brussels-to-USA flights: the Belgium advantage"
      body: |
        Brussels Airport (BRU) is one of the better-connected European airports for direct transatlantic cargo services. Brussels Airlines (Lufthansa Group) flies direct from Brussels to New York JFK, Washington Dulles (IAD), and Chicago ORD. Delta operates direct BRU-to-JFK and BRU-to-ATL services. United flies BRU to Newark (EWR).

        This range of direct US options means you can generally avoid secondary EU hub connections from Belgium, keeping the journey shorter and less complicated for your pet. Confirm live animal acceptance, crate dimensions, and seasonal restrictions with the chosen carrier's cargo desk before booking.
