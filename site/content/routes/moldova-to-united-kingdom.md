  h1: "Pet Transport from Moldova to the United Kingdom"
  overview: |
    Moldova sits on the UK's unlisted country list, the same category as India and China, not the simpler Part 2 list that covers the USA or Canada. That single classification adds a rabies titre test and a fixed 3-month wait to every move from Chisinau to the UK, regardless of how healthy or well-vaccinated the pet is.

    There is a wrinkle specific to this corridor. Wizz Air runs more flights between Chisinau and London than any other carrier, but it will not carry a pet, in the cabin or the hold, except a certified assistance dog. Owners who assume their booked airline handles pets often find out too late that it does not.

    Plan around Austrian Airlines via Vienna, Turkish Airlines via Istanbul, or LOT Polish Airlines via Warsaw instead. All three carry pets on the Chisinau leg and connect through to London on a single ticket. Start the paperwork the day you decide to move: the 3-month wait alone rules out anything faster than about four months from a standing start.

  sections:
    - heading: "Why Moldova is treated as an unlisted country"
      body: |
        The UK divides the world into Part 1 (the EU and EFTA states), Part 2 (a shorter list including the USA, Canada, Australia and Japan) and unlisted, everyone else. Moldova falls into the third group. This matters practically: pets from Part 2 countries need only a valid rabies vaccination and a GB Pet Health Certificate. Pets from Moldova need that plus a titre test and a 3-month wait after a passing result.

        The classification lines up with a wider pattern. Moldova, along with Albania, Montenegro, Serbia, Ukraine and Turkey, sits on the short list of countries whose pets need a titre test to enter the EU as well, despite the country's EU accession candidacy since 2022. Candidate status has not yet changed the rabies risk assessment that drives this rule, and there's no indication it will before your move.

        Dogs face one more UK-specific step: tapeworm treatment for Echinococcus multilocularis, given by a vet 24 to 120 hours before arrival and logged in the health certificate. Miss that window and the treatment does not count, cats are exempt.

    - heading: "The ANSA export process and getting the timing right"
      body: |
        ANSA, Moldova's National Food Safety Agency, handles the government side of pet exports. There's no separate export permit to apply for beyond the standard process: a licensed vet examines the animal, completes the health certificate, and ANSA endorses it. That endorsement has to happen close to travel, the certificate is only valid for 10 days once issued.

        The sequence that actually determines your timeline is upstream of ANSA. Microchip first. Then rabies vaccination, no sooner. Then wait 30 full days before the titre test blood draw. Then wait for lab results, typically two to four weeks. Then the 3-month clock starts, counted from the draw date. Only after that clock runs out can the health certificate and tapeworm treatment happen, right before the flight. Doing any of these out of order resets the wait, not just delays it.

        Because Moldova doesn't recognise every 3-year rabies vaccine the way some countries do for re-entry (Moldova itself requires annual boosters for pets entering the country, worth knowing if this is a return trip), keep vaccination records current and don't let a booster lapse mid-process.

    - heading: "Booking the flight: avoid the Wizz Air trap"
      body: |
        Chisinau International Airport (RMO) has more low-cost capacity to Western Europe than most people expect for a country of Moldova's size, largely thanks to Wizz Air. None of that capacity carries pets. If you book a Wizz Air ticket first and then look for pet transport, you'll need to start the airline search over.

        Austrian Airlines connects through Vienna, its own hub, with a genuine cabin option under 8kg and a hold option for larger dogs (brachycephalic breeds excluded from the hold on both carriers below). Turkish Airlines routes through Istanbul with a flat USD 150 cargo rate regardless of crate weight tier, useful for larger dogs where per-kilo pricing gets expensive elsewhere. LOT Polish Airlines connects via Warsaw and requires cargo bookings to go through an approved shipping agent rather than the normal ticket booking flow, build that extra step into your planning.
