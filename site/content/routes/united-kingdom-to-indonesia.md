  h1: "Pet Transport from the UK to Indonesia: 2026 Requirements"
  overview: "Moving a pet from the UK to Indonesia involves more steps than most Southeast Asian destinations. An import permit must be obtained in advance, a titre test is required, and quarantine on arrival is mandatory. The quarantine period runs from 7 to 30 days depending on documentation quality. There are no direct cargo services from the UK to Indonesia, so the journey routes through Singapore or Dubai. Plan for three to five months from the start of the titre test process to arrival."

  sections:
    - heading: "The import permit and why it needs to come first"
      body: |
        Before any other preparation begins, the Indonesia import permit must be applied for. This comes from the Directorate General of Livestock and Animal Health Services under the Indonesian Ministry of Agriculture. The permit has a limited validity window, so timing the application to align with your planned travel date matters.

        Without the permit, your pet cannot legally enter Indonesia. Applying takes a minimum of four weeks, so if you have a firm travel date, work backwards from it and start the permit application first. A specialist agent in Indonesia or a UK-based pet transport agent familiar with Indonesian requirements can handle this on your behalf.

    - heading: "Titre test: less well-known but required"
      body: |
        Indonesia requires a FAVN titre test confirming a rabies antibody level of at least 0.5 IU/ml. The blood draw must take place at least 30 days after rabies vaccination. This is a requirement that some UK pet owners and even some vets are surprised by, as it is more commonly associated with destinations like the UK, Australia, and New Zealand.

        The titre test adds significant lead time to the process. From vaccination to blood draw (30 days), then laboratory processing (two to three weeks), you are looking at roughly six weeks minimum before you have a result. Account for this in your overall timeline.

    - heading: "Quarantine on arrival"
      body: |
        Indonesia imposes mandatory quarantine on all arriving pets. The duration is typically between 7 and 30 days. The length of the quarantine stay is influenced by how complete and correctly formatted your documentation is when you arrive. Pets with complete, correctly formatted paperwork tend to clear more quickly.

        Quarantine takes place at a government-approved facility. The cost of the quarantine stay is in addition to all other expenses and varies by facility and duration. Having an agent in Indonesia who can liaise with BARANTAN and the quarantine facility makes this stage considerably smoother.

faqs:
  - question: "How long does quarantine last in Indonesia for a pet from the UK?"
    answer: "Between 7 and 30 days. The precise duration depends on the completeness of your documentation on arrival. Pets arriving with all permits, a valid titre test, correct health certificate, and a readable microchip tend to clear in the shorter part of that range. Missing or incorrectly formatted documents extend the stay. Having an experienced agent check all paperwork before travel is worthwhile."
  - question: "Can I visit my pet during quarantine in Indonesia?"
    answer: "Rules vary by facility and location. Some government quarantine facilities in Indonesia allow supervised visits; others do not. Confirm directly with the facility before arrival. A local agent can help arrange this and provide updates on your pet's condition during the quarantine period."
  - question: "Is the titre test for Indonesia done at the same labs as the UK entry titre test?"
    answer: "The laboratories that conduct FAVN titre tests for UK entry are APHA-approved labs. Indonesia does not publish a specific approved lab list in the same way. The same FAVN result from a reputable laboratory is generally accepted. Confirm with BARANTAN or your Indonesian agent which laboratories are currently accepted before booking the test."
  - question: "Does my cat need a titre test for Indonesia?"
    answer: "The titre test requirement in Indonesia applies to both dogs and cats. Cats from the UK travelling to Indonesia should be prepared for the same titre test process. Confirm current requirements with BARANTAN or your agent close to your travel date, as Indonesia's specific requirements can be updated."
  - question: "What is BARANTAN and what role does it play?"
    answer: "BARANTAN is Indonesia's Quarantine Agency, responsible for the biosecurity inspection of all animals entering Indonesia. It oversees the import permit process, inspects documentation on arrival, and manages the quarantine period. All health certificates for pets entering Indonesia must be endorsed in a way that meets BARANTAN requirements. Your agent or the Indonesian consulate in London can confirm the current endorsed format."
