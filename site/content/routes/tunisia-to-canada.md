  h1: "Pet Transport from Tunisia to Canada"
  overview: |
    Tunisia sits on the same CFIA list of countries considered high-risk for dog rabies that triggers a mandatory 28-day quarantine track for a non-compliant dog entering the United States. Cross the border into Canada instead, and that classification barely changes the paperwork for a personal pet: a vaccination certificate or a titre test, no CFIA import permit, no quarantine.

    The classification does bite somewhere. Since 28 June 2022, CFIA bars commercial dogs, ones being sold, adopted out or rehomed, from high-risk countries entirely. A pet travelling home with its own owner isn't caught by that rule.

    Six to eight weeks is realistic, most of it the rabies vaccination timing rather than anything DGSV or CFIA-specific.

  sections:
    - heading: "Same high-risk list, a lighter outcome"
      body: |
        Tunisia, Algeria, Libya, Egypt and Morocco all sit on CFIA's high-risk-for-dog-rabies list. On the US route, that same classification forces a rabies titre test, a reservation at one of six CDC-registered facilities, and entry restricted to a handful of specific airports. Canada's framework doesn't do any of that for a personal pet.

        A current rabies vaccination certificate, in English or French, from a licensed vet, is enough on its own. CFIA offers the RNATT titre test as an alternative for a pet without a satisfactory certificate, not as a mandatory extra step layered on top of one that already exists.

    - heading: "What actually satisfies CFIA"
      body: |
        Dogs and cats three months or older need the vaccination certificate; anything younger is exempt. No import permit applies to a personal pet, that requirement, and the entry ban that goes with it, is reserved for commercial dogs from high-risk countries specifically. There's no post-import quarantine either, for a personal pet from any country.

        Where Tunisia's own rules bite harder than Canada's is the breed question, and it runs the opposite way to what some owners expect. Tunisia's ban on Pit Bull, Rottweiler, Tosa and Mastiff-type dogs governs animals entering Tunisia. It doesn't restrict a dog leaving Tunisia for Canada. Airline carriage rules, not government import rules, are what actually limits which dogs fly this route.

    - heading: "The flight: no shortcut through Europe"
      body: |
        Tunis-Carthage has no Canadian service. Tunisair's own network reaches Paris, Frankfurt, Rome and Madrid, and Air France, which lists Tunisia by name among the countries its pet delivery service covers, picks up the transatlantic leg into Montreal-Trudeau or Toronto Pearson. Lufthansa via Frankfurt is the practical alternative.

        Two carriers means two pet policies to check, and enough connection time for a live animal transfer, not just a passenger one. A Paris CDG connection that looks fine on a booking screen can still fail if the ground handler needs longer than scheduled to move the crate airside.
