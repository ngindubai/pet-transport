  h1: "Pet Transport from the Dominican Republic to Finland: 2026 Guide"
  overview: |
    Finland layers one requirement on top of the standard unlisted-country process that most EU destinations skip entirely: a tapeworm treatment, timed to a narrow window, for dogs entering the country. That sits alongside the FAVN titre test and 90-day wait that the Dominican Republic triggers on every EU-bound route, since its rabies control has not been assessed as equivalent to the EU's own standards.

    For dog owners specifically, that means one extra vet visit, scheduled to the day, in the final week before travel.

  sections:
    - heading: "The titre test and 90-day wait: the same sequence as any other unlisted origin"
      body: |
        The Dominican Republic falls into the EU's unassessed group for rabies control, so the standard sequence applies for entry into Finland: microchip before vaccination, a 30-day wait before the blood draw, a FAVN or ELISA result of at least 0.5 IU/ml, then a fixed 90-day wait from that draw date.

        As with every unlisted-origin route, the clock starts on the day the blood is drawn, regardless of how quickly the laboratory reports back.
    - heading: "Why Finland adds the tapeworm rule and how to time it"
      body: |
        Finland, together with Ireland, Malta and Norway, has kept Echinococcus multilocularis out of its dog population and protects that status with a mandatory praziquantel treatment, given 24 to 120 hours before arrival and documented by a vet.

        Because the window only opens once the 90-day titre wait has passed and closes 24 hours before landing, most owners book the final vet appointment for the days immediately preceding departure, folding the export certificate and the tapeworm treatment into the same visit.
