content:
  h1: "Pet Transport Dominican Republic to Finland: 2026 Guide"
  overview: "Dominican Republic to Finland has two layers of preparation. The first is the standard EU unlisted country process: FAVN titre test at an EU-approved laboratory (blood drawn at least 30 days after vaccination, result 0.5 IU/ml or above), followed by a mandatory 90-day wait from the blood draw date. The second applies to dogs only: an Echinococcus treatment with praziquantel must be given between 24 and 120 hours before arriving in Finland. Missing either requirement causes problems at Helsinki-Vantaa airport. Plan for 20-26 weeks of preparation from first vet appointment to travel date."

  sections:
    - heading: "Finland's import requirements for pets from the Dominican Republic"
      body: |
        Finland's Finnish Food Authority (Ruokavirasto) applies EU Commission Delegated Regulation (EU) 2026/131 for pets arriving from unlisted third countries such as the Dominican Republic. The requirements are: ISO 11784/11785 microchip implanted before the rabies vaccination; rabies vaccination with at least 21 days elapsed since the primary dose; FAVN titre test at an EU-approved laboratory, blood drawn at least 30 days after vaccination, with a result of 0.5 IU/ml or above; 90-day wait from the successful blood draw date; and a DIGEGA-endorsed EU Annex IV health certificate issued within 10 days of travel.

        Border inspection takes place at Helsinki-Vantaa Airport (HEL). Ruokavirasto inspectors check all documents on arrival. Pets must enter Finland at an approved border inspection post; HEL is the designated international airport entry point for most air shipments.

        For cats, the above is the complete process. Dogs have one further obligation.

    - heading: "Echinococcus treatment for dogs entering Finland"
      body: |
        All dogs entering Finland from countries outside Finland's list of exemptions must be treated against Echinococcus before arrival. The Dominican Republic is not on the exemption list. The treatment must be given 1-5 days (24-120 hours) before entry into Finland. The approved treatment contains praziquantel in a dose appropriate for the animal's weight.

        A veterinarian must issue a written treatment record stating the product name, the dose given, and the exact date and time of administration. This record is checked by Ruokavirasto inspectors at HEL alongside the health certificate.

        The SDQ-to-HEL transit time is typically 16-24 hours via a European hub. Work backwards from your scheduled arrival at HEL to determine whether to administer the treatment in Santo Domingo before departure or at the European hub layover. Confirm the timing calculation with your freight forwarder based on the specific itinerary.

    - heading: "Routing from Santo Domingo to Helsinki"
      body: |
        Las Americas International Airport (SDQ) in Santo Domingo does not have direct cargo connections to Helsinki-Vantaa (HEL). The typical routing is: SDQ to Madrid (MAD) via Iberia or SDQ to Paris CDG via Air France, then a final leg to HEL via Finnair or Air France.

        Finnair is the home carrier at HEL and operates cargo services from major European hubs including MAD and CDG. Iberia has the strongest coverage out of SDQ for the transatlantic segment. Book cargo space well in advance, particularly on the HEL second leg, where live animal capacity can be limited.

    - heading: "DIGEGA export documentation from the Dominican Republic"
      body: |
        The Direccion General de Ganaderia (DIGEGA), within the Ministerio de Agricultura (agricultura.gob.do), endorses companion animal export health certificates. A licensed veterinarian prepares the EU Annex IV format certificate, which must include full titre test details (blood draw date, laboratory name, result in IU/ml) and the vaccination record.

        DIGEGA endorses the certificate after review. Allow three to five business days. The certificate is issued within 10 days of travel. The Echinococcus treatment record for dogs is a separate document. Both must travel with the pet to Helsinki.
