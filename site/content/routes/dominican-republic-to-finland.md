  h1: "Pet Transport Dominican Republic to Finland: 2026 Guide"
  overview: "Moving a dog or cat from the Dominican Republic to Finland involves two regulatory constraints beyond the standard EU requirements: the FAVN titre test with its mandatory 90-day wait (the Dominican Republic is classified as unlisted under EU Regulation 2026/636), and for dogs, a tapeworm treatment 1-5 days before arrival. Helsinki-Vantaa Airport (HEL) is the only Finnish airport authorised to accept non-EU pet arrivals by air. Start preparations at least 20 weeks before your intended travel date. No quarantine applies for compliant arrivals."

  sections:
    - heading: "FAVN titre test and the 90-day wait"
      body: |
        The FAVN titre test is the defining constraint for this route. Ruokavirasto (the Finnish Food Authority) requires that pets from unlisted third countries including the Dominican Republic carry a passing FAVN result before the health certificate can be issued. The blood sample must be drawn at least 30 days after the most recent rabies vaccination and must show a neutralising antibody level of at least 0.5 IU/ml.

        The 90-day waiting period runs from the blood draw date. Ruokavirasto grants no exemptions to this wait. Once the 90 days have elapsed, the DIGEGA health certificate can be issued and is valid for 10 days from the DIGEGA endorsement date to the documentary checks at Helsinki-Vantaa. Keep the original laboratory report; it must accompany the certificate through the journey.

    - heading: "Tapeworm treatment for dogs and the HEL-only rule"
      body: |
        All dogs entering Finland must receive tapeworm treatment using praziquantel before arrival. The treatment window is 1 to 5 days before arrival at Helsinki-Vantaa. The treating vet records the product name, dose, and date in the health certificate.

        Finland's border control for non-EU pets is handled exclusively at Helsinki-Vantaa International Airport (HEL). No other Finnish airport has a veterinary Border Control Post for non-EU arrivals. There is no sea route option for non-EU pets arriving from the Caribbean. Plan your routing through a European hub that connects directly to HEL; Finnair has the most extensive HEL connections from major European airports. KLM and Iberia both offer options via Amsterdam and Madrid respectively.

        Cats are not subject to the tapeworm requirement. For dogs, coordinate the treatment timing carefully: if you administer the treatment fewer than 24 hours before arrival or more than 5 days before, the requirement is not met and Ruokavirasto can refuse entry.

faqs:
  - question: "Why is the FAVN titre test required for Finland from the Dominican Republic?"
    answer: "Finland applies EU Implementing Regulation 2026/636, which classifies third countries as listed or unlisted. The Dominican Republic is classified as unlisted, making the FAVN titre test and 90-day waiting period mandatory for all pets entering Finland from there. Listed countries such as the USA are exempt from this requirement."
  - question: "Does my dog need tapeworm treatment to enter Finland?"
    answer: "Yes. All dogs entering Finland must receive tapeworm treatment using praziquantel between 1 and 5 days before arrival. The treating vet records the product, dose, and date in the health certificate. Cats are not subject to this requirement."
  - question: "Can I bring my pet into Finland through Tampere or Turku airport?"
    answer: "No. Helsinki-Vantaa (HEL) is the only Finnish airport designated as a Border Control Post for non-EU pet arrivals by air. Routing through any other Finnish airport is not permitted for non-EU pets. Your cargo must be booked on a service that arrives at HEL."

    answer: "Allow at least 20-26 weeks. The sequence is: chip and vaccinate, wait 30 days, draw blood for FAVN, wait 90 days from the blood draw date, issue the health certificate, administer tapeworm treatment 1-5 days before arrival, and travel within 10 days of the certificate endorsement."
