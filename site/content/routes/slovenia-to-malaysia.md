  sections:
    - heading: "A small corridor, the same full pathway"
      body: |
        Owners moving from a smaller EU country sometimes assume a quieter route means simpler paperwork. It doesn't. DVS classifies Slovenia as non-scheduled, the same category as every other EU member state, so the microchip, vaccination, FAVN titre test and 90-day wait sequence applies without any reduction.

        The blood sample for the FAVN test has to reach a DVS-approved laboratory. UVHVVR, the Administration for Food Safety, Veterinary Sector and Plant Protection, can confirm which laboratories currently hold that approval for samples originating in Slovenia, since the approved list changes and is worth checking before your vet draws blood.

    - heading: "Routing through Istanbul or Vienna"
      body: |
        Turkish Airlines Cargo runs the most dependable long-haul connection out of Central Europe, with an established live animal handling process at Istanbul and steady onward capacity to Kuala Lumpur. This suits larger dogs travelling the whole way as cargo.

        Austrian Airlines via Vienna works well for a small cat or dog that can travel in the cabin on the short Ljubljana-Vienna hop before switching to cargo for the long-haul leg to Kuala Lumpur. Neither routing is reliably cheaper than the other; choose based on your pet's size and which carrier has cargo space on your actual travel dates.

    - heading: "The 14-day quarantine and what happens after landing"
      body: |
        On arrival at KLIA, a DVS officer checks the health certificate, the titre test record and both permits before your pet is transferred to a DVS-designated quarantine facility for 14 days, at your expense, under the December 2024 update. Anything out of order at this checkpoint can extend the stay.

        Book the quarantine facility ahead of arrival where DVS allows it, and confirm current visiting arrangements directly with the facility, since policy on owner visits during the 14 days can change.
