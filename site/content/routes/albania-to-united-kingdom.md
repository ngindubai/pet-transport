content:
  h1: "Pet Transport from Albania to the United Kingdom"
  overview: |
    Albania has no EU-recognised laboratory that can run the rabies antibody blood test a UK-bound pet needs, so the sample usually leaves the country before the animal does. That single fact adds real weeks to a route that already carries GB's full unlisted-country requirements: a titre test, a 3-calendar-month wait measured from the blood draw, and a Great Britain Pet Health Certificate.

    Flights compound the problem. Wizz Air runs the most nonstop Tirana-London services of any airline and carries no pets whatsoever, on any route. British Airways, via IAG Cargo, is the workable direct option; Turkish Airlines through Istanbul is a genuine backup with its own advantage, a dedicated pet lounge for connecting animals.

  sections:
    - heading: "The titre test is the whole timeline, and Albania makes it slower"
      body: |
        Once the microchip and vaccination are in the correct order, blood can be drawn for the titre test 30 days later at the earliest. That result needs to show at least 0.5 IU/ml, and Great Britain then imposes a further 3 calendar months before travel, starting on the day of the blood draw rather than the day the pass comes through.

        In most unlisted-country routes, lab turnaround adds two to three weeks on top of that 3-month wait. In Albania it can run longer still, because samples commonly need to be couriered to an approved laboratory outside the country rather than tested locally. Owners who plan around a domestic lab timeline routinely lose extra weeks they hadn't budgeted for.

        A pet that already carries a valid rabies vaccination and only needs the titre test and the wait can still move in 4 to 5 months from a standing start. One that needs the microchip and first vaccination too should plan for nearer 5 to 6.

    - heading: "AKU and the paperwork that actually moves fast"
      body: |
        Getting a pet legally out of Albania is the easy half of this route. A licensed vet examines the animal, confirms the vaccination record is current, and issues a bilingual health certificate that the Autoriteti Kombëtar i Ushqimit (AKU) then endorses. There's no separate export permit layered on top for a standard companion pet move.

        Don't mistake that speed for a green light on timing. AKU's endorsement confirms the pet is healthy and legally exportable; it says nothing about whether the titre test and 3-month wait on the GB side have cleared. Those two processes run on entirely separate clocks, and the Albanian paperwork will always be ready well before the British one is.

    - heading: "Wizz Air won't fly your pet, whatever the fare says"
      body: |
        Wizz Air dominates the Tirana-London market on frequency, with roughly 37 nonstop flights a week into Luton and Gatwick against British Airways' 13 into Heathrow. It is also the one carrier on this route that will not transport a pet under any circumstances, cabin or hold, beyond a recognised assistance dog.

        British Airways is the workable nonstop option, but bear in mind BA doesn't take pet bookings through its own passenger channel either. All cargo runs through IAG Cargo, arranged via PetAir UK on the export side. Turkish Airlines, connecting through Istanbul, is worth pricing alongside it: the airline operates a dedicated Pet Lounge at Istanbul Airport for animals with a layover of more than 75 minutes, which can smooth a connection that a direct Heathrow booking sometimes can't match on timing.

    - heading: "What the whole move tends to cost"
      body: |
        Albanian-side costs are modest. Vet visits, the vaccination, the blood draw and the AKU certificate together usually land somewhere between EUR 250 and EUR 550, though no government fee schedule is published for the export certificate itself, so this is a market range rather than a fixed tariff.

        The GB side carries most of the cost: the Pet Health Certificate, the crate, and the agent fee for arranging cargo with either BA/IAG Cargo or the Istanbul routing. Air cargo alone typically runs GBP 900 to GBP 2,500 depending on crate size, season and whether the pet flies direct or via Turkey. All-in, most owners land somewhere in the GBP 2,500 to GBP 5,500 range once every stage is added up.

faqs:
  - question: "Is Albania a listed country for pet travel to Great Britain?"
    answer: "No. Albania does not appear on either GB's Part 1 or Part 2 listed-country tables, so it is treated as unlisted. A rabies antibody titre test, a 3-calendar-month wait from the blood draw date, and a Great Britain Pet Health Certificate are all required, not just a vaccination record. Source: GOV.UK, 'Bringing your pet dog, cat or ferret to Great Britain,' 2026."
  - question: "Can I get the rabies titre test done inside Albania?"
    answer: "A vet can draw the blood in Albania, but there is no widely available EU-recognised laboratory in the country to run the test itself. Samples are usually sent abroad for analysis, which typically adds to the turnaround time compared with routes where testing happens locally."
  - question: "Does Wizz Air carry pets from Tirana to London?"
    answer: "No. Wizz Air does not accept pets in the cabin or the hold on any route it operates, only recognised assistance dogs. Despite running the most frequent Tirana-London service, it cannot be used for this move; British Airways via IAG Cargo, or a connection through Istanbul with Turkish Airlines, are the workable options."
  - question: "What does AKU do in the Albania-to-UK export process?"
    answer: "The Autoriteti Kombëtar i Ushqimit (AKU), Albania's National Food Authority, endorses the veterinary health certificate a licensed vet issues before a pet leaves the country. This confirms the animal is healthy and legally exportable, but it has no bearing on whether the pet has met the UK's separate titre test and 3-month wait requirements."
