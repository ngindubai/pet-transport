    Few pets leave Luanda for Canada in any given year, which is exactly why the paperwork here doesn't run on rails the way busier corridors do.   sections:
    - heading: "The same high-risk listing, a different consequence"
      body: |
        Angola appears on both CFIA's and the CDC's high-risk lists for dog rabies, and the two agencies respond in genuinely different ways. The CDC hands a US-bound dog a choice: a valid titre test, or a 28-day quarantine at a CDC-registered facility, entirely at the owner's cost. CFIA does neither for a personal pet. The high-risk classification's real force in Canada falls on commercial dog imports, banned outright from listed countries since 28 September 2022.

        A personal pet, travelling with its owner and not being transferred to anyone else on arrival, follows CFIA's standard process instead: current vaccination, a certificate in English or French, no blood draw, no facility booking.

    - heading: "Why age and accompaniment decide more than health does"
      body: |
        CFIA presumes a dog under 8 months old is commercial if it isn't travelling with the owner named on its papers. That presumption matters here because a dog wrongly classed as commercial from a high-risk country hits the outright ban, not a lighter personal-pet path. Confirm your pet's status directly with CFIA if age or documentation sits anywhere near that line, since the consequence of getting it wrong is real, not just extra forms.

    - heading: "Why this route needs a bigger time buffer than the flight suggests"
      body: |
        TAP Air Portugal already flies Luanda to Lisbon, then Lisbon to Toronto or Montreal nonstop, a genuinely clean single-carrier routing once the paperwork is ready. Lufthansa's Luanda-Frankfurt service is the practical second option.

        What actually stretches the timeline is Angola's side of the process. Pet exports out of Luanda are uncommon enough that ISV doesn't have a well-worn certificate template the way busier export markets do, so allow real time to get the format confirmed and matched to CFIA's requirements before the vet appointment, not after it.
