content:
  h1: "Pet Transport from France to Laos"
  overview: |
    Book a flight to Hanoi from Paris and the ticket says Air France, flight AF5096. The aircraft, the crew and the cargo handling belong to Vietnam Airlines. It's a codeshare, running most days of the week on an Airbus A350, and it's the strongest first leg into this corridor that France has.

    Laos itself is one of the lighter destinations on this site. No titre test, since France's EU rabies-free status keeps it off the Department of Livestock and Fisheries' high-risk list. What DLF does want is an import permit and a rabies vaccination timed to a specific window, no earlier than 30 days before travel, no later than 12 months. Eight to twelve weeks covers most owners.

  sections:
    - heading: "The codeshare worth knowing about before you book"
      body: |
        AF5096 shows up in booking systems as an Air France flight, Paris CDG to Hanoi, and it runs close to daily. Look closer and the operating carrier is Vietnam Airlines, flying its own Airbus A350 on the roughly 11 hour 40 minute sector. That's not a problem for a pet booking, since Vietnam Airlines already carries live animals, but it does mean the airline you'll actually be dealing with for crate specifications and cargo terms is Vietnam Airlines, not Air France.

        Lao Airlines picks up the short final leg, Hanoi to Vientiane. Thai Airways' own nonstop Paris-Bangkok service is the alternative, connecting into Lao Airlines' twice-daily Bangkok-Vientiane run, and it remains the more established routing for the wider region if the Hanoi schedule doesn't line up.

    - heading: "Why Laos asks for so little"
      body: |
        France counts as rabies-free under EU classification, and that keeps this route off the FAVN or ELISA titre test track that dominates timelines elsewhere on this site. In its place is a narrower rule: the rabies vaccination must fall inside a 30-day-to-12-month window before travel.

        A dog vaccinated fourteen months ago at a routine check-up doesn't qualify. One vaccinated ten days before a booked flight doesn't either. Work the vaccination date backward from the intended travel window, not the other way round, and the rest of this route is mostly a matter of getting the DLF import permit filed with enough lead time.
