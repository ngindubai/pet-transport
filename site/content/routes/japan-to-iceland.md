content:
  h1: "Pet Transport from Japan to Iceland"
  overview: |
    Japan's own rabies-free status earns it MAST's Category 1 classification, the same tier as Singapore, the UAE and most of the EU. That one distinction saves a genuine chunk of time. Once the titre test result clears, roughly 30 days after vaccination, the pet can travel immediately. There's no further 90-day wait, the extra step that stretches the US-to-Iceland route on this site out to five months or more.

    The mandatory 14-day quarantine at Reykjanesbae or Mosel still applies, Category 1 or not; it is Iceland's baseline rule for every incoming pet. And with no direct flight from Tokyo to Keflavik, the journey itself runs through a European hub rather than a single sector.

  sections:
    - heading: "What Category 1 status actually buys you"
      body: |
        MAST's two-tier system exists to separate genuinely rabies-free origins from countries where rabies is present but controlled. Japan sits firmly in the first group. Practically, that means the titre test, blood drawn at least 30 days after the rabies vaccination, is the last checkpoint before travel. No further wait tacked on.

        Compare that to a Category 2 country. The US, Canada and Turkey all face an additional 90-day wait from the same blood draw date, adding roughly three months to the schedule for no extra safety margin on Japan's already rabies-free record. A Japan-based owner planning this move can, in practice, expect to be flight-ready two to three months sooner than a US-based owner doing the same paperwork.
    - heading: "Fourteen days at Reykjanesbae or Mosel, whatever the category"
      body: |
        Iceland runs exactly two approved isolation stations, and every arriving pet, Category 1 or Category 2, spends a minimum of 14 days there. It is not a penalty for incomplete paperwork; it is the fixed cost of protecting Iceland's own rabies-free status, and it applies on top of, not instead of, the titre test.

        Book the slot early. Both stations take arrivals on a limited schedule, and summer bookings go first.
    - heading: "Tokyo to Keflavik: one connection, not one flight"
      body: |
        Japan Airlines and ANA both fly Tokyo to major European hubs with reliable cargo capacity, and Icelandair covers the final leg on to Keflavik. It's two bookings rather than one through fare, so allow a realistic buffer between the two legs rather than a tight same-day connection.
