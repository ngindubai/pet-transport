content:
  h1: "Pet Transport from the United States to Angola"
  overview: |
    Search the USDA APHIS website for country-specific pet travel guidance and most destinations on this site turn up a dedicated page. Angola doesn't, at least not yet. That gap doesn't make the move impossible, but it does mean leaning more heavily on your USDA-accredited vet and on direct contact with Angola's Instituto dos Serviços de Veterinária than a US owner might expect.

    Once that groundwork is done, the actual rules are lighter than the missing bulletin page might suggest. No titre test applies from any country of origin, and there's no import permit to file. The catch is timing: an unusually specific 4-to-11-month rabies vaccination window, and a health certificate valid for only 14 days before entry.

  sections:
    - heading: "Why this route starts with a phone call, not a form"
      body: |
        Most of the country pages on APHIS's own export site follow a template: vaccination rules, certificate timing, any titre requirement, all laid out for the specific destination. Angola's page currently returns nothing at that level of detail. It's one of a smaller number of destinations where the standard federal bulletin simply hasn't been published in the same way.

        That makes the first real step of this move a direct conversation, not a checklist. A USDA-accredited vet experienced with African destinations, combined with a direct enquiry to ISV in Luanda, fills the gap that a published bulletin would normally cover. Skipping this step and assuming the standard VS Form 7001 process applies unchanged is the most likely way this particular route goes wrong.

    - heading: "The vaccination window that doesn't look like anywhere else"
      body: |
        Angola's published guidance states that a primary rabies vaccination has to fall between 4 and 11 months before entry, a genuinely unusual shape compared with the simple "at least 21 or 30 days" rule most destinations apply. Vaccinate too early and the pet could be outside the window by the time it flies; vaccinate right before travel and it may not clear the 4-month floor at all.

        This detail comes from a secondary compiled source rather than a live official Angolan page, which is exactly why it belongs on the confirm-directly list from the first step. Get the timing wrong here and the fix isn't a quick top-up appointment, it's a fresh vaccination and a new waiting period.

    - heading: "No titre test, but no direct flight either"
      body: |
        Angola's own position on the rabies titre test is one of the more straightforward facts on this page: it isn't required from any country of origin, the US included. That removes an entire layer of blood draws and laboratory turnaround that complicates plenty of other unlisted-adjacent routes.

        What it doesn't remove is the routing problem. No airline flies Luanda to the US directly. TAP Air Portugal via Lisbon, Lufthansa via Frankfurt, and Ethiopian Airlines via Addis Ababa are the three genuine options, each adding a transit leg and a second carrier's rules. TAAG Angola Airlines has talked about a future Houston route, contingent on an FAA safety rating Angola doesn't yet hold, so it isn't a booking option today regardless of how the conversation develops.
