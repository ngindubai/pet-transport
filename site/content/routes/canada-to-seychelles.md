content:
  h1: "Pet Transport from Canada to Seychelles"
  overview: |
    Four weeks is the fixed minimum a Canadian-origin dog or cat spends at Seychelles' single quarantine facility on Mahe, even with a flawless paperwork trail. That's because Canada's internationally recognised rabies status is "rabies-controlled", not rabies-free, so the National Biosecurity Agency's full import pathway applies without exception.

    There's no ambiguity here of the kind that complicates a genuinely rabies-free origin on this site. Canada's classification settles the question before it's asked: titre test, a fixed three-month wait counted from the blood draw date, and quarantine on landing. Realistically, this route runs five to seven months from a standing start.

  sections:
    - heading: "Why Canada gets the full pathway, no shortcuts"
      body: |
        Seychelles' 2024 import rules split origins into two camps: countries it recognises as rabies-free, and everywhere else. Canada sits in the second group. It's a "rabies-controlled" country under the classification the NBA references, meaning rabies exists in wildlife reservoirs even though domestic dog rabies is absent, which is a meaningfully different status to a country like Australia that has never recorded the disease in any species.

        That distinction matters for planning. A dog owner moving from Sydney can at least ask the NBA whether a lighter pathway applies, given some ambiguity in Seychelles' own published documents. A dog owner moving from Toronto or Vancouver cannot. The full titre-test route, the three-month wait, and quarantine on arrival are simply what apply.

    - heading: "The Toronto-Dubai-Mahe corridor"
      body: |
        Emirates now flies Toronto to Dubai daily, a step up from the seven-weekly schedule that ran through most of 2025, and its own Dubai-Mahe sector operates several times a week alongside that. Etihad's daily A380 service into Toronto from Abu Dhabi offers a genuine second option, with pets routed through the Pet Oasis facility for the connection.

        Neither carrier flies pets in the cabin. Both move them as manifested cargo, checked in well ahead of the passenger flight and tracked separately. If your final home isn't Toronto or Vancouver, factor in an Air Canada domestic leg on either end, since brachycephalic breeds face restrictions on that connection regardless of season.
