content:
  h1: "Pet Transport from Saudi Arabia to the United States: 2026 Guide"
  overview: "Saudi Arabia is classified as a high-risk country by the US Centers for Disease Control and Prevention (CDC) for dog rabies. This changes the entry process significantly for dogs. The key question to answer first is whether your dog was vaccinated in the United States or outside it, because that determines which CDC pathway you follow. Cats, meanwhile, have no federal vaccination or quarantine requirements entering the US from any country."

  sections:
    - heading: "What the CDC high-risk classification means in practice"
      body: |
        The CDC revised its dog import rules in August 2024. Under the current framework, dogs coming from high-risk countries, which includes Saudi Arabia, follow different procedures depending on whether they were vaccinated in the US or abroad.

        If your dog was vaccinated in the US before moving to Saudi Arabia, and you have documentation proving the US vaccination, the return process is more straightforward: a current CDC Dog Import Form and evidence of the US-issued vaccination.

        If your dog was vaccinated outside the US, which is the common situation for expats who acquired their dog in Saudi Arabia, you face additional requirements. Your dog must arrive at a CDC-registered airport, and you will likely need to have reserved space at a CDC-registered animal care facility. This facility reservation must exist before your dog departs.

        Cats are entirely separate. No CDC high-risk rules apply to cats. They can enter the US from Saudi Arabia with just a basic health check. No titre test, no facility reservation, no quarantine.

    - heading: "MEWA export documentation and the dog restriction"
      body: |
        Exporting a pet from Saudi Arabia requires an MEWA export permit via the Anaam electronic platform, and an MEWA-endorsed health certificate from an authorised veterinarian. Allow at least two to three weeks for the permit process.

        There is an important restriction to flag. MEWA allows dog imports into Saudi Arabia only for working dog classifications: guard dogs, guide dogs, and hunting dogs. Companion dogs brought into Saudi Arabia by expats sometimes enter under special permit arrangements. When it comes to exporting, the documentation should reflect your dog's registration status with MEWA. If your dog was properly registered on arrival and is now leaving, the export process is usually straightforward. If the paperwork trail is incomplete, resolve it before attempting export.

    - heading: "Airlines from Saudi Arabia to the US"
      body: |
        Direct cargo options between Saudi Arabia and the US are more limited than routes to Europe. Saudia operates direct and one-stop services to major US gateways including Washington Dulles. Emirates SkyCargo routes via Dubai. United and other carriers connect via European hubs.

        Check that your chosen airline's cargo service is available year-round on the specific route you need. Summer heat embargoes at Riyadh and Jeddah reduce cargo pet acceptance between June and September. Autumn departures (October to November) or spring (February to April) typically offer more reliable cargo availability.

faqs:
  - question: "My dog was vaccinated in Saudi Arabia. Can he enter the US without quarantine?"
    answer: "A dog vaccinated outside the US entering from a CDC high-risk country like Saudi Arabia must follow the CDC's foreign-vaccination pathway. This typically requires: a microchip, a valid rabies vaccination meeting CDC standards, arrival at a CDC-approved airport, and a reservation at a CDC-registered animal care facility. The facility stay is the equivalent of the 28-day quarantine for most dogs on this pathway. The good news is this process is well-established and thousands of dogs complete it each year."
  - question: "Do I need a USDA health certificate to bring my dog from Saudi Arabia to the US?"
    answer: "The US does not require a standalone USDA health certificate for pet imports in the same way that exporting to the UK does. The CDC import process for high-risk country dogs includes its own documentation requirements. Your Saudi Arabia veterinarian will issue a health certificate that is endorsed by MEWA for export purposes. A US-side receiving vet or quarantine facility handles the arrival paperwork."
  - question: "What happens to my cat when we arrive in the US from Saudi Arabia?"
    answer: "Very little. The US has no federal vaccination or quarantine requirements for cats arriving from any country. Your cat will go through standard customs and animal inspection, but there is no CDC high-risk classification for cats, no titre test, and no facility requirement. Some airlines require a basic health certificate for the cat to travel as cargo or in-cabin, but US entry itself is straightforward."
  - question: "Can I bring more than one dog from Saudi Arabia to the US?"
    answer: "Yes, but each dog must meet CDC requirements individually. Each dog needs its own documentation, its own microchip, its own vaccination record, and its own place in the CDC pathway process. If you need a facility reservation for multiple dogs, reserve space for each animal. Airline cargo capacity limits may also apply per shipment."
  - question: "Which US airports accept pets from high-risk countries?"
    answer: "The CDC maintains a list of approved airports for dogs from high-risk countries. Major gateways including JFK, LAX, Chicago O'Hare, Miami, and Atlanta Hartsfield are on this list, but not all airports are. Confirm the specific airport is approved before booking flights, as routing a dog to an unapproved airport will result in the animal being turned back or held. Your transport agent or the CDC website will have the current approved list."
