      body: |
        Finland is one of the EU member states that requires tapeworm treatment for dogs ARRIVING in Finland from certain countries outside the group. This sometimes causes confusion for owners moving FROM Finland, who assume the same rule applies on departure.

        To be clear: the tapeworm treatment requirement is for dogs entering Finland, not leaving it. If you're moving your dog from Helsinki to Tallinn, Estonia does not require tapeworm treatment for dogs from EU member states. Your EU pet passport with a current rabies vaccination is the sole entry requirement.

        Estonia's VTA (Veterinaar- ja Toiduamet) handles intra-EU arrivals. Spot checks at Tallinn Airport are occasional. The document review is quick for a compliant passport. No additional form or pre-notification is needed.
