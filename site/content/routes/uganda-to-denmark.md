  sections:
    - heading: "What Fodevarestyrelsen requires, and what it does not"
      body: |
        The core sequence is unchanged from any other EU destination in this series: microchip before vaccination, a 30-day gap, a titre test reaching at least 0.5 IU/ml, and a 90-day wait counted from the blood draw. What Denmark does not add, unlike Norway or Finland further along this same corridor, is a mandatory tapeworm treatment for dogs. That is one less moving part to schedule against the travel date.

        Fodevarestyrelsen is, however, firm about non-compliance. A pet arriving without complete, correctly ordered documentation can be detained or returned to origin at the owner's cost. There is no informal allowance for an otherwise healthy animal with a paperwork gap.

    - heading: "Routing from Entebbe to Copenhagen"
      body: |
        Ethiopian Airlines via Addis Ababa is generally the most established option for a Nordic destination like Copenhagen, given the airline's long history moving live animal cargo out of East Africa. Turkish Airlines' non-stop Entebbe-Istanbul leg cuts the first stage of the journey, with reliable onward connections north. Qatar Airways via Doha is a workable Gulf alternative, though summer heat restrictions can affect live cargo scheduling.
