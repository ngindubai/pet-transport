  h1: "Pet Transport from United Arab Emirates to Lesotho"
  overview: |
    Two air waybills, one animal. That's the practical shape of every UAE-to-Lesotho pet movement, since Moshoeshoe I International Airport in Maseru has exactly one scheduled international connection, to Johannesburg, and the long-haul leg and the regional Airlink hop are booked and tracked entirely separately.

    Emirates' four-daily Dubai-Johannesburg schedule, running since a March 2025 expansion, gives that handoff more room to work with than a single daily flight would. From O.R. Tambo, Airlink covers the short final leg into Maseru roughly 21 times a week, a journey of about 65 minutes, under its codeshare arrangement with Emirates.

    The regulatory side asks for a vaccination record, a microchip and a permit from Lesotho's Director of Animal Health, nothing that resembles a blood test. Budget six to ten weeks, most of it the permit application and coordinating two separate bookings through Johannesburg.

  sections:
    - heading: "Why two waybills matter more than they sound like they should"
      body: |
        A pet flying Dubai to Maseru isn't on one continuous booking. The Emirates or Etihad long-haul leg into Johannesburg and the Airlink hop into Maseru sit on separate air waybills, often through different cargo terminals at O.R. Tambo. That's not a minor administrative detail; it's the reason a ground agent with specific Johannesburg experience is worth paying for rather than treating the connection as something that sorts itself out.

        Emirates' frequency helps here. Four flights a day from Dubai gives real flexibility if a certificate date or a permit approval slips by a day, something a single daily service, which is what several other origin countries on this site have to work with, doesn't allow.

    - heading: "The permit that has nothing to do with a blood test"
      body: |
        Lesotho's Director of Animal Health requires an import permit because the UAE sits outside the SADC bloc of member states that get a waiver on this step. It's a document requirement, not a medical one; Lesotho's own rules don't ask for a rabies titre test from any origin country. Once granted, the permit runs for six months, giving useful room if a travel date shifts.

        What deserves equal attention is the Airlink sector itself. It needs booking at least 48 hours ahead of the flight, separately from the long-haul leg, and it carries a 32kg maximum crate weight including the animal. Confirm both details against the actual pet and crate before the long-haul booking gets locked in, since discovering a mismatch after ticketing is a far more expensive problem to fix.

    - heading: "Comparing the two UAE departure points"
      body: |
        Emirates from Dubai and Etihad from Abu Dhabi both reach Johannesburg, and the choice mostly comes down to which city is the more convenient home base. Both run entirely as cargo, since neither Emirates nor Etihad carries pets in the passenger cabin to Johannesburg. Whichever is chosen, the Airlink connection at the far end behaves the same way regardless of which long-haul carrier delivers the pet to O.R. Tambo.
