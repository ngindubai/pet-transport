  h1: "Pet Transport from Fiji to Canada"
  overview: |
    Nine countries. That's the entire list the CFIA recognises as fully rabies-free, and Fiji is on it, alongside Australia, the UK, Japan and a handful of others. The practical effect for a dog at least 8 months old: no rabies vaccine required at all, just a vet certificate confirming Fiji's rabies-free status and the dog's residency there.

    Cats and younger puppies should confirm the exact wording with CFIA before assuming the same exemption applies without qualification. What actually shapes the calendar on this route is BAF's own export process in Fiji, not anything Canada asks for on arrival, and the flight itself just got considerably easier with a new direct Vancouver service.

  sections:
    - heading: "Why the Canadian side barely features"
      body: |
        Most routes on this site spend half their word count on the destination country's vaccination and testing rules. This one doesn't need to. Fiji's recognised rabies-free status under CFIA regulations means a dog 8 months or older can travel on a vet certificate alone, no vaccination record, no titre test, no waiting period.

        There's no federal import permit either. The only formality on arrival is a CBSA declaration, where the officer checks the certificate and the dog's general health. That's about as light as a destination process gets on a Pacific-to-North-America route.

    - heading: "A new direct flight, and the asymmetry worth knowing"
      body: |
        Fiji Airways began flying Vancouver to Nadi direct in June 2026, three times a week, with an Airbus A350 taking over from September. Paired with the new WestJet codeshare, that reaches Toronto, Edmonton, Winnipeg and Halifax without the Los Angeles or Auckland connection that older routings needed.

        Here's the catch worth flagging before anyone assumes it runs both ways. A pet travelling from Canada into Fiji faces a mandatory 30-day quarantine at BAF's Koronivia facility, because Fiji's own biosecurity framework, not Canada's rabies-free recognition, decides that direction. One country calling the other rabies-free doesn't make the reverse trip symmetrical.
