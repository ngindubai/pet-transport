content:
  h1: "Pet Transport from the United States to Serbia"
  overview: |
    A USDA-accredited vet and the APHIS eFile system handle the American side of this move without much drama: an export health certificate, an endorsement fee of roughly USD 38, and a straightforward process most US vets have done before. Serbia's side is less settled in the published record.

    Serbia runs a two-tier import system. Pets from the EU and from countries on Serbia's own favourable-risk lists need just a 21-day wait after a first rabies vaccination. Pets from everywhere else need a rabies titre test and a three-month wait. Which side of that line the United States sits on is not clearly published in English anywhere we could check, which matters more here than almost any other fact on this page.

  sections:
    - heading: "The part of this route that needs a phone call, not a guess"
      body: |
        Serbia's Veterinary Directorate splits import countries into a favourable-risk group, which only needs the 21-day wait, and a non-listed group, which needs the titre test and three-month wait. We confirmed this same system puts the UK on the shorter, no-titre-test side. The United States is not named on either side in the guidance pages we reviewed.

        It would be easy to assume the US sits alongside the UK, since most European countries model this kind of list on the EU's own rabies risk categories, where the US is treated as low risk. Assuming is not the same as confirming, though, and a three-month wait sprung on you late in the planning process is the kind of mistake that derails a moving date. Call the Veterinary Directorate, or ask your USDA-accredited vet to check, before you commit to a travel date.

    - heading: "Getting there: one direct flight, with a size limit"
      body: |
        Air Serbia flies JFK to Belgrade non-stop, which is a genuine advantage over most European or Middle Eastern routings for this trip. It only carries pets in the cabin, though, capped at 8kg including the carrier, and it runs no cargo hold pet programme on any route it operates. A larger dog is shut out of the direct flight entirely, titre test question or not.

        For a bigger pet, Lufthansa Cargo through Frankfurt or Turkish Airlines Cargo through Istanbul are the realistic alternatives, each adding a connection, a booking through a freight forwarder or pet transport agent, and a bit more lead time than the direct Air Serbia option would need.
