content:
  h1: "Pet Transport from the United States to Serbia"
  overview: |
    Twenty-one days is the entire waiting period Serbia asks for once a first rabies vaccination is given to a pet leaving the United States. No titre test, no three-month wait, none of the machinery countries like the UK apply to unlisted source countries. Serbia treats the US the same way it treats the UK: as a lower-risk source, needing a microchip, a current vaccination, and a correctly timed certificate.

    The US side of the paperwork is more particular than the Serbian side. A USDA-accredited vet has to issue the health certificate, and APHIS has to endorse it with an actual ink signature and embossed seal, not just an electronic submission, all inside a 10-day window before travel.

    Getting to Belgrade is its own separate question. Air Serbia flies JFK to Belgrade direct, and unlike the airline's UK, UAE and Qatar routes, this one does carry pets, but only in the cabin, and only up to 8kg combined weight. A bigger dog needs Lufthansa Cargo or Turkish Airlines Cargo instead, added to the plan from day one rather than discovered close to departure.

  sections:
    - heading: "APHIS and the US export process"
      body: |
        Exporting a pet from the US starts with a USDA-accredited veterinarian, who examines the animal and completes the health certificate in English, the language Serbia requires. That certificate then needs APHIS endorsement, which in practice means an ink signature and an embossed seal on the physical document. Submitting the paperwork electronically through VEHCS is the first step, not the last one, so budget time for the certificate to make its way back to you as a signed original if your local APHIS office cannot process it same-day.

        The certificate's validity window is short: 10 days from issue to travel, for five or fewer pets, according to APHIS's own published guidance for this specific US-to-Serbia route. That short window is exactly why the certificate has to be the last thing arranged before departure, not the first.

    - heading: "Why Serbia treats the US as a low-risk origin"
      body: |
        Serbia's Veterinary Directorate runs an EU-style framework for pet imports, sorting source countries into risk tiers much as the EU's own Annex II system does. The US sits in the lower-risk tier under this system, the same tier that includes the UK, which means a straightforward microchip-then-vaccination sequence with a 21-day wait, rather than a titre test and a three-month wait reserved for higher-risk, unlisted countries.

        This is a favourable classification, but not a permanent guarantee. Serbia can revise its own risk tiers independently of what the EU or the US do, so confirm the current position with the Veterinary Directorate or your vet close to your actual travel date, particularly if your move is still months away.

        Dogs also need a tapeworm treatment effective against Echinococcus multilocularis, given between 24 and 120 hours before arrival and recorded on the health certificate. Cats are exempt from this step.

    - heading: "Air Serbia's JFK-Belgrade route and the cargo alternative"
      body: |
        Air Serbia has operated this direct transatlantic route since June 2016, and it is one of the airline's few US or long-haul services not subject to the pet exclusion that applies to its UK, UAE and Qatar flights. Cats and small dogs travel in the cabin for a flat EUR 130 fee, provided the combined weight of pet and carrier stays under 8kg and the carrier's total dimensions stay near 94cm.

        For anything bigger, the direct flight is not a realistic option. Nothing in Air Serbia's published pet policy describes a hold or cargo service on the JFK sector. Lufthansa Cargo, connecting through Frankfurt or Munich, and Turkish Airlines Cargo, connecting through Istanbul, are the established alternatives for a larger dog, both routine choices for pet shipments between the US and the Balkans. Book either well ahead: cargo space for live animals is limited per flight and fills up faster around the summer travel season.
