  h1: "Pet Transport from Venezuela to Australia"
  overview: |
    Venezuela sits on DAFF's non-approved list alongside every other Latin American country. That single fact rules out a direct move before anything else is considered: the pet has to live in a DAFF-approved country, the USA, UK and Singapore among the realistic options, for 180 unbroken days before Australia's import process even starts.

    Getting out of Venezuela in the first place is its own separate problem right now. Simon Bolivar International Airport has been shut since the June 2026 earthquake, so the first leg of any staging plan currently runs through Valencia rather than Caracas. Add the 180-day rule, any RNATT titre test the staging country requires, and Mickleham's 10-day quarantine, and 40 to 52 weeks is a realistic floor, not a worst case.

  sections:
    - heading: "Why there is no direct route, ever"
      body: |
        DAFF splits import countries into approved groups and everyone else. Venezuela, like the rest of Latin America, falls into the non-approved category. That means an owner cannot simply fly a pet from Caracas or Valencia to Sydney with the right paperwork; DAFF requires 180 consecutive days of residency in an approved country first, verified against travel and veterinary records for that whole period.

        This isn't new, and it isn't related to the earthquake. It has been DAFF's policy for Venezuela and its neighbours for years.

    - heading: "Choosing a staging country: USA, UK or Singapore"
      body: |
        The choice shapes both the timeline and the cost. Staging via the USA or UK keeps flight connections simpler out of Bogota or Valencia, but both are DAFF Group 3, meaning the pet still needs an RNATT titre test and its own waiting period once the 180 days are up. Singapore is Group 2 and skips that test for the Australia leg entirely, which can save several months, though getting a pet into Singapore first has its own entry requirements to check.

        There's no universally right answer here. Owners with existing ties to the US or UK, family, work, a lease already signed, often find it easier to stage there despite the extra RNATT step, while those with no fixed staging preference tend to save time by choosing Singapore.

    - heading: "The Maiquetia closure only affects the first leg"
      body: |
        It's worth being precise about what the June 2026 earthquake actually changes here. It doesn't touch the DAFF rules, the 180-day residency requirement, or the RNATT test. It affects one thing: how the pet physically leaves Venezuela. Avianca and Copa are both running contingency flights through Valencia in the meantime, connecting onward to Bogota or Panama and from there to the chosen staging country.

        Reconfirm that first leg close to your actual departure date. A schedule that holds today is not guaranteed to still be running in a few weeks, since these are contingency flights, not a restored permanent timetable.

    - heading: "Mickleham, the final step"
      body: |
        Whichever staging country the pet spends its 180 days in, the Australia leg ends the same way: a mandatory 10-day stay at Mickleham Post Entry Quarantine Facility in Melbourne, DAFF's only approved facility for imported cats and dogs. Book the slot as soon as the staging country's own export health certificate date is confirmed, and plan the flight to land on a weekday, since Mickleham does not accept weekend arrivals.
