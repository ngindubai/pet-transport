  overview: |
    Thailand is a popular destination for British expats, retirees, and digital nomads, and it is increasingly common for people to bring their pets when they make the move. Thailand does not impose routine quarantine on compliant pets. If all documentation is in order and the pet appears healthy at the DLD inspection on arrival, the pet is released the same day.

    The requirements are manageable with preparation: a DLD import permit (applied for 7-60 days before departure), current vaccinations (rabies at least 21 days before arrival, plus DHPP and leptospirosis for dogs), deworming and parasite treatment within 7 days of travel, and an APHA-endorsed health certificate issued within 10 days of Thai arrival.

    The main restrictions are breed-based: Pit Bull Terriers and American Staffordshire Terriers are not permitted to enter Thailand. For all other breeds, Thailand is straightforward compared with destinations such as China, Japan, or South Korea.

    - heading: "Vaccinations for dogs and cats entering Thailand from the UK"
      body: |
        Dogs travelling from the UK to Thailand must be vaccinated against:
        - Rabies (at least 21 days before arrival, within 1 year of travel)
        - Distemper, Hepatitis, Parvovirus, and Parainfluenza (DHPP)
        - Leptospirosis

        Cats must be vaccinated against:
        - Rabies (at least 21 days before arrival, within 1 year of travel)
        - Feline Viral Rhinotracheitis, Calicivirus, and Panleukopenia (FVRCP)

        All vaccinations must be within their validity period at time of travel. The health certificate must include details of each vaccination including the product name and batch number. Deworming and external parasite treatment must be completed within 7 days of travel and recorded in the health certificate.
faqs:
  - question: "Is there quarantine for pets entering Thailand from the UK?"
    answer: "No, provided all documentation is correct and the pet appears healthy on arrival. The Department of Livestock Development (DLD) inspects the pet and documentation at Suvarnabhumi Airport. Pets that meet all requirements are released the same day. If documentation is incomplete or illness is detected, the pet may be held, returned to origin, or subject to further action."
  - question: "How do I get the Thailand DLD import permit?"
    answer: "The import permit is issued by Thailand's Department of Livestock Development. It must be applied for between 7 and 60 days before your departure date. The application covers your pet's details, travel information, and vaccination history. Your pet transport agent can assist with the application. The permit is valid for 60 days from issue."
  - question: "Can my Rottweiler enter Thailand from the UK?"
    answer: "Yes. Rottweilers are not on Thailand's banned breed list. The breeds that cannot enter Thailand are Pit Bull Terriers and American Staffordshire Terriers. All other breeds can enter with correct documentation, including Rottweilers, German Shepherds, and other large breeds."
  - question: "Which airline should I use for pets from the UK to Thailand?"
    answer: "Thai Airways and British Airways are the primary options with cargo services from Heathrow to Bangkok Suvarnabhumi. Qatar Airways (via Doha) and EVA Air (via Taipei) are alternatives with one transfer. All pets travel as manifested cargo. Book through an IATA-registered cargo agent and confirm current live animal acceptance before finalising."
  - question: "Which airport do pets enter Thailand through?"
    answer: "Suvarnabhumi International Airport (BKK) in Bangkok is the primary and established entry point for international pet imports. When booking cargo, ensure the destination is Suvarnabhumi, not Don Mueang (DMK), which is used by many low-cost carriers but may not have the same DLD processing facilities. Confirm with your cargo agent."
