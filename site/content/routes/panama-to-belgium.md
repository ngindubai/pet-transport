content:
  h1: "Pet Transport Panama to Belgium"
  overview: |
    Moving a pet from Panama to Belgium requires the full EU unlisted country process. Panama is not on the EU listed country register, so a FAVN titre test, a mandatory 90-day wait from the blood draw date, and a MIDA-DINASA endorsed EU-format health certificate are all required. The total preparation time is 20 to 24 weeks.

    KLM via Amsterdam or Iberia via Madrid are the main cargo options from Tocumen International Airport. FASFC veterinary officers inspect arriving pets at Brussels Airport. Start the titre test process as early as possible: the 90-day wait is fixed and controls the whole timeline.

  sections:
    - heading: "FAVN titre test: why Panama requires one for EU entry"
      body: |
        Belgium applies EU Regulation 2020/692 for pets from unlisted countries. Panama is classified as unlisted, which means every dog or cat entering Belgium from Panama must have a successful FAVN (or ELISA) titre test from an EU-approved laboratory. The minimum acceptable result is 0.5 IU/ml.

        The sequence is: microchip before vaccination, rabies vaccination, blood draw at least 30 days after vaccination, then a 90-day wait from the blood draw date. Shipping a blood sample from Panama to a European laboratory and receiving the result typically takes 2-4 weeks on top of the mandatory wait. The minimum total preparation time from vaccination to Belgium entry is around 20 weeks.

        Panama has veterinary laboratories and vets experienced with international pet movement. Confirm your vet's capacity to handle FAVN test sample shipping to EU-approved laboratories before starting the process.

    - heading: "Cargo from Panama City to Brussels"
      body: |
        Tocumen International Airport (PTY) in Panama City is the departure point for pet cargo. KLM operates cargo from PTY to Amsterdam Schiphol (AMS), and from AMS to Brussels Airport (BRU) is a short onward connection. This is the most established European-hub routing from Panama.

        Iberia via Madrid Barajas (MAD) is an alternative: PTY to MAD on Iberia, then MAD to BRU via Iberia partner carriers or Brussels Airlines. Book cargo during the 90-day wait period and confirm live animal acceptance on all segments before finalising the travel date.

    - heading: "MIDA-DINASA health certificate and FASFC inspection"
      body: |
        The export health certificate must be in EU format, issued by a MIDA-registered vet and endorsed by DINASA (Direccion Nacional de Sanidad Animal) of MIDA. This certificate must be issued within 10 days of travel and include microchip number, vaccination history, titre test result, and laboratory report reference.

        FASFC (Federal Agency for the Safety of the Food Chain) veterinary officers inspect arriving pets at the border inspection post at Brussels Airport. Compliant arrivals are released without quarantine. Check favv-afsca.be for the current requirements for unlisted country non-commercial movements.
