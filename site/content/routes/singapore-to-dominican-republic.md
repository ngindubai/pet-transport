content:
  h1: "Pet Transport from Singapore to Dominican Republic"
  overview: |
    The Dominican Republic asks less of an arriving pet than almost anywhere else in the Caribbean. No titre test, no quarantine, and a 15-day certificate validity window that's noticeably more forgiving than the 10-day standard most countries set. Singapore's own export side, the AVS licence and endorsed health certificate, is the part of this move that actually needs planning.

    The bigger practical obstacle is the flight. Nothing connects Singapore to Santo Domingo directly, and the realistic cargo routing changes hands twice, via Doha on Qatar Airways and then Madrid on Iberia, the same Madrid-Santo Domingo leg already verified on this site's Germany-Dominican Republic route. Budget six to eight weeks, most of it spent confirming cargo space rather than clearing paperwork.

  sections:
    - heading: "Why the Dominican Republic's import side is unusually light"
      body: |
        DGAG, the Direccion General de Ganaderia under the Ministerio de Agriculture, doesn't require a rabies titre test from any origin country, Singapore included. There's no import permit for personal pets travelling with their owner either, up to five animals. The requirements are a microchip, a rabies vaccination given between 30 days and 12 months before entry, current core vaccinations, and a health certificate.

        The certificate's 15-day validity window is genuinely useful. Most comparable destinations only accept a certificate issued within 10 days of travel, which forces a tight final-week appointment. The DR gives an extra 5 days of scheduling room, which matters more than it sounds when coordinating with a two-hub cargo booking.

    - heading: "AVS's export process from Singapore"
      body: |
        Every pet leaving Singapore, regardless of destination, needs an AVS export licence applied for via the GoBusiness Licensing Portal, and a health certificate examined by an AVS-authorised vet and endorsed by AVS itself in a format the destination country accepts. For the Dominican Republic, that means a DGAG-compatible layout.

        Not every private vet in Singapore is authorised for this endorsement step, so confirm before booking. Once the certificate and licence are in hand, the remaining task is matching their validity windows against your confirmed cargo booking, since the flight itself is the longer lead-time item on this route.
