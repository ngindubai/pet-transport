  sections:
    - heading: "Le Shuttle vs ferry: choosing your crossing"
      body: |
        **Eurotunnel Le Shuttle:** The most popular option for pets. You drive your car onto the train at Calais, and your pet stays in the vehicle with you for the entire 35-minute crossing to Folkestone. No crate, no separation, no cargo handling. The catch: only vehicles can carry pets, so foot passengers cannot use this option.

        **Ferries:** Brittany Ferries, DFDS and P&O carry pets. On short crossings (Calais or Dunkirk to Dover, around 90 minutes), pets typically stay in the car. On longer crossings, some ferries offer pet-friendly cabins or on-board kennels. Brittany Ferries in particular has invested in pet accommodation on its longer routes.

        For most people moving from northern France, Le Shuttle is the fastest and least stressful option for a dog or cat. From further south, a longer ferry crossing with a pet cabin may suit a longer journey.

    - heading: "Getting the tapeworm treatment right"
      body: |
        Dogs entering Great Britain require echinococcus tapeworm treatment administered by a vet between 24 and 120 hours before arrival. This is the single most common compliance issue on this route.

        Plan it as follows: confirm your arrival time in Great Britain, then count back. The treatment must be given no more than 120 hours (5 days) and no less than 24 hours before that arrival time. For example, arriving Saturday at noon means the treatment window is Monday noon to Friday noon.

        The vet records the treatment date and time in the pet passport. UK carriers and APHA check this on arrival. Cats and ferrets do not need tapeworm treatment, so this step applies only to dogs.

faqs:
  - question: "Is a French EU pet passport accepted for UK entry?"
    answer: "Yes. An EU pet passport issued in France (or any EU country) is accepted for entry into Great Britain, provided it is valid and complete: a microchip implanted before the rabies vaccination, a current rabies vaccination with at least 21 days passed since the primary course, and for dogs, the tapeworm treatment recorded within 24-120 hours of arrival. Note that a GB-issued pet passport is not valid for this purpose post-Brexit."
  - question: "Can I take my pet through the Eurotunnel as a foot passenger?"
    answer: "No. The Eurotunnel Le Shuttle only carries pets that are travelling in a vehicle. Foot passengers cannot bring pets through the tunnel. If you do not have a car, your options are a ferry that accepts foot passengers with pets (check the specific company's policy), the Eurostar (which does not carry pets except assistance dogs), or arranging a pet transport service to carry the animal across."
  - question: "Does my cat need tapeworm treatment to enter the UK from France?"
    answer: "No. Tapeworm (echinococcus) treatment is required for dogs only. Cats and ferrets are exempt. Your cat needs a valid EU pet passport with a microchip and current rabies vaccination, but no tapeworm treatment and no additional steps at the UK border."
  - question: "How much does it cost to bring my pet from France to the UK?"
    answer: "By road, very little. The Eurotunnel pet fee is around £22 to £45 per pet each way (on top of your vehicle booking), or a ferry crossing is £50 to £180 plus a pet fee. The tapeworm treatment for dogs adds EUR 30 to EUR 80. Total cost for driving is typically £100 to £350. Flying is possible via Air France cargo but is far more expensive and rarely worth it for this short route."
