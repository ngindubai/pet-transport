content:
  h1: "Pet Transport from Armenia to the UAE"
  overview: |
    Zvartnots International sits a little over three hours' flying time from Dubai, one of the shorter distances covered anywhere in this build. FlyDubai runs that route non-stop, which keeps a pet's time in transit down to a single leg.

    Armenia's State Service for Food Safety, SSFS, handles the export side. No formal export permit is needed for a companion pet, but the SSFS must endorse the health certificate, written on MOCCAE's own model format rather than an EU-style document.

    MOCCAE classes Armenia outside its short low-risk list, so the full import sequence applies on arrival: microchip, rabies vaccination, a titre test, and an online permit. Here's the part that surprises owners who've read up on EU rules first. There is no fixed wait after the titre test blood draw into the UAE. Once the result clears 0.5 IU/ml it stands for 365 days, no extra countdown attached.

  sections:
    - heading: "The titre test and permit sequence MOCCAE requires"
      body: |
        A pet leaving Armenia needs a microchip fitted before its rabies vaccination, a vaccination current for at least 21 days if it's the primary dose, and a rabies antibody titre test at an accredited laboratory clearing 0.5 IU/ml. The online import permit runs for 90 days from the date it's granted, so it pays to apply once a firm flight date exists.

        This is the route's real point of difference against EU-bound corridors: no fixed wait sits between the blood draw and travel. MOCCAE checks the result and its 365-day validity, nothing else. Minimum age from Armenia's risk category is 15 weeks.

    - heading: "Exporting from Yerevan and the flight options"
      body: |
        Armenia doesn't require a separate export permit for a personal pet, but the SSFS-endorsed health certificate is non-negotiable, completed on MOCCAE's model format close to the actual travel date, usually within 5 to 10 days.

        Zvartnots International (EVN) is the departure point. FlyDubai's direct EVN-DXB service is the fastest way across, and Qatar Airways Cargo via Doha is a solid second option if FlyDubai's capacity fills up around busy travel periods.
