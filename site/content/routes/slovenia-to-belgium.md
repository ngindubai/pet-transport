  h1: "Pet Transport Slovenia to Belgium: 2026 Guide"
  overview: "Slovenia and Belgium are both EU member states, so this move is covered by the standard EU pet travel framework: microchip, current rabies vaccination, and an EU pet passport or AHC. No quarantine, no titre test, no import permit. The Ljubljana-to-Brussels routing is straightforward via Vienna. Austrian Airlines connects Ljubljana (LJU) to Vienna (VIE) and from Vienna to Brussels Airport (BRU), with small pets able to travel in the cabin on both legs. For larger dogs, the Lufthansa network via Frankfurt provides the cargo option. Journey time with a connection is typically 3-5 hours."

  sections:
    - heading: "The Ljubljana to Brussels routing and why Vienna works well"
      body: |
        Ljubljana Airport (LJU) is a compact airport with limited direct connections to Western European hubs. The most natural routing to Brussels goes via Vienna, where Austrian Airlines operates from Ljubljana as part of its Lufthansa Group network. The LJU-VIE flight is under an hour, Vienna Airport (VIE) has clear transit signage and a reasonable layout for passengers with pets, and the VIE-BRU connection to Brussels Airport (BRU) is well-served.

        Austrian Airlines allows small pets in the cabin on its EU routes, with a combined weight limit (pet plus carrier) that applies per airline policy. The cabin pet fee applies per flight segment, so for a two-leg itinerary you pay twice. Book the cabin pet space when purchasing your ticket; availability on specific flights is limited.

        Lufthansa connections via Frankfurt are also available from Ljubljana, sometimes through a code-share arrangement. This routing can be useful for larger dogs travelling as cargo, given Frankfurt's Animal Lounge and its infrastructure for live animal transit.

    - heading: "The AHC endorsement process through UVHVVR"
      body: |
        Most Slovenian pet owners will have or can easily get an EU pet passport from their vet. This is the simplest document for EU-resident owners and covers travel to Belgium without further endorsement.

        If you are a non-EU national residing in Slovenia and need an AHC instead, the process involves two steps: your vet issues the certificate, and then UVHVVR (the Administration for Food Safety, Veterinary Sector and Plant Protection) endorses it. The endorsement is what makes the document officially recognised in Belgium. Allow 1-3 business days for this process and ensure the timing works so the final endorsed certificate falls within the 10-day window before your entry date into Belgium.

        From April 2026, the EU formally restricted passport use to owners who primarily reside in the EU. For Slovenian nationals and EU citizens living in Slovenia, nothing changes. For non-EU nationals on Slovenian work permits or long-term visas, the AHC is the correct document.

    answer: "Yes. Overland travel between EU member states with a valid EU pet passport is permitted without restriction. A drive from Ljubljana to Brussels is roughly 1,200 km and takes around 11-13 hours, typically via Austria and Germany. Your EU pet passport covers the entire route. Some pet owners prefer this for larger dogs that cannot travel in-cabin, as it avoids cargo logistics. If stopping overnight in any EU country, the passport remains valid throughout."
  - question: "My cat has never flown before. Will it be able to cope with the Vienna connection?"
    answer: "Most cats manage short-haul cabin travel well, particularly on two-leg itineraries with a total journey time of 3-5 hours. Keep the carrier covered with a light cloth to reduce visual stimulation during boarding and transit. Do not sedate the cat before flying; veterinary guidance and EU travel rules advise against sedation, as sedated animals are at higher risk during air travel. If your cat is particularly anxious, discuss Feliway spray or similar non-sedating calming aids with your vet before the journey."
