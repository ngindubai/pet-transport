  overview: |
    Schiphol to Manila is a well-established route, popular with expats, NGO workers, and Filipinos relocating home with pets acquired in Europe. The Netherlands-Philippines connection means there are good flight options -- but the Philippines' 7-day mandatory quarantine means your pet will not be coming home with you on the day you land.

    Plan the timeline carefully. You need a microchip, a valid rabies vaccination, a titre test, and a BAI import permit all in place before departure. The quarantine in the Philippines is a flat 7 days, regardless of documentation quality. Accept it as part of the plan.

  sections:
    - heading: "Airline options: Amsterdam to Manila"
      body: |
        Amsterdam Schiphol has strong connectivity to Manila, which gives Netherlands-based pet owners good choices.

        KLM operates the most direct Amsterdam-Manila route and is the natural choice for Netherlands departures. Their cargo handling at Schiphol is well-established. Philippine Airlines also offers direct service and has familiarity with BAI requirements at the Manila end.

        Emirates and Qatar Airways both offer one-stop connections via Dubai and Doha respectively. Both are major cargo carriers with proper temperature-controlled holds. If you have a flat-faced breed, check their current brachycephalic policies carefully.

        Singapore Airlines and Cathay Pacific route via Singapore or Hong Kong. Both have strong reputations for animal cargo handling. The Singapore or Hong Kong transfer adds time but both hubs are well-organised.

        For all cargo bookings: confirm your pet has been accepted and registered before buying your own ticket. Cargo space for pets is allocated separately and is not guaranteed at general booking.
    - heading: "Philippines import rules and the BAI quarantine"
      body: |
        The Bureau of Animal Industry (BAI) governs all pet imports to the Philippines. Their requirements are consistent and well-documented, which makes preparation straightforward if you follow the steps in order.

        Your pet needs: a microchip (implanted first), a rabies vaccination, a titre test from an OIE-approved laboratory (drawn 30+ days after vaccination, result 0.5 IU/ml or greater), a valid import permit from BAI, and an official health certificate endorsed by the NVWA.

        The 7-day facility quarantine on arrival is the most significant logistical factor for owners. Your animal goes directly to the BAI quarantine station on the day you land. You can visit during the quarantine period, and your pet is cared for by BAI staff. After 7 days, assuming no health concerns, you collect your animal.

        Apply for the import permit well before your flight. BAI can take several weeks to process applications and the permit must be in hand before your pet boards the plane.
    - heading: "Comparing direct vs connecting flights for your pet"
      body: |
        The core trade-off on this route is journey time versus routing convenience. A direct KLM flight minimises the time your pet spends in transit, which most vets recommend as better for animal welfare. KLM's direct Amsterdam-Manila service typically takes around 14-15 hours.

        Connecting flights via Dubai (Emirates), Doha (Qatar), Singapore (SIA), or Hong Kong (Cathay) add transit time but may offer more flexible departure times or lower cargo fees. Weigh these factors against your pet's needs.

        For brachycephalic breeds, shorter journey times matter more than for most animals. A direct route is preferable if available.
