  sections:
    - heading: "What Armenia requires at the border"
      body: |
        Armenia's import rules for companion pets are administered by the State Service for Food Safety (SSFS). For personal animals arriving from Estonia, the requirements are clear: a current rabies vaccination with at least 21 days elapsed since administration, and a health certificate from a licensed official veterinarian in Estonia. No titre test. No quarantine.

        The SSFS limits import to one or two personal companion pets per owner without a formal permit. If you are moving three or more animals, contact the SSFS well ahead of travel. On arrival at Yerevan Zvartnots Airport, a veterinary inspector will check the health certificate and vaccination records.

        Armenia does maintain a restricted breed list including Pit Bull Terriers, Japanese Tosas, and Dogo Argentinos. If your dog falls under one of these categories, check with the SSFS directly before planning the journey.

    - heading: "Exporting from Estonia: the Agricultural Board certificate"
      body: |
        For travel within the EU, Estonian owners use an EU pet passport issued by an authorised veterinarian. For Armenia, that document does not work at the border. You need an official export health certificate, issued by an authorised official vet and endorsed by the Pohllumajandus- ja Toiduamet (Agricultural Board of Estonia) at agri.ee.

        Contact the Agricultural Board to confirm which vets in your area hold the required authorisation and what the endorsement process involves. The certificate must be issued within 10 days of travel, so time the appointment carefully. Confirm the format Armenia currently accepts before the vet drafts the document.

    - heading: "Routing from Tallinn to Yerevan"
      body: |
        There is no direct cargo service from Tallinn Airport (TLL) to Yerevan Zvartnots (EVN). The most common option routes through Istanbul Ataturk (IST) with Turkish Airlines Cargo, which has established live animal handling infrastructure. The IST to EVN leg is a short regional hop of around two to three hours.

        An alternative is to route through Frankfurt (FRA) via airBaltic, where Lufthansa Cargo's animal lounge handles the transit before onward connections to Yerevan. Whichever route you choose, get written confirmation of live animal acceptance on every segment before committing to a booking.
