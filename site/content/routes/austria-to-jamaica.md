content:
  h1: "Pet Transport from Austria to Jamaica"
  overview: |
    Jamaica is one of the more demanding destinations in the Caribbean for pet imports. The Veterinary Services Division (VSD) requires an import permit before travel, a mandatory 14-day quarantine at the Plumb Point National Quarantine Facility in Kingston, and a strict parasite treatment schedule. Getting the permit is the first step and takes 8-12 weeks, so the timeline for this route should start months before you plan to fly.

    Austria's export side involves the standard AGES-registered official vet process. Your health certificate must be signed by an Amtstierarzt and endorsed by AGES, valid for 10 days from issue. This needs to align with the Jamaica import permit, your parasite treatment schedule, and your travel date. There is quite a lot of coordination involved, and using a professional pet transport agent is worth considering for this route.

    Flights from Vienna (VIE) to Norman Manley International Airport (KIN) in Kingston all involve at least one connection. London Heathrow via British Airways, Madrid via Iberia, and Paris CDG via Air France are the most practical cargo routings. Your pet will travel as manifest cargo. Once in Jamaica, animals are collected from the airport by Plumb Point facility staff and held for the full 14-day period before release.

  sections:
    - heading: "Jamaica's VSD Import Permit: Apply First, Book Flights Second"
      body: |
        The import permit from Jamaica's Veterinary Services Division is not a formality. Without it, your pet will not be allowed to enter Jamaica, full stop. Applications go to vsdpermits@moa.gov.jm and should include your pet's species, breed, age, sex, microchip number, and country of origin. The VSD may request supporting documents. Allow 8-12 weeks from application to receiving the permit. Only once it is confirmed should you finalise travel dates and book airline cargo space.

        Jamaica also maintains a restricted breeds list covering Dogo Argentino, Fila Brasileiro, Japanese Tosa, and Pit Bull Terrier. If your dog is one of these breeds, or a cross that could be interpreted as one, contact the VSD directly before applying. There is no appeal process at the airport if entry is refused on arrival.

    - heading: "Parasite Treatments: Timing Is Everything"
      body: |
        Jamaica's VSD requires two rounds of both internal and external parasite treatment before travel, and the timing is precise. For internal parasites, two treatments 14 days apart, with the second within 7 days of transport. For external parasites, two treatments 14 days apart, with the second within 48 hours of transport. Your vet must document both rounds with dates, products used, and dosages.

        This means the second external treatment needs to be given extremely close to departure day. Coordinate your vet appointment for this treatment before you finalise the flight booking. If the 48-hour window is missed due to a schedule change, the whole cycle may need to be repeated. Keep all treatment records with your travel documents: they will be checked at KIN and again at Plumb Point.
