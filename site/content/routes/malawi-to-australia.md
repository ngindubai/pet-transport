content:
  h1: "Pet Transport from Malawi to Australia"
  overview: |
    Malawi's UK page on this site already routes pets through Johannesburg on South African Airways as one of three options. For an Australia-bound pet, that same connection turns into something more useful: South Africa is DAFF Group 3 approved, and Malawi is not approved at all. So the flight most Malawi owners already consider becomes the way to start the 180-day residency Australia demands before it will process any import from a non-approved country.

    None of that shortens what comes after. A rabies titre test still has to clear during the residency, and Mickleham's 10-day quarantine still sits at the end of the journey regardless of where the 180 days were spent. Realistically, this route runs 30 to 36 weeks from a standing start.

  sections:
    - heading: "Why South Africa, not Kenya or Ethiopia"
      body: |
        Malawi's UK and US routes on this site name three hub options: Kenya Airways via Nairobi, Ethiopian Airlines via Addis Ababa, and South African Airways via Johannesburg. Only one of those, South Africa, is DAFF Group 3 approved. Kenya and Ethiopia don't offer the same shortcut, so for an Australia-bound pet specifically, Johannesburg is the sensible choice even though all three routes work equally well for Europe or the US.

        That's worth flagging plainly: an agent used to booking Malawi pets through Nairobi for other destinations needs to switch to the Johannesburg routing here, not out of habit but because only South Africa carries the DAFF approval this route depends on.

    - heading: "Getting the RNATT timing right"
      body: |
        DAFF's own guidance is specific: the 180-day titre wait counts from the day the approved laboratory receives the blood sample, not the vaccination date and not the day the result arrives. Book the vaccination and blood draw early in the South African residency period, once the standard post-vaccination gap has passed, and that wait runs alongside the 180-day residency instead of adding to it.

        Onderstepoort Veterinary Institute in Pretoria is the laboratory most Southern African routes on this site already point to for this test, the same one Botswana's Australia route uses. It's a reasonable default rather than the only option.

    - heading: "Finishing the journey: Perth, Melbourne or Sydney"
      body: |
        Once the residency and titre requirements clear, Qantas flies Johannesburg to Perth directly, and Emirates' fourth daily Johannesburg-Dubai service, added 1 July 2026, connects onward to Melbourne or Sydney. Pick whichever suits the final Australian city, then book the Mickleham quarantine slot early: the facility only accepts weekday arrivals, and spaces fill up.
