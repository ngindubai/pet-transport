  sections:
    - heading: "The mandatory 30-day quarantine"
      body: |
        Unlike many Southeast Asian countries, Myanmar requires all incoming pet dogs and cats to spend 30 days in quarantine at an approved facility. This quarantine is mandatory regardless of origin country, vaccination status, or documentation completeness. Compliant documentation shortens the inspection and paperwork phase but does not waive or shorten the quarantine.

        During quarantine, animals may be subject to testing or treatment if inspectors identify any health concerns. The owner is responsible for all quarantine costs throughout the period. Contact the Livestock Breeding and Veterinary Department (LBVD) for current quarantine facility details, costs, and procedures before committing to travel dates.

        Plan for the quarantine period in practical terms: you will not be able to take your pet home immediately on arrival. If you are relocating with your family, plan accommodation, school start dates, and your own settling-in period around the fact that your pet will not be with you for the first month.

    - heading: "Yangon as the only permitted entry point"
      body: |
        All international pets must enter Myanmar through Yangon International Airport (RGN). Other airports, overland border crossings, and sea ports are not accepted for pet imports. If your routing goes via Mandalay or another city, your pet must still be routed as cargo through Yangon.

        Bangkok Suvarnabhumi (BKK) and Singapore Changi (SIN) are the main connecting hubs for Yangon cargo from the UK. Thai Airways and Singapore Airlines both serve the connection with reasonable frequency. China Southern via Guangzhou is an alternative.

        Confirm with your airline that the specific routing and aircraft type accepts live animal cargo on both the UK-to-hub and hub-to-Yangon segments.
