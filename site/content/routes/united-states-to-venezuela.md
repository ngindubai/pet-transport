  h1: "Pet Transport from United States to Venezuela"
  overview: |
    No titre test, no fixed waiting period. On paper, moving a pet into Venezuela from the United States is one of the lighter import processes covered on this site: a current rabies certificate, a health certificate, the standard vaccination and deworming paperwork, and a permit fee settled on arrival.

    The paperwork was never the hard part of this route. The airport is. Simon Bolivar International Airport at Maiquetia, Venezuela's main gateway and the usual home of INSAI's own permit office, has been shut to scheduled commercial flights since the earthquakes of 24 June 2026. American Airlines' newly resumed Miami service adds to the confusion rather than solving it, since its cargo hold is not currently open to civilian pets at all.

    Budget six to ten weeks, most of it for the USDA endorsement and booking a carrier that genuinely carries pet cargo, and build in slack for the arrival airport to be confirmed close to the date rather than assumed months out.

  sections:
    - heading: "A light import process, on paper"
      body: |
        INSAI's checklist for a pet arriving from the United States is short: a current rabies certificate under a year old, an international health certificate valid one month, the combined vaccination card, and a deworming certificate valid three months, each presented as original plus copy. There is no titre test and no fixed waiting period, unlike the reverse journey into the United States, where Venezuela's CDC high-risk status forces a much longer process.

        The main US-side task is the USDA APHIS endorsement itself. Your accredited vet's signature can be electronic, but APHIS still requires an original ink signature and embossed seal on the certificate before it travels with the pet. Build in time for that endorsement step, since turnaround varies by endorsement office and cannot be rushed at the last minute.
    - heading: "Why Maiquetia's closure matters more than the checklist"
      body: |
        Twin earthquakes struck Venezuela on 24 June 2026, killing more than 4,300 people and cracking the runway, control tower and terminal at Maiquetia badly enough that Venezuela's civil aviation authority closed the airport to normal commercial traffic. Iberia and Air Europa rerouted through Valencia's Arturo Michelena airport, and Avianca has kept a Bogota-Valencia service running to preserve some connectivity while repairs continue.

        INSAI's sanitary mobilisation permit is normally issued from an office inside Maiquetia itself. With that airport shut, it is not yet clearly documented where or how that permit is being processed for pets arriving via Valencia. This is worth confirming directly with INSAI or a receiving agent in Venezuela before committing to flight dates, rather than assuming the usual airport-side process will simply transplant itself to a different terminal.
    - heading: "American is flying again, but that does not help your pet"
      body: |
        It would be easy to see American Airlines' return to Caracas in April 2026, and its new Maracaibo route from 14 July, and assume the pet transport side is solved along with the passenger side. It is not. American, Delta and United have all embargoed civilian cargo pet bookings in 2026, keeping their temperature-controlled cargo holds for active-duty US military and Foreign Service personnel on official orders only.

        Avianca remains the workable option: a genuine pet cargo service, a Miami-Bogota connection already well used on other South American routes, and a current onward leg into Venezuela via Valencia. It keeps the pet with a single carrier for both international legs, which matters when a live animal transfer is involved.
