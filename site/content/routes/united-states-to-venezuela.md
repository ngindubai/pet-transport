  h1: "Pet Transport from United States to Venezuela"
  overview: |
    No titre test, no waiting period. Venezuela's INSAI asks a pet arriving from the United States for the same light paperwork it asks of arrivals from almost anywhere else: a current rabies certificate, a health certificate, and the standard vaccination and deworming records. On paper, this sits among the easier routes covered on this site.

    The flight is the harder part. A magnitude 7-plus earthquake struck northern Venezuela on 24 June 2026 and closed Simon Bolivar International Airport at Maiquetia to normal commercial traffic. Airlines have rerouted through Valencia in the meantime, and even American Airlines' newly restored Miami-Caracas nonstop, flying again from April 2026 after roughly seven years away, has been grounded by the same closure. Budget the paperwork at a few weeks. Budget the flight planning as an open question until the airport situation settles.

  sections:
    - heading: "A lighter import process, unchanged by where the pet started"
      body: |
        INSAI's checklist does not read differently for a US-origin pet than it does for one arriving from the UK or elsewhere: a rabies certificate no more than a year old, an international health certificate valid one month, the combined vaccination card, and a deworming certificate valid three months. A sanitary mobilisation permit, priced at 5 Unidades Tributarias, is normally settled at the INSAI office inside Maiquetia airport on arrival rather than applied for weeks in advance.

        The US-side task is APHIS's International Health Certificate, completed by a USDA-accredited vet and endorsed electronically through VEHCS. The endorsement itself is a straightforward, well-established process, priced around USD 38 on top of the vet's own exam fee. The one thing worth flagging is that Venezuela's requirements "can change without notice," in APHIS's own words, so the vet checking those rules at your appointment matters more than the certificate template itself.

    - heading: "Where the permit gets processed while Maiquetia is shut"
      body: |
        This is genuinely unresolved in the research behind this page. INSAI's published guidance points to the airport office at Maiquetia, floor three, for the sanitary mobilisation permit. Maiquetia has not handled normal commercial arrivals since the June earthquake, and flights are landing at Valencia's Arturo Michelena airport instead under contingency schedules from Avianca and Copa Airlines.

        Whether INSAI has stood up an equivalent office at Valencia, or whether pets arriving there face a different process, is not something current published sources confirm one way or the other. Anyone travelling on this route while the closure continues should raise this directly with INSAI, an airline cargo desk, or a Venezuela-based pet transport agent before departure, rather than assuming the Maiquetia process simply transfers across.
