content:
  h1: "Pet Transport from Canada to Suriname"
  overview: |
    Suriname doesn't run its own dedicated import checklist for pets arriving from Canada, the way it doesn't for the UK or US routes already built on this site. The requirements are the same regardless of origin: a rabies vaccination inside a 21-day-to-12-month window, a health certificate signed within 7 days of arrival, no titre test, no import permit for a pet travelling with its owner.

    What sets this route's stakes apart is the fallback if something's wrong. Suriname's own published guidance doesn't describe a temporary holding period for non-compliant pets. It describes quarantine, return to Canada, or euthanasia, at the owner's cost.

  sections:
    - heading: "A light checklist, the same one Canada's neighbours use"
      body: |
        Suriname's rabies vaccination rule doesn't discriminate by country of departure: any date between 21 days and 12 months before arrival clears it, no antibody test required. That's identical to what a UK or US-origin pet faces on this same corridor, which makes Suriname one of the more consistent destinations on this site regardless of where the flight starts.

        The health certificate is the tightest deadline in the process, valid only 7 days from signature to landing in Suriname. Time the vet appointment against the flight date, not the other way round.

    - heading: "Getting to Paramaribo from Toronto or Montreal"
      body: |
        There's no way around Amsterdam. Surinam Airways operates the only scheduled long-haul flight into Suriname, a nonstop from Amsterdam on a leased Boeing 777, and every Canada-bound or Canada-departing booking connects through that single sector.

        Air Canada and KLM both fly Toronto or Montreal to Amsterdam, giving a genuine choice on the first leg even though the second leg has none. Brachycephalic breeds face restrictions on Surinam Airways' own published policy, worth confirming early for a flat-faced dog or cat.

    - heading: "What happens if the paperwork isn't right"
      body: |
        Most destinations on this site fall back on a fixed penalty-quarantine period when documentation is incomplete. Suriname's guidance is starker: quarantine, return to the country of origin, or euthanasia, all at the owner's expense, with no published fixed-length alternative.

        That's the real argument for getting the 7-day certificate window and the 48-hour advance notice to Veterinary Services right, rather than treating either as a formality. Neither takes long to arrange properly; both carry a genuinely harder consequence than usual if skipped.
