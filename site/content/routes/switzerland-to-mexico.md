  sections:
    - heading: "Why there's no direct option from Zurich"
      body: |
        Zurich has weekly connections to Mexico City through several alliances, but no carrier flies the sector non-stop. Iberia, KLM, Turkish Airlines, Lufthansa and Air France all connect the two cities through their respective hubs, with Iberia via Madrid the most frequent option by weekly flight count.

        For a pet, that means two airway bills instead of one, two sets of ground handling, and two chances for a delay to eat into the health certificate's validity window. Lufthansa Cargo's Zurich to Frankfurt to Mexico City routing keeps the pet under one carrier's handling for the whole journey, which is generally the simplest option to manage even though it isn't the fastest on paper.

        Edelweiss Air, SWISS's leisure subsidiary, flies direct from Zurich to Cancun rather than Mexico City. It's worth knowing about if your actual move is to the Riviera Maya, but don't assume its live animal cargo programme mirrors the mainline carriers without checking directly.
    - heading: "FSVO paperwork and the cantonal vet"
      body: |
        Switzerland doesn't require an export permit for a companion pet. What matters is the health certificate: an FSVO-authorised vet examines the animal, records the microchip and rabies vaccination, and the certificate is then endorsed either by the cantonal veterinary office or directly by the FSVO, depending on canton.

        Build the certificate timing around your confirmed flight, not the other way round, since Mexico treats an undated-expiry certificate as valid for only 15 days.
    - heading: "SENASICA on arrival in Mexico City"
      body: |
        Mexico's SENASICA inspects pets physically at the airport rather than processing an advance permit. Present the endorsed health certificate, the rabies vaccination record and the completed FF-SENASICA-003 form together at the Agriculture Health Inspection Office.

        There's no quarantine for a compliant arrival. If the paperwork is right and the animal appears healthy, clearance is usually 30 to 60 minutes. A missing document, on the other hand, can mean a local vet is called in, adding an hour or two to the wait.
