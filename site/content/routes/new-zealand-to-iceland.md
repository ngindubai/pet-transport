content:
  h1: "Pet Transport from New Zealand to Iceland"
  overview: |
    New Zealand recognises Iceland as rabies-free and waives its own equivalent titre test for a pet travelling the other way. Iceland doesn't return the favour. MAST's Category 1 and Category 2 lists between them cover roughly 40 countries, and New Zealand isn't on either, which means this direction runs on a narrow exception rather than routine paperwork.

    Getting in requires the same exception that applies to any non-listed origin: the pet must have lived with its owner in New Zealand for at least 6 months, and the owner must be genuinely relocating to Iceland. Clear that gate and the process runs to Category 2 rules, complete with a 90-day post-titre wait and Iceland's mandatory 14-day quarantine.

  sections:
    - heading: "An asymmetry that catches owners off guard"
      body: |
        Look at the reverse route on this site, Iceland to New Zealand, and it moves considerably faster: MPI already lists Iceland among its recognised rabies-free countries, alongside Japan and Singapore, which skips the FAVN titre test and its 180-day wait entirely. Coming the other way, MAST offers no equivalent recognition of New Zealand.

        Two regulators, two independent judgements about the same pair of countries. New Zealand trusts Iceland's rabies-free record on the strength of Iceland's own border controls. MAST simply hasn't made a formal classification for New Zealand either way.

    - heading: "The exception that decides whether this move happens"
      body: |
        Without a country classification, MAST's standard process doesn't apply. The workaround exists for exactly this situation: an owner who has genuinely lived with their pet in New Zealand for at least 6 months and is relocating permanently to Iceland. It isn't a route for a pet acquired shortly before the move.

        Clear that gate and the health requirements mirror the US-to-Iceland process already published on this site: vaccination, a titre test 30 days later, then a further 90-day wait from the blood draw, on top of the fixed 14-day quarantine at Reykjanesbae or Mosel.

    - heading: "Auckland to Keflavik in two legs, minimum"
      body: |
        Air New Zealand and Singapore Airlines both offer dependable cargo capacity out of Auckland toward major hubs, with Icelandair covering the final connection into Keflavik. Two separate bookings, not one through fare, so build in a genuine buffer between legs rather than a tight same-day transfer, particularly given how far this route travels.

    answer: "Only if you qualify for MAST's exception for non-listed countries: the pet must have lived with you in New Zealand for at least 6 months, and you must be genuinely relocating to Iceland with it. New Zealand is not on MAST's approved Category 1 or Category 2 list. Confirm your situation with MAST (petimport@mast.is) before starting any vaccination schedule."
  - question: "Why is Iceland to New Zealand so much simpler than this direction?"
    answer: "Because New Zealand's regulator, MPI, already classifies Iceland as a recognised rabies-free country and waives its FAVN titre test for that direction. MAST has not made the equivalent classification for New Zealand, so pets moving this way default to the stricter Category 2 process instead."
  - question: "Does my pet need a titre test for this route?"
    answer: "Yes, once MAST accepts the import under Category 2 conditions: blood drawn at least 30 days after the rabies vaccination, minimum 0.5 IU/ml, followed by a 90-day wait from the blood draw before travel."
  - question: "Is there quarantine on arrival in Iceland?"
    answer: "Yes, a mandatory minimum 14 days at Einangrunarstodin i Reykjanesbae or Einangrunarstodin Mosel, applying to every pet entering Iceland regardless of origin."
