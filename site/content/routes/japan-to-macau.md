content:
  h1: "Pet Transport from Japan to Macau"
  overview: |
    Air Macau's own NX856 covers Osaka to Macau nonstop in three and a half hours. For a person, that's the whole journey. For a pet, it changes nothing, because Air Macau does not carry animals at all, cabin or cargo.

    So the routing that actually works looks like every other long-haul Macau corridor on this site: a cargo sector into Hong Kong, most likely with Cathay Pacific or JAL Cargo, then a ferry or bridge transfer across to clear IAM's inspection. Japan's own rabies-free reputation doesn't appear to buy a shortcut on Macau's titre test either, at least not on any source checked for this guide, so plan for the fuller unlisted-origin pathway rather than assuming a fast track.

  sections:
    - heading: "A direct flight that the pet can't use"
      body: |
        It's an easy trap: Air Macau flies Osaka to Macau nonstop, so surely the pet just goes on the same plane. It doesn't. Air Macau's fleet and cargo arrangements don't extend to animal transport in any form, a genuine gap between what's available to passengers and what's available to their pets on this specific city pair.

        The workaround is the same one this site documents for Macau's other overseas routes: fly the pet to Hong Kong, most commonly with Cathay Pacific Cargo or Japan Airlines Cargo, then cross by ferry or the Hong Kong-Zhuhai-Macau Bridge to reach Macau and clear IAM's own inspection.

    - heading: "Why Japan's rabies-free status may not shorten the wait"
      body: |
        IAM's default rule waives the titre test only for pets from a place with no rabies incidents in the past two years. Japan qualifies on paper: MAFF runs one of the strictest biosecurity programmes in Asia and the country has no endemic rabies. But nothing found for this guide confirms Japan appears by name on an IAM exemption list, and this site's research into Macau's other routes has repeatedly found the same gap for origins with a strong rabies-free case, Australia and the UK included.

        Given that pattern, the honest planning assumption is the fuller pathway: titre test, roughly three months' wait, then entry. If IAM later confirms a Japan-specific exemption, the timeline shortens considerably, so it's worth checking directly rather than building a plan entirely around the conservative estimate.

    - heading: "The Tosa question"
      body: |
        Macau's banned-breed list includes the Japanese Tosa by name, alongside the Tibetan Mastiff, Fila Braziliero, Dogo Argentino and Pit Bull Terrier. Given the Tosa's standing as a recognised breed within Japan itself, this is worth checking specifically rather than assuming any dog with the correct paperwork clears the border. The restriction applies regardless of vaccination or titre status.
