content:
  h1: "Macau to Netherlands: What KLM's Own Aircraft Rules Out"
  overview: |
    Here's a quirk worth knowing before you book anything. KLM flies Amsterdam to Hong Kong direct, roughly four times a week, on flight KL887. It's a Boeing 787 Dreamliner. KLM's own pet policy bars that exact aircraft type from carrying an animal in the hold, so the one airline with its name on the destination city can't actually fly your dog there nonstop.

    Cathay Pacific fills the gap, running both a daily passenger service and a dedicated freighter on the same Hong Kong-Amsterdam sector. Get there first, though: Macau International Airport has no long-haul cargo capacity of its own, so every pet on this route starts with a transfer to Hong Kong. Add the EU's rabies titre test, which Macau doesn't get to skip, and this becomes a four-to-six-month project rather than a quick booking.

  sections:
    - heading: "KLM's aircraft, not KLM's policy on paper"
      body: |
        This isn't a case of KLM refusing pets generally. The airline carries animals on plenty of its network. The specific problem is the jet. KL887, the Amsterdam-Hong Kong service, is flown on a Boeing 787, and KLM's published rules exclude the 787-9 and 787-10 from hold pet transport entirely. Whatever KLM's general pet policy says elsewhere, it doesn't apply on this particular route.

        Cathay Pacific runs the same city pair with both scheduled passenger capacity and Boeing 747-8 freighter services, and that's where the booking should go instead.

    - heading: "The same EU gap Hong Kong doesn't have"
      body: |
        Macau sits outside the EU's list of territories exempt from the rabies antibody titre test. Hong Kong is on it. That single line item means a Macau-origin pet needs blood drawn at least 30 days after vaccination, a result of 0.5 IU/mL or better, and then a 90-day wait counted from the draw date, not the injection date, before the Netherlands will let it in.

        Once that clock has run and the EU health certificate is in hand, entry itself is straightforward: a document and identity check at a designated Traveller's Point of Entry, Schiphol being the one most Dutch-bound pets pass through, with no further holding period.
