  h1: "Pet Transport from Jamaica to the United Kingdom"
  overview: |
    Bringing your dog or cat from Jamaica to the UK takes careful planning and a fixed sequence of steps that cannot be rearranged. The UK requires a rabies titre test result followed by a mandatory 3-month waiting period, which means you are looking at a minimum of 5-6 months from first vet appointment to travel day.

    Start the process as early as possible. The most common mistake is getting the microchip and vaccination right but miscounting the 3-month wait. That wait begins on the day blood was drawn for the titre test, not when results came back or when you paid the lab bill. Get this date right and everything else follows.

    Jamaica has no shortage of good veterinary practices in Kingston and Montego Bay. Your vet can handle the microchip, vaccination, and titre test draw. The health certificate at the end needs to be signed by a government-authorised official vet and endorsed by the Veterinary Services Division.
  sections:
    - heading: "What the UK actually requires from non-listed countries"
      body: |
        Jamaica sits outside the UK's list of approved countries for simplified pet travel. That means the full APHA protocol applies: microchip, rabies vaccination, titre test, 3-month wait, tapeworm treatment (dogs), and an official health certificate.

        The UK Animal and Plant Health Agency (APHA) publishes the current rules at gov.uk/bring-pet-to-great-britain. The requirements do not change frequently but always confirm before booking anything.

        Entry must be through an approved UK port of entry for animals from non-listed countries. London Heathrow Terminal 4 handles most air cargo arrivals. Manchester and Gatwick are also approved depending on the carrier. Your cargo agent or airline will confirm which port applies to your booking.
    - heading: "Comparing your flight options from Jamaica to the UK"
      body: |
        British Airways and Virgin Atlantic offer the most direct routing: Kingston (KIN) or Montego Bay (MBJ) to London Heathrow. Both airlines carry pets as cargo only - no cabin pets on transatlantic routes. Direct is better for your pet: shorter total journey, fewer handling events, less stress.

        American Airlines connects through Miami or New York, which adds a US layover and introduces US import documentation requirements for the transit. If you use this route, your pet will technically need to meet US APHIS import standards as well as UK ones. This complicates things considerably. Use it only if direct options are unavailable.

        Book cargo space well in advance - at least 6 weeks out. Summer and Christmas periods fill quickly. Both BA and Virgin Atlantic World Cargo are used regularly for Jamaica-UK pet moves and know the route well.
    - heading: "The documentation sequence: what to prepare and when"
      body: |
        Work backwards from your planned travel date to build your timeline.

        The health certificate is valid for only 10 days, so it must be one of the last things you do. The titre test result must show at least 0.5 IU/ml and must have been drawn at an EU/UK approved laboratory - not every lab in Jamaica is on the approved list. Check the official approved laboratory list from the EU Commission or UK APHA before booking.

        For dogs, the tapeworm treatment must be given between 24 and 120 hours before you arrive in the UK. The exact timing matters: too early and it falls outside the window; too late and the certificate will be backdated incorrectly. Your vet records the treatment on the health certificate.

        Bring originals of everything. Not copies, not PDFs on your phone. The cargo agent and APHA arrival inspection require original documents with original wet signatures and official stamps.
faqs:
  - question: "How long does it take to move a pet from Jamaica to the UK?"
    answer: "Allow a minimum of 5-6 months from the day you start the process. The titre test requires a blood draw at least 30 days after rabies vaccination, then a 3-month wait from that blood draw date. Add time for lab results, booking cargo space, and getting the health certificate. Starting early is the only way to avoid delays."
  - question: "Does my dog need a tapeworm treatment before entering the UK from Jamaica?"
    answer: "Yes. Dogs must receive a Praziquantel tapeworm treatment administered by a vet between 24 and 120 hours before arriving in the UK. The vet records the treatment on the official health certificate. Cats are exempt from the tapeworm requirement."
  - question: "Can my pet travel in the cabin from Jamaica to the UK?"
    answer: "No. Transatlantic routes to the UK operate cargo-only for pets. There is no cabin pet option on flights from Jamaica to London. Your pet will travel in the temperature-controlled cargo hold as manifested cargo."
  - question: "Which laboratory can I use for the rabies titre test in Jamaica?"
    answer: "The test must be carried out at a laboratory approved by the EU or UK. Not all labs in Jamaica hold this approval. Ask your vet to confirm the lab is on the official approved list before submitting the blood sample. Using an unapproved lab means the result will not be accepted and you will need to start the titre test sequence again."
  - question: "What happens if my pet's paperwork is wrong at UK entry?"
    answer: "Pets arriving without correct documentation may be refused entry, returned to the origin country at the owner's expense, or placed in quarantine at a designated facility (also at owner's expense). Getting the documents right before travel is far less costly and less distressing than correcting problems at the UK border."
