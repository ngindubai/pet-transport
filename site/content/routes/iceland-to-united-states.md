content:
  h1: "Pet Transport Iceland to United States"
  overview: |
    One free online form. That is the entire federal entry requirement for a dog travelling from Iceland to the United States, because Iceland is not on the CDC's high-risk list for dog rabies. No vaccination proof, no import permit, no quarantine. Cats face even less. The catch is not on the US side at all, it is getting the pet out of Iceland with a MAST-endorsed certificate and onto a flight that, since Icelandair ended checked-baggage pet travel in late 2024, now runs through cargo booking rather than the passenger desk.

    Nine Icelandair gateways run year-round from Keflavik into the US, from Boston to Seattle, which gives more routing flexibility than most non-EU pet moves get. The regulatory side of this route is unusually simple. The logistics side, booking cargo space and timing the MAST certificate, still needs proper planning.

  sections:
    - heading: "Why Iceland gets the light-touch US entry rule"
      body: |
        Since 1 August 2024, the CDC runs a single consolidated rule for dogs entering the US, sorted by whether the country of departure is high-risk for dog rabies. Iceland, being rabies-free as an island nation with tight biosecurity of its own, sits off that high-risk list entirely. That single classification decision removes the rabies vaccination certificate, the microchip-before-vaccination sequencing rule, and any waiting period from the process altogether.

        What remains is a free CDC Dog Import Form, completed online any time from six months before travel up to the day itself, plus a universal-scanner-readable microchip and a dog old enough (at least 6 months) and well enough to travel. Cats have it easier again: no federal rabies rule applies to cats from any country, so a health certificate for airline purposes is really the only paperwork on the table.

        This does not mean skip the vet. CBP officers can inspect any animal on arrival and can act if a pet looks unwell, regardless of what the paperwork says.

    - heading: "Nine gateways, but no more checked baggage"
      body: |
        Icelandair's year-round US network out of Keflavik covers Boston, New York JFK, Newark, Washington Dulles, Chicago O'Hare, Denver, Seattle-Tacoma, Raleigh-Durham and Orlando, with seasonal additions such as Miami, Nashville and Pittsburgh in the warmer months. That is a wider spread of nonstop options than most routes on this site into the US, which normally have to route through a European or Middle Eastern hub first.

        The complication is that Icelandair stopped accepting pets as checked or excess baggage on 1 November 2024. Every pet on this route now moves as manifested cargo through Icelandair Cargo, a separate booking channel with its own space allocation and lead times, not something you add at passenger check-in. Book the cargo slot as early as you can, particularly for summer travel when Icelandair's transatlantic schedule runs busiest.

        The MAST-endorsed export certificate still needs to be timed against your actual flight date, not against when you happen to see the vet. Since the US side asks for almost nothing, it is easy to under-prepare the Iceland side by comparison. Do not.
