    - heading: "Titre test requirement from Turkey to Italy: the sequence"
      body: |
        Turkey is not on the EU's listed country register. All pets moving from Turkey to Italy need the FAVN titre test and a 3-month wait from the blood sampling date.

        Fixed sequence: microchip implanted first, then rabies vaccination, then after 21 days blood is drawn at an EU-approved laboratory. The 3-month wait begins from the blood draw date. If blood is drawn on 1 March, travel to Italy is not possible before 1 June.

        The Turkish Ministry of Agriculture and Forestry endorses the EU-format certificate. The endorsed certificate must be dated within 10 days of travel. Total preparation: approximately five months. Source: food.ec.europa.eu, 2026.

    - heading: "Italian breed rules: no national ban since 2009"
      body: |
        Italy removed its national breed ban in 2009 and now uses a behaviour-based approach. There are no federal breed-specific import restrictions at the Italian border. Any breed from Turkey can enter Italy through standard EU import documentation.

        Italian customs and Ministry of Health veterinary officers verify the microchip, EU certificate (with Ministry endorsement), vaccination record, and titre test result on arrival at FCO or MXP. If the documentation is complete, no breed-specific additional requirements apply.

        Some regional or local ordinances in Italy may have their own rules; check your specific destination if your dog is of a breed that has faced restrictions elsewhere.

    - heading: "Turkish Airlines direct to Rome and Milan"
      body: |
        Turkish Airlines operates direct cargo from Istanbul Airport (IST) to both Rome Fiumicino (FCO) and Milan Malpensa (MXP). Turkish Airlines Cargo accepts live animals on these routes, and the flight time from Istanbul to Rome is approximately three hours, making it one of the shorter cargo journeys in the EU.

        ITA Airways (formerly Alitalia) is an alternative for the IST to FCO route. Confirm live animal cargo acceptance on your specific flight and route before booking. Book four to six weeks ahead of travel.
