  sections:
    - heading: "How Portugal's entry system works for EU pets"
      body: |
        Portugal uses the standard EU pet travel framework. The EU pet passport from your Lithuanian vet records the microchip number (which must pre-date the rabies vaccination), the vaccination history, and your vet's registration details. This document covers entry into Portugal without any additional certificates or permits.

        At Lisbon or Porto airport, the DGAV veterinary check verifies the passport details and scans the microchip to confirm it matches the passport record. For compliant pets, this takes a few minutes.

    - heading: "Direct flights and cargo from Lithuania to Portugal"
      body: |
        Ryanair is an important carrier for this route because it operates seasonal direct flights from Vilnius (VNO) and Kaunas (KUN) to Lisbon (LIS), Porto (OPO), and Faro (FAO). These are cabin-only services for pets, but they give small pet owners a convenient, direct option without a lengthy connection.

        For larger pets in cargo, LOT Polish Airlines via Warsaw or Lufthansa Group via Frankfurt both provide cargo-capable connections to Lisbon. TAP Air Portugal accepts live animal cargo at its Lisbon hub from connecting flights. Book cargo spaces at least four weeks in advance.

    - heading: "The Algarve, Lisbon, Porto, and Azores as destinations"
      body: |
        Most international cargo for Portugal arrives at Lisbon. Porto Francisco Sa Carneiro Airport (OPO) also handles live animal cargo arrivals. For owners moving to the Algarve, Faro Airport (FAO) receives some connecting cargo services, though fewer options exist than at Lisbon.

        The Azores and Madeira are autonomous regions of Portugal within the EU. The same EU pet passport rules apply for those islands, but cargo service is more limited. If your destination is an island within Portugal, research cargo availability to the specific island airport when planning.

    answer: "Yes, subject to Ryanair's cabin pet policy. Ryanair accepts small pets in the cabin within a carrier that fits under the seat, within their weight and size limits. For larger dogs that cannot fit in the cabin, Ryanair is not suitable, and a cargo carrier (LOT, Lufthansa, TAP) should be used instead."
  - question: "What size restrictions does TAP Air Portugal have for cabin pets?"
    answer: "TAP Air Portugal accepts pets in the cabin up to a combined pet-plus-carrier weight of approximately 8kg. Carriers must fit under the seat in front. This covers most small dogs and cats. For medium and large dogs, TAP's cargo service handles live animals. Check TAP's current policy when booking as specifications can change."
