    This is one of the more forgiving routes in the whole Caribbean-Gulf-Pacific cluster covered in this series: Australia sits in Category 1 on Trinidad and Tobago's own country list, alongside New Zealand and the UK, which means no rabies titre test at all. Compare that with a US or UAE-origin pet, both stuck in Category 2 and facing the full titre sequence.

  sections:
    - heading: "Why Category 1 makes such a difference"
      body: |
        The Chief Veterinary Officer's country list, last revised in January 2023, sorts every origin into four categories, and Category 1 is reserved for a short list of countries Trinidad and Tobago treats as the lowest risk: Australia, New Zealand, the UK and most of Western Europe. A pet from any of these skips the titre test entirely, needing only the standard microchip-then-vaccination sequence and the import permit.

        Combined with the country's no-quarantine policy for compliant pets, in place since April 2013, this makes Australia-to-Trinidad genuinely one of the simpler international pet moves this site covers, despite the long-haul distance involved.

    - heading: "The real bottleneck: getting there at all"
      body: |
        With the paperwork side this light, the flight routing becomes the main planning consideration. British Airways' nonstop Gatwick-Piarco service is the established option, reached via a connecting flight from an Australian gateway to London. A US gateway connection through Miami is the practical alternative when the London routing doesn't fit the required travel window, though it typically adds a second transfer rather than removing one.
