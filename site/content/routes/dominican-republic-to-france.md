  sections:
    - heading: "Air France direct to Paris CDG: the practical routing"
      body: |
        Air France operates regular direct flights between Santo Domingo Las Américas International (SDQ) and Paris Charles de Gaulle (CDG). For pet cargo, a direct transatlantic service removes the complexity of a hub connection and associated live animal cargo rebooking on a second carrier.

        Air France Cargo handles live animal freight. CDG is France's primary international pet cargo entry point, with dedicated border inspection facilities for incoming animals. French border inspection for compliant pets with correct documentation is a routine check.

        The flight time from SDQ to CDG direct is approximately 9 hours. If the Air France schedule does not suit, Iberia via Madrid (MAD) is the alternative. The SDQ-MAD-CDG routing adds 2-4 hours of total travel time but is well established for transatlantic cargo.

    - heading: "The FAVN titre test process from the Dominican Republic"
      body: |
        The FAVN test is the key medical requirement for pets entering the EU from unlisted countries. The blood sample must be drawn at least 30 days after the rabies vaccination, stored correctly, and shipped to an EU-approved laboratory. Most approved labs are in Europe, with France's ANSES laboratory at Maisons-Alfort being one option given the direct Air France routing.

        Laboratory turnaround from receipt of sample is typically 1-2 weeks. Total time from blood draw to result, including shipping, is 2-4 weeks. The 90-day wait runs from the blood draw date regardless of when the result comes back.

        From the Dominican Republic, international sample shipping requires correct temperature management. Your vet should use a sodium azide preservative medium and an international courier familiar with biological samples. Ask explicitly whether your clinic has handled FAVN test shipping to European labs before. If not, a pet transport specialist can coordinate this.

    answer: "Your pet enters through a border inspection post at CDG. The inspector checks the EU health certificate, titre test result, vaccination record, and microchip. For compliant pets, this is a formality. The SIVEP (Service d'Inspection Vétérinaire et Phytosanitaire) team at CDG handles this inspection. Your cargo agent will advise on the collection process after clearance."
