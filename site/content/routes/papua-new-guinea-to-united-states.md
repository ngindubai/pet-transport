  h1: "Pet Transport from Papua New Guinea to the United States"
  overview: |
    Papua New Guinea does not appear on the CDC's high-risk countries list for dog rabies, last updated 15 April 2026. That single fact keeps this route out of the CDC's toughest bracket: no titre test, no six-airport restriction, no mandatory microchip-before-vaccine sequence.

    What still makes it a genuine planning job is geography. Air Niugini restarted its Port Moresby to Tokyo Narita service on 18 July 2026, flying twice a week, and it also serves Brisbane. Neither city is a US gateway, so every pet needs a second carrier, ANA out of Narita or Qantas out of Brisbane, to finish the journey.

    Cats have almost nothing to do on the US side beyond a general health check. Dogs need the CDC Dog Import Form and, sensibly, a rabies vaccination even though CDC only recommends rather than requires it for a low-risk country.

  sections:
    - heading: "Why the CDC's high-risk list matters more than anything else here"
      body: |
        CDC sorts every country a dog has visited in the past six months into one of two buckets: high risk or everything else. Papua New Guinea sits in the second, confirmed by direct check against the CDC's high-risk countries page, last updated 15 April 2026. That removes the titre test, the requirement to land at one of the six CDC-registered facility airports, and the strict chip-before-vaccine sequencing a high-risk-country dog cannot avoid.

        A US-bound dog from Papua New Guinea can, in practice, arrive at any US airport, seaport or land border. Vaccination is strongly recommended rather than mandatory. Cats face no federal rabies rule at all, whatever their country of departure.

    - heading: "A route that just got a new option"
      body: |
        For years, Brisbane via Qantas was the default first leg out of Port Moresby toward the US. Air Niugini's Tokyo Narita service, restarted on 18 July 2026 after a gap, opens a second path, with ANA picking up the transpacific leg into gateways like Los Angeles, New York JFK or Chicago.

        Both options add a connection; there's no nonstop from Port Moresby to anywhere in the US. Narita runs only twice weekly, on Wednesdays and Saturdays, so it needs to be built into the schedule early rather than assumed available on short notice.

    - heading: "PNG's own paperwork, whichever way you fly"
      body: |
        Whatever gateway you choose, NAQIA's side of the process stays the same: a pre-flight exam within 48 hours of departure, followed by an export health certificate issued within 14 days of travel and endorsed by NAQIA. There's no dedicated export permit form separate from that certificate.

        The lighter US-side rules don't remove PNG's own requirements. A cat still needs the same NAQIA-endorsed paperwork as a dog, even though it faces almost nothing once it lands in the US.
