  sections:
    - heading: "How the DLD import permit works"
      body: |
        The import permit is issued by the Department of Livestock Development (DLD) Animal Quarantine Station at the port of entry: typically Bangkok Suvarnabhumi (BKK) or Don Mueang (DMK). You apply by email to the relevant quarantine station, providing your pet's vaccination records, microchip details and intended travel date. The station reviews the application and sends the permit within approximately seven working days.

        Apply between 7 and 60 days before departure. The permit is valid for 60 days, so there is reasonable flexibility if your travel date shifts. What you cannot do is arrive without a permit; that results in the pet being held at the quarantine station while the paperwork is sorted, which adds cost and stress.

        The permit email with the station's contact details for Suvarnabhumi can be found on the Thai Ministry of Agriculture and Cooperatives website. A Thai-based pet relocation agent can handle the application on your behalf if you prefer not to manage the correspondence directly.

    - heading: "Vaccination requirements for dogs and cats coming from India"
      body: |
        Thailand requires more than just a rabies vaccination for dogs. The full list for dogs includes rabies, canine distemper, canine hepatitis, canine parvovirus and leptospirosis. Cats need rabies and feline panleukopenia (feline distemper). All must be administered at least 21 days before arrival and must be within the past 12 months.

        The microchip must be in place before any vaccination is given. This matters because the Thai DLD links the vaccination record to the microchip. If there is any doubt about whether the chip was implanted before or after a particular vaccination, the DLD may treat that vaccination as invalid.

        Your DAHD-authorised vet in India records all vaccinations in the health certificate, which is then officially stamped and endorsed. Keep the original vaccination booklet alongside the health certificate as supporting evidence. Thai officers sometimes request both.

    - heading: "Airline cargo options from India to Bangkok"
      body: |
        Air India and Thai Airways both operate direct services from Delhi Indira Gandhi (DEL) and Mumbai (BOM) to Bangkok Suvarnabhumi (BKK), and are the two most practical cargo carriers on this route. Both have established live animal handling at BKK.

        For smaller dogs and cats, some carriers allow in-cabin travel in an approved carrier. Check current size and weight limits directly with the airline before booking; policies change. For medium and large dogs, hold cargo is the only option.

        Book cargo space four to six weeks ahead for large crates. At Bangkok's NAIA-equivalent (Suvarnabhumi cargo terminal), Thai Airways Cargo has experience processing live animal arrivals. Ensure the IATA-compliant crate is properly assembled, ventilated and labelled with the DLD permit reference before drop-off.

faqs:
  - question: "Is a titre test required to bring a dog from India to Thailand?"
    answer: "No. Thailand does not require a FAVN titre test for pet imports. The main requirements are a current rabies vaccination (plus core vaccines for dogs), a DLD import permit, and an official DAHD health certificate with a government stamp."
  - question: "Will my dog be quarantined on arrival in Thailand from India?"
    answer: "Not automatically. Quarantine is discretionary in Thailand. For compliant arrivals with all correct documentation, DLD officers typically clear the pet on arrival. A quarantine detention of up to 30 days can be imposed if there are document irregularities or health concerns."
  - question: "How do I apply for the DLD import permit for Thailand?"
    answer: "Apply by email to the DLD Animal Quarantine Station at your port of entry (typically Bangkok Suvarnabhumi or Don Mueang). Include your pet's vaccination records, microchip number and travel date. The permit is issued within about 7 working days and is valid for 60 days. Apply between 7 and 60 days before departure."
  - question: "Which airline is best for moving a large dog from India to Thailand?"

Book cargo space at least 4-6 weeks ahead and confirm the live animal booking explicitly with the cargo division, not just the passenger desk."
