  h1: "Pet Transport from Belarus to the United Arab Emirates"
  overview: |
    Every other Belarus route on this site starts the same way, an overland transfer to Warsaw, because Belavia lost EU and UK airspace access in 2021. This one doesn't. The ban stops at the edge of European airspace, and both Belavia and flydubai keep flying Minsk to Dubai directly, no detour required.

    The paperwork doesn't get the same discount. Belarus isn't on MOCCAE's low-risk list, so a rabies titre test still applies, just without the fixed waiting period that follows a titre result in the EU or the UK.

  sections:
    - heading: "Why the airspace ban doesn't reach this route"
      body: |
        Belavia's aircraft have been barred from EU and UK airspace since the forced landing of Ryanair Flight 4978 in May 2021, a restriction tightened further in December 2025. That ban is geographically specific. It says nothing about Gulf airspace, so Belavia's Minsk-Dubai service, roughly 7 hours 20 minutes nonstop, has continued running throughout.

        flydubai flies the same city pair, giving owners a genuine second carrier rather than a single point of failure. Both accept pets exclusively through their cargo departments, never as checked baggage, which is the one real constraint on either booking.

    - heading: "Why the titre test still applies here"
      body: |
        MOCCAE keeps a low-risk list of roughly 50 countries that skip the rabies antibody titre test entirely. Belarus is not one of them, so a FAVN or RNATT result of at least 0.5 IU/ml is still required, alongside a 15-week minimum import age rather than the 12-week minimum low-risk countries get.

        There's a genuine upside compared with an EU or UK-bound pet, though. MOCCAE doesn't impose a fixed wait once the titre result comes back passing, unlike the EU's roughly 3-month rule. Book the flight as soon as the result and the import permit are both in hand.
