  sections:
    - heading: "Chile is listed for the EU: why that matters for Spain"
      body: |
        When the EU classifies a country as listed, it means pets from that country can enter the EU without a rabies titre test. Chile has this status. Argentina and Mexico share it in Latin America; Colombia and Peru do not.

        For a Santiago to Madrid move, the impact is a 4-month reduction in preparation time and a saving of EUR 85-170 on titre test laboratory fees. The process becomes: confirm microchip predates vaccination, ensure rabies vaccination is current with the 21-day wait satisfied, book cargo, and obtain the SAG-endorsed Animal Health Certificate within 10 days of travel.

        Spanish customs at Madrid Barajas verify the microchip, AHC format, and vaccination record on arrival. The process is well-established for Chilean pets; Iberia and LATAM both carry South American pets regularly on this corridor.

    - heading: "PPP rules in Spain: the detail Chilean owners need to plan for"
      body: |
        Spain classifies certain dog breeds as Perros Potencialmente Peligrosos (PPP). This classification does not prevent import, but it does impose ongoing obligations once in Spain.

        PPP breeds include Pit Bull Terrier, American Staffordshire Terrier, Rottweiler, Dogo Argentino, Fila Brasileiro, Tosa Inu, and Akita Inu. Dogs that physically resemble these breeds can also be classified as PPP on inspection, even if of mixed heritage.

        In public spaces, a PPP dog must be muzzled at all times and on a leash no longer than two metres. The owner must hold a specific civil liability insurance policy and apply for a PPP licence from the local municipality. The PPP licence involves a criminal background check and a basic dog-handling competency assessment, and processing times vary by city. In Madrid, Barcelona, and other major cities, the process is established. Factor this into your timeline for settling in.

        If you are renting property, check with the landlord before arrival: some rental contracts in Spain exclude PPP breeds.

    - heading: "LATAM and Iberia cargo from Santiago: which is better for pets?"
      body: |
        Both LATAM and Iberia operate direct flights from Santiago (SCL) to Madrid Barajas (MAD), and both have established pet cargo services on this route. The Chile-Spain corridor is one of the most active South America to Europe pet transport routes due to the large Chilean community in Spain.

        LATAM Cargo handles live animals as manifested cargo in the pressurised, temperature-controlled hold. LATAM has experience with South American pets moving to Europe. Iberia Cargo similarly accepts live animals and has strong handling practices at Barajas where the inspection takes place on arrival.

        When choosing between the two, compare cargo space availability for your specific travel dates and the crate size requirements. Both airlines require IATA LAR-compliant crates. Book four to six weeks ahead and confirm live animal acceptance per specific flight number. Air Europa is a third option on the route for backup.
