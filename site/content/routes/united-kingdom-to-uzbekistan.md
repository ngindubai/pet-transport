  h1: "Pet Transport from United Kingdom to Uzbekistan: 2026 Guide"
  overview: |
    Uzbekistan doesn't ask UK pet owners for a rabies titre test, the single most expensive and time-consuming step on most long-haul routes. That's the good news. The less good news: the country's own paperwork trail runs through a Soviet-era style veterinary bureaucracy, the State Committee for Veterinary and Livestock Development, and public guidance on exactly what it requires from an accompanied pet is patchy and sometimes contradictory.

    Add a flag carrier that flies London to Tashkent only once or twice a week, and the practical challenge on this route isn't a complex medical protocol. It's timing a weekly flight slot against a permit process that some sources say doesn't apply and others say is mandatory, run through a portal, vet.uz, that most UK owners have never heard of before they start planning.

  sections:
    - heading: "Why there's no titre test, but there is a permit question mark"
      body: |
        Most EU-bound and long-haul routes UK owners research online involve a FAVN or ELISA rabies antibody titre test and a 90-day wait once a destination treats the country of origin as unlisted for rabies control. Uzbekistan skips that step entirely for pets vaccinated within the standard 30-day to 12-month window. That alone makes the medical side of this route simpler than routes to, say, Ireland or much of the EU.

        What replaces it is administrative uncertainty rather than a lab test. Some import guidance describes entry for an accompanied non-commercial pet without a separate permit. A process review dated February 2026 states plainly that the State Committee for Veterinary and Livestock Development requires an import permit filed through its vet.uz portal, plus government endorsement of the UK export certificate, and that owners commonly miss the endorsement step because they assume the certificate itself is enough. Given this is a YMYL move involving a live animal, plan for the stricter reading: apply for the permit, get the certificate endorsed, and treat vet.uz confirmation as a checklist item you tick off in writing, not an assumption.
    - heading: "A dogs-only certificate number, and what that means if you're moving a cat"
      body: |
        Gov.uk's export health certificate finder lists "Export dogs to Uzbekistan: certificate 6653," version 3, last updated by APHA on 20 June 2022. It does not show a parallel certificate specifically for cats. That's unusual: comparable routes, such as certificate 2911 for cats and dogs to Iraq or certificate 3458 for cats or dogs to Kazakhstan, cover both species on one form.

        It doesn't mean cats can't travel. It means the published finder hasn't caught up, or the correct certificate sits outside the standard online list. Either way, call APHA's pet travel line before booking a vet appointment for a cat on this route, so the Official Veterinarian signs the right paperwork the first time.
    - heading: "Flight capacity is the real bottleneck"
      body: |
        Uzbekistan Airways operates flight HY202, a Boeing 787 service from Heathrow to Tashkent, on a weekly or twice-weekly schedule as of the March 2026 timetable. Small pets under 8kg combined weight with carrier can fly in the cabin on this service. Larger animals travel as tracked manifest cargo through Uzbekistan Cargo Services, which still depends on the same limited flight frequency.

        With only one or two direct departures a week, a paperwork delay of even a few days can push the move to the following week's flight. Turkish Airlines via Istanbul, running up to four Istanbul-Tashkent legs daily on top of a daily London-Istanbul service, gives more scheduling room if the direct flight doesn't line up with your permit and certificate dates.
