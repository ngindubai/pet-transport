  sections:
    - heading: "Uganda's import permit and what you need before booking"
      body: |
        The Ministry of Agriculture, Animal Industry and Fisheries (MAAIF) at maaif.go.ug is the Ugandan authority that controls companion pet imports. Before your pet can legally enter Uganda, you need an import permit issued by MAAIF. This permit is not something you apply for on the day of travel. The process takes several weeks and should be started as soon as you know you are moving.

        Contact MAAIF in advance to understand the current application requirements, the fee structure, and the turnaround time. A specialist pet transport agent experienced in East African destinations can help coordinate the permit application alongside the cargo booking.

        Once the permit is in hand and your routing is confirmed, the other requirements are more straightforward: a current rabies vaccination and a VMVT-endorsed export health certificate from Lithuania.

    - heading: "The VMVT export certificate for non-EU travel"
      body: |
        The EU pet passport applies to travel within the EU and EEA. For Uganda, it does not carry any legal weight at the border. The required document is an official export health certificate issued by a VMVT-authorised official veterinarian, endorsed by the Valstybine maisto ir veterinarijos tarnyba (VMVT) at vmvt.lt.

        Contact the VMVT before the vet appointment to confirm the process and to identify an officially authorised vet. Not all vets in Lithuania hold this status. The certificate must be issued within 10 days of travel, so timing the appointment correctly relative to your departure date is important.

    - heading: "Routing from Vilnius to Entebbe"
      body: |
        Vilnius Airport (VNO) is a regional airport with limited direct international cargo capacity to East Africa. The journey to Entebbe International Airport (EBB) requires at least two legs.

        The most reliable option routes via Brussels Airport (BRU) with Brussels Airlines, which operates direct cargo flights to Entebbe. From Vilnius, reach Brussels via LOT to Warsaw then onwards, or via airBaltic through Riga. Brussels Airlines has an established East Africa cargo operation and handles live animals on this route.

        An alternative routes through Addis Ababa (ADD) via Ethiopian Airlines, which serves EBB from its ADD hub. Ethiopian Airlines Cargo handles live animals and has extensive African coverage. The choice between Brussels and Addis Ababa often depends on cargo capacity and scheduling on your specific dates.
