  sections:
    - heading: "Why 20-26 weeks: the EU unlisted country process"
      body: |
        The EU divides origin countries into two categories. Countries on the approved list can send pets quickly. Panama is not on that list. For unlisted countries, EU Regulation 576/2013 requires: ISO microchip before vaccination, rabies vaccination, FAVN blood draw at least 30 days post-vaccination, a 90-day wait from blood draw date, and a health certificate within 10 days of travel.

        Each step in that sequence must complete before the next begins. The result is a minimum five-month preparation window for this route. Planning the blood draw as early as possible after the 30-day post-vaccination wait is key to keeping the overall timeline at the shorter end.

        Once the 90-day period closes and the EU certificate is ready, Italy imposes no quarantine for compliant arrivals. FCO and MXP both have well-organised cargo arrival procedures.

    - heading: "Madrid as the bridge between Panama and Italy"
      body: |
        There is no direct cargo service from Panama to Italy. The most practical routing is Iberia via Madrid (MAD). Iberia operates PTY-MAD and MAD-FCO, making it a logical carrier for cargo on this corridor. Iberia Cargo handles live animals; confirm current acceptance and capacity on your travel dates.

        If Iberia cargo is not available for your dates, Air France via CDG is an alternative. PTY connections through US or Caribbean hubs to CDG, then CDG to FCO or Milan Malpensa (MXP). Copa Airlines, based at PTY, connects to European partners but the routing is less direct.
