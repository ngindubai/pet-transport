  h1: "Pet Transport from the United Kingdom to Italy"
  overview: |
    Italy is a popular destination for British expats and for those relocating for work or retirement. Italy follows EU pet import rules, which means pets from the UK face no quarantine, no titre test, and no import permit. The post-Brexit change is the AHC: you now need a fresh Animal Health Certificate for every trip, as GB-issued pet passports are no longer accepted by EU countries.

    The core requirements are straightforward: an ISO microchip implanted before the rabies vaccination, a current rabies vaccination (with a 21-day wait after the first dose), and an AHC issued by an APHA-authorised vet within 10 days of arrival in Italy. British Airways and ITA Airways both operate cargo services on this corridor.

    Bear in mind that the major low-cost carriers (Ryanair, easyJet) do not accept pets. If you are flying with one of them, your pet needs a separate cargo booking through a full-service carrier.

Most cargo pet shipments from the UK arrive at FCO or MXP, which have the largest cargo handling facilities. faqs:
  - question: "Do I need a titre test to take my dog from the UK to Italy?"
    answer: "No. A titre test is not required for dogs or cats travelling from the United Kingdom to Italy. The UK is listed as an approved third country under EU Regulation 2026/131. The requirements are: ISO microchip (implanted before rabies vaccination), current rabies vaccination (21-day wait after first dose), and a fresh AHC issued within 10 days of arrival in Italy."
  - question: "Can I take my pet on Ryanair or easyJet to Italy?"
    answer: "No. Ryanair and easyJet do not accept pets in cabin or cargo on any route. If you are planning to travel with one of these airlines, your pet needs a separate cargo booking through British Airways, ITA Airways, or another full-service carrier on the same route or a nearby airport."
  - question: "How often do I need to get a new AHC for Italy?"
    answer: "You need a fresh Animal Health Certificate for every trip to Italy (or any EU country). GB-issued pet passports are no longer valid for EU entry as of April 2026. The AHC must be issued by an APHA-authorised official veterinarian within 10 days of your arrival date in Italy."
  - question: "Is there quarantine for pets arriving in Italy from the UK?"
    answer: "No. Italy does not impose routine quarantine on pets arriving from the UK with correct documentation. The border vet at the Italian airport checks the AHC, scans the microchip, and verifies the vaccination record. Compliant pets are cleared on arrival day."
  - question: "Are there breed restrictions for dogs entering Italy from the UK?"
    answer: "Italy does not operate a national PPP-style breed restriction list like Spain. However, some Italian regions and municipalities have local breed-specific rules. Check the regulations for your specific destination city or comune before travel, particularly if your dog is a breed commonly subject to restrictions in Europe (Pit Bull types, Rottweiler, and similar)."
