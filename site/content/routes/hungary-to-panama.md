content:
  h1: "Pet Transport from Hungary to Panama"
  overview: |
    Relocating a pet from Hungary to Panama requires one step that is easy to overlook: the official health certificate must be authenticated by the Embassy of Panama before it leaves Hungary. For Hungarian residents, the Embassy of Panama in Vienna covers this consular function, though this should be confirmed directly before starting the process. The authentication must take place after Nebih endorsement and before departure, all within the 10-day validity window of the EU-format certificate.

    Panama's MIDA (Ministerio de Desarrollo Agropecuario, mida.gob.pa) manages all pet imports at Tocumen International Airport (PTY) in Panama City. There is no import permit, no quarantine, and no titre test. A MIDA inspection on arrival takes around 20 minutes when documents are in order, and a registration fee of approximately USD 20 applies.

    Budapest Ferenc Liszt International Airport (BUD) connects to Panama City (PTY) via Copa Airlines, Iberia via Madrid, and Air France via Paris CDG. Wizz Air operates Budapest to Madrid, which supports the Iberia cargo routing. Allow six to eight weeks from preparation to departure to account for the Nebih endorsement, Vienna authentication, and any courier transit.

  sections:
    - heading: "Authentication via the Embassy of Panama in Vienna"
      body: |
        Panama requires health certificates from third countries to be authenticated by a Panamanian Embassy or Consulate. For Hungary, this function is covered by the Embassy of Panama in Vienna. Contact the Embassy directly at the start of your planning to confirm they handle Hungarian certificates, current processing times, the required documents, and fees. This step is non-negotiable: MIDA at Tocumen Airport (PTY) will not accept a certificate without Panamanian consular authentication.

        If the certificate needs to be sent by courier to Vienna and returned, add three to six days of courier time to the Embassy's processing period. The Nebih endorsement, courier to Vienna, Embassy authentication, and courier back to Budapest must all happen within the 10-day validity of the health certificate. Working backwards from your flight date, the official vet appointment may need to fall on the first or second day of the 10-day window, with tight courier and processing schedules.

    - heading: "Hungarian Export Requirements"
      body: |
        Hungary's EU-format health certificate must be issued by a Nebih-authorised official vet and endorsed by Nebih (Nemzeti Elelmiszerlanc-biztonsagi Hivatal, nebih.gov.hu). Nebih endorsement takes one to three working days after the vet's signature. Not all private vets in Hungary hold Nebih authorisation; confirm your chosen vet's status before booking the appointment.

        Given the tight 10-day window and the Vienna authentication step, the timing of the official vet appointment is critical on this route. Book the appointment after you have confirmed the Embassy's processing times, so you know exactly how much of the 10-day window the Vienna trip will consume. Budapest Ferenc Liszt International Airport (BUD) handles all international cargo departures.

    - heading: "Panama Import Rules at PTY"
      body: |
        MIDA inspectors at Tocumen International Airport (PTY) check the microchip, rabies vaccination record, and the authenticated health certificate. The rabies vaccination must have been given at least 30 days before arrival, and the pet must be over four months old. No titre test is required and there is no quarantine. The MIDA registration fee on arrival is approximately USD 20.

        Documents in Spanish are preferred by MIDA, though English documents are generally accepted. If your vaccination records are in Hungarian, arrange a certified Spanish or English translation before travel. Present the original authenticated health certificate; copies will not be accepted at the MIDA desk.

    - heading: "Airline Options from Budapest"
      body: |
        Copa Airlines offers cargo service to Tocumen International Airport (PTY) and is a natural choice for Panama routes given its PTY hub. Iberia via Madrid (MAD) and Air France via Paris CDG also connect Budapest to Panama City on a cargo basis. Wizz Air operates Budapest to Madrid, which can facilitate cargo transfers to Iberia at MAD.

        Cargo costs from Budapest to PTY run EUR 800-2,000 depending on animal size and crate. Add the Nebih certificate (EUR 100-250), IATA crate (EUR 60-220), Vienna authentication and courier costs (variable), and agent fees if used (EUR 250-600). Most single-pet moves from Budapest to Panama City land between EUR 1,400 and EUR 3,200 all-in.
