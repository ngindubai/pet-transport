  h1: "Pet Transport from Vietnam to Spain: EU Titre Test and Hub Routing"
  overview: |
    Spain applies EU-wide pet travel rules for third-country imports. Vietnam is not on the EU Annex II approved third-country list, requiring an FAVN or ELISA titre test at an EU-approved laboratory and a mandatory 90-day wait from the blood draw date. The Vietnam export process requires an EU AHC format health certificate from a MARD-authorised vet, endorsed by the Department of Animal Health (DAH), with no separate export permit. Total preparation: 20-28 weeks. Source: EU pet travel regulations, ec.europa.eu.

    There are no direct cargo services from Vietnam to Spain. All routings involve a European hub connection: typically Paris CDG via Air France, Amsterdam AMS via KLM, or Frankfurt FRA via Lufthansa. The hub airport is the first EU entry point, which affects which country the AHC references. For families routing via Paris, the AHC is prepared for French entry even though the final destination is Spain.

    Comparing Vietnam-to-Spain with Vietnam-to-France: the requirements are identical but France has a routing advantage. Air France operates direct cargo from SGN/HAN to CDG, and CDG is the final entry airport for France-bound cargo. For Spain, CDG is a transit point. A specialist agent can help confirm which entry point your AHC should reference for the specific routing.

  sections:
    - heading: "EU entry for Spain: the same process as France, Germany, and Netherlands"
      body: |
        All EU member states apply identical requirements for pets from Vietnam. The full process: ISO microchip (implanted before or same day as first vaccination); rabies vaccination primary course (21-day wait); FAVN or ELISA titre test at an EU-approved laboratory (result 0.5 IU/ml or above, blood draw at least 30 days after final vaccination); mandatory 90-day wait from blood draw date; EU-format AHC from MARD-authorised vet in Vietnam, endorsed by DAH, issued within 10 days of EU entry. No quarantine on arrival for compliant pets.

        Spain adds a breed restriction layer. Dogs classified under Spanish law as "perros potencialmente peligrosos" (PPP) face import restrictions. The PPP list includes American Pit Bull Terrier, Rottweiler, Dogo Argentino, Fila Brasileiro, Tosa Inu, Akita Inu, and others. If your dog is on the PPP list, confirm with Spanish authorities before travel as additional licensing may be required. This restriction applies regardless of the EU-level documentation being otherwise complete.

    - heading: "Hub routing to Spain and the EU entry point question"
      body: |
        Because no direct Vietnam-to-Spain cargo services exist, every pet moving on this route will first enter the EU at a hub airport in another EU country. The most common options are: Air France from SGN/HAN to Paris CDG, then connecting cargo to Madrid MAD or Barcelona BCN; KLM from SGN/HAN to Amsterdam AMS, then connecting to Madrid/Barcelona; Lufthansa from Vietnam to Frankfurt FRA, then connecting to Spain.

        For EU pet travel purposes, the first EU entry point is where the AHC is formally presented to border authorities. In practice, if your pet transits through a hub as checked cargo, the customs inspection may occur at the final destination (Spain). However, the AHC should technically be prepared for the first EU entry point. Confirm with your specialist agent or the receiving airline which entry point the documentation should reference for your specific routing. This is one of the complexities that makes a specialist agent valuable on this corridor.

    - heading: "Vietnam-Spain versus Vietnam-France: a routing comparison"
      body: |
        Vietnam-to-France: direct Air France cargo from SGN/HAN to CDG. One carrier, one entry point, no hub transit complications. Vietnam-to-Spain: no direct cargo from Vietnam. Hub connection required via CDG, AMS, or FRA. Additional carrier leg, EU entry point at the hub.

        The requirements are identical: same titre test, same 90-day wait, same MARD health certificate. Spain has a routing disadvantage compared to France from Vietnam. For owners specifically relocating to Spain, this is a planning factor rather than a blocker. A specialist agent familiar with Asia-to-Spain routings can manage the hub documentation effectively. For owners with a choice between France and Spain, France is the simpler routing from Vietnam.
