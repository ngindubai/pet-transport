  h1: "Pet Transport from the United States to Grenada"
  overview: |
    A three-week window. That's the specific rule Grenada's Ministry of Agriculture applies to the international health certificate: it cannot be issued more than three weeks before the pet is due to arrive. Book the vet appointment too early and the certificate is out of date by the time the flight lands; leave it too late and there's no time left to travel at all.

    Set that precise rule against a genuinely light health requirement otherwise. Microchip before vaccine, a rabies shot given 30 days to 12 months ahead, and no titre test confirmed as required in the guidance available for a US-origin pet. The two-week Pet Permit Application Form deadline runs on its own separate clock, alongside the certificate's three-week one, and both have to be built backward from the actual flight date.

    Three nonstop US carriers serve Grenada directly, American from Miami, Delta from Atlanta and JetBlue from New York, which makes this one of the better-connected new routes on this site.

  sections:
    - heading: "The three-week certificate window, and why it cuts both ways"
      body: |
        Most destinations on this site treat a health certificate's validity as a floor: issue it within so many days of travel, and earlier is generally safer. Grenada's rule works differently. The certificate has an upper bound too, three weeks before arrival, so getting the vet appointment scheduled too far ahead of the flight creates the same problem as leaving it too late.

        That makes the flight date the anchor for the whole schedule. Book it first, confirm it's not going to shift, then work the vet appointment and the permit application backward from it.

    - heading: "A permit deadline running on its own clock"
      body: |
        Separately from the certificate, the Pet Permit Application Form needs to reach the Veterinary and Livestock Division at least two weeks before arrival, alongside the health certificate and rabies certificate. These aren't the same deadline, and treating them as interchangeable is a common way this kind of route goes wrong. Track both against the confirmed flight date, not against each other.

    - heading: "Three nonstop options, no shortage of flight choice"
      body: |
        American Airlines' Miami-Grenada service runs up to seven times a week as of July 2026, the most frequent option here. Delta's Atlanta route and JetBlue's JFK route both operate year-round, giving owners real choice depending on which US city they're departing from, without needing to route through a third country first.
