  sections:
    - heading: "Georgia's NFA pet import process"
      body: |
        Pet import into the Republic of Georgia is administered by the National Food Agency (NFA) at nfa.gov.ge. For personal companion animals from Estonia, the requirements are a current rabies vaccination and a health certificate from an authorised official vet in Estonia, endorsed by the Agricultural Board. The NFA does not require a titre test for pets from EU member states, which means the preparation timeline for this route is genuinely short.

        On arrival at Tbilisi International Airport (TBS), a border veterinary officer will inspect the health certificate and vaccination records. If documents are complete and the vaccination is current, entry proceeds without quarantine. Georgia maintains a generally welcoming approach to pet travel compared to many non-EU destinations.

        Worth knowing: some airlines operating into Tbilisi have seasonal capacity limits for live animal cargo. Book your cargo slot as early as possible, particularly if you are travelling in summer or around Georgian public holidays.

    - heading: "Estonia's export certificate for non-EU destinations"
      body: |
        Estonian pet owners moving within the EU use an EU pet passport issued or updated by an authorised veterinarian. For Georgia, that document does not work at the border. An official export health certificate endorsed by the Pohllumajandus- ja Toiduamet (Agricultural Board of Estonia) at agri.ee is required.

        The process starts with an authorised official veterinarian in Estonia, who issues the certificate. The Agricultural Board then endorses it. Contact both well before your travel date to understand the current requirements and whether the Georgian NFA requires a specific certificate format. The certificate must be issued within 10 days of travel, so schedule the vet appointment accordingly.

    - heading: "Flying from Tallinn to Tbilisi"
      body: |
        Tallinn Airport (TLL) does not have a direct cargo route to Tbilisi (TBS). The most practical option is to route through Istanbul Ataturk (IST) with Turkish Airlines Cargo. airBaltic connects Tallinn to Riga and on to Frankfurt or Amsterdam, and from there onward cargo can reach Tbilisi via Turkish connections or through Frankfurt with Lufthansa.

        Georgian Airways operates between Tbilisi and some European hubs but has limited live animal cargo capacity. Check whether the specific route you want accepts cargo pets before relying on this option.

        Get written confirmation of live animal cargo acceptance on every segment. Transit handling at IST for Caucasus-bound live cargo is generally well-established, but booking early and confirming is non-negotiable on this routing.
