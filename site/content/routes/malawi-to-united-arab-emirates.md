  h1: "Pet Transport from Malawi to the United Arab Emirates"
  overview: |
    Emirates added a fourth daily Johannesburg-Dubai flight on 1 July 2026. For a Malawi pet already routing through Johannesburg on South African Airways, a fact confirmed for the UK route on this site, that extra daily capacity matters: more belly-hold space, more choice over which day to travel.

    The regulatory side asks more of Malawi than the UAE asks back. Malawi isn't on MOCCAE's roughly 50-country low-risk list, so a rabies titre test applies, though MOCCAE attaches no fixed wait once that result passes. Budget 10 to 14 weeks, most of it spent on the titre test itself rather than on flight logistics.

  sections:
    - heading: "A fourth daily flight changes the calculation"
      body: |
        Before 1 July 2026, Emirates SkyCargo ran three daily Johannesburg-Dubai services. The fourth flight, added that date, doesn't change any regulatory requirement, but it does mean less competition for cargo space on a route that also carries general freight and other airlines' pet bookings out of Johannesburg. For an owner trying to hit a specific travel window, that extra frequency is a genuine practical advantage worth checking before assuming a booking will be tight.

    - heading: "The titre test, and where the blood actually goes"
      body: |
        MOCCAE checks every origin country against its low-risk list. Malawi isn't on it, so a titre test at an accredited laboratory applies, result at least 0.5 IU/ml. There's no UK-style fixed wait once it passes. What does add real time is where the blood has to go: Malawi's own labs aren't confirmed as internationally accredited for this purpose, so the sample typically travels to South Africa, in practice usually Onderstepoort Veterinary Institute in Pretoria, the same laboratory Malawi's Australia route on this site relies on.

        That courier and processing time, not the MOCCAE rules themselves, is what sets the floor on how quickly this route can realistically move.
