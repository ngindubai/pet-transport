  overview: |
    No 90-day wait. That is the single biggest difference between sending a Uruguayan pet to the UAE and sending one to almost any EU destination covered elsewhere in this build. MOCCAE requires the titre test, since Uruguay is not on its approved low-risk list, but once the blood result comes back at 0.5 IU/ml or above, the UAE clears the pet for travel without an EU-style waiting period.

    That shortens the realistic timeline to three to five months, driven mostly by the titre test's own processing time rather than any mandatory hold afterward.

  sections:
    - heading: "Why the UAE and the EU treat the same titre test so differently"
      body: |
        Both the EU and MOCCAE require a rabies antibody titre test from pets originating in unlisted or unapproved countries, and Uruguay falls into that category for both. Where the two systems diverge is what happens after a passing result: the EU imposes a fixed 90-day wait from the blood draw date, while MOCCAE accepts the result on its own merits, valid for 365 days as long as vaccination stays current.

        For a Uruguayan pet, this means the UAE route can move considerably faster than the equivalent EU route, provided the titre test itself comes back clean and the MOCCAE import permit and export certificate are arranged in the weeks that follow.
    - heading: "No direct flight: routing via Madrid or Miami"
      body: |
        Montevideo has no direct air cargo link to Dubai or Abu Dhabi, so pets travel via a connecting hub. Madrid, reached by Iberia or Air Europa, connects onward with Emirates SkyCargo or Etihad Cargo. Miami, reached through American Airlines Cargo's strong Latin America network, offers a second workable path.

        Either way, factor in the UAE's summer heat embargo, roughly June through September, when Gulf airports restrict live animal cargo due to extreme ground temperatures. Booking outside that window avoids last-minute rescheduling.
