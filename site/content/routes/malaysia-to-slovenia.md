  overview: |
    Turkish Airlines runs a direct Kuala Lumpur to Istanbul service, and that single nonstop leg is what makes Istanbul the obvious connection point into Slovenia rather than routing through the Gulf or a Western European hub first.

    The paperwork side has a genuine surprise in it, too.   sections:
    - heading: "DVS and the Animal Passport portal"
      body: |
        Malaysia's Department of Veterinary Services manages the export process through district offices and the Animal Passport portal at animalpassport.dvs.gov.my. A DVS-registered vet prepares the paperwork, and DVS carries out a physical inspection before the certificate is issued.

        Book this appointment once the vaccination is confirmed, not the final week before travel. Processing usually takes 3 to 7 working days, but the certificate has its own validity window that needs to line up with your actual flight.

    - heading: "Why this route skips the titre test"
      body: |
        Owners moving pets out of Brazil, Indonesia or South Korea face a FAVN blood test and a fixed 90-day wait before entry into an EU country. Malaysia does not carry that requirement, because it appears on the EU's own list of low-rabies-risk third countries. The only fixed clock here is the 21-day gap between a first rabies vaccination and travel.

        This surprises some owners, who assume a longer, more complex process is standard for any non-European origin. It is worth checking your supplier's quote line by line: if a titre test is costed in for this specific route, ask why.

    - heading: "Routing into Ljubljana from Kuala Lumpur"
      body: |
        Turkish Airlines Cargo's direct KUL-Istanbul service, followed by a short onward connection, is generally the most efficient way into Slovenia. Qatar Airways Cargo via Doha is the practical alternative if Istanbul capacity is booked out on your travel dates.

        For larger dogs, ask whether your agent's routing actually terminates at Ljubljana Jože Pučnik Airport or whether it lands at a bigger hub like Vienna or Frankfurt with a road transfer to finish. Both are common on this corridor, and the answer affects how you plan collection.
