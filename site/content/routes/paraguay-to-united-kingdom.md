  h1: "Pet Transport from Paraguay to the UK"
  overview: |
    No airline flies nonstop from Asuncion to London, and Paraguay sits on Great Britain's unlisted country pathway, which means a titre test and a fixed 3-month wait sit in the middle of the timeline. Neither fact is negotiable, so a realistic plan runs closer to five months than five weeks.

    Air Europa's Asuncion-Madrid service is the only nonstop link Paraguay has to Europe, which makes Madrid the sensible transfer point onward to Heathrow rather than routing south through Brazil. SENACSA handles the Paraguayan side with a CVI issued close to departure; the UK side needs its own GB Pet Health Certificate built around the titre test result.

  sections:
    - heading: "Why the titre test sets the whole schedule"
      body: |
        Paraguay is not on GOV.UK's list of listed low-risk countries, so the shortcut available to owners moving from, say, Australia or the United States does not apply here. Every dog or cat leaving Paraguay for the UK needs a rabies antibody titre test result of 0.5 IU/ml or above, drawn at least 30 days after the rabies vaccination, at an approved laboratory.

        Once that sample passes, a 3-calendar-month wait applies before the pet can travel. That clock starts on the day the blood was taken, not the day the lab confirms the result. Miss this and you are not looking at a delay of days: you restart a three-month wait.

        Work backwards from a target flight date. Vaccination, then 30 days, then the test, then 90 days, then booking, then the final certificate. Four and a half to five months from a standing start is realistic. Owners who already hold a titre test from a prior UK-bound move, still within its rabies booster validity, can sometimes skip straight to the waiting period, which is worth checking with your vet before assuming you are starting from zero.

    - heading: "Routing: why Madrid beats the alternatives"
      body: |
        Silvio Pettirossi International Airport (ASU) has one nonstop route toward Europe: Air Europa's service to Madrid Barajas, flown several times a week on a Boeing 787. From Madrid, British Airways, Iberia and Air Europa itself all run multiple daily flights to London Heathrow, and IAG Cargo handles the live animal transfer for the group.

        LATAM's alternative, via Sao Paulo, is not wrong, but it puts the crate through Brazil, a country several export authorities flag as a higher rabies-risk transit point. That can mean extra checks on the connection even if the animal never leaves the airport. For a straightforward handover with one transfer, Madrid is the option most pet transport agents book first for this route.

  - question: "Is there a direct flight from Asuncion to London?"
    answer: "No. There is no nonstop passenger or cargo route between Silvio Pettirossi International (ASU) and any UK airport. Air Europa's nonstop Asuncion-Madrid service, connecting to a British Airways, Iberia or Air Europa flight into London Heathrow, is the most direct realistic routing. LATAM via Sao Paulo is an alternative but transits Brazil."
  - question: "What does SENACSA require before a pet can leave Paraguay?"
    answer: "SENACSA issues the Certificado Veterinario Internacional (CVI) through its Tramite 2 process for companion animals. This requires an ISO microchip, a current rabies vaccination (with a 21-day wait after a first-time vaccination), and a clinical inspection carried out within 10 days of the CVI being issued. Applications go through the SENACSA Digital portal or in person at the Certification Department. Source: SENACSA, senacsa.gov.py, viewed July 2026."
  - question: "Will a pet from Paraguay be quarantined on arrival in the UK?"
    answer: "Not if every document is correct and every deadline in the titre test and vaccination sequence was met. GB does not run routine quarantine for compliant pets. Quarantine at the owner's expense is the fallback for animals that arrive with incomplete paperwork or an invalid titre test window, which is exactly why the timeline needs to be planned months in advance."
