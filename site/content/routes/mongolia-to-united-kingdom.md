  h1: "Pet Transport from Mongolia to the United Kingdom"
  overview: |
    Landlocked between Russia and China, Mongolia has no direct passenger route to the UK, pet or otherwise. Every move starts with MIAT Mongolian Airlines' own long-haul network out of Ulaanbaatar, currently flown on Boeing 787s to Frankfurt and Istanbul, and continues from there.

    Mongolia is also unlisted under UK APHA rules, the same category as Moldova, India and China. That means a rabies titre test and a fixed 3-month wait apply on top of the routing challenge, so this is a route where the paperwork clock and the flight-booking puzzle run in parallel rather than one after the other.

    MIAT's own pet policy adds a wrinkle worth knowing early: its cargo-hold brachycephalic exclusion list runs to 18 breeds, longer than most international carriers, and includes some dogs many owners wouldn't automatically flag as flat-faced. Check your dog against it before you commit to a routing.

  sections:
    - heading: "Unlisted status and what it adds to the process"
      body: |
        UK APHA sorts countries into Part 1 (EU and EFTA states), Part 2 (a shorter list that includes the USA, Canada, Australia and Japan) and unlisted, which covers most of the rest of the world including Mongolia. Unlisted status means a rabies titre test is required: blood drawn at least 30 days after vaccination, sent to an EU or UK-approved laboratory, and a 3-calendar-month wait from the draw date before the pet can enter.

        Dogs also need praziquantel tapeworm treatment for Echinococcus multilocularis, given 24 to 120 hours before arrival and logged in the health certificate. Get the timing wrong on either the titre test or the tapeworm window and you're either waiting longer or starting the relevant step over. Cats skip the tapeworm requirement entirely.

        On the Mongolian side, GAVS (the General Authority for Veterinary Services) processes export documentation through a digital veterinary certificate system that has been in nationwide use since May 2019, a genuine modernisation compared with many countries at a similar development stage, and it means certificate processing is generally faster than the paperwork-heavy systems still common elsewhere.

    - heading: "Routing from Ulaanbaatar: no direct option exists"
      body: |
        MIAT Mongolian Airlines is the obvious starting point, running its own long-haul metal to Frankfurt and Istanbul with two 787s added to the fleet in 2023 and 2024 specifically for these routes. From either city you need an onward connection to London, ideally on the same ticket to avoid a separate cargo handover mid-journey.

        Turkish Airlines offers a genuine single-carrier alternative via Istanbul, useful because the pet stays under one airline's handling chain from Ulaanbaatar through to London rather than transferring between MIAT and a partner. Air China via Beijing is a third option; it works for this UK-bound leg, though it's worth knowing it isn't usable if your onward plans ever include the US, where Air China suspended dog transport in February 2025.

        Whichever carrier you choose, ground temperature restrictions bite on this route more than most: MIAT and Turkish Airlines both suspend hold and cargo transport outside a roughly 7C to 29C window at any airport on the itinerary, which affects both Ulaanbaatar's harsh winters and the Gulf-adjacent heat some routings pass through.

    - heading: "Breed restrictions worth checking before you book"
      body: |
        MIAT's cargo hold exclusion list is longer and broader than most carriers'. Alongside the usual Bulldog, Pug, Shih Tzu and Boxer, it names Cane Corso, Dogue de Bordeaux, Mastiff, Presa Canario and Chow Chow, breeds not every owner thinks of as brachycephalic. If your dog is on the list, cabin travel under 5kg is the only MIAT option, which rules out most medium and large dogs entirely on that carrier.

        Turkish Airlines' brachycephalic policy is narrower but stricter in one sense: flat-faced breeds are excluded from both cabin and hold, not just the hold. For an affected dog, neither of the two main carriers on this route may work, and a pet transport agent familiar with Mongolia's limited airline options is worth the fee to identify a workable alternative before you're locked into travel dates.

There is no separate export permit for personal pets beyond this vet-and-GAVS process."
