  h1: "Pet Transport from Macau to the United Arab Emirates"
  overview: |
    Cathay Pacific's Hong Kong to Dubai passenger route has been grounded since February 2026, and it isn't due back until 1 September. Cathay hasn't said anything at all about restarting a Dubai freighter service, only a Riyadh cargo rotation from 1 August. That single fact decides which carrier actually gets a Macau-origin pet to the UAE right now: Emirates SkyCargo, flying its own Hong Kong to Dubai sector, or Etihad Airways into Abu Dhabi.

    Everything else on this route runs on the usual two tracks. Macau has no long-haul cargo capacity of its own, so every pet crosses to Hong Kong first, by bridge coach or ferry, before the international leg. And Macau doesn't appear by name on MOCCAE's published list of roughly 50 rabies-controlled countries exempt from the titre test, unlike its neighbour Hong Kong. Until that changes, plan for the full blood test and the 90-day wait behind it.

  sections:
    - heading: "The titre test question MOCCAE hasn't answered directly"
      body: |
        MOCCAE's exemption list runs to around 50 countries and territories with recognised rabies control programmes: the UK, Germany, Australia, Japan, Singapore and Hong Kong among them. Macau simply isn't named on it, in any version checked against MOCCAE's own service page or the independent summaries built from it.

        That's a gap worth being honest about rather than guessing past. Nobody has published a specific ruling that treats Macau differently from mainland China here. Given that gap, the safe planning assumption is the same one this site applies to any unlisted origin: blood drawn at least 30 days after vaccination, a result of 0.5 IU/ml or higher, then a 90-day wait from a successful result before the pet can travel. Confirm directly with MOCCAE or a UAE-based agent before you commit to a shorter timeline.

    - heading: "One grounded carrier, two that still fly"
      body: |
        Cathay Pacific suspended all its Middle East passenger routes in late February 2026, following airspace disruption after a US-Israeli strike on Iran. Hong Kong to Dubai and Hong Kong to Riyadh both stopped. Riyadh cargo comes back first, from 1 August 2026, with Riyadh passenger service and the Dubai route both following on 1 September. As of this writing, Cathay has made no announcement at all about restarting a dedicated Dubai freighter rotation.

        That leaves Emirates SkyCargo carrying the weight of this route on its own metal: a Hong Kong to Dubai sector of around nine hours fifteen minutes, recently boosted by a newly converted Boeing 777-300ERSF freighter offering roughly a quarter more cargo volume than the standard 777F. Etihad Airways is the alternative into Abu Dhabi, currently five times a week on Boeing 787s and expected to build toward daily. If the pet's UAE registration sits with Abu Dhabi's ADAFSA rather than Dubai Municipality, Etihad's direct routing avoids an extra internal transfer.

    - heading: "MOCCAE's import permit and the cargo-only rule"
      body: |
        The MOCCAE import permit costs around AED 200, is applied for online, and is valid for exactly 30 days. Apply once flights are booked, not before, or the permit can lapse ahead of the confirmed travel date.

        Every pet enters the UAE as manifested cargo. There's no cabin allowance on this route regardless of how small the animal is, which is worth knowing before assuming a smaller dog or a cat can simply travel in the cabin the way it might on some European routes. Brachycephalic breeds also face Emirates SkyCargo's own seasonal embargo above 29C forecast ground temperature, broadly May through September, applied at Gulf transit points whatever the onward destination.
