content:
  h1: "Pet Transport from Uruguay to Turkey"
  overview: |
    Montevideo to Istanbul isn't a route many pet transport guides cover, and the reason is straightforward: there's no direct flight, and Turkey's own entry rules are stricter than a lot of published information suggests. A rabies antibody titre test is required from Uruguay, with a 90-day wait counted from the blood draw itself, not from when the lab emails back the result.

    That timeline, combined with a connecting flight through Sao Paulo, Buenos Aires or Madrid, makes this one of the longer preparation windows in this build. None of it is complicated in isolation. It's the sequencing, and the distance, that demands the lead time.

  sections:
    - heading: "Why Turkey's titre test rule catches people out"
      body: |
        Some pet travel sites describe Turkey's rabies titre requirement as something most pets can skip. That's not accurate for a country like Uruguay, which sits outside Turkey's short list of low-risk origins. The FAVN test has to be drawn at least 30 days after your pet's rabies vaccination, needs to clear 0.5 IU/ml, and then a full 90 days has to pass from the draw date, not the result date, before your pet can travel. Skip it, and Turkish authorities apply a 21-day quarantine at your expense on arrival.

        A booster vaccination given after the titre test still carries its own 21-day wait, even with a valid result already on file. Time the vaccination and the blood draw together at the start of the process, and don't schedule a booster close to your travel date without checking how it affects the sequence.

    - heading: "Getting from Montevideo to Istanbul"
      body: |
        Carrasco International Airport (MVD) has no direct cargo service to Istanbul, so every pet on this route makes at least one stop. Turkish Airlines itself flies Sao Paulo and Buenos Aires to Istanbul, so routing your pet through either South American hub keeps it on one carrier for the long-haul leg, rather than switching handling agents mid-journey. Iberia via Madrid is a workable alternative but means two separate cargo desks rather than one.

        MGAP, Uruguay's agriculture ministry, doesn't issue a formal export permit for a personal pet; its role is endorsing the health certificate itself, which needs to be reformatted to Turkey's model rather than the EU-style document used on Uruguay's European routes.
