# Pet Transport from the Dominican Republic to Australia

Australia and New Zealand sit at the far end of the pet import complexity scale. Australia's biosecurity rules are among the strictest in the world, and the Dominican Republic's Group 3 classification means the full set of requirements applies: an RNATT titre test, a 180-day wait from the blood draw date, a DAFF import permit, a DAFF-approved health certificate endorsed by DSA, and 10 days of mandatory post-arrival quarantine at ACDP Mickleham in Victoria.

If you are also considering a move to the EU, note that the EU route requires a 90-day wait from blood draw. Australia's equivalent wait is 180 days. That doubling, combined with the import permit and quarantine, is why this route typically takes 30 to 40 weeks from start to travel day.

## How Australia Compares to Other Destinations

| Requirement | Dominican Republic to Australia | Dominican Republic to Italy (EU) | Dominican Republic to Canada |
|---|---|---|---|
| Titre test required | Yes (RNATT) | Yes (FAVN) | No |
| Wait from blood draw | 180 days | 90 days | None |
| Import permit | Yes (DAFF) | No | No |
| Post-arrival quarantine | 10 days (ACDP) | None | None |
| Minimum prep time | 30-40 weeks | 20-26 weeks | 4-8 weeks |

Canada stands apart from both EU and Australia destinations. It requires no titre test, no waiting period, and no quarantine. If you are comparing destinations, the preparation burden differs by months.

## The 180-Day Wait: What It Means

The 180-day wait is the single biggest factor in planning this move. It begins from the date blood is drawn for the RNATT titre test, not from the date the result arrives. A common mistake is to count from the result date, which can leave you two to four weeks short of the required waiting period.

Here is the sequence:

- Microchip in place before vaccination
- Rabies vaccination administered
- Blood drawn for RNATT titre test (send to a DAFF-recognised laboratory)
- Achieve minimum 0.5 IU/ml result
- The 180-day clock runs from the blood draw date
- Travel only after 180 days from blood draw have elapsed

If the titre result is below 0.5 IU/ml, revaccinate and start again. A single failed titre test can add six or more months to your timeline.

## The DAFF Import Permit

An import permit from Australia's Department of Agriculture, Fisheries and Forestry (DAFF) is mandatory. You cannot bring a pet into Australia without one, regardless of how well everything else is prepared. Apply at agriculture.gov.au/biosecurity-trade/cats-dogs as early in the process as possible. The permit specifies the exact conditions your pet must meet, and Australian border officials verify compliance on arrival.

## Quarantine at ACDP Mickleham

All pets from Group 3 countries, including the Dominican Republic, complete 10 days of post-arrival quarantine at the Australian Centre for Disease Preparedness (ACDP) in Mickleham, Victoria. Quarantine must be pre-booked before travel, and fees apply. Do not book your flights until you have a confirmed quarantine booking.

Your pet will be collected at the airport and transported to ACDP. You collect your pet at the end of the 10-day period. DAFF publishes current quarantine fees on its website.

## Airlines and Routing

There are no direct flights from the Dominican Republic to Australia. All routings involve at least two connections and a long transit.

**Via Miami then Qantas (SDQ-MIA-SYD):** The most commonly used routing from the Caribbean. The Miami connection links to Qantas long-haul services to Sydney. Confirm live animal cargo acceptance on all legs and review welfare provisions for the total journey time.

**Via Europe then Australia (SDQ-MAD or CDG, then to SYD or MEL):** An alternative if Miami-routed space is not available. Adds connection time but can offer more scheduling flexibility.

Whichever routing you use, the journey is long. Discuss transit welfare provisions with your vet and with the airline cargo team before travel.

## DSA Export Requirements

The Departamento de Sanidad Animal (DSA) governs all animal exports from the Dominican Republic. You need an export health certificate in DAFF-approved format, prepared by a DSA-authorised veterinarian and endorsed by DSA before departure. DAFF specifies the exact format; standard Dominican export certificates are not sufficient. Work with a DSA-authorised vet who is familiar with Australian import documentation.

## Professional Help on This Route

Given the RNATT titre test logistics, the 180-day wait, the DAFF import permit, the DSA certificate process, and the multi-segment routing, this is the route where professional help from an IPATA-registered agent is most worth the cost. The failure points are numerous, and any one of them can delay your move by months.
