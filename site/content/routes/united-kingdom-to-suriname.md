  h1: "Pet Transport from United Kingdom to Suriname"
  overview: |
    Search GOV.UK's own export health certificate finder for Suriname, filtering for live animals, and it comes back with zero results. No bespoke certificate has ever been negotiated. That's a genuinely different starting point from routes like UK to Venezuela, which has its own numbered EHC, and it means a standard Official Veterinarian's certificate is the practical route in rather than a set government form.

    What waits on the Suriname side is comparatively light. No titre test applies to a UK-origin pet, since the UK is rabies-free and Suriname reserves that requirement for higher-risk countries in its published guidance. The trade-off is a strict penalty for getting it wrong: pets that don't meet the requirements on arrival face quarantine, return to the UK, or euthanasia, a harder line than most destinations covered on this site take.

  sections:
    - heading: "Filling the gap where no APHA certificate exists"
      body: |
        Most routes on this site either have a specific numbered Export Health Certificate, the way Venezuela does with EHC 2916, or a clearly documented alternative, the way Guyana's Ministry of Agriculture simply accepts a UK vet's own signed certificate. Suriname sits closer to the Guyana pattern: its own import checklist asks for endorsement by "a government veterinarian" in the country of departure, without spelling out exactly what that means for a UK-issued document.

        An Official Veterinarian's signature is the sensible default, and it's what UK exporters use for destinations without a bilateral certificate. Given how little English-language guidance Suriname publishes on this specific point, it's worth a direct check with the Directoraat Veterinaire Diensten, or a Paramaribo-based agent, before you assume the OV signature alone will clear customs without a hitch.

    - heading: "Light on tests, strict on consequences"
      body: |
        The rabies vaccination just needs to sit inside a 21-day-to-12-month window before your pet lands in Suriname. No titre test, no fixed waiting period beyond that window, and no import permit for a pet travelling with its owner. On paper this reads as one of the simpler destination-side profiles this site covers.

        What changes the calculation is what happens if something's wrong. Suriname's guidance doesn't describe a temporary penalty quarantine the way GOV.UK does for pets arriving in Great Britain without correct paperwork. It describes quarantine, return to the country of origin, or euthanasia, at the owner's cost. Advance notice to Veterinary Services, at least 48 hours ahead of landing, is part of avoiding that outcome: it's how an inspecting officer ends up actually present when your pet's flight touches down.
