  h1: "Pet Transport from the United Arab Emirates to Suriname"
  overview: |
    Flip this route around and a Suriname-origin pet needs a rabies titre test to clear MOCCAE on the way into the UAE. Coming the other way, a UAE-origin pet needs nothing of the kind. Suriname doesn't ask for a blood test from any country of origin, the UAE included, since the UAE's rabies-controlled status keeps it out of the higher-risk category Suriname reserves that requirement for.

    Light paperwork doesn't mean low stakes. Suriname's own guidance is unusually blunt about what happens if a pet arrives without everything in order: quarantine, return to the country of origin, or euthanasia, all at the owner's expense. That's a firmer line than the UAE's own document-hold approach, and it's the real reason to treat the certificate timing and the advance-notice requirement as non-negotiable, even on a route this administratively light.

  sections:
    - heading: "Suriname's light entry, and its unusually hard line if it's wrong"
      body: |
        A dog or cat vaccinated in the UAE clears Suriname's rabies rule with ease: any date between 21 days and 12 months before arrival satisfies it, no titre test involved. The health certificate has to be completed within 7 days of entry, the tightest deadline in the process, and for a UAE-origin pet that's the MOCCAE-endorsed export certificate doing double duty.

        What raises the bar here isn't the paperwork itself but the consequence of getting it wrong. Suriname's published guidance doesn't describe a temporary penalty-quarantine period the way, say, the UK does for non-compliant arrivals. It describes quarantine, return to the UAE, or euthanasia. Advance notice to Veterinary Services before the flight lands, and a certificate that's genuinely inside its 7-day window on arrival, are what keep this route in the "straightforward" category rather than becoming a real problem at the airport.

    - heading: "A 30-day export permit against a 7-day certificate window"
      body: |
        MOCCAE's own export permit is valid for 30 days from issue, comfortable runway against most connecting itineraries. Suriname's certificate window is far tighter: just 7 days from completion to arrival. In practice this means booking the flight before applying for the MOCCAE permit, then timing the final airport inspection and certificate signing close to departure so it's still valid when the pet lands in Paramaribo, not when it leaves Dubai or Abu Dhabi.

        There's no direct flight to help with that timing. Emirates or Etihad covers the Amsterdam leg out of the UAE, and Surinam Airways' nonstop Amsterdam to Paramaribo service, currently the only scheduled long-haul link into the country, finishes the journey.
