content:
  h1: "Pet Transport from Rwanda to Poland"
  overview: "Five to six months is the realistic minimum for getting a dog from Kigali into Poland with all the paperwork in order. Poland is an EU member state, and Rwanda sits outside the EU's approved country list, so the full unlisted-country sequence applies: microchip, rabies vaccination, an FAVN titre test, and a 90-day wait counted from the blood draw. There's no separate import permit and no quarantine for compliant arrivals, which keeps this route procedurally simpler than some in this batch. The main routing options run through Brussels, Nairobi, or Addis Ababa."

  sections:
    - heading: "The unlisted-country sequence: what Rwanda has to clear for Poland"
      body: |
        Poland doesn't add anything unusual to the standard EU rule set: no special import permit, no additional destination-only test. What it does require is the full sequence that applies to any pet arriving from an EU-unlisted country. Microchip first, rabies vaccination second, then a 30-day wait before blood is drawn for a titre test at an EU-approved laboratory. The result needs a minimum of 0.5 IU/ml. From the draw date, a 90-day wait applies before the pet can legally enter Poland, and that clock is the single biggest factor in the overall timeline. Get the health certificate endorsed by RAB in the 10 days before travel, and make sure the paper trail matches the EU format exactly. Poland's Chief Veterinary Inspectorate (GIW) checks documentation on arrival at Warsaw Chopin (WAW), and an EU-format certificate is non-negotiable there.

    - heading: "Getting from Kigali to Warsaw"
      body: |
        Brussels Airlines is usually the quickest option: a direct KGL-BRU leg, then an onward connection to Warsaw, benefiting from how frequently Brussels connects into Poland. KLM Cargo's joint venture with Kenya Airways runs a well-tested path via Nairobi and Amsterdam, useful if Brussels space is booked out. Ethiopian Airlines Cargo via Addis Ababa is a solid alternative, and RwandAir also serves Brussels as a second carrier on that leg. Book cargo space four to six weeks ahead where you can, and always confirm the connecting flight accepts live animal cargo before finalising the itinerary; not every partner leg does.
