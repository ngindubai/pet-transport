content:
  h1: "Pet Transport from El Salvador to the United Arab Emirates"
  overview: |
    No fixed waiting period. That's the detail that sets this route apart from El Salvador's other unlisted-country destinations. Because El Salvador doesn't appear on MOCCAE's roughly 50-country low-risk list, a rabies titre test applies, same as Great Britain or the US. But once a result of at least 0.5 IU/ml is in hand, the UAE doesn't impose a further calendar wait. The result itself stays valid for 365 days, and the online import permit runs for 90 days, so the practical schedule is set by lab turnaround, not a mandatory holding period.

    On the routing side, mid-July puts this move inside Dubai's summer cargo embargo window, when ground heat regularly forces airlines to suspend or restrict live animal cargo. That, more than any paperwork step, is worth checking before booking anything.

  sections:
    - heading: "Why this titre test moves faster than the UK or US equivalent"
      body: |
        Great Britain's non-listed pathway fixes a three calendar month wait after the blood draw. The US CDC pathway ties timing to a 28-day sample age rule and an animal care facility booking. The UAE does neither. Once the titre result clears 0.5 IU/ml, from a lab approved by El Salvador's own authorities, the result is good for 365 days provided vaccination stays current, and the online MOCCAE import permit is valid for 90 days from issue. There's no artificial delay built in beyond what the vaccination and lab process itself requires.

        That doesn't make the route effortless. If this is the pet's first rabies vaccination, or a lapsed one, a 21-day gap still has to pass before the blood draw. A pet already on a maintained booster schedule skips that wait entirely.

    - heading: "The summer heat embargo, and why the date matters here"
      body: |
        Roughly from June to September, ground temperatures at Dubai's airports climb high enough that most carriers suspend or heavily restrict pet cargo. Emirates SkyCargo, which handles the Miami-Dubai sector on this route, is one of the airlines affected. Since this route is being planned in mid-July, that window is currently active. Confirm directly with the carrier before assuming a booking will go through as normal; a September or October departure sidesteps the issue entirely.

    - heading: "Getting from San Salvador to the UAE"
      body: |
        Avianca and American Airlines both fly San Salvador to Miami nonstop, and Emirates covers Miami to Dubai nonstop year-round outside the summer restriction period. For a dog, American's own embargo on CDC high-risk country cargo bookings can complicate that first leg even though the flight never touches US soil as a final destination, so Avianca is worth checking first. Cats face none of this complication.
