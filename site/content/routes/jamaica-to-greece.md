content:
  h1: "Pet Transport from Jamaica to Greece: 2026 Guide"
  overview: |
    Two Caribbean islands, one shared destination, and completely different paperwork: unlike the Dominican Republic, Jamaica's pets skip the titre test entirely. Jamaica is listed as EU Group 2 under Implementing Regulation (EU) 2024/1130, so Greece treats a compliant Jamaican pet much like it would a pet from any other approved third country. A current rabies vaccination and a VSD-endorsed EU-format health certificate are the two documents that matter.

    That single difference cuts three to four months off the timeline compared with an unlisted country on the same regional route. Six to ten weeks is realistic from a standing start, most of it taken up by booking cargo space rather than waiting on any mandatory rabies test result.

    The journey itself runs through London Heathrow. British Airways carries the pet as cargo from Kingston or Montego Bay, and Aegean Airlines picks up the connection into Athens once the transatlantic leg is done.

  sections:
    - heading: "Why Jamaica does not need the titre test that the Dominican Republic does"
      body: |
        The EU assesses each non-member country's rabies control system and either lists it or leaves it unlisted for pet travel purposes. Jamaica has been granted EU Group 2 status under Implementing Regulation (EU) 2024/1130, which places it alongside a small number of other approved third countries. That listing removes the FAVN titre test requirement altogether.

        This is worth stating plainly because older guidance on this subject, including some earlier pages on this site, incorrectly treated Jamaica as an unlisted country requiring the full test and 90-day wait. That was wrong. The current, corrected position is that a Jamaican pet with a current rabies vaccination and a properly endorsed VSD health certificate meets Greece's entry requirements without a titre test.

    - heading: "The VSD certificate and the route via London Heathrow"
      body: |
        Jamaica's Veterinary Services Division authorises the vets who can issue the export health certificate, and endorses it before departure. The certificate is only valid for 10 days, so book the vet appointment once your flight is confirmed rather than weeks ahead of a tentative date.

        British Airways carries pet cargo from Kingston's Norman Manley International or Montego Bay's Sangster International through to London Heathrow. From there, Aegean Airlines or a partner carrier completes the journey into Athens. Confirm both legs' cargo booking together, since a mismatch in connection timing at Heathrow is the most common cause of delay on this route.
