  sections:
    - heading: "The dual paperwork: USDA endorsement plus MOCCAE permit"
      body: |
        This route has documentation on both ends. On the US side, your pet needs a health certificate completed by a USDA-accredited veterinarian and endorsed by USDA APHIS. On the UAE side, you need an import permit from MOCCAE (the Ministry of Climate Change and Environment) before the pet departs.

        The USDA endorsement step is the one most likely to cause timing stress. The examination must happen within 10 days of travel, but the endorsement itself takes 2-10 business days. Electronic submission through VEHCS is faster than mail. Coordinate the vet examination, USDA endorsement, and travel date carefully so the certificate is endorsed and in hand before departure.

        The MOCCAE permit number must appear on the health certificate, so apply for the permit before the final certificate is issued.

    - heading: "Summer heat embargoes are a real constraint"
      body: |
        UAE summers are extreme, with temperatures regularly exceeding 40°C from June through September. Airlines impose heat embargoes restricting live animal cargo during these months. Emirates, Etihad and Qatar Airways all apply seasonal restrictions.

        Brachycephalic breeds face the strictest conditions and may be refused carriage entirely in peak summer. If your move is unavoidably during summer, options include very early morning departures when ground temperatures are lower, or routing decisions that minimise heat exposure. Discuss this with your airline or agent before committing to a travel date.

faqs:
  - question: "Does my pet need to quarantine on arrival in the UAE from the USA?"
    answer: "No. The UAE does not require quarantine for pets arriving from the USA, provided all documentation is correct: microchip, current rabies vaccination, MOCCAE import permit, and the USDA-endorsed health certificate. Your pet is released after customs inspection at Dubai or Abu Dhabi, typically within 1-2 hours."
  - question: "How long does the USDA APHIS endorsement take?"
    answer: "Electronic submission through VEHCS typically processes in 2-5 business days. Mail submission takes 5-10 business days. Since the health certificate examination must happen within 10 days of travel, time the endorsement carefully. Most owners schedule the vet examination 7-8 days before travel and submit immediately via VEHCS to ensure the endorsed certificate is ready before departure."
  - question: "Can I bring my pet back to the USA later if I'm only in the UAE temporarily?"
    answer: "Yes, but be aware the UAE is classified as a CDC high-risk country for dog rabies. Bringing a dog from the UAE back to the USA involves a more complex process: either a valid rabies titre test from a CDC-approved laboratory, or a 28-day stay at a CDC-registered facility on arrival. If your stay is temporary, plan the return process in advance. Cats face no such requirements in either direction."
  - question: "Which US airports can my pet depart from for the UAE?"
    answer: "Emirates SkyCargo operates from multiple US hubs including New York (JFK), Los Angeles, Chicago, Houston, Dallas, Washington and others. Etihad serves New York and Chicago to Abu Dhabi. Qatar Airways offers connections via Doha from many US cities. Your departure airport depends on the carrier and routing; an agent can advise on the best option from your location."
