content:
  h1: "Pet Transport Australia to Uruguay"
  overview: |
    Qantas flies direct from Sydney to Santiago, one of the only genuine Australia-South America non-stop routes in existence, which makes this corridor better positioned than it might first appear. From Santiago, LATAM Cargo picks up the connection into Montevideo.

    Uruguay's own requirements follow a familiar regional pattern: no rabies titre test, but a mandatory MGAP import permit, plus a specific praziquantel-based echinococcus treatment for dogs, timed to a narrow window before arrival.

  sections:
    - heading: "Why the Sydney-Santiago route matters"
      body: |
        Direct flights between Australia and South America are rare, and Qantas's Sydney-Santiago service is one of the few that exists. That single leg removes what would otherwise be a US-routed detour, shortening the overall journey and reducing the number of separate cargo bookings needed. From Santiago, LATAM Cargo's onward network into Montevideo is well established.

    - heading: "The echinococcus treatment and MGAP's permit"
      body: |
        Dogs need a praziquantel-based echinococcus treatment, given between 72 hours and 30 days before arrival, a step cats don't require. Separately, Uruguay's MGAP-DGSG requires an import permit before a pet can enter, a genuine step that some neighbouring countries, Panama included, don't ask for. Apply for it well before the trip; it isn't a same-week process.

    - heading: "DAFF's export process, unchanged by the destination"
      body: |
        A DAFF-accredited vet examination and health certificate complete the Australian side, issued within 10 days of travel to match Uruguay's own certificate window. No separate export permit is required for a personal pet leaving Australia.
