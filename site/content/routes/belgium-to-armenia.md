  h1: "Pet Transport from Belgium to Armenia"
  overview: |
    Belgium to Armenia is one of the more straightforward international pet moves. Belgium is an EU member state with a controlled rabies programme, and Armenia accepts EU health certificates without requiring a titre test. The preparation is largely administrative: a health check with an FASFC-authorised vet, an EU-format certificate, and cargo booking from Brussels.

    Allow two to four weeks from start to travel date. The timeline is mostly determined by certificate endorsement processing and cargo booking lead times rather than medical preparation.

  sections:
    - heading: "What Armenia requires for EU-origin pets"
      body: |
        Armenia's import rules for personal companion pets from EU countries are relatively straightforward. The key documents are a valid microchip, current rabies vaccination, and an official health certificate endorsed by FASFC. There is no titre test requirement for pets arriving from Belgium or other EU member states.

        The SSFS (State Service for Food Safety) is the competent authority in Armenia. On arrival at Zvartnots International Airport (EVN), SSFS vets inspect the health certificate and check the pet's condition. Provided everything is in order, the pet clears customs without quarantine.

        Armenia's rules are confirmed at ssfs.am. As with any international move, regulations can change. Verify requirements with the Armenian Embassy in Brussels or with an IPATA-registered agent before travel.

    - heading: "Cargo options from Brussels to Yerevan"
      body: |
        There is no direct cargo service from Brussels to Yerevan. All routings involve a hub connection, typically Istanbul (IST) or one of the Gulf airports (Dubai or Doha).

        Turkish Airlines Cargo is the most straightforward option for this route. BRU to IST is a regular service, and Turkish Airlines operates from IST to EVN with cargo facilities for live animals. The IST connection is usually manageable in terms of transit times, and Turkish Airlines Cargo is experienced on Caucasus routes.

        Emirates SkyCargo via Dubai is an alternative, with the BRU-DXB-EVN routing. This is a longer total journey but Emirates has strong pet cargo infrastructure at DXB. The same applies to Qatar Cargo via Doha.

        For very small pets, Wizz Air operates from Brussels (both BRU and CRL) to Yerevan and accepts small animals in the cabin. This keeps your pet with you throughout the journey. Confirm weight limits (typically under 8kg including carrier) and book the cabin pet space in advance, as availability is limited.

    - heading: "FASFC endorsement: the Belgium-specific step"
      body: |
        Belgian export health certificates must be endorsed by FASFC (Federal Agency for the Safety of the Food Chain, fasfc.be). This is the official endorsement that validates the certificate for international travel.

        The endorsement process involves an FASFC-authorised vet completing the EU-format health certificate after a pre-travel examination, then submitting it to the relevant FASFC provincial inspection office for endorsement. Allow one to two working days for the endorsement process, and start this within the 10-day validity window of the certificate.

        The certificate must be issued within 10 days of travel and be endorsed by FASFC before the pet travels. If you are unsure which FASFC office covers your area, fasfc.be lists provincial contact details.
