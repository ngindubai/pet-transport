content:
  h1: "Pet Transport from Lebanon to Austria"
  overview: "Budget six months, not six weeks, for a compliant move from Beirut to Vienna. Lebanon sits outside the EU's list of approved rabies-free or controlled countries, so Austria requires the full unlisted-country sequence: microchip, vaccination, an FAVN or ELISA titre test, and a 90-day wait counted from the blood draw. Add Lebanese processing time for the export endorsement and the realistic window runs 20 to 26 weeks. Cats and dogs both go through the identical process; there is no shortcut for either species."

  sections:
    - heading: "The titre test and 90-day wait, in order"
      body: |
        Nothing about this sequence can be skipped or reordered. Your vet implants the microchip first. Only once that's confirmed on a scanner does the rabies vaccination go in. Wait 30 days, then a blood sample goes to an EU-approved laboratory for an FAVN or ELISA titre test, which needs to come back at 0.5 IU/ml or above.

        From the day that blood is drawn, a 90-day clock starts. Austria will not admit the pet before that period ends, regardless of how quickly the lab returns results. Owners who assume the countdown starts on result day routinely lose weeks off their plan. Assume the draw date is the only date that matters and build backwards from there.

        Once the 90 days pass, the last document is the EU-format Animal Health Certificate, valid for just 10 days, so timing its issue close to the actual travel date is essential.

    - heading: "Getting the Lebanese paperwork endorsed"
      body: |
        Lebanon requires an export permit and an official health certificate from the Ministry of Agriculture before any pet can leave. Both need to be arranged through a vet authorised to deal with the Ministry, and the certificate has to be reformatted to EU standard for entry into Austria.

        This is the step owners underestimate most. Lebanese government processing does not move at the pace of the biological timeline. Two to four weeks is a fair estimate for the endorsement alone, and that clock only starts once the 90-day wait is nearly finished, since the certificate is only valid for 10 days before travel.

    - heading: "Flight options from Beirut to Vienna"
      body: |
        There's no nonstop BEY-VIE cargo service, so every pet on this route connects through a hub. Turkish Airlines Cargo via Istanbul offers the widest choice of onward connections and is usually the first quote worth getting. Lufthansa Cargo via Frankfurt is a strong second, particularly given Frankfurt's dedicated Animal Lounge for the transit. Emirates via Dubai and Qatar Airways via Doha both work but add hours to the journey, and Gulf carriers can suspend live animal acceptance during the hottest summer weeks, so check embargo dates if you're travelling in July or August.

    answer: "Plan for 20 to 26 weeks. The titre test process alone needs at least 120 days (30 days after vaccination before the blood draw, then 90 more days after the draw), and Lebanese Ministry of Agriculture endorsement adds another 2 to 4 weeks on top."
  - question: "Does the 90-day wait start when I get the titre test result?"
    answer: "No. It starts on the date the blood was drawn, regardless of how long the laboratory takes to issue the result. This is the single most common scheduling error on unlisted-country routes."
