  h1: "Pet Transport from the United Kingdom to Uganda"
  overview: |
    UK to Uganda requires an Animal Health Certificate from a UK Official Vet and an import permit from Uganda's Ministry of Agriculture, Animal Industry and Fisheries (MAAIF). There is no titre test required by Uganda for UK-origin pets, keeping the timeline to around 6-10 weeks.

    British Airways operates direct London Heathrow to Entebbe service and BA World Cargo handles pets on this route. The direct connection makes this one of the more straightforward UK-Africa cargo routes. Qatar Airways via Doha and Emirates via Dubai are reliable alternatives if BA cargo space is full.

    The MAAIF import permit is the main planning constraint. Start this process as early as possible, as administrative processing in Uganda can be variable.
  sections:
    - heading: "Uganda import requirements: permit, inspection, and documentation"
      body: |
        Uganda's Ministry of Agriculture, Animal Industry and Fisheries (MAAIF) is the authority for pet imports. Your pet needs a MAAIF import permit obtained before travel, a current ISO microchip, valid rabies vaccination, and a UK Animal Health Certificate.

        The import permit specifies the conditions under which your pet enters Uganda. It must be applied for before booking your flight and carried in original form with the other documentation. Allow 4-6 weeks for MAAIF processing, with some buffer for administrative delays.

        On arrival at Entebbe International Airport, a MAAIF vet inspects your pet. With all documents correct, there is no extended quarantine. Incomplete or missing documents may result in your pet being held at the airport.
    - heading: "British Airways direct to Entebbe: the strongest option for UK-Uganda pet moves"
      body: |
        British Airways flies London Heathrow (LHR) to Entebbe (EBB) directly, and BA World Cargo handles pet shipments on this route. For UK owners, this direct routing is the cleanest option: one aircraft, no hub handling, no connection risk.

        Book BA cargo space 4-6 weeks before travel. BA cargo capacity for live animals is limited and fills during peak periods (summer and pre-Christmas). Confirm current BA World Cargo acceptance policies before booking - restrictions on certain breeds and seasons apply.

        Qatar Airways via Doha and Emirates via Dubai both offer multiple departures daily from Heathrow to East Africa. These add connection time but have well-established pet cargo operations and are good fallbacks if BA direct is full or unavailable on your dates.
faqs:
  - question: "Does my pet need a titre test to travel from the UK to Uganda?"
    answer: "No. Uganda does not currently require a titre test for pets from the United Kingdom. You need a current rabies vaccination, ISO microchip, UK Animal Health Certificate, and a MAAIF import permit. Confirm current requirements with MAAIF or an IPATA agent before finalising documentation."
  - question: "How do I get the Uganda MAAIF import permit?"
    answer: "Apply to Uganda's Ministry of Agriculture, Animal Industry and Fisheries (MAAIF). Allow 4-6 weeks for processing. An IPATA-registered agent with East Africa experience can manage the application and follow up with MAAIF on your behalf. The permit must be in hand before your pet travels."
  - question: "Is there a direct flight from the UK to Uganda for pet cargo?"
    answer: "Yes. British Airways operates direct London Heathrow to Entebbe (EBB) service and BA World Cargo accepts pet shipments. Qatar Airways via Doha and Emirates via Dubai are the main alternatives if BA direct cargo space is unavailable."
  - question: "What is the UK Animal Health Certificate and do I need it for Uganda?"
    answer: "Yes. The AHC is issued by an APHA-authorised UK Official Vet and is required for pets leaving the UK. It is valid for 10 days from the examination date. Uganda may have specific requirements about the contents or endorsement of the AHC - confirm these with MAAIF or an IPATA agent before your OV appointment."
  - question: "What happens when my pet arrives at Entebbe?"
    answer: "A MAAIF (Ministry of Agriculture, Animal Industry and Fisheries) veterinarian will inspect your pet on arrival. They will verify the import permit, Animal Health Certificate, microchip, and vaccination records. With correct documentation, there is no extended quarantine. Bring originals of all documents."
