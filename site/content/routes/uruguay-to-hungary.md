  overview: "Uruguay to Hungary is a long-distance relocation requiring both a long lead time and multiple cargo legs.   sections:
    - heading: "Nébih's Requirements for Uruguay-Origin Pets"
      body: |
        Hungary's National Food Chain Safety Office (Nébih, nebih.gov.hu) applies EU non-commercial pet movement rules to all arriving animals. Uruguay is an unlisted third country, so the full titre test pathway applies.

        At Budapest (BUD) cargo terminal, Nébih checks:

        Microchip: ISO 11784/11785, implanted before vaccination. Nébih scans it and cross-references with the health certificate.

        Rabies vaccination: Current and post-microchip. At least 21 days elapsed since primary vaccination.

        FAVN titre test: Result 0.5 IU/ml or above, blood drawn at least 30 days after vaccination, from an EU-approved laboratory. A minimum of 90 days must have elapsed from the blood draw date to the arrival date at BUD.

        Health certificate: EU Annex IV non-commercial format, DGSG-endorsed, issued within 10 days of travel. All titre test details must appear.

        No quarantine. No import permit. Nébih is precise in checking the 90-day calculation.

    - heading: "MGAP-DGSG: Uruguay's Export Authority"
      body: |
        Uruguay's Ministerio de Ganadería, Agricultura y Pesca (MGAP, mgap.gub.uy) manages companion animal export certification through the Dirección General de Servicios Ganaderos (DGSG). The process is similar to other South American countries with EU-standard health certificate requirements.

        A MGAP-registered veterinarian prepares the EU Annex IV health certificate template, incorporating all vaccination, microchip, and titre test details. DGSG endorses the document, typically within three to five business days.

        The certificate must be issued within 10 days of travel. Plan the DGSG appointment carefully within the 10-day window, allowing buffer time for any processing delays at the ministry.

    - heading: "Routing from Montevideo to Budapest"
      body: |
        Budapest is not served directly from Uruguay. The journey requires two or three cargo legs.

        LATAM Airlines from MVD to São Paulo (GRU) is the most common first leg. GRU then connects to European hubs via LATAM's partner network or other transatlantic carriers. Lufthansa and Air France both operate GRU-FRA and GRU-CDG. From FRA or CDG, the final leg to BUD is short.

        Iberia is a strong alternative, particularly for the transatlantic leg. From EZE (Buenos Aires) or via MVD direct, Iberia flies to MAD. From MAD, onward cargo to BUD is arranged via Iberia, Vueling, or LOT Polish Airlines connections.

        KLM via Amsterdam is another well-trodden path. GRU to AMS on KLM Cargo, then AMS to BUD. Amsterdam's live animal handling facility is reliable, and the AMS-BUD sector is served daily.

        Lufthansa via Frankfurt is often preferred for larger animals. GRU or EZE to FRA, then FRA to BUD. Frankfurt has one of Europe's best dedicated live animal facilities.

    - heading: "Hungarian Breed Legislation: What to Know Before You Move"
      body: |
        Hungary's breed-specific dog legislation is enacted under Government Decree 35/1997 (as subsequently amended). It imposes specific conditions on the keeping of certain breeds, notably those classified as potentially dangerous due to size, morphology, or historical behavioural characteristics.

        Breeds typically affected include: American Staffordshire Terrier, Bull Terrier, Dobermann (over a certain size), Rottweiler, and some others. The specific list and its current application should be verified with Nébih (nebih.gov.hu) or a Hungarian lawyer before travel if your dog is or might resemble one of these breeds.

        The legislation does not prevent import but does require specific steps once in Hungary: registration with authorities, compulsory muzzle and lead in public spaces, and third-party liability insurance. These are manageable but should not come as a surprise on arrival.

    - heading: "Arriving in Budapest with Your Pet"
      body: |
        Budapest Ferenc Liszt International Airport cargo terminal is the arrival point for international pet cargo. Nébih inspectors are stationed at the cargo terminal for border veterinary checks.

        After Nébih clears the animal and documentation, you collect the pet from the cargo terminal. You will need your ID, the cargo airway bill, and the freight forwarder's release paperwork. Allow two to four hours from the aircraft arrival time.

        Hungary requires annual rabies vaccination for all dogs and cats. Register with a local vet (állatorvos) shortly after arrival, and arrange the local registration if your municipality requires it.
