# Pet Transport from the Dominican Republic to Canada

The Caribbean to Canada move is one of the more manageable international pet relocations. Canada does not require a titre test, does not impose quarantine, and has a clear rabies vaccination requirement that most pet owners can meet within a few weeks. If your dog or cat is up to date with vaccinations and you allow enough time for the Dominican Republic export paperwork, this route is well within reach.

## What Makes This Route Manageable

| Factor | Dominican Republic to Canada | Dominican Republic to EU |
|---|---|---|
| Titre test required | No | Yes (FAVN, at approved lab) |
| Wait after titre test | None | 90 days minimum |
| Quarantine on arrival | None | None (if compliant) |
| Preparation time | 4-8 weeks | 20-26 weeks |
| Certificate endorsed by | DSA | DSA |

Canada's entry rules, governed by the Canadian Food Inspection Agency (CFIA), require a current rabies vaccination certificate for dogs over three months old. The certificate must be in English or French. Most Dominican veterinarians issue in Spanish, so arrange a certified translation before you travel.

## The Dominican Republic Export Process

The Departamento de Sanidad Animal (DSA), part of the Dominican Ministry of Agriculture, controls all animal exports. You need:

- An export health certificate prepared by a DSA-authorised veterinarian
- Prior notification to, or an export permit from, DSA
- Official DSA endorsement on the health certificate before departure

The endorsement step takes several working days. Do not book your flights until the certificate is endorsed; an unendorsed document will not satisfy CFIA officers on arrival in Canada.

Microchipping to ISO 11784/11785 standard is strongly recommended. Canada does not make it a legal condition for personal pets from all countries, but airlines often require it for cargo transport.

## Getting There: Airlines and Routing

The most practical routing is SDQ to Miami (MIA), then MIA to Toronto Pearson (YYZ) with American Airlines. American Airlines Cargo handles live animal shipments and operates this route regularly.

**Air Canada** offers connections via Miami or Newark (EWR) into Toronto, Vancouver, and Montreal.

**Copa Airlines** via Panama City (PTY) is an alternative if direct Miami connections do not fit your dates.

Before booking, confirm two things with your chosen airline:

1. Live animal carriage is approved on every segment, not just the first leg.
2. Your pet's crate meets IATA container requirements for that specific aircraft type.

Book your pet's place at least 4 to 6 weeks ahead, especially in summer when cargo space fills quickly.

## On Arrival in Canada

CBSA and CFIA officers at the port of entry review your rabies vaccination certificate and health certificate. There is no quarantine. The process is quick provided documents are in order and the rabies certificate is in English or French. Complete the CFIA animal declaration form before or on arrival.

## Cost Overview

Costs on this route typically include DSA certificate and endorsement fees, DSA export permit or notification fee, airline cargo fees on two segments (SDQ-MIA and MIA-YYZ), an IATA-compliant crate, certified translation if the certificate is in Spanish only, and professional relocation agent fees if you use one.

Allow 4 to 8 weeks from starting paperwork to departure. With the right preparation, this is one of the more straightforward long-haul pet moves from the Caribbean.
