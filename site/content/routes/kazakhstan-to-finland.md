  sections:
    - heading: "The Finnair advantage on this corridor"
      body: |
        Most Caucasus and Central Asia-to-Finland pet moves require at least two cargo segments: first to a major European hub (Istanbul, Frankfurt, Amsterdam), then onto Helsinki. Kazakhstan-to-Finland has a potential single-segment option via Finnair's Almaty-Helsinki (ALA-HEL) service.

        Whether Finnair Cargo accepts live animals on this specific route needs to be confirmed directly with them, as live animal cargo policies are route-specific and can change seasonally. If confirmed, this gives a significantly simpler cargo journey. The pet goes straight from Almaty to Helsinki Vantaa, which is exactly the border inspection post needed for non-EU origin clearance.

        If Finnair Cargo is not an option, the Turkish Airlines routing via Istanbul remains the most practical alternative. IST to HEL is a well-served route and Turkish Airlines Cargo has live animal acceptance at IST.

    - heading: "Finland's requirement and what it means in cargo terms"
      body: |
        Ruokavirasto restricts non-EU origin pet arrivals to two entry points: Helsinki Vantaa Airport (HEL) and the Vaalimaa land border with Russia. For a Kazakhstani pet, HEL is the practical destination.

        The cargo booking must explicitly confirm that Helsinki Vantaa is the final destination and the border inspection post. A pet arriving at any other Finnish airport from Kazakhstan cannot be cleared. Make sure your cargo confirmation documents state HEL as the terminal point and that the airline has confirmed live animal handling at HEL.

        Ruokavirasto border vets at HEL are experienced with unlisted country arrivals. Present all documents in a clear order: health certificate (within 10 days), FAVN titre test result (blood draw date and 0.5 IU/ml or above), vaccination record (dated after microchip and at least 21 days before travel), and microchip number documentation.
