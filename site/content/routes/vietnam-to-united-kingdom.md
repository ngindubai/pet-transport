  overview: |
    Vietnam is not on the GB listed country schedule, which means the standard non-listed country process applies: a mandatory titre test, a three-month waiting period from the blood draw date, an APHA Import Licence, and a DAGAH-endorsed GB Pet Health Certificate. Allow 22-28 weeks from start to travel day.

    Vietnam Airlines, Qatar Airways, and Cathay Pacific all serve the Vietnam to London Heathrow corridor via connections. There are no direct Vietnam-UK cargo services, so your pet will transit via a hub. Vietnam operates two international airports with regular connections to UK routing: Hanoi Noi Bai (HAN) and Ho Chi Minh City Tan Son Nhat (SGN). The DAGAH (Department of Animal Health) under MARD is the Vietnamese authority for pet exports.

    - heading: "DAGAH export documentation: what the health certificate needs to cover"
      body: |
        The DAGAH (Department of Animal Health) is responsible for pet export health certificates from Vietnam. The certificate must be issued by a DAGAH-authorised vet and endorsed by the DAGAH or MARD as Vietnam's official competent authority.

        For GB entry from Vietnam, the certificate must record: the ISO microchip number and implantation date (confirming it pre-dates the rabies vaccination), the complete vaccination history, the titre test result (date, laboratory, and result value), and a statement that the pet was examined and found healthy within 10 days of the planned GB arrival date.

        The 10-day validity window runs from the examination date to the GB arrival date. Plan the final vet visit around the flight booking. For dogs, the tapeworm treatment (praziquantel, 24-120 hours before GB arrival) must also be recorded in the certificate.
