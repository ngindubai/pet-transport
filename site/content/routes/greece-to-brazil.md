    Moving a pet from Greece to Brazil is manageable for an international long-haul route. A Hellenic Ministry of Rural Development and Food-issued official health certificate, a current rabies vaccination, and a microchip are the core requirements for Brazil entry. VIGIAGRO (Brazil's agricultural border inspection service) inspects pets on arrival but there is no routine quarantine for compliant pets. Emirates via Dubai, Air France via Paris, and KLM via Amsterdam all offer viable cargo routing from Athens to São Paulo.

    - heading: "VIGIAGRO arrival process in Brazil"
      body: |
        VIGIAGRO is Brazil's agricultural border inspection service, operating under MAPA (Ministry of Agriculture, Livestock and Food Supply). Pets arriving from Greece are inspected at the border control point at São Paulo Guarulhos (GRU) or Rio de Janeiro Galeão (GIG).

        The inspection verifies the health certificate, microchip, and vaccination records. For EU-origin pets with complete documentation, the process is a documentary check rather than a quarantine hold. The health certificate must be issued by a Hellenic Ministry-authorised official vet and must meet MAPA's current format requirements for imported pets.

        Contact MAPA/VIGIAGRO (gov.br/mapa) for the current health certificate template and entry documentation required from Greece.
