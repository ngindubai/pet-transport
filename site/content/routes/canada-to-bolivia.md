content:
  h1: "Pet Transport from Canada to Bolivia"
  overview: |
    Ottawa is where the Bolivian Embassy handles legalisation for this route, and its own published turnaround, once your paperwork lands, is 1 to 2 business days. That's a noticeably faster embassy step than the equivalent US-to-Bolivia route, where owners describe the postal round trip taking one to three weeks through the Washington DC mission.

    Beyond the embassy step, this move follows the pattern most other Canada-outbound Bolivia routes share: no titre test, a current rabies vaccination, and a SENASAG import permit secured before the flight. The one constraint that doesn't bend is the flight itself, since nothing links Canada and Bolivia nonstop.

  sections:
    - heading: "A quick embassy, a slow postal system"
      body: |
        The Bolivian Embassy in Ottawa doesn't ask for an in-person appointment for this kind of document work. Everything goes by post, a money order rather than a card payment, and a prepaid, tracked return envelope you need to remember to include. Once the embassy has your documents in hand, the stated processing time is short, one to two business days.

        What that quick figure doesn't cover is Canada Post's own transit time each way. Build in a realistic mailing window either side of that 1-2 day figure, particularly if you're posting from outside the National Capital Region.

    - heading: "What Bolivia does, and doesn't, ask for"
      body: |
        No rabies titre test, from any origin country, Canada included. That single fact keeps this route out of the "very high complexity" bracket that non-approved-country destinations in this build tend to sit in. What SENASAG does insist on is the import permit itself, granted before departure, and a certificate built to match requirements confirmed directly with SENASAG rather than assumed from general guidance.

    - heading: "Getting to Santa Cruz"
      body: |
        Copa Airlines through its Panama City hub is the standard connection from Toronto or Montreal into Santa Cruz Viru Viru. A Miami-routed itinerary via Boliviana de Aviación's own service is a workable alternative for travellers whose nearest US gateway makes that connection easier, though BoA's brachycephalic exclusion applies regardless of which routing is chosen.
