  sections:
    - heading: "EU pet passport or AHC: which document do you use?"
      body: |
        This is the question most owners from Romania get stuck on. The short answer depends on when your pet's passport was issued.

        If your dog or cat has an EU pet passport issued in Romania and all vaccinations are current and correctly recorded in it, you can still use that passport to enter Great Britain. The post-Brexit rules allow continued use of existing EU pet passports for travel into GB. What you cannot do is get a new EU pet passport today and use it for GB travel, because new passports are no longer recognised.

        If your pet's passport is out of date, incomplete, or was never issued, you need an Animal Health Certificate. The AHC is issued by an ANSVSA-authorised official vet in Romania and is valid for 10 days from the date of issue for GB entry. It is also valid for 4 months for onward EU travel if needed.

        Most owners taking pets to the UK now use the AHC, as it is straightforward to arrange through official vets in Bucharest and other major Romanian cities.

    - heading: "The tapeworm window: get the timing right"
      body: |
        The tapeworm rule catches more people out than any other requirement on EU-to-UK moves. For dogs (not cats), Praziquantel must be administered by a vet between 24 and 120 hours before the scheduled arrival time in Great Britain. Not departure time, not check-in time, the time you expect to arrive in GB.

        The treatment must be recorded with the date and time in your AHC or EU pet passport, signed by the vet. If treatment was given 121 hours before arrival, it does not count. If given within 24 hours of arrival, it does not count.

        Plan your vet appointment backwards from your expected arrival time in London. Most cargo shipments from OTP to LHR take around 24-36 hours door to door including kennel time. Work the tapeworm appointment into that window carefully, and confirm the timing with the vet who issues your AHC.
