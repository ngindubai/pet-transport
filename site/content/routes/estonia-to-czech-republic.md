  sections:
    - heading: "Czech Republic Entry Requirements for Pets from Estonia"
      body: |
        The Czech Republic follows EU Regulation 576/2013 for non-commercial pet movements. Dogs, cats, and ferrets arriving from Estonia need a valid EU pet passport, an ISO 11784/11785 microchip implanted before or on the date of the first rabies vaccination, and a rabies vaccination that is both current and at least 21 days old following a primary course. Boosters given within the valid period of a previous vaccination carry no waiting period. No titre test is required, as Estonia is a listed EU country. The State Veterinary Administration (SVA, svscr.cz) oversees pet import rules in the Czech Republic and border checks at Prague Vaclav Havel Airport (PRG).

    - heading: "Flights from Tallinn to Prague: Routing Your Pet"
      body: |
        There is no direct flight between Tallinn (TLL) and Prague (PRG), so your pet will travel via a connecting hub. LOT Polish Airlines via Warsaw (WAW) is a well-used option for this corridor, with cargo facilities at Warsaw Chopin Airport. Lufthansa via Frankfurt (FRA) offers Lufthansa Cargo's live animal handling at one of Europe's busiest cargo hubs. airBaltic connects via Riga (RIX) with onward European connections. For small pets travelling in cabin, check that the airline permits in-cabin pets through the full itinerary including the connection. Prague has a growing pet relocation community, so specialist agents familiar with TLL-PRG routing are available if you prefer not to coordinate the hub connection yourself.

faqs:
  - question: "Is the EU pet passport enough to bring my pet from Estonia to Czech Republic?"
    answer: "Yes, provided it is correctly completed. The EU pet passport must show your pet's microchip number, the date the chip was implanted, and a current rabies vaccination recorded after the microchip was in place. The vaccination must be at least 21 days old if it was a primary course. No additional health certificate or import permit is required for this intra-EU move."
  - question: "Are there direct flights from Tallinn to Prague for pets?"
    answer: "No direct flights operate between TLL and PRG. Common routing options include Lufthansa via Frankfurt, LOT Polish Airlines via Warsaw, or airBaltic via Riga with an onward connection. If your pet is travelling in the cabin, confirm with the airline that cabin transport is permitted through the connection point."
  - question: "My dog's rabies vaccination is a booster. Does the 21-day wait apply?"
    answer: "No. The 21-day waiting period applies only after a primary (first-ever) rabies vaccination. If your dog received a booster within the valid period shown in the EU pet passport, the booster is immediately valid and there is no additional waiting period before travel."
  - question: "What happens at Prague Airport when I arrive with my pet?"
    answer: "Czech border veterinary officers at PRG will check your EU pet passport. They verify the microchip number (scanned at the border), confirm the rabies vaccination is current, and check that the chip was implanted before or on the date of the first vaccine. Provided everything is in order, the process is a document check rather than an extended inspection."
