  overview: "Both Estonia and the Czech Republic are EU member states, which makes this one of the more manageable international pet moves.   sections:
    - heading: "What your pet needs to enter the Czech Republic"
      body: |
        The Czech State Veterinary Administration (SVA) requires all dogs, cats, and ferrets entering from another EU country to have an ISO-compliant microchip (11784/11785 standard), a current rabies vaccination recorded in their EU pet passport or AHC, and either of those documents presented at the border or airport.

        For a first-time rabies vaccination, Czech rules require a 21-day wait before the animal can enter the country. If your pet has already been vaccinated and is within the valid booster period, this wait does not apply again. Get this sorted early; it is the one requirement that cannot be rushed.

        The Czech Republic does not operate a national breed ban. Some municipalities impose restrictions on specific breeds in public spaces, but these are local rules rather than national entry conditions. If you are moving a Rottweiler, Pit Bull type, or similar breed, check the rules for your specific destination city.

    - heading: "Flights from Tallinn to Prague"
      body: |
        Prague Airport (PRG) is well connected from Tallinn (TLL), with airBaltic offering connections via its Riga hub and Lufthansa routing via Frankfurt. Travel time including connections is typically 3-5 hours.

        Small pets (under 8kg including carrier) travel in the cabin on airBaltic and Lufthansa services. Larger dogs must travel in the hold or as checked baggage depending on the airline. The cargo hold on commercial aircraft is pressurised and temperature-controlled.

        Czech Airlines (CSA) operates from Prague and can form part of your return journey if needed. For cargo transport of larger dogs, Lufthansa Cargo is a reliable option on this route.

    - heading: "EU pet passport versus AHC: which should you use?"
      body: |
        The EU pet passport is the more practical choice for most owners. An authorised Estonian vet can issue one once your pet is microchipped and vaccinated. It is valid for the lifetime of the animal and accepted across all EU member states and several non-EU countries that have bilateral agreements.

        An Animal Health Certificate is a single-use alternative. It is endorsed by an official vet and must be issued within 10 days of your arrival in the Czech Republic. This is a workable option for a one-way relocation, but you need to time the vet appointment carefully.

        If you are relocating permanently, opt for the EU pet passport. It removes the need to arrange a new document for every future trip.

faqs:
  - question: "Do I need a titre test to take my dog from Estonia to the Czech Republic?"
    answer: "No. Titre tests are only required for travel to certain non-EU destinations such as the UK, Australia, or New Zealand. For EU-to-EU travel, a valid rabies vaccination is sufficient."
  - question: "How early should I book my pet's place on the flight?"
    answer: "As early as possible, ideally at the same time as your own ticket. Most airlines limit the number of pets per flight to 1-3 in the cabin and a few more in the hold. Summer routes book up quickly."
  - question: "Can I drive from Estonia to Czech Republic with my pet?"
    answer: "Yes. The drive is approximately 1,500km and takes around 15-18 hours. Your pet needs the same documentation for a road journey as for a flight: microchip, valid rabies vaccination, and EU pet passport or AHC. The AHC must be issued within 10 days of entry."
  - question: "What breeds are restricted in Czech cities?"
    answer: "There is no national Czech breed ban, but individual cities may require muzzles and leads in public for certain breeds. Prague, for example, has rules about dogs in public transport. Check the local authority rules for your destination city."
