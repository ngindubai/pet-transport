  overview: |
    Sudan asks for no rabies titre test at all, from any country a pet is travelling from. For a US-origin move, that's a genuinely useful fact, since so many of this site's other high-risk-adjacent destinations pin the whole timeline on a blood draw and a lab result. Here, a current vaccination and a working microchip are the operative health requirements.

    What replaces that complexity is operational. Qatar Airways only began flying Doha to Port Sudan International Airport around 2 July 2026, three times a week, and Sudan Airways only restored its Cairo-Khartoum service on 16 May 2026, both genuinely recent developments after the 2023 conflict closed much of the country's international air access. Getting a pet from a US gateway to either Sudanese city still means a connection through Doha, Cairo or Istanbul, and no carrier's live pet cargo acceptance on any of those legs could be confirmed as currently, reliably running.

    Treat the lighter paperwork as real, and the flight logistics as something to verify directly and close to the travel date, not months in advance.
