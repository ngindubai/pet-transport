content:
  h1: "Pet Transport from the United Arab Emirates to Angola"
  overview: |
    Emirates flies this route direct three times a week, Dubai to Luanda, no changeover and none of the diplomatic uncertainty attached to some of the other UAE corridors in this batch. It's a genuinely simple flight.

    The catch sits in the vaccination timing rather than the airline. Angola's published guidance asks for the primary rabies dose to fall between 4 and 11 months before entry, a specific window unlike anything most owners have dealt with before, and it comes from a secondary source rather than a live government page. Get that wrong and the flight being easy won't help.

  sections:
    - heading: "The flight is the easy part"
      body: |
        A direct Dubai-Luanda service, three times weekly on Emirates, means no connection to plan around and no cargo handover between carriers. Compare that with the Algeria route in this same batch, where the direct Emirates service itself faces suspension risk by 2027, and Angola looks like the straightforward option of the three.

        Ethiopian Airlines via Addis Ababa is a fair second choice if the Emirates schedule doesn't line up, though it adds a connection the direct service doesn't need.

    - heading: "No titre test, but a vaccination window that needs checking twice"
      body: |
        Angola doesn't ask for a rabies antibody titre test from any country, UAE included, which matches what the reverse Angola-to-UAE route on this site already confirms and what the UK-to-Angola page finds too. That's the light side of this route.

        The heavier side is a published rule stating the primary rabies vaccination has to fall between 4 and 11 months before the entry date. It's an unusually narrow and specific window, and because it comes from a compiled source rather than an ISV page seen directly, it deserves a phone call or email to ISV, or to an experienced agent, before a vaccination appointment gets booked around it.

    - heading: "The species that can't come at all"
      body: |
        Wolf hybrids are barred from entering Angola outright, no exceptions. Savannah and Bengal cats face a similar rule unless documented as at least five generations removed from a wild ancestor. Neither restriction shows up on a routine vaccination record, so check it early if either might apply.

    - heading: "Budgeting the move"
      body: |
        MOCCAE's own export certificate is a modest fixed fee. Most of the real cost sits in the cargo booking itself, USD 1,200 to USD 2,800 depending on crate size and whether the flight runs direct on Emirates or via Addis Ababa on Ethiopian. All in, most owners land between USD 1,700 and USD 4,000 across a 10 to 14 week process, most of that time spent confirming the vaccination window rather than waiting on a flight.
