content:
  h1: "Pet Transport from Iceland to South Africa"
  overview: |
    South Africa doesn't ask an Iceland-origin dog to sit a rabies titre test. Iceland is on DALRRD's own no-quarantine, no-titre list, the same list that covers most of the EU and the US. What it does ask for instead is less obvious: a blood panel screening for Babesia gibsoni, Brucella canis, Dirofilaria immitis, Trypanosoma evansi and Leishmania, drawn within 30 days of export.

    None of those five diseases occur in Iceland. That's not a problem with the requirement, it's a genuine logistics wrinkle: an Icelandic vet may need to send the sample to an approved laboratory abroad rather than running it locally, which changes the timing math for this route more than the rabies side ever would.

  sections:
    - heading: "No titre test, but a panel most vets here don't run"
      body: |
        DALRRD's import protocol treats Iceland the way it treats most of the EU and the US: no rabies antibody titre test required, provided the vaccination timing and documentation check out. That's the easy part of this route.

        The harder part is a five-disease blood panel that has nothing to do with rabies. Babesia gibsoni, Brucella canis, Dirofilaria immitis, Trypanosoma evansi and Leishmania are all vector-borne or region-specific diseases with essentially no presence in Iceland's cold, isolated environment, so don't assume your local vet already has a pathway to test for them. Ask early whether the practice can run the panel in-house or needs to send the sample elsewhere.
    - heading: "The DALRRD permit and the 30-day window"
      body: |
        Every dog and cat entering South Africa needs a veterinary import permit from DALRRD, plus an Animal Improvement Compliance Permit; expect around 30 days combined for both. The permit sets out exactly which documents and results the state vet checks on arrival.

        The disease panel's 30-day window is the tighter of the two timing constraints on this route. Work backwards from your confirmed travel date, not from when the vaccination was given, since a panel drawn too early won't be accepted at the border.
    - heading: "Keflavik to Johannesburg via London"
      body: |
        Icelandair covers Keflavik to Heathrow, and British Airways runs the direct onward leg to Johannesburg from there, with South African Airways as an alternative worth checking for space and price. It's two bookings, not one, so leave a sensible gap between the connecting flights.
