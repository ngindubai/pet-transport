  sections:
    - heading: "Uruguay's import requirements for EU pets"
      body: |
        Uruguay's animal health authority, MGAP (Ministerio de Ganaderia, Agricultura y Pesca), handles pet imports through its DIGEGRA division. MGAP veterinary officers inspect arriving pets at Carrasco International Airport (MVD) in Montevideo, which is the only air entry point for companion animals.

        The core requirements are: a rabies vaccination given at least 30 days before entry and not more than 12 months before arrival, a health certificate from Germany's competent authority (the state-endorsed AHC), and an import permit obtained from MGAP before travel. No titre test is required from Germany.

        Dogs have one additional requirement: praziquantel treatment against Taenia echinococcus (hydatic cyst). This treatment must be given between 72 hours and 30 days before admission to Uruguay, and a vet must document the product used, the dose, and the date. Treatment given more than 30 days before arrival falls outside the valid period.

        Uruguay also has breed restrictions. Pit Bull Terrier, American Staffordshire Terrier, Bull Terrier, and Dogo Argentino face import restrictions. Confirm your breed's status with MGAP before applying for the permit.

    - heading: "Getting the MGAP import permit from Germany"
      body: |
        The MGAP import permit is a prerequisite for travel, not something you arrange on arrival. Apply to MGAP (mgap.gub.uy) directly, or through the Uruguayan consulate in Germany. Allow at least six to eight weeks for the process.

        The permit specifies conditions that must be met: the vaccinations required, the health certificate format, and the antiparasitic requirements. Read it carefully when it arrives, as the conditions may differ slightly from the general requirements listed here.

        Once the permit is confirmed, finalise your travel date and book cargo from Frankfurt (FRA) or Munich (MUC) to Carrasco Airport (MVD) in Montevideo. The main routing options are Iberia via Madrid, KLM via Amsterdam, or Lufthansa Cargo connections to South America. Confirm live animal cargo acceptance on all legs before committing to a date.

    answer: "Praziquantel is an antiparasitic treatment that eliminates Taenia echinococcus (the hydatic cyst tapeworm). Uruguay requires it for all incoming dogs to protect public and livestock health. The treatment must be given between 72 hours and 30 days before arrival in Uruguay, and documented by a vet."

    answer: "Contact MGAP directly at mgap.gub.uy, or through the Uruguayan consulate in Germany. Apply at least 6-8 weeks before your planned travel date. The permit specifies the conditions your pet must meet before entry."
