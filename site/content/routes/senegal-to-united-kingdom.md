  h1: "Pet Transport from Senegal to the United Kingdom"
  overview: |
    Senegal doesn't appear on the UK's list of recognised pet travel countries. That single fact triggers the longest part of this process: a rabies titre test, followed by a fixed 90-day wait before your pet can board. Nothing shortens it. Not an agent, not a faster lab, not extra paperwork.

    The airline side is more forgiving. Brussels Airlines and Air France both run regular services out of Dakar with genuine live animal programmes, though Brussels Airlines will only take 4 pets per flight, so slots fill up.

    Start the paperwork the day you decide to move. Six months sounds like a long runway. For this route, it's closer to the minimum.

  sections:
    - heading: "Why Senegal's unlisted status sets the timeline"
      body: |
        The UK groups countries into listed and unlisted categories for pet travel. Listed countries, mostly the EU plus a handful of others assessed as controlling rabies effectively, skip the blood test entirely. Senegal sits outside that list.

        Unlisted status means your pet needs a FAVN or ELISA titre test at least 30 days after its rabies vaccination, run at a laboratory on the APHA's approved list. If the result clears the 0.5 IU/ml threshold, the clock starts: 90 days before your pet can travel.

        This isn't a penalty for anything you've done wrong. It's the standard route for any unlisted country, and thousands of pets clear it every year. It just takes planning that a listed-country move doesn't.

    - heading: "Flying via Brussels, Paris, or Casablanca"
      body: |
        Brussels Airlines runs Dakar to Brussels with a genuine cabin and cargo pet programme: small pets under 8kg in the cabin, larger dogs up to 20kg in the hold. The catch is capacity. Only 4 pets per flight are accepted, so this isn't a route to book at the last minute.

        Air France, codesharing with Air Senegal, flies roughly 14 times a week between Dakar and Paris CDG on Boeing 777 aircraft, giving you more flexibility if Brussels is full. Royal Air Maroc via Casablanca and Turkish Airlines via Istanbul are the fallback options, though both add a longer transit for the animal.

        Whichever carrier you choose, book the pet space as early in the process as the titre test allows. Waiting until the 90 days are up before contacting an airline is the most common way this route goes wrong.
