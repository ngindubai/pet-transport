  h1: "Pet Transport from the United Arab Emirates to Honduras"
  overview: |
    A cat leaving Dubai for Tegucigalpa clears MOCCAE's export desk in about the time it takes the online paperwork to get approved: two to three weeks, followed by a certificate that's only good for 10 days once issued. Getting into Honduras afterwards turns out to be the easier half of the trip, not the harder one.

    Honduras asks for a rabies vaccination given between 21 days and 12 months before arrival, an import permit, and a standard health certificate. No titre test, no lengthy waiting period on the Honduras end. The UAE side, with its tighter certificate window and mandatory in-person vet check at Cargo Village, is what actually sets the pace here.

  sections:
    - heading: "MOCCAE's export process, not the destination, drives the calendar"
      body: |
        Every pet leaving the UAE needs the same MOCCAE export health certificate, regardless of species or where it's headed. Apply online two to three weeks ahead, then wait for the certificate itself, which only becomes valid within 10 days of the actual flight. That narrow window means the certificate has to be the last document finalised, timed to the flight date rather than to whatever else is happening in the move.

        The in-person check at the MOCCAE veterinary post at Cargo Village is non-negotiable. It's the final gate before the pet is cleared to fly, and it requires the animal physically present alongside the full paperwork set, not a document drop-off.

    - heading: "Honduras: comparatively straightforward, still worth respecting"
      body: |
        SAG, operating through SENASA under Ministerial Agreement 331-2009, wants three things on arrival: a rabies vaccination that falls inside the 21-day-to-12-month window, an import permit, and a health certificate from a licensed vet in the country of origin. That's a shorter list than most Gulf-to-Latin-America routes require.

        It's tempting to treat "shorter list" as "low stakes." Don't. Honduras's own published guidance states that non-compliant animals can face quarantine where a facility exists, return to the country of origin, or, in the worst outcome, euthanasia. The requirements are few, but they're enforced.

    - heading: "The flight has to go through Miami"
      body: |
        There's no direct UAE-Honduras service. Emirates flies Dubai to Miami nonstop, with pets required to travel as cargo rather than accompanied baggage on this pairing; Qatar Airways' Doha-Miami A350 route is a reasonable alternative first leg. From Miami, American Airlines completes the trip into San Pedro Sula or Palmerola. Two cargo handoffs, not one connecting ticket, so book both legs with an agent who understands the transfer, not just the individual airlines' pet policies in isolation.
