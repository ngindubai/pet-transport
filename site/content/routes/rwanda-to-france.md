The 90-day wait is the detail that catches most people out on this route. You can get the microchip done, vaccinate your dog, and have the titre test result back in your hands within six or seven weeks. But the 90-day clock runs from the date blood was drawn, not from the date results arrived. That means a minimum of roughly five months from vaccination to the earliest possible entry date into France, before you add cargo booking time or any retesting.

Plan for 20 to 26 weeks on this route, and build a buffer for the titre test result.

## The EU Rules: What France Requires from Rwanda

France is an EU member state, and Rwanda is not on the EU's list of approved third countries for pet travel. That means dogs and cats entering France from Rwanda must meet the full EU unlisted third country requirements under Regulation 576/2013 and Commission Delegated Regulation 2020/692.

The steps must happen in this exact order:

1. Microchip implanted (ISO 11784/11785)
2. Rabies vaccination (after microchipping)
3. Wait at least 30 days
4. Blood drawn for FAVN titre test
5. Wait 90 days from blood draw date
6. Travel permitted

The result from the FAVN test must show at least 0.5 IU/ml at an EU-approved laboratory. If the result comes back below that threshold, the animal must be re-vaccinated, a new blood draw taken at least 30 days later, and the 90-day wait starts again from that new blood draw date.

**Key dates to track**

| Milestone | Minimum gap before next step |
|---|---|
| Microchip | Must precede vaccination |
| Rabies vaccination | 30 days before blood draw |
| Blood draw for titre test | 90 days before France entry |
| EU health certificate | Issued within required window before travel |

## The Rwanda Export Side

The Rwanda Agriculture and Animal Resources Development Board (RAB) authorises veterinarians to issue export health certificates and endorses those certificates for international travel. Your vet must be RAB-authorised; this is not a standard private clinic certificate.

No formal export permit is required for personal companion pets. The health certificate must be in the EU format, not a standard Rwandan export certificate. French authorities and customs will inspect for the correct EU format on arrival. Your vet, with RAB guidance, should be familiar with the EU-format requirement, but confirm this when booking the appointment.

The RAB endorsement adds several days to pre-travel preparation. Time this carefully alongside the 90-day countdown so that your EU-format certificate is completed and endorsed within the window required before the flight.

## Getting from Kigali to Paris

Brussels Airlines offers the most straightforward routing from Kigali (KGL) to Paris Charles de Gaulle (CDG), stopping at Brussels (BRU) with an onward connection. Ethiopian Airlines via Addis Ababa (ADD) also reaches CDG via Ethiopian Cargo, and RwandAir routes via Brussels with a short onward leg to Paris. Air France Cargo operates at CDG and may accept live animals on specific legs; confirm directly with the carrier.

Most cargo routes involve one transit stop. Confirm that the transit airport will accept live animals and that connection times meet the airline's minimum live animal transit requirements. Cargo booking from Kigali typically requires four to six weeks of lead time. Book space after confirming your 90-day wait end date to avoid arriving before the waiting period expires.
