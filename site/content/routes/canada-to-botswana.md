  h1: "Pet Transport from Canada to Botswana"
  overview: |
    Own a wolf-dog hybrid, a Savannah or a Bengal cat? Botswana won't let it in, not without proof the animal sits five generations removed from its wild-species parent, and wolf-dog hybrids are barred outright regardless of pedigree. It's a restriction few owners think to check before booking a flight.

    Everything else funnels through one airport. Sir Seretse Khama International in Gaborone is the sole approved port of entry for a pet import into Botswana, reached from Toronto via Ethiopian Airlines' Addis Ababa hub since no carrier flies a direct or Johannesburg-routed service between the two countries.

  sections:
    - heading: "The breed restriction most owners never look for"
      body: |
        Botswana's DVS bars wolf-dog hybrids from import entirely, no exceptions offered in the published regulations. Savannah and Bengal cats face a narrower door: they can enter only if five generations removed from the wild-species parent in their pedigree, a detail easy to miss until DVS asks for paperwork most breeders don't routinely provide.

        Check this before doing anything else on this route. A pet that fails the restriction isn't a paperwork problem to solve later; it's a reason the move doesn't happen at all.

    - heading: "One airport, no alternative"
      body: |
        Every compliant pet has to land at Sir Seretse Khama International near Gaborone. There's no secondary airport, no land border option, nothing to fall back on if a flight is delayed into a different city. Build the itinerary around that single point of entry from the start.

    - heading: "Two permits moving at different speeds"
      body: |
        DVS's own export permit for Botswana-origin pets typically clears in 3 to 5 working days. The import permit for a pet heading into Botswana is a different animal, reportedly closer to 3 weeks. Treat that gap as real rather than assuming symmetry between the two directions, and get the import application in as early as the rest of the paperwork allows.

        On the flight side, Ethiopian Airlines' Addis Ababa hub is the only realistic long-haul connection from Toronto, running roughly 7 times a week. Confirm live animal acceptance on both legs of that routing before locking in dates.
