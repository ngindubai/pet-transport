  h1: "Pet Transport from Hungary to Lebanon"
  overview: |
    Moving a pet from Budapest to Beirut is the most involved of the five Hungary-to-Caucasus-and-Middle-East routes, and the reason is a single requirement: Lebanon demands an advance import permit from the Ministry of Agriculture, and the permit takes 10-12 weeks to process. Everything else, the Nébih health certificate, the cargo booking on Turkish Airlines via Istanbul, the airport inspection at Beirut Rafic Hariri, depends on that permit being in place first.

    Beyond the permit, Lebanon requires a broader vaccination record than most destinations. Dogs need five core vaccines in addition to rabies, and cats need four. These are standard vaccines that most pets in Hungary already have, but they all need to be current and recorded on the official health certificate.

    The reward for the preparation is a well-defined process at BEY. Once the permit is in hand and the paperwork is correct, the Ministry of Agriculture inspection at Beirut is predictable. No quarantine applies for compliant arrivals.

  sections:
    - heading: "The import permit: the first step, not the last"
      body: |
        Lebanon requires an import permit from the Ministry of Agriculture (agriculture.gov.lb) for all companion animals entering the country. The permit must be obtained before the pet travels, and the application process takes 10-12 weeks. This is not a formality or an approximation: it is the minimum processing time and it cannot be expedited.

        Start the permit application as soon as you know the move is happening. Do not book cargo, do not arrange the health certificate, and do not fix a travel date until the permit application is in progress. The permit number will need to be referenced in supporting documentation, and Lebanon's inspectors at Beirut Rafic Hariri (BEY) will check for it on arrival.

        Working through an IPATA-registered agent with Lebanon experience is strongly recommended for this route. The application process involves correspondence with the Lebanese Ministry of Agriculture, and an agent who has done it before will know what documentation is required and how to track the application.

    - heading: "Vaccination requirements beyond rabies"
      body: |
        Lebanon's entry requirements for dogs and cats are more detailed than most European and Caucasus destinations. Rabies is required for both species, administered at least 30 days before entry and no more than 12 months before. Dogs additionally need: distemper, infectious hepatitis, leptospirosis, parvovirus, and parainfluenza. Cats additionally need: viral rhinotracheitis, panleukopenia, calicivirus, and leukemia.

        Most pets in Hungary already receive these vaccines as part of standard annual care. The requirement is not that the vaccines are given specially for Lebanon, but that they are all current and recorded in the pet's vaccination history. The Nébih-endorsed health certificate must list every vaccine with its administration date and batch number.

        If any vaccine is due or close to expiry, update it during the permit waiting period. This is a useful time to schedule the vet appointments: the 10-12 week permit window covers the time needed to ensure all vaccines are current and properly recorded.

    - heading: "Flying from Budapest to Beirut"
      body: |
        Turkish Airlines Cargo via Istanbul (IST) is the standard routing for live animal cargo between BUD and BEY. Budapest has multiple daily Turkish Airlines departures, IST operates regular flights to Beirut, and the airline's cargo handling on this corridor is well established. Total journey time BUD-IST-BEY is approximately 6-8 hours.

        Book through a Turkish Airlines Cargo agent or an IPATA agent. Confirm live animal acceptance on the specific segments and provide IATA crate dimensions when booking. Temperature restrictions apply when ambient temperatures at any point on the routing exceed 29-30 degrees Celsius: Beirut can be very warm in summer and this affects scheduling from approximately June to September.

        Lufthansa via Frankfurt (FRA) is the main alternative. The Frankfurt Animal Lounge at FRA handles live animal connections with temperature-controlled facilities, making this a better option during the hottest months if the Istanbul corridor is under temperature embargo.
