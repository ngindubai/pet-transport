content:
  h1: "Pet Transport from Fiji to the United Arab Emirates"
  overview: |
    Most pets arriving in the UAE from an unlisted country face a rabies titre test and a 90-day wait before MOCCAE will let them in. Fiji's dogs and cats skip that entirely. MOCCAE places Fiji on its approved low-risk list, alongside Australia, the UK and Japan, which means a microchip, a vaccination and a 30-day import permit do the job that a laboratory test and three-month hold would otherwise take.

    What still needs planning is the flight. Nothing connects Nadi to Dubai or Abu Dhabi without a change of aircraft, so a Brisbane or Sydney transfer, booked as manifest cargo, is part of every itinerary on this route.

  sections:
    - heading: "Why the titre test isn't part of this move"
      body: |
        MOCCAE's approved list runs to roughly 50 countries, and Fiji is one of them, a fact that surprises owners who assume a Pacific island nation would sit outside that group by default. It doesn't. The practical effect is that a vaccination given at least 21 days before travel, on a pet at least 12 weeks old, satisfies the rabies side of the requirement without a blood draw.

        That's a meaningfully lighter pathway than the unlisted-country route, which needs the titre result at 0.5 IU/ml or above, and a fixed 90-day wait from the draw date. Skipping that wait alone can cut this route's timeline by two to three months compared with an unlisted-origin move.

    - heading: "Getting to Dubai or Abu Dhabi from Nadi"
      body: |
        Fiji Airways' network stops at Brisbane, Sydney, Auckland and a scatter of Pacific destinations; none of it reaches the Gulf directly. The workable routing is a transfer at Brisbane onto Emirates or Fiji Airways' own onward booking, or via Sydney onto Qantas, connecting to the UAE leg from there.

        Because this is a live-animal transfer rather than a single ticket, book it through a specialist cargo agent who can confirm acceptance on both legs. The MOCCAE import permit's 30-day validity window means this booking and the permit application need to happen close together, not months apart.
