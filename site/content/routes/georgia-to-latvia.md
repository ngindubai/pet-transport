  overview: "Moving a pet from Georgia (the country in the Caucasus, capital Tbilisi) to Latvia requires careful planning over a minimum of five to six months. Latvia is an EU member state and Georgia is an EU unlisted country, which means the full EU unlisted pet travel process applies: microchip, rabies vaccination, FAVN titre test with a 90-day wait, and an NFA-endorsed health certificate.   sections:
    - heading: "Why the 90-day wait applies and what it means for planning"
      body: |
        Latvia, as an EU member state, applies the EU pet travel framework for animals arriving from unlisted third countries. Georgia is classified as an EU unlisted country, which means your pet must complete the FAVN titre test process before entering Latvia.

        The sequence is: chip implanted first, then primary rabies vaccination, then at least 30 days later a blood draw for the FAVN titre test at an EU-approved laboratory. If the result is 0.5 IU/ml or above, the 90-day wait begins from the blood draw date. Travel to Latvia cannot happen until at least day 91.

        The 90-day wait is calculated from when the blood was drawn, not when the results came back. If you draw blood and the results take two weeks, those two weeks count as part of your 90-day wait. Starting the process as early as possible after you know you are moving is the right approach.

    - heading: "What happens at Riga International Airport"
      body: |
        Riga International Airport (RIX) has border veterinary inspection for live animal cargo arriving from non-EU origins. The Partikas un veterinarais dienests (PVD) oversees these controls. Border vets at RIX will check: the NFA-endorsed health certificate (issued within 10 days of travel), the FAVN titre test result, the vaccination record, and the microchip.

        The chip must be scannable and the number must match every document precisely. If the titre test shows a successful result but the 90-day wait is incomplete, entry is refused. Latvian border vets apply EU rules strictly. Documentation needs to be correct and complete before the pet travels.

    - heading: "Routing from Tbilisi to Riga"
      body: |
        There is no direct cargo service from Tbilisi International (TBS) to Riga (RIX). The standard routing uses Turkish Airlines Cargo via Istanbul Ataturk (IST), where live animals are handled, then connects to RIX. Turkish Airlines operates IST to RIX, making this a clean two-leg routing.

        Riga is the hub of airBaltic, so connections from major Western European airports such as Frankfurt or Amsterdam to RIX are well-established. An alternative routing via Frankfurt with Lufthansa Cargo and then airBaltic to RIX is also viable. Whatever routing you choose, book during the 90-day wait and confirm live animal acceptance on every segment in writing.
