content:
  h1: "Pet Transport from Australia to El Salvador"
  overview: |
    DAFF's export permit carries a detail that trips up owners who book their flight after the paperwork rather than before: once issued, it is valid for 72 hours only. Miss that window and the whole export approval has to be redone. That single rule shapes the entire Australian side of this move, since everything else, the Notice of Intent, the pre-export vet check, has to land in the right order before the permit gets requested at all.

    El Salvador's import side could not be more different. MAG asks for no titre test, no lengthy quarantine, just a health certificate and an import authorisation that costs USD 9.04 and can even be completed at the airport itself on arrival if there is no time to apply in advance.

  sections:
    - heading: "Australia's export process: sequence matters more than paperwork volume"
      body: |
        DAFF wants a Notice of Intent lodged at least 10 working days before departure, followed by a health and welfare exam from a DAFF-registered vet within 72 hours of the flight. Only once both are satisfied, and DAFF is confident the pet meets El Salvador's own conditions, does the export permit get issued, alongside the government health certificate.

        The catch is the permit's own shelf life. It is valid for 72 hours from the moment it is issued, a genuinely tight window compared with most destination-side certificates on this route, which run 10 to 30 days. Book cargo space first, confirm the flight date, then time the DAFF application around it, not the other way round.

    - heading: "Why El Salvador's side is the easy part"
      body: |
        MAG does not ask Australian-origin pets for a rabies titre test, and states no routine quarantine for an arrival with complete paperwork. The import authorisation costs USD 9.04, can be applied for at MAG's Santa Tecla office, online, or at the airport itself on the day, and is valid for 30 days, single entry only.

        The only real friction is routing. No airline flies Melbourne or Sydney to San Salvador without at least one, usually two, connections through a US or Central American hub, and pet cargo space on that final Avianca sector into San Salvador is worth booking early during peak relocation season.
