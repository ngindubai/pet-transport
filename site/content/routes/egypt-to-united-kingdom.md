  sections:
    - heading: "The attestation step: timing it correctly"
      body: |
        Egypt requires that health certificates for animal exports be attested by an embassy. For UK-bound pets, this means the certificate issued by your GOVS-authorised Cairo vet must then be attested by either the Egyptian embassy in London or the British embassy in Cairo.

        The practical challenge is timing. The health certificate is valid for 10 days from issue to entry into Great Britain. Embassy attestation takes 1-2 weeks in normal circumstances. This means you need to allow enough time for attestation within that 10-day window.

        The typical approach is to issue the certificate early in the 10-day window, immediately submit it for attestation at the British embassy in Cairo (which is faster for Cairo-based owners than posting to London and back), and travel on day 8 or 9 of the window with the attested certificate in hand.

        A Cairo-based pet transport agent who handles UK export regularly will know which embassy route is faster and how to coordinate the timing. This is one situation where an agent earns their fee.

    - heading: "Egypt's heat and airline cargo windows"
      body: |
        Cairo regularly exceeds 40 degrees Celsius in summer. Airport tarmac temperatures are higher still. Most airline cargo carriers apply heat embargoes on live animal transport during the hottest months.

        For UK-bound pets from Cairo, planning the move in October through April avoids the worst heat restrictions. Moves in May, June, July, or August will require careful airline selection and early-morning departure times to minimise heat exposure.

        Brachycephalic breeds (Bulldogs, Pugs, French Bulldogs, Boxers) face additional restrictions year-round for long or heat-exposed journeys. If your dog is a brachycephalic breed, discuss the travel conditions with your airline and vet before booking.

  - question: "Does my pet need to quarantine when entering the UK from Egypt?"
    answer: "No. Egypt is a listed country under APHA rules. No quarantine is required for compliant arrivals. The requirements are a current health certificate (with embassy attestation), a valid ISO microchip implanted before the rabies vaccination, and a valid rabies vaccination (at least 21 days since primary). Dogs need tapeworm treatment 24-120 hours before UK arrival."
  - question: "What exactly is the Egyptian embassy attestation and why is it needed?"
    answer: "Egypt requires that export health certificates for animals be officially attested (authenticated) by an embassy. For UK-bound pets, you need the certificate attested by either the Egyptian embassy in London or the British embassy in Cairo. This is an administrative verification step that confirms the certificate is genuine. It is not a veterinary check. It takes 1-2 weeks and must be completed within the 10-day certificate validity window."
  - question: "Are any dog breeds banned from Egypt or from being exported?"
    answer: "Egypt bans Pit Bull Terriers from being imported into the country. When it comes to export, the same type of dog may face restrictions in your destination country. The UK prohibits four types under the Dangerous Dogs Act 1991: Pit Bull Terrier, Japanese Tosa, Dogo Argentino, and Fila Brasileiro. Verify both the Egyptian export rules and the UK entry rules for your breed before starting."
  - question: "Which airline should I use for cargo from Cairo to London?"
    answer: "Egyptair and British Airways both operate direct Cairo to London Heathrow services with cargo pet capacity.
