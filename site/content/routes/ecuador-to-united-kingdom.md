  sections:
    - heading: "AGROCALIDAD and what Ecuador's authority issues"
      body: |
        AGROCALIDAD (Agencia de Regulacion y Control Fito y Zoosanitario) is Ecuador's national animal and plant health authority. It handles both the export permit for live animals leaving Ecuador and the endorsement of the health certificate issued by your official vet. Without the AGROCALIDAD endorsement, the health certificate is not accepted at UK border inspection.

        Apply for the export permit online at least four weeks before travel. The permit has a validity period, so obtain it close enough to departure that it covers your travel date. AGROCALIDAD inspectors may also check the animal at the departure airport, so have all documentation readily accessible.

        Your AGROCALIDAD-registered vet issues the health certificate within 10 days of travel. This is the document that covers microchip, vaccination, titre test details, and the tapeworm treatment record for dogs. It needs to be accurate and match all other documentation.

    - heading: "Routing from Quito and Guayaquil"
      body: |
        Ecuador has two main international departure airports. Quito Mariscal Sucre (UIO) serves the capital and the north. Guayaquil Jose Olmedo (GYE) serves the coast and the south. Both have connections to Madrid and other European hubs, but the frequency and carrier options differ. Iberia, which operates the main Ecuador to Madrid route, connects well from both airports, and Iberia Cargo accepts pets on this corridor.

        The transit through Madrid is generally the smoothest option for Ecuador to London pet cargo. Madrid Barajas handles a high volume of South American live animal shipments and the transit to Heathrow on Iberia or via a connecting UK carrier is a well-established route. Air France via Paris and KLM via Amsterdam are viable alternatives. Confirm live animal acceptance and routing specifics with a cargo specialist.

    answer: "Yes, because Ecuador is an unlisted country under UK pet travel rules. The FAVN titre test is mandatory: blood drawn at least 30 days after the primary rabies vaccination, result of 0.5 IU/ml or above at an APHA-approved lab, followed by a 90-day waiting period. This applies to all dogs and cats. Source: GOV.UK listed and unlisted countries, 2026."
  - question: "Which authority endorses the health certificate in Ecuador?"
    answer: "AGROCALIDAD (Agencia de Regulacion y Control Fito y Zoosanitario) endorses the health certificate issued by your official vet in Ecuador. The certificate without AGROCALIDAD endorsement is not accepted by UK border inspection. Apply for the export permit through AGROCALIDAD at least four weeks before travel."

    answer: "Yes. Both Guayaquil Jose Olmedo (GYE) and Quito Mariscal Sucre (UIO) handle international pet cargo. The routing options and carrier availability differ, so check with a cargo agent which airport has better connections to London for your specific crate size and travel date."
  - question: "How long does the whole process take from Ecuador to the UK?"
    answer: "Around 16 to 20 weeks (4 to 5 months) if starting from scratch. The microchip and vaccination take a few weeks, the 30-day wait before blood draw, the titre test lab turnaround (typically 1-3 weeks), then the 90-day wait.
