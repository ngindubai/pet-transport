  h1: "Pet Transport Australia to UK: Your 2026 Guide"
  overview: "Moving a pet from Australia to the UK is more straightforward than the reverse journey. Australia is on the UK's approved list, meaning no titre test is required. The core requirements are an ISO microchip pre-dating the rabies vaccination, the 21-day vaccination wait, and a correctly timed Animal Health Certificate issued by an Official Veterinarian.   sections:
    - heading: "Why Australia to UK is simpler than UK to Australia"
      body: |
        The UK-to-Australia route requires a titre test, a 180-day waiting period, and a 10-day quarantine in Melbourne. The Australia-to-UK route requires none of these. The UK classifies Australia as a listed (approved) country, which means animals from Australia can enter Great Britain with standard documentation and no mandatory quarantine.

        The difference is driven by Britain's rabies-free status and its tiered approved-country list. Australia, also rabies-free and with rigorous biosecurity standards, is trusted as an approved origin. Provided your microchip, vaccination and AHC are in order, your pet walks off the cargo terminal with you after a short inspection at Heathrow.

    - heading: "Finding an Official Veterinarian in Australia"
      body: |
        The Animal Health Certificate must be issued by an Official Veterinarian, not just any registered vet. An OV is a vet who has been accredited to issue government health certificates for international travel. Not all Australian vets hold this accreditation.

        APHA (the UK authority) publishes guidance on finding OVs for non-EU countries. Specialist pet relocation agents in Australia typically have established relationships with OVs and can coordinate the examination and certificate on your behalf. If you are arranging this independently, allow extra time to locate an accredited OV and book the appointment within the 10-day window before travel.

    - heading: "Planning the tapeworm treatment"
      body: |
        Dogs entering Great Britain from any country require tapeworm (echinococcus) treatment within 24 to 120 hours of scheduled arrival. This applies regardless of origin country.

        From Sydney or Melbourne, a direct Qantas flight to Heathrow takes approximately 22-24 hours. A connecting flight via Singapore or Dubai takes 28-36 hours. Work backwards from your scheduled UK arrival time to determine when the tapeworm treatment must be given.

        For a direct Sydney to Heathrow flight arriving Wednesday at 06:00, the treatment must have been given between the previous Saturday at 06:00 and Tuesday at 06:00. Most vets in Australia schedule the treatment 2-3 days before the flight, which fits comfortably in the window for direct routes.

        Have your vet record the treatment time precisely in the AHC. The APHA inspector at Heathrow will check the time as well as the date.

faqs:
  - question: "My dog was vaccinated against rabies in Australia. Is that vaccination accepted for UK entry?"
    answer: "Yes. Rabies vaccination given in Australia by a licensed vet is accepted for UK entry, provided the microchip was implanted before the vaccination and at least 21 days have passed since the first vaccination in the primary course. Australia is a listed country for UK purposes, so there is no titre test requirement. The Official Veterinarian issuing your AHC will review and include the vaccination record."
  - question: "Can I use an Australian pet passport for UK entry?"
    answer: "Australia does not issue a standard EU-format pet passport. What you will have is the vaccination and microchip records in whatever document your Australian vet uses, plus the AHC issued for the journey. The AHC is the primary entry document for Great Britain. There is no format called a pet passport in the Australian system; the AHC replaces that concept for this route."
  - question: "How long before my flight should I schedule the OV appointment?"
    answer: "The examination must fall within 10 days of your scheduled arrival time in Great Britain. Most people schedule the OV appointment 5-8 days before departure, which keeps the certificate well within the 10-day window while allowing time for any administrative steps. Book the OV appointment 2-3 weeks in advance; they can be difficult to schedule at short notice."
  - question: "Can I enter via a ferry port rather than a UK airport?"
    answer: "Yes. Great Britain has approved entry routes that include ferry ports as well as airports. Heathrow, Gatwick, Manchester and Edinburgh are approved airports. Several ferry ports and the Eurotunnel terminal are also approved. However, arriving from Australia by sea is not realistic for the main journey. You would fly to a European hub and then consider crossing by sea, but that adds EU documentation complexity. The direct flight to Heathrow is the standard route."
  - question: "What is the cost difference between using an agent and handling this myself?"
    answer: "Agents for Australia to UK pet moves typically charge AUD 800 to AUD 2,500 for the Australian side of the process. The main value they provide is OV coordination, AHC preparation, airline cargo booking, and ensuring tapeworm timing is correct. The route is manageable independently if you are prepared to research OV contacts and work precisely on timing. The cost saving from going alone is real but so is the risk of a timing error on the tapeworm treatment, which is the most common compliance issue on this route."
