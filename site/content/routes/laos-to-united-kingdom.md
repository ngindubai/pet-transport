  h1: "Pet Transport from Laos to United Kingdom: 2026 Guide"
  overview: |
    Fifteen days: that is the minimum notice Laos's Department of Livestock and Fisheries needs before it will issue an export licence for a dog or cat leaving Vientiane. It is a short window compared with everything else this route demands, because the UK side runs on its own much longer clock.

    Laos sits on the UK's unlisted-country list, so a FAVN titre test and a 90-day wait apply in full, on top of a DLF export process and a cargo routing that always transfers through a regional hub. Realistically this route runs 18 to 24 weeks from a standing start.

  sections:
    - heading: "DLF's export process and the 15-day rule"
      body: |
        The Department of Livestock and Fisheries, sitting under the Ministry of Agriculture and Forestry, handles every animal export from Laos. An application has to reach DLF at least 15 days before the intended export date. DLF then inspects the animal, applies quarantine at its own discretion if it thinks that is warranted, and issues the Animal Health Certificate and Export Licence as a pair.

        In practice, the 15-day window is rarely the binding constraint on this route. The UK's own titre test sequence takes months, so DLF's paperwork tends to run in parallel rather than on the critical path, provided the application goes in once the 90-day wait is close to finishing.

    - heading: "Why the titre test applies and how the sample gets tested"
      body: |
        Laos is not on the UK's list of listed countries, so the FAVN or ELISA titre test is mandatory: blood drawn at least 30 days after the primary rabies vaccination, a result of 0.5 IU/ml or higher, and a fixed 90-day wait from the draw date before the pet can enter Great Britain.

        A practical complication specific to Laos is sample logistics. Few APHA-approved laboratories operate inside the country, so blood usually needs to be couriered out, commonly to a laboratory in Thailand or further afield. Build in extra time for that shipping step beyond the standard lab turnaround, since a delayed or degraded sample means starting the 30-day wait again.
