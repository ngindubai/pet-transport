  sections:
    - heading: "Latvia's Pit Bull ban: the one rule that can block a move"
      body: |
        Latvia is one of the EU countries with a specific ban on Pit Bull-type dogs. Unlike breed handling rules in some other EU states, Latvia's prohibition means American Pit Bull Terrier and dogs classified under this category cannot enter the country at all. This is a national legal prohibition, not an airline policy or local restriction.

        For owners of other breeds, the Latvia process is as simple as EU pet travel gets: valid EU passport, ISO microchip, current rabies vaccination. There is no quarantine and no import permit. The PVD (Food and Veterinary Service at pvd.gov.lv) is the authority to contact if you have any doubt about your dog's breed classification under Latvian law.

    - heading: "Direct Ryanair options from Italy to Riga"
      body: |
        Ryanair's Italy-to-Riga routes are one of the more convenient options in this batch of EU corridors. Ryanair operates from Rome Ciampino (CIA) and Milan Bergamo (BGY) to Riga, and the flight takes around 3 hours. For small pets under 8kg including the carrier, Ryanair's cabin pet policy applies.

        The catch is that Ryanair does not take pets as hold baggage. If your dog or cat is too large for in-cabin travel, Ryanair is not an option. In that case, Lufthansa via Frankfurt is the most commonly used alternative for Italy-to-Latvia cargo movements.

        Lufthansa's Frankfurt Animal Lounge is an IATA-accredited live animal handling facility. For large breeds or multiple pets, routing through FRA provides a higher standard of care than most smaller transit airports.
