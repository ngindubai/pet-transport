  h1: "Pet Transport from Germany to Maldives: 2026 Guide"
  overview: |
    Start with the question most people actually ask: can my dog come? No. Dog import into the Maldives is prohibited outright, a Maldives Customs Service rule that applies without a standard pet-owner exception. Condor now flying Frankfurt to Male four times a week each summer changes nothing about that.

    Cats face a much shorter list. Microchip, rabies vaccination given between 30 days and 12 months before arrival, the core feline vaccines, and a health certificate signed within 10 days of travel. What's thinner is the guidance around permits and titre tests, so this route deserves a direct check with Maldivian authorities rather than an assumption borrowed from a different country's rules.

  sections:
    - heading: "The dog question, answered plainly"
      body: |
        There's no age limit, no extra document and no assistance-animal carve-out that gets a dog into the Maldives. It's a blanket prohibition on the customs list, sitting alongside items like alcohol and pork products. If a service dog is genuinely involved, get written confirmation from the Maldives government directly, not from a general pet transport process built for something the rule doesn't allow.
    - heading: "What a cat from Germany actually needs"
      body: |
        Microchip first, rabies vaccination second, given 30 days to 12 months before arrival. Add viral rhinotracheitis, calicivirus, panleukopenia and feline leukaemia cover, and close the file with a health certificate signed within 10 days of departure. None of it demands the multi-month wait a titre test route would.
    - heading: "Where the guidance runs thin"
      body: |
        Some sources describe no import permit requirement at all for a travelling pet cat; others suggest a general permit applies to all pet imports. There's no single current government page that settles this cleanly. Treat it as an open item for your vet or agent to confirm, not a fact to build a flight date around.
    - heading: "Condor's new route and the fallback that still works"
      body: |
        A direct Frankfurt-Male service sounds like the obvious answer, and it might become one once Condor confirms live animal cargo terms. Until then, Qatar Airways via Doha and Emirates via Dubai remain the dependable options, both needing an IATA-spec crate and a licensed pet shipping agent rather than a same-day cargo drop-off.
