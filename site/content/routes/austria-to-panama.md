content:
  h1: "Pet Transport from Austria to Panama"
  overview: |
    Panama is a straightforward destination for pets from Austria in most respects: no import permit, no quarantine, and a veterinary inspection at Tocumen International Airport (PTY) that moves quickly when the paperwork is correct. The one step that catches people out is the health certificate authentication. Panama requires the AGES-endorsed Austrian health certificate to be authenticated by the Panamanian Embassy in Vienna before travel. That adds 2-3 working days on top of the AGES endorsement, so factoring this into your timeline is essential.

    MIDA (Ministerio de Desarrollo Agropecuario) governs pet imports in Panama. Dogs need current vaccinations against distemper, hepatitis, leptospirosis, and parvovirus; cats need rhinotracheitis, panleukopenia, and feline leukaemia. Rabies vaccination is required for both, given at least 30 days before entry. Pets under 4 months old are not permitted to enter.

    Flights from Vienna to Panama City all connect through at least one hub. Copa Airlines, which is headquartered at Tocumen Airport, is a natural option for this route. Iberia via Madrid and Air France via Paris CDG are alternatives. All pets travel as manifest cargo on this routing.

  sections:
    - heading: "The Panamanian Embassy Authentication Step"
      body: |
        After your Amtstierarzt issues the health certificate and AGES endorses it, the document must go to the Panamanian Embassy in Vienna for a further authentication stamp. This is a consular legalisation process confirming the AGES endorsement is genuine. The Embassy of Panama in Austria is located in Vienna; if you are in another part of Austria, courier the documents. The authentication typically takes 2-3 working days and costs around EUR 30-60 in consular fees.

        Build this step into your schedule carefully. The certificate must be issued within 10 days of travel. If AGES endorsement takes one day and Embassy authentication takes three, you are left with a six-day window at the start. Missing this step or running out of time means starting the certificate process again. Many people find it easiest to use a pet transport agent who handles this step routinely and knows the Embassy's current processing times.

    - heading: "Arrival at Tocumen International Airport: What to Expect"
      body: |
        MIDA veterinary officers carry out the inspection at Tocumen International Airport (PTY) in Panama City. They will check the microchip against the health certificate, verify vaccination dates, and confirm the AGES endorsement and Panamanian Embassy authentication are present. If everything is in order, the process takes around 30-60 minutes. A MIDA registration fee of approximately USD 20 is payable on arrival in cash or card depending on the desk.

        Panama does not quarantine pets from Austria. Once MIDA clears your pet, you can take them directly. Bringing a copy of all documents, as well as the originals, is good practice. If the cargo flight arrives at an unusual hour, confirm in advance whether MIDA officers are available around the clock at PTY, as inspection times can vary.

  - question: "Does Panama require an import permit for a personal pet from Austria?"
    answer: "No. Personal pets do not require an import permit from MIDA. The required documents are a microchip, current vaccinations including rabies, and an official health certificate endorsed by AGES and authenticated by the Panamanian Embassy in Vienna."
  - question: "Why does the health certificate need Embassy authentication?"
    answer: "Panama requires that foreign official documents be authenticated by its embassy in the origin country before they are accepted at the border. This confirms to MIDA that the AGES endorsement is genuine. Allow 2-3 working days for the Embassy in Vienna to process it."
  - question: "My puppy is 3 months old. Can I bring her to Panama?"
    answer: "No. MIDA does not permit pets under 4 months old to enter Panama. You will need to wait until your puppy reaches 4 months and has received her vaccinations, including at least 30 days for the rabies vaccine to be valid."
  - question: "Is there a quarantine period for pets arriving in Panama?"
    answer: "No. Provided your documents are in order, MIDA clears pets at Tocumen Airport (PTY) after inspection and they can leave with you directly."
