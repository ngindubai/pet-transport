  overview: |
    Compare this route with Uruguay's own pets travelling the other way into Greece, and the contrast is stark. A Greek pet heading to Montevideo skips the titre test entirely, since Uruguay does not treat EU member states as rabies-risk origins. What it needs instead is an MGAP import permit, a current rabies vaccination, and, for dogs, a praziquantel treatment against Echinococcus.

    Six to nine weeks covers most of these moves, assuming the import permit application starts early and the connecting flight from Athens is booked with enough buffer.

  sections:
    - heading: "Start with the MGAP import permit, not the vet visit"
      body: |
        Every companion pet entering Uruguay needs an import permit from MGAP, arranged before travel and specifying the conditions your documentation has to meet. Apply as soon as your move is confirmed rather than waiting until the health certificate stage, since the permit terms can shape what your vet needs to record.

        From there, the process is straightforward: a current rabies vaccination, an ISO microchip (recommended though not strictly mandatory), and an endorsed health certificate issued within 10 days of travel.
    - heading: "The echinococcus treatment and the Athens-to-Montevideo routing"
      body: |
        Dogs need a praziquantel-based treatment for hydatid tapeworm, given between 72 hours and 30 days before arrival and recorded on the certificate. Cats are exempt from this step.

        There is no direct service between Athens and Montevideo, so most pets connect through Madrid with Iberia or through Paris with Air France, with a Lisbon-São Paulo routing via TAP as a workable third option. Confirm live animal cargo acceptance on both legs before booking, since Athens-origin capacity to South America is limited.
