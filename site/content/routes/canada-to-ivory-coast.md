  h1: "Pet Transport from Canada to Ivory Coast"
  overview: |
    Two email addresses, not an online portal, are how Cote d'Ivoire's veterinary directorate issues the import permit this route needs. Send the application and a copy of the rabies certificate to carv.dsvci@gmail.com and dsv.sdsa2017@gmail.com, and expect no fixed turnaround time in return. That informality is the real planning risk on this route, not a blood test: Cote d'Ivoire doesn't ask a Canadian-origin pet for a rabies titre test at all.

    What it does insist on is a health certificate in French, or carrying a certified translation, since Ivorian officials at Port Bouet International Airport won't accept an English-only document on its own. Ethiopian Airlines through Addis Ababa and Royal Air Maroc through Casablanca both offer a single-carrier routing into Abidjan, useful given how much of this process already runs on paperwork with no published deadline.
  sections:
    - heading: "No titre test, but two systems that don't talk to each other"
      body: |
        Cote d'Ivoire's own import rules for a companion animal are genuinely light: a rabies vaccination dated correctly, a microchip in practice if not strictly by law, and the DSV import permit. No titre test, no fixed quarantine, no CDC-style facility restriction.

        Worth flagging directly: Canada's own CFIA classifies Cote d'Ivoire as high-risk for dog rabies, the same list that includes Cameroon and Eswatini. That classification governs the reverse journey, a pet coming from Cote d'Ivoire into Canada, and currently affects commercial dog shipments rather than a personal pet. It has no bearing on this direction of travel. Two separate rulebooks, each written by the destination country, run this corridor; treating them as mirror images of each other is the mistake to avoid.

    - heading: "An email permit and a French certificate, not a blood test"
      body: |
        DSV, the Direction des Services Veterinaires under MIRAH, issues the import permit by email. Send the completed application and a copy of the rabies certificate to both addresses, and expect no published turnaround guarantee in return. Applying eight to ten weeks out gives room for a slow reply and a possible follow-up round.

        The health certificate carries its own separate requirement: French language, or a certified translation attached, before Ivorian officials will accept it at Port Bouet International Airport. A CFIA-endorsed certificate that's otherwise perfect but written only in English risks being turned away on this point alone, so build translation time into the schedule alongside the standard vet and endorsement process.

    - heading: "Three carriers, one recurring restriction"
      body: |
        Ethiopian Airlines' Toronto to Addis Ababa nonstop, continuing on the airline's own daily Addis to Abidjan service, keeps a Canadian departure on a single network the whole way. Royal Air Maroc matches that with its near-daily Montreal to Casablanca sector, continuing to Abidjan. Air France, via Paris, is a third option from a wider spread of Canadian departure cities.

        Every one of these carriers restricts snub-nosed breeds from the hold. Confirm that detail against your pet's specific breed before locking in a booking, whichever airline you choose.
