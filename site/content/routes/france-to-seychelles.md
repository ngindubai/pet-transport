content:
  h1: "Pet Transport from France to Seychelles"
  overview: |
    Three months of the year, Paris has a nonstop option to Mahe. Air Seychelles runs the route on a Boeing 787-9 from June to August, and outside that window it simply doesn't fly, which makes the calendar as much a part of route planning here as any paperwork deadline.

    None of it changes what Seychelles asks for. The National Biosecurity Agency quarantines every arriving pet by law, and France's EU rabies-free classification only shortens that stay to around 14 days rather than removing it. Between the titre test, the import permit, and a health screen that runs well beyond rabies for dogs, twelve to sixteen weeks is a realistic floor.

  sections:
    - heading: "A direct flight with a closing date"
      body: |
        Air Seychelles' Paris-Mahe service is real, and it runs on a widebody 787. It's also strictly seasonal, operating June through August and not at all for the rest of the year. Anyone planning a move outside that four-month window needs to build in a Gulf hub connection from the outset rather than assuming the direct option will simply be there.

        Emirates via Dubai and Etihad via Abu Dhabi both run year-round, each with their own dedicated pet handling facility at the connecting hub. For a move planned for, say, October or February, one of these becomes the only realistic routing, not a fallback.

    - heading: "Why the flight schedule doesn't touch the quarantine rule"
      body: |
        Seychelles' National Biosecurity Agency makes quarantine mandatory for every arriving cat and dog, whatever its country of origin and whichever flight it arrived on. France's EU rabies-free status places it in the lighter NBA tier alongside the UK and Germany, which in practice means roughly 14 days at the Mahe facility rather than the extended combination of a fixed post-draw wait plus a longer stay that a rabies-affected origin like the US faces.

        The titre test itself still needs a result above 0.5 IU/mL, drawn at least 30 days after vaccination and tested within 6 months of travel. Whether the pet flies direct in July or via Dubai in November makes no difference to any of that.

    - heading: "A health screen that goes past rabies"
      body: |
        Dogs need clearance for Brucella canis, Trypanosoma evansi, Babesia gibsoni, Leishmania and Erlichia canis, unless the French vet can certify the country free of a given disease. It's a wider panel than most destinations on this site ask for. Raise it with the vet early rather than at the AHC appointment.

        A separate list of breeds, Rottweiler, German Shepherd, Doberman and several others among them, needs mandatory surgical sterilisation before entry, backed by a vet-endorsed certificate. It's a condition of the import permit, not an optional recommendation.
