  h1: "Pet Transport from the United Kingdom to North Macedonia"
  overview: |
    North Macedonia's Food and Veterinary Agency treats a UK-vaccinated pet the same way Brussels does. No blood test, no three-month wait, just a microchip, a current rabies vaccination and a 21-day gap before travel. That puts this route in the simpler tier alongside Albania rather than the far longer process this site documents for Montenegro.

    The reason is specific rather than general goodwill: the UK sits on the EU's own approved third-country list for rabies, and FVA extends that same courtesy on entry that the UK extends to a North Macedonia pet travelling the other way. It is worth checking that classification hasn't shifted before relying on it, since it is set at EU level and reviewed periodically, not written into Macedonian law as a permanent fixture.

    The catch, and there is always one on this route, is that the cheapest, most frequent flight between London and Skopje carries no pets whatsoever.

  sections:
    - heading: "Why the exemption holds: FVA mirrors the EU's own list"
      body: |
        North Macedonia's import rule for rabies serology follows a simple binary. A pet arriving from a country the EU classifies as rabies-controlled skips the titre test. Everywhere else, the test applies. The United Kingdom sits on that approved list, Annex II to Implementing Regulation (EU) 2026/636, and FVA's own guidance carries that classification through into its entry requirements.

        That gives a UK pet the same treatment on entry to North Macedonia that a North Macedonia pet gets on entry to the UK, a genuinely reciprocal arrangement. It is also a different picture to Montenegro, another EU candidate covered elsewhere on this site, whose own published guidance does not describe the same UK exemption.

        None of this is permanent by Macedonian statute alone. The underlying EU list is reviewed from time to time, so a quick check with FVA close to the travel date is worth the five minutes it takes.

    - heading: "Wizz Air flies the route, just not with your pet on board"
      body: |
        Search flights from London to Skopje and Wizz Air's Luton service, around 12 times weekly, usually comes up first. It carries no pets beyond certified assistance dogs, cabin or cargo, full stop.

        Turkish Airlines through Istanbul is the established alternative, with a purpose-built live animal facility separate from general cargo. Austrian Airlines via Vienna feeds into the Lufthansa Group's cargo network and is the other realistic option.

        Book with one of these two rather than assuming the direct budget flight will somehow work for an exception. It won't, and finding that out at the airport is a worse outcome than planning around it from the start.
