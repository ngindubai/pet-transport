content:
  h1: "Pet Transport from United Kingdom to Laos: 2026 Guide"
  overview: |
    Take a pet from the UK into Laos and there's no rabies titre test to arrange, no 90-day wait, none of the machinery this route needs in the opposite direction. What Laos does require is an import permit from the Department of Livestock and Fisheries and a rabies vaccination timed to fall inside a specific window: no earlier than 30 days before travel, no later than 12 months.

    Miss that window either side and the vaccination has to be repeated, which is the single most common way owners derail an otherwise straightforward move. Budget six to ten weeks, most of it DLF permit processing and export certificate timing rather than anything medical.

  sections:
    - heading: "Why Laos doesn't ask for a titre test, and what it asks for instead"
      body: |
        The UK's rabies control status means Laos treats it as a low-risk origin, so the FAVN or ELISA titre test that dominates the reverse route simply doesn't apply here. Laos's Department of Livestock and Fisheries only recommends a titre test for pets arriving from countries it considers high-rabies-risk, and the UK isn't one of them.

        What DLF does insist on is the import permit, applied for before travel, and a rabies vaccination administered inside a 30-day-to-12-month window before the pet arrives. A pet vaccinated 13 months before arrival, or one vaccinated only three weeks before, both fall outside that window and need a fresh dose.

    - heading: "Bangkok, Hanoi or Seoul: the routing options into Vientiane"
      body: |
        No airline flies non-stop from London to Vientiane, so every pet on this route changes planes at least once. Bangkok is the most established option: Thai Airways Cargo's Heathrow service connects into Lao Airlines' twice-daily Bangkok-Vientiane hop.

        Hanoi via Vietnam Airlines is a workable alternative, with Lao Airlines running a daily onward leg. Seoul Incheon on Korean Air is a third option, useful if it fits the travel dates better, connecting into Lao Airlines' daily Seoul-Vientiane service.
