  h1: "Pet Transport from Moldova to the United States"
  overview: |
    Moldova appears on the CDC's high-risk list for dog rabies, alongside more than 100 other countries. For dog owners, that means a passing titre test or 28-day quarantine, on top of the standard CDC Dog Import Form every dog needs regardless of origin. For cat owners the picture is completely different: no federal rabies requirement applies to cats at all, whatever country they're travelling from.

    Geography adds a layer most high-risk-country routes don't have. Chisinau has no direct US service, so every pet on this route connects at least once, through Istanbul, Vienna, Frankfurt or Warsaw. That's manageable if your dog has a valid titre test, since any of those routings work and the pet can land at any US airport with CBP inspection. It becomes a real constraint if you're relying on the 28-day quarantine option instead, because then the flight must land at the exact airport where the CDC-registered facility sits, with no onward domestic leg beforehand.

    Cats travelling this route are, by comparison, straightforward. Get the crate right, get the airline booking sorted, and go.

  sections:
    - heading: "The CDC high-risk pathway for dogs from Moldova"
      body: |
        Since the CDC's 2024 rule change, every country is either dog-rabies-free or low-risk, or high-risk. Moldova sits in the high-risk group. Dogs from high-risk countries need a Certification of Foreign Rabies Vaccination and Microchip form, completed by a Moldovan vet and endorsed by ANSA, plus either a passing rabies serology titre test from a CDC-approved laboratory or 28-day quarantine at a CDC-registered facility on arrival. Source: CDC, cdc.gov/importation.

        The titre route has fixed timing that cannot be compressed: blood drawn at least 30 days after vaccination, and the result needs to land at least 28 days before the flight. Realistically that's two months minimum once the vaccination is done, and closer to three if the laboratory has a backlog. Every dog, regardless of risk category, also needs the CDC Dog Import Form completed online before travel. It's free and takes a few minutes, but skipping it can delay entry even for an otherwise fully compliant pet.

        Cats are unaffected by any of this. APHIS Veterinary Services has no federal animal health requirements for cats entering the US from any country, Moldova included. A basic health certificate for airline purposes is sensible, but it isn't a CDC or USDA mandate.

    - heading: "Why the CDC-facility airport restriction matters more on this route"
      body: |
        If your dog doesn't have a valid titre test and you're relying on the 28-day quarantine option, US rules require the flight to land at the specific airport where the CDC-registered facility is based, and forbid any domestic connection before the pet clears the facility. That's a manageable restriction from a country with lots of direct US flights. It's a bigger deal from Chisinau, where every itinerary already involves a European connection before the transatlantic leg.

        Practically, this means checking CDC-registered facility locations and current availability before booking your European connection, not after. Getting the titre test done removes this constraint entirely and opens up any US gateway airport, which is why most owners on this route choose that path over quarantine if the timeline allows it.

        On the Moldova side, ANSA's endorsement of the Certification of Foreign Rabies Vaccination and Microchip form is only valid for 30 days from the vet's signature. Book your flight, then get the form endorsed close enough to departure that the 30-day window doesn't lapse before your dog boards.

    - heading: "Routing options from Chisinau"
      body: |
        Four carriers cover this corridor with genuine pet transport, each via their own hub. Turkish Airlines through Istanbul offers a flat USD 150 cargo rate regardless of crate weight tier, useful for larger dogs. Austrian Airlines through Vienna keeps handling on a single carrier's own network from Chisinau through to several US cities. Lufthansa through Frankfurt or Munich has the widest US network but bans all brachycephalic breeds from the hold outright, cabin-only for those breeds under 8kg. LOT Polish Airlines through Warsaw serves New York, Chicago and Newark directly but requires cargo bookings through an approved shipping agent rather than the standard passenger system.

        Wizz Air, despite dominating low-cost capacity out of Chisinau to Western Europe, does not carry pets at all beyond certified assistance dogs, and has no US network in any case. It's not a factor on this route, but worth ruling out early if you were assuming it might be.
