    Moving a pet from Greece to South Korea requires an APQA (Animal and Plant Quarantine Agency) import permit, a Hellenic Ministry-issued official health certificate, and arrival at Incheon International Airport.     - heading: "APQA import permit and Incheon arrival process"
      body: |
        The Animal and Plant Quarantine Agency (APQA), under South Korea's Ministry of Agriculture, Food and Rural Affairs, regulates all animal imports. An import permit must be obtained from APQA before the pet departs Greece. The permit specifies entry conditions and required documentation.

        On arrival at Incheon International Airport (ICN), APQA inspectors verify the health certificate, microchip, and vaccination records. For compliant pets from EU member states, no routine quarantine applies. The inspection is primarily documentary. Pets that do not meet the documentation requirements may be placed in quarantine at the owner's expense.

        Contact APQA (qia.go.kr) for the current import permit application procedure and the health certificate format required from Greece.
