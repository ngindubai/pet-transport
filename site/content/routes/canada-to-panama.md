content:
  h1: "Pet Transport Canada to Panama"
  overview: |
    Toronto to Panama City runs direct on Copa Airlines, landing straight into the carrier's home hub at Tocumen. That's a genuine advantage: fewer connections mean fewer chances for a live animal cargo booking to go wrong.

    Panama's own requirements skip the rabies titre test entirely, for any country of origin. The step that catches Canadian owners out is a Panamanian consulate stamp needed on the health certificate after CFIA endorsement, a document most people don't realise they need until close to travel.

  sections:
    - heading: "Why there's no titre test, and what replaces it"
      body: |
        Panama doesn't apply a rabies antibody titre test to pets from any origin country. What it does require: an ISO microchip, rabies vaccination at least 30 days before travel, documented internal and external parasite treatment within 14 days of travel, and a health certificate. Compared with routes elsewhere in this build that hinge on a 90-day titre wait, this is a considerably shorter runway.

    - heading: "The consulate legalisation step"
      body: |
        A CFIA-accredited vet issues the health certificate, but that's not the final step. It also needs to be stamped and signed by a Panamanian consulate or embassy in Canada before the pet travels. Skip this and the certificate won't clear MIDA's inspection at Tocumen on arrival, no matter how correct the underlying paperwork is otherwise.

    - heading: "Copa's Tocumen hub, direct from Toronto"
      body: |
        Copa Airlines operates a direct Toronto-Panama City route, landing at its own home base, which tends to make the arrival-end handling smoother than a route ending at an unfamiliar connecting airport. Air Canada offers seasonal or codeshare alternatives worth checking against Copa's schedule, though Copa's year-round direct service is generally the more dependable option.
