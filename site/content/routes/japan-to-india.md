  overview: |
    Moving a pet from Japan to India is a moderate-complexity route with no titre test requirement and direct flight options from Tokyo Narita (NRT) to Delhi (DEL). The main administrative task is securing the DAHD import clearance from India's Department of Animal Husbandry and Dairying -- apply as early as possible, as processing times vary.

    On the Japan side, your accredited vet issues a health certificate that must be endorsed by the MAFF regional office. That certificate has a 10-day validity window, so time it carefully around your departure date. Your pet needs a current microchip (ISO standard) and a valid rabies vaccination.

    Air India's direct NRT-DEL service is the simplest option: small pets travel in cabin, larger pets as cargo. Allow 6-10 weeks from starting the process. India's import rules can change, so confirm current DAHD requirements with a licensed pet relocation agent before you begin. A good agent will handle the DAHD paperwork and make sure the health certificate format matches the current Indian requirements.
