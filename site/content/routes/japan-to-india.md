  sections:
    - heading: "Japan's export process: MAFF certificate and AQS inspection"
      body: |
        Leaving Japan with a pet is well-organised. Your vet must be authorised by the Ministry of Agriculture, Forestry and Fisheries (MAFF). The MAFF-authorised vet issues the export health certificate confirming the pet is clinically healthy and has current vaccinations. Japan's Animal Quarantine Service (AQS) then conducts an inspection at the departure airport before the pet is accepted onto the flight.

        Japan is recognised as rabies-free by the World Organisation for Animal Health (WOAH). The MAFF certificate notes this status, which is noted by receiving countries. For India's AQCS, it does not remove the quarantine requirement, but it does simplify the health history documentation.

        Allow extra time at the departure airport for the AQS inspection. The inspection is routine for pets leaving Japan and the process is well-established. Book a morning departure if possible to give yourself buffer time.

    - heading: "India's AQCS NOC and the 15-day quarantine"
      body: |
        India requires an NOC (No Objection Certificate) from the Animal Quarantine and Certification Services (AQCS) before your pet can land. This is not an on-arrival form; it must be obtained in advance. The NOC takes approximately five working days to process. Apply well before your travel date to avoid any last-minute problems.

        AQCS quarantine stations are at Delhi, Mumbai, Chennai, Kolkata and Hyderabad. Your pet must arrive at one of these airports. On arrival, AQCS officers inspect the MAFF health certificate, vaccination records and NOC. The 15-day quarantine begins immediately. During this period the pet is under AQCS observation at the airport facility.

        The 15-day quarantine cannot be served at home or at a private kennel. It takes place at the AQCS facility. Some facilities allow owner visits at scheduled times. Confirm the visiting policy with your destination AQCS station before travel. In cases where health concerns arise during observation, the quarantine can be extended to 30 days.

    - heading: "Airlines from Tokyo to Delhi and Mumbai"
      body: |
        Japan Airlines (JAL) and ANA (All Nippon Airways) both operate direct services from Tokyo Narita (NRT) and Tokyo Haneda (HND) to Delhi Indira Gandhi (DEL) and Mumbai (BOM). JAL does not allow pets in the passenger cabin on international flights; all pets travel in the temperature-controlled hold as cargo. ANA operates a similar policy. Both are experienced carriers for live animal shipments on long-haul routes.

        Air India also serves the Tokyo-Delhi route and offers a convenient option given that Delhi is Air India's main hub. Contact the cargo desk of your chosen carrier directly for live animal bookings; pet cargo cannot be arranged through standard ticket booking.

        For cats and very small dogs, some carriers may permit cabin travel in a carrier under the seat; confirm directly. The size and weight limits vary and policies can change. For medium and large dogs, hold cargo is the route to take.

faqs:
  - question: "Does my dog need a titre test to travel from Japan to India?"
    answer: "No. India does not require a FAVN titre test from any country, including Japan. The requirements for a Japanese pet entering India are: ISO microchip, rabies vaccination (more than 1 month before travel, within 12 months), AQCS NOC obtained before departure, and a MAFF health certificate."
  - question: "How long does the quarantine in India last for a pet from Japan?"
    answer: "15 days at an AQCS quarantine facility. This is the standard period for all pets arriving in India. If AQCS officers have any health concerns, the quarantine may extend to 30 days. Japan's rabies-free status does not waive or shorten the quarantine requirement."
  - question: "What is the AQCS NOC and when do I need it?"
    answer: "The NOC (No Objection Certificate) is a clearance from India's Animal Quarantine and Certification Services, required before your pet can land in India. You must apply before the pet's flight departs Japan. Processing takes about 5 working days. Apply at least 2 weeks before travel to have a margin. A pet arriving without an NOC will be held at the airport."
  - question: "Can I fly into any airport in India with my pet from Japan?"
    answer: "No. India only accepts pet arrivals at airports with AQCS quarantine stations: Delhi (DEL), Mumbai (BOM), Chennai (MAA), Kolkata (CCU) and Hyderabad (HYD). Pets arriving at other airports cannot be processed and will not be cleared."
