  h1: "Pet Transport from the United Kingdom to China"
  overview: |
    China has some of the strictest pet import requirements in the world. The process involves a GACC import permit, a FAVN rabies titre test, a mandatory 6-month wait after the titre test blood draw, and a quarantine period on arrival. For dogs, the standard quarantine is 30 days. The minimum total lead time from starting the process to being able to travel is approximately 7-8 months, and using a licensed China pet relocation specialist is strongly recommended.

    The key documents are the GACC import permit (applied for well in advance), a valid health certificate meeting GACC specifications (issued within 10 days of travel), and the titre test result from a GACC-approved laboratory. Additionally, a Customs Declaration must be submitted via the official GACC WeChat mini-programme or app within 24 hours before the flight lands in China.

    Entry is only permitted through designated GACC airports. Beijing Capital (PEK), Shanghai Pudong (PVG), and Guangzhou Baiyun (CAN) are the main options from the UK. Air China and British Airways both fly direct on these routes.

    - heading: "The GACC Customs Declaration: what it is and how to complete it"
      body: |
        Since 2021, China requires all passengers bringing pets to submit a Customs Declaration through the GACC official WeChat mini-programme or the GACC app. This must be completed within 24 hours before the flight's scheduled landing time in China.

        The declaration covers the pet's details, travel document numbers, and health certificate information. On arrival, the GACC officer at the airport matches the declaration to the physical documents and the pet. Missing or incomplete declarations can cause inspection delays.

        Your pet relocation specialist or cargo agent should guide you through this process, but as the owner you are responsible for submitting the declaration. Download the GACC app or have the WeChat mini-programme ready well before travel.
faqs:
  - question: "Why is the wait for China pet travel so long?"
    answer: "China requires a FAVN rabies titre test at a GACC-approved laboratory, followed by a 6-month waiting period from the date the blood was drawn. This 6-month wait is the longest of any country and is set by GACC to ensure the pet's rabies protection is well established before entry. Including the time needed before the titre test (vaccination history, 30-day wait after last vaccination), the total minimum lead time is 7-8 months."
  - question: "Can my pet enter China through any airport?"
    answer: "No. Pets can only enter China through GACC-designated entry ports. As of 2026, these include Beijing Capital (PEK), Shanghai Pudong (PVG), Guangzhou Baiyun (CAN), and Urumqi (URC). Check with GACC or your relocation specialist for the current list, as designated ports can change. Do not book a flight arriving at a non-designated airport."
  - question: "How long is the quarantine in China?"
    answer: "For dogs, the standard quarantine at a GACC-designated facility is 30 days. For cats from certain approved countries, a reduced quarantine of 7 days may apply. The quarantine period can vary based on the pet's origin country and GACC assessment. The owner pays all quarantine facility costs."
  - question: "Do I need a specialist to handle China pet transport from the UK?"
    answer: "A specialist is strongly recommended. The GACC import permit process, the titre test timing, the 6-month wait, the Customs Declaration via WeChat, and the quarantine booking all involve specific steps that are easy to get wrong without experience. An error in timing or documentation can mean the pet is refused entry or quarantined beyond the standard period. Licensed China pet relocation specialists know GACC procedures and can handle the import permit application."
  - question: "Does China have a one-dog-per-household rule?"
    answer: "Yes. Most major Chinese cities, including Beijing and Shanghai, limit households to one dog. This is a domestic local government regulation rather than a GACC import rule, but it applies from the day you are resident. Before deciding to bring a second dog, check the rules for your specific city."
