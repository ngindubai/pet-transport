  h1: "Pet Transport from the UK to China"
  overview: |
    Moving a pet from the UK to China requires working through two regulatory systems: APHA on the UK export side and GACC on the China import side. GACC requires a titre test from an approved laboratory, two lifetime rabies vaccinations, a health certificate in GACC format, and potentially a 30-day quarantine on arrival depending on the documentation outcome. Allow at least four to six months of preparation time.

    One thing to be clear on from the start: China has specific rules about the number of dogs permitted per household (often one per city household) and certain breeds face local restrictions beyond the national GACC regime. Check the rules for your specific destination city before you start the process. The regulatory requirements for arrival are one thing; the practical question of whether your dog can be registered once you arrive is another.

China's system is different: the titre test result is required but the quarantine duration on arrival is potentially avoidable with the right documents.

    - heading: "GACC quarantine: how to reduce the risk of 30 days on arrival"
      body: |
        GACC's standard process includes a 30-day quarantine at a government-designated facility on arrival in China. This is owner-paid and can be significant both financially and for your pet's welfare. However, with a complete and correct documentation set, this quarantine can be waived for pets from certain origins.

        The documents that matter most are the titre test result (GACC-approved lab, 0.5 IU/ml), proof of two lifetime rabies vaccinations with the most recent within 12 months, the ISO microchip confirmation pre-dating all vaccinations, and the GACC-format health certificate issued within 14 days of arrival.

        The honest position is that even with complete documents, GACC inspectors have discretion and policies have shifted over time. An experienced China-side agent who has relationships with the receiving GACC office at your destination airport is the best way to manage this risk. They can brief the GACC office in advance and flag any potential issues before your pet travels.

faqs:
  - question: "Does my pet have to quarantine in China when arriving from the UK?"
    answer: "30-day quarantine at a GACC-designated facility is standard. With a complete documentation set (two lifetime rabies vaccinations, a valid titre test result from a GACC-approved lab, ISO microchip, and GACC-format health certificate), quarantine can be waived in some cases. This is not guaranteed. Use a specialist agent with China-side contacts to manage the process and maximise your chances of a waiver."
  - question: "What is the GACC titre test requirement for UK pets entering China?"
    answer: "GACC requires a rabies antibody titre test using the FAVN method at a GACC-approved laboratory. The minimum passing result is 0.5 IU/ml. Blood must be drawn at least 30 days after the most recent valid rabies vaccination. Not all UK veterinary laboratories are GACC-approved, so confirm the lab with your agent before the test."
  - question: "How many dogs can I bring to China from the UK?"
    answer: "GACC regulates the import of pets nationally, but Chinese cities often have local rules limiting households to one dog. Some cities have breed restrictions. Before you start the process, check the specific regulations for your destination city in China. Pet registration after arrival requires a separate local process."
  - question: "Can my cat travel to China from the UK without quarantine?"
    answer: "Cats are subject to the same GACC import requirements as dogs regarding microchip, vaccination, titre test, and health certificate. The 30-day quarantine applies to cats as well, but can potentially be waived with complete documentation. Cats are not subject to the city-level dog ownership limits, and most Chinese cities have fewer restrictions on cat ownership."
  - question: "Which airline is best for UK to China pet transport?"
    answer: "Air China direct services (LHR-PEK or LHR-PVG) avoid a transit handling step and are the most straightforward routing. British Airways World Cargo via PetAir UK is a solid alternative with a known handling chain from the UK side. Cathay Pacific via Hong Kong adds one transit but has strong live animal handling at HKG."
