content:
  h1: "Pet Transport from the United Kingdom to Zambia"
  overview: |
    Great Britain has a dedicated export certificate for this exact route: EHC 3928, "Export cats and dogs to Zambia," now on its third version, with guidance notes last updated in January 2024. That level of specificity is unusual; most GB export destinations share a generic pet certificate, so Zambia getting its own is a sign the route is established enough to warrant one.

    The certificate itself is the easy half. Zambia's own import permit runs on direct correspondence with the Department of Veterinary Services in Lusaka rather than an online system, and getting that permit in hand before the certificate expires is the part that needs planning.

  sections:
    - heading: "EHC 3928: what applying for it involves"
      body: |
        Exporters in England, Scotland and Wales apply through the government's dedicated online service, which requires a Government Gateway account and a separate Defra account. Applying online has practical advantages: applicants can track the status of an application, always pull the latest certificate version, and copy a previous application if shipping a second pet later.

        The vet who signs the certificate has to be registered too, and APHA checks that registration before the vet can act. Northern Ireland exporters use DAERA's own DECS system instead. None of this is unusual for a GB export certificate, but it does mean the vet appointment can't simply be booked the week before the flight; the registration step has to happen first.

    - heading: "What Zambia wants on the other end"
      body: |
        Zambia's Department of Veterinary Services (formally the Department of Research & Specialist Services) issues the import permit that has to be obtained before the pet travels. There's no published online portal for this. The usual route is direct contact with the department's Lusaka headquarters at Mulungushi House, and the import form comes back with instructions for the vet who'll complete the corresponding export paperwork on the UK side.

        On arrival, the importer has to notify the nearest Zambian Veterinary Officer so the pet and its documents can be inspected. Skipping that step, even with otherwise compliant paperwork, is a documented ground for the animal to be held.

    - heading: "Vaccination timing and the flight"
      body: |
        Zambia's rabies vaccination window is specific: between 30 days and 12 months before entry, not the open-ended "current vaccination" wording used in some countries' rules. A booster given too early, or one that's about to expire on arrival, can fall outside that window and hold up entry.

        On the flying side, nobody operates London to Lusaka nonstop. Ethiopian Airlines via Addis Ababa carries small pets in the cabin and larger ones as cargo, with 1-2 flights daily on the London-Addis leg. Kenya Airways, connecting via its nonstop KQ100 Heathrow-Nairobi service, only carries pets as cargo. South African Airways' historical Johannesburg-Lusaka link is worth checking too, schedule permitting.
