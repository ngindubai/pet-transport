content:
  h1: "Pet Transport from Iceland to the United Kingdom"
  overview: |
    Three different documents will clear an Icelandic pet through UK border control: a pet passport, an Animal Health Certificate, or a GB-specific pet health certificate. That range exists because Iceland sits on the UK's Part 1 listed table, the same tier as the EU member states, Norway and Switzerland. No titre test. No quarantine on arrival.

    The one detail worth knowing before the first vet visit: MAST issues a certificate built specifically for non-commercial travel into Great Britain, a different document from the EU-standard form used for a pet's onward journey to France or Germany. Asking for the right form the first time saves a second appointment.

  sections:
    - heading: "An asymmetric route: easy out, strict in"
      body: |
        A pet leaving Iceland for the UK needs a microchip, a current rabies vaccination held for at least 21 days, and a MAST-endorsed certificate. Dogs also need the tapeworm treatment, timed to the UK arrival window rather than the Iceland departure. That is the whole regulatory picture on the export side.

        This is the mirror image of the UK-to-Iceland direction covered elsewhere on this site, where the same pet moving the other way faces a mandatory 14-day quarantine and a battery of blood tests regardless of its documentation. Iceland guards its own border tightly. It does not extend the same caution to pets leaving the island.

    - heading: "The certificate MAST actually issues for this route"
      body: |
        Because the UK sits outside the EU, Iceland's export process for a UK-bound pet runs on a document called the pet health certificate for the non-commercial movement to Great Britain, not the AHC form used for EU destinations. A MAST-accredited vet completes it, MAST endorses it, and the whole thing is valid for 10 days up to the UK entry check.

        Booking the appointment is done through MAST's own portal, form 2.40, or by email to petimport@mast.is if the owner has no Icelandic ID number. MAST runs appointments out of five offices: Reykjavik, Selfoss, Akureyri, Saudarkrokur and Egilsstadir. Picking the nearest one and booking a few weeks ahead avoids a scramble close to the flight date.

    - heading: "Two nonstop carriers, both cargo only"
      body: |
        Most routes out of Iceland on this site rely on a single nonstop carrier. This one has two: Icelandair and British Airways both fly Keflavik to Heathrow direct, Icelandair around 14 times a week. Neither carries a pet as checked baggage any more, Icelandair having dropped that option from every route on 1 November 2024 and BA never having offered it in the first place.

        In practice this means booking through Icelandair Cargo or through IAG Cargo, BA's cargo division, rather than at a normal passenger check-in desk. Having two carrier options on the same city pair is a genuine advantage if one has no live animal space on your preferred date.

faqs:
  - question: "Does a pet need a titre test to travel from Iceland to the UK?"
    answer: "No. Iceland is a Part 1 listed country under UK pet travel rules, the same tier as the EU member states, Norway and Switzerland. A microchip, current rabies vaccination held for at least 21 days, and the correct certificate are all that is required. No blood test applies."
  - question: "What certificate does my vet in Iceland need to complete for UK travel?"
    answer: "MAST's pet health certificate for the non-commercial movement to Great Britain, a document built specifically for this route. It is not the EU-standard Animal Health Certificate form used for a pet travelling on to an EU country, so ask for it by name when booking the vet appointment."
  - question: "Is there a quarantine for pets entering the UK from Iceland?"
    answer: "No. Iceland's own mandatory 14-day quarantine applies only to pets entering Iceland. A pet leaving Iceland for the UK, with complete documentation, faces no quarantine on arrival at Heathrow."
  - question: "Which airlines fly pets from Keflavik to London?"
    answer: "Icelandair and British Airways both operate Keflavik to Heathrow nonstop, a rare case of two direct carriers on an Iceland route. Neither accepts pets as checked baggage: Icelandair moves them as cargo through Icelandair Cargo, and BA through its cargo division, IAG Cargo."
  - question: "Do dogs need a tapeworm treatment for this route?"
    answer: "Yes. A vet must administer praziquantel or an equivalent product 24 to 120 hours before the dog's arrival in Great Britain, and record it on the certificate. This is a UK entry requirement applying to every dog regardless of where it started, not an Iceland-specific rule."
