  h1: "Pet Transport from United States to Uzbekistan: 2026 Guide"
  overview: |
    No titre test. That's the single fact that separates this route from most of the unlisted-country pathways US owners are used to researching for an EU move. Uzbekistan accepts a US-origin dog or cat on vaccination timing alone, provided the microchip goes in first.

    What isn't settled is whether a separate import permit applies. Public sources split on this: some describe straightforward accompanied entry, a 2026 process review says the Committee of Veterinary and Livestock Development expects a vetgov.uz permit application before travel. Given the stakes of getting a YMYL move wrong, the honest answer is to confirm directly rather than assume the lighter path.

  sections:
    - heading: "Why there's no titre test, and what that doesn't mean"
      body: |
        Uzbekistan doesn't classify the US as a high-rabies-risk origin, so it skips the FAVN or ELISA titre test that dominates the timeline on routes into the EU or UK. What it still insists on is sequencing: the microchip has to be implanted before or at the same appointment as the rabies vaccination, and the vaccination itself has to fall between 30 days and 12 months before arrival. Miss either window and the certificate doesn't hold up at the Tashkent veterinary post.

        This lighter pathway is the reason the whole move can realistically run in 6 to 10 weeks rather than the 4-to-6-month timelines common elsewhere in this series, provided the USDA paperwork and the open permit question are both settled early.

    - heading: "The import permit question nobody has fully answered"
      body: |
        Some accounts of this route describe a pet arriving with its owner, bags and all, on the strength of the health certificate alone. A more recent 2026 process review states plainly that the Committee expects an import permit filed through vetgov.uz ahead of travel. Both can't be fully right for every case, and there's no way to know from outside which applies to a given booking without asking.

        Treat the permit as likely required until the Committee confirms otherwise directly. It costs little to apply and ask; it costs a great deal to arrive in Tashkent and find the paperwork short.
