content:
  h1: "Pet Transport from Namibia to the United Kingdom"
  overview: |
    No airline flies directly between Windhoek and London. Every pet booking connects through Johannesburg on Airlink and British Airways, or through Frankfurt on Discover Airlines, since Air Namibia folded in February 2021 and its planned successor, Namibia Air, still hadn't launched as of mid-2026.

    Namibia doesn't appear on Great Britain's listed-country tables either, so a rabies titre test and a fixed 3-calendar-month wait apply, the same pathway used for most of Africa outside Mauritius and Seychelles. Realistically, this route takes 18 to 24 weeks from a standing start.

  sections:
    - heading: "Why the titre test sets your travel date"
      body: |
        Once the microchip and vaccination are confirmed, the titre blood draw can happen no sooner than 30 days later. Whatever the result, Great Britain then requires a further 3 calendar months to pass before the pet can travel, counted from the day blood was drawn, not the day the lab confirms a pass. Owners who wait for the result before treating the clock as running usually lose two to four weeks they hadn't planned for.

        Add it up from a standing start, microchip, vaccination, 30-day wait, blood draw, 3-month wait, paperwork, and 18 to 24 weeks is realistic. A pet that's already current on its rabies vaccination and just needs the titre test and wait can sometimes move a little faster.

    - heading: "Getting the blood sample to an accredited lab"
      body: |
        Namibia doesn't have a domestic laboratory carrying the international accreditation GOV.UK expects for a titre result. In practice, blood drawn by a Namibian vet is sent to the Onderstepoort Veterinary Institute (OVI) in Pretoria, South Africa, the OIE-recognised reference laboratory that handles FAVN testing for most of Southern Africa. It's a well-worn route for South African and Namibian pet exports alike, but it adds postal transit time on top of the lab's own processing.

        Budget three to five weeks between the blood draw and a confirmed result once courier time to Pretoria is factored in. That window usually falls inside the 3-month wait anyway, so it rarely stretches the overall timeline unless the sample is delayed at the courier or customs stage.

    - heading: "No direct flight: routing through Johannesburg or Frankfurt"
      body: |
        Air Namibia, the country's former national carrier, stopped flying in February 2021. Its planned successor, Namibia Air, was approved in 2024 but still hadn't launched by mid-2026, with officials targeting a start before the end of the year. Until it does, every pet leaving Windhoek connects onward through another hub.

        The standard routing is Airlink to O.R. Tambo in Johannesburg, then British Airways' daily service into Heathrow, with IAG Cargo handling the pet on the long-haul leg. Discover Airlines' year-round Frankfurt service is the alternative starting point for owners who'd rather route through continental Europe.

    - heading: "What Namibia asks for on the way out"
      body: |
        Exporting a pet from Namibia is comparatively light-touch. There's no separate export permit to apply for. A state veterinarian from the Directorate of Veterinary Services, or a private vet with the Directorate's endorsement, checks the animal and signs off a health certificate reformatted to match GOV.UK's required layout.

        That certificate confirms the pet is healthy and legally exportable. It says nothing about whether the titre test has cleared or the 3-month wait has ended. Those are British requirements running on their own separate clock, and they'll always be the limiting factor on this route, not the Namibian paperwork.
