  sections:
    - heading: "Armenia's rules: simpler than most non-EU destinations"
      body: |
        The State Service for Food Safety (SSFS) in Armenia has straightforward rules for personal companion pets arriving from EU countries. A current rabies vaccination (at least 21 days before entry) and a health certificate from the origin country's competent authority are the main requirements. There is no titre test, no formal import permit for one or two pets, and no routine quarantine.

        This differs noticeably from many non-EU destinations, where a titre test and months-long preparation window are the norm. For a pet from Lithuania with a current vaccination, the actual preparation for Armenian entry is measured in weeks, not months.

        The SSFS does maintain a restricted breed list including Pit Bull Terriers, Japanese Tosas, and Dogo Argentinos. If your dog is one of these types or a mix that could be classified under them, confirm with the SSFS (ssfs.am) before booking. Armenia limits personal pet imports to one or two animals without a permit.

    - heading: "The VMVT export certificate from Lithuania"
      body: |
        For travel within the EU, Lithuanian pet owners rely on the EU pet passport. For Armenia, that document does not carry any legal weight at the border. The required document is an official export health certificate issued by a VMVT-authorised official veterinarian, endorsed by the Valstybine maisto ir veterinarijos tarnyba (VMVT) at vmvt.lt.

        Contact the VMVT before the vet appointment to confirm the process and to identify an official vet in your area. The certificate must be issued within 10 days of travel, so timing the appointment correctly is important. Check with the VMVT whether Armenia requires any specific format or wording in the certificate.

    - heading: "Getting from Vilnius to Yerevan"
      body: |
        Vilnius Airport (VNO) is a smaller regional airport with more limited cargo connections than Riga or Warsaw. The most practical option for pet cargo from Vilnius to Yerevan routes via Warsaw with LOT Polish Airlines, then from Warsaw onwards to Istanbul with Turkish Airlines Cargo, and finally IST to Yerevan Zvartnots (EVN).

        Alternatively, airBaltic connects from VNO through Riga to Frankfurt or Amsterdam, from where Turkish Airlines or Lufthansa Cargo can reach Yerevan.

        Turkish Airlines Cargo at Istanbul Ataturk (IST) handles live animals and has regular capacity to Yerevan. Book cargo early and confirm live animal acceptance on every segment.
