  h1: "Pet Transport Portugal to South Korea: 2026 Guide"
  overview: "The Korean QIAS-format certificate, not the familiar EU pet passport, is the document that actually matters here. Portugal's EU membership does remove the FAVN titre test, but South Korea's Animal and Plant Quarantine Agency still runs a 1-3 day inspection at Incheon on arrival, and it wants its own paperwork, signed by a DGAV-authorised official vet rather than any private practice."
  sections:
    - heading: "What Portugal's EU status changes, and what it doesn't"
      body: |
        South Korea's quarantine authority treats EU member states, Portugal included, as lower-risk origins with recognised veterinary certification systems. The practical benefit is real: no FAVN titre test, and none of the months-long waiting period that applies to pets arriving from rabies-endemic countries outside the EU.

        What doesn't change is the inspection on arrival. QIAS still holds EU-origin pets for a health check and documentation review at Incheon, typically 1 to 3 days rather than the 5 to 10 days applied to non-EU pets, but not skipped entirely. Budget for at least one night at the QIAS facility, and check whether visiting during the hold is possible on the dates you're travelling.

        The certificate itself needs care. A DGAV-authorised official veterinarian, not just any vet in Lisbon, has to issue it on the Korean import template and get it endorsed by DGAV. It's valid for 10 days from issue, which means booking the appointment close to your travel date rather than weeks in advance.
    - heading: "Routing from Lisbon: no direct option, two workable connections"
      body: |
        Lisbon has no long-haul service to Seoul, so every pet cargo booking runs through a European hub. Lufthansa's Frankfurt connection is the more established option: FRA-ICN runs nonstop most days at around 11 hours 20 minutes, and Frankfurt's live-animal handling facility is one of the busiest in Europe.

        KLM via Amsterdam is a close second, with a nonstop AMS-ICN sector of about 11 hours 50 minutes. Because TAP, KLM and Korean Air are all SkyTeam partners, the interline cargo handoff at Amsterdam can be simpler to arrange than a cross-alliance connection through Frankfurt.

        Either way, book both legs together rather than separately. A pet transport agent who handles interline live-animal bookings regularly is worth the fee on a two-leg journey like this one.
