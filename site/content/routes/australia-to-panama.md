content:
  h1: "Pet Transport Australia to Panama"
  overview: |
    Panama's own rules stay simple regardless of where the pet is coming from: no titre test, no import permit, just vaccination, parasite treatment, and a health certificate. What adds real complexity here is distance. Getting from Australia to Panama City means a transpacific leg to a US gateway, then an onward connection south with Copa Airlines into its Tocumen hub.

  sections:
    - heading: "Panama's requirements don't change for a long-haul origin"
      body: |
        No rabies titre test applies here, whatever the origin country. An ISO microchip, rabies vaccination at least 30 days before travel, documented parasite treatment within 14 days, and a health certificate cover the requirement. The certificate also needs legalisation by a Panamanian consulate or embassy in Australia, a step distinct from the DAFF endorsement and easy to miss if you're not expecting it.

    - heading: "Two legs, not one"
      body: |
        Qantas covers the long transpacific run into a US gateway such as Los Angeles or Dallas. From there, Copa Airlines picks up the connection south into Panama City, landing at its own Tocumen hub. Confirm the live animal transfer arrangements at the US connecting airport well ahead of booking, since this is a genuine two-carrier itinerary rather than a single through-booking.

    - heading: "Getting the timing right across a longer journey"
      body: |
        Both the parasite treatment (within 14 days of travel) and the health certificate need to remain valid across the entire multi-leg trip, not just the first departure from Australia. Book the full itinerary before finalising either the vet appointment or the consulate legalisation appointment, so both dates align with the actual arrival date in Panama.
