  overview: |
    Two direct flights a day between a rabies-free island nation and the Gulf, and neither Emirates nor Etihad has any reason to make this complicated. MOCCAE names Seychelles outright on its published low-risk country list, so the titre test that dominates most unlisted-origin routes into the UAE simply doesn't apply here.

  sections:
    - heading: "Why MOCCAE treats Seychelles differently"
      body: |
        MOCCAE's import system splits every origin country into two tiers: a named list of low-risk countries and everything else, treated as unlisted-risk and subject to a rabies antibody titre test. Seychelles sits directly on that low-risk list, alongside destinations like the UK, Japan and Australia, a reasonable reflection of its genuine rabies-free status.

        The practical effect: no titre test, no international lab courier, and a minimum import age of 12 weeks rather than the 15-week threshold that applies to unlisted-risk origins. What still applies in full is the standard vaccination package and the MOCCAE import permit, both of which take a matter of weeks rather than months to arrange.

    - heading: "Flying direct from Mahe"
      body: |
        Both of the Gulf's major carriers fly nonstop into Mahe, which is unusual for a route this size and works entirely in the owner's favour. Emirates' Dubai service and Etihad's Abu Dhabi service both include dedicated live-animal handling at their home hubs, so there's no third-country transfer to plan around, no second set of paperwork checks, and no added transit stress for the animal.
