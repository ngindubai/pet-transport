  h1: "Pet Transport Norway to Slovenia: 2026 Guide"
  overview: "Norway to Slovenia involves two practical considerations. First, Norway's status as a listed non-EU country means your pet needs an official health certificate rather than an EU pet passport. Second, Ljubljana is a small airport without direct cargo connections from Oslo, so a hub connection via Vienna or Frankfurt is the standard route.   sections:
    - heading: "Two-leg logistics: Oslo to Ljubljana via Vienna or Frankfurt"
      body: |
        There are no direct scheduled cargo flights from Oslo Gardermoen (OSL) to Ljubljana (LJU). The move requires a connecting hub:

        Via Vienna (VIE): SAS or Norwegian takes the pet from Oslo to Vienna, where Austrian Airlines connects to Ljubljana. The VIE to LJU leg is under an hour. Vienna is geographically close to Ljubljana and Austrian Airlines is familiar with the route.

        Via Frankfurt (FRA): Lufthansa connects Oslo to Frankfurt with onward service to Ljubljana. Frankfurt's Animal Lounge is one of the stronger cargo transit facilities in Europe. The FRA to LJU hop is approximately 45 minutes.

        For small pets in the cabin, either hub works with the standard carrier cabin pet policies. For large dogs in cargo, booking the entire route through a single cargo chain, or at least coordinating between the two legs, reduces the risk of miscommunication at the hub.

        Some owners who need to get a large dog to Slovenia prefer flying to Vienna and completing the last leg by car. Oslo to VIE by air, then VIE to Ljubljana by car (approximately 3.5 hours), avoids a second cargo leg entirely.

    - heading: "Norwegian health certificate: what UVHVVR accepts"
      body: |
        Slovenia's UVHVVR handles intra-EU and listed-country pet arrivals. For a Norwegian pet, they accept the official health certificate in the EU listed-country format. The certificate must come from a Mattilsynet-authorised official vet in Norway and cover the standard requirements: ISO 15-digit microchip implanted before the rabies vaccination, current rabies vaccination, and vet identification details.

        No import permit is required. No titre test is required. No quarantine applies. The document check at Ljubljana Airport is the same in practice as for an EU pet passport arrival: microchip scan matched to the certificate, vaccination dates verified.

        The main thing to sort in advance is identifying a Mattilsynet-authorised official vet in your area in Norway. Not all vets hold this official status, and the certificate must be from an authorised vet for Ljubljana's UVHVVR to accept it.
