  h1: "Pet Transport from the United Kingdom to the Dominican Republic"
  overview: |
    The UK to Dominican Republic route is manageable with 8-12 weeks of preparation. It does not require a rabies titre test (the Dominican Republic does not mandate this from UK pets), which significantly shortens the timeline compared to many international routes.

    The key steps are: confirm your pet's microchip and rabies vaccination are current, arrange a Brucellosis test for dogs, apply for the Dominican Republic import permit from MIDA, and get the UK Animal Health Certificate from an Official Vet.

    There is no direct UK-to-Dominican-Republic commercial flight. The most straightforward pet cargo routing goes via Madrid on Iberia, keeping the connection clean and avoiding the complications of a US transit.
  sections:
    - heading: "What the Dominican Republic requires from UK pets"
      body: |
        The Dominican Republic's Ministry of Agriculture (MIDA) oversees pet imports. The requirements are:
        a current microchip (ISO 11784/11785), current rabies vaccination, a Brucellosis test for dogs with a negative result within 30 days of travel, and an official UK health certificate endorsed by APHA.

        The import permit from MIDA is required before travel. Allow a minimum of 4-6 weeks for this to be processed. Apply to MIDA (agricultura.gob.do) or work through an in-country agent or pet relocation specialist who handles Dominican Republic paperwork regularly.

        On arrival at Santo Domingo Las Americas International Airport (SDQ) or Puerto Plata (POP), a MIDA veterinary inspector will check documentation. Provided everything is correct, there is no routine quarantine.
    - heading: "Comparing routing options from the UK to the DR"
      body: |
        The most straightforward route for pet cargo is via Madrid. Iberia operates regular London Heathrow to Madrid flights with connections onwards to Santo Domingo. The Madrid connection is well-established for pet cargo and avoids the complications of a US transit.

        American Airlines routing via Miami or New York is possible but significantly more complex. Any pet transiting through the USA as cargo is subject to US APHIS import requirements, even if the US is just a connection. This adds documentation, cost, and risk. Use the US routing only if direct Spain-via options are unavailable.

        Air Europa operates a similar Spain hub routing and is worth comparing on price, particularly if you are flexible on dates. Confirm cargo pet acceptance with Air Europa Cargo directly before booking.

        Whichever route you choose, book cargo space early. Direct the cargo agent to the import permit number from MIDA so documentation can be cross-referenced.
    - heading: "The Animal Health Certificate: what UK owners need to know"
      body: |
        Pets leaving the UK need an Animal Health Certificate (AHC) issued by a UK APHA-authorised Official Vet. This is not your standard registered veterinarian - you need an OV (Official Vet) who holds APHA authorisation for the destination country.

        The AHC is valid for 10 days from the date of the vet examination, so it must be one of the last things you complete before travel. Do not get it weeks in advance.

        Find an APHA-authorised Official Vet through the APHA official vet finder at gov.uk or through the RCVS register. Some larger veterinary practices have OVs in-house; others refer to specialist OV services. Book the OV appointment early, as availability varies and some practices need several weeks' notice.
faqs:
  - question: "Does my pet need a rabies titre test to enter the Dominican Republic from the UK?"
    answer: "No. The Dominican Republic does not currently require a rabies titre test for pets from the United Kingdom. You need current rabies vaccination, microchip, Brucellosis test (for dogs), import permit from MIDA, and an official UK Animal Health Certificate. Always confirm with MIDA before travel as requirements can change."
  - question: "What is the import permit process for the Dominican Republic?"
    answer: "You apply to the Dominican Republic Ministry of Agriculture (MIDA, agricultura.gob.do). The permit specifies the conditions your pet must meet and is required before travel. Allow 4-6 weeks for processing. Working with a pet transport agent who regularly handles DR imports can speed this process up."
  - question: "Is there a direct flight from the UK to the Dominican Republic for pet transport?"
    answer: "There is no direct UK-to-Dominican Republic commercial flight that carries pet cargo. The most commonly used routing is via Madrid on Iberia or Air Europa. American Airlines connections via Miami are possible but introduce US APHIS transit requirements, adding complexity."
  - question: "What is a Brucellosis test and does my cat need one?"
    answer: "Brucella canis is a bacterial infection. The Dominican Republic requires dogs to have a negative Brucellosis blood test within 30 days of travel. Cats are not required to have this test. Ask your UK vet to arrange the test and ensure the result is included in your documentation."
  - question: "How do I find an Official Vet in the UK for the Animal Health Certificate?"
    answer: "APHA-authorised Official Vets are listed through the APHA official vet finder and the RCVS register. Not every vet practice has an OV. Book early - some OVs have limited appointment slots and you need the certificate within 10 days of your travel date."
