content:
  h1: "Pet Transport from the United Arab Emirates to El Salvador"
  overview: |
    Every pet leaving the UAE needs a MOCCAE export certificate, without exception, and that certificate only becomes valid once a government veterinarian has physically inspected the animal at the airport. It's a firmer requirement than most origin countries impose, and it applies whether the destination is San Salvador or anywhere else in the world.

    El Salvador's side of this move asks for much less. No titre test, no routine quarantine, just a health certificate and a MAG import authorisation that costs USD 9.04 and, if there's no time to apply ahead, can be finished at the airport on the day the pet lands.

  sections:
    - heading: "The MOCCAE export certificate: an inspection, not just a form"
      body: |
        MOCCAE's export process is applied for online, but the certificate isn't finished there. A government veterinarian has to physically examine the pet at the airport before departure, confirming the paperwork matches the animal in front of them. That inspection appointment needs to sit in the travel-day plan, not as an afterthought once the flight is booked.

        The certificate itself, once issued, is valid for 30 days, which gives more breathing room than some destination-side documents on this route, but the inspection step means the whole process can't be rushed into the final 24 hours before a flight.

    - heading: "El Salvador's import side stays simple"
      body: |
        MAG doesn't ask a UAE-origin pet for a titre test, and states no routine quarantine for an arrival with correct paperwork. The import authorisation costs USD 9.04, and can be applied for at MAG's Santa Tecla office, online, or right at the airport on arrival if that suits the schedule better.

        The genuine complication on this route sits in the middle, not at either end: there is no realistic one-stop flight from Dubai to San Salvador, and a Miami or European connection adds real hours to an already long journey.
