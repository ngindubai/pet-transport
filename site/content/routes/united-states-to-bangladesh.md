  sections:
    - heading: "What the Department of Livestock Services requires"
      body: |
        Bangladesh's Department of Livestock Services (DLS) handles pet import permits. Apply at least 6 weeks before travel with your pet's microchip details, vaccination history, titre test result, and the intended entry date at Hazrat Shahjalal International Airport in Dhaka. The permit must be confirmed before you book cargo.

        The titre test requirement adds time to the preparation process. Blood must be drawn at least 30 days after your pet's most recent rabies vaccination, and laboratory results take 2-3 weeks. Running the permit application and titre test process in parallel saves time, but plan for the titre test to be completed before the permit application goes in, so the result can be included in the application.

    - heading: "Routing via connections and the 7-day quarantine"
      body: |
        No airlines operate nonstop services from the US to Dhaka. The most common connections are via Dubai (Emirates), Doha (Qatar Airways), or via a Southeast Asian hub. Total crate time from a US East Coast hub to Dhaka is typically 18-24 hours including the connection. Before booking, confirm with the airline's cargo team that live animals are accepted on both legs of the journey, as aircraft type restrictions apply.

        On arrival in Dhaka, your pet enters a minimum 7-day quarantine managed by the DLS. The quarantine facility is at the airport. The owner is responsible for all costs throughout the quarantine period. You will not be able to collect your pet on the day they land. After 7 days of compliant quarantine, DLS clears the pet for release.

faqs:
  - question: "Is a titre test required for American pets entering Bangladesh?"
    answer: "Bangladesh requires a titre test for pets from most countries. Since the USA is not classified as a rabies-free country, a titre test is expected to apply to US pets. Confirm this with Bangladesh's Department of Livestock Services before starting preparations. The blood draw must be at least 30 days after your pet's most recent rabies vaccination, and the result must be at least 0.5 IU/ml."

  - question: "Are there any direct flights from the USA to Bangladesh?"
    answer: "No airline currently operates nonstop flights from the US to Dhaka. All routing involves at least one connection, typically via the Gulf (Dubai, Doha) or South/Southeast Asia. Emirates and Qatar Airways are the most commonly used carriers for pet cargo on this route. Biman Bangladesh Airlines operates connections from the US. Confirm that live animals are accepted on both the US-to-hub and hub-to-Dhaka legs, as policies vary by aircraft type."

  - question: "What does the 7-day quarantine involve in Bangladesh?"
    answer: "All imported pets spend a minimum of 7 days in a quarantine facility managed by the Department of Livestock Services at Hazrat Shahjalal International Airport. The quarantine cannot be shortened and the owner pays the facility fees throughout. You will be notified when the quarantine period ends and your pet can be collected. During quarantine, DLS staff monitor the animal's health and verify documentation."

  - question: "How do I get a USDA health certificate for Bangladesh?"
    answer: "Book an appointment with a USDA-accredited veterinarian (not all vets are accredited; search the USDA APHIS website for accredited vets in your area). The vet conducts a health examination and completes the health certificate. You then submit it to USDA APHIS Veterinary Services for endorsement, which costs $38 per certificate and takes 2-10 business days depending on submission method. The endorsed certificate is valid for 10 days from issue."
