  h1: "Pet Transport from Maldives to Spain: 2026 Guide"
  overview: |
    Madrid-Barajas doesn't run one live-animal reception point, it runs four, split between Iberia, Swissport and World Wide Flight Services. Which one your cat passes through depends entirely on which ground handler the connecting airline uses that day, a detail most guides to this route skip entirely.

    The regulatory sequence doesn't change regardless of which gate handles the paperwork. Spain treats the Maldives as an EU-unlisted country, so microchip, rabies vaccination, a titre test at an EU-approved lab and a 90-day wait from the blood draw date all apply before a cat can legally travel.

  sections:
    - heading: "A cat route, not a dog ban"
      body: |
        Nothing stops a resident dog leaving the Maldives under Maldivian law. What limits this route to cats is the Maldives Customs Service's own ban on dog imports, leaving almost no resident dog population to begin with, so Spain's own PPP list of restricted breeds barely comes up in real enquiries.
    - heading: "Exporting from Male with no ministry to sign off"
      body: |
        There's no export permit and no government counter-signature built into a routine departure. A private Male vet issues the certificate, and agents typically accept it as issued. Finding cargo capacity out of Velana International, which handles far more resort freight than live pets, is usually the harder part.
    - heading: "The titre test and Spain's 90-day wait"
      body: |
        Because the Maldives sits outside Implementing Regulation (EU) 2026/636's approved list, Commission Delegated Regulation (EU) 2026/131 requires blood drawn at least 30 days after vaccination to clear 0.5 IU/ml at an EU-approved lab, with the 90-day wait counted from the draw date, not the result. Get that detail wrong and a flight gets booked before the cat is legally cleared to enter Spain.
    - heading: "Four gates at Madrid, and why it matters which one you get"
      body: |
        Madrid-Barajas has four separate Border Control Posts approved for live animal arrivals, operated by three different handlers: Iberia, Swissport and World Wide Flight Services. Your agent should confirm which one your flight routes through before travel day, since paperwork handling and collection logistics vary slightly between them.
