content:
  h1: "Pet Transport India to Indonesia: 2026 Guide"
  overview: "The AQCS export NOC has one of the tightest deadlines on this entire route: lodge documents at least 7 days before travel, and the certificate itself expires 10 days after issue. Line that up against Barantin's rekomendasi permit, which runs on its own 30-day clock once granted, and the India to Indonesia move is really two bureaucratic timers that both need to land on the same travel date."

  sections:
    - heading: "AQCS clearance: the 10-day countdown from India"
      body: |
        The Animal Quarantine and Certification Service, under the Department of Animal Husbandry and Dairying, runs regional offices in Delhi, Mumbai, Chennai and Kolkata. Documents, including two 4x6 inch photographs of the pet and the DAHD vet's health certificate, must be lodged with an appointment at least 7 days before the flight.

        The resulting export No Objection Certificate is valid for only 10 days. If your flight slips outside that window, the whole AQCS appointment has to be repeated. Dogs must be at least 3 months old and vaccinated against rabies more than one month but within 12 months of the actual embarkation date; get the vaccination timing wrong and the certificate cannot be issued.
    - heading: "Routing BOM or DEL to CGK: no direct flight, three real options"
      body: |
        No airline flies Mumbai or Delhi to Jakarta non-stop for live cargo. Singapore Airlines Cargo via Singapore has the shortest final leg, under two hours from SIN to CGK, which matters for an animal that has already spent most of a day travelling. Qatar Airways Cargo via Doha and Emirates SkyCargo via Dubai are both regularly used alternatives from either Indian gateway.

        Book cargo space several weeks ahead and confirm both legs accept live animals for your specific dates, since Jakarta's seasonal heat embargoes can affect acceptance in the hottest months. An agent experienced with the AQCS process on the Indian side and the Barantin process on the Indonesian side is worth the fee on a route with this many moving parts.

faqs:
  - question: "How far in advance do I need to book the AQCS appointment?"
    answer: "Lodge your documents with the AQCS regional office at least 7 days before your flight. Since the resulting export certificate is valid for only 10 days, this appointment should be booked against a confirmed flight date, not an estimated one."
  - question: "Can a puppy travel from India to Indonesia?"
    answer: "Only once it meets both countries' minimum ages. AQCS requires the rabies vaccination to be at least one month old and dogs to be at least 3 months old at export. Indonesia separately requires dogs to be at least 6 months old on arrival, which is the binding constraint for a young puppy."
  - question: "Does my pet need to be in Delhi or Mumbai for this process?"
    answer: "AQCS has regional offices in Delhi, Mumbai, Chennai and Kolkata, so the export side does not require travel to a single city. The airline departure, however, is realistically from BOM or DEL for the strongest cargo connections to Jakarta via Singapore, Doha or Dubai."
  - question: "What if the FAVN titre result comes back below 0.5 IU/ml?"
    answer: "A booster vaccination is required, followed by a fresh 30-day wait before a repeat blood draw. This adds roughly two months to the timeline, so factor in a buffer if your pet has never had a rabies titre test before."
  - question: "Is quarantine shorter for pets with all their paperwork correct?"
    answer: "Yes. Barantin's standard is 7 to 14 days for a complete, compliant document set, regardless of origin country. Missing or incorrect documentation, including an expired AQCS or DAHD certificate, extends the quarantine well beyond that."
  - question: "Which Indian airport has the best cargo connections to Jakarta?"
