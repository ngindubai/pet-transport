  overview: |
    India to Indonesia is a complex corridor from both ends. In India, AQCS/DAHD issues the health certificate and provides export clearance at the departure airport. In Indonesia, BARANTAN issues the import permit and requires a minimum 14-day quarantine on arrival. Singapore Airlines via Singapore is the most practical routing option, given the frequency of Singapore-Jakarta services. Bali has additional restrictions as a declared rabies-free island. Use a specialist agent for this route.

        | Singapore Airlines | SIN | DEL/BOM-SIN-CGK/DPS | SQ Cargo handles live animals. Excellent SIN-Indonesia frequency |

        Singapore Airlines via Singapore is the most commonly used routing for India-to-Indonesia pet cargo, given Singapore's proximity to Jakarta and the frequency of SIN-CGK services. Confirm live animal acceptance on every leg before booking.

    - heading: "AQCS export process and BARANTAN quarantine"
      body: |
        Exporting a pet from India requires an AQCS-endorsed health certificate and export clearance at the departure airport. The AQCS inspector at the airport verifies the health certificate, microchip, and vaccination records before allowing the pet to board cargo.

        On arrival at Jakarta Soekarno-Hatta (CGK) or Bali Ngurah Rai (DPS), pets undergo a minimum 14-day quarantine at a BARANTAN-approved facility. Bali's status as a declared rabies-free island means that entry via DPS may involve additional documentation requirements.

        Contact BARANTAN (pertanian.go.id) for the current import permit application and the health certificate format required from India.
