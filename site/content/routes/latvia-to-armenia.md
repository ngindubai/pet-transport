  sections:
    - heading: "Armenia's import process: what to expect"
      body: |
        Armenia's companion pet import rules are administered by the State Service for Food Safety (SSFS) at ssfs.am. For personal pets from Latvia, the process is uncomplicated: a current rabies vaccination with a minimum of 21 days elapsed since the vaccination date, and an official health certificate from the Latvian authorities. No titre test. No quarantine.

        On arrival at Yerevan Zvartnots International Airport (EVN), a border veterinary officer from the SSFS inspects the certificate and vaccination record. One or two personal pets enter without an import permit. If you are moving three or more animals, contact the SSFS before planning the journey.

        Armenia maintains restrictions on certain dog breeds. Pit Bull Terriers, Japanese Tosas, and Dogo Argentinos are on the list. If your dog could be classified under any of these types, check with the SSFS before booking.

    - heading: "Exporting from Latvia: the PVD certificate"
      body: |
        Latvia uses the EU pet passport for intra-EU travel. For Armenia, that document is not recognised at the destination border. Your Latvian vet needs to issue an official export health certificate, which is then endorsed by the Partikas un veterinarais dienests (PVD, Food and Veterinary Service) at pvd.gov.lv.

        The PVD website lists authorised official vets. The certificate must be issued within 10 days of travel, so the vet appointment needs to be timed close to departure. Before the appointment, confirm with the PVD whether Armenia requires any specific certificate format or additional fields.

    - heading: "From Riga to Yerevan"
      body: |
        Riga International Airport (RIX) is the main airport in Latvia and airBaltic's hub, which gives it better connectivity than many Baltic airports. For live cargo to Yerevan, the most commonly used routing is RIX via an airBaltic connection to Frankfurt (FRA) or Amsterdam (AMS), then onward Turkish Airlines Cargo or Lufthansa Cargo to Yerevan Zvartnots (EVN).

        If routing through Istanbul, Turkish Airlines Cargo handles live animals at Ataturk Airport (IST) and operates regularly to Yerevan. The IST-EVN leg is short.

        Book live cargo at least four to six weeks ahead. Get written cargo booking confirmations on every leg, including explicit confirmation that live animals are accepted on the specific flight dates.

faqs:
  - question: "Does my pet need a titre test to enter Armenia from Latvia?"
    answer: "No. Armenia does not require a FAVN rabies titre test for personal companion pets from Latvia. A current rabies vaccination (minimum 21 days before entry) and a PVD-endorsed export health certificate are sufficient."
  - question: "What is the PVD and why does my certificate need endorsement?"
    answer: "PVD stands for Partikas un veterinarais dienests, Latvia's Food and Veterinary Service. It is the national competent authority for veterinary certification. The endorsement confirms that the health certificate was issued by a properly authorised vet and meets the required standard. Without the PVD endorsement, the certificate may not be accepted at the Armenian border."
  - question: "Can airBaltic carry my pet as cargo to Armenia?"
    answer: "airBaltic carries pets in cargo and cabin on its own routes, primarily within Europe and to its hub destinations. For Yerevan, airBaltic provides the initial leg from Riga to a major European hub. From that hub, a separate carrier (Turkish Airlines, Lufthansa) handles the cargo to Yerevan. Coordinate through a transport agent or by contacting the relevant cargo departments directly."
  - question: "How many pets can I bring to Armenia without a permit?"
    answer: "One or two personal companion animals can enter Armenia without a formal import permit. Three or more pets require contact with the SSFS at ssfs.am and additional documentation. Plan ahead if you are moving more than two animals."
