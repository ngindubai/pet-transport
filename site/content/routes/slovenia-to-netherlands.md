  sections:
    - heading: "Routing from Ljubljana to Amsterdam"
      body: |
        Ljubljana Airport (LJU) is a compact regional airport with limited long-haul and Western European connections. For Slovenia-to-Netherlands travel, the standard route is: Ljubljana to Vienna (VIE) on Austrian Airlines, then Vienna to Amsterdam Schiphol (AMS) on Austrian Airlines or a connecting KLM service.

        For small pets in cabin, Austrian Airlines allows cabin travel within its weight limit. Book the cabin pet space at time of ticket purchase. For larger dogs in cargo, the connection at VIE is manageable but confirm live animal handling at VIE before booking. KLM Cargo at AMS is the final destination facility and one of Europe's best.

        The overland alternative: Ljubljana to Amsterdam is approximately 12-14 hours by car via Austria and Germany. For large dogs or multiple pets, this is worth considering over airline cargo.

    - heading: "NVWA checks at Amsterdam and what to have ready"
      body: |
        The NVWA (Nederlandse Voedsel en Warenautoriteit) carries out veterinary checks at Amsterdam Schiphol for pet arrivals. For EU pets, the check is typically brief: a microchip scan and a review of the EU passport or AHC. The key checks are whether the chip number matches, whether the rabies vaccination is current, and whether an AHC was issued within the 10-day window.

        Ensure before leaving Ljubljana that your EU passport is up to date and the chip number in the passport matches the chip your vet scans. If using an AHC, arrange the UVHVR endorsement at least 2-3 days before your travel date and confirm the AHC issue date is within 10 days of your Amsterdam arrival.

    answer: "Yes. The EU pet passport is valid across all EU member states. A Slovenian-issued EU passport is accepted at Dutch border checks without additional endorsement. The passport must show a current rabies vaccination and the chip number must match your pet."
  - question: "Is there a quarantine for Slovenian pets arriving in the Netherlands?"
    answer: "No. There is no quarantine for EU pets arriving from other EU member states with valid documentation. The Netherlands applies the same EU framework as Slovenia."
  - question: "Can I drive my dog from Slovenia to the Netherlands?"
    answer: "Yes. The entire route from Slovenia through Austria and Germany to the Netherlands is within the EU Schengen area. Your EU pet passport covers the full journey with no additional documentation. The drive is approximately 12-14 hours."
  - question: "What does the UVHVR stamp on my AHC mean for Dutch authorities?"
    answer: "The UVHVR stamp confirms the issuing Slovenian vet is officially authorised to issue EU health certificates. The NVWA at Amsterdam Schiphol looks for this official endorsement when checking AHC documents. An AHC signed only by a private vet without UVHVR endorsement is not valid for EU border entry."
