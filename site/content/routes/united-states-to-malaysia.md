  overview: |
    Moving a pet from the USA to Malaysia involves a mandatory 30-day quarantine at a DVS (Department of Veterinary Services) facility in Kuala Lumpur. The USA is not on Malaysia's Scheduled Countries list, so quarantine applies to all US-origin pets regardless of documentation. Allow three to four months of preparation from start to travel day.

    The US export side uses the standard USDA-APHIS health certificate process. On the Malaysia side, you need a DVS import permit (applied online in advance), a titre test, and core vaccine documentation alongside the rabies vaccination. Cathay Pacific via Hong Kong and Qatar Airways via Doha are the most commonly used carriers for this corridor.

      body: |
        The DVS quarantine station at Kuala Lumpur International Airport (KLIA) is the entry point for international pet imports. All pets from non-scheduled countries complete 30 days here regardless of documentation quality. DVS veterinary staff check all documents on day one and monitor pets throughout the quarantine period.

        Owners can visit their pets during quarantine at scheduled visiting times. Your agent or the DVS office can provide current visiting arrangements and costs.

        After 30 days, DVS issues a release certificate. You collect your pet from the quarantine station and complete the transition to your Malaysian home or temporary accommodation.

        The total quarantine cost depends on the pet's size and species. Get a current quote from the DVS office or your agent before travel. Budget USD 600 to USD 1,800 as a working estimate.

faqs:
  - question: "Is there any way to avoid the 30-day quarantine in Malaysia for pets from the USA?"
    answer: "No. The USA is not on Malaysia's Scheduled Countries list, so all pets from the USA are subject to 30-day mandatory quarantine at the DVS facility at KLIA, regardless of vaccination status or documentation quality. There is no waiver process."
  - question: "What is the DVS import permit and how do I apply?"
    answer: "The DVS (Department of Veterinary Services) import permit is the official Malaysian approval for your pet's import. Apply online via the DVS e-Permit system at dvs.gov.my. You will need your pet's microchip number, vaccination records, and titre test result. Processing takes 2-4 weeks. Do not book flights before the permit is confirmed."
  - question: "My dog is a Rottweiler. Can it enter Malaysia?"
    answer: "Yes, but with extra steps. Rottweilers are on Malaysia's restricted breed list. You must submit a written application to the Director of State Veterinary Services before starting the DVS import permit process. This is separate from the standard permit application and adds lead time. Start this step first."
  - question: "Does my cat have to quarantine in Malaysia when arriving from the USA?"
    answer: "Yes. The 30-day DVS quarantine applies to cats as well as dogs. All pets from non-scheduled countries, including cats from the USA, complete the 30-day period at the DVS facility."
  - question: "How long does the USA to Malaysia pet move take?"
    answer: "Allow 12-18 weeks. The titre test (30 days after vaccination + result processing time), DVS permit (2-4 weeks), cargo booking, and APHIS endorsement all need to be sequenced. For restricted breeds, add additional time for the written application."
