  h1: "Pet Transport from the United States to Malaysia"
  overview: |
    Malaysia applies different quarantine rules depending on where a pet comes from. The United States is not on Malaysia's quarantine-exempt list, so pets from the USA face a minimum 7-day quarantine at a DVS-approved facility on arrival at Kuala Lumpur International Airport. This is in contrast to pets from the UK, Australia, New Zealand, Sweden, Japan, Republic of Ireland, Brunei, and Singapore, which are quarantine-exempt if documentation is correct.

    The other requirements are straightforward: a DVS e-Permit (applied for online 2-4 weeks before arrival, RM 50-100 per animal), a current rabies vaccination given at least 30 days before entry, an ISO microchip, and a USDA-endorsed health certificate issued within 14 days of arrival.

    There are no direct cargo flights from US cities to Kuala Lumpur for pets. All routing involves a hub connection: Hong Kong (Cathay Pacific), Seoul (Korean Air), or Dubai (Emirates, outside June to September). A specialist cargo agent familiar with live animal handling at Asian hubs is recommended.

    - heading: "Why UK pets skip quarantine but US pets do not"
      body: |
        Malaysia's quarantine-exempt list is based on a country's rabies status and bilateral veterinary agreements. The countries currently on the exempt list are: the United Kingdom, Republic of Ireland, Sweden, Australia, New Zealand, Japan, Brunei, and Singapore. All are considered low-risk for rabies in dogs.

        The United States has eliminated canine rabies (the dog-specific strain), but it still has wildlife rabies (bats, raccoons, foxes). Malaysia's DVS has not extended quarantine exemption to the USA under current policy. This means US-origin pets undergo a minimum 7-day quarantine regardless of documentation quality.

        The quarantine is conducted at a DVS-approved facility at or near KLIA. Owner visits are typically permitted but confirm with the facility in advance. The owner bears all quarantine costs.
faqs:
  - question: "Do pets from the USA have to quarantine in Malaysia?"
    answer: "Yes. The United States is not on Malaysia's quarantine-exempt list. All dogs and cats from the USA undergo a minimum 7-day quarantine at a DVS-approved facility on arrival at Kuala Lumpur International Airport. The DVS officer may extend quarantine based on health assessment or documentation review. This is different from pets arriving from the UK, Australia, New Zealand, Sweden, Japan, Republic of Ireland, Brunei, and Singapore, which are quarantine-exempt for compliant pets."
  - question: "How do I get the DVS e-Permit for Malaysia from the USA?"
    answer: "The DVS e-Permit is applied for online through Malaysia's Department of Veterinary Services Animal Passport portal (animalpassport.dvs.gov.my). Apply 2-4 weeks before your planned arrival date. The permit is valid for 30 days from issue and costs RM 50-100 per animal. You will need your pet's microchip number, vaccination details, travel information, and USDA health certificate details. Your pet transport agent can assist with the application."
  - question: "Does my dog's rabies vaccination need to be at a specific timing for Malaysia?"
    answer: "Yes. The rabies vaccination must be administered at least 30 days before entry into Malaysia. A vaccination given within 30 days of arrival does not meet the requirement. Ensure your pet's vaccination is timed accordingly, and that the vaccination date is clearly recorded in the USDA-endorsed health certificate."
  - question: "Which airlines carry pets in cargo from the USA to Malaysia?"
    answer: "There are no direct US-to-KUL cargo services for pets. All routing involves a hub connection. Cathay Pacific via Hong Kong (HKG) from LAX, JFK, or SFO is a well-established option with strong live animal handling at HKG. Korean Air via Seoul Incheon (ICN) is another option. Emirates via Dubai (DXB) works for travel outside June to September, when heat embargoes restrict live animal shipments. Malaysia Airlines connects via an Asian hub from US gateways. Use an IATA-registered cargo agent."
  - question: "Are there breed restrictions for dogs entering Malaysia from the USA?"
    answer: "Yes. Eight dog breeds require a prior written application to the Director of State Veterinary Services before importation into Malaysia: Rottweiler, Doberman Pinscher, German Shepherd, Belgian Shepherd, East European Shepherd, Bull Mastiff, Bull Terrier, and Perro de Presa Canario. This is not an outright ban, but written approval must be obtained before travel. Contact DVS Malaysia (dvs.gov.my) well before your departure date if your dog is one of these breeds."
