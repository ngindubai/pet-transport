    Here's the asymmetry that defines this route: a pet moving from Armenia into Singapore faces a 90-day titre wait and 30 days of quarantine. A pet moving the other way, from Singapore into Armenia, faces neither. Armenia's SSFS simply doesn't classify countries by rabies risk the way AVS does.

An AVS-endorsed health certificate, a current rabies vaccination, and confirmation of Armenia's own (comparatively light) import documentation. Six to ten weeks, most of it cargo booking and certificate timing.

  sections:
    - heading: "Why Armenia doesn't mirror Singapore's Schedule system"
      body: |
        AVS Singapore sorts every origin country into Schedule I, II or III and adjusts requirements accordingly. Armenia's SSFS does not run an equivalent system for imports. There is no titre test requirement tied to where the pet is coming from, Singapore included.

        Practically, this means the demanding part of a Caucasus-Singapore pet move sits almost entirely on the inbound-to-Singapore leg. Going the other way, from Changi to Yerevan, is a shorter and cheaper process by a wide margin.

    - heading: "Getting from Changi to Zvartnots"
      body: |
        Turkish Airlines and Singapore Airlines share the Istanbul-Singapore nonstop sector, a service both carriers run roughly 17 times a week combined. Turkish Airlines then operates onward to Yerevan, giving a genuine two-leg, largely single-carrier itinerary.

        Emirates via Dubai works as an alternative if Istanbul timing doesn't suit. Dubai-Yerevan alone carries around 56 weekly flights across all carriers, so availability rarely becomes the bottleneck on that leg.

  - question: "Why is this route so much simpler than Armenia to Singapore in reverse?"
    answer: "Because the two countries apply different systems. Armenia's SSFS has no equivalent system, so Singapore-origin pets face only the standard microchip, vaccination and health certificate requirements."
