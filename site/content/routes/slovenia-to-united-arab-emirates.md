content:
  h1: "Pet Transport from Slovenia to the UAE"
  overview: |
    Ljubljana is small, its airport smaller, but that doesn't slow this route down. Slovenia's EU membership means MOCCAE treats it as low-risk for rabies, so no titre test stands between a compliant dog or cat and a UAE entry permit. The paperwork is the standard low-risk set: current vaccination, a MOCCAE import permit good for 90 days, and a health certificate endorsed by UVHVVR shortly before departure.

    Getting out of Ljubljana takes a connection either way. Turkish Airlines Cargo via Istanbul handles larger dogs reliably. Austrian Airlines via Vienna is the better fit for a small pet that can travel in the cabin on the first leg.

  sections:
    - heading: "No titre test, thanks to EU membership"
      body: |
        MOCCAE's low-risk list covers every EU member state, and Slovenia is no exception. That means the rabies antibody titre test required on unlisted-country routes simply doesn't apply here. A current vaccination, given at least 21 days before travel for a first dose, is the standard MOCCAE checks against.

        Minimum age for entry from a low-risk country sits at 12 weeks. The import permit, applied for online and valid for 90 days once issued, is still a separate requirement regardless of the risk category, and it needs to be timed against a confirmed departure date.

    - heading: "Getting out of Ljubljana: Istanbul or Vienna"
      body: |
        Turkish Airlines Cargo runs a dependable service via Istanbul, with established live animal handling on arrival and a good fit for larger dogs travelling as cargo. Austrian Airlines via Vienna suits a small cat or dog able to travel in the cabin on the Ljubljana leg before switching to cargo for the onward journey to Dubai.

        Neither routing beats the other on price by any consistent margin. Book based on your pet's size and the airline's cabin or cargo capacity on your actual travel dates.
