  overview: |
    Thailand does not impose routine quarantine on compliant pets. If your documentation is complete and your pet appears healthy on arrival at the DLD inspection, the pet is released the same day. The requirements from the USA are: a USDA-endorsed health certificate (within 10 days of Thai arrival), current vaccinations, a DLD import permit (applied for 7-60 days before departure), and deworming and parasite treatment within 7 days of travel.

    The logistics challenge from the USA is the routing. Most cargo on this corridor involves a connection through an Asian hub: Seoul (Korean Air), Hong Kong (Cathay Pacific), or Tokyo (Japan Airlines). There is no single dominant direct-cargo carrier from the US to Bangkok for pets. A specialist cargo agent familiar with live animal routing through Asian hubs is valuable here.

    The breed restriction is firm: Pit Bull Terriers and American Staffordshire Terriers are banned from Thailand. All other breeds can enter with correct documentation.

faqs:
  - question: "Is there quarantine for pets entering Thailand from the USA?"
    answer: "No, for compliant pets. Thailand's Department of Livestock Development inspects all arriving pets at Suvarnabhumi Airport. If documentation is complete and the pet appears healthy, it is released the same day. If documentation is incomplete or illness is detected, the pet may be quarantined, returned to origin, or subject to further action."
  - question: "How do I get the Thailand DLD import permit from the USA?"
    answer: "The DLD import permit is issued by Thailand's Department of Livestock Development. Apply between 7 and 60 days before your departure date. The application requires your pet's details, travel dates, and vaccination history. Your pet transport agent can assist with the application. Consult the Thai embassy or official DLD portal for current application procedures."
  - question: "Are there direct cargo flights for pets from the USA to Thailand?"
    answer: "Most cargo routing for pets from the USA to Bangkok involves a connection through Seoul (Korean Air), Hong Kong (Cathay Pacific), Tokyo (JAL/ANA), or another Asian hub. There is no dominant direct service. Confirm live animal acceptance at both the boarding and connecting airports, and ensure transfer times are sufficient. A specialist cargo agent with Asia experience is recommended."
  - question: "Which US airports have the best connections to Bangkok for pet cargo?"
    answer: "JFK and LAX have the most connection options to Bangkok via Asian hubs. Korean Air from JFK via Seoul Incheon (ICN) is a well-established routing. Cathay Pacific from JFK or LAX via Hong Kong (HKG) is another option. Confirm current live animal acceptance with the airline or cargo agent at the time of booking."
  - question: "What vaccinations does my dog need to enter Thailand from the USA?"
    answer: "Dogs must be vaccinated against rabies (at least 21 days before Thai arrival, within 1 year), DHPP (distemper, hepatitis, parvovirus, parainfluenza), and leptospirosis. Cats must be vaccinated against rabies, FVRCP (rhinotracheitis, calicivirus, panleukopenia). All vaccinations must be within their validity period at time of travel. Deworming and external parasite treatment must be completed within 7 days of travel."
