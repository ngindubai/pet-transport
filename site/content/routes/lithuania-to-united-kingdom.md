  h1: "Pet Transport from Lithuania to the United Kingdom"
  overview: |
    Lithuania is a UK Part 1 listed country, which makes this one of the simpler international pet transport routes. No titre test, no 3-month wait, no quarantine for compliant pets.

    You need a current rabies vaccination, ISO microchip, EU Animal Health Certificate (or EU pet passport issued before January 2021), and tapeworm treatment for dogs. Ryanair flies direct from Vilnius and Kaunas to multiple UK airports and accepts very small cabin pets. For larger animals, cargo routing via Lufthansa or airBaltic works well.

    Lithuania has a large community in the UK and this is a well-travelled route. The main administrative step most owners miss is the EU pet passport post-Brexit rule: if your passport was issued after 31 December 2020, it is not valid for UK entry.
  sections:
    - heading: "The post-Brexit pet passport rule most Lithuanian owners get wrong"
      body: |
        This is the most common mistake on the Lithuania-UK route. EU pet passports issued before 1 January 2021 are valid for UK entry. Passports issued on or after 1 January 2021 are not valid. The date on the first page of the passport is what matters.

        If your pet's EU passport is from 2021 or later, you need an EU Animal Health Certificate from an official Lithuanian vet. The AHC is valid for 10 days from the examination date. It is issued for a single trip.

        If you are travelling with your pet multiple times a year, you will need a new AHC each time. This is the main ongoing cost of pet travel between Lithuania and the UK since Brexit.
    - heading: "Travelling with a larger dog from Lithuania to London"
      body: |
        For dogs over the cabin weight limit (typically 8kg including carrier), cargo is the option. Lufthansa via Frankfurt handles cargo pets reliably, and the Frankfurt connection allows some flexibility on schedule.

        Alternatively, airBaltic cargo via Riga works well and Riga is close to Vilnius by road or air. For very large breeds, cargo via Lufthansa or Finnair is often the best combination of reliability and schedule frequency.

        Book cargo space 4-6 weeks in advance. Cargo space for live animals is limited and fills during peak travel periods (summer and Christmas).
faqs:
  - question: "Does my pet need a titre test to travel from Lithuania to the UK?"
    answer: "No. Lithuania is a listed Part 1 country for UK pet travel. No titre test is required. You need a current rabies vaccination, ISO microchip, EU Animal Health Certificate (or a valid EU pet passport issued before January 2021), and tapeworm treatment for dogs."
  - question: "My pet's EU passport was issued in 2022. Can I still use it for UK travel?"
    answer: "No. EU pet passports issued on or after 1 January 2021 are not valid for UK entry. You need an EU Animal Health Certificate from an official Lithuanian vet. The AHC is valid for 10 days and covers one trip."
  - question: "Can I take my dog on Ryanair from Lithuania to the UK?"
    answer: "Ryanair accepts very small pets in the cabin on its Vilnius and Kaunas to UK routes. Weight limits are strict - check Ryanair's current cabin pet policy for maximum weight including carrier. Larger dogs cannot travel on Ryanair and need a cargo option."
  - question: "My dog weighs 20kg. How do I get it from Vilnius to London?"
    answer: "Large dogs travel as cargo. Lufthansa via Frankfurt is a reliable option with Lufthansa Cargo accepting larger pets. airBaltic Cargo via Riga is another option. Book cargo space well in advance and ensure your dog travels in an IATA-compliant crate of the correct size."
  - question: "How much does it cost to transport a pet from Lithuania to the UK?"
    answer: "For a small cabin pet, costs are mainly the AHC (approximately EUR 60-120) plus the Ryanair cabin fee (approximately EUR 50-100). For larger cargo pets, airline cargo fees typically range from EUR 250-600 depending on pet weight and crate size. Add veterinary fees for the AHC and tapeworm treatment."
