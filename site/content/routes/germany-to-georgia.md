  sections:
    - heading: "Preparing your export documents in Germany"
      body: |
        Leaving Germany with a pet for a non-EU destination requires more than an EU pet passport. The EU pet passport covers intra-EU travel; for Georgia, you need an EU export health certificate completed by an official veterinarian and endorsed by your Bundesland veterinary authority.

        The Bundesland endorsement process varies by state. Some authorities have a fast-track process; others can take several days. Contact your local Bundesland authority at the start of your planning to understand their specific timeline and how to submit documents. If you are in Frankfurt, your Bundesland is Hesse (Hessen); the relevant authority is the Regierungsprasidium Giessen. Other Bundeslaender have their own equivalents.

        The health certificate must be issued within a set number of days before your pet arrives in Tbilisi. The exact window is specified by Georgia's NFA. Time your vet appointment and endorsement submission to align with your travel date, not the other way around.

    - heading: "Georgia as a destination: what to expect"
      body: |
        Georgia has a growing reputation as a welcoming country for expatriates and their pets, and this is reflected in its import process. The National Food Agency (NFA) requires advance notification before your pet's arrival, but it is a relatively light-touch process compared to some other Caucasus destinations.

        On arrival at Tbilisi International Airport (TBS), the NFA veterinary inspection unit checks your documents. For well-documented pets from Germany, a recognised rabies-controlled country, the inspection is typically brief. Compliant arrivals are not placed in quarantine.

        Tbilisi is a regional aviation hub with good connections from Istanbul, Frankfurt, Munich, and other European cities. Turkish Airlines is the dominant carrier on the IST-TBS leg, and the FRA-IST-TBS routing is reliable for pet cargo. Georgian Airways also operates FRA-TBS and MUC-TBS, and may accept cargo pets; confirm this directly with the airline before booking.

    answer: "No titre test is currently required for pets entering Georgia from Germany. The requirements are advance NFA notification, a current rabies vaccination, ISO microchip, and an EU export health certificate endorsed by the Bundesland authority. Confirm current requirements with the NFA at nfa.gov.ge."
  - question: "What is the difference between an EU pet passport and an EU export health certificate?"
    answer: "The EU pet passport allows non-commercial travel with your pet within the EU and associated countries. An EU export health certificate is a different document required for travel to non-EU countries like Georgia. Your official vet in Germany issues the certificate; the Bundesland authority then endorses it."
  - question: "Is there quarantine for pets entering Georgia from Germany?"
    answer: "No routine quarantine for compliant arrivals from Germany. The NFA carries out a documentation and health inspection at TBS. Pets with correct papers and no health concerns are cleared without quarantine."
  - question: "How far in advance should I notify the NFA in Georgia?"
    answer: "Contact the NFA at nfa.gov.ge at least 8-10 weeks before your travel date to confirm current requirements and complete the advance notification. Do not leave this until the week before travel."
