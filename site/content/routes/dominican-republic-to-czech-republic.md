  h1: "Pet Transport Dominican Republic to Czech Republic: 2026 Guide"
  overview: |
    Bringing a pet from the Dominican Republic to the Czech Republic is a multi-stage process that takes a minimum of five to six months from start to finish. The Czech Republic is a full EU member state, and because the Dominican Republic is classified as an EU unlisted third country, every dog and cat on this route must complete the titre test pathway: a FAVN or ELISA titre test at an EU-approved laboratory, a result of at least 0.5 IU/ml, and a 90-day mandatory wait from the date that result is confirmed by the laboratory.

    The Czech import authority is SVS, the Statni Veterinarni Sprava, based at svscr.cz. Border veterinary posts at Vaclav Havel Airport Prague (PRG) handle incoming animal documentation checks. The DIGEGA-endorsed EU health certificate must arrive with your pet and be valid for the 10-day window from issue. Czech inspectors check all documentation in full, including the laboratory name, titre result, and date of blood draw, so the paperwork must be complete and accurate.

    Cargo from Santo Domingo (SDQ) to Prague (PRG) involves a European hub connection. The main options are via Madrid on Iberia, Paris CDG on Air France, or Amsterdam on KLM. Prague is well served from all three hubs. A pet transport agent who is familiar with the DIGEGA process and can coordinate international titre test sample shipping is strongly recommended for owners making this journey for the first time.

  sections:
    - heading: "Why the Dominican Republic needs a titre test for Czech Republic entry"
      body: |
        The Czech Republic applies EU Regulation 2020/692, which governs the non-commercial movement of pets from third countries. Under this regulation, listed countries have demonstrated veterinary standards considered equivalent to the EU and benefit from a simplified entry process. Unlisted countries, including the Dominican Republic, require the full titre test pathway.

        The process works as follows. Your vet draws blood at least 30 days after your pet's rabies vaccination, and ships the sample to an EU-approved laboratory. The laboratory analyses the serum for the presence of rabies antibodies. A result of 0.5 IU/ml or above is a passing result, and from the date that result is recorded, a 90-day mandatory wait begins. Your pet cannot enter the Czech Republic, or any other EU member state, until those 90 days have elapsed.

        The blood draw date and the date of the result are separate, and the 90-day wait begins from the result date, not the draw date. Keep the original laboratory report, as Czech border inspectors will verify it against the health certificate.

    - heading: "Routing and documentation from Santo Domingo"
      body: |
        Las Americas International Airport (SDQ) serves Santo Domingo and is the main international cargo departure point in the Dominican Republic. Punta Cana (PUJ) is an option for owners in the east of the island, but SDQ has more reliable connections to European hubs. For most Prague-bound shipments, SDQ is the starting point.

        From SDQ, the three most commonly used hub connections to Prague are: Iberia via Madrid (MAD to PRG), Air France via Paris CDG, and KLM via Amsterdam (AMS). All three hubs operate regular services into Vaclav Havel Airport Prague. When booking cargo, confirm with the airline that live animals are accepted on both the transatlantic leg and the European connection, as policies are set per flight and per carrier.

        The DIGEGA export process requires a DIGEGA-authorised vet to examine your pet and issue the EU-format certificate, which DIGEGA then endorses. Not every vet in the Dominican Republic is DIGEGA-authorised for EU export, so identifying the right practice is an early step. The endorsement typically takes one to two working days, and the certificate is valid for 10 days from issue.

    - heading: "How long does it take to move a pet from the Dominican Republic to the Czech Republic?"
      body: |
        The minimum preparation timeline is 20 to 24 weeks. This includes the wait for the microchip and vaccination (week one), a minimum 30-day wait before the blood draw, two to four weeks for the laboratory result, and then a 90-day wait from the passing result date. This sequence takes around 18 to 20 weeks under ideal conditions.

        Beyond the mandatory waiting periods, add time to locate an EU-approved laboratory, arrange safe international shipment of the blood sample from the Dominican Republic, book cargo space on the airline, and complete the DIGEGA endorsement within the 10-day certificate window before departure. Beginning the process six months before your intended travel date is strongly advisable.
