  overview: |
    A Savannah cat's pedigree matters more on this route than its owner's passport. Cameroon bars Savannah and Bengal cats, and wolf hybrid dogs, from import unless the paperwork shows five clear generations removed from that ancestry, and that rule applies whether the cat is flying from Sydney, London or Los Angeles.

    Everything else tracks closely with what UK- and US-origin pets face. No titre test, a 30-day rabies vaccination window, and a 12-month cap on how old a multi-year vaccine can be at the point of entry. Australia's own rabies-free status doesn't buy any shortcut here, since Cameroon's published rules don't appear to run a two-tier system based on the exporting country's own rabies classification the way the Bahamas or Belarus do.

    The practical hurdle is the flight. Douala has no service from Australia at all, so the journey runs through at least one Gulf hub and typically Addis Ababa or Istanbul as well.

  sections:
    - heading: "A breed check that outranks the paperwork"
      body: |
        Wolf hybrid dogs, and Savannah or Bengal cats, need five generations of documented separation from their wild ancestry before Cameroon will let them in. It isn't something a health certificate or a vet exam resolves; it's a registry document, and it's worth confirming with the breeder before spending anything on flights or a crate.

        Most household pets never encounter this rule at all. It's specific to a handful of hybrid and wild-derived breeds, and it's the one part of this route where a mistake can't be fixed with more time or more paperwork close to departure.

    - heading: "Why Australia's rabies-free status doesn't shorten this one"
      body: |
        Some destinations on this site, the Bahamas among them, drop the rabies vaccine requirement entirely for pets from WOAH-recognised rabies-free countries like Australia. Cameroon isn't one of them, as far as we could confirm. Its own published conditions ask for the same 30-day vaccination window and the same 12-month cap on a multi-year dose, regardless of where the pet started its journey.

        That's worth stating plainly rather than assuming a pattern carries across every African destination on this site: it doesn't. Confirm directly with MINEPIA if there's reason to think this has changed since our last check.

        Emirates covers the long first leg from Australia to Dubai, connecting onward to Ethiopian Airlines' Addis Ababa hub and then Douala. Turkish Airlines via Istanbul is a genuine alternative for owners whose nearest Australian gateway suits that schedule better.

        Whichever routing is chosen, confirm live animal acceptance on every leg in writing before booking, since a connection that looks tight but workable on paper can mean a rushed transfer for a crated animal in practice.
