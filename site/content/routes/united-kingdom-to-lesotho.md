  overview: |
    Moshoeshoe I International Airport runs one scheduled international connection, and it goes to Johannesburg. Everything else about this route follows from that single fact: a UK-origin pet flies Heathrow to O.R. Tambo first, then picks up the Airlink hop into Maseru, because there simply isn't another way in.

    The regulatory side is lighter than the routing suggests. Lesotho's Director of Animal Health asks for a vaccination record, a microchip, and an import permit, no blood test involved. That's a notable difference from the reverse direction of this same route, where a pet leaving Lesotho for the UK does need a titre test and a three-month wait.

  sections:
    - heading: "What Lesotho's import rules actually require"
      body: |
        A UK pet arriving in Lesotho needs a rabies vaccination given between 30 days and 12 months before arrival, current core vaccinations, a microchip on the paperwork, and an import permit from the Director of Animal Health, part of Lesotho's Ministry of Agriculture, Food Security and Nutrition. The permit is needed because the UK isn't one of the SADC member states that gets a waiver on this step.

        No titre test features anywhere in Lesotho's own rules, for pets from any country. It's worth flagging precisely because the same route in reverse, Lesotho to the UK, does carry a titre test requirement, driven entirely by the UK's own unlisted-country protocol rather than anything Lesotho asks of its own arrivals.

    - heading: "Johannesburg, twice: once for the flight, once for the paperwork"
      body: |
        British Airways flies Heathrow to O.R. Tambo nonstop in around 11 hours; South African Airways is worth comparing on the same sector. From Johannesburg, Airlink is the only scheduled carrier into Maseru, running the route roughly 21 times a week on a journey of about 65 minutes.

        The two legs sit on separate air waybills, often with different airlines and terminals. A ground agent at O.R. Tambo, used to managing this specific handoff, makes the difference between a same-day connection and a delay. Book the Airlink sector at least 48 hours ahead; it's a smaller regional operation with its own booking rhythm, separate from the long-haul leg.
