content:
  h1: "Pet Transport from Poland to Uruguay"
  overview: |
    Moving a pet from Poland to Uruguay involves two requirements beyond the standard vaccination and health certificate process: an MGAP import permit that must be in hand before travel, and a Praziquantel treatment for dogs timed within a specific window before arrival. Both are manageable with sufficient lead time. Allow six weeks minimum, and eight to ten weeks if the MGAP permit needs to be obtained for the first time.

    MGAP (Ministerio de Ganaderia, Agricultura y Pesca, gub.uy/ministerio-ganaderia-agricultura-pesca) inspects all pets on arrival at Carrasco International Airport (MVD) in Montevideo. There is no quarantine. If documentation is complete, inspection is a straightforward process.

    Warsaw Chopin Airport (WAW) connects to Montevideo (MVD) via LATAM Airlines through Lima or Sao Paulo, or via European hubs with Air France or Iberia. LOT Polish Airlines serves Warsaw with connections to Madrid and other European hubs, which can facilitate cargo transfers. The total transit time can be 24-48 hours, which matters when timing the health certificate.

  sections:
    - heading: "MGAP Import Permit"
      body: |
        The MGAP import permit is required for all dogs and cats entering Uruguay and must be obtained before the animal travels. Apply via gub.uy/ministerio-ganaderia-agricultura-pesca at least six to ten weeks before your intended departure. The permit covers the specific animal and must match the details on the health certificate. A pet transport agent with South American experience can help navigate the MGAP application if you are unfamiliar with the process.

        Keep the original permit with the travel documents; MGAP inspectors at Carrasco Airport (MVD) will check it against the health certificate and vaccination records. Do not begin any veterinary preparation until the permit is confirmed in writing from MGAP.

    - heading: "Praziquantel Treatment for Dogs"
      body: |
        All dogs travelling to Uruguay must receive a Praziquantel treatment given between 72 hours and 30 days before arrival. The purpose is tapeworm (Echinococcus) control. The treatment date and product details must be recorded on the official health certificate by the GIW-authorised official vet.

        Ensure your private vet uses a product with Praziquantel as the active ingredient; a general worming product may not satisfy Uruguay's specific requirement. Given the 24-48 hour transit from Warsaw to Montevideo, plan the Praziquantel treatment date carefully: it should be given after you have confirmed the departure date, and timed to fall within the 72-hour to 30-day window relative to the expected arrival time in Uruguay.

    - heading: "Polish Export Certification"
      body: |
        Poland's official export health certificate must be issued by the Powiatowy Lekarz Weterynarii (district official vet), authorised by GIW (Glowny Inspektorat Weterynarii, wetgiw.gov.pl). This is distinct from a standard private vet. Contact your district veterinary authority to identify the correct official and book the examination. GIW endorsement after the signing takes one to three working days.

        The certificate is valid for 10 days from signing. Given the long transit to Uruguay, schedule the official vet appointment to give sufficient buffer for the full journey. Warsaw Chopin Airport (WAW) is the cargo departure hub.

    - heading: "Airline Options and Journey"
      body: |
        LATAM Airlines is the primary carrier for Poland to Uruguay cargo, routing through Lima (LIM) or Sao Paulo (GRU) before the final leg to Montevideo (MVD). Iberia via Madrid (MAD) and Air France via Paris CDG also offer connecting cargo services to MVD. LOT Polish Airlines connects Warsaw to European hubs, making the transfer to Iberia or Air France at Madrid or CDG practical.

        The Warsaw to Montevideo routing is one of the longer cargo journeys from Central Europe to Latin America. Costs reflect this: EUR 1,000-2,800 for air freight alone, depending on animal size and crate. Total all-in costs for a single-pet move from Warsaw to MVD typically run EUR 1,500-3,500, including the GIW certificate, crate, and agent fees if used.
