content:
  h1: "Pet Transport from Uzbekistan to Australia: 2026 Guide"
  overview: |
    There is no direct pathway from Uzbekistan to Australia for a pet. Uzbekistan doesn't appear on any of DAFF's three approved-country tiers, which means Australia won't process an import application until the animal has spent at least 180 consecutive days genuinely living in an approved country, most practically somewhere in the UAE or Europe given Tashkent's flight network.

    Once that residency period is served, the process converges with the standard Group 3 track: an RNATT titre test, a BICON import permit, and Mickleham quarantine. Taken together, this is one of the longest routes in the entire build, realistically 40 to 52 weeks.

  sections:
    - heading: "Why the staging country has to be a genuine residence"
      body: |
        DAFF's non-approved-country rule is specific: the pet needs to actually live in the approved country, continuously, for at least 180 days, with documentation to prove it, not simply pass through on a connecting flight. For an Uzbekistan-based owner, that most often means the UAE, given Tashkent's direct flight links and the UAE's DAFF approval, though a European approved country works equally well depending on the owner's own circumstances and where they might be relocating to first.

        This is a genuinely demanding requirement on top of everything else this move involves, and it's worth planning around from the very start rather than discovering it partway through.

    - heading: "What happens once the staging period is over"
      body: |
        Once the residency requirement is satisfied, the process runs the same standard Group 3 pathway used across most of this build: microchip and identity verification before the RNATT blood draw, the titre test itself, a BICON import permit application that can take DAFF up to 123 business days to process, and finally Mickleham quarantine, 10 days if the identity verification was done correctly and in good time, 30 days otherwise.
