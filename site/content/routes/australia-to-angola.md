  h1: "Pet Transport from Australia to Angola"
  overview: |
    Australia's routes into Africa don't run through Europe the way British or American ones do. For a pet leaving Sydney, Melbourne, Perth, Brisbane or Adelaide, Emirates already covers Dubai directly, then Dubai to Luanda three times a week, a single carrier for the entire trip and a genuinely different hub to the Lisbon or Frankfurt connections this site's UK-to-Angola page describes.

    The regulatory side is unusually light for a route this geographically awkward. Angola doesn't ask for a rabies titre test from any country, Australia included, and there's no quarantine for a pet that arrives correctly documented. The catch sits in the timing: a published 4-to-11-month vaccination window that's worth confirming directly before it locks in a schedule.

  sections:
    - heading: "Why does this route go through Dubai instead of Europe?"
      body: |
        Geography, mostly. The UK-to-Angola route on this site connects through Lisbon or Frankfurt because that's where British air traffic to Southern Africa already runs. Australia's traffic runs the other way, east toward the Gulf, so Emirates' existing Dubai-Luanda service, three times a week, is the natural fit rather than routing an Australian pet all the way to Europe first.

        It's a genuinely different shape of journey to the UK or US versions of this route, not just a relabelled version of the same map.

    - heading: "What does Angola actually check, and what does it skip?"
      body: |
        Compared with many destinations on this site, Angola's import rules are relatively light regardless of where the pet started. There's no titre test, no import permit for an owner travelling with their own pet, and no quarantine if the paperwork is correct. The microchip standard is the familiar ISO 11784/11785.

        The certificate itself is where the timing gets tight: it has to be completed within 14 days of entering Angola. Book the DAFF-accredited vet appointment around a confirmed flight date, not the other way round, or the certificate risks expiring before travel.

    - heading: "Why does the vaccination timing need double-checking?"
      body: |
        Published guidance states the primary rabies vaccination has to fall between 4 and 11 months before entry, a narrower and stranger window than almost any other destination on this site sets. That detail comes from a secondary compiled source rather than a live official Angolan government page.

        Confirm it directly with the Instituto dos Serviços de Veterinária or an agent experienced with this specific corridor before locking in a vaccination date. Getting the window wrong here means revaccinating and losing weeks, not a minor scheduling headache.
