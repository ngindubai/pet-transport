  sections:
    - heading: "The document chain: USDA to Stockholm"
      body: |
        The health certificate process for Sweden is the same as any EU listed-country destination. A USDA-accredited veterinarian examines your pet and completes the EU Annex IV certificate. USDA APHIS then endorses it, at a cost of USD 38, typically within two to five business days. The endorsed certificate is valid for 10 days from the examination date to the pet's arrival in Sweden.

        SAS Scandinavian Airlines is the natural choice from JFK, ORD, and LAX, with established cargo procedures at Stockholm Arlanda Airport (ARN). Delta has seasonal direct services from Atlanta. Lufthansa via Frankfurt is reliable for other US gateways. All operate pets as manifested cargo, with no cabin option on transatlantic routes.

        Stockholm Arlanda has a Veterinary Border Inspection Post (BIP) for EU border checks. For dogs and cats from the USA with correct documentation, the check at ARN is typically straightforward and completed quickly.

    - heading: "Living with a dog in Sweden"
      body: |
        Sweden is consistently rated among the most pet-friendly countries in Europe. Dogs are welcome in most shops, many restaurants, and virtually all outdoor public spaces. Public transport in Sweden has relaxed rules for well-behaved dogs. Off-lead walking is permitted in many forests and parks outside urban areas.

        Swedish culture places a high value on animal welfare. The Animal Welfare Act (Djurskyddslagen) sets detailed standards for how dogs must be kept, including minimum exercise requirements and rules on leaving dogs alone. The Swedish Board of Agriculture (Jordbruksverket) enforces these rules. For anyone moving with a dog, understanding Swedish welfare standards is worth doing before arrival.

        There is no federal breed ban in Sweden, but dogs must be kept under control in public. The Swedish rules focus on individual dog behaviour rather than breed type, which is a different approach from countries like Denmark or Germany that have specific breed lists.

faqs:
  - question: "Does my dog or cat need a rabies titre test to enter Sweden from the USA?"
    answer: "No. Sweden is an EU member state, and the USA is a listed third country under EU pet travel regulations. Dogs and cats from the USA do not need a titre test to enter Sweden. You need an ISO-compliant microchip, current rabies vaccination, and a USDA-APHIS endorsed health certificate in EU Annex IV format."
  - question: "Which airlines fly pet cargo from the USA to Stockholm?"
    answer: "SAS Scandinavian Airlines operates direct services from JFK, ORD, and LAX to Stockholm Arlanda (ARN). Delta has seasonal direct services from Atlanta. Lufthansa routes via Frankfurt are a good option from other US cities. All accept pets as manifested cargo. No cabin pets on transatlantic routes."
  - question: "Does Sweden have breed-specific restrictions for dogs?"
    answer: "Sweden does not have a federal breed ban but applies strict welfare regulations to all dogs under the Animal Welfare Act (Djurskyddslagen). Dogs must be kept under control in public. Some municipalities may have additional rules, but Sweden's approach focuses on individual dog behaviour rather than specific breeds. The Swedish Board of Agriculture (Jordbruksverket) can advise on current requirements."
  - question: "How long does the USDA health certificate stay valid for travel to Sweden?"
    answer: "The certificate is valid for 10 days from the date of the vet examination to the pet's scheduled arrival in Sweden. USDA APHIS endorsement takes two to five business days after submission. Plan the vet appointment so the certificate is endorsed and valid for the arrival date."
  - question: "Does my dog need to be registered in Sweden after arrival?"
    answer: "Yes. Dogs in Sweden must be registered in the national dog registry (Nationellt hundregister) run by Jordbruksverket. This is done after arrival, not as a pre-travel requirement. The registration includes the dog's microchip number, owner details, and breed. There is a small fee."
  - question: "Are there cold weather restrictions on pet cargo to Sweden in winter?"
    answer: "Yes. Airlines apply cold restrictions when temperatures fall below certain thresholds during transit. Swedish winters are genuinely cold, with temperatures in Stockholm dropping well below freezing from November to February. Short-coated breeds may be refused for cargo shipments during cold periods. Confirm cold weather restrictions with your airline if travelling in winter."
