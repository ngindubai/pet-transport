  overview: |
    A country with no domestic rabies still has to prove it on paper for the EU. Seychelles keeps the disease out through strict controls on incoming animals, but none of that status carries over when a pet leaves Mahe for Frankfurt. Germany classes Seychelles the same way the rest of the bloc does, as a country outside its low-risk list, so the titre test and 90-day wait apply in full.

    The upside is that Seychelles' own export side moves quickly. Once the German clock starts, the remaining paperwork is comparatively light.

  sections:
    - heading: "Why a rabies-free island still needs the full titre process"
      body: |
        It reads as backwards at first. Seychelles enforces mandatory quarantine on every incoming pet regardless of origin, precisely because it wants to stay rabies-free. But the EU's own classification runs on whether a country sits on its published low-risk list, not on the country's actual disease status, and Seychelles is not on that list. A titre test taken before the 30-day mark after vaccination invalidates the whole sequence and forces a repeat.

    - heading: "Routing through Abu Dhabi or Dubai to Frankfurt"

        Etihad Cargo via Abu Dhabi and Emirates SkyCargo via Dubai are the two established options for this corridor, both running dedicated live-animal facilities that give a crate a genuine rest stop mid-journey.
