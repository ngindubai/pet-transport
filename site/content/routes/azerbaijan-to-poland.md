  sections:
    - heading: "Preparing the blood sample for the FAVN test from Baku"
      body: |
        There is no EU-approved FAVN laboratory in Azerbaijan. Your vet in Baku draws the blood and ships it cold to an approved lab in Europe or North America. Most owners on this corridor use labs in Germany, Belgium, or the UK. The European Commission list of approved labs is at food.ec.europa.eu.

        The shipping process: your vet draws blood from a vein (usually jugular or cephalic), stores the serum sample in a suitable preservative (commonly sodium azide at 0.5%), packs it in a biological specimen shipper meeting IATA P650 requirements, and dispatches it via DHL, FedEx, or another international courier with biological sample handling. The courier journey from Baku to a Western European lab takes 2-5 business days. The lab processes the sample within 1-2 weeks of receipt.

        If your vet has not sent titre test samples internationally before, use an IPATA-registered agent who works regularly with Azerbaijani vets and knows which labs give reliable results on Caucasus-origin samples.

    - heading: "What LOT Polish Airlines offers on this corridor"
      body: |
        LOT Polish Airlines (Warsaw-based national carrier) operates from Warsaw Chopin to several hub destinations and has code-share partnerships. For live animal cargo from Baku to Warsaw, LOT Cargo at WAW is a useful arrival handling option, familiar with EU unlisted third country documentation.

        For the Baku leg, LOT does not operate direct services from Azerbaijan. The most practical routing to connect with LOT at WAW is still via Istanbul with Turkish Airlines, or via Frankfurt with AZAL. Your cargo agent can advise on whether LOT Cargo or Turkish Airlines Cargo handles the arrival documentation more smoothly for your specific dates.

    answer: "The GIW (Chief Veterinary Inspectorate) border vet at Warsaw Chopin cargo terminal checks the microchip, verifies the chip-before-vaccination sequence, reviews the FAVN titre test certificate for the blood draw date and result, confirms 90 days have elapsed from the blood draw date to the AHC travel date, and checks the AHC was issued within 10 days. If all checks pass, the pet is released. Conditional quarantine at the owner's expense applies if any check fails."
