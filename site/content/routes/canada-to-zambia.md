  h1: "Pet Transport from Canada to Zambia"
  overview: |
    Fly a dog the other way on this corridor, from Zambia into the US, and the CDC's high-risk list forces a titre test or a facility quarantine booking. Send a Canadian pet into Zambia and none of that applies: Zambia's own import rules don't run an equivalent high-risk framework, so a vaccination timed correctly and a permit from Lusaka cover the requirement.

    Ethiopian Airlines makes the flight side just as clean, running its own nonstop Toronto-Addis Ababa service onward to Lusaka, keeping the whole booking on one carrier.

  sections:
    - heading: "Why this direction is lighter than the return trip"
      body: |
        A pet flying from Zambia into the US faces the CDC's full high-risk-country pathway: microchip before vaccine, a titre test from a CDC-approved lab, arrival restricted to designated facility airports. None of that applies here. Zambia's own rules simply want a rabies vaccination given between 30 days and 12 months before entry, a microchip, and a Veterinary Permit obtained in advance.

        Worth keeping proof of a titre result on file anyway, even though Zambia doesn't require one itself. If the connecting routing passes through a country or airline with its own serology rule, having the paperwork ready avoids a scramble at a transit point.

    - heading: "The permit that runs on correspondence, not a portal"
      body: |
        Zambia's Department of Veterinary Services, formally the Department of Research & Specialist Services, doesn't publish an online application for its import permit. Getting one means writing directly to Mulungushi House in Lusaka and allowing real processing time, since there's no published turnaround figure to plan against.

        The health certificate that accompanies your pet is built using that permit as a template, so the sequencing matters: get the permit application moving well before booking the vet appointment for the certificate itself.

    - heading: "One carrier, start to finish"
      body: |
        Ethiopian Airlines' Toronto-Addis Ababa nonstop, paired with its own onward Addis Ababa-Lusaka service, is the single-carrier option for this route. That matters on a corridor where most bookings need at least one change of airline partway through.

        South African Airways' historical Johannesburg-Lusaka link remains a fallback, schedule permitting, though it needs a separate transatlantic carrier to cover the Toronto or Montreal leg into Johannesburg first.
