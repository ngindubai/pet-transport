content:
  h1: "Pet Transport from China to Iceland"
  overview: |
    China recognises Iceland as rabies-free enough to sit on its own designated country list, at minimum exempting an Icelandic pet from the rabies titre test moving the other way. Iceland does not extend the same courtesy back. MAST's Category 1 and Category 2 lists between them cover roughly 40 countries, and China is on neither, so this direction runs on a narrow exception rather than a standard process.

    The way in mirrors the exception that applies to any non-listed origin: the pet must have lived with its owner in China for at least 6 months, and the owner must be genuinely relocating to Iceland. Clear that gate and the process runs to Category 2 rules, complete with a 90-day post-titre wait and Iceland's mandatory 14-day quarantine.

  sections:
    - heading: "A recognition that doesn't run both ways"
      body: |
        Look at the outbound direction on this site, Iceland to China, and the regulatory picture is noticeably lighter: GACC's own designated country list includes Iceland, alongside Japan, the UK and Switzerland, which removes the rabies titre test requirement entirely. Coming the other way, MAST offers no equivalent recognition of China.

        Two regulators, two entirely separate judgements. China trusts Iceland's rabies-free record. MAST simply hasn't made a formal classification decision about China either way, which leaves this direction dependent on an individual exception rather than a published rule.

    - heading: "The exception that decides whether this move happens"
      body: |
        Without a country classification, MAST's ordinary process has no route open for a Chinese departure. The workaround is the same one applied to any unlisted origin: an owner who has genuinely lived with their pet in China for at least 6 months and is relocating permanently to Iceland. It is not designed for a pet acquired shortly before the move.

        Clear that gate and the health requirements mirror the US-to-Iceland process already published on this site: vaccination, a titre test 30 days later, then a further 90-day wait from the blood draw, on top of the fixed 14-day quarantine at Reykjanesbae or Mosel.

    - heading: "A Chinese hub, a European hub, then Keflavik"
      body: |
        Air China and Cathay Pacific both offer dependable cargo capacity out of major Chinese cities toward European hubs, with Icelandair covering the final connection into Keflavik. Two separate bookings, not one through fare, so build in a genuine buffer between legs given the distance involved and the different handling standards each cargo desk applies to live animals.

faqs:
  - question: "Can I bring my pet from China to Iceland?"
    answer: "Only if you qualify for MAST's exception for non-listed countries: the pet must have lived with you in China for at least 6 months, and you must be genuinely relocating to Iceland with it. China is not on MAST's approved Category 1 or Category 2 list. Confirm your situation with MAST (petimport@mast.is) before starting any vaccination schedule."
  - question: "Why is Iceland to China easier than this direction?"
    answer: "Because China's own regulator, GACC, already places Iceland on its designated list of rabies-free countries and exempts it from the titre test on that leg. MAST has not made the equivalent classification for China, so pets moving this way default to the stricter Category 2 process instead."
  - question: "Does my pet need a titre test for this route?"
    answer: "Yes, once MAST accepts the import under Category 2 conditions: blood drawn at least 30 days after the rabies vaccination, minimum 0.5 IU/ml, followed by a 90-day wait from the blood draw before travel."
  - question: "Is there quarantine on arrival in Iceland?"
    answer: "Yes, a mandatory minimum 14 days at Einangrunarstodin i Reykjanesbae or Einangrunarstodin Mosel, applying to every pet entering Iceland regardless of origin."
  - question: "How long should I budget for this move?"
    answer: "Between 22 and 28 weeks, roughly five to six months. Most of that comes from the 30-day pre-titre wait plus the 90-day post-titre wait, alongside the time needed to gather the residency and ownership evidence MAST wants before it will treat a Chinese departure as eligible at all."
