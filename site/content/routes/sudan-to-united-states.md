    Sudan sits on the CDC's high-risk countries list for dog rabies, last updated 15 April 2026. On this route, it's only half the story.

    The other half is Khartoum International Airport, which only returned to scheduled international flights on 12 February 2026, after nearly three years of closure during Sudan's civil conflict. Twenty-eight aircraft were reportedly destroyed on the ground. Sudanese airspace was suspended again briefly in early May 2026 after a drone attack. No airline's live pet cargo acceptance out of Khartoum could be confirmed as currently, reliably running at the time of this page's research.

This route combines the CDC's toughest paperwork tier with genuine, current uncertainty about whether a pet can reliably leave the country at all. Anyone planning this move needs direct, current, written confirmation from both the Sudanese and airline side before setting a date.
