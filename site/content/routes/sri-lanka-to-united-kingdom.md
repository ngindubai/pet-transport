  sections:
    - heading: "DAPH and the Sri Lanka export certificate"
      body: |
        DAPH (Department of Animal Production and Health), under Sri Lanka's Ministry of Agriculture, is the authority responsible for animal health documentation for exports. The health certificate for UK import must be issued by a DAPH-authorised official vet and endorsed by DAPH with an official stamp.

        The certificate must include: your pet's ISO microchip number, species and breed, vaccination history including titre test result (laboratory name, blood draw date, result in IU/ml, and the date 90 days from the draw), and for dogs the tapeworm treatment details. DAPH offices operate in Colombo and other provincial centres.

        Contact DAPH (daph.gov.lk) for the current list of authorised vets and the certificate format required for UK export. Allow time for DAPH endorsement after the vet completes the certificate: typically 1 to 3 working days. The fully endorsed certificate must be in your possession within 10 days of travel.

    - heading: "SriLankan Airlines direct and Gulf hub options"
      body: |
        SriLankan Airlines operates direct cargo flights from Bandaranaike International Airport (CMB) in Colombo to London Heathrow (LHR). A direct routing from Sri Lanka to the UK is a genuine advantage: it avoids transit country complications and reduces total journey time for your pet.

        Contact SriLankan Cargo (srilankan.com) directly to confirm current live animal policies, crate dimension requirements, and seasonal restrictions. Availability and aircraft type on the direct route should be confirmed before booking.

        If the direct route is full or unsuitable, Emirates (via Dubai), Qatar Airways (via Doha), and Etihad (via Abu Dhabi) all serve Colombo frequently and accept live animals in cargo on suitable aircraft. Gulf transit facilities are generally well-equipped for live animal handling. Confirm on each individual leg.
