  sections:
    - heading: "DAPH's process and why you should start early"
      body: |
        Sri Lanka's Department of Animal Production and Health (DAPH) handles the export permit and endorses the official health certificate for animals leaving the country. The process involves submitting documentation, and in some cases, DAPH conducts a physical inspection of the animal before issuing the permit.

        DAPH processing times can extend beyond six weeks, especially if additional information is requested. Starting the permit application at the same time as the titre test planning, rather than sequentially, allows both processes to run in parallel and reduces the overall timeline. Working with a local agent who has experience with DAPH paperwork is advisable.

        The DAPH-endorsed health certificate has a 10-day validity from the date of examination. The cargo booking needs to fall within that window. Get the DAPH permit first, then finalise the certificate and cargo date together.

    - heading: "Colombo cargo options for London"
      body: |
        Colombo Bandaranaike International (CMB) has reasonable cargo connections to London. SriLankan Airlines offers a direct CMB-LHR service, which is the cleanest routing for UK-bound pet cargo. It avoids additional transits and keeps the journey as short as possible.

        Emirates via Dubai and Qatar Airways via Doha are the main hub-routing alternatives. Both carriers have established live animal cargo operations and experienced ground handling for pets in transit. The Dubai transit (Emirates) introduces summer temperature restrictions: if your cargo travels through Dubai between June and September, confirm the carrier's temperature embargo policy for your pet's breed and size.
