content:
  h1: "Pet Transport from Honduras to the United Kingdom"
  overview: |
    A rabies vaccination certificate on its own will not get a dog or cat through UK border checks from Honduras. Great Britain splits the world into listed and unlisted countries for pet travel, and Honduras sits outside both lists, alongside most of Central America bar Mexico. That single fact drives almost everything about this route: a titre test, a fixed waiting period measured in calendar months, and a GB health certificate replace what would otherwise be a simple 21-day vaccination wait.

    The flying side adds its own friction. Nobody operates Honduras to London nonstop, and Avianca, one of the region's main carriers, will not carry pets on UK-bound flights at all. Most pets travel Honduras to Miami or Houston first, then transfer to a transatlantic carrier for the last leg into Heathrow. Getting the paperwork and the flight timing to line up is the real job here.

  sections:
    - heading: "Why the rabies titre test decides your travel date"
      body: |
        Once the microchip and vaccination are done in the right order, the titre test blood draw has to happen at least 30 days later. Whatever the result, Great Britain then requires a further 3 calendar months to pass before the pet can travel, and that clock starts on the day blood was drawn, not the day the lab confirms a pass. Owners who assume the wait starts when they get the result usually lose 2 to 4 weeks they didn't plan for.

        Add it up from a standing start (microchip, vaccination, 30-day wait, blood draw, 3-month wait, paperwork) and 4 to 6 months is realistic. Pets that already carry a valid rabies vaccination and simply need the titre test and wait can move faster, sometimes in 3 to 4 months.

    - heading: "SENASA's export certificate is the easy part"
      body: |
        Getting a pet legally out of Honduras is straightforward by comparison. A licensed Honduran vet issues a fitness-to-travel certificate, the vaccination card needs to show current vaccines, and the whole thing is submitted online through SENASA's SECEH export platform for a flat Lps 450 fee. Approval usually comes back to your inbox within minutes of submitting, not days.

        Don't let that speed create false confidence about the rest of the move. The SENASA certificate confirms the pet is healthy and legally exportable; it says nothing about whether the pet has cleared the GB titre test and wait. Those two processes run on completely separate timelines, and the SENASA side will always finish first.

    - heading: "Getting from Tegucigalpa or San Pedro Sula to Heathrow"
      body: |
        Palmerola International (XPL), which replaced the old Toncontín airport in 2021, and Ramón Villeda Morales International in San Pedro Sula (SAP) are the two commercial gateways. American Airlines flies XPL to Miami and Dallas-Fort Worth; United flies XPL to Houston. Both give a workable first leg for pet cargo.

        The transatlantic leg is a separate transaction. British Airways' cargo arm, IAG Cargo, receives shipments at Heathrow from US gateway cities, but live animal bookings that don't originate in North America or the Caribbean have to be arranged through an IPATA- or ATA-registered pet relocation agent rather than booked directly. In practice that means budgeting for an agent to handle the Miami or Houston handover, not just the airline fare.

faqs:
  - question: "Is Honduras a listed country for pet travel to Great Britain?"
    answer: "No. Honduras does not appear on either GB's Part 1 or Part 2 listed-country tables, so it is treated as an unlisted country. That means a rabies antibody titre test, a 3-calendar-month wait counted from the blood draw date, and a GB Pet Health Certificate are required, not just a vaccination record. Source: GOV.UK, 'Bringing your pet dog, cat or ferret to Great Britain,' 2026."
  - question: "Does a pet need an APHA import licence to travel from Honduras to the UK?"
    answer: "Not if it meets the standard pet travel rules: microchip, rabies vaccination, titre test, 3-month wait, and GB Pet Health Certificate. An import licence from APHA is only needed when a pet is going into approved quarantine because it hasn't met those rules. Source: GOV.UK, 2026."
  - question: "How much does the SENASA export certificate cost in Honduras?"
    answer: "The Certificado Zoosanitario de Exportación carries a fixed government fee of Lps 450, roughly USD 18-20, plus two Lps 10 veterinary college stamps. Several pets of the same species can be listed on one certificate. Source: SENASA, 'Instructivo para Trámites para la Exportación de Mascotas,' senasa.gob.hn."
  - question: "Can a pet fly from Honduras to London on one airline?"
    answer: "No single-carrier routing exists; there's no nonstop service. Pets typically fly Honduras to Miami or Houston on American Airlines or United, then transfer to a transatlantic carrier serving Heathrow. Avianca, which also serves Honduras, does not carry pets on flights to the UK, so it can't form part of that final leg."
