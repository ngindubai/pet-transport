content:
  h1: "Pet Transport from Tunisia to the United Kingdom"
  overview: |
    Tunisia does not appear on Great Britain's list of Part 1 or Part 2 rabies-controlled countries, so a pet leaving Tunis has to clear the same titre test sequence as a pet leaving anywhere else classed as unlisted. That means a blood test at least 30 days after vaccination, then a further three calendar months before the animal can legally land at a UK airport. There is no way to shorten either wait.

    The upside is that once the biology is satisfied, the rest of the process is routine. DGSV endorsement in Tunis takes a few working days, the GB health certificate is a 10-day window you time close to departure, and Tunisair flies the route direct three times a week. Budget five to six months from a standing start, most of it spent waiting rather than doing.

  sections:
    - heading: "Why Tunisia sits in the unlisted category"
      body: |
        Great Britain splits pet-travel countries into two listed groups (mostly the EU, EFTA states and a short list of others such as the USA, Canada and the UAE) plus everyone else, who fall under the unlisted rules. Tunisia is not on either listed table, based on the current gov.uk guidance checked for this page. The CDC also carries Tunisia on its high-risk list for dog-mediated rabies, which lines up with why the titre test applies here rather than the shorter 21-day listed-country wait.

        In practice this changes one thing: the mandatory blood test and the fixed three-month wait afterwards. Everything else, microchip first, vaccination second, a GB health certificate close to travel, is the same paperwork any pet needs to enter the UK.

    - heading: "The titre test sequence, start to finish"
      body: |
        Blood can't be drawn until 30 full days have passed since the rabies vaccination. Send the sample to a laboratory on the APHA-approved list, not just any accredited vet lab in Tunisia; APHA publishes its own list and a result from an unlisted lab will be rejected regardless of the number on the certificate. Results usually take two to four weeks to come back once the lab has the sample in hand.

        A result of 0.5 IU/ml or higher starts a three-calendar-month countdown from the date of the draw, not the date the paperwork arrives. Get this wrong and the whole schedule slips by however many days you miscounted. If the result comes back under 0.5 IU/ml, the pet needs revaccinating and the 30-day wait starts again before a second sample can be taken.

    - heading: "Getting from Tunis to London"
      body: |
        Tunisair is the only carrier flying nonstop between Tunis-Carthage and London Heathrow, roughly three services a week at just over three hours' flying time. Small pets travel in the cabin under 8kg; larger dogs go as checked baggage or, above 32kg combined weight, as cargo. Book through the airline's special services line well ahead of the date, since pet space on a route with only a handful of weekly departures fills fast.

        If the direct schedule doesn't line up with the compliance dates, Air France via Paris Charles de Gaulle is the practical fallback, with a wider hold allowance and its own cargo arm for anything too large for the hold.
