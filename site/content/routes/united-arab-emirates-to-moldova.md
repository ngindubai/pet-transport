  h1: "Pet Transport from United Arab Emirates to Moldova"
  overview: |
    flydubai flies Dubai to Chisinau nonstop three times a week, Monday, Wednesday and Sunday. It's the only Gulf carrier with a direct scheduled link into Moldova, and it takes pets, but only through its cargo operation, never in the cabin.

    That single fact shapes most of this route. Combine flydubai's cargo-only direct service with Moldova's own light-touch import rule, no titre test for a UAE-vaccinated pet under the country's current EU-aligned classification, and this becomes one of the more straightforward moves on this site involving a Central Asian or Gulf departure point. Budget 5 to 8 weeks, most of it spent on the MOCCAE export paperwork rather than anything Moldova itself demands.

  sections:
    - heading: "Why the flydubai schedule matters more than the paperwork"
      body: |
        Three flights a week sounds like plenty until a MOCCAE export certificate, timed against the flight rather than sitting on a shelf, has to land on exactly the right day. Miss the Monday, Wednesday or Sunday departure and the next option is a multi-day wait, or a switch to Turkish Airlines via Istanbul, which adds a connection and a second airline's paperwork into the mix.

        flydubai carries live animals as cargo only, with the usual snub-nosed breed exclusion applying. A cat or a mid-sized dog in a compliant crate has no trouble with this; a Bulldog or a Pug needs a different carrier from the outset.

    - heading: "Moldova's side of the deal is genuinely light"
      body: |
        No titre test, no import permit for a personal pet, no quarantine for compliant paperwork. Moldova's veterinary authority, ANSA, has aligned much of its animal health framework with the EU as part of the country's accession process, and that alignment extends a titre exemption to a broad set of low-risk origins, the UAE among them under current published guidance.

        The one number worth remembering is five: Moldova caps non-commercial pet movements at five animals per owner. Anyone moving more than that needs the commercial import route, a different process entirely.
