  h1: "Pet Transport UK to Malta: AHC, Tapeworm Treatment and What to Prepare"
  overview: "Malta is an EU member state and the UK holds listed third country status, so UK pets do not need a titre test or routine quarantine to enter Malta.   sections:
    - heading: "The tapeworm treatment requirement"
      body: |
        Malta requires all dogs entering from the UK to receive treatment against Echinococcus multilocularis (commonly called tapeworm) from a veterinarian. The timing window is strict: the treatment must be given no more than 120 hours and no fewer than 24 hours before the dog arrives in Malta.

        The vet who administers the treatment must record the product used, the dose, and the date and time of administration either on the AHC or on a separate signed certificate that travels with the dog. Malta's Veterinary Regulation and Animal Welfare Directorate can refuse entry to dogs where the treatment is missing, administered at the wrong time, or recorded incorrectly.

        Plan the tapeworm appointment around your departure time. If your flight departs at 09:00 and lands in Malta four hours later, the treatment window for the dog is 09:00 minus 120 hours (five days before), at the earliest, to 13:00 minus 24 hours (the day before), at the latest. Book the vet appointment within that window and allow some buffer.

    - heading: "Summer heat and when to plan your move"
      body: |
        Malta sits in the central Mediterranean and summer temperatures regularly climb above 35 degrees Celsius, with peak months (July and August) averaging 32 to 34 degrees Celsius during the day. Airlines apply ground temperature thresholds for pet cargo, and when temperatures at origin, transit, or destination airports breach those thresholds, cargo bookings are cancelled or refused.

        The practical outcome is that pet cargo to Malta is difficult to arrange reliably from late May through September. For brachycephalic breeds (French Bulldogs, Pugs, Bulldogs, Persian cats and similar), the restrictions are tighter still and airlines may refuse these breeds entirely during Mediterranean summer.

        If you have flexibility, plan the move for October through April. Maltese winters are mild (13 to 18 degrees Celsius) and autumn and spring are pleasant. If a summer move is unavoidable, confirm the airline's specific heat embargo temperatures and aircraft type before booking, and build a contingency if the booking is cancelled on the day due to heat.

faqs:
  - question: "Do I need a titre test to take my pet to Malta from the UK?"
    answer: "No. The UK is listed by the EU as an approved third country for pet travel. No titre test is required for personal pets travelling from the UK to Malta. You need a current ISO microchip, a valid rabies vaccination with the primary course completed at least 21 days before travel, and an AHC from an APHA-authorised official vet."
  - question: "What is the tapeworm treatment, and does my cat need it?"
    answer: "The tapeworm treatment is a dose of praziquantel administered by a veterinarian between 24 and 120 hours before your dog arrives in Malta. It targets Echinococcus multilocularis. Only dogs need this treatment. Cats travelling from the UK to Malta are not subject to the tapeworm requirement."
  - question: "Can I use my UK pet passport to travel to Malta?"
    answer: "No. UK pet passports issued in Great Britain (England, Scotland, Wales) have not been valid for EU travel since January 2021. The required document for travelling from the UK to Malta is an Animal Health Certificate (AHC) issued by an APHA-authorised official vet within 10 days of travel."
  - question: "What airlines carry pets from the UK to Malta?"
    answer: "Air Malta (KM Malta Airlines) operates from London Heathrow to Malta International Airport (MLA) and accepts pet cargo. British Airways also serves this route via IAG Cargo. Both are subject to seasonal heat embargo restrictions in summer. Contact the airline cargo desk directly to confirm availability, crate dimensions, and any breed restrictions."
