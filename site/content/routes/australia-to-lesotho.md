  h1: "Pet Transport from Australia to Lesotho"
  overview: |
    Assume, reasonably, that flying a pet from Australia to a landlocked country deep in southern Africa means a Gulf hub, then Johannesburg, then a final short hop. It doesn't have to. South African Airways flies Perth to Johannesburg nonstop, eight times a week, a genuine direct long-haul leg that makes this route a two-flight journey rather than the three-flight one the geography implies.

    From Johannesburg, Airlink is the only scheduled carrier into Maseru, the same single-option connection that applies to every route into Lesotho on this site, UK and US included. The regulatory side is lighter than the routing might suggest: a vaccination record, a microchip, and an import permit, no blood test involved.

    Budget eight to twelve weeks. The import permit application, not the flight booking, is the main reason to start early.

  sections:
    - heading: "A surprise: this route is shorter than expected"
      body: |
        The instinct, moving a pet from Australia to a small enclave state surrounded entirely by South Africa, is to expect a long, multi-hub itinerary: a Gulf carrier first, then a separate long-haul sector into Johannesburg. That's not what actually happens here. South African Airways operates a nonstop Perth-Johannesburg service, roughly eleven hours, eight times a week on an Airbus A340-300, which does the whole long-haul job in one sector.

        That puts this route on the same footing, leg-for-leg, as the UK's path into Lesotho: one long-haul flight straight into O.R. Tambo, then the short Airlink hop on to Maseru. The only extra step for most Australian owners is a domestic connection to Perth if they're not already based there.

    - heading: "What Lesotho's import rules actually require"
      body: |
        A pet arriving in Lesotho needs a rabies vaccination given between 30 days and 12 months before arrival, current core vaccinations, a microchip on the paperwork, and an import permit from the Director of Animal Health, part of Lesotho's Ministry of Agriculture, Food Security and Nutrition. The permit is needed because Australia isn't one of the SADC member states that gets a waiver on this step.

        No titre test features anywhere in Lesotho's own rules, for pets from any country, and that's true for Australia specifically, not assumed from the UK or US pages already published for this destination.

    - heading: "Johannesburg, twice: once for the flight, once for the paperwork"
      body: |
        The two legs, Perth to Johannesburg and Johannesburg to Maseru, sit on separate air waybills, usually different terminals. A ground agent at O.R. Tambo, experienced with this specific handoff, is the difference between a same-day connection and a costly delay.

        Book the Airlink sector at least 48 hours ahead; it runs on its own booking rhythm, entirely separate from the long-haul leg, and has a 32kg maximum crate weight including the animal, worth confirming against your pet's crate before the international booking is locked in.
