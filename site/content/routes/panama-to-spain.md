  overview: |
    Iberia flies direct from Panama City Tocumen (PTY) to Madrid Barajas (MAD), which is the good news. The journey itself is straightforward. It is the preparation that demands your time.

    Spain is in the EU. Panama is not on the EU's approved third-country list, which means your pet needs a FAVN rabies titre test and a 3-month waiting period after the blood draw before entry is allowed. From microchip to Madrid, this route takes 22 to 28 weeks if everything goes to plan. Start the moment your move is confirmed.

  sections:
    - heading: "What does Spain require for a pet arriving from Panama?"
      body: |
        Spain applies EU Regulation 576/2013 to all pets arriving from non-listed third countries. Panama falls into that category, so the full sequence is mandatory: ISO microchip, rabies vaccination, FAVN titre test, 3-month wait, and an official Animal Health Certificate (AHC) issued within 10 days of travel.

        The order cannot be changed. Microchip first. Vaccination second (the pet must be at least 12 weeks old). Blood draw for the titre test no earlier than 30 days after vaccination. The blood goes to an EU-approved laboratory. The result must show at least 0.5 IU/ml of rabies antibodies. The 3-month countdown begins from the date blood is drawn, not from the date you receive the result. Source: food.ec.europa.eu, 2026.

        If all steps are completed correctly, your pet arrives in Spain without quarantine. Spanish veterinary inspectors at Madrid Barajas check the documentation on arrival. A single step out of sequence means the whole process restarts. The authority overseeing pet imports in Spain is MAPA, the Ministerio de Agricultura, Pesca y Alimentacion (mapa.gob.es). Confirm the current AHC format with MAPA or with your receiving vet in Spain before the certificate is issued.

        Spain also has a PPP (Perros Potencialmente Peligrosos) classification covering American Pit Bull Terriers, Rottweilers, Staffordshire Bull Terriers, American Staffordshire Terriers, Dogo Argentino, Fila Brasileiro, Tosa Inu, Akita Inu, and crosses. Import is not blocked for these breeds. However, once in Spain, the owner must hold EUR 120,000 civil liability insurance and the dog must be muzzled and on a lead in all public spaces.

    - heading: "How does the Panama export process work with MIDA?"
      body: |
        On the Panama side, MIDA (Ministerio de Desarrollo Agropecuario) is the official export authority for companion animals. Your official veterinary health certificate must be prepared by an accredited official vet and then endorsed by MIDA before your pet leaves the country. This endorsement is not a same-day service. Allow several working days for the MIDA step, and account for this in your final week of preparation.

        The certificate is the Animal Health Certificate (AHC) format required by EU third-country rules. It must be issued within 10 days of your travel date, which means the MIDA endorsement appointment falls in the final week before departure. Book the appointment as soon as your flight is confirmed.

        For most pets travelling this route, cargo is the only option. Iberia Cargo on the direct PTY-MAD service is the most efficient routing, keeping handling steps to a minimum. Copa Airlines connects via its hub network and offers an alternative if direct space is unavailable. Air France via Paris and Turkish Airlines via Istanbul both add a connection but expand your scheduling flexibility. All carriers require IATA-compliant crates. The crate must allow your pet to stand, turn, and lie down with a few centimetres clearance all round.

    - heading: "How much will it cost, and what are the most common problems?"
      body: |
        Total costs on the Panama to Spain route typically fall between EUR 1,200 and EUR 3,200. Airline cargo fees from PTY to MAD account for the largest variable, running EUR 700 to 1,800 depending on carrier, crate dimensions, and pet weight. The FAVN titre test from an EU-approved laboratory costs EUR 85-170. Add veterinary fees for the microchip, vaccination, blood draw, and health certificate, plus the MIDA endorsement charge.

        The most common mistake on this route is miscalculating the 3-month wait. People book their flight before the 90-day period has expired and then face a difficult choice: delay the move or leave the pet temporarily. Write the earliest permitted entry date in your calendar on the day blood is drawn, and work your flight booking backwards from there.

        The second most common issue is microchip sequencing. If a vet gives the rabies vaccination before confirming the microchip is in place, the vaccination does not count for EU entry purposes and the titre test process must restart from scratch. If your vet has not handled EU-bound pets before, confirm the required order with them before the appointment.

    answer: "The FAVN (Fluorescent Antibody Virus Neutralisation) test measures rabies antibodies in your pet's blood after vaccination. Spain and the EU require it for pets from non-listed countries like Panama to verify that the vaccination produced a sufficient immune response. The minimum passing level is 0.5 IU/ml. Blood must be drawn at least 30 days after vaccination, and the 3-month wait into Spain runs from the blood draw date, not the result date."
