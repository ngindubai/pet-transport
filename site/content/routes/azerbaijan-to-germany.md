  sections:
    - heading: "AZAL direct to Frankfurt: the practical cargo advantage"
      body: |
        Azerbaijan Airlines (AZAL) operates direct flights between Baku Heydar Aliyev International (GYD) and Frankfurt (FRA). For pet cargo, this is a notable convenience: a single carrier, no transit hub, and direct access to Frankfurt's cargo facilities including the Frankfurt Animal Lounge.

        AZAL Cargo accepts live animal freight on select routes. Before booking, confirm in writing that your specific flight accepts live animal cargo in the hold and that the booking includes all required documentation handover at Frankfurt. Cargo acceptance policies can vary by aircraft type and season.

        If AZAL dates do not suit, Turkish Airlines via Istanbul (IST) is the alternative. Turkish Airlines Cargo is well established on the GYD-IST-FRA corridor and IST handles significant volumes of Caucasus-to-Europe pet cargo.

    - heading: "FSAR: the authority for Azerbaijan's health certificates"
      body: |
        The Food Safety Agency of the Republic of Azerbaijan (FSAR), established in 2017, is the government body responsible for food safety, animal health, and veterinary certification in Azerbaijan. Its website is fsa.gov.az.

        For pet exports to Germany, the FSAR authorises the veterinarians who may issue EU-format health certificates and endorses those certificates before departure. The EU health certificate must be issued within 10 days of arrival in Germany and endorsed by the FSAR before travel.

        If your regular vet in Baku is not FSAR-authorised for export health certificates, the FSAR or a pet transport specialist can direct you to an authorised practitioner. Not all private vets in Azerbaijan are set up for international export documentation.

faqs:
  - question: "Does Azerbaijan's partnership agreement with the EU change the pet import rules for Germany?"
    answer: "No. The EU-Azerbaijan Partnership and Cooperation Agreement covers trade and political relations. It does not affect EU veterinary import classifications for pets. Azerbaijan is an EU unlisted country for pet travel purposes, and the full unlisted process (FAVN titre test plus 90-day wait) applies when moving a pet to Germany or any other EU member state."
  - question: "What is the minimum time from starting the process to arriving in Germany?"
    answer: "Approximately 5-6 months. Microchip and vaccination take a day. The 30-day wait before the blood draw adds a month. Sample shipping and laboratory processing adds 2-4 weeks. Then the mandatory 90-day wait from the blood draw date. The health certificate is issued in the final 10 days before travel. Allow buffer for any retest or administrative delays."
  - question: "What EU-approved laboratories can process the FAVN test from a sample shipped from Azerbaijan?"
    answer: "The EU publishes a list of approved laboratories at food.ec.europa.eu. Most approved labs are in Europe, the UK, or North America. From Baku, common choices include BIOR in Latvia or LSA in Ghent, Belgium, though your vet or transport agent should advise based on current turnaround times and shipping logistics. Always confirm the lab is on the current approved list before shipping."
