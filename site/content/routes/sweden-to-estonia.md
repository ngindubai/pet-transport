  sections:
    - heading: "Stockholm to Tallinn: your route options"
      body: |
        The Arlanda to Tallinn sector is short at around two hours. SAS serves it directly on some schedules, and airBaltic offers frequent connections through its Riga hub, with the RIX to TLL leg taking under an hour. Either way, it's one of the easier European cargo routes to arrange.

        For small pets that fit under the seat, cabin travel is often the most practical choice. SAS cabin policies allow small dogs and cats on eligible routes within their size limits. Ryanair also operates Stockholm routes but is cabin-only with no cargo option, so this is only suitable if your pet meets the weight and carrier size requirements.

        For larger dogs, cargo is the route. SAS Cargo and airBaltic Cargo both handle the Stockholm to Tallinn market. Book early, particularly in summer when there are more passengers and less cargo capacity on smaller regional aircraft.

    - heading: "EU pet passport: what Sweden requires and what Estonia accepts"
      body: |
        The EU pet passport is issued by an official vet in Sweden, authorised through Jordbruksverket. The passport records your pet's microchip number, rabies vaccination history, and the issuing vet's details. It's a permanent travel document that stays with your pet.

        Estonia's VTA (Veterinaar- ja Toiduamet) accepts the EU pet passport as the entry document. The requirements are: ISO 15-digit microchip implanted before the rabies vaccination, and a current rabies vaccination. That's it. No additional certificate, no endorsement from any authority, no import declaration.

        The one check worth doing before you book anything is the microchip-vaccination sequence. The microchip must be in place before the rabies vaccination is recorded in the passport. If your vet updated the vaccination at the same appointment as implanting the chip, or if the chip was implanted at a later date, the vaccination entry may not be valid for EU travel. A quick call to your vet to verify the dates avoids a problem at the border.
