  overview: |
    Nobody in the pet transport industry seems fully settled on one question: does Uzbekistan actually require an import permit for an accompanied pet, or not. Public guidance splits down the middle, and this route sits on the lighter side of nearly everything else. No titre test, straightforward vaccination timing, and a genuinely open permit question that's worth confirming rather than guessing at.

    The asymmetry with the reverse direction is stark. A pet travelling from Uzbekistan into Singapore faces AVS's full Schedule III process, a serology test shipped abroad for testing and a month of quarantine. A pet going the other way needs none of that.

  sections:
    - heading: "Why this direction skips so much of what the reverse route demands"
      body: |
        Uzbekistan doesn't classify Singapore as a high-rabies-risk origin, so it skips the FAVN or ELISA titre test entirely, provided two things line up: the microchip goes in before or at the same time as the rabies vaccination, and that vaccination falls between 30 days and 12 months before arrival. Miss either point and the certificate won't hold up at the Tashkent veterinary post, titre test or not.

        This lighter pathway is the reason the whole move can realistically run in 6 to 10 weeks, a fraction of the 18 to 24 weeks the reverse Uzbekistan-to-Singapore route needs, provided the AVS paperwork and the open permit question are both settled early.

    - heading: "The import permit question nobody has fully answered"
      body: |
        Some accounts of this route describe a pet arriving on the strength of the health certificate alone, no separate permit required. A 2026 process review states plainly that the Committee expects an import permit filed through vetgov.uz ahead of travel. Both descriptions can't be fully right for every case, and there's no way to know which applies to a specific booking without asking directly.

        Treat the permit as likely required until the Committee confirms otherwise. It costs little to apply and ask in advance; it costs far more to arrive at Tashkent International Airport and find the paperwork short.

    - heading: "Istanbul is the only practical connection"
      body: |
        Uzbekistan Airways has no route to Singapore, so its own weekly nonstop toward Europe and North America isn't a workable first leg here. Turkish Airlines covers the whole journey instead: Singapore Changi to Istanbul nonstop, then one of several daily Istanbul-Tashkent flights on the same carrier, avoiding a handover to a second airline partway through.
