  h1: "Pet Transport from Uzbekistan to Canada: 2026 Guide"
  overview: |
    Uzbekistan sits on the Canadian Food Inspection Agency's own list of countries at high risk for dog rabies, last updated 13 March 2025. That single fact splits this route in two. A cat travels on a fairly ordinary path: microchip, vaccination, certificate, done. A dog needs a CFIA import permit, has to meet a minimum age, and can only land at one of five Canadian airports built for this kind of inspection. Get the species-specific requirement wrong early and the schedule doesn't just slip, it can restart.

  sections:
    - heading: "Why the high-risk listing changes the whole plan for a dog"
      body: |
        CFIA doesn't ask for a rabies titre test from a high-risk country the way the UK or Australia would. Instead it leans on three things: an import permit applied for in advance, a minimum age of roughly seven to eight months at entry, and arrival strictly through Toronto Pearson, Montreal-Trudeau, Vancouver, Calgary or Halifax. Land a dog anywhere else in Canada and there's a real risk of the animal being held or turned back.

        None of this applies to a cat travelling the same route on the same day. That asymmetry surprises people moving with both species, who sometimes assume one process covers the household.
    - heading: "Getting the paperwork out of Tashkent"
      body: |
        The territorial veterinary service in Tashkent, generally the city office for most movers, issues the export certificate once the vet has confirmed the microchip and vaccination record. It's a straightforward process on the Uzbek side. The complication sits entirely on arrival in Canada: a certificate written to a generic international standard doesn't automatically carry the detail CFIA wants for a high-risk-country dog permit application, so ask the vet and the Committee's office to include everything CFIA's own guidance lists, not just what a lower-scrutiny destination would need.
    - heading: "Flights, and why none of them are direct"
      body: |
        Turkish Airlines through Istanbul currently offers the broadest weekly schedule. Uzbekistan Airways runs a nonstop first leg to Frankfurt on a Boeing 787 roughly seven times a week, which shortens the journey to a single connection once in Europe. Qatar Airways is the newest entrant on this specific city pair, flying Tashkent-Doha only since June 2024, and on a narrowbody aircraft, so a larger dog's crate dimensions need checking against that specific hold before booking.

        Whichever hub is chosen, factor in the connection time as a real leg of the journey, with its own temperature and handling risk, not a formality between two better-known cities.
