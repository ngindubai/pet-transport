content:
  h1: "Pet Transport from Serbia to Australia"
  overview: |
    A single date on a lab report, not a vet's signature, decides when this move can actually happen. Serbia is a DAFF-approved Group 3 country, so there's no need to first move the pet to a third country for 180 days, a trap that catches owners moving from genuinely unlisted origins. What Serbia doesn't avoid is Australia's own clock: a titre test, a fixed 180-day wait counted from the day the lab receives the blood sample, and quarantine at Mickleham on arrival.

    None of that is negotiable, and none of it can be rushed by paying more. Realistic total preparation time, before the flight is even booked, runs 28 to 34 weeks.

  sections:
    - heading: "Group 3 status helps, but it isn't a shortcut"
      body: |
        Some non-EU countries moving pets to Australia have to route the animal through an approved third country for six months first. Serbia skips that step entirely, because DAFF classifies it as Group 3, rabies well controlled rather than absent or unmanaged. That still leaves the full Group 3 sequence: microchip, a separate identity verification visit with a government vet, rabies vaccination, and an RNATT blood draw taken only after that identity check.

        Get the order of the identity check and the blood draw wrong, or combine them into one appointment, and the consequence isn't a delay, it's an extra three weeks tacked onto quarantine at the other end.

    - heading: "Getting to Melbourne: no direct flight, two workable routings"
      body: |
        Nobody flies Belgrade to Australia non-stop. Qatar Airways Cargo via Doha follows the same city pair Qatar's own passenger network uses, a connection of a little over 19 hours in total. Emirates SkyCargo via Dubai is the practical alternative into Sydney or Melbourne, useful when Doha cargo space is tight around peak season.

        Brachycephalic breeds face extra scrutiny on the Qatar routing specifically. If your dog or cat falls into that category, confirm acceptance with the cargo desk before committing to dates, not after.

    - heading: "Mickleham, and the difference a single vet visit makes"
      body: |
        Every dog and cat arriving from a Group 3 country goes into post-entry quarantine at Mickleham, Melbourne. The default stay is 30 days. It drops to a minimum of 10 days, but only if a government veterinarian formally verified the pet's identity before the RNATT blood was drawn, a separate appointment that has to happen in that order.

        Quarantine costs are billed on top of everything else, payable in full before the pet is released, so budget for it as a distinct line item rather than folding it into the flight quote.
