  overview: "Brazil is one of the more straightforward long-haul destinations from the UK, primarily because Brazil does not require a titre test for pets arriving from the UK.   sections:
    - heading: "No titre test: why Brazil is simpler than most long-haul moves"
      body: |
        Many long-haul destinations from the UK require a titre test and a three-month waiting period. Brazil does not. This is the single most important fact about the UK to Brazil route: without the titre test requirement, the minimum preparation time drops from five or six months to three to six weeks.

        The requirements are: ISO microchip, rabies vaccination (at least 21 days must have passed since the first vaccination), an official health certificate from an APHA-authorised OV issued within 10 days of travel, and antiparasitic treatment for dogs administered 1 to 15 days before arrival. That is the full list.

        If your pet is already microchipped and has a current rabies vaccination, you are essentially down to booking the flight, timing the health certificate, and the antiparasitic treatment window for dogs.

    - heading: "The antiparasitic treatment for dogs"
      body: |
        Dogs entering Brazil must receive an antiparasitic treatment covering both internal and external parasites within a specific window: 1 to 15 days before arrival. This is distinct from the tapeworm treatment required for UK entry (which is praziquantel-based and targets a specific parasite). Brazil's requirement is broader and covers both internal and external parasites.

        Your UK vet will administer the appropriate product and record it on the health certificate. The timing matters: treatment done more than 15 days before arrival in Brazil is outside the accepted window. For a vet appointment and health certificate within 10 days of travel, coordinating the antiparasitic treatment in the same visit is the most efficient approach.

    - heading: "Direct flights from Heathrow to Sao Paulo"
      body: |
        LATAM and British Airways both operate direct cargo services between London Heathrow and Sao Paulo Guarulhos (GRU). For pet cargo, a direct flight reduces total journey time and eliminates the complexity of a hub transfer.

        LATAM has an extensive domestic network within Brazil, which is useful if you are relocating to a city other than Sao Paulo. Pets typically arrive at GRU and are cleared through MAPA inspection before domestic onward travel can be arranged.

        Air France via Paris and KLM via Amsterdam are additional options if direct services do not fit your travel dates. Both have strong Sao Paulo connections from their European hubs.

faqs:
  - question: "Does my cat need the antiparasitic treatment for Brazil?"
    answer: "No. The antiparasitic treatment requirement in Brazil applies to dogs only. Cats do not require it. Cats travelling from the UK to Brazil need: a microchip, current rabies vaccination, and a health certificate issued within 10 days of travel meeting MAPA requirements."
  - question: "What health certificate format does Brazil require?"
    answer: "Brazil's MAPA requires an official health certificate from an authorised official veterinarian in the exporting country. For UK pets, this means an APHA-authorised OV issuing the certificate. The certificate must meet MAPA's format requirements and typically includes: microchip number, vaccination history, health examination declaration, and for dogs the antiparasitic treatment details. Your OV should be familiar with MAPA requirements, or a UK-based pet transport agent can advise."
  - question: "Can I bring more than 2 pets from the UK to Brazil?"
    answer: "Brazil's personal import rules cover up to 2 dogs or cats per person. Bringing more than 2 animals may require a different import category. Contact MAPA directly or work with a specialist agent to confirm the current rules for groups of more than 2 pets before making plans."
  - question: "Does my pet need to arrive at Sao Paulo, or can it fly directly to other Brazilian cities?"
    answer: "Most international pet cargo routes from the UK end at Sao Paulo Guarulhos (GRU), which has MAPA inspection facilities for live animal arrivals. Flying directly to other Brazilian cities is technically possible if the destination airport has MAPA inspection capability, but GRU is the established and most reliable entry point. Confirm with your airline and agent which entry airports are practical for your routing."
  - question: "How long before travel should I schedule the health certificate appointment?"
    answer: "The health certificate must be issued within 10 days of travel. For dogs, the antiparasitic treatment must also be administered 1 to 15 days before arrival. The most efficient approach is to book a vet appointment around 8 to 9 days before your flight, covering both the clinical examination for the certificate and the antiparasitic treatment in the same visit. This leaves a small buffer while keeping both within the required windows."
