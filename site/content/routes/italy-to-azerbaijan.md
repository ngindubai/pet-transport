  sections:
    - heading: "AZAL's direct Rome to Baku option"
      body: |
        Azerbaijan Airlines (AZAL) operates direct scheduled services between Rome Fiumicino (FCO) and Baku Heydar Aliyev International Airport (GYD). If AZAL Cargo is accepting live animal freight on this service, it is the most direct option for Italy-to-Azerbaijan pet transport, removing the Istanbul hub transfer that other Caucasus-bound routes require.

        Contact AZAL Cargo directly to confirm current live animal acceptance on the FCO-GYD route, crate size limits, and seasonal restrictions. If AZAL cargo is not available or does not suit your travel dates, Turkish Airlines via Istanbul (IST) is the established alternative. Turkish operates from both FCO and Milan Malpensa (MXP) to Istanbul, with onward Turkish Airlines service to Baku.

    - heading: "AQTA: Azerbaijan's food safety and import authority"
      body: |
        Azerbaijan's Food Safety Agency (AQTA) at aqta.gov.az is the competent authority for companion animal imports. Contact AQTA to understand the current import permit process and requirements. Azerbaijan's specific requirements for companion animals from EU member states are managed by AQTA.

        The permit application typically requires your pet's microchip number, vaccination history, breed, and travel date. Apply 8-10 weeks before travel. Once issued, the permit is checked by AQTA's inspection team at Baku's Heydar Aliyev International Airport (GYD) alongside the Italian health certificate.

        Azerbaijan does not apply EU-style titre test requirements for pets arriving from EU member states. Current rabies vaccination, ISO microchip, and the ASL-endorsed health certificate are the core documentation alongside the import permit.

    - heading: "Italy's ASL export process"
      body: |
        For non-EU destinations including Azerbaijan, the Italian export health certificate comes from a veterinario ufficiale at your local ASL (Azienda Sanitaria Locale). The EU pet passport covers intra-EU travel only and is not the correct document for Azerbaijan.

        Contact your local ASL veterinary department to book an appointment with the official vet. They examine your pet, check microchip and vaccination records, and issue the certificato sanitario per l'esportazione. The document is then endorsed by the regional or national veterinary authority.

        Time the ASL appointment and endorsement within 10 days of travel. ASL offices vary in their appointment availability, so contact them 3-4 weeks before your intended travel date to secure a suitable appointment slot.
