  overview: |
    Kuala Lumpur to Jakarta is one of the shortest direct flights covered on this site, roughly two hours on either Malaysia Airlines or Garuda Indonesia. That short hop doesn't shorten the paperwork: Barantin runs the same FAVN titre test, two-stage rekomendasi permit and mandatory quarantine process it applies to pets arriving from anywhere else.

    The gap between flight time and process time is the defining feature of this route.

  sections:
    - heading: "A genuinely short, direct flight"

        Both Malaysia Airlines and Garuda Indonesia fly KLIA to Jakarta non-stop, a real contrast to most of the longer-haul corridors on this site. It's also one of the cheaper cargo fares in this build, simply because the distance is so short.

    - heading: "The paperwork runs on its own clock, unrelated to flight time"
      body: |
        Barantin's rekomendasi permit needs securing before vaccination or titre testing begins, the FAVN titre test needs a 30-day post-vaccination wait, and quarantine on arrival typically runs 7 to 14 days. None of that compresses just because the flight itself takes two hours. Budget 16-20 weeks overall, the same range applied to Indonesia's longer-haul Tier B routes.
