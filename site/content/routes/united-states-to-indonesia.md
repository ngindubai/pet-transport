content:
  h1: "Pet Transport from the US to Indonesia: 2026 Requirements"
  overview: "Moving a pet from the US to Indonesia requires an import permit obtained in advance, a titre test, and acceptance that quarantine on arrival is mandatory. The permit must come from Indonesia's Ministry of Agriculture, the titre test adds several weeks to the timeline, and quarantine runs from 7 to 30 days. There are no direct cargo services from the US to Indonesia, so the journey routes through Singapore or Tokyo. Allow three to five months from the start of the titre test process to arrival."

  sections:
    - heading: "The import permit: start here before anything else"
      body: |
        The Indonesia import permit is the step that catches the most owners off guard. It must come from the Directorate General of Livestock and Animal Health Services under the Indonesian Ministry of Agriculture, and it needs to be in place before your pet travels. The application process takes at least four weeks.

        The permit is tied to your specific animal (microchip number), the port of entry, and your travel date. Changing these after the permit is issued may require a new application. A specialist agent in Indonesia can handle the application process and liaise with the Ministry on your behalf. Many US-based pet transport agents with Indonesia experience also offer this service.

    - heading: "The titre test requirement"
      body: |
        Indonesia requires a FAVN titre test confirming a rabies antibody level of at least 0.5 IU/ml. The blood must be drawn at least 30 days after the rabies vaccination, and the test must be conducted at a recognised laboratory.

        US pet owners are sometimes surprised by this requirement: Indonesia is less commonly discussed than Australia or New Zealand as a titre test destination. But the requirement is real and adds roughly six to eight weeks to the total process. Your vet draws the blood sample and sends it to an appropriate laboratory, and results typically take two to three weeks to arrive.

    - heading: "USDA APHIS endorsement timing"
      body: |
        The USDA APHIS-endorsed health certificate is a standard requirement for US pets travelling internationally. Your USDA-accredited vet issues the certificate, which must then be endorsed by the USDA APHIS regional office. Standard processing takes three to ten business days; express processing is faster.

        The certificate must be issued within 14 days of travel to Indonesia. This means the USDA endorsement and the titre test timing need to work together: titre test result confirmed, then certificate issued and endorsed, all within the 14-day window before travel. Plan this sequence carefully with your vet.

faqs:
  - question: "Can I avoid quarantine in Indonesia if my documentation is perfect?"
    answer: "No. Quarantine in Indonesia is mandatory for all arriving pets, regardless of documentation. What documentation quality affects is the duration. Complete, correctly formatted paperwork tends to result in a quarantine stay at the shorter end of the 7 to 30-day range. Incomplete or incorrectly formatted documents can extend the stay. Thorough preparation does matter, but quarantine itself cannot be avoided."
  - question: "Where does quarantine happen in Indonesia?"
    answer: "Quarantine takes place at government-approved facilities, which exist at the main ports of entry including Jakarta's Soekarno-Hatta airport and in Bali. The quarantine facility is assigned based on your port of arrival. Your agent in Indonesia can confirm which facility applies to your routing and arrange for someone to be on hand when your pet arrives."
  - question: "Can I fly directly from the US to Bali with my pet?"
    answer: "There are no direct cargo services from the US to Bali. The routing is via Singapore Changi (SIN) or another hub, then onward to Bali (DPS). Singapore Airlines is the most commonly used option for this corridor. Some routings go via Tokyo (NRT) or Hong Kong. Your agent will identify the best current connection based on your departure city and dates."
  - question: "Does the FAVN titre test need to be done at a USDA laboratory?"
    answer: "Not specifically. Indonesia requires a FAVN titre test from a recognised laboratory, but does not specify the same APHA-approved list that the UK requires. The Kansas State University Veterinary Diagnostic Laboratory and several other US labs offer FAVN testing. Confirm with BARANTAN or your Indonesian agent which laboratories are currently accepted for Indonesia entry before booking."
  - question: "How far in advance should I apply for the Indonesia import permit?"
    answer: "Apply at least four weeks before your planned travel date. Six weeks is safer if you have flexibility. The permit is issued with a specific validity window, so do not apply so far in advance that the permit expires before your travel date. Your agent in Indonesia will advise on the current processing time and the validity window when they submit the application."
