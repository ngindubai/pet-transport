  overview: |
    Fourteen breeds are barred from entering the UAE under MOCCAE's import rules, and several of the same names, Cane Corso, Boerboel, American Bully among them, are already barred from leaving Paraguay under SENACSA's own export list. Checking a dog's breed against both authorities before booking anything is the first real step on this route, not an afterthought.

  sections:
    - heading: "Why MOCCAE treats Paraguay as high-risk"
      body: |
        MOCCAE keeps a published list of countries it treats as low-risk for rabies, and it runs to around 50 names: the UK, Germany, Japan, Singapore, Australia and a scatter of others. Paraguay is not among them, which means the standard pathway applies in full: microchip before vaccination, a titre test result of 0.5 IU/ml or higher, and an import permit that has to be applied for once that result is in hand.

        The titre test result itself stays valid for 365 days provided the vaccination doesn't lapse. Where public guidance genuinely splits is whether a further waiting period applies after the blood draw, separate from the 21-day minimum tied to a first-time vaccination. Confirm this directly with MOCCAE rather than assume either version, since getting it wrong either wastes months of unnecessary waiting or risks arriving before the pet is legally cleared.

    - heading: "Two banned-breed lists, not one"
      body: |
        This is the detail most guides for this route skip. SENACSA already refuses to issue an export CVI for several breeds leaving Paraguay, Pit Bull-type dogs, American Bully, Cane Corso and multiple mastiff varieties among them. MOCCAE separately refuses entry to a similar, though not identical, list on the UAE side, checked phenotypically rather than by paperwork alone.

        A Cane Corso or a Boerboel, for instance, would struggle to clear either end of this specific route. Check both lists against the actual dog, not just its papers, before any flight gets booked.

    - heading: "Routing through Sao Paulo, or Madrid"
      body: |
        LATAM's Asuncion-Sao Paulo service, running roughly five times a week, connects into Emirates SkyCargo's nonstop Sao Paulo-Dubai flight, the only direct link between GRU and DXB and the most workable single-connection option here. Etihad has applied for slots to relaunch Abu Dhabi-Sao Paulo service in summer 2026, its first time on that route since 2017; if that goes ahead on schedule it becomes a genuine second option, but confirm current status before counting on it.

        Air Europa's nonstop Asuncion-Madrid flight, feeding into a Gulf carrier's onward Madrid-Dubai leg, is worth pricing as an alternative if Sao Paulo cargo space is tight.
