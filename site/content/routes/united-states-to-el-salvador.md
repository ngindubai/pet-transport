content:
  h1: "Pet Transport from the United States to El Salvador"
  overview: |
    Most US pet export routes run through USDA's APHIS eFile system for an endorsement fee of around USD 38. El Salvador is an exception. APHIS's own country-specific page for this route states plainly that USDA endorsement is not required, just a standard international health certificate signed by a licensed vet and prepared in duplicate. That single detail makes this one of the administratively lighter routes out of the US.

    On arrival, El Salvador does not ask for a titre test and states no routine quarantine for a pet with complete paperwork. MAG's import permit, obtainable in advance for USD 9.04 or at the airport itself on arrival, is the main destination-side requirement, valid for 30 days and good for one entry only.

  sections:
    - heading: "Why no USDA endorsement, and what replaces it"
      body: |
        Checking APHIS's own El Salvador page directly turns up a clear statement: USDA endorsement is not required for this destination. What is needed instead is an ordinary international health certificate, sometimes referred to locally as a Certificado Veterinario de Salud, issued and signed by a licensed veterinarian and prepared in duplicate. No notarisation, no consular sign-off from an El Salvadoran office in the US. The rabies vaccination record should travel alongside it.

        This is worth flagging precisely because it runs against the pattern most owners expect. If your vet's practice defaults to booking an APHIS eFile endorsement for every international pet move, mention that El Salvador is one of the destinations where that step is not required, and confirm before paying the fee.

    - heading: "MAG's import permit, and the 30-day window"
      body: |
        The Direccion General de Sanidad Vegetal y Animal, part of MAG, issues the import permit that clears a pet into El Salvador. APHIS's guidance describes this as obtainable either before travel or at the point of entry itself, which gives some flexibility if your schedule shifts close to departure. Applied for in advance, the fee runs to USD 9.04, and the permit is valid for 30 days from issue, single entry only.

        Because it cannot double as an export document for a later return trip, and because the 30-day window leaves little room for a delayed flight, most owners who apply in advance do so within a few weeks of departure rather than months out. MAG's Division de Cuarentena in Santa Tecla, reachable by phone or at cuarentena@mag.gob.sv, is the point of contact for questions on either the permit or the underlying paperwork.

    - heading: "Getting from the US to San Salvador"
      body: |
        This is one of the better-served Central American routes from the US. Avianca, San Salvador's former TACA hub, runs nonstop service from eight US cities including Miami, New York JFK, Los Angeles, Houston and Boston. American Airlines covers Miami and Dallas-Fort Worth nonstop year-round, and United adds Houston and Los Angeles, with a seasonal Washington Dulles option. None of the CDC high-risk complications that affect a dog travelling into the US from El Salvador apply on this outbound direction, since that classification concerns animals entering the United States, not leaving it. Compare current pet cargo pricing and any breed restrictions, particularly for brachycephalic breeds, with whichever carrier best matches your departure city.
