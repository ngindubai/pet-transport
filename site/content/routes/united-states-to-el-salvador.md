  h1: "Pet Transport from the United States to El Salvador"
  overview: |
    USD 9.04. That is the entire fee MAG charges for the entry authorisation a pet needs to come into El Salvador from the US, and it buys a document valid for 30 days and one use. No titre test, no quarantine stay, no breed ban for dogs. On paper this is one of the more straightforward Latin American routes a US pet owner will encounter.

    The friction sits somewhere else entirely: airline capacity. American flies direct from Dallas-Fort Worth and Miami into San Salvador, but its checked-pet cargo service is currently suspended for the general public and restricted to military and Foreign Service orders. That leaves Avianca and Copa carrying most of the pet traffic on this corridor, each with its own booking lead time and, in Copa's case, a breed exclusion list worth checking early.

    Get the sequencing right and this route moves fast by regional standards. Get the MAG authorisation's 30-day clock wrong, relative to a shifting flight date, and you will be reapplying and repaying the fee.

  sections:
    - heading: "A genuinely light import process, once you find the flight"
      body: |
        El Salvador does not require a rabies titre test from any country of origin, the United States included. It does not impose a quarantine period on a pet arriving with its paperwork in order. It has no published breed ban for dogs, and only asks extra documentation of a narrow set of exotic-hybrid cat breeds. Compared with the CDC's own high-risk-country machinery that a dog coming the other way, from El Salvador into the US, has to work through, this is a noticeably lighter process.

        The one document to get right is MAG's entry authorisation. It costs USD 9.04, needs the import request form, the vaccination record and an official zoosanitary certificate from the country of origin, and is valid for 30 days from issue, single use only. Applying too far ahead of a firm travel date is the single most common way owners waste that fee and have to reapply.

    - heading: "The airline picture has changed more than the paperwork has"
      body: |
        A few years ago, booking a pet in the hold on American out of Dallas-Fort Worth or Miami would have been the obvious choice for this route. That is no longer the case for most owners. American's PetEmbark checked-pet service is currently limited to active military and State Department Foreign Service personnel travelling under official orders, which removes American as a general-public cargo option regardless of how simple El Salvador's own import rules are.

        Avianca and Copa fill that gap. Avianca offers the wider US network, with direct or one-stop options from most major cities, while Copa routes everything through Panama City, a country not on the CDC high-risk list, so nothing extra gets added at that connection. Copa's breed exclusion list rules out several bully-type and brachycephalic breeds from its cargo hold, worth checking before you commit to a specific carrier.

faqs:
  - question: "Does a pet need a rabies titre test to enter El Salvador from the US?"
    answer: "No. El Salvador does not require a rabies antibody titre test from any country of origin, including the United States. A current rabies vaccination record, given within the last 12 months, is what MAG actually asks for."
  - question: "How much does El Salvador's import authorisation cost?"
    answer: "USD 9.04. MAG issues an entry authorisation valid for 30 days and for a single entry, applied for with an import request form, the vaccination record and an official zoosanitary certificate from the country of origin, in person at MAG's Santa Tecla office or online."
  - question: "Can I fly my pet to El Salvador on American Airlines?"
    answer: "For a small dog or cat that fits under the cabin size limit, yes. For a larger dog needing the cargo hold, not currently as a general-public booking, since American's PetEmbark checked-pet service is limited to active military and State Department Foreign Service travel on official orders. Avianca and Copa remain the working options for a pet that needs the hold."
  - question: "Is there a quarantine period for pets entering El Salvador?"
    answer: "Not if the paperwork is complete and correct on arrival. A quarantine permit is issued as a formality at the port of entry, but a pet with a valid rabies vaccination record, health certificate and MAG entry authorisation should clear without physical confinement. Non-compliant pets can face confinement, return to origin, or worse, entirely at the owner's cost, so get the documents right before you fly."
