content:
  h1: "Pet Transport from Trinidad and Tobago to France"
  overview: |
    London is a mandatory stop on this route, not an option. British Airways is the only carrier flying nonstop from Piarco anywhere, and its destination is Gatwick, so a Trinidad-to-Paris pet move always makes a second connection once it lands in the UK.

    France then applies the standard EU sequence for a country outside its low-risk list: a titre test and a fixed 90-day wait from the blood draw, exactly as it does for any unlisted origin.

  sections:
    - heading: "The mandatory London transfer"
      body: |
        Caribbean Airlines ended its only European service in January 2016 and has never resumed a Trinidad-to-Europe route. That leaves British Airways, through IAG Cargo, as the sole nonstop option out of Piarco, landing at Gatwick rather than continental Europe.

        Air France Cargo then handles the connecting leg into Paris CDG, where the Roissy Animal Hotel, run jointly with KLM and Martinair Cargo, manages the arrival inspection. Book this second leg as its own step, since it isn't an automatic connection through the same airline.

    - heading: "The EU titre test sequence"
      body: |
        Trinidad and Tobago sits outside the EU's list of low-risk third countries, so France requires the full protocol: microchip before vaccination, a 30-day gap before the titre blood draw, a result of at least 0.5 IU/ml, then a fixed 90 days from the draw date, under Commission Delegated Regulation (EU) 2026/131.

        The London transfer can be arranged in parallel with that wait; it's the 90-day clock, not the connecting flight, that decides how soon the pet can actually travel.
