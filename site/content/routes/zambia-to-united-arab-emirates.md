content:
  h1: "Pet Transport from Zambia to the United Arab Emirates"
  overview: |
    Emirates flies Lusaka to Dubai nonstop, a seven-hour, ten-minute run on a Boeing 777 under flight numbers EK713 and EK714. That single fact makes this route noticeably more straightforward on the flying side than Zambia's routes to the UK, where every option needs a connection.

    The paperwork carries its own logic. MOCCAE treats Zambia as a high-risk rabies country, since it isn't on the roughly 50-country low-risk list, so a titre test is required. Unlike Great Britain's rules for the same underlying risk classification, though, the UAE doesn't add a multi-month wait once that test clears.

  sections:
    - heading: "High risk, but not GB's timeline"
      body: |
        MOCCAE's low-risk list runs to about 50 countries, among them the UK, Germany, Japan and Australia. Zambia isn't one of them, so the FAVN or RNATT titre test applies, along with a higher minimum import age of 15 weeks rather than 12.

        The test itself has to be drawn at least 21 days after vaccination, with the result staying within 12 months of the travel date. What's genuinely different from the UK route is what happens next: MOCCAE doesn't impose a further waiting period once the titre clears. Once vaccination, the 21-day gap and a valid result are in place, the pet is clear to move on that front, which shaves months off what the same starting point would take heading to Great Britain.

    - heading: "The 30-day permit window"
      body: |
        The import permit itself is quick and cheap by comparison: AED 200, applied for online, and valid for 30 days from issue. That window is the real scheduling constraint on this route. Apply too early and it expires before the flight; leave it too late and there's no buffer if the Zambian vet appointment slips.

        The practical order that works is titre test result confirmed first, then flight booked, then the permit applied for once the departure date sits comfortably inside that 30-day window.

    - heading: "One reliable flight, one clear alternative"
      body: |
        Emirates' direct Lusaka-Dubai service is currently the standout option, and Emirates SkyCargo handles all pet bookings as manifested cargo rather than offering any cabin option. Brachycephalic breeds are accepted, but only with extra conditions and subject to the seasonal heat embargoes the Gulf region runs during the hottest months.

        Etihad Airways doesn't fly Abu Dhabi to Lusaka nonstop, so a pet routed that way needs at least one connection and around 10 hours 40 minutes of total travel time, longer than flying direct into Dubai. Kenya Airways via Nairobi is a further fallback if the Emirates flight doesn't have capacity on the dates needed, though it adds a connection of its own.
