  sections:
    - heading: "Copenhagen to Tallinn: your carrier options"
      body: |
        Copenhagen Kastrup (CPH) is a major Scandinavian hub with multiple connections to Tallinn. SAS serves the route directly on some schedules, with cargo available through SAS Cargo. airBaltic connects through its Riga hub, with the RIX to TLL leg being a short 30-minute hop.

        For small pets in the cabin, both SAS and airBaltic are solid options, each with standard cabin pet policies. For large dogs needing cargo, airBaltic's strength at Riga makes it a practical choice, particularly when SAS Cargo capacity is limited.

        Budget carriers including Ryanair and Norwegian serve CPH-TLL on certain schedules but carry cabin pets only. Not a practical option for anything larger than a small cabin dog.

    - heading: "Denmark's breed legislation: a note"
      body: |
        Denmark has one of Europe's more restrictive breed bans, covering 13 breeds under the Lov om hunde. The banned breeds include Pitbull Terrier, American Staffordshire Terrier, Tosa Inu, Fila Brasileiro, Dogo Argentino, American Bulldog, Boerboel, Kangal, Central Asian Ovcharka, Caucasian Ovcharka, South Russian Ovcharka, Tornjak, and Sarplaninac.

        If you're moving from Denmark to Estonia with a dog that resembles one of these breeds, check your current status in Denmark and Estonia's own breed requirements. Estonia has national and municipal breed legislation separately. Most owners moving a standard family pet won't encounter this issue, but it's worth knowing if your dog is a large guarding breed or a bull-type.

        For cats and most dogs, the breed issue is irrelevant and the EU pet passport is the entire requirement.

faqs:
  - question: "Does Estonia require tapeworm treatment for a dog arriving from Denmark?"
    answer: "No. Estonia does not require tapeworm treatment for dogs from EU member states. The Echinococcus treatment is required for dogs entering certain countries (including Finland, Ireland, Norway, Malta). Denmark to Estonia does not trigger this requirement."
  - question: "What is the Fødevarestyrelsen and do I need to contact them?"
    answer: "Fødevarestyrelsen is the Danish Veterinary and Food Administration. It oversees official vet authorisation in Denmark. For a standard intra-EU pet move, you don't need to contact Fødevarestyrelsen directly. Your official vet handles the EU pet passport under their authorisation."
  - question: "How long does the Copenhagen to Tallinn journey take for a pet in cargo?"
    answer: "Direct SAS services take around 2 hours. Via airBaltic through Riga, the total journey including the connection runs approximately 3 to 4 hours. Cargo handling adds time at both ends, so plan for a full transit day."
  - question: "My Danish vet issued the EU pet passport but I'm not sure if they're an 'official vet'. How do I check?"
    answer: "In Denmark, EU pet passports can only be issued by veterinarians authorised under Fødevarestyrelsen. If your vet issued the passport, they hold this authorisation. If you have any doubt, check with Fødevarestyrelsen or ask your vet to confirm their authorisation status."
