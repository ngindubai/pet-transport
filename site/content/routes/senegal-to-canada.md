  overview: |
    Get the airport right before anything else on this route. Dakar's scheduled commercial flights moved to Blaise Diagne International Airport (DSS) in December 2017; the older Léopold Sédar Senghor Airport, near Yoff, now handles mostly cargo, charter and military traffic. A pet flying out as manifested cargo needs its paperwork built around DSS specifically, since some older guides still reference the airport that closed to scheduled traffic.

    Royal Air Maroc reverses the outbound route neatly: Dakar to Casablanca, then a direct Casablanca-Montreal service, all one carrier. Senegal also sits on CFIA's high-risk-for-dog-rabies list, current as of 13 March 2025, though for an ordinary family pet that mostly means nothing more than confirming personal, rather than commercial, status.

  sections:
    - heading: "Blaise Diagne, not the airport some sources still cite"
      body: |
        Léopold Sédar Senghor Airport served Dakar's commercial traffic for decades before closing to scheduled passenger and cargo flights in December 2017. Blaise Diagne International Airport, built further from the city near Diass, took over. The old airport still operates, mostly cargo, charter and military use, which is part of why some pet travel guides continue to reference it without the update.

        For a pet flying as manifested cargo on Royal Air Maroc, Air France or Turkish Airlines, Blaise Diagne is the airport that matters. Confirm any ground handling arrangement names DSS specifically.

    - heading: "Casablanca beats Paris for a direct connection"
      body: |
        Royal Air Maroc's Dakar-Casablanca service runs close to daily, connecting to the airline's own direct Casablanca-Montreal flight, a genuinely direct single-carrier option that most other North American departure points don't get from this corridor. It's worth booking pet cargo through the airline's own cargo desk specifically, since that runs separately from the standard passenger flow.

        Air France's Dakar-Paris service, codeshared with Air Senegal at around 14 weekly flights, connects to Air Canada's own nonstop Paris service and offers more day-of-week flexibility if the Casablanca schedule doesn't line up. Turkish Airlines via Istanbul is the fallback for a longer transit.

    - heading: "The high-risk listing, and what it actually restricts"
      body: |
        CFIA's list, updated 13 March 2025, groups Senegal with dozens of other countries. Its real force falls on commercial dog imports, stopped entirely since 28 September 2022. An owner travelling with their own dog or cat follows the ordinary process instead: current vaccination, correctly built certificate, no serology test, no quarantine. Worth checking regardless: a dog under 8 months old, not accompanied by its named owner, is presumed commercial by CFIA, a serious classification from a high-risk country.
