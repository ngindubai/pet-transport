content:
  h1: "Pet Transport from Poland to Jamaica"
  overview: |
    The Poland to Jamaica pet transport route demands careful planning and a long lead time. Jamaica requires an import permit from the VSD (Veterinary Services Division) before any veterinary work can begin, mandatory 14-day quarantine at Plumb Point National Quarantine Facility in Kingston, two rounds of parasite treatment timed precisely around the travel date, and strict microchip-before-vaccinations sequencing. Start the process at least 10 weeks before your intended departure; 12 weeks is safer.

    Poland's export process is handled through the GIW (Glowny Inspektorat Weterynarii, wetgiw.gov.pl). The official health certificate must be issued by a Powiatowy Lekarz Weterynarii (district official vet) and endorsed by GIW. Warsaw Chopin Airport (WAW) serves as the departure hub, with cargo connections to Kingston (KIN) via London Heathrow on British Airways, via Amsterdam on KLM, or via Paris CDG on Air France.

    The VSD is the authoritative source for Jamaica's current import rules. Contact them directly early in the process: vsdpermits@moa.gov.jm, +1 876-977-2489. Requirements can change, and the permit cannot be issued without satisfying their current criteria.

  sections:
    - heading: "Getting the Import Permit First"
      body: |
        Nothing else can happen until the Jamaican import permit arrives. Submit the application to the VSD 8-12 weeks before your planned departure. The application should include your pet's species, breed, age, microchip number (if already implanted), and your intended travel dates. The VSD issues the permit once the application is assessed; processing can take several weeks.

        Once the permit is in hand, the full preparation sequence can begin. Start with the microchip: it must be in place before any vaccination is given. If your pet was vaccinated before being microchipped, those vaccinations are invalid under Jamaica's rules and the entire course must be repeated after the microchip is implanted. This is a costly and time-consuming error that is entirely avoidable.

    - heading: "Parasite Treatment Timing"
      body: |
        Jamaica requires two rounds of both internal and external parasite treatment. Each pair of treatments must be spaced 14 days apart. The second internal parasite treatment must be given within 7 days of transport, and the second external parasite treatment must be given within 48 hours of transport. Both must appear on the official health certificate.

        Working backwards from your confirmed departure date: the second external treatment is on the day before or the morning of travel; the second internal treatment is in the week before departure; the first treatments of each type are 14 days before those second doses. Schedule these with your private vet well in advance. The GIW-authorised official vet then uses these records when completing the health certificate.

    - heading: "Polish Export Certification"
      body: |
        The health certificate for Jamaica must be issued by a Powiatowy Lekarz Weterynarii, the district-level official vet authorised by GIW. This is not a standard private clinic appointment. Contact your local district veterinary office (Powiatowy Lekarz Weterynarii) well before the travel date to arrange the pre-export examination. GIW endorsement of the signed certificate takes one to three working days.

        The certificate is valid for 10 days from signing, and Jamaica requires it to have been issued within 10 days of travel. The endorsement must be complete before departure. Build those working days into your schedule when booking the official vet appointment. Warsaw Chopin Airport (WAW) is the cargo departure point for all international cargo shipments.

    - heading: "Quarantine at Plumb Point"
      body: |
        All arriving dogs and cats are transported to Plumb Point National Quarantine Facility in Kingston for up to 14 days. The VSD manages the facility and charges daily fees; contact them for current rates before you travel. Owners cannot visit during the quarantine period, though the VSD provides updates on request.

        Arrival is at Norman Manley International Airport (KIN). VSD inspectors check all documentation before the animal is released to the quarantine facility. If any document is missing or out of date, the animal may be held at the owner's expense while the issue is resolved. Thorough preparation before departure is the only safeguard against this.
