  h1: "Pet Transport United States to Iceland"
  overview: |
    Five months. That is the realistic floor for moving a dog from the United States to Iceland, and most of it comes from a single classification: MAST puts the US in Category 2, the rabies-controlled tier, alongside Canada and Turkey rather than the Category 1 group most of the EU sits in. A Category 1 pet clears its titre test and travels once the lab result comes back. A Category 2 pet then waits a further 90 days from the blood draw date, no matter how healthy the paperwork looks.

    Layer onto that a mandatory 14-day quarantine that applies to every pet entering Iceland, a canine influenza vaccine specifically required for US, Canadian and Singaporean dogs, and a cargo-only booking process since Icelandair stopped taking pets as checked baggage in late 2024, and this becomes one of the more involved routes into Iceland from any origin.

  sections:
    - heading: "Category 2 status: what it actually costs you in time"
      body: |
        USDA-accredited vets in the US are used to the standard 30-day gap between rabies vaccination and a titre blood draw. What often surprises American owners is the second wait that follows. Because MAST classes the United States as Category 2 rather than Category 1, a further 90 days has to pass from the date the blood was drawn before the pet can travel, regardless of how strong the antibody result is.

        Run the numbers from day one: microchip and vaccination, then a 30-day wait to the blood draw, then a 90-day wait from that draw. That alone is four months before you even look at flights, quarantine bookings or the USDA endorsement stage. Compare that to a Category 1 country such as France or Germany, where the same pet clears the titre step and moves on to travel planning immediately once the lab confirms the result.

    - heading: "The canine influenza vaccine most US vets do not expect to need"
      body: |
        MAST requires dogs travelling from the United States, Canada or Singapore to have a canine influenza vaccination given no sooner than 14 days before import, a condition tied specifically to those three countries rather than a blanket EU-wide rule. It sits alongside the standard leptospirosis, distemper, hepatitis, parvovirus and parainfluenza cover MAST asks of every incoming dog.

        A US general practice vet who has not handled an Iceland export before may not flag this dose automatically, since it is not part of a typical domestic US vaccination schedule. Raise it directly at the first appointment, and get the timing right: too early and it falls outside the 14-day window MAST specifies, too late and it delays your departure date.

    - heading: "Fourteen days at Reykjanesbae or Mosel, whatever the paperwork says"
      body: |
        Iceland runs exactly two approved quarantine facilities, Einangrunarstoedin i Reykjanesbae and Einangrunarstoedin Mosel, and every arriving pet spends a minimum of 14 days at one of them. This is not contingent on how complete your USDA certificate is or how clean your titre result comes back. It is the fixed cost of Iceland maintaining its rabies-free island status, and it runs on top of, not instead of, the 90-day Category 2 wait.

        Book your slot as soon as your projected arrival window is realistic, not once every other document has cleared. Both stations take arrivals on a limited schedule, generally between 06:00 and 17:00 on predetermined days, and summer slots fill first. Fees run to roughly ISK 220,000 for a dog's full stay, around USD 1,600, somewhat less for a cat.
