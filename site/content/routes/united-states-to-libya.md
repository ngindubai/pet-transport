  h1: "Pet Transport from the United States to Libya"
  overview: |
    A personal pet cat, travelling with its owner, doesn't need an import permit to enter Libya. That's one of the few genuinely clear facts in an otherwise sparsely documented process, and it's worth knowing early, since it means the cat and dog paperwork on this route diverge more than usual.

    A dog's requirements are far less settled. Whether the same permit exemption applies, whether quarantine is imposed, even how the two rival Libyan administrations enforce entry differently at Mitiga in Tripoli versus Benina near Benghazi, none of it is published with anything close to the clarity this site sees on better-documented routes. That's not a research gap on this page's part; it reflects the genuine state of what's publicly available.

    The US side is comparatively simple: a USDA-accredited vet's certificate, endorsed electronically through APHIS, translated into Arabic. Getting the pet from a US gateway to Libya still means a long-haul connection through Istanbul, Cairo or Amman, since nothing flies there directly.
