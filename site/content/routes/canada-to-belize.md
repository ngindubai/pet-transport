content:
  h1: "Pet Transport from Canada to Belize"
  overview: |
    WestJet's Toronto to Belize City service ramps up to 4 weekly flights at the December-to-April peak, then tapers off entirely by early May. Miss that window and the nonstop simply isn't there; every pet then routes through Miami on American Airlines instead.

    Belize's own import side is, by the standards of this site, unusually well documented. BAHA publishes a fixed BZ$50 permit fee, a clear 90-day validity, and a strict 14-day certificate window, tighter than the 10-day rule used elsewhere. That clarity is the one genuine advantage over Belize's own outbound process to other destinations, which BAHA documents far less thoroughly.

  sections:
    - heading: "A seasonal flight most owners don't expect"
      body: |
        Toronto to Belize City nonstop exists for roughly seven months of the year, late October through early May, and it isn't flat across that period. WestJet runs as few as 2 weekly flights in the shoulder weeks either side of the season and as many as 4 at the December-to-April peak, while Air Canada adds a lighter, dated schedule on Airbus A319 equipment.

        Plan a summer move and neither carrier is flying the route. American Airlines' year-round Miami to Belize City service picks up the slack, but it means booking the Toronto-to-Miami leg separately and adding a connection to what would otherwise be a single flight.

    - heading: "BAHA's clear rules, and the 14-day trap"
      body: |
        Where some destinations leave owners guessing at fees and timelines, BAHA publishes a fixed BZ$50 import permit, good for up to 10 pets and valid 90 days. The certificate rule is equally specific: the international veterinary certificate has to be issued within 14 days of the pet actually landing in Belize, not 14 days of booking the flight.

        That's a narrower window than the 10-day certificate several other routes on this site use, and it's an easy detail to miss if a vet defaults to a template built for a different destination. Get the date right and BAHA's own process runs cleanly.

    - heading: "The two-day notice that keeps the vet on hand"
      body: |
        BAHA asks for the arrival flight's date, time and number at least 2 days before landing, so a veterinary officer is actually present when the pet clears at Philip S.W. Goldson International. It's a small step, easy to overlook once the bigger paperwork is sorted, and worth building into the final week's checklist rather than leaving to the airline to handle.
