  overview: "Israel classifies the UK as a rabies-free country, which removes the titre test requirement that applies to pets arriving from most other nations. That is genuinely good news for UK pet owners relocating to Israel.   sections:
    - heading: "The import permit comes first"
      body: |
        Before you book flights, you need a confirmed import permit from Israel's Ministry of Agriculture Veterinary Services. Applications go through the Veterinary Services portal (veterinary.moag.gov.il) and should be submitted at least 60 days before travel. Processing times can vary, and you should not commit to a flight date until you have the permit in writing.

        The permit application will ask for your pet's microchip number, rabies vaccination history, and confirmation that the pet is not a restricted breed. Israel bans the import of several breeds under its Dangerous Dogs Regulations 2004, including Pit Bull Terrier types, Rottweilers, American Staffordshire Terriers, Bull Terriers, Dogo Argentinos, Fila Brasileiros, and Japanese Tosas. If your dog is a cross-breed with any of these types, contact Veterinary Services before applying.

    - heading: "Health certificate timing and arrival inspection"
      body: |
        The APHA Export Health Certificate must be issued by an APHA-authorised vet and endorsed by APHA, and it is only valid for 10 days from the date of issue. This means the vet appointment cannot happen more than 10 days before your pet's arrival in Israel. Book the appointment as soon as you have your travel date, and confirm the turnaround time for APHA endorsement in your region (typically 1-5 working days).

        On arrival at Ben Gurion Airport, your pet will be held for veterinary inspection. For pets with correct documentation, this inspection hold is 1-3 days. The Quarantine Station at the airport handles arrivals, and staff will check the health certificate, microchip, and permit documentation. Plan for this hold when arranging accommodation or onward travel within Israel.

faqs:
  - question: "Do UK pets need a titre test to enter Israel?"
    answer: "No. Israel classifies the UK as a rabies-free country, which means pets arriving from the UK are exempt from the rabies antibody titre test requirement. You still need a current rabies vaccination documented on the health certificate, an ISO microchip, and a confirmed import permit. The exemption applies because the UK has rabies-free status in Israel's regulatory framework."

  - question: "How far in advance does the Israeli import permit need to be applied for?"
    answer: "Apply at least 60 days before the planned travel date. The permit is issued by the Israeli Ministry of Agriculture Veterinary Services, and processing can take several weeks. Do not book flights or cargo space until the permit is confirmed in writing, as travel without a permit will result in your pet being refused entry or quarantined at your expense."

  - question: "Which airlines carry pets on the UK to Israel route?"
    answer: "El Al Israel Airlines operates direct flights from Heathrow to Tel Aviv and has cargo and limited cabin pet options. British Airways also operates this route with pet cargo via IAG Cargo and PetAir UK. Budget carriers such as Wizz Air and easyJet may allow small pets in the cabin on some routes but policies change, so always verify directly. For dogs over around 8 kg combined weight with carrier, cargo is the standard option."

  - question: "What happens at Ben Gurion Airport when my pet arrives?"
    answer: "All imported pets go through a mandatory veterinary inspection at the Ben Gurion Airport Quarantine Station. With the correct import permit, APHA health certificate, and valid vaccination documentation, the inspection hold is typically 1-3 days. The Veterinary Services team checks the paperwork and examines the animal. If documentation is incomplete or incorrect, the pet may be held longer or refused entry and returned to the UK at the owner's expense."
