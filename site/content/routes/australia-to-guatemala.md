content:
  h1: "Pet Transport from Australia to Guatemala"
  overview: |
    Guatemala's side of this move is almost an afterthought. No titre test, no import permit beyond a port-of-entry inspection, just a rabies vaccination card and a health certificate that costs about USD 14 to clear through OIRSA on arrival. Australia is the country that makes you wait.

    DAFF requires 10 working days' notice through a Notice of Intention to Export before it will even schedule the pre-export vet inspection, and no airline flies direct from Melbourne or Sydney to Guatemala City. Every booking runs through a US gateway, usually Los Angeles or Dallas Fort Worth, before a second carrier picks up the Central American leg.

  sections:
    - heading: "Guatemala's light touch versus Australia's own paperwork"
      body: |
        It's an odd asymmetry. Most routes on this site are hard because the destination country makes life difficult. Here it's reversed. MAGA's published general guidance for pets arriving from any origin, Australia included, asks for a vaccination card, a signed health certificate, and OIRSA's small inspection fee at the border. Nothing about a titre test, nothing about a pre-arrival permit.

        The friction sits entirely on the Australian end. DAFF's export process is procedural rather than difficult: the Notice of Intention, a pre-export health and welfare check, then a certifying veterinary officer signs off. None of it is technically hard. It just has a fixed 10-working-day floor that can't be compressed, whatever the flight schedule says.

    - heading: "Why the flight, not the paperwork, sets your timeline"
      body: |
        Qantas gets a pet as far as Los Angeles or Dallas. Getting from there to Guatemala City needs a second carrier, American Airlines Cargo being the most practical, with United's Houston connection as a fallback. Two separate bookings, two separate ground-temperature embargoes to check, and a total transit that regularly runs past 24 hours.

        Build the routing around the flight, not the certificate. Once you've confirmed cargo space on both legs and checked the 85F embargo dates at your US transit airport, the Australian export documentation and Guatemalan arrival requirements slot in comfortably around it.
