content:
  h1: "Pet Transport from the United Kingdom to Albania"
  overview: |
    A cat or dog leaving Britain for Albania skips the rabies titre test that trips up so many other unlisted-country routes, because the UK itself sits on the EU's approved list of low-rabies-risk countries. Albania's import framework, closely aligned with the EU model even though the country isn't a member state, extends that same treatment to UK-vaccinated pets.

    What's left is a more familiar shape: microchip, a 21-day wait after rabies vaccination, and an APHA-issued Export Health Certificate rather than a passport. Getting a seat, or rather a cargo slot, is its own separate puzzle, since the route's busiest carrier doesn't carry pets at all.

  sections:
    - heading: "What Albania actually checks on arrival"
      body: |
        Rinas airport officials look for three things on a UK-origin pet: a scannable ISO microchip, a rabies vaccination given at least 21 days before entry, and the Export Health Certificate confirming both. No blood test, no quarantine hold, no separate import permit application for an owner travelling with their own animal.

        That said, this exemption isn't written into Albanian law as a permanent guarantee. It follows from the UK's current listing on the EU's own approved third-country table, a classification set at EU level and reviewed from time to time. Confirming the current position with AKU or a receiving vet shortly before travel costs nothing and closes off an unlikely but real risk.

    - heading: "The certificate replaces the passport"
      body: |
        Because Albania sits outside the EU pet passport scheme, a UK pet travelling there needs an Export Health Certificate, not the blue EU passport booklet used for intra-EU moves. Only an APHA-authorised Official Veterinarian can issue one, and the appointment has to be booked with the certification date in mind.

        APHA's own guidance flags a practical detail worth planning around: applications submitted more than seven working days ahead of the intended date are processed seven working days before that date, not sooner. Booking the vet appointment too early or too late against the flight date is the most common way this step goes wrong.

    - heading: "Flying out: one carrier looks obvious and is wrong"
      body: |
        Search flights from London to Tirana and Wizz Air appears first almost every time, with around 37 nonstop departures a week against British Airways' 13. It's also the one airline on this route that won't carry a pet, cabin or hold, under any booking.

        British Airways is the direct option that works, through its cargo partner PetAir UK on the export side. Turkish Airlines, connecting through Istanbul, is a genuine second choice; Istanbul Airport's dedicated Pet Lounge, open to connecting animals with a layover over 75 minutes, makes that routing more comfortable than a typical transfer.

    - heading: "Budgeting the move"
      body: |
        This route costs less than most unlisted-country moves precisely because there's no titre test or 3-month wait to fund. The certificate, vet fees and crate typically come to under GBP 750 combined.

        Cargo handling and the flight itself make up the bulk of the spend. Expect GBP 700 to GBP 1,800 for air cargo depending on crate size, season and whether the pet flies direct via Heathrow or connects through Istanbul, plus an agent fee on top for arranging the PetAir UK booking.
