  h1: "Pet Transport from the United Kingdom to Namibia"
  overview: |
    Namibia doesn't ask for a rabies titre test from any country, the UK included, which puts this route on a shorter timeline than most of Great Britain's outbound trips to Africa. What it does ask for is precision: a rabies vaccination given between 30 days and 12 months before the pet lands in Windhoek. No more, no less.

    There's no nonstop flight either way. British Airways to Johannesburg, then Airlink to Windhoek, is the standard routing; Discover Airlines' direct Frankfurt service is the alternative. Budget 8 to 12 weeks.

  sections:
    - heading: "No titre test, but a tight vaccination window"
      body: |
        Most unlisted-country routes out of the UK mean a rabies titre test and a multi-month wait. Namibia skips that entirely. There's no antibody blood test on its import checklist for any origin country, UK or otherwise.

        The trade-off is precision rather than delay. Namibia's Directorate of Veterinary Services wants a rabies vaccination that's been given no earlier than 12 months and no later than 30 days before the pet arrives. Time the booster wrong in either direction and the paperwork won't clear, even though the process overall moves faster than the EU or GB titre pathway.

    - heading: "The Export Health Certificate: who can actually sign it"
      body: |
        An EHC is a legal requirement for a pet leaving the UK for most international destinations, and only an APHA-authorised Official Veterinarian can complete and sign one. Plenty of general practices don't offer this service day-to-day, so it's worth confirming early rather than discovering the gap a few weeks before the flight.

        The certificate has to be issued within a fixed window before departure that APHA sets for the destination, and it has to reflect Namibia's specific import conditions rather than a one-size-fits-all UK export template.

    - heading: "Routing through Johannesburg or Frankfurt"
      body: |
        Windhoek has no nonstop connection to London. British Airways' daily Heathrow to Johannesburg service, followed by Airlink's regional hop to Hosea Kutako International, is the standard two-leg routing, and IAG Cargo's Heathrow live-animal handling makes the first leg straightforward.

        Discover Airlines, part of the Lufthansa Group, offers a year-round alternative: a direct flight from Frankfurt into Windhoek. That's worth considering if the UK-to-Frankfurt first leg fits the schedule better than a Johannesburg connection, though it adds a European stop rather than removing one.
