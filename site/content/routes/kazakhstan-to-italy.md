  sections:
    - heading: "Kazakhstan to Italy: understanding the unlisted country protocol"
      body: |
        Italy's pet import rules are set at the EU level. EU Regulation 576/2013 divides countries into two groups: listed countries, which can send pets with a simplified process, and unlisted countries, which must follow the full titre test protocol. Kazakhstan is unlisted.

        The practical consequence is a five-step process before your pet can travel: ISO microchip before vaccination, rabies vaccination, FAVN blood draw at least 30 days after vaccination, a 90-day wait from the blood draw date, and then the EU health certificate within 10 days of travel. No step can be skipped or shortened.

        The good news is that once the 90-day wait is complete and your KVCS health certificate is in order, there is no quarantine in Italy. Compliant arrivals clear at FCO or MXP without delay.

    - heading: "Turkish Airlines via Istanbul: the main cargo route"
      body: |
        From Almaty (ALA) or Astana (NQZ), Turkish Airlines Cargo provides the most direct connection to Italy via Istanbul (IST). IST has multiple daily flights to both Rome Fiumicino (FCO) and Milan Malpensa (MXP), giving flexibility on departure day and time.

        Turkish Airlines Cargo handles live animals and is well-experienced on the Central Asia to Europe corridor. Book cargo space 4-6 weeks in advance of your planned travel date. The ALA-IST-FCO or ALA-IST-MXP journey typically takes 12-18 hours total including the Istanbul connection.

        Alternative routings via Frankfurt (Lufthansa or Air Astana to FRA, then connections to FCO or MXP) and via Doha (Qatar Cargo) are also viable if Turkish Airlines capacity does not work for your dates.
