  h1: "Pet Transport from Rwanda to Sweden"
  overview: "Cargo fees for this route typically run from USD 800 to 2,200, and the bigger factor in the schedule is a 90-day wait counted from the titre test blood draw date. Sweden is an EU member state; Rwanda is not on the EU's approved list, so the full unlisted-country sequence applies. No separate import permit, no routine quarantine once documentation is compliant.   sections:
    - heading: "The unlisted-country sequence: microchip to 90-day wait"
      body: |
        Sweden doesn't add anything beyond the standard EU rule for pets coming from unlisted countries. Microchip goes in first. Rabies vaccination follows. Wait at least 30 days, then draw blood for an FAVN titre test at an EU-approved laboratory, needing a result of at least 0.5 IU/ml. From that draw date, a 90-day wait applies before entry into Sweden is permitted. It's a long process on paper, but the vet steps usually take six or seven weeks. The 90 days afterwards is what pushes the total timeline out to five or six months, and it has to be tracked from the blood draw, never the result date. Jordbruksverket, the Swedish Board of Agriculture, checks the paperwork on arrival at Stockholm Arlanda's cargo terminal, and the health certificate needs to be inside its 10-day window at that point.

    - heading: "Routing from Kigali to Stockholm"
      body: |
        RwandAir's regular KGL-BRU service makes Brussels a natural first connection, with an onward leg into Stockholm Arlanda. Brussels Airlines runs the same first leg and is worth comparing on price and cargo space. KLM Cargo, through its joint venture with Kenya Airways, offers a well-tested alternative via Nairobi and Amsterdam. Ethiopian Airlines Cargo via Addis Ababa is a further option if the Brussels-routed flights are full on your date. Book four to six weeks ahead where possible, and check that the connecting leg accepts live animal cargo before confirming the itinerary.
