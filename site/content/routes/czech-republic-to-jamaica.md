content:
  h1: "Pet Transport from Czech Republic to Jamaica"
  overview: |
    This is one of the more demanding pet transport routes from Central Europe. Jamaica requires an import permit that must be in hand before any veterinary preparation begins, mandatory 14-day quarantine at Plumb Point National Quarantine Facility in Kingston, two separate rounds of parasite treatment, and a strict microchip-before-vaccinations rule. Start the process at least 10 weeks before your intended departure date. That is not a conservative estimate; it is the minimum.

    The Czech export side follows the standard EU framework. An SVS-authorised official vet issues the health certificate, which SVS (Statni veterinarni sprava, svscr.cz) then endorses. The certificate must be issued within 10 days of travel. Prague (PRG) connects to Kingston (KIN) via London Heathrow on British Airways cargo, or via Paris CDG or Madrid with Air France and Iberia respectively.

    Jamaica's Veterinary Services Division (VSD) administers all import approvals. Contacting them directly at the start of the process is the right move: vsdpermits@moa.gov.jm, +1 876-977-2489. Requirements can be updated, and the VSD is the only authoritative source for current rules.

  sections:
    - heading: "Getting the Import Permit: Step One, No Exceptions"
      body: |
        The Jamaican import permit is not a formality that can be sorted alongside the vet work. It must be obtained before anything else happens. Apply to the VSD 8-12 weeks before your planned travel date, attaching details of your pet (species, breed, age, microchip number) and your intended travel dates. The VSD will issue a permit if the application is approved; travel without one will result in refusal of entry.

        Once the permit arrives, the rest of the preparation can begin. Microchipping comes first, before any vaccinations. If your pet was vaccinated before being microchipped, the vaccination course is not valid under Jamaica's rules and must be repeated after microchipping. This is a common and expensive mistake. Get the microchip confirmed on record before the first injection.

    - heading: "Parasite Treatment Requirements"
      body: |
        Jamaica requires two rounds of both internal and external parasite treatment, with each pair spaced 14 days apart. The timing of the second treatments is tightly controlled: the second internal parasite treatment must be given within 7 days of transport, and the second external parasite treatment must be given within 48 hours of transport. Both must be recorded on the health certificate by the official vet.

        This means coordination between your private vet (for the treatments) and the SVS-authorised official vet (for the certificate) needs to be precise. If the external parasite treatment falls outside the 48-hour window, the certificate will be invalid and the journey cannot proceed. Plan the treatment dates backwards from your confirmed departure and build in a contingency day.

    - heading: "Czech Export Certification"
      body: |
        On the Czech side, the export process is well-established. You need an SVS-authorised official vet to conduct a pre-travel examination and issue the EU-format health certificate. The SVS then endorses it. The certificate must be issued within 10 days of travel, so the official vet appointment should be scheduled for the week before departure once all parasite treatments are complete and recorded.

        The SVS endorsement typically takes one to three working days after the vet signs the certificate. Book the official vet appointment with that lead time in mind. Prague's Vaclav Havel Airport (PRG) handles all international cargo departures for this route.

    - heading: "Quarantine at Plumb Point"
      body: |
        All dogs and cats arriving in Jamaica are held at the Plumb Point National Quarantine Facility in Kingston for up to 14 days. The facility is managed by the VSD. Fees are charged per day and vary; contact the VSD for current rates before you travel. Owners cannot visit during quarantine, though the VSD can provide updates on request.

        Pets are inspected on arrival at Norman Manley International Airport (KIN) and transferred to Plumb Point if all documentation is in order. If documentation is incomplete, the animal may be refused entry or held at the owner's expense while the situation is resolved. Getting the paperwork right before departure is the only reliable way to avoid problems at KIN.
