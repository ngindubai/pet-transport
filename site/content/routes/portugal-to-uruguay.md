    TAP Air Portugal's direct Lisbon-São Paulo service gives this route one of the more workable connections in the whole build, and the regulatory side matches that ease.   sections:
    - heading: "The MGAP import permit comes first, not last"
      body: |
        Every pet entering Uruguay needs an import permit from MGAP, applied for before travel and specifying the documentation conditions your pet must meet. Owners who leave this until the final weeks sometimes find their vaccination or certificate paperwork does not match what the permit specifies, forcing a redo. Apply as soon as the move is confirmed.

        Beyond the permit, Uruguay's requirements are straightforward: an ISO microchip (recommended rather than mandatory), a current rabies vaccination, and a DGAV-endorsed health certificate issued within 10 days of travel.
    - heading: "Dogs need the echinococcus treatment; cats do not"
      body: |
        Uruguay's parasite control focus for arriving pets is hydatid tapeworm rather than rabies. Dogs need a praziquantel-based treatment given between 72 hours and 30 days before arrival, recorded on the health certificate by a DGAV-authorised vet. Cats travel without this extra step.

        Given the narrow closing end of that window, book the final vet appointment for a few days before your confirmed departure, combining the health certificate exam and the echinococcus treatment record in a single visit.
