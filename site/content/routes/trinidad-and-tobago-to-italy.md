    Italy removed its national breed-specific legislation back in 2009, which counts for something if a restricted breed elsewhere in Europe is part of the picture. It doesn't change how the pet actually gets there, though: with no nonstop Caribbean-to-continental-Europe cargo route, every move on this corridor transfers through London first.

  sections:
    - heading: "Piarco to Rome or Milan, via London"
      body: |
        British Airways, through IAG Cargo, is the only carrier flying nonstop out of Piarco, and its destination is Gatwick rather than continental Europe. From there, Lufthansa Cargo via Frankfurt or Qatar Airways Cargo via Doha complete the connection into Fiumicino or Malpensa.

        Book the London connection as a distinct step in the process. It isn't a single-airline through-booking, and the transfer itself needs its own crate check and handling arrangement.

    - heading: "No breed ban, but the EU clock still governs the timeline"
      body: |
        Trinidad and Tobago sits outside the EU's list of low-risk third countries, so Italy applies the same protocol as its neighbours: microchip before vaccination, a 30-day gap before the titre blood draw, a result of at least 0.5 IU/ml, then a fixed 90 days from the draw date, under Commission Delegated Regulation (EU) 2026/131.

        None of that changes because of Italy's own breed-legislation history. Airline-specific brachycephalic restrictions are a separate matter and should be confirmed directly with the cargo carrier.
