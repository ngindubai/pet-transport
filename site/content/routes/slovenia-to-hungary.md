  sections:
    - heading: "Hungary's breed ban: mandatory pre-check"
      body: |
        Hungary's Government Decree 35/1997 prohibits the American Pit Bull Terrier and American Staffordshire Terrier from entering the country. This applies regardless of the animal's origin, documentation quality, or owner circumstances. These breeds are banned outright.

        Other breeds face conditional restrictions rather than an outright ban. Rottweilers, Bull Terriers, and Staffordshire Bull Terriers can reside in Hungary under specific conditions: registration, third-party liability insurance, compulsory muzzling in public, and in some localities a temperament assessment. If your dog falls into one of these categories, contact NEBIH before making any bookings.

        All other breeds, and all cats, are subject only to standard EU entry requirements.

    - heading: "Routing from Ljubljana to Budapest"
      body: |
        The most convenient routing connects via Vienna. Austrian Airlines flies Ljubljana (LJU) to Vienna (VIE) in under 1 hour, and connecting services from Vienna to Budapest (BUD) are frequent. Small pets (up to 8kg including carrier) travel in the cabin on both legs. Larger dogs go in the pressurised hold.

        Lufthansa via Frankfurt is an alternative, particularly useful for cargo transport of larger animals. Frankfurt has more extensive cargo facilities than Ljubljana or Vienna, and Lufthansa Cargo is well set up for live animal transport.

        Wizz Air has a large presence at Budapest but does not accept pets. Austrian Airlines and Lufthansa are the practical choices.

    - heading: "Documents from Slovenia and border checks"
      body: |
        Slovenia's UVHVVR authorises vets to issue EU pet passports. The passport records all necessary information and is valid for the animal's lifetime. For a one-way relocation, an AHC endorsed within 10 days of Hungarian entry is a valid alternative.

        Hungarian border veterinary officers at Budapest Airport (BUD) check documents on arrival. If paperwork is incomplete or the vaccination has lapsed, NEBIH can impose conditional quarantine at the owner's cost. Planning ahead and keeping documentation current avoids this outcome entirely.
