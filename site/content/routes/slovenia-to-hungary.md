content:
  h1: "Pet Transport from Slovenia to Hungary: 2026 Guide"
  overview: |
    Ljubljana to Budapest is about 350 kilometres by motorway, roughly four and a half hours in the car with a rest stop. For a single dog or cat, that often beats booking a connecting flight through Vienna or Munich, and it's the option most Slovenian owners in this corridor actually choose. There's no titre test either way. Slovenia and Hungary are both EU member states, so the pet passport that already lives in most owners' desk drawers is all the paperwork this route needs.

  sections:
    - heading: "Driving direct vs flying via Vienna or Munich"
      body: |
        A single dog or a cat in a carrier travels the Ljubljana-Budapest motorway route without much fuss for most healthy pets, and it avoids the airport process entirely. Fuel and tolls run somewhere between EUR 60 and 100 depending on the vehicle, well under the cost of a flight with connection fees on both legs.

        Flying makes more sense for multi-pet households, tight schedules, or an owner who's already flying and wants the pet on broadly the same timeline. Austrian Airlines via Vienna and Lufthansa via Munich are the two workable connections, since there's no non-stop service between the two capitals. Reserve cabin space for small pets on both legs at the time of booking.

    - heading: "The paperwork: passport, AHC, and the April 2026 change"
      body: |
        The EU pet passport, issued by a UVHVVR-authorised vet in Slovenia, is valid for the pet's life provided the rabies vaccination inside it stays current. Since 22 April 2026, that passport is restricted to owners primarily resident in the EU, a rule that leaves the overwhelming majority of Slovenian pet owners unaffected.

        Non-EU nationals resident in Slovenia need a single-use AHC instead, issued within 10 days of travel and endorsed by UVHVVR. NEBIH, Hungary's National Food Chain Safety Office, checks the microchip against the passport or AHC on arrival regardless of whether you drove or flew in, so confirm the numbers match before you set off.
