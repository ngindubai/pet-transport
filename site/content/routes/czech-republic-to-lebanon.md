  overview: |
    Czech Republic to Lebanon requires more planning time than most international pet moves. Lebanon's advance import permit requirement means you need to start the process at least ten to twelve weeks before your travel date. The documentation itself follows the standard EU pattern, but the Lebanese administrative process adds time and uncertainty.

    The Czech Republic is an EU member state. The SVS-endorsed EU health certificate is the correct export document. That part is straightforward. The challenge is the Lebanese end, specifically the advance import permit from the Ministry of Agriculture.

  sections:
    - heading: "The Lebanese import permit: apply early and follow up"
      body: |
        The import permit from the Lebanese Ministry of Agriculture (agriculture.gov.lb) is not optional. A pet arriving at Beirut Rafic Hariri Airport (BEY) without an advance permit will be refused entry. The permit takes time to obtain and the Ministry can be slow to respond, particularly during busy periods.

        Apply as early as possible, ideally ten to twelve weeks before your intended travel date. Once you have submitted the application, follow up after one week if you have not received acknowledgement. An IPATA-registered pet transport agent with Lebanon experience can manage this process effectively, as they will have contacts within the Ministry.

        The situation at Beirut Airport has at times been affected by wider logistics and political factors. Check current operational conditions with your transport agent before confirming bookings.

    - heading: "Cargo routing from Prague to Beirut"
      body: |
        There is no direct cargo service from Prague Vaclav Havel Airport (PRG) to Beirut Rafic Hariri (BEY). The route requires a hub connection.

        Turkish Airlines via Istanbul (IST) is the most commonly used option for Czech Republic to Lebanon pet cargo. PRG to IST is well served and Turkish Airlines Cargo operates regularly from IST to BEY. Note that temperature embargoes during the Turkish summer (typically June to mid-September) can restrict live animal cargo. Plan for spring or autumn travel where possible.

        Lufthansa via Frankfurt (FRA) is a strong alternative. Frankfurt's Animal Lounge offers good facilities for transit pets, and MEA (Middle East Airlines) connects FRA to BEY with Lufthansa and MEA having a codeshare relationship on some routes. This is a practical routing if Turkish Airlines is not suitable on your dates.

    - heading: "SVS certification: the Czech export step"
      body: |
        The export health certificate for a move from Czech Republic to Lebanon must be issued by an SVS-authorised official vet and endorsed by SVS (Statni veterinarni sprava) before departure. This is the standard process for any international non-EU move from the Czech Republic.

        The SVS endorsement is valid for 10 days from the date of the clinical examination. Schedule the vet appointment carefully, accounting for both the 10-day window and transit time through your hub airport, to ensure the certificate is valid on arrival in Beirut.

        Information on SVS official vets and the endorsement process is available at svscr.cz.
