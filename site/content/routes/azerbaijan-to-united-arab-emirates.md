  overview: |
    Under three hours separate Baku from Dubai by air, one of the shortest legs in this whole build, and FlyDubai covers it non-stop. Short flight, short crate time, one less thing to worry about for an anxious traveller in the hold.

    Azerbaijan's export side runs through the State Veterinary Service under the Ministry of Agriculture. No formal export permit is needed for a personal pet, but the Service must authorise the vet and endorse the health certificate on MOCCAE's own model format before departure.

    MOCCAE places Azerbaijan outside its short low-risk list, so the full import sequence applies: microchip, rabies vaccination, titre test, online permit. And here's the detail worth knowing before comparing this route to an EU-bound one: there's no fixed wait after the blood draw for the titre test. The result alone, once it clears 0.5 IU/ml, holds for 365 days.

  sections:
    - heading: "MOCCAE's sequence for a pet leaving Azerbaijan"
      body: |
        A pet needs its microchip fitted before rabies vaccination, then a vaccination current for at least 21 days if it's a first dose. After that comes the rabies antibody titre test at an accredited laboratory, with a minimum acceptable result of 0.5 IU/ml.

        No separate waiting period sits between that blood draw and the flight. MOCCAE checks the titre result itself, good for 365 days provided vaccination stays current, a genuinely different approach from the EU's fixed 90-day countdown. Minimum age from Azerbaijan's risk category is 15 weeks, and the online import permit is valid for 90 days once granted.

    - heading: "Baku's export process and getting to Dubai"
      body: |
        Azerbaijan doesn't require a formal export permit for a companion animal. What's needed is the health certificate: examined and completed by a State Veterinary Service-authorised vet on MOCCAE's model format, endorsed close to travel, usually 5 to 10 days out.

        Heydar Aliyev International (GYD) in Baku handles departure. FlyDubai's direct GYD-DXB service is the fastest crossing, and Turkish Airlines Cargo via Istanbul (IST) offers a steady alternative if the direct flight's schedule or capacity doesn't work.
