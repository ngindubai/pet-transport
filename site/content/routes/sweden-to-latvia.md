      body: |
        Riga Airport (RIX) is airBaltic's main hub, and the carrier operates direct Stockholm Arlanda to Riga services multiple times daily. For anyone shipping a dog or cat from Sweden to Latvia, this is the natural starting point.

        airBaltic handles cabin pets within its standard size and weight limits. For larger dogs that cannot travel in the cabin, airBaltic Cargo is the freight option. The RIX hub handles a significant volume of cargo pets given Latvia's position as a logistics crossroads in the Baltic region.

        Alternatives exist. SAS serves this route on some schedules and offers SAS Cargo for hold animals. Ryanair also operates Stockholm to Riga flights but takes cabin pets only, so large dogs are excluded.

    - heading: "PVD and the EU pet passport in Latvia"
      body: |
        Latvia's competent authority for pet import is the PVD (Pārtikas un veterinārais dienests), the Food and Veterinary Service. Under the EU Pet Travel Scheme, PVD accepts EU pet passports from all EU member states including Sweden.

        The requirements are consistent with the wider EU framework: ISO 15-digit microchip, current rabies vaccination, and the microchip implanted before the vaccination was recorded. Your Swedish official vet holds the passport record and can verify the sequence. If you have any doubt about the dates, a quick check with your vet before booking removes any uncertainty.

        Latvia has no additional entry forms, declarations, or pre-notification requirements for intra-EU pet arrivals. You carry the passport, you arrive, and that's the process.
