  h1: "Pet Transport from the United Kingdom to Malaysia"
  overview: |
    Malaysia is one of the most straightforward Asian destinations for UK pets. The United Kingdom is on Malaysia's quarantine-exempt list, alongside the Republic of Ireland, Sweden, Australia, New Zealand, Japan, Brunei, and Singapore. This means dogs and cats from the UK do not face routine quarantine on arrival, provided documentation is correct and the pet is healthy.

    The core requirements are: a DVS e-Permit from Malaysia's Department of Veterinary Services (applied for online 2-4 weeks before arrival), a current rabies vaccination (given at least 30 days before entry), an ISO microchip, and an official health certificate issued within 14 days of arrival. The permit is valid for 30 days from issue and costs RM 50-100 per animal.

    Malaysia Airlines and British Airways both handle cargo on this corridor. The main routing options from the UK to Kuala Lumpur are direct with Malaysia Airlines or via a hub transfer with Emirates (Dubai) or Cathay Pacific (Hong Kong).

    - heading: "Special breed rules in Malaysia for UK pets"
      body: |
        Certain dog breeds require a prior written application to the Director of State Veterinary Services before you can import them into Malaysia. The breeds requiring this application are: Rottweiler, Doberman Pinscher, German Shepherd, Belgian Shepherd, East European Shepherd, Bull Mastiff, Bull Terrier, and Perro de Presa Canario.

        This is not an outright ban: these breeds can be imported, but you must obtain written approval from the relevant state DVS director before travel. Contact the DVS at dvs.gov.my to initiate this process well before your departure date.

        For Bengal and Savannah cats: only F5 generation and beyond (5 or more generations removed from the wild ancestor) are permitted. Check your cat's TICA or GCCF registration to confirm generation status.
faqs:
  - question: "Is there quarantine for pets from the UK entering Malaysia?"
    answer: "No. The United Kingdom is on Malaysia's quarantine-exempt list. Dogs and cats arriving from the UK with correct documentation and appearing healthy are not subject to routine quarantine. The DVS officer at Kuala Lumpur International Airport inspects the documents and the pet. If all is in order, the pet is cleared without quarantine."
  - question: "How do I get the DVS e-Permit for Malaysia?"
    answer: "The DVS e-Permit is applied for online through Malaysia's Department of Veterinary Services Animal Passport portal (animalpassport.dvs.gov.my). Apply 2-4 weeks before your planned arrival date. The permit is valid for 30 days from issue and costs RM 50-100 per animal. You will need your pet's microchip number, vaccination details, and travel information."
  - question: "Does my dog's rabies vaccination need to be recent for Malaysia entry?"
    answer: "Yes. The rabies vaccination must be administered at least 30 days before entry into Malaysia. A vaccination given within 30 days of arrival does not meet the requirement. Ensure your pet's vaccination is timed accordingly, and that the vaccination date is clearly recorded in the health certificate."
  - question: "Which airline is best for pets from the UK to Malaysia?"
    answer: "Malaysia Airlines offers cargo services from London Heathrow to Kuala Lumpur (KLIA) and has established DVS procedures for UK-origin pets. British Airways is an alternative via BA World Cargo. Emirates via Dubai and Cathay Pacific via Hong Kong are options with one transfer. Note that Emirates has summer heat embargoes at Dubai (June-September). Confirm current live animal acceptance before booking."
  - question: "Are there different rules for Sabah and Sarawak in Malaysian Borneo?"
    answer: "Yes. Sabah and Sarawak are separate Malaysian states with their own veterinary import requirements. If your destination is Kota Kinabalu (Sabah) or Kuching (Sarawak), contact the relevant state DVS office for current rules before booking. Do not assume that Peninsula Malaysia rules apply to Borneo destinations."
