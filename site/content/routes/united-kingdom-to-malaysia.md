    Moving a pet from the UK to Malaysia involves a mandatory 30-day quarantine on arrival at a DVS (Department of Veterinary Services) facility. The UK is not on Malaysia's Scheduled Countries list, which means all UK-origin pets complete quarantine regardless of their vaccination or documentation status. Allow three to four months of planning time.

    - heading: "The DVS permit and quarantine: key logistics"
      body: |
        The DVS import permit is applied for online via the DVS e-Permit system (dvs.gov.my). You need your pet's microchip number, vaccination records, and titre test result to complete the application. The permit is valid for 30 days from issue. Apply at least 2-4 weeks before travel to allow processing time. Do not book flights before the permit is confirmed.

        On arrival in Malaysia, your pet goes to the DVS quarantine station at Kuala Lumpur International Airport (KLIA). The station is supervised by DVS veterinary staff. All documentation is checked on day one, and the 30-day clock begins. Owner visits are possible at scheduled times; your agent can confirm current arrangements.

        Restricted breeds need additional approval before the DVS permit is issued. The breeds subject to restrictions include Rottweiler, Doberman Pinscher, German Shepherd, Belgian Shepherd, East European Shepherd, Bull Mastiff, Bull Terrier, and Perro de Presa Canario. If your dog is one of these breeds, apply to the Director of State Veterinary Services before doing anything else on the import permit.

faqs:
  - question: "Does my pet have to quarantine for 30 days in Malaysia when arriving from the UK?"
    answer: "Yes. Malaysia classifies countries into Scheduled and Non-scheduled. The UK is not on the Scheduled Countries list, so all pets from the UK complete a mandatory 30-day quarantine at a DVS facility on arrival. This applies regardless of vaccination history or documentation quality."
  - question: "What is the DVS import permit and how do I get it?"
    answer: "The DVS (Department of Veterinary Services) import permit is the official Malaysia approval for importing your pet. Apply online via the DVS e-Permit system with your pet's microchip number, vaccination records, and titre test result. The permit is valid for 30 days from issue. Allow 2-4 weeks for processing. Do not book flights before the permit is confirmed."
  - question: "Does my dog need vaccines other than rabies for Malaysia?"
    answer: "Yes. DVS requires dogs to have current vaccinations for distemper, hepatitis (canine adenovirus), parvovirus, parainfluenza, leptospirosis, and bordetella (kennel cough) in addition to rabies. Cats need feline panleukopenia, calicivirus, and rhinotracheitis vaccines. Make sure your vet records all of these on the health certificate."
  - question: "How much does the UK to Malaysia pet move cost?"
    answer: "Expect total costs of GBP 2,500 to GBP 6,000 depending on pet size and carrier. The 30-day DVS quarantine and cargo fee are the largest components. DVS permit fee, health certificate, crate, and agent fees make up the balance."
  - question: "My dog is a German Shepherd. Can I bring it to Malaysia?"
    answer: "Yes, but with additional steps. German Shepherds are on Malaysia's restricted breed list, which requires a written application to the Director of State Veterinary Services in addition to the standard DVS import permit process. Start this application before the permit application to avoid delays."
