  h1: "Pet Transport from United Kingdom to Venezuela"
  overview: |
    No titre test, no three-month wait. Venezuela's INSAI asks for a current rabies vaccination certificate, a health certificate and the standard vaccination and deworming paperwork, nothing on the scale of Great Britain's own unlisted-country rules. On paper, this is one of the lighter routes covered on this site.

    The catch sits with the flight, not the paperwork. Caracas's international schedule has been unsteady for years and stayed that way into 2026, so the practical plan is Heathrow to Bogota to Caracas on Avianca, booked and reconfirmed close to the date rather than locked in months out.

  sections:
    - heading: "A lighter import process than most South American routes"
      body: |
        Compare this with the reverse route, UK-bound pets from Venezuela need a titre test and a fixed three-month wait. Pets heading into Venezuela do not. INSAI's checklist is a current rabies certificate (under a year old), an international health certificate valid one month, the combined vaccination card, and a deworming certificate valid three months. A sanitary mobilisation permit, paid at 5 Unidades Tributarias, is settled at the INSAI office in Maiquetia airport on arrival rather than applied for weeks in advance.

        The main UK-side task is APHA's Export Health Certificate 2916. It needs an Official Veterinarian and gets rejected if an outdated version is used, so pull the current PDF from gov.uk before the vet appointment rather than working from memory or an old copy.
    - heading: "Booking around Caracas's unstable schedule"
      body: |
        Several carriers have pulled back from Caracas over the past few years, and 2026 has not been quiet either. Copa suspended its own service earlier in the year citing safety concerns at Maiquetia, and cancellations hit American Eagle and Aeroregional flights again in July.

        Avianca is the steadiest option identified in current research, running its own Bogota-Heathrow long-haul service and a regional Bogota-Caracas leg on the same airline group. That keeps the pet with one carrier rather than transferring between separate airlines mid-journey, which matters when live animal cargo handovers are involved. Still, reconfirm space close to the travel date. A schedule that holds today is not guaranteed to hold in eight weeks.
