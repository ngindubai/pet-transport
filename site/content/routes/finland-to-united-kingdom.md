  sections:
    - heading: "Cabin vs cargo from Helsinki: which makes sense for your pet?"
      body: |
        Finnair allows small pets in the cabin on European routes including Helsinki to London Heathrow. If your dog or cat fits within Finnair's in-cabin weight limit (8kg including the carrier) and you are travelling on the same flight, cabin travel removes the need for cargo booking and keeps your pet in the cabin with you for the approximately 3-hour flight.

        For larger pets, cargo is the route. Finnair Cargo handles live animals in the temperature-controlled hold, and HEL is a well-organised hub for European cargo connections. British Airways World Cargo is a second option on the same route.

        The documentation requirements are identical whether your pet travels cabin or cargo: microchip, current rabies vaccination, AHC issued within 10 days, and tapeworm treatment for dogs. The only operational difference is where your pet sits on the aircraft.

    - heading: "Timing the tapeworm: working backwards from Heathrow"
      body: |
        For a direct Finnair flight from Helsinki to London (around 3 hours), work the tapeworm treatment appointment backwards from your expected GB arrival time. The treatment must be given between 24 and 120 hours before that arrival.

        If your flight lands at LHR at 2pm UK time, the treatment window opens at 2pm two days earlier (120 hours before) and closes at 2pm the day before (24 hours before). The Finnish official vet records the exact date and time in the AHC.

        Book your AHC appointment so the vet can give the tapeworm treatment and issue the certificate in the same visit or within 24 hours of each other. The AHC has a 10-day validity from issue, so the whole process can be planned neatly within the week before travel.

  - question: "Are any dog breeds banned from bringing into the UK from Finland?"
    answer: "Finland does not ban breeds, but the UK does. Under the Dangerous Dogs Act 1991, Pit Bull Terrier type dogs, Japanese Tosa, Dogo Argentino, and Fila Brasileiro cannot be brought into Great Britain unless they hold a valid Certificate of Exemption. If your dog is one of these types or resembles one, confirm its legal position before attempting travel to the UK. Source: GOV.UK, 2026."
