  sections:
    - heading: "Why Finland has no titre test and no tapeworm requirement"
      body: |
        Finland is listed as a Part 1 country by APHA, which means the UK recognises Finland's rabies status as equivalent to the EU and Channel Islands. Pets from listed countries do not need the FAVN titre test or the 90-day waiting period that applies to unlisted countries.

        Finland also holds a separate exemption from the tapeworm treatment requirement. The UK grants this to a small group of countries (including Finland, Ireland, Malta, and Norway) that have historically maintained very low or zero hydatid tapeworm risk. Dogs from Finland do not need praziquantel treatment before entering Great Britain.

        Finland has been rabies-free since 1959. While an up-to-date rabies vaccination is still required, the 21-day post-vaccination wait is the only timing constraint for most pets travelling from Finland.

    - heading: "The AHC and Finnish official vets"
      body: |
        The Animal Health Certificate (AHC) replaces the EU pet passport for travel from the EU to Great Britain since Brexit. It must be issued by an official vet authorised by the Finnish Food Authority (Ruokavirasto) for this purpose, not just any private practice vet.

        The AHC covers the pet's microchip, rabies vaccination status, and species-specific requirements. It is valid for one journey only: once used to enter Great Britain, it cannot be reused for a subsequent trip. For future travel from Finland to the UK, a new AHC will be needed each time.

        Contact your Finnish vet to confirm they are authorised to issue AHCs for UK travel. Most veterinary practices in Finland are familiar with the format following Brexit. The fee is typically EUR 80 to EUR 200 depending on the practice and the time required.
