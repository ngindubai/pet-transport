  h1: "Pet Transport from Canada to Brunei"
  overview: |
    Fourteen days. That's the shortest quarantine Brunei's Department of Agriculture and Agrifood will accept for a pet with a clean rabies titre test, and it applies regardless of how well documented the animal is, because Canada isn't one of the handful of countries Brunei classes as rabies-free.

    No airline flies Canada to Brunei directly. Royal Brunei Airlines, the flag carrier, doesn't touch North America at all, so every pet on this route transfers through a hub, most workably Hong Kong on Cathay Pacific Cargo or Kuala Lumpur on Malaysia Airlines Cargo. Add a breed ban list that applies to dogs but not cats, and this becomes one of the more paperwork-heavy routes this site covers.

  sections:
    - heading: "Two countries, two vets, one appointment that can't slip"
      body: |
        The Canadian side starts simply enough. A licensed vet examines the pet, checks the microchip, and drafts the export certificate. Then it gets harder to schedule: an official CFIA veterinarian has to endorse that certificate in person, at a booked appointment, while the pet is still on Canadian soil. There's no retroactive fix if the appointment gets missed and the flight books first.

        Brunei's side runs on its own clock. DAA wants the import permit application in before travel, built around a rabies vaccination given at least 60 days but no more than a year before departure, followed by a titre test drawn a minimum of 30 days after that jab. Miss the 30-day window on the blood draw and the whole vaccination-to-travel timeline resets.

    - heading: "Why quarantine doesn't disappear with good paperwork"
      body: |
        Brunei sorts countries into two tiers. Scheduled countries, a short list covering Australia, Ireland, New Zealand, Sabah, Sarawak, Singapore and the UK, get pets in without quarantine if the animal has lived there at least six months. Everyone else is non-scheduled. Canada is non-scheduled.

        That means quarantine happens either way. A pet arriving with a passing titre test and a valid rabies certificate faces a 14-day minimum in a government facility, with the rest served at home. Skip the titre test and the figure some sources cite jumps to 30 days minimum. Either way, this is a genuine cost and logistics factor that a route like Canada to the UK simply doesn't carry, and it's worth budgeting the extra weeks into any relocation plan rather than treating it as a footnote.

    - heading: "Dogs face a breed list. Cats don't."
      body: |
        This is worth stating plainly because it's easy to miss: Brunei's import framework applies the same permit, vaccination and quarantine process to both species, but dogs carry an additional layer cats don't face. A list of breeds, including Akita, Pit Bull types, Japanese Tosa, Fila Brasileiro and several mastiff-type breeds, is banned from import outright. Some relocation specialists also report extra conditions, such as neutering or public muzzling, on breeds like German Shepherd and Doberman, though this detail is less consistently documented across official sources than the outright bans, so confirm it directly with DAA if your dog is one of those breeds.

        No equivalent list exists for cats. A Persian, a Maine Coon, a moggy from a Toronto shelter: none of them face a species-specific breed restriction on this route.

    - heading: "Getting the pet onto a plane"
      body: |
        Royal Brunei Airlines is the obvious name to search for, and it's also the wrong starting point. The airline's network runs to London, Dubai, Melbourne, Hong Kong, Kuala Lumpur and Singapore among others, not to Toronto or Vancouver. Its cargo division does say it carries household pets under IATA Live Animal Regulations, but only ever as the final leg into BWN from one of its own hubs.

        Practically, that means two realistic chains. One runs Cathay Pacific Cargo out of Toronto or Vancouver into Hong Kong, then Royal Brunei's own Hong Kong-BWN sector. The other runs a Canadian gateway into Kuala Lumpur, then Malaysia Airlines Cargo, which explicitly carries pets to BWN as manifested cargo rather than checked baggage. Singapore looks like it should work too, given how central Changi is to the region, but Singapore Airlines Cargo excludes Brunei from its live animal network altogether, so that particular hub is a dead end for the final leg.

    - heading: "What the whole move costs and takes"
      body: |
        Budget CAD 3,000 to 5,700 for certificates, the titre test, crate and freight, before adding whatever DAA charges for the permit and the government quarantine days, billed in Brunei dollars. Timeline-wise, the vaccination-to-titre gap alone eats up roughly 90 days if done back to back, so 10 to 14 weeks from a standing start to wheels-up is realistic, and that's before the 14 to 30 days of quarantine on the other end. Owners who start this process the month before a job relocation date almost always end up asking whether the timeline can be compressed. It generally can't, not without gaps in the paperwork that DAA will catch on arrival.
