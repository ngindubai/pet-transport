  h1: "Pet Transport from Paraguay to the USA"
  overview: |
    Paraguay does not appear on the CDC's high-risk countries list for dog rabies, last updated 15 April 2026, so a Paraguay-to-US move skips the titre test and the long wait that routes from unlisted or high-risk countries require. That single fact cuts months off the timeline compared with, say, a Paraguay-to-UK move.

    What replaces the titre test is paperwork on both ends: SENACSA's Certificado Veterinario Internacional on the Paraguayan side, and the CDC Dog Import Form on the US side, each free-standing and each needing to be finished properly. Add in the fact that no airline flies nonstop from Asuncion to the US, and a realistic plan runs 8 to 12 weeks rather than 8 to 12 days.

    Copa Airlines, through its Panama City hub, offers the cleanest single-carrier routing. Sao Paulo connections exist too, but they route the shipment through a country the CDC itself flags as high-risk, which is worth avoiding if a simpler option is on the table.

  sections:
    - heading: "Why this route is lighter than it looks"
      body: |
        A titre test and a fixed waiting period are the two things that turn a pet move into a five-month project. Neither applies here. Because Paraguay sits outside the CDC's high-risk list, a dog travelling with a completed CDC Dog Import Form, a working microchip, and a clean bill of health can be presented at any US port of entry, not just one of the 18 airports reserved for high-risk-country arrivals.

        That does not mean no paperwork. SENACSA still requires its own CVI, a clinical inspection within 10 days of departure, and a documented vaccination history covering rabies plus the core canine or feline panel. The two processes run in parallel rather than one replacing the other, and SENACSA's own guidance page for pet owners links straight through to the CDC form, which tells you how tightly the two are expected to be coordinated.

        Cats have an easier path again: the CDC Dog Import Form is dog-specific, so a cat travelling from Paraguay to the US has no federal form to complete at all, just the SENACSA export side and whatever the airline itself asks for.

    - heading: "The Panama routing versus the Brazil alternative"
      body: |
        Silvio Pettirossi International Airport (ASU) has no nonstop service to the United States. Copa Airlines connects through its Tocumen hub in Panama City to a wide spread of US cities, all on Copa's own network, which keeps the shipment under one airline's cargo handling from Asuncion through to arrival.

        LATAM's alternative runs through Sao Paulo. It is a real option, but Brazil itself is on the CDC's high-risk list for dog rabies, and that status has led some carriers to tighten or pause live-animal acceptance on Brazil-to-US sectors specifically, separate from anything to do with Paraguay's own low-risk standing. A pet that never leaves Paraguay's risk category on paper can still get caught up in a stricter routing if the connection runs through a high-risk country's airport. Copa's Panama routing sidesteps the question entirely.

Paraguay is not on that list, last updated 15 April 2026, so a direct Paraguay departure skips the titre test entirely. Source: CDC, High-Risk Countries for Dog Rabies, checked July 2026."

    answer: "It is an online form the CDC introduced in 2024 for every dog entering the United States, regardless of origin country. It is free, the receipt is valid for 6 months from a low-risk country such as Paraguay, and puppies under 12 months need a photo taken within 15 days of travel. Cats are not covered by this form. Source: CDC, Dog Import Form Instructions, checked July 2026."

    answer: "No. There is no nonstop passenger or cargo route between Silvio Pettirossi International (ASU) and any US airport. Copa Airlines, connecting through its Panama City hub, offers the most direct realistic routing on a single carrier. LATAM via Sao Paulo is an alternative but routes the shipment through Brazil, which sits on the CDC's high-risk list."
  - question: "What does SENACSA require before a pet can leave Paraguay for the US?"
    answer: "SENACSA issues the Certificado Veterinario Internacional (CVI), requiring an ISO microchip, a current rabies vaccination, the core vaccine panel for US-bound pets (parvovirus, distemper, infectious canine hepatitis and leptospirosis for dogs), internal and external antiparasitic treatment, and a clinical inspection carried out within 10 days of the CVI being issued. Non-commercial shipments are capped at 5 animals. Source: SENACSA, senacsa.gov.py, viewed July 2026."
