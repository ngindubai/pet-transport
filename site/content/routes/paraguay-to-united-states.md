  h1: "Pet Transport from Paraguay to the United States"
  overview: |
    Paraguay does not sit on the CDC's list of high-risk countries for dog rabies, last updated 15 April 2026. That single fact strips out the FAVN titre test, the fixed waiting period and the CDC-registered airport restriction that make routes from Bolivia, Brazil, Colombia, Ecuador and Peru run to five months or more. A dog moving from Asuncion can be ready in as little as four to six weeks.

    That does not mean skipping paperwork altogether. Every dog entering the US now needs the CDC Dog Import Form, a rule that took effect in August 2024 and applies regardless of risk category. On the Paraguayan side, SENACSA's export CVI, issued close to departure, remains the main gate. Routing is the other variable worth planning around: there is no nonstop flight, and not every carrier will take a dog on every leg.

  sections:
    - heading: "Why Paraguay is not the same as its neighbours here"
      body: |
        Owners researching this move often start from the assumption that every South American departure needs a rabies titre test and a three-month wait, because that is true for Bolivia, Brazil, Colombia, Ecuador and Peru. Paraguay is different. It is not on CDC's high-risk list, so the standard low-risk pathway applies: a completed CDC Dog Import Form, a working microchip, and a dog at least 6 months old on arrival.

        Cats have it easier again. CDC does not mandate a rabies vaccination for cats at the federal border, and USDA APHIS has no separate pet cat import permit. A destination state can still add its own rules after arrival, so check where the animal is actually headed, not just the port of entry.

        Copa Airlines is the strongest single-carrier option. Its flights connect Asuncion to Panama City's Tocumen hub, then onward to Miami, Orlando, New York, Washington, Denver, Las Vegas or Los Angeles, all under one airline. That avoids handing a crate between two different cargo teams mid-journey, which matters more than most owners expect.

        American Airlines Cargo, via PetEmbark, is a workable alternative through Sao Paulo, and it will still take the booking for a Paraguay-origin dog since the country is not on the high-risk list. LATAM flies the same Sao Paulo routing but has restricted dog cargo into the US to Miami only since mid-2024, a rule built around the CDC's own policy change rather than anything specific to Paraguay. Confirm which entry city your booking actually supports before you commit to dates.
