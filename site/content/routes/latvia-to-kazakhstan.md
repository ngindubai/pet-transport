  sections:
    - heading: "Kazakhstan's companion pet entry process"
      body: |
        The Committee of Veterinary Control and Supervision under Kazakhstan's Ministry of Agriculture manages pet imports. For personal companion pets from Latvia, the entry requirements are a current rabies vaccination and a health certificate from the Latvian PVD. There is no titre test and no quarantine for compliant arrivals.

        Kazakhstan has two main international airports that handle live cargo: Almaty International Airport (ALA) in the south and Astana International Airport (NQZ) in the capital. Your routing determines which one your pet arrives at. Border veterinary officers inspect documentation at both airports.

        Kazakhstan is a large country with significant infrastructure for cargo, particularly at Almaty. Provided documentation is complete and consistent, border inspection at either airport is manageable.

    - heading: "The PVD certificate for non-EU destinations"
      body: |
        The EU pet passport that covers intra-EU travel is not a valid document for Kazakhstan. For this move, a Partikas un veterinarais dienests (PVD) export health certificate from an authorised official veterinarian in Latvia is required.

        The PVD at pvd.gov.lv maintains a list of officially authorised vets. Contact the PVD first to identify an authorised vet in your area. The certificate must be issued within 10 days of travel. Confirm with the PVD whether Kazakhstan has specific certificate format requirements before the appointment.

    - heading: "Routing from Riga to Kazakhstan"
      body: |
        Two main routing options exist for pet cargo from Riga (RIX) to Kazakhstan.

        The first is via Istanbul (IST) with Turkish Airlines Cargo, connecting from Riga to IST via a European hub, then IST to either Almaty (ALA) or Astana (NQZ). Turkish Airlines Cargo has established live animal handling at IST and regular flights to Kazakhstan.

        The second option uses airBaltic from RIX to Frankfurt (FRA), then Air Astana from FRA directly to ALA or NQZ. Air Astana's cargo from Frankfurt is a direct Central Asia route and avoids the IST transit.

        Book cargo early and confirm live animal capacity in writing on every leg. Kazakhstan routing from Riga is manageable but requires coordination, particularly around seasonal restrictions and the choice of Almaty versus Astana as the arrival point.
