content:
  h1: "Pet Transport from United Arab Emirates to Seychelles: 2026 Guide"
  overview: |
    Seychelles markets itself, fairly, as one of the world's genuinely rabies-free island nations. What that reputation obscures is how its own import rules treat almost every visiting pet: the National Biosecurity Agency doesn't grant a UAE-origin animal any shortcut, because the UAE itself isn't on the short list of countries Seychelles recognises as rabies-free.

    That means the full rabies-affected pathway applies: a titre test, a fixed three-month wait from the blood draw, and a mandatory month in Seychelles' only quarantine facility on Mahe, even with flawless paperwork. Realistically, this route runs five to seven months from a standing start.

  sections:
    - heading: "Why rabies-free doesn't mean quarantine-free"
      body: |
        It's a common assumption that a rabies-free destination skips quarantine altogether. Seychelles doesn't work that way. Its 2024 import rules make quarantine mandatory by law for every arriving pet, with the only variable being length: 4 weeks for a fully compliant animal coming from a rabies-affected origin, which covers the UAE and, worth noting, covers the US, UK and most of Europe too, since none of them sit on Seychelles' own recognised rabies-free list.

        On top of the titre test itself, which needs a laboratory result above 0.5 IU/mL, Seychelles adds a fixed three-month wait from the blood draw date before the pet is even eligible to travel, not from the result date. Combined with the NBA's own recommended four-month permit lead time, this is one of the longer routes in the whole Gulf-to-island-nation cluster.

    - heading: "A direct flight, and a genuinely thorough health screen"
      body: |
        The logistics end is more straightforward than the paperwork: both Emirates and Etihad fly nonstop into Mahe, with dedicated live-animal handling at each Gulf hub. What's less commonly known is how far Seychelles' health screening goes beyond rabies. Dogs specifically need clearance for Brucella canis, Trypanosoma evansi, Babesia gibsoni, Leishmania and Erlichia canis, a noticeably broader disease panel than most destinations in this series ask for, unless the UAE's own vet can certify the country free of a given disease.
