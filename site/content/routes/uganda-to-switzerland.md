  sections:
    - heading: "FSVO advance permission: the step Switzerland adds"
      body: |
        Every other EU destination in this guide accepts a completed unlisted-country file at the border. Switzerland does not work that way. Before travel is even booked, the FSVO, Switzerland's federal food safety and veterinary office, has to issue written authorisation for a pet coming from a country it classes as high rabies risk, which includes Uganda.

        This sits on top of, not instead of, the standard sequence: microchip before vaccination, a 30-day gap, blood drawn for a FAVN or equivalent test, and a result of at least 0.5 IU/ml. Switzerland then applies a three-month wait rather than the EU's 90 days, in practice close to the same length, counted from the blood draw date. Skip the FSVO application and none of the rest of the file matters. Build it into the very first week of planning.

    - heading: "Flying from Entebbe to Zurich or Geneva"
      body: |
        Ethiopian Airlines via Addis Ababa draws on the deepest East African cargo network of any carrier touching Entebbe, and is usually the first quote worth getting. Turkish Airlines' non-stop Entebbe-Istanbul leg shortens the front half of the journey, with onward IST connections into either Zurich or Geneva. Qatar Airways via Doha is a solid Gulf alternative, though summer heat embargoes on live cargo can affect scheduling between roughly May and September.
