faqs:
  - question: "Does my pet need a titre test to travel from the UK to Georgia?"
    answer: "No. Georgia does not require a titre test for pets from the United Kingdom. You need a current rabies vaccination, ISO microchip, and UK Animal Health Certificate from an APHA-authorised Official Vet."
  - question: "Is there a direct flight from London to Tbilisi for pet cargo?"
    answer: "Yes. British Airways operates direct London Heathrow to Tbilisi (TBS) and BA World Cargo handles pets on this route. No cabin pets on BA. For very small pets, Wizz Air also flies direct UK to Tbilisi with a cabin pet option - confirm Wizz Air's current weight limits before booking."
  - question: "How long does it take to prepare a pet for transport from the UK to Georgia?"
    answer: "Allow 4-8 weeks. The main steps are confirming your pet's microchip and vaccination records, booking cargo space on BA or Turkish, obtaining the UK Animal Health Certificate within 10 days of travel, and confirming NFA documentation requirements. Georgia's process is relatively straightforward by international standards."
  - question: "What is the Georgia NFA and what does it require?"
    answer: "The Georgian National Food Agency (NFA) is the authority for animal health and veterinary matters in Georgia. It oversees pet imports and conducts inspections at Tbilisi airport. Core requirements include a current microchip, valid rabies vaccination, and official UK health certificate. Confirm current NFA requirements before travel."
  - question: "What happens when my pet arrives at Tbilisi airport?"
    answer: "An NFA veterinarian inspects your pet on arrival at Tbilisi International Airport (TBS). They verify the UK AHC, microchip number, and vaccination records. For compliant pets, there is no extended quarantine. Bring original documents."
