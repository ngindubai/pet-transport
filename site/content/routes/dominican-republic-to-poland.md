  h1: "Pet Transport Dominican Republic to Poland: 2026 Guide"
  overview: |
    Moving a dog or cat from the Dominican Republic to Poland requires working through the full EU unlisted country process, and the timeline is significant. Poland is a full EU member state, and because the Dominican Republic sits outside the EU's list of approved third countries, every pet on this route must complete the FAVN titre test pathway before travel is permitted. From the first veterinary appointment to the day your pet arrives in Warsaw, the process takes a minimum of five to six months.

    Poland's competent authority for animal import is GIW, the Glowny Inspektorat Weterynarii, at wetgiw.gov.pl. Border veterinary inspectors at Warsaw Chopin Airport (WAW) examine all incoming pet documentation, including the original titre test laboratory report and the DIGEGA-endorsed EU health certificate. The health certificate is valid for just 10 days from issue, so the timing of the final vet appointment and DIGEGA endorsement needs careful planning in the final weeks before departure.

    There is no direct cargo route from Santo Domingo to Warsaw. Every shipment routes through a European hub: Madrid (MAD) on Iberia, Paris CDG on Air France, or Amsterdam (AMS) on KLM are the main options. All three hubs connect well to Warsaw Chopin Airport. A professional pet transport agent is useful for this route, particularly for coordinating the titre test sample shipment and managing the DIGEGA endorsement timeline.

  sections:
    - heading: "Why the Dominican Republic needs a titre test for Poland entry"
      body: |
        Poland applies EU Regulation 2020/692 in full. This regulation splits third countries into two groups: listed countries, which have veterinary standards broadly equivalent to the EU and benefit from a simpler import process, and unlisted countries, which require a rabies titre test and a 90-day waiting period. The Dominican Republic is an unlisted country, so the titre test process applies to every dog and cat coming from there.

        The blood draw can only happen at least 30 days after the rabies vaccination. The sample is then shipped to an EU-approved laboratory, which analyses it for rabies antibody levels. A result of 0.5 IU/ml or above passes, and from the date the laboratory records that result, the 90-day mandatory wait begins. This period is fixed by law and cannot be shortened. Your pet cannot enter Poland, or any EU member state, until those 90 days have fully elapsed from the confirmed result date.

        Polish border inspectors at WAW check the laboratory documentation carefully, including the name of the laboratory, the date of the blood draw, and the date the result was recorded. Errors or missing information on these points can cause problems at the border. All original documents should travel with the pet, not just copies.

    - heading: "Routing and documentation from Santo Domingo"
      body: |
        Las Americas International Airport (SDQ) in Santo Domingo is the standard departure airport for pet cargo from the Dominican Republic. Punta Cana (PUJ) is an alternative for owners based in the east of the island, though European hub connections from PUJ are less frequent than from SDQ. For the Dominican Republic to Poland route, SDQ is the recommended starting point in most cases.

        The most reliable routings to Warsaw are: SDQ to Madrid (MAD) on Iberia Cargo, then MAD to Warsaw Chopin (WAW); SDQ to Paris CDG on Air France Cargo, then CDG to WAW; and SDQ to Amsterdam (AMS) on KLM Cargo, then AMS to WAW. KLM's hub at Amsterdam is particularly convenient for Poland, given the strong historical air connections between the Netherlands and Warsaw. Confirm live animal acceptance on each cargo segment before finalising the booking, as terms can differ between carriers and routes.

        The DIGEGA export certificate must be issued by a DIGEGA-authorised vet and then endorsed by DIGEGA before your pet departs. The endorsement process typically takes one to two working days. Because the certificate is only valid for 10 days from issue, the vet appointment and DIGEGA visit must be timed so that the certificate remains valid throughout the journey and arrival at Warsaw Chopin Airport.

    - heading: "How long does it take to move a pet from the Dominican Republic to Poland?"
      body: |
        The minimum realistic timeline from first vet appointment to arrival in Poland is 20 to 24 weeks. The key milestones are: microchip implantation, rabies vaccination, a minimum 30-day gap before the titre test blood draw, two to four weeks for the EU-approved laboratory to process the result, and then a full 90 days from the date of the passing result. That core sequence alone takes close to five months.

        In practice, additional time is needed to source an EU-approved laboratory willing to accept samples from the Dominican Republic, arrange reliable international sample shipping, confirm cargo availability on your chosen airline route, and complete the DIGEGA endorsement within the narrow 10-day certificate window. Starting preparations six months before your planned travel date is the safest approach.
