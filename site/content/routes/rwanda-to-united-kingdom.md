  overview: |
    Moving a pet from Rwanda to the UK requires the full APHA import protocol: microchip, rabies vaccination, titre test, 3-month wait, tapeworm treatment for dogs, and an official health certificate from Rwanda. The timeline is 5-7 months from start to travel day.

    Rwanda has one significant advantage over many African origins: RwandAir offers direct service from Kigali International Airport to London Heathrow, making it possible to move your pet without a connecting hub. This matters because each connection adds handling time, stress for the animal, and potential complications with transit country documentation.

    Kigali's veterinary infrastructure is developing well, and approved laboratories for titre testing do operate in East Africa. Your vet will need to confirm which laboratory holds EU/UK approval before drawing blood for the test.
  sections:
    - heading: "UK pet import requirements for pets arriving from Rwanda"
      body: |
        Rwanda sits outside the UK's approved country list for simplified pet travel. The full APHA protocol applies: ISO microchip implanted before rabies vaccination, current rabies vaccination, titre test with blood drawn at least 30 days post-vaccination (result at least 0.5 IU/ml at an EU/UK approved laboratory), a 3-month wait from blood draw date, tapeworm treatment for dogs (24-120 hours before UK arrival), and an official Rwanda health certificate valid for 10 days.

        Entry must be through a UK port approved for animals from non-listed countries. London Heathrow Terminal 4 handles most air cargo arrivals. Confirm which port applies to your specific flight with your cargo agent or airline.

        All documents must be originals with wet signatures and official stamps from the Rwanda Agriculture and Animal Resources Development Board (RAB). Photocopies are not accepted at UK border inspection.
    - heading: "Airline options from Kigali to London: direct versus connecting"
      body: |
        RwandAir's direct Kigali to London Heathrow service is the most attractive option for pet transport. A direct flight eliminates hub connections and the associated risks of missed connections, temperature exposure during transit, and documentation complications in a third country. Confirm with RwandAir Cargo directly that pet shipments are being accepted on your travel dates.

        Qatar Airways via Doha is the most commonly used alternative and has an excellent track record for pet cargo on Africa-UK routes. The Doha connection adds roughly 5-6 hours to the total journey but Qatar Cargo is well-established. Temperature embargoes may apply during summer Gulf months.

        Ethiopian Airlines via Addis Ababa provides good schedule frequency from Kigali and Ethiopian Cargo handles pets. The Addis Ababa connection is a slight additional step but the airline has deep experience with East Africa routes.

        Kenya Airways via Nairobi is worth noting but check whether a Nairobi transit requires any Kenyan veterinary documentation for your pet. This varies and is worth confirming before booking.
    - heading: "Documentation: what the RAB endorsement means and why it matters"
      body: |
        The UK APHA requires health certificates to be endorsed by the national veterinary authority of the origin country. In Rwanda, this is the Rwanda Agriculture and Animal Resources Development Board (RAB). Your private vet in Rwanda issues the certificate; RAB endorses it.

        Allow time for the RAB endorsement step. Processing can take 1-2 weeks depending on current administrative workload. Factor this into your timeline so the endorsed certificate is ready at least a week before the 10-day window starts.

        If you are using an IPATA-registered agent, they will manage the RAB endorsement process as part of their service. For owners arranging transport independently, contact RAB through the Rwanda Ministry of Agriculture website to confirm current endorsement procedures.
