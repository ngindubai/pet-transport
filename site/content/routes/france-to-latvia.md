  sections:
    - heading: "Latvia's Pit Bull ban: the one thing to check before you book"
      body: |
        Latvia's ban on Pit Bull-type dogs is a national legal prohibition, not just a public order rule. American Pit Bull Terrier and dogs classified as similar types cannot be imported into Latvia. If you own a dog of this type or a mixed-breed dog that could be categorised under the ban, Latvia is not an accessible destination.

        For owners of other breeds, Latvia's entry requirements are those of any EU member state: valid EU pet passport with ISO microchip and current rabies vaccination. The Partikas un veterinarais dienests (PVD) is the competent authority at pvd.gov.lv.

        If you are unsure whether your dog's breed is on Latvia's list, contact the PVD directly. The ban is clearly defined in Latvian law, and it is better to clarify before booking than to arrive at Riga Airport with a pet that cannot be admitted.

    - heading: "From Paris CDG to Riga: the Amsterdam connection"
      body: |
        The strongest routing from Paris CDG to Riga is through Amsterdam Schiphol (AMS) with KLM. CDG to AMS takes about 1.5 hours; AMS to RIX is a direct 2-hour flight. The Air France-KLM Group relationship means you can book both legs together, and KLM Cargo handles live animals on this corridor.

        For cabin pets (under 8kg including carrier), the two-leg CDG-AMS-RIX route is manageable. The AMS connection is efficient and well-organised. For larger dogs in cargo, Amsterdam's live animal handling is well-established.

        airBaltic, the Latvian national carrier, is also worth checking. airBaltic operates from various European cities and offers direct connections to Riga from some French departure points. As the carrier at the Riga hub, airBaltic has home-ground familiarity with live animal handling at RIX.

faqs:
  - question: "Do I need a titre test for France to Latvia?"
    answer: "No. France and Latvia are both EU member states. The EU Pet Travel Scheme does not require a rabies antibody titre test for intra-EU movements. A current rabies vaccination in a valid EU pet passport is the requirement. Source: EU Regulation 576/2013."
  - question: "Is there quarantine for pets entering Latvia from France?"
    answer: "No. Latvia does not impose quarantine on pets arriving from other EU member states with a valid EU pet passport. Spot checks by the PVD may occur at Riga Airport but quarantine is not a routine outcome."
  - question: "What breeds are banned in Latvia?"
    answer: "Latvia prohibits American Pit Bull Terrier and similar Pit Bull-type dogs from entry. Latvia also restricts other potentially dangerous breeds (Rottweiler, American Staffordshire Terrier, Bull Terrier, and others) which must be registered and muzzled in public. Check pvd.gov.lv for the current official list."
  - question: "Can I use a private French vet to issue the EU pet passport?"
    answer: "The EU pet passport must be issued by a veterinaire habilitee, an authorised official vet registered with DGAL. Many private vets in France hold this status, but not all. Confirm with your vet before booking the appointment."
