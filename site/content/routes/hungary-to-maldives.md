  overview: |
    One answer on this route never changes no matter how the question is asked. Dogs cannot be imported into the Maldives, a Maldives Customs Service rule sitting on the same prohibited list as alcohol and pork products, with no standard exception a pet owner can apply through.

    A cat is different. Microchip, rabies vaccination given 30 days to 12 months before arrival, the core feline vaccines and a certificate signed within 10 days of travel cover most of what's published. What's thinner is the guidance on permits and titre tests, worth a direct check before flights are booked.

        No confirmed government pathway exists for a dog to enter the Maldives, assistance dogs included, based on any primary Maldivian document we could locate. Online forums occasionally mention informal exceptions. Without a government source backing that up as a repeatable process, we're not presenting it as one. A service dog situation genuinely needs written confirmation from Maldivian authorities directly, not a guess based on secondhand accounts.
    - heading: "What a cat actually needs"
      body: |
        Microchip first, then rabies vaccination in the window Maldivian guidance specifies, plus the standard feline vaccines. A health certificate signed within 10 days of entry finishes the file. Two points remain genuinely open: whether a general import permit applies to a cat travelling with its owner, and whether a titre test is required at all. Sources disagree. Confirm both directly rather than assuming either way.
    - heading: "A pedigree check for exotic-pattern cats"
      body: |
        Savannah and Bengal cats are treated as restricted breeds unless at least five generations removed from their wild ancestor, per guidance summarised by PetTravel.com. Most cats bred for the pet market clear this comfortably, but check the paperwork before booking if it applies to yours.
