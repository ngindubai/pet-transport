  h1: "Pet Transport from Denmark to Georgia"
  overview: |
    Georgia has become a popular destination for international relocations in recent years, and the country's pet import rules are relatively accessible for pets arriving from the EU. There is no titre test requirement, no quarantine for compliant arrivals, and no import permit for personal companion animals. The essentials are a valid rabies vaccination, an ISO microchip, and an official health certificate issued within 10 days of travel.

    What makes this route require planning is the cargo logistics. There is no direct cargo service between Copenhagen (CPH) and Tbilisi (TBS). Turkish Airlines via Istanbul (IST) is the established route. You will need to book through a cargo agent, confirm pet acceptance on both flight legs, and check for seasonal restrictions if you are travelling in summer.

    Allow three to five weeks for preparation, factoring in the Foedevarestyrelsen endorsement step that is specific to Danish export certificates.

  sections:
    - heading: "Georgia's requirements for imported pets"
      body: |
        Georgia's National Food Agency (NFSA), nfa.gov.ge, oversees pet imports. Inspection on arrival takes place at Tbilisi International Airport (TBS). The entry process is straightforward for pets that arrive with complete documentation.

        The required documents are: evidence of ISO 11784/11785 microchip, proof of valid rabies vaccination (administered at least 21 days before entry, within 11 months), and an EU-format official health certificate issued within 10 days of travel. No titre test is required for pets arriving from EU member states. Georgia does not enforce quarantine on compliant arrivals.

        If you are travelling with a small cat or a dog under 8 kg, check whether any carrier on your routing offers in-cabin transport. For most pets on this route, cargo is the realistic option given the connection through Istanbul. Whatever the transport mode, the documentary requirements are the same.

    - heading: "Getting the health certificate right in Denmark"
      body: |
        The Danish export process for non-EU destinations involves two steps that both have timing implications. First, a Foedevarestyrelsen-authorised official vet must examine your pet and sign the EU-format health certificate. Second, that certificate must be submitted to the Foedevarestyrelsen regional office for endorsement, which takes 1-2 working days.

        Book the official vet appointment so the examination date falls within 10 days of your travel. Then immediately submit the certificate to the regional office. If the endorsement is not back before you leave for the airport, you cannot travel.

        Your regular vet can handle vaccinations, microchipping, and pre-travel health checks, but only an authorised official vet can sign the export certificate. Check fvst.dk for the list of authorised vets in your area, and book the appointment as soon as your travel date is confirmed.

    - heading: "Routing options from Copenhagen to Tbilisi"
      body: |
        Copenhagen does not have a direct cargo service to Tbilisi. Turkish Airlines cargo via Istanbul (IST) is the most commonly used path. Turkish Airlines operates regular cargo flights from CPH to IST and from IST to TBS, and the carrier has established procedures for live animal cargo.

        Book through an approved Turkish Airlines cargo agent rather than through the standard passenger booking channel. The agent handles the live animal declaration, crate size confirmation, required IATA documentation, and coordination between the two legs. If your travel falls between June and September, ask about temperature embargoes explicitly. High summer temperatures can trigger restrictions on certain routes, and this can affect availability.

        A second option is Finnair Cargo from Copenhagen to Helsinki (HEL), with an onward connection. This routing adds a transfer and is more complex to coordinate, but it gives an alternative if Turkish Airlines restrictions rule out the Istanbul route for your specific dates. A specialist cargo agent can compare both options and advise.
