  sections:
    - heading: "What Canada requires: a straightforward checklist"
      body: |
        The Canadian Food Inspection Agency (CFIA) keeps the bar relatively low for personal companion pets. Here is what your dog needs to enter Canada from Lebanon:

        - A current rabies vaccination certificate, in English or French
        - A general health certificate from a vet (not mandatory but strongly recommended and often requested at the border)
        - No import permit required
        - No quarantine on arrival

        The one distinction worth knowing: the CFIA's relaxed rules apply specifically to personal companion pets. If your dog is being imported commercially (for sale, breeding, or rescue rehoming), different and stricter rules apply. Assuming you are relocating your own family pet, the process is genuinely manageable.

    - heading: "The Lebanese paperwork: where the extra time comes in"
      body: |
        While Canada's import side is straightforward, Lebanon's export side adds the main complication. You will need an export permit from the Lebanese Ministry of Agriculture, and your export health certificate from a local vet needs to be officially endorsed by the Ministry.

        Lebanese government processing times vary, and it is not unusual for endorsement to take two to four weeks or more. Factor this into your plan from the start. Starting the process at least six to eight weeks before your intended travel date gives you a reasonable buffer.

        Your rabies certificate must be in English or French for Canadian inspection. If your Lebanese vet issued the certificate in Arabic only, arrange a certified translation before the export process begins.

    - heading: "Getting your pet from Beirut to Canada: the airline picture"
      body: |
        There are no direct flights from Beirut to Canada. Your pet will travel as air cargo via at least one connecting hub. The main options from BEY are via Doha (Qatar Airways Cargo), Dubai (Emirates SkyCargo), Istanbul (Turkish Airlines Cargo), or Paris CDG (Air France Cargo), all of which have onward connections to Toronto (YYZ) and other Canadian cities.

        Confirm with the cargo carrier that they accept live animals on all legs of the journey, including the connecting segment. Transit times at hubs vary, and for the welfare of your pet you want as short a connection as possible. Water and feed schedules for the crate must comply with IATA Live Animals Regulations.
