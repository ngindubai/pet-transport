  h1: "Pet Transport from Canada to the Bahamas"
  overview: |
    USDA APHIS states plainly that a US vet's signature is enough for a Bahamas-bound pet, no extra federal endorsement needed. CFIA hasn't published the same statement for Canadian exporters. That gap, more than anything Bahamian, is the detail worth sorting out before booking a flight.

    Everything on the Bahamian side of the move is settled and well documented. BAHFSA's Click2Clear portal charges USD 50 for a 6-month ePermit, no titre test, and a health certificate that has to be dated within 48 hours of the flight.

  sections:
    - heading: "The one genuine open question on this route"
      body: |
        CFIA's general policy for pet exports is that most destinations need the health certificate endorsed by an official CFIA veterinarian, arranged at a booked appointment, not simply signed by any licensed vet. That appointment system takes 2 to 5 business days depending on the district office, longer in busy periods.

        The US side of this exact comparison is settled: APHIS has confirmed no federal endorsement step applies for a Bahamas-bound American pet. Neither BAHFSA nor CFIA has published the equivalent confirmation for Canada. Assume the endorsement is required, book the appointment early, and treat any information suggesting otherwise as something to verify directly rather than rely on.

    - heading: "BAHFSA's side hasn't changed regardless of where the pet started"
      body: |
        The ePermit process runs identically whether a pet is flying from Miami, London or Toronto. Register for Click2Clear, apply for the permit at USD 50, and the approval covers a 6-month window rather than one fixed date, useful if a Canadian flight gets rebooked. No titre test applies to any country of origin, the Bahamas included in that leniency.

        What does change from city to city is the vaccination timing, since BAHFSA wants a primary rabies dose given between 30 days and 10 months before entry, and a health certificate dated within 48 hours of the actual flight. Book the vet appointment against the confirmed Toronto departure, not an earlier convenient slot.

    - heading: "Three carriers, one short hop"
      body: |
        Air Canada and WestJet both fly Toronto Pearson to Nassau direct, with cabin space for a small pet and a genuine cargo option through Air Canada Cargo or WestJet's own service for anything larger. Porter Airlines has more recently joined the route but keeps its pet policy cabin-only across its entire network, so it isn't an option for a bigger dog regardless of how the paperwork turns out.
