  overview: "GIW, Poland's Chief Veterinary Inspectorate, applies the identical unlisted-country rules to Uganda as it does to any third country outside the EU's approved list. There is no shortcut for cats versus dogs, and no exception for a well-behaved, healthy animal with slightly out-of-order paperwork.   sections:
    - heading: "GIW's documentation checks at Warsaw Chopin"
      body: |
        The Chief Veterinary Inspectorate inspects every arriving pet's paperwork against the animal itself. That means scanning the microchip and matching the number against the MAAIF-endorsed certificate, checking the vaccination date sits before the titre test blood draw, and confirming the 90 days have genuinely passed from the draw date rather than simply looking close.

        A certificate issued more than 10 days before the actual entry date is rejected outright, regardless of how sound the rest of the file is. Because MAAIF endorsement itself can take time, this is usually the step that determines when the whole file, not just the biology, is actually ready to fly.

    - heading: "Getting from Entebbe to Warsaw"
      body: |
        Turkish Airlines' non-stop Entebbe-Istanbul leg is the quickest start to this journey, with Istanbul's network reaching Warsaw reliably. Ethiopian Airlines via Addis Ababa draws on one of the most established East Africa cargo operations of any carrier and is worth quoting in parallel. Qatar Airways via Doha is a further option, though it adds transit hours and can face live animal booking restrictions during the hottest Gulf summer months.
