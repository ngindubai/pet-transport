  sections:
    - heading: "The RAB import permit sets the real timeline"
      body: |
        Rwanda requires an import permit from the Rwanda Agriculture and Animal Resources Development Board before a pet can travel, a step that needs applying for well in advance rather than assumed to process quickly. Alongside it, Rwanda asks for a rabies vaccination given between 30 days and 12 months before entry, a wider window than the strict 21-day-minimum rule used on many other routes in this build.

        Microchipping isn't formally mandated by Rwandan law, but it's strongly recommended, since it's the most reliable way to match the pet against its import permit and health certificate on arrival at Kigali.

    - heading: "No titre test, a genuine advantage over the EU-bound routes"
      body: |
        Unlike the EU destinations elsewhere in this Turkey cluster, Rwanda doesn't require a rabies titre test at all, from Turkey or from any other origin. That removes the 90-day wait that dominates so many other routes in this build. Turkish Airlines flies Istanbul to Kigali direct, and once the import permit and health certificate are both in hand, the journey itself is a single non-stop leg.
