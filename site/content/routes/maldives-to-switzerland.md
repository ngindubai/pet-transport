The Maldives has never recorded a rabies case and sits among the countries the UK and US treat as low risk or free of it. That pattern suggests, but does not prove, that the Maldives might not trigger Switzerland's high-risk pathway either. We attempted to verify this directly against the FSVO's published country list and could not get a clean confirmation either way. Don't take a guess on this. Contact the FSVO at blv.admin.ch before booking a single vet appointment around an assumed timeline.

      body: |
        If the Maldives turns out to be classified as high risk, budget the same way Rwanda or Uganda owners do: microchip, vaccination, a titre test at least 30 days later, and a 3-month wait from the draw date, plus FSVO advance permission applied for at least 3 weeks ahead. That's 20 to 26 weeks, easily. If it isn't classified that way, the process could be as simple as vaccination, a waiting period measured in weeks rather than months, and a certificate near travel. The gap between those two outcomes is the reason this guide gives such a wide estimated range rather than a single confident number.
