    - heading: "DLD documentation: what the health certificate needs to cover"
      body: |
        The DLD (Department of Livestock Development) issues Thailand's official pet export health certificates. The certificate must be issued by a DLD-authorised vet and endorsed by the DLD as Thailand's official competent authority.

        For GB entry from Thailand, the certificate must record: the ISO microchip number and implantation date (confirming it pre-dates the rabies vaccination), the complete vaccination history, the titre test result (date, lab, result value), and a statement that the pet was examined and found healthy within 10 days of the planned GB arrival date.

        The certificate is valid for 10 days from the examination date to the GB arrival date. This window is tighter than it sounds when combined with cargo booking logistics. Your vet needs to examine the pet and issue the certificate within 10 days of the flight, which means timing the final vet visit around the flight booking rather than the other way around.

        Dogs also need the tapeworm treatment recorded in the certificate: praziquantel administered between 24 and 120 hours before GB arrival. Plan this treatment window precisely. If you miss it, the treatment must be done again.
