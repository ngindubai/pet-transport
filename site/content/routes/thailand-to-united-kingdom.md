  overview: |
    Thailand is not on the UK's Pet Travel Scheme approved list because of active rabies in the country. This means all dogs and cats from Thailand must complete a full rabies antibody (titre) test process before entering Great Britain. The process requires a FAVN titre test (blood drawn at least 30 days after a valid rabies vaccination), a passing result of 0.5 IU/ml or above, and then a 90-day wait from the blood draw date. The minimum total process time is 5-6 months.

    The distinction that catches people out is the 90-day wait: it starts from the blood draw date, not the day results are received. If your lab takes 3 weeks to return results, those 3 weeks count against your 90-day wait. You travel on day 91 from the blood draw.

    All pets from Thailand travel as manifested cargo. Thai Airways and British Airways are the main carriers on this corridor. A specialist pet transport agent familiar with the UK APHA requirements and the DLD export process is strongly recommended.

faqs:
  - question: "Why does Thailand require a titre test for UK entry when some countries do not?"
    answer: "The UK's Pet Travel Scheme lists countries where pets can enter without a titre test. These are countries with controlled or absent rabies in dogs and wildlife. Thailand has active rabies and is not on the approved list. All pets from non-listed countries must complete the FAVN titre test with a 90-day wait, regardless of the pet's personal vaccination history."
  - question: "How long does the full titre test process take from Thailand?"
    answer: "The minimum is approximately 5-6 months: microchip and rabies vaccination (if not current), wait 30 days, draw blood for FAVN titre test, receive results (2-3 weeks), then wait 90 days from the blood draw date. If the first titre test fails (below 0.5 IU/ml), re-vaccinate and start the 30-day and 90-day sequence again. Start the process as early as possible."
  - question: "Does my cat from Thailand also need a titre test?"
    answer: "Yes. The titre test and 90-day wait apply to both dogs and cats from Thailand. The UK's non-listed country rules apply to all species. The process for cats is the same: microchip, rabies vaccination, titre test (blood draw 30+ days after vaccination), 90-day wait, then travel with an endorsed health certificate."
  - question: "What is the tapeworm requirement for dogs from Thailand entering the UK?"
    answer: "Dogs entering Great Britain from Thailand must be treated against tapeworm (praziquantel) by a veterinarian between 24 and 120 hours before their scheduled arrival time in GB. This is in addition to the titre test requirement. The treatment must be recorded in the travel document. Time the vet appointment in Thailand around your confirmed UK arrival time."
  - question: "What are the consequences of arriving at the UK with an incomplete titre test?"
    answer: "If a pet arrives in Great Britain from Thailand without a valid titre test result or without the completed 90-day wait, it faces penalty quarantine of up to 4 months (120 days) at an APHA-authorised facility in the UK. The owner pays all quarantine costs. In some cases, the pet may be returned to Thailand. Do not travel until the full titre test process is complete."
