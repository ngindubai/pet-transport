      body: |
        Azerbaijan Airlines (AZAL) operates between Amsterdam Schiphol (AMS) and Baku Heydar Aliyev International (GYD). If AZAL Cargo accepts live animal freight on this service, it is the most direct routing from the Netherlands to Azerbaijan: a single non-stop flight of approximately 5-6 hours, with no hub transfer.

        Contact AZAL Cargo to confirm current live animal cargo acceptance on the AMS-GYD route, crate size limits, and any seasonal restrictions.     - heading: "AQTA: Azerbaijan's import authority"
      body: |
        The Azerbaijan Food Safety Agency (AQTA) at aqta.gov.az manages companion animal imports. Contact AQTA 8-10 weeks before travel to confirm the current import requirements for pets from EU member states and to begin the permit process.

        Azerbaijan does not apply EU-style FAVN titre test requirements for pets from EU member states. Current rabies vaccination, ISO microchip, and the NVWA health certificate are the core health requirements alongside the AQTA permit. The AQTA inspection team at Baku's Heydar Aliyev International Airport (GYD) checks documents on arrival.

    - heading: "NVWA export certificate from the Netherlands"
      body: |
        For non-EU destinations, Dutch pet owners need an official health certificate from an NVWA-authorised vet (Officieel Dierenarts). The EU pet passport is for intra-EU travel only and does not apply to Azerbaijan.

        Book a pre-export examination with an NVWA-authorised vet in the Netherlands. They issue the health certificate after examining your pet and verifying microchip and vaccination records. NVWA then endorses the certificate. The endorsed certificate must be issued within 10 days of travel.

        Amsterdam Schiphol is the natural departure point. Schiphol has good live animal cargo handling facilities and is well connected to Caucasus-bound cargo routes.
