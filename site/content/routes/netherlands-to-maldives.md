content:
  h1: "Pet Transport from Netherlands to Maldives: 2026 Guide"
  overview: |
    Amsterdam has one of the best-equipped pet departure facilities in Europe, the Air France-KLM-Martinair Cargo Animal Hotel at Schiphol, which makes the Dutch side of this move about as smooth as this kind of process gets. None of that changes what happens at the other end.

    Dogs cannot enter the Maldives. It's a flat prohibition under Maldives Customs Service rules, with no standard exception for pet owners. Cats have a genuinely workable path instead: microchip, rabies vaccination given 30 days to 12 months before arrival, the core feline vaccines, and a health certificate signed within 10 days of travel.

  sections:
    - heading: "The dog answer, stated plainly"
      body: |
        There's no extended paperwork route, no age exception, and no assistance-animal carve-out that gets a dog into the Maldives. It sits on the customs prohibited list alongside items like alcohol and pork products, applied without a standard pet-owner exception. If a service dog is genuinely involved, get written confirmation from the Maldives government directly.
    - heading: "Departing Amsterdam through the Animal Hotel"
      body: |
        A cat leaving the Netherlands through KLM benefits from Schiphol's purpose-built animal facility on the way out, staffed around the clock with a vet on call. It's a genuinely different departure experience from a standard cargo counter, even though the destination side has none of that infrastructure to match it.
    - heading: "What a cat actually needs to enter the Maldives"
      body: |
        Microchip, then rabies vaccination given 30 days to 12 months before arrival, plus viral rhinotracheitis, calicivirus, panleukopenia and feline leukaemia cover. A health certificate signed within 10 days of departure closes the file. None of it demands the multi-month wait a titre test route would.
    - heading: "The open questions guidance hasn't settled"
      body: |
        Some sources describe no import permit requirement for a travelling pet cat at all; others suggest a general permit covers all pet imports. No single current government page resolves this cleanly, so confirm with your vet or agent rather than assume either version is correct.
