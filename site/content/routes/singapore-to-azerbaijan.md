  h1: "Pet Transport from Singapore to Azerbaijan"
  overview: |
    Flip this route around, from Baku to Singapore, and a pet faces a 90-day titre wait and a month in quarantine. Go from Singapore to Baku, and neither applies. Azerbaijan's State Veterinary Service simply doesn't sort countries into risk tiers the way AVS does.

    What's left is a compact process: an AVS-endorsed health certificate, current vaccination, and a routing that runs through Istanbul or Dubai since Azerbaijan's own national carrier has no service to Singapore. Six to ten weeks covers it.

  sections:
    - heading: "A one-sided rabies risk assessment"
      body: |
        AVS treats every unnamed country, Azerbaijan included, as Schedule III on entry to Singapore. Azerbaijan's own State Veterinary Service takes a different approach: no titre test at all, for any origin, Singapore included. The two systems are not mirror images of each other.

        That asymmetry means the demanding half of a Baku-Singapore pet relationship sits almost entirely on the inbound-to-Singapore leg. Moving from Changi to Baku is a considerably shorter and cheaper exercise.

    - heading: "Getting from Changi to Baku without AZAL"
      body: |
        Azerbaijan Airlines connects Baku to Europe, Russia, the Middle East and a short list of Asian cities, but not Singapore. Turkish Airlines fills the gap: its own Istanbul-Baku service pairs with the nonstop Istanbul-Singapore sector that Turkish Airlines and Singapore Airlines both fly roughly 17 times a week combined.

        Emirates via Dubai is the backup option. It adds distance and time compared with the Istanbul routing, but it's a dependable choice if the Turkish Airlines schedule doesn't fit your dates.
