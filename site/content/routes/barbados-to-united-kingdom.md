content:
  h1: "Pet Transport from Barbados to the United Kingdom"
  overview: |
    Barbados sits on Great Britain's Part 2 list of listed third countries, the same tier as the United States and Canada, and that single classification does most of the work on this route. No rabies titre test, no 3-month wait, just a 21-day gap between vaccination and travel plus a GB health certificate.

    The flying side is equally uncomplicated by Caribbean standards. British Airways runs Grantley Adams (BGI) to Heathrow every day of the year and, since March 2026, a second daily service to Gatwick, so there's no need to route a pet through a third country to get it across the Atlantic.

  sections:
    - heading: "Why the Part 2 listing matters more than anything else here"
      body: |
        Most of the regulatory weight on a Caribbean-to-UK pet move comes down to one question: is the origin country listed or not? Barbados is, carried over into GB law from the old EU rabies regulation, and that puts it in the same bracket as places like Japan, Singapore and Fiji rather than alongside its unlisted Central American neighbours.

        Practically, that means a Barbados vet vaccinates the pet, waits 21 days, and moves straight to the health certificate. No blood sample, no laboratory wait, no 90-day clock. Owners who've researched routes from unlisted countries and expect a rabies titre test are often relieved, and occasionally suspicious, to learn Barbados doesn't need one.

    - heading: "Getting the Barbados paperwork right before you fly"
      body: |
        The export certificate itself is a short process: a vet at Barbados's Veterinary Services office examines the animal, checks the vaccination record, and issues the certificate for BDS $30.00 in cash. There's no separate export permit to apply for on top of it, since Barbados doesn't require one for a pet leaving the island.

        Timing is the one thing to watch. The exam has to happen within 7 days of departure under the UK's own guidance for this route, so it needs booking against a real flight date rather than an approximate one. Do it too early and the certificate can lapse before travel day arrives.

    - heading: "One non-stop flight, and what to do if it's full"
      body: |
        British Airways' Heathrow and Gatwick services from Bridgetown are the obvious first choice, and the only ones that avoid a change of aircraft. Pets travel via IAG Cargo's temperature-controlled hold on the same Boeing 787s that carry passengers, on a roughly 8.5-hour flight.

        If cargo space on a specific date is full, American Airlines' daily Miami service offers a fallback first leg, with the transatlantic connection booked separately. Amerijet, a dedicated freight operator on the same Miami route, is worth knowing about for larger dogs whose crates don't fit a passenger aircraft's hold.
