  sections:
    - heading: "The CFIA requirements in plain terms for Jamaican pets"
      body: |
        The Canadian Food Inspection Agency (CFIA) does not place Jamaica in a high-risk category that would trigger a titer test requirement for personal pets. For a dog from Jamaica accompanying its owner to Canada, the documents are: a rabies vaccination certificate issued by a licensed vet and written in English, and a health certificate from a VSD-registered Jamaican vet.

        The CBSA (Canada Border Services Agency) checks these documents at the Canadian port of entry. The check is a visual and documentary inspection. A dog that appears healthy with correct documentation typically clears in a few minutes. Cats have no federal vaccination requirement and need only a health certificate.

    - heading: "Routing from Jamaica to Canada"
      body: |
        Direct Jamaica-to-Canada flights exist but are limited. Most Jamaica-to-Canada cargo routes connect via Miami or another US gateway to Toronto Pearson (YYZ), Vancouver (YVR), or Montreal (YUL). Air Canada, WestJet, and Caribbean Airlines are the main options, sometimes requiring a connection through an American carrier or hub.

        Toronto Pearson handles the largest volume of live animal cargo arrivals in Canada and has experienced live animal facilities. Vancouver is the better entry for Western Canada. If your destination in Canada is Eastern or Central, route via YYZ. Check the specific airline's policy for accepting live animals on the Jamaica-to-US and US-to-Canada legs.
