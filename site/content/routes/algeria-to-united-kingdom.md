content:
  h1: "Pet Transport from Algeria to the United Kingdom"
  overview: |
    Air Algérie will not carry a cat or dog in the hold on any flight to or from the UK. That single line in the airline's own baggage policy reroutes almost every Algeria-to-London pet move away from a passenger check-in desk and into a cargo booking instead, whether with Air Algérie Cargo itself or a different carrier altogether.

    The regulatory side follows the pattern GB applies to any country outside its listed tables: a rabies titre test, a 3-calendar-month wait counted from the blood draw, and a Great Britain Pet Health Certificate. Add in Algeria's own requirement for French or Arabic paperwork and this is not a fast route, but it is a predictable one once the sequence is understood.

  sections:
    - heading: "Why the titre test sets the earliest possible travel date"
      body: |
        Blood for the titre test can be drawn 30 days after the rabies vaccination at the earliest, and the result needs to clear 0.5 IU/ml. From that draw date, Great Britain requires a further 3 calendar months before the pet can travel, regardless of how quickly the lab confirms the result.

        Owners who count from the day the result email arrives, rather than the day blood was drawn, typically lose 2 to 4 weeks they hadn't planned for. Starting from scratch, with the microchip and first vaccination still to come, 5 to 6 months is a realistic total. A pet with an already-current rabies vaccination can sometimes move in 4 months.

    - heading: "DSV and the language requirement that catches people out"
      body: |
        Exporting a pet from Algeria itself is comparatively straightforward. A licensed vet examines the animal and issues a health certificate, which the Direction des Services Vétérinaires then endorses. There's no separate export permit layered on top for a standard companion pet move, and DSV's turnaround is generally quick once the paperwork is complete.

        The detail worth catching early is language. Algerian officials expect the certificate in French or Arabic, or accompanied by a certified translation if it was drafted in English. A certificate that's perfectly valid on the British side can still cause friction at the Algerian end if nobody thought to translate it in advance.

    - heading: "Getting a pet on a plane when the obvious carrier says no"
      body: |
        Air Algérie operates direct routes from Algiers to Heathrow, Stansted and Manchester, which makes it the airline most owners look at first. Its own pet policy rules it out for hold travel on any UK route; pets have to move through Air Algérie Cargo as a freight shipment instead of accompanying a passenger.

        British Airways, flying Algiers to Heathrow around five times a week, is the more direct fit: all pet movement runs through IAG Cargo, which operates a Border Inspection Post at Heathrow and partners with PetAir UK on the UK side. Turkish Airlines, via Istanbul, is a solid third option, particularly useful if neither Algiers-based freight slot lines up with the end of the 3-month wait; Istanbul's dedicated Pet Lounge handles connecting animals on layovers over 75 minutes.

    - heading: "Total cost across the move"
      body: |
        Algerian-side costs are modest by comparison with the British side: vet visits, the titre test and the DSV-endorsed certificate typically total EUR 300 to EUR 600, though no fixed government fee schedule is published for the export process itself.

        The bulk of the spend sits in GB-side paperwork and air freight. The Pet Health Certificate, crate, agent fee and the freight cost itself usually bring the total move to somewhere between GBP 2,700 and GBP 6,000, with the wide range driven mainly by crate size and whether the pet flies direct or connects through Istanbul.

faqs:
  - question: "Can I check my pet in as baggage on an Air Algérie flight to London?"
    answer: "No. Air Algérie's own policy prohibits transporting animals in the hold on any flight to or from the UK. Pets must be booked as freight through Air Algérie Cargo, or moved with a different carrier such as British Airways (via IAG Cargo) or Turkish Airlines (via Istanbul)."
  - question: "Is Algeria a listed country for pet travel to Great Britain?"
    answer: "No. Algeria doesn't appear on either of GB's Part 1 or Part 2 listed-country tables, so it's treated as unlisted. A rabies titre test, a 3-calendar-month wait from the blood draw date, and a Great Britain Pet Health Certificate are all required. Source: GOV.UK, 'Bringing your pet dog, cat or ferret to Great Britain,' 2026."
  - question: "Does the Algerian export certificate need to be in a specific language?"
    answer: "Yes. Algerian veterinary authorities expect documentation in French or Arabic, or an English-language certificate with a certified translation attached. This is separate from, and additional to, the UK's own Great Britain Pet Health Certificate requirements."
  - question: "Who endorses the export health certificate leaving Algeria?"
    answer: "The Direction des Services Vétérinaires (DSV), part of Algeria's Ministry of Agriculture and Rural Development, endorses the certificate a licensed Algerian vet issues. This confirms the pet is fit to export but has no bearing on whether the separate GB titre test and 3-month wait have been completed."
