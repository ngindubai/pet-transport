  sections:
    - heading: "The one part of this route that's actually easy"
      body: |
        Most Lebanon-to-EU routes in this series involve a connection, usually through Istanbul, Frankfurt or a Gulf hub. Beirut to Athens is different. Aegean Airlines operates a genuine non-stop service, and Middle East Airlines runs the same routing seasonally. For an owner used to reading about multi-leg cargo journeys elsewhere in this guide, that's a meaningfully shorter, cheaper flight day, with lower cargo fees than a connecting itinerary would carry.

        None of that touches the compliance timeline, though. The 90-day wait sets the calendar regardless of how short the flight itself turns out to be.

    - heading: "Why the titre test still applies"
      body: |
        Greece treats Lebanon exactly as it treats every other country outside the EU's approved list under Implementing Regulation (EU) 2026/636. Microchip first, then the rabies vaccination, then a minimum 30-day gap before blood goes to an EU-approved lab. A result of 0.5 IU/ml or above starts a fixed 90-day countdown from the draw date, and Athens border vets won't clear an arrival before that period ends.

        Owners sometimes assume a direct flight implies a lighter process. It doesn't. The titre test and wait are set by EU rules on the country of origin, not by how the pet actually travels.

    - heading: "Getting the Lebanese side signed off"
      body: |
        The Lebanese Ministry of Agriculture issues the export permit and endorses the EU-format health certificate, which needs to be reformatted specifically for Greece rather than left as a generic export document. Two to four weeks is a realistic estimate for that endorsement step, timed so the certificate's 10-day validity window still covers the actual travel date once the 90-day wait finishes.
