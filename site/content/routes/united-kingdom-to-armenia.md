faqs:
  - question: "Does my pet need a titre test to travel from the UK to Armenia?"
    answer: "No. Armenia does not require a titre test for pets from the United Kingdom. You need a current rabies vaccination, ISO microchip, and a UK Animal Health Certificate. Confirm current SSFS import requirements before travel."
  - question: "Are there direct flights from the UK to Armenia for pet cargo?"
    answer: "Wizz Air operates direct UK-Yerevan routes and accepts very small pets in the cabin (maximum 10kg including carrier). For larger pets, cargo routing goes via Dubai (Emirates) or Doha (Qatar Airways). No major carrier offers dedicated pet cargo direct from London to Yerevan."
  - question: "What is Armenia's SSFS and why does it matter for pet import?"
    answer: "The State Service for Food Safety (SSFS) is Armenia's authority for animal health and veterinary matters. It oversees pet import documentation and conducts inspections at Yerevan airport. All import documentation must meet SSFS requirements. Verify current SSFS requirements before travel as they have been updated in recent years."
  - question: "Can I use Russian carriers to connect from the UK to Armenia?"
    answer: "Russian carriers are not recommended for UK-Armenia pet transport. Routing complexity, cargo acceptance, and current geopolitical restrictions make Gulf or European hub connections (Emirates/DXB, Qatar/DOH, Austrian/VIE) the standard and reliable options."
  - question: "What happens when my pet arrives at Yerevan Zvartnots airport?"
    answer: "An SSFS veterinarian inspects your pet on arrival at Zvartnots International Airport (EVN). They verify the UK AHC, microchip, vaccination records, and any import documentation. For compliant pets, the process is a routine health check. Bring original documents."
