  sections:
    - heading: "Routing from Ljubljana: why the hub choice matters"
      body: |
        Ljubljana Airport (LJU) is Slovenia's main international airport but does not offer direct long-haul services to Australia. The routing typically goes LJU to Vienna (VIE) or Munich (MUC) as the first leg, then from those hubs to a Southeast Asian transit point (Singapore or Doha), then to the Australian city.

        Vienna is the closest major hub and Austrian Airlines operates regular LJU-VIE flights. Vienna is a practical staging point for reaching Lufthansa Group carriers, Singapore Airlines, or Qatar Airways onwards to Australia.

        Frankfurt is further but has the Lufthansa Animal Lounge, a dedicated live animal facility that many pet transport agents recommend for long-haul cargo moves. Routing LJU-VIE-FRA-SIN-SYD (for example) adds legs but gives access to Frankfurt's better live animal infrastructure.

        Whichever routing you choose, confirm pet cargo acceptance and temperature conditions on every individual leg before finalising the booking.

    - heading: "The DAFF paperwork chain for Slovenian pets"
      body: |
        Slovenia's animal health authority is the Administration of the Republic of Slovenia for Food Safety, Veterinary Sector and Plant Protection (UVHVVR). They endorse EU export health certificates for international movement.

        For Australia, the certificate must meet DAFF's specific requirements for Group 3 countries. Not all Slovenian vets will be familiar with the DAFF health certificate format. Ask directly whether your vet has processed an Australia-bound export certificate before, or work with a pet transport agent who specialises in Australia moves from EU countries.

        The DAFF import permit should be applied for as early as possible. DAFF requires specific information about the pet, the RNATT result, the travel dates, and the expected arrival airport. The permit is issued in the owner's name and must travel with the pet.

faqs:
  - question: "Does Slovenia's EU membership give any advantage for importing to Australia?"
    answer: "Practically speaking, the main advantage is that Slovenia's UVHVVR is a recognised EU competent authority, making the health certificate endorsement process reliable and internationally accepted. However, EU membership does not reduce Australia's Group 3 requirements: the RNATT, import permit, and Mickleham quarantine all apply regardless."
  - question: "Can my Slovenian vet handle all the DAFF paperwork?"
    answer: "Some can, but not all. The DAFF health certificate format has specific requirements, and vets who have not processed Australia-bound certificates may need guidance. Ask whether your vet has done this before. If not, a pet transport agent who specialises in EU-to-Australia routes can work with your vet to ensure the documentation is correct."
  - question: "What happens at Mickleham in Melbourne?"
    answer: "Mickleham is the Australian government's Post-Entry Quarantine Facility. Your dog or cat is housed there for 10 days under DAFF supervision. DAFF staff manage all feeding, water, and basic care. Owners do not stay at the facility but can arrange visits in some circumstances. DAFF vets conduct health checks. After 10 days, your pet is released provided all documentation was correct."
