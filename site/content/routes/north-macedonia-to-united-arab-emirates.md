  h1: "Pet Transport from North Macedonia to the United Arab Emirates"
  overview: |
    There's a direct flight from Skopje to Abu Dhabi. Wizz Air flies it several times a week through its Abu Dhabi subsidiary. It just won't take your dog or cat, assistance animals aside. That's the first thing worth knowing about this route, and it catches people out precisely because the flight itself is genuinely convenient for everyone except the pet.

    The regulatory side is the easy part. MOCCAE classifies North Macedonia as low-risk for rabies, the same tier as the UK, so no titre test applies. A microchip, a current vaccination, an import permit from the arrival emirate, and a health certificate cover it.

    Route around the airline gap through Istanbul on Turkish Airlines, and this becomes one of the more straightforward moves on this site.

  sections:
    - heading: "The paperwork side: genuinely simple"
      body: |
        MOCCAE's published low-risk country list includes North Macedonia, sitting alongside most EU states and the UK. That status removes the FAVN or ELISA blood test that applies to unlisted-country pets moving to the UAE. What's left is standard: microchip before vaccination, a 21-day wait, an emirate-level import permit, and a certificate valid for 10 days.

        Compare that with the same pet's Australia route, covered separately on this site, where North Macedonia's absence from DAFF's approved lists forces a 180-day stopover in a third country. The UAE simply doesn't apply that kind of test to this origin.

    - heading: "Two flights that look real and aren't, quite"
      body: |
        Skopje International has one nonstop UAE service: Wizz Air to Abu Dhabi. Wizz Air's pet policy is blunt. No cabin pets, no cargo pets, assistance dogs only. The airline that offers the fastest, cheapest way to Abu Dhabi is also the one that categorically cannot move your pet.

        flydubai used to run its own Skopje connection and announced a comeback for 2025. As of July 2026 it hasn't happened. The airline has cited wider delivery delays affecting its aircraft orders, with no confirmed restart date. Don't build a plan around a route that isn't currently flying.

        Turkish Airlines, via Istanbul, is the option that actually works: a live animal facility at the hub, and onward connections into both Dubai and Abu Dhabi.
