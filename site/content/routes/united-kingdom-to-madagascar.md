  h1: "Pet Transport from the United Kingdom to Madagascar"
  overview: |
    Madagascar is often assumed to run a stricter pet import regime than most of mainland Africa, given its status as an island with endemic wildlife found nowhere else. For a UK dog or cat, that assumption turns out to be only half right: there's no rabies titre test at all, from any country of origin, but the paperwork route runs through a letter rather than a form.

    That letter goes to the Direction des Services Veterinaires (DSV) in Antananarivo, and it has to include the owner's details, the animal's details, a copy of the vaccination record, and the flight and arrival information. There's a small fee to pay on landing too: 30,000 Ariary per pet, handed over at the Livestock Fund counter at Ivato International Airport.

    Allow twelve to sixteen weeks. The DSV letter, not the vet work, is what sets that timeline.

  sections:
    - heading: "No titre test, despite Madagascar's biosecurity reputation"
      body: |
        Madagascar's isolation and its unique wildlife might suggest an entry regime built around extra blood tests and lab results. For a companion dog or cat, that's not what happens. The requirement is a rabies vaccination given between 30 days and 12 months before arrival, full stop, with no titre test asked for regardless of where the pet is travelling from.

        The stricter biosecurity posture shows up elsewhere instead: a separate CITES import permit from the Ministry in charge of the Environment applies to CITES-restricted species, a category that doesn't cover an ordinary pet dog or cat. Madagascar's caution is real, but it's aimed at wildlife trade, not at family pets with valid paperwork.

    - heading: "The DSV letter: apply well before booking a flight"
      body: |
        Rather than a standard online import permit form, Madagascar's process runs through a letter of request addressed to the Directeur des Services Veterinaires, listing the owner's name and address, the animal's name, breed, age and sex, a photocopy of the vaccination record, the country of origin, the arrival date, the transport mode and the airport of disembarkation.

        Processing can take several weeks, and no fixed turnaround is published. Send it at least twelve to fourteen weeks before the intended travel date, and confirm the correct current submission channel with DSV directly, since a change of email address or contact point wouldn't necessarily be reflected everywhere at once.

    - heading: "Four routes into Antananarivo, none of them non-stop"
      body: |
        Air France flies Paris to Antananarivo several times a week and, usefully, will carry a small pet under 8kg including its carrier in the cabin on that sector, with larger animals going via Air France Cargo. Kenya Airways and Ethiopian Airlines both connect through their respective East African hubs, Nairobi and Addis Ababa. Turkish Airlines is the longest option, routing through Istanbul with a scheduled stop in Mauritius.

        Madagascar's own national carrier has had a limited international schedule in recent years, focused mainly on domestic routes. Don't build a plan around it without checking its current route map first.
