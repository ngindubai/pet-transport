  h1: "Pet Transport from Uganda to Spain: 2026 Guide"
  overview: |
    Entebbe International Airport (EBB) sits on the northern shore of Lake Victoria and connects Uganda to the world through four main carriers: Turkish Airlines via Istanbul, Ethiopian Airlines via Addis Ababa, RwandAir via Kigali, and Brussels Airlines. None of them fly direct to Spain, which means every pet cargo move from Uganda to Madrid or Barcelona involves at least two airports and two cargo bookings.

    The regulatory side adds to the planning load. Uganda is a non-listed third country under EU rules, so the full FAVN titre test and 3-month waiting period apply before Spain entry is permitted. Total preparation time runs 22 to 28 weeks. The sooner you start, the smoother it goes.

  sections:
    - heading: "What does Spain require for a pet entering from Uganda?"
      body: |
        Spain applies EU Regulation 576/2013 to all pets from non-listed third countries. Uganda is not on the EU approved list, so the mandatory sequence applies: ISO microchip implanted before the rabies vaccination, vaccination (pet must be at least 12 weeks old), FAVN blood draw at least 30 days after vaccination at an EU-approved laboratory, a result of at least 0.5 IU/ml, and a 3-month wait from the blood draw date before Spain entry. Source: food.ec.europa.eu, 2026.

        The 3-month clock starts from the date blood is drawn, not from the date the laboratory issues the result. This distinction matters more on an East African route than on a European one, because the blood sample may need to be shipped to an EU-approved laboratory outside Uganda. Shipping and processing time can add two to three weeks to the overall timeline. The waiting period, however, does not pause during shipping. Start the blood draw as early as possible.

        Spanish border vets at Madrid Barajas inspect all documentation on arrival. No quarantine applies for compliant pets. The responsible authority in Spain is MAPA (mapa.gob.es). Confirm the current AHC template with MAPA or with a receiving vet in Spain before the certificate is issued by the MAAIF-endorsed vet in Uganda.

        Spain's PPP (Perros Potencialmente Peligrosos) classification covers American Pit Bull Terriers, Rottweilers, Staffordshire Bull Terriers, American Staffordshire Terriers, Dogo Argentino, Fila Brasileiro, Tosa Inu, Akita Inu, and crosses. These breeds can enter Spain but require EUR 120,000 civil liability insurance and must be muzzled and on a lead in all public spaces once resident.

    - heading: "How does the MAAIF export process work from Entebbe?"
      body: |
        MAAIF (Ministry of Agriculture, Animal Industry and Fisheries) is Uganda's official animal health authority for companion animal exports. The official veterinary health certificate must be prepared by an accredited official vet and then submitted to MAAIF for endorsement before the pet travels. The MAAIF endorsement is not optional and is not a same-day service. Allow several working days and book the appointment as soon as your travel date is confirmed.

        The AHC format required by EU third-country rules must be issued within 10 days of travel. In practice, the vet appointment and MAAIF submission both fall in the final week before departure. Coordinate these steps carefully in the days leading up to travel.

        For airline options from Entebbe, Turkish Airlines via Istanbul is a popular choice for East Africa to Europe cargo because the EBB-IST-MAD routing is handled by a single carrier, reducing documentation complexity between sectors. Ethiopian Airlines via Addis Ababa is a well-established African hub carrier with strong European cargo connections. RwandAir via Kigali and Brussels Airlines via Brussels both offer routes to the BRU hub, from where connections to Madrid or Barcelona can be arranged.

        Regardless of routing, every individual sector must be confirmed with the specific carrier for live animal cargo acceptance. Do not assume that a carrier's general policy covers your specific flight number, route, or aircraft type. Get confirmation in writing for each leg.

    - heading: "How much does the Uganda to Spain move cost and what are the common problems?"
      body: |
        Total costs on the Uganda to Spain route typically fall between EUR 1,400 and EUR 3,800. Cargo costs are the main variable, running EUR 1,000-2,700 depending on the routing, carrier, crate size, and pet weight. Connected routings through Istanbul or Addis cost more per kilo than a hypothetical direct flight because multiple legs are billed separately by each carrier. The titre test adds EUR 85-170 plus sample shipping. Add veterinary and MAAIF endorsement fees, plus EUR 80-250 for an IATA-compliant crate.

        The most common planning error on this route is booking the first sector without confirming the second. An owner confirms that Ethiopian Airlines accepts their dog from Entebbe to Addis Ababa, then discovers that the ADD-MAD connection on a different aircraft type is restricted for their breed or crate size. Both legs must be confirmed before any booking is made.

        The second problem is the titre test laboratory. Uganda does not have an EU-approved FAVN laboratory. Blood samples need to be shipped, typically to a laboratory in the UK, Europe, or South Africa. Factor two to three weeks for this into your planning. The sample needs to be packaged and shipped correctly to remain viable on arrival. Your official vet in Uganda should have experience with this; if not, an IPATA-registered agent can handle the logistics.
