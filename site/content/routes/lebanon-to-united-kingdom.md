  overview: |
    Moving your pet from Lebanon to the UK involves one of the longer preparation timelines in international pet transport. The UK requires a titre test result and a 3-month waiting period for pets coming from non-listed countries, which means you are planning a 5-7 month project from first vet visit to landing at Heathrow.

    Beirut has direct air connections to London via MEA and indirect routes via Dubai (Emirates), Doha (Qatar Airways), and several European hubs. Each option has trade-offs for your pet: direct is less stressful but has fewer departure frequencies; via Gulf hubs adds journey time but offers more scheduling flexibility.

    The situation in Lebanon means that administrative steps - export permits, certificate endorsements - can take longer than expected. Build buffer into your timeline, and consider working with an IPATA-registered agent who has current knowledge of Lebanese export procedures.
  sections:
    - heading: "UK entry requirements for pets from Lebanon"
      body: |
        Lebanon is not on the UK's list of approved countries for simplified pet travel. The full APHA protocol applies: microchip (ISO 11784/11785) implanted before any rabies vaccination, current rabies vaccination, titre test result of at least 0.5 IU/ml from an approved laboratory, 3-month wait from the date of blood sampling, tapeworm treatment for dogs (24-120 hours before UK arrival), and an official health certificate valid for 10 days.

        The 3-month wait is fixed. It begins on the day blood was drawn for the titre test. Nothing about having rapid results or paying a premium lab changes this wait. Plan your travel date by counting forward 3 months from your titre test blood draw.

        Entry is through an approved UK port of entry. For air cargo arriving from the Middle East, London Heathrow Terminal 4 handles most arrivals. Your cargo agent will confirm which port applies to your specific flight and airline.
    - heading: "Comparing airline options from Beirut to London"
      body: |
        MEA offers the only direct Beirut-London service and is the most straightforward option for pet cargo. Contact MEA Cargo directly to confirm they are accepting pet transport on your travel dates, as policies and capacity vary.

        Emirates via Dubai and Qatar Airways via Doha are the most commonly used alternatives. Both have established pet cargo operations and are used regularly for Lebanon-UK moves. The trade-off is a Gulf hub connection adding 5-8 hours to your pet's total journey time. Emirates restricts brachycephalic (flat-faced) breeds - if your pet is a Pug, Bulldog, or similar, confirm this in advance.

        Lufthansa via Frankfurt works well for medium to large dogs. If your cat or small dog can travel in cabin on the Beirut-Frankfurt leg, this reduces stress significantly compared to a full cargo journey. Larger pets on both legs travel as cargo.

        Whichever airline you choose, book cargo space at least 4-6 weeks out. Do not assume space is available - confirm it.
    - heading: "Export documentation from Lebanon"
      body: |
        The health certificate needs to be issued by an official Lebanese government vet and endorsed by the Ministry of Agriculture. This is not your regular private vet - it must be an officially authorised veterinarian. Your IPATA agent or the Ministry of Agriculture can direct you to the correct practitioner.

        Allow time for the export permit application. Processing at the Lebanon Ministry of Agriculture can take 2-4 weeks under normal circumstances, longer during periods of administrative disruption. Apply as soon as your titre test is complete and you have your results.

        Bring original documents on travel day. Airlines and UK APHA inspectors require original paper certificates with wet signatures and official stamps. Keep copies separately in case originals are questioned.
