  overview: |
    Lebanon does not require a titre test for EU-origin pets, and there is no quarantine on arrival at Beirut Rafic Hariri International Airport for compliant arrivals. The paperwork process for Belgium is the standard EU export routine, but Lebanon has one timing requirement that requires attention: rabies vaccination must be administered at least 30 days before entry, not the 21 days that apply in many other countries.

    Check current Belgian and Lebanese government travel advisories before booking. The operational situation at Beirut airport varies, and it is sensible to confirm that cargo services are running normally before committing to a date.

  sections:
    - heading: "What Lebanon requires at the border"
      body: |
        Lebanon's veterinary import requirements are administered by the Ministry of Agriculture. Officers inspect documentation at Beirut Rafic Hariri International Airport (BEY) on arrival.

        The core requirements are a valid rabies vaccination administered at least 30 days before entry (note: 30 days, not 21), an official health certificate issued within 10 days of travel, and an ISO-compliant microchip. Lebanon does not require a titre test for EU-origin pets.

        No quarantine applies for compliant arrivals. For 1-2 personal companion animals, no import permit is required. Verify the current position with the Ministry of Agriculture Lebanon before booking, as requirements can change.

    - heading: "FAVV health certificate process from Belgium"
      body: |
        Belgium's export health certificate for non-EU destinations is the EU-format official veterinary certificate signed by a FAVV-authorised official vet and then endorsed by FAVV (Federal Agency for the Safety of the Food Chain). Both steps must complete within 10 days of your departure date.

        Book the vet examination within 10 days of your travel date. After signing, submit the certificate to FAVV for endorsement and allow 1-2 working days. Your regular vet can handle vaccinations and microchipping, but the official health certificate must come from a FAVV-authorised official vet. Check favv-afsca.be for the list of authorised vets.

    - heading: "Cargo routing from Brussels to Beirut"
      body: |
        There is no direct cargo service from Brussels (BRU) to Beirut (BEY). Turkish Airlines Cargo via Istanbul (IST) is the primary established route. Turkish Airlines operates BRU-IST regularly, and IST to BEY is a well-served route.

        Book through an approved Turkish Airlines cargo agent. Confirm pet acceptance on both the BRU-IST and IST-BEY legs. If travelling June to September, check for temperature embargoes on the IST connection. Brachycephalic breed restrictions apply year-round on Turkish Airlines cargo.
