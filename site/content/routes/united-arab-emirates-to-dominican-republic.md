  h1: "Pet Transport United Arab Emirates to Dominican Republic"
  overview: |
    No import permit needed, provided you're bringing five pets or fewer. That single fact makes the Dominican Republic's side of this route unusually light on paperwork, especially compared with the Kazakhstan or Jamaica corridors covered elsewhere in this build.

    What the route lacks in destination-side bureaucracy, it makes up for in distance. There's no direct flight from Dubai to Santo Domingo, so this journey runs through either Madrid on Iberia Cargo or Miami on American Airlines Cargo, both well-trodden Gulf-to-Caribbean paths for pet cargo.

  sections:
    - heading: "Why the Dominican Republic asks for so little"
      body: |
        For up to five personal pets, the Dominican Republic requires no import permit at all. What it does require is straightforward: an ISO microchip, rabies vaccination administered between 30 days and 12 months before entry, and a health certificate issued within 15 days of travel and endorsed by MOCCAE on the UAE side. No titre test applies to pets from the UAE, since that requirement is reserved for countries the DGAG classifies as high-rabies-risk. On arrival at Las Americas International Airport, officials check the microchip against the paperwork, and there's no quarantine for a compliant pet.

    - heading: "The UAE's mandatory export process"
      body: |
        Leaving the UAE means the same MOCCAE process regardless of where the pet is headed: an online application through the MOCCAE portal, an in-person clinic exam in Dubai or Abu Dhabi, and a final check at the airport before the flight. The certificate is valid for 30 days from issue, so it's worth locking in your Madrid or Miami connection before starting the paperwork.

    - heading: "Two workable routings, neither of them direct"
      body: |
        Iberia Cargo via Madrid draws on Iberia's strong Latin American and Caribbean network, making it a natural fit for a Gulf departure. American Airlines Cargo via Miami is the other realistic option, with a short onward hop into Santo Domingo. Either way, this is a two-leg journey, so build in enough time between connections for the live animal cargo transfer, and confirm both legs are booked before finalising the MOCCAE application.
