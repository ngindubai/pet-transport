  overview: |
    Moving a dog or cat from Norway to the Dominican Republic is a manageable international move with the right preparation. The Dominican Republic does not require a rabies titre test for personal companion pets, and there is no quarantine for compliant arrivals. That removes two of the biggest delays common to other non-EU routes.

    The practical requirements are a current microchip, a valid rabies vaccination administered at least 30 days before entry, a health certificate issued by an official vet, and current DHPP vaccination for dogs. Four to six weeks is enough preparation time for most pets whose vaccinations are already current.

    Cargo routing goes via Amsterdam Schiphol with KLM, or via Madrid with Iberia. There are no direct cargo flights from Oslo Gardermoen to Las Americas Airport (SDQ) in Santo Domingo.

  sections:
    - heading: "What the Dominican Republic requires"
      body: |
        The Dominican Republic's pet import rules are administered by the Ministerio de Agricultura through its Direccion General de Ganaderia (DGAG). Veterinary inspection takes place at Las Americas International Airport in Santo Domingo on arrival.

        The requirements for dogs and cats are: a microchip conforming to ISO 11784/11785, a valid rabies vaccination administered between 30 days and 12 months before entry, a health certificate issued within 15 days of travel, and full vaccination records. For dogs, DHPP vaccination (distemper, adenovirus, parainfluenza, parvovirus) must be current. For cats, panleukopenia, rhinotracheitis, and calicivirus vaccinations are expected.

        Importantly, no titre test is required from Norway. There is no quarantine for pets arriving with correct documentation. Up to five personal companion animals can be imported without an import permit. If you are travelling with more animals, you will need a commercial authorisation through VUCE before departure.

    - heading: "Health certificate and export documentation from Norway"
      body: |
        Norway is an EEA member and follows EU-aligned veterinary export procedures. For travel to non-EU countries such as the Dominican Republic, you need an official veterinary health certificate issued by a Mattilsynet-authorised official veterinarian. This is not the same as a certificate from your regular practice vet unless that vet holds official authorisation.

        The certificate must be issued within 15 days of travel (the Dominican Republic allows 15 days, more than the 10-day window used by EU countries). It must include the owner's full name and address, plus the animal's name, breed, sex, colour, age, and microchip number. If the signing vet is accredited but not a government official vet, a countersignature from an official vet is required.

        Check the Mattilsynet website at mattilsynet.no for the list of authorised official veterinarians in your region. Book the appointment early to ensure the certificate is issued within the validity window.

    - heading: "Cargo routing from Oslo to Santo Domingo"
      body: |
        There are no direct cargo flights from Oslo Gardermoen (OSL) to Las Americas International Airport (SDQ) in Santo Domingo. All shipments route through at least one European hub.

        The most straightforward option is via Amsterdam Schiphol (AMS) with KLM cargo. SAS and Norwegian Air operate regular services from Oslo to Amsterdam, and KLM has established cargo routes to the Dominican Republic. Book through a KLM cargo agent and confirm live animal acceptance on both legs before committing to a travel date.

        A second option routes through Madrid (MAD) with Iberia or Air Europa. SAS and Norwegian connect Oslo to Madrid, and both Iberia and Air Europa have services to Santo Domingo. The Madrid routing adds distance but can offer useful scheduling flexibility.
