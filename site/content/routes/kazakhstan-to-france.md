    - heading: "Sample shipping from Kazakhstan: planning the laboratory step"
      body: |
        The FAVN titre test must go to an EU-approved laboratory. These are mostly in Western Europe, the UK, or the USA. From Kazakhstan, international sample shipping is a more involved step than from Georgia or Armenia. The blood must be drawn, correctly preserved, and shipped to the lab by a courier that handles biological samples across international borders.

        Allow three to five weeks from blood draw to receiving your result. Confirm with your vet that they have experience with international FAVN sample handling. If not, a pet transport agent based in Kazakhstan or one with experience on this corridor can coordinate the laboratory, the courier, and the sample documentation. A mislabelled or improperly stored sample means starting again.

Total journey from ALA to CDG is typically seven to ten hours.

faqs:
  - question: "Kazakhstan is not in the EU, but does it have any bilateral agreements that affect pet import to France?"
    answer: "No. Kazakhstan's various agreements with the EU, including the Enhanced Partnership and Cooperation Agreement (EPCA), cover trade and political cooperation but do not alter EU veterinary import classifications for pets. Kazakhstan remains an EU unlisted country and the full titre test plus 90-day wait process applies. Source: Commission Delegated Regulation (EU) 2026/131."
  - question: "Can I use a veterinary laboratory in Russia for the FAVN titre test?"
    answer: "Only if the laboratory is on the EU's approved list. EU-approved laboratories are listed at food.ec.europa.eu. Some laboratories in Russia have historically held EU approval, but the EU-approved list changes. Check the current list before sending samples anywhere. If the lab is not on the EU list, the test result will not be accepted."
  - question: "My dog was vaccinated in Kazakhstan two years ago and the titer was done then. Is the previous result still valid?"
    answer: "A passing FAVN result remains valid for the dog's lifetime if rabies vaccination coverage has been maintained continuously without lapse. If you have kept up with boosters since the original titer was done, the result may still be valid. If there was any gap in vaccination coverage, the result is no longer valid and a new titer must be done."
