    Lithuania to Lebanon is a manageable cargo route with no quarantine for compliant arrivals and no titre test required for EU-origin pets. The requirements are an ISO microchip, a current rabies vaccination (30 days to 12 months before entry), core vaccinations, parasite treatments, and a VMVT-endorsed official health certificate from a Valstybine maisto ir veterinarijos tarnyba-authorised vet.

  sections:
    - heading: "VMVT health certificate and what Lebanon requires"
      body: |
        Lithuania's official veterinary authority for non-EU export is the Valstybine maisto ir veterinarijos tarnyba (VMVT). An official health certificate from a VMVT-authorised vet is the required export document for travel to Lebanon. The EU pet passport does not apply for this route.

        Lebanon's Ministry of Agriculture requires: an ISO-compliant microchip implanted before vaccination, a current rabies vaccination (30 days to 12 months before entry), distemper, hepatitis, parvovirus, and leptospirosis for dogs, and panleukopenia, rhinotracheitis, and calicivirus for cats. Internal and external parasite treatment records are also required on the health certificate. Inspection takes place at Beirut-Rafic Hariri International Airport on arrival. There is no quarantine and no titre test for EU-origin pets. Confirm requirements directly with the Lebanese Ministry of Agriculture before travel.

    - heading: "Cargo from Vilnius to Beirut"
      body: |
        Vilnius Airport (VNO) connects to European hubs via Turkish Airlines, LOT Polish Airlines via Warsaw, Lufthansa via Frankfurt, and others. Turkish Airlines cargo via Istanbul Airport (IST) is the main route to Beirut-Rafic Hariri International Airport (BEY): connect from VNO to IST, then Turkish Cargo to BEY. Turkish Airlines operates regular live animal cargo services on this segment.

        Middle East Airlines (MEA) is a second option from a European hub such as Frankfurt. Book through a cargo agent and confirm live animal acceptance on each segment of the journey before finalising the travel date.
