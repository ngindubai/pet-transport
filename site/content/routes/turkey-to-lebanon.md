content:
  h1: "Pet Transport from Turkey to Lebanon"
  overview: |
    Istanbul to Beirut is a short flight and, by the standards of this build, a genuinely simple one. Lebanon doesn't ask for a rabies titre test from Turkey, so the process comes down to two things: a Turkish health certificate and a Lebanese import permit, applied for ahead of travel.

    That permit is the one step worth starting early. Everything else on this route moves fast once it's granted.

  sections:
    - heading: "No titre test, but a permit that needs lead time"
      body: |
        Lebanon doesn't run the EU-style rabies-risk classification system that drives the titre test requirement on so many other routes in this build. A pet with current vaccination records and a compliant health certificate clears entry at Beirut without a blood test. The step that actually needs planning is the import permit itself, issued by Lebanon's Directorate General of Agriculture, which should be applied for as early as possible rather than assumed to be quick.

        Turkey's own export side is straightforward: no export permit, just a TRGM-authorised vet's health certificate reformatted to Lebanon's required standard.

    - heading: "The shortest, most frequent leg in this build"
      body: |
        Both Turkish Airlines and Middle East Airlines fly Istanbul to Beirut direct, under two hours in the air, several times a day between the two carriers. Cargo space is rarely the bottleneck here. Once the import permit clears and the health certificate is current, the flight itself is the easy part of this move.
