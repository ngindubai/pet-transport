content:
  h1: "Pet Transport from Macau to Germany"
  overview: |
    Germany refuses entry to four dog breeds at federal level, full stop, no matter how complete the paperwork is. That rule sits on top of a gap in Macau's EU status already documented elsewhere on this site: Hong Kong is exempt from the EU's rabies titre test, Macau is not, and that single difference shapes the whole timeline regardless of which EU country the pet is headed to.

    What's specific to Germany is what happens once the titre test and 90-day wait are behind you. Frankfurt is the main entry point, Lufthansa Cargo runs the Hong Kong sector close to daily, and the breed ban is a hard stop for four named breeds with no documentation workaround.

  sections:
    - heading: "The same EU gap, a federal breed ban on top"
      body: |
        Macau isn't named on the EU's list of territories exempt from the rabies titre test, run since 22 April 2026 under Delegated Regulation (EU) 2026/131. Hong Kong is. That gap alone means a Macau-origin pet needs blood drawn at least 30 days after vaccination, a result of at least 0.5 IU/mL, and a 90-day wait counted from the draw date, not the jab date, before Germany will let it in.

        Germany adds something France doesn't: a federal ban on importing four specific breeds, Pit Bull Terrier, American Staffordshire Terrier, Staffordshire Bull Terrier and Bull Terrier, under the Hundeverbringungs- und -einfuhrbeschränkungsgesetz. There's no exception for a pet with otherwise perfect paperwork. If you own one of these breeds, or a cross of one, check this before booking anything else on this route.

    - heading: "What the titre pathway actually requires"
      body: |
        Line up the sequence correctly: microchip first, then the rabies vaccination, valid from 21 days after it's given. The titre blood draw can't happen until at least 30 days post-vaccination, and IAM-recognised vets in Macau handle this routinely for other EU destinations, so ask specifically for the EU non-commercial movement format when booking.

        No quarantine applies for a pet that clears all of this. Entry runs through a document and identity check at a designated Traveller's Point of Entry, Frankfurt handling the bulk of German traffic, with Munich, Berlin Brandenburg, Dusseldorf and Hamburg also approved.

    - heading: "Hong Kong first, then a near-daily lift to Frankfurt"
      body: |
        Macau International Airport carries no long-haul cargo, so the crate crosses by road over the Hong Kong-Zhuhai-Macau Bridge, or by ferry, in around an hour, before clearing Hong Kong's cargo terminal. From there, Lufthansa Cargo's Hong Kong-Frankfurt service runs close to daily, an MD-11F six times a week plus a Saturday AeroLogic 777F rotation, which gives more schedule flexibility than a single weekly connection would.

        One thing that doesn't apply here despite the caution some vets default to: Germany doesn't require the Echinococcus multilocularis tapeworm treatment. That rule belongs to a specific group of countries, Finland, Ireland, Malta and Norway among them, that Germany itself doesn't join.
