  h1: "Pet Transport from Maldives to Hungary"
  overview: |
    Ask a Male-based owner who is handling their cat's move, and the answer is usually somebody in Colombo. The Maldives has no pet shipping agent of its own, so most of the region's traffic runs through IPATA-registered specialists based in Sri Lanka, the nearest hub with the infrastructure to book cargo, arrange crates and manage the paperwork remotely. It's a detail most Maldives route guides skip entirely.

    Getting to Budapest itself needs a Gulf or Istanbul connection; nothing flies Male to Hungary non-stop. Once in the air, Hungary's rules are the standard EU-unlisted-country sequence: microchip, rabies vaccination, an FAVN or ELISA titre test, and a 90-day wait from the blood draw.

  sections:
    - heading: "Why a Male owner ends up calling Sri Lanka"
      body: |
        No agency based in the Maldives itself specialises in pet relocation, as far as we could confirm. The closest established infrastructure sits in Colombo, home to several IPATA-certified operators who have handled international pet shipping since well before Maldives outbound demand grew. It's normal, not a red flag, for a Male-based owner to be coordinating with an agent who has never set foot on the island; the vet work happens locally, the booking and paperwork logistics happen remotely.
    - heading: "The lab problem, again"
      body: |
        No rabies antibody testing laboratory operates in the Maldives, so every blood sample for the titre test leaves the country before testing starts. That adds time on top of the standard 30-day and 90-day windows, and it's worth confirming with your vet exactly where the sample is headed before setting a vaccination date around a fixed deadline.
    - heading: "NEBIH's rule, no shortcut for a compliant arrival"
      body: |
        Hungary treats the Maldives the same as any EU-unlisted country: microchip before vaccination, a 30-day gap, a titre result of 0.5 IU/ml or above, then a 90-day wait from the draw date. NEBIH can impose conditional quarantine if that wait hasn't fully run its course on arrival, so don't fly early hoping the clock will finish mid-journey.
    - heading: "Getting from Male to Budapest"
      body: |
        Qatar Airways via Doha is the steadiest option, with a purpose-built animal facility at the Doha end. Emirates via Dubai and Turkish Airlines via Istanbul both work too, and Turkish's dense Central European network is worth pricing against the Gulf carriers on timing as much as cost.
