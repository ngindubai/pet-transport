  overview: "Estonia and Sweden are both EU member states, which makes this one of the more straightforward international pet moves available. Your pet travels on an EU pet passport, there is no quarantine, no titre test, and no import permit.   sections:
    - heading: "Tallinn to Stockholm: flight options for pets"
      body: |
        airBaltic is the natural first choice for this corridor. The airline is based at Riga (RIX) and connects Tallinn (TLL) to Stockholm Arlanda (ARN) via Riga, with cabin and live animal cargo both available. Direct TLL-ARN services are also available on SAS, which operates SAS Cargo for larger dogs that cannot travel in cabin. Ryanair runs budget services on the Tallinn corridor but is cabin-only, with no cargo hold for animals. Pet spaces on any airline are limited per flight, so book early. For large dogs travelling as cargo, contact SAS Cargo or airBaltic cargo directly to confirm crate dimensions, weight limits, and any breed restrictions.

    - heading: "EU pet passport: what your Estonian vet needs to do"
      body: |
        An official vet in Estonia, authorised by the Pohllumajandus- ja Toiduamet (Agricultural Board, agri.ee), issues the EU pet passport. The passport records your pet's microchip number, rabies vaccination dates, and the issuing vet's details. Sweden accepts this document without any additional health certificate. The sequence matters: microchip implantation must happen before or on the same day as the first rabies vaccination. If the vaccination came first, Sweden may not accept it as valid. Check the dates in the passport at the vet appointment and correct any errors before you travel. A booster given within the valid window of the previous dose does not restart the 21-day wait.

faqs:
  - question: "Do I need a titre test to take my dog from Estonia to Sweden?"
    answer: "No. Titre tests are only required when travelling from unlisted countries to listed ones. Estonia and Sweden are both EU member states, so no titre test is needed in either direction."
  - question: "Can my cat travel in the cabin on the Tallinn to Stockholm flight?"
    answer: "Yes, on airBaltic and SAS, small cats in approved carriers can travel in cabin if they fit under the seat. The carrier and cat together typically must not exceed 8 kg. Check the specific airline's current weight and size limits when booking."
  - question: "How long before travel does my dog need the rabies vaccination?"
    answer: "If this is a primary vaccination, at least 21 days must pass before travel. If your dog has had a previous valid vaccination and the booster was given before the previous one expired, no waiting period applies. The dates in the EU pet passport will show whether the 21-day rule applies."
  - question: "Does my dog need tapeworm treatment before entering Sweden?"
    answer: "No. Sweden does not require tapeworm treatment for dogs arriving from other EU member states including Estonia. Tapeworm treatment is required for dogs entering Finland and Norway from countries outside the EU, which does not apply here."
