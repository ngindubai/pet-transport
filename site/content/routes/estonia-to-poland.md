        LOT Polish Airlines operates a direct service between Tallinn and Warsaw, and the airline accepts small pets and cats in the cabin, up to 8kg combined weight with the carrier, for a EUR 50 fee. Reservations for cabin pets should be made through Manage My Booking at the time of flight booking, since space is limited and not guaranteed at check-in.

    - heading: "EU pet passport or AHC after the April 2026 rule change"
      body: |
        The EU pet passport remains the simplest document for this move, issued once by a VTA-authorised vet in Estonia and valid for the pet's life provided vaccinations stay current. From 22 April 2026, the passport is restricted to owners primarily resident in the EU, which covers the great majority of Estonian pet owners without any change to their process.

        If you are a non-EU national living in Estonia, for example on a work permit, use an AHC instead. It is endorsed by the VTA, valid for a single trip, and must be issued within 10 days of entry into Poland.

faqs:
  - question: "Does my pet need a titre test to travel from Estonia to Poland?"
    answer: "No. Both countries are EU member states, and intra-EU pet travel does not require a rabies titre test. A current rabies vaccination and either an EU pet passport or an AHC are sufficient."
  - question: "Can I bring my cat in-cabin on LOT from Tallinn to Warsaw?"
    answer: "Yes, provided the combined weight of the cat and carrier is under 8kg. Reserve the cabin pet space when booking the flight, not at check-in, since LOT limits the number of pets per flight. The fee is EUR 50."
  - question: "Do I need a new EU pet passport for Poland if mine was issued years ago in Estonia?"
    answer: "No. The EU pet passport does not expire. What matters is that the rabies vaccination recorded inside it is current. If it has lapsed, a VTA-authorised vet can administer a booster and add the entry to your existing passport."
  - question: "What happens if I am a non-EU national living in Estonia?"
    answer: "Since 22 April 2026, the EU pet passport is restricted to owners primarily resident in the EU. Non-EU nationals resident in Estonia should use a single-use AHC, endorsed by the VTA and issued within 10 days of travel to Poland."
