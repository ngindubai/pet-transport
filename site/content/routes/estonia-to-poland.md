  sections:
    - heading: "Flying direct from Tallinn to Warsaw"
      body: |
        LOT Polish Airlines is the standout option for this route. The Tallinn to Warsaw (Chopin Airport, WAW) service is direct and takes under 2 hours. LOT accepts small pets (up to 8kg including carrier) in the cabin, and larger dogs can travel as checked baggage in the pressurised hold.

        Book your pet's cabin space at the same time as your ticket. LOT limits the number of animals per flight. For cargo or checked-baggage dogs, contact LOT Cargo directly to confirm your booking.

        Alternatives include airBaltic via Riga and Lufthansa via Frankfurt. These add a connection but give more scheduling flexibility. Warsaw Chopin (WAW) is the primary hub; Krakow (KRK) and Gdansk (GDN) can be reached via Warsaw with onward domestic connections.

    - heading: "What Poland requires at the border"
      body: |
        Poland's Chief Veterinary Inspectorate (GIW) follows standard EU rules for incoming pets. Your dog, cat, or ferret needs an ISO 11784/11785 microchip, a valid rabies vaccination with the 21-day first-vaccination wait observed, and an EU pet passport or AHC.

        The AHC, if used, must be endorsed by an official Estonian vet within 10 days of your arrival in Poland. If you are driving or taking a ferry, the 10-day clock starts on the day you cross the border.

        Poland has no national breed ban. Certain breeds (listed under the Polish Animal Protection Act and related regulations) must be muzzled and kept on a lead in public areas. This is a domestic regulation that applies after arrival, not a condition for entry.

    - heading: "EU pet passport: the simple way"
      body: |
        An EU pet passport from an authorised Estonian vet is the most convenient document for any pet that may travel more than once. It records the microchip number, vaccination history, and owner details in a single booklet accepted across all EU member states and many non-EU countries.

        Once issued, the passport itself does not expire. What determines validity is the vaccination status recorded in it. As long as your pet's rabies vaccination is current, the passport is valid for travel.

        If you are also planning a return trip to Estonia at some point, the EU passport saves you arranging an AHC for the return journey.

    answer: "No. Titre tests are required when travelling from a non-listed country to an EU destination, or to certain high-biosecurity countries like the UK or Australia. For Estonia to Poland, both being EU states, a titre test is not needed."
