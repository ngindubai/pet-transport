  h1: "Pet Transport Uruguay to Poland: 2026 Guide"
  overview: "Uruguay to Poland is a route that combines South America's longest preparation requirements with one of Central Europe's most thorough veterinary border authorities. Uruguay is an unlisted third country under Commission Delegated Regulation (EU) 2026/131, making the FAVN titre test mandatory. The 20-26 week preparation timeline is driven by the 30-day pre-draw wait and 90-day post-draw wait. The routing from Montevideo to Warsaw typically involves three cargo legs, and Poland's Main Veterinary Inspectorate (GIW) applies detailed checks at Warsaw Chopin Airport."

  sections:
    - heading: "GIW at Warsaw: What to Expect"
      body: |
        The Główny Inspektorat Weterynarii (GIW, wetgiw.gov.pl) is Poland's Main Veterinary Inspectorate and the border authority at Warsaw Chopin Airport (WAW). GIW officials apply EU pet movement rules and are particularly careful in checking documentation from South American routes, where compliance errors are more common.

        For a pet arriving from Uruguay, GIW checks:

        Microchip: ISO 11784/11785, implanted before vaccination. Scanned on arrival and verified against the health certificate.

        Rabies vaccination: Current and post-microchip. 21 days must have elapsed since the primary vaccination.

        FAVN titre test: Result 0.5 IU/ml or above, at an EU-approved laboratory, blood drawn at least 30 days after vaccination. 90 days must have elapsed from the blood draw date to the date of arrival at WAW.

        Health certificate: EU Annex IV non-commercial format, DGSG-endorsed, issued within 10 days of travel. All titre test details in full.

        No quarantine. No import permit. But document quality matters at GIW.

    - heading: "Building the Routing from MVD to WAW"
      body: |
        Getting from Montevideo (MVD) to Warsaw (WAW) is a genuine logistics exercise. No direct cargo service exists, and the journey typically involves three cargo sectors.

        Option A (via São Paulo): MVD to GRU on LATAM, then GRU to FRA or AMS transatlantic, then FRA or AMS to WAW. LATAM handles the first leg well and both Frankfurt and Amsterdam have good South American cargo connections.

        Option B (via Buenos Aires): MVD to EZE on a regional carrier, then EZE to MAD on Iberia Cargo, then MAD to WAW on LOT Polish Airlines. This is a strong routing if Iberia capacity on the South Atlantic sector is available.

        Option C (via multiple hubs): MVD to GRU, GRU to CDG on Air France, CDG to WAW. Air France's South American service is well established and CDG to WAW is served by LOT Polish Airlines regularly.

        In all cases, LOT Polish Airlines is the natural final-leg carrier for Warsaw. Book the LOT sector early as live animal cargo capacity is limited.

    - heading: "The DGSG Endorsement Process"
      body: |
        Uruguay's MGAP-DGSG endorsement is the final official step before travel. Here is how it works in practice.

        Your MGAP-registered vet prepares the EU Annex IV health certificate template, filling in all required fields including the microchip number, vaccination record, and FAVN titre test details. The vet must be registered with MGAP; a non-MGAP vet cannot obtain the DGSG endorsement.

        The document is then submitted to DGSG for review and endorsement. Allow three to five business days for the DGSG to process and stamp the certificate.

        The health certificate is valid for 10 days from the date of endorsement. Do not book the endorsement appointment more than 10 days before the cargo departure from MVD.

        Common issues: the microchip number is copied incorrectly onto the certificate, the blood draw date is written ambiguously (use DD/MM/YYYY format consistently), or the vaccination expiry is not recorded. Check these details before the DGSG appointment.

    - heading: "Titre Test Logistics from Uruguay"
      body: |
        Uruguay does not have a domestically based EU-approved laboratory for FAVN testing. Blood samples must be shipped internationally. The common choices are:

        ANSES (Laboratoire de la Rage, Nancy, France): an EU-approved laboratory that accepts international samples. Results typically return in 10-15 business days after sample receipt.

        Animal and Plant Health Agency (APHA, UK): formerly AHVLA, accepts samples from South America. Results in 10-15 business days after receipt.

        Kansas State University Rabies Laboratory (US): accepts international samples. Results in 5-10 business days after receipt.

        Your MGAP-registered vet ships the blood sample in a chilled container to the chosen laboratory. The laboratory issues the result on official letterhead, which you then include with the DGSG health certificate submission.

        The 90-day wait begins from the blood draw date, not the result date. If the sample takes 21 days to process and be returned, you have already consumed 21 of those 90 days waiting for the result. This is expected and normal.

    - heading: "What Poland Looks Like for Your Pet After Arrival"
      body: |
        Once GIW clears your pet at WAW, it is free to travel and live in Poland without further border checks. Registration with a local vet is the first priority.

        Poland requires dogs to be microchipped (already done) and vaccinated against rabies annually. Some municipalities have dog registration requirements. Check with your local urban authority (urząd miejski) after arrival.

        If you subsequently want to travel within the EU with your pet, a Polish EU pet passport can be issued by your Warsaw vet. This contains the vaccination records and chip number and simplifies travel between EU member states.

  - question: "What happens if the blood draw result comes back as 0.4 IU/ml, just below the required 0.5 IU/ml?"
    answer: "A result below 0.5 IU/ml means the test has failed. You must revaccinate your pet with an approved vaccine, wait at least 30 days, draw blood again, and send to the EU-approved laboratory. If the new result is 0.5 IU/ml or above, a new 90-day wait begins from the new blood draw date. This adds 4-5 months to your total timeline. To reduce the risk of failure, ensure the vaccination is given at the correct dose with an EU-recognised product and that the blood draw is done at or after the 30-day mark."
  - question: "Does our cat need the same titre test as our dog for the Uruguay to Poland route?"
    answer: "Yes. The titre test requirement for unlisted countries applies to all species covered by the EU non-commercial pet movement rules: dogs, cats, and domestic ferrets. There is no species exemption from the titre test for Uruguay-origin animals. Both your dog and your cat must each complete the full microchip, vaccination, FAVN titre test, and 90-day wait sequence."
