content:
  h1: "Pet Transport from Lebanon to Hungary"
  overview: "Twenty weeks. That's the realistic floor for getting a dog or cat from Beirut to Budapest, and flight time has almost nothing to do with it. Lebanon sits outside the EU's list of approved countries, so Hungary requires the full sequence: microchip, rabies vaccination, an FAVN titre test, and a 90-day wait measured from the blood draw. Cats go through the same process as dogs. Add Lebanese export endorsement time and the practical window runs 20 to 26 weeks."

  sections:
    - heading: "The unlisted-country sequence, in order"
      body: |
        Hungary treats Lebanon as an EU unlisted third country under Commission Delegated Regulation 2020/692, which means the shortcut available to EU-to-EU pet moves doesn't apply here. First, the microchip. It has to be an ISO 11784/11785 chip, and it has to go in before the rabies vaccination, not after. Get this order wrong and Hungarian border vets can treat the vaccination as invalid for the sequence, which forces a restart from scratch.

        Once the microchip is confirmed and the vaccination given, wait at least 30 days, then have blood drawn for an FAVN or ELISA titre test at an EU-approved laboratory. The result needs to hit at least 0.5 IU/ml. Assuming it passes, a 90-day clock starts. Not from the result date. From the draw date. Only once those 90 days have run can the pet legally enter Hungary.

        Layer on top of that an EU-format health certificate, issued within 10 days of travel and endorsed by the Lebanese Ministry of Agriculture. Lebanese government processing isn't always quick, so build 2 to 4 weeks into your plan just for that endorsement. Total realistic timeline: 20 to 26 weeks from a standing start.

    - heading: "Getting from Beirut to Budapest"
      body: |
        Turkish Airlines Cargo is usually the strongest option out of Beirut, routing through Istanbul with onward connections that reach Budapest reliably. Lufthansa Cargo via Frankfurt is a solid alternative, particularly given Lufthansa's dedicated Animal Lounge facility at FRA, which many owners prefer for a calmer mid-journey stop.

        Qatar Airways Cargo via Doha and Emirates SkyCargo via Dubai both work too, though both add transit time compared with the European hub routings, and Doha in particular can face summer heat embargoes on live cargo. Whichever airline you choose, book the cargo space only once the 90-day wait is genuinely close to finishing. Booking too early risks a mismatch between your flight date and your legal entry date.

    - heading: "Lebanese paperwork: don't underestimate the endorsement step"
      body: |
        The Lebanese Ministry of Agriculture has to issue an export permit and endorse the health certificate before the pet can leave. Neither step happens overnight. Two to four weeks is a realistic window for the endorsement alone, and that's assuming nothing needs correcting. Start this process while the 90-day titre test wait is still running, not after it, so the two timelines overlap rather than stack.
