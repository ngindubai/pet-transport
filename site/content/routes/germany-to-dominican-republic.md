  h1: "Pet Transport Germany to Dominican Republic: 2026 Guide"
  overview: "Germany to the Dominican Republic is one of the more straightforward outbound routes from Europe. No import permit is needed for personal companion animals (up to 5 pets), and there is no quarantine for compliant arrivals. The main requirements are current core vaccinations (distemper, parvovirus, hepatitis, leptospirosis) alongside rabies, a Veterinäramt-endorsed health certificate issued within 15 days, and a DGAG inspection on arrival at Las Americas Airport in Santo Domingo.   sections:
    - heading: "Dominican Republic requirements for pets from Germany"
      body: |
        DGAG (Direccion General de Ganaderia), within the Ministerio de Agricultura (agricultura.gob.do), governs pet imports. For pets arriving from Germany, the requirements are: ISO 11784/11785 microchip; rabies vaccination administered between 30 days and 12 months before arrival; current core vaccinations (distemper, parvovirus, hepatitis, leptospirosis); and an official health certificate endorsed by the Veterinäramt, issued within 15 days of travel.

        No import permit is required for up to 5 personal companion animals. No quarantine for compliant arrivals. DGAG inspects pets on arrival at Las Americas International Airport (SDQ). The inspection checks the health certificate, microchip, and vaccination records.

    - heading: "Vaccination requirements: rabies and core vaccines"
      body: |
        German pets travelling to the Dominican Republic must have an up-to-date EU pet passport or equivalent vaccination record. The required vaccinations are: rabies (administered between 30 days and 12 months before arrival in the Dominican Republic); distemper; parvovirus; hepatitis; and leptospirosis. All must be recorded in the health certificate with batch numbers, dates, and the administering veterinarian's details.

        German pets with current EU pet passports will typically have all core vaccinations recorded, but the health certificate must reference them explicitly in the format required by DGAG. Discuss the certificate format with your Amtstierarzt before the appointment.

    - heading: "Routing from Germany to Santo Domingo"
      body: |
        Frankfurt (FRA) is Germany's main international cargo hub for Caribbean routes. The most common routing is FRA to Madrid (MAD) via Iberia or Lufthansa, then MAD to Santo Domingo (SDQ) via Iberia. Iberia operates the MAD-SDQ transatlantic service and handles cargo including live animals.

        From Paris CDG, Air France operates CDG-SDQ Caribbean routes. From FRA, Lufthansa connects to CDG for the Air France service. A routing via Miami (MIA) via Lufthansa Cargo, then an American Airlines connection to SDQ, is also possible for some schedules.

    - heading: "German export documentation"
      body: |
        Germany's Amtstierarzt (official veterinarian) prepares the export health certificate in the format required by the Dominican Republic (in English or Spanish). The local Veterinäramt endorses it after checking all vaccination records, microchip, and the vet's attestations.

        Allow three to five business days for the Veterinäramt endorsement. The certificate must be issued within 15 days of travel. Keep the timing as short as possible from issuance to departure to avoid any last-minute complications.

    answer: "Up to 5 personal companion animals may enter without an import permit. For more than 5 animals, or for any commercial movement, contact DGAG (agricultura.gob.do) before travel to determine what additional permits or documentation are required."
  - question: "Does the Dominican Republic require tapeworm treatment for dogs from Germany?"
    answer: "DGAG does not mandate a specific tapeworm or Echinococcus treatment for dogs arriving from Germany. The core vaccination and health certificate requirements are the primary focus. However, confirm with DGAG at the time of travel as requirements can change."
  - question: "Can I use my pet's EU pet passport as the health certificate for entry into the Dominican Republic?"
    answer: "No. The EU pet passport is a vaccination record and a document for travel within the EU/EEA, not an official export health certificate for third countries. For the Dominican Republic, you need an official health certificate prepared by an Amtstierarzt and endorsed by the Veterinäramt."
  - question: "What does the DGAG inspection at SDQ involve?"
    answer: "DGAG inspectors at Las Americas Airport check the endorsed health certificate, scan the microchip to verify the number matches the certificate, and review the vaccination records. For compliant pets, the inspection is routine. The cargo agent at SDQ will guide you through the collection process after DGAG clearance."
