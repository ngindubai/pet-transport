  sections:
    - heading: "Dominican Republic import requirements: the checklist"
      body: |
        The DGAG (Direccion General de Ganaderia) under the Ministerio de Agricultura regulates all pet imports into the Dominican Republic. Your pet is inspected by DGAG veterinary officers at Las Americas International Airport (SDQ) in Santo Domingo on arrival.

        The requirements are: a microchip conforming to ISO 11784/11785, a valid rabies vaccination given between 30 days and 12 months before entry, a health certificate issued within 15 days of travel, and current DHPP vaccination for dogs (distemper, adenovirus, parainfluenza, parvovirus). Cats need rhinotracheitis, calicivirus, and panleukopenia vaccinations on record. All pets need internal and external antiparasitic treatment within 10 days of departure, documented by a vet.

        No import permit is required for personal companion animals (up to five animals with their owner). There is no quarantine for compliant arrivals. The inspection at SDQ is typically quick provided all documentation is in order.

    - heading: "Germany's export documentation: the Amtstierarzt process"
      body: |
        Germany's export documentation for non-EU destinations uses the AHC (Animal Health Certificate) issued and endorsed by the official vet at the local Veterinaramt (Amtstierarzt). Which authority endorses the AHC depends on your Bundesland: LAVES handles Lower Saxony, CVUA-MEL covers North Rhine-Westphalia, LAVET oversees Bavaria, and equivalent bodies operate in all other states.

        Contact your local Veterinaramt to book the appointment. The Amtstierarzt carries out a clinical examination, completes the AHC, and the certificate is then endorsed by the state competent authority. Processing time for the endorsement varies by Bundesland, so confirm with your local office how many days to allow between the vet examination and receiving the endorsed certificate.

        The Dominican Republic accepts the AHC up to 15 days from issue, giving you slightly more scheduling room than most EU destinations. Still, issue the certificate as close to departure as your cargo booking allows.
