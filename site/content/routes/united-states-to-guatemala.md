  h1: "Pet Transport from the United States to Guatemala"
  overview: |
    No titre test and no fixed quarantine apply to a pet moving from the US to Guatemala, on MAGA's published general guidance. What does apply is a specific bilateral document, a two-page certificate written for exactly this US-Guatemala route, sitting alongside the standard USDA-endorsed health certificate most international pet moves need anyway.

    Three airlines fly the US to Guatemala City non-stop with a working pet cargo option in practice, not four. American serves Miami and Dallas-Fort Worth, Avianca serves Los Angeles, New York JFK and Washington Dulles. Delta and United fly the route too, from Atlanta and Houston, but both have restricted pet cargo to military and Foreign Service moves since the pandemic, so a passenger direct flight on either carrier does not mean a pet booking is available.

    On arrival, SEPA/OIRSA inspects the paperwork and the animal at La Aurora International Airport, charging a modest weight-based fee, roughly USD 14. Land during the working day if at all possible; arriving after hours can mean an overnight wait even when every document is in order.

  sections:
    - heading: "The bilateral certificate that makes this route different"
      body: |
        Most countries just want a generic health certificate. Guatemala has gone further for pets arriving specifically from the United States, publishing a named two-page document, Certificado de Salud para la Exportacion de Perros, Gatos de los Estados Unidos a Guatemala, available directly from MAGA/VISAR's own site.

        It runs alongside, not instead of, the standard USDA-endorsed International Health Certificate that a USDA-accredited vet completes and APHIS Veterinary Services signs off, either quickly through the digital VEHCS system or more slowly by paper mail-in. Bring both, in Spanish translation if the vet issued them in English, since MAGA's guidance is explicit that foreign-language paperwork needs translating before the SEPA/OIRSA inspector will accept it.

        Where the picture gets murkier is the import permit. USDA APHIS's own country page for Guatemala notes that MAGA can require a valid permit before the animal even leaves the US, while MAGA's published general guidance for pet owners does not spell out a separate permit process beyond the port-of-entry inspection. That gap is worth closing with a phone call to MAGA (2413-7000) or a Guatemalan agent before flights are booked, not after.

    - heading: "Three real airline options, not four"
      body: |
        A flight search will show four carriers flying the US to Guatemala City non-stop. Only two of them will actually book a pet.

        American Airlines Cargo runs PetEmbark from Miami and Dallas-Fort Worth, though flying to a non-US destination means arranging a customs broker as a separate step from the cargo booking itself. Avianca connects from Los Angeles, JFK and Dulles, offering both cabin travel for small pets and cargo for larger ones, with brachycephalic breeds excluded from the hold either way.

        Delta, from Atlanta, and United, from Houston, both fly the route non-stop, but neither has taken general public pet cargo bookings since a pandemic-era suspension that has stuck: current policy on both restricts the service to active military permanent-change-of-station orders and State Department Foreign Service transfers. If a Delta or United flight looks like the obvious choice on price or timing, confirm pet cargo eligibility with the airline directly before assuming it applies to a normal house move.
