content:
  h1: "Pet Transport from the United States to Guatemala"
  overview: |
    A phone call to MAGA is the step most owners moving from the US to Guatemala don't expect. Guatemala's Ministry of Agriculture documents a specific bilateral protocol for US-origin pets, and part of that protocol is a requirement to secure an import permit before the animal ever leaves the United States, not on arrival.

    Once that's in motion, the rest of the process is familiar territory: a USDA-accredited vet issues the International Health Certificate, endorsed through APHIS eFile for around USD 38, and OIRSA inspects the animal on landing for a fee that scales with weight, typically around USD 14. No titre test and no quarantine feature in MAGA's published general guidance for a compliant pet.

  sections:
    - heading: "The MAGA import permit: the real difference on this route"
      body: |
        Compare this route with a UK-to-Guatemala move and one thing stands out. MAGA's own published material goes into far more procedural detail for US-origin pets than it does for pets arriving from most other countries, right down to naming the specific core vaccines expected.

        That detail comes with an obligation: a valid import permit, obtained ahead of embarkation, arranged by contacting MAGA's Ministerio de Agricultura, Ganaderia y Alimentacion directly. There's no online portal published for this, just a phone number and office hours. Call early. Six to eight weeks ahead gives enough room if MAGA needs supporting documents or a follow-up call.

    - heading: "What the bilateral protocol actually asks for"
      body: |
        Beyond rabies, the US-Guatemala arrangement names distemper, hepatitis, leptospirosis and parvovirus for dogs, and panleukopenia, rhinotracheitis, calicivirus and leukaemia for cats, plus a parasite treatment recorded on the certificate. A vet who only checks the rabies box on a standard export form is not covering what Guatemala's own guidance describes for this specific route.

        None of this changes the OIRSA fee at the other end. That inspection is origin-agnostic: whether a pet arrives from Miami, London or Bogota, OIRSA weighs it, inspects the paperwork, and charges the same roughly USD 14, scaled to the animal's size.
