  h1: "Pet Transport from Namibia to the United Arab Emirates"
  overview: |
    Ethiopian Airlines is currently the only carrier offering a single-ticket route out of Windhoek that ends in the UAE, connecting through its Addis Ababa hub to Dubai. The alternative is Airlink to Johannesburg, then Emirates, which restored a fourth daily Dubai service from Johannesburg on 1 July 2026.

    Namibia sits outside MOCCAE's low-risk country list, so a rabies titre test applies before the import permit can be requested, though the UAE doesn't add the UK's fixed post-test wait. That keeps this route to roughly 12 to 16 weeks, shorter than the equivalent trip to the UK.

  sections:
    - heading: "Titre test, no fixed wait afterward"
      body: |
        MOCCAE sorts every origin country into two groups: named on its low-risk list, or unlisted by default. Namibia falls into the second group, so a titre test at an accredited laboratory is required, blood drawn after vaccination with a result of at least 0.5 IU/ml. Unlike the UK's 3-month rule, MOCCAE sets no mandatory waiting period once that result comes back; the pet can travel as soon as the test passes and the import permit is confirmed.

        That single difference is most of why this route can move faster than the Namibia-to-UK trip, provided the Pretoria lab turnaround itself doesn't stretch things out.

    - heading: "Two workable routings, one dead end"
      body: |
        Ethiopian Airlines serves Windhoek and Dubai from its own Addis Ababa hub, with multiple daily onward flights to Dubai, which makes it the cleanest single-carrier option. The alternative goes through Johannesburg: Airlink for the regional hop, then Emirates SkyCargo, whose Johannesburg-Dubai schedule expanded to four daily flights in July 2026.

        Qatar Airways also appears in flight searches for this route, connecting Windhoek to Doha via Johannesburg. That itinerary is worth ruling out early: it ends in Qatar, not the UAE, and doesn't form part of a workable booking for this destination.

    - heading: "What Namibia asks for before departure"
      body: |
        There's no separate Namibian export permit to apply for. A state veterinarian, or a private vet endorsed by the Directorate of Veterinary Services, checks the animal and signs a health certificate reformatted to MOCCAE's required layout.

        That certificate confirms the pet is fit to travel under Namibian rules. It doesn't touch the UAE's own titre test or import permit requirements, which run on a separate track and are what actually determine whether the pet clears MOCCAE's checks on arrival.
