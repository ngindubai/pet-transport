  sections:
    - heading: "No titre test for Finland: Jamaica's listed status"
      body: |
        EU Implementing Regulation 2026/636 classifies Jamaica as a listed third country. This means pets from Jamaica entering EU member states including Finland are exempt from the FAVN rabies titre test and the 90-day waiting period.

        Ruokavirasto (the Finnish Food Authority) still requires the standard EU-format non-commercial health certificate. The VSD in Jamaica issues this certificate through a VSD-registered official veterinarian. The certificate must be issued within 10 days of travel. The practical preparation involves confirming microchip, updating vaccinations, obtaining the VSD export permit (4-6 weeks lead time), and completing the certificate appointment close to the departure date.

    - heading: "Tapeworm treatment and the HEL entry requirement"
      body: |
        All dogs entering Finland must receive tapeworm treatment using praziquantel between 1 and 5 days before arrival at Helsinki-Vantaa. The treating vet records the product name, dose, and date of treatment in the health certificate. The treatment window is measured from the time of administration to the actual arrival time in Finland, so plan carefully around your flight schedule. Cats are not subject to this requirement.

        Helsinki-Vantaa Airport (HEL) is the only Finnish airport designated as a Border Control Post for non-EU pet arrivals by air. No other Finnish airport has the veterinary inspection capability for non-EU pets. When booking cargo from Jamaica, ensure the final destination is HEL. Finnair is the main carrier connecting European hubs to Helsinki-Vantaa; KLM offers a good option via Amsterdam.
