content:
  h1: "Pet Transport from the United States to Moldova"
  overview: |
    US owners researching this route often brace for CDC-style paperwork out of habit, the titre test, the quarantine option, the high-risk country list. None of that machinery applies here. It governs pets entering the United States, not leaving it, and Moldova has its own separate rules that turn out to be considerably lighter.

    Those rules follow the EU model closely, since Moldova is an EU accession candidate aligning its animal health legislation with the bloc's own framework. The US sits in Annex II, Part 2 of the EU's rabies-exempt country list, the same list that covers the UK, so a US-vaccinated pet skips the titre test entirely on entry to Moldova.

    What's genuinely needed: microchip, a 30-day wait after rabies vaccination, and a USDA-endorsed health certificate. No direct flight exists, so every route connects through a European hub, most reliably Istanbul, Vienna, Frankfurt or Warsaw.

  sections:
    - heading: "Why this route skips both the CDC and Moldova's own titre test"
      body: |
        It's worth being explicit about what doesn't apply here. The CDC's dog import rules, the ones that dominate so many routes on this site, only govern entry into the United States. A dog or cat leaving the US for Moldova has no equivalent federal export gate to clear beyond the standard USDA health certificate.

        On the Moldova side, the country's animal health rules track the EU framework, and the US appears in Annex II, Part 2 of Implementing Regulation (EU) No 577/2013, the same rabies-exempt list that includes the UK. That's why a US-vaccinated pet doesn't need the titre test that would apply from an unlisted country. Confirm the current position with ANSA close to travel, since the underlying EU list is reviewed periodically.

    - heading: "Four workable routings, no direct option"
      body: |
        Turkish Airlines through Istanbul offers a flat USD 150 cargo rate regardless of crate weight tier, useful for a larger dog where per-kilo pricing elsewhere adds up quickly. Austrian Airlines through Vienna keeps the pet on one carrier's own network from a US gateway through to Chisinau. Lufthansa through Frankfurt or Munich has the broadest US network of the four but bans all brachycephalic breeds from the cargo hold outright, cabin travel only for those breeds. LOT Polish Airlines through Warsaw serves New York, Chicago and Newark directly, though live animal cargo has to go through an approved shipping agent rather than the standard booking flow.

    - heading: "What changes if this pet ever returns to the US"
      body: |
        The ease of this outbound leg is worth separating clearly from any future return trip. Moldova is on the CDC's high-risk list for dog rabies, so a dog coming back to the US needs a valid titre test or 28-day quarantine at a CDC-registered facility, a process with fixed timing windows that can't be compressed. Cats face no such requirement in either direction. If a return date is even loosely on the horizon, it's worth starting that paperwork early rather than assuming this trip's simplicity carries through both ways.
