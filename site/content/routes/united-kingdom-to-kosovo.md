  h1: "Pet Transport from the United Kingdom to Kosovo"
  overview: |
    Kosovo doesn't publish a country-by-country rabies risk list the way its neighbour Bosnia and Herzegovina does. Current guidance from pet travel sources is that no titre test applies from any origin country, the UK included, though that rests on published practice rather than a codified regulation, so it's worth a direct check with Kosovo's Food and Veterinary Agency before committing to a travel date.

    What is clearly documented is the Echinococcus treatment condition: a tapeworm treatment recorded on the certificate, distinct from the rule that applies to Kosovo-origin pets entering the UK in the opposite direction.

    Getting out of London by the obvious route doesn't work for a pet. Wizz Air's direct, seasonal Luton to Pristina service carries no animals at all.
  sections:
    - heading: "No published country list, but no titre test either"
      body: |
        Bosnia and Herzegovina's Veterinary Office works from a named, dated Annex IV list splitting the world into exempt and non-exempt groups. Kosovo's Food and Veterinary Agency hasn't published an equivalent instrument. Current pet travel guidance is consistent on one point: no rabies titre test is required to enter Kosovo from any country, UK included.

        That consistency is reassuring but not the same as a legal guarantee written into a gazetted regulation. Given how much weight this fact carries for planning the trip, confirming it directly with FVA, or through a vet who has recently handled a Kosovo entry, is worth the effort rather than relying solely on secondary sources.

    - heading: "Echinococcus treatment: a real, separate condition"
      body: |
        Kosovo's entry conditions specifically list treatment against Echinococcus (tapeworm) as a documented requirement for dogs and cats, recorded on the veterinary certificate. This is a Kosovo-side condition on the way in, not to be confused with the UK's own tapeworm rule that applies to Kosovo-origin pets travelling to Great Britain in the reverse direction.

        Since the timing and recording requirements sit with the certifying vet rather than a fixed public window, build this into the same appointment as the pre-export health check rather than treating it as an afterthought.

    - heading: "Wizz Air won't take your pet out of London"
      body: |
        London to Pristina has one direct option: Wizz Air's seasonal service into Luton, running April to October. It carries no pets whatsoever beyond a certified assistance dog booked well in advance.

        Turkish Airlines via Istanbul and Austrian Airlines via Vienna both offer workable connecting routes that do carry pets, either in the cabin for small animals or in the hold for larger dogs. Swiss International Air Lines via Zurich is an option only for a pet travelling in the cabin with its owner; its cargo division's unaccompanied pet service doesn't currently reach Kosovo.
