  h1: "Pet Transport Azerbaijan to Finland: 2026 Guide"
  overview: "Finland's main pet import restriction for non-EU origin animals is geographic: pets must enter through Helsinki Vantaa Airport (HEL) or the Vaalimaa land border. Combined with the full EU unlisted country process that applies to Azerbaijan, planning this move requires at least 5-6 months. The steps are: microchip, vaccination, FAVN titre test at an EU-approved laboratory, 90-day wait, and SAQV-endorsed health certificate. Cargo from Baku routes through Istanbul or Frankfurt into Helsinki. Ruokavirasto is the Finnish authority that verifies compliance on arrival."

  sections:
    - heading: "The restricted entry point rule and why it matters for Baku cargo"
      body: |
        Finland's Ruokavirasto restricts the entry of pets from non-EU countries to two border inspection posts: Helsinki Vantaa Airport and the Vaalimaa land crossing on the Russian border. This is not a rule unique to Azerbaijan; it applies to all non-EU origin pets.

        For cargo from Baku, the implication is clear: your routing must terminate at HEL. The most practical way to achieve this from Baku is via Istanbul with Turkish Airlines Cargo, which operates services to HEL and accepts live animal cargo at IST. Vienna (Austrian Airlines) and Frankfurt (Lufthansa) are alternative hub options with services into Helsinki.

        When confirming the cargo booking, ask the cargo agent explicitly: "Will the pet clear Finnish border inspection at Helsinki Vantaa?" and get the answer in writing. Cargo itineraries can be complex and the final entry designation must be HEL, not a domestic Finnish leg after entry via another point.

    - heading: "The SAQV endorsement process in Baku"
      body: |
        Azerbaijan's State Veterinary Service (SAQV) is the competent authority for signing off on the official health certificate needed for the move to Finland. The process involves two steps: an authorised vet in Azerbaijan issues the certificate, and then SAQV officially endorses it with a stamp and signature.

        The health certificate is only valid for 10 days from issuance, so this paperwork step must happen close to your departure date. Schedule the vet appointment and SAQV endorsement appointment in the final 10 days before travel, ideally 3-7 days before departure to allow a small buffer.

        Experienced vets in Baku who handle international pet moves are familiar with this process. If your regular vet has not issued EU-format health certificates before, ask your transport agent to recommend one who has.

faqs:
  - question: "Can I ship my pet from Baku directly to a Finnish city other than Helsinki?"
    answer: "No. For non-EU origin pets, Finnish law restricts entry to Helsinki Vantaa Airport or the Vaalimaa land crossing. Secondary Finnish airports do not have approved border inspection posts for live animal arrivals from third countries. All cargo must go to HEL for clearance, even if your final destination is Tampere, Turku, or another Finnish city."
  - question: "What is the Ruokavirasto country tool and should I use it?"
    answer: "Ruokavirasto's website (ruokavirasto.fi/en) includes an interactive tool that lets you select your country of origin and pet type to generate the specific import requirements. For Azerbaijan, this will confirm the FAVN titre test requirement, the 90-day wait, and the health certificate format. It is a reliable, official source and worth checking before you begin your preparation in case any requirements have been updated since your research was done."
  - question: "My FAVN result was 0.5 IU/ml exactly. Is this accepted in Finland?"
    answer: "Yes. The EU Regulation 2020/692 threshold is a minimum of 0.5 IU/ml, and a result of exactly 0.5 IU/ml is compliant. The certificate from the approved laboratory will state this number. Ruokavirasto border vets accept results at or above the threshold. A result below 0.5 IU/ml means the test failed and the process must be restarted with re-vaccination followed by a new blood draw after at least 30 days."
