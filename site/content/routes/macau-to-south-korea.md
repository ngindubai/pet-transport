content:
  h1: "Pet Transport from Macau to South Korea"
  overview: |
    Three airlines fly Macau to Incheon nonstop. Only two of them will carry a pet, and neither will put a large dog in the hold on that specific flight.

    Korean Air and Jeju Air both operate the roughly three-hour-forty-minute MFM-ICN sector, and both accept small pets in the cabin. Air Macau flies the same route and carries none at all. For a dog too big to travel as hand luggage, Korean Air's own restriction on cargo-hold pets aboard Boeing 737 and Airbus A321 aircraft rules out its direct flight too, since that's the exact jet on this route. The workaround, a transfer through Hong Kong, is the same one this site documents on nearly every other Macau corridor. On the regulatory side, this route is comparatively forgiving: South Korea's APQA doesn't add a waiting period once a titre test clears, so once the paperwork is sorted, timing isn't the obstacle it is on many of Macau's other destinations.

  sections:
    - heading: "A direct flight, but only for a carrier under the seat"
      body: |
        This is one of the few Macau routes on this site where a genuine nonstop passenger option exists for a small pet rather than none at all. Korean Air and Jeju Air both fly it several times a week, and both will take a cat or a small dog in the cabin under their usual carrier and weight rules.

        Anything bigger runs into a real gap. Korean Air restricts cargo-hold pet transport on Boeing 737 and A321-family aircraft for international flights, and its Macau service uses an A321neo. That's not a documentation issue or a seasonal embargo; it's the aircraft itself. A large dog goes via Hong Kong instead, crossing the bridge or ferry in about an hour, then flying a widebody Cathay Pacific Cargo sector into Incheon.

    - heading: "The titre test question APQA hasn't answered by name"
      body: |
        APQA keeps a published roster of rabies-free countries and regions that get to skip the titre test. Hong Kong is on it. Japan, Australia and the UK are on it too, alongside roughly fifty other places. Macau isn't named in any version of that list found for this guide.

        That's not the same as saying Macau definitely needs the test; Macau has recorded no human or animal rabies case in over four decades, a record as clean as many places that do get the exemption. But without Macau appearing by name, the honest planning assumption is that the titre test applies: blood drawn at least 30 days after vaccination, a result of 0.5 IU/mL or higher, valid for 24 months. Confirm current status with APQA directly, since a named addition to the list would shorten this considerably.

    - heading: "No quarantine, and no post-test wait either"
      body: |
        Once the paperwork is in order, South Korea treats entry as an inspection, not a holding period. A pet whose microchip, vaccination and any required titre result all match the certificate can clear Incheon and go home with its owner the same day. Extended quarantine, averaging about 10 days, is reserved for pets arriving with missing or mismatched documents, not a routine step every animal goes through.

        Just as usefully, APQA doesn't impose the kind of post-test wait that turns other destinations into months-long projects. There's no 90-day EU-style clock and no 180-day wait like Japan's. Once a titre result comes back passing, the pet can travel.
