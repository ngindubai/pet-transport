  sections:
    - heading: "Austria Entry Requirements for Pets"
      body: |
        Austria follows EU Regulation 576/2013 for non-commercial pet movements. Pets arriving from Estonia need a valid EU pet passport showing an ISO 11784/11785 microchip implanted before or on the same day as the first rabies vaccination, and a current rabies vaccination at least 21 days old after the primary dose. No titre test is required; Estonia is an EU member state and a listed country for Austria. No quarantine applies and no import permit is needed. The BMSGPK (Federal Ministry of Social Affairs, Health, Care and Consumer Protection) oversees animal imports; their guidance is at sozialministerium.at. Austria applies breed-specific legislation at the Bundesland (state) level rather than federally, so the rules for your destination state are worth checking early.

    - heading: "Airlines and Cargo from Tallinn to Vienna"
      body: |
        Tallinn Airport (TLL) does not have a direct service to Vienna (VIE), but connections are available via several hubs. Austrian Airlines is the most natural option given that Vienna is its home hub: the airline accepts pets as cargo and as in-cabin passengers for small pets where aircraft type allows. Vienna Airport has well-organised live animal cargo facilities, making arrival and collection straightforward. Lufthansa via Frankfurt (FRA) is a second strong option: Lufthansa Cargo at FRA has extensive live animal experience and the FRA-VIE leg is short with reliable connections. LOT Polish Airlines via Warsaw (WAW) is a further alternative. Book cargo 6 to 10 weeks in advance to secure your preferred routing.

faqs:
  - question: "Does my pet need a titre test to enter Austria from Estonia?"
    answer: "No. A rabies antibody titre test is only required when entering Austria from a country not on the EU listed countries register. Estonia is an EU member state, so the EU pet passport with a current rabies vaccination is sufficient. The BMSGPK confirms this at sozialministerium.at."
  - question: "What are Austria's breed restrictions for dogs?"
    answer: "Austria's breed restrictions are set at the Bundesland (state) level, not by the federal government. This means the rules differ depending on which Austrian state you are moving to. Some states require muzzles, leads, or competency tests for specific breeds; others ban certain breeds entirely. Check with the authorities in your specific destination Bundesland before booking your pet's travel. The BMSGPK website (sozialministerium.at) can point you to the right regional authority."
  - question: "Which airline is best for shipping a large dog from Tallinn to Vienna?"
    answer: "Austrian Airlines is the most direct option, as Vienna is its hub and it accepts large dogs as cargo on applicable routes. Lufthansa via Frankfurt is a well-established alternative with strong live animal cargo facilities at FRA. For either option, contact the cargo division directly or use a licensed pet transport agent to handle the booking, crate sizing, and documentation."
  - question: "How long does pet transport from Estonia to Austria take to arrange?"
    answer: "Allow 6 to 10 weeks from start to finish if your pet's vaccinations and microchip are already in order. If a rabies vaccination update is needed, add at least 21 days from the vaccination date before travel is permitted. The practical steps are: confirm the passport, book the flight, purchase and acclimatise to the crate, final vet visit within 10 days of departure, and travel."
