  overview: "Estonia and Austria are both EU member states, which keeps this route straightforward. Your pet needs a microchip, a valid rabies vaccination, and either an EU pet passport or an Animal Health Certificate. There is no quarantine, no titre test, and no import permit required."

  sections:
    - heading: "Documentation: EU pet passport or AHC"
      body: |
        The EU pet passport is the preferred document for this route. Any authorised Estonian vet can issue one, and once it is complete with your pet's microchip number and vaccination records, it is valid for the lifetime of the animal. You will not need to renew it unless your pet's rabies boosters lapse.

        The alternative is an Animal Health Certificate (AHC). This is a single-use document endorsed by an official vet, and it must be issued within 10 days of your arrival in Austria. If you are planning a one-way move, the AHC works well. If you expect to travel between EU countries regularly, an EU pet passport is the better long-term option.

        Austria requires the microchip to have been implanted before or on the same day as the first rabies vaccination. If you have an older pet whose microchip was placed after their first vaccine, contact an official vet to discuss how best to record this in the documentation.

    - heading: "Flying from Tallinn to Austria"
      body: |
        Tallinn Airport (TLL) has regular connections to Vienna (VIE) via airBaltic (routing through Riga) and via Lufthansa (routing through Frankfurt). Austrian Airlines also operates services into Vienna. The flight time from Riga or Frankfurt to Vienna is under 2 hours.

        Small pets (generally up to 8kg including carrier) can travel in the cabin on airBaltic and Lufthansa services. Larger dogs must travel as checked baggage or via the cargo hold, which is temperature-controlled and pressurised. Brachycephalic (flat-faced) breeds face restrictions on cargo travel with most airlines; check directly with your chosen carrier if this applies to your pet.

        Book your pet's place as soon as you have confirmed your flight. Most airlines limit the number of pets per flight, and popular summer routes fill quickly.

    - heading: "Austrian state breed restrictions"
      body: |
        Austria does not operate a federal breed ban, but the picture is more complicated at state level. Each of the nine Bundeslaender (federal states) sets its own rules. Vienna, for example, has a list of breeds requiring a Sachkundenachweis (competency certificate) and a behavioural assessment. Other states have different requirements or none at all.

        If you are relocating a breed such as a Rottweiler, American Staffordshire Terrier, Dobermann, or similar dog, check the specific rules for your destination state before you book anything. The relevant authority is the BMSGPK (sozialministerium.at), and each state government publishes its own guidance.

        For most dogs and all cats, this is a non-issue. The core requirements are microchip, vaccination, and passport or AHC.

faqs:
  - question: "Do I need a titre test to move my dog from Estonia to Austria?"
    answer: "No. A titre test is only required when travelling from outside the EU to an EU country (or to certain non-EU countries like the UK or Australia). Estonia and Austria are both EU member states, so a titre test is not needed."
  - question: "Can my cat travel in the cabin from Tallinn to Vienna?"
    answer: "Yes, on most routes operated by airBaltic and Lufthansa. Cats must fit in an approved carrier under the seat in front of you. The combined weight limit is usually 8kg including the carrier. Book the cabin space when you purchase your ticket."
  - question: "How long before travel does my pet's rabies vaccination need to be done?"
    answer: "For a first-time vaccination, there is a mandatory 21-day wait before your pet can enter Austria. If your pet already has a valid rabies booster given within the required timeframe, the wait does not apply again."
  - question: "What happens if my pet's documentation is not in order at the Austrian border?"
    answer: "Austrian border veterinary officers check pet documents on arrival. If paperwork is missing or non-compliant, your pet could face a penalty stay in authorised premises or be returned to Estonia. Getting the documentation right before you travel is much simpler than resolving a problem on arrival."
