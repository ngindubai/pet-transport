  h1: "Pet Transport from Kosovo to the United Arab Emirates"
  overview: |
    MOCCAE keeps a list of around 50 countries it treats as low-risk for rabies, and a pet from any of them skips the titre test entirely. Kosovo isn't on it. So a rabies antibody titre test applies here, the same test this site's Kosovo-to-UK page describes in more punishing detail.

    The good news sits in the timing. Once the blood result clears at 0.5 IU/ml or above, the UAE imposes no further mandatory wait, unlike the UK's fixed 90-day rule counted from the blood draw itself. Most of what slows this route down is laboratory turnaround and flight connections through Istanbul or Vienna, not a regulatory clock.
  sections:
    - heading: "The titre test, and the wait that doesn't apply"
      body: |
        Because Kosovo sits outside MOCCAE's low-risk list, blood has to be drawn at least 21 days after vaccination and tested at an accredited laboratory, with a passing result of 0.5 IU/ml or higher. That part isn't optional.

        What comes next is where the UAE differs sharply from the UK route this site already documents for Kosovo. There, a passing titre triggers a fixed 90-day wait from the date of the blood draw. Here, MOCCAE checks only that the result itself is valid, current within 12 months, and lets the pet travel as soon as the import permit clears. Two very different regulatory philosophies applied to the same blood test.
    - heading: "Getting out of Pristina without Wizz Air"

        Wizz Air dominates Pristina's cheap fares and carries no pets at all, a fact that applies to every route this site has built out of the city, this one included. Austrian Airlines via Vienna is the other realistic option if Turkish's schedule doesn't fit.
