content:
  h1: "Pet Transport from Macau to Australia"
  overview: |
    Macau sits in DAFF's Group 3, the same tier as Hong Kong, the UK and the US, for cat and dog imports. That means the standard, well-established pathway applies rather than the far longer non-approved-country route some of this site's other Southeast Asian corridors carry: a titre test, a 180-day wait from the blood draw, a DAFF import permit, and mandatory 10-day quarantine at Mickleham in Melbourne.

    The complication specific to Macau is getting there at all. Macau International Airport carries no long-haul cargo, so every pet transfers overland to Hong Kong first.

  sections:
    - heading: "DAFF's Group 3 pathway, applied to Macau"
      body: |
        Australia's Department of Agriculture, Fisheries and Forestry runs cats and dogs into three risk-based groups. Macau sits in Group 3, requiring an ISO microchip before vaccination, a completed rabies vaccination course, a titre test result of at least 0.5 IU/ml at a DAFF-approved laboratory, a 180-day post-titre wait from the blood draw date, a DAFF import permit obtained before travel, and a minimum 10-day quarantine at Mickleham on arrival.

        This classification was confirmed through convergent web sourcing rather than a directly loaded DAFF page, since agriculture.gov.au and its biosecurity subdomain returned persistent access issues across multiple research attempts. Given the significance of getting a Group classification right, confirm Macau's current status directly with DAFF before locking in travel plans.

    - heading: "The Hong Kong transfer before the long-haul sector"
      body: |
        Macau International Airport has no transpacific cargo route, so the practical path is a road or ferry transfer across the Hong Kong-Zhuhai-Macau Bridge into Hong Kong International Airport, then a direct Cathay Pacific Cargo or Qantas Freight sector to Melbourne. All pets must land at Melbourne, regardless of the final Australian destination, since it's the only airport equipped for quarantine intake.

        Given the 180-day wait already dominates the schedule, the Hong Kong transfer itself rarely changes the overall timeline, but it does add its own booking step and its own crate handling check that shouldn't be left until the final weeks.
