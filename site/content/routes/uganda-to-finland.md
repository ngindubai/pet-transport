  h1: "Pet Transport Uganda to Finland: 2026 Guide"
  overview: "Not every Finnish airport can process a pet arriving from outside the EU. Ruokavirasto, the Finnish Food Authority, restricts non-EU pet entry to a limited set of approved border inspection posts, and Helsinki-Vantaa is one of them.   sections:
    - heading: "Approved entry points: a detail that catches owners off guard"
      body: |
        Ruokavirasto does not permit non-EU pet arrivals at just any Finnish airport. Helsinki-Vantaa (HEL) is an approved border inspection post; smaller regional airports typically are not set up for this. If your routing includes a domestic Finnish connection after the international leg, confirm the customs clearance happens at HEL itself rather than assuming it can happen further down the line.

        Owners coming from EU-listed countries rarely encounter this restriction, since their pets face lighter checks generally. Coming from Uganda, an unlisted country, the full inspection applies, and Ruokavirasto officials verify the MAAIF-endorsed certificate, the titre test result and the vaccination record against the microchip at HEL specifically.

    - heading: "Tapeworm treatment and the titre test sequence"
      body: |
        Dogs need a Praziquantel tapeworm treatment 24 to 120 hours before arrival, recorded by a vet directly in the health certificate. Cats are exempt from this specific step but not from anything else.

        The core sequence otherwise follows the standard pattern: microchip first, rabies vaccination after, a minimum 30-day gap, then blood drawn for a titre test at an approved laboratory. A result of at least 0.5 IU/ml starts a 90-day countdown from the draw date, not the day the result is confirmed. Only once those 90 days have run can the pet legally enter Finland.
