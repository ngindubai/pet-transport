content:
  h1: "Pet Transport from Turkey to Estonia"
  overview: |
    A pet born and vaccinated in Turkey doesn't get treated like an EU pet the moment it lands in Tallinn. The EU classifies Turkey as an unlisted, higher-risk country for rabies, which means the full FAVN titre test and its 90-day wait apply, the same protocol used across every unlisted origin in this build.

    It's a longer process than the short flight itself suggests, and one of the more common surprises for owners relocating out of Turkey into the EU for the first time.

  sections:
    - heading: "Why an EU destination doesn't mean an easy process here"
      body: |
        The direction of travel matters. A pet moving from an EU country like Estonia to Turkey needs the same full titre test protocol covered on this route's reverse page. A pet moving the other way, from Turkey into Estonia, faces exactly the same requirement, because the EU's classification of Turkey as an unlisted, higher-risk country applies regardless of which direction the pet is crossing the border.

        Blood for the FAVN test must be drawn at least 30 days after the rabies vaccination, the result needs to clear 0.5 IU/ml, and then a full 90 days has to pass from that draw date before travel. A booster given after the test resets its own wait.

    - heading: "Turkey's export paperwork for an EU destination"
      body: |
        TRGM, Turkey's agriculture ministry, doesn't require a separate export permit, but the certificate itself has to follow the EU's own model for unlisted third countries, not Turkey's standard domestic-format certificate, and it needs to reference the titre test result directly. Turkish Airlines flies Istanbul to Tallinn direct, and once the 90-day wait clears, the flight itself is straightforward.
