  overview: |
    Annex II, Part 2 of the EU's Implementing Regulation (EU) No 577/2013 lists the countries the EU treats as rabies-exempt for pet travel purposes. Australia is on it, the same list that carries the UK's own titre-test exemption into Moldova. That single classification means an Australian-vaccinated pet skips the blood test and three-month wait that most non-EU origins face entering this particular country.

    It's worth being precise about why that's not just an assumption: Annex II Part 2 is a specific, named list, not a blanket "rabies-free countries get an easy ride" rule, so Australia's presence on it was confirmed directly for this route rather than copied from the UK page. What's left is familiar: a microchip, a 30-day wait after vaccination, and a straightforward veterinary export certificate.

    Getting there now takes two legs, not three. Turkish Airlines flies its own aircraft from Melbourne and, from January 2026, daily from Sydney, into Istanbul, then on to Chisinau on the same carrier.

  sections:
    - heading: "Why Australia skips Moldova's titre test, the same way the UK does"
      body: |
        Moldova's animal health rules closely track the EU's own framework, a natural consequence of its EU accession candidacy since 2022. Under Implementing Regulation (EU) No 577/2013, Australia appears in Annex II, Part 2, the list of third countries treated as equivalent to EU member states for rabies purposes, the same list that includes the UK and the US. Moldova extends that same treatment to Australian-origin pets, meaning the titre test and its associated wait simply doesn't apply here.

        This is worth reconfirming close to travel rather than assuming permanently, since the underlying EU list is reviewed periodically and a change there would carry through to Moldova's own rules.

    - heading: "Booking the flight: Turkish Airlines now flies straight from Australia"
      body: |
        Before Turkish Airlines started flying to Melbourne in 2024 and expanded Sydney to a daily service from January 2026, this route would have meant connecting through a Gulf carrier to reach Istanbul or Vienna first. Now, a pet can travel on Turkish's own network the entire way, Melbourne or Sydney to Istanbul, then on to Chisinau, using the same established AVIH cargo programme cited on the UK and US routes to Moldova.

        Emirates via Dubai and Qatar Airways via Doha remain workable alternatives if Turkish's still-developing Australian schedule doesn't line up with a preferred travel date.

    - heading: "Moving more than one pet"
      body: |
        Moldova caps non-commercial pet movement at five animals per owner, a specific number worth knowing if this move involves several cats, dogs, or a mixed household. Bringing more than five requires switching to the commercial import process, which carries different documentation and inspection requirements entirely.
