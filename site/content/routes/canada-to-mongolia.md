content:
  h1: "Pet Transport from Canada to Mongolia"
  overview: |
    Mongolia asks less of an arriving pet than almost any destination on this site. No titre test. No import permit for a personal animal. A microchip, a rabies vaccination from within the last year, and the standard CFIA-endorsed certificate cover it.

    Compare this with the UK's own version of this route, and one detail stands out. A UK pet returning from Mongolia faces a titre test and a three-month wait, since Mongolia sits unlisted under British rules. A Canadian pet doesn't carry that same sting home: CFIA does not require a rabies antibody test for dogs re-entering Canada, whatever country they're coming from. Genuinely easy both ways, a rarity on this site.

    The flight is where the real planning goes. Ulaanbaatar has no direct link to Canada, so the pet travels via Frankfurt or Istanbul on MIAT Mongolian Airlines' own long-haul network, or via Istanbul on Turkish Airlines.

  sections:
    - heading: "Why this route doesn't punish a return trip"
      body: |
        Most routes on this site that skip the titre test outbound make up for it somewhere. Not here, not for a Canadian pet. Mongolia's own entry rules ask for a microchip, a rabies vaccination inside the last 12 months, and a CFIA-endorsed certificate. Nothing more.

        The genuinely unusual part is what happens on the way back. A UK pet returning from Mongolia gets treated as arriving from an unlisted country, meaning a titre test and a fixed three-month wait. Canada's own import rules for dogs don't include a titre-test requirement tied to country of origin at all, so a Canadian owner on a fixed-term posting isn't staring down the same clock on return that a UK counterpart is.

    - heading: "Frankfurt or Istanbul: no way around a connection"
      body: |
        MIAT Mongolian Airlines flies its own Boeing 787s and 767s between Ulaanbaatar and both Frankfurt and Istanbul, either one reachable nonstop from Toronto on Air Canada or Lufthansa. Turkish Airlines offers a genuine single-carrier alternative through Istanbul instead of handing the pet between two airlines mid-route.

        A Beijing connection sounds tempting given Air Canada's China network, and it's worth being precise here: Air China's dog transport suspension applies specifically to its US-bound flights, not a Canada routing. That said, live-animal cargo capacity from a Canadian gateway through Beijing to Ulaanbaatar isn't well documented anywhere we could check, so Frankfurt or Istanbul remain the more dependable choice until that changes.

        Check MIAT's cargo hold exclusion list before booking if your dog is any kind of flat-faced breed. It runs to 18 breeds, wider than most international carriers', and includes dogs owners don't always think of as brachycephalic, like the Cane Corso.
