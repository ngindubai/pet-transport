  h1: "Pet Transport from Lebanon to Turkey"
  overview: |
    Under two hours in the air. That's the flight time from Beirut to Istanbul, and it makes this one of the shortest routes anywhere in this build, with both Turkish Airlines and Middle East Airlines running it direct, several times a day.

    None of that speed touches the paperwork. Turkey's titre test rule applies the same way it does to a pet flying twelve hours from Uruguay: a blood draw at least 30 days post-vaccination, a 90-day wait, and no shortcuts because the flight itself is short.

  sections:
    - heading: "The shortest flight in this build, two carriers to choose from"
      body: |
        Beirut-Rafic Hariri International (BEY) to Istanbul is served direct by both Turkish Airlines and Middle East Airlines, Lebanon's national carrier, several times daily between them. That frequency means cargo space is rarely the constraint on this route, unlike some of the longer, less-served corridors elsewhere in this build.

        Lebanon's Ministry of Agriculture requires a formal export permit ahead of departure, on top of the health certificate itself. Both are handled through the same ministry, but the permit application should start before you're chasing the certificate's 10-day validity window.

    - heading: "Why a short flight doesn't mean a short timeline"
      body: |
        It's tempting to assume a two-hour flight means a quick process. It doesn't. Turkey applies its titre test requirement uniformly, regardless of how far the pet is travelling. Blood must be drawn at least 30 days after the rabies vaccination, the result needs to clear 0.5 IU/ml, and a full 90 days has to pass from that draw date before the pet can board, even for a route this short.

        Skip the test, and the pet still faces 21 days of quarantine in Turkey at the owner's expense. The flight being short is a genuine convenience once everything else is in order; it isn't a reason to start preparations any later than for a longer route.
