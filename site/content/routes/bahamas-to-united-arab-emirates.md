  h1: "Pet Transport from the Bahamas to the United Arab Emirates"
  overview: |
    Two authorities, two completely different answers, same country. DAFF puts the Bahamas in Group 3 for Australian entry and demands a titre test with a 180-day wait. MOCCAE puts the Bahamas on its low-risk list for UAE entry and asks for nothing more than a current vaccination.

    The flight is the part that takes planning. Nothing connects Nassau to Dubai directly, so this route rides the same nonstop British Airways service to Heathrow that already carries every UK-bound pet out of Nassau, before a second cargo leg covers the final stretch into the Gulf.

  sections:
    - heading: "Why MOCCAE treats this the way DAFF doesn't"
      body: |
        MOCCAE runs its own low-risk list, and the Bahamas is on it, alongside the UK, Germany and roughly 50 other places MOCCAE judges to carry negligible rabies risk. That single classification removes the antibody titre test that would otherwise apply, along with any waiting period tied to it.

        Compare that with the Bahamas' Australia route on this site, where DAFF's Group 3 classification demands the same country run a full RNATT test and sit through a 180-day wait before export. Same origin, same rabies status on paper, two regulators reaching opposite conclusions about what that status requires.

    - heading: "One flight, then a second one"
      body: |
        British Airways' nonstop Nassau to Heathrow service, the backbone of the Bahamas' UK route, does the same job here. From Heathrow, Emirates SkyCargo or Qatar Airways covers the run into Dubai or Doha, both experienced with live-animal cargo at scale.

        The scheduling risk sits with the first leg, not the second. IAG Cargo pulls live-animal bookings automatically outside a 7C to 29C window at any point in the routing, and Nassau's climate makes that a real constraint for much of the year, not a rare edge case.

faqs:
  - question: "Does a pet need a rabies titre test to enter the UAE from the Bahamas?"
    answer: "No. The Bahamas appears on MOCCAE's low-risk rabies country list, so the antibody titre test that applies to most other Caribbean and Central American origins doesn't apply here. A current rabies vaccination, at least 21 days old if it's a first course, is what's needed."
  - question: "Is there a direct flight from Nassau to Dubai?"
    answer: "No. The standard routing uses British Airways' nonstop Nassau to Heathrow service, then a second cargo leg on Emirates or Qatar Airways into Dubai or Abu Dhabi."
  - question: "Why does the same country face such different rules for Australia and the UAE?"
    answer: "DAFF and MOCCAE run separate classification systems. DAFF places the Bahamas in Group 3, its tier for rabies-controlled countries, which requires an RNATT titre test and a 180-day wait. MOCCAE places the Bahamas on its low-risk list instead, which skips the titre test entirely. The two authorities simply weigh the same rabies-free record differently."
  - question: "How long is the MOCCAE import permit valid?"
    answer: "Roughly 30 days from issue. Apply once vaccination timing and flight dates are realistically confirmed, since applying too early risks the permit lapsing before travel."
