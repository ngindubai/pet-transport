  sections:
    - heading: "The tapeworm treatment window"
      body: |
        Dogs entering Great Britain from any country outside the UK must have tapeworm treatment administered by a veterinarian and recorded in the Animal Health Certificate (or equivalent travel document). The treatment must take place no earlier than 120 hours (5 days) and no later than 24 hours before the dog's scheduled arrival time in Great Britain.

        This window is strict. If the treatment falls outside the 24-120 hour range, your dog is non-compliant and may be refused entry or face quarantine. Plan the treatment date carefully based on your flight arrival time. Cats and ferrets are exempt from this requirement entirely.

        The drug used must contain praziquantel or an active ingredient effective against Echinococcus multilocularis. The vet who administers it must record the date, time, product name, and dose in the travel document. This is one of the most common compliance failures on this route, so allow your South Korean vet time to fill out the record correctly.

    - heading: "Which UK entry point to use"
      body: |
        Pets arriving in Great Britain from South Korea must use an approved travel route and entry point. For air travel from Seoul Incheon, London Heathrow is the practical entry point and is on the approved list. Direct Korean Air flights operate on this route. Confirm the current approved route status on GOV.UK before booking, as the list is updated periodically.

        Your pet will travel as cargo on the flight, as Korean Air does not permit pets in the cabin on international routes. British Airways does not allow cabin pets on any route. The cargo handling team at Heathrow is experienced with international pet arrivals.
