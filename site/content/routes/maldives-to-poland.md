  overview: |
    LOT Polish Airlines flies Warsaw to Male directly. That puts Poland alongside Austria and Switzerland as one of only a handful of European countries with a non-stop service to the Maldives, and it's a detail almost no other Maldives route guide mentions for this destination.

    None of that shortens the paperwork. Poland treats the Maldives exactly as it treats any EU-unlisted country: microchip, rabies vaccination, an FAVN or ELISA titre test, and a 90-day wait from the blood draw before the cat can legally enter.

  sections:
    - heading: "A flag-carrier direct route into the Maldives"
      body: |
        Most European countries reach Male through Dubai or Doha. Poland doesn't have to. LOT Polish Airlines operates the Warsaw-Male sector as a scheduled service, not a one-off charter, though the exact weekly frequency has shifted between seasons. Check the current timetable before locking in a travel date, since a route that runs four times a week in peak season can drop to two or three outside it.
    - heading: "Why the lab result takes longer from Male than from Warsaw"
      body: |
        No laboratory on the Maldives runs the FAVN or ELISA titre test. Every blood sample leaves the island before testing can start, adding a courier delay on top of whatever time the lab itself needs once the sample lands. Confirm with your vet exactly where the sample is going and how, before fixing a vaccination date around a house-move deadline.
    - heading: "GIW's sequence, no shortcuts for cats"
      body: |
        The Chief Veterinary Inspectorate applies the same rule to cats as to dogs: microchip before vaccination, a 30-day gap, a titre result of 0.5 IU/ml or above, then 90 days from the draw date. GIW checks all of this on arrival, and a certificate issued outside its 10-day validity window before entry won't be accepted.
