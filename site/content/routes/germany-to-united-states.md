  h1: "Pet Transport from Germany to the United States"
  overview: |
    Germany to the USA is one of the busiest pet transport corridors in the world. Frankfurt (FRA) has excellent cargo connections to most major US cities, and the German veterinary system is well set up for the USDA documentation process. That said, US pet import rules changed significantly in August 2024, with the introduction of the CDC Dog Import Form, and there are a few extra steps that did not apply before.

    For most healthy dogs from Germany, the process takes 4-8 weeks to organise properly and involves a USDA-endorsed health certificate, a CDC registration, and a cargo flight booking. No titre test, no quarantine, no long waiting periods.
  sections:
    - heading: "The CDC Dog Import rules: what changed in 2024"
      body: |
        In August 2024, the US Centers for Disease Control and Prevention introduced a mandatory Dog Import Form (DIF) for all dogs entering the United States, regardless of country of origin. This was a significant change to a process that had previously been handled entirely by USDA APHIS.

        The DIF is completed through the CDC's online portal before travel. It records your dog's microchip number, rabies vaccination details, and confirms the dog has not recently been in a high-risk country. Germany is not on the CDC's high-risk country list, so the process for German-origin dogs is administrative. You fill in the form, print the confirmation, and present it at the US port of entry alongside the USDA-endorsed health certificate.

        Cats are not covered by the CDC dog import rules. Cats travelling from Germany to the USA need only a health certificate issued within 10 days of arrival. No CDC portal registration is required.
    - heading: "Getting the USDA-endorsed health certificate"
      body: |
        The USDA-format health certificate is the core document for a Germany-to-USA pet move. It must be signed by a USDA-accredited veterinarian (or a vet who holds equivalent EU authorisation) and then endorsed by USDA APHIS before the pet boards the plane.

        USDA APHIS has an office in Frankfurt (the USDA Agricultural Attache, US Embassy Berlin, handles endorsements for Germany). The endorsement costs approximately USD 38 per certificate and takes 1-3 business days. Plan this into your timeline: you need the vet to sign it, USDA to endorse it, and both to happen within 10 days of your pet landing in the USA.

        If you are using a pet transport agent, they will often manage the USDA endorsement step. For first-time moves, this is the step most owners appreciate help with.
    - heading: "Choosing your flight and cargo agent"
      body: |
        All cats and dogs on transatlantic Germany-USA routes travel as cargo. There are no cabin pet options on long-haul flights. Frankfurt Airport's cargo hub is one of the busiest in Europe, and carriers including Lufthansa, United, American, and Delta all offer live animal cargo services on FRA-USA routes.

        Lufthansa Cargo is the most established option from Frankfurt. Their Animal Lounge at Frankfurt handles live animals with temperature control and dedicated handlers. For the FRA-JFK corridor specifically, it is worth requesting the direct service rather than a routing via a European hub, which adds transfer handling.

        Cargo pet slots are limited, especially on summer routes when temperature embargoes restrict brachycephalic breed shipments. Book early, particularly for July-August departures.
faqs:
  - question: "Do I need to complete the CDC Dog Import Form before flying from Germany to the USA?"
    answer: "Yes. Since August 2024, all dogs entering the United States require a completed CDC Dog Import Form (DIF), regardless of origin. You register and complete the DIF through the CDC's online portal before travel. Germany is not a high-risk country under CDC definitions, so the process is administrative. Print the confirmation and present it at the US port of entry alongside the USDA-endorsed health certificate."
  - question: "Can my dog travel in the cabin on a flight from Germany to the USA?"
    answer: "No. Transatlantic flights (9+ hours) do not permit cabin pets. All dogs and cats travelling from Germany to the US travel as cargo in a temperature-controlled hold in an IATA-compliant crate. This applies to all carriers on the route."
  - question: "Does my cat need the same documentation as my dog for US entry?"
    answer: "Cats have simpler requirements. They do not need the CDC Dog Import Form. A health certificate issued within 10 days of arrival in the USA is the main requirement. Rabies vaccination is not federally required for cats entering the US, though some states require it. Check the requirements for your specific US destination state."
  - question: "How far in advance should I book cargo space for a Germany to USA pet move?"
    answer: "At least 4-6 weeks before travel, and 8 weeks or more for summer departures. Cargo pet slots fill up, and temperature embargoes during July-August can further limit availability for brachycephalic breeds. The USDA health certificate process and CDC registration should run in parallel with your booking."
  - question: "Which US airports can receive pets arriving as cargo from Germany?"
    answer: "Most major US airports accept live animal cargo imports. JFK, EWR, ORD, LAX, IAD, IAH, ATL, and BOS are commonly used. Some have dedicated animal facilities. Your cargo agent will confirm import handling at your destination airport before booking. Confirm this before finalising your route."
