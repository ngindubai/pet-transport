  sections:
    - heading: "AQD and the Pakistan export health certificate"
      body: |
        The Animal Quarantine Department (AQD), under Pakistan's Ministry of National Food Security and Research, is the authority responsible for issuing and endorsing health certificates for pet exports. The health certificate for US import must be prepared by an AQD-authorised official vet and endorsed by AQD.

        The certificate must include: your pet's microchip number, species and breed, vaccination history with titre test result (laboratory name, blood draw date, result in IU/ml), owner details, and fitness to travel. AQD has offices in Karachi, Lahore, and Islamabad. Contact AQD (aqd.gov.pk) for authorised vets and the current certificate format.

        Allow time for AQD endorsement after the vet completes the certificate. For US travel, the titre test result and other documentation must satisfy CDC requirements in addition to AQD domestic requirements.

    - heading: "Gulf hub routing from Pakistan to the USA"
      body: |
        Pakistan Airlines (PIA) does not operate transatlantic or transpacific routes to the USA. All cargo routes from Pakistan to the USA involve a Gulf or Turkish hub connection. Emirates (via Dubai), Qatar Airways (via Doha), Turkish Airlines (via Istanbul), and Etihad (via Abu Dhabi) all serve Pakistan's major airports and connect to US gateway airports.

        For CDC compliance, confirm that the specific US destination airport has a CDC-registered animal care facility before booking. JFK, LAX, ORD, MIA, ATL, IAD, and SFO all have qualifying facilities. Advise your freight forwarder or cargo agent of the CDC requirements at the time of booking so that all paperwork is coordinated before the dog departs Karachi, Lahore, or Islamabad.
