  sections:
    - heading: "Getting the EU pet passport right in Italy"
      body: |
        Italy issues EU pet passports through veterinari ufficiali, official vets who may work within the local Azienda Sanitaria Locale (ASL) public health service or hold private authorisation. Not every private vet in Italy has official vet status.

        The passport records your pet's microchip number and rabies vaccination history. It must be signed by the official vet to be valid for travel. A standard private vet appointment that does not result in an official signature is not sufficient for EU travel.

        If you need a new passport or a vaccination update close to your travel date, bear in mind that a first-time rabies vaccination requires a 21-day wait before travel. A booster given within the valid period does not require additional waiting. Book your vet appointment accordingly.

    - heading: "Helsinki and Frankfurt: the main hubs from Italy to Tallinn"
      body: |
        No direct Italy-to-Tallinn scheduled service exists year-round. The two practical options are Finnair via Helsinki and Lufthansa via Frankfurt.

        Finnair connects Rome Fiumicino (FCO) and Milan Malpensa (MXP) to Helsinki (HEL), with direct Finnair HEL-TLL services. Helsinki Vantaa Airport is well-positioned as a Baltic hub and handles international live animal cargo. The HEL-TLL leg is about 50 minutes.

        Lufthansa operates from FCO and MXP to Frankfurt (FRA). The Frankfurt Animal Lounge at FRA is IATA CEIV Live Animals certified and handles pets of all sizes with professional care. The FRA to TLL connection adds time but gives confidence for cargo movements.
