content:
  h1: "Pet Transport India to Mexico: AQCS Certificate and the SENASICA 10-Day Window"
  overview: |
    India to Mexico is a very long-haul route with complex export requirements in India and a strict 10-day health certificate window for Mexico entry. AQCS/DAHD in India issues the health certificate and provides export clearance. SENASICA inspects pets on arrival in Mexico but does not impose routine quarantine on compliant pets. Emirates via Dubai is typically the most reliable cargo routing, though the total journey duration from India will be significant. Allow extra lead time for the AQCS endorsement process to avoid missing the 10-day certificate window.

  sections:
    - heading: "At a glance: India to Mexico vs. other India outbound routes"
      body: |
        | Requirement | India to Mexico | India to Brazil | India to Greece |
        |---|---|---|---|
        | Import permit | No (cert + inspection) | No (cert + inspection) | No (cert-based EU entry) |
        | Quarantine | No routine | No routine | No routine (if compliant) |
        | FAVN titre test | No | No | Yes (India not EU-listed) |
        | Cert validity window | 10 days before entry | At travel date | EU cert validity rules |
        | AQCS export clearance | Yes | Yes | Yes |
        | Complexity | High | High | Very high |

        Mexico and Brazil are similar in accessibility from India: no import permit required, no routine quarantine, and no titre test. Mexico adds the 10-day certificate timing constraint. Greece (and all EU destinations) is more demanding due to the FAVN titre test and 90-day wait requirements.

    - heading: "Airline comparison: India to Mexico"
      body: |
        | Airline | Hub | Route | Notes |
        |---|---|---|---|
        | Emirates | DXB | DEL/BOM-DXB-MEX | SkyCargo. Dubai-Mexico City connections available |
        | Air France | CDG | DEL/BOM-CDG-MEX | AFKLMP Cargo. CDG-MEX connections available |
        | Lufthansa | FRA | DEL/BOM-FRA-MEX | Lufthansa Cargo handles live animals |

        India to Mexico is a very long journey by any routing. Emirates via Dubai is typically the most reliable option, given Dubai's broad range of onward connections. The total journey time will be long: ensure the travel crate is IATA-compliant and the pet has sufficient water and ventilation for an extended trip.

    - heading: "The 10-day SENASICA window: planning around AQCS timelines"
      body: |
        Mexico's SENASICA requires that the health certificate be issued within 10 days of the pet entering Mexico. In practice this means the certificate must be dated no earlier than 10 days before the expected Mexico arrival date.

        From India, the AQCS endorsement process must be factored into this window. The official vet issues the health certificate, which then goes to AQCS for endorsement before the airport clearance on the day of departure. Allow enough time for the endorsement step while still keeping the certificate within the 10-day SENASICA window.

        Contact SENASICA (gob.mx/senasica) for the current health certificate format required for India-origin pets entering Mexico, and DAHD (dahd.nic.in) for the current AQCS export process.
