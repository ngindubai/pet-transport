  sections:
    - heading: "No breed ban, but full document checks"
      body: |
        Italy removed its breed-specific legislation in 2009, so there's no list of restricted breeds at the border, a genuine point of difference from some other EU states. That doesn't mean a lighter checking process, though. Italian Ministry of Health vets inspect documentation on arrival at Rome Fiumicino (FCO) or Milan Malpensa (MXP), and they'll want the full paper trail: microchip record, vaccination certificate, titre test result, and the EU-format health certificate endorsed by RAB. Missing any of it causes the same delay a breed issue would elsewhere. The absence of a breed rule simplifies planning, not paperwork.

    - heading: "The unlisted-country sequence from Rwanda"
      body: |
        Rwanda's status as an EU-unlisted third country means Italy applies EU Regulation 576/2013 and Commission Delegated Regulation 2020/692 in full. The chip has to precede the vaccination. Blood for the titre test can't be drawn until at least 30 days after that vaccination, and the result needs to clear 0.5 IU/ml at an EU-approved lab. The 90-day wait that follows counts from the day blood was drawn, the detail that trips up most owners planning this route, since a quick lab turnaround feels like it should speed things up and doesn't. No quarantine applies to pets that arrive fully compliant.

    - heading: "Kigali to Rome or Milan: airline options"
      body: |
        Ethiopian Airlines Cargo via Addis Ababa is a solid starting point given the flight distance from Kigali, with onward links reaching Rome. Brussels Airlines and RwandAir both connect through Brussels, giving access to either Fiumicino or Malpensa depending on the onward schedule. KLM Cargo via Nairobi and Amsterdam is a further alternate. Confirm in advance which Italian gateway a given routing actually serves, since not every carrier reaches both, and check live animal acceptance on the connecting leg specifically.

faqs:
  - question: "Does Italy have breed restrictions for imported dogs?"
    answer: "No. Italy removed breed-specific legislation in 2009, so there is no national list of restricted or banned breeds at the border. Documentation checks still apply in full to every pet regardless of breed."
  - question: "Which Italian airport should I book cargo into, Rome or Milan?"
    answer: "Both Rome Fiumicino (FCO) and Milan Malpensa (MXP) handle live animal imports and have Italian Ministry of Health vets on site for document checks. Confirm which one your chosen airline actually serves before booking, since not every carrier reaches both."
  - question: "Why does the 90-day wait matter more than the titre test result itself?"
    answer: "Because the wait is fixed to the blood draw date, not the result date. Even a fast lab turnaround doesn't shorten the 90 days, so the draw date, not the day you receive the paperwork, is what should anchor your travel planning."
  - question: "What if the microchip is implanted after the rabies vaccination by mistake?"
    answer: "The vaccination won't count toward the titre sequence. EU rules require the chip before or at the same time as the vaccination. If reversed, a new vaccination is needed after the chip, and both the 30-day and 90-day waits restart from that point."
