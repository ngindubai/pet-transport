content:
  h1: "Pet Transport from Albania to Australia"
  overview: |
    Albania's UK route already has a known weak point on this site: no EU-recognised laboratory runs the rabies titre test locally, so blood samples usually travel abroad before a result comes back. For an Australia-bound pet, that turns out to be an advantage in disguise. Albania sits on DAFF's non-approved list, so 180 consecutive days in an approved country are compulsory anyway, and staging those days in the UK means the titre test finally gets run without a courier delay attached.

    None of that shortens the calendar much. Mickleham's 10-day quarantine still waits at the far end, on a weekday arrival only, and Qantas's Heathrow-Perth sector, one of the longest nonstop passenger routes flown anywhere, is the practical way to close out the journey once the 180 days are behind you.

  sections:
    - heading: "Why does a non-approved country need 180 days somewhere else first?"
      body: |
        DAFF sorts every country into Group 1, Group 2, Group 3, or non-approved. Albania falls into the last category, along with most of the Balkans and a good deal of Africa and Asia. A direct booking simply isn't available, whatever an agent might promise. The pet has to live continuously in an approved country for 180 days, and only then does DAFF start processing the import.

        That's not a paperwork inconvenience. It's a fixed biological quarantine period DAFF applies regardless of how healthy the animal is on paper.

    - heading: "Does staging in the UK fix Albania's lab problem too?"
      body: |
        Yes, and this is the genuine point of difference on this route. Albania's UK page on this site already flags that no EU-recognised laboratory operates domestically for the rabies antibody titre test, so samples usually get couriered out and the result takes weeks longer than it would elsewhere.

        Move the 180-day clock to the UK, though, and the titre test simply runs at an APHA-approved laboratory while the pet is already resident there. No international courier, no extra weeks waiting for a foreign lab to report back. It's the same test DAFF would demand anyway, just run somewhere the infrastructure already exists.

    - heading: "What closes out the journey at the Australian end?"
      body: |
        Mickleham Post Entry Quarantine Facility in Melbourne takes every imported cat and dog for a minimum 10 days, and it only accepts weekday arrivals. Qantas flies Heathrow to Perth nonstop, one of the longest scheduled routes any airline operates, which makes it the sensible final leg once the UK residency period and its paperwork are both done.

        Book that flight with Mickleham's weekday rule fixed in mind. Arriving on a Friday means a weekend spent waiting at the airport rather than moving straight into quarantine.
