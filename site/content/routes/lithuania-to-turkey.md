  h1: "Pet Transport from Lithuania to Turkey"
  overview: |
    Lithuania's routes into Poland and Hungary in this build run on the EU pet passport alone, no titre test, no separate certificate. Istanbul is a different story. Turkey sits outside the EU, and the passport that moves a pet freely across the bloc doesn't get it through Turkish customs.

    What this route actually needs is the same unlisted-origin process used across the rest of this cluster: a standalone export certificate from VMVT, a FAVN titre test, and a 90-day wait before travel.

  sections:
    - heading: "One route that breaks Lithuania's usual pattern"
      body: |
        Every other Lithuanian corridor in this build relies on the EU pet passport doing the heavy lifting. Turkey doesn't recognise it. VMVT, Lithuania's food and veterinary service, has to issue a separate export health certificate reformatted to Turkey's own model, on top of the passport most owners already carry for EU travel. No additional export permit is required beyond that certificate.

        It's an easy step to overlook if you're used to Lithuania's other routes, where the passport alone is enough. Here, it isn't.

    - heading: "The titre test governs the schedule"
      body: |
        Blood for the FAVN test needs drawing at least 30 days after the rabies vaccination, a result of at least 0.5 IU/ml, and then a 90-day wait from that draw date before the pet can travel. A booster given afterward carries its own separate 21-day wait, so plan any revaccination around the titre schedule rather than the other way round.

        Turkish Airlines flies Vilnius to Istanbul direct, and airBaltic's routing via Riga is a workable second option if the direct flight's cargo space is fully booked.
