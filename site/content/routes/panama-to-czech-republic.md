content:
  h1: "Pet Transport Panama to Czech Republic: 2026 Guide"
  overview: "The Panama to Czech Republic route requires the longest preparation timeline of any route in this guide series: 20-26 weeks from start to departure. Panama is an unlisted third country under Commission Delegated Regulation (EU) 2026/131, which means a mandatory FAVN rabies titre test, a 30-day minimum wait from vaccination before the blood draw, a result of 0.5 IU/ml or above from an EU-approved laboratory, and then a 90-day wait from the blood draw date before the pet can enter. The Czech Republic's State Veterinary Administration (SVS) applies these rules strictly."

  sections:
    - heading: "The Unlisted Country Pathway: Step by Step"
      body: |
        Unlike listed countries such as Jamaica, which can bypass the titre test, Panama must follow the full unlisted country protocol under EU pet import regulations. Commission Delegated Regulation (EU) 2026/131 sets the framework from April 2026, consolidating and updating the rules under the previous Regulation (EU) No 576/2013.

        The preparation sequence for a pet moving from Panama to the Czech Republic is:

        Step 1: Microchip your pet with an ISO 11784/11785 transponder. This must happen before any vaccination.

        Step 2: Administer a rabies vaccination using a product approved for the destination country. Your MIDA-registered vet in Panama can advise on approved vaccines.

        Step 3: Wait a minimum of 30 days from the vaccination date. The blood for the titre test cannot be drawn until this waiting period is complete.

        Step 4: FAVN blood draw. The sample must be sent to an EU-approved laboratory (as listed under Commission Decision 2000/258/EC). The result must be 0.5 IU/ml or above.

        Step 5: From the blood draw date (not the result date), a 90-day waiting period begins. Your pet cannot enter the Czech Republic or any EU country during this period.

        Step 6: After the 90 days have elapsed, book your cargo and obtain the DINASA-endorsed health certificate within 10 days of departure.

        The minimum elapsed time from step 1 to travel is approximately 20 weeks.

    - heading: "Czech Republic SVS Requirements"
      body: |
        The State Veterinary Administration (SVS, svscr.cz) is the Czech Republic's veterinary border authority. SVS applies EU non-commercial pet movement rules and checks all documentation at Prague Václav Havel Airport (PRG).

        On arrival from Panama, SVS will verify:

        Microchip: ISO 11784/11785, implanted before vaccination. Scanned and cross-referenced against the health certificate.

        Rabies vaccination: Current, post-microchip, valid at the time of entry.

        FAVN titre test: Result documented in the health certificate. Lab name, date of blood draw, result in IU/ml. The date of the blood draw plus 90 days must predate the arrival.

        Health certificate: EU Annex IV format, DINASA-endorsed, issued within 10 days of travel.

        Any discrepancy or missing information can result in the animal being held. SVS inspectors are thorough.

    - heading: "Getting from PTY to Prague"
      body: |
        Prague Václav Havel Airport (PRG) is well connected to European hubs from which live animal cargo can be arranged. The two-leg journey from Tocumen (PTY) requires a connection at Amsterdam, Madrid, or Frankfurt.

        Copa Airlines operates PTY to AMS, which pairs well with KLM or partner cargo to PRG. Amsterdam Schiphol is an effective transit point with good live animal handling.

        Iberia Cargo flies PTY to MAD (Madrid Barajas). From MAD, onward cargo to PRG can be arranged via Iberia, Vueling, or Czech Airlines partner routes. Madrid is Iberia's hub and live animal cargo from South America and Central America transits through it frequently.

        Lufthansa via Frankfurt is worth considering for larger dogs. FRA to PRG is one of Europe's shorter cargo hops. The Lufthansa animal centre at FRA handles the transatlantic portion reliably.

    - heading: "DINASA Export Process in Panama"
      body: |
        DINASA (Dirección Nacional de Sanidad Animal), the animal health directorate of Panama's Ministry of Agricultural Development (MIDA, mida.gob.pa), endorses all health certificates for live animal exports from Panama.

        The process involves:
        - A MIDA-registered vet examines the animal and completes the EU Annex IV template
        - The vet includes the titre test results and blood draw date in the certificate
        - DINASA reviews and stamps the document

        The certificate must be issued within 10 days of travel. Allow at least three to five business days for the DINASA endorsement, particularly if you are travelling near a Panamanian public holiday. The health certificate cannot be pre-prepared and stored; it must be timed precisely to the 10-day window.

    - heading: "Planning Your 20-26 Week Timeline"
      body: |
        The most common mistake on Panama-to-EU routes is underestimating how long the preparation takes. Families who book their own flights first and then ask about the pet often find there is not enough time to complete the titre test pathway before the planned departure.

        Work backwards from your target arrival date in the Czech Republic:

        - The 90-day wait ends on your target arrival date at the earliest
        - Blood draw is 90 days before that
        - Blood draw is at least 30 days after vaccination
        - Microchipping happens before vaccination

        This means preparations must start at least 20 weeks before arrival. 26 weeks is more realistic if you want buffer time for a potential titre test failure.

faqs:
  - question: "My dog had a rabies vaccination in Panama three months ago. Does the 90-day wait still apply?"
    answer: "It depends. If a FAVN blood draw was taken at least 30 days after that vaccination and the result was 0.5 IU/ml or above, the 90-day wait is counted from that blood draw date. If the 90 days have already elapsed and the titre test result is still valid, your dog may be ready to travel sooner than 20 weeks from now. Bring all vaccination and titre test records to a consultation with PetTransportGlobal so we can assess where you are in the process."
  - question: "Can I use an ELISA test instead of the FAVN for Czech Republic entry?"
    answer: "Yes. The Czech Republic SVS accepts both FAVN and ELISA (enzyme-linked immunosorbent assay) tests, as long as they are performed by an EU-approved laboratory and the result is 0.5 IU/ml or above. The 30-day pre-draw and 90-day post-draw waiting periods apply equally to both test types. Confirm with your chosen laboratory which format they use and whether it is accepted under Commission Decision 2000/258/EC."
  - question: "Does the titre test need to be repeated for the Czech Republic, or does one valid test cover entry for life?"
    answer: "A valid titre test result of 0.5 IU/ml or above, with the 90-day wait elapsed, allows entry to the Czech Republic (and any EU country) for the lifetime of the rabies vaccination. The titre test does not need to be repeated unless the rabies vaccination expires and needs to be renewed. If the vaccination lapses and has to be re-administered, a new blood draw and a new 90-day wait are required before the next EU entry."
