  h1: "Pet Transport United States to Panama"
  overview: |
    Copa Airlines built its entire business model around Tocumen being the connecting point for the Americas, and that works in a pet owner's favour here. Direct flights from a wide spread of US cities land straight into Copa's home hub, more connection options than most Latin American capitals in this build can offer.

    Panama's own requirements are refreshingly simple: no titre test, no import permit, no quarantine for a compliant pet. The one step that trips owners up is a Panamanian consulate's legalisation stamp on the health certificate, a document most people don't expect to need and easy to miss until it's too late.

  sections:
    - heading: "Why Panama skips the titre test"
      body: |
        Panama's import rules don't include a rabies antibody titre test for pets from any country of origin, the US included. What is required: an ISO microchip, rabies vaccination at least 30 days before travel, documented parasite treatment within 14 days of travel, and a health certificate. That's a shorter list than most of the corridors in this build.

    - heading: "The consulate step nobody expects"
      body: |
        After a USDA-accredited vet issues the health certificate and APHIS endorses it, the document still needs to be stamped and signed by a Panamanian consulate or embassy in the US before the pet travels. This is a genuine extra step, distinct from the USDA process, and skipping it means the certificate won't satisfy MIDA's inspection at Tocumen on arrival. Build the consulate appointment into your timeline alongside the vet visit.

    - heading: "Copa's hub advantage at Tocumen"
      body: |
        Copa Airlines operates Tocumen International Airport as its central hub for the Americas, which means direct flights from Miami, New York, Orlando and several other US cities land straight into Panama City rather than routing through a third country. American Airlines Cargo covers the Miami route as a solid alternative. For owners outside the major Florida gateways, Copa's network is usually the more direct option.
