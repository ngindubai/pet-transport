content:
  h1: "Pet Transport from the United States to Guyana"
  overview: |
    Guyana's Livestock Development Authority keeps a short list of countries it trusts enough to waive the rabies titre test on arrival: the UK, Ireland, and a handful of Commonwealth Caribbean neighbours sit on it. The United States doesn't, and that surprises a lot of American owners who assume their country's own rabies-controlled status will count for something at the border. It doesn't. A titre test applies, and so does GLDA's case-by-case quarantine review, up to 90 days, the same review a UAE-origin pet faces on a different route covered on this site.

    The flight side is far simpler. American Airlines runs a nonstop cargo service from both Miami and New York-JFK straight into Georgetown, with no CDC-related booking restriction working against it here, unlike the reverse direction out of Guyana. Getting the paperwork right, not getting a seat on the plane, is what decides how long this move takes.

  sections:
    - heading: "Why the US doesn't get GLDA's easy pathway"
      body: |
        GLDA's own import procedure splits every origin into two camps: countries it treats as rabies-free, and everywhere else. The everywhere-else group gets a titre test, blood drawn more than 30 days after vaccination, and a quarantine decision that isn't guaranteed either way. It's reviewed on the specific application, the test result and the paperwork on file, not applied as a blanket rule.

        Nothing about that changes just because the CDC itself classifies the US as low-risk for dog rabies domestically. GLDA's list is its own, built mostly around the UK and Commonwealth Caribbean states, and a US-origin pet sits outside it. A clean titre result and a complete, correctly dated USDA certificate are the strongest levers an owner has over the quarantine outcome. Neither guarantees a particular result.

    - heading: "USDA's export side, and the flight that's actually simple"
      body: |
        Compared with most origin countries this site covers, the US export process is light. There's no formal export permit, no separate government inspection appointment to book. A USDA-accredited vet examines the pet, usually within 10 days of travel, completes the health certificate, and submits it to USDA APHIS Veterinary Services for endorsement. The fee is USD 38, and turnaround runs 2 to 10 business days depending on whether it's handled electronically through VEHCS or by mail.

        The flight itself is a single-carrier booking. American Airlines flies Georgetown nonstop from both Miami and JFK, and Caribbean Airlines duplicates the JFK route as a second option. Neither carries the CDC high-risk booking restriction that complicates the reverse route out of Guyana, since that rule tracks the animal's country of origin, and a US-origin pet simply isn't affected by it.
