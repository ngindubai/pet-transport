  h1: "Pet Transport from Australia to Russia"
  overview: |
    Russia does not ask for a rabies titre test from any country, Australia included, and does not impose a waiting period or a quarantine on a pet arriving with the right paperwork. Owners moving from almost anywhere else in this build spend months on blood tests and fixed waits. Here, the veterinary side is close to the simplest in the entire site.

    The flight is where the real planning happens. There has never been a direct Australia-Russia route, and both countries' aviation relationships with each other have only got more complicated since 2022. The workable path in mid-2026 goes through Dubai, using Emirates or a Qantas codeshare out of Australia, then a separate FlyDubai, Emirates or Aeroflot booking on to Moscow.

  sections:
    - heading: "Why Russia's own rules are the easy part"
      body: |
        A pet needs a microchip before vaccination, a rabies shot at least 20 days old and no more than 12 months old on arrival, and the standard core vaccines for its species. Up to two pets travel with no import permit and no quarantine, provided the paperwork is complete. That is genuinely lighter than the EU's titre-and-wait model or Australia's own strict entry rules on the reverse route.

        What Russia does need is the export certificate itself, reformatted to Rosselkhoznadzor's model and endorsed close to departure. DAFF doesn't publish a ready-made Australia-to-Russia template, since so few pets travel this direction, so confirm the current format directly rather than assuming a standard Australian export certificate will be accepted as is.

    - heading: "Getting from Australia to Moscow: the Dubai routing"
      body: |
        Sydney, Melbourne and Perth all connect to Dubai on Emirates, with Perth due to regain A380 capacity from 27 July 2026 as the airline rebuilds a network that the Iran-Israel-US conflict badly disrupted between late February and June 2026. From Dubai, three carriers now fly on to Moscow: Aeroflot restarted its Sheremetyevo-Dubai service on 1 June 2026, moving to twice daily from 1 July; FlyDubai runs the Vnukovo route, increased to two flights a day between 26 June and 31 July 2026; and Emirates serves Domodedovo directly in around seven hours.

        None of this existed reliably even four months before this page was written. Book the two legs as separate live-animal bookings, and build in a buffer, since a route this newly restored can still see schedule changes at short notice.

    - heading: "What could still go wrong"
      body: |
        The single biggest risk on this route isn't Russian regulation. It's the durability of the Gulf recovery itself. The ceasefire signed on 19 June 2026 ended a war that had grounded Emirates, Etihad and Qatar Airways capacity for four months, and airlines are still restoring routes cancelled during that period. Treat any schedule you find today as provisional until you've reconfirmed it close to your actual travel date.
