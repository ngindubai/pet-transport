  h1: "Pet Transport from Finland to Kazakhstan"
  overview: |
    Kazakhstan is a large and logistically distinctive destination for pet owners. Two things set this route apart from other Caucasus and Central Asian moves from Finland: the rabies vaccination minimum is 30 days (not the 21 days that applies to many other destinations), and there are two entry airports to choose from depending on whether you are going to Almaty or Astana.

    From Finland, there is no direct flight to either Kazakhstani airport. The main options are via Istanbul with Turkish Airlines, or via Amsterdam with Air Astana (Kazakhstan's national carrier). Each routing has its own booking and transit logistics.

    Kazakhstan's membership of the EAEU (Eurasian Economic Union) means EU-format health certificates are generally accepted, but EAEU veterinary rules are not identical to EU rules, and requirements can shift. Confirming current requirements with the Kazakhstani Embassy in Helsinki before finalising paperwork is a sensible step, not an optional one.

  sections:
    - heading: "The 30-day rabies rule and why it catches people out"
      body: |
        Kazakhstan's import rules require the rabies vaccination to have been administered at least 30 days before entry. This is a longer minimum than the 21 days required by many other countries, and it is the single most common reason for last-minute problems on this route.

        If your pet was vaccinated 25 days before your planned travel date, it is not compliant for Kazakhstan, even though it would be compliant for, say, Georgia or Armenia. The 30-day minimum cannot be waived. Build this into your planning from the start: count back 30 days from your planned entry date and make sure the vaccination date is on or before that point.

        The maximum validity is 1 year from vaccination. As with the minimum, check this carefully if your pet's vaccination is on an annual booster schedule and the booster falls close to the travel date.

    - heading: "Two routes from Helsinki: Istanbul vs Amsterdam"
      body: |
        For cargo from Helsinki-Vantaa (HEL) to Kazakhstan, there are two main routing options. The first is via Istanbul (IST) with Turkish Airlines Cargo, connecting to Almaty (ALA) or Astana (NQZ). Turkish Airlines operates this corridor regularly and has established live animal handling at IST.

        The second option is via Amsterdam Schiphol (AMS) to connect with Air Astana. Air Astana is Kazakhstan's national carrier and flies from AMS to both ALA and NQZ. Because Air Astana's staff at the Kazakhstani end are familiar with domestic import requirements, this routing can reduce the risk of documentation queries on arrival. The first leg from HEL to AMS can be operated by Finnair or KLM.

        Neither routing is inherently superior. The choice comes down to scheduling, cargo booking availability, and whether Air Astana's presence at the destination is a priority for you. An IPATA-registered agent can advise on current capacity and pricing for both options.

    - heading: "EAEU membership and what it means for your Finnish paperwork"
      body: |
        Kazakhstan is a member of the Eurasian Economic Union, a trade and regulatory bloc that has its own veterinary standards. In practice, EU-format health certificates are accepted for companion animal imports, and the Ruokavirasto-endorsed certificate from Finland meets the core requirements.

        However, EAEU veterinary regulations are distinct from EU regulations, and Kazakhstan can apply additional conditions. The safest approach is to contact the Kazakhstani Embassy in Helsinki before finalising your paperwork, and ask them to confirm the current accepted certificate format and any specific requirements for Finnish-origin pets.

        This is not bureaucratic caution for its own sake: there have been cases where pets from EU countries faced complications at Kazakhstani entry points due to documentation discrepancies. A 15-minute call or email to the Embassy months before travel is far less disruptive than a problem at ALA or NQZ.
