Compare the Rwanda to Netherlands journey with a route from, say, Kenya to the Netherlands and the difference becomes clear. Kenya is also an EU unlisted third country, so the core rule set is the same: microchip, rabies vaccination, FAVN titre test, 90-day wait. What the Kigali end adds is the RAB endorsement layer, which Kenya does not have in exactly the same form. The EU side of the journey is identical for both. Both routes take 20 to 26 weeks.

Understanding what drives that timeline helps you plan around it rather than being surprised by it.

## What the EU Requires: The Full Unlisted Country Path

The Netherlands is an EU member state. Rwanda is not on the EU's approved list of third countries for pet travel, which means dogs and cats arriving from Rwanda must follow the full EU unlisted third country procedure under Regulation 576/2013 and Commission Delegated Regulation 2020/692.

The steps are fixed and must happen in this sequence:

| Step | What happens | Minimum wait before next step |
|---|---|---|
| 1. Microchip | ISO 11784/11785 chip implanted | Must precede vaccination |
| 2. Rabies vaccination | Administered post-chip | 30 days before blood draw |
| 3. Blood draw | Sample sent to EU-approved lab | 90 days before Netherlands entry |
| 4. FAVN titre test result | Must be 0.5 IU/ml or above | Result must be received before certificate issued |
| 5. EU health certificate | Issued in EU format, endorsed by RAB | Within required window before travel |

The 90-day wait is the critical planning point. It runs from the date blood was drawn, not the date the result was received. If your titre test result arrives three weeks after the blood draw, the 90-day clock is already three weeks in. The earliest possible entry into the Netherlands is therefore 30 days post-vaccination (for the blood draw) plus 90 days from that blood draw date: a hard minimum of 120 days from the vaccination, plus laboratory time on top.

## Why the Microchip Order Matters

EU rules are explicit: the microchip must be implanted before or at the same time as the rabies vaccination that underpins the titre test. This is not a bureaucratic detail; it is the foundation of the whole sequence.

If a chip is implanted after the vaccination has been given, that vaccination cannot be used to start the titre test process. The animal must be re-vaccinated after the chip is in, and the 30-day wait and titre test sequence starts again from that new vaccination date. In practice, this can add four to five months to the move. Check the chip is in place and reading correctly before the vaccination appointment is booked.

Your RAB-authorised vet should be familiar with the EU sequence. If you are starting from scratch with a young animal, the chip-then-vaccinate order is straightforward to implement from the outset.

## Routing from Kigali to Amsterdam Schiphol

Amsterdam Schiphol (AMS) is a major European cargo hub, and the KLM/Kenya Airways joint venture provides one of the more structured cargo paths from East Africa. The KGL-NBO-AMS routing via Nairobi is a practical choice, with KLM Cargo's established live animal handling at Schiphol on arrival.

Brussels Airlines offers a KGL-BRU-AMS option, with a short hop from Brussels to Amsterdam after the Kigali-Brussels leg. RwandAir operates the same BRU routing. Ethiopian Airlines via Addis Ababa (ADD) also connects to AMS via Ethiopian Cargo.

Whichever routing you use, confirm with the airline that live animals are accepted on each leg and that transit connection times meet the carrier's minimum live animal transit requirements. Build in the cargo booking lead time of four to six weeks from Kigali, and confirm your travel date only after the 90-day wait end date is fixed.

On arrival at Schiphol, Dutch customs (NVWA) will inspect the EU health certificate and titre test documentation. The documentation must be in order before boarding in Kigali; there is no opportunity to correct a missing RAB endorsement after the flight.
