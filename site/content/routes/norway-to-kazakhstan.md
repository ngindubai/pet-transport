  sections:
    - heading: "What Kazakhstan requires at the border"
      body: |
        Kazakhstan's veterinary import requirements are administered by the Committee of Veterinary Control and Supervision under the Ministry of Agriculture. Border veterinary officers inspect documentation at Almaty International Airport (ALA) or Astana International Airport (NQZ) on arrival.

        The core requirements are a valid rabies vaccination, an official health certificate issued within 10 days of travel, and an ISO-compliant microchip. Kazakhstan does not require a titre test for EEA-origin pets, which simplifies preparation considerably compared with some other non-EU destinations.

        For 1-2 personal companion animals, no import permit is required. Contact the Ministry of Agriculture if you are travelling with more animals to clarify the commercial movement threshold. Kazakhstan does not operate a blanket breed ban for common companion animals, but verify with the Committee if your pet is a breed that faces restrictions in other countries.

    - heading: "The Norwegian health certificate process"
      body: |
        Norway's health certificate for non-EU destinations is an official veterinary certificate signed by a Mattilsynet-authorised official vet and then endorsed by the Mattilsynet regional office. The two-step process must complete within 10 days of your departure date.

        Schedule the vet examination within the 10-day window before travel, then submit the certificate to the Mattilsynet regional office immediately after signing. Allow 1-2 working days for the endorsement. If travelling on a Monday, the vet appointment must be no later than the preceding Wednesday to leave time for endorsement.

        Your regular vet can handle vaccinations and microchipping, but the official health certificate must come from a Mattilsynet-authorised official vet. Check mattilsynet.no for the list of authorised official vets in your region.

    - heading: "Cargo routing from Oslo to Kazakhstan"
      body: |
        There is no direct cargo service from Oslo (OSL) to Almaty (ALA) or Astana (NQZ). Turkish Airlines Cargo via Istanbul (IST) is the most established option: IST to ALA or NQZ is a regular scheduled service. Book through an approved cargo agent and confirm pet acceptance on both legs before committing to a travel date.

        Air Astana, Kazakhstan's flag carrier, serves both ALA and NQZ from European hubs. Routing from Oslo typically means connecting via a major European hub such as Frankfurt. Confirm live animal cargo acceptance with Air Astana before booking, as policies vary by aircraft type.

        Lufthansa Cargo from Oslo to Frankfurt, then onward to Kazakhstan via a connecting carrier, is a further option. Frankfurt's Animal Lounge provides regulated conditions for live animal transit and is a reliable transfer point for long-haul cargo moves.
