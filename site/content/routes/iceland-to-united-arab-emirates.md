content:
  h1: "Pet Transport from Iceland to the United Arab Emirates"
  overview: |
    No flight leaves Keflavik for Dubai or Abu Dhabi without at least one stop. That single fact shapes most of what follows on this route: two cargo bookings instead of one, two airlines to coordinate, and a connection that has to work for a live animal, not just a suitcase.

    The Icelandic side of the paperwork is the easy part. MAST endorses a MOCCAE-format certificate, and there is no separate export permit to chase. What is genuinely unresolved is whether Iceland counts as one of MOCCAE's roughly 50 low-risk countries, the ones exempt from the rabies titre test. Sources disagree, and the UAE's own import portal cannot be checked automatically to settle it either way. Until you have a direct answer from MOCCAE, the safer plan assumes the titre test and its 90-day wait apply.

  sections:
    - heading: "Why this route has no verified titre test answer"
      body: |
        MOCCAE's exemption list runs to roughly 50 countries, and the well-documented examples (the UK, Germany, Japan, Singapore, Australia) are large trading and tourism partners with the UAE. Iceland is none of those things at the volume that tends to get a country onto this kind of list, but that is a guess, not a finding. Independent research for this page found operator pages that place Iceland among the exempt countries and others that omit it entirely, with no way to reconcile the two from public information.

        Given that a wrong assumption here costs you a 90-day wait discovered too late, not just an inconvenience, treat the titre test as required until MOCCAE or a UAE-based IPATA agent confirms otherwise in writing. If you get confirmation that Iceland is exempt, the whole preparation window shortens considerably, since the rest of the process (microchip, vaccination, import permit, certificate) does not depend on the answer.

    - heading: "Getting from Keflavik to the Gulf: two cargo legs, not one"
      body: |
        Icelandair's network from Keflavik reaches the Gulf only through a European hub such as Copenhagen, Paris CDG or Munich, where a connecting Emirates or Etihad service picks up the rest of the journey. Since 1 November 2024, Icelandair has not accepted pets as checked or excess baggage on any route, so the Keflavik-to-hub segment goes through Icelandair Cargo as manifested freight, a different booking channel from the connecting Gulf carrier's cargo division.

        This means you are coordinating two separate cargo reservations with two separate airlines, on top of the vet and permit paperwork. Build in buffer time between the two legs rather than a tight connection, and confirm with both cargo desks, not just one, that live animal handover between carriers at the hub is something they actually do on your specific routing.

    - heading: "The import permit clock runs faster than you might expect"
      body: |
        The UAE import permit is only valid for 30 days from the date it is issued. Apply too far ahead of your actual travel date and it will have expired before your pet arrives, forcing a reapplication. Since the permit process itself only takes one to two weeks, there is little reason to start it more than a month before you plan to fly, whatever your travel documentation timeline looks like on the Iceland side.
