content:
  h1: "Pet Transport from Switzerland to Jamaica"
  overview: |
    Jamaica's pet import rules are among the most involved in the Caribbean. The Veterinary Services Division (VSD) requires an import permit before you do anything else, a mandatory 14-day quarantine at Plumb Point National Quarantine Facility in Kingston, and a two-stage parasite treatment protocol that must be timed precisely against your travel date. For a move from Switzerland, add the FSVO/BLV health certificate process on the export side, and you have a route that needs careful planning from start to finish.

    The single most common mistake on this route is beginning with vet appointments and flight bookings before securing the VSD permit. The permit takes 8-12 weeks and is the gating step for everything else. Email vsdpermits@moa.gov.jm with your pet's details as soon as you know you are moving to Jamaica. Only once that permit is confirmed should you book cargo space and schedule the Swiss official vet.

    From Switzerland, cargo routing typically goes via London Heathrow (British Airways), Paris CDG (Air France), or Amsterdam (KLM). Both Zurich (ZRH) and Geneva (GVA) have connections to all three hubs. Your pet travels as manifest cargo throughout. On arrival at Norman Manley International Airport (KIN) in Kingston, Plumb Point facility staff will collect your pet and begin the 14-day quarantine period.

  sections:
    - heading: "VSD Permit and Restricted Breeds: Know Before You Apply"
      body: |
        Jamaica's VSD (Veterinary Services Division) issues import permits after reviewing your pet's details. Email vsdpermits@moa.gov.jm or call +1 876-977-2489. The application should include species, breed, age, sex, microchip number, and country of origin. Switzerland is accepted as a low-risk origin country, but the permit is not automatic. Allow the full 8-12 weeks, and follow up if you have not received a response within three weeks of submitting.

        Jamaica restricts four dog breeds from entry: Dogo Argentino, Fila Brasileiro, Japanese Tosa, and Pit Bull Terrier. If your dog is one of these breeds, or could be assessed as one by a border inspector, contact the VSD before applying. Refusal at KIN means the animal is returned to Switzerland at the owner's expense, and the entire process must begin again. There is no on-the-spot appeal mechanism.

    - heading: "Switzerland's Export Certificate: BLV Process for Non-EU Pets"
      body: |
        Switzerland sits outside the EU, so its health certificates are issued under Swiss federal authority rather than the EU's standard AHC format. FSVO/BLV (the Federal Food Safety and Veterinary Office) authorises official vets, known in German as amtliche Tierarzte, who are the only practitioners permitted to issue the export health certificate. Contact your cantonal veterinary office to identify the correct official vet for your district.

        The certificate must be issued within 10 days of travel. After the examination, BLV endorsement takes approximately one working day. Time the appointment so the endorsed certificate is ready at least 3-4 days before departure, giving you a buffer without risking the 10-day validity. The certificate must travel as an original document: Jamaica's VSD will not accept photocopies or digital versions at Plumb Point or KIN.
