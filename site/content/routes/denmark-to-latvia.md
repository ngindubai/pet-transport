        Riga Airport (RIX) is airBaltic's home hub, and the CPH to RIX route runs multiple times daily. This makes Denmark to Latvia one of the more natural Scandinavian to Baltic pet moves, with cargo infrastructure at RIX set up for the volume of connecting traffic.

    - heading: "PVD and what Latvia requires to see at the border"
      body: |
        PVD (Pārtikas un veterinārais dienests) is Latvia's Food and Veterinary Service, responsible for companion animal import control. For an intra-EU arrival from Denmark, PVD requires the EU pet passport with a valid ISO 15-digit microchip and a current rabies vaccination. No import permit, no pre-notification, no titre test.

        Spot checks at Riga Airport for intra-EU pet arrivals are occasional rather than routine, but carrying a complete and accurate EU pet passport is non-negotiable. PVD inspectors will check the microchip scan against the passport record and the vaccination dates against the travel date.

        Latvia has breed-specific legislation covering certain dog types. This affects a small proportion of breeds but is worth reviewing before travel if your dog is a large guarding breed.
