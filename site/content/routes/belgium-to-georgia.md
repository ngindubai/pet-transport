  h1: "Pet Transport from Belgium to Georgia"
  overview: |
    Moving a pet from Belgium to Georgia is a manageable process. Georgia accepts EU health certificates for incoming pets and does not require a titre test from EU-origin animals. The preparation centres on getting an FASFC-endorsed EU health certificate issued within 10 days of travel, arranging cargo from Brussels, and completing the NFSA inspection on arrival in Tbilisi.

    For small pets, Wizz Air's direct Brussels to Tbilisi service allows cabin travel. Larger pets use cargo routing via Istanbul or Frankfurt.

  sections:
    - heading: "Georgia's import requirements for Belgian pets"
      body: |
        Georgia accepts companion pets from EU member states with a valid EU-format health certificate. The certificate must be endorsed by FASFC and cover the microchip number, rabies vaccination status, and a general health declaration. No titre test is required from Belgium.

        On arrival at Tbilisi International Airport (TBS), the National Food Agency (NFSA) conducts a documentary and physical inspection. The NFSA is Georgia's competent authority for veterinary matters (nfa.gov.ge). Provided the documentation is complete and the pet is in good health, it clears customs without quarantine.

        Georgia's rules are subject to change. Always verify current requirements with the Georgian Embassy in Brussels or at nfa.gov.ge before travel.

    - heading: "Cargo routing from Brussels to Tbilisi"
      body: |
        No direct cargo service operates from Brussels (BRU) to Tbilisi (TBS). All pet cargo moves via a hub.

        Turkish Airlines is the most common choice for this route. The BRU-IST-TBS routing combines Turkish Airlines' European network with its Caucasus services. Transit times at Istanbul are generally reasonable for pet cargo, and Turkish Airlines Cargo is a known quantity on the TBS corridor. Temperature embargoes during Turkish summer months can restrict acceptance, so spring and autumn are the best seasons.

        Lufthansa Cargo via Frankfurt (FRA) is a reliable alternative. Frankfurt's Animal Lounge handles transiting pets professionally, and Lufthansa has onward connections to Tbilisi via Lufthansa or partner carriers.

        If your pet qualifies for cabin travel by size (generally under 8kg including carrier), Wizz Air's direct Brussels to Tbilisi service is significantly more straightforward. Your pet stays with you and avoids the cargo process entirely.

    - heading: "FASFC certificate: what to expect in Belgium"
      body: |
        Belgium's export process for companion pets involves two steps: a pre-travel examination by an FASFC-authorised vet, then endorsement of the health certificate by the relevant FASFC provincial office.

        The vet examination covers microchip readability, vaccination records, and general health fitness for travel. The EU-format health certificate is completed during this appointment. Allow one to two working days for FASFC endorsement after the examination. The 10-day clock on certificate validity starts from the examination date, so schedule the appointment carefully relative to your travel date.

        FASFC provincial contacts are listed at fasfc.be. Some vets are authorised to submit directly to FASFC online, which can speed up the process.
