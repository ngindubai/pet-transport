      body: |
        Pets originating from the Republic of Cyprus fall under EU pet travel rules and use the AHC or valid EU pet passport for GB entry. This is the standard route for anyone based in Nicosia, Limassol, Larnaca, or Paphos.

        North Cyprus (TRNC) is not an EU member state and is not recognised as such for pet travel purposes. If your pet has documentation or residence in the TRNC, you cannot use the EU-listed country rules for GB entry. You would need to contact the Department of Veterinary Services in the Republic and the UK's APHA to understand the applicable requirements.

        The vast majority of pet moves originate from the Republic of Cyprus and follow the standard EU-listed route. If you are in any doubt about which rules apply to your situation, contact the Cyprus Department of Veterinary Services directly.

    - heading: "The tapeworm treatment: planning the timing from Larnaca"
      body: |
        The 24-120 hour tapeworm window is the detail that requires the most planning on this route. Your vet must administer Praziquantel and record the exact date and time in your AHC or EU pet passport.

        For a direct LCA-LHR flight (typically 4-5 hours), work backwards from your expected landing time. If you land at 4pm UK time, the treatment must have been given no earlier than 44 hours before that (4pm minus 120 hours = 8pm two days earlier) and no later than 4 hours before that (4pm minus 24 hours). Book the vet appointment accordingly, and check in advance that your official vet can issue the AHC on the same visit or shortly after.
