  sections:
    - heading: "Getting your pet to Ljubljana from Sweden"
      body: |
        Ljubljana Airport (LJU) serves as Slovenia's main international gateway but is modest in scale compared to Scandinavian or Central European hubs. There are no direct cargo flights from Stockholm to Ljubljana on a regular scheduled basis.

        The two practical routing options are:

        Via Vienna (VIE): Austrian Airlines connects Stockholm to Vienna with regular onward service to Ljubljana. The VIE to LJU leg takes under an hour and Austrian Airlines is well-practised at cargo transfers within the Lufthansa Group network.

        Via Frankfurt (FRA): Lufthansa connects Stockholm to Frankfurt with connections to Ljubljana. Frankfurt's Animal Lounge is one of the best cargo pet facilities in Europe for a transfer hub.

        For pets small enough to travel in the cabin, either option works with the usual carrier size restrictions. For large dogs, booking the cargo connection early is important because Ljubljana's limited capacity means cargo space fills quickly.

    - heading: "What UVHVVR requires for a pet arriving from Sweden"
      body: |
        UVHVVR is Slovenia's competent authority for food safety, veterinary matters, and plant protection. Their requirements for an intra-EU pet arrival are exactly what you'd expect from the EU pet travel framework: EU pet passport, ISO microchip (implanted before the rabies vaccination), and a current rabies vaccination.

        Slovenia has no additional forms, declarations, or pre-arrival notification requirements for companion animals arriving from other EU member states under the non-commercial pet movement rules.

        One practical note on Ljubljana: the airport handles a modest volume of cargo pets compared to Vienna or Frankfurt. If you're sending a large dog through LJU, give yourself more lead time when booking cargo space and confirm the booking directly with the carrier's cargo office rather than relying on automated online booking.
