  sections:
    - heading: "Category 2, and what that actually means"
      body: |
        The Chief Veterinary Officer's country list, last revised in January 2023, splits every origin into four categories. Category 1 countries, among them Australia, New Zealand and the UK, skip the titre test entirely. The UAE sits in Category 2 with the US and most of Europe, meaning a FAVN or RFFIT titre test applies: blood drawn at least a month after vaccination, a result above 0.5 IU/mL, sent directly to the Chief Veterinary Officer by the testing laboratory.

        What doesn't apply, unusually, is quarantine. Trinidad and Tobago has run a no-quarantine policy for compliant pets since April 2013, a genuine point of difference from most of the destinations built into this series, where even a passing titre test still means weeks in a holding facility.

        Piarco International's only realistic long-haul option out of the Gulf runs through London, where Emirates or Etihad's Dubai or Abu Dhabi service connects into British Airways' nonstop Gatwick-Piarco route.
