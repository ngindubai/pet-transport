  h1: "Pet Transport from Kosovo to the United Kingdom"
  overview: |
    Wizz Air is the airline most people flying out of Pristina think of first, and it's the one carrier on this route guaranteed not to take a pet: no dogs, no cats, cabin or hold, only certified assistance animals. That single fact catches out more owners planning this route than any regulatory requirement does.

    The regulatory side is more predictable, if slower. Kosovo sits outside both of the UK's rabies-listed country tables, so the standard unlisted-country pathway applies: a titre test, a 90-day wait after a passing result, and an Animal Health Certificate from an FVA-endorsed vet. None of that is unique to Kosovo, but the 30-plus-90-day clock still sets a floor of four and a half to five and a half months.

    Turkish Airlines, Austrian Airlines and Swiss International Air Lines all offer workable one-stop routings into London via Istanbul, Vienna or Zurich. Swiss is worth a specific mention: its cargo division only imports pets into five countries worldwide, and the UK happens to be one of them.
  sections:
    - heading: "Wizz Air won't carry your pet, and that matters more than you'd think"
      body: |
        Pristina's flight schedule is dominated by low-cost routes to Western Europe, and Wizz Air runs the largest share of them. None of that capacity is available for a dog or cat. Wizz Air's policy excludes all pets from cabin and hold alike, with the sole exception of a certified assistance dog booked at least 48 hours before departure by emailing the airline's special assistance team.

        This isn't a minor footnote. Owners who search flight prices out of Pristina and see Wizz Air's fares at the top of the list, then assume they can simply add a pet to the booking, run into a wall at check-in. Build your itinerary around Turkish Airlines, Austrian Airlines or Swiss from the outset.
    - heading: "The titre test: Kosovo's unlisted status in practice"
      body: |
        Kosovo doesn't appear on the UK's Part 1 list, which covers a small group of European microstates and rabies-free territories, or the longer Part 2 list of approved lower-risk countries. That places it with the majority of the world under the standard unlisted-country pathway.

        In practice, that means the rabies titre test: blood drawn at least 30 days after vaccination, a passing result of 0.5 IU/ml or above, then a fixed 90-day wait before the pet can travel. The wait doesn't shorten regardless of how quickly the lab processes the sample, and it applies identically whether the pet is coming from Kosovo, Cote d'Ivoire, or anywhere else outside the listed groups.
    - heading: "Routing through Istanbul, Vienna or Zurich"
      body: |
        Turkish Airlines' Pristina to Istanbul to London Heathrow connection and Austrian Airlines' Pristina to Vienna to London Heathrow connection both keep the pet on a single carrier's network for the full journey, which simplifies handling compared with switching airlines mid-route.

        Swiss International Air Lines flies Pristina to Zurich and connects onward, but its cargo division, Swiss WorldCargo, is unusually restrictive about where it will deliver a pet: only five countries worldwide, and the UK is one of them. That narrow list is actually good news for this specific route, since it means Swiss cargo is a live option rather than one that looks available on paper but isn't in practice.
