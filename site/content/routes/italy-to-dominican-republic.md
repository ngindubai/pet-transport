  sections:
    - heading: "Dominican Republic import requirements: the checklist"
      body: |
        The DGAG (Direccion General de Ganaderia) under the Ministerio de Agricultura regulates all pet imports into the Dominican Republic. Your pet is inspected by DGAG veterinary officers at Las Americas International Airport (SDQ) in Santo Domingo on arrival.

        The requirements are: a microchip conforming to ISO 11784/11785, a valid rabies vaccination given between 30 days and 12 months before entry, a health certificate issued within 15 days of travel, and current DHPP vaccination for dogs (distemper, adenovirus, parainfluenza, parvovirus). Cats need rhinotracheitis, calicivirus, and panleukopenia vaccinations on record. All pets need internal and external antiparasitic treatment within 10 days of departure, documented by a vet.

        No import permit is required for personal companion animals (up to five animals with their owner). There is no quarantine for compliant arrivals.

    - heading: "Italy's export documentation: the ASL official vet process"
      body: |
        Italy's export documentation for non-EU destinations uses the AHC issued by a veterinario ufficiale at your local ASL (Azienda Sanitaria Locale). The certificate is then endorsed by the Ministero della Salute (Ministry of Health). In practice, contact your local ASL to book the official vet appointment and ask about the endorsement process for international certificates.

        The Dominican Republic accepts AHC certificates up to 15 days from issue, giving you slightly more scheduling room than most EU destinations. Still, book the ASL appointment as close to departure as your cargo booking allows.

        The certificate must include your full name and address, plus the animal's name, breed, sex, colour, age, and microchip number. If your local ASL has limited official vet appointments, book early, particularly if you are travelling during the summer holiday period when demand increases.
