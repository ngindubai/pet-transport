    - heading: "The 6-month wait: why NZ is longer than Australia"
      body: |
        Australia uses a 90-day titre test waiting period for Group 3 countries. New Zealand uses 6 calendar months from the blood draw date. MPI sets this independently of DAFF's framework, based on their own biosecurity assessment.

        For Singapore pets, this means: vaccination, wait 30 days, blood draw, wait 6 months, then travel. The 6-month clock starts on the blood draw date, not the date results arrive. If you draw blood on 1 January, the earliest your pet can arrive in New Zealand is 1 July.

        There are no exceptions and no shortcuts. New Zealand is one of the most biosecurity-conscious countries in the world, and the waiting period is a core part of that system.
