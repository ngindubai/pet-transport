  sections:
    - heading: "No titre test for New Zealand pets entering France"
      body: |
        New Zealand is on the EU's list of approved third countries (EU Regulation 576/2013, Annex II). Pets from New Zealand can enter France without the FAVN titre test or the 3-month wait from blood draw that applies to non-listed countries. Source: food.ec.europa.eu, 2026.

        The documentation required is: ISO microchip, valid rabies vaccination (21-day post-primary wait), and an MPI-endorsed EU-format health certificate issued within 10 days of travel. 4-6 weeks preparation is sufficient for a pet whose vaccination is current.

    - heading: "Auckland to Paris CDG: routing via Singapore or Dubai"
      body: |
        There is no direct cargo service from Auckland to Paris CDG. All routings connect via a hub. Singapore Airlines via Changi (SIN) and Emirates via Dubai (DXB) are the two established options for this corridor. Both operate temperature-controlled live animal cargo.

        Air France Cargo connects to CDG from either hub via partner services. Booking 4-6 weeks ahead is the standard lead time. Confirm live animal acceptance on your specific flights at time of booking.

    - heading: "France Category 1 breed check from New Zealand"
      body: |
        France's Category 1 classification covers Pit Bull type dogs without official LOF or FCI breed registration. These cannot be imported into France. Category 2 (Rottweiler, American Staffordshire with pedigree) can enter with restrictions.

        For most breeds common in New Zealand, including Labrador Retrievers, Golden Retrievers, Border Collies, Huntaways, and German Shepherds, no breed restriction applies. If your dog resembles a Pit Bull type without breed papers, verify classification before booking.
