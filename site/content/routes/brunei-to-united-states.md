content:
  h1: "Pet Transport from Brunei to the USA: 2026 Guide"
  overview: |
    Brunei appears on the CDC's high-risk list for dog rabies, last updated 15 April 2026, which puts this route in the same bracket as Malaysia, Thailand, and the Philippines for US-bound dogs. A serology test and entry through a CDC-registered facility are both required.

    What makes Brunei harder than its neighbours is getting the pet off the ground in the first place. Royal Brunei Airlines has no published live animal cargo product for private pets, so almost every move starts with a road transfer to Singapore or a short regional flight to Kuala Lumpur.

  sections:
    - heading: "The Brunei departure problem"
      body: |
        Bandar Seri Begawan's airport is small, and the national carrier does not sell pet cargo space to the public. This is different from most CDC high-risk origins on this site, where the departure city has an established live animal cargo product.

        Owners typically arrange ground transport to Singapore, a roughly hour-long drive plus a border crossing through Malaysian territory, or a short regional flight to Kuala Lumpur. Either transfer needs its own set of Brunei export paperwork lined up before the pet leaves DAA's jurisdiction.

        Once in Singapore or Kuala Lumpur, the pet joins the same commercial cargo network used by Malaysia and Singapore-origin pets, so the international leg itself is well-trodden ground for cargo handlers.
    - heading: "CDC serology test: the two clocks that matter"
      body: |
        Two separate timing rules apply, and both must be satisfied. The blood draw must happen at least 30 days after the rabies vaccination. Separately, the same blood draw must be at least 28 days before the date the pet actually enters the USA.

        Miss either window and the entry is invalid, regardless of how healthy the paperwork otherwise looks. Plan the travel date around the confirmed blood draw date, never the other way round.

        A passing result, once on a CDC-approved lab's file, is generally valid for the pet's lifetime provided vaccination stays current. If your pet already has a passing titre from an earlier move, ask your vet whether a fresh test is genuinely required before assuming it is.
    - heading: "Airline options once you reach a regional hub"
      body: |
        Cathay Pacific via Hong Kong and Qatar Airways via Doha are the two most reliable carriers for the Singapore or Kuala Lumpur to US leg. Both have established live animal handling at their hub airports, which matters more on a route with an already-complicated origin.

        Confirm CDC-registered facility availability at your chosen US entry airport before booking flights. Not every major US airport has one, and the list can change, so verify against the CDC's current published list rather than an older PDF or blog post.

  - question: "Can I fly my pet directly out of Brunei to the USA?"
