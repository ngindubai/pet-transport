  overview: |
    GAVS, Mongolia's General Authority for Veterinary Services, has run its export certification through a digital system since May 2019, fully in place by early 2020. That's a genuinely modern setup by regional standards, and it takes some of the guesswork out of getting a compliant certificate before the vet appointment even happens.

    Mongolia also carries CFIA's high-risk-for-dog-rabies classification, current as of 13 March 2025. For a family pet, that changes surprisingly little: the listing's practical force falls on commercial dog shipments, banned outright since 2022, not on an owner-accompanied dog or cat.

    What does take planning is the flight. There's no direct link between Ulaanbaatar and Canada, so the route runs through Frankfurt or Istanbul on MIAT's own long-haul aircraft or Turkish Airlines.

  sections:
    - heading: "A digital certificate system worth knowing about"
      body: |
        Getting an export certificate right on paper, in the correct language, with the correct fields, trips up owners moving from countries where the process is still manual and slow. GAVS moved its veterinary certification onto a digital platform in May 2019, with nationwide rollout complete by early 2020. That doesn't remove the need to build the certificate to CFIA's English or French format rather than any other destination's template, but it does mean the underlying process is faster and less error-prone than in several neighbouring countries this site covers.

    - heading: "The high-risk listing, in practice"
      body: |
        CFIA's list, updated 13 March 2025, groups Mongolia with dozens of other countries under a classification that sounds stricter than it turns out to be for a personal pet. Its real bite is a ban on commercial dog imports, in force since 28 September 2022. An owner travelling with their own dog or cat follows the ordinary standard process: current vaccination, correct certificate, no serology test, no quarantine.

        The detail worth checking regardless is CFIA's 8-month age presumption. A younger dog not accompanied by its named owner risks being treated as commercial, a serious outcome from a high-risk country. Confirm directly with CFIA if that could apply to your move.

    - heading: "Frankfurt or Istanbul: no way around a connection"

        MIAT Mongolian Airlines flies its own Boeing 787s and 767s between Ulaanbaatar and both Frankfurt and Istanbul, either reachable onward to Toronto via Air Canada or a partner carrier. Turkish Airlines offers a genuine single-carrier alternative through Istanbul, using its own nonstop Istanbul-Toronto service for the transatlantic leg.

        Check MIAT's cargo hold exclusion list before booking if your dog is any kind of flat-faced breed. It runs to 18 breeds, wider than most international carriers', and includes dogs owners don't always think of as brachycephalic, like the Cane Corso.
