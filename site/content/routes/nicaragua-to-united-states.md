  h1: "Pet Transport from Nicaragua to United States"
  overview: |
    Nicaragua does not appear on the CDC's high-risk list for dog rabies, last confirmed 15 April 2026, so this route skips the titre test and lengthy wait that trips up owners moving pets to the UK or the EU. A completed CDC Dog Import Form, a microchip, and a minimum age of 6 months cover the US side for dogs. Cats face even less: no federal requirement at all beyond arriving visibly healthy.

    The paperwork that actually needs planning sits on the Nicaraguan end. IPSA's export health exam has to happen within 2 weeks of departure, and the export certificate has to be current on the day of travel. Managua's Augusto C. Sandino International Airport has genuine non-stop cargo options to Miami on American Airlines and Avianca, which keeps the flight itself simple even where the paperwork sequencing is not.

    One booking trap is specific to this route. United flies MGA to Houston daily, but its cargo pet programme stopped taking general public bookings in 2018 and now serves only military and State Department moves.
  sections:
    - heading: "Why this route is lighter than Nicaragua's routes to Europe"
      body: |
        The rabies titre test that makes Nicaragua-to-UK or Nicaragua-to-EU shipments run four to five months does not apply here. That test exists because the UK and EU classify third countries against their own listed-country tables, and Nicaragua sits outside them. The US uses a different system entirely: the CDC's high-risk list for dog rabies, and Nicaragua is not on it, confirmed against the CDC's current list, last updated 15 April 2026.

        That single difference removes the blood draw, the 30-day wait after vaccination, and the 3-month wait after the blood draw that otherwise dominate the timeline on this site's Nicaragua-to-Europe pages. What is left for a dog is a microchip, proof of age over 6 months, and a free online form. Cats face no federal entry requirement at all, beyond arriving in good health, since APHIS does not regulate pet cat imports.

        None of that removes the need for a working export certificate from the Nicaraguan side. IPSA's general rules still call for a health exam within 2 weeks of departure and a rabies vaccination no older than 365 days, and those windows are tighter than owners moving to a country with heavier import rules sometimes expect, precisely because the destination side feels so much lighter.

    - heading: "Flying Managua to a US gateway"
      body: |
        American Airlines and Avianca both run daily non-stop service from Managua to Miami, and Avianca adds a second daily non-stop to Fort Lauderdale. That gives this route two working US gateways rather than the single option some Central American pairs are limited to, and both carriers accept live animal cargo bookable by the general public, American through its PetEmbark programme and Avianca through Avianca Cargo under IATA's Live Animal Regulations.

        United's daily Houston non-stop is the odd one out. It looks like the shortest routing on paper for anyone relocating to Texas, but United suspended general public cargo pet bookings in 2018 and has not reinstated them; as of 2026 the programme is open only to active-duty military on PCS orders and State Department staff on official postings. A civilian pet owner booking this route needs to route through Miami or Fort Lauderdale instead, then arrange onward domestic transport if the final destination is elsewhere in the US.
