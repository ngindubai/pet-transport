content:
  h1: "Pet Transport Uruguay to Austria: 2026 Guide"
  overview: "Uruguay to Austria is a long-distance relocation route that typically involves three cargo legs and 20-26 weeks of preparation. Uruguay is an unlisted third country under Commission Delegated Regulation (EU) 2026/131, so the FAVN titre test, a 30-day wait after vaccination, a result of 0.5 IU/ml or above, and a 90-day wait from the blood draw date are all mandatory before entry. The export process is managed through Uruguay's Ministry of Livestock, Agriculture and Fisheries (MGAP) via the DGSG. Austrian import checks are carried out by AGES at Vienna International Airport."

  sections:
    - heading: "Uruguay's Export Process: MGAP-DGSG"
      body: |
        Uruguay's official authority for animal health export certification is the Dirección General de Servicios Ganaderos (DGSG), which sits within the Ministerio de Ganadería, Agricultura y Pesca (MGAP, mgap.gub.uy). For companion animal exports, the process is:

        A MGAP-registered veterinarian examines the animal and prepares the EU Annex IV non-commercial health certificate template. The certificate includes the microchip number, vaccination details, and the FAVN titre test result with blood draw date.

        DGSG reviews and endorses the document. Allow three to five business days for the DGSG stamp.

        The certificate must be issued within 10 days of travel. Do not have it prepared too early, as it expires quickly.

        Uruguay's departure airport is Carrasco International (MVD), Montevideo. MVD handles limited long-haul cargo directly; most Uruguay-to-Europe pet cargo routes via an intermediate South American hub.

    - heading: "The Three-Leg Journey from MVD to VIE"
      body: |
        Getting a pet from Montevideo to Vienna involves more connections than most European routes. The typical structure is:

        Leg 1: MVD to a South American hub. The main options are Buenos Aires Ezeiza (EZE) in Argentina or São Paulo Guarulhos (GRU) in Brazil. Iberia and Air Europa fly MVD to MAD with connections via EZE. LATAM flies MVD to GRU and SCL (Santiago).

        Leg 2: South American hub to European hub. LATAM, Iberia, Air France, KLM, and Lufthansa all operate this transatlantic sector. Common European hubs are Frankfurt (FRA), Amsterdam (AMS), Paris (CDG), and Madrid (MAD).

        Leg 3: European hub to Vienna. From MAD, Austrian Airlines or Iberia cargo to VIE. From FRA, Austrian Airlines or Lufthansa cargo to VIE. From AMS, KLM to VIE. VIE is well connected from all major European hubs.

        Each cargo transfer requires pre-notification and live animal documentation at every stage. Using a specialist freight forwarder with experience on South American routes is strongly recommended.

    - heading: "Austrian AGES Requirements"
      body: |
        The Austrian Federal Agency for Food Safety (AGES, ages.at) and the Federal Ministry for Social Affairs, Health, Care and Consumer Protection (BMSGPK, sozialministerium.at) handle pet import inspections at Vienna International Airport (VIE).

        For a pet from Uruguay, AGES checks:

        Microchip: ISO 11784/11785, pre-vaccination. Scanned on arrival.

        Rabies vaccination: Current and post-microchip, 21 days elapsed since primary.

        FAVN titre test: 0.5 IU/ml or above, blood drawn 30+ days post-vaccination, 90-day wait from blood draw date elapsed.

        Health certificate: EU Annex IV format, DGSG-endorsed, issued within 10 days of travel. Titre test details fully documented.

        No quarantine. No import permit. Inspections are thorough, and documentation must be complete.

    - heading: "Planning the 20-26 Week Timeline"
      body: |
        The preparation timeline for Uruguay to Austria is the same as for other South American unlisted countries. The minimum time is determined by the titre test pathway:

        Week 1-2: Microchip implanted.
        Week 2-3: Rabies vaccination.
        Weeks 3-7: 30-day wait.
        Week 7: FAVN blood draw. Sample shipped to EU-approved laboratory.
        Weeks 7-10: Laboratory result received (allow 2-3 weeks for international shipment and analysis).
        Weeks 7-20: 90-day wait from blood draw date runs.
        Within 10 days of travel: DGSG-endorsed health certificate prepared.
        Travel: After the 90-day wait is complete.

        Add buffer time for potential titre test failure. Starting 26 weeks before your target arrival date in Austria gives you the most comfortable margin.

    - heading: "Crate Training for a Long Journey"
      body: |
        The MVD-to-VIE journey typically takes 30-40 hours from door to door, including cargo transfers at South American and European hubs. This is a long time for any animal.

        Start crate training as soon as you know your relocation is planned. Feed your pet inside the crate. Leave the crate open in your home as a regular sleeping space. Put a worn item of clothing inside to provide familiar scent.

        The IATA crate must be large enough for your pet to stand, turn, and lie down naturally. Measure carefully and buy the correct size. Too large and the animal slides around; too small and it is refused at check-in.

        Attach a water dispenser to the crate door and a food bag to the outside for handlers. Most airlines do not open the crate during transit.

faqs:
  - question: "Is the FAVN titre test result accepted in Austria if it was performed in Uruguay or in a third country?"
    answer: "The result is accepted if the laboratory is on the EU-approved list under Commission Decision 2000/258/EC. The country where the blood was drawn matters less than the laboratory that performed the test. Most Uruguayan veterinary laboratories are not on the EU-approved list. Your MGAP-registered vet will need to ship the blood sample to an approved laboratory outside Uruguay, such as ANSES in France, the AHVLA in the UK, or Kansas State University in the US."
  - question: "Can we fly our pet from Montevideo to Vienna via Brazil to reduce costs?"
    answer: "Routing via São Paulo (GRU) on LATAM or a partner carrier can be cost-effective and is commonly used. GRU is a major live animal cargo hub and connects to European hubs including FRA, AMS, and MAD. The routing is longer in terms of stops but is well established and reliable. Confirm live animal cargo acceptance on each specific leg with the carrier before booking."
  - question: "Does Austria impose breed restrictions that could block our dog's entry?"
    answer: "Austrian regulations restrict certain breeds under national law. Breeds classified as dangerous under Austrian legislation may face specific requirements such as liability insurance and registration, once in Austria. Check current AGES/BMSGPK guidance for your dog's breed before travel. These restrictions affect how the dog is kept in Austria rather than preventing entry through the airport, but it is important to be aware of them before you move."
