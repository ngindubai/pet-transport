# Pet Transport from the Dominican Republic to the Netherlands

The Dominican Republic is an easy holiday destination, but the EU pet import rules treat it as an unlisted third country. That classification triggers the most demanding set of import conditions the EU applies to pets: an FAVN titre test, a 90-day wait, and an EU-format health certificate endorsed by the Dominican authorities. If you are planning this move, start the process at least 20 to 26 weeks before your intended travel date.

## What "Unlisted Country" Means in Practice

The EU divides the world into listed and unlisted countries for the purpose of pet movement. Listed countries have been assessed as having rabies-control standards close to the EU's own. Unlisted countries, including the Dominican Republic, have not been through that assessment. The result is a stricter set of requirements:

| Requirement | Dominican Republic to Netherlands | Dominican Republic to Canada |
|---|---|---|
| Microchip before vaccination | Yes (mandatory) | Recommended |
| Rabies vaccination | Yes | Yes |
| FAVN titre test | Yes (min 0.5 IU/ml) | No |
| Wait period | 90 days from blood draw | None |
| Quarantine on arrival | No (if compliant) | No |
| Minimum preparation time | 20-26 weeks | 4-8 weeks |

The 90-day wait is not a quarantine period. Your pet stays with you during that time. But it does mean you cannot move quickly: 30 days between vaccination and blood draw, then 90 days from blood draw to entry, plus paperwork time on both sides.

## Step-by-Step Preparation

**Step 1: Microchip, then vaccinate (Week 1)**

Under EU Regulation 576/2013, the ISO 11784/11785 microchip must be implanted before the rabies vaccination. If a vet vaccinates first and fits the microchip afterwards, the EU considers the vaccination invalid. You would need to restart the entire process. Confirm the order with your vet before the appointment.

**Step 2: Wait 30 days, then draw blood (Week 5-6)**

Blood for the FAVN titre test must be drawn at least 30 days after the rabies vaccination. The sample goes to an EU-approved laboratory. Arrange international shipping of the blood sample with your vet if there is no approved lab in the Dominican Republic.

**Step 3: Receive your titre result (Week 7-9)**

The minimum passing result is 0.5 IU/ml. If your pet's result is below this, revaccinate and restart the 30-day wait before retesting. A failing result adds at least five to six weeks to your timeline.

**Step 4: Observe the 90-day wait (Weeks 6-20 from blood draw)**

The 90-day clock starts on the date the blood was drawn, not when you received the results. Note this carefully when working out your earliest possible travel date.

**Step 5: Prepare the EU health certificate and DSA endorsement (2-3 weeks before departure)**

A DSA-authorised veterinarian prepares the EU non-commercial movement health certificate. The DSA endorses it officially. Allow several working days for DSA endorsement, and be aware that EU health certificates have a validity window, so timing matters.

## Getting There: Airlines and Routing

**KLM** is the strongest option for this route. KLM operates services between Santo Domingo (SDQ) and Amsterdam Schiphol (AMS), with some services routing via Curaçao (CUR). KLM Cargo is experienced with live animal transport on this corridor. Book well in advance, particularly for larger breeds travelling in cargo.

**Iberia** via Madrid (MAD) is a practical alternative, connecting SDQ to Amsterdam with a stop in Spain.

**Air France** via Paris Charles de Gaulle (CDG) provides scheduling flexibility if neither KLM nor Iberia can accommodate your pet on your preferred dates.

## On Arrival in the Netherlands

Dutch authorities (NVWA) inspect animals at the port of entry. Provided your paperwork is complete and the 90-day wait has been observed, there is no quarantine. If any document is missing or the wait period has not been met, your pet may be held or refused entry. This is not a route where a paperwork issue can be resolved on the day.

Use a professional pet relocation agent if this is your first time managing EU unlisted-country import requirements. The FAVN titre test logistics, the sequencing of microchip and vaccination, and the DSA certificate process all have failure points that are easier to avoid than to recover from mid-journey.
