content:
  h1: "Pet Transport from the Bahamas to the United Kingdom"
  overview: |
    British Airways is the only airline that flies nonstop between Nassau and London, seven times a week on a Boeing 777, and that single route carries every pet moving from the Bahamas to Britain by air. It's the easiest flying leg of almost any long-haul pet move on this site. The paperwork is a different story: the Bahamas doesn't appear on either of GB's listed-country tables, so it's treated exactly like an unlisted origin, titre test, 3-month wait and all.

  sections:
    - heading: "The direct flight that almost makes this easy"
      body: |
        A nonstop, roughly nine-hour flight on a widebody aircraft is about as good as pet cargo routing gets. No transfer, no second airline, no interline handover to manage. IAG Cargo handles the booking at both ends, which keeps the logistics side of this move genuinely simple compared with most Caribbean or Central American routes.

        There's a catch worth planning around. IAG Cargo automatically pulls live-animal bookings when ground temperature at Nassau, Heathrow or any transit point sits outside a 7C to 29C band. Nassau regularly runs hotter than that for months at a stretch, so summer departures often need an early flight slot, not a random one.

    - heading: "Why 'unlisted' still applies to a Commonwealth country"
      body: |
        It surprises a lot of owners that a former British colony, still a Commonwealth realm, sits outside GB's Part 1 and Part 2 listed-country tables entirely. The classification is about rabies risk assessment under retained EU law, not political or historical ties, and the Bahamas has simply never been added to either list.

        The practical result: a rabies antibody titre test, blood drawn at least 30 days after vaccination, followed by a 3-calendar-month wait measured from the draw date. Add the vet visits and the certificate turnaround, and 4 to 5 months from a standing start is realistic, even with the flight itself taking less than a day.

    - heading: "BAHFSA's side of the paperwork"
      body: |
        The Bahamas Agricultural Health and Food Safety Authority runs both the import ePermit system (well documented, USD 11.20 processing fee) and export certification for animals leaving Nassau. The export side has less publicly available detail.

        A Bahamian-registered vet issues the health certificate confirming rabies immunity and fitness to fly. Whether BAHFSA itself needs to add a government endorsement stamp for a UK-bound pet isn't spelled out in the authority's public guidance, so it's worth a direct call before assuming either way.
