  h1: "Pet Transport from Canada to Nicaragua"
  overview: |
    A CFIA endorsement stamp costs CAD 20 to 40 and is usually turned around within about two business days. That's the cheapest, fastest single step on this whole route. IPSA doesn't ask a Canadian-origin pet for a rabies titre test either, so the paperwork on both ends is genuinely light.

    What takes the time is the flight. Nothing connects Toronto or Montreal to Managua without a stop, so the real planning work is picking a routing, Miami, Houston or Panama City, and booking cargo space on both legs.

  sections:
    - heading: "What CFIA actually requires before a pet leaves Canada"
      body: |
        There's no general export permit to apply for. A licensed Canadian vet completes an international health certificate, listing the animal's identifying details and vaccination history, then that certificate goes to the nearest CFIA office for an official endorsement stamp. CFIA typically turns endorsed forms around within about 2 business days, whether the paperwork is dropped off in person or mailed with a return envelope.

        That mail option matters if you're nowhere near a CFIA office. Build the transit time into your schedule rather than assuming the 2-day figure covers the whole process door to door.

    - heading: "Nicaragua's light-touch entry rules"
      body: |
        IPSA doesn't run a country-risk classification system the way MOCCAE, GOV.UK or the EU do, so there's no titre test to schedule and no waiting period tied to a blood draw. A current rabies vaccination, no more than 365 days old at shipment, and the CFIA-endorsed certificate are what IPSA's general requirements ask for.

        Nicaragua calls its entry document a quarantine permit, a name that understandably worries some owners. It's paperwork completed at the port of entry, not a stay in kennels, and it applies the same way whichever country the pet is arriving from.

    - heading: "No direct flight: three realistic routings"
      body: |
        American Airlines via Miami is the most established path, pairing a Toronto or Montreal connection with American's daily Miami-Managua non-stop. United's Houston gateway is a solid alternative when a Miami connection doesn't fit your departure city.

        Copa Airlines via Panama City avoids US transit entirely, which some owners prefer, but its cargo desk only accepts bookings Monday to Thursday, so it needs a mid-week arrival date. Whichever routing you choose, confirm live-animal acceptance on both legs separately. A through-ticket for the passenger doesn't guarantee a through-booking for the pet.
