  sections:
    - heading: "No titre test required: Jamaica's listed status"
      body: |
        Norway applies EU pet travel rules through the EEA Agreement. EU Implementing Regulation 2026/636 classifies Jamaica as a listed third country, meaning pets from Jamaica are exempt from the FAVN rabies titre test and the 90-day waiting period that applies to unlisted countries.

        The practical effect is a much shorter preparation timeline: 4-8 weeks rather than the 20-26 weeks required for unlisted Caribbean origins. The core requirements for Norway are an ISO microchip, current rabies and DHLPP vaccinations, the VSD export health certificate and permit, and for dogs, tapeworm treatment within the 24-120 hour window before arrival.

    - heading: "Tapeworm treatment and advance notification for Norway"
      body: |
        Mattilsynet (the Norwegian Food Safety Authority) requires all dogs entering Norway to receive treatment against Echinococcus multilocularis using praziquantel (minimum 2.5 mg/kg body weight) between 24 and 120 hours before arrival at Oslo Gardermoen. The treating vet must record the product name, dose, exact date, and exact time in the health certificate.

        The timing of the tapeworm treatment relative to your flight schedule is critical. If the treatment is administered fewer than 24 hours or more than 120 hours before the actual arrival time in Norway, the requirement is not met and Mattilsynet can refuse entry. Plan this carefully with your vet and cargo agent.

        Norway also requires 48-hour advance notification before non-EEA pets arrive at OSL. Use the Mattilsynet online notification portal or contact the Border Control Post at Oslo Gardermoen directly. Provide the pet's details, flight number, and certificate information. Cats are not subject to the tapeworm requirement.
