  h1: "Pet Transport India to Denmark: AQCS Certificate, FAVN Titre Test and the 90-Day Wait"
  overview: |
    India to Denmark is one of the most process-intensive pet transport routes. India is not on the EU's approved list of third countries with equivalent rabies status, which means a FAVN rabies titre test and a mandatory 90-day wait from the blood draw date are required before a pet can enter Denmark or any EU member state. Add in the AQCS health certificate and export clearance process in India, and the minimum timeline is around 5 to 6 months from the first vaccination. Emirates via Dubai is the most commonly used cargo routing for this corridor.

    - heading: "The FAVN titre test and 90-day wait explained"
      body: |
        The EU requires pets entering from unlisted third countries (including India) to have a valid FAVN (Fluorescent Antibody Virus Neutralisation) titre test result of at least 0.5 IU/ml. The blood for the test must be drawn at least 30 days after the pet's primary rabies vaccination. The test must be carried out at an EU-approved laboratory.

        Once the blood is drawn (and assuming the result meets the threshold), the pet must wait 90 days from the blood draw date before it can enter Denmark or any other EU member state. This wait period is fixed and cannot be shortened. If the titre test result is below the 0.5 IU/ml threshold, the process must restart with a new vaccination and blood draw.

        Ensure the AQCS-endorsed health certificate lists the microchip number, the titre test result, the blood draw date, the laboratory name, and the full vaccination history.
