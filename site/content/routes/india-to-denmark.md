faqs:
  - question: "Does Denmark require tapeworm treatment for pets from India?"
    answer: "No. The echinococcus tapeworm treatment with praziquantel (administered 24 to 120 hours before entry) is only required for dogs entering Finland, Malta, Ireland, and Norway. Denmark does not have this requirement. For India to Denmark, the requirements are microchip, rabies vaccination, FAVN titre test (0.5 IU/ml), 90-day wait, and EU AHC."
  - question: "Which dog breeds are banned in Denmark?"
    answer: "Denmark bans 13 breeds under the Danish Dog Act: Pit Bull Terrier, Tosa Inu, American Staffordshire Terrier, Fila Brasileiro, Dogo Argentino, American Bulldog, Boerboel, Kangal, Central Asian Shepherd, Caucasian Shepherd, South Russian Shepherd, Tornjak, and Sarplaninac. Mixes of these breeds are also restricted. Check before booking if your dog is one of these breeds or a possible mix."
  - question: "Can my pet enter Denmark from India without an agent?"
    answer: "In principle, yes, but the AQCS inspection in India, the cargo booking, and the TRACES pre-notification for Denmark all involve specific processes. Most owners relocating with pets from India to Denmark use a pet transport agent at least for the cargo and border inspection stages. The FAVN titre test laboratory selection and 90-day tracking are manageable independently with careful organisation."
  - question: "How does the AQCS inspection work in India?"
    answer: "The Animal Quarantine and Certification Services (AQCS) operates inspection offices at Indira Gandhi International (DEL), Chhatrapati Shivaji Maharaj International (BOM), and Chennai International (MAA). On the day of departure, the AQCS officer checks all documents, inspects the pet, and issues an NOC (No Objection Certificate) on the spot. Arrive at the cargo terminal well in advance to allow time for this step."
