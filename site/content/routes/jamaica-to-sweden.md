  sections:
    - heading: "Jamaica's listed status and what it means for Sweden entry"
      body: |
        EU Implementing Regulation 2026/636 classifies third countries as listed or unlisted based on their animal disease control status. Listed countries are exempt from the FAVN rabies titre test requirement. Jamaica is classified as a listed third country, which removes the titre test and the associated 90-day waiting period from the preparation timeline.

        For Sweden, this means the practical preparation is: confirm microchip, ensure rabies and DHLPP vaccinations are current, obtain the VSD export permit and health certificate, and travel. Jordbruksverket (the Swedish Board of Agriculture) still requires the standard EU-format non-commercial health certificate and entry through a designated Border Control Post at Arlanda (ARN) or Landvetter (GOT).

        Allow 4-8 weeks from starting the paperwork to travel day. The VSD permit process takes 4-6 weeks; the health certificate is issued within 10 days of travel.

    - heading: "VSD export process and airline options from Jamaica"
      body: |
        The Veterinary Services Division (VSD) within MICAF (Ministry of Industry, Commerce, Agriculture and Fisheries) handles companion animal exports from Jamaica. The process requires a VSD-registered official veterinarian to examine your pet and complete the health certificate. An export permit is also required, obtained from VSD offices with an approximate fee of J$2,000.

        DHLPP vaccination (distemper, hepatitis, leptospirosis, parvovirus, parainfluenza) is required by VSD in addition to the standard rabies vaccination. Confirm both are current before booking the certificate appointment.

        No direct flight operates from Jamaica to Sweden. British Airways connects KIN and MBJ to London Heathrow (LHR), from where SAS and British Airways serve ARN and GOT. KLM connections via Amsterdam (AMS) are also an option, routing through North American hubs. Confirm live animal cargo acceptance on all legs before booking.

    answer: "The Veterinary Services Division (VSD) is part of Jamaica's Ministry of Industry, Commerce, Agriculture and Fisheries (MICAF). It oversees the export of companion animals from Jamaica. The VSD issues an export permit (fee approximately J$2,000) and authorises the official health certificate issued by a VSD-registered veterinarian. Both documents must accompany the pet."

    answer: "Non-EU pets must enter Sweden through a designated Border Control Post: Arlanda Airport (ARN, Stockholm) or Landvetter Airport (GOT, Gothenburg). No other Swedish airport accepts non-EU pet arrivals. If your routing connects through another EU country first, the entry checks may happen at that EU entry point instead."
  - question: "What vaccinations does the VSD require for export from Jamaica?"
    answer: "The VSD requires a current rabies vaccination and DHLPP vaccination (distemper, hepatitis, leptospirosis, parvovirus, parainfluenza) for companion animal exports. Both must be recorded in the health certificate. Confirm with your VSD-registered vet that all vaccinations are current and properly documented before the certificate appointment."
