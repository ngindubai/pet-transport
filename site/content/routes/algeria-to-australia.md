content:
  h1: "Pet Transport from Algeria to Australia"
  overview: |
    Air France's Algiers to Paris route already turns up as the busiest link out of North Africa on this site's Algeria-to-US page. For an Australia-bound pet, that same flight becomes the first step of something bigger: Algeria is a DAFF non-approved country, so 180 consecutive days of residency in an approved country are compulsory, and France, Group 3 approved, is the natural place to spend them.

    Nothing about that residency period is optional or negotiable. A titre test still has to clear at a French lab during the stay, and Mickleham's 10-day quarantine still sits at the very end, weekday arrivals only.

  sections:
    - heading: "Why can't a pet fly straight from Algiers to Australia?"
      body: |
        DAFF places every country into Group 1, 2, 3, or non-approved, and Algeria sits in the last bucket along with most of Africa. A direct booking doesn't exist at any price. The animal has to live in an approved country for 180 unbroken days first, and only then does DAFF begin processing the actual import.

        It's a fixed biosecurity period, not red tape that a good agent can shortcut.

    - heading: "Why is France the obvious place to spend that time?"
      body: |
        Because owners moving a pet out of Algeria are likely booking the Algiers-Paris route regardless. Air France runs it as one of its busiest North African connections, and this site's own Algeria-to-US page already flags it as one of only two realistic hub options for that separate journey.

        France is also DAFF Group 3 approved, so the 180-day clock can start the moment the pet lands at Charles de Gaulle, no separate long-haul flight required just to reach an approved country. That's a genuine logistical head start compared with routes that need a dedicated detour to somewhere unfamiliar.

    - heading: "What still has to happen during the 180 days?"
      body: |
        A rabies titre test, drawn at least 30 days after vaccination and run at an accredited French laboratory, is non-negotiable. Once it passes, apply for the DAFF import permit through BICON and lock in a Mickleham quarantine slot before booking the final flight.

        For the Australia-bound leg itself, Emirates flies Paris to Dubai with direct onward services to Sydney, Melbourne, Perth, Brisbane and Adelaide, giving the French residency a clean single-region finish. Qatar Airways via Doha is worth pricing alongside it.
