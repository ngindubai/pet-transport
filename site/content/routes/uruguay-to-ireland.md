content:
  h1: "Pet Transport from Uruguay to Ireland: 2026 Guide"
  overview: |
    Ireland's tapeworm rule catches more owners off guard than any other requirement on this route, because it is not reserved for high-risk countries the way the titre test is. Dogs entering Ireland from anywhere except Finland, Malta, Norway or Northern Ireland need the praziquantel treatment, timed to a 24-to-120-hour window before arrival, and Uruguay does not qualify for an exemption.

    Layer that on top of the standard unlisted-country process, since Uruguay carries no EU rabies-control equivalence, and a Montevideo-to-Dublin move needs the full sequence: microchip, vaccination, FAVN titre test, 90-day wait, tapeworm treatment, and an advance notice filing with DAFM before the flight lands.

  sections:
    - heading: "Why the tapeworm rule applies here and not on most other Irish routes"
      body: |
        Ireland has kept Echinococcus multilocularis out of its dog population, and DAFM protects that status by requiring the treatment from almost every arriving country. Only Finland, Malta, Norway and Northern Ireland are exempt, because they share the same disease-free status. Uruguay is not on that list, so every dog travelling this route needs praziquantel given 24 to 120 hours before landing in Dublin, recorded by name, dose and date on the health certificate.

        Cats are not covered by this rule. A cat travelling from Uruguay follows the standard microchip, vaccination and titre-test sequence without the additional treatment.
    - heading: "The 90-day titre wait and the DAFM Advance Notice Portal"
      body: |
        Uruguay's unassessed rabies-control status means the FAVN or ELISA titre test applies in full: blood drawn at least 30 days after vaccination, a result of 0.5 IU/ml or above, then a 90-day wait from the draw date before your pet can enter Ireland.

        Separately, DAFM requires an Advance Notice Portal filing at least 24 hours before your flight lands at Dublin Airport. This is not the same document as the health certificate and is easy to overlook if you are focused on the titre test timeline. Set a reminder for this step once your flight is booked, not when it is confirmed on the day.
