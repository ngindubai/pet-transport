  sections:
    - heading: "FAVN titre test and the 90-day wait"
      body: |
        Ireland applies EU Implementing Regulation 2026/636 in full. Uruguay is classified as an unlisted third country, making the FAVN titre test mandatory. Blood must be drawn at least 30 days after the most recent rabies vaccination. The result must show at least 0.5 IU/ml at an EU-approved laboratory. The 90-day waiting period runs from the blood draw date.

        After the 90 days, the MGAP-accredited vet issues the CVI and MGAP-DGSG endorses it. Allow 1-2 working days for the endorsement step within the 10-day certificate window. The FAVN titre result must be included in the CVI. For dogs, the tapeworm treatment must also be recorded before MGAP-DGSG endorses the document.

    - heading: "Tapeworm treatment, DAFM notification, and Dublin entry"
      body: |
        Ireland requires all dogs to receive praziquantel tapeworm treatment between 24 hours and 5 days before arrival. The vet records the product name, dose, date, and exact time in the CVI. The window is measured against actual arrival at the Irish BCP. Cats are not subject to this requirement.

        DAFM requires 24-hour advance notification via agriculture.gov.ie. Submit the notification and confirm receipt before travel. Dublin Airport (DUB) is the primary BCP; Shannon (SNN) and Cork (ORK) have more restricted coverage hours. Iberia and LATAM connections from Montevideo (MVD) via Madrid (MAD) are the most practical European hub option, with onward connections from Madrid to Dublin.
