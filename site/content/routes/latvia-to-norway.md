  sections:
    - heading: "The Tapeworm Rule for Dogs Travelling to Norway"
      body: |
        Norway requires all dogs entering the country from outside Norway to be treated against tapeworm (Echinococcus multilocularis) before arrival. The drug is Praziquantel. The treatment must be given by a veterinarian between 1 and 5 days before you arrive in Norway, and the vet must record it in your EU pet passport with the date, product name, dose, and their stamp and signature.

        This rule applies even though Latvia is an EU listed country. It is not waived for vaccinated or healthy dogs. Book a vet appointment in Riga a day or two before your flight, confirm the entry is recorded correctly in the passport, and you are compliant. Cats are not subject to the tapeworm rule.

    - heading: "Flying from Riga to Oslo: Airline Options"
      body: |
        airBaltic operates direct flights from Riga (RIX) to Oslo Gardermoen (OSL) and is the most convenient option for most pet owners. Small dogs and cats can travel in cabin with prior booking. Larger dogs travel as cargo. Contact airBaltic's live animal desk at least 4 to 6 weeks before travel to confirm availability and current size limits.

        SAS connects Latvia to Oslo via Copenhagen or Stockholm and offers cargo capacity through SAS Cargo for larger pets. Norwegian Air Shuttle and LOT Polish Airlines are alternatives, though Norwegian does not take cargo animals. Whichever airline you choose, notify them at booking that you are travelling with a live animal.
