  sections:
    - heading: "The unlisted country process in plain terms"
      body: |
        France, like all EU member states, applies EU Regulation 2020/692 to incoming pets from non-EU countries. Countries that have undergone a formal equivalence assessment and meet EU standards are on the listed country register. Georgia is not on this register.

        This means any dog or cat moving from Georgia to France must satisfy the full unlisted requirements. The microchip must be implanted first, before any rabies vaccination. The vaccination must be given and at least 30 days must pass before the blood is drawn for the FAVN titre test. The test result must be at least 0.5 IU/ml. Then a mandatory 90-day wait must pass from the blood draw date before your pet may enter France.

        There is no mechanism to speed this up. The 90-day wait is set in EU law and exists to verify that the immune response is established. No French authority has discretion to waive it.

    - heading: "Paris CDG as the French entry point"
      body: |
        Paris Charles de Gaulle (CDG) is France's primary international airport for pet cargo. It handles a large volume of live animal imports and has dedicated cargo facilities for this traffic. French border inspection takes place at CDG on arrival, and for pets with correct documentation this is a routine administrative check.

        From Tbilisi, the most practical routing is Turkish Airlines via Istanbul (IST) to CDG. Turkish Airlines Cargo is familiar with this corridor and IST is one of the main hubs for live animal freight from the Caucasus region. Air France connections are another option, though these typically also involve a hub stop.

        If your French destination is not Paris, an onward domestic connection from CDG or an alternative entry via Lyon (LYS) or Marseille (MRS) may reduce total transit time. Confirm cargo routing with your agent before booking.

  - question: "What is the DGAL and why does it matter for my pet's entry to France?"
    answer: "DGAL stands for Direction générale de l'alimentation, the French government's food safety and animal health directorate within the Ministry of Agriculture and Food Sovereignty. It is the competent authority that oversees animal import rules in France. At CDG, SIVEP (border inspection posts) operates under DGAL oversight. For your pet, DGAL compliance means the EU-format health certificate from your Georgian vet and NFA endorsement are the documents that need to be correct."
