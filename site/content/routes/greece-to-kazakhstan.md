    - heading: "The KVCS permit: start earlier than you think you need to"
      body: |
        The Committee for Veterinary Control and Supervision (KVCS) in Kazakhstan does not have the same online presence or responsiveness as some other national veterinary authorities in this region. Start the permit application at least ten to twelve weeks before your travel date, and follow up regularly rather than waiting for a response.

        The application typically requires your pet's species, breed, age, microchip number, vaccination records, and intended entry point, either Almaty or Astana. Confirm which airport matches your actual final destination in Kazakhstan before applying, since the two cities are roughly 1,200 kilometres apart.

    - heading: "Athens to Almaty or Astana via Istanbul"
      body: |
        Turkish Airlines is the dominant carrier on this corridor, connecting easily from Athens through Istanbul with onward service to either Almaty or Astana. Air Astana, Kazakhstan's national carrier, also serves several European hubs, though its live animal cargo policy needs direct confirmation before you rely on it.

        Total transit from Athens typically runs nine to thirteen hours including the Istanbul connection. On arrival, the KVCS carries out a customs and documentation inspection rather than a lengthy quarantine process for a compliant, well-documented pet.
