  overview: |
    Estonia to Lebanon is a manageable cargo route with no quarantine for compliant arrivals and no titre test required for EU-origin pets. The requirements are an ISO microchip implanted before vaccination, a rabies vaccination within the 30-day to 12-month window, current core vaccinations, parasite treatment records, and an official health certificate from a Pohllumajandus- ja Toiduamet-authorised vet.

  sections:
    - heading: "Estonian export certificate and Lebanon entry conditions"
      body: |
        Estonia is an EU member state and issues official veterinary health certificates through vets authorised by Pohllumajandus- ja Toiduamet (the Agricultural Board). The EU pet passport is not a valid travel document for Lebanon, so a formal health certificate covering the animal's microchip, rabies vaccination, additional vaccinations, and parasite treatment history is required.

        Lebanon's Ministry of Agriculture requires: an ISO-compliant microchip (implanted before any vaccination), a current rabies vaccination (30 days to 12 months before entry), distemper, hepatitis, parvovirus, and leptospirosis for dogs, and panleukopenia, rhinotracheitis, and calicivirus for cats. Internal and external parasite treatment records are also required. No quarantine applies for compliant arrivals. No titre test is required for EU-origin pets. Confirm requirements directly with the Lebanese Ministry of Agriculture, as they can be updated.

    - heading: "Cargo from Tallinn to Beirut"
      body: |
        Tallinn Airport (TLL) does not have direct cargo connections to Beirut-Rafic Hariri International Airport (BEY). The main routing is via Istanbul Airport (IST) with Turkish Cargo, which operates regular live animal services between Istanbul and Beirut. Connect from Tallinn to Istanbul via Turkish Airlines or via a connecting carrier such as airBaltic to Frankfurt or Amsterdam, then onward to Istanbul.

        Middle East Airlines (MEA) also serves Beirut from several European cities including Frankfurt and Amsterdam, and is a second option from a European hub. Book through a cargo agent and confirm live animal acceptance on all segments before setting your travel date. Journey times from Tallinn to Beirut including connections are typically 10-16 hours.
