  sections:
    - heading: "Two direct carriers compared: Air Mauritius vs British Airways"
      body: |
        Unlike many unlisted countries that require multiple transits to reach the UK, Mauritius has direct flights to London Heathrow. Air Mauritius, the national carrier, accepts pets as cargo on the MRU-LHR route. British Airways operates the same route and also accepts pets via BA World Cargo.

        Both are practical and avoid the complexity of hub routing through Dubai (summer heat embargoes), Johannesburg, or other transits. For a pet that already has a long titre test waiting period to complete, having a direct flight option at the end of the process is a genuine advantage.

        Emirates via Dubai is an alternative if the direct options are unavailable, but summer travel should avoid the Dubai transit given Emirates SkyCargo's June to September heat embargoes.

    - heading: "FAVN titre test logistics from Mauritius"
      body: |
        The FAVN titre test requires blood to be drawn at least 30 days after the primary rabies vaccination, then sent to an APHA-approved laboratory for analysis. The laboratory checks the blood sample for rabies antibodies and reports a result of 0.5 IU/ml or above (pass) or below (fail).

        From Mauritius, sample shipping to an APHA-approved lab requires coordinating with your vet on the logistics: the blood must be stored and shipped correctly to preserve viability. Some APHA-approved labs in Europe accept shipped samples from Mauritius regularly. Your vet may have an established relationship with one, or can advise on sample shipping.

        If the titre test result passes, the 90-day waiting period begins from the date of the successful blood draw. The animal cannot travel to the UK until those 90 days are complete.
