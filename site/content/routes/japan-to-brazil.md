  sections:
    - heading: "The MAPA permit and certificate: Brazil's specific requirements"
      body: |
        Brazil's Ministry of Agriculture, Livestock and Food Supply (MAPA) controls all animal imports through a permit and certificate system. For companion pet imports, the owner (or a Brazilian agent acting on their behalf) must obtain an import permit from MAPA before the official health certificate is issued in Japan.

        The certificate itself must follow MAPA's trilingual model: Portuguese, English, and the language of the origin country (in this case, Japanese). Standard veterinary health certificate templates issued in Japan will not be accepted unless they match this format. Your vet in Japan and/or a MAPA-experienced agent should know which template to use.

        MAPA also requires proof of antiparasitic treatment within 15 days of the certificate's issuance date, and a valid rabies vaccination even though Japan is officially rabies-free. These requirements apply to Japan just as they do to any other country.

    - heading: "Routing Japan to Brazil: the practicalities"
      body: |
        There are no direct Japan-to-Brazil flights. The common routing options are:
        via Frankfurt (Lufthansa Group carriers to GRU);
        via Dubai (Emirates to GRU);
        via the US (JAL to JFK or LAX, then LATAM or another carrier to GRU);
        via Seoul (Korean Air to GRU via connections).

        The Frankfurt routing is popular because Lufthansa and LATAM have an established freight partnership for Latin American routes. The total journey from Tokyo to Sao Paulo is typically 24-36 hours depending on layover times.

        For your pet's welfare, choose a routing with the shortest possible hub layover. A 2-3 hour transit at Frankfurt is preferable to a 10-hour overnight hold at a secondary hub. Book the cargo space across all legs at the same time, confirming that live animal cargo is accepted on every segment.

    - heading: "AQS inspection at Tokyo Narita"
      body: |
        The MAFF Animal Quarantine Service (AQS) inspection is the Japan-side export step. You apply via the NACCS online system at least 10 days before your preferred inspection date. The inspection itself takes 30-60 minutes at the airport cargo facility.

        AQS checks: ISO microchip readable by scanner, rabies vaccination current, pet appears healthy and free from signs of infectious disease. The officer issues the export quarantine certificate. This goes alongside your MAPA-compliant health certificate when you travel.

        Most international vets in Tokyo and Osaka know the AQS process well. If you are based in another city, a Tokyo-based pet transport agent can coordinate both the AQS inspection and the MAPA certificate preparation.

    answer: "No. MAPA requires a valid rabies vaccination certificate regardless of the origin country's rabies status. Your pet needs a vaccination within the past 12 months to enter Brazil from Japan. Carry the original vaccination record."
  - question: "What is the MAPA import permit and how do I get it?"
    answer: "The MAPA import permit is an official Brazilian government authorisation to import a companion animal. Apply through MAPA's SINDI (Integrated Foreign Trade System in Agriculture) or via a licensed Brazilian agricultural importer or agent. Processing typically takes 2-4 weeks. You cannot finalise the health certificate in Japan until the permit number is in hand."

    answer: "Major Brazilian international airports with MAPA inspection facilities include Sao Paulo Guarulhos (GRU), Rio de Janeiro Galeao (GIG), and Brasilia (BSB). Confirm that your chosen arrival airport has MAPA live animal inspection capacity before booking your final destination."
