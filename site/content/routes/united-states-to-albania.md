content:
  h1: "Pet Transport from the United States to Albania"
  overview: |
    Albania's Prime Minister has talked publicly about a Delta Air Lines nonstop between Tirana and New York launching in 2026. Whether that flight is actually confirmed or still being negotiated depends on which report you read, and Delta's own published summer 2026 schedule does not carry Tirana. For anyone planning a pet move now, the practical answer is the same either way: book through Istanbul or Frankfurt, and treat any future direct option as a bonus rather than the plan.

    The paperwork side is genuinely light. A US pet gets treated as coming from a rabies-controlled country, so the microchip and a 21-day-old rabies vaccination clear the bar without a blood test, and there's no import permit to apply for at all.

  sections:
    - heading: "A flight that might not exist yet"
      body: |
        Search "Tirana New York Delta" and you'll find a 2026 wire report headlined as a correction: an earlier story calling the route confirmed was walked back to "Delta Air Lines in talks to launch," not "to launch." A separate Albanian outlet still describes the deal as sealed. Both can't be fully right at once.

        What's not in dispute is Delta's actual published transatlantic map for summer 2026, and Tirana isn't on it. Air Transat's seasonal Toronto-Tirana service remains the only nonstop between North America and Albania as things stand. A US pet owner should plan around Istanbul or Frankfurt now and treat a direct New York option as something to check for later, not something to book against today.

    - heading: "Why no titre test is needed here"
      body: |
        Albania's own guidance splits pets into two groups: those arriving from, or transiting only through, a country on the EU's Annex II list of low-rabies-risk states, and everyone else. The first group needs a current rabies vaccination and nothing more on the antibody front. The second needs a titre test.

        The United States sits in the first group, which is why this route skips the blood draw that trips up so many unlisted-origin moves elsewhere on this site. The catch is the word "transiting." Route a pet through a hub outside that Annex II list and the exemption doesn't automatically survive the connection, so it's worth checking the specific transit airport against the list before booking, not just assuming the US departure point settles the question.

    - heading: "What the health certificate actually needs to show"
      body: |
        A USDA-accredited vet completes the certificate, and the USDA APHIS State Veterinarian's office endorses it, typically within three to five business days. That endorsed document then has to be dated within 10 days of Albania's own health exam window on arrival, so the vet appointment needs to sit close to the flight date, not weeks ahead of it.

        There's no separate Albanian import permit application to run in parallel, which keeps this route noticeably simpler on paper than several of Albania's other US-facing neighbours. AKU's port officials check the microchip, the vaccination date and the certificate together on landing at Tirana.
