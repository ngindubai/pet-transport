  sections:
    - heading: "Panama's requirements for pets from the Netherlands"
      body: |
        Panama's MIDA (Ministerio de Desarrollo Agropecuario, mida.gob.pa) governs pet imports. For pets arriving from the Netherlands, the requirements are: ISO 11784/11785 microchip; rabies vaccination administered between 30 days and 12 months before arrival; pet must be over 4 months old; official health certificate in English or Spanish, endorsed by the NVWA and authenticated by the Panamanian Embassy in the Netherlands.

        No import permit is needed. No quarantine for compliant arrivals. A MIDA inspector at Tocumen International Airport (PTY) checks the documents on arrival and collects a USD 20 inspection fee.

    - heading: "The Embassy authentication step"
      body: |
        Panama requires the health certificate to be authenticated by the Panamanian Embassy in the country of origin. For the Netherlands, this is the Panamanian Embassy in The Hague. Authentication confirms that the NVWA endorsement is genuine.

        The official veterinarian prepares the health certificate in English or Spanish. The NVWA endorses it. You then present the endorsed certificate to the Panamanian Embassy in The Hague for authentication. Contact the Embassy in advance to confirm their requirements, fees, and turnaround time. Plan this step within the 10-day validity window of the health certificate.

    - heading: "Routing from Amsterdam to Panama City"
      body: |
        Amsterdam Schiphol (AMS) is KLM's main hub with strong transatlantic connections. The most practical routing is AMS to Miami (MIA) via KLM Cargo, then MIA to Tocumen Airport (PTY) via Copa Airlines. Copa is Panama's national carrier and operates frequent services from MIA to PTY.

        An alternative goes via Madrid (MAD) with Iberia and Copa codeshares. Confirm live animal acceptance on each segment before booking. The AMS-MIA-PTY routing typically has good live animal cargo availability.

    - heading: "NVWA export endorsement from the Netherlands"
      body: |
        The NVWA (Nederlandse Voedsel- en Warenautoriteit, nvwa.nl) endorses official export health certificates for pets leaving the Netherlands for non-EU destinations. An official veterinarian prepares the certificate in the format and language required by Panama. The NVWA reviews and endorses it.

        Allow three to five business days for the NVWA endorsement. The health certificate must be issued within 10 days of travel. After the NVWA endorses it, take it to the Panamanian Embassy in The Hague for authentication before departure.
