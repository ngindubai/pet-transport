  sections:
    - heading: "The tapeworm rule Norway adds for dogs"
      body: |
        This is the fact that trips people up most on this route. Norway requires tapeworm treatment for every dog entering the country from outside Norway, and it makes no difference whether the dog is coming from an EU member state, an unlisted country like Lebanon, or anywhere else. The treatment has to use Praziquantel, given by a vet between 1 and 5 days before arrival, no earlier and no later.

        Too early and it doesn't count: if the treatment happens 6 or more days before arrival, Norwegian border checks expect a second dose closer to travel. The vet records the drug, dose and date directly in the health certificate. Cats are exempt from this specific rule, but everything else in the unlisted-country sequence still applies to them.

        Because this rule is separate from, and additional to, the microchip, vaccination and titre test sequence, it's easy to plan the whole trip around the 90-day wait and forget the tapeworm window entirely. Build it into your final week before travel, not as an afterthought.

    - heading: "Microchip, vaccination, titre test, 90-day wait"
      body: |
        Lebanon is not on the EU's approved country list, and Norway, though outside the EU, applies equivalent rules through the EEA Agreement. That means the same sequence as everywhere else in this guide: microchip first, rabies vaccination after, then a minimum 30-day wait before blood is drawn for a titre test at an approved laboratory. A result of at least 0.5 IU/ml starts a 90-day countdown from the draw date, and only once that's finished can the pet legally enter Norway. Add Lebanese export processing on top and 20 to 26 weeks is realistic.

    - heading: "Flying from Beirut to Oslo"
      body: |
        Lufthansa Cargo via Frankfurt is generally the strongest option for Scandinavian destinations, with the added benefit of the Frankfurt Animal Lounge for the connection. Turkish Airlines Cargo via Istanbul is a reliable backup with good frequency into Oslo. Qatar Airways Cargo via Doha and Emirates SkyCargo via Dubai both serve the route as well, though both add transit distance, and Doha can face summer heat restrictions on live animal cargo.
