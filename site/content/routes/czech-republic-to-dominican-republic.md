  h1: "Pet Transport from Czech Republic to Dominican Republic"
  overview: |
    Moving a pet from the Czech Republic to the Dominican Republic is a moderate-complexity international relocation. There is no quarantine on arrival, which keeps timelines manageable, but the sequence of vaccinations, official veterinary certification, and SVS endorsement must be followed in strict order. Most dogs and cats travelling from Prague (PRG) reach Santo Domingo (SDQ) on a cargo routing via Madrid, Paris, or Amsterdam.

    The Dominican Republic's import rules are administered by the DGAG (Direccion General de Ganaderia). They require a valid microchip, a rabies vaccination given between 30 days and 12 months before entry, and an official health certificate issued within 15 days of travel. Dogs also need current DHPP coverage; cats need panleukopenia, rhinotracheitis, and calicivirus. Up to five personal pets may enter without an import permit.

    On the Czech side, only an SVS-authorised official vet can issue the EU-format health certificate used for this route, and the SVS (Statni veterinarni sprava) must endorse it before departure. The certificate is valid for 10 days from signing, so appointment timing matters. Allow at least four weeks from start to finish, and ideally six if any vaccinations still need to be given.

  sections:
    - heading: "Czech Export Requirements"
      body: |
        The Czech Republic sits inside the EU, so its export process follows the standard EU pet travel framework. You do not need an export permit. What you do need is an EU-format official health certificate signed by an SVS-authorised official vet and then endorsed by the SVS (Statni veterinarni sprava, svscr.cz) before you travel. The certificate is valid for 10 days from the date of the vet's signature, which means the endorsement and travel must both happen within that window.

        Finding an SVS-authorised official vet in advance is worth doing early. Not all private clinics hold SVS authorisation, and appointments during busy periods can fill up quickly. Vaclav Havel Airport Prague (PRG) is the departure point for all international cargo from this origin. If you are using a pet transport agent, they can usually arrange the official vet appointment and coordinate the SVS endorsement on your behalf.

    - heading: "Dominican Republic Import Rules"
      body: |
        DGAG inspectors at Las Americas International Airport (SDQ) check four things: a valid ISO microchip, a rabies vaccination record showing the jab was given at least 30 days but no more than 12 months before the entry date, an official health certificate issued within 15 days of travel, and current core vaccinations. For dogs that means DHPP (distemper, adenovirus, parainfluenza, parvovirus). For cats it means panleukopenia, rhinotracheitis, and calicivirus.

        There is no quarantine, and no import permit is needed for personal pets, provided you are travelling with five or fewer animals. Inspection at SDQ is generally straightforward once the paperwork is in order. All documents should be in English or Spanish, or accompanied by a certified translation.

    - heading: "Airline Cargo Options from Prague"
      body: |
        Prague's main cargo connections to the Caribbean run through Madrid (Iberia), Paris CDG (Air France), and Amsterdam (KLM). All three airlines accept pets as manifest cargo, subject to crate size and weight limits. You will need an IATA-compliant crate with adequate ventilation, a water container, and absorbent bedding. Cargo booking must be made well in advance of travel, and the airline's live animal desk should confirm acceptance of your specific animal and crate before you commit to the booking.

        Costs vary significantly by animal size. A small dog or cat in a compact crate typically falls in the lower part of the EUR 700-1,800 range; a large breed in a flight crate takes the upper end. Factor in the SVS health certificate (EUR 100-250), the crate itself if you do not already have one (EUR 60-220), and a pet transport agent if you want professional coordination (EUR 250-600). Total costs for most single-pet moves from Prague to SDQ sit between EUR 1,200 and EUR 2,500 all-in.

    - heading: "Timeline and Planning Advice"
      body: |
        Four weeks is the minimum realistic timeline for this route, assuming your pet is already microchipped and the rabies vaccination is current. If either needs to be done, add time: the rabies jab must be administered at least 30 days before entry, so the clock on everything else starts the day it is given. Six weeks from preparation to departure is a comfortable target.

        Book the SVS official vet appointment to fall within 10 days of your flight. Leave at least two working days between the vet appointment and departure so the SVS has time to endorse the certificate. Cargo bookings with Iberia, Air France, or KLM should be made four to six weeks in advance, especially in peak seasons. If you are using a transport agent, they will usually manage the booking and document coordination for you.

    answer: "No. Personal pets (up to five animals) do not require an import permit. Your dog will need a valid ISO microchip, a rabies vaccination given 30 days to 12 months before entry, current DHPP vaccination, and an official health certificate issued within 15 days of travel."

    answer: "The Czech EU-format health certificate is valid for 10 days from the date the SVS-authorised vet signs it. The Dominican Republic requires the certificate to have been issued within 15 days of travel, so both conditions are satisfied when the certificate is issued close to departure."
  - question: "Can my cat travel on this route?"
    answer: "Yes. Cats are accepted. They must be microchipped, have a valid rabies vaccination, and have current panleukopenia, rhinotracheitis, and calicivirus vaccinations. The same health certificate and inspection process applies."

    answer: "Your pet departs from Vaclav Havel Airport Prague (PRG) as cargo, connecting through Madrid, Paris CDG, or Amsterdam, and arrives at Las Americas International Airport (SDQ) in Santo Domingo."

    answer: "No. There is no quarantine. Pets undergo a documentary and physical inspection at SDQ on arrival, but if the paperwork is correct they are released immediately."
