  sections:
    - heading: "Azerbaijan's entry inspection at Heydar Aliyev Airport"
      body: |
        Baku's Heydar Aliyev International Airport (GYD) is the entry point for all live cargo entering Azerbaijan. The State Veterinary Service under the Ministry of Agriculture inspects documents on arrival. For personal companion pets from Lithuania, the inspection focuses on two things: a current rabies vaccination and a health certificate from the VMVT. No titre test. No quarantine.

        Azerbaijan's border veterinary inspection at GYD is professional and systematic. Documentation needs to be complete and consistent. The certificate must reflect the vaccination details accurately, and if your pet is microchipped, the chip number must appear consistently across all documents.

        Azerbaijan restricts certain dog types. If your dog could be categorised as a fighting breed, check with the State Veterinary Service before booking transport.

    - heading: "Lithuania's VMVT certificate for Azerbaijan"
      body: |
        The EU pet passport applies to intra-EU travel. For Azerbaijan, an official export health certificate endorsed by the Valstybine maisto ir veterinarijos tarnyba (VMVT) is the required document.

        Contact the VMVT at vmvt.lt to find a vet with official authority for export health certificates. The certificate must be issued within 10 days of travel. Before the appointment, confirm with the VMVT whether Azerbaijan requires any specific format or additional information in the certificate. Getting this right before the appointment saves a repeat visit.

    - heading: "From Vilnius to Baku via Istanbul"
      body: |
        The practical routing for pet cargo from Vilnius (VNO) to Baku (GYD) uses Istanbul Ataturk (IST) as the main transit hub. From Vilnius, LOT Polish Airlines or airBaltic connections reach Warsaw (WAW) or Riga (RIX), from where Turkish Airlines or other European carriers connect to IST. Turkish Airlines Cargo then handles the IST to GYD leg.

        An alternative routes through Frankfurt (FRA) via airBaltic, with Lufthansa Cargo or Turkish Airlines continuing to Baku. Both options work; the choice depends on cargo availability and cost.

        Vilnius Airport is a regional airport with limited direct international cargo capacity. A pet transport agent with experience on Baltic-to-Caucasus routes is recommended for coordinating live animal cargo across multiple carriers.
