content:
  h1: "Pet Transport from Serbia to the United States"
  overview: |
    Air Serbia flies Belgrade to JFK non-stop, seven times a week outbound, which puts this route ahead of most European origins on paper. The catch is size. Air Serbia carries pets in the cabin only, up to 8kg including the carrier, and offers no hold option on any route it flies, not just the ones it excludes entirely (the UK, the UAE and Qatar). A dog too big for that cabin limit needs a different plan altogether.

    On the regulatory side, this is one of the more forgiving long-haul routes into the US. Serbia sits outside the CDC's high-risk list for dog rabies, confirmed against the CDC's own page (last updated 15 April 2026), so Serbian-vaccinated dogs use the ordinary entry pathway rather than the CDC-registered facility route that adds weeks and cost for owners coming from countries like Turkey or India.

  sections:
    - heading: "A direct flight that only works for small pets"
      body: |
        Belgrade Nikola Tesla Airport (BEG) to New York JFK is a genuine non-stop on Air Serbia's Airbus A330s, close to nine hours in the air. For a small dog or cat under the 8kg combined weight limit, travelling in the cabin at your feet for roughly EUR 130 is about as simple as long-haul pet travel gets.

        It stops being simple the moment your pet is bigger than that. Air Serbia does not run a cargo hold pet programme at all, on any of its routes, which is a different situation from the total UK, UAE and Qatar pet ban already covered on our Serbia-to-UK guide. Here the airline will fly your pet, just only the small ones, in the cabin, full stop.

        For a larger dog, Lufthansa Cargo via Frankfurt or Turkish Airlines Cargo via Istanbul are the realistic options, each adding a connection and a booking through a freight forwarder or pet transport agent rather than a straightforward airline reservation.

    - heading: "Why Serbia's CDC status matters more than the paperwork"
      body: |
        Compare this route to Turkey-to-US, another non-EU Balkans-adjacent origin on this site. Turkey sits on the CDC's high-risk list, which forces dogs vaccinated there through a foreign-vaccination pathway: arrival at a CDC-approved airport, an advance reservation at a CDC-registered animal care facility, or a 28-day quarantine if that reservation is missing.

        Serbia carries none of that. It is absent from the CDC high-risk list entirely, so a Serbian-vaccinated dog needs only a current rabies vaccination, a microchip, and the free CDC Dog Import Form completed online before travel. There is no quarantine and no facility booking for a dog that arrives with that paperwork in order. Cats face essentially no federal requirement at all, from Serbia or anywhere else.

        None of that removes the Serbian side of the process. The Veterinary Directorate still has to examine and endorse the export certificate, generally within a couple of working days, and it helps a great deal to tell your vet up front that the certificate needs to match US entry expectations rather than a generic EU-style template.
