        Seychelles requires mandatory quarantine for every pet entering the islands, a strict policy that keeps the country genuinely rabies-free. That status has no bearing on the EU's own classification, which runs on a published low-risk country list rather than actual disease status. The Netherlands, like every EU member state, applies the standard titre test and 90-day wait to pets from Seychelles.
