  h1: "Pet Transport from Ivory Coast to Canada"
  overview: |
    Cote d'Ivoire sits on CFIA's list of countries classed high-risk for dog rabies, the same list, dated 13 March 2025, that includes Cameroon and Eswatini. On the US route, that kind of classification triggers a genuinely demanding pathway: a titre test, a facility-airport restriction, the works. Canada runs a different system. CFIA's high-risk classification currently governs a 2022 ban on commercial dog imports, and a personal pet flying with its owner sits outside that ban entirely.

    DSV, the veterinary services directorate under Cote d'Ivoire's MIRAH ministry, still checks the export side the way it does for the UK, US or Australian routes: a vet exam, a certificate, an endorsement. What's specific to Canada is the flight. Ethiopian Airlines through Addis Ababa and Royal Air Maroc through Casablanca both offer a genuine single-connection path, a real contrast to the multi-stop routings this corridor sometimes needs.
  sections:
    - heading: "A high-risk label that reads scarier than it currently is"
      body: |
        It's tempting to see "high-risk country" and assume the same rules apply everywhere. They don't. CFIA's September 2022 measure banned commercial dog imports from every high-risk country outright, a real and specific restriction. Personal pets, the notice says, were left out of that ban, with only a stated intent to consider tighter rules for them later.

        Later, as of the most recent published guidance, hasn't turned into anything concrete for personal pets. A rabies vaccination certificate and DSV's own export paperwork carry most of the weight on this route. That's worth stating plainly, since the US-route version of this same classification demands a titre test and a facility-airport restriction that simply has no Canadian counterpart.

    - heading: "DSV's process, and the timing gap worth planning around"
      body: |
        DSV endorses the export health certificate that Abidjan airport staff and the receiving airline both check. There's no published fee schedule and no fixed turnaround guarantee for that endorsement, which makes it the part of this route hardest to plan against a fixed calendar. Start the certificate process with margin to spare rather than against a tight deadline.

        Two carriers make the flight side more predictable. Ethiopian's Addis Ababa to Toronto nonstop, run around seven times a week, keeps an Abidjan departure on one airline's network the whole way. Royal Air Maroc's near-daily Casablanca to Montreal service offers the same single-carrier advantage through a different hub.
