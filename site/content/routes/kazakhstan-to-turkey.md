  overview: |
    Kazakhstan is the rare Central Asian origin in this build with two genuine departure cities. Turkish Airlines and Air Astana both fly Almaty-Istanbul and Astana-Istanbul direct, so where you're moving from inside Kazakhstan actually changes your options, rather than everything funnelling through a single hub.

  sections:
    - heading: "Almaty or Astana: a real choice of departure city"
      body: |
        Most Central Asian countries in this build route every pet through a single commercial hub. Kazakhstan is bigger than that, geographically and in air links: Almaty remains the country's largest city and commercial centre, while Astana, the capital, has grown its own direct international network. Both cities have direct Turkish Airlines and Air Astana service to Istanbul, so an owner relocating from the capital doesn't need to add a domestic flight to Almaty first.

        Kazakhstan's Eurasian Economic Union membership adds one wrinkle: the export health certificate needs to satisfy the Union's own veterinary format requirements as well as Turkey's model, which the KVCS-authorised vet handles as part of issuing the certificate, typically within 5 to 10 days of travel.

    - heading: "The titre test, unaffected by departure city"
      body: |
        Wherever the flight starts, the FAVN titre test rule is the same. Blood must be drawn at least 30 days after the rabies vaccination, the result needs to clear 0.5 IU/ml, and a 90-day wait counted from the draw date has to pass before travel. A booster given after the test carries its own separate 21-day wait.

        A pet without a valid, correctly timed test faces 21 days of quarantine on arrival in Istanbul, at the owner's expense, regardless of whether the flight departed from Almaty or Astana.
