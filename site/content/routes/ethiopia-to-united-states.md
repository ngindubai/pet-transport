  sections:
    - heading: "The Ethiopian Veterinary Authority and export certificate for US travel"
      body: |
        The Ethiopian Veterinary Authority, under the Ministry of Agriculture, is responsible for endorsing health certificates for pet exports. The health certificate for US import must be issued by an authorised official vet and endorsed by the authority.

        The certificate must include your pet's microchip number, vaccination history with titre test result (USDA-recognised laboratory, blood draw date, result in IU/ml), owner details, and fitness to travel. Contact the Ministry of Agriculture in Addis Ababa for authorised vets and the current certificate format.

        For the FAVN titre test in Ethiopia, blood samples typically need to be sent to USDA-recognised laboratories outside the country. A pet transport specialist familiar with Ethiopian logistics can advise on sample shipping from Addis Ababa.

    - heading: "Ethiopian Airlines direct US network: the key advantage"
      body: |
        Ethiopian Airlines operates one of the strongest direct cargo networks from Africa to the United States. Flights from Addis Ababa Bole International Airport (ADD) serve Washington Dulles (IAD), Chicago O'Hare (ORD), Los Angeles (LAX), and New York JFK among other destinations.

        All of these airports have CDC-registered animal care facilities, which are required for dogs from CDC high-risk countries including Ethiopia. The direct routing avoids the transit hub complications that affect many other high-risk country corridors.

        Addis Ababa sits at approximately 2,400 metres above sea level. This can reduce cargo payload capacity on some aircraft. Confirm the specific weight limits for your pet and crate with Ethiopian Cargo (ethiopianairlines.com) before booking. On hot days, payload restrictions can be more pronounced.
