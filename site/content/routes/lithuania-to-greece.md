  h1: "Pet Transport from Lithuania to Greece: 2026 Guide"
  overview: |
    No titre test, no quarantine, no import permit: intra-EU travel from Vilnius to Athens strips away almost everything that makes long-haul relocation complicated. A current rabies vaccination and an EU pet passport, or an AHC where the passport does not apply, cover the whole entry process into Greece.

    The one practical decision left is which connection to book, since there is no non-stop service between the two capitals. LOT Polish Airlines via Warsaw keeps the trip inside a single carrier's network. Turkish Airlines via Istanbul is the wider-reaching alternative, useful when Warsaw connection times do not line up with your travel plans.

  sections:
    - heading: "Choosing your connection: Warsaw or Istanbul"
      body: |
        LOT Polish Airlines flies Vilnius to Warsaw, with onward service into Athens, keeping cabin pet booking within one airline's system on both legs. Turkish Airlines routes through Istanbul, drawing on a dense long-haul and regional network that often gives more flexibility on departure times.

        Neither routing has a meaningful edge for a healthy small pet in good documentation order. Choose on flight timing and price, but reserve cabin or cargo pet space on both legs at the time of booking, since availability is never guaranteed at check-in on connecting itineraries.

    - heading: "The paperwork: passport, AHC, and the April 2026 change"
      body: |
        The EU pet passport, issued by a VMVT-authorised vet in Lithuania, remains valid for the pet's life as long as the rabies vaccination inside it is kept current. Since 22 April 2026, that passport has been restricted to owners primarily resident in the EU, a change that leaves most Lithuanian pet owners unaffected.

        Non-EU nationals resident in Lithuania need a single-use AHC instead, issued within 10 days of travel and endorsed by the VMVT. Whichever document applies, Greece's Directorate General of Veterinary Services checks it against the microchip on arrival, so confirm the chip number matches exactly before you fly.
