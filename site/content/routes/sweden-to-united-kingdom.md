content:
  h1: "Pet Transport Sweden to UK: 2026 Guide"
  overview: "Sweden to UK is one of the more straightforward international routes for pet owners. Sweden is a Part 1 listed country under UK APHA rules, which means no titre test and no quarantine for compliant arrivals.   sections:
    - heading: "The AHC replaces the EU pet passport"
      body: |
        Since the UK left the EU, Great Britain no longer accepts EU pet passports for inbound travel. If your dog or cat has a blue EU pet passport from before Brexit, it is not valid for UK entry.

        The replacement is the Animal Health Certificate (AHC). This is issued by an official vet authorised by Jordbruksverket (the Swedish Board of Agriculture). The certificate covers the same information as the old passport but must be issued within 10 days of travel and is specific to this journey.

        After the pet arrives in Great Britain, the AHC is then valid for up to 4 months for onward travel within Great Britain. Keep it with your pet's other documentation.

    - heading: "Tapeworm timing for dogs: get this right"
      body: |
        All dogs entering Great Britain from abroad must be treated for tapeworm before arrival. The treatment uses praziquantel (or equivalent licensed product) and must be given by a vet between 24 and 120 hours before the pet arrives in Great Britain.

        This window is counted from UK arrival time, not from when you leave Sweden. If you take a connecting flight with a long layover, or a ferry crossing, the timing of the treatment shifts accordingly. The treatment must be recorded in the AHC by the administering vet.

        Cats do not require tapeworm treatment. Dogs travelling without a correctly timed and recorded treatment will be refused entry.

faqs:
  - question: "Does my pet need to quarantine when entering the UK from Sweden?"
    answer: "No. Sweden is a Part 1 listed country under APHA rules, which means UK quarantine is not required for compliant arrivals. Your pet needs a current AHC, an ISO-standard microchip implanted before the rabies vaccination, a rabies vaccination that has been valid for at least 21 days, and (for dogs) a tapeworm treatment given 24-120 hours before arrival. Non-compliant pets may face penalty quarantine."
  - question: "Can I still use my pet's EU pet passport to travel from Sweden to the UK?"
    answer: "No. EU pet passports have not been accepted for entry into Great Britain since January 2021. You need a current Animal Health Certificate (AHC) issued by a Jordbruksverket-authorised official vet within 10 days of your travel date. The AHC is valid for that journey and for onward travel within Great Britain for up to 4 months afterwards."
  - question: "What is the tapeworm treatment rule for dogs going from Sweden to the UK?"
    answer: "Dogs must receive a praziquantel-based tapeworm treatment from a vet between 24 and 120 hours before arrival in Great Britain. The treatment must be recorded in the AHC. The window is measured from arrival time in the UK, not from when you leave Sweden. Cats are not subject to this rule."
  - question: "Which airline is best for moving a large dog from Sweden to the UK?"
    answer: "For large dogs, cargo is the only option. SAS Cargo and British Airways (IAG Cargo) both serve the Stockholm-London route. For smaller dogs that meet the size and weight limits, some airlines allow in-cabin travel in a carrier under the seat. Check current weight and crate size limits directly with your chosen airline before booking, as policies can change."
