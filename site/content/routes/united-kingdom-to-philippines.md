  h1: "Pet Transport from the UK to the Philippines"
  overview: |
    The UK to Philippines route has the longest mandatory waiting period of any Southeast Asian destination: 180 days from the titre test blood draw date. Combined with the 30-day mandatory quarantine on arrival and the BAI Sanitary and Phytosanitary Import Clearance (SPSIC) process, this is one of the most time-intensive pet moves from the UK. Allow 9-10 months from the start of planning to the date your pet can leave quarantine in Manila.

    Philippine Airlines operates cargo services between London Heathrow and Manila, which is the most straightforward routing. The BAI (Bureau of Animal Industry) manages all aspects of the Philippines import process, from the SPSIC application to the quarantine facility.

    - heading: "The BAI SPSIC: what it is and how to apply"
      body: |
        The BAI Sanitary and Phytosanitary Import Clearance is the Philippines import permit for live animals. Without an approved SPSIC, your pet cannot be imported. Applications are submitted online through the BAI portal at bai.gov.ph.

        To apply, you will need: your pet's microchip number and implantation date, current vaccination records (including rabies vaccination, at least 14 days old), the titre test result (at least 0.5 IU/ml, from a blood draw at least 30 days after vaccination), the details of the OV-endorsed AHC, your planned arrival date in the Philippines, and confirmation that your pet is at least 120 days old.

        The BAI processes the SPSIC and issues a clearance document. This clearance number must appear on your AHC. Do not book cargo until you have SPSIC approval in hand.

        The 3-animal limit applies per owner per year. If you are moving with more than 3 pets, contact the BAI directly before starting the process, as there is no simple workaround for this restriction.

    answer: "Yes. All dogs and cats arriving in the Philippines from international origins must complete a 30-day mandatory quarantine at a BAI-accredited facility. This applies regardless of vaccination history or documentation. Quarantine costs are paid by the owner."
  - question: "Can I bring more than 3 pets from the UK to the Philippines?"
    answer: "The BAI limits imports to a maximum of 3 animals per owner per year. If you have more than 3 pets, the excess animals cannot be included in the same import. Contact the BAI directly to discuss options if your situation involves more animals."
