  h1: "Pet Transport Rwanda to Greece"
  overview: |
    Five months is the honest floor for a Kigali-to-Athens move, and the flight is the least of it. Rwanda sits outside the EU's approved country list, so the Directorate General of Veterinary Services applies the full unlisted-country sequence: microchip, rabies vaccination, an FAVN or ELISA titre test, and a 90-day wait from the blood draw before entry. Ethiopian Airlines via Addis Ababa is the most direct of the three practical routings.

  sections:
    - heading: "Why Rwanda to Greece runs 20 to 26 weeks"
      body: |
        Nothing about the sequence can be reordered. The chip goes in first, confirmed on a scanner before any vaccination. Thirty days after that vaccination, blood is drawn for a titre test that has to clear 0.5 IU/ml at an EU-approved laboratory. The result itself isn't the bottleneck; the 90-day wait that starts on the draw date is. Athens border vets will not admit a pet before that period ends, no matter how quickly the lab responds.

        Add RAB's own endorsement timeline and the realistic total sits at five to six and a half months from a standing start.

    - heading: "Three ways from Kigali to Athens"
      body: |
        Ethiopian Airlines routes through its own Addis Ababa hub, which connects directly onward to Athens, the shortest of the three practical options by transit time. Turkish Airlines via Istanbul is a close second, since Istanbul sits near enough to Greece that the final leg is brief. Brussels Airlines is the third option, running north to Brussels before a connection south to Athens, a longer routing worth considering mainly when the other two are full.

    - heading: "RAB's role in the export paperwork"
      body: |
        There's no formal export permit for a personal pet leaving Rwanda, but the EU-format health certificate has to be issued by an RAB-authorised vet and then endorsed by the Rwanda Agriculture and Animal Resources Development Board itself before departure. A generic Rwandan export document will not satisfy Greek import checks; it needs the specific EU format from the outset.
