    Avianca's own Bogota to Heathrow service, daily on a Boeing 787, means a pet leaving Caracas can reach the UK on a single carrier once it clears the short Caracas-Bogota hop.   sections:
    - heading: "Why Venezuela sits on Great Britain's unlisted pathway"
      body: |
        Great Britain splits the world into listed countries (lower risk, no titre test needed) and everyone else. Venezuela falls into the second group, alongside most of South America bar Argentina and Chile.

        That means the full sequence applies: microchip before vaccination, rabies vaccination, a blood draw at least 30 days later to confirm a titre of 0.5 IU/ml or above, then a flat three-month wait from the blood draw itself. Only after that wait clears can the GB Pet Health Certificate be issued, and it is only valid for 10 days once signed. There is no way to compress this. Owners who start the process late are the ones who end up rebooking flights.
    - heading: "Getting out of Caracas: what flying actually looks like in 2026"
      body: |
        This is the part that catches people out. Several major international carriers have suspended or scaled back Caracas services over the past few years, and disruption has continued into 2026: in July alone, American Eagle and Aeroregional cancelled seven scheduled departures to Miami and Bogota inside three days, citing operational issues at Simon Bolivar International Airport.

        Avianca has kept a Bogota connection running and operates its own long-haul Bogota-Heathrow route, so a Caracas-Bogota-London itinerary on Avianca throughout is the most workable option found in current research. It is not, however, a guaranteed fixture. Reconfirm cargo booking and capacity directly with Avianca Cargo in the weeks before travel, not months ahead, and build slack into your plan in case a leg is cancelled or rescheduled.
    - heading: "The dog-specific heartworm test INSAI asks for"
      body: |
        One INSAI requirement catches owners off guard: dogs leaving Venezuela need a Dirofilaria immitis (heartworm) blood test within 30 days of export, on top of the standard vaccination and deworming paperwork. Cats and puppies under six months are exempt. It is a small extra step, but it needs booking alongside the rabies titre test rather than as an afterthought, since both draws are time-limited.
