  overview: "Estonia is an EU member state, which means US pets entering Estonia must follow EU third-country import rules.   sections:
    - heading: "EU AHC: the document that covers US pets entering Estonia"
      body: |
        For US owners moving a pet into Estonia (or any EU country), the EU Animal Health Certificate (AHC) replaces the standard US health certificate. The AHC is a specific EU-format document that must be completed by a USDA-accredited veterinarian and then endorsed by USDA APHIS before the pet can enter the EU.

        The AHC is valid for 10 days from the date of issuance. This means your USDA-accredited vet appointment must happen within 10 days of your arrival in Estonia. The APHIS endorsement then adds 1-3 business days to that timeline. With a 10-day total window, plan your vet appointment for 7-8 days before your departure date, then expedite APHIS endorsement if needed.

        The AHC records your pet's microchip number, vaccination history, and confirms fitness for travel. The Estonian VTA (Veterinary and Food Board) border vet at Tallinn Airport checks the AHC, scans the chip, and confirms the vaccination dates. Have all documents accessible on arrival.

    - heading: "Microchip compatibility: an issue to check before leaving the US"
      body: |
        The EU requires ISO 11784/11785 (15-digit) microchips. Many US vets implant 10-digit AVID or FECAVA chips, which do not register correctly on EU readers. Before departure, ask your vet explicitly whether your pet has a 15-digit ISO chip. If not, a new ISO-compatible chip can be implanted alongside the old one, and the new chip number must be used on the AHC.

        This is the most common reason US pets face delays at EU border crossings. It is easy to resolve before travel and impossible to resolve on arrival. Check the chip number now.

faqs:
  - question: "Does my pet need a rabies titre test to enter Estonia from the USA?"
    answer: "No. The USA is a listed third country under EU pet import regulations. This means the EU waives the rabies antibody titre test requirement for US pets. A current rabies vaccination (administered at least 21 days before entry) and a valid EU AHC from a USDA-accredited vet endorsed by APHIS are the main requirements. Source: EU Regulation 576/2013 and implementing legislation."
  - question: "What is the USDA APHIS endorsement and do I need it for Estonia?"
    answer: "Yes. The EU AHC must be endorsed by USDA APHIS before it is valid for EU entry. The endorsement confirms that the issuing vet is USDA-accredited and the document is authentic. Standard APHIS endorsement takes 2-3 business days; expedited service is faster. Without the APHIS stamp, the AHC is not accepted at the Estonian border."
  - question: "Can my pet fly in cabin from the USA to Estonia?"
    answer: "No airline operates cabin pet travel on transatlantic routes. Pets must travel as cargo (accompanied or unaccompanied) on the US-to-Europe leg. On the shorter European hub-to-Tallinn leg, small pets may travel in cabin depending on the carrier and route. Confirm with the intra-EU carrier when booking."
  - question: "Which European hub is best for connecting to Tallinn from the USA?"
    answer: "Amsterdam Schiphol (AMS) and Helsinki (HEL) are the most practical European hubs for Tallinn connections. KLM operates AMS to TLL directly. Finnair operates HEL to TLL with short transit times. Frankfurt (FRA) is also an option via Lufthansa connections to TLL. Availability and cargo handling standards vary; KLM via AMS is the most commonly recommended."
