    - heading: "Ethiopian via Addis vs Kenya Airways via Nairobi: two main routes compared"
      body: |
        Harare has no direct flights to the UK. All Zimbabwe to UK pet cargo routes transit through an African hub. Ethiopian Airlines via Addis Ababa and Kenya Airways via Nairobi are the two most established options.

        Ethiopian Airlines operates from Harare (HRE) to Addis Ababa Bole (ADD), which has strong onward connections to London Heathrow. Ethiopian Cargo handles significant live animal volumes and has experienced handling at ADD. This is generally the most consistent routing for Zimbabwe to UK cargo.

        Kenya Airways from Harare via Nairobi (NBO) to London is a reliable alternative. Nairobi Jomo Kenyatta connects to Heathrow on Kenya Airways directly. The HRE-NBO segment is short and NBO has solid animal handling facilities.

        Routing via Johannesburg is a third option: the Harare-Johannesburg segment is short (under two hours) and Johannesburg O.R. Tambo connects to London on multiple carriers including British Airways. This routing gives you access to a larger hub but adds a segment.

    - heading: "DVS export permit: apply early, use an agent"
      body: |
        Zimbabwe's Department of Veterinary Services issues the export permit and endorses the official health certificate. The permit application requires details of the animal, vaccination history, and origin documentation. Allow at least six weeks for processing, and be prepared to follow up.

        Given Zimbabwe's current administrative environment, working with a Zimbabwe-based agent who has recent experience with DVS submissions is the most reliable approach. An agent with DVS contacts can track the application, respond to queries, and reduce the risk of delay.

        The FAVN titre test and the DVS permit process should run in parallel, not sequentially. Starting both at the same time is the difference between a 16-week and a 24-week preparation period.
