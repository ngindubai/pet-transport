  h1: "Pet Transport Mexico to Slovenia: 2026 Guide"
  overview: |
    Mexico sits on the EU's short list of low-rabies-risk third countries, under Annex II to Implementing Regulation (EU) 2026/636. That single classification cuts months off this route compared with an unlisted origin: no titre test, no 90-day wait, just a compliant vaccination and the right paperwork.

    Turkish Airlines runs one of the only true direct long-haul links between Mexico City and a major European hub, which makes Istanbul the practical gateway here rather than any US or Canadian stopover.

  sections:
    - heading: "SENASICA: the Mexican export process"
      body: |
        Every pet leaving Mexico for the EU needs a Certificado Zoosanitario para Exportación, completed by a SENASICA-registered vet and endorsed after an OISA inspection at the departure airport, usually Mexico City or Guadalajara. This is separate from the EU-format health certificate Slovenia requires, and the two documents run on different clocks: the SENASICA paperwork is arranged around your flight date, while the EU certificate has a hard 10-day validity window before arrival.

        Book both appointments with your flight already confirmed. Trying to work backwards from an uncertain travel date is the single most common cause of delay on this route.

    - heading: "Why there is no titre test on this route"
      body: |
        Owners moving pets out of unlisted countries such as Brazil or Indonesia face a FAVN blood test and a fixed 90-day wait. Mexico does not carry that requirement. The only fixed clock here is the 21-day gap between a first rabies vaccination and travel, and even that does not apply if your pet already has a current vaccination on record.

        Do not let a generic quote assume a titre test is needed. If a supplier includes one in your costing for this specific route, ask them to check Mexico's listing status before you pay for a laboratory test you do not need.

    - heading: "Getting into Ljubljana: Istanbul versus Frankfurt"
      body: |
        Direct long-haul cargo capacity into Ljubljana itself is thin. Turkish Airlines Cargo's direct Mexico City-Istanbul service, followed by a short connecting flight, is usually the quickest single-connection option and the one this route is built around.

        Lufthansa Cargo via Frankfurt or Munich is the fallback if Turkish Cargo space is not available on your dates. For larger dogs, some agents recommend collecting the pet at Frankfurt or Vienna and driving the final leg into Slovenia by road, which avoids the smaller aircraft that serve LJU directly. Ask your cargo agent which option applies to your crate size before booking.
