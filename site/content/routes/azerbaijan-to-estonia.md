  overview: "Moving a pet from Azerbaijan to Estonia requires careful planning over a minimum of five to six months.   sections:
    - heading: "Why the 90-day wait applies and what it means for planning"
      body: |
        Estonia, as an EU member state, applies the EU pet travel framework for animals arriving from unlisted third countries. Azerbaijan is classified as an EU unlisted country, which means your pet must complete the FAVN titre test process before entering Estonia.

        The sequence is: chip implanted first, then primary rabies vaccination, then at least 30 days later a blood draw for the FAVN titre test at an EU-approved laboratory. If the result is 0.5 IU/ml or above, the 90-day wait begins from the blood draw date. Travel to Estonia cannot happen until at least day 91.

        The 90-day wait is calculated from when the blood was drawn, not when the results came back. Starting as early as possible after confirming you are moving is the most important step on this route.

    - heading: "What happens at Tallinn Airport"
      body: |
        Tallinn Airport (TLL) has border veterinary inspection for live animal cargo arriving from non-EU origins. The Pohllumajandus- ja Toiduamet (Agricultural Board of Estonia) oversees these controls. Border vets at TLL will check: the State Veterinary Service-endorsed health certificate (issued within 10 days of travel), the FAVN titre test result, the vaccination record, and the microchip.

        The chip must be scannable and the number must match every document precisely. If the titre test shows a successful result but the 90-day wait is incomplete, entry is refused. Estonian border vets apply EU rules strictly.

    - heading: "Routing from Baku to Tallinn"
      body: |
        There is no direct cargo service from Heydar Aliyev International Airport (GYD) in Baku to Tallinn (TLL). The standard routing uses Turkish Airlines Cargo via Istanbul Ataturk (IST), where live animals are handled, then connects to TLL. Istanbul is geographically close to Baku and the IST hub handles live cargo reliably.

        Alternative routings go via Frankfurt with Lufthansa Cargo. From Frankfurt, connections to Tallinn are available. AZAL, Azerbaijan's national carrier, operates from Baku to some European airports, but its live cargo acceptance for Tallinn routing should be confirmed in advance. Book during the 90-day wait and confirm live animal acceptance on every segment in writing.
