  sections:
    - heading: "What does EU listed status mean for the UK journey?"
      body: |
        The UK's pet travel scheme creates a two-tier system after Brexit: listed countries (which include all EU member states) have controlled or eliminated dog rabies and meet UK biosecurity standards. Unlisted countries require a rabies titre test and 90-day wait. Slovakia is listed.

        For a Slovak pet owner, listed status means: you skip the titre test entirely. Your dog or cat needs a current rabies vaccination documented in an EU pet passport or AHC, an ISO microchip, and for dogs, a tapeworm treatment between 24 and 120 hours before arriving in Great Britain. That is the main checklist.

        The AHC is the document that replaces the EU pet passport for travel from EU countries to Great Britain since Brexit. It is issued by an official vet in Slovakia within 10 days of travel.

    - heading: "The tapeworm treatment: timing is everything"
      body: |
        Dogs entering Great Britain from EU countries, including Slovakia, must have a tapeworm treatment administered by a vet. The active ingredient that the UK requires is praziquantel, and it must be given between 24 and 120 hours before your dog is scheduled to arrive in Great Britain. Not before, not after.

        The timing is strict because the treatment targets a specific parasite (Echinococcus multilocularis) that can survive in the environment and is not present in the UK. The 24-120 hour window ensures the treatment is active at the point of entry.

        Plan the timing around your departure, including any hub connection. If you are flying Bratislava to Vienna to London, the 24 hour clock starts from when your dog is treated, not when it departs Slovakia. Get the treatment on the correct day, documented by the vet with stamp and signature.

    - heading: "Getting your pet from Slovakia to the UK: the routing"
      body: |
        Bratislava Airport (BTS) is served predominantly by budget carriers that do not accept live animals. For cargo pet transport, the most practical routing is to Vienna International Airport (VIE), which is approximately 60-90 minutes by road from Bratislava. Austrian Airlines and Lufthansa both operate cargo pet services from Vienna to London.

        Alternatively, Prague (PRG) is about 3-4 hours from Bratislava and has better cargo connections to London via CSA Czech Airlines or Lufthansa. From Bratislava itself, you are more limited but not without options. A Slovak pet transport agent can advise on which current routing works best.
