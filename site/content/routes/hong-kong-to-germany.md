  h1: "Pet Transport from Hong Kong to Germany"
  overview: |
    Hong Kong to Germany is a significantly more involved route than Hong Kong to the UK or Canada. Germany is an EU member state, and Hong Kong is not on the EU's listed country schedule. That means the full non-listed country requirements apply: titre test, 3-month waiting period, and EU Annex IV health certificate.

    The total preparation time is 22-28 weeks minimum. The titre test and waiting period account for most of it. Start this process the moment you know you are moving.
  sections:
    - heading: "Why the 3-month wait applies: EU non-listed country status"
      body: |
        The EU maintains a list of countries from which pets can enter with simplified documentation. Hong Kong is not on this list. Under EU Regulation 576/2013, pets from non-listed countries must meet the full import requirements: microchip, current rabies vaccination, a FAVN or ELISA titre test with a result of at least 0.5 IU/ml, and a 3-calendar-month wait from the date of the successful titre test blood draw before entering the EU.

        The 3-month clock starts on the date the blood is drawn, not the date results arrive. If your blood draw is on 1 June and results come back 0.5 IU/ml compliant on 20 June, the earliest your pet can enter Germany is 1 September.

        These rules apply at every EU border. If your pet enters another EU country first (France, Netherlands, etc.) and then travels to Germany, the same rules still govern that first EU entry.
    - heading: "Getting the titre test done from Hong Kong: the lab challenge"
      body: |
        EU Regulation 2020/689 specifies that the titre test must be performed at an EU-approved laboratory. As of 2025, no EU-approved titre test laboratories operate in Hong Kong.

        The blood sample must be drawn by a vet in Hong Kong, packaged to IATA P650 standards for diagnostic samples, and shipped internationally to an approved lab. Commonly used labs are Sciensano in Belgium and Synlab in the UK. Allow 3-4 weeks from blood draw to results, plus international shipping time.

        A pet transport agent with EU experience based in Hong Kong will know which labs are currently accepting HK samples and can coordinate the shipping. This is not a step to handle informally.
    - heading: "The AFCD process on top of EU requirements"
      body: |
        While the EU requirements dominate the preparation timeline, the AFCD export process in Hong Kong is also mandatory. The AFCD live animal export permit must be obtained before departure. The health certificate must be in EU Annex IV format and endorsed by AFCD.

        The Annex IV certificate is more complex than a standard health certificate. It makes specific declarations required under EU law. Your AFCD-authorised vet in Hong Kong must use the correct form and the AFCD must endorse it within the 10-day window before your pet arrives in Germany.

        A pet transport specialist who works regularly on HK-EU moves is the most reliable way to get this right. The paperwork sequence is tight and the consequences of getting it wrong, at the EU border, are significant.
