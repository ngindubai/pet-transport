  sections:
    - heading: "Hong Kong EU-listed: no titre test and the AFCD health certificate process"
      body: |
        EU Regulation 576/2013 lists Hong Kong as a recognised territory for pet movement. Hong Kong pets entering Germany need only a valid ISO microchip, a current rabies vaccination (21-day wait after a primary course), and the AFCD-endorsed EU-format health certificate. No titre test, no 3-month wait. Source: food.ec.europa.eu, 2026.

        The AFCD (Agriculture, Fisheries and Conservation Department) is Hong Kong's official authority for animal export health certification. An AFCD-registered vet issues the EU-format health certificate within 10 days of travel; AFCD endorses it before departure. The process is familiar to experienced vets in Hong Kong, given the volume of international pet relocations from the city.

    - heading: "Cathay Pacific direct to Frankfurt and Germany's Bundesland breed rules"
      body: |
        Cathay Pacific Cargo operates direct from Hong Kong (HKG) to Frankfurt (FRA) and accepts live animal cargo on this route. Lufthansa Cargo also offers a direct HKG-FRA service. Both airlines have live animal handling facilities at Frankfurt, Germany's main cargo entry point for pets from Asia.

        Germany bans Pit Bull Terrier and Tosa Inu at federal level. Each Bundesland then adds its own restrictions; Bavaria is the most restrictive, covering Rottweiler, American Bulldog, and a range of other breeds. If your dog falls into any potentially restricted category, check the specific Bundesland rules for your destination city before committing to the move.
