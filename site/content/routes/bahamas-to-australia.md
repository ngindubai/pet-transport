  h1: "Pet Transport from the Bahamas to Australia"
  overview: |
    Belarus and Bosnia and Herzegovina, the two other countries covered alongside the Bahamas in this build, both sit outside DAFF's approved list entirely. A pet from either has to live in a third country for 180 straight days before Australia will even process the paperwork. The Bahamas skips that step completely. DAFF places it in Group 3, the same tier as the UK, Germany and the US, so a Nassau pet can be prepared for Australia without ever leaving home first.

    That doesn't make it quick. Two rabies vaccinations, an RNATT titre test, and a 180-day wait from the day the blood sample lands at a DAFF-approved lab still apply, the same as any Group 3 pet anywhere. What's different is where all of that happens: in the Bahamas, on a fixed timeline, with no detour built in.

  sections:
    - heading: "Why Group 3 status changes the whole shape of this route"
      body: |
        DAFF sorts every country it will accept pets from into Group 1, Group 2, Group 3 or non-approved. The Bahamas holds Group 3, its tier for places where rabies exists but is considered controlled. That's a genuinely different starting point from a non-approved country, where the pet has to first establish 180 consecutive days of residency somewhere else before any Australian paperwork can begin.

        A Group 3 pet skips that relocation. The 180-day wait still applies, but it runs against the RNATT blood sample reaching a DAFF-approved lab, not against where the animal happens to be living. A Bahamian pet can sit at home in Nassau for most of that wait.

    - heading: "The identity verification step nobody should skip"
      body: |
        One optional-sounding step carries an outsized consequence. A government vet in the Bahamas confirming the microchip and identity of the animal before the RNATT blood draw is what earns the 10-day minimum quarantine at Mickleham. Miss it, and the minimum triples to 30 days.

        It's one extra appointment, arranged before the blood draw rather than after. Compared with three extra weeks of quarantine fees and a longer separation from the animal, it's not a close call.

    - heading: "Getting from Nassau to the only airport that matters"
      body: |
        Every route to Australia on this site from the Bahamas starts the same way British Airways' UK-bound Bahamas route does: a nonstop Nassau to Heathrow flight, seven days a week, through IAG Cargo. From there, Qantas or Singapore Airlines carries the pet on to Melbourne, since Mickleham is the only facility in the country and Sydney, Perth and Brisbane simply don't accept pet imports.

        Book the second leg once the 180-day wait has genuinely cleared, not before. IAG Cargo's temperature embargo on the first leg, outside 7C to 29C, is the more immediate scheduling risk given Nassau's climate for much of the year.
