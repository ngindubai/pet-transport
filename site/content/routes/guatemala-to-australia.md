content:
  h1: "Pet Transport from Guatemala to Australia"
  overview: |
    Australia will not accept a pet import filed straight from Guatemala. DAFF classifies Guatemala as a Non-Approved country, which means there is no version of this move that skips a stopover somewhere else first.

    The workaround, and it is a genuine workaround rather than a shortcut, is 180 consecutive days of residency in a DAFF-approved country such as the United States, the United Kingdom or Canada. Only once that residency clock has run, and the standard vaccination and titre test sequence has been completed inside it, can an import permit application even be lodged. Realistically, that puts the whole process at 40 to 52 weeks from a standing start in Guatemala City.

  sections:
    - heading: "What Non-Approved actually means here"
      body: |
        DAFF sorts every country into groups. Group 1 covers New Zealand and a couple of nearby territories. Group 2 is rabies-free countries like Japan and Singapore. Group 3 is rabies-controlled countries, the US, UK, most of the EU, Canada among them. Guatemala falls outside all three.

        For a Non-Approved country, DAFF's rule is blunt: the pet must reside in an approved country for 180 consecutive days before an import application from that country will be processed. Not visited. Resided. That means genuinely relocating, registering with a local vet, and staying long enough for the clock to complete before any of the Australian-specific paperwork can start.

    - heading: "Running the vaccination and titre steps inside the residency window"
      body: |
        Once the pet has moved to, say, the United States, the process from that point mirrors a standard US-to-Australia move: two rabies vaccinations 30 days apart, a 30-day wait, an RNATT titre test with a passing result, then a 180-day wait from the blood draw. On a US-to-Australia route alone, that's already six to eight months.

        Here's the part that saves some time: the 180-day residency requirement and the vaccination/titre sequence can overlap, provided the vet appointments happen early in the residency period. A well-timed plan doesn't simply add the two 180-day clocks together. It runs them in parallel, which is why the realistic floor sits at 40 to 52 weeks rather than well over a year.

    - heading: "Mickleham, and why the destination city doesn't change anything"
      body: |
        Whatever Australian city the family is actually settling in, the pet arrives at Melbourne Airport and goes to the Mickleham Post Entry Quarantine Facility. There is no alternative. Spaces have to be booked ahead, sometimes months ahead during busy periods, and the minimum stay is 10 days at the owner's cost, typically AUD 2,000 to 4,000.

        Given how much of this route depends on sequencing done correctly the first time, this is not a route to plan without a specialist agent who has actually handled a Non-Approved-country residency workaround before.
