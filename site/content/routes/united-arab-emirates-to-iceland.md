content:
  h1: "Pet Transport from the United Arab Emirates to Iceland"
  overview: |
    The United Arab Emirates sits on Matvaelastofnun's Category 1 list, Iceland's rabies-free tier, in the same group as Switzerland, Japan and the UK. Most owners leaving the Gulf for Iceland assume the opposite, expecting the harder Category 2 path with its 90-day wait. That assumption costs nothing to correct: once the titre test clears, there is no further fixed wait before travel.

    What Category 1 status does not touch is the 14-day quarantine. Every dog and cat entering Iceland serves it, regardless of where it started. Two clocks run on this route: the paperwork clock before departure, now shorter than most owners expect, and the fixed two weeks at an approved isolation station once your pet lands.

  sections:
    - heading: "The UAE's place on MAST's Category 1 list"
      body: |
        MAST splits countries into two tiers for pet import: Category 1, rabies-free nations, and Category 2, countries where rabies is present but controlled. The United Arab Emirates is listed under Category 1, confirmed directly from MAST's own published country list (updated 19 May 2026) alongside Switzerland, Japan, Singapore and the UK, a detail that surprises most owners planning this move, since the UAE is not a country most people associate with rabies-free status in the way they would Australia or Japan.

        The practical effect: your pet's titre test blood draw happens at least 30 days after vaccination, and once the lab confirms 0.5 IU/ml or above, you can book travel without a further mandatory wait. A Category 2 country, Canada or Turkey for example, would face an additional 90 days counted from the blood draw date before travel is permitted.

    - heading: "What Category 1 status does not buy you: the quarantine"
      body: |
        The 14-day isolation period at Einangrunarstoedin i Reykjanesbae or Einangrunarstoedin Mosel applies to every animal entering Iceland, with no exemption for Category 1 origin. This is a genuine biosecurity measure protecting Iceland's disease-free status as an island, not a penalty tied to your paperwork or your country's rabies classification.

        Both stations run on limited intake schedules. Book your slot before locking in a flight date, since arriving with nowhere to send your pet on landing is a real risk if you leave this step until the permit clears. Fees run to roughly ISK 220,000 for a dog's full stay (about EUR 1,450) and somewhat less for a cat, covering housing, feeding and the veterinary checks the station carries out.

    - heading: "No direct flight: the Gulf-to-Keflavik routing problem"
      body: |
        Neither Dubai nor Abu Dhabi has a nonstop service to Keflavik on any carrier. The workable routing pairs Emirates or Etihad's Gulf-to-Europe network with Icelandair's final leg into Keflavik, commonly via Copenhagen or Paris CDG. Etihad flies Abu Dhabi to Copenhagen daily; Emirates serves Copenhagen and Paris CDG from Dubai; Icelandair covers either hub's connection into Keflavik.

        Since 1 November 2024, Icelandair no longer takes pets as checked or excess baggage, so that final leg moves through Icelandair Cargo, a separate reservation from whichever Gulf carrier flies the first segment. Confirm with both cargo desks, not just one, that a live animal handover between carriers at the connecting hub is something they routinely do, and build in buffer time rather than a tight connection.
