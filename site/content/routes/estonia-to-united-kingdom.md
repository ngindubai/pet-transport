  h1: "Pet Transport from Estonia to the United Kingdom"
  overview: |
    Estonia is one of the UK's listed Part 1 countries, which means the process is significantly simpler than for pets travelling from non-listed countries. There is no titre test requirement, no 3-month wait, and no quarantine for correctly documented pets.

    You need a current rabies vaccination, an ISO microchip, a tapeworm treatment for dogs (24-120 hours before UK arrival), and either a valid EU pet passport (issued before January 2021) or an EU Animal Health Certificate from an official Estonian vet. The AHC is valid for 10 days from the vet examination date.

    From Tallinn, airBaltic via Riga and Finnair via Helsinki are the main options. Ryanair flies direct to UK airports and accepts very small cabin pets.
  sections:
    - heading: "EU pet passport versus Animal Health Certificate: what you need to know"
      body: |
        Since the UK left the EU, EU pet passports are accepted only if issued before 1 January 2021. Passports issued on or after that date are not valid for UK entry. If your pet's EU passport was issued after this date, you will need a new Animal Health Certificate from an official Estonian vet.

        The AHC is valid for 10 days from the examination date. It is issued by an officially authorised veterinarian, not your regular private vet (unless they hold the official vet designation). Check with your vet practice that they are authorised to issue AHCs.

        One practical point: if you travel with your pet regularly between Estonia and the UK, you will need a new AHC each time. Unlike the old EU pet passport (which was a permanent booklet), the AHC is a single-use document valid for one trip.
    - heading: "Tapeworm treatment for dogs: why it is still required"
      body: |
        Even though Estonia is a listed country, dogs still require a tapeworm treatment before entering the UK. A vet must administer Praziquantel (minimum 23.4mg) between 24 and 120 hours before your dog's scheduled UK arrival time. The vet records this in the AHC.

        The timing window is exact. Treatment given earlier than 24 hours before arrival, or more than 120 hours before, is not valid. Book the vet appointment to fall within this window.

        Cats do not require tapeworm treatment for UK entry.
