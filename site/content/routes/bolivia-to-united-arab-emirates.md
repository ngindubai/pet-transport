  h1: "Pet Transport from Bolivia to the United Arab Emirates"
  overview: |
    A 90-day wait, counted from the date blood is drawn, sits at the centre of this route. Bolivia doesn't appear on MOCCAE's low-risk list, so the UAE's full unlisted-country protocol applies: a rabies titre test at an accredited laboratory, a result of at least 0.5 IU/ml, and then a fixed three-month clock that nothing can shorten, not a faster lab, not an earlier flight.

    Once that clock is running, the rest of the process is manageable. SENASAG's export side is familiar from every other Bolivia route in this build, and the flight itself is shorter than most South America-to-Gulf pairings get, thanks to Emirates' nonstop Sao Paulo to Dubai service.

  sections:
    - heading: "The 90-day wait, and the permit that runs out first"
      body: |
        The clock starts on the day blood is drawn for the titre test, not the day the lab confirms a pass. Get that date wrong and the whole schedule slips by weeks, not days.

        There's a second timing trap sitting right behind it. MOCCAE's import permit is only valid for 30 days from the date it's issued. Apply too early, before the 90-day titre wait has finished, and the permit can expire before travel is even possible. Apply too late and there isn't enough runway left before the intended date. The two clocks need managing together, not treated as separate steps.

    - heading: "One stop instead of two or three"
      body: |
        Most Gulf-bound pet moves out of South America need an intermediate hub in Europe or North America before a final Gulf sector. This route doesn't, not really. Boliviana de Aviación or Gol get the pet from Santa Cruz Viru Viru to Sao Paulo, and Emirates then flies nonstop from Sao Paulo straight into Dubai, a flight of around 16 hours and one of the airline's longest scheduled routes. That's a genuinely shorter itinerary than the Madrid or Miami routings this build has recorded for comparable South American origins.
