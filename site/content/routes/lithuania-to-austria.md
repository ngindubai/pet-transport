  sections:
    - heading: "Getting from Vilnius to Vienna with a pet"
      body: |
        The most practical routing is LOT Polish Airlines via Warsaw. The Vilnius to Warsaw leg takes under an hour, and LOT's onward service to Vienna (VIE) connects conveniently. Small pets (up to 8kg including carrier) can travel in the cabin throughout. Larger dogs travel in the pressurised hold.

        When booking, confirm with LOT that your pet reservation is valid for the full itinerary, not just the first leg. Airlines handle this differently, and a cabin pet space booked for Vilnius to Warsaw does not automatically carry through to the Warsaw to Vienna leg.

        Lufthansa via Frankfurt is an alternative, offering slightly different departure times and a larger cargo network for bigger dogs. airBaltic via Riga adds an extra connection but can work well depending on your dates.

        One carrier to avoid: Ryanair and Wizz Air both operate services from Vilnius and Kaunas airports in Lithuania, but neither accepts pets. Do not book them for this journey.

    - heading: "Austrian entry requirements: what to prepare"
      body: |
        Austria's entry requirements for pets from EU member states are standard: ISO 11784/11785 microchip, a valid rabies vaccination, and an EU pet passport or AHC. For a first-time rabies vaccination, there is a 21-day wait before the pet can travel. Boosters administered within the valid period take effect immediately.

        The microchip must have been implanted before or on the date of the first rabies vaccination. If your pet has an older chip that was placed after their first vaccine, speak to an official vet about how this is recorded in the documentation.

        Austrian border veterinary officers check documents on arrival at Vienna Airport (VIE). Keep your pet's passport or AHC accessible throughout the journey.

    - heading: "Austrian state breed rules"
      body: |
        Austria has no federal breed ban. However, each of the nine Bundeslaender (federal states) has its own animal welfare and dog-keeping regulations. Vienna requires a Sachkundenachweis (competency certificate) and a behavioural assessment for certain breeds, including Rottweilers and several bully-type dogs. Other states have different requirements or none at all.

        If you are relocating to Vienna or another specific state, check the local rules before you travel. The BMSGPK website (sozialministerium.at) points to individual state guidelines.

        For the vast majority of owners moving dogs or cats of common breeds, Austrian state rules will not be a barrier to travel.

  - question: "What document is better for this route: EU pet passport or AHC?"
    answer: "The EU pet passport is the better long-term option. It lasts the lifetime of your pet and is accepted across the EU and several non-EU countries. The AHC is a one-trip document, valid for 10 days from the endorsement date. For a one-way move, either works. For anyone who may travel again, the EU passport is more convenient."
