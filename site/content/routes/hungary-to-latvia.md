  sections:
    - heading: "Budapest to Riga: cargo options when Wizz Air cannot help"
      body: |
        Wizz Air is Budapest's dominant carrier. It runs an extensive network of European routes from Budapest Ferenc Liszt Airport (BUD). However, Wizz Air does not carry live animals in the cargo hold. This is a hard policy, not a case-by-case restriction. If your dog is too large for the cabin, Wizz Air cannot be used on any Budapest route.

        For large dogs moving from Budapest to Riga, the two practical options are:

        airBaltic direct (where available): airBaltic operates Budapest to Riga on some schedules. Since Riga is airBaltic's home hub, arriving there on airBaltic with a cargo pet means the animal arrives at the carrier's own infrastructure. airBaltic Cargo handles live animal arrivals at RIX with solid processes. Check airBaltic's current schedule for direct BUD-RIX availability on your travel dates, as the frequency varies by season.

        LOT Polish Airlines via Warsaw: Budapest to Warsaw on LOT, then Warsaw to Riga on LOT. Warsaw Chopin has established live animal cargo handling as LOT's home base. The connection adds time but the full routing stays within one carrier, which simplifies the cargo coordination.

        For small cabin pets, Wizz Air may work if the pet and carrier meet their specific size and weight limits. Confirm with Wizz Air directly.

    - heading: "PVD at Riga: what to expect for a Hungarian pet"
      body: |
        Latvia's PVD (Pārtikas un veterinārais dienests) processes intra-EU pet arrivals at Riga Airport. For a Hungarian pet with a valid EU pet passport, the process is the same as for any other EU member state arrival: occasional spot check, microchip scan matched to passport, vaccination dates confirmed.

        The EU pet passport issued by a NÉBIH-authorised vet in Hungary is accepted by PVD without any additional documentation. No import permit is required, no titre test, no quarantine.

        The compliance point to check before travel is the sequence in the passport. The microchip must be recorded before the rabies vaccination entry. This is the most common error on EU pet passports, and it is the first thing a PVD officer checks when they review a document.
