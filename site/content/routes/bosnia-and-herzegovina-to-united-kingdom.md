  overview: |
    Sarajevo to London looks simple on a flight search. Wizz Air and Ryanair both fly it directly. Neither will carry your pet.

    That's the twist on this route. Bosnia and Herzegovina is a UK Part 2 listed country, so the regulatory side is genuinely straightforward: no titre test, no 90-day wait, standard AHC, tapeworm treatment for dogs within the usual window. The complication is entirely logistical. You need Turkish Airlines via Istanbul, Austrian Airlines via Vienna, or Lufthansa via Frankfurt, none of which fly Sarajevo to London nonstop.

    Once you've picked the right carrier, this is one of the shorter timelines on this site. Eight to twelve weeks is realistic for most pets.
  sections:
    - heading: "Why listed status makes the paperwork easy"
      body: |
        Bosnia and Herzegovina sits on the UK's Part 2 list of rabies-listed countries. That single classification removes the titre test and its 90-day wait, the step that adds the most time to routes from unlisted countries. What's left is the standard package: microchip before vaccination, a 21-day wait after the rabies shot, an AHC from a BiH-endorsed vet within 10 days of travel, and tapeworm treatment for dogs in the 24 to 120 hour window before arrival.

        The State Veterinary Office of Bosnia and Herzegovina, working through its Border Veterinary Inspection Department, handles the endorsement side, typically within 2 to 3 working days. That's a fast turnaround by regional standards and one of the reasons this route can move quicker than its neighbours.

    - heading: "The flight booking trap"
      body: |
        Sarajevo has direct flights to London on two carriers, Wizz Air and Ryanair, and both are genuinely useful for a human passenger. Neither carries pets. Wizz Air's policy excludes all animals except certified assistance dogs, in the cabin or the hold. Ryanair's position is the same: no pets in any class, on any route.

        That pushes pet travel onto a connecting itinerary. Turkish Airlines via Istanbul, Austrian Airlines via Vienna, and Lufthansa via Frankfurt all serve Sarajevo and all carry pets, either in the cabin for small animals or as cargo for larger ones. It is worth booking the pet's travel first and building the human itinerary around it, rather than assuming the cheapest direct flight will work for both.

    answer: "No. Bosnia and Herzegovina is on the UK's Part 2 list of rabies-listed countries, which means the titre test and 90-day wait that apply to unlisted countries do not apply here. A standard AHC, current rabies vaccination, and correct microchip timing are what's needed."
  - question: "Can I fly my pet on the direct Sarajevo to London route?"
    answer: "Not on the two carriers that fly it. Wizz Air and Ryanair both operate direct Sarajevo-London flights, and neither carries pets beyond certified assistance dogs. You'll need a connecting itinerary on Turkish Airlines, Austrian Airlines, or Lufthansa instead."
  - question: "Does my dog need tapeworm treatment for UK entry?"
    answer: "Yes. Dogs need tapeworm treatment recorded between 24 and 120 hours before your pet's scheduled arrival time in Great Britain. Cats and ferrets are exempt. Calculate the window from your actual landing time, especially if you're routing through a connecting hub with a layover."
  - question: "How long does the export process take on the Bosnia and Herzegovina side?"
    answer: "The State Veterinary Office endorsement typically takes 2 to 3 working days once the health certificate is prepared by your vet (source: Veterinary Office of Bosnia and Herzegovina, checked 17 July 2026). Combined with the 21-day post-vaccination wait, this is one of the faster origin-side processes covered on this site."
  - question: "Will quarantine apply if something goes wrong with my paperwork?"
    answer: "Yes, potentially. Even though Bosnia and Herzegovina's listed status removes the titre test requirement, a pet that arrives with incomplete or incorrect documentation, or that travelled by a non-approved route, can face penalty quarantine of up to 120 days at the owner's expense. Listed status makes the standard process simpler; it doesn't remove the risk of getting the paperwork wrong."
