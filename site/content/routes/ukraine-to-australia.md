  h1: "Pet Transport from Ukraine to Australia"
  overview: |
    Six months. That is the minimum residency Australia's border authority demands in an approved country before it will even process a non-approved-country pet import, and Ukraine sits outside that approved list entirely. Add the fact that no flight leaves any Ukrainian airport, and this becomes one of the longest routes on the whole site: 42 to 56 weeks, realistically, from a standing start.

    There is a workable overlap here that most non-approved-country owners never get. Because Ukraine's airspace is closed, every pet already has to cross overland into Poland or Romania before a flight is possible. Poland happens to be one of DAFF's own Group 3 approved countries. Time the paperwork right, and that forced stopover pays double duty.

  sections:
    - heading: "Why Ukraine gets DAFF's toughest pathway"
      body: |
        DAFF sorts origin countries into Groups 1 to 3, each with its own document set, and treats everything outside those groups as non-approved. Ukraine falls into that non-approved bracket. Instead of the RNATT-and-180-day-wait process that applies to a Group 3 country like Poland or the UK, a non-approved-country pet needs 180 consecutive days of residency in an approved country immediately before export, assessed against the same RNATT test and the same 180-day clock, only now tied to physical presence as well as paperwork.

        DAFF's own guidance on this reviews applications case by case rather than against a fixed checklist, which is part of why this route runs longer, and less predictably, than a standard Group 3 move.

    - heading: "The Poland stopover that pays double duty"
      body: |
        Most owners moving a pet from a non-approved country have to arrange a deliberate relocation to somewhere like the UK or Singapore purely to sit out DAFF's residency requirement. A Ukraine-origin pet does not need that extra step, because it is already crossing into Poland or Romania anyway, since no flight leaves Ukraine itself.

        Poland is a DAFF Group 3 approved country. Draw the RNATT blood sample early in the Polish stay, rather than waiting weeks or months to get around to it, and the 180-day titre wait and the 180-day residency requirement run on the same calendar instead of stacking one after the other. Get that sequencing wrong, drawing blood late in the stay, say, and the two clocks stop lining up, adding months back onto the route for no biological reason.

    - heading: "Mickleham, BICON, and building a realistic calendar"
      body: |
        Once the 180-day wait clears and the BICON import permit comes through, the pet still needs a booked slot at the Mickleham Post Entry Quarantine Facility outside Melbourne. Quarantine there typically runs 10 to 30 days for a compliant arrival, though DAFF can extend it for a non-approved-country case, and spaces have to be reserved ahead of any flight, not after.

        Realistically, 42 to 56 weeks covers vaccination, the overland crossing, the RNATT wait, the BICON assessment and the Mickleham stay itself. Nothing about that number is fixed by DAFF in writing for non-approved countries specifically, so build in margin rather than planning to the day.
