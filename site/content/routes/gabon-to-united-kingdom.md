content:
  h1: "Pet Transport from Gabon to the United Kingdom"
  overview: |
    Gabon's paperwork has to be in French, and that single detail changes more about this route than the flights do. Nothing here is unmanageable, but it is slower than most Caribbean or European routes on this site: Gabon sits outside both Part 1 and Part 2 of the UK's approved country list, which triggers the full titre test and 90-day wait.

    That wait is the real driver of the timeline, not the flight. Blood has to be drawn at least 30 days after the rabies vaccination, and a successful result then starts a further three-month clock before the pet can travel. Add the local documentation, translated into French, and a routing that always involves a connection, and 16 to 20 weeks becomes the realistic planning window.

    Two carriers cover this corridor without a domestic detour: Air France through Paris, and Ethiopian Airlines through Addis Ababa. Neither flies Libreville to London directly, because no airline does.

  sections:
    - heading: "Why Gabon lands in the unlisted category"
      body: |
        The UK's Part 1 and Part 2 lists trace back to Annex II of Commission Implementing Regulation (EU) No 577/2013, still the reference point for the UK's own post-Brexit list. Part 1 covers a handful of European microstates. Part 2 runs to roughly 50 territories, from Argentina to Antigua and Barbuda. Gabon appears in neither.

        Unlisted status is what pulls in the titre test, the 90-day wait, and closer scrutiny of the paperwork on arrival. It doesn't mean quarantine, provided the documentation and timing are correct, but it does mean nothing here can be rushed.

    - heading: "Getting the titre test timeline right"
      body: |
        The sequence is fixed and unforgiving: microchip first, then rabies vaccination, then a blood draw at least 30 days later, sent to an APHA-approved laboratory capable of running FAVN or ELISA testing. A result of at least 0.5 IU/ml passes. From that pass date, the UK still wants a full three months before the pet can enter.

        Since no confirmed rabies serology laboratory operates inside Gabon, the blood sample usually has to travel before a result comes back, which adds real time on top of the standard lab turnaround. Build that courier delay into the plan rather than assuming a fixed lab date.

    - heading: "The two routes out of Libreville, and what each carrier allows"
      body: |
        Air France's AF926 is the more direct option, a nonstop Boeing 787 service from Libreville into Paris CDG, with an onward connection to London. Small pets under 8kg including carrier can travel in the cabin on the shorter European leg; anything up to 75kg combined can go in the hold, except brachycephalic breeds, which Air France won't carry in the hold at all.

        Ethiopian Airlines goes the other way round, Libreville to Addis Ababa, then the only nonstop Addis-Heathrow service in the market. It's a longer routing on paper but a single carrier the whole way, which some owners prefer for a live animal move. Snub-nosed breeds aren't accepted on Ethiopian at all, so that rules it out for some pets regardless of everything else.
