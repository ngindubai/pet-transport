  sections:
    - heading: "Why the Swiss pet passport works for Lithuania"
      body: |
        Lithuania is an EU member state and part of the Schengen Area. Switzerland, though not EU, has bilateral agreements with the EU under which the Swiss pet passport carries the same legal weight as an EU pet passport for travel between Switzerland and EU countries.

        This means you do not need to obtain an AHC (Animal Health Certificate) for each trip to Lithuania if you hold a valid Swiss pet passport. The passport is issued by your Swiss vet, records the microchip and vaccination history, and is updated at each veterinary appointment. The BLV (Federal Food Safety and Veterinary Office) confirmed this remains the case following EU legislation updates in April 2026.

    - heading: "Cargo routing from Zurich to Vilnius"
      body: |
        Vilnius International Airport (VNO) is Lithuania's main international hub. There is no direct Zurich to Vilnius cargo service for pets. LOT Polish Airlines via Warsaw (WAW) is a reliable routing option: LOT accepts live animal cargo and Warsaw Chopin Airport has good live animal handling facilities.

        For cabin travel, Ryanair operates connections to Vilnius and Wizz Air to both Vilnius and Kaunas. Both accept small pets in the cabin within their size and weight limits. If your pet needs cargo transport, plan the LOT routing at least four weeks ahead.
