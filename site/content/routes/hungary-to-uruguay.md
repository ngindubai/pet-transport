content:
  h1: "Pet Transport from Hungary to Uruguay"
  overview: |
    Moving a pet from Hungary to Uruguay requires an MGAP import permit obtained in advance and, for dogs, a Praziquantel treatment timed within a specific window before arrival. Neither requirement is complicated, but both need to be planned properly. Allow at least six weeks from the start of preparation to departure, and eight to ten if the MGAP permit has never been obtained before.

    Uruguay's MGAP (Ministerio de Ganaderia, Agricultura y Pesca, gub.uy/ministerio-ganaderia-agricultura-pesca) inspects all pets at Carrasco International Airport (MVD) in Montevideo. There is no quarantine. When paperwork is complete and the Praziquantel timing is correctly documented, arrival inspection is a routine process.

    Budapest Ferenc Liszt International Airport (BUD) connects to Montevideo (MVD) via LATAM Airlines through Lima or Sao Paulo, or via European hubs with Iberia or Air France. Wizz Air from Budapest to Madrid supports the Iberia cargo routing. Total transit can be 24-48 hours; this matters when calculating Praziquantel treatment timing.

  sections:
    - heading: "MGAP Import Permit"
      body: |
        MGAP requires an import permit for all dogs and cats entering Uruguay, and it must be in hand before the animal travels. Apply via gub.uy/ministerio-ganaderia-agricultura-pesca at least six to ten weeks before your planned departure from Budapest. The permit covers the specific animal being imported and must match the details on the health certificate: species, breed, microchip number, and owner information.

        A transport agent with South American connections can often expedite the MGAP application process. Keep the original permit with the travel documents; MGAP inspectors at Carrasco Airport (MVD) will cross-check it against the health certificate on arrival.

    - heading: "Praziquantel Treatment Timing for Dogs"
      body: |
        All dogs entering Uruguay must receive a Praziquantel treatment between 72 hours and 30 days before arrival. An important detail for the Budapest to Montevideo route: the timing counts from arrival in Uruguay, not departure from Budapest. The transit can take 24-48 hours, so if you give the treatment on the day before departure, it will typically still be within the window on arrival, but confirm the exact transit schedule before administering the dose.

        The Praziquantel product, dose, and treatment date must be recorded on the official health certificate by the Nebih-authorised official vet. Use a product with Praziquantel as the named active ingredient; general worming products may not satisfy Uruguay's specific requirement. Your private vet administers the treatment; the official vet documents it.

    - heading: "Hungarian Export Certification"
      body: |
        The EU-format health certificate for this route must be issued by a Nebih-authorised official vet and endorsed by Nebih (Nemzeti Elelmiszerlanc-biztonsagi Hivatal, nebih.gov.hu). Not all private clinics in Hungary hold Nebih authorisation; identify the right official vet before making other arrangements. Nebih endorsement takes one to three working days after the vet's signature.

        The certificate is valid for 10 days from signing. Given the 24-48 hour transit to Montevideo, the official vet appointment should be no earlier than 8-9 days before the expected MVD arrival, leaving the full document validity intact for both the journey and the MGAP inspection. Budapest Ferenc Liszt Airport (BUD) is the cargo departure hub.

    - heading: "Cargo Options and Journey Length"
      body: |
        LATAM Airlines is the primary choice for Hungary to Uruguay cargo, routing through Lima (LIM) or Sao Paulo (GRU) before the final leg to Montevideo (MVD). Iberia via Madrid (MAD) and Air France via Paris CDG both offer connecting cargo services to MVD. Wizz Air's service from Budapest to Madrid supports cargo transfers to Iberia at MAD.

        The Budapest to Montevideo journey is one of the longer cargo routes from Central Europe to South America. Air freight costs range from EUR 1,000 to EUR 2,800 depending on animal size and crate dimensions. Total all-in costs for a single-pet move from Budapest to MVD typically run EUR 1,500-3,500, including the Nebih certificate, crate, and agent fees if applicable.

faqs:
  - question: "Do I need an MGAP import permit before starting vet preparation in Hungary?"
    answer: "Yes. The MGAP import permit must be obtained before the animal travels and should ideally be in hand before any veterinary preparation begins. Apply at gub.uy/ministerio-ganaderia-agricultura-pesca at least six to ten weeks before departure."
  - question: "How do I calculate the Praziquantel treatment timing?"
    answer: "Count backwards from the expected arrival time at Carrasco International Airport (MVD) in Montevideo, not from the Budapest departure. The treatment must fall within the 72-hour to 30-day window before MVD arrival. Given a 24-48 hour transit from Budapest, giving the treatment one to three days before BUD departure is usually correct, but confirm with your vet."
  - question: "Is there quarantine in Uruguay?"
    answer: "No. MGAP inspects pets at Carrasco International Airport (MVD) on arrival. If documents are correct, the pet is released immediately."
  - question: "Do cats need the Praziquantel treatment?"
    answer: "No. The Praziquantel requirement applies to dogs only. Cats travelling to Uruguay need a microchip, valid rabies vaccination (30 days to 12 months before entry), current core vaccinations, and the MGAP import permit."
  - question: "Who is the official vet in Hungary for this route?"
    answer: "A Nebih-authorised official vet, with the certificate endorsed by Nebih (nebih.gov.hu). Not all private vets hold this authorisation; confirm the vet's official status before booking the pre-export examination."
