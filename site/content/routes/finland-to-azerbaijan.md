  h1: "Pet Transport from Finland to Azerbaijan"
  overview: |
    Finland to Azerbaijan is a manageable route with one requirement that surprises dog owners: an Echinococcus (tapeworm) treatment that must be administered within a specific window before entry, and recorded on the health certificate. Miss this step and your dog will face delays at Baku's Heydar Aliyev International Airport.

    The broader paperwork is straightforward from Finland. No titre test is required for EU-origin pets. The timeline from starting preparations to travel day runs 4 to 6 weeks, with the main variable being the rabies vaccination window (at least 21 days before entry, not more than 12 months). All entry into Azerbaijan must be via GYD. Other airports are not accepted.

    Finnish owners also need to account for the Ruokavirasto endorsement step on the export health certificate, which adds 1 to 2 working days to the certificate's production timeline.

  sections:
    - heading: "The Echinococcus requirement for dogs: get this right"
      body: |
        Azerbaijan requires dogs to receive an antiparasitic treatment targeting Echinococcus (tapeworm) before entry. The treatment must be administered between 24 and 120 hours before the animal's entry into the country. It must be carried out by a licensed veterinarian and recorded in the health certificate.

        For practical planning, this means the Echinococcus treatment sits inside your health certificate appointment. The official vet who issues the certificate will administer the treatment and record it at the same time. The trick is making sure the treatment date falls within the 24-120 hour window relative to your arrival in Baku, not your departure from Helsinki.

        If your total journey time, including transit in Istanbul or Warsaw, runs to 20 hours, make sure the treatment is given at least 28 hours before you expect to land at GYD. Cats are not subject to this requirement.

    - heading: "Entering at GYD only: why this matters for routing"
      body: |
        Azerbaijan only accepts pet entries through Heydar Aliyev International Airport (GYD) in Baku. This is not a guideline; it is enforced at the point of import. Routing your pet through any other Azerbaijani airport or land border will not be accepted.

        The practical consequence for routing from Helsinki is that your cargo itinerary must terminate at GYD. The most common routing is HEL to Istanbul (IST) then IST to GYD with Turkish Airlines Cargo. LOT Polish Airlines via Warsaw is a second option. Whichever routing you choose, confirm live animal cargo acceptance on both legs separately: the airline does not automatically carry an animal booked on leg one through to leg two without its own cargo booking.

        The Azerbaijan Ministry of Agriculture (agro.gov.az) oversees veterinary inspections at GYD. Inspection on arrival is standard for all incoming animals. Compliant arrivals are cleared on the same day.

    - heading: "Finnish export paperwork and the Ruokavirasto endorsement"
      body: |
        Leaving Finland for a non-EU country means your pet needs more than a standard EU pet passport. The official export health certificate must be issued by a Ruokavirasto-authorised official vet and then formally endorsed by Ruokavirasto before it is valid for export.

        Ruokavirasto was known as Evira until the 2019 merger. The process itself has not changed: the official vet issues the certificate, you or your agent submits it to the regional Ruokavirasto office, and the endorsement comes back within 1 to 2 working days. The certificate is valid for 10 days from the date of examination, so time the vet appointment carefully to leave room for endorsement and any last-minute adjustments.

        Full guidance is at ruokavirasto.fi. If you use an IPATA-registered agent, they will manage the submission on your behalf.
