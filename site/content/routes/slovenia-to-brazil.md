      body: |
        Slovenia's size means Ljubljana simply does not carry the passenger or freight volumes that support a long-haul route on its own. For a Slovenia-to-Brazil pet move, the practical question is not whether there will be a connection, but which one suits your pet best.

        Frankfurt, reached via Lufthansa, plugs into an established Latin America freight operation and tends to be the default recommendation.     - heading: "UVHVVR and the certificate Brazil actually wants"
      body: |
        The standard EU pet passport, familiar to anyone who has moved a pet within Europe, does not satisfy Brazil's requirements on its own. UVHVVR, Slovenia's Administration for Food Safety, Veterinary Sector and Plant Protection, authorises a specific export certificate for third-country destinations, which your vet completes and UVHVVR then endorses.

        This certificate is only valid for 10 days from the endorsement date, and that window has to still be open when your pet lands in Brazil, not just when it leaves Slovenia. Given the connection involved in most routings from Ljubljana, build a little slack into the endorsement date rather than cutting it fine against a long, multi-leg itinerary.

faqs:
  - question: "Can I fly my pet directly from Ljubljana to Brazil?"
    answer: "No. There is no direct long-haul cargo or passenger service from Ljubljana to Brazil. Every pet moving on this route connects through a larger European hub, most commonly Frankfurt, Zurich, Paris or Istanbul, before the transatlantic leg."
  - question: "Does Brazil require quarantine for pets from Slovenia?"
    answer: "No. Brazil does not require quarantine for dogs and cats from Slovenia, provided the microchip, rabies vaccination, antiparasitic treatment record (for dogs) and UVHVVR-endorsed CVI are all in order. MAPA inspects documentation and pet health at the arrival airport rather than holding pets for a set period."
  - question: "Is a titre test needed for a pet travelling from Slovenia to Brazil?"
    answer: "No. Brazil does not ask for a FAVN titre test from Slovenian pets. A current rabies vaccination, with the standard 30-day wait for a first-time vaccination, is what MAPA requires instead."
  - question: "What does UVHVVR do in this process?"
    answer: "UVHVVR is Slovenia's competent authority for animal health and food safety. It authorises the export health certificate your vet issues for the Brazil move, a separate document from the standard EU pet passport, since Brazil is a non-EU destination with its own certificate format."
  - question: "Is the Zurich connection or the Frankfurt connection better for a Slovenia-Brazil pet move?"
    answer: "Zurich has the advantage that SWISS operates a genuine nonstop Zurich-Sao Paulo service, so once your pet reaches Zurich the rest of the journey is a single flight. Frankfurt has more frequent Latin America cargo capacity overall through Lufthansa. Compare live availability on your travel dates rather than assuming one is always better."
  - question: "How much does a typical move cost from Slovenia to Brazil?"
