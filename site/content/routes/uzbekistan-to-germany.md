  sections:
    - heading: "A genuinely direct option: Tashkent to Frankfurt"
      body: |
        Uzbekistan Airways runs its Tashkent-Frankfurt service nonstop, year-round, on Mondays and Thursdays. It's a limited schedule compared with a daily connection, so confirm cargo space early rather than assuming a seat will be available on short notice. Turkish Airlines via Istanbul offers a far more frequent alternative if the twice-weekly direct flight doesn't line up with your titre test timeline.

        Either way, the Frankfurt Animal Lounge handles the German-side arrival, giving the pet a proper live-animal inspection facility rather than a general cargo shed.

    - heading: "Why the EU titre test still applies"
      body: |
        Uzbekistan sits outside the EU's list of low-risk third countries, so Germany requires the standard sequence: microchip before vaccination, a 30-day gap before the titre blood draw, a result of at least 0.5 IU/ml, then a fixed 90 days from the draw date, under Commission Delegated Regulation (EU) 2026/131.

        Few, if any, EU-approved laboratories operate inside Uzbekistan, so plan for the blood sample to travel abroad for testing. On the export side, the territorial veterinary service, part of the Committee of Veterinary and Livestock Development that replaced the old State Committee in 2023, issues the certificate at the TAS border checkpoint close to departure.
