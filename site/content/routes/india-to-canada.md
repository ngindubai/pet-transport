content:
  h1: "Pet Transport from India to Canada"
  overview: |
    Canada is one of the more accessible destinations for pets relocating from India, compared to the UK's unlisted-country requirements or Australia's complete ban on direct Indian imports. The Canadian Food Inspection Agency (CFIA) requires a valid rabies vaccination for dogs and an official health certificate for both dogs and cats, but there is no titre test requirement and no routine quarantine for correctly documented personal pets. Source: CFIA, inspection.gc.ca.

    The India side of the move follows the same pattern as all India-origin international pet exports: an AQCS No-Objection Certificate is required, along with an official health certificate endorsed by AQCS. The AQCS process typically takes 4-6 weeks and involves two clinical examinations. Allow 6-12 weeks from starting preparations to travel day, which is significantly shorter than the UK or Australian pathways.

    This corridor is heavily used by families relocating under Canada's Express Entry immigration programme, post-graduate visa holders, and IT workers. The process is well understood by IPATA agents operating between India and Canada.

  sections:
    - heading: "CFIA requirements: what Canada needs from Indian pets"
      body: |
        CFIA (Canadian Food Inspection Agency) manages all live animal imports to Canada. For personal pet dogs from India, the requirements are: a valid rabies vaccination (the vaccination must not have lapsed; if the most recent rabies vaccine is more than 3 years old, re-vaccination is required before travel), and an official health certificate from a registered vet, endorsed by AQCS. Microchipping is not federally mandated by CFIA for entry, but ISO 11784/11785 chips are required by all airlines for cargo transport and are strongly recommended regardless.

        Cats entering Canada from India require an official health certificate but no rabies vaccination. The health certificate confirms the cat is free from infectious disease and fit to travel. Both dogs and cats are inspected by CBSA (Canada Border Services Agency) on arrival. If documentation is incomplete or the pet appears unwell, CBSA can detain the animal at the owner's expense while the issue is resolved.

        No import permit is required for personal pet imports of 1-2 animals intended for personal use. If you are importing 3 or more dogs, or importing for any commercial purpose, a CFIA import permit is required. Check the current CFIA rules at inspection.gc.ca before travel.

    - heading: "AQCS export documentation: what India requires on departure"
      body: |
        All pet exports from India require an AQCS No-Objection Certificate. Apply to the AQCS regional office nearest your departure city: offices are in Delhi, Mumbai, Chennai, Kolkata, Bangalore, and Hyderabad. Allow 4-6 weeks. Two clinical examinations are required as part of the AQCS process: the first approximately 7 days before travel, the second within 2 days of departure. The health certificate is issued by an AQCS-registered vet following the second examination and endorsed by AQCS. It is valid 14 days from issue.

        The health certificate must be in a format that satisfies CFIA's requirements. An IPATA-registered agent based in India with experience on the India-Canada corridor will know the correct format and can manage the AQCS process from application to endorsement. First-time exporters often underestimate the processing time, so starting the NOC application as soon as you have a firm travel date is advisable.

        Keep original copies of all vaccination records, the AQCS NOC, and the health certificate. CBSA inspectors may want to see original documents. Laminated or photocopied certificates are not acceptable.

    - heading: "Airline options for India to Canada pet cargo"
      body: |
        No airline accepts pets in the cabin on India-Canada international routes. All pets travel as cargo. Small pets that meet airline size and weight limits for cabin travel may have options on short connecting domestic sectors, but the long-haul India-Canada segment is always cargo. Your pet will travel in a pressurised, temperature-controlled hold in an IATA-compliant crate.

        Air India operates direct services between Indian cities and Toronto (YYZ) and Vancouver (YVR), making it a straightforward option for the cargo booking. Air Canada, Lufthansa, British Airways, and Emirates offer connecting options via their respective hubs. Connecting routes add journey time but can offer better scheduling flexibility.

        Canadian winters are cold, and summer in India and the Gulf can be hot. Both extremes create potential temperature embargoes on live animal cargo. Check the restrictions for your travel month with your chosen carrier before booking. Your pet transport agent will flag seasonal restrictions as part of the route planning.
