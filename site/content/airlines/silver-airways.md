Silver Airways is a regional US carrier serving the southeastern United States, the Bahamas, and Caribbean destinations. Based in Fort Lauderdale, it operates turboprop aircraft (ATR 42 and ATR 72) on short-haul routes that are not always well served by larger carriers.

## Pets in the cabin on Silver Airways

Silver Airways accepts small cats and dogs in the passenger cabin on most routes. The key rules are:

- Only cabin (in-cabin) travel is available; no hold or cargo pet travel
- The pet and carrier combined must be within the current weight limit (confirm with Silver Airways at booking as limits can change)
- Soft-sided carriers that fit under the seat in front are required
- Maximum of one pet per passenger
- Pet must remain in the carrier throughout the flight
- Advance booking required: contact Silver Airways when purchasing your ticket
- A per-flight fee applies for in-cabin pets

## Service animals and emotional support animals

Silver Airways follows US DOT guidelines for service animals (trained service dogs assisting passengers with disabilities). Contact Silver Airways for their current policy on service animals, as rules have evolved in recent years.

## International routes and pet documentation

Silver Airways operates some routes to the Bahamas and Caribbean. For pet travel on international Silver Airways routes, you will also need to comply with the destination country's pet import requirements. The Bahamas, for example, requires a health certificate and may require an import permit.

Confirm both Silver Airways' pet acceptance policy and the destination country's import rules before booking.

## Alternatives for larger pets

If you need to travel in the southeastern USA or to Caribbean destinations with a larger dog or a pet that cannot travel in cabin, consider:
- American Airlines (accepts pets as checked baggage on some routes)
- Delta Air Lines (accepts small pets in cabin)
- Contour Airlines or other regional carriers serving similar routes
