JetBlue Airways is a major US airline with a significant domestic network and international routes to the Caribbean, Mexico, Canada, the UK, and parts of South America. JetBlue operates the JetPaws programme for in-cabin pet travel - but unlike many other major US carriers, JetBlue does not accept pets as hold cargo.

**JetPaws: cabin-only pet policy**

JetBlue's approach is cabin or nothing. Small pets (cats and small dogs) can travel in the cabin under the seat. Larger pets cannot travel on JetBlue at all - if your dog is too big for a cabin carrier, you'll need a different airline.

JetPaws requirements:
- Pet must stay in the carrier throughout the flight
- Combined weight of pet and carrier: 20lbs (approx 9kg) maximum
- Carrier must fit under the seat in front (check current approved dimensions)
- One pet per customer
- Cats and small dogs only - no other species
- A JetPaws fee applies per segment

**Breed restrictions**

JetBlue restricts brachycephalic breeds from in-cabin travel. These include Bulldogs (all types), Pugs, French Bulldogs, Boston Terriers, and similar flat-faced breeds. Some cats are also restricted. Check the JetBlue JetPaws breed restriction list before booking.

**No cargo pet option**

This is worth emphasising: JetBlue does not accept pets in the hold. If your pet cannot travel in the cabin, JetBlue is not the right airline for your move. For larger dogs travelling within the USA, consider American Airlines, Delta, or United, which operate cargo pet programmes.

**JetBlue's international routes**

JetBlue operates to London Gatwick and Heathrow, Caribbean destinations, Mexico, and parts of South America. Pet acceptance on transatlantic and international routes varies - confirm whether JetPaws is available on your specific international route when booking.

**Booking**

Add a JetPaws reservation when booking online or via JetBlue's customer service. Cabin pet spaces are limited per flight - book early. Fees are charged per segment.
