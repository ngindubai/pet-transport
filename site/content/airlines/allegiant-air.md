Allegiant Air is a US ultra-low-cost carrier operating primarily point-to-point domestic US routes from secondary airports. Its hub is Las Vegas (LAS) and it connects dozens of smaller US cities without hub transfers.

## In-cabin pets only

Allegiant accepts small dogs and cats in the cabin. The combined weight of the animal and carrier must not exceed 20 lbs (approximately 9 kg). The soft-sided carrier must fit under the seat in front of you throughout the flight.

A pet fee is charged per carrier per one-way segment. Space is limited: add your pet to the booking as early as possible and confirm availability.

## No hold or cargo pet service

Allegiant does not accept pets in the hold or as checked baggage. This is standard for US ultra-low-cost carriers. Large dogs cannot fly on Allegiant.

For large dog owners or international pet transport, full-service carriers such as Delta, United, American Airlines, or Lufthansa provide hold and cargo pet services.

## What Allegiant is good for

Allegiant is useful for short domestic hops with a small dog or cat where you can keep your pet in the cabin. Many Allegiant routes connect mid-sized US cities to popular leisure destinations in Florida, Arizona, and Nevada.

For any international move, Allegiant is not part of the solution for the pet's transport, but you could theoretically use Allegiant for your own connecting flight while your pet travels on a different carrier.

## Booking pets on Allegiant

Add the pet to your booking at allegiantair.com or contact Allegiant's customer service. Pet bookings cannot be added to tickets purchased through third-party booking sites: book directly with Allegiant and add the pet in the same transaction.
