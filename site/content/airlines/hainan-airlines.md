Hainan Airlines, operating under IATA code HU, is one of China's largest private carriers. Based in Haikou on Hainan Island, it operates an extensive domestic Chinese network and international routes to North America (Seattle, Los Angeles, Chicago), Europe (London, Brussels, Prague, Edinburgh), and destinations across Asia and Australia. For pet owners, Hainan Airlines is relevant primarily as a carrier connecting China with international destinations.

## Cabin Pet Policy

**International routes:** Hainan Airlines does **not** accept pets in the passenger cabin on international flights. All pets on international routes travel as cargo hold freight.

**Domestic China routes:** On domestic routes within China, Hainan Airlines accepts small pets in the cabin:
- Combined weight of pet plus soft carrier: under 5 kg
- Carrier must fit under the seat; dimensions within the under-seat limit
- Advance booking required; limited allocation per flight
- Not all domestic routes accept cabin pets; confirm at the time of booking

## International Cargo Hold Policy

For international pet transport via Hainan Airlines cargo:

- Bookings for live animals are made through **HNA Cargo** or an approved freight forwarding agent; direct passenger booking does not cover live animal international cargo
- **IATA-compliant hard-shell crate** required; the crate must meet IATA Live Animals Regulations specifications for ventilation, locking, and interior dimensions
- Combined weight (pet plus crate): confirm the acceptable limit for the specific route and aircraft type with HNA Cargo in advance
- **Temperature embargoes**: Hainan Airlines applies live animal restrictions during periods of extreme heat and cold, particularly in summer (June to September) when tarmac temperatures at Chinese airports can be extreme

## Brachycephalic Breed Restrictions

Hainan Airlines follows China's general airline restrictions for snub-nosed breeds. Flat-faced dogs and cats (Pug, English Bulldog, French Bulldog, Persian, Exotic Shorthair, Boston Terrier, Shih Tzu and similar) are subject to additional restrictions or exclusion on cargo routes. Confirm your specific breed's acceptance with HNA Cargo before booking.

## Importing Pets to China via Hainan Airlines Routes

Regardless of carrier, all pets entering China must comply with Chinese customs and agricultural import rules:

- **Import permit** from the General Administration of Customs (GACC): obtained through the Chinese embassy or an approved agent before arrival
- **ISO microchip**: recorded in all documentation
- **Rabies vaccination**: current and government-endorsed
- **Health certificate**: endorsed by the national veterinary authority of the exporting country; must match GACC-required format
- **Approved entry airports**: only designated ports of entry accept live animals; confirm with GACC; Haikou Meilan (HAK) is one of the airports with live animal import capability, though Beijing (PEK) and Shanghai (PVG) remain the main cargo arrival points for most international routes
- **Quarantine on arrival**: typically 30 days in a government quarantine facility
