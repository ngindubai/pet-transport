Transavia is a low-cost European carrier and part of the Air France-KLM Group. It operates from Amsterdam Schiphol and Paris Orly to a wide range of European and North African destinations. For small pet owners, Transavia's cabin pet policy offers a budget-friendly option for moves within Europe.

## Cabin Pet Policy

Transavia accepts small dogs and cats in the passenger cabin:

- **Maximum combined weight: 10 kg** (pet plus carrier) -- one of the higher limits for budget carriers
- The carrier must fit under the seat in front
- One pet per booking/passenger
- Limited pet spaces per flight -- book in advance
- Fees apply per flight

**Transavia does not accept pets in the hold.** If your pet is too large for cabin travel, you will need a different airline.

## Approved Carrier Requirements

The carrier must:
- Fit under the seat in front (check dimensions for your aircraft type on the Transavia website)
- Have adequate ventilation (mesh panels)
- Be soft-sided
- Have a secure closure
- Be large enough for your pet to stand, turn, and lie down comfortably

## Booking and Fees

To add a pet to a Transavia booking:
1. Book your flight at transavia.com
2. Add the pet via Manage My Booking or call Transavia customer service
3. Pay the per-sector cabin pet fee (check current pricing at transavia.com)

Do not arrive at the airport to declare a pet without a prior booking -- if in-cabin pet spots are full, your pet cannot travel.

## Health Documentation for European Travel

For travel within the EU (Netherlands, France, and EU destinations):
- **EU Pet Passport** with current rabies vaccination

For travel from the UK to the Netherlands or France:
- **Animal Health Certificate (AHC)** issued within 10 days of travel, APHA-endorsed
- **Tapeworm treatment** for dogs (1 to 5 days before arrival in any EU state)
- Current rabies vaccination

## Amsterdam and Paris as EU Entry Points

Transavia's hubs are both major EU ports of entry for pets:

- **Amsterdam Schiphol** has a dedicated NVWA (Netherlands Food and Consumer Product Safety Authority) veterinary inspection post for arriving pets
- **Paris Orly** has a DGAL (French Directorate General for Food) inspection post

Documentation will be checked on arrival.

## Route Network Relevant to Pet Moves

Transavia is particularly useful for:
- UK expats moving to the Netherlands or France (cabin pets on direct routes)
- Amsterdam-based expats moving to Spanish, Portuguese, or Italian destinations
- Short European hops where a small dog or cat can travel in cabin
