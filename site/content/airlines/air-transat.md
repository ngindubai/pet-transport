Air Transat is Canada's leading leisure airline, operating from Montreal and Toronto to destinations in Europe, the Caribbean, Mexico, and Central America. For pet owners, Air Transat has one of the more generous cabin weight allowances among North American carriers.

## Cabin Pet Policy

Air Transat accepts small dogs and cats in the passenger cabin:

- Combined weight limit: **10 kg** (pet plus carrier) -- higher than many competitors
- Carrier must fit under the seat in front
- One pet per passenger
- Maximum number of pets per cabin (typically 4 to 6) -- reserve early
- Advance booking required

The 10 kg combined limit means medium-small dogs may qualify that would exceed the limits on other carriers.

## Approved Carriers for Cabin Travel

Your carrier must:
- Fit comfortably under the seat in front (approximately 21 x 38 x 55 cm -- check the specific aircraft dimensions for your route)
- Have secure closures and adequate ventilation
- Be soft-sided (rigid crates are not appropriate for under-seat storage)
- Provide enough room for the animal to stand, turn, and lie down

Air Transat sells or recommends specific carriers on their website -- check the current approved list.

## Fees

A per-sector cabin pet fee applies. Check current fees at airtransat.com. For transatlantic routes, the fee applies per flight sector, so a return trip means two fees.

## Hold / Cargo Travel

Air Transat also accepts pets as checked baggage (in the hold) on many routes, subject to:
- IATA-compliant rigid crate
- Maximum combined weight of animal plus crate (varies by route -- confirm with Air Transat)
- Temperature restrictions (summer embargoes apply during extreme heat periods)

Brachycephalic breed restrictions apply for hold travel. Check your breed before booking.

## Routes and Restrictions

**Transatlantic (Canada to Europe):** Cabin pets generally accepted
**Canada domestic:** Cabin pets generally accepted
**Sun destinations (Mexico, Cuba, Caribbean):** Policies vary -- some routes do not accept in-cabin pets. Always confirm.

## Booking Your Pet

1. Book your own flight at airtransat.com
2. Call Air Transat customer service (or manage online) to add the pet to your booking
3. Pay the cabin pet fee
4. Receive confirmation with your pet's details recorded

Do not wait until check-in to declare your pet. If the pet spaces are full on your flight, your pet cannot travel.

## Transatlantic Moves with Air Transat

For Canadian families relocating to the UK, France, Spain, Greece, or other Air Transat European destinations, the airline offers a practical direct-route option for small pets travelling in cabin. The Montreal-to-London and Toronto-to-London routes are regular services.

For European entry (EU or UK), you still need to meet destination health certificate requirements regardless of which airline you use.
