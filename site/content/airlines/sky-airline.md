Sky Airline (operated as SKY) is Chile's second-largest airline after LATAM, operating from Santiago's Arturo Merino Benitez International Airport (SCL) and serving Chilean domestic routes as well as South American international destinations. For Chilean residents and expats in South America, Sky Airline is a key domestic and regional carrier.

## In-Cabin Pet Policy

Sky Airline accepts small dogs and cats in the passenger cabin on applicable routes:

- Combined weight of the pet and carrier: typically up to 10 kg
- The carrier must fit under the seat in front of you
- One pet per passenger
- The pet must remain in the carrier for the entire flight
- A pet supplement fee applies

Add your pet when booking your ticket. Cabin pet spaces are limited and cannot be guaranteed if added at the last minute.

## Hold Cargo for Larger Pets

Sky Airline accepts dogs and cats in the hold on applicable routes when the animal exceeds the in-cabin weight limit:

- An IATA-compliant hard-sided crate is required
- The maximum combined weight (pet plus crate) and size dimensions must be confirmed with Sky for your specific route
- Brachycephalic breeds are restricted from hold cargo
- Book through Sky's cargo service or reservations team

## Route Network

Sky Airline's domestic Chile network covers most major Chilean cities:
- Santiago (SCL) hub to Antofagasta (ANF), Calama (CJC), Iquique (IQQ), Arica (ARI), La Serena (LSC), Concepcion (CCP), Temuco (ZCO), Valdivia (ZAL), Puerto Montt (PMC), Punta Arenas (PUQ), and others

International routes from Santiago:
- Lima (LIM), Bogota (BOG), Buenos Aires (EZE/AEP), São Paulo (GRU), Rio de Janeiro (GIG), Medellin (MDE), Guayaquil (GYE), and other South American cities

## Brachycephalic Breed Restrictions

Sky Airline restricts flat-faced breeds from hold cargo due to respiratory risk:
- English Bulldogs, French Bulldogs, Pugs, Boston Terriers, Pekingese
- Persian cats, Himalayan cats, Exotic Shorthair cats

Some restricted breeds may be accepted in-cabin within the weight limit. Confirm with Sky for your specific breed.

## Chile Domestic Pet Travel Notes

For domestic Chilean routes, no specific veterinary documentation is required beyond your pet's regular vaccinations. A health certificate from a Chilean vet is good practice for hold cargo.

For international routes, destination-country import requirements apply. For example:
- Argentina: SENASA health certificate required
- Peru: SENASA Peru import documentation
- Brazil: MAPA/VIGIAGRO requirements (USDA-equivalent for the origin country)

Use a specialist to manage documentation for South American cross-border pet shipments, as each country's requirements differ.
