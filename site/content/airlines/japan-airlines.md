Japan Airlines (JAL), operating under IATA code JL, is Japan's flag carrier and a founding member of the Oneworld alliance. JAL connects Tokyo to destinations across Asia, Europe, North America, Australia and the Pacific, making it a key carrier for pet transport to and from Japan.

## Cabin Pet Policy

Japan Airlines **does not accept pets in the passenger cabin on international flights**. On domestic Japan routes (within Japan), JAL accepts small pets in the cabin subject to the following:

- Combined weight of pet plus carrier: under 10 kg
- Soft-sided carrier required; dimensions within cabin under-seat limit
- Booking required in advance; limited allocation per flight
- Certain breeds excluded (brachycephalic breeds may be restricted on domestic cabin too -- confirm with JAL)

For international routes, all pets travel as **cargo hold freight only**, either as excess baggage (if permitted by the route) or as air cargo through JAL Cargo.

## International Cargo Hold Policy

JAL accepts cats and dogs as cargo on international routes. Key points:

- Pets must travel as **JAL Cargo** (live animal freight) on international routes; this is separate from checked baggage
- IATA-compliant hard-shell crate required; soft crates are not accepted in the cargo hold
- Crate dimensions and combined weight must be within JAL Cargo's live animal acceptance criteria for the specific aircraft type
- Maximum combined weight (pet plus crate): varies by route and aircraft; typically up to 45 kg for standard booking; heavier shipments require advance coordination with JAL Cargo
- **Snub-nosed/brachycephalic breeds**: JAL restricts certain flat-faced breeds on cargo routes; the current breed restriction list is published on the JAL Cargo website; confirm before booking

## Temperature and Seasonal Restrictions

JAL applies temperature embargoes for live animal transport. Periods of extreme heat (Japanese summer, June to September) or extreme cold may result in:

- Refusal of acceptance at certain airports
- Requirement to route through temperature-controlled hubs
- Route-specific embargoes for sensitive breeds

Always confirm temperature acceptance at the time of booking, particularly for summer travel.

## Breed-Specific Notes

**Brachycephalic breeds** (Pug, French Bulldog, Persian cat, etc.) are subject to additional restrictions or outright exclusion on some JAL international cargo routes. Contact JAL Cargo directly for your specific breed before planning.

**Large breeds** (Labrador Retriever and above) are accepted in cargo on most wide-body routes; confirm combined crate weight with JAL Cargo at booking.

## Japan's Animal Quarantine Requirements (AQS)

Regardless of airline, all pets entering Japan must comply with the Animal Quarantine Service (AQS) requirements. Japan is one of the strictest animal import countries in the world:

- **ISO microchip** required
- **Rabies vaccinations**: two vaccinations at least 30 days apart; after the second vaccination, an FAVN titre test at an AQS-approved laboratory; titre result must be 0.5 IU/ml or above
- **180-day waiting period** after a qualifying titre test before entering Japan
- **Import notification** to AQS at least 40 days before arrival
- **Quarantine on arrival**: 12 hours minimum (for qualifying pets from qualifying countries) up to 180 days for non-qualifying arrivals

The full AQS compliance timeline from scratch is typically **7 to 11 months**. Begin preparation well in advance.
