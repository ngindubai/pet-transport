Caribbean Airlines is the flag carrier of Trinidad and Tobago, operating from Piarco International Airport (POS) near Port of Spain. It serves destinations across the Caribbean, as well as New York (JFK), Toronto (YYZ), Miami (MIA), and several South American cities.

## Pets in the cabin

Caribbean Airlines permits small pets in the cabin on some routes, subject to prior approval. The combined weight of the animal and soft-sided carrier must not exceed 10 kg. Only one pet carrier is permitted per passenger.

The carrier must fit comfortably under the seat in front of you. Pets must remain in the carrier throughout the flight and may not be taken out during the journey. Not all routes permit in-cabin pets, so contact Caribbean Airlines customer service to confirm your specific flight before purchasing a ticket.

## Pets in the hold

Larger pets travel in the hold as checked baggage or excess baggage. The crate must meet IATA Live Animal Regulations (LAR): solid floor, ventilated on three sides, secure spring-loaded door, and enough space for the animal to stand at full height, turn around, and lie down naturally.

Notify Caribbean Airlines at the time of booking or at least 48 hours before departure. Hold capacity for live animals is limited.

## Import requirements at your destination

Each destination country has its own rules for pet import. Travelling to Trinidad and Tobago from abroad requires documentation from the Ministry of Agriculture, Land and Fisheries. Travelling from Trinidad to the USA, Canada, or UK requires health certificates and vaccinations as specified by those countries' authorities.

Always research the import rules of your destination country before travel. A pet transport specialist can help coordinate both the airline booking and the documentation.

## Booking and contact

For live animal bookings, contact Caribbean Airlines through their website at caribbean-airlines.com or call the reservations team. The cargo team handles larger dogs travelling as freight.
