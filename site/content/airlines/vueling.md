Vueling is Spain's largest low-cost airline and part of International Airlines Group (IAG), which also includes British Airways and Iberia. Operating primarily from Barcelona and Madrid, Vueling connects Spain to over 100 European destinations. For pet owners moving within Europe, particularly those with Spanish connections, Vueling is a frequently used option.

## Cabin Pet Policy

Vueling accepts small pets in the passenger cabin:

- **Maximum combined weight: 10 kg** (pet plus carrier)
- Carrier must fit under the seat in front
- One pet per passenger
- Limited cabin pet spaces -- book in advance
- A per-sector fee applies

**Vueling does not accept pets in the hold.** The cabin is the only option.

## Carrier Requirements

Your carrier must:
- Fit under the seat in front (check aircraft-specific dimensions at vueling.com)
- Have mesh ventilation panels
- Be soft-sided
- Have secure closures
- Allow your pet to stand, turn, and lie down

Vueling's aircraft include Airbus A319, A320, and A321 types -- under-seat dimensions vary slightly between types. Check the specific aircraft operating your flight.

## Fees and Booking

1. Book your flight at vueling.com
2. Add your pet via Manage My Booking
3. Pay the per-sector cabin pet fee

Current fees are listed at vueling.com. Fees apply per flight segment. Always confirm your pet has been accepted on the flight before arriving at the airport.

## Relevant Routes for Pet Moves

Vueling covers routes particularly useful for:

- **UK to Barcelona, Madrid, Seville, Malaga, Valencia**: UK expats moving to Spain travel frequently on this carrier
- **Barcelona/Madrid to other European cities**: For onward connections within Europe
- **Canary Islands and Balearic Islands routes**: Vueling is the primary carrier between mainland Spain and its island territories

Note: For UK-to-Spain moves, EU entry health certificate requirements apply regardless of carrier.

## Health Documentation for Spain Entry from the UK

For dogs and cats from the UK entering Spain (EU):

1. Microchip (ISO 11784/11785)
2. Current rabies vaccination (at least 21 days old if first vaccine)
3. Animal Health Certificate (AHC) -- APHA-endorsed, issued within 10 days of travel
4. Tapeworm treatment (dogs only) -- 1 to 5 days before arrival in Spain

The AHC is checked on arrival at Barcelona El Prat or Madrid Barajas at the SOIVRE/border veterinary inspection post.

## Practical Notes

- Vueling flights frequently sell out, especially on peak summer routes to Spain. Book early, both for your own seat and the pet space.
- Barcelona and Madrid airports both have clear veterinary inspection procedures for arriving pets with AHCs.
- If your pet exceeds 10 kg combined, Iberia (another IAG carrier, also BCN and MAD based) or an alternative carrier's cargo service will be needed.
