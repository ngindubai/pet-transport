China Airlines, operating under IATA code CI, is Taiwan's flag carrier. Not to be confused with Air China (mainland China, IATA code CA), China Airlines is headquartered in Dayuan, Taoyuan, and serves destinations across Asia, Europe, North America and Australia. China Airlines is a SkyTeam alliance member.

## Cabin Pet Policy

China Airlines accepts small pets in the cabin on selected routes:

- **Eligible pets**: cats and dogs
- **Weight limit**: combined weight of pet plus carrier must not exceed 7 kg
- **Carrier type**: soft-sided, approved carrier that fits under the seat in front
- **Advance booking required**: cabin pet spots are limited; request at time of booking
- **Route eligibility**: cabin pets are accepted on shorter Asian routes and some regional routes; not all long-haul routes accept cabin pets; confirm at time of booking
- **One pet per passenger**: a maximum of one pet per passenger in the cabin; maximum two pets per flight (subject to change)

## Cargo Hold Policy

China Airlines accepts larger pets as checked excess baggage or as air cargo on eligible routes:

- Pets must be in an **IATA-compliant hard-shell crate**
- **Weight limits**: the combined weight of pet plus crate for hold shipment should be confirmed with China Airlines cargo staff at the time of booking
- **Brachycephalic breeds**: China Airlines restricts or excludes flat-faced dogs and cats from cargo hold on some routes, particularly in warm weather; confirm your breed's acceptance with the airline before booking
- **Route-specific restrictions**: confirm hold pet acceptance for your specific route; some routes do not accept live animal cargo

## Breed-Specific Restrictions

China Airlines' current restricted breed list for cargo should be verified directly with the carrier. Typically, breeds subject to restriction include:
- Brachycephalic dogs: Pug, English Bulldog, French Bulldog, Boston Terrier, Shih Tzu, Pekingese and similar
- Brachycephalic cats: Persian, Himalayan, Exotic Shorthair, Scottish Fold (also subject to restrictions at destination)
- Very large breeds with combined crate weight over applicable limits

## Importing Pets to Taiwan

The Bureau of Animal and Plant Health Inspection and Quarantine (BAPHIQ) under Taiwan's Council of Agriculture regulates pet imports:

1. **Microchip**: ISO 15-digit chip required
2. **Rabies vaccination**: current; for pets from most countries, a minimum of two vaccinations at least 30 days apart, with the most recent within the vaccine validity period
3. **Rabies neutralising antibody titre test**: required for pets from most countries; result must be 0.5 IU/ml or above; conducted at a BAPHIQ-approved laboratory; the blood sample must be taken at least 180 days before arrival (this 180-day waiting period is strictly enforced for most origin countries)
4. **Import permit** from BAPHIQ: obtained before travel
5. **Official health certificate**: endorsed by the competent veterinary authority of the exporting country
6. **Quarantine on arrival**: Taiwan requires a quarantine period at an approved quarantine facility after arrival; the duration depends on the country of origin; for qualifying countries with a passing titre test, the quarantine may be reduced to a shorter period (currently 7 days for some routes, but confirm with BAPHIQ)

Taiwan's quarantine and titre test requirements are among the stricter in Asia outside of Japan, Australia and New Zealand. Plan the process at least 9 to 12 months in advance from scratch.
