Bangkok Airways is Thailand's boutique airline, known for its own private airports at Koh Samui, Sukhothai, and Trat. The airline operates domestic routes across Thailand and a small number of short-haul international connections.

## Pets in the cabin on Bangkok Airways

Bangkok Airways accepts small pets (dogs and cats) in the passenger cabin on domestic Thai routes. The rules are:

- Total weight of pet plus carrier must not exceed 5 kg
- Carrier must fit under the seat in front (typically 40 x 30 x 20 cm or similar; confirm with the airline)
- Only one pet per passenger
- Pet must remain in the carrier throughout the flight
- Prior booking is required: contact Bangkok Airways customer service before ticket purchase to confirm availability

For international routes, the policy may differ. Some international services do not permit cabin pets. Always confirm with Bangkok Airways directly for your specific route.

## Pets as cargo or checked baggage

Bangkok Airways accepts pets as checked baggage or cargo on certain routes, subject to crate dimensions and total weight. Hard-sided IATA-compliant crates are required for hold travel. Pre-booking is essential as hold cargo space for live animals is limited.

For routes where Bangkok Airways does not offer cargo pet services, owners typically connect through Bangkok Suvarnabhumi (BKK) on a major carrier with full cargo animal services.

## Connecting through Bangkok for international pet transport

Suvarnabhumi Airport (BKK) is a major hub for Asian and international pet cargo. Thai Airways, Singapore Airlines Cargo, Cathay Pacific Cargo, Qatar Airways Cargo, and Emirates SkyCargo all operate significant cargo operations through BKK. If your pet needs to travel to Thailand from Europe, the USA, or Australia, routing via BKK on one of these carriers is the typical approach.

Bangkok Airways is useful for the final domestic leg once your pet has cleared Thai customs and the DLD import requirements.

## Thai import requirements

All pets entering Thailand require a DLD (Department of Livestock Development) import permit issued in advance. See our Thailand pet import guide for the full documentation requirements.
