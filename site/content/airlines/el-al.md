El Al Israel Airlines (IATA: LY) is Israel's flag carrier, operating from Ben Gurion International Airport in Tel Aviv (TLV) to destinations across Europe, North America, Asia and Africa. For expats, Israelis living abroad and pet owners relocating to or from Israel, El Al is frequently the most direct routing option.

## Cabin Pet Policy

El Al accepts small pets -- cats and small dogs -- in the passenger cabin on selected international routes. Requirements:

- **Combined weight** (pet plus carrier) typically **not exceeding 8 kg**
- Soft-sided carrier that fits under the seat in front of you
- The pet must remain in the carrier for the entire flight
- **Advance booking required** -- contact El Al customer service at the time of ticket purchase; a limited number of cabin pets are permitted per flight
- A pet fee applies; confirm the current rate with El Al at booking

Not all routes or aircraft types accept cabin pets. El Al operates Boeing 737-900s, 787 Dreamliners and 777-200s on various routes; cabin pet acceptance may differ by aircraft.

## Cargo Hold Pet Policy

El Al accepts pets in the cargo hold (accompanied excess baggage or unaccompanied cargo) on international routes. Requirements:

- **IATA-compliant hard-shell crate** (ventilated, secure, water container attached to door)
- Crate sized for the animal to stand, turn and lie in a natural position
- Advance booking with El Al cargo is required
- Temperature restrictions apply in summer; embargoes may affect certain routes in July and August -- confirm with El Al before booking during peak summer

## Israel's Pet Import Requirements

The Ministry of Agriculture and Rural Development (MOAG), Veterinary Services, regulates live animal imports into Israel:

1. **Import permit** from MOAG Veterinary Services -- apply at least 4 to 6 weeks before travel
2. **ISO microchip**
3. **Rabies vaccination** -- current; primary vaccination at least 30 days before travel
4. **Core vaccinations** -- dogs: distemper, parvovirus, hepatitis; cats: panleukopenia, herpesvirus, calicivirus
5. **FAVN titre test** -- required for pets arriving from countries not on Israel's recognised approved list; result must be at least 0.5 IU/ml; conducted at an approved laboratory; a waiting period may apply after a passing result -- confirm with MOAG
6. **Government-endorsed health certificate** from the country of origin, issued within 10 days of travel
7. **Veterinary inspection on arrival** at Ben Gurion Airport (TLV)

**From the UK and USA:** Both are recognised countries for which the titre test requirement has been applied; confirm the current status of the UK and USA on Israel's approved country list with MOAG before assuming no titre test is needed.

## Breed Restrictions

Israel has breed-specific legislation restricting ownership of certain dogs classified as dangerous, including pit bull-type dogs, Tosa Inus and others. Import of these breeds requires specific permits, if permitted at all. Confirm with MOAG and the Israeli Embassy in your home country before arranging transport of any restricted breed.
