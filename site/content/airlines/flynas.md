flynas (IATA: XY) is one of Saudi Arabia's largest low-cost carriers, operating from King Khalid International Airport in Riyadh (RUH) and Jeddah's King Abdulaziz International Airport (JED). The airline serves domestic Saudi routes and a network of Middle East, North Africa and South Asian destinations.

## Pet Policy Overview

flynas operates as a low-cost carrier, and its pet transport policies are limited. **Confirm directly with flynas customer service and cargo division whether live animals are accepted on your specific route before making any travel arrangements.** Low-cost carriers in the Middle East frequently do not accept live animals in the cabin or hold, or accept only specific animals on specific routes.

For international live animal transport to or from Saudi Arabia, larger carriers with established live animal cargo programmes (Saudi Arabian Airlines, Qatar Airways, Emirates, Etihad) are more commonly used by pet relocation professionals.

## Saudi Arabia's Pet Import Requirements

The Ministry of Environment, Water and Agriculture (MEWA) and the Saudi Food and Drug Authority (SFDA) regulate live animal imports:

1. **Import permit** -- apply through SFDA or the relevant MEWA department; processing takes several weeks; required before travel
2. **ISO microchip**
3. **Rabies vaccination** -- current; primary vaccination at least 30 days before travel
4. **Core vaccinations** -- dogs: distemper, parvovirus, hepatitis, leptospirosis; cats: panleukopenia, herpesvirus, calicivirus
5. **Government-endorsed health certificate** -- from a licensed vet in the country of origin, endorsed by the government veterinary authority
6. **Veterinary inspection on arrival** at the Saudi port of entry

Saudi Arabia requires the health certificate to be authenticated by the Saudi Embassy or Consulate in the country of origin in some cases -- confirm whether embassy attestation is required for your country.

## Breed Restrictions

Saudi Arabia bans certain dog breeds including pit bull-type dogs and others classified as dangerous. Additionally, some residential compounds, apartment buildings and municipalities in Saudi Arabia have their own breed or pet restrictions, independent of national rules. Confirm both national and local rules before importing.

## Dogs in Saudi Arabia: Practical Considerations

Culturally, attitudes toward dogs in Saudi Arabia are shifting, but it remains important to understand the context. Dogs are kept by Saudi families and expats, particularly in villa compounds. Public spaces in many areas do not welcome dogs. Veterinary care in major cities (Riyadh, Jeddah, Khobar, Dhahran) has improved significantly in recent years and includes specialist referral practices.
