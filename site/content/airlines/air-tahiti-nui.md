Air Tahiti Nui is the flag carrier of French Polynesia, operating long-haul routes connecting Papeete (Faaa International Airport, PPT) to Paris, Los Angeles, and Auckland. For pet owners relocating to or from French Polynesia, Air Tahiti Nui is the primary dedicated carrier for this corridor.

## In-Cabin Policy

Air Tahiti Nui does not accept pets in the passenger cabin. All pet transport is as hold cargo.

## Hold Cargo for Pets

Air Tahiti Nui accepts dogs and cats as hold cargo on applicable routes. Pets travel in IATA-compliant crates in the temperature-controlled cargo hold. Requirements include:

- **IATA-compliant crate**: Solid base, ventilated sides, secure spring-loaded door, water and food dishes attached inside
- **Health certificate**: Issued by an official government veterinarian within ten days of travel; must meet French Polynesian import requirements
- **Advance booking**: Contact Air Tahiti Nui cargo to reserve space -- not all flights have cargo hold capacity for live animals
- **Destination country documentation**: French Polynesian import requirements apply (see below)

## French Polynesia Pet Import Requirements

French Polynesia has its own biosecurity regulations managed by the Direction des Biosecurites (DBS). Requirements vary by origin country but typically include:

- Microchip (ISO standard)
- Rabies vaccination (current)
- For some origins: rabies titre test with a minimum waiting period
- Health certificate within ten days of departure
- DBS import permit or clearance

French Polynesia is part of the French overseas territory system but has independent biosecurity rules distinct from metropolitan France. Confirm the current requirements for your specific origin country with the DBS well in advance.

## Routes and Network

Air Tahiti Nui's international network connects:
- **Papeete (PPT)** -- French Polynesia main hub
- **Paris Charles de Gaulle (CDG)** -- France connection
- **Los Angeles (LAX)** -- USA west coast connection
- **Auckland (AKL)** -- New Zealand/Pacific connection

## Booking

Contact Air Tahiti Nui cargo directly to confirm live animal availability and make the booking. The long-haul nature of these routes (Paris-Papeete is approximately 17 hours) means proper crate preparation and water access are especially important.
