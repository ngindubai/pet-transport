Volaris is Mexico's largest low-cost airline, operating domestic routes across Mexico and cross-border routes to the USA and Central America. Headquartered in Mexico City and operating from multiple Mexican cities, Volaris is the primary budget carrier for domestic Mexico travel and a significant option for the Mexico-USA corridor. For pet owners based in Mexico, Volaris's in-cabin pet policy is relevant for domestic travel and for some US cross-border routes.

## In-Cabin Pet Policy

Volaris accepts small dogs and cats in the passenger cabin on applicable routes:

- Combined weight of the pet and carrier: typically up to 10 kg
- The carrier must fit under the seat in front of you
- One pet per passenger
- The pet must remain in the carrier throughout the flight
- A pet supplement fee applies

Volaris is a low-cost carrier with a per-item fee structure. The pet fee is charged per flight segment. Add your pet when booking -- do not assume you can add it at the airport.

## US-Mexico Cross-Border Routes

Volaris serves a significant number of Mexico-USA routes, including:
- Mexico City (MEX) and Guadalajara (GDL) to Los Angeles (LAX), Dallas (DFW), Chicago (ORD), Las Vegas (LAS), Houston (IAH), Miami (MIA)
- Multiple Mexican cities to US border-adjacent destinations

For US-bound cross-border flights, US CDC requirements apply for dogs. Since 2023, CDC requirements for dogs entering the USA have become more complex for dogs vaccinated outside the USA. Check current CDC requirements at cdc.gov before travelling with a dog on a Mexico-USA route.

## Accepted Species

Volaris accepts domestic dogs and cats as in-cabin pets. Other small animals may or may not be accepted -- confirm with Volaris customer service for non-dog/cat species.

## Brachycephalic Restrictions

Volaris applies restrictions to brachycephalic (flat-faced) breeds. Affected breeds include English Bulldogs, French Bulldogs, Pugs, Boston Terriers, Pekingese, and Persian cats. Confirm whether your breed is accepted before booking.

## Hold Cargo

Volaris's live animal hold cargo programme is limited compared to full-service carriers. For larger dogs requiring hold transport within Mexico or to the USA, Air Aeromexico or a specialist cargo carrier is a better option.

## Practical Notes for Mexico-Based Pet Owners

Volaris's domestic network covers the major Mexican cities and regional airports, making it a convenient option for moving a small pet around Mexico without cargo-level logistics. The carrier fee is generally reasonable by international standards.

For international relocations (Mexico to Europe, Mexico to Australia, etc.) requiring cargo hold transport, Volaris is not the appropriate carrier. Use Aeromexico or a connecting international carrier.
