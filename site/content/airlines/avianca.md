Avianca is Colombia's national carrier and one of Latin America's largest airlines, operating from its main hub at El Dorado International Airport (BOG) in Bogota. As a Star Alliance member, Avianca connects Colombia and neighbouring countries to Europe, North America, and the Caribbean.

## Pets in the Cabin

Avianca accepts small dogs and cats in the cabin on most routes. The animal and carrier combined must not exceed 10kg and the carrier must fit under the seat in front. Soft-sided carriers are required for cabin travel. Only one pet per passenger is the standard policy.

## Pets as Cargo

Larger pets travel in the cargo hold in an IATA-compliant hard plastic crate. The crate must allow the animal to stand at full height, turn completely, and lie down. Ventilation on at least three sides is required. Water dispensers must be attached inside the crate and a dry food portion secured to the door for long journeys.

## Brachycephalic and Restricted Breeds

Avianca restricts brachycephalic breeds in the cargo hold. Contact Avianca directly to confirm whether your breed is accepted. If your flat-faced dog or cat qualifies for cabin (under 10kg), cabin travel is both permitted and safer.

## Routes and Booking

Avianca operates domestically within Colombia and internationally to the USA, Europe (Madrid primarily), and neighbouring South American countries. Pet policies can vary by route and by the aircraft type used. Always confirm at booking rather than assuming the same rules apply across all routes.

## Colombia Import Rules for Returning Pets

If you are bringing a pet back to Colombia, note that Colombia requires an official health certificate endorsed by the origin country's veterinary authority, ISO microchip, and current vaccination records. Colombia does not currently require a titre test for most origin countries, but confirm current requirements with Colombia's Instituto Colombiano Agropecuario (ICA) before travel.

## Practical Advice

For intercontinental moves involving Colombia, such as Colombia to the UK, pairing Avianca from BOG to Madrid and then a European carrier for the second leg can be an effective routing for cargo pets. An IPATA agent with Latin America experience can structure the itinerary to minimise connection times and handling.
