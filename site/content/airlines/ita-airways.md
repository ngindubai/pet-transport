ITA Airways is Italy's national carrier, operating out of Rome Fiumicino (FCO) and Milan Linate (LIN). Formed in 2021 as the successor to Alitalia, ITA operates a mix of European short-haul routes and intercontinental services to North America, Africa, and the Middle East. It joined SkyTeam in 2024.

## Pets in the Cabin

ITA Airways accepts small pets (dogs, cats, and small birds) in the cabin. The combined weight of the animal and carrier must not exceed 10kg. The soft-sided carrier must fit under the seat in front of you. One pet per passenger is the standard limit, and a limited number of pets are accepted per flight, so early booking is important.

## Pets as Cargo

Larger dogs and cats that exceed the cabin weight limit travel as checked baggage or manifest cargo in an IATA-compliant hard crate. The crate must allow the animal to stand, turn, and lie down. Water dispensers must be attached inside the crate and a dry food portion fixed to the door for journeys over 4 hours.

## Brachycephalic Breeds

ITA Airways restricts brachycephalic (flat-faced) breeds in cargo. Breeds such as Bulldog, Pug, and Persian cat are typically refused in the hold. Confirm the current restricted breed list with ITA Airways before booking if you have a flat-faced breed.

## Booking Process

Pets must be added to the booking by contacting ITA Airways directly -- this cannot be done through the standard online booking flow. Call ITA Airways reservations or visit a ticketing desk. Bring your pet carrier to check-in early and ensure the carrier passes the under-seat dimension check.

## Health and Documentation

For international journeys, your pet will need all destination-country required documentation: microchip, vaccination records, health certificate, and any required permits. ITA Airways does not arrange documentation -- this is the owner's responsibility.

## Practical Advice

For Rome-based moves, FCO is well-equipped for live animal cargo. ITA's cargo division handles intercontinental pet shipments on long-haul routes. For North American moves (Rome to New York JFK, for example), book well in advance and confirm the cargo pet policy for the specific aircraft on your route.
