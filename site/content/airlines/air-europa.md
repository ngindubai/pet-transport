Air Europa is Spain's third-largest airline, operating a hub at Madrid Barajas International Airport (MAD) and serving European, Latin American, Caribbean, and African destinations. For pet owners, Air Europa is particularly relevant for Spain-based expats and for UK/European residents travelling to Latin American destinations.

## In-Cabin Pet Travel on Air Europa

Air Europa accepts small dogs and cats in the passenger cabin on applicable routes, subject to the following conditions:

- Combined weight of the pet and carrier must not exceed 8 kg
- The carrier must fit under the seat in front of you (soft-sided carriers recommended)
- One pet per passenger; maximum two pets per cabin per flight
- The pet must remain in the carrier throughout the flight
- A pet supplement fee applies per flight

Cabin pet spaces are limited. Add your pet to your booking when purchasing the ticket -- it is not guaranteed if added later.

## Hold Cargo on Air Europa

Air Europa accepts larger dogs and cats in the hold on applicable routes:

- Maximum combined weight (pet plus IATA-compliant crate): 32 kg
- IATA-compliant hard-sided crate required
- Live animal acceptance must be confirmed on your specific route and date
- Some long-haul routes (particularly to Latin America from Madrid) accept hold pets

For hold cargo bookings, contact Air Europa's cargo team directly. Have your pet's measurements, species, breed, and weight ready when making the booking enquiry.

## Routes Served -- Latin America Focus

Air Europa's most significant network for international pet owners covers Latin American destinations from Madrid:
- Madrid to Buenos Aires (EZE)
- Madrid to Bogota (BOG)
- Madrid to Lima (LIM)
- Madrid to Havana (HAV)
- Madrid to Santo Domingo (SDQ)
- Madrid to São Paulo (GRU)

For UK, Irish, or Northern European pet owners relocating to Latin America, a connection via Madrid with Air Europa is a commonly used routing.

## Brachycephalic Breed Restrictions

Air Europa restricts brachycephalic breeds from hold cargo travel:
- English Bulldogs, French Bulldogs, American Bulldogs
- Pugs, Boston Terriers, Pekingese
- Persian cats, Himalayan cats, Exotic Shorthair cats

Some restricted breeds may be accepted in the cabin (within the 8 kg combined limit). Confirm with Air Europa for your specific breed.

## Documentation Requirements

For EU destinations, an EU pet passport or EU-format health certificate is required. For Latin American destinations (Brazil, Argentina, Colombia, Peru), each country has specific import requirements -- prepare the destination-country documentation separately. Use a specialist pet transport company familiar with Latin American import rules for these routes.

## Booking Process

- In-cabin: Add during ticket purchase online or via Air Europa customer service
- Hold cargo: Contact Air Europa cargo team directly
- Always confirm live animal acceptance on your specific flight -- not all aircraft types or routes accept pets
