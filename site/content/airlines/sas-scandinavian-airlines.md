## SAS (Scandinavian Airlines) Pet Transport Policy

SAS (Scandinavian Airlines) operates from Copenhagen, Stockholm Arlanda, and Oslo Gardermoen, connecting Denmark, Sweden, and Norway to Europe, North America, and Asia. It is the main carrier for international pet moves from the Scandinavian region.

SAS went through bankruptcy restructuring in 2022-2024 and joined SkyTeam in 2024. The airline continues to operate its core network of European and intercontinental routes.

Small pets meeting the weight limit (carrier and pet combined under 8kg) can travel in the SAS cabin. Brachycephalic breeds are not accepted in cabin. Larger pets and those on long-haul routes travel as cargo.

**Scandinavia and EU rules:** Denmark and Sweden are EU members and issue EU pet passports for resident pets. Norway is in the EEA but not the EU - Norwegian pets are treated as third-country animals when entering some EU destinations. Confirm the exact documentation requirements with SAS for your specific route, as the Norway-EU distinction can occasionally affect health certificate requirements.

**USA routes:** SAS operates Copenhagen-Newark, Copenhagen-Los Angeles, Stockholm-Washington, and other US routes. For dogs entering the USA, updated CDC requirements apply - the microchip must be implanted on or before the date of the first rabies vaccination. A USDA Animal and Plant Health Inspection Service (APHIS)-endorsed health certificate is required.

**Booking:** Add in-cabin pets when booking your ticket via the SAS website. For cargo, contact SAS Cargo directly.
