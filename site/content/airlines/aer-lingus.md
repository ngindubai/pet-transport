## Aer Lingus Pet Transport Policy

Aer Lingus is Ireland's national carrier and a Oneworld member, operating from Dublin (DUB) with secondary hubs at Cork and Shannon. It connects Ireland to the UK, Europe, and the USA, making it the main option for pets leaving or arriving in Ireland on international routes.

Pets travel in the cargo hold on Aer Lingus - there is no cabin pet option. Dogs and cats are accepted as cargo on qualifying routes, though live animal acceptance should always be confirmed when booking.

**Ireland to UK:** Post-Brexit documentation has added a step to this route. Irish pets have EU pet passports (Ireland being an EU member), but pets entering the UK must now have an Animal Health Certificate (AHC) issued by an Official Veterinarian in Ireland and endorsed by the Department of Agriculture, Food and the Marine (DAFM). Dogs also need a tapeworm treatment (praziquantel) administered by a vet 1-5 days before entering the UK. If you're moving a dog from Dublin to any UK destination, that treatment timing is non-negotiable.

**Transatlantic routes:** Aer Lingus operates to several US cities via Dublin. Pet cargo availability on these routes should be confirmed directly with Aer Lingus, as capacity for live animals varies.

**Booking:** Contact Aer Lingus cargo directly. For international moves with complex documentation needs, an IPATA-accredited agent is worth involving early.
