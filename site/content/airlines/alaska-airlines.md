Alaska Airlines is one of the USA's major carriers, headquartered in Seattle and operating a large network of domestic US routes as well as services to Canada, Mexico, and some international destinations. Alaska operates primarily in the western USA, Pacific Northwest, and Alaska itself.

## Pets in the Cabin

Alaska Airlines accepts small dogs, cats, and household birds in the cabin on domestic routes. The combined weight of the pet and carrier must not exceed 11kg (25 lbs). The soft-sided carrier must fit under the seat in front of you. The carrier counts as your personal item allowance.

One pet per passenger, maximum six pets per flight.

## No Checked Pet Service

Alaska Airlines does not offer checked pet (cargo hold) service. If your pet is too large to travel in the cabin, you will need to use a different carrier or arrange separate live animal cargo shipping through a freight forwarder.

## International Routes

Alaska Airlines operates some international routes (Canada, Mexico). Pet cabin policies may differ on international flights compared to domestic. Confirm pet acceptance for your specific route at time of booking.

## Seasonal Embargoes

Alaska Airlines suspends cabin pet service to specific warm-weather destinations when extreme temperatures are forecast. Hawaii routes are subject to seasonal pet embargoes. If you are flying to or from Hawaii with a pet, check the current embargo status with Alaska Airlines well before your travel date.

## Breed Restrictions

Alaska Airlines restricts brachycephalic breeds from travelling as pets under the seat. Flat-faced dogs (Bulldogs, Pugs, Shih Tzus, Boxers) and flat-faced cats (Persians) are not accepted. The restriction applies to cabin pets -- since Alaska does not offer cargo pet service, these breeds cannot travel on Alaska at all.

## Connecting with International Carriers

For international pet moves involving a domestic Alaska leg (Seattle to a West Coast city, then an international carrier), note that the second carrier may have different pet rules. Coordinate the entire itinerary before booking. An IPATA agent can help structure multi-carrier itineraries for international moves.

## REAL ID and Boarding

When travelling with a cabin pet, arrive at the airport with extra time. The pet check-in process (fee payment, carrier dimensions check) takes additional time at the ticket counter.
