Frontier Airlines is a US ultra-low-cost carrier based in Denver, Colorado (DEN). It operates a broad domestic US network with limited service to Mexico and some Caribbean destinations.

## In-cabin pets only

Frontier accepts small pets in the cabin, including dogs, cats, rabbits, guinea pigs, hamsters, and small household birds. The combined weight of the pet and carrier must not exceed 20 lbs (approximately 9 kg), and the carrier must fit under the seat in front of you.

The pet fee is charged per carrier per one-way flight. Space for pets is limited: add your pet to the booking at time of purchase.

## No hold pet service

Like most US ultra-low-cost carriers, Frontier does not accept pets in the hold or as cargo. Large dogs cannot travel on Frontier. For international pet transport or large dog travel, full-service carriers are required.

## International pet travel and Frontier

If you are relocating internationally and plan to fly Frontier for part of your journey, your pet will need to fly separately on a carrier that accepts hold or cargo pets. This is a common arrangement: the owner flies economy on a low-cost carrier while the pet travels as cargo on a full-service carrier or via a specialist freight forwarder.

## Frontier Airlines contact for pet bookings

Add your pet at the time of booking through Frontier's website at flyfrontier.com. Select the pet add-on option during the booking flow or contact Frontier's customer service team. Always confirm space availability, as Frontier limits pets per flight.
