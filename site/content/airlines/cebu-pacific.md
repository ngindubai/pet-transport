Cebu Pacific is the Philippines' largest carrier by passenger numbers, operating an extensive domestic network and regional international routes across Asia. For pet owners, Cebu Pacific's pet policy is restricted: pets travel in the hold on domestic routes only, and are not accepted on international flights.

## Domestic Pet Policy (Philippines Internal Routes)

On Cebu Pacific domestic flights within the Philippines:

- Pets accepted as excess baggage in the aircraft hold
- No in-cabin pets
- IATA-compliant rigid crate required
- Weight limits apply (check current limits at cebupacificair.com)
- Advance booking required

Pets are not accepted in the cabin on any Cebu Pacific flights.

## International Routes: No Pet Transport

Cebu Pacific does not accept pets on international flights. This includes routes to Hong Kong, South Korea, Japan, Australia, Singapore, and other international destinations.

For international pet moves involving the Philippines:

- **Philippine Airlines (PAL)**: Accepts pets as excess baggage in the hold on some international routes. Contact PAL Cargo.
- **Specialist air cargo**: For complex routes or animals that exceed normal baggage limits, specialist live animal freight services operate from Manila Ninoy Aquino and Cebu Mactan airports.

## Philippines Pet Import Requirements

Bringing a pet INTO the Philippines requires:

1. **Import permit** from the Bureau of Animal Industry (BAI) -- apply at bai.da.gov.ph at least 4 to 6 weeks before travel
2. **Rabies vaccination** -- current
3. **Health certificate** from origin country, endorsed by national authority
4. **Rabies titre test** -- required from some countries (check current BAI requirements)
5. **7-day quarantine** at a BAI facility on arrival

The Philippines maintains a strict biosecurity programme for imported pets. Documentation must be complete -- incomplete paperwork can result in extended detention at BAI facilities.

## Philippines Pet Export Requirements

For pets leaving the Philippines:

- Health certificate from a BAI-accredited vet
- Export clearance from BAI
- Microchip (required by most destination countries)
- Destination country-specific requirements

The BAI office at NAIA (Manila) manages export clearance. Allow 2 to 3 weeks for the full process.

## Practical Notes for Manila-Based Expats

Manila has an active international expat community and several veterinary practices experienced in international export certification. BAI-accredited export vets are available in Metro Manila and in Cebu.

Cebu Pacific is excellent for moving around the Philippine archipelago -- for most domestic pet moves within the Philippines, they are a practical and affordable option. For anything involving international travel, you will need PAL or a cargo carrier.
