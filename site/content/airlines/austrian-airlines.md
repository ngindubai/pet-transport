Austrian Airlines is Austria's national carrier and a member of the Lufthansa Group and Star Alliance, operating from Vienna International Airport (VIE). Vienna is a well-connected European hub and Austrian Airlines serves destinations across Europe, North America, the Middle East, Asia, and Africa. For pet owners relocating to Austria or using VIE as a European transit point, Austrian is a reliable option with a clear pet policy.

**Cabin pets**

Austrian Airlines accepts small pets in the cabin on qualifying routes:
- Maximum combined weight (pet plus carrier): 8kg
- Carrier must be soft-sided, ventilated, and leak-proof
- Carrier must fit under the seat in front
- One pet per passenger
- A fee applies per segment (check current rates on the Austrian website)

Cabin pet availability may vary by route - confirm when booking.

**Cargo pets**

Larger dogs and cats travel as checked cargo in IATA-approved hard-sided crates. The crate must be sized so the animal can stand at full height, turn completely, sit, and lie down without touching the walls. Food and water dishes must be accessible from outside without opening the crate.

**Breed restrictions**

Austrian Airlines restricts brachycephalic breeds from cargo. Common restricted breeds include English Bulldogs, French Bulldogs, Pugs, Boston Terriers, Boxers, and flat-faced cat breeds. Confirm your breed's status with Austrian Airlines before booking.

**Vienna as a hub**

Vienna Schwechat is one of Central Europe's main hubs with good live animal handling infrastructure. Non-EU arrivals into Austria must pass through an EU-approved border inspection post (BIP) - VIE has BIP facilities. If you're transiting VIE to reach another EU country, the EU entry formalities are completed at VIE.

**Austria entry requirements**

Austria is an EU member state. EU pets with valid EU pet passports travel freely. Non-EU arrivals (including UK pets post-Brexit) need an AHC in EU format, endorsed by the origin country government authority. Dogs need tapeworm treatment (praziquantel) 1-5 days before arrival in Austria. Once in Austria, dogs must be registered with the local municipality within a short period.

**Booking**

Add cabin pets online or via Austrian's customer service when booking. For cargo pets, contact Austrian Airlines' cargo team. Confirm route-specific live animal acceptance before purchasing tickets.
