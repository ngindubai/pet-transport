Scoot is Singapore's low-cost carrier, operating a medium to long-haul network across Asia, Australia, and Europe from Singapore Changi Airport (SIN). As part of the Singapore Airlines Group, Scoot offers budget-focused travel without the full-service features of its parent airline -- including live animal transport.

## Scoot's Pet Policy

Scoot does not accept pets in the cabin or as cargo on its scheduled services. This is consistent with Scoot's low-cost model, which prioritises simple, standardised operations.

Certified guide dogs and registered assistance animals are the exception and travel in the cabin with their owners at no extra charge. Appropriate certification documentation is required.

## What This Means for Southeast Asia Pet Moves

If you are relocating within Southeast Asia or between Singapore and Australia, Europe, or Japan, Scoot cannot be used for your pet. The main alternatives for pet cargo from Singapore include:

- **Singapore Airlines (SQ)**: extensive live animal cargo programme; one of the best options for Asia-Pacific pet transport
- **Cathay Pacific (CX)**: accepts pet cargo on selected routes from Singapore via Hong Kong
- **Thai Airways (TG)**: accepts pet cargo on some routes
- **Garuda Indonesia (GA)**: accepts pets on selected routes
- **Air New Zealand (NZ)**: for Singapore-Australia routes, accepts cargo pets on approved routings

## Singapore Pet Export Rules

Pets leaving Singapore require an export licence from the National Parks Board (NParks) under the Agri-Food and Veterinary Authority (AVA, now SFA). A health certificate from an AVA-accredited vet is required, along with all destination-country documentation. Start the export licence process 4 to 6 weeks before travel.

## Practical Advice

For budget-conscious moves from Singapore, you can fly Scoot yourself while arranging your pet's transport separately through Singapore Airlines Cargo or another carrier. An IPATA agent can coordinate the cargo side while you travel independently, reducing your overall costs compared to full-service travel.
