Amaszonas, operating under IATA code Z8, is a Bolivian airline headquartered in La Paz. It operates domestic Bolivian routes as well as regional routes connecting Bolivia to Argentina (Buenos Aires EZE, Mendoza MDZ, Cordoba COR), Chile (Santiago SCL), Peru (Lima LIM), Paraguay (Asuncion ASU), Uruguay (Montevideo MVD) and Brazil (Sao Paulo GRU). For pet owners in Bolivia and the surrounding region, Amaszonas is often the primary carrier option for regional travel.

## Cabin Pet Policy

Amaszonas accepts small pets in the passenger cabin on selected routes:

- **Eligible pets**: cats and small dogs
- **Weight limit**: combined weight of pet plus carrier up to approximately 6 to 8 kg; confirm current weight limit with Amaszonas as this varies by route and aircraft
- **Carrier type**: soft-sided carrier that fits under the seat in front
- **Advance booking required**: notify Amaszonas at time of ticket purchase; limited cabin pet allocation per flight
- **Documentation**: health certificate from a licensed veterinarian; for regional routes, a government-endorsed certificate may be required at destination; confirm with Amaszonas and the destination country's veterinary authority

## Cargo Hold Policy

For larger pets:

- **IATA-compliant hard-shell crate** required
- **Advance booking** through Amaszonas cargo is required; live animals are not accepted as walk-up excess baggage
- Confirm weight and crate dimension limits with Amaszonas for the specific route and aircraft type

## The La Paz Altitude Consideration

La Paz (LPB) is served by El Alto International Airport, one of the highest commercial airports in the world at approximately 4,061 metres (13,323 feet) above sea level. This altitude creates specific considerations for pets:

- **Oxygen levels at high altitude are significantly lower** than at sea level; the partial pressure of oxygen at 4,000 metres is roughly 60% of sea level
- Pets -- especially brachycephalic breeds -- may experience acute altitude sickness at La Paz elevations; signs include rapid breathing, lethargy and distress
- **Brachycephalic breeds** (Pug, French Bulldog, English Bulldog, Persian and similar) are at heightened risk at La Paz altitude both in the cargo hold and in the cabin; consult a vet before travelling through La Paz with any flat-faced breed
- Even healthy, non-brachycephalic dogs and cats may need time to acclimatise on arrival at La Paz; allow rest time before onward travel

## Bolivia and Regional Pet Import Requirements

Each country on Amaszonas's network has its own pet import requirements. For Bolivia:

1. **Microchip or tattoo identification**
2. **Rabies vaccination**: current; Bolivia's SENASAG (National Agricultural Health and Food Safety Service) governs imports
3. **Government-endorsed health certificate** from the exporting country
4. **Core vaccinations** current and recorded
5. **SENASAG import permit**: required for some origins; confirm with SENASAG or the Bolivian embassy in your country

For destinations including Argentina, Chile and Peru, each country's veterinary authority sets its own requirements. Argentina requires SENASA certificates; Chile requires SAG endorsement; Peru uses SENASA Peru protocols. Confirm requirements for your specific route.

- La Paz (LPB) to Buenos Aires (EZE): popular route for pet transport between Bolivia and Argentina
- La Paz (LPB) to Santiago (SCL)
- La Paz (LPB) to Lima (LIM)
- Bolivian domestic routes: Santa Cruz (VVI), Cochabamba (CBB), Sucre (SRE), Trinidad (TDD)
