## Aeroflot's Restricted International Status

Aeroflot was once one of the world's largest airlines by passenger numbers, with an extensive international network connecting Russia to Europe, North America, the Middle East, Asia, and beyond.

Since February 2022, Aeroflot's situation has changed fundamentally:
- Suspended from SkyTeam alliance
- Barred from EU, UK, US, Canadian, and many other countries' airspace
- Lost access to hundreds of leased Western aircraft (Airbus and Boeing)
- Most major international routes suspended

As of May 2026, Aeroflot continues to operate from Moscow Sheremetyevo (SVO) to a limited set of destinations, primarily:
- Turkey, UAE, China, India, Iran, Egypt, several former Soviet states (CIS countries)
- Some routes in Southeast Asia and the Indian subcontinent

## Pre-2022 Pet Policy

For historical reference: Aeroflot's pre-2022 pet policy allowed:
- **Cabin travel:** Pets up to 8 kg (pet plus carrier combined) in the cabin on most routes
- **Hold travel:** Pets over 8 kg as checked baggage or cargo
- **Service animals:** Full cabin access

Documentation requirements varied by destination country and generally followed standard international requirements.

## Using Aeroflot in 2026

If you are routing through Moscow on a current Aeroflot route and need to transport a pet:
1. First confirm the specific route is currently operating
2. Confirm Aeroflot's current pet policy for that specific route at aeroflot.ru
3. Confirm the destination country's import requirements

Given the volatility of Aeroflot's current operations, it is strongly recommended to use alternative carriers for any important pet transport, as schedule disruptions and policy changes have been frequent.
