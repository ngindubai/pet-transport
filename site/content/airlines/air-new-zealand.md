## Air New Zealand Pet Transport Policy

Air New Zealand operates from its Auckland hub connecting New Zealand to Australia, Asia, the Pacific Islands, the UK, and North America. The airline accepts dogs and cats as manifest cargo on most international routes, though cabin travel is not available.

New Zealand is a rabies-free country with strict biosecurity rules, which affects pet transport in both directions. Pets arriving in New Zealand face managed isolation requirements administered by MPI (Ministry for Primary Industries). Pets leaving New Zealand for countries that require titre tests will need advance veterinary preparation - the UK, for instance, requires a rabies antibody titre test at least 30 days before departure and a waiting period of at least 3 months after the test before entry.

Air New Zealand restricts a range of brachycephalic (snub-nosed) breeds due to welfare concerns. This includes English Bulldogs, French Bulldogs, Pugs, Boston Terriers, and Shih Tzus among others. Always confirm current breed restrictions with Air New Zealand Cargo before booking.

**Booking:** Contact Air New Zealand Cargo directly or work with an IPATA-accredited pet transport agent experienced with New Zealand biosecurity requirements.
