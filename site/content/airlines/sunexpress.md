SunExpress is a joint venture airline between Turkish Airlines and Lufthansa, positioned as a leisure-focused low-cost carrier connecting Turkey with European cities. It operates primarily from Antalya (AYT) -- Turkey's main tourist and expat hub -- as well as from Istanbul Sabiha Gokcen (SAW), Izmir (ADB), and other Turkish airports to European destinations. For expats and holidaymakers in Turkey with small pets, SunExpress is a relevant carrier.

## In-Cabin Pet Policy

SunExpress accepts small dogs and cats in the passenger cabin on applicable routes:

- Combined weight of the pet and carrier: maximum 8 kg
- The carrier must fit under the seat in front of you (typical dimensions: approximately 40 x 30 x 20 cm -- confirm on the SunExpress website)
- One pet per passenger
- The pet must remain in the carrier throughout the flight
- A pet supplement fee applies per flight segment

Add your pet to your booking through the SunExpress website. Cabin pet spaces are limited -- book early to secure a space on your preferred flight.

## Accepted Species

SunExpress accepts domestic dogs and cats as in-cabin pets. Other small animals may not be accepted -- confirm with SunExpress customer service for non-standard species.

## Brachycephalic Breed Restrictions

SunExpress restricts brachycephalic (flat-faced) breeds from in-cabin travel due to respiratory risk:
- English Bulldogs, French Bulldogs, American Bulldogs
- Pugs, Boston Terriers, Pekingese
- Persian cats, Himalayan cats, Exotic Shorthair cats

If your pet is a brachycephalic breed, confirm current acceptance policy directly with SunExpress before booking.

## Hold Cargo

SunExpress has a limited hold cargo programme for larger animals. Contact SunExpress directly to enquire about hold cargo availability on your specific route. As a leisure-focused low-cost carrier, SunExpress's hold cargo infrastructure is less developed than full-service carriers. For large dog cargo to or from Turkey, Turkish Airlines (a parent company) is a better-resourced option.

## Route Network

SunExpress serves numerous European destinations from Turkey:

**From Antalya (AYT)**:
- Frankfurt (FRA), Dusseldorf (DUS), Munich (MUC), Berlin (BER), Hamburg (HAM), Cologne/Bonn (CGN)
- Vienna (VIE), Zurich (ZRH), Amsterdam (AMS), Rotterdam (RTM)
- London Stansted (STN), Birmingham (BHX), Manchester (MAN)
- Various Scandinavian, French, Italian, and Eastern European destinations

SunExpress also operates domestic Turkish routes connecting Antalya and other southern coastal cities to Istanbul.

## Documentation Requirements

For travel between Turkey and EU countries, the following applies:
- For pets travelling from Turkey to the EU: Turkey is a third country from the EU's perspective. An EU-format health certificate endorsed by the Turkish Ministry of Agriculture (TARIMBAKANLIGI) veterinary authority is required. The EU has specific requirements for third-country imports including microchip and valid rabies vaccination.
- For pets travelling from EU countries to Turkey: Turkish Ministry of Agriculture import requirements apply.

## Practical Notes for Antalya and Turkish Coast Expats

Antalya and the Turkish Aegean and Mediterranean coasts have a large expat community, many of whom have dogs and cats. SunExpress is often the most direct connection back to Germany, Austria, and the UK. For small pets under 8 kg combined, SunExpress's in-cabin option is a practical choice for visits home.

Be aware that bringing a Turkish-acquired pet to the EU requires an EU-format health certificate and current rabies vaccination meeting EU standards. The process takes around two to four weeks to arrange correctly.
