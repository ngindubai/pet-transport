Helvetic Airways, operating under IATA code 2L, is a Swiss regional airline based at Zurich Airport (ZRH). Helvetic primarily operates as a wet-lease partner for other carriers -- most notably Swiss International Air Lines (SWISS) and SunExpress -- flying routes on their behalf under the operating partner's brand and code. Helvetic operates a fleet of Embraer E190-E2 jets.

## When You Are Booking "SWISS" but Flying Helvetic

Many passengers booking through SWISS or other carriers may find that their actual operating carrier is Helvetic Airways. For pet owners, the important question is: which carrier's pet policy applies?

**As a general rule, the operating carrier's policy applies for on-board conditions, but the marketing carrier's pet acceptance rules may govern what is booked.** When booking a pet on a SWISS-branded flight operated by Helvetic, confirm pet acceptance rules with SWISS directly, as Helvetic operates these flights on their behalf and SWISS handles the booking and pet fee.

## Cabin Pet Policy (Helvetic-Operated Flights)

On Helvetic-operated routes, cabin pet acceptance typically follows the policy of the marketing carrier (SWISS, SunExpress, or other). In general terms for SWISS-branded routes:

- **Small cats and dogs** in a soft-sided carrier under the seat in front; combined weight (pet plus carrier) typically up to 8 kg
- **EU or Swiss pet passport** required for intra-European flights; current rabies vaccination and microchip
- **Advance booking required**: contact the marketing carrier (SWISS) to add a pet to the booking; do not assume the pet can be brought without advance notification

## Cargo Hold for Larger Pets

Helvetic's Embraer E190-E2 aircraft have limited cargo hold space, which restricts hold pet transport:

- Hold pet transport on Helvetic-operated flights should be confirmed with the marketing carrier (SWISS)
- For larger pets on routes connecting to long-haul SWISS flights via Zurich, the hold transport on the long-haul segment is handled under SWISS/Edelweiss Air cargo processes; the Helvetic short-haul feeder portion may require a separate booking and separate check-in process

## Swiss Pet Documentation

Switzerland aligns closely with EU pet travel rules through bilateral agreement. For pets on Helvetic/SWISS routes:

**Within Switzerland and EU/EEA:**
- EU pet passport is accepted; Switzerland recognises EU pet passports for intra-EEA travel
- Microchip and current rabies vaccination required

**Arriving from outside EU/EEA (including from the UK post-Brexit):**
- FSVO (Swiss Federal Food Safety and Veterinary Office) requirements apply; ISO microchip, rabies vaccination, titre test (for most non-EEA origins), 3-month wait, and government-endorsed health certificate in FSVO-approved format

## Practical Advice

Because Helvetic operates as a white-label carrier for other airlines, direct communication with Helvetic about pet policies is not always straightforward. For booking purposes:
1. Book through the marketing carrier (SWISS, SunExpress, etc.)
2. Specify the pet requirement at time of booking
3. Ask whether the operating carrier for your specific flight is Helvetic, and confirm that the pet fee and rules apply consistently
