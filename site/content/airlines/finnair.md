## Finnair Pet Transport Policy

Finnair is Finland's flag carrier and a Oneworld member, operating from Helsinki-Vantaa Airport (HEL). It connects Scandinavia and the Nordic region to Europe, North America, and Asia via Arctic routing.

Finnair's position as a key Europe-Asia connector makes it relevant for pets relocating between these regions. The airline operates to Tokyo, Osaka, Seoul, Singapore, Shanghai, Beijing, Bangkok, and other Asian destinations - often the most direct European routing to East Asia. That said, live animal cargo acceptance on long-haul routes should always be confirmed well in advance, as space is limited.

Small pets meeting the weight and size requirements can travel in the Finnair cabin within the EU and on shorter international routes. Brachycephalic breeds are not accepted in cabin.

**EU travel:** Finnish pets have EU pet passports. For pets from non-EU countries entering Finland, an Animal Health Certificate in the format required by EU Regulation 576/2013 is needed, plus a border inspection post clearance for third-country arrivals.

**Japan routes:** Finnair's Helsinki-Tokyo connection is one of the shortest transoceanic routes to Japan. Bear in mind that Japan has some of the most complex quarantine requirements in the world - MAFF documentation must be prepared months in advance. An IPATA agent with Japan experience is strongly recommended.

**Booking:** Add in-cabin pets when booking your ticket online. For cargo, contact Finnair Cargo directly.
