## Ryanair Does Not Carry Pets

Ryanair -- Europe's largest airline by passenger numbers -- does not accept pets on any of its routes. This is absolute policy, applying to:
- Cabin travel
- Hold travel
- Any baggage or cargo service

The only exception is **registered guide dogs and assistance dogs** meeting Ryanair's assistance animal requirements.

## The Low-Cost Model and Live Animals

Ryanair's business model is built on maximum efficiency, rapid turnaround times, and operational simplicity. Live animal transport introduces:
- Documentation verification at check-in
- Specific hold temperature monitoring requirements
- Additional ground handling procedures
- Animal welfare liability during transit

This is incompatible with Ryanair's operational framework. Ryanair, easyJet, Wizz Air, and Jet2 have all reached the same conclusion.

## Assistance Dogs on Ryanair

Registered guide dogs and assistance dogs can travel on Ryanair flights in cabin with their handlers. Requirements:
- Advance notification before booking (at least 48 hours minimum, ideally further in advance)
- Documentation from a recognised assistance dog organisation
- Dog must remain on the floor at the handler's feet

Book assistance dog travel through ryanair.com or by calling Ryanair's special assistance line.

## Alternatives for Ryanair Routes

Ryanair serves an enormous network of European and some North African routes. Most of these destinations are also served by flag carriers or alternative LCCs with pet policies.

**For cabin pets (small dogs and cats, typically under 6 to 8 kg combined):**
- Iberia / Vueling (Spain routes)
- TAP Air Portugal (Portugal routes)
- Aer Lingus (Ireland routes)
- LOT Polish Airlines (Poland routes)
- Aegean Airlines (Greece routes)
- Air France (France routes)
- KLM (Netherlands routes)
- Lufthansa (Germany routes)
- British Airways (many European routes from UK)

**For larger dogs needing hold/cargo:**
Contact British Airways World Cargo, Lufthansa Cargo, or a specialist IPATA-member pet transport agent who can identify the best cargo routing.

## Can I Ship My Pet Separately as Cargo?

Yes -- but not through Ryanair. You need a cargo carrier with a live animal freight service. For European destinations, most major flag carriers (Lufthansa Cargo, Air France Cargo, British Airways World Cargo, KLM Cargo) can transport pets as air cargo independently of any passenger booking. A specialist pet transport agent can manage this process.

## Summary

Ryanair is not usable for pet travel. If your preferred route is Ryanair-only, you will need to travel on a different airline or arrange separate cargo transport for your pet.

*Policy information accurate as of May 2026. Always verify with Ryanair and alternative carriers before travel.*
