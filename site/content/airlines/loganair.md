Loganair (IATA: LM) is the UK's largest regional airline by number of routes, operating from its main base at Glasgow Airport (GLA). The airline provides lifeline services to remote Scottish island communities -- Orkney, Shetland, the Western Isles, Barra, Tiree, Islay -- that would otherwise have no air connection. It also operates routes between larger Scottish airports, northern English airports and a small number of European destinations.

## Pet Policy Overview

Loganair's policies on pets (other than assistance dogs) are limited. This is primarily a function of the aircraft types in the fleet: many Loganair routes are operated on small turboprops (SAAB 340, Twin Otter, BN2 Islander) with very limited cargo hold space and no standard pet cargo infrastructure.

**Assistance dogs:** Accepted on all Loganair routes in the cabin at no charge; advance notice required.

**Pet dogs and cats:** Loganair does not operate a general cabin pet programme. For cargo hold transport of pets, contact Loganair's cargo division directly and confirm whether your specific route can accommodate a live animal. Island routes, in particular, are very constrained in cargo capacity.

For pet owners needing to transport a cat or dog to or from the Scottish islands, **ferry services (Caledonian MacBrayne for the Western Isles; NorthLink for Orkney and Shetland) are often more practical and humane** than attempting air transport on small turboprop aircraft.

## Scottish Island Pet Considerations

Moving to or from Orkney, Shetland or the Outer Hebrides with a pet is a common enough scenario. The NorthLink ferry from Aberdeen to Kirkwall (Orkney) and Lerwick (Shetland) accepts pets in kennels or cabins. CalMac operates Western Isles services from Ullapool, Uig and Oban with pet-friendly cabin options.

For the smallest Loganair routes (Barra beach landing strip, Tiree, Benbecula), sea freight for pets is typically the only practical option for larger animals.

## UK Domestic Pet Travel

For UK domestic travel (Scotland to England, Scotland to Northern Ireland, etc.), no import or export documentation is required for companion animals. The animal should have:
- ISO microchip (mandatory for dogs in Scotland, England and Wales under the Microchipping of Dogs Regulations)
- Current vaccinations (not legally required for domestic travel but good practice)
- A collar with the owner's contact information (legal requirement for dogs in public places)

## Connecting to International Flights

If you are using Loganair to connect from a Scottish or northern English regional airport to an international hub (Glasgow to London, Belfast to Dublin, etc.), plan the connection with sufficient time for cargo animals. A tight connection with live animal cargo is inadvisable.
