Eurowings is a German low-cost carrier, part of the Lufthansa Group, operating a network of European and some medium-haul routes primarily from Dusseldorf (DUS), Cologne/Bonn (CGN), Stuttgart (STR), and Hamburg (HAM). For pet owners on European routes, Eurowings provides an in-cabin option for small animals.

## In-Cabin Pet Travel on Eurowings

Eurowings accepts small dogs and cats in the passenger cabin on applicable European routes, subject to the following conditions:

- Combined weight of the pet and carrier must not exceed 8 kg
- The carrier must fit under the seat in front of you (maximum dimensions typically 55 x 40 x 20 cm, but confirm with Eurowings for your specific flight)
- Maximum one pet per passenger in the cabin
- The pet must remain in the carrier throughout the flight
- A pet fee applies (Eurowings charges a fee per flight segment for in-cabin pets)

Eurowings is a budget carrier and pet acceptance is confirmed at booking, not guaranteed. Book the in-cabin pet space as early as possible as cabin pet spaces are limited.

## Accepted Species

Eurowings generally accepts dogs and cats as in-cabin pets. Other small pets (rabbits, guinea pigs) may be accepted on some routes -- confirm directly with Eurowings. Exotic animals, venomous animals, and birds are not accepted.

## Brachycephalic Breed Restrictions

Eurowings applies restrictions to flat-faced (brachycephalic) breeds due to the increased respiratory risk during flight. Breeds typically restricted include:

- English Bulldogs, French Bulldogs, American Bulldogs
- Pugs, Boston Terriers, Pekingese
- Shih Tzus, Lhasa Apsos
- Persian cats, Himalayan cats, Exotic Shorthair cats

Confirm whether your specific breed is accepted before booking. Breed restriction lists are updated periodically.

## Booking Your Pet on Eurowings

Add your pet to your booking when purchasing your ticket online, or contact Eurowings customer service directly. You cannot add a pet to an existing booking on all fare types -- check the fare conditions for your ticket.

Be aware that Eurowings allows a limited number of pets per flight. If the cabin pet spaces are full on your chosen flight, you will need to choose a different flight.

## Hold Cargo on Eurowings

Eurowings does not operate a dedicated live animal cargo programme in the same way as full-service carriers such as Lufthansa or British Airways. For larger animals requiring hold cargo transport, consider routing via Lufthansa (also in the Lufthansa Group) which has a well-established live animal cargo service.

## Connections Within the Lufthansa Group

Eurowings is part of the Lufthansa Group. For itineraries combining Eurowings and Lufthansa sectors, confirm pet acceptance on each segment separately, as policies differ between the two carriers.

## Key Routes for Pet Owners

Eurowings serves many popular European expat routes from German cities:
- Dusseldorf to London Heathrow, London Gatwick, and Manchester
- Cologne/Bonn to various Spanish and Italian destinations
- Stuttgart and Hamburg to Southern European holiday and expat destinations

For intra-European pet travel with a small dog or cat, Eurowings is a practical option when combined with correct documentation (EU health certificate or pet passport where applicable).
