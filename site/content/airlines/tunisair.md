Tunisair, operating under IATA code TU, is Tunisia's national flag carrier. Based at Tunis-Carthage International Airport (TUN), Tunisair operates routes to destinations across Europe (France, Germany, Belgium, Switzerland, Italy, Spain, UK), other North African countries, the Middle East and within Tunisia. For expatriates, Tunisians abroad, and international residents of Tunisia, Tunisair is a key carrier for pet transport between Tunisia and Europe.

## Cabin Pet Policy

Tunisair accepts small pets in the passenger cabin on selected routes:

- **Eligible pets**: cats and small dogs
- **Weight limit**: combined weight of pet plus carrier typically up to 5 kg (lower than many European carriers; confirm current limit with Tunisair)
- **Carrier type**: soft-sided, approved carrier fitting under the seat in front
- **Advance booking required**: pet booking must be made at time of ticket purchase or well before travel; limited allocation per flight
- **Documentation required**: valid health certificate for the pet from a licensed veterinarian; EU pet passport accepted for pets from EU countries

## Cargo Hold Policy

For larger pets, Tunisair accepts dogs and cats as excess baggage (hold cargo) on eligible routes:

- **IATA-compliant hard-shell crate** required
- **Combined weight**: confirm the maximum accepted weight for the specific route and aircraft with Tunisair cargo
- **Brachycephalic breeds**: confirm acceptance with Tunisair; restrictions may apply on warmer routes in summer
- **Advance notice**: live animal hold cargo must be pre-booked and is not accepted as walk-up excess baggage; contact Tunisair cargo or a registered agent

## Tunisia Pet Import Requirements

The Direction Generale des Services Veterinaires (DGSV) under Tunisia's Ministry of Agriculture regulates live animal imports:

1. **ISO microchip** or tattoo identification
2. **Rabies vaccination**: current; for pets from EU countries, an EU pet passport with a valid rabies vaccination is generally sufficient; for pets from non-EU countries, confirmation of a government-endorsed health certificate is required
3. **Core vaccinations** (dogs: distemper, parvovirus, hepatitis; cats: panleukopenia, herpesvirus, calicivirus)
4. **Official health certificate**: endorsed by the competent authority of the exporting country; for EU-origin pets the EU pet passport serves as the health document for import to Tunisia; for non-EU pets, a government-endorsed certificate is required
5. **Import permit**: not typically required for companion animals (cats and dogs) accompanying their owners; confirm current requirements with the Tunisian embassy or DGSV before travel
6. **No mandatory quarantine**: Tunisia does not impose mandatory quarantine for pets arriving from listed countries with complete documentation; a veterinary inspection at Tunis-Carthage Airport on arrival is standard

## Tunisia Pet Export Requirements

For pets leaving Tunisia, the DGSV issues export health certificates. The requirements differ by destination country. For EU destinations, a DGSV-endorsed certificate in the EU third-country import format is required. Your Tunisian vet completes the certificate and submits it to DGSV for endorsement.

## Key Routes

Tunisair's main routes involving pet transport:
- **Tunis (TUN) to Paris (CDG, ORY)**: popular route with regular frequency; often used for pet transport between Tunisia and France
- **Tunis to London (LHR)**: UK third-country rules apply for pets arriving in the UK; tapeworm treatment required for dogs
- **Tunis to Frankfurt (FRA), Brussels (BRU), Rome (FCO), Madrid (MAD)**
