AirAsia is Southeast Asia's largest low-cost carrier, operating an extensive network from hubs in Malaysia (Kuala Lumpur KLIA2), Thailand (Bangkok Don Mueang), Indonesia (Jakarta Soekarno-Hatta) and the Philippines (Manila). Its reach across ASEAN makes it a frequent option for pet owners relocating within the region -- but its policies require careful reading.

## Does AirAsia Accept Pets?

AirAsia's pet policy varies significantly by entity (AirAsia Malaysia, AirAsia Thailand, AirAsia Indonesia, etc.) and by route. The general position across most AirAsia-branded operations is:

**Pets in the cabin:** AirAsia **does not accept pets in the passenger cabin** on its scheduled services. Unlike some full-service carriers, pets cannot travel as cabin companions on AirAsia flights.

**Pets as checked baggage / cargo:** AirAsia accepts **cats and dogs as checked baggage** on selected routes. This means the animal travels in the cargo hold as part of your checked luggage allowance, not as a separate freight shipment.

The checked baggage pet option is subject to:
- Advance booking (at least 24 hours before departure; some routes require earlier notification)
- Maximum crate dimensions that fit the hold of the aircraft type being operated
- Combined weight limits that vary by route
- Availability -- not all routes or flights accept live animals; some aircraft types on short routes do not have temperature-controlled hold space

AirAsia X (the long-haul division, operating routes to Japan, Australia, the UK and other destinations) has historically had different and more limited pet policies. Always confirm directly with the specific AirAsia entity operating your route.

## Booking a Pet on AirAsia

To add a pet to an AirAsia booking:
1. Book your passenger tickets first
2. Contact AirAsia customer service or the relevant country's AirAsia office to add the pet as special baggage
3. This cannot currently be done via the online booking engine in most markets
4. Obtain written confirmation of pet acceptance before proceeding to the documentation stage

Avoid assuming that a pet can be added at check-in -- this is almost always refused. Advance booking is mandatory.

## Crate Requirements

AirAsia requires IATA-compliant hard-shell crates for pets travelling as checked baggage. The crate must:
- Be the appropriate size (the animal can stand, turn and lie down comfortably)
- Have ventilation on at least 3 sides
- Have secure metal door latches
- Have a water container accessible from outside
- Be labelled with owner's name, contact details, origin and destination

Crate dimensions permitted vary by aircraft type. For routes operated by Airbus A320 family aircraft (the standard AirAsia fleet), the maximum crate height is limited. Confirm with the airline for exact dimensions before purchasing a crate.

## Country-Specific Requirements

AirAsia operates across multiple countries, each with its own pet import rules. Some key destination-specific notes:

**Malaysia (entering):** Pets require an import permit from the Department of Veterinary Services Malaysia (DVS), valid health certificate and current vaccinations. Rabies titre test required for animals from certain countries.

**Thailand (entering):** Import permit from the Department of Livestock Development, health certificate endorsed by the competent authority of origin, vaccination records. The process for entering Thailand is outlined in detail on the Thai government's livestock department website.

**Australia (AirAsia X):** Australia has among the strictest pet import rules in the world. The full pre-arrival protocol including titre testing, approved facilities and strict quarantine applies regardless of the airline used. See the [Australia pet import guide](/blog/australia-pet-quarantine-explained/) for the full process.

## Notes on Low-Cost Carrier Pet Travel

Low-cost carriers typically offer fewer pet handling facilities at airports compared to full-service airlines. At secondary airports where AirAsia operates, the live animal handling infrastructure may be basic. Confirm that your departure and arrival airports have the required facilities before booking.
