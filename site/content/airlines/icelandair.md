Icelandair operates an extensive network connecting Iceland's Keflavik International Airport (KEF) with destinations across Europe and North America. For pet owners, Iceland is both a popular expat destination and a busy transit point -- but the airline and the country's biosecurity requirements deserve close attention before you book.

## Does Icelandair Accept Pets?

Icelandair accepts pets as **checked baggage** (excess baggage in the cabin) and in the **cargo hold**. The rules differ for each option.

**Pets in the cabin (as checked/excess baggage):**
- Cats and small dogs may travel in the cabin in an approved soft carrier
- The combined weight of the pet plus carrier must not exceed 8 kg
- The carrier must fit under the seat in front (maximum dimensions are confirmed at booking -- check current Icelandair guidelines)
- Maximum 1 pet per passenger; maximum 2 pets per flight in the cabin
- Not available on all routes or fare types -- confirm when booking

**Pets in the cargo hold:**
- Larger dogs that exceed the cabin weight limit travel in the pressurised, temperature-controlled cargo hold
- The crate must meet IATA requirements (appropriate size, ventilation on at least 3 sides, secure door latches)
- Maximum combined weight (crate and animal): typically up to 45 kg for standard cargo; heavier animals may require special cargo arrangements

## Iceland's Pet Import Requirements

This is where planning really matters. Iceland is a disease-free island with strict import controls. Whether or not you are flying Icelandair, the following requirements apply to all pets entering Iceland:

**From EU/EEA countries:**
- Valid ISO microchip
- Current rabies vaccination
- EU Pet Passport or equivalent animal health certificate
- For dogs arriving from certain countries: tapeworm (Echinococcus) treatment within 24 to 120 hours before arrival

**From non-EU countries (including the UK and USA):**
- ISO microchip
- Rabies vaccination (primary series complete and within valid period)
- **Rabies antibody titre test (FAVN)** at an approved laboratory; result must be at least 0.5 IU/ml; the test must be conducted after microchipping and vaccination, and Iceland requires the test result to have been valid for at least **3 months** before arrival (similar to EU third-country rules)
- Official health certificate endorsed by the competent authority of the country of origin
- Advance notification to Icelandic Food and Veterinary Authority (MAST) is recommended

Iceland does **not** impose a mandatory quarantine period for pets that meet all requirements.

## Seasonal Restrictions

Icelandair applies temperature-based restrictions on live animal cargo during periods of extreme cold (winter) or heat (rare in Iceland but applicable for European and North American origin points in summer). Brachycephalic (flat-faced) breeds face additional restrictions and may not be accepted in the cargo hold on certain routes or during seasonal peaks.

## Booking Your Pet on Icelandair

Contact Icelandair's customer service team directly to register a cabin pet or arrange cargo transport. Advance booking is required -- live animal spaces are limited and cannot always be added online.

For cargo transport, Icelandair works with its cargo division (Icelandair Cargo). Your pet relocation agent or the airline's cargo desk can confirm current routes and capacity.

## Iceland as a Transit Point

Keflavik Airport (KEF) is a popular transatlantic stopover hub. If your pet is transiting through Iceland on a connecting Icelandair flight, confirm whether Iceland's import rules apply to transit animals -- animals not entering Icelandic territory are typically handled differently, but always confirm with MAST before booking a transit routing.
