Air Madagascar (IATA: MD) is the national airline of Madagascar, operating from Ivato International Airport in Antananarivo (TNR). The airline connects Madagascar to a small number of international destinations including Paris, Reunion, Mauritius and regional African routes, while maintaining an extensive domestic network across the island.

## Pet Policy Overview

Air Madagascar's published information in English is limited. For live animal cargo enquiries, contact Air Madagascar's cargo division directly before making any booking. For international routes from Madagascar or to Madagascar, IATA Live Animals Regulations (LAR) provide the baseline framework, but carrier-specific acceptance, rates and procedures must be confirmed directly.

Given the limited international connectivity and the unique biosecurity environment of Madagascar, working with an IPATA-accredited pet relocation agent familiar with Madagascar routes is strongly advisable.

## Madagascar's Pet Import Requirements

Madagascar has a unique biodiversity profile, and its biosecurity requirements for imported animals reflect this:

1. **Import permit** from the Direction des Services Veterinaires -- apply well in advance; processing can take several weeks
2. **ISO microchip**
3. **Rabies vaccination** -- current; primary vaccination at least 21 to 30 days before travel
4. **Core vaccinations** -- dogs: distemper, parvovirus, hepatitis; cats: panleukopenia, herpesvirus, calicivirus
5. **Government-endorsed health certificate** from the country of origin
6. **Veterinary inspection on arrival** at TNR

A rabies titre test may be required depending on the country of origin; confirm with the Malagasy veterinary authority. Quarantine may be imposed on arrival for animals with incomplete documentation.

## Exporting Pets from Madagascar

Obtaining an export certificate for a pet leaving Madagascar involves the Direction des Services Veterinaires. Allow several days for the process. The destination country's own health certificate format will also apply.

## International Route Network

Air Madagascar's international services (subject to change; confirm current schedules):
- Paris Charles de Gaulle (CDG) -- Europe's main gateway to Madagascar
- Reunion Island (RUN) and Mauritius (MRU) -- Indian Ocean island connections
- Nairobi (NBO) -- East Africa
- Johannesburg (JNB) -- Southern Africa connections

For live animal transport on the Paris route, Air Madagascar interlines with Air France on some schedules; confirm whether cargo arrangements flow through Air France Cargo or Air Madagascar Cargo directly.

## Crate Requirements

IATA-compliant hard-shell crates are required for cargo hold transport. The crate must allow the animal to stand, turn and lie in a natural position. Ventilation on three sides, water container attached to the door and clear live animal labels are all required. Size guides are available from the IATA LAR or from any IPATA pet relocation agent.
