## easyJet Does Not Carry Pets

easyJet -- one of Europe's largest low-cost carriers -- does not accept pets on any of its flights. This applies to:
- Pets in the cabin
- Pets in the aircraft hold
- Pets as checked baggage

The only animals permitted on easyJet are **registered assistance dogs and guide dogs** that comply with easyJet's assistance animal policy.

## Why LCCs Generally Do Not Accept Pets

Low-cost carriers operate on a model of rapid aircraft turnarounds, standardised operations, and minimal ground handling complexity. Live animal transport requires:
- Veterinary documentation checks
- Temperature-controlled hold monitoring on some routes
- Specific loading and unloading procedures
- Emergency animal welfare protocols

These requirements add operational complexity that is incompatible with the low-cost model. easyJet, Ryanair, Jet2, Wizz Air, and TUI all share the same position.

## Assistance Dogs on easyJet

Registered guide dogs and assistance dogs can travel on easyJet flights. Requirements:
- Advance notification (at least 48 hours before departure)
- Documentation of assistance dog registration
- Dog must remain with the handler throughout the flight

Contact easyJet's special assistance team before booking: easyjet.com/en/help/special-assistance

## What to Do If You Need to Fly With a Pet to an easyJet Destination

easyJet's routes primarily serve short-haul European and North African destinations. For these same destinations, alternative carriers typically include:

| Destination | Alternative airlines that accept pets |
|------------|--------------------------------------|
| Spain (mainland) | Iberia, Vueling, British Airways (cabin/cargo) |
| Italy | Alitalia/ITA Airways, British Airways, Lufthansa |
| France | Air France, British Airways |
| Germany | Lufthansa, British Airways |
| Portugal | TAP Air Portugal, British Airways |
| Netherlands | KLM |
| Switzerland | Swiss Air Lines |
| Greece | Aegean Airlines, British Airways |

For the hold/cargo option on longer routes, contact British Airways World Cargo, Lufthansa Cargo, or a specialist IPATA-member pet transport agent.

## Summary

easyJet is not an option for pet travel. If you are planning an international move and hoping to fly your pet on easyJet, you will need to use an alternative carrier. The good news is that most of easyJet's destinations are served by airlines that do carry pets.

*Policy information accurate as of May 2026. Always verify directly with easyJet and alternative carriers before travel.*
