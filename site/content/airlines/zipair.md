ZIPAIR Tokyo is Japan Airlines' low-cost, long-haul subsidiary, launched in 2020. It operates a small fleet of Boeing 787 Dreamliners from Tokyo Narita (NRT) to a growing list of long-haul destinations across the Pacific and to Europe.

## Pet Policy Overview

ZIPAIR operates as a low-cost carrier, and its approach to ancillary services including live animal transport reflects that model. At the time of writing, ZIPAIR's policies on pets in the cargo hold are limited. **Always confirm directly with ZIPAIR whether live animals are accepted on your specific route and aircraft before making any travel arrangements.** Low-cost long-haul carriers often restrict or do not accept live animals in cargo hold due to the cost and logistics of compliance.

Unlike JAL (its parent), ZIPAIR does not have a published live animal cargo programme readily available on its website. This is worth verifying if you are considering this carrier for a pet shipment to or from Japan.

## Japan's Pet Import Requirements

Whatever airline you use to arrive in Japan, the Animal Quarantine Service (AQS) requirements apply:

1. **ISO microchip** -- implanted before any vaccinations in the protocol
2. **Two rabies vaccinations** -- at least 30 days apart; both given after the microchip
3. **FAVN titre test** at an AQS-approved laboratory -- result 0.5 IU/ml or above; at least 30 days after the second vaccination
4. **180-day wait** after the titre test before arriving in Japan
5. **Pre-arrival inspection appointment** booked with AQS at least 40 days before arrival
6. **Health certificate** in the Japanese government format (Ministry of Agriculture, Forestry and Fisheries)
7. **Import confirmation** from AQS

If any step in the sequence is wrong, Japan imposes mandatory quarantine on arrival -- up to 180 days. This is one of the strictest protocols in the world; do not leave any step to guesswork.

## Routes and Network

ZIPAIR flies from Tokyo Narita (NRT) to destinations including:
- Bangkok Suvarnabhumi (BKK)
- Seoul Incheon (ICN)
- Honolulu (HNL)
- Los Angeles (LAX)
- San Francisco (SFO)
- Vancouver (YVR)
- Paris Charles de Gaulle (CDG)
- Singapore Changi (SIN)

The network is expanding; check the ZIPAIR website for current routes.

## Crate Requirements

IATA Live Animals Regulations (LAR) apply to all live animal shipments regardless of carrier. Your crate must be IATA-compliant: hard-shell, ventilated on three sides, with a water container attached to the door and sufficient space for the animal to stand, turn and lie in a natural position. Food must be attached outside the crate for flights over 24 hours.

## Working with a Pet Relocation Agent

Given ZIPAIR's limited published pet transport information and Japan's exceptionally rigorous import protocol, working with an IPATA-accredited pet relocation agent with Japan experience is strongly recommended for this route. An experienced agent will know which airlines and routings best support Japan-bound or Japan-origin pet shipments.
