China Eastern Airlines is one of China's three major carriers, operating from its Shanghai Pudong (PVG) hub across a wide international network including Europe, North America, Australia, Southeast Asia, and the Middle East. For pet owners relocating to or from China, China Eastern is one of the primary options for live animal cargo.

**Cabin vs cargo**

China Eastern does not permit pets in the passenger cabin on international routes. Pets travel as checked cargo (excess baggage or air freight, depending on booking class and route). The hold is pressurised and temperature-controlled on modern aircraft.

**Booking live animal cargo**

Live animal cargo on China Eastern needs to be arranged in advance - it cannot be added at the check-in counter on the day. Contact China Eastern's cargo team or passenger services when planning your flight. Confirm that your specific route accepts live animals: not all aircraft types and route combinations have live animal-approved hold configurations.

**Crate requirements**

China Eastern follows IATA live animal regulations. The crate must:
- Be IATA-approved (hard-sided, ventilated, secure door latch)
- Be large enough for the animal to stand, turn, sit, and lie down naturally
- Have water and food dishes accessible from outside
- Be labelled with your name, contact details, and the words "LIVE ANIMAL / ANIMAL VIVANT"

**Breed restrictions**

China Eastern restricts brachycephalic breeds (flat-faced dogs and cats) from cargo transport. Breeds commonly affected include English Bulldogs, French Bulldogs, American Bulldogs, Pugs, Boston Terriers, Persian cats, Himalayan cats, and others with compressed airways. Confirm your breed is accepted before booking.

**Flying to China**

Pets arriving in China face additional requirements beyond the airline's own policies. China's GACC (General Administration of Customs) requires a specific-format health certificate, quarantine on arrival (typically 7 days minimum), and in some cases embassy endorsement. Book quarantine space before booking your flight. See the China pet import guide for the full preparation process.

**Practical note**

China Eastern's Shanghai hub has good live animal handling facilities. If you're routing a long-haul journey with a transit at PVG, confirm the transit live animal handling process before booking - transit times for live animals need to be managed carefully.
