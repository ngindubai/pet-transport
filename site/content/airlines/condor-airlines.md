## Condor Airlines: Pets on Leisure Routes

Condor is a German leisure airline operating from Frankfurt and other German airports, as well as some UK departure points. It focuses on holiday destinations: Mediterranean, Canary Islands, Caribbean, and select long-haul routes. For pet owners, Condor is a viable option for European leisure moves.

## In-Cabin Pet Policy

Condor accepts small pets in the passenger cabin on most routes:

- **Species accepted**: dogs, cats, and small birds
- **Combined weight limit**: up to 8 kg (pet plus carrier)
- **Carrier dimensions**: maximum approximately 55 x 40 x 20 cm (check current specifications at booking)
- **Carrier type**: IATA-compliant soft or semi-rigid carrier, must fit under seat
- **Per passenger**: one pet in cabin

Advance notification is required. You cannot add a cabin pet at check-in without prior booking. Contact Condor's service line to add the pet to your booking.

## Hold Cargo for Larger Pets

Larger dogs and cats that exceed cabin weight limits travel in the aircraft hold:

- IATA-compliant rigid crate required
- Weight of animal plus crate charged as excess baggage
- Breed restrictions apply (brachycephalic breeds face restrictions on some routes, especially summer)
- Not all routes have hold pet capacity -- confirm at booking

## Seasonal Restrictions

Condor operates many routes to hot summer destinations (Lanzarote, Mallorca, Greece, Turkey). During peak summer months, live animal hold acceptance is often suspended on routes where ground temperatures exceed 29 to 32 degrees C. If you are planning a summer pet move, confirm well in advance that your dates and route are eligible.

## Booking a Pet on Condor

1. Book your own flights first (condor.com or travel agent)
2. Call Condor customer service to add the pet to the booking
3. Provide animal species, breed, and weight
4. Confirm cabin or hold placement
5. Receive written confirmation

Always get written confirmation. Verbal agreements at check-in are not guaranteed.

## Destination Entry Requirements

Condor flies to EU countries, North Africa (Morocco, Egypt), Turkey, and Caribbean destinations. Each has its own pet import rules. Condor does not manage import compliance -- this is entirely the passenger's responsibility. Check the destination country's requirements through the relevant authority before booking.
