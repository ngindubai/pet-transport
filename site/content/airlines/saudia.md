Saudia (Saudi Arabian Airlines) is the national flag carrier of Saudi Arabia, operating from its main hubs in Jeddah and Riyadh to destinations across Europe, Asia, Africa, the Americas, and the Middle East. For pet transport, Saudia routes all animals through its cargo division.

## Pet Policy Overview

Saudia does not carry pets in the passenger cabin. All pet transport is managed through **Saudia Cargo** as live animal shipments. This means your pet travels in a separate cargo hold, typically on the same aircraft as you if you coordinate the booking, or as a separate cargo-only shipment.

## Booking Through Saudia Cargo

Contact Saudia Cargo directly to arrange pet transport:

- Website: saudiacargo.com
- Cargo offices at King Abdulaziz International Airport (JED) and King Khalid International Airport (RUH)

Bookings must be confirmed with Saudia Cargo, not through the passenger reservation system. Confirm availability on your specific route and travel date early -- live animal space is limited.

## Crate Requirements

All pets must travel in IATA-compliant crates (Live Animals Regulations standards):

- Rigid-walled crate with ventilation on at least three sides
- Secure door latch mechanism
- Absorbent bedding on the floor
- Crate large enough for the animal to stand, turn around, and lie down naturally
- Water and food containers accessible from outside
- "Live Animal" and "This Way Up" labels applied

## Health Documentation

For travel to or from Saudi Arabia, documentation requirements include:

**For entry into Saudi Arabia:**
- Official veterinary health certificate from origin country
- Certificate of Origin
- Rabies vaccination current and recorded
- Additional vaccinations as required (varies by species)
- Saudi Ministry of Agriculture import permit may be required

**For departure from Saudi Arabia:**
- Health certificate from a Saudi Ministry of Agriculture-licensed vet
- Export permit from the Ministry of Agriculture

## Breed Restrictions

Saudia applies IATA brachycephalic breed restrictions -- short-nosed dogs and cats are not accepted as cargo during summer months (typically May to September) and may face year-round restrictions on certain routes.

Saudi Arabia also has domestic legislation on certain dog breeds. Verify your breed's specific status with the Saudi Ministry of Agriculture and Saudia Cargo before booking.

## Temperature Embargoes

Saudi Arabia experiences extreme summer temperatures. Saudia applies temperature-based embargoes for live animal cargo during the hottest months. Moving a pet to or from Saudi Arabia is best planned for October through April.

## Service Animals

Guide dogs and hearing dogs are accepted in the Saudia cabin for passengers with disabilities, subject to advance notification and documentation. Contact Saudia's Special Assistance team at the time of booking.

## Practical Notes for Saudi-Based Expats

- Many international families in Riyadh, Jeddah, and Al Khobar use specialist pet relocation agents with Saudi import/export experience -- the Arabic-language documentation can be complex
- The Saudi MEWA (Ministry of Environment, Water and Agriculture) website has current import guidance
- Allow at least 6 to 8 weeks for the Saudi import process end-to-end
