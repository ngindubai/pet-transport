IndiGo is India's largest airline by market share and operates a vast domestic network, plus a growing number of international routes across Asia and the Middle East. For pet owners, IndiGo has an important limitation: **pets are only accepted in the cabin on domestic India routes**. International flights are off-limits.

## Domestic Cabin Pet Policy

On domestic IndiGo flights:

- Small dogs and cats are accepted in the cabin
- Combined weight (pet plus carrier) must not exceed 7 kg
- Pets must fit under the seat in front in an approved soft-sided carrier
- Only one pet per passenger
- Pre-booking required -- pet spaces are limited per flight
- A per-sector fee applies (check current rate at goindigo.in)

## What You Need for Domestic Travel

- Approved soft carrier with secure closure and ventilation
- Health certificate from a registered vet issued within 48 hours of travel (IndiGo's requirement for domestic)
- Proof of vaccination (rabies at minimum)
- No specific breed restrictions for domestic cabin travel, but very large animals (even if under 7 kg) are at check-in discretion

## International Flights: IndiGo Does Not Accept Pets

This is the key point for international relocations involving India:

IndiGo does **not** accept pets on its international routes, either in the cabin or as cargo. If you are moving a pet from India internationally, your options are:

- **Air India**: India's flag carrier accepts pets in cargo on many international routes. Contact Air India Cargo.
- **Emirates, Qatar Airways, Etihad**: All three operate from major Indian cities and accept pets as cargo on most routes.
- **Specialist air cargo**: For complex routes or large animals, specialist pet cargo operators can arrange air freight on commercial cargo aircraft.

## Planning an International Move from India

If you are relocating from India to the UK, Australia, USA, or another destination:

1. Do not count on IndiGo for the international leg
2. Check Air India Cargo (airindiacargo.com) for pet acceptance on your specific route
3. Consider Gulf carriers (Emirates, Qatar, Etihad) which have strong networks from major Indian cities
4. Use a specialist IPATA member agent with India experience -- the export paperwork from India (export certificate from the Animal Quarantine and Certification Service, AQCS) is specific and requires a government-accredited vet

## India Pet Export Process

For pets leaving India:
- Health certificate from an AQCS-approved vet (Animal Quarantine and Certification Service, under DAHD)
- Export NOC from AQCS
- Microchip (required by most destination countries)
- Rabies vaccination current
- Additional tests/treatments as required by the destination

AQCS offices operate at Chennai, Delhi, Kolkata, and Mumbai. Allow 2 to 3 weeks for the full export certification process.
