Aerolineas Argentinas is Argentina's national carrier and the dominant airline for domestic and international routes to and from Buenos Aires (Ezeiza International Airport, EZE, and Aeroparque Jorge Newbery, AEP). For pet owners in South America or those relocating to Argentina, it is often the first carrier to investigate.

## Does Aerolineas Argentinas Accept Pets?

Yes. Aerolineas Argentinas accepts pets both **in the cabin** (small cats and dogs) and **in the cargo hold** (larger animals). The policy applies to both domestic Argentine flights and selected international routes.

## Pets in the Cabin

Cats and small dogs may travel in the cabin on Aerolineas Argentinas flights under the following conditions:

- Combined weight of pet plus carrier must not exceed 8 kg
- The carrier must fit under the seat in front
- The carrier must be soft-sided and ventilated
- Maximum 1 pet per passenger; limits per flight apply
- The pet must remain inside the carrier throughout the flight
- Not available on all routes or fare classes -- confirm at time of booking

For domestic Argentine flights, the cabin pet option is generally available on most routes. For international flights (including long-haul routes to Europe and Spain via Madrid), confirm availability and route-specific restrictions when booking.

## Pets in the Cargo Hold

Larger dogs, or pets that exceed the cabin weight limit, travel in the pressurised cargo hold. Requirements include:

- IATA-compliant hard-shell crate of appropriate size for the animal
- Adequate ventilation on at least 3 sides
- Secure door latches and fittings
- Water container accessible from outside
- No food in the crate during flight (water only)
- Combined weight and crate dimension limits per aircraft type

## International Routes and Pet Acceptance

Aerolineas Argentinas operates long-haul international services from Buenos Aires Ezeiza to:
- Madrid (MAD) -- the primary Europe connection
- Miami (MIA), New York (JFK) -- USA
- Various South American capitals

Not all international routes may accept live animals. Long-haul transatlantic flights with layovers in Madrid are an option, but transit rules through Spain (as an EU member) apply for animals transiting with EU entry.

## Breed Restrictions

Aerolineas Argentinas applies restrictions on brachycephalic (snub-nosed) breeds. Specific breed lists and seasonal temperature restrictions are maintained by the airline. Check the current list with the airline's customer service before booking a brachycephalic breed.

Dangerous dog breeds (as defined under Argentine law or the law of the destination country) may face additional restrictions or require specific documentation.

## Booking Your Pet

Add your pet to your booking by contacting Aerolineas Argentinas customer service directly. In Argentina, the airline operates an extensive domestic network and the process for adding a cabin pet is well-established. For cargo travel, contact the cargo division (Aerolineas Argentinas Cargo).

## Documentation for Argentina Import/Export

**Importing a pet to Argentina:**
SENASA (Servicio Nacional de Sanidad y Calidad Agroalimentaria) regulates pet imports into Argentina. Requirements include an official health certificate from the country of origin, endorsed by the government veterinary authority of that country, current vaccinations and microchip documentation. Some countries require a rabies titre test result.

**Exporting a pet from Argentina:**
SENASA issues the export health certificate for pets leaving Argentina. Book this well in advance as appointment availability can be limited.
