WestJet is Canada's second-largest airline, operating from its Calgary hub across Canada, the USA, the Caribbean, Mexico, and select European routes. It is a popular option for domestic Canadian pet travel and for international moves into or out of Canada.

**Cabin pets**

WestJet accepts small cats and dogs in the cabin on qualifying flights. The requirements:
- Combined weight of pet and carrier must not exceed 10kg (22lbs)
- Carrier must fit under the seat in front (typically 43cm x 30cm x 20cm)
- Carrier must be soft-sided and well-ventilated
- Pet must remain in the carrier throughout the flight
- One pet per passenger

Cabin pets are not accepted on all routes - WestJet's transatlantic routes and certain Caribbean routes have different policies. Confirm when booking.

**Cargo pets (Pets as Cargo)**

WestJet's cargo program accepts larger dogs and cats. Requirements:
- IATA-approved hard-sided kennel sized for the animal (must stand, turn, sit, lie down)
- Kennel must have food and water bowls accessible from outside
- Pet must be at least 8 weeks old and weaned
- Health certificate from a vet (within 10 days of travel for most destinations)

**Temperature restrictions**

WestJet does not accept cargo pets when ground temperatures at origin, destination, or stopovers exceed 29.4C (85F) or fall below -12.2C (10F). In practice this affects summer travel in southern USA and winter travel in northern Canada. Book early and check seasonal restrictions.

**Breed restrictions**

Brachycephalic breeds are restricted from cargo travel. These include English Bulldogs, French Bulldogs, American Bulldogs, Pugs, Boston Terriers, Shih Tzus, Boxers, Pekingese, and flat-faced cat breeds. Some restrictions also apply to cabin travel for these breeds.

**Canada import requirements**

Pets arriving in Canada from international destinations need a health certificate and, for dogs, current rabies vaccination. Canada's CFIA requirements for household pets are relatively straightforward. See the Canada pet import guide for full details.

**Booking**

Add pets to your WestJet booking online or by calling the WestJet contact centre. Cargo pets should be arranged separately through WestJet Cargo. There is a fee for both cabin and cargo pets.
