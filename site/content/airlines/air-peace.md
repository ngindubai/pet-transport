Air Peace is Nigeria's largest carrier, operating an extensive domestic network within Nigeria and a growing number of international routes from its hub at Murtala Muhammed International Airport, Lagos (LOS). For pet owners relocating to or from Nigeria, Air Peace is often the most practical option for intra-African and regional routes.

## Does Air Peace Accept Pets?

Air Peace accepts **small cats and dogs in the cabin** and **larger animals in the cargo hold** on selected routes. The airline's growing international network -- including direct services to London, Dubai, Johannesburg and other destinations -- makes it increasingly relevant for international pet transport.

## Pets in the Cabin

For cabin pet travel on Air Peace:
- Small cats and dogs may travel in the cabin as hand luggage on selected routes
- Combined weight of pet plus carrier must comply with the airline's current limit (confirm directly as this evolves)
- An approved ventilated carrier is required, sized to fit under the seat
- A health certificate issued by a licensed veterinarian is required
- Not all routes or aircraft types accommodate cabin pets -- confirm at booking

## Pets in the Cargo Hold

Larger dogs that cannot travel in the cabin are accepted as live animal cargo on Air Peace's cargo services. This requires:
- An IATA-compliant hard-shell crate of appropriate size
- Advance booking and confirmation with Air Peace Cargo
- Supporting health documentation
- Compliance with both Nigerian export rules and the destination country's import rules

## Nigeria Pet Export Requirements

Exporting a pet from Nigeria requires a health certificate from the Federal Ministry of Agriculture and Rural Development (FMARD) veterinary services. The steps include:

1. Veterinary inspection of your pet at an approved veterinary clinic or government vet
2. Health certificate issued on FMARD-approved format
3. Export permit from FMARD
4. Endorsement of the certificate as required by the destination country (e.g., USDA APHIS attestation for USA; APHA requirements for UK)

This process can take several weeks, particularly for first-time exporters. Start early and use a FMARD-registered veterinary practice that has experience with international export certifications.

## Nigeria Pet Import Requirements

Importing a pet to Nigeria involves:
- Import permit from the Federal Ministry of Agriculture (FMARD)
- ISO microchip
- Current vaccinations including rabies, and for dogs: distemper, parvovirus, hepatitis, leptospirosis
- Health certificate endorsed by the government authority of the exporting country
- The permit application should be submitted several weeks before travel

Nigeria does not currently have a mandatory quarantine period for pets from recognised countries that arrive with complete documentation, but the veterinary inspection at Lagos or Abuja airports is thorough and incomplete documentation results in detention.

## International Routes Relevant to Pet Owners

Air Peace's current international network includes:
- **Lagos (LOS) to London Gatwick (LGW)** -- direct service
- **Lagos (LOS) to Dubai (DXB)** -- direct service
- **Lagos (LOS) to Johannesburg (JNB)** -- direct service
- **Lagos (LOS) to Accra (ACC)**, **Dakar (DSS)**, and other West and Central African destinations
- **Lagos (LOS) to Houston (IAH)** -- USA service

Confirm live animal acceptance on the specific route before booking, as not all routes on all aircraft types accommodate cargo or cabin pets.
