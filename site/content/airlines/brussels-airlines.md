Brussels Airlines is Belgium's flag carrier and a Star Alliance member, operating from Brussels Airport (BRU) to European destinations, North America, and an extensive sub-Saharan African network. As a Lufthansa Group airline, Brussels Airlines shares operational standards with Lufthansa and Swiss International Air Lines.

## Pets in the Cabin

Brussels Airlines accepts small dogs and cats in the cabin. The combined weight of the animal and carrier must not exceed 8kg. The soft-sided carrier must fit under the seat in front of you. One pet per passenger is the limit, and a maximum number of pets per flight applies.

## Pets as Cargo

Dogs and cats over the cabin limit travel as checked baggage or manifest cargo in an IATA-compliant hard crate. Crate requirements follow IATA Live Animals Regulations standards: solid sides, secure door, ventilation on at least three sides, water dispensers, and sufficient headroom and floor space for the animal to stand, turn, and lie down.

## Africa Routes

Brussels Airlines is one of the few European carriers with a dedicated sub-Saharan Africa network. For pet moves between Belgium and Central or East Africa, Brussels Airlines is often the most direct routing. Confirm pet cargo acceptance on your specific African destination route at booking -- not all African routes carry live animals.

## EU Pet Rules

For intra-EU moves, pets need an EU pet passport, ISO microchip, and current rabies vaccination. For pets entering Belgium from outside the EU, the standard EU import pathway applies: AHC, titre test, and three-month wait where required.

## Brachycephalic Restrictions

As a Lufthansa Group airline, Brussels Airlines follows broadly similar brachycephalic breed restrictions to Lufthansa. Flat-faced breeds may be restricted in cargo. If your pet is brachycephalic and under the 8kg cabin limit, cabin travel is the safer and more practical option.

## Booking

Pets must be added by contacting Brussels Airlines customer service. Online booking does not support pet additions. Book as early as possible -- space per flight is limited.
