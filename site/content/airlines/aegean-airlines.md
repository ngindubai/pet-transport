Aegean Airlines is Greece's largest carrier and a Star Alliance member, operating from Athens International Airport (ATH) across Europe and to select long-haul destinations. As Greece's national flag carrier (sharing that role with its subsidiary Olympic Air), Aegean handles the bulk of international passenger traffic into and out of Athens.

## Pets in the Cabin

Aegean Airlines accepts small dogs, cats, and caged birds in the cabin. The combined weight of the animal and the carrier must not exceed 8kg. The carrier must be soft-sided and fit under the seat. One pet per passenger is the standard limit and space per flight is restricted.

## Pets as Cargo

Dogs and cats that exceed the cabin weight limit travel as checked baggage or manifest cargo in an IATA-compliant hard crate. The crate must have solid sides, a secure door, and ventilation on at least three sides. Water dispensers must be fitted inside and visible through the door.

## Restricted Breeds

Aegean restricts certain brachycephalic breeds from the cargo hold. Confirm the current breed restriction list directly with Aegean before booking, particularly for flat-faced breeds like Bulldogs, Pugs, and Persians.

## Greek Domestic Routes

Aegean and Olympic Air operate extensive domestic Greek routes connecting Athens to islands and mainland cities. The same cabin and cargo pet rules apply on domestic flights. For island moves (Crete, Rhodes, Corfu), pets in cabin are common.

## Connecting through Athens

Athens Eleftherios Venizelos Airport (ATH) has cargo facilities operated by Aegean Cargo and ground handlers. For live animal cargo transiting through ATH, confirm handling arrangements with your agent or Aegean Cargo in advance.

## EU Pet Passport

For moves within EU member states, pets travelling with Aegean need a valid EU pet passport, microchip, and current rabies vaccination. No titre test is required for intra-EU moves.

## Practical Advice

For moves involving Greece and countries outside the EU, ensure destination-country requirements are met before travel. Greece follows EU pet travel rules for inbound pets. For pets entering Greece from outside the EU (UK, USA, etc.), the EU AHC and titre test requirements apply as for any other EU member state.
