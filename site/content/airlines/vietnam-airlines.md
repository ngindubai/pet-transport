    Vietnam Airlines accepts small pets in the cabin on eligible routes and larger animals in the hold as cargo. The airline operates out of Hanoi (HAN) and Ho Chi Minh City (SGN).

Vietnam has strict pet import requirements, including microchipping, vaccination records, and a government-issued health certificate. These apply to any pet entering Vietnam, regardless of carrier.

faqs:
  - question: "Can I bring my pet in the cabin on Vietnam Airlines?"
    answer: "Vietnam Airlines allows small pets in the cabin on some routes, subject to weight and size limits. The combined weight of the pet and carrier is typically capped at around 5kg. The carrier must fit under the seat in front of you. Confirm the current cabin pet rules for your specific route before booking."
  - question: "How do I book pet transport on Vietnam Airlines?"
    answer: "Notify Vietnam Airlines when you make your booking. For international cargo, use an IPATA-accredited pet transport agent who can handle the documentation and airline coordination. Allow plenty of lead time. Live animal space is limited on most flights."
  - question: "What are Vietnam's pet import requirements?"
    answer: "Pets entering Vietnam must have: ISO-standard microchip, valid rabies vaccination (at least 30 days before travel), a veterinary health certificate issued within 30 days of departure, and a permit from Vietnam's Department of Animal Health. Requirements change, so confirm the current rules with Vietnam's Ministry of Agriculture and Rural Development or your pet transport agent."
  - question: "Does Vietnam Airlines accept brachycephalic breeds?"
    answer: "Snub-nosed breeds face additional restrictions on Vietnam Airlines. The policy depends on the route and season. Always confirm directly with the airline before booking a flat-faced dog or cat. A vet certificate confirming fitness to fly is typically required."
  - question: "What documents does my pet need to fly with Vietnam Airlines?"
    answer: "A valid veterinary health certificate, microchip proof, and vaccination records are the baseline. International routes require destination-country import documents in addition. Vietnam Airlines may require documentary proof at check-in. Your destination country's requirements apply regardless of which airline you use."
  - question: "What crate does my pet need for Vietnam Airlines cargo?"
    answer: "IATA-compliant rigid crates are required for all cargo pet transport. The crate must allow your pet to stand, sit upright, lie down, and turn around without touching the sides or top. Three-sided ventilation, a water container, and live animal labels are mandatory. Soft-sided carriers are not accepted for hold travel."
