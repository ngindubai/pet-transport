Asiana Airlines is South Korea's second-largest international carrier, operating from Seoul Incheon (ICN) across Asia, Europe, North America, and Oceania. As a Star Alliance member, Asiana connects Korea with a wide global network and is a key option for pets travelling to or from Korea.

**Cabin pets**

Asiana Airlines accepts small pets in the cabin on qualifying routes. Key requirements:
- Combined weight of pet and carrier: 7kg maximum (verify current limit with Asiana)
- Carrier must fit under the seat in front
- Pet must remain in carrier for the entire flight
- One pet per passenger
- Cats and small dogs accepted - not other species

Cabin availability is route-dependent. Confirm before purchasing tickets.

**Cargo pets**

Larger dogs and cats travel as checked baggage or cargo in the hold. IATA-compliant hard-sided crates are required. The crate must allow the animal to stand, turn, sit, and lie down at full size. Water and food dishes must be accessible from outside without opening the door.

**Breed restrictions**

Brachycephalic breeds (flat-faced dogs and cats) are restricted from cargo. The list typically includes English Bulldogs, French Bulldogs, American Bulldogs, Pugs, Boston Terriers, Shih Tzus, and flat-faced cat breeds (Persians, Himalayans, Exotic Shorthairs). Confirm your breed's status with Asiana before booking.

**South Korea import requirements**

Pets arriving in South Korea are subject to quarantine inspection by the Animal and Plant Quarantine Agency (APQA). A health certificate from the origin country's government authority, rabies vaccination, and microchip are required. South Korea is increasingly well-regarded for its pet-friendly culture - the import process is structured but manageable with correct documentation.

**Incheon hub**

Seoul Incheon is one of Asia's top-ranked airports and a common transit hub for long-haul Asia routes. Live animal cargo handling at ICN is well-established. If your pet is transiting ICN, confirm live animal transit procedures with Asiana.

**Booking**

Contact Asiana's passenger services when booking to confirm live animal acceptance. For cargo pets, separate arrangement through Asiana Cargo may be needed.
