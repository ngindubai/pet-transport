Viva Aerobus is one of Mexico's leading low-cost carriers, operating an extensive domestic network as well as routes to the USA, Canada, and Latin America. For pet owners travelling within Mexico, Viva Aerobus offers in-cabin pet acceptance on applicable domestic routes.

## In-Cabin Pet Acceptance

Viva Aerobus allows small dogs and cats to travel in the passenger cabin on domestic Mexican routes. The key requirements are:

- **Combined weight**: The pet plus the carrier must not exceed 10 kg
- **Carrier type**: Soft-sided, fully enclosed carrier
- **Carrier dimensions**: Must fit under the seat in front of you (approximately 45 x 35 x 20 cm -- confirm current dimensions when booking)
- **Pets per passenger**: One pet per passenger
- **Pets per flight**: A limited number of pets are accepted per flight

Pets must remain inside the carrier for the duration of the flight and must not be taken out in the cabin.

## Hold Cargo for Pets

Viva Aerobus does not offer a live animal hold cargo service on most routes. If your pet exceeds the in-cabin weight limit or if you are travelling on a route where in-cabin pets are not accepted, Viva Aerobus is not suitable for your pet's transport.

For international or long-haul routes, consider carriers with full live animal cargo programmes such as Aeromexico or international carriers serving Mexico City.

## Booking a Pet on Viva Aerobus

Pet spaces on Viva Aerobus are limited and must be reserved in advance. Contact Viva Aerobus customer service or add the pet at the time of booking online. A pet fee applies per flight.

Confirm all details -- including breed acceptance, current fees, and carrier dimension limits -- when making your booking.

## Brachycephalic Breed Restrictions

Viva Aerobus applies breed restrictions to flat-faced dogs and cats. Pugs, French Bulldogs, English Bulldogs, Boston Terriers, Shih Tzus, and Persian cats may be refused. Confirm acceptance of your specific breed directly when booking.

## Routes and Network

Viva Aerobus operates from its main hubs at Monterrey (MTY) and Mexico City (MEX), with a domestic network covering most major Mexican cities. The carrier also operates international routes to the USA and other Latin American destinations, though pet policies on international routes may differ from domestic ones.
