PAL Express is the domestic regional subsidiary of Philippine Airlines, operating routes between Manila, Cebu, Davao, and a network of secondary Philippine cities and islands. The airline focuses on domestic connectivity.

## PAL Express and pet transport

PAL Express accepts pets as cargo or checked baggage in the hold on its domestic routes. In-cabin pet travel is not offered on PAL Express services.

For domestic pet transport within the Philippines, the process is:
- Book space with PAL Express cargo in advance
- Provide a hard-sided IATA-compliant crate
- Present a health certificate from a licensed veterinarian (particularly useful for island-to-island routes where there may be local animal movement rules)

## International pet transport to the Philippines

For international pet transport, the relevant carrier is Philippine Airlines (PAL), not PAL Express. PAL accepts pets as cargo on international routes through its cargo division. The process involves:

1. BAI (Bureau of Animal Industry) import permit applied for in advance
2. Health certificate from the origin country, endorsed by the national veterinary authority
3. Approved quarantine facility booking (minimum 7 days)
4. PAL Cargo booking for live animals

See our Philippines pet import guide for the complete international import process.

## Domestic travel with pets in the Philippines

Moving a pet between Philippine islands by air requires coordination, as the archipelago nature of the country means most inter-island travel requires flying. PAL Express and Philippine Airlines are the main options, alongside Cebu Pacific which has its own live animal cargo policy for domestic routes.

All domestic pet shipments within the Philippines should be accompanied by a health certificate from a licensed vet. Local BAI offices at origin and destination may inspect the animal.
