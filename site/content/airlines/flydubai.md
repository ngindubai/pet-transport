flydubai is a Dubai-based low-cost carrier operating from Dubai International Airport (DXB) to destinations across the Middle East, Africa, South Asia, Europe, and the former Soviet states. As a budget carrier, flydubai operates a no-frills service model that excludes live animal transport for most passengers.

## flydubai's Pet Policy

flydubai does not accept domestic pets -- dogs or cats -- in the cabin or as cargo on its scheduled services. The airline focuses on passenger efficiency and does not have the cargo handling infrastructure for live animals that full-service carriers maintain.

Certified guide dogs and registered assistance animals are the only exception, travelling in the cabin free of charge with appropriate documentation.

## Pet Transport Alternatives from Dubai

Dubai is one of the best-connected cities in the world for live animal cargo, thanks to Emirates SkyCargo. For pet owners in Dubai:

- **Emirates (EK, DXB)**: Operates the Emirates SkyCargo Live Animals Centre, one of the world's most modern live animal facilities. Accepts pet cargo on most international routes. Some routes also allow small pets in cabin. Extensive global network.
- **Etihad Airways (EY, AUH)**: Abu Dhabi is 90 minutes from Dubai. Etihad accepts cabin pets on some routes and cargo pets internationally. A strong second option.

## UAE Pet Export Requirements

Pets leaving the UAE need an export health certificate from an approved vet registered with the Ministry of Climate Change and Environment (MOCCAE). The certificate must be endorsed by MOCCAE. All destination-country specific requirements (microchip, titre test, permits) must also be met.

## Practical Note

For UAE-based expats using flydubai for budget travel within the region, the practical approach for relocating with a pet is to ship the pet through Emirates Cargo while travelling separately on flydubai. An IPATA agent can coordinate both sides of this arrangement.
