airBaltic is the national carrier of Latvia, operating from Riga International Airport (RIX) with an extensive network of European routes. It is also a significant carrier for Estonian and Lithuanian passengers, offering good connectivity across the Baltic states and to major European hubs. For pet owners in or relocating to the Baltics, airBaltic is the primary regional carrier.

## In-Cabin Pet Policy

airBaltic accepts small dogs and cats in the passenger cabin on applicable routes:

- Combined weight of the pet and carrier: maximum 8 kg
- The carrier must fit under the seat in front of you (typical maximum dimensions approximately 46 x 36 x 20 cm -- confirm on the airBaltic website for your specific aircraft)
- One pet per passenger; a limited number of in-cabin pets per flight
- The pet must remain in the carrier throughout the flight
- A pet fee applies per flight segment

Book your in-cabin pet space when purchasing your ticket. airBaltic allows online addition of in-cabin pets during booking.

## Hold Cargo

airBaltic accepts dogs and cats in the hold when the in-cabin weight limit is exceeded:

- An IATA-compliant hard-sided crate is required
- Brachycephalic breeds are restricted from hold cargo (see below)
- Maximum dimensions and weight vary by aircraft type -- confirm with airBaltic reservations
- Booking is made through airBaltic reservations, not a separate cargo division

## Brachycephalic Breed Restrictions

airBaltic restricts brachycephalic (flat-faced) breeds from hold cargo travel:
- English Bulldogs, French Bulldogs, American Bulldogs
- Pugs, Boston Terriers, Pekingese
- Persian cats, Himalayan cats, Exotic Shorthair cats

Some of these breeds may be accepted in the cabin within the 8 kg combined limit. Confirm with airBaltic for your specific breed and route.

## Route Network for Pet Owners

airBaltic serves major European destinations from Riga (RIX), with additional connections from Tallinn (TLL) and Vilnius (VNO):
- London Gatwick (LGW), Amsterdam (AMS), Paris CDG, Frankfurt (FRA), Berlin (BER)
- Helsinki (HEL), Oslo (OSL), Stockholm Arlanda (ARN), Copenhagen (CPH)
- Brussels (BRU), Vienna (VIE), Munich (MUC), Dublin (DUB)
- Multiple German, Spanish, Italian, and Greek destinations

airBaltic is also a partner with Lufthansa Group and Star Alliance members, which can be relevant for connection routing.

## EU Pet Travel Documentation

For intra-EU routes (Baltic states to EU member states), the EU animal health certificate or EU pet passport is required. Latvia, Estonia, and Lithuania are all EU member states, so the standard EU pet travel framework applies throughout airBaltic's network.

For routes from/to the UK, the APHA-endorsed health certificate and meeting the relevant entry country's specific requirements are needed.

## Practical Notes for Baltic Expat Pet Owners

The Baltic states (Latvia, Estonia, Lithuania) have good veterinary care in the capitals and major cities. EU pet passports are issued at accredited vets. For UK expats in Latvia, Lithuania, or Estonia, airBaltic is the most convenient carrier for returning pets to the UK for veterinary visits or permanent relocation.
