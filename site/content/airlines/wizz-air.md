## Wizz Air: No Pets Accepted

Wizz Air is an ultra-low-cost carrier operating across Eastern Europe, Central Europe, and the Middle East from its main hub at Budapest Ferenc Liszt Airport (BUD). For pet owners, the position is clear: Wizz Air does not accept pets on any of its flights.

## The Policy

**Cabin pets**: Not accepted. No dogs, cats, birds, or other animals may travel in the passenger cabin on Wizz Air flights.

**Hold/cargo pets**: Not accepted. Wizz Air does not handle live animal cargo.

**Exception -- Certified Assistance Dogs**: Recognised assistance dogs (guide dogs, hearing dogs, and documented service animals) are accepted on Wizz Air flights with advance notice. Full certification and official documentation are required. Contact Wizz Air's accessibility team at wizzair.com before booking.

## What This Means for Pet Owners

If your intended route is served only by Wizz Air and you need to transport a pet, you need an alternative. Options depend on your route:

**Eastern Europe routes:** LOT Polish Airlines, Czech Airlines, TAROM, Air Serbia, and Croatia Airlines all operate within Eastern Europe and have pet-in-cabin and/or hold cargo policies.

**UK to Eastern Europe:** Ryanair and easyJet both serve parts of Eastern Europe. Check their individual pet policies -- policies vary by route.

**Direct routes:** If you are moving to a destination where Wizz Air is the only carrier, consider a two-leg journey via a hub that allows pets.

## Connecting Through Wizz Air Destinations

Even if you are not flying Wizz Air yourself, pets can transit through Budapest, Warsaw, or other Wizz Air hubs provided you are on a pet-accepting airline for the leg in question.
