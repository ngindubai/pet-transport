Norwegian Air Shuttle is a major low-cost carrier headquartered in Oslo, operating extensive European short-haul routes as well as some transatlantic services. Norwegian built its reputation on affordable fares and a simplified service model, which has resulted in a stricter policy on live animals than traditional flag carriers.

## Norwegian's Pet Policy

Norwegian Air Shuttle does not accept live animals -- dogs, cats, or other pets -- in the cabin or as cargo on its scheduled services. This is consistent with many low-cost carriers that have chosen to simplify their operations by excluding live animal cargo.

The one exception is registered guide dogs and certified assistance animals, which are accepted in the cabin at no charge. Owners must provide documentation confirming the dog's certification.

## What This Means for Pet Travel from Norway

If you are relocating from Norway with a pet, Norwegian cannot be your carrier for the pet itself. You can fly Norwegian yourself while arranging your pet through a different carrier, but coordination on this is complex -- your pet may arrive on a different flight and day.

The recommended approach for Norway-based pet moves is to use a single PETS-capable carrier for both yourself and your pet, or to use a professional pet transport agent who will arrange separate cargo shipping with a suitable airline while you travel independently.

## Alternative Carriers from Norway

Airlines operating from Oslo that accept pets include:

- **SAS Scandinavian Airlines**: accepts small cabin pets on many routes; cargo pets on selected intercontinental routes
- **Lufthansa**: accepts cabin pets (under 8kg) and cargo pets; extensive European and intercontinental network
- **KLM**: accepts cabin pets; good European and long-haul connectivity via Amsterdam
- **British Airways**: cargo pets on long-haul routes (no cabin pets); operates Oslo-London Heathrow
- **Finnair**: cabin and cargo pets on selected routes

## Practical Note

Norwegian serves as a useful budget option for the human portion of a relocation if you choose to fly separately from your pet. Work with an IPATA agent to coordinate the pet's shipment on a compatible carrier while you travel ahead.
