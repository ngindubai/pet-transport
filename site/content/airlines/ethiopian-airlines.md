Ethiopian Airlines is Africa's largest airline by multiple measures, operating from its Addis Ababa Bole (ADD) hub to over 125 destinations across Africa, Europe, Asia, the Americas, and Australia. For pet owners relocating within Africa or moving between Africa and other continents, Ethiopian Airlines is often the most direct routing option.

**Cabin travel**

Ethiopian Airlines does not permit pets in the cabin on international routes. All pets travel as air cargo in the hold.

**Cargo transport**

Ethiopian Airlines accepts pets as air cargo through its extensive cargo network. Booking must be made in advance through the airline's cargo division or passenger services team. Confirm live animal acceptance on your specific route - not all routes or aircraft types have live animal-approved hold configurations.

**Crate requirements**

Ethiopian Airlines follows IATA live animal regulations:
- Hard-sided, ventilated, IATA-approved crate
- Animal must stand, turn, sit, and lie down without touching the sides or top
- Secure latch mechanism (no padlocks that block emergency access)
- Water and food dishes accessible from outside
- "LIVE ANIMAL" labelling in English on all sides

**Breed restrictions**

Brachycephalic breeds are restricted from cargo. These include English Bulldogs, French Bulldogs, American Bulldogs, Pugs, Boston Terriers, and flat-faced cats (Persians, Himalayans, and others). If you are travelling with a breed that may be affected, confirm acceptance before purchasing tickets.

**Africa network**

Ethiopian's Addis Ababa hub connects to a broader Africa network than almost any other carrier, including destinations in East Africa, West Africa, Central Africa, and Southern Africa. For pet owners relocating to countries where direct flights from Europe or North America are limited, routing via Addis Ababa is often the most practical option.

**Transit at Addis Ababa**

If your pet is transiting at ADD, confirm live animal handling and transit arrangements with Ethiopian Airlines. Transit times for live animals need to be long enough for safe transfer but not so long as to cause welfare concerns.

**Booking**

Contact Ethiopian Airlines' cargo team or call the passenger services line to arrange live animal transport. Confirm route-specific acceptance well in advance of your intended travel date.
