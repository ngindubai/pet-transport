SriLankan Airlines is the national carrier of Sri Lanka, a member of the Oneworld alliance, and the main operator at Bandaranaike International Airport (CMB) near Colombo. The airline serves routes across Asia, the Middle East, and Europe.

## Pets in the hold on SriLankan Airlines

SriLankan Airlines accepts pets as checked baggage in the hold on many international routes. In-cabin travel for privately owned pets is not offered.

The key requirements for pets travelling as checked baggage are:
- IATA-compliant hard-sided crate (the pet must be able to stand, turn, and lie down comfortably with required clearance)
- Total weight (pet plus crate) within the airline's checked baggage limits for live animals
- Advance booking and confirmation through SriLankan Airlines cargo or the ticketing team
- All documentation required by the destination country

## Routing options for Sri Lanka

SriLankan Airlines connects Colombo (CMB) with London Heathrow, Frankfurt, Paris, several Middle Eastern cities, Indian cities, South East Asian destinations, and Australian cities. For owners relocating to Sri Lanka from Europe or the UK, SriLankan Airlines is one of the few carriers with direct routing to CMB.

British Airways and Emirates also serve Colombo, as does Qatar Airways, all of which may offer cargo pet services on the route.

## Sri Lanka import requirements

The Department of Animal Production and Health (DAPH) under Sri Lanka's Ministry of Agriculture manages animal imports. Requirements include:

- ISO 15444 microchip
- Current rabies vaccination
- Veterinary health certificate endorsed by the national authority
- Import permit from DAPH (apply at least three to four weeks before travel)
- Animals may be subject to inspection at CMB on arrival

Sri Lanka does not routinely impose mandatory quarantine for pets from low-risk countries with full documentation, but DAPH officers have discretion to detain animals for inspection.

## Practical notes

SriLankan Airlines is part of the Oneworld alliance, which means it can be a useful option for frequent flyers with Oneworld status moving to Sri Lanka. Its direct routes from London Heathrow (via a transit at CMB with no stop required) make it a convenient routing for UK-based owners.
