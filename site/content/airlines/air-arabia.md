Air Arabia is the Middle East and North Africa's first and largest low-cost carrier, headquartered in Sharjah, UAE. Operating from Sharjah International Airport (SHJ), Air Arabia provides budget-focused short-haul services across the MENA region, Indian subcontinent, and Europe.

## Air Arabia's Pet Policy

Air Arabia does not accept domestic pets -- dogs or cats -- in the cabin or as cargo on its scheduled services. This is consistent with Air Arabia's low-cost model, which simplifies operations by excluding live animal services.

Certified guide dogs and registered assistance animals are the exception and are accepted in the cabin at no extra charge with appropriate certification documentation.

## What This Means for UAE-Based Pet Owners

If you are relocating from Sharjah or the wider UAE with a pet, Air Arabia cannot be used for the pet. The main alternatives operating from the UAE that do accept pets are:

- **Emirates (DXB)**: accepts pets in cargo on most international routes via Emirates SkyCargo. One of the most established live animal programmes in the Gulf region.
- **Etihad Airways (AUH)**: accepts cabin pets (on some routes) and cargo pets. Abu Dhabi is approximately 45 minutes from Sharjah.
- **flydubai (DXB)**: a budget carrier with slightly more developed pet policies than Air Arabia on some routes -- confirm current policy.

## UAE Pet Import and Export Rules

When moving a pet out of the UAE, the UAE Ministry of Climate Change and Environment (MOCCAE) governs live animal export. An export health certificate from an MOCCAE-approved vet is required, along with all destination-country specific documentation. MOCCAE-registered animal care facilities and vets are listed on their website.

## Practical Advice

For UAE pet owners wanting a budget option: consider whether splitting yourself from your pet is practical. You could fly Air Arabia independently while your pet travels as manifest cargo on Emirates or Etihad. An IPATA agent can manage the cargo side while you fly separately.
