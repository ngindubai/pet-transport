## Flying to Malta with Your Pet

Malta is a small EU island nation in the central Mediterranean, and KM Malta Airlines (which replaced Air Malta in 2024) operates European routes from Malta International Airport (MLA). For pet owners moving to or from Malta, the airline's pet policy and Malta's own biosecurity rules both need careful attention.

## KM Malta Airlines Pet Policy

KM Malta Airlines accepts pets on most European routes:

**In-cabin pets:**
- Small dogs, cats, and small birds in an IATA-compliant soft carrier
- Combined weight (pet plus carrier) typically up to 8 kg
- Carrier must fit under the seat in front
- One pet per passenger in cabin
- Advance booking required -- contact the airline directly

**Hold/cargo pets:**
- Larger dogs and cats travel in the aircraft hold
- IATA-compliant rigid crate required
- Weight, breed, and route restrictions may apply
- Not all aircraft on Malta routes have hold temperature management year-round -- confirm for summer travel given Malta's high temperatures

Always confirm your specific booking with the airline directly. KM Malta Airlines is a newer carrier and policies may evolve.

## Malta's Pet Import Requirements

Malta is an EU member, but as an island, biosecurity matters are taken seriously by the Malta Agriculture and Fisheries Agency (MAFA).

**From other EU countries:**
- Valid EU Pet Passport
- Microchip (ISO standard)
- Current rabies vaccination

**From the UK (post-Brexit):**
- Animal Health Certificate (AHC) -- issued within 10 days of travel by a UK Official Vet
- Microchip
- Current rabies vaccination
- Tapeworm treatment for dogs (1 to 5 days before arrival)

**Rabies vaccine note:** Malta was historically strict about rabies vaccinations. Verify current MAFA requirements before booking, particularly if this is your pet's first rabies vaccination (21-day waiting period applies).

## Malta International Airport (MLA)

MLA has a veterinary inspection facility. Live animal arrivals are processed through the cargo and live animal terminal. Plan for extra time at the airport on arrival.

## Summer Travel Warning

Malta is very hot in summer (July and August temperatures regularly exceed 35 degrees C). Most airlines reduce or suspend live animal cargo acceptance in extreme heat. If you are planning a summer move, confirm with the airline that pets are accepted on your specific dates.
