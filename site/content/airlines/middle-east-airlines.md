Middle East Airlines (MEA), Lebanon's national carrier, operates services from Beirut Rafic Hariri International Airport (BEY) to destinations in Europe, the Middle East and Africa. For the large Lebanese diaspora community and for expats working in Lebanon, MEA is often the primary carrier for pet relocation.

## Does MEA Accept Pets?

Middle East Airlines accepts **small cats and dogs in the passenger cabin** on selected routes. Larger animals may be shipped through MEA's cargo services, though dedicated live animal cargo handling requires advance arrangement.

## Pets in the Cabin

For cabin pet acceptance on MEA:
- Combined weight of pet plus carrier must not exceed approximately 7 to 8 kg (confirm current limit with MEA directly as this can change)
- Soft-sided, ventilated carrier required that fits under the seat
- Maximum 1 pet per passenger; limited pets per flight
- The pet must remain in the carrier and under the seat for the duration of the flight
- Not available on all routes -- confirm route-specific acceptance before booking
- A health certificate issued by a licensed veterinarian is generally required

Pets are accepted on MEA as **excess baggage** (not as freight), which means they are checked in at the passenger terminal, not the cargo terminal.

## Cargo and Larger Animals

For dogs that exceed the cabin weight limit, MEA cargo services may offer options, but live animal cargo on MEA requires direct coordination with MEA Cargo (mecargo.com.lb). Capacity and handling for larger animals varies and not all routes may be available for live animal freight.

## Lebanon Pet Import Requirements

Arriving in Lebanon with a pet requires compliance with the Ministry of Agriculture's animal import rules:

- **ISO microchip**
- **Valid vaccinations**: rabies, distemper, parvovirus for dogs; rabies, feline panleukopenia for cats; all within valid periods
- **Official health certificate** issued by an accredited vet in the country of origin and endorsed by the government veterinary authority of that country
- For dogs, additional vaccinations (hepatitis, leptospirosis, kennel cough) are recommended and in some cases required
- An **import permit** may be required depending on the country of origin -- confirm with the Lebanese Ministry of Agriculture Veterinary Services

Lebanon does not generally impose a quarantine period for pets from recognised countries that arrive with complete documentation, but the veterinary inspector at Beirut Airport has discretion to detain animals if documentation is incomplete.

## Returning from Lebanon with a Pet

Pets leaving Lebanon require an export health certificate from the Lebanese Ministry of Agriculture Veterinary Services. This is issued after a physical inspection and document review at a designated veterinary office. Book this appointment well in advance of travel.

The health certificate must then be endorsed by the destination country's required format. For EU-bound pets, the TRACES system notification and the relevant EU-format certificate apply.

## Key Routes for Pet Owners

MEA's most relevant routes for pet transport include:
- **Beirut (BEY) to London (LHR)** -- direct service
- **Beirut (BEY) to Paris (CDG)** -- direct service
- **Beirut (BEY) to Frankfurt (FRA)** -- regular service
- **Beirut (BEY) to Dubai (DXB)** -- Middle East connections
- **Beirut (BEY) to Larnaca (LCA)** and **Athens (ATH)** -- regional Mediterranean routes
