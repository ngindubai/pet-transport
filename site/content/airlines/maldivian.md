Maldivian, operated by Island Aviation Services, is the Maldives' domestic airline, connecting Male (MLE) with outer atolls and resort islands using turboprop aircraft. It is the primary way to reach many of the country's inhabited islands beyond the main tourist seaplane radius.

## Pet transport in the Maldives context

The Maldives is an archipelago nation with strict biosecurity rules. Most islands are small and ecologically sensitive, and the importation of non-native animals is tightly controlled.

Long-term residence with a pet in the Maldives is unusual and heavily restricted. Most expatriates working in the country on short-to-medium contracts do not bring pets. Tourists on resort stays cannot bring pets.

## Maldivian's domestic pet service

Maldivian's aircraft (ATR 42 and ATR 72 turboprops) are small and primarily configured for passengers and essential cargo. Live animal transport is not listed as a standard service.

If you have a genuine operational need to move an animal between atolls, contact Maldivian directly at maldivian.aero. Special arrangements may be possible for service animals or animals belonging to long-term residents, but this is not a routine service.

## International arrivals at Male (MLE)

Emirates, Qatar Airways, Singapore Airlines, Sri Lankan Airlines, and several other carriers serve Velana International Airport (MLE). For any pet import into the Maldives, the first step is obtaining approval from the Ministry of Fisheries, Marine Resources and Agriculture (MOFMRA). Without this, no airline will accept an animal for travel to the Maldives.

## Import regulations

The MOFMRA controls all live animal imports. The import permit application must be made before any booking. Dogs and cats face very restricted entry pathways, particularly for long-term residence.

Contact the MOFMRA at mofmra.gov.mv for current regulations before making any plans to move a pet to the Maldives.
