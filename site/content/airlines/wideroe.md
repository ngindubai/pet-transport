Wideroe (IATA: WF) is Norway's largest regional airline, serving over 40 destinations across Norway from its main base at Bergen Flesland Airport (BGO). The airline uses a fleet primarily composed of Bombardier Dash 8 turboprops, which are well-suited to Norway's network of short-strip coastal airports.

## Pet Policy Overview

Wideroe accepts small pets in the cabin on most of its Norwegian domestic routes. Small dogs and cats travelling in an approved carrier may be taken on board, subject to:

- Combined weight (pet plus carrier) generally **not exceeding 8 kg**
- The carrier must fit under the seat in front of you
- A pet supplement fee applies
- Only a limited number of pets are permitted per flight; advance booking with Wideroe's customer service is required

For larger pets or hold transport, contact Wideroe directly. The small turboprop aircraft types (Dash 8) have limited cargo hold space, and live animal hold transport on these aircraft is handled differently from wide-body jets. Confirm current availability and procedures with Wideroe's cargo team.

## Norway's Pet Entry Requirements

Norway is not an EU member state, but it is part of the EEA and the Schengen Area and applies equivalent rules to EU pet travel regulations. The Norwegian Food Safety Authority (Mattilsynet) oversees animal import:

**From EU countries:**
- EU pet passport, ISO microchip, current rabies vaccination -- accepted
- No titre test required for EU-to-Norway moves

**From the UK (post-Brexit):**
- UK pets are treated as third-country; similar rules to UK-to-EU travel
- Animal Health Certificate (AHC) in place of EU pet passport
- Rabies titre test and 3-month wait applies if the UK pet does not have a valid prior titre result on record

**From non-EU/non-EEA countries (USA, Australia, etc.):**
- Third-country protocol: microchip, rabies vaccination, FAVN titre test (0.5 IU/ml), 3-month wait, government-endorsed health certificate
- Mattilsynet pre-notification required

## Wideroe's Domestic Network

Wideroe connects some of Norway's most remote communities -- fjord-side towns, Lofoten Islands, Svalbard connections (via Tromso), Arctic north and many communities inaccessible by other air routes. For pet owners living in western or northern Norway, Wideroe is often the only air option for connecting to international hubs.

The typical Wideroe routing for an international pet journey would be: Wideroe regional flight to Bergen or Oslo, then connection with SAS, Lufthansa, British Airways or another international carrier for the onward international segment.

## Connecting Through Oslo or Bergen

Oslo Gardermoen (OSL) and Bergen Flesland (BGO) both handle international arrivals and are the main hubs for pets entering or leaving Norway by air. For pets arriving from non-EU third countries, the border inspection and Mattilsynet check will occur at the first Norwegian entry point.
