Binter Canarias, operating under IATA code NT, is a Spanish regional airline based in the Canary Islands. Binter operates an extensive inter-island network connecting Las Palmas (Gran Canaria), Tenerife, Lanzarote, Fuerteventura, La Palma, El Hierro and La Gomera. It also operates some routes to nearby Africa (Mauritania, Senegal, Morocco, Cape Verde) and seasonal routes to mainland Spain. For residents of the Canary Islands, Binter is often the only realistic option for travelling between islands with a pet.

## Cabin Pet Policy

Binter Canarias accepts small pets in the passenger cabin:

- **Eligible pets**: cats and small dogs
- **Weight limit**: combined weight of pet plus carrier up to approximately 8 kg; confirm current limit with Binter as this may vary
- **Carrier type**: soft-sided carrier fitting under the seat in front; Binter's regional ATR and Embraer aircraft have smaller under-seat spaces than wide-body jets -- measure the specific aircraft type's under-seat dimensions before buying a carrier
- **Advance booking required**: add the pet at time of booking; walk-up pets are not guaranteed
- **Documentation**: EU pet passport with current rabies vaccination and ISO microchip; Spain-standard documentation applies; Canary Islands are part of Spain and therefore part of the EU for veterinary purposes

## Fleet Consideration: ATR Aircraft

Binter Canarias operates ATR 72 regional turboprops on many inter-island routes, alongside Embraer E175 jets. The under-seat carrier dimensions on ATR aircraft are notably smaller than on standard jets:

- The seat pitch and under-seat clearance on an ATR 72 is more limited
- A carrier that fits comfortably under the seat of an A320 or similar may not fit under the seat of an ATR 72
- Confirm the specific aircraft on your route and check current under-seat dimensions with Binter before purchasing your carrier

## Cargo Hold on Regional Routes

For pets that exceed the cabin limit:

- Binter operates hold cargo on its routes, but the cargo hold capacity on regional ATR and Embraer aircraft is limited
- Very large or heavy pets (large-breed dogs with oversized crates) may not be accommodated on some Binter inter-island routes
- Confirm hold pet acceptance with Binter cargo for your specific origin and destination island and the aircraft type

## Connections to Mainland Spain and Africa

Binter also operates routes from the Canary Islands to:
- **Mauritania (NKC)**, **Senegal (DKR)**, **Morocco (RAK, RBA)**, **Cape Verde (SID)**

For routes to African destinations from the Canary Islands, the pet import requirements of the destination country apply. These can differ significantly from EU standards. Confirm requirements with the relevant African country's veterinary authority before travel.

For mainland Spain connections, Binter or other Spanish carriers (Iberia, Vueling) operate Canary Islands to peninsula routes. Standard EU pet passport rules apply for domestic Spain and intra-EU travel.

## Spanish Pet Registration

All dogs, cats and ferrets in Spain must be registered with the National Registry for Companion Animals (REIAC -- Registro de Identificacion de Animales de Compania). Registration is done through a vet. The REIAC database is the reference system used by Binter and Spanish border control when verifying microchip identity. EU pet passports issued in Spain are linked to the REIAC registry.
