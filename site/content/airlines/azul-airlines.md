Azul Brazilian Airlines is Brazil's third-largest carrier by passenger volume, known for its distinctive blue branding and extensive domestic network serving cities not covered by LATAM or Gol. Azul was founded by JetBlue co-founder David Neeleman and has a reputation for good service.

## Pets in the cabin on Azul

Azul accepts small pets in the cabin on domestic Brazilian routes. The rules at time of writing are:

- Maximum combined weight (pet plus carrier): 10 kg
- Carrier must fit under the seat in front (approximate maximum dimensions: 45 x 35 x 22 cm; confirm with Azul)
- Only one pet per passenger
- Pet must remain in carrier throughout the flight
- Advance booking required; contact Azul when purchasing your ticket
- Fee applies per segment

Dogs, cats, and some other small animals are accepted. Confirm accepted species with Azul before booking.

## Pets in the hold on Azul

Azul accepts larger pets as checked baggage in the aircraft hold on domestic routes. Hard-sided IATA-compliant crates are required. The animal must be able to stand, turn around, and lie down comfortably in the crate.

Total weight limits (pet plus crate) apply and vary by route and aircraft type. Contact Azul cargo for the current limits for your specific journey.

## International routes

Azul's international network connects Brazil with Fort Lauderdale (FLL), Orlando (MCO), and European cities including Lisbon. For international pet transport on Azul routes, the destination country's import requirements also apply, and Azul's cargo team will need to confirm acceptance for live animals on international sectors.

For long-haul international pet transport to or from Brazil, many owners use LATAM Airlines Cargo, TAP Air Portugal, or other established cargo carriers with Brazil routes.

## Brazil pet export requirements

Pets leaving Brazil for international destinations need:
- A health certificate from a registered veterinarian in Brazil
- Government endorsement from MAPA (Ministry of Agriculture, Livestock and Food Supply)
- Microchip (ISO standard)
- Current vaccinations as required by the destination country

Brazil is a large country with excellent veterinary care in major cities including Sao Paulo, Rio de Janeiro, and Campinas (near Azul's hub).
