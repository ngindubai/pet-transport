# Pet Transport from Jordan: Export Guide

Moving abroad from Jordan with a dog or cat means preparing the correct export documentation before you leave. The rules depend on where you are heading -- but the starting point is always the same: get your pet microchipped (if not already), vaccinate against rabies, and obtain an official health certificate endorsed by GDVS.

This guide covers what Jordan requires before your pet leaves, the most common destination requirements for Jordan pets, and which airlines serve the main corridors.

## Export requirements for Jordan

Before your pet can leave Jordan, the following must be in place:

- **Microchip:** ISO 11784/11785 standard. Must be implanted before or on the same day as the first rabies vaccination.
- **Rabies vaccination:** Required and must be valid at the time of travel. Allow the full post-vaccination wait before international travel.
- **Titre test (if required):** Required for: Pets from rabies-endemic countries. Minimum: 0.5 IU/ml.
- **Health certificate:** Official health certificate endorsed by GDVS. GDVS endorsement required.
- **Quarantine at destination:** No routine quarantine for compliant pets. Veterinary inspection on arrival.
- **Import permit (destination):** Required (apply 4 weeks in advance from Ministry of Agriculture, General Directorate of Veterinary Services)
- **Banned breeds at destination:** American Pit Bull Terrier, Rottweiler, Dogo Argentino (varies by destination country -- always verify).


## Airlines commonly used from Jordan

- **Royal Jordanian** - cabin and cargo
- **Emirates** - cargo only
- **Turkish Airlines** - cabin and cargo
- **British Airways** - cargo only
- **Lufthansa** - cabin and cargo
- **Qatar Airways** - cargo only


## Quick checklist: leaving Jordan with your pet

1. Microchip (ISO standard) -- must be in before any rabies vaccination
2. Rabies vaccination -- must be current at travel date
3. Titre test (if destination requires it) -- blood drawn 30+ days after vaccination
4. Wait period after titre test (90 or 180 days depending on destination)
5. Apply for destination country import permit
6. Book IATA-compliant crate and cargo/cabin space with airline
7. Obtain official health certificate from GDVS-endorsed veterinarian
8. Travel day -- arrive at airport 3-4 hours early for cargo pets
