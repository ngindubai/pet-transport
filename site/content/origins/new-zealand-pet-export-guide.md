# Pet Transport from New Zealand: Export Guide

Moving abroad from New Zealand with a dog or cat means preparing the correct export documentation before you leave. The rules depend on where you are heading -- but the starting point is always the same: get your pet microchipped (if not already), vaccinate against rabies, and obtain an official health certificate endorsed by MPI.

This guide covers what New Zealand requires before your pet leaves, the most common destination requirements for New Zealand pets, and which airlines serve the main corridors.

## Export requirements for New Zealand

Before your pet can leave New Zealand, the following must be in place:

- **Microchip:** ISO 11784/11785 standard. Must be implanted before or on the same day as the first rabies vaccination.
- **Rabies vaccination:** Required and must be valid at the time of travel. Allow the full post-vaccination wait before international travel.
- **Titre test (if required):** Required for: All pets from all countries (no exemptions). Minimum: 0.5 IU/ml. 180-day wait after successful result.
- **Health certificate:** MPI-issued official health certificate. Australia requires MPI-approved specific format. MPI endorsement required.
- **Quarantine at destination:** Mandatory quarantine at MPI Levin Quarantine Facility, Levin, Manawatu (the only approved facility) (10 (minimum) at MPI Levin facility)
- **Import permit (destination):** Required (apply 8 weeks in advance from Ministry for Primary Industries (MPI), Biosecurity New Zealand)
- **Banned breeds at destination:** American Pit Bull Terrier, Dogo Argentino, Brazilian Fila, Japanese Tosa (varies by destination country -- always verify).

## Quick checklist: leaving New Zealand with your pet

1. Microchip (ISO standard) -- must be in before any rabies vaccination
2. Rabies vaccination -- must be current at travel date
3. Titre test (if destination requires it) -- blood drawn 30+ days after vaccination
4. Wait period after titre test (90 or 180 days depending on destination)
5. Apply for destination country import permit
6. Book IATA-compliant crate and cargo/cabin space with airline
7. Obtain official health certificate from MPI-endorsed veterinarian
8. Travel day -- arrive at airport 3-4 hours early for cargo pets
