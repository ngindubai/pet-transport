# Pet Transport from Spain: Export Guide

Moving abroad from Spain with a dog or cat means preparing the correct export documentation before you leave. The rules depend on where you are heading -- but the starting point is always the same: get your pet microchipped (if not already), vaccinate against rabies, and obtain an official health certificate endorsed by MAPA.

This guide covers what Spain requires before your pet leaves, the most common destination requirements for Spain pets, and which airlines serve the main corridors.

## Export requirements for Spain

Before your pet can leave Spain, the following must be in place:

- **Microchip:** ISO 11784/11785 standard. Must be implanted before or on the same day as the first rabies vaccination.
- **Rabies vaccination:** Required and must be valid at the time of travel. Allow the full post-vaccination wait before international travel.
- **Titre test (if required):** Required for: Pets from non-EU countries not on the EU listed third countries. Minimum: 0.5 IU/ml. 90-day wait after successful result.
- **Health certificate:** EU AHC for non-EU travel. EU Pet Passport for EU/EFTA travel. MAPA endorsement required for non-EU/EFTA destinations.
- **Quarantine at destination:** No routine quarantine for compliant pets. Veterinary inspection on arrival.
- **Import permit (destination):** Not required for most commercial pets. EU pet passport or AHC serves as travel document.
- **Banned breeds at destination:** American Pit Bull Terrier, Staffordshire Bull Terrier, American Staffordshire Terrier, Rottweiler, Dogo Argentino and others (varies by destination country -- always verify).

## Quick checklist: leaving Spain with your pet

1. Microchip (ISO standard) -- must be in before any rabies vaccination
2. Rabies vaccination -- must be current at travel date
3. Titre test (if destination requires it) -- blood drawn 30+ days after vaccination
4. Wait period after titre test (90 or 180 days depending on destination)
5. Apply for destination country import permit
6. Book IATA-compliant crate and cargo/cabin space with airline
7. Obtain official health certificate from MAPA-endorsed veterinarian
8. Travel day -- arrive at airport 3-4 hours early for cargo pets
