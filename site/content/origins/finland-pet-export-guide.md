# Pet Transport from Finland: Export Guide

Moving abroad from Finland with a dog or cat means preparing the correct export documentation before you leave. The rules depend on where you are heading -- but the starting point is always the same: get your pet microchipped (if not already), vaccinate against rabies, and obtain an official health certificate endorsed by Ruokavirasto.

This guide covers what Finland requires before your pet leaves, the most common destination requirements for Finland pets, and which airlines serve the main corridors.

## Export requirements for Finland

Before your pet can leave Finland, the following must be in place:

- **Microchip:** ISO 11784/11785 standard. Must be implanted before or on the same day as the first rabies vaccination.
- **Rabies vaccination:** Required and must be valid at the time of travel. Allow the full post-vaccination wait before international travel.
- **Titre test (if required):** Required for: Pets from non-EU third countries not on EU listed countries list. Minimum: 0.5 IU/ml. 90-day wait after successful result.
- **Health certificate:** EU AHC for non-EU travel. EU Pet Passport for EU/EFTA travel. Ruokavirasto (Finnish Food Authority) endorsement required for non-EU/EFTA destinations.
- **Quarantine at destination:** No routine quarantine for compliant pets. Veterinary inspection on arrival.
- **Import permit (destination):** Not required for most commercial pets. EU pet passport or AHC serves as travel document.
- **Banned breeds at destination:** None (varies by destination country -- always verify).


## Airlines commonly used from Finland

- **Finnair** - cabin and cargo
- **SAS Scandinavian Airlines** - cabin and cargo
- **Lufthansa** - cabin and cargo
- **British Airways** - cargo only
- **KLM** - cabin and cargo
- **Air France** - cabin and cargo


## Quick checklist: leaving Finland with your pet

1. Microchip (ISO standard) -- must be in before any rabies vaccination
2. Rabies vaccination -- must be current at travel date
3. Titre test (if destination requires it) -- blood drawn 30+ days after vaccination
4. Wait period after titre test (90 or 180 days depending on destination)
5. Apply for destination country import permit
6. Book IATA-compliant crate and cargo/cabin space with airline
7. Obtain official health certificate from Ruokavirasto-endorsed veterinarian
8. Travel day -- arrive at airport 3-4 hours early for cargo pets
