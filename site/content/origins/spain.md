sections:
  - heading: "Export requirements from Spain"
    body: |
      **EU Pet Passport:** If travelling within the EU, your pet needs a valid EU Pet Passport issued by an authorised veterinarian in Spain
      
      **Health Certificate:** For non-EU destinations, an official health certificate (Annex IV) must be issued by an authorised veterinarian within 10 days of travel
      
      **Government Endorsement:** The Oficina Comarcal Agraria (OCA) or regional veterinary authority must endorse the health certificate for non-EU destinations
      
      **Microchip:** ISO-compliant microchip must be in place before rabies vaccination
      
      **Rabies Vaccination:** Must be current and administered after microchip. Primary vaccination valid 21 days after injection

faqs:
  - question: "What do I need to export my pet from Spain?"
    answer: "For EU destinations, you need a valid EU Pet Passport with current rabies vaccination. For non-EU destinations like the UK (post-Brexit), you need a health certificate endorsed by the regional veterinary authority, issued within 10 days of travel."
  - question: "Can I take my pet from Spain to the UK?"
    answer: "Yes. Since Brexit, the UK requires an Animal Health Certificate (not the EU Pet Passport) issued by an official veterinarian in Spain within 10 days of travel. Your pet needs a microchip and current rabies vaccination at least 21 days old."
