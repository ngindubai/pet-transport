# Pet Transport from Hungary: Export Guide

Moving abroad from Hungary with a dog or cat means preparing the correct export documentation before you leave. The rules depend on where you are heading -- but the starting point is always the same: get your pet microchipped (if not already), vaccinate against rabies, and obtain an official health certificate endorsed by NEBIH.

This guide covers what Hungary requires before your pet leaves, the most common destination requirements for Hungary pets, and which airlines serve the main corridors.

## Export requirements for Hungary

Before your pet can leave Hungary, the following must be in place:

- **Microchip:** ISO 11784/11785 standard. Must be implanted before or on the same day as the first rabies vaccination.
- **Rabies vaccination:** Required and must be valid at the time of travel. Allow the full post-vaccination wait before international travel.
- **Titre test (if required):** Required for: Pets from non-listed third countries. Minimum: 0.5 IU/ml. 90-day wait after successful result.
- **Health certificate:** EU AHC for non-EU travel. EU Pet Passport for EU/EFTA travel. NEBIH endorsement required for non-EU/EFTA destinations.
- **Quarantine at destination:** No routine quarantine for compliant pets. Veterinary inspection on arrival.
- **Import permit (destination):** Not required for most commercial pets. EU pet passport or AHC serves as travel document.
- **Banned breeds at destination:** American Pit Bull Terrier, American Staffordshire Terrier (varies by destination country -- always verify).


## Airlines commonly used from Hungary

- **Wizz Air** - cargo only
- **Ryanair** - cargo only
- **Lufthansa** - cabin and cargo
- **Austrian Airlines** - cabin and cargo
- **British Airways** - cargo only
- **KLM** - cabin and cargo


## Quick checklist: leaving Hungary with your pet

1. Microchip (ISO standard) -- must be in before any rabies vaccination
2. Rabies vaccination -- must be current at travel date
3. Titre test (if destination requires it) -- blood drawn 30+ days after vaccination
4. Wait period after titre test (90 or 180 days depending on destination)
5. Apply for destination country import permit
6. Book IATA-compliant crate and cargo/cabin space with airline
7. Obtain official health certificate from NEBIH-endorsed veterinarian
8. Travel day -- arrive at airport 3-4 hours early for cargo pets
