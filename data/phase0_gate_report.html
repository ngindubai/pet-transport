3. Data Gaps and Known Limitations

Medium Priority (address during Phase 1, before content publication)

4 countries at medium/medium-high confidence. UAE, Canada, Hong Kong, and South Africa government portals all blocked automated extraction. Data was compiled from published guidelines and working knowledge. Before publishing route pages involving these countries, verify key requirements (quarantine periods, import permit fees, approved ports of entry) against current official sources. None of the data is suspected to be wrong, but it hasn't been cross-checked against the primary government portal.
United Airlines PetSafe cargo suspended. This is a known industry fact, but the suspension has been in place since 2018/2019. Monitor for any reinstatement. For now, USA-origin route pages for large dogs must route through American Airlines cargo or third-party cargo consolidators.
Delta Air Lines cargo restricted to military. Same situation. Confirm current status before publishing USA-origin route pages. If Delta resumes public cargo, update the airline database.
Keyword volume estimates are logic-based. No SEMrush, Ahrefs, or Google Keyword Planner data was used. Volumes are estimated from population size, expat community volumes, known relocation patterns, and route complexity. Acceptable for prioritisation, but should be validated against real search data once tools are available. The relative ordering (Tier 1 vs Tier 2 vs Tier 3) is likely correct even if absolute volumes are off.
Some airline policies from knowledge, not live scraping. Several airline pet policy pages blocked automated extraction. Policies for the 4 Block 7 additions (JAL, Thai Airways, Garuda Indonesia, Philippine Airlines) were compiled from published guidelines. Verify against each airline's current cargo/pets page before content goes live.

Low Priority (nice to have, not blocking)

4. Key Strategic Findings from Phase 0

  • Primary competitive advantage: PetRelocation.com has ZERO route-level pages. No competitor owns "pet transport [country A] to [country B]" queries. This is confirmed, validated, and the entire content strategy is built around it.
  • Australia is the hardest destination: Mandatory quarantine (Mickleham facility only), country group system (1-6), 180-day minimum process for Group 4+ countries. Australia routes will have the longest, most detailed pages.
  • CDC high-risk classification drives content: UAE and South Africa are CDC HIGH-RISK for dog imports to USA. This creates procedural complexity (CDC Dog Import Form, USDA-endorsed vet cert, serology) that becomes detailed, authoritative content.
  • Brachycephalic breeds = high search volume content: "Can I fly my French Bulldog?" type queries have volume. Nearly all airlines ban or restrict brachycephalic breeds from cargo. Breed guide pages are a confirmed content play.
  • 15 Tier 1 routes identified: UK-AU, UAE-UK, UAE-AU, UAE-USA, AU-UK, HK-UK, HK-AU, UK-UAE, USA-UK, USA-AU, UK-FR, UK-SG, SG-AU, AU-SG, SA-AU. These get built first.
  • Cats are underserved: USA has ZERO federal requirements for cat imports. Most countries treat cats as simpler. Cat-specific content is a differentiation opportunity.
  • BNO visa scheme driving HK-UK demand: Hong Kong to UK pet transport is a growing route due to the BNO visa program. Timely content opportunity.
