🐾 Pet Transport Pricing Research Guide Staff Reference

Use this guide to contact pet transport companies across 10 key countries, collect doorstep-to-doorstep prices on benchmark routes, and compare providers. Last updated: May 2026

How to Use This Guide

  1. Pick your country section

    Each country below has 10 verified pet transport companies. Work through one country at a time.

  2. Find the benchmark routes

    We use the same 8 standard routes for every country so results are comparable. See the Routes section below.

  3. Find real doorstep addresses using Google Maps

    For each route, you need a realistic residential pickup address in the origin city and a residential delivery address in the destination city. Pick a typical suburb address (not an airport or commercial address). Copy the full street address.

  4. Send the email using the templates below

    Fill in the highlighted placeholders in the email template, then copy and send. Use Template A for most companies. Use Template B if they require a contact form (note the website link instead of email).

  5. Log the response in the Price Tracker tab

    When a quote arrives, log the price, date received, and any conditions (e.g. crate included, vet paperwork extra) in the tracker spreadsheet.

  6. Follow up after 5 working days

    If no reply, send Template C (the follow-up). Mark status as "Chased" in the tracker.

Important: We want doorstep-to-doorstep pricing only. This means the company collects the pet from a home address, handles all paperwork, travel crates, airline booking, customs clearance, and delivers to a home address on the other side. Make this clear in every email.

What to specify for each quote

  • Pet type: 1 x medium dog (approx 10kg, mixed breed, in good health) — this keeps comparisons fair
  • Service level: Full-service, doorstep pickup to doorstep delivery
  • Paperwork: Ask if vet health certificate, microchipping check and import permits are included
  • Crate: Ask if IATA-approved travel crate is included or extra
  • Timing: Request prices as if travel is 3 months from today (gives time for any titre tests etc.)

Finding Doorstep Addresses

  • Open Google Maps and search for a residential suburb in the origin city (e.g. "Clapham, London" or "Brooklyn, New York")
  • Pick any realistic house number on a residential street — not a hotel, airport or office
  • Copy the full address including postcode / zip code
  • Do the same for the destination city
  • Paste both addresses into the email template where indicated

Tips for Getting the Best Responses

Advice for Staff

  • Send from a professional email address — use your PetTransportGlobal email, not a personal one. Companies are more likely to respond to a business address.
  • Be specific about addresses — the more specific your pickup and delivery addresses, the more accurate the quote. Use Google Maps to find real residential streets.
  • Don't be surprised by form-only companies — many premium operators have moved to quote forms only. Fill them in fully for best results.
  • Note what is NOT included — some quotes will exclude the health certificate vet visit, quarantine costs (especially Australia), or final mile delivery. Note everything.
  • Australia routes are expensive — expect £3,000–£7,000+ for UK/US to Australia due to strict biosecurity. Don't be alarmed.
  • Chase by phone if no email reply — all confirmed phone numbers are listed. A quick call often gets a faster quote than email.
  • Keep a copy of every email sent — BCC yourself on all outgoing emails so you have a timestamped record.
  • IPATA members are more reliable — companies marked IPATA are members of the International Pet and Animal Transportation Association and follow industry standards.
